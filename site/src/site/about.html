PanicStation.org provides calm, practical first steps for situations that can feel sudden, confusing, or panic-inducing.

The goal is simple: help you stabilise the moment, avoid common mistakes, and work out what matters right now versus what can safely wait.

What this site is (and isn’t)

  • It is: short, action-focused guidance designed to get you moving when you feel stuck.
  • It isn’t: a replacement for emergency services, professional advice, or local authority guidance.

How to use PanicStation

  • Pick your jurisdiction (UK/USA) before reading a guide. Processes and terminology differ.
  • Search using plain language (what you would type into Google). The suggestions are designed to catch “real-world” phrasing.
  • Use the guide structure: “Short answer” → “Do not do these things” → “What to do now” → “What can wait”.
  • When in doubt: do the stabilising steps first (reduce immediate harm), then gather facts, then escalate if needed.

What “first steps” means

Most guides aim to help you:

  • stop something getting worse today (fees, penalties, enforcement, safety risks, loss of access)
  • get the right person/department/agency the first time
  • create a short written record you can reuse
  • avoid irreversible actions made under pressure

Content approach

  • Plain language: minimal jargon, clear options, realistic next actions.
  • “Don’t do” warnings: because panic often leads to mistakes that are hard to undo.
  • Scope notes: to explain what the guide covers and where professional help might be needed.

Bottom line: this site is designed to help you take the next sensible step when your brain is saying “I can’t think straight”.
