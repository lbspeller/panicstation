# What to do if… your burglar or fire alarm system is sounding and you cannot silence it or verify why (USA)

## Short answer
Treat it as real until you have a clear reason not to. Get people and pets to a safer place first; if there is any sign of fire, smoke, heat, carbon monoxide symptoms, forced entry, or a possible intruder, leave and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay inside troubleshooting if you smell smoke, see haze, feel unusual heat, hear a fire alarm you cannot explain, or anyone feels dizzy, weak, confused, nauseated, or short of breath.
- Don’t search rooms, open closed doors, or walk through the home looking for the cause if an intruder or fire may be possible.
- Don’t remove batteries, cut wires, shut off breakers, or permanently disable alarms to stop the noise.
- Don’t call 911 just to cancel an accidental monitored burglar-alarm dispatch if your monitoring station or local alarm program tells you to cancel through the monitoring station.
- Don’t enter a neighbor’s property or confront anyone because their alarm is sounding.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer position first.** Get everyone to fresh air outside, or to a locked room with a clear exit route if leaving would take you toward a possible intruder. Bring your phone. Grab keys only if they are immediately reachable.
2. **Notice obvious danger without investigating.** From where you already are, look, listen, and smell for smoke, burning, unusual heat, a carbon monoxide alarm, forced entry, broken glass, voices, or movement. Do not search the home to prove it is safe.
3. **If it may be fire, smoke, carbon monoxide, or an unknown fire alarm:** leave the building and call 911 from outside or from a safe place. Stay out until responders say it is safe to go back in.
4. **If it may be an intruder or break-in:** do not search the house. Leave if you can do so safely, or stay behind a locked door with an exit route, and call 911.
5. **If it is your monitored burglar alarm and you believe it was accidental:** call your alarm monitoring station using the number in your app, keypad, paperwork, or saved contacts. Give the passcode if they ask, say you cannot silence or verify the cause, and follow their instructions about confirming or cancelling dispatch.
6. **If it seems like a nuisance smoke alarm from cooking or steam:** only if you are already confident there is no fire, press the silence button if the unit has one, move smoke or steam away from the alarm, and reassess from a safer position. If it keeps sounding, you cannot identify the cause, or anything feels wrong, leave and call 911.
7. **If it is a neighbor’s or nearby business’s alarm:** stay on public property or inside your own safe space. If you see smoke, fire, forced entry, a crime in progress, or anyone in danger, call 911. If there is no sign of immediate danger, use your local police non-emergency number, 311 if available, building management, or local code/noise process.

## What can wait
- Opening alarm panels, removing devices, replacing batteries, resetting breakers, or doing wiring-related troubleshooting.
- Deciding whether to replace the alarm system, change monitoring companies, or dispute a false-alarm fee.
- Writing a full incident report, checking every sensor, or explaining the situation to neighbors.
- Cleaning up, airing out the whole home, or going back inside for belongings before it has been checked or the alarm cause is clear.

## Important reassurance
A loud alarm is meant to make your body react quickly. Taking the safer option, leaving, getting behind a locked door, or calling for help when you cannot verify the cause, is a reasonable first response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, for the immediate moment when the alarm is sounding and the cause is unclear. Later decisions may need help from your monitoring company, landlord or building management, local fire department, local police non-emergency line, or a licensed alarm or fire-protection professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional safety, security, legal, medical, or technical advice. Follow directions from emergency responders, your alarm monitoring station, building management, and local authorities.
