# What to do if… your phone is stuck in a restart loop after a software update (USA)

## Short answer
Keep the phone charged and cool, try one proper forced restart, then use the official Apple, Google, Samsung, or manufacturer recovery route before choosing anything that says restore, reset, wipe, or erase. Protect account access and photos first wherever possible.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep forcing restarts over and over once the same loop comes back.
- Don’t factory reset as the first step; it can erase data and may not solve the cause.
- Don’t use random “boot loop repair” apps or paid tools before trying official support routes.
- Don’t unplug the phone during an official update or restore unless the official tool tells you to.
- Don’t assume the phone is permanently dead just because it is stuck on a logo.

## What to do now
1. **Make the phone safer to work on.**  
   Plug it into a known-good charger and cable. Remove the case if it is warm. Disconnect headphones, docks, adapters, power banks, and other accessories. If the phone is hot, leave it charging on a hard surface until it cools.

2. **Try one model-appropriate forced restart.**  
   - **iPhone 8 or later:** quickly press Volume Up, quickly press Volume Down, then press and hold the Side button until the Apple logo appears.
   - **Most Android phones:** if the screen is frozen, holding Power for about 30 seconds may restart it.
   - **Samsung Galaxy phones:** if it is stuck on the Samsung logo, hold Volume Down and Power or Side together for about 10 seconds, then choose Normal Boot if that screen appears.  
   If the phone goes straight back into the same loop, stop repeating this and move to official recovery.

3. **If it is an iPhone, use a computer and avoid Restore unless you accept erasure.**  
   Connect the iPhone to a Mac or Windows PC with the current Apple Devices app, Finder, or iTunes as appropriate. Put the iPhone into recovery mode using Apple’s instructions. If you are offered Update or Restore, try Update first if it is available or not already tried. Restore reinstalls iOS and erases all data, so pause before choosing it.

4. **If it is Android, try Safe Mode only if you can reach it.**  
   Safe Mode temporarily turns off downloaded apps. If the phone reaches Safe Mode, use that chance to back up anything urgent, remove any recently installed or recently updated app that may be causing crashes, and confirm you can access your Google account from another device. If Safe Mode is not reachable, use the phone maker’s official support page for that exact model before choosing a factory reset.

5. **If it is a Samsung Galaxy, try Samsung’s official stuck-logo steps.**  
   Check the device and charger for damage, confirm it has some charge, try the Samsung forced reboot, and use Smart Switch on a Mac or Windows PC if Samsung’s instructions say an update is available. Do not disconnect the USB cable while Smart Switch is updating.

6. **Protect photos and two-factor access if the phone briefly boots.**  
   If you get even a short normal boot, do the urgent things first: check that photos or files are syncing, confirm you can sign in to your Apple Account or Google account from another device, and retrieve backup codes from accounts where you are already signed in. Do not spend that short window changing non-urgent settings.

7. **Open a support case before paying for third-party recovery.**  
   If official recovery fails, contact the manufacturer, carrier, retailer, or protection-plan provider. Say plainly: “The phone is stuck in a restart loop immediately after a software update.” Write down the date and time of the update, the exact screen you see, whether a computer recognizes the phone, and the official steps you already tried.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to replace the phone, switch brands, or change carriers.
- You do not need to pay for third-party data recovery or boot-loop software before trying official recovery and warranty routes.
- You do not need to erase the phone unless the official route cannot update or repair it and you decide the data-loss tradeoff is acceptable.

## Important reassurance
A phone that loops after an update is not automatically lost. Slowing down, using official tools, and avoiding early erase options gives you the best chance of keeping data and account access intact.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If official recovery fails, the phone overheats, buttons are stuck, the computer will not recognize it, or there may be liquid or impact damage, later decisions may need manufacturer service, an authorized repair provider, a carrier, a warranty administrator, or a data-recovery specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not professional repair, legal, financial, or data-recovery advice. Button sequences, menus, warranties, service options, and data-loss risks vary by phone model, software version, carrier, plan, and manufacturer.
