# What to do if… water is pouring over a gutter edge in one spot and running behind the downspout into the wall (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the indoor area from electrical hazards, move belongings away from the wet wall, and perform a ground-level visual check to confirm if a downspout or gutter joint has separated.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use a ladder during active rain, high winds, or after dark.
- Do not stand in or walk through puddles that are in contact with electrical outlets, power strips, or submerged appliances.
- Do not reach into gutter outlets or downspouts with bare hands due to sharp metal edges and hidden debris.
- Do not attempt to seal or caulk exterior gaps while the surfaces are wet or while water is actively flowing.
- Do not touch the electrical service panel (breaker box) if the floor is wet or if you are not completely dry.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the immediate wet area if water is near electricity.**  
   If water is tracking down a wall toward an outlet, light switch, or baseboard heater, do not touch the area. **If you can reach the main breaker panel while standing on dry ground and with dry hands**, turn off the power to the affected room; otherwise, do not approach the panel and call an electrician.
2. **Contain and redirect indoor water flow.**  
   Move furniture, electronics, and rugs away from the damp wall. **Place buckets or trays under active drips** and use heavy towels to soak up water on the floor to prevent it from reaching other rooms or floor vents.
3. **Conduct a visual inspection from the ground (do not use a ladder).**  
   Look at the overflow point from a safe distance outside to identify the mechanical failure:  
   - A gap between the back of the gutter and the fascia board (indicating the gutter is pulling away).  
   - A downspout that has slipped down and is no longer seated inside the gutter outlet.  
   - Water spilling specifically from a joint or seam in the gutter or downspout.  
   - A "waterfall" effect where the downspout is completely blocked and water is backing out of the top.
4. **Clear ground-level downspout obstructions if possible.**  
   If the bottom of the downspout or the "shoe" (the curved piece at the bottom) is visible and clogged with leaves, **gently pull the debris out using a tool or gloved hand** to see if the system begins to drain. Do not force or pull on the metal.
5. **Contact the appropriate repair party.**  
   - **If renting:** notify the landlord or emergency maintenance line immediately and state: "Gutter overflow is causing active water intrusion behind the wall near [Location]."  
   - **If owning:** call a gutter contractor or roofer for an emergency repair for "gutter-to-wall water intrusion."
6. **Begin drying procedures once the rain stops.**  
   Open windows and use floor fans or a dehumidifier in the affected room **only if the electrical system has been cleared as safe** by a professional. Early drying within the first 24 to 48 hours is necessary to stabilize the building materials.
7. **Document the damage for records.**  
   Take clear photos or video of the water pouring over the gutter outside and the specific wet areas inside. Save these for insurance adjusters or property managers.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact technical cause (such as improper pitch or missing drip edge) until a contractor arrives.
- You do not need to tear out drywall or insulation immediately unless instructed by a restoration professional.
- You do not need to file a formal insurance claim in the first few minutes; focus on stopping the water and securing electricity first.

## Important reassurance
Water pouring over a gutter in a single spot usually indicates a localized mechanical failure or a specific clog rather than a total roof failure. While it looks dramatic, identifying the overflow point from the ground helps professionals fix it quickly once the weather permits.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Permanent repairs to the gutter system, fascia, or interior wall must be performed by qualified contractors or maintenance personnel to ensure structural integrity and safety.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace professional electrical or structural advice. If water has entered electrical fixtures or the service panel, do not touch anything and wait for a licensed electrician.
