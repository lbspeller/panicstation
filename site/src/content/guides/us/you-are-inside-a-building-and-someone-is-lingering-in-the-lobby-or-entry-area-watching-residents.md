# What to do if… you are inside a building and someone is lingering in the lobby or entry area watching residents (USA)

## Short answer
Create distance behind a locked door and notify building security or management immediately. Call 911 if you observe the person attempting to open doors, following residents, or if you feel you are in immediate physical danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don't confront the person or attempt to "move them along" yourself.
- Don't allow "tailgating" by holding the door open for someone you do not recognize.
- Don't enter isolated or confined common areas such as laundry rooms, stairwells, or basements while the person is present.
- Don't share your apartment number or personal schedule within earshot of the individual.
- Don't exit the building alone if you have to pass directly by the individual to do so.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure location behind a locked door immediately.** If you are in the lobby or a hallway, return to your unit or go to a trusted neighbor’s apartment rather than remaining in a common area.
2. **Contact building security or the property management emergency line.** Provide the specific location (e.g., "North Lobby" or "Main Entryway") and describe the behavior you are observing, such as the person standing by the call box for an extended period or staring at residents as they enter.
3. **Call 911 if the person’s behavior escalates or indicates a crime in progress.** **Call 911 if the person is testing door handles, attempting to force entry, following people into elevators, or preventing anyone from leaving.**
   - Call first; only use text-to-911 if it is unsafe to speak out loud.
   - If you text 911 and receive a "bounce-back" message, your local dispatch does not support text; you must place a voice call.
4. **Use 311 or the local police non-emergency number for suspicious activity that is not an immediate threat.** Provide a clear description of the individual, including clothing, height, and any nearby vehicles, without leaving your secure location to get more details.
5. **Request that building management preserve any available surveillance footage.** Inform management of the exact time you observed the person so they can save relevant video for law enforcement or internal security reviews.
6. **Wait for a neighbor or staff member if you must move through the lobby.** If you need to leave the building, try to time your exit so you are walking with another resident or ask security to escort you to your vehicle or the exit.
7. **Minimize your visibility in common areas until the situation is resolved.** Avoid checking your mail or visiting the package room alone, and do not use the building intercom system if the person is standing near the external speaker.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person or confirm if they are a resident before seeking help.
- You do not need to decide on long-term security upgrades for your unit right now.
- You can file a formal written incident report with the property owners during standard business hours.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard safety protocol to be alert to unauthorized individuals in restricted residential areas. Taking steps to isolate yourself and notify authorities is a practical response to suspicious behavior and does not require you to "prove" the person's intent first.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate encounter with a suspicious individual in a residential common area. It does not cover long-term stalking interventions or legal eviction/trespass procedures.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. Always follow the specific emergency protocols established by your building management and local law enforcement.
