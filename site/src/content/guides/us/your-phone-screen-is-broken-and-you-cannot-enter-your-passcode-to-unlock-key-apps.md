# What to do if… your phone screen is broken and you cannot enter your passcode to unlock key apps (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all capture attempts immediately, clear 1–2 GB of local space by deleting large unneeded apps or offline downloads, and avoid restarting the device until some breathing room is created.

## Do not do these things
- Don't restart or power off the phone if it is currently responsive; extremely low storage can cause a "boot-loop" where the phone gets stuck on the logo.
- Don't delete original photos or videos before confirming they are backed up elsewhere.
- Don't install "storage cleaner" or "booster" apps, which often use more space and may involve deceptive subscriptions.
- Don't assume a "Cloud Storage Full" (iCloud/Google One) message is the same as "Device Storage Full" — check your local settings first.
- Don't continue trying to record video or take bursts of photos; this can corrupt the file index and lead to the loss of previous unsaved data.

## What to do now
1. **Switch to an alternative capture method immediately.**  
   **If you are in a situation where you must record or photograph something right now, use a different device or ask a bystander to capture it and send it to you later.** Continuing to use a "full" phone may result in corrupted files that cannot be recovered.

2. **Verify your actual available local storage.**  
   - **On iPhone (iOS):** Go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage.
   - **On Android:** Go to Settings → Storage (or "Device care" on some models).
   **Check if the "Available" space is under 500 MB; if so, the phone is in a high-risk state for data loss and OS crashes.**

3. **Delete 1–2 large apps you do not need today.**  
   **Navigate to your storage settings and find the list of apps sorted by size.**
   - Choose a large game or social media app (like TikTok or Facebook) that stores data in the cloud.
   - **Select "Delete App" (iOS) or "Uninstall" (Android).**
   - Note: Do not just "Offload" the app on iPhone if you are in a crisis, as this leaves the "Documents & Data" behind, which is often the primary source of the storage bloat.

4. **Remove high-volume offline media.**  
   **Check apps that allow downloads for offline use, as these are the safest and fastest files to recover later.**
   - Open streaming apps (Spotify, Netflix, YouTube) and delete downloaded playlists or movies.
   - Open podcast apps and delete downloaded episodes.
   - Open "Files" (iOS) or "Downloads" (Android) and delete large PDFs or Zip files.

5. **Clear browser cache to reduce "System Data" or "Other."**  
   **This is a safe way to free several hundred megabytes without losing personal files.**
   - **iPhone:** Settings → Safari → Clear History and Website Data.
   - **Android:** Settings → Apps → Chrome (or your browser) → Storage → Clear Cache. (Note: Do "Clear Cache" only; "Clear Data" will log you out of all sites).

6. **Empty the trash to finalize the space recovery.**  
   **Deleted items often stay on the device for 30 days and do not free up space until the trash is purged.**
   - Open the Photos app → Albums → Recently Deleted → Delete All.
   - On Android, open the Files app → Trash → Empty.

7. **Export or back up your most recent, un-synced data.**  
   **Once you have at least 1 GB free, your phone is stable enough for a transfer.**
   - Connect to a stable Wi-Fi network and a charger to allow cloud backups (iCloud/Google Photos) to complete.
   - If using a computer, connect via cable, but ensure the phone is unlocked and "Trust" is selected quickly.

8. **Cancel any "cleaner" app subscriptions immediately (USA specific).**  
   **If you downloaded a paid app in a panic and see a new charge, stop the recurring billing.**
   - **iPhone:** Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions.
   - **Android:** Play Store → Profile Icon → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions.
   - If the app was deceptive or difficult to cancel, report it via the FTC "Report Fraud" portal or contact your bank to dispute the transaction under the Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act (ROSCA).

## What can wait
- You don't need to scroll through your entire photo library to pick individual "bad" photos right now.
- You don't need to resolve iCloud or Google One subscription issues until the local device is stable.
- You don't need to perform a factory reset or "Wipe" the device today.
- You don't need to reorganize your folders or file structure while in this recovery phase.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel panicked when your device stops working during an important moment. Low storage causes a "clog" in the system that makes the phone feel broken, but it is usually a software-level threshold. Once you clear a small amount of space, the phone's ability to save and process data typically returns to normal immediately.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to prevent data loss and device crashes. It does not cover hardware repairs, long-term cloud storage management, or professional data recovery services.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not professional IT or legal advice. Procedures vary significantly by device model and OS version. If the device is managed by an employer or organization, contact their IT department before attempting to delete apps or move data.
