# What to do if… you notice a new groin bulge with increasing pain and nausea (USA)

## Short answer
A new groin bulge with increasing pain and nausea could be an emergency. Go to an emergency department now, or call 911 if you cannot get there safely. Do not wait to see if it settles.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to force the bulge back in, press it, massage it, or keep checking it if it is painful, hard, very tender, or you feel sick.
- Do not keep eating or drinking while arranging urgent care unless a clinician or 911 dispatcher tells you to; ask if you need essential medicine.
- Do not take repeated pain medicine doses to make it easier to wait at home.
- Do not lift, strain, cough on purpose, or bear down to “test” the bulge.
- Do not drive yourself if you are vomiting, lightheaded, faint, confused, or the pain is ramping up.

## What to do now
1. Check for emergency signs. Go to an emergency department now, or call 911 if you cannot get there safely, if any of these apply:
   - worsening or severe groin pain with nausea or vomiting
   - a hard, very tender, stuck, red, purple, or dark bulge
   - fever, faintness, confusion, or feeling very unwell
   - belly swelling or bloating
   - not being able to pass stool or gas
2. If you are unsure, choose urgent care over waiting. A new groin bulge with increasing pain and nausea is enough reason to be checked urgently.
3. While arranging help, lie down or stay still in the most comfortable position, loosen tight clothing, and avoid straining. Do not try to push the bulge back in.
4. Make transport safer. Ask someone to drive you, or call 911 if you are alone, getting worse, repeatedly vomiting, faint, or unable to move safely.
5. Bring only what is quick to grab: photo ID, insurance card if available, medication list, allergy list, and the time the bulge or pain started.
6. If the bulge changes color, gets bigger, becomes harder, or the nausea turns into vomiting, tell the emergency team when that changed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause before getting care.
- You do not need to search for home remedies, belts, trusses, exercises, or ways to “reduce” the bulge.
- You do not need to decide anything about surgery, work, travel, or finances right now.
- You do not need to message multiple people; one trusted person who can help with transport or logistics is enough.

## Important reassurance
Feeling alarmed makes sense. These symptoms are taken seriously because a painful groin bulge with nausea can sometimes mean tissue or bowel is stuck, and getting checked quickly is the safer choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. After you are examined, later decisions about diagnosis, treatment, follow-up, or surgery may need help from emergency clinicians, a surgeon, or another qualified health professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. It is not a medical diagnosis or individualized care. If symptoms are escalating, you are vomiting, the bulge is hard or discolored, or you cannot pass stool or gas, seek emergency care immediately.
