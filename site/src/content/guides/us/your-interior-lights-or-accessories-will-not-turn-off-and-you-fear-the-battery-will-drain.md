# What to do if… your interior lights or accessories will not turn off and you fear the battery will drain (USA)

## Short answer
Park safely and ensure all doors, the trunk, and the glovebox are fully latched while setting the main interior light switch to the OFF position. If the draw persists, disconnect the negative battery terminal to prevent total battery depletion.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to fix or troubleshoot electrical issues while the vehicle is in motion.
- Do not wear metal jewelry, rings, or watches when handling battery terminals.
- Do not disconnect the battery if you smell "rotten eggs" / sulfur; this indicates a dangerously overheating battery that requires professional handling.
- Do not touch orange-colored high-voltage cables in hybrid or electric vehicles.
- Do not leave the key or key fob inside or within 15 feet of the vehicle during the "sleep" test.
- Do not use a metal tool to bridge the space between the positive and negative battery posts.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to a safe, level location immediately.**
   - Pull as far off the roadway as possible, ideally into a parking lot or a wide shoulder.
   - Turn on your hazard lights. 
   - If you are on a high-speed highway, exit the vehicle and stand behind a guardrail or up a slope once the car is parked.
   - Note that under 2026 Move Over laws, other drivers must change lanes or slow down when passing your stopped vehicle.

2. **Perform a manual override of the lighting system.**
   - Locate the dome light or "Room" light switch and move it from "Door" or "On" to the **OFF** position.
   - Check the dashboard illumination dial; many US vehicles have a "click" setting at the top of the dial that forces all interior lights to stay on. Roll it down.
   - Open and firmly slam every door, including the trunk, hatch, and glovebox, to ensure sensors are engaged.

3. **Initiate a vehicle "Sleep Mode" test.**
   - Turn the engine off and remove the key from the ignition.
   - Exit the vehicle, close all doors, and lock the car using the fob or door button.
   - **Move the key fob at least 20 feet away from the vehicle.**
   - Wait 3 minutes without approaching the car; many modern modules require this time to shut down accessories and screens.

4. **Isolate the power source if the lights remain on.**
   - **Open the hood or battery compartment only after the engine has cooled.**
   - Locate the 12V battery (typically under the hood, but may be in the trunk or under a floor panel).
   - Identify the negative (–) terminal, which is usually black or marked with a minus sign.
   - **Loosen the nut on the negative terminal cable and lift the cable entirely off the post.**
   - Secure the cable end so it cannot spring back and touch the metal post.
   - Do not disconnect the positive (+) terminal.

5. **Verify the drain has stopped.**
   - Confirm the interior lights or accessories have gone dark.
   - Close the hood or trunk manually (ensure you can still mechanically unlock the vehicle later if the battery is disconnected).

## What can wait
- Identifying whether the fault is a "parasitic draw" or a broken door switch.
- Re-syncing the clock, radio presets, or power window "auto" functions.
- Purchasing a replacement battery.
- Detailed inspection of the alternator or Body Control Module (BCM).

## Important reassurance
Interior lights and accessories staying on is a frequent result of a simple sensor misalignment or a switch being accidentally bumped. Taking these steps prevents the immediate inconvenience of a dead battery in the morning and does not cause permanent damage to the vehicle's computer systems.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to prevent battery failure. It does not replace a professional electrical diagnostic, which is required if the issue recurs after reconnection.

## Important note
This information is for general use and does not account for specific manufacturer anti-theft systems that may trigger an alarm upon battery reconnection. If you are operating a hybrid or electric vehicle, refer to your owner's manual before touching the 12V battery system. If you see smoke or smell burning plastic, contact emergency services and move away from the vehicle.
