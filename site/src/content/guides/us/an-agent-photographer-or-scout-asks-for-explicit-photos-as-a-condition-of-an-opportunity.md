# What to do if… an agent, photographer, or “scout” asks for explicit photos as a condition of an opportunity (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all communication immediately, do not send any images or money, and save all messages as evidence. Report the individual to the platform and the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).

## Do not do these things
- Don't send any images to prove you are "real," to secure a role, or to stop the pressure.
- Don't pay any money, cryptocurrency, or gift cards to stop threats or have content deleted.
- Don't delete the conversation or account before taking screenshots.
- Don't meet the person in a private location or at an unverified studio.
- Don't negotiate with the individual or try to explain your refusal.

## What to do now
1. **Stop communication immediately and block the individual across all platforms.** 
   - Do not send a final explanation; simply cease contact.
   - Use the platform's internal blocking tools to prevent further messages or views of your profile.

2. **Capture and save all evidence of the interaction before the messages are deleted.** 
   - Take screenshots of the full conversation, including any threats and the user’s profile/ID.
   - Record the URL of their social media profile or website.
   - Save copies of any emails, including the header information if possible.

3. **Report the individual to the FBI through the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).** 
   - Visit ic3.gov to file an official report regarding online extortion or scams.
   - Include the saved screenshots and any contact details provided by the individual.

4. **If you are under 18 or the incident involves a minor, contact the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).** 
   - Call 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678) or use the CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org.
   - If images were already sent, use the "Take It Down" service at takeitdown.ncmec.org to help prevent the spread of content.

5. **Verify the individual's identity through official, independent agency channels.** 
   - Look up the agency's official website via a search engine, not through links they provided.
   - Call the main office number listed on the official site to report the scout and verify if they are an employee.
   - Note that in New York, modeling agencies must be registered with the NYS Department of Labor as of late 2025.

6. **Report the account to the safety team of the app or website where the contact occurred.** 
   - Use the specific "Harassment," "Sexual Solicitation," or "Scam" reporting categories.
   - Most platforms have dedicated teams to handle exploitation and "scouting" scams.

7. **Notify a trusted adult or peer about the situation for immediate safety support.** 
   - Ensure someone else is aware of the contact and the steps you are taking.
   - If you feel physically unsafe or have shared your home address, contact local law enforcement via 911 or their non-emergency line.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to pursue legal action or a civil lawsuit.
- You do not need to figure out how they found your profile or "why" you were targeted.
- You do not need to inform your current employer or other agencies until you have stabilized the situation.
- You do not need to issue a public warning or "expose" the account yourself.

## Important reassurance
Exploitative actors frequently use the "scout" or "agent" persona to create a false sense of professional urgency and authority. Feeling startled or pressured is a common reaction to these tactics. You have the absolute right to end contact at any time, regardless of any "contract" or "opportunity" they claim to offer.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and harm reduction only. It does not replace legal advice or comprehensive law enforcement investigations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal or professional advice. If you are in immediate physical danger or are being actively blackmailed with existing content, contact emergency services or the FBI immediately.
