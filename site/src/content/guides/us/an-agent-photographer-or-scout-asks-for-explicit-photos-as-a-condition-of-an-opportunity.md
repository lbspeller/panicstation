# What to do if… an agent, photographer, or scout asks for explicit photos as a condition of an opportunity (USA)
## Short answer
Do not send explicit photos or keep negotiating. Move to a safer pause, save visible messages only if it is safe to do so, and consider contacting a trusted person or specialist support service.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send explicit images to secure the role, prove you are real, or make the pressure stop.
- Don’t pay money or send gift cards in response to threats.
- Don’t agree to meet alone or go to a private location after an explicit-photo request.
- Don’t download, forward, or ask someone else to send explicit images solely to make a report or use an image-removal tool.
- Don’t let urgency, secrecy, or status pressure you into making a quick decision.
- Don’t delete visible messages before saving them if it is safe to keep a record.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.**  
   Stop the conversation. You do not owe a reply. Mute or block the account when it is safe to do so. If you are in immediate danger, call **911** or local police.

2. **Get support without making a big decision.**  
   Consider telling one trusted adult, friend, family member, school counselor, or specialist support service what happened. You do not need to decide now whether to report, confront the person, or pursue the opportunity.

3. **Keep a limited record if it is safe.**  
   Save visible messages, account details, links, and threats if you can do so safely, but do not download or forward explicit files.

4. **Use the platform’s tools if that feels manageable.**  
   You can report the account for sexual solicitation, harassment, threats, or scams. A trusted person can help you with this.

5. **Use the child-safety route if you are under 18 or an image was taken before you turned 18.**  
   You, or a trusted adult helping you, can use NCMEC’s CyberTipline for suspected online child sexual exploitation. Take It Down is a free NCMEC service that can help limit sharing of nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos taken before age 18 on participating public or unencrypted platforms. Do not download, send, or share an image solely to use the service.

6. **Use the adult image-removal route if the image was taken when you were 18 or older.**  
   StopNCII may be an option if you are currently over 18, you are the person shown, and you still have the image or video on your device. It creates a digital fingerprint for participating platforms without uploading the image itself.

7. **Consider the IC3 route for adult cyber-enabled threats or payment demands.**  
   Threats, blackmail, or payment demands may involve sextortion or another form of online abuse. For cyber-enabled threats or payment demands involving an adult, you can consider filing an IC3 complaint. For suspected exploitation of a minor, use the CyberTipline route.

8. **Verify any real organization independently.**  
   Before any further contact or meeting, find the agency or company’s official website yourself and ask whether the person is affiliated. Do not rely on contact details supplied by the person who asked for the images.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to expose the person publicly, confront them, or keep pursuing the opportunity.
- You do not need to prove whether the person is legitimate before ending the conversation.
- You do not need a complete timeline before asking for support.
- You do not need to choose every reporting option today.

## Important reassurance
This can be an exploitation tactic. Pressure, status, threats, or urgency do not make the request your fault. You can stop contact, take time, and ask for support without proving anything first.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce risk and buy time. Later decisions may need help from a specialist support service, a trusted adult, or an appropriate reporting body.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call **911** or local police.
