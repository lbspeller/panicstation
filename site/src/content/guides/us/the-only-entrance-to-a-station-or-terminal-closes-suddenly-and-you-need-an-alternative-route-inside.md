# What to do if… the only entrance to a station or terminal closes suddenly and you need an alternative route inside (USA)

## Short answer
Move out of the crowd around the closed entrance. Follow posted directions or ask facility staff which open public route leads inside, and ask for an accessible route if you need one.

## Do not do these things
- Do not push into a tight crowd or try to squeeze through closing doors.
- Do not force a locked door or follow someone into a staff-only, security-controlled, or restricted area.
- Do not treat an emergency exit as an alternative entrance. Use emergency exits for emergency egress, or when directed by facility staff or emergency personnel.
- Do not hop barriers, slip through gate arms, or bypass security screening. This may trigger a security response.
- Do not assume that an unmarked door is a public entrance.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move away from the closed doorway and any pushing. Stand where you can check signs or announcements and speak to staff without blocking the entrance.
2. **Look for an authorized route.** Check posted signs, electronic displays, and announcements for an open public entrance or connected public route.
3. **Ask a specific question.** Ask facility staff or security: “Which open public entrance gets me inside for my train, bus, or flight?”
4. **Use only the confirmed public route.** Follow the posted route or staff directions. Do not enter through a restricted corridor, staff door, or emergency exit.
5. **At an airport, follow checkpoint directions.** If the route affects security screening, follow TSA officer and facility staff instructions. Do not attempt to bypass the checkpoint.
6. **Ask for an accessible route when needed.** Tell staff if you need an elevator, an accessible entrance, help with a mobility device, or another access accommodation.
7. **Check official alerts if the route is still unclear.** Use the facility or operator website, app, or service-alert page after you are away from the crowd pinch-point.
8. **Call 911 for an immediate emergency.** Call if there is smoke, fire, violence, a serious medical emergency, a collapse, or dangerous crowd pressure, and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide about refunds, rebooking, compensation, or complaints while you are finding a safe route inside.
- You do not need to work out why the entrance closed before following posted directions.
- You do not need to argue at the closed door. The immediate priority is safe, authorized access or a safe place to wait.

## Important reassurance
A sudden closure can be disorienting, especially when other people are crowding around the same doorway. Stepping aside and getting one clear direction from staff or official signage is a reasonable next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment an entrance closes and you need another route inside. Later decisions about delays, refunds, complaints, or longer rerouting may need help from the operator or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information for the first minutes after a sudden entrance closure. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Procedures vary by facility and incident, so follow official signage and on-site staff, security, or emergency personnel directions.
