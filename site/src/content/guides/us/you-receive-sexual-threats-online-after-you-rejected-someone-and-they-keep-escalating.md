# What to do if… you receive sexual threats online after you rejected someone and they keep escalating (USA)

## Short answer
Cease all communication immediately, document the evidence through screenshots, and contact the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative or local law enforcement if there is a threat of physical harm or image distribution.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "reason" with the person or explain your rejection further.
- Do not delete the messages or the profile links, as these are required for investigations.
- Do not send money, gift cards, or additional images in an attempt to stop the escalation.
- Do not click on any links or download attachments sent by the individual.
- Do not engage in "counter-harassment" or post their details publicly.
- Do not meet the person in a "neutral" location to resolve the issue.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize immediate physical safety.** **If the messages contain specific threats to your physical location or indicate the person is nearby, call 911 immediately.**
   - Move to a location where you are not alone if you feel unsafe at home.
2. **Issue one final directive and stop responding.** **Send a single, clear message stating "Do not contact me again" and then immediately cease all engagement.**
   - Do not reply to any subsequent "apologies," "explanations," or further threats.
3. **Preserve evidence before blocking.** **Take full-page screenshots of all threatening messages, including the user’s profile ID, handle, and the date/time stamps.**
   - Capture the URL of their profile page.
   - Store these images in a dedicated folder or cloud drive that you do not have to look at daily.
4. **Isolate the hazard by tightening account security.** **Lock down your social media profiles to "Private" and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your primary email and accounts.**
   - Use an authenticator app rather than SMS if possible.
   - Check "Login Activity" in settings to ensure no unauthorized devices are currently signed in.
5. **Contact the Image Abuse Helpline for image-based threats.** **If the individual is threatening to share intimate images or deepfakes, call the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative (CCRI) at 1-844-878-2274.**
   - They provide specialized assistance for "revenge porn" and sextortion takedowns.
6. **Report the harassment to the platform and the FBI.** **Use the platform’s internal reporting tool for "harassment" or "threats of violence," and file a report at tips.fbi.gov for interstate threats.**
   - If the threat involves a demand for money (sextortion), file a report with the **Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)**.
7. **Seek confidential advocacy support.** **Call the National Sexual Assault Hotline at 1-800-656-HOPE (4673) to speak with a trained advocate who can help you navigate the emotional impact.**
   - If the person is a former partner or someone you have dated, contact the **National Domestic Violence Hotline** at 1-800-799-SAFE (7233) or text "START" to 88788 for safety planning.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to file a formal lawsuit today.
- You do not need to notify your employer or school immediately unless you believe the harasser will contact them.
- You do not need to "scrub" your entire digital history in one day; securing your current accounts is the priority.
- You do not need to understand "why" this is happening before taking protective action.

## Important reassurance
The panic you are feeling is a natural response to a violation of your boundaries and safety. Harassers often use escalating threats to regain a sense of control after a rejection, but you are not responsible for their behavior or their reactions. Taking a pause and stepping away from the screen is a valid and protective choice.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for online harassment and sexual threats. Long-term legal remedies, civil litigation, or formal criminal prosecution will require consultation with law enforcement or a qualified attorney.

## Important note
This information is for safety and stabilization purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Jurisdiction-specific laws regarding "revenge porn" and cyber-stalking vary significantly by state. If you are in immediate danger, contact local emergency services.
