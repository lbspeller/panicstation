# What to do if… your child support or maintenance payment is missed and enforcement is threatened (USA)

## Short answer
Check whether the payment is truly missed or only delayed, then contact your state or tribal child support agency as soon as you can. Use only the official payment channel for your case, and do not send a duplicate payment until you know whether wage withholding or another payment is already pending.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore an enforcement notice, court notice, or stated response date.
- Do not stop paying because you plan to ask for a change; child support orders generally do not change on their own.
- Do not pay cash or make an off-the-record payment if your case is tracked through a state or tribal payment system.
- Do not send a duplicate payment in panic if your paystub shows support was already withheld.
- Do not argue about custody, blame, or past conflict in payment messages. Keep messages brief, factual, and about payment logistics.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether this is a true missed payment or a posting delay.**
   - Check your bank, card, payroll app, or payment confirmation for a failed payment, returned transfer, or deduction.
   - Check the official child support portal or payment record for your case.
   - If support is taken from wages, check whether your paystub shows the amount was withheld.

2. **Identify the official payment route for your case.**
   - Wage withholding through an employer
   - State disbursement unit payment
   - Tribal child support payment route
   - Online, phone, check, or money order method listed on your official notice or portal
   Write down your case number, due date, amount due, and any confirmation number.

3. **If you are the paying parent, avoid making the record worse.**
   - If your paystub shows support was withheld, ask the child support agency or payment unit whether the payment is pending, delayed, or misapplied before sending extra money.
   - If nothing was withheld and you can pay, pay through the official channel for your case and save the confirmation.
   - If you cannot pay the full amount, pay what you realistically can through the official channel and ask how to document the shortfall.

4. **If you received an enforcement warning, treat it as a deadline-driven record problem.**
   - Contact the state or tribal child support agency listed on your notice.
   - Ask what amount is currently shown as past due.
   - Ask what options may affect the next enforcement step, such as a payment, written payment plan, review request, or hearing request.
   - Ask how to get written confirmation of any payment, agreement, correction, or next deadline.

5. **If your income changed, ask about review or modification now.**
   - Tell the agency if there has been job loss, reduced hours, illness, disability, or another income change.
   - Ask whether your state handles review or modification through the child support agency, the court, or both.
   - Gather only the basics for now: recent pay stubs, proof of job loss or income change, your current work status, and the most recent order or notice you have.

6. **If you are the receiving parent, use the official channel first.**
   - Report that the expected payment has not arrived and ask for an updated payment record or ledger.
   - Confirm that your direct deposit, payment card, mailing address, and contact details are current.
   - If the paying parent says wages were withheld, ask whether the payment is pending, delayed by employer processing, or missing identifying information.

7. **Keep a simple record file.**
   - Save the notice, payment confirmations, screenshots from the portal, and bank or paystub records.
   - For calls, write down the date, time, office contacted, name or ID of the person you spoke with, and what they told you to do next.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to hire a lawyer unless there is a hearing, court deadline, or order requiring action now.
- You do not need to resolve parenting time, custody, or relationship conflict to deal with a missed payment record.
- Budget rebuilding, job planning, and longer explanations can wait until you know what the official payment record shows.

## Important reassurance
A missed or delayed child support payment can feel alarming because enforcement notices are serious. The most stabilising move is to slow the problem down: check the official record, use the correct payment route, contact the agency, and keep proof of every step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Child support payment records, enforcement options, and modification procedures vary by state, tribe, court order, and case type. Later decisions may need help from your state or tribal child support agency, a court self-help service, legal aid, or a lawyer.

## Important note
This guide provides general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Follow any court order or official notice you receive, and ask the child support agency or court named in your papers about deadlines and next steps for your case.
