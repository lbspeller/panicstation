# What to do if… your phone battery is low and your ticket or access pass is stored only on your phone (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately switch to a physical backup by using an airport kiosk or ticket counter to print a boarding document while the phone still has enough power to prove your reservation exists.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep refreshing the ticketing app or searching your email, as constant screen-on time and data usage drain the final battery percentage rapidly.
- Do not wait until you reach the front of the boarding or security line to address the issue.
- Do not assume "Express Transit" or "Power Reserve" will work for third-party barcodes or QR codes; these features usually only support specific NFC-based wallet passes.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to a stranger to "help" you find a charger or login.
- Do not turn the phone off and on repeatedly; the boot sequence consumes significant power.

## What to do now
1. **Activate maximum power-saving protocols immediately.**
   - Enable "Low Power Mode" or "Battery Saver" in system settings.
   - Set screen brightness to the lowest visible level.
   - Close all background applications, especially maps and social media.
   - Disable Wi-Fi and Bluetooth unless the ticket specifically requires a local connection to scan.

2. **Navigate to an official service point before the battery reaches zero.**
   - At airports, go to a self-service kiosk; look for "Reprint Boarding Pass" in the menu (some airlines hide this in a "more options" or "already checked in" sub-menu).
   - If the kiosk is unavailable or restricted, go directly to the "Full Service" or "Special Assistance" desk and state that your phone is dying.
   - At train or bus stations, go to a staffed ticket window immediately.

3. **Use the provider's specific 2026 fallback procedures.**
   - **For Amtrak:** Ask the agent for a "Boarding Document" or receipt; if you are already on the train and the phone dies, the conductor can verify your status using your name and a government-issued photo ID.
   - **For Greyhound:** You are permitted to board by showing a valid photo ID to the driver if the ticket was purchased in your name.
   - **For NYC MTA (OMNY):** If your phone dies, you cannot "reprint" a tap; you must use a physical OMNY card or a contactless credit/debit card as a secondary payment method.

4. **Verify if your phone has a "dead battery" NFC reserve.**
   - If using a modern iPhone or Android for transit taps (NFC), check if "Express Mode" or "Power Reserve" is active.
   - This may allow you to tap through a turnstile or onto a bus for up to 5 hours after the phone's UI has shut down due to low battery.

5. **Locate an emergency power source for a 2-minute "survival" charge.**
   - Look for "Charging Stations" or "Power Poles" near gates or seating areas.
   - If no outlets are visible, ask a gate agent or a lounge receptionist if they have a courtesy charger for a brief top-up.
   - In 2026, many major hubs have "Rentable Power Bank" kiosks; use a physical credit card to rent one for 15 minutes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose why the battery drained faster than expected.
- You do not need to resolve app login issues or password resets right now.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the lack of outlets until you have successfully boarded.
- You do not need to worry about deleting photos or clearing storage to "save power" (this is ineffective).

## Important reassurance
It is a standard occurrence for transit and airline staff to encounter passengers with depleted phone batteries. Most systems are designed with a manual lookup or "reprint" fallback that can be triggered as long as you have a valid photo ID. Taking action while you still have 1–2% battery allows staff to verify your booking faster than a total "black screen" situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for domestic US travel only. It does not cover international border crossings where digital "Journey Passes" or biometric entry requirements may have different technical prerequisites.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee boarding. Travel providers maintain individual policies regarding digital versus paper documentation. Always prioritize instructions given by TSA agents, gate staff, or conductors at the scene, as they have the final authority over boarding and security access.
