# What to do if… a partner insists on recording sex and becomes angry when you refuse (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the sexual situation and move toward a safer pause away from them. Anger when you refuse can be a warning sign, so focus on getting safer and finding support rather than continuing the discussion.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to recording just to calm them down.
- Do not hand over your phone, passcode, cloud password, or account access.
- Do not try to grab their device if that could increase the danger.
- Do not keep explaining or negotiating if they are becoming more angry.
- Do not send more images or videos to try to prevent an argument.
- Do not confront them about a possible recording while you are still unsafe.
- Do not assume you must report to police before asking for support.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a safer pause.** End the sexual situation if you can. Move toward an exit, a trusted person, a public place, or another place where help is available. Take your phone, keys, wallet, and essential medication if you can do so safely.
2. **Use a brief boundary only if it feels safe.** You can say, “No. I do not consent to being recorded.” Skip the sentence and focus on leaving if speaking may increase the risk.
3. **Call for urgent help if you are in immediate danger.** Call **911**, or ask someone nearby to call, if you are in immediate danger or a life-threatening situation.
4. **Contact a specialist support service when you have a safer moment.** RAINN offers free, confidential, 24/7 support: call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, use online chat, or text **HOPE** to **64673**. The National Domestic Violence Hotline also offers live support: call **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)**, use chat, or text **START** to **88788**.
5. **Use online resources only from a device that feels safe to use.** Internet use can be monitored. If you think they check your phone or browser history, use a safer device where possible.
6. **Keep one optional record only if it is safe.** You may keep threatening messages or make a brief note of what happened, but do not do this if it could increase your risk.
7. **Consider seeking medical care promptly if sexual activity happened without your consent or under pressure.** An emergency department may be one option, and a specialist support service can help you ask about local services.
8. **Take one next step at a time if a recording may exist.** A specialist advocate can help you decide what feels safest. If a recording is shared or they threaten to share it, the FTC resource below explains options.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to report to police.
- You do not need to confront your partner about whether a recording exists.
- You do not need to contact every platform or explore legal options today.
- You do not need to make a long-term relationship decision while you are trying to get safer.
- You can choose support first and decide later steps when you have more space.

## Important reassurance
Refusing to be recorded is valid. Agreeing to sex is not the same as agreeing to be recorded. Their anger is not your responsibility to fix, and you deserve support without pressure to take any one next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours. Later decisions may need support from a specialist advocate, a healthcare professional, or another appropriate professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Seek urgent help if you are in immediate danger.
