# What to do if… someone in a group chat is making unwanted sexual comments and others are joining in (USA)
## Short answer
Mute the chat and step away from the thread. If it may help later, save a small record before leaving, then use the app’s report, block, or leave controls when you are ready.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep reading the thread to check whether it stops.
- Do not argue with the group or feel that you have to defend yourself.
- Do not move into a private chat with the person who started it if you feel pressured or unsafe.
- Do not share intimate images, personal details, or your location to try to make the messages stop.
- Do not delete the thread in a rush if you may want a small record later.
- Do not download, forward, or share any sexual image involving a child.

## What to do now
1. Mute the group and turn off message previews if seeing new messages is making it harder to pause. Leave the group if that feels safest and you do not need access to it.
2. If the group is needed for essential school or work updates, stop opening the thread for now and ask a trusted person for a separate way to receive necessary information.
3. If you may want to report later, save a small number of screenshots showing the messages, visible account names or phone numbers, and dates or times, then stop reopening the thread.
4. Use the platform’s report and block tools if they would help reduce contact. If there is an admin or moderator you trust, you can ask them to remove the messages or the people involved.
5. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If messages include threats, stalking, or feared offline harm, you can consider contacting your local police department. For non-urgent cyber-enabled crime concerns, you can use IC3. For concerns involving children, use NCMEC’s CyberTipline instead.
6. If a child is being pressured for sexual images, or you suspect online child sexual exploitation, tell a trusted adult when that is safe and consider reporting to NCMEC’s CyberTipline. If a nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit image or video of you taken before you were 18 may be shared online, NCMEC’s Take It Down service may help; do not download or forward an image to use it.
7. If the messages connect to sexual assault, abuse, or non-consensual intimate imagery, RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline offers support by phone at 800.656.HOPE (4673), by text with HOPE to 64673, and through online chat. Its hotline page explains confidentiality and the limits that can apply in some circumstances.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confront the group or explain yourself publicly.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to make a formal report.
- You do not need to change your phone number, delete accounts, or make major school, work, or relationship decisions today.
- You can decide later whether you want help from a school, workplace, platform, specialist service, or legal professional.

## Important reassurance
Being repeatedly sexualized by a group can feel overwhelming and isolating, even when others call it a joke. You do not need to justify stepping back, and you did not cause the group’s behavior by leaving, muting, freezing, or not knowing what to say.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce contact, create a safer pause, and buy time. Later decisions about school, work, platform, safety, or legal routes may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent first steps. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice.
