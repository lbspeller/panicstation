# What to do if… you are trapped behind a car-park barrier or gate because the payment system is down (USA)

## Short answer
Safely park the vehicle, then use the **HELP/intercom** button on the exit kiosk or call the posted facility support number to request a manual gate override. Document the malfunction with a photo to resolve any future billing disputes.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to lift, push, or drive through the gate arm (risk of vehicle damage, physical injury, and legal liability).
- Don’t "tailgate" or follow another vehicle closely through the gate; many systems are programmed to close immediately after one car passes.
- Don’t leave your vehicle unattended in the exit lane if you can safely pull to the side.
- Don’t reverse blindly into a queue of vehicles or across pedestrian walkways.
- Don’t stand directly under or near the gate arm mechanism (crush hazard).
- Don’t repeatedly press the payment or help buttons, as this may reset your position in a remote queue.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle and assess your immediate surroundings.** **Put the car in park, engage the emergency brake, and turn on your hazard lights if you are blocking an active exit lane.**
   - Listen for mechanical grinding or buzzing sounds which indicate a motor failure.
   - Look for error codes or "Out of Service" messages on the kiosk display.
2. **Press the HELP/Intercom button on the exit machine once.** **Wait at least 60 seconds for a response, as many systems connect to a remote operator or security desk.**
3. **Call the facility support or security number if one is posted on the kiosk.** **If no one answers the intercom, use your mobile phone to dial the number listed for "Assistance" or "Emergency."**
   - Provide your **exact gate number** (if labeled) or your **lane location**.
   - State your **license plate number** and your **approximate entry time**.
   - Explain that the **payment system is unresponsive** and ask for a manual gate lift.
4. **Capture photographic evidence of the malfunction.** **Take a clear photo of the kiosk screen showing the error message, the gate arm in the down position, and any posted signage with contact info.**
   - This serves as proof that you attempted to pay and were prevented by equipment failure.
5. **Request a specific instruction regarding payment to avoid being double-charged.** **If the operator lifts the gate, ask if they have recorded your exit or if you need to submit a payment online later.**
   - Note the name of the person you spoke with if possible.
6. **Escalate to emergency or non-emergency services only if safety is compromised.** **Call 911 if you are in immediate physical danger, experiencing a medical emergency, or if the situation is escalating into a confrontation.**
   - For non-emergency entrapment where the operator is unreachable, search for the **local police non-emergency number** for the specific city or county.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the exact dollar amount of the parking fee while you are still trapped in the exit lane.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or leave a business review immediately.
- You can wait to contact your credit card company or parking app (e.g., SpotHero, ParkWhiz) for a refund until you are safely parked elsewhere.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of panic when a mechanical barrier prevents you from leaving a space. Modern parking systems almost always have a manual override or a remote operator capable of lifting the arm. Most operators prefer to let a vehicle exit for free rather than maintain a dangerous blockage in an exit lane.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for a mechanical or payment system failure at a parking exit. It does not cover long-term legal disputes, property damage claims, or specific municipal parking ordinances which vary by state.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Facilities are generally required to provide safe egress; however, private property owners may have specific protocols for equipment failure. If you feel your physical safety is at risk, prioritize moving to a safe location and contacting emergency services.
