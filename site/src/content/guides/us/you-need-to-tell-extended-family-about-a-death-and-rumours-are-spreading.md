# What to do if… you need to tell extended family about a death and rumours are spreading (USA)

## Short answer
Choose one contact person if practical, send a brief confirmed-facts-only message, and contact the people who should hear directly as soon as practical. You do not need to answer every question now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not share details that you have not confirmed.
- Do not argue about rumors in a family group chat.
- Do not share medical details, screenshots, or private messages to prove a point.
- Do not let helpers improvise different versions of the message.
- Do not rush into a public post because other people are posting.
- Do not feel pressured to explain the cause of death.

## What to do now
1. **Choose one contact person and one backup if practical.** Ask relatives to direct questions to that person. Helpers can use the same agreed message.
2. **Write one short message using confirmed facts only.** For example: “I’m very sorry to share that [Name] died [on date, if confirmed]. We are contacting close family now. We will share confirmed details about arrangements when we can. Please do not forward or post this yet.”
3. **List the people who should hear directly first.** Start with the people closest to the person who died and anyone who may be especially affected by hearing the news indirectly. Call or message them one-to-one as soon as practical.
4. **Give helpers the same message.** Split the remaining relatives into a few contact groups. Ask each helper to note who has been told, who could not be reached, and who needs a personal call.
5. **Use one holding sentence for questions you cannot answer.** For example: “We are not sharing more details until we have confirmed information.”
6. **Correct harmful misinformation once.** Send a brief message from the contact person, such as: “For clarity, the family is not sharing a cause of death at this time. Please avoid speculation or forwarding unconfirmed details.”
7. **Reduce further spreading.** Use smaller messages where practical. Contact anyone who is forwarding or posting details one-to-one and ask them to pause.

## What can wait
- You do not need to write an obituary or public statement now.
- You do not need to answer questions about the circumstances or cause of death now.
- You do not need to decide service details or a complete guest list during this first round of messages.
- You do not need to respond to every call, message, or social media comment.
- You do not need to complete every administrative task while you are trying to reach relatives.

## Important reassurance
Rumors can make a painful situation feel even more out of control. A short message, a small contact plan, and one consistent reply are enough for this first stage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to slow misinformation and tell relatives with the least additional harm. Later decisions, including administrative tasks, estate questions, public notices, or family conflict, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Keep sensitive details private until you have confirmed information and the support you need.
