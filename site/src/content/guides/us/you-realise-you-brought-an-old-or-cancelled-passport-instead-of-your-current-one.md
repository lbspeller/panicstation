# What to do if… you realise you brought an old or cancelled passport instead of your current one (USA)

## Short answer
For international travel, focus on getting the valid physical passport to the airline desk or moving quickly to rebooking. If you are abroad and cannot access a valid U.S. passport, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate for passport help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to board internationally with a cancelled, expired, or otherwise invalid passport to see if it works.
- Do not assume a photo or scan of your current passport will replace the physical passport; it may help staff check details, but it usually is not enough to board.
- Do not assume a passport card is enough for international air travel.
- Do not report your passport lost or stolen unless it truly is lost or stolen; once reported, it is invalid for international travel even if you later find it.
- Do not report an expired passport as lost or stolen just because you brought the wrong one.
- Do not keep changing check-in or document details in a panic; make one clean correction with airline staff if possible.
- Do not hand your passport or payment details to unofficial helpers at the airport or outside an embassy or consulate.

## What to do now
1. Check exactly what is in your hand.  
   Look at the name, date of birth, expiration date, passport number, and whether there are any visible cancellation holes or marks. Check whether you have a passport book or only a passport card, because a passport card is not valid for international air travel.

2. Work out where the current valid passport is.  
   Identify whether it is at home, in another bag, in a hotel safe, at an office, with another person, or genuinely inaccessible. Write down the exact location and who can reach it.

3. If the current valid passport can reach you, arrange one clear handoff.  
   Ask a trusted person to bring the valid passport to the airport, port, station, or airline desk. Give them the terminal, airline, booking name, and a single meeting point. Keep your phone charged and stay where airline staff can still help you.

4. Go to a staffed airline counter as soon as possible.  
   Say: “I arrived with an old passport. My current valid passport is [on the way/at home/not accessible]. Can you tell me my options and update my document details if I can present it?” If you entered the wrong passport number online, ask staff to correct the document details and re-check you in if that is possible.

5. Do not treat an online correction as enough.  
   Correcting the passport number in a booking does not usually replace the need to physically show the valid passport before boarding. Ask the airline what deadline matters for your specific flight, such as check-in closure or document-check closure.

6. If the passport cannot reach you in time, reduce the fallout.  
   Ask the airline about moving the flight, rebooking, or keeping the rest of the booking intact where possible. If you are travelling with others, only split up if everyone has a safe plan, phone access, payment access, and somewhere secure to wait or stay.

7. If you are already abroad and cannot access a valid U.S. passport, contact official consular help.  
   Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate and explain your travel date, route, and what passport or identity documents you have. If travel is urgent, consular staff may be able to issue a limited-validity emergency passport, but acceptance can depend on your destination and route.

8. Keep the situation from spreading into more problems.  
   Put your phone, charger, payment card, booking reference, old passport, any passport copy, and other ID in one secure place. Use one written note for the airline: where the valid passport is, whether it is coming, and what you need next.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about insurance claims, refunds, complaints, or blame.
- You do not need to replace a passport right now if your current valid passport is simply somewhere else and can be retrieved.
- You do not need to solve every booking detail online; focus on one immediate outcome: valid passport delivered, travel rebooked, or embassy or consulate help if you are abroad.
- You do not need to decide whether the whole trip is cancelled until the airline has told you the practical options.

## Important reassurance
This is a stressful document mix-up, but it has clear next steps. The useful goal is narrow: get the valid physical passport in front of the right staff, or move to a safer rebooking or consular route.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about replacement passports, fees, insurance, refunds, complaints, or destination-specific entry rules may need specialist help from the airline, the U.S. Department of State, a U.S. embassy or consulate, or your travel provider.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, or other professional advice. Airlines, border authorities, and destination governments may apply document rules strictly, and requirements can vary by route and country.
