# What to do if… you need to show a vaccination certificate but the QR code will not validate when scanned (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the agent for an immediate alternative verification method, such as a physical CDC card or a manual review of your digital record’s details, and then attempt to refresh or re-download the record directly from your state registry or healthcare provider's portal.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume an "invalid" scan means your vaccinations are unrecognized or missing from the registry.
- Do not download third-party "fixer" apps or unofficial QR scanners while in a public queue.
- Do not attempt to edit, crop, or use filters on a screenshot of the QR code to make it "clearer."
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to an official to be taken out of your line of sight.
- Do not engage in a debate regarding the scanning technology; focus on finding an accepted fallback document.

## What to do now
1. **Request the alternative proof requirements immediately.**  
   **Ask the agent which secondary documents are accepted if the digital scan fails.**
   - Common fallbacks include the original white CDC vaccination card.
   - Printed immunization records from a doctor or pharmacy.
   - Viewing the raw data on your screen (Name, DOB, and dose dates) without the QR code.

2. **Verify the verifier app being used by the agent.**  
   **Confirm if the scanning system is compatible with the SMART Health Card standard.**
   - Most US digital records (state portals, pharmacies like CVS/Walgreens, and VAMS) use the SMART Health Card format.
   - If the verifier is looking for a different standard (such as the EU Digital COVID Certificate), your valid US QR code will naturally return an "invalid" or "unsupported" result.

3. **Maximize screen legibility for a second scan attempt.**  
   **Adjust your phone settings to provide the clearest possible image for the scanner.**
   - Set screen brightness to 100%.
   - Disable "Dark Mode" or "Blue Light" filters.
   - Zoom in so the QR code fills the entire screen without cutting off the corners.
   - Wipe the phone screen and the camera lens of the scanning device if accessible.

4. **Retrieve a fresh copy of the record from the source.**  
   **Log into your state’s immunization registry or your healthcare provider’s portal to generate a new QR code.**
   - Many QR codes are "stale" or expire if they were downloaded months ago.
   - If you used the Vaccine Administration Management System (VAMS), log into the Recipient Portal to view the current "Vaccination Certificate."
   - If your state has a dedicated Digital Vaccine Record portal (e.g., California, New York, Washington), use the "Get My Record" feature to send a new link to your phone.

5. **Compare the certificate data against your travel ID.**  
   **Check that the name spelling and Date of Birth (DOB) on the digital record exactly match your passport or driver's license.**
   - Any character mismatch can cause a verifier to flag the record as "not verified" or "invalid" for the specific traveler.

6. **Switch to physical documentation for specific vaccine types.**  
   **Present the International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP/Yellow Card) for vaccines like Yellow Fever.**
   - Some vaccines required for entry to specific countries are rarely digitized in a scan-compatible format and require the physical yellow card.

7. **Contact the vaccine provider for an emergency digital copy.**  
   **Call the pharmacy (CVS, Walgreens, etc.) or clinic where you were vaccinated to request they email a PDF of your record.**
   - Pharmacies can often regenerate a SMART Health Card link or email a verifiable record within minutes if their internal system is active.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact the state registry to correct a permanent record error while standing at a check-in counter.
- You do not need to troubleshoot the specific "verifier app" settings on the agent's device.
- You do not need to "convert" your US record into an international format unless you have a safe pause and reliable internet access.

## Important reassurance
QR codes fail to scan for many technical reasons that have nothing to do with the validity of your medical history. Lighting, screen protectors, mismatched software versions, and regional format differences are the most frequent causes of "invalid" scan results. Your underlying vaccination status remains valid even if the digital "handshake" fails.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document verification issues during transit or entry. It does not cover the long-term correction of state registry data or legal appeals for denied boarding.

## Important note
Travel requirements and acceptable document types are subject to change by individual airlines and foreign governments. This information does not guarantee entry or boarding. If you are at a TSA checkpoint and lack an acceptable ID or valid proof, you may be directed to the TSA Confirm.ID process for manual identity verification.
