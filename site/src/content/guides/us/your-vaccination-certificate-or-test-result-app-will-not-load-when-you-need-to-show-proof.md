# What to do if… your vaccination certificate or test result app will not load when you need to show proof (USA)

## Short answer
Step out of the line if you can, switch to an offline backup, and ask the staff member checking your proof what alternative formats they can accept.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t delete or reinstall the app while you are at the counter unless staff specifically tell you to; you may lose saved passes or trigger a login problem.
- Don’t assume the CDC can pull up your record on demand. Vaccination records are usually held by your provider, pharmacy, clinic, or state Immunization Information System.
- Don’t hand an unlocked phone to a stranger to fix.
- Don’t keep retrying logins until the account locks. Switch quickly to another proof route.
- Don’t rely on public Wi-Fi as your only option when you are close to the desk, gate, or entrance.

## What to do now
1. **Create a small pause.** Step aside if you can, plug in your phone if power is available, and turn on low-power mode if the battery is low.
2. **Do only the fastest phone checks.**
   - Toggle airplane mode on and off.
   - Try mobile data instead of Wi-Fi, or Wi-Fi instead of mobile data.
   - Force-close and reopen the app once.
   - Restart the phone only if it is frozen and you know your passcode.
3. **Look for proof that is already on your device.**
   - Check Apple Wallet, Apple Health, or Google Wallet if you previously added the vaccination or test pass.
   - Search your photos for a saved QR code or screenshot.
   - Search downloads, files, email, and text messages for a PDF, lab result, pharmacy message, provider portal message, or SMART Health Card.
   - Check whether a printed vaccine record, lab printout, or pharmacy record is in your bag or travel documents.
4. **Ask one clear question to the person checking your proof.** Say: “My proof app will not load. Can you accept a Wallet pass, printed record, PDF, screenshot, provider record, pharmacy record, or lab result instead?”
5. **Use the most direct source first if you need proof today.**
   - Contact the vaccination provider, pharmacy, clinic, hospital, workplace clinic, or lab that issued the test result.
   - Ask whether they can resend the record, print it, email a PDF, verify it through a portal, or tell you what format they can provide.
   - If you cannot reach the provider, use your state Immunization Information System contact page to ask how to access or request a copy of your vaccination record.
6. **If the pass is missing from a wallet app, check another device only if it is already set up.** Some passes may not automatically appear on a different phone. An old phone, tablet, or smartwatch with the pass already saved may be faster than rebuilding the account.
7. **If time is tight, ask for the on-site backup process.** Airlines, border staff, hotels, employers, clinics, schools, and venues may have their own rules for manual review, later upload, extra ID checks, or rebooking. Your immediate goal is to find an acceptable workaround, not to fix the app perfectly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reset every password, reinstall apps, or reorganize your phone right now.
- You do not need to contact multiple agencies at once. Start with the provider, pharmacy, lab, or state record route most likely to have the record.
- You do not need to decide a long-term backup system today. A printed copy, saved PDF, or wallet pass can be handled later after the immediate check is over.

## Important reassurance
This is a common kind of failure: weak signal, a forced update, an expired login, a low battery, or a portal outage can block an otherwise valid record. Staff may still be able to tell you what other proof they can review.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are trying to check in, board, enter, or verify. Later decisions may need help from the provider, pharmacy, lab, state registry, airline, venue, employer, school, or destination authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, travel, employment, or professional advice. Proof requirements and accepted formats vary by airline, venue, workplace, school, destination, and situation. Follow the instructions of the staff or authority verifying your documents.
