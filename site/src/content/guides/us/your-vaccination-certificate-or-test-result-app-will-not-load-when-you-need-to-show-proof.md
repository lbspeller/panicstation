# What to do if… your vaccination certificate or test-result app will not load when you need to show proof (USA)

## Short answer
Step out of line and switch immediately to an offline backup, such as a Wallet pass, screenshot, or physical card. Ask the verifying official what alternative forms of proof they accept while you troubleshoot.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete or reinstall the app while at a security checkpoint or gate.
- Do not attempt multiple failed logins that could trigger a security lockout.
- Do not contact the CDC to request your individual records; the CDC does not maintain a national vaccination database.
- Do not rely on airport or venue Wi-Fi as your primary method for accessing cloud-based records.
- Do not hand an unlocked phone to staff to "fix" the app.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a stable location with power.** Step aside to avoid blocking a queue, plug into a portable charger or wall outlet if possible, and enable low-power mode to preserve remaining battery.
2. **Perform a rapid connection reset.** 
   - Toggle Airplane Mode on for 5 seconds, then off.
   - Disable Wi-Fi to force the phone onto cellular data (or vice versa if cellular signal is weak).
   - Force-close the specific app and reopen it once.
3. **Check for pre-integrated system backups.** 
   - Open the **Apple Wallet** app (on iPhone) or **Google Wallet** (on Android) to see if a health pass was previously saved.
   - Open the **Apple Health** app and navigate to "Health Records" to see if a verifiable SMART Health Card is stored.
   - Search your phone's photo gallery for "vaccine," "QR," or "COVID" to locate screenshots or PDFs.
4. **Identify acceptable alternative proof.** Ask the agent: "My app is failing to load. Will you accept a physical CDC card, a screenshot of my record, or a PDF from my provider?"
5. **Access the state-level registry if the provider app is down.** 
   - Use the **CDC "Contacts for IIS"** page to find your specific state's Immunization Information System (IIS).
   - Most states (such as California, New York, or Washington) provide a direct digital portal where you can retrieve a SMART Health Card QR code by entering your name, DOB, and phone number.
6. **Request a supervisor for manual verification.** If the automated scanner or app is the only hurdle, ask a supervisor if they can manually verify your status using a photo ID and a non-verifiable digital record (like a pharmacy portal log-in).

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the underlying technical glitch with the app right now.
- You do not need to update your phone's operating system or software.
- You do not need to contact your primary care physician for a new record if you have a state registry option available.
- You do not need to worry about permanent account access while in the middle of a transit or entry event.

## Important reassurance
Digital systems frequently fail due to server timeouts, signal dead zones, or expired login sessions at high-traffic checkpoints. Transitioning to a secondary form of proof is a standard procedure that airport and event staff are trained to handle.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate proof-of-status failures. It does not cover the legalities of vaccine mandates or the medical validity of specific tests.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal or medical advice. Acceptance of specific proof types is at the sole discretion of the airline, venue, or border authority. Requirements may change without notice based on local or international health regulations.
