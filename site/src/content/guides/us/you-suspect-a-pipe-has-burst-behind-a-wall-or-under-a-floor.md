# What to do if… you suspect a pipe has burst behind a wall or under a floor (USA)

## Short answer
Shut off the home’s main water supply at the master shut-off valve immediately to stop the flow. Avoid all electrical outlets or panels in wet areas and call a licensed plumber to locate and repair the breach.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the water on to "investigate" the exact location of the leak.
- Do not touch any electrical switches, outlets, or appliances if the wall or floor near them is wet.
- Do not attempt to cut into drywall or flooring yourself, as you may strike live electrical wires or gas lines.
- Do not use a shop vacuum or fans in the area until you are certain the electrical circuits in that room are de-energized.
- Do not ignore the leak if it seems small; hidden water migration can cause structural collapse or mold within 24 to 48 hours.

## What to do now
1. **Locate and close the main water shut-off valve immediately.** 
   - Look in the basement near the front wall, in the garage, near the water heater, or in a crawl space.
   - For wheel-style valves (gate valves), turn clockwise until it stops.
   - For lever-style valves (ball valves), turn the handle 90 degrees until it is perpendicular to the pipe.
   - If you cannot find an internal valve, look for a concrete or plastic "meter box" near the street and use a water meter key to turn the valve clockwise.
2. **De-energize affected electrical zones if it is safe to do so.**
   - **Go to your electrical service panel only if the path is dry.**
   - **Flip the breakers for the rooms where you hear or see water.**
   - **If the panel itself is wet or the floor in front of it is damp, do not touch it; call your utility provider or 911 if sparking occurs.**
3. **Turn off the water heater.** 
   - Shut off the power at the breaker for electric heaters to prevent the heating elements from burning out.
   - Turn gas heaters to the "Pilot" or "Off" setting.
4. **Relieve remaining pipe pressure.** 
   - Open the lowest faucet in the house (such as a basement utility sink) and the highest faucet to drain the vertical columns of water.
   - Once the water stops flowing, close the faucets.
5. **Check the water meter to confirm the flow has stopped.** 
   - If the small "leak indicator" dial or digital readout is still moving after the main valve is closed, the valve may be faulty; contact your water utility immediately.
6. **Alert neighbors or management.** 
   - If you are in an apartment or condo, notify the landlord, property manager, or HOA emergency line.
   - If water is pooling on a floor, notify the occupants of the unit directly below you.
7. **Document the observable damage.** 
   - Take photos of wet drywall, buckling floors, or active drips before any professional arrives.
   - Note the exact time you discovered the leak and the time the water was successfully shut off.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a restoration company or contractor for permanent repairs right now.
- You do not need to determine the "fault" or cause of the burst pipe during the emergency phase.
- You do not need to move heavy furniture that is not in the direct path of the water until the plumber arrives.

## Important reassurance
Hidden leaks are stressful because the damage is internal, but the single most effective way to stop the destruction is already within your control: the main shut-off valve. Once the water is off, the immediate threat to the building's structure is halted.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. It does not include instructions for pipe soldering, mold remediation, or navigating insurance adjusters.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and is not a substitute for professional plumbing or electrical services. If water has entered electrical fixtures, the area is hazardous until cleared by a qualified professional.
