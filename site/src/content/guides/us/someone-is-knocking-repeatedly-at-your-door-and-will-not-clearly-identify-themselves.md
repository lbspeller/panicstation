# What to do if… someone is knocking repeatedly at your door and will not clearly identify themselves (USA)
## Short answer
Keep the door locked and do not open it just because the knocking continues. If the person is trying the handle, making threats, attempting to enter, or making you think immediate help is needed, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door just to end the knocking.
- Do not step outside to speak with the person if you feel unsure.
- Do not rely on a badge, uniform, vest, clipboard, or caller ID by itself.
- Do not use a phone number or website address supplied by the person outside to verify who they are.
- Do not give personal, financial, or identification details at the door.
- Do not argue through the door or say that you are home alone.
- Do not ask a neighbor, friend, or family member to approach the door while the situation is uncertain.
- Do not physically confront the person or argue about their authority at the door.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Keep the door locked. Check that other outside doors are locked if you can do this without moving into view. Move yourself and anyone with you away from direct sight lines through windows or glass panels.
2. **Check without opening the door.** Use a peephole or doorbell camera only if you can do this without moving into view. You do not need to approach an exposed window.
3. **Decide whether to respond.** You do not have to answer. If you feel comfortable speaking through the closed door, say: “I am not opening the door. Tell me your name, who you are with, and why you are here.”
4. **If you live in an apartment or secured building,** do not buzz the person in. Contact building staff or security through a phone number, intercom, or app you already use.
5. **If the person claims to be from a company or utility,** keep the door closed. Use the company’s official website, the number on your bill, or another contact method you already know is genuine to ask whether someone was sent to your address.
6. **If the person claims to be law enforcement or another government official,** do not open the door solely because of the claim. If there is no immediate danger, look up and call the relevant local agency’s publicly listed number. If you feel unsafe, the person is trying to enter, or you cannot verify promptly, call 911. Tell the call-taker what the person is saying and follow the call-taker’s instructions.
7. **Call 911 for an emergency.** Give your address, explain that someone is repeatedly knocking and will not clearly identify themselves, and say whether the person has tried the handle, made threats, or attempted to enter. Follow the call-taker’s instructions.
8. **End unnecessary contact.** If the person appears to be making a delivery or an unplanned service visit, you can say: “Leave it at the door,” or “I am not available. Please leave.”
9. **Alert someone without sending them toward the door.** Text or call a trusted neighbor, friend, or family member. Ask them to stay on the phone or contact help if needed, but tell them not to approach the door.

## What can wait
- Working out exactly who the person was or why they came.
- Posting about the situation or warning neighborhood groups.
- Deciding whether to upgrade locks, cameras, or routines.
- Deciding whether to make a detailed non-emergency report after your immediate safety is stable.
- Explaining your decision to keep the door closed.

## Important reassurance
Persistent knocking can feel unsettling. You can keep the door closed while you assess what to do next. Repeated knocking, a claimed identity, or a uniform is not by itself a reason to open the door.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an uncertain doorstep situation. Later decisions about repeated targeting, harassment, or safety planning may need specialist or local support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or need urgent police, fire, or emergency medical assistance, call 911.
