# What to do if… someone is knocking repeatedly at your door and will not clearly identify themselves (USA)

## Short answer
Keep the door locked and do not open it under any circumstances until identity is verified through an independent source. If the person refuses to leave, makes threats, or you feel unsafe, call 911 immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door even slightly "just to see" who it is or to talk more clearly.
- Do not step outside to confront the person or ask them to leave.
- Do not trust a uniform, badge, or utility vest as proof of identity on its own.
- Do not call any phone number provided by the person at the door for "verification."
- Do not confirm if you are home alone or provide any personal or account information.
- Do not ignore the knocking if you believe they think the house is empty; a brief verbal acknowledgement through a locked door can deter potential burglars.

## What to do now
1. **Secure all entry points immediately.** Ensure the front door, back door, garage door, and all ground-floor windows are locked.
2. **Move to a location with a clear exit.** Stand in a part of the house where you are not visible from the door or windows but have a path to a different exit or a "safe room" with a phone.
3. **Observe through a barrier if possible.** Use a peephole, a side window, or a doorbell camera to look at the person without revealing your position.
   - Note their clothing, any visible ID, and if they have a vehicle nearby.
4. **Speak through the locked door.** State clearly: "I am not opening the door. State your name, your organization, and your purpose." 
   - If they claim to be a neighbor or in distress, offer to call emergency services for them while keeping the door locked.
5. **Verify utility or service workers independently.** If they claim to be from a utility company (e.g., gas, electric), ask for their name and employee ID number through the door.
   - Look up the official customer service number for that utility on your most recent bill or the official website. 
   - Call the company and ask if they have a technician dispatched to your specific address.
6. **Verify law enforcement claims.** If the person identifies as a police officer but is not in a marked vehicle or you have doubts, ask for their name, badge number, and department. 
   - Call 911 or the local police non-emergency line to confirm that officers are at your location for official business. 
   - Real officers will wait while you verify their identity through dispatch.
7. **Contact building security or management.** If you live in a managed apartment complex or gated community, call the on-site security or the management office to report an unidentified person roaming the halls or grounds.
8. **Call 911 if the situation escalates.** Dial 911 immediately if the person:
   - Attempts to turn the door handle or push the door open.
   - Claims to have a weapon or makes verbal threats.
   - Refuses to leave after being told you are calling the police.
   - Circles the house to look in windows or try other doors.
9. **Alert a trusted contact.** Text a neighbor or family member to let them know someone is at your door and you are currently verifying their identity.

## What can wait
- Confirming the person's specific intentions or "hearing them out."
- Checking for mail or packages they might be claiming to deliver.
- Checking your utility account status or payment history.
- Researching local neighborhood social media groups for similar reports.

## Important reassurance
It is your right to keep your door closed to anyone you do not know or cannot verify. Legitimate officials and service workers are trained to handle residents who are cautious and will provide the necessary information for you to verify their identity through official channels. Feeling startled or anxious in this situation is a natural response to a violation of your home's privacy.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate, unknown visitor interaction. It does not cover long-term security upgrades or legal processes for handling ongoing harassment or restraining orders.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Safety protocols may vary by local jurisdiction. Always prioritize your physical safety and contact 911 in any perceived emergency.
