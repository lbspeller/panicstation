# What to do if… a partner keeps pressuring you for sex after you have said no (USA)

## Short answer
End the sexual conversation and create a safer pause. You do not have to keep explaining a boundary. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep debating whether your boundary is fair.
- Do not send sexual content to calm them down or prevent an argument.
- Do not meet alone to discuss it if you feel uneasy about their reaction.
- Do not keep responding after you have ended the conversation.
- Do not use a device to seek support if you think that could increase your risk.
- Do not feel pressured to decide right now whether to report, confront them, or end the relationship.

## What to do now
1. **End the sexual conversation.** Stop replying, leave the chat, end the call, or move away when it is safe to do so. A brief reply such as “I am not discussing this” is optional.
2. **Create a safer pause.** Move toward a trusted person or a public place when safe to do so. If you are apart, mute notifications, pause contact, or block temporarily if that feels safer.
3. **Use the safest available channel, or no channel.** If practical communication is necessary, keep it brief and do not continue the sexual discussion. Consider whether your phone or internet use may be monitored.
4. **Tell one trusted person what is happening.** Ask them to stay on the phone, meet you somewhere safe, or check in with you at a specific time.
5. **Contact specialist support when you are ready.**
   - The National Domestic Violence Hotline offers confidential support 24/7: call 800-799-SAFE (7233), text START to 88788, or use live chat through its website.
   - love is respect offers confidential support 24/7 for teens, young adults, and their loved ones: call 866-331-9474, text LOVEIS to 22522, or use live chat through its website.
   - If sexual abuse or assault may be part of what is happening, RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline offers support 24/7: call 800-656-HOPE, text HOPE to 64673, use online chat, or use WhatsApp chat through its website.
6. **Call 911 for immediate danger.** If you cannot safely call, ask someone nearby to call when possible.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to break up, confront them, report, or use a particular label.
- You do not need to plan every future conversation.
- Decisions about blocking, housing, family, or legal options can wait until you have a safer pause and support.

## Important reassurance
Your boundary does not need their agreement. Repeatedly testing a boundary after you have stated it is pressure, and you do not have to keep discussing it. Feeling confused, guilty, numb, or unsure can happen when someone keeps pushing after you have said stop.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need specialist support. You can ask a hotline advocate about support and privacy before deciding what to share or whether to report.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Hotline privacy and reporting circumstances can vary, so ask about them before sharing details if that matters to you.
