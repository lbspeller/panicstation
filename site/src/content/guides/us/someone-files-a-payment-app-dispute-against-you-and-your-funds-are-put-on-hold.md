# What to do if… someone files a payment app dispute against you and your funds are put on hold (USA)

## Short answer
Save the dispute notice and respond through the official dispute process shown in the app or on the company’s website before the displayed deadline. Do not send a second payment or refund outside that process while the dispute is open.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send money, a refund, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or bank-transfer details to the other party outside the official dispute process shown in the app or on the company’s website.
- Don’t delete messages, receipts, tracking records, listings, emails, or call logs.
- Don’t ignore a displayed response deadline.
- Don’t share verification codes, passwords, password-reset links, or screenshots of security prompts.
- Don’t use support contact details sent by the other party. Open the app or type the company’s official website address yourself.
- Don’t threaten or repeatedly message the other party.

## What to do now
1. **Save the dispute notice and hold details.**  
   Screenshot the case page. Save the transaction ID, amount, dispute reason, date, hold amount, and any displayed response deadline. Download any receipt or statement the app provides.

2. **Ask which process applies.**  
   The app may describe the case as a dispute, review, reversal, or chargeback. Use the official support route in the app or on the company’s website and ask: “What process applies to this transaction, what evidence do you need, and what is my response deadline?” Save the reply.

3. **Check your account for suspicious changes.**  
   Review your linked bank accounts, cards, email address, phone number, and signed-in devices. If anything looks unfamiliar, change your password using the official app or website and remove unrecognized devices or payment methods. Turn on multi-factor authentication or a PIN if the app offers it.

4. **Match your evidence to the request shown in the case.**  
   Keep the response short and relevant. Depending on the claim, useful records may include the original listing or agreed terms, messages confirming the transaction, receipts, tracking or delivery confirmation, appointment or service records, photos, and any refund or return terms shown before payment. Add a brief dated timeline explaining what each attachment shows.

5. **Submit the response before the displayed deadline.**  
   Send the relevant evidence you already have through the official dispute process. If something is missing, ask official support whether you can add it later. Do not assume later uploads will be accepted.

6. **Ask for a written explanation of the hold.**  
   Ask official support to confirm why the funds are held, whether any other transactions are affected, what may trigger release, and whether they need anything else from you. Save the response.

7. **Report suspected fraud separately if appropriate.**  
   If you believe the dispute is connected to a scam, you can report it to the FTC. If you believe it involves cyber-enabled crime, you can also report it to the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center. Keep any confirmation details. These reports are separate from responding to the payment app and do not replace the app’s dispute process.

8. **Use a complaint route if the company does not resolve the problem.**  
   If you cannot get a clear response from the company, you may be able to submit a complaint to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. Include the key dates, amount, transaction ID, your communications with the company, and the practical effect of the hold.

9. **Protect essential payments today.**  
   If the hold affects rent, utilities, insurance, or loan payments, contact those providers and ask whether a short extension or hardship arrangement is available. Use only legitimate funds you can access without bypassing the hold.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to contact an attorney or consider a court claim.
- Making major changes to how you accept future payments.
- Closing the account before you have saved your records and received the dispute outcome.
- Writing a long account of everything that happened. A short dated timeline is enough for the first response.

## Important reassurance
A hold can be part of a payment app’s review process. It does not by itself prove that the other party’s claim is correct. Saving the notice, meeting the displayed deadline, and using official support channels are useful first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The process may depend on the payment app, the funding method, the dispute reason, and the company’s terms. Later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or other professional advice. A report to a government agency does not replace responding through the payment app’s official dispute process.
