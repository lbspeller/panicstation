# What to do if… you notice your phone has new “device admin” or monitoring settings and you fear intimate data is exposed (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the suspected phone for any sensitive activity and contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline (1-800-799-7233 or text "START" to 88788) from a different, safer device. Do not delete apps or change settings yet, as this may alert the person monitoring the device.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use the suspected phone to research help, search for shelters, or plan a departure.
- Do not confront the suspect through the monitored device or via any linked account.
- Do not immediately delete unfamiliar apps or factory reset the phone if you want to preserve evidence of stalking or abuse.
- Do not log into sensitive accounts—such as banking, healthcare, or intimate photo storage—on the device.
- Do not change passwords using the suspected phone, as a keylogger or screen monitor may capture the new credentials.

## What to do now
1. **Switch all sensitive communication to a safer device immediately.** Use a trusted friend’s phone, a library computer, or a new burner device for all safety planning and support.
2. **Contact an advocate from a safer device for technical and safety guidance.** 
   - Call the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233.
   - Text "START" to 88788.
   - Use the live chat at thehotline.org from a private browser on a safer device.
3. **Document the specific settings or apps that caused concern.** Take a photo of the screen using a different camera or write down:
   - The exact name of the "device admin" app.
   - The date and time you noticed the change.
   - Any specific permissions the app has (e.g., location, camera, or "erase all data").
4. **Request a line separation from your wireless carrier if you are on a shared plan.** Under the Safe Connections Act, you have the right to separate your line from an abuser's account within two business days without the account holder's permission.
   - Contact your provider's specialized domestic violence or "Safe Connections" team.
   - Be prepared to provide a copy of a police report, protective order, or a signed affidavit from a qualified professional.
5. **Secure your "master" accounts from a safer device.** Change the passwords and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) for:
   - Primary email accounts.
   - iCloud or Google accounts.
   - Mobile carrier accounts.
   - Financial and banking apps.
6. **Check for "Device Admin" apps on Android devices.** Navigate to Settings > Security > Device admin apps (this path may vary slightly by manufacturer) and note any apps listed that you did not personally authorize.
7. **Use Apple Safety Check on iPhone if your device supports it.** Navigate to Settings > Privacy & Security > Safety Check to perform an "Emergency Reset" or "Manage Sharing & Access" to quickly see who has access to your location and information.
8. **Review active "Linked Devices" in messaging apps.** In apps like WhatsApp, Telegram, or Signal, check the "Linked Devices" or "Desktop" settings to ensure your messages are not being mirrored to a computer you do not control.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a police report immediately.
- You do not need to replace the physical handset today if it can be safely powered off or left behind.
- You do not need to identify the exact software used for monitoring; an advocate can help with this later.

## Important reassurance
Sudden changes in device settings or the appearance of management profiles are valid reasons for concern. Technology-facilitated abuse is a recognized pattern, and your reaction to feeling monitored is a normal response to a breach of privacy. You are entitled to use technology without surveillance.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction for suspected digital surveillance. It does not replace legal advice, forensic investigation, or comprehensive safety planning with a certified advocate.

## Important note
Digital surveillance and stalkerware are often used in the context of domestic violence and stalking, which are crimes in all 50 states. Under the 2026 enforcement of the Safe Connections Act, you have specific federal rights regarding your mobile phone line. Use a safer device to explore these rights to avoid detection.
