# What to do if… you receive an official notice that your vehicle registration may be suspended or cancelled (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the notice as time-sensitive, but do not pay through a link in a text or email. Verify the notice through your state’s official motor vehicle agency, confirm the exact reason, then take only the confirmed step needed to prevent suspension or fix the hold.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click links or pay from a text or email that claims to be from a motor vehicle agency.
- Do not drive if the official state system shows the registration is suspended, cancelled, expired, or not valid.
- Do not assume the notice is only about money; it may involve insurance reporting, inspection, emissions, returned payment, address, toll, ticket, or paperwork issues.
- Do not upload documents to a website you reached from an unverified message.
- Do not ignore a paper notice just because you think it might be a mistake.

## What to do now
1. **Save the notice and the envelope.**  
   Photograph or scan the notice and envelope. Write down the deadline, notice number, plate number, VIN if shown, and the agency name exactly as printed.

2. **Find your state’s official motor vehicle agency yourself.**  
   Start from an official state government website or the USAGov state motor vehicle services directory. Do not use the phone number or link in a text or email until you have confirmed it matches the official site.

3. **Check the vehicle registration status through the official state tool or account.**  
   Look for words such as “status,” “hold,” “suspension,” “cancellation,” “compliance,” “insurance,” or “reinstatement.” Copy any code or requirement exactly.

4. **Check common possible causes against the wording on the notice.**
   - **Insurance lapse or reporting issue:** Confirm your policy is active today, and check that the VIN and plate number match. Ask your insurer whether it must send electronic proof to the state.
   - **Inspection or emissions hold:** Check whether your state requires an inspection or emissions test and whether the pass result has been recorded.
   - **Fees, tolls, tickets, or returned payments:** Check for unpaid registration fees, returned payments, toll holds, court holds, or ticket-related holds, depending on your state.
   - **Address or mail problem:** If you moved, check whether your address is current with the motor vehicle agency and whether earlier notices may have gone to an old address.

5. **Contact the official agency route and ask for the exact next step.**  
   Ask:
   - What is the exact reason for the notice or hold?
   - What is the deadline?
   - What happens if the deadline is missed?
   - What proof, payment, form, inspection, or insurer action is accepted?
   - If this is wrong, what review, hearing, correction, or appeal route is available before the deadline?

6. **Ask whether the vehicle can be driven while you fix it.**  
   Use plain wording: “Is this vehicle legally allowed to be driven today?” and “Is there any temporary permit or temporary operating option in this situation?” Follow the answer from your state’s official agency.

7. **If the message looks like a scam, switch to fraud containment.**  
   If you clicked a link or shared information, contact your bank or card provider, change passwords on affected accounts, and enable multi-factor authentication where possible. Report the message through the FTC and any official state fraud reporting route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to understand the whole backstory today; first confirm whether the notice is real and what single item is blocking the registration.
- You do not need to decide about lawyers, complaints, or a long dispute unless the official agency confirms a real problem that you cannot resolve through the normal correction route.
- You do not need to fix every possible vehicle issue at once; focus on the deadline and the specific hold listed in the official record.

## Important reassurance
Many registration notices come from routine administrative triggers, such as insurance reporting gaps, inspection records, address mismatches, returned payments, or unpaid fees. Once the exact reason is confirmed, the next step is usually much clearer.

## Scope note
These are first steps to verify authenticity, avoid scams, and stabilise the situation. Vehicle registration rules and reinstatement routes vary by state, so your state’s official motor vehicle agency is the source of truth.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, or professional advice. If the official record does not confirm the registration is valid, avoid driving the vehicle unless your state’s official agency confirms that driving is allowed or gives you a lawful temporary option.
