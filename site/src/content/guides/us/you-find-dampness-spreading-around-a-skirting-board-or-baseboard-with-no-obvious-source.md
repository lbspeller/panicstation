# What to do if… you find dampness spreading around a skirting board or baseboard with no obvious source (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the dampness as an active plumbing leak by checking the water meter for movement and isolating electrical hazards in the affected area. Shut off the main water supply if the moisture is actively spreading or the source is unknown.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore spreading moisture or wait for the area to dry on its own; mold colonization can begin within 24 to 48 hours.
- Do not use electrical outlets, baseboard heaters, or appliances located on the damp wall.
- Do not run extension cords across wet flooring or through damp areas.
- Do not perform demolition of walls or flooring without verifying the location of electrical wires and gas lines.
- Do not mix bleach with ammonia or other household cleaners as this creates toxic gas.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the area for electrical safety.**
   - Keep residents and pets away from the damp section of the wall or floor.
   - **Turn off the circuit breaker for the affected room if dampness is near outlets or baseboard heaters.**
   - If the breaker panel is in a damp area, use a dry wooden stick or non-conductive tool to flip the switch; do not stand in water while touching the panel.

2. **Conduct a water meter leak test.**
   - Turn off all water-consuming fixtures, including faucets, ice makers, and irrigation systems.
   - **Locate the water meter and observe the flow indicator or leak dial for 15 to 30 minutes.**
   - If the triangular dial or digital decimal continues to move while all fixtures are off, an active pressurized leak is present.

3. **Isolate the water supply if the leak is active.**
   - **Close the main water shutoff valve to stop further damage.**
   - This valve is typically located in the basement, garage, or utility closet where the main line enters the structure.
   - In apartments or condominiums, contact building maintenance immediately to locate the shutoff and notify neighbors of potential water migration.

4. **Identify high-probability moisture sources.**
   - Check the opposite side of the wall for plumbing fixtures such as toilets, showers, or sinks.
   - **Inspect the HVAC condensate line or drain pan if the unit is located near the dampness.**
   - Look for exterior issues such as clogged gutters or downspouts that may be directing rainwater toward the foundation near the interior damp spot.

5. **Begin controlled drying of the structure.**
   - Blot standing water with towels and remove any wet rugs or furniture from the area.
   - **Use a dehumidifier and fans plugged into a GFCI-protected outlet located in a dry area.**
   - Ensure the area is dried to the touch within 48 hours to prevent mold growth on porous materials like drywall and wood.

6. **Submit formal notification for repairs.**
   - **If renting, provide the landlord or property manager with a written notice and photos of the damage immediately.**
   - If owning, contact a licensed plumber or a water restoration professional to locate the leak and assess structural moisture levels.

7. **Record evidence for insurance purposes.**
   - **Take clear photographs and videos of the damp area, the water meter reading, and any damaged property.**
   - Maintain a log of the time the dampness was discovered and the steps taken to mitigate the damage.

## What can wait
- Determining the precise mechanical cause of the moisture does not need to happen before the water is shut off.
- Purchasing mold testing kits is not necessary during the initial stabilization phase; moisture control is the priority.
- Permanent repairs to drywall or flooring can wait until the leak is professionally repaired and the structure is fully dry.

## Important reassurance
It is common for moisture to appear far from its original source because water follows the path of least resistance along wall studs or subflooring. Taking immediate action to shut off the water and isolate electricity effectively stops the progression of the emergency.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for mystery moisture. Professional leak detection, structural drying, and mold remediation may be required following these initial actions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional plumbing, electrical, or restoration services. If you suspect an electrical hazard or a sewage backup, evacuate the immediate area and contact a qualified professional.
