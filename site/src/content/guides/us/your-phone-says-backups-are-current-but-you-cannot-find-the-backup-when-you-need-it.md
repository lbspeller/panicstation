# What to do if… your phone says backups are current but you cannot find the backup when you need it (USA)

## Short answer
Force a network reconnection by toggling Airplane Mode for 15 seconds and restarting the device. If service is not restored, use Wi-Fi to contact your carrier and verify account standing, SIM provisioning, or local outages.

## Do not do these things
- Do not perform a factory reset, as this may trigger activation locks and will not resolve carrier-side registration failures.
- Do not delete an eSIM profile unless specifically instructed by a carrier representative who has provided a replacement QR code.
- Do not modify APN settings or hidden testing menus based on unofficial online forums.
- Do not assume a hardware failure until account-level blocks or provisioning errors have been ruled out.
- Do not repeatedly toggle settings; if three attempts fail, move to Wi-Fi-based troubleshooting.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a known high-signal area.**
   - Step outdoors or move near a window to rule out environmental interference.
   - Avoid elevators, basements, or areas with thick concrete walls.
2. **Execute a hard network refresh.**
   - **Toggle Airplane Mode ON for a full 15 seconds, then OFF.**
   - **Perform a full restart of the device.**
3. **Verify the cellular line is active in settings.**
   - On iPhone: Go to Settings > Cellular and ensure the primary line is "On."
   - On Android: Go to Settings > Network & Internet > SIMs and confirm the SIM is enabled.
4. **Enable Data Roaming temporarily.**
   - **Turn Data Roaming ON in your cellular settings.** This allows the phone to register on partner towers if your primary carrier's local tower is undergoing maintenance.
5. **Clear the SIM Toolkit or SIM Services cache (Android only).**
   - **Navigate to Settings > Apps > See all apps > Show system (via the three-dot menu).**
   - **Find "SIM Toolkit" or "SIM Services," select Storage & Cache, and tap Clear Cache.**
   - Restart the phone immediately after.
6. **Trigger a manual Carrier Settings update (iPhone only).**
   - **Connect to Wi-Fi and navigate to Settings > General > About.**
   - Wait for 30 seconds; if an update is available, a prompt will appear to "Update."
7. **Reseat the physical SIM or toggle the eSIM.**
   - Physical SIM: Power off the phone, remove the SIM tray, inspect the card for damage or debris, reinsert it firmly, and power on.
   - eSIM: Toggle the eSIM line OFF and then back ON in the cellular settings menu.
8. **Test connectivity via Wi-Fi Calling.**
   - **Join a trusted Wi-Fi network and enable Wi-Fi Calling in settings.**
   - Attempt a standard phone call; if this works, the issue is strictly related to cellular tower registration or local hardware.
9. **Verify account status and outages via carrier app.**
   - Use the Wi-Fi connection to log into your carrier account (e.g., My Verizon, myAT&T, T-Life).
   - Check for flags such as "Suspended," "Barred," "Reported Stolen," or "Payment Required."
10. **Contact carrier support for a signal "re-hit."**
    - Call 611 from a working phone or use the carrier's online chat.
    - Ask the representative to "re-provision the SIM" or "send a refresh signal" to your device.
    - Specifically ask if your IMEI has been "blacklisted" or if there is a known "SS7 registration" outage in your ZIP code.
11. **Dial 911 only for life-safety emergencies.**
    - **Phones showing "Emergency calls only" can still reach 911 by using any available carrier's tower.**
    - State your exact location immediately, as the 911 center may not receive accurate GPS data or be able to call you back if the line is not fully active.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to purchase a new device or switch service providers.
- Downloading third-party "signal booster" or "network repair" applications.
- Investigating the technical root cause of a carrier-side tower failure.

## Important reassurance
The "Emergency calls only" notification indicates that your device is powered on and capable of transmitting, but is currently unable to authenticate with your specific provider's network. This is a common occurrence during local maintenance, account updates, or minor software glitches and is typically resolved through standard reconnection protocols.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for restored connectivity. If the "Emergency calls only" status persists after following these steps, professional diagnostic service from a carrier or device manufacturer is required.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute technical or legal advice. If you are in immediate danger and your primary phone is non-functional, seek an alternative communication method immediately. All cellular devices in the USA are required by the FCC to transmit 911 calls regardless of active service, provided a compatible tower is within range.
