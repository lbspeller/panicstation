# What to do if… you suspect someone has installed a tracker or access to monitor where you go to control you sexually (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer location immediately and contact confidential specialist support using a device the other person has never touched. Do not disable or confront the person about the tracker yet, as sudden changes to your location data or tech habits can escalate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the individual or reveal that you suspect you are being tracked while you are still within their physical reach.
- Do not disable the tracker or turn off location services immediately if doing so will alert the person and increase your risk of harm.
- Do not search for a physical device (like an AirTag) in an isolated or private area where you cannot get help.
- Do not use the suspected device or a shared account to look up safety plans or exit strategies.
- Do not factory reset your phone without talking to an advocate first, as this may delete evidence you might later need.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** If you are in immediate physical danger, call **911**. If not in immediate danger, go to a public space (library, cafe, or store) where you are not alone.
2. **Obtain a clean communication method.** Use a computer at a public library, a friend’s phone, or a new "burner" device that the person has had no access to. Use this for all safety-related planning.
3. **Contact confidential specialist hotlines.**
   - **RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline:** Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)** or text **HOPE to 64673**.
   - **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** Call **800-799-SAFE (7233)** or text **START to 88788**.
4. **Identify the tracking method via your phone settings.** Check for built-in alerts on your device.
   - For iPhone: Go to the **Find My** app, tap **Items**, and look for **Items Detected With You**.
   - For Android: Go to **Settings > Safety & emergency > Unknown tracker alerts**.
5. **Request a technology safety consultation.** Contact the **NNEDV Safety Net Project** (TechSafety.org) or ask a local advocate for a "tech safety plan" to identify if the tracking is via a physical device, account sharing (Google/iCloud), or stalkerware.
6. **Check your vehicles and belongings safely.** Have a trusted person or a mechanic assist you in checking common hiding spots like wheel wells, bumpers, under seats, and inside bag linings. If you find a device, do not discard it yet; keep it as evidence if safe to do so.
7. **Secure your non-tracked accounts.** Change passwords only for accounts the person does not already monitor. Create a brand-new email address on a safe device to receive updates from advocates or legal services.
8. **Document the behavior on a safe medium.** Write down dates, times, and specific instances where the person appeared to know your location or used location data to pressure you for sexual acts. Keep this document in a secure location, such as a locked drawer at work or with a trusted friend.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a police report or end the relationship today.
- You do not need to find the physical tracker to prove what is happening; your intuition is a valid reason to seek support.
- You do not need to "fix" your digital privacy all at once; small, safe steps are more effective than high-risk resets.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel watched, anxious, or "crazy" when you suspect tech-based monitoring. Abusive individuals use these tools specifically to create a sense of omnipresence and control. Taking steps to protect your location is a practical response to a serious safety risk.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate safety. Ongoing technology safety planning and legal options should be managed with a trained advocate or a specialized legal professional.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In a crisis, the most reliable resources are direct lines to national hotlines which provide confidential, 24/7 assistance tailored to your specific jurisdiction and situation.
