# What to do if… you submitted an online travel declaration with an error and cannot edit it (USA)

## Short answer
Check whether the error changes how your details match your passport. If it affects your name, passport details, date of birth, or citizenship, use the official government system for that form and be prepared to submit a new correct application if the system says those fields cannot be edited.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter false details on another form to make records match.
- Do not use a third-party website offering paid edits, faster approval, or guaranteed correction.
- Do not ignore a mismatch in name, passport number, date of birth, passport expiry, or citizenship.
- Do not keep submitting new versions without saving each confirmation number and proof of what changed.
- Do not hide the mistake at airline check-in or U.S. inspection if you are asked about it.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact form or system you submitted.**  
   Use only the official government site or app for that exact form. For ESTA, use the official ESTA website.

2. **Compare the submitted details with your passport.**  
   Check the spelling of your name, passport number, date of birth, passport expiry, citizenship or nationality, and any document number shown on the confirmation.

3. **Treat name, passport, citizenship, and date-of-birth errors as urgent.**  
   CBP guidance says many ESTA fields can be updated, but passport details, country of citizenship, and date of birth require a new application. CBP guidance also says a new ESTA is required if your name, passport, or country of citizenship changes. If one of these details is wrong on an ESTA, use the official ESTA site and be prepared to submit a new correct application.

4. **If edits are allowed, make the correction in the official portal and save proof immediately.**  
   Save, download, or print the updated confirmation. Take a screenshot showing the corrected details if the portal allows it.

5. **If the system does not allow edits, make a new correct submission only through the official route.**  
   Do not try to make the old and new records match by changing accurate information. Keep the old confirmation so you can explain what happened if asked.

6. **Make a small offline proof pack.**  
   Keep copies of the original confirmation, the corrected confirmation or new application confirmation, any application number, and a screenshot or note showing what the error was.

7. **Raise the issue early at airline check-in if the error could affect boarding.**  
   Say plainly that you found an error and corrected it or submitted a new application. The airline cannot rewrite the government record for you, but early handling is safer than waiting until the gate.

8. **At U.S. inspection, be factual if asked.**  
   Use a short statement: “I submitted the online declaration, noticed an error I could not edit, and here is the correct information and confirmation.” Correct anything you are asked to declare rather than hoping the error is missed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to predict whether you will be admitted right now.
- You do not need a long explanation of how the mistake happened.
- You do not need to contact several agencies at once; start with the official system for the exact form you used.
- You do not need to decide future travel plans until the identity-critical details are corrected or clearly documented.

## Important reassurance
Mistakes on online travel forms happen. The most important thing is to avoid creating a second mismatch, keep clear proof, and be straightforward when asked.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the period before travel or at the earliest safe pause during travel. Later decisions, including whether to reapply, delay travel, or get specialist immigration help, may need advice from the official system, the airline, or a qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Requirements can vary by form, traveller status, and route, so check the official guidance for the exact system you used.
