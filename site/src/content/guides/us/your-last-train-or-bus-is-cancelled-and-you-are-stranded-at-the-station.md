# What to do if… your last train or bus is cancelled and you are stranded at the station (USA)

## Short answer
Move to the safest available staffed or busy, well-lit place. Check the carrier’s official status information and start with an official rebooking or carrier-directed option where one is available.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside alone to work out a plan if you can stay in a staffed or busy, well-lit place.
- Don’t accept rides from strangers or unsolicited taxi offers.
- Don’t assume you need to buy a new ticket before checking the carrier’s official options.
- Don’t wait in an isolated platform area, parking lot corner, or pickup area.
- Don’t let your phone battery run down while you decide; use battery-saver or low-power mode.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer waiting point.**  
   Go to a ticket counter, customer-service desk, staffed gate area, or a busy, well-lit place. If the station or terminal is closing, move to an open, staffed public place and ask before settling there.

2. **Confirm what is actually running.**  
   Check station boards and the carrier’s official app, website, or service alerts. Ask staff, if available: “My last connection is not running. What official options are available to reach **[destination]** or a safe stopping point?”

3. **For Amtrak, use an official modification channel.**  
   Use Amtrak.com, the Amtrak app, a station ticketing agent, web chat, or Reservations and Customer Service at **1-800-USA-RAIL (1-800-872-7245)**. Amtrak says assistance after a missed guaranteed connection is provided at its discretion, so ask an agent what applies to your trip.

4. **For an intercity bus, check the carrier’s own instructions.**  
   Use the carrier’s official app, website, help route, or counter if one is open. Policies vary. For Greyhound, if you miss a connecting bus because the earlier part of your Greyhound connection was delayed, Greyhound says it will book you free onto the next available connection and send instructions by text or email.

5. **Choose a safe pause if the next workable service is not until later.**  
   Ask staff whether the building remains open and where they recommend waiting. If you need to move, use an official transport option to reach an open, staffed place rather than waiting alone outside.

6. **Use a controlled pickup if you switch to a taxi or ride-hail service.**  
   Request the ride from inside a staffed or well-lit place, or use an official taxi stand or dispatcher. For a ride-hail service, check the verification details shown in the app before getting in. For an Uber ride, match the license plate, vehicle make and model, and driver photo. If the details do not match, cancel the ride and return to the safer waiting point.

7. **Escalate early if you feel unsafe.**  
   Move toward staff and other people. For an emergency, call **911**. At or near an Amtrak station, crime, emergencies, suspicious items, persons, or activity can also be reported to the Amtrak Police Department at **800-331-0008** or by texting **APD11 (27311)**.

8. **Send one brief update before you move again.**  
   Tell someone you trust where you are, which option you are taking, and when you expect to arrive or check in again.

9. **Keep the basic record.**  
   Screenshot service alerts and keep your ticket and any receipts. You can deal with refund or complaint questions later.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide tonight whether to request a refund, complain, or dispute a charge.
- You do **not** need to find the cheapest possible route before choosing a safe workable option.
- You do **not** need to solve the whole trip at once. A safe stopping point or staffed hub can be the next step.

## Important reassurance
A canceled last connection can make the situation feel more urgent than it is. You can slow the next decision down: stay near other people, verify what is running, and choose one safe next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation. Later decisions about refunds, rebooking, accommodation, or further travel planning may need help from the carrier or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Carrier rules and available support can vary by ticket, route, operator, location, and cause of disruption. In an emergency, call 911.
