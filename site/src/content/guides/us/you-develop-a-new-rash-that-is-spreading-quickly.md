# What to do if… you develop a new rash that is spreading quickly (USA)
## Short answer
Arrange medical assessment today for a new rash that is spreading quickly. Call 911 now for trouble breathing or swallowing, swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or mouth, fainting, confusion, or rapidly worsening severe illness.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait overnight if the rash is continuing to spread quickly or you feel ill.
- Do not try several new creams, ointments, or home remedies before assessment.
- Do not scrub the rash.
- Do not stop or skip prescribed medicines on your own, except as stated below for pain relievers or fever reducers.
- Do not walk into a shared waiting area without calling ahead if you have a new rash with fever and measles-like symptoms, or if you may have been exposed to measles.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now for signs of a severe allergic reaction or rapidly worsening illness.**
   - Trouble breathing, wheezing, throat tightness, or trouble swallowing
   - Swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or inside your mouth
   - Fainting, confusion, severe light-headedness, or rapidly worsening illness
2. **Use prescribed emergency epinephrine if you have it.**
   - If you have signs of a severe allergic reaction and an epinephrine auto-injector, use it as directed and call 911.
3. **Go to the emergency department now for serious skin warning signs.**
   - A purple or bruise-like rash with fever or feeling ill
   - A painful spreading rash with blisters or peeling skin
   - Sores affecting your mouth, eyes, or genitals
   - Call 911 instead if you are getting worse quickly or cannot travel safely.
4. **Otherwise, arrange assessment today.**
   - Contact your primary care office for a same-day visit or go to urgent care.
   - If you are unsure whether you need emergency care, choose the emergency department.
5. **Check recent medicines.**
   - If the rash started while you were taking acetaminophen or another pain reliever or fever reducer, stop that medicine and seek medical attention right away.
   - For any other recently started or changed prescribed medicine, do not stop or skip doses unless a clinician or pharmacist advises this. Seek urgent advice before the next dose.
6. **Write down key details for the clinician.**
   - When the rash started and how quickly it is spreading
   - Fever, headache, stiff neck, sore throat, vomiting, diarrhea, or joint pain
   - New or changed prescription medicines, over-the-counter medicines, supplements, and herbal products
   - Recent illness, travel, sick contacts, insect bites, stings, detergents, or skin products
7. **Take photos if this will not delay care.**
   - Take one close-up photo and one wider photo in good light.
8. **Call ahead if measles may be possible.**
   - If you have a new rash with fever and cough, runny nose, or red watery eyes, or if you may have been exposed to measles, call the clinic or urgent care before arriving and follow their entry instructions.
9. **Use simple comfort measures while arranging care.**
   - Keep cool, wear loose clothing, and use a cool compress.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause before seeking care.
- You do not need to decide whether you need allergy testing or a specialist.
- You do not need to replace skin products or make a long-term treatment plan now.

## Important reassurance
A rash can have many causes, and its appearance alone does not tell you which cause applies. Getting prompt assessment is the appropriate next step and gives a clinician the chance to check for the less common serious causes.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes to hours. Later decisions may need help from a clinician or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical or other professional advice. It is not a diagnosis and does not replace assessment by a qualified healthcare professional.
