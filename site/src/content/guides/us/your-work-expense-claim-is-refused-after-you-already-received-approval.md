# What to do if… your work expense claim is refused after you already received approval (USA)

## Short answer
Ask for the refusal and its reason in writing. Reply with the prior approval, receipts, and a short itemized list, and ask whether any specific correction is needed before payment can be reconsidered.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send angry messages, mass emails, or a resignation message over this today.
- Do not agree in writing that the expense was personal or unauthorized if you disagree.
- Do not make new out-of-pocket work purchases until you understand the current written approval and reimbursement terms.
- Do not rely only on a verbal promise that it will be fixed later; ask for a written update.
- Do not assume the same reimbursement rule or complaint route applies in every state.

## What to do now
1. **Save the core documents.** Keep the written approval, the expense policy that applied when approval was given if available, your submitted report, receipts, the refusal, and any relevant pay stubs.
2. **Ask for a line-by-line written answer.** Ask which items were refused, the policy reason for each refusal, whether any document or correction is missing, whether the decision is final, and who reviews exceptions.
3. **Ask the approving manager to confirm the business purpose and prior approval in writing.** Use the employer’s finance, payroll, HR, or expense-review route as applicable, and send the same short document packet each time.
4. **If you are nonexempt, note any pay impact.** Record the workweek, hours worked, pay received, and the unreimbursed employer-required cost. Under federal wage rules, a required cost that primarily benefits the employer may raise an issue if it cuts into required minimum-wage or overtime compensation.
5. **Check whether your state has a specific reimbursement rule.** California and Illinois have official reimbursement provisions linked below. Illinois has a reimbursement rule for certain necessary work expenses with documentation and written-policy conditions. If you are elsewhere, check your state labor agency before relying on a rule from another state.
6. **Use the official state page if the issue remains unresolved.** California’s Labor Commissioner wage-claim page expressly includes unpaid reimbursements. In Illinois, review the official reimbursement statute and Department of Labor wage-payment page, then use the workplace-complaint page to identify the current route that best matches your facts.
7. **Keep a factual log.** Record the date, the person or team contacted, the documents sent, and the response. Keep the request focused on what is needed to review and pay the approved expense.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to hire a lawyer or start a court case.
- You do not need to write a long history of every workplace problem.
- You do not need to resign or make a major career decision while the reimbursement issue is still being clarified.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to solve every legal or workplace question today. A clear written packet can help identify whether the problem is missing documentation, a policy dispute, or a final refusal.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on your state, pay classification, employer policy, and the facts of the expense, and may need specialist help.
