# What to do if… you suspect someone is trying to pickpocket you in a crowd (USA)

## Short answer
Create space immediately: move toward staff, security, a shop counter, or other visible witnesses, and secure your pockets or bag. If you feel threatened or the attempt is happening now, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stop in an isolated spot to check your wallet or phone.
- Don’t pull out your wallet in the crowd to count everything.
- Don’t let someone guide you into a tighter space, such as a stairwell, elevator, doorway, or space between transit cars.
- Don’t argue with them or try to physically restrain them.
- Don’t chase someone if they run.
- Don’t accept “help” from a stranger who insists on touching your bag, coat, or pockets.
- Don’t keep re-checking the same pocket while you are still in the crowd.

## What to do now
1. **Move your body to reduce access.**  
   Step out of the crush of people. Put your back to a wall, pillar, shop counter, ticket booth, or staffed area. If you’re on transit, move away from doorways and toward other passengers, staff, or a better-lit area.

2. **Secure the exact place they were near.**  
   Bring your bag to the front, close zippers, and keep one hand over the pocket or bag opening they were hovering near. If you are wearing a backpack, bring it forward or hold it close by the top handle.

3. **Break contact without debating it.**  
   Change direction, step into a nearby business, move beside staff, or stand near a group of other people. If you’re with someone, say: “Stay with me. Let’s move over there.”

4. **Ask for help where there are witnesses.**  
   Tell staff, security, a station agent, or a shop worker: “Someone was crowding me with their hands near my pocket or bag. Can I stand here while I check my things?” If you are on transit property, ask for transit security or police if available.

5. **Call 911 if there is immediate danger.**  
   Call 911 if you feel threatened, the person is still following or cornering you, or the theft is happening now. If the danger has passed and you need to report a theft, ask staff to help you contact local law enforcement or transit security through their non-emergency route.

6. **Check essentials only once you are in a safer spot.**  
   Without laying everything out, check for your phone, wallet, cards, keys, and ID. Keep your bag closed between checks.

7. **If a card is missing, lock or report it immediately.**  
   Use your banking app, online account, statement, or a trusted official phone number for the bank or card issuer. Avoid using phone numbers sent by unexpected text or email.

8. **If ID or sensitive information is missing, reduce identity-theft risk.**  
   If your ID, Social Security card, bank cards, or other sensitive documents may be gone, use IdentityTheft.gov and consider a fraud alert or credit freeze.

## What can wait
- Writing a detailed timeline while you are still in the crowd.
- Replacing IDs before you have secured cards, banking access, and your phone.
- Posting about it or trying to identify the person yourself.
- Deciding whether you want to make every possible report.
- Re-checking every pocket repeatedly before you are somewhere safer.

## Important reassurance
You do not have to prove what they intended before moving away. Repeated bumping, crowding, and hands near pockets or a bag are enough reason to create space and ask for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get safer, protect essentials, and reduce fast follow-on harm such as card misuse or identity theft. Later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, your bank, a card issuer, or an identity-theft recovery service.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate safety and damage-limitation, not legal, financial, security, or professional advice. If you are in danger or feel threatened right now, call 911.
