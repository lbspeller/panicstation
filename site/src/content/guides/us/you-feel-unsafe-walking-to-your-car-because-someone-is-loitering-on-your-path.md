# What to do if… you feel unsafe walking to your car because someone is loitering on your path (USA)

## Short answer
Do not walk past them. Go back to a staffed, public, well-lit place and ask for an escort if available, or arrange a different ride from inside. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep walking just to avoid attention or embarrassment.
- Do not confront the person, argue, or try to work out their intentions up close.
- Do not take a quieter shortcut through rows of cars, stairwells, side exits, alleys, or isolated garage levels.
- Do not stand outside searching for keys, looking down at your phone, or wearing headphones that block what is around you.
- Do not unlock your car and then pause outside it.
- Do not get into your car and sit there with the doors unlocked while you wait.

## What to do now
1. **Stop and change direction.** Turn around and go back to the nearest public, staffed, or busy place, such as inside the store, lobby, front desk, security desk, reception area, or main entrance.
2. **Put people and visibility around you.** Stand near staff, a front counter, reception, or a place where other people are passing through. Do not wait alone outside to see what happens.
3. **Ask for an escort if one is available.** Some campuses, hospitals, venues, large workplaces, stores, and parking operators may be able to provide a staff or security walk-out. Say: “I do not feel safe walking to my car. Can someone escort me to row or level X?” If they cannot, ask to wait inside while you arrange another option.
4. **Arrange a safer alternative from inside.** Call a friend, relative, rideshare, taxi, or trusted coworker and have them meet you at the entrance rather than asking you to walk out alone.
5. **Call 911 if it feels like an immediate threat.** This includes situations such as the person approaching you, following you, blocking your route, trying to get you alone, or making you feel you cannot leave safely. Say your location first, including the address, business name, parking lot or garage section, level, row, and entrance if you know them. Then describe what is happening and where you are waiting.
6. **If you decide to go to the car with help, keep the move simple.** Have your keys ready before leaving the safe place, walk with the escort or group, get in, lock the doors immediately, start the car, and leave without sitting there.
7. **If you feel followed after driving away, do not go home.** Drive to an open, busy, well-lit place where other people are present, and call 911 if you need urgent help.
8. **After you are safe, report it to the property.** Tell the store, venue, workplace, campus, or parking operator what happened and where. This may help them respond, send security if available, increase patrols, or notice repeat problems.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether the person is “actually dangerous.”
- You do not need to gather proof, take photos, or confront them to justify leaving.
- You do not need to file a report on the spot if you are shaken.
- You do not need to keep your original plan; getting picked up, waiting inside, or leaving later is allowed.

## Important reassurance
Taking a safer route back inside is a reasonable response. You are allowed to make the situation less private, less isolated, and less rushed without proving anything first.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for creating a safer pause, getting to your car with help, or choosing another way to leave. Later decisions, including follow-up with the property, campus security, workplace security, or police, can be handled after you are safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.
