# What to do if… a partner pressures you to take or send intimate photos or videos (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause and do not create or send intimate photos or videos you do not want to make. Contact a trusted person or a trained advocate from a device your partner cannot access, if that is safer.

## Do not do these things
- Do not create or send an intimate photo or video to calm the situation down.
- Do not let someone take an intimate photo or video when you do not want them to.
- Do not share your phone, passcode, or account access to prove anything.
- Do not meet your partner alone to discuss the request if you are worried they may escalate.
- Do not make rushed decisions about reporting, the relationship, or account changes while you are trying to reach a safer pause.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move to a place where you feel safer, such as a separate space, a public place, or somewhere with another person. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Do not create or send the image.** If it feels safe, use a brief response such as: “No. I do not want to make or send that.” You do not need to debate or justify your answer.
3. **Contact support from a safer device if possible.** Tell one trusted person what is happening, or contact a trained advocate through RAINN or the National Domestic Violence Hotline. If you think your device use is monitored, use a device your partner cannot access if one is available.
4. **If an image or video has already been posted, choose only the next step that feels safe.** You may ask the app or platform to remove it. If it is still online 48 hours after your request, or the platform does not offer a working request process, the FTC resource below explains how to report that problem. If your partner is threatening to share content that has not been posted, you may report the threat inside the app or platform and contact support.
5. **If the image or video was taken before you turned 18, consider NCMEC support.** You may use the CyberTipline or Take It Down. Do not download, send, or share an image solely to use Take It Down.
6. **If the image or video was taken when you were 18 or older, StopNCII may be an option.** Use it only if you are depicted in an eligible intimate image or video that is already on your device.
7. **Keep only a minimal record if it is safe.** If you may want help later, save existing messages or links somewhere your partner cannot access only if doing so will not increase risk.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to report to police.
- You do not need to confront your partner.
- You do not need to gather extensive evidence.
- You do not need to decide what happens to the relationship today.
- You do not need to solve every account, platform, or removal issue in one sitting.

## Important reassurance
Pressure, repeated requests, manipulation, or threats around intimate images can make it hard to think clearly. You are allowed to say no. Needing support or taking time to reach a safer pause does not mean you have done anything wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need support from a specialist advocate or another qualified professional familiar with your situation and your state.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**. If an image involves someone who was under 18 when it was taken, use specialist child-safety support.
