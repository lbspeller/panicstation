# What to do if… you have a positive pregnancy test and are experiencing pain or bleeding (USA)

## Short answer
Call 911 or go to the ER now if you have severe or worsening belly or pelvic pain, shoulder pain, fainting, severe dizziness or weakness, heavy vaginal bleeding, chest pain, trouble breathing, confusion, or you feel very unwell. For any bleeding or new pain after a positive pregnancy test, contact an OB-GYN or another healthcare professional right away; if you cannot reach one, go to the ER.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for symptoms to become severe if you have bleeding with pain, one-sided pelvic pain, shoulder pain, dizziness, fainting, or worsening symptoms.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, dizzy, weak, or very unwell, or if you have severe pain or heavy bleeding.
- Do not use a tampon while you are bleeding. Use a pad so you can describe the amount of bleeding.
- Do not start ibuprofen, naproxen, or another NSAID while arranging care unless a clinician advises it.
- Do not use pain medicine to delay urgent assessment.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 or go to the ER now** if you have severe or worsening belly or pelvic pain, shoulder pain, fainting, severe dizziness or weakness, heavy vaginal bleeding, chest pain, trouble breathing, confusion, or you feel very unwell.
2. **Contact an OB-GYN or another healthcare professional right away** if you have spotting, bleeding more than spotting such as bleeding like a period, new cramps or pain, one-sided pelvic pain, a fever of 100.4°F or higher, bad-smelling discharge, or a feeling that something is wrong. If you cannot reach a healthcare professional, go to the ER.
3. **Use a pad if you are bleeding.** Note whether the bleeding is spotting, like a period, or heavier, and whether it is increasing.
4. **Make a short symptom note for triage:** when the symptoms started, where the pain is, whether it is one-sided, whether it is worsening, the amount of bleeding, any dizziness or fainting, shoulder pain, fever, vomiting, diarrhea, and the date of your last normal period if you know it.
5. **Say this clearly when you call or arrive:** “I have a positive home pregnancy test and I have bleeding, pain, or other concerning symptoms.”
6. **Ask someone else to drive you or call 911** if you do not feel safe to drive. Bring your medication list and allergy information. A photo of the positive test may be useful if it is easy to bring.
7. **Ask a clinician or pharmacist before taking an over-the-counter pain reliever.** Do not delay assessment to see whether medicine makes the symptoms settle.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today what to do about the pregnancy beyond getting assessed for your symptoms.
- You do not need to book routine prenatal visits or calculate dates right now.
- You do not need to work out the cause yourself.
- Work and family explanations can wait. “I am having urgent symptoms and getting checked” is enough.

## Important reassurance
Bleeding or pain after a positive pregnancy test can be frightening. Some early-pregnancy bleeding does not mean pregnancy loss, but certain symptoms can need urgent care. Getting checked is the right next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Follow-up decisions and longer-term planning may need help from a healthcare professional after you have been assessed.
