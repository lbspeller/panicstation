# What to do if… you lock your keys inside your vehicle but still have your phone (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safer spot first. Then use your phone to try any remote unlock you already have set up, call your roadside assistance provider, or call a reputable automotive locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t try routine break-in methods such as wedges, coat hangers, or smashing a window unless there is immediate danger and no safer option.
- Don’t stand in traffic, on a road shoulder, or in an isolated spot while making calls.
- Don’t hand your phone to a stranger or let anyone pressure you into a quick cash payment.
- Don’t click unfamiliar payment links sent by text; use a known business and a normal payment method where possible.
- Don’t treat a routine lockout as a 911 emergency. Use 911 if a person is in danger, a pet appears in immediate distress, or you feel threatened.

## What to do now
1. **Make the scene safer.** Move away from traffic, stay visible, and keep your phone with you. Turn on low-power mode if your battery is low.
2. **Check the vehicle once without forcing it.** Try each door, the hatch, and the trunk. Stop if anything would require prying, bending, or breaking.
3. **Try remote unlock only if it is already set up.** Use your vehicle app or connected service if you are enrolled and the vehicle is equipped for it. Be ready to verify your identity or account.
4. **Call roadside assistance if you have it.** This may be through an auto club, your insurer, a credit card benefit, a manufacturer plan, or a vehicle connected-service plan. Ask whether lockout service is covered, who is being dispatched, the estimated arrival window, and any charge before they send help.
5. **If you call a locksmith, slow the payment down.** Confirm the business name, service-call fee, total likely price range, vehicle type, arrival vehicle, and how they will verify that you are allowed to access the vehicle.
6. **If a child is locked inside, call 911 immediately.** Give the dispatcher your exact location, vehicle description, and the child’s condition. Stay on the line and follow instructions. If the child is unresponsive or appears in heat distress, focus on getting the child out as quickly as possible while help is on the way.
7. **If a pet is locked inside, check for distress and heat risk.** If the pet is panting heavily, drooling, weak, unresponsive, trapped in heat, or otherwise in immediate danger, call 911. If the pet is not in immediate distress, call local animal control or the police non-emergency number and stay near the vehicle if it is safe. Do not break glass unless emergency instructions or immediate danger leave no safer option.
8. **When help arrives, verify before unlocking.** Check the company name, vehicle, ID, and price before work starts. Keep your phone, wallet, and ID with you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about replacing keys, changing locks, or changing keyless-entry settings.
- You do not need to compare every provider while stressed; choose the safest credible route and get help moving.
- You do not need to make insurance or reimbursement decisions until you and the vehicle are safe.
- You do not need to explain or justify the mistake to bystanders.

## Important reassurance
Locking keys inside a vehicle is common and fixable. Having your phone gives you time to choose help without damaging the vehicle or putting yourself at risk.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Costs, coverage, response times, and emergency procedures may vary by provider, vehicle system, city, and state, and later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate steps in the USA. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone is in danger or you feel threatened, call 911.
