# What to do if… your luggage is flagged for inspection at an airport and officials want to check it (USA)

## Short answer
Stay where directed, do not reach into the bag unless asked, and calmly confirm whether this is security screening or a customs inspection. Ask what item is causing concern and what immediate options are available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reach into the luggage or remove an item unless an official directs you to do so.
- Do not argue, threaten, or make jokes about weapons, explosives, or security.
- Do not guess if you do not know what the item is or how it got there.
- Do not assume that surrendering an item at a checkpoint and having property seized by customs are the same process.
- Do not walk away before asking what record, notice, or contact route applies to your situation.

## What to do now
1. **Clarify who is inspecting the luggage.** Ask: “Is this security screening, a customs inspection, or another process?”
2. **Let the official direct the bag opening.** Keep the luggage where requested. If it is locked, ask: “Would you like me to unlock or open it?”
3. **Ask which item is causing concern.** Use a brief question: “Which item do you need to inspect?” If you recognise it, answer simply and accurately.
4. **Ask which immediate options are available.** At a security checkpoint, the available options may depend on the item and the circumstances. Ask whether you may place it in checked baggage, return it to a vehicle, give it to a non-traveling person, voluntarily abandon it, or take another permitted step.
5. **If the item was removed from checked baggage, ask where questions should go.** TSA states that prohibited items removed from checked baggage are not returned through a TSA retrieval process. Ask whether your airline is the right contact for further questions.
6. **If customs officials say the property has been seized, ask for the next-step record.** Ask whether a written notice will be provided or sent, how to confirm your contact details, and which contact point applies. Keep any paperwork and read any later notice promptly.
7. **Ask for a supervisor if the process is unclear.** You can say: “I want to cooperate. Could a supervisor explain what happens next?”
8. **Reduce the immediate travel disruption.** If phone use is allowed, tell your airline or the person meeting you that you are delayed. You do not need to explain more than that.
9. **Ask for language assistance if you need it.** Ask the inspecting official for an interpreter or language assistance. If you are a foreign national and you are arrested or detained, ask about consular notification and access.

## What can wait
- You do not need to debate the rule while the inspection is happening.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to challenge a customs seizure.
- You do not need to explain the delay to everyone. One brief message to the airline or a key contact is enough for now.
- You do not need to solve the rest of the trip until you know whether you can continue through the airport.

## Important reassurance
An inspection can happen because an item triggered an alarm, is not permitted in that part of the journey, or needs a closer look. Being asked to wait does not by itself mean that the final outcome has already been decided.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the inspection and the immediate travel delay. Later decisions may need help from the airline, the relevant agency, or a qualified professional after you have the written information.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, travel, or other professional advice. The correct next step depends on the item, the airport process, and the authority involved.
