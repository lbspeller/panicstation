# What to do if… your passport is hole-punched, clipped, or marked by mistake and you are worried it may not be accepted (USA)

## Short answer
Apply for a replacement passport immediately using Form DS-11 as a damaged document. If traveling internationally within 14 calendar days, you must secure an Urgent Travel Service appointment at a passport agency.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to repair the document with tape, glue, or lamination.
- Do not use white-out, erasers, or pens to cover or explain unofficial marks.
- Do not trim the edges or "clean up" a clipped corner or hole punch.
- Do not attempt to use the passport for travel if the data page or RFID chip area is affected.
- Do not mail a renewal application (Form DS-82); damaged passports must be replaced in person.

## What to do now
1. **Verify if the passport meets the U.S. Department of State criteria for "damaged."**
   - A hole punch through any part of the book or card.
   - Unofficial markings, drawings, or ink on the data page.
   - A clipped or cut corner.
   - A significant tear or loose/missing cover.
   - Water damage, including stains, mold, or warping.

2. **Prepare a replacement application using the DS-11 path.**
   - **Download and complete Form DS-11** but do not sign it until instructed by an official agent.
   - **Write a signed statement** that describes how the damage (the hole, clip, or mark) occurred.
   - **Gather original evidence of citizenship** (such as a birth certificate) and a valid photo ID, as a damaged passport may not be accepted as standalone proof.

3. **Determine your travel urgency window to choose the correct application route.**
   - **If traveling in 14 days or more:** Locate a local passport acceptance facility (post office or clerk of court) and make an appointment to apply in person.
   - **If traveling in less than 14 days (or 28 days for a visa):** Call the National Passport Information Center at 1-877-487-2778 (TTY/TDD: 1-888-874-7793) to schedule an "Urgent Travel Service" appointment.

4. **Prepare for the in-person appointment costs and requirements.**
   - **Obtain a new passport photo** taken within the last 6 months.
   - **Bring the damaged passport** to the appointment; it will usually be retained.
   - **Bring payment for the application fee and execution fee** (check the current 2026 fee schedule for adult vs. minor rates).

5. **Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate if you are currently outside the United States.**
   - **Request an emergency passport** (limited validity) if you have immediate onward travel.
   - **Provide the damaged book and the signed statement of damage** to the consular officer.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the airline "might" let you fly; standard procedure is to replace any passport with a hole punch or data page mark.
- You do not need to contact the entity that caused the damage (e.g., a foreign official or office) before starting the replacement process.
- You do not need to file a police report unless the damage was the result of a criminal act.

## Important reassurance
Physical alterations like hole punches or unofficial ink marks are common reasons for passport replacement. While the timing is inconvenient, the replacement process is a standard administrative procedure. Following the in-person DS-11 route ensures the document is correctly updated in government records.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and the mandatory replacement path for damaged U.S. passports. It does not provide legal advice, airline-specific boarding guarantees, or help with financial claims against third parties.

## Important note
Passports that appear altered, mutilated, or cancelled may be confiscated or rejected by airlines and border authorities. Application processing times vary and are subject to the U.S. Department of State’s current capacity.
