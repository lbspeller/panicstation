# What to do if… you are pressured to meet privately after reporting sexual harassment and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer

You can decline a private meeting in the moment. Say that you do not feel safe meeting one-to-one and ask for written communication or a safer meeting format with an appropriate third person present.

## Do not do these things

- Do not enter a private, isolated, off-site, or after-hours meeting if it feels unsafe.
- Do not let pressure to seem cooperative push you into a one-to-one conversation.
- Do not feel that you must explain the full situation directly to the person involved.
- Do not delete messages or meeting requests connected to the situation.
- Do not make an irreversible job decision in the immediate moment unless you need to leave for safety.

## What to do now

1. Move to a safer place or pause the conversation if you feel at risk. If there is immediate danger, call 911.

2. Send a short written response. For example: “I do not feel safe meeting one-to-one. Please send any questions in writing or arrange a meeting through the usual workplace process with an appropriate third person present.”

3. You may use your employer’s harassment-reporting policy, HR contact, or designated reporting channel where possible. Say clearly that you were asked to meet privately and that you do not feel safe doing so.

4. Ask for a safer format, such as written questions, a remote meeting, or a meeting with an appropriate third person present. If you are union-represented, ask your union what representation may be available.

5. If it feels safe to do so, keep the meeting request and related messages.

6. If you notice pressure, isolation, punishment, or worse treatment after raising concerns, you may note this and consider getting EEOC information promptly. Some forms of retaliation may be unlawful.

7. If the situation involves assault, threats, stalking, or fear of harm, focus on safety first. Contact someone you trust or a specialist support service. Whether to report to law enforcement is your choice.

## What can wait

You do not need to decide today whether to resign, confront anyone directly, file a formal external complaint, or make a final statement. The immediate priority is to avoid an unsafe one-to-one meeting and move communication into a safer channel.

## Important reassurance

It is reasonable to ask for a safer way to communicate. You do not have to resolve this privately or immediately.

## Scope note

These are first steps only. Later decisions about workplace processes, outside complaints, legal advice, or job changes may need specialist help.

## Important note

This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Workplace policies and legal options can vary. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. Confidential specialist support is available through RAINN.
