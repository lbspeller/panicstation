# What to do if… a windowpane develops a large crack that looks like it could fail (USA)

## Short answer
Keep people and pets away from both sides of the window. Do not touch, tape, press, or move the pane; arrange prompt professional assessment and securing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not test whether the glass is loose.
- Do not open or close the window, door, or frame if this could move the pane.
- Do not lean near the glass to close blinds, take close-up photos, or inspect the crack.
- Do not tape, glue, press, or try to repair a pane that looks unstable.
- Do not let children or pets enter the area.
- Do not pick up broken glass with bare hands.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance.** Keep people and pets away from the room or the part of the room near the pane. Keep people away from the outside area too if glass could fall onto a walkway, driveway, yard, or shared space.
2. **Avoid movement.** Leave the window, door, curtains, blinds, and nearby furniture alone if moving them could bring anyone close to the glass or shake the frame.
3. **Arrange help.**
   - **If you rent:** report the hazard promptly through your landlord, property manager, or building emergency-maintenance route. Describe the large crack and any risk to people, weather protection, or security. Keep a record of the report.
   - **If you own:** contact a local glass-repair company or board-up service and explain that the pane looks unstable.
4. **Use emergency services only for an immediate danger.** Call 911 if falling glass is threatening people and the area cannot be kept clear, or if the situation requires immediate help from police, the fire department, or an ambulance.
5. **Document from a safe distance.** Take a photo only if you can do so without standing close to the pane, touching the frame, or entering an area where glass could fall.
6. **If glass has already fallen, do not start cleanup while more glass may fall.** Once the area can be approached safely, wear sturdy closed-toe shoes and work gloves. Use a broom and dustpan for shards; do not use your hands. Do not put broken glass in recycling. Ask your local waste provider how to package and dispose of it.
7. **Cover an opening only after the immediate shard risk is controlled.** Ask the glass company, landlord, property manager, or board-up service about a safe temporary cover if the pane breaks or is removed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out liability, deposits, or insurance details while the pane is still a hazard.
- You do not need to choose upgrades or decide whether the whole window should be replaced now.
- You do not need to investigate the cause before creating distance and arranging help.
- You do not need to install a temporary cover while loose glass may still fall.

## Important reassurance
A large crack can feel alarming. The useful first steps are simple: create distance, avoid movement, keep others clear, and arrange help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a potentially unstable pane. Later decisions about repairs, insurance, rental disputes, or local housing rules may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Rental repair processes and disposal rules vary by state and locality. Prioritize distance and emergency help when there is an immediate danger to people.
