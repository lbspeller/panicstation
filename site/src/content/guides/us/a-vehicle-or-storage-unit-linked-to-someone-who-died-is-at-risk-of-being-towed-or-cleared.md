# What to do if… a vehicle or storage unit linked to someone who died is at risk of being towed or cleared (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the tow operator, property manager, or storage facility now. Ask what will pause the tow, sale, or clear-out today, what documents they accept, and whether they can confirm any pause in writing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter a storage unit, move a vehicle, or remove belongings unless the person controlling the location confirms that access is allowed and any required authority has been provided.
- Do not assume that a death certificate alone will be enough. Ask what documents are accepted for a pause, access, or release.
- Do not ignore a tow notice, impound notice, storage lien notice, auction notice, or clear-out deadline. Fees may continue, and the next step may be time-sensitive.
- Do not send documents or payment until you have independently verified the tow operator, property manager, or storage facility contact details.
- Do not discard keys, notices, account records, vehicle papers, or photographs.

## What to do now
1. **Identify who currently controls the vehicle or storage unit.**
   - For a vehicle, find out whether it is still parked, has been scheduled for towing, or is already at an impound lot.
   - For a storage unit, ask whether there is a clear-out, lien sale, auction, or other disposition date.

2. **Ask what can pause the action today.**
   - Ask for the exact deadline, the current fees, the next scheduled action, and the name of the person handling the account.
   - Ask whether a payment, document, or written request may pause the tow, sale, or clear-out.
   - Ask for written confirmation of any pause and the remaining requirements.

3. **Gather the documents you already have.**
   - Photo ID.
   - A copy of the death certificate, or information about when a certified copy will be available.
   - Any court papers or estate documents already issued.
   - The vehicle plate number, vehicle identification number, unit number, account details, and any notice reference number.
   - Ask the operator or facility what else they accept. Requirements vary.

4. **For a vehicle, ask separately about release and extra time.**
   - If the vehicle is at an impound lot, ask what is needed to pause further action and what is needed for release.
   - If the vehicle is still on private property, ask the property manager or association whether a written extension is available while the estate arranges removal.
   - Use your state motor vehicle agency website to check the deceased-owner title or registration process.

5. **For a storage unit, ask about the notice and account status.**
   - Ask when the most recent notice was sent and where it was sent.
   - Ask what payment, document, or other step may pause a sale, auction, or clear-out.
   - Ask whether the facility can add contact details for the person handling the estate.

6. **If access is confirmed and authorized, record the condition before removing anything.**
   - Take photographs of the vehicle or storage unit as found.
   - Keep a short list of anything removed and where it is stored.
   - Preserve IDs, keys, title or registration papers, insurance papers, account records, and sentimental items.

7. **Get urgent local help if authority is unclear and disposal may happen soon.**
   - Use the state or territory court website to locate the relevant probate court and ask where local process information is published.
   - Contact legal aid or a probate attorney if there is a dispute, the required paperwork is unclear, or the operator says the vehicle or contents may be disposed of soon.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today who should inherit the vehicle or the contents of the storage unit.
- You do not need to complete a full inventory today. Photographs and a short list are enough for the first step.
- You do not need to resolve family disagreements today. Focus on finding the deadline, requesting a pause, and keeping a written record.

## Important reassurance
One written contact, one confirmed deadline, and one clear document list are useful progress. You only need to handle the next time-sensitive step first.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the risk of towing, sale, or clear-out and to buy time. Later decisions about title transfer, estate authority, fees, contracts, or disputed ownership may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. State law, local rules, contracts, and company procedures vary.
