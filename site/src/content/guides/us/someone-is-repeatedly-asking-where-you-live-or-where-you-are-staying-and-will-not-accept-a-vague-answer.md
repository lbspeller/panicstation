# What to do if… someone is repeatedly asking where you live or where you are staying and will not accept a vague answer (USA)

## Short answer
Do not provide your address or destination. Immediately move to a well-lit, staffed, public area and enlist the help of employees or security to create a safe exit or waiting plan.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide "nearby" details such as your neighborhood, street name, hotel brand, or floor number.
- Do not attempt to be "polite" by allowing them to walk you to your car, transit stop, or room.
- Do not ignore your intuition if a staff member announces your room number out loud; this is a violation of standard US hospitality safety protocols.
- Do not use a rideshare or taxi if the person is watching you get in, unless the driver can confirm they will not be followed.
- Do not show your phone screen, maps, or flight/hotel confirmation emails to the person.
- Do not post to social media or "check in" at your current location until you have safely reached your final destination.

## What to do now
1. **State a final boundary and physically move away.**  
   **Say: "I do not share that information / I am not comfortable telling you where I am staying."** Do not wait for a response or an apology; immediately turn and walk toward a staff member or a group of people.
2. **Disable digital location tracking on your devices.**  
   **Turn off Bluetooth, AirDrop (on iPhone), and "Share My Location" settings in your phone's privacy menu.** This prevents the possibility of a "digital tag" or tracking via nearby device signals while you are in close proximity.
3. **Involve onsite staff or security immediately.**  
   **Go to a front desk, security station, or manager and state: "I am being harassed and this person is pressuring me for my home address."** Ask them to monitor the person while you leave or to provide a safe place for you to wait until the person has left the area.
4. **Request "Incognito" or "No-Post" status if at a hotel.**  
   **Tell the front desk: "I need to be placed on Incognito status and ensure my name is not in the guest directory."** This prevents staff from confirming your presence or room number to callers or visitors who might ask for you by name.
5. **Use a "dummy" destination if you suspect you are being followed.**  
   **If you are driving or walking, do not go to your actual residence; instead, go to a police station or a 24-hour staffed business like a hospital or large supermarket.** Only proceed to your home or hotel once you are certain no one is following you.
6. **Capture a description of the individual and their vehicle.**  
   **Note their height, clothing, distinct features, and any vehicle make / model / license plate if visible from a safe distance.** Do not stop to take photos if it puts you in reach of the person; prioritize movement over documentation.
7. **Contact a specialized support line for a safety plan.**  
   **Call or text VictimConnect at 855-4-VICTIM (855-484-2846) for confidential assistance in navigating stalking or harassment.** If you feel you are in immediate physical danger, dial 911.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide if you want to press charges or file a permanent restraining order right now.
- You do **not** need to block the person on social media immediately if you are currently in their physical presence; wait until you are in a locked, safe location.
- You do **not** need to explain to friends or family why you are late or why you changed your plans until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Feeling "rude" or "awkward" is a common reaction to boundary-crossing, but your safety is more important than a stranger's comfort. Persistence after you have said "no" is a red flag, and your decision to seek help or withhold information is a necessary and protective response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate physical and location-based privacy threats. Long-term stalking or persistent harassment may require ongoing documentation and legal intervention through local law enforcement.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal or professional security advice. Safety laws regarding harassment and stalking vary by state. In any situation where you fear immediate violence, contact 911.
