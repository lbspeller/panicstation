# What to do if… someone insists you consented to sex but you remember saying no (USA)
## Short answer
Get to a safer place before responding to them or trying to sort anything out. If you are in immediate danger, call 911; otherwise, contact RAINN or a local sexual assault support service when you are ready.

## Do not do these things
- Do not meet them privately to discuss what happened.
- Do not argue by text or social media while you feel overwhelmed.
- Do not post details publicly while you are under pressure.
- Do not force yourself to decide today whether to report or what label to use.
- Do not pressure yourself to answer messages immediately.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move to a place where you feel safer, such as a trusted person’s home or another place away from them. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Reduce contact if it is safe to do so.** Mute, block, or stop replying for now. You do not need to debate your memory with them.
3. **Tell one trusted person what you need.** You can keep it simple: “Something happened and I need you to stay with me,” or “I need help getting somewhere safe.”
4. **Contact sexual assault support when you are ready.** RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline is available 24/7. Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, use the online chat from the hotline page, or text **HOPE** to **64673**.
5. **Ask about medical care if you want it.** A hospital or clinic can check for injuries, infections, and pregnancy. RAINN can help you find local care. You can ask whether a sexual assault forensic exam is available without deciding now whether to contact police. Ask the provider whether any reporting rules apply to your circumstances.
6. **If you may want options later, try not to delete messages or other information you already have.** A specialist support service can explain options without pressuring you to report.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to contact police.
- Choosing a final label for what happened.
- Replying to the other person.
- Explaining everything to friends or family.
- Making major relationship decisions, unless distance is needed for immediate safety.

## Important reassurance
Someone else insisting that you consented does not change what you remember saying. Freezing, going quiet, or not fighting back does not by itself mean you agreed. You do not have to work everything out at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: reaching a safer pause, reducing pressure, finding support, and asking about medical care. Later decisions may need specialist support and can be made at your pace.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you feel at risk of self-harm or unable to keep yourself safe, call or text 988, call 911 for an immediate emergency, or go to the nearest emergency department.
