# What to do if… multiple drains start gurgling and your home smells like sewage (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all water use immediately and isolate affected areas. If you see rising water or waste in the lowest drains, treat it as a health hazard and call a licensed plumber or your municipal sewer utility.

## Do not do these things
- Don't run any water, flush toilets, or use the dishwasher to "test" if the clog is clearing.
- Don't use chemical drain cleaners; they are ineffective for main-line blockages and create hazardous chemical splash risks.
- Don't enter any area where standing sewage is present without protective gear.
- Don't touch electrical outlets, panels, or appliances if you are standing on a wet or contaminated floor.
- Don't ignore the odor if it smells like natural gas (rotten eggs + hissing); if a gas leak is suspected, evacuate and call 911 immediately.
- Don't attempt to "snaking" the line yourself if multiple fixtures are affected.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all water usage throughout the entire property immediately.**
   - Ensure no toilets are flushed and no appliances (washers / dishwashers) are running.
2. **Check the lowest floor drains or toilets for visible backup or rising water.**
   - Do not touch the water or any residue found around the drain.
3. **Open windows and exterior doors to ventilate the home.**
   - Keep internal doors to the affected rooms closed to contain the odor.
4. **Isolate the contaminated area from people and pets.**
   - Mark the area as off-limits and prevent anyone from walking through sewage.
5. **Shut off the main water supply valve if you suspect others may accidentally use the plumbing.**
   - This prevents further waste from being added to the system.
6. **Turn off electricity to affected areas at the breaker panel only if you are in a dry location.**
   - If the floor near the panel is wet or damp, do not touch the electrical system; wait for professional assistance.
7. **Identify the correct professional for the situation.**
   - **Licensed Plumber:** Call if you suspect a blockage on your property (tree roots / pipe collapse).
   - **Municipal Sewer Utility:** Call if neighbors are also affected or if sewage is overflowing from a street manhole.
   - **Septic Service Provider:** Call immediately if your property uses a septic system.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the blockage (roots / grease / collapse) right now.
- You do not need to begin deep-cleaning or disinfecting until the backup has been fully stopped and the source is repaired.
- You do not need to inventory damaged items for insurance until the immediate health hazard is stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Gurgling across multiple drains is a standard early warning sign of a system-wide blockage. By stopping water use now, you are likely preventing a significantly more destructive and unsanitary full-scale backup into your living space.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for plumbing emergencies only. Long-term repairs, sanitation procedures, and legal/insurance claims must be handled by qualified specialists.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace professional plumbing or public health advice. Sewage contains harmful bacteria and pathogens; prioritize physical distance and professional intervention.
