# What to do if… a neighbour is threatening you or repeatedly knocking on your door (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside, keep the door locked, and do not step outside to confront the person. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger, believe they may force entry, or need an immediate law-enforcement response.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to talk the dispute through while the person is outside.
- Do not step outside to argue, chase them, block them, or try to detain them.
- Do not threaten, taunt, or escalate through the door.
- Do not move closer to the door or windows to film if that increases risk.
- Do not post your location or live details publicly while the situation is active.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause inside.** Lock doors and accessible windows. Move away from the door and front windows. Keep your phone with you. If other people are in the home, ask them to stay away from the door and front windows if it is safe to do so.
2. **Call 911 for immediate danger.** Call 911 if the person is threatening violence, trying to force entry, damaging doors or windows, or creating an immediate safety risk.
3. **Use the local non-emergency route when the immediate danger has passed.** Contact the local law-enforcement agency and ask how to report the threats or repeated visits. Ask whether an incident number is available.
4. **Keep any response brief.** You do not need to respond. If you choose to say anything, keep it short: “I am not opening the door. Please leave.” Then stop engaging.
5. **Record the basics without increasing risk.** Note the time, what happened, the words used as closely as you can remember, how long it lasted, and any witnesses. Preserve existing doorbell footage, security footage, messages, and voicemails. Do not move closer to the door to collect evidence.
6. **Notify building management if relevant.** If you rent or live in a managed building, report the safety issue and ask how repeated incidents should be logged.
7. **Ask about local options if the behavior repeats.** Contact your local court system or a legal-aid provider and ask whether a restraining order or another civil option may apply in your state. Rules and names vary by location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to move, sue, mediate, or repair the neighbor relationship.
- You do not need to produce a perfect timeline while the person is outside.
- You do not need to decide on longer-term housing or legal steps during the immediate situation.

## Important reassurance
Feeling scared, shaky, or overwhelmed can happen during a threatening confrontation. Keeping the door closed, creating distance, and asking local services about the next step are reasonable first actions.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours only. If threats or repeated visits continue, later decisions may need support from local authorities, your housing provider, a victim-support service, or a legal-aid provider.

## Important note
This is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.
