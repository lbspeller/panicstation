# What to do if… someone claims they are underage after sending explicit images and now demands money or favours (USA)
## Short answer
Stop contact. Do not pay or agree to any favors. Keep a basic record of the threats and account details without downloading, forwarding, or circulating explicit images, then get support from one trusted person if you can.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, more images, or any “one last” favor.
- Do not keep debating the age claim or trying to settle the situation in direct messages.
- Do not download, re-save, forward, or circulate explicit images.
- Do not send explicit images to friends or family to ask what they think.
- Do not panic-call the account, a supposed parent, or anyone else who appears and demands payment.
- Do not delete the threat messages or account details before you have kept a basic record that avoids explicit images.

## What to do now
1. End contact. Keep the username, profile details, dates, threats, and payment demands. Avoid screenshots that capture explicit images where possible. Do not download or re-save explicit files.
2. Report the account and the messages inside the app, site, or platform. Then block the account.
3. If you paid or shared card, bank, or payment-app details, contact the provider promptly. Say you were pressured into an extortion or scam payment and ask them to review the transaction and protect the account.
4. If the person may have access to your account, change the affected account password and your email password, then turn on two-factor authentication. If they may be able to reach your contacts, review your privacy settings.
5. If an intimate image of you was taken when you were under 18 and remains on the device on which it was originally taken, Take It Down may help limit its spread. Do not download an image or ask anyone to send it to you just to use the service. If you do not have the original device, the CyberTipline is another route.
6. If an adult intimate image is of you and you have access to it, StopNCII may help limit its spread.
7. If you decide to report, choose the route that fits the situation. For a child sexual exploitation concern involving a minor, the CyberTipline is available. For cyber-enabled extortion or scam reporting, IC3 is available. For formal law-enforcement help, local police or the FBI may be options. If there is immediate danger, call 911 or local police. You do not need to use every route at once.
8. If you can, tell one trusted person today, even if all you can say is: “I’m being blackmailed online and I need help not responding.”

## What can wait
You do not need to decide right now whether the underage claim is true. You do not need to create a perfect timeline before asking for help. You do not need to choose every reporting option today.

## Important reassurance
These threats are designed to create urgency, shame, and confusion. That panicked feeling does not mean you need to comply. Ending contact and getting one other person involved are strong first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about reporting, image removal, legal questions, account security, or emotional support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Keep a basic record of threats and account details, but avoid downloading, forwarding, or circulating explicit images.
