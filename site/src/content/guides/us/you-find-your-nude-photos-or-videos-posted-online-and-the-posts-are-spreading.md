# What to do if… you find your nude photos or videos posted online and the posts are spreading (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a private location and immediately report the content to the platforms using their specific "non-consensual intimate imagery" (NCII) tools. Contact the Image Abuse Helpline at 844-878-2274 for guided removal assistance under federal takedown laws.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t respond to the person posting, negotiate, or send money (payment rarely stops the spread and often leads to higher demands).
- Don’t click any links the poster sends, especially "verification" or login links.
- Don’t engage publicly with the posts or comments, as this can increase the algorithm's reach and visibility.
- Don’t forward the images to others as "proof" or to ask for help; use links and screenshots only.
- Don’t delete your own accounts or history before you have captured the URLs and account handles needed for reporting.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a private space and check your immediate physical safety.** **If you are being physically stalked or someone is threatening to come to your location, call 911 immediately.** 
2. **Document the minimum information required for a legal takedown.** **Save the specific URLs (links) to the posts, the usernames of the accounts posting them, and take screenshots that show the image/video alongside the platform’s interface.** 
   - Do not download the media files to your device if you do not already have them.
   - Use a secure note-taking app or a physical piece of paper to record links.
3. **Report the content to each platform using their "Non-Consensual Intimate Imagery" (NCII) reporting route.** **Under the TAKE IT DOWN Act (TIDA), covered platforms are federally required to remove authentic or AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery within 48 hours of a valid request.** 
   - Select the option for "intimate content shared without consent" or "image-based abuse."
   - If friends offer to help, ask them to report the post through the platform tool rather than sharing or commenting on it.
4. **Contact the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative (CCRI) Image Abuse Helpline for specialist support.** **Call 844-878-2274 (available 24/7) to speak with a trained representative who can help you navigate platform-specific reporting and legal rights.** 
5. **Use automated hashing tools to prevent the content from being re-uploaded.** **If you are an adult, create a case on StopNCII.org to generate a digital "fingerprint" of the images that participating platforms use to block re-sharing.** 
6. **If the images were taken when you (or anyone in them) were under 18, use the NCMEC Take It Down tool.** **Go to takeitdown.ncmec.org to hash images involving minors; if you suspect child sexual exploitation, report it directly to the NCMEC CyberTipline at 1-800-843-5678.** 
7. **Report active extortion or blackmail to federal authorities.** **If someone is demanding money or more images (sextortion), call 1-800-CALL-FBI (1-800-225-5324) or report the crime at tips.fbi.gov.** 

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to tell your employer, school, or family right this second.
- You do not need to identify the person who originally posted the content before starting the takedown process.
- You do not need to pursue a civil lawsuit or criminal charges immediately; removal is the priority.
- You do not need to find every single instance of the image online in one sitting.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of panic or violation you are experiencing is a natural reaction to a serious breach of privacy. You are the victim of a crime, and you are not responsible for the actions of the person who shared your content. Federal laws and specific technologies now exist to help stop the spread and force platforms to act quickly.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and removal actions. Ongoing legal counsel, digital forensic preservation, and psychological support should be sought once the immediate spread has been addressed.

## Important note
This guide provides procedural information and is not legal advice. The TAKE IT DOWN Act (TIDA) provides specific 48-hour removal rights for adults and minors in the USA. If you are in immediate physical danger, contact local law enforcement via 911.
