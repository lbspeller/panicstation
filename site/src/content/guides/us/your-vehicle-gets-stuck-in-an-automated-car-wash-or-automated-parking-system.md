# What to do if… your vehicle gets stuck in an automated car wash or automated parking system (USA)

## Short answer
Stay in the vehicle unless there is immediate danger such as fire, smoke, flooding, fumes, or staff clearly tell you to exit. Use the nearest **EMERGENCY STOP / HELP / CALL / intercom** control or call the site operator; call **911** if anyone is injured, in danger, or the situation is getting worse.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb out into a car wash tunnel, conveyor track, lift space, stacker bay, or machinery area unless staff or emergency responders tell you to.
- Do not force doors, barriers, gates, lift doors, or moving parts.
- Do not repeatedly brake, steer, shift gears, or restart the vehicle on a conveyor; follow the site’s posted or audio instructions and ask for an operator if unsure.
- Do not try to self-rescue from a car elevator, stacker, robotic parking bay, or vehicle lift.
- Do not rev the engine or try to push through a barrier, brush, gate, or system fault.
- Do not stand close to the vehicle if machinery may restart.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate danger.**
   - If there is fire, smoke, strong fumes, flooding, injury, chest pain, breathing trouble, or the vehicle is being pushed into a collision, call **911**.
   - If you can safely speak to staff first, tell them to stop the system immediately.

2. **Stabilise the vehicle.**
   - Turn on hazard lights.
   - Keep hands, arms, children, pets, and loose items inside the vehicle.
   - Keep clear of doors, windows, brushes, tracks, barriers, and lift gaps.
   - Follow posted or audio instructions for the vehicle, such as neutral, car wash mode, engine off, or ignition on. If the instructions conflict with your vehicle or you are unsure, stop changing settings and get an operator.

3. **Use the site’s stop or help route.**
   - Look for **EMERGENCY STOP**, **STOP**, **HELP**, **CALL**, an intercom, or a call box at the entrance, exit, pay station, barrier pedestal, wall panel, or parking lobby.
   - Press the help or call button and say you are inside the stuck vehicle.
   - If you cannot safely reach a control, use short horn beeps, phone the site number from a receipt, payment screen, entrance sign, posted wall sign, or map listing, or call a nearby person to alert staff.

4. **Give a clear location.**
   - Say: “My vehicle is stuck in the car wash tunnel, wash bay, barrier lane, car elevator, or automated parking bay. Please stop the equipment and tell me whether to stay in the vehicle.”
   - Give the site name or address, bay number, level, lane, nearest sign, and your vehicle colour and model.

5. **If you are in an automated parking bay, car elevator, stacker, or lift space, wait for trained help.**
   - Stay seated unless there is immediate danger.
   - Do not squeeze through partly open doors or gaps.
   - Ask the operator to confirm the system is stopped and secured before anyone approaches the vehicle.

6. **If you are stuck behind a parking gate or barrier, avoid forcing it.**
   - Put the vehicle in park if fully stopped and safe to do so.
   - Use the call button, ticket machine help button, posted phone number, or building security desk.
   - Keep other vehicles back if you can do so safely, using hazard lights and short horn beeps.

7. **Make a short record after the situation is stable.**
   - Note the time, location, lane or bay, what happened, and who you spoke to.
   - Take a few photos or a short video only if the vehicle is stationary and it is safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide fault while you are still stuck.
- You do not need to argue with staff, other drivers, or the property owner now.
- You do not need to call insurance immediately unless there is injury, obvious damage, towing, or medical care.
- You do not need to troubleshoot the machinery or prove what went wrong.

## Important reassurance
A stuck car wash, barrier, or automated parking system can feel frightening because you cannot move normally. The safest first steps are usually simple: stop or pause the equipment, reach a human operator, stay out of machinery areas, and call emergency services if danger is present.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce risk and get safely released. Later decisions about damage, towing, complaints, insurance, or legal responsibility may need specialist help after you are out and safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, mechanical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow posted on-site safety instructions, staff directions, and emergency responder instructions. If there is any threat to life, injury, fire, smoke, fumes, medical distress, or worsening entrapment, call **911**.
