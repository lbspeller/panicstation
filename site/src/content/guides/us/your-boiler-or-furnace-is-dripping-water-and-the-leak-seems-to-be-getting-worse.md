# What to do if… your boiler or furnace is dripping water and the leak seems to be getting worse (USA)

## Short answer
Shut off the system power and avoid contact with standing water near electrical components, then contact a licensed HVAC professional or plumber.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the furnace, service switch, or circuit breaker if you are wet or standing in water.
- Do not leave the system running to "see if it clears up" as water can cause permanent electrical damage.
- Do not close water supply valves to a boiler unless the power is already completely shut off.
- Do not attempt to open sealed combustion chambers or internal panels.
- Do not ignore the smell of sulfur or rotten eggs, which indicates a gas leak.
- Do not use a phone or flip light switches if you suspect a gas leak.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize safety if you smell gas or hear a carbon monoxide alarm.**
   - **Evacuate the building immediately** and move to a safe distance outside.
   - **Do not** use any electronics, flashlights, or appliances while inside the building.
   - **Call 911** or your local gas utility emergency line from your outdoor location.
2. **De-energize the system to prevent electrical shock.**
   - **Set the thermostat to the "OFF" position** if it is in a dry area.
   - **Flip the furnace service switch** (usually a toggle switch on the side of the unit or nearby wall) only if you are standing on a dry surface.
   - **Shut off the HVAC circuit breaker** at the main electrical panel if you cannot safely reach the unit switch.
3. **Isolate the water source if it is safe and obvious.**
   - **Turn off the water valve to the humidifier** if the leak is coming from the small water line attached to the furnace.
   - **Close the main water shut-off valve** for the house only if the leak is substantial and the boiler/furnace power is already off.
4. **Contain the water to prevent further property damage.**
   - **Place a bucket or shallow pan** under the visible drip point.
   - **Use towels or a mop** to clear water away from walls, electrical outlets, or stored items.
   - **Avoid skin contact** with furnace condensate water, as it can be acidic and irritating to the skin.
5. **Contact professional help and notify relevant parties.**
   - **Call a licensed HVAC technician** or plumber and state clearly that there is an "active, worsening water leak near a heating system."
   - **Notify your landlord or property manager** immediately if you are a renter.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify if the leak is from a condensate pump, a cracked heat exchanger, or a pressure relief valve before calling for help.
- You do not need to clean up the interior of the unit; a technician must inspect for internal damage.
- You do not need to decide on a system replacement or major repair during the initial emergency call.

## Important reassurance
Water leaks from heating systems are common maintenance issues often caused by clogged drains or failed valves. By shutting off the power and containing the water, you have significantly reduced the risk of electrical failure or structural damage.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. A licensed professional is required to diagnose the source of the leak and ensure the system is safe to operate.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not constitute professional advice. If water is in contact with electrical systems or you suspect a gas/CO leak, treat the situation as a life-safety emergency and evacuate.
