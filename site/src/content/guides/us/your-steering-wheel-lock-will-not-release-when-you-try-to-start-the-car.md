# What to do if… your steering wheel lock will not release when you try to start the car (USA)

## Short answer
Relieve the mechanical tension on the lock by applying steady pressure to the steering wheel in the direction it has slight movement while simultaneously turning the key or cycling the ignition button. Do not use significant force, as this can snap the key or damage the internal locking pin.

## Do not do these things
- Don't jerk, yank, or bounce the steering wheel with high force.
- Don't use tools like pliers to gain extra leverage on the ignition key.
- Don't repeatedly mash the Start button if the vehicle does not respond.
- Don't attempt to disassemble the steering column or ignition housing at the roadside.
- Don't remain in the vehicle if it is stopped in a high-traffic or unsafe lane of travel.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle and assess your surroundings.**
   - **Activate hazard lights immediately.**
   - **Verify the gear selector is fully in Park (automatic) or Neutral (manual).**
   - **Determine if your location is a safety hazard; if you are in a live lane or at risk of being struck, exit the vehicle when safe and call 911.**

2. **Perform the "Pressure and Turn" release for keyed ignitions.**
   - **Insert the key fully into the ignition cylinder.**
   - **Gently turn the steering wheel left and right to identify which direction has a few millimeters of "play."**
   - **Apply and hold steady pressure in that direction (away from the lock pin).**
   - **Attempt to turn the ignition key toward the ON position while maintaining the wheel pressure.**
   - **Repeat with a spare key if the primary key fails to turn after three attempts.**

3. **Perform the release for push-button start vehicles.**
   - **Ensure your foot is OFF the brake pedal.**
   - **Turn the steering wheel gently to the left or right to find the side with movement.**
   - **Hold the wheel in that position and press the Engine Start/Stop button once to enter Accessory (ACC) mode.**
   - **If the lock remains engaged, press the button again to enter the ON mode while maintaining wheel pressure.**

4. **Address potential electronic or fob interference.**
   - **Place the key fob directly against the Start button or in the vehicle's designated backup slot (often in the center console or on the steering column).**
   - **Check the dashboard for "Key Not Detected" or "Steering Lock Fault" messages.**
   - **Observe interior lights; if they are dim or flickering, the electronic lock may not release due to a low 12V battery.**

5. **Reset the transmission interlock.**
   - **Firmly press the brake pedal.**
   - **Move the shifter out of Park and back into Park, ensuring it clicks into place.**
   - **Retry the steering wheel release steps listed in Step 2 or 3.**

6. **Escalate to professional assistance.**
   - **Contact a roadside assistance provider or an automotive locksmith if the lock does not release after five minutes of calm attempts.**
   - **Specify to the dispatcher if the vehicle has an "Electronic Steering Column Lock" (ESCL) or a traditional mechanical lock.**
   - **Request a tow to a repair facility if a locksmith cannot resolve the issue on-site.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the binding (e.g., worn tumblers vs. lock pin failure) right now.
- You do not need to replace the 12V battery immediately unless you have confirmed it is dead and a jump-start is available.
- You do not need to notify your insurance company unless the vehicle requires a tow covered by your policy.

## Important reassurance
Steering wheel locks often jam when the wheels are turned sharply against a curb or when the wheel is moved after the engine is turned off. This creates mechanical tension that prevents the lock from retracting. In most cases, finding the "sweet spot" of pressure on the wheel allows the mechanism to click open without any actual mechanical failure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a vehicle that will not start due to a locked steering column. It does not cover the repair of ignition switches, immobilizer programming, or power steering pump failures.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and harm reduction and does not replace the specific procedures found in your vehicle's owner's manual. Mechanical or electronic steering lock failures may require professional diagnostic equipment. If you are stranded in a location that poses a risk to your physical safety, prioritize reaching a secure area and contact emergency services or a dedicated roadside professional.
