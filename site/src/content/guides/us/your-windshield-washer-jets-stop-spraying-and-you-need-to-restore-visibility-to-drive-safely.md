# What to do if… your windshield washer jets stop spraying and you need to restore visibility to drive safely (USA)

## Short answer
Do not re-enter traffic until you can see clearly through the windshield. If you cannot quickly restore safe visibility from a safe place, call for roadside help and stay in the safest available spot.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive on with a smeared or dirty windshield hoping it clears.
- Don’t keep running wipers on a dry or gritty windshield; it can make the smear worse and may scratch the glass.
- Don’t troubleshoot in an active lane or where fast traffic is close.
- Don’t stand on the traffic side of the vehicle to check nozzles, open the hood, or add fluid.
- Don’t pour plain water into the washer reservoir in freezing conditions if you can avoid it; it may freeze and leave you with the same problem again.
- Don’t let pressure from traffic behind you rush the decision to move before you can see safely.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the stop first.**
   - Keep your hazard flashers on.
   - If it is dark, raining, foggy, dusty, or snowy, turn on your lights so other drivers can see you.
   - If you are on a high-speed shoulder or traffic is close, it is generally safer to stay inside with your seat belt on until help arrives, unless there is a more immediate danger such as fire, smoke, or impact risk.
   - Follow police, highway patrol, or roadside-assistance instructions if they are present.

2. **Make the visibility call before moving.**
   - Look through the windshield from the driver’s seat.
   - If you cannot clearly judge lane lines, vehicles, signs, pedestrians, the shoulder, and glare, treat the vehicle as not safe to drive yet.

3. **Try the quickest inside-the-car fixes.**
   - Turn on the defroster or defogger.
   - Use air conditioning if available, because it can help dry the air inside the vehicle.
   - If you have a clean cloth or paper towel within reach, wipe the inside of the windshield where haze has built up.

4. **Move only if you can do so safely.**
   - If you are already in a parking lot, rest area, gas station, driveway, or fully separated pull-off, stay there while you check.
   - If you are on a shoulder and cannot see well enough to merge safely, do not try to reach a better place by driving blind.

5. **Check washer fluid only from a safe place.**
   - If you can get out away from traffic, check the washer reservoir.
   - If it is low or empty and you have windshield washer fluid, top it up.
   - Use washer fluid rated for the weather if freezing temperatures are possible.

6. **Listen briefly for the washer pump.**
   - From inside the vehicle, activate the washers once or twice and listen.
   - Pump sound but no spray may mean an empty reservoir, frozen fluid, clogged nozzle, or disconnected line.
   - No pump sound may mean a fuse, pump, switch, or electrical problem.
   - Stop testing if repeated attempts do not restore spray.

7. **If freezing is likely, keep it simple.**
   - If temperatures are near or below freezing, frozen fluid or frozen nozzles may not clear at the roadside.
   - Keep the cabin warm and the defroster on.
   - Do not keep cycling the washer pump if nothing is spraying.

8. **If you are safely parked, clear only what is obvious.**
   - Wipe dirt, ice, or slush from the washer nozzle area if you can reach it safely.
   - If a nozzle opening is visibly blocked, a very gentle clear with a fine object may help, but stop if there is resistance or any risk of damaging or misaligning the nozzle.
   - Do not work under the hood or around the front of the vehicle where passing traffic is close.

9. **Get help if clear visibility is not restored.**
   - Call your roadside assistance provider, insurer roadside line, auto club, or a local tow service.
   - If you are in a hazardous location, traffic is a threat, there has been a crash, or someone may be in danger, call 911.
   - In many areas you may be able to dial 511 for traveler information or road-service patrol information; availability and services vary by state and road.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical fault right now.
- You do not need to decide repair costs, parts, warranty, or recall questions while you are still roadside.
- You do not need to fully clean the washer system now.
- You do not need to continue the trip until the windshield can stay clear enough for safe driving.

## Important reassurance
Washer spray can stop for ordinary reasons such as low fluid, frozen fluid, a clogged nozzle, or a pump problem. Pulling over to reassess visibility was a protective decision, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate crash risk and buy time. Later decisions about repair, towing, insurance, or mechanical inspection may need help from a qualified mechanic, roadside-assistance provider, insurer, or local authority.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. If you cannot keep a clear view of the road, do not drive until you have restored visibility or received appropriate help.
