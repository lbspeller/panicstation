# What to do if… you lose your driving licence abroad and need it for car hire or travel plans (USA)

## Short answer
Do not assume you can rent a car or drive today without your physical U.S. driver’s license. Call the rental location directly, ask exactly what they will accept, and start a replacement request with your home state motor vehicle agency.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive if local law, the rental company, or your insurance requires the physical license and you do not have it.
- Do not assume a photo, scan, temporary printout, or digital license will be accepted abroad or at the rental counter.
- Do not buy an “international driver’s license” from a random website; for a U.S. license holder, use only the official issuers currently listed by U.S. government sources: AAA or AATA.
- Do not send passport scans, license scans, or payment details to strangers, unofficial sellers, or unverified email addresses.
- Do not cancel the whole trip or abandon prepaid travel until you have checked the rental company, your home state motor vehicle agency, and no-car transport options.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a secure pause and re-check once.**  
   Check pockets, bags, luggage linings, the hotel safe, reception desk, taxis, rideshares, restaurants, and any venue you just left. Ask the hotel or venue whether anything was turned in.

2. **If your wallet or license may have been stolen, ask local police how to make a report.**  
   A report or incident number may help with insurance, card disputes, or explaining the loss to a rental company, but do not let this trap you in a long process if you are unsafe or need to move.

3. **Call the exact rental car branch, not only the booking website.**  
   Ask:
   - “Do you require the physical U.S. driver’s license to release the car?”
   - “Will you accept any alternative, such as a passport plus another ID?”
   - “Can I add another licensed driver, delay pickup, move the reservation, or cancel without extra penalty because the license is lost?”
   Get the answer by email, text, app message, or written note if you can.

4. **Contact your home state motor vehicle agency.**  
   Replacement rules vary by state. Many states let you request a replacement license or state ID online, but the replacement may be mailed to the address on file or another approved U.S. address. A temporary printout or digital credential may not help abroad unless local law and the rental branch confirm they will accept it.

5. **If you also lost an International Driving Permit, do not treat it as a replacement license.**  
   An IDP is usually used with your valid U.S. driver’s license, not instead of it. If you need another IDP, use AAA or AATA and expect a new application or mailed document rather than an instant duplicate.

6. **Make a no-car plan for the next 24 hours.**  
   Book a train, coach, airport transfer, taxi, rideshare, ferry, or an extra night where you are. The aim is to avoid being forced into unsafe or unlawful driving.

7. **For onward travel ID checks, use your passport first.**  
   A driver’s license is not a passport and is not a substitute for one at international borders. For domestic flights, hotels, ferries, or trains in the country you are in, contact the carrier or provider and ask what they will accept.

8. **If you were robbed, feel unsafe, or also lost your passport, contact U.S. consular help.**  
   A U.S. embassy or consulate cannot issue a state driver’s license, but it can help U.S. citizens abroad with emergencies, crime victim support, lost or stolen passports, and return-travel problems.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve the perfect long-term replacement today; first decide whether you can move safely without driving.
- You do not need to replace every document unless something else is missing, such as your passport, cards, phone, or state ID.
- You do not need to decide today whether to change the whole trip if a one-day transport change buys you time.
- You do not need to argue with the rental desk once they have given a clear answer; ask for the answer in writing and move to the next option.

## Important reassurance
This can feel like the whole trip has collapsed, but the immediate problem is narrower: whether you can legally drive or collect that rental car today. Once that is clear, the next safe step is usually replacement through your state agency and temporary no-car transport.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent unsafe driving, keep you moving, and start replacement. Later decisions about insurance claims, fraud monitoring, charge disputes, identity recovery, or major trip changes may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, insurance, security, or professional advice. Requirements vary by country, U.S. state, rental company, carrier, insurer, and local police process. If you are unsure whether you are allowed to drive, treat that as “do not drive” until you have confirmed it with an official local source or the rental company.
