# What to do if… your powered wheelchair or mobility scooter control panel is on but not responding (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a flat, safe location if possible, then perform a power-cycle reset while ensuring the joystick or throttle is in the center position. Check that the freewheel levers are in "drive" and the charger is unplugged before calling your service provider.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to engage freewheel or manual mode while on an incline; the device may roll uncontrollably without motor braking.
- Don’t keep toggling the power or pushing the joystick repeatedly if the device does not respond; this can escalate electronic faults.
- Don’t try to inspect or repair internal wiring, battery terminals, or the controller housing while outdoors or in wet conditions.
- Don’t assume the device is broken until you have ruled out "Lock Mode" or "Freewheel" interlocks.
- Don’t stay in a lane of traffic or a dangerous intersection while troubleshooting; prioritize reaching the nearest safe pause.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.**  
   If you are in a roadway, crosswalk, or parking lane, ask a bystander to help push you to a flat, stable surface like a sidewalk or lobby.

2. **Check for "Lock Mode" visual cues.**  
   Look at the battery gauge or lights on the controller.
   - If the lights are "cycling" (moving back and forth in a sequence) or flashing in a specific pattern, the controller is likely electronically locked.

3. **Perform a neutral-start reset.**  
   Turn the power off. Ensure your hands and clothing are completely clear of the joystick or throttle levers. Turn the power back on and wait 5 seconds without touching any controls to allow the system to calibrate the "neutral" center position.

4. **Attempt to unlock the controller.**  
   If the lights continue to cycle or the unit beeps, try the standard USA unlock sequence:
   - Turn the power on.
   - Push the joystick forward until it beeps.
   - Pull the joystick backward until it beeps.
   - Release the joystick; the lights should stop cycling and turn solid.

5. **Verify the freewheel / manual levers are engaged.**  
   Locate the levers (usually yellow or red, near the motors or rear tires).
   - **Turn the power off first.**
   - Ensure both levers are clicked firmly into the "Drive" or "Engaged" position.
   - Turn the power back on; many devices will refuse to move if the levers were in freewheel when the unit was powered up.

6. **Check the charging interlock.**  
   Ensure the battery charger cable is completely removed from the port on the joystick or the base of the scooter. Most USA models disable the drive function if the charging plug is even partially inserted.

7. **Ensure the seat is locked (Scooters only).**  
   If you are on a mobility scooter, pull the seat rotation lever and ensure the seat is clicked into a forward-facing, locked position. Some models have a sensor that prevents driving if the seat is swiveled.

8. **Record any error signals.**  
   Count the number of beeps or the number of flashing bars on the battery gauge. Write this down or record it on your phone to give to the technician.

9. **Contact your DME service provider.**  
   Look for a service sticker on the frame of the chair or the back of the controller. Call the provider and report the specific error code (beeps/flashes). If you are stranded and in immediate physical danger, call **911**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the batteries need replacement right now.
- You do not need to look up the specific meaning of the error code if you can reach a technician.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of repairs while you are still in an unsecure location.

## Important reassurance
An "on but not responding" state is frequently a safety lockout designed to protect you, such as the joystick being bumped during startup or a lever being left in push-mode. It is normal to feel a sudden sense of helplessness when a mobility device stops, but following the neutral reset and lockout checks often resolves the issue without tools.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and common user-clearable lockouts. It does not cover internal electrical repairs, motor replacement, or battery diagnostics.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and basic troubleshooting of common mobility device interlocks. It is not a substitute for the manufacturer's manual or professional repair. Always follow the specific safety labels on your device. If you are experiencing a medical crisis or are stranded in extreme weather, contact emergency services immediately.
