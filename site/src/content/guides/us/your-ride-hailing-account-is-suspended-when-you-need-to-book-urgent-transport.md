# What to do if… your ride-hailing account is suspended when you need to book urgent transport (USA)

## Short answer
Get a safe backup ride first: taxi, public transit, a trusted person, clinic help, or emergency services if there is immediate danger. Once you are somewhere safe or your urgent transport is arranged, use the ride-hailing company’s official support route.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep creating new accounts or switching phone numbers to force a booking.
- Don’t repeatedly retry payments, add cards, or remove cards in a loop.
- Don’t accept an off-app ride from a stranger who approaches because you look stuck.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, full payment details, or ID images with anyone who contacts you first outside the official app or help site.
- Don’t spend the next hour trying to prove what happened before arranging a backup ride.

## What to do now
1. **Decide whether this is an emergency.**
   - If someone needs immediate police, fire, or ambulance help, call **911** now.
   - If it is not an emergency but you need local help finding transport or community resources, call **211** or use the 211 website for your area.

2. **Choose one backup transport route and act on it now.**
   - Call a local taxi company directly and ask for the pickup time, approximate fare, and whether they take your payment method.
   - Use public transit for the first safe leg if it gets you closer to a staffed or well-lit place.
   - Ask a trusted person to pick you up, call a taxi for you, or stay on the phone while you arrange the ride.

3. **If the trip is for medical care, ask the appointment site for immediate options.**
   - Call the clinic, hospital, pharmacy, or appointment office and say: “My ride-hailing account was suspended and I may be late. Can you help me with transport options or tell me the safest next step?”
   - If you have Medicaid or a managed care plan, call the member services number and ask: “Do I have non-emergency medical transportation, and is any same-day ride option available?”
   - If you cannot get there on time, ask the office whether you should still come late, switch to telehealth, reschedule, or go somewhere else.

4. **Move to a safer waiting place if you are exposed or stranded.**
   - Go inside a staffed, well-lit place such as a hotel lobby, clinic, station desk, library, shop, or café.
   - Ask staff to help you call a taxi if your phone battery, signal, or payment app is failing.
   - Keep your phone charged enough for calls, location sharing, and support messages.

5. **Save the basic account information without getting stuck on it.**
   - Screenshot the suspension, deactivation, lockout, payment, or verification message.
   - Note the time, city, pickup area, and any recent changes such as a new phone, new number, travel, new card, failed payment, or repeated booking attempts.
   - Keep this short; the goal is to preserve enough detail for support, not to solve the whole account problem now.

6. **Use the official app or help-site route for the locked account.**
   - In Uber or Lyft, look for the account notice, Help, Contact Support, Appeal, payment, or sign-in option shown inside the app.
   - If you cannot access the app, use the official help site for that company and choose the closest rider account, sign-in, payment, suspended account, or deactivated account topic.
   - Write one short factual message: “My rider account was suspended or locked at [time] in [city]. I need to arrange urgent transport. Please tell me the exact steps needed to restore access or verify the account.”

7. **If the message points to payment or device trouble, do one controlled troubleshooting pass.**
   - Update or reinstall the app if the official help page suggests it.
   - Try logging in on another device or through the web help page.
   - Add an alternate payment method or remove and re-add a card only once if the provider’s help steps say to do that.
   - Stop repeating attempts if the lockout continues, and wait for the official support response.

## What can wait
- You do not need to know the exact reason for the suspension before arranging transport.
- You do not need to write a long appeal or gather many documents right now.
- You do not need to decide today whether to stop using that ride-hailing service.
- You do not need to argue with support while you are still stranded; getting to a safer place comes first.

## Important reassurance
A sudden account lock, payment block, or deactivation message is stressful, but it does not by itself prove that you did anything wrong. Using a taxi, public transit, clinic help, 211, or a trusted person as a bridge is a normal way to keep the urgent problem small while the account issue is reviewed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to help you get safe transport and avoid panic mistakes. Restoring the account may require the ride-hailing company’s support process, payment checks, identity verification, or other account-specific steps.
