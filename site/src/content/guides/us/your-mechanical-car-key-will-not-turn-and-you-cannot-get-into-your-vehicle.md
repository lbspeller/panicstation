# What to do if… your mechanical car key will not turn and you cannot get into your vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer place and stop forcing the key. Try the low-damage checks below, then contact your roadside-assistance provider or an auto locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force the key if it resists, bends, or feels as though it may snap.
- Do not try improvised entry methods with hangers, wedges, or other tools. They can damage the vehicle or create a safety problem.
- Do not pour hot water onto a frozen lock or door, and do not heat the key with a lighter or other open flame.
- Do not pry at a concealed keyhole cover unless your owner’s manual shows how it opens.
- Do not leave your phone, keys, or wallet on the ground or on the vehicle roof.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer place first.**  
   Stay out of traffic. Move into a well-lit public place if one is nearby. Keep your phone and keys with you. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

2. **Check the other normal entry points.**  
   Try the other doors and the trunk or hatch. Stop if any lock or handle resists.

3. **Try a spare key if one is available.**  
   If the spare key works, stop using the original key until it can be checked.

4. **Check your owner’s manual or manufacturer app.**  
   Some vehicles have a removable mechanical key inside the fob or a concealed keyhole. Follow the instructions for your specific vehicle. Do not pry at trim if the correct method is not clear.

5. **If the lock may be frozen, use a gentle approach.**  
   If you already have a commercial lock de-icer, use it only as directed on its label. Stop if the key or lock still resists.

6. **Try one gentle re-seat attempt.**  
   Remove the key, insert it normally, and try a light turn without force. Stop if it still does not turn.

7. **Contact help early.**  
   Ask your roadside-assistance provider whether your plan includes vehicle-lockout service. Otherwise, contact an auto locksmith. Ask about entry options, identification requirements, fees, and what happens if the vehicle cannot be opened at the scene.

8. **Treat a child alone in a locked vehicle as an emergency.**  
   Call **911** immediately. If any other person or animal inside appears to be in immediate danger or distress, call **911**.

9. **Wait somewhere safer if you can.**  
   A nearby store, gas station, hotel lobby, or other public indoor place may be a safer place to wait while help is arranged.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact lock fault right now.
- You do not need to decide about a replacement key, lock repair, or an insurance claim while you are outside the vehicle.
- You do not need to attempt a clever entry method. The priority is safe access without forcing the lock.

## Important reassurance
A stuck mechanical lock can have several causes. Stopping before the key bends or breaks keeps the next step simpler.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. After you regain access, a locksmith, roadside provider, dealer, or repair shop may need to check the key and lock.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, emergency-response, or other professional advice.
