# What to do if… you suspect someone is trying to groom you by escalating intimacy very fast and discouraging outside support (USA)

## Short answer
Slow all communication down and contact a confidential support service or one trusted person immediately. Pressure for rapid commitment, isolation from friends, or keeping the relationship secret are indicators that you should create distance and seek outside perspective.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for proof or "certainty" before reaching out for support.
- Do not agree to delete messages, hide accounts, or use disappearing chat modes.
- Do not meet the person in private or go to a location they control.
- Do not share your live location, copies of ID, or financial information.
- Do not provide sexual images or engage in sexual video calls under pressure.
- Do not confront the person with your suspicions, as this may trigger escalation or threats.
- Do not let them become your only source of transportation, money, or housing.

## What to do now
1. **Tell one person outside the situation today.** State clearly that you are uncomfortable with the speed of the relationship and the pressure for secrecy.
2. **Contact a confidential hotline for a pattern check.** Use a safe device to contact a specialist service.
   - For sexual pressure or grooming: Contact RAINN at 800-656-HOPE.
   - For relationship control or isolation: Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 800-799-SAFE or text "START" to 88788.
3. **Set your digital boundaries to private.** Turn off "Find My" or other location-sharing apps, change your social media privacy to "Friends Only," and update passwords on any shared accounts.
4. **Pause all private meetups.** Decline requests to meet in person, especially in private homes or vehicles. If you must meet, choose a busy public space and tell someone where you are.
5. **Document specific behaviors.** Keep a simple log of dates and actions, such as "Pressured me to stop talking to my sister" or "Asked for nudes after knowing me for two days." Save this where the other person cannot see it.
6. **If you are under 18, speak to a safe adult now.** This includes a teacher, school counselor, coach, or relative. If the situation involves online enticement, you or an adult can report it to the CyberTipline at 1-800-843-5678.
7. **Secure your devices.** Ensure they cannot access your phone or computer remotely. Check for linked devices in your messaging app settings (e.g., WhatsApp Web or Apple ID).
8. **Establish a check-in protocol.** Tell a friend "If I don't text you by 6:00 PM, please call me."
9. **Keep original evidence.** Save screenshots of messages where they pressure you for sex, money, or secrecy. Do not delete them even if you feel ashamed; they are your record of the pattern.
10. **Call 911 if you are being followed or threatened.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you are "breaking up" permanently right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report today.
- You do not need to explain your distance to the other person.
- You do not need to have a perfect "story" before calling a hotline.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel confused or guilty when someone alternates between extreme affection and controlling behavior. This confusion is a common result of rapid escalation and isolation. Taking a step back is a valid way to protect your safety and does not mean you have failed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for identifying and interrupting potential grooming or coercive control. It does not replace legal advice or long-term therapeutic support.

## Important note
Hotline advocates are available to help you assess your situation without requiring you to make a police report. If you are a minor, be aware that many professionals (like teachers or doctors) are mandatory reporters if they suspect child abuse or exploitation.
