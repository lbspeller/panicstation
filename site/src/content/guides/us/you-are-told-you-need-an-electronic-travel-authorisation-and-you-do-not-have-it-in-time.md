# What to do if… you are told you need an electronic travel authorisation and you do not have it in time (USA)

## Short answer
Confirm the exact missing authorization using official government information for your destination and any transit points. If this is ESTA for eligible Visa Waiver Program travel to the U.S., submit one accurate application on the official ESTA website and ask the airline about rebooking if approval has not arrived before check-in closes.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use an unofficial ESTA helper site that charges extra or asks for unnecessary personal information.
- Do not assume an airline can override a missing ESTA approval at the gate.
- Do not submit a second ESTA application just to try different answers.
- Do not enter passport details from a different, expired, or replaced passport.
- Do not buy a new non-refundable ticket before asking the airline about changing the current booking.

## What to do now
1. **Ask what is missing.** Ask the airline or booking agent: “Which authorization is required for this itinerary and this passport?” Save a screenshot of any check-in message.
2. **Check the official rule.** Use official government information for the destination and any transit points. If the missing authorization is U.S. ESTA, check whether your nationality, passport, trip purpose, and planned stay fit the Visa Waiver Program requirements. Visa Waiver Program travellers need an e-passport.
3. **Apply once on the official ESTA website if you are eligible.** Enter the passport details carefully and save the application number. Use the official website to check the status. It may take up to 72 hours to find out whether you are authorized to travel under the Visa Waiver Program.
4. **Check any passport problem before submitting again.** If you have received a new passport, submit a new ESTA application using that passport. If details in an existing application are wrong, use the official ESTA help page before starting another application.
5. **Ask the airline about the booking deadline.** Say: “My ESTA status is not approved yet. What is the least expensive way to move this booking if approval does not arrive before check-in closes?”
6. **Use official visa information if ESTA is not suitable.** If you are not eligible for the Visa Waiver Program, or your trip does not fit its requirements, check the official visa information for your intended travel purpose and ask the airline about rebooking. For temporary tourism or business travel, the visitor-visa page is a starting point.
7. **Check transit requirements before paying for a replacement itinerary.** Use official government information for each country where you will change planes, and confirm that any authorization is connected to the passport you will present.

## What can wait
- You do not need to argue at the gate. Focus on the next workable departure.
- You do not need to work out every reason for the problem immediately. Confirm the requirement, use the correct passport details, and check the official application status first.
- You do not need to make an insurance claim or complaint now. Save screenshots, receipts, and booking messages for later.

## Important reassurance
A missing or pending travel authorization can disrupt a departure, but you can narrow the problem down. Check the official requirement, take one accurate step, and ask the airline about the next workable booking option.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. More complicated eligibility, passport, visa, or urgent-travel questions may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Requirements can vary by nationality, passport, itinerary, and travel purpose, and they can change. For travel under the U.S. Visa Waiver Program by air or sea, valid ESTA approval is required before boarding a U.S.-bound carrier. ESTA approval allows you to travel to a U.S. port of entry and request admission; it does not guarantee entry.
