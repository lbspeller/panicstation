# What to do if… you report bullying and are immediately moved teams or taken off duties without explanation (USA)

## Short answer
Follow the instructions of security personnel immediately, then move to a safe location and send a timestamped message to your manager and HR confirming your arrival and availability to work.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to force entry, "tailgate" behind a colleague, or use a side door that you are not authorized to use.
- Do not engage in a verbal confrontation with security staff, as they are typically following automated system prompts or specific orders.
- Do not leave the vicinity without notifying your manager in writing, as this can be classified as job abandonment or an unauthorized absence.
- Do not disclose passwords, hand over personal devices, or sign any documents while standing outside the building.
- Do not use social media or public forums to describe the situation while it is unfolding.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a sheltered and safe location while remaining immediately reachable by phone or email.**  
   If the weather is extreme or the immediate area feels unsafe, inform security or the receptionist that you are moving to a specific nearby location (such as a lobby or cafe) where you will wait for further instructions.

2. **Note the specific behavior of the badge reader and the response from security.**  
   Observe whether the reader displays a red light, no light, or a specific error message, and write down the exact time and the names of any staff you spoke with.

3. **Send a formal, factual message to your manager and HR representative immediately.**  
   State clearly that you arrived for your scheduled shift at a specific time, your badge failed to grant entry, and you are currently waiting at a specific location as instructed.
   - Ask for confirmation of whether you should remain on-site or return home.
   - Ask for the name of the designated point of contact for this issue.
   - State that you are ready and available to work.

4. **Request a written record or ticket number for the access issue.**  
   If security claims it is a technical fault, ask for an IT or security ticket number; if they claim it is a management instruction, ask for the name and title of the person who issued the directive.

5. **Clarify how your time will be recorded for today.**  
   Ask HR in writing if you should "clock in" remotely or how the hours spent waiting will be accounted for in the payroll system.
   - If you are a non-exempt (hourly) employee, note that federal law generally only requires pay for hours actually worked, though specific states like California or New York may require "reporting time pay" if you show up and are sent home.
   - If you are an exempt (salaried) employee, note that federal FLSA rules generally prohibit deductions from your weekly salary for partial-day absences caused by the employer's operating requirements.

6. **Ask for an escorted retrieval if you have essential personal items inside the building.**  
   Request that a security officer or manager accompany you to retrieve necessary items such as house keys, car keys, wallet, or prescribed medication.

7. **Invoke your right to representation if you are in a union and believe an investigation is starting.**  
   If a manager or HR representative arrives to interview you and you reasonably believe the discussion could lead to discipline, you must clearly state: "If this discussion could in any way lead to my being disciplined or terminated, I respectfully request that my union representative be present."

8. **Document every interaction in a private log immediately after it occurs.**  
   Record the "who, what, when, and where" of every conversation, including phone calls and in-person statements, while the details are fresh.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the underlying cause of the deactivation (technical vs. disciplinary) before receiving official word from HR.
- You do not need to decide whether to resign or pursue legal action while standing at the entrance.
- You do not need to contact your coworkers to explain why you are outside or to ask them for information.
- You do not need to sign any separation or disciplinary agreements on the spot; you have the right to take these documents home to review.

## Important reassurance
A badge failure is often a technical synchronization error, an expired certification, or an administrative oversight. Security personnel are usually operating with very limited information and are not the decision-makers regarding your employment status. Taking an organized, documented approach now ensures that your professional conduct remains unquestionable regardless of the outcome.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes and hours following a building lockout. It does not cover long-term employment litigation, grievance filing procedures, or state-specific unemployment applications.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment laws, including the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and National Labor Relations Act (NLRA), are subject to specific interpretations based on your state, your contract, and current federal rulings. If you are placed on administrative leave or terminated, consult an employment attorney or your union representative to discuss your specific rights.
