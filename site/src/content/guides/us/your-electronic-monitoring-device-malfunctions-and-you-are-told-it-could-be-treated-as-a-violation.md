# What to do if… your electronic monitoring device malfunctions and you are told it could be treated as a violation (USA)

## Short answer
Report the problem as soon as possible using the contact route in your paperwork or monitoring app. Keep following your current written conditions, do not alter the equipment unless instructed, and make a timestamped record of the alert and your contact attempts.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remove, loosen, cut, cover, adjust, or test the device or strap.
- Do not reset equipment, reinstall an app, or change required phone permissions or connections unless an authorized contact instructs you to.
- Do not unplug required equipment or stop charging the device unless an authorized contact instructs you to.
- Do not leave an approved location or change your schedule solely to troubleshoot the device unless your written conditions allow it or an authorized contact approves it.
- Do not ignore an alert, low-battery warning, or error message.
- If you think there is an immediate danger or medical emergency, call 911 or seek emergency care first. Notify your supervising contact as soon as it is safe to do so.

## What to do now
1. **Keep following your current written conditions.** Stay within your approved location and schedule limits. Do not make an unapproved change solely because the device, app, charger, or base unit appears to be malfunctioning.
2. **Find the reporting route in your paperwork or monitoring app.** Use the listed supervising office, officer, after-hours contact, provider support line, or other stated route in the order given.
3. **If the route is unclear, contact the listed supervising office or after-hours contact.** Say that the device appears to be malfunctioning, state your current location, and ask what you should do next. Ask whether you also need to contact the monitoring provider.
4. **Ask for the issue to be logged.** Request a ticket number, reference number, or other confirmation where one is available. Record the name or identifier of the person you spoke with and the time of the call.
5. **Follow only the troubleshooting steps you are given.** Continue charging as required by your instructions and keep required equipment powered unless an authorized contact tells you otherwise. Do not reset, remove, adjust, or alter anything on your own.
6. **Make a brief timestamped timeline.** Record when the issue started, the alert or error shown, your location, charging status, any power or service outage, and each call, message, voicemail, or reply.
7. **Save a non-invasive record where safe and allowed.** Keep screenshots of alerts and your call log. Take a photo of any visible error, charger connection, or base-unit light only if you can do so without moving, removing, adjusting, or interfering with the equipment.
8. **If nobody answers, follow any listed backup or after-hours instructions.** Leave a brief voicemail or message where permitted, record each attempt, and keep following your current conditions.
9. **If you are told the issue may be treated as a violation, stay factual.** State when you reported it, where you are, and any reference number. Ask for the next instruction in writing where possible. Contact your existing defense counsel promptly if you have one, or seek legal help if a violation process, hearing, detention, or arrest is threatened.
10. **If law enforcement contacts you, keep the exchange calm and simple.** State that you reported a device malfunction and keep your log available. Do not argue about the allegation on the scene. Ask to contact your lawyer if you want legal advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to write a long explanation right now. A short, accurate timeline is enough for the first contact.
- You do not need to attempt extra troubleshooting beyond the steps an authorized contact gives you.
- You do not need to decide your full legal strategy immediately. Logging the problem, following instructions, and preserving a clear record come first.

## Important reassurance
An alert does not by itself explain what happened. Reporting the problem promptly, following your written conditions, and keeping a timestamped record may help clarify the situation.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about a claimed violation, a hearing, a change to conditions, or legal representation may need advice from a lawyer familiar with your order and local system.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Electronic-monitoring rules, devices, reporting routes, and response procedures vary across federal, state, county, and local systems. Follow your written conditions and the instructions of your authorized supervising contact.
