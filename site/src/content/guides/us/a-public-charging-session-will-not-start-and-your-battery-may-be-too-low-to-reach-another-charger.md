# What to do if… a public charging session will not start and your battery may be too low to reach another charger (USA)

## Short answer
Park the vehicle safely and attempt a single station reset by unplugging and waiting 30 seconds; if it fails, immediately contact the charging network's support line via the number on the station to request a remote start or station reboot.

## Do not do these things
- Do not make repeated failed start attempts as this may result in multiple credit card authorization holds.
- Do not pull or force a charging connector that is locked in place.
- Do not attempt to drive to a distant charger if the estimated range is lower than the distance to the next site.
- Do not use third-party charging adapters that are not approved by your vehicle manufacturer.
- Do not leave the vehicle completely powered off in extreme temperatures while waiting for help if your 12V battery health is low.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle in a safe parking position.** Ensure the car is in Park and the electronic parking brake is engaged. If you are blocking a high-traffic lane and have enough power to move a few yards to a safer stall or parking space, do so immediately.
2. **Perform a visual inspection of the charging hardware.** Look for a red "Emergency Stop" button on the station and ensure it is pulled out (reset position). Check both the vehicle's charge port and the station connector for debris, ice, or physical damage.
3. **Execute a clean station reset.** Unplug the connector from the vehicle, close the charge port door, and wait at least 30 seconds for the station to reset its communication protocol. If the station has a screen, wait for it to return to the "Ready" or "Insert Connector" prompt before trying again.
4. **Try an alternative activation method.** If the mobile app failed to start the session, use a credit card or RFID card at the station reader. If your vehicle supports Plug and Charge, ensure the feature is active in your vehicle settings and try plugging in without using the app.
5. **Call the charging network support line for a remote intervention.** Locate the toll-free number printed on the station or in the network app. Provide the station ID number and inform the representative that your battery level is critical and you cannot reach another charger; ask them to perform a remote "hard reset" or "remote start" on the stall.
6. **Coordinate roadside assistance if the second attempt fails.** Contact your vehicle’s manufacturer roadside assistance or a provider like AAA (1-800-AAA-HELP) while you still have phone battery. Specifically request a flatbed tow to the nearest working charger or a mobile charging vehicle if available in your metro area.
7. **Conserve all remaining vehicle and phone power.** Turn off the cabin climate control, seat heaters, and all exterior lights if it is safe to do so. Use a low-power USB port to keep your phone operational for incoming calls from recovery services.
8. **Contact 911 only if there is an immediate safety threat.** Call emergency services if you see smoke or sparks from the charger, if your vehicle is stranded in an active lane of traffic, or if you feel physically unsafe in your current surroundings.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve payment disputes or authorization hold issues during the crisis.
- You do not need to diagnose why the station is broken; professional technicians will handle hardware repairs.
- You do not need to update your vehicle software or charging apps while at a low state of charge.

## Important reassurance
Charging stalls frequently experience software "handshake" errors that can be resolved by a remote technician in under five minutes. Being stranded at a charger is a recognized scenario for roadside assistance providers, and they are equipped to transport EVs safely using flatbed trucks.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a failed charging session during a low-battery event. It does not cover long-term battery health diagnostics or legal disputes with charging providers.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and does not replace the specific instructions in your vehicle's owner's manual. High-voltage charging equipment can be hazardous if physically damaged; if you see exposed wires or smell burning, move away from the equipment immediately.
