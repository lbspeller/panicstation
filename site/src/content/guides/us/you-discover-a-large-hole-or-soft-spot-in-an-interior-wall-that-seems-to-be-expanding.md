# What to do if… you discover a large hole or soft spot in an interior wall that seems to be expanding (USA)

## Short answer
Keep people and pets away from the damaged area. If water may be leaking, use a water shutoff only if it is safely accessible, then contact your landlord, property manager, or a qualified professional promptly.

## Do not do these things
- Do not press, probe, or cut into the damaged area. Disturbing it can worsen the damage and may expose hidden wiring, plumbing, or mold.
- Do not touch wet outlets, switches, cords, or power strips.
- Do not approach an electrical panel through water or across a wet floor.
- Do not patch or paint over the area before the moisture source has been found and controlled.
- Do not start fans, heaters, or dehumidifiers until the leak is controlled, the area is electrically safe, the water is known to be clean, and mold growth is not suspected.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer zone.** Keep people and pets back. Move furniture away only if you can do so without touching the wall or entering a wet area. If a nearby ceiling is stained, bulging, or sagging, stay out from under it.
2. **Leave the area if danger appears immediate.** Move away and call 911 if anyone may be in immediate danger, or if you notice sparks, smoke, a burning odor, or signs that part of the wall or ceiling may collapse.
3. **Look without disturbing the wall.** Check for fresh wetness, dripping sounds, spreading stains, water pooling, or warped baseboards. Take photos or video from a safe distance.
4. **Stop an active water leak only if it is safe to do so.** If you can safely reach the correct fixture shutoff or main water shutoff, turn it off. Do not enter a wet or unstable area to reach it.
5. **Reduce electrical risk only from a dry, safely reachable location.** If electrical circuits or equipment may have become wet or are near water, turn off power at the main breaker or fuse on the service panel only if you can reach it safely without entering water. Otherwise, stay away and call a qualified electrician. Do not restore power yourself if electrical equipment may have become wet.
6. **Notify the responsible person promptly.**
   - **If you rent:** call or message your landlord or property manager immediately. Send written notice with photos and the time you noticed the change if practical.
   - **If you own a house or condo:** contact a plumber if a leak is likely. Notify building management or your condo association if shared pipes or building areas may be involved.
7. **Arrange an inspection.** Ask for a plumber if moisture or a pipe leak is likely. Ask for a qualified contractor or building professional if the wall is bulging, bowing, crumbling, or continuing to worsen. Ask a qualified water-damage professional whether concealed areas, including the wall cavity, need controlled drying.
8. **Dry the area only after the source is controlled and the area is safe.** Fans or dehumidifiers may help with clean-water damage when electrical hazards, contaminated water, and mold growth are not concerns. Ask a qualified professional before using fans if the water source or mold status is unclear. EPA guidance advises drying water-damaged areas and items within 24–48 hours where possible to help prevent mold growth.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the final repair plan, decoration choices, or fault right now.
- You do not need to open the wall yourself.
- You do not need to start major mold cleanup before the moisture source is controlled and the area has been assessed.
- Insurance questions and non-emergency repair decisions can wait until the immediate hazard is stable. Keep your photos and written notes.

## Important reassurance
A changing wall can feel alarming, but you do not need to solve the whole repair immediately. Keeping clear, stopping water only when safe, documenting the change, and contacting the right person are useful first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Repairs, insurance questions, mold remediation, and tenant issues may need qualified professional help after the immediate situation is stable.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. It does not replace an in-person safety assessment or inspection.
