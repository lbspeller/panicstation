# What to do if… you discover a large hole or soft spot in an interior wall that seems to be expanding (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately move away from the affected area and avoid touching or applying pressure to the wall, as expanding soft spots often indicate active leaks or structural instability.

## Do not do these things
- Do not press on, poke, or peel the damaged area to investigate its depth or moisture level.
- Do not cut into the drywall, as hidden electrical wires, gas lines, or pressurized plumbing may be present.
- Do not stand directly under or near ceiling areas that show corresponding stains or sagging.
- Do not use high-heat devices like hair dryers or space heaters to dry the wall, as this can trap moisture or pose a fire risk.
- Do not apply paint, plaster, or wallpaper over the damage until a professional confirms the source of moisture is resolved.
- Do not touch outlets, switches, or power strips if they are wet or located within the damp area.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the immediate area of people and pets.** Establish a safety perimeter around the wall and avoid placing furniture or weight near the affected section.
2. **Observe the area for immediate hazards from a safe distance.** Check for sensory cues that require immediate evacuation.
   - If you smell gas (sulfur / rotten eggs), leave the building immediately and call 911 or your gas utility.
   - If you see sparks, hear buzzing, or smell burning plastic, evacuate and contact emergency services.
   - If you hear significant cracking or see the wall/ceiling visibly shifting, leave the premises.
3. **Shut off the main water supply if a plumbing leak is suspected.** Locate the main shutoff valve (usually in the basement, crawlspace, or near the water meter) and turn it clockwise to stop the flow of water.
4. **Isolate electrical power to the room if the wall is wet near outlets.** Turn off the specific circuit breaker at the main electrical panel to prevent shock or fire.
5. **Document the damage with photographs and video.** Capture the extent of the hole, the texture of the soft spot, and any visible water trailing or flooring damage.
6. **Notify the appropriate party for inspection.**
   - For renters: contact the landlord or property manager via a written emergency maintenance request.
   - For homeowners: contact a licensed plumber or a water restoration professional.
   - For condo owners: notify the Homeowners Association (HOA) or building management if the leak may originate from common areas or upper units.
7. **Initiate controlled drying only after the leak is stopped.** Use floor fans and dehumidifiers to circulate air, following EPA guidance to dry all materials within 24 to 48 hours to prevent mold colonization.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the leak or structural failure yourself.
- You do not need to choose permanent repair materials or paint colors today.
- You do not need to perform deep mold remediation until the area is fully dried and the moisture source is sealed.
- You do not need to calculate the total cost of damages immediately.

## Important reassurance
Discovering structural or water damage inside your home is startling, and it is common to feel a sense of urgency to fix it. By distancing yourself and isolating utilities, you have already taken the most critical steps to prevent the situation from escalating into a fire or a flood.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for residential wall damage only. Long-term repairs, insurance claims, and mold testing require specialized professional assessment.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace a professional inspection by a licensed contractor, plumber, or structural engineer. Local building codes and tenant-landlord laws vary by state and municipality.
