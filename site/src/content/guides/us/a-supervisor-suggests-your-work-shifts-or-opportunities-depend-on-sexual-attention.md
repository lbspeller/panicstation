# What to do if… a supervisor suggests your work shifts or opportunities depend on sexual attention (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause and create distance from the supervisor. You do not need to answer, agree, confront them, or decide whether to report anything right now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay alone with the supervisor if you feel unsafe or pressured.
- Do not feel you have to answer immediately, explain yourself, or make promises.
- Do not confront the supervisor in a secluded place, in a vehicle, or after hours.
- Do not delete existing messages or schedule records.
- Do not make rushed decisions while you are overwhelmed.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** End the conversation if you can and move near coworkers, customers, security staff, or another safe person. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Reduce one-on-one contact.** Ask for another person to be present during conversations, keep meetings in visible areas, or ask for scheduling questions to go through another manager.
3. **Use a brief boundary if you need one.** You can say: “Please send scheduling decisions in writing,” or: “I need to leave this conversation now.”
4. **Tell one safe person.** This could be a trusted person outside work, a union representative, a higher-level manager, or an HR contact who is not connected to the supervisor.
5. **Choose whether to seek workplace help.** When it feels safe, you may ask an internal contact for a different scheduling route, a different point of contact, or support avoiding one-on-one meetings.
6. **Consider early outside information without committing to a complaint.** The EEOC provides information about workplace sexual harassment, retaliation, and time limits. Time limits can apply, so contacting the EEOC early may help you understand your options.
7. **Use specialist support if this involved sexual contact, threats, or intense distress.** RAINN offers confidential support. If any sexual contact happened without your consent, move to safety and consider urgent medical care or specialist support. Reporting decisions can wait while immediate safety and medical needs come first.

*Optional, only if you may want a record later:* Save existing messages and schedule screenshots without editing them.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to make an internal complaint or contact an outside agency.
- You do not have to work out the legal label for what happened right now.
- You do not need to write a perfect account or explain everything at once.
- You do not need to decide whether to keep working with this supervisor long term.

## Important reassurance
A supervisor suggesting that shifts, schedules, or work opportunities depend on sexual attention may be workplace sexual harassment. Feeling frozen, unsure, or worried about consequences is understandable. You are allowed to create distance and seek support without making every decision now.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the moment, reduce contact, and help you reach support. Later workplace, legal, medical, or personal decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
