# What to do if… you develop new severe thirst, frequent urination, and unexpected weight loss (USA)
## Short answer
Contact a clinician today and ask where to be assessed promptly. New severe thirst, frequent urination, and unexpected weight loss may be caused by high blood sugar, including newly developing diabetes. If you have repeated vomiting, cannot keep fluids down, are confused, are unusually sleepy, have trouble breathing, or are getting worse, call 911 or go to the ER.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for a routine appointment if the symptoms are severe or worsening.
- Do not deliberately stop drinking just to reduce urination, unless a clinician has already told you to restrict fluids.
- Do not take someone else’s insulin or diabetes medication.
- Do not change, stop, or take extra doses of your prescribed medicines unless a clinician tells you to.
- Do not fast, start a cleanse, or make a major diet change today.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, confused, or unusually sleepy.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 or go to the ER now if you have any emergency warning signs:**
   - repeated vomiting or you cannot keep fluids down
   - severe stomach pain
   - fast or deep breathing, trouble breathing, or fruity-smelling breath
   - confusion, unusual sleepiness, severe weakness, fainting, or being hard to wake
   - severe dizziness or rapid worsening

2. **If you do not have emergency warning signs, contact a clinician today and ask where to be assessed promptly.**
   - Tell your primary care clinic that you have new severe thirst, frequent urination, and unexpected weight loss.
   - If the clinic cannot assess you today, use urgent care.
   - Go to the ER if you become very unwell or your symptoms worsen.

3. **If you have access to a blood glucose meter, check your blood sugar now.**
   - Write down the result and the time.
   - Do not delay urgent care while trying to repeat checks or interpret the result yourself.
   - Go to the ER or call 911 if you feel very unwell or are worsening.

4. **If you have access to urine ketone strips or a blood ketone meter, check ketones now.**
   - High ketones are a medical emergency. Call 911 or go to the ER now.

5. **While arranging care, sip water regularly unless a clinician has told you to restrict fluids.**
   - Avoid alcohol.
   - If you can eat, keep food simple. Do not fast or start a cleanse.

6. **Have a few details ready for the clinician.**
   - Note when the thirst, frequent urination, and weight loss started.
   - Note any vomiting, stomach pain, breathing changes, blurred vision, unusual tiredness, recent illness, or infection.
   - Have your medication list ready, especially if you take steroids or diuretics.
   - Mention pregnancy or possible pregnancy if relevant.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose yourself or decide what type of diabetes you might have.
- You do not need to begin a major diet change or exercise plan today.
- Insurance questions, paperwork, and longer-term planning do not need to be resolved before you seek urgent evaluation.

## Important reassurance
Getting checked today is a protective step. This symptom combination needs prompt assessment, but you do not need to work out the cause by yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Testing and any longer-term decisions may need help from a clinician.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice or a diagnosis. If you are rapidly worsening, have repeated vomiting, cannot keep fluids down, are confused, are unusually sleepy, or have trouble breathing, call 911 or go to the ER.
