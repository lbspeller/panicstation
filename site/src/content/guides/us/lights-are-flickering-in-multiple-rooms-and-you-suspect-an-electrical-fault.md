# What to do if… lights are flickering in multiple rooms and you suspect an electrical fault (USA)

## Short answer
Treat flickering across multiple rooms as a possible wiring or utility-supply problem. If there is smoke, fire, sparking, arcing, or a downed line, move away and call 911. Otherwise, reduce electrical load only when safe and arrange help from your utility, landlord, or a licensed electrician.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore repeated flickering across several rooms, especially if it is new or getting worse.
- Do not keep running high-draw appliances to test the problem.
- Do not remove the electrical-panel cover or dismantle outlets, fixtures, or switches.
- Do not approach or operate a panel that is hot, smoking, sparking, arcing, or making unusual noises.
- Do not touch or keep using warm, hot, discoloured, scorched, buzzing, or crackling outlets, plugs, or switches.
- Do not go near a downed power line or anything touching it.

## What to do now
1. **Check briefly for immediate danger.**  
   If you notice smoke, fire, sparks, arcing, a burning smell, buzzing or crackling, unusual heat, discolouration, or a tingling sensation from an appliance, outlet, switch, or panel:
   - Stop using the affected item or area.
   - Do not touch it again to check.
   - Move away from the area.
   - Call 911 for fire, smoke, active arcing, or immediate danger.
   - Otherwise, arrange urgent help from a licensed electrician or your landlord.

2. **Reduce electrical load only if it is safe.**  
   Turn off high-draw appliances, such as space heaters, dryers, microwaves, toaster ovens, hair dryers, and window air conditioners. Unplug an item only if you can do so without approaching a warm, damaged, discoloured, buzzing, sparking, or scorched outlet or plug.

3. **Check for a tripped breaker only if the panel appears safe.**  
   Do not approach the panel if it is hot, smoking, sparking, arcing, smells burnt, or is making unusual noises. If the panel is dry and appears normal, open only the normal access door to view the breaker switches. Look for a breaker in the OFF position or a middle position. After turning off or unplugging appliances on that circuit, you may reset the breaker once by switching it fully OFF and then ON. If the breaker trips again or the problem continues, leave it OFF and contact a licensed electrician or your landlord.

4. **Check whether a utility issue may be affecting the area.**  
   From a safe location, see whether nearby homes or streetlights also appear affected. If several properties seem affected, or you suspect a supply problem, report flickering or voltage fluctuation to your local electric utility.

5. **If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager promptly.**  
   Report flickering across multiple rooms and mention any heat, smell, buzzing, crackling, tingling, sparks, or breaker trips. Avoid using affected outlets or circuits while you wait for instructions or repair.

6. **Note useful details from a safe distance.**  
   Without dismantling anything or moving closer to inspect equipment, note:
   - which rooms or fixtures flicker;
   - whether the flickering is constant or intermittent;
   - whether it happens when an appliance starts;
   - whether any breaker trips;
   - whether there is heat, smell, buzzing, crackling, tingling, sparking, or discolouration.  
   Share these details with your utility, landlord, or electrician.

7. **Treat exterior electrical danger as an emergency.**  
   If you see a downed line, keep people and pets at least 35 feet away from the line and anything touching it. Assume the line is live. If you unexpectedly find yourself close to it, shuffle away with small steps while keeping your feet together. Call 911.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide perfectly whether the problem is inside your home or with the utility supply.
- You do not need to replace bulbs, switches, outlets, or fixtures as a first response.
- You do not need to remove the electrical-panel cover or dismantle anything.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to solve the electrical problem yourself. Take one safe step at a time: move away from warning signs, reduce load only when safe, and contact the appropriate professional.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Utility-supply problems may need help from your electric utility. Home wiring, panel, or service-connection problems may need help from your landlord or a licensed electrician.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If there is smoke, fire, active arcing, a downed line, or immediate danger, move away and call 911.
