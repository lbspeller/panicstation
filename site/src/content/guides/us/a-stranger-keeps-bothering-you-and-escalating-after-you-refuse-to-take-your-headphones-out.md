# What to do if… a stranger keeps bothering you and escalating after you refuse to take your headphones out (USA)

## Short answer
Create distance and move toward a staffed, populated place now. If you need immediate police assistance, call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel obliged to take your headphones out to be polite if you feel uneasy.
- Do not argue, give a long explanation, or answer questions about where you live or where you are going.
- Do not let the person steer you toward a quieter place, a vehicle, an alley, a stairwell, or somewhere out of public view.
- Do not stop near platform edges, between parked cars, or anywhere with no easy exit.
- Do not unlock your phone, show your screen, or open your wallet near the person unless you need your phone to get help.
- Do not continue along your usual route if the person is matching your direction. Move into a staffed public place instead.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a safer pause.** Step back, angle away, and head toward other people or staff. Use a store counter, open business, hotel lobby, front desk, security desk, transit employee, or bus operator if one is nearby.
2. **Use a short boundary line if it feels safe to speak.** Say `No`, `Stop`, `Back up`, or `I am leaving`. Do not debate.
3. **Ask a specific person for help.** Say, `Can I stand with you? This person will not leave me alone.` Ask staff to stay with you or contact security.
4. **Call 911 if you need immediate police assistance.** This includes situations where the person blocks your route, follows you, threatens you, tries to force you somewhere quieter, or will not let you leave. Start with your location, then describe what the person is doing and what they look like. Answer the call-taker’s questions and do not hang up until instructed.
5. **If speaking could put you at greater risk and you cannot safely call, consider texting 911.** Text-to-911 availability varies by area. A voice call is generally the better option when you can make one safely.
6. **If you are on Amtrak, contact Amtrak staff or Amtrak Police.** Approach a uniformed employee or officer, call **800-331-0008**, or text **APD11** to **27311**. For an emergency, call **911**.
7. **Contact someone you trust if it is safe to do so.** Call or message your exact location and ask them to stay available while you move toward staff or other people.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether you are overreacting.
- You do not need to record video while you are still exposed.
- You do not need to confront the person.
- You do not need to write a detailed account until you are somewhere safer.
- You do not need to decide about reporting or follow-up in the moment.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel frozen, embarrassed, or unsure. A stranger escalating after you decline is enough reason to create distance and seek help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. If the person keeps reappearing, contacts you, or follows you again, later decisions may need local specialist support.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice.
