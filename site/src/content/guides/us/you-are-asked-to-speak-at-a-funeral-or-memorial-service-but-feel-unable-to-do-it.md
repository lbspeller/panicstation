# What to do if… you are asked to speak at a funeral or memorial service but feel unable to do it (USA)

## Short answer
Tell the organizer as soon as you can that you may not be able to speak. You can decline, or ask for a backup plan that does not depend on you finishing the tribute yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait until you are standing in front of the room to mention that you may need a backup.
- Do not agree just to end the conversation if you feel unable to do it.
- Do not write a long tribute because you feel you have to. A few sentences can be enough.
- Do not rely on your phone as the only copy of anything you want read.
- Do not pressure yourself to prove that you can get through it.

## What to do now
1. **Tell the organizer what you need.**  
   You can say: “I may not be able to speak during the service. Please arrange a backup reader, or plan for the service to continue without my reading.”

2. **Ask who is coordinating the speaking order.**  
   Depending on the service, this may be the funeral director, officiant, celebrant, clergy member, venue contact, or a family organizer. Ask that person to confirm the backup plan.

3. **Choose the lowest-pressure option.**  
   Ask whether one of these is possible:
   - Someone else reads your words.
   - The officiant or celebrant includes a few sentences from you.
   - Your reading is shortened to a few lines.
   - Your words are included in the printed program, if changes are still possible.
   - A recording is used, if the venue confirms that playback is available.
   - Your speaking part is removed.

4. **Give the backup reader a short printed backup copy.**  
   Use a copy that is easy to follow. Give it to the backup reader or organizer before the service begins.

5. **Decide what should happen if you start but cannot continue.**  
   You can ask the backup reader to take over immediately, or ask for your part to end there. You do not need to explain yourself during the service.

6. **Ask for only the practical support you want.**  
   You can ask whether a microphone, lectern, water, seat nearby, or clear cue is available. You can also ask someone to stand close enough to step in.

7. **Say no if that is what you need.**  
   A simple message is enough: “I am not able to speak at the service. Please plan the order without my reading.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether you will speak at another memorial or gathering later.
- You do not need to write the perfect tribute now.
- You do not need to find a replacement activity for yourself.
- You do not need to manage the whole order of service. Ask the organizer to make the adjustment.

## Important reassurance
Grief may make speaking feel harder than usual, including if you are normally comfortable speaking in public. It is reasonable to ask to decline, shorten the reading, or use a backup reader. You do not need to give a long explanation.

## Scope note
These are first steps for reducing immediate pressure and coordinating the service. Later decisions about tributes, memorials, or other ways to participate may need support from the organizer, funeral director, officiant, celebrant, or another appropriate person.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, funeral-planning, or other professional advice. Service arrangements vary by venue, timing, technology, and faith or community tradition.
