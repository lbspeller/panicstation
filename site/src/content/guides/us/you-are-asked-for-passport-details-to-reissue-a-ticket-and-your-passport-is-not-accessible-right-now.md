# What to do if… you are asked for passport details to reissue a ticket and your passport is not accessible right now (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the request through the airline's official app or website before sharing data. If legitimate, provide only the mandatory Secure Flight Passenger Data (name, DOB, gender) to allow ticketing, and defer full passport details until check-in.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send a photo or scan of your passport via email, text message, or social media DM.
- Do not use any link provided in an unsolicited email or text; scammers frequently spoof airline "urgent reissue" notices.
- Do not guess or estimate your passport number or expiration date; incorrect data can result in a "Do Not Board" status or significant delays at the airport.
- Do not redact or black out parts of a passport scan unless explicitly instructed by a verified official, as automated airline systems will usually reject modified images.
- Do not provide payment card information or bank logins if the request was specifically for "passenger documentation."

## What to do now
1. **Verify the request using a channel you initiate.**
   **Navigate directly to the airline’s official website or mobile app and log in to your reservation using your confirmation code (PNR).** 
   - Check the "Passenger Information" or "Travel Documents" section to see if there is an active alert or missing data flag.
   - If calling, use the customer service number listed on the official website, not the number provided in the request message.
2. **Distinguish between Secure Flight data and APIS data.**
   **Ask the agent if they only require Secure Flight Passenger Data (SFPD) to issue the ticket.**
   - SFPD includes your full name, date of birth, and gender; this is often enough to generate a ticket.
   - Full passport details (APIS) are required for international travel but can typically be added during the check-in process (24 hours before flight) rather than at the moment of ticketing.
3. **Use the official airline app for data entry.**
   **Enter the required information directly into the airline's secure "Manage Trip" portal or mobile app.**
   - This ensures the data is encrypted and linked directly to your reservation without passing through insecure email servers.
4. **Request a ticketing hold or extension.**
   **Inform the agent that you cannot access the physical document immediately and ask for a 12-hour or 24-hour hold.**
   - Legitimate airlines can often place a temporary hold on a reissue request if the passenger is currently in transit or away from their documents.
5. **Check secure digital backups.**
   **Look for your passport details in a secure password manager, an encrypted cloud vault, or your airline's saved "Frequent Flyer" profile.**
   - Only use these details if you are certain they are current; do not use expired passport information for a new ticket.

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide your full passport scan immediately if only the text details (number/expiry) are required for the system.
- You do not need to decide on a flight cancellation or refund while you are still verifying the legitimacy of the document request.
- You do not need to report a scam to the authorities until after you have secured your booking and confirmed no data was actually breached.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency when a "ticket reissue" is mentioned, as it sounds like your flight is at risk. However, airlines have established secure procedures for document collection. Taking five minutes to verify the source of the request will not cause a legitimate ticket to disappear.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate response to a request for passport information for ticketing purposes. It does not cover the replacement of lost or stolen passports or the application process for visas or travel authorizations (like ESTA).

## Important note
Airlines are legally required by the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) to collect specific passenger data before ticketing and boarding. If you cannot provide this information by the check-in deadline, you will be denied boarding. If you suspect your passport details have been stolen or shared with a fraudulent source, visit IdentityTheft.gov and report the compromise to the U.S. Department of State.
