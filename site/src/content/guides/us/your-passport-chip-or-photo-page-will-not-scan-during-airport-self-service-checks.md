# What to do if… your passport chip or photo page will not scan during airport self-service checks (USA)

## Short answer
Stop attempting to use the machine and proceed immediately to a staffed counter or officer booth. Request a "manual document verification" as this is a standard fallback for technical failures or physical document wear.

## Do not do these things
- Don't apply excessive physical pressure or bend the document to force a scan.
- Don't move between multiple kiosks if the first two attempts fail.
- Don't attempt to repair lifting laminate or loose pages with tape or glue.
- Don't assume a scan failure indicates a security flag or travel ban.
- Don't hand your passport to any person not behind an official counter or in a marked uniform.

## What to do now
1. **Discontinue use of the automated system and locate the nearest staff member.** If the screen displays "See Officer for Assistance" or "Processing Completed See Officer," do not restart the process; move directly to the designated exit line or service desk.
2. **Remove all physical obstructions from the document.** Take the passport out of any leather covers, plastic wallets, or sleeves before attempting one final, careful scan.
3. **Wipe the photo page and Machine Readable Zone (MRZ).** Use a soft, dry cloth to clean the bottom two lines of text and the laminate surface to remove oils or dust that interfere with optical character recognition.
4. **Identify the specific failure point for the agent.** Inform the airline agent or CBP officer whether the machine failed to read the optical page (the photo) or the electronic chip (the RFID) to speed up manual entry.
5. **Request a manual ID check if biometrics fail.** If a Touchless ID portal or facial recognition camera cannot match your face to the passport data, state clearly that you wish to undergo manual inspection by an officer.
6. **Be prepared for the TSA ConfirmID process if the physical document is unreadable.** If your passport is deemed too damaged to scan at security, you may be required to pay a $45 verification fee to use the modernized identity fallback system.
7. **Ask for a verbal confirmation of document validity for future travel.** Once the officer or agent completes the manual check, ask if the passport must be replaced before your next trip or if the issue was specific to that machine's hardware.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the chip failed while standing at the kiosk.
- You do not need to apply for an emergency passport until a staff member explicitly confirms the current one is "unserviceable."
- You do not need to worry about Global Entry revocation solely because of a hardware scanning error.

## Important reassurance
Kiosk hardware and RFID readers frequently fail due to dust, sensor calibration, or minor document wear. Being routed to an officer is the primary backup protocol and does not imply you have done anything wrong or that your identity is in question.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization of a failed document scan at U.S. airports. It does not cover the legal requirements for visa eligibility or the long-term process for replacing a damaged passport at a regional agency.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only. U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and Transportation Security Administration (TSA) personnel have final authority over document acceptance. If your passport shows signs of tampering, water damage, or significant tearing, it may be seized or rejected regardless of manual check attempts.
