# What to do if… you think an intimate video call was recorded without your consent (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, stop live contact with the person, and reach confidential support before making big decisions. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay money, send gift cards or crypto, or send more images to try to make it stop.
- Don’t keep negotiating in live calls or messages if the person is threatening or pressuring you.
- Don’t confront the person to get an admission.
- Don’t post about it publicly right now.
- Don’t delete everything in a panic if you may want help later.
- Don’t download, forward, or make new copies of nude or sexual material involving anyone who was under 18 at the time.
- Don’t assume you have to handle this alone because it happened online.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you feel in immediate physical danger, call **911** or local police. If not, move somewhere steadier, such as another room, a public place, or near a trusted person.

2. **Stop the live pressure.** End the call or conversation. Mute, block, or pause notifications from the person if that helps you avoid reacting under pressure.

3. **Keep basic information safely.** Save what you already have, such as the username, profile link, threats, payment demands, dates, times, platform name, and any posted links. Keep this light-touch; you do not need to do detailed investigation.

4. **Get confidential support.** You can contact:
   - **RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline:** call **1-800-656-HOPE (4673)**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use online chat.
   - **Cyber Civil Rights Initiative Image Abuse Helpline:** call **1-844-878-2274** for image-based abuse, sextortion, and takedown planning.

5. **Report it on the platform or app.** Use the app’s report tools for nonconsensual intimate imagery, sextortion, harassment, or threats. Report the account and any specific post, message, or link if something has been shared.

6. **If you were under 18 when the video was made, treat it as urgent.** You or a trusted adult can report child sexual exploitation to **NCMEC CyberTipline**. You can also use **NCMEC Take It Down** for nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos taken before you were 18. Do not download or share the material to use these services.

7. **If there are threats, blackmail, or payment demands, you can make a report.** For immediate danger, call 911 or local police. For online extortion or cyber-enabled crime, you can report to the **FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)**. Type **ic3.gov** directly into your browser to avoid lookalike sites.

8. **If a young person is being exploited, the FBI also accepts reports.** You can call **1-800-CALL-FBI** or submit a tip at **tips.fbi.gov**. A trusted adult, hotline, advocate, or support worker can help with this.

9. **Reduce further account access.** Change passwords for your email and the accounts connected to the video app, turn on two-factor authentication, and review who can contact you, find you, tag you, or view your profile.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide today whether to press charges or bring a civil claim.
- You do **not** need to confront the person.
- You do **not** need to tell everyone in your life.
- You do **not** need to research every state law before getting support.
- You do **not** need to solve takedown, reporting, legal options, and account security all at once.

## Important reassurance
Panic, shame, freezing, anger, or numbness are common after this kind of violation. Consenting to a call is not the same as consenting to being recorded or having intimate material shared. The responsibility for recording, threatening, or sharing without consent belongs to the person doing it.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the moment, reduce immediate harm, and connect you to specialist support. Later decisions about police reports, legal options, school, work, or family can be made with help when you have more support around you.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. Laws and processes vary by state. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If the video or images involve someone who was under 18 at the time, specialist support is especially important.
