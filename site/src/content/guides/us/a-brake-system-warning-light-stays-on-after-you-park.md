# What to do if… a brake system warning light stays on after you park (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive again until you have checked that the parking brake is not the simple cause and the warning clears normally. If a red brake-system warning stays on, the pedal feels different, or you see signs of a leak, treat the vehicle as unsafe to drive and arrange roadside help or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “drive a little” to test the brakes after a red brake warning stays on.
- Do not ignore a red brake warning just because the vehicle stopped normally in a parking lot.
- Do not repeatedly press the brake pedal to see if the problem improves; one careful stationary check is enough.
- Do not add brake fluid just to make the warning go away; low fluid can point to a leak or another brake problem that still needs urgent attention.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle or put any part of your body under it.
- Do not let anyone stand in front of or behind the vehicle while you check the pedal.
- Do not deliberately cycle an electronic parking brake if the vehicle is showing an electric parking-brake fault or multiple brake warnings.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the vehicle secured.** Leave it in Park, keep your foot on the brake if needed, and turn on hazard lights if you are in or near traffic. Do not rely on the parking brake alone while you are unsure what the warning means.
2. **Check whether this is simply the parking brake indicator.** Look at the parking-brake control and the dashboard message. If the parking brake is applied and there is no electric parking-brake fault message, release it normally once. If the warning goes out and stays out after the normal start-up check, that may have been the cause.
3. **Restart once and watch the warning.** Start the vehicle and wait for the normal dashboard self-check to finish. If the red brake warning remains on, assume it is a real brake-system warning until a professional checks it.
4. **Look for obvious “do not drive” signs from outside the vehicle.** Stay out of traffic and do not go under the car. Look for fresh wet spots under the vehicle or near a wheel, smoke, or a sharp burning smell near a wheel.
5. **Check the brake pedal while stationary.** With the engine on and the vehicle still in Park, press the brake pedal firmly once and hold it. If it feels spongy, goes unusually low, slowly sinks, or needs much more force than normal, do not drive.
6. **Check brake fluid only if it is safe and easy.** If you are parked safely on level ground and can open the hood without traffic or hot-part risk, look at the brake fluid reservoir level. If it is clearly below the minimum mark, or you cannot check confidently, do not drive.
7. **Use this conservative go/no-go rule.**
   - **Red brake-system warning stays on:** do not drive; call roadside assistance or arrange a tow.
   - **Red brake warning plus ABS warning:** do not drive; braking stability or regular brake-system operation may be affected.
   - **Any leak, burning smell, smoke, or abnormal pedal feel:** do not drive.
   - **ABS warning only, no red brake warning, normal pedal, no leak, no smell:** braking may still work on many vehicles, but ABS may not. Avoid difficult traffic, high speeds, bad weather, and hard braking; arrange prompt inspection. Tow if you are unsure.
8. **If the stopped location is unsafe, reduce the danger first.** Stay belted if traffic is close, turn on hazards, and call roadside assistance. If you are in immediate danger and cannot safely move away, call 911. Only reposition the vehicle a very short distance at very low speed if the brake pedal feels normal and you can do it without entering fast traffic.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop immediately; focus on not driving an unsafe vehicle and getting help.
- You do not need to keep restarting the vehicle. One restart and a few safe checks are enough to decide not to drive.
- You do not need to decide whether the repair will be small or expensive before the vehicle is inspected.

## Important reassurance
A brake warning can feel alarming because the car may still seem normal. Choosing not to drive after a red brake warning, a low pedal, or possible fluid loss is a reasonable safety choice, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment after a brake-system warning appears. Later decisions, including diagnosis, repair, towing method, and whether the vehicle is safe to use again, may need help from a qualified mechanic, roadside service, or the vehicle manufacturer’s instructions.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. Vehicle warning lights and procedures vary by model; follow your owner’s manual and get professional help if the warning remains on or the brakes feel different.
