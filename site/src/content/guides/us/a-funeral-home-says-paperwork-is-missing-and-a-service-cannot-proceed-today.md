# What to do if… a funeral home says paperwork is missing and a service cannot proceed today (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the funeral home to send a brief written summary naming the exact missing document, what part of today is blocked, who is handling the next step, and when they will update you. Ask for a written explanation before agreeing to a new charge or change, and ask whether the gathering can still go ahead if the burial, cremation, or committal must move.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to a new fee, add-on, or major change only because you are being told it must be decided immediately.
- Do not accept a vague answer such as “the paperwork is not back” without asking for the exact local document name and current status.
- Do not guess who must sign or which office must act. Ask the funeral home to confirm this.
- Do not take on every administrative call yourself. Ask the funeral director to handle the contacts they are responsible for and give you a timed update.
- Do not cancel the entire gathering before asking whether a memorial service can still take place today.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a written summary now.**  
   Ask the funeral home to text or email:
   - the exact local name of the missing document;
   - whether it is missing, incomplete, rejected, awaiting a signature, or delayed by a records mismatch;
   - whether the problem blocks the ceremony, movement of the remains, burial, cremation, or only the committal;
   - the person or office responsible for the next step;
   - what the funeral home is doing now and when they will update you.

2. **Ask the funeral director to contact the responsible person or office.**  
   The funeral director generally handles death-certificate filing. In states requiring authorization for final disposition, the funeral director is generally responsible for obtaining it. Ask them to contact the relevant registrar, vital-records office, medical certifier, medical examiner, coroner, cemetery, or crematory as appropriate. Ask for a conference call or a confirmed contact route if speaking directly would help.

3. **If the problem is a death-certificate filing issue, ask what is incomplete.**  
   Ask whether the hold-up is a missing medical certification, a missing signature, or a mismatch such as the decedent’s name, date of birth, or other identifying detail. Ask the funeral home to send the correction request to the responsible person promptly and confirm when it has been sent.

4. **If cremation is planned, ask what additional authorization is required locally.**  
   Many states require an additional authorization before cremation. Ask whether a required signature, clearance, or other local step is incomplete, who must complete it, and whether the funeral home has contacted that person or office.

5. **Protect today’s gathering.**  
   Ask the funeral home, officiant, and venue whether the service can continue as a memorial service if the burial, cremation, or committal must be rescheduled. Ask for the next available time for the delayed part without making larger decisions today.

6. **Get written price information before agreeing to changes.**  
   If you are discussing arrangements, goods, services, or prices in person, ask for a General Price List that you can keep. If you are speaking by phone, ask for price information for any proposed change. After selections are made and before payment, ask for a written itemized statement showing each selected good or service, each price, and the total. If the arrangements are completed entirely by phone, ask when the completed statement will be sent. Ask for a written explanation of any legal, cemetery, or crematory requirement said to make a purchase necessary.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain, change funeral homes, or take legal action.
- You do not need to settle wider family disagreements while the immediate paperwork issue is being clarified.
- You do not need to make new financial commitments before the proposed changes and prices are written down.
- You do not need to redesign the whole service before asking whether only the burial, cremation, or committal needs to move.

## Important reassurance
A paperwork problem does not automatically mean the entire gathering must be cancelled. It is reasonable to ask for clear written information, a timed update, and a plan that keeps as much of today intact as possible.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours. Later decisions about responsibility, refunds, complaints, or changing providers may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. Death registration, disposition authorization, and cremation requirements vary by state and locality. Ask the funeral home to confirm the rules and contact route that apply where the death and planned disposition are taking place.
