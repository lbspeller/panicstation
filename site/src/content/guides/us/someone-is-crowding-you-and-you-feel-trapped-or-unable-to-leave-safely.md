# What to do if… someone is crowding you and you feel trapped or unable to leave safely (USA)

## Short answer
Move toward staff, security, or other people and ask for help reaching a clear exit. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance help, call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not move somewhere more isolated with them just to be polite.
- Do not keep explaining or negotiating if they continue crowding you.
- Do not accept a ride from them if you feel unsafe.
- Do not worry about making the interaction feel smooth; you can prioritize getting to staff, security, or other people.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move toward staff, security, or other people and a clear route out. Say to an employee: “I need help leaving because someone is making me feel unsafe.”
2. **Ask for practical support.** Ask an employee, security worker, or trusted person to stay with you while you wait for a ride or to walk with you to a staffed, well-lit pickup point.
3. **Contact backup.** Call or text someone you trust with your location and say: “I am leaving now. Please stay on the phone with me.” Share your live location if that feels safe and useful.
4. **Leave without further discussion.** Wait inside or near staff until your ride arrives. Check the vehicle details before getting in.
5. **Call 911 if you need immediate emergency help.** This includes if they block your exit, grab you, threaten you, or you otherwise need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance. Tell the call-taker your location and the nature of the emergency, then answer questions as you can.
6. **Once you are away, pause contact.** You do not need to meet them again or reply tonight.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide right now whether to report anything.
- You do **not** need to write a detailed timeline or gather proof in the moment.
- You do **not** need to send a final explanation or keep the interaction friendly.
- You do **not** need to make longer-term decisions tonight.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to wait for certainty before moving toward other people or asking for help. Feeling crowded or worried about your exit is enough reason to create distance and leave. You do not owe someone closeness or sexual contact because you were flirting, dating, or previously agreed to something.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: create distance, get support, and reach a safer place. Later decisions about support, reporting, or medical care can wait until you are safer and steadier, and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance help, call **911**. If sexual contact happened without your consent, or pressure or manipulation left you unsure about what happened, RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline offers free, confidential, 24/7 support: call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use its online chat.
