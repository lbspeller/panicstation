# What to do if… your vehicle security system or immobiliser prevents the engine from starting (USA)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a safer pause first: turn on hazard lights, stay out of traffic, and call for help if the vehicle is in a dangerous spot. Then try the low-risk key-recognition checks: use a spare key or fob, use the backup start method in your owner’s manual, and replace or work around a weak key fob battery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep pressing start or cranking the engine over and over; it can drain the vehicle battery and make recovery harder.
- Do not try to bypass the immobilizer, jump wires, pull random security fuses, or follow theft-bypass videos.
- Do not let a stranger handle your keys or fob, or plug unknown devices into your vehicle.
- Do not disconnect the vehicle battery as a roadside guess unless your owner’s manual, roadside technician, or a qualified professional tells you to.
- Do not stand in a travel lane, behind the vehicle, or beside traffic while troubleshooting.
- Do not push the vehicle in traffic just to test another fix.

## What to do now
1. **Make the scene safer before troubleshooting.**
   - Turn on hazard lights.
   - Put the vehicle in Park, set the parking brake, and keep your seat belt on if traffic is moving close around you.
   - If you can safely leave the vehicle and get away from traffic, do that.
   - If the vehicle is creating an urgent traffic hazard, or you feel threatened where you are, call 911.

2. **Look for signs that the key is not being recognized.**
   - Check the dash for a security, lock, or key light.
   - Look for a message such as “Key Not Detected,” “No Key,” or “Key Not Found.”
   - These signs may point to a key, fob, or immobilizer recognition problem, but they do not prove that the starter, vehicle battery, or another system is fine.

3. **Try the spare key or spare fob if you have it with you.**
   - If the spare works, stop repeated roadside testing.
   - The original key or fob may need a new battery, repair, replacement, or reprogramming.

4. **Use the built-in mechanical key if you are locked out or the fob buttons do not work.**
   - Many key fobs contain a small mechanical key.
   - Use it to unlock the driver door if needed.
   - If the alarm sounds after manual unlocking, avoid repeatedly opening and closing doors; move to the backup start method.

5. **Use your vehicle’s backup start method.**
   - Check your owner’s manual for “key not detected,” “remote battery weak,” “emergency start,” “backup start,” or “intelligent key backup slot.”
   - Common model-dependent methods include holding the fob against the START button while pressing the brake, or placing the fob in a marked backup slot or pocket in the console, cup holder, or steering-column area.
   - Follow the manual’s exact position before trying again.

6. **Reduce simple signal and fob-battery problems.**
   - Keep any second fob away from the one you are using.
   - Remove the fob from a metal case or crowded bag.
   - Hold the fob directly where the manual says, then try one start attempt.
   - If you can safely get the correct replacement battery, replace the fob battery and test the fob again.

7. **Call roadside assistance or a qualified professional early.**
   - If the spare key and backup start method do not work, call your roadside assistance, insurer, manufacturer roadside plan, or AAA if you have coverage.
   - Ask for a safe tow or on-scene assessment.
   - If no key is recognized, the next step is usually a dealer or reputable automotive locksmith with key programming and immobilizer diagnostic equipment.

8. **Escalate if the roadside situation becomes unsafe.**
   - Call 911 if the vehicle is exposed to fast traffic, blocking a lane, stopped in a dangerous location, or you feel unsafe waiting there.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the fault is the fob battery, transponder, receiver, immobilizer module, starter, or vehicle battery.
- You do not need to choose between a dealer and locksmith while standing in an unsafe place.
- Warranty checks, repair-price comparisons, replacement-key ordering, and complaint steps can wait until you and the vehicle are somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
This is a common stranded-car problem, and simple key-recognition steps often work. If they do not, that does not mean you caused the problem; it means the vehicle may need proper key programming or security-system diagnosis.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a safe pause and low-risk troubleshooting only. Later decisions about towing, key replacement, programming, warranty coverage, or repairs may need help from roadside assistance, a reputable automotive locksmith, the dealer, or another qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, locksmith, roadside safety, insurance, or professional advice. Vehicle security and backup start methods vary by make, model, and year, so use your owner’s manual and qualified help if the vehicle still will not recognize the key.
