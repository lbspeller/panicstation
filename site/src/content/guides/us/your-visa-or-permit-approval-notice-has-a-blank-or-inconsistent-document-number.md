# What to do if… your visa or permit approval notice has a blank or inconsistent document number (USA)

## Short answer
Do not rely on the confirmation alone for travel, work, or a deadline until you know which identifier is missing or inconsistent. First separate whether the issue is with a USCIS notice or document, a visa issued by a U.S. embassy or consulate, or a CBP I-94 record, then use that official correction route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume an “approved” message means a visa has already been printed in your passport.
- Do not enter a random number, passport number, receipt number, or A-Number just to make a required field accept something.
- Do not post your approval notice, passport page, visa image, receipt number, or I-94 publicly.
- Do not refile from scratch or pay an unofficial third party before checking the official record and correction route.
- Do not ignore a mismatch that affects travel, work verification, school, benefits, or a deadline; slow down and use the official channel.

## What to do now
1. **Identify what the form or person actually needs.**  
   U.S. immigration paperwork can involve different numbers:
   - **Visa number:** usually appears on the visa foil printed in the passport.
   - **USCIS receipt number:** the 13-character number on USCIS notices of action.
   - **A-Number:** assigned only in some immigration records.
   - **I-94 admission number:** the CBP admission record number for an entry to the United States.  
   If the field only says “document number,” check the help text or ask the airline, employer, school, agency, or platform which document type they are validating.

2. **Check whether you are looking at a confirmation or the actual official document.**  
   - If this is a **USCIS approval notice or USCIS online account notice**, use the receipt number and notice details shown by USCIS.
   - If this is a **visa case handled by a U.S. embassy or consulate**, an “approved” or “issued” status is not the same thing as the visa foil printed in the passport.
   - If this is about an **I-94**, use the CBP I-94 website to retrieve the record after admission, if a record exists for your travel.

3. **If a USCIS notice, document, card, or record has a USCIS typo, use the USCIS Typographic Error e-Request.**  
   Keep a copy of the incorrect item, the correct spelling or number, your receipt number, and the e-Request confirmation. Do not send original identity documents unless USCIS specifically instructs you to.

4. **If USCIS says an item was issued but you did not receive it, use the matching USCIS non-delivery route.**  
   Choose the category that matches what is missing: notice, document, or card. Save the inquiry confirmation and keep a simple list of dates, mailing addresses used, and any tracking information you have.

5. **If the missing number is a visa number, wait for or check the visa foil in the passport.**  
   A visa number is not normally taken from a generic approval email. If the visa foil is printed and the number or biographic details look blank, damaged, or inconsistent, contact the visa service route for the U.S. embassy or consulate that handled the case.

6. **If the missing number is an I-94 number, check whether you have been admitted for this trip.**  
   An I-94 admission record is usually created or updated by CBP after admission. If you have not yet been admitted, a current I-94 for this trip may not exist yet. If you have been admitted and the I-94 record is wrong or missing, use CBP’s I-94 tools and official CBP help routes rather than guessing a number.

7. **If an airline, employer, school, or agency system blocks you, ask what alternate proof it accepts.**  
   Say that the approval is real but the document number field is blank or inconsistent, and ask which specific identifier they need. Offer only the relevant official document, such as the passport visa foil, USCIS notice or card, or CBP I-94 record.

8. **If travel or a start date is very close, contact the official source and keep a contact log.**  
   Use USCIS for USCIS-issued items, the issuing visa post or official State Department visa contact route for visa issuance questions, and CBP for I-94 records. Keep dates, case numbers, receipt numbers, screenshots, and short notes of what each office told you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to hire an attorney, refile, or cancel every plan before checking which number is actually being requested.
- You do not need to send your passport, visa, notice, or I-94 to multiple people who do not need it.
- You do not need to solve later tasks such as a driver licence, bank account, school record, or benefits record until the core identifier is confirmed or corrected.
- You do not need to explain the whole immigration history to every help desk; start with the document type, the blank or inconsistent field, and what you need to do next.

## Important reassurance
A blank or inconsistent “document number” field can be a systems mismatch rather than a denial or loss of status. The safest move is to pause, identify the exact document being requested, and use the official route for that document.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and route the problem to USCIS, the visa-issuing post, the Department of State visa contact route, or CBP. Later decisions may need help from a qualified immigration lawyer, an accredited representative, the airline, an employer, a school, or the agency asking for the document.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, employment, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. U.S. immigration and travel processes vary by benefit type and can change, so use official agency records and written responses as your source of truth.
