# What to do if… you suspect your entry was not recorded or your passport was not stamped (USA)

## Short answer
Check your official CBP I-94 record now, save a copy, and check that your class of admission and “admit until” date look right. If the record is missing or wrong, use CBP’s official help and correction routes rather than trying to create a stamp yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume “no stamp” means you were not admitted.
- Do not leave and re-enter just to try to get a stamp.
- Do not pay an unofficial agent to get a stamp or change a record.
- Do not alter, invent, or backdate any proof of entry.
- Do not ignore a wrong “admit until” date, wrong class of admission, or missing I-94 record.

## What to do now
1. Go to the official CBP I-94 website and retrieve your most recent I-94.
   - Save a PDF or screenshot.
   - Print it if you can.
   - Also save your travel history page if it is available.

2. Check the key details calmly, one at a time.
   - Your name and passport number.
   - Your most recent date of entry.
   - Your class of admission.
   - Your “admit until” date, if one is shown.

3. If the I-94 says “Not Found,” try simple official-search variations before assuming it is missing.
   - Try your name exactly as it appears in your passport.
   - Try without middle names, spaces, hyphens, or titles if they may have been entered differently.
   - Check that the passport number, passport country, and date of birth are entered correctly.

4. Put together a small entry-record folder.
   - Passport photo page.
   - Visa page or approval notice, if applicable.
   - Boarding pass, itinerary, or ticket for the trip into the USA.
   - Any I-94 result you can access, even if it looks wrong.
   - A short note of your arrival date, airport or port of entry, airline, flight number, and route.

5. If the I-94 is still missing or wrong, contact CBP through an official route.
   - Use CBP’s I-94 help page or CBP’s “Ask a Question” page.
   - Choose the I-94 or traveler compliance topic where offered.
   - Describe only the specific issue: missing I-94, wrong class of admission, wrong entry date, or wrong “admit until” date.
   - Upload or keep ready the entry-record folder from step 4.

6. If CBP tells you to contact a Deferred Inspection Site, use the official CBP Deferred Inspection Sites page.
   - Follow the instructions for the relevant site.
   - Some sites may use email, phone, appointments, or in-person review.
   - Deferred inspection is generally for review or correction of documentation or record issues connected to entry.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide your whole immigration plan right now.
- You do not need to complain to the airline or post publicly about it.
- You do not need to ask unofficial people to “verify” your entry.
- Legal advice can usually wait for the immediate record check, unless a filing, work authorization, school record, departure date, or other deadline depends on this record.

## Important reassurance
U.S. passport stamps are not always the main record. For many non-U.S. visitors, the electronic I-94 is the first thing to check, and finding the official record is more useful than worrying about the missing stamp itself.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the situation, preserve proof, and use official CBP routes. Later decisions about status, filings, employment, study, or travel may need qualified immigration help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, or professional advice. Use official CBP information for U.S. admission records and get qualified help if your status, deadline, work, study, or formal application may be affected.
