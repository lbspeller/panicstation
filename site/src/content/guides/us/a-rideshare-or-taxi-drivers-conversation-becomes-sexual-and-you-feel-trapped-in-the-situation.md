# What to do if… a rideshare or taxi driver’s conversation becomes sexual and you feel trapped in the situation (USA)

## Short answer
Your priority is to exit the vehicle and reach a public, well-lit area as quickly as possible. If you are in immediate danger or the driver refuses to stop, call **911** or use the in-app emergency button immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel obligated to maintain social politeness, smile, or answer personal questions.
- Do not confront or escalate the driver if you feel it may lead to physical aggression.
- Do not wait for the situation to worsen before deciding to end the ride.
- Do not accept "shortcuts" or offers to be dropped off in private or secluded areas.
- Do not share your home address or living situation if you have not already reached your destination.

## What to do now
1. **Identify a safe exit point immediately.** **Look for a staffed, well-lit, and busy location such as a gas station, grocery store, or hotel entrance.**
   - Request the driver pull over at the nearest safe spot.
   - Use a neutral excuse such as "I need to use a restroom" or "I am going to be sick" to avoid immediate conflict.
2. **Use the in-app emergency features.** **Tap the safety shield or "Safety Toolkit" icon in the Uber or Lyft app to access the emergency button.**
   - This can connect you to 911 and share your live GPS location and vehicle details with dispatchers automatically in many cities.
3. **Notify a trusted contact of your status.** **Use the "Share Trip Status" or "Trusted Contacts" feature within the app to send your real-time location to a friend or family member.**
   - If the app is not functional, send a text message with the car's make, model, and license plate number.
4. **Ensure the door is not locked.** **Check that the child locks are not engaged by attempting to pull the interior handle or rolling the window down slightly to reach the exterior handle.**
   - If you feel physically restrained, use the emergency button or call 911 immediately while remaining in the back seat.
5. **Exit the vehicle with your belongings.** **Once the car stops at a public location, exit quickly and move toward other people or staff inside a building.**
   - Do not stay near the vehicle to argue or explain.
   - If the driver follows you, alert staff or bystanders and call 911.
6. **Preserve incident details for later.** **Note the driver's name, vehicle license plate, and the approximate time the conversation became inappropriate.**
   - Take a screenshot of the ride details in the app once you are in a secure location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report right now.
- You do not need to respond to any messages or calls from the driver or the rideshare company immediately.
- You do not need to analyze why the situation happened or blame yourself for the driver's behavior.

## Important reassurance
Feeling a "freeze" response or using politeness to manage a threat is a standard survival mechanism; it is not a sign of weakness or consent. Sexual comments in a confined space are a violation of safety boundaries, and your instinct to get out is a valid response to an unsafe environment.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and physical safety. Decisions regarding legal action, formal reporting, or long-term support can be made once you are in a secure, permanent location.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. For confidential support and to discuss your options with a trained specialist, you can contact the National Sexual Assault Hotline (RAINN) at **1-800-656-HOPE (4673)**.
