# What to do if… your mobility scooter stops working while you are out and you cannot walk home (USA)

## Short answer
Move to the safest nearby place you can reach, preferably indoors or away from traffic, then call for accessible help home. Do not try to push the scooter a long distance or keep driving it if it is cutting out, surging, or hard to control.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep attempting to drive if the scooter is cutting out, surging, braking unpredictably, or losing power near traffic or crowds.
- Don’t push the scooter in the street, across busy intersections, or along shoulders where drivers may not see you.
- Don’t accept help that separates you from your phone, keys, medications, wallet, or mobility device unless it is clearly necessary for immediate safety.
- Don’t leave the scooter unattended in public if you rely on it to get home, unless emergency safety makes that unavoidable.
- Don’t stay outside in extreme heat, extreme cold, heavy rain, or an unsafe location just to protect the scooter.
- Don’t spend your remaining phone battery on repeated troubleshooting if you still need to arrange help.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause point.** Aim for a wide, well-lit place away from traffic, such as inside a store, a transit station lobby, a building entrance, a library, a clinic, or another staffed public place.
2. **Stop the scooter if it is moving unpredictably.** Turn it off if that is safe, keep your feet and hands clear of moving parts, and do not switch it into freewheel or manual mode unless you already know how to keep it from rolling.
3. **Do one quick, low-risk check only if you are safe.** Check the key or power button, battery indicator, charger connection if you have it, and whether the drive or freewheel lever was accidentally changed. If you are unsure, stop troubleshooting and focus on getting help.
4. **Call your safest ride or assistance option first.** Try a trusted contact with a vehicle that can carry you and the scooter, your mobility device provider or repair line, a roadside assistance plan you already have, or a local accessible transportation provider.
5. **If you use ADA paratransit, call the dispatch number you normally use.** Say that your mobility scooter has failed, give your exact location, say you have the scooter with you, and ask what urgent or same-day options exist. Same-day service may be allowed in some systems but is not guaranteed.
6. **Ask clearly for the kind of assistance you need.** Say whether you need curbside pickup, help beyond the curb, space for a mobility scooter, or help reaching the vehicle. ADA paratransit is generally origin-to-destination service for eligible riders, but local procedures and immediate availability can vary.
7. **If paratransit is not available now, ask a nearby staff member for practical help.** Ask to wait inside, charge your phone, confirm the exact address or intersection, call an accessible taxi or transport provider, and keep the scooter in a safe visible spot if possible.
8. **Try an accessible taxi or ride option if one exists locally.** When calling, say “wheelchair-accessible vehicle” or “accessible van” and explain that you have a mobility scooter with you. Availability varies by area, so you may need to call more than one provider.
9. **If you are in immediate danger or becoming ill, call 911.** This includes being stuck in or near traffic, being unable to reach shelter in extreme weather, feeling faint, confused, short of breath, or at risk of falling. Tell the dispatcher your location, that you have a mobility disability, and that your scooter has failed.
10. **Make yourself easier to find while you wait.** Share your live location with a trusted person, note a visible landmark or business name, keep your phone accessible, and conserve battery.

## What can wait
- You do not have to identify the exact mechanical or electrical fault right now.
- You do not have to decide today about repairs, warranties, complaints, or replacing the scooter.
- You do not have to finish errands, attend the appointment, or keep travelling if that could strand you again.
- You do not have to negotiate every cost detail while you are still outside or unsafe.
- You do not have to explain your whole medical history to get basic help with location, shelter, and transport.

## Important reassurance
Being stuck without reliable mobility can feel frightening and exposing. For now, it is enough to focus on three things: getting away from immediate danger, getting sheltered, and arranging a way home with the scooter handled as safely as possible.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment your scooter stops working while you are out. After you are safe, repairs, documentation, complaints, and future backup arrangements can wait until you have support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, transport, repair, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe, unwell, exposed to extreme conditions, or unable to protect yourself where you are, seek immediate help.
