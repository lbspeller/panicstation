# What to do if… your phone shows emergency-calls-only service when you know coverage is normal (USA)

## Short answer
Force a single restart while connected to power, then use a computer to perform an "Update" (not Restore) via official manufacturer software to reinstall the operating system without erasing your data.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep forcing restarts if the first attempt fails; repeated hard resets can corrupt the file system further.
- Do not let the battery drain to zero during the loop, as power failure during a boot cycle can permanently damage the storage controller.
- Do not select "Restore" or "Factory Reset" in recovery menus unless you have a verified cloud backup and have already failed the "Update" path.
- Do not use third-party "system repair" software found in search ads; use only official tools from Apple (Finder/iTunes), Google (Pixel Repair Tool), or Samsung (Smart Switch).
- Do not hit or shake the device; a software-induced boot loop is not a loose connection and physical force may trigger hardware damage.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilize power and temperature immediately.**
   **Plug the device into a wall outlet using an original manufacturer cable and adapter.**
   - Remove any protective case to allow heat to dissipate.
   - If the device feels hot to the touch, leave it plugged in but do not attempt any further steps until it feels room-temperature.

2. **Execute one precise forced restart.**
   **Follow the specific button sequence for your device type and then release immediately if the loop continues.**
   - **iPhone 8 and later:** Press and quickly release Volume Up. Press and quickly release Volume Down. Press and hold the Side button until the Apple logo appears.
   - **Modern Android (Pixel/Samsung):** Press and hold both the Power button and Volume Down button simultaneously for 15 seconds.
   - If the phone successfully reaches the lock screen, back up your data immediately. If it returns to the logo or a black screen, proceed to the next step.

3. **Enter Recovery Mode using a computer.**
   **Connect the phone to a Mac or PC and use the manual button triggers to force the "Connect to Computer" or "Recovery" screen.**
   - **iPhone:** Connect to the computer. Repeat the forced restart sequence (Up, Down, hold Side) but do not release the Side button when you see the Apple logo; keep holding until the recovery screen (cable icon) appears.
   - **Android:** Power the device off (or wait for it to black out between loops), then hold Power and Volume Down (or Volume Up on some Samsung models) to enter the Bootloader/Fastboot menu. Use volume keys to select "Recovery Mode" and the Power button to confirm.

4. **Attempt an "Update" or "Repair Apps" installation.**
   **Choose the non-destructive software repair option in the computer interface to overwrite the corrupted update.**
   - **iPhone:** On the computer, a prompt will appear offering to "Update" or "Restore." Select **Update**. This attempts to reinstall the OS while preserving your photos and files.
   - **Samsung:** Use the "Emergency Software Recovery" or "Fixing Device Issues" path in the Samsung Smart Switch desktop application.
   - **Pixel:** Use the official Google Pixel Repair Tool website in a Chrome browser to "Rescue" the software version.

5. **Verify account security if access is restored.**
   **Check your two-factor authentication (2FA) settings as soon as you regain access to the device.**
   - Log into your Apple ID or Google Account from a different trusted device.
   - Generate and save "Backup Codes" if you rely on app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) that may have been de-synced by the crash.

6. **Exercise your warranty rights (USA-specific).**
   **Contact the manufacturer or carrier and explicitly cite a "failed official software update" to trigger warranty coverage.**
   - Under the **Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act**, if a written warranty is provided, the manufacturer must repair or replace a defective product within a reasonable number of attempts.
   - Document the date of the update, the symptoms, and the specific official recovery steps you attempted.
   - If the device is out of the standard 1-year warranty, check if your credit card or mobile carrier protection plan provides "extended warranty" coverage for software-bricked hardware.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new phone purchase while the device is still in the "Update" recovery process.
- You do not need to contact a professional data recovery service until you have confirmed the "Update" path has failed.
- You do not need to worry about permanent data loss until you have exhausted the computer-based "Update" (reinstall) option.

## Important reassurance
A boot loop after an update is usually a "software collision" where the new code failed to hand off tasks to the old hardware state. It does not necessarily mean your hardware is broken or your photos are gone. In most cases, reinstalling the software via a computer stabilizes the device.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for software-induced restart loops only. It does not cover hardware-related loops caused by liquid damage, broken power buttons, or failing batteries.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and is not a substitute for professional technical support. Specific button combinations may vary based on minor hardware revisions or OS versions. Performing a "Restore" or "Factory Reset" will result in total data loss; ensure you have selected "Update" or "Rescue" unless you have accepted the loss of local data.
