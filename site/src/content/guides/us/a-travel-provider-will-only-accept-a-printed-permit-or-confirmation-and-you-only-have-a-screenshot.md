# What to do if… a travel provider will only accept a printed permit or confirmation and you only have a screenshot (USA)

## Short answer
Obtain an official printable PDF or email confirmation and use a self-service kiosk, hotel business center, or retail print location to generate a physical copy immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to edit or alter the screenshot to appear like an official PDF.
- Do not rely solely on your phone's battery or signal once a physical copy has been requested.
- Do not wait until you are at the front of a boarding or security line to seek a printer.
- Do not leave your phone unlocked or provide account passwords to staff to "fix" the document.
- Do not print a cropped or low-resolution image that obscures the barcode, name, or confirmation number.

## What to do now
1. **Request an official re-send of the document in PDF format.** 
   - Use the provider's "Manage Booking" or "My Trips" section in their app or website to trigger a "Resend Confirmation" email.
   - Search your email inbox for the original message using the "confirmation" or "booking reference" number; check "Junk" and "Spam" folders.
2. **Utilize airport self-service kiosks for flight-related documents.**
   - **Locate a kiosk for your specific airline near the check-in counters.**
   - **Enter your six-character confirmation code or scan your government-issued ID.**
   - **Select the option to "Print Boarding Pass" or "Print Documents."**
3. **Use retail print-and-go services for immediate physical copies.**
   - **Forward the PDF or confirmation email to `printandgo@fedex.com` to receive a retrieval code.**
   - **Visit a FedEx Office location and enter that code at a self-service machine to print.**
   - **Alternatively, locate a UPS Store or Staples and ask to forward the document to their specific store email address for guest printing.**
4. **Access local guest printing at hotels or public libraries.**
   - **Ask a hotel front desk clerk for their guest printing email address to forward your document.**
   - **Check for a "Business Center" in the lobby where you can log in to email and print directly.**
   - **Locate a nearby public library; many offer "Mobile Printing" or "Princh" services where you can upload a file from your phone and pay at the desk.**
5. **Verify the quality of the final printout.**
   - **Confirm that your full name, the provider's name, the date of travel, and the booking reference are legible.**
   - **Ensure the barcode or QR code is printed at a standard size and is not blurry or cut off.**
6. **Go to the staffed service counter if automated options fail.**
   - **Present your ID and the screenshot to a staff member at the ticket counter or "Customer Service" desk.**
   - **Explain that you require a physical reprint due to the provider's specific document requirements.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or request a refund for printing fees right now.
- You do not need to reorganize your digital travel folders or delete old screenshots.
- You do not need to investigate the provider's specific terms and conditions regarding digital documents until you have successfully traveled.

## Important reassurance
Document verification snags are a routine part of travel. Most transit hubs and lodging providers are equipped with specific procedures and hardware to help travelers produce physical copies. Once you have the paper document in hand, the procedural barrier is typically resolved.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for obtaining a physical travel document when a digital version is rejected. It does not cover legal disputes, long-term travel insurance claims, or technical troubleshooting for mobile devices.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional travel advice. Requirements vary by provider, and staff at the scene may have limited discretion. Always follow the instructions of airport security or government officials.
