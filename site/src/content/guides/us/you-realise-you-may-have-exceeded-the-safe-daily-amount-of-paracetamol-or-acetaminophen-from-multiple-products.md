# What to do if… you realise you may have exceeded the safe daily amount of paracetamol or acetaminophen from multiple products (USA)

## Short answer
Stop taking all medications containing acetaminophen (often labeled as **APAP**) immediately and call Poison Control at **1-800-222-1222** for case-specific instructions. If the person has yellowing of the eyes/skin, severe abdominal pain, or cannot be awakened, call **911** now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not take any further doses of any medication until you have spoken to a professional.
- Do not wait for symptoms to appear; liver damage from acetaminophen often has no early signs.
- Do not attempt to induce vomiting or use "home detox" remedies.
- Do not drink alcohol, as it increases the risk of liver injury.
- Do not assume a product is safe just because it is for "cold," "flu," or "sleep" and does not say "Tylenol" on the front.
- Do not drive yourself to a hospital if you feel dizzy, confused, or nauseated.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all intake of acetaminophen or APAP immediately.**
   - Put all current medications in a separate area to prevent accidental habit-based dosing.

2. **Identify every product taken in the last 24 hours.**
   - Check the "Active Ingredients" section on the Drug Facts label for **Acetaminophen** or **APAP**.
   - Common sources include:
     - Tylenol (all strengths).
     - Cold and flu "multi-symptom" liquids or caplets.
     - "PM" or sleep-aid medications.
     - Prescription painkillers (often labeled as Hydrocodone/APAP or Oxycodone/APAP).
     - Sinus or allergy combination products.

3. **Calculate your estimated total dose in milligrams (mg).**
   - **Write down the strength of each pill or liquid dose (e.g., 325 mg, 500 mg, or 650 mg).**
   - **Multiply the strength by the number of doses taken.**
   - **Note the time of the first dose and the most recent dose.**
   - If you are unsure of the math, keep the bottles ready for the operator.

4. **Contact Poison Control immediately.**
   - Call **1-800-222-1222** (available 24/7, free, and confidential).
   - Alternatively, use the `webPOISONCONTROL` online tool at `www.poison.org`.
   - Tell them: "I think I have taken more than the daily limit of acetaminophen across several different products."

5. **Monitor for emergency "Red Flag" symptoms.**
   - Call **911** immediately if you observe:
     - Yellowing of the whites of the eyes or the skin (jaundice).
     - Severe pain in the upper right side of the abdomen.
     - Persistent vomiting or inability to keep fluids down.
     - Extreme sleepiness, confusion, or loss of consciousness.

6. **If advised to go to an Emergency Room, take all packaging with you.**
   - **Bring every bottle, blister pack, and remaining pill involved.** This allows medical staff to verify the exact dosage and formulation (such as extended-release vs. immediate-release).

## What can wait
- You do not need to calculate the exact milligram total perfectly before calling Poison Control; an estimate is sufficient to start the process.
- You do not need to determine if you are "definitely" in danger; the purpose of the call is to have an expert make that determination for you.
- You do not need to worry about legal repercussions; Poison Control is a medical service focused on safety.

## Important reassurance
Accidental overdose via "double-doctoring" or mixing OTC products is one of the most common reasons for Poison Control calls in the US. Reaching out before you feel sick is the most effective way to prevent serious complications. Most cases caught early (especially within the first 8 hours) are highly treatable.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and triage instructions. It does not replace the specific medical guidance provided by Poison Control or emergency medical professionals.

## Important note
The FDA maximum daily dose for healthy adults is 4,000 mg in a 24-hour period, though some manufacturers recommend a lower limit of 3,000 mg. If you suspect you have exceeded these amounts, professional assessment is required regardless of how you feel.
