# What to do if… police ask to search your phone on the spot and you feel pressured to agree (USA)

## Short answer
Say clearly: **“I do not consent to a search of my phone.”** Ask: **“Am I free to go?”** Keep any refusal verbal and do not physically resist.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock your phone, hand it over unlocked, or share your passcode just to make the pressure stop.
- Do not use face or fingerprint unlock just to end the conversation.
- Do not physically resist, grab for the phone, or obstruct an officer.
- Do not argue about the law during the encounter.
- Do not lie or provide false documents.
- Do not start deleting, changing, or hiding things on the phone during the encounter.

## What to do now
1. **Use one clear sentence.**  
   Say: **“I do not consent to a search of my phone.”** Repeat it calmly if needed.

2. **Ask whether you can leave.**  
   Ask: **“Am I free to go?”**  
   - If the answer is yes, leave calmly.  
   - If the answer is no, say: **“I am going to remain silent.”**  
   - Keep any legally required identification or driving documents separate from answering questions.
   - If you are arrested or questioned in custody, say: **“I want a lawyer before answering questions.”**

3. **Ask whether there is a warrant.**  
   Say: **“Do you have a warrant to search my phone?”**  
   - If the answer is yes, ask to see it if that is safe and practical.  
   - Do not physically interfere with an officer.  
   - Keep your refusal verbal. Questions about the warrant, its scope, or any claimed exception can be reviewed later with a lawyer.

4. **Do not volunteer access to the phone.**  
   Say: **“I am not consenting to unlock my phone or provide a passcode.”** Rules about compelled passcodes, face unlock, and fingerprint unlock can vary by jurisdiction and circumstances.

5. **Keep identification separate from the phone search.**  
   If you are driving during a traffic stop, provide the required driving documents when asked. In other situations, identification rules can vary by state and circumstances. You can ask: **“Am I legally required to identify myself?”** Do not lie or provide false documents.

6. **If an officer takes the phone, do not escalate.**  
   Ask: **“Which agency are you with?”** and **“Can I have your name and badge number?”** Ask whether you can get a property receipt. When you are away from the encounter, write down the time, place, agency, witnesses, and the words used.

7. **If you already agreed, state any change clearly.**  
   Say: **“I withdraw my consent to any further search of my phone.”** Stop assisting with access. Do not physically interfere. Whether officers may continue can depend on the circumstances and any other legal authority they claim.

8. **If this is at an international border or port of entry, use caution.**  
   Ordinary street-stop rules may not apply there. Say: **“I do not consent to a search of my device or to unlocking it.”** You can ask for a supervisor. Do not physically resist. Get legal advice when you are able.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain your whole situation during the encounter.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to make a complaint.
- You do not need to work out whether a claimed warrant or exception was valid while you are standing with officers.
- You can deal with legal advice, account security, and follow-up after you are away from the encounter and able to think clearly.

## Important reassurance
Feeling pressured does not mean you have done anything wrong. You can keep your words brief, calm, and repetitive. Refusing consent verbally is not the same as physically resisting.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Rules can vary by state and by context, including street stops, traffic stops, arrest, and international-border encounters. If your phone was searched or taken, later decisions may need help from a lawyer familiar with the relevant state and circumstances.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice. Consent can affect what officers may search. Taking a phone and searching its digital contents are different issues. You do not need to agree merely to end pressure, and you can keep any refusal verbal and calm.
