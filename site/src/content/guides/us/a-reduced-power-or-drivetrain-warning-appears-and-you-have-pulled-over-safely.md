# What to do if… a reduced power or drivetrain warning appears and you have pulled over safely (USA)

## Short answer
Stay stopped, keep the hazard lights on, and arrange help. The warning may indicate a fault or limited performance, and the exact meaning varies by vehicle, so do not guess that it is safe to continue driving.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to reach your destination if the warning remains on or the vehicle feels different.
- Do not repeatedly restart the vehicle to try to clear the message.
- Do not attempt repairs on a high-speed shoulder or near moving traffic.
- Do not wait directly in front of or behind the vehicle.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle at the roadside.
- Do not let passengers move around near traffic.
- Do not place warning devices if doing so requires entering traffic or walking along an exposed shoulder.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stopped vehicle visible and secure.** Keep the hazard lights on. Apply the parking brake and secure the vehicle in the appropriate parked position for your vehicle.
2. **Check for immediate danger.** If there is smoke, fire, steam, a strong burning smell, injuries, or a vehicle position that puts you or other road users in immediate danger, call 911. Tell the call-taker your location and that you are with a disabled vehicle.
3. **Choose the safest available waiting place.** If you can safely leave from the side away from traffic and reach a place behind a barrier or well away from moving vehicles, move passengers there and stay together. If leaving the vehicle would expose you to traffic, remain belted inside and ask 911 or roadside assistance for instructions.
4. **Arrange roadside help.** For a routine breakdown without immediate danger, call your roadside assistance provider and request help or a tow. Give your location, the exact dashboard wording, the vehicle make and model, and any symptoms such as shaking, new noises, or loss of power.
5. **Do not continue driving if there are red flags.** Stay stopped if you notice an overheating warning, steam, smoke, a burning smell, a major change in steering or braking, loud mechanical noises, severe vibration, a significant fluid leak, or a vehicle that will not move normally.
6. **Record the essentials when it is safe.** Take a photo of the warning message and note the highway direction, nearest exit or mile marker, and any symptoms. Keep your time outside the vehicle as short as possible.
7. **Follow instructions from the call-taker or responder.** Do not move into traffic or leave your waiting place unless it is safe to do so.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause at the roadside.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop or discuss prices while you are exposed to traffic.
- You do not need to clear fault codes, disconnect the battery, or test repeated restarts now.
- You do not need to decide whether the vehicle can be driven again until you are in a safer place and have checked the owner’s manual or received professional advice.
- You do not need to check for recalls or manufacturer communications until you are away from the roadway.

## Important reassurance
You have already taken the important first step by pulling over. Staying visible, avoiding traffic exposure, and arranging help gives you time to deal with the warning without guessing.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Towing, fault-code checks, recall checks, and repair decisions can wait until you are away from the roadway and may need help from a roadside provider or repair professional.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Vehicle systems and state rules vary. In an immediate emergency, call 911 and follow the call-taker’s instructions.
