# What to do if… you notice someone filming or recording into your home through a window (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the window, get everyone to a safer spot inside, and call 911 if it is happening now, the person is on your property, or you feel at risk.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to confront them or try to physically stop them.
- Don’t chase them or try to trap them on your property.
- Don’t open the door to see who it is or to argue.
- Don’t move closer to the window to get a better photo or video.
- Don’t post identifiable images online while it is unfolding; save what you have for police or building security.

## What to do now
1. **Get distance and cover immediately.** Step away from any window they can see into. If you can close blinds or curtains without moving into view, do that; otherwise go to an interior room.
2. **Bring others with you.** Move children, vulnerable adults, and pets away from windows and into the safest available room, preferably an interior room with a solid door.
3. **Lock down the home from inside.** Lock doors and accessible windows. If you can turn on exterior lights from inside, turn them on.
4. **Call for help.**
   - **If it is happening now, they are on your property, you feel threatened, or you are worried they may try to enter:** call **911**.
   - **If they have left and there is no immediate danger:** report it through your local police department’s non-emergency number. Look for it on your city, county, or police department website; do not call 911 just to get the non-emergency number.
   - **If you cannot speak safely:** call 911 and stay on the line if you can. If text-to-911 is available where you are, text only when calling is not possible. Give your exact location and what is happening in plain words. If the text does not go through, call 911.
5. **Give dispatch the key facts.** Say your exact address, that someone is trying to film into your home through a window, where the person is or was, whether they are in your yard, porch, driveway, or shared building area, and whether anyone inside is at immediate risk.
6. **From a safe position, note details.** Write down or remember clothing, height, build, vehicle details, what they were holding, and the direction they left. Do not put yourself back in view to get a better description.
7. **Preserve what you already have.** Save doorbell, security camera, or phone clips without editing them. Keep the original file if you recorded from inside. Write down the time it started and ended while it is fresh.
8. **If you are in an apartment, condo, dorm, hotel, or managed building, alert security or management.** Keep it simple: “Someone is outside filming into windows at [location]. Police have been called / are being contacted.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now what specific crime it is; privacy, trespass, voyeurism, harassment, and stalking laws vary by state, city, and the facts.
- You do not need to identify the person, confront neighbors, or investigate where they live.
- You do not need to decide tonight about privacy film, cameras, lighting, landlord requests, trespass warnings, or longer-term safety planning.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel shaken or violated after someone tries to record into your home. Moving away from the window, keeping the door closed, and getting help are enough for the first few minutes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the first minutes to hours. If this repeats, feels targeted, or connects to harassment or stalking, later decisions may need help from local police, a victim advocate, building management, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, safety, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, call 911.
