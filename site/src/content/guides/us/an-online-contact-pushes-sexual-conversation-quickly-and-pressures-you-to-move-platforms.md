# What to do if… an online contact pushes sexual conversation quickly and pressures you to move platforms (USA)

## Short answer
Disengage immediately without explanation and remain on the current platform to maintain safety protections. Document the interaction with minimal screenshots, then block and report the user through the platform’s official safety tools.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t move to an encrypted or secondary app (like Telegram, WhatsApp, or Snapchat) just because they insist it is "safer" or "more private."
- Don’t send any sexual images, videos, or perform actions on camera to "de-escalate" or satisfy their demands.
- Don’t share personal identifiers such as your full name, employer, school, home address, or links to other social media accounts.
- Don’t pay money, send gift cards, or transfer cryptocurrency if they threaten to release images or information.
- Don’t delete the conversation history until you have captured necessary evidence for reporting.
- Don’t argue, negotiate, or try to "shame" them into stopping; this often escalates the behavior.

## What to do now
1. **Create an immediate communication vacuum by stopping all replies.** Do not send a "goodbye" or explain why you are stopping; simply cease all interaction.
2. **Capture minimal essential evidence of the behavior.** Take screenshots of the user's profile, their unique username or ID, and the specific messages where they pushed for sexual content or pressured you to change platforms.
3. **Use the platform's internal report function immediately.** Select the most accurate category, such as "Harassment," "Sexual Solicitation," or "Threats and Blackmail," and attach your screenshots if the tool allows.
4. **Block the account and any secondary accounts they have mentioned.** This prevents them from seeing your status and limits their ability to continue the pressure.
5. **Set all your social media profiles to "Private" for at least 48 hours.** Remove your phone number and email from public viewable fields and disable "contact syncing" or "find me by email/phone" features in your settings.
6. **Protect your images if threats of sharing have been made.** 
   - **If you are 18 or older:** Use **StopNCII.org** to create a "hash" (digital fingerprint) of any intimate images you are concerned about; this helps participating platforms block them automatically.
   - **If you or the person involved is under 18:** Use the **NCMEC "Take It Down"** service to help remove or prevent the sharing of explicit images.
7. **Report extortion or threats to federal authorities.** File a report with the **FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)** at ic3.gov or submit a tip via **tips.fbi.gov**. 
8. **Contact a confidential support service to process the event.** Call the **RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline** at 800-656-HOPE (4673) for anonymous, non-judgmental support and guidance on your rights.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify who the person "really" is right now.
- You do not need to decide if you will tell your friends or family today.
- You do not need to analyze the messages to figure out where the conversation "went wrong."
- You do not need to worry about the person’s reaction to being blocked or reported.

## Important reassurance
Pushing for rapid sexual escalation and platform switching is a documented tactic used by bad actors to bypass safety filters. If you feel startled, embarrassed, or anxious, those are normal physiological responses to a boundary violation. Taking the step to block and report is the most effective way to regain control and protect your digital space.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. If you are being actively extorted, if intimate images have already been shared, or if you feel in physical danger, you may need to engage local law enforcement or specialized legal counsel.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate physical danger or a crime is occurring in your physical location, call 911.
