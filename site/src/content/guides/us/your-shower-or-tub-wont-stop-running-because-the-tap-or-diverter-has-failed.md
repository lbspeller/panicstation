# What to do if… your shower or tub won’t stop running because the tap or diverter has failed (USA)

## Short answer
Close the water supply immediately at the fixture’s local shutoff valves or the home’s main water shutoff valve. Once the flow is stopped, contact a licensed plumber or building maintenance to replace the failed cartridge or diverter.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to disassemble the faucet or handle while the water is still pressurized and flowing.
- Don’t use pliers or wrenches to force a stuck handle; this can snap the valve stem and cause a higher-pressure leak.
- Don’t stand in pooled water while touching any electrical switches, outlets, or appliances.
- Don’t ignore a "trickle" that won't stop, as it can escalate to a full failure or cause hidden structural rot.
- Don’t use the "street-side" valve in a meter box (closest to the road) as this is usually reserved for the utility company.

## What to do now
1. **Contain the water flow to the drainage area.**  
   **Direct the showerhead toward the center of the tub or stall and close the curtain or door.** This prevents splashing onto the floor while you work on the shutoff.
2. **Check for "screwdriver stops" behind the handle plate.**  
   **Remove the decorative metal plate (escutcheon) against the wall if possible to see if there are two small screw-heads on the valve body.** If present, turn these screws **clockwise** with a flat-head screwdriver to stop water at the fixture.
3. **Locate and close an access panel valve.**  
   **Search the wall directly behind the shower (often in a closet or hallway) or in the ceiling below for a removable panel.** Look for two handles (hot and cold) and turn them **clockwise** until they stop.
4. **Shut off the main water supply to the home.**  
   **Locate the main valve where the water line enters the building (basement, garage, or utility closet near the water heater).** 
   - **Lever/Ball Valve:** Turn the handle 90 degrees so it is **perpendicular** to the pipe.
   - **Wheel/Gate Valve:** Turn the wheel **clockwise** until it is tight.
5. **Relieve system pressure to confirm the shutoff.**  
   **Open a sink faucet on the lowest level of the home to drain the remaining water in the lines.** The shower may continue to drip for several minutes as the vertical pipes empty.
6. **Notify emergency repair services.**  
   **Contact a 24-hour emergency plumber or your property management’s emergency maintenance line.** State clearly if you have successfully isolated the water or if the main valve is stuck.
7. **Address the water heater if the hot water was running long-term.**  
   **Turn the water heater to "Pilot" (gas) or flip the dedicated breaker to "Off" (electric) if the tank has been drained significantly.** This prevents the heating elements from burning out while the water is shut off.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific brand or part number (cartridge vs. diverter) until a professional arrives.
- You do not need to mop up minor dampness immediately; focus on stopping the primary flow first.
- You do not need to worry about the water bill impact in the first hour; safety and containment are the priorities.

## Important reassurance
Shower valve failures are a standard plumbing emergency and are typically caused by a worn-out rubber seal or a cracked plastic cartridge. Once the water is shut off, the immediate danger of flooding is gone, and the repair is a routine task for a professional.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a runaway water event. It does not provide instructions for the mechanical repair or replacement of internal plumbing components.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only. If water has entered electrical outlets, light fixtures, or the ceiling of the floor below, do not touch any electrical panels; exit the area and contact an electrician or the fire department immediately.
