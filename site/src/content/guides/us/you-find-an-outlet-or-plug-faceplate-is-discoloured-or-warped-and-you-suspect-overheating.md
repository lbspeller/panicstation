# What to do if… you find an outlet or plug faceplate is discoloured or warped and you suspect overheating (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the outlet immediately and shut off the power at the main breaker panel if it is safe to do so. Do not touch or attempt to unplug anything that feels hot, is melting, or is emitting a fishy or burning odor.

## Do not do these things
- Don't touch the outlet, faceplate, or plug if you see smoke, sparks, or if the plastic looks softened.
- Don't attempt to unplug a device if the plug feels stuck or "welded" to the outlet.
- Don't use a different outlet on the same wall or circuit to "finish a task."
- Don't reset a circuit breaker that has tripped until a professional has inspected the wiring.
- Don't use water or a standard fire extinguisher on an electrical hazard unless it is a designated Class C extinguisher and you are in immediate danger.
- Don't ignore a "fishy," "metallic," or "burnt sugar" smell near walls or appliances.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate and call emergency services if you see active fire or heavy smoke.** **If you see flames, active arcing (bright blue flashes), or thick smoke, alert all occupants and call 911 immediately from outside the building.**
2. **Stop using the outlet and keep a safe distance.** **Do not plug anything new into the outlet or touch the existing plug if the faceplate is discolored, warped, or hot to the touch.**
   - If the device is running and the cord/plug is NOT hot, you may turn off the device using its own power switch.
   - If there is any heat, odor, or visible damage, do not touch the device or cord.
3. **Shut off the power at the circuit breaker panel.** **Locate your electrical panel and switch the specific breaker for that room to the "OFF" position.**
   - If the breakers are not clearly labeled, or if you are unsure which one controls the outlet, switch off the "Main" breaker to cut power to the entire property.
   - Only approach the panel if there is no smoke, heat, or buzzing sounds coming from the panel itself.
4. **Identify and record the sensory cues.** **Take a mental note of what you observed to tell the electrician later.**
   - Common cues include yellow or brown scorch marks, a "fishy" or "metallic" smell, bubbling paint on the wall, or a faint buzzing/sizzling sound.
5. **Secure the area from accidental use.** **Place a piece of tape over the outlet or hang a "DO NOT USE" sign to ensure no one else interacts with it.**
   - Warn all household members that the circuit must remain off.
6. **Contact a licensed electrician for an emergency inspection.** **Call a qualified professional to evaluate the receptacle, the internal wiring, and the appliance that was in use.**
   - If you are a renter, notify your landlord or property manager immediately after the power is secured, as this is a primary safety hazard.

## What can wait
- You don't need to determine the exact cause (e.g., loose wire vs. overloaded circuit) before seeking help.
- You don't need to unscrew the faceplate to look at the wires; this should only be done by a professional once power is verified off.
- You don't need to replace the outlet yourself.
- You don't need to decide if the appliance is "broken" or just the outlet is at fault.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency or alarm when noticing electrical damage. Discoloration and warping are clear physical signals that the system's safety thresholds have been reached. By cutting the power now, you have significantly reduced the risk of the situation escalating into a fire.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and risk reduction for suspected outlet overheating. It does not replace a physical inspection by a licensed electrician, which is required to ensure the circuit is safe to re-energize.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only. Electrical work in the USA is governed by the National Electrical Code (NEC). Always hire a licensed professional for repairs. If you suspect an active fire inside the wall, do not wait; evacuate and call 911.
