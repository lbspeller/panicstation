# What to do if… your cloud documents are read-only because your storage quota is full (USA)

## Short answer
Do not panic-delete. First download or export the documents you need today, then use the provider’s storage page to confirm the quota problem and remove only items you are sure you no longer need, or use the smallest temporary storage upgrade available if you urgently need editing restored.

## Do not do these things
- Do not bulk-delete whole folders before checking what is actually using space.
- Do not permanently empty Trash, Recycle Bin, or Recently Deleted areas until you have checked what is inside.
- Do not keep editing the same document on several devices while sync is failing.
- Do not reset devices, wipe apps, or sign out everywhere as your first response.
- Do not give passwords, verification codes, payment details, or remote access to an unexpected caller, text, email, pop-up, or search-result “support” number.

## What to do now
1. **Protect the files you need today.**
   - Download or export the specific read-only documents you need right now.
   - Save the copy somewhere local, such as your computer or an external drive.
   - If the file is extremely important, save a second copy in a different account or device you control.

2. **Confirm whether this is storage quota, not permissions.**
   - Open the provider’s official storage or account storage page while signed in directly.
   - Check whether the account is over its storage limit.
   - If only one file or folder is read-only, check whether you are in the right account and whether your edit access was changed by the owner.

3. **Find the biggest safe storage win.**
   - Use the storage breakdown or storage management view.
   - Sort by largest files, large email attachments, photos, videos, backups, or shared items if the provider offers those categories.
   - Download anything uncertain before deleting it.

4. **Deal with deleted-item areas carefully.**
   - Check whether the provider says Trash, Recycle Bin, spam, or Recently Deleted items still count against storage.
   - Permanently clear only the items you are sure you do not need.
   - If storage is shared across products, check the linked products too, such as email, photos, backups, or cloud drive files.

5. **Use the web storage page if the app is confusing.**
   - Try cleanup from the provider’s official website rather than only the mobile app.
   - Wait a little after deleting and permanently clearing items, because storage totals may not update instantly.
   - Refresh the storage page and the document before assuming the cleanup failed.

6. **Use a temporary unlock only if you need it.**
   - If you urgently need to edit and cleanup is blocked or too slow, consider the smallest short-term storage upgrade the provider offers.
   - After the urgent work is safe, check the plan terms and cancellation or downgrade controls before relying on it long term.

7. **Reduce version-conflict risk while stabilizing.**
   - Pause sync on devices showing quota or upload errors.
   - Work from only one copy of the document until the cloud service is accepting edits again.
   - Rename any temporary local working copy clearly, for example with “local copy” and today’s date.

8. **Keep records for support or billing.**
   - Screenshot the quota exceeded or read-only notice.
   - Screenshot the storage breakdown and any unexpected subscription, renewal, or billing message.
   - Note when the problem started, which account is affected, and which devices showed sync errors.

9. **Escalate through the right channel.**
   - For a work or school account, contact your IT administrator or help desk because storage limits, retention, and deleted-item rules may be controlled by the organization.
   - For a personal account, use the provider’s official support or billing channel from its website or app.
   - If a paid service dispute remains unresolved, gather your records and use a general consumer complaint route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganize your whole cloud account today.
- You do not need to choose a permanent paid plan before the urgent document is safe.
- You do not need to fix every device at once; first protect the needed document, then get the account under quota, then check sync.
- You do not need to build a perfect backup system right now.

## Important reassurance
A sudden read-only lock can feel like data loss, but it is often a storage restriction rather than the files disappearing. Unless the service says files were deleted or the account is suspended, you may still be able to download files and restore editing after the account gets back under its storage limit or the quota is increased.

## Scope note
These are first steps to protect access, avoid accidental deletion, and regain editing. Later decisions about backups, paid plans, account migration, workplace retention settings, or consumer complaints may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not technical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Cloud storage rules, timing, recovery options, and billing controls vary by provider and account type.
