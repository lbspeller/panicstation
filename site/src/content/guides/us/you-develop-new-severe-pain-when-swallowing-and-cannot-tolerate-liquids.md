# What to do if… you develop new severe pain when swallowing and cannot tolerate liquids (USA)
## Short answer
Go to an emergency department now. Call 911 instead if you have trouble breathing, noisy breathing, cannot swallow your saliva, are drooling because you cannot swallow, have rapidly worsening throat or neck swelling, feel faint or confused, or cannot get to the emergency department safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep testing your swallowing with repeated sips.
- Do not force food, drinks, pills, or home remedies down.
- Do not try to clear a suspected stuck item with bread, rice, banana, your fingers, or an object.
- Do not lie flat if you are coughing, choking, or unable to swallow saliva. Stay upright.
- Do not make yourself vomit if a cleaner, chemical, or unknown substance may have been swallowed.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, very weak, confused, or unsafe to travel.

## What to do now
1. **Stop eating and drinking for now.** Do not force swallows. Difficulty swallowing liquids can create choking and aspiration risk.
2. **Sit upright.** If you cannot swallow your saliva, spit or drool into a tissue or container rather than forcing a swallow.
3. **Call 911 now** if you have trouble breathing, noisy breathing, cannot swallow your saliva, are drooling because you cannot swallow, have rapidly worsening throat or neck swelling, feel faint or confused, or cannot travel safely.
4. **Otherwise, arrange transport to an emergency department now.** At triage, say: **“I have new severe pain when swallowing and I cannot tolerate liquids.”**
5. **Tell the emergency team about any possible trigger:** food or a bone feeling stuck, a pill feeling stuck, a very hot drink or food, forceful vomiting, or a cleaner, chemical, or unknown substance.
6. **If a cleaner, chemical, medicine, or unknown substance may have been swallowed, call Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 while arranging emergency care.** Because you cannot tolerate liquids, do not make yourself vomit or take anything by mouth unless Poison Help or a clinician tells you to. Keep the product container nearby if it is safe to do so.
7. **Bring the minimum useful information:** when the pain started, what you last ate or drank, any choking episode, fever, voice change, neck swelling, allergies, your medication list, and any relevant packaging.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause yourself.
- You do not need to try gargles, sprays, lozenges, or other home remedies.
- You do not need to decide about antibiotics or other treatment now.
- You do not need to make plans beyond getting assessed safely.

## Important reassurance
New severe pain when swallowing with an inability to tolerate liquids is a valid reason to seek emergency assessment. You are not overreacting by getting checked promptly.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from an emergency clinician or a Poison Help specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for professional care. Call 911 for breathing difficulty, inability to swallow saliva, drooling because you cannot swallow, rapid worsening, or any other immediate danger.
