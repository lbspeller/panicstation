# What to do if… you see water dripping from a ceiling light or recessed light (USA)

## Short answer
Treat water dripping from a ceiling light or recessed light as a shock and fire risk. Keep away from the fixture and shut off power at the breaker panel only if you can reach it from a dry, safe place, then get urgent help to stop the leak and have the electrical parts checked before power is restored.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the light, trim, bulb, switch, outlet, or dripping water while the circuit may be energized.
- Do not touch someone who may be receiving an electric shock; call 911 from a safer place.
- Do not stand in water, on a wet floor, or on a ladder under the leak to investigate.
- Do not try to drain the fixture, pull down a recessed light, remove trim, or open the ceiling.
- Do not flip a tripped breaker back on to see if it works.
- Do not use nearby switches, outlets, fans, or appliances if water may have reached them.
- Do not assume it is safe because the breaker did not trip.

## What to do now
1. **Move people and pets away from the area.** Keep clear of the dripping light, wet flooring, wet walls, and anything plugged in nearby.
2. **Leave the room immediately if there are danger signs.** Get out if you see smoke, flames, sparks, repeated arcing, a swollen or sagging ceiling, or smell strong burning.
3. **Shut off power only from a dry, safe position.**
   - If you can reach the electrical panel without walking through water or touching wet surfaces, switch off the breaker for that room or lighting circuit.
   - If you cannot identify the right breaker quickly, switch off the main breaker from a dry, safe position.
   - Do not stand in water or touch wet surfaces to reach the panel.
4. **If you cannot reach the panel safely, get shutoff help.** Call your electric utility and say water is dripping through a ceiling light and you cannot safely shut off power. Stay out of the wet area until power has been isolated.
5. **Stop the water source if you can do it without going near the fixture.**
   - If it may be a bathroom, sink, toilet, washer, or pipe above, stop using that water source.
   - Shut off the nearest fixture valve or the main water shutoff only if you know where it is and can reach it safely.
   - In an apartment or condo, contact building maintenance, the property manager, and the unit above immediately.
6. **Contain water only from a safe distance.** Put a bucket under the drip if you can do it without getting close to the light or standing on a wet surface. Use towels on nearby dry flooring only if you can stay away from the fixture.
7. **Call the right repair help urgently.**
   - Call a qualified electrician, licensed where required, to inspect the light, recessed housing, junction box, switches, breakers, and any water-affected wiring before power is restored.
   - Call building maintenance, a plumber, or a roofer to find and stop the leak source.
8. **Call 911 if there is fire, electric shock, collapse, or immediate danger.** Leave first, then call from a safer place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact leak source before moving away and shutting off power safely.
- You do not need to remove the light, cut drywall, test switches, or dry wiring yourself.
- You do not need to decide on permanent repairs right now.
- Photos, insurance details, landlord paperwork, and cleanup decisions can wait until the area is safe; do not approach the fixture just to document it.

## Important reassurance
This is alarming, but the first steps are simple: keep distance, avoid water and electrical parts, shut off power only if it is safe, and get help to stop the leak. Once the area is isolated and the water source is being handled, the situation is more controlled, but power should stay off until a qualified electrician says it is safe.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about wiring, recessed housings, insulation, drywall, mold, roof repairs, plumbing repairs, landlord duties, or insurance may need qualified electrical, plumbing, roofing, building, landlord, or insurance help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, electrical, insurance, medical, or other professional advice. If you cannot make the area safe, or there are signs of fire, shock, sparking, burning, or ceiling collapse, get to a safer place and contact emergency services and qualified professionals.
