# What to do if… you hear a loud new grinding or scraping noise while driving (USA)

## Short answer
Treat a new loud grinding or scraping noise as a possible brake, wheel, tire, or undercarriage hazard. Keep the stop as safe as you can, check only from a safe position, and arrange roadside help or a tow unless the cause is clearly harmless.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving at normal speed if the sound is loud, metallic, worsening, or linked to braking or steering.
- Don’t crawl under the vehicle on the roadside.
- Don’t touch the wheel, tire, brake area, exhaust, or anything that may be hot.
- Don’t stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or step into a lane to look or listen.
- Don’t try to pull out metal, wire, or debris from near a wheel if the vehicle could move, the part is hot, or traffic is close.
- Don’t accept help from an unknown or unofficial tow truck you did not request; keep doors locked until you can confirm who they are.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safer first.** Put hazard lights on. If the vehicle is partly in a lane, on a narrow shoulder, near a blind curve, or otherwise exposed to traffic, call **911** and say you are stopped in an unsafe position.

2. **Look for immediate danger before inspecting anything.** From a safe place, check for smoke, flames, sparks, a burning smell, a rapidly deflating tire, fuel smell, fluid pouring out, or a growing puddle. If you suspect fire, a fuel leak, injury, or immediate danger, move people away from the vehicle if you can do that safely and call **911**.

3. **Do a quick outside-only check from the safest side of the vehicle.** Use your phone flashlight if needed. Look for:
   - a flat tire, shredded tire edge, bulge, missing tread, or wheel tilted oddly
   - something wedged near a tire or brake area
   - loose plastic trim, splash shield, heat shield, or other undercarriage piece dragging on the road
   - fluid dripping steadily
   - a strong burning smell near a wheel or under the vehicle

4. **Only move an obvious loose piece if it is safe and reachable.** If you can clearly see a loose plastic panel or trim piece scraping the ground, and you can move it without going under the vehicle, without stepping into traffic, and without touching hot parts, you may lift or tuck it out of the way. If it is metal, near a wheel, near the exhaust, hot, sharp, stuck, or not obvious, leave it alone.

5. **Use this “do not drive” rule.** Do not drive if the noise sounds like metal-on-metal, the vehicle pulls to one side, steering feels wrong, braking feels different, a tire looks damaged, a warning light is on, you see leaks, or there is smoke or a burning smell.

6. **Call roadside assistance or a tow.** Use your insurer, auto club, vehicle roadside button or app, or a trusted tow provider. Give the highway or road name, direction of travel, nearest exit, mile marker, intersection, landmark, and whether the vehicle is in a lane or on the shoulder.

7. **Wait in the safest available place.** The safest place depends on traffic, weather, barriers, lighting, and the shoulder width. If you can safely reach a protected area well away from traffic, such as behind a barrier, go there. If you cannot safely move away, stay buckled in the vehicle with hazards on and follow instructions from 911, police, or roadside assistance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical cause right now.
- You do not need to compare repair shops, approve major repairs, or decide whether to keep the vehicle long term while you are on the roadside.
- You may need to approve a tow or a safety-critical roadside service, but detailed repair choices can wait until the vehicle is in a safer place.
- Later, if the failure seems unexpected and may be a safety defect, you can report it to NHTSA after the immediate situation is over.

## Important reassurance
A loud new grinding or scraping sound is a valid reason to stop and choose caution. Even if the cause turns out to be minor, avoiding normal driving until the vehicle is checked can prevent a worse roadside problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps for roadside safety and damage prevention only. Later decisions about diagnosis, repair, insurance, towing costs, or defect reporting may need help from a qualified mechanic, roadside provider, insurer, or relevant public agency.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, mechanical, or professional advice. If you are stopped somewhere unsafe, your vehicle is partly in a lane, or there is smoke, fire, injury, fuel smell, or a suspected fuel leak, call 911.
