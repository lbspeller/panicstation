# What to do if… your flight is delayed and you might miss your onward train, bus, or car rental (USA)

## Short answer
Get the most realistic arrival time, then make the onward leg flexible before buying anything new. Your first goal is to avoid a rushed, non-refundable purchase while you still have change, rebooking, refund, or overnight options.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume a U.S. airline must pay for meals, hotels, ground transport, or missed separate bookings; support often depends on the airline and whether the delay was within the airline’s control.
- Don’t accept a travel credit, voucher, or rebooked flight if what you want is a refund and you have not decided yet.
- Don’t buy a new onward train, bus, shuttle, hotel, or rental-car booking until you have checked whether the existing booking can be moved.
- Don’t abandon a confirmed rebooking path unless you have a better option confirmed in the app, by message, or in writing.
- Don’t rely on memory; keep screenshots, receipts, booking references, boarding passes, and delay notices together.

## What to do now
1. **Get the realistic arrival time.**  
   Check the airline app, text alerts, gate screen, or airline message thread. Screenshot the updated departure time, estimated arrival time, flight number, and the time the screenshot was taken.

2. **Work out whether the onward leg is protected or separate.**  
   - If the next flight is on one ticket or one booking, ask the airline to rebook you through to the ticketed destination.
   - If the onward train, bus, shuttle, tour transfer, cruise transfer, or car rental is a separate booking, assume you must contact that provider yourself.

3. **Ask the airline for the two decisions that matter now.**  
   Use simple wording: “What is the earliest available option that gets me to my destination, including partner options if available?” Then ask: “Do you provide meal vouchers or a hotel if this goes overnight?” Airline commitments for controllable delays and cancellations vary by airline.

4. **If the delay is significant and you choose not to travel, ask about a refund before accepting alternatives.**  
   Say: “I’m choosing not to travel because of the delay. Please tell me my refund option before I accept a credit or rebooking.” DOT generally treats a late arrival of 3 hours or more for a domestic itinerary, or 6 hours or more for an international itinerary, as a significant delay for refund purposes if you choose not to travel or accept the alternative.

5. **Contact the onward provider with one clear request.**  
   Tell them your flight is delayed and ask for the fastest way to save the booking:
   - move the train or bus ticket to a later departure
   - hold or change the shuttle or transfer
   - note a late pickup or after-hours pickup for the rental car
   - move the booking to the next morning if the last service is unrealistic

6. **Check the last safe onward option.**  
   Find the last bus or train, the rental-car counter closing time, the shuttle cutoff, and whether the provider has after-hours instructions. If your updated arrival makes those unrealistic, switch to a safer plan such as staying near the airport and taking morning onward transport.

7. **If you are stuck on the aircraft, do not try to leave unless the crew says it is safe.**  
   DOT tarmac-delay rules generally require airlines to give passengers a chance to get off before 3 hours on domestic flights and before 4 hours on international flights, with safety, security, and air-traffic-control exceptions.

8. **Start one receipts-and-proof folder.**  
   Save screenshots, delay notices, rebooking confirmations, onward-provider messages, hotel receipts, transport receipts, and meal receipts. If you may use travel insurance or credit-card trip coverage, keep the proof now and read the claim rules later.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to argue every policy detail while you are stranded; the immediate priority is rerouting, changing the onward booking, or safely stopping for the night.
- You don’t need to file a formal DOT complaint at the gate unless there is an immediate safety or discrimination issue; collect proof first and deal with complaints later.
- You don’t need to decide today whether to pursue insurance, credit-card coverage, or a refund dispute beyond asking what your current options are.
- You don’t need to solve the whole trip at once; solve the next safe step.

## Important reassurance
A delayed flight can make the whole trip feel as if it is collapsing, especially when the onward booking is separate. You still have useful moves: confirm the arrival time, ask for airline options, contact the onward provider, and avoid spending money until the old booking cannot be saved.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about refunds, insurance claims, credit-card claims, complaints, or missed separate bookings may need help from the airline, the onward provider, your insurer, your card issuer, or another relevant specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, travel-agent, or professional advice. Refund rights, airline support, onward-provider changes, and insurance coverage can depend on the facts, the fare, the booking terms, the airline’s commitments, and whether you accept an alternative.
