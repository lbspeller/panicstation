# What to do if… many conflicted copy versions of your files appear across your folders overnight (USA)
## Short answer
Disconnect the affected device from the internet. Pause syncing where the sync app offers that option, then leave the affected folders alone while you check what happened.

## Do not do these things
- Do not bulk-delete duplicate or “conflicted copy” files yet. One of them may be the version you need.
- Do not rename, move, or reorganize large numbers of files yet. That can make it harder to compare versions and understand what changed.
- Do not open and re-save many files across multiple devices.
- Do not run cleanup scripts, deduplication tools, resets, or reinstalls yet.
- Do not connect backup drives while ransomware or malware is suspected.

## What to do now
1. Disconnect the affected device from Wi-Fi, Ethernet, or mobile data. Where the sync app offers a pause control, pause syncing.

2. Stop editing the affected folders on your other devices for now. If the folders are shared, ask collaborators not to edit or reorganize them until you have checked what happened.

3. Record a small sample before changing anything else. Note a few affected filenames, the folders they are in, visible timestamps, added device names, “conflicted copy” wording, and any sync error messages.

4. Check whether this may be more than a sync conflict. If many files suddenly will not open, a ransom message appears, or files are changing in a way you cannot explain, keep the device offline and contact workplace IT or a qualified technician.

5. If there are unfamiliar sign-ins, security alerts, or unexplained account changes, use another trusted device to review the cloud account. Change the password, turn on multifactor authentication, and sign out unfamiliar sessions if the provider offers those controls.

6. Check the provider’s available activity, version-history, or conflict-resolution tools for a few important files first. Compare versions and identify when the duplicates began. Provider features vary, so do not start a broad restore until you understand the affected timeframe.

7. If this is a work, school, or organization account, contact the IT or help desk before making further changes. If ransomware is suspected, you may also report it through the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center or contact a local FBI field office.

## What can wait
- Choosing the correct copy among hundreds of files.
- Deleting duplicates or merging versions.
- Reconnecting other devices to the affected folders.
- Connecting backup storage or making a large safety copy.
- Running cleanup tools or reinstalling sync software.
- Restoring an entire folder or account to an earlier point.
- Asking collaborators to resend everything.

## Important reassurance
A large number of conflicted copies can happen when cloud-sync services receive competing edits from different devices or users. Pausing further changes first gives you space to inspect a few files carefully before making larger decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce further file changes and preserve recovery options. A full recovery may need help from your cloud provider, workplace IT team, or a qualified technician.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, cybersecurity, financial, or professional advice. If you see signs of ransomware or an unauthorized account change, prioritize isolation and qualified help over trial-and-error fixes.
