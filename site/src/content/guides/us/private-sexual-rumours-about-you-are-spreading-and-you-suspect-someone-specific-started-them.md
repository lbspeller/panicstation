# What to do if… private sexual rumours about you are spreading and you suspect someone specific started them (USA)

## Short answer
Do not confront the person you suspect yet. Save proof quietly, stop engaging publicly, and use the most relevant route now: platform reporting, school or college support, workplace HR, or emergency help if you feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not call out the suspected person publicly or post “receipts”; it can spread the rumor further.
- Do not argue in comments, direct messages, or group chats; screenshots of the argument can become new material.
- Do not forward the rumor to “show people what happened”; that can amplify it.
- Do not keep searching repeatedly once you have enough to report; constant checking can worsen panic.
- Do not threaten anyone, post private information, or encourage other people to confront them.
- Do not delete your own relevant messages yet if you may need to show what happened.

## What to do now
1. **Pick one steady helper.** Message one trusted person: “Private sexual rumors about me are spreading. I need help staying calm while I save evidence and report it.”
2. **Save what is already visible.** Take screenshots showing the post or message, username, date, time, platform, group name, and URL if there is one. Put them in one private folder and start a simple note with what happened and when.
3. **Stop feeding the thread.** Do not reply in the public space. If one sentence is needed, use: “That rumor is false and I’m addressing it through the proper channels.” Then stop responding.
4. **Use platform or group tools.** Report the content through the app or site. Mute the thread if needed. Block accounts if it will not stop you from preserving evidence you already need. In a group chat, message an admin or moderator privately and ask them to remove the posts and stop further sharing.
5. **If this involves a school, college, or education program, ask for the Title IX Coordinator or student conduct office.** Say: “Sexual rumors about me are spreading and it is affecting my access to school. I need to know who handles Title IX or student conduct concerns.” Ask what supportive measures are available now, such as changes to contact, classes, housing, campus locations, or group participation.
6. **If this involves work, report it through a workplace route.** Tell HR, a manager, or another reporting contact: “Sexual rumors about me are being spread at work. I am concerned this may be sexual or sex-based harassment, and it is affecting my ability to work. I want it handled formally and as confidentially as possible.” Ask who will handle it, how to report new incidents, and what interim steps can reduce contact or exposure.
7. **If you feel threatened, stalked, or physically unsafe, use emergency or police help.** If there is immediate danger, call 911. If there is no immediate danger but there are threats, stalking, nonconsensual intimate images, or repeated harassment, contact your local police non-emergency number and ask how to make a report.
8. **If a school or workplace does not respond, consider an outside complaint route.** For education settings, you can ask the U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights about filing a complaint; OCR complaints ordinarily must be filed within 180 days of the last act of discrimination, though OCR can consider waiver requests. For workplace harassment, you can contact the EEOC; EEOC deadlines can be as short as 180 calendar days, and some situations have different time limits.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove who started it today.
- You do not need to share sexual details to defend yourself.
- You do not need to decide today whether to sue, report to police, leave school, quit work, or make a public statement.
- You do not need to monitor every mention; choose one check-in time or ask your helper to watch for major new posts.
- You do not need to answer everyone who asks about it.

## Important reassurance
Sexual rumors can create shame and urgency, especially when you suspect a specific person. Using quiet documentation and formal channels is a reasonable way to protect yourself without making the rumor bigger.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce harm, preserve options, and get through the immediate situation. Later decisions about school processes, workplace complaints, platform escalation, police reports, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
