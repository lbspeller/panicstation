# What to do if… a detention facility tells you mail or funds for someone in custody were rejected and action is needed (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the message through the facility's official contact channel, identify the specific regulatory violation (content, ID number, or vendor), and confirm the person's current physical location before attempting to resend.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use any link or phone number provided in a text or email notification to make a payment.
- Don’t send a second payment through the same channel until you have confirmed why the first one failed.
- Don’t share your account password or social security number with anyone claiming to be "corrections staff" or a "vendor technician."
- Don’t mail cash, personal checks, or stamps unless you have verified the specific facility’s 2026 allowance for these items.
- Don’t ignore a formal rejection notice; most systems have a 10–15 day window to appeal the decision.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the notice through an independent official source.**
   - **Look up the facility's main phone number via a .gov or official county website.**
   - **Call the facility and ask for the "Mailroom" regarding correspondence or the "Business Office / Inmate Trust Fund" regarding money.**
   - **Check the official Inmate Locator (BOP for federal, or State DOC website) to ensure the person has not been transferred to a different facility or a processing center.**

2. **Obtain the specific rejection code or reason.**
   - **Ask the staff for the exact reason for the rejection, such as "unauthorized vendor," "prohibited content," or "incorrect ID number."**
   - **Request a copy of the written "Notice of Rejection" if you are the sender; regulations usually require the facility to notify both the sender and the recipient.**
   - **Identify if the rejection was due to 2026 physical mail limits, such as page counts, paper size (standard 8.5x11), or the ban on "security" envelopes.**

3. **Confirm the required identifier and address format.**
   - **Verify the exact ID format required (e.g., Federal Register Number, State SID, or County Booking Number).**
   - **Ask if mail must be sent to the facility directly or to a third-party "Mail Processing Center" for digital scanning.**
   - **Confirm that the "return address" on the envelope is complete and matches the name of the person who funded the deposit.**

4. **Correct and resend funds through authorized 2026 vendors only.**
   - **Verify which electronic vendor (e.g., Access Corrections, JPay, Viapath/GTL) is currently contracted for that specific facility.**
   - **If federal (BOP), ensure the "Account Number" field in Western Union or MoneyGram follows the strict "RegisterNumber+LastName" format (e.g., 12345678DOE) with no spaces.**
   - **Ask for the "Facility Code" or "Site ID" if using a mobile app to resend.**

5. **Fix mail content triggers before resubmitting.**
   - **Remove all stickers, labels, glitter, perfume, or tape from the envelope and letter.**
   - **Check if photos must now be sent through a specific digital vendor (e.g., Pelipost) rather than included in the envelope.**
   - **Ensure there is no "third-party correspondence" (letters from other people) inside your envelope, as this is a common 2026 security rejection trigger.**

6. **Follow "Special Mail" protocols for legal documents.**
   - **Mark the envelope clearly as "Special Mail" or "Legal Mail — Open Only in Presence of Inmate" if it contains court or attorney documents.**
   - **Verify if the state requires a specific QR code or registration (e.g., TextBehind) for legal mail to be accepted without being photocopied.**

7. **Note the appeal deadline.**
   - **Identify the date on the rejection notice and calculate the deadline for an appeal (often 15 days).**
   - **If the rejection appears to be an error, submit a written appeal to the Warden or the designated Mail Appeal Officer as instructed on the notice.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to hire an attorney to resolve a simple clerical rejection (like a missing ID number).
- You do not need to decide on a long-term communication strategy; focus only on the immediate failed transaction.
- You do not need to resend the entire package if only one specific item (like a photo) was the cause of the rejection; wait for the reason first.

## Important reassurance
Mail and fund rejections are high-frequency events often caused by automated scanning errors, recent facility transfers, or minor formatting mistakes. These issues are generally procedural rather than disciplinary, and obtaining the specific rejection code usually allows for a quick fix.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for responding to a rejection notification. It does not cover long-term legal advocacy or the resolution of lost funds that have already been successfully processed by a vendor but not credited by the facility.

## Important note
Jail and prison regulations are jurisdiction-specific and subject to frequent change. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Always prioritize the facility's current written "Inmate Handbook" or "Visitor Information" page for the most accurate 2026 protocols.
