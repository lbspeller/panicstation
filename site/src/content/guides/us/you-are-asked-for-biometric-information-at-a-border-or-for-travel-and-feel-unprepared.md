# What to do if… you are asked for biometric information at a border or for travel and feel unprepared (USA)

## Short answer
Pause and confirm that the request is part of official U.S. entry processing. Ask the officer what biometric information will be collected and whether alternative processing is available in your situation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide fingerprints or facial images through a message link, QR code, website, or payment request unless an officer confirms that it is part of the official process.
- Do not pay a stranger or third-party fixer to process your entry or arrange biometrics.
- Do not leave the inspection area or ignore an officer’s directions while trying to resolve the request.
- Do not sign a form before asking the officer to explain anything you do not understand.
- Do not delete travel documents, boarding passes, or messages in a rush.

## What to do now
1. Ask the person requesting the biometric information to identify the agency and confirm whether the request is part of U.S. entry inspection.
2. Ask what will be collected, such as a facial image or fingerprints, and whether alternative processing is available in your situation.
3. Keep your passport, travel document, and any entry paperwork together. Show them when the officer asks.
4. Look for posted CBP signage or informational material near the inspection point. If the request came through a message or QR code, ask an officer to confirm it before using it.
5. If you feel overwhelmed, say that you need clarification. Ask whether you may step aside briefly while still following the officer’s directions.
6. If you cannot complete the requested step, explain the practical problem clearly and ask the officer what options are available.
7. After you leave the inspection area, write down the location, approximate time, agency, and what you were told if you still have concerns.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a complaint or request records.
- You do not need to settle broader privacy concerns while you are being processed.
- You do not need to research every possible rule before asking the officer for the next clear instruction.

## Important reassurance
Feeling unprepared does not mean you need to rush. Confirming who is asking, what will be collected, and whether alternative processing is available is a reasonable way to handle the immediate situation.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a biometric-information request during entry processing for the United States. The process can vary by traveler and setting, and later questions may need help from a qualified immigration professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, immigration, or other professional advice. Follow official instructions at the port of entry and ask an officer to clarify anything you do not understand.
