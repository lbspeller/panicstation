# What to do if… someone threatens to share your intimate images with family, friends, or your employer (USA)

## Short answer
Stop replying. If it is safe, save the threatening message and account name, then block the sender. Reporting and removal options can wait until you are ready.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay money, send gift cards or cryptocurrency, or send more images to try to make the threat stop.
- Do not negotiate, threaten back, or keep replying to explain yourself.
- Do not download, forward, or ask someone else to view intimate images.
- Do not delete the threatening messages before saving the basics if it feels safe to do so.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** If you are in immediate physical danger or someone is actively trying to locate you, call 911.
2. **Save only the basics if it feels safe.** Keep a screenshot of the threat, the account name, the demand, and any report confirmation before blocking; do not download or forward intimate content.
3. **Stop contact.** If you choose to report the account on the platform, use the report option for harassment, blackmail, or intimate-image abuse before blocking when practical. Then block the sender. If you have already blocked them, do not unblock them solely to make a report.
4. **Secure any account they may be able to access.** Change the password, turn on multifactor authentication, check recovery details, and review who can see your contacts or send you messages.
5. **Choose a reporting route if you want to make a report.**
   - For an adult online-crime report, you may use the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center.
   - If the images involve anyone under 18, or the person being targeted is under 18, you or a trusted adult can make a report to NCMEC CyberTipline.
6. **Choose a removal tool if it fits your situation.**
   - If the person shown was under 18 when the image or video was taken, including if they are now an adult, use Take It Down only for content already on the device. Do not download or ask someone to send content solely to submit it.
   - If you are currently over 18, were over 18 in the content, are shown in the content, and have access to it, StopNCII may help with eligible real or synthetic intimate images on participating platforms.
7. **Decide whether a brief workplace warning would reduce pressure.** If the sender is threatening your employer and you choose to tell someone at work, keep it short: “I am being harassed online and someone may send private or fake material. Please do not engage with the sender and route any message to HR.”
8. **Reach out for immediate emotional support if you need it.** You can call, text, or chat with 988 for emotional distress or if you need someone to talk to.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to tell family, friends, or colleagues.
- You do not need to write a long explanation or post publicly.
- You do not need to identify the sender or collect everything perfectly.
- You do not need to decide on legal steps while you are overwhelmed.

## Important reassurance
This is not your fault. It is understandable to feel frightened, ashamed, or frozen when someone uses a threat like this. You can take one step at a time and choose which support routes feel right for you.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about platform escalation, workplace communication, or legal options may need specialist help once the immediate pressure has reduced.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
