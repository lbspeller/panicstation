# What to do if… your intimate photos or videos are shared without your consent in a group chat (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, then focus on containment: report the content in the app, ask for removal once, keep only key details, and use child-safety or image-hashing help if it fits your situation.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue for a long time in the group chat; it can escalate attention and re-sharing.
- Don’t send the photos or videos to anyone “for proof.”
- Don’t download, forward, save, or ask anyone to send you intimate images that show someone under 18.
- Don’t pay, send more images, or comply with threats if someone is blackmailing you.
- Don’t repeatedly open the content to check who has seen it; it can make panic worse and does not usually help containment.
- Don’t assume you have no options. Reporting, removal requests, and specialist tools may still help.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause first.** Lock your screen, move away from anyone looking over your shoulder, and contact one trusted person who can sit with you or help you take the next steps. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

2. **Ask for deletion once, clearly.** In the group chat, you can post: *“This was shared without my consent. Delete it and do not forward it.”* Do not debate it. If there is a group admin, moderator, teacher, manager, or server owner, message them directly and ask them to remove the content and restrict the sender.

3. **Report the content inside the app or service.** Use the report, safety, help, admin, or moderator tools to report the media and the account for non-consensual intimate imagery, image-based abuse, sexual content shared without permission, harassment, or blackmail.

4. **Keep only the key details.** Write down the platform, group name, usernames, profile links if visible, date, time, and what was said. If you can do it without capturing the intimate image itself, screenshot chat context showing who shared or threatened to share it. Store this privately and do not forward it.

5. **If you are under 18, or the image or video was made when you were under 18, use child-safety routes.** Report to NCMEC’s CyberTipline. You can also use NCMEC’s Take It Down service for eligible nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos made before you were 18. Use the original device if you have it; do not download or share the image just to use the service.

6. **If you are being threatened or blackmailed, stop responding.** Keep the threatening messages, block only after you have saved the key details if you can do that safely, report the account in the app, and consider reporting to local law enforcement or the FBI. If anyone involved is under 18, also use NCMEC’s CyberTipline.

7. **If you are 18 or older, consider StopNCII.org.** It can create a digital fingerprint of an intimate image or video on your device and share that fingerprint with participating platforms to help detect and remove matching content when it violates their policies.

8. **Use the platform-removal route and FTC route where relevant.** Certain platforms should have a way to request removal of non-consensual intimate images, and may be required to remove the image and known identical copies within 48 hours of a valid request. If a platform does not provide a removal process, the process is broken, or the platform does not remove the content after a valid request, you can report that platform issue to the FTC.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to confront the person, go public, tell everyone, or pursue legal action.
- You do not need to find every copy before asking for help.
- You do not need to write a perfect explanation to the group. One clear removal request is enough.
- You do not need to decide long-term school, work, relationship, or legal steps in this first moment.

## Important reassurance
This is a serious violation. Panic, shame, anger, numbness, or feeling exposed are common reactions to a sudden loss of privacy. The responsibility is on the person who shared or threatened to share the images, and there are established routes that may help slow the spread and request removal.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about state-specific legal options, school or workplace reporting, safety planning, counselling, or other specialist help can come after the immediate containment steps.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**. If you feel unsafe with yourself or might hurt yourself, call or text **988** for the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. If the images involve anyone under 18, do not forward them; use child-safety reporting and removal services.
