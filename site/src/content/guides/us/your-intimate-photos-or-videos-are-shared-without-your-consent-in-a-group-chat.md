# What to do if… your intimate photos or videos are shared without your consent in a group chat (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate yourself from the chat, capture essential proof without re-distributing the media, and use platform reporting tools to trigger federal 48-hour removal mandates.

## Do not do these things
- Don't delete the group chat or the messages immediately; you may need the usernames and timestamps for the report.
- Don't send the images to friends or family to "show" them what happened; this creates new illegal copies.
- Don't negotiate with anyone demanding money or more photos (sextortion); payment does not guarantee deletion.
- Don't engage in an argument with the sender; this often prompts them to forward the content to more people.
- Don't try to "hack" or "shutdown" the person's account yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe, private location and take a sixty-second pause.** If you feel you are in immediate physical danger from someone in the chat, call **911** now.
2. **Identify the specific platform and group details.** Look for the following observable cues:
   - The platform name (e.g., Snapchat, WhatsApp, Discord).
   - The specific group name and the unique usernames of the sender and any admins.
   - Any "receipt" or "viewed" indicators showing how many people have seen the content.
3. **Save proof of the distribution without saving the intimate media itself.** Take screenshots of the chat logs that show:
   - The sender’s profile/username.
   - The date and time the images were posted.
   - Any text or threats accompanying the images.
   - Do not take screenshots of the actual naked images or videos; the context (usernames and timestamps) is what matters for reporting.
4. **Report the content to the platform’s safety team immediately.** Use the in-app "Report" function specifically for "Non-Consensual Intimate Imagery" (NCII). Under the **TAKE IT DOWN Act (TIDA)**, most major platforms are required to review and remove NCII within 48 hours of a valid report.
5. **Contact specialized removal services based on your age.** 
   - If you are under 18: Submit a report to the **NCMEC CyberTipline** or use the **Take It Down** tool at NCMEC.
   - If you are 18 or older: Use **StopNCII.org** to generate a "hash" (digital fingerprint) of the content on your device to help participating platforms block re-uploads.
6. **Block the sender and any group participants who are harassing you.** Once you have recorded the usernames and timestamps, use the platform's block feature to prevent further direct contact.
7. **Contact the CCRI Image Abuse Helpline for procedural support.** Call **1-844-878-2274** (Cyber Civil Rights Initiative) for guidance on state-specific laws and technical removal steps.
8. **Contact the FBI if money is being demanded.** If the person is threatening to share more images unless you pay them (sextortion), file a report at **tips.fbi.gov** or call **1-800-CALL-FBI**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to press criminal charges today.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your parents, employer, or school right this second.
- You do not need to find every single person who might have seen the images.
- You do not need to delete your entire social media presence.

## Important reassurance
What happened is a violation of your privacy and, in many cases, a violation of federal and state law. Feeling a sense of panic, shame, or "freezing" is a standard physiological response to a sudden digital trauma. You are not at fault for the actions of the person who shared your private media, and there are established legal and technical pathways to stop the spread.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and containment for non-consensual sharing. It does not cover long-term legal litigation, school/workplace disciplinary procedures, or comprehensive mental health recovery.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). If the images involve a minor, additional federal mandatory reporting rules apply; do not forward the images to anyone, including law enforcement, unless specifically instructed by them through an official channel.
