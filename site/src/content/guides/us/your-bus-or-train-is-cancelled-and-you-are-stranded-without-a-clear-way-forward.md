# What to do if… your bus or train is cancelled and you are stranded without a clear way forward (USA)

## Short answer
Move to the safest staffed place you can reach, then get the carrier to confirm your next option in writing or in the app. Before spending money on a last-minute alternative, save proof of the cancellation and ask whether the carrier can rebook, reroute, or record a refund or voucher request.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait alone outside, on an empty platform, or at an isolated stop if you can move to a staffed, lit, public place.
- Do not leave a safe staffed area just to “figure it out” unless the place is closing, unsafe, or staff direct you elsewhere.
- Do not throw away tickets, emails, app notices, baggage receipts, or screenshots.
- Do not make a voluntary cancellation in the app until you know whether the carrier has already marked the trip as cancelled or heavily delayed.
- Do not buy several backup tickets at once unless you need one immediate safe option and can afford the risk that some costs may not be refunded.
- Do not argue about blame at the counter; focus on getting a confirmed next trip, documented cancellation, or safe waiting plan.

## What to do now
1. **Make a safe base first.**  
   Go inside the station or terminal near staff, lighting, security, and other travelers. If there is no building, move to a nearby open public place such as a staffed hotel lobby, diner, coffee shop, or store, and keep your phone charged.

2. **Confirm the cancellation through an official route.**  
   Check the carrier app, official website, departure board, staffed counter, text alert, or email. Screenshot the cancellation or delay notice, your ticket, your booking number, and the time shown on your phone.

3. **Ask the carrier for one clear next action.**  
   Use the counter, official chat, phone line, or app and ask:
   - “Can you put me on the next departure?”
   - “Can you reroute me through another station or terminal today?”
   - “If no trip is available, how do you record this as a cancellation or disruption?”
   - “Will this be a refund, voucher, rebooking, or something I need to request later?”

4. **If it is Amtrak, avoid becoming a no-show.**  
   Amtrak fare rules generally depend on fare type and timing, and unused tickets that are not cancelled before departure can lose value. If Amtrak has not already re-accommodated you or told you a different disruption process applies, ask staff or use the official cancellation process before the scheduled departure passes, then save the confirmation.

5. **If it is Greyhound or FlixBus-branded intercity bus service, check whether this is voluntary cancellation or carrier disruption.**  
   Greyhound’s standard online cancellation and change route generally works up to 15 minutes before scheduled departure and is usually handled as a voucher, with the amount depending on timing and fees. If the bus itself was cancelled or seriously delayed, ask first whether you can withdraw from the trip, be rebooked, or have the disruption recorded before you press cancel yourself.

6. **If you may be stranded overnight, switch to shelter and safety.**  
   Ask when the station or terminal closes, where passengers are allowed to wait, and whether staff know the nearest safe open place. If you need lodging, choose the closest safe option you can reach without a long late-night trip across town.

7. **If you are in immediate danger, call 911.**  
   If someone is threatening you, following you, blocking you from leaving, or you need urgent police, fire, or ambulance help, call 911 from where you are. Give your location as clearly as you can.

8. **Send one calm status message.**  
   Send one person your location, carrier, booking status, and next check-in time. For example: “Trip cancelled. I’m safe at the station. I’m asking for rebooking now. I’ll update again after I have the next option.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to write a complaint tonight.
- You do not need to decide whether the carrier was legally at fault right now.
- You do not need to find the perfect route; first secure a safe place, a confirmed next trip, or a documented cancellation path.
- You do not need to explain everything to everyone; one reliable contact is enough.
- You can sort out reimbursement, vouchers, card disputes, or formal complaints after you are safe and rested.

## Important reassurance
A short-notice cancellation can feel trapping, especially late at night or away from home. You only need to solve the next safe step: where you will wait, what the carrier has recorded, and what confirmed option you have next.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first hour of a travel disruption. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, replacement travel, missed connections, lodging costs, or insurance may need specialist help or careful checking of the carrier’s current rules.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Carrier rules can vary by company, route, fare type, delay cause, ticket seller, and timing; ask the carrier to state your options plainly and keep a record of what they tell you.
