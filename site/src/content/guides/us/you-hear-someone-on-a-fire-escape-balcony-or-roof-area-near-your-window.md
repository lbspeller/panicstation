# What to do if… you hear someone on a fire escape, balcony, or roof area near your window (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside, move away from the window, and treat it as a possible immediate safety issue until you know otherwise. If the person is still there, you hear tampering, or you feel unsafe, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the window or door to check.
- Do not step onto the balcony, fire escape, or roof area.
- Do not confront the person or announce where you are.
- Do not stand at the glass to record.
- Do not delay calling 911 while you call friends, neighbors, building staff, or a landlord first.
- Do not assume it is harmless because the area is shared or reachable from another unit.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from the window immediately.** Take yourself and anyone with you to an interior room, hallway, bathroom, or another spot behind a solid wall and out of sight of the glass.
2. **Make the room near the window less visible only if you can do it safely.** Turn off nearby lights from where you are. Close blinds or curtains only if you can do it without stepping close to the window or being seen.
3. **Quietly secure what is already within reach.** Lock doors and windows you can reach without returning to the risky window. Use a nearby window pin, secondary lock, or door chain only if it is already close and safe to reach.
4. **Call 911 if there may be an immediate threat.** Say your exact address, unit number, floor, and that you are inside while someone appears to be on the fire escape, balcony, or roof area near your window.
5. **Give the dispatcher the specific sounds you noticed.** Mention rattling, screen movement, pulling at a frame, voices, footsteps, climbing sounds, or movement toward another window or door. Do not hang up until the call-taker tells you to.
6. **If you cannot safely speak, text 911 where available.** Calling is usually best if you can safely call. If you need to text, put your address and unit number first, then the emergency, and answer the call-taker’s questions. If text-to-911 is not available where you are, you may receive a bounce-back message telling you to contact emergency services another way.
7. **Use cameras or building systems only from safety.** If you can check a peephole camera, intercom camera, hallway camera, or indoor security app without going near the window or delaying 911, do that from your safer spot.
8. **After 911 is contacted, alert building staff or security if they exist.** Ask them to check roof doors, stairwells, fire escape access points, and whether hallway, roof, or exterior access footage can be saved.
9. **Write down details while you wait from a safe place.** Note the time, what you heard, where it seemed to move, any voices, and anything distinctive. Do not go back to the window to get more information.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to file a report, complain to the landlord, talk to neighbors, or contact an HOA.
- You do not need to go outside afterward to confirm what happened.
- You do not need to solve the building security issue tonight unless emergency responders or building staff tell you to take a specific immediate step.
- Lock changes, lighting, cameras, maintenance requests, and follow-up messages can wait until the immediate situation is over.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat unknown movement near your window as serious without needing to prove what it is first. The immediate goal is simple: stay inside, stay away from the glass, stay out of sight, and get help involved if there may be danger.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate moment only. Later decisions about police reports, building security, landlord communication, or repeated trespass may need help from local emergency responders, building management, a tenants’ organization, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you believe a crime is in progress or you feel threatened, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
