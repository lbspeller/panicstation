# What to do if… a stranger stands too close while you enter a PIN or passcode (USA)
## Short answer
Stop entering the passcode or PIN. Cancel the transaction if needed, secure your card or phone, and move to a more public or staffed area.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep entering your PIN or passcode while someone is crowding you.
- Do not hand your card or phone to the person.
- Do not follow them to a quieter place.
- Do not stay merely to avoid seeming rude.
- Do not physically confront them if you can step away safely.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the entry.** Take your hands off the keypad or screen. At an ATM or payment terminal, press **Cancel** or end the transaction. Secure your card and phone.
2. **Move away.** Step away from the ATM, terminal, or screen and go toward staff, security, or a busier public area.
3. **Ask for space or assistance.** In a store, say: “I need space to enter my PIN.” Ask staff or security for help if the person keeps moving closer.
4. **Change the setup before trying again.** At an ATM or checkout, use a different machine or terminal in a safer location. For a phone or device passcode, wait until you have moved away and have privacy.
5. **Avoid an unusual card reader.** Do not use a reader or keypad that looks loose, crooked, damaged, added-on, or otherwise unusual.
6. **Protect a card PIN that may have been seen.** Contact your bank or card issuer promptly using a trusted contact route. Ask whether you should lock or freeze the card and change the PIN.
7. **Protect a phone or device passcode that may have been seen.** From a safer place, change the passcode and keep the device secured. Use the device settings or the device maker’s support page if needed.
8. **Call 911 in an emergency.** If the person follows you, threatens you, blocks you from leaving, or you otherwise need immediate police assistance, move toward safety and call **911**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to complete the transaction now.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to make a non-emergency report.
- Once you are safe, you can note the location, time, what happened, and a brief description if that may help with a later report.
- You can check your account activity after you have moved to a safer place.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to stop and move away when someone stands too close during a private entry. You do not need to prove their intentions before protecting your space, card, account, or device.

## Scope note
These are immediate first steps only. Later decisions may need help from your bank, card issuer, device provider, local law enforcement, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, prioritize reaching a safer place and calling 911.
