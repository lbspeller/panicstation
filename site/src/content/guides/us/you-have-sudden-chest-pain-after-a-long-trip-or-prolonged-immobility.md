# What to do if… you have sudden chest pain after a long trip or prolonged immobility (USA)

## Short answer
Call 911 immediately if you experience sudden chest pain or shortness of breath following travel or immobility exceeding four hours. These symptoms require emergency evaluation to rule out a pulmonary embolism (blood clot in the lung) or heart attack.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself or ask a friend to drive you to the emergency room.
- Do not massage or apply heat to a swollen or painful leg.
- Do not walk around or exercise to see if the pain improves.
- Do not wait for the symptoms to subside before seeking professional help.
- Do not take nitroglycerin if you have used erectile dysfunction medication (e.g., sildenafil, tadalafil) within the last 48 hours.
- Do not take aspirin until you have spoken to a 911 dispatcher or medical professional.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately and state your location and primary symptom.** Tell the dispatcher you have sudden chest pain following a period of prolonged immobility or travel.
2. **Describe specific sensory cues to the operator.**
   - Mention if the pain is "pleuritic" (becomes sharper or worse when you take a deep breath or cough).
   - Report any visible swelling, redness, or warmth in one leg.
   - Mention any coughing up of blood or feelings of lightheadedness.
3. **Sit down and minimize all physical movement.** Stay in an upright or slightly reclined position that allows for the easiest breathing.
4. **Unlock your front door and clear a path for emergency responders.** If you are alone, do this quickly and then return to a seated position; do not exert yourself by cleaning or moving furniture.
5. **Follow the dispatcher's specific instructions regarding aspirin.** If they advise it, chew and swallow one full adult aspirin (324mg) or four baby aspirins (81mg each) unless you have a known allergy or bleeding disorder. 
6. **Gather your identification and a list of current medications if they are within arm's reach.** Do not go into another room or search through drawers to find them.

## What can wait
- Determining whether the pain is muscular, gastric, or vascular.
- Notifying your employer or travel coordinator about your status.
- Looking up specific flight or travel duration details.
- Deciding which hospital you would prefer to visit; EMS will determine the appropriate facility.

## Important reassurance
Hesitation or second-guessing is a normal physiological response to sudden medical symptoms, but it does not mean the situation is minor. Dispatchers are trained to prioritize these calls, and emergency teams prefer to evaluate a "false alarm" than a delayed emergency.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes following symptom onset. A hospital evaluation involving imaging and blood tests is necessary for a definitive diagnosis.

## Important note
This information is not a substitute for professional medical advice or diagnosis. Always follow the direct instructions of the 911 operator and arriving emergency medical personnel.
