# What to do if… you are being pressured to meet in person by someone who already has intimate images of you (USA)

## Short answer
Do not meet them in person. Move to a safer location, document all threats immediately, and contact specialized support services or law enforcement to manage the coercion safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to any meeting location to "explain yourself" or "get the images back."
- Do not send more images, videos, or money to satisfy a demand.
- Do not delete any messages, accounts, or call logs before they are documented.
- Do not use any links, apps, or websites the person sends you.
- Do not attempt to confront or "trap" the individual yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your immediate physical safety.**  
   **If you believe they know your location or are nearby, go to a public, well-lit area or a police station and call 911.**
   - Do not stay in a location where the person expects you to be.
   - Disable all GPS and "find my" location sharing features on your phone and social media apps.

2. **Stop all active negotiation or debating.**  
   **Once you have stated you will not meet, do not continue to reply to their threats or demands.**
   - Every response provides the person with more information about your emotional state.
   - If a boundary message is necessary, keep it brief: "I am not meeting you. Do not contact me again."

3. **Document the evidence quickly before it is deleted.**  
   **Take screenshots of the entire conversation, the person’s profile or account handle, the specific threats made, and any proof that they possess the images.**
   - Include the date, time, and the platform where the communication occurred.
   - Save these screenshots to a secure cloud folder or a device the individual cannot access.

4. **Report the threat to federal authorities.**  
   **Contact the FBI directly for time-sensitive threats involving physical meetups or coercion.**
   - Call 1-800-CALL-FBI (1-800-225-5324) or report online at tips.fbi.gov.
   - For non-immediate cyber-crimes, file a report at the Internet Crime Complaint Center (ic3.gov).

5. **Involve NCMEC if you or the images involve anyone under 18.**  
   **If the images were taken when you were a minor, or if you are currently under 18, report the incident to the CyberTipline.**
   - Use report.cybertip.org or call 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678).
   - Use the "Take It Down" tool at takeitdown.ncmec.org to help remove images taken when you were under 18.

6. **Contact a confidential sexual violence support line.**  
   **Speak with a trained specialist at RAINN for safety planning and emotional grounding.**
   - Call 800-656-HOPE (800-656-4673).
   - Use the confidential online chat at online.rainn.org.
   - Text "HOPE" to 64673.

7. **Use specialized tools to prevent image spread if you are 18 or older.**  
   **If the images were taken when you were 18 or older, use StopNCII.org to proactively block them on participating platforms.**
   - This tool creates a digital "hash" (fingerprint) of the image on your device so platforms can detect and block it without anyone ever seeing the actual image.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to press charges or take the case to court.
- You do not need to figure out exactly how they got the images or who they really are today.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your family, friends, or employer until you feel safe and ready.
- You do not need to have "perfect" or organized evidence before calling a support hotline.

## Important reassurance
Blackmail and sextortion are designed to make you feel isolated and powerless. The person's power depends on your silence and your panic. By stepping away from the screen, documenting the evidence, and involving professional support, you are taking back control and significantly reducing the risk of both physical and digital harm.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for the immediate period following a threat. It does not cover long-term legal strategies or recovery.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. If you are in immediate danger of physical harm, call 911 or visit your local police department.
