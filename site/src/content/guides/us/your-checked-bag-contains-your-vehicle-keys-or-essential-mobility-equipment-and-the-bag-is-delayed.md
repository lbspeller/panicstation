# What to do if… your checked bag contains your vehicle keys or essential mobility equipment and the bag is delayed (USA)

## Short answer
File a mishandled baggage report **at the airport desk immediately** to obtain a file reference number, and explicitly state that the bag contains **essential mobility equipment or vehicle keys** to trigger urgent handling and disability assistance protocols.

## Do not do these things
- Don't leave the airport without a **Property Irregularity Report (PIR)** or a printed file reference number.
- Don't accept an airline's claim that they have a "daily limit" (like $50) for incidental expenses; federal rules require reimbursement for all **reasonable and verifiable** costs.
- Don't assume your car keys or mobility aid are subject to the standard baggage liability cap ($4,700); assistive devices on domestic flights are covered up to their **original purchase price**.
- Don't struggle to move through the terminal if your mobility aid is missing; you are legally entitled to **immediate guided or wheelchair assistance** to your next point of transport.
- Don't wait more than 24 hours to escalate if your wheelchair or scooter is missing; federal rules require delivery within **24 hours** for domestic arrivals.

## What to do now
1. **Report the delay at the airline’s Baggage Service Office before leaving the terminal.**
   - Request a formal **file reference number** (often an 8-10 character alphanumeric code).
   - Ensure the agent provides a printed copy or email confirmation of the report.
2. **Explicitly flag the contents as "Essential Assistive Technology" or "Urgent Vehicle Keys."**
   - Ask the agent to add a manual "Urgent" note to the electronic file specifically citing the need for the medical device or keys.
   - For mobility aids, ensure the report specifies the type (manual, electric, scooter) and any battery requirements.
3. **Request a Complaint Resolution Official (CRO) if you face resistance regarding disability needs.**
   - Airlines are required to have a **CRO available** (in person or by phone) during all operating hours to resolve disability-related disputes.
   - Use this if staff refuse to provide loaner equipment or immediate terminal assistance.
4. **Secure immediate terminal assistance for your departure from the airport.**
   - Request wheelchair or guided assistance from the baggage area to the **ground transportation curb, rental car center, or parking shuttle**.
   - If your personal wheelchair is delayed, the airline must provide a safe and adequate seating accommodation while you wait.
5. **Confirm the delivery plan and contact details on the official record.**
   - Verify that your **local delivery address** and a working **mobile phone number** are in the system.
   - Confirm if you want the bag delivered to your home/hotel or if you prefer to pick it up at the airport (airlines must offer you the choice for mobility aids).
6. **Arrange alternative transportation if your vehicle keys are in the bag.**
   - Switch to a **taxi, ride-hail, or accessible shuttle** immediately rather than waiting at the airport.
   - Save the receipt; while airlines often exclude "valuables" from liability, costs for transportation caused by the delay of the bag may be reimbursable as "incidental expenses."
7. **Document the current state in under 3 minutes.**
   - Take a photo of your **baggage claim checks** (the small stickers).
   - Take a photo of the **Property Irregularity Report (PIR)** given by the agent.
   - Note the exact time you filed the report.
8. **Keep itemized receipts for all essential replacement purchases.**
   - Only buy what is necessary to maintain function and safety until the bag arrives.
   - Ensure receipts show **individual items** purchased, not just a total amount.

## What can wait
- You don't need to file a formal DOT complaint in the next hour; focus on getting to your destination safely first.
- You don't need to calculate the total value of the bag's contents right now; that is only required if the bag is declared "lost" (usually after 5-14 days).
- You don't need to request a baggage fee refund manually; if the delay exceeds **12 hours (domestic)** or **15-30 hours (international)**, the refund is now mandated to be automatic.

## Important reassurance
Losing access to your mobility or your vehicle at an airport is a high-stress event that triggers specific legal protections. By getting the file reference number and involving a CRO, you shift the responsibility to the airline’s specialized disability and baggage teams who have established protocols for these exact scenarios.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and reporting within the first two hours of arrival. It does not cover long-term claims for lost items or permanent replacement of high-value medical equipment.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and is not legal advice. U.S. air travel is governed by 14 CFR Part 382 (Air Carrier Access Act) and Department of Transportation (DOT) baggage rules. Requirements for "automatic" refunds and specific delivery timelines apply to flights operating in 2026. Specific airline contracts of carriage may apply to non-assistive items like car keys.
