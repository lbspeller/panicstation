# What to do if… you have been in a minor car collision and are now safely parked (USA)
## Short answer
Stay safely parked, check whether anyone needs urgent help, and exchange the required information. Document the vehicles and location from a safe place before leaving, unless staying there becomes unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into traffic or stand between vehicles to take photos.
- Do not move vehicles back into their earlier positions to recreate the collision.
- Do not argue about blame or try to settle the matter while you are shaken.
- Do not send money or make a roadside agreement.
- Do not allow broad photos of personal documents when recording the required details is enough.
- Do not leave before recording the required details unless staying there is unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the scene safe.** Leave your hazard lights on. Stay out of traffic lanes. If the location becomes unsafe, anyone is injured, anyone may need urgent medical help, or a vehicle cannot be driven safely and is creating a hazard, call **911**.
2. **Check everyone involved.** Ask whether anyone is hurt. Do not diagnose the situation yourself. If symptoms appear later or you are concerned about an injury, seek medical advice.
3. **Exchange the key details.** Record each driver’s name and contact details, the insurance company and policy details, and each vehicle’s plate number, make, model, and color. State and local rules may also require driver license, registration, or address information, so check the official guidance for the place where the collision happened.
4. **Document the scene from a safe place.** Take photos of the damage to each vehicle, the license plates, and nearby signs or address markers. Note the date, time, exact location, and a short factual description of what happened. Record witness contact details if anyone saw the collision.
5. **Contact police promptly if needed.** If the other driver leaves, refuses to exchange required details, threatens anyone, or you suspect an immediate safety risk, contact police. Use **911** for immediate danger; otherwise follow the local police reporting route.
6. **Check state and local reporting rules as soon as practical.** Reporting thresholds, deadlines, and required forms vary. Use the official state motor vehicle agency and local police guidance. Do not assume that contacting your insurer replaces a required report.
7. **Notify your insurer as soon as practical.** Use the contact details on your insurance card or your insurer’s official app. Save your photos and notes.
8. **Check any child car seat before using it again.** Follow the manufacturer’s instructions and the official NHTSA guidance for car seats involved in a collision.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide fault at the roadside.
- You do not need to arrange repair estimates immediately.
- You do not need to decide whether to accept any payment or settlement.
- You do not need to post about the collision online.

## Important reassurance
A minor collision can leave you feeling shaken or unsure what to do next. Focus only on safety, the required information, a few clear photos, and the reporting check. Those steps are enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a minor collision when you are already safely parked. Later decisions about injuries, reports, repairs, or claims may need help from an insurer, a medical professional, local police, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, insurance, or other professional advice. Reporting duties, deadlines, and required information vary by state and by the facts of the collision.
