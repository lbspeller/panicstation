# What to do if… online check-in is blocked and you need manual document verification (USA)

## Short answer
Plan to speak to an airline counter agent before security. Bring the physical travel documents you will use, details of any required travel authorization, and your booking reference, then allow extra time at the airport.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t repeatedly enter guessed passport or personal details.
- Don’t wait until the gate; the airline may need to check your documents before security.
- Don’t buy a new ticket or pay a change fee before the airline confirms what is wrong.
- Don’t assume a screenshot or U.S. passport card replaces the physical passport needed for international air travel.
- Don’t make non-essential itinerary changes while the airline is still checking the issue.

## What to do now
1. **Save the exact message.** Screenshot the check-in block, any error code, and your booking reference.
2. **Compare the booking details with the physical travel document.** Check the name, date of birth, passport number, issuing country, nationality, and expiration date. Correct only details you can verify, using the airline’s official app, website, or support channel.
3. **Check the documents required for the destination and any transit point.** Use official government information and the airline’s official support channel. If ESTA applies to your trip, check its status on the official ESTA website. A new ESTA authorization is generally needed after a new passport or a change to your name, gender, or country of citizenship.
4. **Contact the airline if the ticket name and passport name do not match.** Ask whether the airline can correct the booking under its rules. Bring any relevant legal name-change document, such as a marriage certificate or court order, but do not assume it guarantees a ticket change.
5. **Save useful information offline.** Keep your booking reference and any approval confirmation available without airport Wi-Fi. Offline copies are backups only; they do not replace required physical documents or a valid authorization.
6. **Ask the airline one direct question through its official support channel.** Ask: “Is this a routine document check at the counter, or is there a specific missing or invalid field or document?”
7. **Go to the full-service airline counter with extra time.** Bring the physical passport you will use for the trip, any required visa or travel authorization details, your booking reference, and any relevant supporting document.
8. **If you are a U.S. citizen flying internationally to or from the United States, bring a valid U.S. passport book.** If you do not have one, check official passport help immediately rather than relying on an airport override.
9. **If you are a U.S. citizen with another nationality, use your U.S. passport to enter and leave the United States.** Carry any other passport needed for the other country.

## What can wait
- Complaints, compensation requests, and insurance claims.
- Non-essential itinerary changes.
- Deciding whether to buy another ticket, unless the airline confirms that the current booking cannot be used.

## Important reassurance
A manual document check does not by itself mean that travel has been refused. The immediate priority is to get the airline to identify the issue while there is still time to deal with it.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If a document is expired, missing, or not accepted, later decisions may need help from the airline or the relevant government service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Requirements vary by destination, transit point, and airline.
