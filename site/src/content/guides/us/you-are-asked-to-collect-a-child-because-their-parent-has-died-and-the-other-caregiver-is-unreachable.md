# What to do if… you are asked to collect a child because their parent has died and the other caregiver is unreachable (USA)

## Short answer
Ensure you have verified authorization from the school or childcare provider before attempting to collect the child. If you are not on the authorized pickup list and no legal guardian is reachable, contact local law enforcement or child protective services (CPS) to facilitate a safe, documented handoff.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to pressure school staff into releasing the child if they cannot verify your authorization.
- Do not take the child to an unverified location without explicit permission from a legal guardian, law enforcement, or a court.
- Do not sign educational or medical documents as a "parent" or "guardian" unless you have legal documentation of that status.
- Do not post details of the death or the child's location on social media or public forums.
- Do not promise the child long-term living arrangements until legal custody is formally established.

## What to do now
1. **Identify exactly who called you and where the child is currently located.** Ask for the caller's full name, their official role, a direct call-back number, and the specific room or office where the child is waiting.
2. **Confirm your status on the school or facility's emergency contact card.** Ask the administrator if your name appears on the "authorized pickup" list; if it does not, ask exactly what documentation they require to release the child to you in an emergency.
3. **Gather your primary identification and any relevant documents.** Take a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport) to the school.
   - If you have any written evidence of the parents' wishes (such as a standby guardianship form), bring it with you.
4. **Instruct the school to hold the child safely while a plan is formed if you are not authorized.** Clearly state: "I am not authorized to take the child yet—please follow your security protocols and keep the child safe in the office while I contact the authorities."
5. **Attempt to reach the other parent and every emergency contact listed on the child's record.** 
   - Use phone calls and text messages; keep communications brief and factual.
   - Ask the school to attempt their own calls to all numbers in the child's file.
6. **Escalate to official authorities if no legal guardian can be reached within a safe timeframe.**
   - Call **911** if the school is closing and no authorized adult is available, or if the child’s immediate safety is at risk.
   - Call the local **police non-emergency line** or the **state-specific Child Protective Services (CPS) hotline** to request an emergency placement assessment or a "safety plan" that allows the child to stay with you temporarily.
7. **Collect the child's essential belongings once release is approved.** Ensure you have the child's backpack, house keys (if safe), and any daily medications or medical equipment.
8. **Request a written record of the release from the facility.** Ask the administrator to note the time of release, the name of the staff member who authorized it, and the contact information for any law enforcement or CPS officer involved.
9. **Go directly to a calm, familiar, and safe environment.** Limit the child's exposure to adult conversations and avoid making unnecessary stops while you await further instruction from the authorities or the family.
10. **Begin the "emergency guardianship" or "emergency custody" process within 24 hours.** Contact your local county court's family division or a legal aid clinic to find the forms required for a temporary court order, as school and medical authorization will expire quickly.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to finalize funeral or memorial arrangements right now.
- You do **not** need to decide on permanent custody or school transfers today.
- You do **not** need to clean out the parent's home or handle estate matters immediately.
- You do **not** need to notify every family member or friend in the first few hours; focus only on the child’s safety and the legal path for the next 48 hours.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant stress when trying to navigate school security during a crisis. Insisting that the school follows its legal protocols is a protective act for the child and ensures that you are not later accused of unauthorized removal.

## Scope note
This guide addresses the immediate stabilization of a child's care in the first few hours following a tragedy. It does not provide legal advice for long-term guardianship, which varies significantly by state law and court jurisdiction.

## Important note
Release policies are determined by individual school districts and state laws. If a school refuses to release a child to you, do not escalate the conflict; instead, ask them to contact local law enforcement to mediate the transfer of the child into protective or temporary custody.
