# What to do if… your dishwasher starts leaking water onto the kitchen floor mid-cycle (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the appliance from its power source and shut off the water supply valve, typically located under the kitchen sink. Avoid touching the dishwasher or standing in the water until power is confirmed off.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand in standing water while touching outlets, plugs, or the electrical panel.
- Do not attempt to "finish the cycle" to drain the water.
- Do not use metal mops or conductive tools in the puddle if the power is still on.
- Do not pull the dishwasher out of its cavity while it is still connected to power or water lines.
- Do not touch the appliance if you hear buzzing, sizzling, or smell burning plastic.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a dry area and keep others away.** Ensure children and pets are moved to a different room to prevent slips or contact with potentially energized water.
2. **Shut off power to the dishwasher safely.** 
   - If the dishwasher is plugged into a visible outlet and you can reach the plug **without stepping in water**, unplug it.
   - If the appliance is hardwired or the plug is inaccessible, go to the circuit breaker panel. 
   - Flip the breaker labeled "Dishwasher" to the **OFF** position only if the floor in front of the panel is dry.
   - If the area around the electrical panel is wet, do not touch the panel; contact a licensed electrician immediately.
3. **Close the water supply valve.** Locate the shutoff valve, which is usually found under the kitchen sink on the hot water line feeding the dishwasher. 
   - Turn the small handle or knob clockwise until it stops. 
   - If you cannot find this valve or it is stuck, shut off the main water supply to the house.
4. **Contain the spread of water.** Place heavy towels or blankets at the edge of the puddle to create a dam.
   - Focus on preventing water from flowing under cabinets, into floor vents, or toward baseboards.
   - Move any items stored in the cabinet under the sink to a dry location.
5. **Clear standing water once power is off.** Use a mop, old towels, or a wet / dry vacuum (if safe and plugged into a dry, distant outlet) to remove the water.
   - Ensure the floor is dried quickly to prevent warping of wood or laminate surfaces.
6. **Notify the appropriate parties if you do not own the property.** If you are a tenant, contact the landlord or property management immediately.
   - State clearly: "Dishwasher leaked mid-cycle; water and power have been shut off; standing water was on the kitchen floor."

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical cause of the leak (e.g., pump, gasket, or hose) right now.
- You do not need to take photos for insurance until the immediate flood is contained and the area is safe.
- You do not need to decide between repairing or replacing the appliance today.
- You do not need to run fans or dehumidifiers until the standing water is fully removed.

## Important reassurance
Dishwasher leaks often look more severe than they are because of how quickly water spreads across flat flooring. Once the power and water are isolated, the immediate hazard is stabilized. Most modern flooring and cabinets can withstand brief exposure to water if it is dried promptly.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance only. A professional appliance technician should inspect the unit before it is powered on again. If water has entered electrical outlets or the subfloor, an electrician or restoration specialist may be required.

## Important note
This guide is for general information and does not replace professional electrical or plumbing services. If you observe sparks, smoke, or the smell of burning, or if water has reached electrical outlets, evacuate the area and contact emergency services or a qualified professional.
