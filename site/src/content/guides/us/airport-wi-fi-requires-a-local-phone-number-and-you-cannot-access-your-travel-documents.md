# What to do if… airport Wi-Fi requires a local phone number and you cannot access your travel documents (USA)

## Short answer
Ask airline staff whether they can retrieve or print the document you need. At the same time, check for an offline copy or use a brief, trusted connection such as your mobile data, a companion’s hotspot, or a reputable SIM or eSIM option.

## Do not do these things
- Do not cancel or change your trip before asking airline staff what they can retrieve or print.
- Do not hand your phone to a stranger or share a verification code with someone offering unofficial help.
- Do not join a look-alike network or scan an unofficial Wi-Fi QR code. Ask airport staff for the official network name.
- Do not type passwords, payment details, or identity information into a page that looks suspicious, even if the page uses a secure connection.
- Do not keep retrying an account login if you may trigger a lockout.

## What to do now
1. **Name the one document you need first.** This may be a boarding pass, booking confirmation, itinerary, visa document, or proof of onward travel.
2. **Check your phone for an offline copy.**
   - Look in your wallet app, airline app, downloads, files, photos, and screenshots.
   - Search your email app for a previously opened message or attachment that may still be available offline.
3. **Ask airline staff for help without waiting for the Wi-Fi issue to resolve.**
   - Ask whether they can retrieve or print the item.
   - Ask what booking details or identification they can use to find your reservation.
   - Ask whether a self-service kiosk can print the document.
4. **Ask airport staff whether the official Wi-Fi has another sign-in route.**
   - Ask whether international phone numbers are accepted.
   - Ask whether there is an official access code, help desk, or other connection option.
5. **Use a brief trusted connection if you still need internet access.**
   - Check your carrier notice or plan before enabling roaming because charges and availability vary.
   - Use a companion’s personal hotspot if available.
   - Consider a local SIM or international eSIM from a reputable seller. Ask about activation and any local registration requirement.
6. **Keep public Wi-Fi use limited and deliberate.**
   - Confirm the official network name with airport staff.
   - Check that websites use a secure connection and make sure the site itself is the one you intended to visit.
   - Avoid banking, shopping, or other unnecessary sensitive activity while you are solving the immediate travel-document problem.
7. **Treat a missing file differently from a lost passport.**
   - If the passport is physically with you but a digital copy will not load, focus on retrieving the specific travel document you need.
   - If a U.S. passport is actually lost or stolen abroad, follow State Department guidance and contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve the airport Wi-Fi permanently if airline staff can retrieve or print the document.
- You do not need to change your phone plan while you are standing at the airport.
- You do not need to change passwords unless you entered them into a suspicious page, shared a verification code, or have another reason to think an account may be at risk.

## Important reassurance
A Wi-Fi sign-in problem does not automatically mean you cannot continue your trip. Airline staff may be able to retrieve the immediate document while you use a simple backup connection or an offline copy.

## Scope note
These are first steps for regaining access to travel documents while time-pressured at an airport. Later decisions about account security, carrier settings, document replacement, or travel changes may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, cybersecurity, consular, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Processes and connectivity options vary by airline, airport, carrier, and destination.
