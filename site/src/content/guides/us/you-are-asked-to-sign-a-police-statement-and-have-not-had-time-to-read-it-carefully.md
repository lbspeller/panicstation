# What to do if… you are asked to sign a police statement and have not had time to read it carefully (USA)

## Short answer
Do not sign a police statement while you are rushed or unable to understand it. Say that you need time to read it, ask whether signing is voluntary, and ask to speak with a lawyer before answering more questions or signing if you are unsure how the statement could affect you.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign because you are told the statement is routine or signing will make things quicker.
- Do not rely only on someone else’s summary of the statement.
- Do not sign a statement with blanks, missing pages, wording you think is inaccurate, or changes you have not reread.
- Do not guess, lie, or provide false information because you feel pressured to respond quickly.
- Do not physically resist, interfere, or argue if the situation becomes tense.

## What to do now
1. Say: “I need time to read this carefully. I am not signing it while I do not understand it.”
2. Ask: “What is this statement?” Then ask: “Is signing this voluntary?”
3. If you are not arrested and you are unsure how the statement could affect you, say: “I am exercising my right to remain silent. I want to speak with a lawyer before answering questions or deciding whether to sign.”
4. Ask: “Am I free to leave?” If the answer is yes, you can calmly leave. If the answer is no, do not physically resist. Repeat that you are exercising your right to remain silent.
5. In some states, during some stops, you may be required to identify yourself. Beyond any information you are legally required to provide, do not keep discussing the incident while you are asking to remain silent.
6. If you choose to review the statement, check that every page is present, there are no blanks, the wording matches what you mean, and any changes are written in and reread before you consider signing.
7. If reading, language, disability, or stress is making the statement hard to understand, say: “I cannot understand this statement right now. I need help reading it before I decide whether to sign.”
8. Ask whether a copy is available. Do not sign the statement if you have not been able to review it adequately.
9. If you are arrested, do not physically resist. Say that you want to remain silent and ask for a lawyer immediately.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain your whole side of the incident right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to sign the statement while you are rushed.
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a complaint about the encounter.

## Important reassurance
Feeling rushed, intimidated, or unsure in this situation is understandable. Taking time to read the statement and asking to speak with a lawyer before making a decision are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The best next step may depend on the statement, whether you are free to leave, whether you have been arrested, and the law in your state. A lawyer can give advice for your specific situation.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice or professional advice. Laws and procedures vary by state and situation.
