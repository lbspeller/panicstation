# What to do if… you start feeling suddenly far more intoxicated than expected and worry your drink was tampered with (USA)

## Short answer
Stop drinking immediately and move to a visible, monitored area with a person you trust to seek medical evaluation. Call **911** if you are losing consciousness, having trouble breathing, or feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not finish the drink or take "sips" to test the taste.
- Do not consume more alcohol, water, or substances to "flush it out."
- Do not leave the venue alone or with someone you only recently met.
- Do not go into private areas like bathrooms, cars, or back rooms alone.
- Do not drive or attempt to walk home by yourself.
- Do not confront anyone you suspect of tampering with your drink.
- Do not worry about "causing a scene" or being wrong about your symptoms.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate sensory cues or physical changes.**
   - Sudden, extreme sleepiness or "heavy" limbs.
   - Confusion, slurred speech, or blurred vision that feels "unearned" by your intake.
   - Nausea or vomiting that starts abruptly.
   - A metallic or "chemical" taste in your mouth.
2. **Alert a trusted person and move to a safer spot.** **Tell a friend or the person you arrived with exactly how you feel.** If you are alone, tell the bartender, manager, or security staff immediately and ask them to keep you in a visible, monitored area until help arrives.
3. **Isolate the drink but do not touch it.** **Leave the glass or bottle where it is.** Do not try to carry it with you or pour it out; if you are in a public venue, tell the staff which drink you are concerned about so they can secure the area if necessary.
4. **Call 911 for emergency symptoms.** **Request an ambulance if you experience any of the following:**
   - Difficulty breathing or shallow breaths.
   - Seizures or tremors.
   - Inability to stay awake or talk.
   - Sudden collapse or loss of motor control.
5. **Contact the Poison Help line for guidance.** **Call 1-800-222-1222 if you are stable but worried about what you might have ingested.** This is a free, confidential service staffed by experts who can provide triage advice.
6. **Go to the nearest Emergency Department.** **Tell the intake staff: "I feel far more impaired than I should be and I believe my drink was tampered with."** This ensures they treat your symptoms as a potential poisoning rather than standard intoxication.
7. **Secure your phone and belongings.** **Keep your phone in your hand or a secure pocket.** Ensure it is charged or ask a trusted person to help you manage your communication.

## What can wait
- You do not need to know the name of the drug or substance right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a police report tonight.
- You do not need to provide a perfect timeline of events to medical staff immediately; your physical stabilization is the priority.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of the ER visit or ambulance during the crisis; focus on reaching professional care.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of losing control over your body or mind when you did not intend to is distressing and disorienting. Hesitation or confusion is a common reaction to this situation. You are not at fault for what is happening, and seeking help is the most effective way to ensure your safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It focuses on immediate physical safety and medical triage within the first minutes of suspecting impairment.

## Important note
This information is for stabilization purposes and does not replace professional medical or legal advice. If you suspect you have been sexually assaulted, you may request a medical forensic exam at the hospital. You can contact the National Sexual Assault Hotline at **800-656-HOPE** for confidential support.
