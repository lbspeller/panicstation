# What to do if… you find an online post about your sex life that identifies you and you feel exposed (USA)

## Short answer
Do not engage with the post. Move to a safer pause, save only the minimum details if you can do that safely, then report the post through the platform and contact specialist support if you feel overwhelmed, threatened, or unsure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply, argue, or try to explain yourself in comments; it can spread the post further or create more screenshots.
- Do not threaten, insult, or rage-message the poster.
- Do not delete accounts, messages, or posts in panic if you may want help with reporting later.
- Do not ask lots of people to view the post to confirm what it says.
- Do not agree to demands for money, images, sex, silence, or meeting up.
- Do not send intimate images, private screenshots, or confidential details through a general contact form.

## What to do now
1. **Create a small safety bubble.**  
   Stop scrolling, put the device down for a moment, and move somewhere more private. If someone near you can sit calmly with you, ask them to stay while you do the next step.

2. **Check for immediate danger.**  
   If the post includes your address, workplace, real-time location, threats to come to you, threats to hurt you, stalking, or blackmail, focus on physical safety first. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

3. **Save only the minimum details if it is safe.**  
   Take screenshots that show the post, the account name or handle, the date and time, and the URL. Save them somewhere private that the poster cannot access. Do not keep searching for more if it is making you panic.

4. **Report the post where it appears.**  
   Use the platform report options closest to harassment, sexual harassment, private information, doxxing, impersonation, threats, nonconsensual intimate content, or bullying. If the post identifies you through your name, workplace, school, address, phone number, or photos, report those identifying details too.

5. **Reduce exposure on your accounts.**  
   Change the password on your email and the affected platform if you think anyone may have access. Turn on two-factor authentication, make profiles private for now, limit tags and mentions, and turn off message requests from strangers.

6. **Get specialist support before confronting anyone.**  
   RAINN can help you talk through tech-enabled sexual abuse and your options. If the post includes intimate images, videos, deepfakes, sextortion, or threats to share intimate images, the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative Image Abuse Helpline may also be relevant.

7. **Use image-specific routes only if images, videos, or deepfakes are involved.**  
   If an intimate image, video, or sexual deepfake of you is shared or threatened, report it to the platform first. If the platform does not remove covered content through the required process, the FTC route may apply. If the content involves anyone under 18, use child-exploitation specialist help immediately and avoid sharing or forwarding the material.

## What can wait
You do not need to decide right now whether to contact the poster, make a public statement, explain yourself, tell your workplace or family, or pursue legal action. You also do not need to read every comment, search your name for hours, or gather perfect documentation today.

## Important reassurance
Feeling exposed after a sexual post identifies you can cause panic, nausea, shaking, shame, anger, or numbness. Those reactions do not mean you did anything wrong. A calm, small next step is enough for this moment.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce harm, preserve your choices, and help you reach support. Later decisions about reporting, legal options, workplace issues, school issues, or takedown strategy may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are under 18, or if the content involves anyone under 18, treat it as urgent and seek specialist help from a child-exploitation reporting service. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
