# What to do if… you need to inform an estranged family member about a death and worry about conflict (USA)

## Short answer
Send a brief, factual written notification via the lowest-contact method available and appoint a single point of contact for all subsequent logistics.

## Do not do these things
- Don't meet in person to deliver the news if the individual has a history of verbal or physical aggression.
- Don't provide the name or address of the funeral home or the deceased's residence if you suspect the individual may disrupt services or remove property.
- Don't include personal grievances, apologies, or justifications in the notification message.
- Don't engage with legal threats or demands for inheritance information in the initial notification.
- Don't answer phone calls from the individual if previous calls have involved shouting, intimidation, or harassment.
- Don't feel obligated to respond to messages within a specific timeframe.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the lowest-contact communication channel.**  
   **Use email, text message, or a physical letter rather than a phone call.**  
   - Direct messages allow for a recorded trail of communication.  
   - If direct contact is unsafe, ask a third party (such as a neutral family friend or an attorney) to send the notice.

2. **Draft a factual notification limited to three sentences.**  
   **State only the essential facts of the death without editorializing.**  
   - Structure the message as: "[Name] died on [Date]."  
   - Follow with: "I am unable to engage in long-range discussions at this time."  
   - End with: "Future updates regarding arrangements will be sent via [chosen channel]."

3. **Establish a communication boundary in the first message.**  
   **Explicitly state how and when you will receive further inquiries.**  
   - Example: "Please do not call; I will review and respond to written messages as I am able."  
   - Example: "All communication regarding this matter must go through [Name of designated contact]."

4. **Designate a single point of contact for the family.**  
   **Appoint one person to receive and filter all incoming messages from the estranged party.**  
   - This can be a relative, a family friend, or the executor of the estate.  
   - Instruct the contact to only pass on time-sensitive, factual information.

5. **Secure physical locations if confrontation is anticipated.**  
   **Ensure all doors are locked and do not grant entry to unannounced visitors.**  
   - If you are staying at the deceased's home, change the locks if the estranged individual has a key or access rights.  
   - Notify the funeral home staff that there is a high-conflict family situation and request they monitor for uninvited attendees.

6. **Preserve a record of all incoming communications.**  
   **Save screenshots of texts, archive emails, and record voicemails without replying.**  
   - If messages contain threats of physical harm, call 911 immediately.  
   - For non-emergency harassment, contact your local police department via their non-emergency line to document the behavior.

7. **Verify eligibility for vital records independently.**  
   **Contact the state or county vital records office in the jurisdiction where the death occurred to order certificates.**  
   - You do not need the consent of other family members to order a death certificate if you are an authorized party (such as a spouse, child, or sibling in most states).  
   - Use official government portals or the authorized VitalChek system to avoid third-party delays.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on the guest list for the funeral today.
- You do not need to discuss the distribution of personal property or assets.
- You do not need to explain the causes of the estrangement or engage in "closure" talks.
- You do not need to review the Will or trust documents immediately.

## Important reassurance
Physical and digital distance is a standard safety protocol in high-conflict situations. It is common to feel a sense of dread or hesitation when managing these notifications; these reactions are normal when dealing with unpredictable or aggressive individuals during a crisis.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. If the situation involves ongoing stalking, domestic violence, or threats of violence, specialized legal and protective services may be required.

## Important note
This guide provides general procedural information and is not a substitute for legal advice or professional security planning. Rules regarding who can make funeral decisions vary by state law and the presence of a legal Will.
