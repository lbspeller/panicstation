# What to do if… you are pregnant and notice bleeding or persistent one-sided pain (USA)

## Short answer
Seek urgent medical evaluation now. Call your OB/GYN, midwife, or prenatal clinic for immediate instructions, or go to an ER; call 911 if you faint, collapse, have severe or rapidly worsening symptoms, or feel seriously unwell.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the bleeding or one-sided pain to become severe before seeking care.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel dizzy, weak, faint, or severely unwell.
- Do not use tampons or douche, and do not have sex until a pregnancy clinician tells you it is safe.
- Do not start ibuprofen, naproxen, or another NSAID without checking with a pregnancy clinician.
- Do not stop any prescribed medicine, including prescribed low-dose aspirin, unless the prescriber tells you to.
- Do not delay assessment while trying to work out the cause yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now if you have emergency symptoms.**  
   Call 911 if you faint, collapse, have trouble breathing, have severe or rapidly worsening belly or pelvic pain, or feel seriously unwell.

2. **Go to an ER now if you have urgent warning signs.**  
   Go to an ER now if you have shoulder pain, heavy bleeding, significant dizziness or weakness, or bleeding with persistent one-sided pain. Ask someone to take you if it is safe to do so. Do not drive yourself if you feel unwell.

3. **Contact your pregnancy clinician now if you are not already going to an ER.**  
   Call your **OB/GYN, midwife, prenatal clinic, or nurse line** and say: “I am pregnant and I have bleeding or persistent one-sided pain.” Follow their immediate instructions. If you cannot reach them promptly, go to an **ER**.

4. **Use a pad and note the key details.**  
   Note when the bleeding started, whether it is getting heavier, the color, where the pain is, whether it stays on one side, and whether you have shoulder pain, dizziness, or faintness.

5. **Tell the clinician about relevant history.**  
   Mention any previous ectopic pregnancy, fertility treatment, pregnancy with an IUD in place, or prior pelvic, abdominal, or fallopian-tube surgery. Say if you have passed clots or tissue, but do not delay care to save anything.

6. **Check before taking medicine.**  
   Ask a clinician or pharmacist before taking any medicine during pregnancy. Acetaminophen may be an option for pain, but taking pain relief should not delay urgent assessment.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide what caused the bleeding or pain before you are assessed.
- You do not need to keep checking internally, repeatedly testing, or searching for symptom comparisons online.
- You do not need to make announcements or major decisions now. Focus on getting assessed safely.

## Important reassurance
Bleeding can happen during pregnancy and does not always mean there is a major problem. Bleeding or persistent one-sided pain still needs prompt assessment because some causes can become urgent.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Diagnosis, treatment, and later decisions may need an in-person medical team.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice or a diagnosis. If you feel faint, your pain is severe, or you are rapidly getting worse, call 911.
