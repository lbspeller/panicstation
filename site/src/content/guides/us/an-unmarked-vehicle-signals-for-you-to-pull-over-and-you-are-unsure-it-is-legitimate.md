# What to do if… an unmarked vehicle signals for you to pull over and you are unsure it is legitimate (USA)

## Short answer
Turn on your hazard lights, slow down, and keep driving predictably toward the nearest safe, well-lit public place. If you can do so safely and lawfully, use hands-free calling or ask a passenger to call 911 to check whether an officer is attempting the stop, then follow the dispatcher’s instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t speed up, make sudden turns, or try to evade the vehicle.
- Don’t drive home or to another private address.
- Don’t handle a phone while driving if you cannot do so safely and lawfully.
- Don’t stop in a dark, isolated place if you can safely reach a nearby well-lit public place.
- Don’t unlock the doors, get out, or fully lower the window while the person’s identity remains uncertain.
- Don’t make sudden movements or reach for documents without explaining what you are doing.

## What to do now
1. **Acknowledge the signal.** Turn on your hazard lights, slow down, and continue driving predictably toward the nearest safe, well-lit public place, such as a busy parking lot. A nearby police-station front parking area may also be suitable if it is close. Do not drive farther than necessary.
2. **Call 911 safely.** If you can do so safely and lawfully, use hands-free calling or ask a passenger to call. Tell the dispatcher:
   - your location as clearly as possible and your direction of travel
   - your vehicle description and license plate number
   - a description of the vehicle behind you
   - that an unmarked vehicle is signaling for you to stop and you are unsure whether it is legitimate
3. **Follow the dispatcher’s instructions.** Ask whether an officer is attempting to stop your vehicle. If you cannot safely call while moving, pull into the nearest safe public place and call immediately.
4. **Once stopped, keep the car secure.** Keep the doors locked. If it is dark, turn on the interior light. Keep your hands visible on the steering wheel. While identity remains uncertain, lower the window only enough to hear the person and pass documents if appropriate.
5. **Ask for identification if uncertainty remains.** Ask the person to state their agency and show an agency-issued photo ID and badge. If the person is not in uniform or you are still unsure, request through 911 that a marked unit or uniformed officer attend.
6. **Stay connected to 911 if the stop cannot be confirmed immediately.** Dispatch may need time to verify a stop, including when agencies overlap. Continue predictably, keep your location updated, and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
7. **If the stop is confirmed, cooperate calmly.** Follow the dispatcher’s instructions and the officer’s instructions. Explain before reaching for documents or moving your hands.
8. **If you feel immediately threatened, say so clearly.** Tell the 911 dispatcher what is happening and keep your location updated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to contest a ticket or make a complaint.
- You do not need to debate the reason for the stop at the roadside.
- You do not need to post online or organize detailed notes during the encounter.
- Once you are safe, you can write down what happened and decide whether you need further help.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to want confirmation when a vehicle is unmarked. Turning on your hazards, driving predictably, contacting 911 safely, and stopping at a nearby public place can show cooperation while giving you time to check what is happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the initial minutes of an uncertain traffic stop. Later questions about a citation, a complaint, or legal advice can wait until you are safe and may need help from the relevant agency or a qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or professional advice. Procedures and laws can vary by state and agency. Follow dispatcher instructions and prioritize immediate safety.
