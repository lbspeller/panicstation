# What to do if… your child tells you an adult made a sexual comment or request (USA)

## Short answer
Focus on your child’s immediate safety, respond calmly, and do not try to investigate it yourself. If there is immediate danger, call 911; otherwise seek guidance today from the appropriate child safety route, such as your state or local child abuse reporting hotline, NCMEC CyberTipline for online exploitation, or Childhelp if you are unsure where to start.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the adult or warn them first; this can increase risk and make next steps harder.
- Do not repeatedly question your child or press for details.
- Do not promise secrecy; promise that you will try to help keep them safe.
- Do not ask your child to open, scroll, search, or help you look for more sexual content.
- Do not forward, download, copy, re-share, or post messages, images, videos, or usernames.
- Do not contact the adult to get a confession by text, call, or social media.
- Do not decide that it “doesn’t count” just because there was no physical contact.

## What to do now
1. **Create a calm, private moment with your child.** Say something simple, such as: *“Thank you for telling me. You did the right thing. You’re not in trouble.”*
2. **Stop access for now.** Do not allow visits, rides, babysitting, coaching contact, direct messages, calls, or being alone with that adult while you get guidance.
3. **Make the next few hours safer.** If the adult is in your home, nearby, or expected soon, move your child to a safer room, a trusted neighbour, a relative, or another supervised place where the adult cannot reach them.
4. **Do not alter digital material.** If messages, usernames, accounts, images, or URLs already exist, do not delete, forward, download, copy, search for more, or ask your child to find anything; ask NCMEC CyberTipline, CPS, or law enforcement what to do next.
5. **Choose the reporting or guidance route that fits the risk.**
   - If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.
   - If the concern involves online exploitation, sexual messages, requests for images, or sexual images of a child, report to NCMEC CyberTipline at https://report.cybertip.org/ or call 1-800-843-5678.
   - If you think your child or another child may be at ongoing risk from an adult, contact your state or local child abuse reporting hotline. If you do not know the right number, use the Child Welfare Information Gateway state reporting directory or contact Childhelp at 1-800-422-4453.
   - If the adult is connected to a school, club, faith group, sport, childcare setting, or other organization, tell the person responsible for safeguarding or child safety there after you have secured your child’s immediate safety. School and child-serving staff are often mandated reporters, but do not rely on the organization as your only route if you think a child is at risk.
   - If you are unsure where to report online exploitation, the DHS Know2Protect Tipline at 1-833-591-KNOW can also point you toward reporting options.
6. **Keep your child’s routine steady tonight.** Offer food, water, a safe adult nearby, and a low-pressure activity. Let them know they can talk more later, but they do not have to explain everything right now.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to file a police report, give a formal statement, or prove what happened before asking for professional guidance.
- You do not need to contact the adult, their family, their workplace, or their online accounts today.
- You do not need to decide on therapy, school changes, legal action, or long-term family decisions in this moment.
- You do not need to tell everyone. Share only with people who need to help keep your child safe or help you contact the right support.

## Important reassurance
Feeling frozen, unsure, angry, or afraid of overreacting is common in this situation. A calm response, a clear no-contact boundary, and one careful step toward specialist guidance can protect your child while avoiding rushed or unsafe actions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from child protection services, law enforcement, a child advocacy center, a lawyer, a clinician, or another specialist, depending on your state and the facts.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, investigative, or other professional advice. If a child is in immediate danger, call 911.
