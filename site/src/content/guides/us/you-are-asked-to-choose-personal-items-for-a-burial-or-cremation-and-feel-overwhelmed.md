# What to do if… you are asked to choose personal items for a burial or cremation and feel overwhelmed (USA)

## Short answer
Ask for a pause and make the decision smaller. For burial, ask whether the funeral home or cemetery has item rules; for cremation, ask the funeral home or crematory to confirm what is permitted before anything is placed with the deceased.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t try to find an item that represents an entire relationship.
- Don’t place anything with the deceased until staff confirm that it is permitted for this burial or cremation.
- Don’t include an irreplaceable valuable unless you are comfortable not getting it back.
- Don’t assume an item is permitted because it seems harmless.
- Don’t let a family disagreement force a rushed final choice; ask what deadline actually applies.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the exact decision and deadline.** Say: “I’m overwhelmed. What do you need me to decide today, and by what time?”
2. **Confirm whether this is burial or cremation.**
   - For burial, ask: “Are there any funeral-home or cemetery rules about personal items?”
   - For cremation, ask: “Please confirm which personal items are permitted and which are not.”
3. **Use a simplifying rule if it helps.** It is reasonable to ask for no personal item, or to choose one small replaceable item after staff confirm that it is permitted.
4. **Separate items into two groups.**
   - “Stays with us”
   - “Approved to accompany them”
5. **For cremation, ask whether any known implanted device needs to be disclosed.** Mention any known pacemaker or battery-powered device. Ask staff to handle the safety checks. Do not try to remove or manage anything yourself.
6. **Write down the final answer.** Note the approved item or write “no personal items,” and record who confirmed it.
7. **Keep paid choices separate from the keepsake decision.** If cost or package pressure is being added, ask for written itemized information from the funeral home and return to the personal-item question only.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to choose the perfect symbol of your relationship.
- You do **not** need to decide what happens to every valuable today.
- You do **not** need to choose something for each relative or friend.
- You do **not** need to settle family disagreements that are not required for the immediate decision.

## Important reassurance
Choosing something simple, or asking to choose nothing, does not mean you cared less. This is a difficult decision during grief, and reducing it to one confirmed next step is reasonable.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce pressure and avoid an irreversible rushed decision. Later decisions can wait and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Rules and policies can vary by state, cemetery, funeral home, and crematory. Confirm the specific requirements with the provider handling the burial or cremation.
