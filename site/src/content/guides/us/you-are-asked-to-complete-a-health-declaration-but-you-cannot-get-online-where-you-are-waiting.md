# What to do if… you are asked to complete a health declaration but you cannot get online where you are waiting (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately notify airline or airport staff of your connectivity issue and request an official alternative, such as a paper form, a dedicated kiosk, or a supervisor's override to complete the requirement upon arrival. Do not stay in a "dead zone" attempting to troubleshoot while your boarding window closes.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t guess or provide inaccurate information on the form just to bypass a loading error.
- Don’t hand your passport or unlocked phone to unofficial "helpers" or strangers in the terminal.
- Don’t pay for third-party "fast-track" services that appear in sponsored search results; use only official government or airline links.
- Don’t delay your arrival at the gate or check-in desk while waiting for a signal to return.
- Don’t connect to "Free Airport Wi-Fi" networks that do not exactly match the official name provided on airport signage (to avoid "evil-twin" hotspots).

## What to do now
1. **Approach the airline check-in agent or gate supervisor and state the problem.**
   - **Explain that you are unable to access the required digital portal and ask for the station's "offline contingency" procedure.**
2. **Request an approved alternative method to satisfy the requirement.**
   - **Ask for a paper version of the health declaration or passenger locator form.**
   - **Check if the airline provides a dedicated tablet or kiosk for passengers without mobile data.**
   - **Confirm if the airline allows "declaration on arrival" at the destination's health or immigration checkpoint.**
3. **If you must use a public or shared device, maximize your privacy.**
   - **Ensure you log out of all sessions, close the browser window entirely, and clear the browser history / cache if the device allows.**
   - **Always select "No" or "Never" when prompted by a browser to save your password or personal details.**
4. **Verify the exact "proof of completion" the airline requires before you leave the desk.**
   - **Ask the agent if they need a QR code, a confirmation number, or an email, and whether they can print it for you if you get it to load.**
5. **Secure your connection if you attempt to use the airport's public Wi-Fi.**
   - **Enable a VPN (Virtual Private Network) before entering any personal data on a public network.**
   - **Confirm the exact spelling of the Wi-Fi network with uniformed staff to ensure you are not on a malicious clone network.**
6. **Escalate to a Station Manager if an agent denies you boarding solely due to technical portal failures.**
   - **Request that they check the latest "Timatic" or carrier-specific entry updates for manual override instructions.**
7. **Contact U.S. Consular Affairs if you are a citizen stranded abroad due to a systemic border-entry failure.**
   - **Call 1-888-407-4747 (from the U.S./Canada) or +1-202-501-4444 (from overseas) for guidance on documented travel emergencies.**

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine if the fault lies with your device, your carrier, or the government website.
- You don’t need to purchase a new data plan or roaming package until you have explored free airport workarounds.
- You don’t need to file a formal complaint about the digital system until you have successfully boarded your flight.

## Important reassurance
Digital travel systems frequently experience outages or local connectivity "black holes." Airline staff are trained to handle these disruptions and usually have a manual process to ensure passengers who are legally eligible to fly can still board. Your inability to connect is a technical hurdle, not a personal travel violation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for connectivity-related travel delays. It does not cover legal visa requirements or health-status eligibility disputes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or official travel advice. Entry requirements are set by destination governments and are subject to change without notice; always defer to instructions from official border or airline authorities.
