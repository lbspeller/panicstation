# What to do if… your online memorial page is being targeted by trolls or scammers (USA)

## Short answer
Secure the account first. Use the strongest posting, comment, and approval controls the platform provides, then report and remove the most harmful content before engaging with anyone.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply publicly to trolls or post more personal details to correct them.
- Do not click donation links, support links, or shortened URLs posted by strangers.
- Do not accept new administrator or moderator requests from people you do not fully trust.
- Do not keep rereading upsetting posts once you have saved the small amount of information you may need.
- Do not pay or share account details with unsolicited contacts claiming they can recover money or fix the page.

## What to do now
1. **Ask one trusted person to help screen the memorial page.** Send a short message such as: “Please do not reply to anyone. Please flag the worst posts while I change the page controls.”
2. **Secure the account that controls the page.**
   - Change the password to a new, strong password that you do not use for another account.
   - Sign out on all devices if the platform provides that option.
   - Turn on two-factor authentication if available. It may also be called two-step verification or multi-factor authentication.
   - Check that the recovery email address and phone number are still correct if the platform shows them.
3. **Tighten the page controls using the options the platform provides.**
   - Require approval before new posts or tributes appear, if available.
   - Limit who can comment or post, if available.
   - Turn on keyword or profanity filters, if available.
   - Temporarily pause comments or new posts if the platform allows it and the unwanted content is continuing.
4. **Work through the most serious content first.**
   - Use the platform’s report option, where available, for threats, exposed personal information, hateful or sexual content, impersonation, scam links, fake fundraisers, and payment requests.
   - Remove or hide the content after reporting it when possible.
   - Block repeat posters if the platform provides that option.
5. **Save a minimal record of serious posts, then stop reviewing them.**
   - Take screenshots that show the post, username, date, and time where visible.
   - Save the page address or post address where available.
   - Save threatening direct messages without replying.
6. **Use memorial or legacy-contact tools if the platform provides them.** These tools may offer controls for tributes or memorialized profiles. Use the platform’s own help center rather than instructions sent by a stranger.
7. **Use the appropriate reporting route if the content goes beyond unwanted posts.**
   - If anyone is in immediate danger, call **911**.
   - For non-emergency threats or harassment, consider contacting your local police department through its non-emergency route.
   - For a fake fundraiser, report it to the fundraising platform first, then use the FTC’s ReportFraud site.
   - For cyber-enabled scams or impersonation, use IC3. Type **ic3.gov** directly into your browser address bar and avoid sponsored search results. IC3 will not ask for payment to recover lost funds or refer you to a company requesting payment for recovery.
8. **Post one short boundary note only if it will reduce confusion.** For example: “This memorial page is moderated. Abusive, false, or promotional posts will be removed and reported.” Do not argue with replies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to make the page private, close it, or publish a detailed statement.
- You do not need to personally answer accusations, rumors, or demands for information.
- You do not need to work out who the posters really are.
- You can postpone reviewing older posts and photos until the page is stable.

## Important reassurance
Managing a memorial page while grieving is already a heavy task. Unwanted posts are not your fault, and it is reasonable to tighten the controls and step away from upsetting material once the urgent moderation is done.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce harm and regain control. If threats, impersonation, harassment, or scams continue, later decisions may need support from the platform, a specialist service, or law enforcement, depending on what is happening.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.
