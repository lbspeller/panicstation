# What to do if… your mobile number shows a pending transfer you did not request (USA)

## Short answer
Assume the device is stolen: **remotely lock the device via official tools, secure your number with your carrier, and do not attempt to retrieve it from the mapped location yourself**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not travel to the address shown on the map to confront individuals or attempt recovery.
- Do not meet strangers claiming to have found the device in exchange for money.
- Do not click links in "Your device has been found" messages or enter passwords on external sites.
- Do not remove the device from your Apple or Google account, as this disables Activation Lock.
- Do not delay contacting your wireless carrier to suspend the SIM or eSIM.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a secure area and access a separate internet-connected device** (such as a laptop, tablet, or trusted friend's phone).
2. **Activate remote security features immediately via official portals:**
   - For iPhone: **Log into iCloud.com/find and enable Mark As Lost**.
   - For Android: **Log into google.com/android/find and select Secure Device**.
   - Provide an alternate contact number to be displayed on the device's lock screen.
3. **Contact your wireless service provider to report the loss and secure your account:**
   - **Request a service suspension** to prevent the unauthorized use of your number for calls, texts, or two-factor authentication (2FA) codes.
   - **Request a device block** using the International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI) or Mobile Equipment Identifier (MEID) number.
   - **Set or confirm a carrier account PIN** to prevent unauthorized "SIM swapping" or "porting out" of your number.
4. **Secure your primary digital accounts in order of priority:**
   - **1. Primary email account** (which often serves as the recovery hub for other services).
   - **2. Apple ID or Google Account** (to ensure the thief cannot change recovery settings).
   - **3. Financial and payment applications** (bank apps, digital wallets, and stored credit cards).
   - **4. Social media and messaging platforms.**
5. **Report the theft to local law enforcement using a non-emergency line.**
   - **Provide the mapped location and timestamp** shown in the tracking tool to the police.
   - Use the 911 emergency number only if there is an immediate threat to physical safety or a crime is currently in progress at your location.
6. **Execute a remote erase only if you have confirmed the device cannot be safely recovered.**
   - **Select the "Erase" option** in your Find My or Find My Device dashboard to delete personal data.
   - Keep the device listed on your account after erasing to maintain the Activation Lock protection.
7. **Initiate an identity theft report if you notice unauthorized account activity.**
   - **Visit IdentityTheft.gov** to create a federal recovery plan and document the incident.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to permanently erase the device (if accounts are already secured).
- Purchasing a replacement handset or tablet.
- Changing every non-essential application password.
- Filing insurance or protection plan claims (ensure the carrier report is filed first).

## Important reassurance
Tracking locations provided by GPS can be delayed or inaccurate by several yards; seeing the phone at a neighbor's house or a nearby street does not always mean it was intentionally taken there. Taking immediate steps to secure your number and accounts is a standard precaution that reduces risk regardless of the device's actual location.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a missing or potentially stolen device. It does not cover long-term device replacement, physical recovery tactics, or insurance claim processing.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal, financial, or professional security advice. Local laws regarding theft reporting vary by jurisdiction. Wireless carriers and financial institutions have specific internal protocols for fraud prevention that you should follow when contacted.
