# What to do if… someone slips a note under your hotel door pressuring you to open it or call a number (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside the room, engage the door locks, and move away from the door. Do not open the door or use the number on the note; contact the hotel through a known hotel contact route and ask staff to check the hallway.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door because the note says the situation is urgent.
- Do not call or text the number on the note.
- Do not scan a QR code or open a link shown on the note.
- Do not go into the hallway to look for the person who left it.
- Do not share personal or payment details with anyone using contact details from the note.
- Do not let someone into the room based only on a claim that they work for the hotel.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause inside the room.**  
   Engage the deadbolt and any secondary door guard. Move away from the door and keep your phone with you.

2. **Contact the hotel through a known route.**  
   Use the in-room front desk button or extension, or use a number from your booking confirmation, hotel app, or the hotel’s official website. Do not use contact details from the note. Tell staff that a note was pushed under your door and ask them to check the hallway and confirm whether any staff member contacted your room.

3. **Call 911 if you need immediate assistance.**  
   Call 911 if someone is trying to enter, threatening you, or you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance. Be ready to give the hotel name, street address, room number, and the nature of the emergency.

4. **Ask before leaving the room.**  
   If hotel staff suggest moving, ask for an escort to a staffed area or another room. Do not leave merely because someone outside the door says it is safe.

5. **Photograph the note only if it is safe.**  
   Take a photo and note the time if you can do so safely. Leave the note where it is unless hotel staff or police ask you to move it.

6. **Ask the hotel to document the incident.**  
   Once the immediate safety check is underway, ask the hotel to record what happened.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the note was a scam, prank, or something more serious.
- You do not need to respond to the note or explain yourself to the person who left it.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to change hotels.
- Optional scam reporting can wait until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Pressure to act immediately is a reason to pause and verify. Staying behind a locked door and using a known hotel contact route is a reasonable first response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from hotel management, law enforcement, or another appropriate service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, or other professional advice. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance, call 911.
