# What to do if… you are waiting for a rideshare and a different car stops and tells you it’s for you (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter the vehicle. Move to a well-lit, populated area and only approach a vehicle that exactly matches the license plate, make, model, and driver photo shown in your app.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get into the car to "verify" details or talk to the driver.
- Do not share your name or destination before the driver has correctly identified you through the app's secure protocols.
- Do not accept excuses such as "the app is glitching," "this is a rental," or "my friend is using my account."
- Do not cancel your official ride at the stranger's request.
- Do not agree to pay via cash, Venmo, or Zelle for an "off-app" trip.
- Do not approach the vehicle if the driver appears aggressive or insistent that you enter.

## What to do now
1. **Increase your physical distance from the vehicle.** Immediately take several steps back from the curb and move toward a building entrance, security desk, or a group of people.
2. **Check your app details against the vehicle from a distance.** Look for three specific identifiers before moving toward any car:
   - **License plate number** (must be an exact match).
   - **Vehicle make, model, and color.**
   - **Driver photo.**
3. **Use the "Verify My Ride" or PIN feature if enabled.** If your app provides a 4-digit PIN, stay at a safe distance and do not provide the PIN until the driver has first confirmed your name.
4. **Ask the driver "Who are you here for?"** If you choose to speak, do not provide your name first; make the driver tell you the name of the passenger they are assigned to pick up.
5. **Contact your assigned driver through the app's messaging tool.** If a different car claims to be your ride, use the in-app "Call" or "Message" button to find the actual location of your verified driver.
6. **Cancel the ride if the vehicle information does not match.** Use the in-app reporting feature to select "Wrong vehicle" or "Suspicious driver behavior" as the reason for cancellation.
7. **Call 911 if the driver follows you or refuses to leave.** If the driver exits the vehicle or attempts to block your path, seek immediate emergency assistance.
8. **Share your live trip status with a contact.** Once you are safely inside your *correct* vehicle, use the "Share Trip" or "Safety Toolkit" feature to send your real-time location to a friend or family member.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain why you are refusing to get in.
- You do not need to figure out if the driver made an honest mistake or has malicious intent before moving to safety.
- You do not need to file a formal safety report with the company until you are in a secure, permanent location.
- You do not need to worry about cancellation fees; safety-related cancellations can be disputed with the rideshare company later.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel pressured or awkward when someone insists they are your driver. You have the absolute right to prioritize your safety over social politeness. Verified rideshare drivers expect passengers to check license plates; a legitimate driver will not be offended by your caution.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and harm prevention during a suspicious rideshare pickup attempt. It does not cover long-term legal actions or company refund policies.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information and does not constitute legal advice. If you feel you are in immediate danger or are being harassed, contact 911 immediately.
