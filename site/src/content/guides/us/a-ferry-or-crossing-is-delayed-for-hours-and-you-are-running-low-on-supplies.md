# What to do if… a ferry or crossing is delayed for hours and you are running low on supplies (USA)
## Short answer
Tell terminal staff or crew now if water, food, battery power, heat, or a health need is becoming a problem. Ask where to get water, where charging is available, what you are allowed to do while waiting, and where the next official update will appear.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait until your water is gone or your phone is nearly dead before asking for help.
- Don’t leave a vehicle line, terminal, or restricted area without checking what will happen to your place, unless staying is unsafe.
- Don’t keep streaming, scrolling, or making non-essential calls while battery power is limited.
- Don’t miss announcements while searching repeatedly for updates.
- Don’t post screenshots that show ticket barcodes, QR codes, reservation numbers, or payment details.
- Don’t give food or drink to someone who is not fully alert or cannot swallow safely. Get urgent help.

## What to do now
1. **Deal with any urgent health problem first.**  
   Tell terminal staff or crew immediately if anyone has chest pain, severe difficulty breathing, confusion, fainting, or signs of serious heat illness. Move to a cooler place if you can do so safely. Call **911** for an emergency if help is not already being arranged.

2. **Ask for the rules of the wait.**  
   Ask a terminal agent or crew member:
   - Where can we get drinking water?
   - Where are the toilets and charging points?
   - Are we allowed to leave the vehicle, line, or terminal and return?
   - Where will the next official update be posted or announced?
   
   Mention any medication, accessibility need, small child, pregnancy, or health concern that makes the wait harder.

3. **Secure water and simple food early.**  
   Buy water and food while vendors are open. If vendors are closed, supplies are limited, or you cannot leave the waiting area, ask staff or crew whether drinking water or basic assistance is available. In hot conditions, if someone may have heat exhaustion but is alert and able to drink, encourage frequent sips of cool water.

4. **Reduce phone use now.**  
   Turn on battery saver mode, lower screen brightness, stop streaming, and close unnecessary apps. Use brief text messages for non-emergency check-ins where possible. Keep enough power available for official updates, calls, tickets, directions, and urgent contact.

5. **Save the essentials while you still have power.**  
   Save or screenshot your ticket, QR code, reservation number, operator contact details, and the latest official delay notice. Keep the screenshots private.

6. **Use one official update route.**  
   Check the operator’s website, app, text alerts, or posted terminal notices. If you have no signal, ask staff where updates will be announced. Choose occasional checks instead of constant refreshing.

7. **Ask before leaving the wait.**  
   If you need to leave for water, food, charging, rest, or a health reason, ask what will happen to your place in line and what the operator needs you to do about the ticket. If staying is unsafe, leave for a safer place and deal with ticket questions afterwards.

8. **Keep a small note for later.**  
   Record the scheduled departure time, the time you learned about the delay, and any instruction that affects your ticket or place in line. A photo of an official posted notice may help later.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide now whether to request a refund or make a complaint.
- You do **not** need to keep your phone online continuously.
- You do **not** need to solve every onward-travel problem before you have a realistic update.
- You do **not** need to make a rushed decision about leaving unless safety or health requires it.

## Important reassurance
A long delay can feel unmanageable when basic supplies and battery power are slipping. Securing water, reducing phone use, and identifying one reliable update route reduces the number of immediate problems you need to handle at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the waiting period only. Later decisions about tickets, refunds, insurance, missed connections, or alternative travel may need help from the operator or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. For an urgent health or safety concern, tell terminal staff or crew immediately and call 911 when emergency help is needed.
