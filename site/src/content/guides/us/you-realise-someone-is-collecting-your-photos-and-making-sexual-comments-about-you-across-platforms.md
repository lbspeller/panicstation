# What to do if… you realise someone is collecting your photos and making sexual comments about you across platforms (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all interaction with the accounts involved, preserve evidence by taking screenshots, and report the content to the specific platforms and the CCRI Crisis Helpline. If there are threats of violence or you feel physically unsafe, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply, argue, or attempt to "educate" the person collecting the photos.
- Do not post public call-outs or share the perpetrator's handle (this can lead to further copies being made).
- Do not delete the posts or your account until you have captured screenshots and URLs.
- Do not pay any money if the situation involves demands or threats (sextortion).
- Do not accept friend requests or messages from "investigators" or strangers offering to help for a fee.

## What to do now
1. **Contact a trusted person to help you manage the immediate steps.** **If you are in immediate danger or the person indicates they are near your physical location, call 911.**
   - Ask them to handle the screenshotting process if viewing the content is too distressing.

2. **Preserve evidence before reporting or blocking the accounts.** **Capture full-page screenshots that include usernames, profile links, timestamps, and the specific platform name.**
   - Copy and paste the direct URLs (links) to the offending posts or profiles into a private document.
   - Save these files in a secure, non-public folder or on a dedicated USB drive.

3. **Report the content directly to the platforms using their harassment and non-consensual imagery tools.** **Under the TAKE IT DOWN Act, major platforms are required to review and remove verified non-consensual intimate depictions within 48 hours of a valid report.**
   - Select the options for "Harassment," "Sexual Harassment," or "Inappropriate Content."
   - If your photos are being used to impersonate you, use the "Impersonation" reporting path.

4. **Use technical removal tools to prevent the further spread of the images.** **If you are under 18, use the NCMEC "Take It Down" tool to create a digital fingerprint that helps platforms find and remove your images.**
   - If you are 18 or older, use **StopNCII.org** to proactively block the sharing of specific intimate images on participating platforms.

5. **Restrict all account privacy and communication settings across every platform you use.** **Set profiles to "Private," restrict Direct Messages (DMs) to "Followers Only," and disable tagging or mentions from accounts you do not follow.**
   - Turn off location services for social media apps and review old posts for identifiable landmarks like school or workplace logos.

6. **Contact the CCRI Crisis Helpline for specialist guidance on takedowns.** **Call 844-878-2274 for free, 24/7 confidential support specifically for image-based sexual abuse and cyber-harassment.**
   - They can provide specific advice on dealing with "mirror sites" or adult platforms where your photos may have been reposted.

7. **File a report with the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).** **Visit ic3.gov to report the harassment, especially if it involves extortion, threats, or crossing state lines.**
   - Use the official government site and do not pay any third-party "reporting" services.

8. **Reach out to RAINN for confidential emotional stabilization.** **Call 800-656-HOPE (4673) or use the online chat at rainn.org if you are feeling overwhelmed or panicky.**
   - This service is for support and does not require you to file a police report.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you will file a lawsuit or pursue criminal charges today.
- You do not need to delete your entire digital footprint immediately; containment is the priority.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your broader social circle or family right now.
- You do not need to identify exactly who the person is before taking safety steps.

## Important reassurance
Experiencing this type of targeting is a violation of your privacy and personal safety. It is common to feel a sense of shock, anger, or intense fear when realizing someone is obsessing over your images. Taking immediate steps to isolate yourself from the person and secure your accounts is a standard and effective way to begin regaining control.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Ongoing safety planning, legal consultation, or technical forensic assistance should be handled through the specialists listed in the steps above.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice or a clinical diagnosis. Procedures for platforms and law enforcement agencies are subject to change. If threats escalate, prioritize your physical safety and contact local emergency services immediately.
