# What to do if… you receive conflicting information about where a person who died is being held (USA)

## Short answer
Before travelling, paying, or signing anything, get one case or reference number and confirm two things: who has physical custody of the remains now, and which office controls release to the funeral home.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t act on an unlogged answer from someone who cannot confirm custody, release status, or a case number.
- Don’t assume a funeral home can collect the remains immediately; if a medical examiner or coroner is involved, release may need investigation, identification, examination, or paperwork first.
- Don’t pay deposits or sign contracts to secure a date while custody and release authority are still unclear.
- Don’t post identifying details publicly online to try to locate the person.
- Don’t rely on secondhand updates from staff who are not assigned to the case or cannot say which system they checked.
- Don’t argue over service plans yet; the urgent task is to identify the current custody location and the releasing authority.

## What to do now
1. **Open one running log.** Write down the person’s full name, date of birth if known, date and place of death, and each call: time, phone number, person’s name or title, and exactly what they confirmed.

2. **Get the best available case identifier.** Ask the person or agency that notified you for any police incident number, hospital medical record or account number, medical examiner or coroner case number, or funeral home reference number.

3. **Start with the place that last had direct contact with the person.**
   - If the person died in a hospital, call the hospital operator and ask for Decedent Affairs, Bereavement Services, Patient Affairs, Pathology, the hospital morgue, or the office that handles deaths.
   - If the death was unexpected, unexplained, injury-related, poisoning-related, unattended, or reported to law enforcement, call the local medical examiner or coroner office for the county, district, or state where the death happened.
   - If a funeral home has already been chosen, ask whether they have received the remains, a release authorisation, or a denial because release is still pending.

4. **Ask one exact custody question.** Say: “Can you confirm whether the remains are physically in your custody right now? If not, can you see where they were transferred or who has the current case?”

5. **Ask who controls release.** Say: “Is this a medical examiner or coroner case, or is release controlled by the hospital or funeral home? Is there any hold right now for investigation, identification, examination, autopsy, paperwork, or transport?”

6. **Ask what the legally authorised person needs to provide.** Say: “What authorisation form or documentation is needed from the legal next of kin or the person legally authorised to control disposition, and how can it be submitted?”

7. **If two places give different answers, ask about transfer status.** Say: “I am getting conflicting locations. Is there a pending or recent transfer? From where to where, at what time, by which transport service or funeral home, and who should confirm receipt?”

8. **Ask for one accountable contact.** Say: “Who is the assigned investigator, decedent affairs contact, release desk, or family contact for updates, and what direct number should I use?”

9. **If you have chosen a funeral home, give them one narrow task.** Give them the case or reference number and ask them to confirm the current custody location and release requirements, then report back with the names, offices, times, and any forms needed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the service date, obituary wording, flowers, music, travel plans, or guest list right now.
- You do not need to decide burial, cremation, donation, or viewing arrangements until you know who controls release and what paperwork is needed.
- You do not need to pay money or sign funeral paperwork before custody and release status are clear.

## Important reassurance
Conflicting information can happen because hospital records, medical examiner or coroner records, transport records, and funeral home records may update at different times. A case number, a current custody location, and one named contact usually make the situation more trackable.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to clarify custody, release authority, and the next contact point. Later decisions about death certificates, permits, funeral contracts, investigation records, religious concerns, payment, or disputes may need local specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. USA procedures vary by state, county, hospital, medical examiner or coroner office, and funeral home, so confirm local requirements before acting.
