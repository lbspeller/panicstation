# What to do if… a shared bike or scooter won’t unlock even though the app shows your trip has started (USA)

## Short answer
Don’t force the lock — gather proof fast (screenshots + vehicle ID), then use in-app support to end the trip and reverse the charge before the timer runs.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pry, kick, or “make it work” by force — it risks injury and damage liability.
- Don’t leave without the vehicle number and a screenshot of the active trip.
- Don’t keep starting new rides on other vehicles until the current trip is ended/canceled.
- Don’t spend a long time troubleshooting while the trip meter is running.

## What to do now
1. **Step out of traffic and take a breath (10 seconds).**  
   The priority is to avoid a rushed mistake and stop unnecessary charges.

2. **Capture evidence immediately (30–60 seconds):**
   - Screenshot the active trip screen (showing it started, time, and any error).
   - Photo the **vehicle ID/number/plate** (near the QR code) and the **locked mechanism**.
   - Note your **exact location** (address/intersection or map pin).

3. **Try the safest “reset” fixes (no force, 1–2 attempts total):**
   - Fully **close and reopen** the app, then try unlock again.
   - **If the app shows a lock/unlock control for the active trip,** tap **lock** then **unlock** once.

4. **Try to end/cancel the trip right away.**
   - Use **End Ride / Cancel / Help** from within the active trip.
   - **If it’s a station/docked bike,** reseat it firmly in the dock and wait briefly for the system to register (if applicable).

5. **Use in-app support while you’re still next to the vehicle.**
   - Start a **chat / report a problem / help ticket** from the trip screen if possible.
   - Send: screenshots, vehicle ID, location, and a one-line description (“Trip started in app, vehicle did not unlock”).
   - Ask explicitly: **close the trip now** and **remove the charge/authorization**.

6. **If you can’t end the trip and the clock is running, stop experimenting and reduce risk.**
   - After a couple of attempts, stop trying to unlock.
   - Place the vehicle **upright and out of the way** (not blocking sidewalks, curb ramps, bike lanes, or driveways).
   - Mark/report it in-app as **won’t unlock / not working** so it’s flagged.

7. **If you’re charged for a ride you couldn’t take, line up a dispute the “right way” for your payment type.**
   - Save your screenshots/photos and any support messages.
   - Ask the operator for a refund first (in-app is usually best), then contact your **card issuer/bank** if it isn’t resolved.
   - **If you paid by credit card:** ask your issuer how to file a **billing error dispute** for services not provided. You may need to follow up **in writing** to preserve your rights, and it’s commonly required within about **60 days** of the statement that shows the charge.
   - **If you paid by debit card:** your bank’s process varies, but the same evidence (active-trip screenshot + vehicle ID + support ticket) helps.

8. **Only if the vehicle is creating a real obstruction or hazard:**  
   - In **some cities**, you can use **311** (phone/app) for non-emergency issues in the public right-of-way. Otherwise, stick to the operator’s reporting tools so it’s flagged for pickup.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide whether to escalate publicly or file any formal complaint right now.
- You don’t need to diagnose the cause (battery, connectivity, zone rules, hardware) on the street.
- You don’t need to keep trying different vehicles until the first trip is closed.

## Important reassurance
This happens to lots of riders — it’s often a connectivity or lock hardware glitch, or a mismatch between the app and the vehicle. You’re not doing anything wrong by stopping, documenting, and getting support to close it properly.

## Scope note
This is first-aid for app/vehicle lock failures: stop charges, document, and get the trip closed. Follow-up (refund processing or card disputes) is easier once you’re somewhere calm with your proof saved.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or financial advice. If your surroundings don’t feel safe, prioritize moving to a safer place before dealing with the vehicle or support chat.
