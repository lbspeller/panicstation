# What to do if… a shared bike or scooter won’t unlock even though the app shows your trip has started (USA)

## Short answer
End the trip in the app immediately to stop the meter, document the vehicle ID and lock status with photos, and contact support to request a refund for the failed unlock.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to force, pry, or kick the locking mechanism, as this can cause injury or result in damage fees.
- Don’t walk away while the trip is still active in the app without capturing the vehicle's ID number.
- Don’t keep trying to start new rides on different vehicles until the first active trip is officially closed.
- Don’t spend more than one minute troubleshooting the hardware while the charging timer is running.

## What to do now
1. **Move yourself and the vehicle to a safe area away from traffic.**  
   Ensure you are on a sidewalk or out of the path of pedestrians and vehicles before looking at your phone.

2. **Check the app map for restricted zones.**  
   **Verify that the vehicle is not located in a "No-Ride Zone" or "No-Parking Zone," as these areas often trigger automatic locks.**

3. **Try to end the trip within 60 seconds of starting.**  
   **End the ride immediately using the "End Ride" or "Cancel" button, as some operators automatically waive charges for trips lasting less than one minute.**

4. **Capture evidence of the failure.**  
   - **Screenshot the active trip screen** showing the timer running.
   - **Take a photo of the vehicle ID number** (usually near the QR code or on the rear fender).
   - **Take a photo of the locked mechanism** (the docked arm or the wheel lock) to show it did not release.

5. **Perform a single software-based reset.**  
   **Close and relaunch the app once, and if a "Lock/Unlock" toggle is visible on the trip screen, tap it one time to see if the signal resends.**

6. **Submit a support ticket through the active trip screen.**  
   **Use the "Report an Issue" or "Help" button to send your photos and a one-sentence message: "Trip started but vehicle remained physically locked; please end trip and refund."**

7. **Secure the vehicle properly before leaving.**  
   - **If the vehicle is dockless, park it upright and out of the way of curb ramps or sidewalks.**
   - **If the vehicle is at a station, ensure it is firmly pushed into a dock even if it never unlocked.**

8. **Prepare for a formal financial dispute if support does not respond.**  
   - **For credit cards:** Note that you must mail a written "billing error" notice to the issuer to reach them within 60 days of the statement date showing the charge.
   - **For debit cards:** Contact your bank immediately, but be aware that Regulation E protections for service failures differ from credit card rights.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical cause of the lock failure (e.g., Bluetooth vs. cellular lag).
- You do not need to contact 311 unless the vehicle is physically blocking a hazard that you cannot move it from.
- You do not need to wait on hold for a live agent if the in-app reporting tool allows photo uploads.

## Important reassurance
Lock failures are a common occurrence due to GPS drift, cellular dead zones, or hardware wear. Documenting the vehicle ID and ending the trip quickly is the standard procedure and is usually sufficient to resolve the billing error.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a failed vehicle unlock and immediate charge mitigation. It does not cover long-term account disputes or personal injury claims.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Consumer protections under the Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA) for credit cards and Regulation E for debit cards involve specific legal timelines and requirements that vary by financial institution.
