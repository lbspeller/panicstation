# What to do if… your car’s locks or windows stop working and you can’t secure the vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Relocate the vehicle to a high-visibility, monitored area immediately; remove all valuables, personal identification, and address-linked documents; and contact a mobile automotive locksmith or a tow service to move the car to a secure facility.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the vehicle unattended in a dark, isolated, or unmonitored location.
- Do not leave "Real ID" documents, passports, or mail showing your home address in the cabin.
- Do not attempt to force a power window up with pliers or tools, as this can shatter the glass or destroy the regulator motor.
- Do not use duct tape directly on car paint to hold a window up; use painters tape and plastic sheeting if necessary.
- Do not call 911 for a mechanical failure unless you are in immediate physical danger or a crime is currently being committed.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a "Safe Pause" location immediately.** If the vehicle is drivable, go to a well-lit area with active security cameras, such as a 24-hour grocery store, a hospital entrance, or a police station parking lot.
2. **Clear the vehicle of all "Identity Targets."** Take the registration, insurance cards, and any mail with you, as these provide a thief with your home address and the means to use your garage door opener if it is programmed into the car.
3. **Activate your hazard lights if stopped on a shoulder.** Under 2026 "Move Over" laws in many states (including CA and WI), passing drivers are legally required to shift lanes or slow down for any disabled vehicle displaying emergency flashers.
4. **Identify the specific point of failure.** 
   - If the key fob fails: attempt to use the physical "emergency key" hidden inside the fob to manually turn the driver’s door lock.
   - If the door won't latch: do not drive the vehicle; the door may swing open during a turn.
5. **Call for specialized technical help.** 
   - Contact an **Automotive Locksmith** for mechanical lock or electronic fob failures, as standard roadside assistance (AAA) often only covers lock-out retrieval, not repair.
   - Contact **Roadside Assistance** if the vehicle is undriveable or requires a tow to a dealership or secure garage.
6. **Log out of in-car digital services.** If your 2026-model vehicle has "Always-On" connectivity or saved payment methods in the infotainment system, use your phone app to remotely lock the system or log out before leaving the car.
7. **Stay with the vehicle if it cannot be moved.** If you are in a high-theft area and the window is stuck down, remain inside the vehicle with the engine off and doors (that still work) locked until a tow truck or locksmith arrives.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the failure is a fuse, a motor, or a software glitch right now.
- You do not need to file an insurance claim immediately unless the vehicle is actually broken into or stolen.
- You do not need to worry about long-term parking solutions until the immediate 24-hour security of the vehicle is established.

## Important reassurance
It is a high-stress situation to have a vehicle that cannot be closed or locked, especially at night. This response is a standard mechanical or electrical failure, and your priority is simply reducing the vehicle's "target profile" until a professional can take over.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for vehicle security failures. It does not cover mechanical repair steps or legal claims following a theft.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional mechanical advice. Safety standards and Move Over laws vary by state. If you feel your personal safety is at risk, contact local law enforcement via their non-emergency line or 911 if an active threat is present.
