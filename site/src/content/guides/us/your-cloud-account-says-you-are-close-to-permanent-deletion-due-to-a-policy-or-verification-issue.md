# What to do if… your cloud account says you are close to permanent deletion due to a policy or verification issue (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the device from the internet to prevent further syncing, then log in to the web-browser version of the service on a different device to determine if the data is missing from the server or just failing to display locally.

## Do not do these things
- Don't empty the Trash, Recycle Bin, or Recently Deleted folders to "clear space" or "reset" the view.
- Don't uninstall and reinstall the application immediately, as this may delete local cached files that have not yet synced.
- Don't create new files with the same names as the missing ones, as this can overwrite version history.
- Don't follow "recovery" instructions from unsolicited messages or third-party "data fix" websites.
- Don't assume the files are permanently gone until you have checked the Activity Log or Events history of the account.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze the current state of your devices.**
   - **Immediately turn off Wi-Fi and cellular data on the device where the files appear empty.**
   - Stop all manual sync attempts or "Refresh" actions to prevent a local "empty" state from overwriting the cloud server.

2. **Establish the "Ground Truth" via a web browser.**
   - **Log in to the official web portal of the service (e.g., drive.google.com, icloud.com, onedrive.live.com) using a different device or an Incognito/Private window.**
   - If your files appear here, the issue is a local software or sync glitch on your primary device.
   - If the web view is also empty, the issue is account-wide.

3. **Verify the account identity and workspace scope.**
   - **Check the profile icon or settings to confirm the exact email address used for sign-in.**
   - Ensure you are not logged into a secondary "Personal" account instead of a "Work" account, or vice versa.
   - Look for a "Toggle" or "Switch Account" option if your provider uses multiple profiles (e.g., Google Profiles or Microsoft Tenants).

4. **Review the Activity Log or Events history.**
   - **Locate the "Activity," "Events," or "Recent Changes" section in the web interface.**
   - Look for entries indicating mass deletions, moves, or permission changes.
   - Identify if the files were moved to a different folder or if "Owner" permissions were revoked.

5. **Search for "Orphaned" or "Unorganized" files.**
   - **Type `is:unorganized` into the search bar if using Google Drive, or check for a "Shared with me" filter.**
   - Files can sometimes lose their parent folder and become "orphaned" but still exist in the account's total storage.

6. **Examine recovery bins and retention periods.**
   - **Check the Trash / Recycle Bin / Recently Deleted folder and do not empty it.**
   - Most personal accounts (Google, OneDrive, iCloud) hold deleted items for 30 days.
   - Standard Dropbox accounts hold items for 30 days, while Professional/Plus may hold them for 180 or 365 days.

7. **Escalate to Enterprise or Workspace Admins if applicable.**
   - **Contact your organization's IT department immediately if this is a work or school account.**
   - Admins can often recover files for up to 25 days after they have been "permanently" emptied from a user's trash using tools like the Admin Console or Google Vault.

8. **Secure the account if unauthorized activity is found.**
   - **Initiate an official password reset and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) if the Activity Log shows logins you do not recognize.**
   - Use the "Sign out of all other sessions" feature to force-close any unauthorized access.

9. **Report suspected identity theft to federal authorities.**
   - **Visit IdentityTheft.gov if you believe your credentials were stolen to gain access to your files.**
   - Use ReportFraud.ftc.gov if the data loss was part of a broader scam or extortion attempt.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new storage provider right now.
- You do not need to manually re-upload backups until you have confirmed the current account is secure and stable.
- You do not need to notify all collaborators until you have confirmed if the files are deleted or if you simply lost "View" permissions.

## Important reassurance
An "empty" screen is a common technical symptom and does not automatically mean the data has been erased from the provider's servers. Syncing delays, cached errors, and accidental sign-ins to secondary accounts cause the majority of these incidents. Most major cloud providers have multiple layers of "soft" deletion and version history specifically designed to prevent permanent loss.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital data loss. It does not cover deep-level forensic recovery or hardware-level drive repair.

## Important note
This guide provides general technical information and is not a substitute for professional IT support or legal advice regarding data privacy. If you suspect a criminal data breach or identity theft, prioritize official recovery channels provided by the Federal Trade Commission and your service provider.
