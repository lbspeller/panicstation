# What to do if… you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without your consent (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer, calmer moment, then contact specialist image-abuse support before confronting anyone or responding to threats. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay money, send gift cards, share account access, or send more images to “make it stop”.
- Don’t keep replying to threats or bargaining if you can avoid it.
- Don’t forward the content to friends “to confirm” what it is.
- Don’t confront the person you suspect while you are distressed or alone.
- Don’t repeatedly re-check posts or re-watch content if you find it; take practical steps, then step away.
- Don’t send any sexual image or video to a helpline, police tip form, platform report, or support service unless that service clearly asks for it and you are sure it is allowed.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you are with the person you suspect, get distance: leave the room, go somewhere with other people, call someone you trust, or move to a place where you can think. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
2. **Contact the CCRI Image Abuse Helpline.** Tell them you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without consent and you want help with takedown, safety, and next steps. You do not need proof before asking for help.
3. **If you have the actual image or video file, consider StopNCII.org.** It can create a digital fingerprint of intimate images or videos so participating platforms can help detect and block matching uploads. If you do not have the file, skip this and focus on reporting where it appears.
4. **If you were under 18 when any image or video was made, use NCMEC’s Take It Down tool.** It is for nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos taken before age 18 and can help participating platforms remove or limit sharing. Do not download or share illegal material to use a tool; use only files already on your own device if the tool says to.
5. **Report it where it appears.** Use the platform’s reporting option for non-consensual intimate imagery, image-based abuse, sexual content shared without permission, or a similar category. If it is on multiple services, start with the most public or fastest-spreading place first.
6. **If you may want to report later, preserve basic details without spreading the content.** Save links, usernames, profile URLs, dates, times, payment demands, and screenshots that show the page and URL bar. Avoid forwarding the image or video itself.
7. **If you are being blackmailed or pressured online, stop feeding the threat.** Do not pay, do not send more images, block or mute if it is safe to do so, and consider reporting to local law enforcement, the FBI, or IC3 if the threat is online or involves payment demands.
8. **Tell one safe person what is happening.** A trusted friend, family member, advocate, campus staff member, or sexual assault hotline worker can sit with you, help you report, and reduce the urge to act alone while panicked.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to go to police.
- You do not need to prove what happened before asking for help.
- You do not need to contact the suspected person right now.
- You do not need to search every site for every possible copy before starting takedown steps.
- You do not need to decide legal, school, work, immigration, or relationship consequences in this first moment.

## Important reassurance
Feeling frozen, sick, ashamed, angry, or frantic to fix it immediately is a common response to a serious violation. This is not your fault, and you are allowed to move one step at a time.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce harm and stabilize the situation. Later decisions about legal options, police reports, protective orders, school or workplace reporting, and longer-term safety planning may need specialist help in your state.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe, are being threatened, or were under 18 when any image was made, prioritize immediate safety and specialist support.
