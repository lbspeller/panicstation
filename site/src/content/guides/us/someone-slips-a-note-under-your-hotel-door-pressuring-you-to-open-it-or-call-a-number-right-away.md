# What to do if… someone slips a note under your hotel door pressuring you to open it or call a number right away (USA)

## Short answer
Do **not** open the door or call the provided number. **Secure the door locks, step away from the entrance, and contact the hotel front desk or security directly using the official room phone or a verified number to request an immediate hallway check.**

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to look into the hallway or check for the person who left the note.
- Do not call the phone number, text the number, or scan any QR codes printed on the note.
- Do not engage with anyone through the door who claims to be staff or security until you have verified their presence with the front desk.
- Do not provide credit card details, personal identifiers, or travel itineraries to any caller prompted by the note.
- Do not handle the note excessively if it contains specific threats.

## What to do now
1. **Engage all physical door locks immediately.**  
   Flip the deadbolt, slide the security latch or bar, and ensure the door is fully latched.
2. **Check the peephole/viewfinder without making noise.**  
   Look to see if anyone is currently standing in the hallway or if the hallway lights are behaving unusually (e.g., flickering due to motion).
3. **Contact the front desk using a verified internal line.**  
   Press the "Front Desk" or "Operator" button on the hotel room phone, or dial the hotel's main number found on your official digital booking confirmation.
   - State your room number and report that a note was slipped under the door.
   - Request that a security officer or manager be sent to your floor to verify the hallway is clear.
4. **Request a specific identification protocol.**  
   If the hotel claims they sent staff, tell the front desk: "I will not open the door until the person outside identifies themselves and I confirm their name and appearance with you over this phone line."
5. **Call 911 if you hear attempted entry or direct threats.**  
   If you hear the handle turning, scratching at the lock, or verbal threats from the hallway, contact emergency services immediately.
6. **Document the note and the hallway state.**  
   Take a clear photograph of the note where it landed and note the exact time you discovered it. 
7. **Ask for an internal incident report number.**  
   Request that the hotel management document the event in their daily security log and provide you with a reference number for your records.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the note was a prank, a legitimate error, or a targeted scam right now.
- You do not need to check out or leave the hotel immediately unless security cannot verify the floor is safe.
- You do not need to file a police report immediately unless there was an attempted break-in or a specific, credible threat of violence.

## Important reassurance
Notes designed to create a sense of "urgent crisis" (e.g., "Emergency: Call Front Desk Immediately regarding your payment") are a known tactic used to bypass logical security checks. Staying behind a locked door and using a verified communication channel is the standard safety response for these situations.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspicious or high-pressure physical message received at a hotel. It does not cover long-term travel security planning or legal actions against lodging providers.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice. If you feel you are being targeted or tracked, or if the hotel staff does not respond to your security request, contact local law enforcement via 911.
