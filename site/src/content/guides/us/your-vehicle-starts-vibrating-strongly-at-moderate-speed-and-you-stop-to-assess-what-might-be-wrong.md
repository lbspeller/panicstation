# What to do if… your vehicle starts vibrating strongly at moderate speed and you stop to assess what might be wrong (USA)

## Short answer
Pull over immediately to a safe area away from active traffic and do not attempt to drive the vehicle further to "test" the issue. Call for roadside assistance if you see any visible tire damage or if the vibration makes the car difficult to control.

## Do not do these things
- Do not increase your speed to see if the vibration smooths out.
- Do not stop in a live travel lane if the vehicle is still capable of rolling to a shoulder or exit.
- Do not exit the vehicle on the side facing active traffic.
- Do not stand between your vehicle and oncoming traffic or directly behind it.
- Do not attempt to crawl under the vehicle to inspect the suspension at the roadside.
- Do not touch the wheels, rims, or brake components as they may be hot enough to cause burns.
- Do not attempt to change a tire if you are on a narrow shoulder with high-speed traffic.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to the furthest possible point from moving traffic.**  
   Activate your hazard lights immediately and steer smoothly toward a wide shoulder, parking lot, or off-ramp. Avoid sudden braking which can exacerbate stability issues if a tire or wheel component is failing.
2. **Determine the safest waiting position for all occupants.**  
   - If a permanent physical barrier (like a guardrail or concrete wall) is present and reachable without crossing traffic, exit through the passenger-side doors and wait well behind the barrier.
   - If no barrier exists or the shoulder is narrow, remain inside the vehicle with seatbelts fastened and hazard lights on.
3. **Perform a brief visual-only inspection from the non-traffic side if safe.**  
   Scan the wheels and tires for specific danger signs:
   - Visible bulges or "bubbles" in the tire sidewall.
   - Exposed metal wires or fabric cords in the tread.
   - Missing lug nuts or a wheel that appears tilted or misaligned.
   - Smoke, a strong burning smell, or fluid pooling near the wheels.
4. **Call for professional recovery or emergency services.**  
   Contact your roadside assistance provider or a towing service. If your vehicle is partially blocking a lane, or if you feel you are in immediate danger of being struck by other motorists, call **911**.
5. **Keep the vehicle stationary until help arrives.**  
   Do not attempt to drive to a repair shop even at low speeds if the vibration was severe. A failing tire or loose wheel can detach or blow out completely, leading to a total loss of control.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the mechanical cause (e.g., balance, alignment, or suspension) right now.
- You do not need to determine repair costs or select a specific mechanic while at the roadside.
- You do not need to take photos for insurance until you are in a fully stabilized, off-road location.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled or anxious when a vehicle begins to shake unexpectedly. Choosing to stop immediately is the standard safety protocol to prevent a mechanical failure from becoming a high-speed collision.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only for immediate roadside safety. It does not replace a professional mechanical inspection required to return the vehicle to service.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute professional mechanical or legal advice. If you are in a life-threatening situation or stranded in an unsafe location, prioritize calling 911 immediately.
