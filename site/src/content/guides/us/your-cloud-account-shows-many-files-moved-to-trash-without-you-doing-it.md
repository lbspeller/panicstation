# What to do if… your cloud account shows many files moved to trash without you doing it (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all deletion or "cleanup" activities and check the web-browser version of your cloud service for the Recently Deleted, Trash, or Archive folders. Verify you are signed into the correct account and check shared album permissions before attempting any technical resets.

## Do not do these things
- Do not empty the Trash, Bin, or Recently Deleted folders on any device.
- Do not use third-party "storage optimizer" or "duplicate remover" apps while files are missing.
- Do not sign out of your cloud account on your primary device until you have verified the web version.
- Do not factory reset your phone or tablet.
- Do not grant access permissions to new third-party apps during the recovery process.
- Do not assume items are permanently gone if they do not appear in the mobile app's main timeline.

## What to do now
1. **Immediately pause syncing to prevent further data loss.** **Turn on Airplane Mode on your mobile device or pause the sync client on your computer to stop the current state from overwriting older data.**
   - If you share a family library, notify all members to stop moving or deleting files immediately.
2. **Access your cloud service through a desktop web browser rather than the mobile app.** **Sign in to the web interface (icloud.com, photos.google.com, or onedrive.com) to see the most accurate representation of what is stored on the servers.**
   - Confirm the email address or Apple ID at the top of the screen is the exact one you use for backups.
   - If you have multiple accounts, log out and check each one individually.
3. **Locate and inspect the recovery folders specifically for your service.** **Look for folders named Recently Deleted, Trash, Bin, Hidden, or Archive.**
   - iCloud: check "Recently Deleted" in the sidebar (items stay for 30 days).
   - Google Photos: check "Trash" (stays for 60 days if backed up, 30 days if not) and "Archive" (which is now called "Hidden" on mobile).
   - OneDrive: check the "Recycle bin" and the "Personal Vault" if it was enabled.
4. **Verify that the backup and sync settings are still active on your primary device.** **Open your device settings to confirm that "iCloud Photos," "Google Photos Backup," or "OneDrive Camera Upload" is toggled to the 'On' position.**
   - Ensure the account listed in these settings matches the one you checked on the web browser.
5. **Review your account sign-in activity to rule out unauthorized access.** **Check the "Security" or "Recent Activity" section of your Apple, Google, or Microsoft account for any devices you do not recognize.**
   - If an unknown device is present, change your password immediately and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
6. **Use official subscription-level recovery tools if you have a paid plan.** **Utilize the "Restore your OneDrive" feature if you are a Microsoft 365 subscriber to roll back your entire library to a point within the last 30 days.**
   - For Google One or Apple+ subscribers, contact official support chat/phone lines directly as they may have narrow windows for server-side recovery.
7. **Report suspected account takeover to the appropriate federal authorities.** **Visit IdentityTheft.gov (FTC) to create a recovery plan if you believe your account was hacked or your personal information was stolen.**
   - Use the local non-emergency police line if a report is required by your service provider; do not call 911 for digital file loss.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to switch to a different cloud provider.
- Organizing or tagging recovered photos.
- Deleting "junk" photos to free up storage space.
- Installing new backup software or hardware.

## Important reassurance
Digital "disappearances" are frequently caused by simple interface changes, such as a sync being paused, a filter being applied to the view, or an accidental move to an Archive or Hidden folder. Accessing the cloud service via a web browser often reveals that the files are still safely on the server and simply not displaying on the mobile device.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only for finding and restoring missing cloud media. If the loss is due to a permanent server-side error or expired retention window, professional data recovery services or direct contact with the provider’s technical support may be required.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional IT, legal, or forensic advice. Recovery windows for deleted items are strictly enforced by providers and can change without notice. Always prioritize using official recovery tools provided by Apple, Google, or Microsoft before attempting third-party software solutions, which may pose security risks.
