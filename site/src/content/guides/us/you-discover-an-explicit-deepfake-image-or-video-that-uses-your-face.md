# What to do if… you discover an explicit deepfake image or video that uses your face (USA)

## Short answer
Document the location of the content and report it immediately to the hosting platform and the CCRI Crisis Helpline for specialized takedown assistance. Under the federal TAKE IT DOWN Act (2025), major platforms must provide a removal process for non-consensual deepfakes.

## Do not do these things
- Don't negotiate with or pay an extortionist (payment often leads to increased demands).
- Don't share or post the content publicly to attempt to "debunk" it.
- Don't send the files to third parties for verification purposes.
- Don't delete all evidence immediately without first documenting URLs and account names.
- Don't provide any additional personal information, identification, or media to the person who posted it.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause.** Stop looking at the content and move to a location where you can record information without further immediate exposure.
2. **Record the technical details of the source.** Note down the specific URL, the platform name, the username of the uploader, and any timestamps.
   - Capture a screenshot that includes the platform interface and the date/time on your device.
   - Save the link to the profile or account responsible for the post.
3. **Submit a formal report to the platform.** Use the reporting tool for "Non-Consensual Intimate Imagery" (NCII), "Sexual Exploitation," or "Harassment."
   - Under federal law as of May 2026, covered platforms are required to remove reported NCII and "digital forgeries" (deepfakes) within 48 hours of a valid report.
4. **Contact the CCRI Crisis Helpline.** This service provides technical guidance on documentation and removal specifically for deepfake abuse.
   - Phone: **1-844-878-2274** (Hours vary; check the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative website for current availability).
5. **Utilize hashing tools to prevent re-uploads.** Use specialized services that create a digital fingerprint (hash) of the content on your device so platforms can block it automatically.
   - For individuals 18 or older: Use **StopNCII.org**.
   - For individuals under 18 (or content showing you as a minor): Use **Take It Down** (operated by NCMEC).
6. **File a report with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).** If the deepfake is being used for extortion, threats, or harassment, submit a report at **www.ic3.gov**.
7. **Consult with a legal professional regarding civil action.** The federal **DEFIANCE Act** allows victims of non-consensual deepfakes to sue creators and distributors for damages in federal court.
8. **Call 911 if there is an immediate threat to your physical safety.** Safety protocols take priority over content removal.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the creator or uploader immediately.
- You do not need to notify your employer, school, or family right now.
- You do not need to make a public statement or social media post today.
- You can wait to decide whether to pursue a full criminal or civil case until you have consulted with a specialist.

## Important reassurance
The shock and distress caused by digital impersonation are standard physiological responses to a violation of privacy. Finding this content does not mean you have lost control of your reputation; specialized tools and federal laws are specifically designed to assist in removing this imagery and holding actors accountable.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and reporting procedures only. It does not replace legal advice or professional security services.

## Important note
Federal and state laws regarding artificial intelligence and digital likeness are evolving. The effectiveness of removal requests depends on platform compliance and the nature of the content.
