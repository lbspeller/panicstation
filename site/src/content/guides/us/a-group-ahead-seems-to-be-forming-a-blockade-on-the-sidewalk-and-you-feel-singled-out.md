# What to do if… a group ahead seems to be forming a blockade on the sidewalk and you feel singled out (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter the obstructed area. Immediately create distance by turning around, crossing the street, or entering a staffed public building, and call 911 if you believe there is an imminent threat to your physical safety.

## Do not do these things
- Don't walk into the blockade to test their intentions or "see what happens."
- Don't argue, use insults, or attempt to reason with a group that is demonstrating hostile body language.
- Don't stop directly in front of the group to record video if it prevents you from exiting the area or increases the level of focus on you.
- Don't allow yourself to be steered into confined areas with no secondary exits, such as alleys, narrow stairwells, or dead-end vestibules.
- Don't go directly to your home or your parked vehicle if you suspect you are being followed or tracked.
- Don't initiate physical contact or attempt to push through the group.

## What to do now
1. **Change your direction of travel early and decisively.** Turn around as if you realized you forgot an item, cross to the opposite side of the street, or turn at the nearest corner before you reach the group. 
2. **Move toward a location that is bright, busy, and staffed.** Identify a store, café, hotel lobby, or bank where employees are present and the public can enter freely.
3. **Seek immediate assistance from employees or security staff.** State clearly: "I feel unsafe outside; may I stay here for a few minutes?" 
4. **Ask staff to call 911 if you are concerned about being intercepted when you leave.** Having a third party call can provide immediate witnesses and professional documentation of the threat.
5. **Call 911 yourself if you believe a physical assault or illegal detention is imminent.** Provide your exact address, a description of the group, and mention if you saw any weapons or heard specific threats.
6. **Maintain body language that indicates you are leaving rather than challenging.** Keep your eyes on your exit path rather than engaging in prolonged eye contact or verbal sparring with group members.
7. **Verify you are not being followed by taking an indirect route once you are away.** Change direction multiple times or remain in a secure, public location until you are certain no one is coordinating with the group to track your movement.
8. **Document the details of the incident once you have reached a secure location.** Write down the time, the exact location, the number of individuals involved, and specific descriptions of their clothing or distinguishing features.
9. **Report the incident to local law enforcement or federal authorities if you believe you were targeted.** If the incident involved bias regarding race, religion, sexual orientation, or identity, you can submit a tip to the FBI at tips.fbi.gov or call 1-800-CALL-FBI.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now if the situation was "serious enough" to justify your reaction.
- You do not need to prove your right to use the sidewalk or "stand your ground" in a non-defensive context.
- You do not need to review any footage or photos you took until you are in a private, locked environment.
- You do not need to notify your social circle or post about the event immediately.

## Important reassurance
Feeling startled or intimidated by a group blockade is a natural safety response. It is common to experience a "freeze" or "hesitation" moment when your path is suddenly obstructed; this is a physiological reaction to a perceived threat and not a sign of weakness. Choosing to avoid a confrontation is a proactive safety tactic.

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate physical stabilization and exiting a high-tension public encounter. It does not cover long-term legal actions, restraining orders, or self-defense training.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional safety advice. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, contact 911 or your local emergency services immediately. For support after a crime, you may contact the VictimConnect Resource Center at 855-484-2846.
