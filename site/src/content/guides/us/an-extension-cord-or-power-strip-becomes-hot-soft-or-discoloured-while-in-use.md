# What to do if… an extension cord or power strip becomes hot, soft, or discoloured while in use (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the device immediately and unplug it from the wall outlet. If the plug is too hot to touch or shows signs of melting, turn off the power at the circuit breaker first.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue using the cord even if the connected appliances still have power.
- Do not plug the cord back in to test if the heat returns.
- Do not touch exposed wires, melted plastic, or scorched metal components with bare hands.
- Do not attempt to repair a cord that has overheated by using electrical tape or replacing the plug.
- Do not run extension cords or power strips under rugs, carpets, or furniture where heat can be trapped.
- Do not plug a power strip into another power strip or an extension cord.
- Do not use extension cords as a permanent solution for home wiring.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the area and check for immediate danger.** If you see active smoke, flames, or hear persistent loud crackling, evacuate the building immediately and call 911.
2. **Disconnect the power source safely.**
   - **Option A:** If the plug and outlet appear normal, grasp the plug firmly and pull it from the wall. Do not pull on the cord itself.
   - **Option B:** If the plug is melting, sparking, scorched, or feels intensely hot, go to the electrical service panel and switch the circuit breaker to the OFF position.
3. **Move the compromised item to a safe cooling area.** Once unplugged, move the cord or strip to a non-combustible, non-conductive surface such as a tile or stone floor. Keep it away from curtains, bedding, or paper products.
4. **Identify and flag the hazard.** Mark the cord or power strip with a "DANGER / DO NOT USE" label or place it in a sealed bag to ensure it is not accidentally plugged in by someone else. 
5. **Inspect the wall outlet for damage.** Check the outlet for black soot, brown discoloration, or a loose fit. If the outlet itself is damaged, keep the circuit breaker off for that area.
6. **Relocate high-draw appliances.** Move items like space heaters, air conditioners, microwaves, or toasters to their own dedicated wall outlets. 
7. **Contact a professional if wiring damage is suspected.** If the wall outlet was hot or the breaker tripped, contact a licensed electrician to inspect the home's fixed wiring and the receptacle.

## What can wait
- You do not need to calculate the exact wattage load of your devices right now.
- You do not need to choose a replacement model immediately.
- You do not need to decide on permanent electrical upgrades or additional outlet installations today.

## Important reassurance
Noticing heat, softening, or discoloration is a vital safety warning that allows you to prevent a fire before it starts. Taking the item out of service immediately is the most effective way to eliminate the risk of an electrical fire or shock in this situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for overheating portable electrical equipment only. It does not cover the repair of fixed home wiring or the diagnosis of complex electrical faults.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not replace the advice of a licensed electrician or fire safety professional. If you rent your home, notify your landlord of any scorched or damaged wall outlets as this is a required safety repair.
