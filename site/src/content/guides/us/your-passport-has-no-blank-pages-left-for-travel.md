# What to do if… your passport has no blank pages left for travel (USA)

## Short answer
Pause and check the blank-page requirements for your destination and any transit points. If your passport book does not meet the requirements, you cannot add pages to it: renew the book, use the urgent-travel appointment route in the United States if you qualify, or contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate if you are abroad.

## Do not do these things
- Do not add, remove, attach, or alter pages. This may make the passport unusable for travel.
- Do not rely on partial spaces, informal advice, or an assumption that the requirement will be overlooked.
- Do not assume an airline can waive a destination’s document requirements.
- Do not book non-refundable onward travel until the document requirement and your next step are clear.

## What to do now
1. **Check the requirement for your exact route.** Review the official requirements for each destination and transit point. Ask the airline or visa provider what blank-page requirement they believe is not met.
2. **Look through the passport book carefully.** Check whether you have the number of blank visa or stamp pages required for the route. Some destinations require two to four blank pages.
3. **Renew the passport book if you need more pages.** Pages cannot be added to a current U.S. passport book. When applying, select the large-book option if extra visa pages would help.
4. **Use the urgent-travel route if you are in the United States and travel is close.** An appointment at a passport agency or center may be available for urgent travel abroad in the next 14 days or for a foreign visa needed in the next 28 days. Follow the official instructions for whether you have already applied. An appointment is not guaranteed.
5. **Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate if you are abroad.** Follow the post’s instructions. Ask what passport service is available, what documents to bring, and whether any proposed passport will be accepted for your onward destinations and transit points.
6. **Pause onward travel until the document issue is clear.** If you are already at an airport or border, ask the airline or travel provider about rebooking options while you arrange the appropriate passport route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve your whole itinerary at once.
- You do not need to decide how to manage future frequent travel today.
- You do not need to argue with airline staff or border officials.
- You do not need to book replacement travel until you know which passport route applies.

## Important reassurance
This is a document problem with a defined next step. Focus only on checking the route requirement and using the appropriate renewal, urgent-travel, or embassy and consulate process.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Destination rules, transit rules, and the available passport service may vary, so later decisions may need help from passport staff, the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate, or your travel provider.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Airlines and border authorities make their own boarding and entry decisions.
