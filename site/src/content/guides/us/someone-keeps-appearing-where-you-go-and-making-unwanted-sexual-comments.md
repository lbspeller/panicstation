# What to do if… someone keeps appearing where you go and making unwanted sexual comments (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the repeated pattern as a safety concern, not a misunderstanding you have to resolve alone. At the earliest safe pause, move near other people, tell someone you trust, and call 911 if there is an emergency requiring immediate assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not dismiss the pattern because each incident seems small on its own.
- Do not meet the person alone to try to settle it.
- Do not share your plans, route changes, or usual locations publicly.
- Do not pressure yourself to keep every routine unchanged.
- Do not pressure yourself to report before you feel ready, unless you need emergency help.

## What to do now
1. Move to the earliest safer pause available. Go near other people or staff, such as a staffed store, front desk, office reception, campus office, transit counter, or busy business. Avoid heading somewhere isolated while you are upset or being followed.

2. Tell one trusted person what is happening in direct terms. Say that the same person keeps appearing where you go and making sexual comments. Ask them to stay on the phone, meet you, walk with you, or arrange a check-in.

3. If it is safe and manageable, keep existing messages or screenshots and make a brief note of what happened.

4. Call 911 if there is an emergency requiring immediate police, fire-department, or ambulance assistance. If there is no immediate emergency, you may choose to contact your local police department through its non-emergency route. A victim advocate can help you consider this option without pressuring you to report.

5. Tell staff or security at the place where it is happening so they know you need support while you are there. If the incidents are connected to a school or college, ask the school who handles Title IX or sex-based harassment concerns. If they are connected to your workplace, you can tell a manager, HR, or workplace security.

6. Make only the routine changes that feel practical for the next few days. This might mean arranging company for arrivals or departures, choosing a busier route, or changing an exact departure time. You are not responsible for stopping the other person’s behavior.

7. Contact a victim support service. VictimConnect can help you find local support. RAINN or a local victim-service provider can help you think through immediate safety and your options. You can seek support whether or not you choose to report.

## What can wait
You do not need to decide today whether to seek a protective order, use a workplace or school process, make a detailed report, or change your routine long term. Focus only on the next safer step and the next supportive contact.

## Important reassurance
Repeatedly turning up and making sexual comments can feel frightening and disruptive, even when individual incidents might look accidental to someone else. You do not have to confront the person or prove the whole pattern before seeking support.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on immediate safety, support, and preserving your options. Later decisions may need help from a victim advocate or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if there is immediate danger or an emergency requiring immediate assistance. A specialist support service can help you consider next steps when there is no immediate emergency.
