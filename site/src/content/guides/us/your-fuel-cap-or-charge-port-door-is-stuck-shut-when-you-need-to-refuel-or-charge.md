# What to do if… your fuel cap or charge-port door is stuck shut when you need to refuel or charge (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safe pause, unlock the vehicle fully, then try gentle pressure at the latch side while using the normal release. If it still will not open, use only the owner’s-manual release method for your vehicle or call roadside assistance instead of forcing it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pry with metal tools against painted edges; this can scratch paint, bend the door, or jam the latch further.
- Do not use open flames, a lighter, or boiling water to thaw a stuck fuel door or charge-port door.
- For an EV, do not pour water into the charge port itself.
- Do not yank on a charging connector that feels locked; some vehicles lock the plug until the car or charging session releases it.
- Do not keep blocking a pump or charging stall while troubleshooting if you can safely move.
- Do not remove trim at the curb unless the owner’s manual clearly tells you to access a manual release there.

## What to do now
1. **Get to the safest practical pause.**
   - If you are stopped where the vehicle could be a hazard, switch on hazard lights.
   - If you are on a shoulder, narrow road, or unsafe forecourt area, prioritize getting away from traffic before troubleshooting.
   - If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

2. **Reset the vehicle lock state.**
   - Fully unlock the vehicle with the fob, app, or door unlock button.
   - Try one calm lock-to-unlock cycle, then try the fuel door or charge-port door again.
   - On some vehicles, the fuel door or charge-port door is tied to central locking.

3. **Try gentle counter-pressure at the latch side.**
   - Press the fuel door or charge-port door inward near the latch side while using the normal release again.
   - Use fingertips or the flat of your hand, not a tool.
   - Stop if the panel bends, scrapes, or feels like it is catching hard.

4. **Check for ice, grit, or a misaligned edge.**
   - Look around the seam and hinge edge.
   - If the outside seam is frozen, use lukewarm water on the outer door seam only, then wipe it dry and retry.
   - Do not put water into an EV charge port.

5. **Use the owner’s manual before searching behind panels.**
   - Search the manual for "fuel door", "fuel filler door", "manual release", "emergency release", "charge port door", or "charge cable release".
   - Some vehicles have a pull-cable, lever, or manual override in the trunk, cargo area, or behind a small panel.
   - Use the manual release only in the direction and order the manual gives.

6. **If an EV connector is locked, release the car and session first.**
   - Make sure the vehicle is unlocked and, if required, stop charging in the vehicle screen, app, or charger screen.
   - If the connector has a release button, press it while using the vehicle’s unlock or charge-release control.
   - If the manual describes a manual connector release, use it gently as directed and do not yank the cable.

7. **At a public charger, use the station support route.**
   - If you can safely move out of the stall before troubleshooting a door problem, do that first.
   - If a charging session or connector lock is involved, use the charger screen, app, or posted support number to stop the session or get help.
   - Keep the car unlocked only as needed for the release attempt, then secure it again.

8. **If it still will not open, call roadside assistance.**
   - Use your manufacturer roadside assistance, insurer roadside assistance, or AAA if you are a member.
   - Tell them the fuel door, fuel cap, charge-port door, or charging connector is stuck and whether you are low on fuel or battery.
   - If the location becomes unsafe, stop troubleshooting and call 911.

9. **Once it opens, avoid re-jamming it.**
   - Refuel or plug in first.
   - Before closing, remove visible grit, ice, or loose debris around the latch area.
   - Close gently and do not test it repeatedly if it already feels weak or misaligned.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the problem is an actuator, cable, hinge, seal, frozen latch, or charge-port mechanism right now.
- You do not need to decide on a dealership, warranty claim, or repair appointment while you are at the pump or charger.
- You do not need to keep trying forceful tricks; a lock reset, gentle pressure, and the manual method are enough for first steps.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful because it blocks fuel or charging, but many first attempts fail because the latch is bound, frozen, misaligned, or still locked. Slowing down protects the door, paint, charge port, and connector from damage that could make the delay worse.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you reach a safe pause, refuel or charge if possible, and avoid making the problem worse. Later decisions about inspection or repair may need a mechanic, dealer, roadside technician, charging-network support, or vehicle manufacturer support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, electrical, safety, or professional advice. Vehicle designs vary widely, so follow your owner’s manual for the exact release location and method.
