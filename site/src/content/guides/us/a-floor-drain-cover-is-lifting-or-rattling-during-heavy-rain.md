# What to do if… a floor drain cover is lifting or rattling during heavy rain (USA)

## Short answer
Treat this as a possible sewer-backup risk. Stop using water in the home, keep people away from the drain, and contact your local sewer utility, public-works department, or a licensed plumber.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep flushing toilets or running water to try to push anything through.
- Don’t remove the floor drain cover or open a sewer cleanout unless your local sewer utility or a licensed plumber specifically tells you to do so.
- Don’t improvise a drain plug, weight, clamp, wedge, or seal. The right response depends on your plumbing setup.
- Don’t enter standing water where outlets, extension cords, appliances, or the electrical panel may be involved.
- If you have a septic system, don’t arrange tank pumping while the drainfield is flooded or saturated.

## What to do now
1. **Stop adding wastewater:** avoid toilets, showers, sinks, laundry, the dishwasher, and other water use while you arrange help.
2. **Check for signs of spreading from a safe position:** without opening anything, look for rising water, overflow, gurgling, slow draining, or a sewage smell at the floor drain and the lowest nearby fixtures.
3. **Keep people away:** block access to the affected room. Keep children and pets out, and avoid contact with any seepage or standing water.
4. **Treat electricity as the first safety issue:** if water is present near electrical equipment, turn off power only from a dry, safe location. If reaching the panel would require entering standing water, stay out and call an electrician.
5. **Move belongings only if it is safe:** move valuables, cardboard, fabric, and other absorbent items away from the floor without approaching standing water.
6. **Call for help based on your setup:**
   - **Municipal sewer:** contact your local sewer utility or public-works department. Ask whether it handles urgent sewer-backup checks and can check the public main. If it does not, or you cannot reach it promptly, call a licensed plumber.
   - **Septic system:** keep water use as low as possible and contact a septic professional or your local health department. Ask when inspection or pumping would be safe if the ground is flooded or saturated.
   - **Immediate danger:** leave the area and call 911 if there is a threat to life, a fire, or suspected electric shock.
7. **Document the situation only if it is safe:** take a few photos or a short video from a safe position and note when the rattling, lifting, seepage, or rainfall started.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether to file an insurance claim.
- You don’t need to begin heavy cleanup or remove flooring during the storm.
- You don’t need to decide on permanent changes, such as a backwater valve, until a plumber or your local sewer service has assessed the cause.

## Important reassurance
A moving floor drain cover during heavy rain is a warning sign, not a diagnosis. Stopping water use and staying away from the drain reduces added wastewater and avoids unnecessary contact while help is arranged.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need a plumber, septic professional, local sewer service, insurer, or other specialist familiar with your home and local system.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If the area is unsafe, leave it. Call 911 for a threat to life, a fire, or suspected electric shock.
