# What to do if… a partner pressures you to have sex in exchange for affection or kindness (USA)

## Short answer
You do not owe sex in exchange for affection, attention, or kindness. Move to the earliest safer pause you can reach and contact a trusted person or specialist support.

## Do not do these things
- Do not blame yourself if you froze, went along, or did what felt safest in the moment.
- Do not keep explaining or defending your reasons for saying no.
- Do not confront them if you think that could increase the danger.
- Do not make yourself decide the future of the relationship right now.
- Do not assume the pressure does not matter because you are dating, living together, or married.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause if you can.** Move away from the pressure and toward a place where you feel safer. If you cannot move safely, focus on contacting emergency help or someone you trust.
2. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.** This includes situations where you fear violence, restraint, or being unable to leave safely.
3. **Contact a trusted person.** You can send a brief message such as: “Can you call me now? I need support.” You do not need to explain everything.
4. **Use words only if that feels safe.** You may say: “I do not want sex.” You do not need to argue, persuade, or continue the conversation.
5. **Contact RAINN for confidential specialist support and local resources.** Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)** or use the online chat through the RAINN hotline page.
6. **Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline if the sexual pressure is part of a wider pattern of control.** Call **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)**, text **START** to **88788**, or use its live chat.
7. **Use a safer device if your phone or internet activity may be monitored.** A trusted person’s phone or a public device may be safer. Check the hotline privacy information before using online support if you are concerned about digital safety.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to report what happened.
- You do not need to choose a perfect label for the situation.
- You do not need to decide whether to stay in or leave the relationship.
- You do not need to collect proof.
- You do not need to explain yourself to your partner.

## Important reassurance
Pressure, guilt, withdrawal of affection, and fear can make it difficult to respond freely. You deserve support and you are not to blame, including if you froze, went along, or tried to keep the peace.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need support from a specialist advocate or another appropriate professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
