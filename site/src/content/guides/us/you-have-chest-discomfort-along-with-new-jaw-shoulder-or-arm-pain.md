# What to do if… you have chest discomfort along with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain (USA)

## Short answer
Call **911 now**. Chest discomfort with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain can be a heart emergency even if you are unsure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not **drive yourself** to the ER or have someone rush you in a car unless 911 tells you to.
- Do not **wait it out** or try to prove it is nothing by walking around, showering, or lying flat.
- Do not take **extra or new** painkillers, such as ibuprofen or naproxen, to see if it improves.
- Do not take **someone else’s** prescription heart medicine.
- Do not take aspirin **before calling 911** unless a clinician previously told you to do that for suspected heart attack.

## What to do now
1. **Stop activity and sit upright**, or half-sitting. Keep movement minimal.
2. **Call 911** and say: “Chest discomfort with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain.”
   - If you are alone, put the phone on **speaker** and keep it with you.
3. **Follow the dispatcher’s instructions exactly.**
   - Ask: “Should I chew an aspirin?”
4. **Only if 911 advises it, or if your clinician previously told you to do this for suspected heart attack:** chew and swallow immediate-release aspirin **162–325 mg**.
   - Do not take aspirin if you are allergic to it or have been told to avoid it.
   - Tell 911 if you take blood thinners, have a bleeding problem, have had a stomach ulcer, or have any major bleeding history.
   - Do not give aspirin to children or teens under 18.
   - If there is any uncertainty, wait for EMS.
5. **If you have prescribed nitroglycerin** for angina or chest pain, use it exactly as prescribed and tell 911 the time and dose. Do not take nitroglycerin if it is not yours.
6. **Make access easy for EMS:** unlock the door, turn on a light, secure pets if you can do that without effort, and have someone wait outside if available.
7. **Gather only what is within reach:** your medication list or medication bottles, allergies, major conditions, emergency contact, and the time symptoms started.
8. If symptoms change or worsen, including more pain, shortness of breath, faintness, or sweating, **tell 911 immediately**.
9. If the person becomes **unresponsive and is not breathing normally**, follow the dispatcher’s instructions, start CPR if directed, and send someone for an AED if one is nearby.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether it is definitely a heart attack.
- Calling your primary care office first.
- Searching symptoms online or trying to self-triage.
- Packing a bag, contacting work, arranging childcare, or finding insurance details.
- Working out what caused it.

## Important reassurance
Many people delay because symptoms feel vague, mild, or embarrassing to mention. Calling 911 for chest discomfort with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain is a reasonable safety step; you do not need to be certain before getting help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few minutes. EMS and the ER can assess the cause and decide what treatment is needed; later decisions can wait until you are medically safer.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for emergency care. If you have these symptoms now, calling 911 is the safest choice.
