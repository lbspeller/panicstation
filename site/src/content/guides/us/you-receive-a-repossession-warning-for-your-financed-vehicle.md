# What to do if… you receive a repossession warning for your financed vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Use your lender or servicer’s official phone number from your statement, app, or website and ask whether a repossession order is active. Request the exact amount needed, the exact cutoff, and written confirmation of any hold or payment arrangement before relying on it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore the warning or assume you will get another notice; repossession timing varies by state and contract.
- Don’t pay anyone who contacts you claiming they can “stop the repo” unless you independently verify they are your lender or servicer.
- Don’t confront a repo agent, threaten them, use force, block a vehicle, or try to physically stop a repossession.
- Don’t sign voluntary surrender papers on the spot while panicked.
- Don’t leave essential personal items in the vehicle, such as medication, ID, payment cards, devices, work gear, house keys, or garage openers.
- Don’t cancel insurance while you still have the vehicle and it remains financed in your name.
- Don’t rely on a verbal promise that repossession is paused unless the lender confirms it in writing.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the warning through an official route.**  
   Use the lender or servicer’s number from your statement, online account, app, or the back of your payment card. Ask:
   - “Is my loan in default?”
   - “Is there an active repossession order?”
   - “What exact date and time does your system show as the cutoff?”
   - “What department can confirm a hold in writing?”

2. **Ask for the amounts in writing.**  
   Request a written reinstatement quote if catching up is available under your contract or state law. Also request a written payoff or redemption amount, and ask how long each quote is valid.

3. **Ask whether your state or contract gives you a cure or reinstatement option.**  
   Say: “Please tell me whether my state or contract gives me a right to cure, reinstatement, or another way to stop repossession by paying a specific amount. Please send the requirement and deadline in writing.”

4. **Request a short hold using a specific payment plan.**  
   Say: “I can pay $___ today and $___ on ___. Can you place a hold on repossession activity while this is reviewed and while the payment posts?” If they agree, ask for written confirmation showing the payment amount, posting time, hold status, and who approved it.

5. **Remove essential personal property from the vehicle now.**  
   Take out medication, identification, cards, keys, devices, child items, work equipment, and anything you cannot replace quickly. If the car is taken, getting belongings back can depend on state rules and the company’s process.

6. **Take quick photos before anything changes.**  
   Photograph all sides of the vehicle, the interior, the trunk, the odometer, the license plate, and any visible damage. This is only to preserve a simple record, not to delay your lender call.

7. **If there is a servicing error, send proof the same day.**  
   If you already paid, have a deferral, have a written extension, or believe the default is wrong, ask for a supervisor and upload or send proof through the lender’s official channel. Keep the receipt, confirmation number, names, times, and copies of messages.

8. **If a repo agent appears, keep the situation calm and safe.**  
   In many states, a lender may use repossession after default without going to court, but repossession generally cannot involve a breach of the peace. Do not argue, threaten, use force, or block the vehicle. Step away and call 911 if anyone is threatened, trapped, injured, or unsafe.

9. **If you are active-duty military, get help before agreeing to anything irreversible.**  
   The Servicemembers Civil Relief Act may require a court order before repossession in covered installment-contract situations. Contact base legal assistance, JAG, or legal aid promptly, and tell the lender you are seeking SCRA review.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to surrender the vehicle, replace it, or take on new financing.
- You do not need to negotiate the best long-term outcome before you verify whether repossession is active and what amount is required.
- You do not need to draft a full legal complaint tonight, but if the warning is based on an obvious payment or servicing error, send the proof now.
- You do not need to discuss the situation with anyone who is pressuring you, shaming you, or demanding money through an unverified route.

## Important reassurance
A repossession warning is stressful, but you still have useful immediate actions: verify the account status, get the numbers in writing, remove essentials from the car, and ask for a documented hold or payment option. Calm, specific steps can reduce the chance of a preventable loss or a rushed decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an urgent warning only. Repossession, notice, cure, reinstatement, redemption, sale, personal-property, and deficiency rules vary by state and contract, so later decisions may need help from legal aid, a consumer attorney, a military legal office, or a state consumer protection office.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, medical, or other professional advice. If an interaction becomes unsafe, prioritize immediate safety. If you suspect a wrongful repossession, servicing error, or unfair vehicle-loan practice, you can consider filing a complaint with the CFPB and contacting your state consumer protection office or state attorney general.
