# What to do if… your phone or wallet is lost and your cards are exposed to misuse (USA)

## Short answer
Contact card issuers immediately to report the loss and block all payment methods, then use remote tools to lock the mobile device and suspend cellular service.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for transactions to appear before reporting a lost or stolen card.
- Do not provide passwords, PINs, or one-time verification codes to anyone claiming to be a bank or carrier representative.
- Do not use links or phone numbers provided in unsolicited "fraud alert" texts or emails; use verified numbers from the back of a physical card or the official bank website.
- Do not assume that a locked phone screen prevents all access to mobile wallets or stored financial data.
- Do not delay notifying your cellular carrier, as delay may increase your liability for unauthorized usage.

## What to do now
1. **Report all missing credit, debit, and ATM cards to the issuing banks immediately.**
   - Request that the cards be blocked or canceled and that replacements be issued with new account numbers.
   - Specifically ask to "report a lost or stolen card" to trigger federal liability protections.
   - Verify that all mobile wallet tokens (e.g., Apple Pay, Google Pay) associated with those cards are also revoked.
2. **Contact your cellular service provider to suspend the line and protect the account.**
   - Instruct the carrier to disable the device's International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI) to prevent the phone from being used on other networks.
   - Confirm or update your account security PIN or passcode to prevent unauthorized "SIM swapping" or account changes.
3. **Initiate remote security protocols for the lost mobile device.**
   - Access Apple's "Find My" or Google's "Find My Device" from a secondary trusted device or computer.
   - Enable "Lost Mode" or "Lock" to secure the screen and display a contact message.
   - Execute a "Remote Erase" command if the device contains sensitive data and recovery is not expected. Note: This will prevent further tracking of the device.
4. **Notify the three major credit bureaus to place a security freeze or fraud alert.**
   - Contact Equifax (800-685-1111 / equifax.com), Experian (888-397-3742 / experian.com), and TransUnion (888-909-8872 / transunion.com).
   - A security freeze is the most restrictive option and prevents creditors from accessing your credit report to open new accounts.
   - You must contact each bureau individually to place or lift a freeze.
5. **Change passwords for core accounts that allow for identity or financial recovery.**
   - Prioritize your primary email account, as this is often used for password resets.
   - Update passwords for banking apps, mobile carrier accounts, and cloud storage (iCloud or Google Account).
   - Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) using an authenticator app rather than SMS if possible.
6. **Document the incident with local law enforcement if theft is suspected.**
   - File a police report via a non-emergency line or online portal to create a formal record.
   - Obtain a case number, as this is often required by banks, carriers, or insurance providers to waive fees or liability.

## What can wait
- Reordering non-financial items such as library cards, gym memberships, or loyalty cards.
- Purchasing a replacement mobile device or hardware.
- Reviewing long-term credit monitoring subscription services.
- Re-entering billing information for automated non-essential payments.

## Important reassurance
The initial period following a loss is characterized by high cognitive load, but following a systematic reporting sequence significantly limits financial exposure. Once cards are reported and the device is locked, the most immediate avenues for financial harm are effectively closed. Federal protections are designed to shield consumers who report losses promptly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate financial and data protection. It does not cover long-term identity restoration, legal proceedings for recovered property, or insurance claim adjustments.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Liability limits and reporting requirements are governed by the Electronic Fund Transfer Act and the Fair Credit Billing Act; specific bank policies may offer additional protections. Always prioritize physical safety if a device was taken by force.
