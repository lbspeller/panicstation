# What to do if… your boarding pass shows the wrong nationality or travel document details (USA)

## Short answer
Take the correct travel document to a staffed airline counter and ask the airline to correct the nationality or document type in its check-in record, then reissue the boarding pass. For an international trip, do this before security if you can.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore the mismatch on an international trip; the airline may stop you at check-in or boarding if the document data does not support entry or transit.
- Don’t “fix” it by selecting a nationality, passport, or document type that is not true.
- Don’t assume TSA can correct airline travel-document data; TSA screening and airline document checks are separate.
- Don’t cancel and buy a new ticket in a panic unless the airline tells you the record cannot be corrected.
- Don’t send passport photos or passport numbers through social media messages, unofficial chats, or random third parties.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the evidence simple.** Screenshot the boarding pass and any check-in page showing the wrong nationality or document type.
2. **Put the correct document in your hand.** Use the passport or ID you actually intend to travel with, plus any visa, authorization, residence card, or transit permission that applies to the route.
3. **Go to the airline’s staffed counter before security if possible.** Say: “My boarding pass shows the wrong nationality or document type. Please correct my travel document details and reissue my boarding pass.”
4. **For international travel, ask specifically about APIS or passport details.** Say: “Please check the passport number, issuing country, nationality, expiry date, and document type for this itinerary.”
5. **For TSA screening stress, separate the issue.** TSA screening mainly needs an acceptable ID at the checkpoint; ask the airline to confirm that your booking and check-in details match the ID you will show.
6. **If the app will not let you edit it, stop trying in the app.** After online check-in, airlines may restrict self-service edits; a counter, help desk, or gate agent can often update or escalate the correction.
7. **If you have a connection or separate ticket, repeat the check with each airline.** A second airline may have its own document record and may need to see the correct passport or permission before boarding.
8. **After the correction, look at the new pass before walking away.** Check the visible name, destination, document indicator if shown, and flight details.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix loyalty profile details, saved traveler profiles, or old app settings right now.
- You do not need to decide who made the mistake at the airport.
- You do not need to decide about complaints, refunds, chargebacks, or compensation until you have dealt with check-in and boarding.

## Important reassurance
This is often a check-in or saved-profile data problem, not a sign that the whole trip is lost. The useful move is to get a human airline agent to check the document record and issue a corrected boarding pass if needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Entry and transit rules vary by destination, airline, citizenship, passport used, and ticket route, and later decisions may need airline guidance or official destination guidance.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel-agent, or professional advice. For international travel, airlines may rely on official rules and travel-document databases, and the final boarding decision may depend on the documents you can show at the airport.
