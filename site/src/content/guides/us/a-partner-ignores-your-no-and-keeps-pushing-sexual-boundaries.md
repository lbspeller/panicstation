# What to do if… a partner ignores your no and keeps pushing sexual boundaries (USA)

## Short answer
Get to the safest available pause where you have more distance or support, and bring in help. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger or need emergency assistance; otherwise, contact RAINN from a safer phone or device if possible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep debating or explaining your boundary if disengaging is safer.
- Do not blame yourself if you freeze, shut down, or give in to get through the moment.
- Do not pressure yourself to confront your partner, end the relationship, or decide whether to report anything right now.
- Do not use a shared or monitored phone, computer, or account to seek help when a safer option is available.
- Do not delete messages in a panic if keeping them is easy and safe and you may want them later.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** If you can do so safely, move to a place where you have more distance or support, such as a locked room, a neighbour’s home, a public place, or a trusted person’s home. Take your phone, keys, and essential medication if they are easy to reach.
2. **Call 911 for immediate danger.** Call 911 if you need immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance.
3. **Bring in one trusted person.** Call or text someone you trust. A simple message is enough: “Please stay on the phone with me” or “Can you pick me up?”
4. **Contact RAINN for specialist support.** Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use RAINN’s online chat. RAINN offers support 24/7 and can connect you with local resources.
5. **Use an additional support route if it fits your situation.** If the pressure is part of broader relationship abuse, contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline by calling **800-799-SAFE (7233)**, texting **START** to **88788**, or using its online chat. Teens, young adults, and their loved ones can contact love is respect by calling **866-331-9474**, texting **LOVEIS** to **22522**, or using its online chat.
6. **Seek medical care promptly if needed.** If sexual contact happened or you are worried about an injury, STI, or pregnancy-related concern, ask a medical provider about your options. You can also ask RAINN about local specialist services.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now what label fits what happened.
- You do not need to confront your partner or decide the future of the relationship today.
- You do not need to decide right now whether to report anything to police.
- You do not need to write a detailed account or collect perfect evidence.

## Important reassurance
Your “no” is enough. Pressure does not become acceptable because the person is your partner. Freezing, shutting down, or giving in because the situation felt unsafe does not make this your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: reach a safer pause, reduce immediate pressure, and connect with specialist support. Later decisions may need help from an advocate, healthcare professional, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This guide provides general first-step information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or need emergency assistance, call 911.
