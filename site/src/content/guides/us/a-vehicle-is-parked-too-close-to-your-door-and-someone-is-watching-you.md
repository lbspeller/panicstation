# What to do if… a vehicle is parked too close to your door and someone is watching you (USA)

## Short answer
Do not walk up to your car or squeeze between the vehicles. Move to a safer, public, staffed place and call 911 if you feel threatened, blocked, followed, or may be at risk of robbery or carjacking.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the person, stare them down, insult them, or try to make them explain.
- Do not squeeze between vehicles to reach your driver’s door if it puts you close enough to be grabbed or trapped.
- Do not unlock your car from a distance if that would give someone access before you are safely inside.
- Do not stand beside your car with the door open while you decide what to do.
- Do not walk deeper into a quiet lot, stairwell, or garage level to “get space.”
- Do not accept a distraction that pulls you closer, such as directions, a claim that your car is leaking, or a request for help.
- Do not film or photograph from close range if that keeps you near the risk.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance immediately.** Turn away from the vehicle and walk toward a busy, staffed, well-lit place such as a customer service desk, store entrance, front desk, café counter, security booth, or open business.
2. **Call 911 if you feel unsafe or blocked.** Say plainly:
   - your exact location, including the address, parking level, row, entrance, or nearby store;
   - that a vehicle is parked extremely close to your driver’s door;
   - that someone is inside the other vehicle watching you;
   - whether you are alone, blocked from entering, being approached, or afraid to return to the car.
3. **Text 911 only if calling could put you in more danger or you cannot speak.** Text-to-911 is not available everywhere, so keep the message short, give your exact location first, and follow any reply instructions.
4. **Ask staff or security for an escort before returning.** Use a simple script: “I don’t feel safe going to my car. A vehicle is blocking my driver’s door and someone is inside watching me. Can you stay with me while we call police or security?”
5. **Do not return alone just to check.** Wait where there are people, cameras, staff, or security until police, security, or a trusted adult can help you approach or decide another way to leave.
6. **If you are already inside your car, lock it.** Keep windows up, put your seatbelt on if it does not delay leaving, and drive away immediately if the path is clear and safe.
7. **If you are inside but cannot move safely, stay locked in and call 911.** Give dispatch your location, describe the other vehicle, and say whether anyone is approaching, blocking you, or trying to get your attention.
8. **Use attention only from a safer position.** If someone approaches while you are locked in or moving toward people, using the horn or car alarm may draw witnesses; do not use it as a challenge or move closer to use it.
9. **Capture details only from safety.** From a staffed area or other safe distance, note the license plate, make, model, color, location, and a brief description of the occupant. Take a photo only if it does not require going back toward the vehicle.
10. **If you think you are being followed, do not drive home.** Drive to a busy staffed place, hospital entrance, fire station, or an open police station if you already know where one is, and call 911.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove the person’s intent right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it is “really” a carjacking attempt.
- You do not need to confront them, leave a note, or film them up close.
- A store complaint, garage report, insurance note, or detailed written account can wait until you are away from the immediate risk.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to step back without apologizing or explaining. Close distance, blocked access, and being watched can make it harder to think clearly; moving toward people and getting help is a calm, practical response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. If the same person, vehicle, or location is involved again, write down what happened later when you are safe and consider discussing it with local law enforcement, property security, or the parking operator.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate safety decisions. It is not legal, security, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in danger or a crime may be happening, call 911.
