# What to do if… a debit card purchase posts twice and your available balance drops (USA)

## Short answer
Report the two posted entries to your bank now as a duplicate debit error. Save screenshots and ask what will happen to any bank fees or essential payments at risk today.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep spending from the affected account if the available balance is low or negative.
- Do not assume there are two posted debits until you check whether one entry is still pending.
- Do not guess whether the cause is fraud, a merchant error, or a bank error. Report the facts: you authorized one purchase and can see two posted debits.
- Do not close the account in a panic. That can disrupt direct deposits, bill payments, and the bank’s follow-up.
- Do not rely only on an informal message if the bank directs error notices to a disclosed phone number, address, or dispute route.

## What to do now
1. Check the transaction details.
   - Confirm whether both entries are posted or whether one is still pending.
   - Record the merchant, amount, date, and any reference numbers shown for each entry.
   - Take screenshots of the entries and the reduced available balance.

2. Report the duplicate posted debit to your bank.
   - Use the debit-card dispute or electronic-transfer error route provided by your bank.
   - If the correct route is unclear, call the number on the back of your card or on your statement and ask to be routed to the team that handles duplicate debit errors.
   - Say: “I authorized one purchase, but the debit posted twice. I am reporting a duplicate debit error.”
   - Ask for a case number and write down the date, time, and reporting channel.

3. Ask what happens next.
   - Ask whether the bank needs written confirmation and where to send it.
   - For covered electronic fund transfer errors, notice generally needs to reach the bank no later than 60 days after the statement first showing the error is sent.
   - A bank may require written confirmation within 10 business days after an oral report. If it does, it should tell you and provide the address.
   - Send requested written confirmation promptly because temporary credit may depend on it.
   - If the issue is not resolved promptly, ask whether temporary credit applies and when you should expect an update.
   - Some investigation deadlines can be longer, including certain new-account and point-of-sale debit-card situations.

4. Ask the bank to address immediate harm.
   - Ask the bank to reverse any overdraft, insufficient-funds, or other bank fees caused by the duplicate debit.
   - Ask whether any payments have already been declined or returned.
   - Ask what the bank recommends for essential payments due before the balance is restored.

5. Protect essentials today.
   - Pause non-essential spending from the affected account.
   - Check whether rent, mortgage, utilities, childcare, transport, or other essential payments are due soon.
   - Use another available payment method for essentials where possible.
   - Contact any biller whose payment may fail and explain that a duplicate posted debit temporarily reduced your available funds. Ask whether a short extension or fee review is available.

6. Keep a simple record.
   - Save screenshots, messages, case numbers, and the dates of calls.
   - If the bank asks for written confirmation, send it promptly and keep a copy.

7. Escalate if you cannot get traction.
   - If the bank does not address the report, submit a complaint to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.
   - Use the USA.gov banking-complaints page if you need help identifying another appropriate regulator.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change banks.
- You do not need to rebuild your full budget today.
- You do not need to cancel every automatic payment or direct deposit unless you see broader unauthorized activity or the bank advises you to do so.
- You do not need to work out the cause before reporting the duplicate posted debit.
- You do not need to contact the merchant before reporting the error to your bank. You can contact the merchant separately after the bank report if that is practical.

## Important reassurance
A duplicate posted debit can sharply reduce the funds available to you. You do not need to solve every consequence at once. The immediate task is to document the entries, report the error, and protect essential payments.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from your bank, a consumer-protection body, or a financial professional.

## Important note
This guide provides general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Account terms, transaction channels, and applicable rules can vary. Follow your bank’s disclosed error-reporting instructions and keep copies of what you submit.
