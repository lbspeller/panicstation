# What to do if… a light switch plate feels hot to the touch or smells unusual (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the switch immediately and turn off the power at the circuit breaker if it is safe to do so. If you see smoke, sparks, or flames, evacuate the building and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don't flip the switch on and off to "test" if the problem is still occurring.
- Don't ignore a fishy, acrid, or "chemical" smell, even if it is faint.
- Don't remove the switch plate or attempt to inspect the internal wiring yourself.
- Don't use any other outlets or appliances on the same circuit until the issue is resolved.
- Don't apply water, damp cloths, or cleaning sprays to the switch or the wall.
- Don't force a tripped breaker back to the "on" position if it immediately clicks off.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the switch immediately.** Leave the switch in the "off" position and do not touch it again.
2. **Check for immediate fire indicators.** 
   - Look for **smoke** rising from the plate or wall.
   - Listen for **sizzling, popping, or crackling** sounds.
   - Look for **scorch marks, browning, or melting plastic** on the faceplate.
   - If any of these are present, **evacuate all occupants** and **call 911**.
3. **De-energize the circuit at the main breaker panel.** 
   - Locate your electrical service panel (breaker box).
   - Find the breaker labeled for that room or circuit and flip it to the **OFF** position.
   - If the panel is not labeled or you are unsure, flip the **MAIN** breaker to shut off power to the entire house.
   - **Do not touch the panel** if it feels hot, smells like burning, or is making loud buzzing sounds; evacuate and call 911 instead.
4. **Isolate the area.** Ensure no one else in the household attempts to use the switch or nearby outlets.
5. **Contact a licensed electrician for an emergency inspection.** 
   - Inform them that the switch plate is hot to the touch or emitting a burning smell.
   - Mention any specific odors, such as a **fishy smell** or the **smell of ozone**.
   - Note if the switch is a dimmer, as these are more prone to overheating if overloaded.
6. **Notify property management if you are a renter.** Provide the specific details of the hazard and confirm that you have shut off the breaker for safety.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical cause of the heat (e.g., loose terminal screws, arcing, or an overloaded dimmer).
- You do not need to buy a replacement switch or tools for a DIY repair.
- You do not need to worry about permanent damage to the home's structure if the power has been successfully cut and there is no smoke.

## Important reassurance
Sensing heat or an unusual smell is a vital early warning. Most electrical fires are preceded by these sensory cues. Shutting off the power is the most effective way to stabilize the situation and prevent a small fault from escalating.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected electrical fault. A licensed electrician must verify that the wiring and the device are safe before power is restored to the circuit.

## Important note
Electrical heat and odors are high-risk indicators of potential fire. Always prioritize evacuation and emergency services if the situation changes or if smoke becomes visible.
