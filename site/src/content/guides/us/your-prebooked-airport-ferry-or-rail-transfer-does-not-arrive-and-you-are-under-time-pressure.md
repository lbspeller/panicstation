# What to do if… your prebooked airport, ferry, or rail transfer does not arrive and you are under time pressure (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a staffed, public help point and run two tracks at once: contact the transfer provider, while securing a safe backup route before the next check-in, boarding, or departure cut-off. Save key receipts, screenshots, booking details, and messages.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait in an isolated place or leave the terminal, station, or ferry building without a clear, safe pickup point.
- Don’t keep waiting past a hard check-in, boarding, or departure cut-off if a backup route is available.
- Don’t cancel your onward flight, train, or ferry in a rush; first ask the operator what they can note, change, or rebook.
- Don’t accept an unofficial ride offer from someone approaching you in the terminal or at the curb.
- Don’t delete app screens, texts, emails, call logs, or receipts.
- Don’t spend time arguing about blame while the next departure is still reachable.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the nearest staffed help location.**  
   Go to an airport information desk, airline counter, station customer service desk, ferry terminal ticket desk, or another staffed public area. Stay where staff, cameras, and other passengers are nearby.

2. **Check exactly what was booked.**  
   Open your itinerary and confirm whether the transfer was part of the same booking as your onward travel or bought separately. Screenshot the pickup time, pickup location, booking reference, driver status, and any meeting instructions.

3. **Contact the transfer provider with one clear request.**  
   Send or say: “My transfer has not arrived. What is the earliest confirmed pickup or replacement transport you can provide, and can you confirm it by text, email, or in the app?”  
   If you booked through a travel platform, message the platform in the app as well so there is a record.

4. **Set a latest safe waiting time.**  
   Use the next check-in, boarding, or departure cut-off to choose the last time you can keep waiting. When that time arrives, stop waiting and move to the safest workable backup.

5. **Use only official or verifiable backup transport.**
   - **Airport:** go to the airport’s designated taxi, shuttle, or rideshare pickup area. Look for official airport signs such as “Ground Transportation,” “Taxi,” or “Rideshare.” For app rides, confirm the driver name, vehicle, and plate in the app before getting in.
   - **Rail:** go to the staffed ticket counter or station agent if available, or use the rail operator’s official app or phone line to ask about changing, rebooking, or taking the next available train.
   - **Ferry:** ask the terminal desk about the next sailing, standby options, late-arrival handling, and whether your ticket can be moved.
   - **Hotel or cruise transfer:** contact the hotel, cruise line, or booking agent and ask whether they can provide an official replacement pickup or tell you the approved pickup area.

6. **Notify the next operator before you miss the departure.**  
   Use this wording: “My booked ground transfer failed. I am on my way and may miss the scheduled departure. Can you note this on my reservation, tell me the latest check-in or boarding cut-off, and tell me my next available option if I do not make it?”

7. **Record what happened while it is fresh.**  
   Keep screenshots showing no driver assigned, delayed status, cancellation, unanswered messages, or missed calls. Save replacement-transport receipts, extra ticket receipts, and any essential expenses caused by the no-show. Write down the time you arrived, the time you stopped waiting, and the names or roles of staff you spoke with if they provide them.

8. **Ask about refunds or payment disputes after you are moving.**  
   If the transfer was not provided, ask the merchant or booking platform for a refund in writing. If that does not resolve it, ask your card issuer promptly about a billing dispute and keep the documents they request.

9. **Prioritize safety if you are stranded or feel at risk.**  
   Stay inside staffed public areas where possible. If there is immediate danger or you need emergency help in the USA, call 911.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to write a full complaint while the next departure is still reachable.
- You don’t need to decide now whether to dispute the charge; save proof first and ask once you are safe or moving.
- You don’t need to prove who is at fault at the counter; focus on the fastest safe route and written confirmation.
- You don’t need to solve the whole trip at once; solve the next reachable step.

## Important reassurance
A missing transfer under time pressure can make everything feel urgent at once. Moving to “backup route plus documentation” often gives you the best chance of reaching the next leg while preserving your options later.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on your ticket type, whether the bookings were connected or separate, the operator’s current policy, travel insurance, card rules, and any written promises made by the provider.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, safety, travel, or professional advice. Transport policies vary by operator, location, ticket type, and the reason for the disruption.
