# What to do if… a fuel station preauthorization hold is using most of your available balance (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the held amount as unavailable for now. Check whether the transaction is pending or posted, stop retrying pay-at-pump purchases, and contact your card issuer now if the hold is blocking essentials or if you do not recognise it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t retry the card at more pumps or stations until you understand the transaction status. Additional attempts may create more pending authorizations.
- Don’t assume a pending amount is the final fuel charge.
- Don’t spend against the held amount while it is reducing your available balance.
- Don’t wait to report a transaction you did not authorise.
- Don’t count on the station being able to restore your available funds immediately.

## What to do now
1. **Check the transaction status in your banking or card app.**  
   Look for wording such as **pending**, **authorization**, **hold**, **preauthorization**, **posted**, or **completed**. Save a screenshot showing the merchant name, amount, date, and time.

2. **Contact your bank, credit union, or card issuer.**  
   Say: “A fuel-station authorization is using most of my available balance. Is it pending or posted? When do you expect it to stop affecting my available balance or available credit? What should I do if essential payments are due first?”  
   If the transaction was not authorised by you, say that immediately.

3. **Avoid another pay-at-pump authorization for now.**  
   If you need fuel, ask the cashier whether you can prepay an exact amount inside. Another payment method may also help if it is manageable for you.

4. **Contact the station if the amount looks duplicated or does not match the fuel purchase.**  
   Give the date, time, location, pump number if known, receipt details, and the last four digits of the card. Ask whether they can confirm the final sale amount and whether the transaction was completed or voided. Ask your issuer when the hold is expected to stop affecting your account.

5. **Protect essential payments.**  
   Check which bills may be attempted before the issuer’s expected release window. If a critical payment may fail, contact the biller now and ask for a retry date or a short extension.

6. **Follow up if the transaction posts incorrectly or the hold remains unresolved.**  
   Keep the receipt and screenshots. Ask your issuer how to report a transaction you did not authorise or dispute an incorrect posted amount or duplicate charge. If fees result, ask the issuer to review them. If the problem is not resolved, you can submit a complaint to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to change banks or open new credit today.
- You do not need to work out the exact cause of the processing delay before protecting essential payments.
- You do not need to argue with staff at the station. A clear request for confirmation is enough.
- You do not need to make wider financial decisions while the immediate hold is being checked.

## Important reassurance
A pay-at-pump authorization can temporarily reduce your available balance or available credit before the final fuel amount appears. This does not by itself mean the final charge is wrong. Timing varies by issuer, card type, merchant processing, and transaction path.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the hold remains unresolved, posts incorrectly, or causes fees, later decisions may need help from your issuer, the merchant, or a consumer financial complaint service.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not financial, legal, or professional advice. Card and account policies vary. Report any transaction you do not recognise to your bank, credit union, or card issuer promptly.
