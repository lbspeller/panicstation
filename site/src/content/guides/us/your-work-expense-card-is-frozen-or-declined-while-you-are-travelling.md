# What to do if… your work expense card is frozen or declined while you are travelling (USA)
## Short answer
Use the number on the back of the card or in the issuer’s official app, then contact your company’s travel or finance contact. Ask for an approved payment route before putting a large charge on a personal card, unless delaying an essential payment would leave you unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the card at multiple vendors.
- Do not cancel a hotel, flight, or transport booking in panic if the vendor can hold it while you make calls.
- Do not pay a large hotel, flight, or transport charge personally without written approval if you can safely wait for approval.
- Do not send the full card number, security code, or account login details by text or email.
- Do not use a cash advance, wire transfer, or cash-like transaction unless your company has explicitly approved it.
- Do not follow payment instructions from an unexpected caller or message. Use a trusted number instead.

## What to do now
1. **Protect tonight’s essentials.**  
   Focus on a safe place to stay, essential transport, food, and water. If you are at a hotel desk or check-in counter, ask whether the reservation can be held while you contact your company.

2. **Check for an issuer message.**  
   Look in the issuer’s official app or your recent messages for a fraud alert, decline notice, or request to verify a transaction. Do not keep making test purchases.

3. **Call the card issuer through a trusted route.**  
   Use the number on the back of the card or in the issuer’s official app. Ask why the card was declined, whether you can verify the transaction now, and whether your company card administrator needs to change anything.

4. **Ask your company for one approved alternative.**  
   Contact your manager and your travel, finance, or accounts payable contact. Ask for a specific option, such as restoring the company card, changing the limit, arranging direct company payment, booking through the company travel provider, or approving a personal-card limit in writing.

5. **Use the support route already attached to the trip.**  
   Check your itinerary, booking confirmation, or company travel app for an after-hours travel provider or company on-call contact. Ask whether they can change the payment method without cancelling the booking.

6. **Ask the vendor what can be held or changed.**  
   Ask whether the hotel or other vendor can hold the reservation, charge only the immediate amount needed, or accept an approved company payment route once your company confirms it.

7. **If personal payment is necessary, document the approval if possible.**  
   Ask for written approval first if you can do so without delaying immediate safety. If you cannot reach an authorised company contact and need essential lodging or transport, spend only what is needed, record your attempts to get approval, and keep itemized receipts. Hide or redact card details before saving or sharing any screenshot.

8. **Write down a short timeline.**  
   Note what failed, when it failed, who you contacted, and what was approved. This can reduce confusion later.

9. **If you are stranded or in immediate danger, escalate now.**  
   Contact your company’s after-hours travel, security, or on-call route if one exists. In the USA, dial 911 for an emergency requiring immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance. Outside the USA, call the local emergency number.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle fault, policy interpretation, or a reimbursement dispute while you are trying to secure essential travel needs.
- You do not need to complete a full expense report now. Keep approvals and receipts.
- You do not need to decide now whether to raise a formal complaint.

## Important reassurance
A frozen work card is disruptive, but you do not need to solve every policy question at once. The immediate task is to secure essential travel needs and get one approved payment route.

## Scope note
These are first steps for stabilizing the trip. Later reimbursement, employment, or wage questions may need help from your employer, a worker-support service, a state labor office, or a qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Employer policies vary. Federal wage rules can matter where work-related costs reduce a covered nonexempt employee’s pay below required minimum wage or overtime compensation. State rules may also apply.
