# What to do if… your vehicle starts but the accelerator feels unresponsive while you are still in a safe place (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter traffic or attempt to drive the vehicle on public roads. Secure the car in its current position, check for pedal obstructions, and call for professional roadside assistance or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull out onto a road or into traffic to "see if the problem clears up."
- Do not repeatedly floor the accelerator pedal, as this can cause sudden surging or mechanical strain.
- Do not reach into the footwell or pedal area unless the vehicle is turned off or securely in Park with the parking brake set.
- Do not ignore a flashing Check Engine light or a red "Stop" warning.
- Do not attempt to "limp" the car to a destination if you must cross high-speed traffic or intersections.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle immediately in its current safe location.**
   - Shift the transmission into **Park (automatic)** or **Neutral (manual)**.
   - Fully engage the **parking brake** or emergency brake.
   - Turn on your **hazard lights** to alert others that your vehicle is stationary and disabled.

2. **Check the dashboard for specific warning indicators.**
   - Look for a yellow or red **lightning bolt symbol**, which indicates an Electronic Throttle Control (ETC) fault.
   - Look for a **Check Engine light** or a message stating "Reduced Engine Power" or "Limp Mode."
   - Note if the **ABS or Traction Control** lights are illuminated, as these systems can sometimes limit throttle response.

3. **Inspect the footwell for physical obstructions only after the vehicle is secured.**
   - Ensure the engine is off or the car is definitely in Park with the brake set before reaching down.
   - Check if a **floor mat** has slid forward and is wedged under the pedal or preventing it from moving through its full range.
   - Remove any loose items, like water bottles or debris, that may have rolled behind the pedals.

4. **Verify the gear selection and transmission engagement.**
   - With your foot firmly on the brake, cycle the shifter through the gears and back to **Drive** (or first gear) to ensure it is fully engaging.
   - If the engine revs when you press the pedal but the car does not move, the issue is likely the **transmission or drivetrain**, not the accelerator pedal itself.

5. **Perform a single controlled electronic reset.**
   - Turn the engine off completely and remove the key (or turn off the ignition for push-button starts).
   - Wait at least 60 seconds for the vehicle's computers to power down.
   - Restart the engine and check if the throttle response has returned while still in Park.

6. **Contact professional assistance if the issue persists.**
   - Call your **roadside assistance provider**, insurance company, or a local towing service.
   - If you are in a location where you are blocking critical access or feel physically unsafe, call **911** for a non-emergency police dispatch or traffic control.

7. **Report the incident if you suspect a manufacturing defect.**
   - If the failure was sudden and without obvious cause, you can file a safety complaint with the **NHTSA Vehicle Safety Hotline at 888-327-4236**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact mechanical or electronic cause of the failure right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop immediately; most tow drivers can recommend a nearby facility if you are undecided.
- You do not need to worry about long-term rental car or insurance claims until the vehicle has been safely moved from its current spot.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frustrated or anxious when a vehicle fails to respond, especially if you were just about to leave. Finding this fault while you are still in a parking space or driveway is a positive outcome, as it prevents a loss of power while moving at high speeds or in dangerous traffic.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and safety actions for a vehicle that is already stationary. It does not cover troubleshooting for vehicles currently moving in traffic or high-speed emergency maneuvers.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific instructions found in your vehicle's owner's manual. Modern vehicles use "drive-by-wire" systems where the pedal is a sensor; if this sensor or the electronic throttle body fails, the car may enter a "fail-safe" mode to prevent unintended acceleration. Always prioritize getting the vehicle to a certified technician for a diagnostic scan.
