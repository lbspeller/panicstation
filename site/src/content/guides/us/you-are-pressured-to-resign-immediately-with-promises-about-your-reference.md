# What to do if… you are pressured to resign immediately with promises about your reference (USA)

## Short answer
Do not send a resignation or sign an agreement in the meeting solely because you are being pressured. Ask whether the employer is ending your employment or asking you to resign, and ask for any proposed terms and reference promise in writing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not email or sign a resignation just to make the meeting stop.
- Do not sign a separation agreement, severance agreement, release, or waiver before you have read it and decided whether to accept it.
- Do not rely only on a verbal reference promise.
- Do not record the meeting before checking the applicable state law and workplace policy.
- Do not post about the meeting on social media or in company chats while you are deciding what to do.

## What to do now
1. **Ask one clear question.** Say: “Are you terminating my employment, or are you asking me to resign?” Ask for the answer, the effective date, and the stated separation status in writing.
2. **Pause your own decision.** Say: “I am not sending a resignation or signing an agreement in this meeting. Please send me the written terms.” Ask for a copy of any proposed resignation, separation agreement, release, or severance terms.
3. **Make the reference promise specific.** Ask who would respond to a reference request, how the request should be sent, and what information or wording would be provided. Ask for that commitment in writing.
4. **Ask for a written separation checklist.** Request the final-pay timing, the applicable unused PTO or vacation policy, the benefits end date, and any continuation-coverage notice or contact route that applies.
5. **Write a factual note while the details are fresh.** Record the date, time, attendees, documents shown, and the wording used about resignation and references. Keep the note outside employer systems.
6. **Send a brief confirmation email.** For example: “Confirming that today I was asked to resign and that a reference was discussed. I requested the proposed terms and reference details in writing and have not submitted a resignation.”
7. **If the pressure continues, repeat the same position.** Ask for a pause. Ask whether an HR contact can join. If you are represented by a union, contact your union representative.
8. **If your employment ends, use the state unemployment filing route promptly.** The state program decides eligibility. Keep accurate records of the separation wording, dates, emails, pay information, and any agreement you received.

## What can wait
- You do not need to negotiate every separation term in the meeting.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to accept a proposed severance agreement.
- You do not need to write a long account of events today.
- You do not need to decide today whether to seek specialist advice.

## Important reassurance
A pressured meeting can make an immediate decision feel unavoidable. You can slow down your own decision, ask for written terms, and keep a clear factual record even if the employer sets a deadline or ends the meeting.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on your state, your employer’s written policies, any agreement you receive, and whether you are represented by a union. Specialist help may be useful for later decisions.
