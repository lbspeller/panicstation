# What to do if… your electric vehicle has very low range and your planned charger is unavailable (USA)

## Short answer
Slow down smoothly, reduce power use, and switch from the planned charger to the nearest reachable safe charging option. If you may stop somewhere unsafe, treat it like a roadside emergency and call for help early.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep driving fast toward a farther charger because it was the original plan.
- Do not drive from station to station while the range is critical; choose the safest reachable option.
- Do not ignore low-battery warnings, reduced-power mode, or a rapidly falling range estimate.
- Do not spend several minutes troubleshooting apps or prices while the car may not reach a charger.
- Do not accept a tow method unless the roadside professional confirms it is suitable for your EV.
- Do not stay exposed beside moving traffic if there is a safer place you can reach.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilize the car’s remaining range.**
   - Turn on eco mode or the car’s equivalent low-energy driving mode.
   - Accelerate gently and reduce speed smoothly where it is safe and legal.
   - Lower cabin heat or air conditioning, and turn off heated seats, heated steering, defrost, and other non-essential loads if you can do so safely.

2. **Choose the nearest reachable charging option, not the ideal one.**
   - Use the car navigation and one charging locator app or website.
   - Filter for your connector, distance, open or public status, charger level, and recent status if the tool shows it.
   - Include Level 2 chargers at parking garages, hotels, public lots, workplaces, and shopping areas if DC fast chargers are unavailable or busy.

3. **Use a direct station locator if your app is failing.**
   - The U.S. Department of Energy Alternative Fueling Station Locator can show nearby EV charging locations.
   - Pick one primary charger you can reach safely.
   - Pick one backup only if it does not add risky extra driving.

4. **If you are already at the unavailable charger, make one quick check.**
   - Look for another working plug at the same site.
   - Check whether the station screen gives a short helpline or restart instruction.
   - If the site is clearly out of service, blocked, or too busy, leave for the nearest reachable backup before the range drops further.

5. **Before the range reaches zero, move to a safer waiting place if you can.**
   - Aim for a well-lit parking lot, service plaza, staffed business, or other public place rather than a shoulder or live lane.
   - If you are stopped or disabled, use hazard lights if it is safe to do so.
   - If you carry a warning triangle or similar safety marker, use it only if you can place it without standing in or near moving traffic.
   - Keep your exact location visible: road name, exit number, mile marker, nearby business, or map pin.

6. **Call roadside assistance early.**
   - Call your insurance, vehicle maker, auto club, rental company, or roadside provider.
   - Say: “Electric vehicle low battery; I may not reach a charger.”
   - Ask whether they can provide mobile charging, tow-to-charger service, or another EV-safe recovery option where you are.

7. **Call 911 if there is immediate danger.**
   - Call 911 if you are stopped in a live lane, trapped somewhere unsafe, threatened, or at risk of being hit.
   - Tell the dispatcher your location, that the vehicle is an EV with very low battery, and whether you are in or near traffic.
   - Follow the dispatcher’s instructions.

8. **If you reach any working charger, take a short safety top-up first.**
   - Charge enough to reach a more reliable charging hub or a safe destination.
   - Once you have a safer range margin, then decide whether to continue charging, reroute, or wait for a better charger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the cheapest charger right now.
- You do not need to solve every app, payment, or account problem while range is critical.
- You do not need to decide the rest of the trip until the car is charging or roadside help is arranged.
- You do not need to report the broken charger until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
A very low range warning can feel urgent, but the next useful move is small: reduce power use, choose the nearest workable charge, and get help before the situation becomes unsafe. A short top-up is enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation only. Later decisions about towing, repair, route planning, battery health, or complaints may need help from your vehicle maker, roadside provider, charging network, insurer, or another qualified service.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, mechanical, emergency-services, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. In immediate danger, follow emergency services or roadside professionals.
