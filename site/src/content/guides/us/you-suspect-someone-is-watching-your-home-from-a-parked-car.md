# What to do if… you suspect someone is watching your home from a parked car (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from windows, lock the home, and call 911 if there is immediate danger or a crime seems to be happening now. If it feels suspicious but not urgent, use your local police non-emergency number.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to confront the person or knock on the car window.
- Don’t follow the car if it drives off.
- Don’t stand in a doorway, on a porch, or at a window where you can be seen.
- Don’t post about it publicly in real time, because it may reveal that you are watching, alone, or distracted.
- Don’t introduce a weapon into an uncertain encounter; focus on distance, locked doors, and getting help.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer spot and reduce visibility.** Get everyone away from front-facing windows. Close blinds or curtains if you can do so without standing exposed.
2. **Lock down quickly.** Lock doors, close and lock accessible windows, and keep car keys, bags, and valuables away from entry areas. If you have an attached garage, do not open the garage door to look.
3. **Turn on lights only from inside.** Use exterior lights or a couple of indoor lights if the switches are reachable from a safe place.
4. **Use security tools you already have.** Arm your home security system if you have one. Save doorbell camera or security camera clips if you can do that from inside.
5. **Write down only what you can safely see.**
   - exact location of the vehicle
   - make, model, color, and license plate, if visible without moving closer or standing in view
   - number of occupants
   - what they are doing, such as watching houses, taking photos, getting in or out, approaching property, or trying doors
   - how long the vehicle has been there
   - whether the same vehicle has returned before
6. **Choose the safest reporting route.**
   - **Call 911** if someone approaches your home, tries doors or windows, threatens anyone, damages property, follows you, or you believe a crime is happening now.
   - If it is suspicious but not urgent, call your local police non-emergency number.
   - If you feel unsafe right now and cannot safely wait or find the non-emergency number, call 911 and calmly explain what you are seeing.
7. **Make the report concrete.** Start with: “There is a vehicle parked across from my home and I am concerned it may be watching my address.” Give your location, the vehicle description, the behavior you observed, and whether anyone vulnerable is inside.
8. **Ask what to do next.** Ask whether an officer can check the area, whether the report can be logged for patrol awareness, and whether you can have a call or incident number. If you live in an apartment, gated community, or managed building, notify building security or management after you contact police.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide right now whether it is casing, stalking, a private investigator, a lost driver, or something innocent.
- You don’t need to confront anyone.
- You don’t need to gather proof beyond what you can safely observe from inside.
- You don’t need to buy cameras, change locks, move, or make long-term security decisions tonight.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to feel unsettled by a vehicle that appears to be watching your home. A calm, specific report is a practical safety step, even if the situation later turns out to have an ordinary explanation.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes to hours. If the vehicle returns, the behavior seems targeted, or the person is someone you know, later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, victim support, building security, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 911.
