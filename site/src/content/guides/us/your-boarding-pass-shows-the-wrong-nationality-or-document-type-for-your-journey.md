# What to do if… your boarding pass shows the wrong nationality or document type for your journey (USA)

## Short answer
Present your correct physical travel documents at the airline’s staffed airport check-in counter to have your Secure Flight and Advance Passenger Information (APIS) records manually updated and a new boarding pass issued.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to board an international flight with incorrect document data, as airlines face heavy fines and will likely deny boarding at the gate.
- Do not assume that a TSA officer can or will correct airline manifest errors; their role is identity verification for security, not document validation for destination entry.
- Do not pay for a new ticket until an airport agent confirms the existing Passenger Name Record (PNR) cannot be edited.
- Do not wait until you reach the boarding gate to report the error; international manifest changes (APIS) often require transmission at least 60 minutes before departure.
- Do not ignore the mismatch if you are a dual citizen; U.S. law requires U.S. citizens to enter and depart the United States using a U.S. passport.

## What to do now
1. **Locate your correct physical travel documents immediately.** Ensure you have the passport, visa, or permanent resident card that is required for your destination and transit points, as the agent must scan these to override the existing data.
2. **Go to the airline’s staffed full-service check-in counter at the airport.** **Do not use a self-service kiosk if the digital check-in already contains incorrect nationality or document type data, as these systems often lock once a boarding pass is issued.**
3. **Inform the agent that your boarding pass contains a document mismatch.** **Specifically ask the agent to "update the Secure Flight Passenger Data (SFPD) and the APIS document details" to match your physical ID.**
4. **Request a printed replacement boarding pass once the data is updated.** **Verify that the new pass reflects the correct name and document indicators before leaving the counter.**
5. **Check if your airline charges an "agent assistance fee" for manual document corrections.** **Be prepared to pay a fee if you are flying with a budget or ultra-low-cost carrier that penalizes manual airport check-in.**
6. **Confirm your identity meets 2026 TSA requirements for the security checkpoint.** **If you do not have a REAL ID-compliant license or a valid passport, you may be required to use the TSA ConfirmID system and pay a $45 fee to verify your identity before entering the screening area.**
7. **Verify document details for any connecting flights on separate tickets.** **If your journey involves different airlines not on the same reservation, you must repeat the data correction process at the transfer desk or check-in counter of the second airline.**

## What can wait
- Updating your permanent frequent flyer profile or "saved traveler" settings can wait until after your trip.
- Investigating how the error occurred (e.g., third-party booking site vs. airline app glitch) is not necessary for immediate travel.
- Filing a complaint or seeking a refund for "agent assistance fees" should be handled after you have reached your destination.

## Important reassurance
Document mismatches are common administrative errors often caused by auto-fill settings or accidental menu selections during mobile check-in. Airline agents have the tools to re-scan your documents and update the flight manifest. As long as you have the correct physical documents for your destination and arrive at the counter before the manifest-close deadline (typically 60 minutes for international), the issue is usually resolvable.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document data errors on a boarding pass. It does not provide legal advice on visa eligibility or immigration status. Individual airline policies regarding change fees and check-in deadlines vary.

## Important note
Airlines are required by federal law to provide accurate passenger manifests to U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) via APIS. Failure to correct document data can lead to boarding denials or significant delays upon arrival at your destination. Always consult the official website of your destination country's embassy for current entry requirements.
