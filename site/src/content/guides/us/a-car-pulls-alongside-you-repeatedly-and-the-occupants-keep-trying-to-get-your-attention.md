# What to do if… a car pulls alongside you repeatedly and the occupants keep trying to get your attention (USA)

## Short answer
Maintain a steady speed toward a well-lit, busy public location and call 911 immediately to report a suspicious vehicle following your movements. Do not stop, pull over, or lower your windows to engage with the occupants.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull over on the shoulder, a side street, or any isolated area to investigate.
- Do not drive to your home, workplace, or the home of a friend.
- Do not lower your windows or unlock your doors for any reason.
- Do not engage in aggressive driving, such as excessive speeding or "brake checking."
- Do not gesture back, shout, or attempt to communicate with the occupants.
- Do not stop at a red light in a way that boxes you in; leave enough space to see the tires of the car in front of you.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your vehicle interior immediately.** Ensure all doors are locked and all windows are fully rolled up.
2. **Maintain a "safety bubble" around your car.** Keep enough distance from the vehicle in front of you so that you can steer around it if the suspicious vehicle tries to block your path.
3. **Navigate toward a high-traffic, well-lit public area.** Head toward a large 24-hour gas station, a hospital emergency room entrance, or a grocery store parking lot with active security cameras.
   - If a police or fire station is nearby, drive directly there.
   - Fire stations are often more likely to have staff visibly present if police officers are out on patrol.
4. **Call 911 using a hands-free system or speakerphone.** Tell the dispatcher you are being followed and feel unsafe.
   - Provide your current street name and nearest cross-street or mile marker.
   - Describe your vehicle (make, model, color).
   - Describe the other vehicle and its occupants as best as you can without looking away from the road.
5. **Verify if you are being followed by making four consecutive right turns.** If the vehicle remains behind you after completing a full circle, you are being intentionally trailed; do not stop and stay on the line with 911.
6. **Alert others to your presence if the situation escalates.** If the vehicle tries to force you off the road or block you, use your horn in short, repeated bursts to attract public attention.
7. **Stay inside your vehicle once you reach a safe location.** Park in a highly visible area under lights, keep the engine running, and wait for law enforcement to arrive before exiting the car.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the occupants' motives or whether this is a "misunderstanding" right now.
- You do not need to check your car for damage or "signs" if you think they hit you; wait for police.
- You do not need to complete your original journey or arrive at your destination on time.
- You do not need to look at or respond to any signs or objects the occupants may be holding up.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of panic or "tunnel vision" when being targeted on the road. Taking these steps is not an overreaction; it is a standard safety protocol designed to remove the element of isolation that a harasser relies on.

## Scope note
This guide is for immediate stabilization during an active road harassment or following incident. Long-term steps, such as filing a formal police report or installing a dashcam, should be handled only after you are in a secure environment.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving a potential threat or vehicle pursuit, contact emergency services (911) immediately. Prioritize your physical safety over traffic convenience or social politeness.
