# What to do if… your manual car is stuck in gear and you cannot move off (USA)
## Short answer
Keep the vehicle stationary: hold the foot brake, engage the parking brake fully, and switch on the hazard lights. Do not force the shifter or start the engine again just to test it. Call 911 if the vehicle is in or very close to active traffic, there is an immediate risk of a collision, or there is another immediate danger; otherwise request roadside assistance or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force, jerk, or slam the shifter.
- Do not rev the engine, rock the vehicle aggressively, or try to power through the problem.
- Do not start the engine again just to test the gear if the vehicle could move unexpectedly.
- Do not release the clutch to see what happens.
- Do not leave the vehicle to troubleshoot near moving traffic.
- Do not step into active traffic.
- Do not place warning devices if doing so would bring you close to moving traffic.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle.**
   - Keep your foot on the brake.
   - Engage the parking brake fully.
   - Switch on the hazard lights.
   - If you are on a slope, keep the vehicle firmly controlled and do not attempt further checks if it may roll or lurch.

2. **Check whether the location is safe enough for any further action.**
   - If the vehicle is in or very close to active traffic, there is an immediate risk of a collision, or there is another immediate danger, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
   - Do not step into active traffic.
   - If waiting inside the vehicle is the safer option, keep your seat belt fastened unless a dispatcher or responder instructs you otherwise.
   - If the vehicle is safely parked away from moving traffic, continue cautiously.

3. **Make one gentle attempt to select neutral with the engine off.**
   - Keep the foot brake applied and the parking brake engaged.
   - Confirm that the engine is off.
   - Press the clutch pedal fully down.
   - Try to move the shifter gently into neutral.
   - Stop immediately if it does not move with light pressure.

4. **Stop mechanical attempts if neutral does not engage easily.**
   - Keep the vehicle secured.
   - Do not keep testing the clutch or shifter.
   - Do not restart the engine just to try again.

5. **Request roadside assistance or a tow.**
   - Tell the provider that the vehicle has a manual transmission, is stuck in gear, cannot select neutral, and cannot be driven.
   - Give your exact location.
   - Mention any slope, nearby traffic, or other safety concern.

6. **Use warning devices only when it is clearly safe.**
   - Keep the hazard lights on.
   - If you have reflective triangles or another warning device, place them only if you can do so without entering or approaching moving traffic.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the cause is the clutch, shifter linkage, or transmission.
- You do not need to keep trying different mechanical techniques.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop while you are still managing the immediate situation.
- You can check the owner’s manual and look for any open safety recall after you and the vehicle are in a safe position.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to force the shifter or solve the mechanical problem where the vehicle is parked. Securing the vehicle and handing the problem over to roadside assistance or a towing provider is a reasonable next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Diagnosis, repair, and any later decisions may need help from a qualified automotive technician.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information. It is not mechanical diagnosis or professional advice. Local roadway conditions and rules vary. In an immediate safety emergency, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
