# What to do if… someone keeps trying to isolate you from your friends at a party (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a public staffed area and contact a trusted friend or staff member now. If you are in immediate danger or physically prevented from leaving, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go somewhere secluded to reduce tension or be polite.
- Do not accept pressure to spend a minute alone with the person.
- Do not feel that you need to explain, negotiate, or stay polite before moving away.
- Do not keep drinking from a drink you left unattended or are concerned about.
- Do not leave alone with the person or go to a parking area alone if you can move with a trusted friend or staff member.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move to a public staffed area, such as the bar, host stand, entrance, or another place where staff are present.
2. **Contact one trusted person.** Text, call, or say: “Come to the bar now. Stay with me.”
3. **Tell staff what is happening.** Say: “I feel unsafe. Someone keeps trying to separate me from my friends. Please help me stay away from them.” Ask for security if available.
4. **Leave with support if you want to go.** Ask a trusted person to leave with you. Ask staff to help you reach your ride or exit safely if needed.
5. **Call 911 for immediate danger.** Call, or ask someone else to call, if you are threatened, physically prevented from leaving, or need emergency help. Give your location and say what is happening.
6. **Get urgent medical help if you feel unexpectedly unwell.** Tell a trusted friend or staff member if you feel much more intoxicated than expected or think your drink may have been tampered with. Call 911 if you need emergency medical assistance.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide now whether to report anything.
- You do not have to confront the person or explain why you are leaving.
- You do not have to decide whether the situation meets any particular label.
- You do not have to keep the group’s plans, rides, or social arrangements intact.

## Important reassurance
Moving away and getting help is a reasonable response. You are not responsible for managing the other person’s feelings or for any unsafe behaviour they choose.

## Scope note
These are first steps for reaching a safer pause. If anything sexual happened without your consent, or you are unsure, specialist support can help you consider options at your pace.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 for immediate danger or emergency medical help. RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline can provide support and help you find local resources.
