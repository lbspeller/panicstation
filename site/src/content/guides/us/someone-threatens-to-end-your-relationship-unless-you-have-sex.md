# What to do if… someone threatens to end your relationship unless you have sex (USA)

## Short answer
You do not owe anyone sex to keep a relationship. Get to a safer pause, stop the sexual situation if you can, and contact a specialist support service or a trusted person.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to sex because you feel you have to keep the relationship.
- Do not stay in a drawn-out argument if leaving the conversation feels safer.
- Do not meet them somewhere private to talk if you feel unsafe or pressured.
- Do not use a device they may monitor if using it could increase your risk.
- Do not pressure yourself to decide what to call the situation right now.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you are with them, move toward other people or a place where you can pause safely, when possible. End the conversation or leave if you can do so safely.
2. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.** Call when you can do so safely.
3. **Use a short boundary only if it feels safe.** You can say: “No.” “Stop.” or “I’m leaving.” You do not need to explain or negotiate.
4. **Contact someone you trust.** Tell a friend, family member, roommate, or neighbor that you need support. Ask them to stay on the phone, meet you, or help you get somewhere safer.
5. **Contact specialist support if that feels helpful.**
   - RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline: call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, use online chat, or text **HOPE** to **64673**.
   - National Domestic Violence Hotline: call **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)**, use chat, or text **START** to **88788**.
6. **Consider healthcare support if there has been sexual contact or you are worried about your health.** A healthcare professional can discuss your options. RAINN can help you look for local support.
7. **If you may want messages later and it feels safe, leave them in place or save a copy somewhere the other person cannot access.**

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to report anything, consider legal options, or end the relationship.
- You do not need to write a detailed explanation or prove what happened.
- You do not need to confront them face-to-face or respond to further ultimatums right now.

## Important reassurance
Threatening to end a relationship unless you have sex is sexual coercion. Feeling frozen, conflicted, or unsure does not remove your right to say no. You deserve support without pressure to take any particular next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for reaching a safer pause and reducing immediate pressure. Later decisions may need specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
