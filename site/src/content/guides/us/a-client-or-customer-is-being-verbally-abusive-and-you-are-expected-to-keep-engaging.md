# What to do if… a client or customer is being verbally abusive and you are expected to keep engaging (USA)

## Short answer
Do not handle the abuse alone. Set one clear boundary, bring in a supervisor or coworker, and pause or hand over the interaction if the abuse continues.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue, insult the person back, or make promises just to end the confrontation.
- Do not share personal details beyond what your workplace requires, such as your schedule, home area, personal phone number, or social media accounts.
- Do not stay isolated with the person if you can move to a visible, staffed area.
- Do not delete messages, tickets, chats, or notes that show what happened.
- Do not keep trying to manage the interaction alone if you feel unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** In person, move closer to coworkers or a staffed area if you can. On a call or online, use a brief hold or pause while you get support.
2. **Use one boundary line.** Say: “I want to help, but I cannot continue while you are using abusive language.”
3. **Bring in backup.** Ask a supervisor or coworker to join the interaction, stand nearby, take over the call, or review the written exchange.
4. **Pause or hand over if the abuse continues.** Say: “I am getting support now.” Where possible, step back, place the call on hold, or pause replies while you seek support through your workplace’s usual route.
5. **Treat immediate danger as an emergency.** If the person threatens violence, moves toward you aggressively, or you believe anyone is in immediate danger, move to a safer place, contact security if available, and call 911.
6. **Record what happened as soon as practical.** Note the time, place or channel, brief exact wording of any threats or slurs, witnesses, and who you asked for help.
7. **Report it through your usual workplace route.** Tell a supervisor or manager and ask how the incident should be recorded. If the conduct may relate to race, sex, religion, disability, or another protected characteristic, also use any available HR, EEO, or harassment-reporting route.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to make a formal complaint beyond your usual workplace report.
- Writing a perfect account of every detail.
- Reviewing external routes or longer-term workplace changes.

## Important reassurance
Needing backup or pausing an abusive interaction is reasonable. You do not have to solve the whole situation while someone is shouting at you or intimidating you.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about workplace procedures, formal complaints, or specialist support may need further help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.
