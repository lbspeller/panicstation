# What to do if… your sliding patio door has jumped its track and you cannot secure it (USA)

## Short answer
Treat it as a home security and glass-safety issue: move people away from the door, block the sliding panel from moving if you can do that without forcing the glass, and arrange urgent repair.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep yanking, slamming, or forcing the panel; it can move further off track or stress the glass.
- Don’t try to lift the door out or re-seat it by yourself if it is heavy, wobbly, cracked, or binding.
- Don’t rely on a latch that “almost” catches; if it does not lock, assume the door can be opened.
- Don’t block the only usable exit route in case of fire.
- Don’t leave the problem obvious from outside, such as open blinds, valuables beside the door, or notes on the glass.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** Bring everyone, including pets, away from the door. Close nearby interior doors. Keep your phone, keys, and shoes with you.
2. **Decide if you need emergency help now.**
   - If someone is trying to get in, you see a person outside, or you feel in immediate danger, call **911**.
   - If you suspect tampering but there is no immediate danger, take photos from a safe position and look up your local police non-emergency number on your city or county website.
3. **If the panel closes without force, block the track.** Slide it as closed as it will safely go. Put a wooden dowel, broom handle, solid bar, or similar sturdy object snugly in the inside bottom track so the sliding panel cannot move open easily.
4. **If the panel will not fully close, hold the safest closed position.** Use a firm doorstop or wood shim only if it holds without pressing hard on the glass. You may place a movable furniture barrier in front of the door, but do not block the only exit route and do not create a trip hazard.
5. **Reduce visibility and attention.** Close curtains or blinds if you can do that safely. Turn on exterior lighting if you have it. Avoid repeatedly testing the door.
6. **Reduce injury risk around the glass.** Keep children and pets away. Do not lean objects against the pane. If the glass is cracked, spidered, or making popping sounds, keep clear and wait for professional help.
7. **Arrange urgent repair.**
   - Homeowner: call a locksmith, glass-and-door repair company, or emergency board-up service and say, “My sliding patio door is off track and cannot be secured.”
   - Renter: contact your landlord, property manager, or emergency maintenance line immediately. Send a written message too: “The sliding glass door has jumped its track and will not lock; the home cannot be secured.” Keep screenshots or records of when you reported it.
8. **If you still cannot make the door feel safe tonight, change the plan.** Stay with someone you trust, or move everyone to the most secure part of the home while keeping the door physically blocked and your phone nearby.

## What can wait
- You do not need to realign rollers, adjust screws, remove the door, or diagnose the track tonight.
- You do not need to decide on permanent upgrades, replacement parts, or a new door right now.
- You do not need to settle cost, lease responsibility, insurance, or liability in the moment; focus on safety, documentation, and urgent repair.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel on edge when an exterior door will not lock. A snug physical stop in the inside track can make a sliding door harder to open while you buy time for proper repair.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilize the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions about repair, replacement, rental responsibilities, insurance, or security upgrades may need a qualified professional or local specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional locksmith, building, legal, security, or emergency-service advice. If someone may be trying to enter or you are in immediate danger, call 911.
