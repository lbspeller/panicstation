# What to do if… you hear a repeated clicking sound from a wall or electrical outlet (USA)

## Short answer
Treat repeated clicking near an outlet as a possible electrical hazard: stop using that outlet, shut off the clearly identified breaker if it is safe, and arrange a qualified electrician or urgent property repair.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore persistent or repeated clicking, especially if it is new.
- Don’t keep using the outlet “just this once.”
- Don’t move the same load to a power strip plugged into that outlet.
- Don’t remove the cover plate, open the wall, or attempt a DIY fix.
- Don’t keep flipping a breaker back on if it trips; leave it off and get it checked.
- Don’t go to the electrical panel if you are wet, standing in water, smell burning near the panel, or feel unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate danger from a safe distance.** If you see smoke, flames, sparks, arcing, charring, or you smell strong burning, get everyone to a safer place and call 911.
2. **Stop using the outlet or wall area.** If something is plugged in and it is safe to touch, turn the device off and unplug it by gripping the plug, not the cord.
3. **Do not touch a suspect outlet or plug.** If the outlet, plug, cord, or wall plate is warm, discolored, sparking, cracked, loose, or smells like burning, keep away from it.
4. **Shut off power only if you can do it safely and clearly.** At the electrical panel, turn off the breaker you know controls that outlet area. If you cannot identify it confidently, do not guess; keep the area unused and call a qualified electrician, your landlord, or property maintenance.
5. **Reduce nearby fire risk while waiting.** If you can do this safely without touching the outlet or wall area, move paper, fabric, curtains, furniture, aerosols, and other easy-burning items away from that spot.
6. **Arrange inspection or urgent repair.** Tell the electrician or property manager the exact location, whether the clicking is constant or only happens when something is plugged in, and whether there is warmth, odor, flickering lights, loose plugs, sparks, or breaker trips.
7. **If you rent or live in multi-unit housing, report it urgently.** Say that there is a repeated clicking sound near an outlet or wall area and that you have stopped using the area because of a suspected electrical fault.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the sound is from the outlet, wiring, HVAC, plumbing, pests, or the building settling.
- You do not need to open anything, test with tools, or take photos close to the outlet.
- You do not need to decide today about upgrades, rewiring, panel work, or replacing appliances; first make the area unused and get it checked.

## Important reassurance
This is a reasonable thing to take seriously. Many harmless house sounds exist, but repeated clicking near an outlet is enough reason to stop using that area and let a qualified person inspect it.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions about repairs, wiring, appliances, landlord duties, insurance, or costs may need help from a qualified electrician, property manager, local code office, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, or other professional advice. If there are signs of fire or immediate danger, such as smoke, flames, sparks, arcing, unusual heat, or a strong burning smell, leave the area and call 911.
