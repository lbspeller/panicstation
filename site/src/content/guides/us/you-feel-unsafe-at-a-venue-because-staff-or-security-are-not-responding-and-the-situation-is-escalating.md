# What to do if… you feel unsafe at a venue because staff or security are not responding and the situation is escalating (USA)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a well-lit, public exit and leave the venue without engaging with the source of the threat. If you believe you are in immediate physical danger and cannot leave safely, call 911 now.

## Do not do these things
- Don't attempt to argue with or "film" staff or security to prove they are failing—this wastes time and may provoke further aggression.
- Don't head toward private or low-traffic areas like bathrooms, basement levels, or dark corners of parking lots.
- Don't wait for your friends to "finish their drink" or for the staff to "check with a manager" before you move toward the exit.
- Don't leave the building with a stranger who offers to "help you out the back way."
- Don't wait for a refund, your coat from a coat check, or a closed tab if the situation is physically escalating.
- Don't use a secondary exit that leads to a confined alleyway unless the main exit is blocked.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward the primary, most populated exit immediately.** Do not wait for security to acknowledge your previous request; if they have already ignored you, your priority is creating physical distance between yourself and the hazard.
2. **Make one final, specific request to the nearest staff member if they are in your path.** Use clear, directive language: **"I am being threatened and I need to be escorted to the front door right now."** If they do not immediately comply, continue moving toward the exit on your own.
3. **Keep your phone in your hand with the emergency dialer ready.** If you are being followed or the crowd is becoming physically aggressive, dial 911. Even if you do not press "call" yet, having the screen active reduces the time needed to get help.
4. **Initiate a 911 call if you cannot reach the exit or if the threat is immediate.** **State the venue name and your specific location inside first (e.g., "The Blue Room, near the DJ booth").** If you are unsure of the address, tell the dispatcher you are at a venue and the Next Generation 911 system will use geospatial data to find your coordinates.
5. **Use the "Silent Call" or Text-to-911 protocols if you cannot speak safely.**  
   - **If you call 911 and cannot talk, do not hang up.** In many jurisdictions, dispatchers will prompt you to press digits on your keypad: Press 1 for Police, 2 for Fire, or 3 for Ambulance. If they ask a question, press 4 for "Yes" and 5 for "No."  
   - **Text-to-911 is available in many US areas.** Send a short text with your location and the word "Help." If the service is not supported, you will receive an immediate "bounce-back" text from your carrier; if you get this, you must make a voice call.
6. **Exit the building and go to a "safe haven" location.** Do not stop just outside the doors where the situation can still spill out. Walk to a nearby open hotel lobby, a 24-hour convenience store, or a well-lit gas station.
7. **Call for transport from a secure interior location.** Only wait for a rideshare or friend once you are inside a secondary, staffed building. If you believe someone followed you out of the venue, tell the staff at the new location and ask them to call the police.

## What can wait
- You do -not- need to close your bar tab or retrieve your credit card; you can call the venue the next day to resolve financial matters.
- You do -not- need to file a formal complaint or write a review in this moment.
- You do -not- need to decide whether you want to "press charges" or take legal action until you are home and physically safe.
- You do -not- need to find every member of your group if it puts you in danger; send a group text stating: "I feel unsafe and am leaving now. Meet me at [Safe Location]."

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of "social pressure" or embarrassment when leaving a venue abruptly, especially if staff are acting like nothing is wrong. However, your internal alarm is a valid data point. Choosing to leave is a proactive safety move, not an "overreaction." If security is not responding, you are effectively your own first responder until emergency services arrive.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for personal safety when venue protocols fail. It does not cover long-term legal reporting, civil lawsuits against venues, or crowd-crush survival tactics.

## Important note
Emergency 911 capabilities vary by county. Text-to-911 and Silent Call Procedures are widely but not universally implemented. Always prioritize a voice call if it is safe to do so. This guide is for informational purposes and does not replace professional law enforcement advice.
