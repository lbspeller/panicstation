# What to do if… someone tries to steer you toward a quieter area and you feel pressured (USA)

## Short answer
Move toward a staffed, visible public place and stop going with them. If you feel in immediate danger or they keep following you, call 911 or ask staff to call.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep walking with them just to avoid seeming rude.
- Don’t go to a quieter area, your car, your home, or a secluded pickup point with them.
- Don’t get drawn into a long explanation or argument. Keep any refusal brief if speaking feels safe.
- Don’t focus on your phone if that makes it harder to move toward staff or notice what is happening.
- Don’t assume you have to handle the situation alone when staff or other people are nearby.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a safer pause.** Go toward staff, a busy counter, a security desk, a hotel front desk, or another visible public place. Go inside when that is safe and practical.
2. **Stop going with them.** If speaking feels safe, use a brief line such as: **“No. I’m staying here.”**
3. **Ask one person for help.** Say: **“I need help. I don’t know this person. Please stay with me.”** Ask staff to stay with you, help you remain in a staffed area, and call 911 if needed.
4. **Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger or the person keeps following you.** Answer the call-taker’s questions. These may include your location, your phone number, the nature of the emergency, and a physical description of the person. If speaking could increase risk, ask staff or a bystander to call.
5. **Use your phone only when it helps.** If it is safe and does not delay reaching staff, call 911 or send a brief message to a trusted person with your location.
6. **Stay in the staffed public place when possible.** Do not go home or to a secluded pickup point while the person is following you.
7. **Once you are safer, note the basics.** Write down the time, place, what happened, the person’s description, and any vehicle details. If the incident happened in a business, ask whether staff can note it and retain any relevant footage.
8. **For non-emergency support later, consider VictimConnect.** VictimConnect offers referrals and support for victims of crime. Call or text **1-855-4-VICTIM (855-484-2846)**.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide whether you were overreacting.
- You don’t need to confront the person or gather proof while you still feel unsafe.
- You don’t need to make detailed later decisions until you are somewhere safer.
- You don’t need to leave a staffed public place just because the person has moved away.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to stop, move back toward other people, and ask for help. Feeling pressured is enough reason to prioritize a staffed, visible place.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. Later decisions may need support from a local victim-service organization or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you need emergency assistance, call 911.
