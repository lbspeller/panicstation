# What to do if… a retirement or pension payment is stopped pending identity verification (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the payment status with your bank, then contact your payer through an official channel to complete identity proofing via Login.gov, ID.me, or an in-person appointment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide your Social Security number or birth date to anyone who calls, texts, or emails you unexpectedly.
- Do not click links in "urgent" texts claiming your Social Security or pension benefits are frozen.
- Do not pay a "verification fee" or "reactivation charge" to any individual or agency to release your funds.
- Do not use contact numbers found in sponsored search engine results; only use official .gov or corporate portal numbers.
- Do not assume a missing payment is a technical error until you have confirmed your identity status with the payer.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the payment status with your financial institution.** Contact your bank or credit union to ask if a deposit from your payer (e.g., SSA, OPM, or a specific pension fund) was attempted, rejected, or is currently held in a "pending" state.
2. **Determine your payer's specific verification track.**
   - **Social Security (SSA):** Go to Step 3.
   - **Federal Civil Service (OPM):** Go to Step 5.
   - **Private/Employer Pension:** Go to Step 6.
3. **Access your 'my Social Security' account via Login.gov or ID.me.** The Social Security Administration requires these secure portals for identity proofing as of 2026.
   - If you cannot access the account, call 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778) to request a "one-time PIN" for phone-based verification or to schedule an in-person identity proofing appointment at a local field office.
4. **Identify the trigger for the SSA payment hold.** Ask the representative if the hold was caused by a recent change to your direct deposit, an address update, or a flag for "unconfirmed identity."
   - If the hold is due to a direct deposit change, you may be required to visit an SSA office in person with valid government-issued photo identification.
5. **Report a missing OPM annuity payment through official channels.** If your federal retiree payment is missing, use the OPM "Report a Missing Annuity Payment" online tool or call 1-888-767-6738.
   - You must have your seven-digit claim number (CSA or CSF) ready.
   - OPM generally requires 3 to 5 business days to research the payment status before issuing a response.
6. **Contact private plan administrators via their verified participant portal.** Log in to your pension or annuity provider's secure website (e.g., Fidelity, Vanguard, Prudential) to check for "Alerts" or "Required Actions" regarding your identity.
   - If you must call, use the number printed on your most recent paper statement.
   - Ask for a "secure upload" link or a physical mailing address for ID documents; do not send sensitive files via standard email.
7. **Report suspected identity theft to federal authorities immediately.** If the payer indicates that someone else tried to change your account details, visit IdentityTheft.gov to create a formal report and recovery plan.
8. **Request a written confirmation of the resolution.** Once you have submitted your identity proof, ask the payer for a confirmation number and a timeline for when the "held" funds will be released to your account.

## What can wait
- You do not need to change your bank account unless the payer confirms the current account was compromised.
- You do not need to contact a lawyer or advocacy group until you have attempted the official verification process.
- You do not need to file a police report unless the payer confirms that funds were actually diverted to a fraudulent account.

## Important reassurance
Identity verification holds are standard security measures designed to protect your lifetime earnings from unauthorized access. While the loss of immediate income is stressful, these holds are typically reversible once your identity is confirmed through the proper, secure channels.

## Scope note
This guide addresses the immediate steps to satisfy identity verification requirements for US-based retirement and pension systems. It does not cover long-term benefit appeals or legal disputes over benefit eligibility.

## Important note
Procedures for identity proofing, especially digital requirements like Login.gov or ID.me, are subject to federal regulatory changes. Always verify the current submission requirements directly with your benefit provider before sending sensitive documents.
