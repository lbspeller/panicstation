# What to do if… your password manager stops unlocking and you cannot access your saved logins (USA)

## Short answer
Stop and **do not factory reset or erase the device**. Confirm the specific account (Apple or Google) and the storage location (Cloud vs. Computer) where the backup was last recorded.

## Do not do these things
- Don't factory reset the device in an attempt to trigger a restore prompt without first verifying the backup exists.
- Don't sign out of your Apple Account or Google Account if you are unsure of the credentials for other potential accounts.
- Don't delete any old backups, large files, or "cleanup" cloud storage to make room, as this may delete the only remaining restore points.
- Don't assume a "successful" backup status includes 100% of app data; many apps (like WhatsApp or Signal) require separate internal backup toggles.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the device and account**
   - **Plug the phone into a power source and connect to a stable Wi-Fi network.**
   - **Log in to your cloud dashboard from a separate computer to verify the account status.**
      - For iPhone: Go to iCloud.com.
      - For Android: Go to drive.google.com and check the "Backups" folder.

2. **Verify the Apple Account (iPhone/iPad)**
   - **Open Settings, tap [Your Name], and confirm the email address matches the account you intended to back up.**
   - **Navigate to iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Backups to see a list of devices currently using storage.**
   - **Tap the specific device name to view the "Last Backup" timestamp and total backup size.**

3. **Verify the Google Account (Android)**
   - **Open Settings → Google → All services → Backup.**
   - **Confirm the "Google Account" email listed matches your primary account.**
   - **Check the "Backup details" section to see the last time "Device settings," "Photos & videos," and "SMS" were successfully processed.**

4. **Check for computer-based backups**
   - **On a Mac: Connect the phone, open Finder, select the device, and click "Manage Backups" under the General tab.**
   - **On Windows: Open the "Apple Devices" app (or iTunes if the newer app is not installed) and check the "Manage Backups" list.**
      - Note: If you have installed the Apple Devices app on Windows 10/11, iTunes will no longer handle backup management.

5. **Cross-reference cloud backups via Windows**
   - **Install or open "iCloud for Windows" and click "Storage."**
   - **Select "Backups" from the list on the left to see every device backup stored in the cloud, even if the phone itself isn't displaying them.**

6. **Check independent app backups**
   - **Verify if specific critical apps have their own cloud settings.**
      - WhatsApp: Settings → Chats → Chat Backup.
      - Signal: Settings → Chats → Chat backups.
      - Google Photos: Tap profile icon → Photos settings → Backup.

7. **Determine if the backup has expired**
   - **Confirm if you recently disabled backup services.**
      - Apple may delete iCloud backups 180 days after you turn off iCloud Backup for that device.
      - Google may delete backup data if the device is inactive for 57 days or if the account is inactive for 2 years.

8. **Contact official support for account-level investigation**
   - **Call Apple Support USA at 1-800-275-2273 if you are a paid iCloud+ subscriber.**
   - **Access Google One Support via the Google One app or website if you are a paid storage member.**
   - **Document the "Last Backup" date you saw previously by taking a screenshot or writing it down before speaking with support.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide which new phone to buy or which service to switch to right now.
- You do not need to clear out your inbox or delete photos to "fix" a storage error during a crisis.
- You do not need to perform a factory reset today; wait until a technician or official support confirms the backup is accessible.

## Important reassurance
Verification errors and "missing" backup lists are common during device transitions or when multiple accounts are involved. Pausing to verify the account and device name prevents the permanent loss that occurs during accidental resets.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for locating missing digital backups. It does not cover data forensic recovery from damaged hardware or legal disputes with service providers.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee data recovery. Backup availability depends on account standing, storage limits, and service-specific retention policies (e.g., Apple's 180-day window after service deactivation). For critical data loss, contact official Apple or Google support channels immediately.
