# What to do if… someone posts online about a death in your family before the immediate family has been told (USA)

## Short answer
Notify the closest immediate family members via direct phone call immediately to ensure they hear the news from family first. Once relatives are informed, use platform-specific reporting tools to request the removal of the post.

## Do not do these things
- Do not comment on the post to correct details or confirm the death, as engagement can increase the post's visibility in algorithms.
- Do not engage in an argument with the poster; a single, clear request for removal is sufficient.
- Do not share or screenshot the post for others, as this further spreads the information you are trying to contain.
- Do not feel obligated to respond to condolences or questions from others on the platform right now.
- Do not attempt to log into the deceased person's account using guessed passwords; use official platform channels instead.

## What to do now
1. **Assign one person to coordinate notifications.** Ensure that the closest relatives (parents, children, spouse, siblings) are told via phone or in person before they have a chance to see the news online.
   - Use a clear script: "I have very difficult news to share about [Name]. I wanted to make sure you heard it directly from me."
2. **Document the post once for reporting purposes.** Take one screenshot that includes the username, the content of the post, and the timestamp, then close the application.
3. **Send a single, direct request for removal if the poster is known to you.** Use neutral language: "The immediate family has not been fully notified yet. Please delete this post immediately to respect our privacy."
4. **Use platform-specific reporting tools for sensitive content.** Most platforms have dedicated paths for reporting the death of a family member or "Moment of Death" media.
   - On X (formerly Twitter), use the "Moment of Death" report form for sensitive media.
   - On Meta (Facebook/Instagram), use the "Request to Memorialize or Remove a Deceased Person's Account" form.
   - Choose categories like "Privacy Violation" or "Harassment" if a specific death-reporting category is not immediately visible.
5. **Update your own privacy and tagging settings.** Enable "Timeline Review" or "Tag Review" on your profiles to prevent others from tagging you in posts about the death, which would make the news visible to your entire contact list.
6. **Delegate the monitoring of the post.** If the post remains up, ask one trusted friend or extended family member to check its status once an hour and report it periodically so you can focus on the immediate family.
7. **Contact 911 only if there is a secondary safety threat.** If the online posts include threats of harm, doxxing, or active stalking of surviving family members, involve local law enforcement.
8. **Contact 988 for immediate emotional crisis support.** If you or a family member are overwhelmed by the news or the online reaction, call or text the **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** for 24/7 confidential support.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on funeral arrangements or public obituaries right now.
- You do not need to "set the record straight" regarding any rumors or misinformation in the comments.
- You do not need to respond to the hundreds of messages or notifications that may follow a viral post.
- You do not need to decide how to handle the deceased person's digital legacy (memorialization vs. deletion) today.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a deep sense of panic or violation when private family news is made public without your consent. Taking immediate control of the family notification process is the most effective way to reduce the impact of the online post. You are not responsible for the actions of the person who posted, and your priority is the well-being of the living relatives.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate aftermath of an unauthorized online death announcement. Long-term management of digital estates, legal privacy claims, or platform appeals may require specialized support later.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Platform policies and reporting forms are subject to change. For emergency emotional support, contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.
