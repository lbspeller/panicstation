# What to do if… a stranger keeps trying to start conversation after you clearly disengage (USA)

## Short answer
Increase the physical distance between yourself and the stranger immediately by moving toward a staffed or crowded area and ceasing all verbal engagement. Call 911 if you are being followed, touched, or blocked from leaving.

## Do not do these things
- Don't stay in the same spot just to avoid appearing rude or "making a scene."
- Don't keep answering questions, explaining your reasons, or arguing.
- Don't provide any personal details, including your destination, neighborhood, or phone number.
- Don't agree to move to a "quieter" or second location, even if it is nearby.
- Don't allow yourself to be moved into an isolated area like an elevator, stairwell, or alley.
- Don't take out your wallet or phone to show them information or "prove" a point.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a safer pause by entering a business or joining a crowd.** Look for a service desk, security guard, or a group of people and put a physical barrier like a counter or door between you and the stranger.
2. **State a final boundary clearly and then stop all talking.** Say "I am not interested in talking; leave me alone" or "Stop following me" once, then turn away and do not respond to any further comments.
3. **Ask for help from staff or bystanders using direct language.** Tell a store employee or security guard "This person is bothering me and won't leave me alone; I need to stay here until they are gone."
4. **Use your phone to contact a friend or family member and state your exact location.** Say the name of the business or the nearest cross-streets out loud so the stranger hears that someone else knows exactly where you are.
5. **Call 911 if the stranger follows you into a building or makes physical threats.** Provide the dispatcher with your location, a description of the person, and tell them you are being harassed or followed.
6. **Stay in your safe location until you are certain the stranger has left the area.** If you are unsure, ask a security guard or staff member to walk you to your vehicle or watch until you are safely on transit.
7. **Contact the VictimConnect Resource Center for support and information on your rights.** You can call or text 855-484-2846 to speak with a specialist 24/7 about reporting options or local safety resources.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether the interaction was "threatening enough" to justify your reaction.
- Reporting the incident to non-emergency police lines or transit authorities.
- Reviewing security footage or trying to identify the person on social media.
- You do not need to decide right now if you will change your daily route or routine.

## Important reassurance
You have the absolute right to end any interaction at any time for any reason. It is a common survival response to "fawn" or act polite when you feel threatened, and doing so does not mean you consented to the interaction. Prioritizing your physical safety over social expectations is the correct choice.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active or immediate interaction with a persistent stranger. It does not cover long-term stalking, workplace harassment, or legal proceedings.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice. If you believe a crime has been committed or you are in immediate physical danger, contact local law enforcement or call 911.
