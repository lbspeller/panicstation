# What to do if… the last ferry of the day is cancelled and you are stranded (USA)
## Short answer
Get to a safe, staffed place at or near the terminal and check the operator’s official service status before leaving. Choose one safe plan for tonight: a verified alternate route, safe nearby accommodation, or a permitted staffed place to wait while you confirm the next available option.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume a hotel, taxi, shuttle, refund, or rebooking is guaranteed until the operator confirms its policy.
- Don’t leave the terminal area before checking the official service alert and asking staff whether any later sailing or operator-provided transfer is available.
- Don’t spend your last money on a long route before checking the travel time, pickup point, and whether the route is workable tonight.
- Don’t accept an unverified ride because you feel pressured to leave quickly.
- Don’t improvise a water crossing or use an unverified private boat.
- Don’t drain your phone battery on non-essential browsing or video.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safe pause point:** go inside the terminal if it is open. If it is closed, move to the nearest open, staffed, well-lit place. Charge your phone if you can.
2. **Check the official status:** look at the operator’s website, app, alert page, departure board, or staff update. Ask whether there is a later sailing, an added trip, an official transfer, or a next available departure.
3. **Ask about your booking:** ask whether rebooking, a refund, a credit, or another official option is available for your ticket and how to request it.
4. **Save the proof you have:** keep your booking receipt and any text or email alerts. Screenshot the official cancellation notice or photograph the departures board if that is useful.
5. **Check one verified alternate route:** ask staff about any operator-provided shuttle or transfer. Check official local transit information and use an established taxi or rideshare pickup point if that is a realistic option.
6. **Choose a safe overnight option if the route is not workable:** consider safe nearby accommodation within your budget. If you are considering waiting in an open staffed place, ask staff whether you may wait there. Ask when the terminal closes and reopens and when the next sailing is expected.
7. **Use the operator’s official channel when practical:** submit any rebooking or refund request through the official website, app, phone route, or terminal process. Save the confirmation.
8. **Call 911 for an emergency:** if you need immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance, call 911. If there is no immediate emergency but you feel unsafe, stay in the nearest open staffed place and ask for help contacting safe transport.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to settle every refund or policy question tonight.
- You don’t need to plan the whole rest of the trip. Secure one safe route or one safe overnight plan first.
- You don’t need to decide immediately whether to raise a later payment dispute. Keep your receipts and alerts. For an unresolved credit-card charge, review your card issuer’s process and official consumer guidance promptly after you are safe.

## Important reassurance
A last-sailing cancellation can leave you feeling trapped and rushed. You only need to secure the next safe step: a verified route tonight or a safe place to stay or wait while you confirm the next available option.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, insurance claims, or a revised itinerary may need operator-specific or specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Available options depend on the ferry operator, route, ticket type, and circumstances. If you need immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance, call 911.
