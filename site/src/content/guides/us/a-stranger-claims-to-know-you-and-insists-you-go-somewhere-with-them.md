# What to do if… a stranger claims to know you and insists you go somewhere with them (USA)

## Short answer
Do not go with them. Move into the nearest staffed public place, stay where other people can see you, and call 911 or ask staff to call.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a quieter spot, back room, parking lot, alley, or vehicle to talk.
- Do not leave alone for your home or vehicle while the person is still nearby.
- Do not let them guide you by touching you or walking you toward an exit.
- Do not share your name, where you are staying, where you are headed, or who you are with.
- Do not accept a ride, help, or a shortcut from them.
- Do not hand over your phone, unlock it for them, or let them get between you and other people.
- Do not keep trying to resolve the situation politely if that prevents you from moving toward help.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause immediately.** Walk into the nearest staffed place, such as a store, hotel lobby, coffee shop, front desk, or security station. Stay near staff and other people.
2. **Use one firm sentence, then stop engaging.** Say: **“I do not know you. I am not going anywhere with you.”** Turn your attention to staff or bystanders.
3. **Recruit help directly.** Point to one person and say: **“Please call 911. This person is trying to get me to go with them.”** Ask staff or security to help you stay separated from the person.
4. **Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger, the person follows you, blocks you, tries to move you elsewhere, or will not back off.** Give your exact location, what is happening, a description of the person, and where they are now. Stay in the staffed place unless a dispatcher directs you elsewhere or the place stops feeling safe.
5. **If speaking may increase the risk, ask staff or a bystander to call if possible.** If you make a voice call, do not hang up unless the call-taker instructs you to do so. If you cannot safely make a voice call, you can try texting 911. Text-to-911 is available only in certain locations. If it is unavailable, you should receive a bounce-back message advising you to contact emergency services another way. Follow any reply you receive.
6. **If you are in a mall, transit hub, campus, hospital, or large venue, go to security or customer service.** Ask them to call 911 and help you remain separated from the person. Stay away from exits while the person is nearby.
7. **Leave only when it feels safe to do so.** Ask staff or security to watch the exit, wait with you, or escort you to a safer pickup point. Wait inside for a ride rather than walking out alone while the person is nearby.

## What can wait
- You do **not** have to decide now whether to make a police report or take any follow-up step.
- You do **not** need to keep talking to the person to work out who they are.
- You can write down the time, place, description, and what was said after you are safe.
- You do **not** need to post about the situation or warn others online in the moment.

## Important reassurance
You may feel frozen, shaky, or worried about overreacting. You do not need to work out whether the person is confused, mistaken, or deliberately intimidating you before moving toward help. Refusing to go with them and asking for help are reasonable safety steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation only. Later decisions about reporting, documentation, or additional support can wait until you are safe and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.
