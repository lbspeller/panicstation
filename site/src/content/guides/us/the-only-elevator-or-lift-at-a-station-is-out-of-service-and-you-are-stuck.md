# What to do if… the only elevator or lift at a station is out of service and you are stuck (USA)

## Short answer
Stay in a safe place away from platform edges and moving escalators. Contact any available station staff or use the station’s posted assistance channel, and ask for an accessible route or another effective way to continue or exit safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go onto the tracks or into restricted areas to look for another route.
- Do not use stairs or escalators if they are unsafe for you.
- Do not force elevator doors or try to repair or reset the elevator.
- Do not leave the station area to try another entrance until you have checked whether the route is accessible and whether you can re-enter safely.
- Do not let a missed train or appointment push you into a fall-risk decision.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the safest nearby place.** Stay back from platform edges, stairs, and moving escalators. Choose a visible area where staff can reach you.
2. **Use the fastest assistance channel available at that station.** Look for station staff, a staffed booth, an intercom, a help point, a call box, or a posted assistance number. If you are safe enough to wait, you can also check the transit agency’s website or app for an outage notice or customer-service number.
3. **State the problem plainly.** Say: “The elevator is out of service. I need step-free access and cannot reach the platform or exit safely. What accessible route or other effective accommodation is available now?”
4. **Ask staff to check the available options.** These may include another elevator or accessible entrance, an accessible way through the fare area, an accessible shuttle or connecting bus around the station, or directions to a nearby accessible station that you can safely use.
5. **If you are already on a train, contact the train crew or use an onboard assistance intercom if available.** Explain that you cannot safely use stairs and ask where you should get off to reach an accessible route.
6. **If you cannot reach staff, stay in the safe area and try another available station contact route.** You can ask a nearby person to alert staff for you if that feels comfortable and safe.
7. **Call 911 for an immediate danger or medical emergency.** This includes being in an unsafe location and being unable to reach transit staff quickly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to file a complaint, request a refund, or follow up with the agency.
- You do not need to solve the full route by yourself.
- You do not need to keep your original schedule.
- Photos, notes, and reference numbers can wait until you are safe, unless recording the details is easy and does not delay help.

## Important reassurance
An elevator outage is an access problem, not a personal failure. Transit providers are generally expected to take reasonable steps to accommodate riders with disabilities when a required accessibility feature is out of order, although the available response can vary by station and agency.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you reach a safe, accessible route or exit. Later decisions, such as complaints, refunds, or further support, can wait until the immediate situation is stable and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Station layouts, staffing, and local transit procedures vary. Seek emergency help if you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical care.
