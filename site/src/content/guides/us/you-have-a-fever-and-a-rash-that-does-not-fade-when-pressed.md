# What to do if… you have a fever and a rash that does not fade when pressed (USA)

## Short answer
Treat fever plus a rash that does not fade when pressed as an emergency. Call 911 now, or have someone take you to the nearest emergency room immediately, because this combination can be linked with dangerous infection that needs rapid treatment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait a few hours to see if it goes away.
- Do not assume it is only hives or an allergy and only take antihistamines.
- Do not use urgent care as the first stop for this combination if 911 or an emergency room is available.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel weak, faint, confused, very sleepy, short of breath, or rapidly worse.
- Do not delay because the rash is small, new, or harder to see on your skin tone.
- Do not keep pressing and rechecking the rash once you are concerned it is not fading.

## What to do now
1. Call 911 immediately. Say: "Fever with a rash that does not fade when pressed." Mention any confusion, extreme sleepiness, trouble breathing, severe headache, stiff neck, vomiting, severe pain, cold hands or feet, or rapid worsening.
2. Sit or lie down somewhere safe while you wait. Put your phone on speaker, unlock the door if you can do so safely, and keep the line open.
3. If you are with the person, check the rash once briefly, then stop testing. Press a finger or clear glass firmly against a few spots for a second or two; if the spots stay visible, treat it as a non-fading rash and focus on emergency care.
4. Look for and mention what the rash looks like. It may look like tiny red or purple pinprick spots, darker purple patches, or bruising under the skin.
5. Gather key information only if it does not slow the emergency call:
   - Current medicines and allergies.
   - Major medical conditions, especially immune problems.
   - Recent infection, recent antibiotics, or close contact with someone seriously ill.
   - Meningococcal vaccine status, if known.
   - When the fever started and when the rash appeared or changed.
6. Take one clear photo of the rash in good light if you can do it quickly. This may help clinicians if the rash changes before you are seen.
7. If the person becomes very drowsy, collapses, has a seizure, or struggles to breathe, stay on the 911 line and follow dispatcher instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact diagnosis before seeking care.
- You do not need to decide whether this is meningococcal disease, sepsis, meningitis, an allergic reaction, or something else.
- You do not need to finish chores, work messages, childcare planning, school messages, or travel arrangements before calling for help.
- You do not need to search for more rash pictures or keep checking whether the rash is spreading.

## Important reassurance
Your concern is reasonable. Fever with a rash that does not fade when pressed is one of the situations where it is safer to act quickly, even if the cause later turns out to be less serious.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for fever plus a rash that does not fade when pressed. Testing, diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up decisions need medical professionals.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for emergency medical care. If you have fever and a rash that does not fade when pressed, seek emergency medical care right away.
