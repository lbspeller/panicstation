# What to do if… your steering shakes or the car pulls after hitting a pothole (USA)
## Short answer
Treat the vehicle as possibly unsafe to drive. Do not rejoin traffic at normal speed; call roadside assistance or a tow unless you can safely move very slowly to a nearby safer place.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t speed up to see whether the shaking goes away.
- Don’t keep driving if you see a tire sidewall bulge, cut, exposed cords, a flat tire, a fast leak, or an obviously bent or cracked wheel.
- Don’t keep driving if the steering feels loose, the car pulls hard, or you hear clunking, grinding, or scraping.
- Don’t crawl under the vehicle on the shoulder or near traffic.
- Don’t assume it is only an alignment problem; tire, wheel, suspension, and steering damage can feel similar at first.
- Don’t stand between your vehicle and moving traffic.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stopped situation as safe as you can.** Keep your hazard flashers on. Stay aware of traffic, and do not step out unless you can do so safely.
2. **Move people before inspecting the car if the roadside is risky.** If you are on a narrow shoulder, curve, ramp, bridge, or low-visibility spot, get yourself and passengers to the safest available place away from traffic if that is possible. If leaving the vehicle is more dangerous, stay belted and call for help.
3. **Do a quick outside check only if it is safe to leave the vehicle.**
   - Look at each tire for a bulge, split, exposed cords, or one tire sitting lower than the others.
   - Look at the wheel that hit hardest for an obvious bend or crack.
   - Look near the front wheels for fresh fluid, dragging plastic, or a loose part.
   - If you see any of these, do not drive.
4. **If you must reposition, only move very slowly to a safer nearby place.** This means a parking lot, wide turnout, service area, or other spot out of traffic. Stop immediately if the shaking or pulling continues, the steering wheel is off center, or a new noise starts.
5. **Call roadside assistance or a tow.** Say that you hit a pothole and now the steering shakes or the vehicle pulls. Mention any visible tire, wheel, fluid, or noise problems so the dispatcher can send the right help.
6. **Ask for a safety inspection before continuing the trip.** Ask the tire shop or repair facility to check the tire, wheel, balance, alignment, suspension parts, and steering parts.
7. **Call 911 if the stopped vehicle is creating immediate danger.** Use 911 if you are stranded in a live lane, cannot get out of traffic, have been hit, are at risk of being hit, or a crash hazard is developing.
8. **Document only what you can without adding risk.** From a safe place, take photos of visible tire or wheel damage and note the road, direction of travel, nearest intersection or exit, and time.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to file an insurance claim.
- You do not need to decide now whether to seek reimbursement from a city, county, or state road agency.
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical cause at the roadside.
- You do not need to continue the trip today if towing and reassessing is safer.

## Important reassurance
Shaking or pulling after a pothole strike can come from damage that is not obvious at a glance, including tire, wheel, suspension, or steering problems. Choosing a tow or inspection is a cautious, reasonable safety decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair, insurance, reimbursement, or fault may need help from a qualified mechanic, insurer, roadside provider, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, insurance, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are stopped, prioritize personal safety and emergency services.
