# What to do if… you suspect someone copied your passport details and you are worried about misuse while abroad (USA)

## Short answer
Secure your physical passport immediately, place a fraud alert or credit freeze with U.S. credit bureaus, and create a preliminary report at IdentityTheft.gov to document the exposure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide more personal information to the party that copied your details to "verify" your identity.
- Do not report your passport as lost or stolen if you still have it / it will be immediately cancelled and you will be stranded.
- Do not assume a passport number alone allows someone to travel in your name / physical documents are required for border crossings.
- Do not wait until you return to the U.S. to take protective financial steps.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm your passport is physically in your possession.** Ensure no one else is currently holding the document and store it in a secure location, such as a hotel safe or a zipped inner pocket.
2. **Contact one credit bureau to place a free initial fraud alert.** This requires businesses to verify your identity before opening new accounts and lasts for one year. Contacting one bureau will automatically notify the other two.
   - Equifax: +1-800-685-1111
   - Experian: +1-888-397-3742
   - TransUnion: +1-800-616-8800
3. **Freeze your credit if you suspect high risk of misuse.** A credit freeze provides stronger protection than an alert but must be placed individually with each of the three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion) via their websites or the phone numbers above.
4. **Create a report on IdentityTheft.gov.** Use the "Get Started" tool to document that your passport information was exposed; this generates a recovery plan and a formal affidavit you can use if identity theft occurs later.
5. **Change passwords for high-value accounts.** Update login credentials for your primary email and banking apps, and ensure multi-factor authentication (MFA) is active.
6. **Monitor your accounts for "verification" notifications.** If you receive unexpected SMS codes or password reset emails, do not click links and immediately contact the relevant institution.
7. **Contact the National Passport Information Center if you have specific fraud concerns.** You can call +1-877-487-2778 (or +1-202-501-4444 from abroad) to discuss whether your passport record requires a "lost or stolen" report, but remember this step is irreversible.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace your physical passport immediately if you still have it and it has not been reported lost.
- You do not need to contact the local police in the foreign country unless you are the victim of a physical theft or direct scam.
- You do not need to close existing bank accounts unless you see unauthorized transactions.

## Important reassurance
Identity theft using only a passport number is difficult because most financial institutions require a Social Security number (SSN) and secondary ID for major transactions. Travel using only a number is impossible. Your proactive steps are designed to close the most likely windows for financial fraud.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passport data exposure while traveling. It does not replace legal advice or the comprehensive recovery steps required if a Social Security number is also compromised.

## Important note
U.S. embassies can assist with replacing lost or stolen physical passports but cannot provide legal or financial services regarding identity theft. If you are in immediate physical danger or being extorted, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or local emergency services.
