# What to do if… a car is following you or trying to get your attention while you are driving (USA)

## Short answer
Keep moving toward a busy, well-lit public place and call 911 if you feel unsafe. Do not stop alone to talk through your window.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull over on a quiet shoulder or side street to talk to them.
- Do not open your window, unlock your doors, or accept anything handed to you.
- Do not engage, argue, gesture back, or try to find out what they want while you are still exposed.
- Do not drive home or to an isolated location if you think they may be following you.
- Do not try to outrun them with dangerous driving.
- Do not text, search, film, or handle your phone while driving. If a passenger can call 911, have them call; otherwise use hands-free calling only if you can do it safely, or call once you are stopped in a busy public place.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the inside of your car.** Lock the doors, keep windows up, keep your seatbelt on, and keep enough space around your car to change lanes or turn without being boxed in.
2. **Keep driving predictably.** Hold a steady, legal speed. Avoid sudden braking, swerving, racing, or trying to challenge the other driver.
3. **Move toward people, lights, and cameras.** Aim for a busy gas station, large store parking lot, hospital entrance, police station, or another staffed public place. Avoid isolated pull-offs, empty lots, and quiet side streets.
4. **Call 911 if you feel threatened or think you are being followed.** Give your location, direction of travel, vehicle description, the other vehicle’s color, make, model, plate if safely visible, and what the occupants are doing.
5. **Do not let them choose where you stop.** If they wave, shout, point, honk, or motion for you to pull over, treat that as a reason to go somewhere more public, not less public.
6. **Do not drive home while they are still nearby.** Stay on busier roads and continue toward a public place or police station.
7. **When you stop, avoid being trapped.** Park near an entrance under lights, leave room to drive away, keep doors locked, and stay in the car if that feels safest.
8. **Once you are safe, write down what you remember.** Note the time, place, direction of travel, vehicle details, number of occupants, what they did, and whether you have dashcam footage.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was a prank, misunderstanding, road rage, or an attempted setup.
- You do not need to confront them, prove intent, finish your original route, or explain yourself to them.
- You do not need to post about it, call several people, or make a detailed report until you are safely stopped.

## Important reassurance
It is common to freeze, second-guess yourself, or feel embarrassed in a situation like this. Choosing a public place and getting help is a calm safety step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. Later decisions, such as whether to make a police report, share dashcam footage, or change a routine, can wait until you are safe and may need help from local professionals or someone you trust.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in danger or someone is trying to force you to stop, call 911.
