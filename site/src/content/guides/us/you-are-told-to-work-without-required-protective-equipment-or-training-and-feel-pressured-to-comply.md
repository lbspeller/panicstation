# What to do if… you are told to work without required protective equipment or training and feel pressured to comply (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safe pause, tell your employer which PPE or training is missing, and ask for the hazard to be corrected or for other work. If there is immediate danger, get to safety first and call 911 if emergency help is needed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter or remain in a hazard area just to prove that you can do the task.
- Do not use damaged, wrong-size, or improvised PPE as a substitute for the equipment required for the task.
- Do not sign a form saying that you completed training or fit-testing if you did not.
- Do not put yourself at risk to take photographs. Notes are enough.
- Do not leave the worksite merely because you filed a complaint. Move to a safe location and ask what safe work is available unless the location itself is unsafe or you are told to leave.
- Do not get pulled into an argument. Keep your words brief and factual.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safe pause.** Step away from the hazard area. If you are operating equipment, shut it down only if you can do so safely without increasing the risk.
2. **Say what is missing.** Tell the supervisor: “I do not have the PPE or training needed to do this task safely. Please correct the hazard or assign other work.”
3. **Ask for the specific requirement.** Ask what PPE is required, where it is available, and what training is needed before the task starts. Ask about fit-testing or supervision where that applies.
4. **Stay available for safe work.** Remain at a safe location and ask for another task while the hazard is being addressed, unless the location itself is unsafe or you are instructed to leave.
5. **Make a brief record.** Note the date, time, worksite, task, hazard, missing PPE or training, who gave the instruction, and what you said. Keep a copy outside workplace systems if retaliation is a concern. Take a photograph only from a safe location and only where permitted.
6. **Send a short written follow-up if you safely can.** State the task, the missing PPE or training, that you paused because of the safety concern, and that you remain available for safe work.
7. **Treat an emergency as urgent.** Call 911 if emergency help is needed. For a workplace fatality or an imminent life-threatening situation, call OSHA at **1-800-321-6742** rather than using an online form or email.
8. **Report an unresolved non-emergency hazard.** You may file a safety and health complaint with OSHA and ask for an inspection. OSHA accepts complaints online, by phone, or by letter. A safety and health complaint may be filed anonymously, and you may ask OSHA to keep your identity confidential. Selecting your state in the online form may route you to an OSHA-approved State Plan.
9. **Act quickly if retaliation starts.** If you are fired, threatened, disciplined, transferred, or have your hours cut after raising the safety concern, contact OSHA promptly. A retaliation complaint under the OSH Act generally must be filed within **30 days** of the alleged retaliation. Other whistleblower laws may use different filing windows. A whistleblower retaliation complaint cannot be filed anonymously. If OSHA proceeds with an investigation, your employer will be notified of the complaint.
10. **Use extra caution with a refusal to perform the task.** OSHA guidance says a refusal may have legal protection only in specific circumstances. These generally include a danger that clearly risks death or serious physical harm, not enough time for an OSHA inspection, a good-faith belief that the danger is real, and a danger that a reasonable person would recognise. Where possible, ask the employer to correct the danger or assign other work, state that you will not perform the task until the danger is corrected, remain at the worksite unless it is unsafe or you are told to leave, and contact OSHA promptly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to quit, hire a lawyer, or speak publicly.
- You do not need to prove the whole safety issue in the moment.
- You do not need to negotiate wider policy changes today.
- You do not need to collect evidence from inside a hazard area.

## Important reassurance
Pressure from a supervisor can make a safety concern feel difficult to raise. A brief safe pause, a clear request for the missing PPE or training, and a factual written record are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The rules and available processes can vary by task, industry, state, and job status, so later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Prioritize immediate safety and emergency help where needed.
