# What to do if… your public transport pass in an app shows as invalid even though you paid (USA)

## Short answer
Do not delete the app as your first move. Preserve what the app is showing, pull up your receipt or bank charge, try the safest display fixes, and ask staff what the agency process is for documenting the problem.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete the app, clear app data, or reset your phone in a panic unless the agency app support tells you to or you know your tickets are safely tied to your account.
- Do not assume a screenshot, confirmation email, or bank charge will count as a valid ticket; many agencies require a live, active, scannable ticket or pass.
- Do not change your phone’s clock to try to make the ticket work.
- Do not hand over your unlocked phone; keep the phone in your hand and show the screen.
- Do not ignore a fare citation, notice, or payment instruction; follow the deadline and dispute steps printed on it.

## What to do now
1. **Make the phone as display-ready as possible.**
   - Move to a safe place where you are not blocking boarding, doors, stairs, or the platform edge.
   - Raise screen brightness and keep the ticket screen open if it appears.
   - Connect a charger or battery pack if you have one.
   - Close other apps that may be slowing the phone.
   - Check that your phone’s date and time look correct, but do not change the clock to force a ticket result.

2. **Collect proof that you paid.**
   - Open the purchase receipt, confirmation email, order number, or app purchase history.
   - Open the matching card, bank, or mobile-wallet charge showing date, time, and amount.
   - Save a screenshot of the error message or order screen if it does not show an active barcode, animation, or protected ticket screen.
   - Do not screenshot or record an activated ticket barcode or animated ticket if the app or agency terms prohibit it.

3. **Try the lowest-risk app fixes.**
   - Toggle cellular data or Wi-Fi once, then reopen the ticket screen.
   - Force-close the app and reopen it.
   - Sign back in if the app has logged you out.
   - Look for an in-app option such as refresh account, restore purchases, reload pass, ticket history, or purchase history.
   - Restart the phone if the app is frozen and you are not in the middle of showing a ticket to staff.

4. **If you are being checked right now, keep it simple.**
   - Say: “I paid, but the app is showing this as invalid. Here is my receipt and the matching charge. What is your process for documenting an app ticket problem?”
   - Ask whether staff can verify the pass by scanning, account lookup, order number, card, or ticket history if that is part of that agency’s system.
   - If they say your receipt is not enough, ask what record you should keep for customer service or a citation dispute.

5. **If you are told to buy another fare to continue the trip, protect the record.**
   - Buy the lowest valid fare you are instructed to buy, if you can.
   - Keep the second receipt.
   - Note the route, train, bus, station, stop, time, vehicle number, and staff name or ID if visible.
   - Keep the original purchase proof and the second purchase proof together.

6. **If you receive a citation or notice, switch to paperwork mode.**
   - Photograph both sides of the citation or notice.
   - Read the dispute or payment instructions before leaving it aside.
   - Save the receipt, bank charge, error screenshot, order number, and any second fare receipt.
   - Submit the dispute through the agency’s stated process before the deadline printed on the notice.

7. **Contact the agency or app support as soon as you can.**
   - Use the official customer service, app support, or fare dispute channel for that transit agency.
   - Give only the useful details: pass type, order number, purchase time, route or station, what the app displayed, and whether staff required another fare or issued a notice.
   - Ask for written confirmation that your purchase exists and whether there was an outage, account-sync issue, or known app problem.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to escalate beyond the normal customer service or dispute process.
- You do not need to replace your phone, reset your account, or change payment cards while you are still travelling.
- You do not need to solve your long-term fare setup until the immediate trip, proof, and any notice are under control.

## Important reassurance
Mobile ticket and payment-sync problems do happen. A calm record of what you paid, what the app showed, and what staff told you is usually more useful than arguing in the moment.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment of failure and the first follow-up. Later decisions may need help from the transit agency, the ticketing vendor, a local consumer-help service, or a legal adviser if a citation, debt, or court-related document is involved.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, or professional advice. Transit rules vary by agency, ticket type, city, and state, so follow the instructions on your app, receipt, citation, and transit agency website.
