# What to do if… you have been in the cold and cannot stop shivering or feel confused (USA)

## Short answer
Assume this could be hypothermia. Get to warmth now and call 911 if there is confusion, severe drowsiness, clumsiness, slurred speech, or worsening symptoms after cold exposure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “tough it out” or keep walking or driving if you are confused, clumsy, or very sleepy.
- Do not use a hot bath, hot tub, heating lamp, or other rapid rewarming to heat up fast.
- Do not rub or massage cold arms, legs, hands, or feet.
- Do not give alcohol to warm up.
- Do not press direct heat onto the skin, such as hot water, heating pads, heat lamps, or space heaters.
- Do not delay calling 911 while trying home fixes if confusion is present.

## What to do now
1. **Get out of the cold immediately.** Move indoors or into the most sheltered warm place you can reach safely. Sit or lie down to reduce the chance of falling.
2. **Call 911 now if you are confused, very drowsy, hard to wake, clumsy, slurring speech, or getting worse.** Say: “Possible hypothermia after cold exposure, with uncontrollable shivering and confusion.”
3. **Get wet clothing off and get dry.** Remove wet layers, dry the skin if you can, put on dry layers, and cover the head and neck while leaving the face clear.
4. **Warm the body gently, starting with the center of the body.** Wrap in dry blankets, coats, towels, or sheets, focusing warmth on the chest, neck, head, and groin. Keep movement gentle.
5. **Use warm, dry compresses only if they are available and easy.** Put them on the neck, chest, or groin. Do not put heat directly on arms or legs.
6. **If you are fully awake and can swallow safely,** sip a warm, sweet, non-alcoholic, noncaffeinated drink. Stop if you cough, choke, vomit, or become more drowsy.
7. **Check temperature only if it is easy and does not delay help.** A body temperature below 95°F (35°C) is an emergency, but confusion after cold exposure matters even without a thermometer reading.
8. **Have someone stay with you and watch your breathing and alertness.** Tell 911 dispatch if you become hard to wake, stop shivering and seem worse, or breathing becomes slow or shallow.
9. **If the person becomes unconscious or is not breathing normally,** start CPR if you can and follow 911 operator instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause before getting warm and getting help.
- You do not need to decide whether this is hypothermia, exhaustion, dehydration, low blood sugar, or something else right now.
- You do not need to finish warming up before calling 911 if confusion or severe drowsiness is present.
- You do not need to decide urgent care versus the emergency room right now; 911 can help triage severe symptoms.

## Important reassurance
Cold can make thinking and movement worse, so people may underestimate how serious the situation is. Treating confusion after cold exposure as urgent is a reasonable first step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the immediate situation. After you are safe, emergency responders or a clinician may need to check for hypothermia, frostbite, heart rhythm problems, injury from falls, and other conditions that can look similar or make cold exposure more dangerous.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for emergency care. If there is confusion, severe drowsiness, trouble breathing, loss of consciousness, or worsening symptoms after cold exposure, call 911.
