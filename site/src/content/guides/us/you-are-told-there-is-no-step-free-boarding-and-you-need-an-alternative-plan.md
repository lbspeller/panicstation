# What to do if… you are told there is no step-free boarding and you need an alternative plan (USA)

## Short answer
Do not attempt boarding that feels unsafe. Ask staff what accessible alternative they can arrange now, such as another accessible boarding point, another accessible route, or an accessible ride option, and keep a brief record of what happened.

## Do not do these things
- Do not let anyone pressure you into using stairs, being carried, or making a transfer that does not feel safe.
- Do not assume the first person you speak to is the only person who can help. Ask who can arrange or escalate an accessible alternative.
- Do not separate from your mobility device unless you choose to and it is safe for you.
- Do not remain near platform edges or crowd flow while arrangements are being made.
- Do not leave without asking how staff will contact you if you need to move to a safer or more comfortable place.
- Do not delete texts, emails, app messages, or alerts about your trip.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause point.** Step back from platform edges, traffic, stairs, and crowd flow. Ask for a seated or sheltered place if you need one.
2. **Ask who can arrange an accessible alternative.**
   - For local transit, ask station staff, the operator, or a customer-help point where available. Ask whether a supervisor or dispatcher can help.
   - For intercity rail, ask station staff or onboard crew where available.
3. **Use clear wording:** “I cannot board safely without step-free access. What accessible option can you arrange now? Could I use another accessible boarding point, another accessible route, or an accessible ride option?”
4. **If this is an Amtrak trip, contact Amtrak as well:**
   - call **1-800-USA-RAIL (1-800-872-7245)** and ask for accessible boarding or station assistance for your reservation; or
   - ask station staff for help contacting Amtrak if you cannot call.
5. **For local bus or rail transit, ask about concrete options:**
   - the next accessible station or stop and how you can reach it accessibly;
   - another route that is accessible now;
   - an accessible shuttle, on-demand option, ride arrangement, or taxi voucher if the agency offers one; or
   - when the next accessible vehicle is due and whether alternative transportation can be arranged if a bus lift or ramp is not working.
6. **For local fixed-route transit, if you are already eligible for ADA complementary paratransit, ask whether it can help with this trip.** Same-day service is not required, so ask what accessible option can be arranged now.
7. **Ask what to do about your fare or ticket.** Ask whether you need a transfer, a ticket change, or anything to avoid an unnecessary second charge.
8. **Make a brief record while it is fresh:**
   - station or stop name;
   - time;
   - route or train number;
   - what failed, such as an elevator, ramp, lift, or accessible platform route;
   - who you spoke to, if known; and
   - screenshots of relevant alerts.
9. **Say plainly if your health or safety is becoming urgent.** Mention needs such as pain, heat, cold, medication timing, or access to a restroom. If you need urgent medical help, call **911**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal complaint.
- You do not need to debate detailed policy wording at the station.
- You do not need to explain private medical information beyond what is needed for safe transport.
- You do not need to solve the rest of the journey until you have a safe next step.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel stressed or exposed when an accessible route fails. Refusing unsafe boarding is a reasonable choice. The immediate task is to reach a safer pause point and get a workable accessible next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you get unstuck and move safely. Later decisions about eligibility, complaints, refunds, or legal questions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. The option available immediately may vary by location, staffing, service type, and the cause of the access failure. Ask what accessible alternative can be arranged now.
