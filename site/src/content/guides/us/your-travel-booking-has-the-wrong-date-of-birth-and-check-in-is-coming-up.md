# What to do if… your travel booking has the wrong date of birth and check-in is coming up (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the airline or booking agent immediately to update the Secure Flight Passenger Data (SFPD) in your reservation. A date of birth mismatch can prevent the issuance of a boarding pass, disable TSA PreCheck, or lead to secondary security screening.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to fly without correcting the data—airlines are required by federal law to transmit accurate Secure Flight data to the TSA 72 hours before departure.
- Don’t book a second ticket for the same flight—duplicate bookings often trigger automatic cancellations by airline revenue management systems.
- Don’t assume a profile update on the airline’s website fixes an existing reservation—most systems lock the passenger data once a ticket is issued.
- Don’t wait until you arrive at the security checkpoint to address the error—TSA officers generally cannot edit airline reservation data at the gate.
- Don’t delete and re-add a Known Traveler Number (KTN) repeatedly—this can cause synchronization failures in the Secure Flight system.

## What to do now
1. **Contact the party that issued the ticket immediately.**  
   - If you booked directly with the airline, call their reservations desk or use their official chat tool.  
   - If you booked through a third-party site (OTA), you must contact that agent first; airlines often lack "ownership" of the record to change core passenger data on third-party tickets.
2. **Request an update to the "Secure Flight Passenger Data" (SFPD).**  
   **The agent must manually update the DOB field in the PNR (Passenger Name Record) to match your government-issued ID.**
   - Confirm if the ticket requires a "reissue" or just a "revalidation" after the change.  
   - Ask for a revised confirmation email showing the corrected data.
3. **Verify the age category status for the traveler.**  
   **If the corrected date of birth moves the passenger into a different age bracket (e.g., child to adult), a fare difference may be required.**
   - This mismatch is the primary reason for online check-in failures.  
   - Ensure the "Infant," "Child," or "Adult" designator matches the new DOB.
4. **Use the AskTSA service for real-time verification help.**  
   **If you are within 24 hours of travel and the airline is unresponsive, message the TSA directly for guidance on screening.**
   - Text "Travel" to 275-872 (AskTSA).  
   - Message @AskTSA on X (Twitter) or Facebook Messenger.
5. **Check your TSA PreCheck status if applicable.**  
   **A mismatch in DOB or Name will prevent the "TSA PRE" indicator from appearing on your boarding pass.**
   - Ask the agent to "refresh" the KTN link once the DOB is corrected.  
   - If the indicator still does not appear, verify your KTN details at the TSA website.
6. **Inquire about TSA ConfirmID if your ID is non-compliant.**  
   **Starting February 1, 2026, travelers with ID discrepancies or lack of REAL ID can use the TSA ConfirmID process.**
   - This involves a $45 fee and an alternative identity verification system.  
   - Ask the airline agent if your specific data mismatch qualifies for this protocol at the airport.
7. **Arrive at the airport at least three hours early.**  
   **If the correction was made within 72 hours of the flight, the electronic transmission to TSA may be delayed.**
   - Bring the physical ID that matches the corrected DOB.  
   - Go directly to the airline's full-service check-in counter (not a kiosk) to have an agent verify the SFPD transmission status.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Department of Transportation (DOT) until after your travel is complete.
- You do not need to determine who made the data entry error while you are still at the airport.
- You do not need to pursue refunds for "name change fees" or "correction fees" until you have reached your destination.

## Important reassurance
Date of birth errors are common typographical mistakes. Most airlines have internal "minor correction" policies that allow these updates without significant penalties, provided the passenger's name remains the same. Once the SFPD is synchronized, check-in usually proceeds normally.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for immediate travel. It does not cover legal disputes over fare differences, long-term redress applications, or international visa requirements that may be affected by data mismatches.

## Important note
Airlines and the TSA operate under strict federal security mandates (49 CFR Parts 1540 and 1560). While minor typos are often corrected for free, some carriers may charge administrative fees or require a ticket reissue. In 2026, the DOT has shifted toward a "compliance first" enforcement model, meaning immediate penalties against airlines for these errors are less common than in previous years.
