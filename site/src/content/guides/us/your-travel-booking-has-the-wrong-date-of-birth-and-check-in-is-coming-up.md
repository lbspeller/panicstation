# What to do if… your travel booking has the wrong date of birth and check-in is coming up (USA)

## Short answer
Try to get the date of birth corrected in the active reservation before you travel. Airlines collect date of birth for TSA Secure Flight screening, and a mismatch can also stop TSA PreCheck from appearing, so contact the airline or ticketing agent now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave it to chance if check-in is close; a date of birth mismatch can affect screening or check-in.
- Do not make a duplicate booking for the same passenger unless the airline tells you to.
- Do not change only your frequent-flyer profile and assume this trip changed too.
- Do not keep retrying online check-in if it is rejecting you; switch to the airline, ticketing agent, or airport counter plan.
- Do not cancel in panic before asking what correction or reissue options are available.

## What to do now
1. **Find who controls this reservation right now.**
   - If you booked direct, contact the airline.
   - If you booked through an online travel agency or travel agent, contact that agent and the airline. The agent may need to change or reissue a ticket they issued.

2. **Use the words “Secure Flight passenger data.”**
   Ask them to correct the date of birth in the reservation so it matches the government ID or passport you will use for travel. Ask whether the ticket needs to be reissued after the correction.

3. **Check whether the wrong date changes the passenger type.**
   If the mistake makes a passenger look like an infant, child, or adult when they are not, say that clearly. Ask whether the fare, ticket type, or seat assignment must be corrected before check-in.

4. **If TSA PreCheck is missing, check the exact details.**
   Ask the airline to confirm that your name, date of birth, and Known Traveler Number are entered exactly as they appear in your TSA PreCheck or trusted traveler enrollment.

5. **If they cannot fix it immediately, get the airport-counter plan.**
   Ask: “Can a ticket counter agent correct the date of birth for check-in, and what is the counter cut-off for this flight?” Arrive earlier than usual so there is time to reach the counter before security and boarding.

6. **Prepare a small proof pack.**
   Bring the physical ID or passport you will use at the airport. Keep your confirmation number, record locator, ticket number if you have one, and any message showing you reported the date of birth error. If you have TSA PreCheck, keep your Known Traveler Number details available.

7. **If travel day still goes wrong, document it for later.**
   Save names, times, screenshots, chat logs, and emails. After the urgent travel problem is over, complain to the airline or ticket agent first, then use the DOT consumer complaint route if the issue is not resolved.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to dispute charges, demand compensation, or make a regulator complaint.
- You do not need to prove who caused the error right now.
- You can deal with refunds, fees, complaints, and written follow-up after the immediate check-in problem is handled.

## Important reassurance
This is often an administrative data problem, not a sign that the whole trip is ruined. The useful next move is to get the active reservation checked by the party that can edit or reissue it.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate period before check-in and departure. Later decisions about fees, refunds, complaints, or consumer rights may need specialist help or the airline’s written policy.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional travel advice. Airline systems, fares, tickets, and agency bookings differ, so follow the instructions given for your specific reservation.
