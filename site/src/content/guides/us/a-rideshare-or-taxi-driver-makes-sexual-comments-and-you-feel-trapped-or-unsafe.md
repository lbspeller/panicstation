# What to do if… a rideshare or taxi driver makes sexual comments and you feel trapped or unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Your priority is to reach the earliest safe pause and move to a public place with other people. If you feel in immediate danger, call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel you have to confront the driver or challenge the comments while you are still in the vehicle.
- Do not feel you have to smile, laugh, or answer personal questions.
- Do not feel you have to agree to a detour, an extra stop, or a more private drop-off point.
- Avoid volunteering extra personal details, such as your living situation or whether anyone is expecting you, where possible.
- Do not wait for proof that the situation is serious enough before trying to reach a safer place.

## What to do now
1. **Ask to stop at a public place with other people.** Keep it simple if that feels safer: “Please stop at the next busy public place.” A staffed store, hotel entrance, or other visible public location may be suitable.
2. **Tell someone where you are.** If it is safe to use your phone, share your ride location or send a brief message such as: “I feel unsafe in this ride. I am trying to get out at a public place.”
3. **Use emergency help if you need it.** If you feel in immediate danger, call **911**. If you are using Uber or Lyft, open the in-app safety tools if they are available and it is safe to do so.
4. **Leave at the earliest safe opportunity.** Move toward staff, other people, or an open public business. Take your belongings only if doing so does not delay a safe exit.
5. **If you cannot safely leave, call for help.** Call **911** if you can. If you reach a staffed location, ask a staff member to call **911** if you need emergency assistance.
6. **Save basic trip details later, if you choose.** Once you are safe, you may save the trip receipt, driver name, vehicle details, time, and any in-app messages. Do not do this while it could increase risk.
7. **Reporting can wait until you are safe.** You may report through the rideshare app. For a taxi, you may check whether your area has a local taxi or for-hire vehicle authority. Reporting is your choice.

## What can wait
- You do **not** have to decide now whether to report to police, the rideshare company, a taxi authority, or anyone else.
- You do **not** need to write a detailed account while you are still shaken.
- You do **not** need to respond to follow-up messages right away.
- You do **not** need to decide what label to use for what happened.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, going quiet, laughing nervously, or trying to keep the situation calm can be automatic survival responses. Sexual comments can feel threatening when you are inside a moving vehicle or dependent on someone else to let you out. Wanting to leave is enough reason to focus on safety.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on reaching safety and getting stabilized. Later decisions about support or reporting may benefit from specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
