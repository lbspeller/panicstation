# What to do if… your wheelchair tyre goes flat or a wheel locks up while you are away from home (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safe location away from traffic immediately, then prioritize arranging accessible transport or specialized mobility roadside assistance rather than attempting repairs in an exposed area.

## Do not do these things
- Don't force the chair to roll on a flat tire or a locked wheel, as this can cause a tip-over or permanent frame damage.
- Don't attempt to disassemble axles or casters while on a sidewalk, roadway, or parking lot.
- Don't accept a lift from a non-accessible vehicle if you cannot safely transfer or if it requires leaving your mobility device behind.
- Don't toggle power chair freewheel levers if you are on any kind of slope or incline.
- Don't reach into the caster housing or motor area while the power is still on.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the nearest safe pause point.**
   - **Navigate to a level, indoor, or protected area** such as a building lobby, wide sidewalk, or bus stop pad.
   - **Call 911 immediately** if you are stuck in an active traffic lane or a dangerous intersection and cannot move to safety.

2. **Secure the device and assess the environment.**
   - **Engage wheel locks or brakes** if they are functional and the ground is level.
   - **Turn off power chair controllers** to prevent accidental movement during assessment.

3. **Identify the failure through observable cues.**
   - **Flat tire:** The tire appears squashed, the rim is touching the ground, or the chair pulls hard to one side.
   - **Mechanical jam:** Visible debris (wire, hair, trash) is caught in the caster fork or wrapped around the axle.
   - **Electronic failure:** The joystick flashes a specific sequence, the chair emits beep codes, or the "wrench" icon appears on the display.

4. **Perform a visual-only check for simple obstructions.**
   - **Check for large debris** like sticks or stones wedged between the tire and the frame.
   - **Verify that manual wheel locks** have not accidentally engaged or shifted into a partially locked position.
   - **Ensure power chair freewheel levers** are fully engaged in the "drive" position on both motors.

5. **Utilize transit or facility assistance if applicable.**
   - **Contact transit staff or the bus operator** if the failure occurs on or near public transportation; ADA rules require agencies to assist with boarding/disembarking and to provide alternative accessible transport if a vehicle feature fails.
   - **Ask facility security or guest services** to stay with you while you wait for transport if you are in a mall, hospital, or campus.

6. **Arrange specialized recovery or transport.**
   - **Call your mobility roadside assistance provider** if you have a membership (e.g., ProtectionWorks or similar services).
   - **Contact an accessible taxi or paratransit dispatch** and explicitly state that you have a disabled mobility device that cannot be folded or rolled easily.
   - **Phone your DME (Durable Medical Equipment) provider** to report the issue, though they may only be able to schedule a future repair rather than providing immediate roadside aid.

7. **Limit roadside "quick fixes" to air inflation only.**
   - **Inflate the tire only if you have a portable pump** and the tire is pneumatic (air-filled).
   - **Do not exceed the PSI/Bar rating** printed on the tire sidewall.
   - **Discontinue repair attempts** if the tire does not hold air or if the wheel remains locked after clearing visible surface debris.

## What can wait
- Finding the exact source of a puncture or identifying a specific electronic error code can wait until you are home.
- You do not need to contact insurance or Medicare for repair authorization while you are still stranded.
- Permanent repairs, such as inner tube replacement or motor servicing, should wait for a controlled environment.

## Important reassurance
A wheelchair breakdown in public is a high-stress event, and feeling vulnerable is a common reaction. Moving to a safe, stationary position is the most effective first step toward resolving the situation without injury.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and safety during a breakdown away from home. It does not provide instructions for technical repairs, tire patching, or electronic diagnostics.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization only and is not a substitute for professional mechanical repair or medical advice. If your breakdown occurs in a location that puts you at risk of traffic accidents or environmental exposure, contact emergency services (911). Wheelchair repairs should be performed by qualified technicians to ensure the structural integrity of the device.
