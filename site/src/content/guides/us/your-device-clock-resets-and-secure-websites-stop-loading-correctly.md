# What to do if… your device clock resets and secure websites stop loading correctly (USA)

## Short answer
Do not call the number or click any buttons in the window. Close the browser tab or force-quit the application immediately to stop the interaction.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t call the phone number, scan any QR codes, or click "Remove Virus" buttons.
- Don’t grant remote access to your device to anyone who claims they found a problem via a pop-up.
- Don’t provide credit card numbers, bank details, or buy gift cards for "support services."
- Don’t enter your login credentials or passwords into any window that appeared unexpectedly.
- Don’t ignore the pop-up by continuing to use the device without closing the affected page.

## What to do now
1. **Force-quit the browser or application immediately to remove the pop-up from your screen.** 
   - On Windows: press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc**, select your browser, and click **End Task**.
   - On Mac: press **Option + Command + Esc**, select your browser, and click **Force Quit**.
   - On iPhone/Android: swipe up or use the app switcher to **swipe the browser app away**; if it persists, **restart the device**.
2. **Clear your browser data to prevent the malicious page from reloading.** 
   - Reopen the browser but **do not restore previous sessions** if prompted.
   - Go to settings and select **Clear Browsing Data** (specifically history and cookies) for the last 24 hours.
   - Check your **Notifications** settings and remove permission for any unfamiliar websites.
3. **Isolate the device if you allowed remote access or shared sensitive information.** 
   - **Disconnect the device from Wi-Fi** and unplug any ethernet cables immediately.
   - Use a different, trusted device to **change your primary passwords**, starting with your email and banking accounts.
   - Contact your bank or credit card issuer using the official number on the back of your card to report a potential scam.
4. **Run a scan using your device's built-in security software.** 
   - Use Windows Security (Windows) or the built-in protection on your Mac/mobile device to perform a full system scan.
   - Follow the software's prompts to remove any identified threats or suspicious files.
5. **Report the incident to official United States authorities.** 
   - Report the scam to the **Federal Trade Commission (FTC)** at **ReportFraud.ftc.gov**.
   - If money was lost or identity information was shared, file a report with the **FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center** by typing **ic3.gov** directly into your browser.

## What can wait
- Determining exactly which website triggered the pop-up can wait until the device is secured.
- Purchasing new or additional antivirus subscriptions is not required if your built-in tools complete a clean scan.
- Reinstalling your entire operating system is usually unnecessary unless a professional confirms a deep compromise.

## Important reassurance
These pop-ups are designed to trigger a panic response through loud noises, flashing colors, and urgent threats. In most cases, the pop-up is simply a website that cannot see your files or "know" your device is infected unless you interact with it or grant it access.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for responding to fake technical support alerts. It does not cover long-term identity restoration or deep forensic cleaning of compromised systems.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or technical professional advice. If you have shared financial information or granted remote access, prioritize direct contact with your financial institutions and use official government reporting channels.
