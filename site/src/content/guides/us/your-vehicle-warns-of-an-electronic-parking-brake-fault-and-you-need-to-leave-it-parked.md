# What to do if… your vehicle warns of an electronic parking brake fault and you need to leave it parked (USA)

## Short answer
Do not leave the vehicle relying on the electronic parking brake alone. Secure it with Park or gear, turn the wheels correctly for the slope, use proper wheel chocks if available, and keep it attended while you call for help if you are not confident it will stay still.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume the electronic parking brake is holding just because a light is on or you hear a motor sound.
- Do not leave the vehicle unattended on a hill if you are not confident it cannot roll.
- Do not keep toggling the electronic parking brake to try to clear the warning while the vehicle is not fully secured.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle or attempt a manual release unless the vehicle is already safely secured and you are following the owner’s manual.
- Do not do any check that could let the vehicle roll toward people, traffic, or property.
- Do not let anyone else move the vehicle without knowing the parking brake warning is active.

## What to do now
1. **Keep your foot on the brake and move to level ground if you safely can.**  
   If the vehicle is driveable and you can move without adding danger, relocate to the flattest safe place nearby, such as a level part of a parking lot, driveway, or shoulder.

2. **Secure it with the transmission before you take your foot off the brake.**  
   - **Automatic:** keep your foot on the brake and shift fully into **P (Park)**.  
   - **Manual:** keep your foot on the brake and leave it in **1st gear** when facing uphill, or **reverse** when facing downhill.

3. **Turn the wheels for the slope before you leave the driver’s seat.**  
   - **Headed downhill:** turn the front wheels into the curb, or toward the side of the road.  
   - **Headed uphill with a curb:** turn the front wheels away from the curb, then let the vehicle settle gently so a front tire touches the curb.  
   - **No curb:** turn the wheels toward the side of the road so the vehicle would roll away from the center of the road, not into traffic.

4. **Use proper wheel chocks if you have them.**  
   Place chocks snugly against the tires on the downhill side. If you only have an improvised object and you are not sure it is stable, will not crush, and will not slide, do not rely on it as a reason to leave the vehicle unattended.

5. **Do a stillness check only if movement would not create danger.**  
   With Park or gear set, wheels turned, and any chocks placed, ease off the foot brake just enough to confirm the vehicle stays still. Press the brake again immediately if it moves, or skip this check if there is not clear safe space.

6. **If you cannot secure it, keep it attended and call for help.**  
   Call roadside assistance through your insurer, AAA, the vehicle manufacturer, or a towing service. Say: “Electronic parking brake fault; I am not confident the vehicle will hold when parked.” If the vehicle could roll into people, traffic, or property and you cannot make it safe, call **911**.

7. **If you must step away for safety, warn others not to move it.**  
   Leave a visible note that says: “Parking brake fault. Do not move until secured.” Do not leave keys where someone could move it unknowingly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact fault now.
- You do not need to reset the warning, disconnect the battery, or try hidden service procedures while the vehicle may roll.
- You can wait to choose a repair shop, check warranty coverage, or look up recalls once the vehicle is safely secured or attended.
- You can wait to decide whether to drive it again until you have checked the owner’s manual or spoken with roadside assistance, a dealer, or a qualified mechanic.

## Important reassurance
A parking brake fault warning is stressful because an unattended rollaway can hurt people or damage property. Using several layers of protection, Park or gear, wheel direction, chocks, and help if needed, is a practical way to lower the immediate risk.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent an unattended rollaway and buy time. Vehicle procedures vary by make, model, year, and transmission, so later decisions may need the owner’s manual, roadside assistance, a dealer, or a qualified mechanic.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, emergency, or professional advice. Follow your owner’s manual for your specific vehicle and get professional help if you cannot secure the vehicle confidently.
