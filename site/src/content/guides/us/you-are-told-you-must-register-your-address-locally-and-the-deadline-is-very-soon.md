# What to do if… you are told you must register your address locally and the deadline is very soon (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the requirement through official government channels immediately and complete the registration in person at a verified government office. Never provide documents or money to unofficial intermediaries or through unverified digital links.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hand your physical passport to anyone other than a uniformed official at a government office or a licensed hotel front desk.
- Do not pay "fines" or "expedited fees" via WhatsApp, cryptocurrency, gift cards, or private payment links.
- Do not provide sensitive data to unsolicited callers claiming to be from "immigration" or "police" without verifying their identity at a physical office.
- Do not ignore the deadline; official registration failures can lead to fines, detention, or future entry bans.
- Do not follow instructions from "agents" who offer to handle the process without you being present at the official bureau.

## What to do now
1. **Document every detail provided to you.**
   - Name and position of the person who gave the warning.
   - The specific office address and the name of the law or rule cited.
   - The exact deadline time and date.
2. **Verify the requirement via the US Department of State.** 
   - Search the "Country Information Page" for your current location on travel.state.gov.
   - Look under the "Local Laws & Special Circumstances" or "Entry, Exit and Visa Requirements" sections for mandatory police or municipal registration rules.
3. **Contact your accommodation management for proof of existing registration.**
   - Ask the hotel or registered host for a copy of the guest registration form they submitted to authorities.
   - Request this confirmation in writing or via a stamped receipt.
4. **Locate the nearest official registration office (e.g., Foreigner Police, Town Hall, or Immigration Bureau).**
   - Confirm the office location using a .gov or official municipal website for that country.
   - Prepare physical copies of your passport, entry stamp, and proof of lodging.
5. **Visit the office in person if the deadline is under 72 hours.**
   - Request an official receipt or a stamped document as proof of your visit or completed registration.
   - Take a clear photograph of any document you receive and upload it to a secure cloud service.
6. **Enroll in the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP).**
   - Use step.state.gov to register your trip; this allows the local US Embassy to send you official alerts if registration rules change or if scams are circulating in the area.
7. **Call for US Consular assistance if you are being coerced or detained.**
   - Contact the nearest US Embassy or Consulate directly.
   - If unreachable, call the State Department’s emergency line: 1-888-407-4747 (from US/Canada) or +1-202-501-4444 (from abroad).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on long-term visa extensions or residency changes today.
- You do not need to pay a third-party lawyer or consultant until you have verified the requirement at the official office.
- You do not need to update your US-based records or banks about a temporary local registration.

## Important reassurance
Mandatory local registration is a standard administrative requirement in many countries and is often resolved with a single office visit and no fee. Being asked to register is common; the primary danger is not the rule itself, but falling for unofficial "fast-track" scams that target travelers under time pressure.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and verification for US citizens abroad facing urgent local administrative deadlines. It does not provide legal advice for complex immigration status issues or long-term residency.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and may not reflect the specific laws of every jurisdiction. If you cannot verify the requirement quickly, prioritize your physical safety and contact the nearest US Embassy.
