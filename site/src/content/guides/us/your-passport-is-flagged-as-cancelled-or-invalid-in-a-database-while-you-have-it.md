# What to do if… your passport is flagged as cancelled or invalid in a database while you have it (USA)

## Short answer
Do not report the passport lost or stolen unless it truly is. Get the exact wording of the error, then contact official U.S. passport help: the National Passport Information Center if you are in the United States, or the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate if you are abroad.

## Do not do these things
- Do not report your passport lost or stolen just to “reset” the record. Once a valid U.S. passport is reported lost or stolen, it is cancelled and cannot be used for international travel even if you later find it.
- Do not keep re-trying check-in without asking what the system actually says.
- Do not book replacement flights, cancel the whole trip, or pay a third party for a passport appointment before you know the official reason for the flag.
- Do not post passport images online or send them to unofficial accounts.
- Do not hand your passport to anyone except airline staff handling your check-in, border officials, U.S. passport staff, or U.S. embassy or consulate staff.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the exact wording of the problem.**  
   Ask the airline, check-in desk, or border official: “Does it show cancelled, reported lost or stolen, revoked, invalid, expired, not found, or a mismatch?” Write down the wording, date, time, airport, airline, flight number, and the name or desk location if you can do that calmly.

2. **Check for a simple data mismatch before assuming the passport is cancelled.**  
   Ask staff to re-check:
   - the passport number, including O and 0, I and 1
   - your full name, date of birth, and passport expiry date
   - the document-issuing country
   - whether the booking name matches the passport
   - whether the machine-readable zone scan was clean

3. **If an airline is refusing boarding, ask for a supervisor and a document-check escalation.**  
   Say: “I have the passport with me. Please escalate the document validation result and tell me the exact result you are receiving.” For U.S.-bound travel, the airline may have its own route to consult CBP carrier support, but passengers usually deal with the airline supervisor and official passport or consular staff.

4. **If you are in the United States, contact the National Passport Information Center.**  
   Tell them you physically have the passport, but a travel-document check is showing it as cancelled, invalid, reported lost or stolen, not found, or mismatched. Give the exact wording and your travel date. Ask whether you need a passport agency or center appointment, a correction, or another official passport route.

5. **If you have urgent international travel, use the official passport agency or center appointment route.**  
   Passport agencies and centers handle urgent travel abroad in the next 14 days, or a foreign visa need in the next 28 days. Appointments are not guaranteed, so use the official route only and avoid anyone charging for appointments. Start gathering proof of travel, government photo ID, your passport, and your notes about the error.

6. **If there is a life-or-death emergency, say that clearly when contacting official passport help.**  
   Use the State Department’s life-or-death emergency route if the situation involves a qualifying emergency and international travel. Gather proof of the emergency and proof of travel, because official staff may ask for documentation.

7. **If you are abroad, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate immediately.**  
   Explain that you have your passport in hand, but an airline or border system says it is not valid. Ask what the fastest local solution is. Depending on the facts, consular staff may discuss a replacement passport, an emergency passport, or another official document option.

8. **Mention recent passport events briefly and factually.**  
   Tell official staff if you recently renewed, replaced, corrected, reported, found, or received a passport, or if an older passport was cancelled. Those details can help them understand why a record may be flagged.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain to the airline or seek reimbursement.
- You do not need to cancel other trips until official passport or consular staff explain what happened.
- You do not need to make a police report unless the passport was actually lost or stolen, or consular staff advise it for your specific situation.
- You do not need to solve every future travel document issue while you are still at the airport or under immediate travel pressure.

## Important reassurance
A cancelled or invalid result does not always mean you did something wrong. It may be a mismatch, a recent renewal or replacement issue, a lost or stolen passport record, or another administrative status problem. The safest first move is to slow the situation down, capture the exact error, and use official passport or consular channels.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilize the moment, avoid irreversible actions, and get official confirmation. Later decisions about complaints, refunds, replacement travel, or specialist help can wait until the passport status is clearer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice or professional travel advice. Passport decisions, airline boarding decisions, and border decisions can depend on the exact document, route, timing, and official records. If you are stranded, unwell, or in immediate danger, move to a safer place and contact local emergency help or U.S. consular help.
