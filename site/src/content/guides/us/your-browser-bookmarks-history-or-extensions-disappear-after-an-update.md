# What to do if… your browser bookmarks, history, or extensions disappear after an update (USA)

## Short answer
Refresh your network registration by toggling Airplane Mode for 30 seconds and check for carrier-level outages before modifying system settings.

## Do not do these things
- Do not perform a factory reset or "Erase All Content and Settings" as a first response.
- Do not share 2FA or verification codes with anyone claiming to help "test" your delayed SMS.
- Do not repeatedly attempt outbound calls if you have a weak signal, as this can drain battery and increase network congestion.
- Do not purchase third-party signal boosters or hardware without carrier-specific diagnostic confirmation.
- Do not ignore the issue if you rely on 2FA for banking, as delayed texts may indicate a wider OS or network routing failure.

## What to do now
1. **Establish an emergency communication fallback:**
   - If you are in immediate danger or have a medical emergency, dial **911** from any available device or landline.
   - Use a **landline** or **Wi-Fi Calling** (if enabled and your internet is stable) to contact your carrier or essential services.
2. **Force a network re-registration (The 30-Second Refresh):**
   - **Toggle Airplane Mode to ON for at least 30 seconds, then toggle it OFF.**
   - If service does not restore, **power the device completely off**, wait 20 seconds, and restart it.
3. **Isolate software-based call blocking:**
   - **Verify that "Do Not Disturb" or "Focus" modes are disabled** in your Control Center or Settings.
   - **Check for "Silence Unknown Callers"** (iOS) or "Block Unknown Numbers" (Android) and turn these features off temporarily.
   - **Disable Call Forwarding** by checking your phone settings or dialing the carrier reset code (e.g., `##004#` for T-Mobile).
4. **Isolate 2FA or automated text delays:**
   - **Determine if peer-to-peer texts are arriving** while automated "short-code" texts (e.g., from banks) are delayed.
   - **If only automated texts are delayed,** this may be a known OS routing bug (such as the iOS 26.2 regression) or a carrier SMS gateway delay rather than a device fault.
5. **Verify carrier status and account standing:**
   - **Dial 611** from your mobile device to reach your carrier's automated diagnostic line.
   - **Check for local outages** using your carrier’s official status page or a third-party monitor like Downdetector for your specific ZIP code.
6. **Physical and Virtual SIM maintenance:**
   - **For Physical SIMs:** Power off the phone, remove the SIM tray, inspect for damage, re-insert firmly, and power on.
   - **For eSIMs:** Navigate to Cellular/Mobile Data settings and toggle the "Turn On This Line" switch off and back on to force a fresh handshake with the tower.
7. **Bypass Wi-Fi interference:**
   - **Turn off Wi-Fi** temporarily to see if calls and texts resume over cellular data alone.
   - Some network configurations (specifically IPv6 on home routers) can intermittently block Verizon or AT&T Wi-Fi Calling packets, causing calls to drop or divert.
8. **Perform a Network Settings Reset (Final Local Step):**
   - **Reset network settings** via your device's System/General reset menu.
   - This will clear saved Wi-Fi passwords and VPN profiles but will not delete your photos or apps.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on switching carriers or buying a new device today.
- You do not need to troubleshoot advanced APN or VPN settings unless specifically directed by a carrier technician.
- You do not need to file a formal FCC complaint until you have attempted a SIM/eSIM refresh and have logged specific times when calls failed.

## Important reassurance
Inconsistent call and text delivery is frequently caused by a "stale" network connection or temporary carrier congestion. Most instances are resolved by a basic network refresh or by identifying a single active setting (like Focus mode) that is performing exactly as designed by diverting calls.

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate stabilization and restoring basic connectivity. It does not cover long-term hardware repair, billing disputes, or forensic analysis of intercepted communications.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute technical or legal advice. If you cannot reach emergency services via your mobile device, seek a landline or third-party assistance immediately. Carrier-specific USSD codes (like 611) are the most reliable way to confirm account status and local tower health.
