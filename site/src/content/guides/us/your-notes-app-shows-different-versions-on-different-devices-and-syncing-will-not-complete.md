# What to do if… your notes app shows different versions on different devices and syncing will not complete (USA)

## Short answer
Stop editing the note on all devices, then copy or export the most complete version before trying to fix sync. The priority is to preserve a version you control so troubleshooting does not overwrite it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep editing the same note on multiple devices while sync is failing.
- Don’t sign out of your cloud account, uninstall the app, close a notebook, remove an account, or reset a device until you have copied out any unsynced notes.
- Don’t delete the “wrong” version yet; it may be the only copy of some text.
- Don’t assume the cloud has the latest version until you have checked it.
- Don’t send private note contents to support unless it is necessary and you are comfortable doing so.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze changes.** Stop editing the affected note on every device. If you are worried another device may overwrite it, put all but one device into Airplane Mode or temporarily turn off Wi-Fi and cellular data.

2. **Pick one source device.** Use the device that shows the most complete or most important version. Do not try to merge versions yet.

3. **Create a safety copy before fixing anything.** On the source device:
   - Duplicate the affected note if the app allows it.
   - Copy the text into a brand-new note titled “BACKUP — 2026-07-01”.
   - If possible, export or share the note as a PDF, text file, or other local file you can open without the notes app.
   - If the note contains private information, store the backup somewhere private and secure.

4. **Capture any unique text from the other versions.** On each other device, look only for words, lists, images, or attachments that are missing from the source version. Copy anything unique into the backup note or into a separate local file. Do not edit the original note yet.

5. **Check that every device is using the same account and folder.**
   - Confirm the same Apple Account, Google Account, or Microsoft account is signed in on each device.
   - In the notes app, check for separate locations such as “On My iPhone”, “iCloud”, “Gmail”, “Outlook”, “OneDrive”, local notebooks, archived notes, Trash, or Recently Deleted.
   - Make sure you are comparing the same note in the same folder, not two similar notes in different accounts.

6. **Check the provider’s official status page.** If the note is backed by iCloud, OneDrive, OneNote, Google Keep, or Google account sync, check the provider’s official status page before changing settings. If there is an outage or service incident, keep your backup and avoid further edits until the service is stable.

7. **For Apple Notes, check the Notes account setting.** If your notes are in iCloud, check that iCloud Notes is enabled. If the note is under another account such as Gmail, Yahoo, or Outlook, check that Notes is enabled for that account.

8. **For Google Keep, check the Google account and sync setting.** Make sure you are signed in to the Google Account that owns the note. On Android, check that Keep sync is turned on for that account, and update Google Keep if an update is available.

9. **For OneNote, check sync status before closing anything.** If OneNote shows a warning triangle, sync error, or saved-offline message, write down the error code or take a screenshot. If a manual sync option is available, use it once and then wait on a stable connection.

10. **Use the web version as a neutral check, if available.** Sign in to the official web version, such as iCloud Notes, OneNote for the web, or Google Keep, using the same account. Check which version is stored in the cloud. If the web version has useful text, copy that text into your backup too.

11. **Do a low-risk refresh.** After your backup is safe, close and reopen the notes app, refresh the notes list if the app has that option, and wait a few minutes on a stable internet connection. Keep the app open and in the foreground if the app needs that to finish syncing.

12. **Restart the device that will not sync.** Restarting is a low-risk step after you have preserved the unsynced content. Reopen the notes app and wait for sync to retry.

13. **Bring devices back online one at a time.** Take one additional device out of Airplane Mode, open the notes app, and let it sit. If it shows different content, do not edit the original note; first copy any unique text into your backup, then let syncing attempt to finish.

14. **If sync is still stuck, contact official support.** Use the app provider’s official help route. Share the app name, account type, device models, operating system versions, app versions, error codes, screenshots of the sync problem, and when it started. Avoid sharing private note text unless support says it is necessary and you decide it is safe.

## What can wait
- You do not have to merge every version perfectly right now.
- You do not have to delete duplicates today.
- You do not have to switch notes apps or cloud services now.
- You do not need to reinstall the app, sign out everywhere, close notebooks, or reset a device as a first step.

## Important reassurance
This kind of “split version” problem can happen when a device was offline, a service was unavailable, or an app could not finish syncing. Preserving the text first gives you a safer position before you troubleshoot.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent note loss and stabilize one reliable copy. Later decisions, such as account recovery, workplace data handling, device repair, or restoring from backups, may need help from the app provider, your workplace or school IT team, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, technical, or other professional advice. Steps vary by app, account type, device, and operating system version. Use official support channels for account-specific instructions.
