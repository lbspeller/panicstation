# What to do if… a stranger will not leave you alone after you try to end the conversation (USA)

## Short answer
Move toward other people or staff and create distance. Call 911 if you need immediate police assistance or fear immediate harm.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay in place only because leaving feels awkward.
- Do not keep arguing, explaining, or answering personal questions.
- Do not share your home area, destination, phone number, or social media details.
- Do not go to another location with the person.
- Avoid isolated or enclosed areas, such as stairwells, elevators, or parking structures, when another route is available.
- Do not take out your wallet or unlock your phone to show the person something.
- Do not head directly home or to an isolated car if you can first move through a staffed or busy area.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** Move into a staffed business, toward security, or toward other people. Use a counter or another available barrier to create distance when possible.
2. **Use a brief boundary only if it feels safe.** Say, “I do not want to talk. Please stop.” Then move away. Skip this step if speaking again feels unsafe.
3. **Ask for help directly.** Tell staff, “Someone will not leave me alone. Can I stay here while I get help?” Ask security to assist if security is available.
4. **Share your current location when safe.** Tell staff, call a trusted contact, or call 911 if you need immediate police assistance. Give the business name, cross street, or nearby landmark without announcing where you plan to go next.
5. **Treat immediate danger as an emergency.** Call 911 if the person blocks your path, touches you, threatens you, displays a weapon, becomes aggressive, or you fear immediate harm. State your location and what is happening.
6. **Consider staying in the staffed area while you decide how to continue safely.** Ask staff or security whether they can help you leave safely, such as by identifying a busier exit or arranging available assistance.
7. **Make brief notes only after reaching a safer pause.** Record the time, place, description, and what happened if doing so does not delay safety or support.
8. **Use local victim-support resources if the behavior repeats or escalates.** The Office for Victims of Crime provides support routes and a directory for finding local services.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether the interaction was serious enough to report.
- Posting online or trying to identify the person.
- Making long-term security changes.
- Writing a detailed account.
- Deciding whether the behavior has a particular label.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to end an interaction and move away. Hesitating or second-guessing yourself does not mean your concern is unimportant.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation. If the behavior repeats, escalates, or turns into ongoing following or monitoring, later decisions may need specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you need immediate emergency assistance.
