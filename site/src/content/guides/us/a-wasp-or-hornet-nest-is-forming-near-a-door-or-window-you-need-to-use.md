# What to do if… a wasp or hornet nest is forming near a door or window you need to use (USA)

## Short answer
Use another entrance if possible, keep people and pets away, and contact a pest management professional or your property manager. If someone has signs of a severe allergic reaction after a sting, use prescribed epinephrine as directed and then call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hit, disturb, probe, or block the nest or its active entrance.
- Do not seal an active gap in siding, a wall, a vent, or a door or window frame. Insects may seek another exit, including into the home.
- Do not use gasoline, fire, or smoke.
- Do not stand in the insects’ flight path or remain close to the nest to investigate.
- Do not let children or pets approach the area.
- Do not use a pesticide unless its label permits the intended use and you can follow the label directions safely.

## What to do now
1. **Switch entrances if possible.** Keep the affected window shut. Ask other people in the home or building to use a different route.
2. **Warn other people.** Put a visible note on the inside of the affected door and tell other occupants not to use it.
3. **Observe only from a safe distance.** Note whether insects are repeatedly entering and leaving one gap, such as a siding crack, soffit, vent, or door or window frame. Do not approach, probe, or block the gap.
4. **Arrange the right help.**
   - **Renting, apartment, or condo:** notify the landlord, property manager, or HOA promptly.
   - **Homeowner:** contact a pest management professional. Ask whether the person doing the work is appropriately certified or licensed under state rules.
   - **Nest inside a wall, attic, vent, or other concealed space:** use professional help rather than trying to treat or seal the opening yourself.
   - **Unsure where to start:** a local Cooperative Extension office can provide local pest guidance.
5. **Do not use the affected doorway while insects are active around it.** If there is an immediate safety emergency and no safe exit, call 911.
6. **Treat a severe sting reaction as an emergency.** Use prescribed epinephrine as directed and then call 911 for trouble breathing, throat or tongue swelling, difficulty swallowing, severe dizziness, fainting, or widespread hives with vomiting.
7. **Use a non-emergency advice route when needed.** For a non-emergency concern after a sting, contact a healthcare provider or Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222. Call Poison Control for questions about a pesticide exposure that is not an immediate emergency.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact insect during the immediate pause. Before treatment, ask the professional to identify what is nesting there.
- You do not need to attempt pesticide treatment now.
- Sealing gaps, repairing screens, and making longer-term changes can wait until the nest has been treated or is confirmed inactive.

## Important reassurance
A nest beside a needed entrance can make ordinary movement feel difficult. Creating distance, using another route where possible, and arranging the right help are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The right treatment depends on the insect, the nest location, and local requirements. Later decisions may need help from a pest management professional, a property manager, or a local Cooperative Extension office.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, pest-control, or other professional advice. Call 911 for a suspected severe allergic reaction.
