# What to do if… your employer demands work authorisation documents urgently or threatens to remove your shifts (USA)

## Short answer
Reply promptly in writing. Ask whether the request is for Form I-9 completion, a correction, or reverification, and ask for the employer’s approved secure process before sending sensitive documents.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the message unanswered. Send a brief written acknowledgement while you check the request.
- Do not send identity documents to a personal email address, text number, or link that you cannot verify through an official employer channel.
- Do not provide extra Form I-9 documents just to be safe. You can generally choose acceptable document or documents from the applicable Lists of Acceptable Documents.
- Do not leave original documents behind after an in-person inspection. Ask when they will be returned if anyone asks to retain them.
- Do not pay anyone who claims they can expedite verification or fix your work authorization.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the sender and the purpose of the request.**  
   Contact HR through an official portal, an established company email address, or a phone number from existing onboarding paperwork. Ask: “Is this request for Form I-9 completion, a correction, or reverification? What is the approved secure way to present documents?”

2. **Ask for the Lists of Acceptable Documents and the deadline they are relying on.**  
   Write: “Please send the current Lists of Acceptable Documents and confirm the deadline and reason for the request.”

3. **Offer the earliest workable secure appointment.**  
   Ask where to present original acceptable documents for inspection. If an in-person appointment is difficult, ask whether the employer offers the DHS-authorized remote examination procedure and request the official instructions.

4. **Ask what any separate document request is for.**  
   If they ask for a Social Security card, passport scan, or other document outside the Form I-9 process, ask whether the request is for payroll, Form I-9, or another stated purpose. Use only the employer’s verified secure process.

5. **Respond calmly if they demand a specific Form I-9 document.**  
   Write: “Please send the current Lists of Acceptable Documents. I will present acceptable document or documents from the applicable lists through the approved process.”

6. **Ask for the basis of any reverification request.**  
   Write: “Please confirm why reverification is required and which record or document the request relates to.” Employers should not reverify U.S. citizens or noncitizen nationals. A Permanent Resident Card expiring by itself generally is not a reason to ask a current employee for new Form I-9 documents.

7. **If you cannot meet the requested timing, ask about the next available option.**  
   Write: “I can present acceptable documents at [time and location], or through your approved remote procedure if available. Is a short extension possible, and will my schedule be affected?”

8. **Keep a simple written record.**  
   Save the request, any statement about your schedule, the documents requested, the stated reason, and your replies offering a secure standard process.

9. **Get specialist guidance if the request still seems wrong.**  
   Consider contacting the U.S. Department of Justice Immigrant and Employee Rights Section if the employer refuses acceptable Form I-9 documents, demands extra or specific documents in a way that may relate to citizenship status, immigration status, or national origin, or pressures you after you raise the concern.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to quit, post publicly, or make legal threats.
- You do not need to send extra identity documents while you are checking the correct process.
- You do not need to explain more personal immigration information than the verified process requires.

## Important reassurance
You can handle this one written step at a time. It is reasonable to ask what the request is for, which secure process to use, and what deadline the employer is relying on.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from an employment or immigration specialist, especially if the employer refuses standard document options or keeps pressuring you after you ask for clarification.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Your documents, employment history, employer process, and timing may affect what happens next.
