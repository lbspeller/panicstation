# What to do if… your contacts list suddenly fills with unknown entries or random names (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter any personal passwords or one-time codes into the device screen or any links received via message. Secure your own primary accounts from a separate, trusted device and verify whether the lock is a legitimate security feature or a remote takeover attempt.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay for third-party “FRP bypass” tools or “iCloud unlocking” services; these are frequently scams that may install malware or steal payment data.
- Don’t attempt repeated factory resets or manual "tricks" found in older tutorials; modern firmware (Android 16+ and current iOS) has patched these, and repeated attempts can trigger longer lockout timers.
- Don’t click sign-in links in texts or emails claiming your device was "found" or "locked"; these are often phishing attempts designed to steal your credentials to unlock the device for a thief.
- Don’t share photos of your ID, original purchase receipts, or serial numbers with unverified sellers or individuals claiming they can "help" remotely.

## What to do now
1. **Document the exact message and all identifiers on the screen.** Take a clear photo of the lock screen showing the specific wording (e.g., "Activation Lock," "Managed by [Organization]," or "Device Protection"). Record the device's Serial Number or IMEI if it is displayed on the screen or the physical tray.

2. **Determine if the device is under Mobile Device Management (MDM).** Look for phrases like **"Managed by your organization"**, **"Company Portal"**, or **"Microsoft Intune"**.
   - If the device belongs to your employer or school, contact their IT help desk.
   - If you bought the device and do not recognize the organization, stop setup immediately and initiate a return through the sales platform.

3. **Contact the seller or platform immediately if the device was purchased used.** Request that the previous owner removes the device from their Find My or Google/Samsung account remotely. 
   - If the seller cannot or will not do this, request a refund through the platform (eBay, Swappa, Back Market) or your payment provider.
   - Decline requests to "just try another reset," as account-level locks cannot be cleared by local resets.

4. **Verify your own account security from a separate trusted computer.** Log in directly to your official Apple (appleid.apple.com), Google (myaccount.google.com), or Microsoft account pages.
   - Change your password if you suspect your credentials were used on the locked device.
   - Review "Trusted Devices" or "Logged-in Sessions" and remove any hardware you do not recognize.

5. **Follow official manufacturer removal protocols for legitimate ownership.** 
   - For Apple devices, use the official Activation Lock support request portal (al-support.apple.com) which requires an original receipt from an authorized reseller.
   - For Samsung devices, check for "Reactivation Lock" in your Samsung Account settings on a web browser to see if the device can be unlinked.

6. **Report suspicious "Device Found" or "Activation Required" messages.** Forward any suspicious text messages to **7726 (SPAM)** to alert your carrier.
   - Do not reply to the message or provide any information.
   - File a report at **ReportFraud.ftc.gov** if you believe you are being targeted by an activation-related scam.

7. **Isolate the device from your network if you suspect a remote takeover.** Turn off Wi-Fi on the device or power it down if you believe someone is currently attempting to access your data or accounts through the link.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to keep or recycle the device right now; keep it as evidence for any pending refund disputes.
- You do not need to contact local law enforcement unless you are in immediate physical danger or have proof the device is stolen property.
- You do not need to spend money on "professional" unlockers; wait for official manufacturer or platform responses.

## Important reassurance
Seeing an unknown account lock is a common occurrence with refurbished or second-hand electronics. These features are automated security protocols designed to protect user data and discourage theft. Following the official channels, while slower, is the only way to ensure the device is safely and permanently cleared.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and risk reduction. Final resolution may require several days for manufacturer verification or platform dispute processing.
