# What to do if… a new crack appears in a wall or ceiling and seems to be widening (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the affected area and keep other people and pets out. Leave the building and call 911 if material is falling, a ceiling is sagging or bulging, a wall is bowing or leaning, you hear shifting noises, or the structure appears at risk of collapse.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand, sit, or sleep beneath a cracked, sagging, or bulging ceiling.
- Do not keep using an affected room if the crack is widening, material is falling, or the wall or ceiling is changing shape.
- Do not cut into drywall, remove material, or pull on loose sections to investigate.
- Do not try to jack, brace, prop up, or remove structural parts yourself.
- Do not enter or re-enter a damaged area just to measure the crack or take a closer photo.
- Do not use electrical switches, doorbells, appliances, or phones inside if you smell gas or suspect a gas leak.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move people and pets out of the affected room. If you can leave the building safely without passing beneath damaged material, do so.
2. **Call 911 for immediate danger.** Call from a safe location if debris is falling, a ceiling is sagging or bulging, a wall is bowing or leaning, you hear shifting or unusual cracking noises, or you think part of the building may collapse.
3. **Treat a possible gas leak as an emergency.** If you smell gas or suspect a leak, leave immediately. Do not operate switches, doorbells, appliances, or phones inside. From outside, call 911 or your gas utility’s emergency number.
4. **Respond cautiously to water leakage.** If water is leaking near the crack, avoid the area. Shut off the main water supply only if the shutoff is safely accessible without entering the damaged area.
5. **Record what you can from a safe place.** Take a photo or video only if you can do so without approaching the crack. Note when you first noticed it and whether there was recent heavy rain, a storm, an earthquake, a plumbing leak, or nearby construction.
6. **Arrange a prompt on-site assessment.**
   - **If you rent:** notify your landlord or property manager immediately in writing and ask for an urgent inspection. If you believe the building may be unsafe and you cannot get a timely response, check your city or county building department or code-enforcement route.
   - **If you own:** contact a qualified building professional, such as a licensed structural engineer where appropriate, for a prompt assessment.
7. **Use the local non-emergency reporting route where appropriate.** City and county systems vary. Some cities use 311 for building-safety reports; otherwise, check your local building department or code-enforcement contact route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the cause is drywall movement, moisture, framing movement, or a foundation issue right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair company or sign a repair contract today.
- You do not need to settle responsibility, insurance questions, or long-term repair plans before the area has been assessed.

## Important reassurance
Cracks can have different causes, and a widening crack deserves prompt attention without assuming the worst. Keeping away from the affected area and arranging an assessment reduces avoidable exposure and gives the next professional clearer information.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from a qualified building professional, your landlord or property manager, your insurer, or your local building authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, engineering, insurance, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you think there is immediate danger, move to a safe location and call 911.
