# What to do if… someone you know is using intimate images to control or intimidate you (USA)

## Short answer
Stop communicating with the person immediately and contact a specialist support service to begin the image removal and safety planning process. Do not comply with demands or pay money, as this typically leads to increased threats.

## Do not do these things
- Avoid paying money, sending additional images, or complying with demands to "end it."
- Do not engage in arguments, pleading, or negotiations with the person making threats.
- Do not delete evidence, such as messages or call logs, even if they are distressing to look at.
- Do not view, save, or possess any images if the person shown is under 18 years old.
- Do not meet the person in an attempt to resolve the situation privately.
- Do not forward the images to others for any reason.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your immediate physical safety.**  
   **If you feel you are in immediate physical danger, call 911.** If the person has access to your location, move to a safer place and contact a trusted person to stay with you.

2. **Cease all contact with the person immediately.**  
   **Stop responding to messages, calls, or threats.** You do not need to explain why you are stopping contact. Mute the person or block them once you have secured the necessary information for a report.

3. **Secure evidence of the extortion or threats without keeping the images.**  
   **Save screenshots of the threats, demands, usernames, and payment requests.** 
   - Note the dates and times of the interactions.
   - Do not keep the actual intimate images on your device if it can be avoided.
   - Record the URLs of any sites or social media profiles where the person is active.

4. **Use "Take It Down" or "StopNCII" to proactively block image sharing.**  
   **Access the appropriate industry-standard tool to prevent your images from being uploaded or spread.**
   - If you (or the person in the image) are under 18: Use **Take It Down** (takeitdown.ncmec.org).
   - If you are 18 or older: Use **StopNCII.org** to generate "hashes" (digital fingerprints) that participating platforms use to block the images automatically.

5. **Utilize your 48-hour removal rights under the TAKE IT DOWN Act.**  
   **Submit a formal removal request to any platform where the content has been posted.** Under the 2026 federal law, interactive computer services are required to provide a notice-and-removal process and must act within 48 hours for non-consensual intimate imagery.

6. **Contact the CCRI Crisis Helpline for expert image-abuse assistance.**  
   **Reach out to the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative at 1-844-878-2274 or via their online portal.** They provide confidential support specifically for victims of non-consensual pornography and sextortion.

7. **Get confidential support from specialized national hotlines.**  
   **Connect with advocates who can help with safety planning and emotional stabilization.**
   - For sexual violence: Call **RAINN** at **800-656-HOPE (4673)** or text **HOPE** to **64673**.
   - For relationship abuse: Call the **National Domestic Violence Hotline** at **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)** or text **START** or **LOVEIS** to **22522**.

8. **Report the extortion to federal law enforcement.**  
   **File a report with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).** Visit **ic3.gov** to report financial extortion or threats involving digital images. If a minor is involved, also report to the **NCMEC CyberTipline** at **report.cybertip.org**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to file a police report or a civil lawsuit.
- You do not need to tell your family, employer, or friends about the situation until you are ready.
- You do not need to identify every single website where the image might exist in the first hour.

## Important reassurance
Intimidation through the use of intimate images is a form of abuse designed to make you feel isolated and powerless. The panic, shame, or "freeze" response you may be feeling is a natural reaction to this type of coercion. There are established legal and technical pathways to help you regain control, and you are not responsible for the actions of the person using these images against you.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions. It is focused on immediate safety and stopping the spread of images. Longer-term legal remedies and forensic recovery may require a dedicated advocate or legal counsel.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for image removal and reporting can vary by platform and state. If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services.
