# What to do if… someone demands live video or explicit content and refuses to accept “no” (USA)

## Short answer
End all contact immediately and do not send any images, videos, or money. Save evidence of the threats, then block the individual and use digital tools like StopNCII.org or Take It Down to protect your privacy.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send a "final" image or video to satisfy them; this usually results in more demands rather than ending the interaction.
- Don’t pay any money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency to stop the threat, as payments often lead to increased extortion.
- Don’t hire for-profit "reputation fixers" or "digital takedown services" that promise to delete content for a fee; these are frequently scams.
- Don’t delete the conversation or profile immediately before you have saved a record of the threats and the user’s account information.
- Don’t forward, download, or save any images or videos if the person involved is under 18, as this may violate laws regarding child sexual abuse material.

## What to do now
1. **Disengage from the interaction immediately without giving an explanation.** **Stop responding to messages and end any active video calls or voice chats to prevent further recording or manipulation.**
   - Do not send a "goodbye" or "leave me alone" message if they have already ignored your boundaries.
   - Close the app or website entirely to create distance.
2. **Secure your hardware to prevent unrequested access.** **Cover your camera lens with a physical barrier and revoke microphone or camera permissions for the app in your device settings.**
   - Check if you have any "always on" or background camera settings enabled.
   - Turn off your device if you suspect it has been compromised by a malicious link.
3. **Capture clear evidence of the coercion and the identity of the person.** **Take screenshots of the demands, the threats to share content, the usernames, profile URLs, and any payment instructions provided.**
   - Include the date and time in your captures if possible.
   - Save these files to a secure location that is not accessible to the person demanding content.
4. **Use reporting tools on the specific platform where the contact occurred.** **Report the account for harassment, sexual solicitation, or extortion before you block them to ensure the platform has a record.**
   - Use the "Report" function within the chat or on the user's profile page.
   - After reporting, use the "Block" function to prevent further messages.
5. **Protect your images from being shared using hashing technology.** **If you are 18 or older, visit StopNCII.org to create a digital fingerprint of your images that prevents them from being uploaded to participating platforms.**
   - If the images involve someone under 18, visit TakeItDown.NCMEC.org to use the minor-specific protection tool.
   - These tools do not "see" your images; they create a unique code that stops uploads.
6. **Contact a confidential advocate for guidance.** **Call or text RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800-656-HOPE or text "HOPE" to 64673 for 24/7 support.**
   - Advocates can help you assess your safety and explain your rights without judgment.
   - If you are under 18, tell a trusted adult or contact the Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline.
7. **Report the extortion to federal authorities.** **File a report with the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) at ic3.gov to document the incident for federal investigation.**
   - If you are in immediate physical danger or the person is threatening to visit your home, call 911 or contact local police directly.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide right now whether to pursue a full criminal investigation or prosecution.
- You do **not** need to tell friends, family, or your employer about the incident until you feel ready and supported.
- You do **not** need to delete your social media accounts entirely; you can simply set them to private or deactivate them temporarily.
- You can wait to perform a full security audit of your other online accounts until the immediate crisis has stabilized.

## Important reassurance
The fear and pressure you are experiencing are the intended results of coercion. It is normal to feel overwhelmed, trapped, or ashamed when someone refuses to respect your boundaries. These feelings do not mean you have done anything wrong, and specialized resources are available to help you regain control.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction measures only. It does not replace legal advice or the long-term support of a qualified advocate or mental health professional.
