# What to do if… your rental vehicle key, keycard, or fob stops working while you are away from the vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safe, well-lit location and contact the rental company’s roadside assistance department immediately. Do not attempt forced entry or hire an unauthorized locksmith, as this may void your contract or result in significant damage charges.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the vehicle unattended if you find that any door or the trunk is actually unlocked or unsecured.
- Don’t use metal tools, wire hangers, or physical force to attempt entry, as modern electronic latches and sensors are easily damaged.
- Don’t hire a private locksmith or tow service without explicit, documented authorization from the rental provider.
- Don’t continue to press buttons on the fob if you suspect signal tampering or observe suspicious individuals nearby.
- Don’t wander away from the vehicle's known location to "test the range," as this increases the risk of becoming disoriented or losing the vehicle entirely.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your immediate physical safety by moving to a secure area.** Find a staffed lobby, store, or well-lit public space before attempting to troubleshoot.
   - If you feel threatened or are in an active danger zone, call **911** immediately.
2. **Retrieve the authorized roadside assistance number from your rental documentation.** This is typically found on the physical rental agreement, the key tag, or within the "Help" section of the rental company's mobile app.
3. **Gather the necessary vehicle information before placing the call.**
   - Rental agreement or confirmation number.
   - Vehicle license plate and state.
   - Exact location, including street address and specific parking identifiers like level or section numbers.
4. **Perform basic, non-invasive checks on the access device.**
   - **Key Fob:** Check for visible damage, moisture, or buttons that are physically stuck in the depressed position.
   - **Keycard:** Ensure the card is dry and has not been stored directly against magnets or high-powered electronics.
   - **Digital Key:** Verify your phone’s Bluetooth and NFC (Near Field Communication) are enabled and that "Nearby Devices" permissions are allowed in the app settings.
5. **Contact the rental company and follow their specific escalation path.** They may attempt a remote unlock command, dispatch a technician, or authorize a local recovery service.
6. **Verify the vehicle's current security state only once you have a safe way to do so.** Check all door handles and the trunk to confirm if the vehicle is truly locked.
   - If any door is open, secure your valuables and remain with the vehicle (if safe) until assistance arrives or the company provides instructions.
7. **Document any authorization provided for outside services.** If told to use a local locksmith or tow, record the agent’s name, the time of the call, and the specific authorization or claim number provided.
8. **Maintain a record of the incident for potential reimbursement or insurance claims.** Take photos of the vehicle’s position and save screenshots of any digital key error messages or app failures.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine liability for the lockout or battery failure while you are still stranded.
- You don’t need to attempt to replace the fob battery yourself unless the rental company explicitly instructs you to do so.
- You don’t need to worry about long-term rental extensions or contract changes until the immediate access issue is resolved.

## Important reassurance
Electronic access failures due to dead batteries, signal interference, or software glitches are common occurrences in modern rental fleets. Following the rental company’s established roadside protocol is the standard method for resolving these issues without incurring personal financial liability for the repair.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate access crisis and does not cover long-term mechanical repairs, lost key replacement fees, or legal disputes regarding rental contracts.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Rental policies regarding roadside assistance and locksmith authorization vary by provider and state law. If you are in a life-threatening situation or an active security emergency, contact 911 immediately.
