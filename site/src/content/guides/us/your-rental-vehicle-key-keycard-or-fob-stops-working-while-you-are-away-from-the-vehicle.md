# What to do if… your rental vehicle key, keycard, or fob stops working while you are away from the vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, staffed place and contact the rental company through your rental agreement, key tag, or app. Do not force entry, arrange a locksmith, or arrange a tow unless the rental company or your contract authorizes it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pry a door or window, use improvised entry tools, or break glass.
- Don’t arrange a locksmith or tow before contacting the rental company unless there is an immediate safety emergency.
- Don’t keep walking farther away while testing the fob or app; stay oriented to the vehicle’s location.
- Don’t leave the vehicle unsecured if a door is open or the trunk opens unexpectedly.
- Don’t confront anyone if the area feels unsafe or someone appears to be watching your access attempt.
- Don’t assume the problem is only a dead battery; keycards, apps, account status, vehicle security systems, and location permissions can also affect access.

## What to do now
1. **Get yourself safe first.** Move to a staffed, well-lit place such as a store, hotel lobby, rental counter, or gas station. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.
2. **Fix the vehicle location in your notes.** Write down the address, parking level, row, space number, nearby landmark, and whether the vehicle is on the street, in a garage, or in a gated lot.
3. **Find the rental company’s official help route.** Check the rental agreement, confirmation email, key sleeve, key tag, or rental app for roadside assistance, emergency roadside service, help, or support.
4. **Call or message that official route before arranging outside help.** Say that you are away from the vehicle and the key, keycard, fob, or digital key has stopped working. Ask what the authorized next step is for that contract and vehicle.
5. **Have the key facts ready.** Give the agent your name, rental agreement or reservation number, exact vehicle location, license plate if you can see it, vehicle make and model if known, and whether the problem is unlocking, locking, starting, app access, or a keycard reader.
6. **Try only low-risk checks while you are safe.** Check whether a fob button is stuck, wipe a wet keycard dry, move the keycard away from magnets or other cards, confirm your phone has signal, and check whether the rental app is signed in. Do not dismantle the fob or try to reprogram anything.
7. **Return to the vehicle only if it is safe to verify.** If the area feels unsafe, ask security, staff, or the rental agent for help before going back.
8. **At the vehicle, confirm the exact access problem.** Try the normal unlock method once or twice, check whether another door or the trunk is actually open, and tell the rental company exactly what happened. If anything is open, secure belongings as best you can and report that immediately.
9. **Follow the rental company’s authorized plan.** They may direct roadside assistance, a replacement key or fob, a branch visit, a remote option if available, a locksmith, or recovery of the vehicle. Ask for a case number, text confirmation, email confirmation, or the agent’s name if outside help is authorized.
10. **Keep a simple record.** Save screenshots of app errors, take a photo of the parked vehicle or location marker if safe, keep receipts, and note the time of each call or message.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now who will pay for the key, locksmith, tow, or service call.
- You do not need to argue policy at the curb; first get the vehicle secure and the next step authorized.
- You do not need to diagnose the fob, keycard, app, or vehicle security system yourself.
- You do not need to continue troubleshooting if the place feels unsafe.

## Important reassurance
A failed rental key, fob, keycard, or app access problem is stressful, but it is a known roadside-assistance situation for many rental companies. The safest move is to keep yourself safe, avoid causing damage, and get the rental company’s instructions recorded before anyone touches the vehicle.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the moment, avoid avoidable damage, and get an authorized plan. Later decisions about charges, coverage, reimbursement, or a replacement vehicle may need the rental company, your card issuer, insurer, roadside membership, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, mechanical, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
