# What to do if… someone is trying to isolate you from friends or coworkers during a night out and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a crowded, staffed area and inform an employee that you feel unsafe and need assistance reconnecting with your group or leaving. If you are being physically blocked or followed, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a quieter area (outside, parking lot, hallway, or a vehicle) to "finish the conversation" or resolve the tension.
- Do not prioritize politeness or social etiquette over your physical safety.
- Do not allow the person to hold your phone, bag, or drink, or "assist" you with transportation.
- Do not accept a ride from them or an "unlicensed taxi" they offer to call for you.
- Do not send photos, videos, or emojis if you are using text-to-911, as these may prevent the message from being delivered.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed position immediately.** Walk directly to the bar, the host stand, or a security guard (bouncer). **If you cannot find staff, stand in the center of a large crowd or directly under a security camera.**
2. **Use a clear signal or direct language with staff.** Tell the employee: "I do not feel safe. Please stay with me while I find my friends / get a ride." **In many US venues, you can ask "Is Angela working?" as a discreet code for help.** 
3. **Contact your group through a digital or verbal "flare."** Send your exact location to a trusted contact or coworker with the message: "I feel unsafe. Come to [specific spot in venue] now." **If your phone is dead, ask the staff to help you reach your group or use their communication system to locate your party.**
4. **Establish a safe exit or waiting zone.** Ask security to let you wait in a staff-only area or a well-lit, monitored spot until your transportation arrives. **Request that a staff member or security officer escort you directly to your vehicle or rideshare.**
5. **Call 911 if you are being followed or prevented from leaving.** If the person is blocking your path, following you to the exit, or acting aggressively, dial **911** immediately. **State your exact location and the specific behavior of the person (e.g., "I am at [Bar Name] and a stranger is blocking me from leaving").** 
6. **Use text-to-911 only if voice calling is unsafe.** If you cannot speak safely, send a text to **911** containing your location and the nature of the emergency. **If you receive a "bounce-back" message stating service is unavailable, you must find a way to make a voice call or have staff call for you.**
7. **Address sudden physical symptoms or intoxication.** If you feel unusually dizzy, ill, or "out of it" after a small amount of alcohol, tell a staff member immediately. **Do not go to the restroom or outside to "get air" alone; stay in a public, staffed area until medical help or your friends arrive.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove that the person’s behavior was "illegal" before asking for help.
- You do not need to explain the full history of the interaction to the staff right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report in this moment.

## Important reassurance
It is a common tactic for people to use social pressure or "friendliness" to isolate someone. Moving to a staffed area or asking for help is a standard safety procedure, and venue employees are trained to handle these requests without making a scene.

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate physical stabilization and the first steps to regain safety during a night out. It does not cover long-term reporting or legal actions.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal or professional advice. Always prioritize reaching a staffed, public environment and contacting emergency services if you feel threatened.
