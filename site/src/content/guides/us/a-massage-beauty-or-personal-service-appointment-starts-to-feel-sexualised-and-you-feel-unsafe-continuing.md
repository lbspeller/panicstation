# What to do if… a massage, beauty, or personal service appointment starts to feel sexualised and you feel unsafe continuing (USA)

## Short answer
End the appointment immediately and move to a public area or exit the building. If you are being prevented from leaving or feel in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don't stay to avoid awkwardness or because the service is already paid for.
- Don't feel obligated to explain your reasons or engage in a debate while in the treatment room.
- Don't worry about being "polite" if your boundaries are being ignored.
- Don't accept a refund or "future credit" as a condition for not reporting the incident.
- Don't wash any clothing you were wearing if you think you might want a forensic exam later.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the service immediately with a clear verbal instruction.**  
   **Say "Stop," "I am done," or "Hands off" and move your body away from the provider.** Sit up or stand up immediately to break the physical contact.

2. **Move to a safer location outside of the private room.**  
   **Get to the lobby, front desk, or your vehicle as quickly as possible.** If you are undressed, grab your clothes or a towel and dress in a bathroom or a public area where others are present.

3. **Contact emergency services if you are blocked from leaving.**  
   **Call 911 if the provider attempts to lock the door, touch you further, or prevent your exit.** You do not need to wait for a physical struggle to call for help.

4. **Bring in a third party for support.**  
   **Alert the front desk staff or manager that you felt unsafe and are leaving.** If you are alone, call a trusted person and ask them to stay on the line or meet you at the location.

5. **Access confidential support through a national hotline.**  
   **Contact the RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800-656-HOPE (4673) or use the online chat at rainn.org.** You can also text "HOPE" to 64673 for confidential assistance from trained staff.

6. **Seek a forensic medical exam if physical contact occurred.**  
   **Go to an Emergency Room and ask for a SANE (Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner).** These exams are typically free in the USA and do not require you to file a police report immediately, but they preserve evidence if you decide to report later.

7. **Secure physical evidence in a paper bag.**  
   **Place any clothing or linens involved in the incident into a clean paper bag if you are keeping them at home.** Avoid plastic bags, as they can trap moisture and degrade biological evidence.

8. **Identify the correct regulatory body for reporting.**  
   **Look up your state’s Massage Therapy Board or Department of Health to file a formal complaint.** Note that Kansas, Minnesota, and Wyoming do not have state-level massage therapy boards, so complaints in those states may need to go through local law enforcement or consumer protection agencies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to press criminal charges.
- You do not need to notify the business owner or corporate office immediately.
- You do not need to have a perfect memory of the timeline to seek medical care or support.
- You do not need to label what happened as "assault" to receive help from a hotline.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frozen, confused, or to "go along" with a service even when it feels wrong. This is a common stress response and does not mean you consented to inappropriate behavior. You have the absolute right to stop any professional service at any point if you feel uncomfortable.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute situation involving professional misconduct or sexualized behavior. It does not cover long-term legal strategies or civil lawsuits.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance only and is not legal or medical advice. Procedures for reporting misconduct vary significantly by state. If you are in immediate danger, contact 911.
