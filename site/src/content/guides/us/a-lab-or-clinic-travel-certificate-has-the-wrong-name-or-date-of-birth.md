# What to do if… a lab or clinic travel certificate has the wrong name or date of birth (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the issuing lab or clinic now and ask for an urgent issuer-generated corrected reissue. Do not edit the certificate yourself or assume the mismatch will be accepted for travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not alter the certificate in an editor, cross out details, or add handwritten changes yourself.
- Do not assume a carrier or destination authority will accept a name or date-of-birth mismatch.
- Do not send your passport or health document to unverified email addresses or random contacts.
- Do not share more identity information than the issuer asks for.
- Do not cancel or rebook solely because of the mismatch before checking the fastest valid correction path and any time-sensitive booking conditions.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the certificate with the identity document you will use for travel.** Note the exact wrong field and the correct spelling or date of birth.
2. **Contact the issuer through an official route.** Use the phone number, portal, or website listed by the lab or clinic. Ask for an urgent corrected reissue and tell them when you are due to travel.
3. **Ask who handles urgent document corrections.** If the first person cannot help, ask for the team or supervisor who can regenerate the certificate.
4. **Check the corrected version carefully.** Confirm the name, date of birth, certificate type, and any QR code, reference number, stamp, or portal verification before relying on it.
5. **Share identity evidence only if the issuer asks for it.** Use a verified secure upload route or another official method, and send only the minimum information needed.
6. **Check the travel requirements separately.** Ask your airline or other carrier about its check-in requirements. For international travel, review official destination information and check the destination country’s embassy or consulate guidance for current entry requirements.
7. **If the error is also in your medical or billing record, ask how to request a correction or amendment to that record.** This is separate from the urgent certificate reissue and may not be fast enough for your departure.
8. **If the issuer cannot reissue the certificate in time, ask what valid replacement path applies before booking anything new.** For a yellow fever International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis, contact the original vaccination provider first. If that provider is unavailable, ask the state health department whether it keeps the record. A yellow fever vaccination provider with the required stamp may be able to reissue the certificate after verifying the vaccination details.
9. **Save the documents securely.** Keep the original certificate, the corrected version, and any written message confirming the reissue.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to file a complaint, dispute a charge, or seek reimbursement.
- You do not need to work out who caused the error before asking for a corrected certificate.
- You do not need to contact multiple agencies at once. Start with the issuer, then check the carrier and official destination guidance.

## Important reassurance
A mismatch is a document problem to resolve with the issuer. You do not need to solve every possible later consequence at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize an urgent travel-document problem. Later decisions may need help from the issuer, your travel provider, your insurer, or a relevant specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Requirements vary by destination, carrier, and certificate type.
