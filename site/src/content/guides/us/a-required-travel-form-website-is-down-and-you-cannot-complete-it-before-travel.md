# What to do if… a required travel form website is down and you cannot complete it before travel (USA)

## Short answer
Document the technical failure immediately with screenshots and switch to official mobile app alternatives or the CBP Traveler Communications Center (TCC) for manual verification.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use unofficial third-party "expedited" websites that charge extra fees, as these are frequently scams and do not bypass technical outages.
- Do not repeatedly resubmit payments if the screen freezes, as this can trigger fraud alerts or multiple non-refundable charges.
- Do not assume that arriving at the airport early will resolve a missing authorization; airlines are legally barred from boarding passengers without verified electronic status.
- Do not use a VPN or public Wi-Fi to submit sensitive data if the site is failing, as these can trigger security blocks on government servers.
- Do not ignore "Scheduled Maintenance" banners; if a window is provided for the site to return, wait until that exact time.

## What to do now
1. **Capture visual evidence of the site failure or error message.** **Take a full-screen screenshot that includes the URL bar, the specific error code (e.g., 500 Internal Server Error or 403 Forbidden), and the system clock showing the date and time.**
2. **Attempt submission via the official mobile application.** **Download the "CBP One" or "ESTA Mobile" app from an official app store to determine if the mobile server is operational while the web interface is down.**
3. **Check the official CBP Alerts and Announcements page for known outages.** **Verify if the system is undergoing unscheduled maintenance by checking help.cbp.gov or the official @CBP X (formerly Twitter) account for status updates.**
4. **Execute limited technical troubleshooting for exactly 10 minutes.** **Clear your browser cache, switch to a different browser engine (e.g., Firefox to Chrome), or attempt the submission using a cellular data hotspot instead of home/airport Wi-Fi.**
5. **Contact the CBP Traveler Communications Center (TCC) for technical escalation.** **Call 1-877-227-5511 (within the US) or +1-202-325-8000 (International) and select the option for "Technical Difficulties" to request a manual status check or report the outage.**
   - Note: The ESTA technical line is generally available 24/7 except on Federal Holidays.
6. **Notify your airline's check-in or customer service desk immediately.** **Present your screenshots of the site failure and ask if they have received an "outage waiver" or "manual verification" notice from CBP for your specific flight or route.**
7. **Request a "Document Verification" note on your booking if boarding is denied.** **Ask the airline agent to record that the failure was due to a government system outage rather than a personal document error to assist with future insurance or refund claims.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a formal Visa (such as a B1/B2) immediately, as these appointments take weeks and will not resolve a short-term website outage.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or "Redress" application (DHS TRIP) while the outage is active.
- You do not need to decide on trip cancellation or insurance claims until you have confirmed with the airline that no manual override is possible.

## Important reassurance
Website outages on high-traffic government portals are recognized technical events. CBP and airlines have established protocols for managing systemic failures, though these can take time to propagate to airport gates. Documenting your attempt to comply is your primary protection against being blamed for the missing document.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for technical failures of official US travel systems (ESTA, I-94, EVUS). It does not provide legal advice for visa denials, criminal inadmissibility, or long-term immigration issues.

## Important note
Official travel requirements are subject to change without notice. Airlines have the final authority to deny boarding if they cannot verify a passenger's legal right to enter the destination country. Technical failure of a website does not automatically grant a right of entry or a waiver of requirements.
