# What to do if… your blood sugar reading is suddenly very high and you feel sick (USA)

## Short answer
Check for emergency signs first. If you are vomiting, breathing fast or deeply, confused, very sleepy, severely dehydrated, have fruity breath, have high ketones, or your blood sugar stays at 300 mg/dL or higher, call 911 or go to the ER now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to “work it off” with exercise if you feel sick or ketones might be present.
- Do not take a guessed extra insulin dose if you do not have a clinician-written correction or sick-day plan.
- Do not stop insulin, especially long-acting or basal insulin, unless a clinician specifically tells you to.
- Do not ignore vomiting, severe belly pain, severe drowsiness, confusion, fruity breath, or deep or rapid breathing.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel weak, confused, very sleepy, faint, or your vision is blurred.
- Do not rely only on a CGM reading if it does not match how you feel; confirm with a fingerstick if you can.
- If you take an SGLT2 inhibitor and have nausea, vomiting, belly pain, trouble breathing, unusual sleepiness, or confusion, do not wait it out at home.

## What to do now
1. **Check for “call 911 now” signs.** Call **911** or go to the **ER** now if any of these apply:
   - You are repeatedly vomiting or cannot keep fluids down.
   - You have severe belly pain, deep or rapid breathing, fruity breath, confusion, fainting, extreme sleepiness, or signs of severe dehydration.
   - Your blood sugar stays at **300 mg/dL or higher**.
   - You have high ketones, or you have ketones and feel very unwell.
   - You cannot test ketones and you are getting worse.
2. **Re-check the reading if you can do it without delaying urgent help.** Wash and dry your hands, use a fresh strip, and check again. If you use a CGM, confirm with a fingerstick if possible.
3. **Test for ketones as soon as possible.**
   - If you are sick and your blood glucose is **240 mg/dL or higher**, or at the level in your personal plan, use a urine or blood ketone test.
   - If ketones are moderate or high, or ketones are present and you feel sick, contact your clinician or on-call service right away. If you feel very sick, are vomiting, or symptoms are worsening, go to the **ER**.
4. **If you do not have a clear sick-day or correction plan, do not improvise.**
   - Call your diabetes clinician, on-call line, or primary care office now.
   - Tell them your glucose reading, ketone result if you have one, symptoms, recent vomiting or diarrhea, and what insulin or diabetes medicines you took.
   - If you cannot reach anyone and you are very high and sick, choose the **ER** rather than routine urgent care.
5. **Follow your personal sick-day or correction plan exactly if you have one.**
   - Take insulin and diabetes medicines as prescribed, including clinician-written sick-day instructions.
   - If you use an insulin pump and this high reading is unusual, check the infusion site, tubing, reservoir, and pump alarms. If you suspect insulin delivery has failed and you are worsening, seek urgent medical guidance now.
6. **Sip fluids if you can swallow safely.** Take small, steady sips of water or other sugar-free fluids. If you cannot keep fluids down, seek emergency care.
7. **If you take an SGLT2 inhibitor, treat ketoacidosis-type symptoms as urgent.** With nausea, vomiting, belly pain, trouble breathing, unusual sleepiness, or confusion, contact urgent medical help now. Tell the clinician the name of the SGLT2 inhibitor before taking more. If symptoms are significant, use the **ER**.
8. **Keep the key details together.** Write down the time of each glucose and ketone result, symptoms, fever if present, vomiting or diarrhea, and any insulin or diabetes medicines taken.
9. **Do not stay alone if you are worsening.** Ask someone to stay with you, check on you, or call 911 if you become harder to wake, confused, faint, unable to keep fluids down, or too weak to travel safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide long-term medication changes today.
- You do not need to keep normal plans, exercise, work, or errands while you are sick and checking for ketones.
- You do not need to organise every past glucose reading before asking for help; the current reading, ketones, symptoms, and medicines are enough to start.

## Important reassurance
High blood sugar with feeling sick is a real warning sign, and taking it seriously is the right response. Checking ketones, avoiding guessed doses, and getting help early can reduce the chance of things escalating.

## Scope note
These are immediate first steps only. Later decisions about medicine changes, illness checks, infection treatment, pump settings, or prevention plans may need help from your diabetes clinician.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, diagnosis, or a substitute for emergency care. Very high blood sugar with illness can become a medical emergency, including diabetic ketoacidosis or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state. If you are unsure, pregnant, on an SGLT2 inhibitor with ketoacidosis-type symptoms, unable to keep fluids down, unable to test ketones, or getting worse, seek urgent ER evaluation.
