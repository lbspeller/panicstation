# What to do if… water starts dripping through your ceiling and you cannot see the source (USA)
## Short answer
Keep people and pets away from the wet area, especially if water is near a light, fan, smoke detector, outlet, or sagging ceiling. Treat it as a possible electrical and collapse hazard: turn off power and water only if the controls are safe to reach, then contact building emergency maintenance or a qualified plumber.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand under a sagging, bulging, cracking, or dripping section of ceiling.
- Do not touch wet light fixtures, switches, outlets, power strips, plugged-in devices, or smoke detectors.
- Do not step into water or touch wet surfaces to reach a breaker panel.
- Do not poke, cut, or drill into the ceiling.
- Do not restore power just because the visible dripping has slowed or stopped.
- Do not delay contacting building management or a plumber because the source is not obvious.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the area.**  
   Move people and pets away from the drip zone. If the ceiling is sagging, bulging, cracking, or dropping material, leave the room and keep the doorway clear.

2. **Turn off electricity only if the panel is safe to reach.**  
   If you can reach the breaker panel without entering the wet area or touching wet surfaces, turn off the breaker for the affected room. If you cannot identify it safely, turn off the main breaker only if that control is also safe to reach.  
   If the route to the panel is wet, the panel is damp, or you are unsure, do not approach it. Keep people away and contact building emergency maintenance or a qualified electrician.

3. **Contain the drip only if the spot is safe to approach.**  
   If the ceiling is not sagging or bulging and the drip is not near electrical equipment, place a bucket or pan beneath it and add a towel to reduce splashing. Move nearby electronics, furniture, and valuables only when you can do so without entering the wet area.

4. **Turn off the water supply only if it is safe and you know the correct valve.**  
   If you can safely reach your home or unit’s main water shutoff and know which valve it is, turn it off. If you cannot reach it safely, cannot identify it, or live in a building with shared controls, leave it to building staff or a plumber.

5. **Contact the right building support.**  
   For an apartment, call the landlord or property manager’s emergency maintenance line. For a condo or co-op, contact building management, the association, or on-site staff. If it is safe and easy to do so, you may ask the occupant above you to check their own unit for an overflowing fixture or leaking appliance and to contact management.

6. **Get urgent help for immediate danger.**  
   Leave the area and call 911 from a safe place if you see sparking, smoke, fire, a burning smell, or signs that the ceiling may collapse. Otherwise, arrange a qualified plumber or building maintenance visit promptly. If wiring, fixtures, or electrical equipment may have become wet, ask for a qualified electrician before affected circuits are restored.

7. **Document the situation after the area is stable.**  
   Take photos or video from a safe distance of the drip, staining, sagging, and affected belongings. Keep affected circuits off until building maintenance or a qualified electrician says they can be restored safely.

## What can wait
- Deciding who is responsible for payment.
- Detailed insurance forms and non-urgent calls.
- Opening the ceiling or removing drywall.
- Moving items that would require entering the wet or unstable area.
- Detailed drying and mold-cleanup decisions until the leak and immediate hazards are controlled. Prompt drying should then be arranged.

## Important reassurance
Not seeing the source straight away does not mean you need to find it yourself. Water may travel along framing, pipes, or other surfaces before appearing through the ceiling. Creating distance, avoiding wet electrical areas, stopping water when it is safe, and calling the right help are enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. After the leak is stopped, building maintenance or qualified professionals may need to check the ceiling, plumbing, electrical safety, and hidden moisture damage.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, electrical, plumbing, insurance, or other professional advice. Use qualified help rather than troubleshooting when the area is wet, unstable, or difficult to assess safely.
