# What to do if… you develop new confusion or severe drowsiness after taking a new medicine (USA)
## Short answer
If the person collapses, has a seizure, has trouble breathing, or cannot be awakened, call **911** now. Otherwise, call **Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222** right away and follow their instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive, use machinery, or do anything that could be unsafe while you are confused or very drowsy.
- Do not drink alcohol, use recreational drugs, or take extra sedating medicines while you are getting advice.
- Do not guess whether to take, skip, stop, or repeat the next dose. Ask Poison Help, the prescriber, or a pharmacist what to do.
- Do not stay alone if you may become difficult to wake.
- Do not eat or drink until Poison Help or emergency services says it is okay.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now if there are emergency warning signs.** Call if the person collapses, has a seizure, has trouble breathing, cannot be awakened, is becoming difficult to wake, or is rapidly getting worse.
2. **If there are no emergency warning signs, call Poison Help now at 1-800-222-1222.** Tell them:
   - the new symptoms and when they started
   - the person’s age, approximate weight, and known health conditions, if available
   - the medicine name, strength, dose, and time taken
   - any other prescription medicines, over-the-counter medicines, supplements, alcohol, or recreational drugs taken
   - whether there may have been a double dose, a wrong pill, or a recent dose change
3. **Keep the medicine packaging nearby.** Put the bottle, box, prescription label, and any other medicines taken where you can read them to Poison Help.
4. **Reduce the risk of a fall or injury.** Sit or lie down somewhere safe. Ask someone to stay nearby when possible.
5. **If the person becomes difficult to wake but is breathing, call 911.** Unless there may be a head, neck, or back injury, place them on their side while emergency help is coming.
6. **If an opioid medicine may be involved, or an unknown pill may contain an opioid, use naloxone as directed if it is available and call 911.**
7. **Contact the prescriber or a pharmacist after the immediate safety call.** Ask what to do about the next dose and whether an in-person assessment is needed.
8. **If a phone call is not practical and the person is alert enough to use it safely, the Poison Control online tool is another option.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to stop the medicine permanently or what to take instead.
- You do not need to work out the cause by yourself while you feel impaired.
- Reporting a possible side effect can wait until the immediate safety issue has been addressed.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel alarmed by sudden confusion or heavy drowsiness. You do not need to work out what is causing it before asking for help; prompt guidance is the safest next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Decisions about restarting, stopping, tapering, or changing a medicine may need help from a prescriber, pharmacist, or another healthcare professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical diagnosis or personal medical advice. If symptoms are severe, rapidly worsening, or you are not sure the person can stay safe, call **911**.
