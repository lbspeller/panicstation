# What to do if… you are served with a restraining or protective order and need to understand immediate restrictions (USA)
## Short answer
For immediate safety, act as though the order is in force now unless the paperwork clearly says otherwise. Stop any contact the paper prohibits, stay away from listed people and places, and read the full order before doing anything else. Find every hearing date and response deadline on the papers, then get local legal help promptly.

## Do not do these things
- Do not contact a protected person unless the order clearly permits the exact contact route and purpose.
- Do not ask friends, relatives, or children to pass on messages.
- Do not use texts, calls, email, social media, comments, likes, gifts, or online accounts to make contact that the order prohibits.
- Do not go to a listed home, workplace, school, childcare setting, or other protected location.
- Do not return to a shared home if the order says you must leave.
- Do not arrange an informal belongings swap with a protected person.
- Do not handle, move, transfer, or store firearms or ammunition based on guesswork.
- Do not ignore a hearing date, request deadline, response deadline, or firearms instruction on the papers.
- Do not delete messages, call logs, photos, or social media history in a panic.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause.**  
   If you are at a place the order says you must leave, leave promptly without arguing or contacting the protected person. If law enforcement is present, follow their instructions.

2. **Read the whole order and mark the exact restrictions.**  
   Look for no-contact terms, stay-away distances, protected people, protected addresses, shared-home terms, child-contact terms, firearms and ammunition instructions, expiration information, hearing details, and any deadline to request a hearing or file a response.

3. **Stop prohibited contact routes.**  
   Do not call, text, email, message, comment, react, send gifts, or ask another person to communicate for you. If the order clearly permits a specific route for a limited purpose, use only that route and purpose.

4. **Deal with location conflicts before going anywhere.**  
   Write down every protected address and any stated distance. Change your route if needed. If you unexpectedly see a protected person, move away calmly and safely without communicating. If the order affects your home, work, school pickup, or belongings, ask the court clerk, a court self-help center, or a local lawyer about the permitted local procedure.

5. **Follow firearms and ammunition instructions exactly.**  
   State law and the written order may require immediate action. Some qualifying orders can also trigger federal restrictions. Follow any written surrender directions first. If the instructions are missing or unclear, promptly use the contact information on the order, contact the court clerk, or ask a local non-emergency law-enforcement route for procedural directions. Ask a local lawyer for legal advice. Do not improvise a transfer, transport, or storage plan.

6. **Find every court date and deadline on the papers.**  
   Note the date, time, location, remote-access details, and any deadline to request a hearing or file a response. Put them in your calendar now. Contact the court using the information on the paperwork if you need procedural information, an interpreter, or an accommodation.

7. **Make a short compliance note.**  
   Record when you were served, where you went after service, what contact routes you stopped using, and any practical conflict created by the written restrictions. Keep it factual and dated.

8. **Get local legal help focused on today’s restrictions.**  
   Ask a local lawyer or legal-aid organisation to help you understand what the order allows, what it prohibits, and what deadline comes next.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prepare a full response tonight unless the papers give you a deadline that requires action now.
- You do not need to explain your side to the protected person, friends, relatives, or social media.
- You do not need to decide long-term housing, parenting, or relationship questions now.
- You do not need to solve every later court issue before you have understood the immediate written restrictions and the next deadline.

## Important reassurance
Being served can create an urgent feeling that you need to explain, apologise, or fix everything immediately. A quieter response is safer: follow the paper, stop prohibited contact, stay away from listed places, and get local help with anything unclear.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Protective-order procedures vary by state and by the wording of the order, so later decisions may need help from a local lawyer or court self-help service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice or professional advice. Follow the written order and seek prompt local legal help if any restriction, deadline, or firearms instruction is unclear.
