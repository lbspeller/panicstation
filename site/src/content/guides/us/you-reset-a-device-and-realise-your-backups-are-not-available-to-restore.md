# What to do if… you reset a device and realise your backups are not available to restore (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the device immediately to prevent data overwriting and check your cloud accounts from a different computer to see if data is syncing rather than backed up. Most "missing" data is either tied to a different account or exists in a "sync" state (like iCloud Photos or Google Photos) that does not require a full device restore to access.

## Do not do these things
- Do not download large files, install multiple apps, or take new photos on the reset device, as this overwrites potentially recoverable data.
- Do not perform another factory reset immediately; if a backup exists, resetting again will not make it appear if you are using the wrong account.
- Do not sign out of your cloud accounts on other devices, such as tablets or old phones, which may still hold cached copies of your files.
- Do not pay for "guaranteed" data recovery software or services that require remote access to your device.
- Do not delete anything from your cloud "Trash" or "Recycle Bin" while trying to free up space for a new restore.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the device to prevent further data loss.**
   - Enable Airplane Mode or disconnect from Wi-Fi and Ethernet.
   - Power off the device if the data is high-value and you intend to seek professional forensic recovery.
2. **Verify the correct account credentials on a separate computer.**
   - Log in to the web version of your cloud service (icloud.com, google.com, or onedrive.com).
   - Check if the data (photos, contacts, notes) is visible in the web interface; if it is, the data is safe and simply hasn't synced to your device yet.
3. **Examine secondary "Recycle Bins" and "Recently Deleted" folders.**
   - Look in the "Recently Deleted" album in Photos and the "Trash" in cloud drives.
   - Check the "Second-stage recycle bin" if using a OneDrive for Work or School account, which may hold data for up to 93 days.
4. **Identify the difference between "Sync" and "Backup" protocols.**
   - **iCloud / Google Photos:** If "Sync" was on, these items are not part of the device backup file; they will download automatically once you sign in and wait for the sync to complete.
   - **Device Backups:** These include settings, SMS, and app data; they usually only appear during the "Initial Setup" wizard immediately after a reset.
5. **Check for "Restore" features in cloud drive settings.**
   - **OneDrive:** Use the "Restore your OneDrive" feature (requires Microsoft 365) to roll back your entire library to a point within the last 30 days.
   - **Google Drive:** Check "Backups" in the left-hand menu of the web interface to see a list of device backups and their last update timestamps.
6. **Search for local backups on physical computers.**
   - Connect your iPhone to a Mac (Finder) or PC (Apple Devices app/iTunes) to see if a local backup was created previously.
   - Check Windows "File History" or macOS "Time Machine" on your computer to see if cloud folders were mirrored locally.
7. **Contact IT administration for managed accounts.**
   - Reach out to your employer or school IT department if the account is managed; administrators often have extended recovery windows (up to 30 or 90 days) for deleted data.

## What can wait
- You do not need to purchase additional cloud storage or upgrade your data plan right now.
- You do not need to manually re-enter all your contacts or settings until you have confirmed a backup truly does not exist.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent data recovery service in the next few minutes; simply keeping the device powered off preserves your options.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of panic when a device appears empty after a reset. In many cases, the data is not actually gone but is either waiting to sync from the cloud or is stored under a different email address you may have forgotten using. Taking a moment to check your accounts systematically is the most effective way to resolve this.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital data loss following a device reset. It focuses on identifying existing cloud data and preventing further overwriting. It does not cover deep-level forensic recovery or hardware repair.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee the recovery of any data. Success depends on the specific device encryption, the length of time since the reset, and whether the data was ever successfully uploaded to a cloud service. For high-stakes data loss, consult a certified data recovery professional before taking further action.
