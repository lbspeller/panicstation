# What to do if… you smell exhaust fumes inside the cabin while stopped or parked (USA)

## Short answer
Turn the engine off immediately and move all occupants to fresh air away from the vehicle. If anyone displays symptoms of carbon monoxide poisoning, such as a headache, dizziness, or confusion, seek emergency medical attention.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep the engine running while you attempt to identify the source of the smell.
- Do not stay inside the vehicle with the windows closed or the air conditioning on.
- Do not set your ventilation to "recirculate" as this can trap existing fumes inside.
- Do not drive yourself to a medical facility if you feel faint, confused, or physically unwell.
- Do not attempt to inspect the exhaust system or muffler while the vehicle is hot or in an unventilated area.

## What to do now
1. **Turn the engine off immediately to stop the production of exhaust gases.** If you are in a garage or enclosed structure, do not attempt to move the car; simply shut it down and exit the building to reach open air.
2. **Move all passengers and pets out of the vehicle and away from the area.** Ensure everyone is at a safe distance where the air is clear, regardless of weather conditions.
3. **Conduct a quick assessment for symptoms of carbon monoxide exposure.** Check yourself and others for:
   - Dull headache or weakness.
   - Dizziness, nausea, or vomiting.
   - Shortness of breath or chest pain.
   - Confusion or blurred vision.
4. **Call 911 immediately if anyone is unconscious, struggling to breathe, or showing significant confusion.** Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless poison that can be fatal within minutes at high concentrations.
5. **Contact Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 for guidance if symptoms are mild or if you are unsure of the exposure level.** This service is free and provides expert toxicological advice 24/7.
6. **Open all vehicle windows and doors to vent the cabin once the engine is off.** Do this only from the outside if you have already exited the vehicle.
7. **Arrange for a professional tow to a certified mechanic and do not attempt to drive the vehicle.** Even with windows open, an exhaust leak can continue to pump dangerous levels of carbon monoxide into the cabin while the engine is running.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the leak is in the manifold, catalytic converter, or tailpipe right now.
- You do not need to check your vehicle's warranty or insurance coverage while still in the immediate vicinity of the fumes.
- You do not need to worry about your destination or schedule; safety and air quality are the only priorities.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of panic or physical "fogginess" when smelling exhaust fumes. Taking immediate action to shut off the engine and move to fresh air is the most effective way to stop further exposure and allow your body to begin clearing any inhaled gases.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for immediate fume exposure in a vehicle. It does not replace a mechanical inspection or a formal medical evaluation.

## Important note
This information is for general stabilization and does not constitute a medical or mechanical diagnosis. Carbon monoxide is a highly toxic gas that can cause permanent injury or death; if exposure is suspected and symptoms are present, professional medical assessment is required. All mechanical repairs must be performed by a qualified technician to ensure the vehicle is safe for operation.
