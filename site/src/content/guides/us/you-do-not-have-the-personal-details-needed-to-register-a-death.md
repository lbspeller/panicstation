# What to do if… you do not have the personal details needed to register a death (USA)

## Short answer
Tell the person handling the death-certificate filing which details you cannot confirm. Do not guess. Ask what can be left pending or recorded as unknown, and how a later correction would be handled.

## Do not do these things
- Do not invent a Social Security number, date of birth, birthplace, or name spelling to fill a blank.
- Do not assume that you must find every detail before the filer can tell you the next step.
- Do not send sensitive records, such as an SSN card or tax paperwork, to an unverified person or email address.
- Do not contact many agencies at once before you know what the filer needs for the death certificate.
- Do not order more certified copies than you need before asking the filer how uncertain details will be handled.

## What to do now
1. **Contact the person handling the death-certificate filing.** If a funeral home is involved, call the funeral home first. If there is no funeral home yet, ask the hospital, care facility, medical examiner, or coroner office handling the case who the filing contact is. If you are still unsure, contact the state or local vital records office for the place of death and ask where to start.

2. **State the problem plainly.** Say: “I cannot confirm some of the personal details, and I do not want to guess.”

3. **Ask for a written list.** Ask whether the filer has a worksheet or written list of the details they need. Mark each uncertain item clearly rather than trying to answer from memory during a phone call.

4. **Ask how missing items should be handled.** Ask which details can remain pending, which can be recorded as unknown, and what you should do if you find the correct information later. Amendment procedures vary by state.

5. **For an unknown Social Security number, let the filer choose the correct entry.** Tell them that you cannot confirm the SSN. The appropriate entry may depend on whether the number might still be supplied, whether the person had no SSN, or whether it is not known.

6. **Check only a few reliable records that you already lawfully have.** Look at readily available official records or a clearly labelled personal file. Stop if the detail is not quickly available. Do not delay asking the filer how to proceed.

7. **Ask whether the funeral home will notify Social Security.** Funeral homes generally report deaths to the Social Security Administration. If no funeral home is involved, or the funeral home does not report the death, use the official Social Security route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to search every file or contact every relative today.
- You do not need to notify every agency or business before resolving this specific filing question.
- You do not need to make estate, probate, or long-term financial decisions right now.
- You do not need to order a large number of certified copies before checking how uncertain details will be handled.

## Important reassurance
Missing details do not mean that you should guess. The safer first step is to state what you cannot confirm, ask how the filer records missing information, and ask how updates are handled if you find the details later.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about amendments, certified copies, benefits, or estate administration may need guidance from the relevant state or local office or an appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Death-certificate filing and amendment procedures vary by state and case. A funeral home handling the filing, or the relevant state or local vital records office, is the practical place to ask what to do next.
