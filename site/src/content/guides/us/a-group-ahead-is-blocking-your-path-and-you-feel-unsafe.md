# What to do if… a group ahead is blocking your path and you feel unsafe (USA)
## Short answer
Do not move into the group. Turn away, cross the street if it is safe, or enter a staffed public place. Call 911 if you need immediate police assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not move closer to test whether the group will let you pass.
- Do not stop nearby to record if doing so keeps you exposed or draws attention to you.
- Avoid arguing, responding to insults, or trying to reason with a group that feels hostile.
- Avoid entering an alley, stairwell, underpass, or other confined area with fewer exits.
- Avoid going directly home while you still think someone may be following you and a safer staffed place is available.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance and reach a safer pause.** Turn around, take another route, cross the street if it is safe, or enter a staffed public place such as a store, hotel lobby, pharmacy, or café.
2. **Tell someone nearby what you need.** Say to an employee, security guard, or other staff member: “I feel unsafe. Can I stay here while I work out what to do?”
3. **Call 911 if you need immediate police assistance.** Give your location, say that a group appears to be blocking your path and focusing on you, and answer the call-taker’s questions. Follow any instructions you are given.
4. **If you think someone followed you, stay in the staffed place.** Ask staff, security, or a trusted person for help. Avoid heading directly home while you still feel unsafe.
5. **Write down details once you are in a safer place.** Note the location, time, number of people, what happened, clothing or identifying features, and any vehicle details you noticed without moving closer.
6. **Consider reporting after you are safe.** For a possible crime, you can contact local police. If you believe you were targeted because of who you are and it may have been a hate crime, the Department of Justice advises reporting to state or local police and following up with the FBI. You can also use the Department of Justice Civil Rights Division form for a possible civil-rights violation or hate incident.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately what the group intended.
- You do not need to confront anyone or collect perfect evidence.
- You do not need to choose a reporting route while you are still trying to get somewhere safer.
- You do not need to post publicly or make long-term safety decisions right now.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to create distance and ask for help when a situation feels unsafe. You do not need to prove that the group intended harm before moving to a safer place.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. Later decisions, including reporting or getting tailored support, may need help from an appropriate specialist or local service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you need immediate emergency assistance.
