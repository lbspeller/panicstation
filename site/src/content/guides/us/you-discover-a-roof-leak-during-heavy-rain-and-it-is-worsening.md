# What to do if… you discover a roof leak during heavy rain and it is worsening (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the area to prevent electrocution or injury from ceiling collapse, then contain the water and contact emergency maintenance or a licensed roofer. **Turn off the electricity at the breaker panel if water is near any fixtures or outlets.**

## Do not do these things
- Don’t climb onto the roof during a storm, high winds, or active rainfall.
- Don’t touch wet electrical outlets, switches, or appliances.
- Don’t stand in water while reaching for the breaker panel.
- Don’t remain directly under a sagging or bulging ceiling.
- Don’t discard damaged items until you have photographed them for insurance purposes.
- Don’t ignore a leak that stops when the rain stops; the moisture trapped in the structure still requires professional drying.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the affected room of people and pets.** If the ceiling is sagging, bulging, or shedding debris, keep everyone out and close the door to contain potential dust or falling materials.
2. **Shut off power to the affected area at the main breaker panel.** 
   - **Only do this if you can reach the panel without walking through or standing in water.**
   - If water is dripping through a light fixture, ceiling fan, or near an outlet, shut off the specific circuit or the main house breaker.
   - If you hear buzzing, see sparks, or smell burning, evacuate the building and **call 911**.
3. **Relieve water pressure if the ceiling is bulging.**
   - If you see a "bubble" forming in the drywall or plaster, place a large bucket underneath it.
   - Using a screwdriver or a small drill bit, carefully poke a hole in the center of the bulge to allow the water to drain into the bucket; this prevents the weight of the water from causing a wider ceiling collapse.
4. **Contain the water and protect your property.**
   - Place buckets, bins, or pots under active drips.
   - Move electronics, rugs, furniture, and important documents to a dry area.
   - Lay down towels or plastic sheeting to prevent water from seeping into subflooring or lower levels.
5. **Contact professional help immediately.**
   - **Renters:** Call your landlord or property manager’s 24-hour emergency maintenance line.
   - **Homeowners:** Call a licensed roofing contractor for emergency mitigation (such as temporary tarping) and contact your homeowners insurance company to start a claim.
6. **Document the damage with photos and video.** Capture the source of the leak, the extent of the water intrusion, and any damage to the building or personal belongings.
7. **Monitor for safety hazards in adjacent rooms.** Water often travels along beams and pipes; check the rooms directly below or next to the leak for signs of moisture.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific hole in the roof or the exact shingles that failed right now.
- You do not need to begin full-scale cleanup or carpet extraction until the leak is stabilized and the area is confirmed electrically safe.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent replacement roof or sign a long-term repair contract today.
- You can wait to dispose of water-damaged materials until an insurance adjuster has authorized their removal.

## Important reassurance
A worsening roof leak is a high-stress event, but your primary goal is stabilization, not a permanent fix. By turning off the power and relieving the weight on the ceiling, you have already addressed the most significant immediate risks to your safety and your home's structure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Professional inspection and high-capacity drying (dehumidification) will be required within 24 to 48 hours to prevent mold growth and permanent structural damage.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace the advice of a licensed electrician, structural engineer, or roofing professional. In older homes (pre-1980), be aware that ceiling materials may contain asbestos; avoid disturbing dry debris or dust.
