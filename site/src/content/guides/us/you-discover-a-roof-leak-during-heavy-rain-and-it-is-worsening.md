# What to do if… you discover a roof leak during heavy rain and it is worsening (USA)

## Short answer
Move people and pets out of the affected room and keep them away from any sagging ceiling or wet electrical equipment. If you can reach the breaker panel from a dry, safe location, switch off the main breaker.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go onto the roof during heavy rain, strong wind, or lightning.
- Do not enter the attic during the storm if there is active leaking, poor visibility, or any electrical concern.
- Do not stand under a sagging, bulging, cracking, or shedding ceiling.
- Do not puncture, cut, or pull down a wet ceiling during the storm.
- Do not touch wet outlets, switches, light fixtures, power strips, appliances, or cords.
- Do not stand in water while touching the breaker panel or anything electrical.
- Do not turn power back on until a qualified electrician says it is safe.
- Do not delay leaving an unsafe area to take photos or move belongings.
- Do not discard damaged items before taking photos when it is safe to document them, unless an item presents an immediate health or safety hazard.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the affected room.** Move people and pets out. Close the door if possible, and keep everyone away from any wet ceiling, ceiling bulge, falling material, or water near electrical equipment.

2. **Switch off electricity only from a dry, safe location.**
   - If electrical equipment is wet or near water, switch off the main breaker if you can reach the breaker panel without entering standing water.
   - Do not touch the breaker panel while standing in water.
   - If the panel is not safely reachable, keep away and call a qualified electrician.
   - Leave power off until a qualified electrician says it is safe to restore.
   - If there is fire, smoke, sparking, a burning smell, or an immediate threat to life, leave the area and call 911.

3. **Contain water only where it is clearly safe to approach.**
   - Place a bucket or container under a clear drip only if you can do so without standing beneath a sagging ceiling or going near wet electrical equipment.
   - Put towels on a safe section of floor to reduce slipping.
   - Move nearby belongings only when the route is dry and the ceiling above you looks stable.

4. **Report the active leak.**
   - If you rent, contact your landlord or property manager using their urgent repair route and say that the roof leak is active and worsening during heavy rain.
   - If you own the home, contact a roofing contractor for temporary mitigation when weather conditions allow safe access.
   - Contact your insurer as soon as practical and ask what documentation they want before cleanup or repairs.

5. **Document from a safe position.** Take photos or video of the leak, visible ceiling damage, wet flooring, and damaged belongings only if you can do so without entering an unsafe area.

6. **Keep the room isolated until conditions improve.** Leave switched-off power off. Do not investigate the roof or attic during the storm. Once the area is safe, arrange inspection and begin drying promptly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact roof failure during the storm.
- You do not need to choose a permanent repair method tonight.
- You do not need to enter the attic or climb onto the roof to confirm where the water started.
- You do not need to begin drying equipment while electrical safety is uncertain.
- You can wait to compare contractors, review repair options, and organise non-urgent cleanup until the immediate hazards are under control.

## Important reassurance
You are not expected to repair a roof during heavy rain. Moving away from the unsafe area, switching off power only when it is safe to do so, limiting water spread where possible, and contacting the right help are useful first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first safe pause during an active storm. Later decisions may need help from a roofing contractor, qualified electrician, insurer, landlord, property manager, or building professional. Drying can wait until the area is safe, then should begin promptly.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, insurance, electrical, structural, financial, or professional trade advice. If you are unsure whether the area is safe, keep people away and seek professional help.
