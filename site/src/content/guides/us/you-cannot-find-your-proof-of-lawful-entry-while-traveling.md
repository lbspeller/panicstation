# What to do if… you cannot find your proof of lawful entry while traveling (USA)

## Short answer
Ask what exact document is needed and why. If the request is for your admission record and an I-94 applies to you, use the official I-94 website to retrieve your most recent record and print or save it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess your status category, entry date, or permitted-stay date.
- Do not use edited screenshots, fake PDFs, or unofficial templates.
- Do not assume that a missing passport stamp means there is no entry record. Many I-94 records are electronic.
- Do not send passport or immigration-document copies to an unverified person or upload them to an unofficial site.
- Do not sign a document you do not understand solely because you feel rushed. Ask what it means and whether you can take time to read it.

## What to do now
1. Ask the person requesting proof: “Which document do you need, and what issue are you trying to resolve?”
2. If they mean your admission record and an I-94 applies to you, open the official I-94 website and use the option to retrieve your most recent I-94. Save or print the record if you can.
3. If the record is not found, enter your name, date of birth, document number, and country of citizenship exactly as shown on the travel document you used when you entered the United States. If you entered using an earlier passport, use that document’s details. Note the error wording and check the official I-94 help page.
4. Gather any records you already have: your passport identity page, visa page if relevant, admission stamp if there is one, and the itinerary or boarding pass from your entry trip. These may help clarify the issue, but they may not replace the document being requested.
5. If the I-94 appears to contain an error made at entry, check the official CBP Deferred Inspection Sites page and follow the instructions for the appropriate office. Ask what that office can handle before sending personal documents.
6. If you are at an airline counter or checkpoint, ask what specific document or correction would resolve the immediate problem and what safe next option is available if it cannot be resolved there.
7. Keep any saved copies secure. Share them only when needed through a verified official or airline channel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reconstruct your full immigration history at the counter.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a complaint while you are stressed.
- You do not need to make longer-term legal decisions until you know what specific record or document is missing.

## Important reassurance
A missing paper record or passport stamp does not by itself mean that there is no lawful-entry record. Many I-94 records are issued electronically, and the immediate task is to identify the exact document requested and check the official record.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an immediate travel-document check. If the record remains missing, appears incorrect, or raises a status question, later decisions may need qualified immigration help.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate stabilization and harm prevention, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. The document required can depend on your status, your trip, and the reason it is being requested.
