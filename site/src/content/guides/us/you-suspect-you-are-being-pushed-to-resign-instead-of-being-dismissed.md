# What to do if… you suspect you are being pushed to resign instead of being dismissed (USA)

## Short answer
Obtain all separation details in writing and do not sign any documents during the initial meeting. Your immediate priority is to secure your final pay, confirm your benefits end dates, and request a written separation notice to support your unemployment claim.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign a separation agreement, waiver of claims, or "acknowledgment" under pressure during the announcement meeting.
- Do not quit or offer to resign voluntarily, as this can disqualify you from unemployment benefits and certain severance rights.
- Do not rely on verbal promises regarding severance amounts, health insurance extensions, or rehire possibilities.
- Do not download, email, or copy proprietary company data or confidential files, which could violate your contract or lead to legal action.
- Do not use company devices or work email for private job searching or discussing the layoff with colleagues.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a written separation summary before leaving the premises or logging off.** This document should explicitly state your final day of employment, the reason for separation (e.g., "Reduction in Force"), and the date your benefits will expire.
2. **Confirm the timing and contents of your final paycheck.** Many states (including California, Colorado, and Massachusetts) require immediate payment of all earned wages upon involuntary termination.
   - Ask if accrued Paid Time Off (PTO) or vacation time will be included in this payment.
   - Request a written breakdown of the gross pay calculation if it is not provided.
3. **If you are age 40 or older, verify your OWBPA review rights.** Federal law (the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act) generally requires that you be given at least 21 days to review a severance agreement that waives age discrimination claims, plus a 7-day period to revoke your signature after signing.
4. **Determine if a WARN Act notice was issued for this event.** Ask HR or your manager if the layoff triggers Federal or state Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) requirements. 
   - Note that for California layoffs in 2026, notices must now include specific information about food assistance (CalFresh) and disclosures regarding the coordination of rapid-response services.
5. **Secure copies of your personal employment records while you still have system access.** Download your most recent pay stubs, W-2 forms, and benefits summaries from the HR portal.
6. **Request a formal separation letter specifically for unemployment purposes.** Most state unemployment agencies require proof that your job loss was due to a "lack of work" rather than misconduct to process your claim quickly.
7. **File your initial unemployment claim in the state where you performed the work.** Do this as soon as your employment officially ends, as most states do not pay benefits retroactively for the weeks before you filed.
8. **Obtain written instructions for the return of company property.** Ensure you receive a dated receipt or shipping confirmation for laptops, badges, and other equipment to prevent future pay deductions or legal disputes.
9. **Use a standard response to defer signing any legal waivers.** If pressured to sign, state: "I need to take these documents home to review them carefully before I can sign."

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on COBRA or Marketplace health insurance today; you generally have at least 60 days to elect coverage.
- You do not need to update your professional social media profiles or notify your entire network in the first 24 hours.
- You do not need to make decisions about rolling over 401(k) or other retirement accounts immediately.
- You do not need to respond to severance offers that have a multi-week review window.

## Important reassurance
Receiving news of a layoff is a high-stress event that often triggers a "fight or flight" response. It is normal to feel overwhelmed, confused, or a sudden urge to fix the situation immediately. Taking a few hours to process the information before making legal or financial commitments is a standard and protective professional practice.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and administrative steps for the first 48 hours following a layoff announcement. It does not cover long-term career transition, legal challenges for wrongful termination, or tax strategies for severance payments.

## Important note
Employment laws vary significantly by state and employer size. While federal laws provide a baseline for WARN notice and COBRA rights, your specific state may offer additional protections regarding final pay deadlines and PTO payouts. If you believe your inclusion in a layoff was discriminatory or retaliatory, consult an employment attorney before signing any waiver of claims.
