# What to do if… your paycheck is late because of a payroll failure (USA)

## Short answer
Ask payroll or HR for a written update stating when payment is expected to be issued or made available, how it will be sent, and the amount if known. If the delay affects essentials, ask whether a manual check or off-cycle payment is available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rely only on a spoken promise. Ask for the clearest written update available.
- Do not send full bank account details through ordinary email or chat.
- Do not quit or stop working in the heat of the moment without considering how that could affect your pay and job status.
- Do not assume the same payday rule applies in every state.
- Do not take a high-cost loan in a panic if another option may be available.
- Do not sign a waiver or agree to an off-the-books payment arrangement without time to read it carefully.

## What to do now
1. **Check the missed payment.** Note the payday that passed, the pay period, your expected pay, and whether a pay stub or payroll notice was issued. Save your time records, schedule, and any payroll-failure message.
2. **Ask for a written payment plan.** Ask payroll or HR when the payment is expected to be issued or made available, how it will be sent, the amount if known, and who will provide updates if the date changes.
3. **Ask about a faster payment option if essentials are affected.** Ask whether the employer can issue a manual check, an off-cycle deposit, or another documented payment method.
4. **Check your direct-deposit details safely.** Confirm that the masked account details on file look correct. Use the employer’s secure process if anything needs to be corrected.
5. **Record any direct costs caused by the delay.** Save notices, receipts, and screenshots for an overdraft fee, returned payment, or late fee. Ask in writing whether the employer will reimburse charges caused by the delay.
6. **Check the current state route.** Payday timing and state processes vary. Start with the federal state-payday summary, then use the official state labor office listing to confirm the current information for your state.
7. **Note any minimum-wage or overtime issue.** Federal wage rules may matter if the delay leaves required minimum wage or overtime unpaid. Overtime earned in a workweek generally must be paid on the regular payday for the relevant pay period, with a limited exception when the correct amount cannot yet be determined.
8. **Escalate if the replacement date passes or you need help understanding the route.** Check your state labor office for the current state process. For federal wage questions or to ask about filing a complaint, contact the U.S. Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division.
9. **Keep one folder of records.** Save the payroll notice, written updates, time records, pay stubs, bank screenshots with sensitive details hidden, and evidence of any fees.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to hire a lawyer or start a formal claim.
- You do not need to investigate the payroll vendor before asking your employer for a written payment plan.
- You do not need to make a major job decision while you are still confirming when and how you will be paid.

## Important reassurance
A late paycheck can disrupt essential payments quickly. Asking for a written date, a clear payment method, and a faster option is a reasonable first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on your state, your work classification, the type of wages involved, and whether the replacement payment arrives.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. State and federal rules may apply differently depending on the facts. If an essential payment is likely to be affected, you may choose to contact the provider and ask what temporary options are available.
