# What to do if… you are told you need a transit visa for your connection and you do not have one (USA)

## Short answer
Request that the airline confirm the exact document requirement in their system, then immediately seek a rebooking on a route that avoids the United States, as U.S. transit requires full entry authorization (ESTA or a visa) which cannot be obtained instantly.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume you can stay "airside" in a U.S. airport; the U.S. does not have a sterile international transit zone, meaning all connecting passengers must clear Customs and Border Protection (CBP).
- Do not apply for an ESTA at the last minute and expect an immediate result; CBP requires applications at least 72 hours before boarding.
- Do not use unofficial third-party websites to apply for travel documents; use only official government apps or sites.
- Do not buy a new ticket via another country until you have verified that country’s specific transit visa rules for your nationality.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the exact blocking requirement with the airline agent.**  
   **Ask the agent to specify which country’s rules are the issue and the exact name of the required authorization.**
2. **Request a manual check of your documents against the Timatic system.**  
   **Ensure the agent has entered your passport nationality and any existing valid visas (like a B1/B2) correctly into the database.**
3. **Download and use the official "ESTA Mobile" app if you are from a Visa Waiver Program (VWP) country.**  
   **Submit a new application only if you have not been denied before, but be aware that approval is not guaranteed and can take up to 72 hours.**
   - The ESTA fee is $40.27 as of 2026.
4. **Identify rerouting options that bypass the United States entirely.**  
   **Ask the airline for a "route-around" itinerary that transits through a country where your passport does not require a transit visa.**
5. **Contact your embassy or consulate if you are stuck in a foreign airport without a valid route.**  
   **Request information on emergency travel documents or local transit concessions if you are being detained or held in a terminal.**
6. **Gather all physical and digital evidence of the denial.**  
   **Take clear photos of the "Denied Boarding" screen, your original itinerary, and any written explanations provided by the airline staff.**
7. **Communicate factually with CBP officers if you have already landed in the U.S.**  
   **Answer questions about your onward itinerary clearly and provide proof of a confirmed ticket to a third country.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine who is at fault for the missing visa until you have reached a safe destination.
- You do not need to pay for a C-1 transit visa application ($185) right now if you are already at the airport, as it requires an embassy interview and cannot be processed on-site.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Department of Transportation while you are still in transit.

## Important reassurance
Missing a transit requirement is a common occurrence due to the lack of "airside" transit in the U.S. Airlines are legally required to verify these documents to avoid fines, so their refusal to board you is a standard regulatory procedure rather than a personal judgement. Focus on the physical step of rerouting.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for travelers facing immediate document-related boarding denials. It does not cover the legal process of applying for long-term visas or challenging immigration decisions.

## Important note
Transit and entry requirements are subject to change and vary based on nationality, travel history, and specific airport protocols. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice from U.S. Customs and Border Protection or the Department of State.
