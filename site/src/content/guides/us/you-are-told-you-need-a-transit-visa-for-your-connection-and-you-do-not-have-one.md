# What to do if… you are told you need a transit visa for your connection and you do not have one (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the airline to identify the exact country, airport, and permission blocking your journey, then rerun its documented requirements check against your passport and exact itinerary. If the required permission cannot be confirmed in time, ask for a compliant route that avoids that transit point before paying for another ticket.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume that being “only in transit” exempts you from U.S. travel-document requirements.
- Do not give inaccurate or incomplete information about your passport, nationality, existing visas, or itinerary.
- Do not pay for a replacement ticket until the new route has been checked for every connection point.
- Do not submit an ESTA application through a website unless you have checked that it is the official website.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the airline to state the exact problem.**  
   Ask which country, airport, and permission are blocking travel. Write down the answer and ask the agent to add it to your booking if possible.

2. **Ask the airline to rerun its documented requirements check.**  
   Show the passport you plan to use and any current visas or residence permits. Ask the agent to check your exact itinerary, including every connection airport, any overnight stop, and any airport change.

3. **If the connection is in the United States, ask which U.S. permission applies to you.**  
   Depending on your circumstances, the answer may be:
   - an existing visa that can be used for transit,
   - Visa Waiver Program travel with an approved ESTA,
   - a transit visa,
   - another permission that applies to your travel documents.

4. **If you may be eligible for Visa Waiver Program travel, check ESTA only on the official website.**  
   Transit through the United States is generally permitted for eligible Visa Waiver Program travellers, but valid ESTA approval is required before boarding a U.S.-bound flight. ESTA approval does not guarantee admission.

5. **If you do not have a compliant route, ask for a reroute.**  
   Ask the airline to check an itinerary that avoids the United States or the other affected transit country. Have the agent check every connection point before the replacement ticket is issued.

6. **If you are already at an airport or border checkpoint, keep the conversation practical.**  
   Follow the instructions you are given. Ask what onward or return arrangement is permitted, what the next step is, and whether you may contact your airline or consulate if needed.

7. **Save the basic record.**  
   Keep screenshots or photographs of your itinerary, boarding documents, the visa-required message, booking references, rebooking offers, and receipts for added costs.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, request reimbursement, or make an insurance claim.
- You do not need to work out every immigration rule yourself.
- You do not need to keep checking unofficial forums while the airline is rerunning the documented check.

## Important reassurance
A document-check stop is stressful, but you only need to handle the next practical step: confirm the exact requirement, then move to a route that meets it. You do not need to solve the whole trip at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A visa application, complaint, refund request, or insurance claim may need separate help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. Entry and transit requirements depend on your passport, nationality, existing visas or permits, and exact itinerary. Follow the instructions given by border officials and your airline.
