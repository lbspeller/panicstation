# What to do if… your bank’s app and website are down and you have a time-critical payment due (USA)

## Short answer
Call your bank using a trusted number and ask what payment method they can process now by phone or in a branch. At the same time, contact the payee through an official number or website, explain that your bank is having an outage, and ask for a short hold while you arrange an alternate payment.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep submitting the same payment repeatedly; duplicates may go through when systems recover.
- Don’t trust new payment instructions sent by text, social media, or email because “the system is down”; verify through the payee’s official website, statement, contract, or a phone number you already had.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, full security answers, your PIN, or full card details with anyone who contacts you.
- Don’t move money to a “safe account” because someone claims there is fraud or an outage.
- Don’t use gift cards, cryptocurrency, person-to-person apps, or a new account number unless the payee’s official channel confirms that exact method.
- Don’t post account details, screenshots with balances, or personal information on social media while asking about the outage.

## What to do now
1. **Write down the payment facts.**  
   Note the amount, due date and time, account or memo reference, payee name, and any late consequence you were told about. This keeps calls short and helps you avoid mistakes.

2. **Call your bank using a trusted number.**  
   Use the number on your card, statement, or the bank’s official contact page. Say: “My app and online banking are down, and I need to make a time-critical payment today. What can you process for me now by phone, automated phone banking, or branch?”

3. **Ask about the fastest bank-supported option for this payment.**  
   If the payment must arrive the same day, ask whether a wire is possible and whether it requires extra verification or a branch visit. If a wire is not needed, ask whether Same Day ACH, official bill pay by phone, a cashier’s check, or another bank-supported method can meet the deadline.

4. **Go to a branch if phone support cannot complete it and a branch is available.**  
   Bring government ID, the payee’s official payment instructions, the amount, and any required account or memo reference. Ask for the fastest appropriate option they can process, and get a receipt or confirmation.

5. **Contact the payee through an official route.**  
   Tell them your bank’s app and website are down and you are trying to pay through phone support or a branch. Ask for a temporary note on the account, grace period, fee hold, service hold, or other available option while you complete payment.

6. **Use only an official alternate payment route.**  
   If the payee offers pay by phone, card, in-person payment, or another official route, use contact details from the payee’s official website, statement, lease, bill, or contract. Do not use a number or link sent to you during the outage unless you independently verify it.

7. **Keep proof of the outage and your payment attempts.**  
   Screenshot the error message without showing full account details, note call times, write down names or reference numbers, and keep receipts or confirmation numbers. If you mail or hand-deliver a payment, keep tracking or a dated receipt.

8. **Ask your bank to review any fees caused by the outage.**  
   If you are charged overdraft, insufficient-funds, late, withdrawal, expedited payment, or similar fees because of the outage, ask the bank to credit or reverse them and document the request.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to switch banks or change your long-term bill-pay setup.
- You do not need to file a complaint before you have tried to secure the payment or a documented hold.
- You do not need to keep troubleshooting your phone or computer if both the app and website are failing and the bank confirms or appears to have an outage.
- You do not need to explain every detail to the payee now; the immediate goal is a documented hold or an official alternate payment method.

## Important reassurance
Banking outages and technology glitches do happen. You still have practical next steps: contact the bank through a trusted route, ask for a bank-supported payment option, contact the payee early, and keep proof of what you tried.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to stabilize the payment deadline and reduce irreversible mistakes. Later decisions about reimbursement, billing corrections, complaint routes, housing issues, credit reporting, or contract consequences may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Cutoff times, payment availability, fee reversal, grace periods, and payee rules vary by bank, payee, account type, and payment type. If missing the payment could trigger serious consequences such as eviction action, foreclosure steps, a court deadline, loss of a home closing, or essential utility shutoff, consider getting specialist help promptly while you continue trying official payment routes.
