# What to do if… a memory card is unreadable on multiple devices and you need the photos (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the card. Do not format, erase, initialize, scan, or repair it while the photos still need recovering.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click **Format**, **Erase**, **Initialize**, or prompts that offer to make the card usable.
- Don’t run Windows **CHKDSK**, macOS **Disk Utility First Aid**, or scan or repair prompts before the photos are recovered.
- Don’t keep repeating detection checks after one careful computer check.
- Don’t use apps that repair the card before recovering files.
- Don’t save recovered files back onto the same memory card.
- Don’t try physical hacks such as heat, freezing, bending, or taping adapters.

## What to do now
1. **Remove the card and keep it safe.**  
   Stop using it in cameras, phones, and other devices. If it is a full-size SD card, you may move the lock switch to the locked position as an extra precaution against accidental writing. Do not rely on the switch alone.

2. **Check whether copies already exist.**  
   Look in places where the photos may already have been imported, synced, or shared:
   - Your phone or computer photo library,
   - Cloud photo storage or file-sync services,
   - Camera or phone internal storage,
   - Messages and email attachments.

3. **Decide whether to stop DIY attempts.**  
   If the photos are irreplaceable, or the card is not detected by any device, consider contacting a data-recovery service before making further attempts. Ask how the provider protects the original card, explains costs before work begins, and returns the original card.

4. **If you continue yourself, do one careful detection-only computer check.**  
   Use a known-good card reader. Do not accept prompts to format, initialize, erase, scan, or repair the card.
   - On **Mac**, open **Disk Utility**, choose **View > Show All Devices**, and look for the card in the sidebar. Do not run **First Aid** and do not erase the card.
   - On **Windows**, open **Disk Management** and look for the card. Do not initialize or format it, and do not run **CHKDSK**.

5. **If the card opens normally, copy the photos somewhere else immediately.**  
   Use a computer drive, external drive, or cloud storage. Do not copy files back onto the unreadable card.

6. **If the card appears but the photos do not open, stop before trying repairs.**  
   Consider a data-recovery service. Self-help recovery software may be an option if you are comfortable using it, but save any recovered files to a different location.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide whether to reuse the card. Treat it as unreliable until recovery attempts are finished.
- You don’t need to choose a replacement card or reorganize your backups now.
- You don’t need to deal with warranty, refund, or complaint options before protecting the photos.

## Important reassurance
Stopping now is useful. Pausing avoids accidental format or repair actions while you decide what to do next.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later recovery decisions may need help from a data-recovery specialist, especially if the card is not detected or the photos are irreplaceable.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. It is not a guarantee of recovery. Avoid repair and format actions while the photos still need recovering.
