# What to do if… someone you care about is taken into custody and you need to arrange support or collect belongings (USA)
## Short answer
Confirm which agency or facility currently has them before travelling anywhere. Ask for the facility’s current property-release steps, including whether the person must authorize collection, what ID you need, and when pickup is available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not travel to a jail, police station, or property unit until the responsible facility or unit has confirmed where to go and what to bring.
- Do not assume booking property, evidence, vehicle contents, and belongings from a home or workplace are handled by the same unit.
- Do not discuss allegations on facility calls or messages unless you have confirmed that the communication is private. Calls and messages may be monitored or recorded, so keep ordinary communications to welfare and logistics.
- Do not send or bring cash, medication, phones, chargers, clothing, or other items until the facility confirms what it accepts and how it must be delivered.
- Do not enter a home, vehicle, or workplace or remove belongings unless you have permission and lawful access.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm where the person is being held.**  
   Start with the arresting agency’s public number or the county jail’s official lookup page, if one is available. Ask for the facility name, booking status, and any booking number or inmate ID. If they may have moved into state prison custody, use the relevant state corrections department. If federal custody is possible, use the Federal Bureau of Prisons locator. A person not shown in BOP custody may still be held elsewhere. If immigration detention is possible, use the ICE locator. A recent detention may not appear immediately.

2. **Ask the facility for its exact property-release process.**  
   Ask:
   - “Can they authorize me to collect any personal property?”
   - “How do they complete the authorization here?”
   - “Which items can be released?”
   - “What photo ID or other information do I need?”
   - “What are the pickup hours, processing time, and pickup location?”

3. **Separate booking property from evidence.**  
   Ask whether the belongings are stored as booking property, held for safekeeping, or kept as evidence. If an item is evidence, ask for the case or report number and the contact route for the property or evidence unit. Do not assume it can be released immediately.

4. **Complete the facility’s authorization step before travelling.**  
   The person in custody may need to name you as the collector or complete the facility’s release form. Bring the required photo ID and any identifying information the facility asks for, such as the person’s full name, date of birth, booking number, or inmate ID.

5. **Keep any outside collection narrow and authorized.**  
   If you are asked to collect belongings from a home, vehicle, or workplace, confirm that you have permission and lawful access. If police seized anything, ask for a receipt or reference number. Do not remove or disturb anything police tell you to leave in place. Collect only requested essentials that you can store safely.

6. **Pass on urgent welfare information through the facility’s route.**  
   Ask facility staff how to pass on urgent medical needs, prescription details, disability-related needs, or immediate childcare concerns, then provide the details promptly. Do not bring medication to the facility unless staff instruct you to do so. If you believe there is an immediate risk to life, call 911 and tell the facility.

7. **Check the approved support routes before sending anything.**  
   Ask the facility how calls, funds, mail, and any permitted items work there. Use only the facility’s current approved method.

8. **Re-check the rules after a transfer.**  
   If the person moves to another jail, state prison, federal custody, or immigration detention, ask the new facility what happened to stored property and what support routes now apply.

## What can wait
- You do not need to send packages, buy supplies, or add funds until the facility confirms the approved route.
- You do not need to post updates online, explain the situation widely, or organize a large group response.
- You do not need to decide every legal or complaint-related step immediately. If there is an urgent legal issue, or the person asks for a lawyer, seek legal help promptly.

## Important reassurance
This can feel disorienting, especially when information changes during booking or transfer. Confirming the current facility, following its property-release steps, and passing on urgent welfare details are useful first actions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Procedures vary between local jails, state prisons, federal custody, immigration detention, and property or evidence units. Later decisions may need help from a lawyer or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow the current instructions from the responsible facility or unit for the specific situation.
