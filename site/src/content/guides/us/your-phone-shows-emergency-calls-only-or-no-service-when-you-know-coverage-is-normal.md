# What to do if… your phone shows emergency-calls-only or no service when you know coverage is normal (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a place where phones usually work, then force a clean cellular reconnect by turning Airplane Mode on for at least 15 seconds, turning it off, and restarting once. If it still says emergency-calls-only, get onto Wi-Fi and contact your carrier to check outage, account, SIM, eSIM, and device-block issues.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t factory reset as a first response; it can lock you out and usually will not fix a carrier-side issue.
- Don’t delete an eSIM unless your carrier tells you to and you know how to restore it.
- Don’t enter APN or carrier settings from random videos or forums.
- Don’t assume the phone is permanently broken just because the change was sudden.
- Don’t keep toggling settings endlessly; do one short check, then use Wi-Fi or another phone.
- Don’t rely on this phone alone for safety today if it is not reliably connecting.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a better signal spot.**  
   Go outside or near a window. Avoid basements, garages, elevators, and thick concrete if you can.

2. **Force the phone to reconnect.**  
   Turn **Airplane Mode** on for at least 15 seconds, turn it off, then restart the phone once.

3. **Check the right line is turned on.**  
   If you use dual SIM, eSIM, or more than one line, check that your usual line is enabled and that mobile service is not turned off for that line.

4. **Check the SIM or eSIM without removing your way back in.**  
   - For a physical SIM: power the phone off, remove the SIM, check it is not bent or dirty, reinsert it firmly, then power on.
   - For an eSIM: toggle the eSIM line off and on if your phone allows it.
   - Do not delete the eSIM unless your carrier instructs you.

5. **Use Wi-Fi so you are not stuck.**  
   Join trusted Wi-Fi. Turn on Wi-Fi Calling if your carrier supports it, then try a call or text. Use messaging apps, email, or another phone for anything time-sensitive.

6. **Check for updates while on Wi-Fi.**  
   Install any pending phone system update. On iPhone, also check for a carrier settings update in Settings if an update prompt appears or the About screen offers one.

7. **Check your carrier account and outage status.**  
   Use your carrier app or website on Wi-Fi. Look for outage notices, a suspended line, billing block, SIM change, lost or stolen status, or a device block.

8. **Contact your carrier and ask for specific checks.**  
   Say: “My phone shows emergency-calls-only in an area where coverage is normally fine. I have toggled Airplane Mode, restarted, and checked the SIM or eSIM.” Ask them to check:
   - local outage or maintenance
   - account or line blocks
   - SIM or eSIM activation and provisioning
   - whether the device IMEI is blocked or barred
   - whether a replacement SIM or eSIM reissue is needed

9. **If you need emergency help while service is broken, try 911 first.**  
   Wireless phones can call 911 even without an active wireless plan. If your phone has no active service, the 911 center may not receive your location and may not be able to call you back, so say your exact location first. If the call fails or drops, use another phone immediately.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to replace the phone.
- You do not need to switch carriers right now.
- You do not need to wipe the phone, delete the eSIM, or install a signal booster app.
- You do not need a perfect explanation before you restore a working contact method.

## Important reassurance
Emergency-calls-only usually means the phone is not properly registered on your carrier’s network at that moment. That can happen because of signal conditions, SIM or eSIM activation, a carrier account issue, an outage, or a device block, and many of those can be checked without drastic steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the problem keeps returning after service is restored, you may later need carrier support, SIM or eSIM replacement, phone software troubleshooting, or device service.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not professional telecom, legal, emergency, or technical advice. If you have an emergency and this phone is unreliable, use another phone or another available emergency contact method right away.
