# What to do if… your social media account is suddenly locked or suspended and you cannot reach support quickly (USA)

## Short answer
First secure the email and phone number that control the account, because a locked social media account can also be a takeover warning. Then use only the platform’s official appeal or account-recovery process and keep a record of what you submit.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay for “instant unlock” services or anyone claiming insider access.
- Don’t share verification codes, backup codes, passwords, or identity-document images with anyone who contacts you first.
- Don’t click “support” links from DMs, comments, texts, or search ads; go to the official app, official website, or a saved bookmark yourself.
- Don’t keep retrying codes or logins over and over; it may trigger more checks or delays.
- Don’t assume it is only a platform mistake; treat it as a possible compromise until your email, phone, and recovery details are secure.

## What to do now
1. **Capture what you can see before it changes**
   - Take screenshots of the lock, suspension, restriction, or disabled-account screen.
   - Save any emails or texts about password resets, new logins, policy violations, recovery codes, or appeal prompts.
   - Write down the account handle, date, time, and any case number or appeal reference shown.

2. **Secure the email account tied to the social media account**
   - Use a trusted device or clean browser profile if you suspect your usual device or browser session is compromised.
   - Change the email password.
   - Check that the recovery email and phone number are yours, and remove anything you did not add.
   - Turn on multi-factor authentication if it is available.
   - Review recent sign-ins, connected devices, and email forwarding rules; remove anything you did not set.

3. **Secure the phone number used for account recovery**
   - Make sure your phone can still receive calls and texts.
   - If your number suddenly stopped working, contact your mobile carrier from its official website, app, or published customer-service route.
   - Ask the carrier to check for recent SIM, number-transfer, or account-security changes if the number may have been taken over.

4. **Use the official platform route only**
   - Open the platform’s official app or type the official website address yourself.
   - Use the in-app or official website path for “I can’t log in,” “account disabled,” “account suspended,” “appeal,” or “hacked account.”
   - Submit only the information the official process asks for.
   - If the form fails, try a different trusted browser, device, or normal connection without a VPN, then save a screenshot of the error.

5. **Cut off common takeover paths**
   - Change passwords on any other account where you reused the same or similar password.
   - If you still have partial access to the social media account, sign out unknown sessions and remove unfamiliar connected apps.
   - Check whether new posts, messages, payment links, ads, or profile changes were made without you.

6. **Warn people without creating more risk**
   - Contact close friends, clients, or team members outside the locked platform and tell them to ignore unexpected messages, links, money requests, or code requests from that account.
   - If the account is used for a business or creator presence, post a short notice on another channel you control saying the account is locked or possibly compromised.
   - Do not accuse a specific person unless you have reliable evidence.

7. **If money, identity details, or fraud are involved**
   - If you sent money or shared card, bank, payment-app, or account details, contact the bank, card issuer, or payment app immediately.
   - Keep receipts, usernames, handles, links, emails, texts, and screenshots; keep originals where possible.
   - Report cyber-enabled fraud or account takeover to IC3 if it fits what happened.
   - Report consumer fraud to the FTC if you were tricked by a fake support service, impersonator, or recovery scam.
   - Use IdentityTheft.gov if your personal information may be used to open accounts, apply for benefits, file taxes, or take out credit in your name.

## What can wait
- You do not need to write a perfect appeal right now; focus on accurate official forms and account security.
- You do not need to delete old posts, wipe your device, or make public accusations while you are still preserving evidence.
- You can wait to do a full privacy, follower, app-permission, and content audit until the account is restored or the immediate risk is contained.
- You do not need to decide today whether to start a new account, change brands, or take legal action.

## Important reassurance
This is common and stressful because social media accounts can hold work, memories, contacts, and income. The safest first move is not to chase fast fixes; it is to secure the accounts that control recovery and use only official routes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce harm, preserve useful records, and get you into the correct recovery lane. Longer disputes, business losses, identity-theft recovery, or legal questions may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical-security, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Platform decisions, account-recovery requirements, and timelines vary, and no guide can promise that an account will be restored.
