# What to do if… someone is pressuring you for sexual content or live video and ignoring your no (USA)

## Short answer
Leave the call or chat and do not send more content. Get to a safer pause before deciding whether to report, block, or ask for support.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send more images, live video, or a final message to try to make the pressure stop.
- Do not send money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency.
- Do not move to another app just because the person demands it.
- Do not keep negotiating after you have said no.
- Do not blame yourself for freezing, replying, or feeling overwhelmed.
- Do not download, forward, or make new copies of sexual images involving anyone under 18.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** Leave the call or chat. Move somewhere you feel steadier. If you are in immediate danger, call **911** or local police.
2. **Stop the live access.** Close the app. If it helps, turn off camera and microphone access for that app.
3. **Save limited details only if you may want support or to report later.** Save the threatening messages, usernames, profile links, dates, and any payment instructions. Do not download, forward, or make new copies of sexual images involving anyone under 18.
4. **Use the platform tools when you feel ready.** Report the account or messages for threats, harassment, blackmail, or intimate-image abuse. You can then block the account. If the platform offers it and blocking feels unsafe right now, you may prefer to mute or restrict first.
5. **Reach confidential support.** RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline offers confidential, 24/7 support. Ask about privacy limits before sharing identifying details.
6. **If the person being pressured or shown in sexual content may be under 18, consider contacting a safe adult.** NCMEC CyberTipline is a specialist reporting route for suspected online child sexual exploitation.
7. **Choose whether to make a report.** For adult cyber-enabled extortion or blackmail, you may use IC3. For suspected child sexual exploitation, use NCMEC CyberTipline. You do not have to decide this immediately unless someone is in immediate danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain yourself or write a perfect final message.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to report to police or another agency.
- You do not need to tell friends or family immediately if that feels unsafe.
- If you think the person may have access to one of your accounts, password changes and account checks can wait until the immediate pressure has stopped. Start with your email account.
- If an image may be shared, later options include Take It Down for images or videos taken before age 18 and StopNCII for adults who meet its criteria. Do not download or resend an image just to use a service. Check each service’s criteria first.

## Important reassurance
Pressure that ignores your no is not your fault. Feeling afraid, ashamed, frozen, or numb does not mean you caused this or have to handle it alone.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce pressure and create a safer pause. Specialist support services and reporting routes may help you consider later options.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911 or local police.
