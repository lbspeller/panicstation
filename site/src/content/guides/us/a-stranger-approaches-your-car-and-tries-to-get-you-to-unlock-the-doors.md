# What to do if… a stranger approaches your car and tries to get you to unlock the doors (USA)

## Short answer
Keep the doors locked and windows up. Move away if you can do so safely, and call 911 if the person tries the handle, blocks the car, threatens you, or you believe immediate help is needed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock the doors or step out to check what the person is pointing at.
- Do not lower the window to speak with the person.
- Do not hand over your phone or keys.
- Do not let the person direct you to another location or pressure you to open the trunk.
- Do not argue or try to physically push the person away.
- Do not drive home if you believe you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Reach the safest available pause:** keep the doors locked, keep the windows up, and stay inside the car.
2. **Create distance if you safely can:** drive away when there is a safe route. When stopped in traffic, leave room in front of the car when possible so you can move when a safe gap opens.
3. **Call 911 yourself:** tell the call-taker your location, what the person is claiming, and what the person is doing. Follow the call-taker’s instructions.
4. **Do not unlock the car to investigate:** if the person says someone is injured, your tire is flat, or there is another emergency, tell 911 and let emergency services assess it.
5. **Move toward a public place if needed:** if you believe you are being followed, keep driving toward a busy, well-lit public place while speaking with 911. Do not drive home.
6. **Use the horn only if it seems safe and useful:** a brief horn blast may draw attention while you remain inside the locked car.
7. **Prioritize your safety if the situation becomes a carjacking:** if someone threatens you or demands the vehicle, do not argue or physically resist. Get away safely if you can and call 911 as soon as it is safe.
8. **Record details after reaching safety:** note the time, location, person’s description, and any vehicle description or plate number. Save dashcam footage if available.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the emergency claim is genuine before protecting your safety.
- You do not need to investigate from inside the car.
- You do not need to post about the incident immediately.
- You do not need to decide how to make a non-emergency report until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Staying inside a locked car does not stop you from helping. Calling 911 allows emergency services to assess the claim without requiring you to unlock the doors or step outside.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation. After reaching safety, you may need to ask local law enforcement how to make a report or seek further specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
