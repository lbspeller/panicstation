# What to do if… your photo library shows optimising and your photos look missing or low-quality (USA)

## Short answer
Do not delete anything yet. Photos that look missing or low-quality are often placeholders while the device catches up, so keep the device on power and Wi-Fi and check whether the same account still shows the originals in the cloud or on another device.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete empty-looking photos, low-quality previews, albums, or apparent duplicates while the library is still optimizing or syncing.
- Do not empty Recently Deleted or Trash until you have checked that the photos you want are safely visible somewhere else.
- Do not sign out of your Apple Account or Google Account as a reset unless you have already confirmed where the originals are.
- Do not factory reset the device, restore from an older backup, or use a “cleaner” app that asks for broad photo-library access.
- Do not turn off iCloud Photos or Google Photos backup as a quick fix. If a prompt says it will remove items from this device, cancel and verify the cloud copy first.

## What to do now
1. **Stop tapping around the photo grid.**  
   Put the device down for a moment if you feel rushed. The main avoidable mistake is deleting previews or folders while trying to fix the problem.

2. **Check the common “not gone” places.**  
   - In Apple Photos, check Recently Deleted and Hidden.  
   - In Google Photos, check Trash and Archive, and check device folders if that option is available.  
   - If you use Apple Shared Library, switch between Personal Library, Shared Library, and Both Libraries if those views are available.

3. **Confirm the account is the right one.**  
   - For Apple Photos, check that the expected Apple Account is signed in and that iCloud Photos is turned on for this device.  
   - For Google Photos, tap the profile image or initial and confirm the expected Google Account is selected.  
   If you recently changed phones, changed a password, restored from backup, or signed in again, the library may still be catching up.

4. **Give the sync or backup process safe conditions.**  
   Connect to reliable Wi-Fi, plug the device into power, and keep the Photos app available for a while. On Google Photos, check the backup status. On Apple devices, check whether iCloud Photos is still syncing or whether Optimize Storage is enabled.

5. **Check where the originals are before changing settings.**  
   Look at the same photo account on another device or in the service’s web photo library. If the full-resolution photos appear there, this device may only be showing space-saving copies or temporary previews. If the photos are missing there too, stop making changes and collect details for official support.

6. **Check storage without deleting photos.**  
   Look at device storage and cloud storage. Low device storage can cause space-saving copies to appear locally, and full cloud storage can affect backup or sync. Do not start deleting photos to make space until you have confirmed what is backed up.

7. **Capture the details support will ask for.**  
   Take screenshots of the optimizing message, any exclamation marks or error icons, the backup or sync status, and device and cloud storage screens. Write down when it started and whether it followed an update, new device setup, sign-in, storage warning, or password change.

8. **Secure the account first if anything looks unfamiliar.**  
   If you see unknown signed-in devices, unfamiliar account details, password-change messages you did not request, or missing photos after suspicious activity, change the account password from the official account settings page, review signed-in devices or sessions, and turn on two-factor authentication if it is not already on.

9. **Escalate through official support.**  
   Contact Apple Support or Google Photos Help with your screenshots and notes. Tell them whether the photos appear in the web view, another device, Recently Deleted, Hidden, Trash, Archive, or device folders.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganize albums, merge duplicates, or clean storage today.
- You do not need to decide whether to switch cloud photo services right now.
- You do not need to pay for recovery software while the library still says it is optimizing, syncing, preparing backup, or waiting for Wi-Fi.
- You do not need to change every photo setting at once; one careful check at a time is safer.

## Important reassurance
Low-quality previews, missing-looking thumbnails, and long sync messages do not automatically mean the originals are gone. The safest first move is to avoid deletion, confirm the correct account, and check the cloud or another device before changing settings.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and check whether the originals still exist. If photos are missing from every checked location, later decisions may need service-specific help from Apple Support, Google Photos Help, or a reputable data-recovery service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical, or professional advice. Follow warnings shown by your device or account service, especially any prompt saying photos will be removed from a device or permanently deleted.
