# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to leave with them by claiming your friends need you (USA)

## Short answer
Do not follow them. Move to the nearest staffed, public place, contact your friends directly, and call 911 if you may be in immediate danger or cannot get away safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside, around a corner, into a parking lot, or to a quieter place to check.
- Do not let them lead you toward a vehicle, rideshare, exit, stairwell, or elevator.
- Do not hand them your phone.
- Do not argue, explain, or keep discussing it.
- Do not follow them if they walk away while telling you to hurry.
- You do not need to be polite or provide a reason for staying where you are.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the nearest staffed, public place without following them.** Go to a counter, reception desk, security desk, or open business. Stay close to other people.
2. **Say one clear sentence.** For example: “No. I’m staying here and contacting my friends directly.”
3. **Contact your friends yourself.** Call or message them through a contact method you already use. Do not rely on information passed through the person asking you to leave.
4. **Tell a specific staff member what is happening.** Say: “I do not know this person. They are trying to get me to leave. Please stay with me and contact security or call 911.”
5. **Call 911 if you may be in immediate danger, including if the person keeps pressuring you to leave, follows you, blocks you, grabs you, or you cannot get away safely.** Give your location first, then briefly describe what is happening.
6. **If you cannot safely speak, text 911 only if text-to-911 is available where you are.** Calling is usually the best option when you can speak safely. If your text is not delivered, seek another safe way to call 911 or ask staff to call.
7. **Stay with staff or other people until the person has gone and you have a safe next step.** Do not leave alone because the person says someone is waiting outside.
8. **After you are safe, write down what you remember.** Note the time, place, what was said, the person’s description, and any vehicle details you noticed without approaching or following them.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether this was a misunderstanding, an attempted abduction, or something else.
- You do not need to confront the person or collect proof while they are nearby.
- You do not need to continue your original plans.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to make a non-emergency report after you are safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to pause, stay near other people, and verify the story directly. You are allowed to refuse to leave without explaining yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps for reaching a safer pause and getting immediate help. Later decisions, including whether to make a non-emergency report, may need support from venue staff, local law enforcement, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.
