# What to do if… you receive a notice that your visa application needs more documents by a short deadline (USA)

## Short answer
Save the notice, follow its exact submission instructions, and submit only through the channel it names. Keep a complete copy and proof showing when and how you submitted it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send documents to a different system from the one named in the notice; the wrong channel may mean the agency or post does not treat them as received.
- Do not treat a USCIS Request for Evidence, an NVC/CEAC correction, and a consular 221(g) document request as the same process.
- Do not assume mailing is enough; check whether the notice says received, submitted, uploaded, couriered, or postmarked.
- Do not upload blurry photos, cropped pages, password-protected files, or files that cut off seals, stamps, signatures, names, dates, or reference numbers.
- Do not send repeated, conflicting versions without a short note explaining what changed.
- Do not ignore a deadline because you are waiting for the perfect version of one document.

## What to do now
1. **Save the notice and copy the key details.** Save a PDF or screenshot. Write down the deadline, time zone if shown, case number, requested documents, file format or mailing rules, and the exact submission channel.

2. **Identify which process the notice belongs to.** This changes the next step.
   - **USCIS Request for Evidence:** the notice should tell you how to respond and where to send or upload the response.
   - **NVC/CEAC immigrant visa document correction:** log in to CEAC, check the document slots, correct the requested items, and use the “Submit Documents” button when the portal is ready.
   - **Embassy or consulate request after interview, often 221(g):** follow the post-specific letter or instruction page for the named upload, courier, drop-off, or contact route.

3. **Make a checklist from the exact wording.** Copy each requested item into a short list and mark it as ready now, obtainable today, or waiting on a third party such as a bank, employer, school, doctor, registry, or translator.

4. **For a USCIS RFE, prepare one complete response unless the notice says otherwise.** Put the RFE notice or cover sheet first if instructed. Add a short cover note matching each request to the attached evidence. If the notice says to mail it, use a trackable delivery method. If it says to respond through a USCIS online account, use that account route and save the confirmation screen.

5. **For CEAC/NVC, upload to the correct slots before submitting.** Use clear scans, match each file to the correct person and document type, and check that the portal shows the corrected items before pressing “Submit Documents.” Save screenshots or PDFs showing the upload status and submission.

6. **For a 221(g) or consular document request, follow the letter exactly.** Use the courier, drop-off, email, webform, or upload route named by that embassy or consulate. Save receipts, tracking numbers, ticket numbers, confirmation emails, and a copy of every document submitted.

7. **If one document is delayed, submit what the instructions allow and document the delay.** For USCIS, do not assume you can add more later unless the notice or official instructions say so. For CEAC or a consular request, follow the portal or letter; if you include a note about a pending item, keep it brief and attach proof that you requested it.

8. **If a portal fails, preserve evidence of the problem.** Take screenshots showing the error, date, time, and case number if visible. Try one different browser or device. Then use the official help, inquiry, or contact route linked from the same portal or notice and save the ticket or message.

9. **If the request is unclear, ask one precise question.** Use the contact route on the notice or portal. Include your case number and ask only the question needed to submit correctly, such as whether a long-form certificate is required or whether a translation must be certified.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to reapply, change travel plans, complain, or pay for extra services.
- You do not need a long personal explanation unless the notice specifically asks for one.
- You do not need to refresh case-status pages repeatedly once you have submitted and saved proof.
- You can organise later follow-up after the immediate task is done: correct channel, requested documents, deadline proof.

## Important reassurance
A document request is not the same as a final answer. The safest immediate move is calm, exact compliance with the notice: correct channel, clear documents, and proof of submission.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need qualified immigration legal help, especially if the deadline affects your lawful status, an appeal period, travel, work authorisation, or family separation.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Deadlines and submission rules can be strict and vary by agency, post, case type, and the wording of your notice.
