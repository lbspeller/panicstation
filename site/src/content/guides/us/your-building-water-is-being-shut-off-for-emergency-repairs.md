# What to do if… your building water is being shut off for emergency repairs (USA)

## Short answer
Confirm the shut-off window, store safe water now for drinking and basic hygiene, and avoid water-fed appliances until service is stable. Follow any official boil water, do not drink, or do not use advisory until officials lift it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait until the last minute to fill clean containers; emergency repair timing can change.
- Don’t run dishwashers, washing machines, fridge water dispensers, ice makers, or other appliances connected to the water line during the shut-off.
- Don’t change water-heater settings, building valves, or shared plumbing equipment unless building management, the utility, or the manual tells you to.
- Don’t drink, cook with, brush teeth with, or make ice from tap water if an official advisory says not to.
- Don’t assume everyone in the building saw the notice; check on nearby neighbors if you can do that safely and briefly.

## What to do now
1. Confirm the basics from the water utility, city water department, landlord, or building manager. Ask for the start time, expected duration, affected areas, update route, and whether bottled water or another water point will be provided if the repair runs long.

2. Store drinking water before the shut-off starts. A practical baseline is at least 1 gallon per person per day for 3 days. Store more if you can for infants, pregnancy, illness, pets, hot weather, or anyone who needs extra water.

3. Fill only clean containers for drinking, cooking, and brushing teeth. If you have unopened bottled water, keep it for drinking and food. Do not use containers that previously held chemicals, fuel, bleach, pesticides, or cleaners.

4. Set aside non-drinking water for toilets and cleaning. Fill a bucket, basin, or bathtub part-way if that is safe in your home. Clearly keep this water away from children and pets, and do not use it for drinking.

5. Reduce water use before the shut-off. Pause laundry and dishwashing, turn off automatic ice making if you know how, and avoid starting anything that depends on a water line.

6. Make hygiene easier. Put out hand sanitizer, wipes, paper towels, trash bags, and a small amount of stored water for handwashing if sanitizer is not enough.

7. Make food simple. Use ready-to-eat food, food that does not need washing, or meals that use very little water. Keep clean cups, plates, or disposable items available if washing dishes will be difficult.

8. If anyone has higher water needs, tell building management now. This includes infants, pregnancy, illness, mobility limits, pets, or water-dependent medical equipment. For medical equipment, contact the device supplier, clinician, or care team for situation-specific instructions.

9. If you rent, make a simple record. Save the notice, take a screenshot or photo, and send one brief written message to the landlord or building manager confirming the shut-off window and asking how updates will be shared if it runs long.

10. When service returns, use a cold tap first. Run it for a few minutes, or for the time your utility or building gives, until the flow is steady. If the water stays cloudy, brown, gritty, has an unusual smell, or pressure is still very low, stop using it for drinking and contact the utility or building manager.

11. If there is any drinking-water advisory, follow the exact advisory. During a boil water advisory, use bottled or properly boiled water for drinking, cooking, brushing teeth, and pets. During a do not drink advisory, use bottled water for drinking and cooking because boiling may not make the water safe. During a do not use advisory, do not use tap water for any purpose until officials say it is safe.

12. If the outage becomes prolonged in a rental, ask local officials what applies where you live. Running water and habitability rules vary by state and city. Contact local code enforcement, the local health department, local legal aid, or 211 for help finding the right local service.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about rent, legal action, moving, or complaints.
- You do not need to buy special emergency equipment if you already have clean containers, drinking water, and hygiene basics.
- You do not need to flush every fixture immediately; start with one cold tap once service is stable or follow the utility’s instructions.
- You do not need to solve the whole building problem yourself; focus on safe water, hygiene, updates, and a simple record.

## Important reassurance
A sudden water shut-off notice can feel urgent because it interrupts basic needs. Storing safe water, avoiding water-fed appliances, and following official advisories are enough to get through the first stage without making rushed decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the first hours to a day. Later decisions about tenant rights, repairs, reimbursement, health concerns, or complaints may need local officials, legal aid, a clinician, the utility, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, plumbing, or other professional advice. Water safety instructions and tenant protections vary by location; follow your local utility, public health department, building department, and emergency officials.
