# What to do if… your electric vehicle will not accept a charge even though the charging station appears to be working (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the session in your app or on the screen, unplug the connector, wait 30 seconds, and re-insert it firmly until it clicks to reset the communication handshake. If it fails again, use the station's support number to request a remote reset or move to a different stall.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force a connector that feels stuck or jam objects into the charging port to improve contact.
- Do not attempt to use a charger if you see exposed wires, soot, melted plastic, or smell burning.
- Do not keep re-initiating a failed session more than three times, as this can trigger a software lockout on the vehicle or station.
- Do not use third-party, non-OEM-approved adapters for DC fast charging (CCS to NACS).
- Do not leave the vehicle unattended until you have confirmed power is actually flowing (kW increasing).

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause away from moving traffic.** Ensure your vehicle is fully in Park and you have a clear space to walk around the charging port without risk from other drivers.
2. **Identify immediate sensory hazards.** Check the connector and cable for unusual heat, sparks, hissing sounds, or a smell of ozone / burning plastic.
   - If active fire or smoke is present, move at least 50 feet away and call **911**.
   - If equipment is damaged but not smoking, do not touch it; move to a different stall.
3. **Perform a clean hardware reconnect.** 
   - End the session via the app or station "Stop" button.
   - Unplug the connector completely.
   - **Wait 30 seconds** for the station and vehicle capacitors to discharge.
   - Re-insert the plug firmly, supporting the weight of the cable until you hear the mechanical latch click.
4. **Check for vehicle-side software inhibitors.** 
   - Ensure the vehicle is **unlocked** (many models lock the port during handshake).
   - Check the dashboard for "Charge Limit Reached," "Scheduled Charging," or "Battery Cold / Hot" messages.
   - Disable any departure timers or climate pre-conditioning that might interfere with the initial handshake.
5. **Verify payment and network authorization.** 
   - Confirm the charger ID in your app matches the number on the physical unit.
   - If using a Tesla Supercharger with a non-Tesla, ensure you have selected "Charge Your Other EV" in the Tesla app and your vehicle model is correctly configured.
   - Try a different payment method (RFID card vs. App swipe vs. Credit Card reader).
6. **Attempt a single stall swap to isolate the fault.** If the first stall fails after a reset, move to the adjacent stall; individual charging modules often fail while the rest of the site remains functional.
7. **Contact the network support line listed on the unit.** 
   - Provide the Station ID and the specific error code shown on the screen.
   - Ask the operator to perform a **Remote Reboot** of the stall.
   - Request a "Remote Start" if the payment system appears to be the bottleneck.
8. **Preserve your remaining range while troubleshooting.** Turn off the cabin heater or air conditioning and heated seats while the car is stationary to save battery for a potential move to a nearby Level 2 charger.
9. **Request specialized EV roadside assistance if range is critical.** If you cannot initiate a charge at two different stalls and have less than 10 miles of range, call your manufacturer's roadside line or insurance provider. 
   - Explicitly request a **flatbed tow truck**; spinning the wheels of a disabled EV during a traditional tow can cause permanent motor damage.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the onboard charger (OBC) or the station's inverter is at fault right now.
- You do not need to resolve billing disputes or "pending" authorization holds on your bank account immediately.
- You can wait until you are at a working charger to report the broken equipment via the network's mobile app.

## Important reassurance
Charging "handshake" failures are common and often caused by minor timing errors in the software or a slightly misaligned plug. In most cases, a single stall swap or a remote reset from the network operator resolves the issue without needing a mechanic.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and troubleshooting for public charging failures only. It does not cover home wall-box repairs or internal vehicle diagnostics.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and does not replace the safety procedures found in your vehicle's owner manual or the charging station's official terms of service. High-voltage DC charging involves significant electrical current; if you observe any physical damage to the equipment or experience a "Service High Voltage System" error on your dashboard, stop all charging attempts immediately and seek professional assistance from an authorized service center.
