# What to do if… your steering wheel lock will not release and the car will not start (USA)

## Short answer
First, make sure you are not staying in a dangerous traffic position to troubleshoot. Once you have a safe pause, use gentle, steady pressure on the steering wheel in the direction it will move slightly while turning the key, or while pressing Start with the brake held down.

## Do not do these things
- Do not yank the steering wheel hard or bounce it repeatedly.
- Do not use pliers, tools, or extra leverage on the key.
- Do not keep twisting the key or pressing Start aggressively.
- Do not take apart the steering column or ignition at the roadside.
- Do not stand near moving traffic while trying to fix the car.
- Do not keep forcing it after a few calm attempts, because that can make a key, lock, or ignition problem worse.

## What to do now
1. **Make the scene safer first.**
   - Turn on your hazard lights.
   - If the car is stopped in a live lane, blocking traffic, or you feel in immediate danger, call 911.
   - If you are already in a safer place, stay out of traffic and keep other people away from the road side of the vehicle.

2. **Try the steering-lock release gently.**
   - Insert the key fully, or make sure the fob is inside the vehicle.
   - Move the steering wheel slowly left and right until you find the side with a small amount of movement.
   - Hold steady pressure toward that side while turning the key toward ON or START.
   - If the key does not turn, stop, breathe, and try the other direction once.

3. **For push-button start, include the brake pedal.**
   - Press and hold the brake pedal firmly.
   - While holding the brake, apply the same gentle steering-wheel pressure and press Start.
   - If the dash says the fob is not detected, keep the fob inside the vehicle and move to the backup fob step below.

4. **Check the simple start interlocks.**
   - For an automatic, make sure the shifter is fully in Park.
   - If it still will not start, hold the brake and try moving the shifter to Neutral, then back to Park.
   - For a manual, put the shifter in Neutral and press the clutch fully if your vehicle requires it.

5. **If it is keyless, use the model-specific backup fob method.**
   - Many vehicles have a backup way to read a weak fob, such as holding the fob near the Start button, a marked spot, or a tray.
   - Check the owner’s manual, quick-start card, or the manufacturer’s app or support page for your exact model.
   - Try the steering-wheel pressure again while using that backup fob position.

6. **Try a spare key if you have one with you.**
   - If the spare key works, stop using the key that failed.
   - A worn, bent, or damaged key can make the ignition bind and may break if forced.

7. **If it still will not release, stop trying and get help.**
   - Call roadside assistance, your insurer’s roadside line, an automotive locksmith, or a tow service.
   - Say: “The steering wheel lock is engaged and the key will not turn,” or “The steering lock will not release on push-button start.”
   - Give your exact location, whether you are in a safe place, and whether the vehicle is key-start or push-button.
   - Ask whether a mobile automotive locksmith is suitable or whether the vehicle needs towing to a repair shop.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is the key, ignition cylinder, steering lock, fob battery, vehicle battery, shifter switch, or immobilizer right now.
- You do not need to buy parts while stranded.
- You do not need to attempt roadside disassembly.
- You do not need to decide which repair shop to use until you and the vehicle are in a safer situation.

## Important reassurance
A steering wheel lock can bind when the wheel is turned after the engine is off, especially if the front wheels are against tension. The useful move is usually steady, gentle pressure while starting, not more force.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk, avoid damage, and get the car safely unstuck or moved. If it happens again, a mechanic, dealer, or automotive locksmith may need to inspect the key, ignition cylinder, steering lock, shifter interlock, fob system, or battery system.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate risk reduction and safer next steps, not legal, medical, financial, mechanical, or other professional advice. If you are stranded in a dangerous location, prioritize personal safety and contact emergency services.
