# What to do if… you hear repeated knocking at your hotel room door late at night but it stops whenever you speak through the door (USA)

## Short answer
Do not open the door. Ensure the deadbolt and security latch are engaged, step away from the doorway, and use the room phone to call the front desk to verify the visitor or request an immediate security sweep.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door even slightly "just to look" or speak to the person face-to-face.
- Do not disengage the deadbolt or swing bar while listening or looking through the peephole.
- Do not tell the person through the door that you are alone.
- Do not go out into the hallway to investigate or follow the person.
- Do not rely on a person's verbal claim of being "staff" or "security" without independent verification from the front desk.

## What to do now
1. **Engage all door locks immediately.** Ensure the deadbolt is turned and the security swing bar or privacy latch is fully flipped into the locked position.
2. **Move away from the door area.** Stand in a part of the room where you are not visible through the peephole or any gaps in the door frame.
3. **Turn on the main room lights.** This indicates the room is occupied and allows you to see clearly; do not remain in the dark.
4. **Use the hotel room phone to call the front desk.** **Prioritize the room phone over your mobile** because, under US law (Kari’s Law), dialing 911 or the front desk from a room phone automatically provides the hotel and emergency services with your exact room number.
   - Tell the operator: "I have repeated knocking at my door (Room [Number]) that stops when I speak. I am not opening the door."
   - Ask: "Did you send a staff member to this room?"
5. **Request a security check of the hallway.** Ask the front desk to send a security officer or manager to the floor to investigate the knocking and remain on the line with you until they arrive.
6. **Verify staff through the phone line.** If a person at the door claims to be staff, ask the front desk operator while you are still on the line: "Is there a staff member named [Name] currently at my door?" If the desk cannot confirm this, do not open the door.
7. **Call 911 if the situation escalates.** **Dial 911 directly from the room phone** if you observe any of the following:
   - The door handle is being turned or shaken.
   - You hear the sound of tools or objects being inserted into the lock.
   - The person begins shouting or making threats.
   - You hear multiple people whispering or moving aggressively outside.
8. **Request a room move if you feel the room is compromised.** If the person was seen tampering with your specific lock, ask the manager to move you to a different floor or a room closer to the elevators / lobby.
9. **Keep your essentials within reach.** Place your shoes, wallet, mobile phone, and car keys on the bed or a nearby table in case you are instructed to evacuate or move rooms by hotel security.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to check out or ask for a refund until the morning.
- You do not need to identify the person yourself; that is the responsibility of hotel security and police.
- You do not need to file a formal written report immediately unless law enforcement is on-site.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled or anxious by unexpected knocking at night. Patterned knocking that ceases upon verbal response is a known "room testing" behavior, and your decision to keep the door locked and seek verification is the standard safety protocol for this situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspicious visitor scenario. It does not cover long-term legal actions or hotel liability procedures.

## Important note
Emergency calls from US hotel rooms do not require a prefix (like dialing 9) to reach 911. Using the room phone ensures the front desk is simultaneously notified of the emergency call so they can assist first responders in reaching your room.
