# What to do if… you realise your travel profile saved the wrong passport number and you already checked in (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the airline immediately to request a manual update of your Advance Passenger Information System (APIS) data and proceed to a staffed airport counter for document verification. Do not rely on a digital boarding pass that contains incorrect data, as it may cause a denial at the gate or during boarding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume that updating your "Saved Profile" or "Frequent Flyer Account" automatically pushes the change to your active reservation.
- Do not cancel your ticket or attempt to rebook; this often triggers non-refundable fees and does not guarantee the new reservation will be correct.
- Do not keep attempting to "Edit Travel Documents" in the app if it returns an error message or fails to save.
- Do not ignore "See Agent," "DOCS REQUIRED," or "INTL VERIFICATION" flags on your digital boarding pass.
- Do not wait until you are at the boarding gate to mention the error; corrections must be made at the check-in desk to ensure the manifest is legally compliant.

## What to do now
1. **Identify exactly which data fields are incorrect by reviewing your "Manage Trip" section.**
   - **APIS Data**: Passport number, issuing country, expiration date, and nationality.
   - **Secure Flight Data**: Legal name, date of birth, and gender (required by TSA).
2. **Attempt one manual update through the airline's official "Manage Reservation" tool.**
   - Navigate to the travel documents or passenger details section.
   - Enter the correct details and click save; if the system allows the change after check-in, it will usually trigger a "boarding pass update" notification.
3. **Contact the airline via official chat or phone if the online system is locked.**
   - State clearly: "I have already checked in, but my APIS passport number is incorrect and the app will not let me edit it. Please update my reservation record manually."
   - Confirm that the Secure Flight data (Name / DOB) matches exactly, as this is separate from the passport number.
4. **Arrive at the airport at least three hours before an international flight and go to a staffed counter.**
   - Even if you have a digital boarding pass, ask the agent to "swipe" your physical passport to override the incorrect manifest data.
   - Request a printed boarding pass once the agent confirms the record matches your document.
5. **Verify that any required entry permissions (ESTA, Visa, or ETA) match the passport you are holding.**
   - Note that an ESTA or ETA is digitally linked to a specific passport number; if the number is wrong on the permission itself, you must apply for a new one immediately.
   - Airline agents can fix the reservation record, but they cannot "fix" a government-issued entry permit.
6. **Ensure you meet the 2026 REAL ID requirements if your trip includes a domestic US connection.**
   - If you do not have a REAL ID-compliant license, your passport must be used for all security checkpoints, including domestic legs.

## What can wait
- You do not need to synchronize your saved traveler profile today; the priority is the specific reservation for your current flight.
- You do not need to troubleshoot the technical reason why the app failed or saved old data.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or request a refund for the "mistake" while still at the airport.

## Important reassurance
Passport number typos and outdated document data are among the most common administrative issues handled by airline agents. Because international regulations require a physical document check at the airport anyway, agents have the tools and authority to correct these details quickly once you present your physical passport.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for administrative travel document errors. It does not cover situations involving expired passports, name changes due to legal status, or denied entry for criminal or security reasons.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or immigration advice. Airline policies and government entry requirements (CBP/TSA) are subject to change. Always follow the direct instructions of authorized airline agents and border officials.
