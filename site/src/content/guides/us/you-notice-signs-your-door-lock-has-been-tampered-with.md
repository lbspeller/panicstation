# What to do if… you notice signs your door lock has been tampered with (USA)

## Short answer
Do not go inside. Move to a safer place nearby and call 911 if there is any chance someone could be inside, you see another open entry point, or you are unsure whether this is an immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter “just to check,” even if the door still seems locked.
- Do not keep trying your key, force the handle, or attempt a quick repair right now.
- Do not call out through the door or confront anyone you cannot see.
- Do not touch the lock, handle, or doorframe more than you already have.
- Do not clean up or move items if you have already gone inside.
- Do not post publicly that your home may be empty or unsecured.

## What to do now
1. **Back away from the door.** Move far enough that you are not standing at the entrance or blocking a possible exit.
2. **Look only from a safe distance.** Notice anything visible without moving closer: an open door or window, disturbed screen, damaged frame, unusual noises, lights changed, curtains moved, or a door that is not sitting normally.
3. **Move to a safer place to make calls.** Use a trusted neighbor’s home, a nearby public place, or your locked car if that is safer than staying at the door.
4. **Call for help using the safest route.**
   - Call 911 if someone may be inside, you see an open entry point, you heard movement, you feel unsafe, or you are unsure what is happening.
   - If it is clearly not urgent and there is no sign of anyone inside, contact your local police non-emergency number.
   Be ready to give your address, what looks tampered with, when you last left, whether anyone should be inside, and where you are waiting.
5. **Wait outside until law enforcement or the dispatcher tells you what to do next.** Stay somewhere secure. If you can see the entrance from a safe place, observe without making yourself obvious or moving closer.
6. **Preserve the scene.** Do not touch the lock, door, doorframe, windows, or anything inside. If you can do it from where you already are and it does not delay help, take a few photos, then stop.
7. **If you rent or live in managed housing, notify the landlord, property manager, or building security after you have contacted police.** Ask how emergency lock changes and authorised access are handled.
8. **If you already went inside and now feel unsure, leave immediately.** Go back to a safer place and call 911.
9. **Ask for the incident or report number.** Write it down so you can refer to it later if you need repairs, insurance, or follow-up contact.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now about replacing locks, adding cameras, changing routines, or making long-term security upgrades.
- You do not need to inventory belongings immediately if the scene has not been cleared.
- You do not need to call a locksmith until you are safe and police or the dispatcher has told you what to do next.
- Insurance calls, landlord follow-up details, and longer-term security choices can wait until immediate safety is addressed.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat possible lock tampering as a safety issue first. Even if it later turns out to be ordinary wear, a failed lock, or maintenance damage, stepping away and getting help is a calm protective choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate moment only. Later decisions about repairs, reports, insurance, housing access, or security upgrades may need help from local law enforcement, your landlord or property manager, a locksmith, an insurer, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you think you may be in danger, call 911.
