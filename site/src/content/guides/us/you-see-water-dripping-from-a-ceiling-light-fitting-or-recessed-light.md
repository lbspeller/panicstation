# What to do if… you see water dripping from a ceiling light fitting or recessed light (USA)

## Short answer
Treat this as an immediate electrical and fire hazard. Move away from the fixture and shut off the main electrical breaker safely to isolate the circuit, then stop the water source.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the light fixture, the bulb, or the dripping water while the power is on.
- Do not use a ladder to inspect the leak or stand on wet flooring near the fixture.
- Do not attempt to "drain" the water by poking holes in the ceiling or pulling at the light.
- Do not toggle the light switch to see if it still works.
- Do not reset the breaker if it has already tripped.
- Do not assume the area is safe because the light is still functioning.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the immediate area if you see smoke or sparks.** If you observe a burning smell, visible smoke, active sparking, or if the ceiling is visibly sagging or bulging, leave the room immediately and call 911.
2. **Shut off the main electrical power at the breaker panel.** Locate your electrical service panel and flip the main breaker to the OFF position.
   - If you can safely identify the specific branch circuit for that room, you may flip that individual breaker off, but the main breaker is the most certain way to ensure the area is de-energized.
   - Do not touch the panel if you must stand in water or if the panel itself is wet.
3. **Contact a licensed electrician immediately.** An electrician must inspect the fixture, junction box, and internal wiring for water damage and corrosion before the power is restored.
4. **Identify and stop the water source if it is safe to do so.**
   - If the leak is caused by a plumbing fixture directly above (such as a bathtub or sink), shut off the local supply valve or the main water shutoff for the house.
   - If you are in a multi-unit building, alert the neighbors above you and contact building maintenance to stop the water flow.
5. **Contain the water from a distance.** Once the power is confirmed to be off, place a bucket or container on the floor under the drip to catch falling water.
6. **Alert your insurance provider or landlord.** Provide a factual account of the leak once the immediate electrical hazard is neutralized and the water flow has been stopped.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact plumbing pipe or roof defect immediately.
- You do not need to clean up the water or dry the ceiling until an electrician has confirmed the area is safe.
- You do not need to decide on permanent repairs or fixture replacements during the initial crisis.
- You do not need to document the full extent of property damage until the area is stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Seeing water interact with electricity is a high-stress event, but taking immediate steps to isolate the power effectively manages the primary danger. It is common to feel hesitant or confused about which breaker to pull; shutting off the main breaker is a standard, safe response that simplifies the process and provides immediate protection.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Professional electrical and plumbing inspections are required to assess hidden damage within the ceiling and electrical components.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace the advice of a qualified professional. If you cannot safely access your electrical panel or if you suspect a fire, evacuate and contact emergency services.
