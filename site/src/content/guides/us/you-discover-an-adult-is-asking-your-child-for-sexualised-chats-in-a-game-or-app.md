# What to do if… you discover an adult is asking your child for sexualised chats in a game or app (USA)

## Short answer
Separate your child from the device, reassure them that they are not in trouble, and preserve the messages as evidence without deleting them. Immediately report the contact to the NCMEC CyberTipline or the Know2Protect Tipline.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete the messages, images, or the app until you have documented the evidence.
- Do not message the person yourself to confront, threaten, or "bait" them.
- Do not blame, shame, or punish your child for the conversation or for responding.
- Do not ask your child to "show you exactly what happened" repeatedly, as this can be re-traumatizing.
- Do not pay any money or send more images if the person is threatening to leak information (extortion).
- Do not share screenshots of the messages or the child's identity on social media or with other parents.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the device and end the active contact immediately.** Take the phone, tablet, or computer away from the child to stop the interaction, but do not turn it off or close the app if the messages might disappear.
2. **Provide immediate emotional reassurance to your child.** Tell them clearly that this situation is not their fault, they are not in trouble, and you are glad they are safe.
3. **Determine the immediate scope of the contact through 2-3 specific questions.**
   - Did the person ask for images, live video, or an in-person meeting?
   - Does the person know your child's real name, address, or school?
   - Has this person contacted them on other platforms or apps?
4. **Document the evidence before it is deleted or hidden.** Take clear photos of the screen using a different device if possible, or take screenshots that capture:
   - The perpetrator’s username and profile ID.
   - The platform or game name.
   - The specific sexualized or enticing messages.
   - Any timestamps or dates shown in the chat.
5. **Report the incident to the appropriate federal authorities.**
   - Contact the NCMEC CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org or call 1-800-843-5678.
   - Call the DHS Know2Protect Tipline at 833-591-KNOW (5669).
6. **Call 911 immediately if there is a direct threat of physical harm.** If the person claims to be nearby, is threatening to come to your home, or is demanding an immediate meeting, treat it as a physical emergency.
7. **Use platform tools to block and report the user.** After documenting the evidence, use the "Report" function within the game or app to alert the developers to "Child Sexual Exploitation" or "Grooming," then block the account.
8. **Use "Take It Down" if sexual images were shared.** If your child shared explicit images, visit takeitdown.ncmec.org to help prevent the spread of those images online.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on permanent "tech bans" or new house rules tonight.
- You do not need to notify other parents or the school until you have spoken with law enforcement or a specialist.
- You do not need to investigate the perpetrator's real identity or location yourself.
- You do not need to understand "why" your child responded or engaged in the chat right now.

## Important reassurance
Online predators are highly skilled at manipulation, often using flattery, gifts, or "shared secrets" to bypass a child's natural caution. Your child’s involvement does not mean they were "bad" or that you failed as a parent; it means an adult chose to target them. Staying calm and supportive now is the most effective way to ensure your child continues to come to you for help.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and reporting for online enticement in the USA. It does not replace the need for professional legal, psychological, or investigative support.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute legal or clinical advice. Procedures for handling digital evidence and reporting can change; always follow the direct instructions provided by law enforcement or NCMEC during the reporting process.
