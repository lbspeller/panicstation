# What to do if… you collect a rental vehicle and notice damage that is not on the handover record (USA)
## Short answer
Where it is safe and practical, document the damage before driving away and ask the rental company to update the condition report or acknowledge your report in writing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive away without documenting the mismatch where it is safe and practical.
- Do not sign or initial a condition report that you know is incomplete.
- Do not rely only on a close-up photo; include wider photos that show where the damage is on the vehicle.
- Do not edit or overwrite your original photos or videos.
- Do not let a verbal reassurance replace a written record.

## What to do now
1. **Pause somewhere safe at the pickup location.** If stopping there is not safe or practical, move to the nearest safe place.
2. **Take clear photos and a short video.**
   - Photograph each side of the vehicle and the license plate.
   - Take close-up photos of each unrecorded scratch, dent, chip, crack, or scuff.
   - Take a slow walkaround video. Include the license plate and, where easy, the odometer and fuel gauge.
3. **Check the handover record.**
   - Compare the damage with the paper condition report, app record, or checkout sheet.
   - Write down each missing item in plain language, such as `rear bumper passenger-side scuff`.
4. **Ask the rental company to correct the record.**
   - Ask the agent to add the missing damage and give you an updated copy.
   - If the agent cannot do this, ask for a manager or another staff member who can help.
5. **Create a written time-stamped record before leaving where possible.**
   - Ask the branch to acknowledge in writing that you reported the listed damage before departure.
   - If staff cannot send a written acknowledgement, send your own email or in-app message to the branch or customer support while you are still on-site. Attach the photos and ask them to confirm receipt.
6. **Keep the records together.**
   - Save the rental agreement and the condition report.
   - Keep the original photos and video.
   - Save your written message, any reply, and the name of the staff member you spoke with.
7. **If you already drove away, act at the first safe opportunity.**
   - Stop only when safe.
   - Take the same photos and video.
   - Contact the rental location or customer support promptly in writing and explain when you noticed the damage.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who is responsible while you are collecting the vehicle.
- You do not need to argue about insurance coverage at the pickup desk.
- You do not need to decide whether to dispute a charge unless a charge later appears.
- You do not need to escalate outside the rental company now if the record is corrected.

## Important reassurance
The immediate task is only to create a clear, time-stamped record and ask for the handover check to be corrected. You do not need to settle responsibility or argue about a future charge at pickup.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the pickup moment. If a damage charge later appears, use the rental company’s written dispute process promptly and keep your records together. A later dispute may need specialist help. If the charge appears on a credit-card statement, check the card issuer’s instructions promptly: deadlines may apply, and the credit-card billing-error process may not cover every rental dispute.

## Important note
This guide provides general information for immediate record-keeping. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Rental-company policies and state rules can vary.
