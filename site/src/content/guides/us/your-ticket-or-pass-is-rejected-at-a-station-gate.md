# What to do if… your ticket or pass is rejected at a station gate (USA)

## Short answer
Do not jump the gate or follow someone through. Step aside with your ticket, pass, card, or phone ready, then ask a station agent or use an available assistance intercom and follow the instructions given.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hop the turnstile, force a gate, or follow closely behind another rider.
- Do not use a marked emergency exit merely to bypass the fare gate, unless there is an emergency or staff direct you to use it.
- Do not throw away the ticket, discard the card, or delete the mobile ticket.
- Do not block the gate while trying repeatedly.
- Do not rush to buy another fare before checking posted instructions or asking for assistance. If you choose to buy another fare, use an official machine, website, or app and keep the receipt.

## What to do now
1. **Step aside from the gate.** Keep the ticket, pass, card, or phone with you so other riders can pass.
2. **Try one careful retry if it is safe to do so.**
   - If another open fare gate is available, try it once.
   - For a mobile barcode, keep the barcode visible and follow any instructions shown in the app or at the scanner.
   - For a tap card or phone wallet, present only the payment method you intend to use and keep other cards away from the reader.
3. **Look for station assistance.** Ask a station agent if one is present. If there is an assistance intercom or Help Point near the gates, use it and say that your valid ticket or pass is being rejected.
4. **If the problem is at exit, say that first.** Tell the station agent or intercom operator that you are inside the paid area and the exit gate will not accept your ticket or pass. Follow the instructions given.
5. **Ask for the next local step.** Keep it simple: “My ticket or pass is being rejected. Could you check it and tell me what to do next?”
6. **Keep a brief note for later.** Record the transit agency, station, approximate time, gate location, and any message shown by the reader.
7. **If several gates appear affected, follow posted instructions or staff directions.** Do not assume that a gate is safe to bypass.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve a refund, account review, replacement card, or formal complaint while standing at the gate.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to buy a replacement fare.
- You can deal with any account or receipt issue after you have moved out of the flow of passengers and received the station-specific instructions.

## Important reassurance
A rejected ticket or pass does not by itself mean that you did anything wrong. The next step may depend on the transit agency, the station, the type of fare media, and the message shown by the reader.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the problem continues, the transit agency or fare-card provider may need to review the ticket, pass, card, or account.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Transit procedures vary by agency, station, fare system, and staffing level. Follow posted instructions and the directions given by transit staff.
