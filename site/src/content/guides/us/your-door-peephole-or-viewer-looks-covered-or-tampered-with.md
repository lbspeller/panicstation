# What to do if… your door peephole or viewer looks covered or tampered with (USA)

## Short answer
Do not open the door. Stay locked inside, move away from the direct line of the door, and check what is outside using a safer method or by getting help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to check, especially if you cannot clearly identify who is there.
- Do not stand directly in front of the door while you inspect the peephole or viewer.
- Do not argue through the door, give personal details, or announce that you are alone.
- Do not go into the hallway, porch, or outside area by yourself if you feel uneasy.
- Do not touch or remove the covering unless you need to do so for immediate safety. If you already removed it, set it aside and avoid handling it further.

## What to do now
1. Lock the door fully. Use the deadbolt if you have one, keep your phone with you, and stand to the side of the door rather than directly in front of it.
2. Listen for urgent cues. If someone is trying the handle, forcing the door, making threats, or refusing to leave, call 911.
3. Check outside without opening the door. Use a doorbell camera, security camera, a safe window angle, or a phone camera from inside if you can do that without exposing yourself.
4. If you are in an apartment, dorm, hotel, or managed building, call the front desk, building security, management, or a trusted neighbor and ask them to check the hallway or outside area.
5. If you feel threatened or think a crime may be in progress, call 911. Say that you are inside, your door peephole or viewer appears covered or tampered with, and you are concerned someone may be outside your door.
6. If it does not feel like an active emergency, use your local police department’s non-emergency phone number or online reporting option and report suspicious activity or possible tampering.
7. While staying inside, take a photo or short video of the covered or changed viewer from your side of the door. Note the date, time, sounds, knocks, handle movement, or anything you saw on camera.
8. Treat the viewer as a repair and security issue. If you rent, contact your landlord or property manager and ask for repair or replacement. If you are in a hotel, ask staff for help and request a different room if you do not feel safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether this was a prank, vandalism, a mistake, or something more serious.
- You do not need to confront anyone, open the door to prove what happened, or go outside alone.
- You can think later about repairs, a new viewer, lighting, cameras, or other security changes once the immediate situation is calm.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to pause and treat a covered or tampered peephole as a safety concern. Staying inside, checking another way, and asking for help are calm protective steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If this happens again, if there is damage, or if you are being harassed, later decisions may need help from local police, building management, a landlord-tenant resource, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, law-enforcement, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime is happening, call 911.
