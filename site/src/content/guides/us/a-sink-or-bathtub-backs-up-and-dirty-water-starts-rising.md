# What to do if… a sink or bathtub backs up and dirty water starts rising (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using water in the home immediately and keep people and pets away from the dirty water. If water is near outlets, appliances, switches, or the electrical panel, do not enter the water or operate switches while standing in water; contact qualified help from a safe location.

## Do not do these things
- Do not run taps, flush toilets, use the garbage disposal, or run the dishwasher or washing machine to test whether the drain clears.
- Do not step into standing water if it is near outlets, appliances, power strips, switches, or the electrical panel.
- Do not pour drain cleaner, bleach, or other chemicals into a backed-up drain while dirty water is rising.
- Do not try to stop the flow through a drain while dirty water is rising.
- Do not let children or pets enter the affected area.
- Do not start cleanup until the flow has stopped and the area is electrically safe.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all household water use.** Do not use sinks, showers, tubs, toilets, the garbage disposal, the dishwasher, or the washing machine.
2. **Keep people and pets away.** Close the door or block access. Open a window for ventilation only if you can do so safely. Do not use fans or other electrical equipment until the area is electrically safe.
3. **Handle electrical risk from a dry location.** If you can safely switch off the affected circuit or main power without entering water, do so. If standing water blocks safe access, call an electrician. Leave the affected area and call 911 from a safe location for immediate danger such as fire or sparking.
4. **Note what is backing up.** Check from a safe position whether the problem is limited to one fixture or involves several drains, a toilet bubbling, or water rising from the lowest drain. This information will help the person you call.
5. **Contact the right help.**
   - Call a plumber or drain and sewer contractor.
   - If several drains are backing up, water is rising from a low drain, or water continues rising when nobody is using water, check your local wastewater utility or public works emergency reporting instructions. Report the backup if directed.
   - If the home uses a septic system, contact a septic service provider.
   - If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager and use the emergency maintenance route.
6. **Document the situation only from a safe position.** Take photos or video for your landlord or insurer if you can do so without entering the affected area.
7. **Treat affected water and materials as potentially contaminated.** Leave cleanup and disposal decisions until the flow is stopped and qualified help has advised what needs cleaning, drying, or removal.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the blockage is in a fixture, your drain line, a public sewer, or a septic system before making the first call.
- You do not need to start cleanup or decide what to discard while water is still rising.
- You do not need to make repair or insurance decisions immediately. A few safe photos and the first calls are enough for now.

## Important reassurance
This is unpleasant and stressful, but the first steps are manageable: stop adding water, keep people away from contact, avoid electrical risk, and call the appropriate help.

## Scope note
These are immediate first steps only. Cleanup, drying, repairs, and responsibility for the blockage may need assessment by a plumber, sewer utility, septic service provider, property manager, insurer, or cleanup professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, financial, electrical, plumbing, insurance, or other professional advice. Dirty drain water may contain germs, and electricity around standing water can be dangerous.
