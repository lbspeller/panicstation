# What to do if… you receive a notice to surrender to custody to begin a sentence (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the surrender instructions through your attorney or the sentencing court, then follow the confirmed instructions exactly. If you cannot attend on the date, contact your attorney or the court immediately; missing it can trigger a warrant or additional consequences.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the notice or assume the date can be changed without court permission.
- Do not rely only on phone numbers, email addresses, QR codes, or links on a notice that seems suspicious; verify contact details independently through an official court or agency website.
- Do not travel to a facility if the date, time, address, entrance, or reporting authority is unclear.
- Do not bring weapons, alcohol, non-prescribed drugs, electronics, large bags, cash beyond what the facility permits, or extra property unless the facility has clearly said those items are allowed.
- Do not post your surrender date, travel plan, or facility details online.
- Do not ask court staff for legal strategy; ask only how to verify the order, contact the correct office, or find the docket entry.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm who issued the notice.**
   - If you have an attorney, contact them first and ask them to verify the surrender date, time, location, and authority.
   - If you do not have an attorney, contact the clerk of the sentencing court using the court’s official website or official phone listing. Ask how to confirm that the surrender notice is genuine and current.
   - Keep the notice, envelope, emails, texts, and any court papers together.

2. **Write down the exact reporting instructions.**
   - Confirm the date, time, full address, entrance, required ID, and whether you must report to a court, a U.S. Marshals Service office, a Bureau of Prisons institution, a county jail, a sheriff’s office, or another named facility.
   - For a federal Bureau of Prisons institution, use the official BOP locations page to confirm the facility phone number and address before you call.
   - Ask whether the facility has a voluntary surrender instruction sheet, intake entrance, parking restriction, or property rule you must follow.

3. **If anything does not match, pause and verify before travelling.**
   - Check whether the name, case number, date, facility, and sentencing court match your court papers.
   - If the notice names a facility different from the one you expected, ask your attorney or the sentencing court to confirm it before you go.
   - If you receive a last-minute call, text, or payment demand about surrender, verify it through official contact details before responding.

4. **If you cannot make the date, act now rather than missing it.**
   - Contact your attorney immediately, or contact the sentencing court and ask what the court requires.
   - If you are supervised by pretrial services, probation, parole, or another officer, contact that officer as well unless your attorney tells you otherwise.
   - Keep proof of the emergency, such as hospital paperwork, travel cancellation records, or other dated documents.

5. **Prepare only the essentials for intake.**
   - Bring government-issued photo ID, the surrender notice or court order, and any judgment, commitment, designation, or reporting papers you were given.
   - Write important phone numbers, addresses, medication details, and emergency contacts on paper.
   - Bring a printed medication list with medication names, doses, prescribers, and pharmacy details. If you bring medication, keep it in original labelled packaging if you have it and declare it at intake; facilities usually will not let you keep personal medications yourself.
   - Bring only property the facility has confirmed is allowed.

6. **Handle the next few days, not your whole life.**
   - Arrange immediate childcare, pet care, and transport to the surrender location.
   - Lock up your home, secure urgent documents, and give one trusted person the information they need to handle immediate issues.
   - Leave valuables, electronics, extra cards, and non-essential belongings with a trusted person before you go.

7. **Arrive early and follow the confirmed route.**
   - Allow extra time for traffic, security screening, check-in, and intake.
   - Bring the papers you verified, follow staff instructions calmly, and say clearly that you are there for a scheduled surrender.
   - If you are delayed on the way, contact your attorney or the confirmed official contact as soon as safely possible.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve classification, housing, commissary, programming, appeals, or long-term finances in the first panic hours.
- You do not need to tell everyone. Tell only the people needed for transport, care duties, urgent bills, or legal communication.
- You do not need to make irreversible decisions such as selling a car, ending a lease, or resigning from a job unless a real deadline requires action today.

## Important reassurance
A surrender notice can make everything feel urgent at once. The key first step is narrower: confirm the instructions through official channels, prepare only what intake requires, and avoid missing the date without court direction.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the period right after receiving a surrender notice. Procedures vary by federal, state, county, court, and facility, and later decisions may need help from an attorney or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anything is unclear, time-sensitive, or suspicious, verify it with your attorney, the sentencing court, or the named agency using official contact information.
