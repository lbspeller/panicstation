# What to do if… your phone storage is full and it stops saving photos, videos, or messages (USA)

## Short answer
Stop repeating the failed save or recording attempt. Free a small amount of device space first, then copy or back up the recent photos, videos, and messages you care about before doing bigger deletions.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t factory reset or wipe the phone as a first move.
- Don’t delete large batches of photos or videos until you have checked that the recent ones you care about are copied or backed up.
- Don’t install random storage-cleaner apps or give new apps broad access to your photos, files, or messages.
- Don’t assume cloud storage being full automatically means the phone’s device storage is full; they can be different.
- Don’t keep recording or sending the same thing over and over; when storage is critically low, the result may not save reliably.
- Don’t tap Clear storage, Clear data, or Delete app data unless you understand what that app will erase.

## What to do now
1. **Preserve the moment another way.**  
   If something important is happening now, switch capture immediately: use another phone or camera, ask someone nearby to record, or ask them to send you copies. If you need message proof, take screenshots of key threads while the phone still opens them.

2. **Check whether the device storage or cloud storage is full.**
   - **iPhone:** Settings → General → iPhone Storage.
   - **iCloud:** Settings → your name → iCloud.
   - **Android:** Settings → Storage.
   - **Google Photos:** check the storage or backup status shown in the Google Photos app if you use it.

3. **Free space with the least risky actions first.**  
   Free enough space to test one photo, one short video, and one message attachment, then test once.
   - Delete downloads you can re-download from the Files app or Downloads folder.
   - Remove offline streaming downloads, podcasts, audiobooks, or offline maps.
   - Uninstall one large app you do not need today.
   - Empty Recently Deleted, Trash, or Bin only for items you are sure you do not need.

4. **Copy or back up recent photos and videos before deeper cleanup.**
   - If cloud photo sync is already set up, connect to reliable Wi-Fi and power, then check that the newest items appear in the cloud account before deleting local copies.
   - If you have a computer, connect by cable and import or copy the newest files, then open a few copied files on the computer to verify them.
   - If you already have a compatible external drive or adapter, copy the newest files there and check that they open.

5. **Use built-in space-saving tools carefully.**
   - **iPhone:** Settings → Photos → Optimize iPhone Storage, if you use iCloud Photos.
   - **iPhone:** Settings → General → iPhone Storage → Offload Unused Apps. This removes the app but keeps its documents and data for reinstalling later.
   - **Android:** use the phone’s built-in storage manager. Clearing cache is usually lower risk than clearing storage or data, which can erase an app’s saved data.
   - **Google Photos:** use Free up space only after checking that the photos and videos are backed up.

6. **Retry the blocked save or download once.**
   - Take one test photo or record a short test video.
   - Reopen the message app and retry downloading or sending the attachment on Wi-Fi.
   - In WhatsApp, Signal, Google Messages, or similar apps, use the app’s own storage screen to find large attachments before deleting whole chats.

7. **Check for accidental storage-cleaner subscriptions if you installed one in a panic.**
   - Check your app store subscriptions and your card statement for a new recurring charge.
   - Cancel through the app store or merchant if you can.
   - If a charge was unauthorized or the merchant will not stop billing after you tried to cancel, contact the credit or debit card issuer using the number on the back of the card or the issuer’s secure app.

8. **If the phone still will not save after freeing space, stop experimenting.**  
   Put it on charge and Wi-Fi, run a device backup through iCloud or Google backup if available, and check the backup status. Pause non-urgent OS updates, new app installs, factory reset steps, and cleanup tools until the backup or copied files are confirmed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganize your whole photo library today.
- You do not need to decide today whether to buy more cloud storage.
- You do not need to troubleshoot hardware until you have copied or backed up what you can.
- You do not need to choose repair, replacement, or warranty steps unless the phone is unusable and your important data is already copied or backed up.

## Important reassurance
A phone with critically low storage can stop saving photos, fail to download message attachments, or make apps crash. Freeing a small amount of safe-to-remove space and then checking a backup often stabilizes the immediate problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate digital loss. Later decisions, such as deeper troubleshooting, paid storage, repair, or data recovery, may need help from the device maker, your carrier, workplace IT, or a trusted repair professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional IT, legal, financial, repair, or data-recovery advice. Steps vary by device model, iOS or Android version, app, carrier, account settings, and whether the phone is personally owned or employer-issued in the USA.
