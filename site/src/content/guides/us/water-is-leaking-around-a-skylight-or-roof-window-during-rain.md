# What to do if… water is leaking around a skylight or roof window during rain (USA)
## Short answer
Move people and pets away from the leak area first. If water is near electrical equipment, or you are unsure, switch off power at the main breaker only if you can reach it from a dry, safe location.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb onto a wet roof or use a ladder during rain or wind.
- Do not touch wet light fixtures, ceiling fans, smoke alarms, outlets, plugs, or electrical equipment.
- Do not step into standing water to reach the electrical panel.
- Do not stand beneath, puncture, or disturb a sagging or bulging ceiling.
- Do not run extension cords across wet floors or into damp areas.
- Do not attempt exterior patching during the storm.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move people and pets out of the room or away from the drip line. Keep everyone clear of any sagging or bulging ceiling area.
2. **Address electricity from a dry location only.** If electrical circuits or equipment have become wet or are in or near water, switch off power at the main breaker or fuse on the service panel if you can reach it safely without standing in water. If you cannot do this safely, leave the area and call a qualified electrician.
3. **Catch the water without standing beneath damaged ceiling material.** If the ceiling is not sagging or bulging and the position is safe, place a bucket or large container under the drip. Put a towel in the container to reduce splashing. A simple plastic sheet may be used to guide drips into the container only if it can be placed from a safe, dry position away from electrical fixtures.
4. **Move vulnerable items out of the splash zone.** Move electronics, documents, furniture, and soft furnishings away from the leaking area if you can do so safely.
5. **Leave the room and get urgent help if the situation worsens.** Call 911 for immediate danger such as fire, sparking, or a ceiling that appears likely to collapse. Leave the area if you notice cracking sounds, falling drywall, a burning smell, buzzing, or water coming through a light fixture, fan, or smoke alarm.
6. **Take a few quick photos or videos from a safe position.** Capture the indoor leak, the surrounding ceiling or wall damage, and a wider view of the room.
7. **Notify the relevant contact.**
   - **Homeowner:** contact your insurer if you may need to make a claim. Arrange a roofing professional to inspect the leak when weather conditions are safe. Check any local contractor requirements before agreeing to work.
   - **Renter or condo occupant:** notify your landlord, property manager, or homeowners association promptly and send the photos or videos.
8. **Arrange electrical assessment if needed.** Do not turn power back on for wet electrical equipment until a qualified electrician has inspected it. If there is any doubt about electrical safety, keep the affected area off limits.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact outdoor failure point during the storm.
- You do not need to decide whether the leak is coming from the skylight, roof window, flashing, or surrounding roof right now.
- Permanent repair choices and cosmetic work can wait until the immediate hazards are controlled.
- Once the leak and electrical hazards are addressed, arrange prompt drying of wet materials.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to diagnose the cause during the rain. The priority is to keep people clear of electrical and ceiling hazards, limit further water spread where it is safe to do so, and arrange inspection when conditions allow.

## Scope note
These are first steps for stabilizing the situation during or shortly after rain. Later decisions about repair, drying, insurance, or responsibility may need help from roofing, electrical, property-management, or restoration professionals.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use a qualified professional for advice about your specific property and any electrical, structural, or repair risks.
