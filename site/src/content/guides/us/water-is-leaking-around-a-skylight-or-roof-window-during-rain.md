# What to do if… water is leaking around a skylight or roof window during rain (USA)

## Short answer
Prioritize electrical safety by moving everyone away from the leak and shutting off power to the area if water is near lights, fans, or outlets. Contain the water using buckets and towels, but do not attempt to access the roof until the storm has passed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb onto a wet roof or use a ladder in rain/wind to attempt a temporary fix.
- Do not touch wet light fixtures, ceiling fans, or any electronics near the dripping water.
- Do not puncture or "drain" a bulging ceiling as this may trigger a collapse or expose live wiring.
- Do not use a wet-vac or electric pump while standing on a wet floor unless the equipment is GFCI-protected.
- Do not leave pets or children in the room where a leak is active.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the immediate area to create a safer pause.** **Move people and valuables out of the splash zone and place dry towels around the perimeter of the leak to prevent water from spreading across the floor.**
2. **Address electrical hazards before touching any water.** **If water is dripping from or near a light fixture, ceiling fan, or wall outlet, turn off the power at the main breaker panel.**
   - Stand to the side of the panel and use only one hand to flip the switch.
   - Do not touch the panel if you must stand in water to reach it.
3. **Contain and redirect the falling water.** **Place a large container or bucket directly under the drip, placing a towel inside the bucket to prevent splashing.**
   - If water is running down a wall, use painter's tape to secure a piece of plastic sheeting (like a trash bag) to the wall above the leak to guide water into the bucket.
4. **Inspect the attic only if it is safe and dry.** **If you have safe interior access to the attic space above the skylight, use a flashlight to identify where water is entering the structure.**
   - Do not walk on ceiling joists if they appear saturated.
   - If you see water traveling along electrical cables, back out immediately and ensure the power is off.
5. **Document the damage with date-stamped photos.** **Take wide shots of the room and close-ups of the leak, including any bubbling paint or sagging drywall, to provide evidence for insurance or a landlord.**
6. **Watch for signs of imminent structural failure.** **Keep the room closed off if you hear cracking sounds, see large sections of drywall drooping, or notice water pouring through electrical fixtures.**
7. **Contact emergency mitigation services if you cannot contain the leak.** **Call a licensed roofer to schedule an emergency "tarp-over" for when the weather clears, and contact your insurance company's 24-hour claim line to report the intrusion.**
8. **Notify your landlord or property manager immediately if you are a renter.** **Provide the photos you took and emphasize any electrical hazards to ensure the issue is prioritized as an emergency repair.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific exterior failure point (e.g., flashing vs. shingles) while it is still raining.
- You do not need to start permanent drying or mold remediation until the exterior leak is professionally sealed.
- You do not need to decide on a contractor for permanent repairs right now; focus only on emergency containment.

## Important reassurance
Skylight leaks are common during heavy storms and are frequently caused by clogged drainage channels or worn seals rather than a total roof failure. Taking quick action to contain the water and manage electrical safety is the most effective way to prevent long-term damage.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Professional assessment by a licensed roofer and a licensed electrician is required to ensure the property is safe and weather-tight.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not replace professional trade advice. If there is any evidence of water entering electrical systems, keep the power off and do not restore it until a licensed electrician has performed an inspection.
