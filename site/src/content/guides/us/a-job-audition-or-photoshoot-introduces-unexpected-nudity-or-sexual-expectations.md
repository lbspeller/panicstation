# What to do if… a job audition or photoshoot introduces unexpected nudity or sexual expectations (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safer place and end the interaction. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t comply because you feel pressured to protect the opportunity.
- Don’t arrange another meeting alone while you feel unsafe or pressured.
- Don’t send nude or sexual images or do sexual acts as a “tryout.”
- Don’t pay upfront fees or share bank details because someone says they are needed for the opportunity.
- Don’t keep negotiating while you feel overwhelmed.
- Don’t contact the person again to gather proof.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.**  
   Leave the room or location if you can do so safely. Go to a public area, contact someone you trust, or ask nearby staff or security for help. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

2. **End the interaction.**  
   You can say: “No. I’m leaving.” or “This is not what I agreed to.” You do not need to explain or debate.

3. **Contact specialist support.**  
   RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline offers support 24/7. Call **800-656-HOPE (800-656-4673)**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use its online chat. You can contact the hotline even if you are unsure how to describe what happened.

4. **Seek medical care if any sexual contact occurred or you are worried about your health.**  
   A medical provider can check for injuries, infections, and pregnancy concerns. Ask a local hospital or RAINN where sexual-assault care or a sexual assault forensic exam is available. You do not have to decide about reporting before seeking care.

5. **Keep only what you already have, if it feels safe.**  
   If it feels safe and you may want support or to report later, keep the messages or documents you already have, but do not contact the person again to gather proof.

6. **Get support with threats involving intimate images.**  
   If they threaten to share intimate images or demand money or sexual acts, you can stop responding and contact RAINN for support. RAINN also has guidance for threats or sharing involving intimate images. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

7. **Use specialist trafficking support only if it fits your situation.**  
   If you think force, fraud, or coercion is being used to make you work or engage in commercial sex, you can contact the National Human Trafficking Hotline at **1-888-373-7888** or text **233733**. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.

## What can wait
- You don’t have to decide right now whether to report to police, an agency, a company, or a venue.
- You don’t have to write a detailed account tonight.
- You don’t have to confront the person or respond to every message.
- You don’t have to decide whether the opportunity was real.
- You don’t have to label what happened before asking for support.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to stop at any point. Pressure can make it hard to think clearly. Freezing, complying at first, or doubting yourself does not mean you agreed to anything further, and you do not owe anyone sexual access in exchange for work.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you reach a safer pause and get support. Later decisions about reporting, contacting an organization, medical care, or digital safety may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
