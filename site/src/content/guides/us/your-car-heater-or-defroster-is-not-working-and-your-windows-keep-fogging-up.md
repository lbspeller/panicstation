# What to do if… your car heater or defroster is not working and your windows keep fogging up (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive until you can see clearly and keep the glass clear. While parked, use fresh outside air, turn recirculation off, set the fan high toward the windshield, turn A/C on if available, and wipe the inside glass with a clean dry cloth.

## Do not do these things
- Do not start driving with fogged glass hoping it clears on the way.
- Do not idle the vehicle in a garage, carport, or other enclosed or partly enclosed space.
- Do not run the engine for long periods with the windows fully closed, especially if snow, mud, leaves, or debris could block the exhaust pipe.
- Do not leave recirculation on if fog is building; it can trap humid cabin air.
- Do not pour hot water on the windshield or use rough tools on the glass, because they can crack or damage it.
- Do not open a hot pressurized coolant cap if overheating, steam, or warning lights are present.

## What to do now
1. **Stay parked and make the stop safer.** Keep the vehicle stopped. Turn on hazard lights if you are near traffic. Keep your seatbelt on if remaining in the vehicle is safer than stepping out.
2. **Call 911 if there is immediate danger.** Call if you are exposed to traffic, have been in a crash, feel medically unwell, smell exhaust, suspect carbon monoxide, or cannot stay safely where you are.
3. **If you are in a garage or enclosed area, turn the engine off.** Get fresh air first. Do not keep the vehicle running there, even with a door open.
4. **Check for a blocked exhaust if it is safe to do so.** If snow, mud, leaves, or debris may be around the tailpipe, turn the engine off before checking. Do not stand in traffic or unsafe weather to do this.
5. **Set the air to clear the windshield.**
   - Select defrost or the windshield icon.
   - Set the fan high.
   - Turn recirculation off.
   - Use outside or fresh air.
   - Turn A/C on if available, even in cold weather, because it can help dry the air.
6. **Vent the humid cabin briefly.** Crack two windows slightly for a minute or two if it is safe and weather allows, then close them enough to stay warm and reassess the glass.
7. **Wipe the inside glass dry.** Use a clean, dry microfiber cloth or paper towels on the inside of the windshield and both front side windows. Use a fresh dry part of the cloth so you are not spreading moisture.
8. **Reduce the moisture inside the car.** Move wet coats, umbrellas, bags, or floor mats away from the airflow if you can do that safely. Ask passengers to step out briefly only if the place is safe.
9. **Recheck before moving.** Drive only if the windshield and front side windows stay clear enough for you to see traffic, mirrors, pedestrians, lane markings, and hazards. If the fog comes back quickly or you need constant wiping, do not drive.
10. **Get help instead of forcing the trip.** Call roadside assistance, a tow service, a trusted person, or the place you were going. Say clearly: “The heater or defroster is not working and visibility is unsafe.”
11. **Treat possible carbon monoxide symptoms as urgent.** If anyone has headache, dizziness, weakness, nausea, vomiting, chest pain, confusion, or unusual sleepiness while the engine has been running, turn the engine off if you can, get fresh air, and call 911.

## What can wait
- Diagnosing whether the cause is coolant, a leak, the thermostat, blower motor, cabin air filter, A/C, or an electrical fault.
- Buying parts or choosing a repair shop.
- Deciding whether the whole trip is still possible.
- Cleaning every window perfectly; the immediate need is safe visibility and a safe next step.

## Important reassurance
Stopping instead of driving with fogged windows is a sensible safety choice. You are not overreacting by waiting, calling for help, or cancelling the drive if the glass will not stay clear.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a parked vehicle with unsafe fogging. Later decisions about repairs, towing, insurance, or legal duties may need help from a qualified mechanic, roadside provider, insurer, or local authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, mechanical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Follow emergency instructions, your vehicle manual, and local law where they apply.
