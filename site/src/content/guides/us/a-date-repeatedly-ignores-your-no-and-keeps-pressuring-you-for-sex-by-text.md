# What to do if… a date repeatedly ignores your no and keeps pressuring you for sex by text (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safer pause: do not meet them alone tonight, tell someone you trust what is happening, and call 911 if you need immediate emergency assistance. You can stop replying, mute or block them, and seek confidential support without deciding anything else tonight.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t meet them alone to clear the air or prove anything.
- Don’t send sexual photos, videos, or more personal details to make the messages stop.
- Don’t share your live location, address, workplace, or routine.
- Don’t feel that you have to keep replying or explain your no again.
- Don’t override your unease because you are worried about seeming rude.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** If you had plans to meet, do not go. You do not have to send another message. If they are nearby or you are already with them and feel unsafe, move toward other people or staff if you can do so safely.
2. **Tell one trusted person now.** You can say: “This person keeps pressuring me for sex by text. Can you check in with me tonight?” Tell them if the person knows where you live, work, study, or usually spend time.
3. **Reduce contact in the way that feels safest.** You do not owe another reply. You can stop responding, mute the messages, or block the person. You may choose muting rather than blocking for now if that feels safer.
4. **Check your privacy from a device you feel safe using.** Turn off any location-sharing you do not want active, avoid real-time posts, and check whether the person can see your online status. If you think your device or internet use may be monitored, use a safer device if available or contact a support service by phone.
5. **Save only what feels manageable.** If you may want support later, you can save a small set of messages with the date and time visible somewhere the person cannot access; you do not need to build a full file tonight.
6. **Call 911 if you need immediate emergency assistance.** Call if you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance, including if the person shows up and you feel in danger.
7. **Use specialist support if it would help.**
   - **RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline:** call 800-656-HOPE (4673), use online chat, or text **HOPE** to **64673**.
   - **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** call 1-800-799-SAFE (7233), use live chat, or text **START** to **88788**.
   - **love is respect:** teens, young adults, and loved ones can call 866-331-9474, use live chat, or text **LOVEIS** to **22522**.
   - **Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline:** if you are under 18 and concerned about abuse or your safety, call 800-422-4453 or text **GO** to **800-422-4453**.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide tonight whether to report the messages or take any other later action.
- You do not need perfect wording or a longer explanation.
- You do not need to label what happened before seeking support.
- You do not need to respond to every new message.

## Important reassurance
Repeatedly pushing after you have said “not tonight” means your boundary is not being respected. Feeling shaken, angry, numb, or confused can happen. You are allowed to stop contact and prioritize your safety without justifying your decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need support from a specialist service, but you do not have to make those decisions while you are overwhelmed.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you need immediate emergency assistance, call 911.
