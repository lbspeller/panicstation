# What to do if… a funeral home or service provider is pressuring you into fast decisions (USA)

## Short answer
Pause the decision-making and ask for itemized price information before you agree to anything else. If you are at the funeral home, ask for the General Price List to keep; if you are on the phone, ask for the exact prices you need to compare.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t sign a contract or authorize charges while you are confused, rushed, or still waiting for itemized prices.
- Don’t accept “this is required” without asking, “Required by law, required by the cemetery or crematory, or your policy?”
- Don’t choose a casket, urn, vault, liner, package, or upgrade before you have seen the relevant prices.
- Don’t pay a large deposit on the spot for add-ons or packages if you have not received an itemized written statement of what you are buying.
- Don’t assume you are locked in because you spoke to one funeral home. You can usually compare providers before choosing.

## What to do now
1. **Use one clear pause phrase.**  
   Say: “I am not making more decisions right now. Please give me your price information and an itemized estimate, and I will contact you.”  
   If the pressure continues, repeat it once and end the conversation.

2. **Ask for the General Price List if you are there in person.**  
   Say: “Please give me your General Price List to keep before we discuss arrangements.”  
   If you are not there in person, ask them to email the GPL or give you price information by phone for the services you are considering.

3. **Ask for casket or outer burial container prices before you are shown choices.**  
   Use these only if they are relevant:
   - “If we are discussing caskets, please give me the casket price list or show me where all casket prices are listed on the General Price List.”
   - “If we are discussing grave liners or vaults, please give me the outer burial container price list or show me where those prices are listed on the General Price List.”

4. **Ask for one written itemized statement before you pay or sign.**  
   Say: “Please give me the Statement of Funeral Goods and Services Selected, with each item, each price, and the total.”  
   Also ask: “Please write down any legal, cemetery, or crematory requirement you say makes an item necessary.”

5. **Separate immediate care from optional purchases.**  
   If something must happen today, ask: “What is the minimum needed today for care or transport, and what can wait?”  
   Authorize only that minimum in writing, and state that you are not approving packages, upgrades, vehicles, flowers, printed materials, or other extras yet.

6. **Write down the pressure while it is fresh.**  
   Note the date, time, person’s name, exact claims such as “required,” “today only,” or “prices go up,” and what you were asked to sign or pay. Save texts, emails, estimates, and photos of paperwork.

7. **Compare at least one other provider if you can pause safely.**  
   Ask another funeral home for the same basic prices: basic services fee, transfer of remains, direct cremation or immediate burial if relevant, casket or container price if relevant, and any cash advance items.

8. **Report suspected deceptive conduct if you want to.**  
   You can report Funeral Rule concerns or bad business practices to the Federal Trade Commission. You can also contact your state consumer protection office, state Attorney General, or state funeral board or licensing agency.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose premium caskets, urns, vaults, flowers, vehicles, printed materials, catering, obituary wording, music, or other extras right now.
- You do not need to agree to a full package in one meeting.
- You do not need to decide every service detail today.
- You do not need to stay with the first funeral home you contacted if you feel pressured or disrespected.

## Important reassurance
High-pressure sales tactics can make it feel as though slowing down dishonours the person who died. It does not. Asking for written, itemized prices is a normal protective step when you are grieving and trying to make clear decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to reduce pressure and buy time. Later decisions about contracts, refunds, disputed charges, or misconduct may need help from consumer protection, a state regulator, or legal services in your state.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Rules and remedies can vary by state and by what has already been signed or provided.
