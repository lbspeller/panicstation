# What to do if… your car fuel gauge is unreliable and you are unsure if you can reach help (USA)

## Short answer
Assume you have less fuel than the gauge claims. Do not drive just to test it; only make a very short, low-risk move to fuel if you are confident, and otherwise call for fuel delivery or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving farther just to see whether the gauge is right.
- Don’t walk along a freeway, interstate, or high-speed shoulder to look for a gas station or signal.
- Don’t siphon fuel or work around fuel lines, the fuel tank, or other fuel parts.
- Don’t smoke, use a flame, or keep restarting the engine if you smell fuel or see a fresh wet spot under the vehicle.
- Don’t get into a stranger’s vehicle or leave your car in a way that feels unsafe.
- Don’t let your phone battery drain while you are still stopped somewhere safe.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the safe stop safe.**
   - Stay in the safe parking area if you are already in one.
   - Keep your phone charging if you can.
   - Use hazard lights if other drivers might not expect your vehicle to be stopped.
   - Send someone your location, preferably a map pin.

2. **Do a two-minute fuel reality check.**
   - Think back to when you last refueled and roughly how much fuel you added.
   - Check the trip odometer or recent miles driven if you know them.
   - Notice whether the low-fuel warning light or message is on.
   - Treat “miles to empty” as unreliable while you are deciding.

3. **Check for signs of a fuel leak before driving anywhere.**
   - From a safe position, look for a fresh wet spot under the vehicle.
   - Notice any strong gasoline smell near or inside the vehicle.
   - Check that the fuel door and fuel cap are closed if you can do that safely.
   - If you smell fuel strongly or see a possible leak, keep the vehicle off and call for a tow.

4. **Decide between a very short move and getting help.**
   - A short drive to fuel may be reasonable only if the gas station is close enough that you would still feel safe if the car stopped, the route is simple, the roads are low-speed, your phone has charge, and there is no fuel smell or leak.
   - If any of those points are not true, stay put and request fuel delivery or a tow.

5. **Request help through the safest available route.**
   - Contact your roadside assistance plan, insurer, automaker app, rental company, or a local tow company and ask for fuel delivery or a tow to a nearby gas station.
   - If you use AAA, request fuel delivery or towing through AAA’s roadside assistance options.
   - If this is an emergency, call 911. Examples include being in or near a travel lane, immediate traffic danger, poor visibility, severe weather creating danger, a possible fire, or someone threatening you.
   - When calling for help, give your exact location, the vehicle description, whether you smell fuel, and whether you are in a safe parking area or near traffic.

6. **Wait in the lowest-risk place.**
   - In a rest area, parking lot, or well-lit business area, it is usually safest to stay with the vehicle with the doors locked.
   - If your stop becomes unsafe because of traffic or another immediate danger, move away from traffic to the safest nearby place you can reach without walking along the roadway.
   - Follow instructions from 911, highway patrol, roadside assistance, or a tow operator.

## What can wait
- Diagnosing whether the problem is the fuel sender, wiring, dashboard display, vehicle software, or another mechanical fault.
- Trying resets, repeatedly restarting the engine, or debating the exact range estimate.
- Deciding on permanent repairs, warranty questions, or which mechanic to use unless you are already safely arranging a tow or shop visit.

## Important reassurance
A wrong fuel gauge is stressful because it removes a basic driving certainty. Treating the gauge as unreliable and getting help early is a reasonable way to avoid ending up somewhere less safe.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation while you are stopped somewhere safe and unsure you can reach help. Later decisions about repair, towing, insurance, or mechanical diagnosis may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, emergency-services, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If your location becomes unsafe, prioritize immediate safety and contact emergency services.
