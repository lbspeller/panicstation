# What to do if… your bank confirms unauthorised payments but says the investigation may take weeks (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the account and formally request **provisional credit** under Regulation E if the investigation for a debit/EFT error exceeds 10 business days. For credit cards, submit a written billing error notice within 60 days to preserve your Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA) rights.

## Do not do these things
- Don't continue using compromised credentials, PINs, or cards associated with the unauthorized activity.
- Don't accept a verbal "it's being handled" as a substitute for the written dispute requirements mandated by federal law.
- Don't pay the disputed portion of a credit card bill while the investigation is active.
- Don't assume the bank has automatically notified credit bureaus; they are only restricted from reporting the debt as delinquent during the investigation.
- Don't wait for a police report to be filed before initiating the bank's internal dispute process; banks cannot legally require a police report to start an investigation.

## What to do now
1. **Secure all access points to prevent further unauthorized withdrawals.** **Lock or freeze the affected card immediately via your banking app or by calling the number on the back of the card.**
   - Change passwords for online banking and the associated primary email account.
   - Enable hardware or app-based multi-factor authentication (MFA).
   - Request a new account number or card number if the current one is compromised.

2. **Establish the "Notice of Error" date and request a written summary of the claim.** **Ask the bank representative exactly what date they have recorded as your official 'notice of error' to track legal deadlines.**
   - Request a written or electronic acknowledgement of the dispute.
   - Verify if the bank requires a written follow-up to provide provisional credit; if they do, you must send it within 10 business days.

3. **Invoke your right to provisional credit for debit or electronic transfers.** **Inform the bank that under Regulation E (12 CFR § 1005.11), they must generally provide provisional credit for the disputed amount if the investigation lasts longer than 10 business days.**
   - Note that for accounts open less than 30 days, this window extends to 20 business days.
   - Ask for the specific address where the written confirmation of error must be sent to ensure you meet the 10-day deadline.

4. **Protect credit card transactions with a formal FCBA billing error notice.** **Send a written dispute letter to the creditor’s 'billing inquiries' address within 60 days of the statement where the unauthorized charge first appeared.**
   - Include your name, account number, the dollar amount of the error, and why you believe it is unauthorized.
   - Use certified mail with a return receipt to prove the creditor received the notice.

5. **Review and purge all secondary payment links.** **Examine all scheduled bill-pays, peer-to-peer (P2P) apps like Zelle or Venmo, and linked external accounts for unauthorized additions.**
   - Remove any payees or linked cards you do not recognize.
   - Ask the bank to specifically block the merchants involved in the fraudulent transactions.

6. **Manage immediate liquidity for essential expenses.** **If the missing funds prevent you from paying rent or utilities, notify the bank of 'financial hardship' and request expedited provisional credit or fee waivers.**
   - Ask for a temporary reversal of any overdraft or late fees triggered by the unauthorized activity.

7. **Place a credit freeze to prevent identity-related escalation.** **Contact Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion to place a free security freeze on your credit reports.**
   - This prevents new accounts from being opened in your name while the current fraud is investigated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific method of the breach (e.g., skimming vs. phishing) before reporting.
- You do not need to close the entire banking relationship immediately; focus on securing the specific compromised account first.
- You do not need to contact the merchants yourself unless the bank specifically provides a reason why they cannot proceed without merchant interaction.

## Important reassurance
Financial institutions are legally required to follow specific timelines once an error is reported. While "weeks" sounds daunting, the provisional credit system is designed to return funds to your account temporarily while the bank performs its due diligence, provided you follow the reporting steps.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and legal protections for unauthorized transactions under Regulation E and the Fair Credit Billing Act. It does not cover long-term identity restoration or secondary legal claims if a dispute is ultimately denied.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and is not legal or financial advice. Deadlines and protections vary significantly between debit cards (Regulation E) and credit cards (Regulation Z/FCBA). If you believe you are a victim of comprehensive identity theft, visit IdentityTheft.gov for a personalized recovery plan.
