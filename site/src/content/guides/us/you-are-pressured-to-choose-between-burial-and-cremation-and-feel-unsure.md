# What to do if… you are pressured to choose between burial and cremation and feel unsure (USA)

## Short answer
Pause before signing any authorization for burial or cremation. Ask the funeral home what can be kept paused while you confirm who may decide under applicable state law and look for any written wishes or prepaid plan.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t sign burial, cremation, or disposition paperwork if you are unsure, do not understand it, or know there is an unresolved dispute.
- Don’t assume that family agreement or seniority automatically decides who has legal authority.
- Don’t treat cremation as a temporary step. Cremation cannot be reversed once it has been carried out.
- Don’t accept price pressure. Ask for telephone price information or, during an in-person arrangements discussion, the General Price List. Before accepting arrangements, ask for a written itemized statement.
- Don’t agree to a package without asking which goods and services are separate choices.

## What to do now
1. **Ask what can pause.** Say: “Please do not carry out burial or cremation while we clarify authority and wishes. What can be paused, and are there care charges or time limits we should know about?”
2. **Ask who may authorize the decision.** Ask: “Under the applicable state rules, who may authorize burial or cremation in this case? What documents or signatures do you need? What happens if instructions conflict?”
3. **Look for recorded wishes and existing arrangements.** Check for a prepaid funeral contract, written funeral instructions, a will, or a document appointing an agent to control disposition. Emails and text messages may help identify wishes, but ask whether any informal message has legal effect.
4. **Ask whether a Medical Examiner or Coroner is involved.** Ask whether that affects timing, required documents, or what can happen next.
5. **Separate the disposition decision from ceremony choices.** Ask for prices for direct cremation and immediate burial. Ask which service, visitation, memorial, and reception choices can be arranged later.
6. **Use the appropriate price-information route.** By phone, ask what the funeral home offers, what each option costs, and what is included. In person, ask for the General Price List. Before accepting arrangements, ask for a written itemized statement showing the selected goods and services and their prices.
7. **Reduce conflicting messages.** Ask the funeral home to record one contact person for communications while authority is clarified. A contact person does not become the legally authorized decision-maker merely by acting as the family spokesperson.

## What can wait
- Casket or urn upgrades, flowers, programs, obituary wording, and reception details.
- Service, visitation, and memorial details that the funeral home confirms can be arranged later.
- Broad announcements beyond the people who need to know immediately.
- Permanent memorial, headstone, and scattering details unless the funeral home says a particular choice is needed now.

## Important reassurance
You do not have to solve every funeral decision at once. It is reasonable to ask what can pause, confirm who may decide, and check for recorded wishes before an irreversible step is taken.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. State laws, local requirements, and funeral-home procedures vary. A dispute or unclear authority may need specialist help, including guidance from the funeral home and legal advice from an attorney.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Authorization rules, required documents, permits, and Medical Examiner or Coroner involvement vary under applicable state and local rules.
