# What to do if… your rideshare or taxi cancels after arriving and you are left waiting somewhere unfamiliar (USA)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a well-lit, populated, indoor location and rebook your ride from that safer position rather than staying at the original cancellation point.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept a ride from any individual who pulls up and offers a "private lift" or "cash deal" because you look stranded.
- Do not remain at the curb or the original "app pin" location if it is isolated, dark, or unpopulated.
- Do not engage in a "cash-only" or "off-app" arrangement with a driver who claims their app is broken.
- Do not provide your full name, home address, or destination to bystanders or strangers offering unsolicited help.
- Do not use your remaining phone battery for non-essential tasks like checking social media or reviews while waiting.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a staffed, public indoor area immediately.** Seek out a hotel lobby, 24-hour convenience store, open restaurant, or gas station where other people are present and visibility is high.
2. **Share your live location with a trusted contact right now.** Send your current GPS coordinates or the specific business name and address to a friend or family member and ask them to monitor your status until you are inside a verified vehicle.
3. **Rebook the ride only once you are in a secure position.** 
   - Set the new pickup point to the specific entrance of the building where you are waiting.
   - Use the "Wait Inside" protocol and only exit the building once the app confirms the driver has arrived at your exact door.
4. **Activate "Verify My Ride" or PIN verification in the app settings.** Enable the four-digit PIN feature (available in Uber and some Lyft markets) which requires the driver to enter a code from your phone before the trip can officially begin.
5. **Verify the vehicle and driver using the 3-point match rule before approaching.**
   - Confirm the license plate matches the app exactly.
   - Confirm the car make, model, and color match the description.
   - Confirm the driver’s face matches the photo provided in the app.
6. **Ask the driver “Who are you here for?” before entering.** Do not volunteer your name first; ensure the driver provides the name associated with the account to confirm they are the assigned provider.
7. **Check the child safety lock immediately upon entering the back seat.** Test the interior door handle before the car pulls away to ensure you can exit the vehicle independently from the inside.
8. **Use the in-app "Safety Shield" if you feel uneasy.** Tap the shield icon to access the ADT safety agent link (or equivalent platform safety tool) for silent monitoring or emergency 911 connection if the situation escalates.
9. **Document the cancellation for later reporting.** Take a screenshot of the cancellation notice and the driver's profile details if they are still visible in your history to dispute any unfair fees or report suspicious behavior.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve refund disputes or service complaints while you are still waiting for a ride.
- You do not need to justify moving from the original pickup point; safety takes priority over "holding the pin."
- You do not need to decide if the driver's cancellation was intentional or a technical error.

## Important reassurance
A sudden cancellation in an unfamiliar area causes an immediate "fight or flight" response, which is a normal reaction to feeling exposed. Taking a few seconds to reach a lit, populated area will lower your stress and allow you to make a safer decision about your next transport option.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate safety after a transport failure. It does not cover long-term legal claims, insurance disputes, or platform deactivation processes.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal or security advice. If you believe you are in immediate physical danger or are being followed, call 911 immediately. Rideshare insurance typically does not cover you once a trip is cancelled in the app.
