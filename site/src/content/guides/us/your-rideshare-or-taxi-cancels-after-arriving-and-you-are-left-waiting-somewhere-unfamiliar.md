# What to do if… your rideshare or taxi cancels after arriving and you are left waiting somewhere unfamiliar (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a staffed, well-lit, populated place first. Rebook only from that safer spot, and get into a car only after you can verify it in the app or through a reputable taxi dispatch.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t accept a ride from a stranger who pulls up and offers “a lift” because you look stuck.
- Don’t stay at an isolated curb just because the app pin is there; move to safety, then update the pickup point.
- Don’t pay cash or go off-app because someone says it will be faster.
- Don’t give your full name, home address, hotel room number, or travel plans to someone trying to “help.”
- Don’t unlock your phone, open payment apps, or show your screen to a stranger.
- Don’t use up your phone battery scrolling or repeatedly refreshing; keep enough charge for calls, maps, and the ride app.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer place immediately.** Go to a staffed public location if you can: a hotel lobby, busy store, café, restaurant, staffed gas station, transit desk, or building entrance with people and lighting.
2. **Stay inside or close to staff while you rebook.** Wait indoors until the app shows the driver has arrived, if that is possible. Avoid standing alone outside with your phone in your hand.
3. **Tell one trusted person where you are.** Send your live location or the business name and address. Ask them to stay available by phone or text until you are in a verified ride.
4. **Preserve your phone.** Lower screen brightness, close nonessential apps, and avoid video or social media. If you are inside a business, ask staff whether there is a safe place to charge briefly.
5. **Rebook from the safer pickup point.** Set the pickup at a clear public landmark, such as the main entrance of a hotel, store, or station. Do not keep the pickup at a dark corner or empty curb because it was the original pin.
6. **Switch methods if cancellations repeat.** Try another ride app, use an official taxi stand if one is present, or ask staff to help you call a reputable local taxi dispatch.
7. **Verify the car and driver before you enter.** Check that the license plate, make and model, driver name, and driver photo match the app or dispatch details. If anything does not match, do not get in.
8. **Make the driver confirm you.** Ask, “Who are you here to pick up?” Do not say your name first.
9. **Use only arranged taxi or for-hire service.** For taxis, use an official taxi stand, a marked taxi where local rules allow street hails, or a dispatch arranged by staff or by you. Avoid unmarked cars offering cash rides.
10. **Escalate if you feel threatened.** If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you are indoors but worried, ask staff, security, or a front desk worker to stay nearby until your verified ride arrives.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve refunds, reviews, complaints, or support chats while you are still outside or unsettled.
- You do not need to keep the original pickup location.
- You do not need to decide whether you overreacted.
- You do not need to explain the cancellation to strangers nearby.

## Important reassurance
A cancellation after arrival can make you feel exposed quickly, especially somewhere unfamiliar. Your priority is not speed or politeness; it is getting to a safer, more public place so you can choose a verified way onward.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions, such as refunds, reports, repeated cancellations, or harassment complaints, may need help from the ride platform, a local taxi regulator, law enforcement, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel at risk, prioritize immediate safety and contact emergency services.
