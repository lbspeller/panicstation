# What to do if… you see someone peering into your windows or lurking outside your home (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the window, lock doors and accessible windows, and call 911 if the person is still there, trying entry points, or you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to confront them or ask what they want.
- Don’t open the door or talk through it.
- Don’t chase them if they leave.
- Don’t stand in a lit window where you are clearly visible.
- Don’t put yourself near glass to get a photo or video.
- Don’t assume it is harmless before you are in a safer spot.
- Don’t hang up on 911 unless the call-taker tells you to.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer spot immediately.** Step away from windows and exterior doors. If possible, go to an interior room where you are not visible from outside.
2. **Secure entry points from inside.** Lock exterior doors, lock accessible windows, close blinds or curtains, and turn on outdoor lights if you can do that without going outside.
3. **Call 911 if the person is still there or you feel threatened.** Give the call-taker:
   - Your exact address, including apartment or unit number
   - Where the person is, such as the front, back, side yard, or a specific window
   - What they are doing, such as peering in, lingering, trying handles, or moving around the home
   - A description of the person, clothing, direction of travel, and any vehicle details
4. **Stay on the line and follow instructions.** The call-taker’s questions help get the right help to you. Do not hang up until they tell you to.
5. **If you can’t safely speak, keep the call connected if you can.** Text-to-911 may work in your area; call if you can, and text only if you can’t. If text-to-911 is not available, you may receive a bounce-back message.
6. **Observe only from a safe position.** From away from the glass and out of sight, note the time, location, description, and whether they touched windows, doors, gates, or screens. You do not need to take risks to get proof.
7. **Bring other people and pets away from exposed windows.** Quietly move children, visitors, or pets into the safer room with you. Keep your phone, keys, and essential medication nearby in case responders advise you to leave.
8. **If you are in an apartment or managed building, notify building security after 911 is contacted.** Ask whether shared entrances can be checked and whether any camera footage may exist, without delaying emergency help.
9. **If the person leaves before officers arrive, update the call-taker.** Share the direction they went, any vehicle details, and whether you still feel unsafe. The call-taker may still advise a report or a non-emergency follow-up.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to install cameras, upgrade locks, or add motion lights.
- Contacting a landlord, HOA, or repair person unless police, building security, or immediate damage makes that necessary now.
- Reviewing doorbell or camera footage in detail.
- Writing a full account of what happened.
- Deciding whether this was a misunderstanding.

## Important reassurance
Feeling alarmed when someone peers into your windows is a normal reaction. You do not need to prove their intent before creating distance, locking up, and contacting help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the moment and reduce immediate risk. If this repeats, feels targeted, or involves someone you know, later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, building security, a victim-support service, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime may be in progress, call 911.
