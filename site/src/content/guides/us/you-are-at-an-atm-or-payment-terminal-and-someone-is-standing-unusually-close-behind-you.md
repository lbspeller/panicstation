# What to do if… you are at an ATM or payment terminal and someone is standing unusually close behind you (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the transaction immediately and step away from the machine. Do not enter your PIN or complete the payment until you have sufficient physical space or have moved to a more secure location.

## Do not do these things
- Don't enter your PIN if someone is close enough to see your hand movements or the screen.
- Don't accept help from any stranger, especially if the machine appears to malfunction or "traps" your card.
- Don't let anyone touch the keypad, card reader, or your person during a transaction.
- Don't feel obligated to finish the transaction just because there is a line behind you.
- Don't count your cash or display your wallet while still at the ATM or terminal.
- Don't stay and argue if you feel the person is intentionally crowding you; prioritize leaving.

## What to do now
1. **Cancel the transaction immediately by pressing the "Cancel" button on the keypad or screen.** **Retrieve your card and any dispensed cash as soon as they are available.**
   - If the card does not eject, do not leave the machine yet; check for a "Cancel" option on the screen once more.
2. **Step out of the transaction area and create physical distance between yourself and the person.** **Move to a position where your screen and keypad are no longer visible to others.**
   - If you are at a retail checkout, tell the cashier you need to pause or void the transaction because you require more space.
3. **Relocate to a safer, more private machine or terminal if you still need to complete your task.** **Prioritize ATMs located inside bank branches, well-lit grocery stores, or areas with active security staff.**
   - Avoid freestanding ATMs in dark areas, isolated parking lots, or "island" kiosks at night.
4. **Use your body and free hand to physically shield the keypad if you must continue at a terminal.** **Stand directly in front of the machine and cover the top of the keypad with your non-typing hand while entering digits.**
   - Use contactless "tap-to-pay" options if available, as these often bypass the need for a magnetic stripe swipe which is more vulnerable to skimming.
5. **Report the incident to the facility manager or bank staff if the person's behavior was threatening or the machine appeared tampered with.** **Look for signs of skimming devices, such as loose components, sticky residue, or misaligned card slots.**
6. **Lock or freeze your card using your banking app immediately if you suspect your PIN was seen.** **If you cannot access an app, call the customer service number on the back of your card or your bank’s official fraud line to report a potential compromise.**
7. **Change your PIN as soon as possible at a known-safe ATM or through your bank's secure portal.** **Monitor your account for unauthorized transactions over the next 48 hours, including small "test" charges.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if the person was "actually" a criminal; the safety protocol is the same for any crowding.
- You do not need to report the person to 911 unless they have threatened you, followed you, or committed a theft.
- You do not need to replace your physical card immediately unless you suspect a skimming device was attached to the reader.

## Important reassurance
Feeling pressured or awkward when someone stands too close is a common tactic used by distraction thieves and "shoulder surfers." Stepping away and canceling is a standard safety procedure, not an overreaction. Your financial security is more important than the convenience of the transaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate physical safety and PIN protection at ATMs and payment terminals. It does not cover long-term fraud recovery or identity theft remediation.

## Important note
This information is for general safety purposes and does not constitute legal or financial advice. If you are in immediate physical danger or being followed, call **911**. Contact your financial institution directly using official numbers for all account-specific issues.
