# What to do if… an after-school program will not release your child after a parent has died (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the program to keep your child safely supervised while the release issue is checked. Ask for the person responsible for release decisions and the exact requirement for today’s pickup.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t forge a signature or claim to be someone else.
- Don’t argue about the release decision in front of your child. Ask to speak with the responsible staff member separately.
- Don’t send sensitive documents until the program explains what it needs, who will review them, and whether a copy is sufficient.
- Don’t assume that an emergency-contact entry automatically allows pickup.
- Don’t agree to permanent record changes on the spot when you are unsure who has authority to approve them.

## What to do now
1. **Ask staff to keep your child safely supervised.**  
   Say: “Please keep my child with staff while we work out the authorized release.”

2. **Ask for the person responsible for release decisions.**  
   Ask for the site director, school office, or provider contact who can explain the program’s procedure.

3. **Ask what the program needs for today’s pickup.**  
   Ask whether there is a temporary release process, who can approve it, how identity will be checked, and whether the program needs a form, a record update, or another type of verification.

4. **Contact the person the program says can authorize a change.**  
   This may be a parent, guardian, or another person already recorded by the program. Follow the program’s stated verification steps rather than sending extra personal documents.

5. **Ask for the written pickup or uncollected-child procedure if release cannot be approved promptly.**  
   Ask what will happen next, who will stay with your child, and who the program will contact.

6. **Keep a simple record.**  
   Note the name of the person you spoke with, the requirement they gave you, and any document or message you sent.

7. **Call 911 only if there is immediate danger or an emergency.**  
   An administrative pickup problem is not, by itself, an emergency.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle every long-term custody, guardianship, or school-record question during today’s pickup.
- You do not need to send every document you have before the program states what is required.
- You can ask about longer-term record updates after your child is safely with an authorized adult.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to ask for a calm explanation of the program’s procedure and a safe temporary plan for your child. You can deal with the immediate pickup first and take later questions one step at a time.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate pickup problem. Longer-term authority and record changes vary by state and program and may need local legal help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If longer-term authority remains unclear, consider contacting local legal aid or a qualified attorney.
