# What to do if… you are asked to verify identity for official processes and you cannot find the right documents (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the requesting agency for their specific "Alternative Documents List" and immediately order certified copies of your birth certificate and Social Security card through official government portals.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use third-party "expediting" websites that charge extra fees; only use official .gov or state vital records sites.
- Do not mail your last remaining original document to any agency; use certified copies or in-person appointments.
- Do not assume an "emergency passport" issued abroad will be accepted for domestic REAL ID or driver's license applications.
- Do not attempt to use photocopies or digital scans for federal or state verification unless specifically told they are acceptable.
- Do not carry your Social Security card in your wallet once the replacement arrives.

## What to do now
1. **Request the "Alternative Document List" from the verifying agency.** 
   **The entire first sentence of each step must be bolded.** Many agencies have a "Secondary ID" or "Tier 2" list that accepts combinations like a certified birth certificate plus a utility bill, a voter registration card, or a W-2 form.
2. **Order certified copies of vital records from the state of occurrence.** 
   **Secure long-form certified copies of birth, marriage, and death certificates through the relevant state's Vital Records office.** 
   - Use the CDC "Where to Write for Vital Records" directory to find the correct state office.
   - If your name has changed, you must also order a certified marriage certificate or court order to serve as a "linking document."
3. **Report a lost or stolen passport immediately to the Department of State.** 
   **Submit Form DS-64 (Statement Regarding a Lost or Stolen Passport) online or by mail to invalidate the old document and protect against identity theft.** If you need a replacement, you must apply in person using Form DS-11.
4. **Apply for a replacement Social Security card through the SSA.** 
   **Log in to your "my Social Security" account to see if you are eligible for an online replacement.** 
   - Online replacement is typically available only if you are a US citizen, age 18+, have a US mailing address, and are not changing your name.
   - If ineligible for online service, you must mail an application or visit a local SSA office with proof of identity (such as a certified birth certificate).
5. **Check for "TSA ConfirmID" if you need to fly within 10 days without ID.** 
   **As of 2026, travelers without a REAL ID or passport can use the TSA ConfirmID service for a $45 fee.** This involves an online identity verification check and is valid for a single 10-day window for airport security.
6. **Obtain a temporary paper ID from the DMV or BMV.** 
   **Visit your state motor vehicle agency to request a replacement license; they will often provide a temporary paper permit that serves as secondary ID while the plastic card is mailed.** Check if your state allows online replacement for lost licenses.
7. **Notify the executor or probate court if documents are missing for an estate.** 
   **If you cannot find the deceased person's documents, inform the probate clerk or the estate attorney immediately.** They can often use their legal authority to request records directly from state agencies that are restricted to the public.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace every membership card or non-official ID immediately.
- You do not need to finalize probate filings until the core identity documents (death certificate and executor ID) are secured.
- You do not need to pay for premium "rushed" shipping on every document; prioritize the one required for your most urgent task (e.g., the death certificate for funeral/banking).

## Important reassurance
It is common for documents to be misplaced during a crisis or after a death. Federal and state agencies have established "secondary proof" protocols specifically for these situations. Starting the replacement process now creates a paper trail that many institutions will accept as "proof of application" while you wait for the final records.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for missing identity documents in the USA. It does not cover legal disputes over identity, international visa requirements, or complex estate litigation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. 2026 REAL ID requirements and state DMV rules vary significantly. Always verify current requirements directly with the requesting agency's official .gov website before submitting fees or sensitive data.
