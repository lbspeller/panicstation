# What to do if… you receive a message claiming there is an explicit video of you and you are sent a screenshot as “proof” (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all communication immediately and do not pay any money or send further images. Document the threat, secure your accounts, and report the incident to the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay any money, cryptocurrency, or gift cards to the extortionist.
- Do not send more images or videos to "prove" who you are or "verify" the content.
- Do not engage in conversation or try to negotiate for more time.
- Do not click on links, download attachments, or install "security" apps sent by the individual.
- Do not attempt to investigate the source or "look closer" at the media provided as proof.
- Do not delete your account or the message thread until you have captured evidence.
- Do not hire third-party "takedown" or "hacker" services that contact you via DMs.
- Do not assume the content is real; screenshots are frequently faked or AI-generated.

## What to do now
1. **Capture evidence before taking any other action.** 
   - Take screenshots of the threat, the profile/username, the ID or phone number, and any payment instructions.
   - Do not open the media if it requires a download; screenshot the message containing it.
2. **Stop contact and block the individual.**
   - Once evidence is saved, block the account on the platform and any associated phone numbers.
   - Set your social media profiles to private and restrict direct messages to "friends only."
3. **Secure your primary accounts immediately.**
   - Change the password for your primary email account first, then your social media and banking accounts.
   - Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all accounts, choosing an authenticator app over SMS if available.
   - Log out of all other "active sessions" or "recognized devices" in your account settings.
4. **Report the crime to federal authorities.**
   - File a report at **ic3.gov** (FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center). Include all usernames and payment details.
   - If the person threatening you is attempting to arrange a physical meeting or threatens immediate physical violence, call **911**.
5. **Use age-specific technical mitigation tools.**
   - If you are age 18 or older: Use **StopNCII.org** to proactively hash and block the sharing of specific images/videos on participating platforms.
   - If you (or the person in the media) are under 18: Use the **NCMEC CyberTipline** at report.cybertip.org and the **Take It Down** tool.
6. **Request platform-level removal.**
   - Report the specific messages or posts to the app or website using their "Harassment" or "Non-consensual Intimate Imagery" reporting tools.
   - Note that under federal law, major platforms are required to review and act on verified intimate imagery takedown requests within 48 hours.
7. **Contact crisis support if you feel overwhelmed.**
   - Call or text **988** to reach the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline for confidential, immediate emotional stabilization.

## What can wait
- Determining if the media is real, a deepfake, or a lookalike.
- Identifying the true identity or location of the extortionist.
- Deciding whether to permanently delete your social media presence.
- Notifying your employer, school, or family (this may not be necessary if the situation is contained).

## Important reassurance
Sextortionists rely on creating a state of high-intensity shame and panic to force you into making quick, irreversible decisions. Receiving a threat like this is a common criminal tactic and does not reflect your character. Taking a moment to pause and follow reporting protocols is the most effective way to stop the cycle.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for online extortion involving sexual imagery. It does not cover long-term legal representation, forensic device analysis, or reputation management.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and is not legal advice. The "Take It Down Act" (2025) provides federal protections for the removal of non-consensual intimate imagery, but you may still need to work directly with platform moderation teams to enforce these rights.
