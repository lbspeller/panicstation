# What to do if… you receive a message claiming there is an explicit video of you and you are sent a screenshot as “proof (USA)

## Short answer
Stop responding, do not pay, and do not send anything else. Keep the messages you already have, secure your accounts, and report through official channels and the platform where it happened.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay, send more images, send money, send gift cards, share codes, or agree to “verify” anything.
- Don’t keep chatting to negotiate, explain, plead, or “buy time.”
- Don’t click links, open attachments, scan QR codes, or install apps they send.
- Don’t delete messages, usernames, payment demands, screenshots, or account details in panic.
- Don’t delete your accounts before checking security settings and saving the basic details you already have.
- Don’t post publicly about it or share their messages widely.
- Don’t pay unsolicited “recovery,” “takedown,” or “investigation” services that contact you after the threat.
- Don’t assume the screenshot proves the video is real; screenshots can be faked, edited, copied from elsewhere, or made from manipulated images.

## What to do now
1. **Stop replying.** Do not argue, threaten, apologise, bargain, or answer new messages from the same person.
2. **Save the basic details you already have.**
   - Keep the message thread, screenshot, username, handle, profile URL, phone number, email address, payment demand, and any wallet, gift-card, or payment details they gave.
   - Write down the date, time, platform, and how they first contacted you.
   - Keep originals where possible; screenshots are useful, but original messages may help if a platform or law enforcement asks for them.
3. **Block and report the account on the platform.**
   - Use the app or site’s reporting tool for sextortion, blackmail, threats, or nonconsensual intimate images.
   - If they create new accounts, do not reply; block and report those too.
4. **Secure your accounts, starting with email.**
   - Change your email password first, then passwords for affected social, messaging, and cloud accounts.
   - Turn on two-factor authentication.
   - Check logged-in devices, active sessions, recovery email addresses, recovery phone numbers, and connected apps; remove anything unfamiliar.
5. **Report through the official US route for online crime.**
   - File a complaint with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center.
   - Keep a copy of what you submit.
   - IC3 generally asks you to type or paste details and does not accept file attachments, so keep original messages and files in a safe place in case an agency asks for them later.
6. **Use child-safety routes if anyone involved is under 18.**
   - If the threat involves suspected online sexual exploitation of someone under 18, report to NCMEC CyberTipline.
   - If the image or video was taken when you were under 18 and you are worried it may be shared online, use NCMEC Take It Down.
7. **Use immediate emergency help only if there is immediate danger.**
   - If someone is trying to meet you in person, threatening immediate physical harm, or you believe you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911.
8. **Bring in one safe person for support.**
   - Send one trusted person a simple message: “I’m being blackmailed online with a sexual threat. I don’t need advice yet; please help me stay calm while I secure accounts and report it.”
   - If you feel at risk of harming yourself or you feel unable to stay safe, call or text 988, or use 988 chat.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether the screenshot is real, fake, edited, or a deepfake.
- Investigating who the person is.
- Warning everyone publicly.
- Reputation management, school or workplace decisions, changing your number, or deleting accounts.
- Collecting large amounts of extra evidence beyond keeping what you already have.

## Important reassurance
This kind of message is designed to create shame, panic, and urgency so you act before thinking. Being targeted does not mean you did anything wrong, and paying or complying is not required for you to start taking protective steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stop escalation, preserve what you already have, secure accounts, and use official reporting or support routes. Later decisions, including legal options, platform takedown steps, school or workplace support, or specialist advocacy, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are under 18, or the imagery involves someone under 18, treat it as a child-safety issue and use child-specific reporting and support routes. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
