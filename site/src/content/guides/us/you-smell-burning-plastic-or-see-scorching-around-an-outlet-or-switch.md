# What to do if… you smell burning plastic or see scorching around an outlet or switch (USA)

## Short answer
Treat any acrid, fishy, or plastic-burning smell from an electrical fixture as an active fire risk: **immediately stop using the outlet or switch and turn off power at the breaker panel**. If you see active smoke, flames, or persistent sparking, **evacuate the building and call 911**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not plug anything into the outlet or flip the switch to "test" it.
- Do not touch any discolored, melted, or hot surfaces with your bare hands.
- Do not reset a circuit breaker or replace a fuse that has tripped or blown more than once.
- Do not replace a blown fuse with one of a higher amperage rating or a different type.
- Do not use water or liquid cleaners on any electrical component that smells or looks scorched.
- Do not remove the faceplate or attempt to inspect the wiring behind the wall yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Check for active fire or smoke immediately.** If you see flames, visible smoke, or continuous sparking, **leave the premises at once, close doors behind you to contain the fire, and call 911 from a safe distance**.
2. **Turn off the power at the main service panel if it is safe to do so.** Locate the circuit breaker for the affected room and **switch it to the OFF position**. 
   - If the specific circuit is not clearly labeled, turn **OFF the Main Breaker** to cut power to the entire property.
3. **Leave the breaker or fuse in the OFF position.** Do not attempt to restore power even if the smell appears to dissipate; **electrical faults often continue to generate heat behind the wall even without a load**.
4. **Unplug devices only if they are not hot or melted.** If the plug or outlet face is scorched, discolored, or feels warm, **leave the device plugged in** and rely solely on the breaker to cut power.
5. **Clear any flammable materials away from the affected area.** Once the power is confirmed off, move paper, curtains, furniture, or debris **at least three feet away** from the scorched outlet or switch.
6. **Contact a licensed electrician for an emergency inspection.** Specify that you have **visible scorching or a burning plastic / fishy odor** and that the circuit is currently isolated.
7. **Notify your landlord or property manager immediately if you rent.** Inform them that you have **shut off the power due to a suspected electrical fire hazard** and require a professional repair.
8. **Document the visible damage with a photograph.** Take one clear photo of the scorch marks or melting **only after the power is off** to provide to the electrician or for insurance purposes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the issue is a loose wire, an overloaded circuit, or a faulty device right now.
- You do not need to decide on long-term electrical upgrades or panel replacements during the crisis.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of repairs until the immediate fire risk is neutralized by a professional.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel anxious or uncertain when you smell something burning in your home. These scents—often described as acrid plastic, rubber, or fish—are the primary early warning signs of electrical failure, and taking immediate action to cut power is the standard safety procedure.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and hazard isolation only. A licensed electrician must be consulted to perform repairs, as electrical faults behind walls can lead to structure fires even after the initial smell fades.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute professional electrical or fire safety advice. If you are unsure of the safety of your home, evacuate and contact emergency services.
