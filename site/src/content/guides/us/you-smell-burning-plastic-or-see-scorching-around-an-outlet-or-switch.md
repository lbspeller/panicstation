# What to do if… you smell burning plastic or see scorching around an outlet or switch (USA)

## Short answer
Assume an electrical overheating or fire risk: stop using that outlet or switch, and turn off power at the breaker panel only if it is safe to do so. If there is smoke, flame, ongoing sparking, or you feel unsafe, leave and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t plug something in to test the outlet.
- Don’t keep flipping the switch.
- Don’t keep resetting a breaker that trips again.
- Don’t touch a hot outlet cover, switch plate, melted plastic, plug prongs, or exposed metal.
- Don’t remove the cover plate or try to look behind the outlet or switch.
- Don’t use water or anything wet on it.
- Don’t use a power strip or extension cord as a workaround on the same outlet.

## What to do now
1. Check for immediate danger. If you see flames, smoke, or ongoing sparking, leave immediately, close doors behind you if you can do that safely, and call 911.
2. If it is safe, dry, and the breaker panel is not hot, smoking, buzzing, or sparking, shut off power at the breaker panel. Turn off the breaker for that room or circuit. If you cannot identify it quickly, turn off the main breaker.
3. If the breaker trips again, leave it off. Do not keep trying to reset it.
4. Unplug devices only if it is safe. If the plug, cord, outlet, or switch is hot, stuck, melted, scorched, smoking, or sparking, leave it alone and keep the power off.
5. If the smell seems tied to one plug-in item, such as a charger, appliance, power strip, or extension cord, keep that item unplugged and do not use it in another outlet. Keep it away from paper, bedding, curtains, furniture, and anything else that can burn.
6. Move nearby combustibles away from the outlet or switch if you can do it without touching the damaged area.
7. Take a quick photo from a safe distance and note which breaker you turned off. This can help the electrician, landlord, or property manager.
8. Call a licensed electrician promptly. Say there was a burning plastic smell, heat, scorching, buzzing, crackling, sparking, or breaker tripping at an outlet or switch, and say whether the circuit is currently off.
9. If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager right away. Tell them the outlet or switch may be overheating and that the circuit is off if you were able to turn it off safely.
10. If the smell, heat, buzzing, smoke, or sparking seems to come from the breaker panel, meter, service cable, or outside utility equipment, do not touch it. Leave and call 911 if there is smoke, flame, heat, or sparking; otherwise contact your electric utility and a licensed electrician for urgent guidance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to turn the power back on to see if the smell stopped.
- You do not need to decide on permanent repairs, costs, replacement parts, insurance, or fault in the moment.
- You do not need to clean scorch marks before the problem has been checked.

## Important reassurance
You are not overreacting by taking this seriously. A hot, scorched, buzzing, sparking, or burnt-smelling outlet or switch can be a warning sign of overheating, and turning power off if safe, keeping the area clear, and getting qualified help are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A licensed electrician may need to check the outlet or switch, wiring, breaker, connected device, and any signs of heat damage before the circuit is used again.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional electrical, fire safety, legal, insurance, or property-management advice. If there is smoke, flame, ongoing sparking, or you feel unsafe, leave and call 911.
