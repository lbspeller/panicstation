# What to do if… you check your ketones and they are high while you feel nauseated or unwell (USA)

## Short answer
High ketones with nausea or feeling unwell can be a medical emergency. Go to the emergency room now; call 911 if symptoms are severe, worsening quickly, or you cannot get there safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delay emergency care while trying repeated home corrections or troubleshooting every possible cause.
- Do not stop insulin unless a clinician specifically tells you to.
- Do not exercise to try to lower glucose when ketones are present.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel weak, dizzy, drowsy, confused, or are vomiting.
- Do not assume you are safe because your glucose is not very high, especially if you take an SGLT2 inhibitor.

## What to do now
1. **Go to the emergency room now.** Call 911 instead if you have trouble breathing, feel confused or unusually drowsy, are vomiting repeatedly, cannot keep liquids down, are getting worse quickly, or cannot safely get to the emergency room.
2. **Arrange safe transport.** Ask someone to take you, or use emergency medical services. Tell them that your ketones are high and you feel unwell.
3. **Record the key details only if this does not delay leaving or calling 911.** Note your ketone reading, glucose reading, the times of the readings, whether you have vomited, and when you last took insulin. Bring your meter, strips, medication list, and pump supplies if they are close at hand.
4. **If you use insulin, continue it as prescribed unless a clinician tells you otherwise.** Do not improvise extra doses unless they are part of your personal sick-day plan.
5. **If you take an SGLT2 inhibitor, tell the emergency room staff immediately.** Some generic medicine names in this group end in `-gliflozin`. Ketone-related illness can still be urgent even when glucose is not very high.
6. **If you use an insulin pump, do a quick check while transport is being arranged.** Look for an obvious leak or disconnection at the pump, tubing, or infusion site. Do not delay emergency care to keep troubleshooting.
7. **If you can keep fluids down, take small sips of water while waiting for transport.** Do not delay emergency care to keep trying fluids.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove that this is definitely DKA before seeking care.
- You do not need to work out why the ketones are high before leaving.
- You do not need to make long-term medication or pump decisions now.
- A detailed sick-day plan can wait until after urgent assessment.

## Important reassurance
You are not overreacting by going to the emergency room. High ketones with nausea or feeling unwell are enough reason to seek urgent assessment now.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. After urgent assessment and once you are stabilized, a diabetes clinician can help with a personal sick-day plan.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice. It does not diagnose DKA or replace urgent assessment by a healthcare professional.
