# What to do if… someone keeps trying to isolate you from your group (USA)
## Short answer
Stay with your group or move beside staff or security. Tell one trusted person immediately that you do not want to be alone with the person who keeps trying to separate you.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go somewhere private with them to be polite or end the awkwardness.
- Do not leave the group alone for the bathroom, outside, the parking area, or a ride pickup while they are still trying to isolate you.
- Do not accept a ride, walk, or shortcut with them.
- Do not feel that you have to manage the situation by yourself.
- Do not blame yourself for not finding the perfect words.

## What to do now
1. Move into the middle of your group or stand beside staff, security, or a staffed front desk.
2. Tell one trusted person clearly: “Please stay with me. I do not want to be alone with that person.” Text them if speaking openly feels unsafe.
3. Ask that person to stay with you for any move away from the group, including the bathroom, an exit, a parking area, or a ride pickup.
4. Tell venue staff or security: “I feel unsafe. This person keeps trying to get me alone. Please help me stay separated from them.”
5. If you are leaving, use a staffed main entrance, wait inside until your ride arrives, verify the vehicle details in your app, and keep your trusted person with you.
6. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If making the call yourself could increase danger, ask staff, security, or a trusted person to call.
7. If you are concerned about sexual assault or abuse, RAINN’s hotline can provide confidential support and referrals. RAINN also publishes safety-planning information for harassment or stalking concerns.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to report the person or confront them.
- You do not have to explain your boundary beyond saying that you are staying with your group.
- You do not have to work out the full story tonight.
- You do not have to decide what this means for any friendship or relationship right now.

## Important reassurance
Wanting to stay with the group is enough reason to stay with the group. Repeated pressure to leave with someone is not your fault, and difficulty responding quickly does not mean that you did anything wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation only. Later decisions may need support from a specialist service or another trusted source of help.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
