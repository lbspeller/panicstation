# What to do if… your engine temperature warning light comes on and you have pulled over safely (USA)

## Short answer
Keep the engine off and give it time to cool. Do not remove a radiator cap or coolant-system cap while the engine is hot, steaming, hissing, or likely pressurized; arrange roadside help or a tow if there is any doubt.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remove a radiator cap or coolant-system cap while the engine is hot, steaming, hissing, or likely pressurized.
- Do not restart the engine repeatedly just to test whether the warning has cleared.
- Do not add coolant or water while the engine is still hot.
- Do not stand close to moving traffic to inspect the vehicle.
- Do not continue driving if the warning remains, returns, or is accompanied by steam, smoke, or a visible leak.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the hazard lights on and stay in the safest available position.** Avoid standing near moving traffic. If the vehicle is in an active lane, you are at immediate risk from traffic, or you suspect a fire, call **911**.
2. **Leave the engine off and wait.** Do not touch cooling-system caps. If there is steam or hissing, stay back from the front of the vehicle until it stops.
3. **Check the owner’s manual before doing any roadside inspection.** Cooling-system layouts and instructions vary by vehicle. It is reasonable to stop here and call roadside assistance.
4. **Only if the engine is fully cool and the inspection can be done safely, look at the coolant reservoir without removing a radiator cap.** Check for a low level, an empty reservoir, or an obvious puddle under the vehicle.
5. **Only if the owner’s manual permits it, add the specified coolant to the reservoir after the engine is fully cool.** If the reservoir is empty, you see a leak, you are unsure which fluid is required, or the engine is not fully cool, do not guess. Call roadside assistance.
6. **Arrange roadside assistance or a tow rather than continuing to drive if there is any warning sign.** This includes a warning that remains or returns, a rising temperature gauge, steam, smoke, an empty reservoir, a visible leak, or uncertainty about whether the vehicle is safe to move.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the mechanical cause at the roadside.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop or make repair-cost decisions while you are still stressed.
- You do not need to decide whether the problem was minor.
- Notes about the warning message, gauge position, steam, smell, or visible leaks can wait until you are in a safer and calmer position.

## Important reassurance
Pulling over safely and switching the engine off was the important first move. Waiting for the engine to cool and avoiding hot cooling-system caps reduces the risk of burns and avoids adding pressure to the situation.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need roadside assistance, a tow operator, the vehicle manufacturer’s instructions, or a qualified mechanic.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger from traffic or suspect a fire, prioritize personal safety and call emergency services.
