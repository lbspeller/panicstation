# What to do if… an airbag or SRS warning light comes on before you drive (USA)
## Short answer
Pause the trip if practical. Check your owner’s manual and your vehicle’s recall status before deciding whether to drive, then arrange prompt advice from a dealer, qualified repair facility, or roadside assistance provider.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore the warning and set off as planned.
- Don’t try to clear the warning by disconnecting the battery, pulling fuses, or using guesswork fixes.
- Don’t unplug, probe, or handle wiring or connectors under seats or near air bags.
- Don’t assume the restraint system is working normally because the vehicle starts and drives normally.
- Don’t drive if an official recall result or manufacturer instruction says `Do Not Drive`.

## What to do now
1. **Pause before setting off.**  
   Leave the vehicle parked while you check the warning and your options. Use another vehicle or another form of transport if that is readily available.

2. **Restart the vehicle once and observe the warning.**  
   Switch the vehicle off, then restart it. If the air bag or SRS warning does not clear after startup, or comes on again, check your owner’s manual and arrange prompt professional assessment.

3. **Check whether this is a separate passenger-airbag-off indicator.**  
   A `PASSENGER AIRBAG OFF` indicator can be normal in some situations, including when the passenger seat is empty or the system senses a small occupant. If the result is unexpected, check your owner’s manual. Do not guess or try to alter the system yourself.

4. **Run an official recall check using your VIN.**  
   Use NHTSA’s recall lookup. If the result or your vehicle manufacturer gives a `Do Not Drive` instruction for an open air-bag recall, follow that instruction and contact a dealer to arrange the recall repair. The lookup has limitations, so ask the manufacturer or dealer to confirm if you remain concerned.

5. **Ask whether the vehicle should be towed.**  
   Contact roadside assistance, a dealer, or a qualified repair facility. Explain that the air bag or SRS warning appeared before you set off and ask whether they recommend towing rather than driving.

6. **Arrange a professional restraint-system diagnostic promptly.**  
   Ask the dealer or qualified repair facility to identify the fault, explain which parts of the restraint system may be affected, and confirm the safest way to get the vehicle to them.

7. **Postpone passenger travel if practical.**  
   Children under 13 should ride in the rear seat. Do not place a rear-facing child seat in front of an active air bag.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact fault yourself.
- You do not need to clear the warning light yourself.
- You do not need to compare repair prices before you know what the diagnostic shows.
- You do not need to decide how to manage the rest of your travel plans until you have paused the immediate trip.

## Important reassurance
An air bag or SRS warning deserves attention, but you do not need to diagnose it yourself. Pause the trip, check the manual and recall status, and ask for professional advice about the next safe step.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and avoid making the problem worse. Later decisions may need help from a dealer, qualified repair facility, roadside assistance provider, or vehicle manufacturer.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not a diagnosis, repair instruction, or substitute for professional advice. Follow your vehicle owner’s manual and any recall or manufacturer instructions for your specific vehicle.
