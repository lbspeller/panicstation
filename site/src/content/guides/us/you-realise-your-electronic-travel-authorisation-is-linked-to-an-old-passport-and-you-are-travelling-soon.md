# What to do if… you realise your electronic travel authorisation is linked to an old passport and you are travelling soon (USA)

## Short answer
Apply for a new travel authorisation immediately using your current passport number, as most digital permissions are non-transferable and tied electronically to the specific passport used during the application. Do not attempt to travel with a mismatch, as airlines will likely block boarding during the digital document verification process.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter your old passport number into the airline’s check-in system to "force" a match.
- Do not use third-party "urgent" processing websites; use only the official government portal for the destination country.
- Do not assume that carrying the physical old passport alongside the new one is sufficient for digital authorisations (ETA/eTA/ESTA).
- Do not wait until you arrive at the airport to address the mismatch.
- Do not ignore transit requirements; even a plane change without leaving the airport often requires a valid authorisation linked to your current passport.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific authorisation details for your destination and any transit points.**
   - Locate your confirmation email or reference number.
   - Note exactly which passport number was used for the original application.
   - Confirm if you are traveling to or through the UK, Canada, Australia, or New Zealand, as these systems have strict "new passport = new application" rules.

2. **Verify the official policy for passport updates for your specific destination.**
   - **UK ETA:** As of early 2026, a new ETA is required if you have a new passport; the fee is £20 (as of April 2026).
   - **Canada eTA:** A new eTA is mandatory for a new passport number. (Note: U.S. Lawful Permanent Residents/Green Card holders are exempt from the eTA but must carry their Green Card and valid passport).
   - **Australia ETA (Subclass 601):** You must submit a brand-new application via the official Australian ETA app; these cannot be transferred from an old passport.
   - **New Zealand NZeTA:** You may request a passport update through the official Immigration New Zealand website, but this typically requires at least 10 days; if traveling sooner, a new application is safer.

3. **Submit a new application using your current, valid passport immediately.**
   - Use the official government website or app only.
   - **Double-check every character of your new passport number, name spelling, and date of birth before submitting.**
   - Ensure the nationality matches the passport you are holding.

4. **Update your Advance Passenger Information (API) with the airline.**
   - Log in to your flight booking and update the passport details to match your new document.
   - **Do this only after you have received confirmation or submitted the new travel authorisation request.**

5. **Monitor your email for approval or requests for further information.**
   - Most systems (like the UK ETA or Canada eTA) provide automated responses within minutes, but some can take up to 72 hours.
   - Check your spam folder frequently for correspondence from government domains (.gov).

6. **Gather a digital and physical "proof bundle" for the airport.**
   - Keep copies of the new approval notice, the payment receipt, and the reference number.
   - **While these do not override the electronic system check, they provide a reference point for airline gate agents if a manual search is required.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to cancel your flights or accommodation until you have received a definitive refusal on the new application.
- You do not need to update secondary ID (like a driver's license) as long as your primary travel passport is correctly linked.
- You do not need to notify the destination's embassy unless the automated system specifically directs you to do so after a technical failure.

## Important reassurance
Passport number mismatches are one of the most common reasons for travel delays. Most destination governments have built their systems to handle rapid new applications for exactly this reason. As long as you have identified the error before heading to the airport, there is usually time to rectify the link through a fresh submission.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for standard electronic travel authorisations (ETA, eTA, ESTA). It does not cover long-term visas, diplomatic passports, or situations involving prior criminal convictions or travel bans, which require direct consular contact.

## Important note
Travel requirements and fees are subject to change by sovereign governments without notice. Airlines have the final authority to deny boarding if their digital systems do not show valid permission. This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal or immigration advice.
