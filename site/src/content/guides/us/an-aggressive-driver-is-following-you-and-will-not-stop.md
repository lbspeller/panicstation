# What to do if… an aggressive driver is following you and will not stop (USA)

## Short answer
Do not engage or drive home. Drive toward a police department or well-lit populated place, and call 911 if you feel threatened or the driver continues following you. Keep your doors locked and windows up, and follow the 911 call taker’s instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive home or to another predictable destination.
- Do not pull over in an isolated place to confront the other driver.
- Do not brake-check, race, or make risky turns or lane changes to lose them.
- Do not make eye contact, gesture, yell, or honk in retaliation.
- Do not record video while driving.
- Do not get out of your vehicle if the other driver stops near you or approaches your window.

## What to do now
1. **Create space without escalating.** Keep your driving steady. If it is safe, change lanes or use a normal route change to let the other vehicle pass or create distance.
2. **Keep moving toward help.** Stay on main roads where possible. Drive toward a police department or well-lit populated place rather than a quiet side street or your home.
3. **Call 911 if you feel threatened or the driver keeps following you.** Ask a passenger to call if you have one. If you are alone, keep control of the vehicle and call as safely as circumstances allow.
4. **Answer the 911 call taker’s questions.** Start with your location, direction of travel, and what the other driver is doing. Give the other vehicle’s description and license plate only if you can observe them safely.
5. **If you reach a safer stopping place, remain in the vehicle if it is safe to do so.** Keep your doors locked and windows up. Do not get out to speak with the other driver.
6. **Avoid further engagement.** Do not make eye contact, gesture, yell, honk in retaliation, or follow the other vehicle.
7. **Document what happened only after the immediate danger has passed and you are safely stopped.** Save any existing dashcam footage and write down the time, route, vehicle description, plate number if known, and what happened.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now how to handle the other driver beyond reaching safety and getting help.
- You do not need to gather perfect identifying details while driving.
- You do not need to confront the driver, follow them, post online, contact insurance, or review footage until the immediate danger has passed and you are safely stopped.
- You do not need to decide about a non-emergency follow-up report until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
An aggressive driver continuing to follow you can be frightening. Reducing engagement, avoiding your home, moving toward other people, and bringing in emergency help are useful first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes only. Later decisions, including non-emergency reporting or insurance questions, may need help from local law enforcement, your insurer, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.
