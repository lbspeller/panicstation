# What to do if… you discover an aquarium, waterbed, or large container is leaking and could cause major water damage (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the water source immediately and disconnect power from a dry location; do not enter standing water if electrical devices are submerged.

## Do not do these things
- Don't step into standing water to reach a wall outlet, power strip, or the main electrical panel.
- Don't touch wet cords, plugs, or aquarium equipment (heaters / pumps) while the floor is damp.
- Don't move a leaking aquarium or waterbed while it still contains water; the weight shifts can cause total structural failure.
- Don't use a standard vacuum or plug-in appliances in the wet area until power is verified off at the breaker.
- Don't ignore water dripping from a ceiling or light fixture; this indicates a hidden hazard in the structure.
- Don't turn breakers back on if you suspect water entered the electrical system.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a dry surface and establish a safe pause.** Keep all residents and pets away from the wet area. Use a battery-powered flashlight rather than a phone light if you need to inspect dark corners.
2. **Identify electrical hazards immediately.** If you see sparks, hear buzzing / crackling, or see water entering a light fixture or outlet, evacuate the room and call **911** for the fire department.
3. **Shut off power from a dry location.** 
   - Use the **emergency outdoor service disconnect** (standard on many homes since 2026) to shut off power to the entire building without entering.
   - If using an indoor panel, stand on a dry surface and use **one hand** and a **dry, non-conductive tool** (like a wooden broom handle) to flip the main breaker.
4. **Stop the water flow at the source.** 
   - If the leak is a plumbing connection (e.g., a waterbed fill-line), close the local isolation valve by turning it clockwise.
   - If the local valve is stuck or submerged, locate the **main water shutoff** for the house or unit and close it completely. 
   - If the main valve is outside, you may need a **water meter key** or T-handle tool to turn it.
5. **Contain the spill if it is safe to do so.** 
   - Surround the area with heavy towels or blankets to slow the spread to other rooms.
   - If water is dripping from a ceiling, place a bucket underneath and move all electronics and furniture from the path of the drip.
   - For aquariums, use a siphon or bucket to drain the water level below the leak point immediately.
6. **Notify emergency contacts.**
   - **Renters:** Call the emergency maintenance line or landlord immediately.
   - **Condo owners:** Notify building management, as water often travels through floors to units below.
   - **Homeowners:** Contact a licensed plumber if the leak is part of the home's integrated plumbing or if you cannot close the main valve.
7. **Document the scene for insurance.** Take clear photos or video of the leak source, the extent of the puddle, and any water that contacted walls or electrical outlets.
8. **Begin professional drying if water persists.** If carpets or drywall remain wet after 24 hours, contact a water restoration specialist to prevent mold growth and structural "wicking."

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide which possessions are ruined right now; stabilization is the priority.
- You do **not** need to begin scrubbing or cleaning floors until the electrical safety is confirmed.
- You do **not** need to worry about permanent repairs or aquarium resealing in the first hour.

## Important reassurance
Sudden water leaks are high-stress events, and the sound of running water can cause immediate panic. Once the water is stopped and the power is isolated, the immediate danger has passed. The transition from crisis to cleanup is a standard process that professionals handle daily.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a sudden large-volume water leak. It does not cover long-term mold remediation, structural repair, or the specialized care required for aquatic life during relocation.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only. If you are unsure if the floor is energized or if the structure is safe, evacuate and wait for professional assistance.
