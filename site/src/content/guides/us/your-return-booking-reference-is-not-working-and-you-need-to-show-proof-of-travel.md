# What to do if… your return booking reference is not working and you need to show proof of travel (USA)

## Short answer
Find your genuine itinerary, receipt, or e-ticket email and ask the airline or ticket agent to resend written confirmation. Avoid buying a replacement ticket until the airline or ticket agent has checked whether the existing booking can be located, unless you need to make an urgent decision.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use altered, fake, or non-ticket documents as proof.
- Do not panic-cancel or rebook before the airline or ticket agent has tried to locate the existing booking.
- Do not assume that one failed website lookup proves the reservation or ticket is missing.
- Do not hand an unlocked phone to someone unnecessarily; keep control of your device while showing documents.
- Do not post itinerary, boarding-pass, or booking-reference images publicly.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a brief pause.** If you are at a counter, say that the booking reference is not loading and ask to step aside while you retrieve the confirmation.
2. **Search for genuine booking documents.** Look in your email and saved files for the airline name, travel date, itinerary, receipt, e-ticket, ticket number, confirmation, or record locator. Open the original email or PDF so it is ready to show.
3. **Retry the lookup carefully.** Copy the booking reference exactly from the original confirmation. Check whether the entered surname matches the confirmation, including spaces, hyphens, apostrophes, and any middle names. A lookup may fail when the entered details do not match the stored format.
4. **Contact the booking route you used.** Ask the airline, travel agent, or third-party site you booked through for the airline record locator, the e-ticket number if one was issued, and a resent itinerary or receipt.
5. **Ask airline staff to check the record.** At the airport, speak to airline staff. Away from the airport, use the airline’s official support route. Ask whether they can locate the booking and confirm what document they need you to show. Give only the identifying details they request.
6. **Keep a ready-to-show document set.** Have the original itinerary or receipt, any e-ticket number, and the seller’s confirmation email open. A payment confirmation can support your explanation, but it may not prove by itself that a usable ticket was issued.
7. **If this is for Visa Waiver Program travel to the United States, show genuine return or onward documentation when requested.** If the reference still does not load, ask the airline or ticket agent for a resent confirmation or another genuine record they can provide.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to buy another ticket unless the airline or ticket agent confirms that the existing booking cannot be used and you need to make a time-sensitive choice.
- You do not need to start a card dispute while you are trying to pass the document check.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint at the counter.
- Later, if you were charged but the seller does not provide the booked service, keep your emails, receipts, screenshots, and notes. Ask the seller to resolve it first. If you paid by card, you can then check your card issuer’s dispute process. You can also check the U.S. Department of Transportation complaint routes.

## Important reassurance
A booking reference that does not load does not automatically mean that the ticket is unusable. The airline or ticket agent may be able to clarify the record once they check the original confirmation and travel details.

## Scope note
These are first steps for getting through the immediate document check. Refunds, card disputes, and formal complaints can be handled later and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. The document accepted for travel depends on the airline, route, booking record, and applicable entry rules.
