# What to do if… an after-school program says pickup permissions must be updated immediately after a parent dies (USA)

## Short answer
Request that the program keep the child in safe supervised care while you provide immediate identification and a temporary written authorization from the surviving parent or a documented legal guardian.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to sign documents using the deceased parent's name or access their digital accounts to change permissions.
- Do not assume staff will bypass safety protocols because of the circumstances; their primary duty is to prevent unauthorized release.
- Do not engage in a heated dispute in front of the child; request to speak with the program director in a private office.
- Do not surrender original legal documents or death certificates unless the program can make copies and return the originals to you immediately.
- Do not make permanent changes to the child's long-term pickup list or school records until legal authority is fully confirmed.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure the child remains under professional supervision while you resolve the authorization issue.** **Tell the program staff:** “Please keep the child safely supervised indoors with a staff member until we verify the release authorization.”
2. **Request a direct meeting with the Site Director or the highest-ranking administrator available.** **Ask for the specific policy requirements for an emergency release today.**
   - Ask if they require a specific form, a verified email, or a portal update from the surviving legal parent.
   - Ask if a phone verification with a password or security question is an option.
3. **Identify the individual who currently holds legal custody or guardianship.** **In the absence of a court order to the contrary, a surviving biological or adoptive parent typically has immediate legal authority to authorize pickup.**
   - If the surviving parent is not present, they should send an email or digital authorization immediately.
4. **Provide the minimum information required for a "one-time emergency release" if the program allows it.** **Ask if they will accept a signed statement (digital or physical) from the legal guardian that includes:**
   - The child’s full name and date of birth.
   - The full name and government-issued photo ID details of the person picking up the child.
   - A contact number where the guardian can be reached for immediate voice verification.
5. **Utilize a Caregiver’s Authorization Affidavit if you are a relative caring for the child.** **In many states, such as California or Massachusetts, a specific statutory form allows a relative caregiver to authorize school-related activities without a court order.**
   - Ask the school or program if they have a standard affidavit form you can complete on-site.
6. **Contact the school district’s central office or student services department if the local program staff cannot resolve the issue.** **Request that the district registrar or legal counsel review the situation and provide an override to the after-school site.**
7. **Call the local police non-emergency line only if the child’s safety is at risk or if no authorized adult can be located.** **Inform the dispatcher that this is a welfare and custody coordination issue resulting from a recent death.**
   - Use 911 only if there is an immediate physical threat or a medical emergency.
8. **Keep a detailed record of everyone you speak with and every document provided.** **Write down the name of the administrator, the time of the conversation, and the specific reasons given for any refusal to release.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide a final death certificate or permanent custody orders to secure a single afternoon's pickup.
- You do not need to settle the deceased parent's estate or long-term financial arrangements today.
- You do not need to decide on permanent changes to the child’s school enrollment or future childcare during this immediate crisis.

## Important reassurance
Program staff enforce strict release policies after a parent dies to ensure the child is not taken by someone without legal standing during a time of high emotional volatility. While the timing feels insensitive, these protocols are designed to protect the child’s safety and the integrity of the legal custody process.

## Scope note
This guide addresses the immediate stabilization of a child-release crisis. It does not provide legal advice regarding long-term guardianship, probate, or custody litigation, which vary significantly by state jurisdiction.

## Important note
This is for informational purposes only and is not legal advice. School and after-school policies are governed by state law and district-specific regulations. If you face continued refusal, consult a family law attorney or your local court’s self-help center for assistance with emergency custody orders.
