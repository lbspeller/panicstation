# What to do if… you accidentally put the wrong fuel in your vehicle and have not started the engine (USA)

## Short answer
Do not start the engine or turn the ignition to the "ON" or "Accessory" position. Immediately notify the station attendant and arrange for a professional fuel drain service or a tow to a repair facility.

## Do not do these things
- Do not press the start button or turn the key—even to listen to the radio.
- Do not attempt to "dilute" the fuel by adding the correct fuel on top; modern fuel systems cannot tolerate even small amounts of contamination.
- Do not attempt to siphon the fuel yourself; this creates an immediate fire hazard and violates EPA/station safety protocols.
- Do not allow the vehicle to be towed with the drive wheels on the ground unless the transmission is verified to be in a safe neutral mode.
- Do not approach the driver's seat with a proximity/keyless fob if the vehicle auto-primes the fuel pump when a door opens.

## What to do now
1. **Remove the key from the ignition or move the keyless fob at least 20 feet away from the vehicle.**
   - This prevents the fuel pump from automatically priming or the engine from being started accidentally by you or a passenger.
2. **Alert the gas station staff immediately.**
   - Ask them to pause the pump if necessary and inform them of the situation. 
   - They may have specific hazmat protocols for vehicles stranded at pumps.
3. **Check your owner's manual for "Neutral Override" or "Tow Mode" instructions.**
   - Many 2026-model vehicles with electronic shifters require a specific sequence to stay in neutral while the engine is off.
4. **Move the vehicle to a safe parking spot only if it can be done without starting the engine.**
   - With the help of the station attendant or bystanders, put the car in neutral and push it to a designated area away from the pumps.
5. **Call your roadside assistance provider or a specialized "fuel drain" mobile service.**
   - Clearly state that you have the **wrong fuel in the tank** and the **engine has NOT been started**.
   - Request a flatbed tow to a certified mechanic if a mobile drain service is unavailable.
6. **Contact the vehicle owner or fleet manager if the car is not yours.**
   - **Rental cars:** Call the rental company's emergency line; using an unauthorized mechanic may void your insurance coverage.
   - **Company vehicles:** Follow the specific reporting procedures for fleet maintenance.
7. **Keep your fuel receipt and take a photo of the pump nozzle and display.**
   - This documentation is essential if you later discover the station's fuel was mislabeled or contaminated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact mechanical impact right now; as long as the engine has not run, the damage is likely limited to the cost of the drain.
- You do not need to file an insurance claim immediately; wait until you have a quote from a mechanic for the fuel system flush.
- You do not need to worry about permanent engine failure if the contaminated fuel never reached the fuel lines or injectors.

## Important reassurance
If the engine has not been started, the contaminated fuel is sitting in the tank and has not reached the high-pressure pumps or injectors. This is the best-case scenario for a misfueling error. Professional draining is a standard procedure that typically resolves the issue without requiring expensive part replacements.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. The procedure for draining a fuel tank varies significantly between traditional gasoline vehicles, diesel trucks, and hybrid systems. A certified technician must perform the actual fuel extraction.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace the specific instructions in your vehicle's owner's manual or the advice of a certified mechanic. Improper handling of fuel or incorrect towing of modern drivetrains can cause significant damage or safety hazards. Always follow local fire and environmental regulations regarding fuel spills.
