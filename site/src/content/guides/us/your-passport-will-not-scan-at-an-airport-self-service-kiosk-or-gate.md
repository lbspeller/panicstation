# What to do if… your passport will not scan at an airport self-service kiosk or gate (USA)

## Short answer
Stop repeated attempts and move to a staffed check. An airline agent or CBP officer can usually inspect the passport and tell you the next step.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t force the passport into the reader or bend the photo page hard enough to crease it.
- Don’t spend your boarding or connection time moving from one machine to another.
- Don’t assume a kiosk failure means you will be denied travel.
- Don’t hand your passport to anyone except uniformed airport, airline, or CBP staff, or personnel behind an official counter.
- Don’t try to peel, flatten, glue, tape, clean with liquid, or alter a damaged photo page or laminate.

## What to do now
1. **Move out of the machine retry loop.** If boarding, bag drop, or a connection is getting close, go to the staffed airline counter, staffed bag drop, gate desk, or CBP booth instead of trying more kiosks.
2. **Try one careful re-scan only if it will not cost you time.**
   - Remove any passport cover or wallet.
   - Open the photo page fully and hold it flat without forcing it.
   - Keep the passport still until the reader finishes.
   - Gently wipe dust or moisture from the photo page with a dry soft cloth.
3. **If this is airline check-in, bag drop, or boarding, ask for a staffed document check.**
   - Say: “My passport is not scanning. Can you do a manual document check?”
   - Show the passport and any message on the kiosk, app, or boarding pass.
   - If check-in is closed, go to the gate agent early and explain that the boarding pass still needs document verification.
4. **If this is U.S. arrival processing, follow the referral to CBP staff.**
   - If a Global Entry kiosk or portal says “See Officer for Assistance,” go to the nearest staffed CBP passport control booth.
   - If any arrival kiosk, app, or portal directs you to an officer, follow that instruction rather than restarting the process.
   - Keep your passport, boarding pass, and any printed receipt or on-screen instruction ready.
5. **Use Mobile Passport Control only if it is already suitable for you.**
   - Try it only if you are eligible, your arrival location supports it, and the app scan works quickly.
   - If the app also cannot read the passport, stop and join the staffed CBP line or follow airport staff instructions.
6. **If the passport looks damaged, say so clearly and stop handling it more than necessary.**
   - Mention lifting laminate, tears, water damage, deep scratches, missing pieces, or anything that looks altered.
   - Ask the airline agent or CBP officer what they can accept for this trip.
   - After the immediate travel issue is handled, check the State Department guidance on damaged passports before using it again.

## What can wait
You do not need to work out whether the problem is the passport chip, the photo page, the scanner, the airport system, or your phone right now. You do not need to decide about passport replacement at the kiosk unless airline staff or CBP tell you the document cannot be accepted for this trip. Replacement forms, appointments, and follow-up can wait until the immediate travel disruption is stable.

## Important reassurance
Passport scan failures happen, and being sent to a staffed check is a normal airport fallback. Moving to staff early is usually calmer and safer than repeatedly trying machines while time runs down.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the airport moment. Later decisions may depend on airline rules, CBP inspection, the condition of the passport, your destination’s entry rules, and official passport guidance.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Airport, airline, and CBP procedures can vary, and officers or airline staff make the decision in front of them.
