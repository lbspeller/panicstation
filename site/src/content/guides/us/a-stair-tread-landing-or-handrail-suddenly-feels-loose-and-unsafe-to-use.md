# What to do if… a stair tread, landing, or handrail suddenly feels loose and unsafe to use (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the affected stairs immediately and block both ends of the staircase to prevent others from stepping on the hazard. Notify the property owner or a qualified professional for an urgent safety repair.

## Do not do these things
- Do not apply weight or pressure to the loose section to "test" its strength or stability.
- Do not continue using the stairs while carrying items that obstruct your view or balance.
- Do not attempt a temporary repair with household tape, glue, or mismatched screws.
- Do not ignore the issue if the stairs "hold" once; structural loosening often worsens without warning.
- Do not lean or put your full body weight on a handrail that has moved or pulled away from the wall.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a stable surface without further testing the hazard.** Carefully step off the affected tread or landing onto a solid floor.
2. **Warn others in the vicinity immediately.** Tell anyone nearby to stay off the staircase until it has been inspected.
3. **Block access at the top and bottom of the stairs.** Use physical barriers such as chairs, boxes, or heavy furniture to prevent entry.
   - Attach a clear, visible sign that states "UNSAFE STAIRS / DO NOT USE".
   - Ensure the area is well-lit so the barrier and sign are easily seen.
4. **Identify an alternative route.** Use a different staircase, elevator, or exit if available; if this is the only exit, minimize trips and do not carry heavy loads until a professional assessment is made.
5. **Call 911 if an injury has occurred.** Request emergency medical services if someone has fallen and exhibits signs of:
   - Inability to move or stand.
   - Severe pain, deformity, or heavy bleeding.
   - Head injury or loss of consciousness.
6. **Document the hazard from a safe distance.** Take photos or video showing the visible movement, gaps, or pulled fasteners without standing on or touching the unstable section.
7. **Notify your landlord or property manager in writing immediately if you rent.** 
   - Use an urgent subject line such as "URGENT SAFETY HAZARD: Loose Stair / Handrail".
   - Request an immediate inspection and a plan to secure the area.
8. **Report the hazard to authorities if the owner fails to act.**
   - Dial **311** (where available) to reach local code enforcement or building safety departments.
   - If the property is HUD-assisted or insured, call the **HUD Multifamily Housing Complaint Line at 1-800-685-8470**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific structural cause (e.g., rotted stringers or loose brackets) yourself.
- You do not need to research local building codes or structural requirements right now.
- You do not need to resolve long-term maintenance disputes or legal claims in the immediate aftermath.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel startled or unsteady when a structural element like a stair or rail moves unexpectedly. Taking immediate action to isolate the area and stop use is the most effective way to prevent a fall. Physical reactions like a racing heart or shaking hands are typical responses to a sudden loss of stability.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a sudden structural safety hazard. It does not replace a professional engineering or building inspection, nor does it provide legal or medical diagnosis.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance only. Structural hazards can lead to collapse or serious injury. If you believe there is an imminent risk of a staircase collapsing or if an injury is severe, contact emergency services immediately.
