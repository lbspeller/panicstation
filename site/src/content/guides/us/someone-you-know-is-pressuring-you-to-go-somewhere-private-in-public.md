# What to do if… someone you know is pressuring you to go somewhere private in public (USA)

## Short answer
Stay in public view and move toward a staffed area or other people. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go somewhere out of view just to keep the conversation private.
- Do not get into a vehicle with them if you feel pressured or uneasy.
- Do not hand over your phone, keys, wallet, or bag.
- Do not remain alone with them while trying to explain or calm the situation.
- Do not leave a staffed or visible area because you feel embarrassed.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move toward a busy store, cafe, hotel lobby, reception desk, security desk, or other staffed public area. If moving feels unsafe, stay near other people and call 911.
2. **Use one clear sentence.** Say: “No. I’m staying here.” You do not need to debate or explain.
3. **Ask staff for immediate help.** Say: “I need help. This person is trying to get me somewhere out of view. Please stay with me.”
4. **Ask a bystander directly.** Say to one person: “Please stay with me,” or: “Please call 911.”
5. **Call 911 for an emergency.** Call if you are in immediate danger, threatened, physically pressured, blocked from leaving, or unable to leave safely. Give your location, the nature of the emergency, and any details the call-taker asks for.
6. **Text 911 only if you cannot safely call.** Call if you can; text if you cannot. Text-to-911 is not available everywhere. Start with your location and what is happening. If you receive a bounce-back message, ask someone nearby to call 911 or call when you can do so safely.
7. **Use nearby facility help without delaying emergency help.** On a campus or in a large building, move to the nearest staffed desk or security point. Do not delay calling 911 in an emergency.
8. **Stay with support once you reach a safer pause.** Ask staff, security, or a trusted person to remain with you. Work out any leaving arrangements only when you are safe enough to do so.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now what this means for the relationship.
- You do not need to confront them or provide a full explanation.
- You can decide later whether to make a formal report.
- You can decide later whether to contact a victim-support service.
- You can write down details later, once you are safe enough to do so.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to stay in public view and ask for help. People may freeze or go along under pressure; that does not mean they did anything wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate moment only. Later decisions may depend on what happened, your ongoing safety concerns, and specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
