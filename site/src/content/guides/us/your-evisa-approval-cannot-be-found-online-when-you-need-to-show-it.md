# What to do if… your evisa approval cannot be found online when you need to show it (USA)

## Short answer
Use any offline proof you have, keep your passport details consistent, and ask airline or border staff to check what they can manually or through their document-check system while you contact the official eVisa support route for the destination country.

## Do not do these things
- Do not submit a new application unless the issuing authority or carrier tells you that is the right step.
- Do not guess passport numbers, dates of birth, name order, or reference numbers.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to a stranger; keep it in your hand and show only the relevant screen.
- Do not rely on one connection, browser, email app, or device.
- Do not delete emails, downloads, screenshots, portal messages, or payment receipts until the situation is resolved.
- Do not argue with airline or border staff; ask what information they need to search or verify the record.

## What to do now
1. Write one short identity note you can read from. Include your full name exactly as in your passport, date of birth, passport number, nationality, destination country, travel dates, and any application, receipt, reference, or payment number.

2. Search for the approval in email, files, photos, and downloads. Search inbox, spam, junk, deleted items, attachments, cloud files, and your phone downloads for “visa”, “eVisa”, “ETA”, “approval”, “approved”, “grant”, “application”, “reference”, the destination country name, and the portal or payment provider name.

3. Try the official portal again using simple access fixes. Switch between cellular data and Wi-Fi, turn off VPN temporarily, try another browser, try private browsing, check that the passport number and name order match your application, and use any official “retrieve application”, “resend confirmation”, or “check status” option.

4. Make showable offline proof as soon as anything appears. Screenshot or save the page showing your name, passport number or partial passport number, approval status, visa or authorization type, validity dates if shown, and reference number. Save it somewhere available without internet.

5. Go to a staffed airline check-in or service counter rather than only using a kiosk. Say: “My eVisa approval is not loading right now. I have my passport details and this approval email, screenshot, PDF, receipt, or reference number. Can you check the travel-document requirement and tell me what proof you can accept?” If useful, ask whether they can check their internal document system or an IATA or Timatic-style requirements source.

6. If you are already at a border desk, stay factual and ask for the next check. Give your passport, exact name spelling, date of birth, and any reference number. Ask whether they can search by passport details or whether a supervisor can check the record.

7. Contact the destination country’s official eVisa help or status channel. Send only the identity details needed for the search and ask: “Please resend the approval notice and confirm whether the approval is tied to my passport number. Please tell me what proof an airline or border officer can use if the portal is not loading.”

8. If you are a U.S. citizen abroad and you are stranded, detained, unsafe, or facing an urgent consular problem, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate. If you cannot reach them in an emergency, call the U.S. Department of State: from the U.S. or Canada, 1-888-407-4747; from overseas, +1 202-501-4444. These numbers are for urgent U.S. citizen help abroad, not for changing a foreign eVisa record.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide complaints, refunds, insurance claims, or a full rebooking plan while you are still trying to verify the approval.
- You do not need to gather every travel document at once; focus first on passport details, approval proof, reference numbers, and the official status route.
- You do not need to set up perfect backups until you are past the desk or checkpoint.
- You do not need to explain the whole history of the application unless staff ask for it.

## Important reassurance
This can happen even when a traveller has applied correctly: portals fail, emails get filtered, records may be searched with slightly different details, and airline checks can depend on the system in front of the staff member. A calm, consistent set of identifiers and any offline proof gives staff the best chance to help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation at an airport, gate, service desk, or border checkpoint. Later decisions about reapplying, rebooking, refunds, complaints, or immigration consequences may need help from the airline, the destination country’s official visa authority, a consular office, travel insurance, or a qualified immigration adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. eVisa systems, airline document checks, and border processes vary by destination country and can change, so rely on the official eVisa authority for the country you are trying to enter and on the carrier’s document-check process.
