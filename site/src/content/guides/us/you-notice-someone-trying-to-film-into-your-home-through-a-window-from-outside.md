# What to do if… you notice someone trying to film into your home through a window from outside (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a room without windows, lock all exterior doors, and call 911 if the person is still present or you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside to confront, shout at, or physically stop the person.
- Do not open the door or window to ask what they are doing.
- Do not attempt to block their camera with your body or hands.
- Do not chase the person or attempt to detain them on your property.
- Do not delete any doorbell or security footage, even if it is blurry or dark.
- Do not post the person's image on social media before speaking to the police.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure interior space immediately.** Get out of the line of sight of all windows. If you are in a room where someone is filming, move to a hallway, bathroom, or bedroom with no windows or closed heavy curtains.
2. **Turn off or dim interior lights.** Reducing light inside makes it more difficult for a camera to capture clear images through glass, especially at night or in low-light conditions.
3. **Lock all entry points.** Ensure all exterior doors and windows are locked. If you have a security system, arm it in "stay" mode.
4. **Call 911 if the person is currently on your property or nearby.** 
   - State your exact location first.
   - Report that an individual is filming into your private residence through a window.
   - Mention if you are alone or if children/vulnerable persons are present.
   - If your county supports Text-to-911 and you cannot speak safely, use the "Call if you can, text if you can't" protocol.
5. **Provide a physical description once safe.** Note the person's height, build, clothing, and the type of device they are using (e.g., smartphone, professional camera, or drone).
6. **Identify the person's location and direction.** Tell dispatch where the person was last seen (e.g., "backyard by the sliding door") and if they left on foot or in a vehicle.
7. **Preserve digital evidence without editing.** Save copies of any doorbell camera, CCTV, or smartphone footage you may have captured. Keep the original files on the device to maintain metadata (time, date, and location tags).
8. **Contact building management if applicable.** If you live in a managed apartment complex, notify on-site security or the management office after calling the police so they can check common area cameras.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the person is a neighbor or a stranger right now.
- You do not need to research specific state voyeurism or "peeping tom" statutes tonight.
- You do do not need to decide on permanent security upgrades like privacy film or motion lighting in the next few minutes.
- You do not need to review long-term legal options regarding restraining orders or trespass warnings.

## Important reassurance
It is a normal physiological response to feel a high state of alert, shaking, or a rapid heartbeat after a privacy violation. These reactions are your body's way of preparing you to stay safe. Moving to a secure area and involving professional help are the most effective steps to regain control of the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. If this is a recurring issue or involves someone you know, you may need to consult with a legal professional or victim advocate regarding stalking and harassment protections.

## Important note
Recording into a private dwelling where there is a reasonable expectation of privacy is a crime in most US jurisdictions. This guide provides general safety information and does not constitute legal advice.
