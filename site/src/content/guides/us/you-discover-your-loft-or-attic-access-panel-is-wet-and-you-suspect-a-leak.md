# What to do if… you discover your loft or attic access panel is wet and you suspect a leak (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the wet attic access area as a possible water and electricity hazard: keep away from wet fixtures, switches, outlets, and wiring. If you can reach the electrical panel safely without standing in water or touching wet surfaces, turn off power to the affected area, then contain drips and arrange a prompt inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch wet light fixtures, switches, outlets, cords, or visible wiring near the wet area.
- Do not enter the attic if it is dark, slippery, actively dripping, or you would have to step on insulation or ceiling framing without safe flooring.
- Do not operate breakers if you would have to stand in water or touch a wet surface to reach the panel.
- Do not run fans, dehumidifiers, vacuums, or extension cords near the wet area until you are sure the affected electrical area is safe.
- Do not climb onto the roof.
- Do not cut, puncture, or pull down a bulging ceiling.

## What to do now
1. **Keep people away from the wet area.**
   - Move people and pets out from under the hatch and the ceiling area around it.
   - Keep furniture, boxes, and stored items away from active drips if you can move them without touching wet electrical items.

2. **Make the electrical risk smaller.**
   - If the electrical panel is dry and safely reachable, switch off the breaker for the affected room or ceiling lights.
   - If you cannot identify the circuit and the panel area is dry and safely reachable, you may shut off the main breaker.
   - If the panel is wet, hard to reach, or you are unsure, do not touch it; call a licensed electrician.

3. **Check only what you can see from the opening.**
   - Use a flashlight from a stable position below or at the hatch.
   - Look for active dripping, wet roof decking, wet insulation, water trails, or a wet area around a vent, pipe, chimney, or roof penetration.
   - Do not climb in to trace the leak if the footing, lighting, or electrical safety is uncertain.

4. **Check for an obvious attic equipment source without entering unsafe space.**
   - Some homes have HVAC equipment, ductwork, condensate pans, or drain lines in the attic.
   - From the hatch only, look for water starting near a drain pan, disconnected drain line, or sweating duct.
   - If the water appears to start near HVAC equipment or a condensate line, contact an HVAC company or plumber.

5. **Contain water without increasing the hazard.**
   - Put a bucket or bin under drips if the area below is safe to approach.
   - Use towels only on the floor or on dry surfaces; keep them away from outlets, cords, fixtures, and wiring.
   - If the ceiling is sagging, cracking, or bulging, keep everyone out of that room and contact a roofing, restoration, or building professional promptly.

6. **Record what happened.**
   - Take photos or video of the wet hatch, ceiling stain, drips, and anything visible from the hatch opening.
   - Note whether it followed heavy rain, wind, snow, ice, a big temperature change, or recent HVAC use.

7. **Contact the right help.**
   - If you own the home, contact a licensed roofing contractor for an urgent roof and attic inspection.
   - If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager promptly with photos and the phrase “possible roof leak or water intrusion.”
   - If water may have reached wiring, fixtures, outlets, or electrical equipment, have a licensed electrician check the area before power is restored.

8. **Limit moisture spread only when it is safe.**
   - Use passive ventilation, such as opening a window in the room, if weather and safety allow.
   - Do not use powered drying equipment near the wet area until the electrical risk has been checked or the affected power is safely off.
   - Once the leak source is stopped and the area is electrically safe, drying wet materials quickly can reduce mold risk.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether this is condensation, a roof leak, an ice dam, gutter overflow, or an HVAC condensate problem.
- Mold cleanup, insulation replacement, ventilation upgrades, and cosmetic repair can wait until the water source is stopped and the area is safe to assess.
- A full insurance decision can wait briefly, but keep photos and check your policy or insurer when you are safe and calmer.

## Important reassurance
A wet attic access panel can look alarming, but the first steps are simple: keep away from electricity, avoid unsafe attic access, contain what you safely can, and get the source checked. You do not have to solve the whole roof or moisture problem in the first few minutes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about roof repair, HVAC repair, electrical safety, mold cleanup, insulation, ventilation, landlord duties, or insurance may need help from qualified professionals.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, electrical, building, insurance, medical, or professional advice. If there is fire, smoke, arcing, sparking, electric shock, or water entering light fixtures or outlets, call 911.
