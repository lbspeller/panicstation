# What to do if… your car keeps stalling at idle and you are stopped somewhere safe (USA)

## Short answer
Make the car visible and secure first: hazard flashers on, transmission in Park or Neutral, parking brake set. If the engine will not idle reliably after one careful restart, stop testing it and call roadside help or a tow service.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep restarting over and over; you can drain the battery and make the situation harder.
- Don’t keep the engine running if you see smoke, smell fuel or burning, or the temperature warning suggests overheating.
- Don’t stand outside the vehicle near moving traffic on a fast road.
- Don’t agree to roadside fixes, pushing, jumping, or towing with a rope or strap in or near traffic.
- Don’t leave the shelter of the car on a high-speed road unless there is an immediate hazard, such as smoke, fire, strong fuel smell, or you can move well away from traffic.

## What to do now
1. **Make it safe and visible.** Turn on the hazard flashers. Put the transmission in Park, or Neutral for a manual, and set the parking brake. Keep your seatbelt on if traffic is close.
2. **Check for immediate danger signs.** If you notice smoke, a strong fuel smell, a burning smell, or unusual heat, turn the engine off. Move people away from traffic if you can do so safely. Call 911 if there is immediate danger, such as fire, smoke, a crash, medical symptoms, or a vehicle blocking traffic.
3. **Try one controlled restart only.** Turn off non-essential electrical loads, such as A/C, rear defroster, heavy audio, and extra chargers. Wait about 10 seconds, then restart once.
4. **If it runs, move only if that is safer.** If you are not fully out of the way, and there are no danger signs, drive slowly to a safer nearby place such as a parking lot, side street, or well-lit area. If it stalls again, stop, secure the car again, and do not try to push it through traffic.
5. **If it will not stay running, stop trying and call help.** Call your roadside assistance, if you have it, or a tow service. Be ready to give:
   - your exact location, including road name, nearest exit, intersection, mile marker, or direction of travel,
   - whether you are on a shoulder, in a parking lot, or in a travel lane,
   - any warning lights, especially oil pressure or engine temperature,
   - any smoke, fuel smell, burning smell, or overheating signs.
6. **Escalate early if the location is unsafe.** If the car is disabled in a travel lane, on a narrow shoulder, near a blind curve, or you cannot get fully out of traffic, call 911 and say you are in a disabled vehicle and give your exact location.
7. **Use local road help only if it is available and safe to call.** In some areas, 511 may provide traveler information or connect to local freeway assistance, but this varies by state or region. Do not rely on 511 for immediate danger; use 911 for emergencies and roadside assistance or a tow service for non-emergency recovery.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause on the roadside.
- You do not need to decide on repairs, parts, or a specific shop right now.
- You do not need to keep testing the car by repeated restarts; one careful attempt is enough before switching to recovery.
- You do not need to open the hood near traffic or inspect the engine if doing so puts you closer to moving vehicles.

## Important reassurance
A car that keeps stalling at idle can feel urgent and embarrassing, but the priority is simple: stay visible, stay out of traffic, and switch to help if it will not run reliably. You are not expected to fix the car at the roadside.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to reduce risk and prevent a roadside situation from escalating. After you are safe or the vehicle has been towed, diagnosis and repair decisions may need a qualified mechanic or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, emergency services, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, in or near a travel lane, see smoke or fire, smell fuel, or have medical symptoms, call 911.
