# What to do if… your cloud photos or videos disappear and you cannot find them anywhere (USA)

## Short answer
Stop entering any passwords or financial data immediately. Switch to a known-safe connection like a mobile hotspot and synchronize your device's date and time settings to resolve certificate validation conflicts.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click "Advanced" and then "Proceed" to bypass warnings on sensitive sites.
- Don’t download or install "security certificates" or "browser patches" offered by pop-up windows.
- Don’t attempt to fix the issue while connected to unverified public Wi-Fi.
- Don’t disable your firewall or antivirus permanently to stop the warnings.
- Don’t assume the warnings are a glitch if they appear on multiple different websites simultaneously.

## What to do now
1. **Close all tabs involving sensitive information.** Exit browser windows for banking, email, healthcare portals, and work-related logins to prevent data interception.
2. **Verify and synchronize the system clock.** Open your device settings and ensure "Set time automatically" is enabled for your current US time zone.
   - An incorrect date or time causes the browser to reject valid security certificates as "expired" or "not yet valid."
3. **Switch to a different network environment.** Disconnect from the current Wi-Fi and connect to a mobile hotspot or a different trusted ISP.
   - If the warnings stop, the original network or router may be compromised or misconfigured.
4. **Trigger the login screen if using public Wi-Fi.** Navigate to a plain HTTP site (such as http://neverssl.com) to force the captive portal or "Accept Terms" page to appear.
   - Many public networks block encrypted traffic until the user authenticates through their specific gateway.
5. **Clear the SSL State in your system settings.** Search for "Internet Options" in Windows, go to the "Content" tab, and click "Clear SSL State."
   - This removes corrupted certificate data that may be cached across all browsers.
6. **Temporarily disable traffic-filtering software.** Turn off VPNs, third-party proxies, and antivirus features specifically labeled "HTTPS Scanning" or "Encrypted Web Protection" to see if they are causing the conflict.
7. **Contact your organization's IT department for managed devices.** If the computer is issued by an employer or school, report that you are receiving "NET::ERR_CERT" or "DLG_FLAGS" errors across multiple sites.
8. **Secure your accounts if you entered credentials during the warning state.** Use a separate, clean device to change your passwords and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
9. **Report suspected network-level interception to CISA.** If you believe a public or corporate network is being actively monitored or spoofed, use the CISA "Report a Cyber Incident" portal.
10. **File a report at IdentityTheft.gov if sensitive data was exposed.** Follow the FTC's recovery plan, which includes placing a free one-year fraud alert on your credit reports via Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reinstall your operating system immediately.
- You do not need to contact the individual administrators of every website showing the warning.
- You do not need to replace your router until you have confirmed the issue persists across multiple devices.

## Important reassurance
Widespread certificate warnings are frequently caused by technical synchronization errors, such as an incorrect system clock or a misconfigured VPN. Following a cautious verification process helps you differentiate between a simple setting error and a genuine security threat without risking your personal data.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for browser-based security warnings. Persistent errors across multiple networks may indicate deep-level malware or hardware failure and require professional technical inspection.

## Important note
This information is for immediate situational guidance and does not replace professional cybersecurity or technical support services. If you suspect financial accounts have been compromised, contact your bank or credit card issuer directly.
