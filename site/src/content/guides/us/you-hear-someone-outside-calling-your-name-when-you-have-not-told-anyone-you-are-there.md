# What to do if… you hear someone outside calling your name when you have not told anyone you are there (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside behind a locked door and do not respond or acknowledge the person. Call 911 immediately if you feel threatened or believe someone is attempting to enter.

## Do not do these things
- Don't open the door or step outside to investigate the source of the voice.
- Don't call back to confirm your identity or indicate that you are alone in the home.
- Don't stand in front of windows, open blinds, or use flashlights that reveal your location.
- Don't unlock the door for any reason, even if the person claims there is an emergency.
- Don't attempt to confront or "scare off" the person by shouting.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure interior room away from doors and ground-floor windows.** Ensure the room has a secondary lock or can be barricaded if necessary.
2. **Confirm all exterior entrance points are locked and secured.** Check the front door, back door, and all accessible windows without standing directly in front of them.
3. **Turn off interior lights and silence your mobile devices.** Reducing light and sound makes it more difficult for someone outside to track your movements or confirm your exact location within the house.
4. **Call 911 if the person refuses to leave or attempts to enter.** Provide the dispatcher with your exact address, your name, and a description of what you are hearing.
   - Stay on the line until the operator tells you to hang up.
   - Follow all instructions provided by the dispatcher.
5. **Use text-to-911 if making a voice call would reveal your location to an intruder.** Use the national guideline: "Call if you can, text if you can't."
   - Enter "911" in the "To" field of your messaging app.
   - Type your exact location and the nature of the emergency.
   - If your area does not support text-to-911, you should receive a "bounce-back" message; you must then place a voice call.
6. **Observe the area only through secure, non-revealing methods.** Use a doorbell camera, peephole, or a window from a concealed angle if it does not require you to expose yourself.
7. **Contact a trusted neighbor or building security if the situation is suspicious but not a direct threat.** Ask them to observe the exterior of your property from their safe location.
8. **Document the incident details once you are in a stable, secure position.** Note the exact time, the specific words used, any recognizable characteristics of the voice, and the direction of any sounds.

## What can wait
- You don't need to determine how the person knows your name right now.
- You don't need to identify the person or their intentions in the moment.
- You don't need to report the incident to property management or non-emergency lines until the immediate situation is resolved.
- You don't need to check your social media or privacy settings until you are no longer in a state of alert.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of alarm or fear when an unexpected person knows your name. Choosing to stay behind a locked barrier and prioritizing your safety is a correct and protective response to an unknown situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for an immediate personal safety concern. It does not cover long-term security planning or legal reporting procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving a potential intruder or threat to life, contact 911 immediately.
