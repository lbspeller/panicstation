# What to do if… your flight is suddenly cancelled and you are at the airport without a clear rebooking option (USA)

## Short answer
Reject unacceptable rebooking offers to trigger your right to an automatic refund to your original payment method. Use the Department of Transportation (DOT) Dashboard to check if the airline must provide meals or lodging for "controllable" disruptions.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click "accept voucher" or "accept credit" in the airline app if you want a cash refund.
- Don’t pay for a new flight on a different airline until you have explicitly rejected the original airline's rebooking offer.
- Don’t assume the airline is legally required to pay for hotels for weather-related cancellations; federal law only mandates refunds, not amenity coverage.
- Don’t leave the airport without a screenshot of the "Cancelled" status and a copy of the airline's written rebooking offer.

## What to do now
1. **Gather all travel documentation immediately.** Locate your six-character reservation code (PNR), ticket number, and original itinerary.
2. **Document the specific reason for the cancellation.** Screenshot the status in the airline app or take a photo of the departure board.
   - Note if the airline cites "weather," "Air Traffic Control," or "technical/crew" issues, as this determines your right to meals and hotels.
3. **Engage multiple support channels simultaneously.** 
   - Enter the physical customer service line at the airport.
   - Call the airline’s customer service number or use the "chat" feature in the mobile app while waiting in line.
4. **Request rebooking on partner airlines if the delay is "controllable."** If the cancellation is due to maintenance or staffing, ask the agent to check for seats on "interline" or "partner" carriers.
   - Major US carriers (e.g., American, Delta, United) have committed to rebooking on partners for controllable disruptions.
5. **Verify your refund eligibility for "significant changes."** If you are not rebooked or if the offered alternative arrives more than 3 hours later (domestic) or 6 hours later (international) than originally scheduled, you are entitled to a full refund.
6. **Explicitly reject the rebooking offer if you choose not to travel.** Inform the agent or select the "Refund" option in the app. 
   - Federal rules require the airline to issue an automatic refund within 7 business days for credit card purchases or 20 days for other payment methods once an alternative is rejected.
7. **Request meal and hotel vouchers for overnight controllable delays.** Consult the DOT Airline Cancellation & Delay Dashboard on your phone to see if your specific airline has committed to:
   - Meal vouchers for delays of 3+ hours.
   - Hotel accommodations and ground transportation for overnight delays.
8. **Retain all receipts for out-of-pocket expenses.** If the airline refuses to provide vouchers but their policy commits to coverage, you will need itemized receipts to request reimbursement later.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to file a formal DOT complaint while at the airport; this can be done once you are home.
- You don’t need to negotiate the dollar amount of "compensation" for lost time, as US law does not mandate cash payments for delays beyond the ticket refund.
- You can review your credit card's built-in trip interruption insurance later to see if it covers costs the airline denied.

## Important reassurance
A flight cancellation is a breach of the original transport contract. While the airport environment is high-pressure, your right to a refund to your original form of payment is protected by federal law regardless of the reason for the cancellation, provided you do not accept the rebooking.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and refund rights at the airport under US Department of Transportation rules. It does not cover international flights departing from outside the US (e.g., EU261/UK261 rules).

## Important note
Airlines are required to provide refunds for the ticket and any unused ancillary fees (like baggage or seat selection) if a flight is cancelled and the passenger rejects rebooking. Amenity commitments (hotels/meals) are largely voluntary and vary by carrier.
