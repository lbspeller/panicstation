# What to do if… an ATM keeps your card and you are left without payment options (USA)

## Short answer
Step away from the ATM, lock the card in your banking app if that option is available, and promptly report the retained card to your bank or card issuer. Ask for the fastest secure way to access money for essentials today.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept help from strangers near the ATM.
- Do not share your PIN, passcodes, or one-time codes.
- Do not transfer money because a stranger tells you to do so.
- Do not retry the transaction if the ATM looks loose, altered, or suspicious.
- Do not try to pry open the machine or retrieve the card yourself.
- Do not rely on an app lock alone without reporting the retained card to the issuer.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer spot.** Step away from the ATM and go to a well-lit public place or inside the branch if it is open. If anyone is threatening you or you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Lock the card and contact the issuer promptly.** Use your bank app to lock the card if that option is available. Then report that the ATM retained the physical card. Use the official app, the issuer’s official website, your account statement, or a trusted saved contact. Do not use a number given to you by a stranger.
3. **Ask whether the card should be canceled and replaced.** A temporary app lock may not be the final step. Follow the issuer’s instructions.
4. **Check recent activity.** Review recent withdrawals and purchases. Report any transaction you do not recognize as unauthorized.
5. **Record the ATM details.** Note the location, time, and ATM ID or terminal number if you can see it safely. Take a quick photo of the ATM ID label or screen message if it is safe to do so.
6. **Contact the ATM operator or branch through a trusted route.** If the branch is open, ask staff what they can do. Otherwise, use contact details from the operator’s official website or your bank. Do not rely on a sticker or number that looks altered.
7. **Ask the issuer about secure access for essentials today.** Ask whether your account has an available branch withdrawal, replacement-card option, digital-wallet option, or cardless ATM feature. Availability varies. Bring photo ID to a branch if you have it.
8. **Use only a trusted payment workaround.** If necessary, tell a merchant, hotel, or transport provider that your card was retained and ask what payment arrangements are available. Use your own banking app if you transfer money to someone you already trust. Do not send money to a stranger.
9. **Report signs of tampering.** Tell the issuer if the ATM looked altered or if someone may have seen your PIN. Ask whether you should change the PIN. Notify branch or property staff through a trusted route. Consider contacting local law enforcement if you believe there is an active fraud device. Call **911** if there is immediate danger.

## What can wait
- Working out exactly why the ATM retained the card.
- Updating saved card details for subscriptions.
- Filing outside complaints unless the issuer does not resolve the problem.
- Building a longer-term backup plan.
- Making decisions beyond getting safe, reporting the card, and securing essentials.

## Important reassurance
A retained card is stressful, but you do not need to solve everything at once. Card retention can happen for different reasons. The priority is to secure the card, check your account, and arrange a safe way to cover essentials.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from your card issuer, the ATM operator, or an appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or financial advice. Follow your bank or card issuer’s instructions. Report any unauthorized electronic fund transfer promptly, because reporting timing can matter under federal rules.
