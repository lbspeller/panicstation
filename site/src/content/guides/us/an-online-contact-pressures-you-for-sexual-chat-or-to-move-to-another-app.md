# What to do if… an online contact pressures you for sexual chat or to move to another app (USA)

## Short answer
Pause the conversation and do not move to another platform because they are pressuring you. Block the account when you are ready.

## Do not do these things
- Do not switch apps because they insist another platform is safer, more private, or easier.
- Do not send intimate images, money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or anything on camera to reduce the pressure.
- Do not share your full name, phone number, address, school, workplace, location, or other account names.
- Do not keep negotiating after you want the conversation to stop.
- Do not confront them or try to identify them yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Create a pause.** Stop replying. Mute the conversation. Turn off read receipts or online-status indicators if the app allows it and that helps you feel less pressured.
2. **Keep the contact on the current platform.** Do not follow links or accept requests to move to another app, phone number, email address, or video call.
3. **Only if it feels safe and useful, save a small record:** the account name, platform, and a few messages showing the pressure or threats, stored somewhere you do not need to keep looking at them.
4. **Block the account when you are ready.** You can also use the current platform’s report tool and choose the closest available category for sexual pressure, harassment, threats, or blackmail.
5. **Limit what the account can find today.** Make public profiles private, remove visible contact details, turn off location sharing, and remove unknown followers or contacts.
6. **If they threaten to share intimate images or demand money, do not send money to try to make the threat stop.** If you are an adult, you can choose to file an IC3 cyber-enabled crime report, submit an FBI tip, or contact local police. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911.
7. **If the situation may involve online sexual exploitation of someone under 18, you can use NCMEC’s CyberTipline.** Tell a trusted adult if you can. If an image or video was taken before age 18, Take It Down may also help limit online sharing. Do not download or forward an image or video in order to use it.
8. **If you are currently over 18, an intimate image was taken when you were 18 or older, and you still have the image or video on your device, StopNCII may be an option.** Do not download an image or video in order to use the tool.
9. **Use support if you need it.** RAINN can offer confidential support. Check its privacy information before sharing identifying details.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out who they really are.
- You do not need to gather a large amount of evidence.
- You do not need to reply to threats, deadlines, or final warnings.
- You do not need to decide today whether to tell other people, make a report, or take any public action.
- You do not need to read the messages again after saving the few details you need.

## Important reassurance
Pressure, secrecy, threats, and rushed requests to switch platforms are enough reason to pause contact. You do not owe the person a reply, an image, money, or an explanation. Feeling frozen, embarrassed, or unsure does not mean you caused the situation.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and reduce immediate harm. Later decisions may need help from a specialist support service, a platform safety team, or law enforcement.

## Important note
This guide provides general information for immediate safety and support. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911.
