# What to do if… someone is using intimate images to threaten or control you (USA)
## Short answer
Get to a safer pause and contact a specialist support service. Do not send more images, money, or anything else under pressure while you decide your next step.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send more images, money, or anything else because someone is pressuring you.
- Do not meet them in person to discuss the images if you feel unsafe.
- Do not forward intimate images to friends, relatives, or other contacts.
- Do not download, save, or screenshot an intimate image if anyone shown may be under 18.
- Do not feel that you must make every decision or contact every service right now.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.**  
   If you are in immediate danger, call **911**. If it is safe, go to a place where you feel safer and contact one trusted person.

2. **Contact an image-abuse specialist.**  
   The CCRI Image Abuse Helpline supports callers in the United States affected by image-based sexual abuse or sextortion. Call **844-878-2274**. The helpline is free and available 24/7.

3. **Reduce the direct pressure where you can.**  
   You do not owe the person more images, money, explanations, or an in-person meeting. Choose the safest low-contact option for now, such as muting messages; block contact only if that feels safe. If it is safe, keep any threatening messages or account details you already have, but do not save or forward intimate images.

4. **Use the relevant image-support route.**  
   - If the image is of you, was taken before you turned 18, and is already on your device, **Take It Down** may help remove or limit online sharing. Do not download or share an image in order to submit it.
   - If you think an image may involve the online exploitation of any child, consider contacting **NCMEC CyberTipline**.
   - If you are currently over 18, the image is of you, you were 18 or older when it was taken, and you still have it on your device, **StopNCII** may help limit sharing on participating platforms.

5. **Report the account or post on the platform if it is safe.**  
   Use the platform’s reporting tool for intimate-image abuse, harassment, or threats. You do not need to report everywhere at once.

6. **Use a relationship-abuse support route if this is connected to a partner or ex-partner.**  
   The National Domestic Violence Hotline is available at **800-799-SAFE (7233)**, by text with **START** to **88788**, and through online chat.

7. **Use an additional support route if you need one.**  
   RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline is available at **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, by text with **HOPE** to **64673**, and through online chat.

8. **Consider an optional cybercrime report if you are an adult.**  
   If the threats are happening online, you can consider reporting them through **IC3**. For suspected online exploitation of a child, use **NCMEC CyberTipline** instead.

9. **Check account access only if it may be relevant.**  
   If the person may have accessed your email, phone, or social-media accounts, use a device they cannot access if possible, change the relevant password, review signed-in devices, and remove access you do not recognize.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a police report or explore legal options.
- You do not need to collect perfect evidence.
- You do not need to contact every platform or support service today.
- You do not need to explain everything to multiple people. One safe person and one specialist support route is enough for the next step.

## Important reassurance
Using intimate images to frighten or control you is not your fault. Fear, freezing, shame, and confusion are understandable reactions to intimidation. You deserve support without pressure or judgment.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and connect you with specialist support. Later decisions about reporting, takedowns, safety planning, or legal options may need help from an advocate or other qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
