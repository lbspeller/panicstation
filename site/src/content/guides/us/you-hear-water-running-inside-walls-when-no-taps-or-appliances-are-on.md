# What to do if… you hear water running inside walls when no taps or appliances are on (USA)

## Short answer
**Shut off your home’s main water valve immediately** to isolate the supply and prevent structural or electrical damage.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the sound or wait to see if it stops on its own.
- Do not use tools or excessive force to turn a stuck water valve, as this can cause a secondary break.
- Do not touch any electrical outlets, switches, or the breaker panel if you see moisture or dampness nearby.
- Do not cut into drywall or flooring to investigate the source yourself.
- Do not run additional water to "test" the plumbing once you suspect a hidden leak.

## What to do now
1. **Locate and close the main water shut-off valve immediately.**
   - Common locations include where the main line enters the house, near the water heater, in a basement / crawlspace, or at the exterior water meter box.
   - Turn the handle clockwise (righty-tighty) until it stops.
2. **Relieve pressure from the system.**
   - **Open a cold water faucet at the lowest point of the house (such as a basement utility sink or outdoor hose bib) to drain remaining water from the lines.**
3. **Verify the leak at the water meter if it is safe to access.**
   - **Check the "low flow" indicator (often a small triangle or star-shaped wheel) or the digital display; if it is moving while all taps are closed, the leak is active.**
   - If the meter stops moving after you close the main valve, the leak is confirmed to be within the home's interior plumbing.
4. **Evacuate the area if you observe structural hazards.**
   - **Step away immediately if you see a sagging ceiling, large bulging drywall, or water dripping through light fixtures.**
5. **Call for professional assistance based on your housing type.**
   - **Homeowners:** contact a 24-hour emergency plumber.
   - **Renters:** call the property management emergency maintenance line.
   - **Condo / HOA residents:** notify building management, as leaks in shared walls can impact multiple units.
6. **Protect your belongings from secondary damage.**
   - **Move furniture, electronics, and valuables away from the damp wall or any areas where water is pooling on the floor.**
7. **Document the scene for insurance purposes.**
   - **Take clear photos and videos of the affected walls, ceilings, and the water meter reading before any repairs begin.**
   - Note the exact time you first heard the sound to meet the 24–72 hour reporting windows common in 2026 insurance policies.
8. **Call 911 if you observe electrical sparking, smoking, or the smell of burning.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific pipe or joint that failed right now.
- You do not need to clean or dry the area extensively until a professional assesses the risk of mold or structural collapse.
- You do not need to file the formal insurance claim in the next few minutes; focus on stabilization first.
- You do not need to decide on permanent restoration or repiping options today.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed by a sound you cannot see. By shutting off the main valve, you have already taken the most effective action to stop the damage from worsening. Professionals deal with hidden leaks daily and have specialized tools to locate the source without destroying your home.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. A licensed plumber must perform the actual repair, and a restoration specialist may be required to dry the interior of the walls safely.

## Important note
This guide provides general emergency stabilization information and is not a substitute for professional plumbing or electrical services. Always prioritize personal safety over property preservation; if you cannot safely access the shut-off valve, exit the premises and wait for help.
