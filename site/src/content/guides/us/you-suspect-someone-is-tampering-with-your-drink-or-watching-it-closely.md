# What to do if… you suspect someone is tampering with your drink or watching it closely (USA)

## Short answer
Stop drinking from it, keep your phone and belongings with you, and move straight to staff, security, or trusted people. Ask for help getting to a safer spot and leaving without the person following or seeing your destination.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep drinking to see whether it is fine or to avoid attention.
- Don’t leave the drink unattended again.
- Don’t let the person isolate you outside, in a hallway, in a parking lot, or in a quieter spot.
- Don’t confront them alone.
- Don’t accept a replacement drink from them.
- Don’t leave with the person, or with anyone you do not trust.
- Don’t stay to prove what happened if leaving is safer.

## What to do now
1. **Stop drinking immediately.**  
   If the drink has been out of your control, treat it as not safe to consume. Put it down, move away from it, and keep your phone, keys, wallet, and bag with you.

2. **Move to a safer, visible place.**  
   Go straight to the bar, host stand, security desk, bathroom attendant, or another staffed area. If you are with friends, say plainly: “I need you with me now. I do not feel safe.”

3. **Tell staff or security what you noticed and ask for specific help.**  
   Say: “Someone keeps hovering near my drink and watching it. I’m worried about tampering. Can you help me get to a safe spot and leave safely?” Ask them to keep the person away from you, help you find your friends, and stay nearby while you arrange transport.

4. **Make your exit hard to track.**  
   Wait for a ride, taxi, or trusted pickup beside staff or security. Do not let the person see your pickup details, car, destination, phone screen, or route out. If you are walking to a car, ask staff or security to escort you.

5. **If you are alone, create an immediate anchor.**  
   Stay close to staff, call a trusted person, keep the call connected, and say where you are. Ask staff to stay with you until your ride or trusted person arrives.

6. **If you feel unwell, treat it as urgent.**  
   Sudden dizziness, confusion, extreme sleepiness, vomiting, slurred speech, trouble moving, trouble breathing, fainting, memory gaps, or feeling much more intoxicated than expected can be serious. Call 911, ask staff to call 911, or go to a hospital emergency room for medical assessment.

7. **Preserve options only if it does not increase risk.**  
   If it is safe, note the time, place, drink, person’s description, and what you noticed in your phone. If keeping the cup or bottle would keep you near the person or slow down leaving, do not keep it.

8. **Use confidential support if you want a calm next step.**  
   RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline can support you by phone, text, or online chat, even if you are not sure what happened and do not know whether you want to report.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to report anything.
- You do not have to confront the person.
- You do not have to identify the substance or prove that the drink was tampered with.
- You do not have to explain your decision to leave.
- You can decide later whether to write a fuller account, seek further support, or make a report.

## Important reassurance
Your discomfort is enough reason to act. Moving away, asking staff for help, and leaving safely are reasonable steps even if you are not certain what happened.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you notice a credible safety concern around your drink. Later decisions about medical care, reporting, evidence, or support may need help from emergency services, a hospital, a sexual assault advocate, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or feel seriously unwell, call 911 or ask someone nearby to call 911.
