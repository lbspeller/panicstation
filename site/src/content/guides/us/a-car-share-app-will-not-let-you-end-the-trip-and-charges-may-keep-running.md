# What to do if… a car-share app will not let you end the trip and charges may keep running (USA)

## Short answer
Ensure the vehicle is legally parked and secured, then immediately contact the car-share provider by phone to manually terminate the trip while capturing time-stamped visual evidence of the return.

## Do not do these things
- Do not abandon the vehicle in an illegal, private, or hazardous parking space to stop the timer.
- Do not continue driving or searching for a better signal once the vehicle is safely parked in a valid zone.
- Do not assume that physically locking the doors or walking away from the vehicle automatically ends the billing cycle.
- Do not delete the mobile application until you have successfully captured screenshots of the active trip and error messages.
- Do not start a second reservation or "re-book" the same vehicle in an attempt to reset the system.

## What to do now
1. **Park the vehicle legally within the designated service zone or home area.**  
   Verify your location against the map in the application to ensure you are not in a "no-park" or restricted zone, as GPS drift often prevents trips from ending.

2. **Attempt exactly one cycle of troubleshooting steps.**  
   - Toggle cellular data or Wi-Fi off and then back on to refresh the connection.  
   - Force-close the application and restart it.  
   - Attempt to use the "End Trip" or "End Rental" button one more time.  
   - If the vehicle has a physical card reader or key fob system, try ending the trip using the physical hardware.

3. **Capture comprehensive visual evidence of the return immediately.**  
   Take clear screenshots and photos including:  
   - The error message or the screen showing the trip is still active.  
   - The current time and your GPS location (on the phone screen).  
   - The vehicle parked in its final position, including the license plate and surrounding landmarks or street signs.  
   - The dashboard showing the fuel level or battery charge and the odometer.

4. **Call the provider’s urgent support line to request a manual trip termination.**  
   State clearly: "I am at the return location and the app is failing to end the trip. Please close this reservation on your end at the current time."  
   - **Zipcar:** Call 866-4ZIPCAR (866-494-7227).  
   - **Free2move:** Call +1 844-543-7332.  
   - **Turo:** Use the "Report Issue" button in the app or contact the host and Turo support immediately if the "Return" flow is blocked.  
   Request a **case number** and a verbal confirmation of the exact time the trip was officially ended.

5. **Lock and secure the vehicle before leaving the area.**  
   If the app will not lock the doors, check if the provider can lock them remotely during your support call. Ensure all windows are closed and no valuables remain inside.

6. **Submit a formal written dispute if overcharges appear on your account.**  
   Email the provider's support team with your case number and the evidence bundle (photos/screenshots) attached. Explicitly request an adjustment for any time billed after your arrival at the parking spot.

7. **Initiate a credit card billing error dispute if the provider refuses a refund.**  
   Under the Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA), you must send a written dispute to your creditor’s "billing inquiries" address so that it is received within 60 days after the first statement containing the error was mailed to you.  
   - If you have an automatic payment plan, notify the issuer at least 3 business days before the scheduled payment to prevent the disputed amount from being deducted.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the underlying technical cause of the app failure right now.
- You do not need to decide on long-term membership changes or cancellations today.
- You do not need to wait for a final manager's approval before leaving the vehicle, provided you have a case number and photographic proof of the return.

## Important reassurance
App-based return errors are common technical occurrences. If you have clear, time-stamped photographs of the vehicle parked legally in the correct zone and a record of your attempt to call support, you have the necessary evidence to successfully dispute any subsequent overcharges.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for ending a car-share trip and stopping active billing. It does not cover long-term legal claims, vehicle damage disputes, or mechanical repair procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Car-share terms of service vary significantly by provider and state. In any situation where you feel physically unsafe, relocate to a secure area before contacting customer support.
