# What to do if… a professional appointment feels sexualised or unsafe and you want to leave (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the appointment and move to a safer pause if you can, such as reception, a public area, or outside the building. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay because you feel awkward about leaving.
- Do not agree to undressing, touching, or continuing an exam that you do not want or do not understand.
- Do not move to a more isolated area if that feels unsafe.
- Do not feel that you must debate, explain, or label what happened before leaving.
- Do not feel pressured to decide whether to complain or report anything right now.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the interaction if it is safe to do so.** You can use a short line such as: “I want to stop now.” Move toward a safer pause, such as reception, a public area, or outside the building.
2. **Get immediate help if you need it.** Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger. If you are still inside the building and it feels safer, ask another staff member, security, or a trusted person to stay with you.
3. **You do not need to continue with the same professional right now.** If you need care today, ask whether you can see a different professional, have another person present, or leave and arrange care elsewhere.
4. **Contact specialist support if that would help.** RAINN offers support. The Office for Victims of Crime lists hotlines and a directory of local victim services.
5. **Keep your options open only if it feels manageable.** If you may want options later, keep existing messages or appointment records and write a brief factual note for yourself.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to make a facility complaint, contact police, or use a professional complaint route.
- You do not need to write a detailed account right now.
- You do not need to respond to messages from the professional or the office today.
- You do not need to decide whether to return to that office.
- You do not need to label what happened before asking for support.

## Important reassurance
You may feel confused, frozen, numb, or unsure how to describe the experience. Leaving, asking to stop, or taking time before making any further decision are all reasonable responses.

## Scope note
These are first steps for reaching a safer pause and keeping your options open. Later decisions may need support from a specialist service or an appropriate professional.

## Important note
This is general information for an acute situation. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you need urgent medical care, seek emergency or urgent care.
