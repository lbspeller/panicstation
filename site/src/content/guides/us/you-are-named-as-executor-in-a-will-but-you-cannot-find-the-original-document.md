# What to do if… you are named as executor in a will but you cannot find the original document (USA)
## Short answer
Do not distribute, sell, transfer, or promise estate property based only on being named in the will. Preserve the papers you have, look for the latest original will, and ask the local court that handles probate what route applies if the original cannot be found.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume that being named in the will means you have already been appointed to administer the estate.
- Do not distribute, sell, transfer, or promise estate property based only on a copy, scan, or memory of the will.
- Do not throw away papers, envelopes, notebooks, emails, or old files while searching.
- Do not let documents be removed for sorting unless there is a clear record of who has them and where they are kept.
- Do not assume that a photocopy or scan will automatically be accepted by the court.
- Do not force access to a home, safe, or safe-deposit box if you are not permitted to enter or open it.

## What to do now
1. **Preserve what you already have.** Keep any copy, scan, email attachment, envelope, attorney letter, or note about the will unchanged. Store it safely and make a simple list of what you found.
2. **Ask for papers and valuables not to be moved.** Take reasonable preservation steps that you are permitted to take. If someone else lawfully controls access to the home, ask them to keep papers, keys, and valuables in place while the search is organised.
3. **Start a search log.** Write down the date, the places checked, the people contacted, and what was found. Search desks, filing cabinets, locked drawers, home safes, fireboxes, and records showing that a safe-deposit box may exist.
4. **Contact likely holders.** Ask the attorney or law firm that drafted the will, any attorney the person used, and trusted relatives or friends whether they hold the original or know where it was stored.
5. **Check the local probate route.** Find the court that handles probate for the place where the person lived. Ask the clerk or check the court website for filing guidance when an original will cannot be located and whether a will may already be on file.
6. **Ask before trying to access a safe-deposit box.** If you find records of one, ask the bank or local probate court what process applies. Access rules vary.
7. **Get local advice if the original remains missing.** A court may ask for evidence about the search, the signing of the will, its contents, and whether it was revoked. The requirements vary by state, especially if anyone may object.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now who receives property, whether a home should be sold, or how personal items should be divided.
- You do not need to commit immediately to serving as executor.
- You do not need to solve every estate question before preserving the documents and checking the local court route.

## Important reassurance
A missing original will does not automatically mean there is no next step. Slow down, preserve the records, avoid irreversible actions, and ask the local court or a probate attorney what process applies.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about filing, notices, debts, property, and any request to use a copy of a will may need state-specific probate advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Probate rules and court procedures vary by state and locality.
