# What to do if… you notice scorch marks or burning smells around a ceiling light fixture (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using that light and turn off power to it at the breaker panel if you can do so safely. If there is smoke, flames, a strong burning odor, crackling, or heat at the ceiling or fixture, leave and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t turn the light on again “just to check.”
- Don’t touch the fixture, bulb, cover, canopy, or ceiling if anything is warm, smells burnt, or looks damaged.
- Don’t change the bulb or shade right now.
- Don’t remove the fixture, loosen screws, or open any electrical box yourself.
- Don’t keep resetting a tripped breaker.
- Don’t remove the breaker-panel cover.
- Don’t cover the fixture area with insulation, fabric, paper, tape, paint, or a temporary patch.

## What to do now
1. **Check from a safe spot:** look for smoke, flames, burning odor, crackling or buzzing, flickering, or heat at the ceiling or fixture.
2. **If there is active danger:** get everyone out, close doors behind you if you can, and call **911**. Say there may be a **possible electrical fire** at a ceiling light.
3. **If there is no active danger, shut it down:**
   - Turn the light switch off and leave it off.
   - If you can do it safely, with dry hands and stable footing, turn off the breaker that feeds that light.
   - If you cannot identify the breaker safely, do not keep testing switches or breakers. Leave the switch off and wait for a licensed electrician or property maintenance.
   - Use the main breaker only if you can do it safely and it will not create a bigger risk, such as shutting off needed medical equipment.
4. **Move easy fuel away:** move paper, curtains, bedding, boxes, or other easy-to-move items away from the area below and around the fixture.
5. **Contact the right person:**
   - **If you rent:** contact your landlord, property manager, or maintenance contact and say: “There are scorch marks around a ceiling light fixture. I have left the switch off, and the circuit may be off. Please send a licensed electrician before anyone turns it back on.”
   - **If you own:** call a licensed electrician and describe the room, the marks, any odor or sounds, whether a breaker tripped, and whether anything recently changed, such as a new bulb or fixture.
6. **Document without touching:** take a wide photo and a close photo, then write down when you noticed it and any symptoms such as buzzing, flicker, odor, warmth, or a tripped breaker.
7. **Watch for changes:** if smoke, flames, strong burning odor, crackling, or heat appears, leave and call **911**. Do not re-enter until emergency responders say it is safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it was the bulb, wiring, insulation, fixture, switch, or breaker.
- You do not need to clean, repaint, patch, or hide the marks.
- You do not need to make insurance decisions unless there has been a fire, wider damage, or your electrician or property manager tells you to document it for a claim.

## Important reassurance
Scorch marks around a ceiling light are a reasonable reason to stop using it and take the issue seriously, even if the light still works. Turning it off, keeping the area clear, and getting it checked helps reduce the chance of escalation.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the situation and avoid harm. Later decisions about repair, replacement, rental responsibilities, or insurance may need help from a licensed electrician, property manager, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, or other professional advice. It is not a substitute for an on-site electrical inspection or emergency services when there may be an immediate fire risk.
