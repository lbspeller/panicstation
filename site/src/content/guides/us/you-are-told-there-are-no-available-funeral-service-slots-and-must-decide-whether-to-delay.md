# What to do if… you are told there are no available funeral service slots and must decide whether to delay (USA)

## Short answer
Ask what is actually unavailable, whether burial or cremation can be scheduled separately from a later gathering, and what written itemized prices apply before you agree to a plan.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to an “only option” before asking what part of the plan is unavailable and whether another arrangement is possible.
- Do not agree to charges before you have written prices and understand any cancellation or refund terms.
- Do not assume embalming is automatically required. Ask whether it is required in your situation, recommended for a particular plan, or a funeral-home policy, and ask about alternatives.
- Do not have several relatives negotiate separately with the provider. Choose one point person so quotes, holds, and decisions do not get mixed up.
- Do not feel that you must decide every ceremony detail today.

## What to do now
1. **Ask what is causing the delay.** Ask the provider to say clearly whether the issue is the funeral-home service schedule, the cemetery or crematory schedule, local paperwork, or another practical constraint.

2. **Ask what can be separated.** Ask whether burial or cremation can take place separately from a memorial service or other gathering. Ask whether a smaller graveside or committal service is available sooner, with a larger gathering later.

3. **Ask for written prices before agreeing.** When speaking with a funeral home, ask for the itemized prices for the options you are considering, such as direct cremation, immediate burial, a graveside service, or a memorial service later. If you discuss arrangements in person, ask for the General Price List at the start of that discussion. Before agreeing to use the funeral home’s services, ask for the written itemized statement showing the goods and services selected and the price of each item.

4. **Ask how the person who died will be cared for while you wait.** Ask what care is available during the waiting period, whether refrigeration is available, what charges apply, and whether the waiting period affects any viewing plan.

5. **Ask whether another service location changes the timing.** If the delay is for a chapel or gathering space, ask whether a place of worship, another venue, or a graveside service is available while keeping the burial or cremation plan separate.

6. **Ask what paperwork applies locally.** Ask what paperwork or authorization is needed for the chosen plan, who handles it, and what must be completed today rather than later.

7. **Choose one next step for today.** Ask for the first available date and any cancellation openings, request a written quote for a two-stage plan, or contact another funeral home for a comparison quote before agreeing to charges.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide flowers, catering, printed programs, or every obituary detail today.
- You do not need to settle every family disagreement today.
- Some disposition-related choices may need to be made sooner. Ask the provider which decisions are genuinely needed today and which can wait.

## Important reassurance
A delay does not mean that you are letting anyone down. Separating the burial or cremation from a later gathering can be a respectful way to reduce pressure and give people time to attend.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. State and local procedures vary, and later decisions may need help from a funeral provider or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Confirm local requirements, availability, prices, and fees in writing before agreeing to a plan.
