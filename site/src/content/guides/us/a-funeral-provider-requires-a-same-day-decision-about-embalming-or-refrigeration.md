# What to do if… a funeral provider requires a same-day decision about embalming or refrigeration (USA)

## Short answer
State clearly that you do not authorize embalming at this time and request refrigeration only. Under the FTC Funeral Rule, you have the right to receive an itemized General Price List (GPL) and get specific price information over the phone without providing your name or address.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume embalming is a federal or state legal requirement for all deaths (it is rarely mandatory except in specific cases like interstate transport or certain contagious diseases).
- Don’t sign an embalming authorization form just to "stop the clock" or because you feel pressured by staff.
- Don’t accept a "standard package" that includes embalming if you only want temporary preservation via refrigeration.
- Don’t allow a provider to begin the embalming process "as a courtesy" without your written or recorded verbal consent.
- Don’t provide your contact information over the phone just to get price quotes; they are legally required to provide them regardless.

## What to do now
1. **Use a specific script to halt the immediate pressure and buy time for a decision.**  
   - You may say: **“I do not authorize embalming today. Please place the deceased in refrigeration while we review our options.”** 
   - Ensure you ask the staff member to note this specific instruction in the file immediately.
2. **Request the General Price List (GPL) immediately if you are at the facility in person.**  
   - **The provider must give you a paper copy of the GPL to keep** before you discuss any specific arrangements or prices. 
   - Check the GPL specifically for the "Refrigeration" and "Embalming" line items to compare daily or flat-rate costs.
3. **Obtain price disclosures by phone if you are not yet at the funeral home.**  
   - **Ask for the itemized cost of refrigeration per day and the cost of the basic services fee.** 
   - If the provider asks for your name, address, or phone number before giving prices, you may decline and state that you are exercising your right to telephone price disclosures under the FTC Funeral Rule.
4. **Identify the specific "preservation clock" that applies in your state.**  
   - **Ask the director: "What is the specific state law regarding the timeframe for preservation in this jurisdiction?"** 
   - Many states (such as California, Texas, or Florida) require preservation—either refrigeration or embalming—only after 24, 48, or 72 hours have passed.
5. **Ask for a written explanation if they claim embalming is legally required.**  
   - **Say: "If you are stating that embalming is a legal requirement for this specific situation, please show me the written state or local regulation."** 
   - If it is a "house policy" rather than a law, they must disclose that to you in writing on the GPL.
6. **Inquire about "Direct Cremation" or "Immediate Burial" to bypass preservation requirements entirely.**  
   - **Ask: "What is the flat rate for direct cremation or immediate burial?"** 
   - These options typically do not require embalming because there is no public viewing or formal service with the body present.
7. **Ask for an itemized "Statement of Funeral Goods and Services Selected" before you leave.**  
   - **This document must list every charge you have agreed to, including the daily refrigeration rate and the non-declinable basic services fee.** 
   - Do not sign this document if it includes "Embalming" unless you have changed your mind and want that service.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to choose a casket, urn, or outer burial container today.
- You do **not** need to finalize the date of the service or the location of the burial immediately.
- You do **not** need to provide an obituary, choose flowers, or select music while making the initial storage decision.
- You do **not** need to decide on a "viewing" or "visitation" until you have seen the price difference between refrigerated and embalmed options.

## Important reassurance
It is completely normal to feel overwhelmed by the technical and financial decisions required immediately after a death. Choosing refrigeration is a safe, temporary "pause button" that prevents irreversible chemical procedures while you gather your thoughts or consult with other family members. Taking a few hours to read the price list is a responsible step, not a sign of disrespect.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to prevent unwanted embalming and ensure price transparency under the FTC Funeral Rule. It does not cover specific state-to-state variations for transport across state lines or international repatriation, which often carry different legal requirements.

## Important note
This information is based on the FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453) as of 2026. While federal law provides these rights, individual states may have specific health department regulations regarding infectious diseases or time limits for body disposition. Always insist on seeing the written law if a provider claims a service is "mandatory."
