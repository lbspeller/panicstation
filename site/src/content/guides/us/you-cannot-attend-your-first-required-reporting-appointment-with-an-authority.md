# What to do if… you cannot attend your first required reporting appointment with an authority (USA)

## Short answer
Use the official contact details on your paperwork as soon as possible, preferably before the appointment. Ask what you should do next and save a simple record of your contact attempts and any reply.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume the appointment will be changed automatically.
- Do not assume that leaving a voicemail, sending an email, or submitting a portal message necessarily changes the appointment or any deadline.
- Do not ignore the appointment because you feel unsure what to say.
- Do not use social media or an unofficial contact route unless your paperwork specifically tells you to.
- Do not send a long explanation when a short factual message will do.
- Do not assume a court hearing, surrender date, or other required appearance can be handled in the same way as a reporting appointment.

## What to do now
1. Check the paperwork carefully. Find the appointment date, time, location, office name, contact details, and any case or identification number. Check whether the instruction is a reporting appointment, rather than a court hearing, surrender date, or another type of required appearance.
2. Use the official phone number, email address, portal, or other route listed on the paperwork. Contact the office as soon as possible and say that you cannot attend the first appointment.
3. Ask for a new instruction. A brief message is enough: “I cannot attend my first required reporting appointment on [date and time]. Please tell me what I should do next and whether the appointment can be changed.”
4. Ask whether there is an approved alternative you can manage, such as a different time, a different date, or another check-in method. Do not assume an alternative is allowed until the office confirms it.
5. Save a simple record. Keep the date and time of each call, voicemail, email, portal message, or reply. Save any confirmation you receive.
6. If nobody answers, leave a brief message when that option is available. Then use another official route shown on the paperwork or the office website, if one is available.
7. If this is an ICE field office check-in, use the official ICE check-in page or ICE Portal check-ins page to review the available appointment route. Keep any confirmation. Do not assume that changing an ICE field office check-in changes an immigration court hearing or another appointment.
8. If the appointment time passes before you receive a reply, contact the office again as soon as you can. State that you could not attend, mention your earlier contact attempt briefly, and ask for the next instruction.
9. If the paperwork mentions a court appearance, surrender, arrest, detention, or a warrant, consider getting prompt help from a licensed attorney familiar with the type of reporting requirement. Do not rely on this guide alone.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain every detail of the situation in the first message.
- You do not need to decide now whether to challenge the reporting requirement.
- You do not need to solve the underlying transport, work, childcare, or health problem before making contact.
- You do not need to send repeated messages every few minutes. Make a clear contact attempt, save the record, and follow any official reply.

## Important reassurance
A missed or difficult appointment can feel urgent and frightening. The useful first steps are to check the paperwork, contact the correct office promptly, ask for a clear instruction, and keep a record of what you did.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Reporting requirements and the consequences of a missed appointment vary by system and by the instructions you were given. Later decisions may need help from the supervising office or a licensed attorney.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice or professional advice. Use the official instructions on your paperwork. If you need immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance, call 911.
