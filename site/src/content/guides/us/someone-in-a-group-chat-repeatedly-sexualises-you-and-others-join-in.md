# What to do if… someone in a group chat repeatedly sexualises you and others join in (USA)

## Short answer
Create immediate distance by muting or leaving the chat and stopping all responses. Save a minimal record of the messages and use the platform's reporting tools to block the individuals involved.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue with the group or attempt to explain why the behavior is wrong; this often encourages further "dogpiling."
- Don’t use emoji "reactions" or read receipts; any interaction signals that you are still watching the feed.
- Don’t keep scrolling through the history to see if they have stopped, as this increases exposure to the harm.
- Don’t delete the entire chat history immediately if you intend to report the behavior to authorities or your school/employer.
- Don’t move to a private one-on-one message with the instigator to "clear things up" if they have already ignored your boundaries.

## What to do now
1. **Create immediate physical distance (30 seconds):** Mute the group notifications, disable lock-screen message previews, and **leave the group chat** if the platform allows. If leaving is not possible (e.g., a mandatory work or school channel), mute the thread and move the app off your home screen.
2. **Cease all forms of engagement:** Stop sending messages, reacting with emojis, or opening the chat. If your settings allow, **turn off read receipts** so others cannot see when you have viewed the messages.
3. **Capture a focused record of the behavior (2 minutes):** Take screenshots that clearly show **usernames or phone numbers**, **the sexualising comments**, and the **timestamps** of the messages. Save these to a secure folder or send them to a trusted contact for safekeeping, then close the gallery.
4. **Utilize platform-level reporting and blocking:** Use the "Report" function within the app (e.g., Discord, Instagram, WhatsApp) to flag the specific messages for harassment or sexual content. **Block the primary instigator** and any other participants who joined in.
5. **Contact an administrator if applicable:** If the chat is hosted on a managed server or workspace, **privately message the moderator or admin** with a brief statement: "I am being repeatedly sexualized in this group. I have left/muted the chat. Please review the logs and remove the offending content and users."
6. **Access confidential specialist support:** Contact **RAINN** (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network) to discuss your options and safety without pressure.
   - **Call:** 800-656-HOPE (4673)
   - **Text:** Text "HOPE" to 64673
   - **Web:** Use the secure online chat at rainn.org.
7. **Address threats or real-world safety concerns:** If the messages contain threats of physical violence, your home address, or if you feel you are in immediate danger, **call 911**. For non-emergency reporting of online threats, contact your local police department or use **tips.fbi.gov**.
8. **Report incidents involving minors or sexual imagery:** If anyone in the chat is under 18 or if there is pressure to exchange sexual images (sextortion), report the activity to the **NCMEC CyberTipline** at report.cybertip.org or call 1-800-843-5687.
9. **Identify financial extortion attempts:** If the sexualization is linked to a demand for money or gift cards, file a report with the FBI’s **Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)** at ic3.gov.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether you want to pursue a formal legal case or a school disciplinary report.
- You do not need to explain your departure to the group or defend your boundaries to those who violated them.
- You do not need to delete your social media accounts or change your phone number immediately; focus on isolating the specific group first.

## Important reassurance
When multiple people in a group participate in this behavior, it can create a false sense that the behavior is acceptable or "just a joke." It is common to feel a "freeze" response or to doubt your own reaction when being dogpiled. This reaction is a standard physiological response to social aggression and does not indicate that you consented to the interaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for ending unwanted contact and securing evidence. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, school/workplace internal investigations, or digital forensics.

## Important note
This information is for immediate harm reduction and does not constitute legal advice. If the situation involves stalking, coercion, or the distribution of non-consensual sexual imagery, consider contacting a specialist legal resource or law enforcement.
