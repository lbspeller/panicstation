# What to do if… you hear buzzing or crackling from your electrical panel or fuse box (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately stop using high-power appliances and, if safe to reach, switch off the main breaker to the building. If you see smoke or flames, evacuate and call 911 immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the panel door or remove the protective cover.
- Do not touch any internal wires, terminals, or metal components.
- Do not stand in water or on a damp floor while interacting with the panel.
- Do not keep resetting a breaker that has tripped or is making noise.
- Do not assume a faint hum is safe if it is accompanied by flickering lights or heat.
- Do not use a fire extinguisher on the panel unless it is specifically rated Class C.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate fire indicators from a distance.** Look for visible smoke, active sparks, or flames, and check for a strong "fishy" or "burnt plastic" odor.
   - If any of these are present, evacuate everyone from the building immediately.
   - Call 911 once you are a safe distance away and report an electrical hazard.
2. **Determine if the floor is dry.** Do not approach the panel if there is standing water, a damp floor, or a known leak nearby.
   - If the floor is wet, do not touch the panel; evacuate and call an emergency electrician or the fire department.
3. **Switch off the main breaker if it is safe to do so.** Locate the large switch at the top or bottom of the panel (often labeled "Main") and flip it to the "Off" position.
   - Only do this if you can reach it without touching the metal enclosure or any signs of heat/damage.
   - If you cannot identify the main breaker, leave the area and call for professional help.
4. **Isolate the area.** Keep pets and other residents away from the panel and avoid using any light switches or outlets that were behaving unusually.
5. **Contact a licensed electrician for an emergency inspection.** Specify that you heard buzzing, sizzling, or crackling sounds from the panel and describe any other cues like flickering lights.
6. **Notify your electric utility provider if the noise is outside.** If the sound is coming from the meter base, service drop, or external service equipment, stay at least 30 feet away and call the utility's emergency line.
7. **Keep the power off until a professional arrives.** Do not attempt to restore power even if the noise stops while the main breaker is off.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify which specific circuit is failing right now.
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is a loose connection, an arc fault, or an overloaded bus bar.
- You do not need to investigate the age or brand of the panel (e.g., Federal Pacific or Zinsco) until the immediate hazard is stabilized by a professional.

## Important reassurance
Hearing unusual sounds from an electrical system can be startling. These sounds are physical indicators of a fault, and choosing to cut the power is the most effective way to prevent the situation from escalating. It is normal to feel uncertain about electrical components; professional electricians deal with these specific sounds daily.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Electrical panels contain lethal voltages even when individual breakers are off. A licensed professional must verify the system's safety before power is restored.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace professional electrical or emergency services. If you feel unsafe or suspect a fire has started inside the wall, prioritize evacuation and contact 911.
