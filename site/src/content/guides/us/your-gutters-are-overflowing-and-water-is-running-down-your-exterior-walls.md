# What to do if… your gutters are overflowing and water is running down your exterior walls (USA)

## Short answer
Stay on the ground, keep away from any water near electricity, and divert roof runoff away from the wall and foundation using only ground-level, safe steps.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb ladders, get on the roof, or lean out of windows while it is wet, windy, dark, or raining hard.
- Do not enter standing water if it could be touching outlets, cords, appliances, the electrical panel, or a downed power line.
- Do not touch electrical equipment, switches, cords, or appliances if they are wet or if you are standing on a wet floor.
- Do not try to shut off power if you would have to stand in water or reach a wet panel.
- Do not force water into a clogged downspout with a hose if you do not know where it will discharge.
- Do not start tearing out drywall, pulling trim, or throwing away damaged items while water is still actively entering; take quick photos first when it is safe.

## What to do now
1. **Move people and pets away from electrical risk.** From a safe, dry place, look for water near outlets, switches, cords, appliances, light fixtures, the electrical panel, or any downed or sagging power line outside. Keep everyone away from those areas.
2. **Only shut off power if it is clearly safe and dry.** Use the breaker panel only if you can reach it without standing in water and the panel area is dry. If the panel is wet or blocked by water, stay out and call an electrician or your utility. Call **911** if there is sparking, a burning smell, fire, electrical shock, rapidly rising water, or anyone trapped or at immediate risk.
3. **Contain water inside.** Put buckets, towels, or pans under drips. Move rugs, furniture, electronics, papers, and valuables away from the affected exterior wall and off the floor, especially on the lowest level.
4. **Check only ground-level outlets for blockage.** If you can do it from stable, dry ground, clear leaves or debris from the bottom of the downspout, the splash block area, and any nearby yard or driveway drain grate that is backing up. Stop if water is moving fast, footing is poor, or you need a ladder.
5. **Divert runoff away from the wall and foundation.** Attach a downspout extension or flexible drain hose and point it to a place that drains away from the house. If you do not have one, use plastic sheeting, a tarp, a storage lid, or a weighted board as a temporary chute to steer water away from siding, brick, vents, door thresholds, basement windows, and window wells.
6. **Reduce splashback at the overflow point.** Put a large container, splash block, tarp, or board under the main spill point if you can place it safely from the ground. This will not fix the gutter, but it can reduce water hammering the same section of wall.
7. **Protect window wells and low openings.** If water is collecting near a basement window, window well, crawlspace vent, or low door, use a temporary barrier such as a tarp, plastic sheeting, sandbags, soil bags, or heavy objects to guide water away without sending it toward a neighbour’s property.
8. **Report public drainage problems if they are contributing.** If a blocked street drain, ditch, culvert, or storm inlet is sending water toward homes, report it to your city or county public works department, 311 where available, a local reporting app, or your local government website.
9. **Document visible damage once it is safe.** Take quick photos or video of water entry, wet walls, wet flooring, damaged belongings, and any temporary diversion you used. Do not delay urgent safety steps to take photos.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the root cause during the storm; it may be a clog, gutter pitch problem, undersized gutter, loose joint, roof runoff issue, grading problem, or blocked drain.
- You do not need to do permanent repairs such as rehanging gutters, resealing joints, replacing fascia, or opening walls while conditions are unsafe.
- You do not need to run fans, dehumidifiers, shop vacs, or power tools until electrical safety is clear and water is no longer actively entering.
- You do not need to decide insurance coverage now. Once safe, quick photos and saved receipts can help if you later make a claim.

## Important reassurance
Overflowing gutters can look dramatic because a large roof area is draining through one failure point. Ground-level diversion and indoor containment can often reduce the most urgent damage until the rain eases and repairs can be assessed safely.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate harm and buy time. Later decisions about gutter repair, drainage, electrical inspection, water damage, mould prevention, structural concerns, or insurance may need qualified specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, engineering, electrical, insurance, or other professional advice. If there is an immediate safety risk, contact emergency services, your utility, or a qualified professional.
