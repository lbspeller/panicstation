# What to do if… your account security questions are changed or reset and you did not do it (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately stop interacting with the browser and do not uninstall it. Most data "loss" after an update is actually a profile switch or a sync logout that can be reversed by signing back into the correct browser profile.

## Do not do these things
- Do not uninstall and reinstall the browser, as this may delete the local backup files (`Bookmarks.bak`).
- Do not use the "Reset settings" or "Refresh browser" options yet.
- Do not toggle Sync on and off repeatedly across different computers or phones.
- Do not run "System Cleaners" or "Registry Cleaners" while data is missing.
- Do not install new extensions that claim to recover deleted data.
- Do not clear your "Browsing Data," "Cache," or "Cookies" until the issue is resolved.

## What to do now
1. **Check the profile icon in the top-right corner of the browser window.**
   - **Verify if the icon shows a "Guest" profile or a generic silhouette.**
   - **Click the icon and see if your primary email address is listed as the active profile.**
   - **If multiple profiles are listed, switch to each one to see if your data is contained there.**

2. **Look for a "Sync Paused" or "Sign in again" alert.**
   - **Check the top-right corner near the profile icon for a yellow or red exclamation mark.**
   - **If you were signed out during the update, your bookmarks may be hidden until you re-authenticate.**
   - **Sign in using your primary Google, Microsoft, or Firefox account credentials.**

3. **Confirm if the Bookmarks Bar is simply hidden.**
   - **Press `Ctrl + Shift + B` (Windows) or `Cmd + Shift + B` (Mac) to toggle the visibility of the bar.**
   - **Open the full Bookmark Manager (usually `Ctrl + Shift + O`) to see if the folders exist but are no longer on the toolbar.**

4. **Identify your "Reference Device" before attempting a cloud sync.**
   - **Check your smartphone or another computer where you use the same browser.**
   - **If the bookmarks are still visible on that second device, do not log out or "Reset Sync" on it.**
   - **Use that device as the master copy to verify that your data still exists in the cloud.**

5. **Locate local backup files on your hard drive.**
   - **Navigate to the browser's User Data folder (e.g., `%LocalAppData%\Google\Chrome\User Data\Default`).**
   - **Look for files named `Bookmarks` and `Bookmarks.bak`.**
   - **Copy these two files to a separate folder or a USB drive immediately to prevent the browser from overwriting them.**

6. **Check the Extensions management page.**
   - **Type `chrome://extensions`, `edge://extensions`, or `about:addons` into the address bar.**
   - **Verify if your extensions are listed but marked as "Disabled" or "Turned off."**
   - **Re-enable them one by one; if they were disabled by a "Work or School" policy, contact your IT department.**

7. **Address potential account security if symptoms are suspicious.**
   - **If you see unknown extensions, a changed homepage, or unauthorized sign-ins, change your browser account password immediately.**
   - **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) if it was not already active.**

8. **Report suspected cyber-enabled fraud or identity theft.**
   - **File a report with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) at ic3.gov if you believe data was stolen for criminal use.**
   - **Visit identitytheft.gov to create a recovery plan if sensitive personal information was accessed via the browser.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to manually re-add hundreds of bookmarks today.
- You do not need to decide on a new browser or backup software right now.
- You do not need to "clean" your computer or optimize performance while data is missing.
- You can wait to export your bookmarks until you have successfully stabilized the correct profile.

## Important reassurance
In the majority of browser updates, data is not deleted; it is simply moved to a different profile folder or hidden because the browser requires you to sign in again. Taking a moment to check your profile identity usually resolves the issue without any technical data recovery.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for Chrome, Edge, and Firefox on desktop systems. It focuses on locating existing data and preventing accidental overwrites. Complex manual restoration of `.bak` files or deep-disk recovery requires a more technical, browser-specific process once the situation is stable.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional IT or legal advice. If your device is managed by an employer or educational institution, their IT policies may override these steps. If you suspect your accounts have been compromised by a third party, prioritize securing your primary email and financial accounts.
