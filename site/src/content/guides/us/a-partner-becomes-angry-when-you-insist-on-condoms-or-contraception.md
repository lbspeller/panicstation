# What to do if… a partner becomes angry when you insist on condoms or contraception (USA)

## Short answer
Prioritize your immediate physical safety by creating distance and ending the interaction. Anger or intimidation regarding condoms or contraception is a safety alert—exit the situation and contact a confidential crisis resource for support and health options.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue sexual activity if you feel threatened, intimidated, or coerced.
- Do not feel obligated to negotiate your boundaries while you are afraid or isolated.
- Do not hand over your phone, car keys, or medical items to de-escalate their anger.
- Do not ignore physical warning signs such as blocking exits, grabbing, or throwing objects.
- Do not blame yourself for freezing or complying; these are instinctive safety responses.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer space immediately.** Exit the room or building if possible. If you cannot leave, move to a room with a lock and a phone (such as a bathroom) or toward an exit.
2. **Contact 911 if you are in danger.** If you cannot speak, stay on the line or tap the phone repeatedly so the dispatcher can hear that the line is active. **Text-to-911** is available in many US jurisdictions; check if your phone allows you to send an emergency text if speaking is unsafe.
3. **Establish a connection with a trusted contact.** Call or text someone who can stay on the line with you while you leave or who can provide transportation. If an excuse is needed to leave, use a neutral statement like "I need to go check on something" or "I am not feeling well and need to leave."
4. **Secure your privacy and medical items.** Take your birth control, wallet, and phone with you. Change your phone passcode if the partner knows it and disable lock-screen message previews.
5. **Seek Emergency Contraception (EC) if unprotected sex occurred.**
   - **Emergency Contraceptive Pills:** Levonorgestrel (Plan B) is available over-the-counter and is most effective within 72 hours, though effectiveness decreases significantly if you weigh over 165 lbs. Ulipristal acetate (Ella) requires a prescription but is more effective for up to 5 days (120 hours) and works better at higher body weights.
   - **IUDs:** A copper IUD or a 52mg hormonal IUD (Mirena/Liletta) can be inserted by a clinician within 5 days of unprotected sex as highly effective EC.
6. **Call a confidential specialist hotline for safety planning.**
   - **National Sexual Assault Hotline (RAINN):** Call 800-656-4673 or use the online chat at rainn.org.
   - **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** Call 800-799-7233, or text "START" to 88788.
   These services are confidential and do not require you to involve law enforcement.
7. **Preserve physical evidence if you are considering a report.** If sexual contact occurred without consent, try to avoid showering or changing clothes immediately. Place any involved clothing or items in a clean paper bag. Your safety and medical needs (testing for STIs or pregnancy) remain the priority over evidence collection.
8. **Reach out to the 988 Lifeline for immediate stabilization.** If you are feeling overwhelmed, hopeless, or in a state of crisis, call or text **988**. Trained counselors provide immediate emotional support and can help you ground yourself before taking the next step.

## What can wait
- You do not need to label the situation or the relationship right now.
- You do not need to confront the partner or explain your reasons for leaving.
- You do not need to decide on long-term legal or relationship actions today.

## Important reassurance
Insisting on contraception or condoms is a standard health boundary. Reactionary anger, threats, or pressure from a partner are forms of reproductive coercion and are not your fault. Physiological responses like freezing or "going along" to prevent escalation are common and do not mean you consented.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for acute situations. Long-term safety planning and medical follow-up should be coordinated with professional providers or advocates.

## Important note
This information is for immediate crisis-adjacent use in the USA and does not constitute legal or medical advice. Procedures and available resources may vary by local jurisdiction. If you are in immediate physical danger, contact 911.
