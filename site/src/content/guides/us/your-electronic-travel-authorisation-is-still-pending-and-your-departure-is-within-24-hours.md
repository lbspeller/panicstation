# What to do if… your electronic travel authorisation is still pending and your departure is within 24 hours (USA)

## Short answer
Assume you may not be allowed to board while the official ESTA status is still pending. Check your status on the official ESTA site, use CBP’s official help route if you cannot retrieve it or think there is an error, and start protecting your booking options now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rely on a confirmation email, payment receipt, or screenshot as permission to travel if the official status is still pending.
- Do not submit repeated new applications in panic unless the official ESTA guidance tells you to.
- Do not use unofficial “expedite” or paid middleman services that claim they can speed up a U.S. government decision.
- Do not wait until you are at the airport to find out whether your airline will board you with a pending status.
- Do not assume the issue can be fixed after arrival; ESTA is checked before travel under the Visa Waiver Program.
- Do not let the booking become a no-show while you keep refreshing the status page.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm this is ESTA and match it to the passport you will use.**  
   Check the passport number, issuing country, expiry date, date of birth, and name format against the ESTA application. A small mismatch can make retrieval or boarding harder.

2. **Check status only on the official ESTA status page.**  
   Use the official “Check Individual Status” page. Save the exact status wording, the application number if shown, and the time you checked. Also check spam or junk mail, but treat the official status page as the source of truth.

3. **If the status says “Authorization Pending,” plan as though this flight may not be possible.**  
   CBP guidance says an ESTA determination is usually available within 72 hours. With departure inside 24 hours, keep checking, but start acting to avoid losing the fare or being marked as a no-show.

4. **Use CBP’s official help route if something looks wrong or you cannot retrieve the application.**  
   Have the application number, passport details, date of birth, and travel date ready. Ask for help checking the status or correcting a retrieval problem, but do not rely on same-day resolution.

5. **Call the airline before you travel to the airport.**  
   Ask, “Will you deny boarding if my ESTA is still pending at check-in?” Then ask, “What is the last time I can change or cancel before I am treated as a no-show?” Write down the answer and the time of the call.

6. **Protect the booking while you keep checking.**  
   Look at the fare rules, change fees, cancellation window, and later flights. If moving the flight preserves value, do that before the airline’s cut-off rather than waiting for a gate refusal.

7. **Set a clear stop point.**  
   For example: “If the ESTA is still pending when online check-in closes, or when I would need to leave for the airport, I will stop and rebook.” This keeps the decision practical instead of frantic.

8. **If the status changes to approved, re-check the basics before travelling.**  
   Confirm the approval is linked to the passport you will carry, then complete check-in again. Remember that ESTA approval allows travel to request admission; it does not guarantee entry to the United States.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve why the application is pending today.
- You do not need to decide future travel plans beyond this departure.
- You do not need to argue at the airport before you know the airline’s boarding rule.
- You do not need to buy a new trip before checking whether your existing booking can be changed.

## Important reassurance
“Pending” is a system status, not a judgement on you. The safest approach is two-track: keep checking the official status while protecting your money, time, and options.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a U.S. ESTA or Visa Waiver Program travel problem in the next 24 hours. Later decisions, including visa options or disputed refusals, may need specialist immigration or travel advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. ESTA approval is generally required before boarding a U.S.-bound air or sea carrier under the Visa Waiver Program, and even an approved ESTA does not guarantee admission to the United States.
