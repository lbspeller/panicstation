# What to do if… someone keeps pressuring you to get into their car to talk privately (USA)
## Short answer

Move toward other people and a public, staffed place. Do not get into the car. If you are in immediate danger or cannot leave safely, call 911.

## Do not do these things

- Do not get into their car or go somewhere more private to continue the conversation.
- Do not keep explaining your boundary while you feel unsafe.
- Do not accept a ride or detour that leaves them in control of where you go next.
- Do not go home directly if you think they may follow you.
- Do not stop to gather information if doing so would make you less safe.
- Do not pressure yourself to decide now whether to report or respond to later messages.

## What to do now

1. **Move toward a public, staffed place.** Go inside a store, restaurant, bar, hotel lobby, gas station, pharmacy, or another place with workers nearby.

2. **Ask another person to stay with you.** Tell staff, security, or a nearby person: **“I need help. This person keeps pressuring me to get into their car, and I do not want to go with them.”** Ask not to be sent outside alone.

3. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.** Call if they block you, grab you, try to move you somewhere private, keep following you, or you cannot leave safely.

4. **Arrange a separate way to leave.** From inside the staffed place, call or text someone you trust. Ask them to stay on the phone, meet you, or help arrange a ride the other person cannot join or change. Share your location only if that feels safe.

5. **Do not lead them home if you think they may follow you.** Stay inside the staffed place and ask staff or security to help you work out a safer departure.

6. **Contact confidential support from a safe place if you need it.** You can contact RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline without deciding whether to report. Use a private device if possible, and ask about privacy before sharing identifying details.

7. **Keep basic information only if it is safe.** You may save messages and note the time, place, car details, or rideshare details for later.

## What can wait

You do not need to decide now whether to report, confront them, reply to messages, explain your boundary again, or work out what this means for the relationship. Focus only on getting to a safer place with other people nearby.

## Important reassurance

You do not need to work out whether this is a misunderstanding while you feel unsafe. You are allowed to leave abruptly, involve other people, and put safety ahead of politeness.

## Scope note

These are first steps only. Later decisions about support, reporting, contact, workplace or school concerns, or other next steps may need specialist help.

## Important note

This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If there is immediate danger, call 911. You can contact confidential sexual assault support without first deciding whether to report.
