# What to do if… a ceiling stain is spreading and you suspect water is trapped above it (USA)

## Short answer
Stay out from under the stained, bulging, or sagging area and keep other people away. From a dry, safely accessible location, stop water use that may be feeding the leak, use a reachable water shutoff if appropriate, and contact building maintenance or a plumber.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand beneath a wet, bulging, or sagging part of the ceiling.
- Don’t touch lights, ceiling fans, outlets, smoke alarms, or switches in the affected area.
- Don’t touch a breaker panel if the path to it, the panel, or the surrounding area is wet.
- Don’t poke, drill, or cut the ceiling to release water.
- Don’t place a ladder beneath the wet area.
- Don’t enter another apartment or unit to look for the source.
- Don’t keep using a bathroom, laundry appliance, kitchen fixture, or other water source that may be feeding the leak.
- Don’t restore power to the affected area until a qualified electrician says it is safe.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the room beneath the stain.** Keep people and pets out. Do not go beneath a bulge or sagging section to move belongings. If there is an immediate danger, such as smoke, sparks, fire, or a ceiling section actively falling, leave the area and call 911 from a safe place.
2. **Stop nearby water use from a safe location.** If a bathroom, laundry appliance, kitchen, or other plumbing fixture above the stain may be involved, stop using it. Close a local supply valve only if it is dry, visible, and safely reachable.
3. **Use the main water shutoff if appropriate and safely accessible.** If the source is unclear and water is still spreading, close the home or unit water shutoff if you can reach it without entering a wet area. This may not stop a roof leak, a shared building line, or water already trapped above the ceiling.
4. **Treat nearby electrical fixtures as unsafe.** If water is near a light, outlet, smoke alarm, ceiling fan, or wiring path, avoid controls in the affected area. Turn off the relevant breaker only if the route and breaker panel are dry and safely accessible. If they are not, keep away and contact building maintenance or a qualified electrician to arrange a safe shutoff.
5. **Notify the right person immediately.** If you rent or live in a multi-unit building, contact your landlord, property manager, or building maintenance and report a spreading ceiling stain with possible trapped water. Ask an upstairs neighbor to stop using water only if you can contact them without entering their unit.
6. **Call a plumber if needed.** If you own the home, the leak continues, or the source is not obvious, contact a plumber. Tell them whether the stain is spreading, dripping, bulging, near electrical fixtures, or below a bathroom or appliance.
7. **Contain drips only from a safe edge.** Place a bucket or towels only if you can do so without standing beneath a sagging or bulging area and without approaching an electrical hazard. Keep walkways dry enough to reduce slipping.
8. **Document briefly and arrange drying.** Take a few photos or a short video from a safe location. Once the leak is stopped and the area has been assessed as safe, start drying wet materials where possible and arrange further inspection. Aim to dry wet materials within 24–48 hours where possible. Do not use fans or electrical equipment in an area with unresolved wiring risk or contaminated water.
9. **Keep away from dirty water.** If the water may contain sewage or other contamination, avoid contact and tell building maintenance, the plumber, or a restoration professional. Cleanup may need additional precautions.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to identify the exact cause before making the room off-limits and contacting help.
- You don’t need to decide who will pay for repairs right now.
- You don’t need to open the ceiling or remove drywall yourself.
- You don’t need to make long-term mold-remediation decisions before the leak is stopped and the area is safe.

## Important reassurance
A spreading ceiling stain is unsettling, but you only need to take the first safe steps now: stay clear of the area beneath it, stop reachable water sources safely, and contact the right person. You are not expected to diagnose the cause yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about inspection, drying, repairs, and cleanup may need your landlord or building management, a plumber, a qualified electrician, or a restoration professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, insurance, electrical, plumbing, construction, or other professional advice. Do not enter a wet area or touch a wet electrical panel to follow any step.
