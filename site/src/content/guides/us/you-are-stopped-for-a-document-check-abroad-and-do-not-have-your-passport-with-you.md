# What to do if… you are stopped for a document check abroad and do not have your passport with you (USA)
## Short answer
Stay calm and cooperative. Explain that your passport is not with you, show any identification or passport copy you have, and ask what acceptable next step is available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not run, physically resist, or offer money to end the stop.
- Do not sign anything you cannot read or do not understand. Ask for an interpreter if you need one.
- Do not say the passport is lost or stolen until you have checked whether it is still at your lodging.
- Do not assume another ID or a photo of your passport will be accepted as a substitute for the original.
- Do not make a sudden refusal or start a confrontation about your phone. Ask what is being requested and ask for interpretation if needed.
- Do not rely on filming the interaction. Local rules vary, so focus on resolving the document check safely.

## What to do now
1. **Explain the situation simply.** If true, say that you are a U.S. citizen. Explain that your passport is at your lodging or another known location.
2. **Show what you have.** Offer any available photo ID and, if you have one, a photo or scan of your passport. These may help explain your identity but may not satisfy the check.
3. **Ask what the official will accept next.** Ask whether you may retrieve the passport, have a trusted companion bring it, return with it, or attend a station if instructed.
4. **Arrange retrieval where permitted.** Contact your lodging to confirm that the passport is there. Ask a trusted companion to bring the original passport if the official allows this.
5. **Ask for an interpreter if needed.** Keep your questions brief and ask where you are being taken and why if you are told to go to a station.
6. **If you are detained, ask for consular notification.** If you are a U.S. citizen, state that clearly and ask the authorities to notify the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate. Ask whether you may contact a trusted person.
7. **If you need emergency consular help, use the official contact routes.** Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate. You can also call +1 202-501-4444 from overseas or 1-888-407-4747 from the U.S. or Canada.
8. **If the passport appears lost or stolen, report it immediately.** Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate for replacement help before further travel.
9. **Record basic details when it is safe to do so.** Note the time, place, station or unit name, and any papers or receipts you were given.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim insurance, or change your wider travel plans.
- You do not need to start replacing the passport unless it is confirmed lost or stolen, or consular staff advise you to do so.
- You do not need to prove every detail during the stop. Focus on clarifying the next acceptable step.

## Important reassurance
Not having your passport with you does not automatically mean it is lost. The immediate task is to explain where it is, ask what the official needs, and arrange retrieval if that is allowed.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the stop and the next few hours. If you are detained, fined, or unable to recover your passport, you may need country-specific legal help and U.S. consular support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal advice. Local identification and passport-carrying rules vary by country. If you feel unsafe or are detained, prioritize calm communication, interpretation if needed, and U.S. consular assistance.
