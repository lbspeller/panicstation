# What to do if… your phone is stuck in recovery mode and asks to be connected to a computer (USA)

## Short answer
Pause before choosing Restore, factory reset, or wipe. Use only the official update or repair route for your phone, and check backups and sign-in access before doing anything that may erase data.

## Do not do these things
- Do not choose Restore, factory reset, wipe data, or initialization just to see if it works; these options can erase the phone.
- Do not download phone recovery utilities from ads, pop-ups, social media links, or unofficial sites.
- Do not keep force-restarting the phone again and again without changing anything else.
- Do not use a damaged cable, USB hub, keyboard USB port, or low-power port if you have a better option.
- Do not sign out of your Apple Account or Google Account pre-emptively; only do it if the official restore process specifically requires it.

## What to do now
1. **Make the next attempt stable.**  
   Plug the phone into power. If it is hot, leave it somewhere safe to cool before trying an update, repair, or restore.

2. **Identify the phone type and the exact screen.**  
   Take a photo of the recovery message with another device. Then choose the official route for that device:
   - For iPhone or iPad, use Finder on a Mac, the Apple Devices app on Windows, or iTunes where Apple says it is still needed.
   - For Pixel, use Google’s official Pixel update and software repair instructions.
   - For Samsung, use Samsung Smart Switch or Samsung support instructions for emergency software recovery when they match the message on screen.

3. **Check backups before choosing anything destructive.**  
   Use another trusted device or a browser to check whether your photos, contacts, and device backup are already in iCloud, Google Photos, Google backup, Samsung backup, or another backup service you normally use. If you cannot confirm a backup, assume some recent data may exist only on the phone.

4. **Connect carefully to the computer.**  
   Use a known good cable and plug directly into the computer. Keep the computer awake, keep it connected to the internet, and do not disconnect the phone while the official tool is updating, repairing, or restoring.

5. **Choose the least destructive official option first.**  
   If the official tool offers Update, Repair, or similar wording that says it will try to reinstall or repair software without erasing data, choose that before Restore, factory reset, wipe data, or initialization. This is not a guarantee that data is safe, but it avoids jumping straight to the most destructive option.

6. **If you see an error code or alert, fix the connection before escalating.**  
   Update the computer software or phone-management app, plug directly into the computer rather than a hub, try a different cable or USB port, and restart the computer if the official instructions say to. Do not treat an error code as a reason to panic-wipe.

7. **Before any wipe, reduce account lockout risk.**  
   Make sure you know the Apple Account or Google Account password, can access the recovery email or phone number, and have another way to approve two-factor sign-in. If your only authenticator app is on the stuck phone, look for backup codes or another trusted device before wiping.

8. **Use official support early if the data matters or the phone may be damaged.**  
   If the phone was dropped, got wet, overheated, failed during an update, is under warranty, or contains important unbacked-up data, pause and contact the manufacturer or an authorized service provider before choosing a destructive restore.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the root cause right now.
- You do not need to decide today whether to replace, upgrade, or repair the phone.
- You do not need to clean up storage, change settings, or optimize the phone before you regain access.
- You can deal with carrier, insurance, trade-in, or replacement paperwork after you know your backup and account situation.

## Important reassurance
A recovery mode screen does not automatically mean the phone or the data is gone. The main thing is to avoid rushing into a wipe before checking backups, account access, and the official repair path.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilize the situation, avoid irreversible data loss, and use the safest official route available for your phone. Later decisions may need manufacturer support, an authorized repair provider, or a specialist data-recovery service.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional technical support, legal advice, financial advice, medical advice, or therapy. Update, repair, restore, and factory reset processes can erase data and may require account credentials afterward.
