# What to do if… a rental car company offers a different vehicle type and you do not feel safe driving it (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive away in a vehicle that you do not feel able to operate safely. Tell the agent what you are not comfortable driving and ask for a suitable alternative at the booked rate, or ask whether the booking can be cancelled without a charge.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign an agreement hoping that you will work out unfamiliar controls after leaving the lot.
- Do not drive away just to test a vehicle if its size, visibility, transmission, or controls already feel unsafe for you.
- Do not accept a paid upgrade without checking the total price.
- Do not let counter pressure rush you into a decision.
- Do not assume that a pending card hold is a final charge. Ask for written confirmation of any hold or charge.

## What to do now
1. **Pause before signing or taking the keys.** Say: “I do not feel able to drive that vehicle type safely. I need another option.”
2. **Name the specific problem.** For example, say that the vehicle is too large, has controls you do not understand, has poor visibility for you, or is not a transmission type that you can drive.
3. **Ask for a suitable vehicle.** Ask whether the company can provide a vehicle category and controls that you can operate safely at the booked rate.
4. **Ask what alternatives are available.** Ask whether staff can check other vehicles on-site, check a nearby location, or tell you when a suitable returned vehicle may become available.
5. **Ask about cancellation before agreeing to anything.** If no suitable vehicle is available, ask whether the booking can be cancelled without a fee. Ask for written confirmation of the answer.
6. **Check the paperwork before signing.** Confirm the vehicle category, total price, any upgrade charge, and any cancellation or no-show fee shown on the agreement.
7. **Save a clear record.** Keep your reservation confirmation. Ask for an email, receipt, or written note showing the vehicle offered, any proposed charge, and the outcome. Note the location, time, and the name of the company representative you spoke with.
8. **Contact the booking service if needed.** If you booked through a third-party website or travel service, contact it while you are still at the counter and ask it to record that the reserved category was not available.
9. **Contact your card issuer promptly if you are charged unexpectedly.** Ask how to dispute the charge, follow the issuer’s process, and keep copies of your booking confirmation, receipts, and notes. Credit-card and debit-card protections can differ, and some steps are time-sensitive.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal complaint while you are standing at the counter.
- You do not need to negotiate the best-value upgrade right now.
- You do not need to contact several organisations at once.
- You can review complaint routes after you are somewhere safe and have saved your documents.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to stop when a last-minute vehicle change leaves you unsure about driving safely. Declining a vehicle that you do not feel able to control is a responsible decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps for avoiding an unsafe drive and reducing pressure at the counter. Later questions about charges, complaints, or a booking dispute may need help from the rental company, the booking service, your card issuer, or a consumer protection office.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Rental policies and card-dispute processes vary by company, location, booking channel, and payment method.
