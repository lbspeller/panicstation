# What to do if… you suspect someone is watching or signalling to others while you handle cash or valuables (USA)

## Short answer
Stop handling the cash or valuables, put them away, and move immediately to a staffed, well-lit place such as a store counter, bank lobby, customer service desk, or security desk. If you think a robbery, theft, threat, or blocking from leaving is happening now, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep counting money, displaying valuables, or trying to finish fast while you feel targeted.
- Don’t challenge them, follow them, stare them down, or try to physically block them.
- Don’t move into a quieter place to get space, such as an empty aisle, stairwell, alley, or parking-lot corner.
- Don’t start filming if it keeps you in place, escalates the situation, or distracts you from moving to safety.
- Don’t unlock your car and reorganize your wallet, phone, cash, or bag at the car door.
- Don’t accept help from a stranger that requires you to hand over your phone, wallet, card, cash, or bag.
- Don’t worry about proving what the signal meant before you move.

## What to do now
1. **Secure what is visible.** Put cash away, close your wallet, zip or close your bag, and keep it in front of you. If you are at an ATM, cancel or end the transaction if you can do that promptly, then put your card and cash away before moving.
2. **Move to a staffed anchor point.** Go to a nearby open business, bank lobby, customer service desk, front checkout, security desk, or other place where staff can see you. If you are already in a store, go directly to an employee and stay near them.
3. **Use one clear sentence to get help.** Say: “I think someone is watching me and signaling to another person while I handle cash. Can I stay here, and can you call security?” Stay close to staff, cameras, and other people.
4. **Avoid being boxed in.** Keep space around you, stay where staff can see you, and avoid letting anyone guide you toward a doorway, aisle end, restroom corridor, stairwell, or parking area.
5. **If you think you are being followed, do not lead them to isolation.** Stay inside the staffed place or move to another busy, well-lit location. Avoid shortcuts, alleys, quiet exits, and going straight to your car alone.
6. **Call based on what is happening now.** Call **911** if there is an immediate threat, someone is trying to take property, someone is threatening you, you are being blocked from leaving, or a crime is in progress. If the people have left and you are safe, ask staff or security to help you contact the local police non-emergency number.
7. **Give simple, useful details from safety.** If staff, security, or 911 asks, describe where you are, what happened, clothing, height or build, direction of travel, and any vehicle description or license plate you noticed. Do not return toward danger to get details.
8. **Check essentials from the safe place.** Check your wallet, phone, keys, card, cash, and bag. If a bank card or phone is missing, use an official banking app, your card issuer’s official number, or your phone provider’s official route to freeze, cancel, or lock access.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove they were coordinating before you move to safety.
- You do not need to confront anyone or test whether they are suspicious.
- You do not need to post about it, warn others online, or review footage yourself right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal report before you are stable and safe.
- You do not need to recover every detail perfectly.

## Important reassurance
A signal may mean nothing, but you do not have to prove intent before taking a low-risk safety step. Putting valuables away, moving toward staff, and asking for help are reasonable actions when something feels wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. If you were threatened, followed, touched, blocked from leaving, or had anything stolen, later decisions may need help from police, victim support, your bank or card issuer, security staff, or another trusted specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional safety advice. If you feel in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, prioritize getting to a safer place and calling 911.
