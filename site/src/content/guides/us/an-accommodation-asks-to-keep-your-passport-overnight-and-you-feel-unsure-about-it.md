# What to do if… an accommodation asks to keep your passport overnight and you feel unsure about it (USA)

## Short answer
Request that the staff record the necessary details immediately while you wait, or offer a photocopy of the identification page instead. Do not leave the original document with the front desk unless you receive a formal, dated receipt and have no other accommodation options.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use your passport as a financial deposit or collateral for room charges.
- Do not leave the reception area while your passport is out of your sight without a resolution.
- Do not agree to an overnight hold based solely on "standard policy" if they cannot explain the specific legal registration requirement.
- Do not provide your passport to staff who appear evasive or who attempt to store the document in an unsecured location, such as an unlocked drawer or open counter.
- Do not escalate the situation into a physical or loud confrontation; maintain a professional tone to ensure you are not removed from the property before recovering your document.

## What to do now
1. **Request an immediate recording of details.** **Ask the staff to transcribe the passport number, name, and expiration date while you remain at the desk.**
   - State that you require the document for identification throughout the evening.
   - Offer to wait 10–15 minutes for them to complete any mandatory government registration forms.
2. **Provide a high-quality photocopy or digital scan.** **Offer a printed copy of your passport photo page as an alternative to the original.**
   - Note that many jurisdictions accept a photocopy for hotel records if the original is presented for verification.
   - Check if a secondary ID, such as a U.S. driver’s license, can be held instead if they insist on collateral.
3. **Observe the storage method if you must surrender the document.** **Verify that the passport is being placed in a localized safe or a restricted-access, locked cabinet.**
   - Identify the specific staff member taking possession of the document.
   - Record the time and date the document was handed over.
4. **Demand a formal written receipt.** **Ensure the receipt includes the hotel's official stamp, the staff member’s signature, your passport number, and a guaranteed return time.**
   - If the hotel refuses to provide a receipt, treat this as a signal to keep the document and seek alternative lodging.
5. **Set an alarm for the return time.** **Return to the desk at the exact time specified on the receipt or registration form.**
   - If the staff member who took the passport is no longer on duty, ask for the manager immediately.
6. **Contact local tourism authorities if the document is withheld.** **Call the local "Tourism Police" or non-emergency services if the hotel refuses to return the document upon request.**
   - In many countries, holding a foreign passport against the owner’s will is illegal unless authorized by specific national security laws.
7. **Notify the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate.** **Email or call the American Citizen Services (ACS) unit if the accommodation continues to withhold the document.**
   - Use the 2026 24/7 Consular Task Force contact details provided in your Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) alerts.
   - Report the situation as a "withheld document" rather than a lost passport.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal legal claim against the hotel chain immediately.
- You do not need to research the specific hospitality laws of the country in the middle of check-in.
- You do not need to cancel credit cards or report identity theft unless there is evidence the document was tampered with or removed from the premises.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel protective of your primary travel document. While many countries require hotels to report guest data to the police, the practice of keeping the physical booklet overnight is becoming less common due to digital scanning. Standing your ground for a few minutes often results in the staff finding a way to process your check-in immediately.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document retention disputes at check-in. It does not cover the legal replacement process for stolen passports or long-term travel insurance claims.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for guest registration vary significantly by jurisdiction; some nations technically require hotels to hold passports for police inspection, though this is rare in 2026. Always prioritize your physical safety over the document; if you feel threatened, move to a safe public location before calling for assistance.
