# What to do if… you hear repeated dripping inside a wall but cannot find any visible leak (USA)

## Short answer
Assume a hidden leak: **shut off your home’s main water supply now**, and avoid outlets/switches near the suspected area.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep running faucets/showers to “see what happens” — if there’s a pressurized leak, you can accelerate damage quickly.
- Don’t open up drywall/ceilings in a hurry (you can hit wiring/pipes or release trapped water suddenly).
- Don’t touch or use outlets/switches near the suspect area if moisture might be present — and don’t operate switches while standing in water.
- Don’t leave the home unattended with water still on if the dripping continues when nothing is running.
- Don’t delay notifying building management/landlord if you rent or live in a condo — leaks can spread across units fast.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Keep people and pets away from the suspect wall/ceiling area—especially if the ceiling looks bowed/sagging or the wall feels soft.
2. **Shut off the main water valve to the home.** Typical locations: where the water line enters the home, basement/utility room, garage, crawlspace, or near the water heater. Close the valve fully.
3. **Do a quick check without delaying help.** If there are any signs of electrical risk (buzzing, sparking, burning smell, wet fixtures) or structural risk (sagging ceiling), skip “waiting to see” and contact help right away.
4. **If you have a water meter, do a “no-use” check.** With everything in the home off (no taps, toilets, appliances), see if the meter’s leak indicator/dial is moving. If it moves, keep the main valve off until a professional assesses it.
5. **Reduce electrical and collapse risk.**
   - Don’t use switches/outlets near the suspected area.
   - Move valuables away from the wall/ceiling line.
   - If a ceiling is sagging or there’s a bulge, keep clear underneath.
6. **Contact the right party immediately.**
   - **If you rent:** contact your landlord/property manager’s emergency maintenance and report a suspected hidden leak; tell them you shut off the main water.
   - **If you own:** call an emergency plumber and describe it as “possible hidden leak inside a wall,” plus what the water meter did (if checked).
   - **If condo/HOA:** notify building management/HOA right away and follow their emergency procedure.
7. **Document briefly (without slowing repairs).** Take a few photos/video of any early signs (staining, swelling paint, bulges) and note the time you first heard the dripping and when you shut off water.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide right now about insurance, restoration companies, or opening walls — first stop water flow and get the source assessed.
- You don’t need to “prove” the leak before calling; professionals have tools to locate hidden leaks.
- You don’t need to start drying equipment until you know the leak is stopped and the area is safe.

## Important reassurance
It’s normal to feel unsure when you can’t see water. Acting early (water off + notify the right people) is the safest way to prevent a small hidden leak from becoming major damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk and limit damage. Locating and repairing a hidden leak may require a licensed plumber and follow-up drying/repairs.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional or site-specific advice. If you suspect water has contacted electrical systems (sparking, buzzing, burning smell, wet outlets/fixtures) or the ceiling/wall looks structurally compromised, prioritise personal safety and get professional help urgently.
