# What to do if… you hear repeated dripping inside a wall but cannot find any visible leak (USA)

## Short answer
Treat repeated dripping inside a wall as a possible hidden leak. If it is safe and accessible, turn off the main water supply and keep away from nearby outlets, switches, sagging ceilings, or soft walls while you contact the right repair or maintenance help.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep running faucets, showers, toilets, washing machines, or dishwashers to “test it” while the dripping continues.
- Don’t cut, drill, or tear into drywall in a hurry; hidden wiring, pipes, or trapped water may be behind it.
- Don’t touch or use outlets, switches, light fixtures, or plugged-in devices near the suspected wet area.
- Don’t stand under a sagging ceiling, a bulging ceiling, or a wall that looks swollen.
- Don’t leave the home unattended with the water still on if the dripping continues and you can safely shut it off.
- Don’t delay telling a landlord, property manager, condo board, or building maintenance if another party controls repairs or shared plumbing.

## What to do now
1. Move people and pets away from the suspected wall or ceiling, especially if you see sagging, bulging, bubbling paint, stains, or soft drywall.
2. Stop using water in the home for the moment, including toilets, sinks, showers, washing machines, dishwashers, and refrigerator water or ice dispensers.
3. If the main water shutoff valve is safe and accessible, close it fully. Common locations include a basement, garage, crawlspace, utility room, near the water heater, or where the water line enters the home.
4. If there are signs of electrical danger, such as sparks, buzzing, a burning smell, wet outlets, wet fixtures, or water near a breaker panel, stay back and contact emergency help, your utility, or a qualified electrician as appropriate for your situation.
5. If you have a water meter and can reach it safely, do a no-use check: make sure no water is being used, then look for a moving leak indicator, spinning dial, active flow display, or changed reading. Any movement or changed reading while everything is off suggests water may still be flowing somewhere.
6. Move valuables, electronics, papers, rugs, and furniture away from the wall or ceiling line without standing under a bulge or touching wet electrical items.
7. Contact the right party now: emergency maintenance if you rent, building management if you are in a condo or multifamily building, or an emergency plumber if you own and control the plumbing.
8. Document only what is easy: take photos or a short video of stains, swelling, dripping sounds, meter movement, and the shutoff valve position, then note when you first heard the sound and when water was turned off.

## What can wait
You do not need to decide right now about insurance claims, restoration companies, opening walls, repainting, replacing drywall, or proving exactly where the leak is. The immediate job is to reduce water flow, avoid electrical and collapse risks, and get the source assessed.

## Important reassurance
A hidden drip is unsettling because you cannot see what is happening. Taking early, simple steps can limit damage and helps the plumber, landlord, or building maintenance team respond faster.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Finding the source, repairing plumbing, checking electrical safety, drying wet materials, and deciding on insurance or tenant issues may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, engineering, electrical, plumbing, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If water may have reached electrical systems or the wall or ceiling may be unstable, prioritize personal safety and get urgent professional help.
