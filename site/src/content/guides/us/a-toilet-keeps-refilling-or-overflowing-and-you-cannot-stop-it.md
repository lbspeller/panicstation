# What to do if… a toilet keeps refilling or overflowing and you cannot stop it (USA)

## Short answer
Shut off the water immediately at the toilet’s supply valve by turning it clockwise until it stops, or use the home's main water shut-off valve.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t flush the toilet again if the bowl is already full or if you suspect a clog, as this will cause an immediate overflow.
- Don’t touch electrical outlets, power strips, or switches if you are standing in water or if water has reached the electrical components.
- Don’t use tools to force a stuck valve; if it does not turn by hand, move to the main water shut-off instead.
- Don’t use the toilet or any nearby water fixtures until a professional confirms the system is clear and repaired.
- Don’t leave the water supply on while you go to find help or tools.

## What to do now
1. **Locate the toilet shut-off valve immediately:** **Look behind the toilet near the floor or wall for a small valve connected to a metal or braided hose.**
2. **Close the valve to stop the water flow:** **Turn the handle clockwise (righty-tighty) until it stops moving.**
   - If it is a multi-turn valve (oval-shaped), it may require several full rotations.
   - If it is a quarter-turn valve (lever-style), turn it 90 degrees until it is perpendicular to the pipe.
3. **Use the main water shut-off if the toilet valve fails:** **Locate the main valve where the water line enters your home (typically in a basement, garage, utility closet, or outside near the water meter).**
   - Turn the wheel handle clockwise or the lever handle 90 degrees to stop all water to the property.
4. **Isolate electrical hazards from a dry position:** **If water is near outlets or wiring, and your breaker panel is in a dry area, shut off power to the bathroom.**
   - Do not attempt this if you must step in water to reach the electrical panel.
5. **Contain the spill to prevent structural damage:** **Place heavy towels at the bathroom doorway to stop water from spreading to other rooms.**
   - Move rugs, furniture, or electronics away from the wet area.
6. **Notify the appropriate repair service:** **Contact your landlord’s emergency maintenance line (if renting) or an emergency plumber (if owning).**
   - Explicitly state that you have shut off the water supply.
7. **Begin drying surfaces within the first 24 hours:** **Mop up standing water and use fans or open windows to increase airflow once the leak is stopped.**
   - Drying materials within 24–48 hours is critical to preventing mold growth.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify why the toilet failed (e.g., a broken flapper or stuck float) right now.
- You do not need to clean the internal tank or bowl until the external flooding is managed.
- You can wait to file insurance claims or document long-term damage until the immediate water flow is neutralized and help is dispatched.

## Important reassurance
Water emergencies are high-stress, but stopping the flow at the source is the most effective way to prevent further damage. Most toilet overflows are caused by simple mechanical failures that a professional can resolve quickly once the water is off.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for residential water leaks. It does not cover technical plumbing repairs, deep-cleaning for biohazards, or electrical restoration.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only. If water has entered electrical systems or if you suspect sewage contamination, contact a qualified electrician or biohazard restoration specialist immediately.
