# What to do if… you hear someone trying your door handle or testing windows from outside (USA)

## Short answer
Move everyone to a safer room, lock that room, and call 911 if you believe someone is trying to get in now or you feel in immediate danger. Block the room door only if you can do it quickly without exposing yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t open the door to check who it is.
- Don’t go outside to confront them or try to scare them off.
- Don’t stand in front of doors or windows where you can be seen.
- Don’t search the home while you are unsure where the person is.
- Don’t post live updates or your location on social media.
- Don’t delay calling 911 because you are not completely sure what is happening.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer room immediately.** Bring your phone, keys, and the people with you, including children, roommates, or guests. Prefer a room with a solid door that locks and, if possible, another way out.
2. **Make that room harder to enter.** Lock the door. If you can do it fast and safely, push a heavy object against the door. Stay low and out of view of windows.
3. **Call 911 if entry seems to be actively attempted or you feel in danger.** Say your address, including apartment or unit number, that someone is trying the door or windows right now, how many people are inside, and where in the home you are.
4. **If speaking could put you at risk, still call 911 if you can.** Stay on the line and follow the call-taker’s instructions. If you cannot safely make a voice call, text 911 only if it is available where you are; a voice call is generally preferred when possible.
5. **Use security tools you can reach without moving through the home.** Trigger an alarm panic button if you have one. Turn on exterior lights from your safer room if you can do it by switch or app. Do not cross the home to do this.
6. **Observe only from a safe place.** If you can check a door camera, peephole, or camera app without being seen, note basic details such as clothing, direction of travel, or vehicle. Do not linger at windows.
7. **If someone claims to be an official or emergency responder, keep the door closed until you are sure.** Ask them to identify themselves through the closed door. If you are unsure, tell the 911 call-taker what is happening and follow their instructions.
8. **Stay in the safer room until responders are confirmed or the dispatcher tells you what to do.** Do not come out just because the noise stops. Wait until you can clearly identify responders or you have clear instructions.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide right now whether it was definitely a break-in attempt. If it feels urgent, treat it as urgent.
- You don’t need to search the property, check every lock, or review all camera footage while you may still be at risk.
- You don’t need to contact a landlord, building management, neighbors, or insurance until the immediate danger has passed.
- You don’t need a perfect description. Give what you know, then focus on staying safe.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shaky, frozen, or scattered is a common body response to threat. Your task right now is simple: get behind a locked door, keep everyone together, stay out of view, and get emergency help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to stabilize the moment and prevent irreversible mistakes. Later decisions, such as reports, repairs, or security changes, may need specialist help once you are safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe someone is attempting to enter your home or you feel unsafe, prioritize immediate safety and contacting emergency services.
