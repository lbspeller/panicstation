# What to do if… you develop new yellowing of your eyes or skin that comes on quickly (USA)

## Short answer
Contact a health care professional now for prompt advice and assessment. Seek emergency care now, or call 911 if needed, if you feel severely unwell, are getting worse quickly, or cannot travel safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait a couple of days to see whether new, fast-onset yellowing goes away.
- Do not drink alcohol while arranging care.
- Do not take extra acetaminophen, also called Tylenol, or combine cold, flu, or pain products that may contain acetaminophen.
- Do not start, stop, or change prescription medicines on your own unless a health care professional tells you to.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel dizzy, drowsy, confused, faint, or in significant pain.

## What to do now
1. Call 911 now if the person collapses, has a seizure, has trouble breathing, or cannot be awakened.
2. Seek emergency care now if you are confused, feel very unwell, are getting worse quickly, have severe or worsening abdominal pain, have repeated vomiting, or have fever or chills with the yellowing. Call 911 if you cannot travel safely.
3. If none of those apply, contact your primary care office or another health care professional now and say: “I have new yellowing of my eyes or skin that came on quickly. I need prompt advice and assessment.” If you cannot obtain prompt advice, use an urgent care clinic or emergency department based on local availability and how unwell you feel.
4. If there is any chance that you took too much acetaminophen, another medicine, or a supplement, call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or use their online tool now. Do not delay the call while gathering details.
5. Bring medicine bottles, supplement containers, or clear photos of the labels. Note when the yellowing started and whether you have dark urine, pale stools, itching, fever, nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain.
6. Tell the health care professional if you are pregnant. Arrange a safe ride if you should not drive yourself.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the cause before seeking care.
- You do not need to start a cleanse, fasting plan, or restrictive diet.
- You do not need to make work, travel, or longer-term decisions now. Focus on arranging a safe medical assessment.

## Important reassurance
New yellowing can feel alarming. Yellow skin alone can have causes other than jaundice, but a new fast change still deserves prompt advice. You do not need to work out the cause by yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. An in-person assessment may include blood tests and sometimes imaging. Later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, diagnostic, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you feel severely unwell, are getting worse quickly, or cannot travel safely.
