# What to do if… you reset a device and cannot find your backups to restore (USA)

## Short answer
Pause new changes on the reset device, then check the right account and any other device or cloud service that may still hold the data. Many cases are a wrong-account problem or a sync-versus-backup problem, so check the cloud data itself before resetting again.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t repeatedly factory reset the device hoping a backup will appear.
- Don’t install lots of apps, download large libraries, or force large syncs before you have checked what still exists elsewhere.
- Don’t sign out of Apple, Google, Microsoft, work, or school accounts on other devices yet; cached or synced copies may still be there.
- Don’t delete cloud files, photos, notes, or messages to “tidy up” while you are still searching.
- Don’t give immediate remote access or payment details to recovery ads or support pages that promise guaranteed recovery.
- Don’t assume a missing device backup means every synced item is gone.

## What to do now
1. **Pause new changes**
   - Stop installing apps, restoring again, downloading libraries, or deleting anything.
   - If a restore is visibly still running, do not interrupt it just to check another option.
   - Where possible, use a computer or another device to check cloud accounts so you do not keep changing the reset device.

2. **Write down the facts**
   - Note the device type, model, what reset or reinstall you did, and roughly when it happened.
   - List the missing data types: photos, videos, texts, notes, contacts, documents, downloads, app data, or settings.
   - List every account that might apply, including personal, family, work, school, old email addresses, and accounts used for purchases.

3. **Check cloud data directly, not only the backup list**
   - Sign in through a browser and check photos, cloud drive files, contacts, calendars, notes, and deleted items or trash areas.
   - Check whether the data is under a different account than the one on the reset device.
   - If you find the data online, verify it looks complete before turning on full sync everywhere.

4. **For iPhone or iPad**
   - Check the Apple Account used during setup and any older Apple Accounts used for purchases.
   - If you want to restore from iCloud Backup, Apple’s restore process happens during setup; if the device is already set up, the usual Apple process requires erasing it again first. Do not erase again until you have confirmed that the backup you need exists.
   - Check iCloud Photos, iCloud Drive, Notes, Contacts, Calendar, and Recently Deleted areas separately, because some items sync to iCloud rather than being inside the device backup.
   - If you ever connected the device to a Mac or PC, check whether that computer has a local device backup.

5. **For Android**
   - Confirm that the phone is signed in to the Google Account that would have held the backup.
   - In Settings, check Google backup details to see what data and apps the device says are backed up.
   - Check Google Photos, including Trash, and Google Drive separately.
   - If the phone is already set up and you cannot find a restore route, avoid another reset until you have confirmed a backup exists. Android restore options can vary by device maker and Android version.

6. **For Windows, macOS, and cloud folders**
   - Check the web version of the cloud service, not only the folder on the computer.
   - If OneDrive was used, check the OneDrive web recycle bin first.
   - If you have Microsoft 365, Restore your OneDrive may be available for file and folder changes within the last 30 days.
   - For other sync services, check deleted items, trash, version history, and activity history on the service’s website.

7. **Check other places that may still have copies**
   - Look at old phones, tablets, laptops, external drives, SD cards, USB drives, email attachments, chat attachments, and shared albums.
   - Ask family members or collaborators only for specific shared folders, albums, or files you know they may have.
   - If the device is managed by an employer or school, contact the IT help desk before trying more restores.

8. **If the missing data is high value**
   - Stop using the reset device as much as practical.
   - Ask official platform support, your work or school IT help desk, or a trusted local repair or recovery provider what is realistically possible.
   - Be cautious: strong encryption and a true factory reset may make recovery impossible on many modern devices.
   - Avoid anyone who guarantees recovery, pressures you to act immediately, or asks for remote access before clearly explaining what they will do.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a new backup plan right now.
- You do not need to buy storage, replace the device, or rebuild every app today.
- You do not need to contact every support channel at once; first check the relevant account, cloud trash, other devices, and any computer backups.
- You do not need to decide whether the data is permanently gone until you have checked the main places it could still exist.

## Important reassurance
This is a common and very stressful problem. Missing backups are often caused by the wrong account, a backup that covered only some data, or data that was syncing somewhere else instead of being stored in the device backup.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and look for remaining copies. Later decisions may need help from official platform support, an IT administrator, a trusted repair provider, or a data recovery specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical, or other professional advice. Recovery is not guaranteed and depends on the device, operating system, encryption, app settings, account setup, and what was backed up or synced before the reset.
