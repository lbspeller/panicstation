# What to do if… you notice bricks or masonry on a chimney or exterior wall have become loose or shifted (USA)

## Short answer
Assume an immediate falling-debris and structural hazard: establish a 15-foot exclusion zone around the area and contact a licensed structural engineer or CSIA-certified chimney professional for an NFPA 211 Level 2 inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Don't climb onto the roof or use a ladder to physically test the stability of the masonry.
- Don't attempt to remove individual loose bricks or "re-seat" shifted stones.
- Don't apply DIY caulking, glue, or mortar patches, as these can trap moisture and hide escalating structural failure.
- Don't use any fireplace, wood stove, or gas appliance that vents through a damaged chimney.
- Don't allow anyone to stand, walk, or park vehicles in the potential "drop zone" below the damage.
- Don't use high-pressure water or power tools near the area, as vibrations can trigger a collapse.

## What to do now
1. **Establish an immediate exclusion zone.** Mark off the area directly beneath and adjacent to the loose masonry using rope, tape, or physical barriers.
   - Extend the zone at least 15 feet from the base of the wall or chimney to account for debris bounce.
2. **Clear the drop zone of portable assets.** If it is safe to do so without standing directly under the hazard, move vehicles, trash bins, and outdoor furniture away from the area.
3. **Deactivate all connected heating appliances.** Shut off fireplaces, furnaces, or water heaters that utilize the affected chimney/flue.
   - Ensure carbon monoxide (CO) alarms are powered and functional.
   - If you detect a "sooty" smell or see smoke escaping through masonry cracks, evacuate and call 911.
4. **Identify immediate life-safety threats.** Call 911 if bricks are actively falling, the structure is visibly leaning toward a public right-of-way, or a collapse appears imminent.
5. **Request an NFPA 211 Level 2 or Level 3 inspection.** Contact a licensed structural engineer (for walls) or a CSIA-certified chimney professional (for chimneys). 
   - Specify that there has been "observed structural movement" or "shifted masonry."
   - Ask for a "stability and falling-hazard assessment."
6. **Capture visual evidence from a safe distance.** Take photographs of the shifting or cracks from ground level without approaching the hazard.
   - Note the date of discovery and any recent triggers such as heavy storms, seismic activity, or nearby construction.
7. **Notify authorities for public hazards.** Contact your local building department or code enforcement (often via 311) if the masonry threatens a public sidewalk, street, or neighboring property.
8. **Provide written notice to landlords.** If you are a tenant, notify the property owner in writing immediately, describing the "structural instability and falling-debris risk" to ensure liability is documented.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the root cause (such as foundation settlement or freeze-thaw cycles) before stabilizing the area.
- You do not need to choose permanent repair materials or matching brick colors during the initial safety phase.
- You do not need to decide between a partial repair or a full rebuild until a professional report is provided.

## Important reassurance
Physical movement in masonry is a serious observation, and it is normal to feel uncertain about the safety of the entire building. By creating a physical distance between people and the hazard and stopping the use of connected appliances, you have already significantly reduced the primary risks of injury and fire.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and hazard isolation only. Structural masonry repairs are governed by local building codes and NFPA 211 standards; permanent fixes require professional engineering or masonry certification.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not a substitute for professional engineering, legal, or emergency services. If masonry appears to be in an active state of collapse, contact local emergency services immediately.
