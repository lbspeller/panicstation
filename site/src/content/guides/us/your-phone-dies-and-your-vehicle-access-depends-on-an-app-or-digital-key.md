# What to do if… your phone dies and your vehicle access depends on an app or digital key (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safe location away from traffic and attempt to use the "tap" method on the door handle, as many digital keys function for several hours after the phone powers off. If the vehicle does not respond, locate a physical backup key or obtain a temporary charge for the device.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remain standing in active traffic lanes or on the narrow shoulder of a high-speed road while attempting to revive the phone.
- Do not attempt to force or pry the door handle, as digital locking mechanisms can be easily damaged.
- Do not factory reset the phone or delete the vehicle app if you manage to get a temporary charge, as this will revoke the digital security token.
- Do not hand your phone to a stranger to "fix" or provide them with your device passcode or vehicle account credentials.
- Do not break a vehicle window unless there is an immediate threat to life, such as a child or pet trapped inside in extreme heat.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize physical safety and scene lighting.**
   - Move yourself and any passengers to a sidewalk, business entrance, or well-lit area.
   - If the vehicle is blocking a lane and cannot be moved, call **911** for a safety standoff.
2. **Test the device's Power Reserve / Express Mode.**
   - **iPhone users (11 or newer):** Hold the top of the powered-off iPhone near the vehicle's NFC reader (usually the driver's door handle). Digital keys in Apple Wallet often function for up to 5 hours after the phone battery reaches the "red" reserve level.
   - **Android users (select models):** Attempt to tap the phone against the reader. Newer devices supporting the CCC Digital Key 2.0 standard may retain enough power for a few hours of basic NFC functionality.
3. **Locate the battery-free backup key.**
   - Search your wallet, phone case, or bag for a plastic **Key Card** (common for Tesla, BMW, and Hyundai).
   - If you have a physical key fob with a dead battery, look for a hidden "emergency blade" inside the fob; use it in the manual lock cylinder (often hidden behind a plastic cap on the driver’s door handle).
4. **Use an alternative authorized device or user.**
   - If you have an Apple Watch or secondary tablet paired with the vehicle, attempt to use it.
   - Call a family member or co-owner who has "shared key" access; they can often remotely unlock and "remote start" the vehicle from their own phone via the manufacturer's app.
5. **Secure a "micro-charge" for the device.**
   - Enter a nearby business and ask to use a charger for 5–10 minutes. 
   - Most digital keys only require the phone to be powered "on" to authenticate via Bluetooth or UWB (Ultra-Wideband).
6. **Contact manufacturer roadside assistance.**
   - Use a borrowed phone to call the manufacturer’s dedicated service line (e.g., Tesla Roadside, BMW Assist, Hyundai Bluelink).
   - **Identify the specific issue:** State clearly that you are locked out due to a dead mobile device and require a remote unlock or a physical lockout service.
7. **Identify the internal start sequence.**
   - Once inside, if the phone is still dead, place it exactly on the vehicle’s designated NFC induction spot (usually the wireless charging pad or a specific tray) to start the engine.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update the vehicle software or the mobile app right now.
- You do not need to troubleshoot Bluetooth pairing issues until the device is fully charged.
- You do not need to decide whether to switch back to a physical key permanently; focus only on the immediate transit.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company unless you require a tow that is not covered by the manufacturer.

## Important reassurance
Digital key failure due to battery exhaustion is a recognized technical limitation, not a personal error. Most modern systems are designed with a low-power buffer specifically for this scenario, and access is usually restored within minutes of finding a power source or using a secondary card.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital lockout scenarios in the USA as of 2026. It does not cover mechanical lock failures, total 12V vehicle battery death, or lost device recovery.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and does not replace the specific emergency procedures outlined in your vehicle's owner's manual. Procedures for digital keys vary significantly between manufacturers and hardware generations. If you are stranded in a remote or dangerous area, contact emergency services immediately.
