# What to do if… you find a fake dating profile using your photos to send sexual messages (USA)

## Short answer
Do not engage with the impersonator. Report the profile in the app and save a small set of evidence so you can escalate if there are threats, blackmail, child sexual content, money loss, or ongoing harassment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not message, threaten, or negotiate with the person running the account.
- Do not pay money or send gift cards, cryptocurrency, or “verification” photos to make it stop.
- Do not post the profile publicly with identifying details; that can spread it further.
- Do not share more personal information with strangers contacting you about the fake profile.
- Do not keep checking the profile repeatedly; capture what you need, report it, then step away.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** If you are in immediate danger, or someone is threatening imminent harm, call **911**.
2. **Save a small amount of evidence, then stop looking.** Take a few screenshots showing:
   - the dating app or site name
   - the profile name, handle, or visible profile link
   - your photos being used
   - the sexual messages or solicitation text
   - any threats, payment demands, or unwanted contact linked to the profile
3. **Keep the evidence private.** Save it somewhere only you can access. Do not download, forward, repost, or ask anyone to send sexual images, especially if any image may show someone under 18.
4. **Report the profile inside the dating app or site.** Use the app’s report option for impersonation, fake profile, harassment, or non-consensual sexual content. Say plainly: “This account is using my photos to send sexual messages as if it is me.”
5. **Limit immediate spread from your own accounts.** Make social profiles private for now, limit who can message you, remove public phone numbers or email addresses from bios, and check whether the stolen photos are still public.
6. **Ask one trusted person for practical backup.** They can help you report the fake profile if they can see it, keep screenshots organised, or sit with you while you make reports. Ask them not to contact the account.
7. **Use the right image-safety tool if intimate or sexual images are involved.**
   - If the image is of you, intimate in nature, you have access to it, and you were over 18 in the image, StopNCII may help reduce re-uploads on participating platforms.
   - If the image or video shows you when you were under 18, Take It Down may help limit spread if you have the image or video on your device.
   - If child sexual exploitation may be involved, or you do not have the original device or file, consider making a CyberTipline report.
8. **Escalate through official reporting channels only when it fits.**
   - If there is a scam, money loss, or a demand for payment, report it to the FTC at ReportFraud.
   - If there are threats, sextortion, blackmail, or broader internet-enabled crime, consider filing an IC3 complaint.
   - If you are being stalked, doxxed, threatened, or repeatedly contacted in person or locally, contact local police and say: “Someone is impersonating me on a dating app using my photos to send sexual messages, and I am receiving unwanted contact or threats.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person behind the account right now.
- You do not need to answer strangers who message you about the fake profile.
- You do not need to decide today whether to pursue legal action, tell work, post publicly, or explain this to everyone you know.
- You do not need to keep searching every dating app tonight.

## Important reassurance
This is violating and frightening, and your reaction makes sense. The priority is to reduce spread, avoid giving the impersonator more attention or material, and use reporting routes that can help. You did not cause this by having photos online.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions, such as legal options, workplace impact, repeated stalking, or platform escalation, may need specialist help from a lawyer, victim-support service, platform safety team, or law enforcement.

## Important note
This guide is general information for the first moments after discovering an impersonating dating profile. It is not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you might harm yourself or cannot stay safe, call or text 988 in the U.S.
