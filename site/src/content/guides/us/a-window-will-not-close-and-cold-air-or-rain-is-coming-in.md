# What to do if… a window will not close and cold air or rain is coming in (USA)

## Short answer
Keep away from any wet electrical equipment, then block the opening from inside with a plastic sheet and towels if you can do so safely. Report the problem promptly to your landlord or property manager if you rent, or contact a repair service if you own the property.

## Do not do these things
- Do not lean out of the window, climb onto a ledge, or attempt an exterior fix in bad weather.
- Do not repeatedly force the sash, frame, latch, or crank. Stop if the window is binding or the glass is under strain.
- Do not touch wet electrical equipment or cross standing water to reach a breaker panel.
- Do not use a generator, grill, charcoal device, camp stove, gas oven, or outdoor fuel-burning equipment indoors to warm the room.
- Do not leave the opening unattended if it is reachable from outside. Treat it as an unsecured access point.
- Do not start permanent repairs or alter the window frame if you rent unless your landlord or property manager agrees.

## What to do now
1. **Move people and hazards away from the window.**  
   Keep children and pets out of the area. Move valuables and portable electrical items away from incoming water if you can do this without touching anything wet or standing in water. Put towels and a container under the leak if it is safe to approach.

2. **Keep clear of electrical danger.**  
   Do not touch wet outlets, cords, appliances, or electrical equipment. Leave the area and call 911 for fire, smoke, or another immediate danger. Turn off electricity only if the breaker panel can be reached safely without crossing wet flooring or approaching wet electrical equipment. Otherwise, do not approach the panel; contact an electrician or emergency help as appropriate.

3. **Try one gentle inside-only check.**  
   Look for visible debris in the track or sill. Remove loose debris that is easy to reach, then gently try the latch or crank once. Stop if the window binds, the frame shifts, or the glass appears strained.

4. **Block the weather from inside.**  
   If it is safe to reach the window, tape a plastic sheet or sturdy plastic bag over the inside of the gap. Place rolled towels along the sill or gap and use a container for drips. Do not lean outside or work near wet electrical equipment.

5. **Reduce access to the room.**  
   Close the room door if possible and move valuables out of sight. For a sliding window, place a removable stop in the track only if one is already available and can be positioned safely.

6. **Report the problem promptly.**  
   If you rent, contact your landlord or property manager and use any maintenance route listed in your lease. State that the window will not close, rain or cold air is entering, and the opening may be unsecured. Follow up in writing and keep a copy.  
   If you own the property, contact a local window repair service. If storm damage may be involved, ask your insurer whether any immediate assistance or documentation is needed.

7. **Record the damage.**  
   Take photos of the gap, the window hardware, and any water entry if it is safe to do so. Note when the problem started, when you reported it, and any temporary supplies you bought.

8. **Use official local routes if a rental problem is not addressed.**  
   Repair and complaint routes vary by state and locality. Use the official USAGov tenant-rights page to find the relevant state agency, and check whether your city or county has a housing or code-enforcement route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the entire window must be replaced.
- You do not need to settle responsibility for the damage immediately.
- You do not need to make legal or insurance decisions before the area is safe and the problem has been reported.
- Full drying, restoration, and permanent repairs can wait until additional water entry and electrical risks are under control.

## Important reassurance
A temporary inside barrier, a safer room, and a prompt repair report are useful first steps. You do not need to solve the entire repair problem at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and damage. Permanent repairs, rental remedies, and insurance decisions may need help from a qualified repair professional, your insurer, or an appropriate local housing service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. For fire, smoke, or another immediate danger, leave the area and call 911.
