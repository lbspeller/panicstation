# What to do if… you hear suspicious movement or noise outside your home at night (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside, lock doors and accessible windows, and move away from entry points. If you can see or hear someone on your property and you feel unsafe, or you think someone is trying to get in, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to investigate, even for a quick look.
- Don’t open the door to challenge, question, or scare anyone off.
- Don’t stand in a lit window or make yourself visible from outside.
- Don’t make threats or try to clear the yard, driveway, or alley yourself.
- Don’t delay calling 911 by posting online or trying to get opinions first.
- Don’t assume you are wasting 911’s time if you genuinely feel unsafe.

## What to do now
1. Lock exterior doors and accessible windows. If you have an alarm, set it to stay or home mode.
2. Move people and pets into a safer room, preferably upstairs or inside the home, away from exterior doors and ground-floor windows. Keep your phone with you.
3. Stay out of view. Close curtains or blinds only if you can do it without standing in a window.
4. Turn on indoor lights. If you can do it without going near windows or doors, turn on outside lights, floodlights, or smart lights.
5. Check doorbell or security cameras from your phone if you have them. Note the location, number of people, clothing, direction of travel, and any vehicle details.
6. If your car is outside and you can lock it quietly from where you are, do that. Skip it if it would flash lights, honk, draw attention, or require you to move toward an exterior door or window.
7. Call 911 if you think someone is trying to enter, you can see or hear someone on your property and feel unsafe, you see suspicious activity that appears ongoing, or you believe a crime may be in progress. Give your address first, then say that you are safely inside and what you can see or hear.
8. If speaking would put you at risk, give your address first if you safely can, keep the connection if possible, and follow any call-taker instructions. In some areas, Text-to-911 is available; if you cannot safely speak and you know or believe your area supports it, text 911 with your address first, then what is happening. If the text does not go through or you do not get a reply, call 911.
9. If someone tries to enter, move to the safest room, lock the room door if possible, put a solid object between you and the door if it helps without trapping you, and stay on the line with 911 until the call-taker tells you what to do.

## What can wait
- You do not need to go outside tonight to confirm what happened.
- You do not need to decide now about cameras, alarms, locks, fences, or security upgrades.
- You do not need to save, edit, or share video clips until you are safe and calm.
- You do not need to contact neighbors before calling 911 if you feel threatened.
- You do not need to perfectly decide whether it counts as an emergency before calling if you may be in danger.

## Important reassurance
Unexpected movement outside can cause a strong adrenaline response, even when you are physically inside. Staying indoors, creating distance, and calling for help when you feel unsafe are reasonable protective steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes to hour. Later decisions, such as police reports, insurance issues, landlord issues, or security changes, may need specialist or local help.

## Important note
This is general safety information for the USA. It is not legal advice, professional security advice, emergency-service advice, or a substitute for instructions from a 911 call-taker or local law enforcement.
