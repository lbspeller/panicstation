# What to do if… you realise your passport was left in an airport security tray after you walk away (USA)

## Short answer
Return to the specific TSA checkpoint immediately to speak with a supervisor; if it is not recovered within minutes, you must file a TSA Lost and Found report and use the TSA ConfirmID process for domestic travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to force your way back through the one-way security exit—locate a staff member or use a designated "re-entry" lane.
- Do not assume the airline gate agent can retrieve it for you—TSA handles checkpoint items separately from the airline.
- Do not delay reporting the loss to TSA; unclaimed identification is often destroyed after 30 days.
- Do not report the passport as "Lost or Stolen" to the State Department until you are certain it cannot be recovered, as this action is irreversible and cancels the document.

## What to do now
1. **Return to the TSA checkpoint entry immediately.** Identify the exact lane number you used.
2. **Inform the document checker or a visible TSA agent that you left your passport in a bin.** Use the phrase: "I have a missing high-value ID left in a screening tray."
3. **Request a TSA Checkpoint Supervisor.** Explain exactly which bin or tray you used and at what time.
   - Passports are often pulled from bins by "lead" officers and placed in a secure locker or supervisor’s station at the checkpoint.
4. **Visit the Airport’s TSA Lost and Found office if the checkpoint does not have it.** Note that in many airports, passports are transferred to U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) or airport police within 24 to 48 hours.
5. **Complete the TSA Lost and Found online form immediately.** Provide your flight number, the specific checkpoint location, and a description of any distinctive passport cover.
6. **Use TSA ConfirmID if you are flying domestically and have no other ID.**
   - Access the TSA ConfirmID portal on your mobile device.
   - Pay the mandatory $45 identity verification fee.
   - Present the digital receipt to the TSA officer to begin the manual identity verification process.
7. **Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate if you are at an international airport and cannot find the document.** You will likely need to apply for an emergency, limited-validity passport to continue your travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to cancel your flight immediately; TSA may allow domestic boarding via identity verification (ConfirmID).
- You do not need to file a police report unless you suspect the passport was intentionally stolen from the tray by another passenger.
- You do not need to apply for a full-price replacement passport while the airport is still within the 24-hour primary search window.

## Important reassurance
Misplacing a passport during the high-pressure screening process is a frequent occurrence. TSA officers have established protocols for securing "high-value" items like identification. If it was left in a tray, it was likely intercepted by staff and is currently in a secure holding area awaiting a claim.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate recovery steps at the airport and short-term domestic travel workarounds. It does not cover the long-term application process for a permanent replacement passport.

## Important note
TSA and airport policies regarding lost property vary by location. TSA generally holds items for 30 days before disposal or destruction. Verification of identity via TSA ConfirmID is not guaranteed and requires successful cross-referencing of government databases.
