# What to do if… your airport, ferry, or rail transfer does not show up and you are stuck with time pressure (USA)

## Short answer
Move to an authorized, staffed help point and initiate a backup travel plan immediately if you are within 60 minutes of a boarding or check-in deadline.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept rides from "curbside solicitors" or individuals offering transport outside of marked Ground Transportation zones.
- Do not wait in isolated areas or outside the terminal/station while waiting for a late driver.
- Do not keep waiting past your "last safe departure" time in hopes that the original provider will eventually arrive.
- Do not cancel your secondary travel (flight/train/ferry) until you have confirmed with the operator that you cannot be rebooked or accommodated.
- Do not delete call logs, text messages from dispatchers, or app notifications showing the driver's location or status.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to the nearest official Ground Transportation or Information desk.** **Stay in a well-lit, staffed public area while you resolve the issue.**
2. **Verify if your transfer was a "Guaranteed Connection" or a separate booking.** **Check your initial confirmation email to see if the transfer was bundled with your flight or rail ticket, as this determines if the main carrier is responsible for rebooking you.**
3. **Contact the transfer provider once and request a specific "Estimated Time of Arrival" (ETA).** **State clearly that you have a hard departure deadline and ask for the dispatch number of the specific vehicle assigned to you.**
4. **Set a "hard pivot" time based on the current airport or station congestion.** **Factor in at least 20 minutes for security screening (TSA) or check-in queues, especially if local construction or new verification protocols like TSA Confirm.ID are in effect.**
5. **Execute a backup route via authorized on-site transportation services.** **Go directly to the signed "Taxi Stand" or "Rideshare Pickup" zone and use only official vehicles or apps that verify driver identity and license plates before you enter.**
6. **Notify your onward carrier of the delay before the scheduled departure time.** **Ask the airline, Amtrak, or ferry agent to "protect" your booking by noting the ground transport failure in your passenger record.**
7. **Collect timestamped evidence of the service failure.** **Take a photo of the empty pickup area with a visible clock or take a screenshot of your phone’s call log showing unanswered attempts to reach the provider.**
8. **Request a refund in writing from the original merchant before initiating any payment disputes.** **Document the merchant’s response or lack of response, as this is required if you later need to contact your card issuer regarding services not rendered.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal Better Business Bureau (BBB) or Department of Transportation (DOT) complaint while you are still traveling.
- You do not need to determine the exact refund amount for your missed connection until you have reached your final destination.
- You do not need to resolve the "why" of the no-show right now; focus only on the "how" of getting to your next gate.

## Important reassurance
Feeling a surge of adrenaline or panic is a standard physiological response to time-sensitive travel disruptions. This reaction is helpful for making quick decisions, but it can be managed by focusing on one physical step at a time, such as moving to a help desk or opening a rideshare app.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for domestic US travel transfers. It does not cover international transit visas, travel insurance claim processing, or long-term legal recovery for missed vacations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional travel advice. Specific rights and refund eligibility depend on the individual terms and conditions of your ticket and the service provider’s "Contract of Carriage." If you feel unsafe or are being harassed by unauthorized solicitors, contact airport security or local law enforcement immediately.
