# What to do if… you are told life insurance paperwork is urgent but you cannot find the policy (USA)
## Short answer
Verify the request through a contact route you know is genuine. The missing paper policy does not prevent you from contacting the insurer, asking what is required, or starting a policy search.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send Social Security numbers, banking logins, one-time passcodes, or document scans to someone who contacted you unexpectedly.
- Don’t pay an unexpected fee to release life insurance money or speed up a claim.
- Don’t assume you must find the original paper policy before contacting an insurer.
- Don’t rely only on a callback number, link, or email address in a message that feels rushed or unexpected.
- Don’t discard mail, emails, or employer benefit records. They may contain the insurer or plan name.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the request in writing.** Ask for the insurer or plan name, the form or document requested, the stated deadline, the consequence of missing it, the claim or reference number, and the submission route.
2. **Verify the request independently.** Use the insurer’s official website, a recent statement, or another contact route you know is genuine. Do not send money or sensitive documents until the request has been verified.
3. **Ask what can be started with the information you have.** Have the deceased person’s full legal name, date of birth, last address, and date of death ready. Ask what documents are required and whether the stated deadline applies to your situation.
4. **Do a limited first search for the insurer or plan name.** Check bank or credit card records for premium payments, search emails for terms such as `policy`, `life insurance`, `premium`, `beneficiary`, and `group life`, and look through insurance or employer-benefit records.
5. **Check possible group coverage.** Contact the deceased person’s current or former employer benefits office, and any relevant union benefits office, to ask whether there was group life insurance and which insurer or administrator handled it.
6. **Use the NAIC Life Insurance Policy Locator.** The locator is a free search tool for policies and annuity contracts belonging to someone who has died. It asks for information from the death certificate, including the deceased person’s Social Security number or ITIN, legal name, date of birth, and date of death.
7. **Order a certified death-certificate copy for the claim.** For a death in the United States, contact the vital records office of the state where the death occurred. Ask the insurer how many certified copies it needs.
8. **Check state unclaimed-property searches.** Insurance benefits may appear in state unclaimed-property systems. MissingMoney.com searches participating states and points users to official state claim routes.
9. **Use your state insurance department if you are stuck.** The NAIC directory can help you find the correct state department contact and complaint route.
10. **Keep a simple record.** Note the date, contact route, name of the person you spoke with, what was requested, and what you sent. Keep copies of submitted documents.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to hire an attorney.
- You don’t need to resolve family disagreements before taking the first verification and search steps.
- You can usually begin asking about coverage before every estate-administration task is finished. Ask the insurer what authority or documents it needs from you.

## Important reassurance
Missing paperwork does not mean there is no next step. A time-sensitive request may be genuine or may be a pressure tactic, so it is reasonable to pause, verify the contact route, and ask for written details.

## Scope note
These are first steps to verify urgency, avoid scams, and start locating coverage or asking about a claim. Later decisions about disputes, denials, payment timing, or legal action may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or financial advice. Claim requirements and timelines can vary by policy and state. Before sending sensitive documents or money, verify the request through an official contact route.
