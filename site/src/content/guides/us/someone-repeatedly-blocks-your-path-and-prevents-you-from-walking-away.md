# What to do if… someone repeatedly blocks your path and prevents you from walking away (USA)

## Short answer
Move now toward a staffed public place and ask a specific person to stay with you. If you believe you are in immediate danger or need emergency assistance, call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep debating or explaining yourself.
- Don’t go somewhere isolated to talk, such as behind a building, into a car, or down a side street.
- Don’t try to push past them if another route toward people or staff is available.
- Don’t focus on recording or texting if it stops you from moving toward help.
- Don’t follow them if they move away.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a staffed public place.** Go inside a store, hotel lobby, restaurant, staffed transit counter, or another visible place with people. If you are near a parking lot, move toward the building rather than toward a vehicle or a quieter area.
2. **Use one clear sentence if it feels safe to speak.** Say loudly enough for nearby people to hear: “Stop. I’m leaving.”
3. **Ask a specific person for help.** Say to an employee, security guard, cashier, or nearby person: “This person is stopping me from leaving. Please stay with me and call 911.”
4. **Call 911 if you believe you are in immediate danger or need emergency assistance.** Tell the call-taker your location, what is happening, and a description of the person. Answer the call-taker’s questions and follow their instructions.
5. **If your route is still blocked, stay visible.** Remain near staff or other people rather than moving to a quieter place to find another way around.
6. **If you are with someone, stay together.** One person can speak to staff while the other calls 911.
7. **Once you are safer, make brief notes if it feels safe to do so.** Note the time, location, what happened, any threats, and any witnesses.
8. **If this has happened before, treat the repeated behavior seriously.** It may be part of a pattern. A victim service provider can help you think through safety planning and local options.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to confront the person, record them, or make a detailed report.
- You do not need to write a full timeline while you are still trying to reach safety.
- You can consider formal reports, legal options, or longer-term safety planning later with specialist help.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to feel alarmed when someone repeatedly blocks your path. You do not owe them a conversation or an explanation. Reaching other people and asking for help is enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours. If the person is known to you or the behavior repeats, later decisions may need specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or need emergency assistance, call **911**.
