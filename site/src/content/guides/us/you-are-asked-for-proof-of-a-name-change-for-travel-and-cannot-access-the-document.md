# What to do if… you are asked for proof of a name change for travel and cannot access the document (USA)

## Short answer
Pause and copy the exact name from the travel ID you plan to present. Contact the airline or booking agent promptly and ask what correction, reissue, or rebooking options are available if you cannot show the requested name-change document.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a screenshot, scan, or photocopy will be accepted if an original or certified copy is requested.
- Do not assume a booking and ID mismatch will be accepted without checking.
- Do not pay an unverified website that claims to provide an instant certified document.
- Do not send scans or photos of your ID or supporting documents through an unverified contact or upload link.
- Do not cancel or pay for a new booking before asking the airline or booking agent to explain the available options.

## What to do now
1. **Copy the name as it appears on the travel ID you plan to present.**  
   For international travel, this will generally be your passport. Ask the airline or booking agent how the name should appear on the reservation.

2. **Contact the airline or booking agent.**  
   Say: “I cannot access my name-change document. The name on the travel ID I plan to present is [name]. What documentation do you require, and can you correct, reissue, or rebook the reservation?”  
   Ask the agent to state the available options and any deadline clearly.

3. **Ask whether an electronic copy can be reviewed.**  
   Do not assume that a scan or photograph will be accepted. If the airline says it can review an electronic copy, use only the secure route it gives you.

4. **Check whether the document can be retrieved safely.**  
   Look for an accessible copy in your records, or ask a trusted person whether they can retrieve the document. Do not send sensitive documents through an unverified contact or upload link.

5. **If you are already at the airport, ask for a supervisor or the airline’s service desk.**  
   Show the travel ID you plan to use and ask for the available compliant option. Keep the request simple: “What can be done with this booking using the ID I have with me?”

6. **If you are a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident and your travel documents show different names, explain the situation if CBP asks.**  
   Ask what proof is needed. Depending on the situation, a marriage certificate, divorce decree, or court document showing the legal name change may be relevant.

7. **Start the correct replacement route when the immediate travel conversation is stable.**  
   - For a marriage certificate issued in the United States, contact the vital records office in the state where the marriage took place.
   - For a divorce decree, contact the clerk of the county or city where the divorce took place.
   - For a divorce certificate, contact the vital records office in the state where the divorce took place to ask whether that state issues one.
   - For a court order changing your name, contact the court that issued the order and ask how to request a certified copy.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update every identity record while you are handling the immediate travel problem.
- You do not need to decide how to manage every future booking today.
- Broader document updates can wait unless the airline or relevant official says a specific update is needed for this trip.

## Important reassurance
This is a stressful problem, but you only need to deal with the next clear step. Ask the airline or booking agent to explain the available options for the ID and documents you have with you.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize an urgent travel-document mismatch. Later decisions about certified replacements, passport updates, or a complex name history may need help from the relevant issuing office or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Airline policies and document requirements vary. Ask the airline, booking agent, or relevant official to confirm what is required for your specific trip.
