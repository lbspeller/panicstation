# What to do if… you realise your travel profile saved the wrong passport number and you have already checked in (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the airline through its official app, website, or reservation line, then use a staffed airport counter to make sure the reservation for this flight matches the passport you are carrying.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume changing your saved traveller profile also changes today’s checked-in reservation.
- Don’t cancel and rebook in a panic unless the airline tells you that is the correct fix.
- Don’t keep trying the same failed app edit over and over if the passport field is locked.
- Don’t rely only on a kiosk if the app will not update the travel-document details.
- Don’t wait until boarding if you can get to airline support or a staffed counter earlier.

## What to do now
1. **Work out what looks wrong.**  
   Open the airline trip details and check the passenger or travel-document section. Note whether the problem is the passport number, passport country, expiry date, nationality, full name, date of birth, gender, or a saved number such as a Known Traveler Number or redress number.

2. **Try the airline’s “Manage reservation” edit once.**  
   If the airline lets you update the passport or travel-document details after check-in, save the change and keep any confirmation screen. If it will not save, stop using the same loop and move to airline support.

3. **Contact the airline and ask for the reservation record to be corrected.**  
   Use contact details from the airline’s website, app, or booking confirmation. You can say: “I already checked in, but the travel-document details in my reservation have the wrong passport number. I need the reservation updated to match the passport I am carrying, and I may need a new boarding pass.”

4. **Go to a staffed airline counter before security or as soon as you can at the airport.**  
   Show the passport you will travel on. Ask the agent to verify the travel-document details in the reservation, not just the saved profile, and to reprint or reissue the boarding pass if their system requires it.

5. **If this is international travel, check any entry permission tied to the passport.**  
   If you need ESTA, a visa, or another travel authorisation, check the official destination-government process to confirm whether it matches the passport you are using. An airline record correction may not fix a separate travel authorisation tied to a different passport.

6. **Do not treat a Known Traveler Number or redress number as the passport fix.**  
   Secure Flight details usually include full name, date of birth, gender, and a redress number if applicable. A Known Traveler Number or redress number can matter for screening, but it does not replace correcting the wrong passport number.

## What can wait
- You do not need to perfect your saved travel profile right now if today’s reservation can be corrected.
- You do not need to troubleshoot every app setting if the airline can handle the correction at support or at a staffed counter.
- You do not need to decide today how you will store passport details for future trips.
- Complaints, refunds, and account clean-up can wait until after the travel-day document issue is stable.

## Important reassurance
This kind of mistake can happen with autofill, an old passport, or one wrong digit. Airlines are used to checking travel documents for international flights, and a staffed agent is usually the right person to confirm what can be corrected for this booking.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise a travel-day document problem. Airline systems, check-in deadlines, and destination entry rules vary, so later decisions may need help from the airline or the relevant government entry authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel-agent, or professional advice. Follow instructions from the airline and the official government authority for the country you are travelling to.
