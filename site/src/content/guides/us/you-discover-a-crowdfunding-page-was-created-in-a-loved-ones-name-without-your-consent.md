# What to do if… you discover a crowdfunding page was created in a loved one’s name without your consent (USA)

## Short answer
Document the page immediately with screenshots and report it directly to the platform's trust and safety team to freeze payouts and initiate a takedown.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send personal identity documents or bank details to anyone claiming to be the fundraiser organizer or an "investigator" via email.
- Don't post confrontational comments on the live fundraiser page, as this often increases the page's algorithm visibility and attracts more donors.
- Don't share the live URL on social media to "warn others," as people may accidentally click and donate; use screenshots instead.
- Don't click links in messages that claim to be from the platform; always navigate to the official website by typing the address directly into your browser.
- Don't assume the fundraiser is legitimate just because it uses accurate family details or photos taken from social media.

## What to do now
1. **Capture comprehensive evidence of the fundraiser immediately.** Take high-resolution screenshots of the fundraiser title, the URL (web address), the organizer’s profile, the "story" section, all images used, and any updates or comments.
   - Note the specific date and time you discovered the page.
   - Save these files in a dedicated folder for law enforcement or platform review.
2. **Report the fundraiser through the platform’s internal reporting tool.** Use the "Report Fundraiser" or "Report Fraud" button located on the campaign page itself and select the option for "Impersonation" or "Unauthorized Fundraiser."
   - State clearly: "This fundraiser uses my loved one's identity without consent and is not authorized by the family or estate."
   - Request an immediate freeze on all payouts to the organizer.
3. **Contact the platform’s Trust and Safety team via their official support channels.** Send a direct message or email (such as `accountsecurity@gofundme.com` for that specific platform) to request a formal investigation and a case number.
   - If you are the executor of the estate or the next of kin, state your legal relationship to the deceased person.
   - Mention that you are prepared to provide official documentation, such as a death certificate, through their secure verification portal if required.
4. **Notify known contacts to prevent further financial loss.** Message close friends, family members, and community groups to alert them that the specific fundraiser is unauthorized and should not be supported.
   - Advise anyone who has already donated to contact their bank or the platform immediately to request a refund.
   - Provide the platform's official support link rather than the scammer's fundraiser link.
5. **File a report with federal consumer protection and cybercrime agencies.** Submit a report to the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3.gov) and the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov to document the incident for law enforcement.
   - If the identity of a deceased person is being used to open financial accounts, file a report at IdentityTheft.gov under the section for reporting on behalf of someone else.
6. **Protect the deceased person's credit profile and government records.** Contact one of the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion) to request a "deceased alert" be placed on the loved one's credit report.
   - Notify the Social Security Administration (SSA) if you suspect the deceased person's Social Security number is being misused.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person behind the page today; the platform and law enforcement handle the investigation.
- You do not need to respond to messages from the organizer or engage in public debates about the fundraiser's validity.
- You do not need to hire a lawyer immediately, as the platform's terms of service usually allow for removal based on unauthorized use of identity.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of violation or urgency when a loved one's name is used for profit. These platforms have dedicated teams trained to handle impersonation and fraudulent memorial funds. Taking these steps focuses your energy on stopping the flow of money and protecting your family's privacy.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for discovering an unauthorized fundraiser. It does not cover long-term legal action, criminal prosecution of the organizer, or international jurisdictional disputes if the platform is based outside the USA.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not constitute legal advice. If you feel physically threatened by the organizer or are being harassed, contact local law enforcement immediately. Always verify that you are communicating with official platform representatives by using their verified ".com" or ".gov" websites.
