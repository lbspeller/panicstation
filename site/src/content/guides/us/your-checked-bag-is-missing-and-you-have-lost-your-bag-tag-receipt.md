# What to do if… your checked bag is missing and you have lost your bag tag receipt (USA)

## Short answer
File a mishandled baggage report with the airline as soon as possible, even without the bag tag receipt. Ask the airline to look up the bag-tag details from your booking and check-in records, and get a file or reference number before you leave the case.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume the missing tag receipt blocks you from reporting the bag.
- Don’t wait until later if the bag is missing now.
- Don’t guess the bag-tag number; say you do not have it.
- Don’t open multiple separate reports for the same bag unless the airline tells you to.
- Don’t buy major replacements straight away; buy essentials only and keep itemised receipts.
- Don’t hand over your only paper or screenshot record unless you keep a copy.

## What to do now
1. **File the mishandled baggage report now and get the file number.**  
   At the airport, go to the airline baggage desk or baggage service office in the baggage-claim area. If you have already left, use the airline’s official delayed or missing baggage form.

2. **Tell the airline clearly that the bag tag receipt is missing.**  
   Give your full name, flight number, date of travel, route, confirmation code, ticket number if you have it, and any boarding pass or app record. Ask them to search their check-in and baggage records for the missing bag-tag number.

3. **Check for any digital trace of the bag tag.**  
   Look in the airline app, your email, wallet app, kiosk receipt, text messages, and screenshots. Search terms such as “bag tag,” “baggage receipt,” “checked bag,” “receipt,” and the airline name.

4. **Give a detailed bag description that does not rely on the tag.**  
   Include the brand, size, colour, hard or soft shell, wheels, straps, stickers, scuffs, locks, ribbons, and a photo if you have one. Add a few distinctive contents only if you can describe them calmly and honestly.

5. **Set a delivery plan you can actually receive.**  
   Give a reliable address, such as a hotel front desk, trusted contact, workplace reception, or home address. Add a reachable phone number and ask whether ID or a signature is needed.

6. **Save proof as you go.**  
   Keep the file number, screenshots of the report, airline messages, delivery updates, boarding passes, receipts, and the names or locations of airline staff you spoke with.

7. **If you need essentials, keep purchases reasonable and keep receipts.**  
   DOT guidance says airlines must compensate passengers for reasonable, verifiable, actual incidental expenses while bags are delayed, subject to liability limits. Airlines are not allowed to set an arbitrary daily cap for those interim expenses.

8. **If you paid a checked-bag fee, ask about the refund process after the report is filed.**  
   DOT rules say a checked-bag fee refund is due if the bag is declared lost or is significantly delayed, and a mishandled baggage report must be on file. A bag is significantly delayed if it is not delivered within 12 hours after arrival for a domestic flight; for an international flight between a U.S. airport and a foreign airport, the threshold is 15 hours if the flight was 12 hours or less, or 30 hours if the flight was more than 12 hours. The clock generally starts when you are given the opportunity to deplane at your final destination and ends when you pick up the bag or it is delivered to the location you and the airline agreed on. Once the delay crosses those thresholds, ask the airline how the refund will be issued.

## What can wait
- You do not need a perfect full inventory immediately; start with the report, bag description, contact details, and file number.
- You do not need to decide now about formal complaints, public posts, third-party claim services, or legal help.
- You do not need to replace everything in the bag today; essentials and receipts are enough for the first stage.
- You can add more details later if the airline asks for an inventory or proof of contents.

## Important reassurance
A missing bag tag receipt is a problem, but it is not the end of the claim. The most useful thing right now is a filed airline report, a reference number, clear travel details, and a description that helps the airline trace the bag.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about reimbursement, formal complaints, insurance, or disputed claims may need help from the airline, your travel insurer, DOT resources, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, travel-agent, therapeutic, medical, or other professional advice. For domestic U.S. flights, DOT currently allows airlines to limit baggage liability to $4,700 per passenger. For most international flights covered by the Montreal Convention, DOT lists the current baggage liability limit as 1,519 Special Drawing Rights per passenger. Actual payment may depend on proof, depreciation, exclusions, the itinerary, and the airline’s process.
