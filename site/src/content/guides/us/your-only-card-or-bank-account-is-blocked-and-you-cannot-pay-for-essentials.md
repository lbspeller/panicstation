# What to do if… your only card or bank account is blocked and you cannot pay for essentials (USA)

## Short answer
Contact your bank or card issuer through a trusted route and ask for the fastest available way to regain access to funds for essentials. If you cannot cover food, medication, or another basic need today, call 211 for local help while the institution reviews the block.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep repeating the same declined transaction while you are trying to find out what happened.
- Do not use a phone number or link from an unexpected text, email, call, or pop-up claiming to be from your bank.
- Do not share a one-time verification code or PIN with someone who contacts you.
- Do not move money to a supposed safe account because someone tells you to do so.
- Do not take out a high-cost loan in panic before checking whether a safer temporary option is available.
- Do not ignore an urgent bill. Contact the provider and ask what temporary options are available.

## What to do now
1. **Use a trusted contact route.**  
   Open your banking app, use a number on a statement, or call the number on the back of your card. Say:  
   **“My only card or account is blocked and I cannot pay for essentials. What is the fastest available way to regain access to my funds?”**

2. **Ask what is blocked.**  
   Ask whether the issue is:
   - a card-payment block
   - an ATM block
   - an online-access issue
   - a wider account restriction
   - a merchant hold reducing your available balance

3. **Check for a simple action in the app or website.**  
   Look for a fraud alert asking you to confirm a transaction, a card lock setting, an identity-verification request, or a message explaining the restriction. Only use the bank’s own app or a website address you already trust.

4. **Ask what access options are available now.**  
   Ask whether the institution can lift the block after verification or offer another way to access funds. Depending on the institution and the reason for the block, options may include a branch withdrawal with ID, a replacement card, or another temporary arrangement.

5. **Use a branch if the institution tells you it may help.**  
   Bring government-issued photo ID and your card or account details if available. Ask whether a teller withdrawal or replacement card is possible while the restriction is reviewed.

6. **Report unfamiliar activity immediately.**  
   If you see a transaction you did not make or allow, tell the bank or credit union now. Do not wait for the block to clear before reporting suspected unauthorized activity.

7. **Contact essential-service providers before a missed payment.**  
   For rent, utilities, or phone service, say:  
   **“My only payment method is temporarily blocked and I am working with my bank. What temporary options are available while this is being resolved?”**  
   Ask what the provider needs from you and what may happen if payment is delayed.

8. **Use local help for essentials you need today.**  
   Call **211** if you cannot cover food, medication, utilities, or another basic need. For emergency food routes, use the USAGov emergency food assistance page.

9. **Escalate if you cannot get a clear response.**  
   Ask the bank or card issuer to record your complaint and give you a case or reference number if available. If the issue is not being handled, submit a complaint to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

## What can wait
- Switching banks or opening new accounts.
- Working out every detail behind the restriction before you have essentials covered.
- Making long-term financial decisions while you are under pressure.
- Closing cards or changing payment arrangements unless the bank tells you an immediate fraud-safety step is needed.

## Important reassurance
A blocked card or account is stressful because it can stop basic purchases without warning. Contacting the institution through a trusted route is the clearest next step, and asking what is blocked can help you focus on the fastest available option. Some restrictions take longer to review, so it is reasonable to use local support for essentials in the meantime.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you regain access and keep essentials stable. Later decisions can wait until the immediate pressure is lower and may need help from the bank, a service provider, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Available options depend on the institution, the type of card or account, and the reason for the restriction. Report suspected unauthorized activity promptly and use local emergency support if you cannot meet basic needs today.
