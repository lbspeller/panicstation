# What to do if… a smoke alarm goes off but you cannot find any smoke or heat (USA)
## Short answer
Treat the alarm as real. Do not delay leaving to investigate: if you cannot immediately identify a harmless cooking-smoke or steam trigger from a safe position, or if there is smoke, heat, a burning smell, another alarm, or any uncertainty, get everyone outside and call 911 from outside.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remove a battery, disconnect a hardwired alarm, cover the alarm, or tape over it to stop the noise.
- Do not search room by room if the cause is not immediately obvious from a safe position.
- Do not stay inside if you smell burning, see haze, notice heat, hear another alarm, or feel unsure.
- Do not touch outlets, cords, appliances, or electrical panels that look damaged or are making unusual sounds.
- Do not use a hush button while there may still be a fire.
- Do not use switches, flames, or anything that could spark if you smell gas.

## What to do now
1. **Pause at the earliest safe point.** If you notice smoke, haze, heat, a burning smell, unusual electrical sounds, another alarm, or anything uncertain, get everyone outside and call 911 from outside. Do not search room by room.
2. **Leave immediately if you notice a gas odor at any point.** Do not investigate, use switches, or use flames. Call 911 from outside.
3. **Only check for a harmless trigger if the alarm has stopped and there are no danger signs.** From a safe position, look for an immediately obvious nearby source such as cooking smoke, burnt food, or shower steam. If the cause is not immediately obvious, leave and call 911 from outside.
4. **Check what kind of alarm sounded only if it is safe to do so.** If it is a combination smoke and carbon-monoxide alarm, or you are not sure what the sound pattern means, leave and call 911 from outside.
5. **Use the hush button only for a known nuisance source.** If you have safely confirmed that cooking smoke or steam caused the alarm, follow the alarm’s instructions and press its hush or silence button. Do not disable the alarm.
6. **Clear a known nuisance source safely.** Open a door or window, or use ventilation only when there is no gas odor, suspected electrical problem, or other reason to avoid switches.
7. **After the immediate situation is calm, follow the manufacturer’s cleaning instructions.** Dust, debris, and grime can cause nuisance alarms. Use only a cleaning method approved for your alarm model and avoid liquids unless the manufacturer says otherwise.
8. **Check the alarm manual before changing batteries or resetting the unit.** Some alarms have replaceable batteries and some have sealed batteries. Replace a damaged, expired, repeatedly malfunctioning, or uncleanable alarm according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
9. **If you rent your home, report repeated alarms or a hardwired-system problem to building management or your landlord.** If the alarm sounds again before the issue is resolved, leave and call 911 from outside.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove the exact cause while you are unsure whether there is danger.
- You do not need to dismantle appliances, remove an alarm, or troubleshoot wiring now.
- You do not need to choose a replacement alarm or plan electrical work in the moment.
- Cleaning, battery checks, and building-management follow-up can wait until the immediate safety check is complete.

## Important reassurance
Cooking smoke, steam, dust, debris, and grime can cause nuisance alarms. Taking the alarm seriously first, then dealing with a confirmed harmless trigger without disabling the alarm, is a sensible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. Repeated nuisance alarms, damaged alarms, and hardwired or interconnected-system problems may need manufacturer support, building management, or a qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional fire-safety, electrical, legal, or other specialist advice. If you suspect a fire, a hidden smoldering source, a gas leak, or an electrical fault, leave and call 911 from outside.
