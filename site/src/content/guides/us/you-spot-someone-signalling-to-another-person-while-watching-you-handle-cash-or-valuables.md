# What to do if… you spot someone signalling to another person while watching you handle cash or valuables (USA)

## Short answer
Stop handling the items immediately, secure them in a zipped bag or pocket, and move to a staffed, well-lit "anchor point" such as a security desk, bank lobby, or store counter.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep counting money or displaying the valuables to "finish the transaction" quickly.
- Don’t confront the individuals or acknowledge that you have seen their signals.
- Don’t move into isolated areas like empty aisles, restrooms, or stairwells to get away.
- Don’t pull out your phone to film them if it slows down your movement to a safer area.
- Don’t walk directly to your car if you believe you are being followed.
- Don’t accept unsolicited help from anyone who approaches you immediately after the signaling.

## What to do now
1. **Secure all items in your hands immediately.** Put cash away, close your wallet or purse, and ensure bags are zipped and held in front of your body.
   - If at an ATM, cancel the transaction if possible and take your card.
2. **Scan the area for the second person.** Look for who the first person is signaling to (via nodding, whistling, or hand gestures) so you do not accidentally move toward the accomplice.
3. **Move to a staffed anchor point right away.** Walk toward a cashier, a bank teller, a security guard, or a customer service desk. 
4. **Request assistance from staff using a clear statement.** Tell an employee: "I am concerned because those two people are signaling each other while watching me. Can I wait here with you, and can you call security?"
5. **Maintain a physical buffer and keep your back to a wall.** Stand where you can see the exits and the individuals who were watching you, ensuring no one can approach you from behind.
6. **Call for help based on the level of threat.**
   - Call **911** if you are being followed, blocked from leaving, or if you believe a robbery is about to occur.
   - Call **311** (or your local police non-emergency number) if the individuals have left but you want to report the suspicious activity and request a patrol.
7. **Wait for a safe escort or a transition in environment.** If you are in a store, ask security to walk you to your vehicle or wait for a large group of people to leave at the same time you do.
8. **Lock your vehicle doors immediately upon entry.** Do not wait to put on your seatbelt or organize your belongings; lock the doors first, then start the engine and leave the area.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confirm their intentions or prove they were working together before seeking help.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report in this exact moment.
- You do not need to check if you still have every single item until you are behind a locked door.
- You do not need to warn other shoppers or people in the area yourself.

## Important reassurance
Coordinated signaling is a known tactic used by distraction thieves and pickpockets to identify targets. Recognizing these cues early and moving to a staffed area is a standard safety protocol that significantly reduces the likelihood of being targeted. Your reaction is a practical response to a potential hazard.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes following the observation of suspicious coordinated behavior. It does not replace professional law enforcement advice or long-term security planning.

## Important note
This information is for general safety awareness and does not guarantee a specific outcome. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, prioritize your physical safety and contact 911 immediately.
