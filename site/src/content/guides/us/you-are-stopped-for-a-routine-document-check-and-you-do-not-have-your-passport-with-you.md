# What to do if… you are stopped for a routine document check and you do not have your passport with you (USA)

## Short answer
Stay cooperative and explain clearly that your passport is at your accommodation; offer any alternative photo ID and ask for the procedure to retrieve the original or verify your identity at the nearest station.

## Do not do these things
- Do not run, argue aggressively, or physically resist the officers.
- Do not offer money, "tips," or gifts to resolve the situation.
- Do not sign any documents that you cannot read or do not fully understand.
- Do not hand over your unlocked phone for inspection unless you are certain of the legal requirement.
- Do not attempt to "test" the officer's knowledge of U.S. law, as you are subject to the local laws of the host country.

## What to do now
1. **State clearly and calmly that your passport is at your lodging.** Use simple phrases like "My passport is at my hotel" or "It is in my room safe."
2. **Provide any alternative identification you are carrying.** This includes a U.S. driver’s license, state ID, or a high-quality photo or scan of your passport's bio-data page on your phone or in print.
   - Note that digital copies are rarely legal substitutes but may assist in verifying your status while you retrieve the original.
3. **Ask for the specific procedure to satisfy the identity check.** 
   - Ask if you can have the passport brought to your current location by a companion.
   - Ask if you are permitted to return to your hotel under escort to produce the document.
   - Ask if you must report to a specific police station within a set timeframe (e.g., 24 hours).
4. **Request an interpreter immediately if there is a language barrier.** Do not guess at what is being asked or what you are agreeing to; wait until communication is clear.
5. **Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate if you are detained or taken to a station.** 
   - Ask the local authorities to notify the U.S. embassy under the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations.
   - For 24/7 emergency assistance from the Department of State, call +1 202-501-4444 (from abroad) or 1-888-407-4747 (from the U.S. or Canada).
6. **Notify your hotel or travel host if you are being moved.** Ask the authorities for permission to make a call to your accommodation so they are aware you have been stopped and may need help retrieving your belongings.
7. **Document the details of the stop as soon as possible.** Write down the names or badge numbers of the officers, the time and location of the stop, and the name of the police station if you are relocated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal complaint or contact a lawyer during the initial stop.
- You do not need to report your passport as lost or stolen until you have confirmed it is actually missing from your accommodation.
- You do not need to worry about permanent records for a routine check if the document is produced and verified.

## Important reassurance
Being stopped for a document check without your passport is a common occurrence for travelers. In most cases, providing alternative ID and arranging to show the passport later or at a station resolves the matter without further escalation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a routine identity check. It does not cover legal defense for criminal charges or the specific entry/exit laws of every individual country.

## Important note
U.S. citizens are subject to the laws of the country they are visiting. Some countries require tourists to carry their original passport at all times. If you are detained, prioritize requesting consular access and remaining calm.
