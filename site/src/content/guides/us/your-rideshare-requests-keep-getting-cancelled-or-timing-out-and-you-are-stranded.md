# What to do if… your rideshare requests keep getting cancelled or timing out and you are stranded (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safer, well-lit place with staff or other people nearby. Pause repeated ride requests while you check a realistic fallback, such as a local taxi, public transit, or a trusted person. If there is an immediate danger or another emergency, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept a ride from a stranger who approaches you because you appear to be waiting for transport.
- Do not keep walking into quieter or more isolated areas to search for a pickup spot.
- Do not get into a vehicle until its details match the ride shown in your app.
- Do not call out private details, such as your home address, where other people can hear.
- Do not drain your phone battery by leaving unnecessary apps open or keeping the screen brightness high.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer waiting place.**  
   Go inside a staffed, well-lit place if one is nearby, such as a venue lobby, hotel lobby, store, diner, or transit station. If you cannot go inside, wait near other people and away from moving traffic.

2. **Choose one clear pickup point.**  
   Use a legal stopping place that is easy to identify, such as a marked rideshare zone, main entrance, taxi stand, or well-lit landmark. Avoid alleys, service roads, and places where a vehicle cannot stop safely.

3. **Check a different way home.**  
   Choose the most realistic option available where you are:
   - Call a local taxi company or ask staff to help call a cab.
   - Check whether nearby public transit is still running.
   - Ask a trusted person whether they can pick you up.
   - Ask venue staff, hotel staff, or security where you can wait safely while arranging transport.

4. **Keep your phone usable.**  
   Close unnecessary apps, lower the screen brightness, and keep enough battery for calls, messages, directions, and ride details. Ask staff whether there is a safe place to charge your phone if needed.

5. **If you request another rideshare, make the pickup easy to identify.**  
   Use the clearest available pickup point. You may send a short in-app message such as: `I am at the main entrance under the lights, wearing a blue jacket.`

6. **Check the ride before entering.**  
   Match the license plate, vehicle make and model, and driver details against the app. Where the service supports it, ask the driver who they are there to pick up. If anything does not match, do not get in. Return to the safer waiting place.

7. **Once inside the correct vehicle, use a seat belt.**  
   Share your trip details or location with a trusted person if you choose and the feature is available.

8. **Treat immediate danger as an emergency.**  
   If someone is threatening, following, or harassing you and this creates an immediate danger, or you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance, call 911. Tell the call-taker where you are and move into the nearest safer staffed place if you can do so safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the cheapest option before choosing a safer way home.
- You do not need to keep retrying the same app.
- You do not need to work out why the rides were cancelled while you are still stranded.
- You do not need to submit a complaint until you are somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
Repeated rideshare cancellations can happen. Moving to a safer place and switching to a taxi, public transit, a trusted pickup, or a safe waiting location is a reasonable response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation. Later decisions, such as reporting an app issue or planning another route, can wait until you are safe.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If there is an immediate danger or another emergency, call 911.
