# What to do if… a partner repeatedly pushes you to have sex in situations where you cannot easily leave (USA)

## Short answer
Physically move to a safer pause by creating distance, then contact a specialist hotline or emergency services for immediate support and a safe exit plan.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to negotiate or argue for your safety while you are in an isolated or trapped location.
- Do not move to a more private or secluded area to resolve the conflict.
- Do not leave your phone, keys, or identification behind if you move to another room.
- Do not use your phone for help-seeking if you believe your screen is being actively monitored.
- Do not assume you are required to report to law enforcement to receive medical assistance.
- Do not blame yourself for complying or staying quiet to prevent the situation from escalating.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause by physically moving to a different space.** Step into a bathroom, go to another room, or walk toward a public area / exit if possible.
2. **Secure your essential items and keep them on your person.** Ensure your phone, keys, wallet, and shoes are with you or immediately accessible.
3. **Position yourself near a visible exit or in a space with other people.** If you are in a vehicle, ask to stop at a well-lit gas station, store, or public parking lot and exit the car there.
4. **Discreetly signal a trusted contact for assistance.** Send a pre-arranged "help" emoji or a short text such as "I need a ride now" to someone you trust.
5. **Call emergency services if you feel you are in immediate physical danger.** Dial **911** if you are being threatened, physically blocked from leaving, or if violence occurs.
6. **Contact the National Sexual Assault Hotline for confidential guidance.** Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)** or use the RAINN online chat to speak with a trained specialist about your options.
7. **Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline if this is part of an ongoing pattern of abuse.** Call **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)** or text **"START" to 88788** for support and safety planning.
8. **Seek medical care if any non-consensual sexual contact has occurred.** You can visit an emergency room for a sexual assault medical forensic exam (SAFE exam) to address health needs and preserve evidence without an immediate requirement to report to police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to end the relationship or confront the partner.
- You do not need to choose a label or legal term for the experience immediately.
- You do not need to decide today whether you want to pursue a formal police report.
- You do not need to have a long-term housing or safety plan ready this minute.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel frozen, numb, or to comply with a partner's demands when you feel trapped or unsafe. These are involuntary survival responses designed to reduce immediate harm. Hesitation or confusion in these moments does not mean you chose the situation or that you are responsible for the partner's behavior.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions only. It does not replace professional legal, medical, or advocacy services.

## Important note
This information is for immediate crisis stabilization. If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services. If you suspect your devices are being monitored, use a public computer or a trusted person's phone to access help.
