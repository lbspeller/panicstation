# What to do if… an automatic gate is stuck open and you cannot secure your entrance (USA)

## Short answer
Keep people away from the gate path and moving parts. Secure the house and garage, then contact your gate service provider. Do not force the gate shut or bypass safety devices.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand in the gate opening or reach through the gate.
- Don’t push or pull the gate while the operator is engaged.
- Don’t disable, cover, tape over, rewire, or bypass photo eyes, safety edges, or other safety devices.
- Don’t open the operator housing or control box or attempt electrical repairs unless you are trained to do so.
- Don’t use loose objects, ropes, or improvised wedges to hold the gate in place.
- Don’t let children, pets, or visitors stay near the gate while it is malfunctioning.
- Don’t use the vehicular gate opening as a pedestrian route. Use a separate entrance where available.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Stop pressing the remote or control button. Keep everyone away from the gate path, hinges, rollers, arms, track, and nearby pinch points.
2. **Secure the building.** Lock exterior doors and windows, secure the garage, and lock the interior door between the garage and the home if there is one.
3. **Move nearby portable valuables inside.** Bring bicycles, tools, and other easily moved items into a locked area if you can do so without approaching the gate machinery.
4. **Check only for an obvious obstruction from a safe position.** With the gate still, look for loose debris in a sliding-gate track or an object blocking a photo eye. Remove loose debris only if you can reach it without entering the gate path or going near moving parts. Do not adjust sensors, edges, wiring, or controls.
5. **Use a separate pedestrian entrance.** Keep children, pets, visitors, and delivery workers away from the vehicular gate opening.
6. **Use manual operation only if you already have clear manufacturer instructions.** Follow the operator manual only if you can do so without force, without entering the gate path, and without going near pinch points. Skip this if you are unsure or the gate is difficult to move.
7. **Request prompt service.** Contact your gate installer, gate service company, property manager, or HOA. You may also check your paperwork, operator manual, or operator label for contact details. Say that the automatic gate is stuck open and the entrance cannot be secured.
8. **Use low-risk deterrents while you wait.** Turn on exterior lighting near the unsecured entrance. Lock any separate pedestrian gate if it has a lock and you can reach it without approaching the gate machinery.
9. **Call 911 for an emergency.** Call 911 if someone is at immediate risk, someone is trying to enter, or a crime is in progress. For suspicious activity that does not require immediate assistance, use your local police department’s non-emergency contact.

## What can wait
- Diagnosing the exact fault, including sensor, latch, track, battery, motor, or controller problems.
- Deciding whether to replace the operator, install cameras, or change the access system.
- Any repair, reset, adjustment, or workaround that is not clearly covered by your operator manual.
- Non-urgent property improvements. The immediate goal is to keep people away from the gate and secure the building.

## Important reassurance
A gate stuck open is unsettling, but you do not need to repair the machinery right now. Keeping people away from the gate, securing the building, and arranging qualified service are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Powered gates can cause serious injury if they move unexpectedly. Repairs, adjustments, and safety testing may need a qualified gate installer or service professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, electrical, engineering, security, or other professional advice. Call 911 if someone needs immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance.
