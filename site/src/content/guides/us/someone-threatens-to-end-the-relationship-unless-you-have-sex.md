# What to do if… someone threatens to end the relationship unless you have sex (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause immediately, end the sexual situation, and contact a confidential crisis line for support. You are never required to have sex to maintain a relationship or prevent a breakup.

## Do not do these things
- Do not engage in sex to avoid a breakup — pressure or ultimatums negate free consent.
- Do not stay in a private or isolated space if you feel manipulated or unsafe.
- Do not attempt to debate or "prove" why you do not want to have sex.
- Do not delete messages, call logs, or recordings that contain the threats.
- Do not use devices or accounts that the other person can monitor if doing so increases your risk.

## What to do now
1. **Create immediate physical distance.** Move to a public area, a room with a lock, or outside. If you are currently in a sexual situation, stop and leave the space.
2. **Contact 911 if you are in immediate danger or feel you cannot leave.**
   - If it is unsafe to speak out loud, text 911 if this service is available in your area.
   - Provide your exact location and the nature of the threat immediately.
3. **Use a single, clear statement to set a boundary.**
   - "I am not doing this."
   - "Stop pressuring me."
   - "I am leaving now."
4. **Notify a trusted person of your location.** Text or call a friend, family member, or neighbor. Ask them to stay on the phone with you until you are in a secure location or have them pick you up.
5. **Contact a confidential support service for stabilization and options.**
   - **RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline:** Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)** or use the secure online chat at rainn.org.
   - **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** Call **1-800-799-SAFE (7233)**, text **START to 88788**, or use the chat at thehotline.org.
6. **Preserve evidence of the ultimatum.** Screenshot any texts, emails, or social media messages where the threat was made. Send these to a safe, private email address or a trusted friend.
7. **Seek medical evaluation if there was physical contact or you feel unwell.** You can visit an Emergency Room and ask for a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner (SANE) or an advocate. You do not have to decide on a police report to receive medical care or a forensic exam.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to end the relationship permanently right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report today.
- You do not need to explain your reasons for saying no to the other person.
- You do not need to confront the person about their behavior once you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Threats and emotional ultimatums can cause the body to freeze or "fawn" (comply to stay safe). This is a biological survival response, not a choice. Feeling confused, guilty, or unsure about what to call the situation is common and does not mean you are wrong to seek help.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Long-term safety planning, legal advice, and relationship counseling require professional consultation with the agencies listed above.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not a substitute for legal advice, medical diagnosis, or professional mental health care. If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services.
