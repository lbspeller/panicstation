# What to do if… you suddenly lose your balance and cannot walk steadily (USA)

## Short answer
If this started suddenly and you cannot explain it, treat it as an emergency. Call 911 now, because sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance, or loss of coordination can be a stroke warning sign.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself to the ER or urgent care.
- Do not try to walk unassisted, take a shower, use stairs, or “push through” while you are unsteady.
- Do not drink alcohol or take sedatives to try to steady yourself.
- Do not take someone else’s medication, or extra doses of your own medication, to try to stop the symptoms.
- Do not sleep it off or wait to see if it stops when the balance problem is sudden, new, severe, or unexplained.
- Do not decide it is “just vertigo” before you have been medically checked if it came on suddenly.

## What to do now
1. Get to a safer pause immediately. Sit or lie down where you are less likely to fall. If you might vomit, lie on your side. Keep your phone within reach.
2. Call 911 if this is sudden, new, severe, unexplained, or you cannot stand safely. Tell the dispatcher: “Sudden loss of balance or trouble walking, possible stroke,” and give your exact location.
3. Use B.E. F.A.S.T. only as a quick check, not as a reason to delay. Sudden balance loss itself counts as the “B” warning sign. Vision changes, face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty, or sudden severe headache also mean you should call 911 immediately.
4. Note the time you were last normal. Write down or say into your phone when you were last known well and when the symptoms started or were first noticed.
5. If you fell, hit your head, collapsed, have a new severe headache, are vomiting, are confused, seem very drowsy, have a seizure, have weakness or numbness, have slurred speech, or symptoms are worsening, tell 911. Stay still unless you need to move away from immediate danger.
6. Make it easier for responders to reach you if you can do so without standing repeatedly. Unlock the door, turn on a light, secure pets, and keep the phone on speaker.
7. Gather only the essentials if they are within reach: medication list, allergies, major medical conditions, and an emergency contact. Do not delay the 911 call to look for them.

## What can wait
- Researching causes online or trying home fixes for vertigo.
- Deciding whether it is “really” a stroke.
- Insurance, billing, and paperwork details.
- Work, school, and non-urgent messages.
- Calling multiple people for opinions before contacting emergency help.

## Important reassurance
Sudden loss of balance can be frightening, and it is common to worry about overreacting. Calling 911 for sudden, unexplained balance trouble is a reasonable safety step; the aim is to get checked quickly and avoid a preventable delay.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the initial window. Later decisions may need emergency clinicians, your own doctor, a neurologist, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or professional care. If symptoms are sudden, severe, worsening, linked to a fall or head injury, or paired with other stroke warning signs, seek emergency care immediately.
