# What to do if… a travel provider requires a printed document and you only have a digital screenshot (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the provider exactly what printed document they will accept. Request the official printable file, usually a PDF or full confirmation email, and use the quickest available printing option.

## Do not do these things
- Do not alter the screenshot to make it look official.
- Do not rely on one phone once the provider has said a printed document is required.
- Do not wait until the boarding line or final checkpoint before asking for help.
- Do not share passwords or leave your phone unlocked with another person.
- Do not crop or obscure any part of a document that the provider may need to inspect or scan.
- Do not assume a printed screenshot will be accepted. Ask first. If the provider confirms that it is acceptable, print the unedited screenshot as a fallback while you request the official version.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the accepted format.** Ask the provider: “I only have a screenshot. What exact printed document will you accept? Can you resend the official printable PDF or full confirmation email now?”
2. **Try to retrieve the original file.**
   - Check the provider’s website or app for booking, trip, document, download, or print options.
   - Search your email using the booking reference and words such as `confirmation`, `permit`, `voucher`, or `ticket`. Check spam and junk folders.
   - If a third party sold the booking, ask both the seller and the operator whether they can resend the accepted document.
3. **For a flight document, try the airline’s airport options.**
   - Use a self-service kiosk if your airline offers document or boarding-pass printing. Follow the kiosk prompts and use the lookup method the airline provides.
   - If the kiosk does not solve the problem, go to the staffed airline counter and ask whether staff can print or reissue the required document.
4. **Use a nearby printing option.**
   - Ask your hotel or lodging provider whether staff can print an emailed attachment.
   - Check whether a nearby public library offers printing from a phone or email, and check its requirements and opening hours before traveling there.
   - Ask a nearby print or copy shop whether it can print the file.
   - At FedEx Office, Print & Go may allow you to email a file, receive a retrieval code, and print at a self-service machine. Check that a nearby FedEx Office location is open before going there.
5. **Check the printout before leaving the printer.**
   - Print one clear copy first. Print a spare if it is easy.
   - Check that the full page is visible and readable.
   - If the document includes a barcode or QR code, make sure the whole code is visible and not blurred, folded, or cut off.
6. **If time is tight, go back to the provider.**
   - Ask for an in-person printout, reissue, or written confirmation of the acceptable alternative.
   - Allow extra time for lines and any identity checks the provider may require.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to complain or request a refund right now.
- You do not need to reorganize every travel file.
- You do not need to write a long explanation. Focus on obtaining one acceptable printed document.

## Important reassurance
You have several practical routes to try. The immediate task is only to confirm the accepted format and obtain one clear printout.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a time-pressured travel-document problem. Complaints, refunds, and policy disputes can wait until you are no longer dealing with the immediate situation, and specialist help may be useful later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Provider rules and local printing options vary. Ask the provider to confirm what it will accept.
