# What to do if… you left your passport in a taxi or rideshare and need to travel today (USA)

## Short answer
Try to recover the passport immediately through the taxi or rideshare, while also preparing for the possibility that you may not travel today. If recovery is not clearly happening very soon, move to the official urgent passport route.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t spend a long time re-checking bags before contacting the driver, company, or dispatch.
- Don’t rely on a passport photo, passport number, or photocopy to fly internationally; you generally need the original physical passport.
- Don’t pay anyone to book a passport agency appointment for you; official passport agency appointments are free and third-party appointment services are not affiliated with the U.S. Department of State.
- Don’t wait until boarding to tell the airline; go to the airline desk early so you can ask about rebooking options.
- Don’t post passport numbers, photos, or trip documents publicly.
- Don’t report the passport lost or stolen while you are still actively expecting it back within minutes, unless the official process or your situation requires it; once reported, it will be cancelled.

## What to do now
1. **Save the ride details now:** keep the ride receipt, pickup and drop-off times, route, driver name, vehicle details, taxi number, and any trip or support ID.
2. **Contact the driver or company immediately:**
   - **Rideshare:** use the in-app lost item process to contact the driver or support.
   - **Taxi or car service:** call dispatch or the company you booked with and give the time, route, cab number, and your contact number.
3. **Tell the airline early if you are at the airport:** go to check-in or customer service and say your passport is missing. Ask what can still be done today, including later flights, rebooking rules, document deadlines, and when you must decide.
4. **Set a short pivot point:** if you do not have clear confirmation that the passport is being returned very soon, stop chasing only one route and begin urgent replacement planning.
5. **Use the official urgent passport route if you are in the United States:** if you have urgent international travel, make an appointment at a passport agency or center when you are within 14 calendar days of travel, or within 28 days if you need a foreign visa. Appointment slots may not be available, so check immediately and be prepared to travel to an agency or center if one is available.
6. **Contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate if you are outside the United States:** say your U.S. passport is lost, your travel is soon, and you need help with replacement. You will usually need to appear in person, and an emergency limited-validity passport may be possible if there is not enough time for a regular one.
7. **Report the passport lost or stolen if it is not clearly coming back very soon:** use the official lost or stolen passport process, or report it while applying for a replacement. Reporting cancels the passport, and it will not be valid for travel even if you later find it.
8. **Prepare the replacement application items:** complete Form DS-11 but do not sign it until instructed; gather photo ID, evidence of U.S. citizenship if available, one compliant passport photo, travel confirmation, and details of where and when the passport was lost.
9. **Reduce follow-on risk if other items were with it:** if cards, phone, laptop, or account details were in the same bag, lock payment cards, secure email, and change travel account passwords from a safe device.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide insurance or reimbursement right now; keep receipts, screenshots, and a short timeline.
- You do not need to file a police report first unless an embassy, consulate, airline, insurer, local authority, or your circumstances ask for one.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip now; focus on recovery, airline options, and the fastest legitimate passport step.
- You do not need to decide future travel plans until you know whether the passport is coming back or an urgent appointment is available.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but it has a clear order: recover fast, tell the airline early, then use the official passport route if recovery is not happening. Staying with official channels helps avoid scams and keeps your choices open.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Later decisions about rebooking, insurance, identity protection, or replacement documents may need help from the airline, the U.S. Department of State, a U.S. embassy or consulate, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Passport requirements, appointment availability, airline rules, and entry rules can change, so follow the current U.S. Department of State instructions and your airline’s rules for your itinerary.
