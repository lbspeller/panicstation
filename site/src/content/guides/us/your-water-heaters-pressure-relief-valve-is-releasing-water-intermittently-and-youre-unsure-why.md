# What to do if… your water heater’s pressure relief valve is releasing water intermittently and you’re unsure why (USA)

## Short answer
Shut off the power or gas to the water heater immediately and stay clear of the discharge pipe. Intermittent release typically indicates high house pressure, thermal expansion, or a failing safety valve that requires professional inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not plug, cap, or block the end of the discharge pipe or the relief valve.
- Do not attempt to "test" the valve by pulling the lever while it is hot or actively discharging.
- Do not install any valve or shutoff device on the discharge line.
- Do not touch the discharge water or the pipe itself, as it can cause severe scald burns.
- Do not ignore the issue if it stops temporarily; safety valves only trigger when a limit is exceeded.
- Do not place a bucket or container directly over the end of the pipe if it is actively steaming or rushing.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the energy source and shut it off immediately.**  
   - **For electric heaters:** Locate the circuit breaker labeled "Water Heater" in your main electrical panel and switch it to the OFF position.  
   - **For gas heaters:** Locate the thermostat dial on the front of the heater and turn it to the "OFF" or "PILOT" position.  

2. **Establish a safety perimeter around the discharge pipe.**  
   Keep children and pets away from the area where the discharge pipe terminates (usually 6 inches above the floor or near a drain). High-temperature water or steam can release without warning.

3. **Check for emergency triggers that require immediate evacuation.**  
   If you hear a loud whistling or hissing sound, or if you see visible steam coming from the pipe, leave the area immediately, ensure all occupants are out of the house, and call **911** or your local emergency services. These are signs of a runaway heating element and potential tank failure.

4. **Close the cold-water inlet valve if the discharge is heavy and continuous.**  
   Locate the shutoff valve on the cold-water pipe leading into the top of the water heater (often marked with a blue ring or handle). Turn it clockwise to stop the flow of water into the tank. If you cannot reach it safely or are unsure which valve it is, do not attempt this step.

5. **Verify the house water pressure if the heater is not overheating.**  
   If the water is lukewarm or cold but the valve still drips, the issue is likely high municipal pressure or a failed expansion tank. Use a screw-on pressure gauge on an outdoor hose bib if you have one, or wait for a professional.

6. **Contact a licensed plumber for a safety inspection.**  
   **Request that the plumber checks the following specific items:**  
   - The static water pressure of the home (should generally be below 80 PSI).  
   - The condition of the thermal expansion tank (if present) to ensure it hasn't lost its air charge or ruptured.  
   - The thermostat and heating elements for malfunctions that cause overheating.  
   - The Temperature and Pressure (T&P) relief valve itself for mineral buildup or mechanical failure.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace the entire water heater immediately unless the tank itself is leaking or compromised.
- You do not need to drain the tank yourself; this is safer for a plumber to perform.
- You do not need to decide on a new heater model or brand while the system is simply shut down and stabilized.

## Important reassurance
The relief valve is performing its primary safety function by releasing pressure before it becomes dangerous to the tank's structure. By shutting off the energy source, you have removed the "heat" variable from the equation, significantly reducing the immediate risk of escalation while you wait for a repair.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization of an intermittently discharging T&P valve only. It does not replace the necessity of a physical inspection by a qualified professional to ensure the system meets local plumbing codes and manufacturer safety standards.

## Important note
Water heater safety devices are regulated by ASME and local plumbing codes. Incorrectly handling a relief valve can result in a tank explosion or severe injury. If you are unable to safely locate the shutoff points, evacuate the immediate area and wait for professional assistance.
