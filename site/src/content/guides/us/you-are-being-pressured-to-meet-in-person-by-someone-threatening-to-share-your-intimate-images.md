# What to do if… you are being pressured to meet in person by someone threatening to share your intimate images (USA)

## Short answer
Do not go to the meeting. Move to a safer pause and tell one trusted person what is happening; call 911 if you are in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not meet them to try to calm them down or make the threats stop.
- Do not send more images, videos, money, or personal information.
- Do not confront them, track them down, or arrange for someone else to confront them.
- Do not keep debating with them once you are in a safer position.
- Do not delete messages in panic if it is safe to leave them in place.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** If you think they may come to your location, are nearby, or you are in immediate danger, move to a safer place and call **911**.

2. **Tell one trusted person now.** Ask them to stay with you in person or on the phone. Tell them that someone with intimate images is pressuring you to meet and that you are not going to the meeting.

3. **Reduce their access to you.** Once you are safer, stop engaging. Turn off location sharing, check whether they can see your location through any app, and mute or block them if that does not create a new safety risk.

4. **Keep only what is easy and safe to preserve.** If it feels manageable, keep a simple copy of the threats and the account details, or ask your trusted person to help. Do not download or forward intimate images.

5. **Choose support without pressure.** RAINN offers confidential support and explains any limits that may apply. If you decide to report, you can use IC3 for a cyber-enabled complaint or contact local police. You do not need to decide about reporting before asking for support.

6. **Use the route that fits the age in the images.** If you are under 18, tell a safe adult. If anyone shown was under 18 when an image was made, use the NCMEC CyberTipline. Take It Down may help limit sharing of images made before age 18.

7. **Use adult image-removal tools only when they fit.** StopNCII may help if you are currently over 18, you were at least 18 when the image was made, you are the person shown, and you still have the image or video. It works with participating platforms. If images are already online, RAINN’s reporting page can help you find platform reporting routes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to report to law enforcement.
- You do not need to respond to every message or explain your decision not to meet.
- You do not need to gather perfect evidence before asking for help.
- You do not need to work out who they really are or how they got the images.
- You do not need to handle every takedown step today.

## Important reassurance
The pressure is meant to make you feel that you must act quickly and alone. You are allowed to slow the situation down, refuse the meeting, and bring in support. This is not your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later choices about reporting, account security, removal requests, and specialist support can be handled when you have more support around you.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
