# What to do if… your computer boots to a recovery screen saying the operating system is missing or damaged (USA)

## Short answer
Protect your files first: stop repeated restarts, unplug external drives, and do not choose reset, erase, reinstall, or remove-files options unless your important files are already backed up or you accept that they may be lost.

## Do not do these things
- Do not choose **Reset this PC**, **Remove everything**, **Erase**, **Factory reset**, or a reinstall option while you still need files from the internal drive.
- Do not keep power-cycling the computer over and over; if the drive is failing, repeated attempts may make recovery harder.
- Do not run advanced command-line boot repairs while panicked, especially if you do not have a current backup.
- Do not install “boot fix” tools from ads, pop-ups, or unfamiliar websites.
- Do not plug in backup drives if there were signs of malware or ransomware; keep backups disconnected until the situation is clearer.

## What to do now
1. **Capture the exact screen.**
   - Take a photo of the whole message, including any error code.
   - Write down what happened right before this started, such as an update, power loss, new hardware, spill, or drop.

2. **Remove possible wrong boot devices.**
   - Shut down.
   - Unplug USB drives, SD cards, external hard drives, external SSDs, docks, and adapters.
   - Start once with only power, keyboard, and mouse connected.

3. **Check that the computer is trying to boot from the internal drive.**
   - If your computer has a boot menu or firmware setup screen, check whether the internal drive appears.
   - If it is trying to start from USB or another external device, switch back to the internal drive.
   - If the internal drive does not appear, or it appears and disappears, treat this as possible drive or hardware failure.

4. **Try the safest built-in repair option first.**
   - **Windows:** from Windows Recovery Environment, choose **Troubleshoot → Advanced options → Startup Repair**.
   - If Windows asks for a BitLocker recovery key, pause and look for the key through your Microsoft account or your device records; do not guess repeatedly.
   - **Mac:** if you see a flashing folder with a question mark, start macOS Recovery and use **Disk Utility → First Aid** on the startup disk before considering erase or reinstall options.

5. **Switch to data-first mode if repair does not clearly work.**
   - Stop before reset, erase, reinstall, or advanced repair commands.
   - Decide how to copy the files before trying more repairs: a trusted technician, the manufacturer’s support route, or a reputable data-recovery provider.
   - If you try a do-it-yourself method, avoid anything that writes to, formats, repairs, or “initializes” the internal drive.

6. **Treat hardware warning signs seriously.**
   - If the drive makes clicking sounds, the disk is not detected, Disk Utility says it cannot repair the disk, or the computer reports no bootable device after basic checks, shut down and prioritize data recovery or manufacturer support.
   - If the computer was dropped or exposed to liquid, stop using it and keep it powered off until it can be assessed.

7. **If malware or ransomware is a realistic concern, isolate the computer.**
   - Disconnect Wi-Fi and Ethernet if you can do so from the recovery screen or firmware screen.
   - Keep backup drives and shared storage disconnected.
   - Do not restore files from backup until the computer has been assessed or rebuilt safely.

8. **If the device may be under warranty, preserve your options.**
   - Keep the error photos, receipt or order confirmation, and a list of what you tried.
   - Contact the seller or manufacturer support with the exact message and error code.
   - Before following any step that may erase data, ask support whether the step affects your files, warranty options, or repair route.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to reinstall the operating system, replace the drive, or replace the computer.
- You do not need to run boot commands, registry fixes, or disk-writing repairs while overwhelmed.
- You do not need to connect backup drives, restore files, or turn syncing back on immediately.
- You do not need to buy recovery software before you know whether the internal drive is detected and whether your files are backed up.

## Important reassurance
A missing or damaged operating system message can happen after an update, power interruption, boot setting change, file-system problem, or drive fault. Slowing down and choosing data-first steps is a sensible response, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps to avoid irreversible mistakes, stabilize the situation, and get you to a safer next decision. If the disk is failing, encrypted, physically damaged, or contains files you cannot replace, specialist help may be needed.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not professional IT, legal, data-recovery, cybersecurity, financial, medical, or therapeutic advice. If you are unsure whether an option erases or changes data, assume it might and pause until you can confirm.
