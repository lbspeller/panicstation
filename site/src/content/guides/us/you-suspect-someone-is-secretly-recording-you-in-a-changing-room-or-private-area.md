# What to do if… you suspect someone is secretly recording you in a changing room or private area (USA)

## Short answer
Get dressed if you need to, leave the private area, and go to a staffed public place. Ask staff or security to intervene now, and call 911 if there is any immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the person yourself or try to grab their phone or device.
- Do not stay alone in the restroom, bathroom, locker room, or changing area to confirm your suspicion.
- Do not touch or remove a suspected device unless you need to move it away from a child or immediate harm.
- Do not share suspected images or videos with anyone to prove it.
- Do not let anyone pressure you to drop it because you are not certain.
- Do not post identifying claims online while you are still unsafe or shaken.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** Leave the private area and go to a public, staffed place such as the front desk, checkout, reception area, or security desk. If you can, ask a trusted person to stay with you.
2. **Tell staff or security plainly what you suspect.** Say: “I suspect someone is secretly recording in or near the changing rooms or private area. I need help right now.”
3. **Ask the venue to stop further access.** Ask staff to stop anyone else entering that stall, cubicle, room, restroom, bathroom, locker room, or changing area until a manager, security officer, or police officer can look at it.
4. **Ask the venue to preserve information.** Ask them to write down the exact time, exact location, who was nearby, who was working, and whether there is relevant security footage or access-log information. You do not need to investigate it yourself.
5. **Contact law enforcement when you are safe.**
   - If you feel in immediate danger, the person is nearby, or someone is threatening you, call **911**.
   - If the danger is not immediate, contact the local police department or sheriff’s office where it happened and ask how to make a report.
6. **Reach confidential specialist support, if you want it.** RAINN can provide confidential support and help you think through options:
   - Call **800-656-HOPE (800-656-4673)**.
   - Text **HOPE** to **64673**.
   - Use online chat only if you are on a private device and connection.
7. **Write down a brief record while it is fresh.** Note the location, date, time, exact area, what made you suspect recording, any description of the person or device, and the names or roles of staff you spoke to.
8. **If a child or teen may be involved, keep the focus on safety and support.** Get them dressed and to a calm public place. You can contact local police, 911 in an emergency, or the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children CyberTipline if there may be child sexual exploitation.

If you may want to report later, avoid deleting messages, photos, notes, or resetting your phone right now. Keep things as they are until you have had support.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to pursue charges, a civil complaint, or a formal complaint to the venue.
- You do not need to identify the device type, the person’s name, or exactly what was captured.
- You do not need to contact the venue’s corporate office today.
- You do not need to search online for yourself.
- You do not need to keep using the facility to see if it happens again.

## Important reassurance
Shock, embarrassment, anger, shaking, and freezing can happen after a suspected sexual boundary violation. Getting help quickly is about safety and preventing further harm; you do not have to be certain before asking staff, security, police, or a confidential support service for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilize the moment, reduce immediate risk, and connect with support. Later decisions about police reports, complaints, image takedown, protective orders, or legal options can wait and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. You can seek support and talk through options without committing to any report.
