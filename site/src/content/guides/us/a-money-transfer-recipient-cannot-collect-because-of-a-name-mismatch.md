# What to do if… a money transfer recipient cannot collect because of a name mismatch (USA)

## Short answer
Compare the recipient name recorded on the transfer with the ID the recipient will present. The sender should contact the provider through its official support route and ask whether the unreleased transfer can be corrected or cancelled.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send another transfer or pay an extra fee to “unlock” the first one.
- Don’t share the full confirmation or reference number in public messages or with an unexpected caller or message sender.
- Don’t assume a nickname, missing hyphen, or different name order will be accepted.
- Don’t keep trying different pickup locations before checking the name recorded on the transfer.
- Don’t follow a third party’s instructions to avoid contacting the provider directly.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the recipient to type the name exactly as displayed on the ID they will present.** Check the spelling, order, spacing, hyphens, and any suffix.
2. **Check the name recorded on the transfer.** Use your receipt, app, or confirmation email. Compare it carefully with the recipient’s ID name. Look for differences such as a nickname, missing middle name, missing hyphen, different surname order, or spelling error.
3. **Check the transfer status through the provider’s official app, website, receipt, or support route.** Confirm whether the funds are still unreleased.
4. **Ask the provider whether it can correct the recipient name.** The sender should explain that the recipient cannot collect because the recorded name does not match the ID they will present. Have the confirmation or reference number and the corrected recipient name ready.
5. **If the provider cannot edit the name, ask about cancellation and refund options before sending again.** Ask whether fees are refunded and whether you need to start a new transfer.
6. **If the transfer shows as paid out but the intended recipient did not receive it, tell the provider immediately.** Ask for its fraud or dispute process and keep a note of when you contacted it.
7. **If this was a remittance transfer from the United States to another country and the issue is not resolved, ask about a notice of error.** For a covered remittance transfer, the sender can generally give the provider an oral or written notice of error no later than 180 days after the disclosed date when the funds were expected to be available. Some banks and credit unions may be exempt from the federal requirement to provide error protections, so ask the provider what process applies.
8. **If the provider does not resolve the issue, consider submitting a complaint to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.** Include the key dates, amount, provider name, and your contact history.
9. **Stop if anyone asks for more money to release the transfer.** Contact the provider through an official route. If you suspect fraud, use the provider’s fraud process and consider reporting it to the Federal Trade Commission.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide immediately whether the problem was an innocent mistake or a scam.
- You don’t need to visit more pickup locations while the recorded name may still be wrong.
- You don’t need to send a replacement transfer until the provider explains the status of the first one.
- You don’t need to work through every escalation route at once.

## Important reassurance
A name mismatch can stop a recipient from collecting a transfer. When the funds have not been released, the provider may be able to correct the information or explain the cancellation options.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the funds were paid out to someone else, the provider refuses to address the issue, or you need help with a formal complaint, later decisions may need specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Provider rules, identity checks, refund options, and legal protections can vary. Use the provider’s official support route and pause if anyone pressures you to send more money.
