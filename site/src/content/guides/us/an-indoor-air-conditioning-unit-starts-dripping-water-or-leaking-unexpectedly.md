# What to do if… an indoor air-conditioning unit starts dripping water or leaking unexpectedly (USA)

## Short answer
Shut off the air conditioning system immediately, contain the water to prevent structural damage, and ensure no water contacts electrical outlets or wiring.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the system running to attempt to "dry out" the unit.
- Do not touch any internal wiring, open access panels, or reach inside the unit while power is connected.
- Do not use extension cords or power strips on floors that are wet or in the path of a drip.
- Do not attempt to repair refrigerant lines yourself; U.S. federal law requires EPA Section 608 certification for refrigerant handling.
- Do not touch stagnant water in the drain pan with bare hands as it may contain bacteria or mold.
- Do not ignore water dripping through a ceiling as this indicates a serious risk of structural collapse.

## What to do now
1. **Turn the AC system off at the thermostat immediately.** This stops the production of new condensate and prevents the blower fan from spreading moisture further.
2. **Disconnect electrical power if water is near outlets or the air handler.** If it is safe to do so with dry hands and dry footing, turn off the dedicated HVAC breaker at the main electrical panel.
3. **Contact emergency services if electrical hazards are active.** If you see sparks, smell burning, or see water entering an electrical box and cannot safely reach the breaker, call 911.
4. **Contain the leak with buckets or trays.** Place a container directly under the drip point to stop water from saturating floorboards or carpets.
5. **Protect the immediate area from water damage.**
   - Move electronics, furniture, and valuables away from the leak path.
   - Use towels to soak up standing water and place plastic sheeting under the towels to protect the subfloor.
6. **Perform a visual external check only.**
   - Look for an obvious kink or disconnection in the visible condensate drain line leading away from the unit.
   - Check if the air filter is visibly blocked with dust, which can cause the internal coils to freeze and then leak.
7. **Notify the appropriate repair party.**
   - **If you rent:** Contact the property manager immediately and report "active water leak from the HVAC unit."
   - **If you own:** Call a licensed HVAC professional. Ensure they are trained for 2026-compliant A2L (mildly flammable) refrigerants if your system was installed recently.
8. **Document the leak with photos.** Capture the source of the drip and any visible damage to the walls or floor without disassembling the unit.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause (such as a cracked pan or clogged line) before calling for help.
- You do not need to flush the drain lines or perform maintenance yourself.
- You do not need to decide on system replacement or expensive repairs until a professional diagnosis is provided.

## Important reassurance
Indoor AC leaks are common occurrences usually caused by a simple blockage in the drainage system or a dirty filter. By turning the power off and containing the water, you have already completed the most important steps to prevent permanent damage to your home.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization only. A professional HVAC technician must inspect the internal components, including the evaporator coil, secondary drain pan, and condensate pump, to ensure the system is safe to resume operation.

## Important note
This guide is for general information only and does not replace professional HVAC or electrical advice. If water is in contact with electrical components, do not touch the unit and seek professional assistance immediately.
