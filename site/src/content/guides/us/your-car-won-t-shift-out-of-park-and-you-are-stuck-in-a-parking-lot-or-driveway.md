# What to do if… your car won’t shift out of park and you are stuck in a parking lot or driveway (USA)

## Short answer
Secure the vehicle first: foot on the brake, parking brake set, and hazard lights on if you may be blocking anyone. Then try the basic brake, ignition, and battery checks; use the shift-lock override only if your manual shows how and you can do it safely.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t yank, slam, or force the shifter.
- Don’t let anyone stand in front of or behind the vehicle while you are trying to shift.
- Don’t ask people to push the vehicle unless a roadside or recovery professional is controlling the move.
- Don’t crawl under the vehicle or try under-car release methods in a parking lot or driveway.
- Don’t use the shift-lock override as a repair. Treat it as a temporary way to make the vehicle movable.
- Don’t keep cycling random ignition or push-button start modes if you are not sure what mode the vehicle is in.

## What to do now
1. **Make the vehicle safe**
   - Keep your foot firmly on the brake.
   - Set the parking brake fully.
   - Turn on hazard lights if the vehicle is blocking a driveway, lane, garage entrance, or parking-lot traffic.
   - Keep passengers, helpers, children, and pets away from the front and rear of the vehicle.

2. **Try the normal shift release once, calmly**
   - Make sure the vehicle is in the correct power mode for shifting. This may be engine running, ignition on, or the vehicle ready mode.
   - Press the brake pedal firmly and hold it down.
   - Press the shifter button if your shifter has one.
   - Try moving from **P** to **N** first, without force.

3. **Check whether the vehicle is detecting the brake**
   - Look for brake lights using a helper standing to the side, a wall, a window reflection, or another safe reflection.
   - If the brake lights do not come on, the vehicle may not be receiving the brake signal it expects.
   - Stop forcing the shifter. Plan for roadside assistance, a tow, or a vehicle-specific shift-lock override only if you can do it safely.

4. **Check for weak 12-volt power**
   - If dash lights are dim, screens flicker, locks behave oddly, or the vehicle will not enter the correct power mode, low 12-volt power may be part of the problem.
   - If you already know how to use a jump pack safely, restore 12-volt power and repeat the normal shift release.
   - If you are not comfortable jump-starting the vehicle, skip this and call roadside assistance.

5. **If the vehicle is on a slope, reduce rollaway risk**
   - Keep your foot on the brake and the parking brake set.
   - Do not let anyone push the vehicle.
   - Do not select **Neutral** until you are ready to control the vehicle and the space is clear.
   - If the shifter still will not move without force, call roadside assistance and say the vehicle is stuck in Park on an incline.

6. **Use the shift-lock override only if the exact vehicle instructions are available**
   - Check the owner’s manual, glovebox quick guide, manufacturer app, or manufacturer roadside line for terms such as “shift lock release,” “shift-lock override,” “emergency shift release,” or “shift interlock.”
   - Before using any override, keep your foot on the brake, set the parking brake, and make sure no one is in the vehicle’s path.
   - Some vehicles have a small covered slot near the shifter; others use a different procedure, and some electronic shifters may not have a simple driver-accessible release.
   - If the override works, move only far enough to reach a safer, flatter, less obstructive spot. Then shift back to **Park**, set the parking brake, and arrange repair.

7. **Call for help based on the risk**
   - Call roadside assistance, your insurer’s roadside program, the vehicle manufacturer’s roadside line, or a tow company.
   - Say: “My automatic vehicle is stuck in Park. I cannot shift to Neutral normally. It is in a parking lot or driveway. The space is flat or sloped. Access is tight or clear.”
   - Call **911** only if there is immediate danger, such as traffic risk, fire, injury, a crime or threat, or you cannot get to a safe place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact failed part right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop before the vehicle is safe and movable.
- You do not need to remove trim panels, open the console, or troubleshoot wiring in the lot or driveway.
- You do not need to decide whether this is a recall issue until the immediate situation is stable.

## Important reassurance
A car stuck in Park is often a safety-interlock, power, brake-signal, or parking-mechanism problem rather than something you caused. The safest move is to avoid force, keep the vehicle controlled, and get vehicle-specific help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are stuck. Later decisions about diagnosis, repair, towing, recalls, insurance, or complaints may need help from a qualified technician, roadside provider, manufacturer, insurer, or official safety resource.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional mechanical, legal, financial, or safety advice. Vehicle designs differ, so use your owner’s manual or manufacturer guidance when available. If there is immediate danger, prioritize safety and call emergency services.
