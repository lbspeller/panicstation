# What to do if… your cloud account shows massive deletions you did not perform (USA)

## Short answer
Pause syncing, secure the account from a trusted device, then start recovery through the provider’s trash, recycle bin, recently deleted, version history, or account restore tools.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave devices syncing while you troubleshoot; it may spread deletions or overwrites.
- Don’t empty trash, recycle bin, or recently deleted folders.
- Don’t approve unexpected sign-in prompts, password reset prompts, or MFA push requests.
- Don’t follow support links from an email or text you did not request; open the provider’s app or type the provider’s site yourself.
- Don’t reuse an old password or a small variation of it if compromise is possible.
- Don’t rush into paying an extortion demand, recovery fee, or “support” fee before using official support and trusted help.
- Don’t factory reset devices yet unless a trusted security professional or your workplace IT team tells you to.

## What to do now
1. **Pause syncing immediately**
   - Disconnect affected devices from the internet by turning off Wi-Fi, using airplane mode, or unplugging ethernet.
   - In the cloud app, choose **pause sync** or **sign out** on each device you can access.
   - If this is a work or school account, notify IT or your administrator now and ask them to block suspicious sign-ins while the account is secured.

2. **Record what you can before it changes**
   - Screenshot or export the sign-in history, security alerts, file activity log, deletion timestamps, device names, and user names shown.
   - Screenshot new sharing links, new collaborators, permission changes, connected apps, or recovery email and phone changes you do not recognize.
   - Write down when you first noticed the deletions and any recent suspicious emails, prompts, or file-sharing messages.

3. **Secure the account from a safer device**
   - Use a different trusted device if possible. If you must use the affected device, update it and run its built-in security scan first.
   - Change the cloud account password to a strong, unique password.
   - Turn on MFA. Use a passkey, security key, or authenticator app if the provider offers one.
   - Use the provider’s option to sign out of other sessions or devices.
   - Remove unfamiliar logged-in devices, connected apps, third-party access, recovery emails, recovery phone numbers, passkeys, or app passwords.

4. **Start file recovery through official provider tools**
   - Check **Trash**, **Recycle Bin**, **Recently Deleted**, and any deleted-items area for the missing files.
   - Check **version history** for files that were overwritten rather than deleted.
   - Check whether your provider has a **restore to a previous time** or whole-drive restore tool.
   - If this is Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Apple Business, or another managed account, ask an administrator to check admin recovery and audit tools.

5. **Contact the cloud provider through official support**
   - Open support from the provider’s app, account page, or official help site.
   - Say: “My cloud account shows mass deletions I did not perform. I need help preserving logs, checking unauthorized access, and restoring deleted or overwritten files.”
   - Ask what recovery windows apply to your account type and whether server-side recovery is still available.

6. **Report only if the situation fits**
   - If this includes cyber-enabled fraud, extortion, stolen money, or a scam, you can file a report with the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center.
   - If personal information may have been used for identity theft, such as new accounts, tax issues, benefits issues, credit problems, or a SIM swap, start at IdentityTheft.gov.
   - If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.

## What can wait
- Rebuilding folder structures, renaming files, or re-sharing permissions can wait until recovery options are checked.
- Deciding whether to wipe or replace devices can wait until the account is locked down and logs are saved.
- Switching cloud providers or redesigning your backup system can wait.
- Broad notifications to contacts, customers, or collaborators can wait until you know what was accessed, shared, or sent.

## Important reassurance
Mass deletions are frightening, but deleted files and older versions are often recoverable for a limited time. The immediate goal is to stop further syncing, remove unauthorized access, and use recovery tools before retention windows close.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the account, preserve useful information, and improve the chance of restoring files. Later decisions may need help from the cloud provider, workplace IT, a cybersecurity professional, law enforcement, or a lawyer if sensitive data, regulated records, or extortion are involved.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, cybersecurity, forensic, therapeutic, or professional advice. Recovery options, retention windows, security controls, and reporting choices vary by provider, account type, and facts.
