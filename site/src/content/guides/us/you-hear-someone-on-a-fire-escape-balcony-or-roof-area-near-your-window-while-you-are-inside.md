# What to do if… you hear someone on a fire escape, balcony, or roof area near your window while you are inside (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the window immediately to an interior room and call 911 if you believe someone is attempting to enter or if you feel threatened. Do not interact with the person or open the window to investigate.

## Do not do these things
- Don't open the window, door, or curtain to see who is there.
- Don't step out onto the balcony, fire escape, or roof.
- Don't shine a flashlight or turn on lights that would reveal your exact position to someone outside.
- Don't shout at the person or announce your presence if they haven't seen you.
- Don't wait to call 911 because you think it might be a neighbor or building maintenance.

## What to do now
1. **Move to an interior room or a space behind a solid wall immediately.** Put as much distance and physical structure between yourself and the window as possible.
   - Bring a phone with you to maintain communication with emergency services.
2. **Turn off the lights in your current room if you can do so safely.** Reducing internal light makes it harder for someone outside to see your movements or determine if the home is occupied.
3. **Lock the door to the room you have moved into.** If the room does not have a lock, stay quiet and remain out of the line of sight of any windows or doors.
4. **Call 911 and provide your exact location.** Tell the dispatcher:
   - Your full address, including the apartment or unit number and floor level.
   - That there is an unauthorized person on your fire escape, balcony, or roof.
   - Any specific sounds of entry, such as glass breaking, wood splintering, or a lock being forced.
5. **Listen for specific cues that indicate the person’s intent or movement.** Note if the sounds are moving toward your window, away from it, or if they are focused on the window frame/lock.
6. **Text 911 only if you cannot speak safely.** If you are hiding and noise would put you at risk, send a text with your address, unit number, and the nature of the emergency.
7. **Wait for a confirmed police arrival before moving from your safe spot.** Do not assume the person has left just because the noise has stopped.

## What can wait
- You don't need to identify the person or their clothing right now.
- You don't need to call your landlord or building management until law enforcement has arrived and secured the area.
- You don't need to check other windows or doors in the home if doing so requires moving back toward the hazard area.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden spike in heart rate or a sense of "freezing" when you hear unexpected noises near your home. These are standard survival responses. By moving to a safe room and involving professional help, you are taking the most effective steps to manage the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active, suspicious presence outside a window. It does not cover long-term security upgrades, legal definitions of trespass, or interactions with building management regarding shared access points.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation where you feel your physical safety is at risk, contact **911** immediately.
