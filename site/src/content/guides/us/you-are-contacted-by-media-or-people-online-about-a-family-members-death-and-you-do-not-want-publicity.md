# What to do if… you are contacted by media or people online about a family member’s death and you do not want publicity (USA)

## Short answer
Cease all live engagement immediately. Send one single "no contact" message through a designated point person, then use technical blocking tools and document every interaction.

## Do not do these things
- Do not answer questions impulsively or attempt to "explain" your side to make contact stop.
- Do not provide photos, home addresses, or location details to anyone who initiated contact.
- Do not debate strangers in DMs or comment sections, as this increases the visibility of the posts.
- Do not assume a caller is a legitimate journalist just because they claim a news outlet name.
- Do not post funeral dates, times, or locations on public social media profiles.
- Do not click links in messages from unknown people claiming to show you "what's being said."

## What to do now
1. **Designate one person to handle all incoming contact for 24–48 hours.**
   **All other family members should stop responding to messages, calls, or comments immediately.**
   - The point person should collect screenshots or voicemails but does not need to reply to them individually.

2. **Send one final boundary statement and then stop communication.**
   **Use a neutral template: "Our family is grieving and we do not want any publicity or contact. Do not contact us again."**
   - After sending this, block the sender immediately. Do not engage with follow-up questions.

3. **Secure your physical and digital privacy.**
   **If journalists are coming to your home, place a clear sign on the front door stating: "No media. We will not be providing any statements or interviews. Please respect our privacy."**
   - Set all social media profiles to "Private."
   - Disable or restrict Direct Messages (DMs) from people you do not follow.

4. **Utilize technical blocking tools for phones.**
   **Forward any harassing or spam text messages to 7726 (SPAM) to report them to your carrier.**
   - Enable "Silence Unknown Callers" in your phone settings.
   - Note: The National Do Not Call Registry only stops commercial telemarketing; it does not legally bar journalists or private citizens from calling.

5. **Preserve evidence without engaging.**
   **Take screenshots of harassing messages, social media posts, and call logs.**
   - Save voicemails.
   - Keep a simple log of dates, times, and the names of anyone who ignores your "no contact" request.

6. **Contact a Victim Service Provider (VSP) if the death involves a crime.**
   **Ask the investigating law enforcement agency to connect you with their victim/witness coordinator.**
   - VSPs can often assist in drafting "no media" statements or acting as a buffer between your family and the press.

7. **Escalate to law enforcement for safety or persistent harassment.**
   **Call 911 immediately if someone is trespassing on your property or making direct threats.**
   - Contact your local police non-emergency line to report persistent stalking or doxxing (the public release of private identifying information).
   - Report cyber-enabled fraud or impersonation to the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether you will ever speak to the media.
- You do not need to correct every rumor or "set the record straight" today.
- You do not need to respond to "urgent" deadlines set by reporters.
- You do not need to deal with long-term digital footprint management or takedown requests yet.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed or invaded when private grief becomes public interest. You have the right to remain silent and to prioritize your family's safety and privacy over the public's curiosity. 

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance intended to reduce immediate intrusion. It is not legal advice. If harassment persists, you may require professional legal or security assistance.

## Important note
Journalists are generally permitted to stand on public sidewalks or roads near your home. However, they are not permitted to trespass on your private property or engage in behavior that constitutes criminal stalking or harassment under state law. If you feel unsafe, contact local law enforcement.
