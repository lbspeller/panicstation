# What to do if… you miss the last train or bus home because a connection was delayed (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, staffed place, then contact the carrier while you are still on-site and ask for tonight’s confirmed plan: rebooking, an alternate route, a safe place to wait, or overnight help if available.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave a staffed, well-lit station or terminal for an isolated area just to look for options.
- Don’t accept rides from strangers or unofficial drivers.
- Don’t spend heavily before asking the carrier what they can do tonight.
- Don’t rely only on the app if you can also speak to staff, security, or official customer support.
- Don’t post your exact location, that you are alone, or where you plan to stay.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the safest available place.**  
   Stay near customer service, security, an information desk, or other passengers in a well-lit area. If anyone is threatening you or following you, move toward staff or security immediately.

2. **Ask the carrier for a written or clear next step.**  
   Say: “My connection was delayed without warning, and I missed the last service home. What is the next available option tonight or tomorrow morning?”  
   Ask them to confirm where you should wait, whether the terminal stays open, and whether they can rebook you or route you another way.

3. **If this was Amtrak, ask specifically about missed-connection support.**  
   Ask whether Amtrak can rebook you on the next available service, arrange alternate transportation, provide food or drink during a long wait, or help with overnight accommodation where available. If station staff cannot resolve it, use Amtrak’s official customer service channels.

4. **If this was an intercity bus, ask for the next available connection.**  
   If your first bus was delayed and that made you miss the connection, ask the bus operator to rebook you onto the next available connection. For Greyhound, check SMS and email because Greyhound says it alerts affected passengers that way.

5. **If this was local transit, check whether the station is closing.**  
   Ask staff or security whether there is a monitored indoor place to wait. If there is not, choose a safer route away from the station, such as an official taxi stand, app-based ride-hail, or pickup from someone you trust.

6. **Verify any ride before getting in.**  
   Match the driver, vehicle, and plate in the app or at the official taxi stand. Share your live location with someone you trust if you can.

7. **If you cannot get home until morning, choose the least-risk overnight option.**  
   Prefer carrier rebooking with a monitored waiting area, a nearby standard hotel reached by official taxi or app-based ride-hail, or pickup by someone you trust.

8. **Use emergency help if your safety changes.**  
   If there is immediate danger, call 911. If it is not an emergency but you feel unsafe, ask station or terminal security to help you stay in a monitored area while you contact the carrier or someone you trust.

9. **Save proof without turning it into a project.**  
   Screenshot delay alerts, schedules showing no more departures, your booking, and any carrier messages. Keep receipts for reasonable essentials such as transport, lodging, and basic food.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide tonight whether to make a complaint or claim money back.
- You don’t need a perfect timeline tonight; screenshots, receipts, and a few notes are enough for now.
- You can handle refunds, reimbursement requests, or formal complaints once you are safe and rested.

## Important reassurance
This is a sudden late-night problem, not something you have to solve perfectly. The immediate goal is only: safe place, confirmed next step, and a way to be reachable.

## Scope note
These are first steps for being stranded after missing the last service because a connection was delayed. Later decisions may depend on the carrier, ticket type, itinerary, local transit rules, and whether the disruption was within the carrier’s control.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, safety, or professional advice. Carrier policies can change, so check the carrier’s current instructions and prioritize emergency services if you are in immediate danger.
