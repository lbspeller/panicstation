# What to do if… your washing machine is leaking and water is spreading across the floor (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the water flow by turning off the valves behind the machine or the main water supply, and avoid all contact with water that is touching electrical outlets or cords.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into standing water if it has reached electrical outlets, baseboard heaters, or power strips.
- Do not touch the washing machine or any metal appliances if you are standing in water.
- Do not attempt to unplug the machine while the floor is wet.
- Do not keep the wash cycle running to try and drain the water.
- Do not ignore water that is moving toward floor vents, stairs, or adjacent units.
- Do not use a wet-dry vacuum or electric pump unless it is plugged into a confirmed dry, GFCI-protected outlet.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the immediate area.** Keep children and pets away from the wet floor to prevent slips or accidental contact with electrical hazards.
2. **Stop the machine only if controls are dry and you are on dry ground.** Press "Stop" or "Pause" on the control panel. If you must step in water to reach the machine, skip this and move to the next step.
3. **Shut off electricity to the laundry area from the breaker box.** Only do this if you can reach your electrical panel without walking through standing water.
   - Locate the breaker labeled "Laundry" or "Washer" and flip it to the "Off" position.
   - If you cannot identify the correct breaker, flip the "Main" breaker to cut power to the entire home.
   - If the electrical panel is in the flooded area, do not enter; call your utility company or an electrician immediately.
4. **Turn off the water supply valves.** Locate the two valves (usually red and blue) on the wall behind the washer.
   - For round knob valves: Turn them clockwise until they stop.
   - For lever-style valves: Turn the lever 90 degrees until it is perpendicular to the water line.
5. **Shut off the main water valve if the washer valves fail.** If the leak continues after closing the washer valves, turn off the water for the entire house at the main shutoff valve (typically located near the water meter or where the main line enters the house).
6. **Contain the water spread.** Place heavy towels or blankets at doorways and around the base of the machine to prevent water from reaching other rooms or soaking into subflooring.
7. **Protect nearby items.** Move rugs, furniture, and electronics away from the path of the water. Lift any power strips or charging cables off the floor if you can do so safely from a dry area.
8. **Notify others if you are in a shared building.** If you live in an apartment or condo, alert the property manager and any neighbors directly below you so they can check for ceiling leaks.
9. **Document the scene.** Take photos of the leak source, the extent of the water spread, and any damaged property for maintenance or insurance records.
10. **Contact professional help.** Call a plumber or appliance repair service to identify the cause of the leak. If water reached electrical components, do not restore power until an electrician has inspected the area.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure (e.g., pump vs. hose) right now.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent replacement for the machine.
- You do not need to fully dry the floor to a professional standard in the first minutes; containment is the priority.
- You do not need to file an insurance claim until the immediate leak is stabilized.

## Important reassurance
A washing machine leak often looks worse than it is because water spreads thin across hard floors. Once the water supply is shut off and power is isolated, the immediate danger is over. Most household leaks are stabilized within minutes by closing the supply valves.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a sudden appliance leak. It does not cover long-term mold remediation, structural drying, or technical appliance repair.

## Important note
This is general information and not a substitute for professional plumbing or electrical services. If you cannot safely reach shutoff points or if water is in contact with live electrical equipment, evacuate the area and contact emergency services or qualified professionals.
