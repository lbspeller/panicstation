# What to do if… your washing machine is leaking and water is spreading across the floor (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the washer only if you can do it from a dry spot, then shut off the washer’s hot and cold water valves. Keep yourself out of standing water near outlets, cords, or electrical equipment.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t step into standing water to unplug cords, touch outlets, or reach the breaker panel.
- Don’t keep the washer running to finish the cycle.
- Don’t pull the washer out while it is powered, moving, or actively leaking.
- Don’t use fans, heaters, dehumidifiers, extension cords, or power strips in the wet area until the outlets and circuits are safe.
- Don’t restart the washer until the hose, valve, drain connection, and surrounding electrical area have been checked.
- Don’t ignore water that may be spreading into a downstairs unit, shared wall, hallway, or adjacent room.

## What to do now
1. **Keep people and pets out of the wet area.** Wet flooring can be slippery, and water near electricity can be dangerous.
2. **Stop the washer from a dry place if you can.** Press pause, cancel, or power on the controls only if you do not have to stand in water.
3. **Stay away from wet electrical items.** If water is near outlets, cords, power strips, the washer plug, or the service panel, do not touch them from the wet area.
4. **Turn off power only from a dry, safe route.** If you can reach the service panel without entering water, turn off the laundry circuit. If you cannot do that safely, leave it alone and contact qualified help.
5. **Turn off the washer’s water supply.** Close both hot and cold shutoff valves behind or near the washer by turning them clockwise. If you cannot reach them safely or the leak does not stop, shut off the home’s main water valve if you can do that from a safe, dry place.
6. **Contain the spread from dry ground.** Use towels to block water from doorways, baseboards, nearby rooms, and shared walls. Put a pan or bowl under an active drip only if you can place it safely.
7. **Move dry items out of the path.** Move rugs, paper items, laundry, chargers, power strips, and small valuables that are not already in standing water.
8. **If you rent or live in a condo, notify the right person.** After the water is stopped and you are safe, contact the landlord, property manager, maintenance line, or HOA. If water may be reaching a downstairs neighbor, notify them too.
9. **Take photos after the immediate danger is controlled.** Photograph the washer, hose area, floor, nearby walls, and any visible water path for maintenance or insurance.
10. **Arrange repair before using the washer again.** Call an appliance repair service or plumber. If water reached outlets, wiring, the washer plug, or the service panel area, have a qualified electrician check the area before power is restored or the washer is used.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact failed part in the first few minutes.
- You do not need to decide about an insurance claim before the water is stopped and the area is safe.
- You do not need to fully dry walls, flooring, or hidden spaces yourself right now.
- You do not need to move the washer if it is unsafe, too heavy, powered, or still leaking.

## Important reassurance
A washer leak spreads fast, so it can feel out of control. Once the water is shut off and you have stayed clear of wet electrical areas, you have handled the most important first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize a sudden washer leak. Water under flooring, inside walls or ceilings, or near electrical equipment may need specialist drying, appliance repair, plumbing, electrical, landlord, HOA, or insurance help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, plumbing, insurance, or other professional advice. If there is fire, smoke, sparks, a burning smell, electric shock, or you cannot safely stay in the area, leave and call 911.
