# What to do if… your employer announces layoffs and you are included without warning (USA)

## Short answer
Ask for the layoff details in writing and do not sign anything under pressure in the meeting. Your first job is to slow the process down enough to confirm your separation reason, dates, final pay, benefits end date, and health coverage options.

## Do not do these things
- Do not quit or agree that this is a voluntary resignation if that is not what happened.
- Do not sign a release, waiver, separation agreement, or acknowledgement under pressure; ask to review it away from the meeting.
- Do not rely on verbal promises about severance, benefits, rehire, references, or dates.
- Do not copy confidential company files, customer data, source code, internal chats, or private company records.
- Do not use work email or work devices for private planning if access may be monitored or cut off.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a written separation summary today.** Request an email or letter stating:
   - whether this is a layoff, reduction in force, termination, or position elimination
   - your effective date
   - your last day worked and last day employed, if different
   - the reason for separation
   - whether severance is being offered, and any conditions
   - what happens to accrued PTO or vacation, if any

2. **Confirm final pay in writing.** Ask when your final paycheck will be issued, what pay period it covers, whether it includes unpaid wages, commission, bonus, expenses, or accrued PTO, and who to contact if something is missing. Federal law does not require immediate final pay, and state rules may require faster payment.

3. **Ask for the separation documents you may need for unemployment.** Ask for a separation letter or written reason for separation that says lack of work, reduction in force, or position elimination if that is true. Save recent paystubs, W-2s, payroll history, and benefits summaries from systems you are allowed to access.

4. **File for unemployment through the correct state system as soon as you are eligible.** You generally file with the unemployment insurance program in the state where you worked. If you worked remotely, worked in more than one state, or live in a different state from the job, use the state system’s instructions or contact your state unemployment office for filing direction.

5. **If this is a larger layoff or site closing, ask a factual WARN question.** Ask HR: “Was a WARN notice issued for this layoff or closing, and can I have a copy?” Federal WARN generally applies to covered employers with 100 or more employees and certain plant closings or mass layoffs; when it applies, it generally requires at least 60 calendar days’ advance written notice, with exceptions and possible state rules.

6. **Confirm health coverage dates before your employer plan ends.** Ask for the exact date your employer health plan ends, the benefits administrator’s contact details, and when COBRA information will be sent. If COBRA applies, you generally have 60 days to elect continuation coverage, starting from the later of when coverage ends or when the COBRA election notice is provided or mailed.

7. **Check Marketplace timing before the window closes.** Losing job-based coverage usually creates a Marketplace special enrollment period. You generally must apply for Marketplace coverage within 60 days of losing job-based coverage, even if you are also eligible for COBRA.

8. **Exit cleanly and keep proof.** Get written instructions for returning laptop, badge, phone, keys, documents, or other equipment. Keep proof of return, such as a photo, receipt, email confirmation, or shipping tracking number.

9. **Use one sentence if they want your signature now.** Say: “I can’t sign today; please send it to me so I can review it.” If the document includes a release of claims, non-disparagement terms, repayment terms, confidentiality terms, or a deadline, consider getting legal or professional review before signing.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to accept severance, elect COBRA, choose a Marketplace plan, move retirement accounts, or make long-term financial decisions.
- You do not need to update social profiles, explain the layoff to everyone, or start a full job search in the meeting.
- You do not need to argue the decision before you have the written dates, reason for separation, pay details, and benefits end date.

## Important reassurance
A sudden layoff can feel personal and unreal, even when it is a business decision affecting many people. Slowing down, asking for written terms, and leaving with your records and dates is a protective response, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first hours and days after being included in layoffs. Employment rules vary by state, employer size, employee status, contract, and the number of employees affected, so later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, tax, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Treat anything unclear as unresolved until you have written confirmation of your status, dates, pay, benefits, and any agreement you are being asked to sign.
