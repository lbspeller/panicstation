# What to do if… you miss a connection because of immigration or document checks and you are now stranded (USA)

## Short answer
Go to your airline’s customer service or transfer desk immediately to request reaccommodation on the next available flight. In the USA, delays caused by government inspections (CBP) are considered "non-controllable," meaning airlines are not legally required to provide hotel or meal vouchers, but they will typically rebook you for free if you are on a single ticket.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the secure side of the airport (if already through security) or the terminal until you have a confirmed rebooking and have located your checked luggage.
- Do not purchase a new ticket from a different airline immediately; this may void the remaining segments of your original itinerary and forfeit your right to free rebooking.
- Do not argue with Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officers about your flight time; they do not have the authority to hold planes or manage airline schedules.
- Do not assume the airline will automatically pay for a hotel; since immigration delays are not the airline's fault, these costs usually fall on the passenger or travel insurance.
- Do not throw away your original boarding passes or any documentation provided by CBP.

## What to do now
1. **Inform the CBP officer of your connection if still in secondary inspection.**  
   - **State clearly that you have a connecting flight and provide the departure time.**  
   - **Request a "Time of Departure" or "Attendance" note if they provide one, though this is not a legal requirement for them.**  
2. **Proceed to the airline's customer service or transfer desk the moment you are released.**  
   - **Locate the desk for the airline operating your missed or upcoming flight.**  
   - **Simultaneously open the airline's mobile app to check for automatic rebooking notifications.**  
3. **Request reaccommodation under the airline's informal "flat tire" or missed connection policy.**  
   - **Explain that the delay was due to government processing (CBP/Immigration).**  
   - **Ask specifically: "Can you reaccommodate me on the next available flight to my destination at no extra charge?"**  
4. **Identify if your travel is on a single itinerary or separate tickets.**  
   - **Single Itinerary:** The airline is generally responsible for getting you to your final destination, even if the delay was non-controllable.  
   - **Separate Tickets:** You have no legal right to free rebooking; ask the agent if they can waive the change fee as a gesture of goodwill.  
5. **Locate your checked baggage.**  
   - **If you cleared customs with your bag but did not re-check it:** Keep the bag with you and take it to the customer service desk.  
   - **If you already re-checked your bag before the delay:** Ask the agent to "track the tag" to see if it is being held at the airport or sent ahead to your destination.  
6. **Determine your overnight status and amenities.**  
   - **Check the "Airline Customer Service Dashboard" (DOT) for your specific carrier.**  
   - **Ask the agent: "Since this was a government delay, do you offer any discounted 'distressed passenger' rates for nearby hotels?"**  
7. **Document the delay for insurance purposes.**  
   - **Take a photo of the flight board showing your missed flight and the current time.**  
   - **Save all digital communications from the airline regarding the missed connection.**  
8. **Contact your travel insurance provider if you have one.**  
   - **Ask what documentation they require to trigger "Trip Interruption" or "Missed Connection" coverage.**  

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Department of Transportation (DOT) right now; this is handled after travel is completed.
- You do not need to decide on a full trip cancellation until you have seen the available rebooking options for the next 24 hours.
- You do not need to apply for DHS TRIP (Redress) immediately; this is a long-term process for passengers who are repeatedly delayed.

## Important reassurance
Missing a flight due to government processing is a standard scenario for airport staff. While it is frustrating, airlines have established protocols (reaccommodation) for these events. Being "stranded" in a major US airport is temporary; there are always subsequent flights, and airport infrastructure is designed to handle overnight stays if necessary.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the minutes and hours following a missed connection due to federal government delays. It does not cover legal challenges to immigration status or long-term compensation claims.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In the USA, immigration and customs delays are considered outside the airline's control. Rights to refunds or vouchers are governed by the airline's "Contract of Carriage" and current DOT enforcement of the 2024/2026 refund rules.
