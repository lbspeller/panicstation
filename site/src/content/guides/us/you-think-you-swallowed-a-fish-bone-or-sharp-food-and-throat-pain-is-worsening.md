# What to do if… you think you swallowed a fish bone or sharp food and throat pain is worsening (USA)

## Short answer
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately for a suspected sharp object ingestion. Sharp items like fish bones require professional evaluation and removal, ideally within 2 to 6 hours, to prevent esophageal perforation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "push the bone down" by swallowing large amounts of bread, rice, or marshmallows.
- Do not induce vomiting, as this can cause a lodged sharp object to tear the esophageal lining.
- Do not perform repeated "test swallows" with food to see if the pain has moved.
- Do not insert fingers or tools into your throat to attempt a manual retrieval.
- Do not wait until the next morning if you are experiencing drooling, chest pain, or an inability to swallow.
- Do not drink large amounts of water or take sedatives while waiting for medical care.

## What to do now
1. **Assess your airway and breathing immediately.** If you are gasping, unable to speak, wheezing, or have a noisy intake of breath (stridor), call 911 right away.
2. **Stop all intake of food and liquids.** Avoid drinking or eating anything to keep your stomach empty in case an emergency endoscopy or surgical procedure is required for removal.
3. **Proceed to an emergency department for evaluation.** Tell the triage staff specifically that you suspect a sharp foreign body ingestion (e.g., a fish or chicken bone) and that the pain is worsening.
   - Mention if you have difficulty swallowing saliva or are drooling.
   - Report any pain that has moved into your chest or neck area.
4. **Identify specific "red flag" symptoms that require immediate 911 transport.** Use emergency services if you experience:
   - Sudden, severe chest or neck pain.
   - Vomiting of blood or blood-streaked saliva.
   - Visible swelling or bruising on the neck.
   - Fever or a rapid heart rate following the incident.
5. **Prepare a brief history for the medical team.** Note exactly what you were eating, approximately when the pain started, and any history of esophageal issues or the use of blood thinners.
6. **Arrange for someone else to drive you to the hospital.** If your pain is escalating or you feel faint, do not operate a vehicle; have a companion drive you or use emergency transport.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the bone is "actually" there or if it is just a scratch; let the medical team use imaging to decide.
- You do not need to try home remedies to "dissolve" the bone with vinegar or other liquids.
- You do not need to contact your primary care physician first if symptoms are acute and worsening.

## Important reassurance
It is very common for sharp food fragments to cause a scratch (abrasion) that feels identical to an object being stuck. However, because a lodged sharp bone can cause a serious tear in the esophagus, seeking an immediate professional check is the standard safety protocol for this specific sensation. It is normal to feel anxious when swallowing is painful, and medical teams are accustomed to evaluating these situations.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization and harm-reduction steps for suspected sharp foreign body ingestion. It does not provide medical diagnosis or instructions for removal, which must be performed by a qualified clinician.

## Important note
Sharp foreign bodies in the esophagus are time-sensitive medical concerns. Delayed treatment can increase the risk of infection or perforation. Always seek professional medical assessment for worsening throat or chest pain after eating.
