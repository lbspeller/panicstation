# What to do if… you think you swallowed a fish bone or sharp food and throat pain is worsening (USA)

## Short answer
If you may have swallowed a fish bone or other sharp food and your throat pain is worsening, stop eating and get urgent medical evaluation, usually at the ER. Call 911 now if you have choking, trouble breathing, noisy breathing, drooling, or cannot swallow saliva.

## Do not do these things
- Do not swallow big bites of bread, rice, marshmallow, or other food to try to push it down.
- Do not keep doing repeated test swallows with solid food.
- Do not stick fingers, utensils, tweezers, or other objects into your throat.
- Do not try to make yourself vomit.
- Do not drink alcohol or take sedating substances to “relax” before you are checked.
- Do not ignore drooling, worsening trouble swallowing, blood, chest pain, neck pain, fever, swelling, or breathing changes.

## What to do now
1. Check breathing first. If you are choking, cannot speak normally, have noisy breathing, have trouble breathing, or feel your airway is blocked, call 911 now.
2. Stop eating solid food. Sit upright and do not force swallowing. If a 911 dispatcher, ER, or clinician tells you not to eat or drink, follow that instruction.
3. Treat worsening throat pain after a suspected sharp bone or sharp food as urgent. Go to the ER now, especially if the pain is escalating or you still feel something may be stuck.
4. Use 911 instead of driving yourself if you feel faint, are vomiting repeatedly, have rapidly worsening pain, have chest or neck pain, are drooling, or have any breathing concern.
5. If you are stable, can swallow saliva, and are unsure what was swallowed, call your clinician’s after-hours nurse line for immediate triage. If you cannot reach a clinician quickly, or symptoms keep worsening, go to the ER.
6. For a child, an unknown swallowed object, or uncertainty about whether the object was poisonous or dangerous, you can call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222. Do not use this to delay 911 or the ER if there are airway symptoms, drooling, chest pain, or worsening sharp-object symptoms.
7. Before you call or travel, write down what you ate, when it happened, what you think was swallowed, what symptoms changed, your medicines, and any history of swallowing or esophagus problems.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it is “just a scratch” or still stuck.
- You do not need to try home remedies to prove what happened.
- You do not need to research tests or procedures before seeking urgent help.
- You do not need to finish the meal, clean up, or explain everything perfectly before getting assessed.

## Important reassurance
A fish bone or sharp food can scratch the throat and cause pain that feels like something is still stuck. Worsening pain, drooling, trouble swallowing, chest or neck pain, or breathing changes are reasons to get checked promptly, not reasons to blame yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk and help you reach appropriate urgent care. Later decisions about examination, imaging, or removal need a clinician.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or a substitute for professional care. For worsening symptoms after a possible sharp swallowed object, urgent assessment is the safer choice.
