# What to do if… a rental vehicle shows a dashboard warning you do not understand before you leave the lot (USA)

## Short answer
Stay parked until the rental company explains the dashboard warning or provides another vehicle. Take a clear photo of the dashboard and speak to the rental counter or exit booth before leaving the lot.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive onto public roads just to see whether the warning clears.
- Don’t assume a warning is harmless because the vehicle appears to drive normally.
- Don’t try to reset the dashboard warning or clear codes yourself.
- Don’t rely only on verbal reassurance if you keep the same vehicle; ask for the warning and the company’s response to be recorded.
- Don’t continue driving if there is smoke, a strong burning smell, an urgent warning message, or another sign of immediate danger.

## What to do now
1. Stay parked in a safe place on the rental property. Put the vehicle in Park, set the parking brake, and avoid blocking traffic.
2. Take a clear photo of the dashboard warning and any message text. Also photograph the odometer and fuel gauge.
3. If safe, wait briefly after startup to see whether the indicator goes out. Some indicators may appear briefly during startup. If the warning stays on, returns, or remains unclear, treat it as something the rental company needs to explain or resolve before departure.
4. Show the photo to the rental counter or exit-booth staff. Say: “This warning appeared before I left the lot. Please record it and arrange another vehicle if it cannot be resolved.”
5. Ask for a vehicle exchange if the warning remains unexplained, staff cannot document the response, or you are not comfortable taking the vehicle.
6. If the company explains the indicator and you decide to keep the same vehicle, ask for the warning and the company’s response to be added to your rental record. Keep any app message, text, email, or updated paperwork.
7. If you notice the warning at an airport exit booth, tell the attendant that you need to stay on the property and ask where to pull over safely for an exchange.
8. If there is smoke, a strong burning smell, an active fire, or another immediate danger, shut off the engine if safe, move away from the vehicle, alert on-site staff or security, and call 911 for an active fire, a medical emergency, or immediate danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact dashboard symbol yourself.
- You do not need to decide whether to request compensation or make a complaint right now.
- You do not need to change your wider travel plans until the rental company has explained the warning or arranged an exchange.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to pause before leaving the lot. Asking the rental company to explain and document the warning, or to provide another vehicle, is a sensible first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Documentation may reduce the chance of a later dispute about a pre-existing issue. Later decisions about charges, refunds, complaints, or travel arrangements may require help from the rental company or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. If the vehicle may be unsafe, stay parked and ask the rental company to address the warning before you leave.
