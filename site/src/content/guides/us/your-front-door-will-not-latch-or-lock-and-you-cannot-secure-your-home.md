# What to do if… your front door will not latch or lock and you cannot secure your home (USA)

## Short answer
If you cannot secure your main entry, move to a safer location or room, establish a temporary interior-only barrier that does not block your exit, and immediately contact your landlord or a verified locksmith. Call 911 if there is an active threat or someone is attempting to force entry.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the property unattended while the main entry door is unsecured.
- Do not use a permanent or heavy barricade that could trap you inside during a fire or emergency.
- Do not attempt to take the lock apart yourself if you are unfamiliar with the hardware, as this can make the door impossible to close at all.
- Do not post about your security vulnerability or your location on public social media.
- Do not pay a locksmith in full before the work is completed and the door is tested.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the immediate security failure.** Check if the latch is "mushy" and won't spring out, if the key is spinning without resistance, or if the door has sagged so the bolt hits the metal strike plate instead of the hole.
2. **Contact your landlord or property manager immediately if you rent.** State clearly that the "warranty of habitability" is affected because the unit cannot be secured; many states, including California and New York, classify this as an emergency requiring a response within 24 hours.
3. **Establish a temporary internal-only stop.** Use a rubber door wedge or a heavy piece of furniture pushed against the door, provided it can be moved in seconds if you need to evacuate.
4. **Verify other entry points are secure.** Lock all windows, secondary doors, and the door leading from the garage to the house.
5. **Secure a reputable locksmith if you own the home.** Look for a provider with a local physical address and ask for a total price range—including service call, labor, and parts—before they depart for your location.
6. **Request to see the locksmith’s identification and license upon arrival.** In states that require licensing (such as California, Oregon, or Virginia), a legitimate locksmith should provide their credentials immediately.
7. **Document the failure for insurance or lease records.** Take a 10-second video of the door failing to latch and any visible damage to the frame or hardware.
8. **Stay in a safe, well-lit part of the home with your phone charged.** If you feel unsafe remaining in the property while it is unsecured, relocate to a trusted neighbor’s home or a hotel until repairs are made.

## What can wait
- You do not need to research high-end security upgrades or "smart" locks while in the middle of a failure.
- You do not need to determine who is financially responsible for the repair (tenant vs. landlord) before the door is physically secured.
- You do not need to fix cosmetic damage to the door or frame tonight.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of vulnerability when a door won't lock. Most lock failures are mechanical—due to building settling or simple wear—rather than a deliberate security breach. Most locksmiths or maintenance teams can restore basic security quickly once they are on-site.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a mechanical door failure. It does not provide legal strategies for rent withholding or long-term home security planning.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving an active intruder or immediate threat to life, call 911 immediately.
