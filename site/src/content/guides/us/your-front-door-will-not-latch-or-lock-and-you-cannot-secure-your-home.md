# What to do if… your front door will not latch or lock and you cannot secure your home (USA)
## Short answer
If you cannot secure your home, treat it as urgent: get to a safer pause, use only a temporary barrier you can remove fast, then contact your landlord or property manager if you rent, or a reputable locksmith if you own.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore immediate danger; call 911 if someone is trying to enter, threatening you, or you feel unsafe right now.
- Don’t leave the home empty with the main entry door unsecured unless you have a safer reason to leave.
- Don’t barricade the door in a way that traps you inside or blocks a fire escape route.
- Don’t authorize expensive lock drilling, replacement, or door work while panicking without a written estimate.
- Don’t post publicly that your home is unsecured or that you are alone.
- Don’t assume there is one nationwide tenant repair rule; landlord and tenant duties vary by state, city, and lease.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate danger.** If someone is trying to enter, has threatened you, or you feel unsafe right now, call 911.
2. **Get to a safer pause.** If you are alone and uneasy, call a trusted person to come over, go to a trusted neighbor, or move away from the entry door while you arrange help.
3. **Check other easy entry points.** Lock other exterior doors, accessible windows, garage-to-house doors, and sliding doors. Close blinds or curtains if you are visible from outside.
4. **Use only a temporary inside barrier you can remove quickly.** A door wedge or heavy object may help for the moment, but only if it does not block your exit and you can move it immediately.
5. **Make a quick record.** Take a short video showing the latch or lock failing, the door gap, the strike plate, and any loose handle or visible damage. Note the date and time.
6. **If you rent, contact your landlord or property manager now.** Use the emergency maintenance line if there is one. Also send a text, email, or app message saying: “The front door will not latch or lock, and I cannot secure the unit.” Keep a copy.
7. **If you need a locksmith, slow the call down.** Ask for the business name, local address or service area, call-out fee, labor estimate, parts estimate, and what they will try before drilling or replacing parts. Ask for a written estimate before work starts.
8. **If the locksmith price changes sharply on arrival, pause.** Ask them to explain the change in writing before they start. If it does not make sense and you are not in immediate danger, call another provider.
9. **If it is not an emergency but you need police guidance, use a local non-emergency route.** Search for your local police department’s non-emergency number or online reporting page.
10. **Choose the safest place for the night.** If the home still cannot be secured and staying feels unsafe, go to a trusted person’s home. If you stay, keep your phone charged and keep a clear exit path.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight about upgrades such as a new door, smart lock, camera, or alarm system.
- You do not need to settle cost responsibility tonight; focus on getting the home secure and keeping records.
- You can deal later with door alignment, strike plate adjustment, replacement hardware, reimbursement, insurance, or a complaint route.
- If you rent and the landlord does not respond, checking local tenant rules, housing inspection, or code enforcement can usually wait until the immediate security problem is contained, unless you are unsafe.

## Important reassurance
A door that will not latch or lock can feel instantly unsafe, even when the cause is something ordinary like a shifted strike plate, loose handle, swollen door, or worn latch. The immediate goal is not to solve everything; it is to make the next hour safer and get the right repair channel moving.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about tenant rights, repair responsibility, reimbursement, insurance, complaints, or permanent security changes may need local housing, legal, insurance, or repair help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, safety, insurance, or professional repair advice. If there is immediate danger, call 911.
