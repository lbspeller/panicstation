# What to do if… you lose your paper boarding pass for a connection and cannot access your booking on your phone (USA)

## Short answer
Go to your airline’s kiosk, customer service desk, ticket counter, or gate agent and ask for a reprint or reissue of your boarding pass. Have your photo ID ready and tell them you are connecting.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the secure area just to find a printer unless airline, airport, or TSA staff tell you to.
- Don’t buy a new ticket in a panic because your booking is not showing on your phone.
- Don’t spend your boarding window guessing random confirmation codes at a kiosk.
- Don’t hand your ID, phone, payment card, or passport to anyone who is not an airline, airport, or TSA official.
- Don’t wait for the final boarding call before asking for help.

## What to do now
1. **Work out whether you are before or after security.**
   - **Before security:** go to your airline’s kiosk or ticket counter.
   - **After security:** go to the gate for your connecting flight, an airline customer service desk, or another staffed gate for the same airline.

2. **Use the shortest official route to your airline.** Say: “I’m connecting, I lost my paper boarding pass, and I can’t access my booking on my phone. Can you reprint or reissue my boarding pass? I have my ID.”

3. **Give details that can help them find the booking.** Start with your name as it appears on your ID, your destination, your connecting route, the flight date, and any flight number you remember. If you have a passport or another accepted ID with you, keep it ready.

4. **Try the kiosk only if it is quick.** A kiosk may let you pull up the trip with a confirmation code, frequent flyer number, credit card used for the booking, passport, or other airline-supported method. If it does not work quickly, stop and go to a staffed airline desk or gate.

5. **If you are at a TSA checkpoint, follow TSA instructions but still deal with the airline.** Some checkpoints can verify identity and flight details from an ID, but this does not replace airline check-in and it does not give you a boarding pass for the gate. You still need the airline to reprint or reissue the boarding pass before you board.

6. **If your phone is the problem, do not make that the main task.** Use airport Wi-Fi or a charging point only if it is immediately available. If not, go straight to the airline and let staff try to retrieve the reservation from your ID and trip details.

7. **If you checked bags, say that immediately.** Ask whether your bags are checked through to the final destination and whether anything changes if your boarding pass is reissued or you are moved to another flight.

8. **Before you leave the counter or gate, check the new pass.** Confirm the name, flight number, destination, date, departure time, gate, and seat if one is assigned. Then go straight to the gate or security line you were directed to.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix the airline app, email account, roaming, password, or phone storage right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to complain, seek a refund, or change later travel plans while the connection is still possible.
- You do not need to reconstruct the whole booking history if the airline can find the reservation from your ID and trip details.

## Important reassurance
Lost boarding passes are a routine airport problem. If you are on a valid booking, airline staff usually have ways to reprint or reissue the boarding pass once they can find the reservation and confirm who you are.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get a valid boarding pass and keep moving. If you miss the connection, are rebooked, or have baggage problems, later decisions may need airline help, travel insurance support, or another specialist contact once the immediate pressure has passed.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, financial, or professional advice. TSA and airline procedures can vary by airport, airline, route, and passenger situation, so follow instructions from TSA, airport, and airline staff on site.
