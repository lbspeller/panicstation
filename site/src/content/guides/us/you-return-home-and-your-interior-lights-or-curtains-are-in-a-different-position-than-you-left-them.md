# What to do if… you return home and your interior lights or curtains are in a different position than you left them (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter the home. Move to a safe location out of sight of the property and call 911 immediately if you suspect an intruder is present.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go inside to "just check" or verify if anything is missing.
- Do not shout into the home or announce your presence.
- Do not touch doorknobs, window frames, or any items that appear moved.
- Do not attempt to block exits or confront anyone leaving the property.
- Do not park your vehicle where it blocks your own path of escape.

## What to do now
1. **Immediately create distance between yourself and the home.** Move to a location where you cannot be seen from the ground-floor windows or doors, such as a neighbor’s house, a nearby business, or a locked vehicle parked a safe distance away.
2. **Call 911 if you believe the situation is in progress or if you feel unsafe.** Tell the dispatcher your exact location and explain that you have returned home to find evidence of an unauthorized entry (such as moved curtains or lights). 
   - State clearly if you believe someone is currently inside.
   - Provide a description of any unfamiliar vehicles or people near the property.
3. **Check security camera or smart home apps only from your safe position.** Do not move closer to the home to get a better signal or view. If the footage shows an intruder or a point of entry, relay this information to the emergency dispatcher.
4. **Wait for law enforcement to arrive and "clear" the property.** Do not re-enter the home until officers explicitly tell you it is safe to do so. If you have a monitored alarm system, you may notify the company that you are waiting outside for police.
5. **Preserve the scene once you are permitted to enter.** Avoid touching surfaces near the entry point or any items that were disturbed until the police have finished their initial walkthrough and documentation.
6. **Request a case or incident report number from the responding officers.** Ask for the best way to obtain a formal copy of the report for insurance or rental purposes later.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the motive for the entry or if it was "definitely" a crime right now.
- You do not need to perform a full inventory of your belongings immediately.
- You do not need to contact your insurance provider until the property is secured.
- You do not need to decide on long-term security upgrades or lock replacements tonight.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of dread or physical "fight-or-flight" symptoms when your home environment has been disturbed. Trusting your observation that something is "off" and prioritizing your physical safety over checking your property is a correct and protective response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for a suspected security breach. It does not cover long-term legal, insurance, or investigative procedures.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization only and does not constitute legal advice. If you suspect a crime is in progress or your safety is at risk, always prioritize immediate evacuation and contact 911.
