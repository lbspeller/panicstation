# What to do if… your private nude photos or videos are being shared online without your consent (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a private, safer pause, save the links and a basic screenshot if you can, then start takedown reports on the platforms where the posts appear. If you are being threatened with physical harm, stalked, or someone is nearby and unsafe, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t negotiate, send money, send more images, or keep replying to the person posting or threatening you.
- Don’t click payment links, “verification” links, or sign-in links they send.
- Don’t share passwords, one-time codes, or recovery codes.
- Don’t reply publicly to accounts sharing it; that can draw more attention to the post.
- Don’t forward, repost, or upload the images or videos to others as proof.
- Don’t delete accounts, chats, or devices in panic before saving the basic reporting information you may need.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause first.**  
   Move away from comments and messages for a minute. Sit somewhere private if you can. If there are threats to your physical safety, stalking, or your address or personal details have been posted, call 911 if you may be in immediate danger.

2. **Save only the basics needed for takedown reports.**  
   Save the post links, usernames, and a screenshot if you can. Do not download, forward, or re-upload the image or video.

3. **Report the post on each platform or website.**  
   Use the platform’s route for non-consensual intimate imagery, intimate content shared without consent, sexual extortion, harassment, impersonation, or deepfake sexual content, whichever is closest. If friends want to help, ask them to report the post, not comment on it or share it.

4. **Contact specialist help for takedown guidance.**  
   Contact the Cyber Civil Rights Initiative Image Abuse Helpline at 1-844-878-2274. CCRI lists it as free and available 24/7. Do not send images or confidential details through a general contact form.

5. **Use StopNCII if the image or video was of you as an adult.**  
   StopNCII can create a digital fingerprint, called a hash, from intimate images or videos on your device. The image or video stays on your device. Participating platforms can look for matching hashes and may remove matching content if it violates their intimate image abuse policy. It cannot remove content from the whole internet.

6. **Use NCMEC routes if you are under 18, or the image or video was taken before you were 18.**  
   Do not share or upload the content to anyone to show what happened. You can report child sexual exploitation concerns to the NCMEC CyberTipline. You can also use NCMEC’s Take It Down service for nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos taken before you were 18.

7. **If there are extortion demands, threats, or ongoing harassment, consider an official report.**  
   You can contact local law enforcement if you want to report. For cyber-enabled crime, you can also consider filing with the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center. Use official websites only, because scammers sometimes imitate help or reporting services.

8. **Reduce incoming contact after the basics are saved.**  
   Once you have links, usernames, and a screenshot if possible, block, mute, or restrict the person if that helps stop incoming threats. If blocking could make them escalate, mute or restrict first and ask the helpline or a trusted support person to help you decide.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to post a public response.
- You do not need to confront the person who posted it.
- You do not need to explain everything to everyone today.
- You do not need to find every repost in one sitting; start with the biggest platforms and the posts causing the most immediate harm.
- You do not need perfect evidence to start takedowns; links, usernames, and a basic screenshot are enough to begin.

## Important reassurance
This is a violation, and panic, numbness, anger, or shame can all happen after something like this. You did not cause someone else to share private sexual content without consent. Even when posts are spreading, reporting, takedowns, specialist support, and hash-matching can still reduce reach and harm.

## Scope note
These are first steps for immediate safety, reporting, and takedown action only. Later decisions about legal options, school or workplace support, ongoing harassment, account security, or emotional support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information only, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
