# What to do if… someone pressures you to drop a complaint by offering money or favours (USA)
## Short answer

You do not have to respond, agree, sign anything, or make a decision in the moment. End the conversation if you safely can, and move the contact into a safer support or complaint channel when you are ready.

## Do not do these things

- Do not agree, sign, or make a rushed decision while you feel pressured.
- Do not meet alone with the person if you feel unsafe or overwhelmed.
- Do not let anyone push you into explaining or defending your decision.
- Do not assume you have to handle this alone.

## What to do now

1. **End the conversation if you safely can.**  
   You can say: “I am not making a decision about this now.” You do not need to explain further.

2. **Move to a safer pause.**  
   Leave the room, stop replying, mute the contact, or ask a trusted person to stay with you while you decide what feels manageable.

3. **Keep any existing records if it feels safe and manageable.**  
   You may want to keep existing messages or records in case you want the option to use them later.

4. **Tell a support person or specialist service.**  
   RAINN can offer confidential 24/7 support by phone, chat, or text. You do not have to decide whether to continue the complaint or make any additional report before asking for support.

5. **Consider telling the contact already handling your complaint.**  
   When you feel ready, you can send a brief message saying that someone approached you about dropping or changing the complaint and ask what safer communication options are available.

6. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.**  
   If there is no immediate danger and you choose to make a police report, use the local law enforcement agency’s non-emergency route.

## What can wait

You do not need to decide in this moment whether to continue the complaint, accept or reject any proposal, make an additional report, or work out what the approach legally counts as. The immediate priorities are a safer pause, keeping any existing records if that feels manageable, and getting support.

## Important reassurance

Feeling confused, pressured, or thrown off is understandable. You do not need to make another decision immediately, and taking time before responding is reasonable.

## Scope note

These are first steps only. Later decisions about complaint procedures, workplace or school processes, police reports, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note

This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Procedures and legal options can depend on the facts and setting, so ask a specialist service or qualified professional for advice about later decisions.
