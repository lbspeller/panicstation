# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to go inside a building, shop, or vehicle and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter the building, shop, or vehicle they are pressuring you to enter. Move toward a visible staffed place or nearby people, create distance, and call 911 if you feel threatened, trapped, or unable to get away safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into a doorway, back room, hallway, stairwell, alley, parking lot edge, or vehicle to keep the conversation going.
- Do not unlock your car or hand over your phone while the person is close to you.
- Do not follow them to an ATM, around the back of a building, or somewhere quieter.
- Do not stay to argue about their intentions or to avoid seeming rude.
- Do not try to take photos, record video, or gather details if doing so keeps you close to the person or slows your move to safety.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a visible safer pause.** Step away from the person and from the doorway or vehicle. Move toward a different staffed business, a checkout line, a front desk, security staff, or a group of people.
2. **Use clear words while you keep moving.** Say: “No. I’m not going.” You do not need to debate or explain. If they follow you or block your path and drawing attention feels safe, say loudly: “I need help. Call 911.”
3. **Ask one nearby person for specific help.** Point to a staff member or bystander and say: “Please stay with me and call 911. This person is trying to move me somewhere and I feel unsafe.”
4. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger, feel threatened or trapped, are being blocked, or need urgent help to get away safely.** Give your location, including the building name, entrance, nearby landmark, or cross streets. Say what is happening and describe the person or vehicle if you can. Answer the call-taker’s questions and follow their instructions.
5. **Text 911 only if you cannot safely make a voice call.** Text-to-911 availability varies by area, and a voice call is usually the best way to reach 911. If you receive a bounce-back message saying the text was not delivered, call 911 if you can or ask someone nearby to call.
6. **If a vehicle is involved, move away from the door area.** Do not unlock your own car while the person is close. If you are already inside a rideshare, taxi, or other vehicle and can safely get out, ask to stop at a busy, visible place. If you cannot safely get out, call 911 if you can. Text 911 where available if you cannot safely make a voice call.
7. **Once you are at a safer pause, contact someone you trust.** Ask them to stay on the phone, meet you, or help arrange a safe way to leave. Do not return alone to retrieve belongings while the person is still nearby.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to report the incident or take any further action.
- You do not need to explain yourself, work out the person’s intentions, or decide whether you were overreacting.
- You can write down a description, vehicle details, the time, and the location after you are away from the person.
- You can ask a business about retaining camera footage later, once you are safe.
- Retrieving belongings and changing your plans can wait until you have support and a safer route.

## Important reassurance
Feeling unsafe is enough reason not to enter a building, shop, or vehicle with someone. You can move away and seek help without first proving that the situation is dangerous or explaining your decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: create distance, reach a visible safer pause, involve nearby people, and contact emergency services if needed. Later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911 right away.
