# What to do if… you hear buzzing, crackling, or sizzling from your electrical panel or fuse box (USA)

## Short answer
Buzzing, sizzling, or crackling from an electrical panel or fuse box can mean overheating or arcing. If you see smoke, flames, sparks, melting, scorch marks, or smell strong burning, get everyone out and call 911; if there is no immediate fire sign and you can safely reach the main breaker, switch it off and call a licensed electrician urgently.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remove the panel cover, expose wiring, or touch anything inside the panel.
- Do not touch the panel if it is hot, wet, smoking, sparking, melted, scorched, or making a loud crackling sound.
- Do not keep resetting breakers to test what happens.
- Do not use water on an electrical fire.
- Do not plug in more devices or keep using outlets, switches, or appliances that were buzzing, flickering, hot, or smelling burnt.
- Do not assume it is harmless because the lights are still on.

## What to do now
1. Check from a safe distance for smoke, sparks, flames, melting, scorch marks, a strong burning or hot-plastic smell, or heat you can notice without touching the panel.
2. If you see or smell signs of fire or active burning:
   - Get everyone outside and away from the building.
   - Call 911 and say there may be an electrical fire or electrical panel hazard.
   - Do not go back inside until responders say it is safe.
3. If there are no fire signs and you can reach the breaker handle without touching a hot, wet, damaged, smoking, or sparking panel:
   - Switch off the main breaker.
   - Step back immediately after switching it off.
   - Remember that some service parts may still be energized even when the main breaker is off.
4. If you are unsure which breaker is the main breaker, or reaching it would put you close to buzzing, crackling, smoke, heat, water, or damaged parts, do not guess. Step away and call 911 if there is immediate danger, or call a licensed electrician urgently if there is no fire sign.
5. Keep people and pets away from the panel, the area around it, and any outlets, switches, lights, or appliances that were flickering, buzzing, hot, or smelling burnt.
6. Call a licensed electrician urgently. Say you heard buzzing, sizzling, or crackling from the electrical panel or fuse box, describe any smell, heat, sparks, flickering lights, or tripped breakers, and say whether the main breaker is off.
7. If the issue appears to involve the meter, service mast, service drop, outdoor service equipment, or a damaged line outside, do not touch it. Keep clear and call your electric utility’s emergency number; if there is sparking, fire, smoke, or immediate danger, call 911.
8. Leave power off until a licensed electrician, or the utility for utility-owned equipment, says it is safe. If power is restored by someone else and the noise returns, move away from the panel; call 911 if there are fire signs, or call the electrician or utility again if there are no fire signs.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide today whether the panel needs repair, replacement, or an upgrade.
- You do not need to test appliances one by one while the panel may be unsafe.
- You do not need to move belongings near the panel if doing so brings you close to heat, smoke, sparks, water, or exposed electrical parts.

## Important reassurance
Taking the sound seriously is reasonable. Stepping away, shutting power off only if it is safe to do so, and getting urgent professional help are normal protective steps for this kind of electrical warning sign.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions about inspection, repair, replacement, insurance, rental responsibilities, or utility equipment may need help from a licensed electrician, the electric utility, a landlord, an insurer, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not electrical, legal, insurance, or professional advice. If there is any sign of fire, active sparking, smoke, strong burning smell, or you feel unsafe, prioritize getting out and calling emergency services.
