# What to do if… you receive a request for money “for funeral costs” from someone you do not fully trust (USA)

## Short answer
Do not send money or share personal information immediately. Verify the funeral arrangements independently with the service provider and only contribute using non-reversible methods directly to the funeral home if you choose to help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not transfer funds under pressure from emotional, urgent, or threatening language.
- Do not use wire transfers, cryptocurrency, gift cards, or Peer-to-Peer (P2P) apps like Venmo or Zelle for unverified requests.
- Do not click links in messages that claim to lead to an invoice, obituary, or payment portal.
- Do not provide sensitive data such as bank logins, Social Security numbers, or one-time passcodes to "verify" your identity.
- Do not engage in a back-and-forth negotiation with the requester if you suspect fraud.

## What to do now
1. **Apply a communication pause.**  
   **Respond with a single, neutral statement indicating you will review the request later.**  
   - Example: "I have received this and will look into it once I am at my computer."  
   - Do not commit to a specific time or amount.

2. **Collect verifiable details in writing.**  
   **Ask the requester for specific identifiers that can be checked against public records.**  
   - Request the full name of the deceased.  
   - Request the name and city of the funeral home or crematory.  
   - Request the scheduled date of the service.

3. **Verify the funeral home independently.**  
   **Locate the funeral home’s official contact information using a search engine or directory rather than provided links.**  
   - Call the funeral home directly.  
   - Ask if they are handling the arrangements for the named individual.  
   - Ask for their official payment procedures for third-party donations or contributions.  
   - Note: Under the FTC Funeral Rule, providers must give you pricing and service information over the phone.

4. **Use secure, direct payment methods only.**  
   **If you decide to contribute, bypass the requester and pay the provider directly.**  
   - Pay the funeral home using a credit card through their official, encrypted website portal.  
   - Request an itemized receipt from the funeral home for your records.  
   - Avoid paying any "middleman" or individual requester directly.

5. **Initiate a fraud report with your financial institution if funds were sent.**  
   **Contact your bank or payment app support immediately to report a suspected scam.**  
   - Ask for a "hold harmless" letter or a transaction recall if a wire was sent.  
   - Flag the specific username or handle on P2P apps (Venmo, Cash App, Zelle).

6. **Preserve a record of the request.**  
   **Take screenshots of the message, the sender's profile, and any payment handles provided.**  
   - Save these to a secure folder.  
   - Do not continue clicking links or downloading attachments from the sender.

7. **Report the incident to federal authorities.**  
   **Submit the details of the request to the appropriate fraud monitoring agencies.**  
   - File a report at ReportFraud.ftc.gov.  
   - For internet-based requests, file a complaint at ic3.gov (FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center).  
   - If you are over age 60, call the National Elder Fraud Hotline at 833-372-8311 for assistance with the reporting process.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confirm if the person is "actually" grieving or if the death is real before choosing not to pay.
- You do not need to explain your verification process or "prove" why you are hesitant.
- You do not need to decide on the future of the relationship with the requester right now.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency or guilt when someone mentions a death, but professional scammers frequently use grief as a tool to bypass your usual caution. Taking time to verify a business transaction is a standard safety practice and does not mean you are being unsupportive.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and financial protection. It does not cover legal disputes over estates or long-term bereavement support.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute legal or financial advice. If you are being physically threatened or extorted, contact local law enforcement via their non-emergency line, or call 911 only if there is an immediate threat to your physical safety.
