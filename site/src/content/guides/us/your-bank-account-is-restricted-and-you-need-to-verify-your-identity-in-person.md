# What to do if… your bank account is restricted and you need to verify your identity in person (USA)

## Short answer
Use only an official bank phone number or the bank’s app or website, reach the fraud, security, or verification team, and ask exactly what is needed to restore access. Tell them clearly that you cannot access funds for essentials and ask what limited, bank-approved access or hardship option may be available while verification is pending.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t share passcodes, login links, one-time codes, or “verification codes” with anyone who contacted you first.
- Don’t pay a third party who claims they can unlock or expedite bank verification.
- Don’t keep trying blocked transfers, card transactions, or repeated logins after the bank says access is restricted.
- Don’t send identity documents through email, text, or upload links unless the bank confirms the secure channel through its official app, website, or phone number.
- Don’t ignore rent, utilities, medication, insurance, or other essentials that may fail while the account is restricted.
- Don’t assume the restriction will clear without a named next step, a case number, or written confirmation from the bank.

## What to do now
1. **Contact the bank through an official route.**
   - Call the number on the back of your card, use the bank’s official app, or type the bank’s website address yourself.
   - Ask for the fraud, security, or identity verification team.
   - Write down the date, time, department, name or ID of the person you spoke with, and any case or reference number.

2. **Ask exactly what will restore access.**
   - Ask what identity documents are acceptable.
   - Ask whether proof of address is needed.
   - Ask whether you need an appointment, whether walk-ins are accepted, and which branches can complete the verification.
   - Ask whether the bank can share the general reason for the restriction, such as routine identity review, suspected fraud, an account mismatch, or another security issue.

3. **Ask for a hardship or essentials option while verification is pending.**
   - Say plainly: “I cannot access funds for essentials while this is pending.”
   - Ask whether the bank can allow a limited cash withdrawal, limited card access, a cashier’s check, a branch-managed withdrawal, or another bank-approved workaround.
   - Ask for the hardship request to be added to the case notes.
   - If the bank says no, ask when the decision can be reviewed and what single action would make access possible.

4. **If you cannot get to a branch quickly, ask about approved alternatives.**
   - If you are ill, disabled, travelling, working during branch hours, or far from a branch, ask whether the bank supports secure video verification, notarized documents, certified copies, or another approved process.
   - If the answer is still branch only, ask which branch can do it soonest and what exact documents to bring.
   - Ask the bank to confirm the requirements in writing through secure message, letter, or email from an official bank channel.

5. **Protect the next 7 days of essentials.**
   - List only the next 7 days of rent, utilities, transport, medication, food, childcare, insurance, and debt payments.
   - Contact the most urgent billers and ask for a short extension, fee waiver, or alternate payment method.
   - If wages, benefits, or other payments are due soon, ask the payer whether a future payment can be delayed or redirected through their approved process to another account in your own name that you can access.
   - Do not redirect money to someone else’s account unless you understand the risk and trust the arrangement.

6. **Create a formal complaint trail with the bank.**
   - Tell the bank you want to open a formal complaint about being unable to access your funds while verification is pending.
   - Ask for the complaint or case number.
   - Ask for written confirmation of what is required, what access is blocked, what access may still be allowed, and when the bank will next review the case.

7. **Use external complaint routes if the bank cannot give you a clear path.**
   - You can file a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau about a checking, savings, card, or other consumer financial account issue.
   - You can also complain to the bank’s primary regulator if you can identify it.
   - If you are not sure who regulates the bank, use FDIC BankFind or an official regulator complaint page to help route the complaint.

8. **If identity theft or account takeover may be involved, secure that issue separately.**
   - If the bank mentions suspicious activity, unauthorized access, or identity concerns, change passwords from a safe device and use the bank’s official fraud route.
   - Follow official identity-theft steps while continuing the bank’s verification process.
   - Keep copies of bank messages, complaint numbers, and any fraud reports in one place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to close the account, switch banks, sue, or move all your banking.
- You do not need to solve every missed payment at once; focus first on essentials due in the next 7 days.
- You do not need to prove your whole financial history right now; focus on the exact verification items the bank says will restore access.
- Longer-term credit cleanup, account switching, and legal options can wait until immediate cash access and essential bills are more stable.

## Important reassurance
A bank requiring in-person verification does not automatically mean you did anything wrong. These restrictions can happen because of identity checks, fraud controls, compliance reviews, or account information mismatches. The most useful next move is to get the exact requirement in writing, ask for an essentials workaround, and keep a clear complaint trail.

## Scope note
This guide is first steps only. Later decisions about disputes, legal claims, switching banks, benefits payments, credit reporting, or financial planning may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, security, or professional advice. Bank policies and regulator routes vary, and some restrictions may involve security or compliance rules the bank cannot fully explain.
