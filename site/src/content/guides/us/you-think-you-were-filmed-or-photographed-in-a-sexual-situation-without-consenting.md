# What to do if… you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without consenting (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a physically safe location, then contact a specialist helpline to initiate takedown procedures under the Take It Down Act before engaging with the suspected individual or responding to threats.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay any money, purchase gift cards, or provide additional images to meet demands.
- Don’t continue communicating with the person making threats or attempt to bargain.
- Don’t send the content to other people to verify what it is, as this increases the spread.
- Don’t confront the person you suspect while you are alone or in a state of high distress.
- Don’t search for the content repeatedly once you have the necessary reporting information.
- Don’t delete the original messages or threats if you intend to report to law enforcement later.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your immediate physical safety.** If you are currently with the person you suspect, leave the area or go to a public space with other people. If you feel you are in immediate physical danger, call **911**.
2. **Contact the CCRI Crisis Helpline at 844-878-2274.** This free, 24 / 7 service provides confidential support and guidance on image documentation and removal processes. You do not need physical proof of the images to call for advice.
3. **Use the NCMEC "Take It Down" tool if the images show you as a minor.** If the content was created when you were under age 18, this service allows you to anonymously "hash" the files so participating platforms can block them.
   - If you do not have the files, use the NCMEC CyberTipline at **report.cybertip.org** or call **1-800-843-5678**.
4. **Use StopNCII.org if you were age 18 or older when the images were taken.** This tool creates a digital fingerprint of the files on your device (without uploading them) to prevent sharing on participating platforms.
5. **Submit a formal removal request to the platform where the content is hosted.** Under the Take It Down Act (2026), "covered platforms" are required to remove non-consensual intimate visual depictions within 48 hours of a valid request.
   - A valid request generally includes your identification, confirmation of a lack of consent, and the location (URL) of the content.
6. **Report extortion or blackmail to the FBI.** If you are being threatened with the release of images in exchange for money or more content, file a report at **ic3.gov** or call your local FBI field office. 
7. **Document the evidence without re-sharing it.** Save URLs, usernames, timestamps, and screenshots that include the browser's address bar. Store these in a secure, private folder.
8. **Report platform non-compliance to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC).** If a platform fails to remove the content within the 48-hour legal window after a valid request, file a report at **reportfraud.ftc.gov**.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide immediately whether to file a local police report.
- You don’t need to have a "perfect" collection of evidence before asking for help.
- You don’t need to identify every single site where the content might exist right now.
- You don’t need to explain the situation to friends, family, or your employer today.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of panic, nausea, or being "frozen" is a physiological response to a violation of your privacy. These reactions are common when personal boundaries are crossed. You have the right to take a pause and move through these steps at a pace that feels manageable for you.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for the immediate aftermath of a suspected non-consensual recording. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, state-specific civil litigation, or ongoing therapeutic support.

## Important note
This guide contains general information and is not a substitute for legal advice or professional security consultation. Requirements for "covered platforms" and enforcement thresholds under the Take It Down Act may vary based on platform size and specific content types.
