# What to do if… a stranger claims you damaged their car and insists you move to a quieter location (USA)

## Short answer
Do not follow the stranger to a secluded place. If you need to move for immediate safety, go only to a nearby visible public place when you can do so safely, keep your doors locked, and call 911 if anyone is injured or you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow them to a quiet street, back lot, alley, or isolated place.
- Do not get out of your vehicle if you feel unsafe.
- Do not hand over your phone or let the stranger take original documents out of your control.
- Do not share extra personal information under pressure.
- Do not admit fault or agree to pay cash under pressure.
- Do not drive home while you believe you are being followed.
- If there may have been contact between the vehicles, do not leave without using a safe way to handle any state-required stop or information exchange.

## What to do now
1. **Use the safest nearby pause you reasonably can.** Do not follow the stranger to a secluded place. If the current position is unsafe because of traffic or immediate danger, move only as needed to a nearby visible public place when you can do so safely. If you call 911, tell the dispatcher where you are and follow their instructions.
2. **Call 911 for injury or immediate danger.** Tell the dispatcher your location, that someone is pressuring you to relocate, whether you are still moving, and whether the other vehicle is following or blocking you.
3. **State one clear boundary from inside your locked vehicle.** Say: “I am not going somewhere secluded. I will stay here or follow the dispatcher’s instructions.”
4. **Use a safer information-exchange method.** Requirements vary by state. When it is safe, display, exchange, or record the information required in your state in a way that lets you keep control of your documents. This may include driver’s license, vehicle registration, insurance, vehicle, and contact information.
5. **Record the basics without escalating the situation.** Note the other vehicle’s plate, make, model, color, the location, and the time. Take photographs or save dashcam footage only if you can do so without getting out or escalating the situation.
6. **Use local law enforcement’s official route when appropriate.** If there is no immediate danger, contact local law enforcement using its official non-emergency route once you are safe. Ask whether a report is needed and what state rules apply.
7. **If you are being followed, avoid driving home.** Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who was at fault right now.
- You do not need to negotiate payment, sign anything, or continue an argument.
- You do not need to decide whether the accusation was a mistake, a scam, or something else.
- You can contact your insurer and check your state motor-vehicle reporting rules once you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Pressure to move somewhere secluded may be unsafe, regardless of the other person’s intent. Choosing a visible place, keeping the interaction brief, and asking for help are reasonable safety steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later reporting, insurance, and legal decisions may depend on your state and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow emergency-dispatcher instructions and check your state’s requirements once you are safe.
