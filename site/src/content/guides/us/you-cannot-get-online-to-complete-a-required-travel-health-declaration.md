# What to do if… you cannot get online to complete a required travel health declaration (USA)

## Short answer
Tell airline or airport staff that you cannot access the required health declaration and ask them to confirm the official next step for your specific trip. Do not guess answers or keep retrying until you risk missing check-in or boarding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess or falsify answers to make the form submit.
- Do not hand your passport, phone, or login details to a stranger offering help.
- Do not pay a site or person unless staff direct you to an official route and you can verify it.
- Do not keep troubleshooting so long that you miss check-in, security, or boarding.
- Do not send passport photos or personal data to an unverified contact.

## What to do now
1. **Go to the airline check-in desk, gate, or an airport information desk.**  
   Say: “I am required to complete a health declaration, but I cannot access the internet here. What official option is available for this trip?”

2. **Ask staff to confirm the accepted route before you spend more time troubleshooting.**  
   Ask whether there is a paper form, kiosk, staff device, official Wi-Fi route, or another option. Use only the option staff confirms for your form and destination.

3. **Ask what proof you need before boarding.**  
   Ask whether staff need a reference number, confirmation page, QR code, email, or paper form.

4. **Try one quick connection only if staff say there is time.**  
   Ask staff or check official airport signage for the correct Wi-Fi network name. Use a form address or app verified through the airline’s official website, the destination authority’s official website, or staff at the desk or gate. In a browser, check that the address is correct and begins with `https://`, but do not rely on HTTPS alone as proof that a site is genuine.

5. **Save proof offline as soon as the form is accepted.**  
   On your own device, take a screenshot of the confirmation page or QR code and save any confirmation email for offline viewing. If you use a shared device, ask staff whether they can provide a printed or written reference instead.

6. **Protect your data if you use a shared device.**  
   Use a private spot if available. Log out, close the browser or app, and decline any prompt to save your password.

7. **Ask for a supervisor if the first staff member cannot confirm the next step.**  
   Say: “Could a supervisor confirm what is accepted so I do not miss boarding?”

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the problem is your phone, roaming, airport Wi-Fi, or the website right now.
- You do not need to keep repeating the same failed steps while boarding time gets closer.
- You do not need to resolve a wider policy dispute at the counter. First get the accepted next step for this trip.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to solve the connection problem before asking staff for help. Staff may be able to confirm an official alternative or the correct place to complete the form.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the risk of a missed flight and protect your personal data. Later questions or corrections may need help from the airline, the destination authority, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Health declaration requirements vary by destination, airline, and route and may change. Follow the instructions given by official airline, airport, or government staff for your trip.
