# What to do if… you develop new calf pain and warmth and are worried about a blood clot (USA)

## Short answer
Contact a healthcare professional as soon as possible for prompt assessment. If you have trouble breathing, chest pain, coughing up blood, lightheadedness, fainting, or a fast or irregular heartbeat, call 911 now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the symptoms to pass before contacting a healthcare professional.
- Do not delay assessment while trying home treatments.
- Do not use a strenuous walk or workout as a test.
- Do not take someone else’s anticoagulants, leftover prescriptions, or extra aspirin just in case.
- Do not stop prescribed medicines unless a healthcare professional tells you to.
- Do not delay assessment in order to take a long trip or wait out the symptoms.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, very unwell, or short of breath.

## What to do now
1. Call 911 now if you have trouble breathing, chest pain, coughing up blood, lightheadedness, fainting, or a fast or irregular heartbeat.
2. If you do not have those emergency symptoms, contact your primary care clinician or another healthcare professional as soon as possible. Say: “I have new calf pain and warmth and I am worried about a DVT or blood clot.”
3. Ask where you can be assessed promptly. If you consider an urgent care center, call ahead where practical and ask whether it can assess a suspected DVT and arrange any needed testing promptly. If prompt assessment cannot otherwise be arranged, go to an ER.
4. Write down when the symptoms started, which leg is affected, and whether you have noticed swelling, redness, discoloration, tenderness, or warmth.
5. Tell the clinician about recent long travel, limited mobility, surgery or injury, pregnancy or a recent postpartum period, cancer treatment, previous clots, family history of clots, estrogen-containing birth control, hormone therapy, and medicines that affect bleeding.
6. Follow the clinician’s instructions. Assessment may include a leg ultrasound and sometimes a blood test.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether this is definitely a clot or a muscle strain before seeking assessment.
- You do not need to repeatedly measure your calf or test it with long walks.
- You do not need to research supplements, circulation remedies, or prevention routines right now.
- You do not need to make decisions about treatment before a healthcare professional assesses you.

## Important reassurance
Several conditions can cause calf pain and warmth. Getting assessed promptly is the practical next step because symptoms alone cannot confirm or rule out a clot.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Testing, treatment, and follow-up decisions depend on a healthcare professional’s assessment and may need specialist input.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for professional care. Call 911 now if you develop trouble breathing, chest pain, coughing up blood, lightheadedness, fainting, or a fast or irregular heartbeat.
