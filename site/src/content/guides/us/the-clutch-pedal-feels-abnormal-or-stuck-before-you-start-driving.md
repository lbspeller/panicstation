# What to do if… the clutch pedal feels abnormal or stuck before you start driving (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive the vehicle. Keep it parked and arrange roadside assistance or a tow for inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull into traffic or onto a roadway to test the clutch.
- Do not force the shifter into gear.
- Do not rely on pumping the clutch pedal or a temporary change in pedal feel as a reason to drive.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle or attempt roadside repairs.
- Do not let schedule pressure push you into driving a vehicle you are unsure you can control safely.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the vehicle parked.** Set the parking brake firmly and turn the engine off.
2. **Deal with any immediate danger first.** If the vehicle is in an unexpected spot and it is safe to do so, turn on the hazard lights. If the vehicle is in a traffic lane or another position that creates immediate danger, move yourself and passengers away from traffic if you can do so safely without driving the vehicle. Call 911 if emergency assistance is needed.
3. **Check the pedal area only.** Make sure the floor mat has not moved and that no object is blocking the clutch pedal. Do not go under the vehicle.
4. **Do one brief stationary check.** With the vehicle fully parked and the engine off, press and release the clutch pedal once or twice. Stop testing if it sticks down, does not return normally, feels unusually soft or hard, or has clearly different travel from usual.
5. **Note what you noticed.** Tell the roadside or repair service whether the pedal is soft, hard, sticking, slow to return, or different from normal. Mention any dashboard warning, visible fluid, or grinding you noticed before parking.
6. **Arrange professional assistance.** Request roadside assistance or a tow to a repair shop. Say that the clutch pedal feels abnormal and that the vehicle should not be driven. If it is a company vehicle, notify your supervisor or fleet contact.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause now.
- You do not need to decide on repairs, costs, or fault now.
- You can choose a repair shop after the vehicle is stable and assistance is arranged.
- You can check the NHTSA recall lookup after the immediate situation is under control.
- You can report a suspected vehicle safety problem to NHTSA later if that seems appropriate.

## Important reassurance
Stopping before driving is a reasonable safety-first decision when a primary driving control feels abnormal. You do not need to prove that something is broken before asking for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A qualified technician may need to inspect the vehicle before it returns to normal use.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use qualified professional assistance and emergency services where appropriate.
