# What to do if… you have sudden severe ear pain with fever and swelling behind the ear (USA)

## Short answer
Go to an ER or emergency department now, or call 911 if you are very sick, confused, faint, too dizzy to travel safely, or getting worse. Severe ear pain with fever and swelling behind the ear can be a sign of mastoiditis or another serious infection that needs urgent medical assessment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for a routine appointment when fever and swelling behind the ear are present.
- Do not put cotton swabs, fingers, tools, peroxide, oils, or ear candles into the ear.
- Do not try to squeeze, drain, or puncture the swollen area.
- Do not take leftover antibiotics or someone else’s antibiotics.
- Do not use ear drops unless a clinician has told you to use them for this episode.
- Do not exceed the labeled dose of acetaminophen or ibuprofen, and do not take two products that both contain acetaminophen.

## What to do now
1. Go to an ER or emergency department. At check-in, say clearly: “severe ear pain, fever, and swelling behind the ear.” Mention if the ear is sticking out, there is drainage, hearing has changed, or the swelling is spreading.
2. Call 911 immediately if there is trouble breathing, confusion, severe drowsiness, fainting, a seizure, stiff neck, a severe or worst headache, new facial weakness, uncontrolled vomiting, a rash that is spreading fast or looks purple, or rapidly increasing swelling or pain.
3. Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, confused, very dizzy, very sleepy, or unsafe to drive. Call 911 if travel is not safe; otherwise, have another adult take you.
4. Use pain or fever medicine only if it is normally safe for you. Follow the package label or prior clinician instructions for acetaminophen or ibuprofen, and write down the dose and time taken.
5. Keep the ear dry and protected. If there is drainage, wipe only the outside of the ear with clean tissue or gauze; do not irrigate the ear or try to clear the canal.
6. Bring only the useful basics: medication list, allergies, recent antibiotics, when symptoms started, highest temperature, any drainage, hearing change, dizziness, vomiting, headache, and a photo of visible swelling or redness if you can take one easily.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether this is “just an ear infection” or something more serious before seeking care.
- You do not need to try home remedies, ear popping, peroxide, oils, or ear candles.
- You do not need to research every possible diagnosis right now.
- Insurance, payment questions, school, work, and follow-up planning can wait until you are medically assessed and the immediate risk is clearer.

## Important reassurance
You are not overreacting by getting urgent help for this combination of symptoms. The useful next step is simple: get assessed quickly so treatment can start if it is needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Diagnosis and treatment need an in-person exam and may need imaging, antibiotics, drainage, hospital care, or ear, nose, and throat specialist input.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for emergency medical care. Fever with swelling behind the ear can indicate a serious infection, so seek urgent same-day evaluation and use 911 if symptoms are severe, unsafe to travel with, or worsening.
