# What to do if… a light switch plate feels hot or smells like burning (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the switch and leave it off. If there is smoke, flames, sparking, crackling, sizzling, or a strong burning odor, leave the building if you can do so safely and call 911 from outside. If you cannot get out safely, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep flipping the switch to test it.
- Don’t remove the cover plate, open the wall box, or touch wiring.
- Don’t spray water or cleaner on the switch or breaker panel.
- Don’t repeatedly reset a breaker that trips.
- Don’t keep using the affected circuit until it has been checked.
- Don’t approach or use a breaker panel that is hot, smoky, sparking, wet, loudly buzzing, or otherwise unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the switch.** Leave it off and keep people away from the switch and the nearby wall.
2. **Check only from a safe distance for immediate warning signs.** If there is smoke, flames, sparking, crackling, sizzling, or a strong burning odor, leave the building if you can do so safely and call 911 from outside. If you cannot get out safely, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions. Do not go back inside until emergency responders say it is safe.
3. **If there are no active warning signs, switch off the affected circuit only if it is safe.** Do not approach or use the breaker panel if the area is hot, smoky, sparking, wet, loudly buzzing, or otherwise unsafe. Use the panel only if you can clearly identify the breaker for that room or circuit. If you are unsure which breaker controls it, do not experiment with breakers.
4. **Do not assume the problem is over because the smell fades.** If the odor continues, the wall becomes hotter, or the condition worsens after the circuit is off, leave the building if you can do so safely and call 911.
5. **Arrange for a qualified electrician to inspect the switch and circuit before using them again.** Mention the hot plate or unusual odor and any flickering lights, buzzing, sizzling, or breaker trips.
6. **If you rent the home, notify the landlord or property manager promptly.** Tell them the switch plate felt hot or smelled unusual, the affected circuit is being kept off if it was safe to switch it off, and a qualified electrician is needed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause yourself.
- You do not need to remove the switch, open the wall box, or inspect the wiring.
- You do not need to decide about repairs or upgrades right now.
- You do not need to keep checking whether the switch has cooled down.

## Important reassurance
A hot switch plate or unusual burning odor can be a warning sign of an electrical problem. Stopping use, keeping clear, and switching off the affected circuit when it is safe to do so are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A qualified electrician may need to inspect the switch box, connections, and circuit before the switch is used again.

## Important note
This is general information, not electrical, legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If a fire may be starting or the breaker-panel area is unsafe, prioritize getting to safety and calling 911.
