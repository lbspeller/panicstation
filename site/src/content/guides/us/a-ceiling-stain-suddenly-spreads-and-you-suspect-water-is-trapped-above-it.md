# What to do if… a ceiling stain suddenly spreads and you suspect water is trapped above it (USA)

## Short answer
Assume an active leak: isolate electrical power to the area immediately, then shut off the main water supply and relieve trapped water pressure by poking a small hole in any ceiling bulges.

## Do not do these things
- Don't use light switches, ceiling fans, or any electrical fixtures in the affected room.
- Don't stand directly underneath a sagging or bulging section of the ceiling.
- Don't leave the HVAC / Air Conditioning running if the unit is located in the attic or above the stain.
- Don't wait for the stain to stop spreading before calling building management or a plumber.
- Don't attempt to enter the space above the ceiling (attic or crawlspace) if the floor feels soft or wet.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the area beneath the stain and any bulges.** Keep all residents and pets out of the room to prevent injury if the drywall collapses.
   - Move electronics and furniture out of the potential splash or collapse zone.
2. **Shut off power to the affected area at the breaker panel.** Turn off the specific circuit for that room; if you are unsure which one it is, shut off the **Main Breaker**.
   - Do not touch the breaker panel if the floor around it is wet or if you hear buzzing.
3. **Turn off the main water shutoff valve.** Locate the main valve where water enters the home (often in a basement, crawlspace, or near the water meter) and turn it clockwise until it stops.
4. **Relieve trapped water pressure in the ceiling.** If there is a visible bulge or bubble, place a bucket underneath it and stand to the side.
   - Use a screwdriver or long nail to **poke a small hole in the center of the bulge** to allow the water to drain in a controlled stream.
5. **Shut off the HVAC / AC system.** If the leak source is unknown, turn the thermostat to "Off" to stop potential condensate overflow from an attic unit.
6. **Notify the relevant parties immediately.** 
   - If you rent, contact **Emergency Maintenance** or your **Landlord**.
   - If you live in an apartment/condo, alert the **upstairs neighbor** so they can stop using water fixtures immediately.
7. **Contact an emergency plumber.** If you cannot identify or stop the leak source, or if it is a burst pipe, call for professional assistance.
8. **Document the damage for insurance.** Take clear photos of the stain, any bulges, and any water that has drained onto the floor or furniture.
9. **Begin drying within 24 hours of stopping the leak.** Once the area is safe and the leak has stopped, use fans and dehumidifiers to remove moisture and prevent mold growth.

## What can wait
- You don't need to decide on a contractor for permanent ceiling repairs or repainting right now.
- You don't need to determine the exact cause of the leak if a professional is on the way.
- You don't need to clean minor staining or debris until the structural stability of the ceiling is confirmed.

## Important reassurance
Ceiling leaks can look and sound worse than they are once the water is released and the source is shut off. Taking quick action to remove electrical hazards and drain the water pressure significantly reduces the risk of a major ceiling collapse.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Identifying the primary leak source (plumbing, roofing, or HVAC) and conducting mold remediation or structural repairs requires specialized professional assessment.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and is not a substitute for professional plumbing, electrical, or structural advice. If you see sparking or if the ceiling begins to crack loudly, evacuate the building and call 911.
