# What to do if… your rental car reservation is cancelled at pickup and you need transport urgently (USA)

## Short answer
Get safe, reliable transport first, then preserve proof that you arrived and no car was provided. Use the counter, the national reservations line, nearby rental locations, taxis, ride-hail, transit, or a hotel shuttle in parallel rather than arguing at one desk.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t agree to a steep walk-up replacement price before asking whether they can honor your booked rate, move you to a nearby location, switch vehicle class, or waive a price difference.
- Don’t let the company mark you as a no-show if you are physically there; ask them to note that you presented for pickup and no vehicle was available or the reservation was not honored.
- Don’t accept add-ons, fuel plans, toll packages, insurance products, or upgrades you do not understand under pressure; ask for the full out-the-door total first.
- Don’t leave the counter without saving the confirmation number, cancellation message, receipts, screenshots, and the name or ID of the person you spoke with.
- Don’t take an unsolicited ride from someone approaching you in the terminal, parking area, or rental-car center.

## What to do now
1. Choose the fastest safe transport path for the next leg:
   - time-critical: taxi or ride-hail to your destination, hotel, meeting point, or another rental-car cluster;
   - budget-critical: airport rail, bus, subway, intercity coach, or a shuttle to a transit hub;
   - luggage, children, disability, or group travel: larger taxi, accessible ride, hotel shuttle, or a different rental company with a suitable vehicle.
2. At the counter, ask for a written record before you step away:
   - the reservation number;
   - the date, time, and pickup location;
   - whether the reservation was canceled, not honored, or no vehicle was available;
   - the reason given, such as inventory, overbooking, ID issue, payment issue, late pickup, or third-party booking problem;
   - the employee name, manager name, or employee ID if they will provide it.
3. Ask the same company for an immediate fix:
   - ask the agent or manager to call nearby branches and hold a car in your name;
   - ask whether they can switch vehicle class, provide an available upgrade, or waive the price difference because the booked vehicle was not supplied;
   - call the national reservations line while still near the desk and ask them to rebook you at the nearest workable location.
4. Run a backup search at the same time:
   - check other rental counters at the airport or nearby;
   - search direct rental-company websites, not only the third-party booking site;
   - check hourly car-share only if you can meet the membership, license, age, payment, and pickup requirements immediately;
   - check direct transit to your destination or to the nearest major hub.
5. If you are at an airport, use the official ground-transport signs, taxi stands, ride-hail pickup areas, shuttle desks, and airport staff directions.
6. If you booked through a third-party site, contact both the rental company and the booking site. Ask each one to state in writing whether the booking was canceled, unavailable, or refused, and who is responsible for the refund.
7. If you were charged but got no car, protect the payment record:
   - save booking pages, cancellation notices, counter photos, receipts, and screenshots showing no vehicle was provided;
   - keep receipts for alternative transport bought today;
   - ask the rental company or booking site for a refund in writing;
   - if you paid by credit card, contact the card issuer promptly and ask how to dispute a charge for a service not provided. To protect billing-dispute rights, you generally need to send written notice within 60 days after the charge appears on your credit-card statement.
8. After you are moving, start the complaint path:
   - first, contact the rental company customer service and ask for a refund and written explanation;
   - if a third-party booking site was involved, open a complaint with that site too;
   - if unresolved, contact your state consumer protection agency;
   - you can also report the issue to the FTC. FTC reporting can help enforcement and pattern-spotting, but it usually will not resolve an individual dispute directly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide fault, compensation, or legal action at the counter.
- You do not need to write a perfect complaint today; names, times, screenshots, and receipts matter more right now.
- You do not need to decide today whether to pursue small claims, a regulator complaint, or a card dispute beyond preserving the deadline-sensitive payment record.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip at once; solve the next safe leg first.

## Important reassurance
Being left without a booked car is stressful and disruptive, especially when you are tired, carrying luggage, or on a deadline. A few minutes spent getting proof and checking backups in parallel can help you leave with more options and fewer avoidable costs.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get moving and preserve options. Later decisions may need specialist help, and your options can depend on the state, the rental terms, whether you booked direct or through a third party, and whether the payment was a hold, deposit, prepaid charge, debit card, or credit card.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, insurance, or professional advice. Rules, company policies, card protections, and complaint routes vary by state, company, booking platform, and payment method.
