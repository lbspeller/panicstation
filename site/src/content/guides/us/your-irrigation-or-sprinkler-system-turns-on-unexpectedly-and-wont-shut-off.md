# What to do if… your irrigation or sprinkler system turns on unexpectedly and won’t shut off (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the water supply immediately by turning the shutoff handles on your outdoor backflow preventer or the irrigation main valve to the perpendicular (OFF) position.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep toggling the controller or timer buttons — if a valve is mechanically stuck, the "OFF" command will not stop the water.
- Do not attempt to unscrew the valve top (bonnet) or remove internal parts while the water supply is still pressurized.
- Do not use tools to force plastic solenoids or bleed screws — these components should only be hand-tightened.
- Do not enter areas with standing water if there are submerged electrical outlets, landscape lighting transformers, or pump wires.
- Do not attempt to dig into the ground to find or pinch buried pipes.

## What to do now
1. **Identify and avoid electrical hazards in the wet area.** Check for spraying water hitting outdoor outlets, extension cords, or landscape lighting before approaching the valves. Ensure children and pets are kept away from flooded zones and slippery walkways.
2. **Set the irrigation controller to the OFF or RAIN position.**  
   - This stops the electrical signal in case the problem is a software glitch or a stuck relay in the timer.  
   - If the water continues to flow after 30 seconds, the failure is mechanical, and you must proceed to physical shutoff.
3. **Locate the backflow preventer and turn both handles to the OFF position.**  
   - This is typically a metal device (brass or bronze) located above ground near the side of the house or the water meter.  
   - **Rotate the two lever handles so they are perpendicular (90 degrees) to the pipe.**  
   - If the device has "butterfly" or round handles, turn them clockwise until they stop.
4. **Locate the dedicated irrigation shutoff valve if the backflow preventer is inaccessible.**  
   - Look in the garage, basement, or a crawlspace near where the main water line enters the home.  
   - **Turn the handle clockwise (for round handles) or perpendicular to the pipe (for lever handles).**  
   - If the valve is in an outdoor "valve box" (usually a green plastic lid at ground level), you may need a "curb key" or a long-handled wrench if it is deep underground.
5. **Shut off the home's main water supply if you cannot find the irrigation-specific valve.**  
   - **Rotate the main house valve clockwise until it stops (gate valve) or a quarter-turn until perpendicular (ball valve).**  
   - Open a cold water tap inside the house to verify the water has actually stopped flowing.
6. **Check the stuck valve for "Manual ON" settings once the water is off.**  
   - If you can safely access the valve box, find the valve for the zone that was running.  
   - **Hand-tighten the solenoid (the cylinder with two wires) clockwise until snug.**  
   - **Hand-tighten the manual bleed screw (a small plastic knob or screw on top of the valve) clockwise until snug.**
7. **Contact a professional if the system cannot be safely reactivated.**  
   - If water was threatening the building structure, call an emergency plumber.  
   - If the water is isolated and the yard is safe, contact a licensed irrigation contractor to replace the failed diaphragm or solenoid.

## What can wait
- You do not need to clean out mud or debris from the valve box until the site is dry.
- You do not need to diagnose whether the failure was caused by a solenoid, a torn diaphragm, or a controller short-circuit right now.
- You do not need to calculate the cost of the lost water or utility bill impact in this moment.

## Important reassurance
Irrigation valves are designed to fail "closed," but debris or age can occasionally cause them to stick open. This is a common mechanical failure and is not a sign of a "burst" pipe or a total system collapse. Once you have closed the isolation valve or the house main, the situation is stable and no further damage will occur.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and water isolation. Final repairs to the internal valve components or electrical troubleshooting require specific tools and should be performed by someone experienced with pressurized irrigation systems.

## Important note
If you shut off the main water supply to the entire home, avoid using the hot water taps. If the main is off for an extended period, you may need to turn off the power or gas to your water heater to prevent damage from thermal expansion or dry-firing.
