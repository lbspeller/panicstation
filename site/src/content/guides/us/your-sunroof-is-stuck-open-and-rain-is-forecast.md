# What to do if… your sunroof is stuck open and rain is forecast (USA)

## Short answer
Move the vehicle to overhead shelter immediately and apply a secure, taut waterproof cover that does not obstruct the driver's view.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to force the glass panel by hand if it feels jammed; this can snap the drive cables.
- Do not use duct tape or high-tack adhesive directly on vehicle paint or rubber weather stripping.
- Do not drive on public roads if the temporary cover blocks any portion of the windshield or side mirrors.
- Do not leave the vehicle unattended in a public area with an open roof, even if covered.
- Do not start the engine if water has pooled in the footwells; this can short-circuit floor-mounted electrical modules.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to a covered location immediately.** Seek a garage, carport, parking structure, or gas station canopy.
2. **Attempt an electronic force-close (Anti-Pinch Override).**
   - Push and hold the "Close" or "Tilt" switch in the forward position.
   - Continue holding for 20 to 30 seconds without releasing, even if the motor clicks.
   - In many 2026-adjacent models, this overrides the safety sensor to force the motor to move.
3. **Locate the manual override tool if the motor is unresponsive.**
   - Check the vehicle's tool kit or glovebox for a small S-shaped hex (Allen) wrench.
   - Look for a small plastic cap on the overhead console near the sunroof switch.
   - Remove the cap, insert the hex wrench into the motor's drive gear, and turn clockwise to manually crank the glass forward.
4. **Apply a temporary waterproof seal if the roof remains open.**
   - Place a heavy plastic sheet or tarp over the opening.
   - Use painter's tape (blue/green) as a base layer on the roof's metal surface to protect the clear coat.
   - Apply stronger tape (packing or gorilla tape) only onto the painter's tape or the plastic itself to provide tension.
5. **Protect the interior electronics from existing moisture.**
   - Remove floor mats and use dry towels to blot standing water from the carpet.
   - Place plastic bags over the center console and gear shifter if water is dripping from the ceiling.
6. **Disconnect the battery if the floor is saturated.**
   - Locate the negative (black) battery terminal.
   - Loosen the nut and pull the cable away from the terminal to prevent the electrical system from energizing wet components.
7. **Contact a specialist for professional stabilization.**
   - Call roadside assistance and specify that the "sunroof is stuck open and the interior is at risk of water damage."
   - Request a flatbed tow if the car is electrically compromised or if you cannot secure the roof cover for driving.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause of the failure (blown fuse vs. motor) right now.
- You do not need to clean or detail the upholstery until the roof is sealed.
- You do not need to contact your insurance agent until the vehicle is in a dry, stable environment.

## Important reassurance
A stuck sunroof is a common mechanical failure. Most modern vehicles are designed with drain tubes for minor leaks, and as long as you prevent heavy, direct rainfall from entering the cabin, permanent electrical damage is often avoidable.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a mechanical sunroof failure during inclement weather. It does not cover permanent track repair or motor replacement.

## Important note
The information provided is for emergency stabilization only. Improper use of manual override tools or force-close procedures can cause permanent damage to the sunroof mechanism. If the vehicle's floor-mounted electronics have been submerged, do not attempt to drive the vehicle until it has been inspected by a certified technician.
