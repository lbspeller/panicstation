# What to do if… your sunroof is stuck open and rain is forecast (USA)

## Short answer
Get the vehicle under shelter if you can do so safely, then cover the roof opening with a secure waterproof cover that will not block your driving view or come loose. Arrange roadside help, a dealership, or a repair shop before driving in rain.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep driving in rain if water is coming into the cabin; water may affect electrical components or safety-related systems.
- Do not drive if any cover blocks the windshield, mirrors, or windows you need to see safely.
- Do not use a loose tarp, plastic sheet, or bag that could fly off the vehicle.
- Do not force the sunroof glass, tracks, switch, or motor if it resists.
- Do not leave the vehicle exposed and unattended with the roof open if you can avoid it.

## What to do now
1. **Move to shelter if it is safe.** Use a garage, carport, covered parking structure, service bay, or gas station canopy. If moving the vehicle would be unsafe, cover it where it is.
2. **Turn the vehicle off and reduce water damage.** Remove valuables and anything easily ruined. If water is already inside, blot standing water with towels and lift out wet floor mats.
3. **Cover the sunroof opening securely.**
   - Use a waterproof tarp, heavy plastic sheeting, or a fitted car cover.
   - Pull the cover tight and smooth over the roof opening so wind cannot easily lift it.
   - Anchor it to solid points where possible, not just rubber seals.
   - Use low-residue tape if you have it, but do not rely on tape alone in wind or heavy rain.
4. **Keep the cover away from visibility areas.** It should not hang over the windshield, side windows, mirrors, lights, or license plate.
5. **Only drive if the cover is secure and your view is clear.** Drive only to nearby shelter or repair help. Stop if the cover shifts, flaps hard, blocks your view, or water starts coming in heavily.
6. **Check the owner’s manual for a model-specific emergency close procedure.** Some vehicles may have a reset or manual procedure, but it varies by model. If the manual does not clearly confirm the procedure, do not force the mechanism.
7. **Call for help that can secure or close it.** Contact your roadside assistance provider, insurer, manufacturer assistance line, dealership, mobile mechanic, body shop, or auto glass shop. Say: “The sunroof is stuck open, rain is forecast, and I need it temporarily secured or closed.”
8. **After the opening is covered, start drying under shelter.** Ventilate while supervised. If there is significant water inside, dashboard warning lights, odd smells, or electrical glitches, avoid starting or charging the vehicle until a qualified technician checks it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the switch, motor, fuse, drains, or tracks right now.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair before the opening is covered.
- You do not need to decide about insurance immediately, although photos of the opening, cover, and any water inside can help later.
- You do not need to fully clean the interior today; first stop more water getting in.

## Important reassurance
The most useful first step is stopping more rain from entering. Once the vehicle is sheltered or the opening is securely covered, you have reduced the most immediate risk.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate damage and safety risk. A permanent fix may need model-specific help from a dealership, repair shop, body shop, auto glass shop, or qualified technician.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, insurance, financial, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, such as on an unsafe roadside shoulder, move yourself to safety and call 911.
