# What to do if… a driver refuses your seating request and you feel unsafe in the vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
At the first safe opportunity, ask the driver to stop at a busy, well-lit public place and leave the vehicle. If the driver will not stop or you think you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue about the seat if you already feel uneasy.
- Do not try to get out while the vehicle is moving.
- Do not spend time collecting details before getting to a safer place.
- Do not continue toward an isolated or private destination when a safe public stop is available.
- Do not worry about being rude or inconveniencing the driver.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a safe public stop.** Keep it brief: “Please pull over at that store,” or, “Please stop at the next safe public place.”
2. **If the driver will not stop, call 911.** Say that you are in a moving vehicle and feel unsafe. Share any details you have, such as your current location, direction of travel, vehicle description, or license plate. Call if you safely can. If you cannot safely make a voice call, text 911 and watch for a bounce-back message saying that the text was not delivered.
3. **Use app safety tools only if it is safe and easy.** A rideshare app may let you share ride details, view vehicle information, or reach emergency support. Features can vary by app and location.
4. **Leave once the vehicle stops safely.** Take your phone and essential belongings. Move toward a staffed business, hotel lobby, transit area, or other place with people nearby.
5. **Tell someone where you are.** When safe, share your location or ride details with a trusted person and say that you have left the vehicle.
6. **Save basic details after you are safe.** Screenshot the trip information or note the vehicle, license plate, driver name if shown, time, and pickup and drop-off areas.
7. **Report the ride when you are ready.** Use the app’s safety or help area, or contact the taxi or car-service company. For a local taxi or for-hire complaint route, check your official city or county website.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether the situation was serious enough to report.
- You do not need to write a detailed account while you are still shaken.
- You do not need to confront the driver.
- Refunds, complaint follow-up, and any later decisions can wait until you feel steadier.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to work out why the driver refused the seat before asking to end the ride. You can ask to leave at a safe place, and you do not need to remain in a ride that leaves you uneasy.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: reaching a safer pause, leaving the vehicle safely, and saving basic details. Later decisions may need support from the service provider, local authorities, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical help, call 911. Seek medical care or specialist support when needed.
