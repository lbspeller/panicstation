# What to do if… you are asked for proof of travel insurance and cannot find your policy documents (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the person or organization requesting proof which document and format they will accept. Then use your insurer’s or coverage provider’s official website, app, or phone number to retrieve or request that document.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t make up a policy number or coverage details.
- Don’t hand over your phone unlocked or share login credentials.
- Don’t buy another policy in a panic before checking whether you already have coverage and whether a replacement policy would meet the request.
- Don’t use links from unexpected messages or install unfamiliar verification apps.
- Don’t assume your regular U.S. health insurance, payment-card benefit, or other program meets the specific proof request.

## What to do now
1. **Clarify the request.** Ask which document is needed, such as a certificate, plan document, purchase confirmation, proof letter, or policy number. Ask whether a PDF, screenshot, or document displayed on your phone is acceptable.

2. **Search for the original document.**
   - Search your email for the insurer or provider name with terms such as **policy**, **certificate**, **plan document**, **purchase confirmation**, and **proof**.
   - Check downloads, cloud storage, and your travel-booking folder.
   - Check your payment record or booking confirmation if you need to identify the insurer, broker, or coverage provider.

3. **Identify the source of the coverage.**
   - For a standalone policy, use the insurer or broker you paid.
   - For possible payment-card coverage, check the card issuer’s official app or website, or use the number on the back of the card.
   - For a possible employer or student program, check the official program portal or administrator contact.

4. **Use an official retrieval route.**
   - Sign in through the provider’s official website or app and look for policy documents, plan details, or purchase confirmation.
   - Use the official account-recovery or policy-lookup options if you cannot sign in.
   - Ask customer service to email or make available a replacement copy of the document the requester will accept.

5. **Check card-benefit details before relying on them.** Ask the issuer or benefits administrator whether the benefit applies to your trip and whether they can provide a document that meets the request.

6. **Share only what is needed.** Ask which secure delivery method the requester accepts. Confirm any email address before sending a document, and keep your device locked when it is not in your hand.

7. **If you cannot produce proof immediately, ask for a pause.** Ask whether processing can be paused while you retrieve the document, whether another document is acceptable, and whether the requester can note the document required if the service cannot proceed.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to read the full policy wording unless the requester asks for a specific coverage term.
- You don’t need to compare policies or make long-term travel decisions right now.
- You don’t need to start a claim just to retrieve proof of coverage.
- You don’t need to make irreversible trip changes before checking the official retrieval routes.

## Important reassurance
Missing the document does not by itself mean that you do not have coverage. Some providers offer online retrieval or replacement copies, so focus on obtaining one acceptable document through an official route.

## Scope note
These are first steps for producing proof in the moment. Later decisions may need help from the insurer, broker, card issuer, program administrator, travel provider, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, insurance, financial, or professional advice. Requirements vary by destination and by the organization requesting proof. Ask the requester to confirm exactly what they will accept.
