# What to do if… your seat assignment disappears at the gate and you are told to wait (USA)

## Short answer
Stay at the gate and ask the gate agent to confirm your current status: confirmed space with seat to be assigned, standby, or not boarding this flight. Ask for an updated or printed boarding pass, or a clear decision time if they are still working on seats.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t walk away from the gate without telling staff; name calls and last-minute seat assignments can be easy to miss.
- Don’t assume you are still confirmed just because you checked in; ask what your current status is in the airline’s system.
- If you can avoid it, don’t gate-check your carry-on until you know you are boarding this flight; if you must check it, ask staff to confirm the bag tag matches the flight you will actually take.
- Don’t argue with other passengers about seats; the gate system status is what matters.
- Don’t accept a different flight, travel credit, voucher, downgrade, or waiver under pressure without asking what it changes and whether the terms can be shown in writing.

## What to do now
1. **Save proof before the app refreshes.** Screenshot or photograph:
   - your booking confirmation and ticket receipt
   - any paid seat selection, upgrade, or cabin receipt
   - your current boarding pass, even if it shows no seat
   - any message such as “seat assigned at gate,” “see agent,” or “standby.”

2. **Ask one direct status question.** Say: “In your system right now, do I have confirmed space with a seat still to be assigned, am I standby, or am I not boarding this flight?” Then ask: “Can you reprint my boarding pass or show me the current status?”

3. **If they say “wait,” ask what the wait is for.** Ask whether they are waiting for no-shows, processing an aircraft or seat change, clearing a standby list, or deciding an oversale. Then ask: “What time will you confirm yes or no?”

4. **If you paid for a seat or cabin, say that clearly.** Say: “I paid for this seat or cabin. If it cannot be provided, please note that in my reservation and tell me what replacement seat, refund, or fare difference process applies.” Keep receipts and any changed boarding pass.

5. **If they say you will not board this flight, ask for the reason in plain words.** Ask: “Is this involuntary denied boarding because the flight is oversold, or is it an operational issue such as an aircraft change, seat problem, weight and balance, safety, security, or health reason?” The reason affects what paperwork and remedies may apply.

6. **If this is involuntary denied boarding due to oversales, ask for the written statement.** Say: “Please provide the written statement explaining denied boarding compensation and your boarding priority rules.” Also ask what alternate transportation they are offering and when it is scheduled to arrive.

7. **If a child, disability accommodation, or safety need is involved, say it now.** If you are travelling with a child age 13 or under, need a disability-related accommodation, or have another specific safety need, tell the gate agent before boarding closes and ask them to note it in the reservation.

8. **Stay ready to board.** Stay near the boarding lane, keep your ID and boarding pass ready, keep your phone charged, and listen for your name. Last-minute seat assignments can happen quickly.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to file complaints during boarding; first secure a clear status, boarding decision, or next flight plan.
- You don’t need to argue policy at the gate; focus on status, reason, documents, and the next available transportation.
- You don’t need to decide a full compensation or refund dispute while people are boarding. Get the offer and reason in writing where possible, and avoid signing or accepting unclear terms under pressure.

## Important reassurance
A missing seat assignment can happen for routine reasons such as an aircraft swap, seat fault, last-minute reconfiguration, or an oversale process. It may still be resolved at the gate; the goal is to turn “wait” into a clear status, a clear reason, and a clear next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the gate window only. If you end up rebooked, denied boarding, separated from a child, downgraded, or missing a paid service, later decisions may need specialist help or a formal airline or DOT complaint.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, or professional advice. Rules and remedies can depend on the route, fare type, check-in timing, gate arrival timing, airline policy, and what exactly happened.
