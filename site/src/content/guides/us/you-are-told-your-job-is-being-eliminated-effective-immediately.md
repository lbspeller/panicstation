# What to do if… you are told your job is being eliminated effective immediately (USA)

## Short answer
Pause and ask for the separation details in writing. Do not sign a severance agreement or release while you are overwhelmed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign a severance agreement or release in the meeting just to end the conversation.
- Do not resign unless that is genuinely your decision.
- Do not delete, forward, download, or take work emails, files, client information, or confidential material.
- Do not obstruct an access shutoff or a required equipment return.
- Do not send an emotional message or argue about the decision on the spot.
- Do not assume that an immediate separation prevents you from filing for unemployment insurance.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a written separation summary.** Ask for the effective date, the recorded separation reason, whether any further pay is due, any severance offer, the treatment of unused vacation or paid time off, and the best HR contact for follow-up.
2. **Ask when and how your final pay will be issued.** Ask whether the payment is expected by direct deposit, mail, or another method. Ask whether accrued vacation or paid time off, commissions, or bonuses will be included under the applicable rules or plan terms.
3. **Ask HR for copies of your personal employment records.** Request your recent pay statements, offer letter, benefits information, and any separation documents. Confirm that HR has your personal email address and current mailing address. Do not take company or client material.
4. **Confirm when your health coverage ends.** Ask who will send information about any available COBRA continuation coverage or other continuation option. Loss of job-based health coverage may also allow you to enroll in Marketplace coverage through a Special Enrollment Period.
5. **Start an unemployment insurance claim when you can manage it.** Generally, file with the unemployment insurance program in the state where you worked. If you worked in more than one state or now live in a different state, ask a state unemployment insurance agency how to file.
6. **Ask whether a layoff-notice law may apply if other roles were also eliminated.** Federal WARN or a state layoff-notice law may apply to some larger layoffs. Ask whether any notice was issued and who can provide a copy.
7. **Read any severance deadline before setting the document aside.** A release can affect important rights. If you are 40 or older and the agreement asks you to waive age-discrimination claims, special federal rules may apply, including at least 21 days to consider an individual offer, at least 45 days for certain group programs, and 7 days to revoke after signing.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to accept a severance offer. Note the deadline and consider getting advice before it expires.
- You do not need to decide today between COBRA, Marketplace coverage, Medicaid if eligible, or a spouse’s or other eligible family member’s plan. Note any enrollment deadlines when the information arrives.
- You do not need to update your resume, contact your network, or make major retirement-account decisions today.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel overwhelmed or pressured during an unexpected separation meeting. You can ask for the details in writing, take time to read documents, and continue the conversation by email.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Final-pay timing, paid-time-off treatment, unemployment eligibility, severance terms, health-coverage options, and layoff-notice protections can depend on state law, plan terms, and the facts. Later decisions may need specialist help.
