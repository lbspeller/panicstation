# What to do if… your passport name includes a middle name but your booking does not and staff say it could be an issue (USA)

## Short answer
Do not cancel the reservation. Request that the airline update the **Secure Flight passenger data** field in your reservation to match your passport exactly, then have them reissue the boarding pass.

## Do not do these things
- Do not cancel and rebook the flight at a higher fare immediately.
- Do not enter your middle name into the last name or surname field.
- Do not assume a shortened name on the printed boarding pass means the underlying security data is incorrect.
- Do not wait until the TSA checkpoint to resolve a known check-in block.
- Do not attempt a "name transfer" to a different person to fix the formatting.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the character strings on your documents.**
   - Open your passport photo page and locate the "Given Names" and "Surname" fields.
   - Open your airline "Manage Trip" page and locate the "Secure Flight" or "Passenger Information" section.
2. **Contact the airline to update the Secure Flight data specifically.**
   - Use the phrase: "I need to ensure the Secure Flight passenger data on my reservation matches my passport name exactly, including my middle name."
   - Distinguish this from a "name change" (which usually involves a different person) to avoid unnecessary fees.
3. **Verify the update and request a new boarding pass.**
   - Ask the agent to confirm the middle name is now in the digital record.
   - If the name on the boarding pass still appears as "FirstMiddle Last" or "First Last," ask the agent if the Secure Flight data is correct; merged names are a common system limitation and are generally acceptable.
4. **Identify the data controller for third-party bookings.**
   - If booked via an agency, ask the airline: "Do you have control over the Secure Flight data fields, or must the travel agent push that update?"
   - If the agency must do it, contact them immediately and request a confirmation email once the data is sent to the airline.
5. **Prepare for the 2026 TSA ConfirmID fallback.**
   - If the airline cannot update the data and you are flagged at the checkpoint, inform the TSA officer you are aware of the mismatch.
   - Be prepared to pay the $45 TSA ConfirmID fee if your identity cannot be verified through the standard automated system due to the mismatch.
6. **Arrive at the airport earlier than the standard window.**
   - If online check-in is blocked, go to the staffed full-service counter rather than a kiosk.
   - Allow an additional 60 minutes for potential manual document verification or secondary screening.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a Redress Number (DHS TRIP) before this specific flight.
- You do not need to determine why the mismatch happened until after you have reached your destination.
- You do not need to argue airline policy regarding "optional" fields if they have already agreed to update the data.

## Important reassurance
Name discrepancies regarding middle names are one of the most common issues at check-in. TSA systems are designed to handle minor variations, and airlines regularly update Secure Flight data without requiring a full ticket re-issuance or cancellation. If the first and last names match the ID, most discrepancies are resolved through a simple data entry update.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for name mismatches on existing US-based flight bookings. It is focused on the next 24 to 48 hours and does not address long-term legal name changes or international visa requirements for non-US citizens.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and does not guarantee boarding. Airline policies and TSA enforcement, including the 2026 ConfirmID requirements and REAL ID mandates, are subject to change. Always follow the direct instructions of airport security personnel and airline gate agents.
