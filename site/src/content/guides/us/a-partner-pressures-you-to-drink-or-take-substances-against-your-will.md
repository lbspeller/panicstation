# What to do if… a partner pressures you to drink or take substances against your will (USA)
## Short answer
Move to a safer pause away from your partner and contact someone safe. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger, have trouble breathing, or are passing out. If you think you may have been drugged, call 911 or go to a hospital emergency room.

## Do not do these things
- Do not take a drink or substance to end the conflict or prove anything.
- Do not accept another open drink, shot, pill, powder, vape, edible, or other substance from them.
- Do not go somewhere isolated with them if you can safely avoid it.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel intoxicated, confused, dizzy, or unwell.
- Do not feel you have to debate, explain, or defend your boundary.
- Do not blame yourself if you already drank or took something. Pressure is not consent.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move toward other people, staff, security, a friend, or another place where you can contact help.
2. **Decline anything further.** Do not take more drinks, food, pills, or other substances from your partner. Ask someone safe to stay with you.
3. **Contact a safe person.** Say clearly that you need help leaving or getting medical care. Share your location if it is safe to do so.
4. **Call 911 for immediate danger or serious symptoms.** Call if you have trouble breathing, are passing out, or cannot safely get away. Ask staff or another person nearby to call if you cannot.
5. **Get urgent medical care if you think you may have been drugged.** Call 911 or go to a hospital emergency room with someone safe. Tell the clinician that you are concerned about possible drugging.
6. **Contact specialist support if sexual pressure or relationship coercion may be involved.** RAINN can provide confidential sexual-assault support. The National Domestic Violence Hotline can provide confidential relationship-abuse support and safety planning.
7. **Use a safer device if needed.** If you think your phone or browsing may be monitored, use another device to contact support if one is available.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now what label fits the situation.
- You do not have to confront your partner or explain your reasons.
- You do not have to decide now whether to report, end the relationship, or tell other people.
- You do not need to collect evidence or write down details right now.
- You do not need to make long-term decisions before you are safer.

## Important reassurance
Pressure is not consent. Drinking alcohol or taking a substance does not make harm your fault. You may freeze, stay quiet, or feel unsure afterward when someone pressures or scares you. You deserve support without having to prove what happened or decide what to call it.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce harm and stabilize the moment. Later decisions may need specialist support when you feel safer.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger, have trouble breathing, or are passing out. If you think you may have been drugged, call 911 or go to a hospital emergency room. You can seek confidential support even if you are unsure what happened or do not want to report.
