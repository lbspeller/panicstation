# What to do if… you are overwhelmed by a flood of messages about a death in your family (USA)

## Short answer
Silence the incoming messages first. You can send one short holding message, or ask a trusted person to send it, so you are not pulled into repeated replies.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to answer everyone individually while the messages are flooding in.
- Do not share names, circumstances, or locations publicly while details may still be unclear.
- Do not let anyone push you into immediate decisions by text about travel, arrangements, money, or family disagreements.
- Do not chase confirmation through multiple message threads.
- Do not use alcohol or drugs to push through the message flood if you already feel overwhelmed.

## What to do now
1. **Create a pause.** Turn on Do Not Disturb or silence nonessential notifications. Allow only chosen contacts through if you need essential updates.
2. **Choose one trusted contact.** Ask: “Can you handle messages and calls for me for now and tell people I can’t respond?”
3. **Send one holding message if that helps.** You or your trusted contact can send:  
   “I’ve seen the messages. I can’t talk or reply right now. Please send essential updates to [NAME]. I will respond when I can.”
4. **If the death has not been confirmed, check through one route.** Ask one trusted family contact what has been confirmed. If you are the appropriate contact, you can ask an involved care provider whether they can confirm anything to you. Keep your holding message neutral until you know more.
5. **Reduce repeated alerts.** Mute group chats, silence nonessential notifications, and hide message previews if seeing them is making the moment harder.
6. **Use immediate support if you need it.** If you are in emotional distress or crisis, call or text **988**, or use the 988 online chat. If the situation is life-threatening, call **911** or go to the nearest emergency room.
7. **Park practical questions.** You or your trusted contact can reply:  
   “I can’t discuss arrangements right now. Please coordinate with [NAME].”

## What can wait
- You do not need to answer everyone today.
- You do not need to decide about travel, memorial plans, money, or family disagreements while messages are flooding in.
- You do not need to write a full announcement or explanation.
- You can keep your phone silenced and let your trusted contact monitor essential updates.

## Important reassurance
Grief can include numbness, confusion, sadness, anger, helplessness, or changes in sleep, appetite, mood, or energy. A flood of messages can make the moment harder. Reducing the incoming pressure is a reasonable first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions and practical tasks may need help from family, care providers, or other appropriate specialists.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, mental health, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in emotional distress or crisis, call or text 988 or use the 988 online chat. If the situation is life-threatening, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.
