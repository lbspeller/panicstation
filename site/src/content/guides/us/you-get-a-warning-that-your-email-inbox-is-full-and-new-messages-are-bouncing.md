# What to do if… you get a warning that your email inbox is full and new messages are bouncing (USA)

## Short answer
Clear at least 1GB of space in the associated account (including Trash and cloud storage like Photos or Drive) until delivery resumes, then ask critical senders to resend any messages sent during the lockout.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click "Upgrade Storage" or "Sign In" links inside an email warning; navigate to the provider's site manually to avoid phishing.
- Don’t mass-delete messages required for legal, tax, medical, or employment purposes.
- Don’t assume rejected emails will automatically "arrive" later; once an email bounces with a permanent failure (5xx error), the sender must resend it.
- Don’t attempt complex cleanup across multiple devices simultaneously; use one web browser to ensure changes sync correctly.
- Don’t pay for storage increases until you have verified the storage usage within your official account settings.

## What to do now
1. **Access your account through an official, trusted path.**  
   **Type the provider’s URL directly into your browser (e.g., gmail.com, outlook.com, icloud.com) or use the official mobile app.** Avoid all links in the warning message itself to ensure you are not on a spoofed site.

2. **Verify the actual storage status in your account settings.**  
   **Locate the "Storage," "Quota," or "Account Management" section to see the live data breakdown.**  
   - Confirm the shared storage totals if your provider links email with other services (e.g., Google Photos/Drive or Microsoft OneDrive).
   - If the account shows plenty of space, the warning email is likely a phishing attempt; report it and stop.

3. **Free up space immediately to trigger the "unblocked" status.**  
   **Delete large, unnecessary items and empty the "Trash" or "Deleted Items" folder to permanently reclaim space.**
   - Delete the 10 largest emails with attachments (search for "size:10mb" in most providers).
   - Empty the **Spam** or **Junk** folder.
   - Delete large files in linked cloud storage (Photos/Drive) if they share the same quota.
   - Aim to clear at least 500MB to 1GB to ensure the server recognizes the change.

4. **Perform a delivery test from an external address.**  
   **Send a simple text-only email to your "full" address from a different account (or ask a contact to send one).**
   - If the test email arrives, the block is lifted.
   - If you receive a bounce-back (NDR) at the sending address, wait 15 minutes for the server to update and try again.

5. **Identify and contact senders of missing time-sensitive mail.**  
   **Review your calendar or recent activity for expected 2FA codes, bills, or work messages that may have failed during the lockout.** Reach out to these senders via phone or a secondary email and ask them to **resend** the specific messages.

6. **Escalate to IT for managed school or work accounts.**  
   **Contact your organization's helpdesk if you cannot delete enough data or if there is a server-side "hold" on your mailbox.** Request an "Archive Mailbox" or a temporary quota increase to restore flow if retention policies prevent you from deleting mail.

7. **Report suspected phishing warnings to the proper authorities.**  
   **If the warning was fake, forward the email to reportphishing@apwg.org and report it at ReportFraud.ftc.gov.** Use your email provider's built-in "Report Phishing" button before deleting the message.

## What can wait
- Organizing remaining emails into folders or labels.
- Unsubscribing from newsletters and marketing lists.
- Setting up long-term automated archiving or backup solutions.
- Buying a larger storage tier if a one-time cleanup solved the immediate bounce.

## Important reassurance
Mailbox storage limits are a common technical hurdle, and providers usually restore service within minutes of space being cleared. While "bounced" emails cannot be retrieved automatically, most senders will receive a notification that the mail failed, making it easier to identify what needs to be resent.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. This guide addresses the technical restoration of email flow and does not cover account recovery for forgotten passwords or compromised security credentials.

## Important note
This guide provides general information regarding digital storage and is not a substitute for professional technical support or legal advice. If you believe your account has been compromised, prioritize changing your password through official channels and enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA).
