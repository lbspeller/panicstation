# What to do if… your lips or fingertips look bluish and you feel unwell (USA)

## Short answer
Call **911** now. Bluish lips, blue nails, or bluish fingertips while feeling unwell may signal a serious breathing, oxygen, or circulation problem.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait it out if this is new, worsening, or comes with weakness, dizziness, confusion, unusual sleepiness, chest pain, or shortness of breath.
- Do not drive yourself to the ER if you feel faint, very breathless, confused, or “not right”.
- Do not lie flat if breathing feels harder that way.
- Do not take alcohol, sedatives, opioids, or extra medicines to try to calm down.
- Do not use someone else’s inhaler, oxygen, or prescription medicine.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911.** Say: “My lips or fingertips look bluish and I feel unwell. I’m worried about breathing, oxygen, or circulation.” Stay on the line. Put the phone on speaker if you are alone.
2. **Sit upright and reduce effort.** Sit up, lean slightly forward if that helps, loosen tight clothing, and stop walking around.
3. **Stay warm without delaying help.** If your hands are cold, cover them or move into a warmer room if you can do that safely. Do not go outside into cold air unless the 911 dispatcher tells you to.
4. **Use your own prescribed rescue treatment if you have one.** If you have a prescribed rescue inhaler, oxygen, or written action plan for asthma, COPD, or another known condition, use it exactly as prescribed while waiting for emergency help.
5. **Use a pulse oximeter only if it is right next to you.** Tell 911 the number, but do not delay the call and do not ignore bluish lips, bluish fingertips, chest pain, confusion, faintness, or severe breathlessness because of a normal-looking reading.
6. **Say immediately if anything is getting worse.** Tell 911 if you cannot speak full sentences, have chest pain or pressure, feel very weak, become confused, feel unusually sleepy, cough up blood or dark mucus, or pass out.
7. **Make it easier for EMS to reach you.** Unlock the door if you can do that safely, turn on a light, secure pets, and gather your medication bottles, allergy list, ID, and key medical history if they are nearby.

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out the cause before calling 911.
- You do not need to decide whether this is urgent care, the ER, anxiety, asthma, circulation, or something else.
- Cost questions, messages, transport plans, and routine appointments can wait until you are safer and assessed.

## Important reassurance
This is a valid reason to call 911. You are not wasting anyone’s time by getting urgent help for bluish lips or fingertips while feeling unwell.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce immediate risk and connect you with urgent evaluation. Later decisions may need help from emergency clinicians or another qualified medical professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for professional care. If you may be seriously unwell, especially with bluish lips or fingertips, breathing difficulty, chest pain, confusion, or fainting, call 911.
