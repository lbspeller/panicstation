# What to do if… someone is sending rapid messages demanding you respond immediately and the tone turns controlling (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the interaction loop by silencing the notifications and moving to a physically secure location. If the sender is nearby or making specific threats of harm, call 911 immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don't respond to every message in an attempt to calm the sender or prove you are listening.
- Don't share your current location, who you are with, or your immediate plans.
- Don't delete any messages, call logs, or images sent during this episode.
- Don't agree to meet the person in private to resolve the issue while the tone is controlling.
- Don't turn off your phone completely if you need it for emergency communication, but do use silence features.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a physically secure location where you cannot be easily reached by the sender.** 
   - Ensure doors are locked if you are at home.
   - Seek a public or populated area if you are currently in transit.
2. **Silence all notifications from the sender to stop the immediate cognitive pressure.** 
   - Use the "Hide Alerts" or "Mute" feature on the specific message thread.
   - Activate "Do Not Disturb" mode while allowing calls from a "Favorites" list of trusted contacts.
3. **Disable features that provide the sender with information about your activity.** 
   - Turn off Read Receipts in your messaging settings.
   - Disable "Last Seen" or "Online" status indicators if the app allows.
   - Turn off "Typing Indicators" if possible.
4. **Identify the presence of immediate threats or proximity.** 
   - Check if the person knows your current location or has mentioned being nearby.
   - Call 911 if there is a direct threat of physical violence or if you believe they are following you.
   - If the situation is not an emergency but constitutes harassment, look up and call your local police non-emergency number to start a log.
5. **Preserve evidence of the messages exactly as they appear.** 
   - Take screenshots that show the sender’s contact information, the full text of the messages, and the timestamps.
   - Save these screenshots to a secure location, such as a cloud folder the sender cannot access or by emailing them to a trusted friend.
6. **Alert a trusted person to the situation without involving the sender.** 
   - Contact a friend, family member, or coworker and state: "I am receiving controlling and rapid messages from [Name]; please stay in contact with me while I resolve this."
7. **Contact a specialist service for guidance on digital safety and stalking.** 
   - Call the VictimConnect Resource Center at 855-484-2846 for stalking and harassment support.
   - Call the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-SAFE (7233) or text "START" to 88788 if the sender is a current or former partner.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to block the person permanently right this second if you fear it will cause them to show up in person.
- You do not need to explain your silence or justify your boundaries to the sender right now.
- You do not need to file a formal police report or seek a protective order in this exact moment unless you are in immediate danger.
- You can wait to perform a full security audit of your accounts and devices until you are in a safe, stable environment.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a high volume of demanding or controlling messages is a tactic used to create a sense of urgency and fear. It is common to feel overwhelmed, trapped, or panicked when notifications are constant. Taking a pause and stopping the response cycle is a recognized safety strategy, not a failure to communicate.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital harassment and controlling messaging. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, domestic violence exit planning, or technical device forensics.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal or professional advice. Contact local law enforcement or a certified victim advocate for assistance with your specific jurisdiction's stalking and harassment laws.
