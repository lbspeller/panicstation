# What to do if… you cannot access your travel documents or proof of trip purpose at the border (USA)

## Short answer
Tell the U.S. Customs and Border Protection officer plainly that your supporting documents exist but are not accessible. Ask what specific information would be useful and whether you may make one brief attempt to retrieve an existing confirmation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess, improvise, or give details you are not sure about.
- Do not present altered screenshots, edited confirmations, or another person’s document as your own.
- Do not argue, joke about enforcement, or make a rushed decision because you feel pressured.
- Do not delete files, change accounts, or create new documents while you are being questioned.
- Do not start booking replacement flights or accommodation unless you are told that this is necessary.

## What to do now
1. **Explain the access problem in one sentence.**  
   For example: “My hotel confirmation and return-flight details are in my email, but my phone has no charge and I cannot open them.”

2. **Ask what the officer wants to confirm.**  
   Ask which supporting information would be most useful. This may include the purpose of the trip, where you plan to stay, how long you plan to stay, or your return or onward travel details.

3. **Give checkable details that you know are accurate.**  
   Provide the details you can remember without guessing, such as:
   - your accommodation name, address, and telephone number
   - the name and telephone number of a person you are visiting
   - your airline, flight number, and return or onward travel date
   - the event, meeting, or activity that explains the purpose of your trip

4. **Ask permission before trying to retrieve a document.**  
   Ask whether you may briefly charge your phone, connect to available internet access, open an existing confirmation, or contact the airline, accommodation provider, host, or travel companion. If the officer says no, stop and continue with the information you can provide.

5. **Use an existing resend option if the officer allows it.**  
   An airline, accommodation provider, host, or travel companion may be able to resend an existing confirmation to an address you can already access or show the confirmation directly.

6. **If you are sent to secondary inspection, keep your answers steady.**  
   Secondary inspection may involve additional checks and more questions. Repeat the same accurate details and say when you do not know or cannot access something. If language is making the situation harder, ask whether language help is available.

7. **If an electronic device is raised, ask for clarification.**  
   Ask what is being requested and whether providing the specific travel confirmation another way would address the immediate question. Do not argue or make a rushed decision. The current U.S. Customs and Border Protection information about electronic-device searches is listed below.

8. **Keep any paperwork you are given.**  
   If you receive a notice or follow-up instruction, keep it and note any stated next step or deadline.

## What can wait
- You do not need to recover every file immediately.
- You do not need to prove unrelated parts of your life while trying to answer the specific travel question.
- You do not need to make new bookings, change your accounts, or solve a wider travel problem unless that becomes necessary.

## Important reassurance
Access problems can happen. The useful next step is to state the problem clearly, provide accurate details you already know, and ask what specific confirmation would help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are being questioned at a U.S. port of entry. Later decisions, including what to do after a refusal of admission or where there is a prior immigration complication, may need specialist immigration help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Entry decisions and inspection steps depend on the individual circumstances. Ask for clarification when you do not understand a question or request.
