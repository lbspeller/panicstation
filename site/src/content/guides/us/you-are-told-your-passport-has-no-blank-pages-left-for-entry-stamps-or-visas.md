# What to do if… you are told your passport has no blank pages left for entry stamps or visas (USA)

## Short answer
You cannot add pages to a U.S. passport book; you must renew the book entirely using urgent or emergency services if your travel is imminent.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to DIY: never glue, staple, or tape extra paper into the book, as this constitutes alteration and invalidates the document.
- Do not assume an airline will make an exception; many are legally required to deny boarding if the destination country's page requirements are not met.
- Do not rely on "partially used" pages where a stamp already exists; most countries require "completely blank" pages for new visas or entry stamps.
- Do not book non-refundable onward travel until you have a confirmed appointment or a new travel document in hand.

## What to do now
1. **Physically count your remaining completely blank pages marked "Visas" at the top.** 
   - Note that many countries require between two and four blank pages.
   - Some destinations, such as Namibia, may require up to six blank pages.
2. **Determine if you qualify for "Urgent Travel Service" at a passport agency.** 
   - This service is for those traveling internationally within 14 calendar days or needing a foreign visa within 28 calendar days.
   - You must have an appointment and provide printed proof of international travel (flight itinerary / hotel reservation).
3. **Schedule an appointment through the Official Online Passport Appointment System.** 
   - If you have already applied and travel is within 14 days, call the National Passport Information Center at 1-877-487-2778.
   - Be prepared to pay the standard renewal fee plus a $60 expedite fee.
4. **Locate the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate if you are currently outside the United States.** 
   - Search for the "U.S. Citizen Services" section on the specific embassy website for the country you are in.
   - Request an "emergency passport" (limited-validity) if you must travel before a full-validity replacement can be processed.
5. **Request the "Large Book" (52 pages) on your renewal application.** 
   - Check the box for the larger book on form DS-82 (for mail renewals) or DS-11 (for in-person).
   - There is currently no additional government fee for choosing the 52-page book over the standard 32-page book.
6. **Apply for "Life-or-Death Emergency Service" only if you meet the strict criteria.** 
   - This is reserved for travel within 3 business days due to the death, hospice care, or life-threatening illness / injury of an immediate family member outside the U.S.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide which countries to visit next year; focus only on the document requirements for your current or immediate next transit point.
- You do not need to "close out" your current passport; the agency will cancel it and return it to you (often separately) so you keep your old stamps.
- You do not need to pay for a third-party "courier" or "expeditor" service unless you have exhausted official appointment options; these services do not have "extra" appointments.

## Important reassurance
Running out of pages is a common issue for frequent travelers and does not imply any fault on your part. While the rules can feel obscure or sudden, the U.S. Department of State has established "Urgent" and "Emergency" pathways specifically to handle these time-sensitive document failures.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for U.S. passport holders facing blank-page entry refusals. It does not cover specific visa application procedures for foreign nations or long-term travel planning.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and is based on 2026 U.S. Department of State protocols. Entry and exit requirements are subject to the sovereign laws of the destination country and the discretion of airline boarding agents.
