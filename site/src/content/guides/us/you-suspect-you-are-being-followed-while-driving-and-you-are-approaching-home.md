# What to do if… you suspect you are being followed while driving and you are approaching home (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive to your home or pull into your driveway. Call 911 immediately and drive to a well-lit, populated public area or a police station as directed by the dispatcher.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive to your residence, enter your driveway, or open a garage door.
- Do not stop or pull over in a dark, isolated, or unpopulated area.
- Do not confront the other driver or engage in hand gestures or shouting.
- Do not attempt to "lose" the vehicle by speeding or driving recklessly.
- Do not lead the follower to the home of a friend or family member.
- Do not exit your vehicle until you have reached a safe, populated location.
- Do not use your turn signals early, which allows the follower to anticipate your route.

## What to do now
1. **Change your destination and stay on main roads.** Do not turn into your neighborhood. Keep your doors locked and windows fully closed.
2. **Call 911 immediately.** Use hands-free settings or a passenger to make the call. Tell the dispatcher:
   - Your current location and direction of travel
   - A description of the vehicle following you (make, model, color, and license plate if safe to see)
   - That you are being followed and do not feel safe going home
3. **Keep the dispatcher on the line.** Do not hang up until an officer arrives or you are safely inside a secure building.
4. **Drive to a "safe haven" location.** Follow the dispatcher's directions or head toward:
   - A 24-hour police station (ensure the lobby is open)
   - A hospital emergency room entrance
   - A well-lit, 24-hour business with staff and customers (e.g., a busy gas station or grocery store)
5. **Wait for a last-second turn if necessary.** If you must turn to reach a safe location, signal only at the moment you begin the maneuver to prevent the follower from blocking your path.
6. **Stay in your vehicle upon arrival unless it is unsafe to do so.** Park in a high-visibility area under lights. If the follower stops near you, sound your horn continuously to attract attention.
7. **Verify any vehicle attempting to pull you over.** If an unmarked vehicle with lights signals you to stop and you are unsure of its legitimacy, keep driving at a safe speed, turn on your hazards, and ask the 911 dispatcher to verify that a real officer is conducting the stop.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confirm the follower’s identity or motive.
- You do not need to check for vehicle damage or "road rage" triggers.
- You do not need to notify your neighbors or homeowners association immediately.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal report until you are physically safe.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your intuition is a safety priority. It is always better to take a detour to a safe location and be wrong than to lead a potential threat to your home. Emergency dispatchers are trained to handle "suspicious vehicle" calls and will not judge you for seeking help.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate physical safety and stabilization during a suspected following incident. It does not cover long-term stalking investigations or legal restraining orders.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute legal advice. In all cases of suspected danger, follow the direct instructions of 911 dispatchers and law enforcement officers.
