# What to do if… you suspect you are being followed while driving and you are approaching home (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive to your home, driveway, garage, or anyone else’s home. Call 911 and stay on well-lit, busier roads while the dispatcher helps direct you to a safer place.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go home to get inside quickly.
- Do not open your garage, driveway gate, apartment gate, or private parking area.
- Do not park on your street or near your home to check whether they stop too.
- Do not drive to a friend’s or family member’s home and involve them.
- Do not stop in an isolated place to call, text, film, or check your phone.
- Do not confront the other driver, gesture at them, block them, or get out of your vehicle.
- Do not try to shake them by speeding, weaving, running lights, or making unsafe turns.
- Do not handle your phone while driving if you can avoid it. Use hands-free, a passenger, or stop only somewhere safer and public.

## What to do now
1. **Do not go home.** Keep driving on well-lit, busier roads. Keep your doors locked, windows up, and enough distance from other cars so you can keep moving if traffic allows.

2. **Call 911 as soon as you can do it safely.** Say: “I think I am being followed while driving, and I am not going home.” Give the call-taker your location, direction of travel, vehicle description, and whether you feel in immediate danger.

3. **Let 911 choose or confirm the safest destination.** Ask: “Where should I drive now?” They may direct you to a police station, fire station, staffed public place, or another meet point.

4. **Drive toward an open, staffed, well-lit place.** Good options usually include a police or fire station, a hospital emergency entrance, a large open store, or a busy gas station. Avoid quiet parking lots, side streets, dead ends, and private homes.

5. **Only do a simple pattern check if it is already safe and does not delay calling 911.** For example, take normal turns on main roads rather than heading home. If the same vehicle keeps matching your route, treat it as real and continue with 911’s instructions.

6. **If you stop at a safer place, stay visible and stay in the car unless 911 or an officer tells you otherwise.** Park under lights near an entrance, keep the doors locked, keep the engine ready if safe, and turn on hazard lights so responders and staff can spot you.

7. **If the person appears to be trying to pull you over and you are not sure it is a real officer, call 911.** If it is safe, turn on your hazard lights, drive calmly without speeding or trying to elude, and ask the dispatcher to verify the stop and tell you where to pull over.

8. **After you are safe, write down details.** Note the time, route, vehicle colour, make or model, license plate if you saw it safely, number of people, and anything distinctive. Do not go back outside to gather more information.

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove you are being followed before calling 911.
- You do not need to decide what this means right now.
- You do not need to call multiple people, post online, or investigate the other driver.
- You do not need to make a full report until you are somewhere safer and have spoken with emergency help.

## Important reassurance
Doubt is normal in this situation. Choosing not to reveal your home, staying in public, and asking 911 for help are reasonable first steps even if the situation later turns out to be a misunderstanding.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few minutes. If this happens more than once, involves threats, or seems connected to a specific person, later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, a victim advocate, an attorney, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911 and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
