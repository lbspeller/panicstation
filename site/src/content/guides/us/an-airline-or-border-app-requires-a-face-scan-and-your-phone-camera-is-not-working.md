# What to do if… an airline or border app requires a face scan and your phone camera is not working (USA)

## Short answer
Discontinue use of the digital application and proceed to a staffed service point for manual document verification.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remain in a self-service or biometric-only queue while attempting to fix hardware issues.
- Do not attempt to bypass the photo requirement by using a static photograph or another person's face.
- Do not perform a factory reset of the mobile device if it contains travel documents or offline boarding passes.
- Do not provide an unlocked device to unauthorized individuals for assistance.
- Do not assume that a failed biometric scan is an automatic denial of travel.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your physical travel documents immediately.** **Locate your physical passport, permanent resident card, or REAL ID-compliant driver's license before leaving the queue.**
   - Ensure you have a physical copy or a secondary digital record (such as a screenshot or PDF) of your confirmation number / record locator.

2. **Alert airline staff to the technical failure if checking in or boarding.** **Proceed to the airline's check-in counter, customer service desk, or the gate agent podium.**
   - State clearly: "My device camera is non-functional and I cannot complete the required face scan. I require a manual identity check and a printed boarding pass."
   - For international departures, note that non-US citizens may be subject to mandatory biometric exit; follow agent instructions for the stationary gate camera instead of the app.

3. **Switch to a manual TSA screening lane if the camera fails at security.** **Inform the TSA officer at the Credential Authentication Technology (CAT-2) unit that you are opting out of biometric verification due to technical failure.**
   - US citizens have the right to opt out of facial recognition at TSA checkpoints and request a manual ID check.
   - If you lack a physical ID and the digital version is inaccessible, be prepared for "TSA Confirm.ID" processing, which may involve additional identity verification steps and a standard fee.

4. **Revert to standard Customs and Border Protection (CBP) processing for entry.** **Exit the Mobile Passport Control (MPC) or Global Entry mobile app flow and join the regular primary inspection queue.**
   - MPC requires a live photo submission; if the camera is broken, the app cannot generate a QR code for the officer.
   - For Global Entry, use the stationary touchless portals at the airport. If the portal fails to recognize you, proceed to the designated "Global Entry / CBP" officer for manual processing.

5. **Request a printed boarding pass at the earliest opportunity.** **Obtain a physical paper boarding pass from a kiosk or agent to ensure you can pass through subsequent checkpoints without relying on the camera.**
   - Checkpoints beyond the initial scan may require re-verification; a physical pass prevents repeated app-based biometric prompts.

6. **Follow officer instructions for stationary biometric equipment.** **Utilize the airport's fixed-mount cameras at gates or immigration kiosks if your personal device camera is the only component failing.**
   - Biometric boarding often uses gate-mounted hardware that does not require your phone camera.

## What can wait
- Technical troubleshooting of app permissions, software updates, or hardware repairs should be deferred until you are through security or have reached your destination.
- Filing a formal complaint regarding app accessibility or biometric requirements is not necessary during the transit process.
- Replacing the mobile device is not required if physical documents are present and accepted.

## Important reassurance
Manual fallback procedures exist at every stage of the US travel process. Technical failures, including broken cameras and biometric mismatches, are anticipated by airline and government protocols. Switching to a manual ID check is a standard operation and does not imply a security risk or a violation of travel rules.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate transit. It does not cover long-term device repair, visa applications, or legal challenges to biometric collection.

## Important note
Procedures and opt-out rights vary between US citizens and foreign nationals. Always follow the specific directives of TSA officers, CBP agents, and airline staff. This guide reflects standardized fallback processes for technical failures as of 2026.
