# What to do if… your messaging app shows your account is linked to a new companion device you do not recognise (USA)

## Short answer
Stop attempting retries and do not uninstall the app yet. Verify your keyboard layout, try accessing the vault from a second known-good device or a web browser, and locate your physical recovery kit or emergency 2FA codes.

## Do not do these things
- Don't uninstall the app or clear its local data yet, as this may delete the only local copy of your encrypted vault before it has synced.
- Don't guess your master password more than five times in a row, which can trigger service-level security lockouts or long time-delays.
- Don't factory-reset your smartphone or computer in an attempt to "fix" the app.
- Don't share your recovery key, secret key, or master password with anyone claiming to be "technical support."
- Don't ignore signs of account takeover, such as emails about "new device sign-ins" you did not initiate.

## What to do now
1. **Verify your physical input and environment immediately.** **Check if the Caps Lock key is active or if your keyboard layout has changed to a different language or region.**
   - Ensure you are not mistaking a "Device Passcode" for your "Master Password."
   - Confirm your device date and time are set to "Set Automatically," as time-drift of even one minute can cause multi-factor authentication (MFA) and encryption decryption to fail.
2. **Attempt access through an alternative official interface.** **Open the password manager's official web vault in a private or incognito browser window to see if the issue is limited to the local app.**
   - If you have a second device (tablet, old phone, or work computer) where you were previously logged in, check if that device still has a cached session or allows biometric unlock (TouchID / FaceID).
3. **Check the provider's official status page.** **Look for service outages or "Degraded Performance" notices on the password manager’s official status website.**
   - If the service is down, your master password may be correct, but the app cannot verify your account status or sync with the server.
4. **Locate your recovery materials.** **Find your physical or digital "Emergency Kit," "Secret Key," or "Recovery Code" that was generated when you first created the account.**
   - For 1Password, look for a PDF file titled "Emergency Kit."
   - For Bitwarden or LastPass, look for a "Recovery Key" or "One-Time Password" you saved in a secure physical location or separate digital file.
   - For Apple (iCloud) or Google, locate your account recovery codes or use a "Trusted Device" to approve the login.
5. **Initiate an official emergency access request if configured.** **Contact a "Trusted Contact" or "Emergency Access" person if you previously set up these features within the app settings.**
   - Note that many providers enforce a waiting period (e.g., 24 to 48 hours) before a trusted contact is granted access to your vault.
6. **Secure your primary recovery email account.** **Change the password for the email address linked to your password manager and ensure it is protected by phishing-resistant MFA (such as a hardware key or passkey).**
   - Review your email settings for any "Forwarding Rules" or "Filters" you did not create, which could be used to intercept reset links.
7. **Contact your organization's administrator for work-managed vaults.** **Reach out to your IT department or system administrator if you are using an Enterprise or Team plan.**
   - Administrators often have the technical ability to "Reset Account" or "Restore Access" for team members without needing the original master password.
8. **Report suspected identity theft if you see unauthorized activity.** **Visit IdentityTheft.gov to create a recovery plan if you observe unauthorized logins, financial transfers, or password changes you did not make.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a new password manager today.
- You do not need to manually reset every individual account password until you have confirmed your vault is permanently inaccessible.
- You do not need to perform a full malware scan of all devices immediately, unless you have specific evidence of a targeted hack.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a high level of stress when your primary security tool fails, but many lockouts are caused by temporary software glitches, time-sync errors, or keyboard input mistakes. Most password managers are designed with "Zero Knowledge" architecture, meaning your data remains encrypted and safe even if you cannot see it right now.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a technical lockout. It does not cover long-term account recovery if all master credentials and recovery keys are permanently lost.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not professional technical or legal advice. Recovery options vary significantly between providers (e.g., 1Password, Bitwarden, Apple, Google). In "Zero Knowledge" systems, if you lose both your master password and your recovery key/kit, the provider may be unable to recover your data. If you suspect fraud, contact your financial institutions immediately using the numbers on the back of your cards.
