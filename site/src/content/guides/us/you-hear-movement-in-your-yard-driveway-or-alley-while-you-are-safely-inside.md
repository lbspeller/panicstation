# What to do if… you hear movement in your yard, driveway, or alley while you are safely inside (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside, lock all entry points, and move to a secure interior room. Call **911** immediately if you see a person on your property or believe a crime is in progress.

## Do not do these things
- Don't go outside to investigate, confront, or "scare off" anyone.
- Don't open the door or shout through it, as this reveals your exact location.
- Don't stand near windows or in lit areas where your silhouette is visible from outside.
- Don't prioritize checking social media or neighborhood apps over securing your home.
- Don't ignore the sound if it is repetitive or accompanied by the smell of smoke or gas.

## What to do now
1. **Lock all exterior doors and windows immediately.** Secure the front, back, and garage-to-house doors, then ensure all ground-floor windows are latched.
2. **Move to a safe interior room.** Gather all residents and pets into a room with a lockable door, preferably one without large windows, and keep your phone with you.
3. **Turn on indoor and outdoor lights from a distance.** Use a smartphone app or wall switches that are not directly in front of a window to illuminate the property.
4. **Silence your phone and observe cameras safely.** Check doorbell or security camera feeds on your mobile device to identify the source of the noise without approaching windows.
   - Look for specific cues: number of people, clothing colors, vehicle descriptions, or tools being carried.
5. **Call 911 if you feel unsafe or see an intruder.** Inform the operator if you are in a multi-unit building and provide your specific apartment or suite number.
   - **Address first:** Clearly state your address and the city you are in.
   - **Nature of emergency:** Report "suspicious person on property" or "attempted entry" as applicable.
6. **Use Text-to-911 only if speaking is dangerous.** If you cannot speak because an intruder is near or you are hiding, text **911** with your address first, followed by "someone outside my window / door." 
7. **Identify environmental hazards that mimic movement.** If you hear rhythmic clicking or see a glow, check for signs of a utility issue (like a gas meter fault or electrical arc) and report this to 911 if you cannot rule out a fire.
8. **Stay on the line until help arrives.** Keep the 911 dispatcher informed of any changes, such as the person moving to a different side of the house or attempting to force a door.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confirm the identity of the person or animal tonight.
- You do not need to check your vehicle for damage until police arrive or it is daylight.
- You do not need to download or share security footage immediately.
- You do not need to notify neighbors until the immediate situation is resolved by authorities.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a racing heart, shaking, or heightened senses when hearing unexpected noises at night. Taking the step to lock down and call for assistance is a proactive safety measure, not a sign of overreacting.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes of a potential security threat. It does not cover long-term security auditing or legal reporting procedures.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety stabilization in the USA. If you believe a life-threatening emergency or crime is occurring, contact 911. Local police non-emergency lines should be used only if the noise has ceased and there is no immediate threat.
