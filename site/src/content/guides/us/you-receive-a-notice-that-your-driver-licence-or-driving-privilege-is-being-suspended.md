# What to do if… you receive a notice that your driver licence or driving privilege is being suspended (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive after the effective date unless the licensing agency, court, or a valid temporary or restricted permit confirms you may. Use your state driver licensing agency’s official website or phone line to confirm your current license status, the effective date, and any hearing or review deadline.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep driving after the effective date, or after an official status check says your license or driving privilege is not valid.
- Do not assume a criminal court case is the only thing that matters; some suspensions are handled through a separate administrative process.
- Do not ignore the notice because you moved recently; some agencies mail orders to the address on file.
- Do not use payment links, phone numbers, or “reinstatement” websites from suspicious texts, emails, or letters.
- Do not pay reinstatement fees until the official agency confirms you are eligible to reinstate and what the payment will do.
- Do not give away your original license, notice, court papers, or insurance documents unless an official process clearly requires it; keep copies.

## What to do now
1. **Get off the road before sorting paperwork.** If you are driving when you realise the notice may already be in effect, park somewhere lawful and safe, then arrange another way to travel.

2. **Find the three facts that control the next step.** On the notice, look for:
   - who issued it, such as a state driver licensing agency, court, or law enforcement agency
   - the effective date and time, or whether the suspension is already active
   - whether there is a hearing, review, appeal, or correction process, and how to request it

3. **Check your license status through the official state agency.** Use your state’s official driver licensing agency website, the agency phone number listed on an official state website, or an official online account. Ask:
   - “Is my license or driving privilege valid right now?”
   - “What is the exact effective date and time?”
   - “Is there a hearing or review request deadline?”
   - “How do I request the hearing or review?”
   - “Does requesting it delay the suspension, or do I need a temporary or restricted permit?”

4. **Treat any hearing or review deadline as urgent.** Some states set short deadlines, especially for DUI, alcohol, refusal, point, insurance, or administrative suspension cases. For example, California DMV materials describe a 10-day hearing request period in certain DUI administrative actions, and Texas DPS materials describe short ALR hearing request windows. Do not use those numbers as your deadline unless your own notice or state agency confirms them.

5. **Check for identity, address, and case errors.** Compare the notice with your license and records:
   - full name
   - date of birth
   - license number
   - current and old address
   - citation, court, crash, insurance, child support, tax, or case number
   If anything looks like the wrong person, wrong case, old address problem, or duplicate record, tell the agency immediately and ask what proof they need to review or correct it.

6. **If the notice is linked to a court case, contact the court listed on the notice.** Ask whether there is an unpaid fine, missed appearance, unresolved ticket, probation condition, or court order connected to the suspension. Do not rely on the court alone if the driver licensing agency also has a separate deadline.

7. **Ask about limited driving only after confirming whether you can drive at all.** Some states may offer a hardship, occupational, conditional, restricted, or temporary license in limited circumstances. Ask the agency or court what exists in your state, whether you are eligible, and whether you must apply before driving.

8. **Make a short no-driving plan.** Cancel, reschedule, or replace any driving for work, childcare, school, appointments, court, or medical needs until your status is confirmed. If you are scheduled to drive for work, do not drive until your privilege is confirmed valid; consider sharing only the minimum needed to reschedule driving duties.

9. **If there is an urgent medical or immediate safety emergency, do not drive yourself if your license may be suspended.** Call 911 if emergency help is needed, or use another safe transport option.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to move, change jobs, sell your car, or explain everything to everyone.
- You do not need to build a full legal argument before confirming the effective date, current status, and hearing or review deadline.
- You do not need to pay every fee today unless the official agency confirms the fee is required now and explains what it changes.
- You do not need to solve insurance, SR-22, reinstatement, and restricted-license questions all at once; first confirm whether you may drive and whether a deadline is running.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel blindsided or alarmed by a suspension notice, especially if you think it is a mistake. The safest first move is simple: do not drive unless official records say you may, confirm the deadline, and keep proof of every contact you make.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Driver licensing rules, hearing rights, restricted-license options, and reinstatement requirements depend on the state and the reason for the suspension or revocation, so later decisions may need help from a qualified traffic lawyer, legal aid service, court clerk, or licensing agency.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow your notice, use official state or court channels, and get specialist help if you are unsure what applies to your situation.
