# What to do if… you notice a new lump or swelling that appeared suddenly and is growing (USA)

## Short answer
Get prompt medical evaluation, usually the same day, if the lump or swelling is growing quickly, painful, red, warm, or you also have fever, chills, or feel unwell. If your lips, tongue, face, or throat are swelling, you have trouble breathing or swallowing, or you might pass out, call 911 now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not squeeze, cut, puncture, or try to pop or drain it.
- Do not wait to seek same-day care if it is getting bigger fast, redness is spreading, or you develop fever or chills.
- Do not take leftover antibiotics, steroids, or someone else’s medication.
- Do not keep repeatedly pressing, massaging, or deeply feeling it to test firmness or movement.
- Do not drive yourself if you are dizzy, faint, or having breathing or swallowing problems; call 911 or ask someone safe to help.

## What to do now
1. **Check for emergency signs first.**
   - Call 911 if you have swelling of the lips, face, tongue, or throat; trouble breathing; wheezing; a tight throat; trouble swallowing; dizziness; fainting; or loss of consciousness. These can be signs of a severe allergic reaction or another emergency.
2. **Choose the safest care route for today.**
   - Go to an emergency room if the swelling is rapidly worsening, you feel seriously unwell, redness is spreading quickly, you have fever or chills, or the swelling affects breathing or swallowing.
   - Go to an emergency room now for sudden or severe scrotal pain, or for rapidly worsening genital swelling with feeling unwell.
   - Use urgent care if it is getting bigger and is painful, red, warm, draining, or you suspect an infected bite, sting, boil, or abscess and you cannot get a same-day clinician appointment.
   - Contact primary care, an OB-GYN, or another regular clinician for the soonest appointment if you are otherwise stable but the lump is still new or growing, especially for a breast, testicle, neck, armpit, or groin lump.
3. **Write down the details before you seek care.**
   - Note when you first saw it and how fast it is changing.
   - Note the location and approximate size.
   - Note whether it is painful, itchy, red, warm, firm, fixed in place, draining, or linked with fever, chills, or trouble swallowing.
   - Note any possible trigger, such as an injury, bite or sting, new medication, new food, recent vaccination, or recent infection.
4. **Document the change without irritating it.**
   - Take one clear photo in good light.
   - If there is a red patch on the skin, gently trace the outer edge with a pen and write down the time. Use this only to show change; do not use it to delay care if the redness spreads quickly or you develop fever or chills.
5. **Treat these locations as prompt-check locations even if you feel otherwise OK.**
   - **Breast lump:** arrange a clinician appointment, especially if it is new, firm, fixed, changing, or linked with nipple discharge, skin dimpling, or a new armpit lump.
   - **Testicle or scrotal lump:** seek medical evaluation promptly. Go to an emergency room now if there is sudden pain, severe pain, nausea, vomiting, or a very tender swollen testicle.
   - **Neck lump:** get checked promptly, especially if it is growing, firm, fixed, painful, warm, linked with fever, or making swallowing or breathing harder.
6. **Protect the area while you arrange care.**
   - Keep it clean and avoid friction from clothing, straps, or shaving.
   - Do not apply drawing salves, strong creams, or home remedies meant to bring it to a head.
   - If fluid is draining on its own, cover it with a clean dressing and still get it evaluated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether you need imaging, antibiotics, drainage, or a biopsy before a clinician examines it.
- You do not need to keep checking it by squeezing or deep pressing.
- You do not need to compare it with online photos.
- You do not need to make long-term decisions today; the next step is getting the right level of assessment.

## Important reassurance
A new lump or sudden swelling can feel frightening, and many causes are common or treatable. Acting early, avoiding home drainage, and choosing the right care route are the most useful things to do in the first moment.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about tests, treatment, monitoring, or specialist referral may need a clinician or another qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not a diagnosis, and not medical or other professional advice. If symptoms are rapidly worsening or you have breathing, swallowing, fainting, or severe allergic-type symptoms, treat it as an emergency and call 911.
