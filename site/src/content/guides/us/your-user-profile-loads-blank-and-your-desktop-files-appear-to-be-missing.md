# What to do if… your user profile loads blank and your desktop files appear to be missing (USA)

## Short answer
Avoid writing new files, running cleanup tools, or resetting the device until you have checked whether you are in the wrong or temporary profile and whether Desktop or Documents has been moved by OneDrive or iCloud. If you see a ransom note, changed file extensions, or security alerts, disconnect the device from the internet before doing more.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t reinstall apps, reset the computer, or “start over” until you have checked the likely places where the files may still be.
- Don’t download recovery, cleaner, or repair tools from search results; unsafe tools can make things worse or overwrite recoverable data.
- Don’t pay a ransom or call a pop-up support number if you see a ransomware-style message.
- Don’t plug in backup drives until you are reasonably confident the device is not infected.
- Don’t sign out of cloud accounts in a panic before checking whether your Desktop or Documents folders are stored there.
- Don’t drag folders between locations until you know whether they are local files, cloud files, or files in a temporary profile.

## What to do now
1. **Preserve what you are seeing.**  
   Take photos or screenshots of the blank desktop, signed-in username, any warnings, unusual file names, and the date and time. This can help IT support, a repair technician, or an official cybercrime report.

2. **If ransomware or malware is possible, isolate the device first.**  
   If you saw a ransom note, sudden file extension changes, unexpected encryption messages, or security alerts, turn off Wi-Fi and unplug Ethernet. If this is a work or school device, contact the helpdesk or security team and stop troubleshooting on your own.

3. **Check whether Windows signed you into a temporary profile.**  
   On Windows, look for a message such as “We can’t sign in to your account” or “temporary profile.” Open File Explorer and check `C:\Users\` for your original user folder. Do not save new work into the temporary profile unless you also copy it somewhere safe, because changes made there may not stay after sign-out.

4. **Check whether you are in the correct account.**  
   On Windows, confirm the signed-in account name and check whether another account on the computer has the expected Desktop and Documents folders. On Mac, check System Settings or System Preferences for the signed-in user, then open Finder and check the correct home folder.

5. **Check cloud locations before moving anything.**  
   Use a browser and type the official address yourself or use a saved bookmark for OneDrive or iCloud. Look for Desktop, Documents, deleted items, recently moved items, and recent activity. Avoid search ads and lookalike support pages.

6. **Check the usual local and synced folder paths.**  
   On Windows, check `C:\Users\<your-username>\Desktop`, `C:\Users\<your-username>\Documents`, `C:\Users\<your-username>\OneDrive\Desktop`, and `C:\Users\<your-username>\OneDrive\Documents`. On Mac, check the Desktop and Documents folders in your home folder and in iCloud Drive.

7. **Check Recycle Bin or Trash, including cloud trash.**  
   Look in the local Recycle Bin or Trash. If OneDrive is involved, also check the OneDrive Recycle bin on the web. If iCloud Drive is involved, check recently deleted files through iCloud.

8. **If this happened after a Windows update, check for older user folders.**  
   If `C:\Windows.old` exists, it may contain older user folders. Search inside it for a distinctive missing file name, or for Desktop and Documents. Do not delete `Windows.old` while you are still looking for files.

9. **If OneDrive is involved, use Microsoft recovery options carefully.**  
   Check OneDrive’s Recycle bin and file version history first. If many OneDrive files were deleted, overwritten, corrupted, or affected by malware, “Restore your OneDrive” may be available, may require Microsoft 365, and may affect changes made after the restore point.

10. **If iCloud Desktop and Documents is involved, verify where Apple is storing them.**  
   iCloud Drive can store Desktop and Documents in iCloud so they appear across devices. In iCloud settings, check whether Desktop and Documents syncing is on, then look in iCloud Drive for those folders before turning syncing off.

11. **If this is a managed device, escalate instead of experimenting.**  
   Tell IT or the helpdesk: “My profile loads blank, Desktop and Documents appear missing, and this may be a temporary profile or folder redirection issue.” Mention any ransomware signs separately.

12. **If you believe this was cybercrime, use official reporting routes.**  
   For ransomware, fraud, extortion, or account compromise, you can file a report with the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center. For an organization, use internal incident-response procedures and CISA ransomware guidance.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to choose a paid recovery service today.
- You don’t need to factory reset or reinstall the operating system right now.
- You don’t need to reorganize folders until you know where the files are stored.
- You don’t need to change every password immediately unless there are signs of account compromise, but avoid signing into sensitive accounts on the affected device if malware is possible.
- You don’t need to decide whether the files are permanently gone until you have checked local user folders, cloud folders, trash, and any temporary-profile warning.

## Important reassurance
A blank desktop often means the computer has opened the wrong profile, a temporary profile, or a cloud-synced location instead of the folder you expected. Slowing down and checking the likely locations first gives you a better chance of finding the files without overwriting anything.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the situation and avoid irreversible mistakes. Later decisions may need help from IT support, a trusted repair professional, cloud-account support, cybersecurity support, or a data-recovery specialist.
