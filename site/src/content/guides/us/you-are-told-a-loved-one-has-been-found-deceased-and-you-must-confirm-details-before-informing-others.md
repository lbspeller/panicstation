# What to do if… you are told a loved one has been found deceased and you must confirm details before informing others (USA)

## Short answer
Do not notify others yet; first verify the caller's identity by calling back the agency's official, publicly listed main switchboard number to confirm a matching case or report number.

## Do not do these things
- Do not share the news on social media, in group chats, or via mass text until you have spoken directly to an official at a verified agency number.
- Do not provide the deceased's Social Security number, your banking details, or any form of payment to a person who called you.
- Do not assume a phone notification from a police department is legitimate; in the USA, official law enforcement death notifications are almost always performed in person.
- Do not drive or operate machinery if you are experiencing physical shock, tremors, or blurred vision.
- Do not feel pressured to "hurry up" and tell others before you are certain of the facts.

## What to do now
1. **Sit down and stop all physical activity.** Reaching a seated position prevents injury if you experience a sudden drop in blood pressure or a shock reaction.
2. **Obtain the caller’s specific credentials.** Ask for:
   - Their full name, rank/title, and specific agency or facility name.
   - A formal case number, report number, or hospital encounter number.
   - The name of the specific county or jurisdiction handling the matter.
3. **Verify the agency through an independent search.** Use a search engine to find the official "main line" or "switchboard" for the police department, hospital, or Medical Examiner office mentioned. Do not use a "direct line" provided by the caller until you have verified it through the main switchboard.
4. **Call the verified main number and ask for the specific department.** 
   - If a hospital called: Ask for the **Nursing Supervisor**, **Patient Relations**, or **Social Work** department.
   - If law enforcement called: Ask for the **Watch Commander** or the specific officer by name and case number.
   - If a Medical Examiner or Coroner called: Ask for the **Investigator on duty**.
5. **Request confirmation of "Positive Identification."** Ask the official if the identity has been confirmed through physical ID, fingerprints, or family recognition, or if it is currently a "tentative" identification. 
6. **Identify yourself as a primary contact.** Ask the official to record your name and phone number as the point of contact for the "Legal Next of Kin" (NOK) so you receive all subsequent updates directly.
7. **Document the conversation in writing.** Record the date, time, the name of the official you spoke with, and exactly what was confirmed (e.g., location of the deceased, time of pronouncement, and the name of the funeral home if the body has already been moved).
8. **Contact one trusted person to assist you.** Ask this person to come to your location to help you manage further phone calls and to ensure you do not have to process the next steps alone.
9. **Inform immediate family in a controlled sequence.** Once confirmed, call the closest relatives individually.
   - Use a factual script: "I have confirmed with [Agency Name] that [Name] has died. We don't have all the details yet, but I wanted you to hear it from me directly."
   - Explicitly request that they do not post anything online until all immediate family members have been reached.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a funeral home or make burial arrangements in the next few minutes.
- You do not need to locate the Will or any legal documents immediately.
- You do not need to notify the deceased's employer, banks, or insurance companies today.
- You do not need to respond to any non-immediate family members or acquaintances.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of disbelief or a need to "double-check" the news multiple times. Taking the time to verify details is a responsible action that prevents the spread of misinformation and protects the privacy of your family during a period of high stress.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for verifying a death notification. It does not cover the legalities of estate administration, probate, or long-term bereavement support.

## Important note
Procedures for death notification and body release vary significantly by state and county. If you suspect a fraudulent call, contact your local police department non-emergency line immediately to report the encounter before taking any other action.
