# What to do if… you feel suddenly far more intoxicated than expected and worry your drink was tampered with (USA)

## Short answer
Stop drinking, get to a safer place with someone you trust, and call 911 now if you are in immediate danger or have serious symptoms such as trouble breathing, collapse, a seizure, or being hard to wake. If you are awake and not in immediate danger, call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 for fast guidance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep drinking alcohol or take other substances to try to counteract it.
- Do not leave alone, drive, or ride with anyone you do not trust.
- Do not go somewhere private with someone who is making you feel unsafe.
- Do not let anyone pressure you to sleep it off away from trusted help.
- Do not confront a suspected person yourself.
- If you think sexual assault may have happened and you might want help later, try not to shower or change clothes yet only if you can stay safe and comfortable; your wellbeing comes first.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward people and light.** If you are at a bar, club, party, campus event, hotel, or venue, go to staff, security, a front desk, or another public monitored place.
2. **Tell one trusted person clearly.** Say: “I feel much more intoxicated than expected and I’m worried my drink was tampered with. Please stay with me.”
3. **Do not travel alone.** Ask the trusted person or venue staff to help arrange a ride with someone you trust, or call emergency services if you cannot stay safely where you are. If you must use a tracked ride as a fallback, share the trip details with a trusted person and ask staff or the trusted person to stay with you until you are safely met.
4. **Call 911 now for emergency symptoms or immediate danger.** Call if you are very drowsy or hard to wake, having trouble breathing, collapsing, having a seizure, vomiting repeatedly, severely confused, injured, or being threatened.
5. **Call Poison Control if you are awake and not in immediate danger.** Call 1-800-222-1222 and say you may have been drugged or poisoned through a drink. Tell them what you drank, when symptoms started, and what you feel now.
6. **Get medical care if you are worried, even if you are unsure.** At an emergency department, or with care directed by 911 or Poison Control, say: “I feel suddenly far more intoxicated than expected and I’m worried my drink was tampered with.”
7. **If sexual assault may have happened, ask for sexual-assault support without committing to a report.** Ask whether a sexual-assault nurse examiner, trained clinician, or advocate is available, and what medical care, evidence-collection, and reporting options exist in your location.
8. **Contact confidential specialist support if you want help deciding next steps.** Call RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800-656-4673 or use RAINN online chat.
9. **Keep one possible item only if it is safe.** If you still have the drink, cup, bottle, or container, set it aside and avoid handling it more than necessary. If keeping it creates risk, conflict, or delay, leave it and focus on getting safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the substance right now.
- You do not need to decide alone right now whether to report to police, request evidence collection, or tell people beyond those helping keep you safe.
- You do not need a perfect timeline. Later, a few brief notes such as where you were, approximate times, what you drank, and who was nearby can help you explain what happened.
- You do not need to prove that your drink was tampered with before seeking medical care or support.

## Important reassurance
Sudden unexpected impairment can feel frightening, confusing, and unreal. It is okay to be unsure, to have memory gaps, and to ask for help before you know exactly what happened.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: get safer, get medical guidance, and preserve choices where possible. Later decisions may need help from medical professionals, sexual-assault advocates, campus or workplace support, or legal specialists.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you think you are in danger or seriously unwell, call 911 now.
