# What to do if… you hit a new daily transfer limit and you need to send a payment today (USA)

## Short answer
Contact your bank immediately to request a one-time limit increase or a same-day bank wire/FedNow transfer; avoid using unverified third-party apps or high-risk workarounds to bypass security blocks.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send funds via gift cards, cryptocurrency, or unfamiliar payment apps to bypass a limit.
- Don't share one-time passcodes (OTPs), login credentials, or "approval" codes with anyone, even those claiming to be bank staff.
- Don't approve a wire transfer or instant payment without verbally verifying the destination details through a known, trusted phone number.
- Don't repeat the transaction attempt multiple times; this may trigger a permanent security freeze or duplicate the payment.
- Don't trust "new" or "updated" payment instructions received via email or text without independent verification.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of limit or hold you have encountered.**
   Check your banking app or website for a specific error message or notice.
   - Determine if it is a **daily dollar cap**, a **new recipient restriction**, or a **fraud detection hold**.
   - Note any specific error codes or reference numbers provided in the notification.

2. **Verify the legitimacy of the payment request (1 minute).**
   Confirm the amount and recipient through an official channel (e.g., a phone number from a previous statement or the entity's verified website).
   - If the request involves extreme pressure ("pay in the next hour or lose everything"), treat it as a potential scam.

3. **Call your bank's official customer service or fraud department.**
   Use the number on the back of your debit card or the bank’s official website.
   - Ask: "Can I receive a **one-time limit increase** for this specific transaction?"
   - Ask: "Does my account support **FedNow** or **RTP (Real-Time Payments)** for instant settlement?"
   - Ask: "What is your **Same Day ACH** or **domestic wire** cutoff time for today?"

4. **Request a Same Day ACH if the transaction is under $1 million.**
   The Federal Reserve settles Same Day ACH in three windows: 10:30 AM, 2:45 PM, and 4:45 PM ET.
   - Confirm your bank can submit the file before the next window.
   - Verify that the recipient’s bank is also a participant in Same Day ACH.

5. **Utilize a domestic wire transfer for high-value urgent payments.**
   Wires are immediate and irrevocable but generally only process Monday through Friday (9 PM ET preceding day to 7 PM ET).
   - Verbally confirm the recipient's routing and account number before initiating.
   - Be aware that wires submitted after 7 PM ET (or your bank's earlier internal cutoff) will not process until the next business day.

6. **Propose a partial payment if the full amount cannot be moved.**
   Send the maximum allowed today and provide the recipient with the transaction confirmation.
   - Get written agreement (email or text) that the remainder will be accepted on the next business day without penalty.

7. **Report suspected fraud immediately if you have already sent money.**
   Contact your bank's fraud department to initiate a "wire recall" or "ACH return" request.
   - File a report at **ReportFraud.ftc.gov** to help law enforcement track the scam patterns.

## What can wait
- You do not need to permanently change your daily transfer limits today.
- You do not need to open new accounts or switch banks in response to a single limit issue.
- You do not need to resolve non-urgent "pending" statuses that are scheduled for next-day settlement.

## Important reassurance
Daily transfer limits and security holds are standard industry practices designed to prevent unauthorized access and large-scale fraud. These blocks do not necessarily mean your account is compromised; they often indicate that a transaction has met "high-risk" criteria, such as a new recipient or an unusually large amount.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. This guide does not cover international "remittance" transfers, which are subject to different regulations and longer settlement times.

## Important note
This guide is for general information and is not financial or legal advice. Transfer availability, cutoff times, and fees are determined by individual financial institutions and Federal Reserve operating hours. Federal Reserve systems (Fedwire/ACH) do not operate on weekends or federal holidays.
