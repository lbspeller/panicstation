# What to do if… you receive an unsolicited meet me now message from an unfamiliar or spoofed account (USA)

## Short answer
Do not go to meet them. Stay where you are, or move to a safer public place, and verify the sender through a contact method you already trust.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to the meetup location to “see what happens,” especially alone.
- Do not click links, open attachments, scan QR codes, or download apps they send to “confirm” who they are.
- Do not share your live location, address, workplace, routine, or travel plans.
- Do not send codes, passwords, payment details, private photos, or identity documents.
- Do not keep arguing, testing, or negotiating with the account; that can reveal more information or increase pressure.
- Do not post identifying screenshots publicly. Save them privately instead.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Stay where you are if it is safe, or move toward a staffed, public, well-lit place. Do not start traveling to meet the sender.
2. **Treat the urgency as a warning sign.** A demand to meet “right now” from an unfamiliar or spoofed account is enough reason to slow down and verify before acting.
3. **Verify through a known-good channel.**
   - If the message claims to be from someone you know, call or message the saved number you already had for them.
   - Use an existing, previously used chat thread if you have one.
   - Ask one clear question: “Did you just message me from a different account telling me to meet right now?”
4. **Check whether anyone is in immediate danger.** If you feel threatened, think someone is nearby waiting for you, or believe someone is in immediate danger, call 911. If it is not an emergency but you want it documented or want local advice, use your local police non-emergency number.
5. **Preserve evidence without engaging.** Take screenshots showing the username, number, message, date, and time. Keep the message thread intact if you can.
6. **Report it through the right route, then block or mute.**
   - For a text message, you can usually forward the spam text to 7726 (SPAM).
   - For a messaging or social app, use the in-app report option for spam, impersonation, harassment, or suspicious contact.
   - If it seems like a scam or phishing attempt, report it to the FTC.
   - If it involves spoofing, phishing, account compromise, cyber-enabled crime, or financial loss, report it to the FBI’s IC3.
   - If it involves unwanted texts or phone-number spoofing, you can also file an FCC complaint.
7. **Do a fast account and privacy check.**
   - Turn off location sharing you do not fully trust right now.
   - Check whether the message came after a password reset, login alert, or suspicious account activity.
   - If you clicked a link, gave a code, or shared a password, change that password using the real app or website and enable two-factor authentication where available.
8. **Tell one trusted person.** Send them a brief message such as: “I got a suspicious urgent message telling me to meet now. I’m not going. I’m verifying it and will check in again shortly.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person behind the account right now.
- You do not need to prove the message is fake before refusing to meet.
- You do not need to confront the sender, explain yourself, or keep the conversation going.
- Deeper account cleanup, longer reports, and decisions about next steps can wait until you are safe and less pressured.

## Important reassurance
A real friend or safe contact can wait while you verify. Pausing, refusing to meet, and using a trusted contact route are reasonable protective steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the immediate situation. If the messages continue, become threatening, include stalking, involve money or account access, or feel targeted, later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, platform support, a cyber-safety specialist, or another appropriate professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe, prioritize immediate safety and contact emergency services.
