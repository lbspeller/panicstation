# What to do if… your thermostat screen goes blank or shows an error and your heating or cooling won’t start (USA)

## Short answer
Treat this first as a possible power loss or safety shut-off, not as a reason to force the system to run. If you smell gas or a carbon monoxide alarm is sounding, leave immediately and call 911 from outside or another safe place.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep forcing the thermostat on and off or doing repeated resets.
- Do not bypass safety switches, remove panels, or jump thermostat wires to make the system run.
- Do not keep resetting a breaker that trips again.
- Do not touch switches, plugs, breakers, phones, appliances, or the thermostat if you smell gas; leave first.
- Do not run a grill, generator, camp stove, car, or other fuel-burning device indoors for heat or power.

## What to do now

1. **Check for immediate danger before touching anything.**
   - If you smell gas, hear hissing near gas equipment, or suspect a gas leak, leave the home now.
   - From outside and away from the smell, call 911. If your gas utility’s emergency number is already available or easy to find without going back inside, call that too.
   - If a carbon monoxide alarm sounds, move everyone to fresh air outdoors and call 911 or the fire department. Do not go back in until responders say it is safe.

2. **Replace or reseat the thermostat power source if there is no gas or CO concern.**
   - Put in fresh thermostat batteries if your thermostat uses batteries, even if the old ones seem recent.
   - If the thermostat clips onto a wall base, press it gently and firmly back into place.
   - If it is a smart thermostat, check that any nearby plug-in module, receiver, or equipment-side power supply has not been unplugged or switched off.

3. **Check only the obvious HVAC power controls.**
   - Do this only if there is no gas smell, CO alarm, smoke, burning smell, water near electrical equipment, or other immediate danger sign.
   - Look at the electrical panel for a tripped breaker labelled Furnace, Air Handler, HVAC, AC, or Heat Pump.
   - If the breaker is tripped, reset it once. If it trips again, stop and call HVAC service or an electrician.
   - Look for a nearby furnace or air-handler wall switch that looks like a light switch, and confirm it is on.

4. **Capture the error before changing anything else.**
   - Take a photo of the thermostat screen, including any error code.
   - If the furnace or air handler has a visible status light or display that you can see without opening panels, take a photo or write down what it shows.

5. **Try one clean restart only.**
   - After replacing batteries or restoring power, wait a few minutes for the thermostat and equipment to respond.
   - Some heating and cooling systems have built-in start delays, so silence for a short time does not always mean the restart failed.

6. **If it still will not run, stop troubleshooting.**
   - A blank screen or repeated error can mean low-voltage power has been cut by a safety condition.
   - On some systems, one possible cause is a condensate overflow switch from a clogged drain; other causes need proper testing.
   - Call HVAC service and tell them what changed, what you checked, whether a breaker tripped, and what the thermostat or equipment display showed.

7. **Stabilise the room while you wait.**
   - For heat, use layers and blankets, close unused rooms, and keep people together in the warmest safe room.
   - For cooling, close blinds or curtains, use safe fans if you have power, drink water, and move vulnerable people to a cooler safe place if the home is getting too hot.
   - Keep pets, older adults, infants, and anyone medically vulnerable away from extreme temperatures.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the thermostat, furnace, air conditioner, or heat pump must be replaced.
- You do not need to open equipment panels, touch wiring, or test internal parts.
- You do not need multiple resets; one careful restart is enough before handing off to a qualified person.

## Important reassurance
A blank thermostat screen often has a simple cause, such as lost power, loose mounting, or batteries. Stopping after basic checks is not giving up; it helps avoid turning a repairable fault into a bigger safety or electrical problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to make the situation safer and create a clear handoff to HVAC service, an electrician, emergency responders, or your gas utility if needed. Later decisions about repair or replacement may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, electrical, HVAC, or other professional advice. If there is a gas smell, suspected gas leak, carbon monoxide alarm, repeated breaker trip, smoke, burning smell, or immediate danger, leave or keep distance and contact emergency help.
