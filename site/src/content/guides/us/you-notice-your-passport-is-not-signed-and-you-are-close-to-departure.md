# What to do if… you notice your passport is not signed and you are close to departure (USA)

## Short answer
Sign your full name in blue or black ink on the "Signature of bearer" line, typically found on the page opposite your photo page, before presenting it to officials.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to travel with an unsigned passport book, as it is considered invalid and may lead to denied boarding or entry.
- Do not sign the passport cover or any page other than the designated signature line.
- Do not use pencil, gel pens that smudge easily, or colors other than blue or black.
- Do not use correction fluid, tape, or erasers if you make a mistake; these are considered signs of tampering.
- Do not sign a child's passport as if it were your own; specific formatting is required for minors under 16.

## What to do now
1. **Locate the "Signature of bearer" line.**  
   Open your passport book to the page immediately following or opposite the plastic-coated data page (where your photo is located). Look for the line labeled "Signature of bearer."
2. **Sign your full name exactly as it appears on the data page.**  
   Use a blue or black ballpoint pen. Ensure the signature is clear and stays within the designated area.
3. **Wait for the ink to dry completely before closing the book.**  
   Closing the passport while the ink is wet can cause smudging or ink transfer to the data page, which may be interpreted as damage or alteration by border officials.
4. **Follow the parent/guardian protocol for children under age 16.**  
   If the passport belongs to a child who cannot sign their own name:
   - **Print the child’s full name** on the signature line.
   - **Sign your own name** (the parent or guardian) next to the printed name.
   - **Write your relationship** to the child in parentheses, such as (Mother) or (Father).
5. **Verify that you are using the passport book and not the passport card.**  
   The U.S. Passport Card does not have a signature line and is valid without a signature; if you are using the card, no action is required.
6. **Seek professional assistance if you have already signed the book incorrectly.**  
   If the signature is illegible, smudged, or signed by the wrong person, do not attempt to "fix" it yourself. Contact the National Passport Information Center (NPIC) at 1-877-487-2778 for guidance on whether the book must be replaced.

## What can wait
- You do not need to notify the State Department that you forgot to sign it once you have corrected the oversight.
- You do not need to apply for a replacement passport if the signature was performed correctly and the book is otherwise undamaged.
- You do not need to rush an appointment at a passport agency if you are able to sign the document properly before your flight.

## Important reassurance
Forgetting to sign a new passport is a common occurrence. As long as you notice the omission before handing the document to an airline agent or border official, it is a reversible error. Taking a moment to sign it correctly now will prevent the document from being flagged as invalid during your journey.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate stabilization of an unsigned but otherwise valid U.S. passport book. It does not address damaged passports, expired documents, name changes, or visa requirements for specific destinations.

## Important note
This information is based on 2026 U.S. Department of State procedures. While a signature validates the document for U.S. authorities, individual airlines and foreign border agencies maintain the right to enforce their own entry and boarding protocols.
