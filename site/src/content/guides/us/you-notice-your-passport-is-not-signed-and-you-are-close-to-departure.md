# What to do if… you notice your passport is not signed and you are close to departure (USA)

## Short answer
Sign your U.S. passport now, before check-in if you can. The U.S. Department of State says an adult must sign their full name in blue or black ink inside the passport.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave it unsigned and hope it will not matter; an unsigned passport may cause delay or extra checks.
- Do not use pencil, erasable ink, correction fluid, tape, stickers, or anything that could damage the passport.
- Do not add marks anywhere else in the passport except the allowed signature area and emergency contact information.
- Do not practise signatures inside the passport book.
- Do not sign a child’s passport like an adult passport; use the child passport format.

## What to do now
1. **Find the signature line inside the passport book.**  
   For an adult passport, sign your full name on the signature line in blue or black ink.

2. **Let the ink dry before you close the passport.**  
   Put the passport on a flat surface, sign once, and leave the page open long enough to avoid smudging.

3. **If the passport is for a child under 16, use the child format.**  
   On the signature line, a parent should print the child’s full name, sign the parent’s name next to it, and write the parent’s relationship to the child, such as mother, father, or guardian.

4. **If you are already at the airport or on the way, get a suitable pen before you hand over the passport.**  
   Use a blue or black pen from your bag, a travel companion, the airline counter, or an airport shop. Sign calmly on a flat surface.

5. **Check only the things that matter right now.**  
   Make sure the signature is inside the passport book, the page is not smudged, the passport is otherwise undamaged, and the name and photo page look correct.

6. **If you signed in the wrong place, badly smudged the page, or added marks you should not have added, stop trying to fix it yourself.**  
   Do not scratch, cross out, cover, or rewrite over the problem. Contact U.S. Passports or ask the airline counter what they need from you before check-in.

7. **If the problem may need official help and you have urgent international travel, use the State Department urgent-travel route.**  
   The State Department describes urgent travel as travel in less than 2–3 weeks. Passport agency and center appointments are required and may be made within 14 calendar days of international travel, or 28 days if you need a foreign visa.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace the passport just because it was unsigned, if you have now signed it correctly and it is otherwise fine.
- You do not need extra paperwork or an office visit for a simple missing signature that you corrected properly.
- You do not need to change your whole trip plan unless an airline, passport official, or border official tells you there is a specific problem.
- You do not need to decide anything about future renewals right now.

## Important reassurance
This is a common last-minute discovery with a new passport. If the passport is otherwise valid and undamaged, signing it correctly is usually a small fix, not a reason to panic.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an unsigned U.S. passport close to departure. Separate issues such as damage, a wrong name, insufficient validity for your destination, visa problems, or a lost passport need different actions and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate first steps and harm prevention. It is not legal, travel, or other professional advice. Airlines, passport officials, and border officials can apply destination-specific rules and make final boarding or entry decisions.
