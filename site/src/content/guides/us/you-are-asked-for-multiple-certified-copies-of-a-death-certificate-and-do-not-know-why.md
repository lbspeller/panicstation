# What to do if… you are asked for multiple certified copies of a death certificate and do not know why (USA)

## Short answer
Do not guess how many copies to buy. Make a short list of the institutions involved and ask each one whether it needs a certified copy, whether it will keep the copy, and whether another form of proof is acceptable before ordering more through the official vital-records route for the state or area where the death occurred.

## Do not do these things
- Do not mail the only certified copy you have unless an urgent, verified requirement leaves no practical alternative.
- Do not assume every organisation needs a certified copy; some tasks may only require a photocopy or another form of proof specified by the organisation.
- Do not pay an unfamiliar third party before checking the official state or area vital-records route.
- Do not rely on a photocopy when an organisation has confirmed that it requires a certified copy.
- Do not try to handle every institution at once. Work through a small batch and record what happens to each copy.

## What to do now
1. **Make a one-page tracker.** Use columns for the organisation, contact details, whether a certified copy is required, whether an alternative is accepted, whether the copy will be returned, and the date you checked.
2. **Choose up to three urgent institutions that are already waiting for proof of death.** Ask each one:  
   “Do you require a certified copy of the death certificate? Will you keep it or return it? Do you accept another form of proof?”
3. **Use the official vital-records route before ordering more.** For a death in the United States, certified copies are generally obtained through the vital records office for the state or area where the death occurred. Costs, eligibility rules, identification requirements, and ordering methods vary.
4. **Ask the funeral home whether it can help with the order.** If it cannot, use the official state or area route.
5. **Track each certified copy individually.** Record whether it was mailed, uploaded, shown in person, returned, or retained by an organisation.
6. **Ask about alternatives before buying additional copies.** Some tasks may only need a photocopy or another form of proof specified by the organisation.
7. **Keep one certified copy in reserve where practical.** Store it in a labelled folder and avoid sending it unless you have a verified need.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the final number of copies today.
- You do not need to contact every organisation in one sitting.
- Most non-urgent estate paperwork can wait for a calmer review. Check any urgent court, tax, benefit, creditor, housing, or insurance deadline with an appropriate specialist before delaying action.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to ask why each copy is needed before ordering more. Different organisations may have different proof-of-death requirements, and checking them one by one can reduce unnecessary orders and lower the risk of losing documents.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce pressure, avoid unnecessary spending, and keep track of important documents. Later estate, probate, tax, benefit, or account decisions may need help from an appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Requirements vary by state, area, court, and organisation. Confirm the requirements that apply to the situation before sending documents or delaying time-sensitive tasks.
