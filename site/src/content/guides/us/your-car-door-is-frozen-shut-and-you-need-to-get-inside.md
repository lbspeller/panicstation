# What to do if… your car door is frozen shut and you need to get inside (USA)

## Short answer
Do not use force. Check for a path to warmth first, try other doors, and use a "push / do not pull" technique to break the ice bond before applying de-icer.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull hard on the door handle or door edge as excessive force may damage hardware or weatherstripping.
- Do not pour boiling or hot water on the door or windows because thermal shock can shatter glass.
- Do not use an open flame to heat your key if it contains electronic chips or a remote fob.
- Do not chip at the door frame or seals with metal tools like screwdrivers or scrapers.
- Do not stay in extreme cold if you feel numb or are shivering; prioritize reaching a warm building.

## What to do now
1. **Check all other entry points immediately.** Attempt to open the passenger door, rear doors, and the trunk or hatch, as these may not be frozen even if the driver-side door is stuck.
2. **Clear snow from the exhaust pipe area.** If you intend to start the car later to warm the cabin, ensure the tailpipe is completely clear of snow to prevent carbon monoxide buildup.
3. **Use the "push / do not pull" method.** If the door is unlocked, lean your weight against the door with your palm and apply firm, rhythmic pressure inward to crack the ice bond at the seal.
4. **Apply lock de-icer or hand sanitizer to the cylinder.** If the lock itself is frozen, use a commercial de-icer; if unavailable, apply a small amount of 60% alcohol-based hand sanitizer to the key and insert it gently without forcing.
5. **Spray de-icer along the door perimeter.** Apply de-icer or a mixture of 70% isopropyl alcohol and water to the rubber seals where the door meets the car body.
6. **Apply targeted dry heat if a power source is available.** Use a hairdryer on a low setting, moving it constantly along the door seam and handle to melt the ice without overheating the paint or glass.
7. **Enter through a different door to warm the vehicle.** If you gain access through another door, start the engine and run the heater and defroster on high to warm the primary door from the inside.
8. **Contact professional roadside assistance for hazardous conditions.** If you are in a remote or unsafe location and cannot gain entry, call your insurer’s roadside assistance or AAA (1-800-AAA-HELP).

## What can wait
- You do not need to prevent future freezing right now; focus only on immediate entry.
- You do not need to clean the residue from de-icer or hand sanitizer until you are in a warm location.
- You do not need to inspect the weatherstripping for damage until the ice has completely melted.

## Important reassurance
A frozen car door is a common winter occurrence. Most doors become stuck because of a thin layer of ice on the rubber gasket, not a mechanical failure. Using a "push" motion rather than a "pull" motion is a standard technique that successfully opens most frozen doors without causing damage.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance for safe vehicle access in freezing conditions. It does not cover long-term winterization or mechanical repairs for damaged latches.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace professional mechanical or emergency services. If you exhibit symptoms of hypothermia or frostbite, seek medical attention immediately. Do not run a vehicle in a closed garage or if the exhaust is obstructed.
