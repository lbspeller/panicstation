# What to do if… you notice new swelling in both legs along with worsening breathlessness at night (USA)

## Short answer
Seek immediate medical evaluation, as this combination of symptoms often indicates acute fluid buildup in the lungs or heart dysfunction. Call 911 if you are struggling to breathe, gasping for air, or feel significantly unwell while lying down.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to wait until morning if your breathing is worsening or you cannot catch your breath while lying flat.
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital if you are short of breath, dizzy, or experiencing chest pain.
- Do not take additional doses of diuretics or "water pills" unless specifically instructed to do so by a clinician for this exact episode.
- Do not ignore rapid weight gain of 3 pounds or more over 24 hours when combined with these symptoms.
- Do not rely on a pulse oximeter reading to determine safety; a "normal" number does not rule out a cardiac or pulmonary emergency.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately if you experience emergency warning signs.**
   - severe difficulty breathing or gasping for air
   - coughing up pink, frothy, or foamy mucus
   - chest pain, pressure, or fainting
   - bluish or gray tint to the lips, face, or nails
   - sudden confusion or disorientation
2. **Seek same-day emergency department evaluation if symptoms are new or worsening.**
   - If you cannot reach your cardiologist or primary care physician for an immediate assessment, go to the nearest emergency room.
   - When speaking to medical staff, use these specific terms: "New-onset bilateral leg swelling and worsening orthopnea (shortness of breath when lying flat)."
3. **Sit upright in a supported chair and remain physically still.**
   - Propping yourself up with multiple pillows or sitting in a high-back chair can help ease the work of breathing.
   - Avoid walking, climbing stairs, or lifting heavy objects, as exertion can rapidly worsen fluid congestion.
4. **Gather a list of your current medications and known allergies.**
   - Include any recent changes to your prescriptions or any new medications started in the last week.
   - If possible, note the time your symptoms started and if one leg is noticeably more swollen, red, or painful than the other.
5. **Monitor for signs of a blood clot or pulmonary embolism.**
   - If the swelling is accompanied by sudden sharp chest pain that worsens with deep breaths, treat it as a Category D or E cardiopulmonary emergency and call 911 immediately.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the underlying cause (such as heart failure or kidney issues) yourself.
- You do not need to implement dietary changes or fluid restrictions until a clinician provides a specific plan.
- You do not need to determine if your symptoms are "serious enough" before seeking professional triage.
- You can postpone all non-essential household tasks and logistics until you have been cleared by a medical professional.

## Important reassurance
It is a normal physiological response to feel high levels of anxiety when your breathing is restricted, especially at night. This reaction does not mean you are failing to handle the situation; it is your body’s signal that medical intervention is required. Getting assessed now is the standard safety protocol for these symptoms.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and triage instructions for acute symptoms only. It does not replace a clinical diagnosis or a long-term heart failure management plan.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. If you are in doubt about the severity of your symptoms, always choose the safer option and call 911 or visit an emergency department.
