# What to do if… you receive an extortion message claiming your files were copied and you are unsure if it is real (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the payment method by immediately canceling the active subscription in your device settings and reporting the charges as unauthorized to your bank or the platform (Apple/Google).

## Do not do these things
- Don't delete the app itself — this does not stop the billing cycle or cancel the underlying subscription.
- Don't click links in text messages or emails claiming to be "refund links" — these are often phishing attempts; use official portals only.
- Don't wait for the physical paper statement to arrive — digital subscriptions renew automatically and often have short dispute windows.
- Don't ignore small "test" charges — small unauthorized amounts are often followed by larger fraudulent purchases.
- Don't share your login credentials with anyone claiming they can "reverse" the charges for a fee.

## What to do now
1. **Cancel the recurring billing immediately through your account settings.**
   - For iPhone/iPad: Open **Settings** → [Your Name] → **Subscriptions** → Tap the unknown item → **Cancel Subscription**.
   - For Android: Open **Google Play** → Profile Icon → **Payments & subscriptions** → **Subscriptions** → Select the item → **Cancel subscription**.
2. **Submit a formal refund request through the official platform portal.**
   - **Apple:** Sign in to **reportaproblem.apple.com**, select **"I'd like to request a refund"**, and choose **"I didn't authorize this purchase"** from the dropdown menu.
   - **Google Play:** Use the "Request a refund" tool in the Google Play Help Center; note that Google typically only handles these requests if filed within 120 days of the transaction.
3. **Notify your financial institution to initiate a dispute for unauthorized use.**
   - Contact your credit card issuer or bank and state that the charges are "unauthorized."
   - Under 2026 CFPB regulations, for disputed amounts over $50, issuers must generally provide a provisional credit within 10 business days while they investigate.
4. **Secure the account associated with the purchases.**
   - Change your Apple Account (Apple ID) or Google Account password to a unique phrase not used elsewhere.
   - Verify that **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** is active.
   - Check the "Signed-in Devices" list and remove any phone, tablet, or computer you do not recognize.
5. **Contact your mobile carrier if the charges appear on your cellular bill.**
   - Request an "Unauthorized Third-Party Billing" dispute.
   - Ask the representative to place a **"Third-Party Billing Block"** on your account to prevent future non-carrier charges.
6. **Preserve existing digital evidence for the dispute process.**
   - Keep any emails from Apple or Google regarding "New Subscription" or "Purchase Receipt."
   - Take a screenshot of the "Subscriptions" screen showing the date the unknown item was started.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific method of compromise (e.g., a "hacked" password vs. a family member's error) before stopping the billing.
- You do not need to factory reset your mobile device unless you see other signs of malware like unexpected battery drain or overheating.
- You do not need to close your entire bank account or Apple/Google account today; isolating the specific payment method is sufficient for stabilization.

## Important reassurance
Unauthorized digital charges are a high-volume issue with established recovery paths. As long as you cancel the renewal and report the charge to the platform or your bank promptly, you have strong consumer protections (including Regulation Z for credit cards) designed to limit your liability for fraud.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for stopping unauthorized digital billing. It does not cover long-term credit monitoring or legal action against third-party developers.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Your specific refund eligibility is determined by the terms of service of the platform (Apple/Google) and the policies of your card issuer. If you suspect your Social Security number was also compromised, visit IdentityTheft.gov for a full recovery plan.
