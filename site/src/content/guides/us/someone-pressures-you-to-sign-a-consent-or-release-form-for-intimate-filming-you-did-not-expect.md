# What to do if… someone pressures you to sign a consent or release form for intimate filming you did not expect (USA)

## Short answer
Prioritize reaching a safer pause. Avoid signing while you feel pressured or unsafe if you can safely do so, then contact someone you trust or a specialist support service before deciding anything.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign while you feel rushed, threatened, intoxicated, intimidated, or unsure what the form means if you can safely avoid it.
- Do not stay alone with the person if you can safely move to a public, staffed, or otherwise safer place.
- Avoid voluntarily handing over your phone, passwords, or account access.
- Do not keep negotiating or prolong contact when it feels unsafe.
- Do not blame yourself if you froze, complied earlier, or did what felt necessary to get through the moment.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move to a public or staffed area, go behind a locked door if that is safer, leave if you safely can, or call someone you trust. If you need immediate police, fire-department, or ambulance assistance, call **911**.
2. **Use a brief refusal if speaking feels safe.** Say: “I am not signing anything now.” You do not need to argue, explain, or make a final decision.
3. **Keep control of your phone and accounts where possible.** Avoid unlocking your phone, sharing passwords, or sending more intimate content.
4. **Contact specialist support from a phone or device that feels safe to use.** Call the RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline at **800-656-HOPE**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use RAINN’s online chat.
5. **If they threaten to share footage, reduce contact when you safely can.** You do not have to keep responding once you are safer. If it is safe and does not prolong contact, keep existing threats or messages rather than deleting them.
6. **Consider medical care if there was unwanted sexual contact or you have a health concern.** This is your choice. A healthcare provider or advocate can explain available care.
7. **Ask one trusted person for practical help.** For example: “Can you stay on the phone while I get somewhere safer?”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to contact police.
- You do not need to decide right now whether to speak to a lawyer.
- You do not need to decide right now whether to contact a platform, school, workplace, or family member.
- You do not need to respond to the person once you are safer.
- You do not need to work out what the form means while you are under pressure.

## Important reassurance
Pressure does not mean you have to make an immediate decision. Freezing, going quiet, or agreeing to something earlier to get through the moment does not make the pressure your fault. You are allowed to pause, leave, and ask for support.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about healthcare, platform reports, police reports, or legal options may need help from a specialist advocate or another qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you need immediate police, fire-department, or ambulance assistance, call 911.
