# What to do if… you are locked out of your email or cloud storage while traveling (USA)

## Short answer
Separate the urgent travel problem from the account-access problem. Start the provider’s official account-recovery route, then contact each travel provider directly to reconstruct the bookings you need next.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click account-recovery links from unexpected messages. Open the provider’s official app or official support page yourself.
- Don’t share passwords, one-time codes, or recovery codes with anyone.
- Don’t keep cycling through password guesses when the official recovery route is available.
- Don’t factory-reset or erase a device while it may still contain useful access, booking details, or verification options.
- Don’t report your physical passport lost or stolen merely because you cannot access its scan. A reported lost or stolen U.S. passport is invalidated.

## What to do now
1. **Protect the physical items you still have.** Keep your physical passport, payment cards, and phone with you. Move somewhere private before entering passwords or recovery details.
2. **Work out what is blocked.** Note whether the problem is a forgotten password, a missing two-factor code, a lost phone, a new SIM, an unfamiliar security alert, or an unexpected password change.
3. **Open the official recovery page for the affected account.**
   - **Google, Gmail, or Drive:** use Google Account Help and follow the account-recovery steps.
   - **Microsoft, Outlook, or OneDrive:** use Microsoft’s sign-in helper. Use the official recovery-form guidance if Microsoft directs you there.
   - **Apple or iCloud:** try Apple’s password-reset steps first. If Apple account recovery starts, follow Apple’s instructions carefully before continuing to use other signed-in Apple devices, because device use may interrupt or cancel the recovery request.
4. **Reconstruct the next booking you need without waiting for inbox access.**
   - **Flight:** open the airline’s official app or website, or contact the airline. Ask how they can locate or resend the booking. Have your name, travel date, route, and payment record ready.
   - **Hotel or rental:** contact the property or booking platform. Have your name, stay dates, property name, and payment record ready.
   - **Ground transport:** use the operator’s official booking-retrieval page or contact route. Have your name, travel date, route, and payment record ready.
5. **Use your bank or card app to identify travel charges.** Note the merchant name, charge date, and amount. This may help you identify which provider to contact.
6. **Use another reachable email address for re-sent booking confirmations.** Keep account recovery inside the locked account provider’s official process.
7. **Write down the minimum details for the next stage of the trip.** Save any recovered confirmation code, ticket number, property address, check-in time, and reachable provider contact route somewhere you can access offline.
8. **If your physical U.S. passport is actually lost or stolen abroad, use the Department of State guidance.** Report it promptly and contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate about replacement. Do not use this step only because the passport scan is unavailable.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to rebuild your full travel archive now.
- You don’t need to overhaul every account-security setting before the next check-in.
- You don’t need to recover every booking at once. Focus on the next flight, stay, or transfer.
- You don’t need to decide about reports or claims unless there was an actual loss, theft, or unauthorized account access.

## Important reassurance
The account lockout and the missing booking details can be handled separately. Travel providers may be able to locate or resend booking information after checking the details they require, even while the account-recovery process is still underway.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from the account provider, travel provider, insurer, or a U.S. embassy or consulate, depending on what happened.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Processes vary by provider and location. Use the relevant provider’s official recovery page and official U.S. Department of State guidance for your situation.
