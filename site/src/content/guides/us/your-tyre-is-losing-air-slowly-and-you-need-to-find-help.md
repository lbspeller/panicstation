# What to do if… your tyre is losing air slowly and you need to find help (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer place, inflate the tire only to the pressure on your driver-door label or owner’s manual, and treat it as temporary until a tire shop or roadside service checks the leak. If the tire looks very low, damaged, bulging, or loses air again quickly, do not keep driving; request roadside help or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving at highway speed if the steering feels off, the car pulls, or the tire looks visibly low.
- Don’t inflate to the PSI printed on the tire sidewall; that is not your vehicle’s recommended tire pressure.
- Don’t rely only on the TPMS light; it may warn only after a tire is significantly underinflated.
- Don’t use a sealant kit as a permanent repair, and tell the tire shop if you used sealant.
- Don’t stop in a travel lane or stand next to traffic to inspect the tire.

## What to do now
1. **Make a safe pause.**  
   Ease off the accelerator, avoid sharp steering or braking, turn on your hazard lights, and move to the nearest safer place you can reach, such as a parking lot, rest area, gas station, or wide shoulder. Keep yourself away from moving traffic.

2. **Check whether it is safe to drive only to help.**  
   From a safe position, look for a tire that is very low, sidewall bulging, shredding, cracking, smoke, or an object stuck in the tire. If you see any of these, do not keep driving; request roadside help or a tow.

3. **Inflate it correctly, only as a temporary step.**  
   Use the recommended cold PSI on the Tire and Loading Information label, usually inside the driver’s door jamb, or in your owner’s manual. If the tire drops again within a few minutes, stop and request help instead of trying to keep topping it up.

4. **Ask for a leak check, not a guess.**  
   Go to the closest tire shop or auto service center you can reach safely and ask them to check for a puncture, valve-stem leak, bead leak, rim issue, and tire damage. The shop can tell you whether repair is possible.

5. **If you don’t know who to call, use available systems.**
   - Check your auto insurance app, automaker app, credit-card benefits, or roadside-assistance membership for a roadside help button.
   - If you are an AAA member, request roadside assistance online, through the AAA app, or by calling AAA; benefits and towing limits can vary by local club and membership level.
   - Search for a nearby tire shop, mobile tire service, or tow service if you are off the roadway and not in immediate danger.
   - In some areas, state or metro highway service patrols may help with minor roadside problems or move a disabled vehicle to safety. 511 is a nationwide travel-information number, but it is not guaranteed roadside assistance everywhere.
   - In California, Freeway Service Patrol may assist on designated congested urban freeways and can be contacted through 511 where available.
   - In Florida, Road Rangers may assist on covered roadways; FDOT says motorists on the roadway can call *347.
   - If you are stuck in a live lane, near a crash, unable to get away from traffic, or in immediate danger, call 911.

6. **Give the key facts clearly.**  
   Say: “I have a slow tire leak. It loses air over about this much time. I inflated it today. The TPMS light is on or off. I have or do not have a spare. I used or did not use sealant.”

7. **Wait in the safest available place.**  
   Keep hazards on, stay away from traffic, keep your phone charged, and share your location with someone you trust if that helps.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide right now whether you need a new tire.
- You don’t need to identify the exact cause yourself.
- You don’t need to compare every shop or tow option; choose the safest reachable help first.
- You don’t need to clear the TPMS warning yourself before the tire is checked.

## Important reassurance
A slow tire leak is common and tire shops deal with it routinely. The immediate goal is not to solve everything at the roadside; it is to avoid a sudden loss of air while driving and get the tire checked safely.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Tire repairability, towing options, and roadside-assistance benefits may depend on your vehicle, tire condition, location, membership, insurance, and local services.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, insurance, or professional advice. If you cannot stay safe where you are, prioritize getting away from traffic and calling professional help.
