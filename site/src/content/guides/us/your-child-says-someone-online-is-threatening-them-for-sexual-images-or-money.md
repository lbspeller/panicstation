# What to do if… your child says someone online is threatening them for sexual images or money (USA)

## Short answer
Tell your child they are a victim of a crime and are not in trouble. Do not pay money, send gift cards, or provide more images; immediately capture the offender's profile URL and report the incident to the FBI and NCMEC.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay the demand, send cryptocurrency, or provide gift card codes (payment rarely stops the threats and often leads to higher demands).
- Don't delete the conversation, the offender's profile, or the account until you have captured the evidence.
- Don't punish or shame your child; they have been targeted by a criminal and need stabilization.
- Don't engage with, threaten, or try to "reason" with the offender.
- Don't forward, download, or share the explicit images of the minor with anyone other than authorized law enforcement.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilize your child and ensure physical safety.** Confirm the offender does not have your child's physical address or current location. If there is a threat of an in-person meeting or physical harm, **call 911 immediately**.
2. **Document the offender's digital identifiers.** Capture the following information before the offender deletes their account or you block them:
   - The full profile URL (e.g., instagram.com/[username]).
   - The exact username and any display names.
   - Screenshots of the threats and payment demands (do not screenshot the explicit images themselves).
   - Any payment IDs, usernames (e.g., Cash App $Cashtag), or cryptocurrency addresses provided.
3. **Report the crime to the FBI and NCMEC.** File reports through the official channels for child exploitation:
   - **Call 1-800-CALL-FBI (225-5324)** or submit a tip at **tips.fbi.gov**.
   - Submit a report to the **NCMEC CyberTipline** at **report.cybertip.org**.
4. **Use the "Take It Down" tool to prevent image spread.** If explicit images were shared, go to **TakeItDown.NCMEC.org**.
   - This tool creates a digital fingerprint (hash) of the image on your device so participating platforms can proactively block it without anyone viewing the actual file.
5. **Report and block on the platform.** Use the app's internal reporting tool to flag "Sexual Exploitation" or "Harassment" and then block the account.
6. **Contact financial providers if money was sent.** If a payment was made via a P2P app (Cash App, Venmo, PayPal) or bank, contact their fraud or safety department immediately to report that the transaction was part of a criminal extortion scheme.
7. **Contact the school if peers are involved.** If the offender claims to be a student or the situation involves school peers, contact the school's administration or designated safeguarding officer to ensure your child's safety at school.
8. **Monitor for acute distress.** If your child expresses thoughts of self-harm or is in extreme crisis, **call or text 988** for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight how to handle this with the rest of the family or school community.
- You do not need to conduct a full forensic investigation of your child's history or other apps right now.
- You do not need to delete your child's social media accounts permanently; pausing them is sufficient for immediate safety.

## Important reassurance
The person making these threats is a criminal using a calculated script to cause panic. Your child is not the offender; they are the victim of a sophisticated exploitation tactic. By coming to you, your child has taken the most important step in stopping the cycle.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization and reporting steps for acute online extortion. It does not cover long-term legal proceedings, criminal investigations, or psychological recovery.

## Important note
This information is not legal advice. Sextortion of a minor is a federal crime in the USA. If you believe a child is in immediate physical danger, contact local law enforcement or call 911.
