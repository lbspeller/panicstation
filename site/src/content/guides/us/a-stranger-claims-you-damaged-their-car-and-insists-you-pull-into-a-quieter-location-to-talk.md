# What to do if… a stranger claims you damaged their car and insists you pull into a quieter location to talk (USA)

## Short answer
Do not follow the stranger to a secluded or secondary location. Stay in a well-lit, public area with cameras, keep your doors locked, and call 911 immediately if you feel threatened or are being pressured to leave a safe spot.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow the other vehicle to an alley, side street, back lot, or any isolated area.
- Do not get out of your vehicle if the other driver is acting aggressively or if you feel unsafe.
- Do not hand over your physical phone or wallet to the stranger.
- Do not apologize, admit fault, or agree to pay cash to settle the matter on the spot.
- Do not drive to your home or place of work while being followed.
- Do not lower your window more than an inch if you must speak to them.

## What to do now
1. **Activate your hazard lights immediately to signal you are acknowledging the situation.** This shows you are not "fleeing" but are looking for a safe place to stop.
2. **Drive to the nearest well-lit, busy public location or a local police station.** If you are already stopped in an unsafe or dark area, put the car in gear and move to a gas station, grocery store entrance, or precinct.
3. **Keep your doors locked and windows up once you have stopped.** Communicate through the glass or a very small crack in the window only. 
4. **Call 911 if the other driver is being aggressive or insists on a secluded move.** Tell the dispatcher your location, the make and model of both cars, and that you feel unsafe exiting the vehicle.
5. **State your boundary clearly and calmly if you choose to speak.** Say: "I am staying here for safety. We can exchange insurance information through the window or wait for the police to arrive."
6. **Capture the other vehicle's license plate number and a description of the driver.** Use your phone to take a photo through your window if it can be done safely without escalating the person's anger.
7. **Exchange only the legally required information.** This typically includes your name, insurance provider, policy number, and driver's license number. You do not need to provide your home address or phone number if you feel it endangers you; the police report or insurance claim can handle these details later.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine who caused the damage or argue about the "thud" you may or may not have heard.
- You do not need to file an insurance claim or a DMV crash report in the next few minutes.
- You do not need to inspect the "damage" closely if doing so requires you to stand near an aggressive stranger.
- You do not need to sign any documents or "statements of fault" provided by the other driver.

## Important reassurance
It is a common safety tactic for scammers or aggressive individuals to try and isolate victims by moving them away from witnesses. Prioritizing your physical safety by staying in a public place or driving to a police station is a standard and responsible reaction to a high-pressure roadside situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for personal safety during a roadside confrontation. It does not cover the long-term legal or insurance processes for vehicle repairs or liability disputes.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and is not legal advice. Traffic laws regarding "stopping at the scene" vary by state, but most jurisdictions recognize the right to move to a nearby safe location if a driver feels in immediate physical danger.
