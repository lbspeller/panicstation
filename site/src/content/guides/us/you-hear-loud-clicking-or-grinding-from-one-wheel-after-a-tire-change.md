# What to do if… you hear loud clicking or grinding from one wheel after a tire change (USA)

## Short answer
Assume the wheel may not be securely mounted. Do not drive normally; only move slowly if the vehicle appears stable and you need to reach a safer place, otherwise stay out of traffic and call for help.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving to see if the noise goes away.
- Don’t get back up to highway speed.
- Don’t crawl under the vehicle on the shoulder.
- Don’t stand on the traffic side of the vehicle while checking it.
- Don’t loosen or re-tighten lug nuts unless you are fully off the road, have the correct vehicle torque specification, and can use the correct pattern with a torque wrench.
- Don’t move the vehicle again if you see missing lug nuts, lug nuts that look backed off, a tilted wheel, fresh metal scraping, or fluid leaking near that wheel.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safer before you focus on the wheel.**
   - Put hazard lights on.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, turn on your lights so the vehicle is easier to see.
   - If the vehicle feels stable and the wheel does not look obviously loose, creep slowly to a safer place, such as a parking lot, rest area, wider shoulder, or farther off the roadway.
   - If the wheel looks tilted, lug nuts appear missing or loose, grinding is harsh or continuous, or the vehicle feels unstable, do not drive it; call for roadside assistance or a tow.

2. **If you get out, keep yourself away from traffic.**
   - Exit on the side away from traffic when possible.
   - Move well away from the roadway.
   - If there is a guardrail or barrier, wait behind it.
   - Keep children and pets secured and away from traffic.

3. **Do a quick visual check only.**
   - Look at the noisy wheel from a safe position.
   - Check whether the wheel looks straight and seated normally, not leaning or shifted.
   - Look for missing lug nuts or lug nuts that look backed off.
   - Look for a loose wheel cover contacting the wheel.
   - Look for fresh scrape marks inside the rim or near the brake area, which may mean something is rubbing.

4. **If anything looks wrong, or you are not sure, call for help and do not drive.**
   - Call your roadside provider, insurer, manufacturer roadside line, or a tow company.
   - Say: “Possible loose wheel or lug nuts after a tire change; clicking or grinding from one wheel; vehicle stopped.”
   - If you are in immediate danger because of fast traffic, a narrow shoulder, poor visibility, or another roadway hazard, call 911.

5. **If the wheel looks normal but the noise was loud and sudden, keep movement to the minimum needed for safety.**
   - Move only slowly and only as far as needed to reach a safer place.
   - Stop again and arrange professional inspection before normal driving.

6. **Contact the tire shop that did the work.**
   - Tell them the noise began after the tire change and that you stopped.
   - Ask whether they want the vehicle to be towed back rather than driven.
   - Note the time, location, and what you saw.
   - Take a few photos only if you can do it from a safe place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is lug torque, wheel seating, a brake dust shield, a wheel cover, or another part.
- You do not need to negotiate with the tire shop right now.
- You do not need to decide who is responsible right now.
- You do not need to remove the wheel at the roadside unless you are trained, equipped, and fully off the road.

## Important reassurance
Stopping to check was a sensible safety move. A loud new clicking or grinding from one wheel after a tire change is a warning sign where slowing down, staying out of traffic, and getting help may help prevent wheel loss or further damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and get the vehicle checked safely. Repairs, fault diagnosis, and any later dispute with the shop may need a qualified mechanic, roadside provider, insurer, or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not mechanical, legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If conditions feel unsafe, prioritise getting away from traffic and contacting emergency services or roadside assistance.
