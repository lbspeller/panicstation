# What to do if… a stranger in a public venue asks you to go to a private area and you feel uneasy (USA)

## Short answer
Do not go with them. Stay in the public area, move to a staffed spot, and call 911 if you are in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow them into a back room, hallway, office, stairwell, restroom area, or outside area.
- Do not let politeness pressure you into going with them or explaining your decision.
- Do not hand over your phone, keys, wallet, or ID.
- Do not move into a doorway, hallway, stairwell, or other less visible area to continue the conversation.
- Do not go to your car, parking lot, or transit stop alone if you think they may follow you.

## What to do now
1. **Stay visible.** Use one clear sentence, such as: “No. I’m staying here.” Move toward the busiest staffed area you can reach safely.
2. **Tell staff or security what is happening.** Go to a front desk, cashier, bar, host stand, or uniformed employee and say: “I need help. A stranger is trying to get me into a private area and I do not feel safe.”
3. **Stay near other people.** Stand in a well-lit, high-traffic area. Ask a nearby person or staff member to stay with you if that helps.
4. **Keep your phone with you.** Call or message someone you trust with the venue name and your location if you can do so without delaying your move to a staffed area.
5. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger or need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance.** Be ready to give your location, explain what is happening, answer the call-taker’s questions, and follow their instructions. Do not hang up until the call-taker tells you to.
6. **Ask staff for specific help.** For example:
   - “Please ask security or a manager to stay with me.”
   - “Please tell this person to stop contacting me.”
   - “Please help me wait in a staffed area.”
   - “Please help me reach a safe pickup point or exit.”
   - “Please record this as an incident.”
7. **Make brief notes only if it is safe.** Note the time, a description, and what was said. Ask whether the venue can retain any relevant camera footage. Do not confront the person to gather proof.
8. **Leave from a safe pause.** Stay inside a staffed public area until you have a safe way to leave. If you think someone is following you, return to a staffed public area if you can do so safely and call 911 if you are in immediate danger.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to make a police report or a formal complaint to the venue.
- Writing a detailed timeline. A few brief notes are enough for now.
- Contacting a head office or posting online.
- Deciding whether the stranger meant harm.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to work out the stranger’s intentions before staying in a public area and asking for help. Feeling uneasy is enough reason to pause and avoid being isolated.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and help you reach a safer pause. Later decisions, including reporting or seeking specialist support, may need additional help and do not have to be made now.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, prioritize reaching a staffed public area if you can do so safely and call 911.
