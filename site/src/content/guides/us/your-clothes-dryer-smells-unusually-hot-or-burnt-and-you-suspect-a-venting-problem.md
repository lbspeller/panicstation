# What to do if… your clothes dryer smells unusually hot or “burnt” and you suspect a venting problem (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the dryer immediately, keep the door closed, and turn off the power at the plug or circuit breaker if safe. If you see smoke or flames, or if the burning smell is strong and increasing, evacuate all occupants and call **911**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep running the dryer to finish the current load of laundry.
- Do not open the dryer door if you suspect a fire or see smoke, as this adds oxygen to the flames.
- Do not attempt to investigate the source of the smell by dismantling the dryer or ductwork while it is hot.
- Do not pull the dryer away from the wall if the vent is smoking or extremely hot.
- Do not throw water on the dryer, as it is an electrical appliance.
- Do not restart the dryer until the entire venting system has been professionally inspected.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate and call 911 immediately if you see smoke, sparks, or visible flames.** If the burning smell is rapidly intensifying or if you feel unsafe for any reason, leave the building immediately and shut the door to the laundry area to contain any potential fire.
2. **Stop the cycle and power down the appliance.** Press the "Stop" or "Cancel" button on the interface. **If the plug is easily accessible and not hot to the touch, unplug the dryer from the wall.** 
   - If the plug is inaccessible, turn off the power to the laundry room at the main electrical breaker panel.
3. **Keep the dryer door shut.** This restricts the airflow to the drum, which can help prevent a small internal smolder from escalating into a full fire.
4. **Allow the appliance and venting to cool completely for at least one hour.** Do not touch the back of the machine or the exhaust duct until the heat has fully dissipated.
5. **Check the exterior vent opening from the outside of the building once it is cool.** Look for obvious obstructions such as:
   - Visible lint clumps protruding from the vent.
   - A stuck or weighed-down vent flap.
   - Debris, bird nests, or snow blocking the exit.
6. **Verify the status of the internal lint screen.** Remove the screen and clean it thoroughly, but do not reach deep into the housing or restart the machine to "test" it.
7. **Contact a professional for inspection and cleaning.** Arrange for a certified dryer vent technician or an appliance repair specialist to clear the internal chassis and the full length of the ductwork before using the dryer again.

## What can wait
- You do not need to clean the lint out of the wall-internal ducting yourself right now.
- You do not need to check for manufacturer recalls immediately.
- You do not need to notify your landlord or property manager until the immediate heat hazard has stabilized.
- You do not need to decide on a replacement appliance until a technician confirms if the internal components were damaged by the heat.

## Important reassurance
Taking a burning smell seriously is the most effective way to prevent a home fire. Most dryer fires are preceded by warning signs like restricted airflow and overheating; by stopping the machine now, you have significantly reduced the risk of escalation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected dryer overheating event. It does not cover fire suppression, gas leak repair, or technical appliance maintenance.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not replace the advice of fire department personnel or certified technicians. If you suspect a gas leak (the smell of rotten eggs) or an active fire, evacuate immediately and call **911**.
