# What to do if… your boarding pass or itinerary disappears from the airline app shortly before your flight (USA)

## Short answer
Switch to a backup channel now: open the airline website or your confirmation email and retrieve the boarding pass from there. If you cannot get a usable pass quickly, go to the airline kiosk or staffed counter and ask for the boarding pass to be issued or printed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t cancel or rebook just because the app looks empty.
- Don’t keep refreshing the app until your phone battery runs down.
- Don’t share your airline password, one-time login code, or full boarding pass screenshot with anyone who is not airline staff.
- Don’t commit to a long security line if you are not sure you are checked in or do not have any usable boarding pass; get airline-side help first.
- Don’t spend time reinstalling the app before trying the airline website, email, kiosk, or counter.

## What to do now
1. **Protect your phone first.**  
   Plug it in if you can. Turn off battery-draining apps. Try airport Wi-Fi and mobile data, but do not lose time repeatedly refreshing the same blank screen.

2. **Find your reservation details outside the app.**  
   Search your email or texts for the airline name, confirmation code, record locator, passenger name, flight number, and travel date. Keep that screen open or write the details down.

3. **Use the airline website as the first fallback.**  
   In a browser, open the airline website and look for “My trips,” “Manage reservation,” or “Check in.” Enter the confirmation code or ticket number and last name. If the trip appears, retrieve the boarding pass there.

4. **Use the email or text boarding-pass option if offered.**  
   Some airlines let you send a mobile boarding pass link to your email or phone after check-in. Open that link and make sure the full barcode or QR code is visible.

5. **Make one offline backup once the pass appears.**  
   Add the pass to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet if offered. A screenshot can help if signal drops, but treat it as sensitive travel information; do not post it online or send it to anyone except airline staff if they ask.

6. **If the website will not show a boarding pass, go straight to the airline kiosk.**  
   Use your confirmation code, ticket number, frequent-flyer number, or ID if the kiosk asks for it. Print a boarding pass if the kiosk offers that option.

7. **If the kiosk cannot help, go to the staffed airline counter.**  
   Say: “My boarding pass disappeared from the app, and I need a boarding pass issued or printed.” Have your ID and reservation details ready.

8. **If departure is close, choose the fastest staffed airline route.**  
   Look for your airline’s check-in, bag drop, customer service, or priority assistance area. Ask an employee which line handles boarding pass reissue.

9. **Handle the security checkpoint in the right order.**  
   At some TSA checkpoints using Credential Authentication Technology, TSA may verify identity and flight details using acceptable ID, but this varies. You still need to be checked in with the airline and have a boarding pass to board the aircraft, so follow TSA instructions and return to the airline if they cannot clear you forward.

10. **For an international trip, expect a document check.**  
   A missing or blocked boarding pass may mean the airline needs to check passport, visa, or entry-document details. Go to the counter rather than spending the last minutes trying to fix the app.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the app problem now.
- You do not need to update your phone, reset your airline password, or reinstall the app unless airline staff tell you to.
- You do not need to change seats, add bags, check loyalty details, or chase missing status indicators in this moment.
- You do not need to contact your credit card, travel insurer, or app support unless you actually miss the flight or are charged unexpectedly.

## Important reassurance
A missing boarding pass in an airline app does not automatically mean your booking is gone. App sync problems, login issues, weak signal, and document checks can all interrupt mobile boarding pass access, and airlines can usually look up the booking and reissue a pass through their website, kiosks, or counters.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get you a usable boarding pass and keep you moving toward screening and the gate. Later decisions about refunds, missed flights, app access, account recovery, or complaints may need airline, travel-agent, insurer, or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, travel-agent, or professional advice. Airport, TSA, and airline procedures can vary by airport, airline, route, passenger, and itinerary; follow instructions from airline and airport staff.
