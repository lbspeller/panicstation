# What to do if… your public transport pass in an app suddenly shows as invalid even though you paid (USA)

## Short answer
Preserve evidence of the error and your proof of purchase, then use a physical contactless card to pay the fare if possible to avoid a citation. Do not delete the app, as this may trigger a fraud lockout or prevent you from accessing transaction history for a refund.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t uninstall and reinstall the app immediately; many transit systems (like Token Transit or MBTA mTicket) limit device transfers and may lock your account for 24 hours if you re-log.
- Don’t rely on a screenshot of a ticket as your "active" pass; most US agencies explicitly reject screenshots due to security animations and may treat them as fare evasion.
- Don’t attempt to "force through" a turnstile or board a vehicle without a secondary payment method, as technical failure is rarely an acceptable legal defense for non-payment during a live check.
- Don’t hand your unlocked phone to an inspector; keep the device in your hand while showing the screen.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilize your device settings to rule out local errors.**
   - **Toggle Airplane Mode on and off** to force the app to reconnect to fare servers.
   - **Verify that Date & Time is set to "Set Automatically"** in your phone settings, as ticket validation often fails if the device clock is even one minute off.
   - **Disable battery-saver mode and any active VPNs**, which can interfere with the app's ability to ping the agency’s verification server.

2. **Secure evidence of the failure for your appeal.**
   - **Take a screenshot of the "Invalid" error message** or the blank screen where the ticket should be.
   - **Perform a screen recording** that shows you attempting to open the ticket and the app failing, ensuring the system clock is visible in the corner.
   - **Locate your email receipt or bank transaction** showing the date, time, and exact amount of the purchase.

3. **Check for an "Ambassador" or station staff member.**
   - **Look for staff in branded high-visibility vests** (e.g., LA Metro Ambassadors or NYC Transit staff) who can often provide a "troubleshooting" tap or override if an outage is known.
   - **Report the app error immediately** to the driver or conductor if boarding, rather than waiting for an inspection check.

4. **Use an alternative payment method if the app remains unresponsive.**
   - **Tap a physical contactless credit/debit card** or your phone’s native mobile wallet (Apple Pay/Google Pay) directly on the reader if the system supports it (e.g., OMNY, Ventra, or TAP).
   - **Purchase a single-trip paper ticket** from a vending machine if you are at a rail station to avoid the higher "on-board" surcharges or citations.

5. **Document the details of the specific trip.**
   - **Note the vehicle number, route, and time** of the incident.
   - **Save any physical receipts** if you are forced to pay a second time on-board.

6. **Submit a formal dispute or refund request.**
   - **Contact the agency's customer service department** via their official website or app "Support" button within 24 hours.
   - **Attach your screen recording and original receipt** to the request, specifically citing "technical failure" as the reason for the secondary payment or citation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to call your bank to initiate a chargeback yet; wait for the transit agency to respond to your refund request first to avoid a permanent account ban.
- You do not need to replace your SIM card or factory reset your phone.
- You do not need to decide on a new long-term ticketing method (like switching back to plastic cards) until you are off the current vehicle and safe.

## Important reassurance
App failures are documented by transit agencies and are often caused by server-side outages or synchronization lags that affect hundreds of riders at once. If you have a timestamped receipt and a record of the error, most major US agencies have a standard administrative process for dismissing citations or refunding duplicate fares.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. If you receive a criminal summons or a notice of debt collection, or if your account is permanently banned, you may need to contact a regional transit advocacy group or legal aid.

## Important note
Transit policies regarding app failures vary by jurisdiction. Many agencies, including NJ Transit and various commuter rails, hold the rider responsible for maintaining a functional device and may require a new fare purchase regardless of technical issues. Always check the specific "Terms of Use" within your transit app for the agency's failure-to-display policy.
