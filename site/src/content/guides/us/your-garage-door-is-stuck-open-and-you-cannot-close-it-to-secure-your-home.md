# What to do if… your garage door is stuck open and you cannot close it to secure your home (USA)

## Short answer
Secure the house side first: lock any door between the garage and the home, move keys and remotes inside, and keep people away from the garage door. If the door will not close easily and safely, stop trying to force it and call a garage-door repair service.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand under a partially open door or try to catch it if it drops.
- Don’t touch or adjust springs, lift cables, bottom brackets, hinges, rollers, or tracks under tension.
- Don’t keep pressing the wall button or remote if the opener is straining, grinding, or reversing.
- Don’t bypass, cover, tape, or defeat photo-eye sensors or other safety devices.
- Don’t try to force the door down if it is crooked, hanging, binding, or unusually heavy.
- Don’t leave the door between the garage and the home unlocked while you work out what to do.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Keep children, pets, and bystanders away from the opening, tracks, springs, cables, and lower door edge. If you think someone is on the property, a break-in is happening, or you are in immediate danger, go inside if you can and call **911**.

2. **Lock down the home side first.**
   - Close and lock the door between the garage and the house, if you have one.
   - Bring car keys, spare keys, garage remotes, wallets, tools, and visible valuables inside if you can do that without going under the door.
   - Lock any car parked in or near the garage.

3. **Do one quick obstruction and sensor check without force.**
   - Move only obvious loose items from the door path, such as a bin, box, tool, or toy.
   - If there are photo-eye sensors near the floor, wipe the lenses gently and make sure nothing is blocking the beam.
   - Try the wall control once after that check only if you can do it from a safe position. If the door strains, reverses, binds, or sounds wrong, stop.

4. **Stop the opener from moving unexpectedly if the door still will not close.**
   - Unplug the opener only if you can reach the outlet without standing under the door or climbing unsafely.
   - If you are confident which breaker controls the opener, switch that breaker off instead.
   - Put garage remotes somewhere children cannot reach them.

5. **Only try manual closing if the door looks stable and you already know how to use the release.**
   - Do this only if the door is fully open or level, not crooked, not dropping, and not hanging from one side.
   - Pull the emergency release only from a safe position, not from under the door.
   - Lower the door gently using the handle if it moves smoothly and feels controlled.
   - If it feels unusually heavy, binds, jerks, drops, or will not move evenly, stop immediately and step back.

6. **If the door still cannot be closed, switch to temporary security.**
   - Move portable valuables out of the garage if you can do it safely.
   - Turn on exterior lights and any cameras you already have.
   - Reduce visibility into the garage with blinds, a tarp, cardboard, or parked items only if you can do that without touching the door mechanism.
   - Use an existing fitted interior lock bar or slide bolt only if it is already installed and meant for that door.
   - Ask a trusted person to stay with you or help watch the property if you feel exposed tonight.

7. **Call for help based on risk.**
   - Call a garage-door repair service and say the door is stuck open and the property cannot be secured.
   - If there is no immediate danger but you are worried about crime risk, use your local police non-emergency number for advice.
   - Call **911** for any immediate threat, suspected intruder, fire, injury, or crime in progress.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact fault right now.
- You do not need to order parts, replace the opener, compare quotes, or plan upgrades tonight.
- You do not need to decide about insurance unless there has been an actual theft, attempted break-in, damage, or injury.
- You do not need to repair springs, cables, tracks, sensors, or opener settings yourself while stressed.

## Important reassurance
A stuck-open garage can feel exposing, but locking the house door, moving keys and remotes inside, keeping people away from the door, and stopping unsafe attempts are meaningful risk reducers. It is reasonable to switch from “fix it now” to “make the home side safer and get help.”

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later repair, security, insurance, or landlord decisions may need help from a qualified garage-door technician, locksmith, insurer, landlord, or local authority.

## Important note
This is general first-step information, not professional repair, legal, security, insurance, or emergency-services advice. Garage doors can cause serious injury if they are unstable, unbalanced, or under tension.
