# What to do if… your travel document is locked in a luggage-storage locker that will not open (USA)

## Short answer
Seek immediate on-site assistance for a manual override or contact the operator for remote unlocking. If you cannot retrieve your passport before your flight, you must pivot to the TSA ConfirmID process for domestic travel or the U.S. State Department emergency replacement process for international travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to pry, shake, or force the locker door, as this may trigger a security alarm or permanent mechanical lockout.
- Do not share your unlock code, QR code, or booking reference with anyone other than verified facility staff or the official support agent.
- Do not report your U.S. passport as lost or stolen to the State Department until you are certain it cannot be retrieved, as reporting it will cancel the document permanently and immediately.
- Do not wait for "off-peak" hours if your travel is imminent; treat locker malfunctions as time-critical logistics failures.

## What to do now
1. **Locate the nearest on-site staff member or security officer immediately.** If you are in an airport, station, or mall, find the customer service desk or transit police. Ask if there is a site manager with a master key or override capability for the specific locker unit.
2. **Call the locker operator’s emergency support number found on the unit or in your booking app.** Use the "Report a Problem" or "Locker Jammed" option in the app to trigger an urgent ticket. Request a remote reset or a dispatch of a technician if site staff cannot assist.
   - For app-based storage (e.g., Bounce, LuggageHero) located in shops: Ask the shop staff directly if they can access the storage area manually.
3. **Capture clear evidence of the malfunction and the locker status.** Take photos or video of the locker number, any error messages on the screen, and your digital receipt. This documentation is required for insurance claims and may be needed by the operator to verify your identity.
4. **Obtain an incident reference number and the name of the staff member assisting you.** If the locker cannot be opened immediately, ensure you have a written or digital record stating that your passport is currently trapped inside a malfunctioning unit.
5. **Assess your travel window and initiate the TSA ConfirmID process for domestic flights.** As of February 2026, if you are traveling within the USA and cannot retrieve your REAL ID or passport, you must complete the TSA ConfirmID form online and pay the $45 fee. Bring your payment receipt to the airport to attempt identity verification.
6. **Contact the National Passport Information Center (NPIC) for urgent international travel.** If you are within the USA and need a passport for an international flight within 14 days, call 1-877-487-2778 to schedule an "Urgent Travel" appointment at a regional passport agency.
7. **Notify your airline or transport provider about the document delay.** Inform them that your identification is physically inaccessible due to a mechanical failure. While they cannot waive security laws, they may provide guidance on rebooking or alternative document requirements for your specific route.
8. **Engage a U.S. Embassy or Consulate if you are currently abroad.** If your passport is stuck in a locker outside the USA and you must travel immediately, contact the nearest U.S. embassy for an emergency limited-validity passport. You will need to appear in person and provide proof of your upcoming travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal compensation claim for the locker fee or travel delays in this moment.
- You do not need to cancel your credit cards or other IDs unless you have evidence that the locker was tampered with or that a theft occurred.
- You do not need to notify your employer or family about a missed flight until the immediate document retrieval or replacement path is confirmed.

## Important reassurance
Mechanical failures in luggage storage are common and are viewed by operators and security staff as technical issues rather than criminal ones. While the physical separation from your document is stressful, established protocols exist both for locker overrides and for secondary identity verification at security checkpoints.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document retrieval and immediate travel pivots. It does not cover long-term legal claims against storage companies or the full renewal process for non-emergency passports.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or official government advice. TSA and Department of State protocols are subject to change. Always verify the current requirements at TSA.gov or Travel.State.gov, especially regarding the TSA ConfirmID fee and emergency passport appointment availability.
