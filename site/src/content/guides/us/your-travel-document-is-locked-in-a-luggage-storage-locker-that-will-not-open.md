# What to do if… your travel document is locked in a luggage storage locker that will not open (USA)

## Short answer
Get the locker operator or site staff involved immediately, stay at the site if it is safe, and create a written record. If your passport will not be recovered in time for international travel, start the official U.S. passport help route for where you are now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pry, drill, kick, or force the locker; it can create damage, security, or ownership problems.
- Do not share your unlock code, QR code, booking link, or ID photo with a stranger offering help.
- Do not walk away without the locker number, an incident or reference number, and a direct contact route for the operator.
- Do not mark a valid U.S. passport as lost or stolen just because the locker is jammed; once reported lost or stolen, it is cancelled and cannot be used even if you later recover it.
- Do not delay until the next day if you travel today or tomorrow, or if the storage site is closing soon.

## What to do now
1. **Find the nearest staffed help point.** Go to customer service, station staff, airport information, mall security, hotel reception, or the storage office. Say clearly: “My passport or travel document is inside this locker and the locker will not open.”
2. **Ask staff to contact the locker operator while you are there.** Ask for a remote unlock, maintenance override, or authorized opening by the operator. If it is an app or kiosk locker, use the in-app support route and any phone number or help button printed on the locker unit.
3. **Keep proof before anyone opens or moves anything.** Photograph the locker number, screen error, QR or booking page, receipt, payment record, time, location, and the visible condition of the door and lock.
4. **Get written confirmation.** Ask staff or the operator to email or write down the locker ID, the time you reported the failure, what they tried, who is handling it, and that you told them your passport or travel document is inside.
5. **Escalate if travel is today or tomorrow, or the site is closing soon.** Ask for a supervisor, control room, duty manager, or after-hours operator route. Ask when an authorized person can physically open the locker and what ID or proof they need from you.
6. **If the locker looks forced, empty, or tampered with, treat it as a security issue.** Ask site security to attend and record what they see. A police report is not always required for passport replacement abroad, but it may help confirm what happened if theft is suspected or an insurer asks for it.
7. **If you are in the United States and need a passport for urgent international travel, check the official U.S. passport agency route.** Passport agencies accept appointments for urgent international travel in the next 14 days, or for a foreign visa needed in the next 28 days. Use the official appointment route and do not pay a third party for an appointment.
8. **If you are abroad and the passport is missing, stolen, or will not be recovered in time to travel, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.** Follow their instructions; you may need to appear in person. If there is not enough time for a regular passport, consular staff may be able to issue a limited-validity emergency passport.
9. **Report a valid U.S. passport lost or stolen only when that is what has happened or you cannot regain control of it in time.** Reporting it does not replace it; it cancels the passport, and you must apply for a replacement before using a U.S. passport for international travel.
10. **Tell your airline or transport provider early.** Explain that the passport is temporarily inaccessible because of a locker malfunction and ask what they require for your specific route. Do not assume you can board international travel without the physical document.
11. **Contact travel insurance if you have it.** Open a claim file and ask what documents they want, such as the incident note, booking receipt, photos, police report if filed, and replacement passport receipts.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim compensation, or pursue reimbursement from the locker operator.
- You do not need to replace every other card or ID unless it is confirmed missing, stolen, or needed for immediate travel.
- You do not need to write a full timeline today; a short note with times, names, locker ID, and reference numbers is enough for now.
- You do not need to decide whether the whole trip is cancelled until you know whether the locker can be opened or an official replacement route is available.

## Important reassurance
A jammed locker can feel like a travel emergency because the document is physically out of reach. The next useful move is simple: keep the issue visible to staff, preserve proof, and switch to official passport help if recovery will not happen in time.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the situation, try to recover the document, and reduce immediate travel disruption. Later decisions about claims, complaints, replacement documents, or travel changes may need help from the locker operator, the transport provider, an insurer, or official passport staff.
