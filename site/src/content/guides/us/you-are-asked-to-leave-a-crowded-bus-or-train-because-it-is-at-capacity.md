# What to do if… you are asked to leave a crowded bus or train because it is at capacity (USA)
## Short answer
Move away from doors, platform edges, and the flow of boarding passengers. Ask operator staff for the next authorized boarding option and whether your ticket needs to be changed before you try another service.

## Do not do these things
- Do not push past staff or other passengers to get back on board.
- Do not stay close to doors or platform edges while the crowd is moving.
- Do not assume your ticket automatically transfers to another departure, route, or carrier.
- For Amtrak, do not leave an unused reservation unresolved. Contact staff or customer service promptly before the scheduled departure and ask whether to keep, change, or cancel it. Amtrak’s no-show policy can forfeit the value of a no-show segment.
- Do not discard your ticket, reservation details, receipt, or any rebooking confirmation.
- Do not let your group split up in the rush. Tell staff immediately if a child or another group member is at risk of being separated.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause point:** move away from doors, platform edges, stairs, and the main flow of boarding passengers. Keep your ticket and phone accessible.
2. **Ask operator staff for the next boarding instruction:** say, “I was asked to leave because this service is at capacity. What is the next service or boarding point I am allowed to use to reach **[destination]**?”
3. **Ask whether your ticket needs action:** say, “Can I use this ticket as it is, or does it need to be changed before I board?” Save any written confirmation or screenshot.
4. **If this is Amtrak:** ask station staff or Amtrak customer service whether you should keep the reservation, change it, or cancel it before the scheduled departure. Save any confirmation they provide.
5. **If this is a subway, bus, light rail, or commuter service:** follow staff directions, check the operator’s official service alerts, and ask whether you should wait for the next vehicle, use another entrance or platform, or take another route. Ask whether your current ticket covers that route.
6. **If you need disability-related boarding assistance:** tell staff what assistance you need for the next safe boarding attempt. Ask whether assistance is available at that station or stop.
7. **If no staff are nearby:** use the operator’s official help point, customer-service channel, or official app or website. Keep your current ticket until the operator confirms what to do.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to file a complaint or request a refund or travel credit.
- You do not need to compare every route or fare while the crowd is moving.
- You can write down the time, route, stop, and what staff told you once you are safely paused.
- You can send a brief delay message once your next boarding option is clear.

## Important reassurance
Being asked to leave a crowded service can be sudden and disorienting. Your immediate task is smaller than solving the whole journey: get clear of the crowd, keep your ticket, and confirm the next authorized boarding option.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the period while you are safely paused and arranging onward travel. Later decisions about refunds, credits, complaints, accessibility support, or alternative travel may need help from the operator or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Ticket, reservation, refund, and crowd-management rules vary by operator and fare type. Ask the operator to confirm your next authorized option before making changes.
