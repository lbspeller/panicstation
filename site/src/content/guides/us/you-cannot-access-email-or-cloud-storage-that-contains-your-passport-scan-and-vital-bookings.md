# What to do if… you cannot access email or cloud storage that contains your passport scan and vital bookings (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the account recovery process from the travel logistics by rebuilding your itinerary directly through provider apps, websites, or phone lines using your name and payment details while initiating official automated account recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click "account locked" or "reset password" links sent via SMS or unexpected emails; these are often phishing attempts.
- Do not share one-time verification codes (OTP) with anyone, even if they claim to be from the provider's security team.
- Do not attempt to guess your password multiple times in a row, as this can trigger a 24 to 72 hour security lockout.
- Do not factory reset your only remaining logged-in device.
- Do not assume you cannot board a flight without a printed scan; airlines can verify your identity using your physical passport and their internal manifests.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your physical environment immediately.** Store your physical passport, credit cards, and any mobile devices in a hotel safe or a secure, concealed bag before attempting recovery in a public space.
2. **Contact your bank or credit card issuer if you suspect a device theft or account compromise.**
   - Request a temporary freeze on your cards if you cannot account for all your devices.
   - Ask for a list of recent travel-related transactions (merchant names, dates, and exact amounts) to help travel providers locate your bookings.
3. **Initiate the official account recovery process for your specific provider.**
   - **Google / Gmail:** Use the official Google Account Recovery page. Use a familiar device and a known Wi-Fi network (like your hotel's) if possible.
   - **Apple / iCloud:** Attempt a reset through "Settings" on a trusted device first. If unavailable, use the Apple Support app on a borrowed device before falling back to the web-based iforgot process.
   - **Microsoft / Outlook:** Use the Microsoft sign-in helper. If two-factor authentication is unavailable, complete the "Account Recovery Form" (ACSR) which allows two submissions per 24 hours.
4. **Reconstruct your flight itinerary without access to your inbox.**
   - **Locate the booking:** Visit the airline’s website or app and use the "Manage Trip" or "Find My Booking" tool.
   - **Search by details:** If you do not have the confirmation code, use your last name, departure city, and date of travel.
   - **Verify identity:** If the online tool fails, call the airline's customer service and provide your passport name and the last four digits of the card used for the purchase.
5. **Recover hotel and ground transport reservations manually.**
   - **Call the property directly:** Provide your name and check-in date. Ask them to confirm the booking status and resend the confirmation to a secondary, accessible email address.
   - **Contact booking platforms:** If you used a third-party site (e.g., Expedia, Booking.com), use their "Guest Support" chat or phone line to retrieve your itinerary using your phone number or card details.
6. **Report a lost or stolen physical passport to the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate.**
   - **Submit Form DS-64:** This is the official statement regarding a lost or stolen passport.
   - **Apply for a replacement:** You must appear in person to file Form DS-11.
   - **Urgent travel:** If you have immediate travel (within 24–48 hours), inform the consular staff to request a limited-validity emergency passport.
7. **Create a temporary "communications" note on your phone or paper.** Record new confirmation numbers, hotel addresses, and the case number for your account recovery as you receive them.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update your recovery phone number or security questions until you have regained access and are in a permanent, secure location.
- You do not need to download or sync your entire cloud library; focus only on the documents required for the next 48 hours of travel.
- You do not need to file a local police report unless your physical documents or devices were stolen and you require a report for insurance or visa replacement purposes.

## Important reassurance
Service providers are accustomed to travelers losing access to their accounts due to lost phones or SIM card changes. Most travel reservations can be verified and managed using your name, passport, and payment history without ever needing to open your original confirmation email.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for accessing travel logistics when digital records are unreachable. It does not cover long-term identity theft protection or permanent account security overhauls.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or official consular advice. Recovery timelines for automated systems (Apple, Google, Microsoft) are managed by the providers and cannot be expedited by third parties or support agents.
