# What to do if… you are pressured to sign a settlement agreement or resignation letter on the spot (USA)

## Short answer
You do not need to sign merely because you are being pressured to decide in the meeting. Ask for a complete copy, say you need time to review it, and do not sign or send a resignation message until you understand what you are being asked to agree to.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign a severance agreement, settlement agreement, release, or resignation letter merely to end the pressure.
- Do not write a resignation email or message if you do not intend to resign.
- Do not rely on unwritten promises about money, benefits, references, or your separation date. Ask for the terms in writing.
- Do not forward, copy, or remove confidential employer information or files you are not authorised to keep.
- Do not argue every detail or provide a lengthy written statement while you are still trying to understand the document.

## What to do now
1. **Pause the signature request.** Say: “I am not signing anything in this meeting. Please give me a complete copy so I can review it.”
2. **Clarify your employment status.** Ask: “Are you terminating my employment, or are you asking me to resign?” Write down the answer, who gave it, and the date and time.
3. **Ask for the full document set.** Request the complete agreement, every attachment, any separation letter, any resignation letter, and any terms about pay, benefits, confidentiality, noncompete restrictions, nonsolicit restrictions, references, and the effective separation date. Ask for a printed copy or another permitted way to retain a personal copy.
4. **Record the immediate facts.** As soon as you safely can, note who attended, what was said, any deadline mentioned, any threat to withdraw the offer, and which documents you received. Keep your note on paper or a personal device, not only on an employer system.
5. **Check for an age-discrimination waiver if you are 40 or older.** If a severance document asks you to waive claims under the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, an individual waiver generally must allow at least 21 days to consider the offer and at least 7 days after signing to revoke it. If the waiver is connected with an exit incentive or other employment termination program offered to a group or class of employees, the consideration period is generally at least 45 days. Different rules can apply to settlement of an already-filed age-discrimination charge or lawsuit.
6. **Be cautious about resigning.** Unemployment insurance rules vary by state, and voluntarily leaving work without good cause can affect eligibility. The state unemployment agency determines eligibility. It is reasonable to say that you are not resigning in the meeting and need time to review the request.
7. **Seek timely help after the immediate pressure has passed.** An employment attorney or legal aid clinic may be able to review the documents. If you suspect discrimination, deadlines may apply, so ask for advice promptly after you have reached a safe pause.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide in the meeting whether to accept severance, negotiate terms, or resign.
- You do not need to give a detailed account of events while you are still processing the request.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to contact an agency or take longer-term action. First secure the documents you are permitted to keep and make a brief factual record.

## Important reassurance
A surprise meeting can make it hard to think clearly. Asking for a copy and time to review the document is a calm, practical response to a high-pressure request.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on the document wording, your state, your employment arrangements, and your circumstances, and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel physically unsafe or threatened, leave the conversation if you can do so safely and seek immediate support.
