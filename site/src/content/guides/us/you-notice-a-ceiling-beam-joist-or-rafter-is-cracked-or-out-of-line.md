# What to do if… you notice a ceiling beam, joist, or rafter is cracked or out of line (USA)

## Short answer
Keep everyone away from the area, avoid adding weight or vibration, and get urgent help from a licensed structural engineer or your local building department/code enforcement. If anything seems to be moving, falling, or at risk of collapse, leave the building and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand under it, climb into the attic for a closer look, or test it by pushing or pulling.
- Do not cut, drill, remove drywall, or remove framing to investigate.
- Do not add load above or near it, including attic storage, people walking overhead, or heavy furniture movement.
- Do not try to jack it back into place or add temporary posts unless a qualified structural professional has told you to do that.
- Do not stay inside to take photos if you hear ongoing cracking, see movement, smell gas, see sparks, or see falling debris.

## What to do now
1. **Clear and block off the area.** Move people and pets out of the room and out of any space above it, such as an attic or upper-floor room. Close doors if you can do so without standing under the damaged area, and prevent re-entry.

2. **Leave and call 911 if there may be immediate danger.** Treat it as urgent if you see active movement, fast-worsening sag, falling debris, loud ongoing cracking or creaking, damaged utilities, or any sign that part of the structure could fail.

3. **Reduce load and vibration from a safe place.** Do not use the attic or upper-floor area above the damaged member. Stop running, jumping, moving heavy objects, or using equipment that shakes the nearby floor or ceiling.

4. **Watch for utility danger signs.**
   - **Gas smell or hissing:** leave immediately. Do not use switches, flames, appliances, or phones inside. Call 911 or your gas utility emergency line from outside and away from the building.
   - **Electrical signs, such as sparks, burning smell, buzzing at fixtures, or exposed wiring:** leave and call 911 if there is immediate danger. Only shut off power at the breaker if the panel is away from the damaged area, there is no gas smell, and you can reach it without going under or near the suspect structure.
   - **Water leak above or near the cracked member:** avoid the area. If the shutoff is safely reachable away from the damage, shut off the water; otherwise wait for emergency responders or a qualified professional.

5. **Contact local unsafe-building help if it is not a 911 emergency.** Call your local building department, code enforcement office, or local non-emergency service line and say you have a suspected unsafe structural member in a home. Availability and names vary by city or county.

6. **Arrange urgent structural assessment.** Contact a licensed structural engineer or reputable engineering firm. If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager immediately in writing and say you have isolated the area because of a suspected structural safety risk.

7. **Document only from a safe spot.** Take a few photos or a short video from outside the room or another safe position. Do not enter the room, climb into the attic, or stand under the damaged area for a better angle. Write down when you noticed it and any possible trigger, such as a storm, leak, impact, or recent work.

8. **After immediate safety is stable, contact insurance if relevant.** If there was a storm, leak, impact, or other event, report “suspected structural damage” to your homeowners or renters insurer. Avoid major non-emergency repair decisions until the area has been assessed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to open the ceiling, remove drywall, or expose framing now.
- You do not need to decide the repair method, contractor, cost, or cause today.
- You do not need to prove blame before making the area safe and asking for qualified assessment.
- You do not need to keep using the room while waiting for answers.

## Important reassurance
A cracked or shifted beam, joist, or rafter can feel alarming, but the safest first response is clear and limited: keep people away, avoid making the load worse, and bring in the right help. Many structural problems are repairable once the area is safely assessed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions may need a licensed structural engineer, your local building department, your landlord or property manager, your insurer, or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not engineering, legal, insurance, financial, or other professional advice. If you think collapse is possible or anyone may be in danger, leave the building and call 911.
