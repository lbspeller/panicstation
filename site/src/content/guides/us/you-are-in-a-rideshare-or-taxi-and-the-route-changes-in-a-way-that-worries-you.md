# What to do if… you are in a rideshare or taxi and the route changes in a way that worries you (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the driver to stop at a busy public place if it feels safe to speak. Share your live trip or location with someone you trust. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel you have to argue with or accuse the driver.
- Do not hand over your phone.
- Do not choose an isolated place to get out if a public place is available.
- Do not open the door or try to get out while the vehicle is moving.
- Do not delay calling 911 if you feel in immediate danger.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a safe public stop if it feels safe to speak.**  
   Choose a specific nearby place with other people, such as a hotel entrance, store, gas station, or hospital entrance. Say: “Please pull over at **[specific place]**. I need to end the ride here.”

2. **Share your location or trip details quietly if you can.**  
   If you booked through an app, use its trip-sharing or safety tools if available. You can also text a trusted person your live location, vehicle description, license plate if visible, and destination.

3. **Check where you are without debating the route.**  
   Look for a street name, cross street, landmark, or direction of travel. If speaking feels safe, ask: “I expected to be going to **[destination]**. Why has the route changed?” Repeat your request to stop at the public place you chose.

4. **Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger.**  
   This includes a situation where the driver does not stop at a safe place after you ask and you feel threatened. Tell the call-taker your location first, then say that you are a passenger in a moving taxi or rideshare. Give your direction of travel and vehicle details if you can. Answer the call-taker’s questions, follow their instructions, and do not hang up until told to do so.

5. **Use a quiet emergency option if speaking aloud may increase danger.**  
   Use an in-app emergency tool if one is available. Text-to-911 availability varies by area. Call 911 if you can, and text 911 if you cannot safely make a voice call.

6. **Leave only after the vehicle has fully stopped.**  
   Move inside the public place or near other people. Contact the trusted person you messaged or call 911 as needed.

7. **Save details only when it is safe to do so.**  
   You can screenshot the trip record or route and note the vehicle details. This can wait until you are safely stopped if doing it now would distract you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide why the route changed while you are still in the vehicle.
- You do not need to write a detailed account, request a refund, or make a formal complaint right now.
- You can decide later whether to report the ride through the app.
- For a taxi, you can check your city or county’s official website later for the local taxi complaint route.

## Important reassurance
A route change can have an ordinary explanation, such as traffic or a navigation error. You do not need to work out the explanation before taking a simple safety step. Asking to stop somewhere public, sharing your location, or calling 911 during immediate danger is reasonable.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. Later decisions may need help from the app provider, a local taxi authority, emergency services, or a specialist support service, depending on what happened.

## Important note
This guide provides general information for immediate harm reduction. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.
