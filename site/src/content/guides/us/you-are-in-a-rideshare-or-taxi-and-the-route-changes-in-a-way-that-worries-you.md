# What to do if… you are in a rideshare or taxi and the route changes in a way that worries you (USA)

## Short answer
Initiate active monitoring immediately by using the in-app safety toolkit to connect with a live agent or calling a trusted contact to provide a witness trail. If the driver refuses to stop or you feel in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay silent to avoid being "rude" if your observations suggest a safety risk.
- Do not hand over your phone to the driver for any reason.
- Do not jump from a vehicle that is moving at high speed.
- Do not engage in a verbal confrontation or use accusatory language that might escalate the driver's behavior.
- Do not wait for an isolated area to ask the driver to stop.

## What to do now
1. **Open your own navigation app immediately to verify the route.** **Compare the car's current path to a secondary map (like Google Maps or Apple Maps) to determine if the detour is a standard traffic bypass or a deviation toward isolated areas.**
2. **Activate the in-app safety features if using a rideshare service.** **Tap the "Safety Toolkit" or "Shield" icon in the Uber or Lyft app to access the ADT Live Help feature, which connects you to a professional monitoring agent who can track your ride in real-time and call 911 on your behalf.**
3. **Establish a verbal witness trail by calling a friend or family member.** **State your current location, the vehicle’s license plate, and your destination clearly while on speakerphone so the driver knows another person is tracking the trip.**
   - Example: "I'm in a silver Toyota, plate [Number], on Main Street heading to the hotel. Stay on the line with me until I'm inside."
4. **Give a firm, clear directive to the driver regarding the route.** **Say "I noticed we've moved off the main route; please return to the GPS path or pull over at the next well-lit public area so I can exit."**
5. **Identify a safe, populated exit point.** **Direct the driver to stop at a visible landmark such as a gas station, hotel lobby, or grocery store rather than a private residence or dark street.**
   - Say: "Please pull over at that [Gas Station Name] right now."
6. **If the driver refuses to stop or locks the doors, call 911 immediately.** **State your location (current street or nearest landmark), the direction of travel, and the vehicle’s description and license plate number.**
   - Keep the line open even if you cannot speak; dispatchers can often use GPS data to find your location.
7. **Note identifying details once you have exited the vehicle.** **Take a screenshot of the ride details in the app or photograph the taxi's medallion number and license plate from a safe distance.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out the driver's intention or "why" they took a detour while you are still in the car.
- You do not need to engage with the app’s refund or customer service system until you are in a secure, indoor location.
- You do not need to decide immediately if you want to file a police report versus a regulatory complaint.

## Important reassurance
Sensory cues like a driver turning off GPS audio, missing multiple turns, or heading toward unlit roads are valid reasons to prioritize your safety. Taking steps to share your location or exit a vehicle is a standard safety protocol, not a personal slight against a driver.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and harm reduction for passengers in transit. It does not provide legal strategy for criminal proceedings or long-term safety planning.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety stabilization. In the USA, rideshare drivers are often independent contractors, while taxi drivers are regulated by local municipal commissions; reporting procedures vary by jurisdiction. If you are in immediate danger, contact 911.
