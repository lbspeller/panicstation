# What to do if… you have a painful erection that has lasted several hours and will not go away (USA)

## Short answer
Go to the **Emergency Room immediately** if an erection lasts more than two to four hours or causes significant pain. This is a medical emergency called priapism that requires professional treatment to prevent permanent tissue damage.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the erection to go away on its own once it has passed the two-hour mark.
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital if you are in severe pain, feel faint, or are impaired.
- Do not take additional erectile dysfunction medications or supplements to try to counteract the issue.
- Do not apply ice packs, take hot baths, or attempt forceful physical manipulation of the area.
- Do not allow embarrassment to prevent you from seeking help, as delays can lead to irreversible erectile dysfunction.

## What to do now
1. **Travel to the nearest Emergency Department immediately or call 911 if you cannot get there safely.** Do not wait for a primary care appointment or a return call from a doctor; time-to-treatment is the most critical factor for recovery.
2. **Tell the triage staff exactly how long the erection has lasted and use the term priapism.** State clearly if the erection is painful and if you have any history of blood disorders.
   - Mention if you have sickle cell disease or leukemia.
   - Mention if the erection followed the use of injections, pills, or a recent injury.
3. **Bring a list of all medications and substances used in the last 24 hours.** This includes prescription medications, over-the-counter supplements, and any substances used for erectile dysfunction.
4. **Request a private area for the initial assessment if you feel uncomfortable.** Hospital staff are trained to handle urological emergencies with discretion and will prioritize your privacy during triage.
5. **Follow all instructions from the medical team regarding blood tests or bedside procedures.** Doctors may need to perform a "penile blood gas" test or use a small needle to drain trapped blood to resolve the condition.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the priapism before seeking emergency care.
- You do not need to decide on long-term medication changes or specialist follow-ups right now.
- You do not need to worry about explaining the situation to family or employers until you are stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Medical professionals in the Emergency Department regularly treat urological emergencies including priapism. It is a known clinical condition, and the staff's priority is relieving your pain and protecting your long-term health. Feeling startled or overwhelmed is a common response to this situation.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and harm reduction only. Long-term management and follow-up care must be handled by a urologist or specialist.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute medical advice or a diagnosis. If an erection persists for more than two to four hours without arousal, seek emergency medical intervention immediately to avoid permanent complications.
