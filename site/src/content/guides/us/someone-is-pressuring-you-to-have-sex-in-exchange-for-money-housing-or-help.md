# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to have sex in exchange for money, housing, or help (USA)
## Short answer
Get to a safer pause and use a private phone or device if one is safely available to contact a sexual violence support service. You do not owe anyone sex for money, housing, or practical help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not meet them alone to talk it out if you feel pressured or afraid.
- Do not hand over your phone, passwords, ID, bank-account information, or medication to keep housing or money.
- Do not feel that you have to confront them or make a report right now.
- Do not put yourself at greater risk to collect messages, recordings, or other information.
- Do not assume that harm only counts if physical force was used.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Move to a place where you are less exposed to the person, such as a public place, a locked room, or somewhere with a trusted person. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Keep essential items with you if it is safe.** Take your phone, ID, medication, keys, and any payment card or cash you may need. Avoid meeting the person alone if you are afraid of what they may do.
3. **Contact confidential sexual violence support.** RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline is available 24/7. Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, text **HOPE** to **64673**, or use its online chat. Before sharing identifying details, you can ask what information is optional and what confidentiality limits apply.
4. **Ask for housing or practical-help referrals.** Call **211** or use **211.org** to find your local 211 service. Ask about housing, food, financial assistance, and other local support that may help you reduce dependence on the person pressuring you.
5. **Contact specialist support if you are concerned this may involve trafficking.** If someone is pressuring or forcing you to have sex for money, housing, or something else of value, contact the National Human Trafficking Hotline at **1-888-373-7888** or text **233733**. Before sharing identifying details, you can ask what information is optional and what confidentiality limits apply.
6. **Seek medical support if you need it.** If you have injuries, concerns about infection or pregnancy, or want medical care after an assault, contact a healthcare professional or hospital. RAINN can help you find a local clinic or hospital.

*If it is safe and you may want more options later, keep existing messages or voicemails; you do not need to gather anything else or decide whether to report right now.*

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to report to police.
- You do not need to confront the person or prove anything to them.
- You do not need to gather a perfect record of what happened.
- You do not need to make every long-term housing or money decision during the first safer pause.

## Important reassurance
Pressure tied to essentials such as money, housing, food, transportation, or paperwork can make consent not freely given. People can react in many ways, including confusion, numbness, compliance, or freezing. What happened is not your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the moment and reduce harm. Later decisions about housing, medical care, safety planning, or reporting may need help from a specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, counseling, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
