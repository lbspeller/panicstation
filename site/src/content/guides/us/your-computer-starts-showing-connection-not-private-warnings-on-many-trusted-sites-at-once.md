# What to do if… your computer starts showing “connection not private” warnings on many trusted sites at once (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all active work and copy your most critical files to an external storage device before clicking any repair or fix buttons. Automatic repairs often resolve errors by deleting corrupted metadata, which can result in the permanent loss of the associated files.

## Do not do these things
- Do not initiate the "Repair," "Fix," or "Scan and Repair" prompt until you have verified a secondary copy of your data.
- Do not restart the computer repeatedly if it fails to boot; every power cycle can further degrade a failing mechanical drive.
- Do not run defragmentation tools, disk cleaners, or third-party "optimization" software.
- Do not assume cloud storage is a sufficient backup if the local files are already corrupted, as the corruption may sync to the cloud.
- Do not ignore audible cues such as clicking, grinding, or high-pitched whirring coming from the computer chassis.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all background activity and new file creation.** Close all non-essential applications to prevent the system from writing new data to the potentially failing sectors.
2. **Select "Cancel," "Later," or "Skip" on the repair notification.** This prevents the operating system from starting an automated process that may overwrite damaged data.
3. **Connect a stable external drive or network storage device.** Use a destination that is physically separate from the drive reporting errors.
4. **Copy the most critical, irreplaceable files first.** 
   - Prioritize personal documents, encryption keys, 2FA backup codes, and specific project folders.
   - If the system reports an error copying a specific file, skip it immediately and move to the next item to minimize stress on the drive.
5. **Listen for mechanical failure symptoms.** If you hear clicking or grinding, stop the copy process immediately and shut down the computer; these sounds indicate physical head-to-platter contact, and further use may cause total data destruction.
6. **Execute a single repair pass only after data is secured.** 
   - On Windows: Use the built-in "Check Disk" tool through the drive properties or administrative command prompt.
   - On macOS: Restart in Recovery Mode (Command-R on Intel; hold Power on Apple Silicon) and use Disk Utility "First Aid" on the startup disk.
7. **Document the exact wording of any failure messages.** Take a photo of the screen or write down the specific error codes (e.g., S.M.A.R.T. Status: Failing) for use with warranty claims or professional recovery services.
8. **Contact the manufacturer if the device is under warranty.** Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, you have the right to seek a replacement for defective hardware, provided you have not caused the damage through unauthorized physical tampering.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical root cause (e.g., whether it is a software bug or a hardware defect) before securing your files.
- You do not need to decide on a replacement drive or a new computer immediately.
- You do not need to worry about organizing or cleaning up your files during the backup process.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a disk error warning is an automated safety feature designed to give you a window of opportunity to protect your information. It does not necessarily mean your data is already lost, but it indicates that the drive's self-monitoring system has detected a threshold of errors that requires your attention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for data preservation. It does not cover advanced data recovery from physically destroyed drives or long-term storage strategy.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional IT, legal, or data recovery advice. If your data is highly sensitive, business-critical, or subject to legal hold, contact a certified data recovery professional or your organization's IT department before attempting any repairs. Excessive "do-it-yourself" repair attempts on a failing drive can make professional recovery impossible.
