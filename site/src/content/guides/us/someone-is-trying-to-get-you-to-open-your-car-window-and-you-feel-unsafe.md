# What to do if… someone is trying to get you to open your car window and you feel unsafe (USA)
## Short answer
Keep your doors locked and windows up. Stay inside unless remaining in the car puts you in greater immediate danger or an attacker demands the vehicle. If you feel threatened, call 911 and move only if you can do so without creating a driving risk.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the window or unlock the door to talk.
- Do not get out to argue, negotiate, or find out what the person wants.
- Do not pass your phone, keys, wallet, or bag through a cracked window.
- Do not let a stranger direct you to a quieter location.
- Do not force a path through people or traffic.
- Do not handle your phone while the vehicle is moving.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the car.** Lock the doors, keep the windows up, keep your seatbelt on, and check whether you have a safe route to move away.
2. **Move only if it is safe.** If you can drive away without entering unsafe traffic or risking a collision, go to a nearby well-lit, populated place.
3. **Call 911 if you feel threatened.** Ask a passenger to call, call while safely stopped, or use hands-free calling only if it is already available. Tell the call-taker your location, what the person is doing, and any description you can safely notice.
4. **If you respond, keep it brief.** Through the closed window, say: “I can’t open the window. I’m calling 911.” Stop engaging after that.
5. **If the person says they need help,** offer to call 911 for them while you remain inside the locked car.
6. **If this started with a minor bump or dispute,** remain inside if you feel unsafe. Call 911 and, if you can move safely, drive to a nearby visible public place and tell the call-taker where you are going.
7. **If someone in an unmarked vehicle signals you to pull over and you are worried they may not be an officer,** acknowledge the signal with your hazard flashers, slow down, and look for a safe, well-lit place to stop. Call 911 without handling the phone while driving, tell the call-taker where you are, and follow their instructions.
8. **If an attacker demands the car or displays a weapon,** do not argue or resist over the vehicle. Prioritize getting yourself and any passengers away from danger and call 911 when you can do so safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the person’s intent.
- You do not need to exchange information, take photographs, or call your insurer while you feel unsafe.
- You do not need to confront the person or justify your decision to stay inside the car.

## Important reassurance
You may feel shaky, frozen, or uncertain. You do not need to judge the situation perfectly before taking simple steps to protect yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. Later decisions about reporting, insurance, or specialist support can wait until you are in a safer place.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Traffic laws and emergency procedures can vary by state and locality. Follow instructions from the 911 call-taker and emergency services.
