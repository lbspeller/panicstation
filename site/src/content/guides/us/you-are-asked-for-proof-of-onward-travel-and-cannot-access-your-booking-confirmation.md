# What to do if… you are asked for proof of onward travel and cannot access your booking confirmation (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the airline staff member what evidence they need to see, then focus on recovering an airline-issued booking confirmation that shows your name and the relevant onward or return journey. If you cannot retrieve it before the check-in or boarding cut-off, ask what later-flight or rebooking options are available before agreeing to any change.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue about the rule while your booking is still inaccessible. Focus first on producing the evidence or asking what alternative is acceptable.
- Do not buy a nonrefundable ticket in a rush unless you understand the price, conditions, and whether staff will accept it.
- Do not delete emails, app data, downloads, or screenshots while you are trying to recover the booking.
- Do not rely on a screenshot unless it clearly shows your name and the relevant journey details.
- Do not assume the same onward-travel rule applies to every nationality, visa category, destination, or route.

## What to do now
1. **Ask what evidence is required.**  
   Say: “What do you need to see for this journey: a booking confirmation, an itinerary, a ticket number, or something else?”

2. **Search for the booking confirmation.**
   - Search your email for the airline or travel-agent name with terms such as “confirmation”, “itinerary”, “receipt”, “ticket”, or “record locator”.
   - Check your airline or travel-agent account for saved trips.
   - Check downloads, saved files, and screenshots for an itinerary or receipt showing your name and onward or return journey.

3. **Use the airline’s booking-recovery page.**
   - Try “Manage booking”, “Find my trip”, or “Resend confirmation”.
   - Use the passenger name and record locator, ticket number, or other identifier you already have.
   - Check any airline loyalty account linked to the booking.

4. **Ask the airline desk to help recover the booking.**
   - Ask which identifiers they can use to locate it, such as your passenger name, travel date, route, record locator, or ticket number.
   - Ask whether they can print the confirmation or resend it through an available channel.
   - Ask a supervisor which alternative evidence is acceptable if the booking cannot be recovered immediately.

5. **If the onward journey is on a separate booking, show the clearest official confirmation you have.**
   - Show a confirmation that includes your name and the relevant journey date.
   - Ask staff whether the confirmation is acceptable for the specific rule they are applying.
   - If your onward travel is not by air, ask staff what evidence is acceptable before relying on a land or sea reservation.

6. **If staff say the request concerns entry to the United States under the Visa Waiver Program, treat it as important.**
   - The official eligibility rules include a return or onward ticket condition for relevant Visa Waiver Program travel, with itinerary-specific conditions and exceptions.
   - Show the booking confirmation or ask staff exactly what evidence they will accept for your trip.

7. **If time is running out, ask about options that buy time.**
   - Ask whether a later flight or rebooking option is available.
   - Ask about any fee or fare difference before agreeing.
   - Contact the airline or travel agent through an official support channel and ask for the confirmation to be resent.

8. **If you are not allowed to board, ask for a clear reason.**
   - Ask the airline to state the reason in writing if possible.
   - Save any messages, receipts, and rebooking details.
   - Note whether the airline says the issue is missing travel documentation or an oversales problem.

9. **Check the stated travel-document requirement if you need a second reference.**
   - Open the IATA Travel Centre information page and follow its Travel Centre link for a nationality-and-destination check.
   - Use the result to understand the request, but keep asking the airline desk what evidence they need to see now.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain or seek reimbursement.
- You do not need to rebuild your whole itinerary. Focus only on the onward or return evidence staff need to see.
- You do not need to settle later travel plans while you are recovering the confirmation.
- You can review any complaint, refund, or reimbursement question after the immediate travel decision is stable.

## Important reassurance
Losing access to a booking does not automatically mean the journey is lost. The immediate task is smaller: recover the confirmation, ask the airline to resend or print it, or ask what alternative evidence they will accept.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are asked for proof of onward or return travel. Later complaint, refund, or reimbursement questions may depend on the airline’s stated reason and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Entry rules and airline document checks can depend on your nationality, destination, visa category, route, and carrier procedures.
