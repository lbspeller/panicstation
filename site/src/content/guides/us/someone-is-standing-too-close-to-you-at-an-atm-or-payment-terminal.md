# What to do if… someone is standing too close to you at an ATM or payment terminal (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the keypad or terminal and create space. Do not enter your PIN or finish the transaction while someone is close enough to watch you.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay at the machine to protect the transaction if you feel threatened.
- Don’t keep entering your PIN while someone is close enough to see it.
- Don’t accept help from a stranger or let anyone touch the keypad, card reader, or your card.
- Don’t argue or stay to prove a point.
- Don’t count cash or reorganize your wallet or purse at the ATM.
- Don’t follow someone to another ATM or a different location.
- Don’t keep using an ATM or payment terminal if the card reader, keypad, or machine looks loose, crooked, damaged, or unusual.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Leave the immediate machine or terminal area. If you can do so without delaying your exit, press **Cancel** and take any card or cash that has already come out.
2. **Create space before doing anything else.** You can say, **“I need a little room, please,”** or leave without saying anything.
3. **Use a safer setup only if you still want to continue.** At a store, ask the cashier to pause or cancel the attempt and use a different register or staffed checkout. For an ATM, choose a different location, preferably a well-lit indoor location when one is available.
4. **Do not resume until you have enough space.** If you choose to continue, stand close to the terminal and shield the keypad with your hand or body while entering your PIN.
5. **Stop if the machine looks unusual or keeps your card.** Move to a safer place, then contact your card issuer or financial institution immediately using its official app, the phone number on its official website, or the number on the back of your card if you still have it.
6. **Get help if the person follows you, refuses to give space, or threatens you.** Go to store staff, security, or a busier place. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
7. **Secure the card if your PIN may have been observed or you notice an unauthorized transaction.** Once you are safe, use any lock or freeze option your issuer offers, contact the issuer promptly, and review recent transactions. Ask the issuer how to secure your card and PIN, which may include changing the PIN or replacing the card.

## What can wait
- You don’t have to finish the withdrawal or purchase right now.
- You don’t have to decide whether the person meant harm.
- You don’t have to confront the person.
- You can decide later whether to make a non-emergency report. Do not put off contacting your issuer if the ATM kept your card, your PIN may have been observed, or you notice an unauthorized transaction.

## Important reassurance
Stepping away is a reasonable choice even when you are not sure what the other person intended. You do not need to stay in an uncomfortable situation to avoid seeming impolite.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you reach a safer pause and protect your card and PIN. Later decisions about suspected fraud or reporting may need help from your card issuer, bank, credit union, or another appropriate service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
