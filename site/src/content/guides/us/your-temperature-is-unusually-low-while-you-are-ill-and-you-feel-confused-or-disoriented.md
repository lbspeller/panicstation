# What to do if… your temperature is unusually low while you are ill and you feel confused or disoriented (USA)

## Short answer
Call 911 now, or have someone call for you, if you are ill, unusually cold, and confused or disoriented. A temperature below 95°F / 35°C strongly supports treating this as an emergency, but do not wait to get help if confusion is present.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to sleep it off, drive yourself, or stay alone while confused.
- Do not use rapid, high-heat rewarming, such as a hot bath, hot shower, heating pad directly on skin, heat lamp, or sitting right against a heater or fire.
- Do not rub or massage arms or legs to warm them up.
- Do not drink alcohol.
- Do not keep taking cold or flu medicines while confused; set the bottles aside for EMS instead.
- Do not keep rechecking your temperature if it delays calling 911 or opening the door for help.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now.** Say: “I am sick, my temperature is low, and I feel confused or disoriented.”
2. **Get another person with you immediately.** If you are alone, call or text a nearby person to come over, unlock the door if you can do so safely, and stay on the phone with someone until help arrives.
3. **Move to a warmer, dry place.** Go to a warm room or shelter. If you are wet or sweaty, change into dry clothing if you can do it without falling or delaying help.
4. **Start gentle warming.** Put on dry layers, wrap in blankets, and cover your head and neck if you can.
5. **Warm the center of the body first.** Focus warmth on the chest, neck, and groin. If you use a hot water bottle or chemical heat pack, wrap it in a towel first.
6. **Sip a warm non-alcoholic drink only if you are fully awake and swallowing normally.** Stop if you cough, choke, vomit, or become sleepier.
7. **If it does not delay care, write down one temperature reading.** Note the number, time, and method, such as oral, ear, forehead, or armpit. Do not keep repeating checks.
8. **Gather medicines for EMS if you can do so quickly.** Include prescription medicines, acetaminophen, ibuprofen, sleep aids, alcohol, and any cold or flu products taken in the last 24 hours.
9. **If the person becomes unresponsive or is not breathing normally, follow the 911 dispatcher’s instructions and start CPR if told to do so.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether this is hypothermia, infection, a medicine effect, or a thermometer problem before calling 911.
- You do not need to take more medicine, eat a meal, shower, pack a bag, or repeatedly check your temperature before getting help.
- You do not need to decide what treatment you need; EMS and emergency clinicians can assess that.

## Important reassurance
Confusion or disorientation during an illness is a strong reason to get urgent help. Calling 911 for low temperature with confusion is a reasonable safety-first step, even if the cause is not clear yet.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to reduce immediate risk and get emergency assessment started. Later decisions about testing, diagnosis, treatment, or follow-up may need medical professionals.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. It does not diagnose the cause of a low temperature or confusion.
