# What to do if… a partner threatens to share private sexual images or information unless you comply (USA)

## Short answer
You do not have to comply. Get to a safer pause, stop negotiating in real time, and contact confidential support so you can choose your next step without pressure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send more images, videos, money, gift cards, passwords, or account access.
- Do not keep arguing or explaining intimate details in writing.
- Do not delete messages in panic if it is safe to leave them in place.
- Do not meet them in person to discuss the threat if you feel unsafe.
- Avoid using a phone or computer they may access or monitor for sensitive steps when you have a safer option.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause first.** If you are with them, move to a safer place when you can. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Stop the live pressure.** You do not need to answer immediately. If you reply, keep it brief and do not bargain or send anything new.
3. **Use a safer device if needed.** If you think they monitor your phone, computer, accounts, or internet use, use a device they cannot access before contacting support or changing settings. If it is safe, consider changing passwords, turning on two-factor authentication, reviewing active sessions, and signing out of unknown devices.
4. **Contact confidential support.** RAINN and the National Domestic Violence Hotline can help you think through immediate safety and your options.
5. **If it is safe and easy, save one copy of the threat.** You do not need to gather every message before asking for help.
6. **If the threat or content is online, choose only the reporting steps that feel safe.** You may report the account or content to the platform and block the account when that feels useful and safe. If intimate images or videos are involved, RAINN’s tech-enabled sexual-abuse page explains reporting and removal options. StopNCII may also help if you are currently over 18, were at least 18 when the image or video was taken, are depicted in it, and already have the file.
7. **If you want to make an official report, you can decide later or do it now.** For online conduct, you may file a complaint with IC3. You may also contact local law enforcement. You do not need to choose a legal label before asking for help.
8. **If sexual exploitation involves someone under 18, or an intimate image or video was taken when the person was under 18, use the NCMEC CyberTipline.** NCMEC’s Take It Down service may also help with an image or video already on the device. Do not download or resend a file to use that service. If there is immediate danger, call **911**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a police report, seek a protection order, or tell family or work.
- You do not need to name a crime.
- You do not need to collect every screenshot or detail before contacting support.
- You do not need to post publicly to defend yourself today.
- You do not need to make major relationship decisions during the threat.

## Important reassurance
Using a threat about private sexual details to pressure you can be coercive. Feeling frozen, ashamed, or unsure is understandable. This is not your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce harm and stabilize the moment. Later decisions may depend on your safety, whether anything was shared, and what help you want from support services, law enforcement, or a specialist advisor.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If taking a step could put you at greater risk, prioritize getting to a safer place and contacting confidential support first.
