# What to do if… you are abroad and realise your residence permit card expires before your return trip and you need a plan (USA)

## Short answer
Identify if your card is a 10-year or 2-year version; most 10-year cards are boardable even if expired (if abroad under one year), while 2-year cards require an original I-797 extension notice or a Form I-131A Boarding Foil from a U.S. Embassy.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to use a photocopy or digital scan of an I-797 extension notice; airlines require the original paper document.
- Do not fly to a land border (Mexico/Canada) to "sneak in" if you have a path to official carrier documentation.
- Do not pay "expediting fees" to third-party websites; all I-131A fees must be paid through the official USCIS online system.
- Do not assume a pending I-90 application automatically allows boarding without the physical extension notice.
- Do not surrender your expired card to airline staff or foreign officials.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the type of Green Card you hold to determine your boarding eligibility.**
   - Look at the front of the card: if it was issued for 10 years, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) policy generally allows airlines to board you with the expired card alone if you have been outside the U.S. for less than one year.
   - If it was issued for 2 years (Conditional Resident), you must have a valid I-797 Notice of Action (extension letter) to board.

2. **Locate your original I-797 Notice of Action if your card is a 2-year version or if the airline is hesitant.**
   - **For Form I-90 (Renewals):** The receipt notice currently extends the card for 36 months from the date of expiration.
   - **For Form I-751 or I-829 (Removing Conditions):** The receipt notice currently extends the card for 48 months from the date of expiration.
   - Check that the name on the notice matches your passport and expired card exactly.

3. **Check for a valid ADIT (I-551) stamp in your unexpired passport.**
   - If you visited a USCIS office before leaving and received a wet-ink stamp that says "Processed for I-551," this serves as a valid Green Card for boarding and entry until the date indicated on the stamp.

4. **Pay the Form I-131A fee online if you lack an extension notice or an ADIT stamp.**
   - Access the USCIS online filing system and pay the "Application for Carrier Documentation" fee ($575 as of early 2026).
   - Print the payment confirmation page immediately; you cannot proceed with the embassy appointment without this specific proof of payment.

5. **Schedule an emergency appointment with the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate.**
   - Request a "Boarding Foil" or "Carrier Documentation" via the embassy’s Consular Section website.
   - Bring your passport, the expired Green Card, the I-131A payment receipt, one passport-style photo, and evidence of your last departure from the U.S. (e.g., boarding pass or flight itinerary).

6. **Notify the airline of the specific CBP Carrier Information Guide rules.**
   - If an agent refuses boarding for a 10-year expired card, ask them to consult the "CBP Carrier Information Guide" or contact the Regional Carrier Liaison Group (RCLG).
   - State clearly: "I am a Lawful Permanent Resident with an expired 10-year card, which is boardable under current CBP policy."

## What can wait
- You do not need to finalize your I-90 renewal application while abroad.
- You do not need to worry about "abandonment" of status unless your total time outside the U.S. exceeds 365 consecutive days.
- You do not need to update your address or employment records until you have successfully re-entered the U.S.

## Important reassurance
Airlines are often more restrictive than the U.S. government because they face fines for boarding undocumented passengers. However, your status as a Lawful Permanent Resident does not expire simply because the plastic card does. If you have been away for less than a year, there is a standard legal process (the Boarding Foil) designed specifically for this situation.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for boarding and immediate re-entry. It does not cover "Returning Resident" (SB-1) visas for those outside the U.S. for over one year, nor does it provide legal defense for residency abandonment hearings.

## Important note
Airlines have the final authority on who they allow to board their aircraft, regardless of CBP policy. Always contact your carrier 72 hours before departure. This information is based on 2026 USCIS and CBP guidelines and is not legal advice.
