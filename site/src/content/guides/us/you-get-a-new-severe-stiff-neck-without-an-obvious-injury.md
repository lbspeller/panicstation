# What to do if… you get a new severe stiff neck without an obvious injury (USA)

## Short answer
New severe neck pain with stiffness needs prompt medical attention, especially if you also feel ill. Call 911 now if you have fever with severe headache and a very stiff neck, confusion, seizure, weakness, trouble walking, chest symptoms, or any sudden stroke-like symptoms.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force your neck to stretch, twist, or “work through it.”
- Do not try to crack your neck or use spinal manipulation.
- Do not use massage devices, traction devices, strong heat, or intense exercises while emergency warning signs are possible.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel weak, dizzy, confused, faint, unsteady, very sleepy, or your speech or vision feels different.
- Do not wait to see if it passes if you feel very ill, symptoms are escalating, or emergency signs are present.
- Do not exceed label doses or combine pain medicines without checking the labels.

## What to do now
1. Stop what you are doing and keep your head and neck in a neutral, supported position. Sit supported or lie down; avoid sudden movements.
2. Ask someone nearby to stay with you, check on you, or make the call for help if you feel faint, confused, weak, or too unwell to manage it alone.
3. Call 911 now if any of these are present:
   - Fever plus severe headache and a neck so stiff that you cannot comfortably bring your chin toward your chest. Do not force this movement to test it.
   - Confusion, extreme sleepiness, hard-to-wake behavior, new agitation, fainting, or a seizure.
   - New weakness, numbness, facial droop, slurred speech, vision changes, trouble walking, or loss of balance.
   - A sudden “worst ever” headache, or severe headache with neck stiffness.
   - Chest pain or pressure, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, vomiting, or pain spreading to the arm or jaw.
   - New loss of bladder or bowel control, or suddenly being unable to urinate normally.
4. Go to an Emergency Department now, or call 911 if you cannot get there safely, if you have fever with stiff neck, severe headache, nausea, vomiting, light sensitivity, confusion, new neurologic symptoms, trouble walking, or bladder or bowel changes.
5. If the pain is severe but stable and you have no fever, no severe headache, no confusion, no neurologic symptoms, no chest symptoms, and no bladder or bowel changes, arrange same-day medical evaluation. Urgent care may be appropriate for severe spasm-type pain, but choose the Emergency Department if you are unsure.
6. While arranging care, keep movement small and comfortable. If you can safely take over-the-counter pain relief, use only your usual medicine exactly as the label says.
7. Make a short note for clinicians: when the pain started, how quickly it worsened, whether you have fever, headache, light sensitivity, nausea, vomiting, confusion, weakness, numbness, balance changes, bladder or bowel changes, recent infection, immune-suppressing medicines or conditions, and any recent procedure or injection.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether this is muscular, nerve-related, meningitis, migraine, or something else.
- You do not need to search for neck exercises, buy a brace, book massage, or plan long-term treatment today.
- You do not need to make work, travel, gym, or routine plans until urgent symptoms have been checked.

## Important reassurance
Sudden neck pain and stiffness can come from muscle spasm or strain, but some serious problems can start in a similar way. Treating new severe symptoms as urgent is a cautious safety step, not a sign that the worst is definitely happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on staying safe, spotting emergency warning signs, and getting the right level of medical help. Later decisions may need a clinician’s assessment.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for professional care. If symptoms worsen, you feel seriously ill, or you are unsure what to do, seek urgent medical care.
