# What to do if… you discover an adult is sending sexual messages to your child in a game or app (USA)
## Short answer

Help your child pause the conversation and reassure them that they are not in trouble. Call 911 if anyone is in immediate danger or you believe a meeting is about to happen.

## Do not do these things

- Do not tell your child to keep chatting with the person.
- Do not reply from your child’s account or confront the person yourself.
- Do not pressure your child to explain everything at once.
- Do not blame your child for replying, engaging with the chat, or not recognizing the risk.
- Do not delete, forward, or publicly share the messages.
- Do not try to identify, trick, or meet the person yourself.
- Do not make major decisions about devices or app access while your child is overwhelmed.

## What to do now

1. Ask your child to pause the conversation and not send any more replies. Stay nearby and focus first on helping them feel safe.

2. Tell your child plainly that this is not their fault and they are not in trouble. Ask only what you need for immediate safety: whether the person knows where they are, whether there is an immediate threat, and whether a meeting is planned soon.

3. Call 911 if anyone is in immediate danger or you believe a meeting is about to happen.

4. If it feels safe and manageable, preserve the conversation without replying, forwarding it, or repeatedly reviewing it.

5. When you are ready, you can use the game or app’s reporting tool and block the account. You can also choose to report suspected online child sexual exploitation through NCMEC’s CyberTipline, call NCMEC’s 24-hour call center at 1-800-843-5678, or ask local law enforcement about making a report.

6. If nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit images or videos taken before your child turned 18 may have been shared, do not bargain with the person or send more material. Take It Down is an optional NCMEC service that may help. Do not download, forward, or ask anyone to resend an image or video in order to use the service.

7. Offer your child the choice of support from another trusted adult or a child-focused support professional. Keep the focus on safety, reassurance, and reducing pressure.

## What can wait

You do not need to decide today whether your child will ever use the game or app again, overhaul every privacy setting, or have a full technology-rules discussion. You also do not need to work out the person’s identity or collect more than a basic record of the conversation.

## Important reassurance

Adults who seek sexual conversations with children may use secrecy, flattery, pressure, or fear. A calm response and clear reassurance can make it easier for your child to accept support and tell you what they need.

## Scope note

These are first steps only. Later decisions about platform safety, emotional support, or reporting follow-up may need specialist help.

## Important note

This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, investigative, or other professional advice. The first priority is your child’s immediate emotional and physical safety.
