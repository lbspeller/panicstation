# What to do if… someone shares sexual rumours about you in a workplace or community group chat (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safer pause: mute the chat and step away from the screen. You do not have to answer publicly or decide everything now. When you are ready, you may privately ask a workplace contact or group admin to stop the sharing and remove the posts.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue in the group chat while you feel overwhelmed; this can escalate the situation or spread the rumor further.
- Do not forward the rumor widely to correct it.
- Do not confront the person alone if you feel unsafe or there is a power imbalance.
- Do not pressure yourself to make a public statement.
- Do not pressure yourself to decide immediately whether to use a workplace or external reporting route.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Mute the chat, put the device down briefly, and move to a place where you feel more settled. Ask one trusted person to stay with you or check in with you if that would help.
2. **Keep one copy only if it feels safe and useful.** If it feels safe and useful, save one copy of the messages before they are removed.
3. **For a community group chat, contact an admin privately.** Ask them to remove the posts and stop further discussion where they can. Use mute or block tools if that would reduce contact.
4. **For a work-connected chat, choose a workplace contact when you are ready.** You may use your employer’s usual complaint route, such as a manager, HR contact, or another designated contact. If the person involved is your usual contact, choose another workplace contact.
5. **Ask for practical limits.** You may ask for the rumor-sharing to stop in work channels and for direct contact with the person to be limited while the situation is reviewed.
6. **Treat immediate danger as an emergency.** If threats, stalking, or another situation create an immediate emergency, move to a safer place and call 911.
7. **Pause larger decisions.** Ask a trusted person or another support person to help you keep the next step small and manageable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to quit, make a public statement, or respond to everyone who saw the messages.
- You do not need to prove why someone shared the rumor before asking for it to stop.
- You do not need to choose an external employment route immediately. If the situation is connected to work and you may want to consider that later, check the official resources below for coverage and time limits. Do not assume an internal complaint pauses an outside filing time limit.
- You can decide how far to take this after you have had a safer pause and support.

## Important reassurance
Sexual rumors can feel humiliating and destabilizing, even when they are shared as text. You are allowed to move slowly, keep your next step small, and choose which support or reporting route feels safest.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: creating a safer pause, reducing contact, and stopping further sharing where possible. Later decisions about workplace complaints, external routes, or ongoing protections may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. An appropriate agency, workplace contact, or specialist support service can explain which options may apply to your situation.
