# What to do if… you get an alert that a garage door, gate, or secure entry opened unexpectedly and you did not do it (USA)

## Short answer
Assume an active breach is possible: move immediately to a secure location or exit the property and call 911 if you cannot confirm the area is vacant.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t enter the garage, yard, or home to investigate if you are unsure if someone is present.
- Don’t confront an intruder or attempt to perform a "sweep" of your property yourself.
- Don’t re-enter a building you suspect has been accessed, even if you think it is likely a technical error.
- Don’t repeatedly open or close the door via the app to "test" it while you are in a vulnerable position.
- Don’t touch or move items in the area once it is cleared, as this may disturb evidence.
- Don’t post real-time updates about your security situation or current location on social media.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe position immediately and stay there.** 
   - If you are currently inside the home, move to a room with a locking door, stay quiet, and keep your phone in your hand. 
   - If you are arriving at the property, do not pull into the driveway or enter the home; drive to a well-lit public area or a neighbor's house.
2. **Use digital tools for verification only if it does not delay your safety.** 
   - Check live camera feeds or doorbell video from your phone to see if there is visible movement or a person present. 
   - If your system allows it, remotely turn on all exterior and interior lights to eliminate hiding spots.
3. **Call 911 if you suspect a person is on the property or if you cannot verify the area is empty.** 
   - Tell the dispatcher you received an unauthorized entry alert and specify which door or gate was triggered. 
   - Provide your exact address, your current specific location (e.g., "locked in the master bedroom"), and whether any other residents are currently in the home.
4. **Notify your professional monitoring service if you have one.** 
   - If your alarm is monitored, contact the company to confirm if any other sensors (motion, glass break, or interior doors) have been triggered. 
   - Ask them to maintain a "live" status on your account until law enforcement arrives.
5. **Contact building security if you live in a multi-unit complex.** 
   - Ask apartment or condo security to observe the specific entry point from a safe distance. 
   - Instruct them specifically not to enter your unit until the police have arrived and cleared the space.
6. **Minimize hazard exposure from a distance only if the area is confirmed clear.** 
   - If your camera feed shows the garage or gate is empty and there are no obstructions, you may close it once via the app to secure the perimeter. 
   - If you cannot see the area, do not operate the motor; a closing door could trap an intruder inside with you or cause a confrontation.
7. **Document the incident details once you are in a stable environment.** 
   - Record the exact timestamp of the alert and any specific details seen on video, such as clothing descriptions or vehicle plate numbers. 
   - Secure and download any relevant video clips immediately so they are not overwritten by newer footage.
8. **Follow all law enforcement instructions exactly when they arrive.** 
   - Stay in your safe spot until officers confirm they are on-site. 
   - Keep your hands visible and empty when approaching or being approached by officers.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if this was a sensor glitch or a hardware failure right now.
- You do not need to check for missing items or property damage until the police have officially cleared the scene.
- You do not need to reset your Wi-Fi, change passwords, or update firmware in the middle of the event.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a security alert triggers an immediate fight-or-flight response, which is a normal reaction to a perceived threat. Prioritizing physical distance and professional assistance is the most effective way to manage the situation, regardless of whether the alert eventually proves to be a false alarm.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for the first minutes of an unauthorized entry alert. Long-term security audits, user permission reviews, and device maintenance should only be performed once the immediate physical threat is resolved.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving a potential intruder or immediate danger to life, contact 911 immediately.
