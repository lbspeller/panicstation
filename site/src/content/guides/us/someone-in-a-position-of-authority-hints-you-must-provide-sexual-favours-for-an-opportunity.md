# What to do if… someone in a position of authority hints you must provide sexual favours for an opportunity (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safe pause and stop any private one-to-one contact with the individual. Preserve all evidence of the interaction and seek confidential support before making any reporting decisions.

## Do not do these things
- Don't say yes or "play along" to protect the opportunity or your safety if you do not want to.
- Don't meet the person alone in a private or isolated place (offices after hours, hotel rooms, cars, or private residences).
- Don't delete any communications including texts, emails, DMs, calendar invites, or call logs.
- Don't sign any documents, non-disparagement agreements, or settlement offers without independent legal review.
- Don't confront the person alone or attempt to "investigate" their intent.
- Don't feel pressured to report immediately to the organization until you understand their confidentiality rules.

## What to do now
1. **End the interaction and create immediate distance.** Use a neutral exit line and move to a public or populated space.
   - "I have a meeting to get to."
   - "I am not comfortable with this conversation."
   - "Please put any professional requests in an email."
2. **Move all future communication to searchable, written formats.** Avoid private phone calls or unrecorded meetings. If a meeting is required, request that a third party be present or meet in a public location.
3. **Make a detailed, contemporaneous record of the event.** Write down exactly what happened while it is fresh in your memory.
   - Include date, time, and specific location.
   - Quote the individual as accurately as possible.
   - Describe the opportunity mentioned (grade, promotion, housing).
   - Note anyone who may have seen you enter or leave the meeting.
4. **Preserve digital and physical evidence immediately.** Take screenshots of messages and save them to a personal device or cloud account that you alone control.
   - Save voicemails and emails.
   - Keep copies of any documents related to the "opportunity" (job descriptions, syllabi, or lease agreements).
5. **Identify a confidential resource before sharing names.** In school (Title IX) or workplace settings, many employees are "mandated reporters" and must report what you tell them.
   - Ask: "Are you a confidential resource who can speak with me without triggering a formal report?"
   - Contact a national hotline or a local advocacy center for truly confidential advice.
6. **Tell one trusted personal contact.** Sharing the burden with a friend or family member can help reduce the isolation of the situation and serve as a "safety check" for your well-being.
7. **Prioritize physical safety if threats are made.** If you feel you are in immediate danger of physical harm, get to a safe location and call **911**.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to file a formal complaint or lawsuit.
- You do not need to prove the person’s "intent" or have a recording of the incident to seek support.
- You do not have to quit your job or withdraw from your program today.
- You do not have to "confirm" with the person what they meant by their hint.

## Important reassurance
When someone uses their power to suggest sexual favors, it is common to feel frozen, confused, or even "polite" to de-escalate the moment. These reactions are biological safety responses and do not imply consent or responsibility for the individual's behavior. Resources exist to help navigate the professional or academic fallout of these situations.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. Navigating legal rights under Title VII (employment), Title IX (education), or the Fair Housing Act involves complex procedural timelines and varying state laws.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and is not legal or medical advice. Procedures for reporting vary by jurisdiction and institution. For specific legal guidance, consult a qualified attorney. For immediate emotional support, contact a national sexual assault hotline.
