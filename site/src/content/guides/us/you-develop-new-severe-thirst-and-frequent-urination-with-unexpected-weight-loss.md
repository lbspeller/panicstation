# What to do if… you develop new severe thirst and frequent urination with unexpected weight loss (USA)

## Short answer
Seek same-day medical evaluation to check for dangerously high blood sugar or new-onset diabetes. If you have vomiting, confusion, or trouble breathing, call 911 or go to the Emergency Room immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the combination of new thirst, frequent peeing, and weight loss.
- Do not wait for a routine appointment if symptoms are progressing.
- Do not restrict your water intake to try and stop the urination.
- Do not attempt to self-treat with someone else’s medication or "water pills."
- Do not perform intense exercise if you feel weak, dizzy, or nauseated.
- Do not drive yourself to the clinic if you feel faint or confused.

## What to do now
1. **Assess yourself for emergency "red flag" symptoms.**
   - Call 911 or go to the nearest ER immediately if you experience:
   - persistent vomiting or inability to keep water down
   - abdominal pain that is sharp or severe
   - shortness of breath or very deep, rapid breathing
   - breath that smells "fruity" (like fruit-flavored gum or nail polish remover)
   - confusion, extreme sleepiness, or difficulty staying awake
2. **Contact your primary care physician or a same-day clinic immediately.**
   - **Tell the scheduler specifically that you have "new-onset severe thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss."**
   - Request a "same-day urgent evaluation" for possible high blood sugar.
   - If your doctor cannot see you within hours, go to an Urgent Care center.
3. **Check your blood sugar if you have access to a glucose meter.**
   - **Treat a reading of 200 mg/dL or higher as an urgent medical priority if accompanied by symptoms.**
   - If the reading is over 300 mg/dL or the meter reads "HI," seek emergency care immediately.
4. **Check for ketones if you have access to urine or blood ketone strips.**
   - **Follow the instructions on the kit and go to the ER if results show "Moderate" or "Large/High" ketones.**
   - High ketones indicate a medical emergency called Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA).
5. **Maintain hydration while waiting for care.**
   - **Sip plain water regularly to replace fluids lost through urination.**
   - Avoid sugary sodas, juices, or alcohol, as these will likely worsen the condition.
6. **Note your specific symptoms for the clinician.**
   - **Write down the approximate date thirst started, how much weight you have lost, and if you are waking up multiple times at night to urinate.**
   - Mention any recent illnesses, infections, or new medications like steroids.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if this is "Type 1" or "Type 2" diabetes right now.
- You do not need to start a restrictive diet or "sugar detox" before your evaluation.
- You do not need to purchase specialized medical equipment today unless instructed by a doctor.
- You can wait to discuss long-term lifestyle changes until after your blood sugar is stabilized.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled or anxious when noticing rapid changes in your body. Identifying these symptoms and seeking care today is the most effective way to prevent the situation from becoming a crisis. Medical professionals see these symptoms frequently and have clear protocols to stabilize your health quickly.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and risk reduction. It does not replace a clinical diagnosis or a professional treatment plan.

## Important note
High blood sugar can become life-threatening if it leads to ketoacidosis. If you cannot keep fluids down or feel mentally "fuzzy," do not wait for a call back from a clinic; seek emergency medical intervention immediately.
