# What to do if… you notice scorch marks around a ceiling light fitting and you’re unsure what caused them (USA)

## Short answer
Stop using the light immediately and turn off the wall switch. If you can safely identify the correct circuit at your breaker panel, turn it off; otherwise, leave the switch off and call a licensed electrician.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t turn the light back on to "test" or show someone else the problem.
- Don’t touch the fixture, bulbs, or the scorched ceiling area while the power is on.
- Don’t attempt to clean, paint over, or hide the marks before a professional inspection.
- Don’t change the bulb or replace the fixture yourself.
- Don’t reset a tripped breaker if it shuts off again immediately.
- Don’t ignore a faint "fishy," "metallic," or "burning plastic" smell.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate fire indicators from a distance:** look for **visible smoke**, **glowing embers**, **active sparks**, or a **strong burning odor**.
2. **Evacuate and call 911 if there is active fire or smoke:**
   - **Get everyone out of the building immediately.**
   - **Close the door to the room with the fixture** if it is safe to do so.
   - **Call 911** and report a "possible electrical fire in a ceiling fixture."
3. **Isolate the power if there is no immediate fire:**
   - **Turn the light switch to the OFF position.**
   - **Locate your electrical breaker panel.**
   - **Flip the breaker for that specific room or light to OFF** only if the panel is clearly labeled and you have dry hands and stable footing.
   - **Leave the panel alone** if you are unsure which breaker is correct or if the panel looks damaged / rusted.
4. **Clear the area below the fixture:** move flammable items like **bedding**, **paper**, **clothing**, or **curtains** away from the space directly under the scorched area to prevent ignition from falling sparks or debris.
5. **Contact a professional for an inspection:**
   - **If you rent:** notify your **landlord or property manager** immediately. State that you have "scorch marks and evidence of overheating at a ceiling fixture" and that the circuit is currently isolated.
   - **If you own:** call a **licensed electrician**. Inform them of the scorch marks and any other symptoms like **flickering**, **buzzing**, or **tripped breakers**.
6. **Take photos without touching the hazard:** capture the marks from several angles to show the electrician or for insurance purposes, but **do not use a ladder** to get closer if the power is still live.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine if the cause was an "over-wattage" bulb, a loose wire, or a fixture failure right now.
- You don’t need to worry about the cosmetic repair of the ceiling until the electrical fault is fixed.
- You don’t need to file an insurance claim until a licensed professional has confirmed the extent of the damage.

## Important reassurance
Noticing scorch marks before a fire starts is a successful catch. Isolating the power is the most effective way to stop the heat generation immediately. It is normal to feel anxious when seeing signs of electrical damage; taking the step to turn off the switch significantly reduces the immediate risk.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. A licensed electrician must perform an on-site inspection to identify the fault (such as arcing or overheating) and certify the repair.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not replace the judgment of emergency responders or licensed professionals. Always prioritize evacuation if you suspect an active fire.
