# What to do if… you notice your water meter is moving even though all taps and appliances are off (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate your property by closing the main house shutoff valve, then check the meter again to determine if the leak is inside your home or in the underground service line.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand in standing water or touch electrical panels if the leak is near outlets, breakers, or wiring.
- Do not force a stuck or corroded main shutoff valve; applying excessive torque can shear the valve stem and cause an uncontrollable leak.
- Do not ignore a moving low-flow indicator, even if it is slow; a small leak can waste over 9,000 gallons annually.
- Do not dig in the area between the meter and your house to "find" the leak without a utility-marked survey (811).
- Do not run any fixtures, icemakers, or irrigation cycles while performing these checks.

## What to do now
1. **Locate your home's main interior shutoff valve and close it completely.** This valve is typically located where the main water line enters the structure, often in a basement, crawlspace, garage, or near the water heater.
2. **Observe the water meter's leak indicator for at least two minutes.**
   - On analog meters, look for a small rotating triangle, star, or gear (the "low-flow indicator").
   - On digital or smart meters, look for a flashing plus sign (+), a water droplet icon, or a non-zero "flow rate" on the LCD screen.
3. **Determine the leak's "zone" based on the meter's movement.**
   - If the indicator **stops moving** when the house valve is closed, the leak is located inside the house or in an irrigation system connected after that valve.
   - If the indicator **continues moving** while the house valve is closed, the leak is located in the service line between the water meter and your house.
4. **Inspect "silent" indoor culprits if the meter stopped moving.** Check all toilets by placing a few drops of food coloring in the tank; if color appears in the bowl after 15 minutes without flushing, the flapper valve is leaking.
5. **Listen for the sound of running water or hissing in quiet areas of the house.** Check the water heater's pressure relief valve, whole-house humidifiers, and evaporative coolers, as these can fail "open" and cause continuous flow.
6. **Photograph the meter dial and record the current reading.** **Wait exactly two hours without using any water and take a second reading.** Any change in the numbers confirms a slow leak that may not be visible on the low-flow indicator.
7. **Contact the appropriate professional based on your "zone" test.**
   - For leaks **inside the house**, contact a licensed plumber.
   - For leaks **between the meter and the house**, contact your water utility to verify responsibility boundaries before hiring a contractor for underground repair.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact hole in a pipe or the specific failed seal immediately.
- You do not need to replace your entire plumbing system based on a single leak.
- You do not need to negotiate billing credits with the utility until the leak is professionally verified and repaired.

## Important reassurance
A moving meter is a clear signal that allows you to take action before structural damage occurs. By using the main shutoff valve to isolate the "zone" of the leak, you have already completed the most difficult diagnostic step used by professionals.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and isolation procedures for suspected continuous water flow. It does not cover repair techniques for specific fixtures or the legalities of utility service line ownership, which vary by municipality.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a substitute for professional plumbing or utility inspections. If you observe structural shifting, water spraying near electricity, or a sudden loss of ground stability (sinkholes), evacuate the area and call emergency services or your utility's 24-hour emergency line.
