# What to do if… someone in a car is circling the area where you are parked (USA)

## Short answer
If you are already inside your car, keep the doors locked and windows up. If you are outside it, move into a nearby staffed building if you can reach it without approaching the other vehicle; call 911 if you believe there is an emergency or a crime may be in progress.

## Do not do these things
- Do not approach the other vehicle or confront the driver.
- Do not get out of your car if you are already in a safer locked position.
- Do not drive somewhere isolated to test whether the vehicle follows you.
- Do not drive straight home if you think you are being followed.
- Do not take photographs or record video if doing so may draw attention or increase risk.
- Do not handle your phone while driving.

## What to do now
1. **Reach the safest available pause.** If you are inside your car, lock the doors, keep the windows up, and buckle your seat belt before moving. If you are outside your car, move into a nearby staffed building if you can reach it without approaching the other vehicle. Ask staff or security for help.
2. **Leave only if you can do so safely.** If you can drive away without approaching the other vehicle or creating a collision risk, use a busy, well-lit route. Head to a staffed public place or a police, sheriff, or fire station. Do not drive home if you think the vehicle is following you.
3. **Call 911 if you believe there is an emergency or a crime in progress.** This includes being approached, blocked in, threatened, or closely followed. Tell the call-taker your location, what the vehicle is doing, and any direction of travel. Follow the call-taker’s instructions.
4. **Use a local non-emergency number only when there is no immediate danger.** For suspicious activity that is not happening urgently, use the police or sheriff non-emergency number listed on the official local agency website.
5. **Ask for a safer handover.** If you are at a mall, campus, hospital, garage, or venue, ask staff or security to remain with you, watch from a safe place, or escort you inside.
6. **Note details only while it is safe.** Useful details include the license plate, vehicle color, make, body style, distinctive features, location, time, and direction of travel. Stop observing if it draws attention or increases risk.
7. **Contact someone you trust from a safe pause.** Tell them where you are and where you plan to go. Do not handle the phone while driving.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the driver’s motive.
- You do not need perfect details before asking for help.
- You do not need to record the vehicle, post online, or confront anyone.
- You do not need to decide about later reporting or property-management follow-up until you are safer.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to prove that the situation is dangerous before creating distance or asking for help. Locked doors, a staffed place, and a calm call for assistance are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. Later decisions, including non-urgent reporting or follow-up with security or property management, may need specialist help or support from the appropriate local service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice, and it cannot guarantee an outcome. If you believe there is an emergency or a crime may be in progress, prioritize reaching a safer pause and calling 911.
