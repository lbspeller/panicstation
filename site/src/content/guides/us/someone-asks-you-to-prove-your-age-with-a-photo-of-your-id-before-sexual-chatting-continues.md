# What to do if… someone asks you to prove your age with a photo of your ID before sexual chatting continues (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all communication immediately and **do not send any photo of your ID**. This request is a primary red flag for identity theft, sextortion, and the creation of non-consensual AI-generated imagery.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send any government-issued ID (license, passport, or birth certificate), as these contain sensitive data like your full name, address, and biometric markers.
- Don’t send a "holding ID" selfie; these are specifically used to bypass security on financial apps and social media accounts to steal your identity.
- Don’t believe that sending the ID will "build trust" or stop the pressure—once they have your ID, they have more leverage to blackmail you.
- Don’t interact with any "age verification" links sent by the individual, as these are often phishing sites designed to steal login credentials or install malware.
- Don’t pay any "verification fee" or "security deposit" requested by a stranger.

## What to do now
1. **Disengage from the conversation immediately.** You do not need to explain why you are leaving or give them a final warning.
   - Stop responding to all messages.
   - Do not click on any links they provide.
2. **Protect your biometric and personal data.** **Assume the individual is not who they claim to be and that any data shared will be used for harm.**
   - Change your privacy settings to "Private" or "Friends Only" on all social media platforms.
   - Check if your profile contains identifiable information like your workplace, school, or location.
3. **Use only official platform verification tools.** **Authentic platforms use secure, encrypted APIs (like Apple or Google verification) and will never require you to send a raw photo of your ID directly to another user.**
   - If the app has a "Verified" badge system, use the built-in settings menu to complete it.
   - If there is no native system, the platform is not safe for sharing intimate or identity-linked information.
4. **Block and report the user within the application.** **Reporting helps the platform identify patterns of predatory behavior or automated scam accounts.**
   - Select the option for "harassment," "coercion," or "scam" depending on the app's options.
   - Take 1 or 2 screenshots of the request for ID before you block the user.
5. **Report to the FBI if threats or extortion occur.** **Use the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) to report digital extortion.**
   - File a report at **ic3.gov**.
   - You can also call **1-800-CALL-FBI** or use **tips.fbi.gov**.
6. **Submit a report to NCMEC if you or the other person are under 18.** **Minors can "self-report" to the CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org.**
   - If you are worried about images being shared, use the **Take It Down** tool at **takeitdown.ncmec.org**.
7. **Flag identity theft concerns if you already shared documents.** **Go to IdentityTheft.gov to create a recovery plan if your government ID was compromised.**

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine if the person was "real" or a "bot" right now.
- You don’t need to tell your family or friends about the situation until you feel ready.
- You don’t need to delete your entire social media presence; blocking the specific individual and their associated accounts is often sufficient.
- You don’t need to decide on long-term legal action today.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of urgency or guilt when someone claims they "need" to see your ID for safety reasons. However, a request for a raw photo of an identity document is never a standard or safe part of online dating or sexual chatting. Protecting your identity is a necessary and responsible action.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. It focuses on immediate identity protection and the prevention of digital extortion. Later steps may involve credit monitoring or formal legal reporting if identity theft occurs.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and is not a substitute for legal or professional security advice. If you are in immediate physical danger or being actively stalked, call 911. Federal authorities prioritize reports of sextortion and child exploitation.
