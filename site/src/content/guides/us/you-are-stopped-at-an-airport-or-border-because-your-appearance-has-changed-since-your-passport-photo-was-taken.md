# What to do if… you are stopped at an airport or border because your appearance has changed since your passport photo was taken (USA)

## Short answer
Show your passport, say that your appearance has changed since the photo was taken, and ask which identity-verification process applies. Keep any other physical ID and your booking details ready, but show additional material only if asked.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue about whether the photo should be accepted.
- Do not joke about security or use threatening language.
- Do not give your passport to an unidentified person or leave it unattended.
- Do not alter your passport or show edited images as substitutes for the passport.
- Do not walk away from a check without telling the staff member or officer what you are doing.
- Do not guess which supporting material is needed. Ask what the staff member or officer wants to see.
- Do not assume that an airline check, a TSA checkpoint check, and a CBP inspection use the same process.

## What to do now
1. **State the issue plainly.** Say: “My appearance has changed since this passport photo was taken. What do you need from me to verify my identity?”
2. **Show the passport and keep supporting items ready.** If you have another physical photo ID or booking confirmation, keep it accessible. Show it only if the staff member or officer asks for it or asks what else you have.
3. **At an airline counter or gate, ask what the airline needs.** If boarding time is close, ask the airline staff member to note that an identity check is in progress and explain the available options if the check delays boarding.
4. **At a TSA checkpoint, follow the officer’s instructions.** TSA lists passports among acceptable checkpoint IDs. If facial comparison does not produce a match, TSA says the officer will use standard passenger identity-verification procedures. Ask what is needed. If TSA cannot verify your identity, TSA says you will not be allowed to enter the screening checkpoint.
5. **At a CBP port of entry, answer questions truthfully and directly.** People arriving at a U.S. port of entry are subject to CBP inspection. If you are referred for further inspection, follow the instructions and ask what documentation is needed.
6. **If you are abroad and need passport help, contact the appropriate issuing authority.** For help with a U.S. passport, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate. For a passport issued by another country, contact that country’s issuing authority or embassy. Contact the airline separately about any boarding decision.
7. **After the immediate check is over, review whether you need a new passport.** For a U.S. passport, the State Department says you only need to apply for a new passport if your appearance has significantly changed and you can no longer be identified from the current photo. Examples include significant facial surgery or trauma, adding or removing many large facial piercings or tattoos, or significant weight loss or gain. For a passport issued by another country, check with the issuing authority.

## What can wait
- You do not need to give a long personal explanation for an appearance change unless the staff member or officer asks for information needed for the check.
- You do not need to decide during the check whether to make a complaint.
- You do not need to start a passport application while you are standing at a checkpoint or counter.
- You do not need to solve future travel plans until the immediate identity check is complete.

## Important reassurance
An appearance question does not itself mean that you have done anything wrong. The immediate task is to follow the requested check, keep your documents together, and ask what happens next.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an immediate passport-likeness concern. Later decisions about passport replacement, travel documents, or a delayed journey may need help from the passport-issuing authority, an airline, or consular staff.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Procedures can vary by setting and by the facts of the situation. Follow the instructions of the staff member or officer handling the check and ask what is required.
