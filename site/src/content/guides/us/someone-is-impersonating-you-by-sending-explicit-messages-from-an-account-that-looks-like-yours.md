# What to do if… someone is impersonating you by sending explicit messages from an account that looks like yours (USA)

## Short answer
Secure your authentic accounts immediately, report the impersonating profile directly to the platform, and use hashing tools like StopNCII.org to prevent the spread of explicit images. Do not engage with the impersonator or send any money or content to meet their demands.

## Do not do these things
- Do not respond to the impersonator or attempt to negotiate with them.
- Do not send money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or additional images to the perpetrator.
- Do not post a public "call-out" or link to the fake account, as this can inadvertently increase the audience for the explicit content.
- Do not delete your own accounts or your evidence until you have made a minimal record.
- Do not click on any links sent by the impersonating account or those pretending to "help" you recover it.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize immediate physical safety.** If you believe the person impersonating you knows your physical location and you feel at risk of immediate harm, call **911**.
2. **Secure your authentic accounts.** 
   - Change the password for the email address associated with your social media accounts first.
   - Change your passwords on the platform where the impersonation is occurring.
   - Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** using an authenticator app rather than SMS if possible.
   - Use the "Log out of all devices" or "Security Checkup" feature on the platform to ensure no unauthorized sessions are active.
3. **Report the account to the platform host.** 
   - Use the specific "Impersonation" or "Pretending to be me" reporting tool within the app.
   - If the account is sending non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII), select that specific violation in the reporting flow.
4. **Prevent the spread of images.**
   - If explicit images of you are being shared or threatened, use **StopNCII.org**. This tool creates a digital "fingerprint" (hash) of the image on your device so participating platforms (Meta, TikTok, Reddit, etc.) can block it without seeing the content.
   - For individuals under 18, use the **Take It Down** tool provided by the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).
5. **Contain the situation with a standard message.** 
   - Send a brief, identical message to key contacts who may have been messaged by the fake account: "An account is currently impersonating me and sending unauthorized messages. Please do not reply, do not click links, and report the account for impersonation. I am working to have it removed."
6. **Create a minimal record of the impersonation.**
   - Take 1-2 screenshots of the impersonating profile showing the username (handle) and the URL.
   - Save a screenshot of at least one explicit message as evidence, ensuring the date and time are visible.
7. **Report cybercrime to federal authorities.**
   - File a report at **ic3.gov**. **Type the URL directly into your browser's address bar** to avoid "spoofed" phishing sites that mimic the reporting portal.
   - If the impersonator is demanding money or performing "sextortion," explicitly state this in the IC3 report.
8. **Check for broader identity theft.**
   - If you suspect the person has access to your Social Security number or financial information, visit **IdentityTheft.gov** to create a recovery plan.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person behind the account today.
- You do not need to explain the situation to every single follower or contact.
- You do not need to decide whether to pursue a civil lawsuit or hire a private investigator.
- You do not need to read every message the impersonator has sent to others.

## Important reassurance
Impersonation involving sexual content is a targeted attempt to cause shame and loss of control. The panic you may feel is a normal response to a violation of your digital and personal boundaries. Platforms have established protocols for this specific type of harm, and taking the steps to report and secure your identity is the most effective way to end the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital impersonation and the unauthorized spread of explicit content. It does not cover long-term reputation management, legal representation, or criminal investigation procedures.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Laws regarding digital impersonation and non-consensual intimate imagery vary by state. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline or seek emergency medical care.
