# What to do if… your passport includes a middle name but your booking does not and staff say it is an issue (USA)

## Short answer
Do not cancel and rebook in a panic. Ask the airline to check the passenger name and security passenger data on your reservation against the passport you will use, then correct it or reissue the boarding pass if the airline says that is needed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not cancel and rebook at a higher fare just because someone says it might be an issue.
- Do not type your middle name into the surname or last-name field unless the airline tells you to.
- Do not assume the name printed on a boarding pass is always the full name stored in the reservation.
- Do not leave it until the airport if online check-in is blocked or staff have already warned you.
- Do not request a change to a different traveller unless the airline specifically says that is what is needed.

## What to do now
1. Compare the name exactly as printed in your passport with the name in your airline booking or “manage trip” page.
2. Contact the airline through its app, chat, phone line, ticket counter, or airport desk and say: “My passport includes my middle name, but my booking does not show it. Can you check whether the passenger name and security passenger data match my passport, and correct it if needed?”
3. Ask whether the airline can add the middle name or middle initial without creating a new booking. If they make a correction, ask them to reissue or refresh the boarding pass.
4. If the airline says no correction is needed for this reservation, ask them to confirm that the booking is acceptable for this itinerary and keep a screenshot or chat transcript.
5. If you booked through a travel agent or third-party site, ask the airline whether it can edit the passenger data directly. If not, contact the booking agent and use the airline’s wording when you ask for the correction.
6. Keep a simple proof bundle ready: your passport, the booking confirmation, the current boarding pass if you have one, and any airline message confirming the update or saying no update is needed.
7. If your flight is soon, use a staffed check-in counter within the airline’s recommended airport arrival and check-in window, especially if online check-in is blocked or staff have already flagged the mismatch.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now how you will enter middle names on every future booking.
- You do not need to argue general policy at the airport; focus on getting this reservation checked or corrected.
- You do not need to clean up trusted-traveller or PreCheck profile details right now unless this trip’s missing PreCheck indicator is the specific problem.
- You do not need to apply for a redress number for a one-off middle-name issue. That can wait unless you repeatedly have screening or misidentification problems.

## Important reassurance
This situation is common and is often fixable without buying a new ticket. The main thing to check now is whether the airline’s reservation and security passenger data are acceptable for the passport you will present.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce the chance of check-in, boarding, or security-screening problems for this trip. Airline systems, third-party bookings, international itineraries, and airport processes can vary, so later decisions may need help from the airline, booking agent, or another travel specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, security, travel-agent, financial, or professional advice. Airline and TSA processes can change, and the airline handling your reservation is the best source for what can be corrected before travel.
