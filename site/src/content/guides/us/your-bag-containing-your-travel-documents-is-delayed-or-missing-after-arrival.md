# What to do if… your bag containing your travel documents is delayed or missing after arrival (USA)

## Short answer
Report the missing bag to the airline’s baggage service office before leaving the airport to obtain a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and reference number. Immediately report any lost U.S. passports via Form DS-64 and initiate the specific replacement process for your immigration status.

## Do not do these things
- Do not exit the arrivals terminal without a printed copy of your baggage report and reference number.
- Do not delay the reporting of missing identity documents to the relevant authorities, as this increases the risk of identity misuse.
- Do not publish photos of your baggage tags, boarding passes, or any partial document scans on social media.
- Do not make expensive emergency purchases without checking the airline's "reasonable expense" guidelines and saving every physical receipt.

## What to do now
1. **Report the missing bag at the airline’s baggage service office in the arrivals hall before passing through the final exit.**  
   Request a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and ensure you receive a 10-character file reference number (e.g., ABCCY12345). Confirm that the agent notes "Travel Documents Inside" in the bag description and provide a precise delivery address for your stay in the U.S.
2. **Document all tracking and claim information in a digital format immediately.**  
   Take clear photos of your PIR paperwork, the original baggage claim stickers, and your flight boarding pass. Email these photos to yourself or save them to a secure cloud folder to ensure you have access if physical copies are lost.
3. **Report a missing U.S. passport as lost or stolen using the State Department’s DS-64 online tool or by calling 1-877-487-2778.**  
   Once this report is submitted, the passport is permanently invalidated for travel; even if the bag is found later, the passport cannot be used and must be replaced using Form DS-11 in person.
4. **Initiate the replacement process specific to your immigration or citizenship status.**  
   - **Lawful Permanent Residents (Green Card):** File Form I-90 online via myUSCIS to replace a lost I-551 card ($465-$540 fee applies in 2026); your receipt notice (I-797C) serves as temporary proof of status.
   - **Non-U.S. Citizens:** Contact your home country’s nearest consulate or embassy within the U.S. for emergency travel document (ETD) or passport replacement instructions.
5. **Use the TSA ConfirmID process if you have onward domestic flights but no physical identification.**  
   If you are missing your REAL ID or passport, you must visit TSA.gov/ConfirmID to pay a $45 identity verification fee before arriving at the airport for your next flight. Present the pay.gov receipt at the security checkpoint and allow at least 30-60 minutes for manual identity verification and enhanced screening.
6. **Request a baggage fee refund if your luggage is significantly delayed.**  
   Under 2026 Department of Transportation (DOT) rules, airlines must automatically refund your checked baggage fee if the bag is not delivered within 12 hours for domestic flights or 15 hours for international flights. Verify with the baggage agent that this refund has been triggered in their system.
7. **Keep all receipts for essential items purchased while your bag is missing.**  
   Airlines are liable for up to $4,700 for domestic U.S. flights and approximately $1,700 for international flights (Montreal Convention limits) for "reasonable and necessary" incidental expenses. Submit these receipts through the airline’s online portal using your PIR reference number once you have settled at your destination.

## What can wait
- You do not need to assume the bag is permanently lost; approximately 95 percent of delayed bags are returned within 48 to 72 hours.
- You do not need to file a formal "lost" claim for the value of the contents until 21 days have passed, at which point the bag is legally considered lost.
- You do not need to finalize your insurance claim immediately; most travel insurance policies allow 21 to 30 days for initial notification.

## Important reassurance
The loss of travel documents is a high-stress event, but it is a procedural issue that can be resolved. Obtaining the baggage reference number and reporting the documents to the issuing authorities are the two most critical steps to protect your identity and begin the recovery process.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate hours after discovering a bag is missing. It does not cover long-term insurance litigation or complex naturalization issues resulting from document loss.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or immigration advice. Replacement fees, processing times, and TSA identity verification requirements are subject to change; always verify current procedures on official government websites such as State.gov, USCIS.gov, and TSA.gov.
