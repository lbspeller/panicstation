# What to do if… your bag containing your travel documents is delayed or missing after arrival (USA)

## Short answer
Before you leave the airport, report the missing or delayed bag to the airline’s baggage service office and get a written reference number. Then use the correct official route for the document that is missing, especially if it is a valid U.S. passport, U.S. visa, Green Card, or foreign passport.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the airport without filing a baggage report and getting the reference number, unless you need to move for immediate safety or medical reasons.
- Do not report an expired U.S. passport as lost or stolen; use the State Department instructions for the document you actually lost.
- Do not assume a found U.S. passport will still work after you report it lost or stolen.
- Do not post images of your passport, boarding pass, bag tags, visa, Green Card, or immigration documents online.
- Do not spend heavily in panic; buy only short-term essentials and keep receipts.

## What to do now
1. **Report the bag at the airline’s baggage service office in arrivals before you leave.**  
   File a delayed or missing bag report and get the airline reference number. Ask the airline to record:
   - your delivery address in the U.S., such as a hotel, home, or trusted person’s address
   - your best phone number and email
   - the bag description, baggage tag number, and that travel documents are inside
   - any urgent onward travel affected by the missing documents

2. **Save the tracing details in two places.**  
   Photograph or type out the airline report reference, baggage tag number, flight number, arrival airport, date, and baggage-office contact. Email it to yourself or save it somewhere you can access if your phone battery dies.

3. **If a valid U.S. passport is missing, report it lost or stolen through the State Department.**  
   Once a valid U.S. passport is reported lost or stolen, it is cancelled and cannot be used for travel even if the bag later turns up. Reporting does not replace the passport; follow the State Department replacement process if you need a new one.

4. **Use the replacement route for the specific document.**
   - **U.S. passport missing:** use the State Department lost or stolen passport process, then apply for a replacement if needed.
   - **U.S. visa missing:** check the State Department lost or stolen visa instructions. A lost or stolen U.S. visa usually cannot be replaced inside the United States, so do not make onward international travel plans until you have checked the official route for your status.
   - **Green Card or Form I-551 missing:** use the USCIS replacement process, usually Form I-90.
   - **Other U.S. immigration document missing:** check the USCIS guidance for how to correct, update, or replace that document.
   - **Foreign passport missing in the U.S.:** contact your country’s embassy or consulate in the U.S. for replacement instructions.

5. **If you have onward U.S. air travel and no acceptable ID, go to the airport early and speak to TSA at the checkpoint.**  
   TSA may use identity verification and additional screening. TSA also describes a fee-based TSA ConfirmID option for some travellers without acceptable ID, so check the TSA ConfirmID page before you travel.

6. **Cover essentials conservatively and keep receipts.**  
   For delayed baggage, airlines may have to reimburse reasonable, verifiable, actual incidental expenses, subject to limits and claim rules. Use your baggage reference number when you submit receipts.

7. **If the bag appears stolen, make a police report after the airline report is filed and you are somewhere settled.**  
   This may help with document replacement or insurance. If it may simply be delayed, start with the airline report and official document routes first.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now that the bag is permanently lost; get the report number and let the airline tracing process start.
- You do not need a full inventory immediately; make one later when you can check photos, packing notes, and receipts.
- You do not need to negotiate compensation at the airport; secure the reference number first, then submit claims through the airline’s process.
- You do not need to solve every onward travel problem in one conversation; first protect the missing documents and confirm what ID you can still use.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but there are established routes for delayed baggage and missing travel documents. The safest first move is to create a traceable airline report, protect any missing identity document, and avoid rushed decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Replacement documents, airline claims, insurance claims, and immigration-status questions may need official guidance or specialist help after you have a report number, a safe place to stay, and the document route started.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, travel, insurance, or professional advice. Requirements can vary by airline, itinerary, citizenship, immigration status, and document type, so use official government pages, your airline’s claim process, or your embassy or consulate for your specific situation.
