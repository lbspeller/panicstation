# What to do if… you receive a notice requiring you to surrender to begin a sentence on a specific date (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the exact time and location with your attorney or the sentencing court, then prepare to arrive early with only a government ID and essential medical/legal documents. Failure to report as ordered is a federal offense that can result in an additional, consecutive prison sentence.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself to the facility and expect to leave your car in the parking lot; it will likely be towed at your expense.
- Do not bring any electronics, jewelry (except a plain wedding band under $100), or excess cash.
- Do not bring medications in anything other than their original pharmacy-labeled bottles.
- Do not ignore the notice if you have a medical emergency; you must still obtain a court order to delay the date.
- Do not arrive under the influence of any substances, as this may result in immediate disciplinary action or specialized high-security intake.
- Do not assume a "satellite camp" designation means you report directly to the camp; many require reporting to the main higher-security facility first.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the specific system and agency designated for your intake.**
   - Contact your attorney immediately to verify if you are reporting to a U.S. Marshals Service (USMS) office or directly to a Bureau of Prisons (BOP) facility.
   - If you do not have an attorney, call the Clerk of the Court that sentenced you to confirm the surrender location and time listed in your Judgment and Commitment (J&C) order.
2. **Obtain the precise reporting deadline and address for the facility.**
   - Verify the "report by" time; if no time is specified for a federal surrender, the standard deadline is usually 2:00 PM.
   - Use the official BOP "Find a Facility" tool or the USMS website to confirm the physical address and the specific entrance for self-surrenders.
3. **Organize mandatory identification and legal paperwork.**
   - **Carry a valid government-issued photo ID (driver’s license, state ID, or passport) and your Social Security card.**
   - **Bring a copy of your Judgment and Commitment order and the surrender notice itself.**
   - **Prepare a single piece of paper with a list of essential contact names, phone numbers, and addresses written in ink.**
4. **Follow strict medication and medical device protocols.**
   - **Ensure all essential medications are in their original pharmacy bottles with your name clearly printed on the label.**
   - **If you require a CPAP machine, walker, or other medical device, contact the facility’s Health Services department at least 24 hours before arrival to ensure the item is pre-approved.**
   - **Bring a printed summary of your medical history and a list of current prescriptions from your doctor.**
5. **Secure immediate dependents and financial obligations.**
   - **Confirm childcare, eldercare, or pet care for at least the next 72 hours, as you will not have immediate phone access.**
   - **Set essential bills to autopay or provide account access to a single trusted person today.**
6. **Execute final travel logistics for early arrival.**
   - **Arrange for a third party to drop you off at the facility entrance at least one hour before the deadline.**
   - **Wear plain, inexpensive clothing (shirt, pants, underwear, and shoes) that you are comfortable having mailed back to your home or discarded.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out your commissary account or "money on the books" in the first hour; funds can be sent via Western Union or MoneyGram once you are processed.
- You do not need to resolve long-term housing or vehicle storage issues if you have at least 48 hours of coverage.
- You do not need to notify your entire social circle; one support person can hold your contact list and handle notifications.
- You do not need to understand prison programming or job assignments before you arrive.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a surrender date is a high-stress event, and it is normal to feel overwhelmed by the logistics of leaving. The most important goal is to avoid an "escape" or "failure to appear" charge, which is preventable by reporting to the correct location on time. Focus only on the identification and medical items required for the next few hours.

## Scope note
This guide covers initial stabilization and reporting procedures for federal and state self-surrenders in the USA. Specific intake rules regarding property and medications vary by individual facility and security level.

## Important note
This information is not legal advice. Failure to surrender for a sentence is a criminal offense under 18 U.S.C. § 3146 and may result in additional prison time and the loss of self-surrender privileges in the future. Always verify instructions with the issuing court or your legal counsel.
