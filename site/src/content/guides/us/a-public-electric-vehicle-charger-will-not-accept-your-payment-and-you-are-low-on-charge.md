# What to do if… a public electric vehicle charger will not accept your payment and you are low on charge (USA)

## Short answer
Preserve your remaining range. Record the charger details, try one supported alternative way to start the session, and move to a reachable backup charger before further troubleshooting risks leaving you stranded.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the same payment method repeatedly. This can waste time and may create additional pending authorizations.
- Do not reduce heating, cooling, or defrosting below what you need for safe visibility and a safe cabin temperature.
- Do not assume that an active screen means the charger can complete payment authorization and start a session.
- Do not give full card details, login codes, or one-time passcodes to anyone who approaches you at the charger.
- Do not wait until your remaining range is too low to reach a backup charger safely.

## What to do now
1. **Park safely and preserve usable range.**  
   Keep the vehicle parked while you troubleshoot. Reduce non-essential power use where safe. Use an Eco or Range mode if your vehicle has one. Keep enough heating, cooling, and defrosting for safe visibility and a safe cabin temperature.

2. **Record the charger details.**  
   Take a photo of any displayed charging-network name, charger ID, stall number, location details, and error message. Note the time.

3. **Try one supported alternative start method at the same site.**  
   Check the charger screen for available options. Depending on the charger, this may include another debit or credit card, tap-to-pay, Apple Pay or Google Wallet, a guest-payment option, the network app, or a vehicle-based charging option if your vehicle and the charger support one. If another compatible stall is available at the same site, try that stall.

4. **Contact the charging network if support details are available.**  
   Look for a support number on the charger, in the app, or on the charging network website. Give support any displayed charger ID and stall number. Ask whether they can help start the session, reset the charger, or identify a working compatible connector at that site.

5. **Protect your phone battery and avoid unnecessary app changes.**  
   Charge your phone from the vehicle if you can do so without risking your ability to reach a backup charger. Prefer calling support over reinstalling apps or repeatedly changing payment details.

6. **Switch to a reachable backup before your range becomes too low.**  
   Use the vehicle navigation, your usual charging app, or the U.S. Department of Energy station locator to identify a compatible backup charger. A compatible slower charger may still help you add enough charge to continue safely.

7. **Arrange roadside help early if a safe backup is not reachable.**  
   Call your roadside provider, insurer, or automaker roadside service. Say that your EV is low on charge and you cannot start a charging session. Ask what EV assistance is available in your area, which may include towing or mobile charging in some locations.

8. **Treat an unsafe roadside situation as an emergency.**  
   Move to a safer place if you can do so safely. Call 911 immediately if you are in immediate danger, facing a medical emergency, or unable to move away from a traffic hazard.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the problem is caused by your card issuer, the app, the charger, or the network system right now.
- You do not need to resolve pending authorizations while you are low on charge.
- You do not need to file a complaint or write a review until you are safely charging or recovered.

## Important reassurance
A charger can appear active while a payment or session-start process is not working. You can stop troubleshooting and move to a reachable backup while you still have enough range.

## Scope note
These are first steps to preserve range, reach a safer pause, and start charging or recovery. Later questions about payment declines, pending authorizations, or repeated charger faults may need help from the charging network, your card issuer, or your roadside provider.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 immediately if you are in immediate danger.
