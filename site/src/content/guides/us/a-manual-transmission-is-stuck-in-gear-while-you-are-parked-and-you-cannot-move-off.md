# What to do if… a manual transmission is stuck in gear while you are parked and you cannot move off (USA)

## Short answer
Secure the vehicle with the parking brake and hazards, depress the clutch fully while the engine is off, and attempt to move the shifter to neutral with steady, moderate pressure. If it remains stuck, call for roadside assistance or emergency services if you are in a travel lane.

## Do not do these things
- Don't use excessive force or "slam" the gear lever, as this can break the linkage or internal shift forks.
- Don't attempt to start the engine without the clutch fully depressed if the vehicle is in gear.
- Don't exit the vehicle into a live lane of traffic to inspect the car.
- Don't keep the engine running if you are unable to disengage the gear, as the car may creep or lurch.
- Don't stand between your vehicle and other traffic while waiting for help.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle immediately to prevent rolling.**
   - Firmly depress the foot brake and **engage the parking brake fully**.
   - Turn on your hazard lights.

2. **Verify your environment for immediate safety risks.**
   - If you are stopped in a travel lane or a high-speed shoulder, stay in the vehicle with your seatbelt on.
   - Call 911 immediately if your position creates a collision hazard for other drivers.

3. **Attempt a manual reset with the engine off.**
   - Ensure the engine is turned completely off.
   - Press the clutch pedal all the way to the floor and hold it there.
   - Apply firm, steady pressure to the shifter toward the **neutral** position. 
   - If you are on an incline, the weight of the car may be "binding" the gears; keep the brakes locked tight.

4. **Try a slight weight-redistribution move if safe.**
   - With the engine off and parking brake engaged, very slightly release the foot brake to see if the car moves a fraction of an inch (if on a slight grade), then re-apply immediately.
   - Sometimes "unloading" the transmission by shifting the car’s weight slightly can free a stuck gear.

5. **Attempt a clutch-depressed start only if you have space.**
   - Ensure there is at least ten feet of clear space in front of and behind the vehicle.
   - Keep the foot brake and parking brake firmly applied.
   - Press the clutch to the floor and start the engine.
   - Try once more to move the shifter to neutral while the engine is running and the clutch is down.

6. **Request professional recovery if the shifter remains jammed.**
   - Contact your roadside assistance provider (e.g., AAA at 800-222-4357) or a local towing service.
   - **Specify that the vehicle is a manual transmission stuck in gear** and cannot be moved into neutral, as this may require a flatbed tow truck or dollies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the failure (such as a broken cable or master cylinder) right now.
- You do not need to check the transmission fluid or inspect the underside of the car.
- You do not need to decide on a repair shop until the vehicle is safely removed from the roadway.

## Important reassurance
It is common for manual transmissions to bind when parked on inclines or if a component has reached its wear limit. This is a mechanical immobilization, and choosing to stop and call for a tow is the safest way to prevent expensive gearbox damage or a roadside collision.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a vehicle immobilized by a stuck gear. It does not cover mechanical repairs, transmission disassembly, or long-term vehicle maintenance.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not a substitute for professional mechanical advice. If you are in a dangerous location on a public highway, prioritize your physical safety and follow all instructions from emergency dispatchers or law enforcement. Many states have "Move Over" laws that require other drivers to change lanes for you, but you must remain vigilant and stay inside the vehicle if traffic is high-speed.
