# What to do if… your vehicle warns the electronic parking brake has a fault when you need to leave it parked (USA)

## Short answer
Do not rely on the electronic parking brake (EPB) to hold the vehicle; secure it using the transmission (Park or gear), turn the wheels toward the curb, and use professional-grade wheel chocks if available. If the vehicle cannot be safely secured against rolling, stay with the vehicle and call for professional roadside assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume the EPB is holding because you hear the motor or see a dashboard light.
- Do not leave the vehicle on a significant incline if there is any doubt about it staying stationary.
- Do not repeatedly toggle the EPB switch to "clear" the error while the vehicle is unsecured.
- Do not attempt to crawl under the vehicle to inspect or manually release the brake mechanism.
- Do not use improvised wheel chocks such as rocks, wood scraps, or bricks, which can slip or shatter.
- Do not perform "test" rolls toward traffic, pedestrians, or structures.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to level ground if the vehicle is still driveable.**
   **Prioritize finding a flat, paved surface away from traffic before turning off the engine.**
   - Avoid parking on slopes, driveways with inclines, or soft shoulders where chocks may sink.

2. **Secure the vehicle using the transmission while your foot is firmly on the service brake.**
   - **Automatic transmission:** Shift the vehicle into **P (Park)**.
   - **Manual transmission:** Shift into **1st gear** if facing uphill or **Reverse** if facing downhill.
   - Keep the brake pedal depressed until the transmission is fully engaged and the engine is off.

3. **Position the steering wheels to use the curb as a physical block.**
   - **Headed downhill:** Turn the front wheels **toward the curb** (right).
   - **Headed uphill with a curb:** Turn the front wheels **away from the curb** (left), then let the vehicle roll back slightly until the tire touches the curb.
   - **No curb (uphill or downhill):** Turn the wheels **toward the side of the road** (right) so the vehicle rolls away from traffic if it moves.

4. **Apply professional wheel chocks to the appropriate tires.**
   - **On a downhill grade:** Place chocks in front of both front tires.
   - **On an uphill grade:** Place chocks behind both rear tires.
   - **On level ground:** Place chocks on both the front and back of a single tire.

5. **Perform a controlled "stillness check" before exiting.**
   **Only if there is clear space and no one is in the vehicle's path, slowly ease off the foot brake.**
   - Confirm the transmission and curb are holding the weight.
   - If the vehicle moves even slightly, immediately re-apply the foot brake and do not leave it unattended.

6. **Shut off the engine and remove the key.**
   **Ensure the ignition is completely off and the steering lock (if equipped) is engaged.**
   - This prevents accidental shifting or movement by passengers or external interference.

7. **Contact roadside assistance if the vehicle is not secure.**
   **Call for a tow or professional assessment if the incline is steep or the transmission does not feel stable.**
   - State clearly: "I have an electronic parking brake fault and the vehicle is at risk of rolling away."

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific electronic failure or sensor error right now.
- You do not need to attempt a system reset or battery disconnection while the vehicle is in an unsecure position.
- You can wait to choose a repair facility or negotiate insurance coverage until the vehicle is physically stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a "parking brake fault" warning is startling, but vehicles are designed with redundant systems. By using the transmission's "Park" pawl or low gears and utilizing curb geometry, you can safely stabilize the vehicle until professional help arrives. Panic reactions are common, but following these mechanical steps prevents the primary risk of a roll-away.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to prevent an unattended vehicle roll-away. It is not a repair manual. Procedures vary significantly by manufacturer; always consult your owner's manual for specific emergency protocols for your vehicle's make and model.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional mechanical advice or manufacturer-specific instructions. Electronic parking brake systems (EPB) are complex and regulated under Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS). If you cannot confidently secure the vehicle, do not leave it unattended, as you may be liable for damages resulting from a roll-away.
