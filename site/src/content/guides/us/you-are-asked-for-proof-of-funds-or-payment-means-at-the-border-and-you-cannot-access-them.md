# What to do if… you are asked for proof of funds or payment means at the border and you cannot access them (USA)

## Short answer
Request a brief pause to retrieve verifiable evidence, such as a downloaded PDF statement, and explain any technical or banking blocks clearly to the officer. U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) evaluates financial self-sufficiency to ensure you will not become a "public charge" during your stay.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue with the officer or provide defensive, long-winded justifications.
- Do not show edited screenshots, "estimated" balances, or non-verifiable digital notes.
- Do not invent a fake job, sponsor, or travel plan to compensate for missing funds.
- Do not offer your device passwords proactively; wait for a specific request and follow lawful instructions calmly.
- Do not accept "loans" or transfers from strangers or unofficial "fixers" at the airport.
- Do not worry if you are moved to secondary inspection; this is a standard procedural step for verification.

## What to do now
1. **Request clarity on what specific evidence is required to satisfy the inquiry.**  
   **Identify if the officer will accept digital PDF statements, physical cards, or a verbal confirmation from a domestic sponsor.**  
   Say: "I am having a temporary technical issue with my account access. What specific forms of proof are acceptable so I can focus on retrieving them for you?"

2. **Attempt to resolve technical barriers using terminal infrastructure if permitted.**  
   - Connect to official airport Wi-Fi if cellular roaming is failed.  
   - Restart the device to clear app cache or authentication errors.  
   - Use a web browser to log in if the banking app is geo-blocked or crashing.  
   - Ask for permission to use a charging station if your device battery is too low to display documents.

3. **Present existing verifiable documents that do not require an active login.**  
   **Show any offline records that demonstrate your ability to support your stay.**  
   - Previous month's bank statement PDF saved in your downloads or email.  
   - Confirmation of prepaid hotels, rental cars, or onward flight tickets.  
   - Physical credit or debit cards in your name (even if currently locked by your bank).  
   - A signed invitation letter from a U.S. host or sponsor that includes their contact details.

4. **Request permission to contact your financial institution to lift a travel block.**  
   **Explain that you need to authorize a "travel notice" or resolve a fraud alert to demonstrate access to funds.**  
   - Use the secure chat function in your banking app if calling is not possible.  
   - Ask: "Can you provide a temporary balance verification or a secure message confirming my account status?"

5. **Provide simple, factual answers regarding your daily budget and spending plan.**  
   **Maintain consistency with your visa or ESTA purpose (e.g., tourism, business meetings, or visiting family).**  
   - State clearly where you are staying and who is covering specific costs (e.g., "My employer has paid for the hotel, and I have $1,000 for personal expenses").

6. **Wait for the officer to complete the verification process in secondary inspection if required.**  
   **Acknowledge that officers have the authority to manually inspect apps or devices to verify your claims under CBP Directive 3340-049B.**  
   - Remain seated and answer only the questions asked during this phase.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix your long-term banking settings or roaming plan while at the inspection booth.
- You do not need to decide whether to cancel your trip or change your itinerary immediately.
- You do not need to provide a multi-year financial history unless specifically requested.

## Important reassurance
Financial verification checks are a routine part of border security, especially under current 2026 "public charge" enforcement guidelines. Technical failures, such as blocked cards or dead batteries, are common and recognized by officers; providing a verifiable alternative usually resolves the concern.

## Scope note
This guide covers the first-step stabilization of a financial inquiry at a U.S. port of entry. It does not cover legal representation for expedited removal proceedings or long-term immigration litigation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Admissibility is determined solely by the CBP officer at the time of entry. Laws and directives, including INA 212(a)(4), are subject to change and vary by individual travel status.
