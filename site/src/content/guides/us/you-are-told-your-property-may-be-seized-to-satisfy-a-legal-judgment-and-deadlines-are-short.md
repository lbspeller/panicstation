# What to do if… you are told your property may be seized to satisfy a legal judgment and deadlines are short (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the case directly with the court clerk or via PACER for federal cases, then file a "Claim of Exemption" or "Motion to Stay" immediately to protect your assets from the levying officer.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay via gift card, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency to stop a "seizure today" — these are indicators of a scam.
- Don't hide, sell, or transfer your assets to friends or family — this can be classified as a "fraudulent transfer" and may lead to additional legal penalties.
- Don't ignore the paperwork even if you believe it is a mistake — legal deadlines for exemptions are strictly enforced and can expire in as few as 10 days.
- Don't provide your Social Security number or bank passwords to anyone claiming to be a "court officer" over the phone or text.
- Don't assume you will be arrested for the debt itself — debtor's prisons do not exist in the USA, though you can be held in contempt for violating specific court orders to appear.

## What to do now
1. **Identify if the notice came via mail, personal service, or digital message.**
   - Physical mail or personal service from a sheriff, U.S. Marshal, or process server is the standard for legitimate enforcement.
   - Text messages or emails demanding immediate payment to a "link" or via "QR code" are almost always scams.

2. **Verify the case number independently with the court named on the document.**
   - **Locate the official phone number for the Clerk of Court** by searching the official ".gov" website for your county or district.
   - **Search PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records)** if the document claims to be from a "U.S. District Court."
   - **Ask the clerk if a "Writ of Execution" or "Notice of Levy" has actually been issued** against your name and case number.

3. **Check your bank accounts for a "Legal Hold" or "Freeze" notice.**
   - If funds are already frozen, ask your bank for the "Garnishee's Notice" or "Notice of Levy" which contains the creditor's information and the court case.
   - **Inform the bank immediately if the account contains federal benefits** (Social Security, VA, SSI), as banks are required by federal law (31 CFR Part 212) to automatically protect up to two months of these deposits.

4. **File a "Claim of Exemption" or "Notice of Rights" form within the deadline.**
   - Most states provide a specific form to protect "exempt" property such as a primary vehicle, tools of the trade, or basic household goods.
   - **Obtain this form from the court's self-help center or clerk's office** and file it with both the court and the levying officer (Sheriff or Marshal).
   -   - Common protected items include:
       - A portion of your primary home’s equity (Homestead).
       - One vehicle up to a state-specific value.
       - Household furniture and clothing.
       - Retirement accounts (401k/IRA) and certain disability benefits.

5. **Request an emergency hearing if you were never served with the original lawsuit.**
   - **Ask the clerk for the procedure to "Stay Execution" or "Vacate Judgment"** based on lack of service or mistaken identity.
   - This typically requires a written motion and a brief explanation of why you did not respond to the original case.

6. **Contact local legal aid or a consumer protection attorney immediately.**
   - Search for your local "Legal Services Corporation" (LSC) grantee or visit LawHelp.org to find free or low-cost assistance.
   - Tell them you are facing "post-judgment enforcement" with an active "Writ of Execution."

7. **Verify the identity of anyone appearing at your property.**
   - **Ask for a badge, a business card, and the physical court order (Writ).**
   - Legitimate officers (Sheriffs or Marshals) will allow you to read the document and will not demand "cash on the spot" to leave.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a long-term debt settlement or bankruptcy filing in the next hour.
- You do not need to provide a full accounting of all your global assets to the creditor’s attorney today unless specifically ordered by a judge in an "Information Subpoena" or "Debtor's Exam."
- You do not need to prove the debt is invalid to stop an immediate seizure; you only need to prove the property is "exempt" or the process was procedurally flawed.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a notice of property seizure is a high-stress event designed to compel payment, but it is a legal process with built-in safeguards. In almost all jurisdictions, you have a window of time to claim exemptions that prevent you from losing your home, your car, or the money needed for basic survival (food and utilities). Feeling a sense of panic or "freezing" is a common physiological response to a threat to your security.

## Scope note
This guide covers the first-step stabilization of a post-judgment property seizure threat. It does not provide strategies for defending the underlying debt or filing for bankruptcy, which require specialized legal counsel.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. Debt collection and property seizure laws vary significantly by state and are subject to strict procedural deadlines. Always verify court documents through official channels and consult with a licensed attorney or legal aid organization in your jurisdiction.
