# What to do if… your mobile ticket will not load at a barrier or checkpoint (USA)

## Short answer
Step aside without bypassing the barrier. Ask a staff member what fallback is available and offer any booking details or proof of purchase you already have.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force the gate, jump the barrier, or follow another person through.
- Do not block the lane while trying to fix the problem.
- Do not delete or reinstall apps, clear app data, or remove saved passes while you are at the checkpoint. A saved pass may become harder to recover quickly.
- Do not buy another ticket before asking staff whether there is a simpler fallback, unless the journey cannot wait.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the side and ask for help.**  
   Stand near the barrier or checkpoint where staff can see you. Say: “My ticket is not loading because the network is down. What fallback can I use?”

2. **Try only the quickest saved options.**
   - Open Apple Wallet or Google Wallet if you added the pass earlier.
   - Check your downloaded files, recent files, and saved screenshots for a barcode, QR code, or PDF ticket.
   - Search your email for the operator name and words such as `ticket`, `barcode`, `PDF`, `boarding pass`, or `reservation`.
   - Open the ticket app and look for a previously loaded ticket or booking.
   - Turn up your screen brightness if a barcode or QR code appears.

3. **Gather the details staff may ask for.**
   - Your name as booked
   - Your confirmation or reservation number
   - The route, date, and approximate travel time
   - Any receipt, booking email, screenshot, or payment record you already have
   - Photo ID if you have it and staff ask for it

4. **Use the closest matching staff route.**
   - **Transit fare barrier:** Ask the station agent or gate attendant what fallback is available for a ticket that will not display. Follow their instructions rather than trying to enter through another lane.
   - **Amtrak or intercity rail:** Check for an emailed PDF ticket first. For Amtrak, an eTicket may also be available in the app, and staffed stations may have ticket-retrieval options. Ask station staff what is available at your location.
   - **Airport checkpoint:** Tell checkpoint staff that your boarding pass will not display. Ask where to go for airline help. An airline desk or kiosk may be available.

5. **Consider a replacement purchase only as a last resort.**  
   If staff say there is no usable fallback and the journey cannot wait, consider buying the minimum correct replacement ticket. Keep the receipt and note the time, location, and reason for the extra purchase.

6. **Keep a record if the issue is not resolved.**  
   Save any receipt, notice, citation, or written instruction. Ask staff how to follow up later about a duplicate charge, denied entry, or replacement ticket.

## What can wait
- You do not need to repair the network outage now.
- You do not need to decide whether to request a refund or make a complaint while you are at the barrier.
- You do not need to try every phone setting. Focus on saved ticket options and staff help.

## Important reassurance
A ticket that will not load is frustrating, but you do not need to force a decision at the barrier. A staff member may be able to identify the available fallback.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate barrier or checkpoint problem. Later questions about refunds, duplicate charges, citations, or reissued tickets may need help from the ticket issuer or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Procedures vary by operator, station, airport, and checkpoint.
