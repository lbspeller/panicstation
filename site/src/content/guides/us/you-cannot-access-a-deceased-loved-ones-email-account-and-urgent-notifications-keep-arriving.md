# What to do if… you cannot access a deceased loved one’s email account and urgent notifications keep arriving (USA)

## Short answer
Do not try to force access to the inbox. Contact any urgent senders through independently verified channels, and use the email provider’s official deceased-user process.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep guessing passwords or repeatedly trying password resets.
- Do not try to bypass two-factor authentication, security controls, or account-recovery checks.
- Do not use links, phone numbers, or contact details from unexpected notification previews.
- Do not assume that being a spouse, child, executor, or other relative automatically gives you access to email contents.
- Do not delete devices, wipe data, close the email account, or discard papers while you are still identifying urgent notices.

## What to do now
1. **Make a short list from the notification previews.**
   - Note the sender name, subject line, date, and time for each urgent-looking alert.
   - Take a screenshot or photo of the previews if that is safe and easy.
   - Do not open links or attachments from the previews.

2. **Contact urgent senders through verified channels.**
   - For a bank, card provider, insurer, utility, employer, subscription service, or benefits office, use a phone number on a statement, card, or official website you find independently.
   - Ask whether the organization has a bereavement, deceased-account, estate-services, or fraud team.
   - Ask what needs attention now, whether automated action can be paused, and how to move future notices to your own contact details.

3. **Gather the documents you already have.**
   - Put together a certified death certificate if available, your ID, and any court appointment documents already issued for the estate.
   - Ask each provider or organization what documents it requires before sending copies.

4. **Use the email provider’s official deceased-user page.**
   - For Google, use the official deceased-user request page. Google may review requests to close an account or obtain data, but it does not provide passwords or login details.
   - For Apple, check whether there is a Legacy Contact access key. Apple also describes deletion requests and U.S. court-order access requests.
   - For Microsoft, use its official deceased-user support page. If account contents are needed, its page says to seek legal guidance about the required formal process.
   - For Yahoo, use its official deceased-account page for closure, billing-service suspension, or a possible content request.

5. **Create a replacement contact route.**
   - Ask urgent senders to use your email address, phone number, or mailing address for follow-up where they are able to do so.
   - Ask for deadlines, required forms, and case numbers in writing to your own contact details.
   - Keep a simple log of the date, organization, person contacted, and next step.

6. **Act on signs of possible identity theft.**
   - If notifications suggest that someone may be misusing personal information, tell the relevant organization that you are concerned about unauthorized activity.
   - Use IdentityTheft.gov for a recovery plan and reporting steps.

## What can wait
- You do not need to read the whole inbox today.
- You do not need to decide today whether to close the email account.
- You do not need to settle every probate question before contacting urgent senders.
- You do not need to respond to every low-priority subscription or routine notice now.

## Important reassurance
Not having access to the inbox does not stop you from taking useful first steps. Contacting urgent senders directly and using the provider’s official process can reduce immediate pressure while the required documents are gathered.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Provider requirements vary, and later decisions about legal authority may need state-specific guidance.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If money, benefits, insurance, or suspected fraud are involved, prioritize direct contact with the relevant organization through a verified channel and keep a written log.
