# What to do if… your boarding pass will not scan at the gate and boarding is in progress (USA)

## Short answer
Step out of the line immediately and alert a gate agent to manually verify your credentials or reissue the pass before the 15-minute domestic or 30-minute international departure cutoff.

## Do not do these things
- Don't repeatedly scan the same pass if the gate reader shows a red light or error message.
- Don't leave the immediate gate area to find a kiosk; the agent at the podium is the only one who can clear a boarding-active hold.
- Don't attempt to force or bypass automated e-gates if they do not open.
- Don't start a full device reboot or app reinstallation while standing at the scanner.
- Don't hand your phone or passport to other passengers in the line to "try for you."

## What to do now
1. **Move to the side of the boarding lane while remaining visible to the gate agent.**
   Keep your **REAL ID-compliant identification or passport in hand** and visible, as the agent will need it to manually process your boarding or clear system flags.
2. **Alert the agent with a single, clear statement of the problem.**
   "My pass is not scanning and the gate is active—can you check for a system hold or reissue the pass?"
   If using an **automated e-gate or biometric scanner**, look for the specific "Assistance" or "Manual Check" button often located near the screen.
3. **Perform immediate hardware adjustments to the device or paper.**
   - Set screen brightness to **100 percent** and disable "Auto-Rotate."
   - Wipe the screen or paper barcode to remove smudges or moisture.
   - If using an **automated facial recognition gate**, remove hats, glasses, and masks to allow the camera a clear view.
4. **Identify the specific error type to the agent.**
   Notice if the scanner shows "Seat Change," "Document Verification Required," or "Invalid Flight."
   Providing the specific message allows the agent to skip diagnostic steps and go straight to the **manual manifest override**.
5. **Request a printed "Gate Pass" if digital versions continue to fail.**
   Ask: "Can you print a paper slip or thermal pass from the podium terminal?"
   A physical slip generated at the gate often bypasses the data sync issues that cause mobile app failures.
6. **Provide international travel documents immediately for itinerary-linked blocks.**
   Many scan failures occur because the system requires a **physical passport swipe** at the gate to finalize the manifest.
   Open your passport to the photo page before reaching the agent.
7. **Verify your boarding status before stepping onto the jet bridge.**
   Ensure the agent confirms you are "Cleared" or "Checked-In" on their screen.
   Failure to be scanned into the **electronic manifest** can result in being marked as a "No-Show," potentially canceling your return flights.

## What can wait
- You do not need to investigate why the app failed or why your group number was not recognized until after you have secured your seat.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the scanner technology or gate agent response while boarding is still underway.
- You do not need to call the airline's central customer service line; they cannot override a gate-level boarding block in real-time.

## Important reassurance
Boarding pass scan failures are common technical occurrences often caused by simple brightness issues, seat reassignments, or required document pings. Gate agents have the tools to manually board you or reprint a pass in seconds if you alert them before the door-close deadline.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate stabilization of a boarding failure at the gate. It does not cover missed flight rebooking or compensation for denied boarding, which require separate procedures at the customer service desk.

## Important note
Airlines maintain strict departure windows and may close boarding 15 minutes before domestic flights and 30 minutes before international flights regardless of technical issues. Possession of a boarding pass does not guarantee boarding if security or documentation requirements are not met. As of 2025/2026, REAL ID requirements are strictly enforced for all domestic travel.
