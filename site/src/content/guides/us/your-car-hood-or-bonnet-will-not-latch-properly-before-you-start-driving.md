# What to do if… your car hood or bonnet will not latch properly before you start driving (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive if the hood is not fully latched. Keep the vehicle parked and arrange roadside assistance or a tow to a repair shop.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive relying on the safety catch alone.
- Do not use straps, rope, bungees, tape, or weight as a substitute for a working latch.
- Do not keep slamming the hood harder; that can bend parts and make alignment worse.
- Do not put hands or fingers near the latch while someone is closing the hood.
- Do not test it by driving at speed.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the vehicle stopped if you can.** If you are in a driveway, parking lot, gas station, or other safe place, leave the vehicle parked.
2. **If the vehicle is in an immediately unsafe spot, move only to the nearest safer pause if that is clearly possible.** Use hazard lights, go slowly, and stop again as soon as you are out of immediate danger. If the hood could lift, do not continue; call for help from where you are.
3. **Try one careful normal re-latch attempt.**
   - Open the hood fully.
   - Remove obvious loose items or obstructions near the latch and striker, such as tools, rags, leaves, or ice.
   - Close the hood using the normal method in your owner’s manual if it is quickly available.
   - If it does not latch with normal pressure, stop.
4. **Check whether it is actually latched.** From the front edge, gently try lifting the hood. If it moves, rattles, pops up, or only catches on the secondary safety catch, it is not secure.
5. **Treat warning signs as unsafe.** A visible gap, a hood ajar message, a loose hood release lever, or a hood that will not sit evenly can mean the primary latch is not engaged.
6. **Stop troubleshooting if it still will not latch.** Common possibilities include a misaligned striker, a sticking or worn latch, corrosion, debris, or a release cable problem. This is not something to solve by driving.
7. **Request roadside help or towing.** Say clearly: “The hood will not latch and may open while driving.” Choose towing over driving to a shop.
8. **After the vehicle is safely parked, check for open recalls by VIN.** If a recall applies, follow the manufacturer or NHTSA remedy instructions and contact a dealer.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical cause right now.
- You do not need to decide about reimbursement, complaints, insurance, or who is responsible until the vehicle is secure.
- You do not need to attempt adjustments beyond removing obvious debris and trying one normal close.
- You do not need to choose a long-term repair option before the immediate risk is controlled.

## Important reassurance
Stopping before driving is the right call. Hood-latch problems are often repairable, and keeping the vehicle parked prevents the worst immediate risk: the hood opening while you are moving.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent a hood opening while driving and blocking your view. Later repair decisions may need a qualified mechanic, dealership service department, roadside provider, insurer, or other specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general safety information, not mechanical, legal, towing, insurance, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow emergency instructions, local road rules, and the advice of qualified responders or repair professionals for your situation.
