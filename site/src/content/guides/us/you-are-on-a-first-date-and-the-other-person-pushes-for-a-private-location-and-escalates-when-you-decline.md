# What to do if… you are on a first date and the other person pushes for a private location and escalates when you decline (USA)

## Short answer
End the date immediately, move to a staffed area of the venue, and do not leave the building until you have a safe, confirmed exit. Call 911 if they block your movement, follow you, or make you fear for your physical safety.

## Do not do these things
- Don't go to a private location (home, car, or secluded area) to try to calm them down.
- Don't stay to explain or justify your "no"—escalation is a sign that reasoning will not work.
- Don't step outside to "talk it out" away from other people.
- Don't let them drive you or walk you to your vehicle.
- Don't accept any more drinks or food once the interaction has turned.
- Don't worry about being "polite" or "making a scene" if you need to get help from staff.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a staffed or high-traffic area of the venue.**
   - Go to the bar, the host stand, or the kitchen entrance.
   - If you are outside, walk back into the nearest open business or well-lit public space.
2. **Tell the other person the date is over in one short sentence.**
   - **"The date is over; I am leaving now."**
   - Do not engage in further debate or answer "why" questions.
3. **Ask staff for immediate assistance in exiting safely.**
   - Speak to a server, bartender, or manager and say: "I am on a date and I do not feel safe. Can you help me leave through a separate exit or wait with me until my ride arrives?"
   - Many bars use "Angel Shot" or similar codes; ask staff if they have a safety protocol for dates.
4. **Arrange for a ride that you control entirely.**
   - Use a rideshare app or call a taxi from inside the building.
   - Do not walk to your car if the other person is waiting near the exit.
5. **Call 911 if the person follows you, blocks your path, or touches you without consent.**
   - State your location and that you are being harassed or followed.
   - If you cannot speak safely, text 911 if that service is available in your jurisdiction.
6. **Alert a contact and share your live location.**
   - Call a friend or family member and stay on the line until you are behind a locked door at home.
   - Send your current location and a description of the person you are with to this contact.
7. **Wait inside the venue until your transport is physically at the door.**
   - Do not stand on the sidewalk waiting for a ride.
   - Ask a staff member or security to watch you enter the vehicle.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to report them to the dating app or platform.
- Blocking their number or social media profiles (do this only once you are physically safe).
- Reflecting on the "red flags" or why the situation escalated.
- Responding to any follow-up texts or apologies they may send.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a "fright" or "freeze" response when someone reacts with anger to a simple boundary. This reaction is your body's safety system alerting you to a genuine risk. You are not responsible for their emotional reaction to your "no."

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate physical extraction from an escalating situation. It does not cover long-term stalking, filing restraining orders, or legal prosecution.

## Important note
This information is for immediate stabilization and is not legal advice. If you have been physically harmed or touched without consent, seek medical attention. For confidential support once you are safe, contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline (call 1-800-799-7233 or text "START" to 88788) or loveisrespect (call 866-331-9474 or text "LOVEIS" to 22522).
