# What to do if… you cannot find the identity documents you need for an official process (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the organization exactly which documents it accepts, whether alternatives are allowed, and which deadline matters most. Order only the replacements needed for the process that is blocked now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use unofficial websites that resemble government services. Start with an official state or federal website.
- Do not order every possible record before confirming what the requester accepts.
- Do not mail your only original identity document unless the requester confirms that it is necessary and you have checked the mailing instructions.
- Do not send full scans of sensitive documents in group chats, forwarded email threads, or unsolicited messages.
- Do not keep retrying an online identity check with guesses. Ask the requester what to do when the usual documents are unavailable.
- Do not report an expired passport as lost or stolen.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the requester for the accepted-document list and the deadline.**  
   Use a clear message: “I cannot find the usual identity document. Please confirm which alternatives you accept, whether copies must be certified, and the deadline for this check.” Ask whether an in-person check, a different document combination, or a temporary pause is available.

2. **Write one short list: missing, available, requested.**  
   Include the document name, the organization asking for it, and the date you requested a replacement. This helps you focus on the immediate blocker without searching the same places repeatedly.

3. **Request the specific vital record you need through the correct official route.**  
   For a birth, death, marriage, or divorce record from the United States or a U.S. territory, use the vital-records office for the place where the event occurred. Requirements, eligibility, fees, and ordering routes vary. If the event occurred abroad, check which official route applies. Ask the requester whether it needs a certified copy before paying for one.

4. **If the process follows a death, ask how many certified death-certificate copies are actually needed.**  
   Some organizations may require a certified copy. For a death in the United States, use the vital-records office for the state where the death occurred. Do not order extra copies until you have checked the immediate requirements.

5. **Replace the missing government-issued ID through the official route.**  
   - For a driver’s license or state ID, use your state motor vehicle agency.
   - For a Social Security card, start with the Social Security Administration’s replacement-card page. Depending on your situation, you may be able to apply online; otherwise, you may need an appointment at a local office. If you know your Social Security number, ask whether a physical card is actually required.
   - For a valid lost or stolen U.S. passport, report it promptly and apply in person for a replacement. Once reported, it is cancelled and cannot be used for travel even if you later find it.

6. **If your passport is missing while you are abroad, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.**  
   You will need to apply in person for a replacement. If your travel is urgent, consular staff may be able to issue an emergency passport.

7. **If personal information may have been stolen or misused, use the official identity-theft guidance.**  
   Record what is missing and when you noticed. Use the USA.gov identity-theft page to check the appropriate reporting and protective steps for your situation.

8. **Ask the requester what it can do while replacements are pending.**  
   Use a simple message: “I have requested the missing document. Can you note my file and tell me whether you can accept another document combination, arrange an in-person check, or allow more time?”

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve every organization’s identity requirement today. Start with the process blocking urgent access or an immediate deadline.
- You do not need to order every possible replacement record.
- You do not need to make wider decisions about probate, estate administration, or family disagreements now.
- You do not need a perfect filing system. One short missing, available, requested list is enough.

## Important reassurance
Missing documents can turn a simple official task into an exhausting problem, especially after a death or during a family crisis. You can reduce the pressure by confirming the accepted list, focusing on the immediate blocker, and replacing only the records that are needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize an identity-document problem. Later decisions about probate filings, estate administration, disputed identity records, or suspected identity theft may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Requirements vary by state, organization, and process. Ask the requesting organization to confirm its accepted documents, deadlines, and any temporary options in writing.
