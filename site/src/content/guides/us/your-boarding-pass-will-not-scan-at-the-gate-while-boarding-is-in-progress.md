# What to do if… your boarding pass will not scan at the gate while boarding is in progress (USA)

## Short answer
Step out of the flow of the line, stay at the podium, and ask the gate agent to check the pass, refresh the record, or reissue it if possible before boarding closes.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep rescanning while blocking boarding; move aside and get the gate agent involved.
- Do not leave the gate area without telling the gate agent what is happening.
- Do not hand your phone, ID, passport, or bags to strangers.
- Do not start slow troubleshooting, such as deleting the airline app or resetting passwords, unless the gate agent tells you to.
- Do not assume you can board later because you are already at the gate; boarding can close quickly under airline procedures.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the side, but stay within sight and hearing of the podium.**  
   Keep your boarding pass open and have your government-issued photo ID ready. Use your passport if this is an international itinerary or if the agent asks for it.

2. **Tell the gate agent the problem in one sentence.**  
   Say: “My boarding pass will not scan and boarding is in progress. Can you check it or reissue it?”  
   If the agent is scanning other passengers, stand where they can see you and wait for the first natural pause.

3. **Do only quick scan fixes while you are waiting.**
   - Turn your screen brightness up.
   - Tilt the phone to reduce glare.
   - Wipe smudges off the screen.
   - Open the pass from the airline app or wallet if one version is lagging.
   - Make sure the full barcode or QR code is visible.
   - If you have paper, flatten it and avoid folding through the barcode.

4. **Ask for the fastest fallback if the phone or barcode is the problem.**  
   Say: “Can you print or refresh my boarding pass here?”  
   If the podium cannot do it, ask whether you should stay there, go to a service desk, or use a nearby airline counter if there is still time.

5. **Let the agent handle any record, seat, document, or verification issue.**  
   A pass may fail because the airline record needs attention, a seat changed, a document check is incomplete, or the pass version is stale. Give the agent your ID or passport if asked, answer only the quick questions needed, and avoid debating while boarding is moving.

6. **For international travel, make document checking easy.**  
   Keep your passport open to the photo page if asked. If the agent needs to check travel documents before boarding, let them complete that check before trying to scan again.

7. **If you are travelling with others, prevent accidental separation.**  
   Tell your group to wait nearby unless the gate agent says they should board. Keep your ID, phone, wallet, medication, and essential documents with you.

8. **If boarding closes before the scan problem is fixed, ask for the next immediate airline step.**  
   Say: “Boarding closed before this was resolved. What are my options now?”  
   Ask the gate agent whether you should stay at the gate, go to the service desk, or use the airline app for rebooking instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why it happened right now.
- You do not need to decide about refunds, compensation, complaints, or blame in the boarding area.
- You do not need to call general customer service while a gate agent is available in front of you.
- You do not need to reinstall apps, search email history, or compare airline policies unless the gate agent says it is necessary.

## Important reassurance
A boarding pass scan failure is stressful, but it does not automatically mean you are off the flight. The fastest useful move is to stop fighting the scanner and get the gate agent to check or reissue the pass while boarding is still active.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate “boarding pass will not scan at the gate” moment. If boarding closes or the airline refuses transport, later decisions may need airline customer service, the airline’s contract of carriage, or aviation consumer rights help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, financial, security, or professional advice. Airlines set boarding procedures and may close boarding under their policies; options can vary by carrier, airport, itinerary, and the reason the pass will not scan.
