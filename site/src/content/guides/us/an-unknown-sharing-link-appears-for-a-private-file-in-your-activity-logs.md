# What to do if… an unknown sharing link appears for a private file in your activity logs (USA)

## Short answer
Use the provider’s official app or website to disable the unexpected sharing link and check who can access the file. Then secure the account and record the activity details shown in the log.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open or forward the unfamiliar sharing URL just to test it.
- Do not delete the file or close the account in panic.
- Do not use a support phone number or email address from an unexpected message. Use the provider’s official help page.
- Do not keep editing or sharing the file until you have checked its current access settings.

## What to do now
1. **Disable the unexpected sharing link.**
   - Open the provider’s official app or website.
   - Find the file’s sharing or access settings.
   - Delete or disable the unexpected link.
   - Use the most restrictive appropriate access setting available, such as restricted access or specific people only.
   - If the provider offers **Stop sharing**, use it when you need to remove all sharing from the item.

2. **Check who can access the file.**
   - Review the file’s current sharing list.
   - Remove access you do not recognize or expect.
   - Remove unexpected edit access and public link access.
   - Check the containing folder as well, because folder access can affect the file.

3. **Record the activity before making further changes.**
   - Take screenshots or notes showing the file name, file path, timestamp, link type, and any activity details the provider displays.
   - Do not forward the sharing URL.
   - If this is a work, school, or organization-managed account, send the notes to your administrator or security team.

4. **Secure the account that owns the file.**
   - Change the password. Change it on any other account where you reused the same password.
   - Turn on multi-factor authentication if it is available.
   - Use the provider’s account-security page to sign out of other devices or sessions if that option is available.
   - Review recent sign-in activity and remove unfamiliar devices.

5. **Review account settings for unfamiliar changes.**
   - Check connected apps or third-party access if the provider shows them, and remove access you do not recognize.
   - Check recovery email addresses and phone numbers.
   - If the account uses email, check for unexpected forwarding settings or inbox rules.

6. **For a work, school, or organization-managed account, contact the administrator promptly.**
   - Say: **“An unrecognized sharing link for a private file appeared in the activity log. I disabled the link and recorded the available details.”**
   - Ask the administrator to review the activity log, current sharing access, and account-security activity.

7. **List any sensitive information that was in the file.**
   - Note whether the file contained information such as a Social Security number, ID scan, banking details, or health information.
   - The sharing-link entry alone does not confirm whether anyone opened or downloaded the file.
   - If personal information may have been misused, use IdentityTheft.gov for a recovery plan.
   - If you suspect cyber-enabled crime or fraud, you may report it through IC3. Type **ic3.gov** directly into your browser and avoid lookalike sites.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to notify every possible recipient.
- You do not need to migrate cloud providers or reorganize your storage now.
- You do not need to wipe your devices solely because a sharing link appeared.
- You can make longer-term decisions after the link is disabled, the account is secured, and the activity has been reviewed.

## Important reassurance
An unexpected sharing link does not by itself prove that someone accessed the file or took over the account. Disabling the link, checking access, securing the account, and recording the log details will reduce uncertainty and limit further sharing while the activity is reviewed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on the platform, whether the account is personal or organization-managed, and what information the file contained. Specialist help may be needed.
