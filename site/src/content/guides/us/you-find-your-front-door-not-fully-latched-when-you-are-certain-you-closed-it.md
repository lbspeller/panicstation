# What to do if… you find your front door not fully latched when you are certain you closed it (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter the premises. Move to a safe distance immediately and call 911 if you suspect an intruder is present or if you feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step inside to "check" or look for missing items.
- Do not call out into the house or announce your presence.
- Do not pull the door shut or lock it from the outside, as this could trap an intruder inside.
- Do not touch the door handle, lock, or door frame to avoid disturbing forensic evidence.
- Do not attempt to "clear" the rooms or search the property yourself.
- Do not confront anyone you see exiting or entering the property.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause at a significant distance from the doorway.** Exit the porch, hallway, or immediate vicinity and find a location where you are not visible from the door, such as inside a locked vehicle or a neighbor's home.
2. **Call 911 immediately if you see signs of a break-in.** Signs include wood splinters, visible pry marks, broken glass, or the sound of movement inside.
   - Inform the dispatcher you have arrived home to find your door open/unlatched.
   - Provide your exact address and current safe location.
   - Stay on the line until police arrive.
3. **Contact your local police non-emergency line if there is no immediate signs of entry.** If the door is simply unlatched without damage and you do not hear or see anyone, use the local non-emergency number (often found on the police department's website) rather than 911. 
4. **Observe from a distance without re-approaching.** Watch for anyone exiting the property or any vehicles parked nearby that you do not recognize.
5. **Check smart-lock or doorbell camera logs only once you are in a safe spot.** If you have remote access, look for "door opened" or "motion detected" events that occurred while you were away, but do not let this delay your call to authorities if you feel at risk.
6. **Alert neighbors or building security.** If you are in an apartment complex, notify on-site security or management after you have contacted the police.
7. **Wait for law enforcement to "clear" the building.** Do not enter until officers have physically inspected the interior and confirmed no one is inside.
8. **Document the scene once cleared.** After police have finished their sweep, take photographs of the latch and door frame to show the condition in which you found it.

## What can wait
- Determining why the latch failed (e.g., thermal expansion, strike plate misalignment).
- Calling a locksmith for repairs.
- Checking for specific missing jewelry or electronics.
- Contacting your insurance provider.

## Important reassurance
It is common for doors to fail to latch due to mechanical issues, air pressure changes, or settling frames. Taking a cautious approach and remaining outside is a standard safety protocol and is not an overreaction, regardless of the eventual cause.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes after discovering an unlatched door. It does not cover burglary investigations, insurance claims, or home security upgrades.

## Important note
In the United States, 911 should be used for any situation where a crime may be in progress or there is a potential threat to life. If you are uncertain about the level of danger, err on the side of caution and treat the situation as an emergency.
