# What to do if… your device shows a pop-up saying you have a virus and it tries to scare you into calling a number (USA)

## Short answer
Do not enter credentials for any account you do not recognize. Verify which account is currently linked to your app store purchases in your device settings and secure your primary accounts from a separate, trusted device.

## Do not do these things
- Do not type passwords into unexpected pop-up windows for accounts you do not own.
- Do not call phone numbers provided in "security alerts" or pop-up prompts.
- Do not factory reset the device immediately, as this may trigger activation locks that are harder to bypass later.
- Do not remove work or school management profiles without authorization, as this may delete required data or network access.
- Do not download "fix-it" tools or "support" profiles from third-party websites or links in text messages.

## What to do now
1. **Treat all unexpected credential prompts as unverified until you check system settings directly.** Avoid clicking links in emails or texts that claim your account is locked; instead, manually navigate to the official app store or settings app on your device.
2. **Determine if the issue is limited to specific applications.** If only one or two apps require a different account, those apps were likely downloaded using a different Apple Account or Google Account (such as an old account or a family member's login).
3. **Check the active purchase account on Apple devices.** Navigate to **Settings**, tap your **Name**, and select **Media & Purchases**.
   - Tap **View Account** to see the specific email address authorized for the App Store.
   - If the prompt is for an old account you recognize, delete the specific app and reinstall it using your current Apple Account.
4. **Confirm the active Google Play account on Android devices.** Open the **Play Store**, tap your **Profile Icon**, and tap the **Dropdown Arrow** next to your email address.
   - Verify if the correct account is selected.
   - If an app was installed under a different Google Account, you must uninstall it and reinstall it while signed into your preferred account.
5. **Review the device for unauthorized management profiles.** Navigate to **Settings > General > VPN & Device Management** (iOS) or **Settings > Google > Work Profile** (Android).
   - If you see a "Configuration Profile" or "MDM" that you do not recognize and did not install for work or school, document the name of the profile.
   - Do not delete the profile yet if you suspect a targeted compromise; seek professional IT support first.
6. **Secure your primary accounts from a different, trusted computer or device.** Change the password for your primary Apple Account or Google Account immediately.
   - Ensure **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** is enabled.
   - Review the list of "Trusted Devices" or "Logged-in Devices" and remove any that you do not recognize.
7. **Initiate an official identity recovery workflow if you entered credentials into a suspicious prompt.** Visit **IdentityTheft.gov** to create a recovery plan if you believe your personal information has been compromised.
   - Report the scam attempt at **ReportFraud.ftc.gov**.
   - Contact your mobile carrier to request a "Port-Out Pin" or "SIM Lock" to prevent unauthorized transfers of your phone number.

## What can wait
- You do not need to merge multiple accounts or consolidate your digital history today.
- You do not need to delete your entire search history or cache.
- You do not need to replace your physical device unless hardware damage is suspected.

## Important reassurance
These prompts frequently occur because of "orphaned" apps—software downloaded years ago under a different email address that is now triggering an update check. Seeing an unfamiliar email does not always mean your device is "hacked," but pausing to verify settings is the correct safety response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for account and device discrepancies. It does not cover deep forensic analysis or long-term identity restoration.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and may not reflect the specific UI changes of every device manufacturer or software version. If you suspect a serious security breach, contact Apple Support or Google Support through their official websites directly.
