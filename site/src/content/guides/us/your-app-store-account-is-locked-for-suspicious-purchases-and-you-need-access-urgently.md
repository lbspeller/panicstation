# What to do if… your app store account is locked for suspicious purchases and you need access urgently (USA)

## Short answer
Stop trying purchases for the moment, secure the affected Apple or Google account, then use the official reactivation or payment-verification route shown in the account. Do not use links from unexpected texts, emails, pop-ups, or callers.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying purchases, rapidly adding cards, or repeatedly changing account details.
- Do not use a link, phone number, QR code, or attachment from a “fraud alert” message unless you reached it from the official Apple, Google, bank, or card app yourself.
- Do not pay anyone who claims they can unlock the account faster.
- Do not share verification codes, recovery keys, passwords, screenshots of IDs, or card images with anyone who contacted you first.
- Do not let anyone remote into your phone or computer to “fix” the lock.
- Do not file disputes in panic; first work out whether a charge is unauthorised, then use your bank and the platform’s official routes.

## What to do now
1. **Pause purchases and save the exact message.**  
   Take a screenshot or write down the exact wording, such as “Your Media & Purchases account has been disabled,” “Apple Account locked,” “Your payment was declined due to an issue with your account,” “Verification needed,” or “Payments profile suspended.” Keep any order number or transaction ID shown.

2. **Check for an Apple restrictions lookalike.**  
   If your Apple Account settings are greyed out, open **Settings → Screen Time → Content & Privacy Restrictions → Accounts** and check whether account changes are set to **Allow**. If this fixes the issue, it was probably a device restriction rather than a purchase-security lock.

3. **Secure the account before sending payment or ID information.**  
   Change the password from the official Apple or Google account page, confirm two-factor security, remove devices or sessions you do not recognise, and remove payment methods you do not recognise.

4. **Use the official route for the platform.**  
   - **Apple App Store:** if the message says **“Your Media & Purchases account has been disabled,”** use Apple’s official reactivation route. If the message says the Apple Account is locked, not active, or disabled, follow Apple’s official account access route rather than third-party advice.
   - **Google Play or Google Payments:** sign in at **payments.google.com**, check **Alerts**, choose **Verify** or follow any request for more information, and keep your name, address, and payment details current.

5. **Answer only the verification request you can see inside the account.**  
   If Google or Apple asks for payment or identity information, provide it only through the official signed-in account page or official support flow. Do not upload documents through a link sent by an unexpected message.

6. **Check whether the bank or card issuer is also blocking the charge.**  
   Use the phone number on the back of the card or the bank’s official app. Ask whether recent app-store transactions were declined, flagged, or reported as stolen, and whether the billing address on file matches the card.

7. **Get urgent app access without worsening the lock.**  
   Use browser versions of essential services, such as email, banking, work tools, or school tools. For a work or school device, contact IT and ask whether they can push the needed app through managed device tools instead of using your personal store purchases.

8. **Escalate through official support if the lock remains.**  
   Give Apple, Google, or your bank the exact message, when it started, the affected account email, and relevant transaction IDs. Keep the request focused on account reactivation or payment verification.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to change phones, abandon the account, cancel every subscription, or create a new app-store account.
- You do not need to review every past purchase immediately; first secure the account and start the official verification route.
- If you later see signs beyond the app store, such as new accounts you did not open, credit alerts, tax issues, or benefits issues, you can use the FTC identity-theft route after the urgent access problem is calmer.

## Important reassurance
A suspicious-purchase lock can be an automated protection, a payment mismatch, a bank decline, or a sign that the platform needs more information. The safest next move is not speed at any cost; it is using the official signed-in route and avoiding anything that exposes passwords, codes, documents, or payment details to the wrong person.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and regain urgent access. Later decisions about refunds, unauthorised charges, identity theft, subscriptions, or account recovery may need help from the platform, your bank, your card issuer, your workplace IT team, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, cybersecurity, or professional advice. Processes, review outcomes, and timing can vary, so rely on official Apple, Google, bank, card issuer, and FTC channels for your specific account.
