# What to do if… someone is live-streaming you or your location in a way that feels targeted (USA)

## Short answer
If you can do so safely, move to a nearby staffed public place without leading anyone toward your home or another private destination. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance, call 911. From a safer pause, turn off any live location sharing you control and report the stream or account through the platform.

## Do not do these things
- Do not physically confront the person or try to take their device.
- Do not lead the person toward your home, workplace, vehicle, or an isolated place.
- Do not post your real-time location or private details in response.
- Do not stay exposed while trying to collect complete evidence.
- Do not argue with viewers or try to manage the stream yourself while you are moving to safety.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you can do so safely, move into a nearby staffed public place, such as a store, hotel lobby, transit desk, or other place with staff. Tell staff or security that someone is streaming your location in a way that feels targeted and that you need a safer place to pause.
2. **Call 911 if you need immediate assistance.** Tell the call-taker your current location and what is happening. Follow the call-taker’s instructions.
3. **Reduce location sharing you control.** From the safer pause, turn off any live location sharing in maps, family, messaging, or social apps if you can do this quickly. Do not spend time changing settings if it keeps you exposed.
4. **Ask one trusted person to help.** Ask them to check the stream and save the account handle, platform, link if available, time, threats, calls for viewers to approach you, and any mention of your address or current location.
5. **Report the stream or account through the platform.** Use the platform’s in-app report tools once you are in a safer pause. A trusted person can do this while you focus on safety.
6. **Use a non-emergency route later if appropriate.** If you do not need emergency assistance but want to report what happened, use your local law-enforcement agency’s published non-emergency contact. For ongoing cyber-enabled conduct, you may also submit an IC3 complaint after your immediate safety is stabilized. IC3 does not replace 911 or local law enforcement.
7. **Choose your next move from the safer pause.** Ask staff, security, a trusted person, or law enforcement for help before leaving if you are concerned that the person or viewers may still be tracking your location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact law or decide whether the conduct meets a specific legal definition right now.
- You do not need to collect every repost, comment, or viewer response before asking for help.
- You do not need to contact the streamer or request deletion in the moment.
- You do not need to make a public statement or respond online.
- You do not need to complete an IC3 complaint until you are in a safer pause.

## Important reassurance
You may feel frozen or exposed. You do not have to prove the person’s intent before taking basic safety steps. Moving to a staffed place, reducing the location sharing you control, and asking for help are reasonable first actions.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions about documentation, further reports, or local protections may need specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance assistance, call 911.
