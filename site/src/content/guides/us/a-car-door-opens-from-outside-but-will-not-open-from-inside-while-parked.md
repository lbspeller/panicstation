# What to do if… a car door opens from outside but will not open from inside while parked (USA)

## Short answer
Get everyone out through another door if you can do that safely. If anyone, especially a child, cannot exit safely, is overheating, is panicking, or feels unwell, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive away while someone may need the affected door as an exit.
- Do not keep yanking the inside handle hard or repeatedly; it may worsen a broken handle, cable, latch, or trim.
- Do not pry the door open with tools in a parking area.
- Do not leave a child, older person, disabled person, pet, or unwell person seated next to the affected door.
- Do not leave anyone alone in the vehicle while an exit is blocked.
- Do not assume a recall search means the door is safe to use; it only shows certain recall information.

## What to do now
1. Put the vehicle in Park, set the parking brake, and keep the key or fob with the driver. Keep everyone calm and still for a moment.

2. If the location is unsafe, use the safest immediate option available: stay locked in the vehicle and call 911, or move only if everyone can exit by another door and moving does not put anyone at greater risk.

3. Get the person out through another door if possible. Have them move slowly across the seat, keep feet clear of pedals and door pockets, and use the easiest working door.

4. If the affected door is a rear door, check the child safety lock:
   - Open the affected door from the outside.
   - Look on the rear edge or shut-face of the door near the latch for a small lever, slot, or switch.
   - Move it to the off or unlocked position if you can identify it clearly.
   - Close the door and test it once from inside while the vehicle is still parked.

5. Try one simple lock reset if no one is in distress. Lock and unlock the vehicle once using the key fob, key, or central lock button, then test the inside handle once. If your vehicle has a security or anti-theft lock mode and you are unsure what it does, stop troubleshooting and use another exit.

6. If the inside handle still does not work, treat that seating position as unusable until repaired. Do not carry a passenger beside that door.

7. If anyone is stuck, overheating, panicking, short of breath, confused, faint, or otherwise unwell, call 911. Say: “We are parked, but a car door will not open from the inside and someone cannot exit safely.”

8. If everyone is out and safe, contact roadside assistance, the vehicle manufacturer’s roadside service, your insurer’s roadside service, a locksmith who handles vehicles, or a repair shop. Describe it as an inside door release, latch, lock, or child-lock problem.

9. Once things are calm, check the official NHTSA recall lookup with the vehicle’s VIN or license plate. If the problem looks like a safety defect, you can also report it to NHTSA after the immediate situation is safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact failed part right now.
- You do not need to remove the door panel in a parking area.
- You do not need to decide about costs, complaints, warranty issues, or legal steps while anyone is still stressed or stuck.
- You do not need to keep testing the door once you know it is not a safe exit.

## Important reassurance
A blocked exit can feel frightening, and it makes sense to take it seriously. Common explanations include a child safety lock, a loose inside handle, a latch problem, or a lock actuator fault; focus first on getting everyone out safely.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first minutes while parked. A door that will not open from the inside should be inspected and repaired before that seat is used again.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not mechanical, legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If anyone cannot exit safely or there is any immediate risk, call emergency services.
