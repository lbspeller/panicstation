# What to do if… a car door opens from outside but will not open from inside while parked (USA)

## Short answer
Exit the vehicle immediately through any other functional door or window. If anyone is unable to move to another exit and is at risk of heat or medical distress, call **911** immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep pulling the internal handle if it feels loose or offers no resistance.
- Do not attempt to exit through a door facing active traffic.
- Do not use tools or screwdrivers to pry the door panel or latch mechanism.
- Do not leave a child or vulnerable person unattended near the malfunctioning door.
- Do not force the car into motion if an occupant is still struggling to exit.
- Do not use "double-locking" or "deadlock" features on the key fob while troubleshooting.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the most accessible alternative exit immediately.** **Move all occupants to the nearest working door, typically the opposite side or the front passenger/driver doors.**
   - Ensure the vehicle is in "Park" with the emergency brake engaged before moving across seats.
2. **Conduct a single power lock reset to rule out electronic errors.** **Press the "unlock" button on the driver’s console or key fob twice, ensuring all door pins or indicators move to the unlocked position.**
3. **Open the affected door from the outside to inspect the child safety lock.** **If the door opens from the exterior, look at the thin vertical edge of the door near the metal latch for a small plastic lever or a key-slot switch.**
   - If the lever is in the "Down" or "Locked" position, move it to the opposite "Off" or "Unlocked" position.
   - Close the door and test the internal handle.
4. **Listen for a "click" or mechanical sound when pulling the interior handle.** **Note if the handle feels completely limp or if it provides normal resistance without releasing the latch.**
   - A limp handle often indicates a disconnected internal cable.
   - A handle with resistance that fails to open may indicate a jammed latch or an active safety lock.
5. **Call 911 if an occupant is stuck and environmental conditions are extreme.** **Request emergency assistance if the vehicle is in direct sun, temperatures are rising, or the person inside is showing signs of panic or medical distress.**
6. **Lower all windows to improve airflow and communication.** **Ensure you can speak clearly to anyone still inside and provide instructions on how to move to a functional exit.**
7. **Flag the malfunctioning door for other passengers.** **Place a piece of tape over the internal handle or verbally instruct all future passengers that the exit is non-functional until repaired.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the failure is a cable, rod, or actuator right now.
- You do not need to check for manufacturer recalls or technical service bulletins while still at the scene.
- You do not need to contact a locksmith or mechanic until all occupants are safely outside the vehicle.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair or part replacement today.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of claustrophobia or panic when a door fails to open from the inside. This is a common mechanical or safety-feature issue, often involving the child-lock system or a simple cable disconnection. As long as other doors are functional, you are not truly trapped.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a stationary vehicle with a single-point door failure. It does not cover emergency egress following a collision or total electrical failure in submerged vehicles.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety stabilization and does not replace professional mechanical inspection. Vehicle door latches are critical safety components; a failure to open from the inside may indicate a compromised latch that could also fail to hold the door closed during a collision. Seek repair from a certified technician before using the affected seat again.
