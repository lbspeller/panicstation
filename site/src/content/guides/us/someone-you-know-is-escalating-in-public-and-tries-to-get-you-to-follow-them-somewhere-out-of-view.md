# What to do if… someone you know is escalating in public and tries to get you to follow them somewhere out of view (USA)

## Short answer
Do not leave the public area or follow the person out of sight. Stay where other people are visible, move toward a staffed counter or security, and call 911 immediately if you feel threatened or blocked.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow the person to a parking lot, vehicle, stairwell, restroom corridor, or empty side room.
- Do not get into any vehicle with them, even if they claim it is only to talk for a moment.
- Do not hand over your phone, car keys, or wallet if they demand them during the escalation.
- Do not stay in a corner or a spot where your exit to a busy area is blocked.
- Do not worry about social embarrassment or making a scene; your safety is the priority.

## What to do now
1. **Maintain visibility and move toward a crowded, staffed area.** Step into the nearest open business, such as a grocery store, hotel lobby, or cafe, and position yourself near a service counter.
2. **State your refusal clearly and briefly.** Use a neutral, firm phrase such as "I am staying here" or "I am not going to the car" and repeat it without further explanation.
3. **Notify staff or security immediately that you are being pressured.** Walk directly to an employee and say: "I am being pressured to go somewhere private and I do not feel safe; please stay with me."
4. **Identify specific bystanders to assist you.** Point to a specific person and say: "You in the blue shirt, please stay here with me" or "You, please call 911 for me."
5. **Call 911 if you are being physically blocked, grabbed, or feel an immediate threat.** Provide the dispatcher with:
   - Your exact location (business name or street address).
   - A description of the person escalating.
   - The fact that you are being pressured to leave the public view.
6. **Text 911 only if you are in a situation where speaking is unsafe.** Follow the "call if you can, text if you can't" rule; if texting, send your location and the nature of the emergency in the first message.
7. **Get to a security desk or information center if you are in a large facility.** Universities, malls, and hospitals have dedicated security stations that are often safer than general public areas.
8. **Create physical distance while remaining in the line of sight of others.** If the person follows you, continue moving toward lights, cameras, and witnesses rather than trying to hide in a secluded spot.
9. **Contact a trusted person once you have reached a secure point.** Tell them your location and briefly summarize that you were being pressured to isolate, so they are aware of the situation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now how this will affect your relationship with this person.
- You do not need to figure out why they are acting this way or what triggered the escalation.
- You do not need to decide immediately if you will file a formal police report or a restraining order.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of "freeze" or a desire to comply to avoid a public scene when someone you know acts out. Choosing to stay in a crowded area and seeking help is a correct and necessary protective response.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization in an active public confrontation where isolation is being pressured. It does not cover long-term safety planning or legal remedies.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. If you are in immediate danger or being physically restrained, contact 911 or local law enforcement.
