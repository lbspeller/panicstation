# What to do if… your key snaps, bends, or breaks and you cannot unlock or start your vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Stop applying pressure to the key immediately. Move to a safe location away from traffic, activate your hazard lights, and contact an automotive locksmith or roadside assistance for professional extraction.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to force the key to turn using pliers or tools if it is already bent or cracked.
- Do not use super glue or adhesives to try and "fish out" a broken fragment.
- Do not insert paperclips, wire hangers, or screwdrivers into the lock or ignition.
- Do not attempt to "bridge" the broken pieces by shoving the head of the key back into the slot.
- Do not spray household oils, WD-40, or grease into the ignition cylinder unless specified by your manual.
- Do not stay inside or stand next to the vehicle if it is stopped in a live traffic lane or narrow shoulder.

## What to do now
1. **Activate hazard lights and move to safety.**
   - Switch on your emergency flashers immediately; in many states, this triggers "Move Over" law protections for your vehicle.
   - If you are on a highway or busy road, exit the vehicle through the passenger side and move behind a guardrail or away from the roadway.
2. **Perform a visual check for a visible fragment only.**
   - Look directly into the keyway to see if the metal blade is flush with the opening or protruding.
   - **If the fragment is protruding by more than a quarter-inch**, you may attempt to pull it straight out with needle-nose pliers, but only if it slides without resistance.
3. **Check for steering wheel "binding" if the key is intact but bent.**
   - If an intact but slightly bent key will not turn, the steering column lock may be pressurized.
   - **Lightly jiggle the steering wheel left and right** while applying very gentle pressure to the key to see if the lock releases.
4. **Use the emergency start method for push-to-start vehicles.**
   - If the mechanical blade is broken but the fob is intact, check your owner's manual for the "backup start" location (often a slot in the console or a spot on the steering column).
   - **Hold the fob directly against the start button or designated reader** to see if the engine will crank without the mechanical blade.
5. **Contact a certified automotive locksmith or your roadside plan.**
   - Call your provider (e.g., AAA, Allstate, or manufacturer-specific assistance) and explicitly state: "I have a broken key fragment in the [ignition/door]."
   - **Request a mobile automotive locksmith** specifically, as standard tow drivers may not have extraction tools.
6. **Gather required ownership documentation for the technician.**
   - Locate your government-issued photo ID.
   - **Retrieve your vehicle registration or title** and note the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) from the dashboard or driver's door sticker.
   - Most professional services in the USA cannot cut or program a new key without verifying these three items.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace the entire ignition cylinder yet; most fragments can be extracted without replacing hardware.
- You do not need to decide between a dealership or a locksmith immediately; a mobile locksmith can usually handle extraction and a temporary key on-site.
- You do not need to worry about the "anti-theft" light flashing right now; this is a standard response to a key failure.

## Important reassurance
Broken keys are a common mechanical failure caused by metal fatigue and are not a sign of vehicle abuse. Most fragments can be removed by a professional in under 20 minutes using specialized extraction tools without requiring a tow or an expensive dashboard teardown.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a vehicle key failure. It does not cover long-term ignition repairs, electronic immobilizer programming, or complex anti-theft system resets which require specialized diagnostic equipment.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific instructions in your vehicle's owner's manual. Anti-theft protocols and ignition designs vary significantly by manufacturer. If your vehicle is in a location that poses an immediate threat to your life or others, call 911 for emergency assistance.
