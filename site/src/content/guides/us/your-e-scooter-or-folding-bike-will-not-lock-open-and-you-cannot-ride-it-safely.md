# What to do if… your e-scooter or folding bike will not lock open and you cannot ride it safely (USA)

## Short answer
Stop riding immediately and move off the road to a safe area. Do not attempt to ride the device even for a short distance, as a failing latch can cause a sudden, total collapse of the frame.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ride "just a little further" or try to hold the stem in place with your hands or weight.
- Do not use tape, zip ties, or improvised wedges to force the latch into a closed position.
- Do not attempt to tighten bolts or adjust the mechanism while you are still on the street or in a high-traffic area.
- Do not carry the device in a partially unfolded state where it can swing open and cause injury or damage.
- Do not ignore "minor" wobble or clicking sounds, as these are often precursors to a structural failure.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize immediate medical care if a fall occurred.** If you are experiencing severe pain, bleeding, or suspect a head injury, call **911** immediately.
2. **Move to a safe pause away from traffic.** Get onto the sidewalk or a shoulder where you can stand without blocking other transit users.
3. **Stabilize the device for manual transport.**
   - If the folding mechanism still works in reverse, fold the device completely and engage the carrying hook or strap.
   - If the latch is jammed open, walk the device slowly, keeping it upright and avoiding any bumps that could cause the frame to buckle.
4. **Perform a brief visual inspection for obvious obstructions.**
   - Look for **pebbles in the hinge**, **caught brake cables**, or **misaligned pins** that might prevent the latch from clicking.
   - If you see **cracked metal**, **elongated bolt holes**, or **bent frames**, stop touching the mechanism.
5. **End the trip immediately for shared/rental devices.**
   - Use the "Report Safety Issue" function in the app.
   - Provide the **Device ID** and exact location to ensure the operator can lock the device and prevent another user from riding it.
6. **Capture documentation for insurance or warranty claims.**
   - Take photos of the failed latch, any visible damage, and the serial number sticker (usually found on the underside of the deck or near the motor).
   - Record a short video of the latch failing to engage if it is safe to do so.
7. **Arrange alternative transportation.**
   - Use a ride-hail service, public transit, or call for a vehicle pickup.
   - If placing the device in a car trunk, secure it so it cannot spring open during transit.
8. **Preserve the device if an injury occurred.** Do not attempt repairs, do not return it to the manufacturer yet, and do not dispose of it until you have spoken with a professional, as it is evidence of the failure.
9. **Report the safety failure to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC).**
   - Visit **SaferProducts.gov** to file an incident report.
   - You may also call the CPSC Hotline at **800-638-2772** (Monday–Friday, 8:00 a.m. – 5:30 p.m. ET).
10. **Search the CPSC database for active recalls.** Look up your specific brand and model to see if a recall has been issued for the folding mechanism.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the specific mechanical cause (e.g., "metal fatigue" vs "improper tension") right now.
- You do not need to contact the manufacturer's support line until you are home and have your documentation ready.
- You do not need to decide on a replacement or refund tonight.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frustrated or inconvenienced when a primary mode of transport fails. Recognizing a mechanical risk and choosing to stop riding is the correct response to prevent a high-speed fall or collision.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a mechanical latch failure. It does not provide repair instructions or technical diagnostics. Professional inspection is required before the device is used again.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute mechanical or legal advice. Riding a micromobility device with a compromised folding mechanism poses a severe risk of injury. Always follow the specific safety protocols listed in your device's owner manual.
