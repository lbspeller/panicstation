# What to do if… your e-scooter or folding bike will not lock open and you cannot ride it safely (USA)

## Short answer
Stop riding now and treat the device as unsafe. Move away from traffic, keep the device under control, and use another way to get home or to a repair location.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ride “just a little farther” or try to hold the stem, hinge, or frame steady with your body; the device may suddenly collapse or steer unpredictably.
- Do not jam the latch with tape, zip ties, wire, string, or improvised wedges.
- Do not tighten bolts, clamps, or hinges by guessing.
- Do not carry it half-unfolded on stairs or into a vehicle while it can swing open.
- Do not keep using it if you see bending, cracking, missing parts, or a lock that reopens after you close it.

## What to do now
1. **Check for injury first.** If you fell and there is serious bleeding, severe pain, loss of consciousness, a possible head injury, trouble breathing, or another emergency, call **911**. If you are not sure whether an injury needs urgent care, contact a medical professional or local urgent care service.
2. **Create a safer pause.** Step off, move to the sidewalk, shoulder, or another place away from moving traffic, and hold the device so it cannot roll into the road.
3. **Make it safe to move without riding.**
   - If it folds fully and has a carry latch or strap, fold it fully and secure it before walking.
   - If it will not fold safely, keep it open, walk it slowly, and avoid curbs, potholes, stairs, or sharp turns that could twist the hinge or stem.
4. **Do a brief obvious-cause check only.**
   - Look for debris in the latch, a snagged brake cable, a misaligned hinge, or a stuck safety catch.
   - If the latch still does not click or hold clearly, stop troubleshooting and do not ride it.
5. **If it is a shared or rental scooter or bike, end the trip if the app allows it.** Note the device ID, exact location, and what failed, then report it as a safety issue in the app so it can be taken out of service.
6. **Record the problem before the device is changed.** Take photos or a short video of the latch, hinge, stem, or clamp failing; also capture the brand, model, serial number, and receipt or order confirmation if you have them.
7. **Get home without riding it.** Use transit, a ride-hail, a taxi, or a friend. If you bring it into a vehicle, keep it folded or otherwise secured so it cannot unfold into doors, legs, or luggage.
8. **Check for a recall.** Search the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission recall page for the brand and model. If you think the product is unsafe, you can report it through SaferProducts.gov online or by phone.
9. **Start the repair, return, or warranty route in writing.** Contact the seller or manufacturer and describe it as a locking or folding mechanism safety failure. Ask whether they want you to stop using it, take it to an authorized service location, return it, or wait for instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether this is wear and tear, misuse, or a product defect.
- You do not need to attempt a full repair while stressed or stranded.
- You do not need to negotiate a refund, replacement, credit, or complaint today.
- You do not need to keep testing the latch once it has failed to hold clearly.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel embarrassed, delayed, or tempted to push through. Getting off the device is the right first move when the folding lock, hinge, latch, clamp, or stem is not holding reliably.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stop riding, prevent a fall, document the failure, and use the proper repair, recall, return, or warranty route. Later decisions may need help from the manufacturer, seller, a qualified mechanic, a consumer protection office, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional mechanical, medical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Do not ride the device again unless the locking or folding mechanism engages reliably and any damage has been inspected and repaired or replaced.
