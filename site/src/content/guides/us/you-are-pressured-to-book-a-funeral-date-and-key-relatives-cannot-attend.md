# What to do if… you are pressured to book a funeral date and key relatives cannot attend (USA)

## Short answer
Ask the funeral provider to pause and send the available options and prices in writing. Before signing or paying, ask whether the time-sensitive decision is about disposition, such as burial or cremation, or about the service date.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t sign a contract or pay a deposit while the required decision, price, or timing remains unclear.
- Don’t accept a package without asking for itemized prices and what each charge covers.
- Don’t make an irreversible burial or cremation decision only because you feel rushed.
- Don’t assume the disposition and the larger family service must happen at the same time.
- Don’t let several relatives make separate changes with the provider while the family is still clarifying the plan.

## What to do now
1. **Create a pause.**  
   Say: “We are not choosing a final service date yet. Please tell us which decision is time-sensitive, why it is time-sensitive, and what options and prices are available.”

2. **Ask what must happen now and what can happen later.**  
   Ask whether the provider needs a decision about burial or cremation, a cemetery or crematory booking, transport, or only the date of the family service. Ask for any state or local requirement that affects timing to be explained in writing.

3. **Ask for clear prices before agreeing to anything.**  
   If you visit a funeral home in person, ask for the General Price List to keep. By phone, ask for accurate prices for the options you are considering and request a written quote.

4. **Ask about plans that allow relatives to attend later.**  
   Ask which of these are available locally:
   - holding the remains for a short period while the family decides, including any added charges
   - direct cremation followed by a memorial later
   - immediate burial followed by a memorial later
   - a small committal now and a larger family service later

5. **Use one family contact for calls.**  
   Ask the provider to note one person who should receive updates. Before anyone authorizes disposition or signs paperwork, ask who is legally allowed to make that decision under the applicable state rules and any existing arrangements.

6. **Step away if the provider will not answer clearly.**  
   Say: “We need to review the written prices and discuss the options. We will call back.” You can contact another funeral provider. If required information is refused or the provider appears deceptive, you can report the problem to the FTC and may also contact your state attorney general or the state funeral-industry licensing board.

7. **If transport is involved, ask about it separately.**  
   If the death happened away from home, ask which documents, charges, and timing constraints apply to the specific transport. Ask whether those affect the disposition timing, the service date, or both.

## What can wait
- Unless there is a specific deadline your family chooses to meet, details such as speakers, music, flowers, obituary wording, printed programs, reception plans, the guest list, and a livestream can usually wait until the immediate disposition questions are clear.
- You do not need to settle every family disagreement today.
- You do not need to decide the format of a later memorial before you have clear written options for the immediate arrangements.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to ask for a pause and written information. Separating the urgent decision from the later family service can make the next step clearer without dismissing anyone’s wish to attend.

## Scope note
These are first steps to slow the pressure and avoid rushed decisions. Later decisions involving finances, disputes, estate issues, religious requirements, or complicated circumstances may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Funeral and disposition rules vary by state and locality. Ask the provider to explain any claimed requirement in writing before you commit.
