# What to do if… your passport is damaged, hole-punched, or marked and you are worried it will be rejected (USA)

## Short answer
Treat the passport as possibly damaged, altered, or cancelled-looking, and move onto an official replacement or urgent passport route now. If you travel internationally very soon, use the passport agency or center appointment route; if you are outside the United States, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to repair it with tape, glue, lamination, trimming, erasing, cleaning products, or a cover-up mark.
- Do not write an explanation on the passport or add any extra notes to its pages.
- Do not assume an airline, cruise line, transit country, or border officer will accept it because the biographic details are still readable.
- Do not mail an application if departure is close without checking the official urgent travel options first.
- Do not rely on a third-party expeditor unless you have checked the official State Department information about what they can and cannot do.
- Do not throw the passport away; you may need to submit it or show it when applying for a replacement.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether the mark is in a damage category or makes the passport look altered.**
   Treat the passport as needing official help if it has a hole punch, unofficial markings on the data page, missing or torn pages, a significant tear, water damage, stains, or mold. A clipped or cut corner may also make the passport look altered or cancelled, so treat it as a travel risk even if you are not sure how it happened.

2. **Keep the passport exactly as it is and document the problem.**
   Take clear photos of the front cover, data page, and the hole, clip, cut, or mark. Save your travel itinerary, ticket, or visa appointment proof because the date of travel affects which official route is available.

3. **If you are in the United States and not travelling imminently, apply in person using the damaged-passport route.**
   Use Form DS-11. Bring or submit the damaged passport, a signed statement explaining how it was damaged or marked, a passport photo, evidence of U.S. citizenship or identity as required, and the required fees.

4. **If you are travelling internationally in less than 2–3 weeks, switch to the urgent travel route.**
   Make an appointment at a passport agency or center when you are within 14 calendar days of international travel, or within 28 days if you need a foreign visa. Appointments are not guaranteed, so check availability promptly and be flexible about location if you can.

5. **If you already applied and then discovered the travel risk, contact U.S. Passport Services.**
   Use the official contact route to ask what options are available for your timing. Have your application number if you have one, your travel date, and your damaged passport details ready.

6. **If you are outside the United States, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.**
   Explain that your passport may be damaged, altered, or treated as cancelled and that you may need onward travel. Ask what passport service or emergency document may be available for your itinerary before you go to the airport.

7. **Before you travel on any replacement or emergency document, check the itinerary.**
   Some countries and transit points may have specific passport validity, visa, or document rules. Check the destination and transit requirements before relying on a limited-validity or emergency passport.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the airline “should” accept the passport; assume there is a risk and start the official route.
- You do not need to settle who caused the hole, clip, or mark before applying.
- You do not need to pursue refunds, complaints, compensation, or blame right now.
- You do not need to perfectly label the problem as “damage,” “wear,” or “cancellation” before asking for official passport help.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful because the passport may still look readable, but airlines and border officials can treat physical changes as a problem. The safest immediate move is to stop handling the passport, avoid any repair attempt, and get onto the official replacement or urgent travel path.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise the travel risk, avoid making the document worse, and connect you to the correct official passport route. Later decisions about missed travel, complaints, insurance, or reimbursement may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, financial, or professional advice. Passport rules, appointment availability, airline decisions, and border requirements can change, so check the official links and the rules for your destination and transit points.
