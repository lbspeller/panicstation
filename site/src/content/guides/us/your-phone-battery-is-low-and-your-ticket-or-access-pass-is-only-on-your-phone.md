# What to do if… your phone battery is low and your ticket or access pass is only on your phone (USA)

## Short answer
Make the ticket visible or saved offline now, then go to the nearest official help point before your phone dies. Ask staff to verify, print, or reissue the ticket or pass while you can still show proof.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep refreshing the app, reloading the barcode, or switching between apps if the ticket is already visible.
- Do not wait until you are at the front of the boarding line, gate, turnstile, or inspection point.
- Do not assume cellular service or Wi-Fi will work on platforms, in terminals, at gates, underground, or inside large venues.
- Do not rely on a screenshot unless the provider or staff says that format is accepted.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to strangers or let anyone take it out of your sight.
- Do not close the ticket screen once the barcode, QR code, reservation number, or pass is visible.

## What to do now
1. **Preserve battery immediately.**
   - Turn on Low Power Mode or battery saver.
   - Lower screen brightness, but keep it bright enough for scanning.
   - Close maps, video, games, and other battery-heavy apps.
   - Keep Wi-Fi or cellular on until the ticket, PDF, wallet pass, or reservation details are saved or visible.

2. **Get the ticket into the safest form you can.**
   - Open the ticket and keep the barcode or QR code on screen.
   - If there is an option to add it to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet, use it.
   - If the ticket was emailed as a PDF or attachment, download it to your phone.
   - If you see a reservation number, confirmation code, order number, seat number, or passenger name, write it on paper or text it to someone you trust.
   - If the pass is already visible, do not close it to test other apps.

3. **Move to an official fallback before the checkpoint.**
   - At an airport, go to the airline kiosk, check-in counter, gate desk, or airline help desk.
   - At a train station, go to the ticket counter, staffed help point, conductor before boarding if allowed, or the operator’s service desk.
   - At a venue, hotel, workplace, school, event, or building, go to the box office, reception, security desk, or access-control desk.
   - Say: “My ticket or access pass is only on my phone and the battery is about to die. Can you verify it, print it, or reissue it before I reach the checkpoint?”

4. **For flights, use your booking details as a backup.**
   - Try a self-service kiosk if one is available.
   - Use your confirmation code, frequent flyer number, passport, or government ID if the kiosk or staff asks for it.
   - Ask staff whether they can print a boarding pass or issue another accepted format for your flight.
   - Keep the full barcode visible if you are using a mobile boarding pass at security or boarding.

5. **For Amtrak, use the eTicket backup routes if they apply.**
   - Look in your emailed receipt for the eTicket PDF and barcode.
   - Open the eTicket in the Amtrak app if you can.
   - If you are at a staffed Amtrak station, ask whether the ticket office can print or help retrieve your eTicket.
   - If there is an Amtrak kiosk, use it if your trip and station support that option.

6. **Get a small amount of power without losing the ticket screen.**
   - Plug into an outlet or charging point near the gate, platform, counter, café, or waiting area.
   - If you have a cable, ask an official counter, café, or vendor whether you can charge briefly.
   - If buying something is the fastest option, choose the correct charging cable or a small power bank.
   - Keep the ticket screen ready while charging if the checkpoint is close.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve refunds, changes, complaints, account recovery, or compensation right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to buy a new phone, replace the battery, or change apps today.
- You do not need a perfect backup system in this moment; focus on getting through the next checkpoint.

## Important reassurance
A low battery does not automatically mean you have lost the ticket or pass. Acting while the phone still turns on usually gives staff more options to verify your booking, print something, or tell you the accepted backup format.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the immediate risk of being stuck without access at boarding, inspection, entry, or check-in. Later decisions about rebooking, refunds, account recovery, complaints, or replacement documents may need provider-specific or specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, travel, or professional advice. Ticket and access rules vary by provider, venue, route, fare, ticket type, airport, station, and device, so follow the provider’s instructions and ask official staff as early as possible.
