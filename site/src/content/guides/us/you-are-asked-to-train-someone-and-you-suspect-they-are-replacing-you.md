# What to do if… you are asked to train someone and you suspect they are replacing you (USA)

## Short answer
Notify your IT department and payroll leadership immediately in writing to request priority account restoration or the activation of the organization's approved manual payroll contingency.

## Do not do these things
- Do not share MFA codes, passwords, or hardware tokens with others to attempt a login on your behalf.
- Do not attempt more than two password resets if the first fails; repeated attempts may trigger a security freeze or IP block.
- Do not export or move employee sensitive data (SSNs, bank details) to personal email or unencrypted storage to bypass the lockout.
- Do not attempt unauthorized "backdoor" entry into the database or server levels without IT supervision.
- Do not wait for the system to "fix itself" while a federal or state deadline is approaching.

## What to do now
1. **Document the specific error and deadline.** Record the exact time of the lockout, the system name (e.g., Workday, ADP, Paychex), and the specific error code or message displayed.
   - Note the exact hour of the internal cutoff and the external bank/tax deadline.
2. **Contact the IT help desk by phone for an urgent ticket.** State clearly that this is a "Production Down" or "Critical Business Impact" incident affecting a time-sensitive payroll run.
   - Provide the ticket number to your manager immediately after the call.
3. **Notify payroll and finance leadership in writing.** Inform them that system access is currently blocked and provide the estimated time remaining until the processing window closes.
4. **Trigger the emergency federal tax deposit path if needed.** If the lockout prevents an online payroll tax deposit, use the EFTPS voice response system (1-800-555-3453) to make the payment.
   - You will need the entity's EIN and the 4-digit EFTPS PIN.
   - Online logins for 2026 now require ID.me or Login.gov; if those fail, the phone system is the primary authorized backup.
5. **Request the "Break-Glass" or delegated access protocol.** Ask IT or the system owner if there is a pre-authorized secondary administrator or a "emergency role" that can be temporarily assigned to a colleague to meet the deadline.
6. **Activate the manual payroll contingency with written authorization.** If the processing window expires, ask leadership to authorize the organization's documented backup plan.
   - This may include issuing manual "off-cycle" checks or estimated "net pay" payments based on the prior period, as permitted by state law.
7. **Maintain a log of all restoration attempts.** Save emails and note the names of IT personnel or third-party support agents contacted, including the "estimated time to resolution" (ETR) provided by them.

## What can wait
- You do not need to investigate the cause of the lockout (e.g., whether it was a cyberattack or a glitch) during the deadline window.
- You do not need to file a formal internal grievance or performance explanation until after the immediate pay/tax obligations are met.
- You do not need to update your MFA settings or security questions until the primary access is restored.

## Important reassurance
System lockouts during high-pressure windows are a known operational risk for which most medium-to-large organizations have established contingencies. In 2026, the IRS provides automatic "First-Time Abatement" (FTA) for many qualifying employers who encounter an isolated failure-to-deposit due to technical issues, provided the error is corrected promptly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for administrators or employees facing a system-access crisis before a deadline. It does not cover long-term IT security audits or legal disputes regarding missed wages.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal, financial, or tax advice. Federal and state laws require that employees be paid on their regular payday; system failures do not generally exempt employers from these requirements. For 2026-specific tax compliance, consult the IRS or a certified payroll professional.
