# What to do if… you notice your door peephole or door viewer looks covered or tampered with (USA)

## Short answer
Treat a blocked or altered viewer as a safety hazard: **do not open the door**, move to a position where you are not visible through the door, and **verify the area using an alternative method** such as a window or camera.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to investigate the obstruction or check the hallway.
- Do not put your eye directly to the peephole if it appears tampered with or if light is leaking through in an unusual way.
- Do not stand directly in front of the door while assessing the situation.
- Do not remove the obstruction with your bare hands, as this can destroy forensic evidence like fingerprints.
- Do not announce through the door that you are alone or that you have called the police.
- Do not exit the property to "catch" someone or inspect the exterior yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the door and relocate.** Immediately engage the deadbolt and any secondary security latches. Move to a position away from the door's line of sight, such as a side wall or a different room.
2. **Listen for environmental cues.** Stay quiet and listen for sounds in the immediate vicinity outside the door.
   - Note footsteps, whispers, or the sound of tools.
   - Listen for the testing of the door handle or clicking of the lock.
3. **Check the internal assembly.** Without placing your face against the viewer, check if the internal part of the peephole appears loose, unscrewed, or crooked. 
   - A loose internal housing can indicate someone attempted to remove the viewer or use a "reverse peephole" tool.
4. **Use alternative viewing methods.** Check the hallway or porch without opening the door.
   - Use a smart doorbell or security camera feed on your phone.
   - Look through a window that does not require you to stand near the door.
   - If in an apartment or hotel, call the front desk or building security and ask them to check the hallway via CCTV or a physical walk-through.
5. **Call 911 for active threats.** If you hear someone attempting to enter, see a person through a camera who is actively tampering with your door, or feel you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services.
   - State that you are inside your home and that your door's security viewer has been tampered with.
   - Report any specific sounds or sightings of individuals.
6. **Report suspicious activity.** If there is no immediate threat but the viewer has clearly been altered (e.g., tape, paint, or stickers), contact your local police non-emergency line to file a suspicious activity report.
7. **Document the tampering.** Take a clear photograph or video of the viewer from the inside. 
   - If you must remove the obstruction for safety, use a tool or wear a glove to preserve fingerprints on the material. 
   - Place any removed items (like tape or stickers) into a clean plastic bag.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the motive (vandalism versus attempted entry) right now.
- You do not need to repair or replace the viewer immediately if the door is otherwise secure.
- You do not need to notify neighbors or post to social media groups until you have secured your own safety.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of intrusion or fear when your home's security is compromised. Taking a cautious, "no-contact" approach by refusing to open the door is a standard and effective safety protocol. Hesitation in this moment is a protective response that allows you to gather more information safely.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for an immediate discovery of door viewer tampering. It does not replace professional security advice or official police procedures for long-term harassment or stalking.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. If you believe a crime is in progress or your safety is at risk, contact 911 immediately.
