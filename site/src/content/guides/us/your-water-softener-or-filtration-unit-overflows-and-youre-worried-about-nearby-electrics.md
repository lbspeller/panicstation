# What to do if… your water softener or filtration unit overflows and you’re worried about nearby electrics (USA)

## Short answer
Keep all people and pets away from standing water and **only attempt to shut off the main electrical breaker if you can reach it from a completely dry, safe position**. If the floor is wet near the panel or the panel itself is damp, do not touch it; contact your local electric utility immediately to request an emergency service disconnection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into standing water to reach any electrical components, including outlets, power strips, or the softener unit itself.
- Do not touch the main breaker panel or any switches if your hands are wet or if you are standing on a damp surface.
- Do not attempt to "test" if the water is energized by touching it or using a tool.
- Do not reset any tripped circuit breakers or GFCI outlets until a professional has confirmed the wiring is dry and safe.
- Do not use a wet / dry vacuum to clean up the spill while the power to that area is still active.
- Do not ignore signs of electrical distress such as buzzing, crackling, or the smell of ozone or burning plastic.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the area of all people and pets immediately.** Ensure no one enters the wet zone or touches any metal objects (like pipes or the softener tank) that may be in contact with the water.
2. **Call 911 if you observe active fire, smoke, or if someone has received an electric shock.** Do not attempt to touch a person who is still in contact with a live electrical source; wait for emergency responders to confirm the power is cut.
3. **Assess your ability to reach the main electrical panel from a dry spot.** **If the floor leading to and underneath the panel is completely dry, and the panel exterior is not damp, switch the main breaker to the OFF position.**
4. **Contact your electric utility for an emergency service disconnection if the panel area is wet.** **Inform them that internal flooding has reached or is approaching electrical equipment and you cannot safely access the main shut-off.**
5. **Shut off the water supply once the area is confirmed to be electrically safe.** **Close the dedicated shut-off valves on the softener / filtration unit or turn the main water valve for the entire house clockwise until it stops.**
   - If the unit has a bypass valve, you may engage it only if you can do so without stepping into water or touching energized components.
6. **Alert your utility company if you have solar panels or a backup battery system.** **Be aware that these systems may keep certain circuits "live" even after the main utility power is disconnected; do not touch any components until an electrician verifies the system is de-energized.**
7. **Document the scene only after all hazards are neutralized.** **Take clear photographs of the water level, the source of the leak, and any affected electrical outlets or appliances for insurance and repair purposes.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the mechanical cause of the overflow (such as a stuck float or clogged injector) right now.
- You do not need to begin the drying or sanitation process until the power is verified off.
- You do not need to decide on permanent repairs or replacements for the water system today.
- You do not need to contact your insurance agent in the first minutes of the crisis.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency when water and electricity are both involved, but the safest action is often to stop and wait for professional help. Choosing not to touch a panel or appliance when you are unsure of its safety is a correct and protective decision.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. It focuses on reducing the immediate risk of electrocution and preventing further water damage. It does not provide instructions for electrical repairs or plumbing diagnostics.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the expertise of a licensed electrician or plumber. Brine (salt water) from softeners is highly conductive; treat any water involved with these units as a high-risk electrical hazard.
