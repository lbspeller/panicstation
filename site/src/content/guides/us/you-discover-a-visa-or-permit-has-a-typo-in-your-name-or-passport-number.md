# What to do if… you discover a visa or permit has a typo in your name or passport number (USA)

## Short answer
Pause before relying on the document. Check whether the error is on a visa, your I-94 admission record, or a USCIS-issued document, then contact the official channel responsible for that document and follow its instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a mismatch is harmless or rely on the document for travel before checking the official correction route.
- Do not alter, remove, cover, or rewrite any part of the visa or document yourself.
- Do not send your passport or an original document until an official channel tells you where and how to send it.
- Do not pay an unofficial person who promises a faster correction.
- Do not contact every agency at once unless more than one document is wrong or an official channel directs you elsewhere.

## What to do now
1. **Save clear copies of the documents.**
   - Photograph the page showing the visa or document error.
   - Photograph the passport biographical page showing the correct name and passport number.
   - If you have already entered the United States, try to retrieve your most recent I-94 online and save a copy.

2. **Identify which document contains the error.**
   - If the printed visa in your passport is wrong, contact the U.S. embassy or consulate that issued the visa.
   - If your I-94 admission record is wrong because of an error made when you entered the United States, contact the nearest CBP Deferred Inspection Site.
   - If a USCIS-issued document contains incorrect information because USCIS made a typographical error, use the USCIS typographic-error service request.

3. **For an incorrect visa, contact the issuing embassy or consulate.**
   - Use the official website for the embassy or consulate that issued the visa.
   - State that the printed visa information does not match your passport.
   - Ask what correction steps are required before you rely on the visa for travel.
   - Keep a copy of the reply.

4. **For an incorrect I-94, check the record and contact CBP when appropriate.**
   - Use the official I-94 website to try to retrieve your most recent record.
   - Compare the displayed name, passport details, admission class, and admitted-until information with your documents.
   - If the error was made at entry, use the official CBP Deferred Inspection Site list to contact the nearest site.
   - Save copies of your request and any reply.

5. **For a USCIS-issued document with a USCIS typo, use the USCIS service request.**
   - Submit the official typographic-error request.
   - Describe the incorrect information and the correct information clearly.
   - Follow any instructions USCIS sends about evidence or returning the document.
   - Save the confirmation.

6. **Follow the official instructions before relying on the document.**
   - Keep your passport and original documents secure unless the responsible official channel tells you to submit them.
   - Keep written copies of the instructions you receive.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out your whole immigration or travel plan today.
- You do not need to submit a new visa application unless the issuing embassy or consulate tells you to do so.
- You do not need to contact every agency immediately. Start with the official channel responsible for the document that contains the error.

## Important reassurance
A typo or mismatched number does not mean you need to solve everything at once. The first task is to identify the document with the error and start the official correction route.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The exact route may depend on the document, how the error happened, and the instructions from the responsible official channel. Later decisions may need help from an immigration lawyer or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, or professional advice. Official procedures and processing times may vary. Use the responsible official channel for instructions about your specific document.
