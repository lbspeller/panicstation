# What to do if… you discover a visa or permit has a typo in your name or passport number (USA)

## Short answer
Do not attempt to travel on a document with incorrect biographical data; contact the issuing U.S. embassy for visa foil misprints or use the CBP Traveler Communication Center for I-94 entry record corrections.

## Do not do these things
- Do not board an international flight assuming minor typos will be ignored, as airlines are liable for transporting passengers with non-matching documents.
- Do not attempt to physically alter the document or use correction fluid/stickers on the visa foil or permit.
- Do not pay "expediting fees" to third-party services; official government corrections for administrative errors are generally processed without new fees if reported promptly.
- Do not assume an incorrect I-94 will "fix itself" upon your next exit; an uncorrected record can lead to overstay accusations or future visa denials.
- Do not delay reporting the error, as many embassies only offer free corrections within one year of the visa issuance date.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific agency responsible for the error to ensure your request reaches the right desk.**
   - If the error is on a visa foil (the sticker in your passport), the issuing **U.S. Embassy or Consulate** is the responsible agency.
   - If the error is on your I-94 (electronic arrival record), **CBP** (Customs and Border Protection) must handle the correction.
   - If the error is on an Employment Authorization Document (EAD) or Green Card, **USCIS** (U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services) is the responsible agency.
2. **Create a digital evidence folder containing clear scans of the mismatch.**
   - Include a high-resolution photo of the incorrect document and your passport biographical page.
   - Save a copy of the original application (e.g., DS-160 or I-765) to prove the error was made by the government and not the applicant.
3. **Submit a "Misprint" request to the U.S. Embassy if the visa foil itself is wrong.**
   - Access the specific "Visa Misprint" or "Contact Us" form on the website of the embassy that issued the visa.
   - State clearly that the request is for a data correction (misprint) to avoid being queued for a standard visa interview.
   - Follow the instructions for returning the passport; many posts allow for a drop-box submission or specific courier service for corrections.
4. **Correct an I-94 admission record through the CBP Traveler Communication Center (TCC) or a Deferred Inspection Site.**
   - Visit the official I-94 website and use the "Ask a Question" or "TCC" portal to report biographical errors.
   - If you are currently at an airport or near a major port, you may be directed to visit a **CBP Deferred Inspection Site** in person.
   - Bring your passport, the boarding pass from your arrival, and any supporting documents showing the correct spelling or number.
5. **Submit an e-Request for "Typographic Error" if the mistake is on a USCIS-issued card.**
   - Use the USCIS online e-Request tool specifically for "Typographic Error" to log the issue.
   - If a physical card must be replaced due to USCIS error, follow the provided instructions to mail the incorrect card to the **Lee's Summit Production Facility**.
   - Keep the mailing tracking number and a copy of the e-Request confirmation for your records.

## What can wait
- You do not need to restart the entire visa application process or pay a new MRV fee if the error was clearly an administrative misprint by the embassy.
- You do not need to cancel your entire trip immediately if your flight is more than two weeks away; most corrections are processed with higher priority than new applications.
- You do not need to contact your local police or home country's embassy, as they have no jurisdiction over U.S. immigration document data.

## Important reassurance
Clerical errors on immigration documents are common and do not invalidate your underlying status. As long as you have identified the error and initiated a correction through official channels, you are following the required protocol to maintain compliance.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for biographical data typos (name, passport number, date of birth) on U.S. visas and entry permits only. It does not address legal name changes or corrections for errors made by the applicant on initial forms.

## Important note
Correction processes vary significantly by embassy and port of entry. If you have an immediate filing deadline with USCIS (such as an H-1B petition), notify your employer or qualified legal representative immediately, as an incorrect I-94 can impact the validity of your filing.
