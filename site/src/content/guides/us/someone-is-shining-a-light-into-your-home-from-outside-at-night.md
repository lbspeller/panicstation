# What to do if… someone is shining a light into your home from outside at night (USA)
## Short answer
Move away from windows and glass doors, secure any entry points you can reach safely, and call 911 if you believe someone may be trying to enter, you feel in immediate danger, or you need immediate police assistance. A light outside can have more than one explanation, so focus on safety rather than trying to identify the person yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside or open the door to investigate or confront anyone.
- Do not stand in front of a window or pull curtains open to get a better look.
- Do not chase, follow, or try to detain anyone.
- Do not post live details publicly while the situation is active.
- Do not move toward an exposed window to lock it if that would put you in view or make you feel less safe.
- Do not go outside to check for damage or collect dropped items while you are unsure whether the area is safe.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from windows and glass doors.** Go to a more interior part of the home if you can do so safely, and stay out of sight.
2. **Secure reachable entry points.** Lock exterior doors and any accessible windows you can reach without moving into an exposed area. Close blinds or curtains only where you can do that safely.
3. **Keep essentials with you.** Take your phone and keys with you as you move. If you have an alarm system, arm it. Bring other people in the home to a safer interior area if you can do so safely.
4. **Check cameras only from a safe place.** If you have a doorbell camera or security camera, you can check the live view or recent clips from inside. Do not delay calling 911 to review footage.
5. **Call for help when needed.**
   - **Call 911** if you believe someone may be trying doors or windows, you hear tampering, you feel in immediate danger, or you need immediate police assistance.
   - If the situation does not seem immediate but you want to report suspicious activity or a possible non-emergency crime, use the official website for your local law-enforcement agency to find its non-emergency number. Do not call 911 only to obtain a non-emergency number.
6. **Give clear details if you call.** Be ready to provide your address, apartment or room number if relevant, your callback number, what is happening, where the light appears to be coming from, and any description you can give safely. Mention any movement near doors or windows, direction of travel, vehicle details, or camera footage. Answer the call-taker’s questions, follow any instructions, and do not hang up until told to do so.
7. **Use building support from a safe place if relevant.** If you live in an apartment or managed building, contact building security or management after any needed emergency call. Avoid hallways, entrances, and parking areas while you are unsure whether they are safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person tonight or decide whether the light was targeted.
- You do not need to go outside immediately to inspect for damage.
- You do not need to decide now whether this was a nuisance, a mistake, or something more concerning.
- If a non-emergency lighting problem continues, you can later check local-government information or ask building management or your landlord about the appropriate local route.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to feel unsettled by a light shining into your home at night. Moving away from windows, securing reachable entry points, and asking for help when needed are sensible first steps. You do not need to solve the situation while it is happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. If the behavior repeats, later follow-up may include contacting local law enforcement, building management, your landlord, or an appropriate local-government service. Those decisions can wait until the immediate situation has stabilized.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or need immediate police assistance, call 911.
