# What to do if… you cannot check into your accommodation because you do not have your ID with you (USA)

## Short answer
Move to the safest staffed place available, such as the property lobby or another nearby staffed location. Ask the property what ID alternative, if any, they can accept while you contact the booking provider through its official support channel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave your passport or ID as collateral.
- Do not let someone take your ID away without understanding why they need it and when it will be returned.
- Do not send full card details or sensitive documents to an unknown number. Use the booking platform’s official messaging or the property’s published contact details.
- Do not cancel the booking before saving the reservation details and contacting official support.
- Do not follow a stranger to another location to fix the problem, especially at night.
- Do not argue with staff. Ask for a manager or the booking provider’s support team instead.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Stay in the lobby or front-desk area if staff allow it. If you cannot wait there, move to another nearby staffed place while you contact support.
2. **Ask what the property can accept.** Ask: “My ID is temporarily inaccessible. Is there any alternative you are allowed to accept tonight?” Ask for a manager if the first staff member cannot decide.
3. **Gather what you can access.** Pull up your reservation number, booking email, guest name, and any other ID or digital copy you can reach. Show only what the property or official support team reasonably needs. Do not send full card details in messages.
4. **If the ID is inside the room or building, say so clearly.** Ask whether a manager can help you retrieve it or use another process to verify that you are the booked guest.
5. **Contact the booking provider through its official app or website if you used one.** Ask the support team to confirm the booking with the property and tell you what alternatives are available, such as another property or a refund review.
6. **If the passport or ID is lost or stolen rather than temporarily inaccessible, separate that problem from tonight’s shelter problem.** For a lost or stolen valid U.S. passport abroad, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate about a replacement. A police report may help document what happened, but the U.S. Department of State says it is not mandatory for replacement abroad.
7. **Before paying for another place tonight, call it first.** Ask whether its staff can accept the ID or other proof you currently have. Prefer a staffed front desk.
8. **Save a basic record.** Keep the booking confirmation, support messages, receipts, and the time of any refusal.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to make a complaint or post a review.
- You do not need to resolve every refund, insurance, or replacement-document question immediately.
- You do not need to reorganize the rest of the trip while you are still trying to find a safe place for tonight.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but you only need to handle the next safe step. Staff may have limited discretion, and a solution may take more than one call, but preserving the booking record and using official support channels gives you a clearer path forward.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about refunds, insurance, replacement documents, or a complaint may need help from the booking provider, the property, a consular service, or another relevant specialist.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent first actions, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. ID requirements and available alternatives vary by property and location, and staff may have limited discretion.
