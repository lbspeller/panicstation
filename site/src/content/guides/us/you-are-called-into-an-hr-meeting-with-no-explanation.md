# What to do if… you are called into an HR meeting with no explanation (USA)

## Short answer
Request the full Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) document in writing and clarify the specific metrics for success before signing or agreeing to terms. Establish a contemporaneous written record of all subsequent interactions with management and HR to ensure expectations are documented and measurable.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign immediately — quitting voluntarily typically disqualifies you from unemployment benefits and may waive legal claims or severance leverage.
- Do not sign the document during the initial meeting if you are confused or disagree with the facts — ask for 24 to 48 hours to review the contents.
- Do not engage in verbal arguments or emotional outbursts; focus entirely on the written metrics and the company's stated policy.
- Do not remove proprietary, confidential, or customer data from company systems for "evidence" — this is often a fireable offense and a violation of most employment contracts.
- Do not record meetings secretly unless you have verified you are in a "One-Party Consent" state (federal law differs from state laws in CA, FL, IL, MA, MI, MT, NH, PA, and WA, where recording without all parties' consent is illegal).
- Do not stop performing your job duties; continue to meet all existing deadlines while the PIP is under review.

## What to do now
1. **Request a complete copy of the PIP document and the company’s formal performance management policy.** **Ensure the document includes specific, measurable goals, the timeline for the review period (e.g., 30, 60, or 90 days), and the frequency of check-in meetings.**
2. **Use a standard pause script if you are in a meeting right now.** **You may state: "I need time to process this information and review the document carefully; I will provide my initial questions or response in writing by [Date]."**
3. **Verify your employment classification if you are a federal employee.** **Under March 2026 OPM rules, certain "Schedule Policy/Career" positions have different protections than the general GS workforce, and some PIP durations may be capped at 30 business days.**
4. **Identify the exact metrics used to define "improvement."** **Ask your manager to provide the specific baseline data (e.g., past sales numbers, error rates, or project deadlines) that the PIP claims were missed, and request concrete examples of what "successful" performance looks like for each goal.**
5. **Request a reasonable accommodation if a medical condition or disability is impacting your performance.** **Submit a written request to HR stating that you have a medical condition and need a specific adjustment (e.g., modified schedule, specialized software, or quiet workspace) to perform your essential job functions under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).**
6. **Determine your eligibility for the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) if you require time off for health reasons.** **Check if your employer has at least 50 employees within 75 miles and if you have worked at least 1,250 hours in the past 12 months before requesting leave for a serious health condition.**
7. **Document every interaction following the PIP meeting in a factual "memo to file" or follow-up email.** **Send a brief summary after each check-in meeting to your manager and HR: "Per our meeting today, we discussed [Topic X], and my understanding is that the next milestone is [Date/Action]."**
8. **File a formal internal complaint if you believe the PIP is retaliatory or discriminatory.** **Use the company’s specific reporting procedures to document concerns that the plan is based on protected characteristics (race, sex, age, religion, etc.) rather than job performance.**
9. **Check your union contract or employment agreement for "just cause" or "progressive discipline" requirements.** **If you are represented by a union, contact your shop steward immediately to ensure the company is following the bargained-for disciplinary process.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to stay with the company or start a lawsuit today.
- You do not need to write a point-by-point rebuttal to every performance criticism tonight; focus on gathering the foundational documents first.
- You do not need to hire an attorney immediately, though you should save all relevant performance reviews and emails to a personal device (that does not contain company-confidential data).
- You do not need to admit fault or agree that the criticisms are accurate during the initial notification meeting.

## Important reassurance
Receiving an unexpected PIP is a common procedural step in corporate environments and does not mean your termination is immediate or inevitable. Many employees use this period to clarify vague expectations, secure necessary work tools, or negotiate a structured exit. Your reaction of shock or confusion is a standard response to a sudden change in job security.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the first 48 hours after receiving a PIP. It does not cover the long-term negotiation of severance packages, filing EEOC charges, or responding to specific performance failures.

## Important note
This information is for general educational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Employment laws, including at-will rules and recording consent, vary significantly by state. If you suspect your PIP is a precursor to an illegal termination, consult with a qualified employment attorney in your jurisdiction to discuss your specific facts and deadlines.
