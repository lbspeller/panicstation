# What to do if… your ride-hailing account is suddenly suspended when you need to book urgent transport (USA)

## Short answer
Secure an immediate alternative transport method (taxi, transit, or a trusted contact) to reach your destination safely before attempting to appeal the account suspension.

## Do not do these things
- Do not create multiple new accounts using different emails or phone numbers, as this often triggers permanent fraud blocks.
- Do not make repeated attempts to add, remove, or edit payment cards while the account is suspended.
- Do not accept "off-app" rides from unidentified drivers who approach you in person.
- Do not share login passcodes or full credit card numbers with social media accounts claiming to "unban" users.
- Do not attempt to use Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT) for life-threatening situations.

## What to do now
1. **Identify if the situation is a safety or medical emergency.**
   - If you are in immediate danger or experiencing a medical crisis, **call 911**.
   - If you are stranded in an unsafe area, move to a **well-lit, staffed location** (such as a hotel lobby, open business, or police station) immediately.
2. **Select the fastest alternative transport option.**
   - Call a **local taxi service** directly; most cities have dispatch numbers that do not require an app.
   - Use a **different ride-hailing platform** (e.g., if Uber is blocked, try Lyft or a local equivalent) if you have an active secondary account.
   - Contact a **trusted friend or family member** to pick you up or to remotely book a taxi/ride for you.
   - Check for **public transit** options if you are near a station and it is a safe time of day to travel.
3. **Contact 211 for local transportation assistance.**
   - **Dial 211** to speak with a community resource specialist who can identify local programs, such as "Ride United" or local municipal "Guaranteed Ride Home" services.
4. **Distinguish between a payment hold and a permanent deactivation.**
   - Open the app and check for a **specific error message** or email notification.
   - If the issue is a **temporary payment hold**, you may be able to resolve it by selecting a different, existing payment method already in your wallet.
   - If the message says "Account Deactivated" or "Suspended for Safety," stop all booking attempts and move to the appeal process later.
5. **Document the suspension for a future appeal.**
   - Take a **screenshot** of the deactivation notice and any error codes shown.
   - Note the **exact time** and your **location** when the lockout occurred.
6. **Initiate the official appeal process through the provider's help center.**
   - Use the **in-app "Help" or "Support"** section to find the "Account Deactivated" form.
   - If locked out of the app, visit the **official website** (help.uber.com or help.lyft.com) to submit a request.
   - State clearly: "My account was suspended while I was attempting to book urgent transport. Please provide the steps for verification."

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the account was flagged right now.
- You do not need to provide a detailed personal history or "good character" references in your initial support ticket.
- You do not need to contact your bank or credit card issuer until you are at your destination.
- You do not need to decide which ride-hailing app to use permanently.

## Important reassurance
Account suspensions are frequently triggered by automated security algorithms due to factors like traveling in a new city, using a new device, or a temporary bank mismatch. These flags are designed to protect your financial data and do not necessarily imply a violation of terms or permanent loss of service.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for users who are stranded or need urgent transport during an account lockout. It does not cover the long-term legal or technical processes required for account reinstatement.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Transportation availability and 211 services vary by location. Residents in California may have specific rights regarding deactivation notices under state-specific gig worker protections. Always prioritize your immediate physical safety when stranded.
