# What to do if… you believe you were drugged and something sexual happened while you were impaired (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safer place and seek urgent medical care. Tell the clinician that you think you may have been drugged and that something sexual may have happened while you were impaired.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay with, meet, or confront anyone you do not feel safe around.
- Do not drive if you feel sedated, confused, dizzy, or unsteady.
- Do not pressure yourself to reconstruct everything right now.
- Do not let anyone pressure you to make a police report before you have had a chance to ask about your options. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance help, call 911.
- Do not delay urgent medical care to preserve possible evidence.
- Do not blame yourself because alcohol, drugs, or memory gaps may have been involved.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.** Go to a trusted person, a public staffed location, or another place where you feel safer. If you need immediate police, fire, or ambulance help, call **911**.
2. **Seek urgent medical care.** Go to an emergency department or another local clinic or hospital that can assess you. Ask someone else to drive you or call **911** if you are too unwell to travel safely.
3. **Mention the suspected drugging promptly.** Tell the clinician that you may have been drugged and ask whether toxicology samples may be appropriate. Collection can be time-sensitive, and testing may not identify what happened.
4. **Ask about sexual assault medical care if you want that option.** Ask whether a **SAFE** is available and whether a **SANE** or other qualified examiner can provide it. You can ask questions, pause, stop, or skip parts of an exam. You can still seek medical care if you do not want a forensic exam.
5. **Contact RAINN for support or local referrals if that feels useful.** You can ask for help before deciding whether you want to make a police report.
6. **Ask a trusted person to stay with you if possible.** They can help with transportation, wait with you, and help you take in information.
7. **If you may want a SAFE and can do so without delaying care,** avoid showering, bathing, or changing clothes until you have spoken with a clinician; if you have already done any of these things, still seek care.

## What can wait
- You usually do not need to decide immediately whether you want to make a police report. Ask a clinician or advocate whether any reporting rules apply where you are.
- You do not need to work out a complete timeline before getting care.
- You do not need to contact the venue, anyone you suspect, or mutual friends today.
- You do not need to make major decisions about relationships, school, work, or housing right now.

## Important reassurance
Memory gaps and confusion can make this feel especially difficult. Not having a clear timeline does not mean you did anything wrong, and you are allowed to seek care and support without having all the answers.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on reaching a safer pause, getting medical care, and keeping options open. Later decisions may need support from a medical professional, advocate, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or seriously unwell, call 911 or seek emergency medical care.
