# What to do if… you are pressured to attend a private work meeting and feel unsafe (USA)
## Short answer
If you feel unsafe, do not go to an isolated location alone. Move toward a safer pause near other people and ask for a visible meeting location, another person present, or a remote option.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow someone into an isolated place, such as a locked office, stairwell, parking lot, car, or offsite location, if you feel unsafe.
- Do not let pressure to meet immediately override your safety. Move near other people first.
- Do not sign documents, agree to disciplinary terms, or quit on the spot while you feel pressured or scared.
- Do not delete texts, messages, emails, or calendar invitations connected to the request.
- Do not rely on secret recording as your main safety plan. Before recording, check the applicable law and workplace policy.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If this is happening now, move to a staffed or visible area, such as reception, an open office, or a break area. Use a neutral delay if needed: “Give me a moment to get my notebook.”
2. **Ask for a safer arrangement.** Say: “I am not comfortable meeting in a private location. Please move the meeting to a visible room, have another person attend, or offer a video call.”
3. **Put your request in writing.** Reply to the calendar invitation or message: “I can meet at 2 p.m. in a visible meeting room, with another person present, or by video call. I am not able to meet in the private location.”
4. **Ask for the topic in writing.** Send: “Please confirm the purpose of the meeting and whether you expect to ask questions about possible discipline.”
5. **Ask whether another person can attend.** Request HR, another manager, or a support person. If the request is refused, repeat the safer-location or remote options and ask to respond in writing where practical.
6. **Use a representation request if you are represented by a union and the meeting may qualify.** If management seeks to question you and you reasonably believe your answers could lead to discipline, say: “I believe this interview could lead to discipline, and I request my union representative.” In a qualifying interview, the employer may delay the interview until a representative is available, end the interview, or ask whether you want to continue without representation or end the interview. If questioning continues after your request is refused, say that your request remains in place and ask to end the interview.
7. **Use an internal reporting route if you feel targeted or unsafe.** Send a brief message to HR or the designated reporting channel: “I am being pressured into a private one-to-one meeting and I do not feel safe. I am requesting a visible location, another person present, or a remote option.”
8. **Make a factual record.** Note the date, time, people involved, requested location, words used, and any witnesses. Preserve relevant messages where permitted. Do not move confidential workplace material contrary to applicable policy or law.
9. **Leave and get help if you feel in immediate danger.** Move to a public or staffed area, contact building security if available, and leave the workplace if needed. Dial **911** if you need emergency assistance.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide now whether to make a formal internal complaint, contact an agency, or seek legal advice.
- You do not need to write a long statement now. Preserve the basic facts and messages first.
- You do not have to confront the person or explain everything. A brief statement that you are not comfortable meeting privately is enough for now.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to ask for safer meeting conditions. Choosing a visible location, asking for another person to attend, or requesting a remote option can help create a safer pause while you decide what to do next.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions may need support from a union representative, an appropriate workplace contact, or a qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. Workplace policies and legal rights can vary. If you need emergency assistance, prioritize reaching a safer place and dialing 911.
