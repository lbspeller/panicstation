# What to do if… a partner keeps waking you up for sex and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Move to the safest available pause for tonight if you can do so without increasing danger, and keep a phone with you if safely possible. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel you have to argue, explain, or prove anything in the moment.
- Do not start a major relationship discussion while you feel unsafe.
- Do not resist over a phone, keys, or other items if doing so could put you in more danger.
- Do not make noticeable changes to a phone, account, or browsing pattern if that could increase risk.
- Do not take extra alcohol or non-prescribed sedating substances to get through the night. Do not change prescribed medication without medical advice.
- Do not feel you must decide tonight whether to report or make a relationship decision. If you are considering medical care or an exam, ask a provider or RAINN about timing.

## What to do now
1. **Reach the safest available pause.** If it feels safer, move away from the bedroom or go to a trusted safe place. If moving could increase danger, stay where you can most safely call or message for help.
2. **Call 911 if you feel in immediate danger.** This includes feeling that your partner may force sexual contact, hurt you, or stop you from leaving.
3. **Keep essentials close if safely possible.** Keep your phone, keys, essential medication, and identification together. Do not go back for items if doing so could put you at risk.
4. **Contact one trusted person or a specialist service.** Tell them you need help deciding where you can safely rest tonight. Ask a trusted person to stay on the phone, come to you if that is safe, or contact 911 if you ask them to.
5. **Use a safer device if you think your phone or accounts may be monitored.** A trusted person’s phone may be safer. Phone and internet use can sometimes be monitored, and activity cannot always be completely erased.
6. **Contact RAINN for sexual violence support.** RAINN can connect you with trained support and local resources. You can ask about privacy limits before sharing your name, location, or other identifying details.
7. **Ask for medical care if you need it.** Seek urgent medical care for injuries, bleeding, severe pain, or if you feel unwell. Call 911 for a medical emergency. You can ask a hospital, a local provider, or RAINN about a sexual assault forensic exam and ask what options and privacy limits apply where you are.
8. **Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline for safety planning.** A live advocate can help you think through a safer place to rest, what you need tonight, and what may reduce risk while you share a home.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight what label fits the situation.
- You do not need to confront your partner, negotiate boundaries, report, or make a relationship decision tonight.
- You do not need to make a perfect plan. The priority is the safest available pause, support, and rest.
- Medical care or a sexual assault forensic exam may be time-sensitive. A provider or RAINN can explain options without pressuring you to report.

## Important reassurance
Unwanted sexual pressure is not your fault. Some people freeze, comply, or go quiet when they are trying to stay safe.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need support from a local sexual assault service, domestic violence advocate, healthcare professional, or another specialist you choose.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911.
