# What to do if… you feel targeted in a busy area because someone keeps shadowing your movements between shops or aisles (USA)

## Short answer
Stop moving through open aisles and go immediately to a staffed location such as a checkout counter, customer service desk, or security station. Tell staff clearly that you believe you are being followed and need to stay in a safe, monitored area.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to lose the person by entering isolated areas like stairwells, parking garages, or empty corridors.
- Do not head to your vehicle or home while you believe the person is still tracking your movements.
- Do not engage in a direct confrontation or make verbal threats that could escalate the situation.
- Do not use your phone in a way that blocks your peripheral vision or distracts you from your surroundings.
- Do not hide in a restroom unless it is a high-traffic, multi-stall facility, and only if you have already alerted staff.
- Do not pretend nothing is happening to avoid "making a scene" if you feel a genuine threat to your safety.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a staffed "anchor" point.** Identify the nearest pharmacy counter, customer service desk, or checkout lane that is currently occupied by employees. 
   - Stay in the direct line of sight of staff and security cameras.
2. **State your concern to an employee using clear, direct language.** Say: "I am being shadowed by someone in the store and I do not feel safe; please keep me here and call security." 
   - If you are with a child or dependent, mention this immediately to prioritize their protection.
3. **Request that security meets you at your current location.** Ask the staff member to stay with you until a security officer or manager arrives. 
   - If the business is small and has no security, ask them to call 911 or the local non-emergency line (311) while you remain behind the counter or in a staffed area.
4. **Call 911 if you believe you are in immediate physical danger.** If the person is closing distance, making threats, or if there is no security present, dial 911. 
   - Provide your exact location, the name of the store, and a description of the person shadowing you.
5. **Document the person's description only if it is safe to do so.** Note their approximate height, clothing, and any distinguishing features in your phone's notepad. 
   - Do not attempt to take photos or video if doing so requires you to turn your back on them or provokes an interaction.
6. **Arrange for a secure exit from the building.** Ask for a security escort to your vehicle or public transit stop. 
   - If using a rideshare service, wait inside the building with staff until the app confirms the driver has arrived at the door.
7. **Switch to a "fixed location" strategy.** Stop moving between aisles or shops, as movement provides the opportunity for the person to continue shadowing you. Staying in one high-visibility spot forces the person to either leave or reveal their intent to witnesses.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether you want to file a formal police report.
- Determining the "true" intent of the person shadowing you.
- Reviewing store security footage (this usually requires a police request).
- Analyzing why you were targeted or if you should have noticed sooner.

## Important reassurance
Feeling hyper-vigilant when your movements are being mirrored is a natural and protective response. You are not "overreacting" by seeking help from staff; businesses are trained to handle "suspicious person" reports and prioritize customer safety over social politeness.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active situation in a public retail or mall environment. It does not cover long-term stalking cases or legal protective orders.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice. If you are in immediate danger, contact 911.
