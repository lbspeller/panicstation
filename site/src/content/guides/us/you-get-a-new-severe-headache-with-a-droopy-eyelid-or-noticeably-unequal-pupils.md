# What to do if… you get a new severe headache with a droopy eyelid or noticeably unequal pupils (USA)

## Short answer
Call 911 immediately. A new, severe headache combined with a drooping eyelid or pupils of different sizes is a medical emergency that requires an immediate neurological evaluation in an emergency department.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital — call 911 for an ambulance so paramedics can begin assessment and alert the hospital.
- Do not wait to see if the symptoms pass — even if the pain or eye changes seem to improve, the underlying cause may still be life-threatening.
- Do not take aspirin, ibuprofen, or other blood-thinning medications — if the cause is a brain bleed, these medications can worsen the situation.
- Do not eat or drink anything — you may require emergency surgery or a procedure that requires an empty stomach.
- Do not assume it is "just a migraine" — even if you have a history of migraines, a new eye-droop or pupil change makes this a different and urgent situation.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately and state that you have a "sudden severe headache with new neurological eye symptoms."**
   - Explicitly mention if one pupil is larger than the other or if one eyelid is drooping.
   - Mention any other symptoms like neck pain, double vision, or weakness.
2. **Note the exact time the symptoms started or the "last known well" time.**
   - This information is critical for doctors to determine which emergency treatments are safe to use.
3. **Perform a quick F.A.S.T. check if possible and report results to the dispatcher.**
   - **Face:** Does one side of the face droop when smiling?
   - **Arms:** Does one arm drift downward when both are raised?
   - **Speech:** Is speech slurred or strange when repeating a simple phrase?
   - **Time:** Call 911 immediately if any of these are present.
4. **Prepare your environment for the arrival of emergency responders.**
   - Unlock the front door, turn on outside and entryway lights, and move any pets to a closed room.
5. **Sit or lie down in a safe, easily accessible location near the entrance.**
   - Avoid stairs if you feel dizzy or weak; stay on the ground floor if possible.
6. **Gather your ID and a list of current medications only if they are within immediate reach.**
   - If you take blood thinners (anticoagulants), ensure you tell the paramedics immediately upon their arrival.
7. **Remain still and avoid any physical exertion while waiting for the ambulance.**
   - Do not try to "walk it off" or perform household tasks.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the cause of the headache or eye change; the medical team will use imaging (CT/MRI) to do this.
- You do not need to call your regular doctor or wait for an appointment.
- You do not need to pack a hospital bag; focus entirely on the next few minutes of getting to the ER.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frightened or overwhelmed by sudden neurological symptoms. Calling 911 is the correct and safest action when a headache is accompanied by physical changes like a droopy eyelid or pupil asymmetry. Emergency teams are trained to prioritize these "red flag" symptoms to ensure the best possible outcome.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and emergency navigation only. It is not a diagnostic tool and does not replace the evaluation of emergency medical professionals.

## Important note
This information is for emergency educational purposes and is not medical advice. A severe headache with new eye symptoms is a time-critical emergency; if you are experiencing this now, stop reading and call 911 immediately.
