# What to do if… someone repeatedly asks for your home address after sexual chatting and you feel pressured to share it (USA)
## Short answer
Do not share your home address. Pause the conversation and focus on reducing what they can use to identify, contact, or locate you.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send your full address, apartment number, ZIP code, building name, or a nearby location as a compromise.
- Do not send live location, delivery screenshots, ride-share screenshots, or photos that could identify your home.
- Do not let them frame sharing your address as a test of trust, attraction, or consent.
- Do not move to another messaging service just because they keep pushing.
- Do not meet them at your home or let them pick you up there.
- Do not keep trying to persuade them after you have said no.
- If you may want a record later, you can save a few screenshots before blocking or reporting them.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** Stop replying while you decide what feels safe. If you think they may come to your home, are nearby, or are threatening immediate harm, call 911.

2. **Stop sharing identifying details.** Do not send your address, neighborhood, work location, daily routine, school, building name, or places you regularly visit.

3. **Use one short boundary message only if you want to reply.** For example: “I’m not sharing my address.” You do not need to justify it or keep discussing it.

4. **Block, mute, or report the account when you are ready.** Use the platform’s unwanted-contact or harassment tools if available.

5. **Check the account they can see.** Remove visible location clues, hide your phone number or email if they are public, review old posts, and turn off location tagging or check-ins.

6. **Check for simple address clues.** Look at recent posts, marketplace listings, package photos, wishlists, pet tags, and event posts that may show your street, building, or neighborhood.

7. **Get support if the pressure continues or becomes threatening.** You can contact RAINN for confidential support and information. The USA.gov resource below lists reporting and support routes if repeated contact or threats continue.

8. **Use specialist guidance if intimate images are threatened or shared.** The FTC resource below explains first steps for image-based abuse.

## What can wait
You do not need to decide right now whether to keep talking to them, make a report, explain the sexual chat, review every account, or make longer-term address changes. The urgent job is to avoid sharing your address and reduce obvious ways they could locate or contact you.

## Important reassurance
You can stop at any point. Sexual chatting does not create an obligation to share your address, meet in person, keep replying, or explain your decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about reporting, account security, or other protective steps may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Laws and reporting routes vary by state. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
