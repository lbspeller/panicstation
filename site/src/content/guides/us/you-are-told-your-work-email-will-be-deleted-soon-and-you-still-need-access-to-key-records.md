# What to do if… you are told your work email will be deleted soon and you still need access to key records (USA)

## Short answer
Treat this as a high-risk technical window: obtain the shutdown date in writing and formally request an IT-approved export or transfer of specific administrative and personnel records before your credentials expire.

## Do not do these things
- Do not forward work emails, attachments, or internal documents to a personal email address or personal cloud storage.
- Do not download proprietary software, client lists, internal templates, or trade secrets to personal devices.
- Do not delete folders, sent items, or chat logs in an attempt to "clean up" before leaving.
- Do not assume you can "log in one last time" after your official final hour of employment.
- Do not use screenshots to bypass security or export restrictions, as this is often logged and flagged by security software.

## What to do now
1. **Get the exact deactivation timeline in writing from IT or HR.** **Confirm the specific date and hour your credentials will expire to establish your window for action.**
   - Ask if the mailbox will be permanently deleted or placed in a searchable archive (eDiscovery hold).
   - Ask who will be the designated "owner" of the data once you depart.
2. **Compile a list of specific personnel and administrative records you are legally entitled to access.** **Focus on documents that establish the terms and history of your employment rather than general work product.**
   - Signed offer letters, employment contracts, and non-compete/non-solicitation agreements.
   - Performance evaluations, commendations, and any written disciplinary actions or rebuttals.
   - Payroll records, commission statements, and benefits enrollment confirmations.
   - Job descriptions and policy manuals you were required to acknowledge.
3. **Request a sanctioned transfer method for handover materials.** **Ask IT or your manager to provide a secure, company-owned location for essential records that must remain with the team.**
   - Moving project-critical threads to a shared department mailbox.
   - Saving essential emails as PDFs into a secure, team-owned SharePoint or Google Drive folder.
   - Creating a "Handover Archive" with restricted permissions for your successor.
4. **Submit a formal written request for your personnel file.** **Check your state laws, as many states (including CA, IL, WA, and CT) mandate specific timelines for employers to provide copies of your personnel records.**
   - Specify whether you want to inspect the file in person or receive a digital copy.
   - Mention any statutory rights relevant to your state (e.g., California Labor Code 1198.5).
5. **Request an official IT export for personal administrative data.** **If company policy allows, ask IT to provide an encrypted export of your pay stubs or tax documents rather than attempting to download them yourself.**
   - This creates an audit trail of approved data removal and prevents security alerts.
6. **Map the locations of critical data without moving the files.** **Create a "data map" in a shared company document that lists system names, folder paths, and key contacts for your successor.**
   - List the names of specific files or threads that will be needed for ongoing projects.
   - Include the names of technical owners for any specialized databases or CRM tools.
7. **If you are a federal employee, verify your agency's 120-day rule for record requests.** **Contact your agency HR immediately to print or download your SF-50s and eOPF contents before your PIV/CAC access is revoked.**
   - If you have been separated for less than 120 days, the request must go to your last employing agency.
   - If more than 120 days have passed, you must submit a signed, written request to the National Personnel Records Center (NPRC) in Valmeyer, IL.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on legal action or external complaints while the technical window is still open.
- You do not need to export every single email; focus exclusively on personnel records and critical project handovers.
- You do not need to resolve disagreements about the reason for the account closure today.

## Important reassurance
The sudden loss of access to years of communication is a high-stress event, and feeling a sense of urgency is a standard response to digital offboarding. Following the official process for records requests protects you from allegations of data theft while ensuring you obtain the documents you need for your future career or legal protection.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and risk-reduction for preserving access to essential employment records. It does not provide legal strategy for employment disputes or technical instructions for bypassing security protocols.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employee rights to personnel records and the legality of data removal vary significantly by state law, federal status, and individual employment contracts. Unauthorized removal of company data can result in immediate termination for cause or civil litigation. For specific legal disputes, consult an employment attorney.
