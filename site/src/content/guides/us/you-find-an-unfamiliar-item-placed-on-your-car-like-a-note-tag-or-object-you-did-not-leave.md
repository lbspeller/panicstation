# What to do if… you find an unfamiliar item placed on your car like a note, tag, or object you did not leave (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a populated, well-lit area immediately and assess the vehicle from a distance. If an object appears attached, magnetized, or installed on the vehicle, do not touch the item or the car and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t touch, open, move, or peel off anything that looks attached (magnets, tape, boxes, wires) or is located underneath the vehicle.
- Don’t get into the car if you feel you are being actively watched or followed—maintain distance and move toward other people.
- Don’t confront individuals you suspect placed the item or respond to notes that request personal contact information.
- Don’t drive the car if you suspect a device is attached to the wheels, engine area, or undercarriage.
- Don’t use your key fob or remote start if you believe you are being watched, as this identifies which car is yours from a distance.

## What to do now
1. **Move yourself to a safer, populated location before attempting to inspect the vehicle.** **Go into a nearby store, office building, or busy public space and stay in the line of sight of others or security personnel.**

2. **Assess the item from a safe distance without touching it.** **Determine if the item is a loose flyer / paper note resting on the glass or a physical object that appears purposefully attached to the body or tucked into a crevice.**
   - Loose items: Flyers, business cards, or paper notes under wipers.
   - Attached items: Magnetic boxes, taped wires, zip ties on handles, or objects wedged into wheel wells / bumpers.

3. **If the item is attached or you cannot identify it, treat it as a potential hazard and call 911.** **Step away from the vehicle, keep other people back, and provide the emergency dispatcher with your exact location and a description of the object.**
   - Mention if the item looks like a "suspicious package" or an "unidentified electronic device."

4. **If the item is a loose note or flyer and you feel safe, document it before removal.** **Take a photograph of the item exactly where it was found and note the time, date, and specific location of the parking spot.**
   - If you choose to remove a loose paper note, handle it as little as possible (using a napkin or bag) in case it is needed for a later police report.

5. **Perform a single, quick visual sweep of the vehicle exterior.** **Walk around the car once to look for other markings, such as zip ties on door handles, tape over sensors, or objects wedged near the fuel cap.**
   - If any secondary items are found, stop the inspection and move away immediately.

6. **Check your mobile device for active "Unknown Tracker" or "Item Found Moving With You" alerts.** **Open your phone settings to verify that Bluetooth and "Tracking Notifications" are enabled, and review any recent security pings regarding Apple Find My or Google Find My Device accessories.**
   - For iOS: Check "Find My" > "Items" > "Items Detected With You."
   - For Android: Check "Settings" > "Safety & Emergency" > "Unknown Tracker Alerts."

7. **Contact property security to request a review of surveillance footage.** **Ask the manager of the lot or store to preserve any video from the time you arrived until the item was discovered.**

8. **Contact VictimConnect if you suspect this is part of a pattern of stalking or harassment.** **Call or text 855-484-2846 to speak with a specialist who can provide confidential referrals and safety planning without requiring a police report.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the motive or identify the person who placed the item right now.
- You do not need to decide if this "qualifies" as a crime before seeking help or moving to safety.
- You do not need to perform a technical search of the car's interior or engine.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of alarm or "gut instinct" when finding an unfamiliar item on your property. Many items placed on cars are harmless advertisements or mistakes, but taking immediate steps to move to a populated area is a standard safety precaution and does not mean you are overreacting.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate discovery. It does not cover long-term stalking investigations, forensic evidence collection, or mechanical inspections of the vehicle.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice or a guarantee of safety. If you believe you are being followed or are in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement via 911 immediately.
