# What to do if… your infant under three months has a fever or is unusually sleepy (USA)
## Short answer
A baby under three months with a rectal or forehead temporal-artery temperature of **100.4°F (38°C) or higher** should go to the ER right away. Call **911** if your baby is hard to wake, cannot wake enough to feed, is limp or floppy, has blue or gray lips or tongue, is struggling to breathe or has a seizure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait at home to see whether a fever improves.
- Do not delay emergency care while repeating temperature checks or trying home remedies.
- Do not rely on touch or a forehead strip alone.
- Do not use an ear thermometer for a baby under 6 months.
- Do not use cold baths, ice or rubbing alcohol.
- Do not overdress or tightly bundle your baby.
- Do not give aspirin.
- Do not give fever medicine before speaking with a clinician.
- Do not give ibuprofen to a baby under 6 months unless a clinician tells you to.
- Do not assume recent vaccines explain the fever without getting clinical advice.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now** if your baby cannot be woken enough to feed, is limp or floppy, has blue or gray lips or tongue, is struggling to breathe, has a seizure or is rapidly getting worse.
2. **Go to the ER right away** if a rectal or forehead temporal-artery temperature is **100.4°F (38°C) or higher**, even if your baby otherwise looks well. You can call your pediatrician while arranging care, but do not delay the trip.
3. **Check the temperature with a digital thermometer if you can do so without delaying care.**
   - A rectal temperature is the most reliable measurement for an infant under three months.
   - A forehead temporal-artery thermometer can be used. A forehead strip is not reliable.
   - An underarm reading is less accurate. If an underarm reading is elevated, or you cannot get a reliable reading and your baby seems unwell, call your pediatrician immediately for direction.
   - Do not delay emergency care if your baby has warning signs.
4. **Treat unusual sleepiness as urgent even without a confirmed fever.**
   - Call your pediatrician immediately if your baby is much sleepier than usual, is feeding less or is not acting normally.
   - Call 911 if your baby is difficult to awaken or cannot wake enough to feed.
5. **While arranging care, keep things simple.**
   - Keep your baby lightly dressed in a comfortable room.
   - Offer a usual feed only if your baby is awake and willing. Do not force a feed.
   - Note the highest temperature, the time, how it was measured, feeding changes, wet diapers, vomiting, rash, breathing changes, recent vaccines and any medicine already given.
6. **Tell the clinician the baby’s age in weeks** and give the notes from step 5.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause.
- You do not need to try to bring the fever down before seeking care.
- You do not need to decide which tests or treatments might be needed.
- You do not need to pack more than is necessary to leave promptly.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to feel alarmed by fever or unusual sleepiness in a very young infant. Prompt assessment is the appropriate next step, and clinicians expect caregivers to seek help for these signs.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A clinician may need to assess your baby and guide later decisions.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice for a specific child. Call 911 for emergency warning signs or seek urgent clinical care if your baby is getting worse.
