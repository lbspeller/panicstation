# What to do if… there is a disagreement about funeral arrangements and the deadline is close (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the funeral home or other provider immediately, say that authority is disputed, and ask what irreversible steps can be paused while the provider checks the applicable state requirements. Ask for written confirmation of what is paused, for how long, and what the provider needs next.

## Do not do these things
- Do not approve cremation, burial, or another irreversible disposition just to end the pressure.
- Do not assume that one relative automatically has authority without checking the applicable state rules and any documents left by the person who died.
- Do not make new non-essential deposits or commitments until authority and the real timing pressure are clearer.
- Do not argue through social media or send repeated conflicting instructions to the provider.
- Do not approve non-routine transport without asking what it is for and whether it affects the dispute.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the provider to record the dispute**
   - Tell the funeral home, crematory, cemetery, or other provider that there is disagreement about the arrangements.
   - Ask whether cremation, burial, or another irreversible disposition can be paused while authority is checked.
   - Ask for written confirmation of what is paused, how long the pause can last, and whether any care or storage charges may apply.

2. **Ask what authority and documents the provider requires**
   - Ask which state rule the provider is relying on.
   - Ask whether it needs any written instructions, prearrangements, designated-agent documents, proof of relationship, identification, or other records.
   - Gather copies of any documents that may be relevant and send only what the provider requests.

3. **Find out what the deadline actually is**
   - Ask whether the pressure is caused by release of the remains, refrigeration or other care arrangements, scheduling, transport, cemetery timing, or a proposed cremation or burial date.
   - Ask what can safely wait and what limited arrangements are needed to buy time without deciding the disputed issue.

4. **Check whether another office is involved**
   - Ask whether a medical examiner, coroner, or other relevant office still needs to release the remains or complete any process.
   - Ask who should be contacted and what is still pending.

5. **Reduce conflicting messages**
   - Where possible, agree on one person to receive updates from the provider.
   - Keep a brief written note of who was contacted, what the provider said it needs, and what pause has been confirmed.

6. **Get prompt local advice if an irreversible step appears imminent**
   - If authority is still unclear and cremation, burial, or another irreversible disposition may happen soon, contact a local attorney and explain the timing.
   - Ask the provider to keep the dispute noted while you seek advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide music, readings, flowers, obituary wording, or memorial details now.
- You do not need to settle the wider family disagreement today.
- You do not need to make long-term decisions about ashes or a memorial now unless a provider explains that a specific immediate decision is required.
- You do not need to compare every provider or service while the urgent issue is authority and timing.

## Important reassurance
A close deadline can make every decision feel immediate. Separating irreversible decisions from details that can wait may create enough space to check the correct process without adding pressure.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. State rules and the effect of any documents can vary, so a continuing dispute may need specialist local help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Ask the provider what applies in the specific situation and seek prompt local legal advice if an irreversible disposition appears imminent while authority remains unclear.
