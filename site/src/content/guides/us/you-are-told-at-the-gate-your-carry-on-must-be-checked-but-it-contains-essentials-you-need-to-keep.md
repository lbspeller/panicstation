# What to do if… you are told at the gate your carry-on must be checked but it contains essentials you need to keep (USA)

## Short answer
Ask for a brief pause before handing over the bag. Remove medication and essential medical supplies, identification and travel documents, valuables, spare lithium batteries, power banks, portable chargers, electronic cigarettes, vaping devices, and lithium-battery-powered devices where practical.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hand over the bag before removing the priority items you need to keep with you.
- Do not leave spare lithium batteries, power banks, portable chargers, electronic cigarettes, or vaping devices in any bag that will be checked, including a bag checked at the gate or planeside.
- Do not scatter small essentials loosely across several pockets if you can place them together in one pouch or small bag.
- Do not assume you will be able to access the checked bag during the flight.
- Do not spend the first moments arguing about policy. Secure the priority items first.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a brief pause.**  
   Say: “Understood. I need a brief moment to remove medication, essential items, and battery items that cannot go in checked baggage.”

2. **Move to the side if staff direct you to do so, and remove the priority items.** Keep these with you:
   - Medication you may need during the journey, including inhalers and epinephrine auto-injectors.
   - Essential medical devices and supplies you may need before you can collect the bag.
   - Identification or passport, wallet, phone, keys, and boarding documents.
   - Spare lithium batteries, power banks, and portable chargers.
   - Electronic cigarettes and vaping devices.
   - Glasses, hearing aids, fragile items, and items that would be difficult to replace.
   - Lithium-battery-powered devices where practical, including your phone, laptop, tablet, and camera.

3. **Protect battery terminals.**  
   Keep spare lithium batteries and power banks in manufacturer packaging where possible. Otherwise, cover exposed terminals with tape and place the items separately so they cannot short-circuit against each other or metal objects.

4. **Tell staff plainly about medical needs.**  
   Say: “I need to keep this medication and these essential medical supplies with me.”  
   If an item is an assistive device or disability-related medication, say so and ask for it to remain with you in the cabin, consistent with safety rules. These items generally should not count against your carry-on limit. If there is a disability accommodation problem, ask for a supervisor or the airline’s Complaints Resolution Official.

5. **Ask where the bag will be returned.**  
   Say: “Will I collect this at the jet bridge or at baggage claim?”  
   Keep anything you will need before you can collect the bag.

6. **Check any lithium-battery-powered device that remains in the bag.**  
   Tell staff if one is still inside and follow the airline’s instructions. A lithium-battery-powered device packed in checked baggage must be completely switched off and protected from accidental activation and damage.

7. **Use any remaining time for simple bag-tracking steps.**
   - Keep the claim tag or receipt.
   - Take a quick photo of the bag and the claim tag.
   - Check that your contact details are on or inside the bag.

8. **If you are being rushed, repeat the priority clearly.**  
   Say: “I will comply. I need a brief moment to remove medication and battery items that cannot go in checked baggage.”  
   If you only have moments, prioritise medication, essential medical supplies, identification, travel documents, spare lithium batteries, power banks, portable chargers, electronic cigarettes, and vaping devices.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve a disagreement about airline policy at the gate.
- You do not need to organise every item perfectly.
- You do not need to make a complaint before boarding.
- Photos and contact-detail checks can wait if staff need the bag immediately after you remove the priority items.

## Important reassurance
A gate-check request can feel abrupt, especially when boarding is already moving. A brief pause to remove priority items can reduce the chance of arriving without something important.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are told your carry-on must be checked. Later questions about airline policy, delayed baggage, damaged baggage, or complaints may need airline-specific help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Follow staff safety instructions and ask for a supervisor if you need help keeping essential items with you.
