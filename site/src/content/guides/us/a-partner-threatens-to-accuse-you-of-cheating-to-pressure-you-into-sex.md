# What to do if… a partner threatens to accuse you of cheating to pressure you into sex (USA)
## Short answer
You do not owe anyone sex to stop a threat. Move to a safer pause if you can, and contact a sexual assault or domestic violence hotline for support choosing the next safe step.

## Do not do these things
- Do not blame yourself for any response you make while trying to stay safe.
- Do not get pulled into arguing about the cheating accusation while you feel unsafe.
- Do not meet your partner alone to resolve this if you feel pressured, intimidated, or scared.
- Do not delete threatening messages in a panic if it is safe to leave them where they are.
- Do not pressure yourself to decide about reporting or the relationship right now.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move away from your partner if you can do that safely. Go to a trusted person, a public place, or another safer location. If you are in immediate danger, call **911**.
2. **Contact one safe person.** Send a short message such as: “My partner is threatening me to pressure me into sex. Can I come over, or can you stay on the phone with me?”
3. **Contact RAINN from a safer device if needed.** Tell the hotline that a partner is threatening to accuse you of cheating unless you agree to sex. Ask for help choosing a safe next step. You can contact support before deciding whether to report.
4. **Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline if the threat is ongoing or you need safety planning around your partner.** If you think your phone or internet use may be monitored, use a trusted person’s phone or another safer device if possible.
5. **Keep the threat only if it is safe.** If it is safe, leave threatening messages undeleted in case you want them later.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now what label fits.
- You do not have to decide right now whether to report to police or explore legal options.
- You do not have to write a detailed timeline tonight.
- You do not have to make a relationship decision in this moment.

## Important reassurance
A threat to accuse you of cheating unless you agree to sex is sexual coercion. Consent must be freely given. Feeling pressured, scared, or confused does not mean you agreed, and this is not your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate harm and buy time. Later decisions may need help from a sexual assault advocate, domestic violence advocate, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you think your phone or internet use may be monitored, consider using a safer device to contact support.
