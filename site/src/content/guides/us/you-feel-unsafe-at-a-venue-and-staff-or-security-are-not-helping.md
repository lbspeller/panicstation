# What to do if… you feel unsafe at a venue and staff or security are not helping (USA)

## Short answer
Move toward a safer, visible place and leave when you can do so without confronting anyone. If you think you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911 now.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue, threaten, or try to “teach them a lesson” as the situation escalates.
- Don’t let staff or security stall you if you feel unsafe; your priority is getting to a safer place.
- Don’t go outside alone with someone who is pressuring you to “talk” or “sort it out”.
- Don’t head into isolated areas, such as back corners, stairwells, or parking garages, unless that is the only way to break contact.
- Don’t stop to film, livestream, or post if it delays leaving or getting help.
- Don’t stay just because you paid, you are waiting on friends, or you feel embarrassed.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance immediately.** Move to the brightest, busiest area with staff presence, such as the bar, cashier, front desk, guest services, or main lobby. Stand where you can see an exit and other people can see you.
2. **Make a direct safety request to one specific person.** Pick one staff member and say: **“I feel unsafe. I need help leaving safely right now.”** If there is a manager, front desk, or guest services point, ask to be escorted to the exit or to a safe waiting area.
3. **If they do not respond, move anyway.** Go toward the main public exit, a staffed lobby, or another visible public area. If you are alone, move with the flow of people or toward a staffed public exit rather than into a side hallway.
4. **Call 911 if danger feels immediate.** Give the venue name, the exact address if you know it, the nearest intersection if you do not, what is happening, and where you are inside, such as “main bar by the front entrance”. If you can do it safely, ask someone at the front to meet responders at the entrance.
5. **If you cannot safely speak, stay connected.** Call 911 and do not hang up unless the call-taker tells you to. If you can safely whisper, do; otherwise stay on the line and follow any instructions you are given.
6. **If you cannot call, try Text-to-911 where available.** Start with your exact location and what help you need, such as police or medical help. Keep it short and use plain words. If Text-to-911 is not available, you should receive a bounce-back message saying the text was not delivered; then use a voice call if you can.
7. **Make your exit quiet, direct, and fast.** Do not announce that you called 911. Do not negotiate. Go straight to a staffed, well-lit public place, such as an open store, hotel lobby, staffed transit point, or safe pickup point.
8. **Stabilize the next few minutes once you are out.** Call someone you trust and tell them exactly where you are. Arrange transportation from a safe, visible pickup point. If you believe someone is following you, go into a staffed location and call 911.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to file a report, complain to the venue, or prove what happened.
- You do not need to resolve refunds, tabs, coats, or property before you are safe.
- You do not need to confront staff or security about their failure in the moment.
- You do not need to explain the whole situation perfectly before you move away from danger.

## Important reassurance
When staff or security do not respond, it is common to second-guess yourself or freeze. Feeling unsafe is enough reason to act. Your goal right now is to reduce risk quickly and get to a safer place.

## Scope note
These are first steps for immediate safety and contacting emergency services. Later decisions, such as documenting what happened, making a complaint, or getting legal support, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you believe you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911. Local emergency systems and venue procedures vary, so keep your actions focused on leaving safely and getting real-time help.
