# What to do if… you miss the last service home because a connection was delayed without warning (USA)

## Short answer
Find a staffed, well-lit area immediately and contact the carrier to confirm your rebooking, alternate transport, or overnight accommodation options before the terminal closes.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the station to "explore options" in unlit or isolated areas if a monitored waiting room is available.
- Don’t accept rides from unlicensed individuals or strangers offering "deals" outside the terminal.
- Don’t pay for a hotel or expensive taxi until you have confirmed the carrier's refusal to provide a voucher or rebooking.
- Don’t assume a mobile app update is final; verify with a gate agent or phone representative while on-site.
- Don’t share your exact location or the fact that you are traveling alone on public social media.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the safest "pause point" in your current location.**  
   **Move to an area with active staff, security, or a high concentration of other passengers.** 
   - Look for "Information," "Customer Service," or "Station Master" desks.
   - If the station is empty or unstaffed, locate a well-lit area near an emergency call button or security camera.
2. **Contact the carrier immediately via their official customer service line.**  
   **State clearly: "I missed a guaranteed connection due to a carrier delay and am stranded for the night."**
   - Amtrak: Call 1-800-USA-RAIL (1-800-872-7245) or text 268725 (AMTRAK).
   - Greyhound/FlixBus: Use the "Manage My Booking" tool in the app or call 1-800-231-2222.
3. **Request specific "Contract of Carriage" remedies for missed guaranteed connections.**  
   **Ask for the next available departure time and whether the carrier will provide a hotel voucher or meal assistance.**
   - For Amtrak: If the delay was within their control and you are stranded overnight, ask for a contracted hotel room or sleeper car accommodation.
   - For Greyhound: If the first leg was delayed by 120+ minutes, you are entitled to free rebooking or a voucher via the "Manage My Booking" feature.
4. **Determine the terminal’s overnight policy.**  
   **Ask security or staff if the building closes and where the designated "safe waiting area" is located for stranded passengers.** 
   - If the terminal closes and no voucher is provided, prioritize moving to a 24-hour public location (like a well-lit hotel lobby) via a licensed ride-hail or taxi.
5. **Verify the identity of any alternate transport provided.**  
   **Check the vehicle markings and driver credentials against the information provided by the carrier or ride-hail app before entering.**
6. **Capture perishable evidence of the disruption.**  
   **Take photos of the "Delayed" status on terminal boards and screenshot any SMS or email alerts regarding the connection failure.**
   - Keep all receipts for "reasonable essentials" such as water, basic food, or emergency transport if the carrier directs you to "pay and claim later."

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or request a full refund tonight.
- You do not need to resolve long-term compensation claims for "lost time" or "consequential damages" right now.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent travel itinerary for the rest of your week.

## Important reassurance
Being stranded late at night is a high-stress event that triggers a natural "fight or flight" response. Feeling disoriented, anxious, or frustrated is a standard physiological reaction to a sudden loss of mobility. These feelings do not mean you are in danger; they are signals to prioritize your immediate environment and safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passengers stranded at US terminals due to missed connections. Carrier obligations are governed by their individual "Contract of Carriage" and may vary based on the cause of the delay (e.g., weather vs. mechanical).

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Transportation policies, including those of Amtrak and Greyhound, are subject to change. Rights to accommodation are often contingent on whether the connection was "guaranteed" (booked on a single itinerary). If you feel in immediate physical danger, contact 911.
