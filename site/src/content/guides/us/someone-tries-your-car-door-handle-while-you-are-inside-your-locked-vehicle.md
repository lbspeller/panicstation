# What to do if… someone tries your car door handle while you are inside your locked vehicle (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside with the doors locked and the windows up. If you can leave safely, drive to a busy, well-lit, staffed place; if it is happening now or you feel threatened, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock the door or open the window to talk, argue, or see what they want.
- Do not get out of the vehicle to confront them.
- Do not chase them if they move away or try to block them in.
- Do not stay parked just to record video if leaving would make you safer.
- Do not search through bags or the glove box while the person is still nearby.
- Do not drive home if you think someone is following you.

## What to do now
1. **Seal the vehicle.** Confirm the doors are locked and the windows are fully up. Keep your seatbelt on. If it is safe, start the engine so you can move.
2. **Move away if the path is clear.** Drive calmly to a busy, well-lit, staffed location, such as an open store, staffed gas station, or other public place with people around.
3. **Use attention-getters from inside the car.** If the person keeps pulling the handle, stays close to the door, blocks you, or tries another door, honk the horn, flash the headlights, or use the vehicle alarm if you have one.
4. **Call 911 if it feels unsafe or active.** Say your location first, such as the address, cross street, parking lot name, store name, or pump number. Then say that someone is trying to enter your occupied locked vehicle.
5. **Give only the details you can safely notice.** From inside the locked car, give the person’s direction of travel, clothing, vehicle, license plate if clearly visible, and whether there were threats or more than one person.
6. **If you cannot leave immediately, stay sealed and ready.** Keep your hands available to drive or call. Watch both sides of the vehicle, and do not open the door to check until you are somewhere safer.
7. **If you think you are being followed, do not drive home.** Keep moving on public roads if safe, call 911, and follow the dispatcher’s instructions. A police, sheriff, or fire station can be an option if it is nearby, accessible, and safer than stopping elsewhere.
8. **After you are away and parked somewhere safe, write down what happened.** Note the time, place, what door was tried, whether the person spoke or threatened you, and any description you remember.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was a mistake, a theft attempt, or something else.
- You do not need to contact insurance unless there is damage, theft, or a later report requires it.
- You do not need to review video, post online, or explain the situation to anyone before you are somewhere safe.
- Non-urgent follow-up with property management, store staff, security, or local police can wait until the immediate moment is over.

## Important reassurance
Being startled, frozen, shaky, or unsure is a common stress response. Staying sealed in the vehicle, creating distance, and calling for help if the situation is active are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions, such as making a non-urgent police report, changing where you park, or asking a business about security footage, may need local help or specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, focus on reaching a safer place and calling 911.
