# What to do if… your electronic travel authorisation shows as “pending” and your departure is within 24 hours (USA)

## Short answer
Assume you will be denied boarding if the status remains "Pending" at the time of check-in or gate arrival. Verify your application on the official U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) portal immediately and prepare a flight rebooking or delay plan.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to the airport expecting a gate agent or border officer to manually "push through" a pending status.
- Do not submit a second application while the first is pending unless explicitly instructed by a CBP official, as this can cause system conflicts.
- Do not pay "expediting" fees to third-party websites; there is no official way to pay for faster processing.
- Do not rely on a payment receipt or application number as proof of authorization to board.
- Do not assume that "Pending" is a soft rejection; it is a neutral administrative hold that requires more time.

## What to do now
1. **Verify your status only on the official CBP ESTA website or official ESTA Mobile app.**  
   Access the "Check Individual Status" section using your passport number, date of birth, and application number. If you do not have the application number, use the "I do not know the application number" option which requires your passport issuance and expiration dates.
2. **Confirm that the official fee paid was exactly $40.27.**  
   Check your bank statement or the portal's payment receipt; if you paid a different amount, you likely used a third-party site which may not have submitted your data correctly to the U.S. government.
3. **Contact the CBP Traveler Communications Center (TCC) immediately.**  
   Call +1-202-325-8000 for 24/7 assistance specifically for ESTA and Visa Waiver Program issues. Have your application number and passport details ready.
4. **Call your airline to determine their specific "denied boarding" cutoff.**  
   Ask the representative at what exact time or stage (e.g., 60 minutes before departure) they require a "Status: Approved" result in their system to allow you on the aircraft.
5. **Set a hard "stop point" for your backup plan.**  
   Identify the latest possible time you can change your flight without forfeiting the ticket value (often 2 to 4 hours before departure). If the status is still "Pending" at this time, execute your rebooking to a date at least 72 hours in the future.
6. **Double-check the biographical data on your pending application.**  
   Ensure the passport number, country of citizenship, and name exactly match your physical passport. Even a small typo in the passport number can cause an indefinite "Pending" status or a later "Travel Not Authorized" result.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a formal B1/B2 visa today, as these appointments typically take weeks or months to secure.
- You do not need to identify why the application was flagged for review; CBP does not disclose specific vetting triggers.
- You do not need to worry about long-term "travel bans" based on a pending status.

## Important reassurance
A "Pending" status is a standard part of the automated vetting process and does not imply negative findings or a permanent rejection. The system often requires a full 72-hour window to complete background checks. Most applications resolve to "Approved" or "Travel Not Authorized" within that timeframe without further user intervention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for travelers facing immediate departure with a pending U.S. Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA). It does not cover traditional visa applications or legal appeals for denied entry.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or immigration advice. Final authority for boarding rests with the airline and final authority for U.S. entry rests with CBP officers at the port of entry.
