# What to do if… your car key or key fob is cracked or damaged and you worry it may fail (USA)

## Short answer
Use a spare key now if you have one. If this is your only working key, reduce unnecessary use, confirm your vehicle’s backup entry and start method, and arrange a replacement through the dealer, roadside-assistance provider, or a verified automotive locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep repeatedly locking, unlocking, or starting the car just to test whether the damaged key still works.
- Don’t force the case open wider, snap it shut hard, or glue it unless you are following the owner’s manual or a professional’s instruction.
- Don’t carry the cracked key loose with coins, other keys, or anything that can bend or press on it.
- Don’t assume a basic hardware-store copy will start a modern vehicle; many keys and fobs need the correct chip, fob, cutting, or programming.
- Don’t post photos of the key blade, fob, tag, or code online.
- Don’t call 911 for a routine lockout. Use 911 for immediate danger, such as a child or pet locked in a hot vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Switch to a spare key if you have one.** Put the damaged key somewhere dry and safe so it is not bent, dropped, or used by accident.

2. **If this is your only working key, make the next use count.**
   - Combine essential trips so you reduce lock, unlock, and restart cycles.
   - Park where help can reach you easily if the car will not unlock or start.
   - Keep your phone charged and take a charger or power bank.

3. **Find your exact backup entry method before you need it.**
   - Check the owner’s manual, manufacturer app, or manufacturer support site for your model year.
   - Many smart fobs have a hidden mechanical key blade for the driver’s door.
   - Some vehicles hide the door key cylinder behind a small cover, so check the correct method before removing any cover.

4. **Find your exact backup start method before shutting the car off somewhere risky.**
   - Many push-button-start vehicles have a backup sensing spot for a weak or dead fob battery.
   - Depending on the vehicle, this may mean holding the fob near the start button or placing it in a marked pocket, tray, slot, cupholder area, or console area.
   - Use the owner’s manual method for your exact vehicle, because the location and steps vary.

5. **Stabilise the damaged key gently for the short term.**
   - Keep it dry.
   - Put it in a snug protective cover if you already have one.
   - If the case seam is spreading, use a small amount of tape around the outside only, keeping buttons, the key blade release, and battery access usable.
   - If the battery door is loose, tape the outside so the battery cannot lift away from its contacts.

6. **Arrange a replacement route now.**
   - For newer smart keys, call the dealer or manufacturer service centre.
   - For an automotive locksmith, confirm they can cut and program your exact make, model, year, and key type.
   - Ask for the business name, total estimate, and proof of identity before agreeing to locksmith work.
   - If you have roadside assistance, ask whether they can help with lockout service, towing, or a locksmith referral.

7. **Have the usual details ready before you call.**
   - Vehicle make, model, and year.
   - VIN, usually visible through the windshield on the driver’s side.
   - Your location.
   - Photo ID and proof that you own or are authorised to use the vehicle, such as registration, title, rental agreement, or insurance document.

8. **If you are away from home and the key feels close to failing, choose one controlled move.**
   - Go straight to home, a dealer, a locksmith, or another safe place where the car can stay if it will not restart.
   - Avoid stopping somewhere isolated, blocking traffic, or turning the vehicle off repeatedly.
   - If the vehicle will not unlock or start, use roadside assistance, the dealer, or a verified automotive locksmith rather than trying to force the key or fob.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to upgrade security, change locks, or buy extra accessories.
- You do not need to diagnose every possible cause before arranging help.
- You do not need to find the cheapest long-term option before securing one reliable working key.
- You do not need to order multiple extra keys until the immediate risk of being locked out or unable to start the car is handled.

## Important reassurance
A cracked fob or key can sometimes keep working for a while, but it is sensible not to rely on it. The immediate goal is simple: protect the key from more damage, learn the backup entry and start method, and get one reliable replacement plan in motion.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the chance of a sudden lockout or no-start. Vehicle key systems vary by make, model, year, trim, and previous repairs, so later decisions may need help from the dealer, roadside-assistance provider, or a qualified automotive locksmith.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, financial, security, or professional advice. For immediate danger, use emergency services; for vehicle-specific instructions, use your owner’s manual or a qualified service provider.
