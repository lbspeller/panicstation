# What to do if… someone posts about a death in your family before you have told the closest relatives (USA)

## Short answer
If the death has not already been confirmed, confirm the basic fact through a reliable family member or an official contact already involved before repeating it. Then tell the closest relatives directly where possible and ask for the online post to be removed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t confirm details publicly while facts are still unclear.
- Don’t comment on or react to the post just to correct it; that can draw more attention to it.
- Don’t start a large group message unless that is the only practical way to reach people quickly.
- Don’t keep rereading replies or searching for reposts yourself.
- Don’t get pulled into a long argument with the person who posted it.
- Don’t share screenshots widely; that can spread the post further.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether the basic fact is confirmed.** If you are not certain, contact a reliable family member or an official contact already involved. Do not repeat unverified details.
2. **Choose one coordinator where possible.** Ask one person to keep track of which close relatives have been contacted so people do not receive conflicting messages.
3. **Tell the closest relatives directly.** Call where possible. If a text is the only practical first contact, keep it brief: “Please call me as soon as you can. I need to tell you urgent family news.”
4. **Save one record of the post, then close the app.** Take one screenshot showing the post and account name, and copy the link if you can. Save extra records only if there are threats, exposed personal information or repeated harassment.
5. **Send one clear removal request if it feels safe.** For example: “Please delete this post. Close family members have not been told yet. Please do not repost or discuss it publicly.”
6. **Use the platform’s report option if appropriate.** Select the post or account, choose the closest available reporting reason and briefly explain the situation. Reporting options vary, and a report may not lead to removal.
7. **Reduce further spread from your own account.** Ask relatives not to comment, react to or reshare the post. Review tag, mention and profile-display settings where the platform offers them. Mute or block the poster if that helps you avoid further contact.
8. **Treat threats or exposed personal information as a separate safety issue.** Save the relevant post or message. If anyone needs immediate police, fire or ambulance help, call **911**. For emotional distress or a crisis, call or text **988**, or use the 988 Lifeline chat.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a public announcement.
- You do not need to answer everyone who has seen the post.
- You do not need to correct every online comment or repost.
- You do not need to keep monitoring the post yourself; ask one trusted person to check it if that is necessary.

## Important reassurance
Having private family news shared online can feel shocking and disorienting. You can focus on the next few calls, limit further attention to the post and leave wider decisions until later.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation. Repeated harassment, impersonation, threats or exposed personal information may need platform escalation or specialist help later.

## Important note
This guide provides general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic or other professional advice. If anyone needs immediate emergency assistance, call 911.
