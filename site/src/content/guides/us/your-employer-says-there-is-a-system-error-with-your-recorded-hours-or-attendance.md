# What to do if… your employer says there is a system error with your recorded hours or attendance (USA)
## Short answer
Reply in writing and ask which dates or time entries may be wrong. Confirm only what you know, and do not approve a timecard that you believe is inaccurate.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess at exact hours or breaks if you are unsure.
- Do not approve a full pay period just to clear the request.
- Do not enter made-up times to make the system accept the timecard.
- Do not send unnecessary personal data, such as a full phone location history, unless you choose to and it is genuinely needed.
- Do not rush your reply. Keep it limited to the affected dates, hours, and requested correction.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the affected entries in writing.** Request the date range, the entries that may be wrong, and whether they want you to confirm attendance, start and stop times, breaks, or total hours.
2. **Ask for a copy of the current timecard.** Ask for the version they want you to review and any available list of missing punches or edits.
3. **Check your own records for the affected days.** Your schedule, calendar, work messages, task logs, delivery records, or job sheets may help you make a good-faith reconstruction.
4. **Reply with a limited confirmation.** State the days and times you can confirm. Clearly label any estimate or uncertainty and ask them to cross-check it against their records.
5. **Review the corrected version before approving it.** If a comment field is available, add a brief correction note and save a copy or screenshot for your records.
6. **If your pay may be affected, ask what happens next.** Ask whether the correction will change your next paycheck, whether an adjustment is planned, and when you can expect an updated time record or pay statement.
7. **Keep copies.** Save the original request, your reply, the timecard before and after correction, and any related messages.
8. **If the record is not corrected and you may be shorted pay, check the next route without rushing.** You can contact the U.S. Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division to ask about the complaint process. A state labor office may also be relevant, depending on your state and situation.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to make a complaint.
- You do not need perfect evidence for every minute before asking for the employer’s records.
- You do not need to make a big job decision while the time record is still being checked.

## Important reassurance
A missing punch or incorrect entry can be checked one item at a time. It is reasonable to ask for the affected entries and confirm only what you know. If you are concerned about retaliation, check the Wage and Hour Division retaliation page because coverage can depend on the law and situation.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the chance of an inaccurate attendance or payroll record. If the problem continues or becomes a pay dispute, later decisions may need state-specific or role-specific specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Wage-and-hour rules can vary by worker classification and state.
