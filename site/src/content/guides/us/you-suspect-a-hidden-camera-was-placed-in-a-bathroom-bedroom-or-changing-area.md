# What to do if… you suspect a hidden camera was placed in a bathroom, bedroom, or changing area (USA)

## Short answer
Exit the area immediately to a safer location and contact local law enforcement or confidential support services. Do not touch the suspected device or confront anyone while you are alone or still on the premises.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch, move, or dismantle the suspected device (this may destroy fingerprint or digital evidence).
- Do not confront the property owner, host, roommate, or staff while you are on-site or alone.
- Do not post images of the device or accusations on social media before speaking with authorities.
- Do not try to "test" the camera by performing actions in front of it to see if it responds.
- Do not assume you must have physical proof before you are allowed to leave or seek help.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause immediately.** **Exit the immediate area where you suspect the camera is located and go to a public space, such as a lobby, a nearby business, or a friend’s home.**
2. **Contact law enforcement from a safe location.** **Call 911 if you feel you are in immediate danger or being followed; otherwise, call the local police non-emergency line to report a potential privacy violation.**
   - Request that an officer be dispatched to the location to secure the evidence.
3. **Minimize further exposure if you cannot leave immediately.** **If you must remain in the space for a few moments to gather belongings, cover the suspected device with a thick, non-transparent item like a towel or clothing without touching the device itself.**
4. **Document the location and appearance of the device from a distance.** **Take a photograph or video of the object from several feet away to show its placement in the room, but do not handle it.**
   - Write down the date, time, and the specific reason you noticed the object (e.g., a strange light, a wire in an odd place, or a reflection).
5. **Contact management or the booking platform after you are safe.** **If you are in a hotel or rental, inform the management or the platform’s safety team (e.g., Airbnb, Vrbo) that you have found a suspected surveillance device and are contacting the police.**
   - Ask them to preserve card-key logs, maintenance records, and any hallway security footage.
6. **Notify campus authorities if the incident is on a school or university property.** **Contact campus security and the Title IX Coordinator, who handles reports of sex-based harassment and voyeurism.**
7. **Access confidential support services.** **Call or text the RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline (800-656-HOPE) or the VictimConnect Resource Center (855-4-VICTIM) for confidential guidance on your rights and local resources.**
8. **Save all digital communications related to the stay or the space.** **Keep copies of check-in instructions, messages from the host, or maintenance notifications that might indicate who had access to the room.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific make or model of the camera right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a civil lawsuit or pursue long-term legal action tonight.
- You do not need to confirm if you were actually recorded before seeking support or moving to a different location.
- You do not need to explain your suspicion to anyone other than law enforcement or confidential advocates.

## Important reassurance
It is completely normal to feel a sense of violation, shock, or hyper-vigilance after discovering a potential privacy breach. Taking steps to reach a safe environment is the priority, regardless of whether you have absolute certainty about the device.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Procedures for evidence collection, criminal investigation, and long-term legal remedies require the involvement of law enforcement and legal professionals.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Laws regarding surveillance vary by state, and a professional legal or law enforcement assessment is necessary to determine if a crime has occurred. Confidential support is available regardless of whether you choose to make a formal report.
