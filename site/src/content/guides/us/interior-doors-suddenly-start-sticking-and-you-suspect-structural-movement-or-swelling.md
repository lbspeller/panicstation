# What to do if… interior doors suddenly start sticking and you suspect structural movement or swelling (USA)

## Short answer
Stop forcing the doors and check that you still have a usable exit. Move away from any area with falling material, a sagging ceiling, a bowed wall, or another immediate safety risk; call 911 if emergency assistance is needed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep slamming or forcing a sticking door.
- Do not sand, trim, or plane the door as a first response.
- Do not enter or remain under an area with sagging ceilings, bowed walls, falling material, or another sign of immediate danger.
- Do not touch wet electrical equipment or use electrical appliances in standing water.
- Do not start permanent repairs before recording the original condition and contacting the appropriate person or organisation.

## What to do now
1. **Check that you have a usable exit.** Make sure at least one safe exit opens freely. Keep your phone and keys with you. Do not force a door if doing so could jam it shut or cause damage.
2. **Leave the affected area if there is an immediate safety concern.** Move people and pets away from falling material, a sagging ceiling, a bowed wall, or another area that appears unstable. Call 911 if emergency assistance is needed.
3. **Look only from a safe position for changes to record.** Check whether one door or several doors are affected. Note new cracks near doorways, new gaps around trim, windows that have also started binding, floors that feel newly uneven, damp smells, water stains, wet flooring, or an active leak. These observations do not identify the cause, but they help show what changed.
4. **Limit water damage only where it is safe to do so.** If you can safely reach the correct shutoff and know how to use it, stop an active plumbing leak. Do not touch wet electrical equipment. Use ventilation, fans, or a dehumidifier only in an area without standing water or another electrical hazard.
5. **Take clear photos and notes before altering anything.** Photograph the full doorway, the point where the door rubs or fails to latch, nearby cracks, water marks, and any other changed areas. Record when you first noticed the problem and whether it followed rain, flooding, a storm, a leak, or recent work.
6. **Contact the responsible party promptly.**
   - If you rent, notify your landlord or property manager in writing using the contact route in your lease. Include photos and say that the doors changed suddenly and there may be moisture or movement.
   - If you own the home and there may be covered damage, contact your homeowners insurer. Ask what temporary steps are appropriate and whether the insurer wants an inspection before permanent repairs.
7. **Ask for the right local inspection route if the cause is unclear.** If several doors or windows are affected, new cracks are present, or floors or walls appear to have changed, contact the local building department or other local building official. Ask which office or qualified professional is appropriate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the cause is humidity, a leak, seasonal movement, foundation movement, or something else.
- You do not need to choose a permanent repair today.
- You do not need to argue about responsibility today. A clear written notice, photos, and notes are enough for the first step.

## Important reassurance
A sticking interior door can sometimes be caused by moisture or seasonal swelling, but it is sensible to pause and check for wider changes. You are not expected to diagnose the cause yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a safer pause, limiting avoidable damage, and keeping a clear record for an inspection or insurance claim. Later decisions may need help from a local building official, insurer, landlord, or qualified professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, engineering, insurance, or other professional advice. For an immediate emergency, move to a safer place and call 911.
