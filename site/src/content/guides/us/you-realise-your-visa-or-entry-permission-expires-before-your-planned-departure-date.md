# What to do if… you realise your visa or entry permission expires before your planned departure date (USA)

## Short answer
Verify your **I-94 "Admit Until Date"** immediately on the official CBP website or CBP One app; this date, not your visa sticker, determines when you must leave. If this date is before your planned flight, you must either **rebook your departure** for an earlier date or **file for an extension** before your current permission expires.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume the "Expiration Date" on the visa sticker in your passport is your permitted length of stay.
- Don’t stay even one day past the "Admit Until Date" without a pending official application, as this can void your current visa and trigger permanent bars to re-entry.
- Don’t leave the United States while an extension or change of status application (Form I-539) is pending, as this is typically treated as abandonment of the request.
- Don’t ignore the date if your passport expires soon; CBP often limits stays to the passport's expiration date or six months prior.
- Don’t attempt to use Form I-539 if you entered under the Visa Waiver Program (ESTA), as ESTA holders are generally ineligible to extend or change status.

## What to do now
1. **Retrieve your most recent electronic I-94 record immediately.**  
   **Access the official U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) I-94 website or the CBP One mobile app.**  
   - Enter your passport details exactly as they appear on your travel document.  
   - Locate the **"Admit Until Date"**. If it says **"D/S"**, skip to step 2. If it lists a specific date, this is your hard deadline to depart.  
   - Save a digital copy (PDF) and email it to yourself.

2. **Check your Form I-20 or DS-2019 if your I-94 says "D/S".**  
   **Confirm that your "Duration of Status" (D/S) is still valid by checking the program end date on your governing document.**  
   - For F-1 students, check the "Program of Study" section on Form I-20.  
   - For J-1 scholars, check section 3 of Form DS-2019.  
   - If the program end date has passed or is before your flight, contact your Designated School Official (DSO) or Responsible Officer (RO) today.

3. **Compare your I-94 date to your passport expiration date.**  
   **Determine if your stay was shortened because your passport expires within the next six months.**  
   - If the "Admit Until Date" matches your passport's expiration date, CBP has likely capped your stay. You must depart by that date regardless of your original plans.

4. **Request a correction if the I-94 contains a clerical error.**  
   **Contact a CBP Deferred Inspection Site or use the Traveler Communication Center (TCC) website if the date does not match the standard admission period for your visa.**  
   - Standard B-1/B-2 admissions are typically six months. If you were given significantly less without explanation (and your passport is valid), provide your travel itinerary and admission stamp to request a review.  
   - Note that corrections are for errors made *at the time of entry*, not for changing plans afterward.

5. **Rebook your travel if you cannot extend your stay.**  
   **Change your flight to a date on or before the "Admit Until Date" if you are ineligible for an extension.**  
   - This applies to ESTA holders and those in categories where extensions are rarely granted or processing times are prohibitive.

6. **File Form I-539 if you are eligible and need to remain longer.**  
   **Submit the Application to Extend/Change Nonimmigrant Status electronically through USCIS before your current "Admit Until Date" passes.**  
   - You must demonstrate that you have sufficient funds for the extended stay and a valid reason for the delay.  
   - Keep your receipt notice (Form I-797) as proof of your "period of authorized stay" while the application is pending.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to apply for a new visa sticker while you are still inside the U.S.; the sticker is only for entry, not for remaining.
- You do **not** need to notify your home country's embassy unless your passport has been lost or stolen.
- You do **not** need to finalize future travel plans for next year; the priority is stabilizing your current legal status today.

## Important reassurance
It is common for travelers to be confused by the difference between a visa's validity and an I-94 admission period. Identifying the discrepancy before your date passes is the most important step in preventing long-term immigration issues. Taking action now—whether rebooking a flight or filing a timely application—is a standard procedural fix.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document discrepancies only. Immigration status is highly fact-specific; certain visa types (such as C, D, or K) have different rules that this guide does not cover.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. U.S. immigration laws are subject to frequent change. If your authorized stay has already expired or you have a complex immigration history, consult a qualified U.S. immigration attorney before taking further action.
