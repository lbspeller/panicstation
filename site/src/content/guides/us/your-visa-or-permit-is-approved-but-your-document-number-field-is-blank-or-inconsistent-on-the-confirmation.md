# What to do if… your visa or permit is approved but your document number field is blank or inconsistent on the confirmation (USA)

## Short answer
Do not travel or present the document for official verification until you identify which specific ID is required and verify it against official agency records. Use the USCIS e-Request tool for notice/card errors, or contact the issuing U.S. Consulate directly for errors printed on a physical visa foil.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to board an international flight or enter a U.S. port of entry if you know your travel document contains a factual error.
- Do not enter placeholder digits (like "0000" or "N/A") into digital forms or airline check-in systems to bypass a blank field.
- Do not assume an "Approved" status on a website allows entry without the physical document (visa foil or original I-797) in hand.
- Do not pay third-party "expediting" services to correct government errors; these must be handled through official agency channels.
- Do not ignore the mismatch even if it seems minor, as inconsistent data can trigger "mismatches" in the E-Verify or SAVE systems later.

## What to do now
1. **Identify exactly which "document number" is being requested based on your current location.**
   - **Visa Number:** This is the red number found on the bottom right of a physical visa foil (sticker) in your passport. It does not exist until the visa is printed.
   - **USCIS Receipt Number:** A 13-character code (e.g., EAC, WAC, IOE) found on your I-797 Notice of Action.
   - **Alien Registration Number (A-Number):** A 7- to 9-digit number often listed as "USCIS#" on cards or notices.
   - **I-94 Admission Number:** An 11-character alphanumeric code generated by CBP only **after** you have been admitted to the U.S.

2. **Verify the "Approved" status against the authoritative agency database.**
   - For USCIS notices (Green Cards, EADs, I-797s): Check the **USCIS Case Status Online** tool.
   - For Consular Visas: Check the **CEAC (Consular Electronic Application Center)** status.
   - For Entry Records: Check the **CBP I-94 official website** to see if a record was generated.

3. **Submit a "Typographic Error" e-Request if the mistake is on a USCIS-issued paper notice or plastic card.**
   - Use the official USCIS e-Request portal and select "Typographic Error."
   - Provide the receipt number, the date the document was received, and a specific description of what is blank or incorrect.
   - If the error is on a physical card (EAD or Green Card), you may be required to mail the original card back to the address provided in the e-Request instructions.

4. **Contact the specific U.S. Embassy or Consulate if the error is on a printed visa foil in your passport.**
   - Look for the "Visa Misprint" or "Correction Request" form on the website of the embassy that issued the visa.
   - Most consulates require you to drop off or mail the passport back for a "re-issue" or "correction" of the foil.
   - Do not use general inquiry forms; look for the specific "Consular Section" or "Visa Section" contact path.

5. **Locate a CBP Deferred Inspection Site if your I-94 (entry record) contains an error after you have entered the U.S.**
   - If you are already in the U.S. and your electronic I-94 has a blank or wrong number/class of admission, contact a **CBP Deferred Inspection Site**.
   - These offices specialize in correcting data entry errors made by officers at the port of entry.

6. **Call the USCIS Contact Center at 800-375-5283 if online tools fail or if the situation is an emergency.**
   - Use this if you have a pending flight within 48-72 hours and discovered the error.
   - Say "Infopass" or "Technical Support" to navigate the automated menu toward a human representative.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) request for a simple typo; the e-Request system is the faster correct path.
- You do not need to update your Social Security record or DMV status until the underlying immigration document is corrected.
- You do not need to re-pay application fees if the error was clearly made by the agency (USCIS, State Dept, or CBP).

## Important reassurance
Administrative errors and blank fields are common during high-volume processing periods. A blank field often means the number hasn't been generated yet (like an I-94) or you are looking at a summary notice rather than the final document. Taking the time to use the official correction portals now prevents much more difficult "refusal of entry" or "illegal employment" issues later.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for data inconsistencies on U.S. immigration documents. It does not cover legal strategies for visa denials or appeals of substantive eligibility decisions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. Immigration regulations and agency contact methods are subject to change. Always refer to official .gov websites for the most current filing addresses and procedures.
