# What to do if… a side window shatters while your vehicle is parked and you need to secure it (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a secure location, document the damage with photos, file a non-emergency police report, and seal the opening with heavy plastic to prevent weather damage.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not** touch glass shards or pellets with bare hands; tempered glass can still cause deep lacerations.
- **Do not** attempt to operate the window switch, as glass in the tracks can burn out the window motor.
- **Do not** use high-adhesion tapes like duct tape directly on the vehicle's paint, as it may cause permanent finish damage.
- **Do not** drive the vehicle if the glass obstructs your view of side mirrors or the road.
- **Do not** clean up the glass until you have completed your photo documentation for insurance and police.
- **Do not** leave the ventilation or AC on "fresh air" mode, which can pull small glass dust into the vents.

## What to do now
1. **Verify immediate safety.**
   - Call **911** only if the crime is currently in progress or you are in immediate physical danger.
   - For all other cases, move the vehicle to a well-lit or secure area if it is safe to drive.
2. **Contact local law enforcement.**
   - Locate the **non-emergency number** for the local police department or sheriff’s office where the incident occurred.
   - Use the **online reporting portal** if available for "Burglary of a Motor Vehicle" (BMV) or "Vandalism/Property Damage." 
   - Record the **case number** or report confirmation ID immediately.
3. **Notify property management.** 
   - If the vehicle was in a managed lot (apartment, garage, or retail), **inform security or the manager**.
   - Request that they **preserve any surveillance footage** from the time frame of the incident.
4. **Document the scene thoroughly.**
   - Take **wide-angle photos** showing the vehicle's position and the surrounding area.
   - Take **close-up photos** of the shattered window, any tool marks on the door frame, and the interior glass distribution.
5. **Identify stolen or sensitive items.**
   - Check for missing registration, insurance cards, garage door openers, or house keys.
   - If a wallet, phone, or laptop was taken, **remotely wipe devices** and **freeze all credit/debit cards** immediately.
6. **Apply a temporary weather seal.**
   - Clear loose glass from the window frame using a brush or gloved hands.
   - **Use a heavy-duty trash bag or clear plastic sheeting** as a cover.
   - Secure the plastic to the **inside** of the door frame or use **painter's tape** on the exterior to avoid paint damage.
   - Ensure the plastic is pulled taut to minimize flapping noise during transit.
7. **Perform a safe-driving cleanup.**
   - Use a vacuum or brush to remove glass from the **driver’s seat, floor mats, and seatbelt mechanism**.
   - Ensure there are no shards on the dashboard that could slide during turns.
8. **Initiate the insurance claim.**
   - Contact your insurer to verify if you have **comprehensive coverage**.
   - **Check state-specific laws** if you live in Florida, Kentucky, or South Carolina, as these states often require zero-deductible glass replacement.
   - Ask for a "mobile glass repair" referral if you cannot safely drive the vehicle to a shop.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to pay out-of-pocket or use insurance can wait until you have a formal repair quote.
- Detailed interior detailing and deep-cleaning of glass fragments from carpet fibers.
- Conducting your own neighborhood investigation or canvassing for witnesses.

## Important reassurance
Discovering your vehicle has been compromised is a high-stress event that often triggers a "flight or fight" response. It is normal to feel violated or anxious. Following a structured sequence—document, report, and secure—will help you regain control of the situation and prevent further loss from weather or theft.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover long-term legal actions, insurance negotiations, or permanent mechanical repairs for window regulators.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional insurance advice. Police reporting requirements vary significantly by municipality. Always prioritize personal safety over property recovery. If you are financing or leasing the vehicle, check your contract for specific repair shop requirements.
