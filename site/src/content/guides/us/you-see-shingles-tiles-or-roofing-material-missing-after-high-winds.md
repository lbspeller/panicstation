# What to do if… you see shingles, tiles, or roofing material missing after high winds (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the area beneath the damage to avoid falling debris, document the loss from the ground, and contact your insurer or landlord to arrange professional emergency tarping.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb onto the roof or use a ladder to inspect damage (wind may have loosened structural supports or created slippery surfaces).
- Do not touch downed power lines or any tree limbs in contact with utility lines.
- Do not sign a contract or pay a large deposit to a door-to-door solicitor before verifying their local license and insurance.
- Do not run a generator indoors, in a garage, or closer than 25 feet to any door, window, or vent.
- Do not discard damaged roofing materials until an insurance adjuster has seen them or you have documented them with clear photos.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the zone directly below the visible damage.** **Establish a safety perimeter by moving people, pets, and vehicles away from the area where shingles or debris could fall.**
2. **Identify immediate life-safety hazards.** **If you see sparking wires, smell gas, or observe interior ceilings bowing/cracking under the weight of water, evacuate the building and call 911.**
   - Listen for loud creaks or groans from the attic or roof structure.
   - Look for fresh cracks in interior walls or ceilings near the leak.
3. **Minimize interior water damage from the ground level.** **Place buckets or plastic bins under active leaks and move furniture or electronics to a dry area.**
   - If water is dripping near electrical outlets or light fixtures, turn off the power to that room at the main breaker box if it is safe to reach.
4. **Document the damage using your phone or camera.** **Take wide-angle and close-up photos of the missing shingles from the ground, any debris on the lawn, and any interior water spots.**
   - Record the time and date the damage was first noticed and the peak wind conditions.
5. **Contact your insurance company or property manager immediately.** **Report the damage and ask for their specific instructions regarding "mitigation of further loss," which often includes professional tarping.**
   - Renters should notify their landlord or property manager in writing to trigger emergency maintenance.
6. **Hire a licensed local professional for temporary weatherproofing.** **If the insurer authorizes it, have a qualified roofer install a temporary tarp or "shrink wrap" to prevent rain from entering the home.**
   - Keep all receipts for temporary repairs, as these are typically reimbursable under "Reasonable Repairs" coverage.
7. **Verify any contractor's credentials before they begin work.** **Check for a local business address, a current state license, and a certificate of insurance for both liability and workers' compensation.**
   - Be aware that "storm chasers" often move into areas after high winds; insist on a written estimate and a physical contract.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a full roof replacement vs. a partial repair today.
- You do not need to sign a final restoration contract until your insurance adjuster has provided a written loss summary.
- You do not need to clean up heavy debris or nails yourself if it presents a puncture or fall risk.

## Important reassurance
Roof damage is a standard part of storm recovery, and insurance processes are designed to handle these specific scenarios. By staying off the roof and focusing on documentation and temporary tarping, you are following the safest protocol to protect both your physical safety and your financial claim.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. Long-term repair decisions, insurance deductible negotiations, and contractor selection involve complex legal and financial choices that should be made after the immediate hazard is contained.

## Important note
This information is for general stabilization and does not constitute legal, engineering, or professional roofing advice. If the structure appears unstable or electrical hazards are present, evacuate and contact emergency services. Use only licensed and insured professionals for any physical repair or inspection.
