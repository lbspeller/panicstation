# What to do if… someone at your gym or class keeps sexualising you and will not stop after you disengage (USA)

## Short answer
Go to a staff member or instructor immediately and ask them to intervene and maintain distance between you and the other person. If you feel you are in immediate danger or are being followed, leave the building and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not move to isolated or low-traffic areas like empty studios, back hallways, or locker rooms to hide.
- Do not agree to meet the person privately or outside the venue to "settle things" or "talk it out."
- Do not continue to explain, justify, or negotiate your boundaries once you have disengaged—transition directly to seeking professional assistance.
- Do not feel obligated to finish your workout or class if you feel unsafe or distracted by the person's behavior.
- Do not attempt to confront the person alone if they have ignored previous requests to stop or if you feel intimidated.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a high-traffic, public area where staff are present.** Walk to the front desk, stand directly next to the instructor, or position yourself in a crowded area of the gym floor.
2. **State one clear, final boundary if you choose to speak, then stop all interaction.** You may say "Stop. Do not talk to me." or "I am not interested, leave me alone." and then immediately turn away to find a staff member.
3. **Ask gym staff for specific, immediate protective actions.**
   - Tell them "I am being harassed and I need you to keep this person away from me right now."
   - Request that they stay with you, speak to the person directly, or move you to a separate area.
4. **Request a meeting with a manager to file a formal internal incident report.** Ensure they record the date, time, specific behaviors or comments, witness names, and the names of the staff who assisted you.
5. **Utilize the venue's immediate safety and membership protocols.**
   - Ask for a "safety escort" to walk you to your vehicle or transit.
   - Inquire about temporary guest/member suspensions for the individual if they continue to violate conduct policies.
   - Request to be moved to a different class session or time slot for the day.
6. **Preserve any existing evidence without seeking out new interactions.** 
   - Take screenshots of any unwanted social media messages or texts if they have occurred.
   - Ask management to "preserve and retain" gym surveillance footage from the specific time and location of the incident.
7. **Identify if the facility is covered by Title IX protections.**
   - If the gym is part of a college, university, or K-12 school, contact the Title IX Coordinator to request "supportive measures."
   - Be aware that a "formal complaint" is typically required for a full investigation, but supportive measures (like schedule changes) are often available without one.
8. **Contact local law enforcement if the behavior involves threats, stalking, or physical touch.**
   - Call 911 for immediate emergencies or if you are being followed.
   - Use the non-emergency line to report a history of persistent harassment or to create a paper trail for a future protective order.
9. **Reach out for confidential, specialized support through RAINN.** You can call the National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800-656-4673 (800-656-HOPE) for 24/7 help in navigating your options.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to cancel your membership or switch gyms permanently.
- You do not need to file an Office for Civil Rights (OCR) complaint immediately; you generally have 180 calendar days from the incident to do so.
- You do not need to respond to any apologies or "check-in" messages from the person.
- You do not need to have a perfect memory of every past comment to report today's behavior.

## Important reassurance
Physical and emotional responses such as freezing, nervous laughter, or "people-pleasing" are common automatic reactions to high-stress or threatening situations. These responses do not mean you consented to the behavior or that you "waited too long" to seek help. Taking action now is a valid step to regain your sense of safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction for persistent sexualization and harassment in fitness environments. It does not replace legal advice or long-term advocacy, which may be needed if the behavior continues.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or are being stalked, contact 911 or your local law enforcement agency.
