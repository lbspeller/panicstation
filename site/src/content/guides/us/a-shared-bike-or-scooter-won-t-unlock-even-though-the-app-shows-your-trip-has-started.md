# What to do if… a shared bike or scooter won’t unlock even though the app shows your trip has started (USA)

## Short answer
Do not force the bike, scooter, dock, or lock. Save the active-trip screen, vehicle ID, and location, then use the operator’s app support to ask for the trip to be closed and any charge to be reviewed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pry, kick, drag, lift, or force the vehicle or lock.
- Do not keep troubleshooting while standing in traffic, a bike lane, a driveway, or a curb ramp.
- Do not start another rental until you have checked whether the first trip is still active.
- Do not delete the app, screenshots, or support messages while the trip issue is unresolved.
- Do not assume that you need to diagnose the cause on the street.

## What to do now
1. Move to a safer nearby spot before using your phone. Keep out of traffic, bike lanes, driveways, and curb ramps.

2. Save the key details:
   - Take a screenshot of the active-trip screen and any error message.
   - Photograph the vehicle ID or number near the QR code.
   - Photograph the lock, dock, or vehicle position without moving or forcing anything.
   - Note the street address, intersection, station name, or map pin.

3. Use only the controls shown in the operator’s app:
   - Check for **End Ride**, **Cancel**, **Help**, or **Report a Problem**.
   - If the app shows a lock or unlock control for the active trip, follow the on-screen instructions and try it once.
   - If a docked bike does not release or register correctly, follow the dock prompt or indicator light. Do not pull or push harder.

4. Contact the operator through the trip screen or in-app support while the details are still visible. Send the screenshot, vehicle ID, location, and a short message such as: “The trip started in the app, but the vehicle did not unlock.”

5. Ask the operator to close the active trip and review any charge. Save the support ticket, chat, or confirmation screen.

6. Stop trying to unlock the vehicle after one or two no-force attempts. If the vehicle is blocking a sidewalk, curb ramp, bike lane, driveway, or roadway, report the obstruction through the operator’s app or the local non-emergency reporting route where one exists.

7. If a charge remains after the operator has reviewed the trip, save your records and act promptly:
   - If you paid by credit card, contact the card issuer and ask how to dispute the charge.
   - If you paid by debit card, contact the debit card company, often your bank or credit union, and ask what dispute options apply.
   - Use the official consumer resources below for the relevant payment method.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the cause was the app, the lock, the dock, the battery, or the network.
- You do not need to decide about a formal payment dispute while you are still on the street.
- You do not need to keep testing other vehicles while the first trip may still be active.
- You can review any operator reply and payment record later from a safer, calmer place.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to make the vehicle work by force or keep troubleshooting on the street. Saving the trip screen, vehicle ID, location, and support record gives the operator the useful details needed to review the problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about a refund, card dispute, or local obstruction report may need help from the operator, your card issuer or bank, or the relevant local service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. If your surroundings do not feel safe, move to a safer place before dealing with the app or support messages.
