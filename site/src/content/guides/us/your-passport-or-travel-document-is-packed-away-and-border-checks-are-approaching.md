# What to do if… your passport or travel document is packed away and border checks are approaching (USA)

## Short answer
Tell the conductor or driver now that your passport is packed away and ask where and when you can retrieve it safely. Get the original document you plan to present for your route into your hand or a secure easy-access pocket before inspection begins.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t unpack in a moving aisle, doorway, or stairwell.
- Don’t block an exit or leave your luggage unattended while searching.
- Don’t hand your passport or ID to another passenger to hold or pass along.
- Don’t assume that a phone photo or copy of your passport will be accepted.
- Don’t switch to a different ID unless staff or the guidance for your service confirms that it is accepted for your circumstances.

## What to do now
1. Tell the conductor or driver: “My passport or travel document is in my luggage. Where can I retrieve it safely before inspection?”
2. Identify the most likely bag and compartment. Do one focused search at your seat or in the area staff direct you to use.
3. Retrieve the original travel document you planned to present for your route. Do not switch to a passport card or another ID unless you know it is accepted for your service and circumstances.
4. Keep any visa, residence document, or status document that you already know your route requires with your travel document. Keep your ticket or booking details easy to reach as well.
5. If the bag is stored overhead, in a luggage rack, or under a coach, ask staff when it is safe to access it. Do not try to move bags while the vehicle is moving if staff tell you to wait.
6. Be ready to follow staff instructions at the border. Some cross-border services require passengers to step off with their documents and belongings for inspection.
7. If you cannot locate the document after one focused search, tell staff promptly. Say: “I cannot find my travel document yet. What is the next step for this service?”
8. If inspection begins before you have the document in hand, stay calm and tell the official that it is in your luggage. Ask whether you may retrieve it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganize all your luggage now.
- You do not need to decide how to pack differently on a future trip.
- You do not need to make wider follow-on calls before telling onboard staff and completing one focused search.

## Important reassurance
A packed-away document is a practical problem. Telling staff early, retrieving it safely, and keeping it easy to reach are the useful next steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next inspection only. If the document cannot be found or is not accepted for the route, later help may be needed from the carrier, the relevant border authority, or your embassy or consulate if applicable.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate practical next steps, not legal, immigration, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Document requirements and inspection procedures vary by route, citizenship, destination, and carrier.
