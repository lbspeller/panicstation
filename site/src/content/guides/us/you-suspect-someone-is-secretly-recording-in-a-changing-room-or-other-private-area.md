# What to do if… you suspect someone is secretly recording in a changing room or other private area (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately leave the private area for a public, staffed location and report the incident to the manager or security so they can secure the scene and call the police.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the suspect directly or attempt to seize their device.
- Do not touch or move any suspected hidden camera device if you have found one.
- Do not stay in the private area to investigate or "catch them in the act".
- Do not delete any evidence if the recording was sent to you or found on your device.
- Do not share suspected images or videos with third parties to "prove" the incident.
- Do not ignore the suspicion because you are worried about being wrong.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a public, staffed location immediately.** Exit the restroom, changing room, or locker room and go to a front desk, checkout counter, or security station.
2. **Alert management or security using clear language.** **State that you believe a crime is occurring or has occurred in the private area.**
   - "I suspect someone is secretly recording in the changing rooms."
   - "I found what appears to be a hidden camera in the restroom."
3. **Request that the venue secure the specific area.** **Ask them to stop other people from entering the room and to keep the area undisturbed until police arrive.**
   - Do not go back into the room yourself; stay in the public area with a staff member.
4. **Call 911 if the incident is in progress or you feel unsafe.** **Use emergency services if the suspect is still present, threatening you, or if you found a physical device that needs immediate police recovery.**
   - If the suspect has already left and there is no immediate danger, call the local police non-emergency line for the city or county where you are located.
5. **Request the preservation of venue evidence.** **Ask the manager to preserve all security camera footage from the hallways or entrances to the private area, as well as any electronic access logs (keycard data).**
6. **Contact RAINN for confidential support (optional).** **If you want to talk through the experience with a trained specialist without immediate reporting pressure, use the National Sexual Assault Hotline.**
   - Call 800-656-HOPE (4673)
   - Text "HOPE" to 64673
7. **Document the basic facts while they are fresh.** **Briefly write down the time, the exact location (e.g., "third stall from the left"), and a description of any person or device you saw.**
   - Include clothing, physical features, or any bags/equipment the suspect was carrying.
   - Note the name and title of the venue staff member who took your report.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific model or type of camera.
- You do not need to decide right now if you want to file a civil lawsuit.
- You do not need to review any footage yourself if a device is recovered.
- You do not need to contact your own lawyer or the corporate headquarters today.

## Important reassurance
Feeling violated, anxious, or unsure is a normal reaction to a breach of privacy. You are not required to have "proof" before asking for help; the responsibility for investigating and confirming the recording lies with law enforcement and the venue management.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for suspected voyeurism in the USA. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, civil litigation, or technical methods for sweeping rooms for electronic devices.

## Important note
Secretly recording individuals in areas where they have a reasonable expectation of privacy (such as restrooms or dressing rooms) is a crime under the federal Video Voyeurism Prevention Act and various state laws. This guide is for informational purposes and is not a substitute for legal advice or a formal police investigation.
