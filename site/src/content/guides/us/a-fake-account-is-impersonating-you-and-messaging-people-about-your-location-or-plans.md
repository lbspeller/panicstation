# What to do if… a fake account is impersonating you and messaging people about your location or plans (USA)

## Short answer
Pause somewhere that feels safer and tell one trusted person what is happening. If you believe someone is on the way to confront you, or anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t engage with the impersonating account. Contact can reveal information or draw you into further messages.
- Don’t post your real-time location, travel plans, or routine details while this is active.
- Don’t click links, open files, or scan QR codes sent by the account or by anyone claiming to offer support.
- Don’t delete the messages or profile details before saving a record.
- Don’t ask friends to argue publicly with the account.
- Don’t assume the situation is harmless because the contact is online.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.**
   - Avoid going to a location mentioned by the account until you have had time to assess the situation.
   - Tell one trusted person where you are and ask them to stay available.
   - If you believe someone is on the way to confront you, you receive a threat that creates an immediate danger, or anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.

2. **Save a record without replying.**
   - Save the profile name, username, profile URL where possible, messages about your location or plans, and visible timestamps.
   - Use screenshots where it feels safe to do so.
   - If you think a screenshot could alert the account, use a trusted second device to photograph the screen instead.
   - Ask anyone who received messages to save a record before blocking the account.

3. **Reduce information exposure.**
   - Temporarily make relevant accounts private where the platform allows it.
   - Hide or remove recent posts, stories, or public details that reveal your location, plans, or routine.
   - Turn off location tagging for new posts where applicable.

4. **Check account security.**
   - If you think someone else may have access to your device or accounts, use a device you trust where possible.
   - If account compromise is possible, start with the email account used for account recovery.
   - Use a new, unique password and turn on multi-factor authentication.
   - Review signed-in devices, active sessions, and recovery details where available. Sign out devices you do not recognise.
   - Review your social-media and messaging accounts next.

5. **Report the impersonating account through the platform.**
   - Use the in-app report option or the platform’s official help page.
   - Choose the option for impersonation or an account pretending to be someone else.
   - Ask contacts who received messages to report and block the account after saving a record.
   - Use official app or help-centre routes rather than links sent in messages.

6. **Warn contacts briefly.**
   - Send one message from a channel you control:
     - “A fake account is impersonating me and messaging people. Please don’t reply or share information. Save a record, then report and block the account.”
   - Ask contacts not to share your current location or plans.

7. **Consider a local report if the safety concern continues.**
   - For immediate danger, call 911.
   - Otherwise, consider contacting your local police non-emergency number if the messages include threats, repeated unwanted contact, or details about where you are or plan to be.
   - Say that an impersonating account is messaging people about your location or plans and that you have saved messages and profile details.

8. **Use a federal reporting route when it fits the situation.**
   - Use IdentityTheft.gov if personal information has been misused or you are dealing with identity theft.
   - Consider filing an IC3 complaint for cyber-enabled crime, including an impersonation account used for scams or threats.
   - Use ReportFraud.ftc.gov if the account is asking people for money, personal information, or other scam-related action.

## What can wait
- Posting a long public explanation.
- Trying to identify the person behind the account.
- Perfecting every privacy setting across every app.
- Deciding whether to take longer-term legal steps.
- Responding to every contact individually.

## Important reassurance
This can feel destabilising, especially when messages mention your location or plans. You do not need to solve everything at once: create a safer pause, save a record, reduce exposure, secure your accounts, and use official reporting routes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the situation continues, later decisions may need specialist help and a longer safety plan.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone is in immediate danger, call 911.
