# What to do if… your rescue inhaler is not helping your breathing symptoms (USA)

## Short answer
Sit upright and follow your asthma action plan. If your rescue medicine is not relieving symptoms, or breathing is still very hard, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait to “see if it passes” if your breathing is getting worse or the inhaler is not helping.
- Do not force yourself to lie flat if sitting upright helps you breathe.
- Do not take repeated extra doses beyond what your asthma action plan or prescription says.
- Do not try to drive yourself if you are in significant breathing distress.
- Do not assume it is “just anxiety” if your rescue inhaler usually works and suddenly does not.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and sit upright.** Keep your phone with you. If someone is nearby, tell them you are having breathing trouble and may need emergency help.
2. **Use your rescue inhaler exactly as your asthma action plan or prescription says.** If you have a written plan with green, yellow, and red zones, go to the worsening-symptoms section.
3. **Check for simple inhaler problems without delaying help.**
   - Make sure it is the rescue inhaler, not a controller inhaler.
   - Check the dose counter if it has one.
   - Check that the canister is seated and the mouthpiece is not blocked.
   - Use a spacer or valved holding chamber if you have one and your plan or prescription uses it.
4. **Call 911 now if any of these are happening:**
   - Your medicine is not relieving symptoms during an asthma attack.
   - Breathing is still very hard after using rescue medicine.
   - You have trouble walking or talking because of shortness of breath.
   - Your lips or fingernails look blue, pale, or gray.
   - You feel confused, drowsy, faint, or unable to stay alert.
   - You are working hard to breathe, breathing very fast, or someone can see your ribs, neck, or chest pulling in as you breathe.
5. **If your asthma action plan says you are in the red zone, treat it as urgent.** Follow the red-zone medicine instructions on your own plan. Some action-plan templates say to get emergency help if you are still in the red zone after about 15 minutes, but your own plan may be different.
6. **If symptoms improve but this was unusual for you, contact your clinician.** Ask what to do next about your inhaler, technique, action plan, and whether you need to be seen.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact trigger right now.
- You do not need to decide long-term medication changes right now unless your written asthma plan already tells you to.
- You do not need to clean devices, compare inhalers, or look up causes until you are breathing comfortably and safe.

## Important reassurance
It is frightening when a rescue inhaler does not work the way you expect. Getting help early for breathing trouble is appropriate, and it is safer than trying to push through.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment symptoms are not responding as usual. Later decisions about diagnosis, medication changes, or a new asthma action plan may need help from a healthcare professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice for your specific condition. If you are in danger or your breathing is not improving, seek emergency care immediately.
