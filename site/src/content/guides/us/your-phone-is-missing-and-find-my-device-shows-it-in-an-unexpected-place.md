# What to do if… your phone is missing and “Find my device” shows it in an unexpected place (USA)

## Short answer
Connect the device to a computer using an official cable and use the manufacturer's software to attempt an "Update" rather than a "Restore." For newer iPhones, you may also attempt a wireless "Restore Nearby Device" if another iOS 18+ device is available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not select **Restore** or **Factory Reset** as your first option, as this will erase all data that is not backed up.
- Do not use third-party "repair" software from unofficial websites; stick to Apple, Google, or Samsung tools.
- Do not disconnect the cable once a process has started, even if the progress bar seems frozen.
- Do not repeatedly force-restart the device while it is connected to the computer, as this can corrupt the file system further.
- Do not use a USB hub or keyboard port; plug the cable directly into the computer's motherboard or chassis port.

## What to do now
1. **Attempt a wireless restore if you have a compatible iPhone.** 
   - If using an iPhone 15 or newer running iOS 18+, place it near another unlocked iPhone or iPad.
   - Look for the **Restore Nearby iPhone** prompt on the secondary device and follow the six-digit code instructions.
2. **Connect the phone to a computer using a high-quality USB cable.** 
   - Use a Mac with Finder (macOS Catalina or later) or a Windows PC with the Apple Devices app or iTunes.
   - For Android, use a Chrome-based browser for the Pixel Repair Tool or the Samsung Smart Switch desktop application.
3. **Identify the device state to trigger the correct software response.** 
   - If the screen shows a "No Command" message (Android), hold the **Power** button and press **Volume Up** once to reveal the menu.
   - If the screen is black but the computer detects a "device in recovery," proceed with the computer-based prompts.
4. **Choose the Update option to preserve your data.** 
   - On the computer prompt, always select **Update** first; this attempts to reinstall the operating system without wiping user files.
   - Only select **Restore** if the **Update** fails multiple times and you have confirmed your data is backed up to iCloud or Google.
5. **Verify your account credentials before performing a full restore.** 
   - Ensure you know your Apple Account (formerly Apple ID) or Google Account password.
   - Factory resetting a device in recovery mode will trigger "Activation Lock," requiring these credentials to use the phone again.
6. **Check your cloud backup status from a different device.** 
   - Log into icloud.com or photos.google.com to see the date of your last successful sync.
   - If your critical data is missing from the cloud, stop and contact a professional data recovery service or an authorized provider like Geek Squad or an Apple Store.
7. **Clean the charging port if the computer fails to recognize the phone.** 
   - Use a non-metallic tool (like a plastic toothpick) to gently remove lint from the port.
   - Try a different USB port on the computer or a different official cable if the connection is intermittent.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the update failed (e.g., storage full, hardware glitch) right now.
- You do not need to buy a new phone until you have attempted a professional "Device Initialization" at a service center.
- You do not need to worry about carrier service or SIM cards; these are generally unaffected by recovery mode.

## Important reassurance
Recovery mode is a standard safety feature designed to prevent your phone from becoming permanently unusable after a software error. In most cases, the operating system can be reinstalled without losing your photos or messages as long as you choose the "Update" path on your computer.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for software-related recovery loops. If the phone was recently dropped or exposed to water, these steps may not work, and hardware repair will be required.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not replace professional technical support from Apple, Google, or Samsung. Performing a restore will erase all data on the device. Ensure you are using the most recent version of your computer's operating system and recovery apps before beginning.
