# What to do if… you develop new weakness in both legs or worsening trouble walking over a few hours (USA)

## Short answer
Call **911** now and say that you have new weakness in both legs or worsening trouble walking that has developed over a few hours. Do not drive yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Do **not** keep walking, use stairs, or repeatedly stand up to test your legs.
- Do **not** drive yourself.
- Do **not** delay the 911 call by going to urgent care first.
- Do **not** ignore new bladder or bowel problems, numbness around the groin or buttocks, spreading weakness, trouble breathing, or trouble swallowing.
- If swallowing feels unsafe, tell the 911 dispatcher before eating or drinking.
- Do **not** cancel the 911 call if the symptoms improve while you wait.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 for an ambulance.**
   - Say: **“I have new weakness in both legs or worsening trouble walking that has developed over a few hours.”**
2. **Sit or lie down somewhere stable while you wait.**
   - Avoid walking and stairs.
   - Keep your phone with you.
   - If you are alone, unlock the front door if it is safe to do so.
3. **Tell the dispatcher immediately if you have any of these symptoms.**
   - Sudden loss of balance, face drooping, arm weakness, speech trouble, vision changes, or a sudden severe headache.
   - New inability to urinate, loss of the usual urge to urinate, loss of bladder or bowel control, or numbness around the groin, genitals, anus, or buttocks.
   - Severe back pain, a recent fall or injury, weakness spreading upward, trouble breathing, or trouble swallowing.
4. **Note when the symptoms started or clearly became worse.**
   - Write down the time or save it in your phone. This may help the emergency team, especially if stroke is possible.
5. **Gather a brief medical snapshot if it is easy to do without delaying help.**
   - Note your medicines, allergies, major medical conditions, recent infections, and recent falls or injuries.
6. **Follow the 911 dispatcher’s instructions.**
   - If an ambulance cannot be sent, ask the dispatcher what to do next.
   - Do not drive yourself.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to work out whether this is a pinched nerve, a back problem, or something else.
- You do **not** need to keep testing your strength by standing or walking.
- You do **not** need to pack extensively or organise routine tasks before calling 911.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to second-guess yourself when symptoms are unfamiliar. Calling 911 for new weakness in both legs or worsening trouble walking over a few hours is appropriate because some possible causes need urgent assessment, and prompt care may reduce the risk of lasting harm.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Emergency clinicians may recommend a neurological examination, tests, imaging, or specialist assessment depending on your symptoms.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not a diagnosis and is not medical or professional advice.
