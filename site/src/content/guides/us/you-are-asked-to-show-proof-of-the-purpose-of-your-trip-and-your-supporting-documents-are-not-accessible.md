# What to do if… you are asked to show proof of the purpose of your trip and your supporting documents are not accessible (USA)

## Short answer
State the technical or physical barrier to the officer immediately and provide all verifiable verbal details regarding your itinerary, accommodation, and contacts. Request permission to use a charging station or airport Wi-Fi if the documents are stored electronically, but follow all instructions if access is denied.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide estimated or "filler" details if you are unsure of specific facts like addresses or dates.
- Do not attempt to access your phone or bags once an officer has instructed you to stop or has taken the device for inspection.
- Do not argue, use sarcasm, or make jokes regarding the inspection process or border security.
- Do not delete browser history, messages, or apps while waiting in the inspection area.
- Do not sign Form I-407 (Record of Abandonment of Lawful Permanent Resident Status) if you are a Green Card holder without understanding that it voluntarily ends your residency.

## What to do now
1. **Explain the access issue clearly to the primary officer.**  
   **Identify the specific reason the documents are unavailable.**
   - Dead battery or lack of a charger.
   - No cellular signal or Wi-Fi connectivity.
   - Forgotten password or locked account.
   - Physical documents left in checked luggage or at home.
2. **Request a brief "contained fix" for electronic access.**  
   **Ask the officer if you may use a charging station or connect to the airport Wi-Fi to retrieve the specific files.** 
   - Accept that this request is often denied in secure areas. 
   - If denied, do not persist; move to verbal verification.
3. **Provide all verifiable trip data from memory.**  
   **Recite the core facts of your visit to demonstrate consistency with your visa or ESTA.**
   - The full physical address and telephone number of where you will stay.
   - The full names and contact numbers of people you are visiting.
   - Your return or onward flight number, airline, and date.
   - Your specific plan for funding the trip (e.g., specific credit cards or bank apps if accessible).
4. **Offer a third-party verification method.**  
   **Provide the phone number of a host, employer, or family member who can answer a call from CBP to confirm your arrangements.**
   - Ensure the contact is available and aware of your travel plans.
   - Be aware that officers are not required to make these calls but may do so at their discretion.
5. **Follow all instructions if referred to secondary inspection.**  
   **Move to the designated waiting area and wait for your name or number to be called.**
   - Secondary inspection is used when primary officers cannot verify admissibility immediately.
   - Expect longer wait times while officers run database checks or contact external parties.
6. **Understand your rights and obligations regarding device searches.**  
   **Note that under 2026 Directive 3340-049B, CBP may conduct a "Basic Search" of information resident on your device without individualized suspicion.**
   - An "Advanced Search" (using external equipment to copy or analyze data) requires reasonable suspicion and supervisory approval.
   - CBP may not intentionally use your device to access data stored solely in the cloud.
   - Non-U.S. citizens may be denied admission for refusing to unlock a device; U.S. citizens cannot be denied entry but may have their device seized for later inspection.
7. **Ask for Form I-515A if the issue is a missing official form.**  
   **If you are a student (F/J) or scholar missing a specific required document like an I-20 or DS-2019, ask the officer if they will issue Form I-515A.**
   - This form allows for 30 days of temporary admission to provide the missing documentation to the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP).
   - This is discretionary and not guaranteed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve your long-term phone or account issues while in the terminal.
- You do not need to re-book flights or hotels unless an officer explicitly states your current ones are invalid or rejected.
- You do not need to provide a complete history of every past trip unless specifically asked.

## Important reassurance
Technical failures such as dead batteries or locked cloud accounts are common during international travel. CBP officers encounter these issues daily. Providing consistent, verifiable verbal information is often sufficient for officers to perform their own database checks to confirm your flight and status.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document access issues at a U.S. port of entry. It does not cover legal defense for visa fraud or procedures following a formal denial of admission.

## Important note
Admissibility is determined on a case-by-case basis by the inspecting officer. 2026 enforcement priorities, including "Meritless Travel" screening, have increased scrutiny on remote work and frequency of visits. If you are denied entry, ask for a written record of the decision and the specific section of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) cited.
