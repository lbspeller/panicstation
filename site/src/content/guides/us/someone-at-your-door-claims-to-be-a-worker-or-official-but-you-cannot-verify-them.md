# What to do if… someone at your door claims to be a worker or official but you cannot verify them (USA)

## Short answer
Keep the door closed and verify the claim using contact information you find independently. Call 911 if there is immediate danger, a threat, or an attempted forced entry.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door just because the visitor has a uniform, badge, clipboard, or vehicle with a logo.
- Do not let an unverified visitor inside because they say the situation is urgent.
- Do not use a phone number, link, or contact detail supplied by the visitor to verify the claim.
- Do not share your date of birth, Social Security number, banking details, account logins, or security codes at the door.
- Do not hand over money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or payment information to avoid a claimed disconnection, fine, or penalty.
- Do not step outside to walk with the visitor if you feel uncertain or pressured.
- Do not argue, confront the visitor, or try to physically block them.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Keep the door closed and locked. Speak through the door, a doorbell camera, or another method that does not require opening the door.
2. **Use a simple boundary line.** Say: “I am going to verify this independently before opening the door.” You do not need to keep explaining or debating.
3. **Ask for basic details only if it feels safe.** Ask for the visitor’s name, organization, reason for the visit, and any work-order number. You can ask them to show identification to a camera or window, but do not move closer if that feels unsafe. Treat a badge or ID as unverified until you check independently.
4. **Contact the claimed organization yourself.**
   - For a utility company, call the number on your bill or use the contact details on the utility’s official website.
   - For a city or county agency, use the main number on the official government website and ask whether the visit can be verified.
   - For law enforcement, call the agency using a publicly listed number and ask how to verify the claim safely. Call 911 instead if there is immediate danger.
5. **Respond cautiously to a claimed hazard.** Do not open the door because of urgency alone. If you smell gas, see smoke, hear an alarm, or notice another sign of immediate danger, move to a safer place using a safe route if you can. Call 911 for an immediate danger. For a possible utility issue, you can also call the utility emergency line from a safer place.
6. **Choose the right police contact route.**
   - Call 911 if the visitor threatens you, tries to force entry, or creates an immediate danger.
   - If the visitor refuses to leave but there is no immediate danger, use a publicly listed contact route for your local law enforcement agency, such as its non-emergency number if one is available, and ask what to do.
7. **If the visitor is already inside, prioritize distance.** Move to a safer place if you can do so without confronting them. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger. Do not try to physically block the visitor.
8. **After the immediate situation is over, record the basics.** Note the time, the claimed organization, the visitor’s description, and any vehicle details you noticed safely. Save doorbell-camera or security-camera footage if available.
9. **Report a suspected scam when you are ready.** Contact the organization the visitor claimed to represent using independently verified contact details. You can also report suspected fraud through ReportFraud.ftc.gov. If personal information was exposed or may have been misused, use IdentityTheft.gov for next steps.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether the visitor was genuine or fake.
- You do not need to investigate, confront anyone, or gather every detail while the visitor is still there.
- You do not need to make a payment, sign anything, agree to work, or share personal information at the door.
- You can deal with reports, footage, and follow-up calls after you are safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to keep the door closed while you check an unexpected visitor’s claim. Taking a pause is not rude. If there are signs of immediate danger, focus on moving to a safer place and getting emergency help rather than trying to verify the visitor at the door.

## Scope note
These are first steps for immediate safety and for avoiding irreversible mistakes. Later decisions may need help from the claimed organization, your local law enforcement agency, your bank when relevant, or a scam-reporting service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call 911 if you need immediate assistance from police, the fire department, or an ambulance.
