# What to do if… you arrive at a station and the ticket gates will not accept your valid ticket or pass (USA)

## Short answer
Step out of the flow of traffic, keep your fare media in hand, and locate a station agent or press the Help Point/intercom button for staff assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not jump over the turnstile, crawl under the gate, or force an emergency exit door.
- Do not follow another rider through the gate ("tailgating") as this may trigger an alarm or fine.
- Do not discard your ticket, card, or receipt, as these are required for staff to verify your payment.
- Do not attempt to pay with a second card or mobile device until you have verified the status of the first attempt.
- Do not block the gate while troubleshooting; move to a side area to allow other passengers to pass.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a different gate within the same bank.** Card readers are individual units and one may be malfunctioning while others remain operational.
2. **Perform a single, controlled retry of your fare media.**
   - **For physical tap cards:** Remove the card from your wallet to avoid "card clash" (the reader picking up multiple NFC signals). Hold the card flat against the target for two full seconds.
   - **For mobile devices (Apple/Google Wallet):** Ensure "Express Transit" or "Fast Fare" mode is enabled in your settings. If the screen is on, hold the top edge of the phone (where the NFC antenna is located) directly against the reader.
   - **For paper/barcode tickets:** Increase phone brightness to 100% for digital barcodes. For paper tickets, ensure the magnetic stripe is clean and facing the direction indicated by the arrows on the gate.
3. **Check the reader's display for specific error messages.** Look for text such as "See Agent," "Insufficient Funds," "Expired," or "Already Used" (which indicates an incomplete tap-out from a previous journey).
4. **Locate the station agent or Help Point.** Look for a staffed booth near the entrance. If the station is unstaffed, find the pillar or gate-mounted intercom (often marked with an "i" or "Help") and explain that your valid fare is being rejected.
5. **Request a manual gate release if you are stuck in the paid area.** Inform staff that you are attempting to exit and the gate is not recognizing your entry tap; they can often release the gate and provide instructions on how to clear the "incomplete trip" error for your next ride.
6. **Ask for a "Status Check" at a ticket vending machine.** If an agent is not available, most modern machines allow you to tap or insert your card to see the remaining balance, expiration date, and last used station, which can identify if the card is technically "bricked."
7. **Document the incident for a future refund claim.** Note the station name, gate number (usually printed on the metal housing), the exact time, and the specific error message displayed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the underlying account issue or balance discrepancy while standing at the gate.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or refund request until you have completed your journey and are in a safe, stationary location.
- You do not need to replace a physically damaged card immediately if the station agent is able to grant you a one-time entry or exit.

## Important reassurance
Fare gate malfunctions are common technical occurrences caused by software lag, sensor interference, or card expiration. Station staff are trained to handle these errors, and using the intercom to request assistance is the standard, legal procedure for resolving a transit access issue.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and station-level troubleshooting for rejected fare media. It does not cover long-term account recovery, legal disputes regarding transit fines, or procedures for lost or stolen cards.

## Important note
Transit agency policies regarding gate malfunctions vary significantly by city. Following staff instructions is mandatory to avoid citations for fare evasion. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice regarding transit regulations.
