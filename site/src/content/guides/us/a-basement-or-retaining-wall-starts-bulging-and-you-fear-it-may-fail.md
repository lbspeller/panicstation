# What to do if… a basement or retaining wall starts bulging and you fear it may fail (USA)

## Short answer
Evacuate the immediate area and establish a safety perimeter at least 1.5 times the height of the wall. Call 911 if you hear snapping noises, see active shifting, or if a collapse would threaten life or public infrastructure.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t enter the basement or stand near the base of the wall to "investigate" cracks or measure the bulge.
- Don’t attempt to brace the wall yourself using jacks, posts, or DIY shoring.
- Don’t park vehicles or place heavy equipment on the ground above a bulging basement or retaining wall.
- Don’t remove soil from the base or dig behind the wall, as this can trigger an immediate collapse.
- Don’t ignore "popping" or "snapping" sounds, which may indicate structural fasteners or mortar joints are failing.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the danger zone immediately and keep all people and pets away.** 
   - Establish a perimeter that is at least 1.5 times the height of the wall in all directions.
   - For retaining walls, stay away from both the top edge (collapse/slide risk) and the bottom (crush risk).
2. **Call 911 if the wall is actively moving or if collapse is imminent.**
   - Use 911 if you see falling debris, hear loud structural noises, or if the wall is leaning toward a neighbor's property or a public sidewalk.
3. **Notify the local Building Official or Code Enforcement department.** 
   - Tell them you have an "unsafe structural condition" involving a bulging wall and request an emergency safety inspection.
   - If this occurs after hours and you believe the structure is unsafe for the public, contact the non-emergency police line.
4. **Identify and isolate utility hazards from a safe distance.**
   - Watch for strained gas lines, sparking electrical wires, or broken water pipes near the wall.
   - Contact your utility providers' emergency lines if you suspect a leak or see downed wires; do not attempt to turn off valves located in the danger zone.
5. **Contact a licensed structural engineer for an emergency evaluation.** 
   - Search specifically for "structural engineer" rather than a general contractor to ensure an unbiased safety assessment.
   - Ask for a "Condition Assessment" to determine if the building is safe for continued occupancy.
6. **Limit vibration and weight on the structure.** 
   - Avoid using heavy machinery, lawn tractors, or vibrating equipment near the affected area.
   - If the bulge is in a basement, do not use the rooms directly above the failing wall.
7. **Document the movement only if it can be done from a safe distance.** 
   - Take photos that show the entire wall and its relation to the house or slope.
   - Note the time and weather conditions, such as recent heavy rainfall or rapid snowmelt.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the cause is hydrostatic pressure, expansive soil, or poor drainage yet.
- You do not need to choose a repair method (such as carbon fiber straps or wall anchors) until the engineer provides a report.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company until the site is physically stabilized.
- You do not need to obtain construction permits until the emergency safety phase is over.

## Important reassurance
It is common for structural issues to manifest suddenly after weather events. Taking the immediate step of creating physical distance is the most effective way to prevent injury. A wall that has moved significantly is a technical problem for experts, and your primary responsibility is maintaining the safety perimeter.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and hazard avoidance only. Long-term repairs, legal liability for retaining walls, and insurance claims require professional consultation following the initial safety assessment.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not replace an in-person evaluation by a licensed structural engineer or local building official. If life safety is at risk, prioritize evacuation and emergency services.
