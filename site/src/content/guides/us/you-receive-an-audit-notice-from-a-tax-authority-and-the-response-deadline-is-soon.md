# What to do if… you receive an audit notice from a tax authority and the response deadline is soon (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the notice's authenticity through your official IRS Online Account immediately, then call the specific phone number listed on the top right of the notice to request a 30-day extension before the deadline passes.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the deadline even if you lack the requested documents.
- Do not assume a notice is legitimate based on the logo alone; scammers frequently spoof IRS headers.
- Do not send original documents or receipts to the tax authority.
- Do not file an amended return (Form 1040-X) for the year under audit until you have spoken with the examiner.
- Do not provide information or documents for tax years not specifically listed in the notice.
- Do not sign any "Agreement to Assessment" or waiver of your right to appeal if you do not fully understand the proposed changes.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the notice through the official IRS website before sharing any data.**  
   **Go to the official IRS website and log in to your "IRS Online Account" to see if the notice appears in your digital record.**  
   - If the notice is not visible, use the "Understanding Your IRS Notice or Letter" search tool on IRS.gov to match the notice number (e.g., CP2000, Letter 525) with official templates.  
2. **Locate the specific examiner's phone number on the notice.**  
   **Look at the top right corner of the first page for the "Contact Telephone Number" and the "Employee ID Number" of the person assigned to your case.**  
   - Do not call the general 1-800-829-1040 line unless no specific number is provided, as they may not have access to your specific audit file.  
3. **Call the examiner today to request an extension.**  
   **Ask for an "initial 30-day extension" to gather records or consult with a tax professional.**  
   - State clearly that you intend to cooperate but need more time to ensure a complete and accurate response.  
   - Write down the name and ID number of the person who grants the extension and the new due date they provide.  
4. **Determine the scope of the audit request.**  
   **Identify exactly which line items (e.g., charitable contributions, business mileage, home office) the IRS is questioning.**  
   - Create a physical or digital folder for the specific tax year mentioned.  
   - Match each requested item to a specific piece of evidence, such as a bank statement, canceled check, or receipt.  
5. **Request a "Tax Information Authorization" (Form 8821) if you need help viewing your own records.**  
   **Use Form 8821 if you need a third party (like an accountant) to see your records without giving them full power of attorney yet.**  
   - If you want someone to speak for you and sign documents, use Form 2848 (Power of Attorney) instead.  
6. **Prepare a cover letter and index for your response packet.**  
   **Write a brief cover letter that lists your name, Social Security Number (or ITIN), the tax year, and the notice number.**  
   - Organize your documents with an index that corresponds to the paragraph or item numbers in the IRS letter.  
   - Place your name and taxpayer ID on every single page of the submission.  
7. **Submit the response via a trackable method.**  
   **Use USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested or a designated private delivery service (like FedEx or UPS) that provides proof of delivery to an IRS P.O. Box.**  
   - If the notice provides a specific fax number or an "IRS Document Upload Tool" link, save the transmission confirmation or screenshot the successful upload screen.  

## What can wait
- You do not need to hire an attorney or CPA today, provided you have successfully secured an extension from the IRS.
- You do not need to reconstruct your entire financial history for unrelated tax years.
- You do not need to pay any proposed balance immediately; the audit is a proposal, not a final bill, and you have the right to challenge it.
- You do not need to decide whether to appeal the findings until after the IRS issues a formal "Report of Individual Income Tax Examination Changes."

## Important reassurance
Receiving an audit notice is a procedural step, not a criminal accusation. Most correspondence audits (the most common type) are resolved simply by providing the missing documentation. Requesting an extension is a standard part of the process, and examiners generally grant the first 30-day request if you contact them before the deadline.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization steps for individual federal tax audits (IRS). State tax authority procedures vary by jurisdiction. This guide does not cover criminal tax investigations or "Field Audits" where an agent visits your home or business, which require immediate professional representation.

## Important note
This information is for procedural guidance only and does not constitute legal or tax advice. Deadlines and rules for 2026 may be affected by legislative changes or agency updates. If you are facing significant tax liabilities or potential fraud penalties, consult a qualified tax attorney or Enrolled Agent immediately.
