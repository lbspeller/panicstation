# What to do if… you spot a major printing error on your passport details page shortly before travel (USA)

## Short answer
Apply for a correction immediately using Form DS-5504. If international travel is within 14 calendar days, you must schedule an "Urgent Travel Service" appointment at a regional passport agency instead of mailing the form.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to alter, write on, or tape over any part of the passport data page.
- Do not assume that minor spelling errors will be ignored by airline staff or immigration officers.
- Do not mail your passport for correction if your departure date is within 14 days.
- Do not use a third-party courier service that claims they can guarantee an appointment for you.
- Do not visit a passport agency without a confirmed appointment.

## What to do now
1. **Compare your passport data page against your travel documents.** **Identify any discrepancies in the spelling of your name, your date of birth, place of birth, sex marker, or nationality.**
   - verify that the machine-readable zone at the bottom of the page matches the printed text
   - check that your photo is clear, not discolored, and correctly aligned
2. **Determine your travel timeline to choose the correct service path.** **Use mail-in service if travel is more than 14 days away, or the Urgent Travel Service if it is sooner.**
   - mail-in: use Form DS-5504 and include your current passport, one new photo, and certified evidence of the error
   - urgent: call the National Passport Information Center at 1-877-487-2778 (TDD/TTY: 1-888-874-7793) or use the online appointment system
3. **Download and complete Form DS-5504 for data or printing errors.** **This form is specifically for correcting mistakes made by the Department of State and usually requires no fee if the passport is valid.**
   - ensure you sign and date the form in black ink
   - explain the specific error in the designated section of the application
4. **Gather your certified supporting documents to prove the correct information.** **You must provide a certified original document, such as a birth certificate or marriage license, that shows the correct data.**
   - photocopies or digital scans of these documents are not accepted as primary evidence
   - you must also provide one new 2x2 inch color passport photo with a plain white background
5. **Secure an appointment for Urgent Travel Service if you meet the eligibility criteria.** **Appointments are only available if you are traveling internationally within 14 calendar days or need a foreign visa within 28 days.**
   - have your flight itinerary or proof of travel ready to show the agent
   - call 1-877-487-2778 between 8:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. Eastern Time on weekdays
6. **Contact your airline once you have a document resolution or a scheduled appointment.** **Confirm whether they can update your Secure Flight Passenger Data once your corrected passport is in hand.**
   - ask if they require the new passport number to be updated in the system before check-in
   - avoid canceling your flight until you are certain the document cannot be corrected in time

## What can wait
- Requesting refunds for travel insurance or non-refundable bookings.
- Filing formal complaints about the printing error with the issuing office.
- Updating Global Entry or TSA PreCheck profiles until the new document is physically present.
- Notifying hotels or ground transport of potential delays until your appointment outcome is known.

## Important reassurance
Printing errors are recognized procedural issues with a dedicated correction track. While the timing is stressful, the U.S. Department of State provides specific slots for urgent travelers to rectify government errors. Following the official appointment process is the most reliable way to secure a corrected document before departure.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for identifying and correcting U.S. passport data errors. It does not cover lost or stolen passports, or errors discovered while already outside the United States.

## Important note
This information is based on 2026 Department of State protocols. Individual airline boarding policies and foreign entry requirements vary. Always refer to travel.state.gov for the most current forms and appointment availability.
