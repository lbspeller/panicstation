# What to do if… your car trunk or boot will not open and you need urgent items inside (USA)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, legal place to stop, then try the spare key, mechanical key blade, manufacturer app, or no-damage interior access. If the item is time-critical and you still cannot get in quickly, call roadside assistance or a reputable auto locksmith rather than forcing the trunk.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pry the trunk open, drill the lock, smash a window, or use improvised tools unless there is an immediate safety emergency and no safer help is available.
- Do not keep stopping on shoulders, ramps, dark roadsides, or traffic lanes just to keep trying the release.
- Do not leave children, vulnerable adults, or pets inside the vehicle while you troubleshoot.
- Do not repeatedly press electronic release buttons if the car battery seems weak.
- Do not accept help from a stranger who offers to break into the vehicle or damage the lock.

## What to do now
1. **Make the scene safe first.**  
   Pull into a parking lot, driveway, service station, or other safe place if you can. Put the vehicle in Park, set the parking brake, keep the key with you, and use hazard lights if you are near traffic.

2. **Decide how urgent the item is.**  
   If the item is critical medication, a medical device, ID needed for immediate travel, or safety-critical equipment, treat this as urgent and move quickly to professional help if the first no-damage checks do not work.

3. **Try the fastest no-damage access routes.**
   - Check whether every door, rear hatch, liftgate, and rear window is actually locked.
   - Try the trunk button on the dashboard, driver door, key fob, and trunk lid if your vehicle has more than one release.
   - If your fob has a hidden mechanical key blade, remove it and try the trunk key cylinder or driver-door cylinder.
   - If you have a manufacturer app already set up, try the remote unlock or trunk release feature.
   - If someone can safely bring a spare key or fob, ask them to do that now.

4. **Check for a trunk lockout or valet setting.**
   - Look for a valet switch, trunk lockout button, or cargo access setting in the glove box, center console, driver-door area, or vehicle settings menu.
   - If valet mode is on, turn it off and try the trunk release again.

5. **Try interior access without forcing anything.**
   - Fold down the rear seats if your vehicle allows it.
   - Use a pass-through or cargo opening if there is one.
   - Look for a visible emergency trunk release handle from inside the trunk area if you can reach it safely. Many passenger cars with a separate trunk have one, but hatchbacks, wagons, SUVs, and vehicles with a rear cargo door may not have the same type of trunk compartment.

6. **If the item is urgent and the trunk still will not open, call professional help.**
   - Call roadside assistance through AAA, your insurer, a rental company, or the vehicle manufacturer if you have coverage.
   - Say clearly: “The trunk release has failed, the item inside is urgent, and I need non-destructive access if possible.”
   - If roadside assistance cannot come soon enough, call a reputable auto locksmith. Ask what credentials they have, what proof of ownership they need, whether they can attempt non-destructive trunk access, and the estimated cost before they come out.

7. **Escalate immediately if someone may be trapped or in danger.**
   - If you believe a person is trapped in the trunk, or anyone is in immediate danger, call 911 now and follow the call-taker’s instructions.
   - If there is no immediate danger but you are stranded somewhere unsafe, call roadside assistance first, then consider your local police non-emergency number for location-safety help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause right now, such as the actuator, latch, wiring, fob battery, valet setting, or body alignment.
- You do not need to remove trim panels, disconnect wiring, or attempt a repair in a parking lot.
- You do not need to decide today whether the repair should be done by a dealer, an independent mechanic, or a body shop.
- You do not need to replace the key fob battery before recovering a time-critical item unless it is immediately available and easy to do.

## Important reassurance
A trunk that will not open can feel urgent, but many failures are caused by a lockout setting, weak fob, weak battery, or stuck latch. Moving early to roadside assistance or a reputable auto locksmith is a practical way to protect the urgent item and avoid expensive damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the moment and recover urgent items safely. Later decisions about diagnosis, repair, insurance, rental-car responsibility, or replacement parts may need help from a mechanic, dealer, roadside provider, insurer, or rental company.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, mechanical, or other professional advice. If you are in an unsafe location or anyone’s safety may be at risk, prioritize immediate safety and emergency help.
