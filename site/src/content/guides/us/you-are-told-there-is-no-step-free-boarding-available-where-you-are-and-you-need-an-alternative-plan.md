# What to do if… you are told there is no step-free boarding available where you are and you need an alternative plan (USA)

## Short answer
Do not attempt an unsafe transfer or use stairs. Immediately request a "Reasonable Modification" in the form of alternative accessible transport (shuttle, taxi, or specialized vehicle) which must generally be provided within 30 minutes if the next accessible vehicle is delayed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t allow staff to carry you or your mobility device manually up or down stairs.
- Don’t separate from your wheelchair or mobility aid unless it is your specific preference and is safe to do so.
- Don’t leave the station or stop until you have a confirmed, time-stamped plan for your alternative trip.
- Don’t accept a "wait and see" response if the next accessible vehicle is more than 30 minutes away.
- Don’t feel obligated to explain your medical diagnosis; you only need to state your functional need for step-free access.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe area away from the platform edge or vehicle door to avoid being pressured by crowd flow.** This creates a "safe pause" where you can communicate with transit staff without physical obstruction or time pressure from departing vehicles.
2. **Identify the staff member with dispatch authority or use the station help point.**
   - For local bus or rail: Ask the driver to radio dispatch or locate the station supervisor.
   - For Amtrak: Look for a "Red Cap" assistant, a station agent, or use the "Assisted Boarding" QR codes if you are at a major hub like Chicago Union or Los Angeles Union (2026 pilot program).
3. **State clearly that you require an "accessible alternative" due to the failure of step-free access.** Use the phrase: "The accessible boarding route is unavailable; I need an alternative accessible ride to my destination as required by the ADA."
4. **Ask for a specific alternative transport option if the wait for the next accessible vehicle exceeds 30 minutes.**
   - Request an accessible shuttle or "shadow" vehicle.
   - Ask for a supervisor-authorized taxi or ride-share voucher for an accessible vehicle.
   - Inquire if the agency can dispatch a paratransit vehicle to pick you up at your current location.
5. **Contact Amtrak national customer service if station-level staff cannot provide an immediate solution.** **Call 1-800-USA-RAIL (1-800-872-7245)** and inform the agent that you have a reservation but are being denied step-free boarding at your current station.
6. **Verify the "fare continuity" before you move to the alternative transport.** Ask the supervisor for a "service failure" receipt or a transfer slip so you are not charged a second time for the alternative leg of the journey.
7. **Record the basic facts of the denial while you wait for the alternative.**
   - Note the station name, the time, and the vehicle/train number.
   - Note the specific barrier (e.g., "Elevator #2 out of service" or "Bus ramp failed to deploy").
   - Ask for the name or employee ID of the staff member who provided the alternative plan.
8. **Communicate urgent physical needs if the delay puts you at risk.** If you require temperature control, seating, or access to medication/restrooms while waiting, state this immediately so they can move you to a climate-controlled area.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal DOT or DOJ complaint while you are still at the station.
- You do not need to decide whether you want a refund or a future travel credit right now.
- You do not need to engage in a debate about the technical cause of the equipment failure.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frustrated or overwhelmed when a planned accessible route fails. You are not "causing a problem" by asking for the alternative transport that you are legally entitled to receive. Staying where you are and insisting on a safe, step-free plan is the most effective way to ensure your safety and reach your destination.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate transit access failure. It focuses on getting you from your current location to your destination safely. It does not cover long-term legal remedies or paratransit eligibility applications.

## Important note
Under FTA regulations (49 CFR 37.163), if a boarding lift or ramp fails and the next accessible vehicle is more than 30 minutes away, the provider must provide alternative service. However, specific "on-the-spot" modifications can vary by transit agency. If you are in a situation where your health or safety is immediately threatened by being stranded, contact emergency services at 911.
