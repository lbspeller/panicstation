# What to do if… your bus or train is cancelled at short notice and you may be stranded without a clear alternative (USA)

## Short answer
Relocate to a staffed, well-lit area and confirm a rebooking or a documented refund through the carrier app or agent before spending money on private alternatives.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the station or terminal until you have a confirmed next step or a digital record of the cancellation.
- Do not purchase a new ticket with a different company before checking if the current carrier will re-accommodate you for free.
- Do not wait in isolated, unlit, or unstaffed areas late at night.
- Do not discard your original physical or digital tickets, as these are required for refunds.
- Do not assume that a "cancelled" status on a screen means no staff are available to help.

## What to do now
1. **Identify observable cues of the cancellation and move to safety.**
   Check the departure board for "Cancelled" or "Contact Agent" status, listen for overhead announcements regarding service interruptions, and observe if ticket counters are closing early.
   - If the station is open, stay near the ticket counter or security desk.
   - If the station is closing or unstaffed, move to a nearby open business or hotel lobby.

2. **Access the carrier "Manage Booking" tool immediately.**
   Open the official app or website to check for automatic re-accommodation.
   - **Take screenshots of the cancellation notice and your current ticket.**
   - **Look for a "Modify Trip" or "Change Flight/Bus" button to see available alternatives.**

3. **Request re-accommodation from a staff member if available.**
   Speak to a gate agent or ticket counter representative.
   - **Ask specifically for the next available departure to your destination.**
   - **Inquire if there are alternate routes or "interline" options (other carriers) they can book you on.**
   - **Obtain a printed or digital "Involuntary Cancellation" confirmation.**

4. **Follow carrier-specific refund protocols if travel is impossible.**
   - **For Amtrak:** Cancel before the scheduled departure to avoid "No Show" forfeiture. Flex and Business fares are 100% refundable. Value fares (purchased after April 13, 2026) incur a 30% fee, while Sale fares incur a 50% fee.
   - **For Bus (Greyhound/FlixBus):** You must typically cancel at least 15 minutes before departure. Refunds are usually issued as vouchers. Note that voucher amounts decrease the closer you are to departure (dropping to 20% if within 48 hours).

5. **Secure emergency shelter if stranded overnight.**
   - **Confirm the terminal closing time with staff or security.**
   - **Use a booking app to find the nearest hotel with a 24-hour check-in.**
   - **Contact 911 if you feel physically threatened or are in immediate danger while waiting.**

6. **Notify a reliable contact of your status.**
   Send a concise message including your current location, your safety status, and when you will next provide an update.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal customer service complaint or "feedback" form right now.
- You do not need to negotiate for a cash refund on a voucher-only fare until you have reached a safe destination.
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the mechanical or weather delay.

## Important reassurance
Service disruptions are common and carriers have established protocols for re-accommodation. Feeling overwhelmed is a normal reaction to a sudden change in travel plans. By focusing on your immediate physical safety and securing a digital record of the cancellation, you have already completed the most critical steps to resolving the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the first 60 minutes of a travel disruption. It does not cover long-term legal claims or international travel regulations.

## Important note
Carrier policies regarding refunds and re-accommodation are subject to change and vary by fare type and company. This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Always refer to the specific Terms and Conditions of your ticket.
