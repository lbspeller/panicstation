# What to do if… a long-distance bus or coach breaks down and you are left waiting without clear instructions (USA)

## Short answer
If there is immediate danger, a serious medical emergency, or an immediate threat of violence, call 911. Otherwise, stay in the safest available place, follow the driver’s or emergency responders’ safety instructions, and ask what help is expected and how updates will be given.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk along a highway shoulder or cross traffic lanes to find help unless emergency responders direct you.
- Do not stand close to moving traffic, between the bus and passing vehicles, or near a blind curve.
- Do not try to repair the bus or open luggage compartments unless the driver authorises it.
- Do not accept a ride from a stranger or get into an unverified vehicle.
- Do not use most of your phone battery before you know the location and the current plan.
- Do not argue with the driver while they are handling an immediate safety issue.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate danger.**  
   Call 911 for smoke or fire, a risk that another vehicle may hit the bus, a serious medical emergency, or an immediate threat of violence. Tell the driver immediately about a fuel smell, smoke, or another hazard. Share your location with 911 when asked.

2. **Follow safety instructions and reach the safest available pause.**  
   Follow the driver’s or emergency responders’ instructions first. Do not step into traffic. If you are told to leave the bus, move calmly to the location they identify. If there is immediate danger and no instructions are available, move away from traffic and the source of danger only when you can do so safely.

3. **Ask one clear question about the plan.**  
   Say: “What are we waiting for, and how will you let us know when there is an update?” The answer may be a replacement bus, a mechanic, a tow vehicle, or emergency assistance.

4. **Note where the bus is stopped.**  
   Write down a nearby exit number, mile marker, service plaza, cross-street, or visible landmark. Save a location pin when your phone can do this safely.

5. **Contact the carrier when it is safe to use your phone.**  
   Use the bus company’s official website, app, ticket email, or customer-support channel. Send your route or trip details, ticket confirmation, bus number if visible, location, what you were told, and any urgent needs.

6. **State accessibility or medical needs clearly.**  
   Tell the driver and the company if someone needs a mobility transfer, access to medication, a restroom urgently, or another practical adjustment. Call 911 for a serious medical emergency.

7. **Keep accessible essentials with you and conserve battery.**  
   Keep your ID, wallet, medication, water, warm layers, and charger with you if they are already accessible. Do not open luggage compartments. Ask the driver whether luggage can be accessed safely. Use low-power mode and dim the screen.

8. **Move away from a passenger who is making you feel unsafe.**  
   Move closer to the driver or sit near calm passengers when possible. Call 911 if you believe there is an immediate threat.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about refunds, compensation, or a complaint.
- You do not need to argue about policies at the roadside.
- You can usually wait to decide about alternative travel until you are safe and have clearer information.
- You do not need to solve every missed connection while the immediate plan is still unclear.

## Important reassurance
A breakdown without clear instructions can feel frightening and frustrating. Focus on the next useful facts: whether you are safe, where the bus is stopped, what help is expected, and how updates will be shared.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate period after a breakdown. Later decisions about missed connections, rebooking, refunds, or complaints may depend on the carrier and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Call 911 if there is an emergency.
