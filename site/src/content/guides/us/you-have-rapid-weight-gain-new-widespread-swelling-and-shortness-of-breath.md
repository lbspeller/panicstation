# What to do if… you have rapid weight gain, new widespread swelling, and shortness of breath (USA)

## Short answer
This symptom combination needs urgent in-person medical assessment today. Call 911 now if breathing is difficult at rest, severe, or worsening, or if any emergency sign below is present.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for a routine appointment or overnight improvement before arranging urgent assessment.
- Do not take extra doses of diuretics or use someone else’s medication.
- Do not make large changes to your fluid intake unless a clinician advises it.
- Do not start or increase ibuprofen, naproxen, or similar pain medicines unless a clinician says it is safe.
- Do not drive yourself if breathing is difficult at rest or worsening, or if you feel faint or confused. Call 911.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 now if breathing is difficult at rest, severe, or worsening, or if any other emergency sign is present.**  
   Call for chest pain or pressure; trouble speaking because breathing is difficult; blue lips, fingers, or fingernails; confusion; fainting; or swelling of the face, tongue, or throat. Also call 911 for hives with breathing trouble, dizziness, or fainting.

2. **Get urgent in-person medical assessment today if none of those signs are present.**  
   An Emergency Department is a reasonable place to go for rapid weight gain, new widespread swelling, and shortness of breath over a day or two. Tell the clinician exactly when the swelling and breathing symptoms began.

3. **Stay upright while arranging care.**  
   Sit upright and keep activity minimal. Avoid lying flat if that makes breathing harder.

4. **Write down a brief symptom snapshot.**  
   Note where the swelling is, how quickly your weight changed, whether you are urinating much less than usual, and whether you have chest tightness, wheezing, a fast or pounding heartbeat, fever, recent infection, or new pain or swelling in one leg.

5. **Take your medication information with you.**  
   Bring your medication bottles or photos of the labels, including supplements. Note any recent medication changes and any known heart, kidney, liver, or lung conditions.

6. **If you are pregnant or gave birth within the last year, get medical care right away and say so immediately.**  
   Trouble breathing and extreme swelling can be urgent maternal warning signs. Call 911 if breathing is difficult or you feel faint, confused, or unsafe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide what is causing the swelling before seeking care.
- You do not need to make major diet or fluid changes now.
- You do not need exact measurements. Approximate timing and symptoms are enough to start.

## Important reassurance
Seeking urgent assessment is a reasonable protective step. You do not need to work out the cause before asking for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions require in-person medical assessment and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice, a diagnosis, or a substitute for professional care. Call 911 for severe or worsening symptoms or if you feel unsafe.
