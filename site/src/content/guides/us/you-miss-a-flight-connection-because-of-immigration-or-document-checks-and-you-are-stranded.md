# What to do if… you miss a flight connection because of immigration or document checks and you are stranded (USA)

## Short answer
Go to your airline’s customer service or transfer desk immediately, and use the airline app or phone line at the same time, to ask for rebooking before you leave the terminal or spend money.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the airport or go to the street until you know your rebooking plan and where your checked bags are.
- Don’t buy a brand-new ticket right away if your flights are on one itinerary; ask for the airline’s rebooking offer first.
- Don’t assume there is a guaranteed U.S. hotel or meal entitlement; ask what the airline will provide before you spend.
- Don’t throw away boarding passes, bag tags, receipts, or any CBP paperwork.
- Don’t stop queueing for help just because one channel is slow; keep trying the desk, app, phone, and chat in parallel.

## What to do now
1. **Make sure you are allowed to move on.**
   - If you are still with CBP, TSA, or airline document-check staff, comply with instructions and ask: **“Am I free to go to my airline now?”**
   - If you are not free to go, ask whether you can **contact your airline** from where you are.

2. **Start rebooking immediately through more than one channel.**
   - Walk to the airline’s **customer service, connections, or transfer desk**.
   - At the same time, use the airline app, phone line, or chat and say: **“I missed my connection after immigration or document checks. Please rebook me to my final destination.”**
   - Ask for the new itinerary to be sent to your email or airline app before you leave the desk.

3. **Work out whether this was one itinerary or separate tickets.**
   - **One itinerary, with one booking reference:** ask the airline to rebook you to your final destination.
   - **Separate tickets:** ask the onward airline what missed-flight options, fees, standby options, or same-day change options are available before buying anything else.

4. **Find out where your checked bag is.**
   - When entering the U.S. from overseas, travellers usually need to pick up checked bags for CBP and re-check them for the onward flight, unless airport or airline staff clearly direct otherwise.
   - Ask the airline: **“Where is my checked bag right now?”**
   - Ask: **“Will it be rerouted automatically, or do I need to collect or re-check it?”**
   - If your bag is missing or not travelling with you, ask the airline to create or update a baggage report.

5. **Ask what the airline will cover before you spend.**
   - Ask: **“If the next flight is tomorrow, will you provide meal vouchers, a hotel, or transport?”**
   - If they say no, ask what they can still do: rebooking, standby, partner-airline routing, or a different connecting city.
   - Keep receipts for any food, transport, hotel, toiletries, or essential items you pay for yourself.

6. **Ask about another carrier or route if the next option is much later.**
   - Say: **“Is there any way to put me on another carrier or route to my final destination?”**
   - If they refuse, ask for the next-best routing they can offer and get it in writing in the app, by email, or on a printed itinerary.

7. **Make a quick record without getting stuck on blame.**
   - Take photos of your boarding pass, bag tag, new itinerary, and any airport screen showing the missed or replacement flight.
   - Note the approximate time you joined the immigration, customs, document-check, or re-check line and when you were cleared.

8. **If you are stuck overnight, make the wait safer and simpler.**
   - Stay in a staffed, well-lit area of the airport while you decide next steps.
   - Keep your passport, phone, wallet, medication, chargers, and valuables with you.
   - If you must book accommodation yourself, choose somewhere close and cancellable where possible.

9. **Tell one trusted person where you are.**
   - Send your airport, terminal, airline, and new itinerary or best estimate.
   - Tell them when you expect to update them next.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who was at fault right now.
- You do not need to write a formal complaint tonight.
- You do not need to decide whether to cancel the whole trip until you have heard the airline’s rebooking options.
- You do not need to sort out insurance or refund claims before you are rebooked, have your bags located, and are somewhere safe enough to think.

## Important reassurance
Missing a connection after immigration, customs, or document checks can happen even when you arrived prepared and followed instructions. The immediate job is to get a new route, locate your bags, keep receipts, and make the wait manageable.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first hours after missing the connection. Refund requests, travel insurance claims, formal complaints, and immigration-document problems may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, insurance, or professional travel advice. In the U.S., what an airline provides can depend on the ticket, airline policy, route, and cause of the disruption. If officials are still handling your immigration or document status, follow official instructions first and ask what you are allowed to do next.
