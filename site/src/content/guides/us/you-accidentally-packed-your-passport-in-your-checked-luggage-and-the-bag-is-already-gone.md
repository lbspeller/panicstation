# What to do if… you accidentally packed your passport in your checked luggage and the bag is already gone (USA)

## Short answer
Go immediately to your airline’s counter or customer service desk. Tell staff that your passport is inside a checked bag you have already dropped and ask whether the bag can still be retrieved.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait at the security checkpoint or gate before telling the airline.
- Do not assume the bag cannot be retrieved. Ask the airline to check.
- Do not try to board an international flight without the passport and other travel documents required for your journey.
- Do not report a valid U.S. passport lost or stolen solely because it is inside the checked bag. A U.S. passport reported lost or stolen is cancelled and cannot be used for travel even if it is later found.
- Do not leave the airline desk without knowing where to wait and how staff will update you.

## What to do now
1. Go to your airline’s counter or customer service desk immediately. Say: “My passport is inside my checked bag. I have already dropped the bag. Can you check whether it can still be retrieved?”
2. Show the baggage receipt or a photo of it. Give staff the baggage tag number, your name, flight number, and booking confirmation if requested.
3. Tell staff whether your flight is international or domestic and how soon boarding starts. For an international journey, ask whether the airline needs to pause your check-in or boarding while the passport is being located.
4. Ask where to wait and how the airline will update you. Keep your phone available and stay where staff direct you.
5. If you are already beyond the security checkpoint, tell an airline gate agent immediately. Follow the airline’s instructions if staff ask you to return to the ticketing area.
6. If the airline cannot retrieve the bag before departure, tell staff immediately if you will not board. Ask what will happen to the checked bag, where it will be held or sent, and where you should go next.
7. For a domestic flight, check whether you have another TSA-accepted identification document. If you do not, ask TSA staff about TSA ConfirmID, including any current fee and added time. Do not assume that you will be able to pass the checkpoint or board.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the rest of the trip while airline staff are checking the bag.
- Claims, reimbursement questions, and detailed rebooking decisions can wait until you know whether the passport and bag can be returned.
- Do not report a U.S. passport lost or stolen unless it is actually missing or stolen after the airline has checked the bag’s location.

## Important reassurance
You have a clear first step: tell the airline immediately and give staff the baggage tag number. The result depends on where the bag is in the baggage system, but prompt action gives the airline the best chance to help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the airport moment only. Later decisions may need help from the airline or another relevant specialist. If a U.S. passport holder is abroad and the passport is lost or stolen, use U.S. embassy or consulate guidance. Holders of other passports should contact the authority that issued the passport.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, or professional advice. Airline, airport, security, and destination requirements can vary. Follow staff instructions and ask for the next step in plain language.
