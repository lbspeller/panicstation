# What to do if… you feel like someone is following you while you are shopping (USA)

## Short answer
Stop moving aisle-to-aisle and go straight to a staffed anchor spot, such as customer service, a checkout, a pharmacy counter, or security. Say: “I think someone is following me. Can you keep me here and call security?” If you believe you need immediate police, fire, or medical help, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to lose them by going into quieter areas like parking garages, stairwells, back corridors, or isolated exits.
- Do not go to your car, rideshare pickup, bus stop, or home alone while they still seem attached to your movement.
- Do not confront them, challenge them, or try to make them explain.
- Do not keep shopping to avoid attention; the goal is to stop being isolated, not to act normal.
- Do not focus on recording video if it delays getting to staff or makes you more visible.
- Do not duck into an isolated restroom as a hiding place; if you must use a restroom, choose the busiest one and tell staff or security first.

## What to do now
1. **Move to staffed help inside the building.** Go to the nearest checkout lane, customer service desk, pharmacy counter, security desk, or staffed shop. Stand where there are staff, cameras, and steady foot traffic.
2. **Use a direct one-sentence request.** Say: “I believe someone is following me between aisles or shops. Please keep me here and call mall or store security.” If you are with a child, add: “I’m with a child. Please stay with us.”
3. **Ask for specific help so you are not left alone.**
   - Ask staff to call security to your exact location, including the store name and nearest entrance or landmark.
   - Ask whether there is a staffed place where you can wait, such as by the service desk, security office, checkout, or another visible staff-controlled area.
   - When security arrives, ask whether they can make an incident note and whether any reference number exists.
4. **Call 911 if you believe you need immediate help.** An emergency is a situation that needs immediate assistance from police, fire, or medical services. If you feel you could be harmed soon, call 911 if you can. If you cannot safely speak, texting 911 may be an option in some areas, but a voice call is usually the best route if you can make one. Be ready to give:
   - your exact location, including the store and nearest entrance;
   - what is happening, such as someone repeatedly shadowing you between aisles or shops;
   - a brief description, such as clothing, approximate age, height, direction of travel, and whether you are with someone.
5. **Do not leave alone. Leave with support.**
   - Ask security or staff whether they can escort you to your car, rideshare pickup, public transit, or another staffed exit.
   - If you ordered a rideshare or taxi, wait inside with staff until it arrives and confirm the vehicle and driver before stepping out.
   - If someone you trust is picking you up, stay with staff until you can see them and get in directly.
6. **Capture only the essentials without escalating.** If it is safe, note the time, store, aisle or shop names, direction of travel, and a brief description in your phone. Skip photos or video if taking them would draw attention, provoke the person, or slow you down.
7. **Switch to fixed-location mode.** Stay in one staffed place until security, police, or your trusted pickup arrives. Moving around can make it easier for the person to keep shadowing you.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether you are sure enough to report; you can ask for help based on how the situation feels now.
- Writing a detailed timeline; just save the basics: time, place, description, and who helped.
- Posting about it or trying to identify the person online.
- Long-term safety planning. Right now the priority is to stay public, get staff or security involved, and leave with an escort or trusted pickup.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat repeated shadowing as a potential safety issue, even in a crowded place. You are allowed to involve staff or security without proving the person’s intent first.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes and the next hour only. If this has happened before, keeps happening, or you later feel unsafe, you may choose to ask local police, mall security, or a victim-support service what options exist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or need immediate assistance, call 911.
