# What to do if… you hear a loud new grinding or scraping noise and you have stopped in a safe place to check (USA)

## Short answer
Assume any loud metallic grinding or scraping is a safety hazard and do not continue driving to test the vehicle. Activate hazard lights immediately, perform a visual-only external inspection from a safe position, and call for professional roadside assistance or a tow.

## Do not do these things
- Don't continue driving at any speed if the sound is metallic, rhythmic, or affects steering.
- Don't place any part of your body under the vehicle while it is on the roadside.
- Don't touch wheels, rims, or brake components as they may be hot enough to cause severe burns.
- Don't stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or between your car and a guardrail.
- Don't attempt to jack up the vehicle on a shoulder or uneven surface.
- Don't accept unsolicited tow offers from "gypsy" or unverified trucks; wait for your dispatched service.

## What to do now
1. **Maximize your visibility and safety position immediately.** Turn on your hazard lights. If you are stopped on a high-speed highway with no protective barrier, or if your vehicle is partially obstructing a lane, call **911** immediately to request a "safety block" or police assistance.
2. **Identify immediate fire or chemical hazards from a distance.** Look for smoke, steam, sparks, or a strong smell of burning rubber or hot metal.
   - If you see smoke or fire, move all passengers at least 100 feet away from the vehicle and upwind, then call **911**.
   - If the vehicle is an Electric Vehicle (EV) and you see smoke or hear a "hissing" sound from the floor area, evacuate immediately and notify dispatch that it is an EV.
3. **Conduct a visual-only external inspection using a flashlight.** Stay on the non-traffic side of the vehicle and look for:
   - **Dragging items:** Loose plastic splash guards, heat shields, or road debris caught in the undercarriage.
   - **Wheel hazards:** A shredded tire, a loose hubcap, or visible debris (like a branch or wire) wrapped around an axle.
   - **Fluid loss:** Active streaming of fluid or a rapidly growing puddle under the engine or wheels.
4. **Follow the "no-crawl" rule for minor fixes.** If you clearly see a piece of plastic trim dragging and you can safely reach it without putting your head or torso under the car, you may attempt to tuck or secure it. If the fix requires tools or getting under the car, stop and wait for a mechanic.
5. **Apply the "Do Not Drive" criteria.** You must not attempt to drive to a shop if:
   - The steering wheel feels "loose" or pulls to one side.
   - The brake pedal feels "spongy," "hard," or vibrates excessively.
   - There is a continuous metal-on-metal screech or heavy thudding.
   - A wheel rim is significantly hotter than the others (observable via heat waves or smell).
6. **Request professional recovery and provide precise location data.** Use your insurance app or call an auto club (such as AAA). Provide the following:
   - Interstate/Route number and direction of travel.
   - Nearest mile marker or exit name.
   - Color, make, and model of your vehicle.
7. **Wait in the safest possible configuration.** If you have a wide shoulder or a barrier, stay inside the vehicle with your seatbelt buckled and doors locked. If you believe the vehicle may be struck (e.g., narrow shoulder, night, or fog), exit through the passenger side and move behind a permanent safety barrier or up an embankment well away from the road.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure (e.g., wheel bearing vs. brake pad) right now.
- You do not need to negotiate repair costs or choose a specific garage while at the roadside.
- Determining if a part failed due to a manufacturer defect is a task for after the vehicle is at a secure facility.

## Important reassurance
Hearing a sudden loud noise is startling, but stopping immediately was the correct safety decision. Most grinding noises indicate a mechanical failure that, if ignored, could lead to a wheel lock-up or brake failure. Choosing a tow over "trying to make it home" prevents secondary accidents.

## Scope note
This guide is for initial roadside stabilization and harm prevention only. It does not provide mechanical diagnostics or repair instructions.

## Important note
Roadside environments are high-risk zones for pedestrian strikes. Under 2026 "Move Over" laws in many states, other drivers are required to change lanes for you, but you must never assume they will. If you smell fuel, see smoke, or are stopped in a live lane, contact 911 immediately.
