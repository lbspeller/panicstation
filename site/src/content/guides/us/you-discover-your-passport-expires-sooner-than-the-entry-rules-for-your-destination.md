# What to do if… you discover your passport expires sooner than the entry rules for your destination (USA)

## Short answer
Confirm the specific validity rules for every country in your itinerary using the IATA Travel Centre database, then secure an urgent appointment at a U.S. Passport Agency if your departure is within 14 calendar days.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a passport is "valid" just because it has not reached its printed expiration date.
- Do not ignore transit countries, as airlines may apply the strictest rule found across your entire route.
- Do not attempt to "risk it" at the gate; airline systems (Timatic) often automatically flag invalidity and prevent boarding.
- Do not book non-refundable changes to your trip until you have confirmed the exact entry requirement for your citizenship and destination.
- Do not forget that for most European countries, the passport must have been issued within the last 10 years, even if it is not yet expired.

## What to do now
1. **Document your specific timeline and requirements immediately.**  
   - Write down your passport issue date, expiration date, citizenship status, and every country you will land in (including layovers).
   - Check the **IATA Travel Centre (Timatic)** website, which is the exact tool airlines use to determine if a passenger is "cleared to board."

2. **Determine which validity rule applies to your route.**  
   - **6-Month Rule:** Many countries in Asia, Africa, and the Middle East require 6 months of validity beyond your arrival or departure date.
   - **3-Month Rule:** Most European (Schengen Area) countries require 3 months of validity beyond your intended date of departure from the zone.
   - **Issuance Rule:** For Europe, your passport must be less than 10 years old on the day you enter.
   - **Duration of Stay:** Countries like Mexico and Canada generally only require validity for the duration of your stay.

3. **Contact your airline to confirm their boarding stance.**  
   - Ask: "Based on my passport expiring on [Date] and my itinerary through [Countries], will the system allow me to check in and board?"
   - Request a written confirmation via email or chat transcript if they confirm you are cleared.

4. **Select the correct U.S. passport service based on your departure date.**  
   - **Traveling in less than 3 business days:** Call 1-877-487-2778 for a Life-or-Death Emergency appointment (requires proof of family emergency).
   - **Traveling within 14 calendar days (or 28 days if a visa is required):** Call 1-877-487-2778 to schedule an "Urgent Travel" appointment at a regional Passport Agency. You must bring proof of travel.
   - **Traveling in more than 3 weeks:** Apply for "Expedited Service" (2-3 weeks processing plus 4 weeks total mailing) by mail or at a post office.

5. **Apply for a Limited-Validity Emergency Passport if you are currently outside the USA.**  
   - Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate.
   - Be aware that some countries (e.g., France, UAE) do not accept these 12-page emergency passports for entry; verify transit rules before flying.

6. **Update all digital travel authorizations once you have a new passport number.**  
   - You must re-apply for ESTAs, ETAs, or eVisas, as these are typically hard-linked to your specific passport number.
   - Update your Advance Passenger Information (API) in your airline booking and your frequent flyer profile.

## What can wait
- You do not need to seek airline compensation or refunds until the immediate document issue is resolved.
- You do not need to research "why" the rules exist; focus only on meeting the requirement for your departure date.
- You do not need to decide on long-term travel insurance claims today.

## Important reassurance
Passport validity issues are one of the most common reasons for boarding denials, and the U.S. Department of State has specific "Urgent Travel" pathways designed exactly for this situation. If you are within the 14-day window, the regional agency system is the standard method for resolving this.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document validity conflicts. it does not cover visa application procedures, legal disputes with airlines, or financial recovery for missed flights.

## Important note
Entry requirements are subject to change without notice and vary by citizenship and residency. Information provided is based on 2026 U.S. Department of State and IATA standards. Always verify requirements with the official embassy of your destination country.
