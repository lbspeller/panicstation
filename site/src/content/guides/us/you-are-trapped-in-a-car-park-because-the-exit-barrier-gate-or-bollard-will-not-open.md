# What to do if… you are trapped in a car park because the exit barrier, gate, or bollard will not open (USA)

## Short answer
Move to a safe waiting position if you can, do not force or drive around the gate, barrier, or bollard, and use the posted help button or contact number. If anyone needs immediate assistance from police, fire, or ambulance services, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ram, lift, pry, or drive around the gate, barrier, or bollard.
- Do not follow another vehicle through a partial opening.
- Do not reverse until you can clearly see that the route is safe.
- Avoid leaving the engine idling unnecessarily while you wait, especially inside an enclosed or partly enclosed garage.
- Do not call 911 only because the exit is delayed. Use 911 when immediate emergency assistance is needed.

## What to do now
1. **Make the vehicle safe.** If you can move out of the exit lane safely, park in a visible waiting area. If you cannot move, put the vehicle in park and turn on your hazard lights.
2. **Switch off the engine while waiting if it is safe to do so.** This is especially important inside an enclosed or partly enclosed garage.
3. **Use the exit help button or intercom.** Say that you are at the parking exit, the gate, barrier, or bollard is not allowing you to leave, and whether the ticket reader, card reader, or pay station is showing an error. Ask the operator to open the exit or send staff.
4. **Call a posted contact number if one is available.** Give the facility name or address, your parking level, the exit lane, your callback number, and the problem shown on the machine.
5. **Try an on-site contact.** A security desk, parking office, lobby desk, or nearby building reception may be able to reach the parking operator. Walk away from the vehicle only if it is safe to do so.
6. **Escalate according to the situation.** Call 911 if there is smoke, fire, a threat, or an urgent medical need. If there is no immediate danger but the operator, posted contact, and on-site routes are not responding, look up a local emergency communications center or police non-emergency number and ask what local option is appropriate.
7. **Record only what is easy and safe to capture.** A photo of the blocked exit, an error message, the posted contact number, and the time may help with a later billing question. Keep any ticket or receipt.
8. **Leave slowly when the route is clear.** Watch for pedestrians, nearby vehicles, and any moving barrier components.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to dispute a charge, request a refund, or make a complaint.
- You do not need to argue with other drivers or decide who is responsible for the failure.
- You do not need to prepare a detailed account while you are still waiting at the exit.

## Important reassurance
A blocked parking exit is a practical access problem. Focus on one step at a time: make the vehicle safe, contact the operator, and use emergency services only if immediate assistance is needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps for immediate safety and for contacting the people who may be able to open the exit. Any later billing dispute, refund request, or complaint may depend on the facility and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. In the USA, call 911 when immediate assistance is needed from police, fire, or ambulance services.
