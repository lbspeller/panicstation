# What to do if… you get a message that a charging session ended unexpectedly and you may not have enough range left (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately secure a safe, level parking location and contact the charging network's 24 / 7 support line for a remote reset; if range remains insufficient, request a flatbed tow to the nearest functional charger via your vehicle's roadside assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue driving if your estimated range is within 5 miles of your next destination; range estimates often drop rapidly in extreme weather or high-speed traffic.
- Do not stop in active travel lanes, on highway ramps, or in blind curves.
- Do not attempt to tow the vehicle with the drive wheels on the ground, as this can cause permanent damage to the electric motors and inverter.
- Do not leave headlights or climate control on while stationary if the traction battery is at 0 % to avoid depleting the 12V battery and "bricking" the vehicle.
- Do not rely on "limp mode" to reach a distant charger; once the vehicle limits power, it may stop completely without further warning.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to a safe, level location immediately.**
   - If you are currently at the charging station, stay parked and engage the electronic parking brake.
   - If you have already left the station, find the nearest parking lot or service area.
   - Only use a highway shoulder if no other option is available; pull as far right as possible.

2. **Verify the physical connection and station status.**
   - Ensure the charging connector is fully seated and the locking pin has engaged.
   - Check the station screen for specific error codes such as "Ground Fault," "Payment Declined," or "Emergency Stop Pressed."
   - If using a NACS / CCS adapter, ensure the adapter is firmly clicked into both the cable and the vehicle port.

3. **Call the charging network’s 24 / 7 technical support number.**
   - Locate the number on the station decal or within the mobile app.
   - Provide the Station ID (usually a 4–8 digit code on the screen or sticker).
   - **Ask the representative to perform a "Hard Remote Reset" of the stall.**
   - Request that they verify if other stalls at the same location are currently functional.

4. **Activate "Tow Mode" or "Transport Mode" in your vehicle settings.**
   - Navigate to the "Service" or "Settings" menu on your vehicle touchscreen.
   - **Enable the mode that allows the vehicle to be winched onto a flatbed without the parking brake engaging.**
   - Keep your key fob or phone key inside the vehicle to prevent the system from locking the drivetrain.

5. **Contact professional roadside assistance for an EV-specific recovery.**
   - Call your vehicle manufacturer’s dedicated line, AAA, or your insurance provider.
   - **State clearly: "This is an electric vehicle that requires a flatbed tow with all four wheels off the ground."**
   - Provide your exact GPS coordinates and current state of charge (SoC).
   - If the 12V battery is low, inform the dispatcher that a "jump start" may be required just to release the electronic parking brake.

6. **Notify emergency services if you are stopped in a hazardous location.**
   - Turn on hazard lights immediately.
   - **Call 911 if you are unable to move the vehicle out of a high-speed traffic lane or off a narrow shoulder.**
   - Exit the vehicle through the passenger side and move to a safe distance behind a guardrail if the area is high-risk.

## What can wait
- You do not need to process a refund request or billing dispute for the failed session right now.
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault was caused by the station, your app, or the vehicle’s onboard charger.
- You do not need to plan your entire remaining trip; focus only on the immediate path to a safe, functional charger or service center.

## Important reassurance
Charging interruptions are common and are often caused by software handshakes or network timeouts rather than vehicle failure. Roadside assistance providers and charging networks have standard 2026 protocols to handle these specific transport and mobility disruptions safely.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a charging failure that creates a range emergency. It focuses on physical safety, hazard isolation, and professional recovery.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace your vehicle's owner's manual or official manufacturer towing guides. Modern EVs have high-voltage systems that must be handled only by qualified professionals. If you are stopped in an unsafe location or feel threatened by traffic, contact emergency services (911) immediately.
