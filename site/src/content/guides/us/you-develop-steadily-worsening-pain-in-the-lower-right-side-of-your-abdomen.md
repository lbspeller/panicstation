# What to do if… you develop steadily worsening pain in the lower right side of your abdomen (USA)

## Short answer
Go to an Emergency Department immediately or call 911. Steadily worsening pain in the lower right abdomen is a hallmark sign of appendicitis or other surgical emergencies that require immediate professional evaluation and imaging.

## Do not do these things
- Do not apply a heating pad or hot water bottle to the abdomen, as heat can increase the risk of an inflamed appendix rupturing.
- Do not take laxatives, use enemas, or attempt to force a bowel movement.
- Do not take pain relievers (aspirin, ibuprofen, or acetaminophen) before being seen, as they can mask the specific symptoms doctors use for diagnosis.
- Do not eat or drink anything other than small sips of water until you have been evaluated, in case you require immediate surgery.
- Do not drive yourself if the pain is severe, or if you feel faint, dizzy, or confused.
- Do not wait until the morning if the pain is constant and increasing.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 or head to the nearest Emergency Department immediately.**
   - Call 911 if you have any "red flag" symptoms:
      - Severe, unbearable pain.
      - A hard, rigid, or swollen abdomen.
      - Fainting, extreme lightheadedness, or confusion.
      - Difficulty breathing or rapid heart rate.
      - Vomiting blood or passing black, tarry stools.
2. **Alert the staff if there is any possibility you are pregnant.**
   - Pain in the lower right abdomen can indicate an ectopic pregnancy, which is a life-threatening emergency.
   - Seek immediate care if the pain is accompanied by vaginal bleeding / spotting or shoulder-tip pain.
3. **Monitor for signs of rupture while in transit.**
   - Be aware that a sudden, temporary relief of pain followed by an increase in severe, spreading pain and high fever may indicate the appendix has burst. 
4. **Prepare a concise summary of the pain timeline.**
   - Note exactly when the pain started and where it was first felt (e.g., "started 4 hours ago near the navel").
   - Confirm if the pain moved from the center to the right side.
   - List any accompanying symptoms such as nausea, loss of appetite, or fever.
5. **Bring essential items but do not delay your departure.**
   - Take your photo ID, insurance card, and a list of current medications and allergies.
   - Ensure your phone is charged or bring a charger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific cause (appendicitis, ovarian cyst, kidney stone, or infection) before seeking help.
- You do not need to pack a bag for a hospital stay; immediate evaluation is the priority.
- You do not need to finish your current tasks or wait for a primary care appointment.

## Important reassurance
Worsening pain in this specific area is a valid reason to seek emergency medical care. Professional triage and imaging (such as a CT scan or ultrasound) are the standard and necessary ways to ensure your safety. Seeking help quickly is the most effective way to prevent complications.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance. A physical examination by a healthcare professional is mandatory to diagnose the cause of abdominal pain.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and does not constitute medical advice or a diagnosis. If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 or visit an emergency room immediately.
