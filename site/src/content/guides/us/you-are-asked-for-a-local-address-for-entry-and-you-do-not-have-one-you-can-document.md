# What to do if… you are asked for a local address for entry and you do not have one you can document (USA)

## Short answer
Provide a legitimate first-night address, such as a confirmed hotel or hostel booking, and ensure this address is identical across your airline check-in, ESTA mobile app, and verbal answers. Do not invent a false address.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t invent a fictional address or copy a random residential address from a map.
- Don’t leave the address field blank if the system requires it for boarding or entry.
- Don’t pay third-party "expediting" services at the airport to "fix" your address details.
- Don’t cancel a booking used for entry proof until you have cleared customs and reached your destination.
- Don’t provide long, defensive explanations; stick to the specific address you have documented.

## What to do now
1. **Determine which entity is requesting the address.**  
   Confirm if the request is coming from an airline agent at check-in, the ESTA mobile application, a Customs and Border Protection (CBP) kiosk, or an officer during a primary or secondary interview.
2. **Use the "Transit" protocol if you are not staying in the U.S.**  
   If your final destination is outside the U.S. and you are only connecting, enter "Transit" in the address line or use the physical address of the airport where your connection occurs.
3. **Secure a verifiable first-night booking immediately.**  
   If you do not have a place to stay, use a booking platform to reserve a room at a hotel or hostel for at least the first night of your arrival.
   - Choose a property that provides an instant email confirmation.
   - Ensure the confirmation shows the full street address, city, and zip code.
4. **Update your ESTA or airline profile via official apps.**  
   Open the official ESTA Mobile App or your airline’s mobile application to update the "Address While in the U.S." field with the new information.
5. **Save a physical or offline copy of the confirmation.**  
   Download the hotel confirmation as a PDF or take a clear screenshot so it is accessible without an internet connection or cellular data at the border.
6. **Provide consistent verbal and written information.**  
   State the name of the hotel and its location clearly if asked by a CBP officer, matching the details on your digital or printed proof.
7. **Request a supervisor if an airline agent refuses a valid booking.**  
   If an agent claims a confirmed hotel booking is insufficient for boarding, ask for a "Station Manager" or supervisor to clarify the specific documentation requirement.
8. **Contact your national embassy if entry is formally denied.**  
   If you are a foreign national being refused entry, ask to contact your country's consulate.
   - For U.S. citizens facing documentation issues while returning from abroad, contact the U.S. Department of State at 1-888-407-4747 (from U.S./Canada) or +1 202-501-4444 (from overseas).

## What can wait
- You do not need to book accommodations for the entire duration of your trip.
- You do not need to provide a lease agreement or formal invitation letter for short-term tourist stays.
- You do not need to finalize your internal travel itinerary beyond the first night's stay.

## Important reassurance
Travelers frequently arrive with flexible plans. Customs and airline systems are primarily looking for a credible, verifiable point of contact for your first 24 hours in the country. Providing one real address typically resolves the immediate hold on your boarding or entry.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for documentation hurdles related to local addresses at U.S. ports of entry or check-in. It does not guarantee admission or override specific visa or ESTA eligibility requirements.

## Important note
Entry to the United States is at the sole discretion of CBP officers. This information is procedural and does not constitute legal advice. If you are detained, remain cooperative and provide truthful information.
