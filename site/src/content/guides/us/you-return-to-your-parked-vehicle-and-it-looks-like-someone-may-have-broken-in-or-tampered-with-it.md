# What to do if… you return to your parked vehicle and it looks like someone may have broken in or tampered with it (USA)

## Short answer
Step back to a public, well-lit place where other people can see you. Call 911 if you feel threatened, think someone may still be nearby, or see anything that could be dangerous.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t get into the vehicle while you feel unsafe or while someone suspicious is still nearby.
- Don’t confront, follow, or try to stop a suspected thief yourself.
- Don’t handle, open, smell, shake, or move an unfamiliar item in, on, or under the vehicle.
- Don’t start driving if you notice a strong fuel smell, leaking fluid, a loose-looking wheel, cut wires, a damaged steering column, or new warning lights.
- Don’t clean up or rearrange things yet if you may need a police report, photos, or insurance documentation.
- Don’t search the area alone to work out what happened.

## What to do now
1. **Move to visibility first.** Go to a staffed place nearby, such as a store entrance, lobby, gas station counter, parking office, or security desk.
2. **Look only from a safe distance.** Check for obvious signs such as a door ajar, broken glass, interior lights on, moved belongings, damaged locks, someone inside, or someone lingering close to the vehicle.
3. **Call 911 if there is any immediate safety concern.** This includes someone still present, a person watching or approaching you, a threat, a weapon, smoke, a strong fuel smell, or an unfamiliar item that looks deliberately placed.
4. **Ask for another person to stay with you.** If you are at a business, apartment building, campus, hospital, hotel, garage, or event venue, ask staff or security to remain with you while you decide what to do next.
5. **Report a non-emergency break-in through the local route.** If the scene feels safe and the person is gone, contact the local law enforcement agency where it happened using its non-emergency number or online reporting if available. Ask how to get a case number.
6. **Document without disturbing the vehicle.** From a safe position, take photos of doors, locks, windows, the inside through the glass, anything out of place, and the surrounding location. Note the time, address, lot name, level, row, and space number if you can.
7. **Check only the essentials before touching things.** With staff, security, or another trusted person present, work out whether your keys or fob, wallet, ID, payment cards, house keys, garage opener, spare keys, phone, laptop, or other device may be missing.
8. **If a wallet, phone, cards, ID, or keys are missing, lock down the most urgent risks from a safe place.** Freeze or report missing payment cards, secure banking access, and consider using IdentityTheft.gov if identity documents or personal information may have been taken.
9. **If the vehicle may be unsafe to drive, do not test it in the lot.** Call roadside assistance, a tow company, your insurer’s assistance line, or a trusted mechanic from the safe place where you are waiting.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide about repairs, insurance claims, replacing the vehicle, or long-term security right now.
- You do not need a perfect list of everything missing before you get to safety.
- You do not need to investigate who did it.
- You can sort out cleaning, repairs, replacement keys, and detailed paperwork after the immediate safety and access risks are under control.

## Important reassurance
Feeling uneasy when your vehicle looks disturbed is a protective response, not an overreaction. Creating distance, bringing in staff or security, and using 911 when there may be danger are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you notice something off. Later decisions about police reports, insurance, repairs, key replacement, credit protection, or legal options may need help from the relevant specialist or local authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, insurance, security, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
