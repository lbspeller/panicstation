# What to do if… you start vomiting blood or see coffee-ground material in vomit (USA)

## Short answer
Go to the nearest emergency room immediately or call 911 if you feel faint, are alone, or are actively vomiting large amounts of blood.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital.
- Do not lie flat on your back.
- Do not eat or drink anything, including water or "stomach-settling" liquids.
- Do not take aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen, or any other blood-thinning medications.
- Do not assume the color is caused by food, supplements, or dyes.
- Do not wait to see if the vomiting stops before seeking medical evaluation.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately if you experience any signs of shock or severe distress.**
   - These signs include fainting, extreme dizziness, confusion, rapid breathing, or cold, clammy skin.
   - You should also call 911 if the vomiting is ongoing, involves large volumes of blood, or if you have no safe way to reach an emergency room.
2. **Position yourself to prevent choking or aspiration.**
   - If you are fully awake, sit upright and lean forward slightly.
   - If you feel very weak or are assisting someone who is drifting in and out of consciousness, use the recovery position: roll onto the left side, tilt the head back slightly to open the airway, and ensure the mouth is angled toward the ground so fluids can drain.
3. **Notify a nearby person to assist you.**
   - Ask them to stay with you and keep the front door unlocked for emergency responders.
   - If you are alone, call 911 rather than trying to manage the situation in isolation.
4. **Prepare a brief summary of symptoms and medications for medical staff.**
   - Note the approximate amount of blood and whether it is bright red or looks like dark, grainy coffee grounds.
   - List any current medications, particularly blood thinners (anticoagulants), aspirin, or daily NSAID use.
   - Mention if you have also noticed black, tarry, or foul-smelling stools (melena).
5. **Take a photo of the vomit or bring a small sample if it is safe and fast to do so.**
   - Do not delay your departure to the hospital or your call to emergency services to perform this step.
6. **Remain "NPO" (nothing by mouth) until a doctor evaluates you.**
   - Avoid all food, water, and medications, as you may require an immediate endoscopic procedure or surgery that requires an empty stomach.
7. **Monitor for worsening symptoms while help is on the way.**
   - If your breathing becomes difficult or your pulse feels very fast, tell the emergency dispatcher or the person assisting you immediately.

## What can wait
- Determining the exact cause of the bleeding.
- Contacting your primary care physician for advice or an appointment.
- Cleaning up the area where the vomiting occurred.
- Deciding whether the amount of blood is "serious enough" to justify an emergency visit.

## Important reassurance
Vomiting blood or coffee-ground material is a high-priority medical event that causes a natural panic response. Emergency departments are equipped to stabilize these situations quickly. Taking immediate action is the standard safety protocol and does not mean you are overreacting.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only and is intended to bridge the gap between the onset of symptoms and professional medical assessment.

## Important note
Under the Emergency Medical Treatment and Labor Act (EMTALA), any person who comes to an emergency department in the USA must be stabilized and treated, regardless of their ability to pay or insurance status. This guide is for informational purposes and is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis or treatment.
