# What to do if… you notice a gas-like smell near an appliance and suspect a gas leak (USA)

## Short answer
Evacuate the building immediately on foot, avoid any action that could create a spark, and call **911** and your gas utility's emergency line from at least 350 feet away.

## Do not do these things
- Do not flip any electrical switches, including lights, or use thermostats.
- Do not plug or unplug any devices, use doorbells, or operate garage door openers.
- Do not use any phone, tablet, or flashlight while still inside the building.
- Do not light matches, use lighters, smoke, or vape.
- Do not start any vehicles or motorized equipment in or near the building.
- Do not attempt to locate, stop, or repair the leak yourself.
- Do not assume someone else has already reported the smell.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate all people and pets from the building immediately.** Do not stop to open windows or collect belongings; prioritize getting everyone to fresh air as quickly as possible.
2. **Move at least 350 feet away from the structure.** Ensure you are upwind of the suspected leak area and avoid standing near manhole covers or sewers where gas can accumulate.
3. **Call 911 and your local gas utility or propane supplier once you are at a safe distance.** Use a cell phone or a neighbor's phone only after you have cleared the danger zone.
   - Inform the dispatcher if you heard hissing sounds or if anyone is feeling dizzy or nauseated.
   - Provide the exact address and the nearest cross street.
4. **Warn others in the immediate vicinity to stay away.** If you can do so safely from your outdoor position, tell neighbors to evacuate and prevent people from entering the area.
5. **Shut off the main propane supply only if the tank is outside and the valve is accessible without entering the hazard zone.** Do not attempt to shut off a natural gas meter unless specifically instructed to do so by the fire department or utility emergency dispatcher.
6. **Wait for official clearance before attempting to re-enter.** Only emergency responders or utility technicians can declare the building safe to occupy; do not go back inside for any reason until they give the "all clear."

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify which specific appliance is leaking.
- You do not need to contact a private plumber or repair service until the utility has secured the area.
- You do not need to worry about utility bills or service restoration procedures during the evacuation.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel uncertain if the smell is faint or intermittent. Natural gas and propane have odorants added specifically to trigger an early warning; acting on a faint smell is a standard safety protocol, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and evacuation. Subsequent inspections and repairs must be performed by certified gas professionals before appliances are placed back into service.

## Important note
Gas odorants can fade over time (olfactory fatigue) or be filtered by soil, meaning a lack of smell does not always mean a leak has stopped. Treat all suspected leaks as high-priority emergencies. Follow all direct instructions from 911 dispatchers and utility personnel.
