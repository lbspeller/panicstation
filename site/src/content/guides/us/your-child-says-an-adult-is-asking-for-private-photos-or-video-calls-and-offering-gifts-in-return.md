# What to do if… your child says an adult is asking for private photos or video calls and offering gifts in return (USA)

## Short answer
Treat this as a serious exploitation risk rather than a disciplinary issue. Stop the contact immediately, preserve evidence without viewing it yourself, and report the incident to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) or law enforcement.

## Do not do these things
- Do not blame, shame, or punish your child for sharing this information with you.
- Do not ask your child to continue the conversation to "catch" the person or gather more evidence.
- Do not delete any messages, apps, usernames, or records of gifts/payments if they still exist.
- Do not confront, message, or threaten the adult yourself.
- Do not attempt to investigate the adult’s identity or location on your own.
- Do not record or photograph explicit images of your child to "save" them as evidence.

## What to do now
1. **Move your child to a quiet, private space and prioritize their sense of safety.** Say clearly: "I am glad you told me. You are not in trouble, and this is not your fault."
2. **End the contact in the most immediate, safe way possible.** Help your child stop responding, block the user, and set the device aside without deleting the app or the message history.
3. **Preserve the evidence exactly as it is without circulating it.** Take note of the platform name, the adult's username or handle, and any specific gifts or money offered (such as gift cards or game currency) without forwarding any sensitive images to yourself or others.
4. **Determine the current level of immediate risk to your child.** Check if the adult has the child's real name, home address, school name, or if an in-person meeting has been suggested or planned.
5. **Report the situation to the appropriate authorities immediately.**
   - Call 911 if there is an immediate threat, a scheduled meeting, or if you believe the person is nearby.
   - Contact the NCMEC CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org or call 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678).
   - Report to the FBI at tips.fbi.gov or call 1-800-CALL-FBI (1-800-225-5324).
6. **Use specialized tools if you believe sexual images or videos have already been shared.** Use NCMEC’s "Take It Down" service to help remove explicit content of minors from participating online platforms.
7. **Notify relevant local organizations if the adult is connected to a specific activity.** If the person is linked to a school, sports team, or club, inform the safeguarding lead or administrator so they can implement immediate protective measures.
8. **Monitor all other digital access points for the next 48 hours.** Check for new friend requests on different apps, unauthorized packages arriving at your home, or messages in payment apps like Venmo or CashApp.
9. **Secure professional support for your child tonight.** Contact your pediatrician or a counselor, or call the Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline at 1-800-422-4453 for guidance on supporting your child through the disclosure.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on permanent device bans or hardware disposal today.
- You do not need to draft a perfect statement or "official" timeline right now.
- You do not need to solve long-term internet safety rules or school-related consequences immediately.
- You do not need to notify other parents until law enforcement provides guidance on doing so.

## Important reassurance
People who target children online use sophisticated tactics like flattery, gifts, and secrecy to create a sense of obligation. It is very common for children to feel confused, "special," or scared of being in trouble. Your child’s decision to tell you is a sign of trust and the most important step in stopping the behavior.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute safety concern. Ongoing investigations and emotional recovery will require coordination with specialized professionals and law enforcement.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and is not a substitute for legal advice or professional mental health services. If you suspect your child is in imminent physical danger or is being blackmailed, contact emergency services at 911 immediately.
