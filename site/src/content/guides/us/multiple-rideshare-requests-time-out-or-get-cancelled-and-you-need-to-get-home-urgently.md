# What to do if… multiple rideshare requests time out or get cancelled and you need to get home urgently (USA)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a well-lit, staffed indoor location and switch to a different transportation method, such as a local taxi or public transit, rather than repeatedly attempting the same failed rideshare app. If you feel physically threatened or followed, call 911 from your secure location.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept a ride from a stranger who pulls over and offers "a lift" outside of an official app or dispatch.
- Do not remain standing on a dark or isolated curb while troubleshooting your phone.
- Do not enter any vehicle if the driver cannot or will not state your name first.
- Do not allow your phone battery to deplete by running multiple high-drain apps simultaneously without a power source.
- Do not walk toward more isolated areas to "meet a driver" half-way unless it is a designated, well-lit pickup zone.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a staffed, well-lit indoor environment immediately.** 
   - Enter a hotel lobby, 24-hour convenience store, diner, or hospital entrance.
   - If no buildings are open, move toward the nearest cluster of people or under a bright streetlamp with visible security cameras.
   - Stay inside the building until your transportation is confirmed and visible at the curb.

2. **Switch to a traditional taxi service or local dispatch.** 
   - Use a search engine to find "taxi dispatch near me" and call a local number rather than using an app.
   - Ask staff at a hotel or venue to call a taxi for you; they often have direct lines to reliable dispatchers.
   - Confirm the estimated wait time and the company name before hanging up.

3. **Utilize public transit if it remains operational.** 
   - Check local transit apps for the last bus or train departures.
   - Move to a major transit hub (subway station or bus terminal) which is more likely to have transit police or staff present.
   - Sit near the driver or conductor if the vehicle is largely empty.

4. **Initiate a "safety contact" protocol.** 
   - Call a trusted friend or family member and stay on the line until you are in a moving vehicle.
   - Share your live GPS location via your phone's messaging app.
   - Inform the contact of the taxi company name or the vehicle description as soon as you have it.

5. **Verify the vehicle and driver before entering.** 
   - **Match the license plate and vehicle make exactly if using an app.** 
   - **Ask the driver "Who are you picking up?" and wait for them to say your name.** 
   - **Check the rear door handle to ensure child safety locks are not engaged before closing the door.** 
   - If the driver seems hesitant or the vehicle does not match, do not get in; return to your safe indoor location immediately.

6. **Address immediate safety threats via emergency services.** 
   - **Call 911 if you are being followed, harassed, or feel in immediate physical danger.** 
   - Provide the dispatcher with your exact location (cross-streets or business name).
   - Tell the dispatcher you are stranded and feel unsafe; do not wait for the situation to escalate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact rideshare support to complain about cancellations until you are home.
- You do not need to worry about the cost difference between a taxi and an app-based ride during a safety-critical moment.
- You do not need to determine why the apps are failing while you are still in an unsecured location.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency or anxiety when transportation options fail late at night. Rideshare platforms experience technical outages and driver shortages frequently. Switching to a different method is a standard safety adjustment and does not mean you are "stranded" permanently.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a transportation failure. It focuses on immediate physical safety and securing an alternative way home. It does not cover long-term travel planning or app account troubleshooting.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional safety advice. Your local environment and current time of day may dictate different transit availability. Always prioritize your physical intuition; if a situation feels unsafe, move to a secure location and contact local authorities.
