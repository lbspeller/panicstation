# What to do if… you are locked out of your home in urgent weather or at night (USA)
## Short answer
Move to a staffed indoor place or other safe shelter immediately. From there, contact the fastest authorized route back inside: a spare-key holder, building management, your landlord, or a reputable locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force a door, break glass, or climb to reach an entry point. You could be injured or create a more serious emergency.
- Do not stay outside while you are getting colder, wetter, overheated, confused, very sleepy, or less able to think clearly.
- Do not hire a locksmith without confirming the business name and asking about the likely charges before dispatch.
- Do not authorize unexplained price changes or unnecessary work under pressure.
- Do not enter through a route you are not authorized to use.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer place first.**
   - Go to a neighbor, an open business, a building lobby, or another staffed indoor place.
   - If anyone has warning signs of hypothermia, such as shivering, confusion, fumbling hands, slurred speech, or drowsiness, seek immediate medical attention. Call **911** when emergency assistance is needed.

2. **Make one brief, non-damaging check.**
   - Confirm that you are at the correct door or unit.
   - Check only for an authorized, ground-level, non-damaging entry route, such as a building lobby or an unlocked door you normally use.
   - If a key is sticking, stop before it snaps.

3. **Contact an authorized keyholder.**
   - Call anyone who has your spare key.
   - If you rent or live in a managed building, call your landlord, property manager, front desk, or after-hours maintenance contact.

4. **Use a locksmith carefully if needed.**
   - Prefer a locksmith recommended by your landlord, building manager, or another trusted source where possible.
   - Before dispatch, ask for the business name, whether the company is local, and the likely charges.
   - Before work starts, ask the locksmith to explain any change to the likely charge and how they will confirm that you are authorized to enter.
   - Do not authorize work until the explanation and likely charge are clear.

5. **Ask for local help if you cannot safely wait.**
   - Call **211** and ask whether any local safe indoor option, shelter, or emergency resource is available.
   - Stay in the safer place while you arrange access.

6. **Call 911 for an immediate emergency inside.**
   - Call **911** if a child, older adult, medically vulnerable person, or pet may be in immediate danger inside.
   - Call **911** if there is smoke, fire, an appliance hazard, or another situation requiring immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance.
   - If you feel unsafe where you are, move to a staffed public place and call for help from there.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to change the lock or install a different type of lock.
- You do not need to authorize locksmith work before the charge is clear.
- You do not need to sort out insurance, receipts, or prevention planning until you are sheltered and safely back inside.

## Important reassurance
A lockout can feel overwhelming when the weather or time of day adds pressure. Moving to a safe place first gives you room to use an authorized route back inside without taking unnecessary risks.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about the lock, building procedures, or a disputed charge can wait and may need help from your landlord, building manager, or a locksmith.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Call **911** when immediate police, fire department, or ambulance assistance is needed.
