# What to do if… you receive a notice that your visa or travel permission has been cancelled while you are abroad (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the notice through the issuing country’s official government portal using a separate browser window. Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate to report the situation and obtain a list of local immigration attorneys.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the notice or assume it is an error that will resolve itself.
- Do not attempt to cross an international border or board a flight until you have verified your status.
- Do not click links or provide payment details in an unsolicited email or text message.
- Do not argue with or physically resist border or immigration officials.
- Do not hand over your physical passport to anyone except uniformed immigration officials; if they retain it, note the officer's name, badge number, and the location.

## What to do now
1. **Review the notice for specific deadlines and instructions.** Look for an "Exit by" date, a requirement to report to a specific office, or a case reference number.
2. **Verify the cancellation using official government channels.** Navigate directly to the host country's immigration website by typing the address into your browser; do not use links provided in the notice.
3. **Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate immediately.** Inform them that your travel permission has been revoked and you are currently in a foreign jurisdiction.
   - Ask for a list of local immigration attorneys who speak English.
   - Ask for the procedure for requesting consular notification if you are detained.
4. **Enroll in the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP).** Use the official state.gov portal to register your location so the U.S. government can reach you with emergency updates.
5. **Request written clarification of your current legal status from the host authority.** Ask specifically if the cancellation affects your current right to remain (stay) or only your right to re-enter (boarding/entry).
6. **Notify your airline if you have an upcoming flight.** Ask if your name appears as "ineligible to board" in their system to avoid being surprised at the airport.
7. **Secure your documents in a "proof pack" for offline access.** Take clear photos of your passport photo page, current entry stamps, the cancellation notice, and any confirmation of your legal entry.
8. **Contact the U.S. State Department’s 24/7 emergency line if the local embassy is unreachable.**
   - From overseas: **+1 202-501-4444**
   - From the U.S. or Canada: **1-888-407-4747**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a long-term appeal or legal challenge today.
- You do not need to finalize your long-term travel plans until your immediate status is confirmed.
- You do not need to contact every family member or friend; prioritize the issuing authority and the U.S. Consulate.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a notice of revocation while in a foreign country is a high-stress event, and feeling a sense of panic or confusion is a normal response. Immigration procedures can be complex, but following a structured process of verification and seeking consular support is the most effective way to prevent the situation from escalating.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for U.S. citizens or travelers experiencing a revocation of travel permission while already abroad. It does not provide legal strategy for immigration appeals or country-specific visa advice.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. U.S. embassies cannot override the immigration decisions of a foreign government. Always verify information through official .gov or equivalent host-government domains.
