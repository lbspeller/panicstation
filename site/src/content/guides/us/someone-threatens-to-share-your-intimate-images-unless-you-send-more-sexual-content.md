# What to do if… someone threatens to share your intimate images unless you send more sexual content (USA)

## Short answer
Do not send more sexual content or money. Move to a safer pause: stop replying, tell one trusted person, and use the steps below to reduce further contact.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send more images, videos, or live video.
- Don’t send money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, or removal fees.
- Don’t keep negotiating, arguing, or pleading.
- Don’t delete messages in a panic if you may want support or reporting later.
- Don’t hire or pay a takedown service while you are under pressure.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause.**  
   Stop replying. If there is an immediate threat of physical harm or someone may come to your location, call 911 or local police and move to a safer place if you can.

2. **Tell one trusted person.**  
   Ask someone you trust to stay with you, call you, or help you work through the next steps without pressure.

3. **Keep only a small record if you may want help later.**  
   If you may want support or reporting later, save a small record such as screenshots showing the account name, threat, and demand; do not collect, download, or forward intimate files.

4. **Reduce further contact.**  
   Block the account where you can. Report the account and messages in the platform or app. Set your accounts to private, remove unknown followers, and limit who can message or tag you.

5. **Use the child-safety routes if any image or video was taken before you were 18.**  
   Tell a trusted adult if that feels safe. You can report child sexual exploitation through NCMEC’s CyberTipline and use NCMEC’s Take It Down service. Do not download, send, or share an image or video to submit it; use Take It Down only for a file already on your device.

6. **Use the adult image-hashing route if it fits your situation.**  
   StopNCII.org may help if you are currently over 18, were 18 or older when the image or video was taken, are the person shown, and still have the file. It creates a digital fingerprint on your device that participating platforms can use to help detect and remove matching uploads.

7. **Report cyber-enabled crime if you choose to.**  
   You can file a report with the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center, IC3. If any image or video was taken before you were 18, use NCMEC’s CyberTipline.

8. **Get support for the panic as well as the threat.**  
   RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline offers confidential support. If you are thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 or use the 988 Lifeline chat. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to report to police or a federal agency.
- You do not need to post a public explanation or warn everyone immediately.
- You do not need to pay for a takedown service while you are panicked.
- You do not need to keep replying to buy time.
- You do not need to make longer-term decisions about the relationship or account today.

## Important reassurance
This is not your fault. Threats like this can create panic and pressure. You are allowed to stop replying, ask for support, and take one step at a time.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about reporting, platform escalation, or specialist support can wait until you have reached a safer pause and may need help from an appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information, not legal, medical, mental-health, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. If you are thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 or use the 988 Lifeline chat.
