# What to do if… you are locked out of your payroll or HR system before a deadline (USA)
## Short answer
Contact your IT or service desk and the responsible payroll or HR owner immediately, including a written message. State the affected task and deadline, and ask for the approved access-restoration or contingency route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not share passwords or MFA codes.
- Do not ask another person to log in using your credentials.
- Do not move employee payroll data through personal email, personal text messages, or unapproved storage.
- Do not keep guessing passwords or repeatedly retrying a failed MFA flow.
- Do not create an unofficial workaround or change pay inputs without approval and documentation.
- Do not delay escalation because you are unsure who owns the issue. Contact IT and the payroll or HR lead so they can route it.

## What to do now
1. **Record the lockout.** Note the system name, time, exact error message, affected task, and deadline. Save a screenshot if your organization's policy permits it.
2. **Use the approved reset route.** Try the official password or MFA recovery process. If it fails, or the deadline is close, stop repeated attempts and contact support.
3. **Open an urgent IT ticket and call the service desk.** State the business impact clearly, such as a blocked payroll run, timecard approval, employee-data update, payment step, deposit, or filing task. Keep the ticket number.
4. **Notify the responsible payroll or HR owner in writing.** Include the affected deadline, IT ticket number, and the action you need from them.
5. **Ask for the approved contingency route.** Depending on your organization's rules, the responsible owner may be able to use a delegated approver, an emergency-access process, an approved manual payment route, or another documented fallback.
6. **Keep a simple incident record.** Note who was contacted, when they replied, and any approved instructions. Do not copy sensitive payroll data into the record unless authorized.
7. **If an employment-tax deposit may be affected, escalate to the authorized tax-payment owner immediately.** EFTPS has an official voice response option at 800-555-3453. The authorized owner should use only the official route and the required credentials or information.
8. **If you are an employee worried about your own pay, send a written message to payroll or HR.** State that you cannot access the system, identify the regular payday, and ask whether your pay is affected and whether an approved emergency-payment process is available.
9. **If your pay is missed and the employer does not correct it promptly, keep your pay records and check the appropriate wage-help route.** Depending on the circumstances, you may contact the U.S. Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division or the appropriate state labor office.

## What can wait
- You do not need to assign blame while the deadline is active.
- You do not need to reinstall apps, change devices repeatedly, or make security changes unless IT directs you.
- You do not need to redesign the payroll process during the incident.
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal complaint unless pay is missed or the issue is not corrected promptly.

## Important reassurance
A lockout is stressful, especially near a payroll deadline. Fast escalation, approved processes, and a clear record give the responsible teams the best chance to restore access, use a permitted contingency, or document the next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate deadline window. Later decisions may need help from payroll, HR, IT security, finance, a payroll provider, a tax professional, or an employment advisor.

## Important note
This guide provides general information, not legal, tax, HR, financial, or professional advice. Employer procedures, payroll-provider processes, tax responsibilities, and wage rules vary. Follow your organization's security rules and use only authorized access, payment, and contingency routes.
