# What to do if… you notice a vehicle has parked extremely close to your driver’s door and someone remains inside watching you (USA)

## Short answer
Do not approach your vehicle or attempt to squeeze into the driver's side. Immediately create distance by walking toward a well-lit, populated area and call **911** if you feel an imminent threat of carjacking or assault.

## Do not do these things
- Don't confront the occupant or attempt to signal them to move.
- Don't attempt to enter your vehicle through the passenger side if it requires you to turn your back to the suspicious vehicle.
- Don't stand near your car while looking for your keys or using your phone.
- Don't head toward secluded areas, stairwells, or empty levels of a parking garage.
- Don't ignore your instincts if the situation feels like a ruse or a "trap" setup.
- Don't unlock your doors remotely until you are certain you are entering the vehicle immediately.

## What to do now
1. **Change your direction immediately and move toward a staffed building.** Walk toward the nearest store entrance, security desk, or busy lobby without stopping to look back.
2. **Call 911 if you believe you are being targeted or followed.** Provide the dispatcher with:
   - Your specific location, including the business name, lot section, or floor number.
   - A description of the suspicious vehicle (make, model, color, and license plate if seen from a distance).
   - The number of occupants and their behavior.
3. **Request a security or police escort to your vehicle.** If you are inside a business, ask a manager or security guard to walk you to your car.
   - If no security is available, wait for a group of people to walk out and stay near them.
4. **Scan your vehicle from a safe distance before approaching again.** Ensure no one is hiding under the car or in the back seat. 
   - Check if the suspicious vehicle is still occupied or if the engine is running.
5. **Use your car’s panic button if you are approached.** Pressing the alarm button on your key fob can deter an assailant by drawing immediate public attention.
6. **Enter the vehicle quickly only if the area is clear.** Have your keys in your hand before you reach the door, enter, and immediately lock all doors.
7. **Drive away immediately once inside.** Do not linger to adjust mirrors, check your phone, or organize belongings while parked in the same spot.
8. **Drive to a police station if you think you are being followed after leaving.** Do not drive to your home or workplace; head to a public, well-lit location or a designated safe zone.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the parking was accidental or intentional right now.
- You do not need to report the parking violation to the property owner until you are in a secure location.
- Obtaining a full license plate number can wait if trying to see it puts you in physical danger.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden "fight or flight" response when your personal space is restricted in a vulnerable setting like a parking lot. Taking the long way back to a safe building is a standard safety protocol used by security professionals and does not indicate an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate personal safety concern in a parking environment. It focuses on harm prevention and distance-creation during a potential confrontation or carjacking setup.

## Important note
This information is for safety awareness and does not replace the professional judgment of law enforcement. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, contact 911 immediately.
