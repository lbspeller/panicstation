# What to do if… a funeral provider pressures you for a same-day decision about embalming or refrigeration (USA)

## Short answer
Say: **“I am not authorizing embalming right now. Is refrigeration available while I decide? Please explain any same-day requirement and the price in writing.”** Ask for accurate, itemized prices by phone and ask for the General Price List to keep if prices or arrangements are discussed with you in person.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume routine embalming is required by law for every death.
- Don’t give express approval for embalming because you feel rushed, guilty, or confused.
- Don’t accept a package without asking for separate prices for the temporary care and services you are considering.
- Don’t assume refrigeration is available at that provider. Ask.
- Don’t accept an unexplained same-day deadline. Ask whether it comes from a state or local requirement, a practical issue, a viewing plan, transport, or the provider’s policy.
- Don’t rely only on a ballpark figure. Ask for accurate, itemized prices and request written confirmation.

## What to do now
1. **Use a clear script to pause the decision.**  
   Say: **“I am not authorizing embalming right now. Please note that in the file. Is refrigeration available while I decide?”**

2. **Ask for the immediate options and prices.**  
   Say: **“What temporary care options are available today? What does each option cost, and how is any refrigeration fee calculated?”**  
   If you are asking by phone, ask for accurate, itemized price information. If prices or arrangements are discussed with you in person, ask for the General Price List and keep it.

3. **Ask why a decision is needed today.**  
   Say: **“Is the deadline based on a state or local requirement, a viewing plan, transport, a practical issue, or your policy? Please explain it in writing.”**

4. **Ask about a viewing only if it affects the immediate choice.**  
   Say: **“Would a private family viewing without embalming be possible? If preservation is needed, is refrigeration available instead?”**

5. **Choose the least-irreversible temporary option that is available and suitable.**  
   If refrigeration is offered and fits the situation, ask to use it while you consider the next step. Ask for the price before agreeing.

6. **Ask for a written, itemized statement before accepting arrangements.**  
   Ask for each selected charge to be listed separately, including temporary care, embalming if discussed, preparation for viewing, transportation, and any additional fees.

7. **Ask about transfer if the provider will not slow down or explain the options.**  
   Say: **“Please tell me the process, timing, and cost if we decide to use another funeral provider.”** Write down the name of the person you spoke with and what they said.

8. **Ask about simpler arrangements if they fit your wishes.**  
   Say: **“What is the price for direct cremation?”** and **“What is the price for immediate burial or direct burial?”** Ask what is included and whether any additional fees apply.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a casket, vault, flowers, obituary wording, or detailed service arrangements just to ask about temporary care.
- You do not need to accept a full funeral package before you have itemized prices.
- Most viewing details can wait. For now, ask only whether the current viewing plan changes the immediate preservation options.
- You do not need to resolve every later decision during the first pressured conversation.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to pause before authorizing an irreversible procedure. You can ask whether refrigeration is available, request clear prices, and ask the provider to explain why any decision is needed today.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce pressure and buy time. Later decisions about state or local requirements, transport, timing, family disagreements, or the available arrangements may need help from a local specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Requirements and available options can vary by state, locality, provider, and situation. Ask for a written explanation of any claimed requirement and consider local specialist help if the position remains unclear.
