# What to do if… you notice someone waiting near your car and watching you approach (USA)

## Short answer
Stop your approach immediately and return to a well-lit, populated, or staffed area. Request a security escort or call 911 if you feel you are in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue walking toward your car to "test" the person or prove you are not afraid.
- Do not try to confront, shout at, or film the person from a close distance.
- Do not unlock your car using a remote fob until you are close enough to enter it immediately.
- Do not look down at your phone, fumble with bags, or wear headphones while walking.
- Do not accept help from a stranger who offers to "check" the area or walk you to the car.
- Do not get into the car if the person is close enough to reach the door before it locks.

## What to do now
1. **Change your direction immediately and move toward a staffed building or a group of people.** Seek environments with high visibility, active foot traffic, and visible security cameras to discourage pursuit.
2. **Request a professional safety escort from building management or security.** If you are at a mall, hospital, campus, or workplace, contact the security desk and state: "I am uncomfortable walking to my car because someone is watching me and waiting near my vehicle; I need a safety escort."
3. **Call 911 if you are being followed or feel an immediate threat of violence.** Provide the dispatcher with your exact location (lot name, level, or nearest cross-street), a description of the individual, and where you are currently seeking shelter.
4. **Remain in a safe, public area until help arrives or the situation is resolved.** Do not attempt to "wait it out" in an isolated area; stay inside a store or lobby where staff can see you.
5. **Scan your vehicle and the immediate surroundings from a distance once help arrives.** Look for anyone sitting in nearby vehicles, doors that appear slightly ajar, or people loitering in the shadows before you reach the door.
6. **Enter the vehicle and lock all doors immediately upon sitting down.** Start the engine and exit the parking area as soon as it is safe to do so; do not sit in the parked car to check your phone or organize items.
7. **Use your horn and lights to draw attention if someone attempts to block your car or approach your window.** Keep your windows rolled up and doors locked; if you cannot drive away safely, stay in the vehicle and call 911.
8. **Report the loitering or suspicious behavior to the property manager or local law enforcement.** Use a non-emergency number for reporting if the immediate threat has passed, as this helps security adjust patrols and review camera footage.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if your fear is "rational" or if you are overreacting right now.
- You do not need to obtain a photo or video of the person if doing so puts you at risk.
- You do not need to drive home immediately if you are physically shaking; you can remain in a safe, staffed area until you are steady enough to operate the vehicle.
- You do not need to confront the person to ask why they are waiting.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your intuition is a fundamental safety skill. Choosing to turn back and seek help is a proactive measure, not a sign of weakness or paranoia.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate personal safety concern in a parking environment. It does not replace professional law enforcement advice or long-term security planning.

## Important note
If you believe a crime is in progress or you are being targeted for carjacking or robbery, call 911 immediately. This information is for general safety awareness and does not constitute legal or professional security advice.
