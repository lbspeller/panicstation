# What to do if… your electric vehicle charging cable is stuck and will not release (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the charging session first, unlock the vehicle, support the cable weight, then try a careful straight release. If it still will not release, use only the vehicle manual’s release procedure and call the support number on the charger or in the charging network app.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t yank, twist hard, or pry with tools; it can damage the latch, connector, adapter, or charge port.
- Don’t use a vehicle’s manual or emergency charge-port release while the session is still actively charging.
- Don’t drive or shift out of Park with the cable attached.
- Don’t pull the cable tight while trying to release it; support the cable and relieve sideways tension first.
- Don’t open charger cabinets, power panels, or breakers.
- Don’t keep repeating the same forceful attempt if the connector is not moving.

## What to do now
1. **Make the scene stable.** Put the vehicle in **Park**, set the parking brake, and keep the cable slack so it is not pulling sideways on the port.

2. **Stop the charging session.**
   - Use the charging station screen, app, card reader, or stop button to end the session.
   - If your vehicle has an in-car or in-app **stop charging** control, use that too.
   - Wait for the station or vehicle to show that charging has stopped before trying a manual release.

3. **Unlock the vehicle and charge port.**
   - Unlock the vehicle with the key fob, phone key, door button, or in-car unlock control.
   - Keep it unlocked while you try to remove the connector.
   - If your vehicle has a separate charge-port unlock button or menu control, use that.

4. **Support the cable and use the handle release.**
   - Hold the cable so its weight is not hanging from the port.
   - Press or hold the connector’s release button or latch, if it has one.
   - Pull straight back, not up, down, or sideways.

5. **Reseat once, then try again.**
   - Gently push the connector fully into the port until it feels seated.
   - Press or hold the release button again.
   - Pull straight back with steady pressure, not a jerk.

6. **Try an unlock cycle.**
   - Press unlock a few times with the key fob or phone key, then try the release again.
   - If you are using a ChargePoint station, their guidance says to press unlock 5 times, check the connector, then toggle lock and unlock 3 times if needed.
   - Stop if the connector still feels locked; do not force it.

7. **If the connector is stuck at a public charger, call charger support.**
   - Use the support number on the charger, the station screen, or the charging network app.
   - Ask them to confirm the session has ended and whether they can reset the station or talk you through that network’s safe release steps.
   - Stay near the vehicle if it is safe to do so.

8. **If it is stuck in the car, use the vehicle manual’s release procedure.**
   - Check the owner’s manual for the exact manual or emergency charge-port release location and steps for your make and model.
   - Use it only after the charging session has stopped.
   - If you cannot confirm the correct release procedure, call your automaker roadside assistance, the dealer support line, or the charging network.

9. **If the place is unsafe or you are blocking traffic.**
   - Move yourself to a safer nearby spot while keeping the vehicle secured.
   - Call the charger support line, roadside assistance, site staff, or property staff.
   - Call 911 only if there is immediate danger, fire, sparking, smoke, a crash risk, or someone may be hurt.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide whether the car, charger, app, adapter, or network caused it right now.
- You don’t need to decide about repairs, reimbursement, complaints, or fault while you are still stuck.
- You don’t need to keep trying for a long time; after a few careful attempts, switch to charger support, the owner’s manual, or roadside assistance.

## Important reassurance
A stuck connector is often a lock, session, or communication problem, not something you did wrong. Slow, straight, low-force steps are more likely to release it and less likely to cause damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you get unplugged and safe enough to leave. Repeated sticking, visible damage, error messages, or adapter problems may need make-and-model support from the automaker, charging network, dealer, roadside assistance, or a qualified technician.
