# What to do if… your car won’t start and you are stranded in an unfamiliar place (USA)

## Short answer
Get out of immediate traffic danger first. Call 911 if you are in a travel lane, cannot get clear of traffic, see smoke or fire risk, are in a crash situation, or feel threatened; otherwise call roadside assistance and make your location easy to find.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t step into moving traffic or stand beside the vehicle on a narrow shoulder.
- Don’t keep trying to start the car if you smell fuel, see smoke, or suspect overheating or fire risk.
- Don’t push the car in or near live traffic.
- Don’t accept a tow from an unsolicited truck unless you can verify who sent it.
- Don’t hand over your keys, wallet, phone, or insurance documents to a stranger who is pressuring you.
- Don’t wander off alone unless staying where you are is clearly less safe.

## What to do now
1. **Decide whether this is a traffic emergency right now.**
   - If you are in a travel lane, on a narrow shoulder, on a curve, in poor visibility, or you feel at immediate risk, turn on hazard lights, stay buckled if getting out would put you in traffic, and call 911.
   - If you are safely off the roadway, such as in a parking lot or wide shoulder well away from traffic, move to the next steps.

2. **Make the vehicle visible without creating a new danger.**
   - Turn on hazard lights.
   - If the vehicle can still move under its own power, or can roll from the driver’s seat without anyone entering traffic, get it farther from moving vehicles.
   - Do not try to push it on a highway shoulder, in a lane, on a curve, or anywhere drivers may not see you.

3. **Choose the safest place to wait.**
   - If getting out would put you into traffic, it may be safer to stay buckled inside with doors locked, hazards on, and help on the phone.
   - If you can reach a protected spot without crossing lanes, such as behind a guardrail, up an embankment, or inside a nearby open business, move there and keep everyone together.
   - If you smell fuel, see smoke, see flames, or feel heat from the vehicle, get away from the vehicle and call 911.

4. **Call for help through a route you can verify.**
   - Call your roadside assistance provider, auto club, insurer, vehicle-maker assistance line, rental-car company, or vehicle SOS button if you have one.
   - If you must find a tow yourself, use a known local company or one arranged through your insurer, auto club, rental company, or police dispatcher when possible.
   - If someone stops to help, speak through a partly opened window or from a safe distance if that feels safer.

5. **Give your exact location.**
   - Say the highway or road name, direction of travel, nearest exit, mile marker, cross street, business name, or landmark.
   - Describe your vehicle by color, make, model, and where it is stopped.
   - Say whether you are blocking traffic, on a shoulder, in a parking lot, or away from the car.
   - Mention children, medical needs, disability, extreme heat or cold, no phone battery, or any reason you feel unsafe.

6. **Reduce the risk of predatory towing.**
   - Before agreeing to a tow, ask who sent the truck, the company name, driver or truck ID, destination, and estimated total cost.
   - Check that the truck matches the company you called or the company your insurer, auto club, rental company, or police dispatcher named.
   - If the driver pressures you to sign, refuses to identify the company, will not say where the car is going, or asks for unusual payment immediately, decline if you safely can and call your insurer, auto club, or 911 if you feel threatened.

7. **Save phone battery and keep one person informed.**
   - Turn on low power mode, dim the screen, and close unused apps.
   - Text one trusted person your location, who you called, and any estimated arrival time.
   - If you have no signal, move only a short distance and only if it is clearly safer than staying put. Do not walk along a highway to look for service.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the problem now.
- You do not need to choose a repair shop while you are exposed or overwhelmed.
- You do not need to argue about costs at the roadside; get safe, record the company name and destination, and handle disputes later.
- You do not need to call multiple people at once unless the first call fails.

## Important reassurance
A car that will not start in an unfamiliar place can make you feel trapped and rushed. Once you are away from immediate traffic danger, visible, and connected to verified help, you have already handled the most urgent part.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair, towing charges, insurance, rental vehicles, or complaints may need help from your insurer, roadside provider, local authorities, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911.
