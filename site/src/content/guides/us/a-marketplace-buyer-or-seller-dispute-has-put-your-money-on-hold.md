# What to do if… a marketplace buyer or seller dispute has put your money on hold (USA)

## Short answer
Open the dispute only through the marketplace or payment service’s official app or website. Save the case details and evidence now, then submit a clear response before the deadline shown in the case.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send a refund, replacement, return, or “verification” payment outside the official case process.
- Don’t click a payment-release or account-verification link sent in a message. Open the official app or website yourself.
- Don’t close or withdraw the dispute until you have checked what that action does inside the platform.
- Don’t delete listings, messages, receipts, tracking pages, photos, emails, or case-status pages.
- Don’t miss a deadline shown in the case.
- Don’t post account details, card details, or personal documents in public messages.

## What to do now
1. **Record the case details.** Save the case ID, transaction ID, amount, deadline, dispute reason, and current status shown inside the official app or website.
2. **Save the transaction record.** Download or screenshot the listing, order page, payment confirmation, messages, return instructions, dispute page, receipts, and any carrier tracking or delivery confirmation.
3. **Identify the type of hold.** Check whether the issue is a marketplace case, a payout review, a refund, a reversal, or a charge on your card statement. Use the instructions for the exact issue shown in your account.
4. **If you are the seller, submit the evidence the case requests.** Add tracking in the case field where requested or available. Upload relevant delivery confirmation, listing details, messages, and any photos or serial-number records you already have.
5. **If you are the buyer, follow the displayed return or dispute instructions.** Keep photos of the item and packaging. If the platform tells you to return the item, use the stated method and keep the receipt and tracking details where available.
6. **Send one clear timeline.** State what was ordered, paid, shipped, delivered, returned, or disputed, with dates. Attach the most relevant records rather than a long argument.
7. **Record every displayed deadline.** Set reminders and submit the requested response before the deadline. Missing a deadline may affect the review.
8. **If a charge appears on your credit-card statement and you think it is a billing error, contact the card issuer right away.** To protect federal billing-error rights, send a written notice to the issuer’s billing-inquiries address early enough to arrive within 60 days after the first statement showing the error was sent to you. Keep a copy and proof that you sent it. A different process may apply to some disputes about the quality of goods or services.
9. **If the issue involves a financial company and remains unresolved, consider a CFPB complaint after meeting urgent case deadlines.** Keep the case number, timeline, and documents ready.
10. **If someone asks for an off-platform payment or you suspect a scam, stop the conversation.** Use the marketplace’s official support route and consider reporting suspected fraud to the FTC.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to bring a court claim or seek broader legal help.
- You don’t need to contact several agencies at once.
- You don’t need to prove anyone’s intent.
- You don’t need to write a long argument before saving the records and meeting the next deadline.

## Important reassurance
A hold does not by itself decide who is right. Saving the records, using the official process, and meeting the next displayed deadline are useful first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilize the situation and reduce avoidable mistakes. Later decisions may need specialist help and will depend on the marketplace rules, payment method, dispute reason, and documents.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Marketplace procedures and payment protections vary. Check the instructions shown in your account and use official support routes.
