# What to do if… you are asked to prove your identity because your passport photo no longer resembles you closely (USA)

## Short answer
Request a manual identity verification or the TSA ConfirmID process if facial recognition fails. Present two secondary forms of identification and prepare for additional screening or a secondary interview with Customs and Border Protection (CBP).

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue with the officer or attempt to explain your medical or personal history unless specifically asked.
- Do not offer to show digitally altered or filtered photos on your phone as proof of appearance.
- Do not walk away from the checkpoint or inspection booth until the officer explicitly dismisses you.
- Do not joke about security, identity theft, or "looking like a criminal" during the verification process.
- Do not touch your bags or documents once they have been taken for inspection unless instructed to do so.

## What to do now
1. **State clearly that your appearance has changed and you are ready for further verification.** Use a neutral statement: "My appearance has changed since this photo was taken; I have secondary documents to help verify my identity."
2. **Present at least two alternative forms of government-issued or official identification.** 
   - A REAL ID-compliant state driver’s license or ID card.
   - A secondary photo ID such as a work badge, student ID, or military ID.
   - Non-photo supporting items like a Social Security card, birth certificate, or a utility bill matching the name on your itinerary.
3. **Request the TSA ConfirmID process if you are at a domestic security checkpoint and cannot be verified.** As of February 2026, if TSA cannot match your identity via facial biometrics or documents, you may be referred to the ConfirmID system.
   - This requires paying a non-refundable $45 fee via the TSA website or at the checkpoint.
   - The verification covers a 10-day travel window; keep your payment receipt (printed or digital) for your return flight.
4. **Follow all instructions for additional physical screening if the photo match is inconclusive.** This may involve a pat-down, a thorough search of carry-on property, and a manual review of your travel history by a supervisor.
5. **Comply with the "Secondary Inspection" request if entering the United States via CBP.** If the biometric kiosk or primary officer cannot verify your likeness, you will be escorted to a separate area.
   - Answer basic questions (name, birth date, address, and purpose of travel) concisely.
   - Be aware that CBP has the authority to search electronic devices during this process if identity remains unverified.
6. **Notify your airline immediately if the delay threatens your boarding time.** Ask the gate agent or airline representative to note in your record that you are undergoing a mandatory identity verification.
7. **Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate if this occurs while attempting to board a flight to the U.S. from abroad.** While they cannot override an airline's decision to deny boarding, they can assist with an emergency passport renewal if your current photo is deemed "no longer a good likeness" by local authorities.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain the "why" of your appearance change (e.g., medical details, personal choices) unless it is relevant to a specific question about your identity.
- You do not need to apply for a new passport while at the airport; the immediate goal is clearing the current checkpoint.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or redress request during the inspection; wait until you have reached your destination.

## Important reassurance
Identity verification delays due to photo mismatches are common and procedures exist specifically to handle them. Being moved to a secondary area or asked for more ID is a standard security protocol, not an accusation of a crime. Most likeness issues are resolved within 30 to 60 minutes once secondary checks are completed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate identity challenge during travel. It does not cover long-term legal disputes or the full passport renewal process.

## Important note
This is general information for travelers in 2026 and does not constitute legal advice. Individual officer discretion and specific airport security levels may alter these procedures. If identity cannot be verified, boarding or entry may be denied.
