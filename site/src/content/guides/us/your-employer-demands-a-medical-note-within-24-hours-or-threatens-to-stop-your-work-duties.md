# What to do if… your employer demands a medical note within 24 hours or threatens to stop your work duties (USA)

## Short answer
Reply in writing today. Ask exactly what note is required, whether you must stop any duties immediately, and whether the request is based on an attendance policy, a fitness-for-duty concern, FMLA, or an accommodation process. Say you are arranging care and ask whether interim proof or a brief extension will be accepted while you obtain the requested note.

## Do not do these things
- Do not forge, alter, or backdate a medical note.
- Do not continue a duty after you have been told to stop, especially if the work may be safety-sensitive.
- Do not send your full medical records automatically. Ask what information is needed, why it is needed, and who should receive it.
- Do not quit or agree to resign impulsively to make the pressure stop.
- Do not assume that a 24-hour deadline is valid or invalid before asking what process the employer is using.
- Do not rely only on a phone call. Follow up in writing so there is a clear record.

## What to do now
1. **Pause the affected duties safely.**  
   If you have been told to stop a task, ask your manager for written instructions about what you should do for the rest of the shift. Ask whether you should wait, leave the work area, perform unaffected duties, or contact HR.

2. **Confirm the request in writing.**  
   Send a brief message to your manager or HR. Ask what document is required, who must provide it, who should receive it, and what “taken off work duties” means. Ask whether it means unpaid time, a schedule change, removal from particular tasks, or another temporary status.

3. **Ask what process the employer is using.**  
   Ask whether the request concerns a routine attendance policy, a return-to-work or fitness-for-duty concern, FMLA leave, a disability-related accommodation request, or a pregnancy-, childbirth-, or related-condition accommodation request. Ask for the relevant policy or form.

4. **Ask about FMLA timing if leave may be involved.**  
   If the employer is requesting an FMLA medical certification, an employer generally must allow at least 15 calendar days after the request, unless providing it within that time is not practicable despite diligent, good-faith efforts. Ask HR whether the request is for FMLA certification and ask for the applicable FMLA notices and any form they want completed.

5. **Request a temporary adjustment if you need one.**  
   If a medical condition is affecting your work, say in writing that you are requesting a temporary adjustment while any requested documentation is pending. If the request relates to a disability, ask what focused information is needed about your limitations and the requested adjustment. If it relates to pregnancy, childbirth, or a related medical condition, say that clearly and ask what temporary adjustment process applies and what information, if any, is needed.

6. **Try to obtain the requested documentation promptly.**  
   Contact your clinician, telehealth provider, urgent care clinic, or another appropriate medical provider. Explain the employer’s deadline and ask what documentation they can provide. If the final note cannot be ready within 24 hours, ask HR whether an appointment confirmation, clinician message, or other interim proof will be accepted while you wait.

7. **Ask for a brief extension and a safe temporary work plan.**  
   State when you expect to provide an update. Ask whether the deadline can be extended and whether any safe temporary work arrangement is available while the documentation is pending.

8. **Save the record and check your workplace documents.**  
   Keep copies of the request, your replies, appointment confirmation, and any note you provide. Check the employee handbook for attendance, medical-documentation, leave, and fitness-for-duty rules. If you are covered by a union agreement, contact your union representative promptly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to resign, file a complaint, or hire a lawyer.
- You do not need to send your full medical history before asking what documentation is required.
- You do not need to solve every employment issue at once. First confirm the request, arrange the note, and create a written record.

## Important reassurance
A 24-hour demand can feel impossible when appointments are hard to obtain. A prompt written reply, a clear question about what is required, and a documented attempt to get the note can help stabilize the situation even if the final document is not ready immediately.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may depend on the reason for the request, your job duties, workplace policy, union agreement, and any applicable federal, state, or local protections. Specialist help may be useful if the issue continues.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. An appropriate professional can advise you about your specific circumstances.
