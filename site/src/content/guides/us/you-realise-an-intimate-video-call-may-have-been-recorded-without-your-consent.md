# What to do if… you realise an intimate video call may have been recorded without your consent (USA)

## Short answer
Stop all contact with the other person immediately and do not pay any money or send further images. Contact a confidential specialist hotline to help you navigate new 2026 federal takedown laws and secure your accounts.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay any money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency to prevent the video from being shared.
- Don't negotiate with the person making threats or provide more images to "appease" them.
- Don't delete your existing message history or the person's profile details in a panic.
- Don't try to "hack back" or retaliate against the person who recorded you.
- Don't wait for the video to be posted before seeking confidential help from a hotline.

## What to do now
1. **End the call and disconnect from the internet immediately.** **Closing the application and turning off your connection stops any ongoing data transmission or recording processes.**
2. **Stop all communication with the person involved without a "final" goodbye.** **Ceasing contact prevents the person from using your reactions to escalate their pressure or demands.** 
3. **Capture a single set of screenshots of the threat and the person’s profile.** **Having a record of the username, platform ID, and any specific demands is necessary for future reporting but do not spend time on extensive investigation.**
4. **Call or text a confidential crisis line for expert guidance.** **The Cyber Civil Rights Initiative (CCRI) Helpline at 1-844-878-2274 and the RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline at 1-800-656-HOPE provide free, 24/7 support for image-based abuse.**
5. **Request a 48-hour federal takedown if the content has been posted.** **Under the Take It Down Act (effective May 2026), covered platforms must remove nonconsensual intimate imagery within 48 hours of a valid request.**
6. **Report the incident to the appropriate federal agency based on your age.** **If you are 18 or older, use the FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (ic3.gov); if you were under 18 when the recording happened, report immediately to the NCMEC CyberTipline.**
7. **Use the "Take It Down" tool for images involving minors.** **This NCMEC service allows you to create an anonymous digital fingerprint (hash) of the video to help participating platforms automatically block it from being uploaded.**
8. **Lock down your privacy settings on all social media and email accounts.** **Changing your passwords and enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) prevents the person from accessing your contact lists or other private data.**
9. **Check for technical indicators of recording if you are unsure it happened.** **Note if you saw a screen-recording notification, an unexpected "red light" icon, or if the other person's behavior suddenly changed or they asked you to perform specific actions for the camera.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether you want to file a local police report.
- You do not need to tell family members, partners, or employers until you have spoken with a confidential advocate.
- You do not need to understand every specific state-level "revenge porn" law immediately.
- You do not need to worry about permanent "internet footprints" while you are in the first minutes of stabilization.

## Important reassurance
Panic, fear, and a sense of betrayal are normal responses to a violation of your privacy. You are not responsible for the illegal or nonconsensual actions of another person, regardless of the nature of the call. Support services exist specifically to help people in this exact situation, and modern federal laws provide clear paths for removal and protection.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions. It is not a substitute for legal advice or long-term therapeutic support.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is current as of March 2026. Reporting requirements and platform responsibilities may vary. If you are in immediate physical danger, contact local law enforcement or 911.
