# What to do if… you see someone suspicious or lingering at your door on your security camera (USA)

## Short answer
Do not open the door. Stay behind a locked door, move away from the entry, and call 911 if you feel in danger or think a crime is happening.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door “just to check”.
- Do not step outside to confront them or “scare them off”.
- Do not say you are alone or that you are coming to the door.
- Do not keep arguing through the door or camera speaker.
- Do not ignore behaviour like trying the handle, peeking in windows, blocking your exit, or refusing to leave.

## What to do now
1. **Put a barrier between you and the door.** Lock the door, lock any nearby exterior doors, and move yourself, children, pets, or visitors away from the entryway and front windows.
2. **Check only from a protected position.** Use the doorbell camera live view, a peephole if you are already safely behind the door, or another place away from glass and the entry. Turn on exterior lights if you can do that without going outside.
3. **Use closed-door communication only if it feels safe.** Speak through the doorbell camera speaker, intercom, or closed door. Keep it brief: “I can’t open the door. Please leave.” Then stop engaging.
4. **Call 911 if there is danger or possible crime in progress.** Call if the person is trying the door, looking into windows, threatening you, blocking an exit, refusing to leave after being told to, or if the situation is escalating.
5. **If it is not an emergency, make a local report without opening the door.** Use a saved police non-emergency number or search the official website for the local law enforcement agency where you are. Do not call 911 just to ask for a non-emergency number.
6. **If speaking could put you at risk, use the safest contact route.** Calling 911 is usually best if you can speak safely. If you cannot speak and your area supports Text-to-911, text 911; if texting is not available, you should usually receive a bounce-back message saying the text was not delivered.
7. **Give dispatch clear facts.** Say your exact address and unit number, what is happening now, when it started, whether the person tried the door or looked inside, and a plain description of the person’s clothing, direction of travel, and anything carried.
8. **Use building help instead of going out.** If you live in an apartment, dorm, hotel, or managed building, call the front desk, concierge, building security, or on-site manager to check shared hallways, lobbies, or entrances.
9. **Save only what you need in case you report it.** Save or export the relevant doorbell clip and note the time, date, and what you saw. Do not post the video online in the moment.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the person was “casing” the home, confused, delivering something, or making a mistake.
- You do not need to investigate who they are, message strangers, post the clip, or search outside.
- You do not need to buy equipment, change locks, or make long-term security decisions while you are still alarmed.

## Important reassurance
A doorbell alert like this can make your body react fast. Staying inside, keeping the door locked, and switching to “observe, don’t engage” is a reasonable way to protect yourself while you work out what is happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes to hour. If this repeats or you still feel unsafe later, a follow-up report or specialist safety advice may help when you are no longer in the immediate moment.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, emergency-management, security, or professional advice. If you think you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, call 911.
