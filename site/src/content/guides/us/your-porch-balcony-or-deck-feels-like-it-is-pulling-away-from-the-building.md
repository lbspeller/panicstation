# What to do if… your porch, balcony, or deck feels like it is pulling away from the building (USA)

## Short answer
Evacuate the structure immediately and restrict all access to the area both on and below the platform. Call 911 if there is visible movement, loud cracking, or an imminent threat to public walkways or neighboring property.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk back onto the structure to retrieve furniture, plants, or personal items.
- Do not "test" the stability by jumping, shaking the railings, or adding weight to see if it holds.
- Do not attempt to "tie" the deck to the house using ropes, hardware store brackets, or temporary nails.
- Do not stand underneath or near the base of the structure, even for a quick inspection.
- Do not ignore "warning sounds" such as metallic pings, wood splintering, or sudden pops.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the structure immediately and ensure all occupants are on solid ground.** If the exit from the home requires crossing the unstable area, move quickly and do not linger or stop to lock doors from the outside.
2. **Block all points of entry to the affected area from inside the building.** Lock sliding doors or gates leading to the porch, balcony, or deck, and place a physical barrier or highly visible "DANGER - DO NOT USE" sign on the door.
3. **Establish a "no-go" perimeter underneath and around the potential fall zone.** If the structure is above a sidewalk, driveway, or common area, use cones, tape, or furniture on the ground level to prevent people from walking beneath it.
4. **Call 911 if the situation represents an immediate threat to life or public safety.** This includes instances where the structure is actively shifting, pieces are falling, or it is located over a public right-of-way where you cannot personally control pedestrian traffic.
5. **Contact 311 or your local municipal building department for non-emergency structural hazards.** Many US cities require an immediate report for "dangerous buildings" or structural failures to ensure a city inspector can verify the site is secured.
6. **Notify the landlord or property manager immediately if you are a renter or in a multi-family building.** Mention specifically that the structure appears to be pulling away from the ledger board (the beam connecting it to the house), as this is a high-priority life-safety issue.
7. **Document the separation from a safe distance once the area is secured.** Take photos of the gap between the building and the structure through a window or from a safe spot on the ground, noting any visible rust on bolts or rotting wood.
8. **Check for secondary hazards like strained utility lines.** If gas lines or electrical conduits are attached to the moving structure, look for hissing, the smell of rotten eggs, or sparking; if found, evacuate the entire building and call 911 and the utility provider.
9. **Verify compliance with local 2026 inspection mandates if in California or similar jurisdictions.** For multi-family buildings (3+ units), ensure the property owner is aware of mandatory "Balcony Law" (SB 721/SB 326) requirements which may dictate specific emergency reporting procedures.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the failure (such as rot, improper flashing, or nail-only connections) today.
- You do not need to seek repair estimates or contact contractors until the area is professionally stabilized.
- You do not need to move heavy outdoor furniture or grills off the structure; leave them in place to avoid personal risk.

## Important reassurance
Structural failures often provide sensory warnings before a total collapse. By noticing the "pulling away" sensation or seeing a gap now, you are acting during a window where injury is still preventable. Hesitating to report a structural concern is common, but these issues do not self-correct and require professional intervention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute structural concern. It does not replace a formal inspection by a licensed structural engineer or a municipal building official.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and is not engineering or legal advice. Structural integrity can only be verified by a qualified professional on-site. If you suspect an immediate collapse, stay clear and contact emergency services.
