# What to do if… your kitchen cabinet base is wet and you cannot find the source of the leak (USA)

## Short answer
Stop the water first: turn off the hot and cold shutoff valves under the sink if they turn easily, and use the home’s main water shutoff if you cannot isolate it. Step away from wet outlets, cords, or appliance plugs, and only use a breaker panel from a dry, safe location.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep using the faucet, dishwasher, garbage disposal, or sprayer to test where the leak is coming from.
- Don’t touch wet cords, outlets, power strips, appliance plugs, disposal wiring, or anything electrical inside or near the cabinet.
- Don’t stand in water to reach a breaker panel.
- Don’t force a stuck shutoff valve or add extra leverage if you are unsure.
- Don’t loosen fittings while the water is still on.
- Don’t leave standing water on a particleboard or fiberboard cabinet base.

## What to do now
1. **Make the area safer.** Put on shoes, move dry items out of the cabinet, and keep children and pets away from the wet area.

2. **Stop water at the closest valve that turns easily.**
   - Turn the hot and cold shutoff valves under the sink clockwise.
   - If the cabinet keeps getting wetter, the valves are missing, or you think an appliance line is involved, shut off the home’s main water valve.
   - If you rent and cannot access the main valve, contact the landlord, property manager, or maintenance line right away.

3. **Check whether the supply is off without running water into the cabinet.**
   - Open a different nearby faucet briefly, away from the leaking cabinet if possible.
   - If flow slows or stops, the supply is likely off.
   - If water keeps flowing normally and you cannot shut off the main valve, stop trying valves and get help from maintenance, a plumber, or your local water utility’s emergency contact route.

4. **If water is near electricity, stop and isolate power only if you can do it safely.**
   - Do not touch wet plugs, cords, outlets, the disposal switch area, or a wet appliance.
   - From a dry location, turn off the clearly labeled breaker for the wet outlet, disposal, dishwasher, or nearby kitchen circuit.
   - If the breaker is not clear, you would need to stand in water, or you are unsure, step back and call a qualified electrician, landlord, or emergency maintenance.

5. **Stop any involved appliance if it is safe to do so.**
   - Stop the dishwasher cycle if you can do it from a dry position without reaching into wet wiring or spilling water toward outlets.
   - Turn off a separate dishwasher supply valve only if you can see it and it turns easily.
   - Leave the garbage disposal off until the leak source and nearby electrical area are checked.

6. **Contain the leak and remove standing water.**
   - Place a bucket, bowl, or baking tray under the wettest drip point.
   - Use towels to stop water spreading out of the cabinet.
   - Wipe or wet-vac standing water if you can do that without using electrical equipment near wet outlets or cords.
   - Leave cabinet doors open so the area can start drying.

7. **Do a quick source check without taking anything apart.**
   - Look for fresh drips at the faucet supply lines, shutoff valves, sprayer hose, P-trap, disposal connections, sink strainer, and any dishwasher hose entering the cabinet.
   - Look for water tracks from above, which may point to the faucet, sprayer, sink rim, or supply lines.
   - Look for pooling below the drain parts, which may point to the P-trap, disposal connection, or sink drain.
   - Only touch dry, non-electrical plumbing parts after the water is off and nearby power risk is controlled.

8. **Call the right help.**
   - If you rent, tell the landlord or property manager what is wet, what valves you shut off, whether water has stopped, and whether any outlet or appliance is wet.
   - If you own, call a qualified plumber, licensed where required locally, if water will not shut off, a valve is stuck, the leak resumes when water is restored, or you cannot identify the source safely.
   - Call a qualified electrician if any outlet, cord, disposal wiring, or appliance electrical connection may have been wet.

9. **Document only what is quick.**
   - Take photos of the wet cabinet base, visible drip points, nearby outlet or appliance location if relevant, and the valve or breaker you used.
   - Then stop investigating until the water and electrical risks are controlled.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact failed fitting right now.
- You do not need to run the faucet, dishwasher, disposal, or sprayer to prove the source.
- You do not need to choose a repair method, replacement fixture, contractor, or insurance route immediately.
- You do not need to clean everything perfectly before the leak is stopped and electrical risks are avoided.

## Important reassurance
Water under a sink often spreads along pipes, cabinet edges, and hose loops before it drips, so the visible puddle may not be directly below the source. Shutting off water, staying away from wet electrical items, and drying the cabinet base are the main first wins.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation. Diagnosis, repair, drying hidden materials, and decisions about contractors or insurance may need help from a qualified plumber, electrician, landlord, insurer, or restoration professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional, legal, insurance, plumbing, electrical, or safety advice. Follow local requirements and get professional help if water will not stop, electricity may be involved, or you cannot work from a dry and safe position.
