# What to do if… you smell gas or suspect a gas leak in your home (USA)

## Short answer
Leave the building immediately without using anything that could spark. From outside and away from the building, call 911, then contact your gas utility or propane supplier if you can do that safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not turn lights on or off, ring doorbells, plug or unplug anything, adjust thermostats, or run appliances.
- Do not use matches, lighters, candles, grills, cigarettes, or vaping devices near the area.
- Do not use a phone, tablet, computer, or landline until you are outside and away from the building.
- Do not open or close windows, operate a garage door, or start a vehicle close to the suspected leak.
- Do not try to find the leak, move the appliance, tighten fittings, or relight a pilot light.
- Do not go back inside until emergency responders, the gas utility, or your propane supplier says it is safe.

## What to do now
1. Stop what you are doing and leave. Tell everyone nearby to get out, including children and pets, and move to fresh air away from the building.
2. Leave without creating sparks. Use stairs instead of an elevator if you can do that safely. Do not touch switches, plugs, appliance controls, thermostats, garage doors, or doorbells.
3. Once you are outside and away from the building, call 911. Say that you smell gas near an appliance and have evacuated.
4. If you know your gas utility’s emergency number, call it from outside after 911. If you use propane, call your propane supplier from outside. Follow their instructions.
5. If you are in an apartment, duplex, shared house, or other multi-unit building, do not go back inside to knock on doors or use doorbells. From a safe outdoor spot, tell 911 there may be other people in the building.
6. If someone has collapsed, fainted, has trouble breathing, chest pain, confusion, severe headache, nausea, dizziness, or symptoms that are getting worse, tell 911 and keep the person in fresh air if you can do so safely.
7. If you use propane and the outdoor tank valve is reachable without going toward the suspected leak, you may turn the main tank valve clockwise to shut it off. Do not delay leaving to do this.
8. If you have natural gas, do not try to shut off the meter unless the gas utility or fire department instructs you to do it.
9. If you rent, notify the landlord, property manager, or building maintenance only after you are outside and emergency calls have been made.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out which appliance caused the smell right now.
- You do not need to collect belongings, paperwork, tools, or valuables.
- You do not need to decide who is responsible, what repair is needed, or who will pay for it while the area may still be unsafe.
- You do not need to test the appliance, relight anything, or check whether the smell has gone away.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to leave even if the smell is faint or you are not sure. With a possible gas leak, getting outside first and calling from a safe place is the safer choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. After the area is declared safe, later decisions about inspection, appliance repair, pipe work, insurance, rent, or replacement may need help from qualified gas professionals, your utility or propane supplier, your landlord, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information only, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, professional, or emergency-service advice. Follow instructions from 911 dispatchers, the fire department, emergency responders, your gas utility, and your propane supplier.
