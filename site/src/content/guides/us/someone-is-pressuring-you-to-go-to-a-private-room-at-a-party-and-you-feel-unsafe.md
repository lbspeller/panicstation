# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to go to a private room at a party and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Move toward other people, staff, or a trusted friend now. You do not owe politeness, an explanation, or more time when you feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a bedroom, back room, car, or quiet spot to keep them from getting upset.
- Do not keep negotiating or trying to prove your reasons.
- Do not accept a drink, vape, edible, or anything else from them if you are concerned about your safety.
- Do not let them separate you from your phone, bag, or friends.
- Do not assume you have to handle the situation alone to avoid drama.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a safer pause.**  
   Go toward other people, staff, the host, or a trusted friend. If it feels safe to say it, use a short boundary such as: **“No. I’m not going anywhere private.”**
2. **Ask one person to stay with you.**  
   Point to a specific person and say:  
   - **“Can you stay with me right now?”**  
   - **“Please walk with me to the host or staff.”**
3. **Tell the host or staff what you need.**  
   At a house party, say: **“I need help. Someone is pressuring me to be alone. Please stay with me while I leave.”**  
   At a bar, club, or event, tell staff or security: **“I feel unsafe. Please help me leave and keep this person away from me.”**
4. **Leave with support where possible.**  
   Call or text a trusted person: **“I feel unsafe. Please stay on the phone while I leave.”**  
   Leave with a trusted person or ask staff to help you reach a safe exit. Arrange a ride while you are in a public, staffed area where possible.
5. **Stay in a place with people and an available exit.**  
   Stay near staff, the host, security, or a group you trust while you wait for support or transportation.
6. **Call 911 if you are in immediate danger.**  
   Call if you safely can. If speaking could increase the danger, you can try texting 911 where that service is available. Text-to-911 is not supported everywhere. A voice call is usually the best way to reach 911 when it is safe to speak. If text-to-911 is unavailable, you should receive a bounce-back message.
7. **Contact confidential support if anything sexual happened without your consent, or you are not sure.**  
   You can contact RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline for support and ask about your options without deciding immediately whether to report. Call **800-656-HOPE (4673)**, use online chat, or text **“HOPE” to 64673**.

*Optional, only if you may want options later:* If something happened, try not to delete texts, chats, or photos right now. Safety comes first.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to report, confront the person, or tell other people details.
- You do not need to work out whether it was bad enough or whether you were clear enough.
- You do not need to write a perfect timeline right now. Get to a safer place and find support first.

## Important reassurance
Your discomfort is enough reason to leave and ask for support. Going quiet, freezing, or trying to keep the situation calm does not make unwanted pressure okay. It is reasonable to prioritize distance and support when someone keeps pushing for privacy.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and reduce risk. Later decisions may need support from a specialist advocate.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical care, call 911. You deserve support regardless of what you drank, wore, said, or did.
