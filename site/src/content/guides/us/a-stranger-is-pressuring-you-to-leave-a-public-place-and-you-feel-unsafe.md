# What to do if… a stranger is pressuring you to leave a public place and you feel unsafe (USA)

## Short answer
Stay in the public area and move directly toward staff, security, or a group of people. Say that you need help. Call 911 if the situation becomes an emergency or you need immediate police assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go with the person to another location to talk, help, or get something.
- Do not let the person guide you toward an exit, parking lot, car, elevator, stairwell, hallway, or restroom.
- Do not stay in a long conversation when you can move toward help instead.
- Do not feel that you must justify ending the interaction or moving away.
- Do not accept a ride, drink, or offer that separates you from the public area.
- Do not go straight to your car or home if you think the person is following you. Move toward a busy, staffed place instead.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a staffed area.** Go to a cashier, host stand, reception desk, security desk, or another clearly staffed point. Stay where other people can see you.
2. **Tell staff what is happening.** Say: “I need help. This person is pressuring me to leave with them and I do not feel safe.” Ask whether they can help you remain in a staffed area and contact security or a manager.
3. **Ask one person directly for help.** Say: “Can you stay with me while I call 911?” or “Can you get security now?”
4. **Call 911 if you need immediate assistance.** Start by giving your location if you can, then explain that a stranger is pressuring you to leave. Answer the call-taker’s questions as safely as possible and follow their instructions.
5. **Avoid leaving alone while the concern continues.** Ask whether staff, security, friends, or another trusted person can stay with you while you arrange a pickup or move to another staffed area. Ask for an escort if you need to reach your car.
6. **Text 911 only when a voice call is not safe or possible.** Text-to-911 availability varies by location. Call if you can and text if you can’t. If text-to-911 is unavailable, you should receive a bounce-back message.
7. **Write down details after you reach a safer pause.** Note what you remember about the person, their clothing, where they went, and any vehicle details. Do not stop in an isolated place to do this.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a non-emergency report.
- You do not need to confront the person or work out their intentions.
- You do not need to sort through messages or social media now.
- You do not need to replay what you think you should have done.
- After the immediate concern has passed, you can check whether the relevant local law enforcement agency has a non-emergency phone line, an online reporting option, or another reporting route.

## Important reassurance
Feeling uncomfortable is enough reason to move toward help and stay in public. Freezing, going quiet, or trying to stay polite can happen under stress. You can end the interaction without explaining yourself.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions may need support from local law enforcement or another appropriate specialist. If the person persists, follows you, threatens you, or you need immediate assistance, call 911 as soon as it is safe to do so.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, call 911.
