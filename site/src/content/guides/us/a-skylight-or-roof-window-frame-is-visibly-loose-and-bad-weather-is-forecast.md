# What to do if… a skylight or roof window frame is visibly loose and bad weather is forecast (USA)

## Short answer
Keep people away from the area below the loose skylight or roof window, do not go onto the roof, and arrange an urgent assessment by a qualified roofer or skylight contractor. If water is close to electrical fixtures or wiring, avoid the area. Only use the electrical panel during a safe pause, when no thunder is heard or lightning is seen, and only if the panel is dry, safely accessible, and the affected circuit is clearly identified.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go onto the roof or use a ladder while there is wind, rain, thunder, lightning risk, or a slick surface.
- Do not push, pry, or try to force the frame back into place.
- Do not stand directly below the skylight or roof window if the frame is moving, glass is cracked, or the ceiling is sagging or bulging.
- Do not touch or unplug plugged-in electrical equipment while thunder is heard or lightning is seen, or while the equipment or surrounding area is damp.
- Do not use fans, heaters, extension cords, or other electrical equipment near the leak or any damp ceiling area.
- Do not touch an electrical panel that is wet, damp, or in an area where you would need to stand in water.
- Do not agree to major replacement work under pressure during the immediate weather window if a safe temporary measure is all that is needed first.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safe zone indoors.** Keep people and pets away from the area directly below the loose frame. Move dry, safely reachable, non-electrical valuables away from the nearby floor area if you can do so without standing under the skylight or roof window. Leave plugged-in or damp electrical items alone.
2. **Watch for signs that the room is no longer safe to use.** Leave the room and close the door if the glass is cracked, the unit is moving, water is reaching electrical fixtures, or the ceiling starts to sag, bulge, crack, or shed material.
3. **Reduce electrical risk only during a safe pause.** If thunder is heard or lightning is seen, do not touch the electrical panel or plugged-in equipment. If water is near a ceiling light, ceiling fan, outlet, smoke alarm, or visible wiring, keep away from the damp area. When there is no active thunderstorm risk, switch off the clearly identified affected circuit only if the panel is dry, safely accessible, and you do not need to stand in water. If the panel is damp, access is unsafe, or you are unsure which breaker controls the area, do not touch it; call an emergency electrician, building maintenance line, or property manager.
4. **Document the condition from a safe indoor position.** Take photos or a short video of the loose frame, any visible gap, and any water staining or drips. Do not climb into the attic or onto furniture to get a better angle.
5. **Report the problem to the responsible building contact if applicable.** In a condo, townhome, rental, or HOA-managed property, report an urgent skylight or roof-window opening and water-entry risk. Ask who is arranging the contractor and whether there is an after-hours maintenance contact.
6. **Arrange an urgent professional visit.** Contact a roofer or skylight contractor and say that the skylight or roof-window frame is visibly loose after wind and rain is forecast soon. Ask whether they can make the area temporarily secure and weather-resistant until a full assessment is safe. Ask whether the contractor is insured and licensed where state or local rules require it.
7. **Use floor-level containment only if the room remains safe.** From outside the drop zone, place a bucket or waterproof covering where drips are landing. Do not work beneath a sagging or bulging ceiling.
8. **Contact your insurer promptly if this may become a claim.** Save photos, notes of calls, contractor details, and receipts for emergency work or materials. Do not delay a safe emergency visit or reasonable temporary protection while waiting for the insurer to respond.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the skylight or roof window needs full replacement, re-securing, flashing repair, or another permanent fix.
- You do not need roof-level photos during unsafe weather.
- You do not need to investigate the attic during the immediate weather window.
- You do not need to agree to a permanent repair before a contractor can inspect the frame and surrounding roof safely.

## Important reassurance
The loose frame and forecast need attention, but the immediate job is limited: keep people away from the hazard, avoid roof access, reduce water entry safely, and arrange qualified help. A contractor may be able to provide temporary protection until the weather allows a full repair assessment.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need a roofer, skylight contractor, electrician, insurer, property manager, HOA, or other appropriate specialist to assess the frame, glass, fasteners, flashing, roof surface, and any water-damaged electrical components.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, electrical, construction, insurance, or other professional advice. If the glass appears unstable, water is reaching electrical components, the ceiling is sagging or bulging, or the area no longer feels safe, prioritize moving people away and getting qualified help.
