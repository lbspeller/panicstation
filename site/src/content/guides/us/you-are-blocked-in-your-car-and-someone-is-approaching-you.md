# What to do if… you are blocked in your car and someone is approaching you (USA)

## Short answer
Stay inside your vehicle with the doors locked and windows closed. If you believe you may be in immediate danger, someone is trying to get into the vehicle, or you cannot leave safely, call 911 while you are parked.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get out to confront the person or find out what they want.
- Do not unlock the door or fully open a window to talk.
- Do not make a blind or sudden move with the vehicle.
- Do not drive toward a person, another vehicle, or an uncertain gap.
- Do not argue or resist over the vehicle if someone threatens violence or demands it.

## What to do now
1. **Stay inside and secure the vehicle.** Lock the doors, close the windows, fasten your seatbelt, and start the engine if it is safe to do so.
2. **Check whether there is a clearly safe route out.** Use your mirrors and cameras, look for pedestrians, and check blind spots. Drive away only if you can leave without contacting a person, vehicle, or object.
3. **Call 911 early if you believe you may be in immediate danger or cannot leave safely.** While parked, tell the call-taker that your vehicle is blocked in and someone is approaching or trying to enter it. Give the store name, street address, parking-lot area, garage level, or nearest entrance. Use hands-free mode if it is available. Stay on the line unless the call-taker tells you to end the call.
4. **If you cannot speak safely, consider texting 911.** A voice call is usually the best option when you can make one safely. Text-to-911 availability varies by location. If you text, include your location and a brief description, then follow any reply. If the text does not go through, call 911 if you can do so safely. Do not text while driving.
5. **Use attention-getting measures only if they seem likely to help and not worsen the situation.** You may use the horn or activate the car alarm or panic function to draw attention to the vehicle.
6. **Keep the barrier in place.** Stay inside with the doors locked. If you choose to speak, use a closed window and keep it brief: “Please step back.”
7. **If someone threatens violence or demands the vehicle, prioritize your safety.** Do not argue or resist over the vehicle, especially if a weapon is involved.
8. **Once you can leave safely, go to a staffed or well-lit public place.** If you believe someone is following you, call 911, avoid going to an isolated location, and follow the call-taker’s instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a non-emergency report, contact parking management, or file an insurance claim.
- You do not need to confront anyone or photograph a license plate while you are still at risk.
- You can write down descriptions, vehicle details, and a timeline after you are safe.

## Important reassurance
Staying inside a locked vehicle, calling for help early, and leaving only through a clearly safe route are reasonable first steps. You do not need to work out the whole situation before taking the next safe action.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first minutes of an immediate safety concern. Later decisions, including reporting, insurance, or specialist support, may need separate professional help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you may be in immediate danger or someone is trying to enter your vehicle, call 911.
