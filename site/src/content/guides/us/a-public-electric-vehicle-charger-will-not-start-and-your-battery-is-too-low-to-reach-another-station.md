# What to do if… a public electric vehicle charger will not start and your battery is too low to reach another station (USA)

## Short answer
Park safely and stop using the charger if anything looks damaged or unsafe. Call the charging network’s support number from the station or app and ask whether they can help start or reset the session or identify another available stall at the same site.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the session repeatedly. Additional attempts can create pending transactions or make the problem harder to track.
- Do not force a connector that will not latch or release.
- Do not handle a charger with exposed wires, smoke, a burning smell, leaking fluid, or obvious impact damage.
- Do not use improvised cables, unapproved adapters, or workarounds that bypass the station instructions.
- Do not drive toward another charger unless you have enough indicated range, a safe route, and a clear plan.
- Do not try to push or tow the vehicle yourself if it cannot be moved safely.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safe pause.** Park legally, put the vehicle in Park, and use hazard lights if conditions require them.

2. **Check for an electrical or physical hazard before touching the charger again.** If you see exposed wires, smoke, a burning smell, leaking fluid, or major damage, stop handling the equipment. Move away if it is safe to do so and call 911 for an emergency.

3. **End the failed attempt cleanly and retry once.**
   - If the station screen or app shows a session in progress, use the available option to end it.
   - Disconnect only if the connector releases normally.
   - Reconnect the connector firmly.
   - Check the vehicle display or app for a paused or scheduled charge and select a charge-now option if one is available.

4. **Try another available option at the same site.**
   - Use another supported start method, such as the app, card, or tap option, if the station offers one.
   - Move to another available stall at the same site if it is safe to do so.
   - Avoid driving to a different location while your remaining range is uncertain.

5. **Call the charging network’s support line.**
   - Use the number printed on the station or the support route in the network’s app or website.
   - Give the station ID or stall number, the exact location, the message shown on the screen, and your battery percentage.
   - Say that you may not have enough charge to reach another site.
   - Ask whether support can help start or reset the session or identify another available stall.

6. **Arrange roadside assistance early if charging is still not available.**
   - Contact your vehicle manufacturer’s roadside assistance, your insurer’s roadside service, or AAA roadside assistance.
   - Say that the vehicle is an EV, provide your battery percentage and exact location, and explain that you may not be able to reach another charger safely.
   - Ask what assistance or towing option is suitable for your vehicle.

7. **Conserve power while staying safe.**
   - Remain parked while you arrange help.
   - Reduce nonessential climate-control and accessory use.
   - Keep lights and safety systems on whenever conditions require them.
   - Keep your phone available for support calls and location details.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose every possible technical cause now.
- You do not need to resolve pending transactions or request refunds while your battery is low.
- You do not need to decide on future charging networks, memberships, or route-planning changes today.

## Important reassurance
A failed charging attempt does not mean you have to solve the equipment problem yourself. Network support may be able to check the station, guide you to another stall at the site, or help you decide when to arrange roadside assistance.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment when a public charging session will not start and your remaining range may be too low to leave safely. Refunds, complaints, and deeper vehicle or charger diagnostics can wait until you are safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If there is smoke, fire, exposed wiring, or immediate danger, move away if it is safe to do so and call 911.
