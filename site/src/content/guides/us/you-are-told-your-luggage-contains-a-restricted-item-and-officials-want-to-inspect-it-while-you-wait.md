# What to do if… you are told your luggage contains a restricted item and officials want to inspect it while you wait (USA)

## Short answer
Stand in the designated area, keep your hands visible, and request a formal written receipt (such as CBP Form 6051D or a TSA property notice) for any item that is removed or seized.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reach toward the luggage or the official's hands while the search is in progress.
- Do not record video or audio; federal security checkpoints and customs areas are generally restricted zones for electronics.
- Do not make jokes about security threats, weapons, or explosives.
- Do not provide guesses about the item; if you do not know what triggered the alarm, state that you are unsure.
- Do not leave the area until you have been explicitly cleared or provided with a reference number for seized property.

## What to do now
1. **Identify which agency is conducting the search.** Determine if you are speaking with Transportation Security Administration (TSA) for safety screening or Customs and Border Protection (CBP) for trade and border compliance.
2. **Follow all verbal directions regarding your physical position.** Step back to the designated line or area and keep your hands away from your pockets and the luggage.
3. **Declare specific high-risk items immediately if asked.** State if you are carrying:
   - powders over 12 ounces (350ml) in carry-on bags,
   - loose or spare lithium batteries in checked luggage (which must be moved to carry-on),
   - currency totaling over $10,000 (representing the whole family or group traveling together),
   - agricultural products, plants, or food.
4. **Clarify the status of the item once it is identified.** Ask: "Is this item prohibited entirely, or can it be repacked into checked luggage or mailed home?"
5. **Request a supervisor if you are asked to pay a fee you do not understand.** If you are told you lack valid identification, be aware that as of February 2026, TSA may charge a $45 identity verification fee (Confirm.ID) to allow you to proceed.
6. **Obtain official documentation for any seized property.** If an item is taken, do not walk away without:
   - a Property Receipt or Notice of Seizure,
   - a case or tracking number,
   - instructions on how to petition for the item's return.
7. **Notify your airline if the inspection will cause a missed flight.** If you are permitted to use your phone, use the airline app or call their support line to request a "re-accommodation" due to security delays.
8. **Request an interpreter if you do not understand the instructions.** If you are a non-US citizen and the inspection moves to a private room (Secondary Screening), you have the right to ask for consular notification.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to legal contest a seizure on the spot; you will typically have 30 days to respond to a mailed notice.
- You do not need to provide a life history or travel itinerary beyond what is on your documents unless specifically questioned in a formal interview.
- You do not need to worry about "shoes-off" rules unless specifically directed; as of 2025, most travelers may keep shoes on, though secondary checks may still require removal.

## Important reassurance
Secondary inspections are common and often triggered by "organic" shapes on X-rays, such as dense food, coiled cables, or stacked paper. Being pulled aside does not necessarily mean you are in legal trouble or that you have committed a crime; it is a routine verification process to ensure the safety of the flight.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and conduct during a baggage inspection. It does not cover the legal defense of criminal charges or the long-term administrative process of recovering seized assets.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Security and customs protocols are subject to change by federal authorities at any time. If you feel your civil rights are being violated, remain polite, note the officers' badge numbers, and contact a legal representative after you have left the secure area.
