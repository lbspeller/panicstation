# What to do if… your luggage is too heavy to manage alone after an unexpected platform change or diversion (USA)

## Short answer
Stop moving immediately, keep your bags within arm's reach, and locate uniformed station staff or a Red Cap to request baggage assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to carry heavy luggage up or down stairs if you cannot lift the full weight comfortably with one hand.
- Do not use escalators with bags that are too heavy to balance or lift clear of the moving steps.
- Do not leave your luggage unattended to "run and check" the new track or platform location.
- Do not accept assistance from individuals who are not in official station or carrier uniforms.
- Do not block narrow walkways, platform yellow strips, or elevator doors while you wait for help.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safe pause point by moving out of the main foot-traffic flow.** Find a wall, a pillar, or a seating area where you can stand with your bags without being jostled by other passengers.
2. **Verify the new track assignment using a mobile app or stationary departure board.** Ensure you have the correct destination and track number before attempting to move any items.
3. **Locate a uniformed station agent or Red Cap assistant immediately.**
   - Look for staff at information booths, ticket counters, or standing near track entrances.
   - On Amtrak, look specifically for staff in red shirts or caps who provide free baggage handling.
4. **State your need for assistance clearly and briefly.** Use a direct phrase such as: "My luggage is too heavy for me to move to Track [Number] safely. Is there a Red Cap or station assistant who can help me?"
5. **Ask for the specific location of the nearest working elevator or ramp.** If a platform change requires moving between levels, do not assume the nearest elevator is functional; ask staff to confirm the "step-free" route.
6. **Move only one bag at a time if you must move short distances without help.** If you are forced to move your own bags a few feet, move the first bag, set it down, and return for the second while keeping both in your line of sight.
7. **Notify a conductor immediately if you are already on the train and a track change occurs at your destination stop.** Tell them: "I have heavy luggage and will need extra time or assistance to get off at the next stop due to the platform change."
8. **Request a rebooking at the ticket counter if the luggage weight makes it impossible to reach your train before departure.** Explain that the sudden track change created a safety barrier; staff can often move you to the next available departure at no extra cost.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide how to reduce your luggage weight for the remainder of your trip right now.
- You do not need to apologize to other passengers for taking up space while you wait for official help.
- You do not need to rush to a new track if the physical exertion feels like it will cause a fall or injury.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden surge of stress when a track change occurs, especially when traveling with heavy items. Pausing to prioritize your physical safety over "making the train" is the correct and professional way to handle station disruptions.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for managing heavy luggage during sudden transit changes. It does not cover long-term baggage shipping or permanent mobility assistance.

## Important note
Staffing levels and the availability of Red Cap services vary significantly by station and time of day. Always verify the identity of anyone offering to handle your bags. If you experience physical pain, shortness of breath, or injury after attempting to move luggage, notify station security or call emergency services.
