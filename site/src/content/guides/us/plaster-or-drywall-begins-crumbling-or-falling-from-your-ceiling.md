# What to do if… plaster or drywall begins crumbling or falling from your ceiling (USA)

## Short answer
Move everyone and all pets away from the affected area immediately. Keep the room off-limits until the area has been assessed and any leak or electrical risk has been addressed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand beneath the damaged area to inspect it, move belongings, or try to hold anything up.
- Don’t poke, pull, scrape, sand, drill, or remove loose material yourself.
- Don’t touch switches, fixtures, appliances, or a breaker panel if they are wet or if you would need to stand in water.
- Don’t dust, sweep, or vacuum debris that may contain asbestos.
- Don’t re-enter the area just to start cleanup or decide who is responsible.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from the affected area.** Leave the room and keep people and pets out. If more pieces are falling, the sagging is spreading, or the room does not feel safe to approach, stay farther back.
2. **Close the room off from a safe position.** Close the door if you can do so without stepping beneath damaged ceiling material. Do not go back in to move furniture or belongings.
3. **Call 911 for an immediate emergency.** Dial **911** if anyone is hurt, material is continuing to collapse, there is fire or smoke, you smell burning, you see sparks or exposed wiring, or you cannot leave the area safely.
4. **Avoid wet electrical equipment.** If electrical circuits or equipment are wet or near water, turn off power at the main breaker or fuse box only if you can reach it by a dry, safe route that does not pass beneath the damaged area. Do not enter standing water or touch wet surfaces to reach it. If you cannot do this safely, leave it alone and call emergency help or a qualified electrician.
5. **Note warning signs only from a safe position.** From outside the room, look for dripping water, stains, sagging, new or spreading cracks, falling material, or unusual shifting, cracking, or creaking sounds. Do not go closer to investigate.
6. **Report the problem urgently.**
   - If you rent, contact the landlord or property manager and say that ceiling material is falling and the room is unsafe. Mention any possible leak, electrical concern, or damage in a unit above.
   - If you own the property and there is an active leak, contact an emergency plumber. For sagging, cracking, or unclear damage, contact a qualified contractor or building professional and ask whether a structural engineer is needed.
7. **Document without disturbing anything.** Take photos or video from a safe doorway or another safe position. Note when the falling material started and whether there was a recent leak, storm, plumbing issue, or building work.
8. **Leave debris alone.** You generally cannot identify asbestos just by looking at ceiling material. If you do not know what the debris contains, do not clean it up, sample it, or disturb it. Ask a trained and accredited asbestos professional about assessment.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to clean up the debris now.
- You don’t need to decide whether to file an insurance claim now.
- You don’t need to move everything out of the room unless a qualified person says it is necessary for safety.
- You don’t need to work out the exact cause or who is responsible before getting people away from the area.

## Important reassurance
Getting out from under the damaged ceiling and pausing before cleanup are useful actions. You do not need to solve the cause immediately.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Repairs, inspections, insurance questions, and any landlord or contractor discussions may need appropriate specialist help after the area is made safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, inspection, or other professional advice. For immediate danger, injury, fire, electrical risk, or continuing collapse, call 911.
