# What to do if… your payment app account is locked and you have money stuck inside (USA)

## Short answer
Isolate the locked account by rerouting all incoming/outgoing payments to a different bank, then submit a formal written request for fund disbursement through official support and escalate to the CFPB or state regulators.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay "recovery specialists" or "account unlockers" on social media; these are secondary scams.
- Don't provide your full SSN, login codes, or passkeys to anyone claiming to be support on X (Twitter), Instagram, or via text.
- Don't delete the app or clear your cache until you have screenshotted your balance and transaction history.
- Don't ignore your linked bank account; a lock on the app may indicate a compromise of the underlying bank credentials.
- Don't repeatedly attempt to log in or reset your password if the system is returning a "blocked" or "restricted" error, as this can reset the security timer.

## What to do now
1. **Reroute all upcoming financial activity to a verified external bank account.**
   - Change your direct deposit settings with your employer or benefit provider immediately to avoid more funds entering the locked app.
   - Update "auto-pay" settings for utilities, rent, or subscriptions that were linked to the app's balance or its virtual debit card.

2. **Secure the primary communication channels linked to the app.**
   - **Update your email and mobile carrier security settings to prevent "SIM swapping" or email takeover.**
   - Enable hardware-based 2-factor authentication (if available) or an authenticator app for the email address associated with the payment account.

3. **Document the current state of the account and the restriction.**
   - **Take screenshots of the specific error message, your current "available" and "pending" balances, and the last five successful transactions.**
   - Save copies of any "Account Restricted" or "Security Review" emails received from the provider.

4. **Request a formal payout path via official support channels.**
   - **Send a message through the provider’s secure in-app help center or verified website requesting a timeline for fund disbursement.**
   - Ask specifically:
     - "What is the specific verification step required to release the remaining balance?"
     - "Can the funds be disbursed via ACH transfer to my linked bank or via physical check if the account remains closed?"

5. **File a formal dispute for any unauthorized activity under Regulation E.**
   - **If the lock was triggered by a transaction you did not authorize, notify the provider in writing that you are invoking your rights under the Electronic Fund Transfer Act.**
   - Contact the bank or card issuer linked to the app to report "unauthorized electronic fund transfers" to prevent further withdrawals.

6. **Escalate to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).**
   - **Submit a complaint at consumerfinance.gov or call (855) 411-CFPB (2372) if the app provider is a large company (processing >50 million transactions/year) and has not released your funds within 10 business days.**
   - Upload your screenshots and the timeline of your support requests as evidence.

7. **Use NMLS Consumer Access to find state-level regulators.**
   - **Look up the provider’s NMLS ID on nmlsconsumeraccess.org and click the "Submit to Regulator" link for your specific state.**
   - This routes you to the state agency (e.g., Department of Financial Institutions) that licenses the app as a "Money Transmitter."

## What can wait
- You don't need to hire a lawyer or file a small claims suit in the first 48 hours.
- You don't need to identify the exact technical cause of the "automated flag" that triggered the lock.
- You don't need to close the account permanently until you have verified the funds have been successfully transferred out.

## Important reassurance
Account locks are often automated responses to "unusual activity" or "compliance reviews" triggered by AI, and they do not always mean your money is gone. While the lack of human support is frustrating, there are established legal paths through Regulation E and the CFPB designed specifically to force these companies to respond to fund access issues.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for accessing held funds. It does not cover legal defense if the lock is related to criminal investigations or federal seizures.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes. Recovery timelines vary by provider and the reason for the hold. If the lock is due to suspected identity theft, visit IdentityTheft.gov for additional steps.
