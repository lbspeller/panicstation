# What to do if… a massage, beauty, or personal service appointment feels sexualised or unsafe and you want to leave (USA)
## Short answer
Get to a safer pause as soon as you safely can: end the service, leave the room, and go to a public area or outside. If you are in immediate danger or need immediate police, fire-department, or ambulance assistance, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay because you already paid or feel pressure to be polite.
- Do not force yourself to explain or debate what happened while you are still in the room.
- Do not accept pressure to continue, reschedule with the same person, or handle the situation privately on the spot.
- Do not make yourself decide right now whether the experience was serious enough to count.
- Do not pressure yourself to report before you are ready.

## What to do now
1. **End the service if you can do so safely.**  
   You can say, “Stop. I’m ending the appointment.” If speaking feels unsafe or too difficult, leave without explaining.

2. **Move to a safer place.**  
   Get covered enough to leave and go to the front desk, lobby, another public area, or outside. Your immediate safety matters more than finishing the appointment or dressing perfectly.

3. **Call 911 if you need emergency help.**  
   Call if you are being prevented from leaving, feel in immediate danger, or need immediate police, fire-department, or ambulance assistance.

4. **Ask for practical support if it feels safe.**  
   If someone is available and it feels safe, ask a staff member or security worker to stay with you or walk you outside. Call or text a trusted person and ask them to stay on the phone or pick you up.

5. **Contact specialist support when you are somewhere safer.**  
   RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline is available 24/7. Call 800-656-HOPE (4673), text `HOPE` to `64673`, or use its online chat. You can contact RAINN even if you are unsure how to describe what happened.

6. **Seek medical care if you are injured, worried about exposure, or want to ask about a forensic exam.**  
   You can seek medical care directly or ask RAINN or a local sexual assault program where to go.

If you think you may want a forensic exam, it feels manageable, and it does not delay urgent care, try not to shower, bathe, clean yourself, change clothes, use the restroom, or brush or comb your hair before speaking with a medical professional or advocate. If you have already done any of these things, you can still seek care.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to contact police, the business, a licensing body, or a lawyer.
- You do not need to decide whether to make a complaint about a massage provider.
- You do not need to write a detailed account while you are still overwhelmed.
- You do not need to choose a label such as harassment, misconduct, or assault before seeking support.
- You do not need to decide what to tell other people today.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, going quiet, smiling, or complying can happen when someone feels unsafe. You did not need to confront the provider or respond in a particular way. Nothing you did or did not do in the moment takes away your right to leave, seek support, or ask for medical care.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about medical care, reporting, complaints, or legal options may need support from a specialist advocate or an appropriate professional.

## Important note
This guide provides general information for immediate safety and support in the USA. It is not medical, legal, investigative, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
