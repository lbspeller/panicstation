# What to do if… you hear repeated knocking at your hotel room door late at night that stops when you speak (USA)

## Short answer
Do not open the door. Lock it fully, move back from the doorway, and call the front desk from inside your room; call 911 now if anyone tries the handle, tampers with the lock, threatens you, or you believe someone is trying to get in.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to check who it is, even if the person says they are hotel staff.
- Do not undo the deadbolt, chain, swing bar, or secondary latch while you talk, listen, or look.
- Do not say that you are alone, or share your name, plans, or travel details through the door.
- Do not go into the hallway to confront the person.
- Do not keep talking through the door if the knocking stops whenever you respond.
- Do not spend time troubleshooting the room phone if you feel unsafe and your mobile phone works.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the door and step away from it.** Use the deadbolt and any built-in secondary latch. Stand away from the door, not directly behind it, and keep your phone, room key, shoes, and wallet within reach.
2. **Make the room easier to move in.** Turn on enough lights to see clearly. Keep the bathroom door, curtains, and any connecting-room door closed and locked if they have locks.
3. **Stop giving information through the door.** If you need to say anything, say once: “I’m calling the front desk now.” Then stop responding through the door.
4. **Call the front desk using the number listed by the hotel.** Say clearly:
   - your room number
   - that there is repeated knocking at your door
   - that the knocking stops whenever you speak
   - that you are not opening the door
   - that you want the hallway checked before anyone comes to your room
5. **Verify any claimed hotel employee through the front desk.** Ask whether any employee has been sent to your room. Ask for the person’s name, role, and reason for being there. If the front desk cannot confirm it clearly, keep the door closed and treat the person as unverified.
6. **Ask for hotel security or the manager on duty.** Ask them to check the hallway and call you back, or stay on the line if they can, until they confirm what is happening outside your room.
7. **Call 911 if there is immediate danger.** Call 911 if someone tries the handle, pushes the door, tampers with the lock, threatens you, refuses to leave, or you believe someone may be trying to enter. Give the hotel name, street address if you have it, your room number, and what is happening. Stay on the line until the call-taker tells you otherwise.
8. **Use your mobile phone if the room phone does not connect promptly.** Many hotel phone systems are designed to reach 911 directly, but do not spend time testing or troubleshooting during a possible emergency. Use the phone that connects fastest.
9. **Text 911 only if you cannot safely speak.** If speaking would put you at risk and text-to-911 is available where you are, send the hotel name, address if known, room number, and the words “someone is at my hotel room door.” If the text does not go through or you receive a bounce-back message, call 911 if you can.
10. **Ask to change the situation once help is involved.** If you still feel unsafe after the hallway is checked, ask the hotel to move you to another room, escort you to the lobby, or help you leave the property safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to make a complaint, ask for a refund, post a review, or change the rest of your trip.
- You do not need to identify the person yourself before getting help.
- You do not need to listen at the door, check the hallway, or gather proof while you feel unsafe.
- Writing down the time, what happened, and who you spoke to can wait until you are behind a locked door, with hotel staff, or otherwise safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat repeated late-night knocking that stops when you respond as a safety concern. Keeping the door closed, verifying through the front desk, and escalating if needed are calm and proportionate steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Hotel security, a manager, or 911 dispatch can help decide the next safe step if the knocking continues or you suspect attempted entry.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, security, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 911 and follow the call-taker’s instructions.
