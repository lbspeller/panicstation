# What to do if… you are asked for proof of a return reservation but your return booking reference no longer pulls up (USA)

## Short answer
Locate your original e-ticket number or payment receipt and ask the airline or booking agent to re-send a verifiable itinerary that includes your full name and flight details.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use non-verifiable "dummy ticket" generators or forged documents.
- Do not buy a replacement ticket until you have confirmed the original is truly purged from the system.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to airline or security staff; display the screen yourself.
- Do not post images of your boarding pass or partial booking details on social media to ask for help.
- Do not assume the airline's website is the final authority if you booked through a third-party Online Travel Agency (OTA).

## What to do now
1. **Move to a lower-traffic area and keep your belongings secure.** If you are at a check-in counter or gate, step aside so you can search your records without the pressure of a queue behind you.
2. **Search your email and cloud storage for the 13-digit e-ticket number.** Look for keywords like "receipt," "itinerary," "confirmation," or the name of the travel agency/airline.
   - An e-ticket number (starting with the airline's 3-digit code) is more reliable than a 6-character booking reference (PNR).
3. **Attempt a manual lookup using variations of your name format.** Systems often fail to find records due to hyphens, apostrophes, or the merging of middle and first names.
   - Try: [Lastname][Firstname]
   - Try: [Lastname] [FirstnameMiddleinitial]
   - Try: Removing all spaces and punctuation from the name field.
4. **Contact the original booking source to request a "Status 1" or "Open" ticket confirmation.** If you booked via an OTA, they must provide the airline-specific record locator, which is often different from the agency's internal reference code.
5. **Request that the airline agent search by your passport number or credit card digits.** If the name and PNR lookup fail, airport staff can often locate a "dangling" ticket record using the form of payment or your secure flight passenger data (SFPD).
6. **Verify that your onward travel meets the specific "Contiguous Territory" rules if traveling on an ESTA.** Under the Visa Waiver Program, your onward ticket must generally be to a country other than Canada, Mexico, or adjacent islands, unless you are a legal resident of those territories.
7. **Download a PDF copy of your bank or credit card transaction showing the ticket purchase.** While not a boarding document, this serves as secondary evidence of a confirmed transaction that staff can use to trace a lost booking.
8. **Follow the 2026 DOT automatic refund protocols if the airline confirms the booking was cancelled without notice.** If you are forced to buy a new ticket because the airline lost your valid reservation, document the denial of service and the new ticket cost immediately for a later claim.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal DOT complaint while standing at the check-in counter.
- You do not need to decide on a legal strategy for a refund or "denied boarding" compensation until you have reached your destination.
- You do not need to clean up your email inbox or organize all travel files now; focus only on the specific return leg.

## Important reassurance
Booking references frequently "disappear" from apps due to schedule changes, synchronisation errors between agencies and airlines, or minor character mismatches. In most cases, the ticket still exists in the Global Distribution System (GDS) and can be retrieved by an agent using your e-ticket number or passport data.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a missing booking reference at the point of travel. It does not cover long-term legal claims, passport replacement, or visa application procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or immigration advice. Entry requirements and boarding permissions are subject to the discretion of the carrier and government authorities. Travelers under the Visa Waiver Program (ESTA) are subject to specific "onward travel" regulations defined in 8 CFR § 217.2.
