# What to do if… you are asked to leave a crowded service because it is at capacity and you need to continue travelling (USA)

## Short answer
Move away from the boarding area immediately to a safe pause point, then use official mobile apps or station staff to confirm your involuntary reboarding rights or refund options.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue with staff or the operator at the vehicle doors or on the platform.
- Do not attempt to force your way back onto the service once asked to exit.
- Do not cancel your ticket in the app until you have confirmed the "involuntary denied boarding" status with a staff member.
- Do not discard physical tickets, receipts, or digital QR codes.
- Do not block the flow of exiting or boarding passengers while checking your phone.
- Do not assume your ticket is automatically valid on the next departure without verification.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause point away from the crowd flow.** **Step away from platform edges, bus curbs, or stairways where movement is restricted.**
   - Locate a wall or a quiet corner of the station/stop.
   - Ensure your personal belongings are secure.
2. **Obtain the official "next-step" instruction from the carrier.** **Ask station staff or a conductor for the specific boarding procedure for the next available service.**
   - Request confirmation if "cross-honoring" is in effect for other transit agencies.
   - Ask if your current ticket requires a manual "reaccommodation" mark in their system.
3. **Contact the carrier's digital support line if staff are unavailable.** **Use the official app or text 268725 (AMTRAK) if you are traveling on Amtrak to request an immediate rebooking.**
   - For city transit (MTA, CTA, SEPTA, etc.), check the official X (Twitter) or agency website for "Service Alerts" regarding overcapacity.
   - Capture a screenshot of any service delay or capacity notice displayed on station screens.
4. **Identify the next departure time and platform.** **Check the station boards or mobile app for the next confirmed departure to your destination.**
   - Confirm if the next service is also expected to be at capacity.
   - Ask if there is a designated "overflow" vehicle or bus bridge being provided.
5. **Request a Refund Authorization Form or "Denied Boarding" credit.** **If the delay exceeds the carrier's threshold (typically 60+ minutes for rail), ask for a refund voucher or fare adjustment.**
   - Note that Value Fares may have specific 2026 cancellation fees (e.g., 25%) unless the carrier acknowledges the service disruption.
6. **Notify your destination contact of the delay.** **Send a single, concise message stating you were denied boarding due to capacity and will provide a new ETA once rebooked.**
7. **Adhere to Passenger Flow Compliance instructions.** **Follow all staff directions regarding where to stand or which exit to use to avoid fines for "exit blocking" or "flow interference."**

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal customer service complaint while still at the station.
- You do not need to calculate the exact refund percentage for your fare tier right now.
- You do not need to decide on alternative transportation (like rideshare) until you have confirmed the wait time for the next scheduled service.
- You do not need to worry about the long-term impact on your travel schedule until you have secured a confirmed seat or boarding pass.

## Important reassurance
Being asked to leave a service due to overcapacity is a procedural safety action, not a personal reflection on your conduct. Carriers have specific protocols for reaccommodation during peak periods or service disruptions. Taking a moment to step out of the crowd will help you make a logical decision about your next connection.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate 5–30 minutes following a denied boarding event due to capacity. It does not cover long-term legal claims or multi-carrier travel insurance disputes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Rules regarding ticket refunds, rebooking, and "involuntary denied boarding" vary significantly between private carriers (like Amtrak or Greyhound) and public transit agencies. Always verify the specific "Contract of Carriage" or "Terms of Service" for your operator. 2026 fare rules for Amtrak Value Fares include specific forfeiture percentages for cancellations not authorized as service disruptions.
