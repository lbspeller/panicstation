# What to do if… a childcare or school payment fails and services may be suspended (USA)

## Short answer
Contact the childcare provider or school office today. Ask what happens next, which payment methods are accepted, and whether services can stay active while you make a payment or agree a short plan.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the notice or assume another payment attempt will fix it.
- Do not send cash with your child unless the school or provider says this is allowed and explains how you will receive a receipt.
- Do not send card or bank details by text message or social media.
- Do not assume services will definitely continue or definitely stop before you ask.
- Do not start a chargeback as your first continuity-of-service step. A dispute may complicate the account. If you think a payment was unauthorized, contact your bank or card issuer promptly.

## What to do now
1. **Save the payment details.**
   - Take a screenshot of the error message or notice.
   - Note the amount, date, payment method, and any confirmation number.

2. **Call the office that controls the service.**
   - For childcare or daycare, ask for the administrator or billing contact.
   - For school fees, ask the main office who handles payments.
   - For a school meal balance, ask for the school or district food services contact.

3. **Ask one clear continuity question.**
   - Say: “A payment failed. What do I need to do today to keep the service active while I fix the payment?”
   - Ask whether a short grace period, partial payment, or dated payment plan is available.

4. **Ask for an accepted backup payment method.**
   - Ask whether you can pay in person or use another accepted method.
   - For a school meal account, ask which fee-free way to add money is available. Participating school meal programs may charge for an online option only when another way to add money is available without an extra fee.

5. **If this affects school meals, ask what your child will receive today.**
   - Ask for the written unpaid meal charge policy.
   - Say: “What meal will my child receive today while we fix the balance?”
   - Ask how to apply for free or reduced-price meals, or how to update your household information, if that may help.

6. **Confirm pickup arrangements if care may stop today.**
   - Ask whether after-school care or childcare pickup plans have changed.
   - Arrange a safe backup pickup if needed.
   - Tell the school or provider in writing who is authorized to collect your child.

7. **Ask for the agreement in writing.**
   - Request an email confirming the amount due, the accepted payment method, any grace period or plan, and whether care or participation continues.
   - Keep receipts and the name of the person you spoke with.

8. **Use a support route if the payment problem is part of a wider difficulty.**
   - If housing is unstable, ask the school district for its liaison for students experiencing homelessness and explain the situation so they can check what support applies.
   - If childcare costs are the problem, use a local child care resource organization or state resource page to look for financial-assistance contacts.

## What can wait
- Arguing about a processing fee before today’s care, meal, or pickup plan is clear.
- A formal complaint about the payment platform.
- Changing providers or making a major financial decision.
- Detailed troubleshooting of a bank app after you have confirmed the immediate plan.

## Important reassurance
A failed payment does not tell you what the final outcome will be. Contacting the office promptly gives them the information they need to explain the next step and consider any available options.

## Scope note
These are first steps for stabilising today’s care, school service, meal, or pickup plan. Later questions about eligibility, fees, contracts, or disputes may need help from the school district, provider, bank, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Policies vary by school district, state, program, and childcare contract. Ask for the written policy or contract if you need to check what applies.
