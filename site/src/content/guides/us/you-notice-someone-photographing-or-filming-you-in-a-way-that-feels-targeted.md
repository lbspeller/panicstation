# What to do if… you notice someone photographing or filming you in a way that feels targeted (USA)

## Short answer
Create distance first: move toward people, staff, security, or an open business, and do not confront the person alone. If you feel threatened, followed, trapped, or in immediate danger, call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t grab their phone, camera, bag, or other property.
- Don’t use physical contact to block the recording.
- Don’t confront them alone if you can move toward staff or other people instead.
- Don’t follow them for proof if it makes you more isolated.
- Don’t go straight home, to your car, or to a quieter place if they may follow you.
- Don’t argue about legal rights in the moment; your first job is to get safer.
- Don’t post the person online while you are still in the situation.

## What to do now
1. **Move toward a safer pause.** Step into a nearby business, lobby, reception desk, transit booth, front desk, staffed counter, or other visible public area. If you cannot go inside, move near a group of people, a well-lit entrance, or employees.
2. **Tell a real person exactly what is happening.** Say to staff, security, a driver, a station agent, a receptionist, or another nearby adult: “That person appears to be filming me and it feels targeted. Can you stay with me while I move somewhere safer?”
3. **Ask for a specific safety action.** Ask staff or security to walk with you to another area, wait with you until your ride arrives, help you use a different exit, or call security or police from the venue phone.
4. **Call 911 if the situation feels immediate.** Call if the person is following you, blocking your movement, threatening you, trying to isolate you, filming into a private area, or targeting a child. Give your exact location, what the person is doing, what they look like, and where they are moving.
5. **Use the local non-emergency police route if the danger is not immediate.** If you are safe but still concerned, contact the local police department where it happened. Many departments have a non-emergency number or online reporting option.
6. **Treat privacy-invasive recording as urgent.** If someone appears to be filming under clothing, into a restroom, into a changing room, through a private window, or anywhere a person is undressing or using a toilet, get staff or security immediately and call 911 if the risk is happening now. Laws vary, but this may be treated very differently from ordinary public filming.
7. **Document from safety, not from confrontation.** When you are away from the person, write down the time, exact location, what they did, what device they used, what they said, their description, and any vehicle details. If it is safe and does not escalate things, you can take a brief photo or video from a distance.
8. **Ask the venue to make a record.** Ask staff or security to note the incident time, preserve any available security footage, and give you an incident number or the name of the person you spoke to.
9. **Name any pattern clearly.** If this has happened before, or if the person has also contacted, followed, threatened, or watched you, say “I am worried this may be stalking or harassment” when you speak to staff, security, police, or a victim advocate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on the spot whether the recording is legal.
- You do not need to demand deletion while the person is still nearby.
- You do not need to make a full written statement before getting help.
- You do not need to decide today whether to take any longer-term legal steps.
- You do not need to explain or justify why the situation felt wrong before moving somewhere safer.

## Important reassurance
Feeling alarmed by targeted filming is understandable. In the USA, recording things plainly visible in public is often protected, and private venues can have their own rules, but that does not mean you have to stay near someone who is making you feel unsafe. You can leave, create distance, ask staff for help, and report threats, following, privacy-invasive filming, or repeated behaviour.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the behaviour repeats, involves someone you know, includes threats, or affects your home, work, school, commute, or child, later decisions may need help from local law enforcement, venue security, a victim advocate, or a lawyer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
