# What to do if… your photo library has duplicates and you are worried about deleting the originals (USA)

## Short answer
Do not delete anything yet. First make a separate backup or export that you will not edit, then use built-in duplicate merge tools where your photo app offers them.

## Do not do these things
- Do not bulk-delete duplicates while you are still unsure what caused them.
- Do not empty Recently Deleted, Trash, or Recycle Bin until you have checked that your backup or export opens.
- Do not install a duplicate-cleaner app as your first step.
- Do not keep switching cloud photo sync on and off across multiple devices.
- Do not move or rename large photo folders while a cloud photo app is still syncing.
- Do not assume the larger file, newer file, or cloud copy is automatically the original.

## What to do now
1. **Pause new changes.**
   - Pick one device to work from, preferably a computer if you have one.
   - On other devices, avoid opening, importing, deleting, or reorganizing the same photo library while you make a safety copy.
   - Leave cloud photo apps time to finish any visible syncing before you start merging.

2. **Create a safety copy you can keep untouched.**
   - **Apple Photos or iCloud Photos:** use Apple’s export or download options for your setup, so you have a copy outside the live-sync library.
   - **Google Photos:** use Google Takeout with Google Photos selected, then store the archive somewhere separate.
   - **Windows, OneDrive, iCloud for Windows, or an external drive:** copy the whole photo folder or library to an external drive or a clearly named backup folder that you will not modify.

3. **Check whether the copies are true duplicates.**
   - Open a few duplicate pairs and compare the filename, date, file size, edits, location, and whether both files actually open.
   - Check whether one item is an edited version, burst, Live Photo, screenshot, downloaded copy, or thumbnail before treating it as safe to remove.
   - Treat storage size as only one clue, because cloud optimized storage can make local size misleading.

4. **Use built-in merge options when available.**
   - **iPhone:** open Photos, tap Collections, tap Utilities, tap Duplicates, then use Merge for duplicate sets.
   - If Duplicates is missing on iPhone, lock the phone, connect it to power, and give Photos time to index the library; the Duplicates area may not appear if no duplicates are found.
   - **Mac Photos:** open Photos, click Duplicates under Utilities in the sidebar, select duplicate sets, then use Merge.
   - Prefer Merge over manual Delete when the app offers Merge.

5. **Stop a common duplication loop.**
   - If this started after a new phone, computer, import, or cloud migration, stop copying camera folders into a folder that is also being backed up to the same cloud account.
   - Check whether the same photos are being backed up from more than one place, such as the phone camera roll, a copied DCIM folder, a desktop sync folder, and a cloud import.
   - In Microsoft Photos or other gallery apps that show OneDrive and iCloud together, check whether you are viewing multiple sources rather than only one library.

6. **Secure the account first if anything looks unfamiliar.**
   - Turn on two-factor authentication or multi-factor authentication for the Apple, Google, or Microsoft account connected to the photo library.
   - Review signed-in devices or sessions and remove anything you do not recognize.
   - Change the account password if you suspect someone else accessed the account.

## What can wait
- You do not need to delete anything today.
- You do not need to organize albums, fix dates, rename files, or clean the whole library in one sitting.
- You can wait to troubleshoot the exact cause until after you have a backup or export you trust.
- You can postpone third-party cleanup tools until you know you have a recoverable copy.

## Important reassurance
Sudden duplicates can happen after sync changes, imports, cloud viewing, or device migrations. Slowing down and making a separate copy first greatly reduces the risk of losing originals.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent irreversible deletion and stabilize the library. Later decisions may need help from the photo service, device maker, cloud provider, or a trusted repair professional who can look at your exact setup.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional IT, legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If your service or device screens do not match these steps, prioritize making a backup or export and follow the provider’s official guidance.
