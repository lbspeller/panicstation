# What to do if… your phone updates and then gets stuck in a restart loop (USA)

## Short answer
Assume an ISP outage or a spontaneous hardware reboot has occurred. Perform exactly one sequential power cycle of your equipment and check your provider’s official outage map using mobile data before altering any internal network settings.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hold the recessed "RESET" button for more than one second unless you intend to wipe all custom Wi-Fi names and passwords.
- Do not repeatedly unplug and replug the power cord as this can trigger a security lockout from the ISP's headend.
- Do not attempt to update router firmware while the connection is unstable or intermittent.
- Do not change your Wi-Fi SSID or password until you have confirmed the router is not currently in a standard reboot cycle.
- Do not dismantle or open the casing of the modem, ONT, or router.

## What to do now
1. **Identify safety-critical dependencies.** Check if medical alert systems, security cameras, smart locks, or VOIP phones are offline. Use a mobile phone as a primary communication backup for emergency services if necessary.
2. **Observe the hardware status lights.** Look for patterns such as a blinking "Online" light, a red "Alarm" light on an ONT, or a power light that is cycling between on and off.
   - Note the color and behavior (flashing vs. solid) for technical support.
3. **Verify physical power and data connections.** Ensure the power adapter is firmly seated in the wall outlet and the router. Check that the Coax, Fiber, or Ethernet cable coming from the wall is tightly connected.
4. **Execute a sequential power cycle (if not using fiber ONT).**
   - Unplug the power from both the modem and the router.
   - Wait at least 60 seconds to allow volatile memory to clear.
   - Plug in the modem first and wait 2 to 3 minutes for a solid "Online" or "Sync" light.
   - Plug in the router and wait another 2 minutes for the Wi-Fi signal to broadcast.
5. **Check for localized outages via your ISP’s mobile app.** Use your cellular data to log into the Xfinity, Spectrum, AT&T, or Verizon app. Look for "Service Outages" or "Network Health" alerts in your specific zip code.
6. **Detect a forced factory reset.** Scan for available Wi-Fi networks on your phone. If your custom network name is gone and you see a generic name (e.g., "NETGEAR84" or "Linksys_Setup"), your router has reverted to factory defaults.
7. **Test a direct Ethernet connection.** If available, plug a computer directly into the router's LAN port. If the wired connection works but Wi-Fi does not, the issue is isolated to the wireless radio or configuration.
8. **Contact ISP support with a summary of steps taken.** Provide the exact time the drop occurred and confirm that you have already performed a single power cycle.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace hardware immediately; most "resets" are caused by ISP maintenance or temporary power surges.
- You can postpone re-pairing smart home devices (bulbs, plugs, thermostats) until the primary connection is stable for at least 30 minutes.
- You do not need to investigate 2026 FCC "foreign-made" router firmware restrictions unless your ISP specifically identifies your hardware as "End of Life" or "Covered."

## Important reassurance
A total network drop is a common occurrence usually tied to external provider maintenance or a simple software hang. It does not necessarily mean your hardware is broken or that your data has been compromised. Most connections restore themselves within a few minutes of a clean reboot.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a sudden loss of connectivity. It is not a guide for long-term troubleshooting of intermittent signals, hardware repair, or advanced network security configuration.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not supersede the specific safety or technical instructions provided by your ISP or equipment manufacturer. If you rely on the internet for life-sustaining medical equipment, contact your provider's priority support line immediately during any outage.
