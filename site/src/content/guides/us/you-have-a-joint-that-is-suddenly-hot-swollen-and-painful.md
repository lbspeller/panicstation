# What to do if… you have a joint that is suddenly hot, swollen, and painful (USA)
## Short answer
Arrange a same-day in-person medical assessment for a joint that has suddenly become hot, swollen, and painful. Go to an emergency department now if you feel seriously unwell, have severe or rapidly worsening symptoms, or cannot get prompt assessment elsewhere.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait several days to see whether a suddenly hot, swollen, painful joint settles on its own.
- Do not take leftover antibiotics or antibiotics prescribed for someone else.
- Do not try to drain, puncture, or squeeze the joint or nearby swelling.
- Do not force movement or keep putting weight on the joint if that sharply worsens the pain.
- Do not let heat, cold packs, or other home measures delay same-day assessment.

## What to do now
1. **Arrange same-day in-person assessment.**
   - Contact a clinician or urgent care service and say that a joint has suddenly become hot, swollen, and painful.
   - Ask whether they can assess you promptly today.
   - If prompt assessment is not available, go to an emergency department.

2. **Use emergency care now if you have warning signs.**
   - Go to an emergency department now if you have fever or chills with the joint symptoms, feel seriously unwell, have severe or rapidly worsening pain or swelling, cannot use the joint or bear weight, or have rapidly spreading redness.
   - Call 911 if you have trouble breathing, severe confusion, fainting or collapse, or you are too unwell to travel safely.

3. **Protect the joint while arranging care.**
   - Stop activity and rest the joint.
   - Avoid putting weight on a painful leg joint.
   - Support a painful arm or hand joint in a comfortable position.
   - Remove rings, watches, or tight items near swelling early.
   - Use a cool pack wrapped in cloth briefly if it helps. Do not place ice directly on your skin.

4. **Make a short note for the clinician.**
   - Note when the symptoms started and which joint is affected.
   - Note any fever, chills, recent illness, skin break, bite, injury, or nearby infection.
   - Note any recent joint injection, procedure, or surgery.
   - List your medicines, allergies, and important health conditions.

5. **Mention factors that may raise concern.**
   - Tell the clinician promptly if you have an artificial joint, diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis, a weakened immune system, or take immune-suppressing medicine.
   - Tell the clinician if the pain followed a recent joint injection, surgery, injury, skin break, or bite.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether this is gout, pseudogout, an infection, or an injury before seeking care.
- You do not need to decide on long-term treatment today.
- You do not need to repeatedly test the joint by moving it or putting weight on it.

## Important reassurance
Several conditions can cause a hot, swollen, painful joint. Getting assessed promptly is a sensible first step because a clinician can check for causes that need fast treatment.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours. Diagnosis and treatment may need an in-person examination and, in some cases, testing of joint fluid or other tests.
