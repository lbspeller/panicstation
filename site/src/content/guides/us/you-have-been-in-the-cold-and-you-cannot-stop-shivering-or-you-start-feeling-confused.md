# What to do if… you have been in the cold and you cannot stop shivering or you start feeling confused (USA)

## Short answer
Assume a medical emergency and **call 911 immediately**. Confusion and uncontrollable shivering are primary signs of hypothermia, which requires professional medical intervention.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "walk it off" or stay active if you feel clumsy, confused, or exhausted.
- Do not use a hot bath, hot shower, or hot tub; rapid rewarming can cause dangerous heart rhythms or shock.
- Do not rub or massage the skin, especially on the arms and legs.
- Do not consume alcohol or use tobacco products.
- Do not apply direct, high heat like heating pads or space heaters directly against the skin.
- Do not try to warm the arms or legs first; focus exclusively on the body core.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately.** Tell the dispatcher you suspect hypothermia due to cold exposure and report any confusion, slurred speech, or loss of coordination.
2. **Move to a warm, sheltered area with extreme gentleness.** Avoid jarring movements or excessive activity, as this can trigger irregular heartbeats in a cold body.
   - If you cannot get indoors, stay out of the wind and insulate yourself from the cold ground.
3. **Remove all wet clothing right away.** Cut the clothing off if necessary to minimize movement. 
4. **Wrap the body in dry blankets, coats, or sleeping bags.** Focus the insulation on the head, neck, chest, and groin area first.
   - Use multiple layers of dry material.
   - Cover the head and neck while leaving the face clear for breathing.
5. **Apply warm, dry compresses only to the center of the body.** Use warm water bottles wrapped in towels or chemical heat packs wrapped in cloth.
   - Place these on the neck, chest, or groin.
   - Never apply heat directly to the skin or the extremities.
6. **Provide warm, sweet, non-alcoholic liquids only if the person is fully conscious.** They must be alert enough to swallow without coughing or choking.
   - Stop immediately if there is any sign of drowsiness or difficulty swallowing.
7. **Monitor breathing and responsiveness continuously.** If breathing becomes dangerously slow, shallow, or stops, be prepared to follow 911 dispatcher instructions for CPR.
8. **Stay with the person until emergency responders arrive.** Do not leave a confused or shivering person alone, even if they claim they feel better.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine your exact body temperature with a thermometer before calling for help.
- You do not need to identify the specific cause of the cold exposure (e.g., exhaustion versus wind chill).
- You do not need to decide if you need an ER or Urgent Care; 911 will make the triage determination.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frightened or overwhelmed when your body is reacting to extreme cold. Hypothermia specifically affects the brain, which can make it hard to think clearly or realize how much danger you are in. By seeking help now, you are taking the necessary steps to stabilize your condition.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes following cold exposure. It is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis or hospital treatment for hypothermia.

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Severe cold exposure and confusion are life-threatening emergencies. Always contact 911 or your local emergency service immediately in these situations.
