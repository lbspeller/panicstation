# What to do if… someone approaches your car at a fuel pump and keeps trying to distract you from your wallet or phone (USA)

## Short answer
Terminate the interaction, secure your valuables on your person, and lock all vehicle doors immediately. Move to a populated area inside the station or lock yourself in the driver’s seat.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pull out your wallet, phone, or cash to show the person or "check" a claim they are making.
- Don’t set valuables on the car roof, the trunk, the pump, or the passenger seat while occupied with the fuel nozzle.
- Don’t leave any car door unlocked or window down, even if you are standing right next to the vehicle.
- Don’t leave your keys in the ignition or on the driver's seat.
- Don’t follow or chase a person who grabs an item from your vehicle.
- Don’t attempt to move the vehicle while the fuel nozzle is still inserted in the tank.

## What to do now
1. **Disengage from the stranger immediately.** Say "No" or "I can't help" once and turn away. 
   - Common pretexts include "you have a flat tire," "something is leaking under your car," or "I need directions."
2. **Secure your valuables and lock the vehicle.** Put your phone and wallet in a front pocket or a secured bag held against your body. 
   - Immediately press the "Lock" button on your key fob or the door panel. 
   - Ensure the passenger-side doors are locked, as "slider" thieves often use the distraction to crawl into the far side of the car.
3. **Scan the adjacent lane for a "trail car."** Look for a vehicle parked unusually close or a driver waiting with the engine running in the next pump lane. 
   - Take a mental note of the make, model, and color if possible, but do not stare or point.
4. **Relocate to a safer position.** 
   - If the fuel is still pumping, stay within reach of the nozzle but keep your back to the car so you can see both the pump and the stranger. 
   - If you feel unsafe, stop the pump, return the nozzle to the cradle, and go inside the store to stand near the staff.
5. **Request assistance from station employees.** Tell the clerk: "There is a person at pump X attempting to distract me; please monitor the security cameras and call the police if they do not leave."
6. **Call for help if the threat escalates.** 
   - **Dial 911** if the person refuses to leave, blocks your path, threatens you, or if you see them touching your vehicle doors. 
   - Call the local non-emergency number (often 311 in many US cities) only if the person has left and you are in a secure location.
7. **Initiate theft protocols if items were taken.** 
   - Use a banking app on a secure device to "Freeze" or "Lock" all credit and debit cards. 
   - If your phone was taken, use a different device to log into "Find My" (Apple) or "Find My Device" (Android) to remotely lock the phone and erase sensitive data. 
   - Report the theft at IdentityTheft.gov to begin a federal recovery plan if IDs or financial documents were lost.

## What can wait
- Determining if the person was "actually" a criminal—assume the risk and prioritize security first.
- Obtaining the gas station's security footage (the police will handle this request during their investigation).
- Filing a formal insurance claim for stolen items (focus on freezing accounts and securing your identity first).
- Replacing physical ID cards or driver's licenses.

## Important reassurance
Distraction thefts are designed to exploit helpfulness or temporary confusion. Reacting with caution—locking doors and ending the conversation—is a standard safety protocol, not an overreaction. If you feel a "gut instinct" that something is wrong, trust it and prioritize your physical safety over social politeness.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for an active or suspected distraction attempt at a US fuel station. It does not cover long-term legal recovery or vehicle repair for "drive-off" damage.

## Important note
This information is for general safety and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving immediate physical threat or active theft, contact 911 immediately.
