# What to do if… your security camera shows someone covering, moving, or disabling it outside your home (USA)

## Short answer
Treat it as possible active property tampering or attempted entry. Stay inside or stay away, get everyone away from doors and windows, and call 911 if there is immediate danger, an attempted entry, threats, or a crime in progress.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside to confront the person or check what they are doing.
- Do not open the door to warn them off.
- Do not stand close to windows or doors to watch, especially with lights on behind you.
- Do not chase, follow, or approach a person or vehicle to get more details.
- Do not post the clip publicly while this is unfolding.
- Do not factory-reset, wipe, or delete camera footage before saving what you can access.
- Do not touch or move anything outside right now if you think someone may still be nearby or may return.

## What to do now
1. Create a safer pause. Lock exterior doors, check ground-level windows from inside, and move people and pets to an interior room away from the front of the home.

2. Decide quickly between 911 and local non-emergency reporting.
   - Call 911 if the person is still there and you feel unsafe, they are trying doors or windows, threatening anyone, damaging property, or you think a break-in or other crime is in progress.
   - If they have clearly left and there is no immediate danger, contact your local police or sheriff non-emergency number, or use that agency’s official online reporting if available.

3. Use only official contact routes. If you do not know the non-emergency number, search for your city or county police or sheriff website and use the number or reporting link listed there.

4. Use deterrents you can control from inside. Turn on exterior lights, interior lights, or an alarm or siren from your phone or control panel if you can do that without going outside.

5. Save useful details from the video only. Write down clothing, height or build, tools or bags, what the person did to the camera, the direction they went, and any vehicle make, model, color, or readable plate that is already visible in the recording.

6. Preserve the footage. Save or download the clips, take screenshots if your app allows it, and note the date, time, camera location, and whether the lens was covered, the mount was moved, power was cut, or the camera went offline.

7. If you are not home, do not return yet. Stay somewhere safe. Call 911 if you think someone may still be there or entry may be happening now; otherwise contact the local non-emergency line and ask what they recommend before you go back.

8. If you live in an apartment, condo, HOA, or managed community, notify building security, property management, or the front desk from a safe place. Ask them to preserve any hallway, entrance, elevator, garage, or parking-lot footage from the same time period.

9. If you call 911 or non-emergency, be ready to give the address, your callback number, what you saw, whether the person is still there, whether anyone is inside, and whether there are weapons, injuries, threats, or attempted entry.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to buy new cameras, add a fence, or change your full security setup.
- You do not need to identify the person yourself before asking for help.
- You do not need to go outside to fix the camera immediately.
- You do not need to make a neighborhood or social media post right now.
- You do not need to decide about insurance, repairs, or longer-term security upgrades until the situation is stable.

## Important reassurance
A camera being covered, moved, or disabled can feel personal and alarming. In the first few minutes, your job is not to solve the whole situation; it is to stay safer, avoid confrontation, preserve what you can, and bring in help if needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repairs, reports, insurance, protective orders, security upgrades, or repeated incidents may need help from local law enforcement, a qualified security professional, an insurer, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you believe someone is outside now, attempting to enter, or putting anyone in immediate danger, prioritize safety and call 911.
