# What to do if… your motorcycle or scooter will not start away from home and you are stuck with it (USA)

## Short answer
Move yourself and the motorcycle to the furthest possible point from active traffic, then call for professional recovery. If you are in a live travel lane or a high-speed shoulder where you feel unsafe, call **911** immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Avoid attempting to diagnose mechanical issues while positioned in or near active traffic lanes.
- Avoid standing in the roadway to direct traffic or waving at passing cars.
- Avoid removing your helmet or protective gear until you have reached a location physically separated from the road.
- Avoid accepting unsolicited offers of a tow or transport from unverified private parties.
- Avoid staying with the motorcycle if the location is unsafe; your physical safety is the priority over the vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a safer pause immediately.**
   - **Move the motorcycle or scooter fully off the roadway** to a shoulder, parking lot, or side street.
   - If the vehicle is stuck in a travel lane and cannot be moved, **leave the bike and move yourself to a position behind a guardrail** or well away from the road edge.
2. **Increase your visibility to other motorists.**
   - **Activate hazard lights** if the vehicle is equipped with them.
   - If hazards are unavailable, **leave the parking lights on** for the initial few minutes to ensure visibility, then switch them off if the battery is failing and you have reached a safe spot.
   - Note that under 2026 "Move Over" laws, drivers are legally required to slow down or change lanes for any stopped vehicle with lights or hazards active.
3. **Establish your exact location for recovery services.**
   - **Identify the highway name, direction of travel, and nearest mile marker** or exit number.
   - Use a smartphone map app to drop a pin or find the nearest cross-street if in a city.
4. **Contact appropriate roadside assistance.**
   - **Call 911** if the breakdown has occurred in a dangerous location (blind curve, narrow shoulder, or live lane).
   - **Call your insurance provider or a motorcycle-specific roadside club** (e.g., AAA Plus/Premier Motorcycle or AMA) if you have coverage.
   - **Specify that you have a motorcycle or scooter** and confirm the tow truck has the necessary equipment (flatbed or specialized motorcycle cradle).
5. **Verify regional motorist aid services.**
   - **Dial 511** to check for state-sponsored Freeway Service Patrols (FSP) which provide free limited assistance (fuel, jumpstarts, or short tows) in specific states like California, Florida, and Maryland.
6. **Perform a high-level safety check only if in a secure location.**
   - **Verify the kill switch is in the "Run" position** and the kickstand is fully retracted.
   - **Confirm the transmission is in neutral** and check the fuel level.
   - If the engine does not start after two attempts, stop to conserve battery for lighting and communication.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific cause of the failure (e.g., stator, spark plug, or fuel pump) right now.
- You do not need to compare long-term repair costs or shop ratings while on the roadside.
- You do not need to notify your insurance company about a non-accident breakdown immediately.

## Important reassurance
A motorcycle breakdown in an unfamiliar area causes an immediate spike in vulnerability, but most issues are resolved through routine recovery procedures. National "Move Over" laws are in place to provide you a margin of safety, and professional recovery operators are trained to handle two-wheeled vehicles safely.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and harm reduction for a stranded rider. It does not provide mechanical repair instructions or legal advice for vehicle recovery.

## Important note
Roadside environments are high-risk areas for motorcyclists due to lack of physical protection. This information is for general guidance; always prioritize local law enforcement instructions and your own safety over the recovery of the vehicle. If you are in immediate danger from traffic or third parties, contact emergency services.
