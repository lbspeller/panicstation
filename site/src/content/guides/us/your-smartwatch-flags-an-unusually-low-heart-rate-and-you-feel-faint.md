# What to do if… your smartwatch flags an unusually low heart rate and you feel faint (USA)

## Short answer
Lie down flat on your back immediately to prevent a fall and **call 911** if you lose consciousness, have chest pain, or if your heart rate is below 40 bpm.

## Do not do these things
- Do not **stand up or walk**, even if you think the feeling is passing.
- Do not **drive yourself** to a hospital or clinic.
- Do not **eat or drink anything** until you have fully recovered and are no longer lightheaded.
- Do not **take any extra medication**, caffeine, or stimulants to try to raise your heart rate.
- Do not **ignore the alert** even if the faintness is mild; symptomatic slow heart rate requires a professional evaluation.

## What to do now
1. **Get flat on the floor immediately.** Lie on your back to ensure blood flow reaches your brain and to prevent injury from a collapse.
   - If there are no other injuries (like a head strike), **raise your legs about 12 inches** off the ground.
   - Loosen any tight clothing around your neck, chest, or waist.
2. **Call 911 immediately if any of the following occur:**
   - Your smartwatch shows a **heart rate below 40 bpm**.
   - You experience **chest pain, pressure, or significant discomfort**.
   - You have **difficulty breathing** or feel gasping for air.
   - You feel **suddenly confused**, disoriented, or unable to speak clearly.
   - You (or someone with you) **lose consciousness for any amount of time**.
   - You have a **severe headache** or sudden weakness on one side of the body.
3. **Notify someone of your location.** If you are not calling 911, call a family member, neighbor, or friend to come to your side immediately.
   - If you are alone, **unlock your front door** while you are still able to move, then return to a lying position.
4. **Perform a 10-second pulse check.** If you can do so without sitting up, feel for a pulse at your wrist.
   - Do not worry about the exact count; note only if the pulse feels **extremely slow, very weak, or highly irregular**.
   - If you cannot find a pulse quickly, stop trying and focus on staying flat.
5. **Prepare information for medical responders.** Keep your phone and your smartwatch visible.
   - Note the exact **heart rate number** the watch displayed.
   - Be ready to list any **medications you take**, especially blood pressure or heart medications (beta-blockers or calcium channel blockers).
6. **Remain lying down until help arrives or symptoms fully resolve.** Even if you feel better, do not attempt to stand up quickly. Transition to a sitting position very slowly only after the dizziness has been gone for several minutes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the watch sensor is dirty or malfunctioning right now.
- You do not need to review your long-term heart rate trends or sleep data.
- You do not need to decide if you need a pacemaker or a lifestyle change in this moment.

## Important reassurance
Feeling like you might pass out is a high-stress experience that often triggers a surge of adrenaline. Lying flat is the most effective way to stabilize your blood flow and prevent a fall-related injury while you wait for your symptoms to stabilize or for help to arrive.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute episode of symptomatic bradycardia (slow heart rate). It does not replace a clinical diagnosis or an EKG performed by a medical professional.

## Important note
A smartwatch notification is a tool, not a diagnosis. However, when a low heart rate alert is combined with physical symptoms like faintness or chest pain, it must be treated as a potential medical emergency. Always seek professional medical evaluation for any new or worsening heart rhythm symptoms.
