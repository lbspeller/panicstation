# What to do if… your bank restricts your account for a compliance review and you cannot access funds (USA)

## Short answer
Call your bank’s compliance or fraud department through an official number to obtain a specific document checklist and a case reference number. Simultaneously, redirect incoming income to a secondary account and notify essential billers to prevent a cascade of missed payments.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t respond to unsolicited calls, texts, or emails—always initiate contact using the number on your physical card or the bank’s verified website.
- Don’t submit altered, cropped, or "corrected" documents; banks use automated forensic tools that flag any digital tampering.
- Don’t attempt to circumvent the restriction by having third parties receive your funds, as this often triggers additional Anti-Money Laundering (AML) flags.
- Don’t assume the bank can tell you exactly why the freeze happened; federal "tipping off" rules may legally prohibit them from disclosing certain triggers.
- Don’t wait for the bank to call you back; proactive daily follow-ups are usually required to keep a compliance file moving.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of hold through official channels.** Call the bank and ask if the restriction is a Regulation CC funds availability hold (for a specific check), a Customer Identification Program (CIP) issue, or a general compliance review.
2. **Request a formal "Information Request" checklist.** Ask the representative to send the specific requirements to your secure online banking inbox.
   - Common requirements include: unexpired government ID, recent utility bills, or "Source of Wealth" proof (e.g., pay stubs, inheritance letters, or property sale HUD-1 statements).
3. **Record every detail of the interaction.** Document the date, time, representative’s name or ID number, and the specific case/reference number for the review.
4. **Submit documents through the bank's secure portal only.** Avoid email for sensitive data like Social Security numbers or bank statements.
   - Ensure scans are high-resolution and all four corners of the document are visible.
5. **Protect your essential survival payments today.** Call your landlord, mortgage servicer, and utility companies immediately.
   - State: "My account is currently under a standard compliance review and access is temporarily restricted. I am working to resolve it and can provide a case number. Can you note my file to waive late fees for 10 days?"
6. **Redirect all incoming "live" money.** Contact your employer’s payroll department or the Social Security Administration to switch direct deposits to a different account immediately.
   - If you do not have a second account, open one at a new institution; 2026 identity verification rules (CIP exemptions) often allow faster, third-party verified account opening.
7. **File a formal written internal complaint with the bank.** Use the words "financial hardship" if you cannot afford food or medicine. This often triggers an internal escalation to a higher-level "High-Priority" queue.
8. **Escalate to the appropriate federal regulator if access is not restored within 10 days.** Use the following routing based on the institution:
   - **National Banks:** File with the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) via HelpWithMyBank.gov.
   - **State-Chartered Banks:** File with the FDIC Consumer Response Center or your State Banking Department.
   - **Credit Unions:** File with the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA).
   - **All Consumer Accounts:** File a parallel complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).
9. **Cite Executive Order 14331 if you suspect unfair debanking.** Mentioning the "Guaranteeing Fair Banking For All Americans" order in your formal complaint can ensure your case is reviewed for compliance with non-discrimination standards.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to close the account permanently—focus only on regaining access to the current balance first.
- Debating the fairness of the bank's automated flags with front-line staff; they rarely have the power to override system-generated compliance holds.
- Paying non-essential debts like credit cards (from the same bank) or entertainment subscriptions.

## Important reassurance
A compliance review is a standard, often automated, regulatory requirement and does not mean you have been accused of a crime. Most restrictions are resolved quickly once the requested documentation is verified against the bank’s internal "Know Your Customer" (KYC) records.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and administrative escalation only. It does not cover legal defense for suspected criminal activity or long-term litigation against financial institutions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal or financial advice. Federal law allows banks to freeze accounts for suspected suspicious activity, and they may be legally barred from providing specific details during the investigation. If you are facing imminent eviction or lack of food, contact local 211 services for emergency assistance while the bank review is pending.
