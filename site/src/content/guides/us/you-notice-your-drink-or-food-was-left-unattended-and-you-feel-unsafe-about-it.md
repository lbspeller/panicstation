# What to do if… you notice your drink or food was left unattended and you feel unsafe about it (USA)

## Short answer
Stop consuming the item immediately, move to a staffed or well-lit area with a trusted person, and alert venue management. If you feel physically unwell or suspect drugging, call 911 or Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222.

## Do not do these things
- Do not finish the drink or food "to be sure" or to avoid waste.
- Do not ask a stranger or an acquaintance you do not fully trust to "test" it for you.
- Do not leave the venue alone if you feel dizzy, confused, or unusually intoxicated.
- Do not confront anyone you suspect of tampering while you are alone or feeling impaired.
- Do not drive any vehicle if you feel even slightly "off," sleepy, or uncoordinated.
- Do not throw the item away if you intend to seek medical testing or report the incident.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a staffed area:** Leave the item where it is (unless you can safely carry it to staff) and walk immediately to the bar, host stand, or security station. **Tell a trusted friend exactly what is happening** and ensure they stay with you.
2. **Observe the item for physical red flags:** Look for specific signs of tampering from a safe distance:
   - Foggy or cloudy appearance in a clear liquid.
   - Excessive fizzing or bubbling in a non-carbonated drink.
   - Sinking ice (ice usually floats in most mixed drinks).
   - Change in the drink's original color.
   - Visible powder, oily film, or residue at the bottom or rim.
3. **Alert venue staff immediately:** Ask to speak with a manager or security. **State clearly that you believe your drink or food was tampered with** and ask if they have a welfare protocol (such as "Ask for Angela") or a safe space for you to wait.
4. **Seek medical guidance for symptoms:** If you feel faint, nauseated, confused, or "drunker" than your consumption warrants:
   - **Call 911** if you are having trouble breathing, losing consciousness, or feel in immediate danger.
   - **Call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222** for free, confidential advice on symptoms and whether you need an emergency room visit.
5. **Preserve the item if possible:** Ask the venue manager to set the drink or food aside in a clean, secondary container (like a plastic cup) for potential testing. **Ask the venue to preserve any security camera footage** covering your table or the bar area from the time you left the item.
6. **Report packaged food tampering:** If this involves a store-bought item:
   - **Notify the store manager** if you are still on-site.
   - **Contact local law enforcement** if you have already consumed the item and feel unwell.
   - **Call the USDA Meat and Poultry Hotline at 1-888-674-6854** if the product contains meat, poultry, or eggs.
   - **Contact the FDA Emergency Line at 1-866-300-4374** for other food product concerns.
7. **Arrange a safe exit:** Do not walk home alone or take a rideshare by yourself if you feel impaired. **Ask your trusted friend to escort you home** or have the venue staff wait with you until a family member or trusted contact arrives.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to file a formal police report.
- Identifying a specific suspect.
- Reviewing credit card statements or venue receipts.
- Contacting a lawyer or seeking legal remedies.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your intuition is a valid safety strategy. You do not need physical proof of a drug or a witness to the tampering to stop eating or drinking and ask for help. Many people feel embarrassed or "dramatic" in these moments, but venue staff are trained to handle these concerns.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for the immediate minutes following a suspicion of tampering. It does not replace professional medical assessment or forensic toxicology testing.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute medical or legal advice. If you suspect you have been drugged or poisoned, contact 1-800-222-1222 or 911 immediately. Reporting requirements for food tampering vary by state and product type.
