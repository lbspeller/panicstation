# What to do if… you are asked for proof of accommodation for your full stay and have gaps in your bookings (USA)

## Short answer
Pause and gather the truthful accommodation confirmations you already have. Ask exactly what document is missing, then add a real booking or a host confirmation only for nights you genuinely expect to use and only if that matches the request.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t fabricate reservations, edit screenshots, or give an address you are not actually using.
- Don’t rush into a large non-refundable purchase before you know what document is needed.
- Don’t assume every destination accepts the same documents.
- Don’t argue about device access. Ask whether a printed confirmation or a specific screen is acceptable, and ask for clarification if you do not understand an official’s request.
- Don’t expose unrelated financial or private information unless it is requested and relevant.

## What to do now
1. **Make a date-by-date accommodation list.** For each night, note the city, accommodation or host, address if available, contact details, and any gap.
2. **Put your existing confirmations together.** Keep booking emails, app confirmations, and host messages in one place. If asked, also gather your onward or return itinerary and a limited proof-of-funds document without exposing unrelated information where possible.
3. **Ask what would satisfy the request.** Ask whether the airline or official needs a confirmation for every night, a host address and message, or another document accepted under the destination’s rules.
4. **Use a truthful option for each gap.**
   - If you genuinely plan to stay in paid accommodation, make a real booking and save the confirmation. Where suitable, choose a cancellation option you understand.
   - If you genuinely plan to stay with someone, ask them for a short written message with your name, their address, the dates, and a contact method.
   - If you are moving between places or still deciding, explain that accurately and ask what evidence is accepted for those dates.
5. **If this is happening with an airline before departure, ask for clarification before spending more money.** Ask which document rule is being applied. If the answer is unclear or boarding may be refused, ask for a supervisor and ask whether the reason can be recorded.
6. **If you are a U.S. citizen abroad and the situation becomes detention or an urgent emergency, contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate.** If you are detained, you can also ask the detaining authorities to notify the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate. If you cannot reach the embassy or consulate during an emergency, call:
   - From the U.S. and Canada: **1-888-407-4747**
   - From another country: **+1 202-501-4444**
7. **Save the documents together.** Keep the accommodation list and the confirmations in one folder so you can show the same accurate information again if asked.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide every later travel detail unless the airline or official specifically requires it.
- You don’t need to upgrade your accommodation or make optional bookings while you are under pressure.
- You don’t need to debate every rule in the moment. First get the exact request and provide truthful, relevant documents.

## Important reassurance
A gap in bookings is not the same as a refusal. Requirements vary by destination and traveler circumstances, and the next useful step is to find out exactly what document is missing.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a U.S. traveler dealing with an accommodation-proof request for travel abroad. They do not determine destination-specific entry eligibility, and later decisions may require official guidance or specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Entry requirements and accepted documents may vary by destination, passport, visa or authorization status, airline, and individual circumstances.
