# What to do if… a large crack appears around a window or door frame and keeps widening (USA)
## Short answer
Move people and pets away from the affected door or window frame. If the building may be unstable or there is immediate danger, leave by a safe route, move away from the building, and call 911.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go closer just to measure, mark, or inspect the crack.
- Do not force a sticking door or window, or use an opening if the frame looks distorted.
- Do not remove drywall, brick, trim, ceiling material, or other parts of the wall to look inside.
- Do not patch, fill, or paint over the crack before it has been assessed.
- Do not move heavy furniture or stacked items near the affected wall.
- Do not remain in or re-enter an area that may be unsafe.
- Do not use switches, flames, or anything that could create a spark if you smell gas or suspect a gas leak.

## What to do now
1. **Make a safer pause.** Move everyone, including pets, away from the affected area using a safe route. Keep away from the wall, the opening, and anything that could fall.
2. **Leave and call 911 if there may be immediate danger.** Warning signs can include visible movement, falling debris, a bulging or sagging wall or ceiling, a distorted frame, a suddenly unstable floor, a gas smell, hissing, sparking, or a safe exit becoming difficult to use.
3. **Keep away after leaving.** Move clear of the building and do not re-enter until emergency responders, local authorities, or a building inspector say it is safe.
4. **Contact the relevant utility from a safe place when needed.** Call 911 first if there is immediate danger. Otherwise, contact the relevant utility if you smell gas, hear hissing, see sparking, or see water affecting electrical areas.
5. **Document only from a safe location.** Take a quick photo or video if you can do so without moving closer to the affected area. Note the time and whether you have seen a change.
6. **Use the route that applies to you.**
   - **Homeowner:** contact your home insurer promptly, describe the possible structural damage, and ask what documentation or inspection they need before repair work begins.
   - **Renter:** contact your landlord or property manager promptly in writing, describe the widening crack, and ask for an urgent safety inspection.
7. **Ask for the local unsafe-structure route if needed.** Contact your city or county government and ask which local office handles a report about a potentially unsafe structure. Local reporting routes vary.
8. **Arrange an independent assessment.** Ask for a licensed professional engineer with structural experience. Check the relevant state licensing board before agreeing to major repair work.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the cause is settling, foundation movement, moisture, framing damage, or another problem right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair contractor, foundation system, or replacement window or door today.
- You do not need to make cosmetic repairs before the area has been assessed.

## Important reassurance
A widening crack near an opening is unsettling. You do not need to diagnose it yourself. Moving away from the area, avoiding further disturbance, and arranging the right local assessment are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need an on-site assessment from a licensed professional engineer with structural experience, guidance from local building officials, and insurer input where relevant.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, engineering, or other professional advice. If you believe there is immediate danger, leave by a safe route, move away from the building, and call 911.
