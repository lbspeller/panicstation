# What to do if… you are told your work device will be remotely wiped and you need to save personal files (USA)

## Short answer
Contact IT or HR immediately and ask whether they can pause the wipe or arrange a supervised export of named personal items through an approved method. Do not try to block the wipe or copy company data.

## Do not do these things
- Do not disconnect the device from the network, power it off, remove a management profile, or change security settings in an attempt to delay or block the wipe.
- Do not copy company files, confidential material, or mixed folders for leverage or just in case.
- Do not use personal email, personal cloud storage, AirDrop, or a personal USB device unless IT or HR confirms in writing that the method is allowed.
- Do not delete messages, tickets, or files to clean up before the wipe.
- Do not assume locally stored items will remain available after the wipe.
- Do not assume a wipe affects only the work profile. Ask IT what is planned.

## What to do now
1. Contact IT or HR through an official channel. Ask which devices are affected, when the wipe is scheduled, and whether the planned action is a full-device wipe, a work-profile removal, or another management action.
2. Ask whether a brief hold or supervised export is possible. Save the wipe notice only if permitted; otherwise, note its wording and the time you received it.
3. Send a short written list of the personal items you need. Keep it narrow: for example, personal photos, personal contacts, personal documents, browser bookmarks, or personal certificates stored locally.
4. Ask IT to confirm the approved export route before copying anything. If a file or folder appears to mix work and personal material, leave it in place and ask for guidance.
5. From another device you trust, if available, check that you can still access alternate sign-in and recovery methods for personal accounts. Then update passwords where needed and, where the account provider offers those options, sign out other sessions and remove the work device from personal account device lists.
6. Use official HR or benefits portals, where available, to save personal employment documents you are permitted to retain, such as pay statements, tax forms, benefits confirmations, and plan contact details. Ask HR before saving anything uncertain.
7. If the device is your own phone or computer, ask IT whether the planned action affects only a managed work profile or the whole device, and whether a personal backup is permitted before the action occurs.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to quit, threaten legal action, or post about the situation publicly.
- You do not need to organise every file. Focus on the small set of personal items that would be difficult to replace.
- A California privacy request can wait until after you send the immediate IT or HR request. If you are a California resident and your employer is a business subject to the California Consumer Privacy Act, employment-related personal information may be within that law. That process does not replace a prompt request for a pause or supervised export.

## Important reassurance
Pausing before copying anything helps prevent a rushed mistake. A narrow written request for a supervised, policy-approved export gives IT or HR a clear and limited decision to make. Some locally stored personal data may become unavailable after a wipe, so focus on the items that matter most.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about employment records, a California privacy request, a workplace dispute, or specialist advice can wait until the immediate device issue is stable.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, cybersecurity, employment, financial, or other professional advice. The available options may depend on device ownership, employer policy, the management setup, state law, and the rules of each personal account provider.
