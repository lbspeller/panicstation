# What to do if… part of your ceiling starts sagging after a leak (USA)

## Short answer
Keep everyone out of the affected room. Treat the sagging ceiling as a possible collapse and electrical hazard until the water source is stopped and a professional says the area is safe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter the room or stand under the sagging or bulging area.
- Do not poke, cut, press, or try to drain the ceiling bulge.
- Do not use switches, outlets, ceiling lights, fixtures, or devices in the wet area.
- Do not touch the breaker panel if the route to it, the floor around it, the panel, or the nearby wall is damp or wet.
- Do not climb onto the roof or enter an attic to look for the leak.
- Do not run fans or dehumidifiers in the affected room until electrical safety is clear.
- Do not start removing ceiling material. Wet debris can fall suddenly, and some older ceiling materials may need professional handling.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move people and pets out of the room. Close the door only if you can do that without entering the room or standing under the sag.
2. **Leave the home and call 911 from a safe location if there is immediate danger.** Do this if the ceiling is actively falling, you see sparks or smoke, you smell burning, or you think there may be a fire.
3. **Stop the water source only if you can do so from a safe, dry location.**
   - For a plumbing leak, use the nearest fixture shutoff valve if you know where it is.
   - If the source is unclear, use the main water shutoff valve if you can reach it safely.
   - For a roof leak during rain, stay off the roof and call an urgent roof repair service.
4. **Leave electricity in the wet area alone.** If you know which breaker controls the affected area and you can reach the panel by a clearly dry, safe route, switch off that breaker. If there is any doubt, do not touch the panel. Contact an electrician or your utility provider for advice.
5. **Call urgent repair help.** Contact a plumber for a plumbing leak, a roof repair service for a roof leak, or a qualified contractor or water-damage professional for the sagging ceiling. Let the professional advise whether further assessment is needed.
6. **If you rent or live in managed housing, notify the landlord or property manager immediately.** Use the emergency maintenance line if there is one. Send a short written message by text, email, or portal saying that the ceiling is sagging after an active leak.
7. **Document only from a safe location.** If you can take photos without entering the room or approaching the sag, photograph the damage and note any water shutoff or breaker action you took.
8. **Arrange drying and cleanup after the area is safe.** If the water may contain sewage or other contamination, avoid contact and tell the cleanup professional.

## What can wait
- You do not need to enter the room to move belongings.
- You do not need to choose a full repair plan now.
- You do not need to start drying equipment until electrical safety is clear.
- You do not need to settle insurance details before the immediate hazard is controlled.

## Important reassurance
Keeping the room empty is useful action. You do not need to fix the ceiling yourself or make every repair decision during the first few minutes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from a plumber, electrician, roof repair service, contractor, water-damage professional, landlord, property manager, or insurer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, emergency, or other professional advice. Prioritize safety and follow instructions from emergency services and qualified professionals.
