# What to do if… you cannot access your ticket because the network is down and you are at a barrier or checkpoint (USA)

## Short answer
Step aside from the barrier immediately and locate a staff member to request manual verification using your confirmation number, photo ID, or the payment method used for purchase.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to bypass the barrier, jump turnstiles, or "tailgate" behind another passenger, as this may lead to detention or fines.
- Do not stay in the path of other travelers while troubleshooting; move to a designated "safe pause" area or the side of the lane.
- Do not delete or reinstall the ticketing application while offline, as this may erase cached data or locally stored offline passes.
- Do not engage in a confrontation with security or gate staff; maintain a procedural tone focused on verifying your purchase.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the side of the queue and identify a staff member.**  
   Stand in a visible area near the checkpoint or gate where you can catch the attention of a Station Agent, Conductor, or Security Officer without blocking the flow of traffic.

2. **Verify if the ticket is cached in your digital wallet or email.**  
   Check these locations in order, as they often store data for offline use:
   - Apple Wallet or Google Wallet (passes added here are designed to work without a data connection).
   - Saved screenshots or PDFs in your "Photos" or "Files" folder.
   - Recent "Offline" views in the carrier’s mobile app.
   - If the app is stuck on a loading screen, toggle Airplane Mode "On" for 10 seconds and then "Off" to force the phone to reconnect to the nearest tower or station Wi-Fi.

3. **Locate your "agent-verifiable" data points.**  
   Prepare the following items to help staff manually find your booking in their system:
   - Confirmation or reservation number (found in the original booking SMS or email subject line).
   - Government-issued photo ID (Note: As of 2025, REAL ID or a passport is mandatory for air travel).
   - The credit or debit card used to purchase the ticket.
   - A screenshot of the bank transaction or the "Success" screen if you took one earlier.

4. **Request the specific manual verification protocol for your location.**  
   Ask for assistance based on the type of checkpoint:
   - **Transit/Metro gates:** Ask the Station Agent for a manual fare verification or an "override" pass if the system outage is widespread.
   - **Amtrak/Intercity Rail:** Speak to the gate agent or the conductor at the boarding point; they can typically look up your reservation on their handheld device using your name and confirmation number.
   - **Airport Security (TSA):** If you cannot access your boarding pass, return to the airline check-in counter or a self-service kiosk to print a physical paper pass. If you lack a REAL ID/acceptable ID, you must pay a $45 fee via Pay.gov to initiate the "TSA ConfirmID" identity verification process at the checkpoint.

5. **Document the interaction if you are required to purchase a new ticket.**  
   If the staff cannot verify your ticket and you must pay again to travel, ensure you:
   - Request a physical receipt for the new purchase.
   - Note the time, location, and name/ID number of the staff member you spoke with.
   - Save the "network error" or "offline" screen on your phone as a timestamped screenshot for your eventual refund claim.

6. **Prioritize human intervention over app troubleshooting.**  
   Once you have tried the basic offline wallet checks, stop looking at your phone and engage with staff; local network outages are often already known to them, and they may have an established fallback procedure.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the technical network issue or contact your mobile provider while at the gate.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or refund request while standing at the barrier; focus only on clearing the checkpoint.
- You do not need to decide which alternative route to take until you have confirmed whether staff can or cannot verify your current ticket.

## Important reassurance
Technical failures at checkpoints are common, and transit agencies generally have manual verification protocols for these exact scenarios. As long as you have proof of purchase or a confirmation number, there is usually a procedural path forward that does not involve an enforcement action.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for clearing a physical barrier during a network outage. It does not cover long-term technical troubleshooting or the legal process for disputing fines.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional travel advice. Policies vary by carrier and jurisdiction, particularly regarding TSA identity verification and transit fare enforcement. Always follow the instructions of uniformed station or security personnel.
