# What to do if… you notice a rash with fever and are unsure if it is serious (USA)

## Short answer
If a fever comes with a new rash and the person is hard to wake, confused, having trouble breathing, has a seizure, has a stiff neck or very bad headache, or has a dark purple or bleeding-looking rash, call 911 or go to an emergency department now. If they are stable but you are unsure, contact a clinician today and call ahead before going in person if measles could be possible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait to “see what it turns into” if the person is getting worse, very sleepy, confused, fainting, or having breathing trouble.
- Do not drive yourself if there is fainting, confusion, inability to walk, severe weakness, or breathing trouble; call 911.
- Do not go in person for suspected measles without calling ahead first.
- Do not take leftover antibiotics or start a new steroid cream “just in case” before a clinician advises.
- Do not dismiss a rash that looks like bruising, bleeding under the skin, or dark purple spots, especially with fever.
- Do not give aspirin to a child or teenager if chickenpox could be possible unless a clinician specifically says to.

## What to do now
1. Check for emergency signs. Call 911 now if any apply.
   - The person cannot be awakened easily, seems confused, has a seizure, collapses, faints, or cannot walk.
   - They have difficulty breathing, blue lips, blue tongue, or blue nails.
   - They have a very bad headache, stiff neck, or rapidly worsening illness.

2. Treat bleeding-looking or dark purple rashes as urgent.
   - If the rash looks like bruises, bleeding under the skin, dark purple spots, or spots that do not fade when you press them, especially with fever, go to an emergency department now.
   - Call 911 instead if the person looks very unwell, is hard to wake, confused, weak, or having breathing trouble.

3. If the rash started after a new medicine, take it seriously.
   - Call the prescriber, pharmacist, urgent care, or poison control now for advice, especially if there is fever, facial swelling, lip or tongue swelling, blisters, peeling skin, mouth sores, eye redness, or eye pain.
   - Use 911 if there is trouble breathing, swelling of the face or throat, fainting, confusion, or fast worsening.

4. Get same-day clinical advice if there are no emergency signs but you are worried.
   - Call your primary care office, pediatrician, nurse triage line, insurer’s nurse line, urgent care, or the nearest emergency department for guidance.
   - Say: “fever with a new rash,” when the fever started, when the rash started, and whether it is spreading, bruising-looking, painful, or changing quickly.

5. If measles could be possible, call ahead before you go anywhere in person.
   - Mention recent travel, known exposure, or fever with cough, runny nose, red watery eyes, followed by rash.
   - Ask where to arrive or wait so the clinic or emergency department can reduce exposure to others.

6. Gather the details a clinician will ask for while you are waiting for advice.
   - Fever start time, highest temperature, how you measured it, and what fever medicine was given and when.
   - Rash start time, where it began, whether it is spreading, whether it fades when pressed, and a photo in good light if safe.
   - New medicines, recent vaccines, recent travel, sick contacts, pregnancy, immune system problems, and the person’s age.

7. Treat higher-risk situations as more urgent.
   - Seek same-day medical advice for an infant, a pregnant person, or someone with a weakened immune system.
   - Escalate to 911 or an emergency department if any emergency sign develops.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact illness right now; focus on the right level of care.
- You do not need to deep-clean your home or notify everyone immediately; first get medical guidance.
- You do not need to decide based on the temperature number alone; use how sick the person looks, the rash appearance, and the emergency signs above.
- You do not need to start leftover medicines or home treatments before asking a clinician.

## Important reassurance
A fever with a new rash can have many causes, and some are not dangerous. Because a few causes need urgent treatment or infection-control steps, asking for same-day guidance is a reasonable and careful response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce risk and reach appropriate medical evaluation. A clinician may need an exam and sometimes tests to determine the cause.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical advice or diagnosis. If you think someone is seriously ill or getting worse quickly, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department.
