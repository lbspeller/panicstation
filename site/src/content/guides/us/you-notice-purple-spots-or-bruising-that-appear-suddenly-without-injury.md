# What to do if… you notice purple spots or bruising that appear suddenly without injury (USA)

## Short answer
If purple spots or bruising appear suddenly with no clear injury, get medical advice today. Go to the ER or call 911 now if you feel very sick, have fever with a rapidly spreading rash or purple spots, feel confused or faint, have trouble breathing, or have heavy or unusual bleeding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait at home if you have fever and feel very unwell, are confused, faint, hard to wake, short of breath, or the spots are spreading quickly.
- Do not stop blood thinners or any prescribed medicine on your own.
- Do not start aspirin, ibuprofen, or naproxen for the spots or bruises unless a clinician tells you to.
- Do not assume it is “just skin” if pinpoint spots or bruise-like patches appeared suddenly without a clear cause.
- Do not drive yourself to the ER if you feel faint, confused, severely unwell, or unsafe to drive.

## What to do now
1. Use this emergency safety check. Go to the ER or call 911 now if any of these apply:
   - Fever with new purple spots, pinpoint spots, or bruise-like patches, especially if you feel or look seriously ill.
   - Confusion, hard-to-wake drowsiness, fainting, seizure, or severe dizziness.
   - Trouble breathing, chest pain, bluish lips or skin.
   - Heavy bleeding, coughing or vomiting blood, black or tarry stool, or blood in urine.
   - Spots that spread quickly, many new bruises appear, or you are getting worse fast.
   - For a child: hard to wake, unusually sleepy, confused, not acting normally, trouble breathing, fainting, seizure, or fever with a rash that is spreading or looks purple.
2. If none of the emergency signs apply, still get checked today. Call your primary care clinic for same-day advice, use urgent care, or follow your health plan’s nurse line instructions.
3. Say your medication risks out loud at check-in. Tell them if you take warfarin, apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban, clopidogrel, daily aspirin, steroids, chemotherapy, or if you recently started or stopped any medicine or supplement.
4. Do a quick bleeding check and write it down. Note nosebleeds, bleeding gums, heavier-than-usual periods, blood in urine or stool, black or tarry stool, coughing or vomiting blood, or many new bruises or pinpoint dots.
5. Take clear photos in good light. Include a coin or ruler for scale, note when you first noticed the spots or bruises, where they are, and whether they are spreading.
6. While waiting to be seen, escalate immediately if you develop fever, feel suddenly worse, become lightheaded, start bleeding unusually, or the spots spread quickly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether this is petechiae, purpura, a rash, or a bruise before asking for care.
- You do not need to change medicines unless a clinician tells you to.
- You do not need to arrange specialists today; the first step is choosing the ER, calling 911, or getting same-day medical evaluation.

## Important reassurance
Sudden unexplained purple spots or bruising can feel frightening. Many causes are treatable, and being checked today is a practical way to reduce risk and uncertainty.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Tests, treatment, medication changes, and follow-up decisions may need a clinician or another qualified specialist.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not a diagnosis, medical advice, or professional advice; if you are unsure or getting worse, choose the safer option and seek urgent medical help.
