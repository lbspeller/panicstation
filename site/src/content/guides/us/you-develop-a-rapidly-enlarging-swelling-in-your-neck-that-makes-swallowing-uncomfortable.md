# What to do if… you develop a rapidly enlarging swelling in your neck that makes swallowing uncomfortable (USA)

## Short answer
**Call 911 immediately** if your neck is swelling rapidly or if swallowing is becoming difficult, as this may indicate a critical airway obstruction or severe allergic reaction.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait to see if the swelling stops growing if it has progressed over minutes or hours.
- Do not attempt to eat or drink anything to test your swallowing ability.
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital if you are experiencing shortness of breath or dizziness.
- Do not take oral antihistamines or other medications as a substitute for emergency care if symptoms are progressing.
- Do not lie flat if you are struggling to breathe.
- Do not ignore the symptoms even if you do not have a rash or hives.

## What to do now
1. **Call 911 immediately if you experience any emergency warning signs.** 
   - These include difficulty breathing, gasping, or noisy breathing (stridor).
   - Watch for an inability to swallow saliva, excessive drooling, or a muffled/hoarse voice.
   - Seek help for sudden confusion, severe dizziness, or a feeling that your throat is closing.
2. **Use your epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen / Auvi-Q) immediately if you have one and suspect an allergic reaction.** 
   - Inject the device into the middle of your outer thigh and hold it for 3 seconds (or as directed by your specific device instructions).
   - Call 911 even if symptoms improve after the injection, as a second "rebound" reaction can occur.
3. **Sit in an upright, supported position to keep your airway as open as possible.** 
   - Avoid lying down unless you feel faint, in which case you should lie flat with your legs elevated while waiting for EMS.
4. **Gather essential medical information if it takes less than 60 seconds.** 
   - Note if you have started any new medications recently, particularly ACE inhibitors (common blood pressure meds like Lisinopril).
   - Identify any recent exposure to new foods, insect stings, or known allergens.
5. **Unlock your front door and keep your phone within reach if you are alone.** 
   - This ensures emergency responders can enter the premises quickly if your condition deteriorates.
6. **Alert a neighbor or housemate that you have called emergency services.** 
   - Having someone present can provide critical information to paramedics if you become unable to speak.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact medical cause (such as infection vs. allergy) before seeking help.
- You do not need to contact your primary care physician or wait for a clinic to open.
- You do not need to pack a bag or prepare for a long hospital stay right now.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a high level of anxiety when swallowing or breathing is affected. These symptoms require immediate professional assessment, and emergency teams are specifically trained to secure airways and stabilize rapid swelling. 

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization instructions for acute, rapidly progressing neck swelling and does not replace professional medical diagnosis or emergency intervention.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and is not medical advice. Rapid neck swelling can become life-threatening in minutes; always err on the side of caution by contacting 911.
