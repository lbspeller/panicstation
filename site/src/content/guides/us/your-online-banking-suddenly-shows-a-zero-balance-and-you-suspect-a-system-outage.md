# What to do if… your online banking suddenly shows a zero balance and you suspect a system outage (USA)

## Short answer
Verify the balance through a secondary channel like an ATM or phone banking before taking action. Contact your financial institution immediately via the number on your physical card to confirm if a known system outage is affecting display data.

## Do not do these things
- Don't transfer money from other accounts or take out emergency loans until the "zero" balance is confirmed as a ledger reality rather than a display error.
- Don't click links in text messages, emails, or social media comments claiming to offer "outage support" or "account restoration."
- Don't scan QR codes sent to you or found on unofficial sites during the outage.
- Don't repeatedly reset your password or security questions; technical outages often cause login sync failures that can lead to permanent account lockouts.
- Don't post screenshots of your empty account or error messages on public social media, as these often contain partial account numbers or routing data.

## What to do now
1. **Document the current display error.** Screenshot the screen showing the $0.00 balance, any specific error codes, and the timestamp.
2. **Attempt to verify your ledger balance via a non-app channel.**
   - Call the automated phone banking line (use the number on the back of your debit card).
   - Insert your card into a bank-owned ATM to perform a balance inquiry.
   - Log in via a mobile browser (web-based) rather than the dedicated app.
3. **Check for official service status updates.** Visit the bank’s official website by typing the address directly into your browser or check the bank's verified (blue-check) social media handles for an outage announcement.
4. **Report the discrepancy to the bank or credit union immediately.** Call the customer service number on your physical card or monthly statement.
   - Ask for the current "ledger balance" (the actual money in the account).
   - Ask if a "display outage" or "sync error" is currently logged.
   - Request a reference or case number for your call.
5. **Identify immediate payment risks.**
   - **Review scheduled ACH transfers** or bill payments due within the next 24 hours.
   - **Contact billers** if a major payment (e.g., rent or mortgage) is likely to fail, requesting a late-fee waiver due to a verified bank outage.
6. **Invoke Regulation E protections if you suspect unauthorized activity.** If the bank confirms the balance is truly zero and not a display error, report it as an "unauthorized electronic fund transfer" immediately.
   - **Report within 2 business days** to limit your liability to $50.
   - Note that the bank must investigate within 10 business days or provide a "provisional credit" while the investigation continues (up to 45–90 days).
7. **Request fee reversals.** If the outage caused overdraft or Non-Sufficient Funds (NSF) fees, request in writing that the bank waive or refund these charges once the system is restored.
8. **Escalate to a federal regulator if the issue remains unresolved.** 
   - File a complaint with the **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)** for most banks.
   - Contact the **National Credit Union Administration (NCUA)** if your account is at a credit union.

## What can wait
- You do not need to close your account or move to a new bank today.
- You do not need to determine the technical cause of the outage (e.g., cyberattack vs. server maintenance).
- You do not need to file a formal regulatory complaint until you have given the bank a reasonable window (usually 24–48 hours for outages) to restore service.

## Important reassurance
A zero balance in an app during a suspected outage is usually a "view-layer" error, meaning the app cannot reach the database where your money is safely stored. It does not typically mean the money is gone or stolen; it simply means the app cannot see it right now.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only for display-related banking crises. It does not cover identity theft recovery or long-term financial planning.

## Important note
This information is for situational stabilization and is not legal or financial advice. Banking regulations and individual account terms vary. Contact your financial institution directly for authoritative account information.
