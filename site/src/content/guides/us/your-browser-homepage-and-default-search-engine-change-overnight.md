# What to do if… your browser homepage and default search engine change overnight (USA)

## Short answer
Preserve remaining battery by enabling Low Power Mode and Airplane Mode immediately, then secure access to critical digital items (2FA/tickets) before attempting a single safe charging test or switching to wireless power.

## Do not do these things
- Do not insert metal objects (paperclips, needles, or SIM tools) into the charging port.
- Do not use compressed air, hair dryers, or external heat sources to dry the port.
- Do not place the device in rice or silica gel packets.
- Do not repeatedly force a cable into the port if it does not click into place or register a charge.
- Do not override "Liquid Detected" warnings unless in an absolute emergency, as this can cause permanent pin corrosion.
- Do not perform a factory reset or software update while the battery is below 20% and the port is unreliable.

## What to do now
1. **Activate maximum battery preservation.**  
   **Turn on Low Power Mode or Battery Saver, reduce screen brightness to the minimum usable level, and enable Airplane Mode.** 
   - Close all background applications.
   - Disable Bluetooth and Location Services (GPS) if not required for immediate safety.
2. **Secure time-sensitive digital assets.**  
   **Screenshot or write down critical information that you will need if the device powers off.**
   - Capture QR codes for travel tickets, event passes, or secondary authentication (2FA).
   - If using an authenticator app, verify you have access to backup codes on a secondary device or paper.
   - Note down essential phone numbers or addresses stored only on the device.
3. **Perform a single, controlled accessory test.**  
   **Inspect the charging cable and the port for visible debris using a bright light, then attempt one connection with a known-good wall adapter and cable.**
   - If the device does not respond within 60 seconds, unplug it immediately.
   - Do not wiggle the connector or apply pressure to the port.
4. **Respond to moisture or debris alerts correctly.**  
   **If an alert appears, tap "OK" (if prompted), unplug the cable, and hold the device with the port facing down.**
   - Gently tap the device against your palm to dislodge liquid.
   - Leave the device in a well-ventilated area with a fan blowing cool air toward the port for at least 30 minutes.
   - On Android devices, if the alert persists after drying, navigate to Settings > Apps > Show System Apps > USB Settings > Storage and tap "Clear Cache."
5. **Utilize alternative charging methods.**  
   **Use a Qi-certified or MagSafe-compatible wireless charger if the device supports wireless charging.**
   - If available, use "Reverse Wireless Charging" from another compatible smartphone by placing the two devices back-to-back.
6. **Document the hardware state.**  
   **Take a clear photo of the port and any displayed error messages using another device.**
   - Note the specific conditions (e.g., humidity, recent exposure to lint) to provide to repair technicians.
7. **Initiate a warranty or service request.**  
   **Contact the manufacturer or an authorized service provider to report the failure of the charging port.**
   - Under the federal Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, manufacturers generally cannot void your warranty for using third-party chargers or independent repair shops unless they can prove those specific actions caused the port failure.
   - Request a "reasonable opportunity to repair" under your written warranty terms.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the port failure (hardware vs. software) while the battery is critical.
- You do not need to purchase a new device or commit to expensive repairs until you have stabilized access to your accounts.
- You do not need to clean the port yourself if you have access to a professional technician later today.

## Important reassurance
Charging port failures are frequently caused by temporary software glitches or compacted lint and do not always indicate a destroyed device. Taking immediate steps to preserve battery and avoid forceful physical contact with the port prevents the situation from escalating into permanent hardware damage.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a failing charging port. It focuses on battery preservation and harm prevention during a power crisis. Long-term repair or replacement decisions should follow once the device power is stabilized.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional technical support. If the device emits a burning smell, shows signs of melting at the port, or becomes excessively hot to the touch, disconnect all power sources immediately and move the device to a non-flammable surface. Unauthorized physical tampering with internal components can lead to battery failure or fire.
