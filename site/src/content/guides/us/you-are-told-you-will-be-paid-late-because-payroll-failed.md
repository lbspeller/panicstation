# What to do if… you are told you will be paid late because payroll failed (USA)

## Short answer
Immediately request a specific, time-limited hold from IT or HR to conduct a supervised export of verified personal items only. Do not attempt to bypass management software or copy proprietary company data, as this can trigger legal action for misappropriation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not copy any proprietary, confidential, or work-product files to personal storage.
- Do not use personal cloud drives, personal email, or USB sticks to move data without explicit, written IT approval.
- Do not disable Mobile Device Management (MDM) profiles or tamper with security settings to stop the wipe.
- Do not delete browser history, Slack messages, or system logs in an attempt to "clean" the device.
- Do not assume that data can be recovered after the wipe occurs, as modern cryptographic erasure (CE) renders data unrecoverable.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact nature and deadline of the wipe.** **Capture a photo or screenshot of the wipe notification and confirm with IT if they are performing a "Full Wipe" of the hardware or a "Selective/Work Profile Wipe" that leaves personal partitions intact.**
2. **Create a granular list of essential personal-only data.** **List specific folders or file types that contain zero work product, such as personal family photos, tax documents (W-2s), or personal certifications, to present to HR/IT for approval.**
3. **Migrate all Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) seeds and authenticator apps.** **Ensure you transfer any personal account MFA tokens (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy) to a personal device immediately, as a wipe will delete the secret keys and lock you out of those accounts.**
4. **Sign out of all personal accounts and clear stored credentials.** **Manually log out of personal email, social media, and banking sites, then remove the device from the "Known Devices" list in your personal account security settings from a separate computer.**
5. **Request a supervised export through official channels.** **Send a formal email to IT and HR requesting a specific window of time (e.g., 30 minutes) to move approved personal files to an IT-sanctioned transfer medium.**
6. **Download employment-related documents from the HR portal directly.** **Access your company’s payroll or benefits portal from a browser to download pay stubs, W-2s, and benefit summaries rather than trying to find them on the local device drive.**
7. **Invoke specific state privacy rights if you are in a covered jurisdiction.** **If you reside in California, notify HR that you are exercising your right to "Access and Portability" of your personal information under the CCPA/CPRA, which requires them to provide your personal data in a usable format.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to organize or curate your personal files right now; focus only on the highest-priority items.
- You do not need to determine the legality of the wipe or the termination at this moment.
- You do not need to respond to IT tickets or work emails that are unrelated to the data recovery process.

## Important reassurance
Remote wipes are standard security protocols for most American companies and are rarely a personal attack. IT departments are generally willing to facilitate the recovery of clearly personal items if the request is made calmly, professionally, and through the correct administrative channels.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for data preservation. It does not provide legal strategy for wrongful termination, trade secret disputes, or forensic recovery of already-wiped devices.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and is not legal or technical advice. Laws regarding employee privacy vary by state, and company policies often dictate that all data on a company-issued device is company property. Unauthorized data transfer can result in civil or criminal penalties under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) or state trade secret laws.
