# What to do if… you find a hidden camera or recording device in your hotel or rental room (USA)

## Short answer
Leave the room and get to a safer public place first. If you feel in danger or someone may return, call 911; otherwise contact the accommodation or booking platform from a safe place and ask to be moved.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep using the room, shower, or bed “just for tonight.”
- Don’t confront the host, cleaner, or hotel staff alone or behind a closed door.
- Don’t take the device apart, remove memory cards, plug it in, or connect it to your phone or laptop.
- Don’t do a full room search while you are alone or unsafe.
- Don’t post details publicly while you are still on-site.
- Don’t let anyone pressure you into returning to the room alone.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** Put on clothes if needed, take essentials such as your phone, keys, wallet, ID, medication, and charger, and go to a public place such as the lobby, hallway near other people, or outside.
2. **Call 911 if there is immediate danger.** Call if you think someone may come back, you are being threatened, you cannot leave safely, or you need urgent police help. Give the dispatcher your exact location, room or unit number, and that you found a possible recording device aimed at a bed or shower.
3. **Ask to be moved without returning alone.**
   - **Hotel:** from the lobby or another public place, ask for the manager or security and say you need a different room now because you found a possible recording device aimed at a private area. Ask them not to disturb the room or device.
   - **Rental or platform booking:** contact the platform safety or support route in the app or website and request urgent relocation or rebooking because of a possible undisclosed recording device in a private area.
4. **Document only if it is safe and quick.** If you can do it without staying in danger, take one or two photos showing the device in place and the wider room location. Write down the date, time, room number or listing address, booking number, and the names of anyone you spoke to. If this feels unsafe, skip it.
5. **Ask for a record of what happened.** Ask the hotel or platform to make an incident or safety report, give you any report or case number, and note that the room and device should not be disturbed. If there may be access logs, staff entry records, or hallway camera footage, ask who can preserve them.
6. **Consider a police report from a safe place.** If you are safe and want to report, call the local police non-emergency number or ask the hotel front desk to connect you while you stay in a public area. Ask for the report or incident number.
7. **Use confidential support if you want it.** RAINN’s National Sexual Assault Hotline can provide confidential support and options 24/7 by phone at 800-656-HOPE (4673), online chat, or text “HOPE” to 64673.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to give a full statement, pursue a case, negotiate a refund, or write a review.
- You do not need to identify the device model, prove whether it recorded, search for every possible device, or work out who put it there.
- Longer messages to the host, hotel, platform, or police can wait until you are out of the room and supported.

## Important reassurance
Finding a possible recording device aimed at a private area can feel violating and disorienting. Leaving, asking to be moved, and getting support are reasonable first steps even before you know exactly what the device is.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: getting safer, reducing further exposure, documenting the basic facts if safe, and preserving options. Later decisions may need help from law enforcement, the booking platform, a victim-support service, or a lawyer.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate safety and next actions, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
