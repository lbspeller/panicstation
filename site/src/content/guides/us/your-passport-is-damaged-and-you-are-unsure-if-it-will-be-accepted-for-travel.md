# What to do if… your passport is damaged and you are unsure if it will be accepted for travel (USA)

## Short answer
Compare the damage against official U.S. State Department "replace if..." criteria, contact your airline to request a document reverification in their system, and schedule an urgent appointment if travel is within 14 days.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt DIY repairs such as using tape, glue, or heat to re-laminate pages, as this constitutes "unofficial alteration" and can lead to immediate refusal or detention.
- Do not use unofficial "expediting" websites that charge high fees without a direct connection to a regional passport agency.
- Do not wait until you arrive at the airport to check validity, as airlines have the final authority to deny boarding regardless of foreign entry laws.
- Do not report the passport as "lost" or "stolen" unless it is truly missing, as this permanently cancels the document and prevents its use for identity evidence.
- Do not provide digital-only identification or mobile driver's licenses at passport facilities; physical IDs are required for the application.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the physical damage against the State Department's mandatory replacement thresholds.**
   - You must replace the passport if it has: water stains, significant tears, unofficial markings on the data page, missing or torn-out visa pages, or a hole punch.
   - You do not need to replace for "normal wear and tear" such as a slightly fanned cover or a small bend.
   - If the data page or photo is obscured, blurry, or the laminate is lifting, treat it as a mandatory replacement.

2. **Call the airline exactly 72 hours before your flight to request a document reverification.**
   - Ask the agent: "Can you please confirm my passport status is cleared in the system for my destination?"
   - Describe the damage specifically and ask if they can add a note to your booking; if they express doubt, proceed with an urgent replacement.

3. **Determine if you qualify for "Urgent Travel Service" based on your departure date.**
   - If you travel within 14 calendar days, or within 28 days if you require a foreign visa, you qualify for an in-person appointment at a Regional Passport Agency.
   - Call the National Passport Information Center at 1-877-487-2778 (TDD/TTY: 1-888-874-7793) between 8:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. ET on weekdays, or 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. ET on weekends.

4. **Prepare a signed and dated "Statement of Condition" for the damaged document.**
   - Write a short, factual note (or use the official PDF template if available) describing how the damage occurred and that it was unintentional.
   - This statement must be submitted along with the physical damaged passport.

5. **Gather required application materials for an in-person Form DS-11 submission.**
   - You must apply in person for a damaged passport replacement; you cannot renew by mail (Form DS-82).
   - Bring the following:
     - Completed (but unsigned) Form DS-11.
     - The damaged passport.
     - Evidence of U.S. citizenship (original birth certificate or naturalization certificate).
     - A clear black-and-white photocopy of your citizenship evidence and your photo ID.
     - One recent 2x2 inch passport photo.
     - Payment for fees ($130 for the book plus a $35 execution fee).

6. **Contact the nearest U.S. Embassy or Consulate if you are currently outside the United States.**
   - Request an emergency appointment to replace a damaged passport; they may issue a limited-validity emergency document to allow for immediate return travel.

7. **Isolate the document to prevent further deterioration while waiting for your appointment.**
   - Place the passport in a dry, flat, protective sleeve or envelope.
   - Do not handle the damaged area, as worsening a tear or stain during inspection may escalate the classification from "damaged" to "mutilated."

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to cancel your flight until you have checked appointment availability or received a verbal assessment from your airline.
- You do not need to hunt for "expedited" mailing envelopes yet; urgent in-person appointments at an agency typically result in same-day or next-day document issuance.
- You do not need to contact the customs/border agency of your destination country until you have first confirmed the document is acceptable to the airline.

## Important reassurance
Panic is a standard response when travel plans are jeopardized by a document issue. This specific situation happens thousands of times a week, and the Department of State has established "Urgent Travel" pathways specifically for travelers in your exact window. Taking the official replacement path now is the only way to eliminate the risk of a gate refusal.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and the immediate official replacement process for damaged U.S. passports. It does not address travel insurance claims, airline refund policies, or specific entry requirements for foreign visas.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Final authority on the validity of a travel document rests with the airline carrier and the border control officers at the port of entry. Requirements and processing times are subject to change based on federal workload and 2026 security protocols.
