# What to do if… your visa or permit shows a different passport number than your current passport (USA)

## Short answer
First identify exactly which document or system shows the mismatch: your visa, your I-94 entry record, another permit or record, or an airline system. A valid U.S. visa in an older passport may not need correction, but a printed visa error or an I-94 error needs the appropriate official route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not alter a document, screenshot, PDF, or record yourself.
- Do not remove a valid U.S. visa from an older passport or try to transfer it into your current passport.
- Do not discard an older passport that contains a valid U.S. visa.
- Do not submit several correction requests before you know which record is causing the mismatch.
- Do not pay an unofficial agent who claims they can quickly change a visa or CBP record.
- Do not make irreversible booking changes until you have identified the document or system that needs attention.

## What to do now
1. **Ask where the mismatch appears.**  
   Ask the person who flagged it whether the questioned number appears on your visa, your I-94 entry record, another permit or record, or an airline system. Write down the number they are seeing and the document or system name.

2. **If your valid U.S. visa is in an older passport, check whether the two-passport route applies.**  
   You may generally travel with your current valid passport and the older passport containing the visa when:
   - the visa is still valid;
   - the visa is not damaged;
   - the visa is appropriate for your purpose of travel; and
   - both passports are from the same country and are the same passport type.  
   Keep both passports together. Do not remove the visa from the older passport. This step applies to a visa in an older passport, not automatically to every permit or record mismatch.

3. **If information printed on the visa is wrong, contact the issuing U.S. embassy or consulate.**  
   Contact the nonimmigrant visa section at the embassy or consulate that issued the visa. Ask what correction process applies before submitting a new application.

4. **If the mismatch appears on your I-94 entry record, retrieve the record and identify who issued it.**  
   Retrieve your most recent I-94 from the official CBP I-94 site. If the record is missing or appears to contain an error made by CBP at entry, use the official I-94 help page and the CBP Deferred Inspection Sites list. If USCIS issued the I-94, use the official USCIS correction guidance instead. Ask which route applies if you are unsure who issued the record.

5. **If another permit or record shows the mismatch, ask who issued that record.**  
   Ask the person who flagged the problem to name the issuing body and the exact record that needs updating. Use that body’s official contact route rather than assuming that a visa or I-94 process applies.

6. **If an airline system is blocking check-in, ask what source record is causing the block.**  
   Show both passports if you are using a qualifying older-passport visa. Ask what document or official confirmation the airline needs before making a new booking decision.

7. **Keep a short record of the issue.**  
   Save the questioned passport number, the affected document or system name, the date, and any case or reference number.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately that you need a new visa.
- You do not need to submit multiple forms while the affected record is still unclear.
- You do not need to make a new booking decision until you know whether the issue is an older-passport visa, a printed visa error, an I-94 error, or another record mismatch.

## Important reassurance
A passport renewal may leave you with an older document number and a current document number. A mismatch does not automatically mean that your visa or permit is unusable. Identifying the exact record is the useful next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for clarifying the mismatch before your next travel decision. A complicated immigration-status issue or an unresolved record problem may need qualified immigration help after the immediate situation is clearer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. A U.S. visa does not guarantee admission. CBP decides whether to admit a traveler at the port of entry. Check the affected record and follow the relevant official route before making a new travel decision.
