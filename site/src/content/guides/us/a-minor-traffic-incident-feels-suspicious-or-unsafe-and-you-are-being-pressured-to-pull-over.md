# What to do if… a minor traffic incident feels suspicious or unsafe and you are being pressured to pull over (USA)

## Short answer
Do not stop in an isolated place to argue or “sort it out.” If your vehicle can be driven safely and nobody appears to need urgent medical help, move only as far as needed to reach a nearby safer place, such as a busy, well-lit public area if available, and call 911 as soon as it is safe to do so if you feel threatened or followed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow the other person to a secluded location or a place they choose.
- Do not get out to argue, inspect damage together, or approach their vehicle if doing so feels unsafe.
- Do not accept a cash deal under pressure.
- Do not hand over your phone, wallet, or keys.
- Do not drive home if you think you are being followed.
- Do not argue about fault while the situation feels unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Reach the nearest safer pause.** If stopping where you are feels unsafe, continue only as far as needed to reach a nearby safer place. Obey traffic laws. Use hazard lights if it is safe to do so.
2. **Call 911 as soon as it is safe to do so if you feel threatened or followed.** When possible, call from a safe stopping place. Tell dispatch that a minor traffic incident occurred and that the other driver is pressuring you to stop somewhere that feels unsafe. Give your location, direction of travel, and the other vehicle’s license plate and description if you can do so safely.
3. **Tell dispatch about any injuries or vehicle damage that affects safe driving.** If anyone may be injured or the vehicle cannot be driven safely, say so immediately and follow the dispatcher’s instructions.
4. **Stay inside if getting out feels unsafe.** Do not approach the other person or open your door to argue. If the situation escalates, update 911.
5. **Record only what you can safely observe.** Note the other vehicle’s license plate, make, model, color, location, and direction of travel. Save dashcam footage later when you are safely stopped.
6. **Ask how to handle the crash requirements safely.** Duties after a crash vary by state. If the immediate stopping place feels unsafe, tell dispatch or local law enforcement and ask where to stop and how to exchange any information required where you are.

## What can wait
- Deciding who was at fault.
- Discussing payment or accepting a cash settlement.
- Calling your insurer until you are safe.
- Reviewing dashcam footage in detail.
- Getting estimates, arranging repairs, or deciding whether to file a claim.
- Posting about the incident or messaging other people.

## Important reassurance
Choosing a nearby safer stopping place is a reasonable response when a roadside interaction feels suspicious or intimidating. You do not need to argue, make decisions about fault, or agree to anything while you feel unsafe.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about insurance, crash reporting, and any follow-up may depend on your state and the specific facts. Specialist help may be useful after the immediate situation is stable.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 911 as soon as it is safe to do so.
