# What to do if… your residence permit card expires while you are abroad and you need to return home (USA)

## Short answer
Start by checking whether your card is a U.S. Permanent Resident Card, Form I-551, and how long you have been outside the United States. If you are a lawful permanent resident with an expired Green Card, you may not need Form I-131A, but check the current USCIS instructions and ask the airline to review your exact documents before changing flights. If your documents are not enough, check whether the official Form I-131A route fits your case.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume that every expired card and every Form I-797 notice work in the same way.
- Do not wait until airport check-in to identify the exact document you have.
- Do not pay an unofficial agent for a letter, shortcut, or appointment.
- Do not throw away your expired card or any original notices.
- Do not rebook flights in panic before checking the official route and asking the airline to review your documents.
- Do not sign or submit a document saying you are giving up lawful permanent resident status unless you understand it and have had a chance to seek qualified advice.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact card.**
   - Check whether it says **Permanent Resident Card** or **Form I-551**.
   - Check whether it was issued for **10 years** or **2 years**.
   - If it is not a Permanent Resident Card, do not use the Green Card route below. Check the document issuer’s instructions or the relevant U.S. embassy or consulate guidance.

2. **Check how long you have been outside the United States.**
   - Write down your departure date and planned return date.
   - If you have been outside the United States for **longer than one year**, or beyond the validity of a **re-entry permit**, contact the relevant U.S. embassy or consulate promptly. The official returning-resident guidance may apply instead of Form I-131A.

3. **Put your documents in one place.**
   - Gather your passport, physical Green Card, return itinerary, any re-entry permit, and any USCIS notices you have.
   - Look for a notice that specifically extends the validity of your card or your conditional resident status.
   - Bring the original paper notice if you have it. Keep a clear scan or photo as a backup.

4. **Match your situation to the current USCIS instructions.**
   - If you have an expired **10-year Green Card**, read the Form I-131A special instructions. You may not need carrier documentation, but ask the airline to review your documents before travel.
   - If you have an expired **2-year conditional Green Card**, check whether you have a valid extension notice that applies to your case.
   - If your card is lost, stolen, destroyed, or your available documents are not enough for boarding, check whether Form I-131A applies to your situation.

5. **Use Form I-131A only if the official instructions fit your case.**
   - Read the USCIS Form I-131A page before paying.
   - Use the filing and payment instructions linked from that official page.
   - Then follow the instructions from the U.S. embassy or consulate serving your location. Appointment and document steps can vary by post.

6. **Ask the airline to review the exact documents before your travel day.**
   - Use a clear message: “I am a U.S. lawful permanent resident. I have a [10-year or 2-year] Form I-551 card that expires on [date]. I also have [list any extension notice or re-entry permit]. Please confirm what your document-check team needs for boarding.”
   - Save the response and bring it with you. Treat it as useful preparation, not a guarantee.

7. **Get qualified help promptly if the route is unclear.**
   - Seek immigration legal advice if you have been abroad for a long time, the airline still refuses boarding after reviewing your documents, you are unsure whether an extension notice applies, or someone asks you to give up your status.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve every renewal question today.
- You do not need to make major travel or life decisions while you are panicked.
- You do not need to build a full legal case before checking the card type, time abroad, official instructions, and airline document review.
- You can deal with routine renewal steps after you have a safe, workable return plan.

## Important reassurance
An expired card does not automatically mean that a lawful permanent resident has lost status. The next step is to identify the card type, check the length of the trip, and use the official route that fits your documents.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and help you find a workable return plan. A long absence, unclear status, an airline refusal, or a request to give up status may need specialist immigration advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use current USCIS and Department of State instructions, and follow the local U.S. embassy or consulate process where required.
