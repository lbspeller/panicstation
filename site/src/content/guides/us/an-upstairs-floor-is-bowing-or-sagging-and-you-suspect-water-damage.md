# What to do if… an upstairs floor is bowing or sagging and you suspect water damage (USA)

## Short answer
Keep people and pets off the bowed floor and out of the room below if its ceiling is wet, sagging, or bulging. From a safe route, stop a suspected leak if you can do so safely and arrange urgent professional help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk across the bowed area to test it.
- Do not move furniture or other heavy items across the area.
- Do not stand under a wet, sagging, or bulging ceiling.
- Do not cut into the floor or ceiling, pull down drywall, or try to drain a ceiling bulge yourself.
- Do not touch wet switches, outlets, light fixtures, appliances, or electrical equipment.
- Do not use candles or an open flame to inspect the area.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** Leave the upstairs room without crossing the bowed area. Keep people and pets out. If the ceiling below is wet, sagging, or bulging, keep everyone out of that room as well. Close doors or block access from a safe route.

2. **Leave the home and call 911 from a safe place if there is an active emergency.** Call 911 if part of the floor or ceiling is falling or visibly moving, someone is injured or trapped, or you notice smoke, fire, sparking, or arcing.

3. **Stop the water only if the shutoff is safely reachable.** If a fixture or appliance is clearly leaking, close its supply valve. If the source is unclear and the main water valve is accessible without entering the bowed area or a wet electrical area, shut off the main water supply. If you cannot reach a shutoff safely, leave it alone and call a qualified plumber or building maintenance contact.

4. **Reduce electrical risk.** Do not touch wet electrical equipment. If the electrical panel is safely reachable by a dry route and you are not standing in water, switch off the affected circuit or the main breaker. If the route or panel may be wet, leave it alone and call a qualified electrician. Use a flashlight rather than ceiling lights in the affected area.

5. **Record only what you can see safely.** After the immediate safety steps, take photographs or video from a doorway or another safe location if you can do so without approaching the bowed floor or standing under the ceiling below. Note when you first noticed the change and whether it appears to be worsening.

6. **Arrange urgent professional assessment.** Ask a qualified plumber to locate and stop the leak. Arrange an urgent assessment by a structural engineer or another qualified structural professional. If you need help finding the appropriate local route, ask your local building department. Ask a qualified electrician to assess the area if water may have reached wiring, outlets, switches, fixtures, or the electrical panel.

7. **Dry the area only after it is safe to enter.** Once the leak is stopped and a qualified professional says the area is safe to access, ask how wet materials should be dried or removed. Moisture control and prompt drying can reduce mold growth.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause before keeping people away from the area.
- You do not need to open the floor or ceiling yourself.
- You do not need to choose a repair plan before the leak is stopped and the structure is assessed.
- You can contact your insurer or property manager after the immediate safety steps. Ask what photographs, records, and next steps they need.

## Important reassurance
You do not need to solve the whole problem immediately. Keeping weight off the bowed floor, staying out from under an affected ceiling, and stopping a safely reachable leak are useful first actions while qualified professionals assess the damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilize the situation and reduce harm. Repair design, mold remediation, insurance questions, and later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not engineering, electrical, plumbing, insurance, legal, or other professional advice. If you suspect an active collapse, injury, entrapment, smoke, fire, sparking, or arcing, move to a safe place and call 911.
