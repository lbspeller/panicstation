# What to do if… you are suddenly transferred to a different team or manager after a conflict at work (USA)

## Short answer
Avoid resigning or responding impulsively. Request all reassignment terms and the official business reason for the move in writing, then begin a private timeline of events leading up to the transfer.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign, walk out, or refuse the transfer immediately while startled.
- Do not send aggressive emails or post about the conflict on social media.
- Do not sign any "acknowledgments" or performance warnings without first requesting a review period.
- Do not rely on verbal assurances that your pay or duties will remain the same.
- Do not confront the individual involved in the conflict without a third party present.

## What to do now
1. **Request the transfer details in writing immediately.** Use neutral phrasing: "Please provide the official notice of reassignment and the expected start date in writing."
2. **Confirm the specific terms of the new role.** Ask HR or the current manager to document:
   - The new supervisor's name and the new reporting structure.
   - Whether the move is temporary, permanent, or pending an investigation.
   - Any changes to job title, hourly rate/salary, or FLSA status (exempt/non-exempt).
   - Changes to work schedule, location, or remote-work eligibility.
   - A list of core duties and how performance will be evaluated in the new team.
3. **Record observable cues of a "materially adverse" move.** Document if the new role includes:
   - Significantly less prestigious or more difficult duties.
   - Loss of supervisory responsibilities or "de-skilling."
   - Physical isolation from colleagues.
   - A schedule that creates a known hardship (e.g., conflicting with childcare).
4. **Draft a factual private timeline.** Include:
   - The date and nature of the original conflict or "protected activity" (e.g., filing a complaint, reporting a safety hazard).
   - Names of witnesses to the conflict or the transfer announcement.
   - The exact time elapsed between the conflict and the transfer notice.
5. **Acknowledge the move professionally while preserving your position.** If you believe the move is retaliatory, send a short email to HR: "I am concerned this reassignment is linked to my report on [Date]. Please confirm the non-retaliatory business reason for this change."
6. **Identify the relevant federal enforcement deadline.**
   - **Safety/Health (OSHA):** You must file a whistleblower complaint within 30 days of the transfer notice.
   - **Discrimination/Harassment (EEOC):** Federal law requires a charge be filed within 180 or 300 days, depending on your state.
   - **Group Rights/Concerted Activity (NLRB):** If filing with the NLRB, be prepared to submit all supporting evidence within 14 days of filing the charge.
7. **Contact support channels.** If you are a union member, contact your steward immediately. If your company offers an Employee Assistance Program (EAP), use it for neutral support to manage stress during the transition.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to file a formal lawsuit or external agency charge.
- Writing a detailed emotional narrative of the conflict.
- Negotiating a return to your original team or an apology.
- Confronting the manager who initiated the transfer.

## Important reassurance
A sudden transfer is often a management tactic to "de-escalate" a situation without making a final determination of fault. Being moved does not automatically mean you are in trouble or that your career is over. Following the formal process and keeping records allows you to stabilize your standing while you assess your options.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for workplace reassignments. It does not cover long-term legal strategy, specific state-level labor laws, or private sector vs. public sector litigation procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. Employment laws vary by state and specific circumstances. If you face immediate threats of violence or feel physically unsafe, call 911 for emergency assistance.
