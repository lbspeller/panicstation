# What to do if… you suspect you swallowed a caustic household product and your throat or mouth is burning (USA)

## Short answer
Call **911** immediately if you are having trouble breathing, drooling, or cannot swallow; otherwise, call **Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222** for step-by-step instructions. Do not induce vomiting or try to neutralize the chemical.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not** induce vomiting or use "ipecac" syrup.
- **Do not** attempt to "neutralize" the substance with vinegar, lemon juice, or baking soda, as this can cause a heat reaction.
- **Do not** drink large amounts of fluids to "dilute" the chemical unless specifically told to by Poison Control.
- **Do not** take activated charcoal.
- **Do not** put anything in your mouth—including water—if you are drooling, coughing, or having difficulty swallowing.
- **Do not** wait for symptoms to worsen before seeking professional advice.

## What to do now
1. **Assess your immediate ability to breathe and swallow.**
   - Call **911** immediately if you experience:
     - Difficulty breathing or wheezing.
     - Inability to swallow or severe pain when swallowing.
     - Uncontrollable drooling.
     - Swelling of the lips or tongue.
     - Repeated vomiting or vomiting blood.
     - Confusion, dizziness, or loss of consciousness.
2. **Contact Poison Control if you are alert and breathing normally.**
   - Call **1-800-222-1222** immediately. This is a free, 24/7 national hotline that routes you to local experts.
   - Stay on the line and follow their instructions exactly, as different chemicals require different responses.
3. **Clear the mouth of any remaining residue.**
   - Spit out any liquid or solid material still in your mouth.
   - If you are alert and can swallow without pain, you may take a **small sip** of water or milk to rinse the mouth/throat, but stop immediately if it causes coughing or gagging.
4. **Maintain an upright posture.**
   - Sit up straight to keep the airway clear and prevent the substance from moving back up the esophagus.
5. **Flush any external contact areas for at least 15 minutes.**
   - If the product touched your skin, remove contaminated clothing and rinse the area under cool running water for 15 to 20 minutes.
   - If the product splashed in your eyes, flush them gently with cool or lukewarm water for at least 20 minutes while waiting for help.
6. **Identify the specific product involved.**
   - Keep the container or label nearby to read the ingredients and brand name to the Poison Control operator or emergency responders.
   - Note the estimated time of ingestion and the approximate amount (e.g., a "sip" vs. a "mouthful").

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to identify the exact pH of the product yourself.
- You do **not** need to search for home remedies or "natural" antidotes.
- You do **not** need to determine if the product was "concentrated" or "diluted" before calling for help.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant anxiety or panic after a chemical exposure. In the United States, Poison Control centers manage millions of these cases annually and can provide the specific stabilization steps needed for the exact product you encountered. Avoiding self-treatment (like inducing vomiting) is the most effective way to prevent further injury right now.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for suspected caustic ingestion. Long-term management may involve hospital observation, endoscopy to check for internal burns, or pain management.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not replace professional medical diagnosis. Always prioritize instructions from **Poison Control (1-800-222-1222)** or **911** dispatchers.
