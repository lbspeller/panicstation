# What to do if… a car hire company refuses your driving licence and you have no backup plan (USA)

## Short answer
Do not drive without the documents the rental company or destination requires. Get the refusal reason in writing, stop avoidable charges, and switch to a safe no-car plan for today while you check official IDP or translation options.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive anyway if the rental is refused; you may not be covered by insurance and you could face local penalties if stopped or in a crash.
- Don’t buy an “instant online IDP” from a random site; many are fraudulent or not accepted.
- Don’t leave your passport, driver’s license, or other original ID with a rental desk or private driver as collateral.
- Don’t cancel hotels, flights, or onward plans in a rush; first secure where you will sleep and how you will get there today.
- Don’t assume a different rental branch or brand will accept the same documents without checking first.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the refusal reason in writing.** Ask: “Is my U.S. license being rejected because local law requires an IDP or translation, or because of your company policy?” Ask them to add the reason to the reservation notes or give you a written refusal.

2. **Check for a fixable document mismatch.** Confirm that your booking name matches your passport and license, your physical U.S. license is present and unexpired, and you are not relying on a photo, screenshot, digital wallet, or photocopy unless the company specifically accepts it.

3. **Escalate once, calmly.** Ask a supervisor whether the problem can be fixed by adding an accepted IDP or translation, correcting the reservation name, using another nearby branch, or changing the booking in a way that directly solves the stated refusal reason.

4. **Stop the fee spiral.** Ask what will happen to no-show fees, same-day rental charges, prepaid amounts, and card holds. If you booked through a third party, ask the desk to record the refusal reason clearly before you contact the booking platform.

5. **Move to a no-car plan for today.** Use official airport taxis, licensed ride-hailing where available, public transport, hotel shuttles, trains, coaches, or a private transfer from a reputable provider. If you are tired or arriving late, book one safe night nearby before redesigning the rest of the trip.

6. **Tell one person you are safe and changing plans.** Send your accommodation, travel companion, host, or emergency contact a short update with where you are and how you plan to get there.

7. **Check official U.S. destination guidance and the rental terms.** Use U.S. Department of State destination information for the country’s driving and transportation rules, then compare that with the rental company’s written terms for IDP, translation, age, license age, and payment-card requirements.

8. **Use only official U.S. IDP issuers.** For U.S. driver’s licenses, AAA and AATA are the two official organizations authorized by the U.S. Department of State to issue International Driving Permits. An IDP translates license information and must be carried with your valid U.S. license.

9. **Assume an IDP may not solve today.** If you are already abroad, an IDP may need physical processing and mailing. Choose a workable next leg now, such as train, coach, licensed taxi, transfer, or moving to accommodation where you do not need a car.

10. **Save proof for later.** Keep the reservation, refusal note, emails, chats, screenshots of the rental terms, card-hold records, and receipts for replacement transport or lodging.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain, dispute a charge, or demand compensation.
- You do not need to rebuild the whole itinerary now; focus on the next safe place to sleep and the next safe journey.
- You do not need to keep negotiating at the counter once the answer is clearly no.

## Important reassurance
This is a common travel failure point because local rules, rental-company policies, and counter checklists do not always match what travelers expected. Getting yourself safe, rested, and mobile today is enough for the first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment a rental car is refused abroad. Later decisions may need help from the rental company, booking platform, card issuer, travel insurer, local authorities, or a qualified professional familiar with the destination.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, travel, or professional advice. Driver’s license rules, IDP acceptance, insurance cover, and rental requirements vary by country, license type, and company policy.
