# What to do if… you feel close to fainting when you stand up and your heart rate jumps (USA)

## Short answer
Lie down flat immediately to restore blood flow to the brain and prevent a fall. Call 911 if you experience chest pain, severe shortness of breath, sudden confusion, slurred speech, or if you actually lose consciousness.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to "walk off" the dizziness or remain upright to see if it passes.
- Do not sit with your head between your knees; if you faint in this position, you may fall forward and suffer a head injury.
- Do not stand up again quickly or repeatedly to "test" your symptoms.
- Do not drive, climb ladders, use stairs, or take a hot shower until the episode has fully resolved and you have been steady for at least one hour.
- Do not take unprescribed salt tablets or adjust heart/blood pressure medications without a clinician’s direct instruction.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize immediate safety by getting low.**
   - **Lie flat on the floor or a firm surface immediately.**
   - **Elevate your legs** about 12 inches using a pillow, chair, or wall to help blood return to your heart and brain.
   - **Loosen any tight clothing**, particularly around your neck, chest, or waist.
2. **Screen for emergency red flags.**
   - **Call 911 immediately if you have any of the following:**
     - Chest pain, pressure, or tightness lasting more than 2 minutes.
     - Severe shortness of breath or difficulty breathing.
     - Sudden confusion, difficulty speaking, or facial drooping.
     - Weakness or numbness on one side of the body.
     - A pulse that feels extremely fast (over 150 bpm) or irregular while you are lying down.
3. **Establish a safety "backstop" if you are alone.**
   - **Keep your phone within reach** at all times while symptoms persist.
   - **Unlock your front door** if you feel a loss of consciousness is imminent, then lie back down.
   - **Call a friend or neighbor** and ask them to stay on the line until you feel stable.
4. **Initiate controlled rehydration.**
   - **Drink 16 ounces (approx. 500ml) of cool water** over 10–15 minutes if you are fully conscious and can swallow safely.
   - **Avoid caffeinated or alcoholic drinks**, as these can worsen heart rate changes and dehydration.
5. **Apply physical counter-pressure maneuvers.**
   - **Tense your leg, glute, and abdominal muscles** for 30 seconds at a time while lying down to help move blood.
   - **Cross your legs and squeeze them together** firmly if you must remain seated.
6. **Execute a staged transition to standing.**
   - **Move only in gradual phases** once the dizziness has passed:
     - Sit up slowly and stay on the edge of the bed or chair for 2 full minutes.
     - Pump your ankles and flex your calves 10 times before standing.
     - Stand up while holding onto a sturdy piece of furniture.
7. **Record the "clinical snapshot" of the event.**
   - **Note specific details while they are fresh**:
     - The time the symptoms started and how long they lasted.
     - What you were doing (e.g., just woke up, after a hot bath, after a large meal).
     - Any recent illness, fever, or changes to your medications.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the underlying cause (such as POTS, dehydration, or anemia) during the acute episode.
- You do not need to purchase compression garments or specialized salt supplements today.
- You do not need to resume your normal schedule or exercise routine until you have been symptom-free for several hours.

## Important reassurance
Feeling your heart race and your vision dim when standing is a common physical response called "orthostatic intolerance." It often occurs when blood pools in the lower body, causing a temporary drop in blood flow to the head. While startling, most episodes resolve quickly once you lie down and allow your blood pressure to stabilize.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm prevention. It is not a substitute for a medical diagnosis. Recurrent episodes require a professional evaluation, which may include "orthostatic vitals" (checking heart rate and blood pressure while lying, sitting, and standing).

## Important note
This information is for educational purposes and does not constitute medical advice. If you experience fainting, chest pain, or severe weakness, seek emergency medical care immediately by calling 911 or visiting the nearest emergency department.
