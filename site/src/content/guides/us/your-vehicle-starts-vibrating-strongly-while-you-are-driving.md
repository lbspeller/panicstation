# What to do if… your vehicle starts vibrating strongly while you are driving (USA)

## Short answer
Treat strong vibration as a possible tire, wheel, brake, or suspension safety problem. Get out of traffic if you can do so smoothly, do only a brief outside check from a safe position, and arrange help rather than driving on to recreate the vibration.

## Do not do these things
- Do not speed up to see whether the vibration improves.
- Do not keep driving at the speed where the vehicle shakes.
- Do not stop in a live traffic lane if the vehicle can still roll to a safer place.
- Do not stand on the traffic side of the vehicle.
- Do not let passengers wait near moving traffic.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle at the roadside.
- Do not touch wheels, tires, or brakes if they may be hot.
- Do not change a tire or attempt a quick repair on a narrow shoulder if traffic makes it unsafe.
- Do not keep driving if you see a sidewall bulge, a split tire, exposed cords, missing lug nuts, a tilted wheel, smoke, fire, or leaking fluid.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safer first.**  
   Turn on your hazard lights. If the vehicle still responds normally enough to steer and roll, slow smoothly and move to the nearest safer stopping place, such as a parking lot, gas station, rest area, side street, or wide shoulder away from moving traffic.

2. **Choose where people should wait.**  
   If everyone can reach a protected place, such as behind a barrier or well away from the roadway, without crossing active lanes or standing close to traffic, move there carefully. If traffic is close, visibility is poor, the shoulder is narrow, or leaving the vehicle would put people nearer to moving traffic, it may be safer to stay buckled inside with hazard lights on while you call for help.

3. **Do a brief outside-only check if it is safe.**  
   From the side away from traffic, look for a visibly flat or low tire, a sidewall bulge, cuts, exposed cords, rubber debris, an object stuck in the tire, missing lug nuts, a wheel sitting oddly, or the vehicle leaning to one corner. Also look for smoke, a burning smell, or fluid leaking.

4. **Stop the check if anything looks unsafe.**  
   If you see tire damage, a tilted wheel, missing lug nuts, smoke, fluid, or anything you cannot explain, do not continue the trip. Move away from fire, smoke, or leaking fluid if you can do so safely.

5. **Call for help.**  
   Call roadside assistance, a tow provider, or your insurer’s roadside number. If the vehicle is partly in a travel lane, there is immediate danger from traffic, there is smoke or fire, or you cannot get to a safer place, call 911.

6. **Move again only to reduce immediate danger.**  
   If staying where you are is more dangerous and the vehicle can still roll, creep only as far as needed to reach the nearest safer stopping place. Use hazard lights, avoid sudden braking or steering, and stop again as soon as you are out of the immediate danger area.

7. **Save simple details for the technician.**  
   Note whether the vibration was in the steering wheel, the seat, or the whole vehicle; whether it happened while braking or accelerating; the speed range; and whether you recently hit a pothole, curb, debris, or rough road. Take photos of visible tire or wheel damage if you can do it from a safe place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical cause at the roadside.
- You do not need to decide repairs, shops, costs, or insurance issues right now.
- You do not need to prove the problem by driving another mile.
- You do not need to remove the wheel, check underneath, or handle hot parts.

## Important reassurance
Stopping to assess was a sensible safety choice. Strong vibration can come from fixable problems, but a damaged tire, loose wheel, brake issue, or suspension damage can become dangerous if you keep driving.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and avoid irreversible mistakes. Later decisions may need a qualified mechanic, tire professional, roadside provider, insurer, or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional mechanical, legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. In immediate danger, prioritize emergency help and follow instructions from emergency responders.
