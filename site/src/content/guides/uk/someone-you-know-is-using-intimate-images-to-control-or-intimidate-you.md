# What to do if… someone you know is using intimate images to control or intimidate you (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause and contact a specialist support service to help stop the pressure. Do not comply with demands or pay money; instead, use new UK laws that require platforms to remove non-consensual images within 48 hours.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay, send more images, or agree to demands - complying usually leads to more threats rather than stopping them.
- Don't delete threatening messages or call logs in a panic - these are vital for reporting and takedown requests.
- Don't confront the person directly if you feel at risk of physical harm or violence.
- Don't share the images with friends or family "for proof" - this can accidentally spread the content further.
- Don't screenshot or store intimate images if any person shown is under 18 - this is a separate legal offence.
- Don't blame yourself - coercion and image-based abuse are tactics used by perpetrators to isolate and control.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and reach out for emergency help if needed.**
   - If you are in immediate danger or fear physical violence, call **999**.
   - If you need to report a non-emergency crime to the police, call **101**.

2. **Stop the direct pressure by pausing communication.**
   - **You do not have to reply to threats.**
   - If you feel you must say something, send one final, short message: "I cannot discuss this now" and then stop responding.
   - Mute their notifications or block them after you have secured evidence (step 3).

3. **Save the minimum evidence needed to prove the threat.**
   - **Capture screenshots of the threats, demands, and the sender's account details.**
   - Keep a record of the platform, the date, and any specific usernames or phone numbers used.
   - Avoid saving or duplicating the intimate images themselves.

4. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for practical takedown support.**
   - **Call 0345 6000 459 (Monday to Friday, 10:00–16:00) or email help@revengepornhelpline.org.uk.**
   - They provide specialist advice for adults (18+) on reporting images and navigating legal options.

5. **Utilise the 48-hour takedown rule on social media platforms.**
   - **Report the content directly through the platform's reporting tools.**
   - Under the 2026 UK legal updates, major platforms are required to remove reported non-consensual intimate images within 48 hours of a valid report.
   - If you are worried about images being re-uploaded, use **StopNCII.org** to create a secure "hash" (digital fingerprint) on your device that helps platforms block the file.

6. **If the person is a current or former partner, contact domestic abuse services.**
   - **Call the National Domestic Abuse Helpline (Refuge) 24/7 on 0808 2000 247.**
   - This is particularly important if the perpetrator has access to your home, children, or financial accounts.

7. **Access 24/7 support for sexual violence if applicable.**
   - **Call the 24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line on 0808 500 2222 (for those aged 16+).**
   - You can also access their free online chat service if you cannot safely speak on the phone.

8. **Secure your digital accounts immediately.**
   - **Change passwords for your email and primary social media accounts.**
   - Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all messaging apps to prevent the perpetrator from accessing your backups or active sessions.

9. **Follow specific protocols if the images involve a minor.**
   - **Report immediately to the CEOP Safety Centre if the images involve anyone under 18.**
   - Do not save, store, or forward images involving minors, as this is a serious criminal offence regardless of your intent.

## What can wait
- You don't need to decide whether to press charges or go to court today.
- You don't need to have a "perfect" file of evidence before asking a helpline for help.
- You don't need to explain the situation to your family or workplace right now.
- You don't need to know exactly how the person got the images to start the takedown process.

## Important reassurance
Threatening to share intimate images is a recognized form of abuse and a "priority offence" in the UK. Many people have faced this situation, and there are established legal and technical processes designed specifically to help you regain control. Your reaction—whether it is fear, anger, or feeling frozen—is a standard response to high-pressure coercion.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for image-based abuse. It is not a substitute for legal advice or a formal police investigation. Long-term safety planning should be done in conjunction with the specialist helplines listed.

## Important note
The UK government and tech platforms are legally required to act on these reports. If a platform fails to remove a reported image within the 48-hour window, specialist helplines can help you escalate the complaint. Always prioritize your physical safety if the perpetrator is someone you know personally and has physical access to you.
