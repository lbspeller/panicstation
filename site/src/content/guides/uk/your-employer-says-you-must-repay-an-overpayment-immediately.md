# What to do if… your employer says you must repay an overpayment immediately (UK)

## Short answer
Unless you are on a formal, paid on-call rota, you have a legal right to uninterrupted rest and a "Right to Switch Off" under current UK employment protections. You should establish a neutral written boundary, log the interruptions, and escalate if the pressure affects your statutory 11-hour daily rest period.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete messages, call logs, or timestamps — these are evidence of "psychosocial risk" and potential breaches of the Working Time Regulations.
- Do not engage in an emotional debate or argument during off-clock hours — respond only during your contracted working time to maintain the boundary.
- Do not feel obligated to explain *why* you are unavailable — your time off is yours by right, not by permission.
- Do not agree to "check in" informally — once you agree to be "available," it becomes harder to claim a breach of rest periods later.
- Do not ignore the impact on your health — if you feel constant anxiety or inability to rest, this is a health and safety issue, not just a management style preference.

## What to do now
1. **Verify your statutory rest entitlements immediately.**  
   Under the Working Time Regulations 1998, you are entitled to:
   - 11 hours of uninterrupted rest in every 24-hour period.
   - 24 hours of uninterrupted rest every week (or 48 hours every fortnight).
   - If after-hours messages require you to "be at the employer's disposal," they may legally count as working time, potentially putting your employer in breach of these rest requirements.

2. **Start a contemporaneous log of all contact (10 minutes).**  
   **Record the date, time, method (WhatsApp / Teams / Phone), and the specific nature of the demand.**  
   Note whether the manager explicitly used words like "urgent," "immediate," or "now." Save screenshots in a folder outside of work-controlled devices (e.g., your personal cloud or email).

3. **Check your internal "Right to Switch Off" or Communications Policy.**  
   **Many UK employers updated their handbooks in late 2025/2026 to include specific "Right to Switch Off" provisions.**  
   Look for:
   - Definitions of "emergency" or "business-critical" contact.
   - Policies on the use of personal devices for work communication.
   - Procedures for reporting out-of-hours pressure.

4. **Issue a concise, neutral boundary notice in writing.**  
   **Send a message during your next scheduled working hour to reset expectations without being confrontational.**  
   Use a standard script:
   - "To ensure I am meeting my rest requirements under the Working Time Regulations, I will be offline and unavailable for messages outside my contracted hours. If there is a formal on-call requirement I should be aware of, please let me know so we can discuss the rota and pay arrangements."

5. **Utilize technical barriers to "switch off" while preserving records.**  
   **Enable 'Do Not Disturb' or 'Focus' modes on your device to automatically silence work apps during your 11-hour rest window.**  
   Ensure your status on Teams or Slack is set to "Away" or "Offline" the moment your shift ends. You do not need to delete the apps, but you must stop the "ping" from reaching you during rest.

6. **Formally request a Stress Risk Assessment if the pressure continues.**  
   **The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) requires employers to manage psychosocial risks, including workload and inability to disconnect.**  
   If the pressure persists, tell HR or your manager: "This constant out-of-hours contact is causing work-related stress. I am requesting a Stress Risk Assessment to identify how these 'Demands' can be managed safely."

7. **Invoke your statutory right to a companion if moving to a grievance.**  
   **If you raise a formal grievance about a breach of your contract or the Working Time Regulations, you have a statutory right to be accompanied.**  
   You can choose:
   - A fellow workplace colleague.
   - A trade union representative or official.
   - Under the 2026 ACAS framework, your employer must agree to your choice if they fall into these categories and the request is reasonable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you are "loyal" or "a team player" — rest is a legal requirement, not a personality trait.
- You do not need to respond to a manager's "quick question" today to prove you are working hard.
- You do not need to finalize a long-term career move or resignation while in a state of high-alert stress.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel "guilty" for not replying, especially if your manager uses social pressure. However, the UK's 2026 regulatory environment explicitly recognizes that an "always-on" culture is a hazard to health. Setting a boundary is a professional act of compliance with health and safety standards.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for individual workers facing immediate after-hours pressure. It does not cover collective bargaining, specialized industries with statutory derogations (like emergency services or high-seas shipping), or complex employment tribunal litigation strategy.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights can vary significantly based on your specific contract, length of service, and any opt-out agreements you may have signed regarding the 48-hour week. If this pressure is impacting your mental health, consult a medical professional or a qualified legal advisor.
