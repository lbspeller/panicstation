# What to do if… you submitted an online entry declaration with an error and you cannot edit it before you arrive (UK)

## Short answer
Apply for a new Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) immediately if the error is in your passport or identity details, as these cannot usually be edited once submitted. For eVisas, use the official "report an error" service to notify the Home Office before you travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to board if you know your passport number or name does not match your digital record exactly.
- Do not use a "close enough" version of your details to bypass system locks.
- Do not pay third-party "fast track" services to fix the error; they have no access to Home Office records.
- Do not assume the airline can manually override a digital mismatch at the gate.
- Do not travel on a different passport than the one linked to your approved ETA or eVisa.

## What to do now
1. **Identify if the error is "identity-critical".**
   **Mistakes in your name, date of birth, passport number, nationality, or passport expiry date are critical and will cause a mismatch in the "No Permission, No Travel" system.** Minor errors like a home address typo are less likely to result in a boarding denial but should still be noted.
2. **Apply for a new Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) if applicable.**
   **If you hold an ETA and the passport details are incorrect, you must submit a new application and pay the fee again.** The ETA is digitally linked to a specific passport; a detail mismatch means the airline's automated check will return a "No Permission" result.
3. **Use the "Report an error" service for eVisas.**
   **If the error is on a UK eVisa (digital immigration status), sign into your UKVI account and select the "Report an error with your eVisa" link.**
   - Provide your name, date of birth, and nationality exactly as they currently appear on the incorrect eVisa.
   - Upload evidence of the correct details if prompted.
4. **Update your UKVI account details for changes.**
   **If you have a new passport and need to link it to an existing eVisa, use the "Update your details" service in your UKVI account.** This is for changes (like a renewed passport) rather than fixing a mistake on a pending or newly granted status.
5. **Verify dual nationality requirements.**
   **If you are a dual British or Irish citizen, you must travel using your British or Irish passport or a digital Certificate of Entitlement.** You are not eligible for an ETA, and using a different nationality's passport without an ETA or valid visa will lead to a boarding denial.
6. **Gather evidence of the correction attempt.**
   **Keep copies of your original (incorrect) confirmation, the receipt for your new application, or the "report an error" reference number.** If the system does not update before you reach the airport, you will need these to explain the situation to airline staff.
7. **Contact the UKVI Resolution Centre if the flight is imminent.**
   **If you cannot view your status or the error prevents you from generating a share code within 24 hours of travel, call the UKVI Resolution Centre.**
   - From inside the UK: 0300 790 6268.
   - From outside the UK: +44 203 875 4669.

## What can wait
- You do not need to request a refund for the incorrect application before applying for a new one.
- You do not need to contact the airline to "pre-warn" them; they will see the digital status during the standard check-in process.
- You do not need to update non-identity details (like a change of hotel) if the core passport data is correct.

## Important reassurance
Digital record errors are common, and the UK Home Office has established specific routes to report them. As long as you address identity-critical mismatches before arriving at the departure gate, most technical errors can be resolved by re-applying or using the official reporting tools.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for correcting digital travel records for the UK. It does not cover visa application appeals, legal advice for immigration, or airline-specific refund policies for missed flights.

## Important note
The UK's "No Permission, No Travel" policy is strictly enforced by carriers. An error in identity-critical data will likely result in a boarding denial regardless of the traveler's actual legal right to enter. Always ensure the digital record matches the physical passport being used for travel.
