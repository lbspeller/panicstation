# What to do if… the engine temperature warning comes on and you have pulled over safely (UK)

## Short answer
**Switch the engine off immediately** and wait at least 30 minutes for it to cool before attempting to open the bonnet or touch any part of the cooling system.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the radiator cap or the expansion tank cap while the engine is hot or hissing.
- Do not restart the engine to check if the light has gone off while the gauge is still high.
- Do not pour cold water quickly into a hot engine as this can crack the engine block.
- Do not stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or between your car and oncoming traffic.
- Do not attempt to "limp home" if the warning light remains red or the gauge does not drop.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your position and passengers immediately.** Turn on your hazard warning lights and, if visibility is poor or it is dark, switch on your sidelights. Turn your steering wheel to the left so the wheels point away from the road.
   - If you are on a motorway, exit the vehicle via the left-hand doors only.
   - Move behind the safety barrier and wait well away from the carriageway.
   - If you are in a live lane and cannot exit safely, stay buckled in with hazards on and call 999.
2. **Switch off the engine and allow it to cool for a minimum of 30 minutes.** Cooling can take over an hour in high ambient temperatures; do not rush this process as the system remains under high pressure while hot.
3. **Open the bonnet only when you no longer hear hissing or see steam.** Stand back as you release the catch to avoid any residual heat or vapour. If steam begins to vent again, close the bonnet and wait longer.
4. **Locate the coolant expansion tank to check the fluid level.** Look for the "MIN" and "MAX" markings on the side of the plastic tank. Do not rely on looking into the radiator.
5. **Top up the fluid only if the engine is cold to the touch.** Use the manufacturer-recommended coolant if available.
   - If using water in an emergency, pour it in very slowly with the engine off.
   - If the tank is completely empty or you see a puddle under the car, do not attempt to drive.
6. **Contact a breakdown recovery provider or National Highways.** If you are in an Emergency Area on a motorway, use the SOS phone provided. For other motorway locations, call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 or use your breakdown service.
7. **Note the specific symptoms for the mechanic.** Note if the fan was running, if there was a specific smell (like boiling sugar), or if the warning came on suddenly or gradually.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure (e.g., thermostat vs. water pump) right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair garage while you are still at the roadside.
- You do not need to worry about the long-term health of the engine until a professional assessment is made.

## Important reassurance
Engine overheating is a common reason for roadside assistance, and modern vehicles are designed to trigger these warnings before catastrophic failure occurs. By stopping safely and allowing the system to cool, you have already prevented the most significant risks of injury and further mechanical damage.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a stationary vehicle following an engine temperature warning. It does not replace professional mechanical diagnosis or recovery procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not supersede the instructions in your vehicle's handbook or advice from emergency services. If you see flames or heavy smoke, move away from the vehicle immediately and call 999. Do not attempt roadside repairs on a motorway.
