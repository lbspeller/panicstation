# What to do if… a station or stop closes suddenly and you need an accessible route to continue your journey (UK)

## Short answer
Pause in a safe area and use a Help Point or locate station staff to request **alternative accessible transport** to the nearest step-free station.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to use escalators, stairs, or steep ramps if your mobility aid or physical condition makes them unsafe.
- Don’t follow general crowd diversions blindly, as these often involve footbridges or narrow subways that may not be step-free.
- Don’t leave the station premises until staff have confirmed that your next destination or interchange is currently step-free and operational.
- Don’t separate from your mobility aid or allow staff to move it without you unless it is a verified emergency evacuation procedure.
- Don’t assume a "step-free" station on a map is working; lift outages can occur mid-journey.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe, staffed location or a Help Point immediately.** If the station is being evacuated, follow staff to the designated assembly point and identify yourself as needing a step-free exit route.
2. **Request an "Accessible Route Continuation" from the person in charge.** **State clearly that you require a step-free path and cannot use stairs or escalators.**
   - Ask: "Is the alternative route step-free from street to platform?"
   - Ask: "Can you confirm the lifts are working at the next two stations on this diversion?"
3. **Invoke your right to "Turn Up and Go" assistance if on National Rail.** **You do not need to have pre-booked assistance to receive help during a sudden closure or disruption.**
   - Staff are required to help you find a way to complete your journey or provide alternative transport if the rail route is no longer accessible.
4. **Demand "Alternative Accessible Transport" if the station cannot provide a step-free route.** **Under the 2026 Accessible Travel Policy, if a station becomes inaccessible, the operator must arrange a suitable vehicle—such as an accessible taxi—to take you to the nearest accessible station or your destination at no extra cost.**
5. **Ask staff to check the live "Passenger Assist" dashboard for lift status.** **Ensure they verify that any lifts you need at your next stop are currently "In Service" before you board a train or bus.**
6. **Use the Passenger Assistance (Transreport) app if you are at an unstaffed station.** **Press the Help Point button or use the app to speak to a controller who can dispatch a mobile assistance team or a taxi to your location.**
7. **Request that staff "Call Ahead" to your destination station.** **Ask them to notify the receiving station of your arrival time and confirm that a ramp or assistance will be waiting on the platform.**
8. **Keep your original ticket or proof of journey.** **You will need this to ensure you are not charged extra for the alternative transport or for any later compensation claims.**

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to fill out a formal complaint form or Redress claim while you are still at the closed station.
- You do **not** need to worry about ticket validity on alternative routes; staff-directed diversions generally override ticket restrictions during disruptions.
- You do **not** need to determine why the station closed or who is responsible for the lift failure right now.
- You can wait until you have reached a safe, accessible location before contacting friends, family, or work about the delay.

## Important reassurance
Accessibility disruptions are a known failure of the infrastructure, not a failure of your planning. It is normal to feel frustrated or anxious when a route suddenly becomes inaccessible. You have a legal right to complete your journey, and transport operators have established protocols to move you safely even when the primary route is closed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate hour following an unexpected station closure. It focuses on securing a step-free path and does not cover long-term legal claims or permanent mobility planning.

## Important note
This information is based on UK rail and TfL accessibility standards as of 2026. While operators are required to provide assistance, staff levels and vehicle availability can vary during major incidents. If you feel you are being asked to take an unsafe or inaccessible route, refuse the instruction and repeat your request for a step-free alternative.
