# What to do if… you are asked to prove your identity because your passport photo no longer resembles you closely (UK)

## Short answer
Request a manual identity verification from a supervisor or border official and provide secondary government-issued photo ID to confirm your details. State clearly that your physical appearance has changed since the passport was issued.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to alter the passport or any documents to "correct" the likeness.
- Do not joke about security, identity theft, or facial recognition technology.
- Do not raise your voice or physically resist if an official asks you to step aside for secondary inspection.
- Do not hand over your unlocked mobile phone; if requested to show digital records, keep the device in your possession.
- Do not leave the immediate inspection area until your passport is returned to you.

## What to do now
1. **Request a manual identity check if an automated e-gate or kiosk fails.** State: "The automated system is not recognizing me; I am requesting a manual verification by an official."
2. **Offer secondary government-issued photo identification to bridge the likeness gap.** Present your UK photocard driving licence or a previous expired passport if the photo in it more closely resembles your current appearance.
3. **Provide a factual, one-sentence explanation for the change in your appearance.** 
   - Examples: "I have undergone gender transition," "I have had significant weight loss," or "I have had facial surgery since this was issued."
4. **Present non-photo evidence that aligns with your travel booking.** If requested, show a bank card or a utility bill (on-screen or paper) that matches the name and address on your travel records.
5. **Ask airline staff to escalate to a duty manager if they refuse boarding.** If an airline agent is unsure of your identity, request that they follow their formal "identity verification escalation" procedure.
6. **Request that Border Force or airline staff note the time the check began.** This is important if you are at risk of missing a flight or a connection due to the duration of the verification process.
7. **Contact the FCDO if you are denied boarding or entry while abroad.** Call the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office 24/7 Global Response Centre at +44 20 7008 5000 for consular assistance if a likeness dispute results in being stranded.

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide medical records or "proof" of surgery/transition unless specifically requested by a senior immigration official in a private interview.
- You do not need to start a passport renewal application until you have completed your current journey.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint against the airport or airline while the identity check is still in progress.

## Important reassurance
Identity challenges due to a change in appearance are a standard part of border operations. Automated biometric systems frequently flag mismatches due to aging, weight changes, or medical procedures. Officials are trained to resolve these through manual checks and secondary questions; a referral to secondary inspection does not imply you are suspected of a crime.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for identity disputes at borders or check-in desks. It does not cover long-term passport renewal requirements or legal appeals for visa denials.

## Important note
Identity verification is at the discretion of the border official or the carrier's security staff. Providing secondary ID does not guarantee acceptance if the official is not satisfied with the likeness. In 2026, many airlines and borders have strict "zero-tolerance" policies for digital record mismatches.
