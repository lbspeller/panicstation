# What to do if… someone is standing too close to you at an ATM or payment terminal (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the transaction, move away from the keypad, and create space before you continue. You can try again at a safer machine, a staffed till, or another payment point once your PIN and card are private.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t continue entering your PIN while someone is close enough to see it.
- Don’t let anyone “help”, coach you through the steps, or touch the machine or terminal.
- Don’t argue, challenge, or try to prove what they were doing if leaving is safer.
- Don’t count cash or organise your wallet or purse at the machine.
- Don’t follow anyone to another spot, including “a better ATM”, if they suggest it.
- Don’t use a machine that looks altered, has loose parts, or behaves oddly.
- Don’t call a number given to you by the person nearby.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the transaction.** Press *Cancel* if you can, take your card and any cash already dispensed, and put them away before moving off. If you are at a payment terminal, ask the cashier to cancel or pause the payment while you step back.
2. **Create distance.** Move away from the keypad and out of arm’s reach. If it feels safe to speak, keep it short: **“Could you give me a bit of space, please?”** If they do not move, leave the machine or terminal.
3. **Use a safer setup before trying again.** Choose an ATM inside a bank branch, a staffed and well-lit location, a payment terminal where you are not boxed in, or a staffed till instead of self-checkout.
4. **Protect your PIN if you continue.** Stand close to the keypad and shield it with your hand and body. If you cannot get enough space to hide the keypad, cancel and use a different location.
5. **If the machine looks tampered with or keeps your card, don’t accept help from anyone nearby.** If it is safe, go to branch staff, shop staff, or security and contact your card issuer straight away using your official banking app, the card issuer’s website, or the number on your card. If it does not feel safe to stay, leave first and contact them once you are somewhere safer.
6. **If the person will not back off or you feel threatened, prioritise leaving over finishing.** Go to staff, security, or a busier well-lit place with other people. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
7. **If you think your PIN may have been seen or you were distracted mid-transaction, protect the card next.** Once you are somewhere safe, freeze or lock the card in your banking app if your bank offers this, or contact your bank using an official route. Ask about changing your PIN and check recent transactions.
8. **If money is missing or the card has been used, contact your bank first.** Then report suspected fraud through Report Fraud, or call the police on **999** if a crime is happening now or you are in immediate danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to finish the withdrawal or payment right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it was definitely suspicious.
- You do not need to confront the person.
- You do not need to report it while still beside the machine if leaving is safer.
- You can check receipts, statements, and follow-up reports once your card, PIN, and immediate safety are handled.

## Important reassurance
Cancelling, stepping away, and trying again somewhere else is a normal and sensible response. You do not have to be certain that someone meant harm before you protect your space, PIN, and card.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you get safer, protect your PIN and card, and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions, such as fraud reporting, reimbursement questions, or disputes about transactions, may need help from your bank, the police, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, financial, security, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
