# What to do if… you hear water running inside walls when no taps or appliances are on (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the situation as an active internal leak: **turn off your main internal stopcock (stop tap) immediately** and call a professional. If you cannot find the stopcock or it is seized, call an emergency plumber or your landlord right now.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait to see if the sound stops on its own, as hidden leaks can cause structural damage or electrical fires within minutes.
- Don’t attempt to cut into or open walls to find the source yourself.
- Don’t touch any light switches, sockets, or the consumer unit (fuse box) if you see water dripping from them or if the wall is damp near them.
- Don’t use a wrench or excessive force on a seized stopcock; if it snaps, you will be unable to stop the water at all.
- Don’t assume the leak is minor because you cannot see a puddle yet.

## What to do now
1. **Locate and close your internal stopcock immediately.**
   - **This is usually found under the kitchen sink, in a hallway cupboard, or under the stairs.**
   - **Turn the valve clockwise until it stops.**
2. **Verify that the water is off by opening a cold tap.**
   - **Open the kitchen cold tap; the flow should slow to a trickle and then stop within a minute.**
   - **If the water continues to flow at full pressure, your stopcock is not working and you must call for emergency help.**
3. **Scan the property for electrical hazards and structural changes.**
   - **Check for bulging plasterboard, sagging ceilings, or damp patches near electrical fittings.**
   - **If water is near any electrics, do not touch the switches; instead, if the consumer unit is in a dry area, turn off the main power switch.**
4. **Identify your responsibility based on your housing status.**
   - **Homeowners:** Call a WaterSafe-approved emergency plumber immediately.
   - **Tenants:** Call your landlord’s emergency repair line; in the UK, active leaks are generally considered an emergency repair that must be addressed within 24 hours.
   - **Flat Residents:** Alert the building manager and neighbours in the units below or adjacent to yours, as water travels quickly through floor voids.
5. **Check the external water meter if it is safe to do so.**
   - **If the internal stopcock is shut but the meter is still spinning, the leak may be on your private supply pipe (the pipe between the street and your house).**
   - **Note this information for the plumber or your water company.**
6. **Mitigate damage without interacting with the leak directly.**
   - **Place buckets or towels under visible drips.**
   - **Move furniture or valuables away from the area where the sound is loudest.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause of the leak (e.g., a failed joint versus a burst pipe) before calling for help.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company until the water is isolated and the immediate risk is contained.
- You do not need to worry about permanent repairs or "drying out" procedures today.
- You do not need to resolve disputes regarding whether a leak is your responsibility or the water company's while the water is still running.

## Important reassurance
Hearing water running behind a wall is an alarming experience, but you have taken the most important step by isolating the supply. Once the stopcock is closed, the immediate threat of escalating damage is paused. Professionals deal with hidden leaks daily and have the tools to locate them with minimal disruption.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Professional leak detection and repair are required to safely resolve the issue.

## Important note
If you suspect the sound is a gas leak (a hissing sound accompanied by a "rotten egg" smell), evacuate immediately and call the National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999. Do not operate any electrical switches.
