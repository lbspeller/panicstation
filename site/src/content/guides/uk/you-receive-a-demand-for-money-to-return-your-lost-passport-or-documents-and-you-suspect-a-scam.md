# What to do if… you receive a demand for money to “return” your lost passport or documents and you suspect a scam (UK)

## Short answer
Do not pay the demand or provide any personal details. Cancel your passport immediately through official UK government channels and apply for an emergency travel document if you are abroad.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send money via bank transfer, gift cards, or cryptocurrency to anyone claiming to have found your documents.
- Do not pay "courier fees" or "processing charges" to a private individual to have a passport returned.
- Do not send photos of other IDs or your visa to "prove" you are the owner of the lost document.
- Do not meet a stranger in person to retrieve documents, especially in private or unlit areas.
- Do not click links in messages from unknown numbers claiming to be "the police" or "the embassy."
- Do not attempt to "verify" the scammer's claims by asking for more photos; this often leads to further data harvesting.

## What to do now
1. **Cancel the lost or stolen passport immediately.** Use the official GOV.UK service to report the loss.
   - Once reported, the passport is cancelled instantly and cannot be used for travel.
   - This action is permanent; even if the passport is later returned, it is no longer valid.
2. **Stop all communication with the person making the demand.** Block the number or account on the platform where they contacted you. Do not tell them you are reporting them to the police.
3. **Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if you are currently abroad.** Use the official UK government online application.
   - An ETD costs £100 and is usually valid for one specific journey only.
   - You may need to attend an appointment at the nearest British Embassy or Consulate.
4. **Report the extortion attempt to the correct UK authority.**
   - **England, Wales, and Northern Ireland:** Report to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) online or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **Scotland:** Report to **Police Scotland** by calling 101.
5. **Secure your financial accounts if you shared any data.** Contact your bank or credit card provider immediately if you provided any payment details or personal information during the interaction.
6. **Report the scam message as phishing.** 
   - Forward suspicious text messages to **7726**.
   - Forward suspicious emails to **report@phishing.gov.uk**.
7. **Contact the local police if you are abroad.** Request a written police report or a crime reference number. You will likely need this for travel insurance claims and for the ETD application process.
8. **Contact your travel insurance provider.** Inform them that your passport was lost or stolen and that you are being targeted by a scam. They can advise on covering the costs of replacement documents.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately which flights to rebook until your ETD or replacement passport is confirmed.
- You do not need to respond to any threats or "deadlines" set by the scammer.
- You do not need to investigate how the person obtained your contact details.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel panicked when your travel documents are missing and someone is demanding money. These scammers rely on your urgency to bypass your judgment. By cancelling your passport through official channels, you strip the document of its value to the scammer and protect your identity from further misuse.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and harm prevention following a document-related scam attempt. It does not cover the long-term process of applying for a full replacement passport or resolving complex visa issues resulting from the loss.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. If you feel you are in immediate physical danger or are being followed, contact the local emergency services (999 in the UK or the local equivalent abroad) immediately.
