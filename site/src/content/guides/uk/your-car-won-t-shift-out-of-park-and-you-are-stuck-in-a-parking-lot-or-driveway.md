# What to do if… your car won’t shift out of park and you are stuck in a parking lot or driveway (UK)

## Short answer
Secure the car first: foot brake on, handbrake on, hazards on if you are obstructing anyone. Then try the basic interlock checks: correct ignition mode, firm brake pressure, and brake lights. If it still will not release, use only the owner’s manual shift-lock procedure to reposition safely, or call breakdown recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t force the gear lever; it may damage the selector or linkage.
- Don’t press the accelerator to try to “free” it.
- Don’t ask people to push the car unless you can already select Neutral, you have clear space, and you can stop the car immediately.
- Don’t try under-car, under-bonnet, or cable-release methods in a car park or driveway.
- Don’t treat a shift-lock override as a repair; use it only to get the car out of immediate obstruction if it can be done safely.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car safe where it is**
   - Keep your foot firmly on the brake.
   - Apply the handbrake fully.
   - Put on hazard warning lights if the car is blocking access, partly in a traffic route, or hard for others to see.
   - Ask nearby people to keep clear of the front and back of the car.

2. **Check the ignition and brake interlock**
   - Make sure the car is in the correct ignition mode for shifting; this is often “on”, “run”, or engine running, but it varies by vehicle.
   - Press and hold the brake pedal firmly.
   - Press the shifter release button if there is one.
   - Try moving from Park to Neutral first, rather than forcing it straight into Drive or Reverse.

3. **Check whether the car is recognising the brake**
   - Look for brake lights using a helper, a wall, a window, or another reflective surface.
   - If the brake lights do not come on, the car may not be detecting that the brake is pressed.
   - Stop forcing the selector and plan for recovery or repair.

4. **Check for a weak 12V battery**
   - If the dashboard is dim, flickering, showing unusual warnings, or not waking up properly, the 12V battery may be too weak for the shift interlock.
   - If you already know how to use a jump pack or jump leads safely, restore 12V power and repeat the brake-and-ignition check.
   - If you are unsure, do not experiment in the car park or driveway; call your breakdown provider.

5. **If the car is on a slope, avoid rolling risk**
   - Keep the handbrake fully applied and your foot on the brake.
   - Do not let anyone stand downhill of the car.
   - Do not try to rock or push the car to free Park.
   - If the selector remains stuck, call breakdown recovery and tell them the car is stuck in Park on a slope.

6. **Use the shift-lock override only if you can do it safely**
   - Check the owner’s manual for your exact vehicle and look for “shift lock release”, “shift-lock override”, “emergency shift release”, or similar wording.
   - Before following the manual: handbrake on, foot brake held, driver in the driver’s seat, and nobody in front of or behind the car.
   - Be ready for the car to roll as soon as Neutral is selected.
   - Move only as far as needed to clear access or reach a flatter, safer spot.
   - Re-secure the car with the handbrake and Park if Park can be selected again.

7. **Get help based on where the car is stuck**
   - In a private car park, tell site management or security if you are blocking a bay, gate, charger, ramp, or access route.
   - On a driveway, warn anyone who may need access and keep people clear of the vehicle.
   - Call your breakdown provider, insurer, lease company, or a local recovery service.
   - If there is immediate danger to people, call 999.
   - If it is not an emergency but you need police help with an obstruction or safety concern, use 101 or the police online contact route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact part now.
- You do not need to dismantle the centre console in the car park or driveway.
- You do not need to choose a repair garage immediately.
- You do not need to decide whether the problem is electrical or mechanical before the car is safely repositioned or recovered.

## Important reassurance
This situation can feel alarming, but the car is designed to stay in Park unless certain conditions are met. Working calmly through the brake, ignition, battery, and manual override checks reduces the chance of damage or uncontrolled movement.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to make the situation safer and get the vehicle repositioned or recovered. A mechanic, recovery technician, dealer, or vehicle manufacturer may be needed for diagnosis and repair later.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional mechanical, legal, or safety advice. Vehicle designs vary, so follow your owner’s manual where possible. If the car is in a dangerous position or you feel unsafe, prioritise personal safety and contact the appropriate services.
