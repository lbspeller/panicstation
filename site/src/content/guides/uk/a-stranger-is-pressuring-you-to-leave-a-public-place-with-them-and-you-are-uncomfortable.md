# What to do if… a stranger is pressuring you to leave a public place with them and you are uncomfortable (UK)

## Short answer
Maintain your presence in a visible, crowded area and move immediately toward staff or a group of people. If you feel you are in immediate danger or cannot move freely, call **999** (press **55** if you cannot speak safely).

## Do not do these things
- Do not move toward "quieter" or less visible areas like corridors, car parks, or stairwells to resolve the conversation.
- Do not prioritize being polite over your physical distance from the person.
- Do not enter any vehicle, lift, or private room with the person.
- Do not attempt to "wait it out" in an isolated spot like a toilet cubicle or a dark corner.
- Do not accept "help" or items (like drinks or a phone charger) that require moving away from the public space.
- Do not head toward your home or car if you believe you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a staffed point or a dense crowd.** Head to the nearest bar, shop counter, security desk, or reception area to create a physical barrier between yourself and the other person.
2. **State your situation clearly to staff using plain language.** Say: "I am being pressured to leave by that person and I am not comfortable; I need to stay here with you until it is safe."
3. **Use the "Ask for Angela" protocol if the venue displays the signage.** Ask a member of staff for "Angela" to discreetly signal that you need assistance leaving the situation safely.
4. **Direct a specific bystander to assist you if staff are unavailable.** Identify one person and say: "I do not feel safe; please stand here with me while I call for help."
5. **Call 999 if the person persists, touches you, or blocks your exit.** If it is unsafe to speak, stay on the line and press **55** when prompted by the automated system to alert the operator that you are in a genuine emergency and require the police.
6. **Use the British Transport Police text service if you are on a train or at a station.** Text **61016** with your location, carriage number, and a description of the person for non-emergency harassment; call **999** for active threats.
7. **Arrange a safe exit from the venue with professional assistance.** Ask security or staff to walk you to a licensed taxi or wait with you in a well-lit, staffed area until a trusted contact arrives.
8. **Record identifying details only once you are in a secure location.** Note the person's approximate height, clothing, distinct features, and direction of travel to assist in a future report.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to press charges or provide a witness statement immediately.
- You do not need to justify why you felt uncomfortable or "prove" the stranger's intent.
- You do not need to check if you "misunderstood" the person's behavior before seeking help.
- You do not need to report the incident to the non-emergency line (101) until you are physically safe and calm.

## Important reassurance
Uncomfortable feelings or a sense of "wrongness" are valid triggers for taking safety precautions. Freezing or appearing "polite" while under pressure are common physiological responses and do not mean you have failed to protect yourself. Moving toward help is the standard procedure for de-escalating stranger-based pressure.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for navigating stranger-based coercion in public spaces. It does not cover long-term legal options, stalking investigations, or forensic evidence collection.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving a crime in progress or immediate physical risk, contact emergency services at 999.
