# What to do if… a rideshare driver or car does not match your app details (UK)

## Short answer
Do not get in. Step back to a safer spot and only use a vehicle that matches your app or booking details, especially the registration or number plate, make/model/colour, and driver details where shown.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get in because the driver sounds confident, impatient, or offended.
- Do not move close to the car to argue, check inside, or read details if you feel uneasy.
- Do not give your name, destination, home address, phone number, or booking details to a stranger.
- Do not accept “the app is wrong”, “I changed cars”, or “my friend is driving” as enough reason to ride.
- Do not cancel in-app because the person tells you to.
- Do not agree to cash or off-app payment for a ride you booked through an app.
- Do not share a PIN, one-time code, or verification code unless it is part of the normal in-app pickup check for the matched vehicle and driver.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance first.** Take several steps back and move towards a well-lit, busier place, such as a shop, station, hotel, venue lobby, or staffed entrance.
2. **Check your phone before engaging.** Compare the vehicle and driver against your app or booking confirmation:
   - registration or number plate
   - make, model, and colour
   - driver name, photo, or licence details where shown
3. **Treat any key mismatch as not your ride.** If the registration, vehicle, or driver does not match, do not get in, even if the person says they are there for you.
4. **Use a no-details response.** If you need to speak, say only: “No thanks.” Do not confirm your name, destination, or booking.
5. **Verify from where you are.** Message or call the driver through the app or booking service. If the app offers a PIN or verification code, use it only with the matched vehicle and driver. If you cannot verify from your phone, do not ride.
6. **Move the pickup if needed.** Cancel or rebook through the app to a clearer, safer pickup point, such as a front entrance, marked bay, taxi rank, hotel entrance, station entrance, or somewhere near staff or security.
7. **If the car will not leave or you feel threatened, go inside and get help.** Ask staff, security, or a nearby trusted person to stay with you. Call 999 if you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress. If it is not an emergency, contact police on 101 or online once you are safe.
8. **Note details only if safe.** Write down the registration or number plate, location, time, vehicle description, and what happened. Do not take risks to get a photo.
9. **For London minicabs and private hire vehicles, do not accept a street offer.** Private hire vehicles and minicabs must be pre-booked through a licensed operator. Black taxis can be hailed in the street or picked up from taxi ranks; private hire vehicles cannot. If you later think a vehicle, driver, or operator was unlicensed, report it when you are safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a formal report.
- You do not need to work out whether it was a scam, a mistake, or a confused driver before getting somewhere safer.
- You do not need to confront the driver or prove they are wrong.
- You do not need to sort out refunds, complaints, ratings, or account settings until you are safe and calm.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to freeze or second-guess yourself when someone confidently says they are your ride. A vehicle that does not match your booking is enough reason to step away. You are allowed to prioritise safety over politeness.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes: creating distance, checking the booking safely, avoiding an unmatched vehicle, and getting to a safer pickup or route home. Later decisions about complaints, reporting, refunds, or account security may need help from the app, the police, TfL, a local council, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 999.
