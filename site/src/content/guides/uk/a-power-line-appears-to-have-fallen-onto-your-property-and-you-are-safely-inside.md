# What to do if… a power line appears to have fallen onto your property and you are safely inside (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside the building, keep everyone away from the side of the property where the wire is, and treat all fallen cables as live. Call **105** (England, Scotland, and Wales) or **03457 643 643** (Northern Ireland) immediately to report the hazard.

## Do not do these things
- Do **not** go outside to investigate, even if there are no sparks or noises.
- Do **not** touch any internal metal fixtures, taps, or running water until you have spoken to the network operator.
- Do **not** open any doors or windows closest to the line.
- Do **not** touch external metal objects like fences, gates, or sheds, as they may be energized.
- Do **not** attempt to move the wire or any debris (branches, poles) touching the wire.
- Do **not** assume a wire is "just a telephone line" simply because of its appearance.

## What to do now
1. **Move all people and pets to an internal room.** Choose a room on the opposite side of the building from where the line fell and keep the door closed.
2. **Avoid contact with plumbing and metal.** Do not use taps, showers, or touch internal metal pipes or radiators until the network operator confirms it is safe, as the building’s grounding system may be affected.
3. **Report the incident to the electricity network operator.**
   - **In England, Scotland, or Wales, call 105.**
   - **In Northern Ireland, call 03457 643 643.**
   - If the line is blocking a public road or path where others are in danger, call **999** as well.
4. **Provide the operator with specific details.**
   - State that an overhead power line has fallen onto your property.
   - Describe what the line is touching (e.g., roof, car, fence, tree).
   - Mention if you see sparks, smoke, or hear buzzing.
   - **Inform them if anyone in the house is medically vulnerable or relies on electricity for medical equipment.**
5. **Warn others to stay away.** Call or message household members who are not currently home and tell them not to enter the property; if safe to do so from a distance, place a clear sign on the inside of the main exit warning people not to leave or enter.
6. **Follow "Step Potential" safety if you must evacuate due to fire.** If you are forced to leave the building because of an immediate fire or smoke:
   - Use an exit as far away from the fallen line as possible.
   - Do **not** walk normally; keep both feet on the ground and **shuffle** your feet so they never leave the ground and never pass each other.
   - Keep moving in a shuffle until you are at least 10 metres away from the line and any objects it is touching.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to find your electricity bill or know who your supplier is.
- You do **not** need to take photos of the damage for insurance yet.
- You do **not** need to clear any storm debris or branches.
- You do **not** need to decide on long-term property repairs.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when you cannot see a physical threat like electricity. Staying indoors and keeping a safe distance is the most effective way to prevent harm. Most network operators will treat a fallen line on a property as a high-priority emergency and will dispatch crews to isolate the power.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization while you are safely inside the property. It does not cover technical repairs or safety procedures for those already outside.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and is not a substitute for professional electrical safety advice. If a fire starts or someone is injured, call 999 immediately.
