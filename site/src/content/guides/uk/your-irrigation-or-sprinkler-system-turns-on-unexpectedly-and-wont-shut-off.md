# What to do if… your irrigation or sprinkler system turns on unexpectedly and won’t shut off (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the water supply immediately by closing the dedicated irrigation shut-off valve or, if that cannot be found within 60 seconds, turning off the property's internal stop tap (stopcock).

## Do not do these things
- Don’t repeatedly cycle the controller power on and off, as this does not address mechanical valve failures.
- Don’t apply excessive force to a stiff stop tap or valve handle, as this can cause the pipe to snap or leak.
- Don’t attempt to dismantle or unscrew any pressurized irrigation valves or solenoids.
- Don’t wade into pooling water if there are outdoor electrical sockets, extension leads, or lighting nearby.
- Don’t ignore the sound of rushing water even if the spray seems to have stopped, as there may be an underground leak.

## What to do now
1. **Reach a safer pause away from moving water and electrical hazards.** **Check the area for slippery surfaces, spraying jets, and water pooling near outdoor power outlets or garden lighting before moving.** Ensure children and pets are moved away from the affected area.
2. **Turn off the power to the irrigation controller or timer.** **Unplug the unit from the socket or switch off the fused spur if it is hard-wired.** This will stop the flow if the issue is purely electrical or a programming error, though mechanical failures will require water isolation.
3. **Locate and close the dedicated irrigation shut-off valve.** **Look for a valve near the point where the garden pipework branches from the main supply, often near an outside tap, in a garage, or inside a green plastic box in the ground.**
   - Turn the handle clockwise to close.
   - For lever-style (ball) valves, turn the handle so it is perpendicular to the pipe.
4. **Close the internal stop tap (stopcock) if a dedicated valve is not found quickly.** **The internal stop tap is typically located under the kitchen sink, in a utility room, under the stairs, or near the front door where the water main enters.**
   - Turn the tap clockwise (to the right).
   - It may require multiple full turns to close completely.
   - It may take several minutes for the water already in the pipes to stop spraying.
5. **Check the solenoid valves if you can safely access the valve box and one zone is still running.** **Grasp the solenoid (the small cylinder with wires) and ensure it is hand-tight by turning it clockwise.** Do the same for any small manual bleed screws on the valve body. Do not use tools; hand-tight is sufficient.
6. **Protect the property from water ingress.** **If water is pooling near the house or garage, use towels or sandbags to divert the flow and move any valuables to higher ground.**
7. **Contact a professional if the water cannot be isolated or if there is damage to the mains.** **Report the issue to your landlord or housing association if you are a tenant, or call a WaterSafe-approved plumber if you are the homeowner and the stop tap is seized or broken.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the mechanical cause (such as debris in the diaphragm or a failed solenoid) right now.
- You do not need to reprogram the controller or test the system.
- You do not need to clear away mud or surface water until the supply is reliably shut off and the area is safe.

## Important reassurance
Irrigation systems frequently fail in the "open" position due to debris or mechanical wear. Turning off the controller often has no effect if the valve itself is stuck. Closing the main water supply is a standard and effective response to stop the flooding.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and water isolation. Professional repair of irrigation components or internal plumbing may be required once the emergency is controlled.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute professional plumbing or electrical advice. If water is in contact with electrical systems or you cannot locate your stop tap, contact an emergency plumber or your water supplier immediately.
