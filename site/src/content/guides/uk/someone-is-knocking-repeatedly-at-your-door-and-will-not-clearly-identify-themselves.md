# What to do if… someone is knocking repeatedly at your door and will not clearly identify themselves (UK)

## Short answer
Keep the door locked and do not open it under any circumstances while you are uncertain. Speak through the door or a closed window to ask for identification, and call 999 immediately if the person refuses to leave, acts aggressively, or tries the handle.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door "just a crack" or "to be polite" while you are still verifying.
- Do not rely on a physical ID badge shown through glass, as these can be forged.
- Do not call any phone number the person provides to "verify" their identity.
- Do not step outside to talk to them or lead them to a side gate or garden.
- Do not pass your phone, bills, or ID through the letterbox for them to see.
- Do not tell the person you are home alone or that you are feeling scared.

## What to do now
1. **Secure all entry points immediately.** Ensure the front door, back door, and all ground-floor windows are locked and the keys are removed from the locks but kept within your reach.
2. **Move to a position where you cannot be seen.** Turn off any lights that might silhouette you against a window or glass door panel, and move away from the direct line of sight of the door.
3. **Communicate through the door without opening it.** Clearly state: "I am not opening the door. Please tell me your name, the company you represent, and why you are here."
   - If they claim to be from the police / council / utility company / landlord, ask for their full name and employee ID number.
4. **Verify their identity using a trusted source.** Tell them to wait outside while you check. Use a number from a recent bill, an official website you find yourself, or call the police non-emergency line (101) to verify a police officer's warrant number.
5. **Call 999 if the situation escalates.** Dial emergency services immediately if:
   - The person tries to turn the door handle or push the door.
   - They refuse to leave after being told to do so.
   - They use aggressive or threatening language.
   - They are peering through windows or moving around the perimeter of your home.
6. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely.** If you call 999 from a mobile and it is unsafe to talk:
   - Stay on the line and listen to the automated instructions.
   - When prompted by the automated message, **press 55** to be transferred to the police.
7. **Request deliveries be left outside.** If the person claims to be a courier, ask them to leave the parcel on the doorstep or with a neighbour and walk away before you retrieve it.
8. **Contact a trusted person for support.** Send a message or call a neighbour, friend, or family member. Ask them to stay on the line with you or, if safe, to observe your front door from their own property.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to report the individual to a local "No Cold Calling" zone coordinator.
- Reviewing or saving doorbell camera footage for non-emergency reporting.
- Purchasing or installing new security hardware like door chains or reinforced locks.
- Checking social media groups to see if others have reported similar callers.

## Important reassurance
You have the absolute right to refuse entry to anyone who does not have a legal warrant. Genuine officials from utility companies, the council, or the police understand that residents must be cautious and will wait or provide valid verification methods without becoming aggressive.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization during an active, uncertain doorstep encounter. It does not cover long-term protection against stalking or persistent harassment.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. Always follow the instructions of emergency dispatchers if you are on a live 999 call.
