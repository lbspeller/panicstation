# What to do if… someone threatens to share your intimate images unless you send more sexual content (UK)

## Short answer
Do not send more sexual content. Pause contact, save only what you need, and get specialist support; this is coercion, and you do not have to deal with it alone.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send more images, videos, live “verification”, or sexual messages.
- Don’t pay money, gift cards, crypto, or “deletion fees”.
- Don’t negotiate, argue, threaten back, or try to shame them into stopping.
- Don’t delete the whole conversation in a panic if you may want help later.
- Don’t post public explanations or warn everyone while you are still panicking.
- Don’t blame yourself or apologise to the person threatening you.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.**  
   Put the phone down for 60 seconds if you can. Move near another person, or message one trusted person: “Someone is threatening to share intimate images of me unless I send more. Please stay with me while I deal with this.”

2. **Save the minimum evidence before blocking, if it is safe to do so.**  
   Take a few screenshots showing the account name, profile link or username, the threat, and any demand for more sexual content or payment. You do not need to build a detailed case file right now.

3. **Stop the access.**  
   On the app or platform where it is happening, block the account and report it as blackmail, extortion, intimate image abuse, or non-consensual intimate image abuse. Tighten privacy settings for who can message you, tag you, see your followers or friends, and view your stories.

4. **Use UK specialist help for intimate image threats.**  
   If you are 18 or over, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for practical help with threats to share intimate images, platform reports, and removal options, or use its 24/7 advice chatbot outside staffed hours. If you are under 18, use Childline’s Report Remove service and speak to Childline or another trusted adult if you can.

5. **Use an image-hashing tool only if it fits your situation.**  
   If you are currently over 18, were 18 or over in the image or video, and still have the file, StopNCII.org may help create a private digital fingerprint that participating platforms can use to reduce sharing or re-uploading. If you are under 18, use Childline Report Remove instead.

6. **Report to police only if and when you want to, unless there is immediate danger.**  
   If you are in immediate physical danger, call 999. Otherwise, you can report to police on 101 or online, but you do not have to decide that right now to get support.

7. **Get immediate emotional support if you feel unsafe with yourself.**  
   If you might harm yourself or cannot stay safe right now, call 999. If you are in England or Wales and want sexual violence support, Rape Crisis England & Wales has a 24/7 support line and online chat.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to report to police.
- You do not need to write the perfect reply; silence and blocking are allowed.
- You do not need to tell everyone pre-emptively.
- You do not need to search the internet for the images tonight.
- You do not need to pay for reputation cleanup, takedown services, or legal letters in the first panic.
- You do not need to decide what this means for the relationship or friendship right now.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, replying, sending something before, or feeling ashamed does not make this your fault. Threatening to share intimate images to force more sexual content is coercive. The first goal is to stop the immediate pressure, get backup, and avoid rushed decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation and reduce harm in the first panic. Later decisions about police, legal options, platform takedowns, or longer-term support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you are under 18, use child-focused support rather than adult image-hashing tools. If you are in immediate danger or might harm yourself, call 999 now.
