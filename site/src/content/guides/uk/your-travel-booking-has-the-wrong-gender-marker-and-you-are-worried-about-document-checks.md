# What to do if… your travel booking has the wrong gender marker and you are worried about document checks (UK)

## Short answer
Correct the travel-document details, often called API or passport details, so the gender marker matches the passport or accepted travel document you will actually present. Save written confirmation or a screenshot of the corrected details.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore it and hope nobody notices; a mismatch can cause avoidable questions, correction requests, or delay.
- Don’t cancel and rebook in a rush if the operator or booking agent can correct the passenger or travel-document details.
- Don’t assume the visible title, such as Mr, Ms, or Mx, is the main field; the border-check data is usually in the API, passport details, or travel document details.
- Don’t offer personal, medical, or transition-related documents at check-in unless you choose to; the practical issue is usually the booking data matching the travel document.
- Don’t travel on an invalid passport or a document you are not allowed to use for that route.

## What to do now
1. **Choose the document you will present.** For most international travel, use your passport as the source of truth. If the operator says another travel document is accepted for your route, use the details on that document.
2. **Find the travel-document details for this booking.** In Manage booking, My booking, or the operator app, look for Advance Passenger Information, API, passport details, or travel document details.
3. **Correct the gender marker in that section.** If the system lets you edit or resubmit the details, update the gender marker so it matches the document you will show. Check the name, date of birth, nationality, document number, issuing country, and expiry date while you are there.
4. **If you cannot edit it online, contact the right booking route.** If you booked direct, contact the airline, rail operator, ferry operator, or coach operator. If you booked through an agent, ask the agent and the operator which one can change the API or travel-document details.
5. **Use clear wording.** Say: “The gender or sex field in my API or travel-document details is wrong. Please update or resubmit it so it matches the passport I will present.”
6. **If travel is soon, ask what is still editable before departure.** Rules vary by operator and route, so ask them to confirm the correction has taken effect in the API or travel-document details.
7. **Keep proof in one easy place.** Save a screenshot, email, chat transcript, booking reference, and journey details on your phone.
8. **If you cannot get a response before travel, arrive earlier and use a staffed desk.** Ask staff at check-in, the ticket office, or the service desk to verify the API or travel-document details before you join the main boarding flow.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, request refunds, or change future booking habits.
- You do not need to explain personal history or provide extra documents beyond the travel document required for the journey unless you choose to.
- You do not need to fix every saved traveller profile tonight; focus on the specific trip you are taking.

## Important reassurance
This is usually handled as a travel-data correction, not something you need to justify. When the API or travel-document details match the document you will present, you have dealt with one common avoidable cause of questions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce disruption and prevent avoidable document-check problems. Later decisions about passport changes, complaints, refunds, discrimination concerns, or destination-specific entry rules may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. Travel operators and border processes can change, and different routes and destinations can apply different checks. If you feel unsafe or expect harassment, consider using staffed service points and travelling with someone you trust.
