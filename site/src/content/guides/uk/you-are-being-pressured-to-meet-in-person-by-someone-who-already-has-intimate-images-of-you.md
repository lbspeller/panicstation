# What to do if… you are being pressured to meet in person by someone who already has intimate images of you (UK)

## Short answer
Do not meet them, do not pay, and do not send more content. Isolate the hazard by stopping all interaction and contacting a specialist support service to manage image removal and safety.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a meeting to "talk them out of it" or "delete the files yourself."
- Do not negotiate, plead, or try to use logic to convince them to stop.
- Do not send "one last video" or "verification" photos to satisfy a demand.
- Do not delete your message history, as these logs are evidence.
- Do not share the images yourself as a way to "take away their power."
- Do not interact with any "friends" or "family" profiles they claim to have.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a location where you are physically surrounded by people or have a safe exit.** **If you believe the person knows your current location and is nearby, call 999 immediately.**

2. **Send one final message stating you will not meet and will not be communicating further.** **Do not engage in a back-and-forth after this message is sent, regardless of their response.**
   - Example: "I am not meeting you. I am reporting this to the police and a takedown service. Do not contact me again."

3. **Capture evidence of the threat and the specific images they claim to have.** **Take screenshots of their profile, the specific messages containing demands or threats, and any contact information (phone number or email) visible.**
   - Ensure the date and time are visible in your screenshots.

4. **Use StopNCII.org to prevent the images from being uploaded to major platforms.** **This tool generates a "hash" (a digital fingerprint) of your images directly on your device so the images themselves are never uploaded or seen by the service.**
   - This works for Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Threads, and Reddit.
   - Under 18s should use the "Report Remove" tool via Childline and the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF).

5. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline if you are aged 18 or over.** **They provide confidential support for adults and can assist with the removal of non-consensual intimate images (NCII) from the internet.**
   - Call 0345 6000 459 (10:00–16:00, Monday to Friday).

6. **Report the blackmail and image abuse to the police.** **If there is no immediate danger, call 101 or report online, stating that you are being blackmailed with intimate images and pressured into an in-person meeting.**
   - Note that under 2026 rules, tech platforms are legally required to remove flagged intimate images within 48 hours.

7. **Contact a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) if any physical contact has already occurred or is being threatened.** **SARCs provide 24/7 confidential medical and practical support without requiring you to report to the police.**
   - Call the national self-referral line at 0800 148 88 88.

8. **Tell a trusted adult or professional if you are under 18.** **This situation is classified as child sexual exploitation (CSE) and requires immediate safeguarding intervention.**
   - You can also report directly to the National Crime Agency’s CEOP Command.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to go to court or press charges today.
- You do not need to explain how they got the images to anyone except the specialist services helping you.
- You do not need to check your accounts to see if they have "posted them yet"—specialist tools will monitor this for you.
- You do not need to respond to "third party" messages that claim to be helping the perpetrator.

## Important reassurance
The pressure you are feeling is a deliberate tactic designed to make you feel isolated and trapped. You are not at fault for having these images, and many others have successfully navigated this situation by refusing to meet and engaging professional support. Takedown technology in 2026 is highly effective at stopping the spread of images once reported.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for acute pressure to meet. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, civil litigation for privacy breaches, or therapeutic recovery.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a substitute for legal or medical advice. Procedures and takedown timelines may vary based on platform compliance and the nature of the content. Always prioritize immediate physical safety over digital evidence.
