# What to do if… your bank’s app and website are down and you have a time-critical payment due (UK)

## Short answer
Use your bank’s telephone banking or a branch to ask what they can do now using the fastest appropriate payment route. Contact the person or company you are paying at the same time, ask for a short hold, and verify any alternative payment method through their official channels.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep retrying the same payment over and over; it could create duplicate payment attempts when systems recover.
- Don’t cancel a Direct Debit or standing order in a panic, because you may create the missed payment you are trying to avoid.
- Don’t trust “new bank details” sent by text, email, or social media because systems are down; verify any change through the payee’s official website, app, published phone number, or existing paperwork.
- Don’t share passcodes, card PINs, one-time codes, full security answers, or app approval prompts with anyone who contacts you unexpectedly.
- Don’t move money to a “safe account” because someone says there is a banking problem.
- Don’t take out high-cost credit or agree to a new repayment plan in panic before you have tried the bank’s phone, branch, and payee options.

## What to do now
1. **Write down the payment basics.**  
   Note the exact due date and time, the amount, the payee name, the account or payment reference, and what happens if it is late.

2. **Check whether the payment may already be scheduled.**  
   If it is normally paid by Direct Debit or standing order, the outage may not stop it. Do not cancel it unless your bank or the payee confirms that is the right step.

3. **Call your bank using a trusted number.**  
   Use the number on your card, statement, or the bank’s official website. Say: “My app and online banking are down and I have a time-critical payment due by [time/date]. Can you help me make it now by phone, branch, Faster Payment, CHAPS, or another available route?”

4. **Ask about timing before you authorise anything.**  
   Ask what payment method they would use, whether it can still be sent today, whether any cut-off time has passed, whether the payment is usually cancellable once sent, and what confirmation they can give you.

5. **Go to a branch with ID if phone banking cannot complete it and you can get there safely.**  
   Ask staff to check whether they can process the payment in branch. Ask for a receipt, payment reference, or written note of what was attempted.

6. **Contact the payee now to reduce immediate harm.**  
   Tell them your bank’s app and website are down, you are trying phone or branch payment, and you need a short hold on late fees, penalties, service suspension, or escalation. Ask them to confirm any official alternative ways to pay.

7. **Use another account only if it is safe and authorised.**  
   If you have another UK bank account you control, you may be able to pay from there using verified payee details. If it is a joint account, only use it if you are authorised and it is within agreed use.

8. **Keep a simple record.**  
   Screenshot error messages and service-status pages, note the time of each failed attempt, keep call times and names where available, and save any message from the payee agreeing to a hold.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to switch banks, change all your bills, or redesign your finances.
- You do not need to write a full complaint before trying to get the payment made or paused.
- You do not need to escalate externally today. Complaints usually start with the bank first, and outside escalation can usually wait until the bank gives a final response or the relevant response deadline has passed.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but it is a practical problem with practical next steps. Even if the app and website are down, some banks may still be able to help by phone or in branch, and many payees are more willing to pause action when you contact them before the deadline and keep evidence of what happened.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on avoiding duplicate payments, avoiding scams, and getting the urgent payment made or paused safely. Later decisions, such as complaints, fee recovery, legal deadlines, housing risk, or credit-file problems, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, banking, or professional advice. Payment availability, cut-off times, limits, branch services, and complaint response times vary by bank, payment type, and account status. If missing the payment could trigger eviction action, repossession steps, court deadlines, a failed property purchase, or another serious consequence, seek specialist help promptly while you continue trying the bank’s offline payment routes.
