# What to do if… your employer demands work authorisation documents urgently or threatens to remove your shifts (UK)

## Short answer
Reply quickly in writing, confirm the request through a verified employer route, and offer the standard right to work route that applies to you today. Do not send sensitive ID to an unverified contact or through an insecure method.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the message if you want to stay on the schedule; reply promptly, even if you are still arranging the check.
- Do not send passport, visa, immigration, or National Insurance documents to a random email address, WhatsApp account, or personal number you cannot verify.
- Do not send original documents by post or courier unless you have checked the process with a verified HR contact and are comfortable with it.
- Do not let a manager keep hold of your original passport or immigration documents.
- Do not provide extra identity or immigration paperwork “just in case”; ask what evidence is needed for the right to work route being used.
- Do not argue only by phone while panicked; keep a short written record of what was asked and what you provided.

## What to do now
1. **Check that the request is really from your employer.**  
   Use a trusted route you already have, such as the HR portal, an official HR email address from onboarding, or a phone number on your contract or payslip. Ask: “Is this a right to work check request, and what is the approved secure way to provide evidence?”

2. **Ask which right to work route they want to use and the exact deadline.**  
   Write: “I can provide right to work evidence. Please confirm whether you want an online check using a share code, a manual document check, an identity service provider (IDSP) check if you offer that, or another approved route.”

3. **If you are eligible for a share code, generate it and send it through the verified channel.**  
   Send the share code, and your date of birth if HR asks for it, through the employer’s verified route only. Do not send scans of extra documents unless HR confirms they are needed.

4. **If you are British or Irish, offer the simplest accepted evidence you have.**  
   If you have a British passport, Irish passport, or Irish passport card, ask to show it to HR or use the employer’s online identity service provider route if they offer one. GOV.UK says a British or Irish passport or Irish passport card can be current or expired for this purpose.

5. **If you do not have a passport or passport card available, ask HR exactly what document combination they will accept.**  
   You may be able to use a birth or adoption certificate, or a certificate of registration or naturalisation as a British citizen, together with an official document showing your name and National Insurance number. Ask HR to check the current right to work checklist before you travel or send anything.

6. **If you cannot provide a share code or acceptable documents immediately, ask whether the Employer Checking Service applies.**  
   Write: “I cannot provide the requested evidence today. Please confirm whether you can use the Employer Checking Service or another approved route, and please confirm what will happen to my scheduled shifts while the check is completed.”

7. **Offer a same-day way to complete the check safely.**  
   Suggest a practical time and place, such as before your shift, after your shift, or at the HR office. The aim is to remove the urgency without sending documents through an unsafe route.

8. **Keep a simple record.**  
   Save the request, the threat to remove shifts, your reply, what you provided, the time you provided it, and any HR response. Screenshots are enough if that is all you can do right now.

9. **If you think you are being singled out or asked for extra documents because of nationality, accent, race, or immigration history, get early advice.**  
   Contact Acas for guidance. Keep the message calm and factual: “Please confirm the same right to work process is being applied to me as to other workers in the same role.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to quit, make a formal complaint, or threaten legal action.
- You do not need to disclose your full immigration history; focus on the evidence needed for the right to work route being used.
- You do not need to solve wider rota, pay, or scheduling problems until the immediate document check is stabilised.
- You do not need to send every identity document you have; wait for HR to confirm the approved route.

## Important reassurance
Urgent right to work requests can happen because an HR file is incomplete, a manager is checking records, or an employer is running a compliance audit. A quick, written, secure response using a standard route often stops the situation escalating.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you respond safely and protect your position while the employer completes the right to work check. If the employer refuses to use standard routes, removes shifts despite your cooperation, or you suspect discrimination, you may need specialist employment or immigration advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, immigration, employment, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Rules and employer procedures can change, and your situation may be specific. If you are unsure which route applies, keep communications in writing, use verified channels, and seek independent advice.
