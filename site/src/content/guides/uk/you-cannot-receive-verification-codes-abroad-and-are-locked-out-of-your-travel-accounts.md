# What to do if… you cannot receive verification codes abroad and are locked out of your travel accounts (UK)

## Short answer
Stop repeated login attempts, get one reliable recovery route, then work in order: email first, then the travel provider account you need next. If travel is imminent, contact the airline, rail company, hotel, or booking provider directly through its official channel and ask them to find your booking without relying on your app login.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying codes or passwords; repeated attempts can trigger longer lockouts or extra fraud checks.
- Do not share verification codes, one-time passwords, backup codes, or screen-sharing access with anyone who contacted you first.
- Do not pay “account recovery” services or people messaging you on social media.
- Do not move, delete, or reset your UK SIM or eSIM until you have checked what it will affect.
- Do not sign out of any device where you are still logged in.
- Do not use a public or shared computer for recovery unless unavoidable; if you must, use a private window, do not save passwords, and fully sign out when finished.

## What to do now
1. **Write down the accounts you genuinely need for the next travel step.**  
   Prioritise your email, the airline or rail booking, accommodation booking, travel insurance, and any document wallet you may need to show.

2. **Try the quick phone checks once, then stop.**
   - Turn Airplane mode on for 10 seconds, then off.
   - Check that your UK SIM or eSIM is switched on.
   - Check that international roaming is enabled for the UK line.
   - If you use dual SIM, make sure the UK line is selected for receiving SMS.
   - Set date and time to automatic.

3. **Contact your UK mobile network through an official route.**  
   Ask them to check whether roaming is enabled, whether your line is barred, and whether inbound SMS or short-code messages are being blocked while you are abroad. Use the network’s official app, website chat, or published contact route over Wi-Fi if you cannot call.

4. **Recover your email first if it is locked.**
   - Use “try another way”, backup codes, recovery email, a trusted device, or an authenticator app if available.
   - If you are still signed in on any device, stay signed in and use that device to update recovery options.
   - Do not change several security settings at once unless the provider asks you to.

5. **Use the official recovery route for the travel provider account.**
   - Look for options such as “can’t access your phone”, “use another method”, “backup code”, “authenticator app”, or “contact support”.
   - Use only the provider’s official website, app, or phone number from your booking confirmation.
   - Ask support whether they can verify you another way and temporarily help you access the booking.

6. **If you must travel before the account is fixed, use the booking details instead of the account.**
   - Ask the airline, rail company, hotel, or booking provider to find the booking using your name and booking reference.
   - Ask them to resend the confirmation to an email address you can access.
   - Keep the payment card used for booking available, because providers may use it as part of identity checks.
   - Ask whether check-in, boarding, or hotel check-in can be handled at a desk or reception without logging in to the account.

7. **If the problem is really a passport or document emergency, treat that as separate.**
   - If your British passport is lost or stolen, use GOV.UK guidance to cancel it as soon as you can.
   - If you are abroad and need urgent travel but cannot use your UK passport, check GOV.UK emergency travel document guidance and eligibility before applying.
   - Do not assume an emergency travel document will be accepted by every country on your route; check the official guidance for the countries you will leave, enter, or transit.

8. **When you regain access, add one non-SMS recovery method before signing out.**  
   Use whatever the provider supports, such as an authenticator app, recovery email, backup codes, or a security key. Save backup codes somewhere you can reach while abroad, not only on the locked device.

## What can wait
You do not need to secure every account today. You do not need to replace your phone, change your number, close accounts, or design a perfect security setup right now. Focus on email, the next travel booking, and any passport or document issue that affects immediate travel.

## Important reassurance
This situation is frustrating, but it is often fixable through a calm sequence: phone network check, email recovery, then the travel provider’s official support route. Booking references and provider support can often get you through the next travel step even when an app login is blocked.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise access and avoid worse lockouts. Later decisions about account security, device changes, insurance claims, or travel disruption may need specialist help from the provider, your mobile network, your insurer, or an official UK government service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, security, or professional advice. Provider rules vary and can change. Use official provider channels for account recovery and GOV.UK guidance for British passport or document emergencies abroad.
