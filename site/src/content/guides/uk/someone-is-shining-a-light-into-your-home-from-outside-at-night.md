# What to do if… someone is shining a light into your home from outside at night (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from windows, secure the doors and windows you can reach safely, and call 999 if someone is trying to get in, a crime seems to be happening now, or you feel in immediate danger. If it has stopped and there is no immediate danger, report it through 101 or Police.uk.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside to confront them or check what is happening.
- Do not stand in a lit window, open curtains wide, or make yourself easy to see.
- Do not unlock a door to speak to them.
- Do not shout threats, chase them, or try to detain anyone.
- Do not post live updates showing that you are home or where you are inside.
- Do not dismiss it if the light is scanning rooms, doors, windows, or private areas.

## What to do now
1. **Move out of sight.** Step back from windows and glass doors. If you can, go to an internal room or a room that does not face the light.
2. **Secure the home from where you are safe.** Lock external doors, close accessible windows, and close curtains or blinds without standing in front of the glass.
3. **Keep your phone and keys with you.** Keep your phone charged or plugged in if possible. Put keys where you can reach them without going near the door.
4. **Use lights carefully.** If you can turn on an outside, porch, or security light from a safe place, do that. Do not move into view to switch lights on or off.
5. **Check cameras without exposing yourself.** If you have a doorbell camera, CCTV, or peephole camera, check it from inside. Do not open the door to get a better look.
6. **Make a short note while it is fresh.** Write down the time, where the light came from, whether it moved across doors or windows, and any vehicle, clothing, voice, or direction of travel you noticed.
7. **Call 999 if the risk feels immediate.** Use 999 if someone is trying to get in, you can hear tampering at a door or window, someone is threatening you, a crime seems to be happening now, or you feel in immediate danger.
8. **Use silent 999 if speaking would put you at risk.** Call 999 and try to speak, whisper, cough, or tap if you can. If you are calling from a mobile and are prompted, press 55 to confirm it is a genuine emergency and be put through to police.
9. **Use 101 or Police.uk if it is no longer immediate.** If the person or light has gone and you are not in immediate danger, report what happened through 101 or the online police reporting route.
10. **If you are in a flat or managed building, avoid communal areas.** Contact concierge, security, or the building manager from inside if you have that option, especially if the light was in a shared garden, car park, corridor, or stairwell.
11. **If other people are home, keep it simple.** Quietly wake them, keep everyone away from windows, and have one person speak to police or building security.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether it was a prank, a neighbour, a delivery person, a mistake, or targeted behaviour.
- You do not need to go outside to check for damage until you feel safe and, if needed, police have attended.
- You do not need to message neighbours, argue with anyone, or gather more proof before asking police for help.
- If it later looks like repeated nuisance lighting rather than an immediate safety risk, you can check your local council’s website or ask police about the right non-emergency route later.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shaky, frozen, or unsure is a normal reaction to seeing a light inside your home at night. You are allowed to act cautiously first and work out the explanation later.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. If this becomes a repeated pattern, later decisions about harassment, anti-social behaviour, nuisance lighting, cameras, locks, or housing support may need specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information only and is not legal, security, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you think you are in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.
