# What to do if… the name on your flight ticket does not match your passport (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the company that issued the ticket now, either the airline or the travel agent, and ask for a passenger name correction so the ticketed name matches your passport. Do not assume this can be fixed at the airport or by changing only your Advance Passenger Information.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rely on only updating your details in “Manage booking” or “Advance Passenger Information” and assume the ticketed name has changed.
- Do not check in and hope for the best if the mismatch is more than a small typo; it may reduce your options or require support to undo.
- Do not buy a new ticket in a rush until you have checked whether a correction or reissue is possible on your fare.
- Do not use a different identity document just because it matches the booking, unless it is valid for this trip and accepted for your destination.
- Do not ask for a passenger swap if the traveller is the same person; ask for a name correction to match the passport.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the ticketed name with your passport, character by character.** Write down exactly what is different, such as a missing middle name, one or two wrong letters, the wrong surname, a shortened first name, or first and last names reversed.
2. **Work out who issued the ticket.**
   - **Booked through a travel agent or travel website:** contact them first; the airline may not be able to amend a ticket the agent issued.
   - **Booked directly with the airline:** contact the airline directly.
3. **Ask for a “name correction to match passport” for the same traveller.** Many tickets are not transferable to another person, but airlines or agents may allow limited corrections, sometimes with a fee or reissue.
4. **Ask these exact questions before check-in and departure.**
   - “Can you correct the passenger name to match the passport and reissue the ticket if needed?”
   - “Will the correction apply to every flight on the booking, including partner or codeshare flights?”
   - “Will the ticket, boarding pass, and airport systems show the corrected name?”
   - “Is there any fee, fare difference, deadline, or document you need from me now?”
5. **If the difference is because of a legal name change, have proof ready.** This might include a marriage or civil partnership certificate, or a deed poll. The airline or agent may ask for evidence before correcting or reissuing the ticket.
6. **Use more than one contact route if departure is close.** Try phone, live chat, app support, and direct message channels, and keep screenshots or notes of what you are told.
7. **If they say it cannot be corrected, ask for the least-worst option in writing.** Ask them to confirm the reason, whether you can cancel and rebook, and whether any refund, credit, or fee reduction applies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain or seek compensation; first focus on whether you can travel.
- For an imminent trip, you do not need to start by changing your passport; first focus on making the ticket match the passport you will use.
- You do not need to debate the whole policy now. Keep the request narrow: “What can you do before departure so the ticket matches my passport?”
- You do not need to decide future naming arrangements for other bookings today.

## Important reassurance
This is a common last-minute discovery, especially after typos, surname changes, or booking through a third party. Acting quickly and keeping the request focused on correcting the same traveller’s name gives you the best chance of resolving it.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. What is possible depends on the airline, ticket issuer, fare rules, route, timing, and whether partner airlines are involved; later decisions may need specialist help from the airline, agent, insurer, card provider, or a consumer advice service.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, travel agency, immigration, or professional advice. Airline policies vary and can be strict; if you are unsure whether a mismatch is minor, treat it as urgent and get the issuer to confirm in writing what name will appear on the ticket and boarding pass.
