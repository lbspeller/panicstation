# What to do if… your authenticator app shows accounts missing after an update or phone change (UK)

## Short answer
Do not click links, call numbers, or "Decline" the invites, as this confirms your account is active. Remove the underlying subscription or auto-add setting in your calendar app to stop the events from appearing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click any links or open attachments within the spam events.
- Do not call any phone numbers listed in the event titles or descriptions.
- Do not select "Accept", "Maybe", or "Decline" on the invite; interacting with these buttons alerts the spammer that your email is monitored.
- Do not use "unsubscribe" links found inside the event text or body.
- Do not install any "cleaning" or "security" software prompted by the calendar events.

## What to do now
1. **Take one screenshot of the spam event without clicking any links.**
   - Ensure the **Calendar Name** or **Source** is visible in the screenshot.
   - Close the event immediately after capturing the image.

2. **Check for and remove unrecognized calendar subscriptions.**
   - **On iPhone/iPad:** Go to **Settings > Calendar > Accounts**. If you see **Subscribed Calendars**, tap it, select the rogue calendar, and select **Delete Account**.
   - **On Google Calendar (Web):** Look at the **Other calendars** list on the left. Hover over unfamiliar entries, click the **X**, and select **Remove calendar**.
   - **On Outlook:** Right-click any unfamiliar calendar folders in your list and select **Delete Calendar**.

3. **Disable the "auto-add" features that allow spam to bypass your inbox.**
   - **Google Calendar:** Go to **Settings > Event settings**. Under **Add invitations to my calendar**, select **When I respond to the invitation in email**.
   - **Google Calendar (Gmail events):** In settings, go to **Events from Gmail** and uncheck **Show events automatically created by Gmail**.
   - **Outlook/Microsoft 365:** Go to **Settings > Calendar > Events from email**. Change all toggles (flights, packages, etc.) to **Don't show event summaries on my calendar**.
   - **Outlook (Tracking):** Under **Calendar > Events and invitations**, uncheck **Automatically process meeting requests and responses**.

4. **Secure the account that hosts the calendar.**
   - **Change your password** for the affected Apple, Google, or Microsoft account.
   - **Enable Two-Step Verification (2FA)** if it is not already active.
   - **Revoke third-party app access** by reviewing the "Connected Apps" or "Security" section of your account settings and removing any unfamiliar services.

5. **Report the activity to official UK channels.**
   - **Forward suspicious emails** that triggered the invites to **report@phishing.gov.uk**.
   - **Report to the official police service:** If you are in England, Wales, or NI and have lost money or shared data, use the **Report Fraud** service at **reportfraud.police.uk**.
   - **Scotland reporting:** If you are in Scotland, call **Police Scotland on 101** to report the fraud.

## What can wait
- You do not need to manually delete every single past spam entry once the subscription or auto-add setting is disabled; they often vanish automatically.
- You do not need to perform a full factory reset of your device unless you physically installed a suspicious file or shared a device passcode.
- You do not need to contact your mobile network provider unless you called a premium-rate number listed in the spam.

## Important reassurance
Seeing a calendar full of "virus alerts" or "urgent threats" is a standard scare tactic designed to make you act impulsively. This does not mean your device has been hacked; it usually means a spammer found your email address and exploited an "auto-add" feature. Once you change the settings, the flood of events will stop.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital calendar abuse. It does not cover technical forensic recovery if your full device or network has been compromised by malware.

## Important note
This information is for guidance purposes and does not constitute technical or legal advice. If this occurs on a work-managed device, you must prioritize reporting it to your organization's IT security department, as they may have specific enterprise-level protocols for clearing calendar cache and managing mail flow rules.
