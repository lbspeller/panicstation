# What to do if… water starts bubbling up in the garden or driveway and you suspect a buried pipe leak (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the area to prevent ground-collapse accidents, turn off your internal stop tap to check for flow, and immediately notify your water company to determine if the leak is on a public or private pipe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk, park, or drive over the area where water is bubbling or the ground feels soft.
- Do not dig into the ground to find the source, as you may strike gas lines or electrical cables.
- Do not use electrical garden tools, lawnmowers, or jet washers near the standing water.
- Do not wait to report the issue until you have "proven" where the leak is located.
- Do not attempt to operate heavy boundary stop-tap covers without proper tools or assistance.

## What to do now
1. **Clear the immediate area and create a exclusion zone.** Move people, pets, and vehicles away from the wet or bubbling patch, as underground erosion can cause sudden sinkholes or surface collapses.
2. **Perform a sensory check for sewage.** Observe the water for a foul smell or debris; if the water is clear and odourless, it is likely a clean water supply pipe; if it smells of sulphur or sewage, it is likely a wastewater pipe.
3. **Conduct a water meter isolation test if one is installed.** Turn off all taps and water-using appliances inside the house, locate your water meter (usually under a small cover in the pavement or garden), and check if the dial or flow indicator is still moving.
   - If the meter moves while internal water is off, the leak is likely on your private supply pipe.
4. **Turn off the internal stop tap to narrow down the location.** Close the main valve inside your home (usually found under the kitchen sink or where the pipe enters the building) and watch the bubbling area for one to two minutes.
   - Continued bubbling suggests the leak is "upstream" of your house, between the boundary and the internal tap.
5. **Contact your water company’s 24-hour emergency leak line.** Provide the specific location (e.g., "three metres from the pavement edge") and describe the flow (e.g., "steady bubbling" or "significant flooding").
   - The company is responsible for the "communication pipe" (from the main to your property boundary).
   - You or your landlord are responsible for the "supply pipe" (from the boundary to the house).
6. **Alert your landlord or letting agent immediately if you are a tenant.** Provide them with the water company’s report reference number and specify if the leak is causing ground instability near the structure.
7. **Contact 999 only if there is an immediate threat to life.** This includes water entering an electrical substation, causing a major road to collapse, or flooding that is actively trapping individuals.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact point of the burst before professional help arrives.
- You do not need to negotiate repair costs or insurance claims in the first few hours.
- You do not need to replace the entire pipe today; temporary isolation is the priority.

## Important reassurance
Seeing water bubble up from the ground is a high-stress event, but water companies have 24/7 protocols for these specific incidents. They can often tell you very quickly whether the repair is their responsibility or yours. Taking the step to isolate the area and stop using water inside is the most effective way to manage the situation right now.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for outdoor water leaks in a UK residential context. It does not replace professional leak detection services or structural engineering assessments required after the leak is resolved.

## Important note
Water supply responsibilities are governed by the Water Industry Act 1991 (updated 2026). If you receive a formal "Section 75" notice from a water company, you may have a legal deadline (often 14 to 28 days) to complete repairs on a private pipe to avoid being charged for the company's intervention.
