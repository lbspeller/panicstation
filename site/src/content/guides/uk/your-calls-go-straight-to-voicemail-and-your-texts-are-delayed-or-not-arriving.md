# What to do if… your calls go straight to voicemail and your texts are delayed or not arriving (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a calling and SMS connectivity fault: make sure you still have a way to call for help, then do one quick reconnect and check for a local network fault before changing anything drastic.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t factory reset or erase your phone in panic.
- Don’t keep making repeated test calls; save battery and use one clear test after each change.
- Don’t assume it is definitely your handset until you have checked for a local network fault.
- Don’t delete an eSIM or order a replacement SIM unless your provider tells you to or you have a working backup route.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, banking codes, or login codes with anyone “helping” you.
- Don’t buy a new phone, booster, or paid repair before you have checked the simple settings, SIM, coverage, and provider status.

## What to do now
1. **Keep an emergency route available first.** If you need urgent help, try calling **999 or 112**. If your own phone will not connect, use Wi-Fi Calling if it already works on your phone, a landline, or someone else’s phone. If you are already registered for an emergency text or relay service and cannot call or speak, use that route.

2. **Check the settings that can silently send calls to voicemail.**
   - Turn off Do Not Disturb, Focus, Sleep mode, or any similar quiet mode.
   - Check that Call Forwarding or call diversion is off.
   - Check that the important callers are not blocked.
   - If your phone can silence unknown callers or private numbers, turn that off temporarily and test again.

3. **Make the phone reconnect to the mobile network.**
   - Turn Flight Mode or Airplane Mode on, wait about 5 seconds, then turn it off.
   - Fully power the phone off, wait briefly, then turn it back on.
   - Send one test text and ask one trusted person to call you once.

4. **Move once and retest.** Try near a window, outside, or in a different nearby place. If it works there, the problem may be local coverage, indoor signal, or a building issue rather than your phone.

5. **Check whether your provider has a fault.**
   - Use your mobile provider’s service status or outage checker.
   - Check expected coverage for your postcode with Ofcom’s mobile coverage checker.
   - Ask someone nearby on the same network whether their calls or texts are affected.

6. **Check the SIM or eSIM carefully.**
   - If you use a physical SIM, power the phone off, remove the SIM, check it is seated properly, then reinsert it and power on.
   - If you use eSIM, check that the line is switched on in mobile settings, but do not delete the eSIM unless your provider tells you to.
   - If you recently changed SIM, eSIM, phone, or provider, tell your provider this when you contact them.

7. **Try one deeper phone-side fix only after the basics.**
   - Install any pending phone software or carrier settings update.
   - As a last resort, use your phone’s reset network settings option. This may remove saved Wi-Fi passwords, preferred networks, VPN settings, or APN settings.

8. **Contact your network provider with specifics.** Say: “Incoming calls go straight to voicemail and SMS arrives late or not at all.” Give your postcode, times, whether data still works, whether it happens indoors or everywhere, and what checks you already tried. Ask them to check for local faults, account or service blocks, line problems, SIM problems, and whether your line needs refreshing or a replacement SIM.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether to change network, upgrade your phone, buy a booster, or pay for a repair.
- You don’t need to change advanced settings such as APNs unless your provider asks you to.
- You don’t need to keep testing constantly; a short log of times, locations, and what failed is enough for now.
- A formal complaint can usually wait until you have given your provider the fault details, unless the loss of service is creating an urgent safety or accessibility problem.

## Important reassurance
Calls going straight to voicemail with delayed or missing texts can happen because of a temporary network fault, poor indoor signal, a SIM or eSIM issue, an account or line problem, or a quiet-mode or forwarding setting. Those checks are much safer than erasing the phone or replacing it immediately.

## Scope note
These are first steps to restore basic calling and SMS, reduce panic, and collect enough information for your provider. If it keeps happening across different locations or over more than one day, later decisions may need provider support, handset diagnostics, SIM replacement, or specialist repair advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical, or other professional advice. If you cannot reliably call for help and you may be at risk, use another available phone, landline, Wi-Fi Calling, emergency relay or text routes you are registered for, or ask a nearby person to contact emergency services.
