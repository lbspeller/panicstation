# What to do if… you discover a post advertising sexual services using your photos or contact details (UK)

## Short answer
**Save evidence of the post and then report it to the platform and the police immediately** without contacting the person who posted it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t reply to or engage with the person who posted the advert or any accounts associated with it.
- Don’t pay any money or provide any further images to "verified" accounts or blackmailers.
- Don’t click "verification" links or chat bots on the adult hosting site.
- Don’t delete the post or the profile before you have captured screenshots and the URL.
- Don’t share the link on your personal social media to ask for help, as this increases traffic to the post.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your physical space and your communication channels.**
   **Enable "Silence Unknown Callers" in your phone settings and turn on "Do Not Disturb" to block immediate incoming harassment.** If you believe the person has your physical address and you feel in immediate danger, call **999**.

2. **Capture the URL and take clear screenshots of the evidence.**
   **Save the direct link to the profile or advert and take screenshots that clearly show your photos, your contact details, the platform name, and the timestamp.**
   - If the website prevents screenshots, use another device to take a clear photograph of the screen.
   - Do not crop the images; ensure the browser address bar is visible if possible.

3. **Report the content to the hosting platform's abuse department.**
   **Use the "Report," "Abuse," or "Contact Us" links on the specific site to flag the post for impersonation, harassment, and non-consensual sharing of personal data.** Keep any automated confirmation emails or reference numbers you receive.

4. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for specialist removal support.**
   **Call the Revenge Porn Helpline on 0345 6000 459 (Open 10:00–16:00, Mon–Fri) or email help@revengepornhelpline.org.uk for help with takedowns on adult sites.** They can act as a "trusted flagger" to escalate removals that standard reporting tools might miss.

5. **Use Report Harmful Content for guided reporting assistance.**
   **Access the Report Harmful Content tool to identify the correct reporting category and escalate your case if the platform fails to act within 48 hours.**

6. **Submit a formal report to the police via the national cybercrime gateway.**
   **Report the incident online at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040 (Report Fraud service) if you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland.**
   - If you are in Scotland, call **101** to report the matter to Police Scotland.
   - Use the specific phrasing: "I am a victim of online impersonation and harassment where my details are being used to advertise sexual services without my consent."

7. **Use StopNCII.org if intimate or AI-generated images are involved.**
   **Visit StopNCII.org to create a digital "hash" of any intimate images (including AI-generated "nudification") to proactively block them from being uploaded to participating social media platforms.** The image stays on your device; only the digital fingerprint is shared with platforms.

8. **Notify your mobile provider about nuisance contact.**
   **Ask your network provider to block specific harassing numbers or to investigate "malicious communications" coming through your line.** If the volume of calls is unmanageable, you may need to request a number change, but only after police have logged your report.

## What can wait
- You do not need to respond to any of the individuals who have contacted you as a result of the advert.
- You do not need to conduct your own investigation or try to find out who created the post.
- You do not need to decide right now whether you want to proceed with a full criminal prosecution.

## Important reassurance
Being targeted in this way is a form of digital violence designed to cause maximum distress and shame. Feeling overwhelmed, nauseous, or fearful is a normal response to an abnormal situation. Specialist services exist specifically to handle these types of removals, and the responsibility for this harm lies entirely with the person who posted the content.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for reporting and removal. It does not cover long-term safety planning, civil legal action for defamation, or employment law advice.

This guide is for information purposes only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, always call 999.
