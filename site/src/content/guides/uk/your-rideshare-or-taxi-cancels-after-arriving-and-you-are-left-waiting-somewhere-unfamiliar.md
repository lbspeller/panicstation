# What to do if… your rideshare or taxi cancels after arriving and you are left waiting somewhere unfamiliar (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a well-lit, staffed place first. Rebook from there using a licensed taxi at an official rank, or a private hire vehicle booked through a licensed operator or app.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t accept a ride from someone who approaches you offering a quick lift.
- Don’t stay in a quiet or isolated pickup spot just because it is the original pin.
- Don’t move a trip off-app, off-record, or into a cash arrangement with someone who has approached you.
- Don’t share your full name, home address, hotel room number, or where you are staying with strangers trying to help.
- Don’t use up your remaining phone battery arguing about the cancellation while you are still exposed.

## What to do now
1. **Move first, troubleshoot second.** Go to somewhere staffed and well-lit, such as a shop, café, hotel lobby, 24-hour petrol station, station concourse, or busy entrance with cameras and other people nearby.
2. **Tell one trusted person where you are.** Send your live location or the exact name and address of the place you moved to. Ask them to stay with you by phone or text until you are safely moving.
3. **Preserve enough phone battery to get out.** Lower screen brightness, close unused apps, and stop non-essential scrolling or support chats until you have a safe route confirmed.
4. **Take quick screenshots if it is safe to do so.** Capture the cancellation screen, vehicle or driver details if shown, and any messages or call logs. Do this only after you have moved to a safer place.
5. **Rebook from the safer place.** Set the pickup to a clear public landmark, such as a main hotel entrance, shop entrance, staffed station entrance, or official taxi rank. Avoid quiet side streets, car parks, and unlit corners.
6. **Check the vehicle before getting in.** Match the registration, driver name, vehicle description, and app or booking details where provided. If the vehicle or driver does not match, do not get in; step back into the staffed place and rebook.
7. **Use the right help route if you feel unsafe.**
   - If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
   - If you are at a railway station or on the rail network and need British Transport Police in a non-emergency, text 61016 or call 0800 40 50 40.
   - If you need police but it is not an emergency and you are not on the rail network, call 101.
8. **If you are in London, avoid unbooked minicabs.** Only black taxis can be hailed on the street or taken from a rank without a booking. A minicab or private hire vehicle should be booked through a licensed operator or app, not accepted from someone who approaches you.
9. **If you must wait outside briefly, make yourself visible.** Stand near lighting, cameras, staff, or steady foot traffic. Keep one ear free and keep looking around rather than staring only at your phone.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, request a refund, or leave a review.
- You do not need to work out whose fault the cancellation was while you are still outside.
- You do not need to keep the original pickup point if it no longer feels safe.
- You do not need to answer every message from the app or operator before you have a safe way home.

## Important reassurance
A last-second cancellation can make you feel suddenly exposed, especially somewhere unfamiliar or late at night. Your next step does not need to solve everything; it only needs to move you towards people, light, and a recorded licensed journey.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce risk and stabilise the moment. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, reporting, or repeated cancellations may need help from the app, the operator, police, a regulator, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel at risk, prioritise immediate safety and contact emergency services.
