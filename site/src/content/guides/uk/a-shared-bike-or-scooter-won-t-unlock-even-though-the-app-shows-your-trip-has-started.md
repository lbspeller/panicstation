# What to do if… a shared bike or scooter won’t unlock even though the app shows your trip has started (UK)

## Short answer
Do not force the lock. Take proof, try one safe app reset, then use the app’s help or support route to close the trip and ask for any charge to be removed or refunded.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t yank, kick, lift, drag, or pry the bike or scooter.
- Don’t walk away without recording the vehicle ID and the active trip screen.
- Don’t start another trip until you have tried to end, cancel, or report the first one.
- Don’t spend a long time troubleshooting while the trip is still running.
- Don’t block a pavement, dropped kerb, cycle lane, road, doorway, or driveway with the vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Step out of the road and pause next to the vehicle if it is safe to stay there.**  
   Your aim is to stop extra charges and avoid being blamed for damage.

2. **Collect quick proof.**
   - Screenshot the active trip screen showing the time, vehicle name or ID if shown, and any error message.
   - Photograph the vehicle ID, plate, QR code area, or dock number.
   - Photograph the locked wheel, cable lock, dock, or other part that has not released.
   - Note the location, such as the street name, docking station, or a map pin.

3. **Try only one or two safe app checks.**
   - Fully close and reopen the app, then check whether the active trip screen offers a safe retry, lock, unlock, cancel, or end option.
   - If the provider suggests entering the vehicle plate number instead of scanning, use the plate number rather than forcing the lock.
   - If the app says the vehicle has an error or needs maintenance, stop trying that vehicle and report it.

4. **If it is a docked or station bike, put the bike fully back into the dock if you can do this without force.**
   - Wait a few seconds for the dock or app to register.
   - If there is a fault button or fault-report option, use it.
   - If the dock shows a red light or the app still shows an active trip, photograph that before leaving.

5. **Use the provider’s in-app help while you are still near the vehicle.**
   - Look for Help, Chat, Report a problem, Trouble starting ride, or Contact support.
   - Send the screenshots, photos, vehicle ID, location, and a short message: “Trip started in the app, but the vehicle did not unlock.”
   - Ask clearly for the trip to be closed and for any charge, hold, or unlock fee for that failed trip to be removed or refunded.

6. **If the trip will not end, minimise the problem.**
   - Stop repeated unlock attempts.
   - Leave the vehicle upright and out of the way.
   - Report it as not working, would not unlock, or trip started but vehicle stayed locked.
   - Keep the support chat, email, ticket number, or complaint reference.

7. **If money is taken for a ride you could not take, deal with it after you are somewhere calm.**
   - Ask the provider for a refund first and attach your proof.
   - If the provider does not resolve it, ask your card provider about disputing the transaction. Chargeback may be possible, but it is not guaranteed.
   - If you complain to your card provider and remain unhappy after its final response, or if it has not responded within the relevant time limit, you may be able to ask the Financial Ombudsman Service to look at the complaint.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether to make a formal complaint, leave a review, or stop using the provider.
- You don’t need to prove why the unlock failed.
- You don’t need to argue over terms and conditions while standing in the street.
- You don’t need to solve the whole refund process now; just keep the proof and close or report the trip.

## Important reassurance
This can happen because of an app, signal, payment, dock, or lock problem. Clear proof and a prompt report usually put you in a much better position than forcing the vehicle or leaving with nothing recorded.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stop the trip, reduce charges, and protect you in the moment. Later refund, complaint, or card-dispute decisions may need help from the provider, your card provider, Citizens Advice, or the Financial Ombudsman Service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, transport, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are, move to a safer place before dealing with the app or the vehicle.
