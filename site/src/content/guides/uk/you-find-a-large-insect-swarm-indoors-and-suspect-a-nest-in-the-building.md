# What to do if… you find a large insect swarm indoors and suspect a nest in the building (UK)

## Short answer
Move people and pets away from the swarm, close internal doors to contain it, and avoid provoking the insects. If anyone has signs of a serious allergic reaction after a sting, call 999 now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not swat, vacuum, or spray a large swarm; this can provoke stinging insects and may spread them through the building.
- Do not seal or plug a suspected entry or exit point unless a competent pest professional tells you to.
- Do not use outdoor-only pesticides indoors, foggers in an occupied room, or mixed cleaning chemicals.
- Do not climb into lofts, roof spaces, wall voids, or tight spaces to look for the nest.
- Do not let children or pets stay near the swarm or “help” with it.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move calmly to a room away from the swarm, close the door, and keep pets with you. If you can do it without going near the insects, put a towel or draught excluder at the bottom of the door.
2. **Check for an emergency medical situation.** If anyone has swelling of the lips, mouth, throat or tongue, is wheezing, struggling to breathe, faint, very dizzy, confused, drowsy, or has collapsed after a sting, call 999 and say you think it may be anaphylaxis. If they have been prescribed an adrenaline auto-injector and it is available, follow the instructions on the device. Keep them still; they should not stand or walk around.
3. **Reduce movement routes only from safety.** If a fan, extractor, or heating and ventilation control is safely reachable without approaching the insects, switch it off for now. Leave any controls that require you to enter the affected area.
4. **Contain the affected room.** Keep internal doors closed. Only open an external window in that room if you can do it without going near the cluster, nest point, or flight path.
5. **Record what you can from a distance.** If safe, take a zoomed photo or short video. Note whether insects are clustering at a window, light fitting, vent, chimney breast, skirting, ceiling, or a single wall or roof point.
6. **Look for a single entry or exit point without approaching it.** Repeated movement through one crack, vent, chimney, soffit, ceiling gap, or light fitting can suggest a nest or colony in the structure.
7. **Arrange appropriate help.** Contact your local council to ask whether they offer pest-control services or advice. If you suspect wasps, hornets, or you are unsure what the insects are, contact a professional pest controller and say this is an indoor swarm with a possible nest in the structure.
8. **If they may be honey bees, use bee-specific help.** A local beekeeping organisation may be able to advise on swarms. If honey bees are established inside the fabric of the building, specialist removal may be needed rather than ordinary swarm collection.
9. **If you are in a flat or shared building, tell the building manager, freeholder, factor, or managing agent promptly.** A nest in a roof, wall, chimney, or service void may affect more than one home or a communal area.
10. **If you rent, tell your landlord or letting agent promptly in writing.** Ask who is arranging pest-control advice and what you should do meanwhile. If the situation is not being dealt with and you think the home is unsafe, ask your local council for advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact species right now beyond whether stings or allergic reactions are a concern.
- You do not need to remove plaster, lift floorboards, open wall voids, or enter the loft today.
- You do not need to buy pesticides, foggers, or repellents in the moment.
- Full sealing and repairs to vents, soffits, gaps, or brickwork can wait until the nest or colony has been assessed.

## Important reassurance
A sudden indoor swarm is frightening, but the immediate risk is often reduced by separating people from the insects, closing doors, and avoiding actions that make insects defensive. You only need to stabilise the situation and get the right advice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Nest removal, bee colony removal, repairs, tenancy responsibilities, and prevention may need pest-control, building-management, landlord, council, or medical help after the immediate situation is contained.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional pest-control, medical, legal, housing, veterinary, or building-safety advice. If someone may be having a serious allergic reaction, use emergency medical services.
