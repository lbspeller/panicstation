# What to do if… your bicycle lock key or combination fails and you cannot free your bike to get home (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a well-lit area, document proof of ownership (photos / frame number / what3words location), and contact the site manager or an MLA-approved locksmith rather than attempting to cut the lock yourself in public.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to cut or grind the lock with power tools in a public space without first notifying the property owner or police.
- Don’t force a binding key or stiff combination dials, as this often leads to snapped keys or irreversible mechanical seizure.
- Don’t leave the bike unsecured or partially locked while you seek help.
- Don’t block fire exits, public footpaths, or emergency access routes with the immobilised bike.
- Don’t leave personal contact details like your home address on a note attached to the bike.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a well-lit and populated area immediately.** If the bike is in a secluded spot, move yourself to a station concourse, shop front, or building reception before making calls. If you feel physically threatened or unsafe while stranded, call **999**.
2. **Perform a brief, non-destructive check of the mechanism.**
   - **For combination locks:** apply light outward tension on the shackle while slowly rotating the dials to feel for a "click" or change in resistance.
   - **For keyed locks:** ensure the key is fully seated in the cylinder; use a small amount of wiggle motion without applying enough torque to bend the metal.
3. **Capture comprehensive proof of ownership and location.**
   - Take clear photos of the bike, the lock, and any identifying marks or accessories.
   - Locate the **frame serial number** (typically engraved under the bottom bracket where the pedals meet).
   - Use the **what3words** app to record the precise 3-metre square location of the bike.
4. **Identify and contact the property authority.**
   - **At a train station:** Speak to the station manager or travel centre staff. For non-emergency security concerns on rail property, text the **British Transport Police (BTP)** on **61016** or call **0800 40 50 40**.
   - **On public streets:** Contact the local council or dial **101** to inform them you are arranging a locksmith, which prevents the action from being reported as a theft in progress.
   - **On private property:** Notify the security desk, office manager, or campus security to obtain authorisation for lock removal.
5. **Secure a safe method of transport home.**
   - Prioritise public transport, a licensed taxi, or a ride-hailing service over attempting to walk long distances with bike gear.
   - If the environment is cold or poorly lit, leave the bike secured and return during daylight hours with professional assistance.
6. **Hire a vetted professional for removal.**
   - Contact a locksmith through the **Master Locksmiths Association (MLA)** and specify that the task involves a bicycle lock.
   - Prepare your ID and ownership evidence (e.g., BikeRegister log, photos, or digital receipts) to present to the locksmith upon arrival.
7. **Notify staff if the bike must stay overnight.**
   - Inform station or building security that the bike is temporarily trapped to prevent it from being tagged as abandoned or removed by the authorities.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide which replacement lock to buy right now.
- You don’t need to worry about the long-term maintenance of the bike frame or components until the lock is removed.
- You don’t need to engage with or explain the situation to every passerby; focus on the authorised staff or the locksmith.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of panic when you are stranded by a mechanical failure. Taking twenty minutes to document the situation and arrange a legitimate removal is safer than rushing into a DIY solution that could result in injury or police intervention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a lock failure. It does not cover long-term security upgrades, theft recovery, or legal disputes over bike removal on private property.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for proving ownership vary by locksmith and property owner. Always prioritise your physical safety over the security of the bicycle. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
