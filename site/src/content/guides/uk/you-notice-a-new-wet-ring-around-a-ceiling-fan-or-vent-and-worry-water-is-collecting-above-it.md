# What to do if… you notice a new wet ring around a ceiling fan or vent and worry water is collecting above it (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the situation as **water near electrics** by **isolating the power to the affected area at the consumer unit (dry hands only)** and **containing the water** while avoiding the area directly beneath any bulge or sag.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t run the fan or keep switching it on to see if it still works or to "dry it out."
- Don’t touch the fan, the vent grille, the light fitting, or any pull-cord if there is moisture nearby.
- Don’t poke, drill, or cut the ceiling to drain the water, as this increases the risk of sudden collapse or electric shock.
- Don’t stand directly under a bulge or put a ladder on a wet floor surface.
- Don’t assume it is just condensation if the mark is spreading, brown, or near electrical wiring.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the electrical supply safely.**
   - **Locate your consumer unit (fuse box) and switch off the circuit for the affected room.** 
   - **If you are unsure which circuit to flip, use the main switch to turn off all power to the property.**
   - **Do not touch the consumer unit if you have wet hands, are standing on a wet floor, or if the unit itself appears damp.**
2. **Contain the water and protect the room.**
   - **Place a bucket or bowl on the floor directly under the wet ring to catch any drips.**
   - **Lay towels or plastic sheeting around the bucket to prevent water from soaking into flooring or carpets.**
   - **Move any portable electrical items, furniture, or valuables away from the area beneath the leak.**
3. **Stop the water at the source if possible.**
   - **Stop using water in bathrooms, kitchens, or appliances located directly above the leak.**
   - **If the leak is heavy and constant, turn off your main stopcock (usually found under the kitchen sink or where the service pipe enters the property).**
   - **Check if the leak is related to a boiler or central heating system in the loft, especially if the water is warm.**
4. **Evacuate the immediate area if structural danger is visible.**
   - **Keep everyone out of the room if you see significant bulging, sagging, or hear creaking sounds.**
   - **Be aware of "ponding," where water collects on top of the ceiling plaster and can cause a sudden, heavy collapse.**
5. **Contact the relevant emergency service or tradesperson.**
   - **If you are a social housing tenant, report the leak immediately; under 2026 Awaab’s Law rules, landlords must investigate emergency hazards within 24 hours.**
   - **If you are a private tenant, contact your landlord or agent’s emergency 24-hour line.**
   - **If you own the property, call an emergency plumber for internal leaks or a roofer if the leak started during rain.**
   - **If you see sparking, smell burning, or see smoke, leave the property and call 999.**
6. **Document the damage for insurance or repair records.**
   - **Take photos or video of the wet ring, the dripping water, and any damage to the ceiling or floor once it is safe to do so.**
   - **Note the time you first noticed the issue and whether it coincided with using a shower, appliance, or heavy rainfall.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause (plumbing vs. roof) before calling for help; the priority is making the area safe.
- You do not need to clean or paint the ceiling until the leak is fully repaired and the area has dried naturally.
- You do not need to decide on permanent repairs or insurance claims in the first few minutes.

## Important reassurance
Ceiling leaks around fans or vents are common and often caused by simple issues like blocked condensation traps or minor pipe leaks. By isolating the power and containing the water now, you are significantly reducing the risk of fire or serious structural damage.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance intended to reduce the immediate risk of electric shock, fire, or ceiling collapse. A qualified professional must inspect the electrics and the source of the leak before power is restored.

## Important note
This guide is for general information only and is not a substitute for professional electrical or structural advice. If you cannot safely reach your consumer unit or if the ceiling appears unstable, keep clear and seek urgent professional assistance.
