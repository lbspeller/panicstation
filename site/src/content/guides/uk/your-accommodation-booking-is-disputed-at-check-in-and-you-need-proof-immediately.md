# What to do if… your accommodation booking is disputed at check-in and you need proof immediately (UK)

## Short answer
Get a simple proof pack on your phone: booking confirmation, payment evidence, and ID. Then ask the property to re-check the booking and contact the booking platform or provider while you are still at reception.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t hand over your phone or let staff scroll through your accounts; show screenshots or PDFs yourself.
- Don’t delete emails, app notifications, texts, bank alerts, or chat messages.
- Don’t pay again in a rush without getting a receipt and a note saying what the extra payment is for.
- Don’t rely on a verbal explanation such as “system issue” without asking what they can check now.
- Don’t leave without asking for something written that says check-in was refused and why.
- Don’t send card details or passport scans through unofficial messages just because someone at the desk or online asks for them.

## What to do now
1. **Step aside and make a proof pack.** Open or download:
   - the booking confirmation in the app or email,
   - the payment line in your banking or card app,
   - your ID or passport.

2. **Screenshot the key details.** Capture:
   - guest name,
   - property name,
   - stay dates,
   - booking reference,
   - payment status,
   - amount paid,
   - merchant or platform name,
   - date and time of payment.

3. **Make the proof usable offline.** Save the confirmation as a PDF if you can. Turn on airplane mode for a moment and check that the screenshots or PDFs still open.

4. **Ask reception to search using specific details.** Give your full name exactly as booked, the arrival date, length of stay, booking reference, and any separate platform or property reference. Ask them to check cancelled, no-show, duplicate, and name-mismatch notes.

5. **Ask for the duty manager and one clear answer.** Say: “Can you check me in tonight based on this proof?” If the answer is no, ask them to email you or write down the reason check-in is being refused, the date and time, and the name or role of the person making the decision.

6. **Contact the booking platform or provider while you are still there.** Use the in-app help route or the contact details in your confirmation email. Say: “I am at reception now. The property says my booking is disputed. Please confirm the booking status to the property and email me the status.”

7. **If your phone will not load, get the proof first.** Ask for guest Wi-Fi, use a companion’s hotspot, or step somewhere nearby with signal only long enough to download the confirmation and payment screenshots. Then return with the proof ready.

8. **If you are asked to pay again for tonight, protect the payment record.** Use a credit card rather than a debit card if you have a suitable one. Get an itemised receipt showing the dates, room type, total paid, and cancellation terms. Ask for the receipt or email to say: “Payment taken to secure a room tonight while the original booking dispute is being resolved.”

9. **If they still will not check you in, switch to safe accommodation.** Book somewhere you can actually check into, and confirm by phone, app chat, or email before you leave if possible. Keep all receipts, messages, and the written refusal from the first property.

10. **Put the money-back evidence in one place.** Keep the proof pack, refusal note, receipts, and platform messages together. If you paid by card, you can later ask your card provider about chargeback. If you paid by credit card and the single item or set of items cost more than £100 and up to £30,000, you may be able to ask about Section 75, but third-party or intermediary bookings can affect whether it applies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight who is legally responsible.
- You do not need to write a long complaint at reception.
- You do not need to start a formal card claim immediately.
- You do not need to choose your final escalation route now.
- You only need somewhere safe to stay, written facts, and proof that you tried to resolve it at the time.

## Important reassurance
A disputed booking at check-in is stressful, but it does not have to be solved all at once. Clear screenshots, written reasons, receipts, and time-stamped messages give you a stronger position once you are safely housed.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation at check-in and preserve evidence. Later decisions may need help from the booking platform, accommodation provider, card provider, travel company, ombudsman, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Policies, payment protections, and outcomes can vary by property, platform, booking route, and payment method. If you feel unsafe at any point, leave for a safe public place and contact local help.
