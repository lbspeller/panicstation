# What to do if… your accommodation booking is disputed at check-in and you need proof immediately (UK)

## Short answer
Gather three pieces of evidence (confirmation email, bank transaction, and ID) to present as a "proof pack," then demand the property provide a written reason for refusal while you contact the booking platform immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don't hand your unlocked phone to staff or allow them to scroll through your personal apps or emails.
- Don't delete any "declined" or "cancelled" notifications, as these timestamps are critical evidence for later claims.
- Don't pay for a new room without a written statement clarifying that the second payment is for a new contract and the first is still in dispute.
- Don't leave the premises until you have the name and job title of the person refusing your check-in.
- Don't assume a "system error" is your responsibility to fix; it is the provider's duty under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 to provide the service with reasonable care and skill.

## What to do now
1. **Collate your immediate evidence into an offline-accessible format.** **Step away from the main queue to a quiet area of the lobby and open your booking confirmation, your banking app showing the transaction, and your ID.**
   - Take screenshots of the booking reference, guest names, dates, and total price.
   - Screenshot the specific line in your bank statement showing the merchant name and date.
   - Save these as PDFs or move them to a dedicated "Hotel Dispute" folder on your phone so they are ready to show without needing Wi-Fi.
2. **Present the "proof pack" to the front desk and request a specific search.** **Calmly ask the staff to search the system using the arrival date, the platform's reference number, and the property's own internal reference if available.**
   - Ask: "Can you check the 'no-show' or 'cancelled' lists for today’s date under my name?"
   - Ask if there are any "linked bookings" or notes from third-party providers like Expedia or Booking.com.
3. **Request a meeting with the duty manager and a formal decision.** **State clearly that you have proof of a valid contract and ask for a room or a written refusal.**
   - If they cannot find the booking, ask: "Will you provide a room based on this proof of payment, or are you officially refusing check-in?"
   - If they refuse, ask for a "Refusal of Service" note or a short email stating the reason (e.g., "no record of booking" or "property overbooked").
4. **Contact the booking platform or travel agent while remaining in the building.** **Use the "urgent help" or "at property" button in the booking app, or call the customer service number on your confirmation.**
   - Tell the agent: "I am at the property now and they are refusing check-in. I need you to speak to the manager here immediately to confirm my reservation."
   - Do not hang up until the platform agent has attempted to speak with the hotel staff directly.
5. **Check if the provider is an ABTA member.** **Look for the ABTA logo at the desk or on your confirmation, as members must follow a strict Code of Conduct regarding overbooking and "walking" guests.**
   - If they are a member, remind them of their obligation to find you comparable alternative accommodation and cover transport costs.
6. **Secure a VAT invoice if you are forced to pay for a new room.** **If the only way to stay is to pay again, use a credit card and ensure the receipt is a full VAT invoice.**
   - Ask them to add a note to the receipt: "Payment taken under protest due to original booking [Reference Number] being disputed."
7. **Initiate a "safe plan B" if check-in is definitively refused.** **Search for nearby accommodation and confirm availability by phone before leaving the current property.**
   - Keep receipts for all additional costs, including taxis to a new hotel and any price difference in the room rate.
8. **Contact your bank to discuss a "Chargeback" for the original payment.** **If you paid by debit or credit card and did not receive the service, a Chargeback is often the fastest way to reverse the transaction.**
   - You typically have 120 days from the date of the problem to start this process.
9. **Prepare for a Section 75 claim if the booking cost was more than £100.** **If you booked directly with the hotel using a credit card and the cost was between £100.01 and £30,000, your card provider is jointly liable for the breach of contract.**
   - Note: This protection often fails if you booked through a third-party agent (intermediary), as the direct "link" between the creditor and supplier is broken.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine who made the technical error (the app or the hotel) right now.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the CMA (Competition and Markets Authority) tonight; this can be done once you are safe.
- You do not need to calculate your "loss of enjoyment" or "compensation for distress" until you have returned home.

## Important reassurance
Booking mismatches are common administrative errors and do not mean you have lost your money. UK consumer law and the 2026 CMA pricing transparency rules are designed to protect you in these specific "denied service" scenarios. By collecting written evidence now, you are making it significantly easier for your bank or a trade body to refund you later.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a check-in dispute in the UK only. It focuses on immediate evidence gathering and harm prevention. It does not cover long-term legal litigation or travel insurance claim processing.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Your rights may vary depending on whether the booking was a "package holiday" or an independent travel arrangement. If you feel threatened or are asked to leave the premises in an unsafe manner, prioritize your physical safety and contact the local authorities if necessary.
