# What to do if… you feel targeted in a busy area because someone keeps shadowing your movements between shops or aisles (UK)

## Short answer
Enter a staffed area immediately—such as a checkout, security desk, or customer service counter—and inform staff that you are being followed. If you feel you are in immediate danger or cannot reach help, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not lead the person to your car, home, or a quiet side street to see if they follow.
- Do not confront the person directly or accuse them of following you.
- Do not leave the safety of a crowded building until you have an escort or a safe transport plan.
- Do not spend time trying to film or photograph them if it delays your movement to a staffed area.
- Do not ignore your instincts or stay in an isolated area out of embarrassment.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed "anchor" point immediately.** Walk directly to the nearest checkout, customer service desk, pharmacy counter, or security station.
   - Look for staff wearing uniforms or lanyards.
   - Stay in the line of sight of CCTV cameras and other shoppers.
2. **Use a direct script to recruit assistance.** Tell the staff member clearly: 
   "I believe I am being followed between shops and aisles. Please keep me here and call centre security or the police."
   - If you are with a child or a vulnerable person, state this immediately to prioritize your collective safety.
3. **Request specific security measures.** 
   - Ask staff to contact the on-site security team or a manager to meet you at your current location.
   - Ask to wait in a non-public area, such as behind a counter or in a staff room, until security arrives.
   - Request that staff note the current time and location to assist security in reviewing CCTV footage.
4. **Initiate an emergency call if the threat is immediate.** 
   - **Call 999** if the person attempts to touch you, blocks your exit, makes threats, or if you feel a crime is about to occur.
   - **Follow the Silent Solution protocol** if you cannot speak: dial 999 and, when prompted by the automated system, press 55 to confirm your call is a genuine emergency.
   - Provide your specific location including the store name, floor, and nearest department or entrance.
5. **Establish a safe exit plan.** Do not attempt to leave the building alone.
   - Ask security to escort you directly to your vehicle, a taxi rank, or a bus stop.
   - Ensure your ride or transport is waiting and visible before you exit the staffed area.
   - Avoid using quiet side exits; always use the main, well-lit entrance.
6. **Alert a trusted contact quietly.** Send a message with your exact location and the situation.
   - Keep your phone in your hand with the screen unlocked but do not let it distract you from your surroundings.
7. **Identify observable cues of shadowing.** Note if the person:
   - Mirrors your turns (e.g., you turn around and they immediately do the same).
   - Lingers at the same displays without looking at items.
   - Enters and exits multiple stores in the same sequence as you.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether the behavior meets the legal definition of stalking or harassment.
- Writing a detailed witness statement or description of the individual.
- Reviewing your previous movements to see where the shadowing started.
- Making a formal complaint to the shopping centre management or head office.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your intuition in a busy environment is a vital safety skill. If someone’s behavior feels targeted or repetitive, it is appropriate to treat it as a risk. Retail and security staff are trained to handle these requests and will not judge you for seeking a safe environment.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active situation in a public retail environment. It does not cover long-term investigation or legal proceedings regarding harassment.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving an active threat of violence or illegal contact, contact emergency services by dialing 999.
