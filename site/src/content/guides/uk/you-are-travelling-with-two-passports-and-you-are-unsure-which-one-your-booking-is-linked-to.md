# What to do if… you are travelling with two passports and you are unsure which one your booking is linked to (UK)

## Short answer
**Update the Advance Passenger Information (API) in your airline's "Manage Booking" portal to match the specific passport you will present at the departure gate.** If you are a British dual citizen entering the UK, you must ensure the record shows your British passport, as other passports are no longer sufficient for boarding as of February 2026.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume a booking is permanently "linked" to the passport used at the time of payment; airlines usually collect travel document data separately.
- Don’t attempt to use a non-UK passport to enter the UK if you are a British citizen, unless it contains a valid Certificate of Entitlement.
- Don’t try to apply for a UK Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) if you are a British citizen; you are ineligible and the application will be rejected.
- Don’t wait until you reach the airport to "see if it works" if your digital records are mismatched.
- Don’t input placeholder data or "near-match" numbers into airline forms to bypass a system error.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the passenger name on your booking confirmation exactly against the passport you intend to use.**  
   Airlines require a 100% match between the ticket name and the ID presented. If there is a discrepancy (e.g., middle names included on one but not the other), contact the airline immediately to verify if a name correction is required before you proceed with API updates.

2. **Access the "Manage Booking" section on the airline’s website or app to find the Advance Passenger Information (API) fields.**  
   Look for headings such as "Passenger Information," "Travel Documents," or "API/APIS." This is the specific data the airline transmits to border authorities to secure your "permission to travel" notification.

3. **Input the details of the passport you will carry and present for boarding.**  
   Enter the passport number, expiry date, and issuing country for the document you will use for the UK-bound leg. If you are a British citizen, this must be your British passport or a foreign passport containing a Certificate of Entitlement to the Right of Abode.

4. **Verify your status if you naturalised as a British citizen after having EU Settlement Scheme (EUSS) status.**  
   Log in to your UKVI account and ensure your current British passport is linked to your digital profile. This ensures the airline's automated check against Home Office records returns a "clear to travel" status.

5. **Contact the airline’s support line if the digital form refuses to update or save your changes.**  
   Ask the agent to "refresh the Travel Document / API record" for your booking reference. Clearly state: "I need to ensure my record matches my [Nationality] passport number [Number] to avoid a digital boarding block."

6. **Arrive at the airport earlier than usual and head to a staffed check-in desk even if you have a mobile boarding pass.**  
   Present both passports to the agent and ask them to confirm that your "Permission to Travel" (the digital handshake with the Home Office) is showing as "OK" or "Confirmed." This is safer than finding an error at the departure gate where systems are harder to override.

7. **Keep both physical passports on your person throughout the journey.**  
   Do not pack one in checked luggage. You may need to show the passport linked to the booking to the airline, and the passport required for entry to the border official.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update your passport details in your frequent flyer profile or general airline account today; only the specific booking matters for immediate travel.
- You do not need to apply for a new passport if yours is currently valid for the duration of your trip.
- You do not need to resolve questions about your long-term tax residency or dual-taxation status during this travel stabilization.

## Important reassurance
It is very common for dual nationals to have mismatched records, and airline systems are designed to allow API updates up until check-in closes. The most important factor is that the physical document you hold at the gate matches the digital data the airline has sent to the UK Home Office.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document mismatches only. It does not cover visa requirements for third countries, residency permit renewals, or legal disputes regarding citizenship status.

## Important note
As of 25 February 2026, the UK enforces a "no permission, no travel" policy. Carriers are legally required to deny boarding to passengers whose digital permission (ETA, Visa, or British/Irish citizenship) cannot be verified. This guide provides general procedural steps and is not a guarantee of boarding or entry.
