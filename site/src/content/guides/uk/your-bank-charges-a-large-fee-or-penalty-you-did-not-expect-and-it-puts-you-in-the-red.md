# What to do if… your bank charges a large fee or penalty you did not expect and it puts you in the red (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank immediately to request a fee reversal and a temporary pause on interest while the charge is reviewed. If the fee resulted from a Direct Debit error, request an immediate refund under the Direct Debit Guarantee.

## Do not do these things
- Avoid making further non-essential transactions which may trigger additional interest or refused payment fees.
- Avoid repeatedly retrying a failed payment, as each attempt may incur a separate "refused payment" charge.
- Avoid taking out high-cost emergency credit, such as payday loans, before speaking to your bank's hardship team.
- Avoid closing the account while disputes are pending, as this complicates the refund process and may lead to missed payments elsewhere.
- Avoid ignoring upcoming priority bills like rent or council tax; contact the providers to explain the temporary bank issue.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilise the account balance immediately**
   - **Transfer funds from a savings or secondary account** if available to bring the balance above £0 or within your arranged limit.
   - **Check for an "overdrawn" alert or "payment failed" SMS** to identify the exact timestamp of the charge.

2. **Verify the specific nature of the charge**
   - **Log in to your banking app** and locate the specific line item (e.g., "Returned Item Fee," "Account Monthly Fee," or "Overdraft Interest").
   - **Capture screenshots** of the specific charge, the transaction that triggered it, and your current balance for evidence.

3. **Invoke the Direct Debit Guarantee if applicable**
   - **Inform the bank** if a Direct Debit was taken on the wrong date or for the incorrect amount.
   - **Request a "full and immediate refund"** (indemnity claim) under the Direct Debit Guarantee rules.
      - Banks must refund the money immediately while they investigate the error.

4. **Request a "Hardship Review" or fee waiver**
   - **Call the bank or use secure in-app messaging** to speak with the customer service or collections team.
   - **Request three specific actions:**
      - A reversal of the specific fee if it was unfair or caused by an error.
      - A "hold" on further interest or charges for at least 14 days while you resolve the balance.
      - Reversal of any "downstream" fees (e.g., a fee from a different company because the bank charge caused your bill to bounce).

5. **Protect essential upcoming payments**
   - **Identify any bills due in the next 48 hours** that might fail due to the negative balance.
   - **Contact those billers (e.g., energy provider, landlord)** to request a brief payment delay to prevent them from attempting to take funds while the account is in the red.

6. **Submit a formal complaint to trigger the 15-day timeline**
   - **Submit your complaint in writing** (email or secure message) to ensure a clear audit trail.
   - **Note the specific response deadline:**
      - For payment service complaints (Direct Debits, transfers), the bank must provide a final response within 15 business days.
      - For most other banking complaints, the limit is 8 weeks.

7. **Escalate to the Financial Ombudsman Service**
   - **Wait for the final response letter** or for the 15-day/8-week deadline to pass.
   - **Refer the case to the Ombudsman** within 6 months of the final response if the bank refuses to refund an unfair charge.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to switch your current account to a new bank today.
- You do not need to provide a multi-year financial history or a full debt management plan to get an initial fee reviewed.
- You do not need to pay off the entire negative balance instantly if the bank agrees to a temporary interest-free period or hardship hold.

## Important reassurance
Seeing a negative balance due to fees can be alarming, but UK banks have a regulatory duty to support customers in financial difficulty. Banks frequently reverse first-time fees or charges caused by technical errors when asked. Taking the step to report the issue immediately often stops the cycle of compounding interest.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step guidance only for stabilising a bank account after unexpected charges. It does not cover long-term debt restructuring or legal claims for historic bank charges.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not constitute financial or legal advice. If you are consistently unable to meet essential costs like rent or food due to bank charges, contact a free debt advice service such as StepChange or National Debtline for specialist support.
