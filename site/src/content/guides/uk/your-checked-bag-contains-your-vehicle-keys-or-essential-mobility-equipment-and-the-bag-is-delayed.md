# What to do if… your checked bag contains your vehicle keys or essential mobility equipment and the bag is delayed (UK)

## Short answer
Report the delay at the airline baggage desk before leaving the airport to obtain a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and formally record that the bag contains essential mobility aids or vehicle keys.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the arrivals hall without a printed or digital Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and a case reference number.
- Don’t assume the airport "Lost Property" office handles checked bags; this is the responsibility of the airline or their ground handling agent.
- Don’t hand over your original physical baggage tag stubs; provide copies or take high-resolution photos of them first.
- Don’t wait until you get home to mention the contents; the "urgent" status must be flagged while you are at the airport.
- Don’t purchase high-value replacements for mobility equipment without confirming the airline's liability limits (usually around £1,300 unless declared higher).

## What to do now
1. **Locate the specific baggage handling desk for your airline in the arrivals hall.**
   - If the desk is unmanned, look for a self-service reporting kiosk or a posted QR code for immediate digital reporting.
2. **Request a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) and ensure it is completed fully.**
   - Ensure your UK mobile number and current delivery address (home or hotel) are entered correctly.
   - **Obtain the 10-character file reference number (e.g., LHRLH12345).**
3. **Verbally and in writing, state that the bag contains "essential mobility equipment" or "sole vehicle keys."**
   - Ask the agent to add a "Priority" or "Urgent Medical/Access" comment to the electronic file.
   - **Verify that this comment appears on your printed receipt or digital confirmation.**
4. **Request a temporary replacement if your mobility aid is missing.**
   - Under UK accessibility regulations, the airport/airline should assist in providing a temporary alternative or arranging onward transport if you cannot move without the missing equipment.
5. **Ask for an immediate "interim expenses" payment or a "night kit" if you are away from home.**
   - Some airlines provide pre-paid cards or cash vouchers at the desk for essential toiletries and clothing.
6. **If you are stranded because your vehicle keys are in the bag, request onward transport.**
   - **Ask the airline to authorize and pay for a taxi or transfer to your destination.**
   - Keep the receipt for any transport you are forced to pay for yourself, specifically noting it was due to "denied access to vehicle keys."
7. **Contact your car insurance or breakdown provider if the keys cannot be found within 24 hours.**
   - They may provide a hire car or locksmith services depending on your UK policy coverage for "lost keys."
8. **Photograph every document before you leave the desk.**
   - Capture the PIR, the baggage tag stubs, and any written instructions given by staff.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal compensation claim for "lost" items today; a bag is only legally "lost" after 21 days of delay.
- You do not need to contact the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) or an Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) provider until the airline has had a chance to resolve the file.
- You do not need a full inventory of every item in the bag right now; focusing on the keys and mobility aids is the priority.

## Important reassurance
Baggage systems at UK airports use sophisticated tracking; most "delayed" bags are simply on the next available flight or were not loaded due to short connection times. Flagging the contents as "essential mobility equipment" moves your file into a higher priority tier for manual handling and courier delivery.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for UK arrivals. It focuses on the immediate transition from the airport to a safe destination when essential keys or mobility aids are missing.

## Important note
This guide provides general procedural information under UK aviation standards and does not constitute legal advice. Airline liability for baggage is governed by the Montreal Convention, which caps compensation regardless of the item's actual value unless a "Special Declaration of Interest" was made at check-in. Always prioritize your physical safety and immediate mobility needs over administrative speed.
