# What to do if… your employer announces layoffs and you are included without warning (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, ask for everything in writing, and do not sign or agree to anything in the meeting. Your first goal is to confirm whether you are only “at risk” or already being given notice of dismissal, and to slow things down enough to protect your pay and options.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign “to make it easier” or to leave with dignity; that may affect redundancy pay, notice pay, and possible claims.
- Do not sign a settlement agreement, resignation letter, or voluntary redundancy form in the meeting if you have not had time to read it calmly.
- Do not hand over your only access to your own employment records until you have saved what you are allowed to save.
- Do not email accusations, arguments, or emotional messages from your work account; keep communications brief and factual.
- Do not take or copy confidential company, client, or colleague data “just in case”.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for a written summary today.** Request an email or letter confirming:
   - whether you are at risk of redundancy or already being given notice of redundancy or dismissal
   - your proposed or confirmed end date
   - your notice period, or whether you will receive payment in lieu of notice or be put on garden leave
   - what is included in your package, including redundancy pay, notice pay, holiday pay, and any relevant bonus or commission position

2. **Clarify the redundancy process.** Ask in writing for:
   - the pool you were selected from
   - the selection criteria and whether you can see your scoring
   - the timetable for individual consultation meetings
   - whether the employer is proposing 20 or more redundancies at one establishment within 90 days, because collective consultation rules may apply
   - whether there are suitable alternative roles you should be considered for instead of redundancy

3. **If you were told to stop work immediately, ask what that means.** Ask: “Is this garden leave, paid leave, or payment in lieu of notice?” Get the answer in writing, including whether you are expected to be available, whether your benefits continue, and how holiday is treated up to the end date.

4. **Save your own employment records lawfully while you still can.** From your HR or pay portal, save only documents you are allowed to access and keep, such as:
   - your employment contract and any contract changes
   - recent payslips, your latest P60 if available, and your holiday balance
   - redundancy letters, meeting invites, and written reasons or criteria given to you
   Keep these in personal storage and organise them by date.

5. **Write a short factual timeline while it is fresh.** In a personal note, not on company systems, record the date and time of the announcement, who attended, the wording used, whether they said “at risk” or “dismissed”, and any deadlines you were given.

6. **Treat deadlines as real even if you are not sure you will act.** If you think the selection is unfair, discriminatory, or rushed, contact your union if you have one, Acas, Citizens Advice, or an employment adviser early. Most employment tribunal claims have a time limit of 3 months minus 1 day, some claims have different limits, and Acas early conciliation can affect time limits.

7. **Ask for the admin you will need after access ends.** Request:
   - the HR contact you can use after work access ends
   - when you will receive your final payslip and P45
   - who will handle reference requests and what reference will be provided

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to challenge the redundancy, accept an enhanced offer, or negotiate wording.
- You do not need to update your CV, contact recruiters, or explain this to everyone now.
- You do not need to make immediate decisions about pensions, long-term budgeting, or your next career move in the first hours.

## Important reassurance
Being blindsided by layoffs can trigger shaking, numbness, racing thoughts, or feeling unreal. That does not mean you are failing. The protective move is to slow the moment down, get clear written information, and avoid rushed decisions you cannot easily undo.

## Scope note
This is first-steps guidance for the first hours and days after a surprise redundancy or layoff announcement. Later decisions about challenging the process, negotiating, signing documents, or bringing a claim may need specialist employment advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Employment rights can depend on your employment status, contract terms, length of service, and what your employer is proposing. If you are unsure whether you were told you are “at risk” or dismissed, treat it as unresolved and ask for written confirmation.
