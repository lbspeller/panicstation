# What to do if… you receive a utilities disconnection notice for non-payment (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the notice as an urgent priority: contact your utility provider today via a verified official phone number to request a stay of disconnection while an affordability-based repayment plan is negotiated.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use payment links, QR codes, or phone numbers provided solely in the notice if you have any doubt about its authenticity.
- Don’t wait until the day of disconnection to make contact, as your provider's ability to halt automated legal or physical processes decreases as the deadline approaches.
- Don’t agree to a repayment figure that exceeds your actual surplus income just to end the stress of the call.
- Don’t use hazardous alternatives for heating or cooking if supply is lost, such as indoor barbecues, camping stoves, or generators.
- Don’t assume your supply is safe to ignore if you have children or health issues; you must still notify the provider to trigger protections.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific utility type and the account holder's name.** **Determine if the notice concerns electricity, gas, water, or telecoms and confirm if the account is in your name or a landlord's.** Note the account number and the exact "date of disconnection" stated on the letter.
2. **Verify the notice is genuine using a known contact method.** **Call the provider using the number found on their official website or a previous undisputed bill.** Ask specifically: "Is this account currently in the disconnection cycle, and what is the minimum action required to pause it today?"
3. **Request a repayment plan based on a standard financial statement.** **Tell the supplier you want to pay what you can afford and ask them to follow the 'Ability to Pay' principles.** Ask for this arrangement to be confirmed in writing via email or post before the call ends.
4. **Disclose any vulnerabilities in the household immediately.** **Inform the provider if anyone in the home is over 66, has a disability, a long-term illness, is pregnant, or if there are children under the age of 5.**
   - Ask to be placed on the Priority Services Register (PSR).
   - Ask if the supplier has signed the "Vulnerability Commitment," which prohibits disconnecting households with children under 6 at any time.
5. **Check for winter protection if the date is between October 1 and March 31.** **Remind the supplier that they are generally prohibited from disconnecting vulnerable customers on the PSR during these months.**
6. **Address prepayment meter risks if you are facing "self-disconnection."** **Tell the provider if you cannot afford to top up your meter or if the meter is in an inaccessible location.** Ask for "emergency credit" or "friendly-hours credit" to maintain supply while a longer-term solution is found.
7. **Handle water arrears based on your specific UK nation.**
   - **England and Wales:** Household water companies cannot legally disconnect your domestic supply for non-payment; ask for a "Social Tariff" or "WaterSure" scheme if you have high essential usage.
   - **Scotland:** Water charges are usually collected with Council Tax; contact your local council and Advice Direct Scotland immediately if you receive a summary warrant or disconnection threat related to these combined arrears.
   - **Northern Ireland:** Domestic water is not currently billed separately; if you receive a "water disconnection notice," treat it as a potential scam or a business-account error and verify with NI Water via their official site.
8. **Dispute any inaccuracies in the billing amount formally.** **If you believe the debt is based on an estimated reading or a previous tenant's usage, tell the provider you are "disputing the charges."** Provide a photo of your current meter reading as evidence.
9. **Escalate to the relevant watchdog if the provider refuses to pause disconnection.** **Request a "deadlock letter" or a formal complaint reference number.**
   - Contact the Citizens Advice Consumer Service (England/Wales) or Advice Direct Scotland.
   - If the issue remains unresolved after 8 weeks, contact Ombudsman Services: Energy.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on switching your energy or broadband supplier today.
- You do not need to settle the entire debt in a single payment right now.
- You do not need to have a professional debt advisor on the line for the initial "pause" call; you can advocate for yourself in the immediate moment.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a disconnection notice is a high-stress event, but it is a procedural step that can often be reversed by making contact. Providers are legally and regulatorily required to work with customers in genuine financial distress. Feeling overwhelmed or anxious is a normal reaction to a threat to your essential services.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It is intended to prevent the immediate loss of utility supply. It does not replace professional debt counseling or long-term financial planning.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and reflects UK utility regulations as of early 2026. Specific protections depend on your supplier, your location (England, Scotland, Wales, or NI), and your individual household circumstances.
