# What to do if… you see someone peering into your windows or lurking outside your home (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from the window, get behind a locked door or into a room where you cannot be seen, and call 999 if the person is still there, you feel unsafe, or you think they may try to get in.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside to confront them or check who they are.
- Do not open the door or window to speak to them.
- Do not chase them if they move away.
- Do not stand where they can clearly see you.
- Do not switch on lights right beside the window if that makes you easier to see.
- Do not post live updates about where you are or what is happening.
- Do not spend time reviewing camera footage while the person may still be nearby.

## What to do now
1. **Move out of sight.** Step away from windows and exterior doors. If you can, move to an internal room or another room where the person cannot easily see you.
2. **Secure the easiest entry points.** Lock outside doors, close and lock accessible windows, and close curtains or blinds. If you can switch on an outside light from inside, do that.
3. **Call the police using the right route.**
   - Call **999** if the person is still there, you feel in danger, a crime may be happening now, or you think they may try to get in.
   - Call **101**, or use your local police force’s online reporting if available, if they have gone and there is no immediate danger.
4. **If you call 999 but cannot speak safely, still make the call.**
   - On a mobile, listen to the operator. Cough, tap, or press keys if you can. If prompted, press **55** or tap when prompted so the call is treated as genuine and transferred to police.
   - On a landline, stay on the line if you can. If the operator can hear background noise, the call may be transferred to police.
5. **Give the operator the details that help fastest.** Say your exact address, where the person is, which window or side of the building they are near, what they are doing, and whether they are trying doors or handles.
6. **Note details only from a safe place.** If you can do it without going near the glass or drawing attention, note the time, clothing, height or build, vehicle details, and direction of travel.
7. **Bring people inside together.** Quietly bring children, pets, or vulnerable people into the safer room. Keep your phone with you, and only pick up keys or essential medication if they are nearby.
8. **If you are in a flat or managed building, alert building support after calling police.** Contact concierge, security, or building management so they can check shared entrances and preserve any CCTV if it exists.
9. **If you have a hearing or speech impairment, use your accessible emergency route if needed.** In an emergency you can use Relay UK textphone **18000**. If your mobile is already registered for emergencySMS, you can text **999**. For non-emergency police contact, textphone **18001 101** is listed by Police.uk.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to install cameras, extra locks, alarms, or motion lighting.
- Contacting your landlord, insurer, or neighbours unless there is urgent damage or an immediate safety reason.
- Reviewing doorbell or CCTV footage in detail.
- Writing a full timeline.
- Deciding whether this was targeted, random, or connected to something else.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shaken, frozen, or angry is a normal response. You do not have to prove the person’s intent before taking basic safety steps and asking for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the immediate situation and reduce risk. If this happens again, feels targeted, or involves someone you know, later decisions may need specialist safety, housing, legal, or police support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call **999**.
