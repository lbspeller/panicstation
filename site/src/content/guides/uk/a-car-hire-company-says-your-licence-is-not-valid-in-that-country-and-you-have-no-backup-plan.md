# What to do if… a car hire company says your licence is not valid in that country and you have no backup plan (UK)

## Short answer
Verify if the refusal is due to local law or specific company policy, then move to your accommodation via public transport or taxi to re-evaluate your transport options in a safe environment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive a vehicle from a different provider or an individual if the primary hire company has warned you that your licence is legally invalid in that jurisdiction.
- Do not attempt to purchase "instant" or "digital" International Driving Permits (IDPs) from third-party websites; legal UK IDPs are physical documents only.
- Do not leave your original passport or driving licence as a deposit or collateral with the rental desk.
- Do not engage in prolonged arguments with counter staff; once a formal refusal is entered into the system, it is rarely overturned at that branch.
- Do not cancel non-refundable travel bookings (hotels or tours) until you have confirmed that no alternative transport exists.

## What to do now
1. **Request the specific reason for the refusal in writing.** Ask the agent if the rejection is based on:
   - Local national law requiring an International Driving Permit (IDP).
   - The specific "Convention" year of an IDP (1926, 1949, or 1968).
   - Internal company policy regarding the age or type of licence (e.g., paper vs photocard).
2. **Generate a live driving record check code immediately.**
   - **For Great Britain licences:** Access the "View or share your driving licence information" service on GOV.UK.
   - **For Northern Ireland licences:** Access the "nidirect" portal (requires a pre-verified account).
   - Ensure the code is fresh, as they expire after 21 days.
3. **Request a supervisor review for "Policy vs Law" distinctions.** Ask if the company can accept a UK photocard without an IDP if you provide a printed translation or the DVLA check code, even if their standard system flags it.
4. **Isolate the financial impact.** Ask the desk to "Status" the booking as a "Provider Refusal" rather than a "No Show" to assist with later refund claims or insurance disputes.
5. **Establish immediate mobility away from the desk.** Do not remain at the airport or rental hub trying to solve the long-term transport issue:
   - Identify the official taxi rank or bus/train terminal.
   - Proceed to your pre-booked accommodation.
   - Inform any waiting parties that your arrival time has changed.
6. **Verify the IDP requirement via official UK government sources.** Use the GOV.UK "Driving abroad" tool to check if an IDP is a legal requirement for your specific destination and stay duration.
7. **Accept that a UK IDP cannot be obtained while already abroad.** UK IDPs must be obtained in person from a participating PayPoint store (no longer available at Post Offices) and require you to be physically present in the UK.
8. **Document the scene for insurance or credit card chargebacks.** Take a photograph of any signage at the desk or the refusal message on the computer screen if permitted. Save all receipts for the alternative transport used today.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new itinerary for the rest of your trip right now.
- You do not need to initiate a formal complaint or legal "Section 75" claim against your credit card provider while at the counter.
- You do not need to determine who is "at fault" for the document oversight immediately.

## Important reassurance
Document mismatches at rental counters are frequent and often result from shifting regulations or conservative company policies. Moving to a safe location now does not mean your trip is cancelled; it simply means the mode of transport has changed for the next 24 hours.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a rental refusal. It does not cover long-term legal appeals, specific local traffic laws for over 140 countries, or the rules for non-UK licence holders.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Driving requirements are subject to change by national governments. Since April 2024, IDPs are only available via PayPoint outlets in the UK. Always refer to the official GOV.UK "Driving abroad" pages for the most current requirements.
