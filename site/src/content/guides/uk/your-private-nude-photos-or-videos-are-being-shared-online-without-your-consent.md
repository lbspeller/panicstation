# What to do if… your private nude photos or videos are being shared online without your consent (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a private, safer pause, save the link details without spreading the content, then start takedown reporting through the platform and specialist UK support. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay, negotiate, or send more images to someone posting or threatening to post; it may not stop re-sharing and can increase pressure on you.
- Do not reply publicly in the thread or argue with accounts sharing it; that can draw more attention to the post.
- Do not send, forward, or upload the images or videos to other people “for evidence”.
- Do not keep refreshing comments or searching for every repost right now.
- Do not delete everything in a panic before saving the basic link and account details you need for reports.
- Do not blame yourself for this. The person sharing or threatening to share the content is responsible.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and keep your phone usable.**  
   Move somewhere private if you can. Silence the app or thread that is overwhelming you, but keep your phone able to receive calls. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

2. **Save minimal proof without spreading the content.**  
   Write down or save the URLs, account usernames, platform names, and approximate times. If you are sure everyone shown is 18 or over, you can take a page-level screenshot that shows the account name and URL. If anyone shown is under 18, or you are not sure, do not screenshot, download, forward, or upload the image or video.

3. **Report the post inside the platform or app.**  
   Use the in-app report flow and choose the closest option to intimate images shared without consent, non-consensual intimate imagery, sexual content shared without consent, harassment, blackmail, or abuse. Deepfake, edited, or AI-generated sexual images can usually be reported through the same closest intimate-image or sexual-image route.

4. **Ask for supported takedown help if you are 18 or over.**  
   Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline on 0345 6000 459, Monday to Friday, 10am to 4pm, excluding bank holidays, or email help@revengepornhelpline.org.uk. They support adult victims of intimate image abuse in the UK and can help with reporting and removal requests. Report Harmful Content can also help adults find reporting routes for intimate image abuse.

5. **Use hash-matching if you are 18 or over and it feels safe to do so.**  
   StopNCII.org can create a digital fingerprint, called a hash, from an image or video on your device. The image or video stays on your device, and participating platforms can use the hash to look for matches that breach their intimate image abuse policies. It cannot remove images from the whole internet.

6. **If you are under 18, or anyone in the image might be under 18, use child-specific help.**  
   Do not screenshot, download, forward, or upload the content to anyone. Use Childline Report Remove for help reporting sexual images or videos of yourself for removal. If you find sexual images or videos of someone under 18 online, you can report them to the Internet Watch Foundation. If there is coercion, blackmail, grooming, or immediate concern about a child’s safety, you can report to CEOP or call 999 if there is immediate danger.

7. **If there are threats, stalking, doxxing, blackmail, or you feel unsafe, you can contact police.**  
   Call 999 if it is urgent or you feel in immediate danger. If it is not an emergency, you can call 101 or use your local police force’s online reporting route. You do not need to work out the exact offence before asking for help; you can say it is intimate image abuse and mention any threats or demands.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to confront the person responsible, post a public statement, or tell everyone you know.
- You do not need perfect evidence before starting removal; URLs, usernames, platform names, and times are enough to begin.
- You do not need to keep monitoring reactions. Focus on the main post, the fastest-spreading reposts, and specialist takedown help.
- You do not need to make legal, school, work, or relationship decisions while you are in the first shock of finding it.

## Important reassurance
Shock responses are common in this situation: shaking, nausea, racing thoughts, numbness, or feeling unreal. None of that means you are overreacting. The responsibility sits with the person who shared or threatened to share the images, not with you, and there are UK services that deal with this kind of takedown work.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the moment and start takedown actions. Later decisions, including legal advice, longer-term safety planning, workplace or school support, can come after the immediate spread is being addressed and you feel steadier.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
