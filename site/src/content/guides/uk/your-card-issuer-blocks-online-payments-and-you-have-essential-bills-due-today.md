# What to do if… your card issuer blocks online payments and you have essential bills due today (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your card issuer using the number on your card or in your banking app, and ask what is blocked and the fastest safe way to restore payments. While that is being sorted, contact each essential biller today to ask for another payment method or a short hold on late action.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep retrying the same online card payment over and over; repeated failed attempts can make the situation harder to sort out or create confusion.
- Don’t use phone numbers or links from unexpected texts, emails, adverts, or calls about a “security block”.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes or approve an in-app prompt unless you started that exact payment or contact yourself.
- Don’t cancel Direct Debits or standing orders in a panic; check whether they are still due to leave your account.
- Don’t send a bank transfer to new payee details received unexpectedly without checking the details through a trusted route.
- Don’t take a credit-card cash advance in a panic unless you understand the cost and have no safer payment route.
- Don’t ignore the biller if the payment has failed; an early call or message can reduce avoidable late action.

## What to do now
1. **List only the essential bills due first.**  
   Write down the biller, due date, amount needed today, account or reference number, and the safest contact route from a statement, biller account, or official website.

2. **Check whether this is a card-control setting you can fix safely.**  
   In your banking app or online banking, look for card controls such as “freeze card”, “online payments”, “contactless”, or a prompt asking you to confirm a transaction. If you clearly find a setting you changed yourself, change it back and try the bill payment once.

3. **Call your card issuer from a trusted route.**  
   Use the number on the back of the card or inside the banking app. Say: “My online card payments are being blocked for security checks and essential bills are due today.” Ask them to confirm:
   - whether the block is on online card payments, the whole card, or a specific transaction;
   - whether they can lift it safely during the call;
   - whether in-store chip and PIN, contactless, or phone payments still work;
   - what to do today if the card needs replacing.

4. **Use a different safe payment route for the urgent bills.**  
   Ask the biller what they can accept for your account today, such as bank transfer from your current account, Direct Debit already in progress, a payment through your logged-in account, payment by phone, or an approved in-person payment route. If bank details are involved, check the sort code, account number, and payment reference using a trusted biller account, a previous statement, or a known customer service number.

5. **Contact each essential biller before the deadline passes.**  
   Tell them the payment failed because your card issuer has placed a security block on online payments. Ask them to note the account, confirm the minimum needed today, and say whether any late fee, service restriction, or enforcement action can be paused while you arrange payment. Keep any chat transcript, email, or reference number.

6. **Keep simple proof of what happened.**  
   Save the decline message, the time and date, the issuer reference number, and the name or reference from any biller you contacted. This can help if you later ask for a fee to be reviewed.

7. **If the security-block message may be a scam, stop and secure the account.**  
   Do not reply, click links, call numbers in the message, or approve prompts. Contact your issuer through the card or app number, explain what happened, and follow their instructions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change banks.
- You do not need to replace every payment method today unless your issuer tells you the card or account is compromised.
- You do not need to make a formal complaint today unless the urgent payment issue cannot be sorted and you need to record the problem.
- You do not need a perfect budget today; the immediate task is to keep essential bills from escalating.

## Important reassurance
A blocked online payment does not always mean you have lost money or done anything wrong. The useful next move is to use trusted contact routes, find out exactly what still works, and tell billers before missed-payment action starts.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate payment problem. Later decisions, such as complaints, fee reviews, debt advice, or changing payment arrangements, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not financial, legal, debt, fraud, or professional advice. If you suspect fraud or see a transaction you do not recognise, contact your issuer immediately using trusted contact details and follow their guidance.
