# What to do if… you miss a connection because of immigration or document checks and you are now stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Go immediately to your airline’s transfer or customer service desk to request rebooking and duty of care assistance before leaving the airport.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t exit through the "Arrivals" doors to the landside area until you have confirmed your baggage status and rebooking options with the airline.
- Don’t purchase a new ticket independently if you are traveling on a single booking reference, as this may void your remaining itinerary.
- Don’t discard boarding passes, bag tags, or documentation regarding the delay, as these are required for any future insurance or airline claims.
- Don’t assume you are ineligible for help; while cash compensation is unlikely for border delays, the airline still has a statutory "duty of care" for through-tickets.
- Don’t wait in a single queue without also attempting to contact the airline via their app, website chat, or phone line simultaneously.

## What to do now
1. **Identify your current location relative to the border and confirm your status with officials.**  
   - If you are still being held for checks, follow all instructions and ask clearly: "Am I permitted to go to my airline's service desk now?"  
   - If you are not permitted to leave, request a single phone call to notify your airline and, if necessary, your national consulate.
2. **Contact your airline through multiple channels immediately to start the rebooking process.**  
   - Locate the airline's connections or customer service desk within the terminal.  
   - While walking, open the airline app or call their UK service number to say: "I have missed my connection due to border delays; I need to be rerouted to my final destination."
3. **Verify if your travel is on a single booking reference or separate tickets.**  
   - **Single Booking (Through-ticket):** The airline is responsible for rerouting you to your final destination at the earliest opportunity.  
   - **Separate Tickets:** You are likely responsible for the cost of a new flight, but you should ask the onward airline for a "rescue fare" or "flat tire" rate which is often cheaper than a standard ticket.
4. **Request mandatory care and assistance if your new flight is delayed by several hours.**  
   - Ask for vouchers for food and drink once the delay exceeds two hours (for short flights) or three to four hours (for longer flights).  
   - Request a means of communication, such as access to Wi-Fi or a phone, which the airline must provide under UK261 regulations.
5. **Confirm the location and status of your checked baggage.**  
   - Ask the airline staff: "Has my bag been offloaded, or is it proceeding to the original destination?"  
   - If your bags are airside but you are landside, go to the "Baggage Enquiries" desk near the carousels to request a manual retrieval.
6. **Ask for overnight accommodation and transport if the next available flight is the following day.**  
   - State clearly: "I require hotel accommodation and transport to and from the airport as per my UK261 rights."  
   - If the airline is unable to book the hotel for you, ask for their "reasonable expense limit" in writing, book a nearby hotel yourself, and retain all itemised receipts.
7. **Ensure you have the required Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) for your new departure time.**  
   - Check if your stay in the UK now requires an ETA (mandatory for most non-visa nationals in 2026) if you are forced to clear customs and enter the country for a hotel stay.
8. **Secure a written record of the refusal if the airline denies assistance.**  
   - Ask for the name of the staff member and a written statement or email explaining why rerouting or care is being denied.
9. **Notify a trusted contact of your current location and revised schedule.**  
   - Send your new flight number, airline, and estimated arrival time to one person.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or claim for expenses right now.
- You do not need to decide on long-term travel changes or cancellations until the airline provides a confirmed new itinerary.
- You do not need to provide a detailed timeline of the border check to the airline; a simple statement of the delay is sufficient for the initial rebooking.

## Important reassurance
Missing a flight due to border queues or document verification is a frequent occurrence at major UK hubs. While the wait is stressful, the airport is a staffed, secure environment. Your immediate priority is simply securing the next available seat and a place to rest.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the first 1–4 hours following a missed connection. It does not cover the legal process for claiming compensation or long-term immigration appeals.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance under UK passenger rights (UK261). Airline obligations vary significantly between "through-tickets" and "self-transfer" (separate) bookings. Border Force delays are generally classified as "extraordinary circumstances," meaning cash compensation is typically not payable, though the right to food and accommodation still applies for single-itinerary passengers.
