# What to do if… you realise your travel profile saved the wrong passport number and you already checked in (UK)

## Short answer
Stop trying to update your saved user profile and contact the airline to manually overwrite the **Advance Passenger Information (API) on this specific booking** using the passport you are carrying.

## Do not do these things
- Don't ignore the error—in 2026, UK airlines perform automated digital checks against Home Office records (ETA/eVisa) and an mismatch will trigger a boarding block.
- Don't cancel or rebook the flight—this is usually a record correction, not a name change or ticket cancellation issue.
- Don't assume updating your "Frequent Flyer" or "Saved Profile" automatically updates a boarding pass that has already been issued.
- Don't try to "test" your boarding pass at the security gate to see if it works; a failed scan here can complicate your status with airport security.
- Don't wait until the boarding gate to speak to staff—gate agents have less time and fewer system permissions than desk staff.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the error on the specific flight reservation.**  
   **Open the airline app or website and navigate to "Manage Booking" for this specific trip.** Check the section labelled "Travel Documents," "API," or "Passenger Information" to see if the number matches the passport in your hand.

2. **Attempt an online edit if the system allows it.**  
   **Some airlines permit API updates even after check-in has been completed.** If you can edit the fields, enter the correct number, save the changes, and check if a new boarding pass is generated with a "Document Verified" or "API OK" status.

3. **Contact the airline via Live Chat or Social Media immediately.**  
   **Ask the agent to "reset the API status" or "manually update the passport number on the PNR (reservation record)."** Digital support channels are often faster than phone lines for simple data corrections and can provide a written confirmation that the change has been made.

4. **Update your UKVI account if you are travelling on an eVisa.**  
   **If your UK immigration status is digital, ensure the passport you are using is linked to your UKVI account.** Use the official "Update your UK Visas and Immigration account details" service; if the airline's record does not match your UKVI record, the automated check may fail.

5. **Verify your destination's entry permission (ESTA / ETA / ETIAS).**  
   **Check if your entry permit is tied to the incorrect passport number.** If your permit (like a US ESTA or EU ETIAS) is linked to a different number, you must apply for a new permit or correct the existing one, as these are usually non-transferable between passport numbers.

6. **Arrive at the airport three hours before departure.**  
   **Go directly to the airline’s staffed customer service or bag drop desk, even if you do not have bags to check.** Present your physical passport and ask the agent to scan it to ensure the Advance Passenger Information (API) is synchronised with the Home Office and destination authorities.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix your permanent travel profile or frequent flyer account today; focus only on the current flight record.
- You do not need to worry about "Name Change" fees; a passport number typo is a data correction, not a change of passenger.
- You do not need to contact the Home Office if your passport itself is valid; the airline is the primary gatekeeper for the data mismatch.

## Important reassurance
Passport number errors and typos are extremely common. Airline systems are designed to allow staff to swipe or scan your physical document at the airport to overwrite incorrect digital entries. While 2026 digital border checks are stricter, they rely on the airline sending the correct data, which can be fixed manually by an agent.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document mismatches on issued boarding passes. It does not cover situations where the passenger's name is spelled incorrectly or where the passport itself has expired.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not guarantee boarding. Entry requirements and airline policies for Advance Passenger Information (API) vary by destination and carrier. Always follow the direct instructions of airline staff and official government travel advice.
