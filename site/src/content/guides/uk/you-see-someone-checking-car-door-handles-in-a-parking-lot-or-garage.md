# What to do if… you see someone checking car door handles in a parking lot or garage (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer position and do not confront them. Call 999 if a crime appears to be happening now or someone may be in immediate danger; otherwise report it through 101 or online and alert site security or staff.

## Do not do these things
- Do not approach, challenge, follow, or try to block them in with your body or vehicle.
- Do not shout across the car park or start a confrontation to scare them off; it can escalate.
- Do not film or photograph from close range, or in a way that draws attention to you.
- Do not touch car doors, handles, or unusual items if police or security may attend.
- Do not put yourself at risk to investigate whether it is definitely a crime.
- Do not post identifiable footage or photos on social media in the moment.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance and cover.** Move calmly to a safer spot, such as inside a building, near staffed areas, or beside other people. If you are in your car, lock the doors, start the engine, and reposition or leave if you can do so safely.
2. **Check whether this is happening now.** If the person is trying multiple cars, attempting entry, acting aggressively, or you feel unsafe, call 999 from a safe place.
3. **Use the right reporting route.**
   - Call 999 if a crime appears to be in progress or someone may be in immediate danger.
   - Otherwise, call 101 or report online to the relevant police force.
   - If you want to pass information anonymously and it is not urgent, you can use Crimestoppers.
4. **Give clear details without guessing.** Share the exact location, such as the car park name, level, bay numbers, nearest entrance, or nearby shop signs. Say what you saw, such as “trying door handles”, “moving from car to car”, or “looking into windows”.
5. **Note safe identifying details.** From a safe distance, note clothing, build, height, direction of travel, and any vehicle involved, including make, colour, or part of a number plate if you can see it without moving closer.
6. **Alert people who can act on site.** Tell car park security, store staff, or the site manager so they can follow their procedures, such as checking CCTV, warning customers, or contacting police.
7. **If you think your own car is being targeted, do not go to it alone.** Stay in a staffed or public area, report what is happening, and ask security or police what to do before returning to the vehicle.
8. **Once you have reported it, stop tracking the person.** If they leave, note the time and last direction from a safe place, pass that on, and then focus on leaving or continuing your day safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was definitely attempted theft or just suspicious.
- You do not need to confront the person, collect more evidence, or check every nearby car.
- You can leave follow-up, statements, CCTV questions, or insurance questions until you are safe and calmer.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to freeze or second-guess yourself in moments like this. Creating distance and reporting what you saw is a sensible, safety-first response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for staying safe and reporting what you saw. Later decisions, such as formal statements, CCTV, or support for an affected owner, may need help from police, site staff, insurers, or another relevant specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe or believe a crime is happening now, prioritise your safety and contact emergency services.
