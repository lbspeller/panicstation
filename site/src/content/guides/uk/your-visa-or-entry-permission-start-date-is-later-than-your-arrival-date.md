# What to do if… your visa or entry permission start date is later than your arrival date (UK)

## Short answer
Do not try to arrive in the UK before your visa or immigration permission is valid. Check the exact valid-from date, then change your travel to arrive on or after that date, or use the official correction route if the date is wrong.

## Do not do these things
- Do not travel anyway to “try your luck” at check-in or the border; you may be refused boarding or refused entry.
- Do not compare only your departure date; overnight flights can mean your UK arrival date is earlier than your permission allows.
- Do not rely on a screenshot or informal explanation if your carrier cannot verify your permission.
- Do not cancel everything in a panic if the immediate fix is simply moving your arrival to the valid-from date or later.
- If you may already have entered the UK before your permission began, do not assume it will be fine; treat it as a possible immigration status issue and get qualified advice promptly.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact valid-from date on your immigration permission.**  
   Check your entry clearance vignette in your passport, or sign in to your UKVI account to view your eVisa. Make sure you are reading the immigration permission date, not a course start date, job start date, booking date, or email date.

2. **Check your UK arrival date, not just your departure date.**  
   Look at the scheduled landing date in UK local time. If your flight lands before the valid-from date, including just before midnight, treat that as arriving too early.

3. **If you have not travelled yet, change your journey before going to the airport.**  
   Rebook so that you land in the UK on or after the valid-from date. This is usually lower risk than asking the airline or Border Force to overlook a date problem.

4. **If the permission date looks wrong, use the right correction route now.**
   - If your eVisa details are wrong, use the official GOV.UK service to report an error with your eVisa.
   - If your passport vignette or sticker details are wrong, contact UKVI for help and contact the visa application centre where you made the application.
   - If you are sponsored by a university or employer, tell the sponsor today so they can check any timing or compliance issue.

5. **Put the key evidence in one place.**  
   Save your passport photo page, vignette page if you have one, UKVI account sign-in details, any share code if you have one, your decision email or letter, and your itinerary showing the planned UK arrival date.

6. **If you are already in the UK and think you entered before your permission began, pause and document the timeline.**  
   Keep your boarding pass, booking confirmation, passport stamp if there is one, travel emails, and screenshots of your permission dates. Then speak to a registered immigration adviser, immigration solicitor, or your sponsor’s compliance team before starting work, study, renting, or relying on any permission conditions that may be affected.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to cancel the whole trip long-term; focus on the UK arrival date and the permission valid-from date.
- You do not need to argue at the airport; fixing the date issue before travel usually reduces the risk.
- You do not need to make irreversible life changes, such as ending a lease, shipping belongings, or quitting a job, until your entry timeline is clear.

## Important reassurance
This is a common panic point, especially with overnight flights and visa date windows. Slowing down, checking the exact arrival date, and keeping clear records gives you the safest next move.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If you may already have entered the UK before your permission began, or if the date error affects work, study, sponsorship, or future applications, later decisions may need specialist immigration help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, or professional advice. UK immigration processes are fact-specific and digital systems can change, so use official routes and get qualified help before taking an irreversible step.
