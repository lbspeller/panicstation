# What to do if… an online contact pushes sexual conversation quickly and pressures you to move platforms (UK)

## Short answer
Stop responding immediately and do not move to a different app or platform. Take minimal screenshots of the pressure or threats, then block the user and report the account using the platform's internal reporting tools.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t move to an encrypted app (like WhatsApp, Telegram, or Snapchat) to "explain" your boundaries or to be polite.
- Don’t send any sexual images, videos, or perform actions on camera to appease the person or prove your identity.
- Don’t share identifying details such as your full name, workplace, school, home location, or phone number.
- Don’t negotiate with or pay anyone threatening to leak images; payment almost always leads to increased demands.
- Don’t delete the conversation history before you have taken screenshots of the username and the specific pressure or threats.
- Don’t argue with the person; once a boundary is ignored, further conversation usually increases the risk of manipulation.

## What to do now
1. **Disengage from the conversation immediately.** Stop replying to all messages and mute the notification settings for that specific chat to reduce immediate stress.
2. **Capture essential evidence of the behavior.** Take screenshots that clearly show the user's profile handle / ID, the platform name, and the messages where they pressured you or asked to move platforms.
   - Note the date and time of the interaction.
   - Do not spend time scrolling through or re-reading the messages.
3. **Use the platform’s block and report functions.** Select the options for "harassment," "sexual solicitation," or "blackmail / extortion" as provided by the app.
   - Reporting helps the platform identify and ban predatory accounts before they reach others.
4. **Tighten your privacy settings across all social media.** Set your profiles to "private," hide your friend lists, and disable the ability for people to find you via your email address or phone number.
5. **Report financially motivated threats to the authorities.** If the person is demanding money or gift cards under threat of sharing images (sextortion), report this to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) online or by calling **0300 123 2040**.
   - If you are in Scotland, report fraud or cybercrime directly to Police Scotland via **101**.
6. **Access technical tools to prevent image sharing.** If intimate images of you exist and you are concerned about them being shared, use professional hashing services.
   - **For adults (18+):** Use **StopNCII.org** to create "digital fingerprints" of your images that participating platforms can use to block uploads.
   - **For those under 18 (or if the images were taken when you were under 18):** Use **Report Remove** (via Childline / IWF) or the NCMEC **Take It Down** tool to stop the spread of the content.
7. **Contact the police if you feel unsafe.** If the person has personal details that make you fear for your physical safety, or if the harassment is persistent, call **101** or report via your local police force’s website. Call **999** in an immediate emergency.
8. **Seek specialist support for the emotional impact.** You do not need to decide on a police report to get help.
   - Contact the **National Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line** (England & Wales) on **0808 500 2222** for 24/7 confidential support.
   - If you are an adult (18+) dealing with image-based threats, call the **Revenge Porn Helpline** on **0345 6000 459** (Monday–Friday, 10:00–16:00).

## What can wait
- You don’t need to investigate the person’s identity or figure out if their profile is "real" or "fake."
- You don’t need to provide a full, detailed statement to anyone immediately; the initial report only needs the basic facts.
- You don’t need to tell your entire social circle what happened right now.
- You don’t need to respond to "final warnings" or countdown timers sent by the contact.

## Important reassurance
Manipulation and "love bombing" that turns into sexual pressure are common tactics used by predatory contacts to bypass your normal caution. Feeling overwhelmed, embarrassed, or scared is a physiological response to a high-pressure situation, not a sign that you have done anything wrong. Taking the step to block and disengage is the most effective way to regain control.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for online harassment and pressure. It does not replace the need for professional legal or psychological support if the situation involves ongoing stalking, physical contact, or significant distress.

## Important note
This information is for guidance purposes and does not constitute legal or clinical advice. Platform reporting requirements and police procedures may vary based on specific circumstances. If you believe a child is at risk, contact the police or your local safeguarding hub immediately.
