# What to do if… you suspect someone is watching or signalling to others while you handle cash or valuables (UK)

## Short answer
Stop handling the cash or valuables, secure them, and move to a staffed, well-lit place such as inside a shop, bank branch, station concourse, or near security. If you think a theft or robbery is about to happen or is happening now, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep counting money, showing valuables, or trying to finish the transaction quickly while you feel watched.
- Don’t confront them, follow them, or try to catch them out.
- Don’t move to a quieter spot to get space, such as a side street, empty car park, stairwell, or the back of a queue.
- Don’t take out your phone and start filming if it keeps you in place or distracts you from getting to safety.
- Don’t walk straight to your car with cash visible, or unlock your car while searching through your bag or wallet.
- Don’t hand your wallet, phone, card, or bag to a stranger who approaches with a sudden urgent request.

## What to do now
1. **Cover and secure what you’re handling immediately.** Put cash away, close your wallet, zip your bag, and hold it close to your body. If you’re at an ATM, finish or cancel promptly, then put your card and cash away before you turn.
2. **Move to a safer anchor point as soon as you can.** Aim for somewhere with staff and visibility: inside the nearest shop, bank branch, station concourse, staffed petrol station, help point, or security desk. If you’re already inside a shop, step to the counter and stay with staff.
3. **Create distance without signalling panic.** Take a few calm steps sideways or back to break proximity, keep your hands free, and keep a clear route to staff, a counter, or another visible place.
4. **Make the situation visible to staff.** In a shop or bank, say simply: “I think I’m being watched for my cash or valuables. Can I stay here a moment, and could you call security?” Stay at the counter rather than near the door.
5. **If you’re on rail or station property, use the right channel.** If it is not an emergency but you are worried, you can text British Transport Police on 61016, or call 0800 40 50 40, with your location, the time, and a brief description. If there is immediate danger or a crime in progress, call 999.
6. **Choose police contact based on urgency.**
   - Call 999 if you feel you are about to be robbed, someone is threatening you, you are being blocked from leaving, or a theft or robbery is happening now.
   - Call 101, or report online, if they have left but you want to report suspicious behaviour, especially if you are in a bank, shop, station, or other place that may be able to check or preserve CCTV.
7. **If you think you are being followed after leaving the anchor point, go back inside to staff or enter another busy staffed place.** Do not lead anyone to your home. If you must move, head towards a well-lit main road or staffed venue, not a shortcut.
8. **If you notice anything missing, act quickly but calmly.** Check your pockets and bag while you are in the safer place. If a card or phone is missing, use your banking app, card issuer, or phone provider to freeze or report it lost or stolen as soon as you can do so safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether they really meant it or whether you misread the signal.
- You do not need to confront them, get proof, or take photos right now.
- You do not need to cancel every plan or decide your whole route home immediately.
- You do not need to give a full statement while you are shaken; first get to a safer staffed place and report the key facts when you can.

## Important reassurance
Noticing signalling, watching, hovering, or distraction around cash and valuables is a reasonable red flag. Securing your items and moving towards staff is a calm, low-drama way to reduce risk and buy time.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few minutes. If anything was stolen, you were threatened, or you still feel unsafe, later decisions about banking, reporting, travel, or support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, professional safety, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, prioritise getting to a safer place and contacting emergency services.
