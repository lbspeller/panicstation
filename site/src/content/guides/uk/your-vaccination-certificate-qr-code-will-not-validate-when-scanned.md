# What to do if… your vaccination certificate QR code will not validate when scanned (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the person checking it what alternative official proof they can accept right now, then open a fresh official version of the record or certificate rather than relying on an old screenshot or forwarded file.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hand over your unlocked phone or let it be taken out of your sight.
- Do not download a random “QR fixer” app or click a link from a text or email while stressed.
- Do not edit the QR image, crop it tightly, add filters, or re-save it through a messaging app.
- Do not assume a failed scan proves your vaccination record is wrong; it may be a display, format, verifier, or connection problem.
- Do not spend the first minutes arguing about policy; keep asking, “What official fallback can you accept right now?”

## What to do now
1. **Ask exactly what proof they need.**  
   Say: “Please tell me the exact vaccination proof you can accept right now: the official app or website view, a downloaded official record, a printed official record, or the International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis if this is yellow fever?”

2. **Check whether the request is for an outdated COVID pass or a current vaccine requirement.**  
   If someone is asking for an NHS COVID Pass, ask them to confirm whether they mean your current COVID-19 vaccine record in the NHS App or a separate destination requirement. Do not assume a COVID QR certificate is still the right document.

3. **Make the QR easy to scan.**
   - Open the certificate from the official app, website, downloaded record, printed certificate, or original document.
   - Turn screen brightness up and remove glare.
   - Hold the phone still and avoid zooming so far that the QR becomes blocky.
   - If it is printed, flatten the paper and keep folds away from the QR code.

4. **Open a fresh official version.**
   - If this is an NHS App COVID-19 vaccine record in England, close and reopen the app or log out and back in, then go to Vaccinations and open the COVID-19 vaccine record again.
   - If the app is slow or Wi-Fi is poor, try the NHS App through the NHS website on the same phone.
   - If you have a saved file or email copy, open the newest official copy you have and avoid screenshots if the original file is available.

5. **Check the details against your passport or travel document.**
   Compare the name, date of birth, vaccine dates, and document holder details. If the details do not match, tell staff it may be a record or document mismatch and ask what fallback proof they can accept for this check.

6. **Ask whether the verifier matches your certificate type.**
   Say: “Is this the right verifier for this certificate type, or do you need a different document format?” Some checks require a specific certificate, app view, or paper document rather than any QR-based proof.

7. **Use the issuer route if the record is missing or wrong.**
   - For an NHS COVID-19 vaccination in England, the NHS says to contact the vaccination site first and ask them to update the record.
   - Contact your GP surgery only in the situations NHS guidance lists, such as if the vaccination site has closed, the vaccination was more than 365 days ago, you have more than one NHS number, or you have had or are planning gender reassignment.
   - If the vaccination was private, overseas, or not recorded in the NHS App service, contact the provider or clinic that gave it and ask for replacement proof.

8. **If this is yellow fever, switch to the required paper certificate route.**
   Some countries require yellow fever vaccination proof in an International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis. If QR-based proof is being rejected, ask whether they need that certificate, then contact a registered yellow fever vaccination centre if you need a replacement.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide about rebooking, cancelling, or changing your trip until you have asked what official fallback they can accept.
- You do not need to reset your phone, reinstall apps, or try several accounts while standing at a counter unless you are in a calm and private place.
- You do not need to prove who is right in the moment; focus on finding an accepted official format.

## Important reassurance
A failed QR scan can happen because of the display, the downloaded file, the verifier being used, the certificate format, or the underlying record. Moving to a fresh official version and asking for an accepted fallback is a sensible first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the high-pressure moment. If the problem is an incorrect record, a missing vaccination, or a specific destination document requirement, later decisions may need help from the issuer, vaccination provider, travel clinic, carrier, or destination authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, travel, or professional advice. Entry, carrier, and document requirements can vary by destination and may change, and staff may require a specific document type.
