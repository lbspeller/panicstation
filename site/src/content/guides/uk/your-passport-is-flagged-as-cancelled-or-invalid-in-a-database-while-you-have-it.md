# What to do if… your passport is flagged as cancelled or invalid in a database while you have it (UK)

## Short answer
Do not report the passport lost or stolen just to “reset” it. Get the exact wording of the error, ask for a supervisor check, and contact HM Passport Office to confirm the passport record and the safest next step.

## Do not do these things
- Do not report your passport lost or stolen unless it is actually lost or stolen; once a travel document is reported lost or stolen, it can be treated as cancelled and invalid.
- Do not destroy, cut, post, or surrender the passport unless an official authority tells you to.
- Do not keep retrying check-in without asking what exact status or error the system is showing.
- Do not book expensive replacement travel in panic before you know whether the record can be checked or corrected.
- Do not share photos of your passport online or with unofficial “passport checking” services.
- Do not argue at the counter; ask for escalation and write down what you are told.

## What to do now
1. **Get the exact wording of the problem.**  
   Ask the airline, check-in desk, gate staff, or border officer: “Is the passport showing as cancelled, invalid, lost, stolen, revoked, not found, unreadable, or a details mismatch?” Write down the exact words, time, location, flight number, and the staff role or badge number if they are willing to give it.

2. **Ask for a supervisor re-check before anything else changes.**  
   Say: “Please check the passport number, name, date of birth, nationality, and machine-readable zone again, and please confirm the exact system result.” This can rule out a simple data-entry, scan, or booking-name mismatch.

3. **Check the obvious details while you are still at the desk.**  
   Compare the booking and passport for name order, middle names, date of birth, passport number, expiry date, and nationality. Pay attention to characters that are easy to misread, such as O and 0, I and 1, or B and 8.

4. **Contact HM Passport Office through an official route.**  
   Use the Passport Adviceline, webchat, or online enquiry form. Say clearly: “I physically have my UK passport, but an airline or border system is showing it as cancelled or invalid.” Give the exact wording, where it happened, your travel timing, and whether anyone mentioned a lost or stolen report.

5. **If you are outside the UK and need to travel soon, check whether an emergency travel document is possible before applying.**  
   A UK emergency travel document may be available if you are outside the UK, need to travel urgently, cannot use your UK passport, and cannot renew or replace it from abroad before travel. Check the GOV.UK eligibility page first, because applying may usually cancel the old passport after payment.

6. **If you do apply for an emergency travel document, gather only the basics first.**  
   Have ready a valid digital passport photo, a contact phone number, an email address, payment card details, and your travel dates. You may be asked for more information or to attend an appointment.

7. **If you think someone wrongly reported the passport lost or stolen, say that plainly to HM Passport Office.**  
   Say: “I believe a lost or stolen report may have been made in error or maliciously. Please check whether that is affecting my passport record and tell me what can be done.” Do not accuse anyone at the desk; keep the request focused on the passport record.

8. **If you are at border control, stay calm and ask what the next safe step is.**  
   Ask whether you can contact HM Passport Office while you wait, whether the officer needs any confirmation from HM Passport Office, and where you should wait. Keep your phone charged and keep notes of each instruction.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim compensation, or change future travel plans.
- You do not need to apply for a full new passport immediately unless HM Passport Office says the record cannot be resolved in time.
- You do not need to post publicly, name staff online, or share passport images to get help.
- You do not need to work out who caused the problem before you get the passport status checked.

## Important reassurance
A passport database problem does not automatically mean you have done anything wrong. It may be a scan problem, a details mismatch, an administrative cancellation error, or a mistaken lost or stolen report. The useful thing now is to slow the situation down, get the exact status, and use official passport routes.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about complaints, refunds, replacement passports, missed travel, or suspected malicious reports may need specialist help or formal advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, or professional advice. Airline, border, and passport processes can vary by country, route, carrier, and personal circumstances.
