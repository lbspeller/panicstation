# What to do if… you notice someone filming or recording into your home through a window (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from the window, get everyone into a safer room, and call the police from a safe place if it is happening now or you feel at risk.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to confront them.
- Don’t open the window or door to argue, shout, or get a better look.
- Don’t lean close to the window to film them back.
- Don’t chase them, block their exit, or try to grab their phone or camera.
- Don’t talk yourself out of basic safety steps because you are unsure what offence it may be.
- Don’t post identifiable footage online while it is unfolding; keep it for the police instead.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from the window.** Get out of their line of sight. Close curtains or blinds only if you can do it without going close to the window; otherwise leave them and move to an interior room.
2. **Get other people away from exposed windows.** Bring children, vulnerable adults, visitors, and pets into a room away from the side where the person is standing.
3. **Secure easy entry points.** Lock doors, accessible windows, patio doors, and side gates if you can do this from inside or without going near the person.
4. **Call the police.**
   - If it is happening now, you feel threatened, or you think they may try to enter, call **999**.
   - If they have left and there is no immediate danger, call **101** or report it to your local police force online.
   - If you are in danger but cannot speak safely on a 999 call, stay on the line, listen to the operator, cough or tap if you can, and press **55** if prompted.
5. **Give simple details from a safe position.** Tell police your address, where the person is standing, whether they are on your property or in a shared area, what they are using to film, what they look like, and which way they went if they leave.
6. **Keep any footage you already have.** Do not edit, delete, crop, or post it. Save doorbell or CCTV clips if you can, write down timestamps, and keep original photos or videos on the device that took them.
7. **Use building support without leaving safety.** If you live in a block or managed building, call the concierge, security desk, caretaker, or landlord’s emergency line and say: “Someone is outside filming into homes through windows at [location]. Police are being contacted.”
8. **If it feels repeated or targeted, say that clearly.** Tell police if this has happened before, if you recognise the person, if they have contacted you in other ways, or if it seems linked to harassment or stalking.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide now whether this is voyeurism, harassment, stalking, trespass, antisocial behaviour, or something else.
- You do **not** need to identify the person yourself.
- You do **not** need to gather more evidence before calling police if there is immediate danger or the incident is happening now.
- You can decide later about privacy film, curtains, lighting, cameras, landlord contact, or longer-term support.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel shocked, exposed, or angry when someone appears to be filming into your home. The immediate job is only to create distance, reduce risk, and get help if there is any current danger.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first minutes to hours. If this is repeated, targeted, or linked to harassment or stalking, later decisions may need specialist support, police advice, housing advice, or legal advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you or someone else is in immediate danger, contact emergency services now.
