# What to do if… you cannot receive verification codes abroad and you are locked out of travel accounts you need (UK)

## Short answer
Stop repeated login attempts to prevent a permanent lockout, perform a "Manual Network Selection" on your phone to force a connection to a local roaming partner, and then prioritize recovering your email account before attempting to access specific travel apps.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep requesting new codes (this often triggers a 24-hour security cooldown or flags your account for fraud).
- Do not swap your UK SIM into a local phone if you have a "Trusted Device" lock, as this may break the hardware-based recognition required for recovery.
- Do not share OTP (One-Time Password) codes with anyone claiming to be from your network or airline support.
- Do not use public Wi-Fi without a VPN for account recovery if sensitive data or passwords must be entered.
- Do not pay third-party "account fixers" on social media; these are scams that compromise your data.

## What to do now
1. **Perform a "Manual Roam" to force the SMS delivery through.**
   - Go to your phone's **Settings > Mobile Data / Cellular > Network Selection**.
   - Turn off **Automatic** selection.
   - Wait for the list of local networks to appear (this may take 2 minutes).
   - Select a different local provider from the list.
   - Repeat this with each available provider until you see a signal bar or receive a "Welcome" text.

2. **Check your UK mobile account for bars or spent caps.**
   - Log in to your mobile provider’s official app or website using Wi-Fi.
   - Confirm that **International Roaming** is enabled.
   - Check if a **Spending Cap** or **Roaming Bar** is active; if you have reached a limit, you may be unable to receive any incoming service.
   - Increase the cap temporarily if necessary to allow line activity.

3. **Try to receive the code via Wi-Fi Calling.**
   - Ensure **Wi-Fi Calling** is toggled to **On** in your phone settings.
   - Connect to a stable, private Wi-Fi network.
   - If your UK network supports it, the phone may receive the SMS over Wi-Fi even if the local mobile signal is too weak.

4. **Attempt recovery using "Other Ways to Sign In" for your email.**
   - Select **Try another way** or **I don't have my phone** on the login screen.
   - Look for options such as **Recovery Email**, **Backup Codes** (if you saved them), or **Trusted Device** (if you have a tablet or laptop with you that is already signed in).
   - If using Microsoft or Google, check if they offer a **One-Time Recovery Code** sent to an alternative email address.

5. **Contact the airline or hotel directly for "Reference-Only" travel.**
   - If you cannot access your account to see your boarding pass or reservation, call the provider’s official UK or international helpline.
   - Provide your **Booking Reference (PNR)** and **Passport Name** to the agent.
   - Ask them to **email a PDF copy** of your documents to a temporary or alternative email address that you can access.
   - For hotels, ask them to verify your identity using the **physical payment card** used for the booking upon arrival.

6. **Manage British Passport or document emergencies.**
   - If your passport is lost or stolen, report it immediately at **gov.uk/report-a-lost-or-stolen-passport**.
   - **Dual-nationals** must ensure they have their British passport for re-entry to the UK, as airlines will deny boarding without a valid passport or Certificate of Entitlement under the 2026 ETA rules.
   - If you have an urgent, essential reason to travel (e.g., funeral or medical emergency) and no valid passport, apply for an **Emergency Travel Document** via the official GOV.UK portal.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update your security settings or change your 2FA methods until you are back in the UK or in a stable location.
- You do not need to notify every service provider (banks, social media) unless you believe your phone has been stolen.
- You do not need to buy a new SIM card immediately if the manual roam fix restores SMS delivery.

## Important reassurance
Being locked out due to roaming SMS delays is a common technical hurdle, not usually a sign of a hacked account. Most UK networks can resolve "short-code" delivery issues once you manually select a compatible local roaming partner. Panic-retrying usually makes the lockout longer, so waiting 15 minutes between attempts is often effective.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for accessing accounts and travel documents while abroad. It does not cover long-term device security or detailed legal advice regarding lost identity documents.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is based on UK-specific network protocols and 2026 travel rules. Account recovery processes for private companies (e.g., Google, Microsoft, Airlines) are subject to their own internal security policies which can change without notice.
