# What to do if… an accommodation asks to keep your passport overnight and you feel unsure about it (UK)

## Short answer
Do not leave your passport at the reception desk for later collection. Wait for the staff to record the necessary details or take a photocopy immediately, and then keep the original document in your possession.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave your passport unattended at a reception desk or with staff for "processing later."
- Do not permit your passport to be used as a financial security deposit or "holding" item.
- Do not engage in an escalating argument; if the request is refused, remain in a public area and request a manager.
- Do not hand over the document if the staff refuse to explain which local law requires retention.
- Do not report the document as stolen if it is being withheld during a dispute until you have contacted consular services.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the specific legal requirement for retention.** 
   - Note that many countries (such as Italy and Spain) require hotels to register guest details with the police, but this usually only requires a brief inspection or photocopy.
2. **Offer to wait while the information is recorded manually.**
   - **Inform the staff that you are happy to wait at the desk while they transcribe the passport number and details.**
3. **Propose providing a photocopy instead of the original.**
   - **Present a high-quality photocopy of your passport ID page and ask if this can be kept in place of the original.**
   - If they insist on seeing the original, keep it in your sight while they compare it to the copy.
4. **Request the immediate return of the document if it has already been taken.**
   - **State clearly that you require the document for your own security and will wait for them to finish the registration now.**
5. **Ask for a written receipt if you choose to leave the document (not recommended).**
   - **Ensure the receipt includes the passport number, the name of the staff member, the current time, and a confirmed time for return.**
   - Ask where the document will be stored and confirm it will be in a locked safe.
6. **Contact the 24/7 UK Consular Assistance line if the document is withheld.**
   - **Call +44 20 7008 5000 if staff refuse to return your passport.**
   - You can also use the "Contact a British embassy, high commission or consulate" tool on GOV.UK to find the specific local contact for the country you are in.
7. **Contact local police if you feel unsafe or a crime is being committed.**
   - **Use the local non-emergency number to report that a document is being withheld illegally if the manager refuses to cooperate.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to check out or find a new hotel in the first few minutes of the discussion.
- You do not need to file a formal report with HM Passport Office unless you are certain the document will not be returned or has been compromised.
- You do not need to resolve whether the hotel's policy is "fair" or "correct" while you are still trying to retrieve the document.

## Important reassurance
It is common for travelers to feel pressured during the check-in process, especially after a long journey. Wanting to maintain control of your primary identity document is a standard safety practice and is encouraged by the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO). Most legitimate requests for a passport can be resolved in a few minutes without the document leaving your sight.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passport retention queries. It does not cover the legalities of every specific jurisdiction or the process for applying for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD).

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for guest registration vary significantly by country. Always check the FCDO travel advice for the specific country you are visiting to understand local laws regarding identity documents.
