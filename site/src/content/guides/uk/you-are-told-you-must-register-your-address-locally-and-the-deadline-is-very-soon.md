# What to do if… you are told you must register your address locally and the deadline is very soon (UK)

## Short answer
Treat it as time-sensitive, but slow down enough to verify the requirement and the correct official route. Use official channels, avoid handing documents or money to unofficial intermediaries, and keep proof of what you did or tried to do.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hand over your passport to someone “to take it to an office” unless you are at the official office and understand why it is needed.
- Do not pay cash fines or expediting fees to an individual unless you can confirm the payment is official and you receive an official receipt.
- Do not send passport, visa, or residence-card photos to an unverified number just because the message sounds urgent.
- Do not rely only on a WhatsApp, text, or verbal instruction if it names an office, deadline, fee, or penalty.
- Do not ignore it out of panic. If it is a real local requirement, missing it may cause avoidable problems later.

## What to do now
1. **Write down exactly what you were told.** Record the deadline, the name of the requirement, which authority is said to handle it, who told you, and any office address, fee, document list, or reference number they gave you.
2. **Check whether your accommodation has already filed anything.** Ask the hotel, host, landlord, or booking provider whether they have submitted your guest or address details. Ask for written confirmation of what they filed, when they filed it, and any receipt or reference number.
3. **Verify the route before sending documents or money.** Check the FCDO travel advice page for the country you are in, then check the official local website for the authority named in the rule. Depending on the country, this may be a city, municipal, migration, immigration, or police authority.
4. **If the deadline is within the next few days, prepare to go in person.** Take what you have, including your passport, entry stamp, visa, residence card if you have one, booking confirmation, rental agreement, host letter, and the address written clearly.
5. **Ask what the office needs before you queue or pay.** Use a phone number, email address, front desk, or website listed on an official website or displayed at the official office. Ask which documents are accepted, whether the host or landlord must attend, and whether there is an official fee.
6. **Keep control of your documents.** Let staff inspect or copy documents only in the official setting. If anyone says they must keep your passport, ask why, for how long, and what written receipt you will receive.
7. **Get proof of compliance or attempted compliance.** Ask for a stamped form, registration slip, receipt, appointment confirmation, email reply, or reference number. Photograph it and save a copy somewhere separate from your phone.
8. **If you are being pressured or threatened, move to a safer setting first.** Leave the conversation if you can, go to your accommodation, a public official office, or another safe public place, and use the local emergency number if you are in immediate danger.
9. **If you are a British national and need urgent help abroad, contact consular support.** Contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate, or the FCDO 24/7 consular team, especially if your documents are being withheld, you are being coerced, or you fear detention.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to extend your stay, change visa type, or move accommodation.
- You do not need to work out who was at fault before you try to meet the requirement or document your attempt.
- You do not need to make a complaint until you are safe, calm enough, and have copies of the key messages and receipts.
- You do not need to pay an unofficial person just to make the panic stop.

## Important reassurance
Address registration rules exist in a number of countries and can be routine. A short deadline is stressful, but the safest first step is usually to verify the official route, act through that route, and keep written proof.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, aimed at avoiding irreversible mistakes, document loss, unofficial payments, and deadline problems. Local rules vary by country, city, accommodation type, nationality, and immigration status, so later decisions may need specialist local advice.

## Important note
This is general information only, not legal, immigration, travel, safety, or other professional advice. Use official local sources and FCDO guidance, and seek specialist help if you cannot confirm the requirement or you feel unsafe.
