# What to do if… you are told you must register your address locally and the deadline is very soon (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the requirement on the official GOV.UK foreign travel advice page for your specific country and locate the nearest official municipal or immigration office. Do not hand over your physical passport to third parties to "process" on your behalf.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay cash "fines" or "urgent fees" to individuals or hosts without visiting an official government building.
- Do not leave your original passport with a host, landlord, or employer for more than the time required to take a photocopy in your presence.
- Do not ignore the deadline; local registration is often a legal entry requirement and failure can lead to fines, detention, or future entry bans.
- Do not use unofficial "registration agents" or third-party links sent via SMS or WhatsApp to submit payment or personal data.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the specific local requirement on the official GOV.UK country travel advice page.** Search "GOV.UK foreign travel advice [Country Name]" and check the "Entry requirements" or "Local laws and customs" sections for mentions of "registration" or "reporting to police."
2. **Check if the "Register Your Presence" service is active for your location.** In 2026, the FCDO has specific registration links for British nationals in high-risk or fast-moving situations; check your country's specific page on GOV.UK to see if you should record your location with the UK government.
3. **Confirm with your accommodation provider if they have already submitted your details.** Hotels and registered guesthouses often complete this automatically; ask for a printed "registration slip" or a reference number as proof of compliance.
4. **Locate the nearest official office if the hotel cannot provide proof.** This is usually the "Municipality" (City Hall), "Immigration Department," or "Local Police Station." Verify the address only through official government websites (.gov or local equivalent).
5. **Gather your original passport, entry visa, and proof of address before traveling to the office.** Ensure you have:
   - Your original passport and any paper arrival cards.
   - A printed copy of your hotel booking or a signed letter from your host.
   - Two passport-sized photographs (often required for manual registration).
6. **Request a stamped receipt or a formal registration document at the counter.** Do not leave the office without a physical or digital confirmation that includes an official seal or a verifiable QR code.
7. **Contact the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) if you are being coerced or your documents are withheld.** If you are in immediate danger or being threatened with detention regarding your registration, call +44 (0)20 7008 5000 or the nearest British embassy.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to change your travel itinerary or extend your visa.
- You do not need to settle disputes with your host or landlord regarding whose responsibility the registration was.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the local process until you have received your official registration proof.

## Important reassurance
Registration requirements are standard administrative procedures in many jurisdictions and are usually resolved by a single visit to an official office. Feeling anxious about deadlines or language barriers is a normal reaction to local bureaucracy. Following official channels is the most effective way to secure your legal status.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for meeting immediate local registration deadlines. It does not cover long-term residency applications, work permits, or legal defense for immigration violations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements change frequently; always prioritize the instructions found on the official GOV.UK travel advice pages or provided by the local authorities at an official government office.
