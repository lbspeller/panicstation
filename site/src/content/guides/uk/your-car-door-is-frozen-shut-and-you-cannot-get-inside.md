# What to do if… your car door is frozen shut and you cannot get inside (UK)

## Short answer
Do not yank the handle. Try another door first, then use de-icer on the seal or lock and gently push the frozen door inward before trying to open it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull hard on the handle or door edge; this can damage the handle or rubber seal.
- Do not pour hot or boiling water on the door, lock, or windows.
- Do not use a naked flame on or near the lock, door, handle, or key.
- Do not force the key if it will not turn.
- Do not scrape around the paint, seal, or lock with metal tools.
- Do not keep adding water if it is below freezing and you cannot dry the seal straight away.

## What to do now
1. **Try another entry point first.** Check the passenger door, rear doors, and boot or tailgate. One door may open even if the driver door is frozen.
2. **Unlock, then push before pulling.** If the car is unlocked, place your hand flat near the frozen door frame and push the door slightly more closed a few times. Use gentle, firm pulses to crack the ice along the seal without stressing the handle.
3. **Spray de-icer along the seal line.** Aim at the gap where the rubber seal meets the bodywork, starting at the top edge, then the sides, then the bottom. Wait a minute, push inward again, then try the handle normally.
4. **If the lock is frozen, treat the lock rather than the handle.** Use proper lock de-icer if you have it. Spray into the lock cylinder or apply a small amount to the key, then work the key gently. Stop if the key will not move.
5. **Warm the seal area only if you can do it safely.** At home, a hairdryer on low or medium can be aimed along the frozen seal or lock area. Keep it moving, keep the plug and lead dry, and do not use it in rain, standing water, or with an unsafe extension lead.
6. **Use lukewarm water only as a last resort.** If nothing else is available, use a small amount of lukewarm, not hot, water on the rubber seal only. Keep it away from window glass, the lock, and electrics, then dry the door edge and seal immediately so it does not freeze again.
7. **Once a door opens, stop the ice returning.** Wipe water from the door edge, seal, and latch area. Close the door gently, then open it once to check it is not sticking again.
8. **If this is happening on a motorway or other high-speed road, prioritise road safety.** Put hazard lights on. If you cannot get to a safer place or get well away from moving traffic, stay in the vehicle with your seat belt on and call 999 for the police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose long-term prevention products now.
- You do not need to diagnose the lock, seal, or central locking system now.
- You do not need to keep working on the driver door if another door opens safely.
- You do not need to decide about repairs unless something has broken or this keeps happening.

## Important reassurance
A frozen car door is common in cold weather. Slowing down and switching to de-icer, gentle inward pressure, or another entry point usually avoids the damage caused by frantic pulling.

## Scope note
These are first steps for getting safely into the car and avoiding immediate damage. If the door keeps freezing shut, later help from a mechanic, garage, or body shop may be useful.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, mechanical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. In severe weather, active traffic danger, or cold exposure, get to safety and seek appropriate help.
