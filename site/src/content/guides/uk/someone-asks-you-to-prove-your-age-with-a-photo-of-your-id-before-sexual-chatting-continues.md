# What to do if… someone asks you to prove your age with a photo of your ID before sexual chatting continues (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately stop the conversation and do **not** send a photo of your passport, driving licence, or any identity document. Treat this request as a high-risk indicator of attempted identity theft, coercion, or sextortion.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send a "selfie with ID" even if the person claims they have sent you theirs first.
- Don't attempt to "censor" or black out parts of the ID and send it; a partial ID still provides enough data for sophisticated fraud or blackmail.
- Don't pay any money or "verification fees" to keep the conversation going or to prove you are real.
- Don't explain why you are refusing; providing justifications gives the person more leverage to manipulate your boundaries.
- Don't delete your account immediately if threats have been made; you may need to preserve the chat history for a police report.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all communication immediately.** If the person pressures you or makes you feel guilty for not "trusting" them, they are using a common grooming or manipulation tactic.
2. **Verify the platform's official tools.** **Check if the app has a built-in "Verified" blue tick or age assurance feature.**
   - Under UK law (2025/2026), regulated platforms must use "Highly Effective Age Assurance" (like facial age estimation or secure third-party verification).
   - Legitimate apps will **never** ask you to send a raw photo of your ID directly to another user in a chat window.
3. **Secure your identity documents.** **Keep your passport, driving licence, and utility bills out of sight and do not take photos of them for online use.**
4. **Block and report the user.** **Use the platform’s "Report" function specifically for "Harassment," "Scam," or "Solicitation."**
   - This alerts the platform's safety team to investigate the account without you having to continue the interaction.
5. **Use prevention tools if images were already shared.** **Register with StopNCII.org or Childline’s "Report Remove" tool if you are concerned that intimate images or your ID may be shared without your consent.**
6. **Report threats to the authorities.** **Contact the police on 101 (non-emergency) or 999 (emergency) if you are being blackmailed or threatened with exposure.**
   - If you are under 18, or worried about a child, report the incident directly to **CEOP (Child Exploitation and Online Protection)** via their online Safety Centre.
7. **Protect against identity fraud.** **Contact Action Fraud (or Police Scotland on 101 if in Scotland) if you have already sent a photo of your ID.**
   - Consider notifying your bank and checking your credit file with agencies like Experian or TransUnion for any unauthorized applications.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether you want to pursue a full criminal case.
- You do not need to check if the person's profile was "fake" or "real" before protecting yourself.
- You do not need to apologize for "breaking the mood" or ending the interaction abruptly.
- You do not need to find the "perfect" way to say goodbye; ghosting is a valid safety strategy here.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of urgency, guilt, or fear when someone demands proof of your identity during an intimate moment. These requests are designed to catch you off-guard and bypass your usual safety instincts. Choosing to prioritize your privacy and identity security over a conversation is always the correct action, regardless of the other person's reaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for identity protection and harm reduction in the context of online intimate interactions. It does not replace professional legal advice or formal police investigations into identity theft or sextortion.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is based on UK safety standards as of March 2026. If you believe your physical safety is at risk or you are being targeted by an organized blackmail group, contact your local police force immediately.
