# What to do if… you lose your company ID badge and are worried it could be misused (UK)

## Short answer
Report it to your employer now so security, facilities, or IT can disable the badge and any linked access. Do not keep searching for long before reporting it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t delay reporting while you keep looking.
- Don’t post about the loss publicly or share photos, codes, badge numbers, or site details.
- Don’t borrow, lend, or share someone else’s badge.
- Don’t ask someone to let you through secure doors unless reception or security has told you to do that.
- Don’t assume it is harmless because the badge also needs a PIN; it may still help someone impersonate you or tailgate.
- Don’t confront anyone you think may have it.

## What to do now
1. **Do one quick obvious check, then report it.** Check the last place you used it, your bag, coat pockets, desk area, car, bike bag, and lanyard. If it is not immediately found, stop searching and report it.
2. **Use the fastest internal route.** Contact workplace security, reception, facilities, or the IT service desk, whichever can disable or block the badge fastest. If you do not know the route, call your line manager and ask them to connect you.
3. **Ask for the badge to be disabled now.** Say: “My company ID badge is missing and could be misused. Please deactivate the badge now and arrange a replacement.” Ask for confirmation that the access credential has been blocked.
4. **Tell them every system it may be linked to.** Mention doors, lifts, car parks, lockers, printers, time clocks, canteen payment, visitor systems, or computer login if any of these use the badge.
5. **Give a short factual timeline.** Tell them when and where you last remember having it, when you noticed it was missing, and any places it may have been lost, such as a train, café, car park, reception area, or shared workspace.
6. **Tell them what was attached or visible.** Report any keys, fobs, parking pass, door codes, locker keys, site name, full name, job title, team name, photo, or other identifying details that were with the badge.
7. **Ask security to check for misuse.** Ask them to flag the badge as lost, block it if presented, and check access logs or any relevant CCTV according to their normal process for the period between when you last had it and when it was disabled.
8. **Report it as a possible information-security or data incident if personal details were visible.** If the badge shows your full name, photo, role, company name, site, or was lost with other identifying items, ask the internal security, IT security, or data protection contact to assess it.
9. **Report theft or a credible threat to police.** If the badge was stolen, someone is using it, or you think you were targeted, report it online or via 101 if it is not an emergency. Use 999 or 112 only if there is immediate danger.

## What can wait
- Replacement badge forms, fees, and routine admin can wait until the badge is disabled.
- A full retrace of your whole day can wait until urgent reporting is done.
- A long written explanation can wait; a short factual timeline is enough for now.
- Worrying about blame or discipline can wait; the useful action now is quick reporting and containment.

## Important reassurance
Losing a company badge can feel exposing, but it is a common security incident that workplaces are usually set up to handle. Once the badge is blocked and reception or security is alerted, the practical risk usually drops.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Your workplace may require extra actions depending on the site, your role, and what the badge controls.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, HR, security, data protection, or professional advice. Follow your employer’s security and reporting procedures. If you feel unsafe or believe someone is trying to access your workplace using your identity, prioritise safety and contact workplace security and the police.
