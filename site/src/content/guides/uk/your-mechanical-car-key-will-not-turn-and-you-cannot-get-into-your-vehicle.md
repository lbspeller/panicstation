# What to do if… your mechanical car key will not turn and you cannot get into your vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Move yourself to a safer place and stop forcing the key or lock. Try the simplest low-damage checks once, then contact your breakdown provider or a vetted automotive locksmith for help getting into the vehicle without causing avoidable damage.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force the key by twisting hard, levering, or trying to snap it round.
- Do not use improvised entry tools such as coat hangers, wedges, or slim-jim style tools; they can damage seals, wiring, airbags, or the lock mechanism.
- Do not pour boiling water on the lock, handle, or glass.
- Do not keep retrying in panic. If two calm attempts do not change anything, stop and switch steps.
- Do not leave your phone, bag, wallet, or keys on the roof, bonnet, pavement, or a car-park machine while you troubleshoot.

## What to do now
1. **Make the situation safer first.**  
   Step away from traffic, keep your phone and keys on you, and move to a well-lit public place if you can. If you feel threatened, someone is in immediate danger, or the situation could become violent, call **999**. If you need non-emergency police help, such as reporting suspicious behaviour or a theft, call **101**.

2. **Check whether another normal entry point works.**  
   Try the other doors and the boot or hatch once. On some vehicles, one door lock can be stiff while another still opens.

3. **Check the key before trying again.**  
   Make sure it is the correct vehicle key. Look for bending, cracks, heavy wear, or dirt on the blade. If you have a spare key available, try the spare gently; if the spare works, stop using the worn or bent key.

4. **If it is cold or the lock looks iced, use gentle de-icing only.**  
   Warm the key in your hand for a minute, then insert it fully and try a gentle turn. If you have proper lock de-icer, use it as directed. If the door seal feels frozen, press gently along the door edge with your palm before trying the lock again; do not yank the handle.

5. **Try one careful re-seat attempt.**  
   Insert the key fully, ease it back out by 1–2 mm, apply light turning pressure, and gently wiggle side to side. If it does not improve quickly, stop.

6. **Use lock-specific product only if you already have the right thing.**  
   If you already have a product clearly meant for vehicle or door locks, use a tiny amount and try once. If you are not sure it is meant for locks, skip this step rather than flooding the cylinder.

7. **Call professional help before damage becomes likely.**  
   If you have breakdown cover through a provider, insurer, bank account, or vehicle package, call them and say: “The mechanical door lock will not turn, I cannot get into the vehicle, and I need non-damage entry if possible.” If you do not have cover, contact a vetted automotive locksmith and ask about call-out cost, proof of identity or ownership needed, and non-damage entry.

8. **If a child, vulnerable person, or animal is locked inside, call 999 now.**  
   Tell the call handler who or what is inside the vehicle, where you are, whether there are signs of distress, and whether the vehicle is in sun, heat, traffic, or another hazard.

9. **Use nearby practical support while help is arranged.**  
   If you are in a car park, workplace, supermarket, station, hotel, or residential site, ask staff or security for somewhere safe to wait, help charging your phone, and help giving your exact location to the recovery provider or locksmith.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the problem is the key, lock cylinder, frozen seal, dead fob battery, or central locking right now.
- You do not need to decide about repairs, replacement keys, insurance, or a garage visit while you are still locked out.
- You do not need to fix the lock permanently today; the immediate aim is safe access without making the lock or door damage worse.

## Important reassurance
A stuck vehicle lock is common, especially with cold, dirt, lack of use, or a worn key. Stopping forceful attempts is not giving up; it is the step that helps prevent a small lock problem becoming a broken-key or damaged-door problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get through the immediate lockout safely. Later decisions about keys, locks, repairs, recovery costs, or insurance may need help from a garage, breakdown provider, insurer, or locksmith.

## Important note
This is general practical information, not legal, mechanical, safety, insurance, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, someone is trapped, or you feel unsafe where you are, contact emergency services.
