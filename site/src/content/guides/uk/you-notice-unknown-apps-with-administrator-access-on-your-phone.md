# What to do if… you notice unknown apps with administrator access on your phone (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the device by enabling Airplane Mode, then manually revoke the unknown administrator or management permissions before uninstalling the suspicious software. Secure your primary email and device accounts from a separate, trusted computer immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use the phone for any banking, shopping, or sensitive communication until the administrator access is removed.
- Don’t open the unknown app to "investigate" it, as this may trigger further permission requests or data exfiltration.
- Don’t perform a factory reset without first recording the name of the app or profile for reporting purposes.
- Don’t assume the issue is resolved just because an app is hidden; you must check the system settings for active permissions.
- Don’t remove management profiles if the device was issued by an employer or school without consulting their IT department first.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the device from all networks immediately.**
   - **Swipe down or into settings to enable Airplane Mode.**
   - Ensure Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are manually toggled off after Airplane Mode is on to prevent remote commands or data uploads.

2. **Document the suspicious profile or app details.**
   - **Take a screenshot or photograph the name of the app and the permission screen using a different device.**
   - Note the exact name, any logo, and the date you first observed the unauthorized access.

3. **Locate and remove unknown profiles on iPhone.**
   - **Navigate to Settings -> General -> VPN & Device Management.**
   - Look for any "Configuration Profile" or "Mobile Device Management" (MDM) entry you do not recognize.
   - Select the profile, tap **Remove Management**, and restart the device.

4. **Revoke Device Admin privileges on Android.**
   - **Go to Settings -> Security & privacy -> More security settings -> Device admin apps.**
   - Toggle the switch to **Off** for any application you did not intentionally install or authorize.
   - Once the admin right is revoked, go to **Settings -> Apps** to find and **Uninstall** the app.

5. **Utilize Safe Mode if the app resists uninstallation.**
   - **Hold the power button and then long-press "Power off" or "Restart" until the "Reboot to safe mode" prompt appears.**
   - In Safe Mode, third-party apps are disabled, allowing you to revoke admin rights and uninstall the persistent app more easily.

6. **Verify and disable "Silent Control" permissions.**
   - **Check Settings -> Accessibility for any "Downloaded Services" that are turned on without your knowledge.**
   - Check **Settings -> Notifications -> Special app access -> Notification access** to ensure no unknown app is reading your private messages or two-factor codes.

7. **Secure your master accounts from a clean device.**
   - **Log in to your primary Email and your Apple ID or Google Account using a different, secure computer.**
   - Change your passwords immediately and select the option to "Sign out of all other sessions" or "Manage devices" to kick the compromised phone out of the account.

8. **Check for malicious email forwarding rules.**
   - **Review your email settings for any "Forwarding" or "Filters" that send your mail to an unknown address.**
   - Attackers often use these rules to intercept password reset links or bank alerts silently.

9. **Report the incident to the appropriate UK authorities.**
   - **Contact Report Fraud (formerly Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040 if you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland.**
   - If you are in Scotland, report the incident to **Police Scotland by calling 101**.

10. **Perform a factory reset as a final safety measure.**
    - **Back up only essential media (photos/contacts) and avoid restoring from a full "System Backup" which may reinstall the malware.**
    - Perform the reset via **Settings -> System -> Reset options -> Erase all data (factory reset)**.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine the identity of the person who installed the app right now.
- You don’t need to purchase or install third-party "antivirus" apps immediately; manual removal and a factory reset are more effective.
- You don’t need to replace your physical SIM card unless your mobile network provider specifically advises it.

## Important reassurance
Finding unauthorized administrative access on a personal device is a serious security event. It is common to feel a loss of privacy or control. Following these structured steps to isolate the device and secure your "master" accounts is the standard professional response to regaining safety.

## Scope note
This guidance is for the immediate stabilization of a compromised mobile device. It does not cover long-term forensic analysis or legal evidence collection for criminal proceedings.

## Important note
Menu paths on mobile devices vary significantly between manufacturers (e.g., Samsung vs. Google) and software versions. If you cannot find a specific menu, use the "Search" bar within your phone's Settings app. Removing legitimate company management profiles may result in the loss of access to work emails or internal apps.
