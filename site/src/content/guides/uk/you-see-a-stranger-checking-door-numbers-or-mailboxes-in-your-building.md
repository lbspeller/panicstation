# What to do if… you see a stranger checking door numbers or mailboxes in your building (UK)

## Short answer
Do not confront them in the corridor. Get behind a locked door or into a staffed public area, alert the concierge, managing agent, or landlord, and call 999 if you think a crime is happening now or anyone is in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t confront them, follow them, or try to block their way.
- Don’t open your flat door to speak to them.
- Don’t buzz them in, hold the communal door, or prop doors open for them.
- Don’t tell them your flat number, who lives where, or who is or is not home.
- Don’t approach to check ID if you feel unsure or unsafe.
- Don’t ask other residents to gather in the corridor to challenge them.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance first.** Step back inside your flat and lock the door, or move calmly to a staffed public area such as a lobby or concierge desk.
2. **Secure the communal entry behind you.** If you have just entered, check that the communal door closes and latches. Do not allow tailgating.
3. **Ask building staff to verify.** Contact the concierge, managing agent, landlord, or building security and ask them to check whether a visitor, contractor, delivery, or appointment is expected.
4. **Give facts, not guesses.** Tell staff:
   - where the person is, such as the floor, corridor, lift area, or mailboxes;
   - what they are doing, such as checking door numbers, reading mailboxes, pressing buzzers, or looking for a specific flat;
   - a brief description, such as clothing, approximate age, bags, tools, or vehicle if safely visible;
   - whether they are trying handles, forcing doors or mailboxes, threatening anyone, or refusing to leave.
5. **Call police if the risk is immediate.**
   - Call 999 if you think a crime is happening now, someone is in immediate danger, the person is forcing entry, trying door handles, threatening anyone, carrying a weapon, or the situation may become violent.
   - Call 101, or report online through Police.uk or your local police force, if it is suspicious but not an emergency.
6. **If they knock, keep the barrier.** Do not open the door. If you choose to respond, speak through the door and say: **“I can’t help. Please contact the resident you’re looking for or the building management.”** Then stop engaging.
7. **Record details safely.** From behind a closed door or another safe place, write down the time, location, what happened, and who you contacted. Only take a photo or video if you can do it without approaching, being seen, or escalating the situation.
8. **Ask for a same-day building notice.** Ask management to remind residents not to buzz in strangers, not to hold communal doors open, and to report tailgating, mailbox tampering, or door-handle trying.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether they were definitely casing flats or simply lost.
- You do not need to investigate, patrol corridors, or organise residents to confront them.
- You do not need to decide about cameras, new locks, or building-wide changes in the moment.
- You do not need to write a perfect report before asking staff or police for help.

## Important reassurance
It is common to freeze, doubt yourself, or worry about overreacting. You are allowed to prioritise distance, a locked door, and help from building staff or police without proving the person’s intent.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions about building security, tenancy issues, repeated incidents, or police follow-up may need specialist help.
