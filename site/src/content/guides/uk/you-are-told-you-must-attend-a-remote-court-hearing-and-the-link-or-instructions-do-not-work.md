# What to do if… you are told you must attend a remote court hearing and the link or instructions do not work (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the court or tribunal immediately using the phone number on your hearing notice to record your attempt to attend. Attempt one quick technical reset, but do not allow technical troubleshooting to delay notifying the court admin or your legal representative.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the technical issue to resolve itself without contacting the court — silence may be recorded as a failure to attend.
- Do not spend more than five minutes troubleshooting before making your first phone call to the court.
- Do not reply only to automated "no-reply" email addresses.
- Do not travel to the physical court building unless specifically instructed by court staff or a judge to do so.
- Do not assume that a "waiting room" screen means you are disconnected — wait at least 10 minutes past the start time before assuming a failure.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific platform and contact details on your hearing notice.** 
   - Look for the **court name**, **case number**, and **hearing time**.
   - Check if the platform is **Cloud Video Platform (CVP)** or the **Video Hearings service (VHS)**.
   - Note the phone number and email for the "Listing Office" or "Admin Team" provided on the notice.

2. **Perform one rapid technical reset of your browser and connection.** 
   - If using a PC/Windows, switch to the latest version of **Google Chrome** or **Microsoft Edge**.
   - If using a Mac, use **Safari** as the primary browser choice.
   - Disconnect from any VPN or work network that may have firewalls.
   - Restart the browser and paste the link directly into the address bar rather than clicking it in an email.

3. **Call the HMCTS technical helpdesk if the platform itself is failing.** 
   - For CVP issues: call **0330 808 9405** (Monday to Friday, 9am – 5pm).
   - For Video Hearings service (VHS) issues: email **video-hearings@justice.gov.uk**.
   - On Saturdays or Bank Holidays, skip this and contact the court’s emergency or duty line directly.

4. **Phone the court or tribunal admin office immediately to report your technical failure.** 
   - State your name, case number, and the scheduled hearing time.
   - Inform them clearly: "I am ready and available to attend but the remote link is failing."
   - Ask for a "dial-in" number and PIN so you can join by telephone instead of video.

5. **Send a formal email to the court admin to create a time-stamped record.** 
   - Use the subject line: "URGENT - TECHNICAL FAILURE - [Case Number] - [Your Name]".
   - Include a brief statement: "I have attempted to join the hearing at [Time] but the link/instructions provided are not working. I am awaiting further instructions on [Your Phone Number]."

6. **Contact your solicitor or legal representative if you have one.** 
   - Tell them exactly what error you are seeing (e.g., "Invalid PIN", "Link Expired", or "No Camera Found").
   - They can often notify the judge or clerk inside the "room" that you are experiencing technical difficulties.

7. **Notify your supervising officer if the hearing relates to bail or probation.** 
   - If you are under supervision, call your probation officer or the staff at your designated location immediately.
   - Ensure they record the time of your call and your reported technical difficulty as evidence of your attempt to comply.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the technical fault (internet speed, software bug, or court error) right now.
- You do not need to prepare a formal complaint about the technology until the hearing issue is resolved.
- You do not need to worry about being "in trouble" for a genuine technical failure as long as you have contacted the court at the time of the event.

## Important reassurance
Technical failures in remote hearings are common and recognized by the judiciary. Courts generally prioritize ensuring a fair hearing over technical perfection. Provided you have made an immediate and documented effort to contact the court admin, you have taken the necessary steps to protect your position.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It focuses on the immediate actions required to notify the court of a technical failure and prevent a "failure to attend" record. It does not cover legal arguments for adjournments or long-term hardware setup.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. Procedures for remote attendance are subject to the directions of the presiding judge or magistrate. If you are in any doubt, prioritize the instructions provided by court staff or your legal representative.
