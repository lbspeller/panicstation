# What to do if… you get a call claiming to be emergency services asking you to confirm details that feel wrong (UK)

## Short answer
Hang up the phone immediately and contact the emergency services yourself through a trusted, independent method such as 101 or 999 to verify the caller's identity.

## Do not do these things
- Don't share or "confirm" any sensitive information, including your full address, date of birth, bank account numbers, or passwords.
- Don't provide any one-time passcodes (OTP) sent to your phone, even if the caller claims they sent it to "verify" you.
- Don't use a phone number provided by the caller to "verify" their identity or call them back.
- Don't transfer money to any "safe account" or agree to hand over cash or high-value items to a "police courier."
- Don't stay on the line if you feel pressured, threatened, or if the caller insists you must not tell anyone else about the call.
- Don't download any apps or software if instructed by the caller.

## What to do now
1. **End the call immediately without providing further information.** You do not need to explain why you are hanging up; simply terminate the connection to stop the interaction.
2. **Wait at least two minutes before using the same landline to make a follow-up call.** This ensures the previous caller has disconnected and is not "holding the line" open to intercept your next call.
   - Use a different phone, such as a mobile, if you need to make a verification call immediately.
3. **Dial 101 to speak with the police if you are not in immediate danger.** Ask the operator to verify if a genuine officer or member of staff is trying to contact you or if there is an incident linked to your details.
4. **Dial 999 only if you believe there is a genuine, immediate threat to life or property.** Tell the emergency operator that you just received a suspicious call claiming to be from the emergency services.
5. **Contact your bank immediately if the call involved your finances or if you shared bank details.** Use a trusted number from the back of your card or use the **159** service to be connected directly to your bank's fraud department.
6. **Report the incident to the official national fraud reporting service.** 
   - **In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland:** Report to **Report Fraud** (the successor to Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **In Scotland:** Report the incident directly to **Police Scotland** by calling 101.
7. **Record specific details about the call while they are fresh in your memory.** Note the time of the call, the name or rank the caller used, the "reference number" they provided, and the number displayed on your caller ID.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the call was a scam or a mistake before hanging up.
- You do not need to contact other family members or neighbors until you have spoken to the official services.
- Changing all your online passwords can wait until you have verified your safety and secured your primary accounts with your bank or the police.

## Important reassurance
Hanging up on a suspicious caller is the safest action you can take. Genuine emergency services and police officers understand the risk of phone scams and will never penalize you for terminating a call to verify their identity through official channels. Feeling startled or pressured by these calls is a normal reaction to high-pressure tactics.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for handling suspicious phone calls claiming to be from UK emergency services. It does not cover long-term identity theft recovery or legal proceedings following a fraud incident.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, always dial 999.
