# What to do if… your key fob stops working and you cannot unlock or start your vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer, well-lit pause first, then use the physical emergency key and the vehicle’s backup start method if you can. If you still cannot unlock or start the vehicle, call your breakdown provider, insurer roadside assistance, dealer, or a reputable auto locksmith rather than forcing entry.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay somewhere isolated, dark, or unsafe to troubleshoot; move to a staffed, well-lit place first if you can.
- Don’t keep pressing the fob buttons for minutes; it may drain a weak fob battery further and makes the situation more stressful.
- Don’t try to force the door with coat hangers, wedges, screwdrivers, or other improvised tools; this can damage the vehicle or safety systems.
- Don’t smash a window unless there is an immediate safety emergency, such as a person or animal trapped inside and in distress.
- Don’t accept an approach from an unknown “locksmith” without checking the company, agreeing the total call-out cost, and asking what proof of ownership they will need.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and protect your phone battery.** Move into a nearby staffed place, such as a petrol station, shop, car park office, hotel, or station, if you are isolated, it is dark, or you feel uneasy. If you feel threatened or someone is in immediate danger, call **999**.
2. **Do a quick check before taking things apart.**
   - Try your spare key or spare fob if you have it with you.
   - Stand close to the driver door and try the fob once or twice.
   - If the fob was dropped, cracked, or got wet, assume it may have failed and move to manual entry and backup start.
3. **Find and use the physical emergency key.**
   - Many key fobs contain a small metal key blade released by a button, catch, or sliding cover.
   - The driver door lock may be visible, or it may be hidden behind a small cover near the handle.
   - Use the emergency key carefully; avoid sharp tools that could scratch the handle or damage the cover.
4. **If the alarm sounds after manual unlocking, keep it simple.**
   - Get in, close the door, and try the backup start method.
   - If the alarm does not stop once the vehicle recognises the key, stop repeatedly locking and unlocking it and call for help.
5. **Try the vehicle’s backup start method.**
   - Many push-button start cars can still read the fob chip if the fob is held against the start button while you press it.
   - Some vehicles have a marked slot, tray, pocket, steering-column point, or cup-holder position where the fob must be placed.
   - Check the vehicle handbook, manufacturer app, or manufacturer website on your phone if you can access it safely.
6. **Check whether this may be the vehicle’s 12V battery, not the fob.**
   - If there are no interior lights, no dashboard lights, and no response from the vehicle, the vehicle battery may be flat.
   - Ask for battery or breakdown assistance rather than focusing only on a replacement fob.
7. **Call the right help and describe exactly what is happening.**
   - Tell your breakdown provider or insurer roadside assistance: “Keyless entry and push-button start; fob not working; vehicle does or does not have dashboard power.”
   - If you need a replacement key or fob, contact the manufacturer, dealer, breakdown provider, insurer, or a vehicle key service.
   - If using an auto locksmith, choose a reputable local firm, confirm the call-out charge and likely total price before dispatch, and ask what proof of ownership they require.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to replace the whole fob, reprogram keys, change your breakdown cover, or buy a new spare.
- You do not need to do roadside electronics troubleshooting beyond the safe access, backup start, and battery checks above.
- Prevention steps, such as replacing the fob battery, checking where the manual keyhole is, and making a spare key plan, can wait until you are safe and unstuck.

## Important reassurance
This is a common vehicle problem, and many cars are designed with a physical emergency key and a backup way to recognise the fob. The priority right now is to stay safe, regain access if you can do so without force, and get practical help if the vehicle still will not respond.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: safe access, a basic backup-start attempt, and getting the right help. Later decisions about fob batteries, water damage, replacement keys, programming, or vehicle battery faults may need a dealer, breakdown service, insurer, or qualified auto locksmith.

## Important note
This is general information, not vehicle-specific mechanical, legal, financial, or professional advice. Keyless systems vary by make, model, and year; if anything would require force, uncertain disassembly, or bypassing security systems, stop and use a breakdown service, dealer, insurer, or reputable auto locksmith.
