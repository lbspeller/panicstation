# What to do if… you submitted an online travel declaration with an error and cannot edit it (UK)

## Short answer
Check the official service for the exact UK permission or declaration you used, and fix any passport or identity mismatch through that official route if it is available. If you cannot edit it, keep the reference details and be ready to explain the mistake early at check-in and, if asked, on arrival.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter made-up or close-enough details to make a screen accept your submission.
- Do not ignore mismatches in name, passport number, date of birth, nationality, citizenship, or passport expiry.
- Do not pay unofficial sites or agents who claim they can edit government submissions.
- Do not submit repeated versions without saving each reference number and confirmation.
- Do not leave a possible passport or permission mismatch until the boarding gate.
- Do not hide the issue if airline staff or a border officer asks about it.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact official system.**  
   Check whether the mistake is in a UK ETA, a UK eVisa or UKVI account, or another official arrival or travel form. Use the official government site or app for that exact system, not a search result ad or an unofficial agent.

2. **Separate identity-critical errors from smaller errors.**  
   Identity-critical errors include your name, passport number, date of birth, passport expiry, nationality, citizenship, and whether you used the same passport you will travel with. Treat these first because they can affect airline checks and border checks.

3. **Use the official correction route if there is one.**  
   If the official service lets you correct the record, do that. If it says to submit a new application or declaration, submit one corrected version and keep both the wrong and corrected confirmations. If the service says it cannot be changed, stop trying to work around it and follow the official help route.

4. **If it is a UK ETA, check the ETA and your passport link.**  
   A UK ETA is linked to the passport used for the application. If that passport has changed or expired, the official GOV.UK service says you need a new ETA. Use the official ETA check service to see whether you have an ETA and when it expires. For ETA help, use the official ETA help route; UKVI does not handle ETA enquiries by phone.

5. **If it is a UK eVisa or UKVI account, use the correct eVisa route.**  
   If your online immigration status details are wrong, use the official “report an error with your eVisa” service. If your UKVI account needs updating because your passport, travel document, name, contact details, date of birth, or nationality has changed or needs correcting, use the official “update your details” service.

6. **Make a small proof pack you can access offline.**  
   Save the confirmation email, reference number, any corrected confirmation, and one screenshot showing the submitted details and the error. Keep your passport with you and check the exact spelling and numbers against the confirmation.

7. **Flag the mismatch early at check-in if it could affect boarding.**  
   Say calmly: “I submitted the online declaration and then saw an error I could not edit. Here is the reference and the correct passport information.” Ask airline staff which check they need to complete.

8. **On arrival, answer plainly if asked.**  
   Say that you submitted the online form, noticed the error, and used the official route available to you. If the mistake relates to something you are required to declare, declare it clearly rather than hoping it will not be noticed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to write a long complaint or explanation now.
- You do not need to decide the final outcome now.
- You do not need to contact several organisations at once; start with the official owner of the exact system.
- You do not need to solve refunds, rebooking, or complaints until the immediate document issue is clearer.

## Important reassurance
People do make these mistakes under time pressure. The safer response is to use the official route, keep clear proof, and be straightforward about the error if asked.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to reduce panic and prevent avoidable boarding or border problems. Later decisions, complaints, appeals, rebooking, or immigration questions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Rules can depend on the exact system, your passport, your status, your route, and the information submitted.
