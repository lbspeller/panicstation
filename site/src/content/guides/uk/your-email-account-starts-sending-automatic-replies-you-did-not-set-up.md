# What to do if… your email account starts sending automatic replies you did not set up (UK)

## Short answer
Unplug the drive immediately and stop attempting to "fix" or "repair" the file system. Every second a failing drive is powered on increases the risk of permanent, unrecoverable data loss.

## Do not do these things
- Don't click **Format**, **Initialize**, **Erase**, or **Repair** if prompted by Windows or macOS.
- Don't run "CHKDSK" or "First Aid" repairs if the drive is making unusual physical noises.
- Don't put the drive in a freezer; moisture and condensation can permanently destroy the internal platters.
- Don't open the hard drive casing or enclosure, as dust particles will instantly damage the storage surfaces.
- Don't swap power cables with those from different devices; using the wrong voltage can "fry" the controller.
- Don't keep replugging the drive to "see if it works now" if you hear clicking, grinding, or beeping.

## What to do now
1. **Remove power and data connections immediately.**
   **Disconnect the USB cable and the power lead from the wall to stop the drive from spinning.** Physical damage worsens every moment the read/write heads are in contact with the spinning disks.

2. **Check for sensory warning signs.**
   **Identify if the drive is emitting a smell of burning plastic, visible smoke, or excessive heat.**
   - If these are present, do not plug the drive back in under any circumstances.
   - Note whether the drive is silent, vibrating normally, or making repeating mechanical sounds (clicking or grinding).

3. **Perform exactly one low-risk hardware check if the drive sounds healthy.**
   **Try one different USB port on the computer and one different (identical) USB cable if available.** 
   - Ensure the drive is plugged directly into the computer, not a USB hub or "dongle," to ensure it receives full power.
   - Only use the original power adapter that was supplied with that specific drive model.

4. **Observe the drive in system tools without modifying it.**
   **Open "Disk Management" (Windows) or "Disk Utility" (macOS) to see if the hardware is detected.**
   - In Windows: Press **Win + X** and select **Disk Management**. 
   - In macOS: Open **Applications > Utilities > Disk Utility**.
   - If the drive appears but the partitions are "RAW," "Unallocated," or "Not Initialized," the hardware is likely failing; do not click any prompts to initialize it.

5. **Prioritize the most critical files if the drive momentarily mounts.**
   **Copy the smallest, most irreplaceable files (like Word documents or specific photos) one by one to a different device.**
   - Do not attempt to copy large folders or "Move" files (which involves a delete command).
   - If the transfer speed drops to zero or the drive starts clicking, stop immediately.

6. **Contact a professional data recovery service for high-value data.**
   **Search for a reputable UK-based data recovery lab if the files are worth more to you than the cost of professional intervention.**
   - DIY software is often unable to handle mechanical failures and can cause the drive to fail completely during the scan.

7. **Seek consumer guidance for faulty hardware.**
   **Call the Citizens Advice consumer helpline at 0808 223 1133 for advice on your rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015.**
   - You may be entitled to a repair or replacement from the retailer if the drive is faulty, but note that retailers are generally not responsible for the data stored on the device.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide which replacement drive to buy yet.
- You do not need to worry about your overall backup strategy until the current data is either recovered or declared lost.
- You do not need to research "forensic" software or complex command-line fixes right now.

## Important reassurance
External hard drive failure is a common mechanical event. Most people’s first instinct is to keep trying different ports or "repair" buttons, but recognizing the failure and powering down early is the most effective way to preserve the possibility of recovery. Feeling overwhelmed by potential data loss is a normal reaction.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and harm prevention. It focuses on preventing the escalation of a hardware or file system failure into permanent data loss.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee data recovery. Storage devices are volatile, and physical failures require specialized clean-room environments for safe repair. If your data is critical for legal, business, or irreplaceable personal reasons, consult a certified data recovery professional before attempting any DIY steps.
