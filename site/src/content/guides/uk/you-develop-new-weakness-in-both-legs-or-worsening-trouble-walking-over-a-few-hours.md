# What to do if… you develop new weakness in both legs or worsening trouble walking over a few hours (UK)

## Short answer
Call 999 for an ambulance immediately and state that you have "sudden or rapidly worsening weakness in both legs." Mention specifically if you have any changes in your bladder/bowel control or numbness in your private areas.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to drive yourself to A&E or use public transport.
- Do not try to "walk it off" or repeatedly test your strength by standing up.
- Do not use stairs, showers, or baths until you have been assessed by a professional.
- Do not eat or drink anything (stay "nil by mouth") if you also have slurred speech or difficulty swallowing.
- Do not wait for a GP appointment or a 111 callback if symptoms are progressing over minutes or hours.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 and request an ambulance for a suspected neurological emergency.**
   - Tell the operator: **"I have new, worsening weakness in both my legs."**
   - Use speakerphone so you do not have to hold the device while moving.
2. **Tell the operator immediately if you have any of the following "red flag" symptoms.**
   - **Bladder changes:** Inability to pass urine, loss of the "urge" to go, or leaking without knowing.
   - **Bowel changes:** Loss of control or not being able to feel when you are passing a bowel motion.
   - **Saddle numbness:** Loss of feeling around your genitals, buttocks, or "back passage."
   - **Altered sensation when wiping:** Not being able to feel the toilet paper when you wipe yourself after using the toilet.
3. **Position yourself in a stable, low-to-the-ground location to prevent falls.**
   - Sit on a firm sofa or lie on a bed; avoid chairs that might tip if your legs give way.
   - If you are alone, unlock your front door if it is safe and physically possible to do so without falling.
4. **Check for stroke signs using the FAST test if walking issues were sudden.**
   - **Face:** Check if one side of the face has drooped.
   - **Arms:** Check if you can raise both arms and keep them there.
   - **Speech:** Check if speech is slurred or garbled.
   - **Time:** If any of these are present, emphasize this to the 999 operator.
5. **Prepare a brief list of your medications and any recent illnesses or injuries.**
   - Include any **anticoagulants (blood thinners)** or recent spinal surgeries/injections.
   - Note down the exact time the weakness started and how quickly it has worsened.
6. **Gather your essential ID and a phone charger while waiting for paramedics.**
   - Place these in a single small bag or pile where you are sitting.
   - Do not move around the house to pack a large bag.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific cause (e.g., slipped disc vs. infection) before seeking help.
- You do not need to pack for a long hospital stay right now; focus only on immediate medical assessment.
- You do not need to notify your GP; the hospital will handle the necessary records after your admission.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant anxiety when your mobility changes rapidly. These symptoms require urgent investigation to rule out time-critical conditions like Cauda Equina Syndrome or spinal cord compression. Paramedics and hospital staff are trained to prioritize these symptoms to ensure the best possible outcome.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute medical crisis. It does not replace clinical diagnosis. Further management will require hospital-based imaging (such as an MRI) and specialist neurological or orthopaedic review.

## Important note
Rapidly worsening bilateral leg weakness is a medical emergency. The 2026 National Suspected Cauda Equina Pathway emphasizes that "time to scan" is critical for preserving function. If you are told to "wait and see" but your symptoms worsen (especially bladder/bowel control), call 999 again immediately.
