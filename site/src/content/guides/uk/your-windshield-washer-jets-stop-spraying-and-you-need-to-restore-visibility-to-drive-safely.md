# What to do if… your windshield washer jets stop spraying and you need to restore visibility to drive safely (UK)

## Short answer
Do not continue until you can see the road clearly through the windscreen. If you cannot restore safe visibility quickly, arrange help rather than trying to push on.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive “just to the next junction” if the windscreen is smeared or dirty enough that you are guessing at hazards.
- Do not keep running the wipers on a dry or gritty screen, because this can worsen the smear and may scratch the glass.
- Do not stop in a live lane to troubleshoot.
- Do not stand near moving traffic to check the washer jets.
- Do not attempt repairs on a motorway hard shoulder or in an emergency area.
- Do not put a warning triangle out on a motorway.
- Do not top up with plain water in freezing conditions if you can avoid it, because it can freeze in the bottle, lines, or jets.

## What to do now
1. Re-check that you are safe where you have stopped.
   - Keep your hazard warning lights on.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights too.
   - If you are on a motorway hard shoulder or in an emergency area, and it is safe and possible to leave the vehicle, get everyone out through the doors furthest from traffic, usually the left-hand doors.
   - Wait well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, behind a safety barrier where there is one.
   - If you cannot leave the vehicle safely, stay in the vehicle, keep your seat belt on, keep the hazard lights on, and call 999 and ask for the police.

2. Decide whether the windscreen is already too unclear to continue.
   - Look through the part of the windscreen swept by the driver’s wiper.
   - If you cannot clearly judge lane lines, vehicles, signs, pedestrians, or hazards, treat the vehicle as not safe to drive until visibility is restored.
   - If glare, spray, mud, salt, or grit is making the view worse, do not rejoin traffic.

3. Stop the view getting worse from inside the car.
   - Use the demister or defogger.
   - Turn air conditioning on if available, because it can help clear moisture.
   - If you have a clean cloth within reach, wipe the inside of the windscreen where mist or film is affecting your view.

4. Check the washer fluid only if you are in a safe place away from traffic.
   - If you are in a car park, services, driveway, or another safe off-road place, check the washer bottle level.
   - If it is low or empty and you have proper screenwash, top it up.
   - In cold weather, use screenwash suitable for the temperature shown on its container.

5. Do one brief washer check only if it is safe.
   - Activate the washers briefly and listen.
   - If you hear the pump but no fluid sprays, the cause may be blocked jets, frozen fluid, a disconnected line, or an empty bottle.
   - If you hear no pump sound, the cause may be a fuse, pump, wiring, or another electrical issue.
   - Do not keep cycling the washers if nothing is coming out.

6. If freezing is likely, avoid roadside fixing.
   - If the temperature is near or below freezing, the washer fluid, lines, or jets may be frozen.
   - Keep the cabin warm and the windscreen demisting.
   - Arrange assistance if visibility is still not safe.

7. If you are safely off the road, try only a gentle surface check.
   - Wipe mud, wax, or ice from the washer nozzle area.
   - If a nozzle opening is visibly clogged, you may gently clear the surface with something fine, but stop if there is any resistance or risk of damaging the nozzle.
   - Do not do this at the roadside near moving traffic.

8. If safe visibility is not restored, get assistance.
   - Call your breakdown provider.
   - If you are in a motorway emergency area, use the emergency roadside telephone if you can reach it safely, and follow the operator’s advice before trying to rejoin traffic.
   - If you are in danger, cannot leave the vehicle safely, or feel at risk from traffic, call 999 and ask for the police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault is the pump, fuse, hose, filter, wiring, or washer jets while you are roadside.
- You do not need to decide repair costs or garage options now.
- You do not need to clean the whole vehicle or flush the washer system now.
- The only decision needed now is whether you can see well enough to drive safely. If not, get help.

## Important reassurance
This is a common fault, and it is usually fixable. The important thing is that you noticed the visibility problem and stopped to reassess before carrying on.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and avoid a visibility-related crash. Later decisions about repair, maintenance, MOT condition, or underlying faults can wait until you are safely away from traffic and may need a qualified mechanic or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow emergency-service, police, traffic officer, recovery operator, and official road-sign instructions where they apply.
