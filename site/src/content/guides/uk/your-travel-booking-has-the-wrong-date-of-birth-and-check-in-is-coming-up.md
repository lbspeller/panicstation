# What to do if… your travel booking has the wrong date of birth and check-in is coming up (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the airline or the company that issued the booking now, and ask them to correct the date of birth in the active booking record before you rely on online check-in.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume it will not matter, especially for international travel where passenger information may be checked against travel documents.
- Don’t create a second booking for the same passenger unless the airline or agent specifically tells you to do that.
- Don’t wait until the airport if you can still contact the airline, agent, or travel company now.
- Don’t change only your saved traveller profile and assume the trip booking has changed.
- Don’t cancel in panic before asking what correction, reissue, or airport check-in options are available.

## What to do now
1. **Find out who controls this booking.**
   - If you booked direct with the airline, use the airline’s manage-booking, app, live chat, phone, or airport-sales support route.
   - If you booked through an online travel agent, high-street travel agent, package provider, or holiday company, contact that company first and ask whether they issued the ticket and can amend passenger details.
   - If the agent says only the airline can help, ask them to confirm that in writing and then contact the airline with the booking reference and ticket number if you have one.

2. **Check exactly where the wrong date appears.**
   Look at the booking confirmation, e-ticket, manage-booking page, online check-in page, and any Advance Passenger Information page. Say clearly whether the wrong date is in the booking record, the saved traveller profile, the ticket, or the passenger information submitted for this trip.

3. **Ask for the date of birth to be corrected in the trip booking.**
   Use simple wording: “The date of birth for this passenger is wrong. I need the date of birth corrected in the booking record and any passenger information for this trip before check-in.” Ask whether the ticket needs reissuing, whether a fee or fare difference applies, and whether you should try online check-in again afterwards.

4. **Check whether the wrong date changes the passenger type.**
   Tell them immediately if the error makes an infant look like a child, a child look like an adult, or an adult look like a child. Age category errors can affect fares, eligibility, seating, documents, and whether online check-in works.

5. **Re-submit passenger information after any correction.**
   If the trip asks for passport or Advance Passenger Information, re-open the passenger-details page after the correction. Check the name, date of birth, nationality, passport number, passport expiry date, and document-issuing country against the travel document you will carry.

6. **If it cannot be corrected before check-in, get a clear airport plan.**
   Ask:
   - “Should I use staffed check-in instead of online check-in?”
   - “Can airport staff amend or verify the date of birth at the desk?”
   - “How early should I arrive for staffed check-in?”
   - “Will the passenger need any extra document check because the date of birth is wrong in the booking?”
   - “Can you add a note to the booking and send me written confirmation of what you have advised?”

7. **Keep a small proof pack ready.**
   Bring the physical passport or ID needed for travel. Keep the booking confirmation, ticket number if available, screenshots of the wrong detail, and messages showing that you reported the error. Store any passport image securely and delete it when you no longer need it.

8. **If you are being passed between companies, ask one control question.**
   Ask: “Who issued this ticket or booking, and who has authority to change the passenger date of birth?” Then continue with the company that says it controls the booking, and keep notes of names, times, and advice given.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim money back, or change travel provider later.
- You do not need to prove whose fault it was before check-in.
- You do not need to resolve every fee, refund, or payment dispute before travel if the booking can still be corrected or safely checked at the airport.

## Important reassurance
A wrong date of birth on a booking is stressful, but it is a passenger-detail problem that airlines and travel companies can assess. The useful next step is to get the record corrected or get written instructions for staffed check-in.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the immediate check-in risk. Later decisions about fees, refunds, complaints, or missed travel may need specialist consumer advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, airline, or professional advice. Airline and agent systems vary, and some changes may require reissuing a ticket, paying a fee, paying a fare difference, or using staffed check-in.
