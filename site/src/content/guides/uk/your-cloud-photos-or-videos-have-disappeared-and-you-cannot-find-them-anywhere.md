# What to do if… your cloud photos or videos have disappeared and you cannot find them anywhere (UK)

## Short answer
Stop making changes that could sync-delete more items. Check the service’s web view and its Recently Deleted, Trash, Bin, Hidden and Archive areas, then secure the account if anything looks suspicious.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t start mass-deleting, bulk-moving, or cleaning up duplicates while you are searching, because changes may sync across devices.
- Don’t empty Recently Deleted, Trash, Bin or Recycle Bin.
- Don’t factory reset your phone or reinstall the photo app as a first step.
- Don’t sign out of the cloud account on every device at once, because one device may still show where the items are.
- Don’t rely on only one view, such as the phone app, to decide the photos or videos are gone.

## What to do now
1. **Pause changes for a few minutes.**
   - Stop deleting, moving, merging or reorganising photos and albums.
   - If the missing items were last visible on one device, avoid making changes on that device. If you can do it without losing access to the account, pause photo syncing or put that device in Airplane mode while you check from another device or browser.
   - If the library or album is shared, ask others not to delete, move or reorganise anything right now.

2. **Check the provider’s web view first.**
   - Sign in through a browser to the service you use, such as iCloud Photos, Google Photos or OneDrive.
   - Check that you are using the exact account that held the photos or videos.
   - If you use work, personal, family or old email accounts, check each one separately.

3. **Check the places where items often sit outside the main feed.**
   - Look in Recently Deleted, Trash, Bin or Recycle Bin and restore anything you recognise.
   - Check Hidden items or Hidden album.
   - Check Archive in Google Photos.
   - Check shared libraries and shared albums.
   - Search for likely file names or patterns such as `IMG_`, `DSC_`, `.MOV` or `.MP4`, and search by a date, place or person if the service supports it.

4. **Check the device that last had the photos or videos.**
   - Open the photo app on that device without making changes to the library.
   - Check whether the items are still visible locally, in a device gallery, in a camera folder, or on a removable memory card.
   - If the items are still visible on one device, avoid deleting or signing out until you have exported or downloaded copies where the app allows it.

5. **Check the backup and sync settings for the service you used.**
   - For iCloud Photos, check whether iCloud Photos is turned on for the device you expected to sync from.
   - For Google Photos, check the account shown in the app and whether Backup is on if you expected automatic backup.
   - For OneDrive, check whether Camera Upload is on if you used it, and unlock Personal Vault before checking the Recycle Bin if the missing items may be there.

6. **Use official recovery tools before more time passes.**
   - For Apple or iCloud Photos, recover recognised items from Recently Deleted where available; Apple says deleted photos and videos remain recoverable for 30 days before permanent deletion.
   - For Google Photos, restore recognised items from Bin where available; Google says backed-up deleted photos and videos stay in Bin for 60 days, and unbacked-up deleted items usually stay there for 30 days.
   - For OneDrive, restore recognised items from Recycle Bin where available; Microsoft says items in a personal OneDrive recycle bin are automatically deleted after 30 days.
   - If many OneDrive files changed suddenly and you have Microsoft 365, check Restore your OneDrive; Microsoft says this can help undo file and folder actions from the last 30 days.

7. **If the account may have been accessed by someone else, secure it carefully.**
   - Secure the email account first, because it may control password resets.
   - Change the cloud account password and turn on two-factor authentication if it is not already on.
   - Review recent sign-ins, trusted devices, recovery email addresses and sharing permissions.
   - Save screenshots of suspicious sign-ins, deletion alerts or recovery messages before contacting support.

8. **If fraud, hacking, money or identity details may be involved, use UK reporting routes.**
   - Contact your bank or card provider immediately if payment details, bank details or identity information may be involved.
   - In England, Wales or Northern Ireland, report cyber crime or fraud through Report Fraud.
   - In Scotland, report cyber crime or fraud to Police Scotland.
   - If there is an immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to switch cloud providers.
- Rebuilding your backup system.
- Sorting albums, removing duplicates, freeing storage, or reorganising the library.
- Deciding who is to blame for the missing items.

## Important reassurance
Cloud photo libraries can look empty when the wrong account is open, a shared library has changed, syncing is still catching up, or items are sitting in a deleted, hidden or archived area. Checking the web view and recovery areas first gives you the best chance of finding recoverable copies without making the situation worse.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce accidental loss, check common recovery places, and secure the account. Later decisions about provider support, device repair, data recovery, fraud reporting, or legal action may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, technical, forensic, financial, or other professional advice. Cloud services differ, features change, and recovery windows can be time-limited, so use official recovery tools and support channels where available.
