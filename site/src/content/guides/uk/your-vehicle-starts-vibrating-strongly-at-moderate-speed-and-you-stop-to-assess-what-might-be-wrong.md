# What to do if… your vehicle starts vibrating strongly at moderate speed and you stop to assess what might be wrong (UK)

## Short answer
Treat any sudden, strong vibration as a safety-critical fault; stop in the first available place of relative safety, evacuate all passengers to a position behind a safety barrier, and arrange professional recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Don't drive faster to see if the vibration "smooths out" or disappears at higher speeds.
- Don't stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or between the vehicle and the safety barrier.
- Don't attempt to change a wheel or perform any repairs on a motorway or high-speed road.
- Don't place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Don't leave your vehicle by the right-hand (driver's side) doors if stopped on a hard shoulder or verge.
- Don't stay in the vehicle if it is safe to reach a position behind a safety barrier.
- Don't let animals out of the vehicle unless it is a life-threatening emergency.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to a place of relative safety immediately.**  
   Use your indicators, pull into the nearest service area, lay-by, or emergency area. If these are unavailable, stop on the hard shoulder as far to the left as possible.
2. **Secure the vehicle and signal your position.**  
   **Turn the steering wheel to the left** so the wheels face away from traffic, apply the handbrake, and switch on your hazard warning lights. If it is dark or visibility is poor, keep your sidelights on.
3. **Evacuate the vehicle using the left-hand doors.**  
   Ensure all passengers exit via the doors furthest from the traffic. Move everyone well away from the road, ideally behind a safety barrier and onto the verge.
4. **Identify your exact location.**  
   Look for the numbers on the nearest marker post or driver location sign. If you are in an emergency area, use the provided SOS phone, which connects directly to National Highways.
5. **Contact the appropriate authority for assistance.**  
   Call National Highways on **0300 123 5000** or your breakdown recovery provider. If you are stranded in a live lane or there is an immediate risk of fire or collision, call **999**.
6. **Perform a visual inspection only if it is safe to do so.**  
   Check for obvious exterior signs such as a flat tyre, a bulge in the sidewall, or missing wheel nuts. Do not touch the wheels or brakes if they are hot, and do not crawl under the vehicle.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the mechanical source of the vibration (e.g., wheel balancing, suspension, or drivetrain).
- You do not need to decide which garage will perform the final repair while at the roadside.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of the recovery until you are in a safe location.

## Important reassurance
Strong vibrations often feel more catastrophic than they are, but stopping immediately is the correct safety protocol. Most vibration issues, while serious, are manageable through professional recovery and do not result in a total loss of control if speed is reduced safely and the vehicle is stopped promptly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for roadside safety only. It does not cover mechanical diagnostics or long-term vehicle maintenance.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the Highway Code or professional mechanical advice. Roadside environments are inherently dangerous; if you cannot reach a safe place or feel at risk from passing traffic, contact emergency services immediately.
