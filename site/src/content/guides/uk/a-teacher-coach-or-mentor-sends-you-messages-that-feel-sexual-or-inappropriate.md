# What to do if… a teacher, coach, or mentor sends you messages that feel sexual or inappropriate (UK)

## Short answer
Stop private one-to-one messaging if you safely can, and tell a safeguarding contact or trusted adult as soon as possible. You do not need to prove anything before asking for help.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t agree to secrecy, “special” private chats, or one-to-one meetings outside normal arrangements.
- Don’t keep replying to be polite, to test what they mean, or to gather more evidence.
- Don’t confront them in person or try to negotiate boundaries alone.
- Don’t delete everything in a panic if you may need help later.
- Don’t post screenshots publicly or send them around to lots of people.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance from the messages.** If you can, stop replying. If you need one final reply for safety or clarity, keep it brief: “Please only contact me through official channels.” Then stop engaging.
2. **Keep a minimal record, then stop.** If it feels safe, save or screenshot a few key messages showing the sender, date, time, and app or platform. Do not keep the conversation going for evidence, and do not forward the messages widely.
3. **Tell the safeguarding person in the setting.** In a school, college, club, or organisation, ask for the named safeguarding or child protection lead. In a sports club, ask for the Welfare Officer, Safeguarding Officer, or safeguarding lead.
4. **If you do not know who the safeguarding person is, tell a trusted adult now.** This could be a parent or carer, another teacher, a club manager, a GP, or another adult you trust. Ask them to help you contact the safeguarding lead and stay with you while you do it.
5. **Ask for it to be recorded as a safeguarding concern.** You can say: “Please record this as a safeguarding concern and follow the organisation’s child protection or safeguarding procedure.”
6. **Use urgent help if you feel at risk now.** If you are in immediate danger, call 999. If it is not an emergency but you are worried about grooming, coercion, or sexual communication from an adult in a position of trust or authority, you can contact police on 101 or ask a trusted adult to do this with you.
7. **Use a specialist online route if the messages are online.** If this is happening through social media, messaging apps, gaming chat, or another online platform, you can make a report to CEOP, especially if you are under 18 or worried about someone under 18.
8. **Get confidential support for yourself.** If you are under 19, Childline can help you talk through what happened and what to say next. If you are an adult worried about a child or young person, the NSPCC helpline can advise you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a formal complaint, police report, or misconduct report.
- You do not need to write a perfect timeline tonight.
- You do not need to decide whether the messages “count” as grooming or abuse before telling someone.
- You do not need to keep talking to the person to find out what they meant.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel confused, flattered, sick, guilty, frozen, or unsure when an adult with authority crosses a line. This is not your fault, and it is reasonable to ask safer adults to take over the handling of it.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce contact, preserve your immediate options, and get safeguarding support. Later decisions about complaints, investigations, misconduct routes, or statements can be made with help from safeguarding professionals or specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, safeguarding, or other professional advice. If you are under 18, or you are raising a concern about someone under 18, sexualised messages from a teacher, coach, mentor, or other adult in authority should be treated as a safeguarding concern. Safeguarding, complaint, and misconduct routes can differ by setting and place, so ask a safeguarding lead, trusted adult, local authority route, or specialist helpline which route applies.
