# What to do if… a tyre pressure warning comes on and you have pulled over safely to check it (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the pressure of all four tyres using a manual gauge and inspect for physical damage; do not resume driving unless pressures are correct and no structural defects are found.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to inspect or repair a tyre if you are standing in or near a live traffic lane.
- Do not use a warning triangle if you are stopped on a motorway.
- Do not allow passengers or animals to exit the vehicle onto the side of the road with moving traffic.
- Do not ignore the light even if the tyres look visually normal; a 20 percent drop in pressure may not be visible to the eye.
- Do not inflate a tyre that has a visible bulge, deep side-wall cut, or exposed internal cords.
- Do not attempt simple repairs yourself on a motorway or high-speed road.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle and maximize your visibility to other road users.**  
   - Switch on your hazard warning lights immediately.
   - Turn on sidelights if it is dark or visibility is poor.
   - Apply the handbrake and turn the front wheels away from the carriageway (to the left).

2. **Move all occupants to a place of relative safety if you are on a motorway.**  
   - Exit the vehicle via the left-hand doors (furthest from traffic).
   - Ensure passengers stay well back from the road and behind a safety barrier if one is present.
   - Leave any animals inside the vehicle unless it is a life-threatening emergency.

3. **Perform an immediate visual inspection of all tyres from a safe position.**  
   - Look for a tyre that is visibly lower than the others or touching the rim.
   - Check for objects such as nails, screws, or debris embedded in the tread.
   - Listen for a hissing sound indicating a rapid air leak.
   - Inspect the sidewalls for bulges, splits, or tears.

4. **Measure the air pressure in all four tyres using a manual gauge.**  
   - Locate the manufacturer's recommended pressures (found in the vehicle handbook, on the driver's door pillar, or inside the fuel filler cap).
   - Use a portable gauge or a petrol station air line to get an accurate reading.
   - Check the pressure while the tyres are cold if possible.

5. **Top up the air to the correct level if no damage is found.**  
   - Inflate the tyre to the specific PSI, Bar, or kPa required for your current load.
   - Re-check the pressure after inflation to ensure it is holding.

6. **Determine if the vehicle is safe to continue the journey.**  
   - **Safe to proceed:** pressures are corrected, no damage is visible, and the warning light has cleared (or can be reset according to the handbook).
   - **Do not drive:** if a tyre will not hold air, has a bulge/cut, or if you feel any unusual vibration or pulling while moving.

7. **Follow the correct procedure for rejoining a motorway or high-speed road.**  
   - If you are on a hard shoulder, build up speed while remaining on the shoulder before moving into a gap in the traffic.
   - If you are in an orange emergency area, you must use the SOS phone to speak to an operator before leaving.

8. **Contact breakdown assistance if you cannot safely complete the check.**  
   - Call your recovery provider if you are in an exposed location or do not have the tools to verify pressures.
   - Call 999 immediately if your vehicle is causing an obstruction in a live lane or you feel in imminent danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the pressure dropped (e.g., temperature change versus a slow puncture) while at the roadside.
- You do not need to replace the TPMS sensor immediately if it is a sensor fault; this can be addressed at a garage later.
- You do not need to reset the dashboard light until you have reached a safe, off-road destination.

## Important reassurance
Tyre pressure warnings are often triggered by small deviations or seasonal temperature drops. Taking the time to stop and check manually is the correct safety response and prevents a minor issue from becoming a high-speed failure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a tyre pressure alert. It does not cover wheel changes or technical repair of the Tyre Pressure Monitoring System.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific instructions in your vehicle's manufacturer handbook. If you are stopped on a motorway or in a location where you cannot safely inspect the vehicle, stay clear of the traffic and seek professional roadside assistance. Driving on an underinflated or damaged tyre can result in a loss of vehicle control.
