# What to do if… you are told to provide work authorisation documents urgently or be taken off the schedule (UK)

## Short answer
Request the exact reason for the failure in writing and invoke your right to a **meaningful human review** under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 before any dismissal or suspension occurs.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign or sign any termination agreements during the initial notification meeting.
- Do not provide unrequested personal history or "confessions" before seeing the specific data flag.
- Do not attempt to contact the vetting agency via their public customer service line without your reference number.
- Do not assume a "fail" equals a criminal record; it is frequently an administrative mismatch of addresses or outdated identity documents.
- Do not use unofficial messaging channels like WhatsApp or Teams for formal disputes.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of check that triggered the alert.** 
   - **Ask HR to confirm if the flag relates to a Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) certificate, a Right to Work (RTW) verification, or a third-party reference check.**
   - **Request a copy of the report or certificate the employer is relying upon immediately.**

2. **Exercise your right to contest automated decisions.**
   - **If the failure was generated by an automated screening platform, invoke your right to "Contestability" under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025.**
   - **Formally request a human-in-the-loop review to correct data mismatches before the employer takes further action.**

3. **Check for "eVisa" or BRP-related errors if you have digital status.**
   - **Verify that the employer used a 2026-compliant digital share code check rather than relying on an expired physical Biometric Residence Permit (BRP).**
   - **Provide a fresh share code if the mismatch was caused by an outdated record in the Home Office "View and Prove" service.**

4. **Initiate a DBS dispute if the criminal record data is incorrect.**
   - **Complete the DBS certificate dispute form (Section A for personal details, Section B for records) within 3 months of the date printed on the certificate.**
   - **Notify your employer in writing that a formal dispute has been lodged with the Disclosure and Barring Service.**

5. **Formalize your request for a "decision hold."**
   - **Send a written request for a 5-day pause to allow for the rectification of inaccurate personal data under UK GDPR Article 16.**
   - **Ask if a temporary reassignment to non-regulated duties is possible while the data is verified.**

6. **Verify your right to accompaniment for any formal meetings.**
   - **Confirm if the upcoming meeting is an "investigatory" meeting or a "disciplinary" hearing.**
   - **If it is a disciplinary hearing that could lead to dismissal, formally notify the employer of your chosen companion (a work colleague or trade union representative).**

7. **Create a physical evidence folder.**
   - **Print and save your signed contract, the original job offer (noting "conditional" clauses), and all correspondence regarding the screening failure.**
   - **Include screenshots of your current Right to Work status and your 5-year address history for comparison.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file an Employment Tribunal claim today.
- You do not need to notify previous employers or referees about the current discrepancy.
- You do not need to provide a "mitigation statement" for a criminal record until you have confirmed the record actually belongs to you.

## Important reassurance
Administrative errors in screening are frequent, especially during the transition to 2026 digital identity standards. A "fail" notification is a procedural flag, not a final judgment on your character. Taking these steps creates a documented trail that prevents the employer from making a snap decision based on potentially flawed data.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and data-rights invocation only. It does not provide legal strategy for unfair dismissal claims or professional representation in regulated-sector appeals (e.g., CQC or OFSTED roles).

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights vary based on length of service and specific contract terms. If you are threatened with immediate dismissal without a notice period, seek advice from ACAS or a qualified employment solicitor.
