# What to do if… you are travelling with a pet and the carrier asks for vaccination or microchip paperwork you cannot access (UK)

## Short answer
Request the specific document name and entry requirement from the carrier staff, then contact your veterinary practice immediately to request an urgent digital copy via email.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to alter or manually edit existing digital records or dates on certificates.
- Do not provide verbal microchip numbers without physical or documented confirmation.
- Do not assume that a UK-issued pet passport is valid for entry into the EU.
- Do not leave the check-in desk until you have a written note of the specific missing data point (e.g., date of vaccination, manufacturer name, or chip implant date).
- Do not hand over original documents to third parties without observing the verification process.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact missing requirement for your specific route.**
   - Ask if the issue is a missing Animal Health Certificate (AHC), a Northern Ireland Pet Travel Document (PTD), or proof of a rabies booster.
   - Confirm if the carrier will accept a digital PDF sent directly from a registered veterinary practice.
   - Record the name of the staff member providing this information.

2. **Contact your primary veterinary practice for an emergency record transmission.**
   - **Call the practice and request an immediate email of the Clinical History or Vaccination Card.**
   - Ensure the document shows the pet's microchip number and the date it was last scanned or implanted.
   - Verify that the rabies vaccination date is recorded as occurring on or after the microchip date.

3. **Verify the pet's microchip number using an on-site scanner.**
   - **Ask the carrier or the port's Animal Reception Centre to scan the pet to confirm the 15-digit ISO number.**
   - Use this scanned number to help your vet locate the correct file if you are calling from a different phone number.

4. **Search digital backups and insurance portals.**
   - **Check your pet insurance policy documents online, as these almost always list the microchip number.**
   - Search email folders for "AHC," "Export," "Health Certificate," or the name of the vet who issued the travel papers.

5. **Request a temporary hold on the check-in process.**
   - **Ask the carrier for the "last possible boarding time" to allow for document retrieval.**
   - If travelling to Northern Ireland, check if you can apply for a PTD instantly via the government portal if you have the microchip and vaccination details.

6. **Check for "Part 1" listed country passports if applicable.**
   - **Confirm if you have an EU-issued or Northern Ireland-issued pet passport.**
   - Note that these remain valid for entry into the UK and EU, unlike passports issued in Great Britain.

7. **Initiate travel rescheduling if documents cannot be produced.**
   - **Ask for a "Refusal of Carriage" note if you are denied boarding.**
   - Contact a local Official Veterinarian (OV) to begin the process of issuing a replacement AHC if the original is physically lost and cannot be replaced digitally.

## What can wait
- You do not need to notify the destination's border authorities until your new travel time is confirmed.
- You do not need to update the microchip database registration details during the check-in crisis.
- You do not need to resolve discrepancies in pet weight or breed descriptions if the primary health documents are the only blocker.

## Important reassurance
Carriers frequently encounter missing or inaccessible paperwork. In many cases, a verified digital copy sent from a veterinary surgery is sufficient to satisfy the carrier's initial check, provided the underlying medical requirements (such as the 21-day rabies wait) have been met. Confusion during travel is a recognized physiological response and does not prevent the eventual retrieval of digital records.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document access issues at UK points of departure or entry. It does not cover long-term export health requirements or commercial animal transport regulations.

## Important note
This information is for procedural guidance and does not constitute legal or veterinary advice. Pet travel regulations are subject to change by DEFRA and the EU. Verification of your pet's eligibility to travel must be confirmed by an Official Veterinarian (OV) or the carrier's compliance team.
