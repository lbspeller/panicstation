# What to do if… you are told your property may be seized to satisfy a legal judgment and deadlines are short (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the legitimacy of the enforcement via independent court contact, then immediately file an application (Form N244 or N245) to stay the execution or vary the payment terms before the notice period expires.

## Do not do these things
- Don't let an enforcement agent (bailiff) into your home if they have not previously entered peacefully or if you have not signed a Controlled Goods Agreement.
- Don't pay any money to a link or number provided in a text or email without verifying the case number on the official Find a Court / Tribunal website.
- Don't hide, sell, or remove goods once a Notice of Enforcement has been served, as this can be a criminal offence or lead to immediate removal of other assets.
- Don't sign a Controlled Goods Agreement (CGA) under pressure if you do not understand that it gives the agent the right to return and break in later if you miss a payment.
- Don't assume that simply speaking to the enforcement agent will stop the legal clock; only a court-ordered stay or a formal agreement stops enforcement.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the case and enforcement agent via official government channels.**
   - **Search the "Find a Court or Tribunal" service to find the phone number for the court named on your paperwork.**
   - **Call the court directly to confirm that the claim number exists and that a Warrant of Control or Writ of Control has been issued.**
   - **Check the Certificated Bailiff Register to ensure the person contacting you is a licensed enforcement agent.**

2. **Calculate your "clear days" deadline exactly.**
   - **Identify the "date of notice" on the Notice of Enforcement and add seven clear days before a visit can occur.**
   - **Exclude the day you received the notice, the day of the proposed visit, Sundays, and bank holidays from this count.**
     - Note: If the notice is for Commercial Rent Arrears Recovery (CRAR), the period is often 14 days or longer under 2026 updated regulations.

3. **Determine which court form is required to pause the seizure.**
   - **Use Form N245 if you accept the debt and want to pay in instalments to suspend a County Court Warrant of Control.**
   - **Use Form N244 if you need an "Urgent Stay of Execution" for a High Court Writ or if you need to set aside the judgment because you never received the original claim.**
   - **Ensure you request an "interim stay" on the form to prevent any action while the judge considers your application.**

4. **Declare any "vulnerable person" status immediately in writing.**
   - **Notify the enforcement agency and the creditor if you or anyone in the house is disabled, seriously ill, elderly, or has young children.**
   - **Use the specific terminology "vulnerable person under Regulation 10 of the Taking Control of Goods Regulations 2013" to trigger mandatory protections and extra time.**

5. **Communicate only through closed doors or via letterbox if an agent visits.**
   - **Keep all doors and windows locked, as enforcement agents can enter through unlocked points of entry.**
   - **Ask the agent to pass their ID and the court warrant through the letterbox for your inspection.**
   - **Tell them clearly: "I am seeking legal advice and have an application pending with the court; I am not admitting you to the property."**

6. **Submit your court application and pay the fee (or apply for Help with Fees).**
   - **Take the completed form (N244 or N245) to the court office in person if the deadline is within 48 hours.**
   - **Apply for "Help with Fees" (Form EX160) at the same time if you are on a low income or certain benefits.**

7. **Contact a free, accredited debt advice service for an immediate triage session.**
   - **Call National Debtline or StepChange and tell them you have a "Notice of Enforcement" with a short deadline.**
   - **Ask them to review your "Statement of Means" (budget) to ensure your offer of payment to the court is sustainable.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle the full balance today if you can prove you only have the means for instalments.
- You do not need to provide an inventory of your belongings to the agent over the phone or at the door.
- You do not need to decide on a long-term debt solution (like an IVA or Bankruptcy) in the next hour; focus only on the stay of execution.

## Important reassurance
Receiving an enforcement notice is a procedural stage of debt recovery, not an immediate loss of your home or belongings. In the vast majority of UK residential cases, enforcement agents cannot force entry for standard consumer or credit debts. Taking the correct administrative step with the court usually provides a legal shield that the agents must respect.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for civil legal judgments in England and Wales. It does not cover criminal fines, TV license fines, or magistrates' court warrants, which have different rules regarding forced entry.

## Important note
This information is not legal advice. Court procedures and fees are subject to change. Always verify the status of your case with the specific court branch handling the enforcement.
