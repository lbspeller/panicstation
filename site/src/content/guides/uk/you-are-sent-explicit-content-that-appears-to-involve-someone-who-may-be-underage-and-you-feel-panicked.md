# What to do if… you are sent explicit content that appears to involve someone who may be underage and you feel panicked (UK)

## Short answer
Close the content immediately and report it to the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) if it is a public link, or the Police if it was sent directly to you. Do not delete or forward the message until you have spoken to authorities, as the act of reporting protects you and the potential victim.

## Do not do these things
- Do not forward the content to anyone else, including friends or family, to ask for their opinion.
- Do not reply to the sender or engage in any further conversation, even to express anger.
- Do not keep looking at the material to try and determine the age or identity of the person shown.
- Do not delete the message or content immediately; it may be needed as evidence once you contact the police.
- Do not search for more information about the sender or the content yourself.
- Do not panic about "possession" if you have received this unsolicited; taking immediate action to report it is the legal and safety priority.

## What to do now
1. **Stop looking at the content immediately.** Close the app, window, or tab where the image or video appeared.
2. **If the content is on a public website or a link, report it to the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF).** 
   - Visit the IWF website to make an anonymous report.
   - This service is specifically for reporting child sexual abuse imagery hosted online.
3. **If the content was sent to you directly (DM, email, or text), contact the Police.** 
   - Call **999** if you believe a child is in immediate danger.
   - Call **101** or use the "Report a Crime" tool on your local police force's website for non-emergencies.
   - Tell them you have received unsolicited content that you suspect involves a minor.
4. **Report and block the sender using the platform's internal tools.** 
   - Most apps (like Instagram, Snapchat, or WhatsApp) have a "Report" button specifically for "Illegal content" or "Child abuse." 
   - After reporting, use the "Block" function to prevent further messages.
5. **If you are under 19, contact Childline for support.** 
   - Call **0800 1111** or use their 1-2-1 online chat. 
   - They can help you process the shock and guide you through the reporting steps without judgment.
6. **If you are worried about a child you know, contact the NSPCC Helpline.** 
   - Call **0808 800 5000** or email help@nspcc.org.uk.
   - They provide professional advice on safeguarding and can help you make a referral if necessary.
7. **If you are a child or young person under 18, use the CEOP Safety Centre.**
   - The Child Exploitation and Online Protection (CEOP) Command allows you to report online grooming or sexual abuse directly to specialized police officers.

## What can wait
- You do not need to know the law or the specific "offence" name to report what you saw.
- You do not need to prove the age of the person in the content; a "suspicion" is enough for a report.
- You do not need to decide right now how to "fix" the situation; reporting puts the responsibility on the experts.

## Important reassurance
It is completely normal to feel sick, anxious, or frightened after seeing disturbing content. Receiving such material unsolicited is not your fault. The law recognizes that people may be sent illegal content without their consent; by reporting it promptly and following official guidance, you are taking the correct and safest path to resolve the situation.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and reporting only. It does not provide legal defense advice or long-term therapeutic support.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a substitute for legal advice or police instruction. Local laws regarding the handling of illegal images are strict; always follow the direct instructions of a police officer or qualified legal professional once you have made contact.
