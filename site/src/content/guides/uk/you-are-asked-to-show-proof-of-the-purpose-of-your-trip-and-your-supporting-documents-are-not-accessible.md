# What to do if… you are asked to show proof of the purpose of your trip and your supporting documents are not accessible (UK)

## Short answer
State clearly that your documents are digital and currently inaccessible, then provide a consistent verbal summary of your plans while requesting a brief opportunity to restore access to your device or accounts.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t guess or invent specific details such as flight numbers or hotel addresses if you are not certain.
- Don’t attempt to search for documents frantically while being actively questioned; wait for permission to use your device.
- Don’t delete any messages, call logs, or browser history during the examination process.
- Don’t provide altered or "placeholder" documents that do not accurately represent your actual bookings.
- Don’t use sarcasm or jokes regarding the security process or your lack of documentation.
- Don’t sign any statements or formal records of interview if you believe the recorded answers are inaccurate or if you do not fully understand the text.

## What to do now
1. **Explain the specific technical barrier to the officer immediately.**
   - State if the issue is a dead battery, lack of signal, a forgotten password, or a malfunctioning app.
   - Example: "My booking confirmations are in my email, but I cannot log in because I do not have a local SIM card for the two-factor authentication code."
2. **Request a brief "pause" or "technical window" to resolve the issue.**
   - Ask if you may connect to the airport Wi-Fi, use a charging station, or call a family member/colleague who can provide the documents.
   - If this is refused, do not argue; proceed to verbal verification.
3. **Provide a verbal summary of your trip that aligns with "Permitted Activities."**
   - **Explain why you are here** (e.g., tourism, visiting specific family, attending a specific business meeting).
   - **State where you are staying** (provide the name of the hotel or the name and relationship of your host).
   - **Explain your financial plan** (confirm you have access to funds via specific cards or bank accounts, even if you cannot show the balance right now).
   - **State your departure plan** (confirm the date and city you are returning to).
4. **Offer alternative verification routes that do not require your phone.**
   - Provide the phone number of your host, employer, or hotel and suggest the officer calls them to verify your arrival.
   - Mention any travel companions who are already through the border or in the queue who may have copies of your documents.
5. **Request an interpreter if you are struggling to understand technical questions.**
   - If your ability to explain your situation is limited by language, ask for interpretation services before the questioning proceeds to a formal stage.
6. **Ask for "slowed down" questioning if you feel overwhelmed.**
   - You are entitled to have questions repeated or phrased differently to ensure your answers are accurate and consistent.
7. **Request a written "Refusal of Leave to Enter" if you are denied entry.**
   - If the decision is made to refuse entry, ensure you receive the official paperwork which must state the specific reasons for the refusal and any rights of appeal or administrative review.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve long-term technical issues (e.g., a broken phone screen) immediately at the desk.
- You do not need to contact your airline to "prove" a flight exists until the officer specifically requests a physical or digital copy of the ticket.
- You do not need to decide on a "plan B" or return flight until a final decision on your entry has been issued in writing.

## Important reassurance
Digital failure at the border is a common occurrence. Border Force officers are trained to assess the "genuine visitor" requirement based on your verbal consistency and credibility, not just the presence of a PDF. Providing calm, consistent answers is often more significant than immediate document retrieval.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for travelers facing document access issues at UK ports of entry. It does not cover legal strategies for appealing a refusal or complex visa litigation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Entry to the UK is at the discretion of the Border Force officer under the Immigration Rules. Decisions are made based on the individual's circumstances, nationality, and the evidence provided at the time of the encounter.
