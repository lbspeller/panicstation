# What to do if… a charging-system or battery warning appears and you have pulled over safely (UK)

## Short answer
Move all occupants to a place of relative safety away from the carriageway and arrange professional recovery; a battery warning indicates a failing charging system that will likely cause the engine to stall or safety systems to fail without further notice.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "limp home" or reach a further destination; the vehicle may lose power, steering assistance, and lights at any moment.
- Do not keep the engine running or repeatedly restart it, as this exhausts the remaining reserve power needed for hazard lights.
- Do not attempt to inspect the battery, alternator, or belts if you are on a motorway, A-road, or any location near moving traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway, including the hard shoulder or emergency areas.
- Do not attempt to jump-start a hybrid or electric vehicle (EV) unless you are using the specific 12V jump points identified in the manual; never touch orange high-voltage cables.
- Do not stand between your vehicle and oncoming traffic or behind the vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle and maximize its visibility immediately.**  
   - Switch on your hazard warning lights.
   - Switch on sidelights if it is dark or visibility is poor.
   - Turn your front wheels away from the road (to the left) so the vehicle does not roll into traffic if struck.
2. **Move all occupants to a place of relative safety.**  
   - On motorways or high-speed roads, exit the vehicle via the left-hand doors (furthest from traffic).
   - Move behind the safety barrier if one is present, or well up the verge.
   - Keep all passengers, children, and pets under control away from the carriageway.
3. **Contact emergency services if you are in immediate danger.**  
   - If you have stopped in a live lane, cannot exit the vehicle safely, or see smoke/fire, **call 999 immediately or press your vehicle's SOS button**.
   - Stay in the vehicle with your seatbelt on and hazards on until help arrives if you cannot reach the verge safely.
4. **Report your location to the relevant road authority.**  
   - **In England:** Call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 or use a roadside emergency phone.
   - **In Scotland:** Call Traffic Scotland on 0800 028 1414.
   - **In Wales:** Call Traffic Wales on 0300 123 1213.
   - Provide your location using marker posts, driver location signs (the small blue or board signs), or your GPS coordinates.
5. **Preserve the remaining battery life for essential safety.**  
   - Turn off all non-essential electrical items including the radio, climate control, heated seats, and infotainment screens. 
   - Ensure the engine is switched off once you are in a safe position to prevent overheating or further mechanical damage.
6. **Contact your breakdown recovery provider.**  
   - Once you are in a safe position and have alerted the road authorities, call your insurance or breakdown service.
   - Inform them if you are a vulnerable motorist, such as if you are traveling alone, are elderly, or have a disability.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific cause of the failure (e.g., alternator vs. battery) while at the roadside.
- You do not need to find a repair garage or compare part prices until the vehicle has been recovered to a safe location.
- You do not need to worry about the long-term health of the battery; focus only on the immediate safety of the occupants.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when a red warning light appears, especially on a fast road. You have already taken the most important step by pulling over safely. Breakdown professionals and road authorities handle these charging failures daily and will prioritize your safety once you have made contact.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK road users who have encountered a battery or charging system warning light. It focuses on immediate safety and hazard isolation rather than mechanical diagnosis.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not replace the advice of emergency services or the official Highway Code. A battery warning light often precedes a total loss of vehicle control systems; prioritize personal safety over the vehicle at all times.
