# What to do if… you get a new severe stiff neck without an obvious injury (UK)

## Short answer
If the pain and stiffness are new and severe, treat it as urgent: call 999 now if you feel very unwell, have meningitis-type symptoms, have stroke-like symptoms, or have new problems walking, coordination, bladder, or bowel control.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force stretching, push through, or try to crack your neck to make it move.
- Do not drive yourself if you cannot move your neck normally, feel faint or confused, or have weakness, numbness, balance problems, vision changes, or speech changes.
- Do not wait for a rash to appear if you feel seriously unwell; serious infection can worsen quickly and a rash may not appear.
- Do not take extra doses or mix pain medicines beyond the label.
- Do not rely on heat, massage tools, traction, or spinal manipulation instead of urgent medical advice.

## What to do now
1. **Get into a safer pause and reduce movement.** Sit supported or lie down with your head and neck in a neutral position. If you live alone, call or message someone nearby so you are not managing this completely by yourself.
2. **Call 999 now if meningitis or sepsis is possible.** This is especially important if severe neck stiffness comes with fever, feeling very unwell, severe headache, confusion, drowsiness, being hard to wake, seizure, dislike of bright lights, vomiting, very cold hands and feet, pale or mottled skin, or a rash that does not fade when pressed. Do not wait for every symptom.
3. **Call 999 now if there are stroke-like or spinal warning signs.** This includes face drooping, new arm or leg weakness, slurred or garbled speech, new trouble walking, new loss of coordination, new severe balance problems, or new loss of bladder or bowel control.
4. **Get urgent same-day advice if there are nerve symptoms but no 999 signs.** Use NHS 111 online or call 111, or ask for an urgent same-day GP appointment, if you have new or worsening pins and needles, numbness, heaviness, weakness, clumsy hands, pain spreading down an arm, or pain between your shoulder blades.
5. **If it is severe or getting worse quickly but none of the emergency signs fit, still get same-day help.** Use NHS 111 online or call 111 for urgent assessment and direction to the right service. If you cannot use 111 online, call 111.
6. **While waiting for help, keep things simple.** Stay supported, avoid sudden neck movements, and use your usual over-the-counter pain relief only exactly as the label says. If you are pregnant, on blood thinners, have kidney disease, stomach ulcer problems, allergies, or are unsure what is safe, ask 111, a pharmacist, or a clinician before taking medicine.
7. **Have the key details ready.** Note when it started, how quickly it escalated, whether you have a temperature, headache, light sensitivity, nausea or vomiting, rash, weakness, numbness, balance changes, bladder or bowel changes, recent infection, immune-suppressing medicines or conditions, and any recent procedure or injection.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause right now.
- You do not need to try internet neck stretches, massage tools, traction, or spinal manipulation today.
- You do not need to decide about work, childcare, travel, or future treatment until you have had medical advice.

## Important reassurance
Many sudden neck pain episodes are caused by muscle spasm or irritation and improve. New severe neck pain with stiffness still deserves urgent advice because a small number of serious problems can start in a similar way.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on safety, warning signs, and getting you to the right level of care. Later decisions may need assessment and advice from a clinician or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not a diagnosis, medical advice, or professional advice. It does not replace urgent medical assessment if you are getting worse, feel seriously unwell, or are unsure.
