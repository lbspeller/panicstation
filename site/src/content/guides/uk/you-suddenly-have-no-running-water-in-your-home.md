# What to do if… you suddenly have no running water in your home (UK)

## Short answer
Test your cold kitchen tap to determine if the issue is internal or a network-wide outage. Contact your water supplier immediately if neighbours are also affected, or a plumber/landlord if the issue is limited to your property.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave any taps in the 'on' position while waiting for the supply to return.
- Don’t drink water from the bathroom taps or 'header tanks' unless you are certain they are mains-fed.
- Don’t use excessive force to turn a seized internal stop tap.
- Don’t use electric immersion heaters or central heating if you suspect the system is dry and could overheat.
- Don’t use bottled mineral water for baby formula without checking the label for high sodium or sulphate levels.
- Don’t wait more than 24 hours to demand alternative water (10 litres per person) from your supplier during a network outage.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the scope of the outage using the cold kitchen tap.**
   - **Check the cold tap in the kitchen first**, as this is usually connected directly to the rising main.
   - **Check with immediate neighbours** or look at street-level activity (e.g., water in the road) to see if the outage is local or regional.

2. **Verify the status of your internal stop tap.**
   - **Locate your internal stop tap**, typically found under the kitchen sink, in a hallway cupboard, or under the stairs.
   - **Ensure the valve is fully open** by turning it gently anti-clockwise; if it was recently closed for maintenance, it may just need reopening.

3. **Check for localized building issues if you live in a flat.**
   - **Contact the building manager or concierge** to ask if a communal pump has failed or if the water has been isolated for repairs elsewhere in the block.

4. **Consult your water supplier’s live incident map.**
   - **Search for your postcode on your water company's website** to check for burst mains or planned works in your area.
   - **Report the outage via the company’s emergency line** if your area is not already listed as having an incident.

5. **Isolate your heating systems if the outage is prolonged.**
   - **Switch off any electric immersion heaters** to prevent the heating element from burning out while the tank is empty.
   - **Check your boiler’s pressure gauge**; if it drops to zero, do not attempt to use the heating or hot water.

6. **Register for priority support if you have medical needs.**
   - **Inform the water company if anyone in the household is on the Priority Services Register (PSR)** or has a medical dependency on water (e.g., home dialysis).
   - **Request emergency doorstep delivery of bottled water** if you are unable to reach a communal collection point.

7. **Prepare bottled water for essential hygiene and nutrition.**
   - **Use only bottled or stored water for drinking** and cleaning teeth until the supply is confirmed as 'wholesome' by the regulator.
   - **Boil bottled water to at least 70°C** if using it to prepare baby formula, as bottled water is not sterile.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact location of a burst pipe in the street yourself.
- You do not need to clean or flush your pipes until the water company confirms the 'boil water' or 'do not drink' notices are lifted.
- You do not need to file a compensation claim immediately; focus on securing 10 litres of water per person per day first.

## Important reassurance
A sudden loss of water is frequently caused by a nearby burst pipe or an airlock, both of which are standard issues for water companies to resolve. Supply is usually restored within hours, and legal protections exist to ensure you are provided with alternative water if the outage lasts longer.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover technical plumbing repairs, private well maintenance, or long-term drought management.

## Important note
This information is not professional plumbing, legal, or medical advice. If you see water near electrical installations or suspect a gas leak related to a boiler fault, evacuate the area and contact emergency services.
