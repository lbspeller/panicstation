# What to do if… you realise you left your passport in a hotel safe and have to travel soon (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the hotel now and get the passport physically back into your hands today. If that is not realistic, check the official UK Emergency Travel Document route before you miss your chance to change your travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait until the morning of travel to contact the hotel; managers, security checks and safe access can take time.
- Do not report the passport lost or stolen unless you believe it is genuinely lost or stolen, because cancelling it can make it unusable even if it later turns up.
- Do not assume a photo, scan or passport number will be accepted for international travel.
- Do not rely on the carrier making an exception at check-in.
- Do not send passport details widely; share only the minimum needed with the hotel, a trusted collector, a courier, your carrier or an official service.
- Do not pay for an Emergency Travel Document unless you understand that your old passport will usually be automatically cancelled after payment.

## What to do now
1. **Call the hotel front desk and ask for the duty manager.** Say clearly: your passport is in the room safe, you travel tomorrow, and you need it released today. Ask what their secure guest-safe access procedure is and what identification they need from you.
2. **If you are still close enough, go back to the hotel in person today.** Take another form of ID if you have one, plus your booking details and room number, so the hotel can verify you as the guest.
3. **If you are not close enough, ask the hotel to find and secure the passport now.** Ask them to confirm in writing that they have opened the safe, found the passport, put it in a sealed envelope, and stored it securely at reception or in the hotel office.
4. **Arrange the fastest safe collection.** Ask the hotel what written authorisation and ID they require for a trusted person to collect it, or what courier procedure they will accept. Use tracking and keep the hotel’s written confirmation.
5. **Keep your proof in one place.** Save your hotel booking, the hotel’s written confirmation, your travel itinerary, courier details, and any messages naming the staff member handling it.
6. **Set a clear cutoff today.** If the hotel cannot confirm a realistic way for you to physically have the passport before you must leave for the airport, station or port, move to the backup plan.
7. **Check whether a UK Emergency Travel Document could work for your route.** It is for British nationals abroad who need urgent travel and cannot use their UK passport. It is usually valid for one single or return journey, and you can travel through a maximum of 5 countries. Before applying, check whether it will be accepted by the countries you will leave, enter and travel through, including any visa rules.
8. **Apply for an Emergency Travel Document only if retrieval is not realistic.** The online route is for people who have had a UK passport issued on or after 1 January 2006. If approved, it is usually ready to collect 2 working days after applying, but it can take longer in some cases, so next-day travel may still need changing.
9. **Tell your carrier once you know the plan.** If you will have the passport, ask whether anything else is needed after the delay. If you may use an Emergency Travel Document, ask what they require at check-in and be ready to rebook if the document timing or route does not fit.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on complaints, compensation or blame right now; getting the document back is the priority.
- You do not need to replace the passport if you recover it and it has not been cancelled or replaced.
- You do not need to make a police report unless you suspect theft, fraud, or your insurer or official process requires it.
- You do not need to solve every part of the journey now; decide only whether you can get the passport in time or need the official backup route.

## Important reassurance
This is a common rushed-checkout problem, and it is often fixable by calm escalation to the duty manager and a clear same-day deadline. Starting the backup route early does not mean you have failed; it gives you more options if the passport cannot reach you in time.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next 12–24 hours only. Later decisions about insurance, complaints, replacement documents or changed travel may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic or other professional advice. Hotel procedures, local rules, carrier requirements and border decisions can vary.
