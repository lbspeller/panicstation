# What to do if… you have chest discomfort along with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain (UK)

## Short answer
Call 999 now for an ambulance. Chest discomfort with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain can be a heart emergency even if you are not sure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself to A&E.
- Do not wait to see if it passes, especially if the pain is new, spreading, or comes with sweating, nausea, breathlessness, or feeling faint.
- Do not push through it, climb stairs, walk briskly, or try to finish a task first.
- Do not take extra or new painkillers to test whether it is serious.
- Do not take someone else’s prescription heart medicine.
- Do not go searching around for aspirin if it is not already nearby.

## What to do now
1. Stop what you are doing. Sit and rest in a comfortable supported position, such as sitting on a chair or sitting supported with your back raised.
2. Call 999 and say: “Chest discomfort with new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain.”
   - If you cannot get through on 999, call 112.
   - If you are alone, put the phone on speaker.
3. Unlock your front door if you can do this without effort, or ask someone else to do it. Move only as much as needed so paramedics can reach you.
4. If someone is with you, ask them to stay nearby, watch for changes, and wait by the entrance if that is easy to do.
5. If aspirin is already nearby and you are not allergic, chew and swallow one adult aspirin, 300mg, while waiting for the ambulance.
   - Do not take aspirin if you are under 16, allergic to aspirin, have been told not to take it, or are unsure whether it is safe for you. Ask the 999 call handler.
6. If you have prescribed angina medicine, such as GTN spray or tablets, use it exactly as you were told. Still follow the 999 call handler’s advice.
7. Keep ready, if it is easy:
   - the time the symptoms started, even roughly
   - your address and postcode
   - your current medicines
   - any allergies, especially aspirin
   - any known heart condition, recent surgery, pregnancy, or major bleeding history
8. Tell the 999 call handler straight away if symptoms get worse, you feel faint, breathing becomes harder, or the pain changes.
9. If the person becomes unresponsive and is not breathing normally, keep 999 on speaker. The call handler can guide CPR and may ask someone to fetch a defibrillator if one is nearby.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether it is definitely a heart attack, angina, indigestion, anxiety, or something else.
- Googling symptoms or checking forums.
- Calling your GP or NHS 111 first for this symptom pattern.
- Gathering paperwork, packing a bag, changing clothes, or notifying work.

## Important reassurance
Many people hesitate because symptoms do not feel dramatic, or because they feel like indigestion or anxiety. With chest discomfort plus new jaw, shoulder, or arm pain, it is safer to treat it as urgent and let clinicians check.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to keep you safer in the next minutes. Later decisions, tests, treatment, and follow-up need help from emergency clinicians or other appropriate specialists.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical or professional advice, and it cannot diagnose what is happening. If you have this symptom combination right now, call 999.
