# What to do if… a banking app update leaves your account marked unverified and transfers are blocked (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank immediately via the number on the back of your card or by dialling 159 to confirm if the block is a technical glitch or a mandatory security re-verification. Request a temporary payment workaround if you have urgent bills or essential spending needs.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt the same transfer multiple times as this can trigger automated fraud alerts or lead to duplicate debits.
- Do not follow links in SMS or email "alerts" claiming your account is unverified; these are frequently phishing attempts.
- Do not share your PIN, full password, or one-time passcodes (OTP) with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank's technical team.
- Do not download remote-access software (such as AnyDesk or TeamViewer) to "help" fix the app.
- Do not move money to a "safe account" suggested by any caller; banks will never ask you to do this.

## What to do now
1. **Identify exactly which services are restricted and which remain active.**  
   **Check if you can still perform low-risk actions such as viewing your balance, making a debit card payment in a shop, or withdrawing cash from an ATM.**  
   - List any payments due within the next 72 hours (e.g., rent, utilities, or loan repayments).  
   - Note the exact wording of any error messages or "unverified" banners.

2. **Check the bank's official service status and perform basic device troubleshooting.**  
   **Visit the bank’s official website or a verified service monitor to see if there is a known nationwide issue with the app update.**  
   - Ensure your phone’s "Date and Time" settings are set to "Automatic," as a mismatch can break security certificates.  
   - Attempt to log in via a web browser on a computer to see if the restriction is app-specific or account-wide.

3. **Contact the bank through a secure, verified channel.**  
   **Call the number on the back of your debit card or dial 159 (the UK’s secure fraud hotline) to speak with the bank's security or customer service team.**  
   - Ask: "Is the 'unverified' status due to a technical app error, a missing Know Your Customer (KYC) document, or a security hold?"  
   - If you are a business director, ask if this is related to the 2026 Companies House identity verification requirements.  
   - Request a clear timeline for when the verification will be processed.

4. **Request an emergency payment workaround for urgent liabilities.**  
   **If you have immediate bills to pay, ask the bank to facilitate the transfer via telephone banking or by visiting a branch with physical ID.**  
   - Ask if they can temporarily increase your cash withdrawal limit if card payments are also blocked.  
   - If a payment to a third party will be late, contact the payee immediately to explain it is a "banking technical restriction" and request they waive late fees.

5. **Review your recent transaction history for unauthorised activity.**  
   **Examine your statement for any small "test" transactions or withdrawals you do not recognise while you are on the phone with the bank.**  
   - If you see suspicious activity, ask the bank to "freeze" the account entirely and issue new credentials/cards.

6. **Initiate a formal complaint if the resolution is not immediate.**  
   **Ask the bank to log a formal complaint regarding "denial of access to payment services" to ensure your case is tracked under FCA-mandated timelines.**  
   - For payment service issues, banks should generally provide a final response within 15 business days.  
   - Keep a log of all call times, staff names, and reference numbers provided.

## What can wait
- You do not need to switch banks immediately; focus on restoring access to your existing funds first.
- You do not need to factory-reset your mobile device or purchase a new one.
- You do not need to involve the Financial Ombudsman Service until the bank has had at least 15 business days (or up to 8 weeks for non-payment issues) to resolve the complaint.

## Important reassurance
Account restrictions following an app update are often automated safety "trips" triggered by new security protocols. This does not usually mean your money is lost or that you have been a victim of a crime. Banks are required to verify identity periodically, and these "unverified" flags are a standard, albeit disruptive, part of regulatory compliance.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK retail banking customers facing account restrictions after a mobile app update. It does not cover long-term credit score repair or complex commercial banking disputes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute financial or legal advice. UK banks have broad powers to restrict accounts for regulatory or security reasons. Always use official contact methods found on your physical bank card or the bank’s verified website.
