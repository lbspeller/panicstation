# What to do if… you find your front door not fully latched when you are certain you closed it (UK)

## Short answer
Do not enter the property; move to a safe distance immediately and call 999 if you suspect a crime is in progress. Only re-enter once you have verified the premises are secure or the police have arrived.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step inside to "check the rooms" yourself.
- Do not shout into the house or announce your presence.
- Do not touch the door handle, lock, or any visible evidence to avoid contaminating DNA or fingerprints.
- Do not try to "clear the house" or look for intruders.
- Do not confront anyone you see exiting or entering the building.
- Do not delete or reset doorbell camera or smart lock logs before they are saved.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe position away from the threshold immediately.** Get to a spot where you are not visible from the doorway, such as a neighbour’s home, a locked vehicle, or further down the street.
2. **Call 999 if you suspect an intruder is still inside or if there are signs of forced entry.** Tell the operator you have returned home to find your door open or unlatched and you suspect a burglary in progress.
   - Note any signs of damage to the lock, frame, or door.
   - Mention if you hear noises or see lights on that should be off.
3. **Contact 101 or report online if there are no signs of a break-in but you still feel unsafe.** Use the non-emergency line if the door appears to have simply failed to latch and you want a police record or advice before entering.
4. **Listen and observe from a distance without approaching the door.** Note any unusual smells, such as cigarette smoke or unfamiliar perfume, or any movement in the windows.
5. **Check your smart home or doorbell camera app from your phone while remaining outside.** Look for "motion detected" or "door opened" events that occurred while you were away.
6. **Wait for a second person to arrive if you are unsure about entering.** Ask a trusted neighbour or a friend to stand with you outside; if you live in a managed building, contact the on-site security or concierge from your safe position.
7. **Document the state of the door by taking photos from a distance.** Capture the position of the door and any marks on the wood or metal without touching the surfaces.
8. **Keep your phone in your hand with the emergency number ready if you choose to enter.** Ensure you have a clear, unobstructed exit path behind you and leave the building immediately if you find any interior rooms disturbed.

## What can wait
- Investigating if the door frame has shifted due to weather or temperature changes.
- Booking a locksmith to realign the strike plate or replace the latch.
- Reviewing hours of historical CCTV footage.
- Checking for missing items or contacting your insurance provider.

## Important reassurance
It is common for doors to fail to latch due to a "soft close" where the bolt does not fully engage, or due to atmospheric pressure changes in a building. Taking these precautions is a standard safety response and does not mean you are overreacting; it is better to be outside and wrong than inside and at risk.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate minutes after discovering an unlatched door. It does not cover forensic reporting, insurance claims, or long-term security upgrades.

## Important note
This guide is for information only and does not constitute legal or security advice. Always prioritize your physical safety over property. For emergencies in the UK, dial **999**. For non-emergencies, dial **101**. If calling from outside the UK to report an issue at your UK property, use +44 20 7230 1212 (Metropolitan Police) or the specific international number for your local force (e.g., +44 141 308 1070 for Police Scotland).
