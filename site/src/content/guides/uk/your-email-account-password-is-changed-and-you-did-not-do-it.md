# What to do if… your email account password is changed and you did not do it (UK)

## Short answer
Sign in to your email account through an official web browser (not a mobile app) and immediately check for unauthorized forwarding rules or filters. Secure the account by changing the password and enabling 2-step verification from the provider's security settings.

## Do not do these things
- Don't delete "Security Alert" emails or "Account Sign-in" notifications; these contain timestamps and IP addresses you may need later.
- Don't click links inside suspicious emails to "fix" the account; always go directly to the provider's official website.
- Don't empty the Trash or Deleted Items folders in an attempt to "clean up" the mailbox.
- Don't continue using third-party mail apps on your phone or tablet until the primary account is secured via webmail.
- Don't assume the deletions will stop on their own if you just wait.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the account by disabling sync.**
   - **Switch your mobile devices to airplane mode or turn off Wi-Fi/data.**
   - **Close all mail applications on computers and tablets.**
   - This prevents a compromised app or sync error from continuing to delete messages while you work on the web version.

2. **Access the account via a secure web browser.**
   - **Log in to your email provider’s official website (e.g., gmail.com, outlook.com) on a trusted computer.**
   - **Navigate to the "Settings" or "All Settings" menu.**

3. **Audit and remove unauthorized rules and forwarding.**
   - **Locate the "Rules," "Filters," or "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" section.**
   - **Delete any rules that include the actions "Delete," "Move to Trash," "Mark as Read," or "Archive" that you did not create.**
   - **Check the "Forwarding" section for any unrecognized email addresses and disable forwarding immediately.**
   - **Verify if "Sweep" or "Auto-archive" features have been enabled without your consent.**

4. **Secure account access and sessions.**
   - **Review "Recent Activity" or "Security Events" for sign-ins from unrecognized locations.**
   - **Select the option to "Sign out of all other sessions" or "Log out of all devices."**
   - **Update your password to a strong, unique phrase (e.g., three random words) that you do not use elsewhere.**
   - **Ensure 2-step verification (2SV) or Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is active.**

5. **Execute recovery of deleted items.**
   - **Open the "Trash" or "Deleted Items" folder and select the messages to "Restore" or "Move to Inbox."**
   - **Look for a link at the top of the Deleted Items folder labeled "Recover items deleted from this folder" if you are using Outlook/Hotmail.**
   - If using Gmail, use the "Gmail Message Recovery Tool" on the official Google Support site if the Trash has been emptied.

6. **Report the incident to the appropriate UK authorities.**
   - **If you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report the cyber incident to the Report Fraud service online at reportfraud.police.uk or by calling 0300 123 2040.**
   - **If you are in Scotland, call Police Scotland on 101 or contact the Cyber and Fraud Centre Scotland on 0800 1670 623 for recovery support.**
   - **Forward any suspicious emails that preceded the deletions to the NCSC at report@phishing.gov.uk.**

## What can wait
- You don't need to notify all your contacts immediately; secure the account first.
- You don't need to rebuild your folder structure or reorganize your archive right now.
- You don't need to decide whether to delete the account entirely until you have regained control.

## Important reassurance
Sudden email deletion is a known tactic used by unauthorized users to hide their activity or bypass security notifications. By moving to a web-based browser and checking your rules, you are taking the most direct path to stopping the loss. Feeling overwhelmed or panicked is a standard response to digital loss; following these physical steps is the fastest way to stabilize the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover the forensic analysis of how the account was accessed or the secondary securing of other accounts that use this email for password resets.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute technical or legal advice. If your account is part of a corporate or government network, you must prioritize contacting your internal IT department or Data Protection Officer. Always use official provider recovery channels.
