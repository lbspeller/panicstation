# What to do if… your photo library has duplicates and you are worried about deleting the originals (UK)

## Short answer
Stop deleting, moving, or importing photos for now. Make a separate backup or export that you will not edit, then use the photo app’s built-in duplicate merge tools in small batches.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t select all duplicates and delete them in one go.
- Don’t empty Recently Deleted, Bin, or Trash while you are unsure what happened.
- Don’t use a third-party duplicate remover as your first move.
- Don’t repeatedly switch sync on and off across devices; it may make it harder to tell which copy is current.
- Don’t move or rename large photo folders while a cloud photo service is still syncing.
- Don’t keep importing the same phone, memory card, DCIM folder, or backup folder into the same library.

## What to do now
1. **Pause changes to the photo library.**
   - Pick one device to inspect from, preferably a computer with enough storage.
   - On the other devices, avoid deleting, importing, renaming folders, or changing sync settings for now.
   - Leave the visible duplicates in place until you have a safety copy.

2. **Make a safety copy you will not clean up.**
   - **iCloud Photos on Mac:** open Photos, choose Photos > Settings, select iCloud, then choose Download Originals to this Mac before exporting or cleaning up.
   - **Google Photos:** use Google Takeout with Google Photos selected, then save the archive somewhere separate from the library you plan to fix.
   - **Windows or external drive:** copy the whole photo folder or photo library to an external drive or a clearly named folder such as DO_NOT_DELETE_PHOTO_BACKUP_2026-05-27.
   - Do not use this safety copy for tidying. It is there so you can recover if the cleanup goes wrong.

3. **Check whether the duplicates are real separate files.**
   - Compare 5 to 10 duplicate-looking pairs before taking action across the whole library.
   - Look at filename, file size, date taken, date imported, edits, album location, and whether one copy is only a lower-resolution preview.
   - Treat storage warnings as a clue, not proof, because cloud-optimised storage can make local storage numbers misleading.

4. **Use built-in duplicate tools before manual deletion.**
   - **iPhone:** open Photos, tap Collections, tap Utilities, tap Duplicates, then use Merge for a small set first.
   - If Duplicates does not appear on iPhone, leave the phone locked and connected to power so it can finish indexing and finding duplicates.
   - **Mac Photos:** open Photos, click Duplicates under Utilities in the sidebar, select a small set, then click Merge.
   - After merging on Mac, do not empty Recently Deleted while you are still checking the result.

5. **Stop the most likely duplication loop.**
   - If this began after a new phone, new computer, restore, migration, or manual import, stop importing from the old source for now.
   - Check whether the same photos are being added from two places, such as the phone camera roll and a copied DCIM folder.
   - If OneDrive is involved and duplicated files have a computer name added to the filename, check Microsoft’s official OneDrive duplicate-file guidance before deleting anything.
   - Wait for one sync cycle to settle before judging whether the duplicates are still multiplying.

6. **If the account may have been accessed by someone else, secure it before cleaning.**
   - Change the password for the photo account you actually use, such as Apple, Google, or Microsoft.
   - Turn on two-step verification or multi-factor authentication where available.
   - Review signed-in devices or sessions and sign out devices you do not recognise.

## What can wait
- You do not need to permanently delete anything today.
- You do not need to decide which copy is best across the whole library right now.
- You do not need to fix albums, dates, filenames, faces, or shared albums until the main library is stable.
- You can leave duplicates visible for a while once you have a separate backup or export you trust.

## Important reassurance
Duplicate photo libraries are often caused by sync changes, migrations, restores, or re-imports. Slowing down is protective: a copied backup plus small built-in merge steps is safer than trying to make the library perfect immediately.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent irreversible loss and stabilise the situation. Later decisions, such as repairing a damaged library, restoring from backup, or untangling several cloud accounts, may need specialist help or the platform’s official support.
