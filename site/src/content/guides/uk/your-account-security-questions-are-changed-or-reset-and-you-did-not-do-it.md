# What to do if… your account security questions are changed or reset and you did not do it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop making changes and avoid "resetting" or reinstalling the browser. First, verify you are signed into the correct browser profile and check if sync has been paused or logged out.

## Do not do these things
- Don't uninstall or reinstall the browser yet, as this can delete the local "Default" profile folder that contains your data.
- Don't use the "Reset settings" or "Refresh" buttons, as these are designed to remove extensions and customisations.
- Don't attempt to bypass "Managed by your organisation" locks on work or school devices.
- Don't enter banking or sensitive passwords if you see unknown extensions, a changed homepage, or strange pop-ups.
- Don't run "system cleaners" or "registry fixers" that claim to recover lost browser data; these often delete profile caches.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze the situation immediately.**
   - **Close all browser windows except this one.**
   - **Do not clear your browsing history or cache**, even if they appear empty.
   - **If the device is managed by an employer**, stop here and contact your IT helpdesk before attempting manual recovery.

2. **Verify your active browser profile.**
   - **Click the profile icon in the top-right corner of the browser window.**
   - **Check if you are currently in "Guest" mode or a profile named "Person 1."**
   - **Switch back to your primary email-linked profile if it appears in the list.**

3. **Locate your "reference copy" of the data.**
   - **Check your phone, tablet, or another laptop where you use the same browser.**
   - **If the bookmarks and history are visible there, do not sign out of that device.** This is your backup copy.
   - **If the secondary device is also empty, do not force a sync**, as it may overwrite any remaining data on the server.

4. **Identify and fix "Sync Paused" errors.**
   - **Look for a small exclamation mark or a "Paused" label next to your profile icon.**
   - **Sign back in with your primary account credentials if prompted.** Data often reappears within 60 seconds of a successful re-authentication.
   - **Verify that "Sync everything" or specifically "Bookmarks/Favourites" and "Extensions" are toggled to ON in the Sync settings.**

5. **Use built-in restoration tools if profiles are correct.**
   - **Firefox:** Press `Ctrl+Shift+B` to open the Library, click "Import and Backup," then "Restore" to select an automatic backup from a previous date.
   - **Chrome/Edge:** Right-click the Bookmarks/Favourites bar and check for "Other Bookmarks" or "Imported" folders that may have been moved during the update.
   - **Edge:** Check the "Favourites" menu for a "Deleted favourites" folder where items may have been moved by a sync conflict.

6. **Check for disabled extensions.**
   - **Navigate to the Extensions/Add-ons page (e.g., `chrome://extensions`).**
   - **Look for extensions that are listed but have their toggle switch turned OFF.** Browsers sometimes disable extensions during updates if they require new permissions.

7. **Report suspected cyber interference.**
   - **If you suspect your data was removed by a third party or malware, change your account password from a different, clean device.**
   - **Report cyber crime or fraud in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland to Report Fraud online or by calling 0300 123 2040.**
   - **Report cyber crime in Scotland to Police Scotland online or by calling 101.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to switch to a different browser.
- You do not need to manually re-add hundreds of bookmarks until you have checked the profile folders.
- You do not need to purchase "data recovery" software; browser data is usually either in a profile folder or on the sync server.

## Important reassurance
In the vast majority of cases, your data is not "deleted"; it is simply "disconnected." Browser updates often reset the "active profile" or sign you out for security reasons. By not clicking "Reset" or "Uninstall," you have likely preserved the files needed to restore everything once you are back in the correct profile.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital data loss after a software update. It does not cover manual file-system recovery from "User Data" folders or deep-level registry edits, which should only be attempted after these basic steps fail.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional technical support or legal advice. If you are using a device provided by your employer or an educational institution, you must follow their specific IT security policies. If you believe your browser data was lost due to a targeted hack or criminal activity, prioritize securing your primary email and banking accounts immediately.
