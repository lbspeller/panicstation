# What to do if… your cloud notes or documents suddenly appear empty after you sign in (UK)

## Short answer
Stop changing anything in the notes or document app, record what you can see, and check that you are signed into the exact account that normally holds the files.

## Do not do these things
- Do not empty Trash, Bin, Recently Deleted, Recycle Bin, or any recovery area.
- Do not move, rename, bulk-delete, or “tidy up” folders to reset the account.
- Do not keep opening, saving, or editing files to test the problem, because this may overwrite recoverable versions or spread a bad sync state.
- Do not uninstall the app, wipe a device, or remove an account yet if that device may still hold local copies.
- Do not give passwords, verification codes, remote access, or payment to anyone who contacts you unexpectedly claiming they can restore the files.
- Do not assume the files are permanently gone before checking the account, filters, shared areas, deletion areas, and restore tools.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the sync situation getting worse.**
   - Stop editing or creating notes and documents in the affected service.
   - On devices that may hold local copies, pause syncing if the app offers it. If you cannot pause sync, briefly disconnect that affected device from Wi-Fi while you check from one safe browser session.
   - Keep at least one device or browser available so you can use the provider’s official recovery pages.

2. **Record what you are seeing.**
   - Screenshot the empty view, the account email or username, any error banner, and the current date and time.
   - Note which devices are affected and whether you recently changed password, changed phone, added a work or school account, left a shared workspace, or installed a sync app.

3. **Check the exact account before doing recovery work.**
   - Open the account or profile menu and compare the exact email address, Apple Account, Microsoft account, Google account, Dropbox account, or work/school account with the one you normally use.
   - Check for similar addresses, old accounts, family accounts, work profiles, school profiles, and browser profiles.
   - If this is the only device that may still hold local-only copies, do not sign out yet. First check whether the missing items are still visible offline or in the device’s local app storage.

4. **Check that you are looking in the right place.**
   - Clear filters such as Offline only, Starred, Shared, Recent, a single notebook, a single folder, or a single workspace.
   - Check shared drives, team spaces, organisation tenants, workspaces, notebooks, and folders that were shared with you.
   - Search for one distinctive file name, note title, or phrase that should be inside a missing document.

5. **Check the built-in recovery areas.**
   - Look in Trash, Bin, Recently Deleted, Recycle Bin, deleted notes, and account activity.
   - Restore only clearly recognised urgent items first if you need them now.
   - Do not empty any recovery area, even if it looks cluttered or confusing.

6. **Check for a mass change or account problem.**
   - Look at activity, audit, or recent changes if the service provides it.
   - Look for unfamiliar sign-ins, new devices, new connected apps, changed recovery details, forwarding rules, mass deletions, moves, renames, or permission changes.
   - If the account is managed by work or school, contact IT or the account administrator quickly and ask whether they can see deleted items, version history, audit logs, or server-side restore options.

7. **Use the provider’s official restore tools.**
   - Use the service’s own recovery features such as file restore, version history, restore previous version, rewind, or deleted-file recovery.
   - Read the provider’s warning screen before confirming a bulk restore, because some restores can undo later changes.
   - If only one document is wrong or blank, look for version history before trying a whole-account restore.

8. **If you suspect the account was taken over, secure the account before making large restores.**
   - Use the provider’s official account recovery page.
   - Check the email account linked to recovery for unknown forwarding rules, filters, or recovery changes.
   - Change the password to a unique one, turn on 2-step verification, sign out other sessions, remove unknown devices, and revoke unknown connected apps.

9. **Contact provider support with a clear recovery request.**
   - Give the account email, screenshots, when the files last appeared normal, when the empty view started, affected folders or notebooks, and any suspicious activity.
   - Ask specifically about deleted-file recovery, version history, account restore, shared-folder recovery, and server-side recovery.
   - Keep the case number and avoid making further changes while support is checking.

10. **If this followed a scam, phishing message, or unauthorised access, report it through UK routes.**
   - Forward suspicious emails to report@phishing.gov.uk.
   - Forward suspicious texts to 7726.
   - If you have been affected by fraud or cyber crime and live in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report it to Report Fraud.
   - If you live in Scotland, report fraud or cyber crime to Police Scotland on 101.
   - If there is immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to change cloud providers, buy recovery software, rebuild your filing system, or contact everyone you have ever shared with.
- You do not need to rename folders, reorganise documents, or start a replacement notes system until you have checked the account, deletion areas, restore tools, and account security.
- You do not need to work out the full cause before protecting recoverable versions and asking the provider or administrator about restore options.

## Important reassurance
An empty cloud account view after sign-in is often caused by the wrong account, a filter, a shared-space change, a sync issue, or a recoverable deletion. Slowing down now gives you a better chance of finding the files without overwriting versions or confirming unwanted changes.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and protect recoverability. Later decisions may need help from the cloud provider, a workplace or school administrator, cyber security support, or specialist IT help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, cyber security, or professional advice. Follow the official recovery process for your provider and get specialist help if the account, device, or data is important or compromised.
