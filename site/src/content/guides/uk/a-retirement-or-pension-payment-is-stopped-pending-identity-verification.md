# What to do if… a retirement or pension payment is stopped pending identity verification (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your pension payer directly using a verified phone number or portal to confirm the exact documents required and the secure method for submission.

## Do not do these things
- Do not follow links in unexpected texts or emails that ask you to upload identity documents or "verify" your account.
- Do not pay any "release fees" or "administrative charges" to have a pension payment restarted; legitimate providers do not charge for identity verification.
- Do not send original documents (like passports or birth certificates) through the post unless specifically requested and sent via a tracked, secure service.
- Do not share your full bank login details or PIN with anyone claiming to be from your pension provider or the DWP.
- Do not cancel essential bill payments (rent, mortgage, utilities) without first contacting the billers to explain the temporary income disruption.

## What to do now
1. **Verify that the payment has officially been paused.** Check your bank statement to confirm the payment was not simply delayed by a bank holiday or weekend. Look for any letters or secure messages in your pension portal regarding "security reviews," "verification," or "information requests."
2. **Contact the correct authority based on your pension type.** 
   - **State Pension (England, Scotland, Wales):** Call the Pension Service on 0800 731 0469.
   - **State Pension (Northern Ireland):** Call the Northern Ireland Pension Centre on 0800 587 0892.
   - **State Pension (Living Abroad):** Call the International Pension Centre on +44 (0) 191 218 7777.
   - **Private, Workplace, or Annuity Payments:** Use the contact details found on your most recent annual statement or the provider's official website.
3. **Ask the representative specific clarifying questions.** 
   - Is identity verification the only reason for the stop?
   - What specific documents are accepted (e.g., passport, driving licence, utility bills)?
   - Can documents be uploaded to a secure portal or must they be sent by post?
   - If sending by post, are "certified copies" acceptable instead of originals?
4. **Submit the required information using the most secure method available.** **Prioritize using an official, encrypted provider portal or app upload over email or post.** If you must use the post, send documents via "Special Delivery" or a similar tracked service and keep the receipt. 
5. **Request a "Hardship" or "Urgent Payment" review if you cannot meet essential costs.** **Inform the provider immediately if the stop has left you without funds for food, heating, or housing.** Some providers and the DWP have emergency protocols to expedite verification or issue partial payments in cases of extreme financial vulnerability.
6. **Report any suspected fraud or impersonation attempts immediately.** **If you believe someone has attempted to hijack your pension account, contact the provider's fraud department.** You should also report the incident to Action Fraud (Report Fraud) at 0300 123 2040 (England, Wales, and NI) or Police Scotland via 101.
7. **Contact MoneyHelper for free, impartial guidance on pension issues.** **Call the MoneyHelper pension helpline on 0800 011 3797 if the provider is unhelpful or the process is unclear.** They can help you understand your rights and the steps needed to resolve administration errors.
8. **Initiate a formal complaint if the delay exceeds reasonable timeframes.** **Ask for the "Internal Dispute Resolution Procedure" (IDRP) for workplace schemes or the standard complaints process for private providers.** If the issue remains unresolved after 8 weeks, you can escalate to the Pensions Ombudsman (for administration) or the Financial Ombudsman Service (for regulated personal pensions).

## What can wait
- You do not need to make any decisions about transferring or consolidating your pensions while a verification hold is active.
- You do not need to update your bank details unless the provider explicitly confirms that a bank mismatch caused the hold.
- You do not need to appoint a legal representative; most identity holds are administrative and can be resolved through direct communication.

## Important reassurance
Identity verification holds are a standard fraud prevention measure and do not necessarily mean you have done anything wrong or that your money is at risk. These checks are often triggered by a change of address, a new bank account, or a routine security update across the provider's system. Once the correct documents are processed, missed payments are typically backdated and paid in full.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for identity-related payment stops only. It does not cover disputes regarding pension value, investment performance, or long-term financial planning.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Timelines for restarting payments vary between the DWP and private providers. If you are in immediate financial crisis, contact your local Citizens Advice or a debt advice charity.
