# What to do if… your electric vehicle shows very low range and the charger you planned to use is unavailable (UK)

## Short answer
Reduce speed and cabin power immediately, then navigate to the nearest reachable charger—including slower AC units—rather than attempting to reach a distant rapid hub. If you cannot reach a safe charging point, stop in a safe location and contact your breakdown provider before the battery reaches zero.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to reach a distant rapid charger if your range is already below 5%; prioritize any nearby plug.
- Do not maintain high speeds; driving faster than 50mph significantly accelerates battery depletion.
- Do not keep heating or air conditioning on high power; these are secondary loads that drain range.
- Do not attempt to "reset" the car by repeatedly turning it on and off, as this consumes 12V and high-voltage power.
- Do not allow an unauthorized vehicle to tow your EV; most require a flatbed or specific recovery dollies to prevent motor damage.
- Do not stop in a live lane of a motorway or A-road unless the vehicle has completely lost power and cannot be moved.

## What to do now
1. **Reduce immediate energy consumption:**
   - **Switch the vehicle to its most efficient mode (e.g., Eco, Eco+, or Range Mode).**
   - **Turn off all climate control systems, including air conditioning, cabin heating, and heated seats/steering wheel.**
   - **Maintain a steady, lower speed (typically 40–50 mph) and avoid rapid acceleration or heavy regenerative braking if the road is slippery.**

2. **Identify the nearest alternative charging point:**
   - **Use your vehicle’s navigation or a charging app to search for any charger within your current displayed range.**
   - **Prioritize "slow" or "fast" AC chargers (7kW/22kW) in car parks or hotels if rapid chargers (50kW+) are unavailable or out of range.**
   - **Identify a second "backup" option within range in case your new primary choice is also blocked or faulty.**

3. **Utilize the mandatory operator helpline if at a faulty unit:**
   - **Locate the 24/7 helpline number displayed on the charger (required by UK Public Charge Point Regulations 2023).**
   - **Call the operator immediately to request a remote reset if the unit is failing to start or recognize payment.**
   - **Limit this attempt to five minutes; if the charger cannot be authorized quickly, move to your backup location.**

4. **Follow safety protocols if stopping on a motorway or major road:**
   - **Exit the motorway at the next junction or services if possible.**
   - **If you must stop, pull into an Emergency Area (marked with orange signs) or onto the hard shoulder.**
   - **Exit the vehicle through the left-hand (passenger) door only.**
   - **Wait behind the safety barrier, well away from the carriageway, and keep your hazard lights on.**

5. **Contact professional assistance:**
   - **Call 999 immediately if you are stopped in a live lane or a location where you feel in danger.**
   - **Call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 for assistance on the strategic road network (motorways and major A-roads) if you are in a safe pause point.**
   - **Phone your breakdown recovery provider and state clearly that you are in an "EV critical battery" situation.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to report the faulty charger to the local council or network owner right now.
- You do not need to calculate the cost difference between different charging networks.
- You do not need to troubleshoot the vehicle's software or app connectivity beyond one restart attempt.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden surge of stress when a planned charging stop fails. Charging infrastructure can be unreliable, and "range anxiety" is a recognized response to these technical failures. Most modern EVs have a small "buffer" below 0%, and following speed-reduction steps is the most effective way to utilize remaining energy.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Long-term route planning, charger reliability reporting, and manufacturer-specific "limp mode" recovery should be addressed once the vehicle is safely connected to power or recovered.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the safety instructions in your vehicle's manual or the directions provided by emergency services. Always prioritize your physical safety over the state of the vehicle's battery. Motorway breakdown is a high-risk situation; follow National Highways safety protocols "Go Left, Get Safe, Get Help" at all times.
