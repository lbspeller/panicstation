# What to do if… a car hire company refuses your driving licence and you have no backup plan (UK)

## Short answer
Pause the rental, get the exact refusal reason in writing if possible, and switch to a safe no-car plan for today while you check what document or record the company is asking for.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue on principle or accuse staff of fraud or bad faith; it can reduce the chance of a manager review or a practical workaround.
- Don’t pay for or download an “instant online international driving permit” from an unknown site; it may not be recognised at a rental counter.
- Don’t leave your passport, driving licence, or other original documents as “collateral”.
- Don’t drive anyway if the rental is refused or you are told your licence is not acceptable.
- Don’t cancel hotels, flights, trains, or tours in a rush until you have checked what can be changed or refunded.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the refusal reason in one sentence.** Say: “Is this because local law requires an International Driving Permit or official translation, or is it your company policy at this location?” Ask them to show you the requirement in their system or rental terms for that pickup country.
2. **Check for a fixable mismatch.** Confirm the name on your booking matches your passport and licence, your licence is physical and unexpired, and you are showing the right document. If you have a Northern Ireland licence, ask whether they need to see the paper counterpart as well.
3. **Ask for one calm escalation.** Ask a supervisor to check whether your UK photocard licence is acceptable for that country and whether a nearby branch of the same company can honour the booking.
4. **Offer a driving-record check code if they need licence details.**
   - For licences issued in England, Wales or Scotland, use the GOV.UK “view or share your driving licence information” service to create a check code. Check codes are valid for 21 days.
   - For Northern Ireland licences, use the nidirect service if you already have access to your account and identity verification. NI check codes are also valid for 21 days.
5. **Ask what happens to charges and card holds.** Before leaving the desk, ask them to confirm what happens to any no-show charge, daily rental charge, deposit, excess hold, or card pre-authorisation. If you booked through a broker or online travel agent, ask the desk to record the refusal reason on the reservation.
6. **Move to a no-car plan for the next few hours.** Use official airport transport, a licensed taxi, public transport, a hotel shuttle, or your accommodation’s recommended transfer. If you are tired or arriving late, book one nearby night before trying to rebuild the whole trip.
7. **Check whether an IDP is actually required.** Use the UK government driving abroad guidance to check the destination country and the type of IDP that may be needed. Also check the rental company’s own terms, because a company can ask for documents even where local rules are less clear.
8. **Assume you cannot get a UK IDP instantly while already abroad.** UK IDPs are obtained in person from certain PayPoint shops, and you must live in Great Britain or Northern Ireland and hold a full UK driving licence. If you cannot get the right document in time, reroute by train, coach, taxi, private transfer, or a local driver rather than continuing the counter dispute.
9. **Keep only the records you need for later.** Save the booking, refusal email or desk note, screenshots of any written policy they allow you to photograph, names or reference numbers, and receipts for alternative transport or one night’s accommodation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain formally, pursue compensation, or leave reviews.
- You do not need to prove who is at fault at the counter; focus on getting safe transport and clear records.
- You do not need to re-plan the whole trip immediately; make the next 24 hours safe and workable first.

## Important reassurance
This can happen even to organised travellers because country rules, rental-company policies, licence formats, and IDP requirements do not always line up cleanly. Getting yourself safely away from the counter and preserving the refusal reason is enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment a rental counter refuses your licence. Later decisions may need the destination country’s rules, your booking terms, your travel insurer, your card provider, or specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, travel, or professional advice. Requirements vary by country, licence type, length of stay, and rental company policy, so rely on official government guidance and the written terms for your booking.
