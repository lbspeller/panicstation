# What to do if… someone insists you consented to sex but you remember saying no (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a calmer, safer place and talk to a specialist sexual violence support service or a Sexual Assault Referral Centre before you respond to the other person.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue your case by text, social media, or voice note right now.
- Do not meet them privately “to talk it out” or accept a private apology meeting.
- Do not post your story publicly while you are still in shock.
- Do not pressure yourself to decide today what to call it or whether to report.
- If you may want options later, try not to delete messages, call logs, photos, or app data in a panic.
- If this happened recently and you can avoid it, try not to wash clothes or bedding before asking a SARC or specialist service for advice.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** If you are with them or feel unsafe, move to a public place, a trusted person’s home, or somewhere with other people. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
2. **Stop contact for now if you can do that safely.** Mute, block, or do not reply. If a short boundary feels safer, use something simple such as: “Do not contact me.” Do not explain or debate.
3. **Tell one safe person what you need right now.** A simple message is enough: “Something happened. I need you to stay with me, help me get home, or help me make a call.” You do not need to give details.
4. **Contact specialist support. You can do this without reporting to police.**
   - **England and Wales, age 16+:** Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 Support Line **0808 500 2222**, by phone or online chat.
   - **Scotland:** Rape Crisis Scotland helpline **08088 01 03 02**, every day, 5pm to midnight.
   - **Northern Ireland:** Domestic and Sexual Abuse Helpline **0808 802 1414**, 24/7.
5. **Consider a Sexual Assault Referral Centre.** A SARC can offer medical care, emotional support, and information about options, whether or not you want police involvement.
6. **Get urgent healthcare if you have injuries, pain, bleeding, or feel medically at risk.** You can go to **A&E** or use **NHS 111** for urgent medical advice. You can ask about pregnancy risk, emergency contraception, STI testing, or other time-sensitive care without having to prove what happened.
7. **Make a short private note for yourself.** Write the date and time you are writing it, who was involved, where it happened, the rough time window, what you remember saying or doing, and what happened immediately after. Keep it somewhere private.
8. **Preserve what is already there without doing anything elaborate.** If you may want options later, keep existing messages and call logs, and keep any relevant clothing unwashed if that is still possible. If you have already washed, changed, or deleted something, you can still seek support and care.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to report to police, and when.
- Writing a perfect account or collecting everything.
- Convincing the other person, their friends, or anyone else.
- Replying to accusations, threats, or attempts to rewrite what happened.
- Telling wider friends, family, your workplace, or posting publicly.
- Any big relationship decisions today, unless immediate safety means you need distance.

## Important reassurance
It is common to doubt yourself when someone insists you consented, especially if you froze, went quiet, tried to keep the situation safe, or your memory feels patchy. Those reactions do not mean you agreed. You deserve support, and you do not have to face the next steps alone.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: get safer, reduce contact and pressure, and connect with specialist support or medical care. Later decisions about reporting, statements, complaints, legal options, or ongoing support can be made at your pace with specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**. If you feel at risk of self-harm or cannot keep yourself safe, seek urgent help now through **999** or **A&E**.
