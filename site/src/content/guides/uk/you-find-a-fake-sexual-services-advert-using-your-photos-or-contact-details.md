# What to do if… you find a fake sexual services advert using your photos or contact details (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a private, safer pause, save the URL and basic details, and report the post for removal without engaging with whoever posted it. Only screenshot intimate images if you are sure everyone shown is over 18.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t contact the poster to argue, threaten, or negotiate.
- Don’t pay money, send gift cards, or share more photos or videos to make it stop.
- Don’t click advert links, verification links, or chat buttons on the page.
- Don’t post the advert publicly on your own social media accounts.
- Don’t delete messages, URLs, or account names before saving the basic details you may need.
- Don’t screenshot, record, download, or share any sexual image if the person shown may be under 18.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and reduce the incoming stress.**  
   Move somewhere private if you can. Put your phone on Do Not Disturb, silence unknown callers, and step away from social media for a few minutes. If someone is outside, has your address, is threatening immediate harm, or you feel in immediate danger, call 999.

2. **Save the minimum proof safely.**  
   Copy the page URL, username, account name, phone number shown, and the date and time you found it. Screenshot the advert only if it does not show a sexual image of anyone who may be under 18. If it may involve someone under 18, save the URL and report it instead of copying the image.

3. **Report the post where it is hosted.**  
   Use the website or app’s report, abuse, privacy, impersonation, harassment, or personal information route. Include that your photos or contact details are being used to advertise sexual services without your consent. Keep any report or ticket number.

4. **Use Report Harmful Content if you are unsure how to report it.**  
   Report Harmful Content can help you find the right reporting route for online harms, including impersonation, harassment, privacy violation, adult content on social media, and intimate image abuse.

5. **If intimate or sexual images of you are involved, use specialist support.**  
   If you are an adult, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for support with takedown requests and next steps. If you still have the image or video, StopNCII may help reduce re-uploads on participating platforms by creating a digital fingerprint of the file on your device; it cannot remove images from the whole internet.

6. **If you are being threatened, blackmailed, stalked, or repeatedly targeted, report it to police.**  
   If it is not an emergency, use 101 or your local police force’s online reporting. Use plain wording such as: “Someone is impersonating me online and advertising sexual services using my photos or contact details. I am receiving unwanted sexual contact, threats, or harassment.” Keep the reference number.

7. **Protect your contact channels for the next day.**  
   Turn on spam filtering or silence unknown callers. Consider a temporary voicemail message that does not say your name. If your number is being overwhelmed, ask your mobile provider about nuisance-call controls or a number change after you have saved the basic details and submitted key reports.

8. **Tell one trusted person what is happening.**  
   Choose someone calm who can sit with you, help track URLs, help submit reports, or be nearby if you contact police.

9. **If you are under 18, or any sexual image may show someone under 18, use child-safety routes.**  
   Do not share the image further. If you are under 18 and a sexual image or video of you is online, use Childline Report Remove. If an adult is pressuring, threatening, grooming, or exploiting a child, report to CEOP. If you find child sexual abuse imagery online, including AI-generated child sexual abuse imagery, report the URL to the Internet Watch Foundation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to pursue legal action, tell your employer, or explain everything to everyone.
- You do not need to reply to strangers or prove anything to people contacting you.
- You do not need to search the whole internet for every copy right now.
- You do not need to make a permanent decision about your phone number before the first reports are submitted.

## Important reassurance
This is not your fault. People who do this often rely on shock, shame, and pressure to make you act fast or stay silent. A steady first response is enough for now: save safe details, report the post, protect your contact channels, and get support.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation, reduce harm, and start reporting or takedown. Later decisions about ongoing monitoring, workplace safety, police contact, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.
