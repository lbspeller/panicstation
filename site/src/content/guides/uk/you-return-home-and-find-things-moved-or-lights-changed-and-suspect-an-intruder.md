# What to do if… you return home and find things moved or lights changed and suspect an intruder (UK)

## Short answer
Stay outside and treat it as a possible break-in until you know otherwise. Move to a safer nearby place and call 999 if you think someone may be inside, the crime may be in progress, or you feel in immediate danger; otherwise call 101 or report it online.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go in “just to check” or call out into the property.
- Do not confront anyone you see or try to block exits.
- Do not touch, tidy, or move things back to normal.
- Do not search room-to-room, including sheds, garages, or outbuildings.
- Do not stand right by the door or windows while deciding what to do.
- Do not post your exact address or live location on social media.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause point.** Step away from the door and any ground-floor windows. Go to a neighbour, nearby shop, or a locked car if that is safer.
2. **Look only from where you are.** Notice anything obvious without going closer: a door not fully shut, a damaged frame, an open window, movement inside, or an unfamiliar person or vehicle nearby.
3. **Call the police.**
   - **Call 999** if you think someone may still be inside, you feel threatened, or you believe the crime may be happening now.
   - **Call 101** if you are outside, safe, and it seems like whoever did it has gone, but you still suspect unlawful entry.
   Tell them your address, what changed, whether anyone with permission could be inside, and anything suspicious you saw.
4. **If you cannot speak safely on a 999 call, stay on the line.** Try to answer by whispering, coughing, or tapping if you can.
   - **On a mobile**, press **55** if prompted so the call can be transferred to police.
   - **On a landline**, stay on the line; if only background noise can be heard and the operator cannot decide whether help is needed, the call may be connected to police.
   Do not assume pressing 55 gives police your exact location. Give your address or location if you safely can.
5. **Wait for police guidance before entering.** If officers attend, ask them to confirm when it is safe to go back in. If you have a monitored alarm, tell the monitoring service that you are outside and police have been called.
6. **Use one trusted support person.** Call or message one person who can stay on the phone with you, come nearby, or help you wait somewhere safe.
7. **When you are told it is safe to enter, change as little as possible at first.** Take a few photos of anything disturbed before you touch it. Avoid handling doors, windows, or obvious disturbed areas more than necessary.
8. **Write down the basics while they are fresh.** Note what seems different, any missing keys, and any missing high-risk items such as car keys, ID, bank cards, phones, or laptops. Ask for the crime reference number if a report is taken.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it definitely was a break-in.
- You do not need to do a full inventory while you are still outside or shaken.
- You do not need to arrange long-term security upgrades tonight.
- You do not need to contact insurers until you are safe, have reported it if needed, and have a clearer picture.
- You do not need to repair or secure anything until the property is safe to approach, unless police or another appropriate responder tells you to arrange urgent securing.

## Important reassurance
A sudden change at home can make your body react as if danger is immediate. Staying outside, keeping distance, and asking for help is a sensible protective choice, even if there later turns out to be an innocent explanation.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stay safe, avoid disturbing possible evidence, and get the right immediate help. Later decisions about repairs, insurance, locks, and longer-term security may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you believe anyone is inside or you feel at risk, prioritise immediate safety and call emergency services.
