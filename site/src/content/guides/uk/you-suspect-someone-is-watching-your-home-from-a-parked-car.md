# What to do if… you suspect someone is watching your home from a parked car (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from exposed windows, secure the house from inside, and contact police if you think a crime may be in progress or you feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside to confront the person or knock on the car window.
- Don’t shout, shine a bright light directly at them, make threats, or try to provoke a reaction.
- Don’t keep returning to a window where you can be seen.
- Don’t post about it publicly in real time, because it may reveal where you are and what you are doing.
- Don’t leave the house just to check the car or drive around after it.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer position inside.** Bring anyone with you away from front-facing windows and glass doors. Choose a room where you can stay out of sight and still leave if you need to.
2. **Secure entry points.** Lock doors, close and lock accessible windows, and move keys, bags, wallets, laptops, and car keys away from the front door area and out of sight.
3. **Use lights only from inside.** If you can do it without going outside or standing in view, switch on indoor lights or exterior lights that help you see and make the house look occupied.
4. **Use safety tools you already have.** If you have a burglar alarm, set it. If you have a doorbell camera or CCTV, check that it is recording and that clips can be saved.
5. **Note details briefly from a safe place.** Only do this if you can avoid being seen. Stop if it draws attention or makes you feel less safe. Useful details are:
   - the exact location of the car
   - make, model, colour, and number plate
   - number of occupants
   - what they are doing, such as watching houses, taking photos, getting in and out, or circling the street
   - when you first noticed it and whether it has happened before
6. **Use the right police route for the urgency.**
   - Call 999 if you believe a crime is happening now, someone is in immediate danger, the person approaches your home, or someone tries doors or windows.
   - Call 101 if it feels suspicious but not immediately dangerous, such as a vehicle watching, returning repeatedly, or circling the street.
   - Some police forces also allow online reporting for non-urgent concerns.
7. **When you speak to police, be concrete.** Start with: “There is a vehicle parked across the street and I am concerned it may be watching my address.” Give the details you safely noted, say if you are alone or if children are present, and ask for an incident or reference number.
8. **Use Crimestoppers only for non-urgent anonymous information.** You can pass on information about suspected crime anonymously. Do not use Crimestoppers if you or someone else is in danger, if it is happening now, or if you have been a victim of crime; use 999 or 101 instead.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether it is definitely criminal or definitely nothing.
- You don’t need to confront anyone or gather proof beyond what you can safely observe.
- You don’t need to make long-term security upgrades tonight.
- You don’t need to tell neighbours publicly in real time unless doing so would make you safer right now.

## Important reassurance
Feeling on edge is an understandable reaction to seeing an unknown vehicle watching or lingering near your home. You are allowed to contact police about suspicious behaviour, especially when you can describe what you are seeing and why it concerns you.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes to hours. If this becomes repeated, targeted, or linked to someone you know, later decisions may need specialist help or a more detailed safety plan.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, therapeutic, or professional advice. Trust your judgement about immediate safety. If you believe you are in danger or a crime is in progress, call 999.
