# What to do if… you are asked for local registration proof and you do not have it (UK)

## Short answer
Stay calm, show your passport and any entry or stay evidence you have, and ask exactly what “registration confirmation” means so it can be checked or replaced through the local official route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not invent a document number, address, date, receipt, or proof you do not have.
- Do not make unofficial payments or pay without an official receipt and a clear written reason for the payment.
- Do not sign anything you do not understand; ask for an interpreter or a written explanation in a language you understand.
- Do not argue about the rule in the street; focus on verification, a written instruction, or being directed to the correct office.
- Do not hand over your passport to someone who has not identified themselves as an official. If an official needs to hold it, ask for the office name and a receipt or written record where possible.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause and clarify the request.**  
   Ask: “What is the exact name of the registration document, and which office or system issues it?” In some places this may be hotel or host registration; in others it may be a local residence, address, or tourist registration.

2. **Show what you do have.**  
   Offer your passport and any entry or stay evidence you can access quickly, for example:
   - visa, residence permit, or entry clearance if you have one
   - entry stamp or arrival record if available
   - accommodation booking showing the address and dates
   - messages from your hotel, host, accommodation provider, or landlord if you are renting locally

3. **Ask for verification instead of arguing about the missing paper.**  
   Say you never received the confirmation and ask whether they can:
   - check the registration in their official system
   - tell you the exact office that can issue a duplicate or confirmation
   - write down the document name, issuing authority, and what you need to bring

4. **Contact the accommodation provider or host immediately.**  
   Ask for:
   - a copy, photo, or PDF of the registration submission or receipt
   - the name of the authority it was submitted to
   - the date and time it was submitted
   - any reference number
   - a written message confirming your stay dates and address

5. **If you are sent to an office, keep it practical and documented.**  
   Ask for the office name, address, opening time, and what documents to bring. Keep any written note or official instruction you are given. If it is safe and allowed, take a photo of it.

6. **If you are detained or taken to a station, ask for UK consular contact.**  
   Ask the local authorities to contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate, or ask to contact them yourself. Keep your explanation simple: you have identification, but you never received the local registration proof and need it checked or replaced.

7. **Ask for the fastest official replacement or confirmation.**  
   At the relevant local office, ask whether they can give you:
   - a printed confirmation
   - a stamped copy
   - an email or SMS confirmation sent while you are there
   - a written note confirming what is still needed

8. **Check the details before you leave.**  
   Check your name, passport number, dates, address, and reference number for typos. Save a copy on your phone and email it to yourself so you can show it again quickly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain about a hotel, host, landlord, or booking platform.
- You do not need to solve wider immigration, residence, or travel-plan questions on the spot.
- You do not need to search for unofficial fixes while stressed; focus on official verification or replacement first.
- You do not need to explain the whole history repeatedly; use the same short explanation each time.

## Important reassurance
This can happen where registration is handled by a hotel, host, accommodation provider, or local office, and where the traveller may not receive a copy automatically. Missing the paper in the moment does not by itself prove you have done something wrong; the next step is to verify your identity and get the registration checked or replaced.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise the situation, avoid irreversible mistakes, and get official verification or replacement proof. Local rules vary by country, and later decisions may need help from the local authority, a qualified local lawyer, or UK consular services.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Local registration rules, document names, enforcement practices, and consular support can vary depending on the country and your circumstances.
