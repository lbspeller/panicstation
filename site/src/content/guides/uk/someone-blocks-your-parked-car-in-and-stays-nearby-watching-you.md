# What to do if… someone blocks your parked car in and stays nearby watching you (UK)

## Short answer
Stay in the locked car if that is the safest place, keep the windows up, and do not engage. If you feel in danger, think violence could happen, or cannot safely get to a staffed place, call **999** now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get out to confront them, argue, or try to reason with them while they are nearby.
- Do not nudge their vehicle, force your way out, or do anything that could look like a deliberate collision.
- Do not unlock the door or roll the window down fully to talk.
- Do not lead them to your home if they later move and appear to follow you.
- Do not hold your phone up at them if that could escalate things; your safety matters more than getting footage.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car harder to access.** Lock the doors, close the windows, keep your keys and phone in hand, and stay belted if moving suddenly would be unsafe.
2. **Treat it as an emergency if danger feels possible.** Call **999** if they are trying the doors, making threats or threatening gestures, blocking you from leaving, you see a weapon, you are isolated, or you cannot safely reach staff or security.
3. **If you call 999 and cannot speak safely, stay on the line.** Listen to the operator. If you can, cough, tap the phone, or whisper. If prompted on a mobile, press **55** so the call is put through to police; staying silent and not responding may lead to the call ending.
4. **If it is not an immediate emergency but you still feel unsafe, call 101.** Say you are blocked in, give the car park or street location, describe the other vehicle, and ask what to do while you remain in the locked car.
5. **Use on-site help without walking past the person.** If you are in a supermarket, retail, office, hospital, station, or multi-storey car park, use a help point or intercom, or call the site’s security or management number and ask them to come to you.
6. **Create a witness channel.** Call someone you trust on speakerphone. Tell them your exact location, the other vehicle’s registration if visible, what the person is doing, and whether you have called police or security.
7. **Record details without provoking them.** Note the registration, make, model, colour, location, time, and a brief description of the person. Take a photo only if you can do it discreetly from inside the locked car without escalating the situation.
8. **Leave the car only for a safer place.** Exit only if you can reach a staffed, well-lit place without passing close to them. Take your phone and keys, keep distance, and ask staff to call police or security. If that route is not safe, stay in the locked car.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal report, complain to the parking operator, or prove what happened.
- You do not need to explain yourself to the person blocking you in.
- You can deal with parking, insurance, management, or complaint issues later, once you are away from the immediate situation.

## Important reassurance
Feeling frozen, shaky, or unsure what to do is a normal response to a possible threat. Treating someone blocking your parked car in while watching you as a safety issue is reasonable.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get through the next few minutes safely. If this is part of repeated intimidation, stalking, harassment, or being targeted by someone known to you, later support from police, security staff, or a specialist support service may help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
