# What to do if… a driver refuses your seating request and you feel unsafe in the vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
End the journey as soon as you can do so safely by asking to stop at a busy, well-lit public place. If you feel in immediate danger, or the driver will not stop, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay in the vehicle just to be polite.
- Don’t get into an argument inside the vehicle if a short, calm request can get you to a safer stop.
- Don’t give your exact home address if you can change the drop-off to a nearby public place.
- Don’t accept a private hire vehicle or minicab you did not book through a licensed operator.
- Don’t post about it while you are still in the vehicle; focus on getting to a safer place first.
- Don’t worry about making a perfect report while you are still trying to get out safely.

## What to do now
1. **Quietly make your phone useful.** Unlock it, open your map, and message someone you trust with your live location, registration plate if you can see it, and a short message such as: “I feel unsafe in this vehicle and I am ending the trip.”
2. **Pick a public stop.** Choose a nearby place with people, lighting, and staff if possible, such as a petrol station, shop, hotel lobby, station, or open takeaway.
3. **Ask for a clear stop.** Say: “Please stop at that shop. I’m ending the trip.” You do not need to explain or debate the seat request.
4. **If the driver refuses to stop, changes direction, blocks you from leaving, or you feel trapped, call 999.** Say you are in a vehicle and feel unsafe. Give your live location or direction of travel, the vehicle registration, and any app or booking details you have.
5. **If you cannot speak on a 999 call, stay connected.** If you are calling from a mobile, press 55 when prompted so the call can be transferred to police.
6. **When the vehicle stops, leave with essentials.** Take your phone, keys, bag, and medication if they are within reach. Move straight into a staffed place or toward other people.
7. **Once you are out and safe, note the details.** Record the time, route, pick-up point, drop-off point, vehicle registration, make and colour, driver name if shown, driver badge or ID if visible, and any app screenshots.
8. **Report it through the route that matches the journey.**
   - For a ride-hailing app, use the in-app safety or help tools and report that the driver refused your seat request and you ended the trip because you felt unsafe.
   - For a booked private hire or minicab operator, contact the operator and ask how they will record it and what reference they can give you.
   - For taxi or private hire licensing, report it to the licensing authority shown on the vehicle or booking details, normally your local council licensing team, or TfL if the journey was in London.
9. **If it is not an emergency but you want police to log it, use 101 or your local police force’s online reporting route.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it was “serious enough.”
- You do not need to confront the driver.
- You do not need to prove what the driver intended.
- Refunds, complaints, fuller statements, and follow-up messages can wait until you are somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
Feeling uneasy in a vehicle is enough reason to end the journey. There can be practical reasons for a driver to prefer a seating arrangement, but you do not have to stay in a situation that feels unsafe.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: getting to a safer place, keeping key details, and reporting through the right route. Later decisions, including formal complaints or legal questions, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999. If you are injured or have been assaulted, seek urgent medical help and specialist support.
