# What to do if… you notice someone seems to be following you and is still nearby (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately into a well-lit, staffed, public building—such as a shop, café, or hotel lobby—and call **999** if you feel at risk. Do not go home or to a secluded area while the person is still visible or nearby.

## Do not do these things
- Don't head towards your home, workplace, or your parked car if it is in a quiet area.
- Don't confront the individual, shout at them, or try to "lead them away" into a quiet side street.
- Don't stop moving in an isolated area to check your phone or wait for them to pass.
- Don't accept a lift from a stranger or get into an unbooked minicab.
- Don't assume you are overreacting—trust your instincts even if you are not 100% certain of their intent.
- Don't hang up if you have dialled 999 but cannot speak; follow the Silent Solution steps below.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a "safe haven" immediately by entering the nearest staffed public building.** Look for shops, petrol stations, hotel receptions, or busy pubs.
   - Avoid empty car parks, alleyways, or residential side streets.
2. **Alert a member of staff or security as soon as you enter the building.** Use clear language: "I am being followed by someone outside and I don't feel safe; can I stay here and call the police?"
3. **Call 999 if you believe the person is waiting for you, approaching you, or if you feel in immediate danger.** If you are now safe inside and the person has left the area, call **101** to report the incident and provide a description.
4. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you are in danger but cannot speak on the phone.** Dial **999** from a mobile and listen to the operator.
   - If prompted by an automated message, **press 55** to let the operator know it is a genuine emergency.
   - If you do not press 55 when prompted, the call will be terminated.
   - If calling from a landline, you do not need to press 55; stay on the line and the operator will listen for background noise.
5. **Contact the British Transport Police if you are on a train or at a station in Great Britain.** 
   - For an immediate emergency, always call **999**.
   - For non-emergencies or to report suspicious behavior discreetly, text **61016** (Note: In Northern Ireland, contact the PSNI via 999 or 101).
6. **Ask staff to help you stay in a secure area away from windows or the main entrance.** If the building has a back office or a counter you can stand behind, move there to avoid being seen from the street.
7. **Provide your exact location and a description of the individual to the police call handler.** Mention street names, shop names, or landmarks. Describe the person's clothing, height, build, and any specific features without looking directly at them.
8. **Stay inside the safe location until the police arrive or until you have a secure way home.** Do not leave alone. Ask the police for advice on how to leave safely, or arrange for a trusted person to collect you from inside the building.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify exactly who the person is or why they were following you.
- You do not need to check your own social media or "check-ins" to see how they might have found you.
- You do not need to decide whether you want to press charges or go to court right now.
- You do not need to worry about being "a nuisance" to the police; they prefer to prevent an incident before it happens.

## Important reassurance
Feeling frightened or physically shaken is a natural response to a threat. Choosing to seek help in a public place is a proactive and sensible safety measure, regardless of whether the person following you eventually moves on.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for a person currently being followed in a public space. It does not cover long-term stalking investigations or legal protective orders.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Always follow the instructions of emergency services operators.
