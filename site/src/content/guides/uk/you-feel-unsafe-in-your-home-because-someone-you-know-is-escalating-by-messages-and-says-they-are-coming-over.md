# What to do if… you feel unsafe in your home because someone you know is escalating by messages and says they are coming over (UK)

## Short answer
Call **999** immediately if you believe they are nearby or arriving soon; you do not need to wait for them to reach your doorstep. Secure all entry points, move to a lockable room, and do not open the door under any circumstances.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to "talk it out" or ask them to leave.
- Do not go outside to see if they have arrived yet.
- Do not respond to further messages; engagement often increases escalation.
- Do not tell the person where you are in the house or that you have called the police.
- Do not feel you have to wait for a physical act of violence before calling 999.
- Do not delete any messages or call logs, as these are evidence of the threat.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 immediately if you feel in danger.**
   - **Explain that someone you know is escalating via messages and is on their way to your home.**
   - **Mention if there are children in the house or if the person has been violent before.**
   - **State if you have a non-molestation order, domestic abuse protection order, or if the person is on bail.**

2. **Use the Silent Solution if you cannot speak safely.**
   - **On a mobile: Dial 999 and, when prompted by the automated system, press 55.** This tells the operator it is a genuine emergency and transfers you to the police.
   - **On a landline: Dial 999 and stay on the line.** Landlines do not use the "55" system; the operator will listen for background noise and transfer you to the police if they hear signs of a disturbance.
   - **Note: Pressing 55 does not allow police to track your precise location; try to whisper your address if possible.**

3. **Secure the property (30 seconds).**
   - **Lock all external doors and windows immediately.**
   - **Use secondary security like door chains, bolts, or bars if available.**
   - **Ensure you have your phone and a charger or power bank with you.**

4. **Move to a "safer room" with any other occupants.**
   - **Gather children or pets and move to a room that can be locked from the inside.**
   - **Stay away from ground-floor windows and doors where you can be seen from the outside.**
   - **Avoid kitchens or garages, which contain objects that could be used as weapons.**

5. **Create a witness by calling a trusted contact.**
   - **If you are not already on the phone with 999, call someone you trust.**
   - **Tell them: "I am at home, [Name] is coming over and I feel unsafe. Stay on the line with me."**
   - **Instruct them to call 999 if the line goes dead and they cannot reach you.**

6. **Set one final boundary only if the person has not arrived.**
   - **If you believe it is safe, send one final text: "I have told you not to come. If you show up, I will call the police."**
   - **Do not read or reply to any messages sent after this.**

7. **If they arrive, do not acknowledge them.**
   - **Remain silent and stay in your safer room.**
   - **If you are on the phone with 999, keep the line open so the dispatcher can hear what is happening at the door.**
   - **Do not shout through the door, as this confirms you are home and may escalate their behavior.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to press charges or make a formal statement right now.
- You do not need to explain your reasons for calling the police to the person outside.
- You do not need to organize your evidence into a timeline tonight.
- You do not need to worry about "wasting police time" if your safety is at risk.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of dread or to "freeze" when someone you know ignores your boundaries and threatens to show up. You are not overreacting by taking these steps. Securing your environment and calling for help are the most effective ways to prevent a physical confrontation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate threat. Longer-term safety planning, such as applying for injunctions or changing locks, should be handled through specialist support once the immediate situation is resolved.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In any emergency, always dial 999. For non-emergency reporting of harassment or stalking, you can call 101 or report online via your local police force website.
