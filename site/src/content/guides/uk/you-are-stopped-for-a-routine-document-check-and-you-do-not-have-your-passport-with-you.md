# What to do if… you are stopped for a routine document check and you do not have your passport with you (UK)

## Short answer
State clearly that your passport is at your accommodation, present any alternative photo identification you have, and ask for the specific procedure to retrieve the original document.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to run, move away abruptly, or physically resist the officers.
- Do not offer money, gifts, or "on-the-spot" cash payments unless a formal fine receipt is provided.
- Do not sign any documents or statements that are not in English or that you do not fully understand.
- Do not hand over your mobile phone for scrolling or inspection unless specifically ordered to do so by a senior official.
- Do not claim you have no identification if you are carrying a driving licence or other photo ID.

## What to do now
1. **State your status and the location of your passport.** Use clear, simple language to explain you are a visitor and your passport is at your accommodation.
2. **Present any alternative photo identification immediately.** Provide a UK driving licence, residence permit, or a photocopy/digital scan of your passport if available.
   - Note that digital copies are rarely accepted as legal substitutes but can verify your details for the initial check.
3. **Ask for the procedure to verify your identity.** Request to be escorted to your accommodation to retrieve the document or ask if a trusted companion can bring it to the current location.
4. **Request an interpreter if there is a language barrier.** If you cannot understand the officers' instructions or the reason for the stop, ask for professional translation.
5. **Contact the British Embassy or Consulate if you are detained.** If you are taken to a police station or held for more than a routine check, call the nearest British mission or the FCDO in London.
   - FCDO 24/7 Global Response Centre: +44 20 7008 5000.
6. **Note the details of the officers and the location.** Record the names, badge numbers, unit name, and the time/date of the encounter as soon as it is safe to do so.
7. **Obtain copies of any paperwork issued.** Ensure you receive a formal receipt or report for any fines paid or documents retained by the authorities.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to file a formal complaint against the local police.
- Contacting your travel insurance provider, unless your passport is lost or stolen.
- Updating your social media or notifying family of the delay.
- Researching the specific local laws that led to the stop.

## Important reassurance
Routine document checks are common in many jurisdictions and do not necessarily imply you are suspected of a crime. Providing a clear explanation and alternative ID usually leads to a practical resolution, such as an escort to your hotel or a deadline to present the document at a local station.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization during an active stop or initial detention. It does not provide legal defence strategies or country-specific law summaries.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not legal advice. Requirements for carrying original identification vary by country. If you are arrested or your passport is seized, prioritize requesting consular access under the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations.
