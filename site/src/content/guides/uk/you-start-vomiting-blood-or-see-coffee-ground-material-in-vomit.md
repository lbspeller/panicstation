# What to do if… you start vomiting blood or see coffee-ground material in vomit (UK)

## Short answer
**Call 999 or go to A&E immediately** if you vomit blood or material that looks like coffee grounds. This is a medical emergency that requires urgent assessment, even if the vomiting has currently stopped.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not** drive yourself to the hospital or ask someone to drive you if you feel faint, dizzy, or short of breath.
- **Do not** take aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen, or any other anti-inflammatory painkillers, as these can significantly worsen internal bleeding.
- **Do not** eat or drink anything, including water, until you have been assessed by a clinician.
- **Do not** lie flat on your back, as this increases the risk of choking if you vomit again.
- **Do not** ignore the symptom even if the amount of blood is small or if you feel better now.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 or 112 immediately for an ambulance.**
   - Tell the operator you are "vomiting blood" or have "coffee-ground vomit."
   - Mention immediately if you feel faint, dizzy, sweaty, or have a fast heartbeat.
2. **Position yourself to protect your airway.**
   - Sit upright and lean forward over a bowl or sink.
   - **If you feel like you might faint, lie on your side in the recovery position** to ensure that any further vomit does not enter your lungs.
3. **Check for other emergency indicators.**
   - Look for or recall if your stools (poo) have been black, tarry, sticky, or unusually smelly (melaena), as this indicates a significant internal bleed.
   - Note if you have severe abdominal pain or chest pain.
4. **Prepare a list of your medications for the paramedics.**
   - Highlight if you take "blood thinners" (anticoagulants such as warfarin, apixaban, or rivaroxaban) or steroids.
   - Mention any history of stomach ulcers or liver disease.
5. **Unlock your front door and secure any pets.**
   - This ensures paramedics can reach you quickly if you become too weak to move.
6. **Save a small sample or take a photo of the vomit if safe.**
   - Do this only if it does not delay your call to 999 or your arrival at the hospital. 
   - Clinicians use the colour and consistency to help determine the source of the bleed.
7. **If you are waiting for an ambulance and your condition worsens:**
   - Call 999 again to provide an update, especially if you become confused, extremely pale, or lose consciousness.

## What can wait
- Cleaning the area or your clothes.
- Searching for causes of "blood in vomit" online.
- Attempting to contact your regular GP surgery.
- Deciding whether the amount of blood is "serious enough" for an emergency call.

## Important reassurance
Seeing blood in your vomit is a high-stress event, and feeling a sense of panic or shock is a normal physiological response to a sudden medical crisis. Emergency services are trained to handle these situations, and seeking help immediately is the correct and expected action to ensure your safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and emergency routing for acute vomiting of blood. It does not replace clinical diagnosis, endoscopy, or hospital-based treatment protocols.

## Important note
Vomiting blood (haematemesis) or coffee-ground material is a sign of internal bleeding that requires immediate hospital assessment. This information is for emergency guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice or treatment.
