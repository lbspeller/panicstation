# What to do if… your computer warns of disk errors and offers a repair that could risk data loss (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately stop using and charging the device. Place it on a hard, non-flammable surface at least two metres away from combustible materials and monitor it from a safe distance for signs of smoke or fire.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t continue using the device to finish a task if it is hotter than usual or smells like burning chemicals.
- Don’t plug the device into any power source to "test" the battery or check if it settles down.
- Don’t leave a hot or swollen device unattended, especially overnight or near sleeping areas.
- Don’t apply pressure to the battery, attempt to "burp" a swollen case, or use sharp tools to pry the device open.
- Don’t place the device in a fridge or freezer; sudden temperature changes can cause further internal damage.
- Don’t dispose of a damaged or failing lithium-ion battery in standard household waste or recycling bins.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the power source.** Unplug the charger from the wall and the device. **Power the device down immediately** if the screen is still responsive, then stop interacting with it.
2. **Relocate the device to a safe zone.** Move the device to a **hard, non-flammable surface** such as a stone floor, a ceramic tile, or a clear metal sink.
   - Keep the device away from curtains or upholstery.
   - Keep the device away from paper or cardboard.
   - Keep the device away from aerosols or cleaning chemicals.
   - Keep the device away from cooking oils or wooden surfaces.
3. **Monitor for escalation.** Observe the device from a safe distance (at least 3 metres) for at least **30 to 60 minutes**. Watch for:
   - Visible smoke or a "sweet" metallic smell.
   - Hissing, popping, or crackling sounds.
   - Rapidly increasing bulging or the case splitting open.
4. **Call 999 if the battery vents.** If you see **smoke, sparks, or flames**, or if the device begins to hiss loudly: **evacuate the room, close the door, and call 999**. Inform the operator that a lithium-ion battery is involved, as these fires require specific firefighting techniques.
5. **Manage toxic fumes.** If the battery vents, it will release toxic gases. **Do not inhale the smoke.** Ensure everyone—including pets—is out of the affected area and open windows only if it is safe to do so without approaching the device.
6. **Report the safety issue.** Once the device is cool and stable, report the incident to the **Office for Product Safety and Standards (OPSS)** via the appropriate channel:
   - In England and Wales, contact the **Citizens Advice consumer service**.
   - In Scotland, contact **Advice Direct Scotland**.
   - In Northern Ireland, contact the **Environmental Health department** at your local district council.
7. **Check the Product Safety Database.** Search the official **Product Recalls and Alerts** list on GOV.UK for your specific make and model to see if a recall is already active.
8. **Secure professional disposal.** Do not keep a failed battery in your home. Take it to a **Household Waste Recycling Centre (HWRC)** and explicitly inform staff that the battery is damaged, swollen, or has overheated, as it must be stored in a specialized fire-safe container.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the battery failed (e.g., age, software, or hardware defect) right now.
- You do not need to attempt to back up data while the device is actively hot or unstable.
- You do not need to contact the manufacturer for a refund or warranty claim until the immediate fire risk has passed.

## Important reassurance
Reacting with caution to an overheating device is the correct safety protocol. Lithium-ion battery failures can be rapid and intense; taking the device out of service and isolating it is a necessary precaution to protect your home and yourself. Feeling anxious or startled by a hot electronic device is a normal response to a legitimate hazard.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and harm prevention for suspected battery failure. It does not provide repair instructions, warranty advice, or long-term technical diagnostics.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not replace the advice of emergency services or certified fire safety professionals. If a device is smoking or on fire, prioritize evacuation over property preservation. Lithium battery fires can reignite hours after the initial incident; always treat a failed battery as hazardous until it is professionally disposed of.
