# What to do if… you lose your driving licence abroad and need it for car hire or travel plans (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive or try to collect a hire car until the hire company confirms what it will accept and you have checked what the country requires. Call the actual hire desk now, ask about alternatives, and start the official DVLA or DVA replacement route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive “just this once” if you do not have what local law, the hire company, and the insurance require.
- Do not assume a photo, scan, or saved copy of your licence will release a hire car.
- Do not buy an “international driving licence” from an unofficial website.
- Do not send passport or licence scans to unknown people, personal email addresses, or messaging accounts.
- Do not cancel every part of the trip before you have spoken to the hire desk, your accommodation, and your next transport provider.

## What to do now
1. **Pause somewhere secure and re-check the obvious places first.**  
   Check your wallet, phone case, bag pockets, luggage linings, hotel safe, hotel reception, airport security tray, taxi, transfer vehicle, restaurant, and the place you last showed ID. If a taxi, venue, hotel, or tour operator may have it, contact them now while the details are fresh.

2. **If it may have been stolen, make a local theft report if you can do that safely.**  
   Ask local police, hotel reception, or your travel provider how to record the theft and get a reference number. Keep a screenshot or photo of the reference.

3. **Call the hire company location, not only the broker or comparison site.**  
   Ask: “What exactly do you require to release the car if my UK driving licence card is lost?” Also ask whether they can postpone pick-up, add another qualified driver, accept a DVLA or DVA check code as part of verification, move the booking to another branch, or cancel with reduced penalty. Ask for the answer in writing by email, text, or app message.

4. **Try to create a driving-licence check code.**  
   For a Great Britain licence, use the DVLA “View or share your driving licence information” service. You will usually need your driving licence number, National Insurance number, and the postcode on your licence. The code is valid for 21 days and can be shared with a car hire company.  
   For a Northern Ireland licence, use the nidirect service. You will need a nidirect account and identity verification; NI check codes are also valid for 21 days. If you have a Northern Ireland paper counterpart, keep it safe and ask the hire company whether it needs to see that as well.  
   A check code may help the hire company verify your record, but it may not replace the physical licence card.

5. **Start the official replacement process.**  
   Use the DVLA replacement service for Great Britain licences or the DVA replacement service for Northern Ireland licences. Check the address details the official service allows and do not use unofficial replacement websites. If your address details are wrong, follow the official address-update route before relying on delivery.

6. **Make a no-driving plan for the next 24 hours.**  
   If the hire desk will not release the car, book the simplest immediate alternative: train, coach, airport transfer, licensed taxi, app-based private hire vehicle, ferry foot-passenger option, or one extra hotel night. This is to keep you safe and moving while the document problem is handled.

7. **For onward travel ID checks, use your passport if you have it.**  
   International travel usually needs a passport or other accepted travel document, not a driving licence. For a domestic flight, ferry, coach, hotel, or tour that was going to use your driving licence as photo ID, contact the carrier or provider and ask what other ID they will accept.

8. **Protect anything else that was lost with the licence.**  
   If bank cards, passport, phone, or keys were in the same wallet or bag, deal with those urgent risks separately. Cancel payment cards, secure phone access, and follow the passport-loss process if your passport is also missing.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the whole trip is ruined; focus on the next safe journey and tonight’s accommodation.
- You do not need to argue with the hire broker before you know the local desk’s actual policy.
- You do not need to replace every document unless it is missing too.
- You do not need to make a long insurance or complaint file right now; keep screenshots and reference numbers, then come back to that later.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful because it blocks plans suddenly, but it is usually a logistics problem rather than a personal failure. The safest next move is to avoid driving until the rules are clear, get written answers from the hire company, and set up a short-term travel alternative.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: protect your immediate safety, avoid driving without confirmed permission, and keep travel moving. Later decisions about refunds, insurance claims, fraud checks, or a rental dispute may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, insurance, or professional advice. Requirements can vary by country, hire company, carrier, and policy, so check the official route and the provider involved before you act.
