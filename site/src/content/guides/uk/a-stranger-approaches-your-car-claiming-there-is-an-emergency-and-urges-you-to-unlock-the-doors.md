# What to do if… a stranger approaches your car claiming there is an emergency and urges you to unlock the doors (UK)

## Short answer
Keep your doors locked and windows fully closed. If you feel threatened or the person attempts to open the door, drive away immediately if safe or call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock the doors to "hear them better" or to "be polite".
- Do not open your window, even a small crack, if you feel uncomfortable.
- Do not exit the vehicle to "check" a problem they are pointing out, such as a flat tyre or fluid leak.
- Do not pass your phone, keys, or wallet through a window.
- Do not follow the person to a different location or a "quieter" area to help.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure the vehicle is fully secured immediately:** lock all doors using the central locking button and roll up all windows.
2. **Create physical distance if possible:** drive away slowly and safely if your path is clear.
   - If blocked by traffic, leave enough space between your car and the vehicle in front to pull around it if necessary.
3. **Offer help from inside the car:** if you believe the emergency might be real, inform the person through the closed window that you are calling for help.
4. **Call 999 yourself:** tell the operator your location and describe the situation.
   - State clearly if the person is trying your door handles or blocking your vehicle.
   - Mention any specific claims they are making (e.g., "they say my tyre is on fire").
5. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely:** if the person can hear you and you are afraid to speak to the operator, call 999 and press "55" when prompted on a mobile.
6. **Move to a busy, well-lit public area:** if you are being followed or the person will not leave, drive to a petrol station forecourt, hospital A&E entrance, or a supermarket car park.
7. **Signal for attention if you cannot move:** use a short blast of the horn or turn on your hazard lights to attract witnesses while remaining inside the locked car.

## What can wait
- You do not need to confirm if the emergency they claimed is "real" before prioritizing your safety.
- You do not need to download dashcam footage or write down registration numbers until you have reached a safe, stationary location.
- You do not need to decide whether to provide a formal witness statement right now.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a conflict between the desire to help someone in distress and the need to protect yourself. Staying inside a locked vehicle while calling professional emergency services is a safe and responsible way to manage a potential crisis without exposing yourself to harm.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate personal safety while inside a vehicle. It does not cover long-term police investigations or insurance claims for vehicle damage.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance only and does not constitute legal or security advice. Always follow the directions of emergency dispatchers when calling 999.
