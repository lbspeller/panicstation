# What to do if… you notice a vehicle has parked extremely close to your driver’s door and someone remains inside watching you (UK)

## Short answer
Do not approach your vehicle or the other car. Move immediately to a busy, well-lit, or staffed area and call **999** if you feel threatened or suspect an imminent crime.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the individual, gesture toward them, or attempt to speak through their window.
- Do not attempt to squeeze into the gap between the vehicles if it brings you within arm's reach of the other car.
- Do not unlock your car from a distance, as this signals your intent to approach.
- Do not stand near your vehicle while deciding what to do; proximity increases your vulnerability.
- Do not look down at your phone for extended periods while in the car park.
- Do not accept offers of "help" from the person in the other vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Create physical distance from the vehicles immediately.** Walk with purpose toward the nearest public entrance, staffed counter, petrol station shop, or security office.
2. **Call for emergency assistance if you feel at risk.** **Dial 999** if you believe a crime is about to be committed, if the person is following you, or if you feel unsafe returning to your car.
3. **Use the "Silent Solution" protocol if you cannot speak safely on a mobile.** **Call 999 and, if you are on a mobile and unable to speak, listen for the automated prompt and press 55.**
   - This alerts the operator that your call is a genuine emergency.
   - If you can, make some sound such as coughing or tapping the handset.
4. **Inform staff or security at your safe location.** **Clearly state that you are being monitored by someone in a vehicle that has blocked your access.**
   - Request that they stay with you.
   - Ask for a security escort to your vehicle once the situation is resolved or the police arrive.
5. **Secure yourself if you are already inside your vehicle.** **Lock all doors, keep windows fully closed, and keep the engine running if it is safe to do so.**
   - If your path is clear, leave the car park immediately.
   - If you are blocked in and feel threatened, stay locked inside and call 999.
6. **Evaluate alternative entry only if the threat is low.** **Only consider entering through the passenger side if the other vehicle is unoccupied and the driver's door is physically blocked by poor parking.**
   - If the other vehicle is occupied, do not approach the passenger side as you may still be within reach or be blocked from exiting quickly.
7. **Record vehicle details from a safe distance.** **Note the number plate, make, model, and colour of the other vehicle only once you are in a secure, staffed area.**
   - Do not linger near the vehicle to take photos.
8. **Drive to a safe public place if you believe you are being followed.** **Do not drive home; instead, head to a busy petrol station or a staffed police station.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the parking was accidental or intentional right now.
- You do not need to leave a note or take photos of the parking violation for insurance purposes while the occupant is present.
- Reporting the incident to the car park management or using the 101 non-emergency line can wait until you are home or in a secure location.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden "spike" of adrenaline, shakiness, or hyper-vigilance in this situation. Your physical reaction is a standard response to a perceived threat. Prioritising distance over the convenience of reaching your car is the correct safety protocol.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate personal safety concern in a car park. It does not cover long-term stalking investigations or legal advice regarding parking disputes.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety guidance. If you are in danger or suspect a crime is occurring, contact the police immediately. This guide does not replace professional security advice or police instructions.
