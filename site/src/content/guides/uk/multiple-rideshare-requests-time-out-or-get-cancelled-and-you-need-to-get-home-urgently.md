# What to do if… multiple rideshare requests time out or get cancelled and you need to get home urgently (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a well-lit, staffed location and switch to a licensed alternative, such as a hailed taxi (Black Cab) or a pre-booked private hire vehicle (PHV) through a local operator. If you feel at risk or are being followed, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter any vehicle that has not been pre-booked through a licensed operator unless it is a licensed taxi (Black Cab) hailed on the street or at a rank.
- Do not accept lifts from strangers or "unbooked" drivers who approach you, even if the vehicle has official-looking stickers or licensing badges.
- Do not stay in isolated or dark areas to "wait for signal" or better pickup pricing.
- Do not share your live location or home address with anyone other than a trusted contact or a licensed dispatcher.
- Do not continue using a failing app if your phone battery is below 15 percent; save the remaining charge for a phone call to a taxi firm.

## What to do now
1. **Relocate to a staffed "Safe Pause" point.**  
   - Enter a 24-hour business such as a supermarket, petrol station, or hotel lobby.  
   - If no indoor space is available, stand in a well-lit area directly under a CCTV camera or near a group of people.  
   - Position yourself with your back to a wall so you can see your surroundings clearly.

2. **Establish a clear and legal pickup address.**  
   - Identify a specific landmark (e.g., "The [Name] Hotel Entrance" or "The [Name] Train Station Rank").  
   - Move to a main road where it is legal and safe for a vehicle to pull over without blocking traffic.

3. **Switch to a secondary licensed transport method.**  
   - **Taxis:** Look for a vehicle with an illuminated "Taxi" sign (Black Cabs in London or similar licensed vehicles elsewhere) and hail it or go to the nearest official taxi rank.  
   - **Local Private Hire:** Call a local minicab firm directly via phone; these bookings are regulated by Local Transport Authorities (LTAs) and provide a digital or paper record.  
   - **Public Transport:** Use the "TfL Go" app (London) or national rail/bus trackers to find the next available safe route, even if it only gets you closer to home.

4. **Verify the vehicle and driver identity before entry.**  
   - **Check the plate:** Every licensed taxi or PHV must display a licensing plate (usually on the rear) showing the authority and expiry date.  
   - **Check the badge:** The driver must wear or display a photographic ID badge.  
   - **Match the booking:** Ensure the vehicle make, model, and registration match the details sent to your phone or provided by the dispatcher.

5. **Share your status with a trusted contact.**  
   - Send your "Live Location" via a messaging app to a friend or family member.  
   - If you are alone, stay on a voice call with someone until you are inside the vehicle and the journey has started.

6. **Address app or payment failures quickly.**  
   - If an app is timing out, check your cellular data settings or switch to a different network/provider if your phone allows.  
   - If payment is declining, check for a "frozen" card in your banking app or use a different card/Apple Pay/Google Pay.  
   - Note that all licensed London taxis must accept card payments; many provincial taxis also have card terminals.

7. **Escalate to emergency services if safety is threatened.**  
   - If someone is harassing you, following you, or making you feel unsafe, call **999** immediately.  
   - If you need non-urgent police assistance (e.g., reporting an unlicensed driver after you are safe), call **101**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose the most cost-effective route; prioritise the first safe, licensed option that gets you moving.
- You do not need to resolve app technical issues or payment disputes right now.
- You do not need to contact the rideshare company for a refund until you are safely home.

## Important reassurance
Rideshare "timeouts" and cancellations are common during periods of high demand, weather changes, or driver shortages. This is a technical and logistical failure of the platform, not a personal error. You are capable of finding an alternative path once you are in a stable, well-lit environment.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for transport-related distress and safety. It focuses on immediate harm prevention and securing a safe route home. Long-term planning or platform complaints are outside this scope.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional safety advice. Licensing regulations for taxis and private hire vehicles are managed by local authorities (LTAs) and may vary by region. Always prioritise your physical safety over convenience or cost.
