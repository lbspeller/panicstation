# What to do if… you discover a tyre has a bulge, deep cut, or exposed cords and you’re unsure if it can be driven on (UK)

## Short answer
The vehicle is illegal and unsafe to drive; you must stop immediately in a safe location and not continue the journey. Under UK law, driving with these defects can result in a fine of up to £2,500 and 3 penalty points per tyre.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to drive to a garage or home if the damage is visible; use a mobile tyre fitter or recovery service.
- Don’t "test" the tyre by driving at low speeds or on side roads; internal structural failure (blowout) can happen even at a standstill.
- Don’t use a warning triangle if you are stopped on a motorway or a dual carriageway.
- Don’t attempt to glue, patch, or tape a sidewall bulge or cut; sidewall damage cannot be repaired legally or safely.
- Don’t leave passengers or animals to wander near the carriageway if you have stopped at the roadside.

## What to do now
1. **Pull over to a safe location immediately if you are currently driving.** Smoothly reduce your speed, avoid sudden steering inputs, and find a flat, stable area well away from live traffic.
   - On motorways, aim for the next exit or service area. 
   - If you cannot reach an exit, pull into an emergency area or onto the hard shoulder, stopping as far to the left as possible with your wheels turned left.
2. **Evacuate the vehicle safely if you are at the roadside.** Exit via the doors furthest from traffic (usually the left-hand side) and move everyone behind a safety barrier or up an embankment.
   - Leave any animals inside the vehicle unless there is an immediate risk of fire or collision.
   - Stand well away from the vehicle and face oncoming traffic so you can see any approaching hazards or debris.
3. **Perform a quick visual check only if it is safe to do so.** Look for the following "automatic failure" criteria defined by UK Construction and Use Regulations:
   - Any bulge, lump, or bubble on the sidewall (indicates internal structural separation).
   - Any cut deep enough to reach the internal cords or ply.
   - Any cut larger than 25mm or 10% of the tyre width that reaches the ply.
   - Any portion of the internal metal or fabric cords visible.
4. **Call for professional assistance rather than attempting a journey.** Contact your breakdown provider or a mobile tyre service and specify that you have "sidewall structural damage" so they send a recovery vehicle or replacement tyre.
   - In England, if you are stopped in a dangerous position on a motorway managed by National Highways, call 0300 123 5000 for assistance.
   - If you are in immediate danger in a live lane and cannot move, call 999.
5. **Locate your locking wheel nut key and spare tyre if applicable.** Ensure you have the equipment ready for the attending professional to speed up the repair.
   - Only attempt to change the wheel yourself if you are in a high-safety area (such as a car park) and have the correct tools and knowledge.
   - Never change a tyre on the side of the vehicle exposed to traffic.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact cause (pothole, kerb, or wear) to make the safety decision to stop.
- You do not need to check the tyre pressure; if the structure is compromised, the pressure reading is irrelevant to safety.
- You do not need to decide which brand of replacement tyre you want until you have reached a place of safety.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frustrated by the sudden interruption to your journey, but identifying this defect now has likely prevented a high-speed blowout. Choosing not to drive on a compromised tyre is the only way to ensure the safety of yourself and other road users.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance only. A qualified tyre technician must inspect the wheel rim and suspension for secondary damage, especially if the tyre defect was caused by a heavy impact.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information and does not replace professional mechanical assessment. UK law (The Road Vehicles Construction and Use Regulations 1986) is strictly enforced regarding tyre condition; driving on a defective tyre may invalidate your insurance and lead to criminal prosecution.
