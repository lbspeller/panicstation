# What to do if… you are asked to show proof of a name change for travel and you cannot access the supporting document (UK)

## Short answer
Ensure your booking name exactly matches your passport name, as carriers are required to verify this against Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) records. If they do not match and you lack the supporting document, request an immediate "name correction" from the airline to align the ticket with your current ID.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a photograph or scan of a document on a mobile device will be accepted as a primary legal link at the boarding gate.
- Do not attempt to pass through security or border control if you know your boarding pass and passport names are different without airline authorization.
- Do not use unofficial "fast-track" certificate websites that are not the General Register Office (GRO), National Records of Scotland, or nidirect.
- Do not delay contacting the airline; many carriers have a 24-hour grace period for free corrections, but fees increase as the departure time approaches.
- Do not sign or pay for a new ticket before asking for a "minor name correction" supervisor review.

## What to do now
1. **Compare the name on your boarding pass or ticket against the machine-readable zone of your passport.** **The names must be identical, including the sequence of surnames and any middle names.** 
   - If you have an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA), ensure the name on the ETA matches the passport exactly.
   - Note any discrepancy (e.g., "Maiden Name" on ticket vs "Married Name" on passport).

2. **Contact the airline or travel agent immediately to request a name correction.** **State clearly that you are the same person but have a name mismatch and cannot produce the secondary linking document (e.g., marriage certificate or deed poll).** 
   - Ask if they can perform a "minor name correction" (usually free or low cost) to match the passport.
   - If the name change is due to a recent marriage or divorce, ask if they will accept a digital confirmation of a replacement certificate order as temporary proof.

3. **Verify if you can re-execute an unenrolled deed poll immediately.** **If your original name change was via an unenrolled deed poll and you have two independent witnesses available, you can sign a new deed poll today.** 
   - Use the standard UK government wording: "I [old name] of [address] abandon the use of my former name and adopt the name [new name]."
   - Ensure both witnesses (not related to you) sign and provide their addresses. This is a legally valid document in the UK.

4. **Order an urgent replacement certificate if the document was issued by a government registry.** **Use the official priority services to generate a paper trail that you can show to airline supervisors.** 
   - **England and Wales:** Use the GRO Priority Service (£38.50) for next-day dispatch if ordered by 4:00 PM.
   - **Scotland:** Use the National Records of Scotland / Scotland's People urgent service for same-day or next-day dispatch.
   - **Northern Ireland:** Use the GRONI priority certificate service via nidirect.

5. **Request a "Customer Service Note" or "Checked Document" flag in your booking.** **If the airline agrees to let you travel despite the mismatch, ask them to update the "Passenger Name Record" (PNR) so the gate staff can see the approval.** 
   - Obtain a reference number for this conversation.
   - If already at the airport, ask for the Duty Manager at the check-in desk to verify the ID.

6. **Check your digital cloud storage and email for a "Certified Copy" or solicitor's scan.** **Search for terms like "registry," "solicitor," "deed," "final order," or "apostille" in all email attachments.** 
   - If you find a scan, check if your airline allows "Digital Document Verification" via their app or website.

## What can wait
- You do not need to update your passport or other permanent IDs while in the middle of a travel crisis.
- You do not need to resolve the underlying reason for the document's absence (e.g., theft or loss) until you have reached a safe pause at your destination.
- You do not need to notify the DVLA or HMRC of the name change until your journey is complete.

## Important reassurance
Name mismatches at check-in are frequent occurrences for UK travellers, particularly following life events like marriage or divorce. Airlines have established "name correction" protocols designed to handle these specific scenarios without requiring a full ticket cancellation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for urgent travel name-mismatch scenarios. It does not cover the long-term legal process of changing a name or the requirements for complex visa applications involving historical name changes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Individual airline policies regarding name corrections and document requirements vary significantly. Always verify the current requirements with your specific carrier or the relevant border authority (UK Home Office / Border Force).
