# What to do if… your email app stops syncing or shows connection errors (UK)

## Short answer
Check whether this is a provider outage or a device/account problem by testing webmail or another device and checking the provider’s official status page. Refresh sign-in safely before deleting apps, wiping devices, or changing lots of settings.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep re-typing your password if the app keeps rejecting it; repeated attempts can sometimes trigger security locks or make the problem harder to track.
- Don’t delete the whole app or wipe your phone as a first move; you may lose settings, offline mail, or useful clues about what changed.
- Don’t click “verify your account” links inside unexpected emails, texts, or pop-ups; phishing often imitates connection errors.
- Don’t change lots of settings at once; change one thing, then test, so you can reverse it.
- Don’t assume you have been hacked just because syncing stopped; outages, expired sign-ins, VPN changes, and background settings are common causes.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether the email service is down.**
   - iCloud Mail: check Apple’s System Status.
   - Gmail or Google Workspace: check the Google Workspace Status Dashboard.
   - Outlook.com or Microsoft 365: check Microsoft’s public service status page. For a work or school Microsoft 365 account, tenant-specific detail may only be visible to an admin or help desk in Microsoft 365 Service health.
   - If there is an outage, stop troubleshooting and use webmail in a browser if it works.

2. **Work out whether the problem is the app, the device, or the account.**
   - Try signing in through webmail in a browser.
   - Check the same mailbox on another device if you have one.
   - If webmail works but the app does not, the cause is more likely to be the app, cached sign-in, device settings, VPN, or network.
   - If webmail also fails, the cause is more likely to be password, account lock, provider outage, or account security.

3. **Do a quick connection reset without deleting anything.**
   - Turn flight mode or Airplane Mode on, wait a few seconds, then turn it off.
   - Switch between Wi-Fi and mobile data.
   - Turn off VPN, private relay, workplace filtering, or security filtering temporarily, then test again.
   - Restart the device.

4. **Check sync settings and storage issues that commonly stop mail arriving.**
   - Make sure automatic date and time is enabled.
   - Check whether your mailbox or cloud storage is full, especially for iCloud Mail or other accounts that share mail storage with cloud storage.
   - On iPhone or iPad, check that the mail account is enabled and that Fetch or Push settings are not paused.
   - On Android, check that account auto-sync is enabled and that battery saver or data saver is not restricting the email app.
   - In Gmail, check that Gmail sync is enabled for the affected account.
   - In Outlook, use the app’s reset or repair option for the affected account if it is available.

5. **Refresh sign-in safely.**
   - Use the app’s official sign-in prompt only if it opens inside the app or provider sign-in page you recognise.
   - If you use two-step verification, complete the fresh sign-in prompt.
   - If the account is for work or school, you may need to re-approve device access or meet a new security requirement.
   - If a sign-in prompt looks unexpected, close it and sign in through the provider’s official website or app instead.

6. **If the app has a reset or re-sync option, use that before removing anything.**
   - Look for options such as refresh, re-sync, reset account, repair account, or clear sync state.
   - Use the option only for the affected account.
   - Test whether new mail arrives before making another change.

7. **If it is still broken, remove and re-add only the affected account.**
   - Before removing it, note any manual setup details such as IMAP, SMTP, Exchange server name, username, or security setting.
   - Remove the account from the mail app or device settings, not the whole app.
   - Restart the device.
   - Add the account back using the provider’s official sign-in flow.

8. **If it is a work or school account, involve IT before going further if any of these apply.**
   - You are asked to install a profile, device management tool, certificate, or new security app you were not expecting.
   - Webmail works but the app keeps asking for credentials.
   - The issue started after a workplace security or access policy change.
   - You see messages about device compliance, conditional access, or administrator approval.

9. **If this started after a suspicious message or login prompt, treat it as possible phishing.**
   - Do not click links or open attachments in the suspicious message.
   - Forward suspicious emails to report@phishing.gov.uk, then delete them.
   - If you entered your password somewhere unusual, change it from the provider’s official website or app as soon as you can and review recent sign-in activity.
   - If money was lost, an account was taken over, or you think fraud or cybercrime has happened, use the official reporting route for where you live in the UK.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to switch email apps or move email providers.
- You do not need to clean up old emails, reorganise folders, or delete large amounts of mail unless your provider clearly says storage is full.
- You do not need to deep-diagnose IMAP, SMTP, or Exchange server settings unless you use a manual setup and the basic checks fail.
- You do not need to reinstall the whole app until you have checked outage status, webmail, network, sync settings, storage, and account sign-in.

## Important reassurance
Email sync failures are common and are often caused by an outage, expired sign-in, VPN or network change, background restriction, storage limit, or provider security check. Small, reversible steps are usually safer than trying to fix everything at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps to restore syncing and avoid irreversible mistakes. Later decisions, especially for a managed work or school account or suspected account compromise, may need specialist IT or security help.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional IT, security, legal, financial, therapeutic, medical, or other professional advice. If this is a managed device or account, follow your organisation’s policies.
