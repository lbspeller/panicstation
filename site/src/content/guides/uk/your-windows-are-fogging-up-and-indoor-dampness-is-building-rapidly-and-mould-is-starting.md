# What to do if… your windows are fogging up and indoor dampness is building rapidly and mould is starting (UK)

## Short answer
Contain the moisture immediately by ventilating affected rooms, wiping down all wet surfaces, and checking for a concealed leak. Report the issue to your landlord or housing provider in writing to trigger 2026 statutory repair timescales.

## Do not do these things
- Do not paint, wallpaper, or apply sealant over active mould growth, as this traps moisture and allows spores to spread internally.
- Do not dry-brush, sand, or vacuum mouldy patches, which releases harmful spores into the air.
- Do not mix different cleaning chemicals, particularly bleach and ammonia-based products, which creates toxic gas.
- Do not block air bricks, trickle vents, or extractor fans in an attempt to retain heat.
- Do not ignore water stains on ceilings or damp patches near electrical sockets, light switches, or fuse boxes.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the most affected rooms to prevent moisture spread.**  
   Close internal doors to bathrooms and kitchens while in use and immediately afterward. Open windows on the latch or ensure trickle vents are fully open in these areas to allow moisture-heavy air to escape.

2. **Wipe away all standing liquid from windows and surfaces.**  
   Use highly absorbent towels or a squeegee to remove water from glass, frames, and sills every morning and after cooking or bathing.
   - Wring out cloths into a sink or drain, not back into the room.
   - Dry any wet walls or corners using a clean cloth to prevent water soaking into the plaster.

3. **Manage laundry and cooking moisture at the source.**  
   Avoid drying clothes on radiators; if drying indoors is necessary, use a single room with the door shut and a window open.
   - Use lids on all pans while cooking.
   - Leave extractor fans running for at least 20 minutes after finishing a shower or cooking.

4. **Conduct a rapid check for structural leaks or plumbing failures.**  
   Inspect the floor under sinks, around radiator valves, and behind the washing machine for pooling water. 
   - Check the external wall outside the damp area for leaking gutters or overflow pipes.
   - If a leak is found near electrical installations, do not touch the area and contact an emergency plumber or your landlord immediately.

5. **Clean early-stage mould patches using safe methods.**  
   If the patch is smaller than one square metre and you do not have respiratory issues, wipe the area with a cloth soaked in soapy water.
   - Wear waterproof gloves and a face mask during cleaning.
   - Dry the area thoroughly after cleaning and leave the window open for ventilation.
   - Stop immediately if you feel wheezy, breathless, or if the mould covers a large area.

6. **Submit a formal written report to your landlord to start the legal clock.**  
   Take clear, dated photographs of the damp, mould, and fogged windows as evidence.
   - **Social Housing (England):** Under Awaab’s Law, your landlord must investigate significant hazards within 10 working days and begin repairs within a further 5 working days. Emergency hazards must be investigated within 24 hours.
   - **Private Rented Sector (UK):** Under the Renters' Rights Act 2026, report the issue in writing; landlords must now follow similar strict timelines to investigate (typically 14 days) and remediate hazards that make a home "non-decent."

7. **Escalate to local authorities if the landlord fails to respond.**  
   If you do not receive an inspection or a repair plan within the statutory window, contact your local council’s Environmental Health department. They can issue an improvement notice or a fine of up to £30,000 for non-compliance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace furniture or carpets immediately unless they are heavily contaminated with mould.
- You do not need to purchase expensive anti-mould paints or specialized equipment before a professional inspection has identified the root cause.
- You do not need to decide on long-term insulation or heating upgrades today.

## Important reassurance
Sudden condensation and early mould growth are common in UK homes during cold weather or after a change in household moisture levels. Taking immediate steps to wipe surfaces and ventilate provides immediate stabilization while you wait for a professional assessment or landlord response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for acute dampness and early mould. It does not replace a professional survey for rising damp, penetrating damp, or structural defects.

## Important note
If anyone in the household is under 5 years old, pregnant, elderly, or has a pre-existing respiratory or immune condition, contact NHS 111 for health advice and treat the property repair as an urgent priority. This guide is for information purposes and does not constitute legal or medical advice.
