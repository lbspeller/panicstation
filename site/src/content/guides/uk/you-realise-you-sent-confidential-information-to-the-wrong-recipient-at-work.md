# What to do if… you realise you sent confidential information to the wrong recipient at work (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all deletion, modification, or movement of data related to the request and acknowledge receipt to the named legal or IT contact. Under the Civil Procedure Rules (CPR), specifically Practice Direction 57AD, you have an immediate individual duty to preserve potentially relevant documents once a hold is issued.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete, rename, or edit any documents, drafts, or communication threads mentioned in the hold notice.
- Do not move files to personal cloud storage, USB drives, or personal email accounts to "keep them safe."
- Do not attempt to "filter" the documents yourself by only saving what you believe is relevant or helpful.
- Do not discuss the contents of the hold notice in group chats, Slack channels, or Teams meetings unless authorized.
- Do not forward the hold notice to colleagues who are not explicitly named in the communication.
- Do not assume that the IT department's standard backups are sufficient to meet your personal preservation duty.

## What to do now
1. **Cease all manual and automated deletion activities immediately upon reading the notice.** This includes emptying your "Deleted Items" or "Junk" folders in Outlook, clearing browser caches where work data is stored, and shredding physical notebooks or printouts.

2. **Disable any personal "auto-archive" or "auto-delete" rules you have configured in your mailbox or chat apps.** **The entire first sentence of each step MUST be bolded.** If you use "disappearing messages" on work-related WhatsApp or Signal threads, disable this feature for the relevant contacts immediately.

3. **Identify the core essentials of the hold notice without moving or exporting any data.** Note the specific date range, the names of projects or individuals involved, and the specific systems (e.g., SharePoint, local drives, or mobile apps) that must be preserved.

4. **Send a formal acknowledgement of the notice to the designated legal, IT, or HR contact.** Confirm that you have received the instruction and state that you have suspended deletion practices for all accounts and devices under your control.

5. **Ask the legal or IT contact to confirm if a "technical hold" has been applied to your accounts.** A technical hold is an automated system-level freeze that ensures data is kept even if you accidentally delete it; knowing if this is active reduces the risk of manual error.

6. **Create a simple list of locations where relevant information exists without performing any "data collection" yourself.** List your local laptop folders, specific Teams channels, SharePoint sites, and any paper files, then provide this list to the legal team so they can manage the collection.

7. **Disclose the existence of work-related data on personal devices or accounts if applicable.** If you used a personal phone for work chats or a personal email for work documents, inform the legal contact of this immediately rather than trying to migrate the data to work systems.

8. **Maintain professional and neutral communication across all work channels from this moment forward.** Assume that any new messages or documents created after the hold notice may eventually be disclosed and reviewed by external parties or the court.

## What can wait
- You do not need to perform a "search" for relevant documents until you are given specific search terms or instructions.
- You do not need to determine the legal "relevance" of your files; the legal team will make those determinations later.
- You do not need to worry about storage quotas; if your mailbox is full because of the hold, notify IT and let them provide the solution.
- You do not need to prepare a written statement or "recap" of events unless explicitly requested by your legal representative.

## Important reassurance
A legal hold is a standard procedural step in the UK legal system and does not necessarily imply that you have done anything wrong or are being investigated personally. It is a protective measure to ensure that if a court or regulator needs to see evidence, it is available and untampered with. Following the instructions precisely is the best way to protect both yourself and your organization.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only to prevent the "spoliation" (destruction) of evidence. Specific procedures for data extraction or witness interviews will be provided by your employer's legal counsel or appointed specialists.

## Important note
This guide is for general informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 and 2026 UK GDPR updates, the "legal obligation" to preserve data generally overrides standard "storage limitation" (deletion) requirements. If you are concerned about your personal data or privacy during a work-related legal hold, consult the named Data Protection Officer (DPO) or seek independent legal advice. Note that under the 2026 Public Domain Documents pilot (PD 51ZH), documents used in High Court hearings may eventually become accessible to the public.
