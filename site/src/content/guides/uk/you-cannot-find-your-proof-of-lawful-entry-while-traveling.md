# What to do if… you cannot find your proof of lawful entry while traveling (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and ask exactly what proof they need. If this is about your UK immigration permission and you have an eVisa, sign in to your UKVI account and get a share code for travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess, bluff, or hand over documents that are not yours.
- Do not say you are “illegal” or “overstayed” just because you cannot find a paper record.
- Do not hand your phone or passport to anyone except official or carrier staff; ask what they need to check and whether it can be done at the desk.
- Do not delete emails, PDFs, photos, or messages that might contain your status details.
- Do not pay a stranger or “agent” at the airport to “fix it”.
- Do not rely on an expired BRP alone as proof for travel if you can access your eVisa or other valid proof.

## What to do now
1. **Ask one calm, specific question:** “What exact proof do you need — a visa, a valid physical immigration document, an online status check, an entry stamp, or an onward ticket?”
2. **Check whether this is about UK immigration permission.** If it is not, ask which country’s entry or transit requirement they are checking before you search through unrelated documents.
3. **If you have an eVisa, sign in to your UKVI account.** Check that your current passport or travel document is added and that your name, passport number, and expiry date match your booking.
4. **Get a share code for travel.** Save the code and the screen that shows it. Write the code down as well, because signal, battery, or airport Wi-Fi can fail.
5. **Gather any valid physical proof you have.** This may include a visa vignette, a sticker or stamp showing indefinite leave to enter or remain, a right of abode certificate of entitlement in a passport, or a Home Office travel document.
6. **Keep helpful backup evidence together.** Put your passport ID page, UKVI decision email or letter, share code screen, and any relevant physical-document photos in one easy-to-find folder on your phone.
7. **If the carrier says they cannot see your eVisa, ask them to contact the UK Border Force carrier support hub.** This is something the carrier should do; you do not need to find a public phone number for it.
8. **If you cannot sign in to your UKVI account, use the official account recovery route.** If you can sign in but your eVisa is wrong or you cannot get a share code because of a technical error, use the official eVisa error-reporting route.
9. **If you are being refused boarding or kept at a document desk, ask for the specific reason and the minimum proof that would change the decision.** Repeat back the requirement in plain words, then show only the documents or screens that match it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to explain your whole immigration history at the desk.
- You do not need to decide whether to complain, escalate, or post online while the immediate check is still happening.
- You do not need to replace every document today if you can produce acceptable digital or physical proof for this specific check.
- You do not need to solve long-term account or record problems before you have dealt with the immediate travel decision.

## Important reassurance
Being asked for proof does not automatically mean your status is wrong. Staff may be using “proof of lawful entry” as shorthand for a specific carrier or border check, and the fastest next step is to identify the exact proof they need.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to help you handle the immediate travel check and avoid making the situation worse. Later decisions about missing records, incorrect eVisa details, refusal of boarding, or immigration status may need specialist immigration advice.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate practical harm-prevention. It is not legal, immigration, travel, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Immigration checks can depend on your route, nationality, document type, and the system the carrier or official is using.
