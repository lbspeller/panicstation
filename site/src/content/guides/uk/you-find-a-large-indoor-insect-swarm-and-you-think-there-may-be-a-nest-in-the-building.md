# What to do if… you find a large indoor insect swarm and you think there may be a nest in the building (UK)

## Short answer
Evacuate the immediate area, close the door to contain the swarm, and check everyone for stings. If anyone shows signs of a severe allergic reaction, such as noisy breathing or swelling of the throat, call 999 immediately and say "anaphylaxis".

## Do not do these things
- Do not swat, vacuum, or use aerosol sprays on a large swarm as this can provoke defensive stinging and spread the insects.
- Do not block or seal the hole where insects are entering or exiting, as this may force them deeper into the building's interior.
- Do not enter roof voids, lofts, or small spaces to investigate a nest without professional protective equipment.
- Do not use "smoke bombs" or outdoor-only chemicals inside a residential property.
- Do not attempt to remove an established nest in a wall or chimney yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Move all people and pets to a separate room and close the door.** **The most effective way to reduce immediate risk is to create a physical barrier between yourself and the swarm.**
   - Place a towel or draft excluder at the base of the door if insects are crawling underneath.
2. **Check every person for signs of a medical emergency.** **Call 999 immediately if anyone has difficulty breathing, noisy breathing, a hoarse voice, or if their skin, lips, or tongue look blue, grey, or pale.**
   - Tell the operator you suspect "anaphylaxis".
   - If the person has a prescribed adrenaline auto-injector, help them use it if they are unable to do so themselves.
3. **Turn off any fans or ventilation systems that could move insects through the property.** **Reducing air movement helps prevent the swarm from being pushed into other rooms or hallways.**
   - Only do this if the controls are located away from the main insect cluster.
4. **Observe the insects from a safe distance to help with identification.** **Note if they are entering or exiting from a specific point, such as a light fitting, vent, or gap in the skirting board.**
   - If safe, take a zoomed photo with a phone to show a professional.
   - Look for a yellow-legged appearance; if you suspect a Yellow-legged Asian Hornet, do not approach, but report it via the official "Asian Hornet Watch" app.
5. **Contact a professional to manage the nest removal.** **Do not attempt to DIY a structural nest, as specialized equipment is required to ensure the entire colony and any honey or comb is removed.**
   - For honey bees, contact a specialist bee removal company (voluntary beekeepers usually only collect external swarms and do not perform structural "cut-outs").
   - For wasps or unknown insects, contact a British Pest Control Association (BPCA) or NPTA certified technician.
6. **Notify your landlord or building manager immediately if you are a tenant or live in a shared building.** **In social housing, severe infestations may be treated as emergency hazards requiring a 24-hour response under Awaab's Law.**
   - Put your report in writing (email or text) to document the time the issue was discovered.
7. **Keep windows and doors in the "swarm room" closed unless they can be opened from the outside.** **Opening an internal window while standing near a swarm can cause the insects to become defensive.**
   - Wait for professional guidance before attempting to vent the room.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact species of the insect right now.
- You do not need to decide on permanent building repairs or "bee-proofing" today.
- You do not need to buy pesticides or DIY traps.
- You do not need to worry about structural damage in the next few hours; the priority is containment and safety.

## Important reassurance
Sudden swarms often appear more dangerous than they are because of the noise and volume of insects. Once you have moved to a different room and closed the door, the immediate risk of being stung is significantly lower. Professional pest controllers deal with these situations daily and can stabilize the building quickly.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. Long-term removal, structural repairs to prevent re-entry, and legal disputes over pest control costs require specialist professional or legal advice.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information and is not a substitute for medical or professional pest control advice. Always prioritize emergency medical services if a sting causes systemic symptoms or respiratory distress.
