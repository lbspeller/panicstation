# What to do if… a group ahead is blocking your path and you feel unsafe (UK)

## Short answer
Do not walk into the choke point. Create space by turning away, crossing the road if it is safe, or stepping into a staffed public place, and call 999 if you think you are in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not push through the group to test whether they will move.
- Do not argue, explain yourself, or respond to taunts.
- Do not stop in front of them to film if it slows your exit or draws more attention.
- Do not let yourself be funnelled into a doorway, alley, underpass, or place with no easy exit.
- Do not go straight home if you think you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Change direction before you are close.** Turn around as if you forgot something, cross the road if it is safe, or take the next open turn. The aim is distance and an easier exit.
2. **Move to somewhere staffed and visible.** Go into a shop, café, hotel lobby, bank, pharmacy, station office, or other public place with staff and other people. Stay near the counter or entrance, not at the back on your own.
3. **Use staff as a safety buffer.** Say: “I feel unsafe. Can I stay here a moment?” Ask them to keep you near staff and call 999 if the group is still outside, following you, threatening you, or blocking you from leaving.
4. **Make your location known without escalating.** Call someone you trust and say clearly: “I’m inside [place] on [street]. I’m staying here because a group outside made me feel unsafe.” Keep the call factual.
5. **If they are closing in, blocking you, threatening violence, or you fear violence, call 999.** If speaking would make you less safe, move closer to staff first and ask for a safer place nearby, such as staying by the counter or in a staff-directed area.
6. **If it is not an emergency but it felt threatening, report it once you are safe.** Call 101 or use your local police force’s online reporting route. Give the location, time, direction of travel, number of people, what they did, and where you went for safety.
7. **If you think hostility towards who you are may have been involved, say that when reporting.** This can include hostility linked to disability, transgender identity, race, religion or belief, or sexual orientation. You can say you are unsure and ask for it to be recorded and assessed.
8. **If this is on or near the rail network, use the rail route when safe.** In an emergency, call 999. For non-emergency reporting to British Transport Police, text 61016 when it is safe to do so.
9. **Make a short note after you are away from them.** Write the landmark, street, time, group size, direction they went, what they did, and any clear clothing or features you remember.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it legally counts as a crime.
- You do not need to confront them, prove anything, or collect perfect evidence.
- You do not need to write a full statement now; a short note is enough.
- You do not need to post about it or warn others online today.
- You do not need to decide your next normal route until you are calm and somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
Avoiding a choke point is a sensible safety move. You are allowed to prioritise distance, visibility, and support over politeness.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and buy time. If this becomes repeated targeting, later decisions may need specialist help or more tailored safety planning.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger or someone is being harmed, call 999.
