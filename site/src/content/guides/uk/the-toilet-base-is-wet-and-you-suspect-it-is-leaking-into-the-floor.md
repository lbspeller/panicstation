# What to do if… the toilet base is wet and you suspect it is leaking into the floor (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the toilet, isolate the toilet water supply if you can, and keep the area dry while you arrange an urgent repair. Water at the base can run under flooring even when the visible puddle looks small.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep flushing to test it if water is appearing at the base.
- Don’t keep topping up towels and assume it is solved; water can track under flooring.
- Don’t put sealant around the outside of the toilet base as a temporary fix; it can trap water and hide the source.
- Don’t touch sockets, switches, lights, or appliances if you are wet or standing in water.
- Don’t force a stuck stop tap or valve; forcing it can damage it.
- Don’t remove the toilet yourself unless you are competent and the water is isolated.

## What to do now
1. **Make the bathroom safer.** Keep people and pets out, put towels down to reduce slipping, and stop anyone using the toilet.

2. **Try to isolate the toilet first.**
   - Look for the small valve on the pipe feeding the cistern.
   - If it has a handle, turn it clockwise until it stops naturally.
   - If it is a slotted isolation valve, a quarter turn usually closes it when the slot is across the pipe.
   - Do not force it. If it will not turn or water continues, move to the stop tap.

3. **Turn off your internal stop tap if needed.**
   - The internal stop tap is often under the kitchen sink, near where the water pipe enters the property, or in a cupboard, utility room, bathroom, cellar, hallway, or garage.
   - Turn it clockwise slowly to close it. It may take a few minutes for water to stop fully.
   - Open a cold tap briefly to check whether the flow slows or stops.
   - In a flat or shared building, you may have a valve inside your flat or a communal valve. If you cannot find or safely use it, contact your landlord, building manager, managing agent, or an emergency plumber.

4. **Stop further wetting.**
   - Put a clear note on the toilet so nobody flushes it by habit.
   - Mop up standing water and wring towels into a bucket.
   - If the water smells foul, looks discoloured, or may be coming from the waste pipe rather than the clean supply, avoid splashes, keep children and pets away, and wash hands thoroughly after contact.

5. **Check where the water may be spreading.**
   - Look at the ceiling below, if there is one, and nearby walls, skirting boards, and flooring.
   - If you see dripping, bulging plaster, a spreading stain, or a soft floor around the toilet, keep the area below clear and treat the repair as urgent.

6. **Handle electrical risk cautiously.**
   - If there is sparking, smoke, a burning smell, electric shocks, or water entering light fittings, leave the area and call 999.
   - If water is close to electrical items and you can reach the consumer unit without standing in water or touching wet surfaces, switch off the affected circuit or the main switch.
   - If you are unsure, do not touch the electrics. Keep away and ask an emergency plumber, electrician, landlord, or emergency operator for help.

7. **Contact the right repair route.**
   - If you rent, report it to your landlord, letting agent, council, housing association, or emergency repairs line immediately. Say it is a toilet leak with possible water going into the floor or ceiling below.
   - If you own the home, contact an emergency plumber or your home emergency provider if you have one.
   - Tell them what you have done: “water at toilet base, toilet not being used, water isolated at the toilet valve or stop tap if possible, possible leak into floor.”

8. **Document quickly, then keep drying.**
   - Take a few photos or a short video of the wet base, the floor, and any marks below.
   - Keep the room ventilated with an open window or extractor fan if available and safe.
   - Keep replacing wet towels until the leak is isolated or help arrives.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether the problem is the toilet seal, pan connector, soil pipe, cistern, or supply pipe.
- You don’t need to pull up flooring or remove the toilet right now unless a qualified person tells you to.
- You don’t need to solve insurance, complaints, or longer repairs before the water is isolated and the area is safe.
- You can check your insurance policy after stabilising the leak, unless your policy or emergency cover requires immediate notification.

## Important reassurance
A wet toilet base is stressful because the source is not always obvious. The useful first steps are simple: stop use, isolate water where you safely can, limit spread, and get the repair route started.

## Scope note
This is first steps only to reduce damage and buy time. Finding and repairing the cause, checking the subfloor, and drying hidden water may need a plumber, landlord repair team, electrician, drying specialist, or insurer.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional plumbing, electrical, legal, insurance, medical, or health advice. If you cannot stop the water, water is near electrics, or dampness is spreading to ceilings, walls, or another property, treat it as urgent and get qualified help.
