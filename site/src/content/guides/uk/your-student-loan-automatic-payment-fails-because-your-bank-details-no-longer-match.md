# What to do if… your student loan automatic payment fails because your bank details no longer match (UK)

## Short answer
Sign in to your official Student Loans Company (SLC) repayment account independently to verify the failure, make a one-off manual payment if a balance is due, and update your bank details or Direct Debit mandate.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click any links in a text or email claiming your payment failed or that details need updating.
- Do not provide your full bank account number, sort code, or login credentials to anyone who contacts you by phone or message.
- Do not ignore the message even if you think it is a mistake, as missed payments can lead to arrears and penalty interest.
- Do not repeatedly attempt the same payment through an app if it has already been rejected once.
- Do not assume your employer-based repayments (PAYE) are affected; bank details only apply to Direct Debits or voluntary payments.

## What to do now
1. **Determine which payment method is actually supposed to be active.**
   - If you are employed in the UK, your repayments are usually deducted automatically from your salary via PAYE; these do not use your bank details.
   - If you are paying via Direct Debit (common for those near the end of their loan or those overseas), check your bank statement to see if a mandate was recently cancelled.

2. **Access your official student loan repayment account via GOV.UK.**
   - Navigate directly to the "Manage your student loan balance" page on the GOV.UK website.
   - Do not use any "Sign in" buttons provided in the notification message you received.
   - Check the "Messages" or "Correspondence" section of your account for official notification of a payment failure.

3. **Make a one-off manual payment if a scheduled amount was missed.**
   - Use the "Make a payment" option within the official portal to cover the missed instalment immediately.
   - For overseas borrowers, use the provided IBAN details for an international bank transfer if the online card payment fails.
   - Keep a record of the transaction reference number once the payment is confirmed.

4. **Update your bank account information in the repayment portal.**
   - Enter your current sort code and account number in the "Bank details" section of your online account.
   - Set up a new Direct Debit mandate if the previous one is shown as "Cancelled" or "Inactive."
   - Ensure the name on the bank account exactly matches the name on your student loan record.

5. **Verify your account status with your bank.**
   - Log in to your banking app or website to confirm if the Student Loans Company mandate still appears in your active Direct Debits.
   - Check for any "Unpaid Item" fees or notifications from your bank that explain why the transaction was blocked (e.g., account closed, insufficient funds, or technical mismatch).

6. **Report suspicious communications to the SLC Economic Crime Unit.**
   - If the original message contained a link or asked for sensitive data, forward it to report@phishing.gov.uk.
   - You can also call the SLC fraud hotline on 0300 100 0059 to confirm if they actually sent the alert.

7. **Submit an Overseas Income Assessment if you have moved abroad.**
   - If you are living outside the UK for more than three months, you must use the separate employment details service to keep your account in good standing.
   - Failure to update this can result in "fixed instalment" charges which are often much higher than income-contingent repayments.

## What can wait
- You do not need to calculate your total remaining balance or settlement figure today.
- You do not need to contact HMRC if you are a PAYE employee; the SLC manages Direct Debit issues independently.
- You do not need to apply for a refund for any bank fees immediately; focus on securing the account first.

## Important reassurance
Payment failures due to technical mismatches are common, especially following a bank switch or a change in personal details. As long as you verify the issue through official channels and cover the missed payment promptly, it is unlikely to have a long-term impact on your account standing.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for a payment failure or bank detail mismatch. It does not cover long-term debt management, disputes over loan balances, or specific tax advice for self-employed individuals.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute financial advice. Student loan repayment rules are subject to change and vary based on your specific loan plan (Plan 1, 2, 4, 5, or Postgraduate). Always follow the instructions provided within the official GOV.UK portal or by Student Loans Company advisors.
