# What to do if… a money transfer recipient cannot collect because of a name mismatch (UK)

## Short answer
Pause before sending any more money. Check the recipient’s name exactly as it appears on the ID they will use for collection, then contact the transfer provider through its official app, website, phone line, or help centre to ask whether the receiver name can be corrected or the transfer must be cancelled and resent.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send a second transfer “just in case” until you know whether the first transfer can be corrected, cancelled, refunded, or is still pending.
- Don’t pay a separate “release fee”, “name change fee”, tax, or clearance charge to a new bank account, gift card, crypto wallet, or unknown person.
- Don’t share the full reference number or collection number publicly, in group chats, or with anyone except the intended recipient and the provider’s official support.
- Don’t guess the name format. Missing middle names, swapped surnames, nicknames, hyphens, spaces, or accents can stop collection.
- Don’t keep sending the recipient to different agent locations before the provider confirms what needs changing.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the recipient to type the name exactly as shown on their ID.** They should type the full name they will use for collection, including order, spelling, spaces, hyphens, middle names, and any accents. Avoid asking for ID photos unless the provider specifically requires them, and do not send ID details through an unsafe channel.

2. **Compare it with the receiver name on your transfer receipt.** Check the provider app, email, SMS, or paper receipt. Look for a nickname instead of a legal name, a missing middle name, a swapped surname order, an extra surname, a missing hyphen, or a spelling difference.

3. **Check the transfer status through the provider’s official tracking route.** Confirm whether it is pending, available for pickup, cancelled, refunded, or paid out. If it says paid out and the intended recipient says they did not receive it, contact the provider immediately and ask them to lock, investigate, or start their fraud or dispute process.

4. **Contact the provider and ask what correction is allowed.** Use only the official app, website, help centre, phone line, or chat. Say: “The recipient cannot collect because the receiver name does not match their ID. Can you amend the receiver name, or do I need to cancel and resend?” Have ready:
   - the transfer or reference number
   - your sender details used for the transfer
   - the current receiver name on the transfer
   - the corrected receiver name exactly as shown on the recipient’s ID

5. **Wait for confirmation before the recipient tries again.** Ask the provider whether the name has been updated, whether the recipient must wait before going back, what ID is accepted at that pickup location, and whether that location needs anything else such as proof of address. Requirements can vary by country, provider, and agent.

6. **If the name cannot be changed, ask about cancellation, refund, and fees.** Some providers may allow a receiver-name change, some may allow only limited edits, and some may require cancellation and a new transfer. Ask how the refund will be returned, whether any fee or exchange-rate difference applies, and how long the refund usually takes for your payment method.

7. **Stop if the situation starts to look like a scam.** Warning signs include pressure to send more money, a new “pickup person”, a request for a separate release fee, instructions not to contact the provider, or a request to send the reference number to someone else. Ask the provider whether the transaction can be cancelled, locked, or reviewed.

8. **If the provider is not helping, make a formal complaint.** Use the provider’s complaints process and keep the receipt, transfer status, messages, and notes of any calls. For many international money transfer complaints, the business must respond within 15 business days, or explain why it needs more time and respond within 35 business days. If you are still unhappy after the final response, or the provider does not respond in time, check whether you can take the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether to stop using the provider forever.
- You don’t need to argue with agent-location staff; the provider’s transfer record is the thing that usually needs checking.
- You don’t need to collect a large evidence file right now. Start with the receipt, the transfer status, the exact ID name, and any provider messages.
- You don’t need to choose between correction and cancellation until the provider tells you what is possible for this transfer.

## Important reassurance
A name mismatch is a known reason for a cash pickup or money transfer payout to be blocked. It does not automatically mean the money is lost; it usually means the provider needs to check the transfer record, the recipient’s ID, or whether the transfer must be cancelled and resent.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, reduce scam risk, and work out whether the transfer can be corrected, cancelled, refunded, or disputed. If money has been paid out to the wrong person, or you suspect fraud, later decisions may need the provider’s specialist fraud team, the complaints process, or independent help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, fraud-investigation, or professional advice. Processes vary by provider, payment method, destination country, and pickup location. If anyone pressures you to send more money, share codes, or avoid official support, pause and verify directly with the provider through its official channels.
