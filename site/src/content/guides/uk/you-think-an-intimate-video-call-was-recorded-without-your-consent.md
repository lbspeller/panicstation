# What to do if… you think an intimate video call was recorded without your consent (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a safer pause, stop contact with the person who may have recorded it, and do not pay, send more images, or negotiate. Then use specialist support and reporting tools to reduce the chance of sharing.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay, send more images, or agree to “prove” anything to make it stop.
- Don’t keep arguing, threatening, or negotiating in live chat.
- Don’t delete messages, usernames, payment demands, threats, or account details in a panic.
- Don’t post about it publicly right now.
- Don’t blame yourself for agreeing to the call or for trusting someone.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you feel in immediate danger, call **999**. If you are not in immediate danger, move somewhere you feel safer, such as another room, a friend’s place, or a place where the person cannot keep pressuring you live.
2. **Stop contact.** End the call or chat. Do not answer new demands. Block or mute the person on the app they are using if that helps you stop replying.
3. **Keep what you already have.** Save screenshots or notes of usernames, profile links, dates, times, threats, payment demands, and any posted URLs. Keep this light-touch; you do not need to build a full evidence pack right now.
4. **Report it on the app or site.** Use the platform’s reporting option for harassment, threats, blackmail, or intimate image abuse. If anything has already been posted, report the post or account on that site too.
5. **Get specialist support.** The **Revenge Porn Helpline** supports UK adults aged 18+ experiencing intimate image abuse and can help with reporting and removal steps. It can be contacted on **0345 6000 459** or **help@revengepornhelpline.org.uk**, usually **Monday to Friday, 10am to 4pm, excluding bank holidays**.
6. **If this is sexual violence support you need, use a confidential support line.** If you are in England or Wales and aged 16+, **Rape Crisis England & Wales** has a free **24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line** and online chat. If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical help, call **999**.
7. **If you are being threatened for money or more images, treat it as sextortion.** If you feel at immediate risk, call **999**. Otherwise, you can contact your local police on **101** or report through the relevant police online route.
8. **Use a prevention tool if it fits.** If you are an adult and you have the image or video file, **StopNCII.org** can create a digital fingerprint of the file on your device to help participating platforms detect and block sharing. If you do not have the file, skip this step.
9. **Reduce further access.** Change the password on the email, phone account, or video app account involved. Turn on two-step verification where available, and check who can contact you or find your profile in the app.

**If you were under 18 at the time, or you are not sure:**
10. **Use youth-specific help.** Tell a trusted adult if you safely can. You or the adult can use the **CEOP Safety Centre** if you are worried about online sexual abuse or grooming. If sexual images or videos of you may be online, use **Report Remove** through Childline and the Internet Watch Foundation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to make a formal police report.
- You do not need to contact the person’s employer, family, school, or friends right now.
- You do not need to explain yourself online.
- You do not need to know the exact legal label before getting support.
- You do not need to search the whole internet tonight.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shocked, ashamed, angry, frightened, or numb is a common reaction when an intimate boundary may have been violated. Recording, threatening, or sharing intimate material without consent is something done to you; it is not something you caused. You deserve support even if you are unsure what was captured.

## Scope note
These are first steps to help you stabilise, reduce immediate harm, and reach specialist support. Later decisions about reporting, takedown escalation, school, work, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**. If you were under 18 at the time of any image or video, treat it as a child protection concern and seek specialist help promptly.
