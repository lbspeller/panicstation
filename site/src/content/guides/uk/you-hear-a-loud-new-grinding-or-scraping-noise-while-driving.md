# What to do if… you hear a loud new grinding or scraping noise while driving (UK)

## Short answer
Treat a new loud grinding or scraping noise as a possible safety fault. Stay out of traffic, do only a quick visual check from a safe position, and arrange help or recovery if you cannot clearly identify a harmless cause.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving at normal speed to see if the noise goes away.
- Don’t put any part of your body under the vehicle at the roadside.
- Don’t attempt repairs on a motorway or other high-speed road.
- Don’t place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Don’t touch a wheel, brake area, or anything near the exhaust if it may be hot.
- Don’t stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or step into the carriageway to look or listen.
- Don’t hand over keys, money, or personal details to an unexpected recovery vehicle until you are satisfied it is the help you requested.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safe before checking the noise.** Put hazard lights on. If the vehicle is in or close to a live lane, near a bend, in poor visibility, or somewhere you cannot safely wait, call **999** and say you are stopped in a dangerous position.

2. **If you are on a motorway, use motorway breakdown guidance.**
   - If you are in an emergency area and there is a free emergency telephone, use it before trying to rejoin or move on.
   - If there is a free emergency roadside telephone nearby and you can reach it safely, use it.
   - On motorways in England, you can call **National Highways on 0300 123 5000** for advice and assistance, then contact your breakdown provider.
   - If you cannot safely leave the vehicle, are stopped in a live lane, are not near a free emergency telephone, or feel in immediate danger, keep seatbelts on, keep hazard lights on, call **999**, and ask for the police.

3. **Check for stop-now warning signs from a safe place.** Look for smoke, flames, sparks, a burning smell, a rapidly deflating tyre, liquid pouring out, or the vehicle sitting unusually low. If there is smoke, fire risk, or a possible fuel leak, move people away from the vehicle if you can do so safely and call **999**.

4. **Do a quick outside-only walkaround if it is safe.** Use a phone torch and stay away from traffic. Look for:
   - a flat tyre, shredded tyre edge, or tyre rubbing against the wheel arch
   - something trapped near a wheel
   - loose plastic undertray, trim, heat shield, or exhaust part hanging down
   - a fresh puddle or steady drip under the vehicle
   - smoke, scorching, fresh dust, or a strong smell around a wheel or brake area

5. **Do not try to fix it at the roadside.** If you are in a genuinely quiet off-road place, such as a car park, and you can move a loose plastic flap or small trapped object without reaching underneath, touching hot parts, or going near traffic, you may do that. If it is not obvious and safe, stop and get help.

6. **Use a conservative drive-or-stop rule.** Do not drive if the noise is metal-on-metal, braking feels different, steering feels wrong, the car pulls to one side, a tyre looks damaged, there is smoke or a burning smell, fluid is leaking, or you suspect a brake, wheel, tyre, bearing, suspension, or exhaust fault.

7. **If the noise stops, still avoid treating it as solved too quickly.** Only move off gently if you are already in a safe off-road place, the cause was clearly harmless, and the vehicle feels normal. Avoid high-speed roads and go to the nearest safe place for a fuller check.

8. **Give a precise location when asking for help.** Use the road number, direction of travel, nearest junction, nearby services or lay-by, and any driver location signs or marker posts. If your vehicle has an eCall or SOS button and you need urgent help, you can use it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact fault right now.
- You do not need to choose a garage, compare repair prices, or make insurance decisions at the roadside.
- You do not need to decide whether the vehicle is worth repairing.
- You do not need to continue the journey just because the destination feels important; safety and getting off the road come first.

## Important reassurance
A sudden grinding or scraping noise is a reasonable reason to stop and check. Taking it seriously is not overreacting; it is a sensible way to protect yourself, other road users, and the vehicle.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Brakes, wheels, tyres, bearings, suspension parts, undertrays, heat shields, and exhaust parts may need a professional inspection before the vehicle is driven normally again.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, emergency, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are stopped, or you suspect fire, a fuel leak, or a dangerous road position, call 999.
