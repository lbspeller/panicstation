# What to do if… you notice a gas-like smell near an appliance and suspect a gas leak (UK)

## Short answer
Evacuate the property immediately, avoid any action that could cause a spark, and call the National Gas Emergency Service on **0800 111 999** from outside. If anyone has collapsed or has severe breathing difficulties, call **999** or **112** once you are in fresh air.

## Do not do these things
- Do not operate any electrical switches, including lights, appliances, or doorbells.
- Do not plug in or unplug any devices.
- Do not smoke, vape, or use matches, lighters, or any naked flames.
- Do not attempt to locate the leak by dismantling the appliance or using a flame.
- Do not use a telephone or mobile phone inside the building.
- Do not enter a cellar or basement to reach the gas meter.
- Do not re-enter the property until a qualified emergency engineer confirms it is safe.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the appliance immediately.** If it is already in use and the controls are safely within reach, turn them to the **off** position.
2. **Open doors and windows to ventilate the area.** Do this only if it does not delay your exit from the building; do not linger if the smell is strong.
3. **Evacuate all people and pets from the property.** Ensure everyone moves to a safe distance away from the building into fresh air.
4. **Extinguish all naked flames.** Ensure no one is smoking or lighting candles as they evacuate.
5. **Turn off the gas at the meter.** Only do this if the meter is located outside or in a ventilated area, and NOT if it is in a cellar or basement.
   - Turn the handle on the emergency control valve (ECV) so it is at a right angle (90 degrees) to the pipe.
6. **Call the National Gas Emergency Service from outside.** Dial **0800 111 999** (free, 24/7).
   - If you have speech or hearing difficulties, use the textphone/Minicom number: **0800 371 787**.
7. **Contact emergency services if life is at risk.** Call **999** or **112** from outside if there is a fire, an explosion, or if someone has collapsed or is finding it hard to breathe.
8. **Stay outside until help arrives.** Wait for the gas emergency engineer to attend and provide further instructions.
9. **Inform neighbours if you live in a multi-occupancy building.** Knock on doors as you leave the building to alert them, but do not use electric doorbells or intercoms.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact source or cause of the leak.
- You do not need to contact your regular gas engineer or plumber until the emergency service has made the site safe.
- You do not need to collect personal belongings or "pack a bag" before evacuating.
- You do not need to determine who is responsible for the repair or the cost right now.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of urgency or panic when smelling gas. These reactions are normal. Following the standard safety protocol—evacuate, isolate, and call—is the most effective way to manage the risk and ensure everyone's safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected gas leak. It does not cover long-term appliance repairs, Gas Safe certification, or legal disputes between landlords and tenants.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not replace the instructions given by the National Gas Emergency Service operators or on-site engineers. Always prioritize the instructions of the emergency operator over this guide.
