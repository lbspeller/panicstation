# What to do if… you are asked to leave a crowded service because it is at capacity and you need to continue travelling (UK)

## Short answer
Step away from the vehicle or platform edge and obtain a verbal or written "authorised alternative" from staff to ensure your ticket remains valid for the next available service.

## Do not do these things
- Don't attempt to re-board or block the doors after being instructed to leave by staff or police.
- Don't board a different operator's service without explicit staff confirmation that your ticket is accepted.
- Don't discard your original ticket or delete digital booking records, as these are required for 2026 Delay Repay claims.
- Don't assume walk-up tickets (Anytime / Off-Peak) can be refunded later; under April 2026 rules, they are generally non-refundable once the travel date begins unless disruption is proven.
- Don't walk onto tracks or unauthorized areas to bypass crowd control barriers.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe distance from the hazard zone:** step away from the platform edge, yellow line, or bus kerbside to avoid being pushed by the remaining crowd.
2. **Identify an "Authorized Next Option" from staff:**  
   - Ask: "Which specific service am I authorised to take now?"  
   - Ask: "Does this operator have a 'ticket acceptance' agreement in place due to this overcrowding?"  
   - Write down the name of the staff member and the time of the instruction.
3. **Trigger Passenger Assist if you have mobility or accessibility needs:** if you are disabled, pregnant, or older and have been displaced, ask staff to arrange priority boarding for the next service or alternative transport (such as a taxi) if the station remains unsafe.
4. **Use your ticket retailer's app to check for "disruption" status:** open the app where you bought the ticket (e.g., Trainline, GBR app, or operator app) to see if the overcrowding has triggered a formal "do not travel" or "ticket flexibility" notice.
5. **Request a ticket endorsement if staff are available:** if possible, ask a staff member to mark your ticket or provide a "disruption note" to prevent fare evasion penalties on the next service.
6. **Apply for a full refund at the ticket office if you choose to abandon the journey:** if the overcrowding prevents you from travelling entirely, you are entitled to a full refund without admin fees, but you must usually request this at the station or through your retailer before 23:59 on the same day.
7. **Document the situation for Delay Repay:** take a photo of the overcrowded platform or departure board showing the "Full" or "Delayed" status. Under 2026 rules, you claim this from your original retailer, not necessarily the train operator.

## What can wait
- You do not need to calculate the exact compensation amount while standing on the platform.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about service quality until you have reached your destination.
- You do not need to decide on long-term route changes for future commutes right now.

## Important reassurance
Being asked to leave a service is a standard safety protocol used to prevent dangerous overcrowding or "crush" conditions. Station staff are trained to manage these flows to keep the network operational. While frustrating, following their instructions is the fastest way to maintain your legal rights for compensation or rebooking.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance for immediate stabilization and travel continuity. It covers the 30-minute window following a denial of boarding due to capacity.

## Important note
Rail and bus regulations in the UK updated significantly in April 2026. Walk-up tickets are now subject to stricter refund windows (23:59 on the day of travel). Always claim compensation or refunds through the specific retailer where the ticket was purchased. If staff are unavailable, use the station Help Point to record your situation before leaving the premises.
