# What to do if… a cash withdrawal fails but your account is still charged (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from the machine only when your card is secure, record the ATM details, and contact your bank or building society through its cash machine or ATM dispute route. If the debit has left you short for essentials or may cause missed payments or fees, contact them now.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep retrying the same withdrawal at the same machine, because it may create more debits.
- Don’t throw away the receipt or leave without noting the ATM location, time, and amount.
- Don’t share your PIN or hand your card to anyone offering to help.
- Don’t argue with shop staff where the ATM is located; they usually cannot access the ATM records.
- Don’t report it as fraud if you made the withdrawal attempt, unless your card, PIN, or account was used without your permission.

## What to do now
1. **Check what your account shows.**  
   Look at your banking app or online banking and note whether the withdrawal is pending or completed. Some banks ask customers to allow a short automatic reversal window for ATM errors, while others say to contact them as soon as possible. Contact your bank now if the transaction is completed, you need the money for essentials, or the debit may cause fees or returned payments.

2. **Capture the details while they are fresh.**  
   Write down or photograph:
   - the date and exact time, as closely as you can
   - the amount you tried to withdraw
   - the amount of cash you actually received, even if it was nothing
   - the ATM location, including shop name, street, and town
   - the ATM operator or owner name, if shown
   - any ATM ID or terminal number on the machine
   - any message on the screen, such as “unable to process”, “cash dispensed”, or a timeout
   - the receipt, if the machine gave one

3. **Contact your bank or building society and raise an ATM dispute.**  
   Use secure in-app chat, secure message, phone, or the bank’s cash machine dispute route. Say: “I attempted a cash withdrawal, received no cash, but my account was debited. Please raise a cash machine or ATM dispute.”

4. **Ask for the practical next steps before ending the contact.**  
   Ask for:
   - a reference or case number
   - whether your bank expects an automatic reversal and what timing it uses
   - whether you need to provide a receipt, photo, or written statement
   - what urgent support is available if this leaves you short for essentials or risks fees

5. **If it was not your bank’s ATM, keep the bank as the main contact.**  
   You can also use the phone number or contact route shown on the ATM, if there is one. It is normal for the ATM operator to tell you to speak to your card provider, because your bank or building society can check your account and contact the machine operator if needed.

6. **Protect essential payments today.**  
   If this debit may affect bills, rent, travel, food, or other essentials, tell your bank it is urgent. Ask what they can do to reduce avoidable charges or prevent knock-on problems while the ATM dispute is investigated.

7. **Keep checking, but keep the evidence.**  
   Check your account for a reversal or refund, and keep your notes, receipt, photos, and case number until the money is back and the dispute is closed.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to switch banks.
- You don’t need to write a long formal complaint before the ATM dispute has been logged.
- You don’t need to chase the shop or venue unless your bank asks for more location details.
- You don’t need to know exactly why the machine failed before reporting it.

## Important reassurance
A failed cash withdrawal with a debit on the account can be frightening, but it is a known type of ATM dispute. Clear details about the time, place, amount, and what happened give the bank the information it needs to check the machine and account records.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and get the correct ATM dispute started. If the issue is not resolved, later steps may include your bank’s formal complaints process and, if needed, the Financial Ombudsman Service after a final response or after eight weeks without a response.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Processes and timescales can vary by bank, building society, ATM operator, card type, and whether the transaction is pending or completed.
