# What to do if… a date repeatedly ignores your no and keeps pressuring you for sex by text (UK)

## Short answer
You do not have to persuade them or keep explaining. Do not meet them privately tonight; get near a trusted person or a safer place, then mute or block them when it feels safe to do so.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep explaining your no; a no does not need to be defended.
- Don’t meet them in private “just to talk” or “to calm things down.”
- Don’t send sexual photos, videos, your address, your workplace, or your routine to make them stop.
- Don’t share your live location or travel plans with them.
- Don’t argue about whether they are a “bad person”; your priority is distance and safety.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Stay somewhere you are not alone if you can, such as with a friend, housemate, family member, reception desk, staffed venue, or other public place. If they know your address or usual places, keep doors secure and avoid going out alone to meet them.
2. **Stop the conversation.** You can stop replying now. If you want to send one final message, keep it short: “I said no. Stop asking me for sex.” Then stop responding.
3. **Mute or block the channels they can use.** Block their number and block or report them on the dating app and social media. If you are worried they may escalate when they notice, mute first while you contact someone you trust, then block when you are in a safer moment.
4. **Do a quick privacy check.** Turn off live location sharing, check apps such as Find My or maps, turn off read receipts or online status if you use them, and avoid posting stories or check-ins that show where you are tonight.
5. **Tell one real person now.** Send something simple: “This person keeps pressuring me for sex by text after I said not tonight. Can you check in with me tonight?” If you had plans with the person, say clearly that you are not meeting them privately.
6. **Keep only what is easy to keep.** If you may want support or to report later, you can take a few screenshots that show the repeated messages with dates or times visible, then save them somewhere only you can access. You do not need to build a full archive tonight.
7. **Use urgent help if the risk changes.** Call 999 if they threaten you, are outside, follow you, or you feel in immediate danger. For non-emergency police advice or reporting, call 101 or use Police.uk.
8. **Contact specialist support if you want help naming what happened.** If you are in England or Wales and aged 16 or over, the 24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line is 0808 500 2222. In Scotland, Rape Crisis Scotland can help with sexual violence support. In Northern Ireland, Nexus NI supports people impacted by sexual abuse. The National Stalking Helpline can give advice if the contact is repeated, threatening, or makes you feel scared. If you are a child or young person, Childline is free on 0800 1111.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to report to the police.
- You do not need to decide whether this was harassment, stalking, abuse, coercion, or “serious enough” before asking for support.
- You do not need the perfect final message.
- You do not need to explain your decision to mutual friends tonight.
- You do not need to make any long-term decisions about dating, work, housing, study, or your social circle right now.

## Important reassurance
Someone repeatedly pushing for sex after you said “not tonight” is not a misunderstanding you have to fix. Feeling frozen, guilty, shaky, angry, or unsure can happen when someone is pressuring you. You are allowed to end contact and put safety ahead of their feelings.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: they are meant to help you stabilise the next few hours and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions about reporting, workplace or campus options, housing, shared friends, or legal protections may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe or at risk of harm, prioritise immediate safety and urgent help.
