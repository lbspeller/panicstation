# What to do if… you are asked to approve time records for others when you cannot verify them (UK)

## Short answer
Professional compliance with documentation requests is a contractual duty, but you should immediately formalize all requests in writing and begin a private log of all restructuring signals while maintaining access to your union or legal representative.

## Do not do these things
- Do not refuse to provide the notes, as this may be treated as a refusal to follow a reasonable management instruction and lead to disciplinary action.
- Do not delete, "sabotage," or password-protect files or systems to make yourself indispensable.
- Do not take company data, client lists, or trade secrets to a personal device, as this often triggers immediate gross misconduct dismissal and legal action.
- Do not make written accusations about "replacement" or "outsourcing" in the documentation itself.
- Do not sign any "settlement agreements" or "voluntary redundancy" papers on the spot; you are entitled to time and independent legal advice.

## What to do now
1. **Request the full scope and purpose of the documentation project in a written email to your manager.**  
   - Ask for specific deadlines, the intended audience for the notes, and the level of detail required (e.g., high-level workflows versus keystroke-level SOPs).
2. **Respond to verbal instructions with a written summary to create a paper trail.**  
   - Use phrasing such as "Further to our meeting, I am beginning the documentation for [Process X] as requested for the purpose of [stated reason], with a target delivery date of [Date]."
3. **Maintain a private, factual diary of events on a personal device or paper.**  
   - Record the dates of requests, the names of those involved, and any specific comments made about "business continuity," "efficiency," or "third-party providers."
   - Do not include any proprietary company data in this diary.
4. **Identify if the request is part of a "Service Provision Change" under TUPE regulations.**  
   - If a specific third-party provider is mentioned, ask if the Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) regulations apply.
   - Under TUPE, if your work is outsourced, your contract and length of service should automatically transfer to the new provider on your current terms.
5. **Check for "at risk" triggers that mandate statutory consultation.**  
   - If the employer proposes 20 or more redundancies at one establishment within 90 days, they must start a 30-day collective consultation (rising to 45 days for 100+ staff).
   - For dismissals from April 2026, the maximum "protective award" for failing to consult is 180 days' uncapped pay.
6. **Clarify your right to be accompanied if any "informal" meeting turns into a performance or redundancy discussion.**  
   - You have a statutory right to be accompanied by a trade union representative or a workplace colleague to any formal grievance or disciplinary meeting.
7. **Complete the documentation to a professional standard without including "hidden" knowledge.**  
   - Provide what is asked for but do not feel obligated to "optimize" the process for the next person unless specifically instructed to do so.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to fight the outsourcing or look for a new job in the first 24 hours.
- You do not need to confront the "new" team or external consultants if they appear on-site; maintain professional distance.
- You do not need to calculate your final redundancy pay until a formal notice has been issued.

## Important reassurance
Being asked for documentation is a standard business process and does not always result in job loss; it is often used for audit, training, or software transitions. Even if outsourcing is the goal, the UK has robust protections (TUPE and the Employment Rights Act 2025) designed to ensure you are either transferred or compensated fairly for your service.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. If you receive a formal "at risk" letter or a TUPE transfer notice, you must seek specific advice regarding your individual contract.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment law is highly dependent on specific contract terms and the 2026 legislative environment. If your employer initiates a formal redundancy or TUPE process, consult Acas or an employment solicitor immediately.
