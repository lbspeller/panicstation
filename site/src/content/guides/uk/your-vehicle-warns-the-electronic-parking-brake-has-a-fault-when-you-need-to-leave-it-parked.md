# What to do if… your vehicle warns the electronic parking brake has a fault when you need to leave it parked (UK)

## Short answer
Move the vehicle to level ground if possible and secure it using the transmission (Gear or 'P') and steering angle before calling for professional recovery. Do not leave the vehicle unattended until you are certain it cannot roll.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume the brake is held just because you pulled the switch or see a light.
- Do not leave the car on any incline if you have any doubt about its stability.
- Do not repeatedly toggle the EPB switch, as this may deplete the battery or worsen a mechanical jam.
- Do not attempt to crawl under the vehicle to inspect the brake cables or actuators.
- Do not "test" the brake by letting the car roll toward other vehicles or pedestrians.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to a flat, level surface immediately if it is safe to drive.** **Prioritise finding a location away from slopes, such as a flat car park or a level side road, to remove the gravity risk entirely.**

2. **Engage a mechanical backup using the transmission while keeping your foot firmly on the service brake.** 
   - For automatic vehicles: move the selector to **P (Park)**.
   - For manual vehicles: select **1st gear** if the car is facing uphill or **Reverse** gear if the car is facing downhill.

3. **Angle the steering wheel toward a physical barrier according to Highway Code Rule 252.** 
   - If facing **uphill**: turn the wheels **away** from the kerb and allow the vehicle to roll back slightly until the tyre gently touches the kerb.
   - If facing **downhill**: turn the wheels **towards** the kerb so any movement is blocked by the pavement.

4. **Apply wheel chocks if they are available in your emergency kit.** **Place the chocks firmly against the tyre on the downhill side to provide a physical obstruction.**
   - If you do not have professional chocks, do not rely on loose bricks or wood unless they are large, stable, and cannot slide on the road surface.

5. **Perform a controlled stillness check before exiting the vehicle.** **With the transmission in gear/Park and the wheels turned, very slowly ease your foot off the brake pedal while remaining ready to slam it back down if the car moves.** 
   - If the car moves even slightly, the vehicle is not secure and must not be left unattended.

6. **Contact a breakdown recovery provider and report a "High risk of vehicle roll-away."** **Explicitly state that the electronic parking brake has failed and the vehicle cannot be safely secured by the driver.**
   - If the vehicle is currently rolling or blocking a live carriage way and you cannot stop it, call **999**.
   - For non-emergency road hazards that are not immediate, use **101** to notify the police.

7. **Leave a visible warning note if you must momentarily step away to meet recovery services.** **Place a sign on the dashboard or window stating: "Brake Fault - Vehicle in Gear - Do Not Move."**

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause of the EPB failure (e.g., motor failure vs. software error) right now.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair garage while the vehicle is still unsecured.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of the repair until the immediate safety risk is managed.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of panic when a primary safety system like a parking brake fails. By using the transmission and steering angle as redundant backups, you are following standard safety protocols that significantly reduce the risk of the vehicle moving.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a parking brake failure. It does not replace the specific emergency procedures found in your vehicle’s owner manual or the expertise of a certified mechanic.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and may not apply to all vehicle makes or models. Always prioritise instructions from your vehicle manufacturer and professional recovery services. If you cannot confirm the vehicle is stationary, do not leave it unattended as you may be liable for any damage or injury caused by a roll-away.
