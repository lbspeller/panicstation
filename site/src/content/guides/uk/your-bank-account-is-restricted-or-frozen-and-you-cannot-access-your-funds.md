# What to do if… your bank account is restricted or frozen and you cannot access your funds (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the bank only through an official route and ask exactly what information they need to review the restriction. At the same time, protect food, housing, work travel, medication, and priority bills by telling the bank and urgent billers that you cannot access funds.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use links, phone numbers, or reply instructions in unexpected calls, texts, emails, or social media messages about the restriction.
- Don’t edit, crop, or “tidy up” documents such as ID, payslips, statements, invoices, or benefit letters.
- Don’t send documents through email, messaging apps, or upload links unless the bank has confirmed that channel is secure.
- Don’t route money through someone else’s account to get around the restriction.
- Don’t ignore rent, mortgage, council tax, energy, childcare, work travel, food, or medication while waiting.
- Don’t close the account or rapidly open and close multiple accounts in panic.
- Don’t assume the bank can tell you every reason for the restriction while checks are still active.

## What to do now
1. **Write down the next 48 hours of essentials.** List food, medication, rent or mortgage, energy, council tax, childcare, work travel, and any payment that could cause immediate harm if missed. Add what money you can access today, including cash or another account in your own name.
2. **Contact the bank through an official route.** Use secure in-app messaging, the number on your card, the number on a statement, or the bank’s official website. Say: “My account is restricted for a compliance review. What specific information do you need from me, how should I send it securely, and what access is currently blocked?”
3. **Ask what is blocked.** Ask whether card payments, cash withdrawals, Faster Payments, standing orders, Direct Debits, online banking, incoming payments, or the whole account are affected. Ask for the answer in a secure message or letter if they can provide it.
4. **Tell the bank if this creates urgent hardship.** Say clearly if you cannot buy food, pay rent, travel to work, pay for childcare, or get medication. Ask whether they have a vulnerability, financial difficulty, or essential-payment team, and whether any limited access or specific essential payment is possible.
5. **Ask for the exact document checklist.** Ask whether they need ID, proof of address, an explanation of particular transactions, evidence of source of funds, or evidence of source of wealth. Ask them to name the transactions or period they need explained if they can.
6. **Send one organised evidence pack through the bank’s secure channel.** Use clear files and short explanations. Common evidence can include photo ID, proof of address, payslips, benefit award letters, invoices, contracts, sale documents, inheritance paperwork, or bank statements showing where money came from.
7. **Keep a simple record.** Note dates, times, names or reference numbers, what the bank asked for, what you sent, and the secure channel used. Save screenshots or downloads of secure messages where possible.
8. **Protect priority bills today.** Contact your landlord or agent, mortgage lender, council tax office, energy supplier, childcare provider, and any must-pay biller. Say: “My bank has temporarily restricted my account during a compliance review. I am working to resolve it. Please note this and tell me what short pause or payment arrangement is possible.”
9. **Redirect incoming money if you can.** Ask payroll whether the next wage can be paid to another account in your own name. For benefits, use your official online account, official letters, or GOV.UK route to check how to update payment details for that benefit.
10. **Consider a basic bank account for new incoming payments.** If you do not have another usable account, you may be able to apply for a basic bank account elsewhere. Approval is not automatic, and the new bank may still carry out identity and financial crime checks.
11. **Make a short written complaint if access is not restored quickly.** Include the date the restriction started, what you cannot access, the hardship caused, what the bank asked for, what you sent, and what you want now: a clear checklist, a realistic update date, and consideration of essential payments.
12. **Check escalation timing if the complaint is not resolved.** For frozen-account or blocked-payment complaints, the Financial Ombudsman Service says the bank should respond within 15 days with a response or an explanation of why it cannot yet give one, then send a response within 35 days. If the bank gives a final response, misses the relevant deadline, or you are unhappy with the outcome, you may be able to take the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

## What can wait
- Choosing a long-term new bank.
- Posting publicly about the bank.
- Paying non-urgent subscriptions, upgrades, or non-priority debts.
- Deciding whether to take legal advice, unless there is a court order, police contact, eviction risk, or another urgent legal deadline.
- Explaining every old transaction that the bank has not asked about.

## Important reassurance
A sudden account restriction can feel frightening and unfair. Banks can freeze accounts or block payments where they suspect fraud, money laundering, illegal activity, or where a court order applies, and they may not be able to explain everything straight away. Your immediate job is to keep essentials covered, respond through secure channels, and keep a clear record.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise access to essentials, reduce knock-on harm, and get the bank to state what it needs where it can. Later decisions may need specialist help depending on what the bank says and whether the restriction continues.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, professional, or debt advice. Banks may be required to restrict accounts in some situations and may be limited in what they can disclose during certain checks. If you are at immediate risk of eviction, utility disconnection, going without food, or going without medication, tell the bank and the relevant billers that this is urgent hardship.
