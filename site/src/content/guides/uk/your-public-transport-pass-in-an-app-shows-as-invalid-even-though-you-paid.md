# What to do if… your public transport pass in an app shows as invalid even though you paid (UK)

## Short answer
Do not delete the app or rush through barriers. Get your proof of purchase visible, try the quickest safe refresh steps, and ask staff what process to follow before boarding or passing gates where possible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete the app, reinstall it, clear storage, or log out in a panic unless the operator’s own help page tells you to do that.
- Do not assume a screenshot, confirmation email, or bank transaction will be accepted as a ticket. Keep them as evidence, but focus on getting the required live ticket, barcode, wallet pass, PDF, smartcard, or app pass to display.
- Do not force your way through a gate or board without asking staff first if staff are available.
- Do not argue with the driver, gate staff, or inspector. Ask what they are issuing, what evidence to keep, and what deadline applies.
- Do not ignore any Penalty Fare Notice, Unpaid Fare Notice, receipt, or written instruction you are given.

## What to do now
1. **Make the phone and ticket screen usable.**
   - Turn off battery saver temporarily.
   - Increase screen brightness.
   - Turn on mobile data or connect to Wi-Fi, depending on what is available.
   - If you use a VPN, switch it off briefly and reopen the ticket app.
   - Set your phone’s date and time to automatic if it is not already.

2. **Find proof that you paid.**
   - Open the purchase confirmation email or receipt.
   - Find the order reference, ticket reference, amount, date, and time.
   - If safe, note or capture the app error message and the order screen. Avoid saving card details or sensitive personal information in a recording.

3. **Try the quickest safe app fixes.**
   - Toggle Airplane mode on and off.
   - Force-close the ticket app and reopen it.
   - Check whether the ticket is in a “my tickets”, “wallet”, “orders”, “expired”, or “not activated” section.
   - Use any in-app option such as “refresh tickets”, “restore purchases”, “download ticket”, or “sync account”.
   - Update the app only if an update is immediately available and you can stay signed in.

4. **If you are at a gate, bus stop, tram stop, or platform now, ask before moving on.**
   - Show staff the app error, receipt, and order reference.
   - Ask whether they can check the reference, open a side gate, advise a temporary ticket, or record that the app failed.
   - If staff advise buying another ticket to avoid a penalty, ask how to claim back or complain later, and keep both receipts.

5. **If you are already on board and your ticket is checked, stay factual.**
   - Say you paid before travel but the app is showing the pass as invalid.
   - Show the app error, purchase confirmation, and payment evidence.
   - Ask what is being issued, what deadline applies, and where evidence should be submitted.
   - Note the service, time, direction, origin and destination, and any staff ID or name shown.

6. **If you are given a Penalty Fare Notice or Unpaid Fare Notice, switch to deadline mode.**
   - Photograph the notice and keep the paper copy if there is one.
   - Follow the instructions on the notice exactly.
   - On many National Rail services in England, a Penalty Fare is generally £100 plus the full single fare, reduced to £50 plus the fare if paid within 21 days. TfL, Transport for Wales, buses, trams, and operator-specific notices can use different rules, so the notice and operator instructions are what count.
   - Appeals are usually time-limited, and many first appeals must be made within 21 days. Treat the deadline on the notice as urgent.

7. **If this is an Oyster or contactless issue on TfL, check the journey later.**
   - Use the same card, phone, watch, or Oyster card for touching in and out.
   - Check your contactless and Oyster account for incomplete journeys or maximum fares.
   - TfL usually says to wait at least 48 hours before applying for an incomplete journey refund, because many refunds are processed automatically.

8. **Report the app fault to the right organisation today if you can.**
   - Contact the ticket retailer or app provider and ask them to confirm in writing that the ticket was bought before travel and what the app status shows.
   - Contact the transport operator if you were delayed, charged again, or issued paperwork.
   - Keep screenshots of the fault, receipts, emails, reference numbers, and any reply.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a formal complaint.
- You do not need to replace your phone, SIM, bank card, or account today unless the operator specifically tells you that this is needed.
- You do not need to chase a TfL incomplete journey refund immediately if TfL’s process says to wait for automatic processing first.
- You do not need to write a long explanation until you know whether you have been issued a notice and what deadline applies.

## Important reassurance
A failing ticket app is stressful, but the useful task is small: keep the evidence, avoid deleting anything, ask staff what process applies, and do not miss any notice deadline. A calm record of what happened is often more useful than trying every possible phone fix at once.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, reduce the risk of extra charges, and preserve evidence. Later decisions about appeals, complaints, refunds, or formal enforcement may need specialist advice based on the exact operator, ticket type, and document you receive.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, therapeutic, or professional advice. Ticket validity, refund rules, penalty fares, and appeal routes vary by operator and by the ticket or pass you bought.
