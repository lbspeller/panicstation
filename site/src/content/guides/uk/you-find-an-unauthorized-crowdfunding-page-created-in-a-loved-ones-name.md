# What to do if… you find an unauthorized crowdfunding page created in a loved one’s name (UK)

## Short answer
Do not engage with the organiser directly first. Capture the page, report it through the crowdfunding platform’s official route, and ask the platform to suspend, remove, or restrict payouts while it checks what happened.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send the organiser passport scans, bank details, death certificates, proof of address, or family documents through messages or informal links.
- Do not click “verification” links sent by the page owner; go to the platform’s official website or app yourself.
- Do not share the live fundraiser link to warn people if a screenshot or written warning will do.
- Do not get pulled into a public argument on the page while it is still collecting money.
- Do not delay reporting because it might be a misunderstanding; the platform can check that without more donations being collected.
- Do not promise donors refunds yourself unless you control the fundraiser money.

## What to do now
1. **Save evidence before reporting.** Screenshot the full fundraiser page, URL, organiser name, beneficiary claims, photos, updates, donation totals if visible, and comments. Save any messages where the organiser claims permission or asks for documents.

2. **Report the page inside the crowdfunding platform.** Use the platform’s report, abuse, fraud, impersonation, or unauthorised fundraiser option. Say: “This fundraiser was created in [loved one’s name] without family or estate consent. Please investigate urgently and restrict donations or payouts if possible.”

3. **Contact the platform through its official help centre.** Ask for a case or reference number. Request removal, suspension, or payout hold while the platform investigates, but be prepared that the platform may need to review evidence first.

4. **Only provide proof through official channels.** If the platform asks for evidence, use its official upload or support route. If true, state whether you are the executor, administrator, close family member, or another person directly affected. Do not send documents to the fundraiser organiser.

5. **Warn close people without spreading the page.** Send a short message to family and friends: “An unauthorised fundraiser is circulating in [name]’s name. Please do not donate or share it. If you already donated, contact the crowdfunding platform through its official help centre.” Use a screenshot rather than the live link where possible.

6. **If money has been taken or you suspect fraud, report it.** If you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, use Report Fraud. If you are in Scotland, contact Police Scotland; 101 is for non-emergencies. If a crime is in progress or someone is in immediate danger, call 999.

7. **If there is a fake website or suspicious link connected to the fundraiser, report the URL.** Use the National Cyber Security Centre suspicious website route or GOV.UK internet scam guidance. Do not keep clicking the link to check whether it is still live.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to contact a solicitor, the media, or everyone who donated.
- You do not need to write a perfect public statement now.
- You do not need to identify every donor before asking the platform to act.
- You do not need to confront the organiser before the platform and any fraud-reporting route have the evidence.

## Important reassurance
Finding a fundraiser in a loved one’s name can feel invasive and upsetting. The useful first move is to stop further spread, preserve what you can see, and get the platform and the correct reporting route involved.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about refunds, estate authority, harassment, identity misuse, platform complaints, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, bereavement, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use official platform, police, government, and cyber-reporting routes rather than replying to suspicious messages or links.
