# What to do if… a freelance platform suspends your account while you have active work in progress (UK)

## Short answer
Protect the active work first: save evidence, secure your files, and use the platform’s official support or appeal route to ask for reinstatement or temporary access so you can complete delivery or handover.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t create a new account or ask someone else to act as your account holder; this may breach platform rules and make reinstatement harder.
- Don’t move payment or communication off-platform if the platform rules prohibit it.
- Don’t send angry threats, repeated messages, or long explanations to support; keep every message short, factual, and consistent.
- Don’t delete messages, drafts, listings, invoices, or files because you are worried; you may remove proof you need.
- Don’t rush into a chargeback, bank dispute, or public accusation before checking the platform’s dispute process and the possible effect on your account.

## What to do now
1. **Do a quick evidence capture.** Save screenshots or screen recordings of the suspension notice, any ticket or case number, the stated reason, timestamps, payout or escrow screens, active contracts, deadlines, and any dispute pages you can still access.
2. **Check the email address linked to the account.** Search inbox and spam for “suspension”, “trust and safety”, “verification”, “chargeback”, “terms”, “appeal”, “review”, and “action required”. If the email gives a specific appeal route or deadline, use that route.
3. **Secure the work product.** Make local copies of drafts, final files, source files, briefs, revisions, delivery notes, and any client approvals. Write a short handover note showing what is complete, what is pending, and where each file is.
4. **Submit one clear appeal or support request through the official platform route.** Include your account email or ID, any ticket number, the affected job or contract IDs, the deadlines at risk, and the practical access you need now: reinstatement, temporary message access, file access, or a way to complete handover.
5. **If you can still message clients inside the platform, send a neutral status update.** Say that your account is under review, you have contacted support, and you may need extra time if access remains limited. Do not blame the platform, argue the case with the client, or suggest prohibited off-platform payment or contact.
6. **Protect payment and delivery evidence.** Save invoices, milestone details, escrow terms, proof of delivery, revision requests, acceptance messages, payment confirmations, and any dispute deadlines shown inside the platform.
7. **If important records are locked away, consider a subject access request.** If UK data protection rights apply to your account, you can ask an organisation for copies of your personal data. This may include account information, messages about you, verification records, or decision notes, subject to exemptions. The organisation may ask for ID or details to find the data; the response is usually due within one calendar month once the request can be dealt with, and may take up to three calendar months for complex or multiple requests.
8. **Make a one-screen timeline.** Write down when the suspension happened, what work was active, what you had delivered, what the client had approved, what money was pending, and every support request you have sent. Use this timeline so later messages stay consistent.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to sue, complain publicly, or take formal legal action.
- You do not need to rebuild your whole business profile elsewhere right now.
- You do not need a perfect explanation for the suspension before contacting support.
- You do not need to settle every tax, contract, or client relationship issue immediately; first protect the records, active work, and deadlines.

## Important reassurance
A sudden suspension may be triggered by automated checks, identity review, payment risk, a client dispute, or a terms concern. It does not automatically mean you have done something deliberate or irreversible. Calm evidence capture and one clear official request usually protect you better than rushed workarounds.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stop the situation getting worse and to protect deadlines, payments, files, and proof. Later decisions may need specialist help depending on the platform’s terms, the amount of money involved, and any allegation made.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, tax, or professional advice. Platform rules and dispute routes vary, and your options depend on the platform’s terms, the reason for the suspension, and the facts of the active work.
