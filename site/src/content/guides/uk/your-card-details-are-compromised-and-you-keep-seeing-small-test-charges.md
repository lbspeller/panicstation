# What to do if… your card details are compromised and you keep seeing small “test” charges (UK)

## Short answer
Freeze the card immediately via your banking app and call your provider or the 159 hotline to cancel the card, revoke digital wallet tokens, and report the unauthorized activity.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore small or "penny" charges, as these are often used by fraudsters to verify that a card is active before attempting much larger transactions.
- Don’t call numbers provided in a text or email about the fraud; only use the number on the back of your physical card, the official app, or the 159 shortcut.
- Don’t share your PIN, full password, or any One-Time Passcode (OTP) with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank's fraud department.
- Don’t assume a charge is a legitimate "hold" if you do not recognize the merchant name or have not initiated a purchase recently.
- Don’t hand over your physical card or a "replacement" to anyone, such as a courier, who claims to be collecting it for the bank.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze the compromised card immediately using your banking app.**  
   **Log into your mobile banking app and select the option to freeze or lock the card to prevent any further transactions from being processed.**
   - If you cannot access your app, move to the next step to call your provider immediately.

2. **Call your bank or card provider using a verified secure line.**  
   **Dial 159 (the UK’s secure fraud hotline) or use the number on the back of your card to reach the fraud department directly.** 
   - Inform them that you are seeing "test" charges and believe your card details are compromised.
   - Request that they **cancel and replace the card** with a completely new card number.

3. **Instruct the bank to revoke all digital wallet and merchant tokens.**  
   **Explicitly ask the fraud team to delete any digital wallet tokens (such as Apple Pay or Google Pay) and "stored card" tokens linked to the account.**
   - Fraudsters often "tokenise" cards to continue spending even after a physical card is cancelled; revoking these tokens stops this specific loophole.

4. **Identify and dispute every unrecognised transaction.**  
   **Review your statement and point out every charge you did not authorise, including those that are still "pending."**
   - Under UK law (Payment Services Regulations), banks must generally refund unauthorised transactions by the end of the next business day after being notified.
   - Note that you have up to 13 months to report unauthorised transactions, but immediate reporting is required to stop further loss.

5. **Withdraw consent for any recurring payments or Continuous Payment Authorities (CPA).**  
   **Tell your bank clearly: "I withdraw my consent for any further payments to [Merchant Name]."**
   - Once consent is withdrawn, the bank is legally required to stop the payments and must refund you if they allow any subsequent charges from that merchant to go through.

6. **Secure the digital accounts that may have leaked the details.**  
   **Change the passwords for your primary email address and any retail accounts (like Amazon or eBay) where the card was stored.**
   - Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on these accounts using an authenticator app rather than SMS if possible.

7. **Report the fraud to the national reporting service.**  
   **File a report with the "Report Fraud" service (the successor to Action Fraud) to obtain a crime reference number.**
   - For England, Wales, and Northern Ireland: Use reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - For Scotland: Call Police Scotland on 101 to report the incident.

## What can wait
- Updating your legitimate Netflix, utility, or gym subscriptions with your new card details can wait until the new physical card arrives.
- Checking your full credit report for signs of wider identity theft; this is important but can wait a few days while you stabilise your primary account.
- Filing a formal complaint if the bank initially refuses a refund; you must follow their internal fraud investigation first.

## Important reassurance
Small "test" charges are a standard tactic used by automated fraud scripts. By freezing the card and revoking digital tokens now, you are cutting off the fraudster's access before they can attempt larger, more damaging purchases. Your bank has established protocols for this, and most consumers are fully protected against loss from unauthorised card use.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate steps for card-based fraud and unauthorised transactions. It does not cover long-term identity restoration or disputes involving goods you actually ordered but were unhappy with.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilisation and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Banking procedures and refund timelines may vary slightly between providers. Always follow the specific security instructions provided by your bank's official fraud team.
