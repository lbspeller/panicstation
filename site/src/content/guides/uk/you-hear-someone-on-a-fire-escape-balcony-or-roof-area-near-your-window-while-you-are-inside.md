# What to do if… you hear someone on a fire escape, balcony, or roof area near your window while you are inside (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from the window immediately to a room with a solid door and call 999. Do not attempt to look out, open the window, or interact with the person.

## Do not do these things
- Don't open the window or curtains to see who is there.
- Don't go out onto the balcony, roof, or fire escape.
- Don't shout at the person or acknowledge your presence if you haven't been seen.
- Don't stand near glass to film or take photos.
- Don't assume the noise is harmless or just a neighbor.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure internal space immediately.** **Create distance between yourself and the window by moving into a hallway, bathroom, or bedroom with a solid door.**
   - Stay low to the floor if you are in a line of sight from the window.
   - Keep any pets or other occupants with you in this secure area.
2. **Turn off internal lights to reduce your visibility.** **Switch off any lights in the room where the noise was heard to make it harder for someone outside to see in.**
   - Do not walk directly in front of the window to reach a light switch; if the switch is near the window, leave the light as it is.
3. **Contact the emergency services.** **Call 999 (or 112) immediately if you believe someone is attempting to enter or if you feel in immediate danger.**
   - Use 101 only if the person has clearly left the area and there is no immediate threat.
   - If you have previously registered for the EmergencySMS service, you can text 999.
4. **Use the Silent Solution if you cannot speak safely.** **Dial 999 and, if prompted by an automated message because you are silent, press 55 on your mobile keypad.**
   - This informs the operator that the call is a genuine emergency and will transfer you to the police.
   - If calling from a landline and you cannot speak, stay on the line; the operator will listen for background noise and may connect you automatically.
5. **Provide specific location details to the dispatcher.** **State your full address, including the flat number, floor level, and which side of the building the person is on.**
   - Use descriptive terms such as "facing the main road" or "facing the rear courtyard."
   - Mention specifically if you heard footsteps, metal rattling, or a window being tried.
6. **Keep the line open even if you must stay silent.** **Place the phone down and remain quiet so the operator can hear what is happening in your environment.**
   - Follow any instructions whispered by the operator if they ask yes or no questions.
7. **Contact building security only after alerting the police.** **If your building has a concierge or on-site security, call them to alert them to a trespasser after you have secured your own safety.**
   - They may be able to monitor communal CCTV or check external access points remotely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person right now.
- You do not need to check if the window is locked if doing so requires moving back toward the hazard.
- You do not need to check communal hallways or stairs yourself.
- You do not need to decide on future security upgrades like window film or sensors tonight.

## Important reassurance
Feeling your heart race or experiencing a physical "freeze" response is a standard reaction to a perceived threat. Prioritizing distance over investigation is a recognized safety protocol, even if the noise later turns out to be a non-threat.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization during a potential intruder event. It does not cover long-term security planning, legal definitions of trespass, or post-incident trauma support.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving a potential threat to life or property, follow the direct instructions of emergency dispatchers or attending police officers.
