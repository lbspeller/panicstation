# What to do if… you notice ice buildup on your roof edge and water marks appearing indoors (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as an active leak risk: protect people and electrics first, contain the water if you can do so safely, then arrange urgent roof or gutter help without going onto the roof.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb onto the roof or use a ladder in icy, wet, or windy conditions.
- Do not chip, hammer, or lever ice from the roof edge or guttering.
- Do not touch wet switches, wet plugs, wet appliances, exposed wiring, or a wet consumer unit.
- Do not stand under, press on, or pierce a bulging ceiling.
- Do not pour boiling water onto gutters or roof edges from below.
- Do not ignore spreading stains, bubbling paint, sagging plasterboard, or new drips.

## What to do now
1. **Move people away from the affected area.** Keep everyone out from under the stain, drip, or sagging patch. Move pets away too. If the ceiling is bowed, swollen, cracking, or making wet plaster sounds, treat it as unsafe to stand beneath.

2. **Reduce electrical risk before handling the leak.** If water is near lights, sockets, switches, extractors, cables, appliances, or the consumer unit, avoid touching anything wet. Switch off the affected circuit, or the main switch, only if you can reach it without standing in water or touching wet equipment. Use a battery torch rather than candles.

3. **Keep using electricity away from the wet area.** Unplug and move portable items only if the plugs and floor are dry and it is safe to do so. If water has reached electrics, keep the power off in that area until a registered electrician says it is safe.

4. **Contain visible water from a safe position.** Put a bucket, bowl, or tray under drips. Lay towels around the container, not directly under a bulging ceiling. Move furniture, electronics, documents, and soft furnishings away from the wet area.

5. **Protect belongings you cannot move.** Cover large items with plastic sheeting or bin bags if you can do this without standing under the damaged ceiling. Keep containers where water is actually falling, because roof water can track along joists before it appears indoors.

6. **Avoid unsafe loft access.** Do not enter the loft if there is heavy water, wet electrics, poor footing, darkness, or any sign the ceiling below is weakening. If you can safely look from the hatch without stepping in, check only for obvious dripping and then close the hatch.

7. **Get roof-edge help quickly.** If you own the home, contact a roofer, guttering specialist, or roofline contractor and say: ice at the eaves or roof edge, indoor water marks or drips, and possible freeze-thaw roof leak. Ask them not to rely on unsafe ladder work in icy conditions.

8. **If you rent, report it immediately.** Contact your landlord, letting agent, council, housing association, managing agent, or building emergency repairs line as relevant. Say there is roof-edge ice and water marks indoors, and mention any electrical risk, sagging ceiling, or active dripping.

9. **Record evidence without delaying safety steps.** Take photos or short videos from ground level of the outside ice, indoor stains, drips, damaged items, and the buckets or towels used to limit damage. Keep receipts for emergency call-outs, temporary materials, and any urgent safety work.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether this is an insurance claim.
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is gutter blockage, roof covering, insulation, ventilation, flashing, or another roofline issue.
- You do not need to choose permanent plaster, paint, insulation, or mould-prevention work until the leak source is controlled and the area is drying.
- You do not need to clean the roof edge yourself.

## Important reassurance
Ice at the roof edge can make meltwater travel in unexpected ways before a mark appears indoors. A new stain does not mean you failed to notice something obvious; the useful first move is to keep people away from danger, keep water away from electrics, and get the roof edge checked safely.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate harm. Later decisions about drying, roof repair, gutter repair, insulation, ventilation, tenancy responsibilities, or insurance may need a qualified roofer, registered electrician, landlord, managing agent, insurer, or housing adviser.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent first actions, not legal, electrical, building, insurance, tenancy, or professional advice. Follow instructions from emergency services, your landlord or managing agent, your insurer, and qualified tradespeople where they apply.
