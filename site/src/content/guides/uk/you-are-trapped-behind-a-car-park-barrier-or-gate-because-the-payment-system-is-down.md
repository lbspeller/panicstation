# What to do if… you are trapped behind a car-park barrier or gate because the payment system is down (UK)

## Short answer
Ensure the lane is safe, then use the exit intercom or the operator’s phone number found on nearby signage to request a manual opening. Document the fault with photos of the error message and the time to protect against future overstay charges.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to force the barrier arm upward or "nudge" it with your vehicle.
- Do not tailgate the vehicle in front of you if their barrier rises; this often triggers secondary enforcement cameras or causes the barrier to drop on your car.
- Do not reverse blindly into moving traffic to find another exit.
- Do not exit your vehicle in a live lane unless it is safe to do so and the handbrake is fully applied.
- Do not use the "pay later" button on a terminal unless you have explicitly confirmed with the operator that this will not result in a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN).

## What to do now
1. **Secure your vehicle in the exit lane.** Put the car in park, apply the handbrake, and activate hazard warning lights to alert drivers behind you.
2. **Locate and press the intercom button.** Look for a button marked **Help**, **Information**, or an icon of a telephone on the exit pedestal. 
   - Press it once and wait at least 60 seconds for a response before pressing again.
3. **Identify the operator and contact number.** If the intercom fails, look for the main tariff board or a fault-reporting sign. 
   - Under the 2026 Single Code of Practice, these must be within 2 meters of the payment point.
   - Call the number provided and provide your vehicle registration and the specific exit lane number if visible.
4. **Invoke the "Full Payment Before Exit" rule.** If the car park uses ANPR, inform the operator you are prepared to pay but the system is failing. 
   - Under 2025 updates to the Industry Code, you cannot be penalised for taking extra time to pay as long as you attempt full payment before leaving the site.
5. **Collect evidence of the system failure.** Take clear photos of:
   - The "out of order" or "system error" message on the screen.
   - The barrier in the "down" position.
   - Any signs that list the operator's contact details.
   - A time-stamp (such as your car's dashboard clock or phone screen).
6. **Request a fault reference number.** If you speak to a human, ask them to log the fault against your registration so you are not charged for an "overstay" caused by the broken equipment.
7. **Escalate only if safety is compromised.** 
   - Call **999** if the barrier failure creates an immediate danger, such as blocking emergency service access or trapping a person in extreme heat.
   - Call **101** only to report non-urgent antisocial behaviour or threats from other drivers if the situation escalates.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle disputes about grace periods or "overstay" fees while still at the barrier.
- You do not need to contact your bank to verify a "failed" app payment immediately.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal complaint until you have safely exited the premises.

## Important reassurance
Equipment failures in car parks are common and are the responsibility of the operator to manage. Feeling a sense of "trapped" panic is a normal physiological response to being physically blocked. Most modern systems have remote overrides, and documenting the fault now provides you with a strong legal basis to cancel any automated charges issued later.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for exiting a car park during a system failure. It does not cover the formal appeals process for Parking Charge Notices (PCNs) or legal disputes regarding contract law.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for signage and equipment maintenance are governed by the British Parking Association (BPA) or International Parking Community (IPC) codes of practice, which are undergoing transition until 31 December 2026. If you feel physically unsafe or a medical emergency occurs, contact emergency services immediately.
