# What to do if… your electric vehicle charging cable is stuck and will not release (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the charging session first, unlock the car, remove any tension on the cable, and try a careful release. If it still will not release, use the vehicle’s manual release only after charging has stopped, or call the chargepoint operator helpline shown on the unit.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t yank, twist hard, or lever the connector with tools; this can damage the port, cable, or lock.
- Don’t use the vehicle’s manual or emergency release while the vehicle is still actively charging.
- Don’t drive off with the cable still attached.
- Don’t switch off or reset charger electrics unless the chargepoint operator or qualified site staff tells you to.
- Don’t leave your own detachable cable unattended if it has released from the car but is still locked in the chargepoint.
- Don’t keep retrying with the cable pulled tight; reduce tension first.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car safe to pause.** Put the car in Park if it has that setting, apply the parking brake, and take pressure off yourself before pulling again.
2. **End the charging session cleanly.**
   - Use the charger screen, app, RFID card, or station stop control to stop the session.
   - If your car has an in-car or vehicle-app “stop charging” control, use that too.
   - Check the charger screen, vehicle dashboard, or app for a sign that charging has stopped.
3. **Unlock the car and keep it unlocked for a moment.**
   - Unlock with the key fob, door handle, vehicle app, or in-car control.
   - Some vehicles only release the charge-port lock after a fresh unlock, so try a few calm lock and unlock cycles.
4. **Remove tension and reset the latch.**
   - Support the cable so it is not pulling down or sideways.
   - Push the connector gently fully in, then pull it straight out without twisting.
   - Check that the cable is not kinked, trapped, or bearing weight.
5. **Try one structured unlock cycle.**
   - With charging stopped and the car secure, try pressing unlock several times, then try lock and unlock a few times.
   - Check after each attempt. Stop if the connector feels jammed or anything looks damaged.
6. **If your detachable cable is stuck in the chargepoint, contact the operator.**
   - Call the service number on the chargepoint or use the operator app.
   - Tell them the cable is locked in the chargepoint and you need it released.
   - Give the chargepoint ID or socket number if it is shown on the unit.
   - If the chargepoint is at a staffed car park, supermarket, depot, or workplace, ask site staff or security to contact facilities.
7. **If the connector is stuck in the car, use the vehicle-specific release route.**
   - Check the owner’s manual for “charge port”, “charging cable release”, or “manual release”.
   - Use the manual release only after the charger, car, or app shows charging has stopped.
   - If the manual release does not work, contact the vehicle manufacturer, roadside assistance, or your breakdown provider.
8. **If you truly need to leave and it still will not release:**
   - Do not force the connector to keep the journey going.
   - Call the chargepoint operator and explain that you are blocked and need to depart.
   - If you are on private land, tell site staff or security so the vehicle or cable is not treated as abandoned.
   - Arrange another way to continue the urgent journey if needed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault is the car, cable, app, or charger right now.
- You do not need to decide about repairs, warranties, complaints, or blame at the charger.
- You do not need to keep trying beyond a few careful release attempts; once those have failed, switch to the operator, vehicle manual, or breakdown support.

## Important reassurance
This can happen when a charging session has not ended cleanly or the vehicle’s charge-port lock has not released. Slowing down, stopping the session, unlocking again, and keeping the connector straight gives you the best chance of freeing it without causing damage.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get you safely unplugged and moving. Later decisions about repairs, the charging network, payment issues, or repeated faults may need help from the vehicle manufacturer, chargepoint operator, installer, or breakdown provider.

## Important note
This guide is general information for urgent situations, not mechanical, electrical, legal, financial, or professional advice. If anything looks damaged, smells hot, sparks, smokes, or feels unsafe, step back and seek professional help; use emergency services only if there is immediate danger.
