# What to do if… water is bubbling up from the ground in your garden or driveway (UK)

## Short answer
Keep people, pets, and vehicles off the wet or bubbling area, turn off your internal stop tap if you can do it safely, and report the leak to your water supplier. If you rent, tell your landlord or letting agent as well.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t dig to find the leak; you could hit other buried services or make unstable ground worse.
- Don’t drive, park, or stand over the bubbling or sinking area.
- Don’t lift a heavy meter cover, force a stuck cover, or put your hand into a flooded meter chamber.
- Don’t delay reporting because you are unsure who owns the pipe.
- Don’t touch wet outdoor electrics, garden lighting, garage sockets, or extension leads.
- Don’t assume it is rainwater if the water is bubbling, spreading, or continuing when indoor taps are not being used.

## What to do now
1. **Make the area safer.** Keep people, pets, bins, deliveries, and vehicles away from the bubbling or wet patch. Use cones, chairs, tape, or another clear marker if you can do this without stepping on soft ground.
2. **Reduce water flow if you can.** Turn off the internal stop tap, often under the kitchen sink, in a utility area, downstairs toilet, cellar, garage, or where the water pipe enters the home.
3. **Check the meter only if it is safe and easy.** If you have a visible water meter, turn off taps and appliances, then see whether the dial or flow indicator keeps moving. Do not lift a heavy cover, force a cover, or lean into a flooded chamber.
4. **Report the leak to your water supplier.** Tell them water is bubbling up from the ground, where it is, whether it is near a road, pavement, boundary, meter, driveway, building, or neighbour’s land, and whether the ground looks soft or sinking.
5. **Use the right reporting route for where you live.** In England or Wales, use your water bill or a postcode supplier finder to identify the company that supplies water to the property. In Scotland, report it to Scottish Water. In Northern Ireland, report it to NI Water.
6. **If you rent, tell your landlord or letting agent immediately.** Send a short written message with photos if you can, and still report it to the water supplier if it may be near the road, pavement, boundary, meter, or public supply.
7. **Record what you can without getting close.** Take a wide photo showing the house, road, boundary, driveway, or garden layout, then a closer photo or video from a safe distance showing the bubbling water, cracks, sinking, or water reaching walls.
8. **Escalate if there is immediate danger.** Call 999 if the ground is giving way, someone could fall into a hole, water is creating an electrical danger, or there is an immediate danger to the public. If there is no immediate danger, keep the area clear and follow the water supplier’s reporting route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who will pay before you report the leak.
- You do not need to prove whether it is a water main, communication pipe, private supply pipe, shared pipe, drain, or groundwater.
- You do not need to dig test holes or expose the pipe.
- You do not need to arrange permanent repairs until the area is safer and responsibility has been checked.
- You do not need to handle insurance, landlord disputes, or leak allowance questions in the first few minutes.

## Important reassurance
Bubbling water from the ground is alarming, but you do not need to diagnose it under pressure. The useful first steps are to keep weight off the area, reduce flow if you safely can, report it, and keep a simple record of what is happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first hours. Later decisions may need help from your water supplier, landlord, insurer, a qualified plumber or leak repairer, or another specialist, especially if the pipe is shared or responsibility is unclear.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional plumbing, legal, electrical, engineering, insurance, or safety advice. If you are unsure whether it is safe to approach the area, the stop tap, the meter, or nearby electrics, keep away and contact the water supplier or emergency services.
