# What to do if… you are abroad and realise your residence permit card expires before your return trip and you need a plan (UK)

## Short answer
You **cannot travel using an expired Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)**. You must immediately confirm your digital immigration status (eVisa) is correctly linked to your current passport in your UKVI account so the airline can verify your permission to travel electronically.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to check in with an expired BRP card; airlines will receive a "no-board" notification from the Home Office system.
- Do not rely on physical BRP cards for travel if the date has passed, even if your underlying leave to remain is still valid.
- Do not book new flights through countries that require transit visas based on your UK residence until you have secured your return document or digital status.
- Do not use third-party "fast-track" agents who claim they can bypass the UKVI account system.
- Do not assume a "share code" alone is enough; the passport you present at check-in must be the specific document linked in the UKVI portal.

## What to do now
1. **Access your UKVI account immediately to verify your digital record.**
   - Sign in to the official GOV.UK "View and prove your immigration status" service.
   - Verify that your status is correctly displayed as "Active" or "Granted".
2. **Link the passport you are currently holding to your eVisa.**
   - Navigate to the "Update your UK Visas and Immigration account details" section.
   - If the passport number in the system does not match the passport you are carrying, use the "Update" function to add your current document.
   - Note that updates involving a photo of the new document (via smartphone) are typically processed faster than manual entries.
3. **Generate a "share code" and save it as an offline backup.**
   - Select the option to prove your status to an "Other" reason.
   - Keep a PDF or screenshot of this code; while airlines use automated checks, having the code provides a manual verification path if the system fails.
4. **Notify your airline of your digital status at least 72 hours before departure.**
   - Contact the airline’s help desk or manage your booking online to ensure your Advanced Passenger Information (API) matches the passport linked to your eVisa.
   - Ask the carrier to confirm they can perform a "digital permission to travel" check.
5. **Apply for a "BRP replacement single-entry visa" if digital access fails.**
   - If you cannot access your UKVI account or your passport cannot be linked in time, apply for this specific visa at a local Visa Application Centre (VAC).
   - Prepare to pay the fee (approximately £154) and provide biometrics.
   - Factor in processing times: standard service is typically 15 working days, while priority (if available locally) is usually 5 working days.
6. **Gather secondary evidence of your right to reside in the UK.**
   - Collect digital copies of your original Home Office grant letter, recent UK utility bills, or a letter from your UK employer/university.
   - These may be required if you need to apply for the emergency single-entry visa.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a full replacement BRP card while abroad; these are being phased out in favour of digital-only status.
- You do not need to update your UK home address or secondary contact details until you have successfully returned to the UK.
- You do not need to contact Border Force at your arrival airport; your status is verified at the point of boarding and at the e-Gates/primary inspection.

## Important reassurance
Finding out a document has expired while overseas is a high-stress event, but the transition to digital eVisas means you are no longer solely dependent on the physical card. If your underlying permission is valid and your passport is correctly linked in the UKVI system, the "expired" card will not prevent you from clearing automated carrier checks.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and return-to-UK planning only. It does not cover visa extensions, citizenship applications, or appeals against visa cancellations.

## Important note
Airlines have a legal obligation to deny boarding to any passenger whose permission to travel cannot be verified via the Home Office's digital interface. This guide is for informational purposes and does not guarantee entry; always consult official GOV.UK guidance for the most current fee and process updates.
