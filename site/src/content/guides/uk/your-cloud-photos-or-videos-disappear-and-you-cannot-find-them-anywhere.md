# What to do if… your cloud photos or videos disappear and you cannot find them anywhere (UK)

## Short answer
Stop entering passwords or payment details immediately. Verify your device’s date and time settings, disconnect from public Wi-Fi, and disable any active VPNs to rule out common configuration errors.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click through browser warnings to enter personal, financial, or login information.
- Do not download or install "security certificates" or "browser updates" offered on a warning page.
- Do not call any technical support numbers displayed within the browser error message.
- Do not ignore the warning on a public Wi-Fi network even if the site is normally trusted.
- Do not permanently disable antivirus web-shielding or firewall settings to bypass the error.

## What to do now
1. **Close all sensitive tabs immediately.** Stop any attempts to sign into email, banking, work portals, or shopping sites while the warnings are active.
2. **Check the system date and time on your device.** Correct the time, date, and time zone if they are inaccurate, as certificates rely on synchronized clocks to validate.
3. **Open a Private or Incognito window and retry one site.**
   - If the site loads correctly, a browser extension or corrupted cache is likely causing the issue.
   - If the warning persists, the problem is likely at the network or system level.
4. **Switch to a different network to isolate the fault.**
   - Disconnect from the current Wi-Fi and use a mobile data hotspot if available.
   - If the warnings disappear on mobile data, the original network (router, ISP, or public Wi-Fi portal) is the source of the conflict.
5. **Trigger the Wi-Fi login page if using a public network.**
   - Disconnect and reconnect to the Wi-Fi.
   - Type `http://neverssl.com` into your address bar; this non-encrypted site often forces the captive portal (login page) to appear without a certificate block.
6. **Disable connection-altering software temporarily.**
   - Turn off any active VPN or proxy server.
   - Temporarily disable "HTTPS Scanning" or "Encrypted Web Scanning" within your antivirus settings to see if the warnings stop.
7. **Report the issue if using a managed device.**
   - Contact your employer's IT service desk for work laptops or school technical support for educational devices.
   - Do not attempt to modify administrative security policies on managed equipment.
8. **Secure your accounts if you entered data after the warning appeared.**
   - Use a separate, known-safe device (such as a phone on mobile data) to change passwords for any accounts accessed during the issue.
   - Prioritize your primary email account and enable two-step verification (2SV) where available.
9. **Report suspected fraud or malicious websites.**
   - If you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland and believe you have lost money or shared details with a scam, contact **Report Fraud** at `reportfraud.police.uk` or call **0300 123 2040**.
   - If you are in Scotland, report the incident to **Police Scotland** by calling **101**.
   - Report the suspicious URL directly to the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) via their official scam website reporting tool.

## What can wait
- You do not need to factory reset your device or reinstall the operating system immediately.
- You do not need to contact the administrators of every website showing the warning if it is happening on multiple sites.
- You do not need to assume your hardware is failing until software and network causes are ruled out.

## Important reassurance
Browser certificate warnings are high-visibility alerts designed to prevent data interception. In many cases, these warnings are triggered by simple issues like an incorrect system clock, an expired local cache, or a public Wi-Fi sign-in page that has not yet been completed. A cautious pause prevents the primary risk, which is the unintended exposure of credentials.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for identifying and isolating the cause of widespread browser security warnings. It does not cover individual website outages or advanced network forensics.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional cybersecurity or legal advice. If you suspect your financial details have been compromised, contact your bank immediately using the number on the back of your card.
