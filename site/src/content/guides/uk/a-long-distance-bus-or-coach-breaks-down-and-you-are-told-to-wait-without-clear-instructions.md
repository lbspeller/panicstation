# What to do if… a long-distance bus or coach breaks down and you are told to wait without clear instructions (UK)

## Short answer
Move to the safest location designated by the driver (usually behind a safety barrier if on a motorway), identify your exact location using what3words or driver location signs, and establish a fixed update schedule with the operator. If there is immediate smoke, fire, or traffic risk, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk along the motorway or dual carriageway verge to "look for the next exit."
- Do not stand between the coach and oncoming traffic or near the rear of the vehicle.
- Do not ask the driver to open the luggage hold while the coach is on the hard shoulder or a live lane.
- Do not cross any live lanes of traffic to reach an emergency phone or better vantage point.
- Do not accept a lift from an unverified private vehicle or "good samaritan" while on a major road.
- Do not use high-drain phone apps (video streaming) until you have a confirmed rescue ETA.

## What to do now
1. **Identify any immediate "red flag" dangers that require an emergency call.** **If you see smoke, smell fuel, or if the coach is stopped in a live lane without a hard shoulder, call 999 immediately.**
   - Request the Police for traffic management or the Fire Service for fire/leaks.
   - Mention if there are passengers with mobility needs, children, or elderly travellers.

2. **Move to a position of safety as directed by the driver or emergency signs.** **If you are on a motorway or dual carriageway, you should generally exit the vehicle by the left-hand doors and move behind the safety barrier.**
   - Stay well away from the carriageway and avoid standing directly behind or in front of the coach.
   - If there is no barrier, move as far up the verge or embankment as possible while keeping the group together.

3. **Establish your precise location using digital and physical cues.** **Download or open the what3words app to find the three-word address for your current 3-metre square.**
   - Look for "Driver Location Signs" (small blue/yellow signs every 500m) or "Marker Posts" (small white posts every 100m) at the back of the hard shoulder.
   - Note the numbers and letters on these signs (e.g., M1 A 45.2).

4. **Request a structured communication plan from the driver.** **Ask the driver for a specific "next update" time rather than a general ETA for a replacement.**
   - Ask: "When will the operator give us the next status update, even if the replacement time is still unknown?"
   - Ask: "Are we waiting for recovery, a mechanic, or a relief vehicle?"

5. **Report the situation to the coach operator via their official digital channels.** **Use the operator's app, live chat, or emergency "help" line to provide your ticket reference and exact what3words location.**
   - Explicitly list "Vulnerable Passenger" status if applicable (e.g., wheelchair users, passengers needing time-sensitive medication, or unaccompanied minors).
   - Inform them that passengers are currently at the roadside/on the hard shoulder.

6. **Keep your essential survival items on your person and secure.** **Ensure you have your ID, phone, power bank, essential medication, and a warm layer of clothing out of the luggage hold.**
   - Assume you will not have access to the main luggage hold until the vehicle is towed to a service area or a secure depot.

7. **Ensure your phone's Location Services and GPS are toggled ON.** **This allows emergency services to find you via "Advanced Mobile Location" (AML) if you need to call 999.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to research refund policies or compensation "Delay Repay" rules until you are on a replacement vehicle or at your destination.
- You do not need to book your own alternative transport (trains or taxis) until the operator has officially stated they cannot provide a relief coach.
- You do not need to worry about missed connections or hotel bookings yet; focus entirely on physical safety and location reporting for the next 30 minutes.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of "information vacuum" during a breakdown. Coach operators often prioritise securing a relief vehicle before they have a confirmed ETA to share. By establishing your own location and a check-in time with the driver, you regain a sense of structure. Being behind a safety barrier or on a wide verge is the standard safety protocol and significantly reduces your risk while you wait for professional recovery.

## Scope note
This guide is for the immediate 0–60 minutes following a vehicle failure on a major UK road. It focuses on physical safety and situational awareness rather than long-term travel planning.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information and does not replace the instructions of the driver, National Highways officers, or emergency services. If the situation escalates or you feel your life is at risk due to traffic or vehicle conditions, call 999 immediately.
