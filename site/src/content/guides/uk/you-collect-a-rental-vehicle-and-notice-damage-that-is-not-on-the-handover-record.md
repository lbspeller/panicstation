# What to do if… you collect a rental vehicle and notice damage that is not on the handover record (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive away until you have clearly recorded the damage and asked the rental company to add it to the agreement, condition report, or handover record. Get a copy or written acknowledgement before leaving the site if you can.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive off assuming photos alone will settle it if the official record still says the vehicle is undamaged.
- Don’t sign, tap, or click “accept” if it confirms the vehicle condition is correct when it is not.
- Don’t leave without asking for the damage to be recorded or acknowledged in writing.
- Don’t edit, crop, filter, or mark up the only copy of your photos in a way that removes the number plate, location, date, or wider context.
- Don’t argue on the forecourt. Stay factual and focus on correcting the record.
- Don’t block traffic or stand somewhere unsafe to take photos.

## What to do now
1. **Pause before leaving the rental site.** Park safely, switch the engine off if appropriate, and keep the keys with you.

2. **Photograph and video the damage before the vehicle moves.**
   - Take wide photos showing the whole vehicle and the number plate.
   - Take close photos of each scratch, dent, chip, scuff, tyre, wheel, glass, or interior mark that is missing from the record.
   - Take the close photos from more than one angle.
   - Take a slow walkaround video if it is safe to do so.
   - Keep the original files on your phone.

3. **Compare the vehicle with the handover check.**
   - Look at the paper diagram, app screen, email, or digital condition report.
   - Mark the missing damage on the paper copy if there is one.
   - If the check is digital, save the screen, take a screenshot, take a photo of the screen, or keep the emailed copy.

4. **Ask staff to correct the record now.**
   - Say: “I’m happy to leave once this damage is recorded on the agreement and I have a copy.”
   - Ask them to add the damage to the rental agreement, inspection form, condition report, or app record.
   - Ask for the staff member’s name if they are handling the correction.

5. **Get proof of the correction.**
   - Ask for a signed paper copy, an updated email, a revised digital record, or a photo of the corrected report.
   - Check that the record includes the registration number, date, time, and a clear description or marking of the damage.

6. **If staff cannot update the record, send a written notice before you leave.**
   - Use the branch email, customer service email, app message, or contact route shown in your booking, rental paperwork, or the company’s official website.
   - Attach the photos if possible.
   - Write that the damage was present at collection and is missing from the handover record.
   - Include the vehicle registration, branch, date, time, and staff name if known.
   - Keep the sent email or message.

7. **Check whether BVRLA may matter later.**
   - Look at the paperwork, app, terms, or branch information to see whether the rental company says it is a BVRLA member.
   - Take a photo or screenshot if you see a membership reference.
   - This does not need a dispute now; it may help later if a complaint remains unresolved.

8. **If you feel pressured to leave without correction, slow the decision down.**
   - Ask for a supervisor or manager.
   - Repeat that you are not disputing the rental, only asking for the pre-existing damage to be recorded.
   - If you must leave because the branch is closing or you have no safe alternative, leave only after taking photos, sending the written notice, and saving the proof that it was sent from the site.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, escalate, claim a refund, or contact your card provider.
- You do not need to debate repair costs, liability, insurance excess, or blame at pickup.
- You do not need to write a long complaint now. A short factual record of the missing damage is enough for this moment.
- You can deal with card disputes, chargeback, Section 75, or ADR later if a charge actually appears.

## Important reassurance
This situation is usually fixable at collection. The main thing is to make the vehicle’s actual condition match the written or digital record before you drive away.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If a disputed damage charge appears later, you may need to use the rental company’s complaints process, your card provider’s dispute process, or a specialist advice route. If the company is a BVRLA member and the complaint remains unresolved after the company’s own process, BVRLA ADR may be available.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate record-keeping and harm prevention. It is not legal, financial, insurance, or professional advice. Rental company processes vary, so keep everything factual, in writing, and backed by clear evidence.
