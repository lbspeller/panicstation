# What to do if… your car keeps stalling at idle and you are stopped somewhere safe (UK)

## Short answer
Make the car visible and secure first, then stop repeated restart attempts and arrange help if it will not idle reliably.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep trying again and again for minutes; repeated cranking can flatten the battery and may make a fault worse.
- Do not keep the engine running if there is an oil pressure warning, overheating warning, smoke, a strong fuel smell, or a burning smell.
- Do not get out into fast traffic or stand between your vehicle and moving traffic.
- Do not open the bonnet if you are on a motorway, fast road, or close to moving traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway.

## What to do now
1. **Make it safe and visible.** Put on hazard warning lights. Apply the parking brake or handbrake. Put the car in neutral if it is manual, or Park if it is automatic. If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights.
2. **Check for immediate danger.** If you notice smoke, a strong fuel smell, a burning smell, overheating, or an oil pressure warning, switch the engine off. Move people away from traffic if it is safe to do so. Call 999 if there is immediate danger, such as fire, smoke, a live-lane stop, or anyone feeling unwell.
3. **Try one calm restart only if there are no serious warning signs.** Turn off non-essential electrical loads such as heated screens, blower, and chargers. Wait about 10 seconds, then restart once.
4. **If it starts, only move if that improves safety.** If you are not already well out of the way, move slowly to a safer nearby place such as a car park, side street, lay-by, service area, or emergency area. If it cuts out again, stop trying to limp through traffic.
5. **If it will not stay running, switch to recovery.** Contact your breakdown provider or a local recovery service. Be ready to give your exact location, direction of travel, whether you are in a live lane, hard shoulder, lay-by, car park, or service area, and any warning lights such as oil pressure, engine temperature, battery, or charging.
6. **If you are on a motorway or fast dual carriageway, follow motorway breakdown steps.** If you can, go left into an emergency area, service area, lay-by, or hard shoulder and stop as far left as possible with your wheels turned left. If it is safe, get out on the side away from traffic and wait behind a barrier. If you cannot get left or cannot get out safely, stay in the vehicle with seat belts and hazard warning lights on, call 999 and ask for the police, or use the SOS or eCall button if the vehicle has one. Use an emergency telephone if one is safely reachable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide on repairs, garages, or costs while you are still roadside.
- You do not need to keep testing the car with repeated restarts; one calm attempt is enough before switching to recovery.
- You do not need to check under the bonnet until you are somewhere safely away from traffic.

## Important reassurance
A car that stalls at idle can sometimes have a simple cause, but repeated stalling can become unsafe quickly if it happens near traffic. Stopping the testing and focusing on visibility, distance from traffic, and recovery is a sensible first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and avoid risky roadside decisions. Diagnosis, repair, insurance, and recovery choices may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, emergency services, financial, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in danger, in a live lane, near smoke or fire, or feeling unwell, prioritise immediate safety and call 999.
