# What to do if… you realise you entered your passport number incorrectly on an entry form and departure is close (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as something that could stop you boarding until you have checked it. First work out whether the wrong passport number is in your carrier’s passenger information or in a destination government travel authorisation, visa, eVisa, or entry form, because the fix is different.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a small typo will be ignored; some travel checks match the passport number exactly.
- Do not keep submitting new forms until you know which system is wrong.
- Do not use paid third-party sites unless you have deliberately chosen to; official government pages are usually the safest route for government travel permissions.
- Do not send passport photos or passport scans to unverified contacts.
- Do not cancel flights, trains, hotels, or plans in panic before checking whether the mistake can be corrected or replaced.

## What to do now
1. **Find the form or confirmation and identify what it belongs to.**
   - If it is inside your airline, ferry, coach, or train booking, it is probably passenger information such as API or APIS.
   - If it has a government reference number, approval email, visa, eVisa, ETA, eTA, ESTA, or travel authorisation wording, treat it as a destination government permission.
   - Compare the submitted number with the passport you will actually travel on.

2. **If the mistake is in carrier passenger information, try the booking or check-in area first.**
   - Open the carrier’s official website or app.
   - Go to Manage booking, Check-in, Travel documents, or Passenger information.
   - Correct the passport number if the system allows it, then save or resubmit the information.
   - Check the saved details again after submitting.

3. **If the carrier system will not let you change it, contact the operating carrier.**
   - Use the carrier’s official contact options.
   - Say: “My passport number was entered incorrectly in my passenger information. Please correct the passport number on my booking.”
   - Have your booking reference, travel date, route, and passport ready.
   - If you booked through an agent, contact the agent too, but do not rely only on the agent if departure is close.

4. **If the mistake is in a destination government permission, use the official government site for that destination.**
   - Look for a status, update, retrieve, or check application option.
   - Check whether the passport number can be changed after submission.
   - If the official instructions say passport details cannot be corrected, you may need to make a new application with the correct passport number.
   - Do not assume it is fixed until the official system or confirmation shows the correct passport number.

5. **If the wrong number appears on an approval email, compare it with your passport now.**
   - Save the approval email or status page offline.
   - If the passport number does not match exactly, treat the approval as unresolved until the official system shows the correct number or a new approval is issued.
   - Keep the old reference number in case you need to explain what happened to the carrier or official helpdesk.

6. **If you rely on UKVI digital immigration status for this journey, check your UKVI account.**
   - Make sure the passport or travel document linked to your UKVI account is the document you will use to travel.
   - If the details are wrong or out of date, use the official GOV.UK update route.

7. **Prepare for the airport, station, or terminal.**
   - Bring the physical passport you will travel on.
   - Keep booking references, approval numbers, and corrected confirmations accessible offline.
   - Arrive earlier than usual so there is time to speak to the carrier before check-in or boarding closes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide about complaints, refunds, insurance claims, or escalation right now.
- You do not need to gather a large evidence file; save the confirmation pages and emails you already have.
- You do not need to rebook anything until you know whether the passenger information can be corrected or the government permission can be replaced in time.

## Important reassurance
This is a common last-minute travel problem. The next step is not to solve every travel rule at once; it is to find the exact place where the wrong passport number sits and follow that system’s correction route.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the risk of a passport-number mismatch affecting boarding or travel. Later decisions about visas, denied authorisations, refunds, insurance, or missed travel may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, or professional advice. Carriers and border authorities apply their own checks, and destination rules can change.
