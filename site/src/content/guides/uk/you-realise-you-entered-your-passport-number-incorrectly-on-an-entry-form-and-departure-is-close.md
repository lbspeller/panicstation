# What to do if… you realise you entered your passport number incorrectly on an entry form and departure is close (UK)

## Short answer
Identify immediately if the error is on your airline's Advance Passenger Information (API) or a government travel authorisation (like an ESTA or ETA). Errors on government authorisations usually require a full re-application, while airline typos can often be corrected at check-in or through "Manage Booking."

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a single-digit typo will be ignored; automated "no-board" instructions are triggered by document mismatches.
- Do not attempt to "fix" a passport number on a submitted ESTA, Canada eTA, or UK ETA, as these fields are usually locked and require a new application.
- Do not wait until you reach the airport gate to mention the error if you can see it now.
- Do not cancel your flight until you have checked the processing time for a new travel authorisation.
- Do not share your passport details or photos with third-party "fast-track" sites that are not official government portals.

## What to do now
1. **Determine which specific system contains the error.**
   - **Carrier API:** This is the information you gave to the airline, Eurostar, or ferry company (often during check-in or in "Manage Booking").
   - **Travel Authorisation:** This is a government-issued permission linked to your passport, such as a US ESTA, Australian ETA, Canadian eTA, or the UK ETA for visitors.
   - **UKVI/eVisa:** If you are a UK resident or visa holder, this is the digital status linked to your passport in your UKVI account.

2. **Correct carrier Advance Passenger Information (API) through the official app or website.**
   - **Log in to the "Manage Booking" or "Check-in" section of your travel provider.**
   - **Locate the "Passenger Information" or "Travel Documents" section.**
   - **Attempt to edit the passport number directly; many carriers allow you to overwrite previous data up to a few hours before departure.**
   - **Save the changes and download a new boarding pass if one was already issued.**

3. **Submit a new application immediately if the error is on a travel authorisation (ESTA/eTA/ETA).**
   - **Go to the official government website (e.g., cbp.gov for USA, canada.ca for Canada, or gov.uk for UK ETA).**
   - **Start a completely new application using the correct passport number.**
   - **Pay the required fee again; these systems generally do not allow passport number corrections on existing approvals.**
   - **Monitor your email for approval; while many are instant, some can take up to 72 hours.**

4. **Update your UKVI account if you are travelling on a UK eVisa.**
   - **Access the "Update your UK Visas and Immigration account details" service on GOV.UK.**
   - **Add the correct passport details to your account to ensure it is linked to your digital status.**
   - **Check that the update is confirmed before you attempt to board, as carriers now perform automated digital status checks.**

5. **Contact the carrier's customer service if you are locked out of online edits.**
   - **Call the airline or operator's urgent assistance line if departure is within 24 hours.**
   - **State clearly: "I need to correct a passport number typo in my Advance Passenger Information."**
   - **Ask for confirmation that the manifest has been updated with the correct document number.**

6. **Arrive at the terminal early with all physical documentation.**
   - **Bring the physical passport you intended to use, regardless of the typo.**
   - **Keep printed or offline digital copies of any new authorisation approvals or API confirmation screens.**
   - **Go to the check-in desk rather than using self-service kiosks if the error remains unresolved online.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact the destination's embassy or consulate for a simple API typo.
- You do not need to request a refund for the first (incorrect) travel authorisation fee immediately.
- You do not need to worry about long-term immigration records for a simple data entry error that is corrected before travel.

## Important reassurance
Entering a document number incorrectly is one of the most common travel errors. Airlines deal with "API mismatches" daily and have procedures to verify your physical passport against the manifest at the desk. As long as you have the correct travel authorisation (ESTA/ETA) for the passport in your hand, most issues can be resolved with extra time at the airport.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document entry errors. It does not cover situations where a passport is lost, expired, or where a visa has been formally denied.

## Important note
Boarding and entry are at the final discretion of the carrier and border authorities. If you are unable to obtain a valid travel authorisation linked to your correct passport number, you may be denied boarding. Always use official government domains (.gov.uk, .gov, .gc.ca) for travel permissions.
