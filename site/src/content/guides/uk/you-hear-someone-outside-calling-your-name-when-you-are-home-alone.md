# What to do if… you hear someone outside calling your name when you are home alone (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside, secure your doors and windows, and do not respond with identifying details. If you think someone is trying to get in, threatening you, or refusing to leave when you feel unsafe, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door “just to check” or step outside to look around.
- Do not call back with your name, who else is inside, or where you are in the home.
- Do not move to a window with curtains open to see who it is.
- Do not let them pressure you into talking through an open door.
- Do not assume it is harmless because they sound friendly.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause inside.** Move away from doors and front-facing windows. Lock the front door, any back or side doors, and any accessible windows.
2. **Make the entrance harder to use without showing yourself.** Use a peephole, intercom, or door camera if you already have one. If you are in a flat, make sure the main entry door is shut and do not buzz anyone in to talk.
3. **Avoid direct engagement.** If you decide to say anything, speak only through a closed, locked door and keep it neutral, such as: “I can’t come to the door.” Do not confirm your name, your exact location inside, or whether you are alone.
4. **Call 999 if there may be immediate danger.** This includes someone trying handles, forcing entry, making threats, refusing to leave, attempting to follow someone into the building, or making you feel unable to stay safe.
5. **If you cannot speak safely on a 999 call, still call.** Listen to the operator. If you are calling from a mobile and are prompted, press 55 to show the call is genuine and to be put through to police.
6. **If they leave and it is no longer an emergency, report what happened.** You can report online or call 101, especially if they tried the door, used your name in a way that felt targeted, looked into windows, or may return.
7. **Use other people without exposing yourself.** Call or text a trusted neighbour, building concierge, security desk, or someone nearby and ask them to check the outside area without you opening the door.
8. **Note details from a safe place.** Write down the time, exact words used, voice, knocks or bangs, clothing if seen through a camera, vehicles, and direction of travel. If safe, record audio or video from inside without revealing your position.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was a prank, scam, mistaken identity, or targeted.
- You do not need to confront anyone, post online, or prove what happened.
- Longer-term steps can wait, including a doorbell camera, lighting, lock changes, speaking to a landlord or building manager, or removing your name from visible buzzers or mailboxes.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel rattled by someone unexpectedly using your name outside your home. Staying behind a locked barrier, keeping information private, and buying time is a sensible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate risk. If this becomes repeated or targeted, later decisions may need specialist help from police, building management, housing support, or a personal safety service.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional security, legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you believe someone is trying to enter your home or you are in immediate danger, prioritise staying behind a locked barrier and getting urgent help.
