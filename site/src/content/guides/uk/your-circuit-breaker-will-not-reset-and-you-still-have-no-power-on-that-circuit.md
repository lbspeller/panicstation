# What to do if… your circuit breaker will not reset and you still have no power on that circuit (UK)

## Short answer
Stop attempting to reset the breaker if it trips immediately after one attempt; leave the circuit switched off and isolate all appliances on that line to prevent fire or equipment damage.

## Do not do these things
- Don't keep flicking the switch back to "ON" repeatedly if it keeps clicking back to "OFF".
- Don't use force to hold the switch in the "ON" position.
- Don't attempt to open the main cover of the consumer unit or touch any internal wiring.
- Don't use any appliance that was running when the circuit first tripped until it has been professionally checked.
- Don't ignore the smell of burning, ozone, or any buzzing and crackling sounds.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate fire or arcing hazards.** If you see smoke, sparks, or smell burning plastic, move everyone out of the room and call **999** immediately; if it is safe to do so without touching metal parts, turn off the main red switch on the consumer unit.
2. **Verify if the issue is restricted to your property or a wider power cut.** Look at street lights or check if your neighbours have power. 
   - If the whole area is off, call **105** (England, Scotland, Wales) or **03457 643643** (Northern Ireland) to report a power cut.
3. **Unplug every device and turn off every light on the affected circuit.** Move through the rooms that have no power and physically pull the plugs out of the sockets.
   - Switch off fixed appliances like ovens, hobs, or electric showers at their individual isolation switches (usually a large red wall switch).
4. **Identify which specific device has tripped in the consumer unit.** Look for a switch that is in the "OFF" position or "down" position compared to the others. 
   - Note if it is an **MCB** (individual circuit), an **RCD** (protecting multiple circuits), or an **RCBO** (combined protection).
5. **Attempt a single safe reset of the device.** Push the switch fully down to the "OFF" position first to reset the internal mechanism, then push it firmly up to the "ON" position.
   - If it trips again immediately, **stop** and leave it in the "OFF" position.
6. **Perform a systematic check if an RCD has tripped.** Turn all individual circuit switches (MCBs) to "OFF", reset the RCD to "ON", and then flip the MCBs back on one by one. 
   - The specific MCB that causes the RCD to trip again is your fault location; leave that one "OFF" and reset the others.
7. **Contact a qualified electrician or your landlord to report the fault.**
   - If you rent, your landlord is legally required under 2026 regulations to ensure the electrical installation is safe and should provide you with a copy of the most recent Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR).
   - If you own your home, find a registered electrician via a trade body such as NICEIC, NAPIT, or SELECT (in Scotland).

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact point of the short circuit or fault yourself.
- You do not need to replace the circuit breaker or any fuses today.
- You do not need to move heavy furniture to access hidden sockets unless you suspect water ingress behind them.
- You do not need to decide on a full rewiring of the property immediately.

## Important reassurance
A circuit breaker that refuses to reset is a safety feature functioning correctly to prevent a fire or a fatal electric shock. By leaving the circuit switched off, you have already significantly reduced the immediate risk to your home. It is normal to feel frustrated by the loss of power, but priority must remain on electrical isolation until a professional arrives.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a tripped electrical circuit in a UK domestic setting. It does not cover industrial 3-phase systems or the repair of internal wiring.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute professional electrical advice. Under the Building Regulations (Part P in England and Wales), most electrical work in dwellings must be carried out by a competent person. If you are unsure of the safety of any electrical component, do not touch it and wait for a qualified electrician.
