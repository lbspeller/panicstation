# What to do if… your home or workplace charger will not deliver power and you need to leave soon (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the suspected faulty equipment immediately, switch to a known-safe alternative power source such as a different wall socket or a power bank, and enable battery-saver settings on your device to preserve remaining power.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue to use a charger, cable, or socket that is hot to the touch, smells of burning plastic, or shows signs of scorching or discolouration.
- Do not attempt to "wiggle" a plug in a loose socket to establish a connection as this can cause electrical arcing and fire.
- Do not touch any electrical equipment with wet hands or if there is liquid spilled nearby.
- Do not attempt to reset a tripped RCD or circuit breaker more than once if it trips again immediately.
- Do not open or attempt to repair a moulded plug or the internal components of a charger.
- Do not leave a device charging unattended if you have noticed any unusual sounds, such as buzzing, popping, or crackling.

## What to do now
1. **Perform a rapid visual safety check and disconnect the power.** Unplug the charger from both the wall socket and your device. Examine the plug pins, the socket faceplate, and the length of the cable for any signs of physical damage, black soot, brown scorch marks, or exposed internal wires.
2. **Switch to a different power source and cable combination immediately.**
   - Connect your device to a different wall socket that is not on the same extension lead or circuit if possible.
   - Use a known-working USB cable, as internal wire fractures are the most frequent cause of charging failure.
   - Use a different manufacturer-approved charging adapter if one is available.
3. **Inspect the device charging port for physical obstructions.** Check the port on your phone or laptop for pocket lint, dust, or debris that may prevent the cable from seating fully. If debris is visible, use a non-conductive, non-metal tool such as a wooden toothpick to gently remove it.
4. **Check the consumer unit if multiple sockets in the area have no power.** If you are at home, locate your fuse box and check if an RCD or circuit breaker has switched to the "OFF" position.
   - Reset the switch once.
   - If the switch trips back to "OFF" immediately, leave it off and unplug all devices on that circuit.
5. **Follow workplace safety reporting procedures if the failure occurs at work.** Stop using the affected socket or equipment and move your workstation to a functional area. Inform the facilities management, IT department, or health and safety officer according to your company policy to ensure the fault is logged and the equipment is professionally inspected.
6. **Apply power-preservation settings to your device to extend remaining battery life.**
   - Enable "Low Power Mode" or "Battery Saver" in your device settings.
   - Reduce screen brightness to the lowest usable level.
   - Disable high-drain features including Bluetooth, GPS, and Wi-Fi hotspots.
   - Close all applications that are not essential for your upcoming departure.
7. **Transition to a mobile backup charging plan.**
   - Utilize a portable power bank if you have one that is already charged.
   - Use a vehicle-based USB port or 12V adapter once you have left the building.
   - Locate a public charging station or "power-up" point at your destination or en route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine whether the fault lies in the internal wiring or the specific appliance right now.
- You do not need to replace the fuse in a plug while you are in a rush to leave.
- You do not need to purchase a new charger immediately if you have enough power to reach your destination.

## Important reassurance
Electrical components such as charging cables and wall sockets frequently fail due to wear and tear. Finding a dead socket or a broken lead is a common occurrence and does not necessarily indicate a wider electrical emergency unless you see active smoke or fire. Taking a moment to switch to a backup source is the most effective way to manage the situation safely.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate power delivery failure. It does not cover the repair of electrical installations or the long-term diagnosis of battery health issues.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional electrical advice. If you encounter scorching, persistent burning smells, or repeated circuit trips, you must stop using the electrical system and consult a qualified electrician registered with a body such as NICEIC or NAPIT. In a workplace, always defer to the designated competent person for electrical safety.
