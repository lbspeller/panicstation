# What to do if… you develop new severe thirst and frequent urination with unexpected weight loss (UK)

## Short answer
Seek an urgent same-day medical assessment to check for dangerously high blood sugar. If you feel very unwell, have "fruity" breath, or are vomiting, call 999 or go to A&E immediately.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not drive yourself to a GP or hospital** if you feel drowsy, confused, or faint.
- **Do not wait for a routine appointment** if you have the "4Ts" (Toilet, Thirsty, Tired, Thinner) as symptoms can escalate to an emergency within hours.
- **Do not restrict fluid intake** to try and stop frequent urination; this can lead to life-threatening dehydration.
- **Do not attempt to self-diagnose** using someone else's medication or by following an "elimination diet" before being seen.
- **Do not perform vigorous exercise** while experiencing these symptoms, as it can worsen metabolic stress.

## What to do now
1. **Check for signs of Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) right now.** **Call 999 or go to A&E immediately if you or the person affected has:**
   - vomiting or inability to keep fluids down
   - stomach pain or rapid, shallow breathing
   - breath that smells like "pear drops," fruit, or nail polish remover
   - extreme sleepiness, confusion, or difficulty staying awake.
2. **Contact your GP surgery immediately for an urgent same-day appointment.** **Tell the receptionist clearly: "I have new-onset severe thirst, frequent urination, and weight loss, and I need an urgent finger-prick glucose test."**
   - In the UK, these symptoms (the "4Ts") are prioritised for immediate clinical assessment.
   - If your GP surgery is closed, call NHS 111 or use NHS 111 online.
3. **Arrange for someone else to drive you or accompany you to the appointment.** **Do not travel alone if you are feeling dizzy, blurred vision, or weak.**
4. **Prepare a brief summary of your symptoms for the clinician.** **Write down or note on your phone:**
   - when the thirst and urination started to increase
   - roughly how much weight you have lost and over what period
   - if you are waking up during the night to pee (and how many times)
   - any other symptoms like blurred vision, thrush, or sores that won't heal.
5. **Keep sipping water or sugar-free fluids regularly while waiting for your assessment.** **Avoid sugary drinks, fruit juices, or full-sugar sodas, as these will raise blood sugar levels further.**
6. **If you have access to a home glucose or ketone meter, you may use it while waiting for professional help.** **Note the reading to tell the doctor, but do not delay seeking professional care regardless of the result.**
   - A blood ketone reading over 3.0 mmol/L or a urine ketone result of 2+ requires immediate 999/A&E attendance.

## What can wait
- **You do not need to identify the "type" of diabetes right now;** the clinical priority is stabilising high blood sugar.
- **You do not need to buy specialized diabetic foods or equipment** until instructed by a medical team.
- **You do not need to notify the DVLA immediately** until a diagnosis is confirmed and your treatment plan (e.g., insulin) is decided.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed or frightened when symptoms appear quickly. These "4Ts" are well-recognised by UK medical professionals, and seeking help today is the most effective way to prevent the situation from worsening. Once blood sugar is managed, many people find their energy and thirst levels improve rapidly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and triage instructions for the first few hours of experiencing acute symptoms. It is not a substitute for professional diagnosis or long-term management.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute medical advice or diagnosis. If symptoms are rapidly worsening or you are concerned about a child, contact emergency services without delay.
