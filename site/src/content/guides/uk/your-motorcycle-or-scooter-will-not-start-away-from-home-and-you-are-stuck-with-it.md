# What to do if… your motorcycle or scooter will not start away from home and you are stuck with it (UK)

## Short answer
Move yourself and the motorcycle to a place of relative safety away from traffic, then contact National Highways (0300 123 5000) or emergency services (999) if you are causing a hazard or stuck in a live lane. Do not attempt roadside repairs in high-risk areas like motorways or dual carriageways.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to fix the motorcycle if you are positioned in or near active traffic.
- Do not stand in the road to direct traffic or wave at other drivers.
- Do not use a warning triangle if you are on a motorway.
- Do not remove your helmet or protective clothing until you are behind a safety barrier or in a secure location.
- Do not attempt to push the motorcycle across live lanes of traffic.
- Do not wait on the motorcycle or sit on it while waiting for recovery.
- Do not assume other drivers can see you, even if your lights are on.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise reaching a place of relative safety immediately.** 
   - On a motorway or A-road, aim for the nearest exit, service station, or Emergency Area (EA).
   - If you cannot reach an exit, move to the hard shoulder (if available) or the furthest left point of the verge.
   - If you are on a smart motorway with no hard shoulder, look for the orange SOS signs and stop in the Emergency Area (EA).
2. **Increase your visibility to other road users.** 
   - Switch on your hazard warning lights if your motorcycle is equipped with them.
   - Keep your sidelights on if it is dark or visibility is poor.
   - Put on high-visibility or reflective gear if it is immediately accessible.
3. **Position yourself safely away from the carriageway.** 
   - Exit the road and move behind a safety barrier if one is present.
   - Stand well away from the motorcycle and the road, ideally on higher ground.
   - Wait in a position where you are facing oncoming traffic so you can see approaching hazards.
4. **Report your location and the hazard to authorities.** 
   - If you are in an Emergency Area (EA), use the orange SOS telephone to contact National Highways directly; this automatically provides your exact location.
   - If you are in a live lane and cannot move, or if you feel in immediate danger, call **999** immediately.
   - If you are in a safe spot on a motorway but need assistance, call National Highways on **0300 123 5000**.
5. **Provide precise location details to help services find you.** 
   - Note the road number (e.g., M1, A14) and direction of travel (e.g., Northbound).
   - Look for marker posts or driver location signs along the edge of the road, which show the motorway name and a distance number.
   - Identify the nearest junction number or service station you passed.
6. **Contact your breakdown recovery provider once the situation is reported.** 
   - Tell them if you are a lone rider or in a vulnerable position.
   - Confirm they have equipment suitable for recovering a motorcycle or scooter.
7. **Perform only safe, minimal checks if you are in a completely secure location.** 
   - Check the kill switch position.
   - Ensure the side-stand is fully up and the bike is in neutral.
   - Verify there is fuel in the tank.
   - Stop immediately if the bike does not start after two attempts to avoid draining the battery.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure right now.
- You do not need to arrange a permanent repair or garage booking while at the roadside.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of recovery until you are in a safe, off-road location.
- You do not need to notify your insurance company until you and the bike are secure.

## Important reassurance
Breaking down is a standard road risk that emergency services and recovery operators handle daily. Once you have moved to safety and reported your location, the primary danger is managed. National Highways can use "Red X" signs to close lanes and protect your position once they are notified.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and safety protocols for motorcycle breakdowns in the UK. It does not provide mechanical repair instructions or long-term transport planning.

## Important note
Motorway and high-speed road environments are inherently dangerous for stationary vehicles. Always prioritise your physical safety over the security of the motorcycle. If you are unable to exit the vehicle or reach a safety barrier, call 999 immediately. Mechanical checks should only be performed in a place of relative safety, such as a car park or wide lay-by.
