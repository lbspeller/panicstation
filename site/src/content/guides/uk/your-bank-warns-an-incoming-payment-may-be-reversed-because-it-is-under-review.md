# What to do if… your bank warns an incoming payment may be reversed because it is under review (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the funds as non-final by not spending the money, not shipping goods, and not initiating a manual refund. Contact your bank directly through their verified number to confirm the specific conditions of the hold and whether the credit can be recalled.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send a manual "refund" to the sender or any other account via bank transfer, card, or cryptocurrency.
- Don't ship items, provide digital keys, or perform services until the bank confirms the credit is irrevocable.
- Don't use the funds to pay bills or transfer the money to a different account to secure it.
- Don't follow links or call numbers provided in the payment reference or in messages from the sender.
- Don't share your online banking login, one-time passcodes, or full card details with anyone claiming to be from the bank or the sender's bank.

## What to do now
1. **Stop any physical or digital delivery associated with this transaction.** If you are selling an item or service, pause all fulfillment immediately until your bank provides written or verbal confirmation that the funds are final and cannot be reversed by the sender.
2. **Isolate the credited amount within your account to prevent accidental spending.** Leave the specific sum untouched and, if possible, maintain a separate balance to ensure you do not drop into an unarranged overdraft if the bank removes the credit.
3. **Contact your bank using the number on the back of your card or the official app.** Ask the representative to identify the payment type (such as Faster Payments, BACS, or a card refund) and confirm if there is an active "clawback" or "recall" request from the sending institution.
4. **Request a clear list of evidence the bank requires to clear the review.** Ask if you need to provide a sale listing, invoice, or communication log to prove the legitimacy of the payment, and obtain a reference number for the call.
5. **Provide a single, non-negotiable response to the sender if they contact you.** Inform them that the payment is under regulatory review by your bank and that you cannot take any action until the review is complete; then cease further communication.
6. **Report any suspicious messages to the 7726 text reporting service.** If the notification of the payment or the "review" arrived via SMS with a link, forward the message to 7726 to alert your mobile provider to a potential smishing attempt.
7. **File a report with the "Report Fraud" service if you suspect a scam.** If you believe the payment is part of an overpayment scam or you have already sent money out, use the reportfraud.police.uk portal or call 0300 123 2040 (England, Wales, and Northern Ireland) or 101 (Scotland).
8. **Keep a log of all interactions and timestamps.** Save screenshots of the bank's warning, any messages from the sender, and the details of your calls to the bank including the agent's name and the time.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to cancel the order or block the buyer permanently.
- Resolving disputes about "late delivery" or "non-payment" with the sender.
- Calculating potential losses or seeking legal remedies for a failed sale.
- Raising a formal complaint against the bank for the delay.

## Important reassurance
Bank reviews for incoming payments are a standard security measure triggered by automated compliance systems and do not necessarily mean you have committed an error. Pausing the transaction is a protective step used to ensure you do not lose both your goods and your money if the original payment was unauthorised.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization steps for UK bank account holders receiving a reversal or review warning. It does not cover long-term debt recovery, Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) claims, or technical banking protocols.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Banking procedures regarding "Payment Services Regulations" and "safeguarding" are subject to individual bank policies and the specific type of transfer used.
