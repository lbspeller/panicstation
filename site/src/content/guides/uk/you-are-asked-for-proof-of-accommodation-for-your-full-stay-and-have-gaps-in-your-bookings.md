# What to do if… you are asked for proof of accommodation for your full stay and have gaps in your bookings (UK)

## Short answer
Do not invent or alter anything. Put your real accommodation plan into a simple date-by-date list, gather the confirmations you already have, and fill each gap with truthful evidence such as a real booking or a clear host confirmation.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t fake bookings, edit confirmations, or invent addresses.
- Don’t book something non-refundable in a panic unless you are sure you can use it or afford to lose it.
- Don’t change your story to match what you think the officer or airline wants to hear.
- Don’t hand over more phone access or personal information than is needed; show the specific confirmation, message, or document while following lawful instructions.
- Don’t argue about whether the question is fair. Focus on showing a clear, truthful plan.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the calmest available pause.** Step aside if you can, open a note, and title it: **Accommodation plan: dates and addresses**.
2. **Make a date-by-date list for every night of the trip.** For each night, write the city or town, the accommodation name or host name, and the address if you have it. Mark any missing nights as **gap**.
3. **Collect what you already have in one place.** Use booking emails, hotel app confirmations, host messages, onward or return travel, and any destination entry documents you already know you need.
4. **Add evidence for each gap, using only what is true.**
   - If you are going to stay in paid accommodation, make a real booking for the missing dates. Prefer a cancellable option if your plans may change.
   - If you are staying with someone, ask them for a short message confirming your name, the dates, their address, and that they expect you.
   - If you are moving around, write your next realistic stops and where you expect to stay, then show that you have enough money to book accommodation.
5. **Keep financial proof limited.** If you need to show funds, show only what is needed and cover account numbers or unrelated transactions where possible.
6. **If this is at airline check-in, ask what exact evidence they need.** Ask whether a booking confirmation, host address, return or onward ticket, or proof of funds would satisfy the check. If the answer is unclear, calmly ask for a supervisor.
7. **If this is at destination border control, answer only the question asked.** Show your date list and the supporting evidence. Say plainly which nights were already booked and what you have now done to cover the gaps.
8. **If you are a British national outside the UK and you are detained, refused entry, or urgently need consular help, contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate, or call the FCDO on +44 (0)20 7008 5000.** If you are not a British national, contact the embassy or consulate of your nationality. Consular staff generally cannot override a border decision, but they may be able to explain what support is available.

## What can wait
- You do not need to perfect the whole trip right now.
- You do not need to choose the best hotel, only a real place you can explain and afford.
- You do not need a long story about why your plans changed.
- You do not need to decide the rest of your route beyond making the missing nights understandable and verifiable.

## Important reassurance
Gaps in accommodation bookings can happen, especially with flexible travel. The immediate task is not to look perfect; it is to be truthful, organised, and able to show where you will sleep and how you will pay for it.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Entry rules and airline checks vary by destination, route, passport, visa status, and individual circumstances, so later decisions may need help from the airline, destination authorities, an immigration adviser, or the relevant consulate.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Always give truthful information and follow lawful instructions from local officials.
