# What to do if… your vehicle starts vibrating strongly while you are driving (UK)

## Short answer
Treat strong new vibration as a possible safety fault. Put your hazard lights on, get to a place of relative safety if you can, and do not continue normal driving if a tyre, wheel, brake or suspension problem is possible or you are unsure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rejoin traffic to “see if it goes away” or drive at the speed where it starts vibrating.
- Do not stand on the traffic side of the vehicle or crouch close to live lanes.
- Do not crawl under the vehicle at the roadside.
- Do not touch wheels or brakes if they may be hot.
- Do not attempt repairs on a motorway, including changing a wheel.
- Do not put a warning triangle out on a motorway.
- Do not keep driving if you can see a tyre bulge, exposed cords, a split tyre, missing wheel nuts or bolts, smoke, leaking fluid, or a wheel that looks out of position.

## What to do now
1. Make the stop safer before inspecting anything. Put hazard lights on. If you are on a motorway or other high-speed road, try to leave at the next exit or pull into a service area. If you cannot, use the next safer place available, such as an emergency area or lay-by, with the hard shoulder only if there is no better safe option.

2. Keep people away from traffic. If it is safe to leave the vehicle, exit by the side furthest from traffic and move well away from the carriageway, ideally behind a safety barrier where there is one. If it is not safe to get out, or you are in or very close to a live lane, stay in the vehicle with seatbelts and hazard lights on, call 999, and ask for the police.

3. Do a brief outside-only check if you can do it from a safe position. Look for a visibly low or flat tyre, a tyre bulge or bubble, cuts, exposed cords, rubber debris, missing wheel nuts or bolts, a wheel that looks off-centre, the car leaning on one corner, fresh fluid, smoke, or a burning smell. If you see any of these, assume the vehicle is not safe to continue.

4. Get help rather than continuing. Contact your breakdown provider. On a motorway, if you have stopped in an emergency area, lay-by or on the hard shoulder, use the free emergency telephone if one is provided, or call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 for advice and assistance. If there is immediate danger, smoke, fire, a collision risk, or you cannot get clear of traffic, call 999.

5. Move the vehicle only if staying where you are is more dangerous. If the vehicle still responds normally and you only need to reduce immediate danger, move very slowly only as far as the nearest safer stopping place. Stop again and arrange recovery or inspection; do not resume normal driving just because the vibration seems to have stopped.

6. Share useful details from a safe place. Tell the recovery provider when the vibration started, roughly what speed it happened at, whether it came through the steering wheel or the whole vehicle, whether braking or accelerating changed it, and whether you recently hit a pothole or kerb. Photograph visible tyre or wheel damage only if you can do so without going near traffic.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact cause now.
- You do not need to decide on repair costs, garages, insurance or blame while you are still at the roadside.
- You do not need to prove it is safe by doing another test drive.

## Important reassurance
Stopping to reassess a strong new vibration is the right first move. You are not expected to identify the fault at the roadside; the immediate job is to keep people away from traffic and avoid a possible tyre, wheel, brake or suspension fault becoming worse.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation and avoid dangerous next moves. A proper diagnosis may need a professional inspection of the tyres, wheels, brakes, suspension and underbody.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not mechanical, legal or professional advice. If traffic or the vehicle’s condition creates immediate danger, prioritise personal safety and emergency services.
