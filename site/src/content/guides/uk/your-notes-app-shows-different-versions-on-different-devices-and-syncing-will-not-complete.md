# What to do if… your notes app shows different versions on different devices and syncing will not complete (UK)

## Short answer
Remotely lock or erase the device immediately, then contact your mobile provider to bar both the SIM and the handset to prevent SMS-based account resets.

## Do not do these things
- Don't attempt to track or confront the thief personally or arrange a meeting to retrieve the device.
- Don't click links in text messages or emails claiming your phone has been found; these are often "smishing" scams designed to steal your passcode.
- Don't use the stolen phone's number for 2-step verification (SMS) until you have a replacement SIM.
- Don't assume biometric locks (Face ID or fingerprint) are sufficient if you suspect the thief observed your passcode.
- Don't delay reporting because you hope the device will be turned in; speed is critical for account protection.

## What to do now
1. **Lock or erase the device remotely using a different computer or tablet.**
   - For iPhone: Sign into iCloud.com/find or use the Find My app on another Apple device to activate "Mark As Lost" or "Erase This Device".
   - For Android: Use Google Find My Device (now rebranding to Find Hub) at google.com/android/find to "Secure Device" or "Erase Device".
   - Select the option to include a contact number or message on the lock screen if you choose not to erase it immediately.

2. **Call your mobile network provider to bar the SIM card and the handset.**
   - Request that the agent "bar the SIM" to stop calls/data and "blacklist the IMEI" to make the handset unusable on all UK networks.
   - Ask the provider to place a "fraud marker" or secondary PIN on your account to prevent unauthorized SIM swaps or porting.

3. **Contact your bank or card issuers immediately to suspend mobile payments.**
   - Call **159** from a secure phone to be connected directly to your bank's fraud department.
   - Specifically ask them to "de-register" the stolen device from your mobile banking app and suspend any digital wallet tokens (Apple Pay / Google Pay).

4. **Secure your primary email account and Apple / Google identity.**
   - Change your email password from a trusted device and select "Sign out of all other sessions" in the security settings.
   - Change your Apple Account or Google Account password and remove the stolen phone from the list of "Trusted Devices".

5. **Update your 2-step verification (MFA) settings.**
   - If you used an authenticator app (like Authy or Google Authenticator) on the stolen phone, use your backup codes to sign in on a new device.
   - Revoke the stolen device's access to generate codes and set up a new MFA method on a secure device.

6. **Obtain your IMEI number for police and insurance records.**
   - If you do not have the box, your mobile provider can provide the 15-digit IMEI number over the phone.
   - Record this number along with the exact time and location the theft occurred.

7. **Report the theft to the police to obtain a Crime Reference Number (CRN).**
   - Call **101** or report online via your local police force's website.
   - If you suspect your data is already being used for fraud in England, Wales, or NI, report it to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or 0300 123 2040.
   - In Scotland, report all fraud matters to **Police Scotland** via **101**.

## What can wait
- Purchasing a replacement handset or upgrading your mobile contract.
- Restoring non-essential apps or social media accounts that do not have payment methods attached.
- Filing a formal insurance claim (the CRN is required first).
- Reviewing old photos or cloud backups.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel vulnerable when a device containing personal data is stolen. By barring the SIM and locking the device remotely, you have closed the most common "front door" to your digital life. Most thieves target the hardware for resale rather than the data, and prompt action significantly reduces the window for account access.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate digital stabilization and harm reduction following a physical theft. It does not provide legal advice for insurance disputes or long-term identity theft recovery.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and may vary based on your specific device model or service provider. If you see active unauthorized transactions, prioritize calling your bank via 159 immediately. Always follow official police instructions if they differ from this guide.
