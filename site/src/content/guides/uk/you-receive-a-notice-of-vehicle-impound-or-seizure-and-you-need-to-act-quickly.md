# What to do if… you receive a notice of vehicle impound or seizure and you need to act quickly (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the authenticity of the notice and contact the designated police pound or recovery operator immediately to confirm the 7-day reclamation deadline and required documents. Failure to act within the statutory timeframe usually results in the vehicle being sold or scrapped after 14 days.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait until the weekend to read the requirements—storage fees are statutory and typically increase every day at midday.
- Don’t pay any fee via a link in a text message or social media DM; legitimate pounds require payment in person or through verified official portals.
- Don’t attend the pound without confirming if you need a "release stamp" or authorisation from a police station first.
- Don’t assume a standard insurance policy is sufficient; many insurers specifically exclude the release of seized vehicles unless stated.
- Don’t attempt to negotiate the statutory fees at the counter; these are set by government regulations and recovery staff cannot change them.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the vehicle's location and the specific reason for seizure.**
   - **Call the number on the official paper notice or 101 if the notice is missing.**
   - **Identify if the seizure is under Section 165A (no insurance/licence), Section 59 (anti-social driving), or for evidence.**

2. **Calculate your 7-day and 14-day deadlines immediately.**
   - **Locate the "Date of Seizure" on your notice.**
   - **Note that you generally have 7 working days to provide all documents and 14 total days before the vehicle is authorized for disposal.**

3. **Verify if you must attend a police station before the pound.**
   - **Ask: "Do I need a police station to verify my documents and issue a release form?"**
   - **Confirm which specific police stations in the force area provide this service, as many have closed or reduced hours.**

4. **Collate your "Release Pack" of original documents.**
   - **Gather the following items:**
      - Valid photo ID (Passport, Driving Licence, or EU National ID).
      - The original Seizure Notice (Form 3708 or equivalent).
      - Proof of ownership (V5C Logbook in your name).
      - A valid certificate of motor insurance that explicitly permits the release of a seized vehicle.
      - A valid MOT certificate (or evidence of a pre-booked MOT appointment at a garage near the pound).

5. **Address missing ownership documents immediately.**
   - **If you do not have the V5C in your name, you must provide the V5C/2 (New Keeper Supplement) and a verifiable Bill of Sale.**
   - **If you have no logbook, contact the pound to ask if they will accept a V62 (Application for a Vehicle Registration Certificate) alongside other proof.**

6. **Check for "Third Party" collection requirements if you cannot drive.**
   - **If the owner cannot attend, they must provide a signed letter of authority and a copy of their ID.**
   - **The person collecting the vehicle must have a valid licence and insurance that covers them for that specific vehicle.**

7. **Pay the statutory charges to stop storage fees.**
   - **Be prepared to pay the removal fee (minimum £150–£192 depending on vehicle weight/condition) and the daily storage fee (typically £20–£26 per day for cars).**
   - **Confirm accepted payment methods (most pounds do not accept American Express or cheques).**

8. **Formally "Disclaim" the vehicle if the costs exceed its value.**
   - **If you do not want the vehicle back, you may be able to sign a disclaimer form at the pound.**
   - **Note that while this stops storage fees and disposal costs, you may still be liable for the initial removal fee.**

9. **Separate the "Release" from the "Dispute."**
   - **Prioritize getting the vehicle out to stop the fees.**
   - **Ask for a "Notice of Formal Representation" or "Appeal" form to contest the seizure or fees after the vehicle is safe.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to prove your innocence of the underlying traffic offence (e.g., speeding or no insurance) to the pound staff; that is a matter for the court or police investigation later.
- You do not need to settle the full legal case today. Reclaiming the vehicle does not count as an admission of guilt.
- You do not need to decide on a solicitor in the first hour, provided you have the documents ready for the pound.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a seizure notice is a high-stress event, and the administrative hurdles can feel overwhelming. The system is designed to be procedural. By focusing only on the document checklist and the 7-day window, you can stabilize the situation and prevent the permanent loss of the vehicle.

## Scope note
This guide covers the first steps for statutory vehicle reclamation in the UK only. It does not cover long-term legal defense for driving offences, court procedures, or claims for damages against the police.

## Important note
Procedures and fees are subject to The Removal, Storage and Disposal of Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Regulations. Always follow the specific instructions printed on your seizure notice over any general guidance. Statutory fees are set by the Home Office and are non-negotiable at the point of recovery.
