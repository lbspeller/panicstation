# What to do if… your security camera shows someone covering, moving, or disabling it outside your home (UK)

## Short answer
Treat camera tampering as a crime in progress and move to a secure interior room immediately. Call **999** if the person is still on the premises or if you suspect an attempted break-in; otherwise, report via **101** or the police website once you are certain the threat has passed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go outside to investigate the camera or confront the individual.
- Do not shout through the door or window, as this reveals your exact location inside.
- Do not open the door under any circumstances to "scare them off."
- Do not post the footage on social media or community groups, as this can alert the suspect and compromise a police investigation.
- Do not edit, crop, or enhance the footage, as UK police require the original, unaltered files for evidence.
- Do not fix or move the camera back until the police have advised you to do so, as the area is a potential crime scene.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate environment.** 
   **Ensure all ground-floor doors and windows are locked and move all occupants to a central, internal room with a lockable door if possible.** Stay away from windows that face the area where the camera was tampered with.
2. **Determine the emergency level.** 
   **Call 999 immediately if the person is still on your property, if you hear sounds of attempted entry, or if the camera was disabled seconds ago.** Call 101 or report online only if you are certain the person has left and there is no immediate danger to life or property.
3. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely.** 
   **If you are hiding and need to call 999 from a mobile, dial the number and, if prompted by the automated system, press 55.** This informs the operator that it is a genuine emergency and puts you through to the police without you having to speak.
4. **Activate remote deterrents only.** 
   **If your security system allows you to trigger an alarm, siren, or external lights via an app, do so from your safe position.** Do not move toward the door to flip manual light switches.
5. **Monitor other views if available.** 
   **Use your phone to check other camera feeds or smart doorbell views to see if the individual has moved to a different part of the property.** Do not look out of windows to do this.
6. **Preserve the original evidence.** 
   **Download the raw video files from your cloud storage or DVR immediately and note the exact time the alert was triggered.** Keep the original files on the device; do not delete them after downloading.
7. **Contact the building manager in shared housing.** 
   **If you live in a flat or managed complex, alert the concierge or security team once you have contacted the police.** Ask them to secure any communal CCTV footage from the hallways or entrances.
8. **Stay in your safe position until help arrives.** 
   **If the police are dispatched, stay on the line with the operator and do not leave your secure room until they confirm officers are on-site.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to repair, clean, or reposition the camera right now.
- You do not need to check with neighbours to see if they saw anything until the police give the all-clear.
- You do not need to decide on security upgrades or new hardware today.
- You do not need to review hours of past footage; focus only on the current incident.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard physiological response to feel a high state of alert, shaking, or a rapid heart rate when your home security is compromised. These reactions are common during a security breach and do not prevent you from following the basic steps of locking doors and calling for help.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active security alert involving camera tampering. It does not cover long-term home security audits, legal claims for criminal damage, or insurance procedures.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. If you believe a crime is happening now, always contact the emergency services on 999.
