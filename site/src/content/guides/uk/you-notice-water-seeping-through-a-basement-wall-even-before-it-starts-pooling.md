# What to do if… you notice water seeping through a basement wall even before it starts pooling (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the area as a potential electrical and contamination hazard by keeping people and pets away, and only attempt to isolate power or water supplies if you can do so from a completely dry location.

## Do not do these things
- Don't touch electrical sockets, extension leads, or the consumer unit if you must stand on damp or wet surfaces to reach them.
- Don't operate any mains-powered equipment, such as dehumidifiers or pumps, in the affected area until a registered electrician confirms the circuits are safe.
- Don't drill, chisel, or attempt to "open up" the wall while water is actively seeping through.
- Don't ignore unusual smells, gurgling sounds from drains, or toilets failing to flush, as these indicate possible sewage backflow.
- Don't store any items, especially textiles or cardboard, against the damp wall.

## What to do now
1. **Establish a safe perimeter around the affected area.** Ensure children and pets are kept away and assume any damp surface is a slip hazard and a potential path for electrical current if wiring is nearby.
2. **Isolate the electricity supply to the basement if the consumer unit is in a dry area.** If you cannot reach the main switch without entering a damp zone, do not attempt to touch it; instead, call a registered electrician or dial **105** to reach your local network operator for safety guidance.
3. **Check for an internal plumbing leak only if it is safe to access the area.** 
   - Look for visible dripping from overhead pipes, boilers, or appliance connections. 
   - If a pipe is the suspected source and the stopcock is in a dry, safe location, turn off the water supply immediately.
4. **Move vulnerable items away from the seepage area.** Prioritize moving electrical goods and organic materials that may grow mould, but only if you can do so without stepping into wet areas or touching damp walls near sockets.
5. **Slow the spread of moisture across the floor.** Place absorbent towels or pads at the base of the wall to prevent water from travelling toward the centre of the room or toward electrical floor sockets.
6. **Capture visual evidence of the ingress while it is occurring.** Use a phone to take photos or video showing exactly where the moisture is emerging, the extent of the damp patch, and any visible cracks or bubbling paint.
7. **Contact your home insurer to report the ingress.** Ask for their specific requirements regarding emergency mitigation and whether they have a preferred contractor for basement water issues.
8. **Call Floodline on 0345 988 1188 for localized groundwater advice.** They can provide information on whether high water tables in your area are currently affecting properties.
9. **Contact your water company if you suspect a sewage issue.** If the water has a foul smell or is accompanied by drain problems, treat it as a biohazard and seek immediate assistance from the utility provider.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on permanent waterproofing solutions, such as tanking or cavity drain systems, right now.
- You do not need to remove wall finishes or plaster until the area is confirmed safe and the source is identified.
- You do not need to begin a full-scale drying operation until the water entry has stopped and the electrics have been certified.

## Important reassurance
Identifying water ingress before it pools gives you a window of time to prevent more significant damage. Focus on safety and documentation; the physical source and permanent repair are for specialists to handle once the immediate risk is stabilized.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for early water ingress. It does not cover long-term structural repairs, drainage design, or the safe operation of professional drying equipment.

## Important note
This information is for immediate harm prevention and does not constitute professional engineering, electrical, or plumbing advice. If you see bulging walls, smell gas, or see electrical arcing, evacuate the area and call emergency services.
