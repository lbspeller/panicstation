# What to do if… your flight is delayed enough that your planned onward transport may no longer work (UK)

## Short answer
Identify your confirmed estimated arrival time, then contact your onward transport provider immediately to move your booking to a later slot before the "no-show" window closes. If you are on a single through-booking, the airline is responsible for your entire journey; if on separate tickets, you must stabilize the onward leg yourself.

## Do not do these things
- Do not purchase expensive replacement tickets before confirming if your existing ticket can be changed or if the airline will provide a reroute.
- Do not assume a car hire company will hold your vehicle without a phone call; many cancel "no-show" bookings within 30 to 120 minutes of the scheduled time.
- Do not board a different train or coach without staff permission, as you may be charged a full new fare or a penalty.
- Do not accept a flight refund if you still intend to travel, as this ends the airline’s "duty of care" obligation to provide food and hotels.
- Do not discard any physical or digital receipts for refreshments, taxis, or emergency accommodation.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm your new arrival time and flight status.**  
   **Check the airline app or airport screens for the "Estimated Time of Arrival" (ETA) at your final destination, rather than just the departure time.** Take a timestamped screenshot of the delay notice for evidence.

2. **Determine if your onward travel is "protected" or "unprotected."**  
   **If your flight and onward transport (e.g., a connecting flight or specific rail link) are under a single booking reference (PNR), the airline must reroute you and provide care.** If you booked the flight and the train/coach/car hire separately, the onward provider has no legal obligation to help you, and you must manage the change yourself.

3. **Request "Care and Assistance" from the airline once thresholds are met.**  
   **Airlines must provide food, drink, and communication if the delay exceeds specific timeframes based on flight distance.**
   - For flights under 1,500km: 2 hours.
   - For flights between 1,500km and 3,500km: 3 hours.
   - For flights over 3,500km: 4 hours.
   - If the delay goes overnight: Hotel accommodation and transport to/from the airport.

4. **Contact car hire or private transfer providers immediately.**  
   **Call the number on your rental voucher to provide your flight number and ETA to prevent your booking being marked as a "no-show."** Most providers have a grace period of 30–120 minutes; once this passes, they may release the car and retain your payment.

5. **Check National Rail ticket flexibility for missed trains.**  
   **If you have an "Anytime" ticket, you can board any valid train; if you have an "Advance" or "Off-Peak" ticket, you generally cannot use it on a later service without staff authorization.** Go to the airport's railway station help desk and ask for a "discretionary" endorsement of your ticket due to the flight delay.

6. **Identify the "Last Service" of the day for your destination.**  
   **Look up the final train, coach, or bus departure for your route to determine if reaching your destination tonight is physically possible.** If the updated ETA makes the last service impossible, use the airline’s "duty of care" to secure airport accommodation rather than attempting to travel and getting stranded at an intermediate station.

7. **Maintain a contemporaneous evidence folder.**  
   **Save all boarding passes, screenshots of delay reasons (e.g., "technical fault" vs "weather"), and itemized receipts for reasonable expenses.** Avoid "luxury" spending, as airlines only reimburse reasonable costs for food and basic accommodation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to submit a formal compensation claim (UK261) while still at the airport; you have up to six years to do this.
- You do not need to determine "fault" (airline vs. air traffic control) immediately; focus only on the physical transition to your next transport.
- You do not need to worry about National Rail refunds for "Anytime" or "Off-Peak" tickets yet if the service you intended to catch was actually cancelled; however, be aware that for voluntary changes, 2026 rules require refund requests by 23:59 the day before travel.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed when a single delay causes a "domino effect" across your travel plans. Most UK transport hubs have established procedures for "stranded" passengers. By securing your arrival time and communicating with your onward provider now, you prevent the most common cause of total ticket loss.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for missed onward connections following a flight delay in the UK. It does not cover long-term legal claims or insurance litigation.

## Important note
This information is based on UK passenger rights (UK261) and National Rail Conditions of Travel as of 2026. Rights vary depending on the airline's country of origin and the nature of your ticket. Always check the specific terms of your onward travel contract. This is not legal or professional travel advice.
