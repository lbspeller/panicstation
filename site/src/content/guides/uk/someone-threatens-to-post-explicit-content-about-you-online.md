# What to do if… someone threatens to post explicit content about you online (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, stop engaging, and record the threat messages and account details. Do not pay, send more images, or bargain; use platform reports and specialist support.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay, send more images, or offer anything to make them stop.
- Do not keep replying once you have enough details to identify the account and the threat.
- Do not click links, open files, scan QR codes, or install apps they send.
- Do not publicly argue with them or post call-out threads while you are panicked.
- Do not delete the whole conversation straight away if you may want to report it.
- Do not screenshot, record, save, forward, or upload any intimate image if anyone shown may be under 18.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** If you are in immediate danger, being followed, or threatened with violence, call **999**. If you can, move near other people and tell one trusted person what is happening.
2. **Record the threat, then stop engaging.** Screenshot or save the messages that show the threat, username, profile link, dates, times, social media handles named, payment demands, and any deadline. If the threat involves an intimate image and anyone shown may be under 18, do not screenshot, record, save, forward, upload, or share the image itself, even to the police.
3. **Reduce how far they can spread it.**
   - Set affected profiles to private where possible.
   - Restrict tagging, mentions, comments, and direct messages from strangers.
   - Hide phone number, email address, follower lists, and friend lists where the platform allows.
   - Change the password and turn on two-factor authentication if you think an account may be compromised.
   - Sign out of other sessions and remove suspicious connected apps where the platform provides this.
4. **Report the account and messages on the platform.** Use the in-app report route for blackmail, intimate image abuse, harassment, impersonation, or non-consensual sexual content. Save any report confirmation or reference number.
5. **Get specialist help.** Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline if you are an adult in the UK. They can give confidential support about threats to share intimate images and possible removal routes.
6. **Consider a police report, without pressure.** For immediate danger, call **999**. For a non-emergency report, use police online reporting or call **101**. You can also give information anonymously to Crimestoppers on **0800 555 111**. Share the account details, links, messages, usernames, platform names, dates, and any payment details you have.
7. **If you are under 18, or the image was taken when you were under 18, use CEOP.** Report to the CEOP Safety Centre and involve a trusted adult if you safely can. Do not copy, send, upload, or share the intimate image.
8. **Use a blocking tool if it fits your situation.** If you are currently over 18, were 18 or older in the image or video, are the person shown, and you have the file on your device, StopNCII.org can create a digital fingerprint so participating platforms can help detect and block uploads.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to go public.
- You do not need to confront them or prove who they are.
- You do not need to write a perfect message to friends, family, work, or school right now.
- You do not need to search the whole internet for your name while you are panicked.
- You do not need to make long-term legal, reputation, or relationship decisions in this moment.

## Important reassurance
This kind of threat is designed to make you rush, bargain, or feel ashamed. Freezing, shaking, feeling sick, or wanting to obey them can be a normal stress response. You can slow the situation down: record the threat, reduce exposure, report the account, and bring in support.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: immediate safety, containment, reporting options, and support. Later decisions about police contact, legal routes, ongoing safety, or wider reputation issues may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. You are allowed to choose what you do next. These steps do not force a report; immediate danger is different because emergency help may be needed.
