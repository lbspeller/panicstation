# What to do if… someone threatens to end your relationship unless you have sex (UK)

## Short answer
You do not owe sex to keep a relationship. Make space from the pressure, get to a safer pause if you can, and contact someone safe or a specialist helpline before deciding what to do next.

## Do not do these things
- Do not treat their threat as something you have to solve by having sex.
- Do not stay in a private sexual situation to keep explaining yourself if you feel pressured, frightened, frozen, or worn down.
- Do not debate, bargain, or give long reasons if that keeps you trapped in the moment.
- Do not let them block you from leaving, sleeping, calling someone, or using your own device.
- Do not delete messages, call logs, or chats if keeping them is safe and you might want support later.
- Do not use a device, account, or shared location app they may monitor if that could increase your risk.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move away from the sexual situation if you can. Go somewhere with an exit, people nearby, or privacy from them, such as a hallway, outside, a shop, a café, a neighbour, a friend, or a room you can safely leave.
2. **If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical help, call 999.** If you cannot speak from a mobile, stay on the line and press 55 or tap when prompted so the call is not treated as accidental. Speak, whisper, cough, or tap if you safely can.
3. **Use one clear line, then stop arguing.** You can say or message: “I’m not having sex,” “Stop pressuring me,” or “I’m leaving now.” You do not need to prove, justify, or soften your no.
4. **Bring in another person quickly.** Call or message someone you trust and ask them to stay with you, come and get you, phone you, or help you leave the situation. Use a safe device if yours may be watched.
5. **Contact specialist support if you can do so safely.** Rape Crisis England & Wales runs a 24/7 support line for people aged 16+ in England and Wales on 0808 500 2222, with online chat available. Rape Crisis Scotland, Nexus NI, and other local services can help route you to support in other parts of the UK.
6. **If this is part of a pattern of threats, control, or fear, use a domestic abuse helpline.** In England, the National Domestic Abuse Helpline is 0808 2000 247 and is open 24 hours. GOV.UK lists domestic abuse helplines for Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.
7. **If any sexual contact happened and you want medical or support help, contact a sexual assault referral service or SARC-type service.** They can offer medical, practical, and emotional support. They can talk through options, including forensic options, without forcing you to report to police.
8. **If you need urgent non-emergency medical advice, use the NHS urgent-care route for your part of the UK.** This may be NHS 111 where available. If it becomes an emergency, call 999.
9. **Keep only what is safe to keep.** If threats are in writing and it will not put you at more risk, keep the messages or screenshots somewhere they cannot access, or send them to a trusted person.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to report to police.
- You do not need to decide today whether to end the relationship.
- You do not need to label what happened before asking for help.
- You do not need to write a long explanation to them.
- You do not need to meet them in person “to talk” or accept a “last chance” ultimatum.

## Important reassurance
A threat to leave unless you have sex can make it hard to think clearly or say no. Freezing, appeasing, going quiet, or feeling confused are common reactions to pressure. You are allowed to prioritise safety, distance, and support.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to help you stabilise and reduce harm in the next hours or day. Later decisions about reporting, housing, money, work, shared accounts, digital safety, or the relationship may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical help, call 999.
