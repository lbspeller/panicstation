# What to do if… you suspect someone has remote access to your computer because the cursor moves on its own (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all purchase attempts immediately to prevent a permanent fraud ban, then use the platform's official "Request Access" (Apple) or "Verify Identity" (Google) portal to submit required documentation for manual review.

## Do not do these things
- Don't attempt to circumvent the lock by creating a new account on the same device or with the same payment card.
- Don't provide one-time passcodes (OTPs) or recovery keys to anyone claiming to be from "Apple Support" or "Google Support" over the phone or via text.
- Don't use third-party "account unlocking" services found on social media or message boards.
- Don't repeatedly add and remove different credit cards in an attempt to find one that works.
- Don't initiate a bank chargeback for the "suspicious" transaction until you have spoken with the platform support, as this often results in a permanent account termination.

## What to do now
1. **Record the exact error message and code displayed.**
   - Common Apple prompts: "Your Media & Purchases account has been disabled" or "Apple Account locked / not active."
   - Common Google prompts: "Your payment was declined due to an issue with your account" or "Payments profile suspended."

2. **Secure the account credentials and active sessions.**
   - Change the account password via official settings (Settings on iPhone or myaccount.google.com).
   - Review "Trusted Devices" or "Active Devices" and remove any hardware you do not recognize.
   - Confirm that two-factor authentication (2FA) is active and pointed to your current UK mobile number.

3. **Check for "Request Access" or "Verify" links within the alert.**
   - **Apple users:** Tap **Continue** or **Request Access** directly on the lockout notification to start the automated reactivation flow.
   - **Google users:** Visit **payments.google.com**, sign in, and check the **Alerts** (bell icon) for a "Verify Now" or "Account Suspended" notification.

4. **Prepare and upload high-quality proof of identity or payment.**
   - Ensure the name and billing address on your store account exactly match your bank statement or Government ID.
   - If prompted for a bank statement, ensure the last four digits of the card and the official bank logo are visible.
   - Note that Google identity verification reviews typically take 1 to 7 working days to complete.

5. **Verify transaction status with your UK bank or card issuer.**
   - Check your banking app for "Pending" transactions or "Fraud Alert" notifications.
   - Confirm if the bank blocked the transaction (a "Bank Decline") or if the platform blocked it (an "Account Lock").
   - Ensure your bank has your current billing address and postcode on file to prevent "AVS" (Address Verification Service) mismatches.

6. **Use browser-based workarounds for essential services.**
   - Access banking, email, or workplace tools through a mobile or desktop web browser instead of the app store version if the app requires an update to function.
   - Use the "managed" app store if using a work-issued device, as IT departments can often bypass personal account locks for business-critical apps.

7. **Contact official support only through verified channels.**
   - Use the **Apple Support app** on another device or the **Google Play Help Center** chat.
   - Provide the specific transaction IDs or order numbers mentioned in the suspicious activity alert.

## What can wait
- You do not need to cancel every active subscription immediately; most platforms will retry the billing once the account is reinstated.
- You do not need to replace the physical SIM card or the mobile device unless you have evidence of a "SIM swap" or hardware-level compromise.
- Reporting to **Report Fraud** (or **101** in Scotland) can wait until you have established whether the "suspicious purchase" was a system error or actual identity theft.

## Important reassurance
Account locks for "suspicious activity" are standard security measures designed to stop unauthorized spending if your details are leaked or if your spending patterns change suddenly. These are usually reversible once you confirm your identity through the official platform channels. A temporary lockout does not necessarily mean your bank account has been drained or your identity has been stolen.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital account lockouts. It does not cover long-term credit repair, legal disputes regarding unauthorized charges, or recovery of permanently banned accounts.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Security protocols for Apple and Google are subject to frequent updates. Always prioritize the instructions provided within the official app or website of the service provider. If you believe your full identity has been compromised, contact your bank and the Report Fraud service (0300 123 2040) for further assistance.
