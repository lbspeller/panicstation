# What to do if… plaster or drywall begins crumbling or falling from a ceiling area without warning (UK)

## Short answer
Evacuate all persons and pets from the room immediately and close the door to contain potential hazardous dust. Treat the area as a structural and inhalation hazard until it is inspected by a qualified professional.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stand underneath the affected area to observe the damage or take photos.
- Do not attempt to catch falling material or "prop up" the ceiling with furniture.
- Do not apply physical pressure to sagging, bulging, or cracked areas, as this can trigger a larger collapse.
- Do not use a household vacuum cleaner to remove dust or debris; this may redistribute fine particulates (including potential asbestos) into the air.
- Do not operate light switches or electrical appliances in the room if there are any signs of moisture, dripping, or burning smells.
- Do not attempt to scrape, sand, or remove damaged Artex or textured coatings, which may contain asbestos.

## What to do now
1. **Move all occupants and animals to a different room immediately.** Ensure no one remains under or near the damaged section of the ceiling.
2. **Isolate the affected room by closing the door.** If you can do so without entering the hazard zone, place a damp towel along the base of the door to prevent the spread of dust.
3. **Assess for immediate emergency triggers.** Call **999** only if:
   - Someone has been physically injured by falling debris.
   - You observe active sparking, fire, or smell electrical burning.
   - The ceiling failure is part of a larger, escalating structural collapse of the building.
4. **Switch off the electricity at the consumer unit (fuse box) if it is safe to do so.** Only do this if you do not have to pass under the damaged ceiling and the consumer unit itself is dry. Focus on the lighting circuit for the affected room.
5. **Report the hazard immediately if you are a tenant.** 
   - Contact your landlord, letting agent, or housing association emergency line.
   - **Inform them that the property has an "emergency hazard" involving structural failure.**
   - Under **Awaab's Law (2025/2026 regulations)**, social landlords and many private landlords are now required to investigate emergency hazards within 24 hours.
6. **Alert neighbours if the damage appears to be caused by a leak from above.** If you live in a flat, contact the resident directly above you to check for overflowing baths, appliances, or burst pipes.
7. **Document the damage from a safe distance.** Use a camera to take photos or video from the doorway once the room is stable. Note any specific observations:
   - The presence of water (dripping, brown staining, or damp smells).
   - Unusual sounds (creaking, cracking, or popping).
   - Visible "sagging" or "bowing" of the ceiling surface.
8. **Contact your local council's Environmental Health department if the landlord fails to respond.** Request an urgent inspection under the Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS) for a "Category 1" structural hazard.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause of the failure yourself.
- You do not need to begin cleaning up the plaster or dust; this should be handled by professionals, especially if the building was constructed before 2000 (potential asbestos risk).
- You do not need to resolve insurance claims or liability questions in the first hour.
- You do not need to move heavy furniture that is already under the debris, provided the room remains vacated.

## Important reassurance
Ceiling failures are often localized and caused by identifiable issues such as slow leaks or aged adhesive. While the event is sudden and distressing, the immediate risk is managed by vacating the room and isolating the power. Following these steps ensures your physical safety while professional help is sought.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction instructions only. It does not replace a professional structural survey, asbestos test, or legal advice regarding housing rights.

## Important note
If you suspect the building is unsafe to inhabit or that a major structural failure is imminent, exit the property entirely and contact your local authority's building control department or emergency services. Asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) in ceilings (such as Artex) are subject to strict UK HSE regulations (2026) regarding disturbance and disposal.
