# What to do if… a dashboard warning says a tyre is overheating and you are stopped in a safe place (UK)

## Short answer
Stay stopped, keep everyone away from the hot wheel area, and do not drive again until the tyre and wheel have cooled and you have checked for obvious danger or got roadside help.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive on from a safe place just to see whether the warning clears.
- Don’t let air out of a hot tyre.
- Don’t make pressure decisions while the tyre is hot.
- Don’t touch the tyre, wheel, or brake area with bare hands.
- Don’t try to cool the tyre or wheel with water.
- Don’t attempt repairs, jacking, or wheel removal at the roadside unless you are fully off the road, away from traffic, and confident it is safe.
- Don’t ignore smoke, flames, a strong burning smell, crackling noises, a visibly distorted tyre, or a tyre that looks flat.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safer.**  
   Switch on hazard lights. Switch the engine off and secure the vehicle so it cannot roll. Keep passengers away from the traffic side.

2. **Move people away from the wheel area.**  
   Keep everyone well away from the affected wheel or wheels. If you are near fast traffic and it is safe to leave the vehicle, wait behind a safety barrier if there is one.

3. **Check for immediate danger without getting close.**  
   Look from a distance for smoke from a wheel arch, flames, a tyre that looks distorted, or a tyre that looks flat.  
   - **If you suspect fire:** move well away from the vehicle and call **999**.

4. **Let the tyre and wheel cool before checking anything.**  
   Do not handle the wheel area while it is hot. For a cold pressure check, wait until the tyres have cooled; this is usually at least **2 hours** after driving, and **3–4 hours** gives a more reliable cold reading after a longer or faster journey.

5. **Once cool, check only what you can check safely.**  
   - Look for bulges, splits, exposed cords, melted or rubbed areas, or obvious damage to the tyre sidewall and tread.  
   - If you have a tyre-pressure gauge, compare the cold pressure with the vehicle manufacturer’s recommended pressure, usually shown on the driver’s door sill, fuel filler flap, or in the handbook. Do not use the maximum number printed on the tyre sidewall as the target pressure.

6. **Choose the safest next move.**  
   - **If there is smoke, fire, visible tyre damage, a repeated warning, a tyre that will not hold pressure, or you are unsure:** do not drive; call your breakdown provider or roadside assistance.  
   - **If a tyre is low once cold and looks undamaged:** inflate it to the vehicle’s recommended pressure only if you can do that safely. If the warning returns, stop again and get roadside help.

7. **If you are on a motorway or in an emergency area.**  
   Use the emergency roadside telephone where provided and follow the operator’s advice before rejoining traffic. If you cannot get to a place of relative safety, cannot leave the vehicle safely, or feel at risk from traffic, call **999** and ask for the police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to choose a repairer, tyre brand, or insurance route at the roadside.
- You do not need to fine-tune all tyre pressures beyond making the vehicle safe.
- You do not need to reset warning systems until the tyre is cool, the pressure is correct, and the vehicle handbook says how to do it.

## Important reassurance
A tyre overheating or tyre-pressure warning is there to make you stop before the situation gets worse. Staying stopped, keeping distance, and checking only once things have cooled are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risks such as burns, fire, tyre failure, and unsafe roadside decisions. A tyre professional, mechanic, or breakdown service may be needed before you return to normal driving.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, medical, or professional advice. If there is smoke or fire, you feel unsafe where you are stopped, or the tyre shows damage once cool, do not drive; prioritise safety and get professional roadside help.
