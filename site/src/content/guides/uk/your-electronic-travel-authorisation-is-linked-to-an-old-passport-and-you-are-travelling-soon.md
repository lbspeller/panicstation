# What to do if… your electronic travel authorisation is linked to an old passport and you are travelling soon (UK)

## Short answer
Act now: if your UK ETA is linked to a different passport from the one you will travel with, you need to apply for a new ETA using your current passport. Check the official status first, then use the official GOV.UK application route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a UK ETA will work just because it has not expired if it was issued against an old passport.
- Do not enter your old passport details into airline check-in to make the ETA appear to match.
- Do not pay another website or app because it says it can get a faster UK ETA decision.
- Do not cancel the whole trip before you have checked the official status and submitted the correct application.
- Do not rely on a screenshot alone at the airport; carriers usually check travel permission electronically against the passport you present.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm exactly what the authorisation is for.**  
   Open the confirmation email or app record and write down the scheme name, reference number, passport number, and expiry date shown.

2. **Check the official UK ETA status.**  
   Use the GOV.UK “Check your ETA” service. You will need the passport used when the ETA was applied for.

3. **If the UK ETA is linked to your old passport, treat it as not usable for your current passport.**  
   GOV.UK says a UK ETA is linked to your passport and you need to apply for a new ETA if your passport has expired or changed.

4. **Apply again using the passport you will travel with.**  
   Use the official GOV.UK ETA route or the UK ETA app. Before submitting, check the passport number, name spelling, date of birth, and nationality against your current passport.

5. **Update your airline or carrier booking to match your current passport.**  
   Change the passport details or Advance Passenger Information in your booking or airline profile. A mismatch between your booking, passport, and ETA can cause check-in problems.

6. **Check transit as well as the final destination.**  
   If you are transiting through the UK, or through another country on the way to the UK, check whether that route requires a travel authorisation linked to the passport you will actually present.

7. **If the authorisation is for another country, use that country’s official status or application route.**  
   For UK travellers, GOV.UK foreign travel advice can help you find entry requirements for your destination and transit countries. Do not rely on a third-party summary when your passport number has changed.

8. **Keep a simple proof bundle for troubleshooting.**  
   Save the new application submission or approval email, reference number, and any official status page showing approval. This does not replace the electronic carrier check, but it can help if a carrier needs to troubleshoot.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about complaints, compensation, or changing the wider itinerary.
- You do not need to fix unrelated accounts or IDs now.
- You do not need to tell everyone about the problem before you have checked the official status and submitted the correct application.

## Important reassurance
A passport renewal can create a document-number mismatch even when the authorisation itself looks current. Finding it before you travel gives you the best chance to fix the passport, authorisation, and carrier details before check-in.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce the risk of a refused check-in or travel document problem. If there are complications, such as dual nationality, a separate visa, a previous refusal, or urgent travel, later decisions may need specialist immigration or travel help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. Requirements depend on your nationality, route, transit points, destination rules, and the carrier’s document checks.
