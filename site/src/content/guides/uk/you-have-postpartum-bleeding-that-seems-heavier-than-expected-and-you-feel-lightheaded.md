# What to do if… you have postpartum bleeding that seems heavier than expected and you feel lightheaded (UK)

## Short answer
Call 999 for an ambulance immediately if you experience sudden, heavy vaginal bleeding or feel faint, dizzy, or lightheaded after giving birth.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive yourself or ask someone to drive you to the hospital; wait for an ambulance.
- Do not stay alone or lock yourself in a room where you cannot be reached.
- Do not use tampons, menstrual cups, or any internal products.
- Do not attempt to walk around or "test" your balance if you feel dizzy.
- Do not wait for your next scheduled midwife appointment or try to "wait and see" if the bleeding slows down.
- Do not take any new medications or painkillers while waiting for help.

## What to do now
1. **Lie down flat on the floor or a firm surface immediately.** This prevents injury if you faint and helps maintain blood flow to your brain. If you have the energy, slightly elevate your legs using a pillow or rolled-up blanket.
2. **Call 999 and request an ambulance.** State clearly that you have recently given birth and are experiencing heavy bleeding and lightheadedness.
   - Mention if you are soaking through a maternity pad in less than an hour.
   - Mention if you are passing clots larger than a 50p coin.
   - Mention if your heart is racing, you feel cold/shaky, or your breathing is fast.
3. **Place your baby in a safe, flat area such as a cot or on the floor.** Do not attempt to hold or carry the baby while you feel faint. If another person is present, have them take the baby into a different room or secure them in a Moses basket.
4. **Identify and prepare your medical records.** Locate your maternity notes (often in a yellow or blue folder) and any discharge paperwork from the hospital. Keep these near you for the paramedics.
5. **Unlock your front door if it is safe and possible to do so.** If you are alone and cannot move without feeling faint, stay on the floor and tell the 999 operator that the door is locked so they can advise the ambulance crew.
6. **Tell the emergency staff about all medications you are taking.** This includes any blood thinners, aspirin, or blood-pressure medication prescribed after your delivery.
7. **Keep any passed clots or heavily soaked pads in a plastic bag.** Only do this if it is within easy reach and does not require you to stand up or move significantly. Paramedics use these to estimate blood loss.

## What can wait
- You do not need to calculate the exact volume of blood lost.
- You do not need to notify your GP or community midwife until after you have been assessed by emergency services.
- You do not need to pack a hospital bag right now; your immediate safety is the priority.
- You do not need to determine if this is "normal" lochia or a medical haemorrhage.

## Important reassurance
Feeling frightened, shaky, or confused is a common physiological response to sudden blood loss and adrenaline. Calling for help is the correct and safest action to take when your recovery does not feel right.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and emergency contact instructions for suspected postpartum haemorrhage. It does not cover clinical diagnosis, surgical procedures, or long-term postnatal recovery plans.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not replace professional medical diagnosis. Postpartum bleeding that causes dizziness or involves soaking pads rapidly requires immediate assessment by qualified clinicians via 111 or 999.
