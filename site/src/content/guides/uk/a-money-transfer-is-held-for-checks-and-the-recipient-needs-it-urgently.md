# What to do if… a money transfer is held for checks and the recipient needs it urgently (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank's fraud or payments team immediately to verify your identity and the purpose of the transfer. Do not send a duplicate payment, as banks may release the original within 72 hours once security checks are satisfied.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send a second "replacement" payment while the first is still pending or held.
- Don't attempt to bypass the hold by moving money through crypto exchanges, gift cards, or different accounts.
- Don't follow links or call numbers provided in a text or email notification about the hold.
- Don't share your card reader codes or app approval prompts with anyone claiming to "fix" the hold over the phone.
- Don't ignore in-app notifications or security prompts, as these are often the primary way to release the funds.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact transaction details for the support agent.**
   - Log into your app or online banking to find the transaction amount, precise timestamp, payee name, and payment reference.
   - Note the specific status shown (e.g., "Pending," "Under Review," or "Held").

2. **Check the app for mandatory security "challenges" or confirmation prompts.**
   - Look for a banner or notification asking "Did you mean to make this payment?" or "Is this a new payee?".
   - Complete these interactive checks within the app only, as this often releases the hold automatically.

3. **Call your bank using the official number on the back of your debit card or via the in-app "Call Us" feature.**
   - If you suspect the hold is related to an unexpected call you just received, hang up and dial **159** to reach your bank safely.
   - Request to speak with the "Payments Specialist Team" or "Fraud Prevention Department."

4. **Provide the specific information required for the 72-hour fraud review window.**
   - **Inform the agent if the payment is for a time-critical expense** like rent or a legal settlement.
   - Be prepared to explain the relationship to the payee and the source of the funds (e.g., "savings for a deposit" or "monthly rent").
   - If the payment is international and over £850, ask if the bank needs further "Funds Transfer Regulation" (FTR) details.

5. **Clarify whether the payment can be cancelled or if it must proceed through the review.**
   - If the bank confirms the payment is "Executed" (sent), it cannot be cancelled via Faster Payments.
   - If the payment is "Pending" (not yet sent), ask the agent if they can stop it so you can use an alternative method, or if they can release it immediately.

6. **Instruct the recipient to check for inbound holds at their own bank.**
   - Provide the recipient with the payment reference and confirmation ID.
   - Ask them to contact their bank to see if the funds are being held on the receiving end for "KYC" (Know Your Customer) or "AML" (Anti-Money Laundering) checks.

7. **Verify the payee details if the status is "Completed" but the money hasn't arrived.**
   - Check for a typo in the Sort Code or Account Number.
   - If a mistake is found, tell your bank: "I need to start the Credit Payment Recovery (CPR) process for a payment made in error."

8. **Coordinate a direct payment to the ultimate biller if possible.**
   - If the recipient needs the money for a specific utility or landlord, check if you can pay the utility provider or landlord directly via their official portal rather than resending cash to the individual.

## What can wait
- You do not need to switch banks or close your account today.
- You do not need to initiate a formal complaint with the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) until the 72-hour review period has passed or the bank has issued a final response.
- You do not need to provide tax documents or long-term financial histories unless specifically requested by the compliance team.

## Important reassurance
Payment holds are a standard regulatory requirement in the UK to prevent fraud and money laundering. A hold does not mean you have done something wrong; it often occurs because the payment is outside your normal spending pattern, to a new payee, or for a large amount. Most security holds are resolved within minutes or hours once the bank has spoken with the account holder.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a held payment. It does not cover long-term dispute resolution, legal claims for damages caused by delays, or the recovery of funds lost to confirmed scams.

## Important note
Under the Payment Services (Amendment) Regulations 2024, UK banks have the legal right to delay payments for up to 72 hours if they have "reasonable grounds" to suspect fraud. This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Specific bank policies and FCA/PSR regulations apply.
