# What to do if… your printer or webcam shows new network settings you did not configure (UK)

## Short answer
Take the printer or webcam offline now: unplug Ethernet, turn off its Wi-Fi, or power it down. Then secure the router login, Wi-Fi password, and any linked camera or printer account before reconnecting it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep using the printer or webcam while it is still connected to your network.
- Don’t approve unexpected remote access, cloud sharing, linking, or pairing prompts.
- Don’t reuse an old password you have used for email, router access, camera accounts, or printer accounts.
- Don’t factory reset the device before taking a few photos or screenshots of what changed, unless you need to stop immediate access.
- Don’t confront someone who may have had physical access to the device or router before you have secured access.
- Don’t assume it is only a glitch if you also see unfamiliar devices, new port forwarding, changed DNS settings, or a guest network you did not create.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause by taking the device offline.**  
   For a printer, unplug Ethernet or disable Wi-Fi on the printer; if you are unsure, power it off. For an external webcam or smart camera, unplug it. For a built-in webcam, disable camera access in settings and cover the lens for now.

2. **Capture what you can see without exploring.**  
   Take photos or screenshots of the Wi-Fi name, IP address, DNS settings, remote access setting, port forwarding message, new user or admin account, device name, and any timestamp shown.

3. **Secure the router from a device you trust.**  
   Log in to your router app or router admin page and:
   - Change the router admin password.
   - Change the Wi-Fi password.
   - Reconnect only devices you recognise.
   - Turn off remote administration if it is on and you do not knowingly use it.
   - If you do not knowingly need UPnP, turn it off after noting any port forwarding rules already listed.

4. **Check the router for unfamiliar changes.**  
   Look for connected devices, DHCP clients, guest networks, DNS settings, port forwarding, and WPS. Note anything unfamiliar before deleting or changing it. Use WPA2, WPA3, or WPA2/WPA3 mixed mode if available; avoid open Wi-Fi or WEP.

5. **Lock down any account linked to the printer or webcam.**  
   Change the password for the device vendor account, camera app, printer app, and the email account used for recovery. Turn on two-step verification if it is offered. Sign out of other sessions if the account gives that option.

6. **Update using official support routes only.**  
   Use the manufacturer’s official app or support page to check for router, printer, camera, hub, or base-station firmware updates. Some updates may need a temporary connection; use a trusted network or direct connection and disconnect again if the settings still look wrong.

7. **Reset and re-add the device only after the router and accounts are secured.**  
   If the printer or webcam still shows settings you did not configure, factory reset that device and set it up again with a new device password. If your router supports it, put smart devices on a guest network after you have changed the main Wi-Fi password.

8. **If this feels like surveillance, stalking, harassment, or domestic abuse, treat it as safety-relevant.**  
   Keep the webcam unplugged or covered. Change passwords from a device the other person cannot access. If someone may be monitoring your device or home network, use a trusted phone, mobile data, or another safe connection to seek help.

9. **Report it if there is clear intrusion, fraud, extortion, stalking, or immediate danger.**  
   If someone is in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999. For non-emergency police contact, call 101. For cyber crime or fraud in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, use Report Fraud. If you live in Scotland, or the incident happened there, contact Police Scotland on 101.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out exactly how the settings changed right now.
- You do not need to replace the printer, webcam, or router today unless you cannot make them safe enough to leave connected.
- You do not need to decide your whole smart-home setup now.
- You do not need to confront anyone while you are still securing access.
- You do not need a full forensic investigation before taking the simple containment steps above.

## Important reassurance
Unexpected network settings can feel invasive, especially on a camera. Taking the device offline, changing router and account passwords, and turning off unnecessary remote access usually gives you a safer pause while you decide what to do next.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce further access. Later decisions, such as deeper investigation, device replacement, police reporting, or specialist cyber support, can wait until the immediate access points are safer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, forensic, technical, security, or other professional advice. If you believe someone is using a camera or network device to monitor, threaten, or control you, prioritise personal safety and use trusted support or police routes.
