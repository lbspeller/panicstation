# What to do if… your cooling system fails during extreme heat and the indoor temperature is rising (UK)

## Short answer
Move to the coolest room immediately and call **999** if anyone shows signs of heatstroke (confusion, seizures, or not improving after 30 minutes of cooling). Use water-based cooling on the skin and keep windows closed if it is hotter outside than inside.

## Do not do these things
- Don't keep windows open during the day if the outside air feels warmer than the indoor air.
- Don't use electric fans if the indoor temperature is **35°C or higher** as they can increase dehydration by circulating hot air.
- Don't aim fans directly at the body for long periods.
- Don't use the oven, hob, or large appliances that generate internal heat.
- Don't attempt to repair complex electrical cooling systems yourself while experiencing heat-related fatigue or dizziness.
- Don't wait for the temperature to drop before assisting vulnerable people.

## What to do now
1. **Identify and move vulnerable people to the coolest available space immediately.**  
   - Priority groups include babies, young children, people over 65, pregnant people, and those with pre-existing heart or lung conditions.
   - Check on anyone taking medications that affect temperature regulation (e.g., diuretics, antipsychotics, or beta-blockers).

2. **Establish one "cool room" and isolate it from the rest of the property.**  
   - Choose a room that is shaded, north-facing, or on the lowest floor.
   - Keep the door to this room closed to retain cooler air and move essential items (water, phone, medicines) there to minimize movement.

3. **Block all direct sunlight from entering the building.**  
   - Close all curtains, blinds, or shutters on windows facing the sun.
   - Hang damp sheets or towels over windows if you do not have adequate blinds to reflect heat and provide evaporative cooling.

4. **Monitor the temperature differential before opening windows.**  
   - Only open windows when the air outside is clearly cooler than the air inside (usually late at night or very early morning).
   - Use "cross-ventilation" by opening windows on opposite sides of the house during these cooler periods to create a breeze.

5. **Apply active cooling to the body rather than the air.**  
   - Sip cool water or rehydration drinks continuously.
   - Apply cool (not ice-cold) water to the skin using a spray bottle, damp sponge, or cool shower.
   - Focus cooling on high-blood-flow areas: the neck, armpits, and groin.

6. **Check the cooling system for simple external issues only.**  
   - **Ensure the thermostat has power and is not displaying a specific error code.**
   - **Check the consumer unit (fuse box) to see if a circuit breaker has tripped, but do not reset it more than once.**
   - **Verify that external vents or condenser units are not blocked by debris or garden growth.**

7. **Determine a "safety exit" threshold.**  
   - If the indoor temperature continues to rise and symptoms of heat exhaustion appear, relocate to a cooler public space such as a library, supermarket, or community "cool space."
   - If you are responsible for a vulnerable person, move them to a cooler environment before they show signs of distress.

8. **Notify the relevant parties to initiate repairs.**  
   - If renting, report the failure to your landlord or agent **in writing** immediately; under 2026 standards, failure to provide a habitable environment during extreme weather may require urgent intervention.
   - If you have home emergency cover, call your provider to request an emergency HVAC technician.

9. **Call 999 immediately for signs of heatstroke.**  
   - **Signs include confusion, lack of coordination, seizures, loss of consciousness, or a temperature over 40°C.**
   - **If someone is unconscious, move them to a cool area and put them in the recovery position while waiting for the ambulance.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the mechanical cause of the system failure right now.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of the repair or potential insurance claims in the heat of the moment.
- You do not need to ventilate the entire house; focus only on the designated cool room.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed or anxious when a home becomes uncomfortably hot; heat naturally impairs cognitive function and increases stress. Focus only on the physical cooling of people and pets in the immediate room; the wider building can be managed later.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate cooling failure. It does not replace professional medical advice or technical HVAC repair procedures.

## Important note
If anyone’s condition does not improve within 30 minutes of active cooling, or if they become confused or lose consciousness, it is a medical emergency. Landlords have a legal duty to ensure properties are fit for habitation, which includes addressing hazards that cause extreme indoor heat.
