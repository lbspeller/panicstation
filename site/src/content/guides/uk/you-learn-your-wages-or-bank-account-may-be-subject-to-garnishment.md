# What to do if… you learn your wages or bank account may be subject to garnishment (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the specific legal order type and jurisdiction immediately to identify applicable protection thresholds and response deadlines. Do not move funds to third parties; instead, apply for a variation or hardship order through the relevant court or enforcement office to protect money for essentials.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore documents labeled "Charge for Payment," "Attachment of Earnings Order," or "Third Party Debt Order" as deadlines for protection are often as short as 8 to 14 days.
- Do not transfer money to friends or family members to avoid a freeze, as this can be viewed as an attempt to defraud creditors and may lead to further legal action.
- Do not quit your job or request "cash in hand" payments to bypass deductions, as this does not resolve the debt and may result in more aggressive enforcement methods.
- Do not provide bank login details or immediate payments to callers claiming to be from a court without first verifying the case through official, independently sourced contact details.
- Do not cancel essential priority bills until you have confirmed exactly how much of your balance is frozen and whether incoming payments will also be blocked.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific order and jurisdiction from the paperwork.**
   **Locate the header of the legal notice to determine which process is active.**
   - **England & Wales:** Look for "Attachment of Earnings Order" (wages) or "Third Party Debt Order" (bank).
   - **Scotland:** Look for "Earnings Arrestment" or "Bank Arrestment" (often following a "Charge for Payment").
   - **Northern Ireland:** Look for notices from the "Enforcement of Judgments Office" (EJO).

2. **Verify the debt and order validity through official channels.**
   **Contact the issuing court or enforcement office using a phone number found on a .gov.uk website, not the number on the letter.**
   - Provide the case reference number to confirm the order is genuine and has been properly served.
   - For Scottish earnings arrestments, confirm you were sent a "Debt Advice and Information Package" (DAIP) at least 12 weeks prior, as this is a legal requirement.

3. **Check for protected minimum amounts that must stay in your account.**
   **Confirm if your jurisdiction automatically protects a portion of your funds.**
   - **Scotland (Bank):** Individual accounts have a "Protected Minimum Balance" (PMB) of £1,000 which cannot be frozen.
   - **Scotland (Wages):** Deductions are calculated using statutory tables that leave a minimum amount of pay untouched based on your pay frequency.
   - **Northern Ireland:** The EJO sets a "Protected Earnings Rate" based on your essential living costs.

4. **Contact your bank’s dedicated legal orders or bereavement team.**
   **Ask specifically for the "Legal Orders" department to understand the scope of a freeze.**
   - Identify the exact amount "ring-fenced" by the order.
   - Confirm if the freeze applies only to the balance at the time of service or if it captures future deposits (typically only the balance at the moment of service is frozen in England/Wales).
   - Verify if joint accounts are affected (in England/Wales, Third Party Debt Orders usually only apply to sole accounts unless the debt is joint).

5. **Request a variation or hardship payment if you cannot afford essentials.**
   **Submit the required court forms immediately to request a reduction in deductions or a release of frozen funds.**
   - **England & Wales (Wages):** Complete Form N56 to request a suspended order or a "Protected Earnings Rate."
   - **England & Wales (Bank):** Apply for a "Hardship Payment Order" using Form N244 if you cannot meet ordinary living expenses.
   - **Northern Ireland:** You must return the Attachment of Earnings form to the EJO within 8 days to provide your financial details.

6. **Confirm payroll details with your employer in writing.**
   **Ask HR or Payroll for a copy of the instruction they received.**
   - Note the start date for deductions and the exact percentage or amount to be taken.
   - Ensure the employer is not deducting more than the statutory limit or the amount specified in the court order.

7. **Contact a regulated free debt advice service and mention "active enforcement."**
   **Inform the adviser that an attachment or arrestment has already started.**
   - Agencies like Citizens Advice or National Debtline can help you draft witness statements for hardship applications.
   - Provide them with your "essentials list" (rent, council tax, food, and utilities) to calculate your required protected income.

## What can wait
- You do not need to negotiate a full settlement of the debt today if you are focusing on immediate survival funds.
- You do not need to decide on long-term debt solutions like bankruptcy or IVAs/Trust Deeds in the first 24 hours.
- You do not need to respond to the original creditor's collection calls while you are dealing directly with the court or enforcement office.

## Important reassurance
Legal enforcement against wages or bank accounts is a structured process with built-in safeguards. While it feels invasive, there are specific legal mechanisms designed to ensure you retain enough money for housing, food, and utilities. Acting within the first few days significantly increases the chance of a court granting a hardship or variation order.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for debt enforcement against wages and bank accounts in the UK. It does not cover bailiff (High Court Enforcement Officer) home visits, vehicle seizure, or business-to-business debt enforcement.

## Important note
Procedures, protected amounts, and court forms vary significantly between England & Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Time limits for court responses are strict and often non-negotiable.
