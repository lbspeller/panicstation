# What to do if… you develop sudden, wave-like flank pain that makes it hard to get comfortable (UK)

## Short answer
Get urgent same-day medical advice now through NHS 111, your GP, or your local urgent NHS advice service. Call 999 or go to A&E now if you are confused, drowsy, faint, very weak, having difficulty speaking, or cannot travel safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to sleep it off if you have fever, shivering, worsening pain, repeated vomiting, blood in your urine, or you have not passed urine all day.
- Do not exceed the label dose of any pain medicine.
- Do not take ibuprofen, naproxen, or another anti-inflammatory if you have been told to avoid them, may have a kidney infection, have kidney disease, are pregnant, take blood-thinning medicine, or have a stomach ulcer, unless a clinician says it is safe for you.
- Do not drink alcohol or take recreational drugs to cope with the pain; they can make dehydration, drowsiness, and assessment harder.
- Do not drive yourself if the pain is distracting, you feel faint, or you might need stronger pain relief; ask someone to take you or call 999 if you cannot travel safely.
- Do not ignore burning when peeing, cloudy or bad-smelling urine, blood in urine, fever, or shivering.

## What to do now
1. **Check for danger signs first.** Call **999** or go to **A&E now** if:
   - you are confused, drowsy, faint, very weak, or having difficulty speaking
   - you feel seriously unwell and cannot travel safely
   - you cannot pass urine and you feel very unwell
2. **If you are not in immediate danger, still seek urgent same-day advice.** Contact **NHS 111**, your **GP**, or your local urgent NHS advice service now if:
   - the pain is severe, hard to control, or comes in waves
   - you have a high temperature, feel hot, cold, sweaty, or shivery
   - you see blood in your urine
   - it burns or stings when you pee
   - you have pain in your side, lower back, groin, or under the ribs
   - you are repeatedly vomiting or cannot keep fluids down
   - you have not passed urine all day
   - you are pregnant, have a single kidney, have kidney disease, or have a weakened immune system
3. **Say the key words clearly when you ask for help.** Use: “Sudden flank or loin pain, coming in waves, and I cannot get comfortable.” Add whether you have fever, shivering, vomiting, blood in urine, burning when peeing, pregnancy possibility, kidney disease, or a previous kidney stone.
4. **Write down three details for triage.**
   - when the pain started and where it travels
   - when you last passed urine, and whether it was painful, bloody, cloudy, or bad-smelling
   - what pain relief you took, including the name and time
5. **Use cautious comfort measures while arranging care.**
   - Sip water if you can keep it down, but do not force large amounts during severe pain or vomiting.
   - If you can take it safely, paracetamol may be used exactly as directed on the packet.
   - Avoid anti-inflammatory painkillers unless you know they are safe for you or a pharmacist, GP, NHS 111, or clinician has advised them.
   - A warm bath or heat pack on the sore side may help some people. Stop if it makes you feel faint, worse, or light-headed.
6. **Prepare for assessment.** Bring your medicines list, allergies, key medical conditions, and any pregnancy information. Be ready to provide a urine sample if asked. Arrange transport because the pain can spike suddenly.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether this is a kidney stone, kidney infection, pulled muscle, appendix problem, or something else.
- You do not need to force lots of fluid while you are in severe pain or vomiting.
- You do not need to plan scans, procedures, prevention, diet changes, or long-term follow-up today.
- The first priority is getting urgent advice, controlling pain safely, and checking for infection or blockage.

## Important reassurance
Wave-like flank or loin pain that makes it hard to get comfortable is a common reason to need urgent assessment. Getting help promptly is a protective step, especially because fever, shivering, vomiting, blood in urine, or trouble passing urine can change how quickly you need care.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Later decisions about tests, imaging, antibiotics, pain relief, procedures, or prevention need assessment by an appropriate clinician.

## Important note
This is general information, not a diagnosis or medical advice. If symptoms are severe, worsening, or you feel unsafe, seek urgent medical care immediately.
