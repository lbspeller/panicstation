# What to do if… you hear buzzing, crackling, or sizzling from your electrical panel or fuse box (UK)

## Short answer
Treat buzzing, sizzling, or crackling from a consumer unit or fuse box as a possible electrical fault. If it is safe, switch off the electricity at the main switch and call a registered electrician urgently; if there is smoke, fire, visible sparking, or a strong burning smell, get everyone out and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the consumer unit or fuse box cover.
- Do not poke, press, or move anything inside it.
- Do not keep flipping breakers or fuses back on to see if the noise stops.
- Do not touch the unit, switches, or nearby metalwork if you see scorching, melting, smoke, sparks, or signs of heat.
- Do not use water on an electrical fire.
- Do not store paper, coats, cleaning products, or other combustible items near the consumer unit, fuse box, meter, or service head.
- Do not ignore the noise just because the lights or sockets still work.

## What to do now
1. From a safe distance, check for immediate danger signs: smoke, flames, visible sparking, melting, scorch marks, obvious heat from nearby, or a strong burning or plastic smell.
2. If any danger sign is present, get everyone out of the property, close doors behind you if you can, and call 999. Say that you suspect an electrical fire or electrical fault.
3. If there is no smoke or fire and the main switch is safe to reach, switch off the electricity at the main switch on the consumer unit or fuse box.
4. If there is more than one main switch, only switch off the ones you can clearly identify and safely reach. Do not remove covers or touch anything damaged, hot-looking, scorched, wet, or sparking.
5. Move away from the unit and keep the area clear. Keep children and pets away from the consumer unit, fuse box, meter area, and any nearby cables.
6. Call a registered electrician urgently. Tell them you heard buzzing, sizzling, or crackling from the consumer unit or fuse box, whether there was any smell, smoke, sparking, scorching, or heat, and whether you switched the main switch off.
7. If the noise, damage, or smell seems to involve the meter, service head, incoming supply cable, outside cables, overhead lines, or street equipment, do not touch it. In England, Scotland, or Wales call 105; in Northern Ireland call NIE Networks on 03457 643 643. If there is fire, smoke, sparks, or immediate danger to life, call 999.
8. Leave the power off until a registered electrician, the network operator, or emergency services says it is safe. If the noise returns at any point, move away and treat it as urgent again.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is a loose connection, faulty breaker, RCD fault, overload, damaged cable, or supply-side fault.
- You do not need to decide today whether the home needs rewiring or a new consumer unit.
- You do not need to test every socket, appliance, or light now.
- You do not need to tidy the cupboard beyond moving away anything combustible if it is safe and quick to do so.
- You do not need to argue with yourself about whether you are overreacting; unusual crackling, sizzling, burning smells, smoke, or sparking near electrical equipment is enough reason to pause and get help.

## Important reassurance
You have noticed a warning sign early, and that is useful. Switching off if safe, keeping away, and getting the right help is a calm and proportionate response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions about repair, replacement, insurance, landlord responsibilities, or wider electrical work may need help from a registered electrician, the network operator, your landlord, your insurer, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not professional electrical, legal, financial, tenancy, insurance, or emergency-service advice. If you feel unsafe at any point, leave the property and call 999.
