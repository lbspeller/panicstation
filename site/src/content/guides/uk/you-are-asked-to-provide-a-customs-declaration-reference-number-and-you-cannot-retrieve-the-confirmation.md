# What to do if… you are asked to provide a customs declaration reference number and you cannot retrieve the confirmation (UK)

## Short answer
Verify exactly which identifier is required (such as an MRN, GMR, or LRN) and search your records for an 18-character string starting with "26GB". Use your tracking or waybill number to contact the carrier's official clearance department to have the reference re-sent.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click payment links in unsolicited texts or emails claiming to be from "HMRC" or a carrier regarding "unpaid customs fees".
- Do not provide your Government Gateway login details to any third-party "customs agent" claiming they need it to find your number.
- Do not send scans of your passport or ID to an unverified email address to "unlock" a customs reference.
- Do not attempt to "guess" a reference number or reuse an MRN from a previous shipment.
- Do not delete your original purchase receipts or shipping labels while the reference is still missing.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of reference number being requested.**  
   **Ask the requester if they require a Movement Reference Number (MRN), a Goods Movement Reference (GMR), a Local Reference Number (LRN), or a postal barcode (CN22/CN23).**  
   - A UK MRN is typically 18 characters long and begins with the year and "GB" (e.g., 26GB...).  
   - A GMR (Goods Movement Reference) is required for some port crossings and starts with "GMR".  

2. **Verify the legitimacy of the request through a known channel.**  
   **Log in directly to the carrier’s official website or app using your tracking number to see if there is an active "action required" flag or "missing information" alert.** If the request came via text or email and does not appear on the official tracking portal, treat it as unverified and do not provide data.

3. **Perform a high-precision search of your digital records.**  
   **Search your email, cloud storage, and browser downloads for the specific strings "MRN", "LRN", "GMR", "CDS", "Customs", "Declaration", and "Reference".**  
   - Look for emails from "HMRC", "Post Office", "Royal Mail", or your specific courier (e.g., DHL, FedEx, UPS).  
   - Check "Spam", "Junk", and "Deleted Items" folders for notifications sent within the last 72 hours.

4. **Request a reference re-issue from the carrier or broker.**  
   **Contact the carrier's customs clearance or "Brokerage" department directly via their official phone number found on their main website.** Provide your tracking number (Air Waybill) and recipient details; they can usually locate the declaration and provide the MRN or GMR if they filed it on your behalf.

5. **Retrieve postal barcodes from your shipping account.**  
   **If you used Royal Mail Click & Drop or the Post Office online tool, log in to your account and navigate to "Order History" or "View Documents" to download a PDF of the customs form.** If you have the physical receipt from a branch, the reference is usually the 13-character barcode number printed on the "Proof of Posting".

6. **Consult your customs agent if the shipment was handled professionally.**  
   **Contact the agent or broker who submitted the declaration and request the "C88" or "CDS Declaration" summary document.** This document contains the MRN in the top right-hand section (Box A) and the LRN provided by the software.

7. **Log the request details to prevent duplication.**  
   **Note the name of the individual or department asking, the time of the request, and any internal "case number" they provided.** This ensures that when you find the reference, you are quoting it to the correct department.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to pay the duty or tax immediately while you are still searching for the reference.
- You do not need to reconstruct the full technical content of the declaration; once you have the MRN, the carrier can access the digital data.
- You do not need to register for a new EORI number unless the carrier confirms your existing one is invalid.

## Important reassurance
It is common for customs references to be separated from the primary tracking number in carrier systems. Legitimate brokers and carriers have procedures to re-link these numbers using your tracking ID or Air Waybill, and losing a digital confirmation does not mean the declaration has vanished from the HMRC system.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and recovery of missing UK customs identifiers only. It does not cover the technical process of filing a new declaration, appealing customs charges, or managing complex commercial import audits.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional customs advice. Requirements for MRN, GMR, or LRN identifiers vary depending on whether goods move via the Goods Vehicle Movement Service (GVMS) or the Customs Declaration Service (CDS). Always use verified government or carrier portals before sharing sensitive documentation or making payments.
