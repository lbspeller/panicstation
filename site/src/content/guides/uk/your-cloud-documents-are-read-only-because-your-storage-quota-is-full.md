# What to do if… your cloud documents are read-only because your storage quota is full (UK)

## Short answer
Protect anything time-critical first: download or export the documents you need today and save them outside the locked cloud account. Then use the provider’s storage breakdown to get back under quota, including the relevant bin, trash, spam, or recently deleted areas, or consider a short-term storage upgrade if you need editing restored quickly.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t mass-delete folders in a panic; you can delete the wrong thing and still not free space if it is sitting in a bin or recently deleted area.
- Don’t permanently empty bin, trash, spam, or recently deleted areas until you have copied anything you may need.
- Don’t keep editing the same file across several devices while sync is failing; you may create conflicting versions.
- Don’t sign out everywhere, factory reset, or reinstall apps as your first move.
- Don’t share passwords or accept help from unknown callers, texts, emails, or pop-ups claiming to be support.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the work you cannot lose today.**
   - Download or export the specific documents you need, such as DOCX, PDF, spreadsheet, or plain text copies.
   - Save them to your device.
   - If possible, save a second copy somewhere separate, such as an external drive, USB stick, or another account you control.

2. **Check whether this is really a quota problem.**
   - Open the storage or usage page for the account that owns the files.
   - Check that you are in the right account, especially if you have both personal and work or school accounts.
   - If only some documents are read-only, check whether the owner or administrator changed your edit access.

3. **Use the provider’s storage breakdown before deleting anything.**
   - Look for the largest files, backups, photos, videos, or emails using the most space.
   - Delete only one or two items you are confident you do not need.
   - If you are unsure, download a copy first, then delete the cloud copy.

4. **Free space where it actually counts.**
   - Empty only the relevant bin, trash, spam, or recently deleted area after copying anything you may need.
   - Check whether the same quota is shared across drive storage, email, photos, backups, or app data.
   - If the biggest usage is email or photos rather than documents, clear space there instead of deleting document folders.

5. **Use the web interface if apps are failing.**
   - Try the provider’s website from a browser, because it may show clearer storage information than a phone or desktop sync app.
   - Avoid making the same change in several places at once.
   - Refresh the storage page after deleting, but allow for the provider’s update delay.

6. **Consider a short-term upgrade only if you need fast access.**
   - A temporary storage upgrade may restore editing or syncing, but it may not show immediately.
   - Keep screenshots of the plan, price, renewal date, and any confirmation email.
   - After access returns, finish the urgent work before doing a wider cleanup.

7. **Pause syncing while you stabilise the account.**
   - Pause sync on devices showing errors until the account is back under quota.
   - If you must keep working, work in the downloaded copy only.
   - When syncing is normal again, compare file names and modified times before replacing any cloud version.

8. **Keep a simple record for support.**
   - Screenshot the quota exceeded or read-only message.
   - Screenshot the storage breakdown and any plan, renewal, payment, or failed billing notice.
   - Write down when you first saw the issue and what changed recently, such as a new backup, photo upload, shared folder, expired plan, or work account policy change.

9. **Use the right support route.**
   - For a work or school account, contact your IT team or administrator; they may control quotas, retention settings, shared folders, and recycle bins.
   - For a personal paid plan, contact the provider’s official billing or support route and share your screenshots and timeline.
   - If the problem involves misuse, insecurity, or mishandling of your personal data, complain to the organisation first; if you are unhappy with the response or they do not respond, you can complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change provider.
- You do not need to reorganise every folder.
- You do not need to set up a perfect backup system right now.
- You do not need to troubleshoot every device before fixing the account quota.
- You only need to protect the urgent documents, restore enough space, and avoid accidental deletion.

## Important reassurance
A read-only message after a storage limit is exceeded does not usually mean the documents have vanished. It often means the provider has stopped new edits, uploads, or syncing until the account is back within its storage limit.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise access and reduce the chance of accidental loss. Later decisions about backups, storage plans, retention settings, complaints, or workplace policies may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, technical, or professional advice. Cloud providers vary, and workplace accounts may have extra rules, retention settings, legal holds, or administrator-controlled bins that change what you can delete and when.
