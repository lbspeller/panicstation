# What to do if… a person near you appears intoxicated or agitated and their behaviour is escalating (UK)

## Short answer
Create distance immediately and move to a staffed or well-lit public area. If you believe violence is imminent or someone is unresponsive, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don't argue, challenge, or attempt to reason with the person while they are agitated.
- Don't make sudden movements or block their path to an exit.
- Don't touch or attempt to restrain them unless necessary for immediate self-defence.
- Don't stare or use your phone to film in a way that draws their attention.
- Don't follow them or stay in the area to see what happens next.
- Don't corner yourself in a confined space like a lift or small hallway.

## What to do now
1. **Move yourself to a safer location immediately.** Step back, turn your body slightly away to avoid a confrontational stance, and walk toward an exit or a populated area.
2. **Put a physical barrier between yourself and the individual.** Use objects such as a table, a parked car, or a shop counter to create a buffer without making it look like a tactical maneuver.
3. **Alert staff or security if you are in a managed space.** Inform a bus driver, train guard, shop assistant, or venue staff clearly that there is an aggressive individual and you require assistance.
4. **Determine the appropriate emergency response based on current behaviour.**
   - **Call 999** if a weapon is visible, violence has started, someone is being physically blocked, or the person is unresponsive.
   - **Call 101** or report online if the situation has de-escalated and you are now in a safe place.
5. **Monitor for medical emergencies if it is safe to observe from a distance.** If the person becomes unconscious or shows signs of alcohol poisoning (seizures, irregular breathing, or pale skin), call 999.
   - If they are unconscious but breathing, and you are not at personal risk, place them in the recovery position on their side.
   - Ensure their head is tilted back slightly so the airway remains clear and fluid can drain from the mouth.
6. **Use the Silent Solution protocol if you cannot speak safely on a 999 call.** Dial 999 and listen for the operator's prompts.
   - **On a mobile, press 55 when prompted** to confirm your emergency is genuine so the call is transferred to the police.
   - **On a landline, stay on the line without hanging up**; if the operator hears background noise suggesting an emergency, they will transfer you to the police automatically.
7. **Coordinate with others if you are not alone.** Give one clear instruction to your companion, such as "Move to the shop entrance now," to ensure you stay together and reach safety.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the cause of the behaviour (alcohol, drugs, or health issues).
- You do not need to decide right now whether you will provide a formal police statement.
- You do not need to "stand your ground" or ensure the person faces consequences immediately.

## Important reassurance
Adrenaline and a fast heartbeat are standard responses when facing unpredictable behaviour. Choosing to walk away or call for help early is a proactive safety measure, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for personal safety in public or semi-public spaces. It does not cover long-term security planning or legal proceedings.

## Important note
This information is for immediate stabilization and is not a substitute for professional emergency services or legal advice. If you are in danger, contact 999.
