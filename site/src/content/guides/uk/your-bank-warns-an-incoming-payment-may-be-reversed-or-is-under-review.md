# What to do if… your bank warns an incoming payment may be reversed or is under review (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the money as not safely yours to use yet: do not spend it, do not ship or release anything, and do not send any “refund” yourself. Contact your bank through a trusted route, such as the number on your card, secure in-app support, or online banking, and ask whether the credit is available, pending, held, or may be returned.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send any money back to the sender or to a “third party” by bank transfer, card, crypto, gift card, or any other route.
- Don’t spend the money just because it is showing in your banking app.
- Don’t move the money to another account to “hide it”, “lock it in”, or make it harder for the bank to review.
- Don’t release goods, keys, passwords, tickets, digital files, or services while the payment is under review.
- Don’t click links or call numbers from the message, payment reference, or sender; use your bank’s official contact routes.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, full login details, card details, or screenshots that reveal sensitive account information.
- Don’t let the sender rush you into a separate payment while your bank is still reviewing the incoming money.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze anything that depends on this payment.**
   - If this is for a sale, do not deliver, hand over the item, post the parcel, transfer tickets, release keys, or provide digital access.
   - If this is for work or an invoice, do not treat the invoice as settled until your bank confirms what the payment status means.
   - If the payer says they overpaid, do not send a separate refund; ask them to speak to their own bank.

2. **Keep the credited amount untouched.**
   - Leave at least the full credited amount in the account.
   - If you can, keep an extra buffer so you do not go overdrawn if the bank removes, returns, or blocks access to the credit.
   - Avoid setting up new payments, withdrawals, or transfers that rely on this money being available.

3. **Contact your bank through a trusted route and get the situation defined.**
   - Ask what type of payment it is, such as bank transfer, card-related credit, refund, cheque, or international transfer.
   - Ask whether the credit is available, pending, held, restricted, subject to return, or still being reviewed.
   - Ask whether the review affects your account, including outgoing payments, cash withdrawals, card use, or transfers.
   - Ask what, if anything, the bank needs from you, such as an invoice, payslip, sale listing, receipt, or message trail showing what the payment was for.
   - Write down the time, date, name of the person you spoke to, and any reference number if provided.

4. **If the sender is contacting you, keep the reply short.**
   - A safe reply is: “My bank has the payment under review. I can’t send anything back. Please speak to your bank.”
   - If they pressure you, change the story, ask for money to be sent to a different account, or ask for crypto or gift cards, stop replying.
   - Keep their messages in case your bank asks for them.

5. **If you were expecting the payment, verify it through a route you already trust.**
   - For salary, invoice, rent, or a sale, contact the payer using an official number or email you already had before this happened.
   - Check whether the payer name, amount, reference, timing, and account details match what you expected.
   - If anything does not match, tell your bank before taking any further action.

6. **If you were not expecting the payment, let the bank handle any return.**
   - Tell your bank you were not expecting the money.
   - Say clearly that you do not want to make a new outgoing payment based on this incoming credit.
   - If the money should go back, ask for it to be handled through the bank’s normal process rather than by you sending a separate transfer.

7. **Save a simple record while it is fresh.**
   - Save screenshots of the bank warning and transaction details, without sharing them publicly.
   - Keep messages, emails, receipts, listings, delivery records, and the payer’s contact details.
   - Note what you have paused, such as a parcel, handover, account access, refund, or service.

8. **If you suspect fraud, or you already sent money out, act quickly.**
   - Contact your bank and say you think this may be a scam or fraudulent payment situation.
   - Ask to speak to the fraud team if the first contact route cannot help.
   - In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, you can report fraud through the national fraud reporting service. In Scotland, report fraud to Police Scotland using 101 or Police Scotland’s reporting routes.
   - If there is immediate danger or a crime is happening now, contact the police using 999.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to continue the sale, job, invoice, or arrangement.
- Deciding whether to block the sender permanently.
- Any goodwill refunds, discounts, replacements, or extra deliveries.
- Complaints or escalation, unless your bank tells you to start one now.
- Working out who is to blame. The immediate goal is to avoid making a second payment or releasing something valuable while the incoming payment is uncertain.

## Important reassurance
A bank review can happen for several reasons, including fraud checks, compliance checks, mistaken payment concerns, or issues on the sender’s side. It does not automatically mean you have done something wrong. Pausing anything irreversible until your bank explains the payment status is a sensible protective step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, aimed at preventing you from being left out of pocket or releasing something valuable while the payment is uncertain. Later decisions, such as complaints, reimbursement claims, disputes with a buyer, or legal recovery, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, fraud investigation, or professional advice. Payment types, bank processes, and account restrictions can differ. Follow your bank’s official instructions and avoid sending any new payment based on an incoming credit your bank says is under review.
