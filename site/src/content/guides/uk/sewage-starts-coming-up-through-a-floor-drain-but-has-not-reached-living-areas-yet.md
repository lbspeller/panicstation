# What to do if… sewage starts coming up through a floor drain but has not reached living areas yet (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using water immediately, keep people and pets away from the drain area, and call your wastewater or sewerage provider if there is any chance it is a public or shared sewer issue. If it is clearly only your private drain, call an emergency drainage contractor; if you are unsure, call the provider first and keep a record.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t flush toilets, run taps, use the dishwasher or washing machine, or drain a bath or shower “one last time”.
- Don’t use chemical drain cleaners on a sewage backup.
- Don’t step into, kneel in, or handle contaminated water unless you must do so to stay safe.
- Don’t let children, pets, or visitors near the drain area.
- Don’t use a household vacuum, mop, pressure washer, or spray hose while sewage is still backing up.
- Don’t use portable fans or air movers that blow air from the affected area into the rest of the home.
- Don’t ignore it because it is “only a little”; backups can surge if water is used.

## What to do now
1. **Block access to the area.** Close internal doors, keep children and pets away, and put a towel at the door gap if that helps slow spread.
2. **Stop all water use in the property.** Tell everyone in the home not to flush, run taps, use appliances, or drain baths or showers. Only turn off the stopcock if you can reach it without going near contaminated water.
3. **Reduce electrical risk only if it is safe.** If sewage is near sockets, plugs, appliances, extension leads, or the consumer unit area, stay out of the wet area. Do not touch wet switches, plugs, or appliances. If you can safely do it from a dry place, switch off power to the affected area at the consumer unit.
4. **Call the right help now.**
   - If neighbours are affected, outside manholes or gullies are overflowing, the lowest drain in the property is backing up, or you are not sure where the blockage is, call your wastewater or sewerage provider and report possible sewer flooding or blockage.
   - If the problem appears clearly limited to your own private drain within your property boundary, call an emergency drainage contractor or plumber.
   - If you rent, contact your landlord, housing association, or letting agent emergency repairs line as well.
5. **Contain spread without touching the drain.** Put old towels, absorbent pads, or disposable material around the drain and at thresholds. Treat anything used for containment as contaminated afterwards.
6. **Move clean items away from the floor.** Lift boxes, shoes, soft furnishings, pet items, laundry, and electrical items away from the affected room if you can do it without stepping into contamination.
7. **Ventilate without spreading contamination.** Open an external window in the affected room if you can reach it safely. Keep the door closed and avoid fans that blow air into other rooms.
8. **Take quick evidence.** Take photos or video of the drain, any visible sewage, nearby items, and the room layout. Keep a short note of times, calls made, and advice given.
9. **Use basic hygiene.** If you touched contaminated water or items, wash hands with warm water and soap. Bag contaminated clothing or towels separately and keep them away from clean areas.

## What can wait
- Deep cleaning, disinfecting, drying, and disposal decisions can wait until the backup has stopped.
- Detailed arguments about who is responsible can wait; make the calls first and keep notes.
- Full insurance paperwork can wait; photos, videos, and a short timeline are enough for now.
- Long-term prevention, surveys, non-return valves, or drainage upgrades can wait.

## Important reassurance
Catching this before it reaches living areas is useful. The most important immediate move is to stop adding water to the system and get the right responder involved before the backup spreads.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, reduce contamination, and buy time. Cleanup standards, drying, repairs, insurance, landlord responsibilities, and drain ownership may need help from a qualified contractor, your provider, your insurer, your landlord or agent, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional plumbing, legal, medical, insurance, or property advice. Sewage can carry harmful germs. If anyone has concerning or worsening symptoms after exposure, such as vomiting, diarrhoea, fever, or worsening skin, eye, or breathing irritation, seek medical advice promptly.
