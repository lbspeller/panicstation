# What to do if… a brake system warning light stays on after you park (UK)

## Short answer
Do not set off again until you have checked that the parking brake is fully released and the warning goes out normally. If the brake warning stays on, especially if it is red, treat it as not safe to drive and arrange breakdown or recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “try your luck” and drive off normally to see if it clears; brake faults can worsen suddenly.
- Do not assume it is fine because the car felt okay when you parked.
- Do not keep pressing or pumping the pedal repeatedly if it feels soft, spongy, unusually low, or is sinking.
- Do not top up brake fluid just to make the light go out; low fluid can indicate a leak or serious wear that still needs urgent attention.
- Do not use hazard warning lights as a substitute for indicating; use them to warn others of an immediate hazard, such as being stopped, obstructing traffic, or moving unusually slowly because of danger.

## What to do now
1. **Stay parked and keep the car secure.** Keep it in Park if it is an automatic, or in gear if it is a manual, and apply the parking brake firmly. Do not start moving until you have done the checks below.
2. **Rule out the simplest cause: the parking brake indicator.** Fully apply the parking brake, then fully release it again. If it has an electronic parking brake, cycle it once. If the warning goes out and stays out, that is a good sign.
3. **Restart once and watch the warning pattern.** Turn the ignition on and start the engine. Warning lights often appear briefly during a self-check and then go out. If the brake warning remains on after the self-check, treat it as a real fault.
4. **Look for signs that mean do not drive.**
   - Fresh wet patches under the car or near a wheel, which may be brake fluid.
   - A strong burning smell, smoke, or unusual heat near a wheel, which may mean a dragging brake.
   - Any message on the display telling you to stop, check brakes, or not continue.
5. **Check pedal feel while still stationary.** With the engine on, press the brake pedal firmly once and hold it. If it feels spongy, goes unusually low, sinks while held, or needs much more force than normal, do not drive.
6. **If you can safely access it, check the brake-fluid level.** If it is clearly below “MIN”, or you cannot check confidently and safely, do not drive; arrange help. Low brake fluid can mean a leak or severe wear.
7. **Use this go or no-go rule, and err on safety.**
   - **Brake warning light stays on, especially red:** do not start a journey; call breakdown or recovery.
   - **ABS light only, with no brake warning, no leaks, no smell, and normal pedal feel:** the main foot brake may still work, but ABS may not. Check the vehicle handbook, take extra care, and arrange inspection before driving far. If you are unsure, choose recovery.
   - **ABS and brake warning together, or any leak, pedal issue, smoke, smell, or stop message:** do not drive.
8. **If you are already somewhere exposed, such as a motorway or fast road, treat it like a breakdown.** If braking feels completely normal and you can move safely, go left and reach a place of relative safety, such as an emergency area, lay-by, hard shoulder, service area, or exit. Switch on hazard warning lights once you are stopped or causing a hazard. If you cannot move safely, cannot exit safely, are in a live lane, or anyone is in immediate danger, keep seatbelts and hazard warning lights on and call 999, asking for the police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact fault now.
- You do not need to choose a garage while you are still stressed; focus on safety and getting assistance first.
- You do not need to keep restarting the car; one restart plus checks is enough for a safe first decision.
- You do not need to decide whether the repair will be expensive before the car has been inspected.

## Important reassurance
Feeling unsure here is normal. Brake warnings are one of the few situations where choosing the cautious option, not driving and arranging help, is often the safest and least stressful path.

## Scope note
This is first-steps-only guidance to prevent a risky next move. A technician may need to inspect for brake-fluid loss, leaks, worn components, parking-brake faults, or brake and ABS system issues.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. Dashboard symbols and warning logic vary by vehicle; if the brake warning persists or anything about braking feels different, it is safest not to drive.
