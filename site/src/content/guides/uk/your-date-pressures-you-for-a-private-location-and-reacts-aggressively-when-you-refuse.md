# What to do if… your date pressures you for a private location and reacts aggressively when you refuse (UK)

## Short answer
Get back into a public, staffed place and end the date. If you feel in immediate danger, or they block, threaten, grab, or follow you, call 999 or 112.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go to a private location to calm them down, avoid awkwardness, or prove you are being fair.
- Don’t get into their car or let them drive you anywhere if you feel uneasy.
- Don’t argue your reasons or negotiate; a repeated “No, I’m leaving” is enough.
- Don’t let them walk you to a quiet place, your car, public transport, or your home if you do not want that.
- Don’t share your address, exact route, live location, or onward travel plans with them.
- Don’t accept another drink or food from them if you are now concerned about your safety.
- Don’t wait alone outside to be polite; move toward staff, security, or other people.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed, public place.**  
   Go to the bar, reception desk, security, ticket desk, or a busy shop next door. If you are outside, go back inside or into the nearest open, well-lit place with staff.

2. **End the date with one clear line.**  
   Say: “I’m not going. I’m leaving now.” Then stop explaining and keep moving toward staff or other people.

3. **Ask staff for direct help.**  
   Say: “I feel unsafe. Please help me leave and keep this person away from me.” If you are in a participating venue, you can also ask for “Angela” to signal that you need discreet help.

4. **Arrange a safe departure without them.**  
   Ask staff to call you a taxi, help you wait inside, or let you wait near staff or security. Leave without the other person. If they try to follow, go straight back to staff and say: “They are following me.”

5. **Call emergency help if the risk is immediate.**  
   Call 999 or 112 if they threaten you, block you, grab you, follow you, or will not let you leave. Say where you are, what they look like, and what they are doing right now.

6. **Use silent 999 only if you cannot speak safely.**  
   If you call 999 from a mobile and cannot speak, listen to the operator. Try to cough, tap, or press 55 if prompted so the call can be put through to police.

7. **Put another person on the line if it is safe.**  
   Call a trusted friend, family member, or housemate and say: “I am leaving a date and I feel unsafe. Stay on the line until I am safely away.” Use speakerphone only if it does not escalate the situation.

8. **If they are close outside, make the situation visible.**  
   Turn back into a public place, stand near other people, and say clearly to staff or security: “I need help. I feel unsafe.”

9. **When you are safely away, save basic details if you can.**  
   Note their name or profile, what they looked like, where you were, the time, and anything threatening they said or did. Screenshot messages in the dating app if doing so feels safe.

10. **Report only when it is safe and if you choose to.**  
   For an emergency, call 999 or 112. For a non-emergency, you can call 101 or use online reporting through your local police force.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to confront them, explain more, or give them another chance.
- Deciding whether to make a formal report, unless there is immediate danger now.
- Writing a perfect message, review, warning, or post.
- Working out exactly how to label what happened.
- Replying to any messages from them.

## Important reassurance
A person escalating after you decline a private location is a safety signal. You do not owe them politeness, privacy, another explanation, or a softer exit.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: getting out of the immediate situation, reaching safer people, and buying time. If this person keeps contacting, following, threatening, or harassing you afterward, later decisions may need specialist support or advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are injured, feel unwell, or have been sexually assaulted or otherwise seriously harmed, seek urgent medical help or specialist support when you are safe.
