# What to do if… you lose your paper boarding pass for a connection and cannot access your booking on your phone (UK)

## Short answer
Stay on the signed Connections, Flight Connections, or Transfers route and go to an official airline transfer desk, assistance desk, gate, or self-service kiosk. Ask for the boarding pass to be reissued using your passport or other accepted ID, flight details, and any itinerary information you still have.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the connections or transfer route to “sort it out” unless airport or airline staff tell you to.
- Don’t go landside or leave the airport unless you know you can pass UK border control and return through security in time.
- Don’t assume you are stuck because your phone will not load.
- Don’t join a random check-in queue before looking for a Connections, Transfer, airline assistance, or self-service option.
- Don’t hand your passport or phone to anyone except official airline, airport, security, or border staff.

## What to do now
1. **Stay on the connection route if you can.** Look for signs saying **Connections**, **Flight Connections**, or **Transfers**, and follow them instead of heading to arrivals or baggage reclaim unless staff direct you there.
2. **Get your next flight details from the departure boards.** Write down or photograph the airline, flight number, destination, departure time, terminal, and gate if shown.
3. **Go to the nearest official airline help point.** Try an airline transfer desk, assistance desk, self-service kiosk, or the gate for your next flight if boarding is soon.
4. **Use a simple sentence at the desk.** Say: “I’m connecting, I’ve lost my paper boarding pass, and I can’t access my booking on my phone. Can you reissue it using my passport or ID?”
5. **If you do not know your booking reference, give identifying details instead.** Give your full name as it appears on your passport, date of travel, route, airline, flight number, and destination. Staff or kiosks may be able to find the booking from those details.
6. **Ask before transfer security if you have no pass.** At some airports, staff may be able to direct you through with an itinerary or flight details so you can collect the boarding pass at an airline desk or machine, but do not assume this.
7. **Mention checked baggage straight away.** Ask whether your bag is checked through to your final destination. If the flights were booked separately, ask whether you need to go to arrivals, collect the bag, and check in again.
8. **Check the new pass before leaving the desk or kiosk.** Check the name, flight number, date, destination, boarding time, terminal, and gate if shown, then go straight back to the connection route or gate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix your phone, app, email, password, roaming, or airport Wi-Fi right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to complain, claim costs, or argue about airline policy while boarding time is close.
- You do not need to make a new travel plan until airline or airport staff confirm whether you can still take the connection.

## Important reassurance
This is a known airport problem, and it is often fixable at an official airline desk, self-service kiosk, assistance desk, or gate. A lost paper pass is not the same as losing your ticket, and staff can tell you the next safe step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you stay on the correct airport route and try to get boarded. If you miss the connection, are rebooked, or have separate tickets, later decisions about rerouting, costs, baggage, or complaints may need airline, travel insurer, airport, or specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, immigration, or professional advice. Airline, airport, security, baggage, and border procedures vary by carrier, airport, route, booking type, and destination, so follow instructions from official staff on site.
