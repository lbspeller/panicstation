# What to do if… your lips or fingertips look bluish and you feel unwell (UK)

## Short answer
If your lips, tongue, face, or skin suddenly look blue or grey and you feel unwell, call 999 for an ambulance now. If it is only your fingers or toes and you otherwise feel stable, get urgent medical advice today through 111 where available, your GP out-of-hours service, or your local urgent care route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait to see if it passes if the blueness is new, spreading, or you feel worse.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, breathless, confused, unusually drowsy, or your lips or face look blue or grey.
- Do not lie flat if that makes breathing harder.
- Do not take someone else’s medication, including leftover antibiotics, sedatives, or strong pain medicines.
- Do not keep yourself in the cold to test whether it is circulation if you already feel unwell.

## What to do now
1. Call 999 now if your lips, tongue, face, or skin suddenly look blue or grey, or if you have difficulty breathing, chest pain, confusion, unusual drowsiness, dizziness, collapse, or symptoms that are getting worse.
2. If it is only your fingers or toes that look bluish, and you are otherwise stable, move somewhere warm and get urgent medical advice today through 111 where available, your GP out-of-hours service, or your local urgent care route.
3. While help is being arranged, try a position that makes breathing easier. Sitting upright or leaning slightly forward may help. Loosen tight clothing around your neck or chest.
4. Tell the call handler or clinician the key details: “My lips or fingertips look bluish and I feel unwell. I am worried about breathing or oxygen.” Mention chest pain, breathing difficulty, confusion, drowsiness, dizziness, fainting, or collapse.
5. Use only your own prescribed rescue treatment if you have one. If you have asthma or COPD and a prescribed reliever inhaler or action plan, follow your own instructions. If you have prescribed home oxygen, use it exactly as prescribed.
6. Make it easier for responders to reach you. Unlock the door if you can, turn on a light, secure pets, and put your medication list or medicine boxes nearby.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether this is “circulation” or “breathing” before getting help.
- You do not need to tidy up, shower, change clothes, or look presentable.
- Work messages, travel plans, and explanations can wait.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel frightened by blue or grey colouring and feeling unwell. Getting urgent help is a protective step, and call handlers are used to helping people who are unsure what is happening.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to keep you safe and get timely assessment. Later decisions may need a clinician or another specialist, depending on what is causing the colour change and how unwell you are.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not medical advice or a diagnosis. If you think you or someone else is seriously unwell, especially with blue or grey lips, breathing difficulty, chest pain, confusion, drowsiness, dizziness, or collapse, seek emergency help immediately.
