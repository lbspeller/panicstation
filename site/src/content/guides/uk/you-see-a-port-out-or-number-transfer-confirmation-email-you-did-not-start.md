# What to do if… you see a “port-out” or “number transfer” confirmation email you did not start (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately disconnect the device from the internet by turning off Wi-Fi or unplugging the Ethernet cable, then shut down the computer to stop active unauthorized control.

## Do not do these things
- Do not type any passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details while the device is connected to the internet.
- Do not try to "talk" to the intruder via on-screen notes or chat windows.
- Do not keep the computer on to "see what they are doing" as they may be downloading data in the background.
- Do not download "cleaner" software recommended by pop-ups or unexpected callers.
- Do not log into sensitive accounts (banking, email, or social media) from the suspected device until it has been professionally wiped or cleared.

## What to do now
1. **Sever the internet connection immediately.**  
   **Turn off the Wi-Fi router, toggle the "Airplane Mode" switch on your laptop, or physically pull out the Ethernet (network) cable from the back of the machine.**
2. **Perform a hard shutdown of the computer.**  
   **If the cursor is actively moving and preventing you from clicking "Shut Down," press and hold the physical power button on the case or keyboard for approximately 10 seconds until the screen goes black.**
3. **Use a separate, known-safe device to secure your accounts.**  
   **Using a different computer or a smartphone (connected to mobile data, not the compromised Wi-Fi if possible), change the passwords for your primary email and banking accounts.**
   - Change your email password first, as it is used for all other password resets.
   - Use the "Sign out of all other sessions" or "Manage devices" option in your account settings to kick the intruder out of your active sessions.
4. **Contact your bank or financial providers.**  
   **If you saw the cursor move toward banking windows or if you have saved payment details in your browser, call your bank's fraud department to place a temporary alert on your accounts.**
5. **Report the incident to the appropriate authorities.**  
   **In England, Wales, and Northern Ireland, contact Report Fraud (the national reporting service) at 0300 123 2040 or via their official website.**
   - In Scotland, call Police Scotland on 101 to report the cybercrime.
6. **Seek professional technical support before turning the device back on.**  
   **Keep the computer offline and take it to a reputable IT professional or, if it is a company device, contact your employer's IT department immediately.**
   - Do not attempt to fix the registry or system files yourself if you are not trained, as this can hide evidence of the breach.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the IP address or geographic location of the intruder right now.
- You do not need to delete your accounts; changing passwords and enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is sufficient for initial stabilization.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to buy a new computer; most software compromises can be resolved by a professional factory reset.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of violation or panic when a device is compromised, but taking the step to disconnect the internet stops the intruder's ability to interact with your machine instantly. Cursor movement can sometimes be caused by hardware glitches (such as a failing trackpad or a low-battery wireless mouse), but treating it as a security event is the safest precaution.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to stop active remote control and protect financial assets. It does not cover deep forensic recovery, malware removal, or long-term identity theft protection.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional technical advice. If you believe you are in immediate physical danger or a crime is currently in progress, contact emergency services by dialing 999.
