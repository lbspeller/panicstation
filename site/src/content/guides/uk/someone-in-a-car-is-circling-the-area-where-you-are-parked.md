# What to do if… someone in a car is circling the area where you are parked (UK)

## Short answer
If you feel unsafe, stay in or get back into your locked car and move to a busy, well-lit place with people, staff, or CCTV if you can do so safely. If you believe you are in immediate danger, or something is happening right now, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t get out to confront the driver or check what they want.
- Don’t unlock your door or lower your window to speak to them.
- Don’t drive to an isolated place to see whether they follow.
- Don’t go straight home if you think you are being watched or followed.
- Don’t stare them down, gesture, shout, or escalate from inside the car.
- Don’t focus on filming or photographing them if it makes you less aware of what is happening.
- Don’t stay in the exact spot just to prove what is going on.

## What to do now
1. **Lock down the car:** keep the doors locked, windows up, keys ready, and phone within reach. If you are going to move, put your seatbelt on before driving.
2. **Check whether you can leave safely:** if your exit is clear, drive away calmly and head for a busy, well-lit, staffed place such as a petrol station, supermarket entrance, hotel drop-off, or 24-hour venue.
3. **If you are blocked in or approached, treat it as urgent:** stay locked in, do not open the door, use your horn or lights to attract attention if needed, and call 999.
4. **Call the right police route:**
   - Call 999 if you are being approached, blocked in, followed closely, threatened, or you feel in immediate danger.
   - Call 101, or report online through Police.uk, if it feels suspicious but not immediately dangerous and you want advice or to log what happened.
   - If you are in danger but cannot speak safely, call 999. On a mobile, press 55 when prompted if you cannot speak; this does not give police your location, so give or share your location if you safely can.
5. **Use nearby help:** if the car park has security, help points, barriers, attendants, or intercoms, use them. If you can get inside a building safely, tell staff or security: “A car keeps circling near where I’m parked; I don’t feel safe walking out alone.”
6. **Let someone trusted know where you are:** call or message them, share live location if available, and stay on the line while you move somewhere safer.
7. **Record only what you can safely notice:** number plate, make, model, colour, direction of travel, time, and anything distinctive. Do not risk getting closer for a better look.
8. **If you think you are being followed after you leave:** do not go home. Stay on main roads and go to a busy, staffed place. Pull over somewhere safe to call 999 if you can; if you cannot stop safely, keep driving safely and call as soon as you can.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide why they are circling right now.
- You do not need to confront them, prove anything, or get a perfect description.
- You do not need to report from the exact place where you first noticed it; getting somewhere safer comes first.
- You do not need to decide tonight whether this was a one-off, a pattern, or something to raise with site management later.

## Important reassurance
Feeling alarmed by repeated drive-bys is a normal protective response. Creating distance, keeping the car locked, and moving toward people, staff, or CCTV is a reasonable way to reduce risk and buy time.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few minutes. Later decisions, such as making a fuller report, asking for CCTV to be preserved, or contacting car park management, can wait until you are somewhere safer and calmer.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal advice or a guarantee of outcomes. If you feel in immediate danger, prioritise getting to a safer location if you can and calling emergency services.
