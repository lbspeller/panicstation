# What to do if… you are contacted by media or people online about a family member’s death and you do not want publicity (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all real-time engagement and request an immediate Privacy Notice from IPSO. Set social media accounts to private and use a single "no comment" statement for all further inquiries.

## Do not do these things
- Do not answer "just one question" or give off-the-record details to build rapport.
- Do not engage in arguments or emotional pleas in public comments or private messages.
- Do not share copies of private documents such as death certificates or home addresses.
- Do not post public updates about the situation, as this often generates more media interest.
- Do not hand over family photos or personal statements until you have had 48 hours to reflect.
- Do not open the door to speak with anyone who identifies themselves as a reporter or researcher.

## What to do now
1. **Appoint a single point of contact for all communication.**
   Select one person to monitor all incoming messages and calls for the next 48 hours so other family members can disconnect. Ensure everyone else in the family forwards messages to this person without replying.

2. **Contact IPSO to request an emergency Privacy Notice.**
   **Call the Independent Press Standards Organisation (IPSO) on 07799 903 929 for their 24-hour emergency harassment service.** They can issue a formal notice to all UK newspaper and magazine editors stating that you do not wish to be contacted, which is highly effective at stopping persistent approaches.

3. **Place a physical "no media" sign on your front door.**
   **Tape a clear note to your door or gate stating that you do not wish to be disturbed by the press and will not be making any comments.** This provides a physical barrier and clear evidence that further approaches constitute harassment.

4. **Send a final "desist" message and block the sender.**
   **Reply once to any digital inquiry with the following text: "The family is grieving and does not wish to be contacted by the media; please remove this number/account from your lists and do not contact us again."** Block the account immediately after sending; do not wait for a response.

5. **Lock down all social media privacy settings immediately.**
   **Set all personal accounts to "Private" and deactivate the ability for strangers to send direct messages or tag you in posts.** Check the settings for the deceased person's accounts if you have access, or use the platform's reporting tools to request "memorialization" to stop public commenting.

6. **Silence unknown callers on all mobile devices.**
   **Go to your phone settings and enable "Silence Unknown Callers" so that any number not in your contacts goes directly to voicemail.** This prevents the shock of answering a reporter's call unexpectedly.

7. **Document every unsolicited contact in a simple log.**
   **Record the time, date, platform, and name of the person or outlet that contacted you, along with a screenshot of the message.** This documentation is required if the behavior continues and you need to report harassment to the police (101) or escalate an IPSO complaint.

8. **Contact the broadcaster directly for TV or radio intrusions.**
   **If the contact is from a TV or radio station, send your "do not contact" statement to the broadcaster's duty office or newsdesk.** If they broadcast footage or audio without consent after being asked to stop, you can later escalate a privacy complaint to Ofcom.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to give a "tribute" or "statement" for several weeks.
- You do not need to correct inaccuracies or rumors circulating online right now.
- You do not need to respond to messages from well-meaning strangers or distant acquaintances.
- You do not need to deal with the permanent removal of online articles until the immediate harassment has stopped.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel exposed and distressed when your private grief becomes the subject of public interest. You have a right to privacy, and setting a firm boundary is not "rude" or "suspicious." Most professional journalists will stop immediately once a formal desist request or IPSO notice is issued.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK residents to stop immediate media harassment. It does not cover legal action for defamation or long-term reputation management.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, harassment is a criminal matter if there are two or more incidents that cause alarm or distress; if you feel physically unsafe or are being threatened, call 999 immediately.
