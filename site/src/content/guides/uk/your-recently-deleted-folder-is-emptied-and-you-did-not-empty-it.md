# What to do if… your “recently deleted” folder is emptied and you did not empty it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop adding new data to the device immediately and verify your cloud accounts via a web browser on a separate machine. Many missing backups are caused by signing into the wrong account or confusing "cloud sync" with a "device backup."

## Do not do these things
- Don’t continue the setup process by downloading large apps or syncing new photo libraries, as this may overwrite traces of recoverable data.
- Don’t repeatedly factory reset the device in an attempt to make a backup appear; this will not fix a missing server-side file.
- Don’t sign out of your cloud accounts on other devices, as those devices may still hold locally cached copies of your data.
- Don’t pay for "data recovery" software that promises 100 percent results for modern encrypted smartphones.
- Don’t delete any existing old backups or "temporary" files in your cloud storage while you are searching.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the device to prevent data overwriting**
   - **Put the device into Airplane Mode or disconnect from Wi-Fi immediately.**
   - **Avoid taking new photos, installing apps, or creating new files on the reset device.**

2. **Verify the account credentials on a different device**
   - **Log into the platform's web interface (e.g., iCloud.com, Google.com, or OneDrive.com) using a computer.**
   - **Check if you have more than one account (e.g., an old Gmail, a work email, or a different Apple Account).**
   - **Verify that the data exists in the "web view" of the service, even if the "Restore from Backup" list was empty.**

3. **Check the Recycle Bin or Recently Deleted folders online**
   - **Navigate to the "Trash" or "Recently Deleted" section of your cloud service.**
     - Apple Account (iCloud): Check the "Recently Deleted" album in Photos and the "Recently Deleted" folder in iCloud Drive.
     - Google Account: Check the Google Photos Trash (items stay for 60 days if backed up) and Google Drive Trash (30 days).
     - Microsoft Account: Check the OneDrive Recycle Bin (30 days for personal; 93 days for work / school).

4. **Address platform-specific restoration requirements**
   - **iPhone / iPad:** Remember that an iCloud Device Backup can only be applied during the initial "Hello" setup screens. If you have already reached the home screen, you must select "Erase All Content and Settings" to try the restore process again.
   - **Android:** Check Google One settings to see if the backup was "Waiting to sync" or if it is tied to a different device listed in your account.
   - **Samsung:** Check if your photos were syncing to the new Samsung Cloud Gallery or the older OneDrive integration, as these services shifted in April 2026.

5. **Utilize "Point-in-Time" recovery for cloud drives**
   - **For Microsoft 365 subscribers, use the "Restore your OneDrive" feature to roll your entire file library back to a state from the last 30 days.**
   - **For Google Drive, check the "Activity" pane on a file's info section to see if previous versions are available.**

6. **Exercise your legal rights under the UK Data (Use and Access) Act 2025**
   - **If you suspect the service provider still has your data but it is not appearing in the UI, you may have the right to submit a Subject Access Request (SAR).**
   - **Contact the provider’s privacy office to request a copy of the data associated with your account.**

7. **Contact managed IT support for work or school devices**
   - **Stop attempting DIY recovery if the device is managed by an organization.**
   - **Ask your IT admin if they have a "litigation hold" or a secondary backup solution like AvePoint or Veeam that can restore your profile.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the data is gone forever; cloud systems sometimes have synchronization delays of up to 24 hours.
- You do not need to inform everyone in your contacts that you lost their number until you have checked your email provider's contact list.
- You do not need to buy a new device or storage plan until you have confirmed if a physical backup exists on a home computer or external drive.

## Important reassurance
Backup systems are technically complex and often rely on specific conditions like being connected to power and Wi-Fi simultaneously. A missing backup file often indicates a configuration error—such as an incorrect account login—rather than a total deletion of your data. Taking a systematic approach to checking your accounts is the most effective way to locate missing files.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover physical hardware repair or the use of forensic data recovery tools. If the missing data is required for legal or professional purposes, contact a certified digital forensics specialist.

## Important note
Data recovery is never guaranteed, especially on modern devices using file-based encryption. This information is for general guidance and may not apply to all operating system versions or third-party backup applications. Always prioritize official support channels for Apple, Google, or Microsoft when dealing with account-level data loss.
