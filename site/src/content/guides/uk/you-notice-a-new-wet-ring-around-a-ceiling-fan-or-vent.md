# What to do if… you notice a new wet ring around a ceiling fan or vent (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as water near electrics: stop using the fan or vent, isolate power to that area at the consumer unit if you can do so safely with dry hands, and keep people away from the wet area until it is checked.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t run the fan to “dry it out” or keep flipping switches to test it.
- Don’t touch the fan, vent grille, light fitting, pull-cord, switch, or cover plate if anything nearby is damp.
- Don’t poke, drill, cut, or open the ceiling to “let water out”.
- Don’t stand under a bulge, sag, crack, or active drip.
- Don’t put a ladder directly under the wet area.
- Don’t assume it is just condensation if it is new, spreading, stained, dripping, or near electrical fittings.
- Don’t restore power to that circuit until affected electrics have been checked by a qualified electrician.

## What to do now
1. Make the area electrically safer first.
   - Do not touch the fan, vent, switch, pull-cord, or nearby fitting.
   - If you can reach the consumer unit safely with dry hands and a dry floor, switch off the circuit for that room or area.
   - If you are not sure which circuit it is, use the main switch only if you can do that safely.
   - If the consumer unit looks wet, damaged, scorched, or risky to reach, do not touch it. Keep people out of the room and get urgent help from a qualified electrician.

2. Keep people away from the wet ceiling area.
   - Move children, pets, and anyone else out of the room.
   - Avoid standing, sitting, or working below the ring, fan, vent, or any bulge.
   - If the ceiling is sagging, cracking, creaking, or dripping steadily, close the door if safe and stay out.

3. Contain water without touching the fitting.
   - Put a bucket or bowl under the drip if you can do this from a safe position.
   - Lay towels or plastic sheeting on the floor away from the fitting.
   - Move valuables and portable electrical items away from the area below.

4. Reduce the chance of more water feeding the problem.
   - If there is a bathroom, kitchen, boiler, loft tank, or pipework above, stop using water in that area.
   - If the wet ring appeared after rain, avoid using the room below the stain and treat it as a possible roof or loft leak.
   - If you know exactly where your stop tap is and can reach it safely, turn off the water supply. If you are in a flat or are unsure about shared supplies, do not force anything.

5. Get the right urgent help.
   - If you rent, report it as an urgent repair to your landlord or agent and use any out-of-hours repairs route they provide.
   - If you own, call an emergency plumber if a pipe, tank, bathroom, kitchen, or boiler may be above it.
   - If it followed rain or there is loft or roof access above, contact a roofer or appropriate property repair service.
   - Tell anyone attending that the water is around a ceiling fan or vent and may involve electrics.

6. Escalate immediately if there are signs of fire or electrical danger.
   - If there is sparking, smoke, a burning smell, visible scorching, fire, or immediate danger, get out if safe and call 999.
   - Do not try to remove the fan or vent, and do not throw water at any electrical fitting.

7. Record what you can without disturbing anything.
   - Take photos or video from a safe distance.
   - Note when you first saw it, whether it is spreading, whether rain or water use happened just before it appeared, and who you reported it to.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it is roof damage, plumbing, condensation, or a ventilation fault.
- You do not need to open the ceiling, remove the fan, remove the vent grille, or start drying equipment.
- You do not need to resolve liability today.
- You do not need to restore power today if the fitting or ceiling may still be wet.

## Important reassurance
A new wet ring around a fan or vent is a reason to pause and make the area safe, not a reason to panic. Keeping away from the fitting, isolating power if safe, limiting further water, and reporting it quickly are the main first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce shock, fire, ceiling-collapse risk, and damage while you arrange proper inspection. Later decisions about repairs, insurance, tenancy responsibility, or electrical testing may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, electrical, building, insurance, or professional advice. If you cannot safely isolate power, the ceiling looks unstable, or there are signs of fire or electrical danger, keep clear and get urgent professional or emergency help.
