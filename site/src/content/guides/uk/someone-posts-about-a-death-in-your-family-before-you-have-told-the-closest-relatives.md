# What to do if… someone posts about a death in your family before you have told the closest relatives (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, choose one calm lead person, and tell the closest family members directly before the online post spreads further. Take one limited record of the post, then ask for removal and report it without getting drawn into public comments.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t reply publicly in anger or correct details in comments; this can draw more attention to the post.
- Don’t confirm the death online while you are still unsure who has been told.
- Don’t ring lots of people at once without a simple order of who needs to hear first.
- Don’t argue with the poster in direct messages if they are hostile; keep any request short, then report or block.
- Don’t share screenshots in group chats unless there is a clear need; this can spread the post further.
- Don’t keep refreshing comments yourself if another trusted person can handle reporting and updates.

## What to do now
1. **Take 30 seconds and choose one lead person.** If possible, choose the closest family member, or the calmest trusted person, to coordinate the first calls.

2. **Tell the immediate family first, directly.** Call rather than text where you can. Use a simple opener such as: “I’m so sorry. I need to tell you something important, and I didn’t want you to find out online.”

3. **Use only what is confirmed.** If details are not fully clear, say: “This is what I’ve been told. I’m still confirming the facts.” Do not fill gaps or repeat uncertain details as fact.

4. **Capture the essentials once, then stop looking.** Take one screenshot showing the account name, date or time, and the post. Copy the post link if you can. Then close the app or ask someone else to monitor it.

5. **Send one short removal request if it feels safe.** You can write: “Please delete this post now. Immediate family have not been told yet. Please do not repost or discuss this publicly.” Do not debate if they refuse.

6. **Report the post on the platform.** Use the in-app report option for the specific post or account. Choose the closest available category and add one short explanation: “Immediate family have not been informed and this is causing distress.”

7. **Slow the spread from your side.**
   - Ask key relatives not to comment, react, tag others, or share screenshots.
   - If you are being tagged, turn on tag or mention review if the platform allows it.
   - Mute the person, keywords, or conversation if you need a break.
   - Block the person if contact is making the situation worse.

8. **Use police routes only if there is a safety or crime concern.** If the post includes threats, targeted harassment, personal details shared without consent, or a crime in progress, save what you have and report it. Use 999 if someone is in immediate danger. Use 101 or online police reporting if it is not an emergency.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide now whether to take legal action.
- You don’t need to write a public statement, obituary, or official post today.
- You don’t need to answer every extended family member, friend, or colleague before the immediate family has been told.
- You don’t need to keep monitoring the post yourself if a trusted person can handle reporting.
- You don’t need to settle every factual detail before saying, calmly, that the post should not be shared.

## Important reassurance
This is a deeply upsetting situation, and feeling shocked, angry, or protective is understandable. You can still restore some control by telling the closest people directly, limiting further sharing, and avoiding a public argument.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation, reduce harm, and buy time. Later decisions about wider family messages, press contact, ongoing harassment, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe, or the online posting becomes targeted harassment or threats, prioritise immediate safety and contact the appropriate services.
