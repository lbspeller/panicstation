# What to do if… your connection becomes an overnight layover and you are unsure if you can enter the country (UK)

## Short answer
Do not go through UK passport control just to see what happens. First ask your airline or airport transfer staff whether you can stay airside, and check whether going landside would require an ETA, visa, or other permission for your passport and route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not join the arrivals or passport control queue if you are not sure you have permission to enter the UK.
- Do not assume that “transit” means you can leave the secure area, collect bags, sleep in a hotel, or change terminals without extra permission.
- Do not leave the airside area for food, a cheaper hotel, or fresh air until you know you can legally go landside and still continue your journey.
- Do not throw away boarding passes, bag tags, disruption messages, booking references, or screenshots of the changed itinerary.
- Do not rely on another passenger’s answer, because UK transit requirements depend on your passport, route, documents, airline, baggage, and whether you pass border control.
- Do not book a landside hotel until you know you can enter the UK and return for your onward flight.

## What to do now
1. **Stop before the passport control route and find an airline or airport transfer desk.**
   - Say: **“My connection has become an overnight layover. Can I stay airside, or will this itinerary require me to pass UK border control?”**
   - Keep your passport, boarding pass, bag tag, booking reference, and disruption notice in your hand or in one folder.

2. **Confirm whether you are airside or landside.**
   - Airside generally means you do not pass through UK border control before your onward flight.
   - Landside generally means you do pass through UK border control, even if you only plan to leave the airport briefly or return for a connecting flight.
   - Ask: **“Is there an airside route for my onward flight, and is it open overnight for passengers in my situation?”**

3. **Check whether anything will force you landside.**
   - Ask the airline: **“Is my bag checked through to my final destination, or must I collect it in the UK?”**
   - Ask: **“Do I need a new boarding pass, a terminal change that goes through arrivals, a change of airport, or a check-in desk landside?”**
   - If any of these apply, say: **“I may not have UK entry permission. What option avoids making me enter the UK?”**

4. **Before going landside, treat it as entering the UK.**
   - Check the current GOV.UK visa and ETA pages for your passport nationality and route.
   - If you are unsure, tell the airline or transfer staff: **“I am not sure I have permission to enter the UK. Please do not route me landside without checking.”**
   - Remember that an ETA is permission to travel and does not guarantee entry at the border.

5. **If your baggage is the problem, ask for airline options before leaving airside.**
   - Say: **“My checked bag may need collecting, but I may not be able to enter the UK. Can you check whether it is through-tagged, can be handled without me, or whether I should be rerouted?”**
   - Ask the airline to put the disruption and any agreed plan in your booking notes.

6. **If staff direct you toward passport control, pause and ask who can advise before you commit.**
   - Say: **“I am in transit and unsure I can enter the UK. Who can I speak to before I go through passport control?”**
   - Keep the explanation short: **“My connection changed to an overnight layover, and I am trying not to get stranded landside without the right documents.”**

7. **Make the safest overnight plan once your status is clear.**
   - If you can stay airside, ask where transit passengers may wait overnight and when the departure area, transfer route, or next gate opens.
   - If you are allowed to go landside, choose a close airport hotel or official waiting area, keep documents and medication with you, and set alarms for your return.
   - If neither option is clear, ask for the airline duty manager and say: **“I need a route or overnight plan that does not require UK entry unless I have the correct permission.”**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to complain, claim compensation, or change future travel plans.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip at once; focus on not crossing the wrong control point and keeping a lawful onward route.
- Food, chargers, hotel prices, and comfort items can wait until you know whether you are staying airside or going landside.

## Important reassurance
This is a reasonable thing to be unsure about. UK transit depends heavily on whether you pass border control, so pausing before passport control and getting a clear airside or landside answer is the right first move.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Later decisions about rebooking, complaints, refunds, compensation, immigration status, or missed onward travel may need help from the airline, airport staff, an official government source, or a qualified specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, or professional advice. UK entry and transit rules can change and depend on your nationality, documents, route, baggage, and whether you pass border control; an ETA does not guarantee entry.
