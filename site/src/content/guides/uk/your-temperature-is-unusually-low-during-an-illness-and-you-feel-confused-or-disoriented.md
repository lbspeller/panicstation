# What to do if… your temperature is unusually low during an illness and you feel confused or disoriented (UK)

## Short answer
**Call 999 immediately** and state that you are ill, have an unusually low temperature, and are experiencing confusion; specifically ask the operator, "Could it be sepsis?"

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "sleep it off" or wait for the temperature to rise naturally.
- Do not apply direct heat to the skin, such as hot water bottles, heat lamps, or electric pads.
- Do not enter a hot bath or shower, as rapid heating can cause a dangerous drop in blood pressure.
- Do not rub or massage your limbs or skin to generate friction heat.
- Do not drink alcohol.
- Do not take further doses of paracetamol or "cold and flu" medications until advised by a clinician, as confusion increases the risk of accidental overdose.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 for an ambulance immediately.**  
   - **Provide the operator with your specific temperature reading if known.**  
   - **State clearly: "I am unwell, my temperature is low, and I am confused—could it be sepsis?"**  
2. **Alert someone nearby to stay with you.**  
   - **Call a neighbour, friend, or family member and ask them to enter your home and remain with you until paramedics arrive.**  
   - **Unlock your front door if you are currently able to do so safely.**  
3. **Prevent further heat loss by adding dry layers.**  
   - **Cover your head with a hat or hood, as significant heat is lost through the scalp.**  
   - **Wrap yourself in dry blankets, a duvet, or a sleeping bag.**  
   - **Change out of damp or sweat-soaked clothing only if you can do so without significant physical exertion.**  
4. **Warm the room to approximately 25°C (77°F).**  
   - **Turn up the central heating or use a space heater positioned at a safe distance from your bedding/clothing.**  
5. **Focus warmth on the torso only.**  
   - **Place additional dry layers specifically over the chest and stomach.**  
   - **Do not attempt to warm the arms or legs first, as this can draw cold blood back to the heart.**  
6. **Only consume a warm, non-alcoholic drink if you are fully alert and can swallow easily.**  
   - **Stop immediately if you feel increasingly drowsy or experience coughing.**  
7. **Prepare a brief list of facts for the ambulance crew.**  
   - **Include your most recent temperature reading and the time it was taken.**  
   - **List all medications taken in the last 24 hours, including over-the-counter flu remedies.**  
   - **Note if you have stopped passing urine or if your skin looks mottled/blue/blotchy.**  

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the illness (e.g., flu, chest infection, or urinary tract infection) right now.
- You do not need to continue checking your temperature once emergency services have been called.
- You do not need to gather a hospital bag or tidy the room.

## Important reassurance
A low temperature (hypothermia) during an illness is a recognized medical emergency. Confusion is a physical symptom of the brain's response to low temperature or severe infection; it is not a personal failure to think clearly. Paramedics are trained to manage this exact combination of symptoms.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute medical crisis. It does not replace clinical diagnosis or treatment for sepsis or environmental hypothermia.

## Important note
A body temperature below 35°C (95°F) combined with mental status changes is a critical emergency in the UK. This information is based on 2026 emergency protocols and is intended for immediate harm reduction while professional help is en route.
