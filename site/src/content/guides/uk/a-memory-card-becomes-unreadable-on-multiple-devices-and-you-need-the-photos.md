# What to do if… a memory card becomes unreadable on multiple devices and you need the photos (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the card immediately and physically isolate it to prevent any further data degradation or accidental overwriting. Do not attempt to "repair" or "format" the device if the photos are irreplaceable, as these actions may permanently destroy the data.

## Do not do these things
- Don't click **Format**, **Erase**, or **Initialize** if prompted by your computer or camera.
- Don't use "Scan and fix" or **CHKDSK** commands, as these can alter the file structure and make recovery harder.
- Don't keep inserting the card into different devices, as repeated power cycles can worsen internal hardware failure.
- Don't use abrasive materials, erasers, or "cleaning" fluids on the gold contact points.
- Don't attempt the "tape trick" or other physical hacks found online.
- Don't save or recover any files **back onto the same card** if you manage to find a recovery tool.

## What to do now
1. **Remove and physically lock the card.**  
   **Pull the card out of the device immediately to stop all power flow.** If it is a full-size SD card, slide the small plastic "lock" tab on the side to the "Lock" position to prevent any device from writing new data to it.
2. **Search for existing copies or syncs.**  
   **Check every potential secondary location where the images might have been copied automatically.** 
   - Cloud services like iCloud, Google Photos, or OneDrive.
   - Internal storage of the camera or phone (some devices save to both internal memory and card).
   - Messaging apps or emails where you may have shared a preview.
   - Previous imports on other laptops or tablets.
3. **Perform a single "Detection Only" check.**  
   **Use a high-quality external card reader on a computer to see if the device is acknowledged by the system at a hardware level.** 
   - On **Mac**: Open **Disk Utility**; look for the card in the sidebar. Do not run "First Aid."
   - On **Windows**: Open **Disk Management**; check if a "Removable" disk appears. Do not "Initialize" or "Format."
4. **Identify the threshold for professional help.**  
   **Decide if the photos are "once-in-a-lifetime" (weddings, births, legal evidence) or if they can be risked with DIY software.** If the files are high-stakes, stop all activity and place the card in a protective case or envelope for a professional lab.
5. **Secure evidence for a potential Consumer Rights claim.**  
   **Take a photo of any specific error message (e.g., "Card error" or "Please format disk") and keep your original receipt.** Under the UK Consumer Rights Act 2015, you have a right to a refund or replacement from the **retailer** if the product is faulty.
   - Within 30 days: Short-term right to reject for a full refund.
   - Within 6 months: Retailer must prove the card was not faulty at the time of sale.
6. **Contact Citizens Advice for escalation.**  
   **Call the Citizens Advice consumer helpline (0808 223 1133) if the retailer refuses to acknowledge the fault.** Note that while they can help you get a refund for the card, they generally cannot help you recover the cost of professional data recovery unless negligence is proven.

## What can wait
- You don't need to decide which new card or brand to buy today.
- You don't need to argue with the shop staff immediately; your priority is the data on the card.
- You don't need to learn how to use complex data recovery software right this second.
- You don't need to worry about "fixing" the card for future use; a card that has failed once should be discarded after recovery.

## Important reassurance
Memory card "unreadable" errors are frequently logical (file system) rather than physical. As long as you have not formatted the card or saved new files over the old ones, the digital information often remains on the chips. By stopping now, you have preserved the highest possible chance for a successful recovery.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and the preservation of consumer rights in the UK. It does not provide technical tutorials for specific data recovery software or instructions for physical "chip-off" hardware repair.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee data recovery. If your photos are critical for legal, medical, or high-value professional reasons, consult a professional data recovery service before taking any DIY steps. Consumer rights regarding "consequential loss" (the cost of the recovery vs. the cost of the card) are complex and usually limited; speak to a legal professional for specific advice on damages.
