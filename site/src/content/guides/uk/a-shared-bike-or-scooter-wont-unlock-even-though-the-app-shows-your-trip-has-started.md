# What to do if… a shared bike or scooter won’t unlock even though the app shows your trip has started (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately capture evidence of the locked vehicle and use the app to end or cancel the trip to stop the billing timer.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to force, pry, or kick the locking mechanism as this can cause injury or lead to damage charges.
- Don’t leave the scene without a photo of the vehicle ID and a screenshot of the active timer.
- Don’t attempt to start new trips on other vehicles until the current session is closed or reported.
- Don’t wait for the timer to reach a high amount before seeking help; treat the first 120 seconds as the critical window for cancellation.

## What to do now
1. **Move yourself and the vehicle to a safe position away from traffic and stop all physical attempts to unlock it.**
   - Ensure you are not blocking a cycle lane, pavement, or dropped kerb.
2. **Check for a physical manual lock that may require a second step.**
   - Look at the rear wheel for a pin or "horseshoe" lock that must be manually pulled out.
   - For e-bikes with integrated cables, check if the cable is snagged or requires a firm pull after the app click.
3. **If using a docked system like Santander Cycles (TfL), re-secure the bike and use the docking station hardware.**
   - Firmly push the bike back into the dock.
   - **Press the red "Fault" / spanner button on the docking point within 10 seconds of docking.**
   - Note the docking station name and the specific dock number.
4. **Capture photographic evidence of the failure immediately.**
   - **Screenshot the app screen showing the active trip and any error messages.**
   - **Take a clear photo of the vehicle’s QR code and the physical ID number (usually printed on the frame or rear mudguard).**
   - **Take a photo of the vehicle showing it is still physically locked (e.g., the wheel lock is still engaged).**
5. **Use the "End Ride" or "Cancel Trip" function in the app immediately.**
   - Look for a "Problem unlocking?" or "Report Issue" link on the active trip screen.
   - If the app refuses to end the trip because you are not in a parking zone, use the "Help" or "Chat" feature to request an emergency stop.
6. **Submit a formal report through the operator's support channel before leaving the vehicle.**
   - Attach the photos of the vehicle ID and the locked mechanism.
   - State clearly: "Trip started in app but vehicle remained physically locked. Requesting immediate trip closure and refund."
7. **Verify that the trip has actually ended on your screen.**
   - Do not assume it has closed just because you sent a message.
   - If the timer is still running, take a final screenshot of the elapsed time and your location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to wait by the vehicle for a support response once you have submitted your evidence and moved the vehicle to a safe, upright position.
- You do not need to file a bank dispute (chargeback) immediately; wait for the operator's initial response to your report.
- You do not need to determine the technical cause of the GPS or Bluetooth failure.

## Important reassurance
Technical glitches involving Bluetooth connectivity or GPS sync are common in shared mobility schemes. If you have clear photos of the vehicle ID and the locked state, UK operators generally approve refund requests for "failed unlocks." Your immediate priority is stopping the clock, not fixing the bike.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for rental transport failures in the UK. It does not cover road traffic accident procedures or long-term legal claims.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Ensure you follow the specific safety instructions provided by your local operator (e.g., TfL, Lime, Voi, or Tier). If you are in an unsafe location, prioritize your physical safety over the vehicle status.
