# What to do if… water starts leaking from your refrigerator or freezer and you cannot find the source (UK)

## Short answer
Get water away from electrics first. If you can do it without standing in water or touching wet switches, switch the fridge or freezer off, then shut off any water supply to the appliance and contain the leak.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand in water while touching plugs, sockets, extension leads, switches, or the appliance.
- Don’t touch switches or electrical equipment with wet hands.
- Don’t keep the fridge or freezer running if water is still appearing or moving toward electrics.
- Don’t pull the appliance out sharply; a thin water line behind it can kink, loosen, or split.
- Don’t use a hair dryer, heater, or other heat source to dry the area while water is present or near wiring.
- Don’t ignore water tracking under units, behind kickboards, or toward sockets or the consumer unit.
- Don’t remove panels or start dismantling internal parts while you are unsure what is wet or live.

## What to do now
1. **Make the electrics safer first.** If the floor and your hands are dry, switch the appliance off at the plug. If the plug is hard to reach, it may be controlled by a switched fused connection unit or switched spur above the worktop; use that only if it is dry and safe to touch.
2. **Shut off the appliance water supply if it has one.** If the fridge or freezer has a water dispenser or ice maker, look for a small isolating valve on the supply line, often under the kitchen sink or on nearby pipework, and turn it off. Do not force a stiff valve.
3. **Use the main stop tap if water is still coming and you cannot find the small valve.** The inside stop tap, also called a stopcock or stop valve, is often under the kitchen sink, near where the water pipe enters the home, or in a utility area. Turn it clockwise to shut the water off, and stop if it will not move without force.
4. **Contain the spread.** Put towels or cloths around the water, then use a tray, baking sheet, washing-up bowl, or shallow container to catch drips. If water is heading under cabinets, press towels along the kickboard edge to slow it down.
5. **Check the simple door and loading causes.** Make sure the fridge or freezer door is fully shut, no packaging is blocking the seal, the seal is not folded over, and food is not preventing airflow or closure.
6. **Look for visible outside clues without forcing anything.** With power off, and water off if the appliance has a water feed, gently pull the appliance forward only far enough to look. Check for dripping from a water line, filter housing, connection point, or a visible drain pan or drip tray. Do not remove panels.
7. **If it looks like meltwater or a blocked defrost drain, keep it simple.** Leave the appliance off for now, keep catching and wiping up water, and avoid poking tools into drain holes or hidden parts while you are rushed.
8. **Protect food while you stabilise the leak.** Keep the doors closed as much as possible. As a general estimate, food in a fridge may stay cold for up to 4 hours, and food in a freezer may stay frozen for up to 48 hours if full or about 24 hours if half full, but this depends on the appliance, room temperature, and door opening.
9. **Move only the most at-risk food if needed.** If the appliance will be off for longer, move high-risk chilled food such as meat, fish, dairy, cooked leftovers, and ready-to-eat items to another working fridge, a cool box, or a clean container with ice packs if you can do that safely.
10. **Get the right help if the leak is not stopping.** If you rent, notify your landlord or agent and send photos of the water, the appliance, and any wet flooring or nearby electrics. If you own, contact an appliance repair service; if a pipe, valve, or hidden connection appears to be leaking, contact a plumber.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact internal part right now.
- You do not need to empty the whole fridge or freezer immediately unless food safety or access makes it necessary.
- You do not need to decide whether to repair or replace the appliance today.
- Deep-cleaning, removing kickboards, pulling panels, checking warranties, and arguing over responsibility can wait until the water is stopped and the area is dry and safe.
- If you rent, detailed tenancy checks can wait until you have reported the problem and reduced the immediate risk.

## Important reassurance
A leaking fridge or freezer is stressful, but the first safe steps are simple: avoid wet electrics, stop any water feed, contain the spread, and keep the doors closed. You do not have to solve the whole fault before making the situation safer.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Diagnosis, repair, food decisions, and any landlord or insurance issues may need help from an appliance technician, plumber, electrician, landlord or agent, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety and damage-limitation information, not legal, electrical, plumbing, food-safety, financial, insurance, or other professional advice. If water may have reached sockets, wiring, a fused spur, or the consumer unit, get qualified help before restoring power.
