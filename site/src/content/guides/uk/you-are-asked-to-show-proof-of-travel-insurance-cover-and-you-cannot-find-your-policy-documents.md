# What to do if… you are asked to show proof of travel insurance cover and you cannot find your policy documents (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the requester for the specific document type they require, then use your banking app or email search to retrieve a digital PDF of your policy schedule or certificate of insurance immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to create, buy, or use "dummy" or "fake" insurance certificates to bypass a check-in requirement.
- Don’t guess or provide approximate policy numbers or dates if asked for official verification.
- Don’t hand over your unlocked mobile device to a third party to "look through" your apps or emails.
- Don’t assume a Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC) or EHIC will be accepted as a substitute for private travel insurance.
- Don’t purchase a new, duplicate policy until you have confirmed your existing cover is unretrievable, as this can complicate future claims.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact documentation required by the official or provider.** 
   - Ask if they need a physical printout, a PDF on a screen, or just the policy provider name and reference number.
   - Clarify if they require a specific "Letter of Verification" (common for some visa applications) or just a standard policy schedule.

2. **Search your primary email account for keywords related to the purchase.** 
   - Use search terms such as "policy," "certificate," "schedule," "confirmation," or "insurance" combined with the year.
   - Check "Spam" or "Junk" folders if the policy was purchased or renewed recently.
   - Look for emails from comparison sites (e.g., MoneySuperMarket, Confused.com) if you did not buy directly from an insurer.

3. **Check your banking app or online banking portal if you have a packaged account.** 
   - Many UK banks (including Barclays, Lloyds, NatWest, and HSBC) provide "instant" insurance certificates via the "Manage Insurance" or "Benefits" section of their app.
   - Look for a "Get Proof of Cover" or "Download Certificate" button specifically designed for travel and visa requirements.

4. **Contact the insurer’s customer service line or use their live chat feature.** 
   - Request that they email a PDF copy of your "Schedule of Insurance" or "Certificate of Insurance" while you remain on the line.
   - Have your full name, date of birth, and postcode ready to pass security if you do not have your policy number.

5. **Provide the documents safely if you are at a physical check-in or desk.** 
   - Ask for an official company email address where you can forward the PDF if they cannot view it on your screen.
   - Ensure the email address provided is an official domain (e.g., @airline.com or @cruisefirm.co.uk) before sending personal data.

6. **Request a formal record if you are denied service or boarding.** 
   - Ask for the refusal and the specific reason to be provided in writing or via email.
   - This record is essential if you need to make a claim for "denied boarding" or "missed departure" later.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to review the full 50-page policy wording or terms and conditions right now.
- You don’t need to compare your current cover to other providers while at the check-in desk.
- You don’t need to notify the insurer of any potential claims unless an actual incident (theft, illness) has occurred.
- You don’t need to worry about "duplicate cover" issues until after your immediate travel deadline has passed.

## Important reassurance
Losing access to digital documents during travel is a frequent occurrence. Most UK insurers and banks have established protocols for reissuing proof of cover instantly via apps or email. As long as your policy is active, the provider can verify your status.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate retrieval of existing proof of cover for administrative purposes. It does not cover legal disputes regarding policy validity or the process of making a medical or cancellation claim.

## Important note
Travel insurance requirements are set by individual carriers (airlines, cruise lines) and national border authorities. While this guide assists in document retrieval, it does not guarantee that your specific policy will meet the minimum coverage levels required by your provider.
