# What to do if… your vehicle starts but the accelerator feels unresponsive while you are still in a safe place (UK)

## Short answer
Keep the vehicle stationary, secure it with the parking brake, and do not attempt to enter traffic. Check for physical pedal obstructions and warning lights before arranging professional recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to drive into traffic or onto public roads to see if the problem clears.
- Do not repeatedly rev the engine to high RPMs while stationary.
- Do not keep the engine running if you see smoke, smell burning, or see a red "Stop" warning light.
- Do not reach into the footwell while the engine is in gear or the parking brake is off.
- Do not ignore "Limp Home" or "Reduced Power" dashboard messages.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle immediately**
   - **Apply the parking brake (handbrake)** and ensure the vehicle is in Park (automatic) or Neutral (manual).
   - **Switch on hazard warning lights** if your position could be an obstruction to other drivers or pedestrians.
   - **Press the SOS button** if your vehicle is equipped with one and you believe your position is immediately dangerous.

2. **Check for physical obstructions in the footwell**
   - **Visually inspect the area around the accelerator pedal** for loose objects like bottles, rubbish, or displaced floor mats.
   - **Remove any item** that may be wedged behind or under the pedal.
   - **Ensure the floor mat is clipped into its factory anchor points** and is not bunched up under the controls.

3. **Check the dashboard for specific fault indicators**
   - **Look for a red engine light, a lightning bolt symbol (Electronic Throttle Control), or a "Check Engine" light.**
   - **Note any text messages** such as "Engine Power Reduced" or "Limp Home Mode" which indicate the car's computer has restricted the throttle to prevent damage.
   - **Switch the engine off immediately** if a red warning light or "STOP" command is visible.

4. **Verify gear selection and clutch operation**
   - **Manual vehicles:** confirm the clutch pedal returns fully to its upward position and that the vehicle is fully engaged in 1st gear.
   - **Automatic vehicles:** confirm the selector is fully seated in "Drive" or "Reverse" and that any "Electronic Park" triggers have released.
   - **Treat the issue as a transmission or clutch failure** if the engine revs freely but the wheels do not receive power.

5. **Perform a single controlled system reset**
   - **Switch the ignition completely off** and remove the key (or move the fob away from the sensor).
   - **Wait at least 30 seconds** to allow the electronic control units to discharge and reset.
   - **Restart the engine** and check if the throttle response has returned; if it has not, do not attempt further restarts.

6. **Alert other road users if you are an obstruction**
   - **Place a warning triangle** at least 45 metres (147 feet) behind the vehicle on the same side of the road, unless you are on a motorway.
   - **Call 999** if your vehicle is in a position where it is an immediate hazard to other road users and cannot be moved.

7. **Arrange professional recovery**
   - **Contact your breakdown provider** or a local mobile mechanic for a diagnostic scan.
   - **Provide the specific warning lights or messages** you observed to the recovery operator to ensure the correct equipment is sent.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify which specific sensor (TPS, MAP, or ECU) has failed.
- You do not need to decide if the car is a "write-off" or requires a new engine.
- You do not need to check the engine oil or coolant levels unless a specific light directs you to do so.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of powerlessness when a vehicle fails to respond to your inputs. Discovering this fault while in a safe place, such as a driveway or car park, is the best possible outcome for a mechanical failure as it prevents a loss of power while at speed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. A vehicle with an unresponsive accelerator requires a professional diagnostic tool (OBD-II) to identify and clear the underlying electronic or mechanical fault.

## Important note
This information is for guidance in the immediate aftermath of a vehicle fault and does not replace the advice of a qualified mechanic or the specific instructions in your vehicle's owner manual. If you are in a live lane of traffic or on a motorway, prioritize the safety of all occupants by exiting the vehicle toward the verge and calling emergency services.
