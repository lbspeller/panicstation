# What to do if… your steering wheel lock will not release and the car will not start (UK)

## Short answer
Make yourself safe first, then try to release pressure on the steering lock: hold the wheel gently in the direction where it has a little movement, or move it gently left and right, while you turn the key or press Start with the correct pedal down. Do not force the key, the Start button, or the wheel.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t yank the steering wheel hard or use sudden jerks.
- Don’t force the key with pliers or extra leverage.
- Don’t keep twisting the key or repeatedly pressing Start in frustration.
- Don’t spray oil, penetrating fluid, or general lubricant into the ignition as a first step.
- Don’t attempt steering-column or ignition repairs at the roadside.
- Don’t keep troubleshooting if the car is stopped somewhere unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Make the place safer before troubleshooting.**
   - Put your hazard warning lights on.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, put sidelights on as well.
   - If you are on a motorway or another fast road, try to be in a place of relative safety before doing anything else.
   - If you are stopped in a live lane, cannot get safely away from traffic, or feel in immediate danger, stay belted in with hazards on and call 999, asking for the police.

2. **Try the usual steering-lock release.**
   - Make sure the key is fully inserted, or that the keyless fob is inside the car.
   - Turn the steering wheel slowly left and right.
   - Find the direction where the wheel moves a small amount.
   - Hold steady, gentle pressure in that direction.
   - While holding that pressure, turn the key towards ON or START.

3. **If the car has push-button start, add the normal start position.**
   - Press and hold the brake pedal firmly, or the clutch pedal if your manual car normally needs that to start.
   - Move the steering wheel gently left and right.
   - While doing that, press Start once.
   - If the display says the key is not detected, move the fob closer to the Start button or use the emergency start method in your owner’s manual.

4. **Check the start interlocks that can feel like a steering-lock fault.**
   - Automatic: make sure the selector is fully in Park. If safe and normal for your car, try Neutral, then return to Park.
   - Manual: make sure the car is in Neutral and press the clutch fully if your car normally requires that to start.
   - If the brake pedal is needed for your car, keep it pressed while trying Start.

5. **Try the spare key if you have it with you.**
   - If the spare key works easily, stop using the main key for now.
   - The main key may be worn, bent, damaged, or not being recognised.

6. **Stop after a few calm attempts.**
   - If the wheel or key still will not release, stop before you damage the key, barrel, or steering lock.
   - Once you are as safe as you can be, call your breakdown provider or recovery service.
   - Say: “The steering lock will not release and the key will not turn,” or “The steering lock will not release on push-button start.”
   - Give your exact location, including road name, direction of travel, junction, service area, marker post, or nearby landmark if you can see one.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to identify the exact fault right now.
- You don’t need to decide whether the problem is the key, ignition barrel, steering lock, fob battery, 12V battery, or immobiliser.
- You don’t need to remove covers, dismantle anything, or search for parts at the roadside.
- You don’t need to decide on repairs today; the first job is to stay safe and avoid making the lock or key worse.

## Important reassurance
A steering lock can bind when the front wheels are resting against pressure after parking, especially if the wheels were turned. Calm, steady movement is more useful than strength, and stopping before something breaks is a sensible choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, avoid damage, and get safer help. If the problem keeps happening, a mechanic, breakdown technician, dealer, or auto locksmith may need to check the key, ignition barrel, steering lock, keyless fob, 12V battery, or starting system.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate harm-prevention and safer next steps. It is not professional mechanical, legal, insurance, or recovery advice.
