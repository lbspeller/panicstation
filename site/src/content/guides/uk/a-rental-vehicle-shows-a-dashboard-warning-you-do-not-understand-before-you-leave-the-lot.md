# What to do if… a rental vehicle shows a dashboard warning you do not understand before you leave the lot (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive off while an unexplained warning is still showing. Take a clear photo of the dashboard, then go straight back to the hire desk or agent and ask for the warning to be checked, recorded, and resolved, or for a different vehicle.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the lot hoping the warning will go away.
- Do not assume a warning is harmless because the car has just been handed to you.
- Do not ignore a red warning light, an amber warning that stays on, an alarm, smoke, overheating, loss of brake assistance, or a strong burning smell.
- Do not reset or clear warnings yourself.
- Do not disconnect anything or open parts of the vehicle unless the hire company specifically tells you to and it is safe.
- Do not sign or accept a check-out record saying the vehicle is fault-free if the warning has not been recorded.
- Do not let the queue, flight timing, or a rushed handover push you into taking a vehicle you think may be unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Keep the vehicle safely parked.** Stay in the bay or pull into the nearest safe bay inside the lot. Apply the parking brake, keep the vehicle in Park or neutral as appropriate, and do not join public roads.

2. **Take quick evidence before anyone moves the car.**
   - Photograph or video the dashboard warning, including any written message.
   - Photograph the odometer, fuel or battery level, and the vehicle bay if visible.
   - Keep the photos even if the warning later clears.

3. **Check whether it is only the normal start-up self-check.**
   - Many dashboard lights briefly appear when the ignition starts and then go out.
   - If the warning stays on, comes back, flashes, sounds an alarm, or is red or amber, treat it as something the hire company needs to deal with before you leave.

4. **Go back to the hire desk or contact the agent immediately.** Show the photo and say: “This warning is showing before I have left the lot. I do not understand it and I am not comfortable taking the vehicle unless it is checked and resolved.”

5. **Ask for a clear decision, not a vague reassurance.** Ask the agent to do one of these:
   - explain the warning using the vehicle handbook or their vehicle record;
   - check the vehicle on site and confirm it is safe to take;
   - move you into a different vehicle.

6. **Get the warning recorded in writing.** Ask for the warning and the agent’s decision to be noted on the rental agreement, check-out sheet, app record, email, text message, or branch incident note. Make sure the note says the warning was present before you left the premises.

7. **If the warning is red, relates to oil pressure, brakes, overheating, steering, tyres, or charging, ask for a replacement vehicle.** Do not accept the same vehicle unless the hire company can clearly explain why the warning has cleared and why it is safe to use.

8. **If you are at a barrier or exit already, do not continue onto the road.** Pull into the nearest safe bay inside the site and contact the hire desk using the number on the agreement, app, or booking email.

9. **If you are pressured to take it anyway, escalate calmly.** Ask for a supervisor or manager and repeat that you are reporting a possible safety or roadworthiness issue before leaving the premises.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact symbol yourself.
- You do not need to diagnose the fault or decide whether it is mechanical, electrical, or a sensor issue.
- You do not need to settle any argument about liability, excess, insurance, refunds, or compensation right now.
- You do not need to continue the journey in that vehicle just because paperwork has started.
- Your immediate job is only to avoid driving an unexplained warning onto the road and to make a clear record before the hire begins.

## Important reassurance
This is a reasonable thing to pause for. Dashboard symbols can be unclear, and the easiest time to deal with an unexplained warning is before you leave the lot, while the hire company can still inspect, record, or swap the vehicle.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: they are meant to help you avoid driving a potentially unsafe hire vehicle and to create a clear record of a warning present at pick-up. Later decisions about complaints, refunds, breakdown cover, repairs, or disputed charges may need help from the hire company, your insurer, your card provider, or a relevant advice service.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, financial, insurance, or professional advice. If you believe the vehicle may be unsafe, prioritise not driving it and getting the hire company to resolve the warning before you leave.
