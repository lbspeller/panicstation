# What to do if… your bike lights will not turn on and you realise before it gets dark (UK)

## Short answer
Do not attempt to ride after sunset without working lights; instead, secure an alternative transport method or a daylight-only route immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume street lighting is a legal or safe substitute for bike-mounted lights.
- Do not attempt to ride while holding a mobile phone torch in one hand.
- Do not continue cycling in the hope that mechanical vibration will fix a loose connection.
- Do not take a less visible route to avoid being seen without lights.
- Do not dismantle light housings or wiring while positioned on the roadside or in traffic.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe, stationary position away from the flow of traffic to inspect the equipment.** **Toggle the power switch multiple times and check for a "lockout" mode that may have been accidentally activated.**
2. **Perform a basic power check if the device remains unresponsive.**
   - For battery-powered lights, ensure the compartment is fully closed and batteries are oriented correctly.
   - For USB-rechargeable lights, check for a charging indicator light and attempt to connect a power bank if one is available.
3. **Wipe any visible moisture or grit from the electrical contacts.** **Use a dry cloth or tissue to clean the terminals on both the light unit and the battery or mount.** Ensure the unit is snapped firmly into its bracket to maintain a consistent connection.
4. **Identify the legal requirements for your remaining journey.** **In the UK, between sunset and sunrise, your cycle must have a lit white front light, a lit red rear light, a red rear reflector, and four amber pedal reflectors.** If your bike was manufactured after 1 October 1985, the pedal reflectors are a mandatory legal requirement for night riding.
5. **Switch to an alternative transport plan while daylight remains.**
   - Walk the bicycle to the nearest bus, train, or tram station.
   - Request a taxi or private hire vehicle capable of carrying a bicycle, such as an estate car or a van.
   - If you must continue on the bike, use only the most direct route and ensure you are off the road before sunset.
6. **Increase your passive visibility using any available equipment.** **Put on high-visibility clothing or reflective bands and ensure your existing reflectors are clean and unobstructed.**
7. **Relocate to a well-lit public area if you become stranded as it gets dark.** **Find a petrol station, shopfront, or transport hub where you can wait safely for assistance or alternative transport.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose why the light failed, such as water ingress or circuit failure, right now.
- You do not need to purchase replacement lighting or upgrade your system today.
- You do not need to evaluate the technical British Standards (BS6102 / 3) of your current equipment while in transit.

## Important reassurance
Equipment failure is a standard mechanical issue that affects many cyclists. Identifying the fault during daylight hours allows for a managed transition to a safe alternative, preventing a high-risk situation after sunset.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a lighting failure detected before dark. It does not cover technical repairs or long-term maintenance of bicycle electrical systems.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. UK law requires specific lighting and reflectors between sunset and sunrise; flashing lights must flash between 60 and 240 times per minute to be legal. Always prioritize your physical safety and legal compliance by not riding an unlit vehicle after dark.
