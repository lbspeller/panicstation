# What to do if… you cannot find the identity documents you need for an official process (UK)

## Short answer
Pause before sending anything and ask the organisation for its accepted ID alternatives and secure submission route. Then order only the replacement documents you clearly need, starting with any death certificate copies or core photo ID that are blocking urgent steps.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send your only original document in the post unless the organisation says it is required and explains how it will be returned.
- Do not keep sending random scans or photos without asking what the organisation will accept.
- Do not use unofficial “fast” document websites that look like government services.
- Do not hand over your only remaining ID to several organisations at once.
- Do not send ID through casual routes such as social media messages, family group chats, or forwarded email chains if the organisation offers a secure upload or agreed route.
- Do not assume one organisation’s ID rules will be accepted by another.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the organisation for its accepted alternatives.**  
   Contact the organisation and ask: “What documents can I use if I do not have my passport, driving licence, or certificate right now?” Ask whether they can accept:
   - another form of photo ID;
   - proof of address plus another document;
   - a certified copy;
   - an in-person check;
   - a named person or place they approve;
   - a temporary pause while replacements are being ordered.

2. **Ask for the safest way to send documents.**  
   Ask whether they have a secure upload portal, branch appointment, postal address, or named email route. Write down the date, the person or team you spoke to, and exactly what they asked for.

3. **Make one short “missing, have, ordered” list.**  
   On paper or in a phone note, write:
   - missing: passport, driving licence, birth certificate, marriage certificate, death certificate, proof of address, or other document;
   - have: anything still available, even if it is not the first-choice ID;
   - ordered: what you have requested and the date you requested it.  
   Keep this list beside you when you contact banks, government services, insurers, pension providers, or probate-related organisations.

4. **If this is after a death, secure the death-registration paperwork first.**  
   If the death has been registered, ask what official death certificate copies you need for the urgent organisations you are dealing with. If Tell Us Once is available to you, use the unique reference number within 28 days of receiving it; if you cannot use it, ask the registrar what route applies.

5. **Order missing certificate copies through the official route.**  
   For births, deaths, marriages, civil partnerships, adoptions, and similar records registered in England and Wales, use the General Register Office or the relevant local register office. If the event was registered in Scotland or Northern Ireland, use the register service for that part of the UK.

6. **Replace missing photo ID in parallel where it is clearly needed.**  
   If your driving licence is lost, stolen, damaged, or destroyed, use the official replacement route for Great Britain; Northern Ireland has a different route. If your passport is lost or stolen, cancel it as soon as possible before applying for a replacement.

7. **Ask for a temporary workaround while replacements are in progress.**  
   Use plain wording: “I am dealing with a recent bereavement and the documents are missing. I have ordered replacements. What can you accept in the meantime?” Ask whether they can record that ID is being replaced, offer an appointment, extend a deadline, or accept a certified copy later.

8. **If documents may have been stolen or misused, contain the risk.**  
   Make a short list of what is missing and when you noticed. Contact the document issuer, contact the police if documents were stolen, and use the UK fraud-reporting route if someone appears to be using your identity details.

## What can wait
You do not need to solve every organisation’s ID rules today. Deal first with the one or two processes that have real urgency, such as access to money, housing, benefits, funeral or death-registration steps, or a stated deadline.

You do not need to decide probate strategy, family disputes, or long-term paperwork systems now. One clear list and the first replacement requests are enough for this moment.

## Important reassurance
Missing documents are common after a death, a move, a hospital stay, or when papers were kept by someone else. Being stuck at an identity check does not mean you have failed; it usually means the organisation needs a different route or a replacement document.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get unstuck with identity checks and missing documents. Later decisions about probate, estates, disputes, fraud, or complex identity problems may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general practical information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Organisations set their own evidence rules, so ask them to confirm their requirements in writing if anything is unclear.
