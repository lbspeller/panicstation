# What to do if… you think someone may have put a tracking device in your bag, car, or belongings (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a safer public place and avoid going straight home, to work, or somewhere predictable. If you feel at risk right now, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go directly to your home, workplace, or another private place to check first.
- Don’t confront someone you suspect, or message them to warn them.
- Don’t post about it on social media or in public groups.
- Don’t immediately destroy, throw away, or hide any device you find.
- Don’t do a long, focused search in a secluded place.
- Don’t turn off safety alerts before saving screenshots or details, because some alert information may be cleared.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** If you’re out, go into a busy shop, café, staffed building, open police station, or well-lit petrol station. If you’re driving, park somewhere busy, keep your doors locked, and avoid leading anyone to where you live.
2. **If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.** Say where you are and that you believe you may be being tracked and do not feel safe.
3. **If it is not an emergency, contact police on 101 or report online.** Ask for the incident to be logged and write down any reference number.
4. **Choose your next location carefully.** Use a public, staffed place you do not usually go to, such as a large supermarket, transport hub, open police station, or trusted public venue. Avoid your home, workplace, school run, or a friend’s address until you have thought through safety.
5. **Check your phone for unknown tracker information, without relying on it as proof either way.**
   - **iPhone or iPad:** Open **Find My** → **Items** → **Items Detected With You**. If there is an alert, follow the on-screen options, such as map details or **Play Sound** if offered.
   - **Android:** On many devices, open **Settings** → **Safety and emergency** → **Unknown tracker alerts** → **Scan now**. If you cannot see it, search your phone settings for **unknown tracker alerts**. Manual scans and alerts can miss some situations, so a clear scan does not prove there is no tracker.
6. **Do a quick, safe check of obvious places only.**
   - **Bag or belongings:** outer pockets, lining gaps, clipped-on keyrings, under a base insert, inside a rarely used compartment, inside luggage, or inside a coat pocket.
   - **Car:** under seats, seat-back pockets, centre console, boot area by the spare wheel, and any loose object you do not recognise. Also glance for a plug-in device in the diagnostic port, which is often under the dashboard. If checking would mean crawling around, being exposed, or staying somewhere unsafe, stop.
7. **If you find a device, prioritise safety and preserve what you can.**
   - Take photos of it where it was found, including how it was attached or hidden.
   - Note the time, date, and location where you found it.
   - If you need to stop tracking immediately for safety and you can do so calmly, follow the device’s own disable instructions if available.
   - If you do not need to disable it right now, leave it as found and involve police.
   - Keep it secure so it is not lost, such as in a sealed envelope or small container.
8. **Save digital evidence.** Screenshot notifications, maps, scan results, serial numbers, and any device details your phone shows. Store copies somewhere safer, such as sending them to yourself or a trusted person.
9. **Get specialist support if this could be stalking, harassment, or domestic abuse.** The National Stalking Helpline can advise on stalking concerns. The National Domestic Abuse Helpline can help if you are worried about a partner, ex-partner, family member, or someone close to you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fully take your car apart or do an exhaustive search right now.
- You do not need to decide today whether to report, move, replace devices, or support any investigation.
- You do not need to prove who did it before taking safety-first steps.
- A deeper vehicle check, such as through a trusted garage, and longer-term safety planning can wait until you are safer and supported.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to act on a serious concern before you can prove it. Moving to a safer public place, avoiding predictable locations, and saving what you can see are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on immediate safety and avoiding irreversible mistakes. If this is part of stalking, harassment, theft, or domestic abuse, later decisions may need specialist support or legal advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical security, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe or threatened, prioritise immediate safety and contact emergency services.
