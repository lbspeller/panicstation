# What to do if… you see someone suspicious or lingering at your door on your security camera (UK)

## Short answer
Do not open the door. Stay behind a locked door, move away from the doorway, and call 999 if you feel in immediate danger, the person is trying the door, threatening you, or you believe a crime is happening now.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to see what they want, even a crack.
- Do not go outside to confront them.
- Do not argue through the letterbox or stand close to the door while speaking.
- Do not say you are alone, vulnerable, or about to come to the door.
- Do not rely on a door chain if you feel unsafe; it is not a guarantee of safety.
- Do not post the footage publicly in the moment.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Lock the front door, any back or side door, and any internal door between you and the entrance. Move yourself, children, pets, and anyone vulnerable away from the doorway and front windows.
2. **Check without exposing yourself.** Use the doorbell camera, intercom, peephole, or a side window only if you can do so without being seen or putting yourself near the door.
3. **Make the area visible if you can do it safely.** Turn on outside lights or indoor hallway lights. If you have a safe alarm or siren option, use it only if you believe it will not escalate the situation.
4. **Speak only if it feels safe.** Use the video doorbell, intercom, or a closed door. Keep it short: “I can’t open the door. Please leave.” Then stop engaging.
5. **Call 999 if there is immediate risk.** Call 999 if the person is trying the door or windows, threatening you, carrying a weapon, refusing to leave while you feel unsafe, or you believe a crime is happening now.
6. **Use a silent 999 call only when you cannot speak safely.** Call 999, listen to the operator, and speak or whisper if you can. If you cannot speak, cough or tap if safe, and press 55 if prompted from a mobile so the call can be treated as a genuine emergency.
7. **Use 101 if it is suspicious but not immediate.** Call 101 or use your local police online reporting route if the person has left, there is no immediate danger, and you want to report what happened or ask for advice.
8. **If you are in a flat or shared building, use the building’s safety layer.** Contact the concierge, building security, a housemate, or a trusted neighbour instead of going into the corridor or shared entrance yourself.
9. **Save details only if it is safe and quick.** Save the camera clip, note the time, what the person did, and which way they went. Do not stay near the door to collect more footage.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it was a scam, casing, mistaken address, or harmless visit.
- You do not need to identify the person yourself.
- You do not need to post footage online.
- You do not need to change locks, buy equipment, or make long-term security decisions while the person may still be nearby.

## Important reassurance
A door alert can feel frightening, especially if you are alone or it is late. Staying inside, reducing contact, and asking for help when the behaviour feels threatening is a sensible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next short period only. Later decisions, such as repeated incidents, harassment concerns, building security, or lock changes, may need specialist help from the police, your landlord, building management, or a qualified security professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not personal safety, legal, policing, or professional advice. If you think anyone is in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.
