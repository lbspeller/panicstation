# What to do if… your garage door is stuck open and you cannot close it to secure your home (UK)

## Short answer
Secure the home side first, especially any door between the garage and the house. Then switch off or unplug the garage door opener if it is safe to reach. If you cannot close the door safely, make the garage less tempting from outside and contact urgent help.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand under a partially open door or try to hold it while someone else presses the button.
- Don’t touch, loosen, or adjust springs, lift cables, brackets, rollers, or other high-tension parts.
- Don’t keep cycling the opener if it is struggling, jerking, grinding, or reversing unexpectedly.
- Don’t force the door down against resistance or put fingers into the tracks.
- Don’t leave the door between the garage and the house unlocked.
- Don’t start drilling, fitting locks, or making changes to the door mechanism while rushed or tired.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and check for immediate risk.** Keep children and pets away from the opening, tracks, and moving parts. If you think someone is on the property, a break-in is happening, or anyone is in immediate danger, go inside if you can, lock the main house doors, and call **999**.

2. **Secure the house side first.**
   - If there is a door from the garage into the home, shut and lock it.
   - Bring car keys, spare keys, garage remotes, and fobs into the house and out of sight.
   - Lock any other outside doors or windows that are reachable from the garage.

3. **Stop the opener moving unexpectedly.**
   - If it is safe to reach, switch off or unplug the garage door opener.
   - If you are confident which circuit controls it, you can switch that circuit off at the consumer unit.
   - Keep remotes out of vehicles parked outside or in the garage.

4. **Do one quick, low-risk check for obvious causes.**
   - Move easy obstructions away from the floor line and track area, such as a bin, tool, box, or debris.
   - If there are photo-eye sensors near the floor, gently wipe them and make sure nothing is blocking the beam.
   - Look from a safe distance for a snapped cable, loose spring, bent track, or door hanging at an angle. If you see any of these, stop trying to move the door.

5. **Only try the manual release if you already know how yours works and the door looks stable.**
   - Do not pull the release while standing under the door.
   - Try one gentle manual close only if the door is straight in its tracks and not dropping, slanting, or shaking.
   - If it feels unusually heavy, binds, drops, or moves unevenly, stop and step back.

6. **If the garage still cannot be closed, reduce what can be seen or taken quickly.**
   - Move small high-value items you can carry safely, such as bikes, tools, keys, chargers, and visible boxes, into the house.
   - Put large items out of sight if you can do that without staying exposed.
   - Turn on exterior lights and close curtains or blinds that give a view into the garage.
   - If you already have a fitted internal locking bar, ground anchor, garage defender, alarm contact, or hasp system, use it if it does not put you under the door or near the mechanism.

7. **Get help without taking more risks.**
   - Contact a garage-door repairer or locksmith that offers urgent call-outs.
   - If the property cannot be secured and you feel at risk, call **101** or use your local police force’s online contact route for non-emergency advice.
   - If the situation becomes an immediate danger or a crime starts happening, call **999**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact mechanical fault now.
- You do not need to choose a new opener, new door, or permanent security upgrade now.
- You do not need to decide about insurance now unless there has been a confirmed break-in, theft, or damage.
- You do not need to tidy or fully reorganise the garage before making the home side secure.

## Important reassurance
A stuck-open garage door feels exposing, but the first useful move is not to fix everything at once. Locking the internal door, keeping keys and remotes inside, disabling the opener if safe, and reducing visible valuables can lower the immediate risk while you arrange proper help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate security risk and prevent injury. Later decisions about repair, replacement, locks, alarms, insurance, or crime reporting may need help from a suitable professional, your insurer, or the police.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, electrical, engineering, insurance, or other professional advice. Garage doors can be dangerous if unstable, unbalanced, or damaged. If you believe a crime is in progress or anyone is in immediate danger, call **999**.
