# What to do if… you realise you are being recorded in a public place and the person won’t stop when asked (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all interaction with the person filming and move immediately to a staffed, well-lit, or crowded area to create a physical barrier between you.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to touch the person, grab their device, or block the lens with your hands.
- Do not follow, corner, or engage in a prolonged argument about the legality of filming.
- Do not share your name, address, or personal details while the camera is active.
- Do not go into an isolated area (like a quiet side street or empty park) to escape.
- Do not upload footage of the person to social media while the situation is active, as this may escalate their behavior.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed or secure location immediately.** Put physical distance and barriers (reception desks, shop counters, or security barriers) between you and the person filming.
2. **State your boundary once and stop engaging.** Use a single, clear phrase like "I do not consent to being filmed; I am leaving now," then turn away and do not respond to further comments.
3. **Alert staff or security in your current vicinity.**
   - In shops or venues: Inform a member of staff that you are being followed or harassed by someone filming you and ask for a safe place to wait.
   - On public transport: Move toward the driver, guard, or a group of other passengers.
4. **Use the British Transport Police (BTP) text service if on the rail network.** **Text 61016 with your location, train service, and a description of the person.**
   - This service is free on all major UK mobile networks.
   - If the situation is an emergency or you are being physically blocked, **call 999** instead.
5. **Call 999 if the behavior involves threats or follows you.** Treat the incident as an active safety threat if the person continues to follow you after you have moved away or if they use abusive language.
6. **Report the incident as a Public Order or Harassment offence.** If the filming is targeted at you because of your sex, mention this specifically to the police, as the Protection from Sex-based Harassment in Public Act 2026 provides specific protections for this.
7. **Request that local CCTV be retained.** Ask venue staff or security to log the time and location of the incident and to save any relevant security footage for potential police inspection.
8. **Record a description of the individual privately once you are safe.** Note their approximate height, clothing, hair color, and any specific words they used.

## What can wait
- Determining the specific legal "right" to film in public spaces.
- Checking social media platforms to see if the video has been uploaded.
- Formulating a long-term plan for privacy settings or legal action.
- Demanding that the person deletes the footage in the moment.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a surge of adrenaline, anger, or vulnerability when being recorded without your consent. Taking steps to reach a staffed area and involving authorities is a proactive way to manage your safety and does not mean you have done anything wrong.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and harm prevention in a public encounter. It does not cover long-term legal remedies, civil privacy claims, or workplace-specific filming policies.

## Important note
In the UK, while filming in public is generally legal, it becomes a criminal matter if the behavior is intended to cause harassment, alarm, or distress. Always prioritize your physical exit from the situation over proving a legal point.
