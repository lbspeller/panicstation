# What to do if… a floor drain cover lifts or rattles during heavy rain and you suspect backpressure in the system (UK)

## Short answer
Assume a sewer surcharge: immediately stop using all water in the property and contact your water company's emergency line to report internal or external flooding.

## Do not do these things
- Don't flush toilets, run taps, or use the washing machine "to see if it clears," as this adds volume to a system already at capacity.
- Don't remove the drain cover or attempt to investigate the pipe with tools, as trapped air or water under pressure can release suddenly.
- Don't touch electrical switches or appliances if you are standing on a wet floor or if water is near electrical outlets.
- Don't enter any flooded area where the water depth prevents you from seeing the floor, as manhole covers may have been displaced by pressure.
- Don't assume the water is clean rainwater if it is gurgling up from a drain; treat all backflow as contaminated sewage.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all water use immediately to reduce pressure on the internal system.**
   - Ensure no one in the property flushes toilets, showers, or runs appliances like dishwashers.
2. **Locate your water company’s 24-hour emergency number and report the incident.**
   - Tell them if the flooding is "internal" (inside the building) as they prioritize these calls.
   - Clarify if there is a foul smell or visible debris (toilet paper), which indicates a foul sewer surcharge.
3. **Isolate the affected area and keep people and pets away.**
   - Close the door to the room with the lifting drain to contain potential spray or odors.
   - If the drain is in a communal area or basement, prevent access entirely.
4. **Isolate the electricity supply only if it is safe to do so.**
   - **Switch off the power at the consumer unit (fuse box) ONLY if you are standing in a dry area and the unit itself shows no signs of water ingress, arcing, or smoke.**
   - If there is water inside the consumer unit or near it, do not touch it and wait for professional help.
5. **Move items away from the immediate vicinity of the drain.**
   - Move electronics, furniture, and absorbent materials (rugs, cardboard) to a higher floor or onto stable, elevated surfaces.
6. **Steady the cover only if it is rattling without current overflow.**
   - **Place a heavy, stable object (like a sandbag or heavy bucket) over the cover to prevent it from becoming fully displaced.**
   - Do not attempt to create an airtight seal or wedge the cover shut, as the pressure must be allowed to vent somewhere.
7. **Verify if the issue is originating from the public highway.**
   - Check if road gullies or street manholes are also surcharging; if the flooding is primarily from the street, contact your local council’s Highways department.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the blockage is in your private pipe or the public sewer before calling for help; the water company will assess this.
- You do not need to begin cleaning or disinfecting the area until the surcharge has subsided and the pressure has equalized.
- You do not need to take detailed photos for insurance purposes until the immediate physical risk is managed.

## Important reassurance
A rattling or lifting drain cover is a mechanical response to air and water pressure within the pipe network. It serves as an early indicator that the system is overwhelmed by heavy rainfall. Stopping water use immediately is the most effective way you can prevent further pressure from building up inside your own property.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Once the weather event has passed, a specialist drainage survey or a follow-up from the water company may be required to inspect for structural damage or blockages.

## Important note
This information is for general emergency stabilization and does not constitute a technical diagnosis of your plumbing system. If you suspect an immediate electrical hazard or if water levels are rising rapidly toward living areas, evacuate the property and call 999.
