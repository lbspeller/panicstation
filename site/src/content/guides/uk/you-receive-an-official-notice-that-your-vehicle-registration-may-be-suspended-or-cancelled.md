# What to do if… you receive an official notice that your vehicle registration may be suspended or cancelled (UK)

## Short answer
Use official GOV.UK routes to verify the notice, write down the deadline, and check the vehicle’s tax, SORN, MOT, and insurance status. Until those basic checks are clear, do not use the vehicle on roads or in public places.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click payment links, scan QR codes, or give personal or bank details from a message or letter until you have checked the route independently on GOV.UK.
- Do not ignore the deadline; missed deadlines may lead to penalties or enforcement action depending on the notice.
- Do not use the vehicle on roads or in public places if you cannot confirm that insurance is active today.
- Do not use the vehicle on roads if official checks show it is untaxed, recorded as SORN, or missing a required MOT.
- Do not assume it is about a driving offence; it may be about keeper details, vehicle tax, SORN, insurance records, or a database mismatch.
- Do not send documents before asking what evidence is needed; keep photos or copies of anything you send.

## What to do now
1. Make a quick notice pack.

   Take clear photos of the full notice, all pages, the envelope, any reference numbers, and the deadline. Write down the date you received it.

2. Check the vehicle’s official status.

   Use official services to check and save screenshots of:
   - vehicle tax or SORN status
   - vehicle details
   - MOT status, if the vehicle needs an MOT
   - insurance status, using your policy documents and the recognised insurance database check route

   If a tax or SORN application was only just approved, note that online records can sometimes take up to 2 working days to update.

3. Check the common mismatch points.

   - Insurance: confirm the policy is active today and that the registration mark and vehicle details are correct. If you changed plates, changed vehicle, renewed, or corrected an error, ask your insurer when the update should show on askMID or the insurance record they use.
   - SORN or off-road status: if the vehicle is off the public road and not being used, check whether it is shown as SORN.
   - Keeper details: check your V5C log book. If you moved and did not update the V5C address, you may have missed earlier reminders or notices.
   - Vehicle details: compare the registration mark, make, colour, and V5C issue date with what the official vehicle check shows.

4. Contact DVLA through GOV.UK, not through an unverified link.

   Use the GOV.UK Contact DVLA page to find the right contact route. Ask:
   - what record triggered the notice
   - what deadline applies
   - what action is needed now
   - what evidence they will accept
   - where to send it or which online route to use

   Write down the date, time, department, and what you were told.

5. If the notice mentions an Insurance Advisory Letter or Continuous Insurance Enforcement, focus on insurance or SORN.

   In England, Scotland and Wales, continuous insurance enforcement can apply when a vehicle appears to be uninsured and is not recorded as SORN. The stabilising step is usually to make sure the vehicle is correctly insured, or if it is genuinely off-road and unused, correctly recorded as SORN. If you are in Northern Ireland, ask the official contact route which rule and office apply before relying on a continuous insurance enforcement step. If you believe the record is wrong, ask what proof is needed and keep copies of what you provide.

6. Reduce the risk while you clarify.

   Do not use the vehicle on roads or in public places unless you can confirm insurance is active, the official check shows the vehicle is taxed rather than SORN, and the MOT is valid where required. If any of those checks are unclear, keep the vehicle parked off-road if you can while you confirm what is needed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain, contest, or escalate; first find out exactly what record triggered the notice.
- You do not need to write a long explanation right now; focus on the deadline, the vehicle status, and the specific action required.
- You do not need to work out who caused the problem until the immediate registration, tax, SORN, MOT, and insurance position is clearer.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel blindsided by an official vehicle notice. Some notices are triggered by record issues such as address changes, tax or SORN status, insurance database timing, or vehicle-detail mismatches. A calm sequence of verify, check status, contact the right route, and fix the mismatch can reduce the chance of avoidable enforcement.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to verify the notice, avoid scams, and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions, especially if enforcement or court action has already started, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Vehicle notices, deadlines, and consequences vary, so use official routes to confirm what applies to your notice.
