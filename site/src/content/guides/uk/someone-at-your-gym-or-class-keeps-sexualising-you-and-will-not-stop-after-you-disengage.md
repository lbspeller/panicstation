# What to do if… someone at your gym or class keeps sexualising you and will not stop after you disengage (UK)

## Short answer
Go to a staff member or the reception desk immediately and ask them to intervene and keep the person away from you. If you feel trapped or the situation escalates, leave the premises and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not move to isolated areas like empty studios, quiet corridors, or unlit sections of the car park to avoid them.
- Do not agree to discuss the issue privately or "go outside" with the person.
- Do not feel obligated to continue your workout or finish a class if you feel unsafe.
- Do not engage in further explanations once you have stated your boundary.
- Do not attempt to confront the person alone if they are being persistent or intimidating.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a highly visible area with witnesses.** Go to the main reception, a staffed desk, or stand directly next to the instructor in a class setting.
2. **Issue one final clear boundary if it is safe to do so.** Use a short, direct statement such as: "Stop. I do not want to talk to you. Do not approach me again."
3. **Alert staff using specific, clear language.**
   - Tell them: "I am being sexually harassed by another member and I need you to intervene now."
   - If the venue uses the "Ask for Angela" scheme, you can use this phrase to signal you need help discreetly.
4. **Ask the staff for immediate protective actions.** 
   - Request that they stay with you until the person has left the area.
   - Ask them to tell the person to stay away from you for the remainder of your visit.
5. **Request that a manager logs the incident in writing before you leave.** Ensure the record includes:
   - Date, time, and specific location within the gym.
   - A brief description of what was said or done.
   - The name or a physical description of the person.
   - Names of any staff or members who witnessed the behaviour.
6. **Ask about your rights regarding your membership and safety.** 
   - Inquire if the gym can facilitate a class transfer or time-slot change.
   - Ask for a staff escort to your vehicle or public transport if you feel unsafe leaving alone.
7. **Secure any digital evidence without initiating new contact.** 
   - Screenshot any unwanted direct messages (DMs), texts, or social media comments.
   - Ask the gym to preserve CCTV footage from the specific time and location of the incident.
8. **Report the behaviour to the police if it is a "course of conduct".** 
   - Harassment is generally defined as two or more related incidents that cause alarm or distress.
   - Use **101** or the online reporting tool on your local police force's website for non-emergencies.
   - Report anonymously via **Crimestoppers** on 0800 555 111 if you prefer not to give your details.
9. **Access specialist support for safety planning.** 
   - **Victim Support:** Call 0808 16 89 111 (24/7) for confidential help regardless of whether you report to the police.
   - **24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line:** Call 0808 500 2222 if you have experienced any form of sexual harassment or are unsure what occurred.
   - **National Stalking Helpline:** Call 0808 802 0300 if the person’s behaviour is fixated or follows you outside the gym.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to cancel your membership or move to a different gym.
- You do not need to provide a full, detailed witness statement immediately; capturing the basic facts with staff is sufficient for the first hour.
- You do not need to respond to any apologies or messages from the person involved.

## Important reassurance
Physical and emotional reactions such as freezing, feeling "polite," or struggling to find words are common responses to harassment. These reactions do not mean you consented to the behaviour or that you are overreacting to the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate incident at a gym or fitness class. Long-term safety planning or legal action may require further consultation with the specialist services listed above.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Gyms have a duty under the Equality Act to take reasonable steps to prevent harassment of their members. If you are in immediate danger, always call 999.
