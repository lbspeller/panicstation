# What to do if… an outlet sparks or flashes when you plug something in (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the socket immediately and do not attempt to re-insert the plug. If you see smoke, flames, or a persistent burning smell, evacuate the building and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don't try the plug again "one more time" to see if it sparks again.
- Don't touch the socket or the plug if there are visible scorch marks or if it feels warm.
- Don't use water on any electrical spark or fire.
- Don't ignore the spark if it was yellow / white, loud, or accompanied by a smell.
- Don't repeatedly reset a circuit breaker or RCD that has tripped after a spark.
- Don't attempt to unscrew the socket faceplate unless you are a qualified electrician.
- Don't use adaptors or extension leads to bypass a faulty wall socket.

## What to do now
1. **Remove yourself and others from the immediate area if there is active smoke or fire.** 
   - **Evacuate the property** and close doors behind you to contain the air.
   - **Call 999** once you are in a safe location.
2. **Isolate the power at the consumer unit if it is safe to do so.** 
   - **Locate your consumer unit** (fuse box) and flip the main switch to the 'OFF' position if you are unsure which specific circuit is affected.
   - **Do not touch the consumer unit** if your hands are wet or if the unit itself is making a buzzing sound.
3. **Unplug the appliance only if there is no heat, smoke, or visible damage.** 
   - **Grip the plastic plug housing** firmly rather than pulling on the cable.
   - **Check the plug pins** for any signs of blackening, pitting, or melted plastic.
4. **Identify the type of spark you observed to help a professional later.** 
   - **Note if the spark was a tiny blue flash** (often normal arcing for high-draw appliances like vacuums) or a large yellow/white spark (indicating a fault).
   - **Check for sensory cues** such as a fishy smell, a smell of ozone (like burnt wire), or a persistent buzzing/crackling sound.
5. **Keep the affected circuit switched off.** 
   - **Leave the breaker in the 'OFF' position** even if the rest of the house's power is back on.
   - **Place a piece of tape** over the socket to ensure no one else tries to use it.
6. **Find a Registered Competent Person to inspect the installation.** 
   - **Search for an electrician** registered with a government-approved scheme such as NICEIC or NAPIT.
   - **Request an inspection** of the socket terminals and the internal wiring of the appliance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the technical cause (e.g., loose terminals vs. carbon buildup) yourself.
- You do not need to replace the appliance immediately until its plug has been inspected.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of repairs until the hazard is safely isolated.

## Important reassurance
It is common for a small blue spark to occur when a high-power device is plugged in while switched on; this is known as arcing. However, taking the precaution to isolate the power when you are unsure is the correct safety-first action and prevents potential electrical fires.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for domestic electrical sparking. It does not replace a formal Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) or a professional fault-finding service.

## Important note
Electrical faults can escalate rapidly into fires. If you cannot safely reach your consumer unit or if there is any visible smoke, prioritize life safety by evacuating and contacting the fire brigade. All electrical repairs must be performed by a registered professional to comply with UK Building Regulations.
