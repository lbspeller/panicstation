# What to do if… a travel provider requires a printed document and you only have a digital screenshot (UK)

## Short answer
Get the official printable version if you can, then print it using the provider’s desk or kiosk, a station machine, your hotel, a library, or a print shop. If time is tight, print the clearest unedited screenshot as a fallback while you ask the provider what they will accept.

## Do not do these things
- Do not edit, crop, “enhance”, or rebuild the screenshot to make it look like an official document.
- Do not rely on battery life, mobile signal, or one device if the provider has said “printed only”.
- Do not cancel and rebook in a rush unless the provider confirms that rebooking is the only way to re-issue the document.
- Do not hand over your phone unlocked for longer than necessary.
- Do not print a page that cuts off your name, reference number, dates, barcode, or QR code.
- Do not assume a printed screenshot will be rejected if staff say they will accept it; print it cleanly and keep trying to get the official version.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the exact acceptance rule.** Say: “What exactly will you accept: the original PDF or permit, a printed email confirmation, or any printout showing my name, reference and the full code?” Ask staff to re-send the official printable PDF or confirmation email immediately.

2. **Recover the original file from the source.**
   - In the provider’s app or website, look for **Manage booking**, **My trips**, **Documents**, **Print**, **Download PDF**, **Voucher**, **Permit**, or **Ticket**.
   - In email, search your booking reference with **pdf**, **voucher**, **permit**, **ticket**, and **confirmation**. Check junk and spam.
   - If you booked through an agent or comparison site, contact both the seller and the operator, because either one may hold the printable document.

3. **Use a provider printing route if one exists.**
   - For flights, go to the airline’s check-in kiosk or staffed check-in desk and ask for a boarding pass, permit, or confirmation reprint. Availability and fees can vary by airline, airport, and ticket type.
   - For rail tickets that require station collection, use the ticket collection reference and the card requested in your booking instructions. Bring the payment card if you have it, because some operators require it.
   - If a machine will not issue the document, go straight to the staffed desk or ticket office if there is one.

4. **Print locally if you are away from the provider.**
   - Ask your hotel, hostel, host, or nearby business centre to print the PDF or email confirmation.
   - Use a public library that offers printing from a phone, tablet, email, or USB drive.
   - Use a print or copy shop if the document needs a clearer printout than a hotel or library can provide.

5. **Make the printout easy to check.**
   - Print two copies if you can.
   - Check that the page shows your full name, provider name, travel date, time, booking or permit number, and the full barcode or QR code.
   - Use portrait or landscape settings that keep the code complete and readable. Do not shrink the page so much that the code becomes hard to scan.

6. **If you cannot get an official printable version quickly, escalate at the counter.**
   - Show the screenshot, booking reference, payment confirmation, and ID if requested.
   - Ask staff to reprint, re-issue, or mark in their system that your document has been checked.
   - If they still insist on paper, ask where the nearest working print option is and what exact wording or file they need printed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who is at fault right now.
- You do not need to write a complaint before you travel; save screenshots of chats, emails, and failed downloads for later.
- You do not need to reorganise every travel document. Focus on producing this one acceptable printout.

## Important reassurance
This is a common travel problem, and it often has a practical fix: an official PDF, a reprint at a desk or kiosk, or a clean paper copy with the full reference and code visible. Staff have less to decide when the printout is complete, readable, and linked to your booking.

## Scope note
These are first steps for dealing with a printed-document problem today. Refunds, compensation, formal complaints, and policy disputes may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Rules vary by provider, route, ticket type, and country, so ask the provider to confirm what they will accept.
