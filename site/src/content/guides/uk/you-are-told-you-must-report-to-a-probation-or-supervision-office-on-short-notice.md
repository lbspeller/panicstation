# What to do if… you are told you must report to a probation or supervision office on short notice (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your assigned Probation Practitioner or the local Probation Service office using a verified number from your official paperwork to confirm the instruction is genuine and mandatory, then attend as directed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the message or "wait and see," even if the notice period is less than 24 hours or feels impossible to meet.
- Do not assume a text or call is genuine if the sender cannot confirm your full name, your practitioner’s name, or the specific office address you usually attend.
- Do not consume alcohol or non-prescription drugs before attending, as presenting as "unfit" at an appointment can be treated as a breach or a failed contact.
- Do not miss the appointment without speaking to a member of staff first to explain your situation.
- Do not bring items that are prohibited by your licence conditions or that could be interpreted as weapons.
- Do not go into the office with an aggressive or argumentative tone; focus on the practical facts of your attendance.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the instruction using a trusted contact method (2–5 minutes).**  
   **Call the official number for your Probation Service office or your specific Probation Practitioner found on your previous appointment cards or letters.** Do not rely solely on the number that just called or texted you. Ask: "I have received a short-notice instruction to report; can you confirm the time, location, and which officer this is with?"

2. **Check the communication for specific official markers.**  
   **Ensure any written text or email includes your correct name and refers to the specific office address where you are managed.** Genuine instructions from the Probation Service (HMPPS) will usually come from a gov.uk email address or a professional landline.

3. **If you have a physical barrier to attending, explain it immediately and propose a specific alternative.**  
   **Clearly state the specific reason you cannot arrive on time (e.g., "The next bus does not arrive until 3pm") and offer to attend at the earliest possible time later that day or the next morning.** If you cannot attend in person, ask if a "phone check-in" or video link is acceptable as an interim contact to prove you are not "absconding."

4. **Preserve evidence of your efforts to comply and any reasons for delay.**  
   **Take a screenshot of your call logs showing you tried to contact the office, and keep any proof of travel delays or medical issues (such as a bus ticket or a photo of a doctor's appointment card).** This evidence is essential if the Probation Service later considers issuing a formal warning or starting breach proceedings.

5. **Prepare for your arrival at the office to ensure you are processed quickly.**  
   **Take your photo ID, a copy of your licence or community order paperwork, and a mobile phone that is fully charged but switched to silent.** Arriving 10–15 minutes early helps prevent a late arrival from being recorded as a "failure to attend."

6. **Create a traceable record of your contact attempts if you cannot reach anyone.**  
   **If the phone line is busy, leave a clear voicemail and follow up with an email or text (if you have an official contact mobile) stating your name, that you are trying to confirm the report time, and your availability.** Keep a record of these messages as proof that you did not ignore the instruction.

7. **Contact your legal representative if you are threatened with "Fixed Term Recall" or "Breach."**  
   **If a practitioner states they are starting a recall to prison or a court breach because of this short-notice event, call your solicitor immediately to inform them of your attempts to comply.** If you are on licence, consequences for missing an appointment can result in an immediate return to custody.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle disputes about the "reasonableness" of the short notice right now; handle the attendance first and complain later.
- You do not need to write a long statement explaining your life circumstances; keep initial communications brief and focused on the time and date of reporting.
- You do not need to request a change of Probation Practitioner during this crisis moment.
- You do not need to decide on long-term legal strategies until the immediate reporting requirement is resolved or an alternative is agreed.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a sudden instruction to report is a high-stress event, but making prompt, traceable contact is the most effective way to protect yourself. Most practitioners prefer a phone call explaining a delay over no contact at all. Taking these steps demonstrates that you are still engaging with your supervision and reduces the risk of being marked as non-compliant.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization when receiving a sudden instruction to report to the Probation Service in the UK. It does not cover long-term breach defense or complex legal appeals. Later decisions regarding your supervision may require specialist legal advice from a criminal defense solicitor.

## Important note
This guide provides general practical information and is not legal advice. Probation requirements are governed by specific licence conditions or court orders which vary by individual. Always verify instructions directly with your official Probation Practitioner and keep detailed records of all interactions.
