# What to do if… your irrigation or sprinkler system turns on unexpectedly and won’t shut off (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the water to the irrigation system: close the irrigation supply shut-off if you have one. If you cannot find it quickly, turn off your home’s internal stop tap to limit flooding and damage.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep switching the controller on and off while water is still running.
- Don’t force a stiff stop tap, stop valve, or irrigation valve.
- Don’t open or dismantle an irrigation valve while it is pressurised.
- Don’t dig around valve boxes or pipes in a panic.
- Don’t touch outdoor electrics with wet hands or while standing in pooled water.
- Don’t step into pooled water if it may be touching sockets, extension leads, pumps, or lighting.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** Watch for slippery paving, spraying jets, and water near outdoor sockets, pumps, lights, or extension leads. Move children and pets away from the spray and any pooling.

2. **Turn off the irrigation controller power if you can do this safely.**
   - If it is a plug-in timer or controller, switch it off or unplug it with dry hands from a dry position.
   - If it is hard-wired, use the safe local switch or fused spur only if you know which one it is.
   - This may stop the system if the problem is electrical or programming-related, but water can still keep flowing if a valve is stuck open.

3. **Shut off the water supply to the irrigation system.**
   - Look for a dedicated shut-off feeding the sprinklers or irrigation pipework.
   - It may be near where the irrigation pipe branches off, near an outside tap, in a garage or utility area, beside a valve box, or near the pipe entering the garden.
   - Turn the valve slowly to OFF. Many wheel valves close clockwise; many quarter-turn valves are off when the handle is across the pipe.

4. **If you cannot quickly find a dedicated irrigation shut-off, use your internal stop tap.**
   - This will usually shut off water to the whole property.
   - It is commonly under the kitchen sink, in a kitchen cupboard, garage, utility room, downstairs toilet, cellar, under stairs, near the front door, or close to where the water pipe enters the property.
   - Turn it slowly clockwise to close. Do not force it. It may take a few minutes for water already in the pipework to stop flowing.

5. **If only one zone is running and you can safely reach the valve box, check for a manual-open valve.**
   - Look for the valve serving the running zone.
   - A solenoid often looks like a small cylinder with two wires on top of the valve.
   - If the solenoid has been loosened, gently hand-tighten it clockwise until snug.
   - If there is a small bleed screw or bleed knob on top of the valve and it is loose, gently hand-tighten it.
   - If you are unsure, leave the valve alone and keep the water isolated.

6. **Limit damage while the water is off.**
   - Move valuables, tools, plant pots, and stored items away from the wet area.
   - If water has entered a house, garage, or shed, keep people away from wet electrical fittings.
   - If it is safe to reach the consumer unit from a dry position, switch off affected circuits or the main switch only if you are confident what to switch. If water is in or near the consumer unit, keep clear and get urgent professional help.

7. **Get help if it will not stay off or you cannot isolate it safely.**
   - If you rent, report it to your landlord, letting agent, council, housing association, or repairs line as an urgent water issue.
   - If you own the property or can arrange repairs, contact a plumber or irrigation engineer.
   - If the internal stop tap will not turn or will not shut the water off, contact a plumber. If you cannot locate or use an external stop tap, contact your water supplier.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact fault now.
- You do not need to reprogram the controller, reset the whole system, or replace parts while water is running.
- You do not need to open the irrigation valve, clean the diaphragm, or test the solenoid in the moment.
- Cleanup, drying, pressure testing, and schedule changes can wait until the water is reliably isolated and the area is safe.

## Important reassurance
This can happen even when the controller is off, because a sprinkler or irrigation valve can be stuck open or left manually open. The useful first step is not to solve the whole system; it is to stop the water, reduce slip and electrical risk, and make space to get help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about valves, solenoids, diaphragms, wiring, controller settings, backflow prevention devices, or repairs may need a plumber, irrigation engineer, electrician, landlord, insurer, or water supplier.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not professional plumbing, electrical, legal, insurance, or safety advice. If you cannot safely isolate the water, if water is affecting electrics, or if flooding is escalating, keep people away from the hazard and get urgent professional help.
