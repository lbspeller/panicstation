# What to do if… a funeral provider pressures you for a same-day decision about embalming or refrigeration (UK)

## Short answer
You can usually say: **“I do not authorise embalming today. Please keep them in refrigerated care, or confirm in writing where refrigerated care is being provided.”** Ask for the request, the reason, and the costs in writing before you sign anything.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t agree to embalming just because you feel rushed, embarrassed, or afraid of doing the wrong thing.
- Don’t assume embalming is required for burial or cremation in the UK; ask separately if overseas transport is involved.
- Don’t sign an authorisation or care-of-the-deceased form in a hurry without checking whether it includes embalming or another invasive procedure.
- Don’t accept a vague statement that embalming is the only way to delay decisions; ask about refrigerated care and where it will be provided.
- Don’t choose a full funeral package today just to secure basic care of the deceased.

## What to do now
1. **Set a clear boundary.**  
   Say: **“I am not authorising embalming today. Refrigeration only while we decide. Please confirm that in writing.”**

2. **Ask for written confirmation before embalming happens.**  
   Ask them to confirm by email or text: **“No embalming will take place unless I give informed written permission.”** Also ask them to contact you before any emergency invasive procedure is considered.

3. **Ask why they say the decision is needed today.**  
   Use: **“What exactly changes today: condition, timing, viewing, transport, your facilities, or your internal policy?”** Then ask: **“Is refrigerated care sufficient for now, and where will it be provided?”**

4. **Find out where your person is right now.**  
   Ask: **“Are they in your care, at a hospital mortuary, with a coroner’s office, or somewhere else?”** If they are not yet with the funeral provider, contact that place directly and ask what storage is in place and what timing applies.

5. **Ask for the price lists and terms before agreeing to paid extras.**  
   Ask for the **Standardised Price List**, the **Additional Options Price List**, and the provider’s **terms of business**. Ask which charges start now, which charges need signed authorisation, and which charges apply if you choose refrigeration only today.

6. **Ask for an itemised comparison.**  
   Ask for separate prices for refrigerated care, embalming, viewing preparation, out-of-hours care, transport, and any daily storage charges. Ask them not to bundle these into a package while you are deciding.

7. **Use the least-irreversible option if you need time.**  
   If you must choose something now, refrigerated care is usually the option that buys time while keeping more choices open.

8. **If the pressure continues, ask about transfer.**  
   Say: **“Please send me the process, timing, and costs to transfer them to another funeral director.”** Write down the name of the person you spoke to, the time, and exactly what they said.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today on coffin type, flowers, cars, notices, order of service, music, catering, or the full service style.
- You do not need to choose a full funeral package today to make sure the deceased is cared for.
- You do not need to decide today about viewing unless the provider gives you clear written reasons why timing affects that choice.

## Important reassurance
Being asked for a same-day decision while you are shocked or grieving can feel overwhelming. You are allowed to slow the conversation down, ask for refrigerated care, and ask for written costs and reasons before authorising embalming.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and prevent an irreversible decision under pressure. Later decisions about timing, viewing, transport, faith requirements, family disagreement, complaints, or changing funeral director may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Rules and practical options can vary by provider, location, membership scheme, and circumstances, including coroner involvement or overseas transport. If anything is unclear, ask for written confirmation before authorising embalming.
