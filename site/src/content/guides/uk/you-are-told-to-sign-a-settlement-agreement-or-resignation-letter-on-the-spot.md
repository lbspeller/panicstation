# What to do if… you are told to sign a settlement agreement or resignation letter on the spot (UK)

## Short answer
Request the exact end date and reason for cancellation in writing from your employer immediately and verify the status of your cover directly with the insurance or pension provider.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume verbal notification of cancellation is final until you have checked your latest payslip and contacted the provider.
- Do not sign any document acknowledging a change in terms or "restricted variation" under pressure.
- Do not stop necessary medical treatment or prescriptions before checking your entitlement to NHS care or grace periods.
- Do not delete digital evidence such as portal "inactive" screenshots or automated termination emails.
- Do not engage in workplace-wide speculation or social media posts while the cause of the issue is unverified.

## What to do now
1. **Request a written explanation of the cancellation from HR or Payroll immediately.**
   - Request the specific benefit names affected (e.g., Private Medical Insurance, Life Assurance, Income Protection).
   - Request the effective date the cover ceased and the internal reason code (e.g., administrative error, policy renewal failure, eligibility change).
   - Request confirmation of whether dependants or family members were also removed.

2. **Download and save your last three payslips along with your P11D or P60.**
   - Check if deductions for the specific benefit are still being taken from your gross or net pay.
   - Look for "Salary Sacrifice" lines that match the cost of the benefit.
   - Verify if the employer's pension contribution matches the percentage stated in your contract.

3. **Contact the insurance or pension provider directly to verify your policy status.**
   - Provide your member number or National Insurance number and ask if the policy is "Active," "Lapsed," or "Cancelled."
   - Ask for the date the instruction to terminate was received and the name of the requesting entity (usually your employer or their broker).
   - Inquire if there is a "grace period" for claims made during an administrative gap.

4. **Use NHS 111 or the NHS App for urgent health needs while the cover issue is resolved.**
   - Access the NHS App or 111.nhs.uk for a clinical assessment if you have a non-emergency health concern.
   - Call 999 or attend Accident & Emergency (A&E) only for life-threatening situations such as chest pain, heavy bleeding, or loss of consciousness.

5. **Submit a formal request for reinstatement if the cancellation is confirmed as an error.**
   - Ask for written confirmation that the cover will be backdated to ensure no gap in protection.
   - Request a "letter of indemnity" or similar assurance if you have ongoing medical treatment that requires authorization.

6. **Identify if the change is a "restricted variation" under the Employment Rights Act 2025.**
   - Ask your employer if the removal of the benefit is a proposed permanent change to your contractual terms.
   - Request the consultation documents or notice period provided for this change.
   - Note that from 2026, enhanced protections exist against unilateral changes to core contractual benefits.

7. **Verify pension contributions and prepare for escalation if payments are missing.**
   - Check your pension portal to see the date of the last successful contribution.
   - Note that while administrative delays can occur, you may report concerns to The Pensions Regulator (TPR) if you suspect bad faith or if payments are over 90 days late.

8. **Contact your trade union representative or workplace "Fair Work" lead.**
   - Request assistance in reviewing the legality of the benefit withdrawal if it is not an error.
   - Follow the internal grievance procedure if the employer fails to rectify a confirmed administrative mistake within five working days.

## What can wait
- Evaluating long-term legal claims for constructive dismissal.
- Purchasing private replacement insurance with your own funds.
- Negotiating new salary terms to compensate for lost benefits.
- Deciding whether to remain with the employer long-term.

## Important reassurance
Benefit cancellations are frequently the result of automated system migrations, payroll software errors, or insurer renewal delays rather than intentional misconduct. While the loss of cover is serious, administrative errors can typically be backdated once identified, meaning your continuity of cover can often be restored without financial loss.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for identifying the cause of benefit loss and ensuring immediate medical or financial safety. It does not cover long-term employment tribunal strategy or the technical specifics of insurance policy wording.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal, medical, or financial advice. Benefit entitlements depend on individual contracts and specific provider terms. If you are in medical distress, contact the NHS immediately. For persistent contractual disputes, consult ACAS or a qualified legal professional.
