# What to do if… you realise your paycheck or wages are short for the last pay period (UK)

## Short answer
Check the pay-period numbers: hours, rate, overtime, bonus, commission and deductions. Then raise it with payroll, your manager or HR and ask for written confirmation of the shortfall and when it will be paid.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume it will sort itself out next payday without a clear written plan for when and how it will be corrected.
- Do not sign anything you do not understand, such as documents about changing hours, pay rate or deductions, while you are panicking.
- Do not accuse anyone of theft or fraud before you have checked whether it is a payroll mistake.
- Do not quit on the spot or walk out mid-shift as your first response.
- Do not accept cash top-ups, informal IOUs or vague promises like “we’ll add it later” without a proper payroll correction.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm what is missing.**  
   Check your payslip, pay portal and bank payment for that pay period. Compare the hours paid, hourly rate or salary, overtime, commission, bonus and deductions. Write down the exact shortfall and the pay period end date.

2. **Check whether the payslip itself is part of the problem.**  
   Your employer must usually give you a payslip on or before payday. If your pay varies by hours worked, the payslip should show the number of hours paid. If you cannot access a payslip or pay statement, screenshot the portal error if there is one and keep the bank payment record.

3. **Save the minimum proof.**  
   Keep the payslip or pay statement, your contract or offer letter, your rota, approved timesheets, clock-in or clock-out records if available, and messages approving overtime or shifts. Screenshot anything that could change.

4. **Raise it the same day if you can, then create a paper trail.**  
   Tell payroll, your manager or HR: “I believe I have been underpaid by £X for the pay period ending [date]. Please confirm the amount owed and when it will be paid.” If you discuss it verbally, follow up with a short email or message confirming what was agreed.

5. **Ask for a specific correction plan.**  
   Ask them to confirm in writing:
   - the calculation they are using
   - when they will pay the shortfall
   - whether they can pay it before the next payday
   - how it will appear on your payslip so tax, National Insurance and other deductions are handled properly

6. **If it is not fixed promptly, raise a formal grievance in writing.**  
   Check your workplace grievance policy if there is one. Attach the payslip, timesheet and contract evidence, and clearly state what you want: payment of the shortfall, corrected payroll records and written confirmation.

7. **If you might be close to a deadline, contact Acas early.**  
   There are strict time limits for employment tribunal claims. For unpaid or underpaid wages, it is usually 3 months minus 1 day from the date you should have been paid, or from the last underpayment if it happened more than once. In most cases, you need to notify Acas within the tribunal time limit before you can make an employment tribunal claim, although some claims are exempt.

8. **If the underpayment may take you below the National Minimum Wage, use the minimum-wage complaint route as well as getting advice.**  
   You can make a pay and work rights complaint through GOV.UK. Minimum-wage complaints are directed to HMRC. If you are also considering an unlawful deductions claim or tribunal route, get advice quickly so you do not miss time limits.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to bring a legal claim.
- You do not need to escalate to senior leadership or threaten a tribunal to fix a one-off payroll error.
- You do not need to audit every historic payslip right now; stabilise the last pay period first.
- You do not need to resign or job-hunt in the first wave of panic.

## Important reassurance
A short pay packet is destabilising, especially when bills are due. Many underpayments are payroll or admin errors, such as hours not processed, a rate not updated or a deduction misapplied. Clear figures, saved records and a written correction plan give you a steadier next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps after you notice an underpayment. If it repeats, involves disputed deductions, affects multiple workers or gets close to a deadline, later decisions may need specialist help from Acas, a union, Citizens Advice or an employment adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, tax or professional advice. Employment situations vary and deadlines can be strict. If you feel pressured, threatened or are close to a deadline, get independent advice before taking irreversible steps.
