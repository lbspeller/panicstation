# What to do if… you receive a notice to surrender to custody to begin a sentence (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the notice using contact details you find yourself from the relevant court service, your solicitor, or the named prison. Then confirm the exact place, date, time, entrance, and documents before you travel; missing the time can create serious legal problems.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the notice or assume it will be corrected without you doing anything.
- Do not rely only on phone numbers, email addresses, links, or payment instructions printed on a suspicious notice.
- Do not bring drugs, alcohol, weapons, phones, smart devices, cash, valuables, or anything you are unsure about.
- Do not make travel plans that leave no margin for delays.
- Do not post your surrender location, timings, notice, or case details online.
- Do not wait until the last minute to raise a genuine problem with attending.

## What to do now
1. **Check that the notice is genuine.**
   - Contact your solicitor first, if you have one, and ask them to confirm the surrender requirement, the issuing court, and the destination.
   - If you do not have a solicitor, contact the court that sentenced you using official contact details for the UK nation where the case was sentenced.
   - If the notice names a prison, check the prison on the official prison service listing for that UK nation and use those contact details, not details from a suspicious notice.

2. **Confirm the reporting instructions and write them down.**
   - Confirm the date, time, full address, reception point or entrance, and any reference number you should quote.
   - Ask what identification and paperwork you should bring.
   - Ask what to do if you arrive and staff say you are at the wrong place, so you know who to contact immediately.

3. **If you may genuinely be unable to attend, act before the deadline.**
   - Contact your solicitor and the court as soon as possible.
   - Explain the specific problem, such as hospital admission or another emergency, and ask how it should be recorded.
   - Keep proof, such as hospital paperwork, appointment evidence, travel disruption records, and notes of who you spoke to and when.

4. **Prepare only reception-ready essentials.**
   - Bring the notice, any court paperwork you have, and photo ID if you have it.
   - Write key phone numbers and addresses on paper, including your solicitor, one trusted person, dependants’ carers, your GP, and urgent household contacts.
   - If you take medication, bring a written list with the name, dose, prescriber, and pharmacy, plus prescription proof or original packaging if you have it. Declare any medication at reception or healthcare and do not expect to keep it yourself unless prison staff later allow this.
   - Bring glasses, hearing aids, walking aids, or other essential health aids you rely on.

5. **Stabilise dependants and the next few days.**
   - Arrange urgent childcare, adult care, or pet care.
   - Tell one trusted person where you are required to attend and give them spare keys or essential documents if needed.
   - Secure your home, urgent bills, and immediate work messages only as far as you can do quickly and safely.

6. **Travel with extra time and as little property as possible.**
   - Aim to arrive early enough for delays, security, and finding the right entrance.
   - Keep paperwork easy to reach.
   - Expect property to be searched, recorded, and stored or refused according to prison rules.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out your release date, sentence calculations, transfers, work, courses, visits, or long-term prison arrangements right now.
- You do not need to make big irreversible decisions about housing, possessions, relationships, or employment in the first panic hours unless a fixed deadline forces it.
- You do not need to explain everything to everyone today; keep communication limited to your solicitor, the court or prison, and one trusted support person.

## Important reassurance
A surrender notice can make many problems feel urgent at the same time. The safest first move is narrow: confirm the notice through official routes, prepare essentials, protect dependants, and arrive at the right place in the right way.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the hours and days immediately after receiving a notice to surrender. Later decisions can depend on the UK nation, the court, the prison, the sentence, and your personal situation, and may need help from a solicitor or specialist support organisation.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Procedures can vary by UK nation, court, and prison. If anything is unclear, urgent, or suspicious, verify it directly with your solicitor, the relevant court, or the relevant prison service using official contact details.
