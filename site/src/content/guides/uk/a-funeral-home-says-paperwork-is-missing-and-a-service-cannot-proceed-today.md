# What to do if… a funeral home says paperwork is missing and a service cannot proceed today (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the funeral director to name the exact missing document, who must issue it, and the final cut-off time. Then contact the issuing office straight away, while you pause any rushed decisions about extra costs or cancellation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to extra charges, upgrades, or rushed changes until you know exactly what document is missing and who is responsible for it.
- Do not assume you personally lost paperwork; some documents may be sent directly or electronically between offices, the funeral director, the crematorium, or the burial authority.
- Do not let the issue stay vague as “the paperwork”; ask for the exact document name and whether it is missing, wrong, unsigned, or not yet received.
- Do not rely on social media to solve the urgent paperwork issue.
- Do not cancel everything immediately if guests are already travelling; ask whether a service or gathering can still happen today without the burial or cremation taking place today.

## What to do now
1. **Get the missing item defined in one sentence.**  
   Ask the funeral director:
   - “What is the exact name of the missing document?”
   - “Who issues it?”
   - “Is it missing, incorrect, unsigned, or not yet received?”
   - “What is the final cut-off time today?”
   - “Can you text or email me the exact wording of what is needed?”

2. **Ask where the document should have gone.**  
   Ask the funeral director whether the document should have been sent to:
   - the funeral director;
   - the crematorium;
   - the cemetery or burial authority;
   - the register office;
   - the coroner or equivalent office;
   - the medical examiner office, if the death was in England or Wales and the case is not with the coroner.

3. **If the issue is the certificate for burial or cremation, contact the register office.**  
   Say: “The funeral is scheduled today. The funeral director says the certificate for burial or cremation, sometimes called the green form, has not been received. Has it been issued, and where was it sent?”  
   Ask whether they can confirm the destination and resend it directly to the funeral director, crematorium, or burial authority if their process allows this.

4. **If the medical certification is not complete, contact the healthcare route that handled the death.**  
   If the death was in hospital, call the hospital bereavement office. If the death was in the community, call the GP surgery or the local contact you were given.  
   In England and Wales, ask whether the medical certificate of cause of death and any medical examiner review needed before registration are complete enough for registration. Ask for the specific hold-up, not a general update.

5. **If the case is with the coroner or equivalent office, ask what release is still needed.**  
   Ask the funeral director: “Is the coroner or equivalent office involved, and are you waiting for release of the body, permission for cremation or burial, or another document?”  
   Then contact that office and ask what can be released today, who it will be sent to, and whether the funeral director needs to do anything.

6. **Ask the funeral director to act in parallel.**  
   Ask them to call their contact at the register office, crematorium, cemetery, coroner, or equivalent office while you are present or copied in.  
   Ask them to check whether the problem is a mismatch, such as name spelling, date of birth, date of death, or crematorium details.

7. **Protect today’s gathering if the document will not arrive before the venue cut-off.**  
   Ask the funeral director, venue, and officiant:
   - “Can we hold the service, readings, music, prayers, or gathering today without the committal?”
   - “Can the burial or cremation be moved to the next available slot?”
   - “What fees are being charged because of this change, and can you confirm them in writing?”

8. **Write down the live record.**  
   Note each name, role, direct number, time called, what they said was missing, and what they promised to do next. Ask for written confirmation of any cancellation reason, change of plan, or fee.

9. **If you feel blamed or pressured, ask for a supervisor.**  
   Say: “I am not refusing to make a decision. I need the exact missing document, issuing office, cut-off time, and any cost confirmed in writing before I agree to changes.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change funeral provider, make a formal complaint, or pursue refunds.
- You usually do not need extra certified copies of the death certificate just to unblock the funeral; focus on the exact document needed for burial or cremation.
- You do not need to resolve wider family disagreements today. Focus only on the missing document, the cut-off time, and whether today’s service can still happen in some form.

## Important reassurance
A last-minute paperwork problem can happen when several offices are involved. It does not mean you have failed, and it does not mean you must accept expensive or irreversible decisions before the problem is clearly identified.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise the situation today, identify the specific missing document, and preserve the service or gathering where possible. Later decisions about complaints, contracts, costs, or changing provider may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Death registration, certification, coroners, procurators fiscal, cremation, and burial processes vary across England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland, and local areas, so ask the relevant office to confirm the exact document and process for your case.
