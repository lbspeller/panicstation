# What to do if… a smoke alarm goes off but you cannot find any smoke or heat (UK)

## Short answer
Treat it as real until you have done a quick, safe check. If you see smoke, smell burning or gas, feel heat, the alarm keeps sounding, it re-triggers without a clear harmless cause, or you are unsure, get everyone outside and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not remove the battery, cover the alarm, or disconnect it to stop the noise.
- Do not ignore a repeat alarm, a burning or electrical smell, heat from an appliance, scorch marks, or any smell of gas.
- Do not assume it was cooking, steam, or dust until you have checked the likely source.
- Do not spend a long time searching inside if anything feels unsafe; leave and call 999.
- Do not open doors into a room if you can see smoke underneath the door, feel heat, or suspect fire behind it.

## What to do now
1. **Pause where you are and check for danger.** Listen for the alarm starting again, look for haze or smoke, and smell for burning, electrical smells, or gas. If anything seems wrong, leave immediately.
2. **If there is any sign of fire, leave and call 999.** Get everyone out, close doors behind you if you can do so safely, stay out, and ask for the fire service.
3. **If you smell gas or suspect a gas leak, stop checking and leave.** Do not use light switches, plugs, matches, lighters, or naked flames. Once outside, call the National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999, or call 999 if there is immediate danger.
4. **If it feels safe, do a fast check starting near the alarm.** Check the kitchen, hob, oven, toaster, air fryer, microwave, extractor area, bathroom steam, heaters, tumble dryer, washing machine, plug sockets, extension leads, chargers, and anything that was recently switched on.
5. **Use the hush or silence button only if there is still no sign of fire.** Press the alarm’s test, hush, or silence button according to the alarm’s instructions so you can think clearly. Do not take it down or remove its power unless you are replacing it immediately.
6. **Reduce obvious harmless triggers.** Open a window, use an extractor fan, move smoky cooking away from the alarm area, close a steamy bathroom door, and keep the area around the alarm clear.
7. **Clean the alarm once it is quiet and safe.** Vacuum gently around the vents or grille to remove dust or insects. Use a stable step ladder if needed, not a chair.
8. **Check the battery or end-of-life signal.** If the alarm has a removable battery and has been chirping or acting oddly, replace the battery. If it is a sealed 10-year alarm, damaged, out of date, or still misbehaving after cleaning, replace the whole alarm.
9. **If you are in a rented home, report it promptly.** Tell your landlord, agent, housing association, or building manager that the alarm sounded without visible smoke or heat, what you checked, and whether it has happened before.
10. **Escalate early if it repeats or you are not confident.** If the alarm keeps sounding, several interlinked alarms sound, you suspect a hidden fire or electrical fault, or you cannot identify a safe cause, get everyone out and call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fully diagnose the exact cause right now if the alarm has stopped, the home seems safe, and you have checked the likely sources.
- You do not need to choose replacement alarms in the moment unless the alarm is clearly faulty, damaged, missing, or cannot be restored to working order.
- You do not need to decide who caused it or argue about cooking, steam, or dust.
- You do not need to clean every alarm immediately; deal with the alarm that sounded first, then check the others later when calm.
- You do not need to contact the landlord, agent, or manufacturer before leaving if there is any possible fire, gas, or electrical danger.

## Important reassurance
A brief alarm with no smoke or heat can happen because of cooking fumes, steam, dust, insects, battery problems, or an alarm reaching the end of its life. Taking it seriously first is the right response, even if it turns out to be harmless.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes. If alarms keep sounding, the alarm is old or damaged, or you suspect an electrical, gas, or building fault, later decisions may need help from your fire and rescue service, landlord, alarm manufacturer, gas emergency service, or a qualified electrician.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional fire-safety, electrical, gas, legal, housing, or emergency-service advice. If you suspect fire, gas, carbon monoxide, hidden smouldering, or an electrical fault, leave and call the appropriate emergency service.
