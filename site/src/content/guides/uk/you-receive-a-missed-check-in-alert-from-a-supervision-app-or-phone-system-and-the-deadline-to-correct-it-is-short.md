# What to do if… you receive a missed check-in alert from a supervision app or phone system and the deadline to correct it is short (UK)

## Short answer
Complete the check-in immediately if the system allows; if it fails, contact your Probation Practitioner or the Electronic Monitoring Service (EMS) helpline right away to create a timestamped record of the technical issue.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the notification or wait until the next day to report a failure.
- Do not turn off your phone, remove the SIM card, or delete the app to "reset" it.
- Do not delete any call logs, SMS alerts, or error messages from your device.
- Do not attempt to open, unscrew, or physically repair the monitoring unit or tag.
- Do not leave your approved residence if you are under curfew, even if you think the equipment is broken.
- Do not send aggressive or abusive messages to monitoring staff or your supervisor.

## What to do now
1. **Take a screenshot or photo of the alert and any visible error codes.**  
   **Record the exact time you saw the alert and the deadline provided.**  
   - Note the specific wording of the error (e.g., "Location could not be verified" or "Biometric failure").

2. **Verify your device status and retry the check-in once.**  
   **Ensure your phone has at least 20 percent battery, Wi-Fi or mobile data is active, and GPS location services are turned on.**  
   - If using the app: force-close the application and restart it.
   - If using a phone system: ensure you are calling from the primary number registered with probation.
   - If the check-in succeeds, take a screenshot of the "Successful" or "Completed" screen.

3. **Contact your Probation Practitioner immediately if the app or phone system remains unresponsive.**  
   **Call the office number provided in your induction pack and, if there is no answer, leave a voicemail stating your name, the time, and the specific technical failure.**  
   - Send a brief, factual email or message through the authorised channel if one exists.

4. **Call the Electronic Monitoring Service (EMS) helpline if the alert relates to a tag or curfew.**  
   **Contact the 24/7 monitoring centre using the number on your monitoring unit or your paperwork.**  
   - For curfew and location tags: call **0800 137 291**.
   - For alcohol monitoring tags: call **08081 780 058**.
   - Tell the operator exactly when the alert occurred and what steps you took to try and comply.

5. **Remain at your approved address and stay reachable by phone.**  
   **Keep your phone ringer on and stay within 10 metres of the base unit if you have a curfew requirement.**  
   - Do not assume that a technical fault gives you permission to miss other requirements or leave a restricted area.

6. **Gather your evidence of attempted compliance.**  
   **Keep a log of every call you made to the helpline or your practitioner, including the duration and the name of anyone you spoke to.**  
   - Save any automated confirmation SMS or emails received after your report.

## What can wait
- Sending a detailed formal complaint about the equipment.
- Reinstalling your phone’s operating system or buying a new handset.
- Discussing the incident with friends or family; focus only on official reporting.

## Important reassurance
Technical failures with apps and monitoring equipment are documented occurrences. If you have taken immediate action to report the fault and have kept evidence of your attempts to check in, you are following the correct procedure to protect your status.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only for addressing an immediate technical check-in failure. It does not replace the specific instructions in your court order, licence, or probation induction pack.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. If you are arrested for a breach of condition, you have the right to free legal advice from a duty solicitor at the police station.
