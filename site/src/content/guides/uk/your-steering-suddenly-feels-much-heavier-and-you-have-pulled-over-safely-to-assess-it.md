# What to do if… your steering suddenly feels much heavier and you have pulled over safely to assess it (UK)

## Short answer
Treat sudden heavy steering as a safety-critical fault. Stay stopped in the safest place you can and arrange help rather than testing the car in traffic.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t rejoin a fast road just to see if it clears, especially if the steering is still heavy or a steering warning light is on.
- Don’t keep turning the steering hard against full lock.
- Don’t open a power-steering fluid cap or reservoir while the engine is hot, and don’t reach near belts or fans.
- Don’t crawl under the car at the roadside.
- Don’t stand between your vehicle and moving traffic.
- Don’t place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Don’t attempt repairs on a motorway hard shoulder or in an emergency area.

## What to do now
1. **Follow the right motorway rule for where you are stopped, if this is on a motorway.**
   - **If you are stopped in a live lane and cannot reach a place of relative safety:** stay in the vehicle with your seat belt on, keep your hazard warning lights on, and call 999 or use the in-car SOS button if you have one. Ask for the police.
   - **If you are on the hard shoulder or in an emergency area and it is safe to exit:** leave by the side furthest from traffic, keep well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, and wait behind the safety barrier where there is one.
2. **If you are not on a motorway, keep yourself away from traffic first.** Keep hazard warning lights on if the vehicle is causing an obstruction. Stand somewhere you can stay clear of the vehicle, the carriageway, and moving traffic.
3. **Look at the dashboard before touching anything.** Note any steering wheel, EPS, power-steering, battery, engine-temperature, or tyre-pressure warnings. Do not try to diagnose them at the roadside.
4. **Only if it is genuinely safe, do a quick outside check from a safe standing position.**
   - Look for a flat or soft front tyre, because a puncture can make steering suddenly feel heavy.
   - Look for fresh fluid on the ground near the front of the car without touching it or crouching near traffic.
   - If you are on a motorway shoulder, in an emergency area, on a narrow road, or anywhere exposed to traffic, skip this check.
5. **Do not drive on if the steering still feels heavy or a steering warning light is on.** Treat it as needing investigation before the vehicle is driven again.
6. **Arrange assistance.** Contact your breakdown provider, insurer, lease company, or a recovery service and say: “sudden heavy steering, possible loss of power steering assist.” Tell them your location, any warning lights, and whether you suspect a puncture or leak.
7. **Move only if you must for immediate safety.** If you can steer reliably at walking speed, move only the shortest possible distance to a safer nearby place, such as fully off the road or into a car park, then stop again. If the steering feels unpredictable, do not move the vehicle; wait for help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the cause is a leak, belt, electric steering fault, battery issue, tyre problem, or software issue right now.
- You do not need to decide on repairs, costs, or which garage while you are still roadside.
- You do not need to keep trying fixes once help has been arranged.
- You do not need to unload the vehicle or gather belongings unless you can do so without stepping into traffic risk.

## Important reassurance
A sudden change in steering effort is frightening, and pulling over was the right first step. Waiting for assistance is a reasonable, safety-first choice, even if the car still starts and the problem seems intermittent.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes or hours. A proper diagnosis needs a mechanic, recovery operator, or garage, and later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information only, not legal, mechanical, insurance, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are, prioritise personal safety and emergency help.
