# What to do if… a stranger pressures you to reveal your phone number in person (UK)

## Short answer
Move toward other people, keep hold of your phone, and calmly refuse the “call it now” request. You do not need to prove it is a scam before leaving.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t hand your phone over “just for a second”.
- Don’t unlock your phone while it is within their reach.
- Don’t let them type into your phone, scan anything from your phone, or watch your screen.
- Don’t read out or show any texted verification or security code.
- Don’t stay still while deciding; move toward people, staff, a shop, or an exit.
- Don’t argue about whether it is a scam.
- Don’t follow them somewhere quieter to “sort it out”.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause first.** Step back, turn your body toward an exit, a busy counter, transport staff, shop staff, security, or another public place, and keep your phone in your hand or pocket.
2. **Use one short refusal.** Say: “No, I don’t share my number,” or “Sorry, I can’t do that.” Repeat it once if needed, then stop explaining.
3. **Leave the interaction.** Walk toward staff, a lit or busy area, a queue, or a place where you can ask for help. If they keep talking, you can say: “I’m going now.”
4. **Only offer another contact route if you genuinely want contact.** Take their number instead, or use a profile or username that does not reveal your phone number, home area, workplace, full name, or private contacts.
5. **If they block you, grab you, follow you, threaten you, or the situation could become violent soon, call 999.** If speaking would make you less safe, move to staff or another person and ask them to call.
6. **If your phone already rang and they now have your number, get away first.** Once safe, save any call log or messages if you may need them, then block the number and consider silencing unknown callers.
7. **If any texted code arrives, do not share it.** A real bank, email provider, messaging app, delivery company, or public body should not need you to tell a stranger a code on the spot.
8. **If you think a code, password, or sign-in prompt may have been exposed, secure the account it relates to.** Change the password from the official app or website, turn on two-step verification if available, and check for unfamiliar devices, sessions, or account changes.
9. **If suspicious messages follow, report them when you are safe.** Forward suspicious text messages to 7726 if your provider supports it. Forward suspicious emails to report@phishing.gov.uk.
10. **If you lost money, were hacked, or think fraud or cybercrime happened, use the UK route for where you live.** In England, Wales or Northern Ireland, use Report Fraud. In Scotland, contact Police Scotland on 101 or use Police Scotland online reporting for non-urgent matters.
11. **If it becomes harassment or stalking, keep the messages and call logs if you safely can.** For non-emergency police contact, use 101 or your local police online reporting route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it was definitely a scam, flirting, sales pressure, harassment, or just an awkward request.
- You do not need to justify your refusal.
- You do not need to fix all privacy settings immediately.
- You can review call screening, app privacy, blocked numbers, and secondary contact options later, when you are calm and somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat forced “on the spot” contact-sharing as a safety issue. You are allowed to say no, leave, and protect your phone number without proving anything to the other person.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment and the next hour or two. If the person keeps contacting you, knows where you live or work, or you feel targeted, later decisions may need help from police, your phone provider, or a specialist support service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe or threatened, prioritise getting to a safer place and contacting emergency services.
