# What to do if… your bank account is restricted and you need to verify your identity in person (UK)

## Short answer
Use the bank’s official app, website, card number, or branch route to ask what exact verification is needed and whether they can offer any limited essentials access or hardship support while the restriction is in place.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t give one-time passcodes, card reader codes, remote-access app approval, or banking app approval prompts to anyone who called or messaged you first.
- Don’t pay anyone who claims they can unlock your account or speed up verification.
- Don’t keep retrying failed logins if the bank has told you to wait or attend verification.
- Don’t ignore rent, utilities, council tax, travel-to-work costs, food, or medication needs while waiting for the bank.
- Don’t hand original documents to anyone outside an official bank process, branch process, or clearly confirmed postal route.

## What to do now
1. **Contact the bank through an official route and ask for the team handling the restriction.**
   - Use the number on your card, the bank’s official website, secure in-app messaging, or a branch.
   - Say: “My account access is restricted and I cannot access essential funds. Please connect me to the team handling the verification or account restriction.”
   - Ask them to put a note on the account that the restriction is causing immediate difficulty with essentials.

2. **Ask exactly what must happen before access can be restored.**
   - Ask: “Why is in-person verification needed, in general terms?”
   - Ask: “Which documents are accepted, do they need to be originals, and do I need proof of address?”
   - Ask: “Do I need an appointment, which branch can do this, and can you confirm the opening times before I travel?”

3. **Ask whether there is any safe temporary access or hardship route.**
   - Ask whether they can allow any limited access for essentials, such as a small cash withdrawal, essential transfer, or payment to a named biller.
   - Ask what they can do if rent, bills, food, travel to work, or medication are affected.
   - Ask for the answer in writing by secure message, email, or letter where possible.

4. **Ask about approved alternatives if you cannot attend a branch quickly.**
   - If you are housebound, away from home, caring for someone, disabled, or far from a branch, say this clearly.
   - Ask whether any approved alternative verification route is available for your circumstances.
   - If there is no alternative, ask for the fastest realistic branch or appointment route and what you need to bring.

5. **Protect the next seven days of essentials.**
   - Write down the next seven days of must-pay or must-have items: rent, mortgage, utilities, council tax, food, travel to work, childcare, and medication.
   - Contact the most urgent billers today and say there is a temporary bank restriction; ask for a short grace period or another way to pay.
   - Keep screenshots, secure messages, names, dates, times, and reference numbers.

6. **If wages, benefits, or another payment is due soon, contact the payer early.**
   - Ask whether the next payment can be paused or redirected to another account in your name that you can access.
   - If it cannot be changed in time, ask what written confirmation, emergency payroll route, or later payment route they can offer.
   - Do not use someone else’s account unless the payer confirms it is allowed and you understand the risk of delay or rejection.

7. **Start a formal complaint with the bank if essentials are affected or the route is unclear.**
   - Say: “I want to make a formal complaint about being unable to access my funds while verification is pending.”
   - Ask for a complaint reference and a written explanation of what they will do next.
   - Ask them to treat the impact on rent, bills, food, travel, or medication as urgent hardship information.
   - For many UK financial complaints, the firm usually has up to 8 weeks to send a final response. For payment services and e-money complaints, the usual response time is 15 days; if the firm cannot yet give a response, it should explain why and usually send a response within 35 days. If you receive a final response you disagree with, or the relevant time limit passes, you can usually ask the Financial Ombudsman Service to look at it. There is usually a six-month limit from the date of the final response.

8. **If you think this may involve identity fraud, use the right reporting route.**
   - Tell the bank if you see transactions, applications, address changes, or account activity you do not recognise.
   - In England, Wales, and Northern Ireland, fraud and cyber crime are generally reported through Report Fraud.
   - In Scotland, report through Police Scotland or by calling 101.
   - Keep any report reference number and give it to the bank if they ask for it.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to switch banks, close accounts, start legal action, or claim compensation.
- You don’t need to write a perfect complaint today; focus first on verification, essential access, and written records.
- You can leave credit-file checks, longer-term budgeting changes, and wider disputes until you can cover basics again.

## Important reassurance
An in-person verification request does not automatically mean you have done anything wrong. The useful first steps are to use official bank channels, get the exact requirement in writing, protect essentials, and create a clear record.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, reduce immediate harm, and preserve an escalation route. Later decisions about complaints, compensation, debt, benefits, fraud markers, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Bank policies vary, and banks may be required to apply security and financial crime checks. Use official channels and prioritise safety if you feel pressured, coerced, or targeted by fraud.
