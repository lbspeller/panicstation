# What to do if… your email inbox is deleting messages and you did not set any rules (UK)

## Short answer
Use a web browser, not a mail app, to check your email account settings for unknown rules, filters, forwarding, sweep, cleanup, or auto-archive settings. If anything looks suspicious, remove it, change your password from the official account page, sign out unknown sessions, and turn on 2-step verification.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep testing across several phones, tablets, and mail apps; syncing can spread moves or deletions.
- Don’t empty Trash, Deleted Items, Spam, or Junk while you are still trying to recover messages.
- Don’t click links in security-alert emails; open your provider by typing the address yourself or using a saved bookmark.
- Don’t assume it is only a glitch if messages are still being deleted or moved.
- Don’t delete suspicious rules or forwarding entries before taking a quick screenshot, if you can do that safely.

## What to do now
1. **Pause syncing where you can.**
   - Close mail apps on phones and tablets.
   - If a device seems to be causing the deletions, disconnect it from the internet for now.
   - Use one trusted device, preferably a computer, for the next checks.

2. **Check webmail before changing folders in an app.**
   - Sign in through the email provider’s website.
   - Check Inbox, Trash or Deleted Items, Spam or Junk, Archive or All Mail, and any folders you do not usually use.
   - Search by sender, subject word, and date to see whether the messages were moved rather than deleted.

3. **Remove unknown rules, filters, forwarding, and cleanup settings.**
   - In webmail settings, check rules, filters, forwarding, sweep, cleanup, blocked senders, auto-archive, and POP or IMAP settings.
   - Disable or delete anything you did not create, especially settings that delete, move, archive, mark as read, or forward messages.
   - If the setting shows an unknown forwarding address, take a screenshot before removing it.

4. **Secure the account from the provider’s official account page.**
   - Check recent sign-ins, security events, and signed-in devices.
   - Sign out of any session or device you do not recognise.
   - Change your password after removing unknown forwarding or rules.
   - Turn on 2-step verification.
   - Check that recovery phone numbers and recovery email addresses are ones you control.

5. **Recover messages while recovery options may still be available.**
   - Move messages from Trash, Deleted Items, Spam, or Junk back to Inbox or to a new folder called “Recovered”.
   - If your provider offers a recover or restore option for recently deleted items, use that official option.
   - For Outlook.com or Outlook on the web, check Deleted Items and look for “Recover items deleted from this folder” if the missing messages are not there.

6. **Check whether a mail app or device is causing the moves.**
   - After webmail is stable, reopen one mail app at a time.
   - If messages start moving again after one app reconnects, remove that account from the app or check that app’s rules, archive, and delete settings.
   - If this is a work or school account, stop testing and contact IT or the helpdesk.

7. **Keep a small record of what you found.**
   - Save screenshots of unknown rules, forwarding addresses, sign-ins, recovery changes, and provider warnings.
   - Write down roughly when the deletions started and which folders were affected.
   - Keep this record brief; the priority is still stopping further loss.

8. **Use official help if you cannot stop it.**
   - Use your provider’s official compromised-account or account-recovery route.
   - For a work, school, or organisation mailbox, ask IT to check mailbox rules, forwarding, delegated access, audit logs, and account compromise signs.
   - If fraud or cyber crime is involved in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report it through Report Fraud.
   - If you live in Scotland or the crime happened in Scotland, contact Police Scotland on 101.
   - If there is an immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to abandon the email address.
- You do not need to reorganise every folder now.
- You do not need to message all contacts immediately unless suspicious emails were sent from your account.
- You do not need to work out the whole cause before you stop forwarding, rules, and unauthorised access.

## Important reassurance
Disappearing email can come from a mail app, sync setting, archive setting, rule, forwarding setting, or account compromise. The first safe actions are the same: stabilise in webmail, stop unknown moving or forwarding, secure the account, and recover what the provider still makes available.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions, such as checking linked accounts, reviewing financial risk, or rebuilding access after a confirmed compromise, may need help from your email provider, workplace IT, bank, police reporting route, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical security, or professional advice. Use official provider pages and appropriate UK reporting routes for your situation.
