# What to do if… your computer will not start and you need to recover files stored on it (UK)

## Short answer
Do not reset, reinstall, or erase anything yet. Your first aim is to copy the files out, either from a backup, from the drive using another computer, from Mac Target Disk Mode or Share Disk if available, or through a reputable data-recovery service.

## Do not do these things
- Do not start a reset, reinstall, factory reset, Startup Repair, disk repair, or wipe if your priority is the files.
- Do not keep forcing restarts for a long time; repeated failed starts can worsen some faults.
- Do not install recovery software onto the same drive you are trying to rescue.
- Do not open a sealed drive casing or try home remedies such as freezing, tapping, or heating it.
- Do not guess BitLocker or FileVault recovery keys over and over.
- Do not assume a shop, warranty repair, or manufacturer repair will preserve your data unless you get that confirmed in writing.

## What to do now
1. **Write down the symptoms before changing anything.**  
   Note whether there are lights, fans, beeps, a logo, an error message, a black screen, clicking noises, a recent spill, a drop, a power cut, or an update just before the failure. Photograph any error screen.

2. **Do only low-risk checks.**  
   Unplug USB devices, docks, memory cards, and external drives. Try a known working charger, cable, or wall socket if that is easy. If it still will not start, stop trying to fix the boot and switch to file-first steps.

3. **Check for copies before touching the drive.**  
   On your phone or another computer, check OneDrive, Google Drive, iCloud Drive, Dropbox, email attachments, work storage, school storage, and any external drive you normally use. Check Desktop, Documents, Pictures, and Downloads in synced accounts.

4. **If it is a Mac and it powers on, try the Mac file-sharing route before repair.**  
   If the broken Mac is Intel-based and you have another Mac, Target Disk Mode may let it appear as a drive on the other Mac. If it is a Mac with Apple silicon, macOS Recovery may let you use Share Disk with another Mac. Copy the most important folders first.

5. **If the storage is removable and you can do it safely, use another computer to copy files.**  
   For a desktop tower, the internal drive may be removable and can sometimes be connected to another computer with the correct USB adapter or enclosure for that drive type. For a laptop, only do this if the drive is clearly removable and access is straightforward. If you are unsure, or if it needs risky disassembly, stop and use professional help.

6. **Be ready for encryption.**  
   A Windows drive may ask for a BitLocker recovery key. Check the recovery key ID, then look in your Microsoft account, work or school account, printed papers, or a saved USB key. A Mac drive may need the Mac login password, your Apple Account recovery route, a FileVault recovery key, or help from the organisation that manages the Mac.

7. **If the drive is not detected, makes clicking or grinding noises, smells burnt, was soaked, or holds irreplaceable files, power it off.**  
   Contact a reputable data-recovery company and say clearly that you need file recovery without wiping. Ask whether they will assess the drive before attempting any repair or write operation.

8. **If it is under warranty or recently bought, separate the data issue from the repair issue.**  
   Contact the retailer and say the device appears faulty but the storage contains files you need. Ask in writing whether repair will erase, replace, or remove storage, and whether a data-preserving route is possible before you send it away. Consumer advice routes can vary by where you are in the UK, so check the right route for your part of the UK before making a formal complaint.

9. **If it is a work or school device, contact IT before removing storage.**  
   Managed devices often use encryption, policies, and remote backups. IT may already have the recovery key or a safe recovery process.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to repair, replace, upgrade, or complain.
- You do not need to organise every file now; copy the most important documents, photos, and work folders first.
- You do not need to run deeper repair tools until the files are copied or a specialist has advised you.
- You do not need to decide the final consumer-rights route before you have asked what will happen to the storage.

## Important reassurance
A computer that will not start does not always mean the files are gone. The safest first moves are to avoid erasing or writing to the drive, look for synced copies, and copy out the most important files before trying to fix the computer.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on reducing the chance of permanent data loss. Later decisions about repair, replacement, complaints, warranty, or data-recovery cost may need specialist technical or consumer advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional technical, legal, financial, or data-recovery advice. If the files are irreplaceable or time-critical, stopping DIY early and using reputable professional support may reduce the risk of permanent loss.
