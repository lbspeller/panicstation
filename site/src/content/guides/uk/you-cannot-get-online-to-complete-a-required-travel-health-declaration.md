# What to do if… you cannot get online to complete a required travel health declaration (UK)

## Short answer
Tell official airline, airport, border, or health staff that you cannot get online and ask what approved fallback they will accept. Do not guess answers, pay an unknown helper, or keep retrying until you miss check-in or boarding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not invent answers or tick boxes just to get through; an inaccurate declaration can create problems later.
- Do not hand your passport or phone to a stranger offering to do it for you.
- Do not pay an unknown third-party site or person to process the declaration.
- Do not keep troubleshooting for so long that you miss check-in, security, or boarding.
- Do not send passport photos, health details, or payment information to unverified contacts.
- Do not use a public or shared device unless it is clearly provided or authorised by official staff.

## What to do now
1. **Go to official staff and state the problem clearly.**  
   Say: “I am required to complete a health declaration, but I cannot access the internet here. What official fallback can I use?”
   Try the airline check-in desk, gate staff, airport information desk, or any clearly identified border or health desk.

2. **Ask what they will accept for this exact journey.**  
   Ask whether you can use a paper form, an airline or airport kiosk, a staff tablet, a manual document check, or completion on arrival.

3. **Get the fallback instruction before spending more time on your phone.**  
   Ask: “Can you confirm what I need to show so I do not miss check-in or boarding?”

4. **If the issue is an airline app or website, ask for manual handling.**  
   Say that the app or site is not working where you are and ask whether the desk or gate can record the check another way.

5. **If you are told to connect to Wi-Fi, confirm the official network name first.**  
   Use the airport or airline network shown on official signage or confirmed by staff. Avoid lookalike Wi-Fi names.

6. **If you use a shared official device, protect your details.**  
   Enter only what the form asks for. After submitting, log out, close the browser or app, decline any save-password prompt, and do not leave the screen unattended.

7. **Save proof in a form you can show offline.**  
   If the declaration submits, take a screenshot of the confirmation or QR code and save any confirmation email for offline viewing.

8. **If someone says you need a UK passenger locator form, pause and ask for route-specific confirmation.**  
   UK COVID-19 travel rules have changed since passenger locator forms were widely used. Ask official staff whether the form is for the UK, your destination, a transit country, or the airline’s own check.

9. **If time is tight, escalate politely.**  
   Say: “Can a supervisor confirm the accepted workaround now, please? Boarding time is close.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the fault is your phone, roaming, airport Wi-Fi, or the declaration website right now.
- You do not need to argue the policy at the counter before getting the accepted workaround.
- You do not need to upload extra documents beyond what official staff say is needed for this journey.
- You do not need to decide how to complain about the app, website, airline, or airport until after you are through the immediate travel step.

## Important reassurance
This is a common travel failure point: airport signal can be poor, roaming can fail, and travel forms can stop loading at the worst time. Official staff can usually tell you what they will accept or who can authorise the next step.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent missed travel and unsafe shortcuts with your personal data. Later decisions, including complaints, refunds, corrections, or route-specific entry questions, may need specialist airline, government, insurance, or legal help.

## Important note
This is general practical information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Travel requirements vary by country, airline, route, and time, so follow official instructions for your specific journey.
