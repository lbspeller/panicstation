# What to do if… a long-distance bus or coach breaks down and you are left waiting without clear instructions (UK)

## Short answer
Get to the safest position available, stay clear of traffic, and ask the driver for one clear next update time. If there is immediate danger, such as fire, smoke, a collision risk, violence, or a medical emergency, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t walk along a motorway, dual carriageway, or live road to find help.
- Don’t stand between the coach and oncoming traffic, or close to the carriageway edge.
- Don’t try to access the luggage hold, move luggage from the roadside, or attempt repairs.
- Don’t step back into the coach or towards traffic just because you are cold, wet, embarrassed, or frustrated.
- Don’t get into an unmarked vehicle or accept alternative transport from someone who is not clearly connected to the operator or emergency services.
- Don’t use up your phone battery on streaming, long calls, or repeated messages before you know the plan.

## What to do now
1. **Do a quick safety scan.**
   If there is fire, smoke, a crash, a passenger collapsing, violence, or the coach is stopped where passengers cannot stay safely clear of traffic, call 999. Say you are a passenger on a broken-down coach and give the best location you can.

2. **Follow the driver or emergency services on where to wait.**
   If you are told to leave the coach, move calmly to the safest available place away from traffic, such as behind a safety barrier if there is one and it is safe to reach. If leaving would put you into traffic, or there is no safe place to wait, stay seated and keep your seat belt on if there is one until the driver or emergency services tell you otherwise.

3. **Make the coach locatable.**
   Check your phone map location and, if safe, look for motorway marker posts, driver location signs, a junction number, a service area name, a road name, and the direction of travel. Save the details in a note or message so you can repeat them clearly.

4. **Ask for one operational update.**
   Calmly ask the driver: “Are we waiting for a replacement coach, a mechanic, recovery, police, or another instruction from control?” Then ask: “When will the next update be?” If they do not know an arrival time, ask for a regular next update, for example in about 15 minutes.

5. **Contact the operator through an official route.**
   Use the operator’s app, live chat, booking email, or a phone number printed on your ticket or on the operator’s official website. Send the route or service number, ticket reference, location, what passengers have been told, how long you have been waiting, and any urgent needs such as disabled passenger assistance, a mobility aid, accessible information, medication, children travelling, toilet urgency, or a passenger who is unwell.

6. **Keep essentials with you, not in the hold.**
   Keep your phone, power bank, charger, medication, wallet, ID, water, warm layer, and tickets with you. Assume under-coach luggage may not be accessible until staff say it is safe.

7. **Set up a low-stress passenger check.**
   If you are near calm passengers, agree to share updates rather than everyone asking the driver at once. If someone has a medical need, mobility need, or no working phone, help them make that known to the driver or operator.

8. **If you feel unsafe because of another passenger, move and escalate.**
   Move closer to the driver or a calmer group of passengers. If you believe you are at immediate risk, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now about refunds, compensation, or a formal complaint.
- You do not need to book new travel until you have a confirmed fact, such as a replacement coach, recovery plan, service-area move, or operator instruction.
- You do not need to sort out hold luggage until the coach is in a safe place and staff say luggage access is possible.
- You do not need to explain the whole situation to everyone now; send one short update to anyone waiting for you and preserve your battery.

## Important reassurance
A breakdown with no clear instructions can make people feel trapped, angry, or panicked. Your immediate job is smaller than solving the whole journey: stay clear of traffic, make sure the coach is locatable, protect essentials, and create a next update point.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate breakdown period. Later decisions about onward travel, complaints, refunds, compensation, or missed connections may need the coach operator, a passenger advice body, or another specialist source.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, transport, or professional advice. If there is immediate danger or a medical emergency, call 999.
