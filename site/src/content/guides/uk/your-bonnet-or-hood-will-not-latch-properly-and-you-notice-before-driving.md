# What to do if… your bonnet or hood will not latch properly and you notice before driving (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive the vehicle. An insecure bonnet is a "dangerous condition" under UK law and poses a risk of flying open and obstructing your vision while moving.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive relying on the secondary safety catch alone.
- Don’t use bungee cords, rope, or tape to secure the bonnet for a journey.
- Don’t continue to slam the bonnet with excessive force if it does not engage.
- Don’t place fingers inside the latch mechanism while another person operates the internal release.
- Don’t attempt to drive to a garage if the bonnet is not fully flush and locked.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure the vehicle is parked in a safe location with the engine off.** If you are in a hazardous position, such as a narrow road, use your hazard warning lights and only move the vehicle a few metres to a safe spot if the bonnet does not block your direct line of sight.
2. **Conduct a visual inspection for obvious physical obstructions.**  
   - Open the bonnet fully and secure it with the prop or gas struts.
   - Check for debris, rags, leaves, or ice around the latch on the slam panel and the striker loop on the bonnet underside.
   - Verify that the internal release lever in the cabin has returned to its "closed" position and is not stuck in the "open" pull.
3. **Attempt one controlled closing procedure according to your vehicle handbook.**  
   - Lower the bonnet to the height recommended by the manufacturer (usually 12 inches / 30cm) and let it drop, or press down firmly if the handbook specifies.
   - Listen for a distinct "click" of the primary latch engaging.
4. **Physically test the security of the primary latch.** **With the bonnet in the closed position, grasp the front edge and attempt to pull it upwards.**  
   - If the bonnet moves at all or is held only by the safety catch (leaving a visible gap), it is not secure.
   - If there is any horizontal "play" or wobbling, the latch is not fully engaged.
5. **Request mobile breakdown assistance or professional recovery.** **Contact your breakdown provider and specify that the vehicle is unroadworthy due to a "bonnet security failure."**  
   - Mention if the internal release handle feels loose, as this indicates a snapped or detached cable.
   - Inform the technician if the latch appears seized or corroded, which is common in UK winter conditions due to road salt.
6. **Identify if recent engine bay activity preceded the failure.** **Note if the issue occurred immediately after checking fluids, charging the battery, or a recent MOT/service.** 
   - Report this context to the attending mechanic to assist their initial assessment.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific component failure (cable vs. spring vs. latch) yourself.
- You do not need to worry about MOT implications immediately; however, be aware that this must be fixed before your next test as it is a fail point.
- You do not need to source replacement parts until a professional has confirmed what is broken.

## Important reassurance
Noticing this issue before setting off has prevented a high-speed vision obstruction. This is a common mechanical failure often caused by simple cable stretching or latch corrosion, both of which are standard repairs for UK mobile mechanics.

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate stabilization and safety checks to prevent a dangerous driving incident. It does not provide instructions for mechanical repair, cable replacement, or latch adjustment, which require tools and technical competence.

## Important note
Under Section 40A of the Road Traffic Act 1988, driving a vehicle in a dangerous condition can result in a fine of up to £2,500 and 3 penalty points. An insecure bonnet is considered a serious safety defect. If you are at the roadside and feel unsafe, stay inside the vehicle or move to a safe distance behind a barrier while waiting for assistance.
