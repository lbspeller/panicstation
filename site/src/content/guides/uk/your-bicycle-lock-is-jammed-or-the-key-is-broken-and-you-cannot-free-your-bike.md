# What to do if… your bicycle lock is jammed or the key is broken and you cannot free your bike (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself into a safer, well-lit spot first. Gather simple proof that the bike is yours, then ask station staff, reception, security, property management, or a mobile locksmith for authorised help rather than trying to cut the lock in public.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t start cutting, grinding, or forcing the lock in public on impulse; it can look like theft and tools can cause serious injuries.
- Don’t keep twisting a key, shackle, or dial once it is binding; a snapped key or damaged mechanism can make the problem harder.
- Don’t leave the bike half-secured while you go looking for tools or help.
- Don’t block entrances, ramps, fire routes, rails, gates, pavements, or cycle lanes while you work out what to do.
- Don’t give strangers unnecessary personal details just because you feel embarrassed or pressured.
- Don’t argue with bystanders; use staff, security, property management, or a locksmith so the situation is clear.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause point first.** Move yourself to a well-lit place near other people, such as a station concourse, shop front, building reception, or staffed area. If you feel in immediate danger, or a crime or violence is happening now, call **999**.

2. **Try only gentle checks for two or three minutes.**
   - **Combination lock:** line the dials up carefully, apply light tension to the shackle or chain, and turn each dial slowly.
   - **Keyed lock:** check the key is fully inserted, then try a gentle wiggle without bending the key.
   - **Frozen or dirty lock:** warm the key in your hand and try again gently. Do not pour boiling water into the lock.

3. **Stop if the lock still does not open.** Treat the bike as temporarily stuck, not as a problem you must solve with force right now.

4. **Document that the bike is yours.**
   - Take clear photos of the whole bike, the lock, what it is attached to, and any unique marks.
   - Note the exact location, nearby landmark, and time.
   - If you can see it safely, note the frame or serial number, often under the bottom bracket.
   - Keep any purchase receipt, bike registration record, insurance record, or photo of you with the bike ready on your phone.

5. **Ask the person or organisation controlling the place.**
   - **At a train station:** speak to station staff first. For a non-emergency rail safety or suspicion concern, British Transport Police can be contacted on **0800 40 50 40** or by texting **61016**. Use **999** for emergencies.
   - **At work, a supermarket, campus, flats, or a car park:** ask reception, security, facilities, or management what they need before any lock removal.
   - **On a public street:** local rules and removal processes vary. Use an authorised locksmith where possible, and avoid cutting the lock yourself unless the relevant staff or authority has clearly authorised it.

6. **Make temporary leaving safer if you cannot free it now.**
   - Tell staff, security, or property management that the bike is temporarily stuck and that you are returning.
   - If you leave a note, keep it brief, such as: “Owner returning today because lock has failed.”
   - Use only contact details you are comfortable sharing publicly.

7. **Choose the safest way home.** Use public transport, a taxi, ride-hail, or a lift from someone you trust. If it is late, cold, dark, or you are exhausted, going home safely and returning in daylight may be the safest next step.

8. **Arrange authorised removal if needed.** Call a mobile locksmith and say clearly that it is a bicycle lock in a public or shared place. Ask what proof they need before they attend, such as photo ID, purchase proof, bike registration details, or photos of the bike.

9. **Get urgent help for an injury.** If a key, tool, metal fragment, or chemical gets into your eye, or you have a serious cut, heavy bleeding, or loss of vision, seek urgent medical help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to replace the lock, upgrade your bike security, claim on insurance, or complain to anyone.
- You do not need to solve the lock brand, warranty, or spare-key problem right now.
- You do not need to prove the whole history of the bike to bystanders.
- You only need a safe way home, a clear record of ownership, and a legitimate route to retrieve the bike.

## Important reassurance
A failed bike lock feels urgent because it strands you, but rushing can create bigger problems: injury, bike damage, or a misunderstanding. A calm, documented approach gives staff or a locksmith a clear reason to help.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and get you home. Later decisions, such as replacing the lock, arranging spare keys, dealing with removal notices, or making a theft report if the bike disappears, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Local rules, site policies, and staff procedures vary. If there is an emergency, call 999.
