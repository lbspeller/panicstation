# What to do if… you suspect someone is secretly recording in a changing room or other private area (UK)

## Short answer
Exit the area immediately to a public space and inform the duty manager or security so they can secure the site and contact the police.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the suspect directly or attempt to seize their device.
- Do not stay in the private area to investigate or search for the device alone.
- Do not delete any evidence if you have accidentally found a device.
- Do not share descriptions of the incident on social media before speaking to the police.
- Do not assume you must have "proof" before reporting; suspicion of voyeurism is sufficient for a police report.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause.** Step out of the changing room or toilet area immediately into a populated public space like a shop floor or reception desk.
2. **Alert the venue management immediately.** State clearly that you suspect a privacy breach or voyeurism is occurring in the specific area (e.g., "Cubicle 4").
   - Ask them to stop others from entering that area.
   - Request that the duty manager or security attend.
   - Ask them to preserve CCTV footage and door-entry logs.
3. **Contact the police.**
   - **Call 999** if the suspect is still present, if you feel in immediate danger, or if a crime is in progress.
   - **Call 101** if the incident has concluded and you are in a safe location.
4. **Access specialist support if you believe you have been recorded.**
   - **In England:** Contact a **Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC)** via **nhs.uk/sarcs** for confidential medical and practical advice.
   - **In Scotland:** Call the **NHS Scotland SARCS self-referral line** on **0800 148 88 88** (24/7).
   - **Across the UK:** Call the **Rape Crisis England & Wales** 24/7 support line on **0808 500 2222** for confidential support regardless of whether you report to the police.
5. **Note physical and sensory details.** Briefly write down what caused the suspicion:
   - Visible lenses in objects (e.g., smoke detectors, power adapters, or hooks).
   - Phones held at unusual angles or under gaps.
   - Descriptions of the suspect (clothing, height, bags) if seen from a safe distance.
6. **Protect evidence on your own devices.** If you have received messages or seen images related to the incident, do not delete them; the police may need to forensicly secure these as part of "intimate image abuse" investigations.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether you want to go to court or provide a full witness statement.
- You do not need to identify the exact type of recording technology used.
- You do not need to contact the corporate head office of the venue immediately.
- You do not need to justify your reaction to anyone who minimizes the situation.

## Important reassurance
Feeling a sense of shock, hyper-vigilance, or embarrassment is a standard physiological response to a violation of private space. Taking immediate action to reach a public area and notify staff is a proactive safety step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected privacy violation in the UK. It focuses on immediate safety, reporting, and accessing initial support services. Long-term legal or psychological follow-up will require specialized professional assistance.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Legislation, including the Sexual Offences Act 2003 and the Crime and Policing Bill 2026, governs these incidents. If you are in immediate danger, always call 999.
