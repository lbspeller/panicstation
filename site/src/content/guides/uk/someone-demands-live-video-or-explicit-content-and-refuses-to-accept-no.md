# What to do if… someone demands live video or explicit content and refuses to accept “no” (UK)

## Short answer
Cease all communication immediately without sending further content or money. Secure your accounts, preserve evidence of the threats (not the images), and contact specialist UK support services.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send more images, videos, or "proof" to appease the person; compliance usually leads to higher demands.
- Do not agree to a "final" video call to explain yourself; these calls are frequently used to record more footage.
- Do not pay any money, crypto, or gift cards; payment does not guarantee the deletion of content and identifies you as a high-value target.
- Do not screenshot or save any images or videos if the person involved is (or appears to be) under 18; possessing such material is a criminal offense in the UK.
- Do not delete your social media accounts immediately if you intend to report; deactivating is better as it preserves data for police investigation.
- Do not attempt to negotiate or "reason" with the person once a threat has been made.

## What to do now
1. **End all interaction and create a safe distance.** Close the app or website immediately and move to a physical space where you feel secure. 
2. **Preserve evidence of the threats only.** Take screenshots of the messages containing threats, usernames, profile URLs, and any payment instructions. 
   - Note the date, time, and specific platform where the interaction occurred.
   - Do not save or screenshot the actual explicit images or videos if they involve a minor.
3. **Report the user to the platform and then block.** Use the internal reporting tools for "blackmail," "harassment," or "intimate image abuse" before blocking. 
   - If you are worried about an immediate reaction, you can "restrict" or "mute" the user first while you gather support.
4. **Use StopNCII.org if you are an adult (18+).** Access this free tool to create "hashes" (digital fingerprints) of your intimate images. 
   - This allows participating platforms to detect and block the images from being uploaded or shared without the images ever leaving your device.
5. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline (UK adults).** Call **0345 6000 459** (Mon–Fri, 10 am – 4 pm) for confidential advice on removing content and legal rights. 
6. **Access Rape Crisis support for emotional stabilization.**
   - In England and Wales: Call the 24/7 Support Line on **0808 500 2222** (for ages 16+).
   - In Scotland: Call **08088 01 03 02** (daily, 5 pm – midnight, for ages 13+).
7. **Protect minors via CEOP and Childline.** If the person targeted is under 18, report the incident to the **National Crime Agency’s CEOP Safety Centre**. 
   - Use the **Childline "Report Remove"** tool to help take down sexual images or videos of anyone under 18.
8. **Contact the police if you feel unsafe.** Call **999** if there is an immediate threat to your safety or **101** to report the crime of sextortion or harassment.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to pursue a full criminal prosecution tonight.
- You do not need to notify your friends, family, or employer right now.
- You do not need to review your entire digital history today; a broad security sweep can happen once the immediate crisis is stabilized.
- You do not need to "fix" the situation by talking to the perpetrator.

## Important reassurance
Being pressured or blackmailed for sexual content is a crime, and you are the victim of that crime regardless of how the interaction started. Feeling a sense of panic, shame, or "freezing" is a biological response to a high-stress situation; these feelings do not mean you have done anything wrong. UK specialist services handle these cases daily and can provide a path forward without judgment.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for online sexual coercion. It does not replace legal advice or long-term therapeutic support.

## Important note
If you are in immediate physical danger or are at risk of self-harm, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E. This guide is for informational purposes and focuses on immediate safety in the UK jurisdiction.
