# What to do if… your vehicle’s main screen goes blank and you have lost defrost or climate controls (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a visibility and roadside safety issue. Do not set off again unless you can keep the windscreen clear; get safe where you are, try only low-risk checks, then arrange help or recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rejoin the road if the windscreen is misting or fogging and you cannot reliably clear it.
- Do not stop in a live lane to try fixes unless you have no safer option.
- Do not stand close to the carriageway, between your vehicle and traffic, or where the vehicle could be pushed into you.
- Do not attempt roadside repairs, open fuse boxes, or remove panels while exposed to fast traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on the carriageway on a motorway.
- Do not keep restarting the car repeatedly in panic if you are in an exposed place; prioritise being safe and visible first.
- Do not drive while wiping the inside of the windscreen with your hand or a cloth.

## What to do now
1. Secure the stop you already have.
   - Keep hazard warning lights on.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights if they still work.
   - Stay as far from moving traffic as you safely can.

2. If you are not in a place of relative safety, act as an emergency.
   - If you are stopped in a live lane, cannot get left, or feel at risk of being hit, stay in the vehicle with your seat belt on, keep hazards on, and call 999 asking for the police.
   - Use the vehicle SOS button if fitted and you can reach it safely.

3. If you are on a motorway and it is safe to leave the vehicle, get away from traffic.
   - Leave by the side furthest from traffic.
   - Move well away from the carriageway, behind a safety barrier if there is one and it is safe to reach.
   - Do not return to the vehicle just because it is raining, cold, or inconvenient.

4. If it is not safe to get out, stay belted in.
   - Keep hazards on.
   - Call 999 asking for the police, or use the vehicle SOS button if fitted.
   - Tell them you have lost screen-based climate or demist controls and cannot be sure you can keep the windscreen clear.

5. Protect visibility before thinking about driving again.
   - Crack two windows slightly if it is safe to do so, to reduce moisture inside the vehicle.
   - If any physical controls still work, set fresh air rather than recirculation, increase fan speed, and direct airflow to the windscreen.
   - Check for separate front demist, rear screen heater, heated windscreen, fan, or temperature buttons that may still work without the main screen.
   - If the windscreen is still misting while stationary, assume it may become unsafe again as soon as you move.

6. Try one calm reset only if you are safely stopped.
   - Put the vehicle in Park or neutral as appropriate and apply the parking brake.
   - Turn the ignition or vehicle power off, wait briefly, then restart once.
   - Do not keep cycling power if you are roadside-exposed or the screen remains blank.

7. If you are in a motorway emergency area, use the emergency telephone before leaving.
   - Use the orange emergency telephone if you can reach it safely.
   - Follow the operator’s advice before rejoining; a lane may need to be closed so you can leave safely.
   - Tell the operator if demist or defrost is unavailable and visibility may be affected.

8. Arrange recovery if the controls do not return.
   - Call your breakdown provider, insurer roadside helpline, or another recovery service.
   - Say: “The main screen is blank and I have lost defrost or climate controls.”
   - Give your exact location using the road name, junction, emergency area number, marker post, driver location sign, map pin, or nearby landmark.

9. Capture only quick repair information if it does not delay safety.
   - Note the time, weather, and what stopped working, such as front demist, fan, temperature, rear screen heater, or heated windscreen.
   - Take a photo of the blank screen or warning lights only if you can do it safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether this is software, a fuse, a battery issue, a screen module, or a climate-control fault right now.
- You do not need to decide about warranty, repair cost, or which garage to use while you are still roadside.
- You do not need to keep testing features; one safe reset attempt is enough for now.
- You can check for safety recalls after the immediate roadside risk has passed.

## Important reassurance
A blank main screen can make the vehicle feel unusable, especially if climate controls are screen-based. The immediate question is simpler: if you cannot keep a clear view through the windscreen, choosing help or recovery is a safe and reasonable decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce danger and protect visibility. A garage, recovery service, or the manufacturer may need to diagnose the screen, electrical system, or climate controls later.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not mechanical, legal, financial, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are, or you are stopped in a dangerous position on a motorway, prioritise emergency help.
