# What to do if… you get a new severe headache with a droopy eyelid or noticeably unequal pupils (UK)

## Short answer
**Call 999 immediately or go to the nearest A&E.** This combination of symptoms requires an emergency neurological assessment to rule out serious vascular issues, such as a brain aneurysm or carotid artery dissection.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not drive yourself to the hospital.** Use an ambulance or have someone else drive you if an ambulance is delayed.
- **Do not wait to see if it passes.** These symptoms are time-sensitive.
- **Do not "self-test" your eyes.** Avoid shining bright flashlights directly into your eyes or poking at the eyelids to check for sensation.
- **Do not take aspirin or ibuprofen "just in case."** Do not take any medication until a clinician has assessed you, as some causes involve bleeding.
- **Do not ignore the symptoms even if the headache starts to fade.** The eye changes remain an emergency even if the pain lessens.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 and state clearly:** "I have a new, severe headache with a sudden droopy eyelid and unequal pupils."
   - Mention if you also have double vision or if the pain started suddenly (like a "thunderclap").
2. **Note which side is affected.** **Identify which eyelid is drooping and which pupil looks larger or smaller.**
   - If someone is with you, have them take a clear photo of your eyes with a phone flash from a distance (do not hold the light close to the eye). This helps doctors see the initial state if symptoms shift.
3. **Write down the exact "Time Last Normal."** **Record the minute the headache started and when the eye changes appeared.**
   - Include any triggers, such as coughing, straining, or physical exertion.
4. **Unlock your door and contain pets.** **Ensure emergency responders can enter your home without delay.**
   - If you are alone, leave the front door unlocked and sit in a visible area near the entrance.
5. **Gather your "Red Flag" essentials if they are within reach.** 
   - A list of medications, especially blood thinners (anticoagulants).
   - Any history of high blood pressure or recent head/neck injury.
6. **Position yourself for safety.** **Sit or lie down with your head elevated.** 
   - If you feel nauseated or vomit, roll onto your side (recovery position) to keep your airway clear.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact your GP surgery or wait for a callback.
- You do not need to pack a hospital bag; focus only on immediate departure.
- You do not need to determine why this is happening; the diagnosis is for the hospital team.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant anxiety when your vision or face changes suddenly. Calling 999 for these symptoms is the correct and expected response in the UK healthcare system. Medical staff prefer to assess and rule out serious causes rather than have you wait at home.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and emergency navigation only. Clinical diagnosis requires specialist neuroimaging (CT or MRI) and physical examination by a doctor.

## Important note
This information is for emergency guidance and does not constitute medical advice. If you are experiencing these symptoms, contact 999 or go to A&E immediately.
