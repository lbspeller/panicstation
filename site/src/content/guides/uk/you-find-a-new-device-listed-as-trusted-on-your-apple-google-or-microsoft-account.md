# What to do if… you find a new device listed as “trusted” on your Apple, Google, or Microsoft account (UK)

## Short answer
Secure your account from a device you already control, update your password, and remove the unknown device from your trusted list immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t respond to the device directly by messaging it or clicking links in security alert emails or texts.
- Don’t just remove the device; if you do not change your password, the attacker may simply re-add it.
- Don’t use the unrecognised device or a public computer to perform security resets.
- Don’t ignore minor changes like new email forwarding rules or "remembered" browser sessions.
- Don’t factory reset your own known devices yet if they are your only way to receive two-factor authentication (2FA) codes.

## What to do now
1. **Access your account from a known, secure device immediately.** Use your own phone or computer that you know is safe; avoid using public Wi-Fi or shared machines while performing these steps.
2. **Update your password and review your two-factor authentication (2FA) settings.** 
   - Change your password to a unique, long phrase not used elsewhere.
   - Ensure you have at least one 2FA method active that you alone control (such as an authenticator app or your personal phone number).
   - If "Passkeys" have been added that you do not recognise, remove them.
3. **Remove the unknown device from your account’s trusted device list.** 
   - **Apple Account:** Go to Settings > [Your Name] and scroll to the device list; tap the unrecognised device and select "Remove from Account."
   - **Google:** Go to the "Security" tab, select "Your devices," and click "Sign out" on any device you do not recognise.
   - **Microsoft:** Go to the "Devices" page to unlink the device; then go to "Advanced security options" and select "Sign out everywhere."
4. **Trigger a remote sign-out for all active sessions.** Use the platform's "Sign out of all sessions" feature to force every device to re-authenticate.
   - Note: For Microsoft accounts, this process can take up to 24 hours to complete and does not automatically sign you out of Xbox consoles.
5. **Audit your recovery information for unauthorised changes.** Check that your recovery email addresses, backup phone numbers, and security questions have not been altered to point to the attacker.
6. **Check for "silent" access points like email forwarding or third-party apps.** 
   - Look in your email settings for any rules that forward your mail to an external address.
   - Review "Apps with access to your account" and revoke permission for any tool you did not install.
7. **Review your financial history for unauthorised purchases or linked cards.** Check the App Store, Google Play, or Microsoft Store for recent transactions and verify that no new payment methods have been added.
8. **Report the incident to the national reporting centre if fraud has occurred.** 
   - **England, Wales, and Northern Ireland:** Report to **Report Fraud** online or by calling 0300 123 2040.
   - **Scotland:** Contact **Police Scotland** via 101.
   - Call 999 if there is an immediate threat or a crime is currently in progress.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace your hardware immediately unless you have specific evidence of a local virus or malware.
- You do not need to notify all of your contacts until you have confirmed whether your email or messaging apps were actually used to send spam.
- You do not need to decide on a long-term password manager today; focus first on securing the primary affected account.

## Important reassurance
Finding an unfamiliar device is a common security event often caused by automated credential testing. Methodically updating your credentials and removing the device is the standard and effective way to regain full control. You have noticed the change, which is the most important step in stopping further access.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for personal digital accounts. If the compromised account is linked to a business, government, or high-security organization, you must also follow your employer's internal security reporting procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional cybersecurity advice. Digital platform interfaces and security terminology are subject to change by providers; always verify you are on an official .apple.com, .google.com, or .microsoft.com domain before entering credentials.
