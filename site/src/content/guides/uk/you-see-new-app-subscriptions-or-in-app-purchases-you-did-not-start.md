# What to do if… you see new app subscriptions or in-app purchases you did not start (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately cancel the subscription in your device settings to stop future billing and submit a formal refund request through the official Apple or Google portal.

## Do not do these things
- Don't ignore small "test" payments, as these are often followed by larger unauthorized transactions.
- Don't uninstall the app thinking it will stop the billing; subscriptions must be cancelled through your account settings.
- Don't click links in "receipt" emails or texts that look suspicious, as these may be phishing attempts; go directly to the official platform websites.
- Don't wait for the bank to "fix it" first; the platform (Apple/Google) usually requires you to attempt a refund through them before a bank dispute can proceed.
- Don't assume your account is "hacked" until you have checked if a family member with access to the device or "Family Sharing" made the purchase.

## What to do now
1. **Capture evidence of the transaction immediately.**
   - Take screenshots of the subscription list in your settings and the specific entry in your "Purchase History" or "Order History."
   - Note the app name, the exact amount, the date, and the Order ID or Transaction ID.
2. **Cancel the active subscription to prevent the next bill.**
   - **For Apple:** Open **Settings** → [Your Name] → **Subscriptions** → Select the item → **Cancel Subscription**.
   - **For Android:** Open the **Google Play app** → Profile Icon → **Payments & subscriptions** → **Subscriptions** → Select the item → **Cancel**.
3. **Submit a formal refund request through the platform's automated tool.**
   - **Apple users:** Go to **reportaproblem.apple.com**, sign in, select "I'd like to," then "Request a refund" and choose the reason "I didn't authorize this purchase."
   - **Google Play users:** Go to **play.google.com**, click your profile icon → **Payments & subscriptions** → **Budget & order history** → **Report a problem**.
4. **Secure the account used for the purchase.**
   - Change your Apple Account or Google Account password immediately to a unique, strong password.
   - Ensure **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** is active and review the list of "Trusted Devices" or "Devices signed in," removing any you do not recognize.
5. **Contact your bank or payment provider if the refund is denied or the amount is large.**
   - Call your bank via the number on the back of your card or dial **159** (the UK's secure hotline for banks) to report unauthorized transactions.
   - If the charge appeared on your mobile phone bill, contact your network provider and check the **Phone-paid Services Authority (PSA)** website to identify the service provider.
6. **Report the incident to the national fraud reporting service.**
   - If you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) online at reportfraud.police.uk or by calling **0300 123 2040**.
   - If you are in Scotland, report the incident to **Police Scotland** by calling **101**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine exactly how your details were obtained before taking the steps above.
- You do not need to replace your physical device or factory reset it immediately unless you suspect deep malware infection.
- You do not need to wait for the "pending" status on your bank statement to change before starting the platform's refund process, though some systems may require the transaction to "clear" first.

## Important reassurance
Digital purchase errors and unauthorized account access are common and usually manageable if caught quickly. Following the cancellation and refund sequence is the standard way to resolve these issues. Feeling frustrated or startled is a normal response to finding unexpected charges.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK users experiencing unauthorized digital charges. It does not cover long-term identity theft recovery or legal proceedings against developers.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Refund eligibility is determined by the specific policies of Apple, Google, or your financial institution. If you suspect your entire identity is compromised, seek further assistance from CIFAS or your local police.
