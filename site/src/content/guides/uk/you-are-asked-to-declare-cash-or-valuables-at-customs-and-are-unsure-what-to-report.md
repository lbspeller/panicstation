# What to do if… you are asked to declare cash or valuables at customs and are unsure what to report (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and say clearly that you want to declare correctly. Ask whether the officer means a cash declaration for money you are physically carrying, or a goods declaration for items you are bringing in.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t say “nothing to declare” just to get through faster if you are unsure.
- Don’t split cash between people or bags to avoid a threshold; if you are travelling as a family or group, explain the combined total.
- Don’t hide, move, re-pack, or pass cash or valuables to someone else once you are being asked about them.
- Don’t guess the rules for Northern Ireland if your journey involves Northern Ireland; ask which rule applies to your route.
- Don’t treat money in a bank account, on a bank card, or in an ordinary payment app as “cash” unless the officer is asking about funds more generally.
- Don’t argue about definitions at the desk; ask the officer what they need you to declare.

## What to do now
1. **Ask which declaration they mean.** Say: “I want to do this correctly. Do you mean cash I am physically carrying, or goods and valuables I am bringing in?”

2. **If they mean cash, give a careful total.** Add together notes, coins, travellers’ cheques, bearer bonds, and any signed cheques not made out to a person or organisation. If you are carrying cash in or out of Northern Ireland, also mention money orders, gold coins, bullion, nuggets, and prepaid cards if you have them.

3. **Check the route before relying on a threshold.**
   - For Great Britain, you must declare cash of £10,000 or more when carrying it between Great Britain and a country outside the UK.
   - For Northern Ireland, you must declare cash of €10,000 or more when carrying it between Northern Ireland and any non-EU country.
   - You must also declare cash of €10,000 or more when arriving in Northern Ireland from Great Britain.
   - You do not need to declare any amount of cash when travelling from Northern Ireland to Great Britain.

4. **If you are travelling as a family or group, say the group total.** The declaration rule can apply to the total carried by the family or group, even if each person is carrying less than the threshold.

5. **If they mean valuables or goods, separate what you travelled with from what you acquired abroad.** Say which items are used personal belongings you travelled with, such as your everyday watch, jewellery, phone, or laptop. For new purchases, gifts, duty-free items, or anything that may be over your allowance, say that you want to declare them and show any receipts you have.

6. **Use the official declaration route if needed.** For cash, you can declare online from 72 hours before travel, or at UK customs when you arrive by following “goods to declare” or “red channel” signs and telling a Border Force officer you want to declare cash. For goods, you can usually declare online from 5 days before you are due to arrive in the UK, or use the red channel or red point phone if you are already at the border.

7. **Keep proof simple.** Have ready any currency exchange receipt, bank withdrawal slip, purchase receipt, gift note, valuation, or a written breakdown of amounts by currency and item.

## What can wait
- You do not need to make a perfect exchange-rate calculation before speaking; give the most honest total you can and ask for help if needed.
- You do not need to decide now whether you agree with any later tax, duty, penalty, or seizure decision.
- You do not need a long explanation; short, factual answers are enough.
- You do not need to solve every future customs question at once; deal first with the declaration being asked about now.

## Important reassurance
This confusion is common because “declare” can mean cash, goods, valuables, or commercial items depending on the question and the route. Saying you are unsure and asking to declare correctly is a practical way to reduce the risk of a simple mistake becoming more serious.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are being asked at or near the UK border. Later decisions about tax, duty, penalties, seized cash or goods, appeals, or commercial imports may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are unsure what applies to your journey or items, ask the customs officer to confirm the correct declaration route.
