# What to do if… your phone is stuck in a restart loop after a software update (UK)

## Short answer
Stop trying random fixes. Keep the phone powered and cool, do one proper forced restart, then use the phone maker’s official update, recovery, or repair route before choosing anything that says restore, reset, erase, or wipe.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep forcing restarts over and over; if one proper forced restart does not break the loop, move on.
- Don’t factory reset as your first move, because it can erase photos, messages, app data, and two-factor authentication access.
- Don’t install random “boot loop repair” tools on a computer; use Apple, Google, Samsung, or your phone maker’s official route where possible.
- Don’t unplug the phone while an official update, recovery, or restore is already running unless the official tool tells you to.
- Don’t keep the phone in a tight case, under bedding, or in direct heat while it loops.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilise the phone first.**  
   Put it on a known-good charger and cable. Remove the case if it feels warm. Disconnect headphones, docks, adapters, and other accessories. If it is hot, stop pressing buttons and let it cool somewhere safe while it stays plugged in.

2. **Do one proper forced restart, then stop.**  
   - **iPhone models using the current button sequence:** quickly press Volume Up, quickly press Volume Down, then press and hold the Side button until the Apple logo appears. Older iPhone models can use different buttons, so check Apple’s guide for your model.
   - **Samsung Galaxy phones:** many models use Power or Side key plus Volume Down for a forced restart.
   - **Other Android phones:** the button combination varies by maker and model. Use your exact model’s official support page if Power alone does not work.  
   If the phone returns to the restart loop, do not keep repeating this step.

3. **If it is an iPhone, use Apple’s recovery route and avoid Restore unless you accept erasing.**  
   Connect the iPhone to a Mac or PC and follow Apple’s recovery mode instructions for your model. If the computer offers **Update** or **Restore**, choose **Update** first if available. **Restore** reinstalls iOS and erases all data on the device.

4. **If it is Android, try Safe Mode only if you can reach it.**  
   Safe Mode can help if the loop is caused by a downloaded app. If the phone boots in Safe Mode, use that brief access to back up essentials, remove recently installed apps, and check that your Google account recovery options work. If the loop continues even in Safe Mode, it is less likely to be a normal downloaded-app problem.

5. **Use Recovery Mode cautiously if it appears.**  
   If an Android recovery menu appears, choose **Reboot system now** first. Do not choose **Wipe data/factory reset** unless you are ready for the phone to be erased. Only use a cache option if your phone maker’s support page for your exact model says that option is safe and does not erase personal files.

6. **If the phone gives you even a brief normal boot, protect access before investigating.**  
   First check that you can sign in to your Apple Account or Google Account from another device. Then copy or sync the most important photos, contacts, and notes you can reach. If you rely on app-based two-factor codes, check from a computer or another device whether you already have backup codes or another sign-in method.

7. **Before paying for repair or agreeing to a wipe, note the basics.**  
   Write down the phone model, the update that just happened if you know it, the time the loop started, what appears on screen, and the steps you have already tried. If the phone is new, recently bought, under warranty, or supplied with a contract, contact the retailer, manufacturer, or provider before paying a third party. Citizens Advice can help you check UK consumer routes if you are unsure.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the update, the phone, an app, or you caused this.
- You do not need to buy third-party repair software to make progress.
- You do not need to open the phone, remove parts, or choose a factory reset while there are still official non-erasing options to try.
- You do not need to sort every account now; prioritise the accounts that could lock you out if the phone has to be erased.

## Important reassurance
A restart loop after an update is frightening because it looks like the phone has gone, but there are still safe first steps. The safest order is power and cooling first, one correct restart attempt, then official recovery or repair routes that avoid erasing data where possible.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and reduce the chance of accidental data loss. If official recovery fails, the phone overheats, a button is stuck, the computer will not recognise it, or there is physical damage, the next step may need manufacturer, authorised repair, retailer, provider, or consumer-rights help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not device-specific repair, legal, financial, or professional advice. Button combinations, recovery menus, warranty routes, and consumer options can vary by model, software version, retailer, contract, and location within the UK. Anything described as restore, reset, erase, or wipe may delete data, so pause before choosing it.
