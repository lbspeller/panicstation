# What to do if… you are asked for multiple certified copies of a death certificate and do not know why (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and do not buy a large number straight away. Make a short list of who needs to be told, then ask each organisation whether it needs an original certified copy of the death certificate and whether it will return it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume every organisation needs to keep an original certificate.
- Do not send your only certified copy unless you know whether it will be returned.
- Do not pay a third party just because they say you need lots of copies; check directly with the organisation involved.
- Do not present a photocopy as if it were an official certified copy.
- Do not try to notify everyone at once if you are overwhelmed; work through the list in small batches.

## What to do now
1. **Make a simple “who needs proof” list.** It may include banks or building societies, insurers, pension providers, an employer, utilities, a landlord or mortgage provider, investment platforms, and anyone helping deal with the estate.
2. **Contact the most urgent organisations first.** Ask: “Do you need an original certified copy of the death certificate, and will you keep it or return it?” Write the answer next to each name.
3. **Use Tell Us Once if it applies.** In England, Scotland and Wales, Tell Us Once can report a death to most government organisations in one go after the death is registered or after you have a final or interim death certificate from the coroner. It does not tell private organisations such as banks, insurers, utility companies or private pension providers.
4. **If you have no certified copies yet, start with a small number.** More than one can be useful so you are not blocked by one organisation holding a certificate, but order more only when you know who needs originals.
5. **If you are registering the death now or soon, ask the registrar about copies.** Ask how to get official copies now and how to order more later, so you do not feel forced into a large decision on the spot.
6. **Keep one document-handling rule.** Keep one certified copy at home in a safe folder. For any copy you send away, use a trackable or recorded method where you can, or follow the organisation’s secure document process.
7. **If someone is pressing you for copies, ask for the requirement in writing.** An email saying what they need, whether they accept a scan or branch check, and whether they return originals can prevent repeat requests and lost paperwork.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the perfect number of certificates today.
- You do not need to notify every organisation immediately; start with the ones affecting urgent access, immediate bills, insurance, or essential accounts.
- You do not need to understand probate, tax or the whole estate process before checking which organisations actually need proof.

## Important reassurance
Being told you need “multiple copies” is common and does not mean you have missed something. Different organisations use different checks, and many simply need to see official proof before they can update or close an account.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce pressure, protect original documents and help you avoid unnecessary costs. Later decisions about probate, estate administration, tax or disputes may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial or professional advice. Requirements vary by organisation and official certificate routes differ across England and Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland, so check directly with the registrar, the relevant official ordering service, or the organisation asking for proof.
