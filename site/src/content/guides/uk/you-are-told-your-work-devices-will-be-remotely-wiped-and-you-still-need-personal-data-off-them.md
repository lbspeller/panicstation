# What to do if… you are told your work devices will be remotely wiped and you still need personal data off them (UK)

## Short answer
Gather neutral evidence of the advert immediately and request a private, formal meeting with your manager to clarify its purpose. Do not resign or make written accusations until you have received a formal explanation.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign in a "pre-emptive" strike or threaten to quit during the first conversation.
- Do not send aggressive or emotional messages to management, HR, or recruitment agencies.
- Do not download, forward, or print confidential company files or client lists as "leverage."
- Do not confront colleagues or post about the situation on public social media platforms.
- Do not sign any settlement agreements or contract variations on the spot without legal review.
- Do not record meetings covertly unless you have checked your specific employer’s policy and the legal implications for 2026 privacy standards.
- Do not assume the advert automatically means you are being fired; it may relate to business expansion or a different department.

## What to do now
1. **Capture a neutral record of the advert using a personal device.** Screen-record or screenshot the full listing, ensuring you include the date, the job title, the salary/grade, the specific duties, and the URL. Save these files to personal storage rather than work-monitored cloud accounts.
2. **Compare the advert details against your current contract and job description.** Check for subtle differences such as "Fixed Term," "Maternity Cover," or a different reporting line that might indicate it is not a direct replacement for your specific seat.
3. **Establish a factual timeline log on a personal document.** Record exactly when you saw the advert, any recent performance feedback you received, and any verbal comments made by management regarding team structure. Keep this log objective and store it outside of company systems.
4. **Request an immediate, private meeting with your line manager and HR.** State clearly: "I have seen an advert that appears to match my current role and would like to understand what this relates to and if my position is at risk." Use a neutral, inquisitive tone.
5. **Ask for a written summary of the explanation provided during the meeting.** If the manager claims it is an error or a different role, ask for this to be confirmed via email so you have a formal record of their position.
6. **Check your length of service and current redundancy rights.** Note that from 2026/2027, the qualifying period for unfair dismissal is shifting; verify if you have passed the relevant 6-month or 2-year threshold for full protection.
7. **Contact the Fair Work Agency or Acas if the explanation is evasive.** If your employer refuses to meet or gives contradictory information, seek guidance on whether this constitutes a breach of the "trust and confidence" implied in your contract.
8. **Continue performing your duties to a high standard while seeking clarity.** Maintaining professional conduct prevents the employer from using a "drop in performance" or "conduct issues" as a secondary reason for dismissal while the advert is live.
9. **Engage your trade union representative if you are a member.** Early involvement of a rep can provide a witness for meetings and ensure that any potential redundancy or restructuring process follows the 2026 statutory codes.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to start a legal claim for constructive dismissal.
- You do not need to update your CV or start applying for new roles in a state of panic.
- You do not need to inform your entire team or "warn" others about the advert.
- You do not need to accept any verbal offers of a "payout" or "mutual separation" immediately.

## Important reassurance
Finding your own job advertised is a high-stress event that often triggers an immediate "fight or flight" response. It is normal to feel betrayed or anxious, but these feelings do not have to dictate your next move. Taking a 60-second "safe pause" to breathe before responding to any internal communication will help you maintain your professional standing and protect your legal rights.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. It is designed to stabilise the situation and prevent irreversible errors like "resignation in the heat of the moment." Further steps regarding grievances or employment tribunals require specific legal assessment.

## Important note
This guide is for general information and does not constitute legal advice. Employment law is subject to specific contractual facts and 2026 legislative updates. If you are being pressured to sign documents or feel you are being targeted due to a protected characteristic, contact Acas or a qualified employment solicitor immediately.
