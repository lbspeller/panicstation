# What to do if… you have been in the cold and you cannot stop shivering or you start feeling confused (UK)

## Short answer
Treat these symptoms as a medical emergency (hypothermia). Get to a warm, sheltered area immediately and call **999** for an ambulance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use a hot bath, hot water bottle, or heat lamp to warm up.
- Do not rub or massage your skin, arms, or legs.
- Do not drink alcohol or caffeinated drinks.
- Do not try to warm up rapidly with direct heat (like sitting right next to a heater).
- Do not move or walk more than absolutely necessary if you feel clumsy or confused.
- Do not ignore the symptoms if the shivering stops but you still feel cold and confused.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a warm, sheltered environment immediately.** Go indoors or into a vehicle as quickly as possible.
2. **Call 999 or 112 and request an ambulance.** State clearly that the person (or you) has been exposed to the cold and is experiencing confusion and uncontrollable shivering.
3. **Handle the person with extreme gentleness.** Avoid sudden movements or jostling, as this can trigger dangerous heart rhythms in severe hypothermia.
4. **Remove wet clothing and replace with dry layers.** If you cannot change fully, remove the wettest items and wrap the person in dry blankets, coats, or sleeping bags.
5. **Cover the head and neck.** Use a hat, hood, or dry towel to prevent further heat loss from the head.
6. **Insulate the person from the ground.** If waiting for help, ensure the person is sitting or lying on a dry, insulated surface (like a rug, coat, or cardboard) rather than a cold floor or earth.
7. **Provide a warm, sweet, non-alcoholic drink ONLY if the person is fully conscious and can swallow safely.** Stop immediately if there is any coughing or choking.
8. **Monitor breathing and responsiveness constantly.** Keep talking to the person to ensure they stay awake until paramedics arrive.
   - If they become unconscious and stop breathing normally, follow the 999 operator's instructions for CPR.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the exact body temperature with a thermometer before calling for help.
- You do not need to identify the exact cause (e.g., wind chill versus duration) right now.
- You do not need to decide if the symptoms are "severe enough" for an ambulance; confusion is always an emergency trigger.

## Important reassurance
Shivering is a natural response to cold, but confusion and drowsiness indicate your body’s core temperature has dropped significantly. It is normal to feel frightened or disoriented in this state; focus only on the immediate steps of getting dry and getting help.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for suspected hypothermia only. Medical professionals must assess for underlying complications such as frostbite, blood sugar issues, or cardiac strain.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis or treatment. Hypothermia is life-threatening and requires immediate clinical intervention.
