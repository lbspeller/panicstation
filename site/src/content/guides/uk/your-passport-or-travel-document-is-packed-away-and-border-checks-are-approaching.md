# What to do if… your passport or travel document is packed away and border checks are approaching (UK)

## Short answer
Move your passport into your hand, coat pocket, or small bag before you reach the checkpoint or before officials ask for it. If you need space or help opening luggage safely, tell transport staff early and briefly.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t start a frantic unpack in the aisle while the vehicle is moving or while people are boarding or getting off.
- Don’t hand your passport to a stranger to “pass forward”.
- Don’t walk away from your luggage or seat area with your passport still buried inside it.
- Don’t rely on a photo or copy of your passport unless a staff member or border officer specifically tells you to use it for that situation.
- Don’t hide the problem until the last moment if you genuinely cannot reach or find the passport.

## What to do now
1. **Pause for 10 seconds:** identify the bag, pocket, or layer where the passport is most likely to be. Ignore everything else until the passport is in reach.
2. **Choose the safest place to open the bag:** use your seat area if you can. If you are blocking people, ask staff where you can briefly open the luggage without getting in the way.
3. **Keep the bag controlled:** keep one hand on the bag, loop a strap around your wrist or leg, or keep the case directly against you while you search.
4. **Get the passport presentation-ready:** take it out of any cover or wallet, close the bag again, and keep the passport in your hand, coat pocket, or small bag.
5. **If you are on a coach approaching UK border control:** be ready to get off the coach with your travel documents. Move your passport now so you are not searching while stepping off.
6. **If you are on Eurostar to or from London:** remember that passport and luggage checks are usually before boarding. If you are still in the station queue, get the passport out before the document check point.
7. **Keep relevant documents together:** put your passport with any visa, share code, ETA confirmation, ticket, or valid physical immigration document if relevant to you.
8. **If the passport is under heavy items:** stop wrestling with the bag in a crowded aisle. Ask staff whether there is a safer moment or place to move the luggage.
9. **If you cannot find it after one focused check:** check the most likely places once, such as outer pockets, document sleeve, day bag, and jacket. Then tell staff clearly: “I cannot find my passport yet, and border checks are coming.”
10. **If officials ask before you are ready:** stay where you are, make eye contact, and say: “My passport is in my luggage at my seat. May I retrieve it now?” Go directly to it and keep your luggage in sight.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganise your whole bag now.
- You do not need to work out why the passport was packed away.
- You do not need to decide about complaints, compensation, rebooking, or future packing systems until you know whether there is an actual delay or refusal.
- You do not need to contact anyone else unless you cannot locate the passport.

## Important reassurance
This is a common travel scramble. A calm, visible, direct request to staff is usually safer than trying to fix it silently while the queue or vehicle is moving.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. If you cannot locate the passport, later decisions may need help from the transport operator, border officials, or the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate if you are abroad.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate practical next steps, not legal, immigration, travel, or professional advice. Border and carrier procedures can vary by route, nationality, document type, and official decision.
