# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to go to a private room at a party and you feel unsafe (UK)

## Short answer
Move back towards people, light, staff, the host, or another public area, and get another person involved. You do not need to be polite or give reasons before you move away.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go somewhere private to keep things calm if you feel uneasy.
- Don’t keep negotiating, debating, or explaining your reasons.
- Don’t accept a drink, shot, or water from them if you did not see it poured or open it yourself.
- Don’t let them take your phone, block an exit, or steer you away from other people.
- Don’t blame yourself for mixed signals or worry that you are overreacting if you feel unsafe.
- Don’t stay alone with them while deciding what to do next.

## What to do now
1. **Move first, explain later.**  
   Say one clear line if you can: **“No. I’m staying out here.”** Then step towards people, light, noise, staff, the host, or the main room.

2. **Pick one person and ask for help directly.**  
   Speak to a friend, the host, bar staff, door staff, or another nearby person, not the person pressuring you. Say: **“Can you stay with me?”** or **“Can you walk me to the host, staff, or outside?”**

3. **Make the situation visible.**  
   If it is a house party, go to the host, kitchen, living room, or a group of people and say: **“I need help. Someone won’t leave me alone.”** If it is a venue, go to the bar or door staff and say: **“I feel unsafe. Please keep them away from me while I leave.”**

4. **Switch from managing them to leaving safely.**  
   Call or message a trusted person: **“I need you on the phone. I’m leaving now.”** If you came with friends, say to one named person: **“I’m going. Please come with me now.”**

5. **Get to a safer pause point.**  
   Aim for a staffed area, the host’s side, a group of people you trust, a well-lit main road with other people nearby, or a bathroom where you can lock the door briefly and message someone. Keep your phone with you.

6. **If you are in immediate danger, call 999.**  
   Try to speak to the operator if you can, even by whispering. If you cannot speak safely, listen to the operator and follow the prompts; on a mobile, you may be told to press 55 or tap so the call can be connected to police.

7. **If anything sexual happened without consent, or you are not sure, you can get confidential support without deciding about police now.**  
   The 24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line is for people aged 16 or over in England and Wales and can be contacted on 0808 500 2222 or by online chat. A Sexual Assault Referral Centre can also offer confidential medical and practical support; you do not have to report to police to ask for help.

8. **Optional, only if something happened and you may want options later:**  
   Try not to delete messages, photos, call logs, or location information right now. Put your focus on getting safe first.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to report, complain, confront them, or tell a group what happened.
- You do not need to work out whether it “counts”, what their intentions were, or whether you were clear enough.
- You do not need to write a perfect account now.
- You do not need to make long-term decisions while you are still trying to get safe.

## Important reassurance
Feeling pressured, frozen, confused, or suddenly unsure can happen when someone is pushing past your boundaries. Trusting discomfort is a protective response, not an overreaction. If someone is trying to isolate you after you have shown reluctance, that is enough reason to move towards help.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk and stabilise the moment. Later decisions, including medical care, reporting, complaints, or ongoing support, may need specialist help and can be taken at your pace.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate safety and support. It is not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or need urgent medical help, call 999.
