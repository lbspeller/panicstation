# What to do if… your work authorisation or security clearance is suddenly marked under review (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and get the exact reason in writing: what is “under review”, which team or system marked it, and what they need from you. Do not continue restricted work or access sensitive material if your access has been paused.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign, “quit before you’re fired”, or agree to a rushed contract change while you are shocked.
- Do not keep doing restricted work if you have been told your access, clearance, pass, or account is paused.
- Do not send work files, sensitive material, screenshots, or internal messages to personal email or cloud storage.
- Do not guess the cause or confront colleagues in a heated way.
- Do not pay a “fixer” or share passport, visa, clearance, or identity details outside proper HR, security, adviser, or solicitor channels.
- Do not ignore dates or response instructions in any written notice.

## What to do now
1. **Get clarity in writing on what is being reviewed.** Ask HR, your manager, or security:
   - whether this is about your **right to work**, your **security clearance or vetting**, your site or system access, or more than one of these;
   - the exact wording being used, such as “right to work re-check”, “Home Office check”, “clearance review”, or “access suspended pending review”;
   - what they need from you, how to send it safely, the response date, and who is handling the review internally.

2. **If it is about right to work, use the official proof route rather than arguing from memory.**
   - If you can use digital immigration status, generate a fresh right-to-work share code and give it to HR.
   - If the share code route is not available or does not show what HR needs, ask HR which right-to-work route they are using for your documents or status.
   - If you cannot prove your right to work through the usual route, or you are awaiting a Home Office decision, ask HR whether they need to use the Home Office Employer Checking Service.
   - If HR receives a Positive Verification Notice, ask what it says about the work you can do and when the follow-up check is needed. A Positive Verification Notice usually gives the employer a statutory excuse for six months from the date stated in the notice.

3. **If it is about security clearance or vetting, use your organisation’s security route.**
   - Ask who you should contact now: your line manager, local security team, personnel security team, or Security Controller/Manager.
   - Ask whether your access is temporarily suspended, restricted, lapsed, or withdrawn, and what that means for your badge, site access, remote access, devices, and need-to-know limits.
   - Follow written handling instructions for any sensitive material, equipment, documents, passes, or accounts.
   - If a UKSV case question is needed, ask your organisation which UKSV route applies. Some information may need to go through a sponsor or security contact, and very sensitive information should only be sent through a secure route.

4. **Stabilise the next working day.**
   - Ask: “Am I expected to stop work entirely, or can I be temporarily reassigned to non-restricted duties while this is checked?”
   - If you are sent home, locked out, or told not to work, ask for that instruction in writing.
   - Ask what happens with pay, rota, attendance records, and deadlines while the review is ongoing, without accepting any new contract terms on the spot.

5. **Create a simple record pack.**
   - Save the message saying “under review”, any access or work instructions, and any request for documents.
   - Keep a short timeline of dates, times, who you spoke to, what you were asked for, and what you provided.
   - Keep copies of share codes, document lists, case references, and submission confirmations where you can do so without breaching security rules.

6. **Get the right support if the problem is not quickly explained.**
   - For immigration or right-to-work issues, consider a registered immigration adviser or immigration solicitor.
   - For clearance, access, suspension, or contract issues, contact your union or professional association if you have one.
   - Keep the discussion factual: what was paused, what evidence was requested, what you provided, and what written decision or update you are waiting for.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to resign, change jobs, leave the UK, or start over.
- You do not need to write a long explanation of your life history unless a proper adviser or official process asks for it.
- You do not need to tell colleagues personal details about your immigration status, finances, vetting, family, or private life.
- You do not need to solve every possible outcome now; the immediate task is to identify the process, follow safe access rules, and keep records.

## Important reassurance
A sudden “under review” marker can begin as a records mismatch, routine re-check, access-control issue, or internal security trigger. It is not always a final decision, and your safest first move is calm written clarity, official verification routes, and not creating a separate security or conduct problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stop the situation getting worse and buy time. Later decisions about immigration strategy, employment rights, grievances, appeals, vetting reviews, or legal action may need specialist help based on the exact reason for the review.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, employment, security, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Employer policies, contracts, sponsorship duties, right-to-work evidence, and national security vetting procedures vary. If you are unsure whether you are allowed to keep working, entering a site, accessing a system, or handling sensitive material, pause and follow your security team’s written instructions.
