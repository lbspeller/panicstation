# What to do if… someone slips a note under your hotel door pressuring you to open it or call a number (UK)

## Short answer
Do not open the door or call the number on the note. Lock the door, move away from it, and contact the hotel front desk using the in-room phone or the hotel’s official number so staff can check the corridor.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door “just to check” or because the note claims it is urgent.
- Do not call, text, or message the number on the note, and do not scan any QR code it includes.
- Do not step into the corridor to investigate, argue, or confront anyone.
- Do not rely on someone claiming to be hotel staff unless the front desk confirms it through an official route.
- Do not share your name, room number, travel plans, payment details, or personal information with an unverified caller.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.**  
   Engage all locks, including the deadbolt or secondary latch if present. Turn on the lights and move away from the door, such as to the far side of the room.

2. **Contact the front desk through a trusted route.**  
   Use the room phone’s reception option if available, or call the hotel’s main number from your booking confirmation, hotel app, or the hotel’s official website. Say: “A note was slipped under my door pressuring me to open it or call a number. I’m in room [X]. Please send a manager or security to check the corridor now.”

3. **Ask the hotel to handle the corridor, not you.**  
   Ask staff to send security or a manager to your floor, stay nearby while they assess, and tell you when it is safe to open the door or move. Ask them not to send anyone into your room unless you have agreed it through the front desk.

4. **Ask for the incident to be recorded.**  
   Ask the hotel to log the time, your room number, what the note said, and who checked the corridor. Ask them to preserve or review corridor camera footage and electronic access records if the hotel uses them.

5. **Call police if there is immediate danger.**  
   Call 999 if someone is trying to enter, threatening you, refusing to leave, or you believe a crime is happening now. Call 101, or use an official police online reporting route, if the situation is suspicious but not an emergency.

6. **Keep the note without focusing on it for too long.**  
   Take a clear photo of the note, note the time, and put it in an envelope or clean bag. Avoid repeated handling.

7. **Move only with support if you do not feel safe staying put.**  
   Ask the front desk to escort you to the lobby or another staffed area. If you do not feel comfortable staying in the room, ask for a room move, ideally away from the same corridor or floor.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether it was a scam, a prank, or a targeted threat.
- You do not need to respond to the note, explain yourself to anyone outside the hotel, or prove anything.
- You do not need to pack, check out, or confront anyone before hotel staff or police have helped you reach a safer pause.
- Once you are safe, you can report a suspicious phone number by texting `Call` followed by the number to 7726.
- If you lost money or shared financial details, you can report fraud through Report Fraud in England, Wales or Northern Ireland, or to Police Scotland by calling 101 in Scotland.

## Important reassurance
Urgency and pressure are common ways to push someone into opening a door or making contact before they can think. Pausing, locking the door, and routing everything through the front desk is a normal and sensible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions, such as formal complaints, police reports, refunds, or travel changes, can wait until you are safe and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, travel, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 999.
