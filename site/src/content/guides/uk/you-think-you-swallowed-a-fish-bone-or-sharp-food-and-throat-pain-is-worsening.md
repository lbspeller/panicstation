# What to do if… you think you swallowed a fish bone or sharp food and throat pain is worsening (UK)

## Short answer
If you may have swallowed a fish bone or sharp food and the pain is worsening, stop trying to push it down with food and get urgent medical advice now. Call 999 if you have breathing trouble, choking, or cannot swallow saliva.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep eating bread, rice, marshmallow, or other solid food to try to force it down.
- Do not take big gulps to push it through.
- Do not put fingers, tweezers, cutlery, or other objects into your throat.
- Do not try to make yourself vomit.
- Do not keep doing repeated test swallows with solid food if it hurts.
- Do not lie flat if you feel something is stuck, you are drooling, or swallowing is getting harder.
- Do not put off urgent advice if the pain is worsening or swallowing is becoming difficult.

## What to do now
1. Check for emergency breathing or choking signs first. If you have trouble breathing, noisy breathing, choking, blue lips, severe distress, or you cannot speak in full sentences, call 999 now.
2. Stop eating. With a possible sharp bone or shard and worsening pain, do not keep testing it with food.
3. If you can swallow comfortably, take only small sips of water. If swallowing water is difficult, painful, or makes you cough or choke, do not force it.
4. Get urgent medical advice using the route for where you are:
   - In England, use NHS 111 online or call 111.
   - In Scotland, call NHS 24 on 111.
   - In Wales, use NHS 111 Wales or call 111.
   - In Northern Ireland, use nidirect health information or local urgent-care arrangements; call 999 for emergency symptoms.
5. Go to A&E now, or call 999 if you cannot get there safely, if you cannot swallow saliva, are drooling, have rapidly worsening or severe throat, neck, or chest pain, have blood in spit or vomit, repeated vomiting, fever, neck swelling, faintness, voice change with worsening symptoms, or any breathing concern.
6. When you speak to urgent care or arrive at A&E, say clearly: “I may have swallowed a fish bone or sharp food, and the throat pain is worsening.”
7. Write down the details while they are fresh: what you ate, when it happened, what you think was swallowed, what has changed, and whether you take blood thinners or have swallowing or oesophagus problems.
8. If you go for care, bring your medicines or a medication list. Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, breathing is affected, or symptoms are progressing.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it is definitely stuck or just a scratch.
- You do not need to keep checking by swallowing more solid food.
- You do not need to search for home-removal tricks or tools.
- You do not need to work out the likely treatment before you have been assessed.

## Important reassurance
A fish bone or sharp food can scratch the throat and feel as if something is still there. Worsening pain, trouble swallowing, drooling, bleeding, or breathing symptoms are still good reasons to get urgent help quickly.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on staying safe, avoiding common mistakes, and getting the right urgent assessment. Later decisions may need a clinician or specialist team.

## Important note
This is general information only, not medical or professional advice. If symptoms are worsening after a possible sharp object or fish bone, use urgent medical advice or emergency care.
