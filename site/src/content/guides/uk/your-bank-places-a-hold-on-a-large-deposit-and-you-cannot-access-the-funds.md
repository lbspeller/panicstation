# What to do if… your bank places a hold on a large deposit and you cannot access the funds (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank's official support line immediately to determine if the hold is a clearing delay or an account restriction, and request a specific list of evidence needed to release the funds.

## Do not do these things
- Don't transfer or forward any portion of the held funds to a third party if the deposit was unexpected, as this may be part of a "money mule" or overpayment scam.
- Don't attempt to bypass the hold by making multiple smaller transactions or "layering" money between different accounts.
- Don't provide sensitive login credentials, passcodes, or full ID scans to anyone who contacts you by phone or text regarding the hold.
- Don't repeatedly open new support tickets or phone the bank from different numbers, as this can trigger further security flags and reset internal review timers.
- Don't assume the funds will clear automatically by a specific deadline without receiving written confirmation from the bank.

## What to do now
1. **Identify all essential payments due within the next 72 hours.** 
   - List critical costs including rent, mortgage, utilities, childcare, travel for work, or life-sustaining prescriptions.
   - Contact these payees now to request a temporary payment extension or to pause late fees while the bank hold is active.

2. **Call your bank using the number on the back of your card or the official app.** 
   - **Ask specifically if the issue is a "deposit hold" (clearing delay) or an "account restriction" (compliance block).**
   - **Request a clear list of the documents required to release the funds.**
   - **Ask for the expected resolution timeframe and a reference number for the call.**
   - **Request that the bank confirms these details via a secure message in your online banking portal.**

3. **Prepare "Source of Funds" evidence matching the origin of the deposit.** 
   - Gather original digital or physical copies of:
   - A signed completion statement or solicitor’s letter for property sales.
   - Probate documents or executor letters for inheritance.
   - An insurance settlement letter.
   - A gift letter from the donor plus a bank statement showing the donor's original transfer.
   - A salary bonus letter or three months of payslips.
   - **Upload these documents only through the bank's verified secure portal or deliver them to a local branch if instructed.**

4. **Declare immediate financial hardship if you cannot cover material essentials.** 
   - State the following: "I am experiencing financial difficulty and cannot meet essential needs due to this hold."
   - **Ask if the bank can waive upcoming overdraft or late payment fees triggered by the hold.**
   - **Enquire about temporary access to a small portion of the balance for food or emergency bills.**
   - **Check eligibility for the Crisis and Resilience Fund (CRF) through your local authority if the bank cannot provide immediate liquidity.**

5. **Initiate a formal complaint to trigger regulatory response deadlines.** 
   - Request that the bank records your call as a formal complaint and provides a complaint reference number.
   - **Verify which timeline applies: 15 business days for payment service complaints (transfers/e-money) or 8 weeks for general banking holds.**
   - **Record the date the complaint was logged to track the window for escalation to the Financial Ombudsman Service.**

6. **Cease contact with the sender if the deposit source is unknown or suspicious.** 
   - Stop all communication with any individual who asks you to "test" the account or return a portion of the money.
   - **Notify the bank's fraud department if you suspect the deposit was made under false pretences or as part of a scam.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose a new bank or start an account closure process today.
- You do not need to contact the Financial Ombudsman Service until the bank’s internal complaint timeline (15 days or 8 weeks) has expired or you have received a "Final Response" letter.
- You do not need to hire a solicitor unless the hold involves an Account Freezing Order (AFO) served by a court.

## Important reassurance
Bank holds on large or unusual deposits are often triggered by automated anti-money laundering (AML) or fraud detection systems. These holds are frequently temporary and are designed to protect both the bank and the account holder. Most cases are resolved once the bank verifies the "narrative" of how the funds were accumulated and sees the required audit trail.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for accessing held funds and mitigating immediate financial fallout. It does not cover legal defense for suspected criminal activity or long-term debt management.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Bank disclosure limits may prevent staff from explaining the exact nature of a compliance review. Mandatory thresholds for court-ordered Account Freezing Orders (AFOs) in the UK are currently set at £3,000 as of June 2025.
