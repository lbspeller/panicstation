# What to do if… you smell a strong musty odour and find mould spreading quickly after dampness (UK)

## Short answer
Keep people out of the affected room, especially children, older people, pregnant people, and anyone with asthma, another lung condition, or a weakened immune system. Stop the damp source only if it is safe and obvious, then report it urgently to whoever is responsible for repairs.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t scrub, sand, dry-brush, or disturb mould on porous or dusty surfaces.
- Don’t paint over it, seal it in, or cover it with new wallpaper.
- Don’t blow air from the affected room into the rest of the home.
- Don’t vacuum mouldy dust or disturbed mould as a first response.
- Don’t keep sleeping in the room if the smell is strong or anyone is coughing, wheezing, short of breath, or getting eye or skin irritation.
- Don’t delay reporting because you are worried you will be blamed for ventilation, heating, or “lifestyle”.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from the smell.** Step out of the room, close the door, and keep children, pets, and higher-risk people away from it.

2. **Treat wet electrics as unsafe.** If dampness, leaks, or standing water are near sockets, switches, appliances, lights, or the consumer unit, do not touch wet electrics. If you can switch off power safely from a dry position, do that; do not touch the consumer unit if it is wet or damaged.

3. **Limit spread.** Keep the affected room door closed. If air is flowing out under the door, put a towel along the gap. If you can open a window in that room without lingering, open it a little so air vents outside.

4. **Stop the moisture source only if it is safe and obvious.**
   - If there is a fresh plumbing leak and you know where the stopcock is, turn the water off.
   - If rain is coming in, move essential items away from the wet area and use a bucket or towels to catch drips.
   - If condensation is adding to the damp, run an extractor that vents outside and avoid drying clothes in that room for now.

5. **Record what is happening.** Take photos or video of the mould, the damp patch or leak if visible, damaged items, and a wide shot of the room. Write down the date, time, smell, rooms affected, and any symptoms.

6. **Report it urgently and keep a written record.**
   - If you rent, report it today through your landlord, agent, council, housing association, or repairs line. Use email, an app, webform, or message if possible so there is a record.
   - Say that mould is spreading quickly after dampness, describe any visible leak or wet area, and mention any children, older people, pregnancy, asthma, lung condition, weakened immune system, or breathing symptoms in the home.
   - If you are in covered social housing in England, Awaab’s Law applies to significant damp and mould hazards. Significant damp and mould hazards must generally be investigated within 10 working days of being reported and made safe within 5 working days after the investigation finishes; emergency hazards have shorter timeframes.

7. **Escalate if the repair route is not acting and you are worried about safety.** Contact your local council’s housing or environmental health team and ask for help with damp and mould hazards. Tell them you have photos and copies of your reports.

8. **Only wipe a very small hard-surface patch if it is low risk.** A small patch on tile, glass, or metal can be wiped with detergent and water, then dried fully. Stop and focus on isolation and repair if it is on plasterboard, wallpaper, carpet, soft furnishings, insulation, a large area, or it keeps coming back quickly.

9. **Get health advice if symptoms are worrying.** Use NHS 111 or the urgent NHS advice route for your part of the UK if you are unsure what to do about cough, wheeze, breathlessness, fever, or coughing up blood. Call 999 for severe breathing difficulty or another life-threatening emergency.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the type of mould today.
- You do not need to buy a mould test kit before reporting it.
- You do not need to decide now whether to throw belongings away.
- You do not need to deep-clean the whole home; focus on keeping people away from the affected room and getting the damp source dealt with.

## Important reassurance
A strong musty smell with fast-spreading mould after damp usually means something is still wet. The right first move is to reduce exposure, avoid disturbing the mould, document it, and get the repair route moving.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Properly fixing mould usually means finding and resolving the moisture source, and later decisions may need help from a qualified repair professional, housing adviser, health professional, or local authority.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone’s breathing is affected or the property feels unsafe, prioritise immediate safety and urgent support.
