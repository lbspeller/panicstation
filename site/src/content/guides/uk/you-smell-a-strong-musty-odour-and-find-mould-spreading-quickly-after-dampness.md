# What to do if… you smell a strong musty odour and find mould spreading quickly after dampness (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the affected area by closing doors, stop the moisture source if safe to do so, and immediately notify your landlord or property manager to trigger statutory repair timeframes under Awaab’s Law or the Renters’ Rights Act 2026.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t scrub, brush, or vacuum mouldy surfaces, as this releases spores into the air.
- Don’t attempt to paint over or cover mould with wallpaper or sealant.
- Don’t use a fan to blow air from the affected area into other rooms.
- Don’t sleep or spend extended time in the room if you have a respiratory condition, weakened immune system, or are experiencing symptoms.
- Don’t ignore a "faint" smell; musty odours often indicate hidden growth behind furniture or under flooring.
- Don’t use bleach on porous surfaces like plasterboard or wood, as it does not kill the root of the mould and adds moisture.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the immediate area and close the door.** Move people and pets to a room with fresh air, particularly those with asthma, allergies, or weakened immune systems.
2. **Identify and isolate any electrical hazards.** If the dampness is near sockets, light switches, or the fuse box, **do not touch the area**. If safe, turn off the electricity at the consumer unit (fuse box) for that specific circuit.
3. **Ventilate the room to the outside only.** If you can do so in under 30 seconds, open a window in the affected room and immediately exit, closing the door behind you to prevent spores from entering the rest of the house.
4. **Stop the water source if it is safe and accessible.** 
   - Turn off the internal stopcock if a plumbing leak is suspected.
   - Use buckets or towels to contain water from a roof leak, provided you do not need to linger in the mouldy air.
5. **Take photographic evidence for your report.** Capture clear images of the mould, the damp patch, and any damaged belongings from a distance to minimize exposure.
6. **Report the issue to your landlord or housing provider immediately.**
   - **For Social Housing:** State clearly that you are reporting a "Significant Hazard" to trigger Awaab’s Law timeframes (investigation within 10 working days, repairs started within a further 5 working days).
   - **For Private Rentals:** Use your legal rights under the Renters’ Rights Act 2026 to demand a prompt investigation of the root cause, not just a surface clean.
7. **Contact Environmental Health if repairs are delayed.** If your landlord does not respond within the statutory windows or if the property feels uninhabitable, notify your local council's Environmental Health department.
8. **Seek medical advice for physical symptoms.** Contact NHS 111 if anyone develops a persistent cough, wheezing, or watery eyes. 
   - Call 999 immediately if someone experiences severe difficulty breathing, chest pain, or coughs up blood.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to buy air purifiers or mould-testing kits today; the landlord is responsible for professional assessment.
- You do **not** need to decide which furniture to throw away until the moisture source is identified and the area is dry.
- You do **not** need to clean the entire house; focus only on containing the affected room.

## Important reassurance
Finding rapidly spreading mould is distressing, but the musty smell is a biological signal that allows you to act before the problem becomes permanent. UK law in 2026 provides clear, mandatory deadlines for landlords to address damp and mould; you are not required to diagnose the cause or "fix" the growth yourself.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction. It does not cover long-term structural repairs, professional remediation, or legal litigation for property damage.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute medical or legal advice. Specific repair timeframes may vary depending on the severity of the hazard and the vulnerability of the occupants.
