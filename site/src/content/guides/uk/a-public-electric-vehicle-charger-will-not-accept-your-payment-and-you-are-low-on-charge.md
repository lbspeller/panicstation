# What to do if… a public electric vehicle charger will not accept your payment and you are low on charge (UK)

## Short answer
Stop repeated retries, preserve range, record the charger ID, and move quickly to another start method, the operator helpline, or a nearby backup charger while you still have enough charge to move safely.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t sit with heating, air conditioning, heated screens, or other high-power features running while you troubleshoot.
- Don’t keep attempting the same payment repeatedly; it may trigger bank security checks, pending holds, or app lockouts.
- Don’t assume it is only your card before checking the connector is latched, the correct charger is selected, the app is logged in, and your phone has signal.
- Don’t move the car into an unsafe or illegal position just to reach a plug.
- Don’t share full card details, one-time passcodes, or app login codes with anyone who contacts you unexpectedly claiming to help.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car safe and save range.**  
   Park safely and legally. Turn off cabin heat, air conditioning, heated screens, and high-power accessories. Use Eco or Range mode if your car has it.

2. **Record the charger details before you move on.**  
   Take photos of the charger ID or number, operator name, location, connector used, error message, price screen, and time. These details help the operator identify the exact unit.

3. **Try one quick alternative payment route.**
   - If a contactless card failed, try the same card inserted only if the terminal offers that option, or try a different contactless card.
   - If a phone wallet failed, try the physical card.
   - If the app failed, check you selected the exact charger ID and connector shown on the unit.
   - Do not spend your remaining phone battery reinstalling apps or changing passwords unless the operator tells you to.

4. **Try a different usable connector or post at the same site.**  
   If another connector, bay, or dispenser is available and safe to use, move once and try that unit. A neighbouring unit may authenticate even when the first one will not.

5. **Use the operator’s helpline.**  
   Look for the free 24-hour staffed helpline displayed on or at the charging site, or use the operator’s in-app help if that is faster. Give the charger ID and ask whether they can check the unit status, reset it, release a stuck session, or start the session remotely if their system allows.

6. **If phone signal or phone battery is the blocker, keep it simple.**
   - Plug your phone into the car’s USB power if you can.
   - Move only as far as needed to improve signal if it is safe and you can still see the car.
   - Prefer the displayed helpline, contactless terminal, or another charger over account resets on the spot.

7. **Set a short limit before your remaining range becomes the emergency.**  
   If the charger still will not start after one or two sensible attempts and a helpline attempt, use your remaining range for the nearest realistic backup charger, even if it is slower. A slower charge can be enough to get you unstuck.

8. **If you are on a motorway or another high-speed road, treat low charge as a safety issue.**
   - Aim for a service area, junction, emergency area, or hard shoulder before the battery reaches zero.
   - If you must stop, switch on hazard warning lights, stop as far left as possible, turn wheels left, and get everyone away from traffic and behind a barrier if it is safe to leave the vehicle.
   - If you stop in an emergency area, lay-by, or on the hard shoulder, use the emergency roadside phone if there is one, or call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 for advice and assistance.
   - If you cannot leave the vehicle safely, are stopped in a live lane, or there is immediate danger, keep seatbelts on, keep hazards on, call 999, and ask for the police.

9. **If you cannot reach another charger, call recovery early.**  
   Call your breakdown provider, insurer, lease provider, or manufacturer assistance line. Say: “EV low battery and unable to start charging because of a payment or authentication issue.” Give your exact location, charger operator, charger ID, and remaining range.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose why the payment failed right now.
- You do not need to resolve pending holds or bank checks before you are safe and charging.
- You do not need to complain, chase refunds, or leave reviews until you have enough charge or recovery is arranged.
- You do not need to decide whether to keep using that charging network today.

## Important reassurance
A charger can be powered on and still fail at the payment or authentication stage. The safest next move is usually not more repeated retries, but switching start method, using the operator helpline, or moving to a backup charger before your remaining range disappears.

## Scope note
These are first steps to keep you safe, preserve enough charge to move, and get a charging session started or arrange recovery. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, bank holds, or charge point compliance may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, especially on a motorway or high-speed road, prioritise personal safety and emergency services.
