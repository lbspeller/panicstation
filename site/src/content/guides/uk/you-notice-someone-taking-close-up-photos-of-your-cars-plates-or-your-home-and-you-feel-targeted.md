# What to do if… you notice someone taking close-up photos of your car’s plates or your home and you feel targeted (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a secure location behind a locked door immediately, then report the activity to the police via **999** if the person is still present and you feel threatened, or **101** if they have left.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the person, block their vehicle, or attempt to snatch their camera / phone.
- Do not go outside alone to investigate or "see what they are doing" if you are currently safe inside.
- Do not post the person's photo or your car registration on local social media groups right now.
- Do not touch any physical objects (such as stickers or small electronic tags) found on your car; leave them for police inspection.
- Do not assume the behavior is harmless if the person reacts aggressively or attempts to hide when noticed.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure position and lock all entry points.** **If you are at home, go inside immediately, lock all doors and windows, and stay away from the front of the property.** If you are in your car, keep the doors locked and drive to a well-lit, populated area such as a staffed petrol station or a supermarket car park rather than heading straight to your home address.
2. **Determine if an emergency response is required.** **Call 999 if the person is currently attempting to enter your property, is acting in a threatening manner, or if you believe a crime is in progress.** For all other reports where the person has left or there is no immediate threat, use the non-emergency **101** number or the online reporting tool for your local police force.
3. **Utilize the Silent Solution if you cannot speak safely.** **If you call 999 from a mobile but cannot speak for safety reasons, listen to the operator and press 55 when prompted by the automated system.** This signals to the emergency operator that you are in a genuine emergency and require police assistance without verbal communication.
4. **Record specific details from a safe distance.** **Once you are secure, write down the time of the incident, a description of the individual, any vehicle make or colour, and the registration number if they used a car.** If it is safe to do so from behind a window or door, take a photo or video, but do not compromise your safety or alert the individual to get the footage.
5. **Secure digital evidence and doorbell footage.** **Download or "save" any recordings from your doorbell camera or CCTV immediately to ensure they are not overwritten by the system.** Ensure the footage includes the minutes before and after the interaction to capture the person's arrival and direction of travel.
6. **Minimize the immediate visibility of your assets.** **Close curtains or blinds at the front of your house and, if possible, move your vehicle to a garage or a more visible, well-lit area without exiting your home alone.** Ensure all outbuildings, such as sheds or garages, are locked and that no valuables are visible through your car or house windows.
7. **Obtain an incident reference number.** **Request a reference number from the police when you make your report and keep it in a safe place for future use.** If similar activity occurs again, provide this number to the police so the incidents can be linked as part of a potential pattern of harassment or casing.

## What can wait
- Determining the specific motive (such as car cloning vs. burglary casing) can wait for police investigation.
- Installing new security systems, such as high-grade CCTV or anti-climb paint, is a secondary step.
- Replacing your car registration plates or changing house locks is not an immediate requirement unless damage has occurred.
- Contacting your car insurance provider is only necessary if you receive future fines or notices for crimes you did not commit.

## Important reassurance
It is entirely normal to feel a sense of violation or high alert when your personal space is photographed. This behavior is often a precursor to administrative crimes like vehicle cloning rather than a physical threat, but your instinct to seek safety first is correct. Taking immediate action to document the event and report it to the authorities is the most effective way to protect yourself.

## Scope note
This guide is intended for first-step stabilization and immediate safety only. Long-term security planning, legal action for harassment, or dealing with the administrative fallout of a cloned registration plate will require specialist professional advice.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving an immediate threat to life or property, always contact **999** immediately. Reporting suspicious behavior helps the police build an intelligence picture of local crime patterns.
