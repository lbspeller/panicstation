# What to do if… your handbrake or parking brake will not release and you are stuck where you parked (UK)

## Short answer
Ensure the vehicle is secured by the transmission, then attempt one correct release sequence specific to your brake type. If the brake remains engaged, stop immediately and call for professional breakdown recovery to prevent mechanical damage.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "power through" the resistance by revving the engine or forcing the car to move.
- Do not use a propane torch, open flame, or boiling water to thaw frozen brake components.
- Do not crawl underneath the vehicle to pull cables or strike components while in a public area or roadside.
- Do not repeatedly force a jammed manual lever or electronic switch.
- Do not ignore the smell of burning, visible smoke, or grinding noises from the wheels.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle using the transmission before troubleshooting.**
   - For manual cars: ensure the vehicle is in **1st gear** or **Reverse**.
   - For automatic cars: ensure the selector is firmly in **Park (P)**.
   - Switch on your **hazard warning lights** if your position creates a distraction or obstruction to others.

2. **Assess your immediate surroundings for safety hazards.**
   - If your vehicle is causing a dangerous obstruction in a live lane or on a blind bend, call **999** immediately.
   - If you are in a safe parking bay or off-road area, remain with the vehicle and do not attempt to push it.

3. **Perform one controlled release attempt based on your brake type.**
   - **Manual Lever:** Pull the lever up slightly, press and hold the button, then lower the lever completely to the floor.
   - **Electronic Parking Brake (EPB):** Ensure the ignition is on, press the main footbrake pedal down firmly, and then push (or pull, depending on model) the EPB switch once.
   - **Foot-Pedal Brake:** Press the main footbrake pedal firmly, then press the parking brake pedal again fully to trigger the release mechanism.

4. **Check the dashboard for specific fault lights or messages.**
   - If a "Brake System Fault" or red "P" warning light remains illuminated or flashes after a release attempt, the system is not safe to operate.
   - Note any messages such as "Press Brake Pedal to Release" which indicate the car is waiting for a specific user input.

5. **Stop all attempts if you detect heat, smells, or physical resistance.**
   - If the car lurches or the rear "squats" when you try to move, the brakes are seized.
   - Continuing to drive with a seized brake will cause a fire hazard or total brake failure.

6. **Contact your breakdown assistance provider for recovery.**
   - Provide your exact location (using a digital map or car park level/bay number).
   - State clearly: **"The parking brake is stuck on and the vehicle is non-mobile."**
   - If you are on a motorway or major A-road and cannot reach a safe place, contact **National Highways** or the police via **999**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the failure is caused by a snapped cable, a seized caliper, or an electronic actuator right now.
- You do not need to choose a repair garage or estimate costs while you are still at the roadside.
- You do not need to attempt "rocking" the car back and forth if the first release attempt failed.

## Important reassurance
Parking brakes frequently seize due to cold weather, moisture, or prolonged inactivity. This is a mechanical failure that cannot usually be fixed at the roadside without professional tools. Calling for recovery is the standard and safest way to handle this situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and safety actions. It does not cover mechanical repairs, emergency cable cutting, or dismantling of electronic parking brake systems.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not replace the specific instructions found in your vehicle's owner manual. If your vehicle is in a position that endangers yourself or other road users, contact emergency services immediately. Do not attempt to drive the vehicle if any part of the braking system is unresponsive or displays a fault light.
