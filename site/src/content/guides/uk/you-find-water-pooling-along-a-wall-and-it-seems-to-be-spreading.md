# What to do if… you find water pooling along a wall and it seems to be spreading (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the area from electrical hazards, then shut off the main water supply at the internal stop tap (stopcock) to stop the spread.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into or touch pooled water if it is near sockets, extension leads, or the consumer unit / fuse box.
- Do not attempt to investigate by drilling into walls or pulling up floorboards while the water is still actively spreading.
- Do not use electrical appliances, switches, or heaters in the affected area until a professional confirms they are safe.
- Do not stand directly under areas where the ceiling is sagging, bulging, or showing visible cracks.
- Do not force a stopcock that is seized / stuck; using tools may snap the valve and worsen the leak.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe distance and observe the area.** Identify if water is coming into contact with any electrical points or if you can hear buzzing / crackling. 
2. **Turn off the electricity only if it is safe to reach the consumer unit.**
   - If the consumer unit / fuse box is in a dry area and you do not have to cross water to reach it, flip the main switch to 'OFF'.
   - If you must walk through water or if the unit itself is wet, stay away and call **999** for the Fire Brigade.
3. **Locate and close your internal stop tap (stopcock).**
   - Look under the kitchen sink, in the hallway, under the stairs, or in a utility cupboard.
   - **Turn the valve clockwise (to the right) slowly until it stops.**
   - This may take a few minutes to fully stop the flow in the pipes.
4. **Identify the water type without touching it.**
   - If the water is clear, it is likely a supply pipe.
   - If the water is murky, smells, or contains debris, treat it as sewage / contaminated and keep all people and pets away.
5. **Alert neighbours if you live in a flat or terraced house.** If the water is coming from a high point on the wall or the ceiling line, knock on the door of the property above or adjacent to see if they have an active leak.
6. **Contain the spread with physical barriers.** Place towels or blankets at the "dry" edge of the pool to slow the water from reaching other rooms. Place a bucket under any active drips.
7. **Contact your landlord or an emergency plumber.**
   - **Renters:** Call your landlord’s emergency or out-of-hours number immediately.
   - **Homeowners:** Call a WaterSafe-approved emergency plumber.
8. **Call your water company if the stop tap does not stop the leak.** If the water continues to spread even with the internal stop tap closed, there may be a fault with the incoming main that requires the external street valve to be shut.
9. **Move items at risk of immediate damage.** If safe and dry to do so, lift rugs, electronics, and documents off the floor in the path of the spreading water.
10. **Open windows for ventilation.** This helps reduce immediate moisture levels once the electrical safety is confirmed and the leak is stopped.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact location of the pipe failure right now; stopping the water is the priority.
- You do not need to clean or dry the area thoroughly until a professional has confirmed the electrics are safe.
- You do not need to decide on flooring or plaster repairs today.
- You do not need to establish liability or fault in the first few minutes.

## Important reassurance
It is normal for water to appear far from the actual leak source as it travels along beams or behind plaster. Taking the immediate step of turning off the stopcock is the single most effective way to prevent further property damage.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active indoor leak. It does not cover long-term drying, insurance claims, or plumbing repairs.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and is not a substitute for professional plumbing or electrical advice. If there is a risk of structural collapse or major electrical hazard, evacuate the property and call 999.
