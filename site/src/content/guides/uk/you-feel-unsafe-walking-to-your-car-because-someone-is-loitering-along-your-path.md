# What to do if… you feel unsafe walking to your car because someone is loitering along your path (UK)

## Short answer
Do not walk past the person or toward the hazard. Return immediately to a well-lit, populated area such as a staffed reception, shop, or security desk and request assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk past the person to avoid appearing rude.
- Do not confront the person or ask them why they are there.
- Do not use shortcuts through dark areas, stairwells, or between parked vehicles.
- Do not look down at a phone or wear headphones while walking.
- Do not search for keys while standing in an exposed area.
- Do not unlock the vehicle until you are ready to enter immediately.
- Do not remain in a stationary vehicle with the doors unlocked.

## What to do now
1. **Turn around and go back to a safe, populated building.** Locate the nearest staffed area such as a shop, petrol station kiosk, building reception, or hotel lobby.
2. **Identify yourself to staff or security immediately.** State clearly that you feel unsafe reaching your vehicle and ask for a security escort or for permission to wait in a visible, well-lit area.
3. **Use a designated Help Point or intercom if you are in a large car park.** Most modern UK car parks have emergency buttons or intercoms near pay stations or lifts; press these to speak directly to a control room.
4. **Arrange for someone to meet you at the entrance if an escort is unavailable.** Call a friend or family member to pick you up at the main doors, or book a licensed taxi to take you from the building entrance to your car.
5. **Call 999 if the person approaches you, follows you, or blocks your exit.**
   - **State your location:** Give the name of the car park, the level, and the nearest landmark or street.
   - **Use the Silent Solution if you cannot speak:** Dial 999 and, if prompted by the automated system, **press 55** to signal a genuine emergency.
   - **Stay on the line:** Even if you cannot talk, keep the line open so the operator can listen to the environment.
6. **Enter the vehicle and lock all doors immediately once you reach it.** Ensure the engine is started and leave the area without pausing to check messages or adjust navigation.
7. **Use the vehicle horn to attract attention if you are blocked while inside.** If the person prevents you from driving away, a continuous horn blast can alert others and deter the individual.
8. **Drive to a busy, well-lit location if you believe you are being followed.** Go to a police station or a 24-hour petrol station and call 999 if the other vehicle continues to follow you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the person's intent before acting.
- You do not need to take photographs or videos of the person.
- You do not need to justify your decision to staff or security.
- You do not need to report the incident to the car park owners until you are home and safe.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your intuition when a situation feels "off" is a protective response, not an overreaction. It is normal to feel a physical "flight" response (increased heart rate or tension) when your path is blocked or observed; prioritizing your safety over politeness is the correct action.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate personal safety. It does not cover long-term reporting or legal actions regarding stalking or harassment.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not legal advice. In any situation where a crime is in progress or there is an immediate threat to safety, call 999.
