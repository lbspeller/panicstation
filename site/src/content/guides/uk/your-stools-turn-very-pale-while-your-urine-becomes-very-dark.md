# What to do if… your stools turn very pale while your urine becomes very dark (UK)

## Short answer
Contact **NHS 111** or your **GP surgery** immediately for a **same-day urgent appointment**. This combination of symptoms (known as obstructive jaundice) indicates that bile is not reaching your gut correctly and requires professional clinical assessment within hours.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume that **drinking more water** will fix this; this combination is not caused by simple dehydration.
- Do not wait to see if it **clears up on its own** over several days.
- Do not take **liver detox supplements**, herbal remedies, or "flushes," as these can worsen underlying liver or gallbladder stress.
- Do not stop any **prescribed medications** unless specifically instructed by a doctor, even if you suspect they are the cause.
- Do not consume **alcohol** until you have been medically cleared.
- Do not ignore the symptoms even if you **feel otherwise well** or have no pain.

## What to do now
1. **Check for yellowing (jaundice):**
   - **Examine the whites of your eyes** in a mirror under natural light.
   - **Check your skin colour**, particularly on the palms of your hands or the soles of your feet, for any yellow or orange tinge.
2. **Access urgent medical care immediately:**
   - **Call your GP surgery** and request an emergency same-day appointment.
   - **Call NHS 111** if your GP surgery is closed or you cannot get through.
   - **State clearly:** "I have the triad of jaundice symptoms: very pale stools, very dark urine, and [mention if your skin/eyes look yellow]."
3. **Go to A&E or call 999 if you have emergency "red flag" symptoms:**
   - **Severe pain** in the upper right side of your abdomen.
   - **High fever** or shivering (rigors).
   - **Confusion**, extreme drowsiness, or feeling very faint.
   - **Persistent vomiting** or being unable to keep fluids down.
4. **Prepare information for the clinician:**
   - **Note the exact colour** of your stool (e.g., white, grey, light beige, or "clay-like").
   - **Keep a stool sample** in a clean container if possible, or take a clear photograph to show the doctor.
   - **List all medications**, including over-the-counter pills, supplements, and vitamins you have taken in the last month.
5. **Monitor for worsening symptoms while waiting:**
   - **Stay near a phone** and keep your battery charged for call-backs from 111 or the GP.
   - **Rest** and avoid any strenuous physical activity.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the specific cause (such as gallstones or hepatitis) yourself.
- You do not need to fast or change your diet beyond avoiding alcohol.
- You do not need to book routine blood tests independently; the urgent clinician will arrange the necessary "Liver Function Tests" (LFTs).

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when you notice significant changes in body waste colour. These symptoms are very distinct, which helps doctors quickly narrow down the possible causes. Most causes, such as gallstones, are highly treatable once identified by a professional.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and urgent triaging only. It does not cover long-term management of liver or biliary disease.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not replace professional medical diagnosis. If you cannot reach a GP or 111 and your symptoms are worsening, attend your nearest Accident and Emergency department.
