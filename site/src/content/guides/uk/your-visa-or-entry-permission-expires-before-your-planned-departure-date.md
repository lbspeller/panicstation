# What to do if… your visa or entry permission expires before your planned departure date (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as time-critical: either leave the UK on or before the date your permission ends, or get urgent immigration advice about whether you can make a valid in-time application from inside the UK before it expires. Do not assume a flight after the expiry date is close enough.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume the longest date on any document is the date that controls your stay.
- Don’t overstay “just for a few days” to match your original plan.
- Don’t make an application you know you do not qualify for just to stop the clock.
- Don’t continue paid work, voluntary work, or other work-like activity if your right to work is unclear or may have ended.
- Don’t leave the UK while an in-country application is pending unless a regulated adviser or the official route guidance has confirmed what that does to your application and your status.
- Don’t give your passport, money, or personal login details to anyone promising a guaranteed outcome or shortcut.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact date that controls your stay.**  
   Check your UKVI account or eVisa, your grant email or decision letter, and any vignette or BRP you still have. Write down the permission end date. If documents show different dates, do not assume the latest date is safe; treat it as a mismatch that needs checking.

2. **If you can leave in time, change your travel now.**  
   Rebook your flight, ferry, coach, or train so you leave the UK on or before the permission end date. If you can, build in a buffer day in case of cancellation, delay, illness, or transport disruption.

3. **If the expiry date is very close, contact the travel provider directly.**  
   Use the fastest route available: app change, airline or travel company phone line, airport ticket desk, or travel agent. Save screenshots and emails showing the new booking or your attempt to change it.

4. **If you may be eligible to extend or switch from inside the UK, check before filing.**  
   Only rely on an application if the route allows an in-country application and you can submit a valid application before your current permission ends. Save the submission receipt, payment confirmation, reference number, and screenshots showing the date and time.

5. **If your right to work may end with your permission, pause and check it.**  
   Look at your online status or right-to-work share code information. If you cannot clearly show you still have permission to work, tell your employer you need to check your status urgently before doing more paid work, voluntary work, or work-like activity.

6. **If your permission has already expired, contain the harm.**  
   Do not ignore it. Get urgent advice from a regulated immigration adviser, solicitor, or trusted advice service, and plan to leave as soon as you realistically can unless you are advised that a specific application route is still open to you. Keep proof of why you missed the date, such as medical evidence, cancellation notices, emergency records, or booking records.

7. **If the problem is a document or online-record mismatch, save the evidence.**  
   Take screenshots of your eVisa or UKVI status, keep your grant email or letter, and keep images of any BRP or vignette showing a different date. Use official UKVI routes to update details or report an eVisa problem, and get advice before relying on a disputed date.

8. **Keep proof of your timeline and departure.**  
   Keep boarding passes, tickets, booking changes, cancellation notices, adviser emails, and screenshots in two places, such as your phone and email.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether you will return to the UK in the future.
- You do not need to write a long explanation before you have secured the key dates, evidence, and travel or advice route.
- You do not need to argue your full case at the airport; the immediate priority is leaving before expiry or confirming whether a valid in-time application is actually available to you.
- You do not need to solve every immigration consequence today before taking the safest next step.

## Important reassurance
This is a common mistake, especially when travel plans, eVisas, BRPs, vignettes, and grant letters show different information. Acting quickly can reduce the chance that a date problem becomes a more serious immigration problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to avoid or limit an overstay and preserve your options. UK immigration rules are strict and route-specific, and later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, medical, or other professional advice. If you are unsure whether you can apply from inside the UK, whether you still have permission to work, or whether your permission has already expired, get advice from a regulated immigration adviser, solicitor, or trusted free advice service before taking an irreversible step.
