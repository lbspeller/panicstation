# What to do if… you are told to surrender your passport or travel documents due to a legal order (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, ask to see the written order or conditions, and check exactly what document must be surrendered, where, and by when. Hand documents only to the named authority or official route, and ask for a written receipt.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the order or miss the deadline because you feel unsure; first check the wording and the named handover route.
- Do not try to travel, book travel, or use another passport to get around a restriction.
- Do not hand a passport to someone who cannot show official identification, written authority, and the correct handover route.
- Do not argue at a police station, court, port, or official building; calmly ask for the written basis and the next step.
- Do not hide a second passport, dual-nationality passport, identity card, or other travel document if the order might cover it.
- Do not rely on memory of what someone said; use the written terms.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the written basis.**  
   Ask: “Please show me the written order, notice, bail condition, or other written authority.” Check the exact wording, the deadline, the named authority, and whether it says “passport”, “all passports”, “travel documents”, “identity documents”, or a named document.

2. **Check the receiving place before handing anything over.**  
   If you are not already at the named place, ask which police station, court office, reporting centre, solicitor, or other authority is allowed to receive the document. The written order controls the route, and the process may vary depending on the legal process and place in the UK. Use an official contact route, such as the relevant police force website, court paperwork, or 101 for a non-emergency police enquiry, if you need to confirm the location.

3. **Ask what counts as compliance.**  
   Ask whether you must surrender the original document in person, what identification you need to bring, whether there is a specific desk, unit, appointment, or reference number, and whether any written confirmation is needed after handover.

4. **Make a secure record before handover, if you can do this lawfully and safely.**  
   Note the passport or travel document number, expiry date, issuing country, and any visa or status page you may need for work, housing, benefits, study, or immigration checks. Store any copy securely where other people cannot easily access it.

5. **Get a receipt at handover.**  
   Ask for a written receipt or confirmation showing your name, the document type and number if recorded, the date and time, the place, the person or office receiving it, and a contact route for return enquiries.

6. **Ask what happens next.**  
   Ask who will hold the document, what event may trigger its return, how you can ask for it back, and whether you need a court decision, police decision, written variation, or end of proceedings before it can be returned.

7. **If the order seems wrong or impossible, ask about the proper variation route.**  
   Say: “I want to comply, but I need to know the correct process to ask for this condition to be changed.” Ask for a criminal defence solicitor, the duty solicitor if available, or the named legal advice route at the police station or court.

8. **If you have more than one travel document, clarify it directly.**  
   Ask whether the order covers all passports, dual-nationality passports, emergency travel documents, refugee travel documents, identity cards used for travel, or a child’s travel document. Write down the answer and who gave it.

9. **If a child’s passport is involved, slow down.**  
   Act only on clear written authority. If you are unsure whether the order covers the child’s passport, get urgent legal advice before voluntarily handing it over.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to challenge the whole order; first secure the written terms, the correct handover route, and proof of handover.
- You do not need to explain your full case at the counter or desk; keep the immediate task to verifying the paperwork and documenting the surrender.
- You do not need to cancel every future plan at once; avoid new travel commitments until you know the restriction dates and the proper variation process.
- You do not need to solve identity replacement issues immediately unless you have an urgent appointment or status check.

## Important reassurance
Passport surrender conditions are used in some legal processes to reduce the risk of someone leaving the UK while the matter is active. It is reasonable to ask for written terms, use an official handover route, and get a receipt.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment you are told to surrender a passport or travel document. Later decisions about challenging, varying, replacing, or recovering documents may need specialist legal advice based on the exact paperwork.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you may be arrested or treated as breaching an order, prioritise calm compliance through the correct official route and get legal advice as soon as you safely can.
