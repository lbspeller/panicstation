# What to do if… you are abroad and your bank blocks your card for suspected fraud (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank immediately via the official app or the number on the back of your card to verify transactions and request a block removal. Do not follow links in unsolicited fraud alert texts, as these are often scams designed to steal login credentials.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use any phone number provided in a text message or email about "unusual activity" or "fraud."
- Do not move money to a "safe account" even if the caller claims to be from your bank or the police.
- Do not share your full Online Banking password, PIN, or any one-time passcodes (OTPs) over the phone.
- Do not keep attempting the same transaction repeatedly, as this can escalate a temporary hold to a permanent card cancellation.
- Do not let your card out of your sight or hand it to a merchant to "fix" a decline.
- Do not ignore the block; banks rarely "self-correct" a fraud freeze without user verification.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure location away from the point of sale to manage the situation.** If you are at a checkout or reception desk, explain that your bank has placed a security hold and you need 15 minutes to call them.
2. **Identify the exact error message or cue from the terminal or ATM.** Look for specific phrases like "Refer to Issuer," "Invalid Transaction," "Transaction Not Permitted," or "Declined - 05."
3. **Open your official banking app to check for automated fraud prompts.** Many UK banks (such as Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds, or NatWest) will send an in-app notification asking you to "Confirm this transaction" with a "Yes" or "No" button.
4. **Call your bank using a verified international contact number.** Use the number printed on the physical card or the specific international fraud line found on the bank's official website.
   - If calling from abroad, dial +44 followed by the number (dropping the first '0').
5. **Request a specific status update for your card and account.** Ask the agent if the card is "temporarily blocked for verification," "permanently hot-listed/cancelled," or if there is a "regional block" on the country you are visiting.
6. **Verify recent transactions one by one with the bank representative.** Confirm the exact amounts and locations of your last three successful payments to prove your identity and location.
7. **Ask for an Emergency Cash Disbursement if the card cannot be unblocked.** If the card must remain cancelled, ask the bank to arrange emergency cash for collection at a local agency (e.g., Western Union or MoneyGram).
8. **Contact the card network global assistance lines if you cannot reach your bank.** Use these numbers for collect calls from any local landline:
   - Visa Global Customer Assistance: +1 303 967 1090
   - Mastercard Global Service: +1 636 722 7111
9. **Use a secondary payment method to cover immediate essentials for the next 24 hours.** If you have a second card, digital wallet (Apple / Google Pay), or a travel companion, use those for immediate food and transport while the primary block is resolved.
10. **Contact the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) only if you are completely destitute and at risk.** Dial +44 20 7008 5000 to discuss emergency options.
   - Be aware that a government emergency loan for repatriation is a last resort, usually only covers a flight to the UK, and typically requires you to surrender your passport in exchange for an Emergency Travel Document.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the bank's security protocols while still abroad.
- You do not need to order a standard replacement card to your UK home address until you have confirmed if a courier delivery to your current location is possible.
- You can delay updating all your direct debits or subscription services until you are back in the UK or have a permanent new card.

## Important reassurance
A fraud block is a standard automated security response and does not necessarily mean your money has been stolen. In most cases, a five-minute phone call to verify your location and recent activity will restore full access to your funds.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a technical or security-related card block. It does not cover long-term fraud investigations, legal disputes with merchants, or insurance claims for stolen items.

## Important note
This information is not financial or legal advice. Terms for emergency cash and card replacement vary significantly between UK banks and specific card tiers. Always prioritize your physical safety and secure accommodation before attempting to resolve financial issues.
