# What to do if… your boarding pass will not scan at the gate while boarding is in progress (UK)

## Short answer
Step out of the scanner lane but stay beside the gate desk, and tell gate staff immediately that your boarding pass will not scan. Ask them to check whether there is a document hold, a pass error, or a replacement pass option while boarding is still open.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep trying to scan over and over while blocking the boarding lane.
- Don’t leave the gate area to fix it elsewhere unless gate staff tell you where to go and that there is still time.
- Don’t hand your phone, passport, or boarding pass to another passenger or a stranger.
- Don’t leave bags unattended while you speak to staff.
- Don’t rely on a screenshot, PDF scan, or damaged printout if staff say their scanner cannot read it.
- Don’t start arguing about blame in the boarding lane; the urgent task is getting a pass or clearance that staff can accept.

## What to do now
1. **Move to the side of the boarding area, still in clear sight of the gate desk.**  
   Keep your phone or printed pass, passport or ID, and any visa or residence document ready in your hand.

2. **Get gate staff’s attention with one clear sentence.**  
   Say: “My boarding pass will not scan and boarding is in progress. Can you check whether there is a hold or issue the fastest usable replacement?”

3. **Do the quickest scan fixes only while you are beside staff.**  
   Turn your phone brightness up, clean the screen, open the pass in the airline app or Wallet if you have it there, and make sure the barcode or QR code is fully visible. If it is a printed pass, flatten it and show staff any crease, tear, or faded area.

4. **Be ready for a document check.**  
   For an international flight, staff may need to check your passport and sometimes a visa, residence permit, or destination entry information. Hand documents directly to gate staff when asked.

5. **Ask for the replacement route that fits your airline.**  
   Say: “Can you refresh the mobile pass, print one if your airline can issue paper passes, or clear me through from your system?” Some airlines use digital-only processes, so gate assistance may be the fastest route.

6. **If your phone is the problem, say exactly what failed.**  
   Tell staff if the battery is low, the app will not load, the screen is cracked, the pass disappeared, or you only have a PDF or screenshot. This helps them choose the right fix without guessing.

7. **If you are travelling with others, stop accidental separation.**  
   Ask them to wait beside the gate area and keep each person’s passport or ID with that person. Keep your own phone, payment card, medication, and essential documents with you.

8. **If staff say you are not cleared to board, ask one calm question.**  
   Say: “Is this a document check, a boarding pass reissue, or a booking problem?” Then follow the staff route for that specific issue.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim compensation, or work out who caused the problem.
- You do not need to reinstall the airline app, reset passwords, or do long phone troubleshooting unless gate staff specifically ask for it.
- You do not need to search every email or old download if staff can refresh the pass from the gate system.
- You do not need to argue airline policy while the aircraft is boarding.

## Important reassurance
A boarding pass that will not scan can be caused by a damaged barcode, a mobile pass problem, a document check, a seat or booking update, or a system mismatch. The best first move is to stay at the gate and get staff to identify the specific block while boarding is still open.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment your pass will not scan while boarding is underway. Later decisions, such as rebooking, missed-flight procedures, complaints, or passenger rights questions, may need airline staff, airport staff, your travel agent, insurer, or specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information only and is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Airline and airport processes can differ, and gate staff decisions depend on safety, security, document checks, airline rules, and whether boarding has closed.
