# What to do if… your phone is stuck in recovery mode and asks to be connected to a computer (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and treat this as a data-risk moment. Use the phone maker’s official computer route, choose the least destructive update or repair option first if it is offered, and do not choose restore or factory reset until you have checked backups and sign-in access.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t choose Restore, Factory reset, or Initialize until you have accepted that it may erase the phone.
- Don’t install “one-click recovery” tools from ads, pop-ups, or random search results.
- Don’t keep force-restarting the phone over and over; one official restart attempt is different from repeated guessing.
- Don’t use a loose cable, damaged cable, keyboard USB port, or USB hub if you can avoid it.
- Don’t sign out of your Apple, Google, or Samsung account pre-emptively.
- Don’t hand the phone to an unofficial shop without first saying clearly that avoiding data loss is the priority.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilise the phone and the connection.**  
   Put the phone on charge. If it is hot, leave it somewhere safe to cool before connecting it. Keep the phone and computer still once you start any update or repair.

2. **Identify the device route before clicking anything.**  
   - iPhone or iPad: the official route is Finder on a Mac, the Apple Devices app on a Windows PC, or iTunes on older setups.
   - Google Pixel: use Google’s Pixel Update and Software Repair route if your model is supported.
   - Samsung: use Samsung Smart Switch emergency software recovery if that is the route shown for your phone.
   - Other Android phones: use the named maker’s support page or support app, not a generic recovery tool.

3. **Check backups and account access first.**  
   From another device or browser, check what you can still access:
   - Apple: check whether iCloud Photos is on for photos and whether iCloud Backup exists for device backup.
   - Android: check Google Photos, Google Drive, and any manufacturer cloud backup you used.
   - Any phone: make sure you know the Apple, Google, or Samsung account sign-in details, because the phone may ask for them after repair or restore.

4. **Try the least destructive official option first.**  
   If the official tool offers Update, Repair, or Emergency Software Recovery before Restore, choose that first. It is intended to be less destructive than a full restore, but it is not guaranteed to save all data.

5. **Make the computer setup reliable before starting.**  
   Update the computer’s device software if the official instructions ask for it. Plug the phone directly into the computer with a known good cable. Keep the computer awake and do not disconnect the phone while the process is running.

6. **If an error appears, adjust the setup before escalating to restore.**  
   Note the exact error code or message. Then try the official fixes that match it, such as a different USB port, a different cable, updated Apple Devices or iTunes software, updated computer software, or Samsung Smart Switch recovery from another computer if Smart Switch gives a recovery code.

7. **If the only remaining option is Restore, Factory reset, or Initialization, decide deliberately.**  
   If the phone must be usable and you can tolerate losing on-device data, continue only through the official route. If photos, notes, authenticator access, or other on-device data may be critical and you cannot confirm a backup, stop before choosing the wipe option.

8. **Get hands-on help before a wipe if the data matters.**  
   Contact the phone maker’s support, an authorised repair provider, or the retailer if the phone is newly bought or may be faulty. Say: “The phone only shows a recovery or restore screen. I am trying to avoid data loss. What is the least destructive official option?”

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why the update failed right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to buy a new phone today.
- You do not need to clean, optimise, or reorganise the phone.
- You can deal with retailer, warranty, or consumer-rights steps after you have protected access and data as far as possible.
- You can change passwords later unless you have a separate reason to think the account is compromised.

## Important reassurance
A recovery or restore screen does not automatically mean the phone is dead. The safest next move is slow and boring: protect power, use the official route, try update or repair before restore if offered, and stop before any option that says it will erase the device.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and avoid an accidental wipe. Later decisions about data recovery, repair cost, replacement, warranty, or retailer rights may need specialist technical or consumer advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not device-specific technical support, legal advice, financial advice, or professional repair advice. Update, repair, restore, and recovery processes can fail or erase data, and they may require account credentials afterward.
