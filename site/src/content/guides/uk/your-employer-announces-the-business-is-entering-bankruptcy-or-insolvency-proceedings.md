# What to do if… your employer announces the business is entering bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings (UK)

## Short answer
Request a written breakdown of the owed amount and a specific payment date immediately, then notify the employer that you will initiate Acas Early Conciliation if the deadline is missed. Final pay must be paid according to your contract terms or without unreasonable delay to avoid an unlawful deduction of wages claim.

## Do not do these things
- Do not accept verbal-only promises or vague timelines like "soon" or "next run" without written confirmation of the specific date and amount.
- Do not sign any "full and final settlement" or "paid in full" declarations if the money has not yet cleared in your bank account.
- Do not delay taking formal action beyond 3 months minus 1 day from the date the payment was due, as this is the strict deadline for most employment tribunal claims.
- Do not return company property (laptops, keys) without getting a signed receipt, but also do not "hold property hostage" as this can give the employer a legal reason to withhold pay for losses.
- Do not delete correspondence, rota screenshots, or timesheets until the dispute is fully resolved and the money is received.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the expected payment date against your employment contract.** Your final pay date is usually your final regular payday or the last day of your notice period, depending on your specific contract terms. If no date is specified, the law requires payment without unreasonable delay.

2. **Send a formal "Letter Before Action" via email to the payroll department and HR.** **You must include your employee number, your final working day, a clear list of what is missing (basic pay, accrued holiday, commission, or expenses), and a deadline (e.g., 5 working days) for payment.** 
   - State clearly: "If payment is not received or a concrete date provided by [Date], I will begin the Acas Early Conciliation process."

3. **Request your final itemised payslip and P45 immediately.** **Employers are legally required to provide a payslip showing gross pay and deductions, and the P45 is essential for your next employer to avoid placing you on an emergency tax code.** 
   - If they claim the "system is locked," ask for a manual statement of the calculated figures.

4. **Calculate your exact "accrued but untaken" holiday entitlement.** **In the UK, you are legally entitled to be paid for any statutory holiday you have earned but not used during the current leave year up to your final day.** 
   - Use the standard 5.6 weeks per year (pro-rata) unless your contract provides a higher contractual limit.

5. **Initiate Acas Early Conciliation if the employer's deadline passes.** **This is a mandatory legal step before you can lodge a claim in an Employment Tribunal for unpaid wages or holiday pay.** 
   - Contacting Acas "pauses the clock" on your 3-month tribunal deadline for up to 12 weeks while a conciliator attempts to resolve the dispute for free.

6. **Gather all physical evidence of hours worked in your final period.** **Secure copies of rotas, timesheets, clock-in records, or emails showing work completed to prevent the employer from disputing the number of hours owed.** 

## What can wait
- You do not need to hire a solicitor immediately; the Acas process is designed for individuals and is free to use.
- You do not need to worry about the specific legal terminology of your claim (e.g., "breach of contract" vs "unlawful deduction") during the initial email to HR.
- You do not need to finalize your exact tax calculation today, as HMRC will eventually reconcile any overpaid tax once your P45 is processed.

## Important reassurance
It is very common for payroll departments to experience "leaver errors" where a profile is deactivated before the final payment is triggered. Taking a firm, documented approach by setting a deadline and mentioning Acas is a standard professional response and does not require you to be aggressive or confrontational.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and the first legal steps for unpaid final wages in the UK. It does not cover complex redundancy calculations, insolvency (where the company has gone bust), or pension dispute resolutions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment law deadlines are extremely strict in the UK; missing a deadline by one day can permanently bar you from recovery. Note that while current limits for most claims are 3 months minus 1 day, the Employment Rights Act 2025 is scheduled to extend these to 6 months starting in October 2026. Always verify the current deadline with Acas or Citizens Advice.
