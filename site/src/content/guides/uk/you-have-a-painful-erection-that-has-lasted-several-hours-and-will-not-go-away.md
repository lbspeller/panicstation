# What to do if… you have a painful erection that has lasted several hours and will not go away (UK)

## Short answer
This is a urological emergency: **go to A&E immediately** if a painful erection has lasted **more than 4 hours**, or **more than 2 hours** if you have sickle cell disease. Delaying treatment beyond these windows increases the risk of permanent erectile dysfunction.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the erection to go down on its own once the 2 or 4-hour threshold is passed.
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital if you are in severe pain, feel lightheaded, or have used substances.
- Do not take additional doses of erectile dysfunction medication or use further injections to attempt to resolve the issue.
- Do not apply extreme heat or forceful pressure to the area to try and force detumescence.
- Do not assume that passing urine means the danger is over if the penis remains rigid or painful.
- Do not allow embarrassment to delay your arrival at an emergency department.

## What to do now
1. **Travel to your nearest A&E department immediately if your erection meets the emergency time thresholds.**
   - For most people, this is an erection lasting more than 4 hours.
   - For those with sickle cell disease, this is an erection lasting more than 2 hours.
   - If you cannot travel safely or the pain is incapacitating, call 999.
2. **Note the exact time the erection started and any possible triggers.**
   - List any medications taken, including sildenafil (Viagra), tadalafil (Cialis), or penile injections.
   - Note any recent injury to the pelvic or genital area.
   - Note any recreational drug or alcohol use, as this is vital for correct treatment.
3. **Wear loose-fitting clothing such as joggers or loose trousers for the journey.**
   - Tight clothing can exacerbate pain and increase the risk of tissue damage.
4. **Inform the triage staff clearly of the situation upon arrival to ensure you are prioritised.**
   - Use the phrase: "I have a painful, persistent erection (priapism) that has lasted [X] hours."
   - State immediately if you have sickle cell disease, as this requires specific haematology involvement.
5. **Request a private area for triage if you feel uncomfortable discussing the symptoms at the front desk.**
   - You can write down your symptoms on your phone or a piece of paper and show it to the staff if you find it difficult to speak.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the underlying cause of the priapism yourself right now.
- You do not need to research long-term effects or recovery until the acute episode is resolved.
- You do not need to decide on changes to your regular medications or treatments today.
- You do not need to worry about explaining the situation to anyone other than the medical team.

## Important reassurance
Priapism is a recognised clinical emergency, and medical staff are trained to treat it with professional urgency. You are not "wasting time" by seeking help; emergency departments prefer to see you early to prevent permanent damage. Reaching the hospital quickly is a proactive step toward protecting your long-term health.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Once at the hospital, clinicians will perform assessments to differentiate between types of priapism and determine the necessary medical or surgical intervention.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and does not constitute medical advice or a diagnosis. If you are experiencing a persistent, painful erection, seek professional emergency medical care immediately at an A&E department or by calling 999.
