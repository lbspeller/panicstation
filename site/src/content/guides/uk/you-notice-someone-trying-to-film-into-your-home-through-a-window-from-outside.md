# What to do if… you notice someone trying to film into your home through a window from outside (UK)

## Short answer
Move away from the window immediately to a room without a line of sight from the outside, lock all entry points, and call 999 if the person is still there or you feel threatened.

## Do not do these things
- Don't go outside to confront the person or attempt to snatch their device.
- Don't stand by the window to record them back if it keeps you in their line of sight.
- Don't open doors or windows to shout at them or ask what they are doing.
- Don't share the footage on social media groups immediately, as this can alert the person or interfere with police evidence.
- Don't assume you are overreacting if you feel your privacy or safety is being compromised.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure part of the home immediately where you cannot be seen.** Move away from the window the person is using and go into a room with no windows or where the curtains are already closed.
2. **Ensure all external doors and ground-floor windows are locked.** Check that the front and back doors are secure and that any other potential entry points are fastened.
3. **Alert other people in the house to move away from windows.** Ensure children, housemates, or family members are moved to a central or upstairs room away from the perimeter of the house.
4. **Call the police to report the incident as it is happening.**
   - **Call 999 if the person is still present, attempting to enter, or you feel in immediate danger.**
   - **Call 101 or report online if the person has already left and the immediate threat has passed.**
5. **Follow the Silent Solution protocol if you are in danger and cannot speak.**
   - **If calling 999 from a mobile:** Listen to the operator. If you cannot speak, cough or tap your handset. When prompted by the automated system, press 55 to be transferred to the police.
   - **If calling 999 from a landline:** Stay on the line. Landline calls provide location data automatically; if the operator hears only background noise and is concerned, they will connect you to the police.
6. **Note down a description of the person and their actions without returning to the window.** Record details such as their clothing, approximate height, the type of device they were using, and whether they were on your private property (garden/driveway) or public land.
7. **Preserve any automated evidence from security systems.** If you have a doorbell camera or CCTV that may have captured the person, ensure the footage is saved and not overwritten, but do not attempt to view it until you are in a safe, locked room.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific law (such as voyeurism or harassment) being broken before calling for help.
- You do not need to check with neighbours to see if they saw the person.
- You do not need to install new privacy film or security cameras in this exact moment.
- You do not need to decide whether you want to press charges immediately.

## Important reassurance
It is completely normal to feel a sudden sense of violation, fear, or adrenaline when your private space is invaded. Taking the step to move away and secure your home is a logical, protective response. You are not "making a scene" by involving the police in a situation where someone is recording your private life without consent.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate privacy breach. It does not cover long-term stalking injunctions or civil privacy claims, which may require legal counsel or specialist support services later.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, filming into a private residence can fall under various criminal statutes including harassment, voyeurism, or new 2026 protections against non-consensual filming; always rely on police assessment for active incidents.
