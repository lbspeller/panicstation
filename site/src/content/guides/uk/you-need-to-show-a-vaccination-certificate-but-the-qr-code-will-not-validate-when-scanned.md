# What to do if… you need to show a vaccination certificate but the QR code will not validate when scanned (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the official what alternative proof of vaccination they can accept immediately (such as a printed record or a visual check of the digital details) while you refresh your official record in the NHS App.

## Do not do these things
- Do not hand over an unlocked phone or allow staff to take the device out of your sight.
- Do not download third-party "QR fixer" or "scanner" apps from advertisements or search results.
- Do not edit, crop, or apply filters to the digital certificate image, as this often corrupts the embedded data.
- Do not assume an "invalid" scan means your medical record is incorrect; it frequently indicates a hardware or software error on the verifier’s device.
- Do not get into an argument regarding the technology; request a manual verification or alternative document check.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the fallback verification method.**
   **Ask the official: "The QR code is not scanning; what alternative evidence can you accept right now?"**
   - Inquire if they can accept a visual check of the details on your screen.
   - Ask if a PDF download or a printed copy is acceptable.
   - Note that for certain vaccines like Yellow Fever, the physical International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP) "yellow book" is the legal standard and may not require scanning.

2. **Optimise the digital display for scanning.**
   **Adjust your device settings to ensure the QR code is legible to the scanner.**
   - Set screen brightness to the maximum level.
   - Clean the phone screen or camera lens to remove smudges or glare.
   - If using a PDF, ensure the zoom level does not make the pixels appear blocky or blurry.
   - Turn off "Dark Mode" if it is active, as some scanners require a high-contrast black-on-white display.

3. **Generate a fresh official record.**
   **Log out and log back into the NHS App to pull the most recent version of your record.**
   - Navigate to "Check your COVID-19 vaccine record" or "Vaccinations" within the NHS App.
   - If the app is slow, try using the NHS website via a mobile browser to access the same record.
   - Download the record as a PDF immediately so it is available offline if signal is lost.

4. **Perform a manual data comparison.**
   **Check that the name and date of birth on the certificate exactly match your passport.**
   - Verify that the vaccine type and dates of administration are clearly visible.
   - If there is a spelling mismatch, inform the official that this is a clerical error and provide secondary ID if possible.

5. **Verify the scanner compatibility.**
   **Confirm with the official that they are using the correct verifier app for your specific document.**
   - Some scanners are only configured for EU Digital COVID Certificates (DCC) and may fail on UK-specific formats if not updated.
   - Ask if there is a different terminal or handheld device available to try.

6. **Address missing or incorrect data entries.**
   **If the record itself is missing vaccinations, contact the issuing clinic or your GP practice.**
   - For vaccinations administered in England, the NHS App support service can help if data is missing after 5 working days.
   - For private vaccinations (such as some travel vaccines), you must contact the private provider directly for an official certificate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to troubleshoot your GP record or clinical system errors while at a check-in desk or border.
- You do not need to contact 111 or 119 for a QR code scan failure; these services cannot fix a hardware scanner issue.
- You do not need to decide on changing your travel plans until you have exhausted the manual verification options.

## Important reassurance
QR code scan failures are frequently caused by external factors such as screen glare, poor lighting, or outdated verifier software. They are rarely an indication that your vaccinations are "invalid." Most transit points have secondary procedures for manual verification of official digital or paper records.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a technical failure at a checkpoint. It does not cover the long-term process of correcting medical records or the legal requirements for entry into specific foreign jurisdictions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or travel advice. Entry requirements are determined by the destination country and the carrier; they are subject to change without notice. Always carry a printed backup of essential health documents when travelling.
