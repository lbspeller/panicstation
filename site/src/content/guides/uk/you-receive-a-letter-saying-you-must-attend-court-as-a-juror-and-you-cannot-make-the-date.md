# What to do if… you receive a letter saying you must attend court as a juror and you cannot make the date (UK)

## Short answer
You must respond to the summons within 7 days (14 days in Northern Ireland) and request a "deferral" to change the date. Do not ignore the letter, as failing to respond or attend can result in a fine of up to £1,000.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the letter or throw it away — the court will assume you are attending and failure to show is a criminal offence.
- Do not ask a third party to handle it for you; you are legally required to respond personally unless you have a power of attorney or a confirmed disability that prevents you from doing so.
- Do not wait for the "perfect" time to reply — the 7-day deadline (14 in NI) is strict.
- Do not book flights, holidays, or childcare based on the hope of a deferral; wait for the written confirmation from the court.
- Do not send original passports or birth certificates as proof unless specifically requested; photocopies or digital scans are usually sufficient.

## What to do now
1. **Locate your juror number (or citation number in Scotland) and the date you are expected to start.** This information is printed at the top or on the front page of your summons.
2. **Reply to the summons within 7 days (England, Wales, Scotland) or 14 days (Northern Ireland).** 
   - **England & Wales:** Use the online "Reply to a jury summons" service on GOV.UK or post the completed form back in the provided envelope.
   - **Scotland:** Follow the instructions on your "jury citation" to respond via the Scottish Courts and Tribunals website or by calling the court listed.
   - **Northern Ireland:** Complete the "Form of Return" online or by post to the Customer Service Centre.
3. **Select the "request a deferral" option if you can serve at another time in the next 12 months.** You must provide a valid reason, such as:
   - A pre-booked holiday (provide booking dates).
   - An operation or urgent medical appointment.
   - An exam or important work deadline.
   - Primary caring responsibilities for children or vulnerable adults.
4. **Suggest three separate dates or 1-week periods within the next 12 months when you ARE available.** The Jury Central Summoning Bureau (England/Wales) or the local court office (Scotland/NI) will attempt to move your service to one of these dates.
5. **Attach digital copies or physical photocopies of evidence if required.** 
   - For medical reasons: a doctor's letter (GPs should not charge for this under the General Medical Services Regulations).
   - For work reasons: a letter from your employer explaining how your absence would cause "substantial injury" to the business.
   - For holidays: flight or hotel booking confirmations.
6. **Request an "excusal" only if you cannot serve at any point in the next 12 months.** This is reserved for exceptional circumstances, such as permanent disability, being a new parent who cannot serve for the full year, or having served on a jury in the last 2 years.
7. **Keep a record of your submission.** Note the date you replied online or keep a proof-of-posting receipt if you used the mail.
8. **Contact the court office directly if your start date is less than 7 days away.** If you have only just realized you cannot make the date and the trial is imminent, call the court listed on the summons immediately to explain the situation.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to contact your local MP or a solicitor to get out of jury duty; the deferral process is the standard first step.
- You do **not** need to worry about being paid yet; loss of earnings and expenses are handled once your service date is confirmed or after you have served.
- You do **not** need to prove you are "innocent" of anything; jury selection is random and being summoned does not imply you are in trouble.

## Important reassurance
The jury summoning system is designed to handle thousands of deferral requests every month. As long as you respond within the legal timeframe and provide three alternative dates, the court will typically accommodate your request for a first-time deferral. Feeling anxious about the process or the potential for a fine is a common reaction, but taking the first step of replying usually resolves the immediate pressure.

## Scope note
This guide covers the initial stabilization and response required when a jury summons date clashes with your schedule. It does not cover specific legal disqualifications (such as criminal records) or long-term exemptions for specific professions. 

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Procedures vary between the England and Wales Jury Central Summoning Bureau, the Scottish Courts and Tribunals Service, and the Northern Ireland Courts and Tribunals Service. Always follow the specific instructions on the physical letter you received.
