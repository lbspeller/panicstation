# What to do if… airport Wi-Fi requires a local phone number and you cannot access your travel documents (UK)

## Short answer
Stop treating the Wi-Fi as your only route. Go to the airline desk and ask them to retrieve or print what you need while you try one brief connectivity fallback, such as roaming, a trusted hotspot, or an official airport SIM or eSIM.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t type passport details, card details, or sensitive account passwords into unknown public Wi-Fi pages if you can avoid it.
- Don’t hand your phone to strangers offering to “receive the code” or “fix the Wi-Fi” for you.
- Don’t scan random QR codes or pay unexpected “official Wi-Fi” prompts unless airport staff confirm they are legitimate.
- Don’t keep retrying email or airline logins until you lock yourself out.
- Don’t cancel flights, rebook, or make irreversible changes in panic before you have tried airline staff and offline options.

## What to do now
1. **Name the one thing you need in the next 30 minutes.** It is usually one of these: booking reference, PNR, boarding pass QR code, e-ticket number, visa or ETA PDF, or proof of onward travel.

2. **Check for offline copies first.**
   - Look in Wallet, the airline app’s “Trips” area, Files, Downloads, and your photo gallery for screenshots or saved PDFs.
   - Search your email app for the airline or booking name; some email apps keep already-opened messages and attachments available offline.
   - Check messages from the airline, booking site, travel agent, or visa provider for reference numbers.

3. **Use the airline desk now.**
   - Go to check-in, bag drop, ticketing, or the service desk.
   - Say: “I can’t get internet here. I need my booking or boarding pass retrieved.”
   - Ask whether they can find the booking using your name, passport, flight number, destination, or booking reference.
   - Ask whether they can print a boarding pass, reissue it, or tell you exactly what document they need to see.

4. **Ask the airport for an official non-SMS option.**
   - At an information desk, ask for the exact official Wi-Fi network name.
   - Ask whether Wi-Fi access has a voucher code, help-desk code, international-number option, or another non-SMS route.
   - Ask whether there are public internet kiosks, airline support desks, or official terminal shops that can help with short internet access.

5. **Use a brief connectivity fallback.**
   - Turn on mobile data roaming only long enough to download or screenshot the document you need, if your mobile plan allows it.
   - If you are travelling with someone you trust, ask for a personal hotspot for a few minutes.
   - If you are alone, consider an official airport SIM or eSIM from a terminal shop rather than an informal reseller.
   - Once the document opens, screenshot it and save it to Files or Wallet so you are not dependent on the connection again.

6. **If you must use public Wi-Fi, keep the task narrow.**
   - Confirm the exact network name with airport staff before connecting.
   - If a VPN is stopping the Wi-Fi sign-in page from opening, you may need to pause it only to complete the portal, then reconnect before logging into accounts.
   - Use secure websites or official apps where possible, open only the travel document you need, and avoid banking or shopping.

7. **Make a simple offline proof bundle.**
   - In Notes or on paper, write your full name as on your passport, date of birth, airline, flight number, departure time, destination, and any booking, visa, ETA, or e-ticket reference numbers you can find.
   - Keep your passport and any saved screenshots ready for airline staff.
   - Tell staff which documents are stuck online rather than trying to explain the Wi-Fi problem in detail.

8. **If this is actually a passport problem, separate it from the Wi-Fi problem.**
   - If your British passport is lost, stolen, left behind, or unavailable abroad and travel is urgent, GOV.UK has guidance on UK emergency travel documents.
   - If you cannot get online yourself, focus first on getting brief official connectivity or staff help so you can apply, receive messages, and be contactable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix the airport Wi-Fi today if the airline can retrieve or print what you need.
- You do not need to change passwords unless you think you used a fake network, shared a code, or entered credentials somewhere suspicious.
- You do not need to choose a long-term phone plan, roaming package, SIM, or eSIM while you are under check-in pressure.
- You do not need to reorganise all your travel documents now; get the one document needed for the next airport step.
- If you later think an account was compromised, handle account recovery after you are out of the immediate check-in or boarding pressure.

## Important reassurance
This is a common airport problem for travellers without a local phone number. Even if you cannot reach the exact email or app screen you expected, airline staff can often tell you what they can retrieve, print, or accept at that airport.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get through the immediate airport blockage and avoid risky choices on public networks. Later decisions about account security, replacement documents, mobile service, visas, or missed travel may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, travel, immigration, or other professional advice. Airport, airline, visa, and document processes vary by country, carrier, and terminal. If anything looks fraudulent, such as look-alike Wi-Fi networks, unexpected payment pages, or pushy helpers, stop and use official airline or airport desks.
