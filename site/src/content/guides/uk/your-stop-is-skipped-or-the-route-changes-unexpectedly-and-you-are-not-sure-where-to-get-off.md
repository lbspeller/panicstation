# What to do if… your stop is skipped or the route changes unexpectedly and you are not sure where to get off (UK)

## Short answer
Stay on the vehicle until the next safe, well-lit stop or station and consult official staff or a help point to confirm your location and the safest return route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to exit the vehicle while it is moving or between designated stops
- Do not force doors open or use emergency releases unless there is an immediate threat to life, such as fire
- Do not step onto the tracks or live roadway to investigate your surroundings
- Do not wander away from a station or stop into poorly lit or isolated areas if you are disoriented
- Do not engage in a prolonged argument with the driver while they are operating the vehicle
- Do not pull the passenger alarm for a missed stop unless you feel personally unsafe or there is a medical emergency

## What to do now
1. **Identify the next safe arrival point.**  
   **Stay on the vehicle until it reaches a designated stop or station that is well-lit and populated.** If the current area feels unsafe or isolated, it is usually safer to remain on the vehicle until a major interchange or a staffed station is reached.

2. **Verify your current location using onboard tools.**  
   **Check the visual display screens or listen for automated audio announcements, which are mandatory on most UK buses and trains as of 2026.**  
   - Use a smartphone map app to view your current position via GPS.
   - Look for station name signs on platforms or stop names on street furniture.

3. **Communicate with the driver or onboard staff.**  
   **Briefly inform the staff that your stop was missed or the route has changed and ask for the best way to return.**
   - **Bus:** Wait until the bus is stationary at a stop before approaching the driver.
   - **Train:** Locate a conductor or guard, or use the non-emergency "Information" button on the onboard intercom if you cannot find staff.

4. **Seek assistance at the station for rail overtravel.**  
   **Speak to staff at the ticket office or use a platform help point if you have been carried past your station.**  
   - State clearly: "The train skipped my stop / I was carried past my station; what is the correct way to return?"
   - Staff can often authorise a "pass" to return to your destination without you needing to purchase a new ticket, provided you report the issue immediately.

5. **Utilise station help points if staff are not visible.**  
   **Locate the yellow or green help point pillars found on most platforms and concourses.** Press the "Information" button (not the "Emergency" button unless in immediate danger) to speak to a remote operator who can track your location and provide travel directions.

6. **Prioritise personal safety if you feel threatened.**  
   **Move to a "Wait Safe" area, such as near CCTV cameras, under bright lighting, or close to other passengers.**
   - If you feel unsafe or are experiencing harassment on the rail network, text the **British Transport Police** on **61016** or call **0800 40 50 40**.
   - In any immediate emergency, dial **999**.

7. **Record the service details for later reporting.**  
   **Note the time, the service number (e.g., Bus 142 or the 14:05 train to Manchester), and the vehicle number if possible.** This information is required if you wish to claim a refund or file a formal complaint regarding the service failure.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to file a formal complaint against the operator
- Calculating potential compensation or refund amounts
- Researching the technical reason for the diversion or missed stop
- Contacting family or friends to explain the delay (unless they are waiting for you at the missed stop)

## Important reassurance
Missing a stop or experiencing a sudden diversion is a frequent occurrence on public transport networks. Transport staff are trained to deal with "overtravel" and "missed connections" and will generally assist you in finding a way back to your intended destination. Feeling disoriented in an unfamiliar area is a natural response; staying in a public, well-lit transport hub is the most effective way to resolve the situation safely.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passengers who are lost or disoriented due to service changes. It does not cover long-term service planning or detailed legal claims for transport delays.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Passenger rights and "discretionary travel" policies vary between individual train operating companies and local bus authorities. If you are in immediate physical danger, always contact the emergency services by dialling 999.
