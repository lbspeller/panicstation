# What to do if… your passport will not scan at an airport self-service kiosk or gate (UK)

## Short answer
Stop treating the machine as the problem to solve. Ask for a staffed or manual document check now, so you do not lose the time you need for the flight or border queue.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t force the passport into the reader, bend it, scrape the photo page, or pick at the laminate.
- Don’t keep trying different machines until boarding or connection time is close.
- Don’t assume you have done something wrong because an eGate, kiosk, or bag-drop machine rejects the scan.
- Don’t hand your passport to anyone except airline staff, airport staff, Border Force, police, or another clearly official person.
- Don’t use tape, glue, heat, cleaning chemicals, or water on the passport.

## What to do now
1. **Move out of the machine flow and protect your time.** Keep the passport in your hand or secure bag, check your boarding or connection time, and look for the nearest staffed desk or official help point.
2. **Try one careful re-scan if there is time, then stop.**
   - Remove any passport cover or wallet.
   - Open the photo page fully and place it exactly as the screen shows.
   - Keep the page still and flat without bending the passport.
   - Gently wipe dust or smudges with a dry soft cloth only.
3. **If this is a UK border eGate or passport control point, go to the staffed lane.** Say: “My passport will not scan at the eGate. Can I be checked by an officer?”
4. **If this is airline check-in, bag drop, or a document-check kiosk, go to the airline desk.** Say: “My passport will not scan at the self-service machine. Can you do a manual document check or tell me where document verification is?”
5. **Show visible damage calmly, without guessing the cause.** Point out anything obvious, such as lifting laminate, a torn or creased photo page, water damage, deep scratches, or a page that looks altered. Ask what they can do for this journey and whether you should contact HM Passport Office afterwards.
6. **If you are abroad and the passport problem blocks travel, focus on today’s travel first.**
   - Ask the airline whether they can complete a staffed document check, move you to a later flight, or tell you exactly what they cannot accept.
   - If your UK passport cannot be used and you need urgent travel, check the GOV.UK emergency travel document route.
   - Use the GOV.UK embassy, high commission, or consulate finder if you need local consular contact details.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide at the airport whether the passport is officially “faulty” or “damaged.”
- You do not need to start a replacement application while you are still trying to get through check-in or passport control.
- You do not need to argue with the machine or prove the cause of the scan failure.
- You can deal with HM Passport Office follow-up after you are through the immediate travel problem, or after the airline has rebooked you.

## Important reassurance
A failed scan does not automatically mean you have done anything wrong. The next normal step is to ask for a person to check the passport or tell you where the right staffed desk is.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the airport or border moment only. Later decisions about replacing a passport, reporting a fault, rebooking travel, or getting an emergency travel document may need help from the airline, Border Force, HM Passport Office, or British consular services.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, or professional advice. Airport, airline, and border procedures vary, and officials may require extra checks or refuse to accept a document that appears damaged, altered, or unreadable.
