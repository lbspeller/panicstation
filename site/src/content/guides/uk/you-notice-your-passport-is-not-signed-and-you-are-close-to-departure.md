# What to do if… you notice your passport is not signed and you are close to departure (UK)

## Short answer
Sign the passport immediately using a black ballpoint pen on the designated "holder's signature" line. A UK passport is not a valid travel document until it has been signed by the holder (if required to do so).

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to travel with an unsigned passport; airlines and border authorities may refuse boarding or entry.
- Do not sign anywhere other than the "holder's signature" line, which is usually located on the page above your photo and personal details.
- Do not use any ink other than black ballpoint; do not use gel pens, pencils, or felt tips.
- Do not use correction fluid, tape, or attempt to cross out a mistake if you sign incorrectly.
- Do not let another person sign for you unless you are legally authorized as a proxy due to a recognized disability.

## What to do now
1. **Verify if a signature is legally required for your specific document.**
   - **Check the personal details page** (the page with your photo).
   - **Look for the phrase "the holder is not required to sign."** If this is present, do not add a signature.
   - **Confirm the age of the holder.** Children aged 11 or under do not need to sign. Children aged 12 to 15 must sign their own passport.

2. **Locate the correct signature page and line.**
   - **Open the passport to the page directly above or facing the photo page.**
   - **Identify the "holder's signature" line.** It is a pre-printed horizontal line specifically for this purpose.

3. **Sign the document using a black ballpoint pen.**
   - **Use your standard signature** that matches your other official identification.
   - **Ensure the signature stays entirely above the line** and does not overlap with any other text or security features.
   - **Apply steady pressure** to ensure the ink is clear and legible.

4. **Allow the ink to dry completely before closing the booklet.**
   - **Leave the passport open for at least 60 seconds** to prevent the signature from smudging or transferring onto the facing page.

5. **Obtain a suitable pen if you are already in transit.**
   - **Visit an airport shop, airline service desk, or hotel reception** to borrow or purchase a black ballpoint pen if you do not have one.
   - **Complete the signing before joining the check-in or security queue.**

6. **Address signing errors immediately if they occur.**
   - **Stop immediately if you sign the wrong name or the ink smudges significantly.**
   - **Contact the Passport Adviceline on 0300 222 0000** for instructions if the document is rendered illegible or contains incorrect information.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact HM Passport Office if you have signed the document correctly and followed the standard instructions.
- You do not need to apply for a replacement passport unless a significant error (like the wrong name) was made during the signing process.
- You do not need to seek "validation stamps" or additional official markings once the signature is in place.

## Important reassurance
In most cases, an unsigned passport is a simple procedural omission that can be corrected in seconds. As long as you use the correct pen and the correct line, signing the document immediately restores its validity for travel. It is a common occurrence that airport and airline staff are accustomed to seeing.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a valid UK passport that has simply not been signed after receipt. It does not cover damaged passports, expired documents, or name changes that require a full renewal.

## Important note
Acceptance of travel documents is at the discretion of the airline and the border authorities of your destination country. While HM Passport Office provides these signing instructions, individual carriers may have specific internal policies regarding document condition.
