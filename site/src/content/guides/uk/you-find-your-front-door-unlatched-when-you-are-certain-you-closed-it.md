# What to do if… you find your front door unlatched when you are certain you closed it (UK)

## Short answer
Do not go straight in. Move to a safer place outside and contact the police if you think someone may have entered or you feel unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step inside “just to check quickly”.
- Do not call out into the home or announce that you are outside.
- Do not touch the door, handle, lock, frame, or anything that looks disturbed unless you need to move away safely.
- Do not try to search or “clear” the home yourself.
- Do not confront anyone you might see or hear.
- Do not reset, delete, or overwrite doorbell, CCTV, or smart-lock history.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from the threshold.** Step back from the doorway and go somewhere safer, such as inside a locked car, to a neighbour’s door, to a staffed reception area, or to a public place nearby.
2. **Look and listen from outside only.** Notice whether there is damage around the lock or frame, marks near the door, lights you did not leave on, movement, voices, or noises from inside.
3. **If you smell smoke, gas, or a strong chemical smell, move further away.** If there is fire, illness, or immediate danger, call 999. If the concern is gas and there is no immediate danger, use the UK gas emergency route from outside.
4. **Check doorbell, CCTV, or smart-lock events from outside if you can do this safely.** Use your phone and avoid deleting, resetting, or changing anything.
5. **If you think someone may be inside or you feel at risk, call 999.** Say that you have arrived home, the front door is unlatched or ajar, and you are outside. Follow the call handler’s instructions.
6. **If it does not feel like an emergency but still seems suspicious, call 101 or report online through the relevant police route.** Do this from a safe place and ask what they want you to do before entering.
7. **Ask for another person to stay with you outside if that helps you stay safe.** This could be a neighbour, housemate, friend, concierge, building security, landlord, or letting agent.
8. **Photograph only what you can see from outside.** Take clear photos of the door position, lock area, frame, and any visible marks or damage before touching anything.
9. **Only consider going in once the immediate concern has been ruled out.** Keep your phone with you, keep an exit available, and leave again if anything looks disturbed or you feel uneasy.

## What can wait
- Working out whether the latch sometimes fails because of drafts, door alignment, temperature changes, or a worn latch plate.
- Calling a locksmith, landlord, or letting agent unless the door cannot be secured at all.
- Reviewing CCTV in detail.
- Messaging lots of people.
- Listing missing items.
- Changing passwords.
- Contacting insurers.

## Important reassurance
A door can fail to latch because it did not fully click shut, the frame shifted slightly, or the latch is misaligned. Treating it as a safety issue until you are sure you are safe is a reasonable first response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first few minutes after noticing the door is not latched. Later decisions about repairs, reporting, insurance, or security upgrades may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, security, insurance, or professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger or think someone may be inside, call 999. For non-emergencies, call 101 or report online through the relevant police route. 101 generally only works from within the UK.
