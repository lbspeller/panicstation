# What to do if… a stranger approaches your car and tries to get you to unlock the doors (UK)

## Short answer
Keep your doors locked, keep the windows mostly or fully up, and create distance if you can do so safely. If they are trying the handle, blocking you, threatening you, or you believe someone is in immediate danger, call 999 or 112.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock the doors “just for a second”.
- Do not lower the window enough for someone to reach in.
- Do not pass your phone, keys, wallet, bag, or documents out of the car.
- Do not get out to check a tyre, bumper, boot, leak, or item they point at while they are still close to the car.
- Do not follow them to a quieter spot or a second location.
- Do not drive home if you think you are being followed.
- Do not argue through the window or try to prove whether the emergency is real.

## What to do now
1. Lock the car immediately: press the central locking button, keep the windows up, keep your seatbelt on, and keep the engine ready if it is safe and legal to move.
2. Create distance if you safely can. Drive away calmly, or if you are blocked in traffic, leave space in front of your car so you can move when there is an opening.
3. Help without unlocking. If they claim there is an emergency, call 999 or 112 yourself and say where you are, what they are claiming, and what they are doing at your car.
4. Treat it as urgent if they try the handle, crowd your door, demand that you unlock, block your car, threaten you, or refuse to move away. Stay inside and call 999 or 112.
5. Give the call handler simple facts: your road name or landmark, direction of travel if you are moving, the person’s description, any vehicle registration, and whether there are other people nearby.
6. Go to a busier, well-lit place rather than home, such as a staffed petrol station forecourt, supermarket car park, or other public place with people around.
7. If you think you are being followed, keep driving toward a public place and stay on the phone to 999 or 112. Do not stop somewhere isolated to check.
8. If it seems safe, use a short horn press or hazard lights to attract attention while staying locked inside. Stop if it appears to increase the danger.
9. Once you are safe, note the time, location, description, direction they went, and any registration number. Save dashcam footage if you have it.
10. If the immediate danger has passed, report the incident through 101 or your local police force’s online reporting route, especially if there was attempted entry, threats, blocking, or the same approach may be used on other drivers.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the person was genuine or trying to trick you.
- You do not need to make a full formal statement before you have reached safety.
- You do not need to gather proof while the person is still close to the car.
- You do not need to reply politely or explain your decision to keep the doors locked.
- You do not need to inspect the car until you are somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
You can take a claimed emergency seriously without unlocking your car. Calling emergency services yourself, staying inside, and moving to a safer public place is a reasonable first response when someone is pressuring you at your vehicle.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first few minutes only. Later decisions, such as making a fuller police report, repairing damage, sharing dashcam footage, or getting support after a frightening incident, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999 or 112.
