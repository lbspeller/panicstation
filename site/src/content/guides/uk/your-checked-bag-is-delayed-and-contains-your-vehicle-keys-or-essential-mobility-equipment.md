# What to do if… your checked bag is delayed and contains your vehicle keys or essential mobility equipment (UK)

## Short answer
Report the delayed bag to the airline or its baggage handling agent before you leave the airport if you can, get a written case reference, and ask them to record that the bag contains vehicle keys or essential mobility equipment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the airport, or end the call or chat, without a written delayed-bag reference if you can get one.
- Do not assume airport lost property is the right route for checked baggage; checked-bag delay is usually handled by the airline or its ground handler.
- Do not delay saying what is inside the bag. Tell staff straight away if it contains vehicle keys, a wheelchair, crutches, a walking stick, a scooter part, a charger, or another essential mobility item.
- Do not hand over your only baggage tag, boarding pass, booking details, or written case note without taking a photo first.
- Do not make large replacement purchases just to feel prepared. If you need something for safety or basic mobility, keep it minimal, ask the airline what they treat as essential, and keep receipts.
- Do not attempt a difficult transfer, long walk, or unsafe journey just because you feel you should manage.

## What to do now
1. Go to the airline or baggage handling desk in arrivals and report the bag as delayed.
   - Ask for a written case reference. This is often called a Property Irregularity Report, or PIR.
   - If the desk is closed or there is no desk, use the airline’s delayed-baggage form, app, QR code, website, phone line, or ground-handler contact route and save the confirmation.

2. Give the bag details clearly.
   - Use the baggage tag number from check-in.
   - Give a plain description of the bag: colour, size, brand, distinctive marks, and any photo you have.
   - Confirm the flight number and the name on the booking.

3. Ask staff to add the urgent contents to the case.
   - Say: “This delayed bag contains my vehicle keys or essential mobility equipment. I need urgent access.”
   - Ask them to read back the note or show written confirmation if possible.

4. Ask what their tracking shows now.
   - Ask whether the bag appears to still be at the departure airport, at your arrival airport, in transit, or not yet located.
   - Ask what the next update route is and how often you should check it.

5. Confirm delivery and contact details before you leave.
   - Check your mobile number, email address, delivery address, hotel name, room or reception details, and any access instructions.
   - Ask whether delivery can be made to where you will actually be, not just the address originally on the booking.

6. If the delayed bag contains mobility equipment, ask for airport assistance now.
   - Ask the airline, ground handler, or airport assistance desk for help getting to transport, a pickup point, a hotel shuttle, or a safe waiting place.
   - Ask whether an airport wheelchair, temporary mobility support, or contact with the airline’s assistance team is available.
   - If a temporary item is offered, check whether it is safe for you before using it.

7. If the delayed bag contains your vehicle keys, choose a safe stop-gap.
   - Explain that you cannot access your vehicle and ask whether any urgent handling or urgent delivery option is available.
   - Consider a taxi, public transport with step-free routes, a hotel shuttle, or a trusted pickup.
   - If your vehicle is in airport parking, ask the parking operator what options exist for leaving the vehicle there safely while you wait.

8. Save proof in one place.
   - Photograph the baggage tag, boarding pass, case reference, any screen or printout, and any written advice from staff.
   - Write down the time you reported it, who you spoke to if you know, and that you told them about the keys or mobility equipment.

9. If you must buy essentials, keep it narrow.
   - Buy only what you need to stay safe, mobile, contactable, and able to wait for delivery.
   - Keep receipts and ask the airline how to submit them later.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain to a regulator, start a formal claim, or argue about compensation limits.
- You do not need a full inventory of everything in the bag right now.
- You do not need to replace high-value items immediately unless your safety or basic mobility depends on it.
- You can deal with written claims, insurance, and escalation after the bag is reported, the urgent contents are recorded, and you have a safe way to leave or wait.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful because keys and mobility equipment affect your immediate movement, not just your luggage. The most useful first steps are to open the delayed-bag case, make the urgent contents visible on the record, ask for practical assistance, and get to a safe place to wait.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance for the first hours after discovering the checked bag is delayed. Later decisions about claims, reimbursement, insurance, complaints, or replacement equipment may need specialist help or the airline’s formal process.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, travel, or professional advice. Airline and airport processes vary, and a case reference can support a later claim but does not guarantee a specific outcome.
