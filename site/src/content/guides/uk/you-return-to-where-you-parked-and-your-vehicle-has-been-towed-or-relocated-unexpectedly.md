# What to do if… you return to where you parked and your vehicle has been towed or relocated unexpectedly (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the official tracing service (TRACE in London) or the DVLA’s enforcement contractor (NSL) to confirm if the vehicle was removed for parking, tax, or safety reasons before reporting it as stolen to the police.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t report the vehicle as stolen until you have checked council, police, and DVLA removal databases.
- Don’t leave the scene without documenting the exact location, including photos of any nearby signs or temporary suspension notices.
- Don’t pay any individual on the street who claims they can "find" or "release" your car for a cash fee.
- Don’t delay your search; storage fees at vehicle pounds usually accrue daily and can increase significantly after the first 24 hours.
- Don’t assume your vehicle was stolen if there are roadworks, events, or filming taking place in the immediate vicinity.

## What to do now
1. **Check the immediate and adjacent streets for a "relocation" (5–10 minutes).**  
   **Walk approximately 200 metres in both directions from your original parking spot.**
   - Vehicles are often moved to the nearest legal bay rather than impounded if there is an emergency, road repair, or event.
2. **Photograph the empty parking space and surrounding signage immediately.**  
   **Capture clear images of the ground markings, any "Suspension" signs, and the nearest lamp column or street name.**
   - This evidence is vital if you later need to prove that signage was missing or obscured at the time of parking.
3. **If you are in London, use the TRACE service first.**  
   **Visit the TRACE website (trace.london) or call the 24/7 helpdesk on 0300 077 0100.**
   - This database tracks vehicles removed by London Boroughs for parking contraventions.
4. **If your car is untaxed or TRACE has no record, contact NSL.**  
   **Call the national vehicle tracing line for tax-related impounds on 0343 224 1999.**
   - NSL enforces vehicle excise duty on behalf of the DVLA and operates pounds across the UK.
5. **Contact your local council or call the police non-emergency line (101) if still lost.**  
   **Ask the operator if the vehicle has been "seized" (for insurance/licence issues) or "removed" (for obstruction or safety).**
   - If the police have no record, they will provide a reference number to help you proceed with a theft report.
6. **Confirm the required documents before traveling to the pound.**  
   **Ensure you have your V5C (logbook), valid photo ID (Passport/Driving Licence), and valid insurance certificate.**
   - If the vehicle was towed for being untaxed, you must pay the tax or a "Surety Fee" (typically £160 for cars) before it is released.
7. **Retrieve the vehicle as quickly as possible to stop storage fees.**  
   **Request an itemised receipt for all charges, including the removal fee, PCN, and storage costs.**
   - Fees vary; a council removal is typically £200+ plus daily storage, while police statutory charges start around £150–£192 depending on vehicle weight.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to appeal the removal right now; your priority is securing the vehicle and stopping storage costs.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company for a claim until you have official confirmation from the police that the car is not in any pound.
- You do not need to resolve disputes over signage fairness at the pound desk; gather the paperwork and appeal through the formal process later.

## Important reassurance
Returning to an empty space is highly stressful, but most "missing" vehicles are in the hands of authorities rather than thieves. Following the official tracing steps (TRACE/NSL/101) is the fastest way to gain certainty and locate your property.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and tracing for vehicles removed by authorities or relocated for safety. It does not cover long-term legal appeals, insurance claim processing, or recovery of vehicles from private land by non-authority bailiffs.

## Important note
This information is for immediate procedural guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Requirements for vehicle release and the cost of statutory fees can vary by jurisdiction and the specific legislation used for the removal. Always confirm the location and current fees with the relevant pound before traveling.
