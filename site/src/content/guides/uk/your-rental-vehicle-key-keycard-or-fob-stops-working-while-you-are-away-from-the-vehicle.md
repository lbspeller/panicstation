# What to do if… your rental vehicle key, keycard, or fob stops working while you are away from the vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a safe, well-lit area and contact the rental company’s specific 24/7 roadside assistance line provided in your agreement or app. Do not attempt forced entry or hire a private locksmith without their explicit authorisation, as this often voids your liability protection.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to force the door or wedge the window — modern rental vehicles have sophisticated deadlocks and alarm systems that make DIY entry nearly impossible and highly damaging.
- Don’t leave the vehicle unattended in an unlit or isolated area while you search for help if it is currently unlocked.
- Don’t pay for a private locksmith or recovery service upfront unless the rental company has given you an authorisation code or written confirmation.
- Don’t repeatedly press the unlock button if you suspect signal jamming — this can occasionally "de-sync" some older rolling-code fobs.
- Don’t disassemble the fob or attempt to replace the battery yourself if it requires tools, as damage to the casing is usually chargeable.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause point immediately.** Step into a staffed building, such as a shop or hotel, or a well-lit area with CCTV coverage.
2. **Check the fob for a hidden manual override key.** Many modern UK car fobs have a small release catch or sliding panel that reveals a physical metal key blade. 
   - Look for a small keyhole on the driver's door handle (sometimes hidden behind a plastic cap that can be gently popped off).
   - If found, try unlocking the door manually.
3. **Locate the specific 24-hour assistance number.** Check the following locations in order:
   - The key tag or fob sleeve.
   - The rental agreement (often found in the glovebox, but if you are locked out, check your email confirmation).
   - The rental company’s official UK app.
4. **Prepare your information before calling.** Have the vehicle registration (number plate), your booking reference number, and your exact location (postcode or "What3Words" address) ready.
5. **State clearly if the vehicle is currently secure or open.** If the car is unlocked and cannot be locked, tell the agent immediately; they may prioritise a faster response to prevent theft.
6. **Ask the agent for the "Job Reference" or "Case Number".** If they dispatch a third-party recovery service (like the RAC, AA, or a local contractor), you will need this to identify the person who arrives.
7. **Verify the technician's identity upon arrival.** Do not hand over the non-working key or allow access to the vehicle until the technician confirms your name and the rental company they are representing.
8. **Follow the rental company’s instructions for key replacement.** If a technician cannot bypass the fault, you may be directed to the nearest rental branch for a swap or they may arrange a vehicle recovery.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to determine the cause of the failure (battery vs. electronic fault) yourself.
- You don’t need to argue about the "call-out fee" at the roadside; this is handled during the final billing and return process.
- You don’t need to worry about "losing your deposit" immediately; equipment failures are common and often covered if no negligence is found.

## Important reassurance
Key and fob failures are a frequent occurrence in the rental industry due to high usage and battery wear. Rental companies have established protocols for "lockout" and "key failure" scenarios. As long as you follow their official assistance route, you are following the standard procedure to resolve the issue safely and legally.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a vehicle access crisis. It does not cover long-term dispute resolution regarding rental charges or mechanical repairs beyond the immediate lockout.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific Terms and Conditions of your rental agreement. If you are in immediate physical danger or witness a crime in progress, call 999. Under the UK Consumer Rights Act 2015, you may be entitled to a refund or price reduction if the vehicle equipment was not of "satisfactory quality" at the start of the hire.
