# What to do if… your water softener or filtration unit is overflowing and there is water near electrical equipment (UK)

## Short answer
Get everyone away from the wet area and switch electricity off only if you can do it from a dry, safe position. If you cannot safely isolate the electricity, treat the area as live and get emergency help or urgent professional advice.

## Do not do these things
- Do not step into pooled water to reach plugs, sockets, extension leads, appliances, or your consumer unit or fuse box.
- Do not touch switches, plugs, valves near electrical items, or appliances if your hands are wet or you are standing on a wet floor.
- Do not test lights, pumps, sockets, timers, softener controls, or filtration controls while water is nearby.
- Do not reset tripped breakers or RCDs, or turn circuits back on, until the affected electrics have been checked.
- Do not start mopping, vacuuming, or moving wet items while the electricity may still be live.
- Do not ignore a hot or burning smell, buzzing, crackling, scorch marks, smoke, sparking, or flickering.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Keep people and pets out of the wet room or area. If there is smoke, fire, visible sparking, or anyone may have had an electric shock, leave the area and call **999**.

2. **Switch electricity off only if it is safe.**
   - If you can reach the consumer unit or main switch from dry ground, and the board itself looks dry, switch the **main switch OFF**.
   - If there is water, damp, staining, dripping, buzzing, heat, scorch marks, or arcing at or near the consumer unit or fuse box, do not touch it.
   - If you would have to cross wet flooring to reach it, do not touch it.

3. **If you cannot safely isolate the electricity, keep away and call for help.**
   - For immediate danger, smoke, fire, sparking, or suspected shock, call **999**.
   - For electricity network advice in England, Wales and Scotland, call **105** free and explain that there is a water leak or overflow and you are worried about live electrics.
   - In Northern Ireland, call **03457 643643** for the electricity network operator.
   - For internal wiring, sockets, appliances, or the consumer unit, arrange a qualified electrician before power is turned back on.

4. **Stop the water only if you can do it from a dry, safe position.**
   - If the softener or filtration unit has labelled inlet and outlet isolation valves, turn them off if they are accessible and dry.
   - If the unit has a bypass valve and it is safe to reach, set the unit to bypass using the installer’s labels or manual if obvious.
   - If you cannot quickly identify the unit valves, turn off the property’s main stopcock or stop valve, but only if you can reach it without standing in water.

5. **Limit the spread without entering the wet electrical area.**
   - From dry ground, place towels at the edge of the water to slow it spreading towards sockets, extension leads, the boiler, or the consumer unit.
   - Do not move wet plugs, wet extension leads, wet chargers, or wet power strips.
   - If power is off and an item’s plug and lead are dry and reachable from dry ground, you can move it away from the wet area.

6. **Leave the softener or filtration unit alone once the flow is stopped.**
   - Do not open control heads, remove covers, handle timers, or touch any electrical parts while anything is wet.
   - Do not restart regeneration, pumps, UV lamps, booster pumps, or powered filtration controls.
   - If water is still flowing and you cannot stop it safely, keep out of the wet area and contact an emergency plumber.

7. **Record what happened from a safe place, then contact the right trades.**
   - Take a few photos from a dry, safe position: the water path, affected sockets or appliances, the unit, and any valves you used.
   - Contact an emergency plumber, the installer, or the maintenance provider for the leak or overflow.
   - Contact a qualified electrician to check any socket, circuit, appliance, boiler control, pump, softener control, filtration control, consumer unit, or fuse box that may have got wet before power is restored.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault is the float, brine tank, drain line, filter housing, pressure valve, hose, or control head right now.
- You do not need to decide about repair, replacement, warranties, insurance, or water quality settings immediately.
- You do not need to clean perfectly or dry the whole area while there may still be an electrical risk.
- You do not need to turn the water softener or filtration unit back on today.

## Important reassurance
Water near electrics is a situation where caution is the right response. Keeping away, stopping the water only from a dry safe position, and getting the electrics checked before turning power back on are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk from electric shock, fire, and spreading water damage. Later decisions about plumbing repair, electrical testing, insurance, or replacement may need help from a plumber, qualified electrician, installer, landlord, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional electrical, plumbing, legal, insurance, medical, or emergency-service advice. If there is immediate danger, suspected electric shock, smoke, fire, sparking, or you cannot safely isolate power from a dry position, keep away and contact emergency services or urgent professional help.
