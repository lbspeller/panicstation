# What to do if… a date pressures you to let them inside your home after you said you are not comfortable (UK)

## Short answer
Do not let them in under any circumstances. Immediately close and lock the door and end the interaction from the other side.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to "be polite" or to finish the conversation.
- Do not justify, argue, or explain your decision—this provides openings for more pressure.
- Do not allow them in for "just a minute" to use the bathroom, charge a phone, or get water.
- Do not step outside or move to a more private area (like a stairwell) to talk.
- Do not worry about appearing rude; priority is physical separation.
- Do not consume substances that may lower your environmental awareness while the person is still present.

## What to do now
1. **Create a physical barrier by closing and locking the front door immediately.** If the door is already open, step back, pull it shut, and engage all locks, chains, or bolts.
2. **Give one final verbal instruction through the closed door and then stop speaking.** Say clearly: "I am not letting you in. Please leave now."
3. **Bring in a third party immediately by calling a friend or family member.** Put them on speakerphone and state: "I am at my door and a date is refusing to leave; stay on the line with me until they are gone."
4. **Call 999 if the person refuses to leave, attempts to force entry, or makes threats.** Under 2026 national standards, 999 calls are expected to be answered within 10 seconds, with urban police response targets of 15 minutes.
5. **Contact building security or a trusted neighbour if you are in a shared complex.** Use an intercom or phone to ask them to come to your floor or watch the entrance while you remain behind your locked door.
6. **Report the harassment via 101 if the person leaves but you remain concerned for your safety.** This creates a record of the incident with the police even if it is no longer an immediate emergency.
7. **Document the incident once you are in a secure room away from the door.** Note the time, exactly what was said, and any specific behaviors (e.g., foot in door, repeated knocking, refusal to move).
8. **Contact a specialist support line if the interaction involved coercion or sexual threats.**
   - England and Wales: Rape Crisis 24/7 Support Line on 0808 500 2222.
   - Scotland: Rape Crisis Scotland (5pm – midnight) on 08088 01 03 02.
   - Northern Ireland: Domestic & Sexual Abuse Helpline (24/7) on 0808 802 1414.
9. **Access a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) if any unwanted physical contact occurred.** These centres provide medical care and can store evidence without requiring a police report.
   - In Scotland, call the NHS 24 SARCS self-referral line on 0800 148 88 88.
   - In England/Wales, find your local SARC via the NHS website or 111.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to block them on apps or social media right this second.
- You do not need to decide if you want to make a formal police statement.
- You do not need to "process" why they behaved this way or if you sent "mixed signals."
- You do not need to respond to any follow-up texts or apologies they send tonight.

## Important reassurance
Feeling "frozen," "polite," or "uncertain" is a standard physiological response to social pressure or perceived danger. You are not "overreacting" by locking your door; your body’s alarm system is responding to a boundary violation.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and the immediate termination of doorstep pressure. It does not cover long-term legal options or therapeutic recovery.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and is not a substitute for legal or medical advice. In an emergency, always dial 999.
