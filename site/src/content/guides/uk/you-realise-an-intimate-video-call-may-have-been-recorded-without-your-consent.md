# What to do if… you realise an intimate video call may have been recorded without your consent (UK)

## Short answer
End the call immediately, stop all contact with the person involved, and move to a safe location. Use confidential specialist takedown tools and reporting services to prevent or remove shared content.

## Do not do these things
- Don't pay money, send additional images, or perform any actions to "prove" your identity or compliance.
- Don't negotiate or argue with the person making threats, as this often leads to increased pressure.
- Don't delete message history or contact details in a panic; this information is needed for reporting and evidence.
- Don't post about the incident on public social media, which can increase the visibility of the content.
- Don't attempt to "hack" or retaliate against the person who recorded the call.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a secure location and disconnect.** **End the video call immediately and ensure your camera is physically covered or the app is fully closed.** If you feel at risk of physical harm because the person knows your location, call **999** immediately.
2. **Cease all communication with the individual.** **Block or mute the person on the application used for the call and any other platforms where they may try to reach you.** Do not respond to any further demands or threats.
3. **Capture a record of the threat or recording.** **Take screenshots of any messages, usernames, profile pages, or payment demands associated with the incident.** Ensure these are saved to a secure folder or a device that only you can access.
4. **Report the incident to the platform where it occurred.** **Use the in-app reporting tools for "harassment," "non-consensual intimate content," or "sexual abuse."** Under UK law as of 2026, major platforms are required to review and remove flagged intimate images shared without consent within 48 hours.
5. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for takedown assistance.** **If you are aged 18 or over, call 0345 6000 459 (Mon–Fri, 10am–4pm) or use the 24/7 REIYA chatbot on their website for immediate guidance.** They can help coordinate removals across multiple platforms and provide a single point of contact for takedowns.
6. **Access specialist sexual violence support.** **Call the Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 Support Line on 0808 500 2222 or use their online chat service.** This service is confidential and available to anyone aged 16+ who has experienced any form of sexual violation or non-consensual recording.
7. **Use StopNCII.org to prevent future uploads.** **If you have access to the original video or image file, use this tool to create a digital fingerprint (hash) that allows participating platforms to block the content from being shared.** The file itself is never uploaded and remains on your device at all times.
8. **Report the crime to the police.** **If you are not in immediate danger, report the incident online via the National My Police Portal or call 101.** Intimate image abuse—including the threat to share—is a criminal offence under Section 66B of the Sexual Offences Act 2003.

**If you were under 18 at the time of the recording:**
9. **Use the Report Remove tool immediately.** **Access the "Report Remove" service via the Childline website to have the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) identify and remove the content from the internet.** You can also make a report to the **CEOP Safety Centre** if you are concerned about grooming or online sexual exploitation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to pursue a full criminal prosecution today.
- You do not need to notify your employer, school, or family members immediately unless you feel they are at risk.
- You do not need to justify your actions or explain why you were on the call to anyone.
- You do not need to understand the technical details of how the recording happened.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of panic, shame, or violation when you realise a private moment has been recorded without your permission. These feelings are a normal response to an invasive act. Regardless of whether you consented to the original call, the act of recording or threatening to share that content is a violation of your rights and, in many cases, a crime. Support is available to help you regain control.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK residents facing non-consensual recording or threats of intimate image abuse. It focuses on immediate safety, content removal, and connecting with established UK support frameworks.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or fear for your physical safety, contact emergency services on 999. Reporting and takedown processes may vary depending on the specific platform and the nature of the content.
