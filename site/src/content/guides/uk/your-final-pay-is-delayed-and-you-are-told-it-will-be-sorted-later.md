# What to do if… your final pay is delayed and you are told it will be sorted later (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your payroll department or manager immediately to confirm if payment was issued and request a specific time for when the funds will arrive. Do not wait for the employer to notice the error.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait several days before reporting the missing payment.
- Do not resign or stop working immediately without legal advice, as this may be a breach of contract.
- Do not send aggressive or emotional messages to payroll or management.
- Do not accept verbal promises of "next month" without a written agreement for an immediate fix.
- Do not share banking passwords or two-factor codes with anyone claiming to "verify" the transaction.

## What to do now
1. **Verify your expected pay date and bank details.** Confirm the date specified in your employment contract or most recent written pay schedule.
   - Check that your bank details (sort code and account number) on your last payslip or HR portal are correct.
   - Note if this is a first payment, a bank holiday, or if you have recently changed accounts.
2. **Review your bank account for pending or rejected transactions.** Log in to your banking app to see if there is an incoming "pending" credit or a notice of a failed transfer.
3. **Contact your payroll department or HR representative immediately.** Ask them to confirm if the payment was sent, the method used (e.g., BACS, Faster Payments), and the exact time it is expected to clear.
4. **Request an immediate manual or emergency payment if an error is confirmed.** If the employer admits a mistake, ask for a "same-day" payment to resolve the issue before close of business.
5. **Document the situation in a written timeline.** Save copies of emails, sent messages, and any responses received from management.
   - Record the name of the person you spoke to, the time of the call, and what was promised.
6. **Report any bank charges or penalties incurred due to the delay.** Keep evidence of late fees or overdraft interest resulting from the missing pay.
   - Request in writing that the employer reimburses these costs as they are "consequential losses" from their breach of contract.
7. **Contact your trade union representative if you are a member.** Ask them to intervene or provide support in raising an urgent formal pay dispute.
8. **Raise a formal grievance if the issue is not resolved within 24 hours.** Use your company’s internal grievance procedure to submit a formal written complaint regarding the "unauthorised deduction from wages."
9. **Seek external advice from Acas.** Call the Acas helpline at **0300 123 1100** (8am–6pm, Monday to Friday) for guidance on your rights.
   - Be aware that most legal claims for unpaid wages must be started through "Early Conciliation" within 3 months minus 1 day of the missing payday.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on long-term legal action today.
- You do not need to notify senior executives or stakeholders outside of your immediate management or HR.
- You do not need to finalize a new budget until you have a confirmed date for the incoming funds.

## Important reassurance
Pay delays are often caused by technical banking errors or administrative payroll mistakes rather than a refusal to pay. It is standard to feel a high sense of urgency when expected funds are missing. Following a structured reporting process is the most effective way to trigger a manual correction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a missing payday. It does not cover long-term insolvency (employer going bust) or complex tax disputes with HMRC.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Employment laws and tribunal time limits are strict; ensure you verify current deadlines with Acas or a qualified solicitor if you intend to pursue a legal claim.
