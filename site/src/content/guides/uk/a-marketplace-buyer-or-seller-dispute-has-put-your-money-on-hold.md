# What to do if… a marketplace buyer or seller dispute has put your money on hold (UK)

## Short answer
Keep everything inside the marketplace or payment platform, save the evidence now, and submit a clear response before any deadline shown in the official case thread.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t move the conversation to private email, WhatsApp, or any off-platform refund, return, or payment request.
- Don’t close a dispute unless you are satisfied and the platform clearly says what closing it will do to your options.
- Don’t send extra money, gift cards, crypto, or “verification” payments to release the hold.
- Don’t ship a replacement or accept a return without using the platform’s return process and tracked delivery.
- Don’t delete messages, listings, receipts, tracking pages, packaging photos, or item photos.
- Don’t post threats or accusations in the case thread; keep it short, factual, and document-based.

## What to do now
1. **Capture the basic facts in one place.** Write down or screenshot the case ID, transaction ID, amount, item, dates, platform name, payment provider, and the reason given for the hold, such as “item not received”, “not as described”, “return pending”, “chargeback”, or “account review”.
2. **Save the evidence before anything changes.** Download or screenshot the listing, order page, payment confirmation, messages, dispatch receipt, tracking page, delivery confirmation, return instructions, and dispute status page.
3. **Check whether the money is pending, held, or completed.** In your bank, card, PayPal, or marketplace account, note the exact wording. If it is only pending or authorised, the bank may not be able to release it on request; still follow the marketplace or payment-provider dispute steps and keep checking whether it settles or drops off.
4. **If you are the seller, put delivery proof where the reviewer will see it.** Enter tracking in the marketplace or payment system, not just in chat. Upload proof of delivery, delivery photo or signature if available, dispatch receipt, packaging photos, item condition photos, and any serial number or unique mark you recorded.
5. **If you are the buyer, keep your protection inside the platform flow.** If a return is required, use the return label or method the platform tells you to use. Use tracked delivery, keep the receipt, photograph the item and packaging before sending, and keep proof of exactly what you returned.
6. **Post one calm case response.** Use a short timeline: ordered, paid, shipped, delivered, issue raised, evidence attached. Attach the key documents rather than a long argument.
7. **Set reminders for every platform deadline shown.** Missing a response window can mean the platform decides using only what has already been submitted.
8. **If a payment provider or bank is holding the money and support is not resolving it, make a formal complaint.** Use the firm’s complaints route, not only live chat. Save a copy of the complaint and the date sent.
9. **Use the UK financial complaint route only when it fits.** For payment services or e-money complaints, firms usually have 15 business days to respond. For most other financial complaints, firms usually have 8 weeks. You can usually go to the Financial Ombudsman Service after a final response, or if the firm has not responded within the relevant time.
10. **If you paid by card and the platform route is failing, ask your card provider what protection applies.** Chargeback may help with many debit or credit card payments, but it is not a legal right and is not guaranteed. If you used a credit card, Section 75 may apply to items costing more than £100 and up to £30,000, but marketplace and payment-intermediary setups can make this more complicated.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to sue, post publicly, or start several complaint routes at once.
- You do not need to prove intent or win an argument right now.
- You do not need to accept any quick fix outside the platform to get relief.
- You do not need to send extra documents unless the platform, payment provider, bank, or ombudsman route asks for them.

## Important reassurance
A large hold can feel as if your money has disappeared, but dispute holds and account reviews are a process. The safest early moves are to keep it official, keep it documented, and meet the deadline.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise a marketplace or payment dispute and reduce irreversible mistakes. Later decisions may need specialist help, especially if the amount is high or the platform, bank, or payment provider gives a final decision you disagree with.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Rules and outcomes can vary by marketplace, payment provider, card scheme, dispute reason, evidence, and deadlines.
