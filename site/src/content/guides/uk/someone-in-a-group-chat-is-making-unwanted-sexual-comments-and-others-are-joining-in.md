# What to do if… someone in a group chat is making unwanted sexual comments and others are joining in (UK)
## Short answer
Create distance first: mute the chat, stop replying, and step away from the screen. If it is safe to do so, save a small record before blocking, leaving, or reporting; if there is immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t try to “out-joke” it or prove you can take it.
- Don’t keep explaining yourself in the group while you are panicking.
- Don’t delete the whole chat history in a rush if you may want help later.
- Don’t move into a private chat with the person who started it unless you genuinely feel safe and want to.
- Don’t share intimate images, personal details, or “proof” to make them stop.
- Don’t let anyone pressure you into reporting, confronting, or forgiving before you have had a pause.

## What to do now
1. **Create immediate distance:** Mute the group, turn off message previews if they are showing on your lock screen, and put the phone down for a moment. If leaving the group will not make things worse and you do not need the chat history, leave it.
2. **Stop feeding the pile-on:** Do not reply, react, argue, or apologise in the group. If your app allows it, turn off read receipts or “last seen” for now.
3. **Save a minimal record if you can:** Take a few screenshots showing the group name, the people involved, the sexualised comments, and dates or times. Save only enough to show what happened so you do not have to keep re-reading it.
4. **Use the app’s safety tools:** Report the messages, the users, or the group inside the app. Block the main person and anyone else who keeps escalating, unless blocking would increase a safety risk.
5. **Use one safe person, not the whole group:** Tell one trusted person what happened and ask them to stay with you while you mute, block, report, or decide the next step.
6. **If there is a trusted admin:** Message one admin privately: “I’m being repeatedly sexualised in the group. I’ve muted it. Please remove what you can, stop the pile-on, and remove the person if you have that power.” If you do not trust any admin, skip this.
7. **If there are threats, stalking, or you feel unsafe:** If danger is immediate, call 999. For non-emergency police contact in the UK, you can usually call 101 or use the relevant online police reporting route.
8. **If intimate images are involved:** If someone has shared, threatened to share, or pressured you for intimate images, get specialist help promptly. The Revenge Porn Helpline supports adults in the UK; if you are under 18 or worried about a child or young person, CEOP can help with online sexual abuse concerns.
9. **If this connects to an offline sexual assault:** You can contact an NHS Sexual Assault Referral Centre for medical, practical, and emotional support. You can ask for support even if you are not sure about reporting to police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to confront the group.
- You do not need to decide right now whether it is “serious enough”.
- You do not need to explain yourself to everyone.
- You do not need to change your number, delete your account, or make big social decisions today.
- You can stabilise first and decide later with support.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, feeling embarrassed, or doubting yourself can happen when a group turns sexual and humiliating. Other people joining in does not mean you consented, and it does not make the behaviour your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce contact, lower panic, and buy time. Later decisions, including school, workplace, platform, police, or support-service options, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent first steps, not legal, medical, therapeutic, safeguarding, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe, or if the behaviour includes threats, stalking, coercion, or intimate images, consider contacting police or a specialist support service.
