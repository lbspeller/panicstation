# What to do if… you find an outlet or plug faceplate is discoloured or warped and you suspect overheating (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the socket immediately and turn off the power at the consumer unit (fuse box) if it is safe to do so. Arrange for a registered electrician to inspect the installation before turning the power back on.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the socket, plug, or cable if it appears melted, soft, or feels hot to the touch.
- Do not use water on any electrical fire or smoking component.
- Do not attempt to remove the socket faceplate or investigate the internal wiring yourself.
- Do not ignore "fishy" smells or the scent of burning plastic, even if there is no visible smoke.
- Do not continue to use other sockets on the same circuit until the issue has been assessed.
- Do not plug the appliance into a different socket, as the appliance plug itself may be the source of the heat.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance from the hazard.** If you see active smoke, flames, or hear persistent loud crackling, evacuate all occupants from the property immediately.
   - Call **999** once you are outside.
2. **Isolate the power at the consumer unit (fuse box).** If it is safe to move through the property, go to your consumer unit and switch off the master switch or the specific circuit breaker (MCB) for the affected area.
   - If the consumer unit itself is hot, smells of burning, or is making noise, do not touch it; evacuate and call **999**.
3. **Unplug the appliance only if it is cold.** If the power is off and the plug/socket is not hot or melted, remove the plug from the socket by pulling the plug body, not the cable.
   - If the plug is stuck or "welded" to the socket, **do not force it**; leave it in place with the power off.
4. **Treat any electrical burns immediately.** If anyone has touched the hot surface and sustained a burn, cool the area under cool running water for at least 20 minutes.
   - All electrical burns must be assessed at a hospital Accident & Emergency (A&E) department, even if they appear minor.
5. **Prevent accidental use by others.** Place a physical note or tape over the affected socket and the consumer unit switch to ensure no one restores power or plugs anything in.
6. **Contact a registered professional.**
   - If you are a tenant, contact your landlord or letting agent immediately; this is an urgent safety matter.
   - If you are a homeowner, contact a registered electrician (check the NICEIC or Napit registers).
7. **Keep the affected appliance for inspection.** Do not throw away the appliance that was plugged in, as the electrician may need to check the plug and fuse for faults.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause (such as a loose terminal or an overloaded circuit) right now.
- You do not need to replace the hardware yourself.
- You do not need to decide whether the appliance is permanently broken until it is tested.

## Important reassurance
Signs of overheating like browning or warping are clear indicators that a safety mechanism or connection is failing. Taking the action to isolate the power now is a standard and effective way to prevent an electrical fire. Feeling concerned by these signs is a normal response to a significant hazard.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization only. These steps are intended to remove the immediate fire risk. A full technical inspection by a qualified professional is required to determine if the wiring remains safe to use.

## Important note
This guide is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute professional electrical or medical advice. If you suspect an active fire, always prioritize evacuation and contact emergency services.
