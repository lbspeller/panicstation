# What to do if… you cannot pay for essentials because your only card or account is temporarily blocked (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank immediately using the number on the back of your card or via their official app to confirm if the block is a temporary security hold that can be lifted with identity verification. If you cannot access funds for food or heating today, contact your local council to apply for the Crisis and Resilience Fund or a Crisis Grant.

## Do not do these things
- Don't keep retrying the payment — multiple failed attempts can escalate a security hold into a total account lock.
- Don't click links in "security alert" texts — only use the phone number printed on your physical card or the official website.
- Don't move money to a "safe account" if someone calling you claims your bank is under attack — banks never ask you to do this.
- Don't take out high-interest emergency loans or payday credit before checking for local authority crisis grants.
- Don't give away your full PIN or one-time passcodes (OTPs) to anyone, even someone claiming to be from your bank's fraud team.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the nature of the block using a secure channel.** **Check your banking app for "frozen" toggles or "verify transaction" alerts that allow you to unblock the card yourself.**
   - Look for specific notifications about a suspicious transaction.
   - Confirm if the block is for a single card or the entire account.

2. **Call your bank's fraud or security department directly.** **Use the telephone number printed on the back of your bank card or found on the bank's verified official website.**
   - State clearly: "My only card is blocked and I cannot pay for essentials like food and heating today."
   - Ask for "same-day access to funds" or a "counter withdrawal" at a local branch.

3. **Request an immediate branch withdrawal if the card cannot be unblocked over the phone.** **If your bank has physical branches, take photo ID to a branch and ask to withdraw cash over the counter despite the card block.**
   - Take a passport or driving licence as primary identification.
   - Ask if they can issue an "emergency cash" code for use at an ATM if you cannot reach a branch.

4. **Contact essential payees to prevent late fees or service cuts.** **If a priority bill like rent, electricity, or water is due today, call the provider and explain you have a temporary banking restriction.**
   - Ask them to note your account and pause any automated escalation for 48 hours.
   - Keep a record of the time you called and the name of the person you spoke to.

5. **Apply for emergency local authority support if you remain without funds for basics.** **In England, contact your local council to apply for the Crisis and Resilience Fund for help with food and fuel.**
   - In Scotland, apply for a Crisis Grant through the Scottish Welfare Fund (decisions are usually made by the end of the next working day).
   - In Wales, apply for an Emergency Assistance Payment via the Discretionary Assistance Fund (DAF).
   - In Northern Ireland, contact the Finance Support Service to apply for Discretionary Support.

6. **Initiate a formal complaint if the bank cannot provide a clear reason or timeline for the block.** **Ask the bank to register a "formal complaint" regarding your lack of access to essential funds and request a reference number.**
   - Banks must generally provide a final response to payment-related complaints within 15 business days.
   - Remind them of their duty to ensure you have access to "exempt funds" such as disability benefits or child support if applicable.

## What can wait
- Switching your current account to a different bank or building society.
- Disputing older, non-essential transactions that are not related to the current block.
- Researching long-term budgeting tools or savings accounts.
- Replacing non-essential direct debits for streaming services or memberships.

## Important reassurance
Security blocks are frequently automated and do not always mean your money is gone or your account is closed permanently. These holds are often triggered by a sudden change in spending patterns or a location change; once you have successfully passed the bank's security identity checks, access is usually restored immediately.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate access to money and essentials. It does not cover long-term debt management, legal challenges to permanent account closures, or complex fraud investigations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance only and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Regulations regarding account blocks and local authority funding can change; always verify the current application process with your specific council or bank.
