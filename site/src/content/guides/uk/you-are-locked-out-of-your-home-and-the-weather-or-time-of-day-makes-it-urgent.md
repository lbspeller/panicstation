# What to do if… you are locked out of your home and the weather or time of day makes it urgent (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a warm, sheltered location (neighbour, open business, or 24-hour public venue) and then contact your landlord, a trusted keyholder, or an MLA-approved locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to climb through windows, scale walls, or force doors, as this causes injury and may lead to police intervention.
- Do not remain outdoors in freezing, wet, or extreme conditions while waiting for help.
- Do not hire a locksmith from a search engine result without verifying they have a physical address and a Master Locksmiths Association (MLA) logo.
- Do not allow a locksmith to drill or destroy your lock as the first option if non-destructive entry is possible.
- Do not pay a locksmith in cash or via untraceable methods before receiving an itemised invoice.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise physical warmth and shelter immediately.**
   - **Move to a staffed location** such as a shop, hotel lobby, or supermarket if you are alone or without a coat.
   - **Check for signs of hypothermia** in yourself or others, including shivering, slurred speech, confusion, or cold/pale skin.
   - **Call 999 immediately** if someone is becoming drowsy, stops shivering while still cold, or has shallow breathing.

2. **Isolate the person from the cold while waiting.**
   - **Move the person to a sheltered place** and remove any wet clothing if possible.
   - **Wrap them in blankets** or whatever dry material is available.
   - **Provide a warm, non-alcoholic drink** and sugary food only if they are fully conscious and can swallow normally.

3. **Exhaust all safe, non-technical entry points.**
   - **Check all secondary entrances** including back doors, side gates, or communal access points.
   - **Contact a co-resident or nearby keyholder** (family, friend, or trusted neighbour) who may have a spare.
   - **Call your landlord or letting agent** if you rent; they are legally required to manage emergency repairs and usually have a 24-hour out-of-hours number.

4. **Verify a locksmith before they arrive on-site.**
   - **Check the Master Locksmiths Association (MLA) website** to find a vetted, criminal-record-checked professional.
   - **Confirm the total price range** over the phone, including the call-out fee, hourly rate, and VAT.
   - **Ask specifically** if they use non-destructive entry techniques before they agree to the job.

5. **Prepare proof of residency for the attending professional.**
   - **Have photo ID ready** if it is on your person.
   - **Access digital proof** such as a tenancy agreement, utility bill, or council tax portal on your phone if your physical ID is inside.
   - **Identify a neighbour** who can vouch for your residency if you have no phone or documents.

6. **Contact the police only if there is a threat to safety.**
   - **Call 999** if a child or vulnerable person is locked inside or if you are in immediate physical danger.
   - **Report non-emergency safety concerns online** via your local force website or call 101 if you cannot access the internet.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on permanent lock upgrades or security systems tonight.
- You do not need to process insurance claims for "Home Emergency" cover until you are warm and inside.
- You do not need to resolve disputes over locksmith pricing at the doorstep; request an invoice and contact Trading Standards or Citizens Advice later if you suspect a scam.

## Important reassurance
Being locked out in the dark or cold is a high-stress event that triggers a natural panic response. Most lockouts are resolved within two hours once a professional or keyholder is contacted. Your immediate priority is your physical safety from the weather, not the door.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and emergency entry only. It does not cover long-term security planning or legal disputes between tenants and landlords regarding lock changes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or medical advice. For medical emergencies involving cold exposure, contact 999. For locksmith disputes, consult Citizens Advice.
