# What to do if… you are told HR needs to discuss a concern and asks for an immediate call (UK)

## Short answer
Provide your Right to Work evidence through a secure, verified channel immediately to establish the employer's "statutory excuse" and protect your shifts. Use the Home Office online share code service if you have digital status, as this is the standard compliant method for most workers in 2026.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the request; UK employers face fines of up to £60,000 per worker for non-compliance and often suspend shifts to avoid legal liability.
- Do not send photos of your passport or visa via WhatsApp, personal social media, or unverified personal email addresses.
- Do not hand over original physical documents for an employer to keep; they are only permitted to verify them in your presence and take a copy.
- Do not provide more information than required; a share code or the specific documents listed in Home Office "List A" or "List B" are sufficient.
- Do not assume a previous check is enough; employers must perform follow-up checks when visas expire or when transitioning to the 2026 digital-only eVisa system.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the request comes from an official company source.**
   - Contact your HR department or manager via a known internal phone number or official email address to confirm the request is legitimate.
   - Ask for the specific secure portal or office location where the evidence should be provided.

2. **Determine which of the three 2026 checking methods applies to you.**
   - **Online Share Code:** Use this if you have an eVisa, settled or pre-settled status, or a Frontier Worker permit.
   - **Manual Document Check:** Use this if you are a British or Irish citizen without a valid passport (using a birth certificate and NI number) or in very limited physical document cases.
   - **IDSP (Identity Service Provider):** Use this if your employer uses a certified digital service to verify British or Irish passports remotely.

3. **Generate and provide a "W" prefix share code if you have digital status.**
   - **Access the "Prove your right to work" service on GOV.UK.**
   - **Select the option to prove your status to an employer.**
   - **Send the 9-character code (beginning with 'W') and your date of birth to HR.**
   - Note that this code is valid for 90 days from the date of generation.

4. **Request a face-to-face meeting if a manual check is required.**
   - **Bring your original documents to the workplace or HR office.**
   - **Ensure the employer checks the documents in your presence.**
   - **Confirm that the employer has taken a clear, dated copy for their records.**

5. **Request the Employer Checking Service (ECS) if you have an application pending.**
   - **Provide HR with your Home Office reference number if you have an outstanding appeal or application.**
   - **Ask the employer to request a check via the Home Office ECS portal.**
   - A "Positive Verification Notice" from the ECS grants you a statutory right to work for 6 months while your application is processed.

6. **Request written confirmation of your schedule status.**
   - **Send a brief email stating: "I have now provided [Share Code / Documents / ECS details]. Please confirm my shifts are secured and I remain on the schedule."**

7. **Document the interaction.**
   - **Keep a log of the time, date, and method used to provide your evidence.**
   - **Save a screenshot of the share code you generated or the confirmation email from HR.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide a full history of previous visas or travel unless it is directly required for the current check.
- You do not need to decide on long-term employment changes or formal grievances while the immediate check is being processed.
- You do not need to provide "proof of address" or utility bills for a standard Right to Work check unless they are part of a specific document combination for those without passports.

## Important reassurance
Employers are often under intense pressure from Home Office audits and increased 2026 enforcement penalties. An urgent request usually indicates a missing file or a routine compliance "tidy-up" rather than a personal issue. Providing a standard share code or showing a valid passport typically resolves the matter within minutes.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for urgent Right to Work requests. It does not cover complex immigration appeals, long-term visa renewals, or detailed employment law litigation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and reflects UK Right to Work rules as of March 2026. Employers must follow the Home Office "Code of Practice on preventing illegal working." If you believe you are being targeted due to your nationality or race, or if an employer refuses a valid share code, you should seek advice from ACAS or a qualified immigration solicitor.
