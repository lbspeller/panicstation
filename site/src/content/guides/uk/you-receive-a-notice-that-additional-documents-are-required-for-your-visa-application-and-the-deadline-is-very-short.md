# What to do if… you receive a notice that additional documents are required for your visa application and the deadline is very short (UK)

## Short answer
Capture the exact request and reference number, gather the evidence immediately, and submit it through the specific channel named in the notice within five working days to avoid automatic refusal.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore the notice even if you think you already provided the document; a second submission is safer than a refusal.
- Don’t use a different submission route (like general enquiry forms) unless the notice explicitly directs you to an alternative.
- Don’t upload files with names longer than 25 characters or containing special symbols, as this can trigger portal errors.
- Don’t send self-translated documents; UKVI requires professional certification for any document not in English or Welsh.
- Don’t crop or edit scans in a way that removes the page edges, headers, footers, or barcodes.
- Don’t wait for the final hour of the deadline to attempt an upload in case of system-wide outages.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact submission window and channel (2 minutes).** Locating the deadline is critical; UKVI typically allows 5 working days for additional information requests, but the notice may specify a shorter window.
2. **Standardise your file formats immediately.** 
   - Convert all documents to PDF or JPEG.
   - Ensure each file is under 6MB (or the limit stated on your specific portal).
   - Use descriptive, short filenames (e.g., BankStatementJan2026.pdf).
3. **Verify translation requirements against 2026 standards.** If a document is not in English or Welsh, it must include a certified translation that specifically contains:
   - A statement from the translator confirming it is an accurate translation of the original.
   - The date of the translation.
   - The translator's full name and signature.
   - The translator's or translation company's contact details.
4. **Execute a single, complete upload session.** Gather all requested items and upload them together rather than in fragments, as some portals restrict the number of times you can "confirm" or "submit" a response.
5. **Address unobtainable documents with a formal explanation.** If a specific document cannot be obtained within the timeframe (e.g., a bank letter taking 10 days):
   - Upload a short, factual cover letter explaining the delay.
   - Attach proof that the document has been requested (receipts or sent emails).
   - State the exact date you expect to receive and forward the item.
6. **Escalate technical portal failures immediately.** If the upload fails, take a screenshot of the error message including the date and time, then contact:
   - UKVI Resolution Centre: +44 (0) 300 790 6268 (Inside UK) or +44 (0) 20 3080 0010 (Outside UK).
   - Technical Support Email: ApplyOnlineE-Support@homeoffice.gov.uk.
7. **Retain the submission receipt or confirmation email.** Most systems generate a "Submission Successful" page; save this as a PDF or screenshot immediately, as it is your only proof of compliance if the application is later contested.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact your Member of Parliament (MP) until the stated deadline has passed and you have received no confirmation of receipt.
- You do not need to withdraw the application while waiting for a technical support response unless specifically advised by a qualified legal professional.
- You do not need to provide "character references" or extra narrative unless they were specifically requested in the notice.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a request for more information is a routine part of the caseworking process and indicates that your application is under active consideration. Most applications proceed to a standard decision once the requested evidence is verified, provided you meet the deadline.

## Scope note
This guidance covers the immediate response to a formal request for additional evidence. It does not cover administrative reviews, appeals, or legal challenges if an application is refused.

## Important note
Immigration rules are subject to rapid change, including "Visa Brake" restrictions for specific nationalities and routes in 2026. This guide provides procedural stabilization and does not constitute legal advice. For complex cases involving potential overstaying or human rights claims, consult a regulated immigration solicitor.
