# What to do if… you get an unexpectedly low blood pressure reading and feel weak or lightheaded (UK)

## Short answer
Lie down flat on your back immediately and raise your legs above the level of your heart. Stay in this position until the lightheadedness passes and you feel steady.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to walk, climb stairs, or "push through" the dizziness.
- Do not drive any vehicle, cycle, or operate machinery until symptoms have completely resolved.
- Do not take a hot bath or shower, as heat can further lower blood pressure.
- Do not take extra doses of any medication to try and change the reading.
- Do not stand up repeatedly to re-check the blood pressure monitor.
- Do not ignore the symptoms if the monitor shows a "normal" number but you still feel faint.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe position on the floor immediately to prevent a fall.**
   - **Lie flat on your back and raise your legs** by resting them on a sofa, chair, or pile of cushions.
   - If you cannot lie down, **sit on the floor with your back against a wall** and place your head between your knees.
2. **Call for help if you are alone in the house.**
   - Shout for a family member or neighbour, or use a phone to call someone nearby and ask them to stay with you until you feel steady.
3. **Check for emergency "red flag" symptoms and call 999 if they occur.**
   - **Call 999 immediately** if you or the person experiencing the symptoms:
     - Collapse and cannot be woken up within 1 minute.
     - Have new chest pain or a very fast / irregular heartbeat.
     - Have sudden difficulty breathing or are gasping for air.
     - Show signs of a stroke (face drooping on one side, arm weakness, or slurred speech).
     - Experience a seizure (uncontrolled shaking or jerking).
4. **Remain still for at least five to ten minutes after the dizziness stops.**
   - **Do not attempt to stand up quickly.** When you do move, transition slowly from lying to sitting, then wait a minute before attempting to stand.
5. **If you are pregnant and feel faint, lie on your left side.**
   - **Do not lie flat on your back** if you are in the later stages of pregnancy; side-lying prevents the weight of the baby from pressing on main blood vessels.
6. **Re-check your blood pressure only once you are sitting comfortably and feel steady.**
   - **Ensure the cuff is fitted correctly** on a bare arm and your arm is supported at heart level. 
   - A reading is generally considered low in the UK if it is below 90/60 mmHg.
7. **Contact NHS 111 if symptoms persist or if you actually fainted.**
   - **Call 111** if you feel unwell after resting, if the cause is unknown, or if you have recently started a new medication.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific medical cause of the low reading right now.
- You do not need to adjust your long-term diet or salt intake immediately.
- You do not need to notify the DVLA about a single episode of feeling faint today, though this is required if fainting becomes a recurring medical issue.
- You do not need to book an emergency GP appointment if symptoms resolve quickly and completely with rest, though a routine review is advisable.

## Important reassurance
Feeling sudden weakness or lightheadedness is a common response to temporary factors such as dehydration, heat, standing up too quickly, or missing a meal. Most people find that lying down with their legs raised resolves the immediate feeling of faintness within a few minutes.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute episode of low blood pressure symptoms. It does not replace a clinical diagnosis or a review of long-term health conditions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute medical advice. If you have fainted, have heart palpitations, or have symptoms that do not improve with rest, seek professional medical assessment via 111 or your GP.
