# What to do if… your mobility scooter stops working while you are out and walking the distance isn’t realistic (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself and the scooter to a safe, off-road position immediately and call Motability Assist (0800 953 5000) or your specialist breakdown provider rather than attempting to push the vehicle a long distance or troubleshooting in a hazardous area.

## Do not do these things
- Don't remain in the road or at a junction while trying to restart the scooter.
- Don't repeatedly cycle the power if you smell burning, see smoke, or feel the battery area getting hot.
- Don't attempt to push the scooter long distances on narrow or uneven pavements as this can lead to falls or collisions.
- Don't leave the scooter in a position where it obstructs other pedestrians or emergency access points.
- Don't accept a lift from a stranger who cannot safely accommodate both you and your mobility device.
- Don't worry about "bothering" businesses; most are legally required to provide reasonable access and assistance.

## What to do now
1. **Move yourself and the scooter to the nearest safe pause point immediately.** **If you are in the road or at a crossing, engage the freewheel lever (usually a red or yellow handle near the rear motor) to allow the scooter to be pushed to the pavement.**
2. **Contact your primary breakdown recovery service while you are in a safe, stationary position.** 
   - If on the Motability Scheme, call Motability Assist on 0800 953 5000.
   - If you have private insurance (e.g., Fish, Mark Bates Ltd), call the emergency number on your ID card or policy.
   - If you have no cover, call a friend or family member with a vehicle capable of transporting a scooter.
3. **Use the 'Motability Recovery' or 'myRAC' app to share your exact location if you are unsure of your surroundings.** **These apps use GPS coordinates to help recovery vehicles find you on footpaths or in parks where street addresses may not be visible.**
4. **Identify your location using nearby landmarks or a smartphone map and share this with your contact.** **Look for shop names, house numbers, or bus stop codes (usually a two-letter/number code on the sign) to give the operator a precise arrival point.**
5. **Request practical assistance from a nearby shop, café, or public building.** **Ask if you can wait inside their premises for warmth and safety while the recovery vehicle is en route.**
   - Request help confirming the postcode of your current location.
   - Ask to use their charging facilities if your mobile phone battery is low.
6. **Book a Wheelchair Accessible Taxi (WAV) if you do not have breakdown recovery and need to get home.** **When booking, explicitly state that you have a mobility scooter and provide its approximate dimensions (Length/Width/Weight).**
   - Note: In the UK, taxi and private hire drivers cannot legally charge extra for carrying your scooter or helping you board.
7. **Call 999 if you are in immediate physical danger or feel medically unwell.** **If you are stranded in a high-risk area and cannot reach a safe place, or if you feel vulnerable and have no other support, call 101 to request police advice/assistance.**

## What can wait
- You don't need to determine why the scooter failed right now (e.g., battery health vs. motor fault).
- You don't need to contact the manufacturer or retailer for a permanent repair today.
- You don't need to worry about the cost of the recovery if you are on the Motability Scheme, as this is included in your lease.
- You don't need to apologise for the inconvenience caused to others while waiting in a public space.

## Important reassurance
It is a normal and expected reaction to feel vulnerable or stressed when your primary means of mobility fails in public. Prioritising your immediate safety and warmth over the "fix" is the correct and most effective choice. Recovery services and accessible transport are specifically designed to handle these exact scenarios.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a breakdown that occurs while you are away from home. It does not cover technical repairs, long-term maintenance, or the procedures for reporting faults to the DVLA for Class 3 vehicles.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you are on a busy road or "Smart Motorway," prioritize reaching an emergency refuge area or the far side of a barrier before making any calls. Always follow the instructions provided by emergency services or your breakdown provider's dispatcher.
