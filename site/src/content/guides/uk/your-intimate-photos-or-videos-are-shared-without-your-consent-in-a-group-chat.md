# What to do if… your intimate photos or videos are shared without your consent in a group chat (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate yourself and your device, report the content immediately to the platform to trigger the 48-hour takedown rule, and use the Revenge Porn Helpline (18+) or Report Remove (under 18) for specialist removal assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not engage in arguments or try to shame the person who shared the content within the group.
- Do not forward the images or videos to anyone else, even to friends or "as proof" of what is happening.
- Do not comply with demands for money, more images, or silence if the sharer is attempting to blackmail you.
- Do not keep the group chat open to monitor reactions; this increases cognitive load and distress.
- Do not assume that content shared in encrypted apps (like WhatsApp) cannot be removed or suppressed via platform reporting.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a private, secure location and stay off the group chat.** If you are in immediate physical danger or being threatened with physical harm, call **999**.
2. **Post one clear deletion request in the group and then stop interacting.** Type: "This content is private and shared without my consent. Delete it immediately and do not forward it." 
   - If there is a group admin, **message them privately** and ask them to remove the content and the sender from the group.
3. **Report the content using the platform's internal reporting tools immediately.** Select the option for "non-consensual intimate imagery" or "harassment."
   - Under 2026 UK regulations, major platforms are required to act on these reports within **48 hours**.
4. **Collect basic evidence without saving the intimate media itself.** Note the **group name**, **platform**, **username or phone number of the sharer**, and the **time/date** of the post. 
   - If taking screenshots, capture the **chat headers and usernames** rather than the explicit content. 
5. **Use StopNCII.org to prevent further uploads (for those 18+).** This tool creates a "digital fingerprint" (hash) of the image on your device so it can be blocked by participating platforms (Meta, TikTok, etc.) without the image ever leaving your control.
6. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for expert removal support (for those 18+).** Call **0345 6000 459** (10:00–16:00, Mon–Fri) or use their 24/7 chatbot for guidance on forcing platform takedowns and legal rights.
7. **Use Childline’s "Report Remove" tool if you or the images are of someone under 18.** This service, run with the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF), helps remove sexual images from the internet. Proving your age with ID is **optional**; you can still report without it.
8. **Report the incident to the police if you feel safe doing so.** Call **101** or report online to your local force. Intimate image abuse (IIA) and "cyberflashing" are priority offences in the UK.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide right now whether you want to pursue a full criminal prosecution.
- You do **not** need to explain yourself to the other members of the group chat.
- You do **not** need to find every single person who might have seen the content before seeking help.
- You do **not** need to worry about the 48-hour takedown window expiring; platforms have an ongoing duty to remove once notified.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of intense panic, exposure, or violation. This situation is the result of someone else’s illegal or harmful actions, not your own. Specialist UK services handle thousands of these cases and can help you navigate the removal process systematically.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and containment actions for UK residents. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, civil litigation for damages, or therapeutic support for trauma.

## Important note
This information is based on UK law and the 2026 Online Safety Act framework. It is not legal or clinical advice. If you are under 18, do not share or forward the content to anyone; use the official "Report Remove" channels. For immediate emotional crisis support, contact **Samaritans on 116 123**.
