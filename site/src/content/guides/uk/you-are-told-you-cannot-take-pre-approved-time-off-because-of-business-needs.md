# What to do if… you are told you cannot take pre-approved time off because of business needs (UK)

## Short answer
Request the extension details in writing immediately, specifically the new end date, the performance criteria required to pass, and the support provided. Check your contract to see if your employer has the specific right to extend the period.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign immediately or verbally threaten to quit during the meeting.
- Do not sign an extension agreement on the spot if it changes your pay, notice period, or core duties without a review.
- Do not accept vague reasons like "not quite there yet" without asking for measurable targets.
- Do not assume that an extension pauses your statutory rights; rights to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) and Paternity Leave are now "day-one" rights regardless of probation status.
- Do not ignore the 6-month threshold; if your extension takes you past 6 months of service, you likely gain ordinary unfair dismissal protection under the 2026 rules.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for the extension to be confirmed in a formal letter or email within 24 hours.**
   The written confirmation must include:
   - The exact new end date of the probation.
   - The specific reasons for the extension (e.g., specific skills or behaviors).
   - The frequency of scheduled review meetings during the extension.
   - Confirmation of whether any benefits or notice periods change during this time.

2. **Verify the "Extension Clause" in your original employment contract or handbook.**
   **Check if the contract explicitly allows the employer to extend the probation period.**
   - If there is no clause allowing an extension, the employer may be attempting a "unilateral variation of contract."
   - If you do not agree with the extension but wish to keep your job, you may need to state in writing that you are "working under protest" while seeking advice.

3. **Request a written Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or a list of 3–5 measurable objectives.**
   **Ensure targets are objective (e.g., "Complete X reports per week") rather than subjective (e.g., "Have a better attitude").**
   - Ask what specific training, mentoring, or resources will be provided to help you meet these goals.
   - Ensure these objectives are added to the diary with a mid-point check-in date.

4. **Identify if the extension is linked to a "Protected Characteristic" or health issue.**
   **If the extension is due to absence caused by disability, pregnancy, or maternity, request "Reasonable Adjustments" in writing.**
   - Under the Equality Act 2010, employers must not disadvantage you for reasons related to a disability or pregnancy.
   - Explicitly ask how the extension accounts for your health or caring responsibilities.

5. **Send a "Memorandum of Understanding" email after the meeting.**
   **Document your understanding of the conversation to create a contemporary record.**
   - Use factual language: "Following our meeting on [Date], I understand my probation is extended to [New Date] to allow me to demonstrate [Objective A] and [Objective B]."

6. **Confirm your notice period rights if the situation worsens.**
   **Note that if you have worked for more than one month, you are legally entitled to at least one week of statutory notice.**
   - Your contract may specify a longer notice period. An extension of probation does not usually reduce your statutory notice rights.

7. **Contact Acas or your Trade Union if the process feels retaliatory or discriminatory.**
   **Use the Acas helpline (0300 123 1100) for confidential guidance on contract disputes.**
   - If you are called to a formal grievance or disciplinary meeting regarding your performance, you have a statutory right to be accompanied by a colleague or union representative.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to stay with the company long-term today.
- You do not need to provide a point-by-point rebuttal of performance critiques immediately; focus on getting the "pass" criteria in writing first.
- You do not need to start a formal grievance today unless you believe the extension is a direct act of discrimination or a breach of contract.

## Important reassurance
A probation extension is often a procedural tool used by managers who are unsure how to assess a new starter, rather than a precursor to firing. By forcing the criteria into writing, you remove the ambiguity that causes the most stress and create a clear path to successful confirmation.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for UK employment law as of 2026. It does not cover specific industry-regulated probations (e.g., teachers or police) or Northern Ireland-specific industrial tribunal variations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights in 2026 are subject to specific qualifying periods under the Employment Rights Act 2025 and individual contract terms. If you face potential dismissal, consult a qualified legal professional or Citizens Advice.
