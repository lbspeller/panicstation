# What to do if… someone at your hotel or rental door demands entry and you cannot verify them (UK)

## Short answer
Keep the door locked and verify through an official channel you trust, such as reception, the hotel switchboard, or your booking app. If you feel unsafe, threatened, or someone tries to force entry, call 999 or 112.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t open the door “just for a second” while you are unsure.
- Don’t rely on caller ID, a knock, a uniform, or a badge shown briefly as proof.
- Don’t call a phone number the person outside gives you, or follow links they text you.
- Don’t step into the corridor to “sort it out” if you feel unsafe or alone.
- Don’t hand over ID, payment details, your room number, or travel plans through the door.
- Don’t ignore a building-wide fire alarm; if it is sounding, treat it as real and leave by a safe route.

## What to do now
1. **Lock down first, quietly.** Keep the door locked and bolted. Use the peephole or viewer if you have one. If you speak, do it through the closed door.

2. **Create time with one sentence.** Say: “I can’t open the door right now. Please wait while I confirm with reception or management.”

3. **Verify using a route you choose, not one provided by the person outside.**
   - **Hotel:** call reception or the switchboard using the in-room phone, a number printed on your key-card pack or room information, or a number from the hotel’s official website.
   - **Rental:** message or call the host in the booking app. Keep communication in the app if possible, and don’t switch to WhatsApp, SMS, or a new link because you are being pressured.

4. **Make the confirmation come to you without opening the door.** Ask reception, management, or the host to confirm:
   - the person’s name and role;
   - the exact reason for contact;
   - whether entry is genuinely needed now;
   - whether they can call you back on the room phone or confirm in the booking app;
   - whether a duty manager or second staff member can attend and state their names at the door.

5. **Only open if you have verified it or you must leave for safety.** If you choose to open after verification, or you need to leave because of a real safety issue, keep distance and use any chain or door limiter only briefly if it helps. Do not leave a chain or limiter on in a way that could slow your exit in a fire.

6. **If the person will not leave, keeps pressuring you, or tries the handle, call 999 or 112.** Tell the operator:
   - the hotel or rental name and address, or as much location detail as you have;
   - your room, flat, or unit number;
   - that someone is demanding entry and you cannot verify they are staff.

7. **If they claim an immediate life-safety hazard, use what you can observe.**
   - **Fire alarm sounding, smoke, or fire smell:** take your phone and key if they are beside you, put shoes on if you can do so immediately, follow fire signs to the nearest safe exit, and go to the assembly point if safe.
   - **Gas smell or possible carbon monoxide:** get to fresh air if you can. For natural gas in England, Wales and Scotland, call 0800 111 999. In Northern Ireland, call 0800 002 001. For LPG, use the emergency number on the bulk storage vessel or meter if it is safe to check. Call 999 or 112 if there is immediate danger or someone is unwell.
   - **If you cannot safely leave:** call 999 or 112 and explain what you can hear, see, or smell, including alarms, smoke, gas smell, or someone at the door.

8. **Move the interaction to a safer place if verified but you still feel uneasy.** Ask to meet staff in the lobby, at reception, or with another staff member present, rather than opening your room door to one person you do not fully trust.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether it was a scam, a misunderstanding, or a real hotel issue.
- Complaints, refunds, reviews, or debates about hotel or host policy.
- Non-urgent reporting, unless you need help now for safety.
- Packing, changing accommodation, or changing travel plans, unless staying there now feels unsafe.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to slow the situation down. Genuine staff and hosts can usually work with basic verification without you opening your door to an unconfirmed person.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk and buy time. After the situation stabilises, later decisions about complaints, refunds, reporting, or changing accommodation may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, professional security, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger or someone is trying to force entry, call 999 or 112.
