# What to do if… your voicemail PIN or greeting changes and you did not update it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the device and account immediately to prevent new data from overwriting the deleted files. Check for secondary recovery tools on the provider's web dashboard and review your account's "Recent Activity" for unauthorised sign-ins.

## Do not do these things
- Do not download large files, take new high-resolution photos, or sync new data to the affected account.
- Do not run "cleanup" or "storage optimiser" software, as these often purge remaining temporary files.
- Do not click support links in unsolicited emails or texts; only use official web addresses.
- Do not sign out of the account on your primary device until you have noted down the list of other active sessions.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the device from the network to pause syncing (1 minute).**  
   **Switch the device to Airplane Mode or turn off Wi-Fi and mobile data immediately.**  
   - This prevents the "empty" state from syncing further if other devices have not yet updated.
   - If you use a computer, disconnect the Ethernet cable or disable the network adapter.

2. **Access the account dashboard via a web browser on a different device.**  
   **Log in to the official web portal (e.g., iCloud.com, OneDrive.com, or photos.google.com) to see if the files are still visible there.**  
   - Web versions often lag behind local app syncing and may still show the items.
   - Check the "Bin" or "Trash" on the web version specifically.

3. **Check for "Second-Stage" recovery options (5 minutes).**  
   **Look for advanced recovery tools that exist outside the standard "Recently Deleted" folder.**  
   - For iCloud: Go to the "Data Recovery" section in Account Settings and select "Restore Files."
   - For OneDrive (Work/School): Scroll to the bottom of the Bin and select "Second-stage recycle bin."
   - For Google Photos: Check the "Archive" folder in case items were moved rather than deleted.

4. **Review the list of active sessions and security events.**  
   **Go to the account security settings and check "Where you're signed in" or "Recent activity."**  
   - Look for unknown device names, unfamiliar IP addresses, or locations you have not visited.
   - Note the exact time of any suspicious activity for your report.

5. **Secure the account credentials immediately.**  
   **Change your password and ensure Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is active.**  
   - If you suspect the account was compromised, choose the "Sign out of all other sessions" option after changing the password.
   - Verify that the recovery email and phone number have not been changed.

6. **Contact the service provider’s official support channel.**  
   **Open a support ticket specifically requesting "server-side data recovery" due to unexpected deletion.**  
   - State clearly that you did not initiate the "Empty Bin" action.
   - Ask if they can see a log of which device or IP address performed the deletion.

7. **Report suspected cyber crime to the appropriate UK body.**  
   **If you see evidence of unauthorised access (hacking), report the incident to the police.**  
   - England, Wales, and Northern Ireland: Use Action Fraud (Report Fraud) at `reportfraud.police.uk` or call 0300 123 2040.
   - Scotland: Call Police Scotland on 101 or contact the Cyber and Fraud Centre Scotland on 0800 167 623.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new backup strategy or hardware purchase until the current recovery window is closed.
- You do not need to perform a factory reset of your device unless support confirms it is infected with malware.
- You do not need to delete your account; securing it is sufficient for now.

## Important reassurance
It is common for "Recently Deleted" folders to appear empty due to sync delays, accidental bulk selections, or storage-management settings. Finding the folder empty is a high-stress moment, but many modern cloud services have hidden secondary bins or server-side backups that are not immediately visible in the mobile app.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital data loss and account security. It does not cover forensic data recovery from physical hardware or legal proceedings regarding data privacy.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee the recovery of any data. Success depends on specific provider retention policies and the time elapsed since deletion. Be wary of third-party "data recovery" services that ask for upfront payment or remote access to your computer; always use official provider support first.
