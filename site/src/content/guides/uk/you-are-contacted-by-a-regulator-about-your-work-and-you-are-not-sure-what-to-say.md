# What to do if… you are contacted by a regulator about your work and you are not sure what to say (UK)

## Short answer
Request immediate written confirmation from HR or your manager to clarify if you are on garden leave or suspension. Secure a statement confirming that your full pay, contractual benefits, and notice end date remain unchanged.

## Do not do these things
- Do not start a new job or engage in self-employment until your notice period has officially ended, as this is typically a breach of contract.
- Do not resign "in response" or attempt to shorten your notice period without advice, as this may forfeit certain rights.
- Do not assume the leave indicates guilt or a move toward dismissal without written evidence.
- Do not sign any new agreements, such as settlement offers or revised restrictive covenants, while in a state of shock or uncertainty.
- Do not delete, "tidy," or move any work files, emails, or data, as this can be misinterpreted as evidence tampering.
- Do not vent about the situation on work-related channels, social media, or to colleagues in writing.

## What to do now
1. **Request a formal written statement confirming your current employment status and the reason for the leave.** **Ask specifically if you are on garden leave or under precautionary suspension.**
2. **Obtain written confirmation that your salary and all contractual benefits will continue as normal.** **Verify that your pension contributions, healthcare, and any allowances remain active throughout the period.**
3. **Clarify your specific obligations and restrictions while you are away from the workplace.** **Ask if you are required to remain available for contact during working hours or if you are prohibited from contacting clients and colleagues.**
4. **Locate and review your employment contract for clauses relating to garden leave and suspension.** **Check for specific terms regarding the return of company property and any post-termination restrictions.**
5. **Secure an alternative method for receiving final documents and payroll information.** **Request that your final payslip, P45, and any holiday pay calculations be sent to a personal email address or physical residence.**
6. **Create a private, chronological record of all events leading up to and including the instruction to take leave.** **Note the names of individuals involved, the exact time access was removed, and any verbal explanations provided.**
7. **Propose an orderly handover via an approved, company-controlled channel.** **Offer to answer outstanding questions or provide status updates through a nominated manager rather than accessing systems directly.**
8. **Ask for the specific nature of the next steps if the leave is described as a suspension.** **Inquire whether an investigation is underway and if you are entitled to be accompanied at future meetings.**
9. **Contact ACAS or your trade union representative if your employer refuses to provide written clarity or if your pay is interrupted.** **Seek external guidance immediately if there is any indication that the leave is a disciplinary sanction rather than a neutral act.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal grievance or legal claim in the first few hours.
- You do not need to provide a detailed "defense" or explanation of your performance unless a formal meeting is scheduled.
- You do not need to update your professional network or LinkedIn status regarding your departure.
- You do not need to return equipment immediately unless specifically instructed on how to do so safely.

## Important reassurance
Being placed on paid leave during a notice period is a common corporate procedure used to protect business interests and does not necessarily imply personal failure or misconduct. Feeling anxious or confused is a standard response to a sudden loss of routine and system access; however, your legal status as an employee remains intact during this period.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK employment crises involving sudden leave. It focuses on securing pay and clarity. It does not cover long-term legal strategies, tribunal procedures, or the negotiation of settlement agreements.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights in the UK are subject to the specific terms of your contract and the circumstances of your departure. Under 2026 regulations, statutory sick pay and other protections may have specific application; consult with a qualified professional or ACAS for advice tailored to your situation.
