# What to do if… you notice signs your door lock has been tampered with (UK)

## Short answer
Do not go inside to check. Move to a safer nearby place and call 999 if someone may be inside, a crime may be happening now, or you feel in immediate danger; otherwise report it to the police on 101 or online.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go in “just to check quickly”, even if the door still seems locked.
- Do not keep trying the key, force the handle, or attempt a quick repair now.
- Do not call out through the door or confront anyone you cannot see.
- Do not touch the lock, handle, letterbox, or doorframe more than you already have.
- Do not start cleaning up if you have already gone inside.
- Do not post publicly that your home may be empty or unsecured.

## What to do now
1. **Step back from the door.** Move away from the threshold. From a safe distance, notice only obvious things such as an open window, moved curtain, damaged frame, unfamiliar marks, changed lights, or sounds from inside.
2. **Move somewhere safer to make calls.** Go to a neighbour’s home, a nearby shop, a café, or your locked car rather than staying by the door.
3. **Contact the police using the safest route.**
   - **Call 999** if someone may be inside, you can see an open entry point, you heard something, a crime may be happening now, or you feel in immediate danger.
   - If there is clearly no immediate danger, **call 101** or use the police online reporting route.
   Tell them your address, what looks different about the lock or door, when you last left, and whether anyone should be inside.
4. **Wait in a safer nearby place until police advise what to do.** Do not re-enter just because the door is closed or quiet.
5. **Preserve what you can without handling it.** If it is safe and you are not delaying urgent help, take a few photos from a distance of the lock, doorframe, nearby windows, and any obvious marks. Then stop and avoid touching the area.
6. **If you rent or live in managed housing, contact the landlord, housing provider, concierge, or managing agent after contacting police.** Ask how emergency lock repairs will be handled and who is authorised to attend.
7. **If you already went inside and now feel unsure, leave.** Get to a safer place and call 999 if there is any possibility someone could still be there.
8. **Ask what reference number to keep.** When you speak to police, ask whether there is an incident number or crime reference number you should record for later use.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to change locks, upgrade security, or buy alarms or cameras.
- You do not need to do a full inventory of anything missing until you are safe and police have advised what to do.
- You do not need to book a locksmith before the immediate safety question is dealt with, unless police, your landlord, or your housing provider tells you to.
- Insurance calls, repair quotes, and wider neighbour updates can wait until you are safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat possible lock tampering as a safety issue first. There may be an innocent explanation, but staying out, getting to a safer place, and letting police advise you is a sensible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the minutes and hours after noticing possible door lock tampering. Later decisions about repairs, insurance, housing responsibilities, and longer-term security may need specialist help.
