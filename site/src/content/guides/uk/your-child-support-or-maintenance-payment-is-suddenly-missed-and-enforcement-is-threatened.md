# What to do if… your child support or maintenance payment is suddenly missed and enforcement is threatened (UK)

## Short answer
Identify if you are on Direct Pay or Collect and Pay, then immediately report the miss or make the payment via the official Child Maintenance Service (CMS) portal to stop the new 6-week Administrative Liability Order process.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore a notice titled **Administrative Liability Order** or any SMS/portal alert from the CMS.
- Do not pay the other parent via cash, vouchers, or cryptocurrency as these are untraceable and often ignored by the CMS in arrears disputes.
- Do not follow payment links sent via text or WhatsApp that do not lead to the official .gov.uk portal.
- Do not attempt to negotiate the legal validity of the debt with an enforcement agent (bailiff) if they have already arrived at your door.
- Do not assume a 22-week court delay exists; the CMS can now fast-track enforcement to 6–8 weeks without a court hearing.

## What to do now
1. **Locate your 12-digit customer reference number and identify your arrangement type.** 
   - **CMS Direct Pay:** The CMS calculates the amount, but you pay each other.
   - **CMS Collect and Pay:** The CMS collects the money and passes it on (involves 20% paying-parent and 4% receiving-parent fees).
   - **Private / Family-Based:** No official CMS involvement; these are not legally binding.
   - **Court Order:** Set by a judge (often a "Consent Order" during divorce).

2. **Verify the status of the payment through your official bank records or the CMS online portal.** 
   - Check for "insufficient funds," "payment returned," or "standing order cancelled" notifications.
   - For receiving parents: check all accounts including those with non-geographic sort codes used for privacy.

3. **Report the miss to the CMS immediately if you are the receiving parent on Direct Pay.** 
   - **Sign in to your Child Maintenance Service account** and select the "missed payment" option.
   - **Request a move to Collect and Pay** if you want the CMS to take over collection and enforcement (be aware this triggers fees for both parties).
   - Understand that the CMS cannot take enforcement action while a case remains on the Direct Pay system.

4. **Make the full payment today through the official CMS "Pay Now" service if you are the paying parent.** 
   - Use the **online portal** or call the automated payment line on 0800 171 2345.
   - Keep a digital copy of the receipt or a screenshot of the "Payment Successful" screen.
   - If you live in Northern Ireland, contact the Child Maintenance Service NI on 0800 232 1956.

5. **Submit a "Change of Circumstances" form online if you cannot pay due to a drop in income.** 
   - **Do not simply stop paying** or pay a lower amount without CMS authorization.
   - Providing proof of a 25% or more change in income can trigger a recalculation and pause enforcement.
   - Request an "Arrears Installment Plan" if you have already built up debt.

6. **Check for any notices of an Administrative Liability Order (ALO) in your portal.** 
   - This notice means the CMS is skipping the court process to seize funds directly.
   - **Contact a free debt advice service** (like StepChange or Citizens Advice) the same day you see this notice to understand your appeal rights.

7. **Compile a "Proof of Payment" folder for the last 6 months.** 
   - Gather bank statements showing the exact amount leaving/entering your account.
   - Match these against the CMS "Payment Plan" schedule provided in your last annual review.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle wider disputes about child contact or custody to fix a maintenance payment miss.
- You do not need to respond to unofficial threats from the other parent's solicitor if a CMS case is already active; the CMS takes precedence.
- Long-term appeals against the original calculation can wait until the immediate threat of asset seizure is stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Sudden missed payments are often caused by administrative errors or technical bank failures. The system is designed to allow for "reasonable excuses" if you communicate the same day the error occurs. Prompt reporting or payment usually resets the enforcement clock and prevents the use of bailiffs or deduction-from-earnings orders.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for missed statutory maintenance (CMS) and court-ordered payments in the UK. It does not cover international "REMO" cases, complex asset-tracing for self-employed parents, or long-term legal appeals against paternity.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. CMS rules in Great Britain (England, Scotland, Wales) differ slightly from Northern Ireland (managed by the Department for Communities). Enforcement powers including driving license removal and prison sentences are real and can be triggered rapidly under 2026 administrative rules.
