# What to do if… you receive a notice that your visa application needs more documents by a short deadline (UK)

## Short answer
Save the notice, copy the exact deadline and submission route, then send the requested documents through that route as soon as you can. Keep proof of what you submitted and when.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess what they want or upload unrelated extra documents.
- Do not use a different route because it feels quicker; it may not be matched to your application in time.
- Do not send original documents unless the notice specifically asks for them.
- Do not crop scans or photos so that page edges, stamps, reference numbers, barcodes, or signatures are cut off.
- Do not upload conflicting versions of the same document without a short factual note saying which one is correct.
- Do not withdraw and reapply in panic unless you have checked that this is really required.

## What to do now
1. **Capture the request.** Save the notice as a PDF or screenshot. Write down the deadline, any time zone shown, your application or reference number, the exact documents requested, and the exact submission method.

2. **Use only the route named in the notice.** If you applied inside the UK, you may be told to use UKVCAS, your online account, or another named route. If you applied outside the UK, you may be told to use a visa application centre platform, a commercial partner platform, your online account, or a reply route. Follow the notice over any general advice.

3. **Turn the wording into a checklist.** Copy each requested item exactly. Mark each one as ready now, obtainable today, or dependent on someone else, such as a bank, employer, university, registry office, translator, or sponsor.

4. **Prepare the documents that are ready.** Use clear scans or photos where the full document is visible. Include all pages, and include the back of a document if it has stamps, marks, conditions, or reference details. Use plain descriptive filenames, such as “bank-statement-april-2026.pdf”.

5. **Attach the ready documents before final submission.** Upload or attach every requested item you already have through the stated route. Check that each file appears as uploaded or attached before you submit.

6. **If a requested item cannot be obtained in time, say that briefly if the route allows.** Add a short factual note with the missing item named, what you have done to obtain it, proof such as a receipt or confirmation email, and the earliest realistic date you expect it. Do not assume this extends the deadline or that late evidence will be accepted.

7. **If translation is the problem, do not make up your own certified translation.** If the document is not in English or Welsh and a certified translation is required, upload what the notice allows, such as the original document, proof that a translation has been requested, and a brief note through the stated route. Keep the certified translation ready to send if they ask for it.

8. **If the upload route is not working, collect proof and use official help promptly.** Take screenshots showing the error and time, try one other browser or device once, then use the help or support route linked from the same platform or GOV.UK. Keep any ticket number or confirmation.

9. **If the request is unclear, ask one precise question.** Use the stated route, include your reference number, quote the unclear item, and ask the narrow question you need answered. Avoid adding a long explanation.

10. **Save a receipt trail.** Keep the notice, every file you submitted, upload confirmations, sent emails, tracking numbers, help tickets, and screenshots in one folder named with the date and time.

## What can wait
- You do not need to rewrite your whole application today.
- You do not need to decide travel changes until after you have submitted what you can and saved proof.
- You do not need to chase processing times before dealing with the document request.
- You do not need to seek qualified immigration advice immediately unless you cannot understand the request, your lawful status may be affected, or the notice warns of refusal or another serious consequence.

## Important reassurance
A short deadline can feel alarming, but the next task is narrow: identify exactly what was requested, use the stated route, and keep a clear record. A calm, complete submission trail is more useful than a rushed pile of unrelated documents.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for responding to a short-deadline document request. Later decisions about follow-up, complaints, travel plans, advice, reapplying, or challenging a decision may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. Visa processes vary by route and personal circumstances, and a late, incomplete, or misrouted response may affect your application.
