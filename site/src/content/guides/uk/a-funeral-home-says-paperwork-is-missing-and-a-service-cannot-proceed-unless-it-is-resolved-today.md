# What to do if… a funeral home says paperwork is missing and a service cannot proceed unless it is resolved today (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the funeral director to name the exact missing form (e.g., Green Form, Form 14, or Coroner's Order), then contact the issuing Medical Examiner Officer or Registrar immediately to confirm if it was sent electronically.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to pay additional "emergency" or "cancellation" fees until you have verified exactly which agency is responsible for the delay.
- Do not assume you have lost the paperwork; most critical funeral documents (MCCD and Green Forms) are now transferred electronically between doctors, medical examiners, and registrars.
- Do not attempt to visit a GP surgery or hospital in person without calling first, as the relevant Medical Examiner Officer (MEO) may be based at a different site.
- Do not cancel the gathering for guests if the committal is blocked; you can often proceed with a memorial service or "tribute" today and move the physical burial or cremation to a later date.
- Do not accept "we haven't received it" as a final answer from the funeral home without asking them to check their digital "spam" or "junk" folders and verifying the recipient email address.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific name of the missing document and its intended source.**  
   Ask the funeral director to state clearly if they are missing the "Green Form" (England/Wales), "Form 14" (Scotland), a Coroner’s Order (Form 101 or Form 6), or the application for cremation (Form 1).
   - Ask for the exact email address or portal where they expect the document to arrive.
   - Confirm if the issue is a "missing" form or a "mismatch" in data (such as a misspelt name or incorrect date).

2. **Contact the Medical Examiner Officer (MEO) if the death occurred in England or Wales.**  
   **Since late 2024, the MEO must scrutinise all non-coroner deaths and send the Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD) directly to the registrar.**
   - Call the hospital bereavement office or the regional Medical Examiner office.
   - Ask: "Has the MCCD been electronically forwarded to the Register Office yet?"
   - If it has not, ask for the estimated time for completion and if there is a specific "query" holding it up that you can resolve.

3. **Call the Register Office if the death has already been scrutinised by a doctor.**  
   **Ask the registrar to confirm if the "Certificate for Burial or Cremation" (the Green Form) or "Form 14" has been issued.**
   - If they have issued it, ask for the exact time it was sent and the destination email address used.
   - If they have not issued it, ask if they are waiting for you to complete a "telephone registration" or attend an appointment.
   - In urgent faith-based cases (e.g., immediate burial), ask if the registrar can issue the Green Form before full registration is complete.

4. **Verify the status of the Coroner's release if an investigation is active.**  
   **Contact the Coroner’s Officer directly to ask if the "Order for Burial" (Form 101) or "Certificate for Cremation" (Form 6 / Form E) has been sent to the funeral director.**
   - Confirm that the Coroner has "released" the body for the funeral.
   - If the paperwork is signed but not received, ask the Coroner's Officer to re-send it to a specific, verified email address at the funeral home.

5. **Request a "Service of Thanksgiving" or "Memorial" if the committal cannot happen.**  
   **Ask the venue (Crematorium/Cemetery) and the officiant if they can host the service today even without the physical coffin or the final committal.**
   - This allows families who have travelled to pay their respects while the administrative delay is resolved.
   - Ask the funeral director to store the deceased in their mortuary until the new committal slot is confirmed.

6. **Create a chronological log of all communications including names and direct dial numbers.**  
   **Record the time of every call to the MEO, Registrar, and Coroner's office.**
   - Document exactly who said they sent the form and when.
   - This log is essential if you later need to dispute cancellation fees or professional negligence by the funeral provider.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve disputes over who is "at fault" during the hours leading up to the service; focus entirely on locating the single missing form.
- You do not need to pay for the "Death Certificate" (certified copies) immediately; these are for probate and bank use, whereas the "Green Form" or "Form 14" is what unblocks the funeral.
- You do not need to finalize the "Tell Us Once" service today; this can be done anytime within the statutory registration window.

## Important reassurance
Paperwork bottlenecks are a known issue in the UK funeral industry, particularly following the 2024 death certification reforms. This delay is usually an administrative error between offices rather than a personal failure or a sign of an underlying problem with the death itself. It is often resolvable with a few direct phone calls between the issuing officer and the funeral home.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for immediate paperwork crises on the day of a funeral in the UK. It does not cover legal challenges to coroner decisions or long-term probate issues.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and reflects UK procedures as of 2026. Rules differ between England/Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. Always confirm the specific requirements of the crematorium or cemetery authority involved, as they may have local cut-off times for document submission.
