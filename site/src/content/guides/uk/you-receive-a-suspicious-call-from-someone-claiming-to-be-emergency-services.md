# What to do if… you receive a suspicious call from someone claiming to be emergency services (UK)

## Short answer
End the call, then contact emergency services or the police yourself using a trusted route. Call 999 if someone is in immediate danger or a crime is happening now; otherwise call 101.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay on the line to be polite if the call feels wrong.
- Don’t confirm your address, date of birth, passwords, PINs, one-time codes, or bank details.
- Don’t call back using a number the caller gives you or the number shown on caller ID.
- Don’t transfer money, withdraw cash, buy high-value items, or hand anything to a courier.
- Don’t download apps, install remote-access software, or follow technical instructions from the caller.
- Don’t keep the caller on while you check something; end the call first and verify separately.
- Don’t let the caller’s urgency stop you from using 999 if someone is at immediate risk.

## What to do now
1. **End the call.** You can say, “I’m going to hang up and call back through an official route.” Then hang up.
2. **If anyone is in immediate danger or a crime is happening now, call 999.** Dial the number yourself, not from a link, message, redial, or callback prompt. Tell the call handler you received a suspicious call claiming to be emergency services.
3. **If it is not an emergency, call 101.** Say that you had a suspicious call claiming to be emergency services or police, and ask how to verify whether any genuine contact was being made.
4. **If you are using a landline, use another phone if you can.** If you cannot, wait at least five minutes before calling back, because criminals may try to keep a landline open after you hang up.
5. **If the caller mentioned money, bank accounts, safe accounts, evidence, couriers, cards, or PINs, contact your bank separately.** Use the number on your card, in your banking app, or dial 159 if your bank and phone provider take part.
6. **Write down the details while they are fresh.** Note the time and date, what the caller claimed, what they asked you to confirm, any reference number, and the number shown on caller ID.
7. **Report the suspicious call when the immediate risk is under control.** In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, use Report Fraud. In Scotland, contact Police Scotland by calling 101.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the call was definitely a scam.
- You do not need to confront the caller, call them back, or prove anything to them.
- Blocking numbers, changing passwords, checking accounts, and wider clean-up can wait until you have verified any immediate safety or money risk.

## Important reassurance
Scam calls often copy official language, numbers, and urgency to make you act quickly. Hanging up and verifying through 999, 101, your bank, or another trusted route is a reasonable and responsible response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce immediate risk and prevent fast-moving loss. Later decisions may need help from your bank, police, Report Fraud, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, technical, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel threatened or someone is at immediate risk, call 999.
