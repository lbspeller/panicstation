# What to do if… your sunroof is stuck open and rain is forecast (UK)

## Short answer
Move the car under cover immediately and secure a waterproof sheet over the opening using the car doors or windows to clamp it in place, ensuring no obstruction to the driver's view.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t drive if any part of your temporary cover blocks the windscreen or front side windows; this carries a fine of up to £1,000 (£2,500 for commercial vehicles) and 3 penalty points.
- Don’t force the glass or the mechanism by hand if it feels jammed; this can snap the plastic guide clips or burn out the motor.
- Don’t use standard duct tape or packing tape directly on the car’s paintwork if avoidable, as it may cause permanent finish damage or leave hard-to-remove residue.
- Don’t leave the car unattended in an unsecure area while the roof is open.
- Don’t spray WD-40 or similar oils into the tracks; these attract dirt and can worsen the jam.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to overhead shelter.** Seek a garage, carport, multi-storey car park, or even a petrol station forecourt (if safe and permitted) to stop immediate water ingress.
2. **Check the tracks for physical obstructions.** 
   - Look for leaves, twigs, or debris in the metal channels. 
   - Carefully remove any visible blockages that might be triggering the "anti-pinch" safety sensor.
3. **Attempt a system reset if the motor is still making noise.** 
   - Turn the ignition to the 'On' position. 
   - Press and hold the 'Close' button or turn the dial to 'Close' and hold it firmly for 15–20 seconds without letting go. 
   - If the roof moves slightly and stops, repeat the hold.
4. **Deploy a waterproof barrier using the "door-clamp" method.** 
   - Place a heavy plastic sheet or tarp over the roof. 
   - Open the passenger and driver doors, pull the edges of the sheet inside the cabin, and then close the doors to clamp the sheet tightly. 
   - This secures the cover without needing tape on the paintwork.
5. **Clear the glass of any obstructions before moving.** 
   - **Ensure the windscreen and front side windows are completely clear.** 
   - If the sheet flaps or hangs down over the glass, secure it with bungee cords or string passed through the cabin, keeping it taut.
6. **Protect the interior electronics.** 
   - Place dry towels over the centre console, gear stick, and any visible screens. 
   - Move all valuables, electronics, and paper documents to the boot or a dry area of the home.
7. **Contact a breakdown provider or mobile mechanic.** 
   - Specifically state: "Sunroof stuck open with rain forecast and risk of interior water damage." 
   - Ask if they can perform a "manual override" or "emergency closure."

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure (motor, switch, or cable) right now.
- You do not need to deep-clean or shampoo the carpets until the car is fully sealed and the rain has passed.
- You do not need to contact your insurance provider until you have assessed if any electrical components have actually failed.

## Important reassurance
Sunroof failures are a common occurrence in the UK due to grit and debris in the tracks. By getting the car under cover and using a clamped sheet, you have already prevented the vast majority of potential electronic damage.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and temporary weatherproofing only. Modern panoramic roofs often require specialized tools or software resets that must be handled by a qualified technician.

## Important note
Driving with an obstructed view is an offence under the Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations. If you cannot secure the roof cover without blocking your vision, do not drive the vehicle on public roads; arrange for a recovery truck instead.
