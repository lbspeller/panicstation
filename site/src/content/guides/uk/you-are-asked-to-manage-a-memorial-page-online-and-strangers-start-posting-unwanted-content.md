# What to do if… you are asked to manage a memorial page online and strangers start posting unwanted content (UK)

## Short answer
Restrict the page's privacy settings immediately to prevent new posts, secure the administrator account with a new password, and use platform reporting tools to flag illegal or abusive content.

## Do not do these things
- Do not engage in public arguments with individuals posting unwanted content.
- Do not provide personal information, such as home addresses or private phone numbers, to verify facts or "set the record straight."
- Do not click links in comments or private messages, especially those claiming to be for fundraisers or support services.
- Do not grant administrator or moderator access to any individual you do not personally know and trust.
- Do not spend extended time reviewing offensive material once a record has been made for reporting purposes.

## What to do now
1. **Restrict all posting and commenting permissions immediately.**
   - Access the page or profile settings and change the posting privacy to "Only Admins" or "Private."
   - Disable the ability for others to tag the page in external posts if the platform allows.
   - Turn on any available "Post Review" or "Comment Moderation" filters so new content cannot appear without your approval.
2. **Secure the primary administrator account.**
   - Change the password to a unique, complex phrase not used on other sites.
   - Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) if it is not already active.
   - Review the "Logged In Devices" list and sign out any sessions you do not recognize.
3. **Report content using the platform-native tools.**
   - Prioritize reporting content that involves threats of violence, doxxing, hate speech, sexual harassment, or impersonation.
   - Select the most accurate reporting category (e.g., "Harassment" or "Fraud") to ensure the platform’s safety team reviews it under the correct priority.
   - Block the accounts responsible for the unwanted content immediately after reporting.
4. **Utilize UK-specific escalation services for harmful content.**
   - If the platform fails to remove content that violates their terms, use the **Report Harmful Content** service for independent advice and escalation.
   - Note that under the Online Safety Act 2023, platforms have a duty to remove illegal content once they are made aware of it.
5. **Preserve a record of criminal or threatening material.**
   - Capture clear screenshots that include the username, the date/time of the post, and the content itself.
   - Save the direct URL (link) to specific problematic posts or comments.
   - Do not delete the material until after it has been screenshotted if you intend to involve the police.
6. **Report financial scams or fraud to the national service.**
   - If strangers are posting fake charity links or attempting to collect money, report this to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) via their online portal or by calling 0300 123 2040.
   - If money has already been lost, contact your bank immediately using the number on the back of your card.
7. **Contact the police if there is a threat to safety.**
   - Call **999** if there is an immediate threat to life or a crime is currently in progress.
   - Use the national police online reporting tool or call **101** for persistent harassment that does not pose an immediate physical danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the long-term status of the page, such as whether to delete it or keep it permanent.
- You do not need to identify the real-world identity of anonymous accounts.
- You do not need to explain the situation to every person who comments or asks questions.
- You do not need to clean up older, non-problematic posts while the situation is active.

## Important reassurance
Sudden influxes of unwanted content on memorial pages are a known phenomenon and do not reflect on the character of the deceased or your management of the page. Taking swift action to restrict access is a standard safety measure used to protect a digital legacy.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for managing online disruption. It does not cover long-term legal action for defamation or complex estate disputes regarding digital assets.

## Important note
This information is for guidance purposes and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you receive credible threats of physical harm, prioritize your immediate safety and contact emergency services.
