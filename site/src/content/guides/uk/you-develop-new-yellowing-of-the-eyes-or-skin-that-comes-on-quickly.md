# What to do if… you develop new yellowing of the eyes or skin that comes on quickly (UK)

## Short answer
New yellowing of the eyes or skin (jaundice) is a medical urgency that requires **same-day assessment** via an **urgent GP appointment** or **NHS 111**. If the yellowing follows a suspected paracetamol overdose or is accompanied by confusion or severe pain, call **999** or go to **A&E** immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for the yellowing to disappear or "sleep it off" to see if it improves tomorrow.
- Do not consume any alcohol, as this significantly increases the strain on the liver during an acute crisis.
- Do not take any further paracetamol or products containing paracetamol, such as multi-symptom cold and flu remedies.
- Do not drive yourself to a medical facility if you feel confused, dizzy, drowsy, or are in severe pain.
- Do not ignore the symptom because you "feel fine" otherwise; jaundice can precede rapid clinical deterioration.

## What to do now
1. **Check for emergency symptoms that require an immediate 999 call or A&E visit:**
   - you have yellowing and believe you may have taken too much paracetamol (even if you feel well)
   - new confusion, unusual sleepiness, or difficulty staying awake
   - severe pain in the upper right side of the abdomen (under the ribs)
   - vomiting blood or passing black, tarry, or bloody stools
   - high fever with shivering or shaking (rigors)
2. **Contact urgent care for a same-day assessment if no emergency symptoms are present:**
   - **Call your GP surgery** and request an "urgent same-day appointment" specifically for "new-onset jaundice."
   - **Call NHS 111** (or use 111 online) if your GP surgery is closed. State clearly: *"I have developed new yellowing of my skin and eyes today."*
3. **Inspect specific areas if the yellowing is difficult to see on the skin:**
   - **Check the whites of the eyes** in bright, natural light.
   - **Check the palms of the hands and soles of the feet**, where yellowing may be more apparent on darker skin tones.
   - **Check the inside of the mouth** (gums or roof of the mouth) for a yellowish tint.
4. **Prepare a summary for the medical team while waiting for your appointment:**
   - **List all medications** taken in the last 48 hours, including herbal supplements, vitamins, and over-the-counter painkillers.
   - **Note any changes to your waste**, specifically if your urine has become very dark (like tea or cola) or your stools have become very pale (like clay or putty).
   - **Record the exact time** you first noticed the yellowing and any accompanying itchiness or nausea.
5. **If you are currently pregnant:**
   - **Contact your maternity unit or obstetric triage line immediately** rather than a general GP. Jaundice in pregnancy is always treated as an urgent obstetric priority.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the specific cause (such as hepatitis, gallstones, or liver injury) before seeking help.
- You do not need to perform any "liver flushes," fasts, or dietary changes right now.
- You do not need to contact your workplace or insurance regarding long-term leave until the initial assessment is complete.

## Important reassurance
Jaundice is a visible signal from the body that the liver or bile ducts need immediate attention. While the change can be startling, seeking medical help today ensures that the underlying cause is identified before it can cause further complications. Many causes of sudden yellowing are highly treatable when caught early.

## Scope note
This guide is for the first-step stabilization and triage of new-onset jaundice in adults. It does not replace the need for clinical examination, blood tests (liver function tests), or diagnostic imaging.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute a medical diagnosis. If you feel your condition is rapidly worsening or you are becoming less alert, treat it as a life-threatening emergency and call 999.
