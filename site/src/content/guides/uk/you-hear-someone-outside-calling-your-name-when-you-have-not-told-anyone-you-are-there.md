# What to do if… you hear someone outside calling your name when you have not told anyone you are there (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside, secure all entry points, and do not acknowledge the person or confirm your identity. If you feel threatened or suspect an attempted break-in, call 999 immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door or window to see who is there or to ask them to leave.
- Do not shout back or provide any information, even to say you are alone or busy.
- Do not switch on external lights or move curtains, which signals your exact location inside.
- Do not step outside to investigate if the voice stops or moves away.
- Do not assume a person is legitimate because they know your name; names can be obtained from discarded mail, electoral rolls, or delivery labels.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate environment without making noise.** Move to a room with a lockable door, preferably away from the front of the property.
   - Lock all doors and close/latch windows.
   - Silence your mobile phone but keep it in your hand.
2. **Observe using technology or existing gaps only.** If you have a doorbell camera or CCTV, check the feed on your phone.
   - If you do not have a camera, do not approach the door to look through a peephole if it requires you to be visible to the person outside.
3. **If you must speak, do so from behind a solid, locked barrier.** If the person is persistent, state clearly: "I cannot come to the door; please leave your details in the letterbox."
   - Do not use your name.
   - Do not open the door even if they claim to be an official (police/utility) unless you have verified them through an official number you have looked up independently.
4. **Use the 'Silent Solution' if you are in danger and cannot speak.** If you call 999 from a mobile and it is unsafe to talk:
   - **Wait for the automated system to prompt you.**
   - **Press 55 when prompted** to let the operator know it is a genuine emergency; this will transfer you to the police.
   - If calling from a landline, the operator will listen for background noise and may connect you automatically if they suspect a problem.
5. **Report suspicious behavior that does not feel like an immediate threat.** If the person leaves but the interaction was intimidating or targeted, call 101 or report it via your local police force's website.
   - Provide the specific time, the exact words used, and any description of the voice (accent, tone, estimated age).
6. **Notify a trusted contact.** Text or call a neighbor or friend to let them know someone is outside; ask them to keep a lookout from their own property without approaching the person.

## What can wait
- You do not need to figure out how they got your name right now.
- You do not need to confront the person to "set them straight" or find out what they want.
- You do not need to check your mailboxes or bins for missing items until you are certain the area is clear.
- You do not need to decide on permanent security upgrades (like smart doorbells or motion lighting) until the immediate situation is resolved.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a "jolt" or high anxiety when an unexpected person uses your name. This is a survival instinct. Staying behind a locked door and refusing to engage is the most effective way to maintain control of the situation. You are not "overreacting" by prioritizing your safety.

## Scope note
This guide is for immediate stabilization when a voice outside is causing alarm. It does not cover long-term stalking, harassment cases, or legal disputes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional police advice. In any situation where you believe a crime is being committed or you are at risk of physical harm, call 999.
