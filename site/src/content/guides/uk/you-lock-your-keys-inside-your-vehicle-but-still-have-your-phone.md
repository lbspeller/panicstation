# What to do if… you lock your keys inside your vehicle but still have your phone (UK)

## Short answer
Move yourself to a safer place first, then use your phone to contact your breakdown provider or a reputable automotive locksmith. Do not try to force the vehicle open unless emergency services tell you to.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t try DIY opening tricks, wedges, coat hangers, fishing tools, or smashing glass for a routine lockout.
- Don’t delay calling 999 if a child, vulnerable adult, or animal appears trapped or in immediate danger inside the vehicle.
- Don’t hand your phone, wallet, payment card, or vehicle documents to a stranger.
- Don’t pay up front by bank transfer to an unknown number or follow payment links from random texts.
- Don’t leave the vehicle unattended in an unsafe place while you wander around looking for help.
- Don’t call 999 for a routine lockout. Call 999 only for immediate danger. If you need police help and it is not an emergency, call 101.

## What to do now
1. **Get yourself out of traffic and into a safer pause.** Stand away from moving vehicles, use a well-lit public place if it is dark, and keep your phone with you.
2. **Check whether anyone or any animal is inside.** If a child, vulnerable adult, or animal appears trapped, overheating, unwell, or in immediate danger, call 999 now and follow the operator’s instructions.
3. **Keep your phone usable.** Turn on low-power mode if the battery is low, share your location with someone you trust if that helps, and avoid using the battery on repeated searches.
4. **Do one calm check without forcing anything.** Walk around once and check whether any door, the boot, or a hatch is actually unlocked. Stop if anything needs force.
5. **Try a connected-car option only if it is already set up.** If your vehicle app can unlock the doors remotely and it is already working on your phone, try that from a safe place.
6. **Check for a spare key if it is realistic.** If you are near home or someone trusted can safely bring a spare key, ask them before paying for a call-out.
7. **Call your breakdown provider if you have cover.** Give your exact location, vehicle registration, membership details, where the keys are, and whether anyone or any animal is inside.
8. **If you do not have breakdown cover, call a reputable automotive locksmith.** Use an established automotive locksmith route, ask for the estimated total price before they attend, and ask how they will check that you are entitled to access the vehicle.
9. **Wait somewhere safe and visible.** If you feel unsafe in the car park, roadside, or street, go into a nearby petrol station, supermarket, café, hotel, or staffed public place and make calls from there.
10. **Check the person who arrives before work starts.** Ask for identification, confirm the price again, and expect them to ask for proof that the vehicle is yours or that you have permission to access it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to claim on insurance.
- You do not need to decide now whether to replace keys, change locks, or change keyless-entry settings.
- You do not need to work out a clever way into the vehicle.
- You do not need to cancel everything until you know the recovery or locksmith timing.
- You can deal with spare keys and prevention later, once you are safely back in the vehicle or safely home.

## Important reassurance
This is a common and fixable problem. Because you still have your phone, you can slow the situation down, stay somewhere safer, and arrange help without damaging the vehicle or rushing into a risky payment.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you stay safe and get access to the vehicle. Costs, timings, proof checks, and entry methods vary by vehicle, location, provider, and the circumstances, so later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate practical first steps in the UK. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If someone is in immediate danger, call 999.
