# What to do if… you find a hidden camera or recording device in your hotel or rental room (UK)

## Short answer
Leave the room and get to a safer public place first. If you feel unsafe now, call 999; if you are safe, contact police, accommodation staff, or platform support from outside the room and ask for urgent help moving.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay in the room to see what happens or keep using the shower or bed.
- Don’t confront the host, cleaner, manager, or anyone you suspect while you are alone or still in the room.
- Don’t dismantle the device, remove a memory card, or plug it into anything.
- Don’t start searching the room for more devices while you are alone.
- Don’t post about it publicly while you are still there.
- Don’t let anyone pressure you into returning to the room alone.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance first.** Put on clothes, grab essentials such as your phone, keys, wallet, passport or ID, and medication, and go to a public place such as the corridor, lobby, reception, or outside.

2. **Choose the safest contact route.**
   - If you feel unsafe now, someone may still have access to the room, or the situation may be happening in real time, call **999**.
   - If you are safe and want to report it, call **101** or use the police online reporting route for the country you are in.
   - If you are not ready to report to police, you can still contact the accommodation, platform, or specialist support from a safe place.

3. **Ask to be moved immediately.**
   - **Hotel:** ask reception or the manager to move you now and to make a formal incident record. Ask for the incident or reference number.
   - **Rental or platform stay:** contact the platform’s safety or support route and ask for urgent relocation and a safety case to be opened.
   - Ask that any communication happens in the lobby, by phone, or through the platform, not inside the room.

4. **Preserve the situation with minimal handling, only if it feels safe.** If you can do it without going back alone or increasing risk, take 1–2 quick photos showing the device in place and the wider room location. Note the time, date, room number or listing details, and who you spoke to. If this feels unsafe, skip it and prioritise leaving and getting help.

5. **Ask for records to be kept.** Ask the manager or platform to retain anything that may matter later, such as key-card or access logs, staff entry records, booking messages, maintenance records, and any relevant corridor CCTV. They may have retention limits, so asking early can help preserve options.

6. **Use confidential support if you want it.** If you are in England or Wales, the 24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line from Rape Crisis England & Wales is for anyone aged 16+ affected by sexual violence or unsure about what happened; call **0808 500 2222** or use their online chat. If you are in Scotland, Rape Crisis Scotland has a free helpline on **08088 01 03 02**. If you are in Northern Ireland, nidirect lists specialist support for victims of sexual assault and rape. You can also ask about a Sexual Assault Referral Centre or local equivalent for medical, practical, and emotional support; you do not have to report to police to ask for help.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether to make a formal statement, support an investigation, complain, seek compensation, or continue the trip.
- You do not need to prove what the device is, find every possible device, or work out who placed it.
- You can delay reviews, public posts, detailed complaints, and longer messages until you are safe and supported.

## Important reassurance
Finding something like this can feel shocking, violating, or unreal, even before you know whether anything was recorded. Leaving the room, asking to be moved, and getting support are reasonable first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation, reduce immediate risk, and preserve options. Later decisions about reporting, complaints, legal advice, or platform claims may need specialist help once you are safe.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate safety and next actions, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
