# What to do if… you develop severe vomiting and diarrhea after eating foraged mushrooms or an unknown plant (UK)

## Short answer
Call **999** or go to **A&E** immediately. Suspected mushroom or plant poisoning is a medical emergency that requires professional assessment even if symptoms appear to settle.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for symptoms to improve or "see how it goes."
- Do not try to induce vomiting or stick fingers down your throat.
- Do not eat or drink anything, including water, unless specifically told to by a medical professional.
- Do not take anti-diarrhea medications, painkillers, or antibiotics.
- Do not drink alcohol to "disinfect" or settle your stomach.
- Do not assume you are safe if vomiting stops, as some toxins have a dangerous "false recovery" period.

## What to do now
1. **Seek emergency medical help immediately.** 
   - **Call 999 or attend the nearest A&E department** and state clearly that you have eaten foraged mushrooms or an unknown plant and are now ill.
   - If you are unable to travel or are alone, **call 999** and tell them your exact location / unlock your front door.
2. **Secure a sample of what was eaten (30 seconds).**
   - **Place any remaining mushrooms, plants, or prepared food into a clean bag or container** to take to the hospital.
   - If you have photos of the plant or mushroom in its original growing environment, **keep them ready on your phone** for clinicians to see.
3. **Save a sample of vomit if it occurs (only if natural).**
   - **Place a small amount of vomit in a sealed container** if you have already been sick; this can help the National Poisons Information Service (NPIS) identify the specific toxin.
   - **Wash your hands thoroughly** after handling any samples or contaminated clothing.
4. **Prepare information for the medical team.**
   - **Write down or remember the approximate time of ingestion**, the time symptoms started, and exactly how much was consumed.
   - **Identify if anyone else ate the same food**, even if they do not yet have symptoms.
5. **Position yourself for safety while waiting.**
   - **Sit or lie on your side** (the recovery position) to ensure your airway remains clear if you vomit again or feel faint.
   - **Stay near your phone** and keep it on loud so emergency services can call you back.

## What can wait
- Attempting to identify the species yourself using books, apps, or the internet.
- Notifying foraging groups or social media to ask for an identification.
- Cleaning up the area where the food was prepared or consumed.
- Deciding whether the illness is "just a stomach bug."

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant anxiety when experiencing severe physical symptoms after foraging. UK emergency services are equipped to deal with suspected poisonings, and early intervention is the most effective way to manage potential toxins. Medical teams prefer to assess a "false alarm" than to treat a late-stage poisoning.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and emergency contact procedures only. It does not replace the clinical judgment of the NHS or emergency medical staff.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute medical advice or diagnosis. Mushroom and plant toxins can cause delayed organ damage; always seek professional medical assessment following accidental or symptomatic ingestion.
