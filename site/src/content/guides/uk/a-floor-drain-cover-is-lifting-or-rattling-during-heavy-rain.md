# What to do if… a floor drain cover is lifting or rattling during heavy rain (UK)

## Short answer
Treat it as a possible sewage backflow risk: stop using water in the property, keep people away from the drain, and contact your water company’s sewer flooding or emergency route.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep flushing toilets, running taps, using showers, or starting the washing machine or dishwasher to see whether it clears.
- Don’t lift the drain cover, open inspection chambers, or put tools, rods, hands, or hoses into the drain.
- Don’t stand over the drain if it is lifting, bubbling, smelling foul, or starting to overflow.
- Don’t operate electrical switches, plug or unplug appliances, or approach the consumer unit if you are standing in water or water may have reached electrics.
- Don’t mix cleaning chemicals, especially bleach with other cleaners.
- Don’t assume it is only rainwater if there is a foul smell, discolouration, toilet paper, wipes, or grey water.

## What to do now
1. **Stop adding water to the system:** stop all non-essential water use, including toilets, showers, sinks, washing machines, and dishwashers. Tell everyone in the property.
2. **Keep the affected area clear:** keep children, pets, and visitors away. Close the room off if you can do that without stepping through water.
3. **Check nearby low fixtures without touching drains:** look at the downstairs toilet, bath, shower tray, utility sink, or basement drain for gurgling, slow draining, bubbling, rising water, or foul smell.
4. **Make electrics safer only if you can do it safely:** if water is present or likely, switch off power to the affected area at the consumer unit only from a dry, safe position and only if there is no sign water has reached the consumer unit. If you are unsure, stay away from electrics.
5. **Reduce immediate damage without sealing the drain:**
   - Move valuables, cardboard, rugs, cleaning chemicals, and anything absorbent up off the floor.
   - Put towels, absorbent pads, or a shallow tray around the edge to catch small seepage if you can reach it from dry ground.
   - If the cover is only rattling or lifting and there is no overflow, you may place a heavy, stable item beside or over it to reduce movement and trip risk only if you can do this from a dry, safe position. Do not wedge, clamp, tape, seal, or stand over it.
6. **Contact the right organisation early:**
   - If sewage, foul water, toilet paper, wipes, or a sewer smell may be involved, contact your water company’s sewer flooding or emergency route.
   - If you do not know your wastewater company, use the Water UK supplier finder or check a recent water bill.
   - If the problem is mainly road gullies, public drains, or flooding on the street, contact your local council or highways route.
   - If there is immediate danger, such as risk of electrocution, structural collapse, or a medical emergency, call 999.
7. **Record what is happening:** take photos or short videos from a safe dry position. Note the time, rainfall conditions, which fixtures are affected, whether the water is rising or falling, and whether neighbours have the same problem.
8. **Keep contaminated items separate:** if water has escaped, avoid skin contact, wash hands after being near the area, and keep shoes or tools that touched the water away from clean areas.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the problem is a private drain, a lateral drain, or the public sewer.
- You do not need to start deep cleaning, lift flooring, move heavy appliances, or handle contaminated water during the event.
- You do not need to buy specialist flood products tonight; focus on stopping water use, staying away from electrics and contaminated water, and reporting the problem.
- You do not need to argue responsibility while the water is still rising; report the symptoms first and keep a record.

## Important reassurance
A rattling or lifting drain cover during heavy rain can be an early sign that the drainage system is under pressure. Stopping water use, keeping away from contamination and electrics, and reporting it early are useful first steps even before the exact cause is confirmed.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce harm. Later decisions, such as responsibility for repairs, insurance, cleaning, or whether the issue is a private drain or public sewer, may need help from your water company, landlord, insurer, local authority, drainage contractor, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general practical information for urgent home situations, not legal, medical, financial, electrical, plumbing, insurance, or other professional advice. Follow emergency services, your water company, your insurer, and qualified professionals where their advice applies.
