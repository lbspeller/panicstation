# What to do if… a fuel station preauthorization hold is using most of your available balance (UK)

## Short answer
Treat it as a temporary reserved amount unless it has posted as a completed charge. Do not create another pay-at-pump hold; check the transaction status, keep the receipt or screenshots, and contact your bank or card issuer if it is blocking essential spending or payments.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep trying the same card at more pumps; this can create more pending holds.
- Don’t assume the full pending amount is the final fuel charge.
- Don’t rely only on your available balance if rent, bills, food, travel, or medication costs are due.
- Don’t delete screenshots, app messages, or receipts.
- Don’t ignore it if the final posted amount is wrong, duplicated, or does not match the fuel you bought.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether the transaction is pending or completed.**  
   In your banking app, open the transaction details and look for wording such as pending, card authorisation, reserved, or completed. Save a screenshot showing the merchant name, amount, date, and time.

2. **Check what you actually spent on fuel.**  
   Keep the receipt if you have it. If you do not, write down the station, pump number if known, approximate time, and the amount of fuel you bought.

3. **Do not use pay at pump again with the same card for now.**  
   If you need more fuel, pay at the till where available, or use another safe payment method, so you do not trigger another automated fuel preauthorisation.

4. **Protect essential payments first.**  
   List anything due today or tomorrow, such as rent, direct debits, food, travel, or medication. Treat the held amount as unavailable until your bank confirms otherwise.

5. **Contact your bank or card issuer using clear wording.**  
   Say: “A pay-at-pump preauthorisation hold is using most of my available balance. Can you confirm whether it is pending or completed, tell me how release usually works, and tell me what you can do if this causes charges or missed payments?”  
   If you cannot buy essentials, say that plainly.

6. **Contact the biller before a payment fails, if needed.**  
   If a direct debit, rent payment, subscription, or other bill may fail today or tomorrow, contact the company and ask whether they can pause, retry, or extend the payment date. Keep notes of who you spoke to and when.

7. **Use the fuel retailer only for transaction details.**  
   If your bank asks for more information, contact the fuel station or operator for the site address, receipt copy if available, pump number, and confirmation of the fuel amount. Do not rely on the retailer alone to release the hold.

8. **If the transaction posts incorrectly, dispute the posted transaction with your bank.**  
   If the completed charge is wrong, duplicated, or does not match your fuel purchase, contact your bank or card issuer promptly. Keep the receipt, screenshots, and any messages from the retailer.

9. **If the bank will not put things right, use the complaints route.**  
   Complain to your bank or card issuer and ask for the outcome in writing. You can usually take the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service after a final response, or if the firm has not replied within 8 weeks. Some payment-service complaints may have shorter response times, so check the firm’s complaint information if timing matters.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change banks.
- You do not need to make a formal complaint while the transaction is still pending unless the bank will not help with urgent hardship or charges.
- You do not need to prove fraud just because the pending amount is much higher than the fuel you bought.
- You do not need to resolve the whole dispute before protecting today’s essentials.

## Important reassurance
This can look as if money has been taken, but pay-at-pump systems can temporarily reserve more than the fuel you actually buy. The immediate aim is to avoid extra holds, protect essential payments, and keep evidence in case the final charge is wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the next hours or days. Later decisions about disputes, complaints, bank charges, overdrafts, or debt pressure may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not financial, legal, debt, or professional advice. Processes can vary by bank, card type, fuel retailer, and whether the transaction is pending or completed.
