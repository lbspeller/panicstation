# What to do if… you find a small device aimed at your bed or shower in a hotel or rental (UK)

## Short answer
Exit the room immediately and go to a safe, public location like a lobby or a different building. Once safe, call the police and the booking platform to report the incident and arrange secure relocation.

## Do not do these things
- Don't touch, move, or dismantle the device, as this can destroy fingerprint or DNA evidence.
- Don't stay in the room to "investigate" or search for more devices while you are alone.
- Don't confront the host or hotel staff while you are still inside the private area.
- Don't use the shower, bed, or changing areas once you have spotted a suspicious device.
- Don't plug the device into your own computer or phone to "see what is on it."

## What to do now
1. **Exit the room and find a safe pause in a public area.** **Gather your essentials (phone, passport, wallet, and medication) and leave the room immediately to go to a lobby, hallway, or public street.** 
2. **Contact the police to report a potential crime.** **If you feel you are in immediate danger or a crime is currently in progress, call 999; otherwise, call 101 to report the discovery of a covert recording device.**
   - Advise the operator that you have found a device aimed at a private area (bed / shower).
   - Ask for an incident reference number.
3. **Notify the accommodation provider or booking platform from your safe location.** **Inform the hotel management or the rental platform's emergency safety line that you have found an undisclosed device and require immediate relocation.**
   - For platforms like Airbnb or Booking.com, use the "Report a Safety Issue" feature in the app.
   - Request that they open a formal safety case and provide you with a case number.
4. **Preserve the scene without physical contact if possible.** **If you can do so safely without returning to a vulnerable state, take a photograph of the device from a distance to show its placement and orientation.**
   - Note the exact time you discovered it.
   - Do not attempt to remove memory cards or power cables.
5. **Request that the provider secures digital logs.** **Ask the manager or platform to preserve all electronic records, including key-card access logs, staff entry times, and corridor CCTV footage.**
   - Formal preservation requests are often time-sensitive as some systems overwrite data every 24–72 hours.
6. **Access confidential support services if needed.** **You can contact specialist services for emotional and practical guidance regardless of whether you choose to pursue a police report.**
   - **England & Wales:** Call the 24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line on 0808 500 2222.
   - **Scotland:** Call Rape Crisis Scotland on 08088 01 03 02 (6pm–midnight).
   - **Northern Ireland:** Call the 24-hour Domestic and Sexual Abuse Helpline on 0808 802 1414.
   - You may also contact a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) for confidential advice and forensic options.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify exactly what the device is or how it works right now.
- You do not have to decide today if you want to give a full witness statement or go to court.
- You do not need to post a review or social media warning immediately; your physical safety and evidence preservation are higher priorities.
- You do not need to prove that you were recorded to have your concerns taken seriously by the police or platform.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden sense of violation, shock, or panic when your privacy is compromised. Taking immediate steps to move to a public space and involve authorities is a standard and appropriate response to this situation. Your intuition that the situation is "wrong" is enough to justify leaving and seeking help.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the minutes and hours following the discovery of a suspicious device. It focuses on physical safety and evidence preservation. Decisions regarding legal action or long-term safety planning should be made with professional support once you are in a secure environment.

## Important note
This information is for guidance in a crisis-adjacent moment and does not constitute legal advice. Jurisdictional processes in the UK can vary; always follow the direct instructions of the police (999/101) in your specific location.
