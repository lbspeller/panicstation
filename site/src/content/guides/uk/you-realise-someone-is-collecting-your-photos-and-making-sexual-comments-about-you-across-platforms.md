# What to do if… you realise someone is collecting your photos and making sexual comments about you across platforms (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the situation by switching all social accounts to private and stopping all interaction with the person. Preserve copies of the content as evidence, then report the behavior to the platforms and contact specialist helplines for technical removal assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply to, argue with, or attempt to "reason" with the person collecting the photos.
- Do not post public screenshots of the harassment or "call out" the perpetrator on your own feed.
- Do not delete the offending posts or accounts before you have captured clear screenshots for evidence.
- Do not ignore the behavior if it has happened two or more times; treat repetition as a pattern.
- Do not assume that "locking" one app is sufficient if your photos are appearing on multiple platforms.

## What to do now
1. **Remove yourself from the immediate digital environment and involve a witness.**  
   **Log out or close the apps where you discovered the activity and ask a trusted person to sit with you while you complete the reporting steps.** This provides a physical safety check and a second set of eyes for documenting evidence. If you feel at physical risk, **call 999**.

2. **Preserve a high-quality record of the behavior for legal or platform use.**  
   **Capture screenshots or screen recordings of the specific comments, the photos being used, the perpetrator’s profile, and any direct URLs.**
   - Include the date and time in the capture.
   - Save these to a private, password-protected folder or a dedicated note.
   - Note down the specific platform names and the usernames involved.

3. **Enact immediate privacy restrictions across all connected platforms.**  
   **Switch all social media accounts to 'Private' and restrict interactions to 'Followers/Friends Only' in your settings.**
   - Turn off all location-sharing features and remove routine-related info (e.g., gym tags, workplace, or local landmarks) from your bio.
   - Check "Tagged Photos" settings and set them to "Manually Approve" to prevent new content from appearing on your profile.

4. **Report the content using the specific 'Harassment' or 'Intimate Image' triggers.**  
   **Use the in-app reporting tools on every platform where the content is found, selecting 'Sexual Harassment' or 'Non-Consensual Intimate Image' (NCII) where available.** Under the UK Online Safety Act, platforms are required to review and remove flagged intimate image abuse within 48 hours.

5. **Engage specialist UK helplines for technical and legal guidance.**  
   **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline (0345 6000 459) if nudes or sexualised images of you are involved, or the National Stalking Helpline (0808 8020 300) for advice on patterns of obsessive behavior.**
   - Use **StopNCII.org** to create "hashes" (digital fingerprints) of your images to proactively block them from being uploaded to participating platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Reddit.
   - If you are under 18, report immediately to **CEOP**.

6. **Contact the police if there is a pattern of two or more incidents.**  
   **Call 101 to report harassment or stalking if the behavior has occurred more than once and causes you distress or fear.** If the perpetrator makes threats, mentions your home address, or tries to contact you offline, this is an escalation that requires a formal report. Use official channels via **police.uk**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether you want to pursue a full criminal prosecution.
- You do not need to conduct an exhaustive search of the entire internet to find every possible copy.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your broader social circle or "clear your name" publicly.
- You do not need to permanently delete your social media accounts right now; temporary deactivation or privacy locking is sufficient.

## Important reassurance
Panic, anger, and a sense of violation are common responses when your digital boundaries are crossed. The situation is now being documented and contained. Focusing on the immediate technical steps—blocking, reporting, and preserving—is the most effective way to regain control over your images.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK users facing image-based harassment or stalking. It focuses on immediate harm reduction and evidence preservation. Ongoing safety planning and legal advice should be sought through the National Stalking Helpline or the Revenge Porn Helpline.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Jurisdiction-specific laws, such as the Protection from Harassment Act 1997 and the Online Safety Act, govern these incidents in the UK. Always prioritise your physical safety and contact emergency services if you are in immediate danger.
