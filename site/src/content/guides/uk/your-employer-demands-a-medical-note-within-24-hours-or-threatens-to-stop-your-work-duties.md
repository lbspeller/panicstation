# What to do if… your employer demands a medical note within 24 hours or threatens to stop your work duties (UK)

## Short answer
Reply in writing now, ask whether this is about sickness absence evidence or fitness and safety for particular duties, and offer self-certification if you have been off sick for 7 calendar days or less. Ask for a short written extension while you arrange any further evidence they genuinely need.

## Do not do these things
- Do not forge, alter, or borrow a medical note.
- Do not resign on the spot, agree to unpaid leave, or accept a change you do not understand just to make the pressure stop.
- Do not ignore the message; a short written reply is safer than silence.
- Do not overshare your diagnosis or private medical history with a manager; describe work impact and restrictions instead.
- Do not pay privately for a letter before asking what evidence they will accept and whether they will cover any fee.
- Do not keep doing a task you believe is immediately unsafe; ask for a safe temporary pause or alternative duty while the evidence issue is clarified.

## What to do now
1. **Get the demand into writing, or confirm it in writing.**  
   Send a short message recording the 24-hour deadline, who set it, what “taken off duties” means, whether pay is affected, and whether the issue is sickness absence, Statutory Sick Pay, contractual sick pay, or safety for specific duties.

2. **Ask what evidence they will accept by the deadline.**  
   Ask for the relevant sickness absence, fit note, medical evidence, or fitness-for-duty policy. Ask whether they will accept self-certification, an email giving sickness dates, proof that you have requested a GP or NHS appointment, an appointment confirmation, or a hospital or clinic attendance note.

3. **Use the UK self-certification position calmly if you have been off sick for 7 days or less.**  
   Write: “My understanding is that for sickness absence of 7 calendar days or less, employees generally do not need a fit note or other medical evidence from a healthcare professional. I can self-certify now and will arrange further evidence if it becomes needed.”

4. **If Statutory Sick Pay is involved, ask HR to confirm the rule they are applying.**  
   For SSP fit-note proof, employers can only ask for a fit note once someone has been off sick for more than 7 days in a row, including non-working days. They also cannot withhold SSP just because a fit note is sent late, although you should still explain any delay and give an expected date.

5. **Create proof of action within the 24 hours.**  
   Do at least one of these:
   - send your employer’s self-certification form or a short email with your sickness dates
   - use GOV.UK form SC2 if your employer needs something in writing for SSP and you do not have an internal form
   - request a GP or NHS fit note route if you have been off sick for more than 7 calendar days, or if your clinician says one is appropriate
   - keep a copy or screenshot showing that you requested an appointment, consultation, fit note, or records from a hospital or clinic

6. **If they want clinician evidence within the first 7 days, ask about fees before paying.**  
   Write: “If you require clinician evidence before a fit note is normally needed, please confirm exactly what evidence you need, why self-certification is not enough, and whether the organisation will cover any fee.”

7. **If this is about safety or fitness for specific duties, propose a temporary work-focused alternative.**  
   Ask whether you can do temporary adjusted duties, avoid a specific task, work remotely, change hours, or have a brief safe pause while you obtain evidence. Ask whether occupational health is available, and what referral process they want to use.

8. **If the issue relates to a disability, long-term condition, or recurring health problem, keep the wording practical.**  
   Say: “I am asking for temporary adjustments while I obtain medical evidence focused on work impact and restrictions.” You do not need to give your full medical history in the first reply.

9. **Bring in support if you have it.**  
   If you are in a union, send the written demand to your rep. If not, keep a dated timeline of messages, calls, deadlines, and what you provided, and consider contacting Acas for guidance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to raise a grievance, make a tribunal claim, or leave your job.
- You do not need to disclose detailed medical history today; dates, work impact, restrictions, and evidence steps are enough for a first reply.
- You do not need to prove everything in real time; your immediate job is to respond, document, self-certify if appropriate, and ask for a realistic evidence route.

## Important reassurance
A 24-hour medical-note deadline can feel impossible, especially when appointments are limited. A prompt written reply, self-certification where appropriate, proof that you are trying to get evidence, and a request for a proportionate process can reduce the risk of the situation escalating through panic or silence.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce panic decisions and protect the next few hours. Later decisions about pay, formal absence processes, disability-related adjustments, grievances, or legal time limits may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Workplace policies vary, and urgent health or safety concerns may need a faster local response.
