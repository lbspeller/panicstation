# What to do if… your mobile carrier account PIN or security code is changed without you (UK)

## Short answer
Do not initiate a factory reset or wipe the device; keep the phone powered and connected to Wi-Fi while you attempt a secondary local backup or manual data export.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t perform a factory reset to "fix" a backup error, as this permanently deletes the only remaining copy of your data.
- Don’t sign out of your Apple Account or Google account if you are unsure of your password or do not have access to your secondary two-step verification method.
- Don’t disable "Find My" or device protection features until you have confirmed a successful data transfer or backup exists elsewhere.
- Don’t assume a "Cloud Sync" (like Google Photos or iCloud Photos) is the same as a full device backup; syncs do not always include app data or settings.
- Don’t rely on Microsoft Authenticator cloud backups if you are switching between Android and iOS, as these backups are platform-specific and cannot be transferred across operating systems.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the device and establish a stable environment for data transfer.**  
   Connect the phone to a wall charger and a known, stable Wi-Fi network to prevent a shutdown during background processes. Disable "Low Power Mode" and stop high-bandwidth activities like video streaming or large app updates to prioritize backup traffic.

2. **Verify the exact timestamp and content of your last successful cloud backup.**  
   - For iPhone (iOS 18+): Go to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > iCloud Backup and check the "Last successful backup" time.  
   - For Android: Go to Settings > Google > Backup and check the status of specific categories (Photos, SMS, Call History).  
   If the date is not current, treat all data on the physical handset as the sole existing copy.

3. **Attempt a manual local backup to a computer using a physical cable.**  
   - On a Mac (Catalina or later) or PC with the Apple Devices app: Connect the phone and select "Encrypt local backup" to ensure Health, Activity, and Keychain data are included.  
   - On Android: Connect to a PC via USB in "File Transfer" mode and manually copy the "DCIM" (Photos) and "Downloads" folders to the computer's drive.  
   Note: An encrypted local backup requires a password you must remember to restore the data later.

4. **Enable the 2026 native "Downloads" backup toggle if using a modern Android device.**  
   Navigate to Settings > Google > Backup and ensure the "Files" or "Downloads" toggle is switched to "On." This new feature creates static copies of PDFs, tickets, and documents in your Google Drive, which were previously excluded from standard Android backups.

5. **Perform an emergency "Lifeboat Export" of irreplaceable individual items.**  
   If full backups continue to fail, manually share or upload your top 20 most critical photos, recent documents, or work files to a third-party cloud service (like Dropbox, OneDrive, or a self-sent email). Focus on items created since the last successful backup timestamp.

6. **Generate and record "Backup Codes" for all authentication and security apps.**  
   Open your primary security app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Apple Account settings) and find the "Recovery Codes" or "Backup Codes" section. Write these codes down on physical paper or store them in a separate secure location; these are often the only way to log in if your device is wiped and the app backup fails to restore.

7. **Request a "Data Preservation Pause" if a third party is requiring a device reset.**  
   If a repair shop or retailer insists on a reset for service, inform them that the backup is failing and ask for a 24-hour window to attempt data recovery elsewhere. Ask the technician to confirm if a "Logic Board" replacement is required, as this will result in immediate data loss regardless of reset status.

## What can wait
- You do not need to organize your photo gallery or delete "clutter" files right now unless storage is 100% full.
- You do not need to troubleshoot why the backup failed (e.g., software bugs) until after the data is safely copied elsewhere.
- You do not need to finalize your trade-in or insurance claim immediately; most UK consumer service windows allow for a brief delay to ensure data safety.

## Important reassurance
It is common for backups to fail during high-stress moments, often due to temporary server congestion or minor Wi-Fi drops. Hesitating to reset the phone is a correct and safe response. Most data can still be extracted manually even when the automated "one-button" backup system reports an error.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover professional forensic data recovery for physically broken screens or "dead" motherboards.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee data recovery. Procedures may vary based on your specific OS version and device manufacturer. If your data is of high financial or legal importance, consider contacting a professional data recovery specialist in the UK before proceeding with any reset.
