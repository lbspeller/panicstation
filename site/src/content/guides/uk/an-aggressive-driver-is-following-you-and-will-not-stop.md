# What to do if… an aggressive driver is following you and will not stop (UK)

## Short answer
Do not engage and do not drive home. Keep driving steadily towards a busy, well-lit public place, and call 999 if you feel in danger or the driver is still following, threatening, blocking, or trying to force you to stop.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive home, to work, to school, or to another predictable personal place.
- Do not stop somewhere isolated to talk, argue, apologise, or prove a point.
- Do not brake-check, speed, race, or make risky turns to try to lose them.
- Do not make eye contact, gesture back, shout, or film while driving.
- Do not get out of your car if they stop near you or approach your vehicle.
- Do not hold and use your phone while driving unless it is a genuine emergency and it is unsafe or impractical to stop.

## What to do now
1. **Make the inside of the car safer.** Lock the doors, keep your seatbelt on, keep windows mostly up, and focus on smooth, predictable driving.
2. **Create space without escalating.** If it is safe, change lanes, let them pass, or take a normal signed route change. Avoid sudden braking, repeated loops, or turning into quiet side streets.
3. **Move towards people, light, and cameras.** Head for motorway services, a busy petrol station, a supermarket car park, or another open public place. A police station can be an option if it is clearly staffed and open, but do not rely on finding one in the moment.
4. **Call 999 if the risk is happening now.** Call if you are in fear for your safety, they are trying to force you to stop, they are blocking you, they have made threats, or they are still following after you have tried to disengage.
5. **Use your phone in the safest lawful way you can.** If you have a passenger, ask them to call. If you are alone, use hands-free or voice control if you can. If it is a genuine emergency and it is unsafe or impractical to stop, you can call 999 or 112 while driving, but keep the call focused on getting police help and reaching a safer stopping place.
6. **If you stop, stay protected.** Park in a visible space near an entrance, keep the doors locked, and leave room to drive away if safe. If they stop behind you or approach, do not engage; call 999 or stay on the line if already connected.
7. **Give simple details.** Say where you are, your direction of travel, your car description, their car description, and what they are doing. Give the registration only if you can do it without distraction.
8. **If the immediate danger has passed, report safely.** Once parked somewhere safe, use 101 or your local police force’s online reporting route for a non-emergency report.
9. **Preserve only what you already have.** When you are parked and steady, save dashcam footage if available and write down the time, place, vehicle description, registration if known, and what happened.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a full statement or pursue anything further.
- You do not need to identify the driver or gather perfect evidence.
- You do not need to call your insurer, post online, or message lots of people while you are still shaken.
- You do not need to replay the incident in detail until you are safe and stopped.

## Important reassurance
This can feel frightening and personal, and it is normal to feel shaky, angry, or blank. The safest outcome is often the least dramatic one: do not engage, do not lead them to your life, and move towards people and help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes to reduce danger and prevent irreversible mistakes. Later reporting, insurance, legal, or support decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 999.
