# What to do if… your front door will not latch or lock and you cannot secure your home (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the entry point and contact your landlord or a vetted emergency locksmith immediately. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is in progress, call **999**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the property unoccupied while the external door is insecure.
- Do not attempt to force a jammed multi-point lock (the long strip on uPVC doors) as this can permanently strip the internal gearbox.
- Do not use a locksmith found through an unvetted social media ad or a search result that does not provide a physical business address.
- Do not barricade the door in a way that prevents a rapid exit in the event of a fire.
- Do not agree to have your lock drilled out until the locksmith has attempted non-destructive entry or explained why it is impossible.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause and assess immediate risk.** If someone is currently attempting to enter or you feel physically threatened, call **999**. If the situation is stable, remain inside or with a trusted neighbour while arranging repairs.
2. **Check for Home Emergency Cover.** Look at your home insurance policy or bank account benefits to see if "Home Emergency" is included.
   - If included, call the insurer's 24-hour number first; they will dispatch a vetted locksmith and usually cover the call-out cost.
3. **If you rent: notify your landlord or agent immediately.** Under Section 11 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, landlords are responsible for maintaining the structure and exterior of the property, including external doors and locks.
   - In 2026, emergency security hazards must be attended to or made safe within 24 hours.
   - Send the notification in writing (email or text) even if you have already called, to create a dated record.
4. **If you must hire a locksmith, verify them through the Master Locksmiths Association (MLA).** Use the MLA "Find a Locksmith" tool to ensure the technician is vetted, inspected, and qualified.
   - Ask for a "total price" estimate including the call-out fee, hourly rate, and potential parts (e.g., a standard Euro cylinder or a 5-lever mortice lock).
5. **Secure secondary internal barriers.** Lock any internal doors that lead to the entryway, such as a hallway or lounge door, to create an additional layer of security while waiting for help.
6. **Gather evidence of the fault for insurance or tenancy records.** Take a 30-second video showing the door failing to latch, any misalignment of the strike plate, or the movement of the handle.
7. **Verify the locksmith's identity upon arrival.** Ask to see their ID and confirm they are from the company you contacted before allowing them to begin work.

## What can wait
- You do not need to pay for the repair tonight if you are a tenant and the fault is due to wear and tear.
- You do not need to decide on permanent security upgrades (CCTV, smart locks, or door replacement) until the immediate fault is corrected.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the fault or the landlord’s response time until after the property is secure.

## Important reassurance
A broken lock is a common mechanical failure often caused by temperature changes affecting door alignment or simple component wear. Once a professional attends, most doors can be made secure or fully repaired during the first visit. Taking a moment to verify your locksmith or landlord's procedure prevents further complications.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a physical security failure. It does not cover legal disputes over repair costs or long-term property improvements.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or security advice. If you believe a crime is being committed or your life is in danger, contact emergency services at 999.
