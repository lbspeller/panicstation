# What to do if… your front door will not latch or lock and you cannot secure your home (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a time-sensitive security problem: get to a safer pause, avoid blocking your escape route, and arrange urgent repair through your landlord, emergency repair route, home assistance provider, or a reputable locksmith.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the property empty while the front door will not secure.
- Don’t barricade the door in a way that traps you inside or blocks your fire escape route.
- Don’t agree to drilling, replacement, or expensive parts without a clear explanation and a clear total price, including call-out, labour, VAT if charged, and parts.
- Don’t post publicly that your home is unsecured or that you are alone or away.
- Don’t assume it is automatically your cost if you rent; report it first, because responsibility can depend on your tenancy, the fault, and the cause.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.** If someone is trying to get in now, you are being threatened, or there is immediate danger, call **999**. If there is no immediate danger but you need to report a police concern, use **101**.
2. **Keep an exit available.** Make sure you can still get out quickly in a fire. Keep keys accessible, and do not block the only usable exit.
3. **Secure other entry points.** Lock other external doors, accessible windows, patio doors, side gates, and any internal door that creates a safer separation from the front door, if you can do this without delaying urgent help.
4. **Use only a temporary inside-only measure you can remove instantly.** A door wedge or light movable item may help slow the door opening while you arrange help, but do not use anything that traps you, blocks escape, or would stop emergency services reaching you.
5. **Make a quick record of the fault.** Take a short video showing the door failing to latch or lock, the handle or key movement, and any visible gap, dropped hinge, loose handle, damaged frame, or misalignment. Note the date and time.
6. **If you rent, report it as urgent in writing now.** Use your landlord, agent, housing provider, or emergency repairs route if you have one. Say: “The front door will not latch or lock, so I cannot secure the property. Please confirm when someone can attend urgently.” Keep a copy.
7. **If you need a locksmith, reduce scam risk before anyone attends.** Ask for the business name, who is attending, a realistic total price range, and what they will try before drilling or replacing parts. Consider using a vetted locksmith, such as a Master Locksmiths Association approved locksmith.
8. **If the home cannot be secured tonight, choose the safest immediate arrangement.** This could mean staying with a trusted person, asking someone trusted to stay with you, using emergency home assistance cover, or remaining nearby while urgent help attends. Prioritise not leaving the home empty and unsecured.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to choose a new door, smart lock, alarm, or long-term security upgrade now.
- You do **not** need to settle who pays tonight; focus on getting the door secure and keeping your record of the fault and messages.
- You can deal with alignment checks, replacement parts, spare keys, insurance, complaints, or reimbursement after the immediate security problem is under control.

## Important reassurance
A front door that will not latch or lock can feel alarming because it removes a basic sense of safety. The immediate job is narrow: stay safe, keep an escape route, get the fault recorded, and arrange urgent help without being pressured into rushed expensive decisions.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair responsibility, replacement, complaints, insurance, or recovering costs may need specialist housing, consumer, insurance, or legal help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, safety, or professional locksmith advice. If you believe there is immediate danger, call 999.
