# What to do if… your card is declined due to a merchant category block you did not set (UK)

## Short answer
Stop retrying the same payment. Use another safe payment method if you have one, then contact your card provider through the app or the number on the back of your card and ask what they can tell you about the decline reason.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep attempting the same payment repeatedly; repeated declines can sometimes lead to extra checks or blocks.
- Don’t hand your card or phone to anyone to “fix it”, or let a stranger take it out of your sight.
- Don’t share your PIN, one-time passcodes, full card details, or app login details, even with someone claiming to be your bank or card provider.
- Don’t cancel or replace the card in a rush unless you see transactions you don’t recognise or the provider tells you there may be fraud.
- Don’t set up a new bank transfer in a panic if you are stressed, rushed, being watched, or being pressured.

## What to do now
1. **Pause and write down the details.** Note the merchant name, location or website, time, amount, and what you were trying to buy. If your app shows a message such as “merchant category blocked” or “spending category blocked”, take a screenshot.

2. **Try one controlled alternative, then stop.**
   - If you used contactless, try chip-and-PIN once.
   - If the payment is online, try one safe alternative you already use, such as a different card already in your digital wallet.
   - If that also declines, stop retrying and contact your card provider.

3. **Check only the card controls you recognise.** In your banking or card app, look for controls such as merchant categories, spending categories, online payments, contactless, ATM withdrawals, international use, gambling blocks, or other card security controls.
   - If you find a relevant setting that is clearly switched on by mistake, switch it off only if you understand what it does.
   - If the block looks protective, relates to gambling, or you are not sure what it does, leave it alone and ask your provider before changing it.

4. **Contact your card provider the safe way.** Use in-app chat, the number on the back of your card, or the official contact route shown inside your app. Do not use a number sent by text or given by someone pressuring you at the point of sale.

5. **Ask direct questions to separate the possible causes.** You can say:
   - “Can you tell me the decline reason for this transaction?”
   - “Does it look like a merchant category restriction, a fraud or risk block, or a verification issue?”
   - “Which merchant category appears to be blocked?”
   - “Has the merchant been coded as something unexpected, such as gambling, money transfer, cash-like services, or premium-rate services?”
   - “Is this a setting I can change, a restriction from you, or a restriction that cannot be overridden?”
   - “Can you remove the block if it is a setting on my card?”
   - “Can you tell whether this block was enabled by me, by an authorised user, by your systems, or by a product rule?”

6. **Buy time safely if you need the goods or booking now.**
   - Ask the merchant to hold the goods, booking, or order while you speak to your provider.
   - Use another card or cash if you have it and can pay calmly.
   - If the merchant suggests bank transfer, only do it if you already trust the merchant and can carefully check the payee details before sending money.

7. **Treat pressure or unfamiliar requests as a warning sign.**
   - If someone tells you to share codes, move money, install an app, or pay urgently another way, stop.
   - If you see transactions you do not recognise, freeze or lock the card in-app if you can and tell the provider you suspect fraud.
   - If you think your app, card, or phone has been taken over, use another device or phone line to contact your provider through official details.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out who caused the block right now.
- You do not need to make permanent changes to all your card settings today.
- You do not need to switch banks or card providers today.
- You can ask about a formal complaint later if the provider will not fix the issue or will not explain what it can. The financial business usually has up to eight weeks to respond to a complaint, although some payment service or e-money complaints may have shorter response times. If you are unhappy with the final response, or you do not get a response within the relevant timeframe, you can usually ask the Financial Ombudsman Service to look at it. You normally need to do this within six months of the final response.

## Important reassurance
A merchant category block can be caused by automated coding, card controls, product rules, or security checks. It does not mean you did anything wrong, and the safest next step is to get a clear decline reason before changing settings or trying new ways to pay.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you get unstuck, avoid unsafe payments, and reduce the chance of extra card blocks. If the issue keeps happening, you may need specialist help from your card provider, a consumer advice service, or a complaints body.

## Important note
This is general information, not financial, legal, security, or professional advice. If you think you are being scammed or pressured to pay in an unsafe way, stop and contact your card provider using official contact details.
