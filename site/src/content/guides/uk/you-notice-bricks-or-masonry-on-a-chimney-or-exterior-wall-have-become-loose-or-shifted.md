# What to do if… you notice bricks or masonry on a chimney or exterior wall have become loose or shifted (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the area beneath the masonry immediately to prevent injury from falling debris and contact your local council's Building Control department or a structural professional for an urgent safety assessment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk, stand, or park vehicles directly underneath the affected area.
- Do not climb ladders or go onto the roof to inspect the damage yourself.
- Do not attempt to push, pull, or "test" the stability of the masonry.
- Do not apply DIY sealants, foams, or mortar to "glue" loose pieces back in place.
- Do not use any fireplaces, boilers, or appliances that vent through the affected chimney.
- Do not ignore small fallen fragments, as they often precede a larger collapse.

## What to do now
1. **Clear and secure the area immediately.** Ensure no people or pets are in the "fall zone" (the area directly below and several metres outward from the loose masonry).
   - Use bins, chairs, or tape to block off paths, driveways, or garden areas.
   - If the masonry is above a doorway, use an alternative exit if available.
2. **Contact the local council Building Control "Dangerous Structures" team.** Search "[Council Name] report a dangerous structure" to find their 24-hour emergency line.
   - They have a statutory duty to assess structures that pose a risk to the public.
   - If the debris is falling onto a public pavement or road, this is an immediate priority.
3. **Call 999 if there is an immediate threat to life.** Use this if a large section of wall or a chimney stack appears to be actively collapsing or if debris is currently falling onto a busy public area.
4. **Shut down relevant fuel-burning appliances.** If the chimney is affected, **turn off any boilers, wood burners, or gas fires** that use that flue.
   - Damage to the masonry can indicate internal flue damage, which may lead to carbon monoxide leakage or fire.
5. **Follow gas emergency protocols if you smell gas.** If you suspect a gas pipe or flue is damaged, call the **National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999** from a safe distance outside.
6. **Alert neighbors or the landlord.** If you share a wall (party wall) or live in a terrace, **inform the neighbors** so they can avoid the area on their side of the boundary.
   - If you rent, notify the landlord or agent in writing immediately after securing the area.
7. **Document the scene from a safe distance.** Take photographs or video **without approaching the hazard** to provide to building control, your insurer, or a contractor.
8. **Contact a structural professional or specialist contractor.** Arrange for a **Chartered Structural Engineer** or a **competent chimney specialist** (e.g., HETAS or Gas Safe registered depending on the appliance) to provide a formal repair plan once the area is made safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair method (repointing vs. rebuilding) until a professional has stabilised the structure.
- You do not need to clean up small fallen bits of mortar or brick until the area is declared safe to enter.
- Cosmetic matching of brickwork or decorative finishes can wait until structural integrity is confirmed.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a high level of stress when a building structure appears to be failing. However, most masonry issues do not lead to instantaneous total collapse if the area is isolated and the weight is not disturbed. By creating an exclusion zone, you have already significantly reduced the primary risk of injury.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and reporting instructions only. It does not replace a physical inspection by a qualified building surveyor or structural engineer.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance only and does not constitute professional engineering or legal advice. If you believe a structure is at imminent risk of falling onto a public highway, contact the emergency services and your local authority immediately.
