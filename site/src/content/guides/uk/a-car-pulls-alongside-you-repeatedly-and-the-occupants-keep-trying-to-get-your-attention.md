# What to do if… a car pulls alongside you repeatedly and the occupants keep trying to get your attention (UK)

## Short answer
Keep your vehicle moving toward a busy, well-lit public area or a police station and call 999 immediately if you feel threatened. Do not pull over, stop, or open your windows to communicate.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull over to the side of the road or onto a hard shoulder to "see what they want."
- Do not drive to your home or workplace where the occupants can see where you live or work.
- Do not lower your windows or unlock your doors at any point.
- Do not make eye contact, engage in gestures, or attempt to shout back at the occupants.
- Do not use risky driving maneuvers, such as speeding or running red lights, to escape.
- Do not stop in isolated areas, such as dark side streets or empty car parks.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure all doors are locked and windows are fully closed.** This creates a physical barrier and reduces the immediate risk of someone attempting to enter your vehicle if you are forced to stop.
2. **Navigate toward a busy, well-lit public location or the nearest police station.** Aim for places with high foot traffic, CCTV coverage, and staff presence, such as a large 24-hour petrol station or a supermarket car park.
3. **Call 999 if you feel you are in immediate danger or are being followed.** While UK law generally prohibits hand-held phone use, there is a specific exception for calling 999 or 112 in a genuine emergency when it is unsafe or impractical to stop.
   - Use hands-free if available to maintain full control of the vehicle.
   - If you cannot speak, use the "Silent Solution" by calling 999 and pressing 55 when prompted to alert the operator that you are in danger but cannot talk.
4. **Provide the emergency operator with specific identifiers for both vehicles.** State your current location, direction of travel, and the make, model, colour, and registration number of the other vehicle if you can see it safely.
5. **Keep your vehicle moving and maintain a safe gap from the car in front at junctions.** Ensure you have enough space to maneuver or drive around the vehicle in front if the suspicious car attempts to box you in or the occupants exit their vehicle.
6. **Perform a "follow check" only if it is safe to do so on main roads.** Make four consecutive turns in the same direction (forming a square) or take an unnecessary exit at a roundabout that you can safely recover from; if the vehicle stays with you, assume you are being targeted.
7. **If you are forced to stop, stay inside the vehicle with the engine running.** Keep the car in gear so you can drive away immediately if an opening appears, and do not exit the car even if the other occupants approach your window.
8. **Record the incident using a dashcam or by noting details once you are safely parked.** Ensure any footage is saved or locked to prevent it from being overwritten, and write down the physical descriptions of the occupants as soon as you are in a secure location.
9. **Report the incident via 101 or the local police website if the immediate threat passes.** Even if no physical confrontation occurred, reporting "pacing" or harassment helps police identify patterns of suspicious vehicle behavior in the area.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if they were trying to warn you about a vehicle fault (like a flat tyre) until you are in a safe, populated, and well-lit area.
- You do not need to review dashcam footage or check for vehicle damage while the other car is still in the vicinity.
- You do not need to decide whether to press charges or make a formal statement in the first few minutes of the encounter.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a high level of "fight or flight" stress when being paced or signaled by strangers in another vehicle. Prioritising your safety by refusing to stop and heading toward help is a standard and correct defensive driving response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active road harassment or following incident. Long-term reporting or legal action should be handled after reaching a secure location.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal or professional safety advice. Always follow the directions of emergency services operators. If you are in a location with no signal, continue driving toward a populated area.
