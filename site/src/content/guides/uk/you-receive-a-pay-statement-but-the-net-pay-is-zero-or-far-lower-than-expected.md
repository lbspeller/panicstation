# What to do if… you receive a pay statement but the net pay is zero or far lower than expected (UK)

## Short answer
Request written confirmation of the change and the specific rationale immediately, while stating that your original bullying report remains active. Preserve all evidence of your previous duties and avoid signing any permanent contract variations until you have received independent advice.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign or "walk out" in the immediate aftermath of the change.
- Do not send emotive or accusatory emails while in a state of shock.
- Do not accept verbal-only explanations for the move; they are difficult to verify later.
- Do not sign any "agreement to transfer" or "new job description" paperwork immediately.
- Do not delete any evidence of your prior work performance, shift patterns, or duties.
- Do not attempt to "work it out" privately with the individual you reported.
- Do not discuss the situation on public social media or internal group chats.

## What to do now
1. **Document the exact timeline of the change (10 minutes).**
   **Record the time, date, and method of communication (e.g., email, meeting, phone call) used to notify you of the move.**
   - Include the name of the person who delivered the news.
   - List the specific duties removed or the team you have been moved to.
   - Save copies of your schedule before and after the change.

2. **Request a formal written explanation for the change.**
   **Send a neutral email to HR or the relevant manager asking for the business rationale for the sudden move.**
   - Ask if the move is temporary or permanent.
   - Ask for the date when this decision will be reviewed.
   - Ask for confirmation that your pay, seniority, and contract terms remain unchanged.
   - Mention that ACAS guidance states a complainant should not typically be moved against their will as it can be perceived as a detriment.

3. **Clarify your immediate daily responsibilities.**
   **Ask for a written list of your new duties and who your interim supervisor will be.**
   - Request instructions on what to do with pending work from your previous role.
   - Ensure you know where you are expected to be physically or digitally for your next shift.

4. **Confirm the status of your bullying report.**
   **Ask in writing for an update on the progress of your initial complaint and who is investigating it.**
   - Reference the date you first made the report.
   - Ask for a copy of the company's grievance or bullying/harassment policy if you do not have it.
   - State clearly if you wish for the matter to be handled as a formal grievance.

5. **Identify if your report constitutes a protected act or disclosure.**
   **Determine if your report involved discrimination (linked to age, disability, sex, race, etc.) or sexual harassment.**
   - From April 2026, sexual harassment is explicitly a "qualifying disclosure" for whistleblowing protection.
   - If the move feels like a penalty for reporting these issues, it may constitute "victimisation" or "detriment" under the Equality Act 2010 or whistleblowing law.

6. **Exercise your right to be accompanied if called to a meeting.**
   **Inform the employer that you wish to have a trade union representative or a colleague present at any formal meeting regarding the move or your report.**
   - If you are a union member, contact your regional office or local rep immediately.

7. **Submit a Subject Access Request (SAR) if information is withheld.**
   **Request copies of internal emails or notes where your name or the move was discussed.**
   - Organizations must respond within one calendar month of receipt.
   - Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2026, you should follow the organization's internal complaint process first if they fail to provide the data.

8. **Contact ACAS for neutral guidance.**
   **Call the ACAS helpline to discuss the specific details of your transfer and the status of your complaint.**
   - They can provide guidance on whether the employer's actions align with the ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to take the matter to an Employment Tribunal.
- Filing a claim for constructive dismissal (this requires careful legal timing).
- Demanding a face-to-face apology from management.
- Applying for new jobs while the internal investigation is still in the early stages.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel targeted or "pushed out" when duties are removed following a report. While some employers use separation as a temporary safety measure, you have the right to ensure this measure does not disadvantage you. Taking a few minutes to request written clarity is a standard professional step, not an escalation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK employees facing sudden role changes after reporting bullying. It focuses on documentation and procedural rights and does not replace legal advice from an employment solicitor.

## Important note
Workplace rights in the UK are subject to specific eligibility criteria, including length of service and the nature of the complaint. Protections against detriment for whistleblowing or victimisation for discrimination apply from Day 1 of employment. If your mental health is severely affected, consult a medical professional.
