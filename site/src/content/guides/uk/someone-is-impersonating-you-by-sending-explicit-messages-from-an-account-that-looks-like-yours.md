# What to do if… someone is impersonating you by sending explicit messages from an account that looks like yours (UK)

## Short answer
Secure your primary accounts immediately, report the impersonating profile to the platform for removal, and avoid engaging with the perpetrator.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply to the impersonator or attempt to negotiate with them.
- Do not send copies of your ID or "real" photos to the impersonator to prove who you are.
- Do not post a public, emotional response or call-out while the situation is active.
- Do not delete your own accounts or clear all messages before securing a minimal record of the impersonating account.
- Do not blame yourself; these tactics are designed to cause distress and provoke a panicked reaction.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe digital space and pause (5 minutes).** If you believe you are in physical danger or being followed, call **999** immediately; otherwise, stop looking at the messages for a moment to allow your heart rate to steady.
2. **Secure your real accounts and identity (Lockdown).** 
   - **Change your email password first, as this is the primary recovery method for all other accounts.**
   - **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your social media and messaging accounts immediately.**
   - **Log out of all other sessions in your account settings to ensure no one else has access.**
3. **Report the impersonating account directly to the platform.**
   - **Use the specific "Impersonation" or "Pretending to be me" reporting tool provided by the app (e.g., Instagram, WhatsApp, X).**
   - **Ask 2–3 trusted friends to also report the account for impersonation to increase the likelihood of a rapid takedown.**
4. **Issue a neutral warning to your close contacts.**
   - **Send a short, factual message to your primary circles: "An account is currently impersonating me and sending explicit messages; please block and report it, and do not click any links it sends."**
   - **Avoid providing details about the content of the messages to prevent further spread of the explicit material.**
5. **Capture minimal evidence for reporting (only if you can tolerate it).**
   - **Take a screenshot of the impersonating account's profile page (showing the handle/username) and one example of the explicit messages.**
   - **Note the URL of the fake profile and the date and time you discovered it.**
6. **Access specialist support for intimate image issues.**
   - **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline (0345 6000 459, Mon–Fri 10am–4pm) if any sexual images or videos are involved.**
   - **Use the StopNCII.org tool to proactively "hash" and block the spread of specific intimate images across major platforms.**
7. **Report the incident to the appropriate UK authorities.**
   - **If you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report the cyber-impersonation to Report Fraud (the service replacing Action Fraud) online or by calling 0300 123 2040.**
   - **If you are in Scotland, report the harassment to Police Scotland by calling 101.**
   - **Report any direct threats of physical harm or extortion (sextortion) immediately via 999 (emergency) or 101 (non-emergency).**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify who is behind the account today.
- You do not need to explain the situation to every person you have ever met.
- You do not need to look at every single message the impersonator has sent to others.
- You do not need to decide on long-term legal action in the next few hours.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of violation and panic you are experiencing is a standard biological response to identity theft and sexualised harassment. This situation is an attack on your digital boundary, not a reflection of your character. By reporting and securing your accounts, you are already reclaiming control.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital impersonation and harassment. It does not cover long-term reputation management or detailed legal proceedings.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you are under 18, please speak to a parent, teacher, or contact Childline at 0800 1111.
