# What to do if… your bonnet or hood release fails and you cannot check under the hood after a warning (UK)

## Short answer
Treat any dashboard warning as an immediate hazard: stop the vehicle in a safe location, switch off the engine, and contact a breakdown service rather than attempting to force the latch.

## Do not do these things
- Don't keep driving if you have a red warning light or an amber light for oil / temperature / charging.
- Don't attempt to pry the bonnet open with a screwdriver or crowbar.
- Don't pull the release cable with pliers while standing near moving traffic.
- Don't stand between your vehicle and oncoming traffic at any time.
- Don't attempt to touch the radiator or coolant caps if the engine is hot / steaming.
- Don't stop in a live lane to investigate a warning light unless the car is unable to move.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle to the safest possible location immediately.** 
   - If on a motorway, leave at the next exit or service area if the vehicle is still driveable. 
   - If you cannot exit the motorway, move to an emergency area (marked with orange SOS signs) or onto the hard shoulder.
2. **Position the vehicle for maximum safety before stopping.** 
   - Pull as far to the left as possible, ideally onto the verge if safe.
   - **Turn your steering wheel fully to the left** so that the vehicle will roll away from traffic if it is struck from behind.
3. **Switch on your hazard warning lights and turn off the engine.** This prevents further mechanical damage if the warning relates to oil pressure or overheating.
4. **Exit the vehicle via the left-hand doors only.** 
   - Ensure all passengers exit on the side away from traffic.
   - Move well away from the road, ideally behind a safety barrier or up an embankment.
5. **Check for external signs of danger from a distance.** Look for smoke, visible flames, or fluids pooling under the engine bay. If you see fire or heavy smoke, move further away and call **999** immediately.
6. **Call for professional roadside assistance or the relevant road authority.** 
   - **England:** National Highways **0300 123 5000**
   - **Scotland:** Traffic Scotland **0800 028 1414**
   - **Wales:** Traffic Wales **0300 123 1213**
   - **Northern Ireland:** DfI Roads / Traffic NI (Use emergency roadside phones where provided or call **999** in an emergency).
7. **Clearly state the dual nature of the problem to the operator.** Use the phrase: "I have a [warning light / symptom] and my bonnet release has failed, so I cannot check the engine."
8. **Provide your exact location using nearby markers.** 
   - Use motorway marker posts (small boards at the side of the road with numbers).
   - Use the ID number of the emergency area if you have stopped in one.
   - Use a digital location app / pin if available on your phone.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose why the cable snapped or the latch jammed while at the roadside.
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical fault triggered by the warning light.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair garage right now.
- You do not need to attempt to "unstick" the bonnet; this is a task for a technician with the correct tools in a controlled environment.

## Important reassurance
Bonnet release failures are common mechanical issues often caused by cable stretch, corrosion, or latch misalignment. While it is frustrating to be unable to check your engine, the safest response is to treat the warning light as legitimate and wait for a professional to open the bonnet safely.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. Professional mechanical tools are required to open a failed bonnet latch, and a diagnostic scan may be needed to address the underlying warning light.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific instructions in your vehicle’s handbook. If you notice smoke, fire, or a strong smell of fuel, prioritise evacuation and emergency services over mechanical checks. Roadside repairs on high-speed roads are extremely hazardous and should only be performed by certified recovery technicians.
