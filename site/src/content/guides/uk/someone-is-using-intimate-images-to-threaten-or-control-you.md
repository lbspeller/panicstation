# What to do if… someone is using intimate images to threaten or control you (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, stop direct pressure where you can, and contact a specialist intimate image abuse service. Do not pay, send more images, or agree to demands to make it stop.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay, send more images, or agree to demands; it can increase pressure and risk.
- Don’t get pulled into long arguments, threats, or “prove it” exchanges.
- Don’t delete messages, call logs, voicemails, accounts, or report threads in a panic unless keeping them puts you in immediate danger.
- Don’t forward or share the images with anyone “for proof”.
- Don’t screenshot, store, or forward intimate images if there is any chance anyone shown is under 18.
- Don’t confront the person in person if you feel unsafe, trapped, monitored, or at risk of violence.
- Don’t try to handle every platform, person, or possible outcome at once.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause first.**  
   Move away from the person if you can do that safely. If you feel in immediate danger, call **999**. If it is not an emergency but you need police help, call **101** or use your local police force’s reporting route.

2. **Reduce the direct pressure.**  
   You are allowed to stop replying. If you need to send one message, keep it short, such as: “I can’t talk about this now.” Then mute, restrict, or block them if that is safe for you.

3. **Save only the key details that show what happened, if it is safe.**  
   You do not need perfect evidence to ask for help. If you can, save screenshots or exports of the threats, demands, usernames, phone numbers, profile links, dates, and times. Avoid saving the intimate images themselves.

4. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline if you are 18 or over.**  
   The Revenge Porn Helpline supports adults in the UK affected by intimate image abuse. They can help with practical next steps, reporting content, and takedown options.

5. **If you are under 18, or any person shown may be under 18, treat this as child safety.**  
   Do not store, forward, or share the images. Save only the threatening messages and account details if you can do that safely. Report online sexual abuse concerns through the CEOP Safety Centre. If an image or video of you is already online, Childline Report Remove may help you report it for removal.

6. **Report the threat or abuse on the platform, one place at a time.**  
   Use the in-app report option for non-consensual intimate images, threats, harassment, blackmail, or impersonation. Ask a trusted person to sit with you while you report if that helps, but do not send them the images.

7. **Use StopNCII.org if you are 18 or over and are worried adult intimate images may be shared.**  
   StopNCII.org can create a digital fingerprint of your image or video on your device so participating platforms can help detect and block matches. The image or video itself should not need to be uploaded to the site.

8. **Protect the accounts they could use against you.**  
   Change passwords for your email, messaging, cloud storage, and social accounts. Turn on two-factor authentication. Check for unknown logged-in devices and sign them out. If they know your phone PIN, change it.

9. **If this is happening in a relationship, by an ex, or alongside monitoring, threats, or isolation, treat it as abuse.**  
   Contact a domestic abuse support route, especially if you share a home, children, money, devices, or they know where you are. You do not have to decide about leaving or reporting before asking for support.

10. **If there has been sexual assault or sexual abuse, get specialist health support.**  
   A sexual assault referral centre can offer medical, practical, and emotional support. You can ask for help even if you are unsure what to call what happened.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to make a formal statement, pursue charges, take legal action, or tell everyone.
- You do not need to collect lots of material or write the perfect report right now.
- You do not need to check every website or platform at once.
- You do not need to make big relationship, housing, work, or family decisions while you are in the first panic wave.

## Important reassurance
This is a coercive tactic, and it can make people feel frightened, ashamed, frozen, or trapped. The shame belongs with the person using the images to control you, not with you.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation, reduce immediate risk, and connect you with specialist support. Later decisions about police reports, legal options, ongoing safety planning, or repeated takedowns may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe, prioritise immediate safety and urgent help.
