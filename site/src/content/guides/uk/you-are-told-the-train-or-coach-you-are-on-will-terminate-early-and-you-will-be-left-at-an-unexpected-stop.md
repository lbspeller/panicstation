# What to do if… you are told the train or coach you are on will terminate early and you will be left at an unexpected stop (UK)

## Short answer
Stay on the vehicle until you have confirmed the official onward travel plan, then move immediately to a staffed area or a well-lit Help Point to secure proof of the disruption for later claims.

## Do not do these things
- Do not exit the station or coach stop area until you have identified a safe waiting zone or spoken to staff.
- Do not immediately purchase a new ticket; your existing ticket is usually valid for alternative routes or operators during major disruption.
- Do not rely on verbal rumors from other passengers; seek information from official display boards, apps, or uniformed staff.
- Do not accept unlicensed taxi offers from individuals outside the station; use official ranks or operator-arranged transport.
- Do not discard physical tickets or delete digital booking QR codes, even if the service is cancelled.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact termination details before alighting.** 
   - Listen for the final onboard announcement or ask the driver/guard for the specific reason for termination and the "official alternative" being advised.
   - **Take a photograph of the onboard information screen or the platform departure board** showing the "Terminated" status or "Cancelled" notice.

2. **Identify the safest waiting area at the unexpected stop.** 
   - Move to a staffed ticket office or concourse; if the station is unstaffed, locate the yellow or green "Information / Emergency" Help Point on the platform.
   - **Text 61016 (British Transport Police)** if you feel unsafe or notice suspicious behavior, or call 999 if there is an immediate threat to life.

3. **Request the "onward travel" authorization from staff.** 
   - Ask station staff if "Ticket Acceptance" is in place with other operators or if a "Rail Replacement" bus has been ordered.
   - **Ask for a "disruption note" or an endorsement on your ticket** if a staff member advises you to take a route not normally permitted by your ticket type.

4. **Trigger the operator’s "Duty of Care" if you are stranded late at night.** 
   - State clearly to staff or via the Help Point: "I am stranded at this station due to your service termination and cannot complete my journey by rail tonight."
   - **Request that the operator arranges a taxi or overnight accommodation** if the terminated service was the last connection to your destination.

5. **Document all additional costs and times.** 
   - Keep physical receipts for any water, food (if delayed over 60 minutes), or essential transport you are forced to pay for.
   - **Note the exact time you arrived at the unexpected stop** and the time you eventually reached your final destination for Delay Repay calculations.

6. **Check refund eligibility before leaving the network.** 
   - If you choose not to complete your journey because the termination makes your trip pointless, you are entitled to a full refund from your original retailer.
   - **Submit your Delay Repay claim within 28 days** (noting that under 2026 rules, most claims should be processed through the platform where the ticket was purchased).

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or "Rail Ombudsman" case while still at the station.
- You do not need to calculate the exact percentage of your refund or compensation immediately.
- You do not need to engage in arguments with staff regarding the cause of the delay; focus only on the onward solution.

## Important reassurance
Sudden service terminations are physically and mentally jarring. It is common to feel a sense of urgency to "just get moving," but taking ten minutes to secure official information in a safe, well-lit area is the most effective way to regain control of the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passengers stranded by early service terminations on UK heavy rail or national coach networks. It does not cover local bus service cancellations, international travel (Eurostar), or long-term legal disputes regarding consequential loss.

## Important note
Rail and coach operators have different legal obligations; rail operators generally have a stronger "duty of care" to get you to your destination or provide accommodation under the National Rail Conditions of Travel. Coach operators often limit liability to a ticket refund or a seat on the next available service. Always prioritize your physical safety over the cost of a ticket. As of April 1, 2026, refund rules for non-disrupted travel have changed, but your rights during active service termination remain protected.
