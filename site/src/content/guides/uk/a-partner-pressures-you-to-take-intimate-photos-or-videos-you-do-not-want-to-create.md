# What to do if… a partner pressures you to take intimate photos or videos you do not want to create (UK)

## Short answer
You do not need to negotiate your consent: stop the interaction immediately, move to a safer location, and access a device your partner cannot monitor to contact a specialist service.

## Do not do these things
- Do not create or send any image "just to stop the pressure" or to prove your feelings.
- Do not allow a partner to film or photograph you "just for them" if you are uncomfortable.
- Do not hand over your phone, passcode, or cloud login details under pressure.
- Do not delete evidence of threats or pressure if you may want to report it later.
- Do not confront them if you feel it may escalate their behavior or put you in danger.
- Do not use a shared device or account to look for help if they might see the history.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause immediately.** End the conversation and move to a different room or a public space where you can think clearly.
   - If you cannot find safety at home, go to a designated "Safe Space" in a participating pharmacy (Boots/Superdrug) or bank (HSBC/NatWest) and ask a member of staff to use their Safe Space.
   - If you are in immediate danger of physical harm, call **999**.
2. **State your boundary once and stop engaging.** Use a clear, short phrase such as "I am not doing that; do not ask me again."
   - Do not provide reasons or enter a debate.
   - If they continue, put the phone away or leave the area.
3. **Use a safer device to access support.** If you suspect your partner monitors your phone or accounts, use a device they cannot access (e.g., a friend's phone or a computer in a library or Safe Space).
   - Use "Private" or "Incognito" browsing modes.
   - Log out of all accounts when finished.
4. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline.** This is the primary UK service for adults (18+) facing intimate image abuse or threats.
   - Call **0345 6000 459** (10:00–16:00, Monday–Friday).
   - They can provide advice on threats, intimidation, and the 2026 takedown procedures.
5. **Report to the police via the specialized online portal.** If you wish to report the pressure or threats, use the **National My Police Portal** specifically designed for intimate image abuse.
   - Threats to share and the creation of AI deepfakes without consent are criminal offences under the Crime and Policing Bill 2026 and the Data Act 2025.
   - Call **101** for non-emergencies if you cannot access the portal safely.
6. **Preserve evidence if it is safe to do so.** Save screenshots of the pressure, demands, or threats.
   - Include the date, time, and the platform used.
   - Keep this evidence in a "hidden" folder, a new password-protected email account, or send it to a trusted friend for safekeeping.
7. **Secure your digital accounts.** If it will not alert the partner and increase your risk, change your email and cloud passwords (e.g., iCloud, Google Photos).
   - Ensure "2-Step Verification" (2FA) is active.
   - Check if your device is currently sharing its location or photos with a partner's device.
8. **Under 18s must prioritize specialist child safety.** If you are under 18, contact **Childline** on **0800 1111** or use the "Report and Remove" service provided by the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the future of the relationship right now.
- You do not need to have "perfect" evidence before speaking to a helpline.
- You do not need to understand the technical details of how images are shared; specialists will help with this.
- You do not need to worry about the "48-hour takedown" yourself; helplines will assist in flagging content to platforms if something has already been shared.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel trapped, guilty, or afraid when a partner uses your relationship to pressure you into sexual acts or images. This behavior is a form of coercion and digital abuse; it is not your fault, and your refusal is a valid boundary that must be respected. Taking a moment to step away and seek help is a protective action, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK residents facing pressure or threats involving intimate images. Ongoing coercion often requires a comprehensive safety plan developed with domestic abuse or image-abuse specialists.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal or professional advice. UK laws (including the 2026 takedown mandates) apply to platforms and criminal conduct. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**. If you are under 18, seek support from a trusted adult or Childline immediately.
