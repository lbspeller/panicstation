# What to do if… you lose a paper boarding pass for a connection and you cannot access your booking on your phone (UK)

## Short answer
Follow the purple **Flight Connections** signs to reach your airline’s transfer desk or a self-service kiosk, where staff can reissue your boarding pass using only your passport and 2026 Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) details.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t exit through **Arrivals / Baggage Reclaim** unless specifically instructed by staff or if you are self-connecting on separate tickets.
- Don’t attempt to guess your booking reference (PNR) multiple times on a kiosk; this may temporarily lock the record.
- Don’t panic about a dead phone; airlines have digital records of all checked-in passengers and can verify your identity via passport.
- Don’t skip the **Flight Connections** desk to go straight to security unless you have a valid digital or paper pass.
- Don’t forget that as of February 2026, you must have a valid ETA or visa to transit most UK airports, even if staying airside.

## What to do now
1. **Follow the purple signs for Flight Connections immediately.** Stay within the airside transit area to avoid clearing immigration and security twice.
2. **Locate a departure screen to identify your airline and terminal.** Note the flight number and destination for your onward journey.
3. **Go to your airline’s Transfer Desk or a self-service kiosk.** 
   - Provide your passport to the agent.
   - Inform them that you are checked in but have lost your physical pass and cannot access the digital version.
   - If the desk is unmanned, use a kiosk and select **Print Boarding Pass** using your passport scan or last name and flight number.
4. **Confirm your 2026 Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) status with the agent.** Since February 25, 2026, the system may block boarding pass printing if your ETA is not linked to your passport or is unverified.
5. **Ask for help if you are directed toward transfer security without a pass.** At Heathrow, you may be permitted to pass through security using a printed itinerary or by showing your passport to staff, but this varies by terminal.
6. **Disclose any checked baggage to the transfer agent.** Verify that your bags are "checked through" to your final destination so you do not need to exit to landside to reclaim them.
7. **Check the printed pass for accuracy before leaving the desk.** Ensure the gate, terminal, and boarding time match the current departure screens.

## What can wait
- You do not need to charge your phone or find a Wi-Fi signal to board your flight.
- You do not need to retrieve your original booking email or PNR if you have your passport.
- You do not need to pay a "reissue fee" if you have already checked in online (most UK carriers, including Ryanair, waive this for lost/dead devices in 2026).
- You do not need to contact your travel insurance provider until you are safely at your final destination.

## Important reassurance
Losing a boarding pass mid-journey is a standard occurrence that airport systems are designed to handle. Your booking is a digital record tied to your identity; the paper or phone screen is merely a way to display it. As long as you have your passport and valid travel permissions (ETA/Visa), a new pass can be generated in seconds.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for passengers in transit at UK airports. It does not cover lost passports or situations where a passenger failed to check in before the deadline.

## Important note
Procedures for airside transit vary significantly between UK airports. Heathrow and Manchester offer dedicated airside routes, while smaller airports may require you to pass through Border Force. Always follow the specific directions of uniformed airport staff.
