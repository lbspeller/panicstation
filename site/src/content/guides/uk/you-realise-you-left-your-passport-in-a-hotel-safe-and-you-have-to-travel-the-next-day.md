# What to do if… you realise you left your passport in a hotel safe and you have to travel the next day (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the hotel duty manager immediately to secure the passport and arrange a same-day transfer. If physical retrieval today is impossible, apply online for a UK Emergency Travel Document (ETD), which costs £125 and typically takes at least two working days to process.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait until the morning of travel to alert the hotel; safe access often requires senior management or technical override.
- Do not report the passport as lost or stolen on the GOV.UK website until you are certain you cannot retrieve it; once reported, the passport is permanently cancelled and cannot be used even if found.
- Do not pay for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if you believe you can still recover the passport today, as payment usually triggers automatic cancellation of the original document.
- Do not assume a photograph, scan, or photocopy of your passport will be accepted for boarding or border control.
- Do not expect an airline to make an exception at the gate; carriers face heavy fines for boarding passengers without valid physical documents.

## What to do now
1. **Call the hotel front desk immediately and ask to speak with the duty manager.** 
   - State clearly that your passport is in the room safe.
   - Demand they use the "master safe procedure" (electronic override code or physical master key).
   - Confirm if they have found the passport and ask them to place it in a sealed envelope in the hotel’s central secure office.
2. **Arrange for the physical recovery of the document today.** 
   - If you are in the same city, return to the hotel in person with secondary ID (e.g., driving licence) and your booking confirmation.
   - If you are too far to return, authorise a trusted third party to collect it or hire a reputable same-day international courier with real-time tracking.
3. **Check the entry requirements for your destination and any transit countries.**
   - Verify if an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) is accepted at your destination.
   - Confirm if a specific visa is required for an ETD, as this often differs from standard passport requirements.
4. **Set a firm cutoff time for physical retrieval.**
   - If the passport is not physically in your hand or confirmed to be in transit by this time, you must initiate the backup plan.
5. **Apply for a UK Emergency Travel Document (ETD) online.**
   - You can only apply online if your last passport was issued on or after 1 January 2006.
   - You will need a digital passport photo, a contact number, and a credit or debit card to pay the £125 fee.
   - Note that an ETD is typically valid for a single journey through a maximum of five countries.
6. **Notify your airline or travel provider once you have an application reference or a retrieval plan.**
   - If you must use an ETD, ask the airline if they require specific details at check-in.
   - Be prepared to rebook or delay your flight if the document will not be ready in time (standard processing is 2 working days).
7. **Contact your travel insurance provider.**
   - Inform them of the situation and the potential for missed travel. 
   - Keep receipts for the ETD fee and any additional accommodation or transport costs for a potential claim.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a police report unless you suspect the passport was stolen or your insurance provider specifically requires a crime reference number for a claim.
- You do not need to apply for a full replacement passport until you have completed your current travel.
- You do not need to resolve disputes regarding hotel negligence or liability until you are back in the UK.

## Important reassurance
Losing access to a passport at the end of a trip is a common occurrence handled frequently by consular staff and hotel management. While it may require a change in travel dates, there is a clear, established process for obtaining emergency documentation to ensure you can return to the UK.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate 24-hour window following the discovery that a passport is left in a hotel safe abroad. It does not cover long-term passport replacement or detailed insurance litigation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional travel advice. Entry requirements and document acceptance are at the sole discretion of the destination country’s border authorities and the carrier's boarding policies.
