# What to do if… you report bullying and are immediately moved teams or taken off duties without explanation (UK)

## Short answer
Relocate to a safe, sheltered area nearby, then send a factual message to your manager and HR confirming you are on-site and awaiting written instructions regarding your access.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to tailgate through doors or bypass security barriers, as this may be classified as gross misconduct.
- Do not engage in a verbal confrontation with security or reception staff who are following instructions.
- Do not leave the vicinity without notifying your manager in writing of your exact location and availability.
- Do not post details of the situation on social media or internal messaging groups.
- Do not hand over personal devices or provide passwords for personal accounts if requested.
- Do not sign any documents acknowledging fault or agreeing to immediate terms while in a state of shock.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a sheltered and safe waiting location immediately.** If the weather is poor or the street feels unsafe, inform security that you are moving to a specific nearby public location (such as a lobby or cafe) where you will remain reachable by phone.
2. **Document the exact time and the specific words used by the person denying you entry.** Write down who gave the instruction, their job title, and whether they stated the issue was a technical failure or a management instruction.
3. **Send a brief, factual email or text to your line manager and HR department.** **State clearly that you have arrived for work, your badge is not functioning, and you have been directed by security to wait outside.**
4. **Ask for explicit written confirmation of your status for the remainder of the day.** **Request that they confirm if you are being formally suspended, if you are expected to remain on "standby" at your current location, or if you should return home.**
5. **Inquire about the retrieval of essential personal items if you are denied entry.** **If medication, house keys, or your wallet are inside, ask for a manager or security officer to escort you to collect these items or to bring them to the entrance for you.**
6. **Identify the nature of any immediate meeting if you are asked to speak with management.** **Ask if the meeting is an informal chat, a formal investigatory meeting, or a disciplinary hearing.**
   - By law, a disciplinary hearing cannot take place without prior written notice of the allegations and evidence.
   - You have a statutory right to be accompanied at a disciplinary hearing, but not at a purely investigatory meeting (though you may still request it).
7. **Clarify the terms of your pay if you are told you are being suspended.** **Ask for confirmation that the suspension is a "neutral act" and that it is on full contractual pay, as per ACAS 2026 guidelines.**
8. **Note the details of the Fair Work Agency (FWA) if pay is withheld or procedures are ignored.** **Keep a record of all correspondence in case you need to report a breach of employment rights to the FWA later.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide a formal statement or defense against allegations in the next few minutes.
- You do not need to decide whether to file a grievance or resign right now.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your colleagues or clients today.
- You do not need to search for a solicitor while still standing outside the building.

## Important reassurance
A deactivated badge is often the result of a technical synchronization error or an administrative oversight. If it is a deliberate management action, remember that suspension is legally considered a neutral act in the UK and does not mean a final decision has been made. Following the procedure and remaining professional protects your position.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate workplace access crisis. It does not cover long-term legal strategy, specific disciplinary outcomes, or the nuances of gross misconduct allegations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights in the UK are governed by the Employment Rights Act and ACAS Codes of Practice, which were updated in 2025 and 2026. If you are suspended or face disciplinary action, contact a trade union representative or an employment law specialist to review your specific contract and circumstances.
