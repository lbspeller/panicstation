# What to do if… your boiler or furnace is dripping water and the leak seems to be getting worse (UK)

## Short answer
Switch off the boiler and the electrical supply at the wall, then isolate the water supply if safe to do so while avoiding all wet electrical components.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch any electrical switches, plugs, or sockets if they are wet or if you are standing in water.
- Do not attempt to open the boiler casing or tighten any internal pipes or valves.
- Do not keep restarting the system to see if the leak stops or if the pressure holds.
- Do not use a hairdryer or heater to dry out the boiler or the area around it.
- Do not ignore signs of gas or carbon monoxide (CO) even if you believe the issue is "only" a water leak.

## What to do now
1. **Turn off the boiler using its own controls.** Set the heating and hot water to the "off" position or use the main power button on the front of the unit.
2. **Isolate the electrical supply to the boiler.** Locate the fused spur (the wall switch near the boiler) and flip it to the "off" position, but **only if the switch and your hands are completely dry**.
3. **Check for secondary hazards immediately.** If you smell gas, see soot marks, hear a carbon monoxide alarm, or feel a sudden headache, nausea, or dizziness:
   - **Open all doors and windows** to ventilate the area.
   - **Turn off the gas at the meter** (unless the meter is located in a cellar).
   - **Evacuate the property immediately** and ensure everyone moves to fresh air.
   - **Do not use any electrical switches**, phones, or doorbells while inside.
   - Call the **National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999** from outside the building.
   - Call **999 or go to A&E immediately** if anyone has symptoms of CO exposure.
4. **Contain the water to prevent property damage.** 
   - Place a bowl, tray, or bucket directly under the drip.
   - Use towels to mop up standing water, ensuring you stay away from any electrical cables or sockets.
5. **Shut off the water supply to the leak.**
   - If the leak is from a pipe leading to the boiler, locate your **main stopcock** (usually under the kitchen sink or near the water meter) and turn it clockwise until it stops.
   - If you can see a **filling loop** (a silver flexible hose) and the valves are open, turn the small handles so they are at a 90-degree angle to the pipe.
6. **Contact a qualified professional.**
   - **If you are a tenant:** Call your landlord or letting agent immediately to report an emergency repair for the heating and water system.
   - **If you are a homeowner:** Book a **Gas Safe registered engineer** and specify that the leak is worsening and affecting the system's stability.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify which specific internal part (e.g., heat exchanger, pump, or pressure relief valve) is failing.
- You do not need to clean the internal components of the boiler.
- You do not need to decide on a new boiler installation or long-term heating upgrades.

## Important reassurance
A worsening leak is a standard mechanical failure in pressurized systems. While it requires an urgent response to protect your floor and electrics, taking these steps to isolate the power and water effectively stabilizes the risk until a professional arrives.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. A Gas Safe registered engineer must perform all internal inspections, repairs, and safety checks.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace professional technical advice. If water has entered electrical components, do not restore power until an electrician has inspected the installation.
