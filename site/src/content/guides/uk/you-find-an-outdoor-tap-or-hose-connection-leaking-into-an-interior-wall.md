# What to do if… you find an outdoor tap or hose connection leaking into an interior wall (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the water supply immediately using the internal stopcock and treat all electrical outlets or switches on that wall as hazardous until inspected by a professional.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue using the outdoor tap or hose, even for a few seconds.
- Do not touch, plug in, or switch off any electrical appliances, sockets, or light switches located on the damp wall.
- Do not apply excessive force or use tools on a seized stopcock, as this can cause a pipe fracture.
- Do not peel back wallpaper or cut into plasterboard until the water and electricity are safely isolated.
- Do not attempt to "test" if the leak has stopped by turning the water back on before a repair is completed.

## What to do now
1. **Locate and close your internal stopcock immediately.**
   - **Turn the valve clockwise to stop the water flow.**
   - **Check under the kitchen sink, under the stairs, or in a utility cupboard for the valve.**
   - **Open a cold tap on the lowest floor to drain the remaining pressure from the system.**
2. **Isolate the electrical supply to the affected area.**
   - **Switch off the relevant circuit at the consumer unit (fuse box) if it is safe and dry to do so.**
   - **Do not approach the consumer unit if you see water dripping near it or if you must stand in a puddle to reach it.**
   - **Keep all people and pets away from the damp wall and any wet floor areas.**
3. **Locate the external stop tap if the internal valve is seized or ineffective.**
   - **Look for a small cover on the pavement or near your property boundary marked 'W' or 'Water'.**
   - **Use the external valve to shut off the supply if the leak continues after closing the internal valve.**
4. **Identify your reporting requirements based on your tenure.**
   - **Social housing tenants:** Contact your landlord immediately; under 2026 safety standards (Awaab's Law), emergency hazards must be addressed within 24 hours.
   - **Private tenants:** Notify your landlord or letting agent in writing immediately to establish their legal duty to repair the structure and exterior.
   - **Homeowners:** Contact a WaterSafe-approved plumber and a registered electrician if water has contacted any electrical fittings.
5. **Document the damage for insurance or maintenance records.**
   - **Take photographs of the outdoor tap fitting, the interior damp patch, and any bubbling plaster or wet flooring.**
   - **Note the exact time you discovered the leak and the time you isolated the water supply.**
6. **Protect floor coverings and furniture.**
   - **Place towels or containers at the base of the wall to catch dripping water or slow the spread across the floor.**
   - **Move furniture and electronic items away from the damp area, provided you can do so without touching wet electrical fittings.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault is a split pipe, a failed joint, or a faulty tap body right now.
- You do not need to begin drying procedures or use dehumidifiers until a professional confirms the electrical circuits are safe.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company's claims department in the first minutes; isolation and safety take priority.
- You do not need to decide on permanent wall repairs or redecoration today.

## Important reassurance
Internal wall leaks from outdoor taps often occur after a freeze-thaw cycle or due to high-pressure strain. Isolating the water and power immediately is the most effective way to prevent structural damage and ensures the situation is stabilized while you wait for a professional.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. It does not replace a professional inspection by a plumber or electrician, which is required to restore services safely.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not constitute professional plumbing, electrical, or legal advice. If water has entered electrical fittings or the consumer unit, do not attempt to restore power yourself.
