# What to do if… your bike lights will not turn on and you are worried about riding in the dark (UK)

## Short answer
Do not plan to ride after sunset without working cycle lights. Use the daylight you still have to fix the simple fault, buy or borrow lights, walk the bike, or switch to transport that does not depend on riding in the dark.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “chance it” because the road has streetlights.
- Do not ride while holding a phone torch; it reduces control, can dazzle, and is not a substitute for fitted legal cycle lights.
- Do not keep riding after sunset hoping the lights will come back on.
- Do not choose darker clothes, unlit back roads, or faster roads just to avoid a delay.
- Do not take apart wiring, battery cases, or mounts in traffic or at the kerb.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe place and do a 60-second check.**  
   Get off the carriageway, stop somewhere visible, and try the switch again. Check for a lock mode, low-power mode, or a button that needs holding down.

2. **Check the mount and the contacts.**  
   Reseat the light on its bracket, make sure it is facing the right way, and check that battery contacts or charging contacts are lined up.

3. **Check power in the simplest way available.**  
   For replaceable batteries, try fresh batteries if you have them and make sure they are the right way round. For USB lights, try a power bank, a different cable, or a nearby socket long enough to see whether a charging light appears.

4. **Dry and clean what you can without dismantling it.**  
   If it has been wet, wipe the outside, battery contacts, and terminals with a dry tissue or cloth. Remove grit, close the battery cover firmly, and try again.

5. **If you cannot get a white front light and red rear light working, make the no-dark-riding decision now.**  
   Between sunset and sunrise, cycles need a white front light, a red rear light, and a red rear reflector. Pedal reflector details vary within the UK, so check the official rules for where you are; do not use uncertainty about the details as a reason to ride without working lights.

6. **Use the remaining daylight to choose a safer way home.**  
   Walk the bike to a bus, train, tram, or other local transport stop; call a taxi or lift and say you have a bike; or go to a nearby shop where you can buy basic front and rear lights before continuing.

7. **If you continue briefly in full daylight, aim to be off the road before sunset.**  
   Choose a direct, familiar, lower-speed route. Avoid unlit lanes, fast roads, and any route that depends on arriving “just before” it gets dark.

8. **Add visibility you already have, but do not treat it as a replacement for lights.**  
   Put on reflective or light-coloured items, use reflective bands if you have them, and keep any reflector or light that still works clean and unobscured.

9. **If it is already getting dark, stop riding and move to a safer waiting point.**  
   Get off the carriageway and wait in a lit public place such as a station, shopfront, supermarket, or petrol station while you arrange transport or replacement lights.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact fault now.
- You do not need to choose the perfect new lighting system today.
- You do not need to decide whether the old light is repairable.
- You do not need to argue with yourself about how risky it is; treat missing lights before dark as a simple stop condition.

## Important reassurance
This is usually a small, fixable problem: a flat battery, loose mount, wet contact, or missed charge. Noticing before dark is useful because you still have safer options.

## Scope note
These are first steps to keep you visible and help you get home safely. Repairs, replacements, route planning, and any detailed legal questions can wait or may need specialist help later.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal or professional advice. Cycle lighting rules include technical requirements and can vary within the UK, so check the official resources for where you are and avoid riding after sunset unless your lights are working.
