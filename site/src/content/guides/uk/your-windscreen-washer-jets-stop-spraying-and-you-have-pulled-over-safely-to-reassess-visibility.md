# What to do if… your windscreen washer jets stop spraying and you have pulled over safely to reassess visibility (UK)

## Short answer
Do not continue driving if you cannot see the road clearly. If visibility cannot be restored immediately through safe means, remain in a secure location and call for professional breakdown assistance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive to the next junction if you are guessing at the position of lane lines or hazards.
- Do not keep running wipers on a dry, gritty windscreen, as this creates a permanent opaque smear.
- Do not attempt to repair or inspect washer jets while standing on a motorway hard shoulder or near live traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Do not use boiling water to thaw frozen jets or the windscreen, as the thermal shock can crack the glass.
- Do not exit the vehicle on the right-hand side (driver's side) when stopped on a motorway or busy A-road.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure your current location is as safe as possible.**
   - Switch on your hazard warning lights immediately.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, keep your sidelights on.
   - If you are on a motorway or dual carriageway, move as far to the left as possible with your wheels turned to the left.
2. **Follow the "Go Left, Get Safe, Get Help" protocol on motorways.**
   - **Go Left:** Pull into an Emergency Area, service station, or onto the Hard Shoulder.
   - **Get Safe:** If you can reach a place of relative safety, exit the vehicle via the left-hand (nearside) doors.
   - Wait behind the safety barrier, well away from the carriageway and the vehicle.
   - **Get Help:** Use a roadside emergency telephone or call National Highways on 0300 123 5000.
3. **Call 999 immediately if you are in a high-risk position.**
   - If you have stopped in a live traffic lane or cannot safely exit your vehicle on a motorway, stay inside with your seatbelt fastened and call 999.
4. **Assess if visibility meets legal safety standards.**
   - Look through the driver’s "swept area" (the part of the glass cleared by the wipers).
   - If dirt, salt, or smears prevent you from clearly seeing pedestrians, signs, or lane markings, the vehicle is legally unroadworthy.
5. **Check for simple causes only if in a non-motorway safe location.**
   - If you are in a car park or service station, check the washer fluid reservoir.
   - If the reservoir is empty, top it up with concentrated screenwash if available.
6. **Listen for the washer pump operation.**
   - Briefly pull the washer stalk and listen for a "whirring" sound from the front of the car.
   - **No sound:** This indicates a blown fuse or pump failure; do not attempt roadside electrical repairs.
   - **Sound but no spray:** This indicates a blockage, a disconnected hose, or frozen fluid.
7. **Address suspected freezing using the vehicle's heat.**
   - If temperatures are below freezing, run the engine and set the cabin heaters to the "windscreen/demist" setting at maximum temperature.
   - This may eventually thaw the fluid in the lines or nozzles, but this often takes 15–30 minutes and may not work while stationary in extreme cold.
8. **Contact professional recovery if the fault persists.**
   - If you cannot restore spray and the windscreen remains obscured, do not attempt to "limp" home.
   - Use your breakdown cover or the roadside emergency phone to arrange a recovery or mobile repair.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the failure is caused by a fuse, a motor, or a hose leak right now.
- You do not need to clean the entire car or the rear window if the front visibility is restored.
- You do not need to worry about MOT failure consequences until you are off the road and in a safe environment.

## Important reassurance
Sudden loss of visibility is a high-stress event, and pulling over immediately was the correct safety decision. This is a common mechanical or environmental issue that recovery services handle daily.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a visibility crisis. It focuses on immediate roadside safety and legal compliance regarding driver vision. Detailed mechanical diagnostics should be performed only once the vehicle is in a workshop or a permanent safe location.

## Important note
Under the Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations, it is an offence to drive with an obscured windscreen or non-functioning washers. If you cannot maintain a clear view of the road, you must not continue your journey. This information is for general guidance and does not replace the Highway Code or professional mechanical assessment.
