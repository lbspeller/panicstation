# What to do if… the only lift or elevator you need is out of service and you cannot reach the platform or exit (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a visible safe point and contact station staff immediately via the Help Point, ticket office, or gate-line to request an alternative step-free route or a free accessible taxi to your destination.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to use stairs, escalators, or manual lifts if doing so causes pain, fatigue, or a risk of falling.
- Do not enter tracks, staff-only tunnels, or emergency-only exits that lead onto live rails.
- Do not attempt to force, wedge, or manually reset lift doors or equipment.
- Do not follow mobile map directions that suggest non-step-free shortcuts or steep footbridges.
- Do not allow yourself to be pressured by moving crowds into attempting an unsafe route.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause point away from platform edges and moving machinery.** Choose a well-lit area near a Help Point, ticket office, or staff member where you are clearly visible.
2. **Use the closest intercom or Help Point if no staff are visible.** Press the "Information" or "Emergency" button and state: "The lift I need is out of service and I require step-free assistance to reach the [platform / exit]."
3. **State your specific assistance requirements clearly.** 
   - If you have pre-booked Passenger Assist, give your booking reference or name and train time.
   - If you have not booked, state your destination and that you cannot use stairs.
4. **Request the station’s alternative step-free plan.** 
   - Ask if there is a staff-only service lift or alternative street-level entrance you can be escorted through.
   - Ask for a "double-back" route (going one stop further to a station with working lifts and returning on the opposite platform) if this is safer.
5. **Ask staff to arrange an accessible taxi if no other step-free route exists.** Under National Rail and TfL Accessible Travel Policies, the operator must arrange alternative accessible transport (usually a taxi) at no extra cost to take you to the nearest accessible station or your destination.
6. **Ask the staff member to stay with you or provide a specific meeting point.** Do not move to a different area of the station until they have confirmed the plan for your taxi or escort.
7. **Note the time, station name, and lift ID number if a notice is posted.** This information is required if you need to claim "Delay Repay" or "Redress" for failed assistance later.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint or fill out "redress" forms while still in the station.
- You do not need to worry about the original validity of your ticket; staff will authorise travel on alternative routes or taxis.
- You do not need to prove the extent of your disability to justify needing the lift.

## Important reassurance
Lift failures are a known operational issue, and transport providers have mandatory legal obligations to assist you. It is the operator’s responsibility to find a solution, and you are entitled to request a taxi if the station is no longer accessible to you.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate physical stabilization and local navigation when a lift failure occurs. It does not cover long-term legal claims or non-emergency travel planning.

## Important note
This information is based on UK railway and London Underground accessibility standards as of 2026. Staffing levels and available facilities vary by station. If you are in immediate physical distress or trapped in a lift, use the internal lift alarm or call 999.
