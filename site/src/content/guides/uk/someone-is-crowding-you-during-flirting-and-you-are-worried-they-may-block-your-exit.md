# What to do if… someone is crowding you during flirting and you are worried they may block your exit (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately toward a staffed area or a group of people to create distance. If you cannot leave safely or feel in immediate danger, call **999** and use the **Silent Solution** (press 55) if you cannot speak.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t move to a more private area to "discuss" your boundaries (such as toilets, corridors, or outside).
- Don’t worry about social awkwardness, being "rude," or making a scene if you feel physically restricted.
- Don’t accept a "guided" exit where they hold your arm or back to lead you out of the building.
- Don’t agree to go to a car or a second location to "cool off" or end the conversation.
- Don’t stay to finish a drink or wait for a "polite" gap in the conversation.

## What to do now
1. **Reposition yourself to keep a clear line to the exit.** **Take one step back or to the side and place a physical object like a chair, barstool, or table between yourself and the other person.**
   - Ensure your back is never to a corner or a dead-end corridor.
   - Keep your eyes on the nearest staffed point or group of people.
2. **Move directly to a member of staff or security.** **Walk to the bar or entrance and state clearly that you need help leaving because someone is making you feel unsafe.**
   - You do not need to explain the history of the interaction.
   - Ask them to "Keep an eye on me while I get my things" or "Help me get to a taxi safely."
3. **Use the "Ask for Angela" code if the venue displays the poster.** **Approach the bar and ask for "Angela" to signal that you are in an uncomfortable situation and need assistance.**
   - Staff should take you to a non-public area to plan a safe exit.
   - They can help call a taxi or contact a friend without the other person seeing.
4. **Initiate an external safety anchor on your phone.** **Send a message to a trusted contact with your exact location and a statement that you are attempting to leave now.**
   - Share your live location if possible.
   - If it feels safer, call a friend and stay on the line until you have reached a transport point.
5. **Request a supported departure from the venue.** **Ask security or staff to walk you to your vehicle or wait with you at the taxi/transport point.**
   - Ask them to ensure the other person does not follow you out.
   - If the person tries to follow, return inside immediately and alert staff.
6. **Escalate to emergency services if your movement is physically blocked.** **Call 999 immediately if they will not let you move or if you feel a threat of violence.**
   - If you cannot speak, listen to the operator and press **55** when prompted (Silent Solution) to alert police that it is a genuine emergency.
7. **Prioritise distance over closure once you are away.** **Go to a well-lit, busy area or a friend’s house and do not respond to any messages or calls from the person.**
   - Do not agree to meet later to "explain" why you left.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight if you want to report the incident to the police or the venue management.
- You do not need to justify your feelings or prove that their behavior was "bad enough" to justify leaving.
- You do not need to worry about any items left behind that are not essential (such as a half-finished drink).
- You do not need to provide a "final word" or resolution to the social interaction.

## Important reassurance
Your physical intuition is a valid protective tool. Feeling a "need to get away" when someone closes your personal space or blocks your path is a normal response to a boundary violation. You have the absolute right to leave any situation that makes you feel uneasy, regardless of whether the other person intended to be threatening.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for exiting an uncomfortable or potentially dangerous social situation. It does not cover long-term reporting or legal action.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and is not a substitute for professional or legal advice. In any emergency, call **999**. For non-emergency reporting in the UK, you can call **101**. If you have experienced sexual harassment or assault and need support, you can contact the Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 support line at **0808 500 2222**.
