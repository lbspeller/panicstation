# What to do if… you receive repeated calls from an unknown number claiming they are outside your location and you cannot confirm it (UK)

## Short answer
End the call immediately, secure your physical environment, and call **999** if the caller claims to be at your location or if you feel in immediate danger. Do not interact with the caller or go outside to investigate the claim.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t open your door or go outside to "check" if someone is actually there.
- Don’t engage in conversation, argue, or try to reason with the caller.
- Don’t confirm any personal details, including whether you are alone or your exact floor/room number.
- Don’t follow any instructions from the caller, such as "come to the window" or "look at your porch."
- Don’t post about the incident on social media in real-time, as this can reveal your location and vulnerability.
- Don’t assume the caller ID is accurate; numbers are easily spoofed to look local or "unknown."

## What to do now
1. **End the call and silence your phone immediately.** Let any further calls from unknown or withheld numbers go to voicemail without answering.
   - If using a smartphone, enable "Silence Unknown Callers" or "Do Not Disturb" (allowing only your contacts) to prevent further intimidation.
2. **Secure all entrances to your current location.** Lock all doors and close/lock all windows, including those on upper floors.
   - Draw curtains or blinds to prevent anyone outside from seeing your movements or confirming if you are alone.
3. **Move to a central or upper-level room.** Stay away from windows and doors where you might be visible from the street or communal hallways.
4. **If you believe someone is actually outside or the caller is making direct threats: call 999.** Tell the operator you are receiving threatening calls from someone claiming to be outside your address.
   - Provide your exact location first so help can be dispatched even if the line is cut.
5. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely when calling 999 from a mobile.** If you are in a situation where making noise would put you at risk:
   - Dial **999**.
   - Listen to the operator's questions.
   - If you cannot speak, cough or tap the handset if possible.
   - Press **55** when prompted by the automated system to confirm it is a genuine emergency. This will transfer you to the police.
   - Note: This system does **not** apply to landlines; on a landline, staying silent or making background noise will usually trigger a police transfer automatically as "doubt exists."
6. **Check cameras or peepholes only if it does not expose you.** Use a smart doorbell or security camera feed on your phone if available. Do not look out of windows if you have to move curtains or stand in a lit area.
7. **Contact a trusted neighbor or friend.** Text or call someone nearby to inform them of the situation and ask them to stay on the line with you.
8. **Report the incident to your phone provider's nuisance call team.** Once you are in a safe pause, use their official reporting channels to log the malicious communication.

## What can wait
You do not need to deal with these things right now:
- Deciding whether to change your phone number permanently.
- Installing new security systems or additional locks.
- Reviewing your social media privacy settings.
- Making a formal complaint to Ofcom or other regulatory bodies.

## Important reassurance
Threatening calls claiming the caller is "just outside" are a common tactic used to cause immediate distress and trick you into revealing your location or opening your door. Taking physical security precautions and involving the police is a standard and appropriate response to this type of intimidation. Your priority is your physical safety, not verifying the caller's honesty.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate threat or intimidation attempt via telephone. It does not cover long-term stalking investigations or legal proceedings.

## Important note
If you feel your safety is at risk or you suspect a physical intruder, always contact the emergency services on **999**. This guide is for informational purposes and does not replace professional police or security advice.
