# What to do if… you have sudden worsening pain, redness, or discharge after a recent procedure or dental work (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your treating surgical team, clinic, or dentist immediately for a same-day assessment, as these are primary signs of a surgical site or dental infection. Call **999** if you experience difficulty breathing, throat tightness, or rapidly spreading facial swelling.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for symptoms to improve if they are actively worsening or spreading.
- Do not touch, squeeze, or attempt to drain pus from the wound or gum area.
- Do not apply unprescribed creams, undiluted antiseptics, or household chemicals to the site.
- Do not take any antibiotics that were not specifically prescribed for this current issue.
- Do not remove dressings, tapes, or stitches unless instructed by your clinical team.
- Do not drive yourself to a medical facility if you feel dizzy, confused, or have restricted vision from swelling.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate life-threat red flags that require 999.** **Call 999 or go to A&E immediately if you have trouble breathing, difficulty swallowing, drooling, or a sensation of the throat closing.**
   - Also act if you have rapidly increasing swelling of the neck or face.
   - Seek emergency help for new confusion, slurred speech, or blue / pale / blotchy skin.
2. **Contact your primary treating team for urgent clinical advice.** **If you had a medical or surgical procedure, call the post-operative contact number provided in your discharge paperwork (such as the ward, specialist nurse, or on-call registrar).**
   - If the issue is dental, contact your dental practice; note that as of 2026, all NHS high street dentists are required to offer minimum urgent appointment slots.
   - If you cannot reach your treating team or dentist, use **NHS 111** (online, app, or phone) for urgent triage and direct booking into emergency slots.
3. **Prepare a concise summary of your symptoms before calling.** **Have your procedure date, current temperature, and a description of the site (redness, heat, smell, or colour of discharge) ready to report.**
   - Mention if the redness is spreading away from the original cut.
   - State clearly if you have systemic symptoms like shaking, chills, or a high fever.
4. **Isolate and protect the affected area without interfering with it.** **Keep the site clean and dry, following your specific aftercare instructions exactly until you are seen by a professional.**
   - Wash your hands thoroughly before and after checking any dressings.
   - If a dressing is soaked through with blood or fluid, follow the specific replacement steps provided by your hospital.
5. **Lower your threshold for seeking help if you are in a high-risk group.** **Seek same-day medical attention if you have a weakened immune system, diabetes, a heart valve replacement, or if the procedure involved a permanent implant.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific type of bacteria or "prove" it is an infection before calling.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of urgent dental assessment if you have a valid exemption; triage must happen regardless.
- You do not need to wait for a GP appointment if your surgical team or 111 provides a faster route to assessment.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when a recovery does not go as expected, but noticing these changes early allows for much more effective treatment. Medical teams expect "post-op" calls and would rather assess a minor issue than treat a late-stage infection.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and triage guidance only. It does not replace the need for an in-person clinical examination, which is required to prescribe antibiotics or perform wound care.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute medical diagnosis. If you suspect sepsis or a severe allergic reaction, or if your condition deteriorates while waiting for a callback, use **999** immediately.
