# What to do if… your rideshare requests keep getting cancelled or timing out and you are stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a safer, well-lit place with staff or other people, then switch away from repeated app attempts and use a licensed taxi, a pre-booked private hire vehicle or minicab, or the first safe public transport route. If someone is following, threatening, or putting you in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get into a private hire vehicle or minicab that you have not pre-booked through a licensed operator, app, phone line, or office.
- Do not accept a lift from a stranger, a driver who approaches you, or a car that looks official but does not match a booking.
- Do not keep walking around for signal or a better pickup point if it makes you more isolated.
- Do not announce your full home address where other people can hear it if you feel uneasy.
- Do not let your phone run down on retries, maps, video, or scrolling while you still need it for calls, payment, or directions.
- Do not stay at a dark or awkward pickup spot if you can move to a staffed place, taxi rank, station entrance, hotel entrance, or main road.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause point first.**  
   Step into a staffed place if you can, such as a station concourse, hotel lobby, late-night shop, petrol station kiosk, venue entrance, or transport hub. If you cannot go inside, stand somewhere lit, visible, and near other people.

2. **Make your pickup point easy and specific.**  
   Use a clear landmark rather than a vague pin: a named hotel entrance, station entrance, taxi rank, main road corner, shopfront, or marked pickup area. Avoid side alleys, restricted entrances, and places where a car cannot safely stop.

3. **Stop repeating the same failed rideshare request.**  
   Choose one licensed fallback now:
   - Go to a nearby taxi rank or hail a licensed taxi where this is permitted.
   - Pre-book a private hire vehicle or minicab through a licensed operator, app, phone line, or office.
   - Check live public transport and take the first safe route that gets you closer to home or to a safer interchange.
   - Ask staff at a venue, hotel, station, or shop to help you call a licensed taxi or private hire operator if your phone signal, battery, or app is failing.

4. **If you are in London, avoid touts and unbooked minicabs.**  
   Do not use anyone offering an unbooked ride, even if the vehicle has stickers or the driver says they are licensed. Use a licensed taxi you hail or find at a rank, or a private hire vehicle or minicab you have pre-booked.

5. **If you are outside London, use local licensed options.**  
   Taxi and private hire licensing is handled by local licensing authorities. Use a taxi rank, a known local taxi or private hire operator, a venue-recommended licensed operator, or official local taxi licensing information if you can check it quickly.

6. **Before you get in, check the vehicle is the right one.**  
   For a booked private hire vehicle or minicab, check the registration, driver details, and booking confirmation against the car and driver. If the details do not match, do not get in. For a taxi, use a licensed taxi from a rank or one that is lawfully available for hire in that area, and check for the usual licence plate, sign, or driver badge where displayed.

7. **Reduce avoidable cancellations.**  
   Stand at the pickup point before confirming. Add a short note such as “main entrance under lights, wearing blue coat.” If payment is failing, change payment method if you can, or book by phone with a licensed taxi or private hire operator.

8. **Bring in another person while you arrange transport.**  
   Call or message someone you trust and tell them where you are and what option you are trying next. Share your live location if you can. If you are in a venue or staffed place, ask to wait inside while you book.

9. **Preserve phone battery.**  
   Turn on low power mode, dim the screen, close battery-heavy apps, and keep enough charge for calls, location sharing, payment, and showing booking details. If staff are nearby, ask whether you can briefly charge your phone or use their phone to call a licensed operator.

10. **If this becomes a safety problem, treat it as safety first.**  
   Go back inside or move toward staff, a busy transport hub, or a lit public place. If a crime is in progress or someone is in immediate danger, call 999. If it is not an emergency but you need police help or advice, call 101.

11. **If transport is not coming soon, choose a holding plan.**  
   Stay in a staffed public place, return to a busy transport hub, wait in a hotel lobby where staff allow it, or go back to a venue you just left. The aim is to wait somewhere safer until you can pre-book a licensed ride or use public transport.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the cheapest route right now; prioritise being somewhere safer and getting moving.
- You do not need to keep trying the same app if it is failing; switching method is enough.
- You do not need to decide whether to complain, request a refund, or review the driver until you are home or somewhere safe.
- You do not need to explain the whole situation to everyone nearby; give only the details needed to get help or book transport.

## Important reassurance
Repeated timeouts and cancellations can happen for ordinary reasons such as demand, signal, app problems, payment issues, or difficult pickup locations. It does not mean you have failed; the next useful move is to get visible, use licensed options, and stop relying on one failing route.

## Scope note
These are first steps for getting safer and moving. Later decisions, such as complaints, refunds, reporting an unlicensed driver, or planning backup routes, can wait and may need help from the operator, transport provider, local licensing authority, TfL in London, police, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you feel in danger or threatened, prioritise immediate safety and contact emergency services.
