# What to do if… you are locked out of your email or cloud storage while traveling (UK)

## Short answer
Work on two tracks at once: start the official recovery route for the account that unlocks your documents, and separately contact each airline, hotel or travel provider to reconstruct the bookings from your name, dates and payment details.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use password-reset links from unexpected emails, texts or calls; open the provider’s official app or type the provider’s website yourself.
- Don’t keep guessing passwords until you trigger a longer lockout.
- Don’t read one-time codes aloud to anyone, and don’t let a stranger take control of your phone.
- Don’t factory reset or wipe your device unless you are sure you can sign back in afterwards.
- Don’t assume you must have your passport scan to move forward; many bookings can be found another way.

## What to do now
1. **Secure what you still have.** Put your passport, payment cards and phone somewhere secure on your person. If you need to enter passwords, avoid doing it over an untrusted public connection where possible.
2. **Name the failure before trying fixes.** Check whether the problem is:
   - password or username not working,
   - 2-step verification codes not arriving,
   - a lost phone, changed SIM or new device,
   - or possible account takeover, such as unexpected alerts, a changed password or unfamiliar devices.
3. **Start official recovery for the one account that unlocks the most.** Pick the email or cloud account that contains the passport scan and booking emails, then follow its official flow end-to-end:
   - **Google/Gmail/Drive:** use Google’s “Can’t sign in” account help and select the issue that matches what is happening.
   - **Microsoft/Outlook/OneDrive:** use Microsoft’s sign-in helper, verification-code help or account recovery guidance.
   - **Apple/iCloud:** try a trusted Apple device first. If that is not possible, use Apple’s official password reset or Apple Account recovery steps.
4. **Rebuild the urgent bookings from the outside.**
   - **Airline:** use the airline website or app to find, manage or resend the booking. If you do not have the booking reference, ask the airline to search using your full name, route, travel date and the card used to pay.
   - **Accommodation:** call or message the hotel or property using its official website or phone number. Give your name and dates, then ask them to confirm the booking and resend details to an email address you can reach.
   - **Rail, coach or ferry:** use the operator’s retrieve-booking tool or support route with your name, travel date and payment details.
5. **Use your bank app to find payment clues.** Note the merchant name, amount and payment date for each booking. This may help the provider find your reservation even without the original email.
6. **Create a temporary reachable inbox if you have no working email.** Use it only for re-sent confirmations and recovery updates for now. Do not make it your permanent recovery email until you can secure it properly later.
7. **If you suspect account takeover, treat it as a security problem first.** Use only official recovery pages, change the password when you regain access, sign out of unfamiliar sessions where the provider gives that option, and check the recovery phone and recovery email.
8. **If your UK passport is lost, stolen or unusable abroad, use GOV.UK.** Cancel a lost or stolen passport through GOV.UK. If you need urgent travel and cannot use a valid UK passport, check GOV.UK for whether you can apply for a UK Emergency Travel Document from your location.
9. **Before applying for an Emergency Travel Document, check the route.** Check whether it will be accepted by the countries you need to leave, enter or travel through, including transit countries.
10. **Make an offline minimum pack.** On paper or in an offline note, write your booking references, ticket numbers, hotel address, provider phone numbers, insurer emergency contact and the email address you can currently reach.

## What can wait
- You do not need to reorganise your whole document archive now.
- You do not need to rebuild your long-term security setup today.
- You do not need to decide about police reports unless there was theft, or your insurer, travel provider or local situation requires it.
- You do not need to solve every booking at once; start with the next journey or next place to sleep.

## Important reassurance
Being locked out while travelling is stressful, but it does not automatically mean the trip is lost. Airlines, hotels and other providers can often find bookings from your name, dates and payment details, even when the original confirmation email is inaccessible.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you regain access, reconstruct urgent bookings and avoid avoidable mistakes. Later decisions may need specialist account-security help, insurer support, travel-provider help or consular guidance.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, security, travel, consular, medical, therapeutic or other professional advice. Requirements vary by provider and by the country you are in, so rely on official recovery pages, official travel-provider routes and GOV.UK guidance for your situation.
