# What to do if… you spot a major printing error on your passport details page shortly before travel (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the HM Passport Office Adviceline immediately on 0300 222 0000. If the error was made by the passport office, you are entitled to a free ("gratis") urgent replacement, but you must report it within 28 days of receiving the document.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to travel with a passport where the name, date of birth, or gender does not match your booking or visa.
- Do not pay for a new passport application or an urgent appointment until you have spoken to the Adviceline; you may be eligible for a free replacement.
- Do not write on, use correction fluid, or attempt to "fix" any text on the passport details page.
- Do not use the standard online renewal service if your travel is within 2 weeks, as this may not trigger the urgent fault-replacement process.
- Do not assume a "minor" typo (like one letter in a middle name) will be ignored by border control or airlines.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the mismatch against your travel documents (60 seconds):** Check the spelling of your name, your date of birth, gender, and nationality against your airline booking, visas, and Electronic Travel Authorisations (ETA). 
   - Note exactly which characters are incorrect to describe them to the agent.
2. **Call the Passport Adviceline on 0300 222 0000 immediately:** Use the phone line rather than the web enquiry form for travel within 14 days.
   - The line is open 8:00 to 20:00 Monday to Friday, and 9:00 to 17:30 on weekends and bank holidays.
   - From outside the UK, call +44 (0)300 222 0000.
3. **Request a "Digital Gratis" replacement link for an observation error:** If the error was made by HMPO, inform the agent you need an urgent replacement for a faulty document.
   - If travel is within 2 weeks, ask for the "Urgent Gratis" log to be updated so a counter manager can contact you for an immediate appointment.
4. **Prepare evidence of your upcoming travel:** Have your flight or ferry booking confirmation ready to email or present. 
   - HMPO requires proof of travel (typically within the next 2 weeks) to bypass standard processing queues for fault corrections.
5. **Wait for the specific application link or appointment confirmation:** Do not book a standard Premium or Fast Track appointment unless instructed, as these require you to pay a fee that you should not owe for an HMPO error.
6. **Return the faulty passport only as directed:** You will usually be asked to send the faulty passport back or bring it to a regional passport office.
   - Use a tracked delivery service (e.g., Royal Mail Special Delivery) if posting.
7. **Notify your airline once the replacement path is confirmed:** If you are issued a new passport, your passport number will change. 
   - Contact your airline or travel agent to update your Advance Passenger Information (API) and check if your visa/ETA needs to be re-linked to the new document.

## What can wait
- Claiming back postage costs or travel expenses caused by the delay.
- Filing a formal complaint about the error; focus on getting the document first.
- Updating non-travel IDs (like driving licences) if they are not needed for your immediate trip.
- Changing hotel or activity bookings until the delivery date of the new passport is confirmed.

## Important reassurance
HM Passport Office has established protocols (the "Gratis" system) specifically for printing errors. If you have imminent travel and the error is theirs, they can often prioritise a replacement within 24 to 48 hours once you are in the "Urgent" workflow. Errors are handled differently than standard renewals to ensure you are not penalised for their mistake.

## Scope note
This guide covers the first steps for UK citizens who have received a faulty passport from HMPO. It does not cover lost/stolen passports or errors made by the applicant during the initial application.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Document acceptance is ultimately at the discretion of the airline and the border authorities of your destination country.
