# What to do if… someone is trying to isolate you from friends or coworkers during a night out and you feel unsafe (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately into a well-lit, staffed area such as the bar or security desk and inform staff you feel unsafe. If you are in immediate danger or believe a crime is occurring, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to go to a "quieter area," outside, or to a vehicle with the person to resolve the situation.
- Do not let the person hold your phone, keys, or coat under any pretext.
- Do not worry about "causing a scene" or being impolite if you need to walk away mid-conversation.
- Do not accept a lift or a taxi arranged by the person who is making you feel unsafe.
- Do not go to the toilets or a smoking area alone if you feel you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed or crowded area immediately without explaining yourself.** Walk directly to the bar, the cloakroom, or toward door security. If possible, place a physical barrier like a counter or table between yourself and the person following you.
2. **Alert venue staff or security that you feel unsafe or are being followed.** Say: "I don't feel safe, this person is following/isolating me."
   - If the venue displays "Ask for Angela" posters, you can go to the bar and ask for "Angela" to discreetly signal you need an exit strategy.
3. **Use your phone to contact your group while standing next to staff.** Send a clear message to your friends or coworkers: "I feel unsafe, come to the [specific location, e.g., main bar] now."
   - If your phone is dead, ask staff to use their phone to call your friends or a licensed taxi.
4. **Request a safe escort if you need to leave the venue.** Ask security to watch the person while you are escorted to a pre-booked licensed taxi or your friends' location.
   - Do not wait for transport outside on the street alone.
5. **Call 999 if you believe you are in immediate danger.** If you cannot speak out loud on your mobile, listen to the operator and then press 55 when prompted by the automated system.
   - This "Silent Solution" confirms your emergency is genuine and will transfer the call to the police.
6. **Seek medical help immediately if you feel suddenly unwell or unusually intoxicated.** Tell staff or your friends if you experience:
   - Sudden confusion or disorientation
   - Nausea or vomiting
   - Loss of coordination or blurred vision
   - Hallucinations or paranoia
   - If symptoms are severe, call 999; for urgent medical advice that is not a life-threatening emergency, call 111.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal police report right now.
- You do not need to figure out how you got separated from your group until you are in a safe location.
- You do not need to justify your feelings of unease to the person involved or to bystanders.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a "gut instinct" when someone is trying to isolate you. Moving toward staff and crowds is a proactive safety measure, and venue workers are trained to assist in these exact situations. Taking action to protect yourself is not "being dramatic."

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate personal safety in a nightlife environment. Long-term actions, such as reporting incidents to the police or venue management, should occur after you are in a secure location with trusted people.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. If you are in immediate danger, prioritize reaching emergency services via 999.
