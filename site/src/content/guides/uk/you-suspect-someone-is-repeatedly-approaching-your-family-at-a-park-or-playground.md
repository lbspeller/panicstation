# What to do if… you suspect someone is repeatedly approaching your family at a park or playground (UK)

## Short answer
Move your family towards other adults, staff, or a public exit, and leave the area if you can. If you feel in immediate danger, the person blocks you, threatens you, tries to separate a child from you, or a crime seems to be happening now, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay put to avoid making a scene if your gut says it is unsafe.
- Do not let your children move out of arm’s reach while you decide what to do.
- Do not confront the person alone, follow them, or try to physically block them.
- Do not argue, justify yourself, or give personal details such as names, school, routines, or where you live.
- Do not post publicly in real time, because it can reveal your location.
- Do not assume you need proof before you can ask for help or report repeated unwanted behaviour.
- Do not delay leaving in order to record, photograph, or investigate the person.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance immediately.** Gather your children close, pick up only what you need, and move towards a busier area such as a café, park office, entrance, staffed building, or a cluster of other adults. If you can, leave the park and go somewhere public and staffed.
2. **Keep children inside your body line.** Put yourself between the person and your children. Hold hands, keep a buggy on your exit side, and use a calm, firm instruction such as “stand right next to me now.”
3. **Use one clear boundary if it is safe.** In a normal voice, say: “Please stop approaching us. Do not speak to my children.” Then stop engaging and keep moving. The aim is to make your boundary clear, not to persuade the person.
4. **Bring another adult into it.** Approach a nearby parent, group, café worker, park staff member, warden, or security staff and say: “I do not feel safe. Can you stay with us while we leave or while I call the police?”
5. **Choose the safest police route.**
   - **Call 999** if you feel in immediate danger, the person is escalating, will not let you leave, is trying to separate a child from you, is making threats, or you think a crime is happening now.
   - **Call 101 or report online** if you are safe now but the behaviour is repeated, targeted, or concerning and you want advice or to make a report.
   - **If you cannot safely talk**, call 999, stay on the line, and follow the operator’s instructions.
6. **Note details only once you are moving away or safe.** Quickly note the time, exact location, what the person did or said, a description, direction of travel, and any visible vehicle details such as colour, make, model, or registration. If it is clearly safe, you may record or take a photo from a distance, but do not slow your exit or do anything that could escalate the situation.
7. **Ask staff for practical help if the park has staff or CCTV.** Ask them to walk with you to the exit, note the incident in their log, and tell you whether CCTV covers the area and how footage can be preserved or requested.
8. **After you are away, write a short incident log.** Write 5–10 lines: what happened, where, when, who was present, what you said, what the person did next, and whether anyone else saw it. If it happens again, this helps you describe the pattern clearly.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether this legally counts as stalking or harassment.
- Working out who the person is or why they are doing it.
- Writing a long statement.
- Searching laws online.
- Posting warnings on local groups.
- Confronting the person publicly.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to treat repeated unwanted approaches around your family as a safety issue. You are allowed to move away, involve other adults, and contact the police even if you are not certain what the person intends.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours: create distance, get support, and make a clear report if needed. If the behaviour continues over days or weeks, later decisions may need specialist safety or legal help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you or your children are in immediate danger, call 999.
