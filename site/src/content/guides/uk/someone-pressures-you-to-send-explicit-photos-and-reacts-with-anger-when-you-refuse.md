# What to do if… someone pressures you to send explicit photos and reacts with anger when you refuse (UK)

## Short answer
Stop replying, do not send anything, and move yourself into a safer pause: block or mute them, tighten privacy, and tell one trusted person what is happening. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send a “small” or “safer” photo to calm them down.
- Don’t negotiate, argue, or keep explaining once they have reacted with anger.
- Don’t pay money, send gifts, or offer favours if they turn this into blackmail.
- Don’t meet them in person to sort it out if they have used anger, pressure, or threats.
- Don’t delete everything in a panic if you might want help later.
- Don’t keep checking every app repeatedly; that can make panic worse.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safety buffer first.** If they might come to your home, workplace, school, or another place where you are, move near other people or contact someone you trust. Call **999** if there is immediate danger.

2. **Stop the conversation cleanly.** You do not have to answer. If it feels safer to send one final message, keep it short: “I’m not sending anything. Do not contact me again.” Then stop replying.

3. **Block or mute them on the apps they are using.** If blocking may make them escalate, mute or restrict them first so you can stop seeing messages while keeping a record.

4. **Lock down what they can access.**
   - Set social accounts to private for now.
   - Remove them from followers, friends, close friends, shared albums, and group chats where possible.
   - Turn off location sharing, live location, Snap Map, “active status”, and “last seen”.
   - Limit who can message, tag, mention, or add you to groups.
   - Change passwords if they know them or might have access to your accounts.

5. **Save a small amount of evidence only if you can do it safely.** Take screenshots showing their username or phone number, the demand for explicit photos, the angry or threatening messages, and any threat to share images. Save them somewhere they cannot access.

6. **Tell one steady person now.** Choose a friend, sibling, housemate, parent, teacher, manager, or another trusted person. Tell them who is pressuring you, what platform it is on, whether you fear escalation, and whether you need company or help blocking/reporting.

7. **Report the account on the platform.** Use the platform’s report tools for harassment, threats, sexual coercion, or intimate image abuse. Report any fake, backup, or new accounts they use to keep contacting you.

8. **Use the right support route for your situation.**
   - If you are **18 or over** and worried about intimate images being shared, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for practical support.
   - If you are **under 18**, or the person targeted is under 18, use CEOP Safety Centre and tell a trusted adult if you can.
   - If a sexual image or video of someone under 18 is online or may be online, use Childline and IWF Report Remove.
   - If there are threats, blackmail, stalking, or immediate safety worries, you can contact police by calling **101**, reporting online through your local police force, or calling **999** in immediate danger.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a formal police statement.
- You do not need to work out the exact offence or legal label before asking for help.
- You do not need to collect perfect evidence.
- You do not need to write a long explanation to the person pressuring you.
- You do not need to decide what this means for the whole relationship today.
- You do not need to check every platform again and again; set one later time to review with someone you trust.

## Important reassurance
Pressure plus anger after a refusal is not your fault. Freezing, feeling tempted to appease them, or worrying that you caused the anger are common stress reactions. You are allowed to stop contact, protect your accounts, and ask for help without proving anything first.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to reduce immediate risk and help you get through the next part safely. Later decisions about police, platforms, school, work, housing, relationships, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999. If you are under 18 and any sexual image is involved or being requested, seek help from a trusted adult or a child-focused service such as CEOP, Childline, or Report Remove.
