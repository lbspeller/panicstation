# What to do if… your prebooked airport, ferry, or rail transfer does not arrive and you are under time pressure (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a staffed, public help point and run two tracks at once: contact the missing transfer provider while arranging the safest backup route to your next check-in, gate, platform, or terminal. Keep screenshots and receipts as you go.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait in an isolated pickup spot if there is a staffed desk, help point, ticket office, or information desk nearby.
- Don’t spend the whole connection window refreshing an app; set a latest-departure time and switch to a backup when you reach it.
- Don’t get into a vehicle offered by an unofficial tout or by someone whose vehicle, plate, and driver details you cannot check.
- Don’t cancel or rebook your onward flight, ferry, or rail ticket in a panic before asking the operator what options remain.
- Don’t throw away tickets, booking confirmations, screenshots, receipts, or chat logs.

## What to do now
1. Move to the nearest staffed help point.
   Use the airport information desk or airline desk, the station ticket office or help point, or the ferry terminal customer service desk. Stay in public view, especially if you are alone or carrying luggage.

2. Check what kind of booking you have.
   Open your email or app and confirm whether the transfer and onward journey are part of one booking or separate bookings. Screenshot the pickup time, pickup point, booking reference, provider name, phone or chat details, and any instructions such as “call on arrival”.

3. Contact the transfer provider with one clear question.
   Send or say: “My transfer has not arrived. What is the earliest confirmed pickup or alternative you can provide, and can you confirm it by text, email, or in-app message?” If it is a rail replacement bus or coach, ask station staff where it actually departs from and whether it is still running.

4. Set your latest safe departure time.
   Check the next operator’s latest check-in, boarding, gate, platform, or vehicle-loading time. Decide the latest minute you can keep waiting for the missing transfer; when you reach it, stop chasing and use the safest backup.

5. Use a traceable backup route.
   For an airport, use the official taxi rank, an airport-approved transport desk, or a licensed private hire vehicle booked through a reputable firm or app. Before getting in, match the vehicle, registration plate, driver name, and pickup point to your booking.

6. Ask rail staff before buying a replacement rail route.
   If the missing transfer is connected to a rail disruption or rail replacement service, ask staff for the quickest alternative route and whether ticket acceptance or route acceptance is in place. Do not assume this is automatic.

7. Ask ferry staff what keeps your place alive.
   If a ferry connection is at risk, ask the terminal desk whether you should check in now, whether standby is possible, whether the next sailing has space, and what written confirmation they can give you.

8. Notify the next operator before you miss the departure.
   Use the airline, ferry company, train company, booking agent, or travel provider’s app, chat, desk, or phone line. Say: “My ground transfer has failed and I am on my way. Please note this on my booking and tell me the latest check-in or boarding time and any rebooking options.”

9. Make a quick evidence pack while moving.
   Take a photo of the pickup point or stand, screenshot unanswered messages or app status, note the times you called or spoke to staff, and keep receipts for replacement transport or extra tickets.

10. Preserve payment options without relying on them now.
   Pay by card where possible and keep confirmations. Later, if the transfer was not provided, you can ask the provider for a refund and may be able to ask your card provider about chargeback or, for some credit-card purchases, Section 75; these routes have conditions and are not guaranteed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to write a full complaint now.
- You do not need to decide now whether to escalate to a regulator, ombudsman, card provider, or insurer.
- You do not need to prove fault at the counter; focus on reaching the next workable departure and getting written confirmation where possible.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip at once; solve the next safe move first.

## Important reassurance
A missing transfer is stressful because it compresses several decisions into minutes. Moving to staff, setting a latest-departure time, and using a traceable backup route gives you a practical way through without having to settle every refund or complaint question immediately.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise the situation, try to keep the journey moving, and preserve evidence. Later refund, compensation, insurance, or complaint decisions may need specialist help and will depend on your booking terms, ticket type, operator policy, and what actually happened.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, insurance, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe, stay in a staffed public area and ask on-site staff or emergency services for immediate help.
