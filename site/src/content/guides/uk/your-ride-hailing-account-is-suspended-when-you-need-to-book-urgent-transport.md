# What to do if… your ride-hailing account is suspended when you need to book urgent transport (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the transport problem and the account problem as separate. Use a licensed taxi or private hire fallback now, and only deal with the account review once you are moving or safely waiting.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep creating new accounts, changing phone numbers, or repeatedly retrying different emails in a panic; this may make account checks harder.
- Don’t keep adding, removing, and retrying payment cards over and over.
- Don’t get into a private hire vehicle or minicab that was not pre-booked through a licensed operator.
- Don’t accept an off-app ride from someone you do not know because they say they can “help”.
- Don’t send full card numbers, one-time passcodes, or identity documents to anyone who contacted you first; use only the provider’s official app or help site.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether this is an emergency.**
   - If someone is seriously ill, injured, or their life may be at risk, call **999** now.
   - If it is urgent medical help but not life-threatening, use your local NHS urgent-care route, such as **NHS 111** where available.

2. **Book a safe fallback without using the suspended account.**
   - Call a local licensed taxi or private hire operator and ask for the estimated arrival time, vehicle details, and price estimate.
   - Go to a staffed taxi rank if one is nearby and safe to reach.
   - Where local rules allow it, hail a licensed taxi; do not hail or accept a private hire vehicle or minicab from the street.
   - Before getting in, check the vehicle or driver details match what the operator gave you, if you pre-booked.

3. **Use another practical transport route if it is safer or faster.**
   - Ask a trusted person to book a licensed taxi or private hire vehicle for you.
   - Use public transport for the first leg if it is running, safe, and gets you closer to help.
   - Move to a staffed, well-lit place such as a station, hospital reception, hotel lobby, or open shop while arranging the journey.

4. **If the journey is for NHS care and you cannot safely arrange transport, ask the NHS service involved.**
   - For a hospital or clinic appointment, contact the department, booking team, GP, or referrer and say you cannot arrange safe transport.
   - Ask whether non-emergency patient transport may be available.
   - If cost is the barrier, ask whether the Healthcare Travel Costs Scheme may apply.

5. **Save only the information needed for the account issue.**
   - Take a screenshot of the suspension message, any error code, and the time it appeared.
   - Note recent changes such as a new phone, new number, new payment card, travel, failed payments, or repeated booking attempts.
   - Do not spend time writing a long explanation while you still need transport.

6. **Use the provider’s official account-access route once you are safe.**
   - Open the app or the provider’s official help site.
   - Use the account access, review, help, or contact support option shown by that provider.
   - Keep the message short: “My rider account was suspended at [time]. I need to arrange urgent transport. Please tell me what verification, payment, or account step is needed.”

7. **If it looks like a payment or security block, do one calm check only.**
   - Update the app and try signing in once.
   - Check for any payment, wallet, identity, or verification prompt in the app.
   - If the provider’s help page tells you to update a payment method, do it once, then stop repeated changes and wait for support.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why the account was suspended right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to change ride-hailing providers permanently.
- You do not need to gather detailed evidence beyond the message, time, and basic notes.
- You do not need to argue with support while you are still stranded; the immediate task is safe transport.

## Important reassurance
A suspended ride-hailing account is disruptive, but it does not remove all transport options. Licensed taxis, private hire operators by phone, taxi ranks, public transport, and trusted-person help are normal fallbacks while the provider reviews the account.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get urgent transport safely and avoid making the account problem harder to resolve. Later, you may need specialist help from the provider, your bank, the NHS service involved, or a local licensing authority.
