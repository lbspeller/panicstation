# What to do if… someone online is pressuring you for explicit photos and demanding secrecy (UK)

## Short answer
Stop replying and do not send photos, videos, money, or more information. Break the secrecy pressure by telling one safe person and using a UK support or reporting route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send any photos, videos, or “proof” to make them stop.
- Do not pay money, gift cards, crypto, or fees.
- Do not negotiate, apologise, argue, or try to shame them into stopping.
- Do not delete your accounts or message threads in a panic if you may want help later.
- Do not keep it secret because they told you to.
- Do not use paid “takedown” or “hacker” offers from strangers who contact you.

## What to do now
1. **Create a short safety pause.** Put the device down briefly if you can, move away from other people looking over your shoulder, and decide: no more replies and no more sending.
2. **Stop the conversation from continuing.** Do not answer new demands. Mute notifications if they are making you panic.
3. **Tell one safe person now.**
   - If you are under 18, tell a parent, carer, another trusted adult, or a safeguarding lead at school or college.
   - If you are 18 or over, tell a trusted friend, relative, partner, or support worker.
4. **Use the app or platform report tool.** Report the account and the messages for sexual exploitation, blackmail, harassment, or intimate image abuse, depending on the wording the platform uses. After reporting, block the account if it is safe to do so.
5. **Use the right UK route for your age and urgency.**
   - If you are under 18, or worried a child is involved, use the CEOP Safety Centre or ask a trusted adult to help you contact police on 101. Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.
   - If you are 18 or over, contact police through 101 or Police.uk. Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.
   - If you are 18 or over and intimate images have been shared or threatened, the Revenge Porn Helpline can give confidential support.
6. **Avoid wiping everything in a rush.** If it is safe, keep the chat, account name, and platform visible for now rather than deleting accounts or messages immediately.
7. **If an image has already been shared or you think it might be shared, use an image-removal route.**
   - If you are under 18, Childline Report Remove can help with sexual images or videos of yourself.
   - If you are 18 or over, StopNCII may help prevent intimate images being shared on participating platforms, and Report Harmful Content may help after you have reported content to a platform.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to make a formal police report.
- Writing a detailed timeline.
- Explaining everything to multiple people.
- Making a public post about it.
- Cleaning up all your social media accounts.
- Deciding what this means for your relationship, school, work, or future.

## Important reassurance
This pattern, an explicit request followed by secrecy or threats, is a coercion tactic. Feeling shocked, ashamed, frozen, or frightened is a common response to pressure; it does not mean you caused the abuse or have to handle it alone.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get safer, reduce pressure, and reach appropriate support. Later decisions may need help from police, safeguarding staff, a specialist helpline, or another trusted professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
