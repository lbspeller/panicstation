# What to do if… you hear persistent scratching in a wall or ceiling and suspect an animal is trapped (UK)

## Short answer
Do not block any holes or attempt to drill into walls to extract the animal. Keep the immediate area quiet and contact a professional pest controller or the National Bat Helpline if a bat is suspected.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t seal air bricks, vents, or gaps until you are certain the animal has left, as this traps it inside and risks property damage or death in voids.
- Don’t use poison, bait, or chemical repellents in wall cavities, which can lead to carcasses rotting in inaccessible spaces.
- Don’t attempt to handle any wild animal, especially bats, with bare hands due to the risk of injury and disease.
- Don’t light a fire or use gas appliances if you suspect an animal is trapped in a chimney or flue.
- Don’t start dismantling walls or ceilings yourself without professional guidance on structural safety.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the area to reduce animal distress.** Close doors to the room where the noise is loudest and keep pets and children away from that part of the house.
2. **Observe sensory cues to estimate the species.**
   - **Birds:** High-pitched chirping or frantic flapping, often in chimneys or near external vents during daylight.
   - **Bats:** Light scratching, clicking, or cheeping sounds in the loft or eaves, particularly active at dusk and dawn.
   - **Rodents/Squirrels:** Heavier scurrying, gnawing, or thumping sounds, often occurring throughout the night.
3. **Contact the National Bat Helpline if bats are a possibility.** **Call 0345 1300 228 immediately if you suspect bats are present.**
   - All UK bats and their roosts are protected by law; disturbing them or blocking their exits is a criminal offence.
   - Follow their specific instructions before allowing any building or pest control work to proceed.
4. **Follow specific protocols for birds in chimneys.**
   - **Open windows and doors in the room** and draw back curtains to create a single, bright exit point if the bird enters the room.
   - **Do not use the fireplace.**
   - **Contact a professional chimney sweep or builder** if the bird is stuck in an inaccessible part of the flue, as the RSPCA/SSPCA often lack the equipment to dismantle chimneys.
5. **Arrange professional pest or building assistance.**
   - **Contact your local council's pest control service** or a private contractor if you suspect rodents or squirrels.
   - **Inform your landlord or letting agent in writing immediately** if you are a tenant, as they are responsible for arranging structural repairs and pest management.
6. **Hire a Gas Safe registered engineer if an animal is trapped behind a gas fire.** **Do not attempt to move the gas fire yourself.**
7. **Leave exit points open while waiting for help.** Maintain any existing gaps or holes that the animal may have used to enter, as these are its only way to escape voluntarily.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact subspecies of the animal immediately.
- You do not need to commit to permanent proofing or "pest-proofing" work until the animal has been safely removed or has exited.
- You do not need to clean or decontaminate the area until professional removal is complete.

## Important reassurance
Hearing an animal trapped in the structure of your home is loud and unsettling, but it is rarely an immediate threat to your physical safety. Most animals will attempt to find their own way out if the environment remains quiet and exit routes are not blocked.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for suspected trapped animals in UK properties. It does not cover long-term pest management, structural repair, or wildlife rehabilitation.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional advice from pest control experts, structural engineers, or licensed ecologists. If you suspect bats are present, you must consult the Bat Conservation Trust to avoid legal penalties under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981.
