# What to do if… online check-in is blocked because your documents need manual verification (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the blockage as a mandatory "counter check-in" requirement; ensure your Advance Passenger Information (API) exactly matches your physical documents and proceed to a staffed airport desk at least three hours before departure.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to re-enter data multiple times if the first correction fails, as this can trigger fraud flags in airline systems.
- Don’t assume a digital copy or photo of your passport will suffice for manual verification.
- Don’t pay for a flight change or cancellation before speaking to a staff member at the airport.
- Don’t attempt to use the self-service bag drop or automated kiosks if the app explicitly states "manual verification" is needed.

## What to do now
1. **Screenshot the exact error message.** Capture the specific code or text displayed by the airline app or website to provide to ground staff if the booking becomes locked.
2. **Verify your Advance Passenger Information (API) in "Manage Booking."** 
   - Check that the name on the ticket matches the machine-readable zone (bottom strip) of your passport exactly.
   - Confirm the passport number, expiry date, and date of birth are recorded without typos.
3. **Check the "10-year rule" for EU/Schengen travel.**
   - Ensure your passport was issued less than 10 years before the date you enter the destination country.
   - Confirm your passport is valid for at least three months after the day you plan to leave the Schengen area.
4. **Generate a UKVI Share Code if travelling to the UK on an eVisa.**
   - Access the "View and Prove" service on GOV.UK.
   - Select "Something else" to generate a share code for the airline.
   - Download or print the PDF as a manual backup if the airline's digital check (IAPI) fails.
5. **Confirm ETA status for non-visa nationals entering the UK.**
   - Verify your Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) is linked to the specific passport you are carrying.
   - If you are a dual British citizen travelling on a foreign passport, you must present your valid UK passport or a digital Certificate of Entitlement.
6. **Prepare physical evidence of name changes.**
   - If your ticket name differs from your passport due to marriage or deed poll, carry the original certificate to the check-in desk.
7. **Arrive at the airport early for staffed counter service.**
   - Aim to be at the staffed check-in desk three hours before international departures.
   - Carry all original documents, including visas, residence permits, and proof of onward travel.

## What can wait
- Requests for compensation or "denied boarding" claims (these cannot be processed until after the flight has departed).
- Updating your UKVI account with a new passport if you are already at the airport (updates can take 48–72 hours to sync).
- Cancelling non-refundable accommodation or car hires.

## Important reassurance
Manual verification is a standard safety and immigration protocol often triggered by routine audits, system sync delays, or destination-specific visa requirements. It does not necessarily indicate a problem with your eligibility to travel; it simply requires a physical check by a trained agent.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for online check-in failures related to document verification. It does not replace official immigration advice or guarantee boarding if documents are legally insufficient.

## Important note
Airlines are legally required to verify travel documents and can be fined for carrying passengers without valid entry clearance. Final boarding decisions rest with the carrier and border authorities.
