# What to do if… your professional certification or license is expiring soon and you cannot renew it in time (UK)

## Short answer
Confirm your exact status today, then tell the right manager, supervisor, compliance, or governance contact so you can be moved away from any work that requires current certification before it lapses.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep doing regulated, protected-title, sign-off, or certification-required work after expiry unless the regulator or certifying body confirms in writing that you may do so.
- Do not assume there is a grace period or that a pending renewal lets you keep working.
- Do not hide the expiry from the person responsible for safe staffing, compliance, supervision, or credential checks.
- Do not backdate forms, use someone else’s registration or membership number, or describe yourself as currently certified if you are not.
- Do not pay renewal fees or upload ID through links in unexpected messages; use the official portal or contact route.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact expiry status.**
   - Log into the certifying body, regulator, or professional register account.
   - Check the expiry date, expiry time if shown, renewal window, renewal status, and any wording about what you may or may not do after expiry.
   - Save a PDF or screenshot of your current status, renewal submission, payment receipt, or error message.

2. **Work out whether the certification is legally required or employer-required.**
   - If your role may be regulated by law, check the GOV.UK regulated professions list or search register and identify the relevant regulator.
   - If it is not legally regulated, your employer may still require it under your contract, policy, insurance, client contract, or internal compliance rules.
   - Treat both situations as urgent, but be clear which one you are dealing with.

3. **Check the official renewal and late-renewal rules.**
   - Look for wording such as late renewal, restoration, readmission, revalidation, returning to practice, inactive status, non-practising status, or temporary permissions.
   - Do not rely on forum posts, colleagues’ memories, or old screenshots.
   - Note any rules about practising, signing off work, supervising others, or using a protected title after expiry.

4. **Contact the certifying body or regulator through its official channel.**
   - Give your name, registration or membership number, expiry date, and the specific blocker.
   - Ask what your status will be between expiry and approval.
   - Ask whether any late-renewal or temporary status exists.
   - Ask what work, title, sign-off, supervision, or client-facing statements you must stop at expiry.
   - Keep the reference number and any written reply.

5. **Tell your employer the same day.**
   - Email your line manager and the person or team responsible for credential checks, such as HR, compliance, governance, practice management, or rota management.
   - State the expiry date, what is blocking renewal, what you have already done, and when you expect the next update.
   - Ask for written confirmation of what duties you should stop, what duties you can safely continue, and who will cover any required sign-off.

6. **Ring-fence certification-dependent work before the deadline.**
   - List tasks that may require current certification, such as signing documents, authorising work, supervising regulated activity, using a protected title, making professional declarations, or being named as the certified person on a job.
   - Ask for those tasks to be reassigned before expiry.
   - If a client, patient, pupil, service user, project, or statutory record is affected, ask your manager how disclosure or handover should be handled.

7. **Clear the smallest renewal blocker first.**
   - Identify the one thing preventing submission, such as CPD hours, declarations, payment, employer countersignature, identity checks, DBS-related checks, or portal access.
   - Complete only what is needed to submit a truthful and compliant renewal.
   - If a required check cannot finish in time, plan for a temporary gap unless the certifying body or regulator says in writing that you may keep working while it is pending.

8. **Keep a simple record.**
   - Record dates, who you told, what you asked, what you were told, and what duties were changed.
   - Keep copies of emails, portal messages, receipts, screenshots, and rota or duty changes.
   - If your employer says you may not continue in the role or duties without the credential, contact your union if you have one, or Acas, before making rushed decisions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to resign, change career, make a formal complaint, or explain every personal detail.
- You do not need to write a long statement; a factual email with dates, status, and requested duty changes is enough for the first step.
- You do not need to solve every employment consequence before you have confirmed the renewal rules and stopped any certification-dependent work.

## Important reassurance
This is often an admin, timing, CPD, payment, portal, or checking problem rather than a character problem. The safest credibility-preserving move is to be prompt, factual, and careful about not doing work that depends on a current credential until your status is clear.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Exact rules vary by profession, certifying body, regulator, employer policy, contract, and whether the role is legally regulated.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, employment, financial, regulatory, or professional advice. If the role involves a protected title, regulated activity, or required sign-off, check the regulator or certifying body before continuing that work after expiry.
