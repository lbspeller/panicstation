# What to do if… you are asked to backdate documents or signatures to fix an audit issue (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the transaction immediately and do not pay any fees until you have independently verified the employer's identity and received a written contract stating that work-finding services are free.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send money via bank transfer, cryptocurrency, gift cards, or voucher codes to "secure" a position.
- Do not click links in unsolicited WhatsApp, Telegram, or SMS messages claiming to be from recruiters.
- Do not provide photos of your passport, driving licence, or bank details to unverified contacts.
- Do not accept "reimbursement" promises for high-value items (like laptops) purchased through a "specific supplier" provided by the recruiter.
- Do not pay a recruitment agency any fee for finding you work; this is illegal under the Employment Agencies Act 1973.

## What to do now
1. **Cease all communication and pause any pending payments.** 
   **Inform the contact that you cannot proceed with payments until you have received and reviewed your Written Statement of Employment Particulars.**
   - By law, all workers in the UK must receive these written terms on or before their first day.

2. **Verify the organization using official independent channels.**
   - **Check Companies House to confirm the business is a registered Limited Company or LLP.**
   - **Verify the recruiter's identity by calling the company's head office using a number found on their official "Contact Us" website page, not a number provided in a message.**
   - **Check the email address domain; legitimate corporate emails rarely come from @gmail.com, @outlook.com, or @hotmail.com.**

3. **Check the legitimacy of any agency fees if they claim to be for "extras."**
   - **Identify if the agency is charging for work-finding (illegal) or optional services like CV writing or training (legal but regulated).**
   - **Demand written terms for any optional services, which must include a clear cooling-off period (usually 5 or 10 working days).**
   - **Confirm that the agency has not made finding you work conditional on you paying for these extra services.**

4. **Report the incident to the relevant UK authorities.**
   - **Visit JobsAware (jobsaware.co.uk) to report recruitment scams and receive specialist help for worker rights.**
   - **Report the fraud to "Report Fraud" (the successor to Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040 (England, Wales, and NI).**
   - **Contact Police Scotland on 101 if you are based in Scotland.**

5. **Contact your financial institution if money has already been moved.**
   - **Call your bank's fraud department immediately to request a "chargeback" or "Section 75" claim if you paid by card.**
   - **Ask your bank to place a temporary freeze on your account if you shared your full bank details or online banking login information.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to respond to "urgent" deadlines or threats that the job will be given to someone else.
- You do not need to provide a reason for pausing the process beyond wanting to verify the contract.
- You do not need to hire a solicitor at this stage; ACAS or JobsAware can provide the necessary initial guidance.

## Important reassurance
It is a normal and protective response to feel suspicious when asked for money before you have earned any. Legitimate UK employers have established payroll and procurement processes and will not pressure you to use personal funds for essential business equipment or "onboarding fees." Slowing down to verify the offer is a standard professional practice.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for suspected recruitment scams or illegal agency fee requests. It does not cover long-term employment disputes regarding training bonds once employment has already commenced.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Recruitment regulations can vary based on sector (e.g., entertainment and modelling have different fee rules). If you are unsure of your rights, contact ACAS or the Employment Agency Standards (EAS) Inspectorate.
