# What to do if… you are asked for proof of funds or payment means at the border and cannot access them (UK)

## Short answer
Ask what proof they will accept and request a short pause to produce something verifiable, such as a bank statement, secure bank message, sponsor confirmation, or evidence that costs have already been paid. Keep your answers brief, consistent, and limited to what you can actually show.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue, joke, or get sarcastic; stay polite and practical.
- Do not show edited screenshots or anything that could be mistaken for a fake document.
- Do not invent details about your job, address, sponsor, length of stay, or travel plans.
- Do not casually give out bank passwords, card PINs, or security codes. If anyone asks for access to a device or account, ask what exactly is being requested and whether another form of proof will be accepted.
- Do not accept last-minute “help” from strangers, such as unexpected transfers or payments, if you cannot clearly explain who they are and why they are helping.

## What to do now
1. **Ask for time and ask what formats they accept.**  
   Say: “I can’t access my funds right now because of a banking or phone issue. What forms of proof of funds or payment means will you accept, and can I take a moment to retrieve them?”

2. **Try quick fixes that create real evidence.**  
   - Join the terminal Wi-Fi or switch mobile data off and on.
   - Restart your phone and turn off any VPN.
   - Try your bank through a web browser if the app is failing.
   - If your phone is low or dead, ask if you can charge it briefly.
   - If you have another trusted device with you, try logging in through that only if it is safe and allowed by your bank.

3. **Use any offline or printed proof you already have.**  
   Offer any of the following if you have them saved locally or printed:
   - A recent bank statement or downloadable bank document that shows your name, account details, and available funds.
   - A secure message or balance letter from your bank, if you can get one quickly.
   - Cards in your name as evidence of payment means, while being clear that a card itself does not prove the available balance.
   - Receipts or confirmations showing accommodation, tours, transport, or return or onward travel have already been paid, if true.
   - An employer or sponsor letter confirming support, if it is accurate and checkable.

4. **Contact your bank or card provider and ask for immediate help.**  
   Ask:
   - “Is there a fraud or security block because I am abroad?”
   - “Can you unblock my card, app, or online banking now?”
   - “Can you provide a secure message, downloadable statement, or other quick confirmation of my available funds?”
   If you cannot call, try secure chat, a browser login, or ask whether you can use your own device, a companion’s phone, or an airport desk phone if one is available.

5. **If someone else is funding you, make that support clear and checkable.**  
   Ask your sponsor, host, employer, or family member to be ready to:
   - Answer a call immediately.
   - Send a short email confirming what support they are providing, their full name, and their relationship to you.
   - Provide evidence, only what they are comfortable sharing, that they can provide the support.
   - Keep their explanation consistent with your travel plans.

6. **If this is the UK border, keep your explanation aligned with your real visitor status.**  
   UK visitor checks can include whether you can support yourself, whether a genuine third party can support you, and whether you can pay for your stay and return or onward journey. Keep it simple: where you will stay, how long you will stay, who is paying, and how you plan to leave, then show whatever evidence you can produce.

7. **If you are a British national abroad and you are stuck because you cannot access money, use consular support appropriately.**  
   Contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate for help understanding options and contacting family, your employer, insurer, or bank. The FCDO generally cannot provide money, pay bills, make a foreign authority admit you, or override a local border decision.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to cancel the whole trip or change long-term plans.
- You do not need to fully fix your banking setup at the counter; you only need a credible way to show support, funds, or payment means now.
- You do not need to explain every technical detail of the banking problem. Focus on producing something verifiable.

## Important reassurance
Bank security blocks, app failures, dead batteries, and poor connectivity are familiar travel problems. Calm cooperation and checkable evidence are more useful than a perfect explanation.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the moment and avoid irreversible mistakes. If you are refused entry, held for extended questioning, or told that a formal immigration decision is being made, you may need specialist immigration advice later.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, professional, medical, or therapeutic advice. Border authorities have discretion, and what they ask for can depend on your citizenship, visa status, route, and reason for travel. If you feel unwell or unsafe, say so clearly and ask what your options are.
