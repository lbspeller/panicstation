# What to do if… you wake up undressed or with clothing changed and cannot remember consenting to sex (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a location where you feel safe, then contact a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) for confidential medical care and support without any requirement to report to the police. If you are in immediate physical danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don't force yourself to confront the person you were with until you are in a safe place and feel supported.
- Don't delete any digital evidence such as texts, call logs, or location history, even if they feel distressing.
- Don't feel pressured to make a police report immediately; you can access medical care and forensic evidence collection first while you decide.
- Don't blame yourself for gaps in your memory, for "freezing," or for a lack of physical resistance.
- Don't wash, brush your teeth, or smoke if you can avoid it, as this can preserve forensic evidence, but seek help even if you have already done these things.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your immediate physical safety.**  
   **If you are currently in a location where you feel unsafe or the person is still present, move to a "safer pause" such as a locked room, a friend's house, or a public space.** If you are in danger or have been threatened, **call 999** immediately.

2. **Contact a specialist Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC).**  
   **SARCs provide specialist medical care, emergency contraception, and the option to collect forensic evidence (even if you don't want to involve the police right now).**
   - In England and Wales: Find your nearest SARC via the NHS website or call the 24/7 Support Line on **0808 500 2222**.
   - In Scotland: Call the dedicated NHS Scotland SARC self-referral line on **0800 148 88 88** (available 24/7).
   - In Northern Ireland: Contact **The Rowan (SARC)** on **0800 389 4424**.

3. **Seek urgent medical attention for injuries or suspected drugging.**  
   **If you have severe pain, heavy bleeding, or feel physically unwell and suspect you were drugged (spiked), go to your nearest A&E or call 999.** If the situation is urgent but not an emergency, call **111** (NHS 24 in Scotland).

4. **Preserve early evidence if it feels manageable.**  
   **If you think you may want evidence collected later, try to follow these steps, but do not let them delay your medical care.**
   - If you need to urinate, try to collect the first sample in a clean, dry container (like a jar) and keep it.
   - Place the clothes you woke up in into a paper bag (avoid plastic if possible to prevent moisture buildup).
   - Avoid washing your body or cleaning the area where you woke up.

5. **Access specialized emotional support.**  
   **You do not need to be certain about what happened to talk to a specialist service.**
   - England & Wales: Rape Crisis England & Wales (0808 500 2222).
   - Scotland: Rape Crisis Scotland (08088 01 03 02, 5pm–midnight).
   - Northern Ireland: Domestic and Sexual Abuse Helpline (0808 802 1414, 24/7).
   - Under 19s: Childline (0800 1111).

6. **Record a brief "memory snapshot."**  
   **Write down the last thing you remember, the location, anyone you were with, and what you noticed when you woke up (e.g., clothing state, physical sensations).** Store this in a secure place like a locked phone note.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to press charges or involve the police today.
- You do not need to identify the exact substance or reason for your memory loss before seeking help.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your family, employer, or friends until you feel ready.
- You do not need to "prove" what happened to receive medical care or support.

## Important reassurance
Waking up with no memory of the previous night is a distressing experience that can cause feelings of shock, numbness, or shame. These are standard reactions to a potential trauma. Your priority is your own well-being and safety; you are entitled to care and support regardless of how much you can remember or what you were doing before the memory gap occurred.

## Scope note
This guide is for immediate stabilization and first-step medical/support access only. Long-term recovery, legal decisions, and detailed forensic processes will involve professional guidance from SARC staff or support workers.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not replace professional medical or legal advice. Forensic windows for certain types of evidence (especially "spiking" substances) are very short—often 12 to 24 hours—so contacting a SARC or hospital quickly is advised if you want those options.
