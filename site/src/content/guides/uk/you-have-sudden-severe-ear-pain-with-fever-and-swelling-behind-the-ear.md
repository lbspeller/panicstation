# What to do if… you have sudden severe ear pain with fever and swelling behind the ear (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a medical emergency requiring same-day assessment at A&E or an Urgent Treatment Centre. Swelling behind the ear combined with fever often indicates mastoiditis, a serious infection of the bone that requires immediate professional treatment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait for a routine GP appointment if the area behind the ear is swollen, red, or tender.
- Do not insert cotton buds, fingers, or any objects into the ear canal to investigate pain or discharge.
- Do not use leftover antibiotics or borrow medication from others.
- Do not use ear drops unless specifically prescribed for this current episode by a clinician who has examined your eardrum.
- Do not attempt to "pop" or drain any swelling behind the ear yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate life-threatening "red flags" and call 999 if they occur.**
   - Seek immediate emergency help for:
   - **Seizures or fits.**
   - **Severe confusion or difficulty staying awake.**
   - **Neck stiffness or inability to look at bright lights.**
   - **A new rash that does not fade when a glass is pressed against it.**
2. **Go to A&E or an Urgent Treatment Centre for urgent symptoms.**
   - **Seek a same-day assessment** if you observe:
   - **Swelling or redness behind the ear** that makes the ear stick out further than the other.
   - **A high temperature** (38°C / 100.4°F or higher).
   - **New facial weakness** (drooping on one side of the face).
   - **Severe dizziness or loss of balance.**
3. **Contact NHS 111 if you are unsure where to go.**
   - **Call 111 or use 111 online** and clearly state you have: "severe ear pain, a fever, and swelling behind the ear bone."
   - Follow their direction to the nearest available urgent care facility.
4. **Administer safe pain and fever relief.**
   - **Use paracetamol or ibuprofen** as directed on the packaging to manage pain and temperature while waiting for medical review.
   - Ensure children under 16 do not take aspirin.
   - Note the exact time and dose taken to inform the medical team.
5. **Protect the ear from further irritation.**
   - **Avoid getting water in the ear** by skipping hair washing or swimming until seen by a doctor.
   - If there is fluid leaking out, **wipe only the outer ear gently** with a clean tissue; do not plug the canal.
6. **Gather essential information for the clinician.**
   - **Check if the ear looks pushed forward or downward** compared to the healthy side.
   - **Note when the pain started** and if you have had a recent cold or previous ear infections.
   - **Prepare a list of current medications** and any known allergies to antibiotics (like penicillin).

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific cause of the infection yourself.
- You do not need to clean the internal ear canal or remove wax.
- You do not need to worry about long-term hearing outcomes right now; focus on immediate stabilization and treatment.

## Important reassurance
Ear pain accompanied by swelling is physically distressing and a valid reason to seek urgent care. Clinical teams in A&E and Urgent Treatment Centres are trained to assess these symptoms quickly to prevent complications.

## Scope note
This guidance is for first-step stabilization only. Mastoiditis and severe middle ear infections require in-person clinical diagnosis, often involving specialized tools or imaging that cannot be performed at home.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a medical diagnosis. If you have symptoms of a severe infection such as high fever, swelling behind the ear, or neurological changes, you must be seen by a healthcare professional immediately. Call 999 for life-threatening emergencies or 111 for urgent medical advice in the UK.
