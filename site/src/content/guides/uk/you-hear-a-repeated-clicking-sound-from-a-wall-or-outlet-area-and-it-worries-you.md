# What to do if… you hear a repeated clicking sound from a wall or outlet area and it worries you (UK)

## Short answer
Treat any repeated clicking, buzzing, or crackling as a potential electrical arcing fault: stop using the socket immediately, turn off the power at the consumer unit if safe, and contact a registered electrician.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore the sound to see if it stops, especially if it is intermittent or happens when an appliance is running.
- Don’t plug anything else into the socket to "test" it.
- Don’t attempt to unscrew the socket faceplate or investigate the wiring behind the wall.
- Don’t use water to extinguish any sparks or small flames if an electrical fire starts.
- Don’t keep resetting a tripped RCD or circuit breaker if it continues to flip back to the "off" position.
- Don’t touch a plug or socket that is discoloured, visibly melting, or hot to the touch.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate fire indicators.** If you see **smoke, sparks, or flames**, or if you notice a **strong fishy or acrid burning smell**, evacuate the property and **call 999**.
2. **Isolate the affected area.** If a device is currently plugged into the clicking socket and it is safe to touch, **switch off the appliance** and then **unplug it by the plug body**.
   - If the plug feels unusually warm or is stuck, **do not pull it**.
3. **Turn off the power at the consumer unit (fuse box).** Locate your consumer unit and **switch off the circuit marked for sockets** (often labelled "Ring Main" or "Sockets").
   - If you are unsure which switch is correct, **turn off the large "Main Switch"** to isolate the entire property.
4. **Move flammable materials away.** Ensure there are no **curtains, bedding, papers, or aerosol cans** within one metre of the suspect socket.
5. **Contact a registered electrician.** Find a professional listed on the **NICEIC, NAPIT, or CPS (Competent Person Scheme)** registers.
   - Inform them if the sound is constant, if it only happens under load (when an appliance is on), or if you noticed a fishy smell.
6. **Apply first aid if a thermal burn occurred.** If you touched a hot socket and sustained a burn, **cool the skin under cool running water for at least 20 minutes** and seek medical advice via **NHS 111**.
7. **Notify your landlord if you rent.** In the UK, landlords have a **legal duty** under the 2025/2026 safety regulations to ensure electrical installations are safe. 
   - Inform them that you have isolated the circuit due to a suspected fault and request an urgent inspection.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify whether the fault is in the wall wiring or the socket itself; an electrician will use a multimeter for this.
- You do not need to check the rest of the house's sockets today if you have isolated the fault circuit.
- You do not need to provide proof of the sound to the electrician; they will perform a physical inspection and continuity test regardless.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when hearing unexpected sounds from your walls or electrics. A clicking or buzzing sound is a technical indicator that a connection may be loose or arcing. By isolating the power and stopping use, you have significantly reduced the risk of escalation.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization of a suspected electrical fault. It does not replace a professional Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR).

## Important note
Electrical faults can escalate into fires if left unaddressed. Always treat sensory cues like fishy smells, heat, or persistent clicking as urgent. If you are a tenant, your landlord is legally required to provide a safe electrical environment and must address reported hazards within 28 days or less for urgent faults.
