# What to do if… your door peephole or viewer looks covered or tampered with (UK)

## Short answer
Do not open the door while you cannot clearly check who or what is outside. Move away from the door, lock it, and use another safe way to check, such as a camera, a window from a safe position, a trusted neighbour, building staff, or the police.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door just to see what is going on.
- Do not stand directly in front of the door while inspecting the viewer.
- Do not argue through the door or say that you are alone.
- Do not go outside to investigate on your own if you feel uneasy.
- Do not remove or handle anything covering the viewer unless you need to for immediate safety.

## What to do now
1. Move away from the direct line of the door. Lock the door, use any bolts or chain you already have, and keep your phone with you.
2. Listen from a safer position. Notice whether there are footsteps, voices, knocking, scraping, someone trying the handle, or movement at the letterbox.
3. Check without opening the door. Use a doorbell camera, internal viewer screen, side window, upstairs window, or another safe viewpoint. Do not press your face against glass or put yourself in view if that feels unsafe.
4. If you live in flats, student housing, a hotel, or managed accommodation, call reception, concierge, security, the building manager, or a trusted neighbour and ask whether they can safely check the corridor or entrance.
5. If you feel threatened, think someone may be trying to get in, or think a crime may be happening now, call 999. If it is not an emergency but you want to report suspicious activity or tampering, call 101 or use your local police online reporting route.
6. Take a quick photo or video from inside if you can do it without opening the door. Note the time, what changed, and anything you heard.
7. If someone claims to be an official caller, delivery driver, contractor, or neighbour, keep the door closed while you check. Use a known phone number or official route, not a number they give you at the door.
8. If you must open the door for an unavoidable reason, keep a chain or door restrictor on if you have one, keep the opening small, stay back, and close the door again promptly.
9. If you rent or the door is part of managed accommodation, report the damaged or blocked viewer as an urgent repair or safety issue after the immediate situation is stable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it was a prank, vandalism, an attempted distraction, or something more serious.
- You do not need to confront anyone, inspect the outside area, or remove the covering right now.
- You do not need to choose new locks, cameras, lighting, or other upgrades tonight unless the door will not secure.

## Important reassurance
A covered or tampered door viewer is a reasonable reason to pause. You are not overreacting by keeping the door closed, checking another way, and asking for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours only. If this repeats, if there is damage, or if you are being harassed, later decisions may need help from police, housing staff, a landlord, a locksmith, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, prioritise getting to safety and contacting emergency services.
