# What to do if… your interior lights or accessories will not turn off and you fear the battery will drain (UK)

## Short answer
Get safely parked first. Then try the quick door, boot, lock and light-switch checks; if the light or accessory is still on, either contact roadside help or, only if you are safely parked and confident, isolate the drain using the correct fuse or the 12V battery negative terminal.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving while pressing switches, reading the manual or looking for the fault.
- Don’t stop in a dangerous place just to save the battery.
- Don’t attempt repairs or battery work in a live lane, on a hard shoulder, or anywhere traffic is close.
- Don’t disconnect the battery with the engine running or the vehicle still powered on.
- Don’t let metal tools bridge between battery terminals or between a terminal and the bodywork.
- Don’t touch orange high-voltage cables, orange covers or high-voltage warning areas on a hybrid or electric vehicle.
- Don’t pull random fuses in the dark if you cannot clearly identify the correct one.
- Don’t force a door, boot or bonnet latch; a small adjustment may be enough, but forcing it can make the problem worse.

## What to do now
1. **Make the vehicle position safe first.**
   - If you can, leave the carriageway at the next exit or pull into a service area, lay-by, car park or another safer place.
   - Put hazard warning lights on if you are stopped where other traffic may not expect you.
   - If you are on a motorway or high-speed road and cannot get to the left lane or a place of relative safety, and cannot exit safely, do not try to fix the light at the roadside. Stay in the vehicle with your seatbelt on, keep hazard lights on, and call 999 or use the vehicle SOS button if fitted.

2. **Try the simple checks that often stop the drain.**
   - Set the interior light switch to OFF, not ON or DOOR.
   - On some vehicles, turn the dashboard dimmer down if it is at the top setting.
   - Open and firmly close each door, one at a time.
   - Open and firmly close the boot.
   - Check that the glovebox is fully shut.
   - Check that the bonnet is fully latched if your car has an under-bonnet light or bonnet alarm switch.

3. **Try letting the car go to sleep.**
   - Switch the vehicle fully off.
   - Remove the key from the ignition if it has one.
   - Lock the car using the fob or physical key.
   - Step away from the vehicle with a keyless fob for a few minutes, because some vehicles keep systems awake when the fob is nearby.
   - Look again from outside the vehicle; do not sit inside with the door open while checking.

4. **If the light or accessory is still on, choose a safe isolation option.**
   - If you can clearly identify the correct fuse from the owner’s manual or fuse-box cover, you may remove that fuse and keep it safe to refit later. Labels such as interior, room, dome or courtesy lamp may appear, depending on the vehicle.
   - If you cannot clearly identify the fuse, do not guess. Use roadside help, a garage or the battery option below if it is safe for you.
   - If you are safely parked and confident working around the 12V battery, switch the vehicle fully off, remove the key from the ignition if fitted, keep a keyless fob away if possible, locate the 12V battery, and loosen the negative terminal clamp first.
   - Lift the negative cable off the battery post and secure it so it cannot spring back onto the post.
   - Stop if the battery is hard to access, damaged, leaking, unusually hot, or if you cannot clearly identify the negative terminal.

5. **Get help rather than diagnosing the fault at the roadside.**
   - Contact your breakdown provider, insurer, lease company, garage or an auto electrician.
   - Say: “My interior lights or accessories are staying on and I’m worried about battery drain.”
   - If you are on a motorway and have stopped in an emergency area, use the emergency telephone if one is provided, or follow official motorway breakdown guidance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide tonight whether the battery needs replacing.
- You do not need to test for parasitic drain at the roadside.
- You can leave door latch switches, boot latch faults, glovebox lamps, aftermarket alarms, dashcam wiring and control-module checks for daylight or a garage.

## Important reassurance
This is often caused by something simple, such as a light switch position, a door or boot not fully latched, or a keyless car staying awake. The immediate aim is not a perfect diagnosis; it is to keep you safe and reduce the chance of a flat battery.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later diagnosis or repair may need a mechanic, auto electrician, breakdown patrol or vehicle manufacturer guidance, especially for modern, hybrid or electric vehicles.

## Important note
This guide is general information for urgent first steps and is not professional mechanical, legal, emergency, financial or safety advice. Follow your vehicle handbook and get professional help if you are unsure or if the vehicle is not in a safe place.
