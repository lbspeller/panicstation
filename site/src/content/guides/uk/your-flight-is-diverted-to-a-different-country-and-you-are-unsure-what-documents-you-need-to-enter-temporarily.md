# What to do if… your flight is diverted to a different country and you are unsure what documents you need to enter temporarily (UK)

## Short answer
Stay in the international transit / airside area and ask airline staff to run a Timatic document check specifically for "emergency diversion" before you approach any border control.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t cross the "Nothing to Declare" or Customs line until you have confirmed your entry rights.
- Don’t hand your passport to anyone other than uniformed immigration officers or airline staff at an official service desk.
- Don’t sign any "voluntary departure" or "entry refusal" forms without contacting consular support first.
- Don’t assume a hotel voucher automatically gives you the legal right to enter the country.
- Don’t discard boarding passes or baggage tags from the original flight; these are your primary evidence of the diversion.

## What to do now
1. **Identify your current physical boundary.**
   - Look for signs indicating "Arrivals / Baggage Reclaim" versus "International Transfers / Transit".
   - If you see baggage carousels or customs desks, you are approaching the border; stop before crossing if you are unsure of your visa status.

2. **Confirm if the airline is requesting "landside" or "airside" transit.**
   - **Ask the ground staff: "Is this an airside stop or must we clear immigration?"**
   - If they say you must clear immigration for a hotel or baggage, request a Timatic check for your specific nationality.

3. **Request an IATA Timatic check for "Force Majeure".**
   - **Ask the airline agent to check if the diversion country allows "emergency landing" or "landing permission" for passengers without visas.**
   - Reference ICAO Annex 9 standards regarding emergency assistance for diverted passengers if the airline seems unsure.

4. **Communicate your lack of documents to the airline supervisor.**
   - **State clearly: "I do not have a visa for this country and I need to remain airside or receive emergency entry permission."**
   - Ask the airline to coordinate directly with the local Border Force or Immigration department on your behalf.

5. **Show proof of onward travel to border officials.**
   - **Keep your original boarding pass and itinerary visible.**
   - If questioned by immigration, explain that this is an unscheduled stop and show your ticket for the final destination.

6. **Call the FCDO 24/7 Consular Assistance line if you are denied transit or entry.**
   - **Dial +44 (0)20 7008 5000 if you are a British national and feel stuck or are being threatened with detention.**
   - Ask for the "Consular services for British nationals" option.

7. **Verify UK-specific entry requirements if the diversion is into the UK.**
   - **Confirm if you need an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) to transit the UK as of February 2026.**
   - If you are a dual British citizen without your UK passport, inform the border officer immediately of your citizenship status.

8. **Document the instructions given by staff.**
   - **Write down the name of the airline representative who told you to enter the country.**
   - Take a photo of the "Flight Diverted" status on the airport terminal screens.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle claims for "Duty of Care" (food / drink) or compensation right this second.
- You do not need to rebook your own flight if the airline is already processing the group.
- You do not need to worry about your checked luggage if the airline confirms it is being transferred automatically airside.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel disoriented when landing in a country you did not plan to visit. Airport and airline staff deal with diversions regularly; the process of "emergency entry" or "landing permission" exists specifically for these situations. Taking a moment to verify your status before crossing a border is a standard safety procedure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate hours of an international flight diversion. It does not cover long-term immigration appeals, legal representation, or specific visa application processes for the diversion country.

## Important note
Immigration rules are subject to the sovereign laws of the diversion country. While international guidelines (ICAO) exist, local officials have final authority. From 25 February 2026, UK entry and transit rules for many nationalities require a digital Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA).
