# What to do if… your water heater is leaking or making loud banging noises (UK)

## Short answer
If there is any gas smell, carbon monoxide alarm, severe illness, wet electrics, or violent banging, move away from the hazard first. If it is safe, turn off the heater’s heat source and stop the water feeding it, then get urgent help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore a new leak, repeated popping, banging, or visible discharge from a tundish or overflow pipe.
- Do not touch a wet switch, plug, fused spur, consumer unit, or appliance.
- Do not stand in water while handling electrics.
- Do not operate electrical switches if you smell gas.
- Do not smoke, use naked flames, or create sparks if you smell gas.
- Do not block, cap, tape, or plug a discharge pipe, overflow pipe, or tundish.
- Do not dismantle valves or pipework on a pressurised or unvented hot water cylinder.
- Do not keep heating water if you suspect overheating, very hot water, loud banging, or a safety discharge.

## What to do now
1. **Move away from immediate danger first.**
   - If you smell gas, suspect carbon monoxide, or a carbon monoxide alarm sounds, open doors and windows if you can do so without delay, leave the building, and get everyone into fresh air.
   - Turn off the gas at the meter only if it is safe and the meter is not in a cellar or basement.
   - In Great Britain, call the National Gas Emergency Service on **0800 111 999**.
   - In Northern Ireland, call the 24-hour Northern Ireland Gas Emergency Service on **0800 002 001**.
   - If anyone has symptoms such as breathing difficulty, sudden confusion, collapse, feeling weak, or chest pain, call **999**.

2. **Keep clear of wet electrics.**
   - If the switch, plug, fused spur, consumer unit, or floor around them is wet, do not touch it.
   - If you can switch off the relevant circuit from a dry, safe position, do that. If you cannot, keep away from the wet area and tell the emergency repair person that water may be near electrics.

3. **Turn off the heater’s heat source if it is safe.**
   - For an immersion heater or hot water cylinder, switch it off at the fused spur or isolator only if the switch and your hands are dry.
   - For a gas-fired water heater or boiler, use the appliance controls only if there is no gas smell and it is safe to access.
   - If there is any gas smell, do not use switches or controls; follow Step 1.

4. **Stop water feeding the heater.**
   - Turn off the cold-water inlet or isolating valve on the pipe feeding the cylinder or heater if you can identify it safely.
   - If you cannot find it, or the leak is fast, turn off the home’s internal stop tap or stopcock.
   - Do not force a stuck valve.

5. **Limit damage without taking risks.**
   - Put a bucket or tray under drips if you can do so safely.
   - Use towels to guide water away from ceilings, sockets, switches, appliances, and the consumer unit.
   - Move nearby belongings away from the leak, especially electrical items.

6. **Treat discharge from a tundish or safety pipe as urgent.**
   - If water is running or dripping through a tundish, discharge pipe, or overflow pipe, keep the heater off.
   - For an unvented hot water cylinder, ask for a plumber or heating engineer competent to work on unvented hot water cylinders.
   - Do not cap or block the discharge.

7. **Contact the right help.**
   - If you rent, contact your landlord or agent’s emergency repairs line and say there is a leaking or noisy hot water heater and that you have isolated heat and water if safe.
   - If you own, contact a qualified plumber or heating engineer.
   - If any gas appliance is involved, use a Gas Safe registered engineer and check they are qualified for the type of gas work needed.

8. **Record only what is quick and safe.**
   - Take photos or a short video of the leak, tundish flow, pipe area, and any damage.
   - Stop once you have enough to show the repair person, landlord, or insurer.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the cause is scale, sediment, corrosion, pressure, a valve fault, or a thermostat fault.
- You do not need to drain the whole cylinder unless a competent professional tells you to.
- You do not need to decide today whether the heater must be repaired or replaced.
- You do not need to clean everything immediately if electrics, ceilings, gas, or very hot water may be involved.

## Important reassurance
A leak or sudden popping noise can feel alarming because it involves water, heat, pressure, and sometimes gas or electricity. The useful first step is not to solve the fault; it is to get people away from danger, stop heat and water where safe, and bring in the right help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair, replacement, insurance, landlord duties, electrical checks, gas work, or cylinder safety may need a qualified professional or specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, electrical, gas-safety, plumbing, insurance, or professional advice. If there is immediate danger, suspected carbon monoxide, gas smell, severe illness, fire, or unsafe electrics, use the relevant emergency route first.
