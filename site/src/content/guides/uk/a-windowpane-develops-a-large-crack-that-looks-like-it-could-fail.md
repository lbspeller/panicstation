# What to do if… a windowpane develops a large crack that looks like it could fail (UK)

## Short answer
Keep everyone away from the window, do not move the frame or touch the glass, and arrange urgent help to make it safe.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t press on the glass, test it, or try to push the crack back together.
- Don’t open or close the window or door if doing so bends the frame or makes the crack move.
- Don’t pick at the crack, remove loose pieces, or use tools near the pane while it is still in place.
- Don’t use heat from a hairdryer, heater, or hot water to dry or stabilise the glass.
- Don’t leave children or pets unsupervised near the window, even if it still looks intact.
- Don’t attempt a full DIY repair with resin, epoxy, or replacement glass if the pane looks like it could fail.

## What to do now
1. **Make a safe zone immediately.** Keep people and pets out of the room if you can. If the window is above a path, driveway, garden, balcony, or shared area, keep people away from the outside as well.
2. **Reduce knocks without touching the pane.** Move chairs, tables, toys, plant pots, and anything else that could hit the window. Do not lean over furniture to reach the glass.
3. **Use curtains or blinds only if they can be moved safely.** If you can close them without touching the glass or shaking the frame, do that to help catch small fragments. If closing them would pull on the frame, leave them alone.
4. **Only tape a stable, reachable pane.** If the glass is flat, not loose, not bowing, and you can reach it without pressing on it, put on sturdy shoes and thick gloves, and apply wide clear packing tape in long strips across the crack. Stop if the crack moves, the glass flexes, or the frame shifts.
5. **If the glass is loose, bowing, or in a moving door panel, do not tape it.** Move away and arrange an emergency glazier or boarding service to secure it.
6. **Use the right reporting route for your home.**
   - **If you rent:** report it to your landlord or letting agent straight away in writing, such as by text or email, and say it is a safety or security risk. Keep photos and the message trail.
   - **If you own:** contact a glazier, emergency boarding service, installer, or manufacturer to make it safe and replace the glass.
   - **If it is council or housing association housing:** report it through the urgent repairs line or portal and keep the reference number.
7. **Call 999 if there is immediate danger you cannot control.** Use this if glass is actively falling, the pane may drop into a public or shared area, or someone could be injured before a repair service can make it safe.
8. **Take simple photos from a safe distance.** Photograph the whole window and the crack ends or edges if you can do this without going near unstable glass. Note when you first saw it and any obvious trigger, such as impact, wind, temperature change, or frame movement.
9. **Prepare for weather or security only after the area is safe.** Keep cardboard, plywood, plastic sheeting, and tape nearby in case an opening needs covering after the glass fails or after a professional removes it. Do not drill or screw into the frame while cracked glass is still under tension.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today who is at fault or argue about charges, deposits, or insurance.
- You do not need to clean up unless glass has already fallen, and even then keeping people away comes first.
- You do not need to choose a full window upgrade now; the urgent job is making the pane safe and secure.
- You do not need to prove the exact cause before reporting it.

## Important reassurance
A large crack can look still for a while and then change suddenly. Distance, fewer knocks, and quick help to secure the pane are the safest first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair costs, deposits, insurance, warranties, disputes, or full replacement may need specialist help after the window is safe.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent harm prevention, not legal, financial, building, glazing, or professional safety advice. If there is immediate danger to people, prioritise distance and emergency help.
