# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to have sex in exchange for money, housing, or help (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause first. You do not have to decide about reporting now; contact a specialist sexual violence service or urgent housing help before dealing with the person again.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to sex because you feel cornered, scared, indebted, or afraid of losing housing.
- Do not meet them alone in person if they are pressuring you.
- Do not hand over your phone, passwords, ID, bank cards, tenancy papers, or benefit letters.
- Do not delete messages, adverts, voicemails, payment requests, or threats if it is safe to keep them.
- Do not confront them to prove a point.
- Do not blame yourself or wait until you are sure what to call it before getting support.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move to a public place, lock a door, go to a neighbour or trusted person, or ask someone safe to stay on the phone while you leave. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**.

2. **Stop one-to-one access for now.** If it is safe, use a short message such as: “I’m not meeting in person. Message only.” If you still need belongings or access to housing, avoid being alone with them.

3. **Keep what already exists.** Save messages, adverts, payment requests, “you owe me” messages, eviction threats, or any wording linking money, housing, or help to sex. Put copies somewhere the other person cannot access, such as a safe email account or with a trusted person.

4. **Contact specialist sexual violence support.**
   - In **England or Wales**, if you are aged **16+**, call the free **24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line on 0808 500 2222** or use its online chat. It is not an emergency service.
   - In **Scotland**, contact **Rape Crisis Scotland on 08088 01 03 02**. Their helpline is usually open every day, 5pm to midnight.
   - In **Northern Ireland**, contact **Rape Crisis Northern Ireland on 0800 0246 991** and check their site for current opening hours.

5. **Ask about medical and support options if anything sexual has already happened.** A Sexual Assault Referral Centre or NHS service can explain health care, emotional support, and forensic options. You can ask what is available without deciding about police.

6. **Treat unsafe housing as urgent.**
   - In **England**, use your local council homelessness route.
   - In **Scotland**, contact your local council homelessness service.
   - In **Wales**, contact your local authority homelessness service.
   - In **Northern Ireland**, contact the Housing Executive.
   Say plainly: “I am being pressured for sex in connection with housing, money, or practical help, and I do not feel safe.”

7. **Ask one safe person to help with calls or forms.** They can sit with you, type while you talk, or stay on speaker while you contact support.

If you may want to report later, try not to wash away physical evidence or delete messages, but you do not need to decide anything about reporting right now.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to report to police.
- You do not need to confront the person or explain yourself to them.
- You do not need to collect perfect evidence.
- You do not need to decide your long-term housing plan in the next hour if the immediate goal is getting through the next safe night.
- You do not need to know the exact legal name for what happened before asking for help.

## Important reassurance
Being pressured into sex by someone controlling money, housing, or practical help is serious and it is not your fault. Feeling frozen, numb, compliant, panicked, confused, or unsure is a common response to being cornered.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get safer, reduce pressure, and reach specialist support. Later decisions about housing, health care, reporting, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, counselling, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**. If you are unsure what is safest, try to choose the option that gets you away from being alone with the person and into contact with specialist support.
