# What to do if… your phone is missing and “Find my device” shows it in an unexpected place (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the device by plugging it into a power source and avoid choosing "Restore" or "Factory Reset" until you have verified your cloud backups and account passwords. Attempt an "Update" or "Repair" via official manufacturer software first, as these options attempt to preserve data.

## Do not do these things
- Do not select **Restore** or **Wipe Data** until you have confirmed you have a backup or have accepted that all local data will be erased.
- Do not use third-party "one-click" repair software from unofficial websites; use only official tools from Apple, Google, or Samsung.
- Do not disconnect the cable once a process has started, as this can permanently corrupt the device's firmware.
- Do not attempt to "force restart" the device repeatedly if it returns to the same screen every time; this indicates a system failure that requires external software to fix.
- Do not use USB hubs or damaged cables; connect the device directly to a computer's main USB port.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilize the device power and temperature.**  
   **Connect the phone to a wall charger immediately to ensure it does not power off during a recovery attempt.** If the device feels excessively hot, leave it to cool for 20 minutes before attempting to connect it to a computer.
2. **Identify the specific recovery environment.**  
   **Observe the screen to determine which manufacturer-specific tool is required.**
   - iPhones/iPads showing a laptop and cable icon require the Apple Devices app (Windows), iTunes (older Windows/Mac), or Finder (macOS).
   - Pixel devices showing a "No Command" or "Recovery" text menu require the web-based Pixel Update and Software Repair Tool.
   - Samsung devices showing "Emergency Software Recovery" require the Smart Switch PC application.
3. **Check for a wireless restoration option (iOS 18+ only).**  
   **If you have an iPhone 16 or newer and another updated iPhone/iPad nearby, bring them together.** Follow the "Restore Nearby Device" prompts on the working device to see if a wireless system repair is possible without a computer.
4. **Verify your data backups and account credentials.**  
   **Sign in to your cloud account (iCloud.com or Google Photos/Drive) from another device to see the date of your last backup.** Ensure you know your Apple Account or Google Account password, as "Factory Reset Protection" will require these credentials once the phone is restored.
5. **Execute an "Update" rather than a "Restore" via a computer.**  
   **Connect the phone to your computer and open the official tool, then select the "Update" or "Repair" option when prompted.**
   - This attempts to reinstall the operating system without deleting your photos, messages, and apps.
   - If the tool only offers "Restore" or "Factory Reset," stop and re-evaluate your backup status before clicking.
6. **Troubleshoot connection failures systematically.**  
   **If the computer does not see the phone, swap the USB cable for an original or certified one and use a different USB port.** Ensure the computer's OS and the recovery software are fully updated to the latest 2026 versions.
7. **Document any specific error codes.**  
   **Write down any numerical codes (e.g., iPhone Error 4013 or Samsung Initialization errors) that appear on the computer screen.** These codes help technicians identify whether the fault is software-based or a hardware failure (such as a faulty storage chip).
8. **Consult official support if the update fails.**  
   **Contact the manufacturer's official UK support line or visit an authorized service center if the "Update" process loops back to the recovery screen.** Inform them that you are attempting to avoid data loss and provide the error codes recorded in Step 7.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the software crash right now.
- You do not need to purchase a new device until a professional confirms the hardware is unrepairable.
- You do not need to worry about storage management or app updates until the operating system is stable.
- You can delay investigating warranty or Consumer Rights Act claims until the technical status of the phone is confirmed.

## Important reassurance
Seeing a recovery screen is a standard safety feature of modern smartphones; it signifies that the software has detected an error and paused to prevent further corruption. While the situation is urgent, the device is currently in a "stable" recovery state. Most devices can be returned to a working state through official software tools, even if the last update failed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for devices stuck in recovery mode. It does not cover physical hardware repairs, motherboard-level data recovery, or advanced command-line debugging.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional technical support. Software restoration carries an inherent risk of data loss, especially if backups are not current. Under UK law, if a device is less than 30 days old and develops this fault, you may have a short-term right to a refund. Between 30 days and six months, the retailer is generally entitled to one attempt at repair or replacement before a refund is issued.
