# What to do if… you suspect someone is trying to pickpocket you in a crowd (UK)

## Short answer
Create space now: move to a staffed, well-lit or busier spot, put your bag in front of you, and cover the pocket or bag opening they are near. If the behaviour continues, a crime is happening, the person stays nearby, or you feel in danger, ask staff for help and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stop in a secluded spot to sort your bag out; move to a staffed, well-lit or busier area first.
- Don’t pull out your wallet or phone in the middle of the crowd to check it.
- Don’t let yourself be steered into a tighter space, such as a doorway, stairwell, lift or narrow platform edge.
- Don’t get into a shouting match or try to physically stop them; they may be working with someone else, and confrontation can escalate.
- Don’t chase someone if they run; you may lose sight of your own belongings or move into more danger.
- Don’t accept “help” from a stranger who insists on touching your bag, coat or pockets.

## What to do now
1. **Move your body first.**  
   Step sideways out of the flow of people. Put your back to a wall, pillar, shop counter or ticket barrier area, or move near staff, security, a help point, a shop counter or a well-lit place.

2. **Make the pocket or bag harder to reach.**  
   Put your bag in front of you, close zips and flaps, and keep one hand over the pocket or bag opening they were near. If you have a backpack, bring it to your front or hold it close by the top handle.

3. **Break the pattern without arguing.**  
   Change direction, step into a nearby shop or café, or stand beside staff or a visible group of people. If you are with someone, say clearly: “Stay with me. Let’s move over there.”

4. **Create witnesses.**  
   Tell staff or security: “Someone keeps crowding me and reaching near my pockets or bag. Can I stand here, and can you help me contact police if needed?”

5. **If this is on a train or at a station, use railway help routes.**  
   Move towards staff, a help point, a busier carriage or other passengers. Call 999 if a crime is happening, the person is still nearby, or someone is in danger. If it does not need an emergency response, text British Transport Police on 61016 or call 0800 40 50 40.

6. **If this is not on the railway and it is not an emergency, use police non-emergency routes.**  
   If you want to report what happened, or you think something was taken but there is no immediate danger, call 101 or report it online through police.uk.

7. **Check essentials only once you are in a safer spot.**  
   Without laying items out, check for your phone, wallet or cards, keys, travel pass and ID. Keep your bag closed while checking.

8. **If a card, wallet or bank details may be missing, contact your bank first.**  
   Use your banking app, call your bank, or ask staff or a trusted person to help you contact the bank. Freeze or cancel affected cards and report any unauthorised payments.

9. **If your phone may be missing, protect accounts next.**  
   Contact your mobile network to bar the SIM when you can. Secure your email account first, then banking, phone-provider and payment apps. Change passwords from a device you trust, and turn on 2-step verification where available.

## What can wait
- Writing a perfect description of the person or replaying the moment in detail.
- Replacing documents, filing insurance claims or making a full timeline.
- Deciding whether it was definitely intentional if you are not sure.
- Checking every pocket repeatedly while you are still in the crowd.
- Confronting the person or trying to prove what happened.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to freeze, doubt yourself or feel embarrassed in a crowd. You do not need to prove anything before you move away, protect your belongings and ask for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and limit quick follow-on harm, such as card, phone or account access. Later decisions, such as formal reports, replacements, complaints or insurance, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate safety and damage-limitation, not legal, financial, security or professional advice. If you feel unsafe right now, prioritise getting to a safer staffed place and contacting emergency services.
