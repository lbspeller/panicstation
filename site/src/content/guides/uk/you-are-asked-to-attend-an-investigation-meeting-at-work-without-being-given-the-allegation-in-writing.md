# What to do if… you are asked to attend an investigation meeting at work without being given the allegation in writing (UK)

## Short answer
Decline to spend your own money until you have written approval and a confirmed reimbursement method from a person with the authority to approve it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use personal cash or credit cards if you cannot afford to wait for repayment.
- Do not accept verbal promises or vague statements like we will sort it out later as valid approval.
- Do not exceed any agreed spending limit, even for minor additions like VAT or delivery fees.
- Do not provide your personal bank details to external suppliers or click payment links from unknown sources.
- Do not discard physical or digital receipts, invoices, or the original written instruction to buy the items.
- Do not take out a personal loan or use an overdraft to cover business operating costs.

## What to do now
1. **Request written clarification of the requirement and the repayment process.** Reply to the requester via email or a tracked messaging system and ask for:
   - a specific description of the items or services required
   - the exact maximum total budget authorised
   - the name and job title of the individual approving the expenditure
   - the specific method of reimbursement (e.g., expenses software, bank transfer, or payroll)
   - the exact documentation required for a claim (e.g., VAT invoice or physical receipt)
2. **Propose an alternative business-funded payment method.** Ask if the business can provide:
   - a corporate credit or debit card
   - a Purchase Order (PO) to be sent to the supplier
   - a direct invoice to be sent to the finance department
   - a petty cash advance or float provided before the purchase is made
3. **Establish a boundary to protect your personal finances if the business insists.** State clearly that you will only process the payment once written approval and the reimbursement route are documented, and confirm you will not exceed the stated cap.
4. **Gather all evidence immediately if you have already spent your own money.**
   - Save original digital receipts as PDFs and take clear photographs of physical receipts.
   - Record the date, time, and specific business purpose of the purchase.
   - Note the platform and time where the instruction was given (e.g., screenshot of a chat message).
5. **Submit a formal expense claim following the company policy exactly.**
   - Locate the staff handbook or intranet policy to identify approval thresholds and deadlines.
   - Forward the claim and all evidence to the finance or payroll department immediately.
6. **Trigger a formal grievance if repayment is refused or unreasonably delayed.**
   - Submit a written complaint to HR or a senior manager stating the facts and attaching evidence.
   - Request a formal grievance meeting; you have a statutory right to be accompanied by a colleague or a trade union representative at this hearing.
7. **Contact HMRC or the Fair Work Agency if the cost reduces your pay below the National Minimum Wage.** If the cost of equipment or supplies is not reimbursed, it is treated as a deduction from your pay.
   - Check if your hourly rate (Total Pay minus Unreimbursed Costs divided by Hours Worked) falls below £12.71 (for those 21+).
   - Use the GOV.UK "Pay and work rights" online form to report a suspected breach.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today if this is grounds for resignation or a constructive dismissal claim.
- You do not need to look for tax relief for unreimbursed costs, as the flat-rate homeworking tax relief was abolished in April 2026.
- You do not need to engage in emotional arguments with managers about why the request was made.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard professional practice to require a paper trail before using personal funds for business costs. Most employers expect staff to follow a formal expenses policy; asking for that policy is a protective step, not a sign of distrust or lack of commitment.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate financial protection and the creation of a paper trail. It does not cover specific legal strategies for Employment Tribunals or complex contract disputes.

## Important note
This information is for guidance purposes only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Employment rights regarding expenses depend on your specific contract and the nature of your role. If the amounts are high or your employer refuses to pay, seek advice from Acas or a qualified employment specialist.
