# What to do if… your visa or permit is approved but your document number field is blank or inconsistent on the confirmation (UK)

## Short answer
Do not attempt to travel or complete mandatory right-to-work or right-to-rent checks using only the confirmation notification. Sign in to your UKVI account to verify your digital record and use the official "Report an error with your eVisa" service if the live data is incorrect.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a confirmation email or PDF letter is a valid travel document or proof of status.
- Do not enter placeholder numbers or incorrect passport details into official forms to bypass a blank field.
- Do not book travel that requires a "Permission-to-Travel" check until you have confirmed your passport is correctly linked to your eVisa.
- Do not share copies of the inconsistent confirmation on social media or public forums for advice.
- Do not delete the confirmation notification, as it contains your Unique Application Number (UAN) or Global Web Form (GWF) reference.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific reference numbers provided in your approval notification.**  
   - Look for the 16-digit Unique Application Number (UAN).  
   - Look for the Global Web Form (GWF) number.  
   - Locate your UKVI Customer Number if present.  

2. **Access the "View and prove your immigration status" service on GOV.UK.**  
   - Sign in using the identity document used for your application (passport or national ID).  
   - Check if the digital record (eVisa) matches your actual details.  
   - Confirm if the "document number" field in the live system is also blank or contains an error.

3. **Verify that your current passport is correctly linked to your digital status.**  
   - Use the "Update your UK Visas and Immigration account details" service if your newest passport is not listed.  
   - Ensure the nationality and document expiry dates match your physical document exactly.

4. **Report the discrepancy via the "Report an error with your eVisa" portal if the live record is wrong.**  
   - Select the specific field that is incorrect (e.g., name, status, or document number).  
   - Provide your UAN or GWF number as the primary reference for the correction.  
   - Save a screenshot or PDF of the submission confirmation page.

5. **Generate a share code to test the validity of your status for third parties.**  
   - Attempt to create a share code for "Right to Work" or "Right to Rent."  
   - If the system produces a 9-digit alphanumeric code, your status is likely active even if the initial notification was poorly formatted.

6. **Contact the UKVI Resolution Centre if you are locked out or see "No Record Found."**  
   - Call +44 (0)300 790 6268 and select the option for technical issues.  
   - Have your UAN and identity document ready before calling.

7. **Notify your employer, landlord, or university of the technical delay if an immediate check is required.**  
   - Advise them that you are awaiting a manual correction from the Home Office.  
   - Provide them with the UAN from your approval notice as evidence that an application was successful.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint against the Home Office in the first 72 hours.
- You do not need to request a new biometric document, as physical BRPs and vignettes are no longer the primary proof of status.
- You do not need to determine the cause of the system error (e.g., template glitch vs. data entry error).

## Important reassurance
Inconsistent or blank fields on automated approval emails are often caused by notification template errors rather than underlying record failures. As long as you can sign in to your UKVI account and see your correct status and dates, the blank field on the email is usually a formatting issue that does not invalidate your permission to stay.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only for stabilizing a documentation discrepancy. It does not cover legal appeals, administrative reviews, or complex identity disputes.

## Important note
This guide is for general information and does not constitute legal advice. Immigration rules and digital systems in the UK are subject to frequent technical updates; always verify current procedures on official GOV.UK portals.
