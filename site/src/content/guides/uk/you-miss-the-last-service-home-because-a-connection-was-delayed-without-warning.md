# What to do if… you miss the last service home because a connection was delayed without warning (UK)

## Short answer
Locate a member of staff or a station Help Point immediately to request that the train operating company arrange alternative onward transport or overnight accommodation under the National Rail Conditions of Travel. Do not leave the station area until a plan for safe transport is confirmed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to walk long distances home or to another station via unfamiliar or unlit routes at night.
- Do not accept offers of transport from unlicensed individuals or strangers offering lifts outside the station.
- Do not pay for a private taxi or hotel room without first attempting to contact the operator, as they may refuse to reimburse costs they did not authorize.
- Do not assume your rights are void if you have split tickets; as long as you allowed the minimum connection time, you are still protected.
- Do not wait in isolated or dark areas of the station; stay in view of CCTV, near Help Points, or in staffed areas.

## What to do now
1. **Move immediately to a well-lit, monitored area such as a staffed ticket office, gated entrance, or near a station Help Point.** Staying visible under CCTV coverage or near other passengers increases your immediate safety while you resolve the situation.
2. **Contact a member of staff or use the "Emergency" or "Information" button on a station Help Point to report your situation.** State clearly: "I have missed my last connection because my previous train was delayed; I am now stranded and need the operator to arrange onward travel to my destination."
3. **Confirm that your tickets met the required "Minimum Connection Time" for that specific station.** 
   - Most stations require 5 to 15 minutes; check the National Rail website or ask staff to verify this.
   - If you used split tickets, confirm the train you arrived on was scheduled to call at the station where your tickets "split."
4. **Ask the staff or Help Point operator to arrange a specific alternative under Section 28 of the National Rail Conditions of Travel.** 
   - Request a taxi (arranged and paid for by the operator) to your final destination.
   - Request a rail replacement bus if one is being dispatched.
   - Request overnight hotel accommodation if no road transport can be sourced and it is unsafe to remain at the station.
5. **Note the name of the staff member you speak to and the time of the conversation.** If they refuse to help, ask for the reason and request that they log the refusal in their system or provide a "failure to provide transport" note.
6. **Use the British Transport Police (BTP) text service on 61016 if you feel unsafe or see suspicious behavior.** Text your location and the nature of the issue; this is a non-emergency line monitored 24/7 for railway-specific safety concerns. Call 999 only if there is an immediate danger to life or a crime in progress.
7. **Take photos of the station departure boards showing "No further services" and keep all original tickets.** These will be required for any subsequent Delay Repay claims or reimbursement requests if you are eventually forced to self-fund a taxi.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal "Delay Repay" claim or a complaint against the operator tonight.
- You do not need to determine which specific operator was "at fault" for the delay if multiple companies were involved; the operator at the station where you are stranded is responsible for assisting you.
- You do not need to worry about the April 2026 refund rule changes right now; those rules regarding "no refunds after validity starts" do not apply when travel is prevented by disruption.

## Important reassurance
Railway operators have a regulatory obligation to ensure passengers are not left stranded mid-journey due to circumstances within the rail industry's control. While the process of arranging a taxi or bus can take time late at night, a solution exists. Focus on staying in a visible, safe location until that transport arrives.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for passengers stranded at a UK railway station due to a missed connection. It does not cover claims for consequential losses (such as missed flights or events) or detailed legal appeals through the Rail Ombudsman.

## Important note
Under Section 28.2 of the National Rail Conditions of Travel, train companies must provide alternative travel or overnight accommodation if disruption prevents you from completing your journey. However, they may not be liable if the delay was caused by events outside the railway's control (such as extreme weather or police activity), though they still have a duty of care to ensure passenger safety.
