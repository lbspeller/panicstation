# What to do if… your car heater or defroster is not working and your windows keep fogging up (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive until you can see clearly through the windscreen, front side windows, and mirrors. Switch to dry air: fresh air in, recirculation off, fan high, and air conditioning on if available. If the windows will not stay clear, arrange help instead of setting off.

## Do not do these things
- Do not just go slowly with a misted windscreen or side windows; if you cannot see clearly, do not move off.
- Do not run the engine in a garage or other enclosed space, even with the door open.
- Do not sit for a long time with the car sealed up and the engine running; keep fresh air coming in.
- Do not use hot water on the windscreen, because it can crack the glass.
- Do not wipe the glass with anything gritty, because it can scratch, smear, or worsen glare.
- Do not keep recirculation on if fogging is getting worse, because it can trap moisture inside.
- Do not open the bonnet and remove caps from a hot cooling system, or reach near moving parts.

## What to do now
1. **Make the situation safe to pause.** Stay parked. If you are in a risky place, such as a narrow verge or awkward lay-by, only reposition if you have a clear enough view to move safely. If you cannot see well enough to move, stay put and use hazard lights if appropriate.
2. **If you are in a garage or other enclosed space, get outside first.** Turn the engine off, open the door, and move the car outside only once you can see enough to do so safely, or ask for assistance.
3. **Switch to dry air settings.**
   - Select **windscreen demist/defrost** mode, if available.
   - Set **fresh air intake**.
   - Turn **recirculation off**.
   - Set **fan high**.
4. **Turn air conditioning on if the car has it, even if the heater is not warming.** Air conditioning can help remove moisture from cabin air. If the heater will not warm, air conditioning, fan high, and fresh air may still help clear fog.
5. **Let damp air out.** If it is safe, open two windows slightly for a short time, then reassess.
6. **Clear the inside glass properly.**
   - Wipe the **inside** of the windscreen and front side windows with a clean, dry microfibre cloth or clean paper towel.
   - If you use wipes or glass cleaner, finish with a dry wipe to reduce smearing.
7. **Remove obvious moisture sources.** Move wet coats, umbrellas, bags, and cloths off the dashboard and footwells. Bag wet items if you can. If mats are soaked, lift or stand them up to drain when safe.
8. **Re-check whether it is safe to drive.** Only set off if you can keep:
   - the **full windscreen clear**,
   - both **front side windows** clear enough for mirror and junction checks, and
   - mirrors clear and usable.
   If fog returns quickly or visibility stays patchy, **do not drive**.
9. **If you cannot keep it clear, get assistance.**
   - Call your breakdown provider or a local recovery service and say: “Heater or defroster is not working; windows are fogging; visibility is unsafe.”
   - If you are stranded in a dangerous location and feel at immediate risk, call **999**.
   - If anyone has symptoms that could fit carbon monoxide exposure, such as headache, dizziness, confusion, weakness, chest pain, or trouble breathing after the engine has been running, get into fresh air and seek urgent medical help.

## What can wait
- Diagnosing the cause, such as coolant level, heater matrix, blower motor, cabin filter, or air conditioning faults.
- Any DIY repairs beyond basic cabin clearing.
- Booking a garage appointment, unless that is your safest way to avoid driving.
- Deciding whether the car is worth repairing; the priority now is safe visibility and a safe next step.

## Important reassurance
This is a common problem and it can feel urgent because visibility drops quickly. Stopping, drying the cabin air, and refusing to drive until you can see clearly is a safe and reasonable decision.

## Scope note
This is first-steps guidance for the moment you realise the windows will not stay clear. Later decisions about the fault, repair, recovery, or onward travel may need help from a mechanic, breakdown service, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, mechanical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Do not drive if you cannot maintain clear visibility. If you notice overheating, steam, a burning smell, warning lights, or possible carbon monoxide symptoms, treat it as urgent and seek appropriate professional help.
