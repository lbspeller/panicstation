# What to do if… your car’s locks or windows stop working and you can’t secure the vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Move the vehicle to a well-lit, busy area, remove all valuables and ownership documents, and contact a professional auto locksmith or breakdown service to secure the vehicle.

## Do not do these things
- Don't leave the vehicle unattended in an unlit or isolated area while it is insecure.
- Don't leave the V5C logbook or any documents displaying your home address inside the car.
- Don't attempt to force a window up or a lock closed using tools, as this may shatter the glass or damage the actuator.
- Don't confront individuals if you suspect the failure is due to attempted tampering or theft.
- Don't leave the keys in the ignition or anywhere inside the vehicle even if it cannot be locked.

## What to do now
1. **Move the vehicle immediately to a safer, well-lit, and highly visible location if it is safe to drive.** Seek a "Park Mark" accredited car park or a location with active CCTV coverage to deter opportunistic theft while the vehicle is vulnerable.
2. **Remove all portable valuables and sensitive documentation from the cabin and boot.** Ensure you take the V5C logbook and any insurance or utility bills, as these can be used by thieves to clone the vehicle or identify your home address.
3. **Check if the vehicle has a manual deadlocking or "double locking" function that still operates.** Many UK vehicles allow you to turn the physical key twice in the door lock to mechanically engage deadlocks even if the central locking or key fob has failed.
4. **Call 999 if you believe a crime is in progress or if the vehicle has been freshy tampered with and the suspect is nearby.** For non-emergency reporting of suspected tampering where there is no immediate threat, use the 101 service or the police online reporting tool.
5. **Contact an approved mobile auto locksmith or your breakdown recovery provider.** Inform them specifically if a window is stuck open or a door will not latch, as this may require a "low loader" recovery if the vehicle cannot be safely secured for roadside repair.
6. **Ensure you have access to your motor insurance certificate or digital proof of cover.** Under 2026 enforcement rules, police may require immediate proof of insurance if they stop a vehicle that appears unsecured or suspicious.
7. **Document the specific nature of the failure with photos or video once you are in a safe position.** Capture the state of the locks or windows and the immediate surroundings for use in potential insurance claims or police reports.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical cause of the electrical or mechanical failure right now.
- You do not need to initiate a full insurance claim until the vehicle is either repaired or secured in a permanent location.
- You do not need to worry about long-term anti-theft upgrades while focus is on immediate stabilization.

## Important reassurance
Sudden mechanical or electrical failures in locking systems are common and often result from simple fuse issues or motor wear. By removing targets and placing the vehicle in a visible area, you significantly reduce the likelihood of a security incident while waiting for professional assistance.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an insecure vehicle only. It does not cover mechanical repair procedures or legal disputes regarding vehicle warranties.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional mechanical advice. Personal safety must always take priority over vehicle security. In any situation involving a crime in progress or a threat to safety, contact emergency services immediately.
