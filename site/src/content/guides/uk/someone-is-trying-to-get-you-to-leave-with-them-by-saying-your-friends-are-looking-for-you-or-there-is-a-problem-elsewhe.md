# What to do if… someone is trying to get you to leave with them by saying your friends are looking for you or there is a problem elsewhere (UK)

## Short answer
Do not move to a second location with the person. Stay in a well-lit, populated, or staffed area and contact your friends directly using your own phone to verify their location.

## Do not do these things
- Don't step into a "quiet place," around a corner, into an alley, or toward a vehicle to "discuss" the situation.
- Don't hand your phone to the person or let them dial a number for you.
- Don't worry about being rude; safety is more important than politeness when someone is trying to move you.
- Don't follow them to "check" if their story is true.
- Don't get into any taxi, car, or lift they claim to have arranged for you.
- Don't leave your personal belongings (bag or coat) behind if it would leave you without your keys or phone.

## What to do now
1. **Stop moving and create physical distance.** Plant your feet and refuse to walk further, especially if they are leading you toward an exit or a less crowded area.
2. **Move toward a position of authority or a crowd.** Step toward a bar, a shop counter, a security guard, or a group of people and stay there.
3. **Use a clear, firm refusal.** State: "I am staying here. I am calling my friends myself." Speak loudly so that others nearby can hear you.
4. **Call or message your friends directly.** Verify their location yourself rather than believing the stranger. If your friends do not answer, stay with staff or in the crowd.
5. **Ask for help from venue staff or security immediately.** Go to the nearest staff member and say: "I do not know this person and they are trying to get me to leave. Please stay with me."
6. **Use the "Ask for Angela" code if in a participating venue.** Approach staff and ask for "Angela." They are trained to move you to a safe space and help you leave safely or contact the police.
7. **Call 999 if the person becomes aggressive or blocks your path.** Treat any attempt to grab you, follow you closely, or prevent you from staying in a safe area as a high-priority emergency.
8. **Utilise the Silent Solution if you cannot speak on a 999 call.** 
   - Dial **999** from a mobile and listen. 
   - An automated message will play for 20 seconds. 
   - When prompted, **press 55** to alert the operator that it is a genuine emergency. 
   - If you do not press 55, the call may be terminated.
9. **Identify specific witnesses.** Point to someone and say: "I don't feel safe, can I stand with you while I call my lift?" This makes the situation visible to others.
10. **Note descriptions only once you are secure.** If you are safely behind a locked door or with staff, write down the person's appearance, the exact words they used, and any vehicle details you saw.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the person was "actually" a threat or just confused right now.
- You do not need to confront the person or prove they are lying.
- You do not need to provide a formal police statement until you have reached a safe, permanent location.
- You do not need to stay at the venue or continue your night; it is acceptable to go home with a trusted contact.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of "social panic" or a desire to be polite when someone presents an urgent problem. This type of lure is designed to exploit your helpfulness or your fear for your friends' safety. Choosing to stay in a crowded area and verify the information yourself is a standard safety precaution, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step guidance only for avoiding immediate isolation and securing help in public spaces. Long-term reporting or legal actions are secondary to reaching a safe location.

## Important note
This information is for general safety purposes and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving an immediate threat to life or safety, always contact emergency services by dialing 999 or 112.
