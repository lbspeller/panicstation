# What to do if… your computer suddenly will not start and you need files that were only stored on it (UK)

## Short answer
Disable contact synchronization for your primary accounts and secure your Apple, Google, or Microsoft account before attempting to delete any entries.

## Do not do these things
- Don't delete any contacts immediately, as you may accidentally remove legitimate data or lose the ability to roll back the changes.
- Don't reply to, call, or message any unknown numbers to verify their identity.
- Don't install third-party "contact cleaner" or "duplicate remover" apps from any app store.
- Don't share multi-factor authentication (MFA) codes or password reset links with anyone.
- Don't assume the issue is limited to your physical phone without checking the web-based version of your account.

## What to do now
1. **Pause contact synchronization on your mobile device immediately.**
   - Navigate to your device settings (e.g., Settings > Mail/Contacts > Accounts).
   - Locate your primary accounts (iCloud, Google, Outlook).
   - Toggle the **Contacts** switch to **Off**.
   - If prompted, select **Keep on My iPhone/Device** to prevent further cloud changes from affecting your local storage.

2. **Access your account via a web browser on a separate trusted computer.**
   - Log in to your primary contact provider (e.g., icloud.com, contacts.google.com, or outlook.live.com).
   - Verify if the unknown entries appear in the web view.
   - If the entries are only on the phone, the issue is likely a local app or a recently added account.
   - If they are on the web view, the account itself or a connected service is the source.

3. **Check for and revoke unrecognised linked devices and third-party apps.**
   - Review the **Security** or **Devices** section of your account.
   - Sign out of any devices you do not recognise.
   - Revoke access for any third-party applications that have permission to "Manage Contacts."
   - Check messaging apps like WhatsApp or Signal for "Linked Devices" and remove any sessions you did not authorize.

4. **Secure the account by updating credentials and security protocols.**
   - Change your account password using a unique, strong phrase.
   - Enable or re-verify **Two-Step Verification** (MFA).
   - Check for any new recovery email addresses or phone numbers you did not add.

5. **Utilize official recovery tools to restore your contacts list.**
   - **For iCloud:** Log in to icloud.com on a computer, go to **Data Recovery**, and select **Restore Contacts** to revert to an archived version from the last 30 days.
   - **For Google Contacts:** Click the **Settings (gear icon)** and select **Undo changes** to revert the list to a state from any point in the last 30 days.
   - **For Outlook:** Use the **Restore deleted items** or **Restore contacts** feature in the People/Contacts web view.

6. **Check for installed Management Profiles (MDM).**
   - On iPhone, go to **Settings > General > VPN & Device Management**.
   - On Android, check **Settings > Security > Device Admin Apps**.
   - If a work or school profile was recently added, it may be syncing a global directory into your personal list.

7. **Report suspected cyber crime if you believe your account was compromised.**
   - In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, contact **Report Fraud** at 0300 123 2040 or via reportfraud.police.uk.
   - In Scotland, call **Police Scotland** on 101.

## What can wait
- You do not need to perform a factory reset of your device at this stage.
- You do not need to notify your entire contact list about the incident unless you have evidence that messages were sent from your account.
- You do not need to manually delete individual random entries until after the sync-side restoration is attempted.

## Important reassurance
Sudden changes to a contacts list are often caused by a configuration error, such as a new email account syncing a public directory or a one-time import mistake. Even if the account has been compromised, most major providers offer a 30-day window to revert changes. Following a methodical "pause and check" approach prevents permanent data loss.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital contact list corruption or unrecognised syncing. It does not cover the full recovery of a stolen identity or the removal of deep-level malware, which may require specialized technical support.

Recovery options are subject to the terms and technical limitations of your specific service provider. If you use a device provided by your employer, contact your internal IT department before removing any management profiles or accounts.
