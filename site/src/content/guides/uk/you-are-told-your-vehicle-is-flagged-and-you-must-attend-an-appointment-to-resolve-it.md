# What to do if… you are told your vehicle is flagged and you must attend an appointment to resolve it (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the contact is genuine through an independent official channel and gather physical evidence of your identity, ownership, and insurance before attending any meeting.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay any money, fees, or "fines" over the phone or via link to "clear" a vehicle marker or flag.
- Don’t call back any number provided in a text message or email without verifying it via the official police website.
- Don’t drive the vehicle to the appointment if the flag relates to a lack of insurance, a stolen report, or cloned plates.
- Don’t ignore a verified request from the police, as this can lead to the vehicle being stopped and seized under Section 165A of the Road Traffic Act 1988.
- Don’t hand over original documents to anyone without keeping high-quality digital scans or photocopies for your own records.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the identity of the person contacting you using an independent route.**
   - Call the non-emergency police number (**101**) or use the official "Report Fraud" service portal if you suspect a scam.
   - Ask the switchboard to confirm the name of the officer and the existence of the specific appointment or reference number provided.
2. **Identify the specific reason for the vehicle flag or marker.**
   - Ask if the flag relates to suspected cloning (your plates on another car), insurance discrepancies, an "Information Only" marker, or a "Lost/Stolen" report.
   - Write down the name of the police station, the unit involved (e.g., ANPR Intercept Team), and the specific documents they require you to bring.
3. **Verify your vehicle’s official status using GOV.UK services.**
   - Check your vehicle's MOT and basic tax status on the GOV.UK "Check if a vehicle is taxed" page.
   - Use the **askMID** (Motor Insurance Database) "Check Your Vehicle" service to ensure your insurance policy is correctly recorded as active.
4. **Collate a physical "proof pack" for the appointment.**
   - Include your photocard driving licence and a secondary proof of address (e.g., utility bill).
   - Bring the V5C log book and the original certificate of motor insurance (printed or official PDF).
   - If the vehicle was recently purchased, include the bill of sale or receipt.
5. **Arrange for safe transport to the police station.**
   - Use a different vehicle or public transport if there is any risk that the flagged vehicle is currently uninsured or incorrectly registered.
   - If you must take the flagged vehicle, ensure you have a passenger who can drive it away if you are detained or if the vehicle is prohibited from further use.
6. **Seek legal advice if the flag implies criminal involvement.**
   - Consult a solicitor before attending if you are told the vehicle is linked to a serious crime, a "fail to stop" incident, or if you believe you may be interviewed under caution.
   - Identify a local legal aid solicitor or duty solicitor contact details in case they are needed during the appointment.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to file a complaint about the marker being placed.
- You do not need to contact the previous owner or the dealership until you have confirmed the exact nature of the database error.
- You do not need to delete or change your insurance policy before the meeting; wait for the police to confirm what the discrepancy is.

## Important reassurance
Vehicle markers are frequently the result of administrative errors, data entry delays by insurers, or precautionary measures due to similar vehicles being involved in crime. Receiving a request to attend an appointment does not automatically mean you are a suspect or that you have committed an offence; it is often the standard procedure to verify physical documents against database records.

## Scope note
This is first-step stabilization guidance focused on verifying official contact and preparing for a voluntary or requested appointment. It does not cover the legal defence of criminal charges or the long-term process of removing markers from the Police National Computer (PNC).

## Important note
This information is for general guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Police procedures for vehicle markers can vary by force. If you suspect you are being targeted by a scam, report it to the "Report Fraud" service (formerly Action Fraud) at 0300 123 2040 or via their website.
