# What to do if… your smart lock or keypad shows repeated failed entry attempts while you are inside (UK)

## Short answer
Treat repeated failed entry attempts as a possible attempted break-in until you are sure it is not. Move everyone away from the door and front-facing glass, stay inside, and call 999 if someone may be trying to get in or you feel at risk.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to check or argue through it.
- Do not go outside to investigate, even if it goes quiet.
- Do not stand in front of glass panels, sidelights, or windows facing the door.
- Do not unlock the door remotely to reset the lock.
- Do not shout details such as “I’m alone” or “I’m calling the police”.
- Do not share entry codes or confirm who is inside if someone calls or messages claiming they need the code.
- Do not assume it is only a glitch if the attempts are repeated, close together, or matched with noises at the door.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer inside position.** Bring children and anyone dependent on you to the safest reachable interior room, away from the entry door and front-facing glass. Take your phone and, if they are safely within reach, your keys.
2. **Create distance from the entry point.** Quietly close and lock any internal door between you and the entry door, such as a hall door, stair door, or room door. Stay out of the door’s line of sight.
3. **Check without approaching the door.** Use a doorbell camera, peephole viewer, lock app, or indoor camera if you already have one. Do not go to the door for a better look.
4. **Save what you can without exposing yourself.** If your app or camera lets you save the alert, screenshot the failed attempts, or record a clip from where you are, do that. Do not spend time reviewing long logs while the situation is active.
5. **Make the doorway less inviting only from safety.** If you can control an outside light, hall light, or camera light from where you are, turn it on. Do not move into view of the door or windows to do this.
6. **Use lock controls carefully.** If your lock or app clearly offers keypad lockout, vacation mode, or temporary keypad disabling after failed attempts, enable it only if you can do so calmly and without unlocking the door. Avoid repeated settings changes that could accidentally unlock it.
7. **Call for help based on risk.**
   - Call 999 if you believe someone is trying to break in, you hear the handle or door being tested, you can see someone at the door, the attempts are continuing, or you feel in danger.
   - If you call 999 and cannot speak safely, stay on the line if you can and follow the operator’s prompts. If possible, whisper, cough, or tap to show the call is real. From a mobile, press 55 if prompted to be put through to the police.
   - If it has stopped and you are confident there is no immediate danger, use 101 later for non-emergency reporting or advice.
8. **If you live in a block or managed building, stay inside.** Contact building security, concierge, or the managing agent if you have an immediate route to them, and ask them to check communal areas or CCTV while you remain inside. If you can quietly contact a trusted neighbour, ask them to stay alert and call police if they see someone at your door.
9. **Keep key details ready.** Have your exact address, which door or entrance is involved, how many people are inside, what you are seeing or hearing, and any camera description ready for the call handler.

## What can wait
- Resetting the smart lock, changing codes, adding or removing users, or updating firmware.
- Calling the lock manufacturer, installer, landlord, or managing agent unless they are part of your immediate building-security route.
- Reviewing the full lock activity history or trying to identify the person.
- Touching the outside door, keypad, handle, or lock area if there are signs of tampering. Take photos later, once you are safe.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to take repeated failed entry attempts seriously. Smart locks and keypads can show failed attempts for innocent reasons, but you do not need to prove what is happening before putting distance between you and the door.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and avoid risky moves. After you are safe, later decisions about reporting, lock settings, building security, or replacement hardware may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, or other professional advice. If you feel at risk or unsure, choose the safer option and contact the police.
