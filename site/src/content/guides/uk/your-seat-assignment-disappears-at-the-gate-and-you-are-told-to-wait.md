# What to do if… your seat assignment disappears at the gate and you are told to wait (UK)

## Short answer
Stay at the gate and ask staff to state what status their system shows for you now, such as confirmed, seat to be assigned at the gate, standby, or not travelling on this flight. Ask for a printed boarding pass, written note, or visible booking note before boarding closes.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the gate area unless you tell gate staff first and know how they will call you back.
- Don’t assume “wait” means you are definitely still confirmed; ask what the system shows.
- Don’t hand over a cabin bag without asking where it will go if you are not boarded on this flight.
- Don’t argue with another passenger about a seat; keep the conversation with gate staff.
- Don’t accept a lower cabin or different flight under pressure without asking what reason is being recorded and what options are being offered.

## What to do now
1. **Keep proof before screens refresh.** Screenshot your booking confirmation, receipt, seat selection record if you have one, and the current boarding pass screen showing no seat or a changed seat.

2. **Go to the gate podium and ask one clear question.** Say: “What status does your system show for me right now: confirmed, seat to be assigned at the gate, standby, or not travelling on this flight?” Then ask: “Can you print a boarding pass or show me the note on my booking?”

3. **Ask staff to check the practical causes.** Say: “Could you check whether this is due to an aircraft change, blocked or faulty seat, duplicate seat assignment, seating hold, split booking, or paid-seat issue?” You are asking them to look, not debating policy.

4. **If you paid for a seat or cabin, say it plainly.** Say: “I paid for or selected this seat or cabin. If it cannot be provided, please note that on my booking and tell me what you are offering instead.”

5. **Turn “wait” into a decision point.** Ask: “What is the next point when you will know, and what is the latest point before boarding closes when you can tell me yes or no?” Stay close enough to hear your name or see the gate screen.

6. **If you have a tight connection or time-critical reason, say it now.** Ask staff to add it to the booking note and tell you whether the current delay at the gate risks that connection or commitment.

7. **If you are travelling with a child, need to stay with a companion, or have disability access needs, state that clearly.** Ask staff to record the need and confirm what seating or boarding assistance can still be arranged before boarding closes.

8. **If staff say you will not board this flight, ask for the recorded reason and your immediate options.** Say: “Is the recorded reason lack of space on the aircraft, aircraft change, safety or security, documentation, or something else?” Then ask what rerouting, refund, care and assistance, or downgrade information they can give you now.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim compensation, or escalate formally.
- You do not need to calculate compensation amounts at the gate.
- You do not need to argue about fairness or airline policy. Focus on your recorded status, whether you are boarding, and what happens next if you are not.

## Important reassurance
A missing seat assignment can happen for operational reasons and may still be fixed at the gate. Your task is not to solve the airline’s seating system; it is to get your status, the recorded reason, and the next decision point made clear.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the gate and boarding window only. If you are denied boarding, rebooked, downgraded, separated from someone you need to travel with, or lose a paid seat, later decisions may need specialist passenger-rights support or a formal airline complaint.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, medical, or other professional advice. Your rights and options can depend on whether UK passenger-rights law covers the flight, the route, the airline, the recorded reason, and whether you met check-in and boarding requirements.
