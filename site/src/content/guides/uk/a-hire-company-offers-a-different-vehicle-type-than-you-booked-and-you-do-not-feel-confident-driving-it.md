# What to do if… a hire company offers a different vehicle type than you booked and you do not feel confident driving it (UK)

## Short answer
State clearly that you cannot safely operate the vehicle provided and refuse to sign the rental agreement for that specific car. Request a vehicle within the booked category or a full cancellation without penalty based on a breach of the booking description.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign the rental agreement or "accept" the digital keys if the vehicle group or transmission does not match your booking.
- Do not drive the vehicle out of the rental car park "just to try it," as this usually constitutes legal acceptance of the car's condition and type.
- Do not accept an "upgrade" to a much larger vehicle (e.g., a van or large SUV) if you are not comfortable with its dimensions or visibility.
- Do not let staff rush the process; a signature under pressure is still a binding contract that can complicate future refund claims.
- Do not argue about the reasons for the shortage; maintain focus only on the fact that the offered vehicle is not suitable for your safe use.

## What to do now
1. **State your refusal using safety-focused language.** Use a neutral, firm statement: "This vehicle does not match my booking, and I am not confident that I can operate it safely."
2. **Verify the "Vehicle Group" on your original booking confirmation.** Check the ACRISS/SIPP code (e.g., EBMR for Economy) on your paperwork to confirm that the offered car is a mismatch in size, doors, or transmission.
3. **Request a "Like-for-Like" replacement or a downgrade with a partial refund.** If they cannot provide the exact class, ask for a smaller vehicle that you feel safe driving, and insist that the price is adjusted downward to reflect the lower category.
4. **Ask the desk agent to check the availability of "Incoming Returns."** If no cars are currently available, ask for a specific timeframe for the next arrival of a vehicle in your booked class.
5. **Request a "Manual Release" of any pre-authorisation if you choose to cancel.** If the company cannot provide a suitable car, ask them to void the transaction on their terminal immediately and provide a receipt showing the cancellation/void.
6. **Note the name of the staff member and the exact time of the refusal.** Record these details in your own notes along with the reason given by the company (e.g., "no stock in category").
7. **Contact the broker or booking agent immediately if you did not book direct.** Call the customer service line while still at the counter so they can speak to the branch manager and potentially find a vehicle at a competing company nearby.
8. **Check if the company is a member of the BVRLA.** Look for the British Vehicle Rental and Leasing Association logo on the desk or entrance, as this grants you access to their conciliation service if the dispute remains unresolved.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal written complaint while standing at the desk.
- You do not need to contact the BVRLA or your credit card issuer for a "Section 75" claim until you have left the premises and have a final decision from the company.
- You do not need to decide on an alternative transport plan (like a train or taxi) until the rental company has confirmed they definitely cannot provide a car.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard consumer right under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 that a service must be provided as described. If you booked a specific category and transmission, the company is in breach of contract if they cannot provide it. Prioritising your safety over the company's stock issues is the correct professional and legal choice.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate stabilization of a car hire dispute at the point of collection in the UK. It does not cover long-term legal litigation or disputes regarding damage discovered after the rental has ended.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. While UK consumer law protects you against "misdescription," car hire companies often include terms allowing for "similar" substitutions. Your refusal is based on your personal safety and confidence, which is a subjective but valid reason to decline a contract. If a pre-authorisation hold remains on your card, it may take 5 to 30 days to disappear depending on your bank's policies, even after the merchant releases it.
