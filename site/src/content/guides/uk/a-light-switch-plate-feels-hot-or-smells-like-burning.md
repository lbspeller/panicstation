# What to do if… a light switch plate feels hot or smells like burning (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a possible electrical overheating problem. Stop using the switch and turn off power to that circuit at the consumer unit if it is safe to do so. If there is smoke, sparking, crackling, a strong or worsening burning smell, or any sign of fire, get everyone out and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep switching it on and off to test it.
- Don’t ignore a burning or hot-plastic smell, buzzing, crackling, scorch marks, or a switch plate that is getting hotter.
- Don’t spray water, cleaner, or air freshener around the switch.
- Don’t remove the faceplate or touch anything behind it.
- Don’t reset a breaker repeatedly if it trips again.
- Don’t cover the switch with tape, cloth, paper, or furniture.
- Don’t press on the plate or hold something against it to cool it down.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the switch immediately.** If it controls a light, leave it off.
2. **Look for immediate danger from where you are.** If there is smoke, visible scorching, sparking, flames, crackling, sizzling, or a strong or worsening burning smell, get everyone out, close doors behind you if you can, and call 999.
3. **If there is no active fire and it is safe to move around, isolate the electricity.** Go to the consumer unit, often called the fuse box, and switch off the breaker for that lighting circuit.
4. **If you cannot identify the right circuit, switch off the main switch if you can do that safely.** Do not go near the consumer unit if it smells of burning, is smoking, feels hot, or is close to the problem area.
5. **Keep people away from the switch and nearby wall.** Ask others not to touch it to check whether it is still hot.
6. **Leave the affected circuit off until it has been checked.** Do not turn it back on because the smell has faded or the plate has cooled.
7. **Contact a registered electrician urgently.** Tell them the switch plate felt hot, smelled unusual or burning, and whether there was buzzing, crackling, visible damage, flickering lights, or a tripped breaker.
8. **If you rent, live in shared housing, or the switch is in a communal area, report it immediately.** Contact the landlord, agent, housing provider, building manager, or responsible repair line and say you have isolated the circuit if you have done so.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether the issue is a loose connection, dimmer fault, overloaded circuit, damaged switch, or wiring problem.
- You do not need to replace bulbs, change the switch, open the plate, or inspect the wiring yourself.
- You do not need to decide about wider rewiring now; the immediate task is to keep the area safe and get the fault checked.

## Important reassurance
You are not overreacting by taking this seriously. Heat, burning smells, buzzing, crackling, scorch marks, or unexplained breaker trips can be warning signs, and switching the circuit off while you arrange help is a sensible first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A registered electrician may need to inspect the switch, test the circuit, and repair or replace damaged parts before it is safe to use again.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, electrical, fire-safety, insurance, or other professional advice. If there may be a fire or immediate danger, prioritise getting everyone to safety and calling 999.
