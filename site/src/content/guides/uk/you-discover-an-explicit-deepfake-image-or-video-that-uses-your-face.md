# What to do if… you discover an explicit deepfake image or video that uses your face (UK)

## Short answer
Preserve evidence of the content and its location, report it directly to the platform, and contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for specialist assistance in removing the material from the internet.

## Do not do these things
- Don't interact with or reply to the person who shared or created the content.
- Don't post the deepfake or links to it on public social media to "warn others," as this increases visibility.
- Don't send the file to friends or family to confirm it is you; use descriptions instead.
- Don't pay any money or provide further images if you are being blackmailed.
- Don't delete the content or the source account until you have captured the URL and evidence.

## What to do now
1. **Secure a private space and capture evidence before taking any other action.** 
   - Record the exact URL (web address) where the content is hosted.
   - Take screenshots that show the content, the date/time, and the username of the uploader.
   - Save any accompanying text or direct messages if the content was sent to you.
2. **Report the content immediately using the platform’s internal reporting tools.** 
   - Select options for "Non-consensual intimate imagery," "Impersonation," or "Harassment."
   - If the platform is a major social media site, it has a legal duty under the Online Safety Act to remove such content.
3. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for expert removal support.** This service supports adults (18+) in the UK and has direct "trusted reporter" status with many platforms.
   - Phone: **0345 6000 459** (Monday–Friday, 10am–4pm).
   - Email: **help@revengepornhelpline.org.uk**.
4. **Use StopNCII.org to prevent the content from being re-uploaded.** 
   - This tool generates a "hash" (a digital fingerprint) of the file locally on your device.
   - The actual image or video never leaves your device or is uploaded to the service.
   - Participating platforms use this hash to automatically block or remove identical matches.
5. **Report the incident to the police if you wish to pursue criminal charges.** In the UK, it is a criminal offence to share, threaten to share, or *create* non-consensual intimate deepfakes (as of February 2026).
   - Call **101** for non-emergencies or report via the Police.uk website.
   - Call **999** if you are in immediate physical danger or being actively stalked.
6. **Follow the "Report Remove" process if the deepfake depicts someone under 18.** 
   - Use the **Childline / IWF Report Remove** tool to have the imagery assessed and deleted from the internet.
   - Contact **CEOP** if an adult is using the content to pressure or exploit a minor.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to inform your employer, school, or family right now.
- You do not need to determine the identity of the creator today.
- You do not need to read every comment or track every share of the content.
- You do not need to decide on long-term legal action or civil litigation immediately.

## Important reassurance
The discovery of digitally manipulated explicit content is a significant violation of privacy and often causes immediate distress, nausea, or shock. These are standard physiological responses to digital abuse. In the UK, the law and platform regulations have been updated to treat these "synthetic" images with the same severity as real photographs. You are not responsible for the creation of this material, and there are professional teams dedicated to its removal.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK residents. It focuses on evidence preservation, immediate reporting, and connecting with removal services. It does not cover long-term litigation or psychological recovery.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Laws regarding AI-generated content are subject to rapid change; always verify current procedures with the Revenge Porn Helpline or a legal professional.
