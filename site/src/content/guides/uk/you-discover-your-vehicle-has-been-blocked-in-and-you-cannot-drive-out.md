# What to do if… you discover your vehicle has been blocked in and you cannot drive out (UK)

## Short answer
If you are on a public road or trapped on your driveway, contact the driver if possible, or report the obstruction to your local council (parking enforcement) or the police via **101**. Do not attempt to move the blocking vehicle yourself, as this is a criminal offence under the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to tow, push, or physically move the other vehicle, as you may face criminal charges for vehicle interference or criminal damage.
- Do not use your own vehicle to "nudge" or force a gap, as this creates liability for any damage caused.
- Do not confront the driver with threats or aggression, even if you are significantly delayed or stressed.
- Do not "block them back" or park them in as retaliation, as this may also be classified as an illegal obstruction.
- Do not call **999** unless the blockage is causing an immediate life-safety risk, such as preventing emergency services from passing or blocking a fire exit during an active alarm.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe observation point.**
   **Identify a position where you can see your vehicle without standing in the flow of traffic or in a dangerous blind spot.** If you are in a dark or isolated car park, return to a staffed area or stay inside your locked vehicle if you feel unsafe.

2. **Conduct a visual assessment of the obstruction.**
   **Identify exactly where the vehicle is positioned relative to yours.**
   - Check for a driver nearby (e.g., a delivery driver at a nearby door).
   - Look for any notes on the dashboard of the blocking vehicle.
   - Note if the vehicle is blocking a dropped kerb or is parked on a public highway.

3. **Capture photographic evidence.**
   **Take clear photos of the blocking vehicle, including its registration plate and its position relative to yours.** Include images of any nearby signage, bay markings, or "no parking" notices. This documentation is essential if you need to report the incident to the council or police.

4. **Attempt to locate the driver through local channels.**
   **Use the quickest available method to request the driver moves the vehicle.**
   - **Supermarkets/Retail:** Ask customer services to make a tannoy announcement using the registration number.
   - **Workplaces/Residential Blocks:** Contact the building manager, concierge, or security team.
   - **Residential Streets:** If you feel safe doing so, ask nearby neighbours or shopkeepers if they recognize the vehicle.

5. **Contact the relevant enforcement authority.**
   **Determine the correct agency based on your location.**
   - **On a public road/blocking a driveway:** Contact your local council’s parking enforcement team if the area is under Civil Parking Enforcement (CPE). If not, or if you are physically trapped and cannot leave your property, call the police on **101**.
   - **On private land (e.g., retail park, private lot):** Contact the site manager or the private parking operator listed on the signage. Note that they cannot legally tow the car but may be able to identify the owner or issue a ticket.

6. **Initiate your fallback travel plan.**
   **Arrange alternative transport if you have a time-critical commitment.** Keep all receipts for taxis or public transport, as these may be required if you pursue a civil claim for costs later. Notify anyone expecting you that you are delayed by a vehicle obstruction.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to file a formal complaint against the driver.
- You do not need to contact your insurance company unless physical damage has occurred to your vehicle.
- You do not need to research long-term legal remedies for persistent parking issues right now.

## Important reassurance
Being blocked in is a high-stress situation that triggers a feeling of being trapped. It is normal to feel an immediate surge of frustration or anger. Most blockages are resolved within minutes once the driver is alerted. Taking systematic steps to report the issue is more effective than direct confrontation.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and the first hour of a parking obstruction. It does not provide legal strategy for long-term parking disputes or debt recovery for missed appointments.

## Important note
The police and local councils have different powers depending on whether the vehicle is on a public highway or private land. Under the Protection of Freedoms Act 2012, it is a criminal offence for a private individual or landowner to clamp or tow a vehicle without lawful authority. If the vehicle is parked on your actual driveway, this is considered civil trespass; the police generally cannot remove it unless it is abandoned or stolen.
