# What to do if… you suspect you are being followed while driving and you are approaching home (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive to your home or pull into your driveway. Continue driving to a well-lit, busy public location and call 999 immediately if you believe you are being targeted.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pull into your driveway, garage, or usual parking space.
- Do not stop in a quiet side street, dark lay-by, or near your home to "check" the situation.
- Do not get out of your vehicle to confront the other driver or take photos.
- Do not attempt to "lose them" by speeding, driving through red lights, or making high-speed maneuvers.
- Do not drive to the home of a friend or relative, as this exposes their address as well.
- Do not lead the follower to a dead-end road or a secluded area.

## What to do now
1. **Continue driving past your home and stay on main, well-lit roads.** Keep your doors locked and all windows fully closed.
2. **Perform a safe "pattern check" to confirm you are being followed.**
   - Take three or four consecutive left or right turns to complete a circle.
   - Go all the way around a roundabout and take the exit you originally entered from.
   - If the vehicle remains behind you after these maneuvers, treat the situation as an active pursuit.
3. **Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.**
   - **Legal note:** You are permitted to hold and use a mobile phone while driving to call 999 or 112 in a genuine emergency if it is unsafe or impractical to stop.
   - Tell the operator: "I am being followed in my car and I do not feel safe stopping. I am currently at [Road Name] heading towards [Location]."
   - Provide your car make, model, and colour, and the registration of the vehicle following you if you can see it safely.
4. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely.**
   - Call 999 and remain silent.
   - Listen for the automated prompt.
   - Press **55** when prompted; this informs the operator that your call is a genuine emergency and connects you to the police.
5. **Drive to a staffed "safe haven" rather than a secluded spot.**
   - Head to a 24-hour petrol station, a large supermarket car park with CCTV, or a hospital A&E entrance.
   - If you know the location of a police station that is likely to be staffed, drive there.
   - If you are on the phone with the police, ask them for the specific location they want you to head towards.
6. **If a vehicle with blue lights signals you to stop and you are unsure if they are genuine police, signal your intent to comply.**
   - Do not speed up or drive erratically.
   - Put on your hazard lights or use your indicator to show you have seen them.
   - Drive at a steady speed to the nearest well-lit public place (like a petrol station) before stopping.
   - Once stopped, keep your doors locked and windows up; ask the officer to show their warrant card through the glass.
7. **Stay inside your vehicle until you are certain help has arrived.** Park under a light, keep the engine running, and keep your doors locked. If the person following you approaches your car, use your horn to draw attention.

## What can wait
- You do not need to be certain of the follower's intent before calling for help.
- You do not need to check if you know the person or if it is a misunderstanding right now.
- You do not need to gather evidence or record video while the vehicle is in motion.
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal statement until you are physically safe.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden surge of adrenaline, shaking, or panic when you realize you are being followed. Choosing to drive past your house and head to a public area is a controlled, professional safety response that protects your home's privacy and your personal safety.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization only. It is designed to prevent you from being trapped at your home address and to move you toward professional assistance.

## Important note
This information is for emergency guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, you are legally required to stop for a police officer in uniform; however, safety protocols allow you to proceed to a well-lit area if you have genuine concerns about the legitimacy of an unmarked vehicle. Always prioritize 999 instructions over this guide during an active incident.
