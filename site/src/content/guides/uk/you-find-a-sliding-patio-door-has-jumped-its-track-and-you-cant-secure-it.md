# What to do if… you find a sliding patio door has jumped its track and you can’t secure it (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the area and immediately block the door from the inside using a solid physical barrier to prevent it from sliding or being lifted.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue forcing the door along the track, as this can shatter the glass or permanently deform the metal runners.
- Do not attempt to lift the heavy glass panel alone; sliding doors are frequently top-heavy and can cause crushing injuries if they fall.
- Do not assume a door is safe because it looks closed; if the multi-point lock does not engage, the door remains a primary security vulnerability.
- Do not block a designated fire exit route unless there is an alternative safe way out of the property.
- Do not leave the property unattended until a temporary or permanent security measure is in place.

## What to do now
1. **Move all occupants to a safer area of the property.** Ensure children and pets are kept away from the unstable door and close any internal doors leading to that room to create a secondary barrier.
2. **Determine if an immediate police response is required.** If you see signs of an active break-in or believe a crime is in progress, call **999**. For non-emergencies or to report evidence of a past attempt, call **101**.
3. **Attempt to slide the door to the fully closed position without using force.** If the door is stuck or skewed, move it only as far as it will go smoothly; forcing it can cause the glass to "explode" if the frame is under tension.
4. **Place a physical "security bar" in the bottom track.** Use a cut-to-size wooden dowel, a sturdy broom handle, or a dedicated patio lock bar placed snugly between the sliding panel and the side frame to prevent the door from being opened from the outside.
5. **Check for "anti-lift" vulnerability.** If the door has jumped the track, it may be possible to lift it straight up and out of the frame; if a gap exists at the top, wedge a piece of wood or a folded towel into the upper track to prevent the panel from being raised.
6. **Obscure the view from the outside.** Close curtains or blinds immediately so that the malfunction and any valuables inside are not visible to passersby.
7. **Initiate an emergency repair request.**
   - **Tenants:** Contact your landlord or letting agent immediately by phone and follow up in writing; under the **Renters’ Rights Act 2026**, security-related failures like a broken external door are typically classed as emergency repairs requiring action within 24 hours.
   - **Homeowners:** Contact a member of the **Master Locksmiths Association (MLA)** and specify that a "sliding patio door is off its track and cannot be secured."
   - **Social Housing Tenants:** Report the issue to your local authority or housing association emergency line; under **Awaab’s Law**, hazards to health and safety must be addressed urgently.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific cause of the roller failure (e.g., debris, worn bearings, or warped track) right now.
- You do not need to obtain multiple quotes for a full door replacement while the property is currently insecure.
- You do not need to clean the tracks or apply lubricants until the door is safely back on its runners.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of panic when a main entry point cannot be locked. By installing a physical track barrier and obscuring the view, you significantly increase the difficulty for an intruder, buying time until a professional arrives. Focus on the physical barrier rather than the mechanical lock.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for security and safety. It does not cover technical repairs, glass replacement, or legal disputes regarding property maintenance.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. Always follow the instructions of emergency services or qualified locksmiths.
