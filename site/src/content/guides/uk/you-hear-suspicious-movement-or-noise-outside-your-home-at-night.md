# What to do if… you hear suspicious movement or noise outside your home at night (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside, lock up, and move to a safer position away from doors and windows. If you think someone is trying to get in, a crime is happening now, or anyone is in immediate danger, call **999**.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go outside “just to check”, even if it feels quicker.
- Don’t open the door to challenge anyone or “see who it is”.
- Don’t shout through a window or reveal where you are in the house.
- Don’t grab a weapon and go looking; it can escalate the situation and you could be mistaken for a threat.
- Don’t waste time posting on social media or messaging group chats first.
- Don’t assume it is “probably nothing” if you have a clear reason to feel unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the house in place.** Lock external doors, close and lock accessible windows, and if you have an alarm, set it or set it to home mode.
2. **Move people to a safer spot.** Bring everyone, including pets, into one room upstairs or an inner room, away from ground-floor doors and windows. Keep the hallway and entry area clear.
3. **Reduce easy targets without exposing yourself.** If you can do it quickly without going near windows or the front door, move car keys, handbags, and valuables away from doors and ground-floor windows. If that would take you into an exposed area, skip it.
4. **Turn on lights strategically.** Switch on indoor lights and, if you can do it without going near windows, turn on outside lights or use an app for smart lights. Avoid standing silhouetted at a window.
5. **Look and listen safely.** From a protected position, behind a wall rather than in the window, check any camera or doorbell feed you have. Note only what you can see safely: number of people, direction of travel, and any vehicle details.
6. **Call the police using the right number.**
   - Call **999** if you believe someone is attempting to enter, a crime is happening now, someone nearby may have just committed a crime, or anyone is in immediate danger.
   - Call **101** or report online if it seems suspicious but is not an immediate threat, for example a brief noise with no sign of an entry attempt.
7. **If you can’t safely speak on a 999 call:**
   - **On a mobile:** call **999** and listen. Try to speak if you can, even by whispering. If you cannot speak, you may be asked to cough or tap. If prompted, press **55** to confirm it is a genuine emergency so the call can be put through to the police.
   - **On a landline:** call **999** and stay on the line. If you cannot speak, follow any operator prompts as best you can and do not hang up.
   - If you can speak safely, speaking is usually the fastest way to get help.
8. **If someone starts trying to get in:** get everyone into the safest room, lock it if you can, place solid furniture as a barrier if possible, and call **999** or stay on the line if you already called.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to go outside tonight to “confirm what it was”.
- You do **not** need to decide right now whether to install new security devices or change locks.
- You can deal with neighbours, community group messages, and saving or sharing any CCTV or doorbell clips later, after you are calm and safe.
- You do **not** need to make a perfect judgement about “emergency or non-emergency” before calling if you feel threatened.

## Important reassurance
A sudden noise outside can trigger a strong fear response even when you are physically safe indoors. Choosing to stay inside, lock down, and get help if needed is a sensible, safety-first response.

## Scope note
This is first-steps guidance for the next minutes to hour. Follow-up actions, such as reports, cameras, security upgrades, or neighbour coordination, can come later and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information for the UK. It is not legal, security, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice, and it cannot assess your specific risk. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
