# What to do if… you run out of fuel and your vehicle is safely off the road (UK)

## Short answer
Make the vehicle visible, get yourself and any passengers into the safest waiting position for where you have stopped, then call for roadside or official help to come to you.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t try to restart or keep moving if the engine is cutting out and you are already safely off the road.
- Don’t walk along a motorway or hard shoulder to look for fuel.
- Don’t stand between your vehicle and moving traffic.
- Don’t wait near the rear of the vehicle close to the carriageway.
- Don’t place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Don’t attempt repairs or refuelling beside fast-moving traffic.
- Don’t feel pressured to accept help from a stranger; use official, known, or breakdown help where possible.
- Don’t smoke or use a naked flame near the vehicle.

## What to do now
1. **Make the vehicle visible and stable.** Keep it fully out of running lanes if it is already safely off the road. Apply the handbrake and switch on hazard warning lights. If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights as well. On a motorway or other fast road, turn the front wheels to the left if you can do this safely.

2. **Choose the safest waiting place.**
   - **Car park, service area, or fully separated place:** wait away from moving traffic and do not stand behind the vehicle.
   - **Lay-by or roadside bay:** wait on the side furthest from traffic if possible, and keep passengers together.
   - **Motorway hard shoulder or emergency area:** if it is safe, leave by the side furthest from traffic and wait well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, behind a safety barrier if there is one.
   - **If it does not feel safe to get out:** stay in the vehicle, keep seatbelts and hazards on, and call 999.

3. **Get your exact location before calling.**
   - On a motorway or major road, note the road name, direction of travel, nearest junction, and any driver location signs or marker posts.
   - On other roads, use the road name or number, nearest town, landmark, postcode if visible, or your phone’s map location.
   - Tell the call handler if anyone is disabled, older, travelling alone, a child, unwell, or otherwise more vulnerable.

4. **Call for the right help.**
   - If you have breakdown cover, call your breakdown provider and say you have run out of fuel and need fuel delivery or recovery.
   - If you are on a motorway or major road in **England**, use a free emergency telephone if it is safe and nearby, or call **National Highways on 0300 123 5000** for advice and assistance. If you cannot wait safely, call **999**.
   - If you are on a motorway or major road in **Wales** and need urgent traffic help, call **Traffic Wales on 0300 123 1213**. If it is an emergency, call **999**.
   - If you are on a motorway or trunk road in **Scotland** and need traffic information or support, call **Traffic Scotland on 0800 028 1414**. For urgent danger, call **999**.
   - If you are in **Northern Ireland**, use your breakdown provider for non-emergency recovery. If you are in immediate danger, cannot wait safely, or are obstructing traffic, call **999**.

5. **Keep the call short and clear.** Say: “I have run out of fuel,” your exact location, vehicle make, colour and registration, how many people are with you, whether you are off the road, and whether anyone is vulnerable.

6. **Wait safely.** Keep everyone together and away from traffic danger. Keep your phone charged. Avoid repeated restart attempts, especially if the vehicle has fully run dry; wait for recovery or roadside help.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to work out why the fuel ran out right now.
- You don’t need to decide whether the gauge, range display, or fuel warning was wrong while you are still roadside.
- You don’t need to arrange complaints, insurance admin, garage checks, or reimbursement until you are somewhere safe.
- You don’t need to explain or justify what happened to anyone who is not helping you get safe.

## Important reassurance
Running out of fuel can feel embarrassing and frightening, but the immediate priorities are simple: stay visible, stay away from traffic danger, and get help to come to you.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about vehicle checks, fuel-system problems, breakdown membership, insurance, or complaints may need help from a breakdown provider, garage, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, cannot wait safely, or are stopped in a live lane, call 999.
