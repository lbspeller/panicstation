# What to do if… your wiper blades tear or detach and you realise before driving in bad weather (UK)

## Short answer
Do not operate the vehicle if rain or spray is likely; instead, replace the damaged blades immediately or seek alternative transport. Driving with ineffective wipers is a safety hazard and a criminal offence under UK road vehicle regulations.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to drive into forecast rain or surface spray with compromised wipers.
- Do not let the spring-loaded metal wiper arm snap back onto the glass while the blade is removed (this can easily shatter the windscreen).
- Do not use makeshift repairs like tape, glue, or zip-ties, which can fail at speed or cause permanent gouges in the glass.
- Do not assume that screenwash or "rain repellent" coatings are a legal or safe substitute for functional wiper blades.
- Do not leave the bare metal wiper arm in contact with the glass, as it will not clear water and may cause expensive surface damage.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of failure on both front wipers while the car is parked.**  
   Check if the rubber has split, if the blade assembly has unclipped from the arm, or if the rubber is "chattering" due to hardening.
2. **Consult the vehicle handbook or a digital fitment guide to identify the correct replacement part.**  
   Wiper blades use various attachment systems (Hook, Side Pin, Push Button, or Pinch Tab) and specific lengths that must match your vehicle exactly for safe operation.
3. **Lift the wiper arm to its service position and securely re-seat the blade if it has only unclipped.**  
   You must hear or feel a distinct "click" as the locking mechanism engages; if it remains loose, the plastic clip is likely fatigued and the entire blade must be replaced.
4. **Place a thick towel or cloth over the windscreen glass while the wiper blades are removed.**  
   This provides a buffer in case the spring-loaded arm is accidentally knocked, preventing it from striking and cracking the glass.
5. **Acquire and fit new blades before attempting any journey where visibility may be affected.**  
   - Purchase blades from a local automotive retailer or petrol station if they stock your specific fitment.
   - Use a taxi or public transport to reach the shop rather than driving the defective vehicle in the rain.
   - If a mobile mechanic or breakdown service is available, request assistance for on-site fitment if you are unable to perform the replacement.
6. **Test the new or re-seated blades using the windscreen washer function before setting off.**  
   Confirm the blades clear the entire swept area without leaving streaks, smears, or "blind spots" in your field of vision.
7. **Ensure the screenwash reservoir is topped up with appropriate seasonal fluid.**  
   Under Regulation 34 of the Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations 1986, both wipers and washers must be maintained in efficient working order at all times while the vehicle is on a road.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the blade failed (e.g., UV damage vs. frost) before making the decision to replace it.
- You do not need to replace the rear wiper blade immediately if the front visibility is fully restored, though it should be addressed soon.
- You do not need to book a full vehicle service if the only fault is the consumable wiper blade.

## Important reassurance
Wiper blade failure is a standard maintenance issue that occurs due to environmental exposure. Identifying the problem before you are on the road is the ideal time to resolve it, as it prevents the stress of a sudden loss of visibility during a journey.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a vehicle with defective wiper blades identified before a journey. It does not cover repairs for wiper motors, linkages, or electrical failures.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Under Section 40a of the Road Traffic Act 1988, using a vehicle in a dangerous condition (including seriously impaired visibility) can result in a fine of up to £2,500 and 3 penalty points. If you are unsure of the vehicle's roadworthiness, do not drive.
