# What to do if… you are in a hotel or apartment building and someone is trying multiple doors in the corridor (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside, engage all physical locks immediately, and contact building security or dial 999 if you believe an intruder is attempting to enter. Do not open the door to investigate or challenge the person.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to see who is there, even if the person claims to be staff or emergency services.
- Do not step out into the corridor to look for the source of the noise.
- Do not shout threats or announce that you are alone.
- Do not assume the person is a confused guest if they are trying more than one door.
- Do not rely on a simple latch if a deadbolt, chain, or swing-bar is available.
- Do not use a door wedge if it prevents the fire door from closing fully in an emergency.

## What to do now
1. **Engage every manual lock and security device on your door immediately.** Turn the deadbolt, slide the chain, or flip the swing-bar into place as quietly as possible.
2. **Move away from the door and stay out of the direct line of sight from the spyhole.** Keep your phone with you and move to a part of the room where you cannot be seen if the door were to be forced, such as the bathroom or a far corner.
3. **Use the door viewer or spyhole only if you can do so without alerting the person.** If you use it, cover the viewer with your finger before approaching to prevent the person outside from seeing a change in light that indicates someone is inside.
4. **Call the hotel reception or building concierge immediately using your mobile or the room phone.** State clearly: "I am in room/apartment [Number] and someone is moving down the corridor trying multiple door handles right now; please send security to this floor immediately."
5. **Call 999 if the person attempts to force your door or if you feel in immediate danger.** If the person remains outside your door or if you hear them successfully entering another room, treat this as a crime in progress.
6. **Use the Silent Solution if you are on a mobile and cannot speak safely after dialling 999.** Stay on the line, and when prompted by the automated system, press 55 to inform the operator that your emergency is genuine.
7. **Listen for specific details without approaching the door.** Note the direction the person is moving, the number of distinct voices or footsteps, and any specific sounds like the jingling of keys or heavy equipment.
8. **Request a security escort if you are instructed by staff to move rooms or exit.** Do not open the door until you have verified the identity of the person outside via the spyhole or by calling the front desk to confirm they have arrived.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person or their intentions right now.
- You do not need to decide if you want to press charges or make a formal statement immediately.
- You do not need to check on your neighbours personally; let security or police handle floor-wide safety.
- You do not need to review your own travel or tenancy arrangements until the immediate threat has been cleared by authorities.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of hesitation, fear, or a racing heart during this type of event. Testing multiple door handles is a known method for opportunistic theft and unauthorized entry; taking it seriously is a necessary safety precaution, regardless of the eventual outcome.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active security concern in a shared residential or hospitality building. It does not cover long-term legal rights or post-incident trauma support.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In a life-threatening emergency or if a crime is occurring, always dial 999 and follow the instructions provided by the emergency operator.
