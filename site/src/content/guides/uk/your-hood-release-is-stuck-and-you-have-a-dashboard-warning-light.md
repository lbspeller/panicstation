# What to do if… your hood release is stuck and you have a dashboard warning light (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the warning as real: get to the safest place you can, switch the engine off, and arrange help rather than forcing the bonnet open.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving to see if the warning goes away if it involves temperature, overheating, oil pressure, charging, smoke, steam, or a strong burning smell.
- Don’t force, pry, or tug at the bonnet release at the roadside, especially near traffic or if the engine may be hot.
- Don’t put your face, hands, or tools into bonnet gaps if there is steam, heat, smoke, or a hot smell.
- Don’t remove a coolant, radiator, or expansion tank cap while the engine is hot.
- Don’t stop in a live lane to check the vehicle.
- Don’t attempt roadside repairs on a motorway.

## What to do now
1. **Get to the safest stop you can.**
   - If you are on a motorway or fast road and the vehicle still moves, leave at the next exit or pull into a service area if you can.
   - If you cannot leave, move left and aim for an emergency area or a hard shoulder where one is present.
   - Stop as far left as possible, leaving space to get out away from traffic.

2. **Make yourself safer immediately.**
   - Put your hazard warning lights on.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, use your sidelights.
   - If it is safe and possible, leave the vehicle by the side furthest from traffic and wait well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, behind a safety barrier if there is one.
   - If you are in a live lane, cannot leave the vehicle safely, cannot reach a safer place, or feel in immediate danger, keep your seat belt on, keep hazards on, and call **999**.

3. **Switch the engine off once stopped.**
   - This is especially important if the warning suggests overheating, low oil pressure, smoke, steam, or a burning smell.
   - Do not restart the engine just to test whether the warning has cleared.

4. **Check for obvious danger without opening the bonnet.**
   - From a safe position, look for flames, heavy smoke, steam, or liquid pooling under the front of the vehicle.
   - If you see flames, heavy smoke, or a growing spill, move further away and call **999**.

5. **Leave the bonnet closed if it will not open easily.**
   - Do not force the release cable, latch, or bonnet edge.
   - If overheating is possible, let the vehicle cool and wait for breakdown or recovery help.

6. **Get help for the road you are on.**
   - On a motorway or major road in England, use a free emergency telephone where provided, or call National Highways on **0300 123 5000** for advice and assistance.
   - In Scotland, contact Traffic Scotland on **0800 028 1414** for road travel and traffic support; call **999** for an emergency.
   - In Wales, call Traffic Wales on **0300 123 1213** for urgent road traffic incidents or hazards; call **999** for an emergency.
   - In Northern Ireland, use an emergency roadside telephone where available; call **999** for an emergency.
   - Also call your breakdown provider or arrange recovery.

7. **Say exactly what is wrong.**
   - Tell them: “The bonnet release will not open. I had a warning for temperature, oil, battery, smoke, smell, or another dashboard warning. I cannot check under the bonnet safely.”

8. **Share your location clearly.**
   - Use the road name or number, junction number, service area, emergency area number, marker post, driver location sign, nearby landmark, or a phone location pin.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the warning light now.
- You do not need to top up oil, coolant, or screenwash now.
- You do not need to decide whether the vehicle needs repair, recovery, or a garage inspection until you are safe and help has been arranged.
- You do not need to prove what is wrong by forcing the bonnet open.

## Important reassurance
A stuck bonnet release can happen because of a latch, cable, alignment, or freezing issue. The safest first step is not to solve the latch at the roadside; it is to get yourself away from traffic, stop the engine, and get roadside or recovery help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. The bonnet release fault and the warning light may need a breakdown technician, recovery service, or garage once the immediate situation is safe.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not vehicle-specific mechanical, legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Follow emergency services, road authority, breakdown provider, and vehicle manufacturer instructions where they apply.
