# What to do if… someone tries your car door handle while you are inside your locked vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside with the doors locked and the windows up. If you can safely drive away, go to a busy, well-lit place; if you feel in immediate danger or it is happening now, call 999 or 112.

## Do not do these things
- Do not unlock the door to ask what they want.
- Do not crack the window to talk.
- Do not get out to confront them or check the outside of the car while they are nearby.
- Do not follow them if they walk away.
- Do not stay parked only to film if leaving would make you safer.
- Do not block them in or try to stop them yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Seal the vehicle.** Check the doors are locked and the windows are fully up. Keep the car in a state where you can move off safely.
2. **Move away if your route is clear.** Drive calmly to a busy, well-lit place such as a staffed petrol station, an open shop, or another place with people nearby. Do not pull into an isolated spot.
3. **Attract attention without opening up.** If they keep trying the handle, stay sealed in, sound the horn, flash your headlights, and use your alarm or panic button if you have one.
4. **Call for help if there is immediate risk.** Call 999 or 112 if the person is still trying to get in, you are being threatened, you are blocked in, they appear to be targeting other vehicles, or you believe a crime is in progress.
5. **If you cannot speak safely on a 999 call, listen carefully.** Make a sound if you can. If you are calling from a mobile and are prompted, press 55 so the call can be put through to police.
6. **If you are now safe and it is not urgent, report it as a non-emergency.** Use 101 or an online police reporting route, especially if it happened in a public place such as a car park, street, or petrol station.
7. **Give only simple details.** Share your location, what happened, the person’s direction of travel, and any vehicle details you already noticed. Do not open the door or lower the window to get more information.
8. **Write down what you remember once you are safe.** Note the time, place, what was tried, and whether there is any damage. Take photos later only in a safe location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now what their intent was.
- You do not need to write a perfect account immediately.
- You do not need to check for damage until you are somewhere safer.
- You do not need to contact insurers in the moment unless there has been damage, theft, or a collision.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shaken, frozen, or angry after this is a normal stress response. Staying sealed in, creating distance, and calling for help if the risk is immediate are sensible first steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes only. Later decisions, such as reporting repeat incidents near your home, work, or usual parking place, may need specialist help or police advice.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, prioritise getting to a safer place and contacting emergency services.
