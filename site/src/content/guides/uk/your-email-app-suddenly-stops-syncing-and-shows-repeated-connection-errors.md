# What to do if… your email app suddenly stops syncing and shows repeated connection errors (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately secure the primary Apple or Google account by changing the password and signing out all other active sessions. Once the account is locked down, remove any unknown members from the family group and disable purchase sharing.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t use links in "security alert" emails or texts to sign in — manually type the official website address or use the app.
- Don’t delete the entire account immediately — you may lose access to the evidence needed for fraud disputes.
- Don’t assume a "removed from group" notification is an error — it often indicates an intruder is clearing the group to take full control.
- Don’t ignore small "test" charges — these are often followed by larger unauthorized purchases once the payment link is confirmed.
- Don’t share your master account password with family members — give them their own accounts and link them via the official sharing settings.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the controlling account first.**
   - **Change your password immediately to a long, unique phrase that you have not used elsewhere.**
   - **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) if it was disabled, or verify that the 2FA phone number/email is still yours.**
   - **Force a logout of all other sessions through the "Security" or "Devices" section of your account management page.**

2. **Remove unknown access points.**
   - **Check for and remove any unrecognized "Trusted Devices" in your Apple Account or "Your Devices" in your Google Account.**
   - **Review "Third-party apps with account access" or "Sign in with Apple / Google" settings and revoke access for any apps you do not recognize.**
   - **Verify that no new recovery emails or backup phone numbers have been added to your profile.**

3. **Reclaim the Family Group.**
   - **Apple users: Go to Settings > [Your Name] > Family and remove any unrecognized members or organizers.**
   - **Google users: Open the Google Play app > Profile Icon > Settings > Family > Manage family members and remove any unknown users.**
   - **Toggle "Purchase Sharing" to OFF until you have confirmed every member in the group is legitimate.**

4. **Protect your payment methods.**
   - **Contact your bank or card issuer to report unauthorized activity and request a temporary block on the card linked to the store.**
   - **Check for new subscriptions in the "Subscriptions" menu of your App Store or Play Store and cancel any you did not authorize.**

5. **Report the incident for the record.**
   - **Report the cyber crime to Report Fraud (formerly Action Fraud) via reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040 (England, Wales, and NI).**
   - **If in Scotland, report via the Police Scotland 101 non-emergency line or use the official Police Scotland online reporting form.**
   - **Save screenshots of the changed settings, the names/emails of added members, and any transaction receipts.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to use family sharing long-term; disabling it now for safety is the priority.
- You do not need to notify the other (legitimate) family members before taking action; secure the account first to prevent the intruder from locking you out.
- You do not need to update your payment details immediately; it is safer to leave the card blocked until the account security is verified.

## Important reassurance
If you still have access to your account, you can usually stop the unauthorized activity by following a strict security sequence. Changes to family settings are often the first visible sign of a compromised password, and resetting your credentials and signing out other devices is an effective way to regain control.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for UK users experiencing unauthorized changes to digital family sharing accounts. It does not cover deep forensic recovery or legal action against specific individuals.

## Important note
This information is for guidance purposes and does not constitute professional cybersecurity or legal advice. If you are unable to regain access to your account, you must contact Apple Support or Google Support directly through their verified official channels. Fraud reporting in the UK should be directed to the appropriate police authorities.
