# What to do if… your travel booking lists the wrong country of citizenship and you cannot edit it online (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the carrier or travel agent to update your Advance Passenger Information (API) to match your passport exactly, as an uncorrected mismatch may trigger a boarding refusal under the UK’s 2026 Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) enforcement rules.

## Do not do these things
- Do not apply for a UK Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) if you are a British citizen; you are ineligible and the application will be rejected or cause further record conflicts.
- Do not attempt to "force" the online check-in by entering false information to bypass a locked field.
- Do not cancel the flight until you have spoken to the airline's "Specialist Help" or "Passenger Data" desk, as most nationality errors are corrected without rebooking.
- Do not rely on verbal assurances from customer service; always request a revised booking confirmation or a screenshot of the corrected API screen.
- Do not wait until you reach the departure gate to address the mismatch.

## What to do now
1. **Identify if the error is in the core booking data or the Advance Passenger Information (API) section.**  
   Most airlines distinguish between the "Passenger Name Record" (PNR), which often requires a fee to change, and the "API / APIS" data (passport number, nationality, expiry), which should usually be editable for free even if the field is currently locked.
2. **Collect your booking reference (PNR) and a digital copy of the passport you intend to use.**  
   Ensure the "Country of Citizenship" and "Country of Issue" on your booking match the machine-readable zone (MRZ) of your passport exactly.
3. **Contact the airline’s passenger data or check-in assistance team directly.**  
   **-** If you booked through a third-party agent, you must contact the agent first to push a "remarks" update to the airline's system.  
   **-** Use the specific phrase: "I need to update my Advance Passenger Information (API) because the citizenship field is locked and does not match my travel document."
4. **Request a manual override if the system insists you need a visa or ETA based on the incorrect citizenship.**  
   Since February 2026, carriers use automated "permission to travel" checks; if the booking lists a nationality that requires an ETA but you do not have one (because you are British), the system will automatically block boarding.
5. **Verify your proof of British citizenship if you are a dual national without a valid UK passport.**  
   Under 2026 transitional rules, carriers may, at their discretion, allow you to board for travel to the UK if you present:  
   - An expired British passport (issued in 1989 or later).  
   - A valid passport from a non-visa national country (e.g., USA, EU, Australia).  
   - Biographic details that match both documents exactly.
6. **Check your UKVI digital account if you use a Certificate of Entitlement (CoE).**  
   As of early 2026, many Certificates of Entitlement are digital eVisas; ensure your current foreign passport is linked to your UKVI account so the carrier's automated check can verify your right of abode.
7. **Arrive at the airport three hours before departure to visit the staffed check-in desk.**  
   If the airline cannot fix the data over the phone, the airport supervisor can often manually verify your documents and override the API error to issue a boarding pass.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a new passport immediately if your current one is valid and you have an alternative proof of citizenship accepted under transitional rules.
- You do not need to resolve potential name-change fees if only the nationality field is incorrect; these are usually separate processes.
- You do not need to decide on a formal complaint or "denied boarding" claim until after you have successfully travelled.

## Important reassurance
Nationality mismatches in airline systems are a frequent occurrence. While the 2026 digital border rules make these errors more visible to carriers, airline staff at the airport have the authority to manually verify physical documents and correct the electronic record to facilitate boarding.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for data mismatches on travel bookings. It does not provide legal advice on immigration status or long-term visa eligibility.

## Important note
This information is based on UK carrier liability and Home Office guidance as of March 2026. Different airlines may have varying technical capabilities for editing locked passenger data. Always verify the specific document requirements for your destination and any transit countries.
