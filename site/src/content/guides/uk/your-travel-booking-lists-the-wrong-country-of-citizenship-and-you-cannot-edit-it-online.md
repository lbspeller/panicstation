# What to do if… your travel booking lists the wrong country of citizenship and you cannot edit it online (UK)

## Short answer
Ask the airline, travel agent, or tour operator to correct the passenger information, often called API or Advance Passenger Information, so it matches the passport or travel document you will use. If online changes are locked, prepare to ask for help at staffed check-in and arrive earlier than normal.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t cancel and rebook in a panic unless the airline, agent, or tour operator tells you it is the only workable fix.
- Don’t enter a nationality or citizenship just to get past the form if it does not match the travel document or permission you are relying on.
- Don’t assume it is harmless if your route involves visas, ETA rules, transit checks, or return travel to the UK.
- Don’t send passport scans to unknown “booking fix” services or through insecure channels.
- Don’t wait until the gate if you already know online check-in is blocked or the passenger details are wrong.

## What to do now
1. **Work out exactly which field is wrong.**  
   Check whether the error is in nationality, country of citizenship, passport issuing country, passport number, expiry date, or date of birth. Write down what the booking shows and what it should say, using the passport or travel document you will actually present.

2. **Save proof before you start calling or messaging.**  
   Take screenshots of the passenger details page, the locked field, and any “cannot edit” or failed check-in message. Keep your airline booking reference, passenger name record, and any separate travel agent or tour operator reference.

3. **Contact the organisation that controls the booking.**  
   If you booked direct with the airline, contact the airline and ask them to update your passenger information or API. If you booked through a travel agent or tour operator, contact them first and ask whether they must update the airline booking record.

4. **Use wording that avoids a name-change queue.**  
   Say: “My reservation shows the wrong country of citizenship or nationality and I cannot edit it online. Please update my passenger information or API so it matches the passport I will travel with.” Ask for confirmation of what has been changed.

5. **If they cannot fix it before travel, plan a staffed check-in attempt.**  
   Some carriers may be able to help at the check-in desk, depending on the route and their systems. Arrive earlier than normal, go to staffed check-in rather than only self-service, and show the booking reference, screenshots, passport, and any visa or permission for the route.

6. **For UK-bound travel, check what document the carrier will expect.**  
   GOV.UK says carriers check passports and other travel documents before boarding and send this information to Border Force. For British citizens entering the UK, GOV.UK lists a valid UK passport, a Gibraltar identity card, or a certificate of entitlement showing right of abode. If you are a dual citizen and your UK passport is expired or not available, do not guess; check the current GOV.UK guidance and raise it with the carrier before you travel.

7. **If you are abroad and travel is soon, make a simple check-in pack.**  
   Keep offline copies of your booking references, screenshots, airline or agent messages, passport or travel document, and any visa, ETA, eVisa share code, certificate of entitlement, or other permission relevant to the journey.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, seek a refund, or change providers.
- You do not need to solve every future passport or nationality issue today.
- The immediate task is only to get the booking data, travel document, and route permissions lined up as far as possible before check-in.

## Important reassurance
Many passenger-data errors can be corrected by the airline, agent, tour operator, or at staffed check-in, especially when you act early and keep clear proof of the problem. A locked online field does not always mean the whole booking is unusable.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about visas, right of abode, dual nationality, refunds, complaints, or disrupted travel may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. Airline systems, carrier checks, and border-document requirements vary by route and traveller status, so use official guidance for your exact journey and ask the carrier what it needs to see.
