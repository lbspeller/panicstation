# What to do if… a benefits payment is suspended for review and you have essential bills due (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the department that pays the benefit immediately to identify the specific evidence required to lift the suspension, then notify your landlord and utility providers of the delay to request a temporary payment hold.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore deadlines for evidence — DWP "Targeted Case Reviews" often have a strict 14-day limit before further action is taken.
- Do not assume a suspension will resolve itself automatically without you providing information or attending an appointment.
- Do not take out high-interest emergency loans or "payday" credit while a review is pending.
- Do not cancel direct debits for essential bills without first speaking to the provider to arrange a "hold" or "breathing space."
- Do not share login credentials or bank codes with anyone claiming to "help" with your review over the phone or via text.

## What to do now
1. **Identify which authority has suspended the payment and contact them through their primary channel today.** 
   - **Universal Credit (DWP):** Use your online journal or call the UC helpline (0800 328 5644).
   - **Pension Credit / PIP / ESA (DWP):** Call the specific helpline number listed on your most recent award letter.
   - **Housing Benefit / Council Tax Support:** Contact your local authority (Council) benefits department directly.
   - **Northern Ireland:** Contact the Department for Communities (DfC) or your local Jobs and Benefits office.
2. **Request a precise list of the "blockers" or evidence required to reinstate your payment.** 
   Common requirements include bank statements (often for the last 4 months), proof of housing costs, identity verification, or a "Fit Note" for health-related benefits.
   - Ask if an in-person appointment at a Jobcentre is mandatory, as some reviews (such as capital/savings verifications) cannot be completed online.
3. **Submit all requested documentation immediately and retain digital or physical proof of submission.** 
   If uploading to a portal, take a screenshot of the "successfully uploaded" message. If providing documents in person, ask for a dated receipt.
4. **Notify your landlord and energy supplier that your income is temporarily suspended for a formal review.** 
   - **Landlords:** Ask for a written agreement to pause eviction proceedings or late fees for a set period (e.g., 14 days).
   - **Energy Suppliers:** Ask to be placed on the Priority Services Register if you are vulnerable, and request an emergency payment plan or a "vulnerability hold" on your account.
5. **Apply for jurisdiction-specific emergency grants that do not require the suspended benefit to be in payment.** 
   - **England:** Contact your local council to apply for the Household Support Fund or local welfare assistance.
   - **Scotland:** Apply for a Scottish Welfare Fund Crisis Grant through your local council.
   - **Wales:** Apply for an Emergency Assistance Payment (EAP) via the Discretionary Assistance Fund (DAF).
   - **Northern Ireland:** Contact the Finance Support Service to apply for Discretionary Support (for those with an annual income below £29,741.40).
6. **Protect your account from enforcement action using statutory debt protections if applicable.** 
   - **England and Wales:** Ask a debt adviser (such as StepChange or Citizens Advice) about a "Standard Breathing Space," which legally pauses most enforcement and interest for 60 days.
   - **Scotland:** Ask about the Debt Arrangement Scheme (DAS) or "Moratorium" protections.
7. **Verify if you are eligible for a Hardship Payment if your benefit was stopped due to a sanction.** 
   Note that Hardship Payments are generally only available for sanctioned claims, not those suspended for administrative review or evidence verification.

## What can wait
- You do not need to agree to a long-term debt repayment plan today; a temporary "hold" is sufficient for the first 48–72 hours.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint about the review process until you have first submitted the requested evidence to restart the money.
- You do not need to provide original documents by post unless specifically told that digital copies are unacceptable; high-quality photos are usually sufficient for initial reviews.

## Important reassurance
A benefit suspension for "review" is a standard administrative procedure used by the DWP and local authorities to verify that details remain accurate. It is not an accusation of fraud. Most suspensions are resolved quickly once the requested documents are provided and processed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the immediate financial gap caused by a benefit suspension. It does not cover the legal process of appealing a final decision or "Mandatory Reconsideration" once a review is completed.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Access to emergency funds is discretionary and subject to local availability and eligibility criteria. If you have no food, heat, or are at immediate risk of losing your home, state this clearly as "emergency hardship" when contacting the benefit office or your local council.
