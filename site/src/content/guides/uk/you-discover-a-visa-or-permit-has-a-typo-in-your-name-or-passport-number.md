# What to do if… you discover a visa or permit has a typo in your name or passport number (UK)

## Short answer
Treat a name or passport-number mismatch as urgent for travel: report it through the correct official route and keep written proof that you reported it. Do not travel on a document mismatch unless UKVI, the Visa Application Centre route, or another official route tells you in writing what to do.

## Do not do these things
- Do not travel and hope it will be fine if your visa, eVisa, vignette, or permit details do not match your passport.
- Do not alter the document yourself with edits, stickers, overwriting, or reprinting changed pages.
- Do not rely on a phone call alone; keep a written reference, email, screenshot, or submission confirmation.
- Do not book more non-refundable travel until you know the correction route and whether your passport or account details need action.
- Do not start duplicate correction requests unless an official route tells you to; keep one clear record of what you have reported.

## What to do now
1. **Make a quick evidence pack.**
   - Take a photo or screenshot of the visa, eVisa, vignette, or permit showing the typo.
   - Take a photo of your passport photo page showing the correct name and passport number.
   - Save your visa grant email, decision letter, GWF number, UAN, or UKVI customer number if you have it.

2. **Identify where the wrong detail appears.**
   - **eVisa or online immigration status:** the wrong name, passport number, date of birth, nationality, or status appears when you view your online immigration status.
   - **Vignette sticker:** the wrong detail is printed on the sticker in your passport for travel to the UK.
   - **BRP or older physical permit card:** the card has wrong details, or you are comparing the card with your eVisa.

3. **If your eVisa details are wrong, use the GOV.UK eVisa error service.**
   - Report the eVisa error through the official GOV.UK route.
   - Save the submission confirmation or take a screenshot before closing the page.
   - Keep the confirmation with your passport and evidence pack.

4. **If your passport or contact details have changed, update your UKVI account.**
   - Use the GOV.UK UKVI account update route for a changed passport, travel document, address, email, phone, photo, or name.
   - If the detail has not changed and is simply wrong on the eVisa, use the eVisa error route instead.
   - If you are waiting for a visa decision, check the GOV.UK route before trying to change name or passport details.

5. **If the error is on a vignette sticker and you have not travelled yet, ask for correction before travel.**
   - Contact the Visa Application Centre route or application service provider you used, using the contact details from your application or decision communications.
   - If the error could affect travel, you can also use the official UKVI contact route to ask what to do next.
   - Do not send your passport anywhere until you have written instructions from the official route you are using.

6. **If you are already in the UK and the problem is on a BRP or older physical permit card, check your eVisa first.**
   - BRPs have now expired and cannot be used for travel.
   - If you still have permission to stay in the UK, check whether you can access your eVisa.
   - If the same wrong detail appears on your eVisa, report an eVisa error.
   - If the eVisa is correct, keep evidence of the mismatch and use GOV.UK or UKVI support routes if someone asks you to rely on the old physical document.

7. **Reduce immediate travel and check-in risk.**
   - If travel is soon, contact the airline before going to the airport and ask what it will accept when UK immigration details do not match the passport.
   - Carry your passport, evidence pack, visa grant or decision letter, and correction submission confirmation.
   - If the airline, border staff, UKVI, or the Visa Application Centre gives you instructions, write down the date, route, and reference number.

8. **Slow down if someone demands urgent payment or an unusual fix.**
   - Use GOV.UK, UKVI, and the legitimate application service provider you used.
   - Treat pressure to pay quickly by bank transfer, gift card, cryptocurrency, or an unofficial messaging app as a warning sign.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether the typo will be ignored at the border; assume it needs checking and let the official route confirm.
- You do not need to reapply for a new visa immediately unless an official route tells you the error cannot be corrected.
- You do not need to contact every possible organisation at once; start with where the wrong detail appears: eVisa, UKVI account, vignette, or older physical card.
- You do not need to solve future travel plans today beyond protecting any imminent journey from a document mismatch.

## Important reassurance
Clerical errors do happen. The safest first move is to get the mismatch logged through the right official route, keep proof, and avoid turning a fixable document problem into a refused boarding or border problem.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stabilise the situation, protect travel, and start the official correction route. Later decisions may need specialist immigration advice, especially if travel is urgent, the error affects your status, or you have already been refused boarding or entry.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, or professional advice. UKVI and Home Office processes can change, and your circumstances may affect what you are asked to do.
