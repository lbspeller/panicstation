# What to do if… you discover a visa or permit has a typo in your name or passport number (UK)

## Short answer
Report the error through the official GOV.UK eVisa correction service and pause travel until you receive a confirmation of the fix or specific boarding instructions. Data mismatches between your passport and digital status will likely cause airline boarding denials or border delays.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to travel if your physical passport details do not match your digital status or vignette.
- Do not manually alter any physical document, including stickers or letters.
- Do not submit multiple conflicting reports for the same typo.
- Do not pay third-party agents claiming they can "expedite" Home Office clerical corrections.
- Do not assume a typo "will not matter" because your photo is correct.

## What to do now
1. **Compile the specific data showing the mismatch.** 
   - Note the exact spelling or digits as they appear on your visa/eVisa.
   - Note the exact spelling or digits as they appear on your current passport.
   - Identify your Unique Application Number (UAN) or Global Web Form (GWF) reference from your original application.
2. **Access the "Report an error with your eVisa" service on GOV.UK if the mistake is in your digital record.** 
   - Log into your UKVI account to confirm the error is visible in the "View and Prove" service.
   - Use the official reporting form to submit the correction request.
   - Save the submission reference number immediately.
3. **Contact the UKVI Resolution Centre if you cannot access your account or the online form fails.** 
   - Call 0300 790 6268 (inside the UK) or +44 (0)300 790 6268 (outside the UK).
   - Select the option for digital status and account-related enquiries.
   - Request a technical incident reference for the data mismatch.
4. **Notify your Visa Application Centre (VAC) or the Home Office immediately if the typo is on a physical vignette sticker and you are still outside the UK.** 
   - Follow the specific "vignette correction" instructions provided in your grant letter.
   - Do not send your passport for correction until you have received an official instruction to do so.
5. **Update your passport details in your UKVI account if the "typo" is actually an old passport number being linked to your new status.** 
   - Use the "Update your details" section of your UKVI account.
   - Upload a clear image of your new passport's bio-data page.
   - Wait for the confirmation email stating your new document is successfully linked.
6. **Inform your carrier or airline if you have imminent travel booked.** 
   - Ask for their specific policy on boarding passengers with a pending UKVI correction report.
   - Prepare to show your "evidence of report" (confirmation email or reference) if they agree to check your status manually.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a completely new visa if the error is a clerical typo made by the Home Office.
- You do not need to replace expired physical documents like BRPs, as these are no longer used for travel in 2026.
- You do not need to contact your employer or landlord until you have tried the primary reporting routes, as they cannot fix Home Office data errors.

## Important reassurance
Clerical errors in name spellings or passport numbers are recognized administrative issues with established correction pathways. Reporting the error promptly creates a paper trail that protects your immigration history even if the fix takes several days.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for clerical data errors on UK visas and eVisas only. It does not cover appeals for visa refusals or substantive changes to visa conditions.

## Important note
Official UKVI processes for eVisas and digital status are subject to technical updates; always verify current reporting links on the GOV.UK website. This information is procedural and does not constitute legal advice.
