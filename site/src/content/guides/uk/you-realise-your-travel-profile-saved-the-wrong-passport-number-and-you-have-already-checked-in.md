# What to do if… you realise your travel profile saved the wrong passport number and you have already checked in (UK)

## Short answer
Focus on the booking for this flight, not the saved profile. Ask the airline to update or verify the passport and Advance Passenger Information details using the physical passport you are travelling with.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ignore it on an international trip; airlines may use passport details for advance checks.
- Don’t cancel and rebook in a rush unless the airline tells you to.
- Don’t keep editing your saved travel profile and assume the checked-in booking changed too.
- Don’t keep trying to check in again across different devices; it may not update the actual booking and can waste time.
- Don’t arrive late if you can avoid it; a staffed desk may need time to check the document.

## What to do now
1. **Check the actual booking, not only your saved profile.**  
   Open the trip in the airline app or website and look for passenger details, travel document, passport details or Advance Passenger Information. Note exactly what is wrong, such as one digit, the expiry date, or an old passport number.

2. **Try the airline’s official Manage booking edit if it is available.**  
   If the booking lets you edit passport or API details after check-in, enter the details from the passport in your hand and save a screenshot or confirmation.

3. **If online edits are blocked, stop troubleshooting and contact the airline.**  
   Use the airline’s official chat, phone line or airport contact route. Say: “I have already checked in, but the passport number on the booking is wrong. Please update the travel document or API details on the reservation.” Ask whether the boarding pass needs to be reissued.

4. **Go to a staffed airport desk even if you already have a boarding pass.**  
   Ask the agent to scan or enter the passport you are travelling with, verify the passport details on the booking, and reprint or reissue the boarding pass if needed.

5. **If you rely on a UK eVisa, check your UKVI account before travel.**  
   Make sure your UKVI account has the current passport you will use, and that the passenger information you give the airline matches it. If the passport details are out of date, update your UKVI account through the official GOV.UK route.

6. **If your destination uses a visa, ETA, ESTA or similar permission, check whether it is linked to the passport number.**  
   If the permission is linked to a different passport from the one in your hand, treat that as urgent. Check the destination’s official process and the airline’s instructions; you may need to correct or reapply as well as fixing the airline booking.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix every saved travel profile today; the priority is the reservation record for this flight.
- You do not need to keep arguing with the app if it blocks edits; use airline support or a staffed desk.
- You do not need to start a complaint or compensation request now; focus on getting the booking aligned to the correct passport.

## Important reassurance
This kind of mistake can happen when an old passport, autofill or a saved profile is used. Airline staff are used to checking travel documents for international travel, and they may be able to correct or verify the booking record when they see your passport.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the travel-day problem. Airline rules, border checks and entry requirements vary by route, carrier and destination, so later decisions may need airline, immigration or specialist travel-document help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, therapeutic or professional advice. Use the airline’s official support and the relevant government guidance for your route.
