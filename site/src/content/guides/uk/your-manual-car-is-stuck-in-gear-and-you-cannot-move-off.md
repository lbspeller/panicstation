# What to do if… your manual car is stuck in gear and you cannot move off (UK)

## Short answer
Make the situation safe first: keep the car still, put the hazard lights on if you could affect other traffic, and do not force the gear lever. If one or two gentle engine-off attempts do not get neutral, stop trying and arrange breakdown recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force, slam, or yank the gear lever.
- Do not start or rev the engine while the car is still stuck in gear unless a professional tells you to.
- Do not keep trying different “tricks” while the car might roll or lurch.
- Do not release the handbrake to test anything unless you are sure the car cannot move into danger.
- Do not get out into live traffic lanes to look under the car or troubleshoot.
- Do not use a warning triangle on a motorway.

## What to do now

1. **Secure the car where it is.**
   - Keep your foot on the footbrake.
   - Put the handbrake fully on.
   - Put the hazard lights on if the car is in or near a road, blocking access, or could surprise other road users.
   - If you are on a slope, keep your foot on the brake while you do anything else.

2. **If you are on a motorway or other high-speed road, prioritise getting safe before troubleshooting.**
   - If you can reach a place of relative safety, such as a service area, lay-by, emergency area, or hard shoulder, do that only if you still have full control of the car.
   - If it is safe and possible to leave the vehicle, leave by the side away from traffic and get well away from the vehicle, behind a barrier where there is one.
   - If you cannot get left or cannot leave safely, stay in the vehicle with your seatbelt on and hazard lights on, then call 999 and ask for the police, or use the vehicle SOS button if it has one.

3. **Try one gentle engine-off neutral attempt.**
   - Turn the engine off.
   - Keep the footbrake on.
   - Press the clutch pedal fully down.
   - Try to move the gear lever gently into neutral.
   - Stop immediately if the lever feels solid or you feel you are having to force it.

4. **Check for a simple pedal or cabin obstruction.**
   - With the engine still off and the handbrake on, look for a floor mat, loose bottle, bag, or object stopping the clutch pedal from going fully down.
   - Move the obstruction only if you can do it without stepping into traffic or letting the car move.
   - Try neutral once more, gently, with the clutch fully down.

5. **If you get neutral, do not rush to drive away.**
   - Keep the handbrake on and the footbrake under control.
   - Check that the gear lever moves normally without force.
   - If the clutch pedal feels wrong, the gear lever jams again, or you are not sure the car is safe, call breakdown recovery instead of driving.

6. **If it is still stuck, stop experimenting.**
   - Leave the engine off.
   - Keep the handbrake on and hazards on.
   - If you are worried the car could creep or roll, keep your foot on the brake until help is arranged.

7. **Arrange professional help.**
   - Call your breakdown provider or a recovery service.
   - Say: “manual car stuck in gear, cannot select neutral, cannot move off.”
   - Tell them whether you are blocking a road, on a slope, in a car park, on a motorway, or in another high-risk place.
   - Give your exact location, including junction numbers, road names, car park level, or nearby landmarks.

8. **Warn other road users only if it is safe.**
   - Keep the hazard lights on if the vehicle is an obstruction.
   - On a non-motorway road, if you have a warning triangle and can place it without risk, it is generally placed at least 45 metres or 147 feet behind the vehicle on the same side of the road.
   - Never place or retrieve a warning triangle if doing so would put you in traffic or make you unsafe.
   - Never use a warning triangle on a motorway.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether the fault is the clutch, selector linkage, gearbox, hydraulics, or cables right now.
- You do not need to choose a garage, approve repairs, or think about costs while you are still in an unsafe place.
- You do not need to keep trying once gentle engine-off attempts have failed.

## Important reassurance
A car stuck in gear can feel serious because it stops you moving, but the immediate job is simple: keep the vehicle still, keep people away from traffic, and get help. Stopping further attempts is often the safest choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce risk and avoid making the fault worse. Later decisions about inspection, recovery, repair, or whether the car can be driven may need help from a qualified mechanic or recovery professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are stopped, prioritise personal safety and contact emergency services or breakdown recovery.
