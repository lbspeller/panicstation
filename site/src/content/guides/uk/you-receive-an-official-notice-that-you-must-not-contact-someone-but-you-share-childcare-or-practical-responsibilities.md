# What to do if… you receive an official notice that you must not contact someone, but you share childcare or practical responsibilities (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all direct and indirect contact immediately and check the written document for specific "child contact" or "legal proceeding" exceptions. If no exception is written, use a neutral third party for essential logistics while you apply for a formal variation or clarification from the issuing authority.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send any messages, even if they are strictly about the children, unless the order explicitly permits that specific method of communication.
- Do not ask friends, family members, or children to pass messages if "indirect contact" is prohibited by the notice.
- Do not enter any "exclusion zones" or go near the other person's home, workplace, or the child’s school if the notice lists these locations.
- Do not rely on the other person’s verbal permission to break the order (e.g., "I won't call the police if you just come to the door") as only a court or the police can vary the terms.
- Do not post any information about the order, the other person, or the children on social media, as this can be interpreted as indirect contact or harassment.
- Do not attempt to "test" the boundaries of the order to see what is permitted.

## What to do now
1. **Locate the specific section detailing "Prohibitions" and "Exceptions" in the document.**
   - Look for phrases such as "except for the purpose of child arrangements" or "via solicitors only".
   - Note the exact end date of the order or the next hearing date.

2. **Identify the issuing authority to determine the correct variation process.**
   - **Family Court (Non-Molestation Order):** Apply to the court using the reference number to vary the order; use Form FL435 to respond to an application made "without notice".
   - **Police (Pre-charge Bail):** Contact the Officer in Charge (OIC) or the Custody Sergeant to request a variation; note that police must consult the victim before changing conditions.
   - **Criminal Court (Restraining Order or Court Bail):** Contact your solicitor immediately as only a Magistrate or Judge can vary these conditions.

3. **Establish a "no-contact" handover protocol for children immediately.**
   - **Third-party handover:** Use a neutral adult (not a child) to physically transfer the child so you do not see or speak to the other parent.
   - **School/Nursery drop-off:** Use the school as a buffer (one parent drops off, the other picks up) only if your order does not ban you from the school's location.
   - **Contact Centre:** Seek a referral to a supported or supervised contact centre for handovers if a neutral third party is unavailable.

4. **Isolate all practical belongings for exchange without interaction.**
   - Leave medication, school bags, or documents with the third-party handover person.
   - Do not include notes, letters, or gifts for the other person in the child's bags.

5. **Use only permitted communication channels for essential updates.**
   - If the order allows communication "via solicitors," send all child-related updates (illness, school changes) to your legal representative to pass on.
   - If a specific parenting app is mentioned as an exception, use it only for factual childcare logistics with no personal commentary.

6. **Submit a formal request for clarification if any term is ambiguous.**
   - Ask the issuing authority or your solicitor for a "lawful method for essential child-related communication."
   - Keep a copy of your request and any response as a record of your attempt to comply while managing responsibilities.

7. **Contact emergency services for immediate child-safety threats.**
   - **Call 999** for life-threatening emergencies or imminent danger.
   - **Call 111** for urgent medical advice that is not life-threatening.
   - Do not use a medical emergency as an excuse to contact the protected person directly unless the order explicitly provides for "emergency communication."

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a permanent long-term childcare schedule today.
- You do not need to resolve financial disputes or child maintenance issues immediately.
- You do not need to respond to personal allegations or "set the record straight" outside of the formal court or police process.
- You do not need to arrange "makeup time" for any contact missed due to the sudden imposition of the order.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a no-contact notice while managing shared responsibilities is a high-pressure situation. It is common to feel overwhelmed by the sudden loss of communication. Following the written terms strictly is the standard procedure for stabilizing the situation, and legal mechanisms exist specifically to create safe, workable exceptions for childcare.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization to prevent legal breaches. It does not replace legal advice from a solicitor or the formal process of a court hearing.

## Important note
Breaching an official notice, non-molestation order, or bail condition is a criminal offence in the UK and can lead to immediate arrest. If you are unsure if an action is allowed, assume it is prohibited until you have written confirmation from a legal professional or the issuing authority.
