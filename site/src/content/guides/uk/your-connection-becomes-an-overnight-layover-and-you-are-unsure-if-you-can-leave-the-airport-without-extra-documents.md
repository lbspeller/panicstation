# What to do if… your connection becomes an overnight layover and you are unsure if you can leave the airport without extra documents (UK)

## Short answer
Do not pass through UK Passport Control until you have confirmed your entry eligibility and demanded a "duty of care" solution (hotel and transport) from your airline. If you lack a required Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) or visa, you must stay in the designated airline transfer area or request Border Force assistance before committing to an entry queue.

## Do not do these things
- Do not join the "Arrivals" or "Passport Control" queue just to ask a question if you suspect you do not have the right to enter the country.
- Do not assume that "transit" allows you to leave the airport; if you pass a border officer to reach a hotel or baggage belt, you are legally entering the UK.
- Do not leave the secure airside area if your terminal closes at night until the airline confirms they have a "holding" solution for passengers without visas.
- Do not discard any physical boarding passes, baggage tags, or digital rebooking notifications as these are your proof of transit.
- Do not attempt to "self-transfer" (collect bags and re-check) if you do not have a valid UK Visa or an ETA (mandatory for most non-visa nationals as of February 2026).

## What to do now
1. **Identify if your ticket is a "Single Contract" or a "Self-Transfer" immediately.** 
   - Check your booking: if all flights are under one reference (PNR), the airline is legally responsible for your transit and "duty of care."
   - If you booked separate flights (Self-Transfer/Multi-city), the airline has no obligation to help you with entry documents, hotels, or missed connections.

2. **Locate the airline’s "Transfer" or "Customer Service" desk before reaching Passport Control.** 
   - **Ask specifically: "Do I need to go landside for my bags or a terminal change, and what is my entry status?"**
   - If the airline says you must go landside but you lack a visa/ETA, tell them: "I do not have permission to enter the UK; I need a solution that keeps me airside or an emergency Border Force referral."

3. **Request a written "Duty of Care" voucher if the delay is the airline's responsibility.** 
   - **Under UK261 regulations, the airline must provide a hotel, transport, and meals for an overnight delay.**
   - Do not leave the desk until you have a voucher or clear instructions on which hotel they have booked for you.

4. **Confirm if you need a 2026 Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) to pass the border.** 
   - **As of February 25, 2026, almost all visa-exempt nationals (including US, EU, and Canadian citizens) must have an ETA to enter the UK.**
   - If you do not have one, you generally cannot go landside to a hotel; ask the airline to facilitate an "emergency entry" with Border Force or provide an airside sleeping zone.

5. **Clarify the status of your checked baggage before moving toward the exit.** 
   - **Ask: "Is my bag 'checked through' to my final destination or is it 'short-tagged' to the UK?"**
   - If it is short-tagged but you cannot enter the UK, insist the airline "re-flight" the bag or hold it in the secure area so you do not have to clear immigration to retrieve it.

6. **Speak to a Border Force official at the "Assistance" or "Transfer" point if you are stuck.** 
   - **Explain clearly: "I am a transit passenger with an unexpected overnight stay and I am unsure if my documents allow me to clear the border."**
   - They have the discretion to grant "Direct Airside Transit" or temporary entry in genuine emergencies, but only if you speak to them *before* an entry refusal occurs.

7. **Secure your sleeping location and set multiple alarms for the morning.** 
   - **If landside: Ensure you know exactly which terminal your morning flight departs from and how long the shuttle/train takes.**
   - If airside: Locate the "Flight Connections" waiting area and confirm what time the security/transfer desks reopen (usually 04:00 or 04:30).

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal compensation claim for the delay while you are still at the airport.
- You do not need to worry about "missed" events at your destination yet; focus entirely on securing a bed and legal status for the next 8 hours.
- You do not need to pay for your own hotel until the airline explicitly refuses to provide one (and even then, keep all receipts for a later claim).

## Important reassurance
Terminal staff and Border Force deal with hundreds of "stuck" passengers daily. Being unsure about your visa or ETA status is a common result of sudden flight changes. By stopping to ask before you cross the border, you are protecting your immigration record and ensuring the airline meets its legal obligations to look after you.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only for the immediate hours following a connection failure. It does not cover long-term visa applications or complex immigration appeals.

## Important note
This guide provides general information regarding UK transit and does not constitute legal or immigration advice. Entry rules, including ETA requirements implemented in 2026, are subject to change based on nationality and specific circumstances. Always verify your status with official UK Government sources or airline staff before attempting to cross the border.
