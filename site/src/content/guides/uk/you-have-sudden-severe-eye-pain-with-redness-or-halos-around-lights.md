# What to do if… you have sudden severe eye pain with redness or halos around lights (UK)

## Short answer
Treat sudden severe eye pain with redness, halos around lights, or vision change as an emergency eye problem. Go to A&E now, or call 999 if you cannot get there safely.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sleep it off or wait for a next-day appointment if the pain is severe, your eye is red, or your vision is affected.
- Do not drive yourself if your vision is blurred, you are seeing halos, or you feel unwell.
- Do not keep wearing contact lenses, and do not put a lens back in to test whether it helps.
- Do not use leftover prescription eye drops, steroid drops, or anyone else’s drops unless a clinician tells you to.
- Do not patch or tape the eye closed.
- Do not press on or rub the eye.
- Do not delay emergency care to try drops, home remedies, or online symptom checking.

## What to do now
1. **Go to A&E now.**  
   Severe eye pain with a red eye, halos, blurred vision, headache, nausea, or vomiting needs urgent in-person assessment.

2. **Use an emergency eye department or eye casualty only if you already know it is open and right for you.**  
   Some services have limited hours, eligibility rules, or telephone triage first. If you are unsure, go to A&E.

3. **Call 999 if travel is unsafe.**  
   Call 999 if you have sudden significant vision loss, severe headache with nausea or vomiting, confusion, collapse, fainting, or no safe way to get to A&E. Do not drive yourself.

4. **Use 111 or your local urgent-care phone service only if it will not delay emergency care.**  
   If someone is with you, they can call while you get ready or while you travel as a passenger. If you are alone and symptoms are severe, leave for A&E or call 999 first.

5. **Make the trip safer.**  
   Ask someone to take you, use a taxi, or use an ambulance if needed. Keep lights low if that is more comfortable, and wear sunglasses if they help you keep the eye open.

6. **Remove contact lenses and bring useful items.**  
   Remove the lens if you can do it easily. Bring your glasses, lens case, lens solution, a list of medicines, any eye drops you have used, and note when the symptoms started.

7. **Say the key symptoms clearly at triage.**  
   Say: “Sudden severe eye pain with a red eye and halos around lights,” and mention any blurred vision, headache, nausea, vomiting, contact lens use, recent eye surgery, eye injury, or chemical splash.

8. **If a chemical splash is possible, rinse immediately and go to A&E.**  
   Start rinsing the eye straight away with clean lukewarm running water or sterile saline if available. Remove contact lenses during rinsing if they come out easily.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether this is glaucoma, infection, injury, inflammation, or something else.
- You do not need to decide about tests, drops, laser treatment, or procedures right now.
- You do not need a perfect timeline; just remember the rough start time and what changed: pain, redness, halos, blurred vision, headache, nausea, or vomiting.
- You do not need to contact your usual optician before getting emergency help.

## Important reassurance
These symptoms can feel frightening and disorienting. Going to A&E is a reasonable and safe response, because some serious eye problems are most treatable when assessed quickly.

## Scope note
This guide is only for first steps: reduce delay, avoid unsafe actions, and get urgent care. Later decisions about diagnosis, treatment, follow-up, and medicines need an eye clinician or other appropriate health professional.

## Important note
This is general first-step information, not a diagnosis, medical advice, or professional advice. If you have sudden severe eye pain, halos around lights, a red eye, or any change in vision, seek urgent in-person care immediately.
