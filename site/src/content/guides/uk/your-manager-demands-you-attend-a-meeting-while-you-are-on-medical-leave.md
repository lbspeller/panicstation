# What to do if… your manager demands you attend a meeting while you are on medical leave (UK)

## Short answer
Obtain written confirmation of your current employment status and check your contract for guaranteed minimum hours. Act immediately to secure alternative income or statutory payments while the situation is clarified.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign immediately to protest the change; doing so may waive your rights to redundancy pay or certain benefits.
- Do not accept verbal-only explanations for the lack of work; always follow up with a written request for clarification.
- Do not sign new "temporary" contracts or variations of terms without independent advice.
- Do not stop communicating with your employer; maintain a record that you are ready and available for work.
- Do not assume you are restricted from taking other work if you are on a zero-hours arrangement; exclusivity clauses are generally unenforceable.

## What to do now
1. **Secure physical and digital evidence of the change immediately.** Save screenshots of the empty rota, work app schedules, or messages stating shifts are cancelled.
   - Gather your last three months of payslips to prove your "normal" working pattern.
   - Keep a log of every time you contact management and their specific response.
2. **Review your written employment contract for "guaranteed hours" or "minimum hours" clauses.** If your contract guarantees a set number of hours per week, your employer may be in breach of contract if they do not pay you for those hours.
   - Look for "lay-off" or "short-time working" clauses that allow the employer to reduce work temporarily without pay.
   - Identify whether you are classified as an "employee" or a "worker," as rights to guarantee pay differ.
3. **Send a formal written enquiry asking for your current employment status.** Use email or a recorded letter to ask if you are still employed, if this is a formal lay-off, or if it is a disciplinary action.
   - Explicitly state the date your shifts stopped and ask for a date when they will be reviewed.
   - Request confirmation of what you will be paid during this period.
4. **Assert your availability for work in writing if you have contracted hours.** Send a message stating: "My contract guarantees [Number] hours. I am ready and available to work; please confirm when my next shift is or how my guaranteed pay will be processed."
5. **Request Statutory Guarantee Pay (SGP) if you are an employee laid off without work.** From 6 April 2026, the SGP rate is £41 per day for a maximum of 5 days in any 3-month period.
   - You must have been employed for at least one month to qualify.
   - You must be available for work and not have refused suitable alternative work.
6. **Check your eligibility for Universal Credit or other income support today.** If your income drops significantly or stops, you may be eligible for benefits even if you are still technically employed.
   - Use an online benefits calculator to check thresholds for rent and council tax support.
   - Apply as soon as possible, as there is often a waiting period for the first payment.
7. **Contact ACAS or your trade union representative if you receive no response within 48 hours.** Professional advice is necessary if the employer is "quietly firing" you or ignoring requests for pay you believe is contractually owed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to take the matter to an Employment Tribunal.
- You do not need to file a formal grievance in the first 24 hours while waiting for a response to your status inquiry.
- You do not need to determine if this is "redundancy" until the employer confirms the lack of work is permanent or prolonged.
- You do not need to update your CV or start job hunting immediately, though you are legally permitted to seek other work if your hours are zero.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of professional instability when a schedule is suddenly cleared. This situation is often a procedural or financial issue on the employer's side rather than a reflection of your performance. Following a factual, written process is the most effective way to protect your rights and your income.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a sudden loss of scheduled hours in the UK. It does not cover the long-term legal process of claiming for constructive dismissal or the specific procedures for collective redundancies.

## Important note
Employment law in the UK is subject to specific contract terms and the Employment Rights Act 2025. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Statutory rates and rules regarding unfair dismissal change frequently; professional consultation with ACAS or a qualified solicitor is recommended for specific disputes.
