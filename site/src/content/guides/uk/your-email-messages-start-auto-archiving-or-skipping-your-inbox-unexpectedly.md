# What to do if… your email messages start auto-archiving or skipping your inbox unexpectedly (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a possible email account compromise: stop relying on the inbox for anything important, then check forwarding, rules, filters, and account access before you tidy folders.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t request password resets for important accounts until you have checked whether this email account is forwarding or hiding messages.
- Don’t click “security alert” links from emails, texts, or pop-ups about this issue; open your email provider’s site or app directly.
- Don’t mass-delete Archive, All Mail, Other, Junk, or Deleted Items while you are stressed.
- Don’t assume it is only a display problem if you also see unknown sign-ins, unknown devices, new rules, or unexpected forwarding.
- Don’t keep using a work or school mailbox without telling IT if you suspect someone else has access.

## What to do now
1. **Pause anything that depends on this email account.**  
   For the next few minutes, avoid password resets, identity checks, or account changes that send links to this mailbox. If something is urgent, use a recovery method you already control, such as an authenticator app, a saved backup code, or a phone prompt.

2. **Check for unknown forwarding first.**  
   Open the email provider’s settings from the official app or by typing the provider’s website address yourself. Look for forwarding, redirect, POP, or IMAP settings. Turn off any forwarding or redirect address you did not set.

3. **Check rules and filters that move or hide incoming mail.**  
   Look for rules, filters, sweeps, or automations with actions such as “skip inbox”, “archive”, “move to folder”, “mark as read”, “delete”, “forward”, or “redirect”. Disable or delete anything you do not recognise, especially broad rules that apply to most messages.

4. **Secure the account.**  
   Change the email account password to a strong, unique one. Turn on 2-step verification if it is not already on. Then use the provider’s option to sign out other sessions, devices, or apps if you can.

5. **Check account access.**  
   Review recent sign-ins, signed-in devices, connected apps, browser extensions, mail add-ons, and delegated or shared mailbox access. Remove anything you do not recognise. If this is a work or school account, contact your IT or security team now and follow their process.

6. **Confirm whether mail is arriving but being moved.**  
   Send yourself a test email from another account if you can. Search for its subject or sender rather than relying only on the inbox. Check Inbox, All Mail or All Messages, Archive, Other, Focused, Junk, and Deleted Items.

7. **Rule out a device or mail app re-moving messages.**  
   If you use Outlook, Apple Mail, Thunderbird, a phone mail app, or another synced device, temporarily sign out of the least trusted mail app or pause its mail sync. Then check whether new messages still skip the inbox or whether the unwanted rule returns.

8. **Check the device you use for email.**  
   Update the browser and mail app. Remove browser extensions you do not recognise. Run your usual security scan if your device has one.

9. **If you cannot regain control quickly, use official recovery.**  
   Use the provider’s official account recovery page from a typed or bookmarked address. Note when the problem started, where messages are going, and any suspicious forwarding addresses, rules, devices, or apps. Screenshots can help if you need provider, bank, or IT support.

10. **If there is any sign of fraud, take the relevant UK reporting step.**  
   If money may be involved, contact your bank using the number in your banking app, on your card, or on the bank’s official website. If you live in England, Wales or Northern Ireland, report cyber crime or fraud to Report Fraud. If you live in Scotland, report via 101.

## What can wait
- Sorting labels, folders, and old archived messages.
- Replying to contacts before the account is secured.
- Working out whether the cause was a bug, a sync issue, or a compromise.
- Cleaning up your whole mailbox history.
- Changing every linked account password before this mailbox is under control.

## Important reassurance
Mail suddenly skipping the inbox can happen because of a rule, filter, forwarding setting, Focused or Other inbox setting, or a synced mail app. Checking it as a security issue first is a sensible way to stop further harm while you work out the cause.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions, such as a full device clean-up, linked-account audit, workplace incident report, or fraud support, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, technical, cybersecurity, or professional advice. For a work, school, banking, or high-risk account, use the organisation’s official support or security process.
