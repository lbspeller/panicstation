# What to do if… your work expense card is frozen or declined while you are travelling (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your employer’s travel, finance, or card administrator and the corporate card provider using trusted contact details. Ask for the card to be unblocked, the limit changed, or an approved alternative payment arranged before you put major costs on your personal card.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the card at multiple places, or many times at the same terminal. Repeated attempts may make the block harder to resolve or create confusion about what failed.
- Do not use the hotel desk or airline counter to “test” a large payment. If the card provider or your employer asks you to test it, use one small transaction only.
- Do not pay large hotel, flight, or onward travel costs personally unless you have written approval confirming what is approved and any limit.
- Do not send the full card number, expiry date, security code, or screenshots showing full card details by plain text email, chat message, or direct message.
- Do not trust a phone number sent in an unexpected email or text. Use the number on the card, in the corporate card app, in your employer’s travel system, or in an internal directory you already trust.
- Do not take cash out or use cash-like transfers unless your employer explicitly authorises it. These may breach policy, create fees, or be hard to reimburse.
- Do not cancel bookings in panic if you can avoid it. Ask the hotel, airline, rail company, or travel agent to hold the booking or extend the payment deadline while you resolve the card issue.

## What to do now
1. **Cover immediate essentials first.**  
   Focus on somewhere safe to stay, getting to or from the work site, and food or water. If you are at a hotel desk, station, airport, or check-in counter, ask them to hold the reservation or booking while you contact your employer.

2. **Check the simple, low-risk causes.**  
   Confirm that you are using the correct card and the expected payment method, such as chip and PIN rather than contactless if contactless has failed. Check the card app, travel app, or work email for alerts such as suspected fraud, limit reached, country restriction, merchant restriction, or receipt overdue. If the card provider or your employer asks you to check whether it now works, do one small transaction only.

3. **Call the corporate card provider using a trusted number.**  
   Use the number on the back of the card or inside the corporate card app. Ask:
   - whether the card is blocked for suspected fraud
   - whether you can verify yourself now
   - whether the problem is a country block, merchant category block, daily limit, weekly limit, or account administration issue
   - whether the company card administrator needs to approve the unblock or limit change

4. **Message the right people at work in parallel.**  
   Contact your line manager and whoever handles travel, finance, accounts payable, or corporate cards. Keep the message short: say where you are, what payment is failing, what essential cost is due, and the next deadline. Ask for one clear fix:
   - the card to be unfrozen
   - a temporary limit increase
   - the employer or travel provider to pay directly
   - a virtual card, payment link, or direct bill arrangement
   - written approval for you to use a personal card up to a stated amount for essential work costs

5. **Use any travel support already attached to the trip.**  
   Check your itinerary, booking confirmation, travel app, or employer intranet for an out-of-hours travel helpdesk or travel management company. Ask them to switch payment, reissue the booking, contact the hotel, or arrange a company-approved alternative.

6. **Ask the merchant for a temporary practical workaround.**  
   If a hotel, airline, rail company, taxi firm, or venue needs payment now, ask whether they can hold the booking, charge only the first night or essential part, accept a company payment link, accept a virtual card, or invoice your employer directly.

7. **If you must pay personally, make it clear and reimbursable.**  
   Before paying, get written approval saying what the spend is for, the maximum amount, and that it will be reimbursed as a business expense. Pay only essential work costs such as lodging, necessary work travel, and meals within policy. Keep itemised receipts, booking confirmations, proof of the decline if available, and a note of the merchant, amount, currency, time, and who approved the personal spend.

8. **Create a short record while it is fresh.**  
   In one note on your phone, record the date and time, what payment failed, who you contacted, what they told you, and what workaround was approved. Forward approvals and receipts to your work email or expense system when you can.

9. **If you are stuck late at night or feel unsafe.**  
   Move to a safer public place if you can, such as a staffed hotel lobby, station office, airport help desk, or open business. Escalate to your employer’s out-of-hours manager, travel support, or security contact and state that you need immediate help to cover safe accommodation or travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to debate the expense policy wording while you are mid-trip.
- You do not need to work out who caused the card block right now.
- You do not need to complete a perfect expense claim now; collect receipts, approvals, and proof.
- You do not need to decide today whether to raise a grievance or complaint.
- You do not need to personally solve your employer’s card administration problem before you are safe and able to continue the trip.

## Important reassurance
Corporate cards can be frozen during travel for ordinary reasons, including fraud checks, country restrictions, merchant blocks, spending limits, or admin flags. This does not mean you have done something wrong; your immediate task is to keep essential costs covered and get a clear written workaround from your employer.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate travel disruption only. Later decisions about reimbursement, expense policy, deductions, complaints, or employment rights may need specialist help from your employer, union, Acas, or another appropriate adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, tax, employment, or professional advice. Workplace expenses depend on your contract, your employer’s policy, and the facts. If you are asked to cover significant work costs personally, denied reimbursement, or left effectively underpaid because of required work expenses, seek advice once the immediate travel problem is stable.
