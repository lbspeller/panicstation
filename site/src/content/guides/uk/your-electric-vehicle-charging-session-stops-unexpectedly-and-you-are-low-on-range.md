# What to do if… your electric vehicle charging session stops unexpectedly and you are low on range (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, such as a service area, car park, or well-lit side street, then call the charge point operator’s helpline shown on or near the public charge point while you arrange recovery if you may not safely reach another charger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep driving “to see if it’ll be fine” if the remaining range is tight.
- Don’t stop in a live lane, on a slip road, or anywhere that blocks traffic.
- Don’t argue with other drivers or rush for another bay; keep the situation calm and safe.
- Don’t repeatedly try payment cards, apps, or QR codes if the charger is clearly faulting; keep enough phone battery to call for help and share your location.
- Don’t let anyone tow the EV on its wheels unless the manufacturer instructions and a recovery operator say it is safe.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car and you safe first.**
   - If you are still at the charger, stay on the charging site if it is safe and well-lit.
   - If you are already driving and the remaining range is uncertain, aim for the nearest safer stopping place rather than pressing on to a risky last-chance charger.
   - On a motorway or other high-speed road, prioritise a service area or another place of relative safety if you can reach one safely.

2. **Check the simple “ended session” causes once.**
   - Confirm the connector is fully seated and latched.
   - Look at the charger screen for a clear message such as fault, timeout, payment ended, authorisation failed, or emergency stop.
   - If the charger has a clear start or retry prompt, try once. If it fails again, move to calling for help.

3. **Call the charge point operator’s helpline shown on or near the public charge point.**
   - Ask them to check whether the session ended, whether payment or authorisation failed, and whether the unit is faulted.
   - Ask whether they can restart or reset the charger remotely.
   - If they cannot restart it, ask whether another bay at the same site is working and whether they can identify the nearest working unit.

4. **Choose one realistic next move.**
   - Use the car’s built-in navigation or one trusted charging app to pick one nearby charger that is within a comfortable range buffer.
   - Prefer a location where you can stop safely if the charger is unavailable, such as a service area, retail park, or large car park.
   - Avoid using your last few miles to reach an isolated charger unless there is no safer option.

5. **If there is a real chance you will not reach a charger, line up recovery now.**
   - Call your breakdown provider or vehicle manufacturer roadside assistance before the battery reaches zero.
   - Say: “EV with very low battery; the charging session ended unexpectedly; may need EV-appropriate recovery to a charger.”
   - Tell them where you are, the remaining indicated range, whether the car can still move, and whether you are in a safe place.

6. **If you are stopped on a motorway or high-speed road and cannot get safely clear, get official help.**
   - Keep seat belts and hazard warning lights on if you cannot safely exit the vehicle.
   - Call 999 and ask for the police if you cannot get to the left lane or a place of relative safety, or if it is not safe to get out and wait away from traffic.
   - If you are safely stopped near an emergency roadside telephone, use it for advice and assistance.
   - On England’s motorways and major A-roads, you can call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 for advice and assistance, then contact your breakdown service.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, request a refund, or report the charger.
- You do not need to fix every account, app, payment, or roaming issue on the spot.
- You do not need to research the best charging network; pick the safest reachable option or arrange recovery.
- You do not need to work out blame while you are still low on range.

## Important reassurance
A charging session ending unexpectedly is stressful, but it often has a practical next step: make the situation safe, contact the operator early, and arrange recovery before the battery is fully depleted.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce the chance of becoming stranded. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, charger faults, vehicle faults, or recurring problems may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information for urgent first steps, not legal, mechanical, financial, medical, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are, prioritise immediate safety and emergency help.
