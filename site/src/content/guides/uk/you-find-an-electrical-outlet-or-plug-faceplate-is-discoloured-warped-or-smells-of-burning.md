# What to do if… you find an electrical outlet or plug faceplate is discoloured, warped, or smells of burning (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using that socket or plug now. If there is smoke, flame, sustained crackling, or you feel unsafe, leave the property and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep using the socket, plug, adaptor, or extension lead “just once.”
- Don’t touch the faceplate, plug pins, cable, or appliance if anything feels hot, soft, sticky, melted, or smells of burning.
- Don’t remove the socket faceplate or try to tighten wiring yourself.
- Don’t test it by plugging in another appliance.
- Don’t pull the plug out by yanking the cable.
- Don’t fit a higher-rated fuse or move the same load to an adaptor or extension lead without the cause being checked.
- Don’t ignore scorch marks, a hot-plastic smell, buzzing, crackling, smoke, repeated tripping, or flickering nearby lights.

## What to do now
1. Make the area safer. If you see smoke or flames, hear sustained crackling, or think the socket may be actively burning, get everyone out, close doors behind you if you can, and call 999.
2. Stop the load only if it is clearly safe. If the socket has a switch and you can reach it without touching hot or damaged parts, switch it off. If the plug and cable are cool and not stuck or melted, unplug by holding the plug body, not the cable.
3. If the plug, socket, cable, adaptor, or appliance is hot, softened, stuck, smoking, or smells of burning, do not touch it. Move away and turn off power from the consumer unit only if you can reach it safely.
4. At the consumer unit, switch off the labelled circuit for the affected sockets if you can identify it. It may be labelled “sockets,” “upstairs sockets,” “downstairs sockets,” “ring main,” or similar. If you are unsure, use the main switch.
5. Do not open the consumer unit or stand close to it if it feels hot, smells of burning, is smoking, or is crackling. Leave and call 999.
6. Move flammable items away from the affected socket if you can do this without touching the socket, plug, cable, or appliance. Keep paper, curtains, clothes, bedding, aerosols, and cleaning products away from it.
7. Put a clear “do not use” note or tape over the socket, and tell everyone in the property not to use it. Keep the appliance, plug, adaptor, or extension lead that was connected out of use too.
8. Arrange an urgent check by a registered electrician. Describe the discolouration or warping, any heat, smell, buzzing, crackling, sparks, tripping, flickering lights, and what was plugged in.
9. If you rent, report it urgently to your landlord, letting agent, housing association, or property manager. Keep the socket and the connected appliance out of use while you wait.
10. If power keeps tripping, nearby lights flicker, another socket feels warm, or you notice a burning smell elsewhere, leave that circuit or the main switch off until it has been checked.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide why it happened right now.
- You don’t need to replace the socket, plug, fuse, adaptor, or extension lead yourself.
- You don’t need to test the socket to see whether it is still working.
- You don’t need to choose a permanent repair before the immediate risk has been checked.

## Important reassurance
Discolouration, warping, heat, buzzing, crackling, or a burning smell are all reasonable reasons to stop using the socket and get help. Stopping use and isolating power early is a sensible safety step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation and avoid making it worse. Later decisions may need a registered electrician to inspect the socket, wiring, plug, appliance, adaptor, or extension lead.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not electrical, legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If there is smoke, flame, sustained crackling, or immediate danger, leave the property and call 999.
