# What to do if… a person claims to be a tow operator for your vehicle but you did not request service (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a safer pause, such as inside a locked car, indoors, or near other people, and verify independently before you agree to anything. If you feel threatened, they are trying to force access, or they will not let you leave, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t hand over your keys, V5C logbook, bank card, ID, or photos of these “to confirm ownership”.
- Don’t sign anything, scan a QR code, or open a payment link while you are under pressure.
- Don’t follow them to a second location “to sort the paperwork”.
- Don’t pay cash or transfer money on the spot to “stop the tow”.
- Don’t argue face-to-face or physically try to block a tow.
- Don’t let them into your home or garage because they say they are “official”, unless you have independently verified who they are and why they are there.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause immediately.**  
   If you are in or near the vehicle, get inside if that is safe, lock the doors, and keep windows mostly up. If you are at home, go indoors, lock the door, and speak through a window or door if you speak at all.

2. **Call police if there is immediate risk.**  
   Call 999 if you feel threatened, they are trying to force access, you think theft is in progress, or you cannot safely leave. Call 101 if it is not an emergency and you want police advice or to report what is happening.

3. **Ask for identification without stepping closer.**  
   Through the window or door, ask for their name, company name, vehicle registration, job reference, and who instructed the job. Ask them to hold ID and paperwork up to the glass, or put paperwork through a letterbox or under a door. Do not take documents from their hand if that would bring you closer.

4. **If they claim police, council, court, creditor, or other official authority, verify through a route you choose.**  
   Ask which organisation instructed the removal and ask for a reference number. Then contact that organisation using a number or website you find yourself. Do not use a number, QR code, or link they provide while they are pressuring you.

5. **Check your own vehicle-related contacts.**  
   Call your insurer, breakdown provider, leasing company, finance company, or employer fleet contact from your own policy, app, paperwork, or saved number. Ask whether they requested recovery, repossession, or enforcement for your vehicle.

6. **If this is on private land, do not rely on the operator’s claim alone.**  
   If you are at flats, a supermarket, a workplace, or a car park, contact site management or security using details you find yourself and ask whether they called an authorised recovery service. If the person is already trying to take the vehicle and you cannot verify why, treat it as urgent and call police.

7. **If they claim debt enforcement, slow it down and verify.**  
   Do not let them in. Ask them to show ID, their company details, and the paperwork through a window or letterbox. Terms and processes can differ by location and by type of debt, so verify by contacting the named organisation, court, creditor, or official advice route using details you find independently.

8. **Document from a safe position.**  
   If it is safe, note or photograph the tow truck registration plate, company branding, the person’s clothing, any phone numbers on the vehicle, and any job reference. If you have a dashcam, doorbell camera, CCTV, or phone footage, save the clip.

9. **If they start attaching equipment to the vehicle, prioritise safety over the car.**  
   Do not physically intervene. Move back, call 999 if there is danger or suspected theft in progress, and keep documenting only from a safe place.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether this is a scam, a mistake, or a lawful removal.
- You do not need to negotiate fees, storage costs, release payments, or paperwork while the person is in front of you.
- You do not need to sort an insurance, finance, parking, or debt dispute in the moment.
- You do not need to make a detailed statement until you are somewhere calm and safe, unless police ask you for immediate information.

## Important reassurance
It is common to freeze or feel pressured when someone acts confident and official. Slowing things down and verifying independently is a reasonable safety step, and a legitimate operator should be able to identify who authorised the job.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk in the moment and prevent irreversible mistakes. Later decisions may need help from police, your insurer, your breakdown provider, property management, a debt adviser, or legal advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information for immediate safety and scam-risk reduction. It is not legal, financial, insurance, or professional advice. If you believe a crime is in progress or you feel in danger, call 999.
