# What to do if… an app update signs you out and your downloaded content or offline data is missing (UK)

## Short answer
Stop making changes that could overwrite recovery options. Confirm you are signed in to the same account as before, then use the app’s Restore purchases, Restore downloads, Sync, or Re-download option before uninstalling or clearing anything.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t uninstall and reinstall the app until you have checked whether it can restore or sync, because reinstalling can remove local or offline data that may not be recoverable.
- Don’t clear the app’s cache, storage, or data unless you are sure the missing content is safely synced somewhere else.
- Don’t repeatedly log in and out of several accounts to “try things”; it can make it harder to tell which account owns the content.
- Don’t follow sign-in links from emails or texts about the sign-out. Open the app, the official app store, or the service’s official website yourself.
- Don’t factory reset your phone, tablet, or computer while you are still checking what can be recovered.
- Don’t pay anyone who messages you offering “recovery” unless it is the official app provider and you started the contact.

## What to do now
1. **Pause and record what you can see.**  
   Take screenshots of the signed-in email or username, the empty library, any error message, and the app version. Note when the update happened.

2. **Check the account inside the app.**  
   Open Account, Profile, Settings, or Library and check the email, username, phone number, or sign-in method. If you normally use Apple, Google, Facebook, email, or a work account to sign in, make sure you used the same method as before.

3. **Check the store account on the device.**  
   On Apple devices, check the Apple Account used for App Store purchases and subscriptions. On Android, check the Google account used in Google Play. If you have more than one account, the content may be linked to another one.

4. **Use the app’s own recovery option before changing anything else.**  
   Look for Restore purchases, Restore downloads, Sync library, Re-download, Manage downloads, or Refresh library. Run that option once and give the app time to rebuild the library.

5. **Check whether the content is still downloading or re-indexing.**  
   Keep the device on power and Wi-Fi. Open the app’s downloads, library, queue, storage, or offline section and look for pending, paused, expired, or waiting items.

6. **Protect local-only data if it matters.**  
   If notes, recordings, drafts, maps, or other local-only files are still visible, do not edit or delete them. If the app appears to be replacing local data with an empty cloud state, stop using that app until you have screenshots and have checked the app’s support guidance.

7. **Check purchase and subscription records.**  
   For Apple purchases, check App Store purchase history and subscriptions under the same Apple Account. For Google Play purchases, check order history, library, and subscriptions under the same Google account.

8. **Secure the account if the sign-out feels suspicious.**  
   If you see unknown logins, unfamiliar purchases, password-change messages, or charges you do not recognise, change the password through the official account page, review recent account activity, and turn on two-factor authentication, two-step verification, or passkeys where available.

9. **Contact the app’s official support with a short evidence bundle.**  
   Send the screenshots, device model, operating system version, app version, update date, signed-in account, and what type of content is missing. Ask whether the missing items are stored locally or in the cloud, whether your account has prior server-side data, and whether purchases can be linked to the correct account.

10. **Use a Subject Access Request only if personal data may be held by the company.**  
   In the UK, you can generally ask an organisation for copies of personal information it holds about you. This may help with account records or data the company stores, but it is not the same as restoring downloaded media, offline files, or app entitlements.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to switch apps, cancel subscriptions, leave reviews, or buy another device.
- You do not need to diagnose whether this was a bug, account mismatch, sync delay, expired download, or local data loss before taking the protective steps above.
- You do not need to pay for recovery services or reset the device while official restore, account, and support routes are still being checked.

## Important reassurance
This kind of problem often happens after updates because apps may sign users out, rebuild libraries, expire offline downloads, or show the wrong account first. Pausing before reinstalling, clearing storage, or resetting the device gives you the best chance of preserving whatever can still be restored.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation and avoid irreversible loss. Later decisions about refunds, formal complaints, developer escalation, data requests, or moving to another service may need specialist support.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, technical, therapeutic, or professional advice. Apps vary in how they store downloads, purchases, subscriptions, encryption, cloud sync, and offline data, so use official support routes for the specific app and account.
