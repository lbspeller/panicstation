# What to do if… your phone screen is broken and you cannot enter your passcode to unlock key apps (UK)

## Short answer
Stop attempting to save new files immediately, free a minimum of 2 GB of device space by deleting large non-essential downloads, and verify that your device is not confusing account/cloud storage with physical hardware storage.

## Do not do these things
- Don't perform a factory reset or "erase all content and settings" as your first troubleshooting step.
- Don't delete original photos or videos until you have verified a successful backup elsewhere.
- Don't download third-party "cleaner" or "booster" apps, as these often request dangerous permissions or charge hidden subscriptions.
- Don't assume that buying more iCloud or Google One storage will immediately fix a full physical phone.
- Don't continue attempting to film or photograph the same event repeatedly; this can cause file corruption or system crashes.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific bottleneck in your settings.**  
   **On iPhone, go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage; on Android, go to Settings > Storage (or Battery and Device Care > Storage).**  
   - Determine if the "Full" status refers to the "Internal Storage" (the physical phone) or "Cloud Storage" (iCloud/Google Account).  
   - If the internal storage has less than 500 MB free, the system may stop saving any new data, including incoming texts.

2. **Delete large, high-bandwidth files that are easily replaceable.**  
   **Open your Files app (iOS) or File Manager (Android) and navigate to the "Downloads" or "Recent" folder.**  
   - Delete large PDF documents, zipped files, or work documents you have saved elsewhere.  
   - Locate "Offline Media" in apps like Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, or YouTube and remove downloaded movies or playlists.

3. **Use the "Offload" or "Clear Cache" functions to reclaim space without losing data.**  
   **On iPhone, go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage and select "Offload Unused Apps"; on Android, go to Settings > Apps > [Select App] > Storage > Clear Cache.**  
   - Offloading on iOS removes the app but keeps its documents and data.  
   - Clearing cache on Android removes temporary files but keeps your login and personal settings.  
   - Avoid "Clear Storage" or "Clear Data" on Android unless you are prepared to log in again and potentially lose in-app data.

4. **Manage high-volume chat attachments in messaging apps.**  
   **In WhatsApp, go to Settings > Storage and Data > Manage Storage to see a list of files larger than 5 MB.**  
   - Delete large videos or forwarded memes that are already saved or no longer needed.  
   - Ensure you empty the "Recently Deleted" folder in your Photos app, as deleted items occupy space for 30 days unless manually purged.

5. **Transfer the most recent, unsaved items to an external source.**  
   **Connect your phone to a laptop or PC via a USB-C or Lightning cable and manually copy the newest photos/videos.**  
   - For UK users with a Mac, use "Image Capture" or "Photos" to import.  
   - For Windows, use the "Photos" app or "File Explorer" to move files to a hard drive.  
   - Confirm the files open correctly on the computer before removing them from the phone.

6. **Cancel any "subscription trap" apps installed during the crisis.**  
   **In the UK, if you accidentally signed up for a paid cleaner app, go to your app store subscriptions to cancel immediately.**  
   - Under UK Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) rules, you have an absolute right to cancel a Continuous Payment Authority (CPA) by contacting your bank directly if the merchant refuses to cooperate.

## What can wait
- You do not need to sort or label your entire photo library today.
- You do not need to purchase a new device or an expensive storage upgrade immediately.
- You do not need to troubleshoot why cloud syncing isn't working until you have cleared enough physical space for the system to run its background processes.

## Important reassurance
It is normal for modern smartphones to behave erratically when storage is critically low. This does not usually indicate a hardware failure or a permanent loss of all data. By clearing a small amount of "breathing room" (1–2 GB), most system functions like messaging and camera saving will resume within minutes.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for devices that have reached critical storage limits. It does not cover deep-level data recovery for corrupted OS partitions or hardware repair for faulty NAND flash memory.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional technical or legal advice. Software interfaces vary significantly between device manufacturers and OS versions (e.g., iOS 18 vs Android 16). In the UK, if your device is provided by an employer, consult your internal IT policy before attempting to offload apps or clear system caches.
