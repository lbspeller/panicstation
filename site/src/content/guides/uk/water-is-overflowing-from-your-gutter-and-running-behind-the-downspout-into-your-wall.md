# What to do if… water is overflowing from your gutter and running behind the downspout into your wall (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as active water ingress. Make the inside safe, keep away from wet electrics, limit further wetting where you safely can, and get the gutter or downpipe fault reported or repaired urgently.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb a ladder, lean out of a window, or go onto a roof in wind, heavy rain, darkness, or while you are alone.
- Do not touch wet sockets, switches, light fittings, boiler controls, the consumer unit, or anything electrical in the wet area.
- Do not plug in fans, heaters, dehumidifiers, extension leads, or other electrical equipment in the wet area until it has been made safe.
- Do not put hands or tools into a downpipe, hopper, or gutter outlet where you cannot see what is inside.
- Do not use tape, foam, sealant, or filler as the main fix while water is still being driven into the wall.
- Do not start stripping plaster, drilling, or opening wall cavities while the leak is active and the electrics have not been checked.

## What to do now
1. **Make the inside safe first.**  
   Move people, pets, valuables, furniture, and soft furnishings away from the wet wall or floor. Put towels down and place a bucket, tray, or bowl under any drips.

2. **Keep away from wet electrics.**  
   If water is near sockets, switches, light fittings, boiler controls, or the consumer unit, keep everyone away from that area. Only switch off the affected circuit or main switch if you can reach it with dry hands, dry footwear, dry floor, and no sign that water has reached the consumer unit. If you are unsure, do not touch it; contact a qualified electrician. If there is immediate danger, call 999.

3. **Check whether water is coming inside.**  
   Look for a damp patch growing during rain, water tracking down an internal wall, dripping around a window reveal, a ceiling stain, bubbling paint, swollen skirting, or a wet socket or switch face. If any of these are changing during rainfall, treat it as urgent.

4. **Look outside from ground level only.**  
   From a safe place, check whether the gutter is overflowing at one point, whether the downspout or downpipe has slipped away from the gutter outlet, whether a joint has separated, whether the gutter has pulled away from the fascia, or whether water is backing up at the outlet.

5. **Do only a safe ground-level action.**  
   If a lower downpipe joint has visibly slipped and you can reach it safely from the ground, gently reseat it and secure the clip if it is obvious how it fits. If the fault is above head height, at the gutter, on a roof edge, or needs a ladder, stop and arrange help.

6. **Report or arrange the repair now.**  
   If you rent, report it to your landlord, agent, repair line, housing officer, factor, freeholder, building manager, or managing agent as urgent exterior water ingress. Say: “Water is overflowing at one gutter point near the downspout and running behind it into the wall. Internal damp or drips: yes/no. Electrics affected: yes/no.” If you own, contact a guttering contractor or roofer and use the same wording.

7. **Reduce ongoing damage once it is safe.**  
   Keep collecting drips, move wet items away from the wall, and ventilate the room if weather and security allow. Use normal heating only if it is safe to do so. Do not use electrical drying equipment in or near the wet area until the electrics are safe.

8. **Record what is happening.**  
   Take photos or short videos from inside and outside, without climbing. Include the overflow point, the downspout or downpipe, the wet wall, any internal damp, and the date and time.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it is a blockage, failed joint, wrong gutter fall, loose bracket, or misaligned outlet.
- You do not need to open walls, lift flooring, strip plaster, or start major drying work until the water source is controlled and the electrics are safe.
- You do not need to decide about insurance, complaints, or permanent redecoration before the leak has been stopped and documented.

## Important reassurance
This can look alarming because a lot of rainwater is being concentrated into one spot. Many cases are caused by a blocked outlet, slipped joint, misaligned downpipe, failed seal, or loose gutter bracket, and those are usually repairable. The priority is to stay safe around electrics, reduce ongoing wetting, and get the fault dealt with promptly.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Permanent repair, safe clearing of gutters, resetting gutter fall, replacing seals or outlets, checking fascia or soffit damage, and assessing hidden damp may need a competent contractor, landlord repair service, building manager, surveyor, or qualified electrician.

## Important note
This is general information for an urgent home and property situation. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, building, insurance, surveying, or other professional advice.
