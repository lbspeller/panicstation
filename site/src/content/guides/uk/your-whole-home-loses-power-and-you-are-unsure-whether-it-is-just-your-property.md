# What to do if… your whole home loses power and you are unsure whether it is just your property (UK)

## Short answer
Check if your neighbours or streetlights are also out; if the outage is local, call **105** from any UK phone to reach the network operator. If only your home is affected, safely check your consumer unit (fuse box) for a tripped switch.

## Do not do these things
- Do not approach or touch **downed power lines** or any objects (fences, trees) in contact with them.
- Do not use **candles** for light due to the high fire risk during outages; use torches or phone lights.
- Do not repeatedly attempt to reset a **trip switch** that immediately falls back to the OFF position.
- Do not open the **fridge or freezer doors** unless absolutely necessary to preserve the internal temperature.
- Do not use **outdoor heaters, barbecues, or camping stoves** inside the home for warmth or cooking.

## What to do now
1. **Conduct a sensory safety scan of your immediate area.** If you smell burning, see smoke, or hear loud buzzing / arcing sounds, locate your consumer unit and turn off the **Main Switch** if it is safe to reach.
   - If there is visible fire or smoke, evacuate and call **999** immediately.
2. **Observe the street and neighbouring properties for signs of power.** Look out the window to see if **streetlights** or **lights in nearby homes** are off, as this distinguishes a local network fault from a property-specific fault.
3. **Inspect your consumer unit (fuse box) safely using a torch.** Check if the **Main Switch** or any individual **RCD / trip switches** have moved to the OFF position.
   - If a switch is OFF, turn off or unplug large appliances (kettle, oven, heaters) and try to flip the switch back to ON **one time only**.
   - If the switch immediately trips again, leave it OFF and seek a qualified electrician.
4. **Report the outage to the Distribution Network Operator (DNO).** Call **105** from a mobile or landline (free across the UK) or call **03457 643643** specifically if you are in Northern Ireland.
   - The operator can confirm if there is a known fault in the area and provide an estimated restoration time.
5. **Protect your appliances and monitor the situation.** Unplug sensitive electronics like computers and TVs to prevent damage from power surges when electricity returns.
   - Leave **one lamp switched ON** in a main room so you are alerted the moment power is restored.
6. **Verify the safety of household members with medical or accessibility needs.** If a medical device requires power and has no battery backup, or if the battery is low, contact the equipment provider or call **999** if the situation becomes a medical emergency.
7. **Manage your food storage based on the duration of the outage.** Keep fridge doors closed to maintain safety for up to **4 hours**.
   - Keep freezer doors closed; a full freezer usually remains safe for **48 hours**, while a half-full freezer remains safe for **24 hours**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide about **food disposal** until the 4-hour (fridge) or 24-hour (freezer) thresholds are reached.
- You do not need to pursue **compensation claims** or insurance paperwork while the power is still out.
- You do not need to **troubleshoot individual sockets** or light fittings if the main trip switch will not stay on.

## Important reassurance
Temporary power outages are common and usually managed by network operators within a few hours. Checking your fuse box and the streetlights is a standard procedure that helps services identify the fault location quickly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the initial minutes of a power loss. It does not cover long-term emergency planning or technical electrical repairs which require a certified professional.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional electrical or medical advice. Always prioritize immediate evacuation and contact 999 if you detect fire, smoke, or life-threatening medical complications.
