# What to do if… someone repeatedly steps into your path to stop you from walking away (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately toward light, witnesses, or a staffed venue to create physical distance. If you are being prevented from leaving or feel an immediate threat of violence, call **999**.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t continue to explain why you are leaving or engage in "negotiation" once your path has been blocked.
- Don’t follow the person to a quieter or more private area "to talk" or resolve the issue.
- Don’t try to push through or initiate physical contact unless it is the only way to avoid immediate physical harm.
- Don’t take shortcuts through isolated routes like alleys, parks, or unlit side streets to avoid the person.
- Don’t look down at your phone for extended periods while moving; maintain visual awareness of your exits.

## What to do now
1. **Change your direction toward the nearest staffed or well-lit area.** **Identify and move toward a shop, petrol station, hotel lobby, or a venue with visible security or staff members.**
2. **State a clear boundary once without stopping.** **Say "Please move, I am leaving" in a firm, loud voice to alert any nearby bystanders that you are being restricted.**
3. **Use physical barriers to break the line of sight and movement.** **Move diagonally or around objects such as parked cars, bollards, or tables to make it harder for the person to step directly in front of you.**
4. **Enlist the help of a third party or staff member immediately.** **Approach a specific person or group and state: "This person is stopping me from leaving, can I stay with you?"**
5. **Request "Angela" if you are in a participating licensed venue.** **Approach the bar or staff and ask for "Angela" to signal you need discreet assistance to leave safely.**
6. **Alert transport authorities if the incident occurs on the rail network.** **Contact a member of station staff or the British Transport Police by texting 61016 for non-emergencies.**
   - Call **999** for any emergency on a train or at a station.
7. **Initiate a silent 999 call if you cannot speak safely.** **Dial 999 from a mobile and, if you cannot speak, listen to the operator and press 55 when prompted to be put through to the police.**
   - Stay on the line even if you cannot talk.
   - Note that pressing 55 does not automatically share your precise location.
8. **Contact 101 only once you are in a safe, secure location.** **Use 101 or online police reporting for incidents that did not result in immediate harm but where a crime, such as the 2026 public harassment offence, occurred.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if the person's behavior was a "misunderstanding" before seeking help.
- You do not need to provide a formal statement to the police in the immediate minutes following the event.
- You do not need to check if you have a "valid" reason to feel unsafe; your priority is establishing distance.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden surge of adrenaline or confusion when your movement is restricted. Feeling startled or shaky does not mean you are overreacting; blocking someone's path is a significant breach of personal boundaries and can be a criminal offence under 2026 public harassment laws.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active or very recent incident of physical blocking in public. It does not cover long-term legal proceedings or domestic abuse support.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Under the Protection from Sex-Based Harassment in Public Act (2026), intentionally blocking a person's path in public can be a specific criminal offence. Always contact **999** in an emergency.
