# What to do if… your passport is with an embassy or consulate for processing and urgent travel comes up (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the office holding your passport immediately to request an urgent document return, which usually requires withdrawing your application and forfeiting fees. If you are outside the UK and a British national, you may be eligible to apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD).

## Do not do these things
- Do not book non-refundable travel until the passport is physically in your hand.
- Do not attempt to travel using only a photocopy, digital scan, or an expired passport unless you have written confirmation from your carrier and the destination's border authority.
- Do not assume you can "borrow" the passport and return it later; most embassies treat a return request as a formal withdrawal of the visa application.
- Do not apply for an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) on a second passport if you are a British dual national, as this is not permitted under 2026 rules.
- Do not send additional identity documents to the holding office unless specifically requested through an official channel.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the exact physical location of your passport.**
   - Check your latest tracking number, submission receipt, or portal status to confirm if the document is at a Visa Application Centre (VAC), a regional hub, or the embassy itself.
   - Record your application reference number and the passport number before making contact.

2. **Submit a formal "Request for Return of Documents" to the holding body.**
   - Use the specific webform or email address provided during your application for "urgent queries" or "withdrawals."
   - State clearly: "Urgent request for passport return due to [Reason, e.g., bereavement / medical emergency] on [Date]."
   - Ask if "Keep My Passport" service can be applied retrospectively, though this is rarely possible once processing has begun.

3. **Use the GOV.UK online service if your passport is held by UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).**
   - Access the "Cancel your visa, immigration or citizenship application" page on GOV.UK.
   - Be aware that if you applied within the UK, document return usually takes up to 10 working days after the withdrawal is processed.
   - Note that you will likely not receive a refund for application fees if processing has already started.

4. **Apply for a UK Emergency Travel Document (ETD) only if you are currently outside the UK.**
   - Visit the GOV.UK "Travel urgently from abroad" portal to start an application if you are a British national and your passport is with a foreign embassy or consulate abroad.
   - Prepare to pay the £125 non-refundable fee and provide proof of your urgent travel (e.g., flight booking or death certificate).
   - Check if your transit countries and destination accept ETDs, as some have strict entry requirements for non-standard documents.

5. **Review 2026 dual-nationality requirements if you hold a second passport.**
   - Note that as of February 2026, dual British nationals must generally enter the UK on a British passport or have a Certificate of Entitlement in their foreign passport.
   - Contact your carrier to confirm if they will allow boarding on your second passport without a valid British passport or ETA/visa evidence.

6. **Gather supporting evidence for compassionate travel.**
   - Obtain a digital copy of a doctor’s letter or death certificate if the travel is for a family emergency.
   - Keep this evidence ready to upload if the embassy or FCDO requests proof to prioritize your case.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new visa submission date until your passport is returned.
- You do not need to pursue a formal complaint against the embassy while the return is still being processed; focus entirely on the retrieval.
- You do not need to notify the police unless you have reason to believe the passport has been lost or stolen rather than just being processed.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard administrative procedure for passports to be retained during visa or immigration processing. While the timing of a return request can be stressful, these offices have established protocols for withdrawing applications and returning documents, even if it requires cancelling your current request.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization for retrieving a passport held by a third-party government or UKVI. It does not cover legal advice for immigration status or long-term visa strategies.

## Important note
Embassy policies are subject to change and vary significantly by country. Withdrawing an application usually results in the loss of all application fees and the cancellation of the visa process. Emergency Travel Documents are not a guaranteed substitute for a full passport and are issued at the discretion of the FCDO.
