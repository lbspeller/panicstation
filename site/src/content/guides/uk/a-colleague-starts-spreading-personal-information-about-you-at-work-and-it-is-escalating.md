# What to do if… a colleague starts spreading personal information about you at work and it is escalating (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your employer’s finance department and the card issuer's emergency line immediately to verify the block and request an unfreeze or alternative payment method. Do not use personal funds for significant business costs without receiving explicit written confirmation of reimbursement approval.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the card at the same terminal or across multiple merchants; repeated declines often escalate a temporary security flag to a hard block.
- Do not attempt to guess your PIN if you have forgotten it, as three incorrect attempts will permanently lock the chip.
- Do not pay for high-value items (flights, hotels, or client entertainment) using personal accounts until you have a written evidence trail of employer authorization.
- Do not share your full card number, expiry date, or CVV over unencrypted email, Slack, or SMS channels.
- Do not use personal cash for business expenses if your contract forbids it, as you may lose the right to reimbursement.
- Do not assume that "it will be sorted later"; solve the payment issue before checking out or departing for your next location.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate safety and lodging first.**  
   - If you are at a hotel check-in desk, ask the staff to hold your reservation for 60 minutes while you resolve the payment issue. 
   - Ensure you have enough personal cash or a backup card for immediate essentials like water, local transport, or a phone charge.

2. **Check for automated notifications from your card issuer.**  
   - Review your work email, SMS, and card management app (e.g., Barclaycard for Business or Amex GBT) for "suspected fraud" alerts. 
   - If a fraud alert is present, follow the specific "Yes, I recognize this transaction" prompt within the app to trigger an automated unfreeze.

3. **Call the card issuer's dedicated 24/7 overseas support line.**  
   - Use the number on the back of your card or these verified 2026 emergency lines:
     - Barclaycard Business: +44 1604 269452
     - American Express Corporate: +44 1273 696933
     - HSBC Innovation/Corporate: +44 20 7367 7852
   - Ask the representative to identify the specific block type: fraud flag, merchant category restriction, or credit limit breach.

4. **Notify your line manager and the company card administrator in writing.**  
   - Send a brief email or message stating your location, the exact item that was declined, and the merchant's deadline for payment.
   - Request a specific resolution: a temporary limit increase, the use of a virtual card number, or a direct "bill-back" to the company.

5. **Request a "bill-back" or direct invoice from the merchant.**  
   - Ask the hotel or travel provider if they can accept a direct payment from your head office or an emailed authorization form.
   - Professional establishments often have "pro-forma" invoicing options for corporate clients that bypass the physical card.

6. **Obtain written indemnity if you must use personal funds.**  
   - If the employer asks you to pay personally, reply with: "I am happy to cover this temporarily provided you confirm in writing now that this specific spend of £[Amount] is approved and will be reimbursed in full by [Date]."
   - Save a screenshot or PDF of their affirmative response.

7. **Document the decline and keep itemised receipts.**  
   - Request a "decline slip" from the merchant or take a photograph of the terminal screen showing the error message.
   - Keep the original itemised receipt for the transaction, not just the card machine stub, to ensure VAT compliance and easier reimbursement.

8. **Log the communication timeline.**  
   - Create a simple note on your phone listing the time of the decline, the name of the bank agent you spoke to, and any reference numbers provided.
   - Forward this log to your work email to ensure it is timestamped on company servers.

## What can wait
- You do not need to investigate why the card was frozen or whose administrative error caused it while you are in transit.
- You do not need to submit the formal expense claim or scan receipts until you have returned to your home or office.
- You do not need to worry about the impact on your personal credit score; corporate liability cards generally do not report to personal credit files unless they are "individual pay" models.

## Important reassurance
Card blocks during business travel are frequent and usually result from automated security algorithms triggered by unusual geographical locations or high-value foreign transactions. This is a procedural hurdle, not a reflection of your professional standing or financial reliability. Most issues are resolved within one phone call to the issuer or a limit adjustment by your finance team.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate travel disruption and payment failure. It does not cover long-term employment disputes regarding expense policy or tax implications for business travel.

## Important note
Reimbursement for work expenses in the UK is primarily a matter of contract law rather than a statutory right. While employers are expected to repay legitimate business costs, they are only legally obligated to do so if your contract specifies it or if failing to reimburse results in your effective pay falling below the National Minimum Wage. If you face persistent refusal to reimburse or are pressured to use personal credit beyond your means, seek advice from Acas or your trade union once the immediate travel crisis is resolved.
