# What to do if… your direct debit or automatic payment has been changed without your permission (UK)

## Short answer
Check what type of payment it is, then use your bank or card issuer’s official contact route to stop the next payment. If a Direct Debit has been set up or collected in error, ask your bank for help under the Direct Debit Guarantee.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click links or call numbers from the message that told you about the change.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, PINs, card details, or online banking passwords with anyone.
- Don’t assume it is only an admin change if the payee, amount, date, reference, or bank details changed and you did not ask for it.
- Don’t close your bank account in a rush before you have stopped the payment and saved the key details.
- Don’t keep paying just to avoid trouble if you are unsure the payment is genuine.

## What to do now
1. **Open the payment in your banking app or online banking.** Note the payee or service user name, amount, date, reference, and whether it is listed as a **Direct Debit**, **standing order**, or **recurring card payment**. Take screenshots if you can.

2. **Stop the next payment using the right route.**
   - **Direct Debit:** cancel it in your bank’s Direct Debit or regular payments area, or ask your bank to cancel it.
   - **Standing order:** cancel it in your bank’s standing orders or scheduled payments area.
   - **Recurring card payment:** contact your card issuer and say you are withdrawing consent for future payments. Your card issuer should stop the payments even if you have not contacted the company first. Do this as soon as possible, and ask whether a payment due very soon can still be stopped.

3. **Contact your bank or card issuer using a trusted route.** Use the banking app, the number on the back of your card, or the number on a statement. Say: **“This regular payment was changed without my permission. Please stop further payments and confirm it is cancelled.”**

4. **If a Direct Debit has already taken money, ask about the Direct Debit Guarantee.** Say: **“I believe this Direct Debit was set up or collected in error. Please check it and tell me whether I am entitled to a full and immediate refund under the Direct Debit Guarantee.”**

5. **Verify with the company only through contact details you already trust.** Use an old bill, the company’s official website, or a trusted directory. Ask whether they changed the Direct Debit details, amount, date, or reference, and ask them to put their answer in writing.

6. **Check for other changes now.** Look in your banking app for new Direct Debits, standing orders, saved payees, scheduled payments, or card subscriptions you do not recognise. Also check that your email address, phone number, and postal address have not been changed.

7. **Secure access to the account.** Change your online banking password if your bank allows it, check that app approval or biometrics are still under your control, and sign out of other devices if that option is available. If you reused the same password anywhere else, change it there too.

8. **Report it if you suspect fraud or cannot explain the change.**
   - **England, Wales, or Northern Ireland:** report cyber crime or fraud through Report Fraud.
   - **Scotland:** report fraud to Police Scotland by calling 101, or use Police Scotland’s online contact route for non-urgent matters.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide today whether to switch banks, replace your phone, or make a formal complaint.
- You do not need a perfect timeline before contacting your bank. Screenshots and the payment details are enough to start.
- You can deal with contract arguments or billing explanations after the payment route is stopped and your account is secure.

## Important reassurance
It is understandable to feel shaken when a payment changes without your permission. The useful first move is not to solve the whole story at once; it is to stop further payments, preserve the details, and use your bank or card issuer’s official process.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about complaints, refunds, contracts, identity theft, or specialist support may need help from your bank, the company involved, police reporting routes, or a qualified adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe or under pressure from someone else, prioritise immediate safety and contact trusted people or official services.
