# What to do if… your only card or bank account is blocked and you cannot pay for essentials (UK)

## Short answer
Contact your bank or card issuer through the app, online banking, or a phone number from its official website, and ask for the fastest safe way to access money today. If you cannot buy essentials within hours, use your local council or national emergency support route while the bank issue is being handled.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep retrying the same payment over and over; repeated declines may make checks harder or leave you with more failed payments.
- Do not call numbers from texts, emails, pop-ups, or search adverts claiming to be your bank.
- Do not share one-time passcodes, full PINs, or security answers with anyone who contacts you.
- Do not move money to a “safe account” because someone on the phone, by text, or online tells you to.
- Do not take a high-cost loan in panic if there is any other safe way to cover today’s essentials.
- Do not ignore urgent rent, energy, water, or phone payments; tell the payee there is a temporary banking block and ask what can be noted or paused.

## What to do now
1. **Get the basics in front of you.**  
   Have your phone, photo ID, proof of address if you have it, and any account details you can safely find, such as the last 4 digits of the card, your sort code, account number, and recent transactions.

2. **Check whether the app or online banking shows the reason.**  
   Look for a fraud alert, a request to confirm a payment, a frozen-card button, a spending-limit setting, or a message asking for identity or security checks. If you can safely unfreeze the card yourself in the app, do that and try one small essential payment.

3. **Contact the bank or card issuer using a trusted route.**  
   Use in-app chat, the phone number inside the app, the number on the back of the card if you have it, or a number from the bank’s official website. Say:  
   **“My only card or account is blocked and I cannot pay for essentials today. What is the fastest safe way for me to access funds?”**

4. **Ask what kind of block it is.**  
   Ask whether it is a card block, an online banking lockout, a fraud check on a payment, or an account restriction. Then ask exactly what they need from you to lift it, and write down the answer, the time, and any reference number.

5. **Ask for a same-day workaround for essentials.**  
   Depending on the bank and the reason for the block, ask whether they can offer:
   - unblocking after you confirm recent transactions
   - help resetting locked app or online banking access
   - a cash withdrawal in branch with ID
   - a temporary or replacement card where available
   - any other same-day cash access or essential-payment option they can safely offer

6. **Go to a branch if remote support cannot solve it quickly and your bank has branches.**  
   Take photo ID and proof of address if you have it. Say you need same-day access to money for essentials and ask whether the branch can verify you, help with an app lockout, or arrange a counter withdrawal.

7. **Protect urgent bills while you wait.**  
   For rent, energy, water, phone, travel, childcare, or other essentials due now, contact the payee and say:  
   **“My bank has temporarily blocked my only payment method. I am resolving it today. Can you note my account and tell me what is needed to avoid escalation?”**  
   Keep any reply or reference number.

8. **Use emergency essentials support if you cannot buy food, heat, or basic travel today.**  
   In England, contact your local council and ask what emergency help with essentials is available; the scheme name may vary. In Scotland, ask your council about a Scottish Welfare Fund Crisis Grant. In Wales, check the Discretionary Assistance Fund. In Northern Ireland, check Discretionary Support. If you are not sure which route applies, Citizens Advice can help you find the right local option.

9. **If you think fraud or a scam caused the block, slow down and secure the account.**  
   Tell the bank immediately about any transaction you do not recognise. Change your banking password only from a trusted device and network. If changing it causes more lockouts, stop and do it with the bank’s help.

10. **Ask for a complaint reference if the block leaves you without essentials or the bank cannot explain the next step.**  
   Ask the bank to record a formal complaint and give you the reference number. If the bank’s response does not resolve it, or the complaint route is open to you, the Financial Ombudsman Service may be able to look at complaints about frozen accounts and blocked payments.

## What can wait
- Choosing a new bank or switching accounts.
- Setting up a backup card or payment method.
- Writing a long explanation unless the bank asks for one.
- Gathering lots of paperwork unless the bank asks for specific documents.
- Making big money decisions, such as borrowing from several places or selling belongings.
- Sorting out non-urgent disputes. For now, focus on essentials, safe contact with the bank, and stopping missed payments from escalating.

## Important reassurance
A blocked card or account can feel frightening because it stops ordinary spending without warning. Banks can block cards or accounts for security, fraud prevention, compliance checks, or mistakes, and many blocks are resolved once the right team confirms what they need from you.

## Scope note
These are first steps to regain access and cover essentials safely. Later decisions, such as complaints, debt help, switching accounts, or preventing repeats, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Bank processes vary by provider and by the reason for the block. If you cannot meet essential needs today, prioritise emergency local support while the bank issue is being resolved.
