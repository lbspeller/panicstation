# What to do if… a bank transfer for rent or mortgage is still processing on the payment deadline day (UK)

## Short answer
Save proof of the payment now, contact the landlord, agent, or lender today with the reference, and ask your bank whether the payment has been released or is still held. Do not send a second payment until you know whether the first one can still complete, unless the recipient agrees how any duplicate will be handled.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send the same payment again “just in case” before checking whether the first payment can still complete.
- Don’t ignore it and hope it resolves, especially for a mortgage payment or any account with existing arrears.
- Don’t move money out of the paying account if that could leave the original payment short.
- Don’t share banking login details, one-time passcodes, or screenshots showing more than the recipient needs.
- Don’t use gift cards, crypto, or a new payee suggested under pressure as a way to “fix” rent or mortgage payment timing.
- Don’t argue about blame before you have the payment status, reference, and a written record of what each side says.

## What to do now
1. **Save the payment proof.** In your banking app or online banking, capture the status text, amount, payee name, payment reference, and the date and time you authorised the transfer. Save a screenshot and also write the details down, because screenshots can be cropped, blurry, or hard to read later.

2. **Check what type of transfer it is.** Look in the transaction details for wording such as Faster Payment, CHAPS, Bacs, standing order, card payment, or internal transfer.
   - Faster Payments are usually quick, but some can take longer, especially if checks are happening.
   - CHAPS is a same-day system on working days, but your bank’s customer cut-off can matter.
   - Bacs is usually not the route to rely on for a same-day deadline.
   If you cannot tell, ask your bank: “What payment type is this, and what is the expected arrival date?”

3. **Contact the rent or mortgage recipient today.**
   - For rent, message the landlord or agent with the amount, date and time sent, payee used, and payment reference. Ask them to note that you authorised the payment today and to confirm what they will record while the bank confirms it.
   - For a mortgage, contact the lender through its secure app, online account, or payments phone line. Give the reference and ask them to note the payment attempt today.

4. **Ask your bank two direct questions.**
   1. “Has this payment been released to the payment system, or is it still held by the bank?”
   2. “Can it still be cancelled, or is it expected to complete?”
   If the bank says it is held for checks, ask exactly what they need from you. If the bank raises fraud or scam concerns, slow down and answer carefully.

5. **Ask the recipient what counts for today.** Ask whether they need money received today, proof that it was sent today, or a payment through a specific approved route. Write down the answer or keep the message.

6. **Only use another payment route with a duplication plan.** If the first payment cannot arrive today and the recipient needs a same-day payment, use only a route the recipient confirms is accepted, such as the lender’s own payment portal or another agreed account. Before paying again, either get the bank to confirm the first payment has failed or is cancelled, or get the recipient to confirm how a duplicate would be credited or returned.

7. **Create a short written record if late fees or account markers are mentioned.** Send a calm message such as: “Payment authorised today at [time]. Amount: [amount]. Reference: [reference]. My bank says it is [processing or held]. Please confirm what you will record while this is being checked.” Keep replies, call times, and names.

8. **For a mortgage, ask about payment support if the money may not arrive.** Tell the lender this is a pending transfer and ask what support or temporary note can be placed on the account while it is checked.

## What can wait
- Complaints, compensation, and arguments about whose fault the delay is.
- Changing banks, changing payment habits long term, or redesigning your budget.
- Detailed legal or housing decisions unless the recipient mentions arrears action, possession, eviction, or default.
- Sending repeated updates after you have given the reference, bank status, and a request for confirmation.

## Important reassurance
A “processing” or “pending” status can mean a normal banking delay, a cut-off issue, or a security check. The useful thing right now is to prove what you did today, avoid an accidental duplicate payment, and get the bank and recipient to put their positions in writing.

## Scope note
These are first steps for today’s payment deadline only. If arrears, fees, credit-file impact, eviction, or possession action is mentioned, the next steps can depend on where you live in the UK, your tenancy or mortgage documents, and your account history, so specialist housing, debt, or mortgage help may be needed.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, debt, housing, mortgage, or professional advice. Payment timing, whether a payment counts as on time, and any fee or account impact can depend on the payment type, the bank’s checks and cut-offs, and the recipient’s terms.
