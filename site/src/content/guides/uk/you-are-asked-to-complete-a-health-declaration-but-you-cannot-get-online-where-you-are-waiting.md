# What to do if… you are asked to complete a health declaration but you cannot get online where you are waiting (UK)

## Short answer
Notify airline or airport staff immediately that you lack internet access and request the approved offline fallback or technical assistance. Do not continue troubleshooting until you miss your boarding window; official staff must provide a workaround if a digital form is a condition of carriage.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t invent medical details or "guess" answers to speed up the form—incorrect legal declarations can lead to fines or denied entry.
- Don’t hand your passport or mobile device to an unknown person offering "free hotspot" or data entry help.
- Don’t pay any third-party website to "expedite" a government form; use only official GOV.UK or airline links.
- Don’t delay your progression through security or toward the gate while trying to fix your signal.
- Don’t send photos of your ID or sensitive data to unverified email addresses or messaging apps provided by non-staff.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific requirement holding you up.**
   - Determine if the requirement is for your destination country, a transit point, or a UK-specific permission (like an Electronic Travel Authorisation/ETA).
   - Check if the request is coming from the airline app or an official government portal.
2. **Alert official staff to the connectivity failure.**
   - **Go to the airline check-in desk, gate agent, or airport information point.**
   - **State clearly: "I am required to complete this digital declaration, but I cannot access the internet. What is the approved offline procedure?"**
3. **Request a physical or assisted alternative.**
   - Ask for a paper version of the health declaration or passenger data form.
   - Inquire if there is a dedicated terminal or kiosk available for passengers without personal devices.
   - Ask if the airline can manually verify your details at the desk without the app.
4. **Verify the official network if you must attempt to reconnect.**
   - **Only use Wi-Fi networks confirmed by staff or official airport signage.**
   - If you successfully submit the form, **take a screenshot of the confirmation page or QR code immediately** so it is available offline.
5. **Protect your privacy if using shared devices.**
   - **Do not allow the browser to save your password or personal details.**
   - **Log out of any portals and close the browser tabs before walking away.**
6. **Confirm if completion on arrival is an option.**
   - Note that for some permissions (like the UK ETA), completion *before* boarding is mandatory and you will be denied travel if it is not resolved at the departure point.
   - If the form is a destination health declaration, ask if you can complete it upon landing in the arrival hall.
7. **Escalate to a supervisor if boarding is imminent.**
   - **Politely request a supervisor to confirm the fallback route if the agent is unsure.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to troubleshoot your mobile roaming settings or phone hardware during the check-in window.
- You do not need to determine why the airport Wi-Fi is failing.
- You do not need to provide feedback on the digital-only policy until you have secured your passage.

## Important reassurance
Digital failure at the border or gate is a recognized operational issue. Airlines and airports have protocols for passengers with device failures, dead batteries, or no connectivity. Technical errors on the government or airline side are also common and staff are trained to manage these disruptions.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and harm reduction for passengers facing digital entry requirements without internet access. It focuses on first-step communication with officials to prevent missed travel.

## Important note
Travel requirements, including the Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) and destination-specific health forms, are subject to change by individual governments. Always follow the specific instructions of uniformed airline or border staff at your location.
