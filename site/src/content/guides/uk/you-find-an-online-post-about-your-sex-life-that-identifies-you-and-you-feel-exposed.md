# What to do if… you find an online post about your sex life that identifies you and you feel exposed (UK)

## Short answer
Do not reply to the post or argue in public. Save the safest basic details, report the post for removal, and contact specialist support if intimate images, threats, blackmail, stalking, or under-18 content are involved.

## Do not do these things
- Do not reply, correct the record, or argue in comments while you are panicked; that can draw more attention to the post.
- Do not send threats, ultimatums, or repeated messages to the person who posted it.
- Do not ask lots of friends to look for it or report it first; that can spread the link further.
- Do not screenshot, download, forward, or send intimate images if anyone in them may be under 18.
- Do not delete accounts, chats, emails, or messages in a rush if you may need help removing or reporting the post later.
- Do not meet the person offline to sort it out.

## What to do now
1. **Check immediate safety first.**  
   If someone is threatening violence, is nearby and you feel unsafe, or you are in immediate danger, call 999. If there is no immediate danger, move to a private place and stop scrolling for a few minutes.

2. **Use a device and account you trust.**  
   Use a personal device if you can, not a shared work, school, family, or public device. If you think someone has access to your account, use another trusted device.

3. **Save only the basic evidence you can safely save.**  
   Record the URL, platform, account name or handle, date and time, and the words or details that identify you. If it is safe and does not capture an intimate image involving someone under 18 or someone whose age is unclear, take screenshots showing the post, handle, date, and URL.

4. **If you are under 18, or the post includes sexual images of anyone who may be under 18, use child-focused help.**  
   Do not copy, forward, or send the image around. Use Childline Report Remove for sexual images or videos of you, and use CEOP if there is grooming, exploitation, threats, or an adult pressuring a child or young person.

5. **Report the post where it is hosted.**  
   Use the platform’s closest options for harassment, sexual harassment, sharing private information, impersonation, threats, or non-consensual intimate content. Include the identifying details, such as your name, workplace, school, address, phone number, or tagged accounts.

6. **If intimate images or videos are involved or threatened, contact specialist support.**  
   If you are an adult in the UK, the Revenge Porn Helpline can help with reporting and removal steps. If you are over 18 in the image and you have access to it, StopNCII may help reduce re-uploads on participating platforms.

7. **If you want to report to police, keep it simple and factual.**  
   You can report through your local police route, including online routes where available. Mention threats, blackmail, stalking, repeated targeting, impersonation, intimate images, under-18 concerns, and any real-world risk. Reporting is your choice unless there is immediate danger or a child is at risk.

8. **Reduce further spread by limiting identifiers.**  
   Make key social profiles private for now, limit tagging and mentions, turn off location sharing, and remove public phone numbers, workplace details, school details, or address clues where you can.

9. **Secure the accounts most connected to this.**  
   Change the password for your email and the affected platform. Turn on two-step verification. Sign out of other sessions or devices if the platform allows it.

10. **Tell one trusted person exactly what help you need.**  
   Ask them to sit with you while you report the post, help you avoid replying publicly, and write down what you reported, where, and when.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to confront the person, make a public statement, or explain this to everyone who might see it.
- You do not need to read every comment, search your name for hours, or gather perfect evidence.
- You do not need to decide today whether to take legal action. Focus first on safety, removal, account security, and support.

## Important reassurance
Feeling exposed, ashamed, angry, or panicked is a normal response to a violating situation. This is not your fault. Quiet, practical steps can help you regain some control without giving the post more attention.

## Scope note
This is first-steps-only guidance to stabilise the situation and reduce harm. Later decisions about police reporting, school or workplace support, longer-term safety, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are under 18, or if the content involves anyone under 18, seek specialist help urgently and avoid copying or sharing sexual images. If you feel unsafe or at risk of harm, contact emergency services.
