# What to do if… your windshield wiper blades are torn or detached before driving in bad weather (UK)

## Short answer
Do not set off into rain, spray, fog, or poor visibility with a damaged, missing, or loose wiper blade. Pause the trip, restore safe visibility, or use another way to travel.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t “chance it” because it is dry where you are if rain or heavy spray is likely on the route.
- Don’t use the wipers if the bare metal arm could touch the glass.
- Don’t drive into bad weather with a blade missing, hanging loose, or not clearing the windscreen.
- Don’t rely on washer fluid to make up for torn or ineffective blades.
- Don’t use tape, cloth, cable ties, loose rubber, or another makeshift repair that could detach, jam the mechanism, or block your view.
- Don’t start a long or high-speed journey hoping you can deal with it at the next stop.

## What to do now
1. **Stop before the journey becomes a visibility problem.**  
   Stay parked if you have not left yet. If you are already moving and visibility is becoming unsafe, slow down gently and stop somewhere safe and legal.

2. **Check what has failed.**  
   With the vehicle parked, engine off, and wipers switched off, look at both front blades. Check whether the rubber is torn, the blade is unclipped, the blade assembly is missing, or the wiper arm is close to the glass.

3. **Keep the wiper arm off the windscreen if the blade is missing.**  
   Do not let a bare wiper arm scrape across the glass. If it is safe to do so while parked, lift the arm away from the glass or leave it in a position where it will not be switched on by mistake.

4. **Refit only if it locks securely.**  
   If the blade has simply unclipped, re-seat it only if it fits the correct way round and locks firmly into place. If it does not lock, feels loose, or could detach again, treat it as not fixed.

5. **Replace the failed blade before bad-weather driving.**  
   If you have the correct spare blade and can fit it safely while parked, replace it now. Test briefly with screenwash and confirm the blade clears the driver’s view without leaving heavy smears, gaps, or juddering.

6. **If you cannot fix it where you are, switch to a fix-first plan.**  
   Arrange help from a garage, mobile fitter, breakdown provider, or someone who can bring the correct blade. You can also use public transport, a taxi, or a lift instead of driving into the weather.

7. **Only move the vehicle if conditions are dry and visibility is safe.**  
   If you need to move it a short distance to a motor factors, garage, or safe place to fit replacements, do that only while the windscreen can be kept clear. If rain, spray, fog, or poor visibility starts, stop somewhere safe rather than continuing with ineffective wipers.

8. **Do one final visibility check before leaving.**  
   Make sure the windscreen is clean, the washers have fluid, the wipers sweep properly, and your view through the windscreen is clear. GOV.UK guidance says required wipers and washers must be maintained in efficient working order, and the Highway Code says windscreens and windows must be clean and free from obstructions to vision.

9. **Send one simple delay message if someone is waiting.**  
   “My wipers have failed and I’m not driving into bad weather. I’m sorting it and will update you when I know more.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to choose premium blades right now.
- You do not need to diagnose the whole wiper system before making the immediate safe decision.
- You do not need to decide whether the journey is cancelled for the whole day.
- You do not need to argue with yourself about whether the weather might improve; first make sure the vehicle can keep the windscreen clear.

## Important reassurance
This is a common vehicle problem, and the safe response is ordinary: pause, fix the visibility issue, or travel another way. The risky part is usually feeling rushed and driving anyway.

## Scope note
These are first steps to prevent a visibility-related crash and avoid windscreen damage. If blades keep detaching, the wiper arm is bent, the washer does not work, or the wiper motor seems faulty, later decisions may need help from a garage, breakdown provider, or other suitable specialist.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate safety. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice.
