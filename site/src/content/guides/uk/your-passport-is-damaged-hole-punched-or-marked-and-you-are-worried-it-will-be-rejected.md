# What to do if… your passport is damaged, hole-punched, or marked and you are worried it will be rejected (UK)

## Short answer
Assume the passport may not be accepted for travel and start a damaged-passport replacement, urgent passport check, or Emergency Travel Document check now, depending on where you are and when you travel.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to repair it with tape, glue, lamination, trimming, erasing, or cover-up marks.
- Do not add any new marks or let anyone stamp it except border officials.
- Do not rely on check-in staff making an exception on the day.
- Do not throw it away; keep it safe because you may need it for the replacement or urgent route.
- Do not pay unofficial “passport help” sites before checking the GOV.UK route.

## What to do now
1. Check whether it matches GOV.UK damaged-passport signs. A passport may need replacing if, for example, your details cannot be read, pages are ripped, cut or missing, there are holes, cuts or rips in the cover, the cover is coming away, or pages are stained by ink or water.

2. Take clear photos for your own records before doing anything else:
   - the front cover
   - the photo and details page
   - the page, cover, corner, hole, clip, stain, or mark that worries you
   - any packaging, envelope, or document that helps show how the damage happened

3. Write down the practical facts you will need:
   - your travel date and time
   - your route and any transit countries
   - whether you are in the UK or abroad
   - whether the passport was damaged by a person, machine, post, courier, office, or by mistake

4. If you are in the UK, start the GOV.UK damaged-passport replacement route. If standard replacement may take too long, check GOV.UK’s urgent passport service. The 1 week fast track route can be used for a damaged passport if you are eligible; the 1 day premium route is limited to renewing an adult passport.

5. If you are outside the UK, do not use the UK urgent appointment service. Check whether you can apply for an Emergency Travel Document if you need to travel within 6 weeks, cannot use your UK passport, and cannot renew or replace it from abroad before travel.

6. If the hole-punch, clipped corner, or mark looks like cancellation, treat it as high risk. Prioritise a replacement, urgent passport route, or Emergency Travel Document route rather than planning to argue it through at the airport.

7. If you are travelling for urgent medical treatment, government business, or because a friend or family member is seriously ill or has died, contact the Passport Adviceline route listed on GOV.UK.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the airline “should” accept it.
- You do not need to make a complaint or seek compensation before securing a valid document route.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip at once; first work out whether you need replacement, urgent service, or an Emergency Travel Document.
- You do not need to explain the situation perfectly; use a short factual description of what happened.

## Important reassurance
It is reasonable to feel alarmed because the passport may look almost usable. Small physical changes can still create a real travel problem, and acting early gives you more options than waiting until check-in.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about complaints, refunds, missed travel, complex routes, visas, or transit rules may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Airlines, carriers, and border authorities may make their own decisions about whether a passport is acceptable for travel.
