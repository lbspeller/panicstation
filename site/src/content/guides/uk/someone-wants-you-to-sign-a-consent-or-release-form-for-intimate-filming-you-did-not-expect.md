# What to do if… someone wants you to sign a “consent” or “release” form for intimate filming you did not expect (UK)

## Short answer
Leave the location immediately without signing any documents and contact a specialist support service like the 24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line on 0808 500 2222.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t sign any document to "end the situation" if you feel pressured, frightened, or confused.
- Don’t stay in a private or isolated room to "talk through" the paperwork.
- Don’t hand over your phone, identification, or passwords to the other person.
- Don’t attempt to negotiate or "trade" other images for the deletion of the new footage.
- Don’t delete any existing messages or threats until you have spoken to a support advocate.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise your physical exit from the location.** If you feel unsafe or are being prevented from leaving, call **999** immediately. 
2. **Refuse to sign using a repetitive script.** Say: "I am not signing anything today and I am leaving now," then move toward the exit without engaging in further debate.
3. **Contact the 24/7 Rape and Sexual Abuse Support Line.** Call **0808 500 2222** for confidential advice on your rights and immediate safety, regardless of whether you intend to report to the police.
4. **Preserve evidence of any threats or the documents themselves.** Take photos or screenshots of the "consent" form and any digital threats to share footage, then store them in a secure, private location.
5. **Seek medical and forensic support at a SARC if required.** Contact your local **Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC)** via the NHS website for confidential healthcare, even if you do not want police involvement.
6. **Report the threat to the Revenge Porn Helpline.** If you are 18 or over, call **0345 6000 459** (Mon-Fri, 10am–4pm) for help with image removal and legal guidance on intimate image abuse.
7. **Secure your digital accounts.** Change your passwords and enable two-factor authentication if you believe the person has access to your devices or accounts.
8. **Use the StopNCII.org tool if images are shared.** This tool allows you to proactively "hash" (digitally fingerprint) images so they can be blocked from being uploaded to major social media platforms.
9. **Record a factual timeline of the event.** Document the date, time, location, and exactly what was said or shown to you while the details are fresh.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether you want to pursue a formal police report.
- You do not need to confront the person or demand they delete the footage while you are alone.
- You do not need to explain your refusal to the person who pressured you.
- You do not need to review the legal definitions of "consent" or "coercion" by yourself today.

## Important reassurance
Being pressured to sign a "release" after unexpected filming is a known tactic of coercion and control. If you felt unable to say no in the moment, or if you froze, this is a natural survival response and is not your fault. You are entitled to support and safety regardless of any documents you may have been forced to sign under duress.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate safety and evidence preservation. Legal advice and long-term support should be sought from qualified solicitors or specialist victim advocates.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, threatening to share intimate images is a criminal offence under the Sexual Offences Act 2003, even if the images do not exist.
