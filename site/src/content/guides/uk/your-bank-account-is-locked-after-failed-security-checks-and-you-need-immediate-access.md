# What to do if… your bank account is locked after failed security checks and you need immediate access (UK)

## Short answer
Stop trying to log in, then contact your bank using a trusted route, such as the number on your card or the bank’s official website. Ask the security or fraud team what verification route can restore access or provide safe access to essential funds today.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep retrying passwords or security answers; repeated failed attempts can make access harder.
- Don’t use numbers or links from unexpected texts, emails, search adverts, or social media messages claiming your account is locked.
- Don’t share a one-time passcode, full PIN, full password, or full memorable information with anyone.
- Don’t pay an unlock fee or let someone remote-control your phone or laptop to fix access.
- Don’t add a new payee or move money while rushed unless you are certain the request is genuine.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the lockout spiral.**  
   Stop login attempts for now. Keep your card, ID, phone, and any bank letters or app messages nearby before you contact the bank.

2. **Use a trusted contact route only.**  
   Open the banking app directly, type the bank’s website address yourself, or call the number on the back of your card. Do not tap a link in a message about the lockout.

3. **Ask for the right team and state the urgent need.**  
   Say: “I’m locked out after failed security checks. I need access to essentials today. Please transfer me to the fraud or security team and tell me the fastest safe verification option available today.”

4. **Ask what proof they can accept today.**  
   Ask whether they can use an in-branch identity check, secure in-app message, video verification, phone verification, or another route. If they suggest a branch, take photo ID if you have it, and ask what they accept if you do not.

5. **Ask about essential access, not just app access.**  
   Say: “If online access cannot be restored today, can you arrange a safe way for me to access essential funds, such as cash at a branch or another bank-approved emergency option?”

6. **Check whether the lock suggests attempted fraud.**  
   Ask the bank to check for suspicious login attempts, new payees, changed contact details, and recent transactions. Ask what they are placing on the account and what could trigger another lock.

7. **Reduce immediate damage from missed payments.**  
   If rent, food, travel, childcare, a Direct Debit, or a standing order is affected, contact the landlord, biller, employer, benefits office, or payer today. Say your bank account access is temporarily restricted and ask what short grace period or safe temporary payment option they can offer.

8. **Write down the essentials.**  
   Note the date, time, team, person you spoke to, reference number, what they asked you to provide, and what they said would happen next. If they say you must wait, ask what exactly is being reviewed and when you should next contact them.

9. **Make a formal complaint if the urgent issue is not being handled.**  
   Ask the bank to log a formal complaint and confirm the complaint reference. For frozen-account or blocked-payment complaints, the firm should respond within 15 days, or explain why it cannot yet respond and then send a response within 35 days. If you are unhappy with the response, or they do not reply in time, you can take the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to switch banks.
- You don’t need to solve every future payment problem on the first call.
- You don’t need a long explanation before asking for urgent verification and essential access.
- You don’t need to post publicly about the lockout; keep contact focused, private, and documented.

## Important reassurance
A lockout after failed security checks does not automatically mean you have done something wrong. It usually means the bank is not confident enough to give access yet, so the immediate task is to use a trusted route, complete safe verification, and protect essential payments.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to regain urgent access and reduce immediate harm. If the bank says the restriction relates to fraud investigation, a court order, insolvency, bankruptcy, money laundering checks, or another formal process, the next steps may differ and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, or professional advice. Banks can restrict access for security and fraud-prevention reasons, and the fastest available route depends on the bank, the account, and the reason for the lock.
