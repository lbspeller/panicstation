# What to do if… your building intercom keeps buzzing from someone you are not expecting (UK)

## Short answer
Do not buzz anyone in just to stop the noise. Keep your own door locked, verify only from inside your flat, and call 999 if someone is trying to force entry, making threats, or you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not press “unlock” to make the buzzing stop.
- Do not go down to the communal entrance alone to check who it is.
- Do not open your flat door to speak to someone you are not expecting.
- Do not argue over the intercom or give personal details, such as your name, whether you are alone, or your routine.
- Do not prop open, wedge, or disable any communal door.
- Do not assume it is a delivery driver, neighbour, or maintenance worker without checking through your building’s usual process.
- Do not post real-time details publicly.

## What to do now
1. **Pause inside your flat.** Keep your front door locked. Use the intercom, door viewer, peephole, or a safe view from inside only if you can do that without opening the door.
2. **Use the intercom only to verify.** If you choose to answer, ask: “Who are you here to see?” and “What flat number?” If they cannot clearly answer, end the call and do not buzz them in.
3. **Give a simple boundary.** Say: “I cannot let in anyone I am not expecting. Please contact the person you are visiting, the concierge, or the managing agent.”
4. **Get another safe person aware.** If the buzzing continues, contact concierge or building security if you have them. If not, message or call a trusted neighbour, housemate, friend, or family member so you are not handling it alone.
5. **Make a short note without confronting anyone.** Write down the time, how many times the intercom rang, what was said, and anything you can safely observe from inside.
6. **Call 999 if it becomes urgent.** Call 999 if there is attempted forced entry, threats, violence, a crime happening now, or someone is in immediate danger. If you are in danger but cannot speak safely, call 999 and follow the operator prompts for a silent call.
7. **Use 101 or online reporting if it is suspicious but not an emergency.** If the person has left or there is no immediate danger, contact police on 101 or use the online reporting route for your local police force.
8. **Report the building issue when safe.** Tell the landlord, managing agent, concierge, or building security that an unknown person was repeatedly buzzing your flat. Ask them to check whether the communal door closes and locks properly, whether the entry panel is causing wrong-flat calls, and whether any CCTV or access-log review is available through their usual process.
9. **Reduce the noise without weakening security.** If your handset has a mute, volume, or ringer setting, you can use it temporarily. Keep another way to contact trusted people, and turn the ringer back on when the situation has passed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to install cameras, change locks, or make a formal complaint.
- You do not need to confront anyone in the hallway or at the entrance to prove what is happening.
- You do not need a full written timeline tonight; a short note of times and what happened is enough for now.
- You can wait until you feel calmer to follow up with your landlord, managing agent, building security, or the police.

## Important reassurance
Wrong-flat buzzing, delivery drivers trying several flats, and confused visitors can happen in shared buildings. It is still reasonable to refuse entry to anyone you are not expecting.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours. If the buzzing becomes repeated harassment, stalking, antisocial behaviour, or an ongoing building security problem, later decisions may need help from the police, your landlord, managing agent, local council, or another specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, housing, security, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe or believe a crime is happening now, trust your judgement and contact the emergency services.
