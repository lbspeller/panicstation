# What to do if… your building intercom keeps buzzing from someone you are not expecting (UK)

## Short answer
Do not grant access to anyone you cannot verify. Ensure your own flat door is locked and contact building security or the police on 999 if you believe an unauthorized entry is being attempted.

## Do not do these things
- Do not press the release or unlock button simply to silence the noise.
- Do not leave your flat to investigate the communal entrance or hallway alone.
- Do not engage in long conversations or provide personal details via the intercom.
- Do not prop open any internal fire doors or external security doors.
- Do not assume a caller is a delivery driver or tradesperson without official ID shown to a camera or verified via a management app.
- Do not attempt to physically block or confront someone at the door.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate environment.** Ensure your flat door is closed and the deadbolt or security chain is engaged. Move away from the door if you hear noise directly outside in the hallway.
2. **Use the intercom for verification only.** If you choose to answer, keep the interaction brief. Ask only for the caller's name, the company they represent, and the specific flat number they are intending to visit.
   - If the caller is vague or provides the wrong details, **terminate the call immediately**.
   - If the person claims to be "maintenance," ask for a job reference number before taking further action.
3. **Check for visual or audible cues of a wider issue.** Listen for whether other flats are being buzzed in sequence or if there is shouting or banging at the main entrance.
4. **Silence the handset if the noise is causing distress.** Use the "mute" or "privacy" switch if your model has one. If you must take the handset off the hook to stop the noise, remember that this may prevent legitimate visitors or emergency services from reaching you later.
5. **Contact on-site security or concierge.** If your building has SIA-licensed security or a concierge service, call them immediately to report a "suspicious caller at the main entrance."
6. **Escalate to the police based on the threat level.** 
   - **Call 999** if you see someone attempting to force the door, climb a balcony, or if they are making direct threats.
   - **Use the Silent Solution** if you are on a mobile and cannot speak safely: dial 999 and, when prompted by the automated system, **press 55** to signal a genuine emergency.
   - **Call 101** or report online if the buzzing is persistent but does not involve an immediate threat of violence or entry.
7. **Request a security log review from your landlord.** Under 2026 property standards, you can ask the managing agent to check access logs or CCTV if the system is being used for harassment or "buzzer bombing."

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to move out or install additional private security cameras.
- You do not need to identify the person's face or clothing description if you cannot see them safely via a camera or peephole.
- You do not need to respond to messages from neighbours asking "who is at the door" until the situation has stabilized.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled or anxious when a security boundary like an intercom is repeatedly challenged. Choosing not to answer or not to let someone in is a standard safety procedure and does not make you responsible for the caller's inconvenience.

## Scope note
This guidance is for first-step stabilization during an active or recent unexpected intercom event. Long-term disputes regarding building access or faulty hardware require follow-up with a managing agent or local authority.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information and does not constitute legal or security consultancy. If you are in immediate danger or a crime is occurring, contact the emergency services immediately.
