# What to do if… you feel unsafe on public transport because someone keeps moving closer or blocking your exit (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a busier area or near staff without confronting the person. Text the British Transport Police on 61016 for rail/tube issues, or call 999 if you are in immediate danger or physically blocked.

## Do not do these things
- Do not engage in an argument, ask "why" they are doing it, or attempt to de-escalate through conversation.
- Do not move into an empty carriage or isolated part of the vehicle to get away.
- Do not wait for your scheduled stop if you feel you need to leave the service earlier at a safer location.
- Do not squeeze through a narrow gap if they are physically blocking you, as this may lead to a struggle.
- Do not ignore your instincts if you feel "silly" or think the person might just be "clumsy."

## What to do now
1. **Create physical distance immediately by moving toward others.**  
   **Stand up and walk to a busier part of the carriage or bus where there are more witnesses.** If possible, put a physical barrier between yourself and the person, such as a luggage rack, seat back, or partition.

2. **Alert the transport staff as soon as it is safe to do so.**  
   - **On a bus:** move to the front and speak directly to the driver; they can stop the bus or call for assistance.  
   - **On a train / tube:** look for a conductor or use the emergency alarm / passenger talk-back button if you are being physically threatened or blocked.

3. **Report the behavior discreetly using the 61016 text service.**  
   **Text 61016 (British Transport Police) with your location, the service you are on, and a description of the person.** This service is monitored 24/7 for the rail network (including London Underground, DLR, and trams). Provide:
   - Your current station or the next stop.
   - The carriage number (found near the internal doors).
   - A brief description of what the person is doing.

4. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you must call 999 but cannot speak.**  
   **Dial 999 and, if you are prompted but unable to talk, press 55 on your mobile keypad.** This alerts the operator that it is a genuine emergency and you are in a situation where speaking is dangerous.

5. **Exit at the next well-lit, busy station or stop.**  
   **Step off the vehicle at a location where there are other people or visible staff.** Do not worry about your original destination; your priority is reaching a staffed or high-visibility environment like a ticket hall or a shop near a bus stop.

6. **Stay in a visible area once you have exited.**  
   **Head toward a Help Point, ticket office, or a group of other passengers.** If you believe you are being followed off the service, go directly to a member of staff or into a busy, open business and ask them to call the police for you.

7. **Record specific details only when you are in a safe pause.**  
   **Note the time, the route number or train line, the direction of travel, and exactly where the incident occurred.** Keep these details for a formal report once you are no longer in the immediate vicinity of the individual.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether you want to press charges or provide a full witness statement right now.
- You do not need to justify your decision to leave the service early or use an emergency alarm.
- You do not need to check if the person has left the area or "monitor" them once you are safe.
- You do not need to review any photos or videos you may have taken until you are in a secure environment.

## Important reassurance
It is a normal and protective response to feel alarmed when your personal space is invaded or your exit is restricted. You are entitled to take up space, move away, and seek help based on your perception of safety, even if the other person has not made physical contact or verbal threats.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate safety on UK public transport. It does not cover long-term legal options or the specifics of reporting historical harassment.

## Important note
This information is for immediate harm reduction and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving an active threat or physical restraint, prioritize reaching emergency services via 999.
