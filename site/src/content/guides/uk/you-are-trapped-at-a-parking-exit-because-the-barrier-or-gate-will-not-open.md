# What to do if… you are trapped at a parking exit because the barrier or gate will not open (UK)

## Short answer
Make the stopped lane safe, then use the exit intercom, help button, app support, operator number, premises reception, or security desk to ask for the barrier to be opened manually. While you wait, briefly record the fault and the time so you can explain any delay or extra charge later.

## Do not do these things
- Do not force, lift, climb over, or nudge the barrier with your car.
- Do not tailgate another vehicle through the barrier.
- Do not reverse blindly into traffic, ramps, pedestrians, or a queue.
- Do not leave your vehicle blocking an exit, fire route, ramp, or pedestrian route unless staying there would be more dangerous.
- Do not pay twice without first asking the operator how to handle a failed payment or confirmed payment.
- Do not give card details through a link or QR code unless you are sure it is the official payment route for that car park.

## What to do now
1. **Make the lane safe first.** Stop fully, apply the handbrake or parking brake, keep your seat belt on if traffic is moving around you, and put hazard lights on if you are stopped in a live exit lane.
2. **Keep people away from the barrier arm.** Ask passengers to stay in the vehicle unless it is unsafe to do so, and do not stand under or beside the barrier arm while it might move.
3. **Use the exit help route.** Press the intercom, help, assistance, or call button once, then wait for a reply. If there is a visible camera or speaker, stay near your vehicle and speak clearly.
4. **Use the site’s backup contact route.** Check the exit column, payment machine, app, ticket, wall signs, reception desk, security desk, or operator notice for a phone number or help route. On BPA or IPC member private parking sites covered by the sector code, payment-machine information should normally include contact details for payment queries, machinery queries, and reports of malfunctioning payment equipment.
5. **Say only the practical facts.** Tell the person:
   - you are at the exit barrier or gate;
   - the payment system, ticket scanner, app, ANPR exit, or pay machine is not working;
   - you need the barrier or gate opened;
   - your vehicle registration number;
   - your entry time, payment reference, or receipt details if you have them.
6. **Ask them to log the fault against your vehicle.** Ask for the fault, failed payment attempt, queue, or delay to be noted against your vehicle registration and stay. Ask for a reference number, the operator’s name, or the time of the call if they can give one.
7. **Take a short evidence record while you wait.** Photograph or briefly video the error message, the barrier not opening, any “out of order” notice, the payment machine, the operator contact sign, and your phone clock showing the time. Stop recording if it distracts you from traffic or people nearby.
8. **If you already paid, avoid double payment.** Tell the operator what you paid, how you paid, and the approximate time. Ask whether they can verify it or open the barrier while they investigate.
9. **If the lane becomes unsafe, move only if you can do so safely.** Follow any marshal, attendant, security, or operator instruction where it is safe to do so. If you need to reverse or change lane, use mirrors, cameras, passengers, or a bystander only as a safety lookout, not as someone to argue with other drivers.
10. **Escalate only for safety.** Call 999 if there is immediate danger, a crime in progress, urgent medical need, people trapped in live traffic, or emergency access blocked. Call 101 only for non-emergency police concerns such as threats, aggression, damage, or a police enquiry when no site staff can help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, request a refund, or appeal any later charge.
- You do not need to argue about grace periods, overstay rules, or legal liability while you are still stuck at the barrier.
- You do not need to organise every receipt or screenshot before leaving; just keep enough proof of the fault, payment attempt, time, and contact attempt.
- You can write a clear complaint or dispute after you and the vehicle are safely out.

## Important reassurance
Payment systems, ticket readers, apps, ANPR exits, and barriers can fail. In the moment, your job is not to solve the whole parking dispute; it is to keep the exit safe, reach a human help route, and keep a simple record of what happened.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: getting safely out of the car park and avoiding immediate mistakes. If you later receive a parking charge, refund refusal, complaint response, or demand for payment, the next steps may depend on the operator, the landowner, the site rules, and whether the car park is private, council-run, NHS, airport, railway, or covered by byelaws.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Follow emergency instructions, reasonable site staff directions where it is safe to do so, and official help routes where safety is involved.
