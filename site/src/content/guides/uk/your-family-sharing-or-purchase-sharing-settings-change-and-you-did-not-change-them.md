# What to do if… your family sharing or purchase-sharing settings change and you did not change them (UK)

## Short answer
Secure your primary email account immediately using another device to prevent password resets, then use remote "find/erase" tools to lock the hardware. Report the theft to the police and notify your bank if financial documents were stored on the device.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to track the device and confront the thief yourself; provide any location data to the police instead.
- Do not log into your accounts from shared public computers or unsecured Wi-Fi; use a trusted phone or friend's device.
- Do not wait to see if the device is returned before changing passwords; the first hour is critical for preventing data misuse.
- Do not ignore your email account security; it is the most common path for attackers to reset your other passwords.
- Do not click on links or provide codes to anyone claiming they have found your device, as this is a common "phishing" scam to unlock the hardware.

## What to do now
1. **Change your primary email password and sign out of all other sessions.** 
   - This prevents the thief from using "forgot password" links to take over your other accounts.
   - Review "forwarding rules" or "recovery emails" in your settings to ensure they have not been changed.

2. **Trigger a remote lock or erase command via your device's tracking service.**
   - For Windows, use the Microsoft "Find My Device" portal.
   - For Mac, use the Apple "Find My" app on another device.
   - If the laptop is for work, notify your IT department immediately so they can trigger an enterprise wipe.

3. **Secure your financial accounts and password manager.**
   - **Contact your bank's fraud department if the laptop contained bank statements or scans of cards.**
   - Change your password manager's master password if you had one installed on the device.
   - Review your cloud storage (e.g., OneDrive, iCloud, Google Drive) and revoke the stolen laptop's access.

4. **Report the theft to the police to obtain a Crime Reference Number (CRN).**
   - **Call 999 if the theft has just happened and the suspect is still nearby.**
   - In England, Wales, and Northern Ireland, report online via `reportfraud.police.uk` or call the Report Fraud service at 0300 123 2040.
   - In Scotland, report the theft by calling 101 or visiting a local police station.

5. **Register for Cifas Protective Registration if high-level identity documents were on the device.**
   - This service costs £30 for two years and places a flag on your credit file.
   - It requires lenders to carry out extra identity checks when someone applies for credit in your name.

6. **Check your credit reports for unauthorized searches.**
   - Use the statutory (free) reports from Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion.
   - Look for any credit applications or new accounts you do not recognise.

7. **Notify relevant government agencies if identity documents were lost.**
   - **Report a lost or stolen passport to HM Passport Office.**
   - Notify the DVLA if a digital copy or physical driving licence was in the laptop bag.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace the physical hardware in the first few hours.
- You do not need to contact your insurance provider until you have obtained a police Crime Reference Number.
- You do not need to change passwords for low-risk accounts (e.g., streaming services) until your email and banks are secured.

## Important reassurance
Losing a device filled with personal data is highly stressful, but most opportunistic thieves are interested in the resale value of the hardware rather than your specific data. By securing your email and financial accounts now, you are taking the most effective steps to prevent that data from being used against you.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and identity protection following a theft. It does not cover the technical recovery of lost files or long-term legal disputes regarding data breaches.

## Important note
This information is provided for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Jurisdictional reporting procedures in the UK changed in late 2025; ensure you are using the "Report Fraud" service rather than legacy portals.
