# What to do if… you check your ketones and they are high while you feel nauseated or unwell (UK)

## Short answer
If you feel nauseated or unwell and your ketones are high, treat it as urgent. If blood ketones are over 3.0 mmol/L, or urine ketones are over 2+, call 999 or go to A&E now; for lower raised ketones, call your diabetes care team now or call 111 for urgent advice.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “wait and see” if you feel sick, have stomach pain, are vomiting, are breathing more deeply than usual, feel drowsy, or feel confused while ketones are raised.
- Do not stop taking insulin because you are not eating.
- Do not exercise to try to bring glucose down while ketones are raised.
- Do not drive yourself to A&E. Ask someone else to take you or call 999 for an ambulance.
- Do not keep trying repeated home fixes if ketones stay raised, you are getting worse, or you cannot keep fluids down.
- Do not assume you are safe just because your glucose is not very high, especially if you take an SGLT2 inhibitor.

## What to do now
1. Get support in the room if possible. If you are alone, call or message someone and say: “My ketones are high and I feel unwell.” Keep your phone nearby.
2. Re-check and write down your blood glucose, ketones, the time of the reading, and whether you have vomited. If you can, check your temperature. Keep your meter, strips, insulin, pump supplies, and medicines with you.
3. If your blood ketones are 0.6 to 1.5 mmol/L, test again in 2 hours. If you feel unwell at this level, call your diabetes care team now or call 111 now.
4. If your blood ketones are 1.6 to 3.0 mmol/L, you are at risk of DKA. Speak to your diabetes care team now or call 111 now, even if you do not feel very unwell.
5. If your blood ketones are over 3.0 mmol/L, call 999 or go to A&E now.
6. If you used urine strips and the result is over 2+, call 999 or go to A&E now.
7. If your blood glucose is high and your insulin treatment is not working to reduce it, call your diabetes care team now or call 111 now, even if your ketones are normal.
8. If you use an insulin pump, treat pump delivery failure as a possibility. Check the infusion site, cannula, tubing, reservoir, alarms, and whether insulin could be expired, overheated, or frozen. Follow your personal sick-day plan if you have one; if you do not, use the ketone steps above to get urgent help.
9. If you take an SGLT2 inhibitor and you feel unwell with raised ketones, do not wait because your glucose is normal or only mildly raised. Call your diabetes care team, call 111, or use the emergency steps above if ketones are high.
10. If you are pregnant and have raised ketones, contact your diabetes or pregnancy emergency care service urgently, or go to A&E, even if you do not have other symptoms.
11. Sip fluids if you can keep them down. Take frequent small sips of water or an oral rehydration drink. If you are vomiting or cannot keep fluids down, treat that as a worsening sign and get urgent medical help.
12. Prepare a quick handover for 111, A&E, or ambulance staff: your readings and times, symptoms, insulin or pump details, all medicines, when you last took insulin, whether you use an SGLT2 inhibitor, and any likely trigger such as infection, missed insulin, or a pump issue.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it is definitely DKA before seeking urgent help.
- You do not need to clean up, go to work, finish plans, or push through symptoms before contacting your diabetes team, calling 111, calling 999, or going to A&E.

## Important reassurance
High ketones with nausea or feeling unwell is a situation where early action is protective. Getting urgent advice or emergency care is a sensible safety step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide covers first steps only. Later decisions about insulin doses, sick-day rules, pump settings, medication changes, or prevention should be made with your diabetes team or another appropriate healthcare professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not a diagnosis or personal medical advice. If you have diabetes and feel unwell with raised ketones, it can become serious quickly; when in doubt, seek urgent help through your diabetes care team, 111, or emergency services.
