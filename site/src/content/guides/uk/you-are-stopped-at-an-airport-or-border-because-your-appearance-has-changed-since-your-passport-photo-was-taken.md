# What to do if… you are stopped at an airport or border because your appearance has changed since your passport photo was taken (UK)

## Short answer
Stay calm, keep your explanation short, and cooperate with the airline, airport, or border identity check. Ask for a supervisor or further checks if the person at the desk is unsure.

## Do not do these things
- Do not argue, joke about security, raise your voice, or try to rush the person checking you.
- Do not alter, edit, cover, or “improve” any passport photo or identity document.
- Do not walk away while your passport or booking is being checked; ask where you should wait.
- Do not hand over extra documents or personal information before you are asked for it.
- Do not refuse a lawful request from an official; if a phone or document is requested, ask what they need to see and show the least sensitive information that answers the request.
- Do not invent a reason for the difference in appearance. Use a short factual explanation only.

## What to do now
1. Say calmly: “My appearance has changed since this passport photo. I understand you need to check my identity.”

2. Ask what check is needed next:
   - “Would you like another form of photo ID?”
   - “Can this be checked by a supervisor?”
   - “Where should I wait while you check it?”

3. Offer one strong supporting document at a time, if you have it and are asked:
   - a UK photocard driving licence
   - another valid passport you legitimately hold
   - a travel booking that matches the passport name
   - a recent official document showing your name, only if staff ask for more evidence

4. Keep the reason for the change brief. For example: “I have lost weight,” “I have changed my hair,” “I have had medical treatment,” “I have transitioned,” or “I have had surgery.” You do not need to give private medical or personal details unless an official specifically requires them.

5. If this happened at an ePassport gate, follow the staff member’s instructions and be ready to go to an officer for further checks. Changed physical appearance can be one reason an eGate sends you to an officer.

6. If boarding time is close, ask airline staff to note that an identity check is happening. Ask what options may be available if the check means you miss boarding, but do not assume the airline can rebook you for free.

7. If you are abroad and you are told you cannot travel today because of the passport likeness issue, contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate through official GOV.UK channels. Consular staff may explain options and provide support, but they cannot override airline, border, or foreign authority decisions.

8. After the immediate check is over, reduce the chance of a repeat by renewing your passport with a recent photo if your appearance has changed enough to cause repeated identity questions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain.
- You do not need to explain every detail of why your appearance changed.
- You do not need to start a passport renewal while you are still at the desk, gate, or border.
- You do not need to solve future travel plans until the current identity check is finished.

## Important reassurance
A passport photo likeness question is stressful, but it does not automatically mean you have done anything wrong. Calm cooperation, a short explanation, and one or two relevant supporting documents can make it easier for staff to complete the check.

## Scope note
These are first steps for an immediate identity challenge during travel. Later decisions, such as renewing your passport, complaining to an airline, or dealing with a missed journey, may need specialist help or advice from the relevant carrier, authority, insurer, or adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Processes can vary by airline, airport, border, route, and country.
