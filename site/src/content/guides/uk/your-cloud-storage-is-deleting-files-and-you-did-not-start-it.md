# What to do if… your cloud storage is deleting files and you did not start it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the deletion from spreading: use the official app or type the provider’s web address yourself, pause syncing on your devices, secure the cloud account and linked email account, then start recovery from Trash, Bin, Recently deleted, and version history.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave devices online and syncing while you work it out; syncing can spread deletions to other devices.
- Don’t click links in the deletion email or notification to confirm, stop, or appeal it unless you reached the service through the official app or by typing the address yourself.
- Don’t delete, rename, reorganise, or “tidy up” affected folders right now.
- Don’t reuse an old password, a similar password, or a password used on another account.
- Don’t assume it is only the cloud account; if your email account is compromised, someone may be able to reset cloud passwords again.
- Don’t pay an extortion demand or reply to threats before securing the account and getting advice.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer login route.**
   - Use the official app or type the provider’s address into your browser.
   - Avoid using a shared, public, or work-managed computer for account recovery unless there is no safer option.
   - If you must use a shared device, do not save passwords, and sign out when finished.

2. **Pause syncing before you investigate.**
   - On each computer, pause syncing in the cloud sync app or sign out of the sync app.
   - On phones and tablets, pause backup or sync for that cloud service.
   - If you cannot pause syncing quickly, disconnect the device from the internet until the account is secured.
   - Start with the devices that were most recently active or that hold the affected folders.

3. **Check whether the deletion is real and where it is coming from.**
   - Open the cloud account’s security, recent activity, devices, or events page.
   - Look for unfamiliar sign-ins, unknown devices, mass deletions, new shared-folder collaborators, new connected apps, or admin or policy changes.
   - If it is a work or school account, contact your IT or security team before changing settings that they manage.

4. **Secure the cloud account immediately.**
   - Change the password to a new, unique password.
   - Sign out of all other devices or sessions if the service offers that option.
   - Turn on 2-step verification.
   - Remove connected apps, browser extensions, devices, or third-party access you do not recognise.
   - Save any new recovery codes somewhere safe and separate from the affected cloud account.

5. **Secure the email account linked to the cloud service.**
   - Change the email password to a new, unique password.
   - Turn on 2-step verification for the email account.
   - Check email forwarding rules, filters, and recovery options for anything unfamiliar.
   - Remove unknown phone numbers, backup email addresses, app passwords, or trusted devices.

6. **Start file recovery inside the cloud service.**
   - Open Trash, Bin, Recently deleted, or the provider’s recovery area.
   - Restore the most important folders first.
   - Use version history or previous versions for files that were changed, encrypted, or overwritten rather than simply deleted.
   - Provider recovery windows can be limited, so start with the most important data before doing any clean-up.
   - If this is a business, school, or managed account, ask the admin to check audit logs, retention settings, and any admin-level recycle bin.

7. **Make a quick record while details are fresh.**
   - Screenshot the deletion notice, recent activity, device list, recovery settings, and any sign-in or password-reset emails.
   - Write down the approximate time it started, which devices were online, and which folders or photo libraries were affected.
   - Keep the record separate from the affected cloud account.

8. **Use the provider’s official support route if you see account takeover or mass deletion.**
   - Use the provider’s help centre and search for account compromised, recover account, deleted files, bulk restore, or version history.
   - Ask whether they can stop automated deletion, restore a folder in bulk, or recover files from a provider-side retention area.
   - If you receive threats, ransom demands, or suspicious payment instructions, keep copies and do not reply through links in the message.

9. **Report fraud or cyber crime when appropriate.**
   - If money was lost, extortion was involved, or you believe a cyber crime occurred in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report it to Report Fraud.
   - In Scotland, report cybercrime to Police Scotland online, by phone, or in person; use 101 for non-emergency phone reporting.
   - If there is immediate danger, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify exactly how it happened before pausing sync and securing the account.
- You do not need to reorganise your cloud storage, rename folders, or tidy duplicates today.
- You do not need to decide whether to change provider now.
- You can leave deeper device checks, malware scans, and long-term backup planning until the account is secured and recovery is underway.
- You can decide later whether to tell contacts, clients, or colleagues, unless the cloud account is sending messages or sharing files without your consent.

## Important reassurance
A deletion notice can feel personal, but mass deletion is often caused by sync problems, compromised accounts, malicious connected apps, or automated attacks. Acting in the order of pause syncing, secure access, and recover files gives you a better chance of limiting the loss.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stop ongoing file loss, protect account access, and begin recovery. Later decisions, especially for work accounts, legal issues, malware, extortion, or major data loss, may need help from the cloud provider, an IT professional, your organisation’s security team, or the police.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical, or other professional advice. Use the cloud provider’s official recovery process, and get specialist help if the account is managed by an organisation, the loss is serious, or you suspect malware or extortion.
