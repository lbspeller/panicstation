# What to do if… an agent, photographer, or scout asks for explicit photos as a condition of an opportunity (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the conversation, do not send any explicit images, and save what has already been said. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send explicit images to keep the opportunity, prove your identity, or make pressure stop.
- Do not pay money, buy gift cards, send bank details, or send more images if they threaten you.
- Do not agree to meet them alone after this request.
- Do not move to disappearing messages if you can avoid it.
- Do not delete the chat before saving the key messages, profile details, usernames, links, emails, and payment demands.
- Do not blame yourself or try to argue them into behaving properly.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause.**  
   Move away from any planned meeting, stop replying, and stay near another person if you feel shaken or pressured. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

2. **Send one clear boundary only if it feels safe.**  
   You can write: “No. Do not contact me again.” Then stop responding. Blocking or muting is okay after you have saved what you can.

3. **Save the important details without sharing them around.**  
   Screenshot the messages, the profile, the username or handle, the date and time, the platform, email addresses, phone numbers, links, payment requests, and any “contract” or “casting” information. If disappearing messages are on, turn them off if you can safely do that.

4. **Report the account on the platform where they contacted you.**  
   Use the platform’s report option for sexual harassment, sexual solicitation, blackmail, impersonation, or scam behaviour, depending on what happened.

5. **If you are under 18, or the person thinks you are under 18, involve a safer adult or specialist service now.**  
   If you are in immediate danger, call 999. Otherwise, you can contact police on 101 and consider making a report to the NCA’s CEOP Safety Centre about online sexual abuse or grooming concerns. If there is no trusted adult nearby, Childline can support you.

6. **If there are threats, blackmail, or demands for money, treat it as sextortion.**  
   Contact police on 101, or 999 if you feel at immediate risk. If the demand is financial and you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, you can also report it through Report Fraud. If you are in Scotland, use Police Scotland’s reporting routes.

7. **If intimate images exist or you are worried they may be shared, use the right removal-support route.**  
   If you are currently over 18, were 18 or older when the image was taken, and still have the image or video, StopNCII may help participating platforms detect and stop sharing. If you are under 18, Report Remove can help with reporting sexual images or videos of yourself online.

8. **Check any real organisation through public contact details only.**  
   If they claim to represent an agency, studio, brand, school, publication, or casting company, use the organisation’s official website to find a phone number or email. Do not use contact details the “scout” gave you.

9. **Tell one safe person what is happening.**  
   Ask a trusted friend, relative, safeguarding lead, tutor, manager, or support worker to sit with you while you save messages and report. The aim is steadiness and safety, not making every decision now.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether the opportunity was real.
- You do not need to confront them, warn everyone publicly, or write a perfect report.
- You do not need to investigate them yourself.
- You do not need to decide today what to do long term about modelling, photography, casting, work, school, or social media.

## Important reassurance
A real opportunity should not depend on you sending explicit images privately. People who make sexual demands under pressure are using shock, hope, and embarrassment to isolate you; stopping contact and getting support is a reasonable first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, meant to stabilise the situation, reduce risk, and prevent irreversible mistakes. If threats continue, if any images are involved, or if a child or young person may be affected, specialist support, platform support, or police guidance may be needed.

## Important note
This guide provides general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, safeguarding, or professional advice.
