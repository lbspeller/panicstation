# What to do if… you get an alert that a garage door, gate, or secure entry opened unexpectedly and you did not do it (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the alert as a potential intruder until proven otherwise; move to a secure location immediately and call 999 if you believe someone is currently on the premises.

## Do not do these things
- Don't enter the property or the specific area (garage / shed / gate) to investigate if you are currently outside.
- Don't move through the house to "clear rooms" or search for an intruder if you are already inside.
- Don't shout or announce your presence, as this may escalate a confrontation.
- Don't repeatedly trigger the door or gate from your app to "test" the mechanism while the area is unverified.
- Don't touch surfaces, door handles, or moved items until the police have confirmed it is safe and no forensic evidence is needed.
- Don't post about the incident on social media while you are still in a vulnerable position.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer position immediately before checking your devices.** 
   - If you are inside the home, go to a room that locks (such as a bedroom or bathroom) and stay quiet. 
   - If you are outside or arriving home, stay in your locked vehicle or move to a public, well-lit area at a safe distance from the property.
2. **Review available camera or doorbell feeds from your safe position.** 
   - Look for recent motion events, unidentified vehicles, or people on the property. 
   - Check if the alert was triggered by a known family member or a scheduled service.
3. **Call 999 if you see an intruder or have a strong reason to believe one is present.** 
   - State clearly that you have an "unauthorised entry in progress" based on a security alert. 
   - If you cannot speak because you are hiding, use the "Silent Solution": call 999 and, when prompted by the automated message, press **55** on your mobile to be transferred to the police.
4. **Contact your professional monitoring service if your system is managed.** 
   - Ask the operator to verify which zones were triggered and if they have dispatched a guard or notified the police. 
   - Provide them with your current safe location so they can update emergency responders.
5. **Turn on external or internal smart lighting remotely if it is safe to do so.** 
   - Use your home automation app to illuminate the area, which may deter an intruder without requiring you to move. 
   - Do not do this if you are hiding and the light would reveal your specific location.
6. **Secure other entry points through your smart home app.** 
   - Check the status of all other smart locks or sensors to ensure the rest of the property remains sealed. 
   - If the alert came from a garage or gate, ensure the door connecting the garage to the house is locked.
7. **Record specific details for the police without re-entering the area.** 
   - Note the exact time of the alert and any sounds you heard, such as engines, footsteps, or voices. 
   - Download and save any relevant video clips to your phone immediately so they are not overwritten by newer footage.
8. **Wait for the police to arrive and confirm the area is secure before leaving your safe spot.** 
   - Stay on the phone with the emergency operator if instructed. 
   - Keep your hands visible and follow all officer instructions when they arrive on the scene.

## What can wait
- You don't need to determine the cause of the alert (e.g., sensor malfunction, wind, or software glitch) right now.
- You don't need to inventory missing items or assess property damage until the police have cleared the site.
- You don't need to change passwords, reset the hub, or contact the manufacturer for a repair until you are out of the immediate crisis.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden spike in heart rate or a sense of dread when receiving a security alert. This is a survival response designed to make you alert and cautious. Taking these steps to stay at a distance or hide is a professional safety protocol, not a sign of unnecessary panic.

## Scope note
This guide focuses on immediate physical safety and the first minutes of receiving an unauthorised entry notification. Long-term security upgrades, forensic recovery, and insurance claims should be addressed only after the property is confirmed secure.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving a potential intruder or threat to life in the UK, always prioritize calling 999.
