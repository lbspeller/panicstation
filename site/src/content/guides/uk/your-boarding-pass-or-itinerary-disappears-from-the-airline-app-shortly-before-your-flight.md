# What to do if… your boarding pass or itinerary disappears from the airline app shortly before your flight (UK)

## Short answer
Do not rely on the app alone. Find your booking through your confirmation email or the airline website, then get a usable boarding pass saved offline, added to a wallet, or issued by airline staff if available. At UK airport security, you should be ready to present, and often scan, your boarding pass.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume you can sort it at the gate; you usually need a boarding pass before you can get through security.
- Do not cancel or rebook the flight just because the airline app looks empty.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to a stranger offering to fix it.
- Do not sign out of the app unless you have your booking reference and can sign back in.
- Do not let your phone battery run down while repeatedly refreshing the app.
- Do not keep reinstalling the app if departure is close and there is an airline desk, kiosk, or staff member you can go to.

## What to do now
1. **Stop moving for one minute and secure your phone.**  
   Plug into a charger or power bank. Switch between airport Wi-Fi and mobile data. Turn the screen brightness up so any barcode or QR code can scan.

2. **Find your booking outside the app.**  
   Search your email and texts for the airline or travel agent confirmation. Write down or screenshot the booking reference, passenger name as booked, flight number, date, and departure airport.

3. **Use the airline website as the first fallback.**  
   Open the airline website in a browser and use “manage booking”, “my booking”, or “check in”. Try the booking reference and surname. If a boarding pass is available, download it as a PDF or open it in the browser.

4. **Save a backup that does not depend on the app.**  
   Save the PDF to your phone files, add the pass to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet if offered, and keep the confirmation email open. If screenshots are allowed and the barcode stays clear, take a screenshot as an extra backup.

5. **If you cannot get a boarding pass online, go to the airline kiosk, bag drop, check-in desk, or customer service desk now.**  
   Take your passport or accepted ID and your booking reference. Say: “My boarding pass disappeared from the app. I need a usable boarding pass for security and boarding.” Ask whether they can issue or reissue one and whether any fee applies before accepting a paid service.

6. **If time is tight, go straight to the shortest route to airline staff.**  
   Look for a queue marshal, self-service kiosk helper, bag drop assistant, or check-in desk for your airline. Show your booking reference and say you need help before security because the boarding pass is no longer visible.

7. **If the carrier directs you to use a digital pass or app, still secure a fallback route.**  
   Keep your phone charging, use airport Wi-Fi if mobile data is failing, and keep the airline website booking page open. If your phone is lost, dead, or unusable, ask airline staff what they can issue or do at that airport for an already checked-in passenger.

8. **For international flights, treat a missing pass as a possible document-check issue.**  
   Some routes or passengers may need passport, visa, ETA, or other travel-document checks before a boarding pass fully displays. If the website or app will not issue the pass, go to airline staff with your original travel document rather than repeatedly trying the same failed screen.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why the app failed right now.
- You do not need to change seats, buy extras, add bags, or manage upgrades unless airline staff say it is necessary to complete check-in.
- You do not need to keep troubleshooting the app once you have moved to a kiosk or desk plan.
- You do not need to complain to the airline until you have a boarding pass or clear staff instructions.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but it is a common airport problem: apps log out, bookings fail to sync, signal drops, batteries die, and document checks can block a pass from displaying. The immediate goal is not to fix the app; it is to get a boarding pass or clear airline instruction before the security pinch point.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you stabilise the moment and keep moving through the airport. Later questions about app access, fees, missed flights, refunds, or complaints may need airline, travel insurance, card provider, or specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, or professional advice. Airline and airport procedures vary by carrier, terminal, route, check-in status, and destination. If departure is soon, follow the instructions of your airline and airport staff.
