# What to do if… your onboarding portal shows you as not hired after you accepted a job offer (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all restricted work immediately and request a written explanation of which specific system (Right to Work or National Security Vetting) has triggered the review. Use the official GOV.UK "Prove your right to work" service or contact your organisation’s Security Controller to initiate a formal re-verification.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign or agree to any immediate contract variations while the review is ongoing.
- Do not perform any restricted work tasks or access sensitive systems if told your clearance is suspended.
- Do not transfer work files, emails, or data to personal accounts or devices for any reason.
- Do not attempt to guess the cause or speculate on the trigger with colleagues or managers.
- Do not provide sensitive identity documents to anyone other than official HR, Security, or Legal channels.
- Do not ignore deadlines for responding to requests for information from UKSV or your employer.

## What to do now
1. **Request immediate written confirmation of the review's scope and legal basis.** Ask your HR or Security department to specify if the review concerns your **Right to Work** (immigration status) or your **National Security Vetting** (security clearance), the exact system label being used (e.g., "manual re-check," "ECS check," "UKSV review"), and the specific deadline for your response.

2. **Generate a new Right to Work share code via the official GOV.UK service.** Access your digital eVisa or UKVI account to create a 9-character code.
   - Ensure the generated code begins with the letter **"W"** (designating it for work, not rent).
   - Provide the code and your date of birth to HR immediately.
   - Note that since January 2026, physical BRP cards are no longer valid proof for these checks.

3. **Request an Employer Checking Service (ECS) enquiry if your digital status is unavailable.** If you have a pending application or your eVisa cannot be accessed, instruct HR to use the Home Office ECS.
   - A **Positive Verification Notice (PVN)** from the ECS provides a statutory excuse for the employer for **6 months** from the date on the notice.
   - Retain a copy of the PVN for your own records to confirm your legal right to remain in the role.

4. **Contact your organisation’s Security Controller or Vetting Team for clearance issues.** Locate the specific Security Officer responsible for your site or department rather than relying on a line manager.
   - Ask for a copy of your **Aftercare** or **Change of Personal Circumstances (CPC)** requirements.
   - Verify if your site pass or system access is restricted and follow all "need-to-know" handling rules.
   - If you need to contact the **UKSV Helpdesk** directly, call **0330 058 8290** (Lines: Mon–Thu 8am–5pm, Fri 8am–1pm).

5. **Clarify your employment and pay status for the immediate review period.** Ask HR to confirm in writing whether you are being suspended on full pay, reassigned to non-sensitive duties, or placed on unpaid leave.
   - Request the specific internal policy document that governs "suspension pending vetting or right-to-work verification."

6. **Consult a regulated professional for advice on status complications.** Contact an **OISC-regulated immigration adviser** or a solicitor if the Right to Work check fails.
   - For security clearance issues, contact your trade union or professional association representative for support during the review process.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to seek new employment or leave the country today.
- You do not need to provide a comprehensive narrative of your personal history unless specifically asked via a formal Security Questionnaire or UKSV interview.
- You do not need to discuss the review with your wider team or external clients.

## Important reassurance
A "review" flag is often a procedural requirement triggered by an expiring notice, an administrative data mismatch between government databases, or a routine audit. It is a standard compliance step and does not necessarily indicate that your status or clearance will be revoked.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and administrative verification steps for UK-based employees only. It does not provide legal strategies for immigration appeals or national security vetting challenges.

## Important note
Right to work and security vetting procedures are governed by strict UK legislation, including the Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006 and the Cabinet Office’s National Security Vetting policy. Verification requirements for eVisas became mandatory for most non-British/Irish nationals in early 2026. Always follow the specific instructions provided by your employer's Security Controller or the Home Office.
