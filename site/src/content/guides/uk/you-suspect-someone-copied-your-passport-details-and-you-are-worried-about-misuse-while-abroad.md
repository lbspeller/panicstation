# What to do if… you suspect someone copied your passport details and you are worried about misuse while abroad (UK)

## Short answer
Keep your physical passport safe, stop any further copying, secure the accounts most likely to be targeted, and make a clear record of what happened.

## Do not do these things
- Do not post or forward images of your passport, even cropped images, to anyone who does not strictly need them.
- Do not email or message your passport scan again to “check” or “compare” unless it is required and you trust the route and recipient.
- Do not give passwords, one-time codes, card details, or banking security answers to anyone who contacts you unexpectedly.
- Do not report your passport as lost or stolen unless it is actually missing or may have been taken.
- Do not confront a person or business if that could make you less safe; leave the situation and keep your own record instead.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your passport and stop further copying.** Put it somewhere only you control. If a business is holding it “for later”, ask for it back now.
2. **Write a simple incident note.** Record the date, time, place, business or person involved, what they could have copied, and whether the physical passport stayed with you. Keep receipts, messages, booking details, and screenshots together.
3. **Secure your main email and travel accounts.** Change weak or reused passwords, turn on two-step verification where available, and check for unfamiliar login alerts or password-reset emails.
4. **Contact your bank using a trusted route.** Use the app, the number on your card, or the bank’s official website. Turn on payment and login alerts, and ask whether they can add extra verification for phone or in-app support.
5. **Watch for specific signs of misuse.** Check for unfamiliar bank activity, unexpected mobile phone or credit applications, debt letters, booking changes, new account messages, or login alerts. If any appear, contact the affected provider first to secure the account and dispute the activity.
6. **Protect your UK credit identity if the risk feels higher than routine copying.** Consider credit file alerts or monitoring. If your identity is at heightened risk, Cifas Protective Registration may add extra checks when services are applied for in your name, but genuine applications can take longer.
7. **Report actual fraud or attempted fraud promptly.** Use Report Fraud for fraud or cybercrime and keep the reference. If you live in Scotland, use the route indicated by Report Fraud, usually 101.
8. **Use the lost or stolen passport route only if the physical passport is missing or may have been taken.** Report it as lost or stolen through GOV.UK. If you are abroad and need to travel without a valid UK passport, check the emergency travel document process before booking travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to replace your passport if it is still safely in your possession and there is no sign of misuse.
- You do not need to contact every agency at once if nothing suspicious has happened.
- You do not need to solve every future risk today; focus on the document, your bank, your main email, and a clear record.

## Important reassurance
In some countries, hotels, transport providers, or tour operators may ask to see or copy passport details for check-in or local processes. That does not mean misuse will happen, and the steps above can reduce what can happen next and help you respond quickly if something changes.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation while you are away. Later decisions, such as replacing a passport, disputing fraud, or handling a data-protection complaint, may need help from the relevant provider, official service, or specialist adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Processes can vary by the country you are in and by the provider involved. If your passport is missing, or you feel unsafe, prioritise immediate safety and official travel-document support.
