# What to do if… you suspect someone copied your passport details and you are worried about misuse while abroad (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the situation as a risk of identity fraud by securing the physical document, recording the details of the incident, and notifying your bank and UK credit services.

## Do not do these things
- Do not post or forward images of your passport to unverified contacts.
- Do not report your passport as lost or stolen if you still have it, as this cancels the document and prevents travel.
- Do not ignore unexpected bank notifications or verification codes.
- Do not email scans of your passport to yourself or others as a way of "checking" for errors.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the physical passport immediately and prevent further access.** Ensure the document is in a safe or a location only you control.
   - Ask for the document back if a business is holding it without a clear legal requirement.
2. **Make a precise note of the incident while details are fresh.** Record the date, time, location, the name of the individual or business involved, and exactly what information they could have copied.
   - Keep any receipts or messages related to the interaction.
3. **Enhance security on your UK bank accounts via your banking app.** Enable push notifications for all transactions and logins. 
   - Use the app's secure chat or call function to ask if an additional passphrase can be added to your profile.
4. **Notify your airline or travel provider if the copied details were part of a booking.** Confirm that your booking remains secure and that no unauthorised changes have been made.
5. **Apply for Cifas Protective Registration if you believe misuse is likely.** This service places a flag on your UK credit file for two years (cost is £30) which requires lenders to perform extra verification before opening accounts in your name.
6. **Check your UK credit report for any unauthorised applications.** Use a statutory credit report service or a reputable monitoring app to see if new credit searches appear.
7. **Report suspected fraud to the relevant UK authorities if you see signs of misuse.** 
   - Report to **Report Fraud** (the UK's national reporting centre, formerly Action Fraud) via their website or on 0300 123 2040.
   - If you are in Scotland, report via the **Police Scotland 101** service.
8. **Consult the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) "Getting help" page for your specific country.** This provides local contact details for consular assistance if the situation escalates.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace your passport immediately if it is still in your possession and no fraud has occurred.
- You do not need to contact the UK Passport Office unless the physical document is lost, stolen, or damaged.
- You do not need to file a local police report unless a crime (like theft or extortion) has been committed in that jurisdiction.

## Important reassurance
It is a standard requirement in many countries for hotels and car rental agencies to take copies of passports for local law enforcement or tax purposes. A copy of your details does not automatically mean your identity will be stolen, and taking these preventative steps significantly reduces the risk of successful misuse.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK citizens abroad. It does not cover the process for replacing a lost or stolen passport or the long-term resolution of identity theft.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Jurisdictional requirements for document handling vary by country. If you are in immediate danger or your document is physically stolen, contact local emergency services and the nearest UK embassy or consulate.
