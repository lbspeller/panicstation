# What to do if… you see a large cash withdrawal on your account but you still have your card (UK)

## Short answer
Freeze your card immediately through your banking app and contact your bank’s fraud department via a trusted number (such as 159 or the number on the back of your card) to report an unauthorised transaction.

## Do not do these things
- Don't call back any number provided in a text or email alert; use official channels only.
- Don't keep using the card for any transactions, including contactless payments, until the bank confirms it is secure.
- Don't share specific details of the transaction, such as the ATM location or exact timestamp, on social media or public forums.
- Don't wait for "pending" transactions to clear before reporting them; banks can act on pending items.
- Don't attempt to visit the ATM yourself to look for "skimming" devices or evidence.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze the card immediately.** Use your mobile banking app’s "freeze" or "block" function to stop all further activity while you prepare to call.
2. **Contact your bank via a secure route.** Dial **159** from your phone to be connected to your bank’s fraud team, or use the number printed on the back of your physical card.
   - Banks participating in 159 include Barclays, Co-operative Bank, HSBC, Lloyds, Nationwide, NatWest, Santander, Starling, and TSB, among others.
3. **State clearly that you are reporting an "unauthorised transaction."** Tell the operator: "I have a cash withdrawal I did not make, and I am still in physical possession of my card."
4. **Request specific actions from the bank staff.**
   - **Formally dispute the transaction** and ask for a fraud investigation to be opened.
   - **Cancel the current card permanently** and request a replacement with a new PAN (card number) and PIN.
   - **Ask for a temporary refund** (under Payment Services Regulations 2017, banks must generally refund unauthorised transactions by the end of the next business day).
5. **Note the case details for your records.** Write down the time of the call, the name of the person you spoke to, and the fraud case reference number.
6. **Check for other unauthorised changes.** Ask the bank to verify if your contact details (phone, email, address) or login credentials have been changed recently.
7. **Report the crime to the national authorities.**
   - **In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland:** Report to **Report Fraud** (the 2026 successor to Action Fraud) online at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **In Scotland:** Report the incident to **Police Scotland** by calling **101**.
8. **Secure your digital access.** Change your online banking password and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) if it is not already active.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact method of theft (such as skimming or a cloned card) right now; the bank's investigation will handle this.
- You do not need to decide whether to switch banks or close the entire account today.
- You do not need to contact the Financial Ombudsman Service until the bank has provided a "final response" letter (usually within 15 days for payment disputes).

## Important reassurance
Discovering missing money while your card is safe in your wallet is a confusing and alarming experience. It is a common sign of "card cloning" or "skimming." Taking the step to freeze the account is the most effective way to prevent further loss. Once the bank is notified, your legal liability for any subsequent fraudulent transactions is generally zero.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for UK bank account holders experiencing unauthorised cash withdrawals. It does not cover long-term credit repair or legal action against third parties.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Under the Payment Services Regulations 2017, you are typically not liable for unauthorised transactions unless the bank can prove gross negligence. If you feel you are being watched or are in physical danger near an ATM, move to a safe location before calling.
