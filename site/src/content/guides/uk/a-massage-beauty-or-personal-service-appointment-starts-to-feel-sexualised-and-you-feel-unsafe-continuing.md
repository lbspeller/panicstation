# What to do if… a massage, beauty, or personal service appointment starts to feel sexualised and you feel unsafe continuing (UK)

## Short answer
End the treatment immediately and leave the room to reach a safer area, such as a public reception or outside the building. If you are prevented from leaving or feel in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not stay to avoid being impolite or causing a scene.
- Do not attempt to explain your feelings or negotiate boundaries once you feel unsafe.
- Do not allow the practitioner to "start over" or promise to change their behavior.
- Do not accept discounts, free treatments, or requests to keep the incident quiet.
- Do not blame yourself for freezing, laughing, or "going along" with the situation; these are common biological safety responses.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the service immediately with a short, firm statement.**  
   **Say "Stop," "I need to end this now," or "I am not comfortable."** 
   - Move away from the practitioner and toward the door.
   - Do not wait for their permission to stop.

2. **Move to a safer location before dressing fully if necessary.**  
   **Prioritise your physical safety over being fully dressed or "properly" covered.**
   - Grab a robe or towel and move to a public area like a hallway or reception.
   - If the practitioner tries to block you, call out for help loudly.

3. **Incorporate a third party into your immediate surroundings.**  
   **Go to the reception desk or find another member of staff and state that you need to leave immediately.**
   - Ask for an escort to your car or a taxi.
   - If you are at a private home, leave the building and call a trusted person to stay on the line with you.

4. **Use the emergency services if you cannot leave safely.**  
   **Call 999 if you are in danger, being followed, or held against your will.**
   - If calling from a mobile and you cannot speak, listen to the operator and **press 55** when prompted (The Silent Solution).
   - Note that pressing 55 does not automatically track your location; stay on the line if possible.

5. **Contact a specialist support service for guidance.**  
   **Call the Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 Support Line on 0808 500 2222.**
   - This service is for anyone aged 16+ who has experienced sexual violence or inappropriate behavior.
   - If you are under 16, call Childline on 0800 1111.

6. **Preserve evidence without feeling pressured to report.**  
   **Keep any booking confirmations, texts, or receipts from the appointment.**
   - If physical contact occurred, try to avoid washing the clothing you were wearing until you have spoken to a specialist service or a SARC (Sexual Assault Referral Centre).
   - Write down the name of the practitioner and exactly what was said or done while it is fresh in your mind.

7. **Locate your nearest SARC for medical care or forensic options.**  
   **You can access a SARC without reporting to the police.**
   - They provide medical check-ups, emergency contraception, and STI testing.
   - Search "NHS SARC" to find your local centre.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to file a formal police report today.
- You do not need to contact the business owner or leave a review right now.
- You do not need to define exactly what happened (e.g., "harassment" vs "assault") before seeking support.
- You do not need to worry about the "cost" of the unfinished appointment.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel confused, startled, or "frozen" when a professional boundary is crossed. Your safety is the only priority, and you have the absolute right to stop any personal service at any time for any reason. Ending an appointment because you feel unsafe is a proactive step toward your own protection.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction guidance only. It does not provide legal advice or long-term therapeutic strategies.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance in the UK. It does not replace professional legal or medical advice. In any immediate emergency, always call 999.
