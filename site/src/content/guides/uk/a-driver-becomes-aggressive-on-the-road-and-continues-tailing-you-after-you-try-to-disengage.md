# What to do if… a driver becomes aggressive on the road and continues tailing you after you try to disengage (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive home or stop in isolated areas; instead, drive to a busy, well-lit public place such as a petrol station or supermarket car park and call 999. Stay inside your locked vehicle and follow the instructions provided by the emergency operator.

## Do not do these things
- Don't drive to your home, workplace, or any location that reveals your daily routine.
- Don't stop at the side of the road or in a quiet area to confront or talk to the other driver.
- Don't use "brake-checking" (tapping brakes to annoy the follower) or speed excessively to escape.
- Don't make eye contact, return gestures, or engage in shouting matches.
- Don't get out of your vehicle if the other driver stops near you or approaches your door.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure your vehicle is secure immediately.** Lock all doors, close windows, and ensure your seatbelt is fastened while maintaining focus on the road ahead.
2. **Signal clearly and drive predictably.** Use your indicators early and maintain a steady speed to avoid giving the other driver a reason for further escalation or an excuse for a collision.
3. **Change your route to a high-traffic area.** Head toward **motorway services**, a **24-hour petrol station**, or a **large supermarket car park** where there are people and active CCTV coverage.
   - Avoid narrow side streets or dead ends where you could be blocked in.
   - Stay on main roads with multiple lanes and traffic lights where other witnesses are present.
4. **Call 999 if you feel under threat.** 
   - **Use your phone even if driving.** UK law allows a handheld phone to be used to call 999 or 112 in a genuine emergency if it is unsafe or impractical to stop.
   - Tell the operator you are being followed by an aggressive driver and provide your current location and direction of travel.
5. **Identify the vehicle if safe to do so.** Note the **make, model, colour**, and **registration number** of the car following you, but only if you can do so without taking your eyes off the road for more than a second.
6. **Stay in the car if you must stop.** If you reach a public place and the other driver stops nearby, keep the engine running and the doors locked. 
   - Leave enough space between you and the car in front so you can drive away if the person approaches your vehicle.
   - Sound your horn if you need to attract immediate public attention.
7. **Wait for police arrival or further instruction.** Stay on the line with the 999 operator until they confirm a patrol is nearby or that it is safe for you to move to a different location.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to press charges or give a formal statement right now.
- You do not need to check your car for minor damage or "marks" until you are in a secure, police-approved location.
- You do not need to download or review dashcam footage while you are still in the vehicle or shaken.
- You do not need to notify your insurance company or employer about the incident immediately.

## Important reassurance
Feeling a sense of panic, physical shaking, or hyper-vigilance is a natural response to being followed and threatened. These reactions are your body’s way of keeping you alert. By moving toward public spaces and involving the authorities, you are taking the most effective steps to neutralise the situation and ensure your safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active road rage or following incident in the UK. It does not cover long-term legal reporting or insurance claim procedures.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional security advice. In any situation involving immediate threat or danger, always dial 999.
