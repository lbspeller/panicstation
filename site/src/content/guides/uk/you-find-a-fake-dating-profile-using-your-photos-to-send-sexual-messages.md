# What to do if… you find a fake dating profile using your photos to send sexual messages (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, do not engage with the account, and focus on getting the profile taken down: report it in the app or site and save only enough evidence to show what is happening.

## Do not do these things
- Do not message, threaten, or bargain with the person running the account.
- Do not send money, gift cards, cryptocurrency, login codes, ID documents, or more photos to make it stop.
- Do not post public call-outs with the profile link or screenshots that reveal your details.
- Do not hand over account access to anyone claiming they can remove it for you.
- Do not keep re-checking the profile over and over.
- Do not assume it is your fault or that you have to deal with it alone.

## What to do now
1. **Get to the safest pause you can.** If you are in immediate danger, someone is coming to your location, or you are being threatened with immediate harm, call **999**.
2. **Save minimal evidence, then stop looking.** If it is safe to do so, take a few screenshots showing:
   - the profile name or handle and the photos used
   - the sexual messages, bio, or profile text
   - the app or site name, and any profile URL or report reference  
   Keep the screenshots private. Do not forward, repost, or keep collecting sexual material. If anything appears to involve someone under 18, stop collecting images and move to reporting.
3. **Report the profile inside the dating app or site.** Use the platform’s report route for **impersonation**, **fake profile**, **harassment**, or **pretending to be someone else**. Include the screenshots or profile link only where the platform asks for them.
4. **Ask one trusted person to help with the platform report if you are overwhelmed.** They can help you write a short report, keep track of the profile link, or report the account from their own account if they can see it.
5. **Use a reporting guide if the platform response is slow or confusing.** Report Harmful Content has guidance on online impersonation and links for reporting to some platforms, including some dating platforms.
6. **If actual or threatened intimate images of you are involved, contact a specialist service.** If you are 18 or over, the Revenge Porn Helpline can give confidential UK support and may be able to help with removal routes. StopNCII.org may help reduce re-uploads on participating platforms if you have access to the intimate image or video.
7. **If any content may involve someone under 18, treat it as a child safety issue.** Do not forward it to anyone. Report online child sexual abuse imagery to the Internet Watch Foundation. If a child is in immediate danger, call **999**; otherwise report to police via **101** or the relevant police online reporting route.
8. **Reduce immediate exposure.**
   - Limit who can message you on social media.
   - Hide public phone numbers, email addresses, workplace details, or location tags if they are visible.
   - Ask trusted friends to ignore and report the fake profile if they see it.
9. **If there are threats, blackmail, stalking, repeated unwanted contact, or strangers turning up, contact police.** Use **999** for immediate danger or a crime in progress. Use **101** or an online police report if it is not an emergency.
10. **If the profile is being used to scam people for money, use the fraud route as well.** In England, Wales, and Northern Ireland, report cyber crime or fraud through **Report Fraud**. In Scotland, report to **Police Scotland** using **101** or the Police Scotland online route.
11. **If you feel emotionally unsafe or unable to settle, contact immediate support.** Samaritans can be called free on **116 123**, day or night.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify who made the account right now.
- You do not need to write a perfect statement or collect lots of proof.
- You do not need to keep checking whether the profile is still live.
- You do not need to decide today whether to make a formal complaint, take legal action, tell work, or tell a wider group of people.

## Important reassurance
Finding a sexualised fake profile using your photos can feel exposing and frightening. The first priority is not proving everything or explaining yourself; it is getting the account reported, reducing exposure, and bringing in support where needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to reduce harm and buy time. Later decisions about police reports, legal options, workplace impact, or ongoing harassment may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
