# What to do if… your electronic travel authorisation is still pending and your departure is within 24 hours (UK)

## Short answer
Treat “pending” as not approved yet. For a UK ETA, you must wait until you receive confirmation that you have an ETA before travelling to the UK, so check official messages now and make a backup plan before you become a no-show.

## Do not do these things
- Do not rely on a screenshot, payment receipt, application reference, or “submitted” email as proof that you can travel.
- Do not submit another UK ETA application for the same person unless official UKVI guidance specifically tells you to.
- Do not use third-party sites or apps promising a faster UK ETA decision.
- Do not change to a different passport at check-in if the ETA application was made with another passport.
- Do not wait until the airport to find out your airline’s boarding and rebooking cut-offs.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm it is a UK ETA and check the passport used.**  
   Match the passport number, issuing country, expiry date, and name format on the application to the passport you will use for travel.

2. **Check the decision route, not just the payment route.**  
   Look for an email from UK Visas and Immigration and check your spam or junk folder. If someone else applied for you, ask them to check their email and send you the 16-digit ETA reference number if they have it.

3. **Do not rely on the GOV.UK ETA checker if you are still waiting.**  
   The online “Check your ETA” service is for checking whether you already have an ETA and when it expires. It is not for people who recently applied and have not received one yet.

4. **Look for any action-needed message.**  
   Check whether the application or email asks you to fix a photo, provide more information, confirm identity, or use a different route. Respond only through the official UK ETA app, GOV.UK service, or UKVI contact route.

5. **Contact your airline now.**  
   Ask: “If my UK ETA is not confirmed before check-in, will you refuse boarding, and what is the latest time I can change or rebook without being marked as a no-show?” Write down the answer, time, and person or team you spoke to.

6. **Use the official UKVI ETA help route if you need help.**  
   Use the GOV.UK UKVI ETA contact page or webchat. Provide only what the official route asks for, including your 16-digit ETA reference number if you have one.

7. **Set a fallback point before leaving for the airport.**  
   Choose a clear time such as online check-in closing, bag drop closing, or your last safe rebooking time. If the ETA is still not confirmed at that point, act on the backup plan instead of hoping it will resolve at the gate.

8. **Protect your options.**  
   Check your ticket change rules, no-show penalty, later flights, accommodation cut-offs, and travel insurance documents. Tell one trusted person in your travel group or at your destination that the ETA is still pending.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why the ETA is pending today.
- You do not need to decide the whole trip now.
- You do not need to argue the immigration rules at the airport.
- You only need to protect this departure, your ticket options, and your next safe decision point.

## Important reassurance
A pending ETA can feel like it is nearly finished, but for travel it is safest to treat it as not confirmed. Making a backup plan is a calm way to reduce avoidable loss while you wait for the official decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next 24 hours only. Later decisions about visas, rebooking, complaints, refunds, or immigration options may need specialist travel, airline, insurance, or immigration advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. Boarding and entry depend on UK rules, your nationality, your passport, any exemptions, and what the official system shows at the time of travel.
