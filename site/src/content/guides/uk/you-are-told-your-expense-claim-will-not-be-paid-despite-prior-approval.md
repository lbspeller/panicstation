# What to do if… you are told your expense claim will not be paid despite prior approval (UK)

## Short answer
Submit a formal written request for rectification under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, clearly identifying the error and providing the correct factual information.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send aggressive or accusatory messages to the suspected author of the entry.
- Do not attempt to delete, overwrite, or physically alter any record yourself.
- Do not resign or sign settlement agreements as a "solution" to a disputed record.
- Do not share details of the inaccuracy on social media or internal staff groups.
- Do not rely on verbal promises from managers that the record will be fixed.

## What to do now
1. **Document the exact error as it currently appears in your record.**  
   Note the specific document name, the date of the entry, the name of the author if visible, and the exact text you are disputing.
   - Save a screenshot or print the specific page if you already have authorized access.
   - Ensure you do not access files you are not permitted to view.

2. **Submit a formal written request for rectification to the HR department.**  
   Under Section 16 of the UK data protection framework (updated by the 2025 Act), you have a legal right to have inaccurate data corrected.
   - Quote the specific wording that is incorrect.
   - Provide the corrected factual information.
   - Attach objective evidence of the error (such as email chains, timesheets, or medical certificates) if available.

3. **Ask HR to restrict the use of the disputed record while they verify its accuracy.**  
   Request that the organization "restricts processing" (stops using or sharing) the specific entry while the dispute is investigated.
   - Ask that a note is immediately added to the file stating the entry is "Disputed by Employee".
   - Confirm this request in writing to ensure there is a timestamped record.

4. **Identify if the false information is being used in a live process.**  
   If you are currently undergoing a disciplinary, redundancy, or performance management process, notify the decision-maker in writing that the record they are using is formally disputed.
   - Provide them with a copy of your rectification request.
   - Request that no decisions are finalized based on the disputed data until HR completes its review.

5. **Request a supplementary statement be added if the employer refuses to delete the entry.**  
   In cases where the "truth" is a matter of opinion rather than objective fact, the employer may refuse to erase the original note.
   - Provide a short, factual rebuttal or "supplementary statement".
   - Insist that this statement is permanently attached to the original record so it must be read alongside it.

6. **Check if the inaccurate information has been shared with third parties.**  
   Ask HR for a list of any "recipients" (such as external pension providers, occupational health, or prospective employers for references) who have seen the error.
   - Request that HR notifies these recipients of the correction or dispute once the review is complete.

7. **Raise a formal grievance if the matter is not resolved within one month.**  
   UK employers generally have one month to respond to a rectification request, and failing to follow the ACAS Code of Practice can lead to a 25% increase in future tribunal awards.
   - Use the internal grievance procedure to escalate the failure to correct the record.
   - Request a formal meeting and exercise your right to be accompanied by a trade union representative or colleague.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) today; they usually require you to finish the employer's internal process first.
- You do not need to hire a solicitor immediately, as the rectification request and grievance are standard internal procedures.
- You do not need to decide if you are leaving the company while the record is still being reviewed.

## Important reassurance
Finding a mistake in an "official" file is highly stressful and can feel like a personal attack. However, UK law provides clear mechanisms to challenge data accuracy. Taking a methodical, written approach is the most effective way to protect your professional reputation.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate steps for stabilizing and correcting an inaccurate HR record under UK data protection and employment law. It does not cover legal strategies for defamation, constructive dismissal, or complex discrimination claims.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and does not constitute legal advice. The 2026 regulatory environment under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 requires specific handling of personal data. If your situation involves sensitive "special category" data or high-stakes disciplinary action, consult a trade union representative or a qualified legal professional.
