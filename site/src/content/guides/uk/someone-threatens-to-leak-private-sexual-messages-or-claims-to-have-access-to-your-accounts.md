# What to do if… someone threatens to leak private sexual messages or claims to have access to your accounts (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, do not pay, and do not reply to the threat right now. Secure your email first, then your main accounts, and use specialist UK support so you are not handling this alone.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay, send gift cards, send crypto, or negotiate; there is no guarantee it will stop the threats, and it may lead to more demands.
- Do not send more intimate content to prove anything or to try to satisfy the threat.
- Do not click links they send, install “verification” apps, or share one-time passcodes.
- Do not rush into deleting chats, emails, or accounts before saving the basic details, unless you need to lock an account quickly for safety.
- Do not post public explanations or name-and-shame them while your accounts or messages may still be exposed.
- Do not assume this is your fault or that you have to manage it privately.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Put the phone down for a moment and decide: “I am not paying and I am not replying right now.”
2. **Save only the basic details, if you can do it safely.**
   - Screenshot the threat, the username or number, the profile page, and any payment demand.
   - Note the date, time, platform, and which account they claim to access.
   - Do not spend time collecting everything before you protect your accounts.
3. **Stop contact and reduce access.**
   - Do not reply again.
   - Report the account and the messages on the platform.
   - Block the account or number after you have saved the key details, if you can.
4. **Secure the account that can reset everything.**
   - Start with your email account.
   - Change the password to a long, unique password.
   - Turn on 2-step verification.
   - Check recovery email addresses and phone numbers, and remove anything you do not recognise.
5. **Secure the accounts most likely to expose messages or images.**
   - Change passwords for main social media, messaging, cloud photo storage, and payment accounts.
   - Sign out of other sessions or devices where the service allows it.
   - Remove unknown devices, connected apps, forwarding rules, or filters.
6. **Check for simple signs of account compromise.**
   - Look for unexpected login alerts, password reset emails, new devices, new recovery details, or messages you did not send.
   - If you suddenly lose mobile signal or get a SIM-change notice, contact your mobile provider using its official website or app.
7. **Use the right image-removal support.**
   - If you are an adult living in the UK and intimate images may be shared, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for support with threats and removal options.
   - If you are an adult and worried intimate images may be shared on major platforms, StopNCII.org can help participating platforms detect matching images where they violate policy. It does not remove content from the whole internet.
   - If you are under 18, or any sexual image involves someone under 18, use CEOP and Childline Report Remove for safeguarding-focused support.
8. **Use emergency help if there is immediate danger.**
   - Call 999 if there is an immediate real-world risk, stalking, threats of violence, or you feel at immediate risk of harm.
   - If there is no immediate danger, you can report to police by calling 101.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to tell everyone, post publicly, confront the person, or explain yourself.
- You do not need to prove whether they truly have access before taking basic security steps.
- You do not need to write a perfect report or gather every detail.
- You do not need to make big decisions tonight about relationships, work, moving, or your future.

## Important reassurance
These threats are designed to create shame and urgency so you will comply. Freezing, panicking, or feeling embarrassed does not mean you did anything wrong. You can slow this down, protect the most important accounts, and get help from people who deal with this often.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation and reduce harm. Later decisions about police, platforms, account recovery, legal options, or emotional support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger or feel at risk of harm, call 999. If anyone under 18 is involved in any sexual image, prioritise safeguarding support through CEOP, Childline Report Remove, or emergency services if there is immediate danger.
