# What to do if… you receive an unexpected collect call from a detention facility and you do not know who it is (UK)

## Short answer
Do not confirm personal details or authorize any charges by pressing phone keys. End the call immediately and verify the situation by contacting the specific prison or police station through an official phone number you find independently.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide a full name, home address, date of birth, or workplace "to confirm the recipient".
- Do not press any numeric keys (e.g., "Press 1 to accept") if the caller is unknown, as this can authorize reverse charges on a phone bill.
- Do not make payments via bank transfer, card, gift cards, or cryptocurrency to "bail out" or "fund the account" of an unknown caller.
- Do not call back any phone numbers provided by the caller or the automated recording.
- Do not believe claims that a situation is "top secret" or requires "immediate payment" to prevent an arrest or legal action.

## What to do now
1. **Terminate the call if the caller is unknown or the request is suspicious.**  
   If an automated voice asks for authorization to accept charges, hang up immediately. If a person is on the line, state "I do not know who this is" and end the connection without further engagement.
2. **Document the call details for verification purposes.**  
   Note the time and date of the call, the number displayed on the caller ID (if available), and the specific name of the facility mentioned in the recording.
3. **Locate the official contact details for the named facility.**  
   Search for the prison or police station on GOV.UK. If a specific prison was named (e.g., HMP Wandsworth), use the main switchboard number listed on the official HMPPS directory.
4. **Contact the facility directly to verify the contact attempt.**  
   - **For Prisons:** Call the main switchboard and ask to speak to the security or communications office to report an unexpected call from an unknown person.  
   - **For Police Custody:** Call 101 and request to be put through to the custody suite of the station mentioned. Ask if a detainee has listed you as a "nominated person." Be aware that staff may not confirm a person's presence due to data protection, but they can verify if an official call was placed.
5. **Report the incident to Action Fraud if money was requested.**  
   Use the Action Fraud online reporting tool or call 0300 123 2040. Provide any numbers or names used by the caller, as this helps build a profile of active prison-impersonation scams.
6. **Block the number using the Unwanted Prisoner Contact Service.**  
   If the calls are persistent or you want to ensure no further contact from the prison estate occurs, use the official GOV.UK service.
   - You can call the HMPPS Unwanted Prisoner Contact Service on **0300 060 6699** (Monday to Friday, 9 am to 4 pm).
   - You can also email **unwantedprisonercontact@justice.gov.uk** with your phone number and the name of the prison.
7. **Contact your phone provider to dispute unauthorized charges.**  
   Check your mobile or landline bill for "third-party" or "reverse charge" billing. If charges appear from a detention facility call you did not intend to authorize, ask your provider to apply a bar on all premium or reverse-charge services.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to provide financial support or "help" a person today.
- You do not need to investigate the identity of the caller yourself.
- You do not need to respond to any "urgent" court or legal threats made during the call.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled or pressured when a call claims to be from an official facility. Ending the call to verify the information is the safest protocol and is standard advice from both the police and prison services. Authentic officials will not penalize you for hang up and calling back through a verified channel.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction for unexpected contacts. It does not cover legal defense for detainees or long-term communication management for established prison contacts.

## Important note
This information is for guidance and does not constitute legal advice. If you are being threatened, harassed, or believe you are a victim of an active fraud, contact the police on 101 (non-emergency) or 999 (emergency).
