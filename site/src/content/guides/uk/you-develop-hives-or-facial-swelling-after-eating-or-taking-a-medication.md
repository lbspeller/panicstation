# What to do if… you develop hives or facial swelling after eating or taking a medication (UK)

## Short answer
If you have facial swelling, throat tightness, breathing difficulty, or feel faint, use an adrenaline auto-injector (if available) and call 999 immediately. Say "anaphylaxis" (ana-fill-axis) to the operator.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait to see if symptoms improve if you have breathing trouble or swelling of the mouth/tongue.
- Do not stand up or walk, even if you feel better; stay lying down.
- Do not drive yourself to the hospital or walk to a GP surgery.
- Do not take more of the suspected food or medicine to "test" the reaction.
- Do not use a single antihistamine as a substitute for emergency care if red flags are present.
- Do not leave the person alone if you are assisting someone else.

## What to do now
1. **Identify emergency red flags immediately.** 
   - Check for: tongue or throat swelling, difficulty swallowing, hoarse voice, noisy breathing (wheeze or stridor), persistent cough, dizziness, or confusion.
2. **Use an adrenaline auto-injector (AAI) if you have been prescribed one.**
   - **Follow the instructions on the device immediately.** 
   - **Inject into the outer mid-thigh.** 
   - **Use a second injector after 5 minutes if symptoms do not improve or if they worsen.**
3. **Call 999 and say the word "anaphylaxis" (ana-fill-axis).**
   - **Provide your exact location clearly.**
   - **State that a person is having a severe allergic reaction.**
4. **Position yourself or the person for safety.**
   - **Lie down flat and raise the legs.** 
   - **If breathing is very difficult, sit up slightly but do not stand up.**
   - **If pregnant, lie on the left side.**
   - **If unconscious but breathing, place in the recovery position.**
5. **Isolate the suspected trigger.**
   - **Stop eating the food or taking the medication immediately.** 
   - **Do not attempt to wash the mouth out or induce vomiting.**
6. **Gather evidence for medical teams once help is summoned.**
   - **Keep the packaging of the food or the blister pack of the medicine.**
   - **Take a photo of the rash or swelling if it is safe to do so.**
   - **Note the exact time the reaction started.**
7. **Contact NHS 111 if symptoms are mild and restricted to the skin only.**
   - **Seek advice if you have hives (itchy rash) only, with no facial swelling or breathing issues.**
   - **Request an urgent GP appointment if the rash persists for more than 48 hours.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific allergen or ingredient right now.
- You do not need to notify the manufacturer or restaurant until you are medically stable.
- You do not need to book an allergy specialist appointment today; the hospital or GP will arrange this later.
- You do not need to decide on future medication alternatives in the next few minutes.

## Important reassurance
It is normal for your heart to race or for you to feel shaky after using an adrenaline injector or experiencing a sudden reaction. This is often a physiological response to the situation and the medication. Taking immediate action by calling 999 is the standard protocol and is not an overreaction when swelling or breathing changes occur.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for acute allergic reactions only. Long-term management, including allergy testing and medication reviews, must be conducted by a healthcare professional.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not replace professional medical diagnosis. Anaphylaxis is a life-threatening emergency that requires immediate hospital treatment even if symptoms seem to improve after using an injector.
