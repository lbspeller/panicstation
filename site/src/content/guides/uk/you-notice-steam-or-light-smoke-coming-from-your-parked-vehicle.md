# What to do if… you notice steam or light smoke coming from your parked vehicle (UK)

## Short answer
Stay well away, keep other people back, and call 999 for the Fire and Rescue Service if there is any chance it is a fire or you are not sure.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t walk back towards the vehicle for a closer look.
- Don’t touch or open the bonnet.
- Don’t lean over the bonnet, grille, wheel arches, or underside of the vehicle.
- Don’t try to drive the vehicle away.
- Don’t go back towards the vehicle to use an extinguisher.
- Don’t collect belongings from the vehicle while steam or smoke is still present.

## What to do now
1. Move further away and ask others nearby to keep back. If there is a wall, barrier, or solid structure nearby, stand behind it.
2. Notice only what you can see, hear, or smell from where you are: whether smoke is getting thicker, whether there are flames, whether it smells of burning, whether there is popping or crackling, and whether smoke is coming from under the vehicle.
3. If it could be a fire, or you are unsure, call 999. Ask for the Fire and Rescue Service and say there is steam or light smoke from a parked vehicle.
4. Give the clearest location you can: car park name, level, bay number, road name, nearby entrance, landmark, postcode if you know it, or a what3words location if that helps.
5. If you are in a car park, workplace, supermarket, petrol station, charging area, or managed site, alert staff or security from a safe distance so they can keep people and vehicles away.
6. If the vehicle is electric or hybrid, or if it is plugged into a charger, say this on the 999 call. Do not go near the cable, charge point, or vehicle to check.
7. Stay where the operator tells you to stay. If the call has ended and the situation clearly worsens, call 999 again with the update.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether it is coolant steam, oil smoke, an electrical fault, or a fire.
- You do not need to arrange recovery, repairs, insurance, or a mechanic until the immediate risk is over.
- You do not need to move nearby vehicles yourself.
- You do not need to get photos or retrieve anything from inside the vehicle.

## Important reassurance
Treating unexpected steam or smoke as potentially serious is reasonable. Keeping your distance and getting the Fire and Rescue Service involved if you are unsure is a safe, practical response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the immediate moment when you are already at a safe distance. Later decisions about recovery, repair, charging equipment, insurance, or replacement transport may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, mechanical, fire-safety, or other professional advice. If you believe there is immediate danger, call 999 and follow the operator’s instructions.
