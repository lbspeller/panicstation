# What to do if… you notice spreading redness or red streaks around a cut (UK)

## Short answer
If redness is spreading from a cut, or you see a red streak moving away from it, get urgent same-day medical advice. Call 111 where available, use NHS 111 online in England, or contact your GP for an urgent same-day appointment; call 999 or go to A&E if you seem seriously unwell or worried about sepsis.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t wait and see if the redness is spreading, streaking, or you are getting more unwell.
- Don’t squeeze, cut into, or try to drain the wound yourself.
- Don’t wrap the area tightly or leave anything on that affects circulation.
- Don’t take leftover antibiotics or someone else’s antibiotics.
- Don’t scrub the wound hard or use harsh chemicals on it.
- Don’t ignore swelling around rings, watches, or tight jewellery on the affected limb.

## What to do now
1. **Check for emergency warning signs.** Call **999** or go to **A&E** now if you have spreading infection and any of these: breathing very fast or difficulty breathing, confusion or slurred speech, blue, grey, pale or blotchy skin, a rash that does not fade when pressed, severe shivering, a very high or very low temperature, passing very little urine, feeling dangerously unwell, or the affected skin is turning dusky, purple, or blistered.
2. **If there are no 999 signs, get same-day urgent advice.** Call **111** where available, use **NHS 111 online** if you are in England, or ask your GP practice for an urgent same-day appointment. If 111 is not available where you are in the UK, use your local urgent out-of-hours service. Say clearly: **“I have spreading redness or red streaks from a cut.”**
3. **Tell them the details that change urgency.** Say whether you have fever, worsening pain, swelling, pus, red streaking, swollen glands, a bite, a dirty wound, diabetes, immune suppression, pregnancy, a very young child affected, or infection on the face or near an eye.
4. **Mark and record the spreading area if you can do it safely.** Take your temperature and note the time. Draw a light pen line around the edge of the redness and write the time next to it, then take a clear photo in good light. Do not delay urgent help to do this.
5. **Do simple wound care while arranging help.** Wash your hands, gently rinse the cut with clean running water, pat it dry, and cover it with a clean non-stick dressing or plaster. Change the dressing if it becomes wet or dirty.
6. **Reduce tightness and swelling.** Remove rings, watches, or tight jewellery from the affected limb before swelling increases. If the cut is on an arm or leg, rest it and raise it where comfortable while you are getting advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether you will need tests, antibiotics, hospital treatment, or time off work.
- You do not need to identify the germ causing it.
- You do not need to repeatedly deep-clean the wound or apply several antiseptics.
- You do not need to make long-term plans; the first step is prompt clinical assessment.

## Important reassurance
You are right to take spreading redness or streaking seriously. Getting help early gives clinicians the best chance to treat the infection before it becomes more difficult to manage.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. A clinician may advise monitoring, antibiotics, wound care, urgent treatment centre assessment, or hospital assessment depending on how you look and feel.

## Important note
This is general information, not a diagnosis, and not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If symptoms are spreading or you feel significantly unwell, seek urgent professional care now.
