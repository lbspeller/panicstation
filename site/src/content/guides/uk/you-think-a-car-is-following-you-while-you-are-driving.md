# What to do if… you think a car is following you while you are driving (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive home. Stay on main roads, head towards a busy staffed place, and call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive home or to a quiet place to see if they stop.
- Do not stop in a lay-by, side street, or isolated car park where someone can approach you.
- Do not confront the other driver, gesture, brake-check, or get out of the car.
- Do not speed, run red lights, or make sudden turns to test them.
- Do not hold or use your phone while driving unless you need to call 999 or 112 in an emergency and it is unsafe or impractical to stop. Hands-free or a passenger calling is safer.
- Do not lead them to a friend’s home for help.

## What to do now
1. **Make the car harder to approach.** Lock the doors, keep windows up, keep your seatbelt on, and keep moving at a safe, legal speed.
2. **Avoid revealing where you live.** Do not take your usual home turn. Stay on well-lit main roads or a route with shops, traffic, petrol stations, or other people nearby.
3. **Choose a busy staffed destination.** Aim for a large petrol station, motorway services, supermarket entrance, hospital entrance, or another public place with people, lights, and cameras. A police station can be an option if it is nearby, but do not add a long isolated drive to reach one.
4. **Call 999 if you feel threatened or the car keeps following.** Use hands-free if you can, or ask a passenger to call. Say you believe you are being followed by a vehicle, give your location and direction of travel, describe your car, describe the other vehicle, and give the registration number only if you can see it safely.
5. **If a vehicle is signalling you to stop, separate certainty from uncertainty.** If you are sure it is police, pull over and stop as soon as it is safe. If you are not sure it is police, do not panic-stop somewhere isolated: signal that you have understood, such as by using your hazard lights if safe, drive steadily to the nearest busy, well-lit public place, and call 999 to explain what is happening. When you stop, keep doors locked until you are satisfied it is police.
6. **When you reach the public place, stay visible.** Park near the entrance, pumps, staff, or other people. If you are on the phone to 999, tell the operator exactly where you are and follow their instructions.
7. **When the immediate risk has passed, record what you remember.** Write down the time, roads, direction of travel, vehicle colour, make, model, registration, and anything the driver did. If it is no longer an emergency, you can report it later through 101 or online.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out why this is happening right now.
- You do not need to decide whether it was definitely deliberate before getting to safety.
- You do not need to review dashcam footage while you are still driving.
- You do not need to call several people for opinions before calling 999 if you feel at risk.
- You do not need to post online or message a group chat while you are still on the road.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to second-guess yourself in this situation. Treat it as a safety problem, not a proof problem: staying visible, not going home, and asking for help are reasonable precautions even if it later turns out to be a coincidence.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you reach a safer pause and reduce the chance of an encounter. Later decisions about reporting, evidence, repeat incidents, or ongoing safety may need police or specialist support.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use your judgement based on the immediate risk, the road conditions, and any instructions from emergency services.
