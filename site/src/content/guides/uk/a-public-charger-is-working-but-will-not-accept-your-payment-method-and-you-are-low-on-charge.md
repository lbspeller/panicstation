# What to do if… a public charger is working but will not accept your payment method and you are low on charge (UK)

## Short answer
Preserve your remaining battery immediately and call the free 24/7 operator helpline displayed on the unit to request a remote start or status check. If the unit is a rapid charger (50kW+), it is legally required to offer a contactless payment option as a backup to apps or cards.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t run the cabin heating, air conditioning, or heated seats while troubleshooting.
- Don’t attempt the same payment method more than twice, as this may trigger a bank fraud block.
- Don’t move the vehicle into a dangerous position or block traffic just to reach a different cable.
- Don’t share your bank PIN, full card number, or one-time app passcodes with anyone claiming to be "support" over the phone.
- Don’t ignore a low-battery warning to drive to a distant "preferred" network; stop at the nearest available unit of any speed.

## What to do now
1. **Minimise power consumption immediately.**  
   **Park the vehicle safely and turn off the ignition and all non-essential electronics.**
   - Switch off climate control (heating/AC) and heated windows.
   - Switch the vehicle to "Eco" or "Range" mode if available.

2. **Locate the charger ID and the mandatory free 24/7 helpline number.**  
   **Every public charge point operator in the UK must display a free-to-call support number on the unit.**
   - Take a photo of the charger ID number (often a 5 or 6-digit code).
   - Note the specific error message displayed on the charger screen.

3. **Attempt the legally required contactless backup if the app fails.**  
   **If you are using a rapid charger (50kW+) or a unit installed after Nov 2024, it must accept contactless bank cards or mobile wallets (Apple/Google Pay).**
   - Try a physical debit/credit card if the mobile wallet fails.
   - If the unit has multiple connectors (e.g., CCS and CHAdeMO), try the other compatible cable if the car permits.

4. **Call the operator's helpline and ask for a remote session start.**  
   **State that you are low on charge and the payment terminal is failing to authenticate.**
   - Provide the charger ID you recorded.
   - Ask the operator if they can "vinit" or "remote start" the session; many operators can do this if the hardware is online but the payment terminal is faulty.

5. **Set a 10-minute "Move On" limit.**  
   **If the session has not started within 10 minutes of arrival, stop troubleshooting and use your remaining range to reach a backup site.**
   - Use a roaming provider app or a generic map to find the absolute nearest alternative, even if it is a slow "Destination" charger.

6. **Follow breakdown safety if you are on a motorway or high-speed road.**  
   **If your battery is too low to reach the next junction safely, you must treat the situation as a vehicle breakdown.**
   - Pull as far left as possible into an emergency area or onto the hard shoulder.
   - Turn your wheels to the left and put your hazard lights on.
   - Exit the vehicle via the left-hand doors and wait behind the safety barrier.
   - Use a roadside emergency telephone in preference to your mobile; it connects directly to National Highways and automatically provides your exact location.

7. **Contact your recovery provider if you are stranded.**  
   **Call your breakdown service and specify that you have an "EV out of charge due to infrastructure failure."**
   - Provide your exact location (road name, direction, and nearest marker post).
   - Confirm if you are in a vulnerable position (e.g., travelling alone or at night).

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why your bank declined the transaction right now.
- You do not need to resolve "pending" authorization holds on your account until you are home.
- You do not need to report the faulty charger to mapping apps or local councils until you have reached safety.

## Important reassurance
It is a known issue for public chargers to have functional power but faulty payment communication. This is why UK regulations now mandate 24/7 helplines and contactless backups. Feeling anxious when the "low battery" light is on is a normal response; focusing on the helpline or moving to a backup site is the standard way to resolve the delay.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for payment-related charging failures. It does not cover technical vehicle faults or long-term battery health management.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance regarding UK public charging regulations and road safety. If you are stopped in a live traffic lane or feel you are in immediate physical danger, call 999. National Highways can be reached on 0300 123 5000 for non-emergency assistance on English motorways.
