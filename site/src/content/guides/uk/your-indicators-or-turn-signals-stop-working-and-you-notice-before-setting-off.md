# What to do if… your indicators or turn signals stop working and you notice before setting off (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive the vehicle on public roads if you cannot communicate your intentions to other road users. Arrange for a repair or use an alternative method of transport until the direction indicators are functional.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a journey is safe just because the vehicle is mechanically mobile; indicators are legally "obligatory" equipment.
- Do not use hazard warning lights while moving as a substitute for turn signals; this is confusing and often illegal.
- Do not attempt to drive at night, in fog, or in heavy rain where hand signals will not be visible to others.
- Do not enter motorways, high-speed dual carriageways, or complex multilane roundabouts.
- Do not continue "testing" the stalk repeatedly while the engine is off, as this may deplete the battery.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact nature of the failure while stationary.**
   - Switch on the ignition (do not start the engine unless necessary).
   - Test the left indicator, right indicator, and hazard warning lights separately.
   - Observe the dashboard for "hyper-flashing" (very fast clicking/flashing) or a complete lack of light/sound.
   - Step outside the vehicle to identify which specific bulbs are dark (front, rear, or side repeaters).
2. **Determine if the fault is a single bulb or a system-wide failure.**
   - If only one corner is dark and the others "hyper-flash," it is likely a single blown bulb.
   - If one entire side is dead, it may be a fuse or a wiring issue.
   - If both sides and the hazards are all silent and dark, it is likely a fuse, relay, or main switch fault.
3. **Check the fuse box only if you have the manual and spare fuses.**
   - Locate the fuse for "Direction Indicators" or "Hazards" using the vehicle handbook.
   - If the metal wire inside the fuse is broken, replace it with a fuse of the exact same Ampere (color) rating.
   - If the new fuse blows immediately, stop and seek professional electrical repair.
4. **Decide if the journey must be cancelled.**
   - If the fault cannot be fixed immediately, delay the trip or use public transport.
   - Driving with defective obligatory lights is a traffic offence and may result in a Fixed Penalty Notice.
5. **Use Highway Code arm signals only for an essential, short trip to a nearby repair facility.**
   - Ensure it is broad daylight and visibility is clear.
   - Use the following signals (performed with the right arm through the driver's window):
     - **Right turn:** Extend the right arm horizontally with the palm facing forward.
     - **Left turn:** Extend the right arm and rotate it in a slow, circular forward motion.
     - **Slowing down or stopping:** Extend the right arm with the palm down and move it slowly up and down.
6. **Book a professional repair immediately.**
   - Contact a local garage or a mobile mechanic.
   - If you have breakdown cover, check if "electrical lighting failure" is covered for home attendance.
7. **Note that this is an MOT failure.**
   - A non-functioning direction indicator is classified as a "Major" defect under the MOT inspection manual.
   - The vehicle will not pass its annual test until this is rectified.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific wire or relay component yourself.
- You do not need to replace all bulbs if only one has failed.
- You do not need to worry about the MOT status if your current certificate is still valid and you are currently arranging a repair.

## Important reassurance
Spotting a lighting failure before you join traffic is a successful safety check. Many indicator issues are caused by simple bulb or fuse failures that are inexpensive and quick for a professional to rectify. Taking the time to address this now prevents a potential collision or police intervention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a vehicle lighting fault discovered while stationary. It does not cover complex electrical diagnostics or long-term vehicle maintenance.

## Important note
Direction indicators are required by the Road Vehicles Lighting Regulations 1989. Driving a vehicle with defective obligatory lamps is a breach of road traffic law. Arm signals are a secondary method of communication and do not satisfy the legal requirement for functional indicators for extended use or in poor visibility.
