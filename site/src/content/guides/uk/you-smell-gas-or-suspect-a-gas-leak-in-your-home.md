# What to do if… you smell gas or suspect a gas leak in your home (UK)

## Short answer
Get everyone into fresh air, avoid anything that could spark, and call from outside: for natural gas in Great Britain call **0800 111 999**; in Northern Ireland call **0800 002 001**. If there is fire, an explosion, someone collapses, or you cannot leave safely, call **999** from outside or as soon as you can.

## Do not do these things
- Do not turn lights or electrical appliances on or off, or use doorbells, entry phones, plug sockets, or phones inside if you can avoid it.
- Do not smoke, vape, light matches, light candles, or use any naked flame.
- Do not try to find the leak with a flame or by dismantling an appliance.
- Do not stay inside to see if the smell goes away, even if the smell is faint.
- Do not enter a cellar or basement to reach a gas meter or valve.
- Do not turn the gas supply back on yourself after it has been turned off.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the appliance immediately.** If it is safe and within reach, turn the appliance controls to **off** without using any electrical switch.
2. **Open doors and windows as you leave** to ventilate the property. Do not linger to do this if the smell is strong or you feel unsafe.
3. **Get everyone into fresh air, including pets.** If you are in a block of flats, move to a safe outdoor area away from the building.
4. **Avoid sparks until you are outside.** Do not use a phone, light switch, plug, doorbell, entry phone, or electrical appliance inside or near the smell.
5. **Call the gas emergency number from outside.** For natural gas in Great Britain, call **0800 111 999**. For natural gas in Northern Ireland, call **0800 002 001**. Follow the operator’s instructions.
6. **If you use LPG,** use the emergency contact number shown on the bulk tank, storage vessel, meter, or supplier information if you can do so from outside. If you cannot find it quickly, call **999** if there is immediate danger.
7. **Only if you can do it quickly and safely on your way out,** turn off the gas at the emergency control valve by the gas meter. Do not do this if the meter is in a cellar or basement, if the smell is strong there, or if finding it would delay leaving.
8. **If anyone feels unwell,** keep them in fresh air. Call **999** if symptoms are severe, worsening, or someone collapses, and say there may have been gas or carbon monoxide exposure.
9. **If you are renting or in managed housing,** once you are outside and the emergency call has been made, tell your landlord, agent, or building manager that a gas emergency has been reported and you are waiting for emergency advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who is at fault right now.
- You do not need to troubleshoot the boiler, cooker, hob, meter, or pipework.
- You do not need to search for the source of the smell.
- You do not need to discuss repairs, costs, insurance, or landlord responsibility until the immediate risk is controlled.
- You do not need to collect belongings beyond essentials you can safely take as you leave.

## Important reassurance
A gas-like smell can be frightening, and it is normal to freeze or second-guess yourself. Leaving, avoiding sparks, and calling from outside is the safe default even if the smell turns out not to be a gas leak.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the first minutes and hours. Later decisions about repairs, appliance checks, landlord contact, insurance, or returning to the property may need a Gas Safe registered engineer, your landlord, your insurer, or another suitable specialist.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. In a suspected gas emergency, follow the emergency operator’s instructions.
