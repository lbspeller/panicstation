# What to do if… you need to update emergency contacts for children quickly after a death in the family (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the school or childcare provider currently supervising your child to remove the deceased person from the records and provide at least two live, reachable contacts for emergency and collection purposes.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume the school will automatically update their internal system just because they know about the death — administrative records must be formally amended.
- Don’t rely on a verbal message or a text to an individual staff member; ensure the **official office record** is the one being updated.
- Don’t send a new person to collect the child without notifying the setting in writing or via their official app first.
- Don’t attempt to resolve complex legal guardianship or long-term custody arrangements during the immediate hours when you only need to secure a safe pick-up list.
- Don’t feel obligated to share the cause of death or private family details with administrative staff; a "family bereavement" is sufficient context.

## What to do now
1. **Identify every setting currently responsible for the child.**
   - **Create a checklist including the school, nursery, childminder, and any after-school or sports clubs.**
   - **Include transport providers or private tutors who have been given emergency contact details.**

2. **Call the primary school or nursery office immediately.**
   - **Inform the administration that a listed contact has died and you are providing an updated list of authorised collectors.**
   - **Confirm that the setting now has at least two reachable emergency contacts, which is a statutory requirement under the updated 2026 safeguarding frameworks.**
   - **Update or reset the collection password (if the setting uses one) to ensure security during the transition.**

3. **Provide specific "can collect" and "cannot reach" instructions.**
   - **List full names, relationships, and current phone numbers for at least two people who can physically reach the setting within 30–60 minutes.**
   - **Explicitly distinguish between people who are "emergency phone contacts only" and those who are "authorised for collection."**

4. **Address safeguarding and identity verification for new collectors.**
   - **Ask the setting exactly what they require to release the child to a new adult (e.g., photo ID check, a specific email from the primary parent, or a pre-shared code word).**
   - **Clarify that if you cannot be reached, the staff should proceed down the emergency list in the order you have provided.**

5. **Verify Parental Responsibility (PR) if the death changes the child's living situation.**
   - **Tell the school’s Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) if the primary carer has changed and ask what documentation they need on file to reflect this.**
   - **Note that in England and Wales, schools must treat all individuals with Parental Responsibility equally unless they are provided with a specific court order (such as a Child Arrangements Order) stating otherwise.**

6. **Update the Child Health Information Services (CHIS) via the GP.**
   - **Contact the child’s GP surgery to remove the deceased person from the child’s medical record and update the primary emergency contact.**
   - **Ask the surgery to update any "proxy access" for online services (such as the NHS App) if the deceased person held the primary login for the child.**

7. **Maintain a single record of the new contact details.**
   - **Write down the names and numbers of the new contacts and reuse this identical list for every club or organization to ensure consistency across all records.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide death certificates or formal legal proof of the death to the school today just to update a contact list.
- You do not need to finalize permanent guardianship or long-term wills immediately.
- You do not need to inform the child's dentist or optician in the first 24 hours unless an appointment is scheduled.
- You do not need to notify the "Tell Us Once" service immediately; this can be done within several days of the death being registered.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed or "foggy" when handling administrative tasks after a death. Focusing on the immediate safety of your child by updating pick-up lists is a practical and protective act. Schools and nurseries are familiar with bereavement and will generally prioritize these updates without requiring you to engage in long conversations.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for urgent contact and collection safety. It does not cover long-term legal guardianship, inheritance, or complex family law disputes.

## Important note
This is general information and not legal advice. If you are concerned about a child’s immediate safety or a custody dispute, contact the setting’s Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) or a family law specialist. For immediate emotional support for children, you can contact Child Bereavement UK (0800 02 888 40) or Winston’s Wish (08088 020 021).
