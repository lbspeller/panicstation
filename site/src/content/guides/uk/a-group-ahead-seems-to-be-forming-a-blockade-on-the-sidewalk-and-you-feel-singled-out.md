# What to do if… a group ahead seems to be forming a blockade on the sidewalk and you feel singled out (UK)

## Short answer
Increase the distance between yourself and the group immediately by turning away or crossing the road, and move toward a staffed, public area. Call 999 if you are being followed, blocked from leaving, or feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not walk into the center of the group or attempt to "push through" the blockade.
- Do not stop to argue, ask them to move, or respond to verbal taunts.
- Do not attempt to "test" the group by walking slowly or waiting for them to move.
- Do not enter narrow spaces with no secondary exit, such as alleys, underpasses, or enclosed doorways.
- Do not record the group if doing so prevents you from exiting the area quickly or increases their focus on you.
- Do not walk directly to your home if you believe you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Increase your distance from the group immediately.** Turn around, cross the street, or take a different route before you reach the group's position.
2. **Move toward a "staffed and visible" location.** Enter a shop, supermarket, hotel lobby, or petrol station where staff are present and visible.
3. **Position yourself near a service counter or staff member.** State clearly: "I feel unsafe, please may I stay here for a moment?" and ask them to monitor the entrance.
4. **Use your phone to establish a connection with support.** Call a trusted contact and state your exact location and the fact that you are safe inside a building.
5. **Call 999 if the group follows you, blocks your exit, or threatens violence.** If you cannot speak safely, use the "Silent Solution" by dialling 999 and pressing 55 when prompted by the operator.
6. **Report the incident to the police via 101 once you are in a secure location.** Provide details including the exact location, time, and a description of the group's behavior and appearance.
7. **Identify if the behavior was motivated by hostility toward a protected characteristic.** If you believe you were targeted due to race, religion, disability, sexual orientation, transgender identity, or sex (under the 2023 Harassment Act), inform the police during your report.
8. **Contact the British Transport Police if the incident occurs on the rail network.** Call 999 for emergencies. For non-urgent reporting on trains or at stations, text **61016** with details of the situation and your location.
9. **Log the specific sensory details of the event.** Note the number of people, distinctive clothing, any specific words used, and the direction they were moving.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the behavior met a specific legal threshold for a crime right now.
- You do not need to justify your decision to turn around or avoid the area to anyone.
- You do not need to provide a formal witness statement until you are in a calm, safe environment.
- You do not need to review CCTV or find witnesses yourself; the police will handle evidence collection if a report is made.

## Important reassurance
Trusting your physical sensation of unease is a valid safety response. Choosing to avoid a potential confrontation is an act of situational awareness, not a lack of confidence. It is common to feel a rush of adrenaline or "freeze" in these moments; moving your body away from the hazard helps break that response.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for public intimidation. It does not cover long-term security planning or legal proceedings following a reported incident.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation involving an immediate threat to life or safety, always contact emergency services by dialling 999.
