# What to do if… your charging port stops working and your device is close to running out of power (UK)

## Short answer
Download the specific files you need to edit immediately to your local device. Free up space by deleting large items and emptying the provider's trash/recycle bin, or purchase a temporary storage upgrade to restore editing access (note that upgrades may take 24–48 hours to sync).

## Do not do these things
- Do not mass-delete files in a panic; you may delete critical data and still not free space until the "Recycle Bin" or "Trash" is manually emptied.
- Do not click links in "Storage Full" or "Account Frozen" emails to log in; visit the provider’s website directly to avoid phishing scams.
- Do not keep editing across different devices while sync is failing, as this creates "conflicting copies" that are difficult to merge later.
- Do not sign out or factory reset your device as a first step; this can trigger a re-sync that may fail or lead to data loss during the lockout.
- Do not assume the files are gone; "read-only" usually means the data is preserved but locked for modifications.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the specific files you need to work on right now.**
   - **Download the documents directly to your computer or phone storage** rather than trying to edit them in the browser or sync app.
   - Save these as new versions (e.g., "Document_v2_Local") to ensure you are not reliant on the cloud connection.

2. **Check your official storage breakdown via the web interface.**
   - **Log in to the provider’s website (e.g., onedrive.live.com, drive.google.com, or icloud.com) to see the true usage stats.**
   - Look for the "Storage" or "Manage Storage" section to see which service is using the most space (e.g., Gmail attachments often fill up Google Drive, or Outlook attachments fill up OneDrive).

3. **Empty the 'hidden' storage in trash and deleted folders.**
   - **Permanently delete items from the provider's Trash, Recycle Bin, or Recently Deleted folder.**
   - Note that in many systems (like Microsoft 365), items in the "Recycle Bin" still count against your quota until they are purged.

4. **Isolate and remove one or two very large files.**
   - **Sort your file list by "Size" and move the largest 2–3 items to an external drive or a different storage service.**
   - Once deleted from the cloud and the cloud's trash, wait a few minutes for the "read-only" lock to refresh.

5. **Apply a temporary storage upgrade if you cannot free space quickly.**
   - **Purchase the smallest available storage increment if you have an urgent deadline.**
   - Be aware that it can take up to 48 hours for the provider to process the payment and unlock all "read-only" restrictions across all synced devices.

6. **Pause the local sync client to prevent errors.**
   - **Right-click the cloud icon (OneDrive/Google Drive/iCloud) in your taskbar or menu bar and select "Pause syncing."**
   - Leave it paused until you have confirmed via the web interface that your account is back under its limit.

7. **Follow the internal complaint process for persistent UK data access issues.**
   - **Submit a formal written complaint to the provider's Data Protection Officer (DPO) if the storage readout is demonstrably incorrect.**
   - Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, you must follow the organization's internal 30-day complaint process before escalating to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

## What can wait
- You do not need to organize your entire folder structure right now; focus only on the top 5 largest files to break the lock.
- You do not need to cancel your subscription or switch providers immediately.
- You do not need to worry about "losing" the files today; most providers keep data for at least 6 months after a quota is reached before considering deletion.

## Important reassurance
This is a standard protective measure used by cloud providers to prevent data corruption when an account cannot accept new data. Your files are generally safe and readable; the "read-only" status is a software lock, not a sign of a hardware crash or permanent file corruption.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital storage lockouts. It does not cover long-term backup strategies or technical troubleshooting for corrupted sync databases.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute technical or legal advice. Storage policies vary significantly between personal and corporate (Work/School) accounts. If you use a managed organization account, your administrator may have "legal holds" or "retention policies" that prevent you from freeing up space manually.
