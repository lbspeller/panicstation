# What to do if… you feel unsafe at a venue because staff or security are not responding and the situation is escalating (UK)

## Short answer
Move immediately to a busy, well-lit area and leave the venue if you can do so without confrontation. If you believe there is an immediate threat of violence or a crime is in progress, call 999 (or 112) now.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t attempt to argue with or "de-escalate" an aggressive person yourself if you feel unsafe.
- Don’t stay in the venue to finish a drink, wait for a tab, or collect a coat if the risk is rising.
- Don’t allow staff or security to move you to an isolated "back room" or "quiet office" to talk.
- Don’t go outside to "settle things" or talk further with someone who has been aggressive.
- Don’t assume you are overreacting if your instincts suggest a situation is becoming dangerous.
- Don’t hide in a toilet cubicle or stairwell, as these are isolated areas where you may be trapped.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a "neutral" high-visibility zone immediately.** Head to the most crowded, brightest part of the venue, such as the main bar or the front entrance, keeping your back to a wall if possible.
2. **Identify a staff member and use a direct safety request.** Approach a bartender or host and state clearly:  
   **"I do not feel safe here and I need help to leave the building now."**  
   If the venue displays "Ask for Angela" posters (often found in washrooms), you can use the code phrase "Is Angela working?" to signal staff to assist you discreetly.
3. **Leave the premises via the busiest public exit.** If security or staff are not responding, do not wait for their permission. Move toward the exit and stay in the company of other patrons if you are alone.
4. **Call 999 (or 112) if you see a weapon or an immediate assault.** State the name of the venue, your specific location inside (e.g., "by the cloakroom"), and a brief description of the threat. Keep the line open even if you have to put the phone in your pocket.
5. **Use the "Silent Solution" if it is unsafe to speak.** Dial **999** and stay on the line. If you are on a mobile, you will hear an automated message; **you must press 55** when prompted to confirm it is a genuine emergency, or the call will be terminated. On a landline, if you remain silent, the operator will connect you to police if they hear any signs of distress.
6. **Exit to a staffed, public location rather than a side street.** If you suspect you are being followed, go into a late-night supermarket, hotel lobby, or a nearby open venue with visible security.
7. **Contact a trusted person to monitor your journey home.** Share your live location via your phone and arrange for a taxi or pickup at a well-lit, busy location rather than waiting on a dark street corner.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to worry about the cost of a lost coat or an unpaid bar tab right now.
- You do **not** need to decide whether to file a formal police report or licensing complaint in this moment.
- You do **not** need to confront the venue management about their lack of response while you are still on the premises.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of "social pressure" to stay or avoid making a scene, but your physical safety is the priority. Trusting your physical response to an escalating environment—such as a racing heart or a "gut feeling"—is a valid safety mechanism, not a sign of overreacting.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization steps for navigating an escalating situation at a venue. It does not cover long-term legal remedies or post-incident trauma support.

## Important note
This information is for safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In an emergency, always dial 999. Under the Terrorism (Protection of Premises) Act 2025 (Martyn’s Law), venues have specific duties regarding public safety; if you believe a venue has failed in these duties or if security staff (SIA licensed) acted inappropriately, you can report this to your local council's licensing department or the Security Industry Authority (SIA).
