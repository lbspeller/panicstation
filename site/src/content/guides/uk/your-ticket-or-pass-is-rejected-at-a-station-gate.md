# What to do if… your ticket or pass is rejected at a station gate (UK)

## Short answer
Do not force the gate or follow someone through. Step aside, keep your ticket or pass ready, and ask station staff or use a help point or intercom to have it checked and be let through.

## Do not do these things
- Do not push the barrier open, jump it, or tailgate behind another passenger, even if you are sure your ticket is valid.
- Do not throw away the ticket, delete the mobile ticket, close the barcode, or switch off the phone showing it.
- Do not keep trying the same gate again and again while people are queuing behind you.
- Do not switch between contactless cards, phones, watches, Oyster cards, or other passes during the same pay-as-you-go journey.
- Do not buy another ticket in a panic if staff can check the ticket or pass you already have.

## What to do now
1. **Step out of the queue and keep the ticket or pass ready.** Keep hold of the paper ticket, smartcard, pass, contactless card, Oyster card, or mobile barcode you used for the journey.
2. **Make one careful retry if it is calm and safe.**
   - Try a different gate, because a single reader can fail.
   - For a paper ticket, insert it smoothly and wait for it to come back before pulling it out.
   - For a mobile barcode or QR code, turn the brightness up and hold the code flat and still over the reader.
   - For contactless or Oyster pay as you go, present only one card or device and keep other cards away from the reader.
3. **Go to the staffed gate, often the wide or accessible gate.** Say: “This ticket or pass is valid, but the barrier will not accept it. Can you check it and let me through?”
4. **If no staff are visible, look for a help point or intercom near the gateline.** Explain that you have a valid ticket or pass that will not scan, and ask for the gate to be released or for staff to attend.
5. **If you are trying to exit and are stuck behind the gates, make that the immediate point.** Say: “I need to exit the station; my ticket or pass will not open the barrier.” Let staff sort out the ticket details once you are out of the gateline.
6. **If this is a contactless or Oyster pay-as-you-go journey, keep the journey consistent.** Use the same card or device you used to touch in. If the gate problem means you cannot touch out, ask staff for help and later check your contactless or Oyster account for an incomplete journey or maximum fare.
7. **Make a quick note only if it stays calm.** Note the station, date, time, gate number if visible, and the message shown on the reader. A note is enough; do not take photos if it would cause an argument or block the gate area.
8. **If you have to buy another ticket because there is no usable staff help, use an official channel and keep proof.** Keep the rejected ticket or pass, the new ticket, receipts, screenshots, and any account record so the operator or retailer can check it later.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim a refund, challenge a charge, or prove that you were right.
- You do not need to work out the exact gate fault while you are standing in the way of the barriers.
- You can deal with refunds, incomplete journeys, complaints, or penalty-fare disputes after you have got through the gateline and are calm.

## Important reassurance
Valid tickets and passes can be rejected by gates for ordinary reasons, including a faulty reader, a barcode glare problem, a ticket that needs manual checking, or contactless card clash. Asking staff to check and open a gate is common and does not automatically mean you have done anything wrong.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, focused on getting through the gates without making the situation worse. Later decisions about refunds, incomplete journeys, complaints, or penalty fares may need help from the train operator, station operator, TfL, the ticket retailer, or the Rail Ombudsman.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Gate behaviour, ticket validity, and staff processes can vary by operator, station, ticket type, and payment method. If you feel unsafe or threatened, move to a staffed area and ask for help.
