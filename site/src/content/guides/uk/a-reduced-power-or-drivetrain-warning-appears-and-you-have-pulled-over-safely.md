# What to do if… a “reduced power” or drivetrain warning appears and you have pulled over safely (UK)

## Short answer
Remain in your safe position and call for professional breakdown recovery. Do not attempt to continue your journey, as "reduced power" or "limp mode" typically indicates the vehicle has restricted its own operation to prevent catastrophic mechanical failure or safety hazards.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to drive to the next junction or service station if you have already reached a safe stopping point.
- Do not place a warning triangle if you are on a motorway or a high-speed dual carriageway.
- Do not repeatedly restart the engine to try and clear the warning.
- Do not open the bonnet or stand near the vehicle if you are on a motorway verge or hard shoulder.
- Do not stand between your vehicle and oncoming traffic or behind the vehicle.
- Do not attempt to rejoin the motorway from an emergency area without contacting National Highways first.

## What to do now
1. **Ensure the vehicle is as visible and stable as possible.** Keep your hazard lights on and, if it is dark or visibility is poor, turn on your sidelights. Turn the front wheels toward the verge or away from the live lanes.
2. **Prioritise personal safety over the vehicle on high-speed roads.**
   - Exit the vehicle via the left-hand door (farthest from traffic).
   - Move well away from the carriageway and stay behind a safety barrier if one is present.
   - If you cannot safely exit or reach a place of relative safety, stay in the vehicle with your seatbelt fastened and call 999 immediately.
3. **Use the SOS telephone if you are in an emergency area.** These phones are located in the orange-paved bays on smart motorways and connect directly to the National Highways control room.
   - If the SOS phone is unavailable or broken, call National Highways on 0300 123 5000.
4. **Identify immediate "do not drive" hazards.** You must remain stationary and request emergency recovery if you notice:
   - Visible smoke, steam, or a smell of burning.
   - Fluid leaking rapidly from under the engine or transmission.
   - Red warning symbols appearing alongside the text message.
   - Loss of power steering or braking assistance.
5. **Contact your breakdown provider with specific details.** Provide the exact wording of the dashboard message and describe any physical symptoms, such as vibrations, unusual noises, or specific smells.
6. **Note your exact location to assist recovery teams.** Look for marker posts (small boards at the side of the road with numbers), junction names, or direction of travel (e.g., M1 Northbound between Junction 14 and 15).
7. **Follow control room instructions for rejoining traffic.** If you are in an emergency area, you must not leave until the National Highways operator confirms it is safe. They may close the nearside lane (Red X) to allow you to build up speed safely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific mechanical cause (e.g., sensor failure vs. turbocharger issue) at the roadside.
- You do not need to check oil or coolant levels while in a high-risk area like a motorway verge.
- You do not need to decide which garage will perform the final repairs or research part costs.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious when a vehicle suddenly loses power or displays a drivetrain warning. These systems are designed to protect you and the vehicle by slowing down before a total failure occurs. Pulling over and staying put is the standard safety procedure for this type of malfunction.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and safety steps after stopping with a drivetrain or reduced power warning. It does not provide mechanical diagnostics or repair instructions.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance in a breakdown situation. Motorway and high-speed road rules are governed by the Highway Code. Always follow the directions of police, traffic officers, or National Highways control room staff over general advice.
