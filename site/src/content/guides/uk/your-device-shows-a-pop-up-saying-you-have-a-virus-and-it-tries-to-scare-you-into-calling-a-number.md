# What to do if… your device shows a pop-up saying you have a virus and it tries to scare you into calling a number (UK)

## Short answer
Do not enter credentials for any account you do not recognise. Confirm if the app was originally downloaded using a different personal account, a family member's account, or a work profile before taking further action.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t enter your password into any pop-up that displays an email address you do not own or recognise.
- Don’t factory reset your device immediately; this can trigger activation locks that make recovery much harder.
- Don’t remove device management profiles on a work or school device without consulting your IT department first.
- Don’t download "fix" tools or third-party app stores that claim to resolve update errors.
- Don’t provide remote access to your device to anyone claiming to be "support" over the phone or via a pop-up.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the account currently signed into your device store.** **Open the App Store (iOS) or Play Store (Android) and tap your profile icon to see which email address is currently active.**
   - If the email shown is correct but the prompt shows a different one, the specific app was likely purchased or downloaded under that other account.
2. **Check for Family Sharing or shared account history.** **Confirm if the app was originally installed by a family member or using an old email address you no longer use.**
   - If you recognise the account as yours or a family member's, you may sign in to update it or delete the app and reinstall it using your current account.
3. **Identify if the device is under management.** **Check your settings for any "Management" or "MDM" profiles that may be controlling app distribution.**
   - On iPhone/iPad: Go to **Settings > General > VPN & Device Management**.
   - On Android: Go to **Settings > Google > Work Profile** or check for a "Work" tab in your app drawer.
4. **Isolate the affected apps.** **Determine if the prompt happens for every app or just one specific title.**
   - If it is only one app, the safest solution is to delete that app and download it fresh using your own account.
   - Note that deleting the app will remove its local data unless that data is synced to a cloud service.
5. **Secure your primary account if you suspect a compromise.** **If an unfamiliar email appears in your main system settings (not just the App Store), sign out of all other sessions and change your password from a different, trusted device.**
   - Check your "Recovery" phone number and email address to ensure they have not been changed.
6. **Report suspected cyber crime if you have lost money or access.** **Use the official UK reporting channels if you believe your device has been remotely compromised or if you entered payment details into a fake prompt.**
   - In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland: Report via the **Report Fraud** website or call 0300 123 2040.
   - In Scotland: Report to **Police Scotland via 101** or use the Police Scotland online crime reporting form.
7. **Contact your bank immediately if financial details were exposed.** **If you entered your credit card or bank login into a prompt you now believe was fraudulent, use the number on the back of your card to freeze your accounts.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to delete your entire account history today.
- You do not need to replace your hardware or device.
- You do not need to contact the police if no personal data was stolen and no money was lost.
- You do not need to fix every individual app immediately; you can continue using the device for other tasks safely once you have confirmed your main account is secure.

## Important reassurance
Seeing an unfamiliar email prompt is often caused by "legacy" apps—apps downloaded years ago on a different account that stayed on your phone during a data transfer. It does not automatically mean you have been hacked. By following these verification steps, you can identify the source without risking your data.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for account-related update prompts. It does not cover full device forensic recovery or advanced network security. If you confirm an unauthorized management profile is active on a personal device, seek professional technical support.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is not a substitute for professional technical or legal advice. Software interfaces change frequently; always prioritize official support documentation from Apple or Google. If you are in immediate danger of identity theft or financial loss, contact your local authorities.
