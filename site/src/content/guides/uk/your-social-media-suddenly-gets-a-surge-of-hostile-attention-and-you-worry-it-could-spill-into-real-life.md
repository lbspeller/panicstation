# What to do if… your social media suddenly gets a surge of hostile attention and you worry it could spill into real life (UK)

## Short answer
Cease all engagement immediately and activate platform-level safety tools to restrict public access to your profiles. If you encounter credible threats or believe your physical location is compromised, notify the police via 101, or 999 if you are in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t respond, retaliate, or post defensive updates, as this provides fresh content for harassers to interact with.
- Don’t post real-time locations, landmarks, or identifiable routines that could be used to track your physical movements.
- Don’t delete the abusive content immediately; you must preserve evidence first for potential police or legal action.
- Don’t attempt to negotiate with or "reason" with anonymous accounts or coordinated groups.
- Don’t go to any physical location to confront or meet someone claiming they want to "settle the issue".
- Don’t ignore your physical security if your address or workplace has been mentioned or leaked.

## What to do now
1. **Initiate a complete digital pause.**  
   **Immediately stop reading comments and stop refreshing notifications to break the cycle of high-stress input.** Set your device aside for a few minutes to lower your immediate physiological response before taking technical steps.

2. **Notify a trusted contact in the physical world.**  
   **Tell a friend, family member, or colleague what is happening and confirm your current location.** If you feel vulnerable at home, arrange for someone to stay with you or relocate to a trusted environment until you have secured your accounts.

3. **Activate maximum privacy settings on all accounts.**  
   **Switch every active profile to "Private" or "Followers Only" mode immediately to stop the influx of new hostile messages.**
   - Restrict Direct Messages (DMs) to "people I follow" or disable them entirely.
   - Use the 2026 "Pile-on Mitigation" tools now required by Ofcom on major platforms to auto-filter or hide coordinated mentions.
   - Disable tagging, mentions, and the ability for strangers to comment on your existing posts.

4. **Sanitise your public-facing information.**  
   **Remove any bio text, pinned posts, or links that mention your employer, school, city, or professional associations.** 
   - Scan your most recent 10-15 posts for location tags or visual cues (street signs, house numbers) and delete or archive them.
   - Ask friends and family to avoid tagging you or engaging with the hostile surge on your behalf.

5. **Document the harassment systematically.**  
   **Capture screenshots of the most serious threats, doxxing (leaking of personal info), or stalking behavior, ensuring usernames and timestamps are visible.** 
   - Store these in a dedicated folder or use an evidence-preservation tool like Hollie Guard.
   - Prioritize capturing content that implies real-world harm or reveals your location.

6. **Utilise official reporting channels.**  
   **Report serious violations—such as doxxing, intimate image abuse, or threats of violence—directly to the platform and the True Vision police portal.**
   - Under the Online Safety Act (2026), platforms are required to prioritize and remove "Priority Offences" like intimate image abuse or credible threats.
   - If the harassment is motivated by race, religion, sexual orientation, or disability, report it as a hate crime via the True Vision website.

7. **Contact the police if you feel unsafe.**  
   **Call 101 to report harassment and establish a crime reference number if you believe a law is being broken.**
   - **Call 999** immediately if someone is at your door, you are being followed, or you receive a specific, imminent threat to your life or safety.
   - Provide the police with the evidence you captured and inform them if your home or work address is circulating online.

8. **Secure your physical environment.**  
   **Ensure all doors and windows are locked and be cautious about answering the door to unexpected callers or deliveries.** If your workplace is identifiable, discreetly notify your manager or security team so they can monitor for unusual inquiries or visitors.

## What can wait
- Explaining your side of the story or issuing a public apology.
- Determining exactly who started the surge or why it happened.
- Deciding whether to permanently delete your social media presence.
- Initiating long-term legal claims or civil defamation suits.
- Reading through every quote-post or mention to see what people are saying.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of intense vulnerability, nausea, or a racing heart is a normal physiological response to a sudden digital attack. Your brain is processing a high volume of hostility as a physical threat. Taking the step to lock down your accounts and notify a real-world contact is the most effective way to regain control of the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. If harassment continues or escalates into physical stalking or persistent doxxing, you may require specialized support from a stalking advocacy service or professional security advice.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and is not a substitute for legal advice or formal police guidance. If you are in immediate danger, always contact the emergency services.
