# What to do if… your steering shakes or the car pulls after hitting a pothole (UK)

## Short answer
Treat shaking or pulling after a pothole as a potentially unsafe fault. Do not rejoin traffic at normal speed; arrange breakdown or recovery unless you can safely move only a very short distance to a safer place.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “test it” by accelerating to see if it clears at speed.
- Do not keep driving if there is any tyre sidewall bulge or cut, a flat or rapidly deflating tyre, a cracked or bent wheel, fluid leaking, or the steering feels loose.
- Do not attempt roadside repairs or checks close to traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Do not continue a long journey “gently” if the car is pulling, vibrating, clunking, or grinding.
- Do not assume it is “just alignment” if the steering wheel shakes or the car pulls after an impact.

## What to do now
1. **Keep yourself away from traffic first.**
   - Keep hazard warning lights on.
   - Put on high-visibility clothing if you have it and it is within easy reach.
   - If you are on a motorway or other high-speed road and it is safe to leave the vehicle, exit by the side away from traffic and wait well away from the vehicle, behind a barrier if there is one.
   - If you are stopped in a live lane, cannot get to a safer place, or feel at immediate risk, stay belted, keep hazards on, call **999**, and ask for the police.

2. **Do a quick, no-tools walk-around check only if it is safe.**
   - Tyres: look for bulges, splits, cuts, exposed cords, or a tyre that looks flatter than the others.
   - Wheels and rims: look for fresh bends, cracks, or missing chunks.
   - Under the front of the car: look for new fluid leaks.
   - If you see any of these, do not drive. Arrange recovery.

3. **If nothing obvious is visible, only move the car if it reduces danger.**
   - Move very slowly and only as far as needed, such as into a nearby lay-by, service area, quiet side road, or car park.
   - This is not a test drive.
   - If the steering still shakes, the car pulls, you hear clunks or grinding, or the steering wheel is not centred as normal, stop again as soon as safe and do not continue.

4. **Arrange recovery or a professional check.**
   - Use your breakdown provider if you have one.
   - Say: “pothole impact”, “steering shakes or pulls”, and describe any visible tyre, wheel, fluid, clunking, grinding, or braking symptoms.
   - If you do not have breakdown cover, arrange recovery to a garage or tyre centre rather than driving on.

5. **Use location information if you need help.**
   - Use the road name, direction of travel, nearby junction, service area, landmark, marker post, or driver location sign.
   - If you can safely use an emergency roadside telephone, it can help identify your location.
   - If you are in an emergency area on a motorway, follow the operator’s advice before driving out of it.

6. **Record basic details only while already safe.**
   - Photograph visible tyre or wheel damage if you can do it without going near traffic.
   - Note the road name, direction of travel, nearest junction or landmark, date, and time.
   - Do not walk back along a motorway or high-speed road to photograph the pothole.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who will pay, whether to claim, or whether to use insurance right now.
- You do not need to diagnose the exact mechanical cause at the roadside.
- You do not need to complete the journey today if recovery is safer.
- You do not need to collect pothole evidence from the road if doing so would put you near traffic.

## Important reassurance
A pothole impact can cause damage that is not obvious from the driver’s seat, including tyre, wheel, alignment, steering, suspension, or brake-related problems. Stopping and choosing recovery when the car shakes or pulls is a reasonable safety decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A garage, tyre specialist, recovery service, or insurer may need to help with later decisions.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not mechanical, legal, insurance, financial, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe where you are stopped, prioritise personal safety and emergency services.
