# What to do if… the clutch pedal feels abnormal before driving and you are unsure you can control the vehicle safely (UK)

## Short answer
Do not attempt to drive the vehicle on public roads. Keep the car stationary with the handbrake applied and arrange for a professional mechanical inspection or recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "test" the vehicle in traffic, on hills, or near other road users.
- Do not rely on "pumping" the pedal to restore pressure for a journey; this indicates a hydraulic failure that may worsen suddenly.
- Do not force the gear stick if it resists movement, as this can cause permanent gearbox damage.
- Do not ignore a change in the "bite point" position even if the car still moves.
- Do not drive if you have any doubt about your ability to stop or pull away safely.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle immediately.** **Apply the handbrake firmly, ensure the gear lever is in neutral, and switch off the engine if it is running.**
2. **Check for physical obstructions around the pedals.** **Look under the dashboard for a displaced floor mat, loose objects, or debris that might be trapped behind or underneath the clutch pedal.**
   - Remove any obstruction and depress the pedal fully to see if normal travel is restored.
3. **Conduct a static pedal test with the engine off.** **Press the clutch pedal several times and observe the resistance and return speed.**
   - Stop if the pedal feels "spongy," has no resistance, stays on the floor, or returns slowly.
4. **Test gear selection while stationary.** **With the handbrake on, start the engine and attempt to select first and reverse gears.**
   - If you hear grinding (crunching) or the stick will not enter the gear smoothly, the clutch is not fully disengaging; switch off the engine immediately.
5. **Check for external signs of a hydraulic leak.** **Look at the ground directly beneath the engine bay and check the carpet inside the footwell near the pedal for damp patches or oily fluid.**
   - If your car uses a shared reservoir for brake and clutch fluid, check the level; a low level is a critical safety failure affecting both systems.
6. **Verify the vehicle's safety recall status.** **Use the GOV.UK "Check if a vehicle has been recalled" service using your registration number.**
   - If an outstanding recall exists for the clutch or transmission, follow the manufacturer's instructions exactly and do not drive.
7. **Arrange for professional recovery or mobile repair.** **Contact your breakdown provider or a local garage to have the vehicle towed for inspection.**
   - Inform the provider that the vehicle has a "primary control failure" to ensure appropriate recovery equipment is sent.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical cause (e.g., slave cylinder vs. pressure plate) right now.
- You do not need to decide on a repair centre immediately if the car is currently in a safe, legal parking spot.
- You do not need to check the gearbox fluid or perform any under-car inspections yourself.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel frustrated or uncertain when a vehicle control behaves unexpectedly. Choosing to remain stationary is a proactive safety decision that prevents potential loss of control or a breakdown in a dangerous location.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected clutch failure before driving. It does not cover roadside repairs or long-term maintenance.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the advice of a qualified mechanic. A faulty clutch can lead to a total loss of drive or the inability to stop the engine without stalling, which is dangerous in traffic. Always prioritise professional assessment if vehicle controls feel abnormal.
