# What to do if… someone approaches your car at a fuel pump and keeps trying to distract you from your wallet or phone (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate your valuables and lock the vehicle immediately to prevent access. Stop the conversation, move to a safe location like the shop or the locked driver’s seat, and do not re-engage with the individual.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pull out your wallet or phone to show the person information, check a map, or prove you have no cash.
- Don’t place your phone or wallet on the car roof, the passenger seat, or the pump ledge while you speak.
- Don’t leave your car keys in the ignition or on the seat while you are standing at the pump.
- Don’t allow the person to lead you away from the driver’s door or out of sight of your open windows.
- Don’t move the vehicle while the fuel nozzle is still inserted in the tank.
- Don’t attempt to physically block or chase the person if they grab an item and run.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your valuables on your person immediately.** Place your wallet and phone in a zipped pocket or inside your clothing rather than leaving them on a seat or the car roof. **Do not continue the conversation while your items are exposed.**
2. **Lock all vehicle doors and close any open windows right now.** Use your key fob or the internal central locking button even if you are still standing next to the car. **Pay particular attention to the passenger side door, as thieves often use a distraction on the driver's side to allow an accomplice to open the far door.**
3. **Step back to a position where you can see both sides of your vehicle.** Maintain a distance of at least two metres from the person while keeping the pump and your car doors in your line of sight. **Keep your hands visible and free of items.**
4. **Enter the petrol station shop or the locked driver’s seat to create a physical barrier.** If you feel vulnerable, walk directly into the kiosk and stand near the staff counter. **If you stay in the car, ensure all doors are locked and windows are fully up while you observe the surroundings.**
5. **Terminate the refuelling process only if it is safe to do so.** If you are mid-fill, click the nozzle shut, return it to the pump cradle, and ensure the fuel cap is replaced before locking yourself in the car. **Never drive away with the nozzle still attached to the vehicle.**
6. **Call for assistance based on the level of threat.** 
   - **Call 999** if the person is acting aggressively, blocking your exit, threatening you, or if a theft is currently in progress.
   - **Call 101** if the person has left the area and you are safe, but you wish to report suspicious behaviour or an attempted theft.
7. **Verify your belongings once you are in a secure, locked environment.** Check for your phone, wallet, and keys only after you have locked the car doors or entered the shop. **If an item is missing, use a secure phone to contact your bank and freeze your accounts immediately.**

## What can wait
- Deciding whether the interaction was a genuine mistake or a planned theft.
- Reviewing your bank statements for older transactions.
- Checking your vehicle for minor scratches or "marks" the stranger may have pointed out.
- Formally requesting CCTV footage from the station manager (the police will handle this if a crime is reported).

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel startled, confused, or even rude when someone approaches you unexpectedly in a high-task environment like a fuel pump. Criminals rely on social politeness to create the distraction they need. Prioritising your physical space and the security of your vehicle is a standard safety response and does not require an apology.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active distraction attempt at a UK fuel forecourt. It does not cover long-term fraud recovery or legal proceedings following a completed theft.

## Important note
This information is for immediate safety guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Jurisdictional procedures for reporting crime in the UK may vary slightly by local force policy. Always follow the instructions of emergency dispatchers if you call 999.
