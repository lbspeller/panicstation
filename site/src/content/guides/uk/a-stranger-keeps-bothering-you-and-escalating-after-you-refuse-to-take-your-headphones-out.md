# What to do if… a stranger keeps bothering you and escalating after you refuse to take your headphones out (UK)

## Short answer
Create distance and move toward a safer, busier place with staff or other people now. If you feel in immediate danger, they are blocking you, threatening you, touching you, or refusing to let you leave, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not take your headphones out to be polite if your instincts say no.
- Do not let them draw you into a debate, explanation, apology, or “just one second” conversation.
- Do not go somewhere quieter, around a corner, into a doorway, down steps, or away from other people to talk.
- Do not stop where you can be boxed in, such as between walls, railings, parked cars or a platform edge.
- Do not unlock your phone, open your wallet, or show personal information near them.
- Do not walk straight toward home if they are matching your direction; change direction into a staffed, public place instead.

## What to do now
1. **Change the space around you.** Step sideways and back to widen distance, then start moving toward a staffed, well-lit, busy place such as a shop counter, café, station office, busier carriage, hotel reception or open business. Aim for people, cameras and staff.
2. **Use one firm, short line.** Keep your voice steady and use as few words as possible: “No.” “Stop.” “Please step back.” If they keep coming, use: “I’m not talking. I’m leaving.”
3. **Make it visible to others.** Turn your body toward other people, not toward the stranger. If you can, address one specific person: “Hi, can I stand with you? This person won’t leave me alone.”
4. **Go to staff quickly.** If you are in a bar, club, café or similar venue, go straight to staff and say plainly: “I feel unsafe. Can you help me get away from this person?” In participating venues, asking for “Angela” can be a discreet request for help, but plain words are enough.
5. **Use your phone while you keep moving.** Call someone and put them on speaker: “I’m at [place]. Someone is bothering me. Stay on the line.” Avoid stopping to type a long message while you are still exposed.
6. **If you are on the rail network, use rail help routes.** Move toward rail staff, security, a help point, or a busier carriage or platform area. If it is not an emergency, you can text British Transport Police on 61016 or call 0800 40 50 40 with your location, train or station, and a brief description. Texting 61016 may cost a small amount depending on your mobile provider.
7. **Call 999 if the risk rises.** Call 999 if they are following closely, blocking your way, grabbing you, threatening you, a crime is happening, or you feel in immediate danger. Say where you are, what they are doing, and what you need: “I’m being harassed and followed. I feel unsafe. I need police help.”

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether it “counts” as a crime right now.
- You do not need to confront them, educate them, or prove that you are right.
- You do not need to write a perfect account before getting to safety.
- You do not need to post online or message multiple people while you are still exposed.
- You do not need to go home by the quickest route if a staffed public place is closer and safer.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, second-guessing yourself, or feeling guilty for seeming rude are common reactions. Someone escalating after you say no is enough reason to create distance, involve other people, and prioritise your safety.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few minutes. If this becomes repeated behaviour, they find you again, contact you, or you keep seeing them, later decisions may need specialist safety, police, legal or support advice.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, security, medical, therapeutic, financial or other professional advice. If you feel at risk, prioritise getting to a safer place and contacting emergency services.
