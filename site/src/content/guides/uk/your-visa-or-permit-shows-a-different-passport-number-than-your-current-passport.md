# What to do if… your visa or permit shows a different passport number than your current passport (UK)

## Short answer
Pause before relying on the document for travel, work, or renting until you know whether the record is correctly linked to an older passport or contains an actual error. If you use an eVisa, update or add your current passport or travel document in your UKVI account, and report wrong eVisa details through the official error route.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to travel “hoping it’ll be fine” once a mismatch has been flagged, unless you have checked the official route for your exact document and have the documents it asks for.
- Do not edit, annotate, or recreate visa, permit, eVisa, share-code, or passport evidence yourself.
- Do not discard your old passport if it contains a valid UK visa, stamp, or vignette.
- Do not submit a new visa application in panic unless an official route or qualified adviser tells you to; it may complicate the situation.
- Do not hand your passport or UKVI account access to unofficial agents who promise a quick fix.

## What to do now
1. **Write down exactly what is not matching.**  
   Note the passport number shown on the visa, permit, eVisa, or check result; your current passport number; and who flagged the mismatch, such as an airline, employer, landlord, carrier, or border officer. If allowed, keep a clear screenshot or photo of the message.

2. **Check whether the number belongs to an older passport.**  
   Compare the visa or permit record with your previous passports. If the number is from an expired passport that contains a valid UK visa, GOV.UK says you can use that valid visa when travelling to and from the UK if you travel with both the expired passport and your new passport. If the number is not from any passport you have held, treat it as a possible record error.

3. **If you have an eVisa, update or add your current passport or travel document.**  
   Sign in to your UKVI account and update your passport or travel document details before relying on the record for travel, work, rent, or another check. You may need the document you use to sign in and access to the email address or phone number on the account.

4. **If the eVisa information itself is wrong or unusable, report the error.**  
   Use the official GOV.UK eVisa error route if your eVisa shows wrong details, such as an incorrect name, date of birth, nationality, or immigration status. Use the same route if you can view your eVisa but cannot get a share code, cannot view your eVisa because of a technical error while signed in, or have never been able to sign in to a UKVI account set up by the Home Office.

5. **If you need to prove your status, use the official view-and-prove route.**  
   If you can access your eVisa, generate a fresh share code where the check asks for one. A share code is time-limited, so use the current code for the specific check rather than relying on an old saved code.

6. **If travel is soon, gather only the proof that matters now.**  
   Keep your current passport, any old passport containing the visa or vignette, your Home Office grant email or decision letter, and any current eVisa or share-code evidence you can access. Use the same spelling, date of birth, nationality, and passport details consistently when speaking to the airline, carrier, or official check route.

7. **If you are abroad and stuck, stay with official support routes.**  
   If you cannot update your UKVI account, cannot access your eVisa, cannot get a share code, or the mismatch is stopping travel, use the official GOV.UK update, error, view-and-prove, or transfer or replace a visa route. Keep a short timeline of dates, flights, account attempts, and who told you what.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to replace, transfer, or reapply for anything until you know whether this is an old-passport link, an eVisa account update, or a record error.
- You do not need to argue in detail at a desk or in a queue; focus on consistent documents, the old and current passport where relevant, and starting the official correction route.
- You do not need every historic immigration paper straight away; start with your current passport, any old passport with the visa or vignette, your grant decision, and UKVI account access.

## Important reassurance
This can happen after a passport renewal because older visa evidence or digital status checks may still refer to an older identity document. A mismatch is stressful, but it is often something to sort by checking the old passport, updating the UKVI account, using a current share code, or reporting a specific eVisa error.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, avoid avoidable travel or status-check problems, and start the official correction route. Later decisions about replacing, transferring, or challenging a record may need specialist immigration advice for your exact status and travel plans.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, or professional advice. Requirements can vary by your immigration route, the document you hold, and whether you are inside or outside the UK. If your identity documents and permission record still do not line up, avoid relying on them for travel until you have used the relevant official route or received clear advice you can rely on.
