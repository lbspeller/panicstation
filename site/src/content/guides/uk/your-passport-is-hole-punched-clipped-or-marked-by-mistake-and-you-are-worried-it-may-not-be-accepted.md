# What to do if… your passport is hole-punched, clipped, or marked by mistake and you are worried it may not be accepted (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the passport as invalid for travel and **apply for an urgent replacement through the 1-week Fast Track service immediately**. Do not attempt to use the document at an airport or border, as carriers are legally required to refuse boarding for passports that appear cancelled or significantly altered.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to repair the document with tape, glue, or laminating sheets.
- Do not trim the edges or cut more of the document to "clean up" a clip or mark.
- Do not use chemical cleaners, erasers, or white-out to remove ink stains or accidental marks.
- Do not book travel or travel to the airport hoping for staff discretion; airline staff face fines for boarding passengers with damaged documents.
- Do not attempt to use the 1-day Premium service, as this is only for undamaged renewals.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm if the damage meets HM Passport Office criteria for a replacement.** **HM Passport Office considers a passport damaged and unfit for travel if:**
   - There are holes, cuts, or rips in the cover or any page.
   - The corners of the cover or the bio-data page have been clipped or cut.
   - There are ink stains, water damage, or chemical marks on any page.
   - Personal details or the machine-readable zone (the two lines of text at the bottom of the photo page) are illegible.
   - The laminate on the photo page is peeling or raised.

2. **Secure a 1-week Fast Track appointment as soon as possible.** **You must use the 1-week Fast Track service to replace a damaged passport; you cannot use the 1-day Premium service for this.**
   - Visit GOV.UK to book an appointment at one of the seven UK passport offices (London, Liverpool, Peterborough, Newport, Durham, Glasgow, or Belfast).
   - Collect a paper application form from a Post Office.
   - The cost in 2026 is £178 for an adult and £145 for a child.
   - You must bring your damaged passport and two printed photos to the appointment.

3. **Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if you are currently outside the UK.** **If you are abroad and need to travel within 6 weeks, use the online ETD application service.**
   - This service costs £125.
   - It is generally available only if you have had a UK passport issued on or after 1 January 2006.
   - The document is usually valid for a single or return journey through a maximum of 5 countries.

4. **Document the current state of the passport for your records.** **Take high-resolution photographs of the front cover, the photo page, and the specific area where the hole, clip, or mark is located.**
   - These photos serve as evidence of the condition at the time of your replacement application.
   - Keep a note of any upcoming flight references or hotel bookings to provide to the passport office if they ask about the urgency.

5. **Notify your travel insurer of a potential claim for disruption.** **If you have to cancel or delay travel due to the accidental damage, check your policy for "loss of travel documents" or "document damage" coverage.**
   - Most policies require you to take "reasonable care" of the document, but some cover accidental damage occurring shortly before travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine who was at fault for the mark or hole right now; obtaining a valid document is the priority.
- You do not need to cancel your entire trip immediately; wait until you see the available Fast Track appointment dates.
- You do not need to contact the airline to "verify" if they will accept it; their standard response will be to follow HMPO guidelines, which define these marks as damage.

## Important reassurance
Discovering a mark or hole in a passport is a high-stress event, particularly when travel is imminent. It is common for users to feel a sense of urgency or frustration when a document appears "mostly functional." Using the official Fast Track or ETD routes is the standard, reliable method for resolving this and ensuring you are not turned away at the boarding gate.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and the identification of the correct UK replacement routes for physically altered passports. It does not cover long-term compensation claims, visa transfers to new documents, or complex dual-citizenship entry requirements.

## Important note
Airlines and border authorities have strict legal obligations to prevent travel on documents that appear tampered with, cancelled (by hole-punching or clipping), or damaged. Even if a mark was made by mistake, the physical presence of the mark can trigger automated and manual security flags.
