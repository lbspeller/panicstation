# What to do if… someone claims they will post explicit content about you using your name and social media handles (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all communication immediately, document the threat with screenshots, and report the incident to both the platform and the Revenge Porn Helpline (0345 6000 459). Under 2026 UK law, threatening to share intimate images is a priority criminal offence, and platforms are required to act on removals.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pay any money, send more images, or negotiate with the person making the threat.
- Do not delete the messages or the person's profile before you have taken screenshots as evidence.
- Do not attempt to "reason" with them or explain the impact, as this often reinforces their leverage.
- Do not click on any links or download files they send, as these may contain malware to steal more data.
- Do not post public "warnings" about the person on your own social media, which may inadvertently spread the threat.
- Do not panic-delete your entire social media account; deactivating is safer if you want to preserve data for the police.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise your physical safety and distance.** If you are in immediate danger or believe the person knows your physical location and is coming to find you, **call 999**.
2. **Collect evidence of the threat immediately.** Take **screenshots** that clearly show the person’s username, their profile URL, the date and time, and the specific threat they made (including mentions of your name or handles).
   - If they have sent a list of your followers or friends as a threat, capture that list too.
   - Save these images to a secure folder or a device that the perpetrator cannot access.
3. **Stop all communication and block the accounts.** Once the evidence is captured, **mute or block** the individual on all platforms and do not respond to further messages.
4. **Lock down your social media privacy settings.** Set your profiles to **private**, restrict who can tag or mention you, and disable direct messages from people you do not follow.
   - Change your passwords and enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** if you suspect they have accessed your accounts.
   - Review your "Following" and "Followers" lists and consider hiding them if the platform allows.
5. **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline for specialist support.** Call **0345 6000 459** (open Monday to Friday, 10am–4pm) or use their anonymous "Whisper" tool online for help with content removal and reporting.
6. **Use the StopNCII.org tool if you have the images in question.** This tool allows you to create a "digital fingerprint" (hash) of the images on your own device so that participating platforms can **block them from being uploaded**.
   - Note: The images themselves are never uploaded to the tool; only the hash is shared with platforms.
7. **Report the threat to the platform(s) using their internal tools.** Report specifically for **"Intimate Image Abuse," "Harassment," or "Blackmail."**
   - Under the Online Safety Act (2026 update), regulated platforms are legally required to remove flagged non-consensual intimate images within 48 hours.
8. **Report to the police if you wish to pursue criminal charges.** Call **101** or use your local police force's online reporting portal to report "threats to share intimate images."
   - If you are under 18, or the images were taken when you were under 18, report via the **CEOP Safety Centre**.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide today whether you will tell your employer, school, or family.
- You do **not** need to perform a "deep web" search for your name right now.
- You do **not** need to identify exactly who the person is if they are using an anonymous account; the police and platforms handle identification.
- You do **not** need to draft a public statement or explanation.

## Important reassurance
Threats involving intimate images are designed to create an immediate sense of shame and urgency. Feeling sick, shaky, or unable to think clearly is a standard physiological response to this type of digital violence. These threats are common tactics used by perpetrators to gain control, and there are established legal and technical systems in the UK specifically designed to protect you and stop the spread of content.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active threat. It focuses on immediate harm reduction, evidence preservation, and connecting to UK-specific removal and legal services. It does not cover long-term legal representation or psychological therapy.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Threatening to share intimate images is a criminal offence in the UK under the Online Safety Act 2023. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
