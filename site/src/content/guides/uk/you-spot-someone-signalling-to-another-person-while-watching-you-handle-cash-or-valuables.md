# What to do if… you spot someone signalling to another person while watching you handle cash or valuables (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the transaction immediately, secure all items in a bag or pocket, and move directly to a staffed, well-lit indoor location like a shop or bank. Call 999 if you believe a robbery is imminent or a crime is in progress.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t continue counting money or display the contents of your bag/wallet.
- Don’t confront the individuals or try to "stare them down" to show you have noticed.
- Don’t move into isolated areas like side streets, alleyways, or quiet car parks to get away.
- Don’t use your phone to film the suspects if it prevents you from moving to safety.
- Don’t walk to your car or home while you suspect you are being followed.
- Don’t assume the threat has passed just because the individuals have moved out of your direct line of sight.

## What to do now
1. **Secure all cash and valuables out of sight immediately.** Close your wallet, zip your bag, and hold it firmly to your front. If you are at an ATM, cancel the transaction if possible, or take the card and cash and put them away before turning around.
2. **Move to the nearest staffed "anchor point" without delay.** Enter a busy shop, bank branch, staffed petrol station, or approach a security guard/concierge. Stay in the sightline of staff and cameras once inside.
3. **Inform a member of staff or security about the situation.** Use a direct statement: "I am being watched and followed by people signalling to each other; I need to stay here until it is safe."
4. **Use British Transport Police channels if you are on the rail network.** **Text 61016 or call 0800 40 50 40 to report suspicious behavior on trains or at stations in Great Britain.** This is for non-emergency reports where you feel uncomfortable or see "team" behavior.
5. **Call 999 if you are in immediate danger or a crime is happening.** **Dial 999 if someone is blocking your path, threatening you, or attempting to take your property.**
6. **Call 101 to report the incident if you are now in a safe place.** **Provide descriptions of the individuals and the specific location so police can check local CCTV.**
7. **Arrange for a safe exit from your secure location.** **Ask staff to call a registered taxi to pick you up directly from the entrance, or wait for a friend or family member to meet you inside.** Do not walk alone if you still feel observed.
8. **Check for your belongings only once you are behind a locked door or with staff.** **If you realize a card or phone is missing, use a secure device to freeze your accounts and report the theft to your bank immediately.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the "intent" of the people watching you; prioritize your safety over accuracy.
- You do not need to take photos or gather evidence yourself; let professional CCTV handle recording.
- You do not need to finish your original errand or shopping trip right now.
- You do not need to decide whether to press charges or give a full statement in the first few minutes.

## Important reassurance
Recognizing coordinated behavior like signalling is a vital safety skill. Moving to a staffed area and securing your items is a standard, low-risk response to a red flag. Your intuition regarding your personal space and property is a valid tool for harm prevention.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for the minutes following a suspicious encounter. It does not replace professional security advice or legal reporting procedures for completed crimes.

## Important note
This information is for general safety awareness and does not constitute legal advice. In any situation where you feel threatened or a crime is occurring, contact the police via 999 (emergency) or 101 (non-emergency).
