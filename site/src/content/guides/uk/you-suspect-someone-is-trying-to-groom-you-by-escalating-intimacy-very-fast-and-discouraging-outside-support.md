# What to do if… you suspect someone is trying to groom you by escalating intimacy very fast and discouraging outside support (UK)

## Short answer
Slow the interaction down immediately and contact one trusted person or a specialist support service. Rapid escalation of intimacy, demands for secrecy, and attempts to isolate you from friends or family are significant indicators that you should step back and seek an outside perspective.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to keep messages, gifts, or the nature of the relationship secret to avoid conflict.
- Do not meet the person alone, travel with them, or visit their home while you feel pressured or unsure.
- Do not delete evidence, such as messages or call logs, if you might want them for a report or professional assessment later.
- Do not send personal documents, copies of your ID, bank details, or intimate images.
- Do not cut off contact with your GP, school staff, or family members because the person claims they are "against the relationship."
- Do not confront them about your suspicions if you feel they may become aggressive, retaliatory, or more manipulative.

## What to do now
1. **Tell one trusted person exactly what is happening today.** **Explain that the person is pushing for rapid intimacy, demanding secrecy, and discouraging you from speaking to others.** Ask this person to check in with you at specific times over the next 24 hours.

2. **Establish a physical and digital boundary immediately.** **Do not agree to private meetings, do not get into their vehicle, and do not move your conversation to encrypted or disappearing message apps.** You are entitled to pause contact without providing a detailed justification.

3. **Secure your private information and location settings.** **Turn off location sharing on all social media platforms and review who has access to your digital calendars, shared photos, or smart devices.** Change passwords if you suspect they have been compromised or shared.

4. **Contact a specialist support service for a confidential assessment.** **If you are 16 or over in England or Wales, call the 24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line on 0808 500 222; in Scotland, call 08088 01 03 02.** These services allow you to talk through your concerns without any obligation to report to the police.

5. **If you are under 18, speak to a safe adult or safeguarding professional now.** **This could be a teacher, a school safeguarding lead, a parent, or a social worker.**
   - Contact Childline on 0800 1111 for 24/7 confidential support.
   - Contact the NSPCC Helpline on 0808 800 5000 if you are worried about your safety or the safety of another child.

6. **Report online-specific concerns to CEOP.** **If the grooming behavior is happening via social media, gaming, or messaging apps, use the CEOP (Child Exploitation and Online Protection) reporting tool.** This notifies specialist police officers who deal with online exploitation.

7. **Record specific instances of pressure or isolation while they are fresh in your memory.** **Write down dates and descriptions of "love bombing," requests for sexual images, threats of self-harm if you leave, or attempts to stop you seeing friends.** Store this information in a password-protected note or give it to a trusted friend.

8. **Contact the "Report Fraud" service if there is a financial element.** **If the person is asking for money or gifts, or if you suspect a romance scam, call 0300 123 2040 or use the reportfraud.police.uk portal.** Note that this service replaced Action Fraud in early 2026.

9. **Use the 999 Silent Solution if you are in immediate danger and cannot speak.** **Dial 999 and, when prompted by the operator, press 55 to indicate you are in danger but cannot talk.**

10. **Consult a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) if any sexual contact has occurred.** **SARCs provide medical and emotional support 24/7, regardless of whether you choose to report the incident to the police.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to end the relationship permanently right now.
- You do not need to have a "perfect" or complete timeline of events before asking for help.
- You do not need to prove that the person’s intentions are harmful to justify stepping away.
- You do not need to decide today whether you want to involve the police or pursue a formal investigation.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a mix of flattery, guilt, confusion, and fear when intimacy escalates this quickly. Grooming tactics are specifically designed to make you doubt your instincts and feel dependent on the other person. Taking a pause and seeking support is a protective action, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions only. It does not replace professional safeguarding, legal advice, or therapeutic support.

## Important note
This information is provided for stabilization purposes and does not constitute legal or medical advice. If you are under 18, your safety is a priority and you should involve a local children's social care team or a trusted professional. If you are an adult, specialist charities can help you navigate your rights and safety options without pressure.
