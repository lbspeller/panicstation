# What to do if… your brake lights stay on after you turn the vehicle off and you worry the battery will drain (UK)

## Short answer
Get the vehicle to a safe parked position first, then stop the drain only in a way you can do confidently: usually by disconnecting the negative (-) battery terminal, or by getting breakdown assistance. Do not drive again until the brake lights work normally.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep pressing the brake pedal repeatedly in panic; it can make it harder to tell what is stuck.
- Don’t disconnect the positive (+) battery terminal first.
- Don’t leave the car like this if you can avoid it; the battery may drain.
- Don’t drive on public roads with brake lights that are stuck on, unreliable, or disabled because a fuse has been removed.
- Don’t attempt repairs at the roadside, on a motorway, or anywhere you are exposed to moving traffic.
- Don’t pull wiring or connectors under the dashboard if you are not sure what they are.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause first.** If you are in a live lane, on a motorway, or somewhere exposed to traffic, move left if you safely can, use hazard lights, and stop in a service area, emergency area, lay-by, hard shoulder, or other safer place.
2. **Secure the vehicle.** Put the handbrake on, select Park if automatic, or leave it in gear if manual, turn the ignition fully off, and remove the key if your vehicle has one.
3. **Check the simple causes.**
   - Move your foot fully away from the brake pedal.
   - Pull the brake pedal gently upward with your hand or toe.
   - Check that the driver’s floor mat, a bag, or loose item is not pressing the pedal.
4. **Check from outside if it is safe.** Stand where you are away from traffic and confirm that the brake lights, not just rear lights or sidelights, are still lit.
5. **Stop the drain if the brake lights are still on and the car is safely parked.**
   - Use the vehicle handbook if you have it.
   - If you are confident, disconnect the negative (-) battery terminal first. The negative terminal is usually marked with a minus sign and is often black or blue.
   - Loosen the negative terminal clamp, lift it clear, and tuck it aside so it cannot spring back onto the battery post.
   - Expect small resets such as the clock or radio settings.
6. **Use the fuse option only as a parked-only measure.** If the fuse-box diagram or handbook clearly identifies a stop lamp or brake light fuse, you may remove that fuse temporarily to stop the lights. Put the fuse somewhere safe and do not drive until it is refitted and the brake lights work normally.
7. **Call breakdown assistance if anything is unclear.** Say: “My brake lights stay on with the ignition off, and I need help preventing the battery from draining.”
8. **If you are still in danger or cannot make the vehicle safe, call 999 or 112.**
9. **Once safe and calm, check for a safety recall.** Use the official UK recall checker and follow the manufacturer’s instructions if an open recall applies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact part tonight.
- You do not need to buy a brake light switch now.
- You do not need to decide whether to keep, sell, or replace the vehicle now.
- You do not need to watch repair videos while you are tired or parked somewhere unsafe.
- The next useful decision is simply how to get the brake lights working normally before driving again.

## Important reassurance
This problem is often caused by a pedal, switch, fuse, or adjustment issue. Stopping the battery drain and avoiding unsafe driving are enough for the first step.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. A competent mechanic or breakdown technician may need to check the brake light switch, pedal return, wiring, fuse box, or vehicle-specific battery procedure before the car is driven again.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not professional mechanical, legal, financial, or emergency-service advice. Follow your vehicle handbook where it gives vehicle-specific instructions, and use breakdown or emergency help if the situation is unsafe or uncertain.
