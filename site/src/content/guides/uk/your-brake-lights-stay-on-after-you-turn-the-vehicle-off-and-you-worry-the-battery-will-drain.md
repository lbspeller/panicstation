# What to do if… your brake lights stay on after you turn the vehicle off and you worry the battery will drain (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the electrical drain immediately by disconnecting the negative battery terminal or removing the correct brake light fuse, then arrange a professional repair before driving the vehicle again.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive on public roads with brake lights stuck in the "on" position, as following drivers cannot tell when you are actually slowing down.
- Do not disconnect the positive (+) battery terminal first, as touching your tools to the car's metal body while doing so can cause a dangerous short circuit.
- Do not leave the lights on "just for tonight" if the battery is older; brake lights typically draw enough power to flatten a battery within a few hours.
- Do not attempt to "force" the brake pedal upward with excessive strength, which can damage the delicate switch or linkage.
- Do not remove any fuse unless you are certain it is for the "Stop Lamps" or "Brake Lights" using the official vehicle handbook.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe location and activate hazard lights immediately.** If you are on a motorway, try to exit at the next junction or service station; if you cannot, pull into an Emergency Area or as far left on the hard shoulder as possible.
2. **Secure the vehicle completely.** Ensure the handbrake is firmly on, the engine is off, and the keys are removed from the ignition.
3. **If you are stopped in a live motorway lane and cannot move, stay in the vehicle with your seatbelts on and call 999 immediately.** Do not attempt any repairs in a live traffic lane.
4. **Check for simple physical obstructions (30 seconds only).**
   - Look at the driver's footwell to ensure the floor mat is not wedged against the brake pedal arm.
   - Gently pull the brake pedal toward you with your hand to see if it has failed to return to its "home" position.
5. **Disconnect the negative (–) battery terminal to stop the drain.**
   - Open the bonnet and locate the battery (usually a black rectangular box).
   - Identify the negative terminal, which is typically marked with a minus (–) sign and has a black or blue cable.
   - Use a 10mm wrench (standard for most UK cars) to loosen the nut on the negative clamp and lift the cable clear of the terminal post.
   - Secure the cable so it cannot spring back and touch the terminal.
6. **Alternatively, pull the brake light fuse if you have the handbook.** Locate the fuse box (often under the dashboard or in the engine bay) and use the diagram to find the "Stop Lamp" or "Brake Light" fuse; pull it out to break the circuit.
7. **Contact a breakdown provider (AA, RAC, or Green Flag).** Inform them that your brake lights are stuck on and you have disconnected the battery/fuse to prevent a flat battery.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact mechanical failure (such as a perished brake switch stopper or a shorted wire) right now.
- You do not need to worry about resetting your car's digital clock or radio presets; these are secondary to preventing a dead battery.
- You do not need to book a garage appointment until you have spoken to a breakdown technician who can often provide a temporary roadside fix.

## Important reassurance
Brake lights staying on is a common mechanical or electrical fault, usually caused by a simple switch failure or a small plastic part breaking near the pedal. By disconnecting the battery or fuse now, you have prevented a flat battery and ensured the car will be ready for a technician to inspect.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. It focuses on preventing battery failure and ensuring scene safety. It does not provide instructions for the permanent replacement of brake light switches or complex electrical diagnostics.

## Important note
Vehicles with "Start-Stop" technology or high-voltage electric vehicle (EV) systems may have specific procedures for battery disconnection. If you are unsure or the battery is not easily accessible, do not attempt to disconnect it; instead, secure the car and call for professional breakdown assistance immediately.
