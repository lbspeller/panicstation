# What to do if… your mobility scooter stops working while you are out and you cannot walk home (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to the safest nearby stopping place you can, then arrange help to get both you and the scooter home. If you are on the Motability Scheme, call Motability Assist; otherwise call your own breakdown cover, insurer, dealer, support contact, or an accessible transport provider.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep trying to force the scooter to move if it is cutting out, jerking, or not responding.
- Don’t push the scooter in the road, across junctions, or along a narrow pavement where you could be forced into traffic.
- Don’t put yourself at risk trying to lift the scooter, remove a battery, or move it over kerbs alone.
- Don’t accept a lift with a stranger unless you can verify who they are, where they are taking you, and that someone you trust knows.
- Don’t leave the scooter unattended in a public place unless you have no safer option and have arranged where it will be collected.
- Don’t feel you must continue your journey if you are cold, overheated, dizzy, frightened, or running out of phone battery.

## What to do now
1. **Move only as far as needed to be safer.** Aim for a wide pavement, shop doorway, bus stop shelter, café, station, public building, or another visible place away from traffic and crowds.

2. **Make yourself visible and settled.** Put lights or hazard lights on if your scooter has them, use a reflective item if you have one, and sit or wait somewhere you are less likely to be knocked or blocked in.

3. **Do a brief simple-cause check only if it is safe and familiar.** Check the key or power switch, battery indicator, charger port cover, and whether a drive, neutral, or freewheel lever has been moved. If anything is unclear, stop checking and arrange help.

4. **Find your exact location before calling.** Use a nearby shop name, station name, street sign, postcode, map pin, or bus stop code. Ask a nearby business or passer-by to help confirm the address if needed.

5. **If you are on the Motability Scheme, call Motability Assist on 0800 953 5000.** Say that your scooter or powered wheelchair is undrivable, whether you are indoors or outdoors, your exact location, whether you are alone, and that walking home is not realistic.

6. **If you are not on Motability, call the help you already have first.** This may be your scooter insurer, breakdown cover, retailer, manufacturer, dealer, maintainer, or a trusted contact who can help arrange collection and transport.

7. **Ask a nearby business for practical help while you wait.** You can ask, “Could I wait just inside?” “Could you help me confirm this address?” or “Could I charge my phone enough to arrange transport?”

8. **Arrange transport that can take the scooter, not just you.** Ask specifically for a wheelchair-accessible taxi or private hire vehicle that can take a mobility scooter, and give the approximate scooter size, whether it folds or dismantles, and whether you can transfer to a seat.

9. **Use emergency help if the situation becomes unsafe.** Call 999 if you are in immediate danger, stuck in or next to traffic, at risk of violence, or seriously unwell. If you need police help or advice and it is not an emergency, call 101. If you need medical help now but it is not life-threatening, use the NHS urgent-help route for where you are; in England, use NHS 111 online or call 111.

10. **Once you are safely home or indoors, stop troubleshooting.** Turn the scooter off if you can, keep any fault messages or photos, and contact your dealer, maintainer, insurer, or breakdown provider for repair advice.

## What can wait
- You do not have to diagnose the fault while you are stranded.
- You do not have to decide today whether the scooter needs repair, replacement, servicing, or a complaint.
- You do not have to work out who is responsible for the cost while you are still outside.
- You do not have to keep appointments or finish the journey if doing so could leave you stuck again.
- You can sort out missed plans, charging routines, paperwork, and future backup arrangements after you are safe.

## Important reassurance
This is a real access problem, not an overreaction. When walking is not realistic, the priority is to stay safe, keep your phone usable, and get you and the scooter to a safe place.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the moment your scooter stops working while you are out. Later decisions about repair, insurance, replacement, complaints, or mobility support may need help from your dealer, insurer, Motability, local services, or another specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, transport, insurance, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe, at risk, or seriously unwell, treat that as urgent and seek immediate help.
