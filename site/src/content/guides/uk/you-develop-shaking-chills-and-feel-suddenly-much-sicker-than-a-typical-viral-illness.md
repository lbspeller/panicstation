# What to do if… you develop shaking chills and feel suddenly much sicker than a typical viral illness (UK)

## Short answer
Treat sudden shaking chills with a clear step-change in how ill you feel as urgent. Call 999 or go to A&E now if you have red-flag symptoms; otherwise contact your local urgent medical help route now, such as 111 where available.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to “sleep it off” if you feel rapidly worse, unusually weak, confused, faint, or difficult to wake.
- Do not delay getting help by repeatedly checking your temperature or waiting for medicine to work.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, confused, very weak, breathless, or are shivering hard.
- Do not take leftover antibiotics or someone else’s prescription.
- Do not take more than the recommended dose of paracetamol or ibuprofen, and check labels before combining medicines.
- Do not drink alcohol or take sedating drugs to try to knock yourself out.

## What to do now

1. **Check for emergency red flags now. Call 999 or go to A&E if any apply:**
   - Acting confused, slurred speech, not making sense, or difficult to wake
   - Blue, grey, pale or blotchy skin, lips, or tongue
   - Difficulty breathing, breathlessness, or breathing very fast
   - A rash that does not fade when you roll a glass over it
   - Collapse, feeling unable to stay upright safely, or feeling that you are rapidly deteriorating

2. **If you do not have those red flags, still treat this as urgent. Contact your local urgent medical help route now, such as 111 where available, if any apply:**
   - You feel very unwell or like something is seriously wrong
   - You have not had a pee all day
   - You keep vomiting or cannot keep fluids down
   - You have swelling or pain around a cut or wound
   - You have a very high or low temperature, feel hot or cold to the touch, or are shivering

3. **Use clear words when you ask for help:**
   “I have shaking chills or rigors, and I feel suddenly much sicker than a typical viral illness.”

4. **If you are waiting for a call back or transport, write down the basics:**
   - When the chills started and how fast you worsened
   - Your temperature, if you can check it safely
   - Whether you are breathless at rest or breathing much faster than normal
   - Whether you can answer simple questions clearly, such as your name, where you are, and the date
   - Whether you have passed urine today
   - Any new severe chest, abdomen, back, flank, head or neck pain
   - Any relentless vomiting, diarrhoea, rash, wound pain or rapidly spreading redness

5. **Make it easier for clinicians to act quickly:**
   - Keep your medication list, allergies, major conditions and pregnancy status ready.
   - Mention recent infection symptoms, wounds, dental problems, surgery, new medicines, travel or close contact with someone ill.
   - Say early if you have a weakened immune system, chemotherapy, transplant medicines, high-dose steroids, pregnancy or a major long-term illness.

6. **Reduce risk while you wait:**
   - Ask someone to stay with you, or stay on the phone with you, until you are assessed.
   - Sip fluids if you can keep them down.
   - Take paracetamol for fever or aches only if you can take it safely and follow the label; do not let feeling temporarily better delay urgent assessment.

7. **Escalate if anything changes.**
   If you become confused, very breathless, develop a non-fading rash, collapse, or feel you are rapidly deteriorating, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the exact cause right now.
- You do not need to decide what tests or treatment you might need.
- Work messages, routine plans and non-urgent logistics can wait until you have contacted urgent medical help or emergency care.

## Important reassurance
Shaking chills and suddenly feeling much sicker can happen with infections that need prompt assessment. Getting checked quickly is a sensible protective step, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you get safely assessed and avoid dangerous delay. Later decisions may need a clinician or other specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information only and is not medical, legal, financial, therapeutic or other professional advice. If you may be seriously unwell, are worsening quickly, or have red-flag symptoms, seek urgent emergency care.
