# What to do if… your key or smart key is cracked or damaged and you worry it may fail before you get help (UK)

## Short answer
Switch to a spare key immediately if available and book a replacement via an auto locksmith or dealership before the damaged key becomes non-functional.

## Do not do these things
- Do not repeatedly test the lock or start functions to "see if it still works," as each cycle increases the risk of final failure.
- Do not apply glue near the buttons, internal circuit board, or battery contacts.
- Do not use the key if the internal transponder chip (a small, often loose plastic or glass pellet) is at risk of falling out of the crack.
- Do not post images of the key blade or serial numbers on social media.
- Do not attempt to force a bent blade back into position.

## What to do now

1. **Switch to your spare key immediately and store the damaged one in a dry, secure location.** Do not keep the damaged key on your active keyring or in a pocket where it may be compressed.
2. **Locate the vehicle's V5C logbook and your photo ID.** Ensure the name and address on your ID match the registered keeper details on the V5C.
   - Most reputable locksmiths and dealerships will refuse service without these matching documents.
3. **Check your owner's manual for the "emergency start" or "backup entry" procedure.** 
   - Many smart fobs contain a hidden mechanical blade to unlock the door.
   - Keyless-start vehicles usually have a specific spot (near the steering column or in a center cubby) where a weak or damaged key must be held to bypass the immobiliser.
4. **Isolate the damaged key to prevent further deterioration.** 
   - If the casing is split, use a small piece of clear tape to hold the shell together, ensuring you do not cover the buttons or the signal transmitter.
   - Ensure the battery remains seated firmly against its contacts.
5. **Contact an approved specialist to arrange a replacement.** 
   - Search for a member of the Auto Locksmiths Association (ALA) or a Master Locksmiths Association (MLA) approved company that explicitly lists automotive services.
   - Alternatively, contact your local manufacturer-authorised dealership.
6. **Reduce "start-stop" cycles if you must use the vehicle before the new key arrives.** 
   - Group necessary errands into a single journey.
   - Avoid stopping the engine in high-stress or remote locations where a failure would cause a significant obstruction.
7. **Verify your breakdown cover for "Key Assist" or "Key Replacement" add-ons.** Check if your policy provides mobile locksmith attendance or recovery if the key fails entirely.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a permanent "third" spare key right now.
- You do not need to perform a DIY repair of the internal circuit board or soldering.
- You do not need to research long-term security upgrades for the vehicle.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel anxious about being stranded when a key is visibly damaged. Most keys continue to function intermittently for a short period after a casing crack, providing a window of time to arrange a professional replacement.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a damaged physical or smart key. It does not cover the technical steps for programming or electronic repair, which require specialist diagnostic equipment.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional mechanical or security advice. If your key fails and you are in an unsafe location, contact emergency services or your breakdown provider immediately.
