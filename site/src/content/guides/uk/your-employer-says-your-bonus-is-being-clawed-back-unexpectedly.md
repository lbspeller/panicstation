# What to do if… your employer says your bonus is being clawed back unexpectedly (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the "not hired" status as a data discrepancy while you verify the legal standing of your offer. Secure digital copies of your acceptance and contact HR immediately in writing to confirm your employment status. Do not make irreversible changes to your current employment until you have a human response.

## Do not do these things
- Do not resign from your current role or commit to new financial obligations until a human confirms the portal status is accurate.
- Do not assume a portal status change automatically overrides a signed or accepted contract.
- Do not repeatedly upload sensitive documents like passports or bank details if the portal appears to have reset or shows an "unsuccessful" status.
- Do not send aggressive or accusatory emails to the recruiter; maintain a neutral, evidence-based tone.
- Do not rely on verbal promises from a hiring manager without a follow-up email confirming the start date and contract status.

## What to do now
1. **Capture evidence of the current portal state (2 minutes):** Take a full-page screenshot of the "not hired" status, ensuring the URL, date, and time are visible in the frame.
2. **Retrieve your acceptance trail:** Locate and save your offer letter, your digital or email acceptance, and any confirmation of receipt from the employer or their automated system.
3. **Audit your offer conditions:** Check your offer letter for terms such as "subject to references," "subject to right-to-work checks," or "subject to Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) clearance."
   - Identify which conditions have been met and which are still pending.
   - Note if any background check providers have contacted you recently.
4. **Send a formal status inquiry to HR:** Email the HR contact listed on your offer letter (not through the portal's internal messaging system if possible).
   - State the date you accepted the offer and your agreed start date.
   - Attach the screenshot of the portal and ask for written confirmation that your employment status remains active.
5. **Ask for the specific status of pre-employment checks:** If the offer was conditional, ask if the "not hired" status is linked to a specific pending check so you can provide any missing information immediately.
6. **Inquire about notice pay if the offer is confirmed as withdrawn:** If HR confirms the offer is revoked and it was unconditional (or all conditions were met), ask them to confirm in writing how they will fulfill the notice period pay specified in your contract.
7. **Contact your current employer if you already gave notice:** If you have already resigned and the new offer appears genuinely rescinded, immediately ask your current manager if your resignation can be retracted or your notice period extended.
8. **Verify your "Day One" eligibility for April 2026 rules:** Confirm with HR that your start date is correctly logged in the payroll system to ensure you are covered by 2026 statutory protections for sick pay and parental leave from your first day.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a legal claim or tribunal application in the first 24 hours.
- You do not need to update your LinkedIn profile or public status until the discrepancy is resolved.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your references or former employers yet.

## Important reassurance
Onboarding portals frequently trigger "not hired" or "unsuccessful" statuses due to automated workflow steps, such as moving a candidate from a recruitment module to a payroll module. A system status is a data point, not a final legal verdict; human error in HR software is common and often reversible with a single administrative correction.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. If the employer confirms the offer has been withdrawn and you have already suffered financial loss or resigned from your previous job, you will need to seek specialist advice from Acas or a qualified employment solicitor.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and does not constitute legal advice. Employment rights in the UK depend on whether an offer is conditional or unconditional and whether a legally binding contract was formed upon acceptance. Under 2026 regulations, several statutory rights now begin on day one of employment, but the right to claim unfair dismissal generally requires a longer qualifying period. Always communicate with employers in writing to maintain a clear record of events.
