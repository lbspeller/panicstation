# What to do if… a stranger claims to know you and insists you go somewhere with them (UK)

## Short answer
Do not go anywhere with them. Move into the nearest staffed, public place and ask staff or a specific bystander to call 999 if you feel threatened or they will not back off.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a quieter spot, side street, car park, doorway, toilet area, or vehicle to talk.
- Do not let them steer you by the arm, shoulder, bag, clothing, or by guiding you.
- Do not give them personal details, including your full name, address, hotel, workplace, route, or where you are going.
- Do not follow them to prove you are not rude or to make the awkwardness end.
- Do not show ID, unlock your phone for them, share your live location with them, or hand your phone over.
- Do not head straight home if you think they may follow; prioritise a staffed place and help first.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Turn toward people, light, and staff. Go into the nearest open shop, café, supermarket, hotel lobby, petrol station, station entrance, ticket office, reception desk, or busy queue.
2. **Use one clear refusal once.** Say: **“I don’t know you. I’m not going anywhere with you.”** Then stop debating and put your attention on staff or bystanders.
3. **Make it a public problem quickly.** Point to a specific person and give them a clear job: **“You in the blue jacket, please call 999. This person is trying to make me go with them and I’m scared.”**
4. **Ask staff to create distance.** Say: **“Please keep them away from me. I do not know them. Please call 999 if they stay, follow, block me, touch me, or threaten me.”**
5. **Call 999, or ask someone else to call, if you feel in danger.** Give your exact location, what the person is doing, what they are saying, and a quick description such as clothing, build, height, bags, vehicle, or direction of travel.
6. **If you cannot safely speak on a 999 call, still dial 999 and stay on the line.** Listen to the operator or recorded prompts. Cough, tap the keys, or make another small sound if you can. If prompted, press 55 or tap to confirm it is a genuine emergency and be put through to police.
7. **If this is happening on the rail, Tube, tram, or station network, move toward staff or other passengers.** Dial 999 if a crime is happening, the person is nearby, or anyone is being threatened or in danger. If it does not need an emergency response, you can text British Transport Police on 61016 or call 0800 40 50 40.
8. **If you are in a bar, club, or licensed venue, ask staff directly for help.** If the venue uses Ask for Angela, you can ask for “Angela”. Otherwise say plainly: **“I feel unsafe. Please help me get away from this person and call 999 if needed.”**
9. **Do not leave alone while they can still see you.** Ask staff to let you wait inside, watch the door, help you leave by a different exit if appropriate, or wait with you until police, a trusted person, or transport arrives.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide now whether to make a formal report or statement.
- You do not have to work out who they are or prove they are lying.
- You do not need to reply to messages, post online, warn others publicly, or explain yourself.
- You can write down the time, place, description, and what happened after you are safe and calmer.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, feeling confused, or worrying about seeming rude is a common reaction. Being blunt, moving toward staff, and asking for help is a reasonable safety response when someone ignores your refusal.

## Scope note
This is first-steps-only guidance to help you reach a safer pause and bring in help quickly. Later decisions about reporting, ongoing safety, harassment, or legal options can be handled with specialist help if needed.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are at immediate risk, call 999. If you are unsure but feel threatened, move to a staffed public place and ask for help.
