# What to do if… your washing machine is leaking and water is spreading across the floor (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the water supply immediately at the appliance valve or the main internal stopcock and stay clear of any water that has reached electrical sockets or cables.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t step into standing water to reach a plug, extension lead, or consumer unit.
- Don’t attempt to move the machine while it is still plugged in or while water is actively spraying.
- Don’t use a vacuum cleaner to suck up water unless it is specifically rated as a "wet and dry" vacuum.
- Don’t touch the consumer unit (fuse box) or electrical meter if you see water dripping onto them or if you hear buzzing sounds.
- Don’t ignore a leak that has stopped; residual water can still cause structural damage or electrical faults.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise physical safety.** Move people and pets away from the wet area to prevent slips and potential electric shock.  
2. **Assess the electrical risk before acting.** If water has reached floor-level sockets, extension leads, or the power cable of the machine, do not enter the water.  
   - **If it is safe and dry to do so, switch off the power at the wall socket.**  
   - **If you cannot safely reach the socket, go to your consumer unit and switch off the 'Sockets' or 'Kitchen' circuit, or use the Main Switch.**  
3. **Shut off the water at the nearest isolation point.**  
   - Look behind the machine for a small valve (usually blue for cold water) on the pipe and turn it 90 degrees so it is perpendicular to the pipe.  
   - **If you cannot reach these valves, turn off the main internal stopcock.** This is most commonly found under the kitchen sink, in a hallway cupboard, or under the stairs.  
4. **Contain the spread of the water.** Lay towels or heavy cloths at the edges of the pool to create a barrier (dam) to prevent water from reaching other rooms or fixed electrical fittings.  
5. **Alert others if you are in a flat or shared building.** If water is deep or has been leaking for more than a few minutes, notify neighbours below immediately so they can check their ceilings and light fittings.  
6. **Protect vulnerable items on the floor.** Lift any rugs, cardboard boxes, or electrical items out of the wet area.  
7. **Contact the relevant assistance based on your tenure.**  
   - **If you rent:** notify your landlord or letting agent immediately as this is an emergency repair.  
   - **If you own:** contact a qualified plumber or an appliance repair engineer.  
8. **Call 105 if water has entered the electrical meter or the main service head.** This connects you to your local electricity network operator for safety advice.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific part (e.g., pump, seal, or hose) that failed right now.
- You do not need to clean the interior of the machine or salvage the laundry inside if the area is still wet.
- You do not need to decide on a replacement machine or file a full insurance claim in this moment.

## Important reassurance
Washing machine leaks often look worse than they are because water spreads thin across hard floors. Once the water supply is isolated, the "flood" is contained. Taking a moment to ensure you are standing on dry ground before touching any switches is the most effective way to manage the situation safely.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only. It does not cover the repair of the appliance or the long-term drying of floorboards, joists, or electrical systems. Specialist assessment is required if water has entered walls or ceilings.

## Important note
This guide provides general safety information. If you suspect water has entered your electrical system or if you cannot safely access your water shut-off valves, exit the area and contact a professional immediately.
