# What to do if… you have a fever and a rash that does not fade when pressed (UK)

## Short answer
Call **999 now** or go to **A&E immediately**. Fever with a rash that does not fade when pressed with a glass can be a sign of meningitis or sepsis and needs urgent medical assessment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not watch and wait to see if it improves.
- Do not assume it is only an allergy, heat rash, or a minor virus.
- Do not wait for a routine GP appointment, pharmacy visit, or NHS 111 callback if the rash still shows under a glass and there is fever or the person seems unwell.
- Do not drive yourself if you feel faint, confused, very sleepy, breathless, or rapidly worse; call 999.
- Do not delay because the rash is small, only in one area, or hard to see on your skin tone.

## What to do now
1. **Call 999 immediately.** Say: “Fever and a rash that does not fade when a glass is pressed over it.” Tell them how the person is now, including any confusion, severe headache, stiff neck, vomiting, cold hands or feet, fast breathing, sleepiness, collapse, or rapid worsening.
2. **Get ready for help without slowing the call.** Sit or lie somewhere warm and easy to find. If you can do so safely, unlock the door, keep your phone charged, and put it on speaker.
3. **Check the rash once, briefly, if you have not already done this.** Press the side of a clear glass gently but firmly over a few spots. If the spots still show through the glass, treat it as non-blanching and focus on getting emergency help.
4. **If the rash is hard to see on brown or black skin, check paler areas quickly.** Look at the palms, soles, tummy, roof of the mouth, whites of the eyes, and inside the eyelids while emergency help is being arranged.
5. **Take only what helps clinicians, without delaying.** Bring current medicines or a photo of them, allergy details, and key medical history such as immune problems, no spleen, or recent serious infection.
6. **Take a clear photo of the rash if it does not delay help.** Use good light and include one wider photo showing where it is on the body.
7. **If the person becomes very drowsy, collapses, or has trouble breathing, stay on the line with 999.** Follow the call handler’s instructions, including starting CPR if they tell you to.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact cause of the rash.
- You do not need to decide whether this is meningitis, sepsis, or something else.
- You do not need to finish packing, cleaning, work tasks, school messages, or non-urgent calls.
- You do not need to keep repeating the glass test.

## Important reassurance
You are not overreacting by getting urgent help for this combination. A rash that does not fade with pressure is one of the situations where acting quickly is the safer choice.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for fever with a rash that does not fade when pressed. Diagnosis, tests, treatment, and follow-up decisions may need urgent assessment by healthcare professionals.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical or professional advice. For fever with a rash that does not fade when pressed with a glass, seek urgent medical help immediately.
