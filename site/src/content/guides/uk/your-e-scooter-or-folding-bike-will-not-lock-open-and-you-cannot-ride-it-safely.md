# What to do if… your e-scooter or folding bike will not lock open and you cannot ride it safely (UK)

## Short answer
Stop riding immediately and treat the device as unsafe. Move yourself out of traffic first, then secure the e-scooter or folding bike for walking, carrying, collection, or repair.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t ride it slowly, coast on it, or “test it one more time” if the lock, latch, hinge, stem, or clamp will not hold.
- Don’t wedge the latch with coins, cable ties, tape, string, cardboard, or improvised spacers.
- Don’t tighten bolts, clamps, or hinge parts at the roadside unless the manufacturer’s manual tells you to do that exact action and you are already somewhere safe.
- Don’t force a hinge, stem, or catch that feels bent, cracked, jammed, or misaligned.
- Don’t carry it on stairs, escalators, trains, or buses while it is half-folded, swinging, or able to snap shut.
- Don’t ride a privately owned e-scooter on public roads, pavements, cycle lanes, or other public land in the UK.

## What to do now
1. **Check yourself first.** If you fell, hit your head, feel faint, have severe pain, serious bleeding, chest pain, breathing trouble, or another life-threatening emergency, call **999**. If you need medical help now but it is not life-threatening, use **NHS 111** or call **111**.
2. **Get to a safer pause.** Step off the device. Move yourself to a pavement, verge, station concourse, shop front, or other clear area away from moving traffic before handling the device more than necessary.
3. **Make the device safe to move, not safe to ride.**
   - If it can fold fully, fold it fully and engage the carry latch, strap, or magnet if it has one.
   - If it cannot fold safely, keep it open and walk it by hand.
   - If it swings, drops, or snaps while being moved, stop and hold it low and close to your body until you can put it down safely.
4. **Do one short visual check only.** Look for an obvious reversible cause, such as grit in the latch, a trapped cable, a safety pin not seated, a clamp not aligned in its seat, or a secondary catch stuck halfway. If anything looks cracked, bent, loose, or will not click positively into place, stop checking and do not ride it.
5. **If it is a rental e-scooter, follow the app’s safety fault route.** Stop using it, note the scooter ID and exact location, report a locking, folding, stem, or latch fault, and park or end the ride only as the app instructs.
6. **If it is your own e-scooter or folding bike, record the fault without recreating it.** From a safe place, take photos or a short video of the current latch, hinge, clamp, stem, serial number, and any visible damage. Do not ride it or force the joint to make the fault happen again.
7. **Choose onward travel that does not depend on riding it.** Walk it, carry it only if it is secure, arrange pickup, use a taxi or private hire vehicle, or move to a staffed station, shop, café, or well-lit public place while you sort transport.
8. **Start the repair or return route when you are safe.** Check the manual for the exact folding and locking sequence, then contact the retailer, manufacturer, rental operator, a suitable cycle mechanic for a folding bike, or a qualified technician for an e-scooter. Describe it as a locking, folding, hinge, stem, or latch safety failure.
9. **Keep the seller route in mind if you bought it recently or it may be faulty.** In the UK, consumer remedies for faulty goods usually start with the seller or retailer, not only the manufacturer. Keep messages, receipts, photos, and fault reports.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether the fault is wear, damage, design, misuse, or a manufacturing issue.
- You do not need to strip it down, grease it, adjust hinge tension, replace parts, or search forums at the roadside.
- You do not need to argue with support while you are still stranded or shaken.
- You do not need to choose a permanent repair, replacement, refund, or upgrade before you have got yourself and the device somewhere safe.

## Important reassurance
Stopping early is the right response. A folding lock or latch that does not hold can fail suddenly, and avoiding one more ride is a sensible way to prevent a mechanical fault becoming an injury.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: stop riding, stabilise the moment, get to a safer place, and preserve basic information. Diagnosis, adjustment, warranty decisions, repair, replacement, or a consumer claim may need the manual, the seller, the rental operator, a qualified mechanic, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional mechanical, legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If the hinge, latch, clamp, frame, fork, handlebar stem, or folding joint shows cracking, bending, repeated unlocking, or uncertain engagement, treat the device as unsafe until it has been inspected and repaired by an appropriate professional.
