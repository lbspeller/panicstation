# What to do if… someone online asks for explicit photos and immediately demands secrecy (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all communication immediately, block the user across all platforms, and tell a trusted adult or official support service. Do not send money or further images, as compliance usually increases the demands rather than stopping them.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send any further photos, videos, or "verification" images, even if they promise to stop.
- Do not send money, gift card codes, or cryptocurrency "fees" to the person.
- Do not negotiate or try to reason with them; they are using a scripted pressure tactic.
- Do not delete your social media accounts immediately; deactivating them or changing privacy settings is safer for evidence preservation.
- Do not attempt to "test" the situation by sending edited or fake photos.
- Do not keep the situation secret; the demand for secrecy is designed to isolate you from help.

## What to do now
1. **End all contact and block the account.** **Use the block and report functions built into the app or platform where the request happened.**
   - Block them on any other platforms they have mentioned or used to contact you.
   - Set your profiles to "Private" and restrict who can message you or see your follower lists.
2. **Tell a safe person immediately.** 
   - **If you are under 18, tell a parent, carer, teacher, or another trusted adult.**
   - **If you are 18 or older, tell a trusted friend or family member to break the isolation.**
3. **Report the incident to the correct UK authority based on your age.**
   - **If you are under 18:** Report to **CEOP** (Child Exploitation and Online Protection Command) via their online safety centre. You can also call the police on **101** or **999** in an emergency.
   - **If you are 18 or older:** Report to the police via **101** or their online reporting tool. If you are in immediate danger of physical harm, call **999**.
4. **Preserve evidence without re-engaging.** **Take screenshots of the demands, the profile handle, and any threats, but do not reopen or look at the explicit media itself.**
   - Keep a note of the platform name and any secondary usernames they used.
   - Store these screenshots in a secure folder or send them to your trusted person for safekeeping.
5. **Contact specialist support for image removal if content was shared.**
   - **For those under 18:** Use the **Report Remove** tool (run by Childline and the IWF) to help get images taken down from the internet.
   - **For those 18 and older:** Contact the **Revenge Porn Helpline** (0345 6000 459) for advice on "StopNCII" and other removal processes.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether to pursue a full criminal investigation (you can get support first).
- Deleting your entire online presence or changing your phone number.
- Explaining the situation to everyone you know; only the people helping you need to know the details right now.
- Reviewing your past messages to find "where it went wrong."

## Important reassurance
The "secrecy demand" is a standard part of online coercion and grooming; it is a tool used to keep you from accessing the support that would stop the person. Feeling embarrassed or fearful is a natural reaction to this type of pressure, but you are not responsible for the illegal actions or threats of another person.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for online coercion and blackmail. It does not replace the need for professional safeguarding or police intervention, especially if threats escalate or involve physical safety.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and is not a substitute for legal advice or emergency services. If you are under 18 and feel threatened, your priority is informing a trusted adult and contacting CEOP or the police.
