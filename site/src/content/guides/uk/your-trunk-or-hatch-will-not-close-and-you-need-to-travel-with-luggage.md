# What to do if… your trunk or hatch will not close and you need to travel with luggage (UK)

## Short answer
Do not start the journey with luggage in a boot that will not latch. Move only as far as needed to get out of immediate danger, then make the luggage secure inside the vehicle or change plans.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive the trip with the boot open, half-latched, or held down by hand.
- Do not rely on tape, string, one light bungee cord, or a suitcase strap as the main way to keep the boot shut.
- Do not drive if luggage, straps, or the boot lid cover the rear lights, reflectors, or rear number plate.
- Do not drive if anything could fall out of the vehicle or slide into the road.
- Do not put heavy luggage loose on seats, parcel shelves, or above the rear seatbacks.
- Do not force the latch with a key, screwdriver, or improvised object.
- Do not keep driving if you smell fumes, notice exhaust smell, or anyone develops headache, dizziness, nausea, weakness, confusion, chest pain, muscle pain, or shortness of breath. Carbon monoxide may not smell.

## What to do now
1. **Get to the safest pause you can.** Pull off the road if you can do so safely, switch on hazard lights if needed, keep people away from traffic, and do the next checks without standing in the road.
2. **Empty the latch area first.** Remove suitcase corners, coat straps, carrier bags, loose trim, parcel shelves, luggage nets, and anything touching the latch or striker.
3. **Check whether the latch has clicked shut while the boot is open.** If the latch tongue looks closed, use the normal boot release button, key fob button, or handle to release it, then try closing the boot gently again.
4. **Try one normal close only after clearing the obstruction.** Do not slam repeatedly. If it shuts, pull up firmly on the boot lid to confirm it has latched, then continue only after the luggage is secure.
5. **If the boot still will not latch, take luggage out of the boot.** Put heavy items low and forward inside the cabin, such as in rear footwells where possible. Use seatbelts through suitcase handles or around bags so they cannot move forward in a sudden stop.
6. **Check visibility before moving.** Make sure the rear lights, indicators, reflectors, and rear number plate are clean, clear, and not blocked by bags, straps, people, or the boot lid.
7. **If luggage cannot be secured inside, use a different plan.** Send bulky luggage by courier or parcel drop-off, use a taxi or minicab for the luggage, borrow another vehicle, hire a larger vehicle, or delay the trip.
8. **If the boot is closed but the latch feels unreliable, treat it as not ready for the trip.** Use roadside assistance or a nearby garage before travelling with luggage in the boot.
9. **If you must move from an unsafe position, keep it only to the shortest safe repositioning move.** Move to a nearby safe stopping place, then stop and use a different plan rather than continuing the journey.
10. **If fumes or symptoms appear, stop using the car for the journey.** Get everyone into fresh air. Call NHS 111 if anyone may have symptoms from fumes or carbon monoxide; call 999 if someone is finding it hard to breathe, is suddenly confused, has lost consciousness, feels very weak, or has chest or muscle pain.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the exact mechanical fault now.
- You do not need to decide today whether the latch, striker, trim, or boot struts need replacing.
- You do not need to sort refunds, bookings, or blame while you are still under roadside pressure.
- You do not need to make the original travel plan work if the luggage cannot be carried safely.

## Important reassurance
This is a common last-minute travel problem, especially when the car is fully packed. Slowing down, unloading, and choosing a safer way to carry the luggage is a sensible response, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and prevent a rushed decision. A garage, roadside technician, insurer, hire company, or luggage carrier may still be needed for later decisions.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, medical, financial, or professional advice. If you are unsure whether the vehicle is safe or compliant to drive, do not use it for the journey until the boot closes and latches properly or the luggage can be carried securely without obstructing required visibility.
