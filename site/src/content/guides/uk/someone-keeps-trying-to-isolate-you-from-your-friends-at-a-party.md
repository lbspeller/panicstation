# What to do if… someone keeps trying to isolate you from your friends at a party (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a safety issue, not a social problem. Move back to friends, staff, security, or another visible group now, and do not go anywhere private with the person.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go somewhere quieter, outside, upstairs, to a car, or into a hallway just to stop the pressure.
- Do not accept a private chat as the price of them leaving you alone.
- Do not let embarrassment stop you involving friends, bar staff, door staff, or security.
- Do not split up to keep the peace, such as sending a friend away so you can handle it alone.
- Do not keep drinking anything you left unattended or did not see poured. Stop drinking it, get water or a fresh drink, and tell a friend or staff you are concerned.
- Do not confront them alone or try to prove their intent before taking safety steps.

## What to do now
1. **Move to people and light.** Go to the busiest visible place, such as the bar, the edge of the dancefloor, a staffed area, or near door staff. If you are seated, stand up and relocate.
2. **Give a clear regroup instruction.** Text or say: "Come to the bar now", "Stay with me", or "Walk with me to the toilet." Use names and do not negotiate with the person trying to separate you.
3. **Use a buddy lock.** Link arms with a friend, hold their sleeve, stand shoulder-to-shoulder, or ask a trusted group: "Can I stand with you for a minute? Someone is making me feel unsafe."
4. **Tell staff exactly what is happening.** Say: "I feel unsafe. Someone keeps trying to separate me from my friends. Can you help me rejoin them and keep that person away from me?"
5. **Use Ask for Angela if it helps.** At some participating venues, asking staff for "Angela" is a discreet way to signal that you need help. If staff do not recognise it, ask plainly for security or assistance.
6. **Leave without relying on the person.** Ask staff or a trusted friend to walk you back to your group, stay nearby while you collect your things, help you get into a licensed taxi or booked ride, or wait with you until you are safely away.
7. **If they block you, grab you, follow you, or you feel in immediate danger, call 999.** If speaking is not safe, move into view of staff or other people and signal for help.
8. **After you are safe, write a few notes only if it helps you feel less foggy.** You can note their name or description, where it happened, who saw it, and which staff helped. You do not have to decide what to do with that information now.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide tonight whether to report anything to police.
- You do not have to confront them, explain yourself, or prove anything to anyone.
- You do not have to decide whether you overreacted before taking safety steps.
- You do not have to make longer-term decisions while you are still trying to get safe and steady.

## Important reassurance
Getting help early is a reasonable response when someone keeps trying to isolate you. You are allowed to move away, involve staff, stay with friends, and leave without giving a full explanation.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stop isolation and increase immediate safety. If anything sexual happened without your consent, or you are not sure, specialist support can talk through options at your pace.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999. If something sexual happened, you can seek confidential support and medical help without being forced to report to police.
