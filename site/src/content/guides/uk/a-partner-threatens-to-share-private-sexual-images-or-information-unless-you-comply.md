# What to do if… a partner threatens to share private sexual images or information unless you comply (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, stop the live back-and-forth if you can, and do not send anything new or agree to new demands. If you are in immediate danger or they are threatening violence, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to “just this once” demands if you can avoid it; it may increase their leverage.
- Do not send new sexual content, money, passwords, location details, or “proof” to calm them down.
- Do not argue in detail, justify yourself, or share more intimate details in messages.
- Do not delete messages, accounts, or posts in panic; you may want basic records later.
- Do not confront them in person if that could put you at risk.
- Do not use a device, email account, cloud account, or messaging account they can access if you suspect monitoring.
- Do not screenshot, save, forward, or upload sexual images if anyone in them may be under 18.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** If you are with them, step away for a normal reason, move near other people, or contact someone you trust. If you feel in immediate danger, or they are using or threatening violence, call **999**.
2. **Stop giving them new material.** If you can, stop replying. If you need to reply to slow the situation down, keep it brief and non-negotiating, such as: “I’m not discussing this now.”
3. **Save only safe, basic proof of the threat.** Keep messages that show the demand, the threat, their username or number, the platform, and dates or times. Do not copy or share intimate images themselves, especially if anyone may be under 18.
4. **Move copies somewhere they cannot reach.** Only do this if it is safe. Use a trusted person, a new email account they do not know about, or secure storage they cannot access.
5. **Report in the simplest safe way.** Threats to share intimate images can be reported to police, and sexual blackmail or threats involving sexual information can also be reported without you needing to name the exact offence. Use online reporting or **101** if it is not an emergency; use **999** if you are unsafe now.
6. **Use specialist intimate-image support if images or videos are involved.** The Revenge Porn Helpline supports UK adults with threats, takedown options, and next steps. StopNCII.org may help reduce re-uploading on participating platforms if you are eligible and still have the images or videos.
7. **If you are under 18, use child-focused support.** You can report online sexual abuse or sexual communication concerns through the CEOP Safety Centre. You can also contact Childline for confidential support.
8. **If you live together or fear retaliation, treat safety as the priority.** Keep your phone charged, avoid announcing plans to leave, consider where you could go at short notice, and contact a domestic abuse service from a safe device if you can.
9. **Secure the account they are using to reach you.** If safe, change your email password first, then the relevant app passwords. Turn on two-factor authentication and sign out of sessions or devices you do not recognise.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to make a formal statement, pursue charges, tell family, or tell work.
- You do not need to write the perfect message to them.
- You do not need to collect every screenshot before asking for help.
- You do not need to negotiate terms with them, even if they promise they will stop.
- You do not need to decide the future of the relationship right now.

## Important reassurance
Being threatened with exposure is frightening and destabilising. Freezing, replying, complying, or feeling ashamed in the moment does not make this your fault.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to help you stabilise the situation and reduce immediate harm. Later decisions may need specialist support, legal advice, police input, domestic abuse support, or child protection help depending on your situation.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If any step could increase risk because they monitor you, live with you, or have access to your accounts, prioritise immediate safety and confidential specialist support.
