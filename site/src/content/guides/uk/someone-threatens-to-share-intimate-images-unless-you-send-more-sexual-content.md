# What to do if… someone threatens to share intimate images unless you send more sexual content (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all interaction immediately and do not send any further images or money. This is a form of coercion known as intimate image abuse; complying with demands almost always leads to further threats rather than a resolution.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send any more images, videos, or live camera "verification" even if promised it is the last time.
- Don't pay money, purchase gift cards, or send cryptocurrency to prevent a leak.
- Don't delete the platform account or conversation history yet, as you may need this evidence for reporting.
- Don't negotiate, apologize, or argue with the person making the threat.
- Don't assume that following their instructions will make them delete the content.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the hazard by cutting off communication.**  
   **Block the account** on the platform where the threat is occurring and adjust your privacy settings to "private" or "friends only." Ensure the person cannot see your friend lists, followers, or contact information to prevent them from contacting people you know.

2. **Secure evidence for potential reporting.**  
   **Take screenshots** of the conversation, the specific threats made, and the profile or username of the person threatening you. Capture the URL of their profile page if you are using a web browser.

3. **Report the abuse to the platform immediately.**  
   **Use the "Report" function** on the app or website and select options related to "intimate image abuse," "harassment," or "extortion." Under UK law (2026), major platforms must remove non-consensual intimate images within 48 hours once notified.

4. **Access specialist support for UK adults (18+).**  
   **Contact the Revenge Porn Helpline** on 0345 6000 459 (Monday to Friday, 10 am – 4 pm) or via their website. They provide technical and legal advice on removing content and dealing with blackmailers.

5. **Access specialist support for under-18s.**  
   **Use the Childline 'Report Remove' tool** to help get images or videos taken down from the internet. You can create a confidential locker to track your report; age verification via ID is optional and not required to start the process.

6. **Protect the content from being re-uploaded.**  
   **Use StopNCII.org** to create a secure digital "hash" (fingerprint) of the images or videos they have. Participating platforms (including Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and Reddit) use these hashes to automatically block the content from being uploaded.

7. **Report the crime to the police.**  
   **Use the National 'My Police Portal'** to report intimate image abuse online or call 101. If you are in immediate physical danger, call 999.

8. **Contact immediate emotional support if needed.**  
   **Call the Rape Crisis 24/7 Support Line** on 0808 500 2222 if you are in England or Wales. This service is free, confidential, and available for anyone aged 16+ affected by sexual violence or coercion.

## What can wait
- You do-not need to decide whether to tell your family or friends right now.
- You do-not need to decide if you want to pursue a full criminal prosecution today.
- You do-not need to hire a private "reputation management" or "hacker" service to delete the content.
- You do-not need to respond to any further messages or "final warnings" from the perpetrator.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel panic, shame, or the urge to comply when threatened with exposure. These are normal reactions to a high-stress situation. This is a crime of coercion, and the responsibility for the harm lies entirely with the person making the threat. You are taking the necessary steps to regain control and protect yourself.

## Scope note
This guide provides immediate stabilization and first-step safety actions. It does not cover long-term legal strategies or ongoing psychological support, which should be accessed through the specialist services listed above.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, sharing or threatening to share intimate images without consent is a serious criminal offence. If you are at risk of self-harm or in immediate danger, contact emergency services (999) or attend your nearest A&E.
