# What to do if… someone you just met is pushing for your address or ride home and will not drop it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop explaining and move immediately to a staffed or crowded area to secure a safe exit. If you feel you are being followed or are in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide a fake or "nearby" address to appease them; this still gives them your general location.
- Do not accept a lift, share a taxi, or allow them to walk you to your transport.
- Do not move to a quieter area (like a hallway or car park) to explain your refusal.
- Do not let them see your phone screen while you are arranging travel or messaging for help.
- Do not go directly to your home if you believe you are being followed.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a staffed or high-traffic area immediately.** Head to a bar, reception desk, security station, or a busy shop counter. Stay in the line of sight of staff or CCTV.
2. **State one final boundary and cease further engagement.** Use a direct statement: "I am not sharing that information and I am leaving now." Do not offer excuses or "soften" the refusal.
3. **Alert venue staff or security directly.** Tell them: "This person is pressuring me and I do not feel safe leaving alone." Ask them to monitor the person while you wait for transport.
4. **Use the 'Ask for Angela' protocol if in a participating venue.** If you see "Ask for Angela" posters (common in bars, clubs, and some shops), tell a staff member you are "looking for Angela." This signals that you need a discreet exit or a safe space.
5. **Secure transport that you control and verify.**
   - Ask staff to call a licensed taxi or private hire vehicle (PHV) for you. 
   - If using a ride-hailing app, stay inside until the vehicle is at the door. 
   - Verify the vehicle’s council-issued plate (on the rear) and the driver’s ID badge before entering.
6. **Establish a 'witness' on an active phone call.** Call a friend or relative and tell them: "I am leaving [Location] now in a taxi; please stay on the line until I am inside my house." Give them the vehicle's registration number.
7. **If you believe you are being followed, divert to a safe hub.** Do not go to your front door. Go to a 24-hour petrol station, a hospital A&E, or a staffed police station. 
8. **Call 999 if the situation escalates or you feel at immediate risk.** If the person follows you into a vehicle or to your destination, stay inside the vehicle (if safe) or enter a public building and dial 999. Use 101 only to report the incident after you are in a secure, locked location.

## What can wait
- Deciding whether you want to press formal charges for harassment.
- Analyzing whether you were "too rude" or overreacted.
- Blocking their social media profiles or deleting messages (keep these as evidence for now).
- Explaining to mutual acquaintances why you left abruptly.

## Important reassurance
Internal physical signals like a racing heart, "gut feelings," or a desire to be overly polite (fawning) are standard responses to boundary-pushing. Choosing to prioritize your physical safety over social harmony is a valid and necessary protective action.

## Scope note
This guide is for immediate stabilization and safe extraction from a persistent or threatening social interaction. Long-term stalking or repeated harassment requires a different set of reporting and evidence-gathering procedures.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Safety protocols like "Ask for Angela" are voluntary and may not be available in all venues. Always rely on 999 for active emergencies where life or safety is threatened.
