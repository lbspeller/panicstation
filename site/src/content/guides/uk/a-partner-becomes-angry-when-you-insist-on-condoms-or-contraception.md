# What to do if… a partner becomes angry when you insist on condoms or contraception (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the interaction immediately and move to a separate physical space or a public area. Anger, intimidation, or pressure regarding sexual health is a critical risk indicator that requires prioritizing your immediate physical safety over the sexual encounter.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue with sexual activity to try and "de-escalate" or calm the other person down.
- Do not stay in a locked or isolated room if you feel physically intimidated or cornered.
- Do not accept "proof of love" or "trust" arguments as a valid reason to bypass your boundaries.
- Do not leave your phone or regular contraception (pills, patches) where the other person can take or hide them.
- Do not minimize the situation if you feel a sense of dread or physical fear.

## What to do now
1. **Interrupt the situation and move to another room immediately.** If you are in a bedroom, move to a bathroom or kitchen where there is a door you can close, or leave the property entirely if it is safe to do so.
2. **Contact emergency services if you are in immediate danger.** Call **999**. If you are on a mobile and cannot speak safely, wait for the operator to prompt you, then press **55** (the Silent Solution) to be transferred to the police.
3. **Notify a trusted contact that you need to leave or need a check-in.** Send a brief text asking for a call or stating where you are. If you need a reason to leave the room or building, state you are feeling physically unwell or need to check something in another room.
4. **Secure your personal items and medication.** Take your phone and any portable contraception (such as pill packets or patches) with you. Keep these items on your person or in a secure bag.
5. **Access emergency contraception if unprotected sex has occurred.** 
   - Contact a **sexual health clinic**, your **GP**, or call **NHS 111** for local service information.
   - In England, you can access the emergency contraceptive pill for free from many high-street pharmacies without an appointment under the NHS Pharmacy Contraception Service.
6. **Speak to a specialist support service for confidential advice.** You do not need to report a crime to access these services:
   - **National Domestic Abuse Helpline (Refuge):** Call **0808 2000 247** (24/7, freephone).
   - **Rape Crisis England & Wales:** Call **0808 500 2222** (24/7, freephone).
7. **Preserve physical evidence if a non-consensual act occurred and you may want to report it later.** If possible, avoid washing, smoking, or eating, and place any worn clothing or used items in a clean, separate paper bag. Only do this if it does not delay your safety or increase your risk.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to end the relationship right now.
- You do not need to find the "perfect" words to explain why you are leaving or stopping.
- You do not need to determine if this meets a legal definition of a crime before seeking help.
- You do not need to justify your need for protection or contraception to anyone.

## Important reassurance
It is a basic right to set boundaries regarding your body and sexual health. If a partner reacts with anger, shouting, or intimidation when you express these boundaries, your reaction of fear or the "freeze" response is a normal survival mechanism. You are not at fault for their behavior or for prioritizing your safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Long-term safety planning and medical follow-ups should be conducted with the assistance of the specialist services listed above once you are in a secure location.

## Important note
This information is specific to the United Kingdom and is current as of March 2026. It is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or medical advice. Always call 999 in an emergency.
