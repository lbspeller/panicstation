# What to do if… a GFCI or RCD outlet keeps tripping and you cannot restore power safely (UK)

## Short answer
Treat repeated RCD, RCBO, or RCD-protected socket tripping as a safety warning. Stop trying to force it back on, unplug what you safely can, and get a registered electrician or your landlord’s repair service to investigate.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t hold a reset button in, tape it down, or keep rapidly resetting a switch that trips again.
- Don’t use the socket or circuit if there is a burning smell, heat, buzzing, crackling, scorch marks, smoke, or visible damage.
- Don’t touch plugs, switches, sockets, or the consumer unit if you are wet, barefoot on a damp floor, or near standing water.
- Don’t use extension leads or adaptors to work around the tripping socket or circuit.
- Don’t open the consumer unit, socket, plug, or appliance casing, and don’t touch exposed wiring.
- Don’t keep using outdoor, garage, kitchen, or bathroom equipment that seems linked to damp, rain, or condensation.

## What to do now
1. **Make the area safer first.** If you see smoke, flames, or a strong burning smell, move away from the area and call 999. If there is water near the socket, appliance, or consumer unit, keep people and pets away and do not touch anything electrical there.
2. **Stop the reset loop.** If the RCD, RCBO, or RCD-protected socket trips again immediately or will not reset, stop trying for now.
3. **Unplug what you can safely reach.** Unplug appliances that were in use when it tripped, especially kettles, heaters, washing machines, dishwashers, dehumidifiers, outdoor tools, EV chargers, pond equipment, and anything used in damp areas.
4. **Switch off only clear, safe isolators.** If a fused spur or isolator is clearly labelled and safe to reach, switch it off. Do not remove covers or investigate wiring.
5. **Try one careful reset only if everything is dry and safe.** At the consumer unit, move the tripped RCD or RCBO fully to OFF, then back to ON. If it trips again, leave it off and go to step 7.
6. **If it stays on, narrow it down gently.** Plug in or switch on one item at a time, waiting briefly between each. If one appliance makes it trip, leave that appliance unplugged and do not use it until it has been checked or replaced.
7. **Get the right help.** If you own the home, contact a registered electrician and say the RCD or RCBO repeatedly trips or will not reset. If you rent, report it to your landlord, letting agent, housing provider, or repairs line as an urgent electrical safety issue, especially if you have no safe power for heating, refrigeration, medical equipment, or lighting.
8. **Check for a wider power cut if power is out beyond one circuit.** If neighbours are also without power, it may be an outage rather than your RCD. In England, Scotland, and Wales, call 105 free to reach the local electricity network operator. In Northern Ireland, contact NIE Networks on 03457 643643.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide what caused it right now.
- You do not need to replace the socket, RCD, RCBO, fuse, or consumer unit parts yourself.
- You do not need to test every appliance if the circuit is still tripping or the area is damp.
- You do not need to run high-power appliances today if you can avoid it.
- You do not need to argue about responsibility before making the immediate safety report if you rent.

## Important reassurance
An RCD or RCBO is meant to cut power when it detects a problem that could be unsafe. A sudden trip can feel alarming, but stopping, unplugging likely items, and getting qualified help is the safer response.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and avoid dangerous mistakes. Finding the underlying fault, whether it is an appliance, wiring, moisture, or the protective device itself, usually needs proper testing by a competent electrician.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not professional electrical, legal, housing, or emergency advice. If anything looks damaged, smells hot, involves water, or feels unsafe, do not touch it; move away and get qualified or emergency help.
