# What to do if… your router shows many unknown devices connected to your network (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately secure the email address associated with the account and use only official platform recovery links. Do not engage with third-party "recovery experts" or pay for unlocking services, as these are almost always scams.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay anyone on social media (or elsewhere) who claims they can "retrieve" or "unlock" your account.
- Don’t click links in emails or texts that demand immediate action to "save" your account unless you have verified the sender's address matches the official platform domain.
- Don’t repeatedly try to log in or request codes if the system is failing, as this can trigger a 24-48 hour security cooldown.
- Don’t provide your 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) codes or email login details to anyone, even if they claim to be "support."
- Don’t delete the initial "password changed" or "suspicious login" emails, as these often contain unique "revert" links.

## What to do now
1. **Document the exact error message or notification you received.**
   - Take screenshots of any lockout screens, "Account Suspended" banners, or emails stating your password or recovery details were changed.
   - Note the exact time you lost access and which specific details (email, phone number, username) were linked to the profile.

2. **Secure the primary email account used for your social media login.**
   - Change your email password to a strong, unique phrase that you have never used before.
   - Check the "Active Sessions" or "Devices" list in your email security settings and select "Log out of all other sessions."
   - Review your email "Forwarding" and "Filters" settings to ensure an attacker has not set up a rule to automatically divert and delete emails from social media support.

3. **Navigate directly to the official platform help center via a fresh browser window.**
   - Use the specific "Hacked Account," "Disabled Account," or "Appeal a Decision" pathways provided by the platform (e.g., instagram.com/hacked).
   - If the automated form fails, try again once from a different device or network connection (such as using mobile data instead of home Wi-Fi) to rule out IP-based blocking.

4. **Alert your close contacts through a different communication channel.**
   - Use SMS, phone calls, or a different app to tell friends and family that your account is compromised or locked.
   - Specifically warn them to ignore any messages from your account asking for money, "help with a code," or clicking on suspicious links.

5. **Report the incident to the appropriate UK national body.**
   - Report the cyber crime to **Report Fraud** (formerly Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040 if you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland.
   - Contact **Police Scotland via 101** or use the **Cyber and Fraud Hub Scotland** (0808 281 3580) if you are in Scotland.

## What can wait
- You do not need to start a new account immediately; wait until the official appeal process has a result to avoid being banned for "ban evasion."
- You do not need to hire a lawyer or digital forensics firm in the first 24 hours.
- You do not need to reply to "recovery" bots that flood your other posts or comments.
- You do not need to perform a full factory reset of your phone unless you have evidence that the device itself (not just the account) is compromised.

## Important reassurance
Losing access to a digital identity is jarring and can cause significant distress, particularly if it involves your business or years of personal memories. Panic often leads to the second wave of harm—scams. By securing your email first and following official routes, you have already taken the most effective defensive steps possible.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. It does not cover the specifics of long-term account litigation, data recovery from platform servers, or complex intellectual property disputes.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not guarantee account restoration. Social media platforms are private entities with their own terms of service; they retain the final decision on account status. If you are a victim of financial fraud as a result of the lockout, contact your bank immediately.
