# What to do if… an ex keeps contacting you from new accounts with sexual messages after you blocked them (UK)

## Short answer
Cease all interaction immediately and use platform settings to block "existing and future" accounts while logging the contact pattern for a specialist or police report.

## Do not do these things
- Don't respond to any message, even to say "stop" or "leave me alone," as any engagement can reinforce the behavior.
- Don't delete the messages immediately before taking a screenshot, as you may need them for a report later.
- Don't negotiate or argue with the sender through any channel or third parties.
- Don't post about the harassment publicly on social media, as this can escalate the situation and compromise your privacy.
- Don't ignore the situation if you feel your physical safety is at risk; online harassment can transition to offline behavior.

## What to do now
1. **Remove yourself from the immediate digital environment to reduce distress.** 
   Place your device on "Do Not Disturb" mode, close the relevant apps, and step away for several minutes to break the cycle of immediate notifications.

2. **Commit to a "no response" rule regardless of the message content.** 
   If you have already clearly stated that you do not want contact, do not repeat this; any further communication from you provides the sender with the attention they are seeking.

3. **Capture a minimal, factual record of the contact before blocking.** 
   Save a single screenshot of each new account that includes:
   - The username and profile handle (e.g., @example).
   - The date and time of the message.
   - The content of the sexual or harassing message.

4. **Use platform-level tools to block the person and any future accounts they may create.** 
   Most major social media platforms now provide an option to "block [user] and any other accounts they may have or create." Ensure this specific option is selected.

5. **Adjust your privacy settings to restrict who can send you message requests.** 
   Navigate to the "Privacy" or "Messages" section of your settings and limit incoming messages to "Friends only" or "People I follow" to prevent new accounts from reaching your primary inbox.

6. **Contact 101 or report online if the behavior is persistent or threatening.** 
   If the contact continues after blocking or if you feel unsafe, report the pattern to the police as stalking or harassment.
   - Use 999 only if you believe there is an immediate threat to your life or if the person is at your location.

7. **Reach out to a specialist service for practical safety planning and support.** 
   Contact the National Stalking Helpline for advice on digital security and legal rights.
   - National Stalking Helpline: 0808 802 0300 (various hours).
   - Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 Support Line: 0808 500 2222 (for the sexual nature of the messages).

8. **Secure your other accounts if you suspect the sender has access to your information.** 
   If they are finding you across multiple platforms or mention things they should not know, use a different, secure device to change your passwords and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to pursue a court injunction or a Non-Molestation Order immediately.
- You do not need to organize a full chronological timeline of every past message right now.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your wider social circle or mutual friends today.
- You do not need to delete your social media accounts permanently to stop the contact.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed, hyper-vigilant, or frustrated when blocking does not immediately stop the behavior. These reactions are common responses to a breach of your boundaries. Focusing on repeatable, technical steps—block, report, and log—is a constructive way to manage the immediate situation without engaging the sender.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Ongoing stalking or harassment often requires a coordinated response involving police and specialist advocacy services to address long-term safety.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If the sender makes direct threats of physical harm or shares non-consensual intimate images, contact the police immediately. Always prioritize your physical safety and use secure devices if you believe your technology has been compromised.
