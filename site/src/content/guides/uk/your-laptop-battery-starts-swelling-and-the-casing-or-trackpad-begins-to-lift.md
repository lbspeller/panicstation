# What to do if… your laptop battery starts swelling and the casing or trackpad begins to lift (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately unlink or log out the unknown device from within the messaging app's settings and enable two-step verification (or a Registration Lock) to prevent the device from re-connecting.

## Do not do these things
- Don't send messages, codes, or screenshots to the unknown device or any contact claiming to be "support" to resolve the issue.
- Don't click on links or scan QR codes in messages that claim you need to "verify" your account to remove the device.
- Don't ignore the notification if you didn't personally link a device; linked devices can see your live messages and history.
- Don't attempt to "hack back" or investigate the device yourself.
- Don't change your primary phone number until you have attempted to secure the existing account.

## What to do now
1. **Unlink the unknown device immediately from within the app settings.** 
   - **Open the app and find the menu for linked devices.** 
   - **On WhatsApp, go to Settings > Linked Devices.** 
   - **On Signal, go to Settings > Linked Devices.** 
   - **On Telegram, go to Settings > Devices.** 
   - **Select the unrecognised device and choose "Log out" or "Unlink".**
2. **Take a single screenshot for evidence before removing the device.** **Ensure the screenshot captures the device name, the date it was linked, and its last active status.** 
3. **Enable two-step verification (2SV) or a Registration Lock immediately.** 
   - **For Signal: Go to Settings > Account > Registration Lock and turn it on.** 
   - **For WhatsApp: Go to Settings > Account > Two-step verification > Turn on.** 
   - **Create a PIN that is not easily guessed and provide a recovery email you control.**
4. **Enable a Passkey if your app supports it.** **Go to your app's security settings and set up a Passkey (local device authentication) to add a layer that requires your thumbprint or face ID for account changes.**
5. **Check for SIM-swap indicators.** **If your phone has suddenly lost all network signal (calls/texts/data) while others around you have signal, contact your mobile network provider immediately using a different phone to report a suspected SIM swap.**
6. **Review your linked email account's security.** **If your messaging app uses an email for recovery, log into that email and check for any new forwarding rules, filters, or unfamiliar recovery phone numbers.**
7. **Notify your close contacts via a different communication method.** **Send a short text or call key people to say: "My messaging account was accessed by an unknown device. Please ignore any unusual links or requests for money sent from my account."**
8. **Report the incident to the appropriate authorities if you suspect a crime.** 
   - **In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland: Report to "Report Fraud" (the successor to Action Fraud) via their official website or by calling 0300 123 2040.** 
   - **In Scotland: Use the Police Scotland Online Crime Reporting Form or call 101.** 
   - **Call 999 if you are in immediate physical danger or a crime is currently in progress.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the physical location of the unknown device right now.
- You do not need to replace your physical phone handset unless you confirm the device itself is infected with malware.
- You do not need to delete your entire messaging account or chat history immediately.

## Important reassurance
Seeing an unknown linked device is a common security event, often caused by "QR code phishing" or "session hijacking." By unlinking the device and enabling two-step verification, you effectively "kill" the attacker's session and prevent them from easily re-linking, which stops the compromise in most cases.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for unrecognised linked devices on encrypted messaging platforms. It does not cover deep forensic device analysis or long-term stalking protection.

## Important note
The information provided here is for immediate harm reduction and does not constitute legal or technical advice. If the unknown device reappears after removal, or if you are part of a high-risk group (such as journalists, government officials, or victims of domestic abuse), seek specialist support from the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) or a certified security professional.
