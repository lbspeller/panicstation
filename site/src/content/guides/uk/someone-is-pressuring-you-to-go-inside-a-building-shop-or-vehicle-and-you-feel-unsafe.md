# What to do if… someone is pressuring you to go inside a building, shop, or vehicle and you feel unsafe (UK)

## Short answer
Do not enter the building, shop, or vehicle. Move toward a staffed, public place and get another person involved straight away.

## Do not do these things
- Do not enter a more private place, such as a back room, hallway, vehicle, side street, or area behind a shop, to be polite or avoid awkwardness.
- Do not let them move you towards a doorway, around a corner, into a lift, or closer to a vehicle while you are talking.
- Do not argue at close range if you can step away.
- Do not hand over your phone, unlock it for them, or let them use it.
- Do not accept a lift, a quick stop, or sitting in a vehicle to talk.
- Do not follow them to a cashpoint or somewhere else to sort it out.
- Do not stay quiet to avoid a scene if you feel unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause first.** Step away while saying something short, such as: “No. I’m staying here.” Move towards people, light, and a staffed place, such as a shop counter, café, station entrance, hotel lobby, or open reception desk.
2. **Put something or someone between you and them.** Move behind a counter, into a checkout line, next to staff, or near a group of people. If you are outside, change direction, cross the road, or go inside only if the place is open and staffed.
3. **Tell staff clearly what is happening.** Say: “I need help. This person is pressuring me to go somewhere and I feel unsafe. Please stay with me and call 999 if they will not leave or if they try to move me.”
4. **Use Ask for Angela where it is available.** In some participating bars, clubs, and venues, asking staff for “Angela” is understood as a discreet request for help. You can still say directly, “I feel unsafe and need help,” if that is clearer or faster.
5. **Call emergency services if there is immediate danger.** Call 999 or 112 if they are trying to physically move you, blocking your exit, following you closely, threatening you, or you cannot get away safely.
6. **If you call 999 and cannot safely speak, stay on the line.** Try to speak, whisper, cough, tap, or respond to the operator if you can. If you are calling from a mobile and are prompted, press 55 or tap so the call can be transferred to police.
7. **Use your phone to make a live witness only if it is safe.** Call someone you trust and say where you are while moving into a staffed place. If calling could increase the risk, send one short message with your location and: “I’m being pressured to go somewhere and I feel unsafe. Please stay available or call 999 if I stop replying.”
8. **If a vehicle is involved, move away from the doors.** Do not stand where you can be pulled or boxed in. Move towards a staffed place, a group of people, or a physical barrier such as a counter, bollard, or wall with people nearby.
9. **If you are already in a taxi or private hire vehicle and feel unsafe, ask to stop somewhere public.** Ask to get out at a busy, well-lit place with people nearby, such as a shop, petrol station, station, hotel, or staffed venue.
10. **Record details only once you have a safer pause.** Text yourself the location, time, description, direction they went, and any vehicle colour, make, or registration. If you are inside a business, ask staff to keep any CCTV if they can.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal report.
- You do not need to prove your feeling or explain yourself fully.
- You do not need to confront them or work out their motive.
- You do not need to write a full account until you are somewhere safer.
- You can think about support, reporting, or practical next steps later.

## Important reassurance
You are allowed to act on feeling unsafe before anything obvious happens. Pressure to move you into a building, shop, or vehicle can be enough reason to step away, involve other people, and call for help.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: creating distance, getting to a staffed or public place, involving other people, and contacting emergency help if needed. Later decisions about reporting, support, or safety planning may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in immediate danger, call 999 or 112 now.
