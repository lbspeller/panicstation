# What to do if… your heating system stops working and the indoor temperature is dropping fast (UK)

## Short answer
Move everyone to one room, add layers/blankets, and check for a gas leak or carbon monoxide symptoms immediately. Report the failure to your landlord or heating engineer now and arrange a warm place to stay if the temperature cannot be stabilised.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use a gas oven, hob, BBQ, or camping stove to heat your home; this creates a fatal carbon monoxide risk.
- Do not leave portable electric heaters unattended or running while you are asleep.
- Do not plug high-wattage heaters into extension leads or multi-plug adapters as this causes fire risks.
- Do not attempt to repair or dismantle gas pipes, valves, or internal boiler components yourself.
- Do not ignore symptoms like sudden headaches, nausea, or dizziness occurring in more than one person.
- Do not waste hot water if you have a small amount stored; save it for hot water bottles or hygiene.

## What to do now
1. **Gather everyone into one small, easy-to-heat room and isolate it from the rest of the house.** **Close all internal doors, pull curtains or blinds shut, and block draughts at the bottom of doors using rolled-up towels or blankets.**
2. **Check for immediate gas or carbon monoxide hazards before investigating the heating system.** **If you smell gas (rotten eggs) or a carbon monoxide alarm is sounding, exit the property immediately and call the National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999.**
   - If safe to do so, turn off the gas at the meter (Emergency Control Valve) by turning the handle 90 degrees to the pipe.
   - If anyone is dizzy, confused, has collapsed, or is breathless, call 999 immediately.
3. **Perform a quick check of your energy supply and thermostat settings.** **Confirm your gas and electricity meters have credit (if prepayment) and that the thermostat is set to "On" with a target temperature higher than the current room reading.**
   - Check if the boiler is displaying an error code, but do not attempt to fix it.
   - Check if other neighbours have power to rule out a local area outage.
4. **Contact your landlord, letting agent, or emergency repair service immediately if you rent.** **State clearly that you have a total loss of heating and the temperature is dropping, and follow up your call with a timestamped email or text for evidence.**
   - For social housing tenants, under Awaab’s Law (2026), your landlord must investigate emergency hazards like "excess cold" within 24 hours.
5. **Call your boiler cover provider or a Gas Safe registered engineer if you own the home.** **Book the earliest possible emergency call-out and ensure the engineer shows you their Gas Safe ID card upon arrival.**
6. **Protect your plumbing from freezing if the indoor temperature is likely to drop below 5°C.** **Open cupboard doors under kitchen and bathroom sinks to allow what warmth remains to reach the pipes, and keep a cold tap dripping slightly to maintain water movement.**
7. **Use only safe methods to maintain body heat while waiting for repairs.** **Wear multiple layers of clothing (especially wool or thermal), use hot water bottles filled with hot (not boiling) water, and drink warm liquids frequently.**
   - If using a portable electric heater, plug it directly into a wall socket and sit at least one metre away from it.
8. **Activate your backup plan if the home becomes unsafe or you are on the Priority Services Register.** **If the temperature cannot be maintained above 16°C and you have children, elderly residents, or medical conditions, move to a friend's house or hotel early before it gets dark.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific broken part or research boiler models tonight.
- You do not need to negotiate compensation or rent reductions while the emergency is active.
- You do not need to contact your energy supplier about billing disputes or smart meter issues right now.
- You do not need to clean the area around the boiler unless it is blocking access for the engineer.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of rising panic when the temperature drops indoors, as your environment is changing rapidly. Focus on the immediate safety of the people in the house first; the building itself can be repaired later. You are taking the correct steps by isolating one warm room and calling for professional help.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a sudden heating failure in the UK. It does not cover technical boiler repair, long-term energy efficiency, or legal disputes regarding tenancy.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and is not a substitute for professional technical or medical advice. Always prioritize evacuation if a gas leak or carbon monoxide is suspected. If you are vulnerable, ensure you are registered on the Priority Services Register with your energy network operator for future priority support.
