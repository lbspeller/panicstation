# What to do if… your cloud account shows massive deletions you did not perform (UK)

## Short answer
Pause syncing and secure the account from a trusted device before you do anything that could permanently remove recoverable files. Then use the provider’s deleted-items, recycle bin, version history, or previous-time restore tools as soon as you can.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep every device online and syncing while you investigate; it can spread deletions or overwrites.
- Don’t empty the cloud trash, recycle bin, or recently deleted area.
- Don’t permanently delete anything while checking what happened.
- Don’t reinstall, reset, or wipe devices yet if they may show account activity, local file copies, or recovery prompts.
- Don’t click links in emails or texts claiming to be support.
- Don’t use phone numbers or chat links sent in unexpected messages.
- Don’t approve unexpected sign-in or MFA prompts.
- Don’t reuse the same password, or a slightly changed version, if compromise is possible.
- Don’t pay a ransom, stranger, or “recovery fee” before getting guidance from the provider, your bank, or the police if money or extortion is involved.
- Don’t assume it is only a sync glitch until you have checked sign-in activity, sharing changes, and connected apps.

## What to do now
1. **Pause syncing and stop new changes**
   - Disconnect affected devices from the internet: turn off Wi-Fi, unplug ethernet, or use airplane mode on phones.
   - Pause the cloud sync app if you can do that without deleting or moving files.
   - Leave the cloud trash, recycle bin, recently deleted area, and version history untouched.
   - If this is a work or school account, tell IT or the account admin now and ask them to temporarily block suspicious sign-ins while recovery is checked.

2. **Record what you can see**
   - Take screenshots, photos, or exports of recent activity, sign-in history, deletion events, device names, IP or location details if shown, sharing links, new collaborators, and permission changes.
   - Write down the first time you noticed the deletions, the device you were using, and the cloud account affected.
   - Save this record somewhere outside the affected cloud account, such as a local folder, another trusted account, or a written note.

3. **Secure the account from one trusted route**
   - Use a device you normally use and trust, or another device you trust.
   - Type the provider’s website address yourself or use the official app.
   - Change the cloud account password to a strong, unique password.
   - Turn on 2-step verification if it is not already on.
   - Use the provider’s security settings to sign out other devices and sessions where that option is available.
   - Remove devices, sessions, connected apps, third-party access, recovery emails, or recovery phone numbers you do not recognise.

4. **Check the email account linked to the cloud account**
   - Sign in to the linked email account through its official site or app.
   - Change its password too if you suspect anyone else may have access.
   - Check for auto-forwarding, mailbox rules, filters, recovery details, or connected apps you did not create.
   - Remove anything suspicious, because email access can be used to reset cloud passwords.

5. **Start file recovery using the provider’s tools**
   - Check Trash, Recycle Bin, Bin, Recently Deleted, or Deleted Files.
   - Check version history for files that were overwritten rather than deleted.
   - Look for any “restore your drive”, “rewind”, “restore account”, or “restore to an earlier time” feature.
   - Restore a small, important test item first if the provider allows it and you are unsure what the recovery action will change.
   - If this is an organisation account, ask the admin to check admin recovery, audit logs, retention, backup, or previous-version options.

6. **Contact the cloud provider through official support**
   - Use in-product help or the provider’s official support pages, not links sent by email or text.
   - Say clearly: mass deletions, not done by you, approximate time window, affected folders, suspected account takeover, and any unknown app or session you found.
   - Ask what deleted-item, version-history, previous-time restore, admin recovery, and server-side retention options apply to your account or plan.

7. **Report fraud or extortion if money, threats, or scams are involved**
   - If you have lost money, were asked for money, or suspect cyber crime or fraud in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report it through Report Fraud.
   - If you live in Scotland or the crime happened in Scotland, contact Police Scotland on 101.
   - If an emergency is happening now, call 999.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change cloud providers.
- You do not need to rebuild your whole backup system before trying recovery.
- You do not need to notify every contact, customer, or collaborator until you know more about what was accessed or shared, unless your workplace or regulator requires an immediate internal report.
- You do not need to reinstall operating systems, replace hardware, or do deep device forensics before preserving evidence and trying account recovery.
- You do not need to reorganise folders, tidy permissions, or re-share files until the account is stable.

## Important reassurance
This is alarming, but it is not automatically permanent. Many cloud services keep deleted items, versions, or account-restore options for a limited period, so careful action now can improve the chance of getting files back.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to stabilise the situation, secure access, and maximise the chance of recovery. If this involves a workplace, school, customer files, client files, regulated data, or a targeted compromise, tell the owner, manager, IT/security team, or data-protection contact before making wider decisions.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, technical, security, or professional advice. Cloud providers’ recovery windows, logging, and admin options vary, so use the provider’s official support steps and treat any time limits as urgent.
