# What to do if… your external hard drive suddenly will not mount and important files are on it (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the device immediately: shut it down, unplug the charger, and move it to a non-flammable surface outdoors or in a well-ventilated area away from people. Treat the device as a fire hazard until the battery is safely removed or the unit is disposed of via a specialist hazardous waste route.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep the device plugged into a power source or continue using it.
- Don’t press down on the bulging casing or try to force the trackpad back into place.
- Don’t attempt to "deflate" the battery by puncturing, bending, or crushing it.
- Don’t put the device or battery in household rubbish, kerbside recycling, or standard battery bins.
- Don’t store the laptop on flammable surfaces like beds, sofas, or near curtains.
- Don’t attempt to remove the battery yourself if it is glued in or requires prying.

## What to do now
1. **Power off the device immediately.** **Perform a standard software shutdown if the screen is responsive; otherwise, hold the power button until the lights turn off.**
2. **Disconnect all cables.** **Unplug the charger from the wall and the laptop, and remove any connected USB devices or accessories.**
3. **Isolate the device in a safe location.** **Move the laptop to a hard, non-flammable surface such as stone, tile, or a metal tray.** 
   - If weather permits, place it outdoors in a dry, sheltered area.
   - If indoors, keep it in a cool, well-ventilated room away from exits and flammable materials.
4. **Monitor for signs of immediate failure.** **Watch the device from a safe distance for smoke, sparks, hissing sounds, a sweet chemical smell, or rapid heat buildup.**
5. **Call 999 if active fire or smoke occurs.** **State clearly that you have a "swollen lithium-ion battery fire" and keep everyone away from the area until the fire service arrives.**
6. **Protect your data without powering on.** **Do not turn the laptop back on to retrieve files, as energising a failing battery can trigger a fire.** 
   - A professional repair technician can safely remove the internal storage drive to recover your data once the device is stable.
7. **Contact the manufacturer or retailer.** **Report the issue as a "swollen battery/safety hazard" to see if they provide a specific courier or take-back service for defective units.**
8. **Arrange for specialist disposal.** **Contact your local council to find a Household Waste Recycling Centre (HWRC) that specifically accepts "damaged or swollen lithium batteries."** 
   - Most standard in-store battery bins are not equipped to handle swollen cells safely.
9. **Report the safety issue.** **If the device failed under normal use, report it to the relevant consumer body for your region.**
   - England and Wales: Citizens Advice consumer service.
   - Scotland: Advice Direct Scotland.
   - Northern Ireland: Your local district council.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to determine why the battery failed right now.
- You do **not** need to decide on a replacement laptop today.
- You do **not** need to wipe your personal data until the device is professionally stabilized.

## Important reassurance
Battery swelling is a built-in safety mechanism designed to contain gas buildup and prevent a sudden fire. By stopping use, unplugging the device, and moving it to a safe spot, you have already significantly reduced the risk of a serious incident.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and harm prevention for defective lithium-ion batteries. It does not provide instructions for technical repair or long-term storage of hazardous materials.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not replace professional technical or emergency services advice. If you observe smoke, flames, or intense heat, prioritize life safety and evacuate the area before calling emergency services.
