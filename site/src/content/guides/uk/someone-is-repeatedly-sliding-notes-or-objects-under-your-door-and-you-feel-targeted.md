# What to do if… someone is repeatedly sliding notes or objects under your door and you feel targeted (UK)

## Short answer
Treat repeated notes or objects at your door as a safety issue. Stay inside with the door locked if that feels safest, record what happened, and call 999 if there is an immediate threat; otherwise contact police on 101 or through your local force online.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to confront the person or try to catch them yourself.
- Do not follow anyone into the corridor, stairwell, lift, car park, or street to investigate.
- Do not reply to the notes, slide messages back, or try to negotiate through the door.
- Do not throw the notes or objects away before you have photographed and logged them.
- Do not post publicly in building groups in a way that could escalate the situation or identify you as the reporter.
- Do not delay reporting just because you do not know who is doing it.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause and secure your space.** Lock the door, use the chain or deadlock if you have one, and move away from the doorway.
2. **Call 999 if there is an immediate threat.** This includes someone trying to force entry, threatening you, refusing to leave your door, or making you feel unsafe right now.
3. **Record the incident before moving anything.** Take photos of the note or object where it was found, the door gap, any marks, and the surrounding area if it is safe to do so.
4. **Write a simple incident log.** Note the date, time, what was left, where it was found, and anything you heard or saw, such as knocking, footsteps, voices, or someone in the hallway.
5. **Keep the items safely.** Put each note or object in a clean envelope or bag, label it with the date and time found, and keep everything together.
6. **Tell one trusted person what is happening.** Send them a brief message and agree a check-in for later today, especially if you live alone.
7. **Report repeated incidents to police as non-emergency.** Call 101 or use your local police force’s online reporting route for stalking or harassment. Say the behaviour is repeated, it is happening at your home, you feel targeted, and you have kept a log and the items. Ask for the reference number.
8. **Notify whoever controls the building.** Contact your landlord, letting agent, building manager, or concierge in writing if possible. Ask them to log the incident, check access to the corridor, preserve any CCTV for the relevant times because footage may be overwritten, and confirm any immediate steps to reduce access to your door area.
9. **Contact specialist stalking support if you want help without pressure.** The National Stalking Helpline can help you think through safer next steps and what to document.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now who it is or why they are doing it.
- You do not need to confront neighbours, run your own investigation, or prove everything before reporting.
- You do not need to make big decisions tonight, such as moving out, changing jobs, or ending a relationship.
- Formal complaints, legal options, and longer-term housing decisions can wait until the immediate incidents are logged and you have contacted police, building management, or a specialist service.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shaken by repeated targeted behaviour at your own door is understandable. Taking it seriously, documenting it, and asking for help early is a reasonable response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation, reduce risk, and get the right people involved. If the behaviour continues or escalates, later decisions may need specialist help from police, your housing provider, a stalking support service, or a qualified adviser.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, security, housing, or other professional advice.
