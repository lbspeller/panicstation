# What to do if… your charging port is not working and your device is running out of power (UK)

## Short answer
Preserve power first, then try one safe charging route without forcing the plug. Save anything you may need to show or use if the device dies, such as tickets, QR codes, 2FA access, and a contact number.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t jam pins, needles, knives, paperclips, cotton buds, or anything metal into the port.
- Don’t keep forcing the plug in if it will not sit normally.
- Don’t keep swapping chargers rapidly in a panic.
- Don’t use a damaged, frayed, hot-smelling, wet, or unknown high-power charger or cable.
- If you see a liquid or debris warning, don’t use heat, compressed air, rice, or foreign objects to dry or clear the port.
- Don’t factory reset, wipe, reinstall, or “clean install” the device while you are low on power unless you have confirmed backups and no other option.

## What to do now
1. **Buy power immediately.**  
   Turn on battery saver or low power mode, reduce brightness, close video or navigation apps, turn off Bluetooth, and use airplane mode unless you need mobile data right now.

2. **Save the things you may need if the device dies.**  
   Screenshot or save tickets, boarding passes, QR codes, collection codes, booking references, addresses, and any current warning message. Send one trusted person a short message such as “my phone may die; if I go quiet, try me on this number or email”.

3. **Protect account access before the battery reaches zero.**  
   If you rely on app-based 2FA, use an already-set-up backup code, trusted device, printed code, or alternative login route if you already have one. Avoid changing passwords, moving accounts, or setting up new security methods while rushed unless you must.

4. **Do one controlled charging test.**  
   Use a known-good wall socket, known-good plug, and known-good cable that matches the device. Connect it once without force and wait a few minutes. Stop if there is heat, a burning smell, visible damage, a liquid warning, or no response after the controlled test.

5. **Check the port without poking inside it.**  
   Look into the port in good light. If you see loose lint or debris, hold the port facing downward and gently tap the device against your hand. Do not scrape inside the port; if the debris does not fall out easily, leave it for manufacturer guidance or repair support.

6. **Use another charging method if your device supports one.**  
   Try Qi or your device’s supported wireless charging method, a wireless power bank, or reverse wireless charging from a compatible device. Make sure the back of the device is dry before using wireless charging.

7. **Aim for enough charge, not a full fix.**  
   Once you get a little power, keep battery saver on and use the device only for the urgent task: identity checks, payment, travel, calls, messages, tickets, or directions.

8. **If the device may be faulty, keep the repair and consumer route clean.**  
   Do not open the device, bend the port, or do risky cleaning if it is under warranty or was bought recently. Keep proof of purchase, note what happened, save any warning messages, and contact the retailer if the fault may have happened during normal use. Check the consumer advice route for your UK nation if you need help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether it is water damage, lint, a bad cable, a charger problem, or a hardware fault.
- You do not need to run diagnostics, reinstall the operating system, deep clean the port, or argue about blame today.
- You do not need to get the battery to 100%; enough power to keep access and finish the urgent task is enough for now.

## Important reassurance
A charging failure often comes from a cable, socket, lint, moisture warning, temperature issue, or protective lockout rather than total device failure. The safest next move is to avoid making the port worse, preserve access, and get one reliable charging route.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate low-power situation only. Later decisions about repair, replacement, warranty, backups, or consumer rights may need device-specific support or specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not device-specific repair, legal, financial, or professional advice. If you see heat, a burning smell, melting, corrosion, swelling, visible damage, or repeated liquid or debris warnings, stop charging attempts and use authorised support or repair.
