# What to do if… an accommodation asks to keep your passport overnight and you feel uncomfortable (UK)

## Short answer
Keep your passport in your control if you can. Ask the accommodation to inspect it, record the details they need while you wait, and return it immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t hand over your passport overnight just to end the conversation if you feel uneasy or they will not explain what they need.
- Don’t let your passport be kept as a deposit or security if there is another safe option.
- Don’t argue at the desk if you feel unsafe. Stay calm, stay in a public area, and focus on getting your document back.
- Don’t leave without knowing who has your passport, where it is stored, when it will be returned, and who is responsible for returning it.
- Don’t report your passport as lost or stolen unless it really is lost, stolen, or they refuse to return it.

## What to do now
1. Ask one clear question: “Can you tell me exactly why you need to keep the original passport overnight?”

2. Offer a safer alternative immediately:
   - “I can wait here while you take the details you need.”
   - “Can you inspect the passport and note the details without keeping it?”
   - “If you need a copy, can you explain why, how it will be stored, and when it will be deleted or destroyed?”

3. Keep the passport in your sight while they process it. Ask them to complete the check at the desk, or ask for a manager to do it now.

4. If they insist on keeping it overnight, use a calm boundary: “I’m not comfortable leaving my passport overnight. If that is required, I may need to cancel check-in and stay elsewhere.”

5. Before leaving the desk, check your practical options. Look for another accommodation, contact your booking platform if you used one, or call someone you trust so you are not deciding alone.

6. If you choose to leave the passport with them anyway:
   - Get a written receipt with your name, the staff member’s name, the date and time, and the exact return time.
   - Include the passport number only if they have already recorded it or it is needed to identify the document.
   - Ask where it will be stored, whether it will be locked away, and who can access it.

7. If they already have your passport and you want it back now, ask for the manager and say: “I need my passport returned now. I’m happy to provide the details you need, but I cannot leave it overnight.”

8. If they refuse to return it, move to a safer public place if you can. Use the local police or local emergency route for the country you are in, and contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate for help.

9. Reduce the immediate damage. Keep your bank cards, phone, other ID, and bags with you; find any secure photo or copy of your passport details; and write down the accommodation name, address, staff names, and times.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal complaint, ask for compensation, or leave a review.
- You do not need to start replacement steps unless the passport is genuinely not returned, lost, stolen, damaged, or unusable.
- You do not need to prove who is right at the desk. The priority is your document, your safety, and a place to stay.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel pressured at check-in, especially abroad or late at night. Wanting to keep your passport with you is a reasonable safety instinct, and it is okay to slow the situation down.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate situation only. If your passport is withheld, lost, stolen, or you cannot travel without it, later decisions may need help from local authorities, consular staff, your insurer, or your travel provider.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, travel, or other professional advice. Local rules vary by country, and some places may require accommodation providers to inspect or record passport details, so check the country-specific travel advice and prioritise getting to a safer place if you feel at risk.
