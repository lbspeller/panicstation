# What to do if… you accidentally put the wrong fuel in your vehicle and have not started the engine (UK)

## Short answer
Keep the engine off, do not turn the ignition to the "on" position, and call a professional fuel-drain service or your breakdown provider immediately.

## Do not do these things
- Don't start the engine or turn the ignition on to move the car even a few meters.
- Don't put the key in the ignition or press the "start" button to listen to the radio or use the air conditioning.
- Don't try to "top up" the tank with the correct fuel to dilute the mixture.
- Don't attempt to siphon the fuel out yourself using a hose or mouth-suction.
- Don't let an untrained person attempt to tow the vehicle with the wheels on the road if it is an automatic or has an electric handbrake.
- Don't panic or leave the vehicle unattended at the pump without informing the station staff.

## What to do now
1. **Leave the engine switched off and remove the key from the ignition or move the keyless fob away from the vehicle.** In many modern vehicles, the fuel pump primes (begins circulating fuel) the moment the ignition is turned to "on" or even when the driver's door is opened.
2. **Inform the petrol station staff immediately that you have misfuelled.** They can stop the pump, provide safety cones, and may have contact details for local specialist fuel-drain services.
3. **Put the vehicle in neutral and push it to a safe parking bay if it is blocking a pump.** Seek help from staff or bystanders to push the car so you do not have to turn the engine on.
   - Note: If your vehicle has an electric parking brake or automatic transmission that requires the ignition to be "on" to select neutral, consult the station staff or your breakdown provider before touching the controls.
4. **Call a specialist fuel-drain service or your breakdown provider’s "Fuel Assist" line.** Specifically state that you have misfuelled and that the engine has **not** been started.
   - AA Fuel Assist: 0370 240 3985
   - RAC Fuel Patrol: 0330 332 8456
   - If you have standalone misfuel insurance, call their emergency number.
5. **Collect your fuel receipt and note the pump number and time.** This documentation is essential for insurance claims, fleet management reports, or rental car agreements.
6. **Contact your rental or leasing company immediately if the vehicle is not yours.** Most rental agreements have specific protocols for misfuelling, and failing to follow them can void your damage waiver.

## What can wait
- You do not need to worry about permanent engine damage yet; as long as the fuel has not circulated, the fix is usually a straightforward drain and flush.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent repair garage right now; the mobile technician can usually perform the drain on the forecourt.
- You do not need to research the cost of new injectors or fuel pumps, as these are unlikely to be damaged if the engine remained off.

## Important reassurance
Misfuelling happens to approximately 150,000 drivers in the UK every year. Because you realized the error before starting the engine, you have likely prevented the most expensive and complex damage. This is a common situation that recovery professionals handle every day.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for a vehicle that has not been started. If the engine has been turned on, even briefly, the situation requires a more extensive fuel system flush and professional mechanical inspection.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace the specific mechanical instructions found in your vehicle’s handbook. Different vehicle manufacturers have varying tolerances for fuel contamination; always defer to the advice of the certified recovery technician or your vehicle's authorized service center.
