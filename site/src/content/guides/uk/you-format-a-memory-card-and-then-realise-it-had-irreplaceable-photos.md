# What to do if… you format a memory card and then realise it had irreplaceable photos (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the memory card immediately and remove it from the device to prevent any new data from overwriting the formatted files. In many cases, the data remains on the card until it is replaced by new files.

## Do not do these things
- Don't take new photos or videos using the affected card.
- Don't format the card a second time, even if prompted by a device.
- Don't use "chkdsk," "disk repair," or "error checking" utilities on the card.
- Don't save or move any new files onto the card.
- Don't attempt to "preview" files if the card appears empty.
- Don't accept unsolicited offers for remote-access data recovery.

## What to do now
1. **Remove the card from the device immediately.**  
   **Physically isolate the media to ensure no background processes or automated file indexing can write to the storage.**
2. **Engage the physical write-protect switch.**  
   **If using a full-sized SD card, slide the "Lock" tab on the side of the casing to the downward position before inserting it into a computer.**
   - This prevents the operating system from accidentally writing metadata to the card.
3. **Check for existing synchronised copies.**  
   **Verify if the photos were previously uploaded to a cloud service or imported to a computer hard drive before the format occurred.**
   - Search "Recently Deleted" or "Trash" folders in cloud photo apps.
   - Check local "Pictures" or "Downloads" folders on any recently connected laptops.
4. **Choose between professional services or specialized software.**  
   **Determine if the value of the images justifies the cost of a professional laboratory recovery, which is the safest option for critical data.**
   - If attempting a DIY recovery, use a dedicated card reader rather than connecting the camera via USB.
   - Only use reputable recovery software that performs a "Read-Only" scan.
   - Always save recovered files to a different physical drive, never back onto the formatted card.
5. **Report suspected recovery scams.**  
   **If you are targeted by fraudulent services demanding immediate payment for "guaranteed" recovery, report the incident.**
   - In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland: Use the **Report Fraud** online service or call 0300 123 2040.
   - In Scotland: Contact **Police Scotland** via the 101 non-emergency number or use their online crime reporting form.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to discard or keep the card for future use right now.
- You do not need to investigate why the format happened until the recovery attempt is complete.
- You do not need to sort or organize other photo libraries until the status of the lost data is confirmed.
- You do not need to pursue retailer claims under the Consumer Rights Act until you have attempted data retrieval.

## Important reassurance
Accidental formatting is a frequent occurrence and does not necessarily result in permanent data destruction. Most formatting processes only clear the file directory, leaving the actual image data intact until new information is written over it. By stopping use immediately, you have maintained the highest possible chance of a successful recovery.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for digital data loss. It does not cover physical hardware repair or forensic investigation for legal proceedings.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute technical or professional advice. Data recovery outcomes are never guaranteed and depend on the specific device, file system, and subsequent usage of the media. Be cautious of any service promising 100% success rates.
