# What to do if… your student loan automatic payment fails because your bank details are incorrect or outdated (UK)

## Short answer
Check your official student loan repayment account by typing the GOV.UK address yourself, not by using message links. If the account shows a failed or due payment, make one official payment only, then update your bank details or Direct Debit.

## Do not do these things
- Do not click payment links in texts or emails, or call numbers from them, until you have checked your account independently.
- Do not keep retrying the same payment; it may create duplicate payments or possible fees.
- Do not assume a “bank details mismatch” message is real if you normally repay through PAYE payroll.
- Do not ignore a failed Direct Debit, recurring card payment, or overseas repayment notice once it appears in your official account.
- Do not share login codes, secret answers, full card details, or full banking details with anyone who contacted you first.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether you normally repay from payroll or from a bank payment.**
   - If you repay through **PAYE payroll**, repayments are usually taken from your salary, not from your bank account. Check your payslip and your official repayment account before treating a “bank details mismatch” message as real.
   - If you use a **Direct Debit, one-off card payment, recurring card payment, overseas payment, or voluntary extra repayment**, your bank details or payment method may need fixing.

2. **Sign in to your official student loan repayment account.**
   - Go to GOV.UK yourself and use the student loan repayment account service.
   - Check whether there is a failed payment, missed payment, message from SLC, changed bank detail, or Direct Debit problem.
   - If you cannot sign in, use the official account recovery or SLC contact route, not details from an unexpected message.

3. **If the account shows money is due, pay once using an official route.**
   - Use the repayment account or the official extra repayment page.
   - If you pay by bank transfer or another manual method, use the payment reference GOV.UK asks for, usually your customer reference number for student loan payments.
   - If you are overseas, use the official online account or the current official overseas payment details; do not copy bank details from an email or text.
   - Save the confirmation, amount, date, and time.

4. **Fix the automatic payment method.**
   - In the repayment account, check and update your bank details.
   - If you use Direct Debit, set up or amend the Direct Debit there.
   - Check the sort code and account number carefully, and make sure the account can accept Direct Debits.
   - If you use recurring card payments from overseas, check whether the card details or local-currency repayment setup need updating.

5. **Ask your bank what happened.**
   - Ask whether the Direct Debit instruction is active, cancelled, expired, or linked to an old account.
   - Ask whether a bank switch, account restriction, incorrect details, or insufficient funds caused the rejection.
   - If the bank charged a fee, ask whether it can be waived or reduced.

6. **Update employment details if you are leaving or have left the UK for more than 3 months.**
   - Use the separate official employment-details service.
   - Do this if you are leaving the UK for more than 3 months, returning after more than 3 months away, or SLC asks you to update your details.
   - This is separate from updating bank details; it helps SLC work out whether you should be repaying and at what rate.

7. **Contact SLC if the account still does not make sense.**
   - Tell them the payment method, the failed payment date, the amount, and what you have already done.
   - Ask them to confirm whether your account is up to date, whether there are arrears, and what they expect next.
   - Make a note of the date, time, and summary of the conversation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide anything about long-term repayment strategy today.
- You do not need to work out who is at fault before stopping missed or duplicate payments.
- You do not need to calculate a settlement figure unless your official account or SLC says that is the immediate issue.
- You do not need to respond to every message about the payment; focus on the official account and official contact route.

## Important reassurance
A failed automatic payment can happen after a bank switch, changed account details, cancelled mandate, card change, or overseas payment change. The immediate job is to check that the message is real, pay once if the official account shows money is due, and reconnect the correct payment method.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce panic, prevent duplicate payments, and avoid missed-payment problems. Later decisions, such as challenging charges, correcting records, or changing repayment arrangements, may need specialist financial guidance or direct help from SLC.

## Important note
This is general information, not financial, legal, debt, tax, or professional advice. Student loan repayment routes can differ depending on PAYE, Self Assessment, overseas status, voluntary repayments, loan plan, and account status, so follow what your official repayment account and SLC confirm for your situation.
