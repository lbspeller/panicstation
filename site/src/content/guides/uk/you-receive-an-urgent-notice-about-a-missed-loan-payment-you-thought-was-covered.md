# What to do if… you receive an urgent notice about a missed loan payment you thought was covered (UK)

## Short answer
Prioritise verification of the notice over immediate payment to avoid scams. Gather proof of your payment and contact the lender via an independently verified number to log the account as disputed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click links, download attachments, or call phone numbers provided in an unexpected text or email.
- Don’t make a "manual" second payment before confirming the first one failed; this can cause double-billing and complicates the refund process.
- Don’t ignore the notice while waiting for a bank statement; small delays can trigger automated credit reporting or late fees.
- Don’t cancel an existing Direct Debit mandate unless you have confirmed the notice is a scam or the lender has instructed you to do so in writing.
- Don’t share your full banking password or any one-time passcodes (OTP) with anyone claiming to "verify" your payment error.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the source of the notice using a trusted channel.**  
   **Log into your lender's official app or type their website address directly into your browser to check your account status.** Do not use any contact details found within the suspicious notice itself.

2. **Secure evidence of the payment attempt.**  
   **Find the specific transaction in your banking app or statement that shows the money leaving your account.** 
   - Note the exact date, time, amount, and reference used.
   - If the payment was a bank transfer, locate the "transaction ID" or "confirmation of payee" receipt.

3. **Contact the lender’s arrears or customer service team immediately.**  
   **Explain that you have proof of payment and ask them to place a "reporting hold" on your credit file while they investigate.** 
   - Ask them to confirm if the payment was "unallocated" (received but not applied to your balance).
   - Ask for a reference number for the dispute.

4. **Identify the failure point for Direct Debits.**  
   **Check your bank account for any "unpaid" or "returned" transaction notifications linked to the lender.** 
   - If the bank or lender made an error in the collection (e.g., wrong date or amount), you are entitled to a "full and immediate refund" under the Direct Debit Guarantee by contacting your bank.
   - Be aware that a Guarantee refund does not cancel the underlying debt; you must still ensure the lender receives the correct payment.

5. **Request a waiver of late fees and interest.**  
   **Instruct the lender to pause any late payment charges while the dispute is active.** Under FCA rules (CONC 7.3), lenders must treat customers in arrears with "forbearance and due consideration," which includes investigating genuine payment errors before escalating.

6. **Check your credit report for "missed payment" markers.**  
   **Access your file via Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion (using a free service like ClearScore or Credit Karma) to ensure no negative markers have appeared.** If an error is visible, you can add a "Notice of Correction" to your file while the lender fixes the record.

7. **Escalate to a formal complaint if not resolved within 15 days.**  
   **Submit a formal written complaint if the issue involves a "payment service" error (like a failed bank transfer or Direct Debit).** Lenders must generally provide a final response for payment service complaints within 15 business days. For general loan disputes, they have up to 8 weeks.

8. **Report suspected fraud to the authorities.**  
   **Contact Action Fraud / Report Fraud (0300 123 2040) if you believe the notice was a scam attempt or if your account details have been compromised.** If you are in Scotland, report via 101.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to decide on a debt management plan or consolidation loan today.
- You do **not** need to worry about your "numerical credit score" immediately; focus on correcting the factual accuracy of your payment history first.
- You do **not** need to provide a detailed explanation of your wider financial situation if the issue is a simple technical payment error.

## Important reassurance
Sudden arrears notices are frequently the result of automated systems failing to match a payment to an account reference or a minor banking delay. These are technical errors that can be corrected without lasting damage to your credit record, provided you log the dispute promptly.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only to prevent immediate financial escalation and protect your credit record. If you find you cannot afford the payment, seek free, independent debt advice from services like StepChange or National Debtline.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Timelines for reporting and complaint handling are subject to Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) regulations and individual lender terms.
