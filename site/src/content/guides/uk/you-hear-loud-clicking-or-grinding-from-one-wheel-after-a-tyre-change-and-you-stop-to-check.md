# What to do if… you hear loud clicking or grinding from one wheel after a tyre change and you stop to check (UK)

## Short answer
Stop the vehicle immediately in a safe location and do not continue the journey until a professional inspection confirms the wheel is securely torqued. If you are on a motorway and cannot reach an exit, move to the hard shoulder or an emergency area and exit the vehicle.

## Do not do these things
- Do not increase speed to "test" if the noise disappears.
- Do not attempt to tighten the wheel bolts with the basic boot tool kit if you are on a live carriageway or hard shoulder.
- Do not stand on the side of the vehicle exposed to moving traffic to inspect the wheel.
- Do not place any part of your body underneath the vehicle or into the wheel arch.
- Do not ignore the noise even if it seems to stop intermittently.

## What to do now
1. **Pull over to a safe location as soon as you hear any unusual metallic noise.**  
   - If you are on a local road, find a car park or wide lay-by.  
   - If you are on a motorway, follow the signs for an "Emergency Area" (orange markings) or move to the hard shoulder if an emergency area is not reachable.  
   - Avoid stopping on bends or just over the brow of a hill where you are less visible.

2. **Follow motorway breakdown protocols if you have stopped on a high-speed road.**  
   - **Switch on your hazard warning lights.**  
   - **Exit the vehicle via the passenger door (left side) only.**  
   - **Move behind the safety barrier and wait on the embankment well away from the traffic.**  
   - **Call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 or use an SOS phone if available.**  
   - **If you are in a live lane or feel in immediate danger, call 999.**

3. **Conduct a visual inspection only if you are safely off the road and away from traffic.**  
   - Look for missing wheel bolts or nuts.  
   - Check if the wheel appears to be leaning or not centered on the hub.  
   - Look for "shiny" metal shavings or fresh scrapes on the rim or around the bolt holes.  
   - Check for radial rust streaks or "clean" spots on the wheel that suggest the nuts have moved.

4. **Contact a breakdown recovery service or the garage that performed the tyre change.**  
   - State clearly: "I have a suspected loose wheel following a tyre change."  
   - Provide your exact location using a digital mapping tool or a "What3Words" address.  
   - Stay in your safe position (behind the barrier or away from the road) while waiting for assistance.

5. **Locate your vehicle's first aid kit while waiting if it is safe to do so.**  
   - Ensure you have your BS 8599-2 compliant kit accessible, as required by 2026 UK road safety regulations.  
   - Do not re-enter the vehicle on a motorway to retrieve items once you have exited.

6. **Request a professional torque check before moving the vehicle again.**  
   - Even if the bolts look "fine" to the eye, they may not be tightened to the manufacturer's specification.  
   - Insist on a torque wrench check, not just a manual check with a lug wrench.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical failure (e.g., cross-threading versus under-torquing).
- You do not need to settle payment or liability disputes with the tyre fitter.
- You do not need to check the tyre pressure or other wheels if you have identified a noise from one specific corner.

## Important reassurance
Stopping immediately is the correct safety response. A clicking or grinding noise after wheel work is a primary indicator of a potential "wheel-off" incident, and your decision to stop prevents a total mechanical failure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a suspected mechanical failure. It does not cover technical repairs, legal claims, or long-term vehicle maintenance.

## Important note
Driving a vehicle with loose wheel components can lead to a complete loss of control. If you have any doubt about the security of the wheel, do not drive the vehicle. National Highways and the Police advise that personal safety takes priority over vehicle recovery.
