# What to do if… you are billed for a subscription you cancelled and the company will not reverse it quickly (UK)

## Short answer
Identify the payment type (Direct Debit, card, or PayPal), instruct your bank or card provider to cancel the payment authority immediately, and request a refund through the specific protection scheme for that payment method.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t assume that cancelling or replacing your physical bank card will automatically stop a Continuous Payment Authority (CPA).
- Don’t delay acting because you are waiting for a reply from the company; bank dispute time limits are often strict.
- Don’t close your bank account to stop a payment, as this can prevent you from receiving the refund later.
- Don’t provide unnecessary sensitive personal documents or ID to the company just to "verify" a cancellation you have already made.
- Don’t ignore the charge if it pushes you into an unarranged overdraft; contact your bank immediately to prevent fees.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact payment method used for the transaction.**
   - Check your banking app or statement to see if the payment is listed as a **Direct Debit**, a **card transaction** (Continuous Payment Authority), or a **PayPal** billing agreement.
   - Note the **merchant name**, **amount**, and **transaction date** precisely as they appear.

2. **Cancel all future payment permissions for that merchant.**
   - **For Direct Debits:** Cancel the instruction via your banking app, online portal, or by phone.
   - **For Card Payments (CPA):** Contact your card issuer (bank) and tell them you are **withdrawing consent** for any future payments to this merchant. Under FCA rules, they must stop the payments upon your request.
   - **For PayPal:** Go to "Settings," then "Payments," and "Manage Automatic Payments" to cancel the specific billing agreement.

3. **Secure evidence of your original cancellation.**
   - Locate and save a copy of the cancellation confirmation email, a screenshot of the "cancelled" status in your account settings, or your sent message requesting the cancellation.
   - Save a screenshot of the bank transaction showing the charge was taken after this date.

4. **Contact the company one final time in writing.**
   - State clearly: "I cancelled this subscription on [date]. You have taken an unauthorised payment of [amount] on [date]. I require a full refund within [number] days."
   - Request a formal complaint reference number if they do not agree to the refund immediately.

5. **Initiate the correct refund process based on the payment type.**
   - **Direct Debit:** Ask your bank for an immediate refund under the **Direct Debit Guarantee**. This applies if a payment is taken in error or after cancellation.
   - **Debit or Credit Card:** Ask your bank to start a **Chargeback** claim. Provide your evidence that the subscription was cancelled before the payment was taken.
   - **Credit Card (over £100):** If the total value of the subscription contract (not just the monthly charge) was over £100 and up to £30,000, ask about a **Section 75** claim for breach of contract.

6. **Submit a formal complaint to your bank if they refuse to stop the payments.**
   - If a bank allows a payment to leave after you have withdrawn consent, they are liable for the loss and any resulting charges.
   - Use the phrase "I am making a formal complaint regarding an unauthorised transaction."

7. **Escalate to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) if the bank does not resolve the issue.**
   - For complaints about "payment services" (like Direct Debits), firms usually have **15 business days** to provide a final response.
   - For other banking complaints, the firm has **8 weeks**.
   - If you are unhappy with their final response, or the time limit passes, contact the FOS.

## What can wait
- You do not need to rewrite your entire consumer history or explain why you wanted to cancel; the date of cancellation is the only vital fact right now.
- You do not need to initiate legal action or Small Claims Court proceedings until you have exhausted the bank dispute and Ombudsman routes.
- You do not need to worry about the company's "no refund" policy if the payment was taken after a valid cancellation, as this is an unauthorised transaction.

## Important reassurance
Unwanted recurring charges are common due to processing delays or merchant errors. UK banking regulations are specifically designed to protect you in these moments. Withdrawing consent is a legal right, and the Direct Debit Guarantee and Chargeback schemes are the standard tools for recovering these funds.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and the immediate recovery of funds taken after a cancellation. It does not cover disputes where the cancellation was not completed according to the merchant's contract terms or disputes over the quality of service provided.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Refund success depends on providing clear evidence of a timely cancellation. If an unauthorised charge has caused you to miss priority bills (rent, mortgage, or utilities), tell your bank's "financial difficulties" team immediately.
