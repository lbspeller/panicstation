# What to do if… you are told a document must be presented in its original format but your file is only a photo or scan (UK)

## Short answer
Request a written definition of "original format" from the requester and immediately order an official replacement or a certified copy from the issuing authority. In 2026, most UK status documents are digital-only (eVisas), while civil certificates (birth/marriage) must be physical, embossed originals or FCDO-legalised copies.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to "enhance" or crop the scan to make it look like an original file; this can trigger fraud alerts.
- Do not assume a high-resolution printout of a photo will be accepted as a physical original.
- Do not send your only remaining physical original to an overseas authority without using a tracked, insured courier service.
- Do not pay for "online notarisation" services unless the receiving country explicitly accepts remote electronic notarisation for that specific document type.
- Do not argue about the digital nature of modern life; procedural rules for international documents often require physical stamps or verifiable digital "share codes."

## What to do now
1. **Verify the specific definition of "original" required by the requesting party.**  
   - Ask if they require a physical paper document with a "wet" signature or embossed seal.
   - Ask if they accept a "digitally issued original" (an official PDF with a verifiable cryptographic signature from the issuer).
   - Ask if they accept a "certified copy" or if it must be the "primary original."
2. **Determine if your document is now handled via the UK digital immigration system.**  
   - If the request concerns UK residency or right to work, generate a 9-character "share code" via the "View and Prove" service on GOV.UK.
   - Note that share codes for work begin with "W" and codes for rent begin with "R"; ensure you provide the correct type.
   - Provide the requester with your date of birth and the share code so they can access the "original" digital record directly from the Home Office.
3. **Order a priority replacement for civil documents (birth, marriage, or death certificates).**  
   - Access the General Register Office (GRO) online portal to order a "certified copy" (which legally functions as an original).
   - Use the "Priority Service" for £38.50 to ensure the document is dispatched the next working day.
   - Ensure the new document has the standard multicoloured background and the embossed seal of the Registrar General.
4. **Identify an FCDO-registered solicitor or notary if a "certified copy" is permitted.**  
   - Use the Law Society or Notary Locator to find a professional whose signature is already on file with the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office.
   - Present the original (if found) or the newly ordered GRO certificate to them.
   - Ensure they write the exact phrase: "Certified to be a true copy of the original seen by me," followed by their signature, date, and professional stamp.
5. **Apply for legalisation (Apostille) if the document is for use outside the UK.**  
   - Check if the destination country requires a paper apostille (£45) or an e-Apostille (£35).
   - Submit the physical document or certified copy to the FCDO Legalisation Office in Milton Keynes via tracked mail.
   - Note that e-Apostilles are only available for certain documents, such as those signed by a UK notary or solicitor.
6. **Request a "Letter of Enrollment" or "Official Transcript" for academic or professional records.**  
   - Contact the registrar of the university or the HR department of the previous employer.
   - Request that they send the "original" directly to the requester or provide a secure digital link (e.g., Gradintelligence or HEDD) rather than a scan you provide.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find your original lost document if an official replacement has already been ordered and accepted.
- You do not need to complain to the requesting agency about their "outdated" paper requirements until your immediate deadline is secured.
- You do not need to apply for a new passport if you only have a scan of your current one; you can usually order a "Certified Copy" of the identity page from a solicitor for non-travel purposes.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel overwhelmed when a scan is rejected, especially when modern systems are increasingly digital. Many international and banking protocols still rely on physical "chains of trust" (seals and signatures) that a simple photo cannot provide. Obtaining a new, official version is a standard administrative task and usually resolves the block within days.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document format disputes in the UK. It does not cover specific visa application requirements for foreign embassies or complex forensic document verification.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Document requirements are set by the receiving authority and can change without notice. Always verify fees and procedures on the official GOV.UK website before making payments.
