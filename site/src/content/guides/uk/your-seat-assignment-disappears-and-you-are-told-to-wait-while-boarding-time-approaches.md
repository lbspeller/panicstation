# What to do if… your seat assignment disappears and you are told to wait while boarding time approaches (UK)

## Short answer
Stay at the boarding gate and demand a clear confirmation of your booking status (Confirmed, Seat to be Assigned, or Standby) before the flight doors close. Ensure you are not "timed out" of your UK261 rights by leaving the gate area without explicit instruction.

## Do not do these things
- Don't leave the immediate gate vicinity unless a member of staff explicitly tells you to go to a customer service desk.
- Don't assume "wait" is a legal status; under UK261, you are either boarding or you are being denied boarding.
- Don't check your cabin baggage until you have a confirmed seat, as retrieving it from a flight you are not on is difficult.
- Don't engage in arguments with other passengers who have been assigned "your" former seat.
- Don't volunteer to give up your seat for a voucher until you have heard the airline's "Involuntary Denied Boarding" offer, which may be higher.

## What to do now
1. **Take a screenshot of your original booking, seat selection, and your current "no seat" boarding pass immediately.** This prevents the airline app from "refreshing" and removing evidence of your initial confirmed reservation.
2. **Approach the gate podium and ask for your "System Status" using these specific terms.** Ask: "In the system right now, am I **Confirmed**, **Seat to be Assigned**, **Standby**, or **Involuntarily Denied Boarding**?"
3. **Verify that your 2026 Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) or visa is correctly linked to your booking.** If the seat disappeared due to a documentation flag, you must resolve the "permission to travel" status before the gate agent can assign a seat.
4. **Ask the agent for a "Decision Trigger" or a "Hard Time Check" to avoid being left at the gate as the doors close.** Say: "If I am not assigned a seat by 15 minutes before departure, what is the specific process for my re-routing and Right to Care?"
5. **State any "Protected Passenger" status clearly if you are travelling with children, have disability needs, or are an unaccompanied minor.** UK airlines are required to prioritize these groups during aircraft swaps or overbooking situations.
6. **Ask for a printed "Denied Boarding" notice if they confirm you will not be on the flight.** Under UK law, the airline must provide you with a written notice explaining your rights to compensation and assistance.
7. **Request your "Right to Care" vouchers immediately if the wait extends beyond two hours.** This includes food, drink, and a means of communication (or reimbursement for reasonable costs) regardless of the reason for the seat issue.
8. **Check if the airline is "Downgrading" you to a lower cabin class rather than bumping you entirely.** If you are moved from Business to Economy, you are entitled to a reimbursement of 30%, 50%, or 75% of the flight price depending on the distance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal compensation claim while standing at the gate; focus only on getting a seat or a rebooking.
- You do not need to calculate the exact distance of your flight to know your rights; the airline is required to provide the information notice.
- You do not need to decide on a "refund vs. reroute" immediately; you have the right to take a moment to check alternative flights on your phone.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sense of "invisibility" when a seat disappears from a digital system, but your legal rights are tied to your original booking, not the current app display. Gate agents often have "GHA" (Ground Handling Agent) powers to override seat blocks once the final passenger count is settled.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization at the boarding gate. It does not cover long-term legal claims or court action if an airline refuses a UK261 claim later.

## Important note
Passenger rights in the UK are governed by UK Reg (EU) No. 261/2004 (known as UK261). Entitlement to compensation depends on the flight distance, the reason for the seat removal, and the length of the delay to your final destination. Always keep your boarding passes and receipts for any food or transport you pay for yourself if the airline fails to provide vouchers.
