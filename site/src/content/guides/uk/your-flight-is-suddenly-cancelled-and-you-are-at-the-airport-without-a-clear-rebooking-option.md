# What to do if… your flight is suddenly cancelled and you are at the airport without a clear rebooking option (UK)

## Short answer
Locate the airline's service desk or contact their customer service line to request either rerouting to your destination at the earliest opportunity or a full refund, and demand immediate "duty of care" assistance (food, drink, and accommodation if delayed overnight).

## Do not do these things
- Don't accept vouchers or travel credit if you prefer a cash refund, as accepting them can waive your right to a monetary reimbursement.
- Don't book a new flight with a different airline before requesting that your original airline reroutes you, or you may forfeit the right to have that cost covered.
- Don't leave the airport without a written or digital "Notice of Rights" or a cancellation confirmation from the airline.
- Don't pay for luxury hotels or alcohol if you are forced to make your own arrangements, as airlines only reimburse "reasonable" expenses.
- Don't forget to collect your luggage if you are no longer flying or if your rerouted flight departs from a different terminal or airport.

## What to do now
1. **Gather your travel documents and find a power source.** Keep your passport, boarding pass, and booking reference accessible, and ensure your phone remains charged for communications.
2. **Obtain proof of the cancellation immediately.** Take a photograph of the departure board showing your flight as "Cancelled" and save screenshots of the cancellation notice in the airline's app or email.
3. **Request your legal options using specific terminology.** Inform the airline agent or phone representative: "Under UK261, my flight is cancelled. I require either rerouting to my final destination at the earliest opportunity or a full refund."
   - If you choose rerouting, ask if they can book you onto a different airline if their own next available flight is significantly delayed.
4. **Demand "Duty of Care" assistance based on the delay duration.** Request food and drink vouchers if your wait exceeds:
   - 2 hours for flights under 1,500km.
   - 3 hours for flights between 1,500km and 3,500km.
   - 4 hours for all other flights.
5. **Secure overnight accommodation if rerouted for the following day.** Ask the airline to provide a hotel room and transport between the airport and the hotel; if they cannot arrange it, ask them to confirm in writing that you should book your own "reasonable" accommodation for later reimbursement.
6. **Clarify refund responsibility if you booked via a third party.** If you used a travel agent, the airline is still responsible for rerouting and care at the airport, but the agent may be responsible for processing a cash refund if you choose that option.
7. **Keep every original receipt for out-of-pocket expenses.** Retain itemised receipts for food, water, transport, and hotels if the airline fails to provide vouchers or bookings directly.
8. **Communicate your status to an external contact.** Send your current location and any new flight details to a friend or family member, including a backup way to reach you if your phone battery fails.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal compensation claim for "extraordinary circumstances" while at the airport; this can be done once you are home.
- You do not need to decide immediately whether to accept a different arrival airport if you are unsure how to get to your final destination from there.
- You do not need to argue with ground staff about the specific cause of the cancellation; simply record the reason they give you and move on to rebooking.

## Important reassurance
Terminal disruptions are high-stress environments, and it is normal to feel overwhelmed by shifting information. Focusing on the immediate logistics—securing a written cancellation and confirming your next meal or bed—is the most effective way to regain control of the situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for passengers at a UK airport immediately following a flight cancellation. It focuses on exercising immediate statutory rights to care, rerouting, and evidence collection. It does not cover long-term legal litigation or complex insurance claims.

## Important note
The rights described apply to flights departing from UK airports or arriving in the UK on UK/EU airlines under UK261 regulations. During major disruptions, airline staff may be unable to provide immediate physical assistance; in such cases, maintaining a record of all interactions and expenses is vital for later reimbursement. This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice.
