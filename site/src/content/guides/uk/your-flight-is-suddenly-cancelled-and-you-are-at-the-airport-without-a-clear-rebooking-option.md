# What to do if… your flight is suddenly cancelled and you are at the airport without a clear rebooking option (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the airline through the desk, app, phone line, or chat and ask for your cancellation options in writing. If your flight is covered by UK rules, the key immediate choices are usually a refund or rerouting, and the airline should provide care such as food, drink, communication help, and overnight accommodation if needed.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t accept a voucher or credit unless you are sure it solves your problem and you understand whether you are giving up a cash refund route.
- Don’t buy a new flight in panic before you have asked the airline for rerouting and care, and recorded what they offered.
- Don’t leave the airport until you have checked whether the airline is moving you to another terminal or airport, and whether you need to collect or re-check bags.
- Don’t rely only on verbal promises; take screenshots or photos of the cancellation and any rebooking offer.
- Don’t spend heavily on hotels, taxis, or meals unless you have no practical alternative; keep costs reasonable and keep receipts.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilise your essentials first.** Sit somewhere safe, charge your phone, connect to Wi-Fi, and put your booking reference, passport, payment card, and any bag receipts together.
2. **Capture proof of the cancellation.** Take screenshots of the airline app or status page, a photo of the departure board, and any message that gives the reason for cancellation.
3. **Ask the airline for the core options in writing.** Say: “My flight is cancelled. Please confirm my options for rerouting to my final destination or a refund in writing.” If you still need to travel, add: “Please check the earliest available rerouting, including later today, tomorrow, and any suitable connection.”
4. **If you are part-way through a journey, ask about the unused journey and getting back.** Say: “If I do not continue, please confirm whether I can get a refund for the unused part and a flight back to my original departure point.”
5. **Ask for care and assistance while you wait.** Ask for food and drink vouchers, help with communication costs if needed, and hotel accommodation with transport if the rerouting means staying overnight.
6. **If staff cannot arrange care during disruption, keep it basic.** Buy only reasonable essentials such as meals, transport to a nearby hotel, and a standard room if you need one. Keep every receipt and note why you had to pay yourself.
7. **Ask for three specific rerouting checks.** Ask the airline to check later same-day flights, nearby airport options that you can realistically reach, and routes with a connection if the direct flight is unavailable.
8. **If you booked through a travel agent or third-party site, contact them too.** Ask the airline who controls the ticket, then message or call the agent with the same evidence and ask who will handle any refund or rebooking.
9. **If this is part of a package holiday, contact the travel company or package organiser.** Tell them you are at the airport, the flight is cancelled, and you need their options for the flight and the rest of the package.
10. **Before booking your own replacement flight, pause and record the gap.** Only consider this after asking the airline for rerouting and care. Save proof that no clear or suitable rerouting was offered, and be ready to justify why the replacement cost was reasonable.
11. **Tell one trusted person your current plan.** Send your location, booking reference if safe to share, current status, and a backup way to reach you if your phone battery runs down.
12. **Start one simple claim note on your phone.** Record the flight number, date, time of cancellation, what you were offered, who you spoke to, screenshots taken, and each cost as it happens.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to pursue compensation.
- You do not need to argue at the desk about the full cause of the cancellation; collect evidence first and dispute it later if needed.
- You do not need to write a full complaint while standing in the airport.
- You do not need to solve the whole trip at once; focus on the next confirmed route, a safe place to wait, food, sleep, and phone battery.

## Important reassurance
This situation can feel chaotic because airline systems, desks, apps, agents, and airport boards may update at different speeds. One written confirmation, one realistic travel option, and receipts for any necessary costs are enough for the first stage.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the airport moment only: getting safe, getting a clear refund or rerouting position, and protecting your ability to recover reasonable costs. Later decisions about complaints, compensation, insurance, package holiday rights, or legal escalation may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, professional, or travel-agent advice. UK flight disruption rules depend on the route and airline, so ask the airline to confirm your options in writing, keep receipts, and avoid accepting vouchers or credits unless you are sure they meet your needs.
