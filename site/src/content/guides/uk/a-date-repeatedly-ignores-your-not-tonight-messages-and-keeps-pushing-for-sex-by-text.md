# What to do if… a date repeatedly ignores your “not tonight” messages and keeps pushing for sex by text (UK)

## Short answer
Cease all communication immediately and block their access to you across all platforms. Prioritize your physical safety by securing your location and informing a trusted person of the situation.

## Do not do these things
- Don't continue to explain or justify your "no" — repeat explanations provide an opening for further coercion.
- Don't agree to meet in person to "clear the air" or "explain one last time."
- Don't send any images, videos, or personal details (such as your specific location or work schedule) to placate them.
- Don't engage in an argument or try to convince them to respect your boundary.
- Don't delete the message thread yet, as it may be needed for evidence later.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate physical environment and change your short-term plans.** If this person knows where you live or work, ensure all doors and windows are locked. If you had planned to meet them or go to a place where they might find you, cancel those plans and stay in a populated, safe area or with a friend.
2. **Send one final, clear boundary message or simply stop responding.** If you feel it is safer to state a final boundary, send a short, one-sentence message like "I have said no, do not contact me again" and then immediately move to blocking.
3. **Block their phone number and all social media or dating app profiles.** Ensure they cannot see your "active" status or read receipts. If you are concerned that blocking will cause them to show up at your home, you may choose to "mute" or "archive" the conversation first while you reach a safe location, then block them once you are secure.
4. **Disable all location-sharing features on your device.** Check apps like "Find My," Snapchat, or WhatsApp to ensure your live location is not being shared with them or anyone who might tell them.
5. **Contact a trusted friend or family member and tell them what is happening.** Use a clear statement: "I am being pressured for sex by someone I was dating and I feel unsafe. Can you stay on the phone with me or come over?"
6. **Take screenshots of the messages to preserve evidence.** Ensure the date, time, and contact details are visible in the screenshots. Save these to a secure location, such as a private folder or a separate device, then close the app to avoid re-reading them.
7. **Contact the police if you feel you are in immediate danger or if they show up at your location.** Call **999**. If you cannot speak, dial **999** and then press **55** when prompted (on a mobile) to let the operator know you are in danger but cannot talk. For non-emergencies, call **101**.
8. **Reach out to a specialist support service for confidential advice.** These services can help you manage the emotional impact and plan for your safety:
   - **Rape Crisis England & Wales 24/7 Support Line:** Call 0808 500 2222 (for those 16+).
   - **National Stalking Helpline (Suzy Lamplugh Trust):** Call 0808 802 0300 (check website for specific weekday hours).
   - **National Domestic Abuse Helpline (Refuge):** Call 0808 2000 247 (24/7).
   - **Galop (LGBT+ Support):** Call 0800 999 5428.
   - **Childline (if you are under 19):** Call 0800 1111 (24/7).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to press charges or make a formal police report right now.
- You do not need to analyze why they are acting this way or if you "misread" the situation.
- You do not need to inform your wider social circle or mutual acquaintances immediately.
- You do not need to verify if their behavior meets a specific legal definition of "harassment" or "coercion" before seeking safety.

## Important reassurance
It is a common and normal reaction to feel frozen, guilty, or anxious when someone repeatedly ignores your boundaries. Their refusal to accept your "no" is their responsibility, not a result of you being unclear. You have the absolute right to end contact and prioritize your safety at any time.

## Scope note
This guide is intended for immediate stabilization and safety in the minutes and hours following persistent sexual pressure via text. It does not cover long-term legal proceedings or workplace grievance processes.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or professional advice. If you are in immediate fear for your safety, contact emergency services.
