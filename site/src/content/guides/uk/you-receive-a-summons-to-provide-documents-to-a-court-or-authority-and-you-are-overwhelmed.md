# What to do if… you receive a summons to provide documents to a court or authority and you are overwhelmed (UK)

## Short answer
Do not ignore the document. Preserve all potentially relevant records immediately by stopping any deletion or destruction, then verify the authenticity of the request through an official channel before responding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not discard, ignore, or overlook the document even if it seems informal.
- Do not delete emails, messages, call logs, or digital files to "clean up" your records.
- Do not shred or destroy physical papers, even if they appear redundant.
- Do not alter any documents, including editing filenames, metadata, or adding backdated notes.
- Do not provide original documents unless the notice specifically demands them; usually, copies are sufficient.
- Do not send sensitive or legally privileged material (such as correspondence with a lawyer) without specific advice.
- Do not engage in excessive disclosure of unrelated personal data "just in case."

## What to do now
1. **Extract the core compliance data onto a single sheet of paper.**  
   **Identify and write down: (a) the issuing court or authority, (b) the specific document categories requested, (c) the date range of the request, (d) the deadline for submission, and (e) the case or reference number.**

2. **Verify the request using a known official contact method.**  
   **Find the official switchboard number for the relevant court or authority via a verified ".gov.uk" website and call to confirm that the summons and case number are legitimate.**  
   - For a **court witness summons**, contact the specific court office listed.  
   - For an **HMRC information notice**, verify the officer's details via the official HMRC digital assistant or helpline.

3. **Enact an immediate "document hold" across all platforms.**  
   **Stop all routine deletion, archiving, or shredding of materials that fall within the scope of the request.**  
   - Disable auto-delete settings on messaging apps and email clients.  
   - Notify any relevant IT staff or family members who share access to files that nothing should be deleted.

4. **Confirm if "conduct money" or expenses have been provided.**  
   **Check if the summons was accompanied by a payment for travel and time, as a witness summons in the UK is generally only binding if "conduct money" is offered at the time of service.**

5. **Determine if the request falls under the 2026 Online Procedure Rules (OPR).**  
   **Check the document for instructions to upload files to a specific digital portal, as many civil and tribunal matters now require submission through the OPR system rather than post or email.**

6. **Seek initial legal guidance to check for "Legal Professional Privilege."**  
   **Contact a solicitor or Citizens Advice to determine if any requested documents are "privileged" (protected from disclosure), especially if they involve legal advice you received in the past.**

7. **Triage the requested materials into three distinct groups.**  
   **Physically or digitally separate the documents into: (1) clearly requested and available, (2) unclear or requiring clarification, and (3) sensitive or potentially privileged.**

8. **Apply to vary or set aside the summons if it is "oppressive" or "fishing."**  
   **If the request is too broad or impossible to fulfill, you must apply to the court to "set aside" or "vary" the summons before the deadline expires.**

9. **Produce the documents in a trackable, indexed format.**  
   **Create a simple list (an index) of everything you are providing and send the package via a method that provides a signature and proof of delivery.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to provide a narrative statement explaining the background of the case yet.
- You do not need to organize documents that fall outside the specified date range or categories.
- You do not need to decide whether you "agree" with the underlying legal case today.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a formal demand for documents is a high-stress event, and feeling a sense of panic or paralysis is a common physiological response. These systems are built for administrative efficiency rather than human ease. By preserving the data and verifying the source, you have already mitigated the highest immediate risks of non-compliance.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for receiving a demand for documents. It does not cover the nuances of criminal warrants or dawn raids. Later stages of this process will require detailed review by a legal professional.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. In the UK, failing to comply with a valid court summons or statutory notice can lead to fines or a finding of contempt of court. If you suspect you are being investigated for an offence, seek a criminal defense solicitor immediately.
