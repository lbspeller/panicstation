# What to do if… you discover a roof leak during heavy rain and it is worsening (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate electricity to the affected area immediately, evacuate the room if the ceiling is bulging, and contain the water using buckets while notifying your landlord or insurer for emergency "make-safe" repairs.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t climb onto the roof during heavy rain, high winds, or in darkness.
- Don’t touch light fittings, switches, or sockets that are wet or near damp patches.
- Don’t operate the consumer unit (fuse box) if it is wet or if you must stand in water to reach it.
- Don’t stay in the room if the ceiling is sagging, bulging, or dropping pieces of plaster.
- Don’t attempt to investigate the leak in a loft space if there is standing water near electrical cables.

## What to do now
1. **Move people and pets out of the affected room.** If you see the ceiling sagging or bulging, close the door and prevent anyone from re-entering due to the risk of a sudden collapse.
2. **Isolate the electrical supply safely.**
   - If water is dripping through a light fitting, smoke alarm, or near sockets: **switch off the electricity at the consumer unit**.
   - **Use the Main Switch** to cut all power if you are unsure which circuit is affected.
   - **Do not** touch any electrical equipment if you are standing on a wet floor or if the equipment itself is wet.
3. **Puncture a bulging ceiling to prevent collapse (if safe).**
   - If you see a distinct bulge where water is pooling, **carefully poke a small hole in the centre of the bulge** using a screwdriver or long-handled tool.
   - Position a bucket directly underneath before doing this to catch the released water and reduce the weight on the ceiling structure.
4. **Contain the water to prevent further property damage.**
   - Place buckets, pots, or bins under active drips.
   - Put a towel or rag in the bottom of the container to prevent splashing.
   - Move electronics, soft furnishings, and valuables to a dry room or cover them with heavy-duty plastic sheets or bin liners.
5. **Report the emergency immediately.**
   - **If you rent:** Call your landlord or letting agent's emergency number. Under 2026 safety standards, they are required to act rapidly on hazards that threaten your safety or property integrity.
   - **If you live in a flat:** Check if the water is coming from an upstairs neighbour and notify them or the building manager immediately.
   - **If you own:** Contact your home insurance emergency helpline or a roofer offering 24/7 "make-safe" services (e.g., emergency tarping).
6. **Capture evidence for insurance or repair records.**
   - **Take photos and video** of the active leak, any structural damage to the ceiling, and any damaged furniture or floors.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific broken tile or failed flashing right now.
- You do not need to start drying or dehumidifying the area until the leak is stopped and an electrician confirms the wiring is safe.
- You do not need to agree to permanent repair quotes or long-term roofing contracts during the storm.
- You do not need to move heavy furniture that is not in the immediate path of the water.

## Important reassurance
Ceiling leaks are common during extreme weather and are physically manageable if you prioritize safety. Your primary role right now is to isolate hazards and contain water spread; the actual repair is a job for professionals once the weather stabilizes.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only for the duration of an active storm. Final repairs, professional electrical testing, and structural assessments must be conducted by qualified specialists after the immediate weather threat has passed.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance. If you see smoke, smell burning, or see sparking from electrical points, evacuate and call 999 immediately. Structural or electrical work in the UK must be carried out by competent persons in accordance with Building Regulations and BS 7671.
