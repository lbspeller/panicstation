# What to do if… a company warns it will retry a failed direct debit within 24 hours and you cannot cover it (UK)

## Short answer
Cancel the Direct Debit instruction via your banking app or online portal immediately to block the retry, then notify the company to arrange an alternative payment date. If the bank allows the payment to leave after you have cancelled it, you are entitled to a full and immediate refund under the Direct Debit Guarantee.

## Do not do these things
- Don't wait for the retry to happen—automated systems (re-presentation) can trigger even if the first attempt failed only hours ago.
- Don't assume the payment is "stopped" just because you told the company—you must cancel the instruction at the bank level to ensure the money cannot be pulled.
- Don't ignore the warning—multiple failed attempts can result in both bank "unpaid item" fees and company late payment charges.
- Don't move money from essential bills like rent or council tax to cover a non-priority retry—protect your housing and liberty first.
- Don't close your bank account—this does not stop the legal debt and makes receiving a Direct Debit Guarantee refund much harder.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the exact payment type in your banking app.**  
   **Confirm if the entry is listed under "Direct Debits" or if it is a recurring card payment (Continuous Payment Authority).**  
   - Direct Debits use a sort code and account number.
   - Recurring card payments (CPAs) use your 16-digit debit card number.
   - If it is a CPA, you must tell your bank to "withdraw consent" for the specific merchant.

2. **Cancel the Direct Debit instruction through your bank immediately.**  
   **Use your mobile app or online banking to delete the specific Direct Debit mandate for that company.**  
   - Most UK banks have a cut-off time (often 8:00 PM) on the day before a payment is due.
   - Because the company has warned of a 24-hour retry, the payment may already be "in flight." Cancelling now is still necessary to trigger your rights under the Direct Debit Guarantee.

3. **Contact the company to report the cancellation.**  
   **Inform the billing company that you have cancelled the instruction and cannot cover the retry at this time.**  
   - Ask them to stop any further automated collection attempts (re-presentation).
   - Request a "payment holiday" or a specific date in the future when you will have funds available.
   - Request that they waive any late payment fees due to your proactive communication.

4. **Monitor your account balance closely for the next 48 hours.**  
   **Check your "available balance" frequently to see if the retry successfully left the account despite the cancellation.**  
   - If the payment shows as "pending" or has cleared, note the exact time and amount.

5. **Claim an immediate refund if the payment is taken after cancellation.**  
   **Contact your bank's "Direct Debit Disputes" or "Indemnity" team and state that a payment was taken after the instruction was cancelled.**  
   - Under the Direct Debit Guarantee, you are entitled to a full and immediate refund from your bank if a payment is made in error.
   - The bank must credit your account immediately; they cannot force you to wait for the company to return the money.

6. **Save all digital evidence of the interaction.**  
   **Screenshot the "retry" warning message and the confirmation screen showing the Direct Debit is now cancelled.**  
   - If you spoke to the company, note the agent's name and the time of the call.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle the full debt today; the priority is preventing an immediate overdraft or fee.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Financial Ombudsman yet—give the bank the chance to process the refund first.
- You do not need to change your bank account or move all your direct debits to a new provider.

## Important reassurance
Financial institutions are required by law to follow your instructions to stop payments. Direct Debit failures are automated events and do not mean you are in legal trouble. The Direct Debit Guarantee is a powerful consumer protection specifically designed to fix these exact situations by putting the money back in your account first and asking questions later.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a 24-hour payment crisis. It does not cover long-term debt management, insolvency, or legal disputes regarding the validity of the underlying contract.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Terms and conditions vary between banks, particularly regarding daily cut-off times for cancelling instructions. If you are in persistent financial difficulty, contact a free debt advice service such as StepChange or National Debtline.
