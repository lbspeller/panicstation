# What to do if… you cannot find proof of lawful entry and you are asked for it during travel (UK)

## Short answer
Identify the specific document requested and provide a UKVI share code to prove your digital immigration status if you have an eVisa. If the carrier cannot verify your status, ask them to contact the Border Force Carrier Support Hub.

## Do not do these things
- Do not provide documents belonging to another person or attempt to guess your status details.
- Do not admit to being "illegal" or an "overstayed" visitor simply because a physical record is missing.
- Do not allow staff to remove your passport or phone from your sight without a clear explanation of the destination and duration.
- Do not delete communication from the Home Office or UKVI that may contain status references.
- Do not attempt to pay third parties or unofficial agents at the airport to resolve document issues.

## What to do now
1. **Ask the official for the specific type of proof required.**
   - Determine if they need a visa vignette, an entry stamp, a share code for an eVisa, or evidence of an onward journey.
2. **Access your UKVI account to generate a share code.**
   - Log in to the "view and prove your immigration status" service.
   - Select the option to prove your status for travel.
   - Note the 9-character alphanumeric code; these codes are valid for 90 days and can be used multiple times.
3. **Request that the carrier contact the UK Border Force Carrier Support Hub.**
   - If the airline states they cannot verify your digital status, ask them to call the Hub (available 24/7 for carriers only) to confirm your permission to travel.
4. **Call the Passenger Support Helpline if you cannot demonstrate your status.**
   - Use the 24/7 Home Office line for travellers in immediate difficulty: 0800 876 6921.
   - If calling from outside the UK or unable to use 0800 numbers, call +44 203 337 0927.
5. **Verify that your current passport is correctly linked to your eVisa.**
   - Check your UKVI account to ensure the passport number, expiry date, and nationality match the document you are holding.
   - If you have a new passport that is not yet linked, keep your old passport (even if expired/clipped) available as it may be the document linked to your status.
6. **Gather alternative evidence of your status if the digital system is inaccessible.**
   - Locate your original Home Office decision email or PDF letter.
   - Present a physical Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) or Biometric Residence Card (BRC), even if the physical card has expired, as it contains details staff can use to verify your record.
7. **Contact the nearest British Embassy or Consulate if you are a UK citizen without a valid passport.**
   - Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if your passport is lost, stolen, or expired while you are abroad.
8. **Document the reason for any boarding refusal.**
   - Request a written statement from the carrier explaining why you are being denied boarding.
   - Keep all receipts for additional travel, accommodation, or food costs incurred due to the delay.

## What can wait
- You do not need to submit a formal "Report an error with your eVisa" form while at the check-in desk, as this process takes several working days.
- You do not need to provide a complete, chronological history of every UK entry and exit.
- You do not need to initiate a formal complaint against the airline or Home Office until you have resolved the immediate travel hurdle.

## Important reassurance
Digital-only status is the standard for the UK in 2026, and technical glitches at check-in are recognized issues. Not having a physical stamp or sticker does not mean your immigration status is invalid; carriers have established protocols to verify your permission to travel through Home Office channels.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for immediate travel document checks only. It does not cover long-term immigration appeals, visa applications, or complex legal disputes regarding residency.

## Important note
Immigration requirements and carrier enforcement vary by route and nationality. Verification of digital status depends on the airline's systems and Home Office availability. Always use official GOV.UK tools for status verification and follow the instructions of border officials.
