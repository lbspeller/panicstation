# What to do if… your passport is stolen while you are abroad and you still need to travel onward (UK)

## Short answer
Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) online, which typically takes two working days to process and covers a specific itinerary of up to five transit countries. Do not book non-refundable travel until your application is approved and you have confirmed your carrier accepts the ETD.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to cross international borders or check in for flights using only a police report or a photocopy of your passport.
- Do not pay third-party websites or "agents" promising faster processing; only use the official GOV.UK portal.
- Do not use a passport you have previously reported as stolen if you happen to find it later, as it is electronically cancelled and will trigger an alert at border control.
- Do not assume that every country or airline accepts an ETD for entry or transit without checking their specific requirements first.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your immediate surroundings and check for remaining essentials.** **Move to a stable, populated area such as a hotel lobby or a local government office to assess your situation.**
   - Verify if other items were taken, such as bank cards or your phone, and contact your bank to freeze accounts if necessary.
   - Look for any digital copies or photographs of your stolen passport on your email or cloud storage.

2. **Cancel your stolen passport through the official application process.** **If you are applying for an Emergency Travel Document, your old passport will usually be cancelled automatically once you pay the fee.** 
   - If you do not need to travel urgently (within 6 weeks), use the standard "cancel a lost or stolen passport" service on GOV.UK immediately to prevent identity fraud.
   - Note that once cancelled, the document is permanently invalid for travel.

3. **Report the theft to the local police and obtain a written record.** **Visit the nearest police station to file a formal report and request a crime reference number or a physical copy of the report.**
   - While not always mandatory for the ETD application, a police report is frequently required for travel insurance claims and by some foreign border authorities.
   - If the local police cannot provide a report, record the station's name, the officer's name, and the time you attempted to report it.

4. **Confirm your eligibility for an online Emergency Travel Document application.** **You can apply for an ETD online if you are a British national outside the UK and had a passport issued on or after 1 January 2006.**
   - If your last passport was issued before 2006, you will likely need to apply for a standard replacement passport, which takes significantly longer.
   - The ETD is intended for those who need to travel within the next six weeks.

5. **Submit the ETD application with your specific travel itinerary.** **Complete the online form on GOV.UK and pay the £125 fee, which now includes the cost of courier delivery for your document.**
   - You must provide details for your entire journey, including all transit countries (up to a maximum of five).
   - You will need a digital passport photo that meets the standard UK requirements and has not been used on a previous passport.

6. **Monitor your email for updates regarding your appointment or collection.** **Check your inbox regularly for instructions on whether you must attend an in-person interview at the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate.**
   - Most ETDs are ready for collection or dispatch within two working days of the application being approved.
   - Ensure you have a way to pay for any local travel required to reach the consular office.

7. **Verify that your carrier and destination countries accept the ETD.** **Contact your airline, ferry, or train operator directly to confirm they will allow you to board with an Emergency Travel Document.**
   - Check the entry requirements for your destination and any transit points on the FCDO foreign travel advice pages.
   - If you had a visa in your stolen passport, you must contact the authority that issued it to ask about a replacement or a "transfer of conditions."

8. **Notify your travel insurance provider about the theft and the replacement costs.** **Call your insurer’s emergency assistance line to open a claim and confirm what evidence they require for reimbursement.**
   - Keep all receipts for the ETD fee, police reports, and any additional accommodation or transport costs incurred due to the theft.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a full 10-year replacement passport while you are still mid-journey if the ETD covers your route home.
- You do not need to update your passport details on non-travel related accounts (such as utilities or subscriptions) until you return to the UK.
- You do not need to replace non-essential ID cards, such as a driving licence or library card, while abroad.

## Important reassurance
Passport theft is a common occurrence that consular staff are trained to handle daily. Obtaining an Emergency Travel Document is a standardized process designed to restore your ability to move across borders legally. Once you have submitted your application and secured a police reference, the administrative path to continuing your journey is established.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for British nationals who need to travel urgently after a passport theft. It does not cover the long-term process of applying for a full replacement passport or resolving complex residency visa issues in foreign countries.

## Important note
This information reflects UK government procedures as of 2026. Fees, processing times, and eligibility criteria are subject to change. Always refer to official guidance on GOV.UK and follow instructions provided by British consular staff.
