# What to do if… you discover an adult is messaging your child and asking for sexual photos (UK)

## Short answer
Ensure your child is in a safe, supervised space and stop all contact immediately by blocking the user, then report the incident to the police (999 for emergencies or 101/online) or CEOP.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront, threaten, or attempt to negotiate with the adult, as this can lead to the deletion of evidence or escalation of threats.
- Do not comply with any demands for money, further images, or information.
- Do not download, save, or screenshot any indecent images of your child or other children, as possessing or distributing these images is illegal in the UK, even for the purpose of evidence.
- Do not ask your child to show you the images or send them to your device.
- Do not delete the conversation or the account until you have noted the necessary identifiers for the police.
- Do not pressure your child for an immediate, detailed explanation; prioritise their immediate safety and emotional stability.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause and stay with your child in a supportive environment.** If there is any immediate threat of physical harm, an active meeting request, or if your child is currently being blackmailed with the release of images, treat this as an emergency.
2. **Stop all contact between the adult and your child immediately.** Block the accounts involved on the specific platform and any associated apps. 
   - Ensure privacy settings are set to private.
   - Disable location-sharing features on the device and within individual apps.
3. **Preserve essential identifiers without capturing indecent imagery.** Make a written or typed note of the following details:
   - The platform or app name.
   - The adult’s username, handle, and display name.
   - The profile URL or link if it is accessible.
   - Any phone numbers or email addresses visible in the profile or messages.
   - The dates and times when the requests or threats occurred.
4. **Report the incident to the appropriate statutory authority.** 
   - Call **999** if the child is in immediate danger or if a meeting is being planned for right now.
   - Call **101** or use your local police force’s online reporting tool for non-emergencies.
   - Alternatively, report via the **CEOP (Child Exploitation and Online Protection)** Safety Centre if you have not already contacted the police.
5. **Use the reporting tools within the social media platform or app.** Report the user for "child sexual exploitation," "grooming," or "solicitation" after you have noted their account details. This allows the platform to take internal action and preserve data.
6. **Provide your child with access to confidential specialist support.** Offer the **Childline** number (**0800 1111**) or their 1-2-1 online chat service. Remind your child that this is a safe space for them to talk without judgment.
7. **Contact the NSPCC Helpline for adults for professional guidance on your next steps.** You can call **0808 800 5000** (check current hours; generally 10:00–16:00 on weekdays) or email **help@nspcc.org.uk** at any time. They provide advice for parents and carers concerned about a child’s safety.
8. **Engage the Report Remove service if any images have been shared online.** Support your child in using the **Report Remove** tool (a collaboration between Childline and the Internet Watch Foundation) to seek the removal of sexual images or videos from the internet.

## What can wait
- You do not need to notify the child’s school or other parents immediately unless there is a direct link to those environments.
- You do not need to conduct an exhaustive forensic search of your child’s entire digital history right now.
- You do not need to identify the real-world identity of the adult yourself; this is the role of law enforcement.

## Important reassurance
Manipulative adults use grooming techniques to make children feel responsible or as though they are in a "relationship." Your child is the victim of a crime, and their actions—including sending photos—are often the result of coercion or fear. Your role now is to provide safety and take over the communication barrier, which significantly reduces the adult's power.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions. It does not cover long-term therapeutic support, school safeguarding procedures, or the details of police investigations.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Possessing indecent images of children is a criminal offence; always follow police instructions regarding the handling of digital evidence. If a child is at immediate risk, call emergency services.
