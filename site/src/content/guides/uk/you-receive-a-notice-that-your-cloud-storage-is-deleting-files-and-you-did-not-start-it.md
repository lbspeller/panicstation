# What to do if… you receive a notice that your cloud storage is deleting files and you did not start it (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate your devices from the internet to stop syncing, then log in via a secure web browser to change your credentials and restore files from the provider's trash or version history.

## Do not do these things
- Don't click any links or buttons within the deletion notification email, as these are frequently used in phishing attacks to steal login credentials.
- Don't leave your computer or phone connected to the internet if you see files disappearing in real-time.
- Don't delete "leftover" files or empty the trash/bin to "clean up" the account, as this may permanently destroy recovery data.
- Don't use the same password for your cloud account and your recovery email account.
- Don't ignore the notice even if your files look fine; mass deletions can be scheduled or hidden in subfolders.

## What to do now
1. **Disconnect all syncing devices from the internet immediately to prevent the deletion command from spreading.**
   - Switch off Wi-Fi or unplug ethernet cables on all computers linked to the account.
   - Put mobile phones and tablets into Airplane Mode.
   - This prevents the "delete" instruction from reaching local copies of your files that have not yet synced.

2. **Log in to your cloud account using a secure, web-based browser on a device you know is safe.**
   - Type the official website address (e.g., drive.google.com, onedrive.com, icloud.com) directly into the browser address bar.
   - Do not use a link from an email or a search engine advertisement.

3. **Check the recent activity and device list to identify unauthorized access.**
   - Locate the "Security," "Activity," or "Recent Events" section of your account settings.
   - Review the list of "Signed-in devices" and "Recent sign-ins" for locations, IP addresses, or device types you do not recognize.
   - Note the time and date of any suspicious activity for your records.

4. **Force a logout of all active sessions and change your security credentials.**
   - **Select the option to "Sign out of all sessions" or "Remove all trusted devices" if available.**
   - **Update your password to a unique, complex string or create a Passkey if the provider supports it.**
   - **Verify that your Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) settings have not been changed.**
   - **Check for and remove any unauthorized "Third-party apps" or "Connected services" with access to your files.**

5. **Secure your recovery email account as this is a common secondary target.**
   - Change the password and enable MFA on the email account linked to your cloud storage.
   - Check the email settings for "Forwarding rules" or "Filters" that might be redirecting your security alerts to a third party.

6. **Initiate the file restoration process within the cloud provider's interface.**
   - Open the "Trash," "Bin," or "Recently Deleted" folder and select "Restore" for all affected items.
   - Use "Version History" or "File Restore" (often found under settings) to roll back the entire drive to a point in time before the deletions began.
   - Prioritize restoring critical folders first, as some providers have time-limited retention periods (often 30 days).

7. **Report the incident to the official UK cybercrime authorities.**
   - **England, Wales, and Northern Ireland:** Report to **Report Fraud** (the successor to Action Fraud) at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **Scotland:** Report to **Police Scotland** by calling 101 or using their online cybercrime reporting form.
   - Forward any phishing emails that triggered the event to the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) at report@phishing.gov.uk.

## What can wait
- You do not need to perform a full malware scan of your devices until the account itself is secured.
- You do not need to contact individual people about shared folders until you have verified the extent of the loss.
- You do not need to purchase additional storage or upgrade your plan to "fix" the issue.
- You can delay investigating the specific entry point (e.g., which specific app was breached) until your data is recovering.

## Important reassurance
Sudden file deletions are often the result of automated scripts or misconfigured sync settings rather than a targeted personal attack. By cutting the internet connection to your devices, you have likely preserved "offline" copies of your files, and most major cloud providers maintain a secondary safety net (the Bin) that allows for full recovery if accessed promptly.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for personal cloud accounts. If the affected account belongs to a business or employer, you must notify your IT department or Data Protection Officer immediately, as they may have admin-level recovery tools and mandatory reporting requirements under UK GDPR.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or technical expert advice. Digital recovery outcomes depend on the specific terms and retention policies of your service provider. If you suspect your identity has been stolen or financial details were stored in the deleted files, contact your bank and refer to official NCSC guidance on identity theft.
