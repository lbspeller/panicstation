# What to do if… an ATM keeps your card and you are left without payment options (UK)

## Short answer
Use your banking app to report the card as "lost or stolen" to trigger an immediate digital replacement, then generate an emergency "Get Cash" code if you need physical currency right now. If you suspect fraud, dial 159 from a safe phone to be connected to your bank's security department.

## Do not do these things
- Don't accept assistance from anyone standing near the ATM or use a phone they provide to call your bank.
- Don't leave the immediate area until you have electronically secured the account or confirmed the machine is not on a delayed-release cycle.
- Don't attempt to prize the card slot open with keys, tools, or fingers.
- Don't share your PIN or one-time passcodes (OTPs) with anyone, including people claiming to be bank staff or police officers.
- Don't re-enter your PIN if the machine keyboard feels loose, spongy, or looks damaged.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe distance and observe the machine.** Stand 2-3 metres away for at least two minutes to ensure the card is not ejected during a delayed reboot or timeout.
2. **Report the card as "lost or stolen" in your banking app.** 
   - Selecting "lost or stolen" rather than "freeze" often triggers the bank to automatically update your digital wallet (Apple Pay/Google Pay) with a new card number within minutes.
   - If you cannot use an app, **dial 159** from your phone to be routed to your bank's official fraud line.
3. **Generate an emergency cash code if you need physical money.** 
   - Many UK banks (including NatWest, RBS, and Barclays) allow you to generate a 6 or 8-digit code in their app.
   - You can enter this code at a compatible ATM to withdraw a limited amount of cash (usually up to £100–£130) without a physical card.
4. **Identify the ATM and record its details.** 
   - Note the bank or operator name (e.g., Link, Cardtronics, Euronet).
   - Find the Terminal ID or Machine Number, usually located on a small sticker on the front of the unit.
   - Note the exact time of the incident and any error message displayed.
5. **Locate the nearest Branch or Banking Hub.**
   - If the ATM is attached to a branch that is currently open, enter with photo ID (passport or driving licence).
   - If the branch has closed, look for a "Banking Hub" (shared community space) where your bank may have a representative present.
6. **Ask for a temporary spending limit increase on your digital wallet.** 
   - If you have an urgent large payment (e.g., a hotel or train fare), ask the bank on the phone if they can authorise a one-time high-value contactless transaction while your physical replacement is in the mail.
7. **Notify the police if you see "skimming" equipment.**
   - If the card slot has a bulky plastic overlay or there is a pinhole camera above the keypad, do not touch the machine further. 
   - Report the location to the police via 101 or the Action Fraud website once you are in a secure location.

## What can wait
- Retrieving the physical card. In most cases, retained cards are automatically destroyed by the machine or the bank for security and will not be returned.
- Updating recurring subscriptions (Netflix, Amazon, etc.). Most banks offer a "switch" service that carries these over to the new card number automatically.
- Filing a formal complaint about the machine's malfunction. 

## Important reassurance
ATM card retention is a standard security protocol used when a machine detects a technical error, a damaged chip, or suspicious activity. It is a protective measure designed to prevent the card from falling into the wrong hands. Once the card is reported as lost or stolen in the app, your liability for any subsequent unauthorised transactions is typically zero.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for card retention and immediate liquidity. It does not cover long-term fraud investigations or disputed transaction claims.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Terms for emergency cash and digital wallet updates vary by provider. Always prioritize your physical safety over the retrieval of a plastic card.
