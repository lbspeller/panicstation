# What to do if… you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without consenting (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, private space and contact the Revenge Porn Helpline (UK) or use their anonymous Whisper tool to start takedown and prevention procedures before you communicate with the person involved.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the suspect immediately if you feel unsafe or panicked, as this may lead to the deletion of evidence or escalation of the threat.
- Do not pay any money or negotiate if you are being threatened with the release of images (sextortion).
- Do not delete your own copies of threat messages or the content itself yet, as these are needed for takedowns and police reports.
- Do not forward the content to others to "ask for help," as this technically constitutes further sharing of the image.
- Do not agree to "one last video" or "one more photo" to make the person stop or delete the original.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a location where you cannot be seen or heard by the suspected person.** If you are currently in the room where you suspect a camera is hidden or a recording happened, leave the premises or go to a locked, safe room.
2. **If you are 18 or older, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline via phone (0345 6000 459) or their anonymous "Whisper" tool.** They provide specialist assistance for intimate image abuse, including cases where you only *suspect* a recording was made.
   - They are open Monday to Friday, 10:00–16:00.
   - They can help with immediate takedown requests and provide advice on UK law.
3. **If you are being blackmailed for money or more images, stop all communication immediately.**
   - Do not pay. 
   - Do not delete the chat history.
   - Take screenshots of the threats and the perpetrator's profile, ensuring the URL or account handle is visible.
4. **Call 999 if there is an immediate physical threat or if the person is trying to prevent you from leaving.** For non-emergency reporting of the recording or blackmail, call **101** or report via your local police website.
5. **Use StopNCII.org if you have access to the file and are 18 or older.** This tool creates a digital "hash" (fingerprint) of the file on your device so participating platforms (like Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and Reddit) can block it from being uploaded. 
   - The file never leaves your device.
   - This only works if you actually have the file in your possession.
6. **If you were under 18 when the recording occurred, use the Childline "Report Remove" tool.** This service is specifically for images of minors and works with the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) to track and remove content globally. You can also report directly to **CEOP**.
7. **Check for observable signs of hidden recording devices if it is safe to do so.**
   - Look for unusual "glints" of light from lenses in common objects like clocks, smoke detectors, or chargers.
   - Check for unfamiliar Wi-Fi networks appearing in the immediate vicinity (e.g., "Cam_123").
   - Do not dismantle devices if you intend to involve the police, as this may contaminate forensic evidence.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to press criminal charges today.
- You do not need to tell your family, partner, or employer right now.
- You do not need to have "proof" that a recording exists before seeking support from the Helpline.
- You do not need to review the footage or images yourself.

## Important reassurance
The feeling of violation, "skin-crawling," or extreme panic you may be experiencing is a standard physiological response to a breach of sexual privacy. These reactions do not mean you have lost control; they are your body's way of signaling a boundary has been crossed. You have the right to take a safe pause before deciding on any permanent action.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm reduction for residents of the UK. It does not cover long-term legal litigation or private civil suits, which should be discussed with a qualified solicitor.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal advice. Legislative updates in 2026, including amendments to the Crime and Policing Bill, are strengthening takedown requirements, but immediate safety remains the priority. If you are under 18, or the content involves a minor, contact Childline or the police immediately.
