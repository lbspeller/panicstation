# What to do if… your payment app account is locked and you have money stuck inside (UK)

## Short answer
Move anything urgent away from the app first, then contact the provider through official support and make a clear formal complaint asking for restored access or a payout of your remaining balance.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay anyone who says they can unlock the account for a fee.
- Don’t send passcodes, passwords, card details, or identity documents through social media messages, WhatsApp, or unofficial numbers.
- Don’t keep retrying failed logins or identity checks again and again; it may add delays or confusion.
- Don’t close a linked bank account or card in panic unless your bank tells you to.
- Don’t assume the money is gone just because the app is locked or support is slow.

## What to do now
1. **Cover the next essential payments without the app.**  
   Check what is due in the next few days, such as rent, food, travel, bills, childcare, or debt payments. Use another bank account, card, or trusted payment method where you can. If wages, benefits, or regular payments go into the app, contact the payer and ask to change the destination for the next payment.

2. **Secure the account and the linked email.**  
   Change the password for the payment app if you can. Change the password for the email account linked to it. Turn on two-step verification. Check for new-device alerts, password reset emails, unknown payments, or unfamiliar linked cards and bank accounts.

3. **Make a simple evidence pack.**  
   Save screenshots of the lock message, visible balance, recent transactions, withdrawal errors, verification requests, and any support messages. Write down the date and time you lost access, the amount stuck, and any case or ticket numbers.

4. **Use only the provider’s official support route.**  
   Use the in-app support route or the provider’s official website. Ask:
   - why the account is locked, if they can tell you;
   - what verification they need;
   - how to submit documents safely;
   - whether your balance can be paid out to your bank account while the review continues;
   - when you should expect the next update.

5. **Make it a formal complaint.**  
   Send a message headed “Formal complaint: account locked and funds inaccessible”. Include your account email or phone number, any case ID, the amount stuck, when access stopped, and the immediate impact, such as being unable to pay essential bills. Ask for either restored access or payout of the remaining balance to your bank account, and ask for a written final response. In many payment-services and e-money cases, firms should usually respond within 15 business days, or explain the delay and send a final response within 35 business days.

6. **Ask your bank or card provider about any connected card or bank dispute.**  
   If the locked app account involved a card top-up, card-funded purchase, scam, duplicate payment, or unauthorised transaction, contact the bank or card provider and ask what dispute options may apply. Chargeback can apply to some card payments, but it is not guaranteed and does not cover every payment type.

7. **Escalate if the provider does not sort it out.**  
   If you are unhappy with the provider’s final response, or they do not respond in time, you can usually take the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service. Keep your screenshots, complaint message, final response, and any ticket numbers together.

8. **Report suspected fraud or account takeover.**  
   If you think someone accessed the account, used your details, or tricked you into sending money, tell any linked bank or card provider straight away. In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report fraud through Report Fraud. In Scotland, report fraud to Police Scotland by calling 101.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide today whether to take legal action.
- You don’t need to post publicly or argue on social media to make progress.
- You don’t need to open a new account with the same app before you have saved evidence and started the complaint route.
- You don’t need to write a perfect legal letter; a clear complaint with dates, amount, impact, and the outcome you want is enough to start.

## Important reassurance
Payment apps can lock accounts because of security checks, identity checks, unusual activity, or compliance reviews. That is stressful, especially when money is stuck, but a clear paper trail and the right complaint route can help move the issue out of vague support messages.

## Scope note
These are first steps to secure your money position, preserve evidence, and start the traceable complaint route. Later decisions, especially where there is fraud, identity misuse, or serious hardship, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Rules and routes can vary by provider, account type, and the reason for the lock.
