# What to do if… a rideshare or taxi driver’s conversation becomes sexual and you feel trapped in the situation (UK)

## Short answer
Exit the vehicle at the nearest well-lit, public, or staffed location and call **999** if you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t feel obligated to be polite, laugh at jokes, or provide a "socially acceptable" reason for leaving.
- Don’t argue or attempt to explain why the behaviour is inappropriate if you are still inside the vehicle.
- Don’t accept offers for a "short cut," a "scenic route," or a stop at a private location.
- Don’t share your home address if you have not already reached it; choose a nearby public landmark instead.
- Don’t worry about the fare, the driver's rating, or "making a scene" while you are still in the car.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the nearest public safe point and instruct the driver to stop.** Use a firm, practical excuse that requires an immediate halt.
   - "I am going to be sick, pull over at that petrol station / shop / bus stop now."
   - "I've forgotten something, I need to get out here."
   - "I need to meet a friend at this corner, please stop the car."
2. **Use the in-app emergency features if you are using a rideshare service.** Most apps (Uber, Bolt, Free Now) have a "Safety Toolkit" or "Emergency Button" (often a blue shield or red icon) that can alert the company and dial emergency services while sharing your live GPS location.
3. **Call 999 if you cannot safely exit or if the driver refuses to stop.** If it is not safe to speak, use the "Silent Solution" protocol.
   - **Dial 999 from your mobile.**
   - **Listen to the automated prompts.**
   - **Press 55 when prompted** to let the operator know it is a genuine emergency. This will transfer your call to the police without you needing to speak.
4. **Share your live location with a trusted contact immediately.** Use the "Share Trip" feature in the app or "Share My Location" via WhatsApp / iMessage / Find My. Send a brief text: "In a cab, driver is making me unsafe, I am getting out at [Location]."
5. **Exit the vehicle and move toward other people or a staffed building.** Do not stay on the pavement near the car. Walk directly into a shop, hotel lobby, or petrol station and tell the staff you feel unsafe. 
6. **Note the vehicle and driver details only once you are at a safe distance.** 
   - Private hire vehicles (minicabs/Uber) must display a licence plate (usually yellow or white) on the rear and a smaller one in the windscreen.
   - Taxi drivers (Black Cabs) must display their badge number.
   - Take a screenshot of the app screen showing the driver’s name, car model, and registration.
7. **Report the incident to the relevant licensing authority.** 
   - In London: Report to **Transport for London (TfL)** via their online tool or by calling 0343 222 4000.
   - Outside London: Use the "Find your local council" tool on Gov.uk to identify the licensing body for that area (the council name is usually on the vehicle's licence plate).

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to press criminal charges right now.
- You do not need to pay the fare or handle payment disputes while you are still shaken.
- You do not need to check your "rating" or respond to automated "How was your ride?" prompts.
- You do not need to provide a statement to the licensing board immediately; this can be done the following day.

## Important reassurance
Instinctive responses such as "freezing," "fawning" (being overly nice to de-escalate), or going quiet are survival mechanisms and are not your fault. If a conversation feels threatening or "trapping," it is a valid safety concern regardless of whether physical contact has occurred.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for exiting an unsafe vehicle. It does not cover the legal process of reporting sexual offences or long-term recovery support.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes. If a crime has been committed, contact the police on **999** (emergency) or **101** (non-emergency). Professional support is available through organizations like Rape Crisis England & Wales or Rape Crisis Scotland.
