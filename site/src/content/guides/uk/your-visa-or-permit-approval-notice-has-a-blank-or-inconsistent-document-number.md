# What to do if… your visa or permit approval notice has a blank or inconsistent document number (UK)

## Short answer
Do not rely only on a confirmation email or PDF with a blank or inconsistent identifier. Check your UKVI record, eVisa, share code, or vignette before using it for travel or official checks.

## Do not do these things
- Do not guess a “document number” by typing a passport number, GWF number, UAN, BRP number, or other reference unless the form clearly asks for that exact number.
- Do not book non-refundable travel or make irreversible moving, work, rent, or study decisions based only on a notice with missing or inconsistent details.
- Do not pay unofficial agents or send your documents to people who are not part of the official process.
- Do not share screenshots or copies of your approval notice more widely than necessary.
- Do not submit a new visa application from scratch unless UKVI or a qualified immigration adviser tells you that is needed.

## What to do now
1. **Pause and identify where the blank or inconsistent number appears.**  
   Write down whether the issue is in an approval email, a PDF decision letter, an online account, a share-code page, an airline form, an employer check, a landlord check in England, a university check, or a vignette sticker in your passport.

2. **Check the authoritative record for your type of proof.**  
   If you have an eVisa, sign in to your UKVI account and view your immigration status. If you have a vignette sticker in your passport, check the printed vignette. Compare your name, date of birth, nationality, passport or travel document details, validity dates, and any work or study conditions.

3. **If your eVisa is wrong or you cannot get a share code, report the eVisa error through the official service.**  
   Use this route if the details shown in your eVisa are wrong, if you can view your eVisa but cannot get a share code, or if a technical problem stops you viewing the eVisa while signed in. Keep the confirmation or reference from the report.

4. **If there is an error in a vignette before you travel, contact UKVI before travelling.**  
   Tell UKVI exactly what is wrong on the vignette and follow the instructions they give you. Keep your passport and application references available, but do not send original documents anywhere unless the official route tells you to.

5. **If someone needs to check your status now, use the UK method designed for that check.**  
   Get a share code from the eVisa service if you can. Give the checker the share code and your date of birth so they can use the official “check someone’s immigration status” service. For an employer, use the right-to-work route. For a landlord or letting agent, remember right-to-rent checks apply in England.

6. **If travel is coming up, check the passport or travel document linked to your eVisa.**  
   Sign in to your UKVI account and make sure the current passport or travel document you will use for travel is added or updated. If a carrier or booking form asks for a number that does not match your proof type, ask what proof they can accept and do not invent a number.

7. **Keep a simple private record.**  
   Note what is wrong, where it appears, the date and time you checked it, which official service you used, any reference numbers, and what response you received.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to complain, escalate, get paid legal advice, or make a fresh application.
- You do not need to prove every possible right at once; first confirm whether the problem is in your UKVI record, your vignette, or only a third-party form.
- You do not need to argue with an airline, employer, landlord, or university system before you have checked which identifier is valid for your status type.

## Important reassurance
A blank or inconsistent field does not automatically mean your permission has been refused or cancelled. Different records and forms can ask for different identifiers, so the safest next step is to check the official UKVI record and correct any real error through the official route.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions may need help from UKVI, your visa application centre route, your sponsor, your university, your employer, your landlord or letting agent in England, or a qualified immigration adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, travel, employment, housing, or professional advice. Immigration systems and carrier checks can change, so rely on official UKVI records and official correction routes rather than assumptions from one email, PDF, or form field.
