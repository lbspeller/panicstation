# What to do if… you are told your passport number on a visa or permit does not match your current passport (UK)

## Short answer
Update the identity document linked to your UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) account immediately online and carry both your old and new passports if your visa is a physical sticker (vignette). Do not attempt to board a flight if an airline has already flagged a digital record mismatch, as automated 2026 "permission-to-travel" checks will likely block your boarding.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to travel if a carrier has already confirmed a digital mismatch (you will likely be issued a "no-board" instruction).
- Do not assume that carrying a physical new passport "overrides" an outdated digital eVisa record.
- Do not edit or alter any physical visa stickers or digital PDF status documents.
- Do not discard your old passport, especially if it contains a valid physical visa vignette.
- Do not apply for an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) as a "fix" if you already hold a UK visa or residency (this can cause record conflicts).
- Do not pay unofficial third parties to "expedite" a Home Office record update.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the specific type of mismatch you are facing.**
   - Determine if the error is on a physical sticker (vignette) in an old passport.
   - Determine if the error is in your digital UKVI account / eVisa record.
   - Note the exact numbers: the passport number on the record versus your current document.
2. **Access the "Update your UK Visas and Immigration account details" service.**
   - **Log in to your UKVI account** using your previous document details if necessary.
   - **Select the option to "add a new identity document"** to link your new passport.
   - **Upload a clear image of your new passport** if prompted by the system or the UKVI app.
3. **Report a technical error if the self-service update fails.**
   - **Use the official "Report an error with your eVisa" form** if your name, nationality, or passport number is displayed incorrectly and cannot be changed via the standard update tool.
   - **Keep the reference number** provided after submitting the error report.
4. **Gather physical evidence of your status for manual inspection.**
   - **Carry both your current passport and the expired passport** containing your valid visa vignette.
   - **Download and save a "View and Prove" share code** and a PDF copy of your status if the system allows you to generate one.
   - **Locate your original Home Office decision letter** or email as secondary proof.
5. **Request the carrier to contact the Home Office Support Hub if blocked abroad.**
   - **Ask the airline to contact the UK Border Force Carrier Support Hub** (+44 20 3337 0927) if they are refusing boarding solely due to a document number discrepancy that you have evidence for.
   - **Show the airline proof that you have initiated an update** (such as a confirmation email from UKVI) if travel is imminent.

## What can wait
- You do not need to apply for a "transfer of conditions" (BRP replacement) immediately if you have already transitioned to an eVisa.
- You do not need to contact your local MP or a lawyer for a simple passport renewal update unless the Home Office fails to process the update within several weeks.
- You do not need to worry about the "validity" of your permission; a passport number mismatch affects your ability to *prove* status, not the status itself.

## Important reassurance
Passport renewals are the most common cause of immigration record discrepancies. Since the 2026 shift to "digital by default" borders, these mismatches are frequently flagged by automated airline systems. This is a procedural synchronization issue rather than an underlying problem with your right to be in the UK. Most mismatches can be resolved through the official UKVI update portal.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document mismatches. It does not cover legal appeals for visa denials, complex name changes, or lost/stolen passport procedures which require specialized reporting.

## Important note
This information is based on UK immigration protocols as of March 2026. Different rules apply to British/Irish dual nationals and those with right of abode. Automated carrier checks are strictly enforced; always ensure your digital record is updated at least 72 hours before travel.
