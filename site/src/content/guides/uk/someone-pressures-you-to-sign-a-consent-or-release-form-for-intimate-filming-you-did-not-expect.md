# What to do if… someone pressures you to sign a consent or release form for intimate filming you did not expect (UK)

## Short answer
Do not sign anything while you feel pressured, rushed, scared, intoxicated, or confused. Move towards a safer pause and contact specialist sexual violence or intimate-image support before making any decision.

## Do not do these things
- Do not sign “to make it go away” if you did not expect the filming or do not feel free to choose.
- Do not stay alone with the person to “talk it through” if that makes you feel less safe.
- Do not hand over your phone, passwords, ID, passport, or bank card to “verify” anything.
- Do not argue about the law, the wording of the form, or what they might do next if they are threatening you.
- Do not send more intimate images or videos to bargain for deletion.
- Do not blame yourself if you froze, delayed, complied earlier, or are not sure what happened.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safety pause.** Move away from the person if you can: step outside, go to a staffed area, call someone you trust, or leave with another person. If you feel in immediate danger, call **999**.
2. **Use one short sentence.** Say or message: “I am not signing anything today.” You do not need to explain, debate, or prove why.
3. **Do not give them a signature, initials, video statement, or written confirmation.** A rushed “consent” or “release” form can wait while you get support.
4. **If you need time to leave safely, ask for distance rather than a decision.** You can say: “Send it to me later. I am leaving now.” Use a safer contact method if giving your usual number or email feels unsafe.
5. **Keep control of your devices and documents.** Put your phone, ID, keys, and payment card somewhere they cannot easily take them. If your phone is not safe to use, ask staff or a trusted person to call someone for you.
6. **If they threaten to share footage or images, stop negotiating.** Move to safety and contact specialist intimate-image support when you can.
7. **Tell one trusted person the minimum they need to know.** For example: “I’m being pressured to sign a form about intimate filming. Please stay on the phone while I leave.”
8. **Use specialist support without committing to a report.** A sexual assault referral centre, where available, a sexual violence support service, or an appropriate intimate-image abuse service can help you think through safety, health, reporting, and takedown options.
9. **If it is safe and does not keep you engaged with them, keep existing messages, voicemails, forms, or threats; do not put yourself at risk to collect proof.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to report to police.
- You do not need to decide now whether to take legal action.
- You do not need to decide now whether to contact a platform or ask for takedown.
- You do not need to write a full account of what happened right now.
- You do not need to answer their calls, messages, or deadlines once you are away from immediate pressure.

## Important reassurance
Being pressured to sign a “consent” or “release” form after intimate filming you did not expect can be frightening and confusing. Freezing, going quiet, complying for a moment, or needing time does not mean you agreed freely. You are allowed to slow the situation down and get support before deciding anything.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about reporting, medical care, legal options, communication, or image removal may need help from a specialist support service, advocate, solicitor, healthcare professional, or other appropriate professional.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you feel unsafe or at risk of immediate harm, call 999.
