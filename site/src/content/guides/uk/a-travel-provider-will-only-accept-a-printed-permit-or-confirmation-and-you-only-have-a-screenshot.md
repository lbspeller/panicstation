# What to do if… a travel provider will only accept a printed permit or confirmation and you only have a screenshot (UK)

## Short answer
Obtain the original PDF or email confirmation and print it immediately using a service point (desk, kiosk, hotel, or library), as screenshots are often legally invalid for travel in the UK.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not edit or crop the screenshot.** Altering a document image can be flagged as fraudulent activity.
- **Do not wait until the gate or boarding point to address the issue.** Staff at these points often lack the facilities to help you print.
- **Do not rely on showing a screenshot to a rail inspector.** Under National Rail conditions, a screenshot is not a valid ticket and can result in a Penalty Fare or prosecution.
- **Do not hand over your phone unlocked.** If someone else is printing for you, send them a specific file rather than giving them access to your device.
- **Do not delete the original booking email.** You need the live data or the attachment to generate an official printout.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the exact document required.** Ask the provider's staff if they require the original PDF, a specific booking voucher, or if any printout of the booking reference will suffice.
2. **Retrieve the original source file.** 
   - Check the **Manage Booking** section of the provider’s website or app for a "Download PDF" or "Email Ticket" option.
   - Search your email inbox for the booking reference number plus the terms **pdf**, **voucher**, or **attachment**.
3. **Use on-site printing facilities at the terminal.**
   - **Airports:** Use a self-service check-in kiosk to reprint your boarding pass or visit the staffed check-in desk (note that budget airlines may charge a fee for this).
   - **Rail Stations:** Use a ticket machine to collect physical tickets. 
     - You will need the **8-digit collection reference**.
     - You must insert a payment card for identification; use the **original card** used for the booking as many machines still require it.
4. **Locate a local printing service if you are not at a terminal.**
   - **Hotels:** Download the document as a PDF and email it to the front desk for printing.
   - **Public Libraries:** Most UK libraries offer "Princh" or similar mobile printing where you scan a QR code on the printer to upload and pay for a document.
   - **High Street Shops:** Search for "print and copy" shops or large pharmacies that offer document printing services.
5. **Verify the printout quality before leaving the printer.**
   - Ensure the **QR code or barcode** is sharp and not distorted.
   - Confirm your **full name, travel dates, and reference number** are all visible on the page.
6. **Arrive early for your departure.** Allow at least 30 extra minutes to handle potential printing delays or queues at the provider's assistance desk.

## What can wait
- **Disputing the policy.** You do not need to argue about the "paper-only" rule right now; focus on obtaining the document.
- **Requesting a refund for printing fees.** Save any receipts from kiosks or shops to claim back later if the provider's system was at fault.
- **Technical troubleshooting.** Do not try to fix why your app isn't working while you are in a time-sensitive queue.

## Important reassurance
Requiring a paper copy is a common procedural fallback, especially for international permits or specific rail routes. Once you have a physical printout of the official document, the technological barrier is removed and you can proceed through security and boarding normally.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for document-related travel delays. It does not cover visa applications, passport replacement, or long-term legal disputes regarding travel terms.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only. Individual travel providers, including airlines and Train Operating Companies (TOCs), have different terms of carriage. Presenting an invalid document format can lead to denied boarding or penalty fares.
