# What to do if… you hit a daily transfer limit and need to send a payment today (UK)

## Short answer
Pause long enough to verify the payee and bank details, then contact your bank through a trusted route and ask what bank-supported same-day option is available.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t rush because someone is pressuring you with “today only” or “you’ll lose it” language; urgency can be a scam tactic.
- Don’t send money to a “safe account”, “holding account”, or replacement account because someone tells you to.
- Don’t accept changed bank details by text or email without independently confirming them through a known contact method.
- Don’t share one-time passcodes, approval prompts, passwords, PINs, or security details with anyone.
- Don’t keep retrying the same transfer repeatedly; it may trigger more checks or create confusing payment attempts.
- Don’t switch to gift cards, crypto, or an unfamiliar money-transfer route just to get the payment done today.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether the payment request itself is safe.**  
   Look at the invoice, contract, payment reference, amount, account name, sort code, and account number. If the request was unexpected, urgent, or says bank details have changed, stop and verify using a phone number or email address you already trust, not the contact details in the new message.

2. **If anyone is on the phone helping you make the payment, end the call.**  
   Re-contact your bank using the number on the back of your card, the number inside your banking app, or a branch route listed by your bank. If your bank supports it, you can dial 159 to be connected to your bank; if 159 does not work for your bank or phone, use the trusted card or app route instead.

3. **Find the exact limit or block message.**  
   In your app or online banking, look for wording such as:
   - “Daily limit reached”
   - “Per-transaction limit”
   - “New payee” or “first payment” limit
   - “Payment held for checks”
   - “Confirmation of Payee no match” or “partial match”

   Take a screenshot or write down the exact wording before you call the bank.

4. **Ask your bank for the quickest safe route they can actually support today.**  
   Say: “I have hit a daily transfer limit and need to make a time-critical payment today. What verified route can you offer?” Ask whether they can:
   - temporarily increase the limit after extra verification;
   - make the payment through telephone banking;
   - arrange it in branch with identification;
   - arrange a CHAPS payment today, if the amount and timing make that appropriate.

   CHAPS is usually a working-day, same-day route with bank cut-off times, so ask your bank what is still possible today before promising the recipient.

5. **Ask the recipient for a safe fallback that does not require risky shortcuts.**  
   Depending on who you are paying, ask whether they can accept:
   - a card payment for all or part of the amount;
   - a verified Pay by Bank or Open Banking checkout from a business you recognise, after checking what receipt and payment protection you will get;
   - a part-payment today with written confirmation that the balance can follow tomorrow;
   - written confirmation that the payment will be treated as on time if you send proof that your bank is processing it.

6. **Use Confirmation of Payee as a stop signal, not a box-tick.**  
   For UK domestic transfers, your bank may show an account name-check before you pay. If the name does not clearly match what you expected, stop and verify the details independently before sending anything.

7. **If you think this may be a scam, or you already sent money to the wrong place, contact your bank immediately.**  
   Say clearly: “I am worried this is a scam or misdirected payment.” Ask the bank to try to stop or recall the payment and to record it through its fraud process. After contacting the bank, you can report fraud through Report Fraud if you are in England, Wales, or Northern Ireland; in Scotland, use Police Scotland by calling 101.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to change banks, permanently raise limits, or switch payment apps.
- You do not need to explain every detail to the recipient right now; focus on a safe payment route or a written extension.
- You do not need to make several backup payments “just in case”; that can create duplicates and more stress.
- You do not need to resolve long-term account settings before the immediate payment is safely handled.

## Important reassurance
Hitting a daily transfer limit is common, especially with a new payee, a larger amount, or several payments in one day. It does not mean you have done anything wrong; it means the next step is to slow the moment down and use a bank-verified route.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for getting a time-critical payment handled safely and reducing the risk of an irreversible mistake. If the payment is tied to a contract deadline, rent, a deposit, or completion funds, later decisions may need specialist help from the payee, your bank, a regulated adviser, or a legal professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, banking, fraud investigation, or professional advice. Banks set different limits, security checks, fees, and cut-off times, and what is possible today can depend on the bank, the payment type, the amount, and whether it is a working day.
