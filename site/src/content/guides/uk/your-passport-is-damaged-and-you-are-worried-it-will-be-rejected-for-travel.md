# What to do if… your passport is damaged and you are worried it will be rejected for travel (UK)

## Short answer
Treat the passport as something that could be refused. Do not try to repair it; check with your carrier, check the official replacement or urgent-passport route, and use the Emergency Travel Document route if you are abroad and eligible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to repair it with glue, tape, ironing, relaminating, trimming, or removing pages; this can make it look more suspicious.
- Do not rely on “it worked last time”; check-in staff, carriers, and border officials can make a different decision on the day.
- Do not use unofficial passport agents, copycat sites, or anyone promising certainty about border acceptance.
- Do not post your passport anywhere unless you are following an official application instruction and using a secure, trackable method.
- Do not keep handling the damaged area to show people; further damage can make the risk worse.

## What to do now
1. **Stop trying to improve the passport.**  
   Put it somewhere flat, dry, and safe. Do not peel, press, tape, clean, scrape, or laminate any part of it.

2. **Check whether the damage matches official UK damage examples.**  
   HM Passport Office says a damaged passport must be replaced and you may not be able to travel with it. Damage can include unreadable details, ripped, cut, detached or missing pages, cover damage, a cover coming away, stains from ink or water, laminate lifting, unreadable security details, or a machine-readable part that cannot be scanned.

3. **Write down exactly what is wrong.**  
   Use plain wording such as “water staining on visa pages”, “laminate lifting on the photo page”, “cover coming away”, “page torn”, or “chip not scanning”. Take clear photos for your own records, but do not rely on photos as a substitute for the actual passport.

4. **Contact your airline, ferry operator, train operator, or other carrier now.**  
   Describe the exact damage and ask whether they are likely to allow travel with that passport. Ask whether they can note the contact on your booking, and keep your own record of the date, time, person or team contacted, and what they said. This does not guarantee boarding, but it can help you decide whether to stop and replace the passport instead of gambling at check-in.

5. **Check the entry rules for your destination and every transit country.**  
   Use GOV.UK foreign travel advice and the destination country’s rules where available. A passport that fails an e-gate, chip check, or machine-readable scan may still be assessed by a person, but it may also be refused.

6. **If you are in the UK, start the official damaged-passport replacement route.**  
   Use GOV.UK to replace a damaged passport. If travel is very soon, check the GOV.UK urgent passport service rather than using unofficial agents or waiting until the airport.

7. **If you are abroad and need to travel soon, check whether you can apply for an Emergency Travel Document.**  
   You may be able to apply if you are a British national outside the UK, need to travel within 6 weeks, cannot use your UK passport because it is damaged, and cannot renew or replace your UK passport from abroad before you travel. GOV.UK says you usually need to have had a valid UK passport issued on or after 1 January 2006; if not, an Emergency Travel Document is usually only considered for urgent and unplanned travel in exceptional circumstances.

8. **Before relying on an Emergency Travel Document, check your route.**  
   GOV.UK says you should check whether an Emergency Travel Document will be accepted by the countries you plan to leave, enter, or travel through, including whether you need a visa.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether the whole trip is lost; first check carrier acceptance and the official replacement, urgent-passport, or Emergency Travel Document route.
- You do not need to prove the damage is “minor”; uncertainty is enough reason to act.
- You do not need to start insurance, refund, or complaint arguments right now unless a deadline is imminent.
- You do not need to contact every person on the trip before you know whether replacement or urgent travel paperwork is possible.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful because a damaged passport can turn into a last-minute refusal. Moving from guessing to official checks, carrier contact, and the correct passport route is the safest way to reduce that risk.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the chance of being refused boarding or entry. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, insurance, replacement timing, or changing travel plans may need help from the carrier, insurer, passport service, or another specialist body.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, travel, financial, or professional advice. Carriers and border officials make their own decisions, and acceptance can vary by route, country, document condition, and timing.
