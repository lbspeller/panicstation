# What to do if… you left your passport in a taxi or rideshare and need to travel today (UK)

## Short answer
First, try to get the passport back through the driver, app, booking company, or black-cab lost property route. At the same time, speak to your airline or transport operator before security, because same-day travel without the correct passport is usually not possible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep searching your bags repeatedly after one careful check; switch to contacting the driver or operator.
- Do not post your passport number, photo, or personal details publicly.
- Do not go through security hoping the missing passport will be dealt with later; ask the airline or transport operator before security.
- Do not cancel the passport while the driver or company is actively returning it to you, unless you have accepted it is lost or stolen.
- Do not pay anyone who claims they can unofficially “fix” passport or boarding checks.
- Do not assume an urgent UK passport service will rescue same-day travel after a lost passport.

## What to do now
1. **Do one controlled check:** look in the passport’s usual place, the last bag pocket, your coat, the seat area, and any document folder. Stop after one careful check so you do not lose retrieval time.
2. **Save the ride details:** screenshot the receipt, booking reference, pickup and drop-off times, route, vehicle details, driver name, app chat, and payment record.
3. **Contact the fastest retrieval route:**
   - **Rideshare or app booking:** use the app’s lost-item route for that trip and choose the option to contact the driver if available.
   - **Minicab or private hire vehicle:** contact the company you booked with and ask them to contact the driver urgently.
   - **London black cab:** make a TfL lost property enquiry. If you also have cab or driver details, keep them ready, but do not rely on TfL as a guaranteed same-day return route.
4. **If the driver replies:** agree a safe, public handover point that you can reach quickly. Ask the driver to confirm they have the passport before you travel to meet them.
5. **If you are at or near the airport, port, or station:** go to your airline or transport operator desk before security. Say your passport is missing and ask what choices are still open today, such as a later departure, rebooking, or document requirements for your route.
6. **Set a short decision point:** use the check-in, bag-drop, boarding, or departure cut-off to decide how long you can keep trying retrieval. If return is not confirmed before that point, switch to cancellation, replacement, or rebooking steps.
7. **If the passport is lost or stolen in the UK:** cancel it through GOV.UK as soon as possible once you accept it is lost or stolen. You must cancel a lost or stolen UK passport before applying for a replacement.
8. **If you are in the UK and need a replacement:** check the GOV.UK urgent passport page. For a lost passport, urgent services usually do not provide a same-day replacement; check the current eligibility and appointment options before making travel promises.
9. **If you are abroad:** check whether you can apply for a UK Emergency Travel Document. If you apply for an Emergency Travel Document, you do not need to cancel the passport separately because cancellation is handled as part of that application.
10. **Protect anything else that was lost with it:** if your passport was in a wallet, phone case, or bag, freeze payment cards, secure access to your phone, and protect travel or email accounts that were exposed.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make an insurance claim; keep receipts, screenshots, and a short timeline.
- You do not need to write a long explanation for every organisation; focus on retrieval, the airline or operator decision, and the official passport route.
- You do not need to rebook everything until you know whether the passport is being returned in time for the next usable departure.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but it has a clear order: one careful search, fastest retrieval route, airline or operator decision, then official passport steps. Even if today’s journey is not possible, you can still reduce the damage by keeping records and using the legitimate replacement or Emergency Travel Document route.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Later decisions about replacement, Emergency Travel Documents, rebooking, complaints, or insurance may need specialist help from the relevant official service, travel operator, or insurer.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Processes, appointment availability, document requirements, and operator rules can change, so follow the official service and your airline or transport operator’s instructions for your specific journey.
