# What to do if… you develop new severe pain when swallowing and cannot tolerate liquids (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as urgent. Stop trying to force liquids down and get urgent medical help now; call 999 if you have breathing difficulty, drooling because you cannot swallow, rapidly worsening swelling, or severe distress.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep testing sips of water if each attempt causes severe pain, choking, or brings it straight back up.
- Do not try to push food down, swallow bread, or wash it down to clear a suspected blockage.
- Do not take tablets or capsules unless a clinician tells you to, because they may lodge or worsen irritation.
- Do not lie flat if you are struggling with saliva, coughing, or choking; stay upright.
- Do not put fingers or objects down your throat to check or remove anything.
- Do not make yourself vomit.
- Do not try to neutralise a swallowed chemical with food, drink, milk, acid, or alkali.
- Do not delay because you think it is just a sore throat if you cannot tolerate liquids.

## What to do now
1. **Stop eating and drinking for now.** If liquids are not going down, continuing may increase choking or aspiration risk and may worsen pain.
2. **Sit upright and keep your airway clear.** If saliva is pooling, let it dribble into a tissue or bowl rather than repeatedly forcing swallows.
3. **Call 999 now if any immediate danger sign is present:**
   - trouble breathing, noisy breathing, choking, gasping, or feeling you cannot get air
   - drooling because you cannot swallow
   - rapidly worsening throat, tongue, face, or neck swelling
   - a new muffled voice, severe inability to swallow, or extreme distress
   - faintness, confusion, seizure, or loss of consciousness
4. **If a cleaner, chemical, medicine overdose, or unknown harmful substance may have been swallowed, call 999 or go to A&E now.** Do not eat, drink, vomit, or try to neutralise it while you are getting help. Take the container or packaging with you if you can do that safely.
5. **If you are not in immediate breathing danger and there is no possible chemical or poisoning exposure, use the urgent-care route for where you are:**
   - **England:** use NHS 111 online or call 111, and say: **“new severe pain when swallowing and I cannot tolerate liquids.”**
   - **Scotland:** contact your GP urgently if it is open. If your GP, pharmacy, or dental practice is closed, phone NHS 24 on 111. Call 999 or go to A&E if it feels life-threatening.
   - **Wales:** use NHS 111 Wales or call 111, and say: **“new severe pain when swallowing and I cannot tolerate liquids.”**
   - **Northern Ireland:** contact your GP urgently if open; if it is closed, telephone your GP out-of-hours service first. Go to A&E or call 999 if you cannot get urgent advice, you are getting worse, or danger signs appear.
6. **Say any likely trigger early because it changes triage.** Mention a possible fish or chicken bone, food bolus, pill stuck in the throat, very hot drink or food, recent vomiting, recent throat procedure, chemical exposure, allergy symptoms, or new neck swelling.
7. **If you are alone, ask someone to stay with you or check in.** If you need urgent care or A&E, do not drive yourself if you feel weak, dizzy, drowsy, are drooling, are vomiting, or might have swallowed something harmful.
8. **Have key details ready while you wait.** Note when it started, what you last ate or drank, whether there was choking, fever, rash, allergy symptoms, swelling, breathing symptoms, medicines you take, blood thinners, steroids, immune-suppressing medicines, and any relevant health conditions.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out the cause right now.
- You do not need to decide alone whether it is serious enough for A&E; use the urgent-care route above, or 999 if danger signs appear.
- You do not need to try gargles, lozenges, sprays, home remedies, or more sips if liquids are not tolerable.
- You do not need to cancel ordinary plans, message everyone, or search for a diagnosis before getting urgent advice.

## Important reassurance
Severe new pain when swallowing with inability to tolerate liquids is a valid reason to seek urgent help. Getting assessed quickly is about preventing avoidable problems such as dehydration, aspiration, or airway swelling, not overreacting.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the next few hours. Later decisions may need a clinician or specialist, depending on the cause and how your symptoms change.

## Important note
This is general information and is not medical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If symptoms are severe, rapidly worsening, or you have any breathing difficulty, treat it as an emergency and call 999.
