# What to do if… a car-share app will not let you end the trip and charges may keep running (UK)

## Short answer
Park the vehicle legally in its designated zone, ensure the engine is off and handbrake is on, and contact the provider immediately to end the trip manually while documenting the time and location with photos.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the car in an illegal or unsafe location (such as double yellow lines or blocking a driveway) to save time.
- Do not keep driving to find a "better signal" once you are already at the correct return location.
- Do not assume that simply locking the car with the key or app has ended the rental period.
- Do not delete the app or clear your phone's cache until you have taken screenshots of the active trip and any error messages.
- Do not start a second booking or "extend" the current one to try and "reset" the system unless specifically told to by support.

## What to do now
1. **Verify that the vehicle is physically ready to be returned.**  
   - Ensure the ignition is fully switched off and the key (if physical) is returned to its holder in the glovebox.
   - Check that the handbrake is firmly engaged and all windows/doors are fully closed.
   - Confirm you are within the "Home Zone" or specific "Dedicated Bay" required by the app.

2. **Attempt one technical reset of the app.**  
   - Toggle Airplane Mode on and off to refresh your data connection.
   - Force-close the app entirely and reopen it.
   - If the car has an internal PIN pad or touchscreen (e.g., Enterprise Car Club), look for an "End Trip" or "Return" button on the hardware itself.

3. **Collect visual evidence of the return while the clock is running.**  
   - Take a screenshot of the app showing the "End Trip" error or the screen stating you are "Not in Zone" while the car is clearly in the bay.
   - Take a photo of the car parked in the bay with the dashboard visible (to show mileage / fuel) and a photo of the bay's signage or street name.
   - Record the exact time you attempted to end the trip.

4. **Call the provider's emergency support line immediately.**  
   - Use the "Call" button in the app or find the number on the provider’s website (e.g., Enterprise Car Club: 0345 266 9290).
   - State clearly: **"I am at the return location, the vehicle is secure, but the app will not end the trip. Please end the trip manually from your end now."**
   - Note down the name of the agent and a case or reference number.

5. **Secure the vehicle manually if you must leave.**  
   - If support cannot be reached, lock the car using the app (if possible) or the physical key/fob.
   - Send an immediate email or in-app message to support with your photos and a statement that the vehicle is parked and secure at [Location] as of [Time].

6. **Monitor your billing and prepare for a dispute.**  
   - If you are overcharged, reply to the booking confirmation email with your evidence bundle (photos/screenshots/times).
   - Reference the 2026 Parking Code of Practice regarding "grace periods" if the app delay caused a parking fine from a private operator.

## What can wait
- You do not need to settle the final bill or refund amount while still standing at the car.
- You do not need to contact your bank or card issuer until the provider has made a final decision on your refund request.
- You do not need to leave a public review or social media post until you have attempted the official support channels.

## Important reassurance
App and connectivity failures are a known issue for car-share operators. If you have clear photos of the car in the correct location at the correct time, the provider’s GPS data will usually verify your claim, making it straightforward to have excess charges reversed.

## Scope note
This guide covers immediate stabilization and evidence collection to stop ongoing charges. It does not cover long-term legal disputes or car insurance claims for damages.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Terms of service vary between providers (such as Enterprise Car Club, Hiyacar, or Turo). If you feel unsafe in your current parking location, prioritize your personal safety and move to a well-lit public area before continuing to contact support.
