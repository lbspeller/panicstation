# What to do if… your vehicle gets stuck in an automated car wash or automated parking system (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside the vehicle and use the nearest **EMERGENCY STOP**, help button, or intercom to alert an operator to halt the machinery. Only call **999** if there is an immediate fire, medical emergency, or physical danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not exit the vehicle into a car wash tunnel, conveyor track, or moving machinery zone unless specifically instructed by staff or emergency services.
- Do not leave the engine running if you are trapped in an enclosed car lift, stacker, or robotic parking bay.
- Do not attempt to force open car wash doors, lift gates, or parking barriers.
- Do not try to climb out of a vehicle that is stuck between levels in a car lift or robotic stacker.
- Do not repeatedly change gears or apply heavy braking if the vehicle is on a moving conveyor.

## What to do now
1. **Identify the type of system you are currently in.**
   - Determine if it is a conveyor wash (vehicle moves), a rollover wash (brushes move around you), or an automated parking lift / robotic bay.

2. **Secure the vehicle interior and occupants.**
   - **Switch on hazard warning lights.**
   - **Ensure all windows and sunroofs are fully closed.**
   - **Keep hands and limbs away from glass and door edges.**
   - **Locate your Emergency Contact Card (ECC) in the glove box or sun visor if the vehicle was registered after January 2020.**

3. **Summon help using the site communication equipment.**
   - **Press the EMERGENCY STOP or HELP button located on the intercom panel, pay station, or nearby wall.**
   - **Look for the yellow bell pictogram on the intercom to confirm the alarm is active.**
   - **Wait for a green person pictogram or audio confirmation that a human operator is listening.**
   - **Use the vehicle horn in short bursts if no intercom is reachable from the driver’s window.**

4. **Provide essential details to the operator.**
   - **State your exact location, including the bay number, parking level, or site name.**
   - **Confirm whether there are children, pets, or individuals with medical needs in the vehicle.**
   - **Ask the operator to confirm that the power to the machinery has been isolated.**

5. **Follow instructions for vehicle positioning.**
   - **Wait for the operator to specify if the vehicle should be in Neutral or Park.**
   - **Do not move the vehicle until the operator confirms that brushes, spray arms, or lift platforms are clear.**

6. **Escalate to emergency services only for critical safety risks.**
   - **Call 999 immediately if you see smoke or fire, if someone is injured, or if the vehicle is being crushed.**
   - **Notify the operator if you are calling 999 so they can provide access for the Fire and Rescue Service.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the cause of the mechanical failure right now.
- You do not need to assess vehicle damage until you are outside the machinery area.
- You do not need to discuss liability or compensation with the site staff while still trapped.

## Important reassurance
Mechanical systems are designed with multiple fail-safes and emergency stops. Being stuck is a controlled technical fault, not an immediate physical threat in most cases. Operators are trained to handle these stalls and will guide you out once the system is reset or isolated.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for mechanical entrapment in UK car washes and parking systems. It does not cover vehicle repair, legal claims, or long-term recovery of property.

## Important note
Safe release from lifts and automated machinery is a controlled procedure that must be performed by trained personnel or emergency services. Do not accept help from untrained bystanders to pry open doors or override safety interlocks. If the situation involves a medical emergency or fire, call 999.
