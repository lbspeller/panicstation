# What to do if… your vehicle gets stuck in an automated car wash or automated parking system (UK)

## Short answer
Stay inside the vehicle if it is safe to do so, avoid entering any machinery area, and use a reachable emergency stop, help button, intercom, barrier call point, or phone number on the signs to get the operator to stop the equipment. If anyone is injured, in immediate danger, trapped with a worsening risk, or there is smoke or fire, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not get out into a car wash tunnel, conveyor area, lift shaft, stacker bay, robotic parking bay, or machinery zone unless staff or posted emergency instructions clearly tell you to.
- Do not reach through windows, lean out, or put hands near brushes, rollers, barriers, lift doors, gates, tracks, or moving structures.
- Do not try to force doors, barriers, lift gates, shutters, or vehicle doors against the equipment.
- Do not restart, drive, brake, steer hard, or change vehicle mode repeatedly unless the site instruction or operator tells you to.
- Do not try to release a car lift, stacker, robotic parking system, barrier mechanism, or trapped vehicle yourself.
- Do not argue about fault while the vehicle is still stuck; getting the equipment stopped and getting a human operator is the priority.

## What to do now
1. Work out which system you are in, just enough to describe it.
   - A conveyor or tunnel wash is moving your car along.
   - A rollover or bay wash has the car still while the wash machine moves around it.
   - A barrier or gate problem means you cannot exit a lane or car park.
   - A car lift, stacker, or robotic parking bay means the vehicle is inside an automated lifting or storage mechanism.

2. Make the inside of the vehicle safer.
   - Put hazard lights on if you can.
   - Keep windows closed unless you need a small gap to speak to staff and it is safe.
   - Keep everyone seated and away from doors, windows, and machinery.
   - Follow the posted or spoken vehicle-mode instruction for that site. If you are unsure, stop changing settings and ask the operator.

3. Alert the operator without leaving a safe position.
   - Press a reachable emergency stop, stop button, help button, intercom, or call point.
   - Check the pay kiosk, barrier pedestal, wash entrance or exit sign, wall sign, lift lobby, payment machine, or pedestrian door area for a help point or phone number.
   - If you cannot safely reach a control, use short horn beeps, call the site phone number if displayed, or call someone outside the vehicle and ask them to alert staff.

4. Say only what the operator needs first.
   - Say: "My vehicle is stuck in the car wash tunnel, wash bay, barrier lane, car lift, stacker, or robotic parking bay. Please stop the system and tell me whether to stay in the vehicle."
   - Give the site name, level, bay or lane number, nearest sign or marker, vehicle colour and registration, and whether anyone is injured or unwell.

5. If you are in a car lift, stacker, car elevator, or robotic parking bay, be extra still.
   - Stay in the vehicle unless staff or emergency services tell you otherwise.
   - Do not squeeze out through a gap or step between levels.
   - Ask staff to confirm the equipment has stopped before anyone approaches the vehicle.

6. If there is immediate danger, call 999.
   - Call 999 if there is smoke, fire, a medical emergency, heat or fumes making someone feel unwell, a crushing or collision risk, water rising inside the vehicle, or any trapped-person situation that could worsen quickly.
   - If you are safe but delayed, keep using the operator, help point, or displayed site contact route first.

7. Once the vehicle is out and everyone is safe, make a simple record.
   - Note the time, site name, exact location, bay, level or lane, and what staff told you.
   - Take photos or a short video only from a safe place.
   - Keep receipts, booking details, barrier tickets, wash codes, and any messages from the site.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who is at fault while you are still stuck.
- You do not need to agree to repairs, towing, payment, or settlement while you are still in the machine or barrier area.
- You do not need to contact your insurer, write a complaint, or gather detailed evidence until everyone is safely out and calm.
- You do not need to restart the wash, retry the parking system, or test the barrier yourself.

## Important reassurance
A stopped car wash or automated parking system can feel very frightening because the vehicle is boxed in and the equipment may be close to you. The safest first steps are slow and practical: keep clear of moving parts, get the system stopped, and get a human operator or emergency service involved if there is danger.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce risk and help you get released safely. Later decisions about vehicle damage, complaints, insurance, repairs, or site responsibility may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Follow on-site safety signage and operator instructions where it is safe to do so. If there is any immediate risk to life, injury, fire, smoke, fumes, crushing, collision, or worsening entrapment, call 999.
