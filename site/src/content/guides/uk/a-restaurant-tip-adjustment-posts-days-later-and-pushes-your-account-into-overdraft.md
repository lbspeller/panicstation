# What to do if… a restaurant tip adjustment posts days later and pushes your account into overdraft (UK)

## Short answer
Transfer funds immediately to bring the balance above £0 and contact your bank to dispute the settled amount while requesting a waiver of overdraft interest and refused-payment fees.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume a pending transaction amount is the final settlement figure until it moves to the "posted" or "completed" section of your statement.
- Do not ignore the negative balance if you have Direct Debits or standing orders due within the next 48 hours.
- Do not cancel the card unless you suspect the merchant is fraudulent rather than simply making a processing error.
- Do not wait for the restaurant to respond before notifying your bank that the account is in an unarranged position.
- Do not delete banking app notifications or digital receipts related to the original meal.

## What to do now
1. **Move enough funds into the account to cover the negative balance immediately.** **This stops the daily accrual of overdraft interest and prevents the bank from declining incoming payment requests for essential bills.**
   - Check for any upcoming payments (rent, utilities) scheduled for the next two business days.
   - If you cannot cover the balance, contact the payees of those bills to request a 48-hour delay.

2. **Capture the specific details of the transaction as it appears in its settled state.** **Note the merchant's registered name, the date the funds actually left the account (posting date), and the exact difference between the expected bill and the final charge.**
   - Take a screenshot of the transaction details in your mobile banking app.
   - Look for the "Authorisation Code" or "Transaction ID" if visible in the app.

3. **Locate the physical or digital receipt from the visit.** **This is the primary evidence required to prove that the amount you authorised (base bill plus any handwritten or keyed-in tip) does not match the amount the merchant eventually claimed.**
   - If the receipt is lost, check your email for a digital copy or look for a photo of the bill taken at the table.
   - Write down the approximate time of the meal and whether the tip was added via the card machine or written on a paper slip.

4. **Contact the restaurant management to request a manual correction.** **Ask them to verify their "end of day" settlement reports against your receipt and request a refund of the overcharged difference to be processed immediately.**
   - Request an email confirmation of any refund they agree to process, including the date and the specific amount.
   - Note the name of the manager or staff member you spoke with.

5. **Notify your bank's dispute or fraud department about the discrepancy.** **State clearly that the final settlement amount exceeds the authorised amount and ask them to "waive or refund any overdraft interest and refused-payment fees" triggered by this specific transaction.**
   - Use the phrase "I am disputing the transaction amount" rather than claiming fraud if you did eat at the establishment.
   - Mention Regulation 79 of the Payment Services Regulations 2017 if the bank hesitates, as this gives you a right to a refund when the exact amount was not specified at authorisation and the final amount is higher than reasonably expected.

6. **Request a formal temporary "hold" on fees while the dispute is investigated.** **Many UK banks will pause the application of interest or fees if a transaction is formally disputed as a merchant error.**
   - Ask for a reference number for this request.

## What can wait
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Financial Ombudsman Service today; they only step in after the bank has provided a Final Response Letter (usually 8 weeks).
- You do not need to close the bank account or switch providers while the dispute is active.
- You do not need to provide a full written statement of the event until the bank's dispute team requests it.

## Important reassurance
It is common for restaurants to "authorise" a base amount and "settle" a higher amount later once tips are processed. Banks are familiar with this "tip adjustment" lag and have established chargeback and dispute protocols to correct human entry errors or processing delays that cause account instability.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for UK current accounts affected by delayed merchant settlements. It is not intended for credit card Section 75 claims or long-term debt management.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance regarding UK banking procedures and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Individual bank terms and conditions regarding overdraft interest and dispute timeframes (usually 13 months for unauthorised payments) will vary.
