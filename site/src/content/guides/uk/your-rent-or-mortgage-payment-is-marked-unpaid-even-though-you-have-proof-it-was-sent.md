# What to do if… your rent or mortgage payment is marked unpaid even though you have proof it was sent (UK)

## Short answer
Avoid issuing a duplicate payment immediately. Compile your evidence of the successful transfer and provide it to the landlord, letting agent, or mortgage lender in writing with a request to trace the funds and pause any automated arrears actions.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send a second payment unless explicitly advised in writing by your bank that the first has failed or been returned.
- Do not ignore formal arrears notices, texts, or letters even if you have proof of payment.
- Do not rely on verbal agreements or phone conversations without a follow-up written record.
- Do not cancel existing Direct Debit or Standing Order instructions until the current discrepancy is resolved.
- Do not provide bank details or make payments to any "updated" account numbers received via unexpected text or email.

## What to do now
1. **Compile a digital "proof of payment" bundle.**
   - Save a copy of your bank statement showing the specific transaction line.
   - Capture a screenshot of the payment confirmation or receipt from your banking app.
   - Note the exact payment reference used (e.g., your tenancy ID or mortgage account number).
   - Record the recipient's sort code and account number as shown in your "sent" history.
2. **Notify the recipient in writing via email or their official portal.**
   - **State the exact date, time, and amount (£) of the payment.**
   - Attach your proof of payment bundle to the message.
   - Request that they perform a manual search of their incoming "suspense account" or unallocated payments using the amount and date.
   - Ask for a written confirmation that arrears escalation or late fees will be paused while the payment is traced.
3. **Obtain the Transaction Reference Number (TRN) from your bank.**
   - **Contact your bank's customer service or use their secure chat to request the TRN or Faster Payment ID (FPID) for the transaction.**
   - Ask the bank to confirm if the payment reached the recipient's bank (a "successful" status) or if it was rejected or "pended" for security checks.
   - Provide this specific reference number to your landlord or lender to help their bank locate the funds.
4. **Request an updated rent or mortgage ledger.**
   - **Ask for a full statement of account covering the last three months.**
   - Compare the missing payment date against the entries on their ledger to see if it was credited to the wrong month or account.
   - If the payment appears but has been applied to fees rather than the principal/rent, request a correction in writing.
5. **Utilise the Direct Debit Guarantee if the error involves a Direct Debit.**
   - **Contact your bank immediately if a Direct Debit was collected for the wrong amount, on the wrong date, or after you cancelled it.**
   - Request an "Indemnity Claim" under the Direct Debit Guarantee for a full and immediate refund.
   - Note that this only applies to Direct Debits (where the company pulls the money) and not Standing Orders (where you push the money).
6. **Report suspected payment redirection scams to Action Fraud.**
   - **Contact your bank's fraud department immediately if you suspect you were directed to pay a fraudulent account.**
   - Report the incident to Action Fraud online or by calling 0300 123 2040.
   - If you are in Scotland, report the matter to Police Scotland via 101.
7. **Seek urgent legal advice if you receive a "Notice of Possession".**
   - **Contact the Housing Loss Prevention Advice Service (HLPAS) for free legal advice and representation if you receive court papers.**
   - Under the Renters' Rights Act 2025, private landlords must provide a valid legal ground to evict you; "no-fault" Section 21 notices are no longer valid for new or converted tenancies.
   - Prepare your proof of payment bundle to present as evidence that no arrears exist.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a new bank or a permanent change in payment method today.
- You do not need to file a formal complaint with the Financial Ombudsman or a Property Redress Scheme until the initial trace is complete.
- You do not need to calculate compensation for stress or time spent until the account is balanced and safe.

## Important reassurance
Payment processing errors, such as "missing references" or "posting delays," are common administrative issues. Having a clear bank record of the funds leaving your account is strong evidence that you have fulfilled your contractual obligation. Most mismatches are resolved once a manual trace is conducted by the recipient's finance team.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for payment discrepancies. It does not replace legal advice in the event of an ongoing possession claim or complex bank errors.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or financial advice. If you are at immediate risk of eviction or repossession, consult a qualified solicitor or a specialist advisor from Shelter or Citizens Advice.
