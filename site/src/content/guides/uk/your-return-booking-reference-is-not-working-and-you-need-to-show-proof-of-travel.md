# What to do if… your return booking reference is not working and you need to show proof of travel (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and switch to proof by documents: find your itinerary, e-ticket receipt, ticket number, or payment receipt, then ask the airline or booking agent to re-send written confirmation you can show.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use fake or altered proof.
- Do not rely on a document that is only a temporary hold unless the airline or border staff accept it.
- Do not cancel or rebook in a panic unless you are sure the original ticket is unusable.
- Do not hand over your unlocked phone; show the screen yourself or use a printout.
- Do not post boarding passes, itineraries, barcodes, or booking codes publicly.
- Do not assume the booking has gone just because one website or app cannot find it.

## What to do now
1. **Step out of the pressure point if you can.** Ask to stand aside, keep your passport and phone with you, and open only the documents you need to show.

2. **Search for proof that does not depend on the booking reference.** In email, files, wallet apps, and screenshots, search for:
   - e-ticket
   - itinerary
   - receipt
   - ticket number
   - ETKT
   - confirmation
   - the airline name
   - your travel date

   The strongest quick proof is usually something showing your name, route, travel dates, and an e-ticket or ticket number.

3. **Try the booking lookup again with the exact booked name.** Lookups can fail because of spacing, hyphens, middle names, apostrophes, or surname order. Try the surname exactly as it appears on the itinerary or receipt, then try it without punctuation.

4. **Use the airline desk or live support to search by passenger details.** Say: “I have a return booking but the reference will not pull up. Can you search by name, route, date, and ticket number, and email or print the itinerary?” Give only the details they need, such as your booked name, route, travel date, and ticket number if you found one.

5. **If you booked through a travel agent or third party, contact them as well as the airline.** Ask them to send:
   - the airline record locator or PNR
   - the e-ticket number
   - a PDF itinerary or receipt
   - confirmation that the ticket is issued, not just reserved

   If you have an ATOL Certificate, check the booking details on it and contact the ATOL holder named there if the agent cannot help quickly.

6. **Build a small show pack.** Put these in one folder or email thread you can open quickly:
   - itinerary or e-ticket receipt
   - ticket number or airline confirmation
   - booking agent confirmation
   - card or bank transaction showing the airline or agent, date, and amount

   A bank transaction is supporting evidence, not usually enough on its own.

7. **Check the destination rule only if the desk is asking about entry requirements.** Use GOV.UK foreign travel advice for the destination and look at the entry requirements section. Some countries or airlines may ask for proof of onward or return travel; the exact rule depends on the destination and your circumstances.

8. **Use only legitimate last-minute options.** If you cannot retrieve the original booking and you must show proof immediately, consider buying a real onward or return ticket that is clearly refundable or changeable. Save the receipt and fare rules before paying.

9. **If you paid but the ticket was not issued, preserve the trail.** Once you are out of the immediate pinch, save emails, screenshots, receipts, chat logs, and times of calls. Ask the seller to fix or refund it first. If that does not work, contact your bank, card provider, or lender promptly and ask whether chargeback or Section 75 protection may apply.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide who is at fault right now.
- You do not need to write a full complaint at the desk.
- You do not need to decide today whether to dispute the payment.
- You do not need to explain every detail if the immediate need is simply to show a valid return or onward booking.

## Important reassurance
A failed booking lookup does not, by itself, prove that there is no ticket. It can be a name-format issue, a third-party booking issue, a schedule-change issue, or a system issue. The next useful step is to get written confirmation from the airline or seller.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, aimed at proving return or onward travel and avoiding rushed expensive decisions. Later refund, complaint, immigration, or insurance questions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional advice. Boarding and entry-document checks vary by destination, airline, booking route, nationality, and ticket type.
