# What to do if… your passport is damaged and you are unsure if it will be accepted for travel (UK)

## Short answer
Assume the document will be refused if any personal details are unreadable, the laminate is lifting, or pages are missing; do not attempt repairs and immediately book an urgent replacement or an Emergency Travel Document.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to repair the document using tape, glue, or heat, as this is often classified as fraudulent tampering.
- Do not rely on the fact that the passport was accepted on a previous journey.
- Do not use unofficial third-party websites or "passport agents" that charge extra fees.
- Do not attempt to travel if the photo page is significantly blurred, water-damaged, or the laminate has peeled back.
- Do not send the passport via standard untracked post when applying for a replacement.

## What to do now
1. **Document the current state of the passport without making further contact with the damage.** **Take high-resolution photographs of the photo page, the cover, and any specific areas of concern under natural light.** This provides a record for your insurance or application should the condition deteriorate further.
2. **Review the HM Passport Office (HMPO) official damage criteria to confirm if a replacement is mandatory.** **A passport must be replaced if details are indecipherable, the laminate is lifted enough to allow photo substitution, pages are detached, or the cover is coming away.** If the electronic chip is visible through the back cover or fails to scan, it is also considered damaged.
3. **Contact your airline or travel carrier immediately to explain the nature of the damage.** **Ask the carrier if they will permit you to board and request that their decision or advice be noted on your booking record.** Be aware that even if an airline allows you to fly, border officials at your destination may still refuse entry.
4. **Determine if you require a UK-based urgent replacement service.** **If you are in the UK and need a passport quickly, book an 'Online Premium' appointment (same-day) or a '1-week Fast Track' appointment online at GOV.UK.** Note that the Online Premium service is only for adult renewals where the passport is in your possession.
5. **Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if you are currently outside the UK.** **Use the official GOV.UK portal to apply for an ETD if you have travel planned within the next 6 weeks and cannot get a full replacement in time.**
   - If your passport was issued after 1 January 2006, the process is typically streamlined.
   - If your passport was issued before 2006, you may only be granted an ETD for truly essential travel (e.g., medical emergencies or funerals).
6. **Keep the damaged document in a protective, dry environment until it is time to surrender it.** **Place the passport in a clean sealable bag and avoid any further handling that could cause the binding or laminate to degrade.** You will usually need to send the damaged passport to HMPO as part of your replacement application.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on cancelling your entire trip until you have confirmed the earliest possible appointment for an urgent replacement.
- You do not need to contact your travel insurance provider for a claim until you have a confirmed refusal of travel or a receipt for your urgent replacement.
- You do not need to investigate the technical cause of a chip failure today.

## Important reassurance
Uncertainty regarding passport condition is a frequent issue for travellers, and check-in staff are trained to look for specific safety indicators. Moving from a state of "hoping it works" to taking a defined administrative step (like booking a 1-day Premium appointment) significantly reduces the risk of a high-stress refusal at the airport gate.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for document uncertainty. It does not cover long-term passport applications, visa requirements for specific countries, or the legal process of claiming compensation from carriers.

## Important note
The final decision to accept a document rests solely with the carrier's staff and the border control officers of the destination country. This guide is for information only and does not guarantee that following these steps will result in a successful journey. Always use official GOV.UK channels for document applications.
