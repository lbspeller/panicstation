# What to do if… your vaccination certificate or test-result app will not load when you need to show proof (UK)

## Short answer
Step out of the queue immediately, locate any offline backup (screenshot, PDF, or email), and ask the official for the specific alternative proof they accept.

## Do not do these things
- Do not delete and reinstall the app while in the queue, as this may trigger security lockouts or require two-factor authentication you cannot access.
- Do not assume you will be denied travel without first asking staff what alternative documentation is permitted.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to anyone else to troubleshoot; keep the device in your possession at all times.
- Do not keep attempting to refresh the app if you have no signal, as this will drain your battery and may cause the phone to overheat.
- Do not rely on airport or station Wi-Fi if the connection is unstable; switch to offline methods immediately.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a stationary location away from the flow of the queue.** This allows you to focus on the device without the pressure of the line moving behind you.
2. **Preserve your remaining battery and connectivity.**
   - Enable Low Power Mode or Battery Saver.
   - Plug into a portable power bank if available.
   - Toggle Airplane Mode on and then off to force the phone to reconnect to the strongest available mast.
3. **Check for existing offline digital copies on your device.**
   - Open your photo gallery or "Recents" folder and search for a **screenshot** of the QR code or certificate.
   - Open your "Files" or "Downloads" app and search for "NHS," "Vaccine," "Certificate," or "PDF."
   - Check **Apple Wallet** or **Google Wallet** for any previously saved health passes.
   - Search your email inbox for keywords like "vaccination," "test result," or "certificate" to find confirmation messages.
4. **Access the core health record if the specific "Pass" section is failing.**
   - If using the NHS App (England), navigate to the "Health" or "GP Record" section to view your vaccination history directly, rather than the certificate QR code.
   - Use the specific app for your home nation:
     - NHS Scotland COVID Status app.
     - NHS Wales (via the web browser if the app fails).
     - COVIDCert NI (Northern Ireland).
5. **Request an alternative verification from the desk official.** State clearly: "The app is not loading. I have my vaccination history/email/screenshot here; is this acceptable, or can you verify me via my booking reference?"
6. **Ask if the destination still requires the specific COVID Pass format.**
   - Inform the staff that the formal "NHS COVID Pass" service has been updated/withdrawn and ask if a standard GP vaccination record is sufficient.
   - Note if a printed International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP) is required for non-COVID vaccines (e.g., Yellow Fever).

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix the app's technical bugs or login errors right now.
- You do not need to update your phone's operating system while in the terminal.
- You do not need to contact your GP surgery or the NHS helpdesk while at the gate.

## Important reassurance
App failures and connectivity "dead zones" at international hubs are common. Staff at check-in desks and border control are usually trained to handle passengers whose digital documents fail to load and can often advise on the specific manual verification process they use.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance only for resolving an immediate document-access crisis at a checkpoint. It does not cover long-term technical support for health apps.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not guarantee boarding or entry. Requirements for health proof are set by individual carriers and sovereign governments and are subject to change without notice. Always follow the direct instructions of airline, rail, or border officials.
