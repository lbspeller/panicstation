# What to do if… your vaccination certificate or test result app will not load when you need to show proof (UK)

## Short answer
Step out of the line if you can, get to a calmer spot, and switch to an offline backup such as a screenshot, downloaded file, wallet pass, or printed copy. Ask staff what alternative proof they can accept right now.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t panic-delete and reinstall the app while you’re in the queue; you may lose offline data or trigger a login check.
- Don’t assume “no app” means “no travel” without asking what alternative proof is acceptable.
- Don’t hand your unlocked phone to a stranger. Keep the device in your hand and show only the screen needed.
- Don’t keep refreshing until your battery is low. Move quickly to a backup route.
- Don’t rely on mobile signal or public Wi-Fi as your only route when you are about to reach the desk or gate.
- Don’t argue about the rule at the desk. Ask what exact document is needed and what format they can accept.

## What to do now
1. **Create a small safety buffer.** Step aside if you can, plug into a charger or power bank, and turn on low-power mode. Keep your passport, ticket, booking reference, and any paper documents together.
2. **Try only the fastest phone fixes once.**
   - Turn airplane mode on, then off once.
   - Switch between mobile data and Wi-Fi once.
   - Force-close the app and reopen it once.
   - Restart the phone only if it is frozen and you know the passcode and any SIM PIN.
3. **Look for an offline copy you already have.**
   - Check your photos for a screenshot of the QR code, certificate, or test result.
   - Check downloads and email attachments for a saved certificate or test-result file.
   - Check Apple Wallet or Google Wallet for a health pass you saved earlier.
   - If you have a printed copy, put it with your passport and boarding pass.
4. **Ask the desk or gate staff one simple question.** Say: “My proof app will not load. What alternative proof can you accept right now: a printed copy, saved file, screenshot, wallet pass, or provider record?”
5. **If someone asks specifically for an NHS COVID Pass, clarify the request calmly.** The NHS COVID Pass service has closed and is no longer required for international travel, but a destination, operator, venue, or test provider may still ask for a different health document. Ask: “Which exact document do you need for this trip, and what formats can you accept?”
6. **If the proof is a test result, contact the test provider or check the original message.** Look for the booking email, lab email, text message, reference number, or provider portal link. Ask staff whether the provider email, a saved result, or the booking reference is enough for them to check.
7. **Avoid risky app changes while time is tight.** Do not update the app, reset your login, or change passwords in the queue unless you have stable internet, enough battery, and you know the login details.
8. **Use on-site help if the desk cannot decide.** Ask for the airline, rail operator, port, airport, station, venue, or customer-service desk that handles document checks. Give them your booking reference and ask what proof route they can accept.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix the app permanently right now.
- You do not need to reorganise all your travel documents in the queue.
- You do not need to decide whether the rule is fair or whether the app should have worked.
- You do not need to change passwords unless that is the only route staff say will solve the immediate proof problem.

## Important reassurance
This can happen because of poor signal, a forced login, a wallet sync problem, a dead battery, or an app fault. The immediate goal is not to make the app perfect; it is to show any proof the checker can accept.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the moment you are being asked to show proof. Later, you may need help from the travel operator, the test provider, NHS App support, NHS login support, or destination-specific travel guidance.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, travel, technical, or professional advice. Requirements can vary by destination, operator, venue, provider, and your circumstances. Follow the instructions of the operator, venue, test provider, or destination authority checking your documents.
