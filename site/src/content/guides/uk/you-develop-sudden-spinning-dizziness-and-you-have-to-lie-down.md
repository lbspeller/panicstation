# What to do if… you develop sudden spinning dizziness and you have to lie down (UK)

## Short answer
Lie flat in a dark, quiet room and check for emergency signs: if you have face drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech, sudden vision changes, sudden hearing loss, or a sudden severe headache, call **999** immediately. Do not attempt to walk unaided or drive.

## Do not do these things
- Do not try to walk without support as the risk of falling is high during a vertigo episode.
- Do not drive any vehicle or operate machinery.
- Do not move your head quickly or bend over, as this often worsens the spinning sensation.
- Do not take any medication that was not prescribed for you, including someone else's anti-sickness tablets.
- Do not enter a bath or shower alone while the room is spinning.
- Do not drink alcohol or use caffeine, as these can aggravate inner ear or neurological issues.

## What to do now
1. **Lie down immediately in a safe, dark, and quiet place.**  
   Minimize sensory input by closing your eyes or keeping them fixed on a single, non-moving object. Keep your head as still as possible to reduce the intensity of the spinning.
2. **Perform a self-check for emergency neurological symptoms.**  
   Check for:
   - Face: is one side of your face drooping or unable to smile?
   - Arms: can you raise both arms and keep them there?
   - Speech: is your speech slurred or garbled?
   - Other: do you have sudden double vision, sudden hearing loss, or a "thunderclap" (sudden, severe) headache?
3. **Call 999 if any emergency signs are present or if this is the first time you have had severe vertigo.**  
   Tell the operator you are experiencing "sudden onset spinning vertigo" and report any other symptoms you found during your check. Do not wait for symptoms to go away before calling.
4. **Contact NHS 111 or your GP for an urgent same-day appointment if you are vomiting or have a high temperature.**  
   If you do not have the 999 emergency signs but are unable to keep fluids down or feel very unwell with a fever or shivers, you require urgent medical assessment today.
5. **Alert someone nearby or call a friend or family member.**  
   Do not stay alone if possible; ask them to sit with you so they can monitor you and assist you if you need to move to a different room or use the toilet.
6. **Note the exact time your symptoms started and what you were doing.**  
   Record if the dizziness began when you moved your head, rolled over in bed, or stood up, as this information is vital for clinicians to determine the cause.
7. **Move only when absolutely necessary and do so very slowly.**  
   If you must get up, sit on the edge of the bed or sofa for several minutes before attempting to stand. Use walls or furniture for support and have someone walk with you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if this is an inner ear issue or something else right now; focus on stabilization and assessment.
- You do not need to try "repositioning manoeuvres" (like the Epley manoeuvre) at home during an acute first episode.
- You do not need to worry about long-term driving restrictions or work until the acute phase has been assessed by a professional.

## Important reassurance
Sudden spinning dizziness (vertigo) is often caused by temporary issues with the inner ear, such as an infection or displaced particles, which are treatable. While the sensation is overwhelming and can cause intense nausea, lying still is the most effective way to manage the immediate discomfort while waiting for medical advice.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an acute episode of vertigo and identifies emergency triggers. It does not replace a clinical diagnosis or a full neurological examination.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute a medical diagnosis. If you are in doubt about the severity of your symptoms, always seek professional medical help via 999 or 111.
