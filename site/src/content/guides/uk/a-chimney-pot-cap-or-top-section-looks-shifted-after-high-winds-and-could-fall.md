# What to do if… a chimney pot, cap, or top section looks shifted after high winds and could fall (UK)

## Short answer
Evacuate the area directly beneath and around the chimney, **do not go onto the roof**, and call 999 if debris is falling or the structure threatens a public path or road.

## Do not do these things
- Do not climb onto the roof, use ladders, or lean out of windows to inspect or stabilize the chimney.
- Do not stand directly below the chimney stack or under the eaves of the building to take photographs.
- Do not use any fire, stove, or boiler that vents through the affected chimney until it has been professionally certified as safe.
- Do not attempt to pull down loose components with ropes or poles.
- Do not assume a neighbour or local authority is aware of the risk if the chimney is on a shared stack or near a public highway.

## What to do now
1. **Establish a clear exclusion zone around the building.**  
   **Keep all persons and pets away from the side of the property where the chimney is located.** Debris can be carried by wind; maintain a distance from the building at least equal to the height of the chimney stack.
2. **Relocate vehicles only if it is safe to do so.**  
   **Move cars away from the fall zone only if you can do so without walking or driving directly under the unstable chimney.** If the vehicle is already directly underneath, leave it in place to avoid personal risk.
3. **Contact the Local Authority Building Control department immediately.**  
   **Report the "dangerous structure" to your local council, as they have emergency powers to make the site safe if there is a risk to the public.** Most UK councils provide a 24-hour emergency line for dangerous buildings.
4. **Identify immediate life-safety risks for emergency services.**  
   **Call 999 if the chimney is actively shedding masonry or if a large section (like a pot or the entire stack) appears imminently ready to fall onto a public pavement or road.**
5. **Isolate all appliances connected to the affected flue.**  
   **Turn off gas fires, wood burners, or boilers that utilize that chimney stack to prevent carbon monoxide (CO) buildup or fire.** Damage to the external stack often indicates internal liner failure which can lead to fumes leaking into the property.
6. **Notify the property owner or insurer.**  
   - **If you rent:** Contact your landlord or letting agent immediately to report an urgent structural safety issue.
   - **If you own:** Contact your buildings insurance emergency helpline to initiate a "make safe" visit by a professional contractor.
7. **Perform a safe internal check for structural cues.**  
   **Check internal chimney breasts on upper floors for new cracks, fallen soot, or brick dust in the hearth without moving the appliance.** If you smell combustion fumes or sulfur, evacuate to fresh air immediately.

## What can wait
- Arranging permanent masonry repairs or rebuilding the stack — the immediate priority is stabilization and removing the fall risk.
- Negotiating with neighbours regarding shared costs for a communal stack.
- Clearing away fallen mortar or small debris from the ground while the overhead risk is still active.
- Comparing multiple quotes for long-term aesthetic restoration.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel significant anxiety when a part of your home appears structurally unstable. Chimney stacks are high-exposure points during UK storms and are designed to be repaired or replaced by specialists. The primary objective is to maintain physical distance from the fall zone until a professional can secure the site.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and safety actions only. Long-term repairs to chimney stacks may be subject to the Party Wall etc. Act 1996 if the stack is shared, and will require a qualified roofer, chimney specialist, or structural engineer to certify the flue for future use.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute professional structural or legal advice. If you are in immediate danger or a public right-of-way is threatened, contact emergency services.
