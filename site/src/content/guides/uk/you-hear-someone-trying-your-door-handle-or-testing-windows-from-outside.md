# What to do if… you hear someone trying your door handle or testing windows from outside (UK)

## Short answer
Move everyone to a secure room, lock the door, and call 999 immediately if you believe a break-in is being attempted. Do not confront the person or open the door to investigate.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to "check" or shout through the wood.
- Do not go outside to confront the person or "scare them off."
- Do not stand in front of windows or doors where you can be seen from the outside.
- Do not turn on internal lights that would make you visible to someone in the dark.
- Do not assume it is a mistake if handles or windows are being tried systematically.
- Do not use a torch or phone light in a way that reveals your exact position.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe room immediately.** Gather all residents and pets into a single room, preferably one with a lockable door and a secondary exit if possible.
2. **Lock and barricade the door.** Engage the lock and, if it can be done silently and quickly, move a heavy piece of furniture (like a chest of drawers or chair) in front of the door.
3. **Silence all devices.** Switch your mobile phone to silent or vibrate to ensure you do not reveal your location, but keep the screen brightness low.
4. **Call 999 if the person is still attempting to gain entry.** Provide your full address and postcode first. State clearly that someone is attempting to enter your home **right now**.
5. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you cannot speak safely.** If you are on a mobile and cannot talk, wait for the automated prompt and **press 55**. This alerts the operator that your call is a genuine emergency and connects you to the police.
   - If on a landline, stay on the line; if the operator hears background noise or a disturbance, they will evaluate the call for police dispatch.
6. **Stay low and away from the door.** Position yourself on the floor, ideally behind a bed or heavy furniture, out of the direct line of sight from the door or any windows.
7. **Listen for movement.** Try to determine if the person is moving to a different part of the house (e.g., from the front door to a side window) so you can update the emergency operator.
8. **Verify any "police" presence.** If someone knocks and claims to be the police, do not open the door. Ask them for their warrant number through the closed door and tell them you are verifying this with 999. A genuine officer will wait.

## What can wait
- You do not need to check if other doors or windows in the house are locked once you are in your safe room.
- You do not need to review CCTV or doorbell footage while the person is still outside.
- You do not need to contact your landlord, neighbors, or insurance company until the police have arrived or the situation is resolved.
- You do not need to identify the person or provide a detailed description until you are in a secure environment.

## Important reassurance
It is normal for your heart to race or for your hands to shake. This is your body's natural response to a threat. By staying behind a locked door and calling for help, you are taking the most effective actions to ensure your safety. Most opportunistic intruders will leave if they cannot gain entry quickly and quietly.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization and immediate safety during a potential intrusion. It does not cover long-term home security upgrades or legal procedures following an incident.

## Important note
This information is for emergency guidance only. If you are in immediate danger, always prioritize your physical safety and contact the emergency services via 999.
