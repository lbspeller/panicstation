# What to do if… someone offers you a job, audition, or “photoshoot” and then introduces nudity or sexual expectations (UK)

## Short answer
End the interaction immediately, move to a public space or safe location, and do not provide any further images or information. If you are in immediate danger or have been physically or sexually assaulted, call **999**.

## Do not do these things
- Don't stay to "talk it through" or negotiate if you feel unsafe or the terms have changed to include nudity / sexual acts.
- Don't agree to a follow-up meeting in a private location.
- Don't send "test" nude or sexual images to prove your professional suitability.
- Don't pay any money for "portfolio fees" or "admin" if sexual demands have been made.
- Don't delete existing communications yet, as they may be needed for your safety or reporting.
- Don't blame yourself for "not seeing it coming" or for freezing; these are normal reactions to professional manipulation.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritize your physical exit from the situation.**  
   **Leave the room or building immediately without providing a detailed explanation.** If you are at a remote location, move toward a public area or call a taxi / friend to collect you. If you are being prevented from leaving, call **999**.

2. **Establish a complete communication block.**  
   **Stop responding to all messages, calls, or emails from the individual or "agency" involved.** Do not argue with them or try to convince them their behavior is wrong; simply stop the flow of information.

3. **Access 24 / 7 confidential specialist support.**  
   **Contact a dedicated service to talk through what happened and assess your next steps.**
   - **England & Wales (16+)**: Call the Rape Crisis England & Wales 24 / 7 support line on **0808 500 2222**.
   - **Scotland**: For 24 / 7 NHS medical / forensic self-referral, call **0800 148 88 88**. For emotional support (5pm–midnight), call Rape Crisis Scotland on **08088 01 03 02**.
   - **Northern Ireland**: Call the 24-hour Domestic & Sexual Abuse Helpline on **0808 802 1414**.
   - **Under 19 (UK-wide)**: Call **Childline** on **0800 1111**.

4. **Seek medical care or forensic options if you were touched or forced.**  
   **Visit a Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC) for specialist medical care and the option of a "self-referral" forensic exam.** You can access a SARC without involving the police. They can provide emergency contraception, STI testing, and store forensic evidence if you are undecided about reporting.

5. **Report sextortion or blackmail if images are being used as a threat.**  
   **Do not pay money or send more images if they threaten to "expose" you.**
   - In England, Wales, or NI: Use the **Report Fraud** service at **reportfraud.police.uk** or call **0300 123 2040**.
   - In Scotland: Call **101** to report cybercrime / extortion to Police Scotland.
   - For image removal: Use **StopNCII.org** (18+) or the **Report Remove** tool via Childline / IWF (under 18) to help take down shared content.

6. **Secure your digital evidence without engaging the harasser.**  
   **Take screenshots of the job advert, messages, and any profiles involved, then save them to a secure folder.** Once captured, you can silence or block the accounts. Do not delete the evidence until you have spoken with a support service or the police.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide if you want to make a formal police report today.
- You do not need to notify your professional network or agent immediately.
- You do not need to provide a statement or "side of the story" to the person who pressured you.
- You do not need to know the legal definition of what happened before seeking help.

## Important reassurance
It is common to feel a sense of professional obligation or to "freeze" when someone in a position of power introduces sexual demands. This is a tactic used by predators to exploit your career ambitions. Your safety and consent are more important than any job opportunity, and you have the absolute right to withdraw from any agreement that makes you feel unsafe.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction actions for the immediate aftermath of professional sexual coercion or "casting couch" scams. It does not cover long-term legal claims or industry-specific blacklisting protections.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and is not a substitute for professional legal or medical advice. Procedures and contact numbers are verified for the UK as of March 2026. If you are in immediate danger, always call **999**.
