# What to do if… you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without your consent (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause, do not confront the person while you are overwhelmed, and contact a specialist UK support service for intimate image abuse. You do not need to be certain before asking for help.

## Do not do these things
- Do not confront the suspected person while you are panicked or alone.
- Try not to pay, negotiate, or keep replying to threats.
- Do not send more images to prove anything or to calm someone down.
- Do not forward, repost, or send intimate content to friends for advice.
- Do not keep searching or re-watching content if it is making you spiral.
- If someone is threatening to hurt you, find you, or pressure you in person, do not handle it alone; call 999.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.** Move away from the person if they are nearby, go somewhere with other people if that feels safer, or call someone you trust to stay with you by phone or in person.
2. **If you are an adult in the UK, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline.** Tell them you think you were filmed or photographed in a sexual situation without consenting and you need help with next steps, reporting routes, or removal options.
3. **If you are being blackmailed or threatened, stop engaging for now.** Do not pay or send more images. If you feel at immediate risk, call 999; otherwise you can contact police on 101 when you are ready.
4. **If you have the image or video file and you are 18 or over, consider StopNCII.org.** It can create a digital fingerprint of the file on your device so participating platforms can help detect and remove matching uploads.
5. **If anything is already online, report it where it appears.** Use the platform’s non-consensual intimate image or intimate image abuse reporting option. If you are not sure where to report it, use Report Harmful Content to find the right route.
6. **If it is easy and safe, keep basic records without spreading the content.** Save threat messages, account names, profile links, URLs, and dates, but do not forward the intimate image or video to anyone.
7. **If you are under 18, or the image was made when you were under 18, use child-safety routes.** Do not send the image or video around. Use Childline’s Report Remove page and, if there is online sexual abuse or worrying online communication, make a report to CEOP or ask a trusted adult to help you do it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to report to police.
- You do not need to prove exactly what happened before asking for support.
- You do not need to contact the person who may have recorded or photographed you.
- You do not need to collect every possible piece of evidence before getting help.
- You do not need to decide what this means for your relationship, work, school, or future right now.

## Important reassurance
Feeling shocked, sick, frozen, angry, or unable to stop checking can be a normal response to a serious boundary violation. You are allowed to slow down, get support, and choose your next steps without being rushed or blamed.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce immediate harm. Later decisions about legal options, police reporting, school or workplace safeguarding, or longer-term support may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe or someone is threatening immediate harm, prioritise getting to safety and call 999.
