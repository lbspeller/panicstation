# What to do if… you develop sudden back pain with new leg numbness or weakness (UK)

## Short answer
Sudden back pain with new leg numbness or weakness needs urgent medical advice today. Call 999 or go to A&E now if it affects both legs, is severe or getting worse, follows a serious accident, stops you standing or walking safely, or comes with bladder, bowel, or groin-area numbness.

## Do not do these things
- Do not wait until tomorrow if numbness or weakness is new, severe, or getting worse.
- Do not drive yourself if your leg feels weak, unreliable, or numb.
- Do not try to prove it is okay by heavy stretching, lifting, gym work, or walking until your leg gives way.
- Do not book massage or spinal manipulation before urgent medical assessment when weakness or numbness is new.
- Do not use alcohol or sedating drugs to get through it; they can make falls and symptom assessment harder.
- Do not hide bladder, bowel, genital, buttock, or inner-thigh numbness because it feels embarrassing.

## What to do now
1. Check for emergency red flags now. Call 999 or go to A&E now if you have back pain with any of these:
   - pain, tingling, weakness, or numbness in both legs;
   - weakness or numbness in both legs that is severe or getting worse;
   - numbness or tingling around your genitals, anus, buttocks, or inner thighs;
   - new trouble starting to pee, being unable to pee, not feeling your bladder properly, or loss of bladder control;
   - not noticing when you need to poo, loss of bowel control, or new loss of feeling when passing a bowel motion;
   - you cannot safely stand or walk, or your leg keeps giving way;
   - it started after a serious accident, such as a car crash or a significant fall.
2. If you have new leg numbness or weakness but none of the emergency red flags, arrange same-day urgent assessment.
   - Contact your GP surgery if it is open and ask for urgent same-day advice.
   - Use NHS 111 where available, or your local urgent out-of-hours service if your GP is closed.
   - Say: “I have sudden back pain with new leg numbness or weakness,” and say whether it is spreading or getting worse.
3. Make the next hour safer while help is arranged.
   - Sit or lie in the most comfortable safe position.
   - Ask someone to stay nearby if your leg is weak, numb, or unreliable.
   - Avoid stairs, carrying children, lifting, driving, and anything that could make you fall.
4. Write a short symptom snapshot to read out or take with you.
   - Time and date it started.
   - Which leg is affected, or whether both legs are affected.
   - Whether numbness, tingling, or weakness is spreading or worsening.
   - Any bladder, bowel, genital, buttock, or inner-thigh numbness or change.
   - Any recent serious fall, crash, direct back injury, fever, or feeling very unwell.
5. If you are going to A&E, use safer transport.
   - Do not drive yourself.
   - Ask someone else to take you if you can move safely.
   - Call 999 if you cannot move safely, your leg is giving way, symptoms are affecting both legs, or you have bladder, bowel, or groin-area symptoms.
   - Take your medicines list or medicine packets if this is easy and does not delay urgent help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether this is sciatica, a slipped disc, a trapped nerve, or something else right now.
- You do not need to search for exercises, stretches, or self-tests.
- You do not need to decide about physiotherapy, private scans, work, travel, or long-term treatment today.
- You do not need to finish a shift, journey, workout, or household task before getting urgent advice.

## Important reassurance
Most back pain is not dangerous, and many leg symptoms come from irritated nerves that can settle. The reason to act promptly is that new numbness or weakness, especially with bladder, bowel, groin-area, or both-leg symptoms, can sometimes signal a problem where timing matters.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to help you reduce risk and get urgent assessment. Later decisions may need support from a clinician or another appropriate specialist after you have been checked.

## Important note
This is general information, not medical, legal, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. It cannot diagnose the cause of your symptoms; if symptoms are severe, worsening, or you are unsure, seek urgent medical help.
