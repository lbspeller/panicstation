# What to do if… you receive a notice that your case has been referred to a prosecutor and you are asked to respond (UK)

## Short answer
Seek legal advice from a criminal defense solicitor before providing any information or statement. Acknowledge the deadline and the authority involved, but do not provide a version of events until your solicitor has reviewed the request.

## Do not do these things
- Don't provide a written or verbal "explanation" of the events to the investigator or prosecutor without legal counsel.
- Don't ignore the notice or any stated deadlines for a response.
- Don't contact the person who made the allegation or any potential witnesses to discuss the notice.
- Don't post about the referral or the case details on social media or messaging platforms.
- Don't delete, alter, or hide any physical or digital evidence, even if you believe it is irrelevant.
- Don't assume that a referral to a prosecutor means you have already been charged or found guilty.

## What to do now
1. **Identify which specific prosecution authority sent the notice and the jurisdiction it falls under.** The legal procedures vary significantly between England and Wales (Crown Prosecution Service / CPS), Scotland (Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service / COPFS), and Northern Ireland (Public Prosecution Service / PPS). Check the letterhead or digital footer to confirm the agency and the specific police force that referred the matter.

2. **Check if the notice is a mandatory statutory requirement or a voluntary request for a response.** Certain notices, such as a Section 172 notice in road traffic matters, require the recipient to provide specific information (like the driver's identity) by law, whereas a general "invitation to respond" to an allegation is usually voluntary. Your solicitor will need to know which type of notice you have received to determine if silence carries a legal penalty.

3. **Secure a criminal defense solicitor immediately to handle all communications with the prosecutor.** If you do not have a solicitor, you can use the Law Society’s "Find a Solicitor" tool or check your eligibility for legal aid through the Legal Aid Agency (England/Wales), SLAB (Scotland), or LSA (Northern Ireland). Even if you are not in custody, you are entitled to professional advice before responding to a request for a statement or interview.

4. **Verify the authenticity of the notice using independent contact details.** Do not use the phone numbers or links provided within the notice itself if you have any doubt about its origin. Call the official switchboard of the named prosecution service or the relevant police station to confirm that the case reference number and the request are genuine.

5. **Provide a "holding response" only if a deadline is imminent and your solicitor is not yet instructed.** A holding response should strictly confirm receipt of the notice, state that you are in the process of seeking legal advice, and provide the solicitor’s contact details once known. Do not include any details about the incident, your defense, or your personal opinion on the case.

6. **Create an inventory of all evidence and a private timeline of events for your solicitor's eyes only.** Write down everything you remember about the incident, including dates, times, and potential witnesses, but do not share this document with anyone except your legal representative. Gather and preserve relevant emails, text messages, receipts, and photos in their original format.

7. **Clarify the format of the response requested by the prosecutor.** Note whether they are asking for a written statement, a response to specific questions, or for you to attend a voluntary interview under caution. Ensure your solicitor is aware of these specific requirements before they contact the prosecutor on your behalf.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to plead guilty or not guilty at this moment.
- You do not need to gather "character references" or prepare for a court hearing today.
- You do not need to explain the situation to your employer or family until you have legal guidance on your reporting obligations.
- You do not need to find "counter-evidence" to disprove the allegation tonight; focus on preserving what currently exists.

## Important reassurance
Receiving a notice of referral means the investigation has moved to a review stage, but it is not a conviction. Prosecutors regularly review cases and decide not to proceed if the "test for prosecution" regarding evidence or public interest is not met. Taking time to get legal advice is a standard procedural step and is not viewed by the system as a sign of guilt.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for receiving a referral notice only. It does not cover court procedures, sentencing, or specific legal defenses, which must be handled by a qualified legal professional.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal advice. Laws regarding police cautions, "statements under caution," and prosecution reviews differ between England & Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. Always consult a qualified solicitor before responding to official legal notices.
