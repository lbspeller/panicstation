# What to do if… you hear buzzing or crackling from your electrical panel or fuse box (UK)

## Short answer
Treat buzzing or crackling from a consumer unit or fuse box as an active electrical fault (arcing). If it is safe to do so, immediately switch off the main switch on the unit and contact a registered electrician; if there is smoke, fire, or a strong smell of burning plastic, evacuate the property and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the consumer unit cover or attempt to inspect the internal wiring yourself.
- Do not repeatedly reset breakers or fuses if they trip while the noise is present.
- Do not touch the box, switches, or surrounding wall if you see smoke, sparks, or discolouration.
- Do not use water on any suspected electrical fire.
- Do not ignore the sound even if the electricity appears to be working normally.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate danger signs such as smoke, flames, or a strong smell of burning plastic.** If any of these are present, evacuate everyone from the property immediately.
2. **Call 999 once you are at a safe distance from the building.** Tell the operator you have a suspected electrical fire or a high-risk fault in your consumer unit.
3. **If there is no smoke or fire and you can reach the unit safely, turn off the electricity at the main switch.** This is usually a large red or black switch labeled "Main Switch" on the consumer unit.
4. **Move away from the consumer unit once the power is isolated.** Arcing (electricity jumping between connections) can cause components to fail or eject heat; do not stand directly in front of the unit longer than necessary.
5. **Contact a qualified, registered electrician to report the buzzing or crackling.** Inform them that you have isolated the power and suspect arcing or a loose connection within the unit.
6. **Report the issue to your District Network Operator (DNO) if you suspect the noise is coming from the service head or meter.**
   - In Great Britain, call 105.
   - In Northern Ireland, call 03457 643 643 (NIE Networks).
7. **Keep the power switched off until a professional has inspected the installation.** Do not attempt to restore power yourself until the electrician confirms the unit is safe to energise.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the specific cause of the noise (such as a loose terminal or a failing breaker) right now.
- You do not need to decide on a full consumer unit replacement or upgrade today.
- You do not need to check other appliances or sockets once the main power has been isolated.

## Important reassurance
Hearing unusual noises from electrical equipment is a clear indicator that something requires attention, and acting immediately is the standard safety protocol. These sounds are often caused by loose connections or arcing which, while serious, are identifiable and repairable by a professional once the power is off.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and harm-reduction steps only. It does not replace the requirement for a physical inspection by a registered electrician or a network operator.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization and does not constitute professional electrical advice. If you are unsure of your safety or the condition of the equipment, isolate the area and contact emergency services.
