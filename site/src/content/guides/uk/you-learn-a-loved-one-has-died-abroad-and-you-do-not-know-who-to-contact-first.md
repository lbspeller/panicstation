# What to do if… you learn a loved one has died abroad and you do not know who to contact first (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) in London immediately on 020 7008 5000 to verify the report and open a consular case.

## Do not do these things
- Do not send money, bank details, or passport scans to any person or agency claiming to represent a hospital, police force, or funeral home until the FCDO verifies the contact.
- Do not sign any contracts or agreements for repatriation or local burial offered by third parties (tour operators, hotels, or local fixers) in the immediate hours following the news.
- Do not rely on information provided solely through social media messaging or unofficial emails.
- Do not publicly post the name of the deceased or the location on social media while identity and next-of-kin status are still being verified.
- Do not pay for "urgent" local services via cryptocurrency, wire transfer (e.g., Western Union), or gift cards.

## What to do now
1. **Record all available details of the notification immediately.**
   - Full name and date of birth of the person concerned.
   - The location where they were staying and where the death is alleged to have occurred.
   - The name, phone number, and organization of the person who contacted you.
   - The specific time you received the message or call.

2. **Call the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) 24/7 consular line.**
   - In the UK: Call 020 7008 5000.
   - From abroad: Call +44 20 7008 5000.
   - Select the option for "Consular services for British nationals."
   - State clearly that you have received an unconfirmed report of a death abroad and require assistance with verification.

3. **Request a specific caseworker and a case reference number.**
   - **Write down the reference number provided by the FCDO staff.**
   - Request the name and direct contact details of the consular officer assigned to your case.

4. **Verify the identity and location of the deceased through official channels.**
   - **Ask the consular staff to contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate in the relevant country.**
   - Request that they confirm the report with local police or hospital authorities.
   - Provide the deceased's passport number if you have it available.

5. **Locate the travel insurance policy for the deceased.**
   - **Check bank statements or emails for evidence of travel insurance, as most policies include 24-hour emergency assistance for death abroad.**
   - Inform the insurance company immediately once the FCDO has opened a case, as they often take over the management of local funeral directors and repatriation.

6. **Implement a "call-back" rule for all local contacts.**
   - **Tell any local authorities, tour operators, or medical staff that you will call them back through the official embassy switchboard.**
   - Do not accept incoming calls as proof of identity; always hang up and dial the official, verified number for the British consulate in that country.

7. **Determine if the death occurred during a package holiday.**
   - **Contact the tour operator’s emergency welfare team if the trip was booked as a package.**
   - Ask them to coordinate with the British consulate to ensure local procedures are followed.

## What can wait
- Deciding between local burial, local cremation, or repatriation (bringing the body back to the UK).
- Appointing a UK-based funeral director.
- Informing the deceased's bank, employer, or utility providers.
- Applying for a UK death certificate (the local death certificate is usually the primary document).
- Finalizing the guest list for a memorial service.

## Important reassurance
Receiving news of a death abroad is a highly disruptive and confusing experience. It is common to feel a sense of urgency to take action, but the priority is establishing a single, verified line of communication. Hesitation in the first few hours is a protective measure against fraud and administrative errors.

## Scope note
This guide covers the immediate 24-hour period following a notification of death abroad and focuses on verification and initial contact with UK authorities.

## Important note
Consular staff can provide practical guidance and lists of local service providers but cannot pay for repatriation, funeral costs, or legal fees. Processes for registering a death and investigating circumstances vary significantly by jurisdiction.
