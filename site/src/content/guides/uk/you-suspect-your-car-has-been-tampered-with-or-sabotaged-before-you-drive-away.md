# What to do if… you suspect your car has been tampered with or sabotaged before you drive away (UK)

## Short answer
Do not drive the car. Move yourself to a safer place first, then use 999 if there is immediate danger or a crime may be happening now; otherwise use 101 or online police reporting and arrange recovery or a professional inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not test the car by driving a short distance.
- Do not confront anyone you suspect, follow them, or go looking for them.
- Do not touch, remove, or fix anything that looks altered, because you could injure yourself or disturb evidence.
- Do not lean under the car or put your hands into gaps where you cannot see clearly.
- Do not clean the car or the area around it yet.
- Do not post your location or detailed suspicions on social media while you are still there.

## What to do now
1. **Make yourself safer first.**  
   If you are already in the car, lock the doors, keep the windows up, and keep your phone ready. If you are outside, step back and move to a well-lit public place nearby, such as a shop entrance, reception area, petrol station forecourt, or busy staffed area.

2. **If there is an unfamiliar object or device on, under, or next to the car, move away and call 999.**  
   Do not approach it, touch it, photograph it close up, or try to work out what it is yourself. Call from a safer distance.

3. **Choose the police route based on immediate risk.**  
   Call **999** if you think you are in immediate danger, the crime may be happening now, someone is nearby watching or threatening you, or you feel unsafe staying there. If there is no immediate danger, report the suspected tampering or criminal damage through **101** or your local police force’s online reporting route.

4. **Do a quick no-touch visual check only if it is safe to do so.**  
   From a safe position, look for obvious signs such as:
   - a suddenly flat tyre, deep cuts, bulges, or something lodged in a tyre
   - fresh fluid, an unusual puddle, or a strong fuel or chemical smell
   - anything hanging down underneath the car
   - a wheel that looks loose, misaligned, or different from the others
   - signs of forced entry, disturbed wiring, or anything clearly out of place inside the car

   If anything looks wrong, treat the vehicle as unsafe to drive until it has been checked.

5. **Record what you can without handling the car.**  
   Take photos or video from a safe position of all four sides of the car, the tyres, the ground around it, and any suspicious area. Include wider shots showing the parking bay, street sign, nearby landmarks, and any visible CCTV cameras. Note the time, exact location, and when you last drove the car without noticing a problem.

6. **Tell the site owner or security team if you are in a managed place.**  
   If the car is in a car park, workplace, hotel, supermarket, petrol station, block of flats, or other managed site, ask staff or security to preserve CCTV for the relevant time window and to record that you reported a possible vehicle tampering incident. Do not argue about viewing footage on the spot.

7. **Arrange recovery and inspection instead of driving it to a garage.**  
   Call your breakdown provider and ask for recovery to a garage for a safety check, especially of the tyres, wheels, steering, brakes, underbody, locks, and any visible wiring. If you do not have breakdown cover, call a reputable recovery service or your insurer for guidance.

8. **If you need to leave the area, leave the car and take essentials only.**  
   Take your phone, keys, wallet, medication, and anything you need for the next few hours. Avoid disturbing the suspicious area of the car.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out who did it right now.
- You do not need to confront anyone or send accusations.
- You do not need to decide today whether to make an insurance claim, complaint, or formal statement beyond any immediate police report.
- You do not need to do a detailed mechanical inspection yourself.
- You do not need to move the car yourself if it may be unsafe.

## Important reassurance
Stopping and not driving is a sensible response. Even if this turns out to be a normal fault, minor damage, or a misunderstanding, you have avoided the main immediate risk: driving a vehicle that may not be safe.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: get yourself safe, avoid disturbing anything, report urgent risk through the right route, and get the car checked. Later decisions about repairs, insurance, police follow-up, or ongoing safety may need help from a qualified mechanic, police, insurer, or another specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call **999**. If you think the car may be unsafe, do not drive it; use recovery and a professional inspection.
