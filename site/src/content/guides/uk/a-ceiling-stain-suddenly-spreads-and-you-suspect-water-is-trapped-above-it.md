# What to do if… a ceiling stain suddenly spreads and you suspect water is trapped above it (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the electricity for the affected area at the consumer unit (fuse box) immediately, then shut off the water supply using the internal stopcock and evacuate the room beneath the stain.

## Do not do these things
- Don't touch light switches, pull-cords, or any electrical appliances in the room if the ceiling or walls are damp.
- Don't stand directly underneath a sagging patch or a "bubble" in the plasterboard.
- Don't try to move heavy furniture while water is actively dripping; prioritize your physical safety.
- Don't assume the leak has stopped if the dripping slows down; water may be pooling elsewhere in the ceiling void.
- Don't use a ladder to inspect the ceiling while the floor is wet or the structure is unstable.

## What to do now
1. **Evacuate the immediate area and keep all people and pets out of the room.** Water-soaked plasterboard can lose structural integrity and collapse without warning; ensure no one is standing beneath the spreading stain or any visible bulge.
2. **Turn off the electrical circuits for the affected room at the consumer unit (fuse box).** **If the water is near a light fitting, smoke alarm, or extractor fan, you must switch off the power to that circuit immediately.** If you are unsure which breaker controls the room, use the main "On / Off" switch to isolate the entire property.
3. **Locate your internal stopcock (stop tap) and turn it fully clockwise to shut off the water.** **This is usually found under the kitchen sink, in a downstairs toilet, or under the stairs.** Closing this valve stops the flow of water to your fixtures and may prevent the leak from worsening if the source is within your own pipework.
4. **Identify the source of the water if you can do so safely.** **Check the room directly above the stain for overflows, leaking radiator valves, or washing machine failures.** If you live in a flat and the source is likely above you, alert your neighbour immediately and ask them to stop using water.
5. **Report the emergency to your landlord or property manager if you rent.** **Under 2026 UK standards, an active water leak causing a ceiling hazard is classified as an emergency repair that typically requires a response within 24 hours.** Use the emergency out-of-hours number provided in your tenancy agreement.
6. **Place a bucket or container beneath the leak to catch drips and protect the flooring.** **If the ceiling is bulging significantly and water is trapped, you may carefully use a small tool to poke a single, small hole in the center of the bulge to drain the water into a bucket.** Only do this if you are not standing directly underneath it and the electricity is confirmed off; this can prevent a sudden, larger collapse.
7. **Contact an emergency plumber or your water company if the leak is on the supply pipe.** **If you cannot find the stopcock or it is seized, call an emergency trade professional immediately.** If you suspect the leak is on the external pipework leading into your property, contact your regional water supplier (e.g., Thames Water, United Utilities).
8. **Document the damage with photos and videos only once the area is stabilized.** **Take clear images of the stain, any bulges, and any damaged belongings for insurance or landlord records.** Do not begin cleaning or drying until a professional has confirmed the ceiling is structurally safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to attempt to clean the stain or repaint the ceiling today; the priority is moisture removal and structural safety.
- You don't need to decide on a long-term repair contractor until the leak source is fully identified and stopped.
- You don't need to worry about mould growth in the first few hours; the immediate focus is stopping the liquid ingress.

## Important reassurance
Ceiling leaks are high-stress events, but taking immediate action to cut the power and water significantly reduces the risk of fire or total ceiling failure. Once the water is off, the immediate danger is stabilized.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for internal water ingress and ceiling hazards. It does not cover long-term structural repairs, insurance negotiation, or professional electrical testing required after water exposure.

## Important note
This information is for emergency stabilization only. If you see smoke, hear loud cracking sounds from the ceiling, or see water entering the consumer unit (fuse box), exit the property immediately and call 999. Always use a qualified electrician to inspect circuits that have been exposed to water before turning the power back on.
