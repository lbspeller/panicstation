# What to do if… the name on your ticket does not match your passport and check-in is coming up (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the ticket issuer immediately (the airline or travel agent) to request a "name correction" to match your passport exactly. Do not attempt to travel or check in until the name on the ticketed record is corrected, as modern digital border systems (ETA / EES) will flag the mismatch.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to check in online if you know there is a significant mismatch; once a boarding pass is generated, it is often harder for agents to "unlock" and edit the record.
- Do not assume that updating your Advance Passenger Information (API) or Secure Flight data online fixes the name on the ticket itself.
- Do not buy a new ticket before speaking to the original issuer; most major UK carriers have specific policies for "corrections" (typos) versus "changes" (swapping people).
- Do not use a secondary ID (like a driving licence) that matches the typo if that document is not valid for international entry at your destination.
- Do not wait until you are at the airport; gate agents often lack the authority to reissue tickets for complex partner/codeshare bookings.

## What to do now
1. **Verify the exact nature of the error by comparing your passport's machine-readable zone to your booking confirmation.** Identify if the error is a minor typo (usually defined as 3 characters or fewer), a missing middle name, a maiden/married name discrepancy, or reversed first and last names.
2. **Identify the party responsible for the ticket issuance to determine who must fix it.**
   - If you booked through a third-party travel agent or website (e.g., Expedia, Skyscanner, local agent), you must contact them first, as they "own" the booking until travel begins.
   - If you booked directly with the airline, contact their customer service or "Manage Booking" support line.
3. **Request a "Name Correction" specifically, emphasizing that the passenger is the same person as the passport holder.** Use the term "correction" rather than "change" to avoid being told the ticket is non-transferable.
4. **Gather legal evidence immediately if the mismatch is due to a change in marital status or a legal name change.**
   - Have a digital copy of your marriage certificate, civil partnership certificate, or deed poll ready to email to the airline's support desk.
5. **Confirm that the correction will be "pushed" to all digital border and security systems.** Ask the agent specifically:
   - "Will this correction update the Secure Flight Passenger Data (SFPD) for US flights?"
   - "Will this name match my Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) or the EU Entry/Exit System (EES) record?"
   - "Does the ticket need to be revalidated or reissued to show the new name?"
6. **Escalate the contact method if phone lines are busy or the first agent is unhelpful.** Switch to official social media direct messages or "Live Chat" functions, as these often have shorter queues and provide a written transcript of the advice given.
7. **Request a written confirmation or an updated "e-ticket receipt" once the correction is processed.** Do not rely on verbal timing; ensure the name change is visible in the airline's official app or "Manage Booking" portal before you attempt check-in.

## What can wait
- You do not need to resolve the underlying cause of the error (e.g., an agent's mistake) before you travel; prioritize the fix first and the complaint later.
- You do not need to worry about frequent flyer points or seat assignments during the correction; these can usually be re-linked at the airport once the name is correct.
- You do not need to apply for a new passport if the current one is valid; it is always faster and cheaper to change the ticket to match the passport.

## Important reassurance
Name typos and surname mismatches are one of the most common issues handled by airline helpdesks. Most major carriers (including British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and EasyJet) have established "minor name correction" policies designed to resolve these situations without requiring you to purchase a full-price new ticket.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for name mismatches on flight bookings. It does not cover situations where you wish to transfer a ticket to a different person, which is generally prohibited by most non-flexible airline fare rules.

## Important note
Airlines have different thresholds for what they consider a "minor" typo. While many allow up to 3 characters to be corrected, some low-cost carriers or international partners may be stricter. Always obtain a confirmation email or reference number for any correction made.
