# What to do if… someone you just met is pressuring you for your address or a ride home (UK)

## Short answer
Move towards staff, security, or a busy public place, and leave using transport you control. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not give your real address to make the pressure stop.
- Do not get into their car, share a taxi with them, or let them walk you home.
- Do not go somewhere quieter or more private to explain yourself.
- Do not keep debating once you have said no.
- Do not let them see your phone screen while you book travel, message someone, or share your location.
- Do not go straight home if you think they may follow you.

## What to do now
1. **Move to witnesses.** Go to a staffed, busy area such as a bar, reception desk, security point, shop counter, hotel lobby, or staffed transport area. Stay near people, not near a side exit, car park, or quiet street.
2. **Use one clear sentence.** Say: “I’m not sharing my address. I’m leaving now.” Then stop explaining and move away.
3. **Tell staff exactly what is happening.** Say: “This person will not stop pressuring me for my address or a ride home. I need help leaving safely.” Ask staff to keep them away from you and to watch while you leave.
4. **Use Ask for Angela if the venue has it.** At participating venues, asking for “Angela” can signal that you need help. If you are unsure, say directly: “I do not feel safe. Can you help me leave?”
5. **Leave by transport you control.** Ask staff to call a licensed taxi or private hire vehicle, help you wait inside, or walk you to a staffed pickup point. If using an app, wait inside until it arrives and check the number plate and driver details before getting in.
6. **Put someone safe on the phone.** Call or message a trusted person: “Stay on the phone with me while I leave. I’m at [place].” Keep the call going until you are safely away.
7. **If they follow you, do not go home.** Go to a staffed, busier place such as a shop, hotel, venue, or transport hub. Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.
8. **If it is not an emergency but you still feel unsafe, call 101.** You can ask for police advice, especially if they followed you, threatened you, blocked you, kept contacting you, or you already shared your address.
9. **Once you are safe, save basic details.** Note the time, place, what happened, any name or profile, what they looked like, and any vehicle details. Save messages or call logs if you have them.
10. **If you already shared your address, treat it as exposed for now.** Do not go there alone tonight if you feel at risk. Go to a trusted person, a staffed safe place, or somewhere else you can stay while you get advice.

## What can wait
- Deciding what to call what happened.
- Deciding whether to make a formal report.
- Writing a perfect account.
- Replying to them, explaining yourself, or smoothing it over.
- Any confrontation, closure conversation, or follow-up meeting.

## Important reassurance
Freezing, fawning, or trying to stay polite can happen under pressure. A person who will not accept a simple no is giving you a real safety signal. You do not owe them your address, a lift, a walk home, or more conversation.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next minutes and hours only. If this turns into repeated contact, threats, harassment, stalking, or being followed, later decisions may need specialist support or police advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999. If it is not an emergency but you feel unsafe or harassed, call 101 or use an official police reporting route.
