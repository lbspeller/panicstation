# What to do if… you notice someone waiting near your car and watching you approach (UK)

## Short answer
Stop approaching your car. Turn back to a busier, well-lit place and ask staff, security, or another adult to help you leave safely. Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t walk past them to “just get it over with” or squeeze between parked cars to reach your door.
- Don’t confront, argue, or approach to film them up close. If you want to note details, do it from a safer place.
- Don’t unlock your car while distracted by bags, your phone, headphones, or searching for keys.
- Don’t get into your car while someone is close enough to rush the door.
- Don’t accept a stranger’s offer to “help you find your car” or “check something” on your vehicle.
- Don’t drive off if you feel too shaken to do it safely. Get to a safer place first.

## What to do now
1. **Create distance immediately.** Change direction and go back into the nearest staffed place, such as a shop, café, station, or reception. If you are outdoors, move towards light, people, and cameras.
2. **Ask for an escort, not advice.** Tell staff: “Someone is waiting by my car and watching me. Can someone walk with me to my car, or call security?” If there is car park security, ask them to meet you inside and walk you out.
3. **If you feel threatened right now, call 999.** Give your location, such as the car park name, level, nearest entrance, or nearby shop, and say what is happening. Give a short description if you can, such as clothing, height, direction of travel, or vehicle.
   - If you cannot safely speak, still call 999. If you are calling from a mobile, you may be prompted to press 55 or tap to confirm it is a genuine emergency. If you are calling from a landline, stay on the line if you can.
4. **Delay reaching your car until you have support.** Wait inside with staff or security until the person leaves, you have an escort, or police or security advise what to do next.
5. **When you do approach the car with an escort:**
   - Scan around and inside the car from a short distance.
   - Have your key ready so you are not searching in a bag at the door.
   - If anything feels off, such as someone moving closer, a door or boot being ajar, or other people loitering, turn back immediately.
6. **If you get into the car:** get in, lock the doors immediately, start the engine, and leave if you can do so safely.
   - If someone approaches the door or window, do not open it.
   - If you cannot safely drive away because you are blocked in, stay locked in, use your horn or lights to draw attention, and call 999.
7. **Once you are safe, report it if you want to.**
   - If it is not an emergency, you can report to police via 101 or your local force’s online contact options.
   - If you want to report anonymously, you can contact Crimestoppers online or by phone when you are safe.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether you are “overreacting” right now.
- You do not need to confront them, identify them, or gather proof.
- You do not need to post online, warn others publicly, or argue with anyone about what you saw.
- You do not need to drive home immediately if you feel shaky. You can stay in the staffed place until you are steady.

## Important reassurance
Noticing something off in a car park and backing away is a normal protective response. You are allowed to prioritise distance and help over politeness or speed.

## Scope note
This is first-steps-only guidance for the next few minutes. Later steps, such as formal reporting, workplace or venue complaints, or asking about CCTV, can happen once you are safe and calmer.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal advice or professional security advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.
