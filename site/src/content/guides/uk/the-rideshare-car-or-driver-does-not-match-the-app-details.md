# What to do if… the rideshare car or driver does not match the app details (UK)

## Short answer
Do not get in. Move to a safer, busier place, cancel the ride in the app, and use a different way home. If you feel threatened or the person will not leave you alone, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t get in "just to check" or because you feel awkward saying no.
- Don’t accept an off-app ride, including cash, bank transfer, or "same price".
- Don’t hand over your phone, show a verification code or PIN, or share personal details until you have the correct vehicle and driver.
- Don’t stand close to the window or lean in to argue; keep space and an exit path.
- Don’t let urgency, rain, lateness, or surge pricing override a mismatch.

## What to do now
1. **Create space and get to a safer pause.** Step away from the car and move to a well-lit, busier place, such as a shop entrance, hotel lobby, station concourse, or venue doorway with staff or security.
2. **Check only what the app shows.** Compare the arriving car with the in-app details: number plate, make, model, driver name, and driver photo. If any key detail does not match, treat it as the wrong vehicle.
3. **Cancel the ride in the app.** Choose the closest safety-related reason if offered, such as the driver, car, or plate not matching. If a cancellation fee appears, leave that for later.
4. **Stop the interaction quickly if the driver approaches.** One calm line is enough: "This isn’t my ride." Then stop engaging and move nearer other people or inside.
5. **Save only what you can safely save.** Screenshot the trip screen. If you can do it discreetly from a safe distance, note the actual car’s number plate, the time, and the location.
6. **Report it once you are safe.** Use the app’s help or safety reporting flow for a wrong vehicle, wrong driver, or plate mismatch, and attach the screenshot if the app lets you.
7. **Choose a safer way onward.** Rebook from a staffed or public pickup point, use another licensed service, use public transport, go to a staffed taxi rank, or call someone you trust.
8. **If you suspect an unlicensed taxi or minicab, do not use it.** When you are safe, report it to TfL if you saw it in London, or to the local council licensing team if you saw it elsewhere in Great Britain. There is a different process in Northern Ireland; use the GOV.UK page to find the right route. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.

## What can wait
- You don’t need to decide whether it was a mistake, scam, or misunderstanding right now; mismatch means no ride.
- You don’t need to resolve cancellation fees, ratings, refunds, or complaints while you are still at the pickup point.
- You don’t need perfect evidence; a screenshot and basic notes are usually enough to start a report.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to freeze or second-guess yourself in this moment, especially if you are tired, alone, or being pressured. A mismatch is a valid reason to refuse a ride, and leaving the situation is enough for now.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and get you somewhere safer. Follow-up reporting, refunds, and any formal complaints can wait until you are calm and secure, and later decisions may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999. If you are unsure but concerned, it is reasonable to cancel, move to a safer place, and use a different way onward.
