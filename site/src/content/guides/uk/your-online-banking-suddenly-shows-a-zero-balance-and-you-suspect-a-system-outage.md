# What to do if… your online banking suddenly shows a zero balance and you suspect a system outage (UK)

## Short answer
Verify the outage through a second channel before moving funds. Use an ATM balance enquiry or call 159 (the secure banking hotline) to confirm your "ledger balance" rather than relying on the app's "available balance" display.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t click links in "outage alert" texts or emails, even if they look official.
- Don’t transfer your remaining funds to a "safe account" suggested by anyone over the phone.
- Don’t repeatedly attempt to log in or reset passwords, as this may trigger a security lockout.
- Don’t cancel active Direct Debits or Standing Orders until the bank confirms they are failing.
- Don’t post screenshots of the error on social media, as these often contain partial account numbers or names.

## What to do now
1. **Pause all transactions for 10 minutes.** Avoid making transfers or payments until you have confirmed whether the "zero" is a display glitch or a fund issue.
2. **Capture time-stamped evidence of the error.** Take a screenshot of the £0.00 balance and any specific error codes or banners displayed in the app.
3. **Verify the bank's service status.** Check the bank's official "Service Status" page via their website or look for a verified blue-tick update on their official social media profile.
4. **Test a second banking channel.** If the mobile app shows zero, check via a desktop browser, or use a physical ATM to perform a "balance enquiry" only.
   - Note: If the ATM also shows zero or is unavailable, the outage may be affecting the core accounting system.
5. **Call 159 to reach your bank securely.** This is the UK's universal secure number for banking fraud and security. Ask the agent specifically for your **ledger balance** (the actual money held) rather than the **available balance** (what the app is currently displaying).
6. **Request confirmation of payment status.** Ask the bank if scheduled payments like rent, mortgage, or Direct Debits will be processed manually or if they are paused.
7. **Contact essential payees if payments are blocked.** If a mortgage or utility payment is due today and the bank confirms it cannot be sent, notify the provider that your bank is experiencing a technical outage and request a late-fee waiver.
8. **Claim an immediate refund for Direct Debit errors.** If the system outage caused a Direct Debit to be taken twice or for the wrong amount, tell your bank you are claiming an "Indemnity Claim" under the **Direct Debit Guarantee** for an immediate refund.

## What can wait
- You do not need to move your money to a different bank today.
- You do not need to prove the bank was at fault immediately; the bank's own internal logs will record the system failure.
- You do not need to file a Financial Ombudsman complaint today; you must first allow the bank up to 8 weeks to resolve any financial loss or fees.

## Important reassurance
A sudden zero balance in a banking app is a frequent symptom of a "syncing error" between the app's front-end and the bank's back-end servers. It rarely indicates that the money has actually left the account. Banks have a regulatory requirement under the UK Consumer Duty to ensure customers are not left in financial distress due to their IT failures.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for suspected IT outages. It does not cover steps for confirmed fraud or long-term debt management resulting from banking errors.

## Important note
This information is not financial or legal advice. If you suspect your account has been compromised by a third party rather than a system error, contact your bank's fraud department immediately.
