# What to do if… your travel companion loses their passport and you need to manage bookings and plans immediately (UK)

## Short answer
Find a secure location with Wi-Fi, report the passport as lost or stolen to HM Passport Office immediately to cancel it, and apply online for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) which costs £125.

## Do not do these things
- Do not use the passport if it is found after you have reported it as lost or stolen; it is no longer valid for travel and will be flagged at borders.
- Do not delay the cancellation while searching; reporting it immediately reduces the risk of identity fraud.
- Do not pay third-party websites claiming to offer "fast-track" emergency services; use only the official GOV.UK portal.
- Do not change non-urgent bookings until you have a confirmed timeline for the Emergency Travel Document.
- Do not hand your own identity documents to anyone other than official consulate staff, police, or airline personnel.

## What to do now
1. **Find a secure location with power and internet access to act as a temporary base.** Use a hotel lobby, airport lounge, or café where you can safely use devices and make phone calls without rushing.
2. **Conduct a final five-minute physical search of all immediate areas.** Check bags, pockets, hotel safes, and the last transport seats used before concluding the document is unrecoverable.
3. **Cancel the missing passport online immediately via the GOV.UK service.** You can report a lost or stolen passport for someone else if they are unable to do it themselves; this triggers the cancellation and notifies the authorities.
4. **Report the loss or theft to the local police and obtain a written report or reference number.** Most travel insurers and some consulates require this official record to process claims or applications.
5. **Apply for an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) if the companion needs to travel within the next six weeks.**
   - The person must have held a UK passport issued on or after 1 January 2006 to use the online application.
   - If their last passport was issued before 2006, they must contact the nearest British embassy or consulate directly.
6. **Gather the necessary information for the ETD application and airline updates.**
   - A digital passport-style photo taken on a smartphone against a plain background.
   - Details of the travel itinerary including flight numbers and dates.
   - A credit or debit card to pay the £125 non-refundable fee.
7. **Contact the airline to inform them of the situation and the ETD application.** Ask specifically if they accept the ETD for the upcoming flight and what passenger details need to be updated in the booking.
8. **Categorise upcoming travel plans into immediate requirements and flexible items.** Identify what must be addressed in the next 24 hours, such as tonight's accommodation or a flight tomorrow morning, versus tours that can be cancelled later.
9. **Contact your travel insurance provider to open a claim for emergency assistance.** Ask what specific evidence they require to reimburse the cost of the ETD or any necessary itinerary changes.
10. **Verify if any upcoming border crossings or visas are linked to the lost passport number.** Note that an ETD has a different number and may require you to update electronic visas (e.g., US ESTA or Australia eVisitor) or entry permits.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide on a full passport replacement today; the ETD is the priority for immediate travel.
- You do not need to cancel bookings for later in the week until you have the ETD processing timeline, which is usually 2 working days.
- You do not need to settle the financial split of emergency costs while in the initial scramble.

## Important reassurance
Losing a passport is a standard consular emergency that authorities and airlines handle daily. Once the passport is cancelled and the ETD application is submitted, you have moved from a state of uncertainty to a regulated recovery process. Confusion or frustration in the first hour is a typical response to sudden travel disruption.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for managing travel plans and securing emergency documentation. It does not cover the process for applying for a full ten-year replacement passport or long-term legal advice regarding lost identity.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and is based on UK government procedures as of 2026. Specific requirements may vary based on the country you are visiting, the airline’s individual policies, and the specific circumstances of the passport holder.
