# What to do if… the last ferry of the day is cancelled and you are stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, warm pause point, then ask the ferry operator or terminal staff to confirm your immediate rerouting, refund, and overnight-assistance options.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t leave the terminal area in a rush without first checking whether the operator is arranging a replacement sailing, coach, or reroute.
- Don’t pay for a hotel or long taxi journey before asking what the operator can provide, authorise, or reimburse, and what receipts they need.
- Don’t rely on rumours or social media posts; use the operator’s official updates or speak to staff.
- Don’t argue every rule at the counter while you are cold, tired, or unsafe; get the immediate options, a reference number if possible, and somewhere safe to wait.
- Don’t take unsafe shortcuts, walk along unlit roads, or accept lifts from strangers because you feel under time pressure.

## What to do now
1. **Get to a safer pause point first:** go inside the terminal if it is open, or to the nearest staffed, well-lit place such as a hotel reception, café, railway station, or taxi office. Put your phone on low power mode and charge it if you can.
2. **Get the official position:** ask the operator or terminal staff:
   - “Is there a replacement sailing, coach, or other transport tonight?”
   - “What is the earliest reroute you can offer to my final destination?”
   - “Where should I wait, and how will you update passengers?”
3. **Ask for rerouting or refund options:** for covered ferry services, if the sailing is cancelled or the operator reasonably expects departure to be delayed by more than 90 minutes, passengers should usually be offered a choice between rerouting to the final destination at no extra cost under comparable conditions, or reimbursement of the ticket price. Ask staff to confirm which options apply to your booking tonight.
4. **If you are stuck overnight, ask about care and assistance before booking anything yourself:** where a cancellation or delay makes an extra night necessary, passengers may be offered accommodation and transport to and from it. UK guidance gives an accommodation cap of £70 per person per night for up to 3 nights, and this assistance may not apply where the cancellation or delay is caused by weather conditions endangering safe operation of the ship. Ask:
   - “Are you providing accommodation tonight, or should I self-book?”
   - “What limit should I stay within?”
   - “How do I get authorisation?”
   - “What receipts or reference numbers do you need?”
5. **Build one workable Plan B while you are still near help:** ask whether the operator can reroute you through a different port, later sailing, coach, train, or other onward connection. If you have a vehicle, ask whether there is a realistic road alternative from this terminal. If you are travelling on foot, ask which late public transport still leaves from the terminal area and where it stops for the night.
6. **Keep simple proof of what happened:** screenshot the cancellation notice in the operator app or website, photograph the departure board, and keep emails, texts, booking references, receipts, and staff notes.
7. **If you must arrange things yourself tonight, keep it safe and documented:** book a safe place to sleep first, then transport if needed. Use an official taxi rank, licensed taxi, or pre-booked private hire vehicle where possible, confirm the pickup point, keep itemised receipts, and tell someone you trust where you are staying and your plan for the morning.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide tonight whether to make a formal complaint; keep evidence now and deal with the complaint later.
- You do not need to calculate compensation tonight; focus on safe waiting, rerouting, refund options, and overnight help.
- You do not need to choose a complex late-night reroute if you are exhausted and a safe nearby overnight option is available.
- If you later complain to the operator or terminal operator, UK guidance says this should usually be done within 2 months of the service date, but that does not need to be handled at the counter tonight.

## Important reassurance
This is a common disruption scenario, and it is understandable to feel stuck when the last sailing is cancelled. Your next hour only needs three things: get warm and safe, get the operator’s clear position, and make one practical plan for tonight.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few hours only. Later decisions about reimbursement, compensation, insurance, missed connections, or a formal complaint may need specialist help or the operator’s own claims process.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, insurance, or professional advice. Exact rights and practical help can depend on the route, operator, ticket, whether the service is covered by the rules, and the reason for the cancellation or delay. If you feel unsafe or at immediate risk, prioritise safety and seek help from on-site staff or emergency services.
