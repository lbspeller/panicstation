# What to do if… your phone battery is low and your ticket or access pass is only on your phone (UK)

## Short answer
Open, download, activate, or save the ticket or pass now, then go to staff or a help point before your phone dies. The aim is to prove what you have while the screen can still show it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep the ticket app open at full brightness while you wait.
- Do not wait until you are at the barrier or being inspected to mention the problem.
- Do not assume you will have signal inside stations, underground areas, on platforms, or on board.
- Do not rely on a screenshot unless the operator, venue, or app clearly says screenshots are accepted.
- If you are using pay as you go or contactless on a phone, do not start a journey if the phone is likely to die before any required touch out.
- Do not hand your unlocked phone to a stranger. Keep control of it and ask staff instead.

## What to do now
1. Turn on battery saver or low power mode.
   - Reduce screen brightness.
   - Close other apps.
   - Turn off Wi-Fi and Bluetooth unless the ticket, pass, or wallet needs them.
   - Keep the phone locked when you are not actively showing the ticket or pass.

2. Make the ticket or pass available without signal.
   - Open the ticket, barcode, QR code, or pass now.
   - Use the app’s download, activate, add to wallet, or save option if it has one.
   - If the ticket is a PDF in an email or receipt, download the PDF to the phone so it can open offline.
   - If you still have enough battery, briefly check that the ticket opens with mobile data off, then turn data back on if you need it.

3. Get help before you are stuck.
   - At a station, go to the ticket office, staffed gate line, information desk, or help point.
   - At a venue, go to the entrance staff, box office, reception desk, or access desk.
   - Say: “My ticket or pass is only on my phone and the battery is nearly dead. Can you check it now or tell me what to do before it turns off?”

4. Keep the proof ready in the simplest form.
   - Leave the barcode, QR code, wallet pass, booking reference, or PDF on screen only long enough for staff to scan or read it.
   - Note the booking reference, seat, coach, route, venue, time, or account email on paper if you can.
   - If you are travelling with someone trusted, tell them the booking reference or order number.

5. If you are already on board, show it early when practical.
   - Approach a member of staff if it is safe and normal to do so.
   - Show the ticket while it still loads.
   - Ask what they need from you if the phone dies before the next check.

6. Find power without losing time.
   - Ask staff whether there is a charging point nearby.
   - Use your own cable or power bank if you have one.
   - If you need to buy something immediately, prioritise the correct charging cable or a small power bank.

7. If you are using pay as you go or contactless on a phone, reduce the risk now.
   - If you have not started yet and you have a physical contactless card, use the physical card instead.
   - Keep the phone alive for any required touch out.
   - A dead phone can leave a journey incomplete, which may mean a maximum fare or, on some systems, a penalty fare.

## What can wait
- You do not need to argue a policy right now.
- You do not need to chase refunds, appeals, or fare adjustments while you are at the barrier, gate, or platform.
- You do not need to solve your battery problem for every future journey today.
- You do not need to work out why the battery drained; stabilise this trip first.

## Important reassurance
This is a common travel problem, and acting early can reduce the chance of it becoming a bigger issue. The useful move is to show the ticket, pass, or booking details to the right staff while you still can.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get you through the next barrier, inspection, boarding, or entry point. Later decisions about refunds, fare adjustments, complaints, penalty fares, or access arrangements may need the operator, venue, card issuer, or a specialist adviser.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel, or professional advice. Rules vary by operator, venue, app, ticket type, and payment method, so follow the instructions for your ticket or pass and ask staff as early as possible.
