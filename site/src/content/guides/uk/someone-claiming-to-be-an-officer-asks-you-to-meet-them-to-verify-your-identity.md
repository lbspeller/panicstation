# What to do if… someone claiming to be an officer asks you to meet them to verify your identity (UK)

## Short answer
Pause, do not agree to meet, and do not share more details. End the contact and check independently with police on 101; call 999 instead if you feel in immediate danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go to a meeting location just because the person sounds confident.
- Do not call back or message using the number, link, email address, or app account they gave you.
- Do not send photos of your passport, driving licence, bank cards, or a selfie with ID.
- Do not confirm your address, date of birth, National Insurance number, passwords, banking details, or one-time codes.
- Do not transfer money, pay a fine, buy vouchers, hand over cash, hand over bank cards, or give items to a courier.
- Do not let pressure such as “you must do this today” or “you will be arrested if you do not attend” make you skip verification.
- Do not open the door or leave with someone claiming to be police if you are unsure and can safely pause.

## What to do now
1. **Stop the contact.** Say: “I’m going to verify this independently through 101.” Then end the call or stop replying.
2. **Write down what they gave you.** If you already have it, note their name, rank, collar or shoulder number, force, station, unit, reference number, phone number, email address, and the place they asked you to attend. Do not give your own details in return.
3. **Check through an independent route.** Call **101** using your own phone keypad or the official police website contact route, not a number from the message. If the suspicious contact was a phone call, especially on a landline, wait five minutes before calling or use another phone if you can. Say someone claiming to be an officer has asked you to meet to “verify your identity”, and ask whether the contact is genuine.
4. **If the contact says they are from the NCA, use the NCA officer verification route.** You can still call 101 or 999 if you feel threatened, but the NCA also has an officer verification page for checking someone who says they are an NCA officer.
5. **Only arrange any meeting through an official route.** If police confirm the contact is genuine, ask for the appointment to be arranged through 101, the force website, the relevant station or unit, or a police station public enquiry counter where available.
6. **If someone comes to your door claiming to be police, keep the door closed while you check.** Ask them to show their warrant card or identification where you can see it safely, then call 101 to verify before opening if you are unsure. Call 999 if you feel in immediate danger.
7. **If you clicked a link, replied, shared ID, or think it was a scam, report it.** In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland, report cyber crime or fraud to **Report Fraud**. In Scotland, contact **Police Scotland on 101**. If any money moved or bank details were shared, contact your bank using the number on your card, your banking app, or the bank’s official website.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to attend a meeting or interview.
- You do not need to prove your identity to a stranger in order to “clear things up”.
- You do not need to send documents, explain your whole situation, or answer personal questions before verification.
- You can decide what to do next after you have checked the contact through an official route.

## Important reassurance
Feeling unsure is a valid reason to pause. Real officers should be able to let you verify who you are dealing with, and scammers often use urgency, official-sounding language, or partial personal details to make you act quickly.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce risk, stabilise the situation, and buy time. If the contact is confirmed as genuine, later decisions about meetings, interviews, documents, or legal rights may need specialist advice.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you feel threatened or unsafe, prioritise immediate safety and contact emergency services.
