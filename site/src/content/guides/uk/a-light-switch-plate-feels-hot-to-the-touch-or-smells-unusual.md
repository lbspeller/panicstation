# What to do if… a light switch plate feels hot to the touch or smells unusual (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the switch immediately and turn off the power to that circuit at your consumer unit. If you see smoke, sparks, or flames, evacuate the property and call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Do not toggle the switch "on and off" to test if the heat or smell returns.
- Do not assume the issue has resolved itself if the plate cools down after use.
- Do not spray water, cleaning fluids, or fire extinguishers into the switch plate.
- Do not attempt to unscrew the faceplate or touch the internal wiring yourself.
- Do not reset a circuit breaker (MCB) or replace a fuse if it has already tripped.
- Do not cover the switch with tape or furniture to hide the smell or heat.

## What to do now
1. **Stop using the switch and any connected lights immediately.**
2. **Check for signs of active fire or arcing.**
   - If you see **smoke, flames, or glowing embers**, or if you hear **loud crackling or continuous sizzling**, evacuate the building and **call 999**.
3. **Isolate the electrical circuit at the consumer unit (fuse box).**
   - Locate your consumer unit and switch **OFF** the circuit breaker labeled for those lights.
   - If you cannot identify the correct circuit, switch **OFF the Main Switch** to isolate the entire property.
   - **Do not touch the consumer unit** if it is also hot, making noise, or smelling of burning; evacuate and call emergency services instead.
4. **Warn all other occupants in the property.** 
   - Ensure everyone knows not to touch the affected switch or attempt to turn the power back on.
5. **Check for scorch marks or discolouration.**
   - Look for brown or black marks on the faceplate or the wall directly above it, but do not touch the surface if it appears melted.
6. **Contact a registered electrician (NICEIC or NAPIT).**
   - Inform them you have an "emergency electrical fault" involving an overheating switch and a burning or fishy smell.
7. **Notify your landlord or building manager if you rent.**
   - Report this as an urgent safety hazard. If you have isolated the power, state this clearly so they understand the urgency of the repair.

## What can wait
- You do **not** need to diagnose whether the cause is a loose terminal, an overloaded circuit, or a failing dimmer module.
- You do **not** need to buy a replacement switch or bulbs before an inspection has occurred.
- You do **not** need to worry about the cost of rewiring the entire house until a professional assessment is completed.

## Important reassurance
Recognising a hot switch or an unusual "fishy" or "plastic" smell is a vital early warning. Taking the immediate step of isolating the power significantly reduces the risk of an electrical fire. It is normal to feel anxious when dealing with utilities, but you have taken the correct protective action by stopping use.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. A professional inspection is required to determine if the wiring, back-box, or the switch itself has been permanently damaged by heat.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute professional electrical advice. If any signs of fire are present, prioritise life safety and contact the Fire and Rescue Service via 999.
