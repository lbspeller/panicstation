# What to do if… a hire vehicle shows a dashboard warning you do not understand before you leave the lot (UK)

## Short answer
Do not exit the rental premises. Capture a photograph of the warning and return immediately to the rental desk to request a roadworthy replacement or a certified explanation in writing.

## Do not do these things
- Do not drive the vehicle onto public roads or past the exit barrier.
- Do not ignore an amber or red light because the vehicle "feels fine" to drive.
- Do not attempt to reset the dashboard or disconnect the battery to clear the fault.
- Do not accept verbal-only reassurances that the light is "just a glitch" without written documentation.
- Do not sign a final condition report that lists the vehicle as fault-free if a warning is visible.
- Do not assume the rental company is aware of the light; previous renters may have failed to report it.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle in its parking bay immediately.** Apply the parking brake and ensure the vehicle is in Park or Neutral before investigating the dashboard further.
2. **Confirm the light is persistent rather than a self-test.**
   - Switch the ignition off and back on.
   - Observe if the light remains illuminated after the initial 3-5 second system check.
   - Any red or amber light that stays on indicates a fault that requires attention before a journey begins.
3. **Document the exact state of the dashboard.**
   - Take a **clear photograph** of the illuminated symbol and any accompanying text message.
   - Take a photograph of the **odometer (mileage)** to prove the fault was present at the start of the hire.
   - Note any **sensory cues** such as a burning smell, fluid leaks under the car, or a vibrating engine.
4. **Locate your rental agreement or digital check-out report.**
   - Verify if the fault was pre-recorded by the rental agent.
   - If the fault is not listed, you must have it added before leaving.
5. **Return to the rental desk or speak to the lot marshall immediately.**
   - State: "This vehicle is showing a [red/amber] warning light that was not disclosed. Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, I require a vehicle that is of satisfactory quality and roadworthy."
   - Request **either** a vehicle swap **or** a technician to clear the fault and provide a signed statement of safety.
6. **Handle exit barrier scenarios without crossing the sensor.**
   - If you have already reached the exit barrier, pull into a safe bay before the gate.
   - Use the barrier intercom or your phone to contact the office; do not drive through and "resolve it later."

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical meaning of the OBD-II fault code.
- You do not need to contact your own breakdown provider; the rental company is responsible for the vehicle's state at pickup.
- You do not need to worry about being "difficult"; you have a legal right to a safe vehicle.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel pressured or rushed during a car pickup, especially at busy airports or depots. Dashboard warnings are often binary (on/off) and do not always mean the car will break down instantly, but reporting them now is the only way to protect yourself from liability and ensure your journey is safe.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for dashboard faults discovered at the point of collection only. It does not cover faults that develop during a journey or long-term rental disputes.

## Important note
Under UK law, including the Road Traffic Act and Consumer Rights Act 2015, a rental provider must supply a vehicle that is roadworthy and fit for purpose. Driving with a red warning light may invalidate your insurance or lead to prosecution if the vehicle is found to be dangerous. Always prioritize professional mechanical assessment over convenience.
