# What to do if… your motorcycle or scooter will not start and you are stranded away from home (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a place of relative safety first, then make the bike visible and arrange recovery. Call 999 and ask for the police if you are in immediate danger, stuck in a live lane, there has been a collision, someone is injured, or the bike is causing a dangerous obstruction you cannot make safe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep trying to start it in a live lane, junction, blind bend, narrow verge, motorway emergency area or hard shoulder.
- Do not push the bike across traffic lanes or stand in the carriageway to wave people around.
- Do not remove protective gear or work on wiring next to fast traffic.
- Do not use a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Do not place or retrieve a warning triangle on any road unless you can do it safely.
- Do not accept an unsolicited tow or lift unless you can verify who it is and you have chosen it.
- Do not leave the bike where it is likely to be hit if there is a safe, short move that reduces the danger.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safer pause.**
   - If the bike is still rolling, signal and move left into the safest reachable place, such as a service area, lay-by, car park or quiet side road.
   - On a motorway, leave at the next exit or pull into a service area if you can. If you cannot, move left and use an emergency area or hard shoulder if one is safely reachable.
   - If you are stuck in a live lane or cannot get clear of traffic, do not try to fix or push the bike. Call 999 and ask for the police.

2. **Make yourself and the bike easier to see.**
   - Put hazard warning lights on if fitted.
   - Keep lights on if visibility is poor or it is dark.
   - Put on high-vis or reflective clothing if you have it and can reach it safely.
   - Stabilise the bike only if you can do it without stepping into traffic.

3. **If you are on a motorway, use the emergency route.**
   - If there is a free emergency telephone and you can reach it safely, use it for advice and assistance.
   - If you are in an emergency area, use the emergency telephone before leaving or rejoining, and follow the operator’s instructions.
   - If you cannot safely get away from the bike, cannot reach an emergency telephone, or feel exposed to traffic, call 999 and ask for the police.

4. **Wait away from traffic if you safely can.**
   - If there is a safety barrier and you can reach it without crossing traffic, wait behind it and well away from the carriageway.
   - Check for hidden hazards behind the barrier, such as drops, uneven ground or debris.
   - Do not stand where the motorcycle could be pushed into you if another vehicle hits it.
   - Do not walk along a motorway carriageway to find help.

5. **Arrange motorcycle-capable recovery.**
   - Call your breakdown provider, insurer assistance line, or a reputable recovery service and say clearly that it is a motorcycle or scooter.
   - If you are alone, say so. If you are vulnerable, disabled, injured, cold, stranded at night, or feel unsafe, say that early.
   - If another person is with you, one of you can handle calls and location details while the other watches traffic from a safe place.

6. **Give one clear location description.**
   - Give the road number or name, direction of travel, nearest junction, services, town or landmark.
   - Say whether you are in a live lane, hard shoulder, emergency area, lay-by, car park or side road.
   - On a motorway or high-speed road, use marker posts, driver location signs, a mapping app, an emergency telephone location, or an SOS button if available.

7. **Only do quick no-start checks after you are off the motorway and away from fast traffic.**
   - In a car park, service area, lay-by or quiet side road, check the kill switch, side stand, neutral, fuel level, key or fob, immobiliser warning light and any obvious loose battery lead.
   - Try once or twice only. If it still will not start, stop and wait for recovery rather than draining the battery or losing focus on safety.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the fault at the roadside.
- You do not need to decide now about repairs, claims or complaints.
- You do not need to compare recovery prices while you are exposed to traffic.
- You do not need to prove what caused the problem before getting yourself and the bike recovered.

## Important reassurance
A motorcycle or scooter that will not start can feel urgent because you are exposed and away from home. The immediate problem is usually made smaller by getting out of danger, making yourself visible, giving a clear location and getting recovery moving.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate risk and get you unstuck. Fault diagnosis, repairs, insurance questions and complaints can come later and may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not legal, mechanical, insurance, medical or other professional advice. If traffic conditions feel unsafe or you are at risk, prioritise 999, an emergency telephone, or an SOS button if your vehicle has one, and follow the instructions you are given.
