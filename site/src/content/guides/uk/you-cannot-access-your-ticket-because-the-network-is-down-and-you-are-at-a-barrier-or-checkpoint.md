# What to do if… you cannot access your ticket because the network is down and you are at a barrier or checkpoint (UK)

## Short answer
Step out of the queue immediately, attempt to find a cached version of the ticket in your phone's wallet or email attachments, and use the station help point or approach a staff member with your booking reference.

## Do not do these things
- **Do not** jump over the barrier, tailgate another passenger, or force the gate open as this is classified as a criminal offence under railway byelaws and may lead to prosecution.
- **Do not** tap a different contactless card or device to "test" the barrier if you have already paid for a digital ticket, as this may result in an automatic maximum fare charge or "card clash."
- **Do not** delete and reinstall the ticket app while on a weak connection, as this often wipes the local cache and prevents offline access to previously loaded tickets.
- **Do not** block the flow of other passengers at the gateline; moving to the side allows you to troubleshoot without escalating the situation.

## What to do now
1. **Move at least two metres away from the barrier to a quiet area and check your phone's offline storage.**  
   - Open **Apple Wallet** or **Google Wallet** directly from your home screen rather than through the ticket app, as these often store tickets locally without needing a live data connection.
   - Look for a **PDF attachment** in your email inbox; many ticket retailers send the actual barcode as a file that can be opened even when the app itself fails to load.
   - If your screen is dark, **increase your phone brightness to 100 percent** and disable "dark mode" or blue-light filters, which can prevent the barrier's scanner from reading a dimly loaded barcode.

2. **Locate your booking reference number or payment confirmation if the barcode will not appear.**  
   - Have the **8-to-10 digit alphanumeric reference** ready to show; this is usually found in the subject line of your confirmation email.
   - Prepare to show a **bank notification or transaction record** from your mobile banking app if the purchase was recent.
   - Ensure you have your **physical or digital Railcard** ready, as 2026 validation rules require staff to verify the discount associated with the ticket.

3. **Identify a member of the gateline staff or use the station help point.**  
   - Look for staff wearing high-visibility vests near the wide-aisle gates.
   - If no staff are present, press the **"Information" button on the station help point** (usually a tall pillar near the entrance) and explain you have a valid ticket that will not load due to network failure.
   - Say clearly: "I have a valid digital ticket but my network connection has failed. I have my booking reference and proof of purchase ready for verification."

4. **Request an official manual check or an Unpaid Fare Notice if you must travel immediately.**  
   - Staff may be able to verify your booking reference on their handheld devices.
   - If they cannot verify it but allow you to proceed, ask for an **Unpaid Fare Notice (UFN)**; this allows you to complete your journey and provides a formal 21-day window to provide your ticket details online later without a penalty.
   - If you are issued a **Penalty Fare Notice**, accept the paperwork calmly; you have a statutory right to appeal within 21 days by providing the digital ticket once your connection is restored.

5. **Follow specific steps for TfL (London Underground/DLR/Elizabeth Line) incomplete journeys.**  
   - If you are forced to pass through a gate without touching in or out because of a technical failure, you must **wait 48 hours** for the journey to appear in your TfL account.
   - Access the **TfL website or app** after this window to apply for an "Incomplete Journey" correction to avoid being charged the maximum fare.

## What can wait
- **You do not need to fix your mobile data connection** at the barrier; once you are on the train or in a different part of the station, the signal may return naturally.
- **You do not need to contact the ticket retailer immediately** while at the gate; focus on getting through the barrier first and resolve refund or technical issues later.
- **You do not need to decide whether to appeal a notice** in the next five minutes; you have a minimum of 21 days to submit evidence for any penalty fare or unpaid fare notice issued.

## Important reassurance
Network outages and app failures at barriers are frequent occurrences that station staff are trained to manage. As long as you have a booking reference or proof of payment, there is a standard procedural route to resolve the issue. Technical failure on the part of the retailer or network provider is a valid ground for appeal if a penalty is issued.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for immediate access to platforms or exits during a network failure. It does not cover long-term technical troubleshooting for mobile devices or the legal specifics of individual train operating company contracts.

## Important note
Rail transit rules in the UK are governed by the National Rail Conditions of Travel and specific operator byelaws. Under the 2026 update to these conditions, passengers are expected to ensure their device is charged and capable of displaying a ticket, but staff have discretionary powers to assist in the event of documented network-wide or app-specific failures. If a penalty is issued, you must follow the formal appeals process stated on the documentation provided.
