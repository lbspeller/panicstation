# What to do if… you notice your doorbell camera or security alert shows someone lingering at your door (UK)

## Short answer
Do not open the door under any circumstances. Ensure all entry points are locked, move to a safe position away from windows, and call 999 immediately if the person tries the door or you feel threatened.

## Do not do these things
- Do not open the door to "check" or speak to the person, even if you have a door chain.
- Do not indicate through the door that you are alone or feel frightened.
- Do not go outside to confront the individual or ask them to leave.
- Do not assume a "delivery uniform" means the person is legitimate if they are lingering or acting suspiciously.
- Do not use the "siren" or "alarm" feature on your camera if you are in a vulnerable position, as it may escalate the person's behavior.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your physical environment immediately.** **Lock your front door, back door, and any ground-floor windows.** Move to a room where you cannot be seen from the doorstep and, if possible, put a locked internal door between yourself and the entryway.
2. **Monitor the situation through your device only.** **Use your smartphone or tablet to view the live camera feed rather than looking through a window or peephole.** If you can do so from your device, turn on any external smart lights to show the area is illuminated.
3. **Use the "Silent Solution" if you need to call 999 but cannot speak.** **If you are in danger and cannot talk, dial 999 from a mobile and press "55" when prompted by the automated system.** This alerts the operator that the call is a genuine emergency and will transfer you to the police.
   - If calling from a landline and you cannot speak, stay on the line; the operator will listen for background noise and may transfer you to police.
4. **Call the police based on the immediate threat level.** 
   - **Call 999** if the person is trying your door handle, attempting to force entry, peering through windows, or if they are loitering specifically near a vulnerable or elderly person’s home.
   - **Call 101** (or report online) if the person has left and you want to provide a description and footage for the local neighbourhood policing team.
5. **Communicate briefly and firmly through the intercom if you feel safe doing so.** **State "I cannot come to the door; please leave now" using the camera’s two-way audio.** Do not engage in a conversation or answer any questions they ask.
6. **Alert others in the property or building.** **Quietly inform other residents of the situation so they also stay away from doors and windows.** If you live in a block of flats, do not go into the communal hallway to investigate.
7. **Preserve the evidence for police.** **Save the specific video clips of the lingering behavior to your device’s permanent storage or the cloud.** Note the exact time, a description of the person's clothing, and any vehicle they may be using.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the person or determine their motive (e.g., "scam" vs "delivery") right now.
- You do not need to post the footage to social media or community groups immediately; prioritize your physical safety first.
- You do not need to review your entire home security system or purchase new hardware in the middle of the incident.

## Important reassurance
It is completely normal to feel a "surge" of adrenaline or anxiety when a security alert shows unexpected activity. Staying behind a locked door and using technology to observe rather than physical confrontation is the most effective way to maintain your safety.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an active loitering or suspicious doorstep incident. It does not cover long-term security auditing or legal proceedings.

## Important note
This information is for general safety guidance and does not replace professional police advice. Always prioritize your immediate physical safety and call 999 in any situation where you believe a crime is in progress or life is at risk.
