# What to do if… someone is live-streaming you or your location in a way that feels targeted (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safer pause: move towards people, staff, security, or a well-lit public place, and do not lead the person to your home, workplace, car, or anywhere isolated. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue for the camera, grab their phone, or physically confront them; it can escalate.
- Don’t go home, to work, to your car, or to a quiet place while they may still be following or broadcasting you.
- Don’t post your real-time location to explain what is happening.
- Don’t wait to collect perfect evidence if it keeps you exposed.
- Don’t contact the streamer’s audience or try to negotiate with them while you are still unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safer pause with witnesses.** Go into a staffed place such as a shop, café, hotel lobby, station, library, or public building. Stay near staff, security, or other people.
2. **If there is immediate danger, call 999.** Say where you are and that someone is live-streaming you or your location in a way that feels targeted. If you cannot speak safely, call 999 and try to whisper, cough, tap, or respond when prompted. From a mobile, if you are prompted and still cannot speak, press 55 or tap so the call is not treated as accidental.
3. **If it is not an emergency, report it to police when you can safely do so.** Call 101 or use your local police force’s online reporting route. You can say: “Someone is live-streaming me and my location in a way that feels targeted. I am worried it could escalate.”
4. **Stop your own phone leaking your location.** Turn off live location sharing in map, family, ride-share, and social apps. Turn off location access for social apps until you are somewhere safer.
5. **Ask for immediate help from a real person near you.** Tell staff, security, a transport worker, or a trusted person: “Someone is filming and live-streaming me and I do not feel safe. Can I stay here while I call someone?”
6. **Report the live stream in the app if you can do it without staying exposed.** Use the platform’s report option for the live stream and the account. If a trusted friend can see the stream, ask them to report it while you focus on getting safe.
7. **Keep only quick, safe notes.** From a safer place, write down the platform, account name, link if visible, time, location, what was said, any threats, and a description of the person or vehicle. A screenshot or screen recording can help, but do not delay leaving, calling police, or asking for help to get it.
8. **If this is part of a pattern, contact stalking support once you are safe.** If the person has followed, filmed, posted about, contacted, or monitored you before, the National Stalking Helpline can help you think through next protective steps.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether this is legally stalking, harassment, doxxing, or something else.
- You do not need to prove the person’s intent before asking for help or reporting it.
- You do not need to find every clip, identify every viewer, or write a full statement right now.
- You do not need to contact the streamer directly to ask for removal while the situation is active.

## Important reassurance
Feeling exposed, shaky, or panicky is a normal reaction to being broadcast in real time. The priority is not to win the argument online; it is to create distance, get witnesses, and make simple reports from a safer place.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the moment and reduce immediate risk. Later decisions, including repeated incidents, platform takedown attempts, police follow-up, or safety planning, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or other professional advice. If you think you or someone else is at risk of immediate harm, call 999.
