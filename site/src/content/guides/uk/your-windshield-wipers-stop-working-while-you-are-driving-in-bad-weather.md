# What to do if… your windshield wipers stop working while you are driving in bad weather (UK)

## Short answer
Stay stopped somewhere safe and visible, and do not drive again until you can see clearly through the windscreen. Keep your hazard warning lights on while stationary, and call for help if your location feels unsafe.

## Do not do these things
- Do not set off “just to get home” while rain, snow, spray, or fogging is stopping you seeing clearly.
- Do not stand between your vehicle and moving traffic to look at the wipers.
- Do not attempt repairs on a motorway or other fast road if it puts you outside the vehicle near live traffic.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Do not stop under a bridge, on a bend, or just over a crest if there is a safer place you can reach without driving blind.
- Do not walk in live traffic lanes or along fast traffic to find help.

## What to do now
1. **Make the stop safer.**
   - Keep your seatbelt on while you decide whether it is safer to stay in the vehicle or get out.
   - Switch on hazard warning lights.
   - If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights.
   - If you can move without driving blind or re-entering danger, aim for a safer place such as a service area, car park, lay-by, or exit.

2. **If you are on a motorway or smart motorway, follow motorway breakdown steps.**
   - If you can, get left and stop in an emergency area, lay-by, service area, or on a hard shoulder.
   - If you are in an emergency area, use the emergency telephone provided and follow the operator’s advice before trying to rejoin.
   - If you are on the hard shoulder and a free emergency telephone is nearby, use it only if you can reach it safely. Otherwise use your mobile.

3. **Choose the safest place to wait.**
   - If you are in a live lane, keep your seatbelt and hazard lights on. Call 999 immediately and ask for the police.
   - If you have reached the hard shoulder, an emergency area, or another place of relative safety but it is not safe to leave the vehicle, stay in the vehicle with seatbelts and hazard lights on. Call 999 and ask for the police.
   - If it is clearly safer to get out, leave by the side furthest from traffic.
   - Wait well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, behind a safety barrier if there is one and it is safe to reach. Keep children close and under control.

4. **Get help moving.**
   - If you feel in immediate danger because of traffic, poor visibility, spray, or your stopping position, call 999 and ask for the police.
   - If you are stopped in an emergency area, lay-by, or on the hard shoulder in England, you can use the free emergency telephone where provided or call National Highways on 0300 123 5000 for motorway advice and assistance.
   - Contact your breakdown provider if you have one.
   - Give your road number, direction of travel, nearest junction, marker post or driver location sign, and what has failed.

5. **Do only safe, simple checks while you wait.**
   - Do not open the bonnet or inspect the wiper arms if that puts you near moving traffic.
   - If you have a high-vis item within easy reach, put it on before opening a door.
   - Keep your phone charged and close by.
   - Do not drive on unless the wipers and visibility are good enough for the weather you are in.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the fault is the motor, fuse, linkage, switch, washer fluid, or blades right now.
- You do not need to choose a garage, compare repair costs, or decide whether to continue the journey while you are still stopped in bad weather.
- Insurance, receipts, and repair paperwork can wait until you and the vehicle are in a safe place.

## Important reassurance
Pulling over was the right first move. The priority now is simple: stay visible, avoid moving traffic, get appropriate help, and do not restart driving until you can see properly.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the next few minutes to hour. Later decisions, including repair, recovery, onward travel, or whether the vehicle is roadworthy, may need help from a breakdown provider, mechanic, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, mechanical, emergency-service, or professional advice for your specific vehicle or location. If you feel in immediate danger, call 999 and ask for the police.
