# What to do if… you lose your vehicle keys while you are out and you are stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Get yourself to a safer place first, then contact your breakdown provider or a reputable auto locksmith and say clearly that your vehicle keys are lost, locked inside, or may have been stolen.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stand close to moving traffic to search, argue, or make calls.
- Don’t try to force the door, boot, window, or lock with tools; it can damage the vehicle and slow down proper help.
- Don’t post your exact location publicly to find a locksmith.
- Don’t hand over money or vehicle details to someone who cannot clearly identify their company, price, and call-out terms.
- Don’t ignore signs that the keys may have been taken, such as a disturbed bag, missing coat, or someone staying close to you.
- Don’t leave the vehicle insecure with valuables visible if you walk away to get signal or help.

## What to do now
1. **Make the situation safe before you problem-solve.**  
   If you are near fast traffic, move to a safer place if you can, such as behind a barrier where there is one, or into a staffed building such as a service station or shop.
   - **If your vehicle is stopped in a live lane and you cannot get to a safe place:** stay in the vehicle with your seat belt on, put hazard warning lights on, and call **999**.
   - **If you are stopped somewhere safer, such as a hard shoulder or emergency area, and it is safe to exit:** leave by the side furthest from traffic and wait well away from traffic, behind a barrier where there is one.

2. **Work out exactly where you are.**  
   Use your phone map to copy a location pin. Also note the nearest landmark, car park name, services, road number, direction of travel, junction number, marker post, or driver location sign.

3. **Do one focused key check, then stop searching.**  
   Check pockets, bag lining, under seats if safe, the nearby counter, toilet, café table, and the ground where you last stood. If you use a key tracker app, open it now. Keep the search short so you do not stay exposed or miss help.

4. **Call the right help.**
   - **If you feel unsafe, someone is in immediate danger, or the vehicle is stuck in a live traffic lane:** call **999**.
   - **If you are on a motorway or other high-speed road:** follow the Highway Code breakdown advice. Use the free emergency telephone where provided and safe to reach. If you are in England and stopped in an emergency area, lay-by, or hard shoulder, you can call **National Highways on 0300 123 5000** for advice and assistance.
   - **If you have breakdown cover:** call your provider and say, “Lost keys,” “locked out,” or “cannot start vehicle.” Give your exact location and say whether the vehicle is blocking anything.
   - **If you do not have cover:** call a reputable auto locksmith. Ask for the call-out charge, the likely work charge, and whether they can handle your key type, such as a basic key, transponder key, or keyless fob.

5. **If the keys may have been stolen, treat it as more than a lockout.**  
   Move yourself somewhere safe. If there is immediate danger or a crime is in progress, call **999**. For non-urgent reporting, use **101** or your local police force’s online reporting route. Ask your recovery provider, locksmith, or dealer about removing the missing key from the vehicle’s system and reprogramming replacements.

6. **Have the details ready before help arrives.**  
   Keep your vehicle registration, make, model, approximate year, and proof that you are entitled to the vehicle ready. A provider may check this before opening the vehicle or making a replacement key.

7. **Choose the quickest safe route back to mobility.**
   - **Keys locked inside:** a breakdown provider or auto locksmith may be able to gain entry.
   - **Keys missing entirely:** an auto locksmith may be able to cut or programme a replacement, or a dealer may need to arrange one.
   - **Keyless fob missing or suspected stolen:** ask specifically about disabling the missing fob and programming a replacement.
   - **Too late, unsafe, or far from home:** get yourself to a safe place first, then arrange recovery or replacement from there.

8. **Tell one trusted person what is happening.**  
   Message your location, vehicle description, who you have called, the company name, and any arrival estimate they give you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to buy trackers, change your routine, or upgrade vehicle security.
- You do not need to settle arguments about fault or cost on the spot.
- You do not need to do a full search of everywhere you have been right now; a short focused check and a clear help call is enough.
- You do not need to choose the cheapest long-term replacement option while you are stranded.

## Important reassurance
Losing vehicle keys while out is stressful because it traps you in the moment, but it is usually a solvable situation. Once you are away from traffic and have made one clear call for help, the next step is usually clearer.

## Scope note
These are first steps to keep you safe, protect the vehicle, and get help started. Later decisions about cost, insurance, replacement keys, reprogramming, or security may need advice from your breakdown provider, insurer, locksmith, dealer, or the police.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, professional, or emergency-services advice. If you are in immediate danger, prioritise emergency help and follow instructions from the police, road operators, and recovery professionals.
