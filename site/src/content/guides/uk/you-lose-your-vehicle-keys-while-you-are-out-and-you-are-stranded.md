# What to do if… you lose your vehicle keys while you are out and you are stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a safe location away from traffic and contact either your breakdown provider or a mobile auto locksmith to gain entry or program a replacement key.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to smash a window or force a door lock, as modern security systems may deadlock or trigger immobilisers.
- Do not stand in or near live traffic lanes or on the verge of a high-speed road to search for your keys.
- Do not post your specific location and the fact that you are stranded on public social media groups to find help.
- Do not leave the vehicle unlocked or with valuables visible if you must walk away to find a phone signal.
- Do not assume a standard locksmith can help; most modern vehicles require an "auto locksmith" with specific transponder programming equipment.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise your physical safety before searching for the keys.** **If you are on a motorway or high-speed road, move the vehicle to the hard shoulder or an emergency area if possible, exit via the left-hand door, and wait behind the safety barrier.** If the vehicle is stopped in a live lane and you cannot exit safely, keep your seatbelts on, switch on your hazard lights, and call **999** immediately.

2. **Establish your exact location using digital or physical markers.** **Open a map app to find your GPS coordinates or use the "What3Words" app if you have it.** On motorways, look for the small blue/white marker posts at the side of the road which indicate your road number, direction (e.g., M25 A), and exact location in kilometres.

3. **Perform a single, systematic search of your immediate person and surroundings.** **Check all pockets, bag linings, and the ground around the driver's door once.** If you have a key-tracking app (such as Tile or AirTag), check the last known location now. Avoid repeated, frantic searching that keeps you exposed to hazards or weather.

4. **Contact National Highways if you are stranded on a motorway or major A-road.** **Call 0300 123 5000 to notify them of your position, especially if you are in an emergency area or on the hard shoulder.** They can monitor your vehicle via CCTV and may provide a "standard recovery" to a safer location if you are causing an obstruction.

5. **Call your breakdown recovery provider or a specialist auto locksmith.** **Inform them clearly that you have "lost keys" or a "lockout" and specify if the vehicle is in a hazardous location.**
   - Ask for a "total price" including call-out, VAT, and programming to avoid "drip pricing" fees.
   - Verify that the locksmith can provide "on-site transponder programming" for your specific vehicle make and year.

6. **Gather the necessary documentation to prove ownership.** **You will typically need to show your V5C logbook (or a digital copy / photo), a form of photo ID (such as a driving licence), and proof of address.** Most reputable locksmiths and breakdown providers are legally required to verify these before opening the vehicle or cutting new keys.

7. **Report the keys as stolen only if there is evidence of theft.** **If your bag was snatched or the keys were taken from a table, call 101 or report it via the local police force website to get a crime reference number.** For "lost" property, police usually redirect you to private reporting services like "Report My Loss," which is often required for insurance claims.

8. **Notify a trusted contact of your situation and ETA.** **Send your location pin and the name of the recovery company you are waiting for to a friend or family member.** If it is dark or you are in an isolated area, stay on the phone with them until help arrives if safe to do so.

## What can wait
- You do not need to contact the vehicle manufacturer or main dealership immediately; an auto locksmith is usually faster and cheaper for roadside assistance.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of a replacement locks/barrels right now; most situations only require a new programmed key.
- You do not need to decide on a permanent spare key strategy while you are still stranded.

## Important reassurance
Losing your keys while out is a high-stress event, but it is a routine mechanical recovery for professionals. Auto locksmiths are equipped to generate new keys from the vehicle's computer even without the original present. Once you have made contact with help and are in a safe spot, the most dangerous part of the situation has passed.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization for being stranded due to lost keys in the UK. It does not cover the technical steps of key programming or long-term insurance claim processes.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice. In any roadside emergency, follow the instructions of the police, National Highways officers, or recovery professionals. Safety on high-speed roads is governed by the Highway Code; always prioritise reaching a place of relative safety.
