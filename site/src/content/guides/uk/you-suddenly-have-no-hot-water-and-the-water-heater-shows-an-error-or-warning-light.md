# What to do if… you suddenly have no hot water and the water heater shows an error or warning light (UK)

## Short answer
If you smell gas, hear a carbon monoxide (CO) alarm, or notice soot marks or yellow flames, immediately evacuate the property and call the National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999. If there are no safety signs, photograph the error code for a Gas Safe engineer and do not attempt to dismantle the appliance.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep resetting the appliance if it fails again after one attempt.
- Do not remove any covers, panels, or sealed casings.
- Do not attempt to adjust internal components or the gas valve.
- Do not ignore physical symptoms like sudden headaches, nausea, or dizziness while the boiler is running.
- Do not use a person who is not on the Gas Safe Register to inspect or repair a gas appliance.
- Do not use electrical switches or naked flames if a gas leak is suspected.

## What to do now
1. **Check for immediate sensory danger signs that require evacuation.**
   - Look for soot, scorching, or black/brown marks on the boiler casing.
   - Observe the pilot light if visible; a yellow or orange flame instead of blue indicates a serious fault.
   - Note any unusual smells, such as a "rotten egg" gas smell or a metallic burning odour.
   - Check for heavy, unusual condensation on nearby windows or walls.

2. **Evacuate and call for help if any danger signs are present.**
   - **Smell gas or CO alarm?** Get everyone into fresh air immediately.
   - **Call the National Gas Emergency Service: 0800 111 999.**
   - **Turn off the gas at the meter** only if you know where the lever is and it is safe to reach.
   - **Do not** turn any lights or electrical appliances on or off during evacuation.

3. **Capture the fault information for a professional.**
   - **Take a clear photograph of the digital display showing the error code or the pattern of flashing lights.**
   - **Find the data plate on the appliance and photograph the make, model, and serial number.**
   - **Note if any recent events occurred,** such as a power cut, extremely cold weather, or work on the water mains.

4. **Verify the external supplies without using tools.**
   - **Check your consumer unit (fuse box)** to see if a circuit breaker for the boiler or immersion heater has tripped.
   - **Check other gas appliances,** such as a gas hob, to see if the gas supply to the property is active (only if you do not smell gas).
   - **Observe the pressure gauge;** if it is below 1.0 bar on a combi boiler, note the exact reading but do not attempt to repressurise the system unless you have been trained to use the filling loop.

5. **Arrange a professional repair through the correct channels.**
   - **Tenants:** contact your landlord or letting agent immediately to report a "Total loss of hot water with error code" and provide the photos you took.
   - **Homeowners:** search the Gas Safe Register for a local qualified engineer for gas boilers, or a qualified electrician for immersion heaters.
   - **Request an emergency call-out** if the loss of hot water or heating poses a risk to vulnerable residents (elderly, infants, or those with medical conditions).

6. **Perform a single reset only if the manual allows it.**
   - **Locate the reset button** on the front control panel (refer to the manufacturer's website if the manual is missing).
   - **Press the reset once;** if the error returns immediately or within minutes, stop and wait for an engineer.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify what the specific error code means or buy any replacement parts yourself.
- You do not need to decide between repairing or replacing the unit until a qualified professional has performed a physical diagnostic.
- You can wait to adjust timers, smart thermostats, or heating schedules until the core system is verified as safe.

## Important reassurance
Boilers are designed with "lockout" safety mechanisms that automatically shut the system down when a fault is detected to prevent a dangerous escalation. While having no hot water is disruptive, the error light indicates the safety system is functioning as intended.

## Scope note
This guide covers first-step stabilization and hazard recognition only. It does not provide instructions for mechanical repairs, repressurising systems, or electrical testing, all of which require specific tools and certification.

## Important note
This information is for safety awareness and does not replace the advice of the emergency services or a Gas Safe registered engineer. Under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998, it is illegal for an unqualified person to perform work on gas appliances in the UK.
