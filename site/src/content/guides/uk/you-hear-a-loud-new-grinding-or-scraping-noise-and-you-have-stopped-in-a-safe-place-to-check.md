# What to do if… you hear a loud new grinding or scraping noise and you have stopped in a safe place to check (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the vehicle in a safe location and conduct a visual external check for dragging components or debris. If the cause is not visible or involves safety systems like brakes or wheels, do not continue driving and request professional recovery.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue driving at speed if the noise is loud, persistent, or accompanied by changes in handling.
- Do not place any part of your body underneath the vehicle while it is at the roadside.
- Do not attempt roadside repairs or debris removal on a motorway or high-speed road.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway.
- Do not touch wheels, brake discs, or exhaust components due to high temperature risks.
- Do not stand between your vehicle and oncoming traffic or on the traffic-facing side of the car.
- Do not accept assistance from unauthorized or unverified recovery vehicles.

## What to do now
1. **Secure the vehicle and occupants.** Activate hazard warning lights immediately. If you have stopped in a location that puts you at risk of collision (e.g., a live lane, narrow shoulder, or blind bend), call **999** or **112** and state that you are a "broken-down vehicle in a dangerous position."
2. **Contact the relevant highway authority for your specific location.** 
   - In England, call National Highways on **0300 123 5000**.
   - In Scotland, call Traffic Scotland on **0800 028 1414**.
   - In Wales, call Traffic Wales on **0300 123 1213**.
   - If you are on a motorway, use the nearest emergency roadside telephone if available, as this automatically provides your exact location.
3. **Identify your exact location for responders.** Note the road number, direction of travel, and look for blue Driver Location Signs or small marker posts every 100 metres.
   - Provide the numbers and the letter (A or B) shown on the sign to pinpoint your side of the carriageway.
4. **Perform a safe visual inspection from the verge.** Use a torch to check the following from a position away from moving traffic:
   - **Underbody:** Look for dangling plastic shields, loose heat shields, or trapped debris like branches or litter.
   - **Wheels:** Check for visible objects stuck in the wheel rim or shredded tyre debris.
   - **Fluids:** Check for active leaks or puddles forming under the engine or wheel areas.
5. **Assess for immediate fire or chemical hazards.** If you smell burning, see smoke, or witness sparks, move all passengers well away from the vehicle, ideally behind a safety barrier and upwind. Call **999** or **112** immediately.
6. **Evaluate the vehicle's roadworthiness based on the noise type.** 
   - **Do not drive** if the noise is heavy metal-on-metal, if the steering pulls, or if the brake pedal feel has changed.
   - If the noise was caused by a clearly identified, non-critical item (e.g., a plastic trim piece) and you have secured it without going under the car, drive slowly to the nearest service area for a full inspection.
7. **Wait for assistance in a safe position.** If you are on a motorway or high-speed road, wait behind the safety barrier or up the embankment. Keep your sidelights on if visibility is poor or it is dark.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the specific mechanical part failure (e.g., wheel bearing vs. brake pad).
- You do not need to negotiate repair costs or choose a garage while at the roadside.
- You do not need to report the incident to your insurance provider until the vehicle is in a stable, off-road location.

## Important reassurance
Hearing a sudden, loud mechanical noise while driving is a high-stress event. Stopping to investigate is the standard safety protocol and prevents potential mechanical escalation or loss of control. Panic or confusion in this moment is a normal physiological response to an unexpected vehicle fault.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and safety guidance only. It does not replace a professional mechanical diagnostic. Any vehicle that has experienced a significant grinding or scraping noise must be inspected by a qualified technician before returning to normal use.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute professional mechanical or legal advice. If you are in a live lane of traffic or feel your life is in danger, treat the situation as an emergency and contact the emergency services immediately on 999 or 112. Highway rules and emergency contact numbers are subject to change by national transport authorities.
