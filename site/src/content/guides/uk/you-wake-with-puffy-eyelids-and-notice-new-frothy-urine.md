# What to do if… you wake with puffy eyelids and notice new frothy urine (UK)

## Short answer
Get urgent medical advice today: contact your GP for an urgent appointment or use the correct urgent-care route for where you are in the UK. Call 999 now if you have breathing trouble, chest pain, severe allergic-type swelling, confusion, or you are passing very little urine and feel very unwell.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore new puffy eyelids with new frothy urine if it is unusual for you, especially if it persists, worsens, or comes with swelling elsewhere.
- Do not take NSAIDs such as ibuprofen, naproxen or diclofenac unless a clinician has told you they are safe for you.
- Do not start water tablets or diuretics, including leftover prescriptions, to reduce swelling.
- Do not suddenly force lots of fluids or severely restrict fluids or salt in a panic.
- Do not drive yourself to emergency care if you feel faint, very unwell, confused, or short of breath.

## What to do now
1. **Check for “call 999 now” red flags.** Call 999 if you have:
   - severe difficulty breathing, gasping, choking, chest pain, or you cannot speak in full sentences
   - sudden swelling of your lips, mouth, tongue or throat, wheezing, a tight throat, trouble swallowing, or widespread raised itchy rash
   - blue, grey or pale lips, tongue or skin; on brown or black skin, check the palms of the hands or soles of the feet
   - sudden confusion, severe drowsiness, fainting, or you cannot be woken normally
   - very little urine and you feel severely unwell

2. **If it is not a 999 emergency, get urgent advice today.**
   - In England, use NHS 111 online or call 111, or contact your GP surgery and ask for urgent same-day advice.
   - In Scotland, phone NHS 24 on 111 if your GP surgery is closed or you think you may need urgent care but it is not life-threatening.
   - In Wales, use NHS 111 Wales online or call 111, or contact your GP surgery.
   - In Northern Ireland, contact your GP surgery or GP out-of-hours service. If you think you need urgent or emergency care but it is not immediately life-threatening, use your local Phone First route.
   - Say clearly: “I woke with puffy eyelids and I have new frothy urine.”

3. **If you are pregnant or recently gave birth, say so immediately.**
   - Tell the midwife, maternity unit, labour ward, GP, or urgent-care service about the swollen face or eyelids and frothy urine, even if you are not sure it is related.
   - Also mention any headache, vision changes, pain below the ribs, vomiting, feeling very unwell, or swelling of the hands or feet.

4. **Check what is changing, without delaying urgent advice.**
   - Look for swelling in your ankles, feet, lower legs, fingers, hands or belly.
   - Notice whether rings, shoes or waistbands feel suddenly tight.
   - Check whether the urine is frothy every time, tea-coloured, blood-coloured, or much less than usual.
   - If you already have a home blood-pressure cuff, take a reading and write down the time. Do not delay contacting urgent care to find a blood-pressure check.

5. **Gather the details a clinician is likely to ask for.**
   - When the eyelid puffiness and frothy urine started.
   - Whether the froth is persistent or happened once.
   - Any fever, sore throat, recent infection, rash, joint pain, breathlessness, tiredness, or swelling elsewhere.
   - Any new medicines, antibiotics, supplements, NSAID painkillers, or recent heavy exercise.
   - Any kidney problems, diabetes, high blood pressure, autoimmune disease, or previous abnormal urine tests.

6. **Ask what checks you may need.**
   - Ask whether you need a urine test for protein and blood, a blood test for kidney function, a blood-pressure check, or any other checks they recommend.
   - If symptoms are getting worse while you wait, contact the urgent-care service again or call 999 if emergency symptoms develop.

7. **Use a safe holding pattern until you are assessed.**
   - Drink normally; do not force fluids.
   - Avoid alcohol for now.
   - Avoid very salty foods for now.
   - Rest, and remove tight rings or jewellery if swelling is increasing.
   - If eyelid swelling is uncomfortable, keep your head slightly raised when resting.

## What can wait
- You do not need to work out whether this is kidney-related, allergy-related, pregnancy-related, or something else right now.
- You do not need to start a special kidney diet, detox, supplement plan, or exercise plan today.
- You do not need to collect multiple urine samples at home unless a clinician asks you to.
- You do not need to decide on long-term treatment before you have been checked.

## Important reassurance
Puffy eyelids with frothy urine can feel frightening, and it is sensible to take it seriously. The useful next step is not to diagnose yourself, but to get prompt advice and basic checks so serious causes are not missed and you avoid risky self-treatment.

## Scope note
These are first steps only: they are for getting you safely assessed and avoiding common mistakes in the first few hours. Later decisions may need help from your GP, urgent-care service, maternity team, renal specialist, or another appropriate clinician.

## Important note
This guide is general information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice, and it is not a diagnosis or a substitute for urgent care when symptoms are severe or worsening.
