# What to do if… you see water dripping from a ceiling light fitting or recessed light (UK)

## Short answer
Isolate the electricity supply at the consumer unit immediately if it is safe to do so. Stay away from the wet area and contact a qualified professional to stop the leak and inspect the wiring.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the light fitting, bulb, switch, or the dripping water.
- Do not stand on a wet floor or use a metal ladder while power is live.
- Do not attempt to "drain" the light by poking tools into the fitting.
- Do not turn the light back on to see if it still works after the dripping stops.
- Do not reset a tripped circuit breaker (RCD) if it has already shut off.
- Do not stand directly under a sagging or bulging ceiling.

## What to do now
1. **Move all people and pets away from the affected room.** If you see smoke, sparking, or a bulging ceiling that looks "heavy" with water, evacuate the room and close the door behind you.
2. **Switch off the electricity at the consumer unit.**
   - **Only do this if the consumer unit is dry and you can reach it without standing in water.**
   - Switch off the specific circuit labelled "Lights" or "Lighting."
   - If the circuits are not clearly labelled, switch off the "Main Switch" to cut all power to the property.
3. **Contact emergency electrical help if you cannot isolate the power.**
   - If you cannot safely reach the consumer unit, call an emergency electrician.
   - You can also call **105** (free from landlines and mobiles) to reach the local electricity network operator for safety advice. In Northern Ireland, call **03457 643 643**.
4. **Isolate the water source if it is safe and accessible.**
   - Turn the main stopcock (usually found under the kitchen sink or under the stairs) clockwise to shut off the water supply.
   - If the leak is from a flat above, alert the occupant and ask them to stop using all plumbing immediately.
5. **Contain the water at floor level without approaching the fixture.**
   - Place a bucket or basin on the floor directly under the drip to prevent further floor damage.
   - Use towels to soak up water on the floor, but only if the electricity is confirmed as off.
6. **Report the emergency if you are a tenant.**
   - Contact your landlord, housing association, or letting agent immediately. 
   - State that there is an "emergency electrical hazard involving water ingress through a light fitting."
7. **Arrange for professional inspections.**
   - A plumber must fix the leak source before an electrician can begin work.
   - A qualified electrician must inspect the fitting and the wiring in the ceiling void before power is restored to that circuit.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact pipe or roof tile that is leaking right now.
- You do not need to clean the light fitting or remove the bulb.
- You do not need to decide who will pay for the repairs during the initial stabilization.
- You do not need to start drying the ceiling with heaters or hair dryers.

## Important reassurance
Water entering an electrical system is a serious but manageable situation once the power is isolated. Taking the step to stay back and cut the supply removes the primary risk of shock or fire. It is normal to feel overwhelmed by the sight of water near electrics; following these steps stabilizes the environment.

## Scope note
This guide is for first-step stabilization only. Professional assessment by a registered electrician and a plumber is required to resolve the underlying faults and ensure the property is safe for continued use.

## Important note
This guide is for general information and does not replace professional trade advice. If you see smoke, flames, or persistent sparking, call **999** immediately and evacuate. Electrical work in the UK must comply with BS 7671 (Wiring Regulations).
