# What to do if… your passport includes a middle name but your booking does not and staff say it is an issue (UK)

## Short answer
Do not buy a new ticket on the spot just because someone says it could be an issue. Ask the airline to check the live booking record and either confirm that your first name and surname are enough, or record a same-person name correction if they say one is needed.

## Do not do these things
- Do not buy a replacement ticket before you have asked the airline to check its own rule for this booking.
- Do not try to change the traveller to someone else; that is different from a same-person correction and may be refused or charged.
- Do not add the middle name into the wrong field, such as the surname field.
- Do not rely only on how the boarding pass prints; ask staff to check what is stored in the booking record.
- Do not ignore it if check-in, gate, or airline staff explicitly flag the mismatch.
- Do not hand over money for a new booking without asking what exact rule requires it and whether a correction is possible.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a clearer pause.** If you are at the airport, step slightly aside but stay near the desk or queue so you can focus and still be helped.

2. **Check that the only difference is the missing middle name.** Compare your passport photo page with the booking, e-ticket, or app passenger details. Focus on:
   - first or given name
   - middle name
   - surname or family name
   - spelling order

3. **Ask the airline the direct question.** Say: “My first name and surname match my passport. My passport has a middle name and the booking does not. Is that acceptable for this flight, or do you need to correct the booking record?”

4. **Ask them to check the booking record, not only the printed boarding pass.** If they say it may be a problem, ask: “Can you add the middle name as a same-person name correction, or add a note confirming that the first name and surname match the passport?”

5. **Find out who can make the correction.** If you booked through a travel agent, online travel site, or package provider, ask the airline: “Can you edit this ticket now, or does the original booking agent need to do it?” Then contact the party that controls the booking and ask for written confirmation of any correction or advice.

6. **Keep simple proof together.** Keep your physical passport ready, save the booking confirmation, and screenshot any airline or agent message saying the missing middle name is acceptable or showing that a correction has been made.

7. **If you are told to rebook, slow the decision down.** Ask for the exact reason in writing, or write down the staff member’s name, desk or gate, time, and what they said. Keep receipts if you pay because of the issue; any complaint or claim can wait until after the immediate travel problem is stable.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now how to enter middle names on every future booking.
- You do not need to argue about what should be acceptable in general.
- You do not need to start a complaint, refund request, chargeback, or passenger-rights claim now; those can wait until you know whether you were actually refused travel, charged, or delayed, and whether a claim applies.
- You do not need to contact the passport office unless the passport itself is wrong.

## Important reassurance
A missing middle name is a common booking mismatch. Several airlines say they do not require middle names when the first name and surname match the travel document, but the safest immediate step is still to get today’s booking checked by the airline or the agent that controls it.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the risk of check-in or boarding problems today. Airline rules, ticket-control rules, and route document checks can vary, so later decisions may need help from the airline, booking agent, travel insurer, card provider, or a passenger-rights adviser.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, financial, travel-agent, or professional advice. Airline staff may apply document and security checks close to departure, so if staff say the name could be an issue, treat it as a cue to get the booking formally checked or corrected rather than guessing.
