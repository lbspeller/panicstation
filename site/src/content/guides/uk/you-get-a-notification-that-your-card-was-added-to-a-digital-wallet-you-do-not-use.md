# What to do if… you get a notification that your card was added to a digital wallet you do not use (UK)

## Short answer
Do not approve the request or click any links; immediately freeze your card in your banking app and contact your bank’s fraud department using the number on the back of your card or 159.

## Do not do these things
- Do not tap links in the notification, email, or text to "secure" the account.
- Do not call any phone number provided within the suspicious message.
- Do not share one-time passcodes (OTPs), verification codes, or in-app approval prompts with anyone, even those claiming to be from your bank or the police.
- Do not continue using the card; digital wallet "tokens" allow immediate spending even if you still have the physical card.
- Do not hand your physical card to anyone, including "couriers" claiming to be from your bank.
- Do not ignore the alert even if no money has left your account yet.

## What to do now
1. **Isolate the potential hazard by ignoring the notification.**
   - Close the message or alert immediately.
   - Do not interact with any "Decline" or "Approve" buttons inside a text or email, as these may be phishing links.

2. **Freeze or lock your card using your official banking app.**
   - **Open your banking app manually** (do not use a link).
   - **Locate the "Cards" or "Security" section.**
   - **Select the "Freeze" or "Block" option** for the card mentioned in the alert.

3. **Verify the request through trusted channels only.**
   - Check your banking app's "Activity" or "Messages" hub for genuine "Card added" or "Provisioning" alerts.
   - Look for any "Pending" transactions you do not recognise.

4. **Contact your bank's fraud department immediately.**
   - Call the **number on the back of your card**.
   - Alternatively, dial **159** (the secure shortcut for most UK banks).
   - Tell the agent: "I received an alert that my card was added to a digital wallet I do not use."
   - Specifically ask them to **"Delete all digital wallet tokens"** associated with that card.

5. **Report the suspicious message.**
   - Forward any suspicious text messages to **7726** (free of charge).
   - Forward suspicious emails to **report@phishing.gov.uk**.

6. **Secure your associated digital accounts.**
   - If the alert mentioned Apple Pay, change your **Apple ID password** via official settings.
   - If the alert mentioned Google Pay, change your **Google Account password** via official settings.
   - Check "Signed in devices" in these accounts and remove any you do not recognise.

7. **File an official fraud report.**
   - **England, Wales, and Northern Ireland:** Report to the **Report Fraud** service (formerly Action Fraud) online at reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **Scotland:** Report to **Police Scotland** by calling **101**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to replace your mobile handset or hardware immediately.
- You do not need to contact Apple or Google support before speaking to your bank; the bank is the primary authority for card security.
- You do not need to determine exactly how your details were leaked before taking protective action.

## Important reassurance
Receiving this notification means your bank's security systems are functioning and have alerted you to an attempt to link your card. By freezing the card and requesting a "token removal" from your bank, you effectively cut off the fraudster's access to your funds before they can spend them.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a digital wallet alert. It does not cover long-term identity theft recovery or the specifics of disputing transactions that have already cleared.

## Important note
This information is for immediate crisis stabilization and does not constitute financial or legal advice. Always follow the specific security instructions provided by your bank's official fraud team.
