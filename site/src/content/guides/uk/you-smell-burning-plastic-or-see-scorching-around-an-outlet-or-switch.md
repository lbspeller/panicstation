# What to do if… you smell burning plastic or see scorching around an outlet or switch (UK)

## Short answer
Treat these signs as an immediate electrical fire risk: **isolate the power at the consumer unit (fuse box) if it is safe to do so and stop using the affected outlet or switch**. If you see smoke or active flames, **evacuate the building immediately and call 999**.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep testing the outlet or switch by turning it on and off or plugging devices back in.
- Do not touch a hot faceplate, melted plastic, or exposed internal wires.
- Do not remove the faceplate or insert any tools or objects into the outlet.
- Do not use water or wet cloths to cool the area or extinguish sparks.
- Do not attempt a DIY repair or tighten loose screws on a scorched accessory.
- Do not ignore the smell even if it stops temporarily; the underlying fault remains.

## What to do now
1. **Identify immediate fire indicators.** If you observe smoke, sustained sparking, or visible flames, **get everyone out of the building, close doors behind you, and call 999**.
2. **Isolate the electricity to the affected circuit.** Locate your **consumer unit** and flip the switch to **OFF for the specific circuit** (usually labelled as “Sockets” or “Lights”).
   - If the labels are unclear or missing, flip the **Main Switch to the OFF position** to cut all power to the property.
3. **Leave the circuit breaker or RCD in the OFF position.** If a protective device has tripped automatically, do not attempt to reset it until an electrician has inspected the system.
4. **Unplug devices only if they are cool and the power is isolated.** If a plug is melted into the socket or feels excessively hot, **leave it in place** and ensure the power remains off at the consumer unit.
5. **Move suspected faulty appliances to a safe surface.** If the heat or smell is linked to a specific device (like a charger or heater), keep it unplugged and place it on a **dry, non-combustible, non-conductive surface** such as a stone floor or ceramic tile away from curtains or paper.
6. **Report the fault to the relevant party.**
   - If you rent, contact your landlord or letting agent’s **emergency repair line** immediately.
   - If you own the property, contact a **registered electrician** (look for NICEIC, NAPIT, or SELECT certification) for an urgent inspection.
7. **Contact the network operator for supply-side issues.** If the burning smell or heat is coming from the consumer unit itself, the electricity meter, or the incoming service cable:
   - In Great Britain, **call 105**.
   - In Northern Ireland, **call 03457 643 643**.
8. **Document the damage from a distance.** Once the power is isolated and the area is stable, take a photograph of the scorching or melted plastic to show the electrician or landlord.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the issue is a loose connection, an overloaded circuit, or old wiring.
- You do not need to clear the consumer unit of other circuits; keeping non-affected circuits off is often safer until a professional arrives.
- You do not need to decide on permanent replacement parts or faceplate styles now.

## Important reassurance
Panic or hesitation is a common reaction when smelling burning or seeing scorch marks. These are significant safety cues, and taking the step to isolate the power is the most effective way to prevent an electrical fire from developing.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for overheating electrical accessories. It does not replace a professional Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) or technical repair by a qualified person.

## Important note
This information is for safety stabilization and does not constitute professional electrical or legal advice. If at any point you see smoke or fire, or if you feel the situation is escalating, evacuate and call 999.
