# What to do if… a car-share app will not let you end your trip and charges keep running (UK)

## Short answer
Park the car safely and legally, then contact the provider straight away and ask them to end the trip from their side. Keep screenshots, photos, and times before you leave the vehicle.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave the car somewhere unsafe, illegal, or outside the provider’s return rules just because the app is failing.
- Do not keep driving around for signal if the car is already in a safe legal return place.
- Do not assume locking the car has ended the trip.
- Do not delete the app, clear app data, or wipe messages before saving screenshots and trip details.
- Do not start a new booking, extend the trip, or make a second payment to reset the app unless the provider tells you to.

## What to do now
1. **Park safely and as close as possible to the provider’s return rules.**  
   Use the booked bay, home bay, return zone, or app return instructions if you can. Avoid yellow lines, loading restrictions, disabled bays without entitlement, blocked access, private spaces without permission, and places that would create a penalty or obstruction.

2. **Secure the car before troubleshooting the app.**  
   Put the vehicle in park or neutral as appropriate, switch off the engine, apply the handbrake, remove or return the key as the provider instructs, and check doors and windows before you leave the vehicle unattended.

3. **Try one short end-trip reset, then stop looping.**  
   Check that the app shows the correct return area. Try stable mobile data or Wi-Fi, force-close and reopen the app, then press the provider’s end-trip or lock-and-end option once more. If the provider offers an in-car reader, fob, key holder, or other official end method, try that too.

4. **Collect proof while the trip is still showing as active.**  
   Take screenshots of the active trip screen, error messages, the app location or return-zone screen, and your phone time. Take photos of the parked car, bay or zone signs, nearby street signs, dashboard fuel or charge level if relevant, and any confirmation that the vehicle is locked.

5. **Contact the provider immediately and ask them to close the trip.**  
   Use the in-app call or help route first if it is available. If that fails, use the provider’s official phone, chat, web form, or email route. Say: **“I am parked and ready to end the trip, but the app will not let me. Please close the trip from your side and confirm the end time.”**

6. **Ask for a written record.**  
   Ask for a case number, the end time they will apply, and confirmation that charges after that time will be reviewed. If you speak by phone, send a short follow-up message through chat, web form, or email with the time, location, vehicle registration, and screenshots.

7. **If you have to leave before support answers, leave a clear trail.**  
   Lock the vehicle exactly as the service instructs, take a final photo, and send support a message saying where the car is, when you stopped using it, that the app would not end the trip, and that you have evidence.

8. **If extra charges appear, dispute them in writing.**  
   Send the provider one clear message with your timeline, screenshots, photos, support case number, and the refund or correction you want for the period after the car was returned. If the provider does not resolve it, use its formal complaint process; if it is a BVRLA member, BVRLA ADR may be available after that process is exhausted. You can also ask your card provider about chargeback or Section 75 where it applies. Section 75 usually depends on a credit card purchase over £100 and up to £30,000, and eligibility can depend on how the payment was made.

## What can wait
- You do not need to argue about blame while the trip is still active.
- You do not need to decide whether to keep using the service.
- You do not need to post publicly or chase social media before you have saved evidence and contacted the official support route.
- You do not need to work out every complaint option now; first get the end time recorded.

## Important reassurance
An app failure at the end of a car-share trip is stressful, but the immediate job is simple: park safely, secure the vehicle, save proof, and contact support. A clear timeline usually gives you a better chance of correcting charges later.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stop ongoing charges, protect the vehicle, and preserve evidence. Later complaints, card disputes, or reimbursement claims may need specialist consumer, financial, or legal help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, insurance, or professional advice. Provider rules, payment protections, parking rules, and complaint routes can differ, so use the provider’s official instructions and get specialist help if the dispute continues.
