# What to do if… your boarding pass shows the wrong nationality or travel document details (UK)

## Short answer
Do not rebook in a panic. Take the correct passport or travel document to the airline’s staffed check-in desk, bag drop, document check desk, or official support channel and ask them to update your travel-document details for this journey.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore a wrong nationality, document type, passport number, or expiry date if the airline’s travel-document record may be wrong.
- Do not enter made-up details, a different nationality, or the wrong document type just to get past an online form.
- Do not assume the boarding pass text proves which document you can travel on.
- Do not cancel and rebook unless the airline tells you the booking cannot be corrected.
- Do not send passport screenshots through unofficial messages or give your passport to anyone who is not airline staff, airport security, or a border officer.

## What to do now
1. **Pause changes in the app or website.** Keep the current boarding pass, booking reference, and any confirmation emails available.
2. **Work out the exact mismatch.** Check whether the problem is the nationality, document type, passport number, expiry date, or the wrong passport being attached to the journey.
3. **Use the airline’s official route first.** Open the airline app, website, or official help channel and look for “passenger details”, “travel documents”, “document check”, “visa check”, or “Advance Passenger Information”.
4. **Go to a staffed airline point early if you are at the airport or cannot fix it online.** Say: “My boarding pass or passenger details show the wrong nationality or document type. Please check and update the travel-document details for this journey.”
5. **Have the real documents ready.** Keep together the passport or travel document you will use, any visa or permit proof for the destination, and any extra proof the airline may need to check your permission to travel.
6. **If you use an eVisa or online UK immigration status, check the passport linked to it.** Sign in to your account if you can, make sure the passport you will travel with is added or current, and get a share code if you may need to show your status. If the account will not let you update it, use the official UKVI help route rather than guessing.
7. **If you hold more than one nationality, be clear which passport you are travelling on.** Show the airline the passport you want registered for this flight and ask them to check that the destination rules are being applied to that document.
8. **If you have a connection or separate ticket, repeat the check with each airline.** A document correction with one carrier may not automatically update another airline’s record.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim costs, or change your future travel profile.
- You do not need to rebuild your whole airline account before departure; the urgent task is the document record for this journey.
- You do not need to print a new boarding pass at home if the airline can issue or refresh one through its official route.

## Important reassurance
This is often a check-in data problem rather than a new travel problem. The safest next step is to get the airline to check the real document against the journey requirements before you reach the gate.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Airline systems, destination entry rules, eVisas, residence permits, dual nationality, and one-way travel can need airline or official immigration help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Use the airline’s official document-check route and official travel advice for the country you are travelling to.
