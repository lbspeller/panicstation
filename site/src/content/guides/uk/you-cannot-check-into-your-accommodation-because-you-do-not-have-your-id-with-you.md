# What to do if… you cannot check into your accommodation because you do not have your ID with you (UK)

## Short answer
Get to a safe, staffed place, then contact the property and your booking provider while you gather any identity proof you can access. Keep the booking open until you know whether the property, platform, or insurer needs the record.

## Do not do these things
- Do not leave your passport or ID as a deposit, and ask for any copying or scanning to happen in your sight where possible.
- Do not share full card details over the phone or by message; use official booking-platform messaging or the property’s published contact details.
- Do not get into a stranger’s car or go to a second location to sort it out unless it is an official, safe place you chose.
- Do not escalate into a confrontation at the desk; you need staff cooperation and time to unlock options.
- Do not cancel the booking in panic if you may need your booking record for support, insurance, or a card dispute.

## What to do now
1. **Stabilise your position first.** Stay in the reception, lobby, or another public, staffed area. If you feel unsafe outside, ask staff if you can wait inside while you make calls and pull up documents.
2. **Ask for their exact policy and what they can accept tonight.** Calmly ask: “What can you accept if my passport or ID is temporarily inaccessible?” Some properties may be able to accept a different original ID, a photo or scan, booking-platform verification, or manager approval, but others may not have discretion.
3. **Pull together any proof you can access right now:**
   - Booking confirmation email or reservation number.
   - The payment card used for the booking, if you have it.
   - Any other photo ID, such as a UK driving licence, photo work ID, or photo student ID, if you have one.
   - A digital copy of your passport or ID from email, cloud storage, or your booking account.
   - Any message from the property confirming your name, dates, and room.
4. **If your ID is inside the room or building, ask for a manager.** Ask whether staff can escort you to retrieve it, or whether they can verify you through the booking platform, payment card, or another accessible document first.
5. **Ask the booking platform for real-time help, if you booked through one.** Use in-app chat or call support and ask them to confirm the booking and guest name to the property, resend access instructions, or help relocate you if the property will not admit you. Keep communication in-platform where possible.
6. **If your phone holds the only accessible ID, prioritise power and account access.** Ask reception for a charger, use a public charging point you trust, or ask booking support to resend documents to an email account you can access on another device.
7. **If your passport or ID is lost or stolen, not just inaccessible, take the document route.** If you are abroad, report the loss to local police where appropriate and ask for a reference or report if they will give one. Use the official GOV.UK service to cancel a lost or stolen UK passport, unless you are applying for a UK emergency travel document, in which case the cancellation is handled as part of that application.
8. **If you need to travel soon without your UK passport, check the emergency travel document route.** GOV.UK says this is generally for British nationals abroad who need urgent travel and cannot use or replace their UK passport before travelling. Check the official page before relying on it.
9. **If you need somewhere to sleep tonight, check ID rules before paying.** Ask any alternative accommodation what ID they require before you book. Prefer somewhere with a staffed reception so you can explain the situation before paying.
10. **Protect your records.** Save screenshots or photos of the refusal, any ID-required message, support chats, call logs, and receipts for extra costs such as taxis or alternative accommodation.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to complain, leave a review, or pursue compensation.
- You do not need to cancel other bookings or rebook the whole trip in the moment.
- You do not need to start complex document replacement steps tonight unless you must travel soon or the document is lost or stolen.
- You do not need to explain everything perfectly; focus on getting one safe place to wait and one live route for help.

## Important reassurance
A refusal at the desk is often about policy, local requirements, or staff limits, not a judgement about you. Your job right now is to stay safe, get the right decision-maker or booking support involved, and keep your options open.

## Scope note
These are first steps to get you safely housed and buy time. Later decisions about refunds, complaints, insurance, document replacement, or travel changes may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information for urgent first actions, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, professional, or travel advice. Requirements for guest identification vary by country, property type, booking route, and local rules, and staff may have limited discretion.
