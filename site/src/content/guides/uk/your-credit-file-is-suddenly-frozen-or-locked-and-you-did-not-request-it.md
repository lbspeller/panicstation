# What to do if… your credit file is suddenly frozen or locked and you did not request it (UK)

## Short answer
Obtain your statutory credit reports from Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion immediately to identify the source of the restriction. Contact the specific agency or lender that applied the lock to determine if it was triggered by a security setting, identity mismatch, or a fraud-prevention marker.

## Do not do these things
- Don't submit multiple new credit applications to "test" if the lock has been removed.
- Don't follow links in unsolicited emails or texts claiming to "fix" or "unlock" your file.
- Don't pay third-party "credit repair" services that promise to remove fraud markers.
- Don't share identity documents with any contact details provided in an unexpected phone call.

## What to do now
1. **Identify which credit reference agency is reporting the lock.**  
   Ask the lender or organization that notified you of the restriction exactly which agency they checked.
   - Request that they pause your application rather than rejecting it outright while you investigate the file.
   - Note the exact terminology used, such as "file suppressed," "password required," or "identity not verified."

2. **Request your free statutory credit report from all three main UK agencies.**  
   Lenders often check different agencies, and a lock on one may not appear on others.
   - Use the official statutory report portals for Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion.
   - Review each report for unauthorized "hard searches," accounts you do not recognize, or linked addresses that are incorrect.

3. **Check for fraud-prevention markers or protective registration notices.**  
   Look for a "CIFAS" section or any "Victim of Impersonation" or "Victim of Takeover" entries.
   - If a marker is present, identify the "filing member" (the bank or lender that added it).
   - If you did not request a "Protective Registration" but one is active, treat the situation as an account takeover.

4. **Contact the agency's security or fraud team directly to query the restriction.**  
   State clearly that you did not authorize a lock or freeze on your personal data.
   - Request the specific date the restriction was applied and the method used to apply it (e.g., via a personal online account or a lender request).
   - Ask for a "passkey" or unique reference if the lock is part of an enhanced security feature.

5. **Lodge a formal dispute if the lock is caused by an identity mismatch.**  
   Check that your name, date of birth, and electoral register status are identical across all three reports.
   - Provide the agency with evidence of your current address, such as a utility bill or council tax statement, if the lock was triggered by an "unverified address" flag.
   - Use the agency’s online dispute resolution tool to flag incorrect data entries.

6. **Report suspected identity fraud to the national reporting centers.**  
   Keep all crime reference numbers provided for your correspondence with lenders.
   - **England, Wales, and Northern Ireland:** Report via reportfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040.
   - **Scotland:** Report to Police Scotland by calling 101.

7. **Contact the fraud department of any lender listed on your report that you do not recognize.**  
   Inform them that the application or account is fraudulent and request they update the credit reference agencies.
   - Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, you must follow the organization's internal complaints process first before escalating to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

8. **Consider Cifas Protective Registration if your data has been compromised.**  
   This service adds a flag to your file for 2 years (costing £30 as of 2026) that forces lenders to perform manual ID checks.
   - Only apply for this if you have confirmed that your personal details are being actively misused to apply for credit.

## What can wait
- You do not need to pay for a "premium" credit monitoring subscription immediately; statutory reports provide the necessary data for free.
- You do not need to worry about your numerical "credit score" until the underlying file errors or fraud markers are resolved.
- You do not need to notify the Financial Ombudsman Service until you have received a final response from the lender or agency involved.

## Important reassurance
A "locked" status is often an automated safeguard triggered by a minor data discrepancy, such as an old address or a misspelling by a lender. It is a protective measure designed to prevent unauthorized access to your credit facilities. Following the verification steps across all three agencies will identify whether the issue is procedural or criminal.

## Scope note
This guide covers urgent stabilization and identification steps. Long-term correction of credit records or challenging fraud markers through the Financial Ombudsman Service requires following specific regulatory timelines.

## Important note
This information is for first-step stabilization and does not constitute legal or financial advice. Access rights to your credit data are governed by the Data Protection Act 2018 and the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025. Procedures for unlocking files vary by agency.
