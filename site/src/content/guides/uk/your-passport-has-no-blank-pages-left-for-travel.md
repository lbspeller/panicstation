# What to do if… your passport has no blank pages left for travel (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as a “do not travel until confirmed” problem. Check the blank-page rule for your destination, every transit point, and the airline’s document check; if acceptance is not clear, use an official renewal, urgent passport, or Emergency Travel Document route if eligible.

## Do not do these things
- Do not tear out pages, separate pages, remove staples, cover stamps, or alter the passport; this may make it unusable for travel or look tampered with.
- Do not assume you will be accepted because you travelled with the same passport before.
- Do not book or lock in non-refundable onward travel until you know what document you can use.
- Do not hand your passport to anyone offering unofficial “extra pages”, “stamp removal”, or a quick fix.
- Do not rely on a visa in an old passport until the destination, transit country, and airline have confirmed that this is accepted.

## What to do now
1. **Ask what exact rule is being applied.**  
   Ask the check-in agent, visa desk, or border official to identify the issue: for example, no page for an entry stamp, no full page for a visa sticker, or not enough blank pages for the route.

2. **Check the whole route, not just the final destination.**  
   Check the GOV.UK entry requirements for the destination and every transit country where your documents may be checked. Some countries ask for one blank page; others may ask for several.

3. **Ask the airline or travel provider for a clear boarding decision.**  
   Ask whether they will board you with the passport as it is. If they say yes, ask for the decision to be noted on your booking or given in writing, such as an email or chat transcript.

4. **If you are in the UK and travel is soon, use the official urgent passport route if you are eligible.**  
   Use GOV.UK “Get a passport urgently”. Do not use unofficial agents or third-party pages that are not needed to apply.

5. **If you can wait, renew the passport before travelling.**  
   A full passport is usually solved by getting a replacement passport. Do not try to add pages yourself or “make space”.

6. **If you are outside the UK and need urgent travel, check whether you can use a UK Emergency Travel Document.**  
   GOV.UK says this may be available to British nationals abroad who need to travel within 6 weeks, whose UK passport is full, and who cannot renew or replace it before travel. Before relying on it, check whether an Emergency Travel Document will be accepted by the countries you will leave, enter, and travel through.

7. **If a valid visa is in the full passport, check the visa rule before moving documents around.**  
   Some destinations may allow travel with the old passport containing the visa and the new passport for identity, but this is not universal. Check the destination’s rule and the airline’s document check before relying on two-passport travel.

## What can wait
- You do not need to solve every future trip today; focus on the next border crossing or flight.
- You do not need to argue at the desk if the rule is unclear; get the specific requirement, then choose an official document route.
- You do not need to decide whether to cancel the whole trip until you know whether renewal, urgent passport service, or an Emergency Travel Document is possible.
- You do not need to use every remaining bit of space in the passport; the safer aim is avoiding denied boarding or denied entry.

## Important reassurance
This is a common frequent-traveller problem, and it is usually fixable with the right official document route. The stressful part is the uncertainty; once the blank-page rule for your route is clear, the next step is usually renewal, an urgent passport service, or an Emergency Travel Document if you are abroad and eligible.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce the risk of denied boarding or denied entry. Passport, visa, airline, and border requirements can change, and later decisions may need help from HM Passport Office, the airline, the destination authority, or a travel specialist.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, immigration, financial, or professional travel advice. Airlines and border authorities may refuse travel based on document checks and entry requirements. If you cannot confirm acceptance for your route, assume you need an official replacement document before travelling.
