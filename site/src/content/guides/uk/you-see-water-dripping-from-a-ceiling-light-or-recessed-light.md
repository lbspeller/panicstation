# What to do if… you see water dripping from a ceiling light or recessed light (UK)

## Short answer
Treat this as an electrical danger. Keep away from the fitting and switch off the affected circuit, or the whole property, at the consumer unit only if you can do it without touching water or wet equipment.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the fitting, bulb, trim, switch, or any dripping water while the power may still be on.
- Do not stand on a wet floor or use a ladder under the leak to investigate.
- Do not poke anything into the fitting, remove a downlight, or try to drain it.
- Do not reset breakers or turn power back on to test it after the dripping stops.
- Do not use mains-powered appliances, extension leads, or a vacuum near the wet area.
- Do not ignore a small drip; water in or near electrics can be dangerous.

## What to do now
1. Move people and pets away from the area. If there is buzzing, sparking, smoke, a burning smell, or the ceiling looks swollen or sagging, leave the room immediately.
2. Switch off electricity safely.
   - If you can reach the consumer unit without going near water or standing on a wet floor, switch off the affected circuit.
   - If you cannot identify the circuit quickly, switch off the main switch for the property.
   - If the consumer unit or fuse box looks wet, damaged, hot, or you would be standing in water to reach it, do not touch it.
3. If you cannot safely isolate the power, get help immediately.
   - In England, Scotland, or Wales, call 105 to reach your local electricity network operator for electricity network safety concerns.
   - In Northern Ireland, call NIE Networks on 03457 643643 for electricity network faults or hazards.
   - If there is smoke, fire, flames, or ongoing arcing, get out and call 999, asking for the fire service.
4. Stop the water only if it is clearly safe.
   - If the leak is likely to be from plumbing above, turn off the water at the stop tap if you know where it is and can reach it without going near the wet electrics.
   - If someone is above you, ask them to stop using water now, including showers, baths, sinks, and flushing toilets, until the source is found.
5. Contain the water without approaching the fitting.
   - Put a bucket under the drip from a safe position.
   - Lay towels to protect flooring if you can do this without going under the wet fitting.
   - Do not climb up, open the ceiling, or remove the recessed light.
6. Arrange urgent repairs.
   - Contact a plumber or building repair service to find and stop the leak source.
   - Ask a registered electrician to inspect the affected circuit, fitting, and ceiling void before power is restored.
7. If you rent, report it as an emergency repair now.
   - Contact your landlord, letting agent, or housing repairs line.
   - Say: "Water is leaking through a ceiling light fitting. Power has been switched off for safety," or "The power cannot be safely switched off."

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today who is at fault.
- You do not need to argue about liability, insurance, or deposits while the light is still wet.
- You do not need to open ceilings, remove downlights, or dry wiring yourself.
- You do not need to restore power until a registered electrician says the affected electrics are safe.
- Photos and notes for insurance, your landlord, or your own records can wait until the immediate electrical and water risks are under control.

## Important reassurance
You are right to treat this seriously. Moving away, keeping hands off the fitting, isolating power if safe, and getting the leak and electrics checked are the stabilising steps.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Finding the leak path, checking hidden damage, repairing the ceiling, and confirming electrical safety may need a plumber, builder, landlord repair service, insurer, or registered electrician.

## Important note
This guide is general information for an urgent home safety situation, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, building, or other professional advice.
