# What to do if… your phone storage is full and it stops saving photos, videos, or messages (UK)

## Short answer
Stop repeated saving attempts, free some device storage using low-risk items first, then copy or back up the newest photos, videos, and messages before doing any big cleanup.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t factory reset, erase all content, or reinstall the operating system as a first step.
- Don’t delete lots of photos or videos in a panic before you have copied or backed up the recent ones you cannot replace.
- Don’t install random cleaner apps or give unknown apps access to your files, photos, or messages.
- Don’t assume iCloud storage or Google storage being full is the same thing as the phone’s own device storage.
- Don’t keep taking more photos or videos to test it until you have freed space; partial or duplicate files can make it harder to see what saved.
- Don’t clear an app’s storage or data unless you are prepared to lose that app’s local data.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safe pause and protect the moment.**  
   If something important is happening now, use another phone, a camera, or ask someone nearby to take photos or send you copies. If messages matter, forward key messages, photograph the screen with another device, or take screenshots only if they actually save.

2. **Check whether device storage or cloud storage is full.**  
   - **iPhone:** Settings → General → iPhone Storage.  
   - **Android:** Settings → Storage. Wording can vary by manufacturer and Android version.  
   If the device storage is at or very near full, focus on freeing space on the phone itself.

3. **Free space using the least risky items first.**  
   Try one or two of these, then test one photo or one message:
   - Delete downloads you can get again from the Files or Downloads folder.
   - Remove offline videos, music, podcasts, maps, or streaming downloads.
   - Uninstall one large app you do not need today.
   - Remove large files you have already copied somewhere else.
   - Empty Recently Deleted, Bin, or Trash only for items you have checked and are sure you can lose.

4. **Copy or back up the newest photos and videos before deeper cleanup.**  
   Choose one route you can do now:
   - If you already use iCloud Photos or Google Photos, connect to reliable Wi-Fi and power, then confirm the newest items are visible in iCloud, Google Photos, or the web version before deleting device copies.
   - Connect the phone to a computer by cable, import or copy recent photos and videos, then open a few copied files on the computer to check they work.
   - Use external storage only if you already have a compatible USB-C drive, adapter, or manufacturer-supported option.

5. **Use built-in storage-saving tools carefully.**  
   - **iPhone:** Settings → General → iPhone Storage can show recommendations. Offload Unused Apps frees app storage while keeping documents and data. If you use iCloud Photos, Settings → Photos may offer Optimise iPhone Storage.
   - **Android:** Settings → Storage → Free up space may show files, photos, videos, and apps to remove. Clear cache deletes temporary data; Clear storage or Clear data can permanently delete the app’s local data.

6. **Fix message and media problems after space is available.**  
   After freeing device space, reopen the message app and try downloading or saving one attachment. For WhatsApp, Signal, or similar apps, use the app’s own storage management area to find large videos, voice notes, or attachments, and remove only items you have copied or do not need.

7. **If you paid for a cleaner app in a panic, stop possible repeat charges.**  
   Cancel the subscription in the app store or with the merchant if you can. Check your bank or card app for any new recurring card payment. In the UK, a recurring card payment is also called a continuous payment authority, and you can ask your card issuer to cancel it.

8. **If the phone still will not save after you have freed space, stop experimenting.**  
   Put it on charge, connect to Wi-Fi, and attempt a full iCloud, Google, or computer backup. Avoid app installs, app updates, system updates, resets, and repairs until you have secured the data you can still access.

## What can wait
- You do not need to organise your whole photo library now.
- You do not need to decide today whether to pay for more cloud storage.
- You do not need a perfect cleanup; the immediate goal is enough space to save again and back up.
- You do not need to troubleshoot hardware faults until your newest data is copied or backed up.

## Important reassurance
A critically full phone can stop saving reliably, and apps can behave strangely when there is almost no device storage left. Freeing safe space first can often make the phone usable enough to back up and check what saved.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce immediate digital loss and stabilise the phone. Later decisions, such as repair, replacement, account recovery, workplace IT support, or a larger backup plan, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not professional IT, legal, financial, or other professional advice. Phone menus and features vary by model, operating system version, app version, and account settings. If the phone is work-issued in the UK, follow your employer’s IT process before making major changes such as wiping, repair, or replacement.
