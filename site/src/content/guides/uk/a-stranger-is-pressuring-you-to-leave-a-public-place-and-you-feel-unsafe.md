# What to do if… a stranger is pressuring you to leave a public place and you feel unsafe (UK)

## Short answer
Stay in public and move straight towards staff, security, a counter, or a group of people. Say, “No. I’m staying here,” and get help immediately; call 999 if you feel in danger.

## Do not do these things
- Do not go somewhere quieter to talk.
- Do not let yourself be steered towards exits, cars, stairwells, toilets, corridors, car parks, or other less visible areas.
- Do not argue or explain for long; keep it short and focus on moving towards help.
- Do not worry about seeming rude or causing a scene if you feel unsafe.
- Do not accept drinks, lifts, directions, or help that separates you from the public place.
- Do not leave alone if you think they may follow you; change plan and go to a staffed, busy place.

## What to do now
1. **Move first, talk second.** Step away and head to the nearest staffed point, such as bar staff, a shop counter, reception, security, a transport help point, or a ticket office. Put a counter, table, queue line, barrier, or group of people between you and them.
2. **Use plain words to staff.** Say: “I need help. This person is pressuring me to leave with them and I’m not comfortable.” Ask staff to keep you inside, get security or a manager, and not let the person follow you into a back area.
3. **Use Ask for Angela only if it fits the place.** In a participating venue, or if you see signs for it, asking staff for “Angela” can discreetly signal that you need help. If you are not sure the venue uses it, use the plain words above.
4. **Make one bystander your helper.** Choose one person and give a direct request: “Can you stand with me and get staff?” or “Can you call 999 if I say ‘now’?”
5. **Call 999 from a safer spot if you feel threatened, trapped, or followed.** If you cannot speak safely, call 999 and try to whisper, cough, tap the phone, or answer the operator if you can. If you are calling from a mobile and cannot speak or answer, press 55 when prompted so the call is transferred to police.
6. **If you are on the rail network, go to staff or a help point.** For a non-emergency incident on a train or at a station, you can text British Transport Police on 61016 with what is happening and your location. Do not use 61016 for emergency help; call 999 if you need an urgent police response.
7. **Do not leave alone.** Ask staff or security to help you stay inside while you call someone trusted, reunite with friends, arrange a pickup, or move to a safer staffed place nearby. If you leave, leave with staff, friends, or into a licensed taxi arranged from inside the venue where possible.
8. **Once you are safer, note useful details.** Write down or text yourself what you remember, such as appearance, clothing, direction of travel, name used, and any vehicle details. Do not stop in a vulnerable place to do this.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal report or give a statement.
- You do not need to prove anything or work out whether you misread it.
- You do not need to message them, confront them, or explain yourself.
- You do not need to decide how you will get home until you are with staff, security, friends, or another safer support.
- Once you are safe, you can decide whether to report a non-emergency via 101, online police reporting, British Transport Police if it happened on the rail network, or the venue’s security or management.

## Important reassurance
Feeling uncomfortable is enough reason to act. Freezing, going quiet, or trying to stay polite under pressure is a normal stress response. The safest move is to stay public, involve staff or other people, and create distance.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce immediate risk and buy time. Later decisions, including whether to report, make a statement, complain to a venue, or get specialist support, may need help from the police, the venue, a support service, or another appropriate professional.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in danger or a crime is in progress, call 999.
