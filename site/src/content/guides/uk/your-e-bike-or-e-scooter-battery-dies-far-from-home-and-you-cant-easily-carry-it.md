# What to do if… your e-bike or e-scooter battery dies far from home and you can’t easily carry it (UK)

## Short answer
Move the device off the carriageway immediately, power it off completely, and arrange for a vehicle pickup or a pedestrian-safe route home.

## Do not do these things
- Do not attempt to charge the device using non-original chargers or public power outlets.
- Do not continue to handle, push, or transport the device if the battery is hot, swollen, leaking, or emitting smoke or sharp smells.
- Do not attempt to enter London Underground, Overground, Elizabeth Line, or DLR stations with a non-folding e-bike or any e-scooter.
- Do not leave the device in the middle of a pavement, cycle lane, or near building exits.
- Do not attempt to ride the device manually if the motor or brakes feel seized.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe pause point off the road.** Get the device away from active traffic, junctions, and narrow walkways. Seek a well-lit area near a landmark such as a petrol station, supermarket entrance, or staffed building.
2. **Power the device off and observe the battery casing.** Turn the power switch to off and remove the key if applicable.
   - If you see **smoke, sparks, or liquid leaking**, or hear **hissing or popping**, **step back at least 5 metres**, keep others away, and call **999**.
   - If the battery casing feels **painfully hot to the touch or appears deformed**, do not attempt to move it further; call for emergency assistance.
3. **Handle rental devices via the official app.** If you are using a trial rental e-scooter or e-bike, use the "End Trip" or "Report Issue" function immediately. Park it in a designated bay or an upright position that does not block ramps or doors, take a photo of the location, and leave the device for the operator to collect.
4. **Arrange a private vehicle pickup for personal devices.** Use a taxi or minicab app to request a "Large" or "XL" vehicle (such as a multi-purpose vehicle or van) that can accommodate the device without lifting. 
   - Inform the driver that you have a powered-off e-bike or e-scooter before they arrive. 
   - If the device shows any signs of battery heat or damage, **do not place it inside an enclosed vehicle**.
5. **Verify rail restrictions before walking to a station.** 
   - **TfL services:** Non-folding e-bikes and all e-scooters are prohibited on the Tube, Elizabeth Line, Overground, and DLR.
   - **National Rail:** Most operators (including Northern, ScotRail, and Southeastern) prohibit all e-scooters and may refuse e-bikes that appear modified or non-compliant with EAPC standards.
6. **Secure the device if you must leave it to find help.** Use a lock to secure the frame to a permanent fixture. Ensure it is not obstructing a fire exit, public right of way, or tactile paving for the visually impaired. Note the exact street name or use a digital "pin" to mark the location.
7. **Use a pedestrian-safe route if pushing the device home.** Walk on the pavement or shared-use paths. If it is dark, use any remaining battery power for integrated lights or use a mobile phone torch to increase your visibility to others. Stop and rest if the weight of the device causes physical strain or loss of balance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine why the battery failed or attempt any technical resets right now.
- You do not need to decide on repairs or battery replacement today.
- You do not need to research long-term transport policies while you are still at the roadside.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel a sudden surge of stress or frustration when a heavy device becomes immobile far from home. These devices are difficult to manage without power, and your physical reaction to the sudden weight and the collapse of your travel plan is expected. Taking a moment to secure the device and yourself is the most effective way to prevent the situation from escalating.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for a battery failure in a public space. It does not cover technical repairs, warranty claims, or long-term legal advice regarding the use of private e-scooters on UK roads.

## Important note
Private e-scooters are currently illegal to ride on UK public roads and pavements; pushing one while it is powered off is generally treated as being a pedestrian in possession of property, but you may still be stopped by authorities. If there is any suspicion of a lithium-ion battery fire, prioritize your physical distance and call emergency services immediately rather than attempting to save the device.
