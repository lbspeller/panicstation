# What to do if… someone uses money, housing, or practical help to pressure you into sex (UK)

## Short answer
Move to a safe location, stop one-to-one contact with the person, and contact a specialist sexual violence or housing support service. You do not owe anyone sex for practical help, and you can access support without involving the police.

## Do not do these things
- Do not agree to sexual acts to prevent an immediate eviction or financial loss.
- Do not attend meetings alone with the person if you feel unsafe or pressured.
- Do not hand over your identification documents, passport, or bank cards.
- Do not delete evidence such as text messages, emails, or advertisements that mention "arrangements" or "help" for sex.
- Do not assume you are "choosing" this if your only other option is homelessness or extreme poverty.

## What to do now
1. **Move to a safe location immediately.** If you are in the property, go to a public place, a library, or a trusted friend's house to give yourself space to think. 
   - If you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
2. **Stop all private communication for the moment.** You are not required to explain why you are leaving or why you are unavailable. 
   - If you must communicate, do so in writing only.
3. **Save existing evidence securely.** Take screenshots of all messages, rental listings, or threats tied to money or housing. 
   - Email these to a new address the other person cannot access or upload them to a secure cloud account.
4. **Contact a specialist sexual violence support service.**
   - In **England and Wales** (aged 16+): call the **24/7 Rape & Sexual Abuse Support Line** at **0808 500 2222** or use their online chat.
   - In **Scotland**: call the **Rape Crisis Scotland** helpline at **08088 01 03 02** (open daily 5pm to midnight).
   - In **Northern Ireland**: call the **Rape Crisis NI** information line at **0800 024 6991** (Mon–Thu 10am–12pm and 6pm–8pm).
5. **Access healthcare and forensic options if needed.** You can contact a **Sexual Assault Referral Centre (SARC)** in England or the **Sexual Assault Response Co-ordination Service (SARCS)** in Scotland.
   - For Scotland's 24/7 self-referral service, call **0800 148 88 88**.
   - These services provide medical care and can store evidence even if you do not want to report to the police right now.
6. **Seek urgent housing advice to protect your rights.** Under the 2026 Renters' Rights Act, landlords in England cannot use "no-fault" (Section 21) evictions.
   - Call the **Shelter Emergency Helpline** at **0808 800 4444** (8am–8pm weekdays, 8am–5pm weekends).
   - Contact your **Local Authority Housing Team** and inform them you are at risk of homelessness due to exploitation or abuse.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide whether to make a formal police report today.
- You do not need to find a long-term apartment in the next few hours.
- You do not need to calculate exactly how much money or "debt" you think you owe the person.
- You do not need to clear out your belongings immediately if doing so puts you in physical danger.

## Important reassurance
Sexual exploitation using housing or money is a form of coercion. If you feel you have to say "yes" to avoid being on the street, that is not free consent. Your reaction—whether you feel numb, panicked, or like you are "going along with it" to survive—is a common response to an impossible situation.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Future steps regarding housing law, criminal proceedings, or long-term therapy will require specialized professional advice.

## Important note
This information is for guidance only and does not constitute legal or medical advice. Legal protections for tenants vary between England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Always prioritize your physical safety over property or money.
