# What to do if… you return to where you parked and your vehicle is missing (UK)

## Short answer
First check whether the vehicle was moved nearby, then use the official tracing route: TRACE in London, your local council outside London, and 101 if you still cannot confirm where it is. Treat it as possible theft only after those checks, unless there is immediate danger or clear evidence of a crime in progress.

## Do not do these things
- Do not assume theft before checking official removal and relocation routes, unless there is immediate danger or clear evidence of theft.
- Do not pay or give details to anyone who approaches you offering to “find” the vehicle; use contact routes you look up yourself.
- Do not leave the area before taking quick photos of the bay, lines, signs, suspension notices, and nearby street name if it is safe to do so.
- Do not assume the first pound or authority you contact will have it; the remover may be a council, police, or another official enforcement route.
- Do not delay once you know where it is, because release and storage charges may increase.

## What to do now
1. **Check the exact place you parked and nearby streets.**  
   Look at the street name, nearest building number, side of the road, bay markings, and any temporary signs. Walk the same street and the immediately adjacent streets for a few minutes, because vehicles are sometimes relocated nearby for roadworks, events, emergency access, or safety.

2. **Photograph what you can before leaving.**  
   Take clear photos of the bay, yellow or red lines, time plates, temporary suspension notices, cones, roadworks signs, and a wider photo showing the location. These are for your own record; do not argue about them now.

3. **If you are in London, check TRACE.**  
   Use the official TRACE service to check whether a London borough has reported the vehicle as removed or relocated. TRACE may show whether it has been taken to a pound or recorded as moved nearby.

4. **If you are outside London, check your local council first.**  
   Search your council’s parking or removed vehicle page, or use the GOV.UK local council finder if you are not sure which council covers the location. Ask whether the vehicle was removed, relocated, or reported to a pound.

5. **If council or TRACE checks do not locate it, contact police on 101.**  
   Ask whether the vehicle has been removed, recovered, impounded, or recorded as stolen. Call 999 only if a crime is happening now, someone is in immediate danger, or the situation could become violent.

6. **If tax enforcement, clamping, or DVLA-style removal may be involved, use the GOV.UK impounded vehicle guidance.**  
   The GOV.UK route explains what to do for some clamped or impounded vehicles and may direct you to the relevant release contact. Use it as a specific route, not as a replacement for council, TRACE, or police checks.

7. **When you locate the vehicle, ask exactly what to bring before travelling.**  
   Requirements vary. You may need photo ID, a driving licence, proof that you are entitled to collect the vehicle, proof of insurance, payment, keys, and sometimes MOT or address evidence. Confirm the pound address, opening hours, payment methods, and whether someone else can collect it for you.

8. **Recover the vehicle when you can do so safely.**  
   Bring your phone, charger, keys, documents, and a way to pay. Ask for an itemised receipt, the recorded reason for removal, and any reference number.

9. **If official routes still cannot locate it, report it as suspected stolen.**  
   Use the police non-emergency route unless there is an emergency. Tell your insurer after you have a police reference or when the insurer asks for one.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to challenge a penalty, removal fee, or storage charge.
- You do not need to argue about the signs while you are still trying to find the vehicle.
- You do not need to contact multiple private third parties; focus on the official remover, council, TRACE, police, or GOV.UK route.
- You do not need to gather every possible document until the pound or authority tells you what is required.

## Important reassurance
A vehicle missing from a parking place is often a removal or nearby relocation rather than theft. A steady sequence of checks usually gets you from panic to a clear answer: nearby streets, official tracing, council, then police if needed.

## Scope note
These are first steps to find and recover a towed, impounded, or relocated vehicle. Later decisions about penalties, appeals, refunds, insurance, or legal disputes may need specialist help once the vehicle is located and you have the paperwork.

## Important note
This is general information for immediate next steps, not legal, financial, insurance, or professional advice. Processes, documents, fees, and release rules vary by area and by who removed the vehicle.
