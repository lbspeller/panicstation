# What to do if… someone you rely on for care or assistance begins making sexual comments and you fear retaliation (UK)

## Short answer
Contact the agency or local Adult Social Care department immediately to request an urgent replacement worker and a safeguarding review. Do not feel obligated to handle the situation or the individual directly if you feel unsafe or fear that your care will be withdrawn.

## Do not do these things
- Do not feel required to confront the person if you fear they will withhold food, medication, or physical assistance.
- Do not delete any text messages or recordings if they have been sent to you, but do not go looking for new evidence.
- Do not accept "favours" or changes to your care routine that involve more one-on-one time or secrecy.
- Do not assume that you must have physical proof of sexual comments before you are allowed to ask for a new worker.
- Do not let the person convince you that no one else will be able to provide the specific care you need.

## What to do now
1. **Prioritise your immediate physical safety and call for help if needed.**
   - Call **999** if the person is currently with you and you feel in immediate danger of physical or sexual harm.
   - Call **111** for non-emergency advice if you are worried about your health or need to speak to someone about your care options outside of office hours.

2. **Contact the care agency or organisation manager immediately.**
   - **Call the office of the provider that employs the person.**
   - **State clearly that you feel unsafe and require an immediate change of worker.**
   - **Ask for a "no-contact" instruction to be issued to the current worker so they do not attend your next visit.**
   - **Request a single point of contact (a manager) to handle your case so you do not have to explain it to multiple staff members.**

3. **Notify your local Adult Social Care (Social Services) department.**
   - **Search for your local council website and look for the "Adult Safeguarding" or "Report a concern about an adult" section.**
   - **Tell them you are a person with care and support needs and that you are experiencing sexual harassment and fear retaliation.**
   - **Ask for an urgent safety plan to ensure your care needs (medication, washing, mobility) are met by someone else.**

4. **Arrange for a temporary "safety person" to be present if visits must continue.**
   - **Ask a friend, family member, or neighbour to be present or on a video call during any essential care visits until a replacement is confirmed.**
   - **Keep your phone or a personal alarm within reach at all times during the visit.**

5. **Contact a specialist support service for confidential advice.**
   - **Call Rape Crisis England & Wales (if 16+) at 0808 500 2222 (available 24/7) for support regarding sexual harassment.**
   - **Call Hourglass (Safer Ageing) at 0808 808 8141 (available 24/7) if you are an older person experiencing care-related harm or abuse.**
   - **Call Rape Crisis Scotland at 08088 01 03 02 or Nexus NI in Northern Ireland at 028 9032 6803 if you are outside England and Wales.**

6. **Make a brief note of what happened when it is safe to do so.**
   - **Write down the date, the time, and a short summary of the specific comments made.**
   - **Store this note somewhere the care worker cannot find it, such as a password-protected note on your phone or with a trusted friend.**

7. **Alert the relevant regulator if the service is not responding.**
   - **Contact the Care Quality Commission (CQC) in England, the Care Inspectorate in Scotland, or the Care Inspectorate Wales if the agency fails to replace the worker or protect you from retaliation.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether you want to involve the police for a criminal investigation.
- You do not need to have a "perfect" memory of every comment made before asking for help.
- You do not need to worry about the carer's employment status or what happens to them after you report.
- You do not need to make a formal written complaint immediately; the verbal request for safety is the priority.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel hesitant or afraid when you rely on someone for your basic daily needs. Fearing retaliation is a common response to a power imbalance. Requesting a new worker is a standard procedure for care agencies and you have the right to feel safe and respected in your own home.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and safety actions for people in the UK receiving care. It does not cover long-term legal claims, employment law for personal assistants, or formal disciplinary procedures.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not constitute legal or professional advice. Always contact 999 in an emergency. Adult safeguarding procedures vary slightly by local authority, so contacting your local Social Services is the most effective way to secure specific local protections.
