# What to do if… you find a strong sewer smell coming from one specific drain even after running water (UK)

## Short answer
Treat a strong smell from one drain as a possible drain fault or drain-gas problem: ventilate, avoid mixing chemicals, stop using that fixture for now, and arrange repair help if the simple surface checks do not quickly resolve it.

## Do not do these things
- Do not pour multiple drain cleaners, bleach, acids, ammonia-based products, or descaler into the drain to try to burn the smell out.
- Do not put your face close to the drain or keep testing the smell if it is strong.
- Do not dismantle, loosen, tighten, or force pipework if you rent, cannot see the problem clearly, or are unsure what you are doing.
- Do not keep using the affected sink, bath, shower, or floor drain if the smell persists or water is draining slowly.
- Do not assume it is sewage if there is any chance it is a fuel gas leak. If you suspect gas, get fresh air, avoid flames and electrical switches, and use the relevant gas emergency route. In Great Britain, National Gas lists 0800 111 999 for gas emergencies.

## What to do now
1. **Ventilate and create a safer pause.** Open a window if you can do so safely, keep the room door shut to limit spread, and move children, pets, or anyone feeling unwell away from the room.

2. **Check whether it is truly one drain without deep sniffing.** Briefly check nearby drains from a distance. If several drains smell, water is backing up, a toilet is gurgling, or there is sewage outside, stop using affected fixtures and move to step 7.

3. **Check the visible parts of the affected fixture.**
   - For a sink or basin, check whether the smell is strongest at the overflow hole near the top of the bowl.
   - For a bath or shower, check the waste cover or grate for trapped hair, sludge, or standing dirty water.
   - For a floor drain, check whether it looks dry or unused, because some traps may let odour through when the water seal has gone.

4. **Clean only the surface and overflow area safely.** Use warm or hot water that is not boiling, washing-up liquid, and a small brush for the overflow, plughole edge, waste cover, or grate. Rinse with water. Do not add chemical cleaners after this.

5. **Look for leak signs without taking anything apart.** Check under the sink or around the bath panel area for damp patches, dripping, staining, swollen wood, loose-looking joints, or a smell strongest inside a cupboard. Take a photo if you see damp or staining.

6. **Stop using that fixture if the smell remains.** Put the plug in if there is one, keep the room ventilated, and use a different sink, bath, or shower if available.

7. **Choose the right contact route.**
   - If you rent, report it to your landlord, letting agent, housing association, or repairs line as a persistent sewer smell from one specific drain, and mention any damp, slow drainage, gurgling, or backing up.
   - If you own the home and it appears limited to pipework inside your property, contact a reputable plumber or drainage contractor.
   - If several homes may be affected, there is a smell from an outside inspection chamber or manhole, or there is sewage outside, contact your water or wastewater company.
   - If the smell appears to come from a neighbouring property, local business, waste site, or industrial site rather than your own drain, contact your local council environmental health route.

8. **Get fresh air and urgent health advice if symptoms appear.** If anyone has headache, nausea, dizziness, breathing difficulty, eye or throat irritation, faintness, or confusion, move them to fresh air and use NHS 111 or your local urgent health advice service where available. Call 999 for life-threatening symptoms.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the exact plumbing fault now, such as trap siphoning, venting trouble, a failed seal, a blocked waste pipe, or a dry trap.
- You do not need to decide responsibility immediately. Report the issue, keep notes, and take photos of damp, staining, backing up, or visible sewage if it is safe.
- You do not need to buy strong drain chemicals or attempt a full repair today.
- You do not need to keep using the affected drain to prove the smell is still there.

## Important reassurance
A strong smell from one drain is often caused by a fixable plumbing or drainage issue. The safest first move is simple: ventilate, avoid chemical mixing, stop using that fixture, and get the right repair route involved if surface cleaning does not settle it.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about repair, liability, insurance, tenancy responsibility, or drainage work may need help from a landlord, reputable plumber or drainage contractor, water or wastewater company, council, insurer, or other specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, environmental health, plumbing, gas safety, insurance, or professional advice. If you suspect a fuel gas leak, prioritise gas emergency guidance. If anyone feels unwell or the smell is overpowering, prioritise fresh air and urgent health advice.
