# What to do if… you find a strong sewer smell coming from one specific drain even after running water (UK)

## Short answer
Ventilate the area immediately and stop using the affected fixture until you have confirmed it is a plumbing issue rather than a gas leak.

## Do not do these things
- Do not ignore the smell or attempt to "mask" it with air fresheners.
- Do not mix different drain cleaning chemicals, as this can produce toxic chlorine or chloramine gas.
- Do not pour boiling water directly into the drain, as it may damage plastic pipes or crack porcelain.
- Do not stick your fingers into the drain or overflow to remove debris; use a tool or wear heavy-duty gloves.
- Do not dismantle any pipework or traps if you are a tenant or are not qualified to do so.
- Do not lean over the drain to "deeply" sniff the odour if it makes you feel dizzy or nauseated.

## What to do now
1. **Open all windows and doors in the affected room.** This ensures that any accumulating sewer gases (such as hydrogen sulphide or methane) are diluted and moved out of the living space.
2. **Rule out a natural gas leak immediately.** Sewer gas and natural gas can both smell like "rotten eggs" (sulphur). 
   - If you hear a hissing sound or the smell is concentrated near a gas appliance, evacuate the building.
   - Call the **National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999** if there is any doubt.
3. **Run water into every drain in the property for 30 seconds.** This ensures that all "P-traps" (the U-shaped pipe under the sink) are full of water, which acts as a physical seal against sewer gases.
4. **Identify the specific entry point using your eyes only.** 
   - Check the **overflow hole** (the small hole near the top of the sink or bath).
   - Check the **floor waste** or shower grate for visible debris or hair.
   - Check **under the sink** for visible wet patches or drips on the pipework.
5. **Isolate the affected fixture.** Place the plug in the drain and close the door to that room to prevent the smell from spreading through the rest of the home.
6. **Determine the correct contact for repair.**
   - **For tenants:** Report the issue to your landlord or letting agent immediately as a health and safety repair.
   - **For homeowners:** Contact a Gas Safe registered plumber (who can also check for gas leaks) or a drainage specialist.
   - **For shared issues:** If neighbours also smell sewage, contact your regional **water and sewerage undertaker** (e.g., Thames Water, United Utilities) to report a possible public sewer blockage.
7. **Seek medical advice if you experience physical symptoms.** If you have a persistent headache, nausea, or dizziness, move to fresh air and call **NHS 111**.

## What can wait
- You do not need to identify the technical cause (e.g., siphoning, venting failure, or broken seals) yourself right now.
- You do not need to decide whether the pipework needs a full replacement or just a cleaning.
- You do not need to perform an "inspection" of the outdoor manhole or sewer system today.

## Important reassurance
Sewer smells from a single drain are usually caused by a simple mechanical failure, such as a dried-out trap or a build-up of organic matter in an overflow. These are standard plumbing issues that professionals deal with daily and are rarely representative of a wider structural failure.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization and hazard differentiation only. It is not a DIY repair manual or a diagnostic tool for plumbing systems.

## Important note
This information is for general guidance and does not replace professional plumbing or medical advice. If you suspect a gas leak, prioritise evacuation and contact the National Gas Emergency Service.
