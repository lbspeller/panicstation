# What to do if… someone keeps pressuring you to send sexual voice notes or videos you do not want to record (UK)

## Short answer

Do not send the recording. Get yourself to a safer pause, stop replying in real time, and tell one safe person or specialist service what is happening.

## Do not do these things

- Do not send “just one” voice note, video, or clip to calm them down.
- Do not keep arguing, explaining, or negotiating once you have said no.
- Do not meet them alone to “sort it out” if they are angry, threatening, or controlling.
- Do not forward sexual messages, recordings, screenshots, or images to friends for opinions.
- Do not screenshot, record, save, or share any sexual image or video of someone who may be under 18, even to show someone what happened.
- Do not blame yourself because the pressure was sexual, manipulative, or came from someone you know.

## What to do now

1. **Stop making anything for them.** A plain reply is enough: “I’m not sending that.” You do not need to justify it.

2. **Create a safer pause.** Put the chat on mute, step away from the app, move near another person if that helps, and avoid live voice or video calls with them for now.

3. **Keep only what is safe to keep.** If it feels safe and you may want help later, keep screenshots or short notes showing what was said, who sent it, and when. You do not have to gather evidence before asking for help or reporting.

4. **Lock down the contact route they are using.** Block, restrict, or mute the account if that feels safest. Turn off message requests, story replies, camera or microphone access, and any setting that lets them pull you into quick voice, video, or disappearing-message contact.

5. **Tell one trusted person today.** Say: “Someone is pressuring me to make sexual voice notes or videos and I do not want to.” Ask them to stay with you while you block, restrict, report, or contact support if doing it alone feels hard.

6. **Use specialist support if the pressure is frightening, persistent, or tied to a relationship.** Rape Crisis England & Wales can support people aged 16+ in England and Wales affected by sexual violence or harassment. GOV.UK lists domestic abuse support routes for England, Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland if this is part of control, threats, or abuse.

7. **If anything has already been sent, do not panic or send more.** If you are 18 or over, the Revenge Porn Helpline supports adults in the UK affected by intimate image abuse, including threats to share intimate content and webcam blackmail. If you are under 18, use a trusted adult or CEOP rather than trying to handle it alone.

8. **If you are under 18, or the person may be targeting a child, get adult or safeguarding help now.** Tell a parent, carer, teacher, safeguarding lead, youth worker, or another trusted adult. Online sexual abuse or worrying sexual communication involving a child can be reported to CEOP.

9. **If you feel in immediate danger, call 999.** If it is not an emergency, you can still choose to report to the police later, including through Police.uk guidance on sextortion.

## What can wait

You do not need to decide today whether to make a formal report, explain everything perfectly, confront them, or have one final conversation. You also do not need to work out the whole relationship, friendship, or future contact right now.

## Important reassurance

Pressure to make sexual voice notes or videos is not harmless just because it happens through chat, voice, or video. Freezing, feeling embarrassed, or not knowing what to call it is common. You are allowed to stop, pause, block, and get help.

## Scope note

This is first steps only. It is focused on helping you stabilise the next few hours, reduce pressure, and avoid making the situation harder. Later decisions about reporting, safety, the relationship, or legal options may need specialist help.

## Important note

This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or professional advice. Adults usually have choices about whether to report, but immediate danger or child safety needs urgent help from emergency services, CEOP, or a safeguarding adult.
