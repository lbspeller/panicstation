# What to do if… you lose your driving licence abroad and you need it for car hire or onward travel plans (UK)

## Short answer
Stop driving immediately and contact the car hire company to report the loss. You cannot legally drive in most jurisdictions without the physical photocard, and you must apply for a replacement from the DVLA (GB) or DVA (NI) to be sent to your registered UK address.

## Do not do these things
- Do not continue to drive a vehicle if you no longer possess the physical licence required by local law and your insurance policy.
- Do not attempt to use an International Driving Permit (IDP) as a standalone document; it is only a translation and is invalid without the original national licence.
- Do not pay for "fast-track" replacement services from non-government websites.
- Do not assume a photograph or digital scan of the licence will be accepted by police or rental desks.
- Do not provide personal data or licence scans to unofficial third-party "verification" services.

## What to do now
1. **Find a secure location and conduct a systematic search of all belongings.**
   - Check luggage linings, hotel safes, the vehicle you arrived in, and any clothing worn recently.
   - Look for the sensory cues of theft: unzipped bags, tampered wallets, or a sudden loss of weight in pockets.

2. **Contact the local police to report a theft if you suspect your licence was stolen.**
   - Obtain a written police report or a crime reference number.
   - This documentation is often required by travel insurance providers and car hire companies to waive certain cancellation fees.

3. **Call the car hire company's local branch immediately.**
   - **Ask specifically if they will accept a DVLA "check code" and a passport for verification.**
   - **Ask if the rental can be transferred to a different driver in your party who has a physical licence.**
   - **Request a written email confirming any alternative arrangements they agree to.**

4. **Generate a driving licence "check code" using the official government service.**
   - **GB licence holders should use the "View or share your driving licence information" service on GOV.UK.**
   - **NI licence holders should use the NI Direct "View or share your driving licence" service.**
   - If you do not have your licence number, you can find it on old insurance documents or by calling the DVLA contact centre at +44 300 083 0013 (Monday–Saturday).
   - Note that these codes are valid for 21 days but many foreign rental desks are not equipped to use them.

5. **Apply for a replacement licence online.**
   - **Submit an application via the DVLA or DVA website.**
   - A replacement costs £20. The new licence will only be sent to the UK address already held on record by the licensing agency.
   - Arrange for a trusted person at that address to notify you when it arrives.

6. **Secure alternative transport for the next 24 to 48 hours.**
   - **Book trains, buses, or licensed taxis to reach your next destination.**
   - Do not risk driving without the physical card, as this can lead to vehicle impoundment and the voiding of your insurance.

## What can wait
- You do not need to notify the UK police about a lost licence; only local police if it was stolen abroad.
- You do not need to update your address on the licence until you return to the UK.
- You do not need to cancel your entire trip immediately; most travel plans can continue using public transport or a different driver.

## Important reassurance
Losing a vital document while travelling is a high-stress event that triggers an immediate sense of being "trapped." This is a standard logistical hurdle that car hire companies and authorities encounter frequently. While it may disrupt your original itinerary, moving to a Plan B for transport is a manageable step that prevents the far more serious legal consequences of driving unlicensed.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for UK citizens who have lost their driving licence while travelling. It does not cover long-term residence issues, IDP applications from abroad, or insurance claim processing.

## Important note
Licensing laws are jurisdiction-specific. This guide refers to UK-issued licences (DVLA/DVA). Acceptance of digital codes or scans is at the sole discretion of local law enforcement and individual rental providers.
