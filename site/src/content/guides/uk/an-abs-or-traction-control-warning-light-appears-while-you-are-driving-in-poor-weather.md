# What to do if… an ABS or traction-control warning light appears while you are driving in poor weather (UK)

## Short answer
Stay safe where you are and assume ABS, traction-control, or stability assistance may be reduced or unavailable. If a red brake warning appears, the brake pedal feels wrong, or you are unsafe on a motorway or other high-speed road, do not continue; arrange recovery or call 999 and ask for the police.

## Do not do these things
- Do not test the brakes with a hard stop or sharp turn on wet, icy, snowy, or loose ground.
- Do not carry on at normal speed just because the car still moves.
- Do not switch off safety systems unless your vehicle handbook specifically tells you to for the situation you are in.
- Do not get out into a live lane or crawl along a live lane to inspect the car.
- Do not try repairs on a motorway.
- Do not place a warning triangle on a motorway; on any fast road, do not get out to place one if traffic, visibility, weather, or road layout makes it unsafe.

## What to do now
1. **Make your stopped position safer first.** Keep your hazard warning lights on. If it is dark or visibility is poor, use sidelights so the vehicle is easier to see.

2. **If you are on a motorway or other high-speed road, follow breakdown safety before thinking about the warning light.**
   - If the car can still be controlled and you are not in a place of relative safety, move only as far as the next safer place, such as a service area, exit, emergency area, lay-by, or hard shoulder.
   - If it is safe to leave the vehicle, get out by the side furthest from traffic and move well away from the vehicle and moving traffic, behind a safety barrier if there is one.
   - If you cannot leave safely, stay in the vehicle with your seat belt and hazard warning lights on, then call 999 and ask for the police.
   - If you need assistance, use the nearest free emergency telephone where provided and safe to reach, or use a mobile phone to call National Highways on 0300 123 5000, then contact your breakdown provider.
   - If you are in an emergency area, use the emergency telephone and follow the operator’s advice before trying to rejoin the carriageway.

3. **Check exactly what is lit up.** Look for:
   - an ABS warning, traction-control warning, stability-control warning, ESP, or ESC message, often amber;
   - a brake system warning, often red;
   - any “STOP” message or warning telling you not to drive.

4. **Treat these signs as “do not drive”.** Arrange recovery, or call 999 if you are in an unsafe roadside position, if any of these apply:
   - a red brake warning is showing;
   - an ABS warning appears together with a brake warning;
   - the brake pedal feels soft, sinks, pulses unusually, or feels very different;
   - braking is clearly weaker;
   - you hear grinding, smell burning, see smoke, or see fluid leaking;
   - you do not feel safe rejoining traffic in the conditions.

5. **If it is only an amber ABS, traction-control, stability-control, ESP, or ESC warning, and braking feels normal, move only if conditions allow.** Drive only to a safer place, a nearby garage, or a recovery meeting point. Use a speed appropriate to the weather and traffic, leave much more space than usual, and avoid sudden braking, steering, or acceleration.

6. **Brake and steer as if the assistance systems are not there.** Brake early, gently, and progressively. On ice, snow, standing water, or slush, avoid last-second braking and steer smoothly.

7. **Save the details for the garage or recovery operator.** From a safe position, take a photo of the dashboard warning and note what happened: rain, ice, snow, standing water, slush, braking feel, any noise, and whether the warning stayed on or flashed.

8. **Check the vehicle handbook only after you are safe.** Dashboard symbols and safe-next-step wording vary by make and model, so use the handbook or in-car message to confirm what that specific warning means.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose the fault at the roadside.
- You do not need to keep restarting the car to clear the warning.
- You do not need to decide repair costs or choose a garage now.
- You do not need to continue the journey just because you are close to home or late.

## Important reassurance
An ABS, traction-control, or stability warning often means an extra assistance system may not be working normally, not that the car has no brakes at all. In poor conditions, that extra help matters, so slowing the decision down and choosing the safest next move is the right response.

## Scope note
These are first steps only, to stabilise the situation and avoid a risky roadside decision. If the warning stays on, returns, or appears with any brake warning or abnormal braking, the next step may need a qualified mechanic, breakdown provider, or recovery service.

## Important note
This is general safety information, not vehicle-specific mechanical, legal, or professional advice. Dashboard symbols, braking systems, and manufacturer instructions differ by make and model; if anything suggests reduced braking or you are in an unsafe roadside position, prioritise emergency help or professional recovery.
