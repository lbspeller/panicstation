# What to do if… you miss a flight connection because of immigration or document checks and you are stranded (UK)

## Short answer
Get to the airline’s transfer or customer service desk, and start the same request through the airline app, phone or chat while you move. Ask for rebooking to your final destination before you buy anything or leave the airport area.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t go landside, leave the terminal, or move to another terminal until you have asked whether it affects your rebooking, baggage, entry conditions, or ability to return airside.
- Don’t buy a replacement ticket straight away, especially if both flights are on one booking reference.
- Don’t separate your passport, visa or entry documents, boarding passes, bag tags, booking reference, and any airline messages.
- Don’t argue with border, security, or document-check staff. Ask calmly what you are allowed to do next.
- Don’t assume the airline must provide compensation or a free new flight when the missed connection was caused only by immigration or document checks.
- Don’t spend heavily on hotels, taxis, or new flights before you have asked the airline and checked your travel insurance.

## What to do now
1. **Make sure your immigration or document position is clear first.**
   - If you are still with border, security, or document-check staff, follow their instructions.
   - Ask: **“Am I free to go to my airline now?”**
   - If you are not free to go, ask whether you can contact your airline from where you are.
   - If you are outside the UK and you are a British national who needs urgent consular help, ask how you can contact the nearest British embassy, high commission, or consulate.

2. **Start the rebooking request through two routes at once.**
   - Go to the airline’s connections, transfer, or customer service desk.
   - At the same time, use the airline app, phone line, or chat.
   - Say: **“I missed my connection after immigration or document checks. Please rebook me to my final destination and tell me what happens to my checked baggage.”**

3. **Check whether both flights are on one itinerary or separate tickets.**
   - If both flights are under one booking reference, ask the airline to treat it as a missed connection and reroute you.
   - If the flights are on separate bookings, ask the onward airline what missed-flight options, change fees, standby options, or rescue fares are available before buying a new ticket.
   - If a travel agent booked the journey, contact them as well as the airline.

4. **Ask the airline for the next safe, workable route.**
   - Ask: **“What is the earliest available route to my final destination?”**
   - Ask: **“Can you confirm the new itinerary in the app or by email?”**
   - If the next flight is tomorrow, ask: **“Do you provide meal vouchers, accommodation, or transport for this situation?”**

5. **Find out where your checked baggage is.**
   - Ask: **“Is my checked bag still checked through, held here, or needing collection?”**
   - If you already collected bags to re-check, ask where you can store or re-check them while you are being rebooked.
   - Do not abandon checked bags or leave them unattended.

6. **Be careful with passenger-rights claims in the moment.**
   - UK air passenger rights may help if there was a covered flight delay, cancellation, or denied boarding on a flight within UK coverage.
   - If you missed the connection only because of immigration, security, or document checks, the airline may say this is outside its control or outside the ticket protection.
   - Still ask for help, but treat compensation and complaints as something to deal with later.

7. **Make a small record without creating a project.**
   - Save screenshots of boarding passes, delay messages, rebooking messages, and any new itinerary.
   - Note the time you joined the immigration or document-check queue and the time you were cleared.
   - Keep receipts for reasonable essentials.
   - Do not photograph restricted border or security areas, officials, or documents if signs or staff tell you not to.

8. **If you are stuck overnight, keep the decision small.**
   - Stay in a staffed, well-lit part of the airport while you work out the next step.
   - Keep passport, documents, phone, cards, medication, and valuables with you.
   - If you must book accommodation yourself, choose somewhere close, cancellable if possible, and not unusually expensive.
   - Keep the receipt and booking confirmation.

9. **Tell one trusted person where you are.**
   - Send your airport, terminal, airline, booking reference if safe to share, and the next expected update time.
   - Keep your phone charged or ask the airline, airport desk, or information point where you can charge it.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide now whether to make a formal complaint.
- You do not need to argue compensation at the desk if the urgent issue is getting rebooked.
- You do not need to write a long account tonight; short notes, screenshots, and receipts are enough for now.
- You do not need to cancel the whole trip until you know the airline’s rebooking options and the cost of alternatives.

## Important reassurance
This is stressful, but it is a known airport problem. The useful next step is not to solve fault or rights immediately; it is to get a rebooking answer, locate your bags, and avoid expensive or irreversible choices while you are tired.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance for the first hours after a missed connection. Later decisions about claims, insurance, complaints, immigration documents, or replacement travel may need help from the airline, insurer, travel agent, airport staff, consular services, or another suitable specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. What applies depends on your route, ticket terms, booking type, airline policy, passenger-rights coverage, and the reason the connection was missed.
