# What to do if… your mobile ticket will not load at a barrier or checkpoint (UK)

## Short answer
Step out of the flow, do not force the barrier, and ask staff for help. Show the ticket if it appears; if it will not load, show your booking reference, confirmation email, payment evidence, and Railcard if relevant, then follow staff instructions.

## Do not do these things
- Do not jump the barrier, tailgate someone else, or push through; this can be treated as travelling without showing a valid ticket.
- Do not argue on the gateline or block the gates; move to the side and keep the interaction calm and short.
- Do not delete or reinstall the ticket app in panic; you may lose saved access or make login harder.
- Do not assume proof of payment alone is enough to travel; ask staff what they can accept at that location.
- Do not buy another ticket before trying staff help, unless you need to travel now and staff tell you that buying a ticket is the workable option.

## What to do now
1. **Move out of the queue but stay near the barrier.**  
   Stand where staff can see you are stuck, not trying to slip through.

2. **Try the fastest offline places for the ticket.**
   - Check Apple Wallet or Google Wallet if you added the ticket there.
   - Search your email for the retailer, “PDF”, “eTicket”, or the journey date.
   - Open the ticket app and check “My tickets”, “Saved tickets”, “Downloads”, or similar.
   - Check your files or downloads folder for a PDF ticket.
   - Turn flight mode on for 10 seconds, then off, and try the wallet or app again.
   - Turn screen brightness up if a barcode or QR code appears.

3. **Get your proof of purchase ready in one place.**  
   Show staff whatever you can access quickly:
   - booking reference or order number
   - confirmation email
   - journey details, including origin, destination, date, and train company if known
   - payment card, phone wallet, or banking transaction showing the ticket purchase
   - Railcard, if the ticket depends on one

4. **Ask staff for a manual check or the correct next step.**  
   Say: “My ticket is on my phone but the network is down and it will not load. Here is my booking reference and payment evidence. Can you help me verify it, or tell me what I need to do?”  
   Staff may be able to check the ticket, direct you to a ticket office or help point, open an assisted gate, or tell you to buy a ticket before travelling.

5. **If staff cannot let you through, protect your position calmly.**  
   Ask where the issue can be checked. If there is an incident number, help point, or ticket office instruction, note it. Also note the station, time, staff location, and what you were told.

6. **If you are told to buy another ticket to travel now, decide based on urgency.**
   - If you must travel and this is the only workable option, buy the cheapest ticket that correctly covers the journey you are about to make.
   - Keep the receipt or confirmation.
   - Note the time, station, and what staff told you.
   - Keep trying to retrieve the original ticket once you have signal again.

7. **If you are issued a Penalty Fare or notice, do not argue at the barrier.**  
   You may still be treated as unable to show a valid ticket at that moment, even if you later find proof that you paid. Take the paperwork, check the appeal instructions and deadlines on it, and keep all evidence of the ticket, payment, network problem, and staff conversation.

8. **If this is TfL pay as you go with Oyster or contactless, focus on touching in or out correctly if you can.**  
   Use the same card, device, or Oyster card for the journey. If you could not touch in or out because of a gate or system problem, ask staff for help, then later check your Oyster or contactless account for an incomplete journey, maximum fare, correction, or refund option.

## What can wait
- You do not need to fix your phone, app, or network connection at the barrier.
- You do not need to decide now whether to complain, claim a refund, or appeal.
- You do not need to prove every detail immediately; focus on getting staff help and keeping evidence.
- You do not need to contact the ticket retailer while blocking the gate.

## Important reassurance
A ticket that will not load at a gate is stressful, but it is a practical problem to slow down, move aside, and deal with through staff. Calm cooperation, visible proof of purchase, and clear notes give you a better chance of resolving it without making the situation worse.

## Scope note
These are first steps for the immediate barrier or checkpoint problem only. Refunds, duplicate tickets, complaints, and Penalty Fare appeals depend on the operator, retailer, location, ticket type, and paperwork.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, professional, or transport operator advice. Follow staff instructions at the station and check the operator or retailer’s own rules for your ticket.
