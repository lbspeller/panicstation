# What to do if… you feel a tingling sensation when touching a tap or metal appliance and suspect an electrical fault (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using the item immediately and, if it is safe to reach, switch off the electricity at the main switch of your consumer unit. A "tingling" sensation is a critical warning sign of a potentially lethal earthing or neutral fault that requires urgent professional inspection.

## Do not do these things
- Do not touch the item again to "test" if the sensation is still there.
- Do not touch any metalwork (taps, pipes, radiators) while you are wet or barefoot.
- Do not assume a "mild" sensation means the danger is low; it can escalate to a full shock instantly.
- Do not attempt to investigate the wiring or remove socket covers yourself.
- Do not keep resetting a tripped RCD or circuit breaker if it continues to flip.
- Do not use extension leads to bypass a faulty socket or area.

## What to do now
1. **Immediately break contact and move away from the hazard.** Ensure no one else (including children or pets) touches the appliance, tap, or metal surface.
2. **Isolate the power at the consumer unit (fuse box).** **Switch the "Main Switch" to the OFF position.**
   - Do not attempt to isolate only one circuit unless you are absolutely certain of the labels.
   - If the consumer unit is near the source of the tingling or in a damp area, do not touch it; skip to step 4.
3. **Unplug the specific appliance only if it is safe to do so.** **Only attempt this if you are dry, standing on a dry floor, and can reach the plug without touching any metal parts.** If the tingle came from a fixed pipe or tap, do not touch anything.
4. **Report the fault to the electricity network operator if you suspect an external issue.** **Call 105 (national) or 03457 643643 (Northern Ireland) to report a potential supply fault.**
   - Tell them you are experiencing "tingling from metalwork/taps."
   - This is particularly important if neighbors are experiencing similar issues, as it may indicate a "broken neutral" in the street.
5. **Contact a registered electrician or your landlord immediately.** **Inform them that you have a suspected earthing or bonding fault and have isolated the power.**
   - If you rent, your landlord is legally required to ensure the electrical installation is safe.
   - Do not turn the power back on until a qualified professional has inspected the system.
6. **Check for signs of fire or serious damage.** **If you see smoke, sparks, or smell burning, call 999 or 112 immediately.**
7. **Seek medical advice if a shock was received.** **Call NHS 111 or go to A&E if you feel unwell, have chest pains, or notice any burns.**

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine if the fault is in the appliance or the house wiring yourself.
- You do not need to identify which specific earthing component has failed.
- You do not need to worry about the cost of repairs before the situation is made safe.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to feel uncertain when a sensation is "mild" or "tingly" rather than a sharp shock. You are not overreacting by turning off the power; these sensations are well-known indicators of serious electrical faults that can become dangerous without warning.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for domestic electrical sensations. It does not replace a professional Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) or a formal inspection by a qualified electrician.

## Important note
Electrical injuries are not always visible. Internal damage can occur even without external burns. If anyone experiences palpitations, confusion, or persistent numbness after contact, seek emergency medical help via 999.
