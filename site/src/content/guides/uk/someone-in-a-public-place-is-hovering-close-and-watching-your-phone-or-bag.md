# What to do if… someone in a public place is hovering close and watching your phone or bag (UK)

## Short answer
Create distance, secure your phone and bag against your body, and move to a staffed or clearly visible place. If you feel in immediate danger or a theft is happening, call 999.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t stay still and keep scrolling while someone is hovering close.
- Don’t “test” them by displaying your phone, wallet, purse, or open bag.
- Don’t confront, argue with, block, follow, or chase them if that could escalate the situation.
- Don’t leave your phone or bag on a table, chair back, buggy, or the floor.
- Don’t unlock your phone for a stranger, hand it over, or let them “show you something” on it.
- Don’t worry about proving their intent before you move away.

## What to do now
1. **Secure your phone and bag immediately.**  
   Put your phone away, or hold it with both hands with the screen turned inwards. Zip or close your bag, bring it in front of your body, and keep one hand near the strap or zip.

2. **Create space without making it a confrontation.**  
   Take a few steps sideways, change direction, join a queue, step into a shop, or move towards a busier area. The aim is to break their position near your pocket, hand, bag opening, or phone.

3. **Move to staff, security, or a visible place.**  
   Go to a staffed counter, ticket barrier, shop doorway, café till, station help point, security desk, or a place covered by visible staff presence.

4. **Avoid being boxed in.**  
   Keep your bag in front of you, keep your back near a wall, pillar, counter, or open space, and avoid letting anyone stand close behind you at arm’s length. Be aware that distraction theft can involve more than one person.

5. **Use one calm boundary if they keep closing in.**  
   Turn your shoulders enough to show you have noticed and say, “Please give me space.” Then move straight towards staff or a busier area.

6. **If you are on a train or at a railway station, use the rail route if it is not an emergency.**  
   Text British Transport Police on 61016 with where you are and what is happening. If you need urgent police help, a crime is happening, someone suspected of a crime is nearby on the rail network, or someone is being threatened or is in danger, call 999.

7. **If you think something has been taken, get safe before sorting the details.**  
   Move to staff or security first. If the crime is happening now or you feel in immediate danger, call 999. If you are safe and it is not an emergency, report to police online or by calling 101. If you want to pass on crime information anonymously, use Crimestoppers.

## What can wait
- You do not have to decide right now whether it “really was” a pickpocket.
- You do not have to make a perfect description while you are still close to the person.
- You do not have to confront anyone to get certainty.
- If nothing has been taken, you can decide once safe whether to report suspicious behaviour or share information.
- If something was taken, blocking cards, changing passwords, insurance paperwork, and replacement admin can wait until you are somewhere safer and steadier.

## Important reassurance
It is normal to freeze, doubt yourself, or worry that you are overreacting when someone is subtly crowding your space. Moving away, putting your phone away, and going towards staff are reasonable first steps even if you are not sure what the person intended.

## Scope note
These are first steps only to reduce risk and stabilise the moment. If anything was stolen, your phone was unlocked, your cards are missing, or you feel you are being followed, you may need specialist or practical follow-up once you are safe.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you believe you are in danger or a crime is in progress, call emergency services.
