# What to do if… your online card payments are being declined due to an address verification mismatch (UK)

## Short answer
Stop retrying the same online payment. First confirm the exact billing address your card issuer currently has on file, then either enter that exact billing address at checkout or ask your bank or card provider to update it.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep trying the same payment over and over; repeated declines may trigger extra fraud checks or temporary blocks.
- Don’t guess different flat numbers, address lines, or postcode formats.
- Don’t use an address that is not genuinely yours just to get the payment through.
- Don’t send your full card details by email, text, or chat.
- Don’t assume it is only a website glitch if this started suddenly; check for security alerts or unexpected account changes.

## What to do now
1. **Pause the checkout and check the billing address your card issuer has on file.**  
   Use your bank or card app, online banking, or the phone number on the back of the card. Check the address attached to the card, not just the delivery address in the shop account.

2. **Retry once using the issuer’s exact on-file billing address, only if it is genuinely yours.**  
   Enter the billing address exactly as the issuer shows it, including flat or unit details and postcode. Keep the delivery address separate if the item needs to go to your current address.

3. **If the issuer still has your old address, update it properly.**  
   Ask your bank or card provider to update the card billing address and confirm when online address checks should start using the new details. Until then, avoid repeated failed attempts.

4. **Clear saved checkout details before retrying.**  
   Remove old billing addresses from the merchant account, your browser autofill, wallet app, and any express checkout profile so the wrong address is not reinserted silently.

5. **Check for card controls or security prompts.**  
   In your bank app, look for online payment settings, card freeze, spending limits, merchant blocks, or a message asking you to approve a recent payment attempt. Deal with any prompt before retrying.

6. **Ask the merchant what failed.**  
   Ask whether the decline was an address verification or AVS mismatch, a postcode mismatch, a card security check, or a general decline. Ask whether they can take the payment another way without asking you to send full card details by email or chat.

7. **Use a safer backup payment route if the payment is due today.**  
   Consider another card, a bank transfer using bank details you have verified independently, or paying by phone only after you call the company using a trusted number from a bill, official app, or genuine website.

8. **If you did not move or change details, treat the sudden mismatch as a security warning.**  
   Check recent transactions, contact-detail changes, new devices, and bank messages. If anything looks wrong, contact your bank or card provider and ask them to secure the account.

9. **If the bank or card provider cannot resolve it, use their complaints process.**  
   Ask them to log a formal complaint and keep copies of messages, dates, decline messages, and screenshots that do not show full card details. If you receive a final response or are left waiting, check whether the Financial Ombudsman Service can help.

## What can wait
- You do not need to cancel the card immediately unless your bank tells you to, or you see signs of fraud.
- You do not need to switch banks today.
- You do not need to update every account everywhere right now.
- You do not need to apply for new credit to solve this.
- Focus on the urgent payment, the card issuer’s billing address, and any security alerts.

## Important reassurance
Address checks can fail after a move, after saved checkout details change, or when a merchant applies stricter fraud settings. This is usually a fixable records or checkout-detail problem, and pausing to match the issuer’s billing address is safer than repeated retries.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. If the problem continues after your issuer confirms the billing address is correct, you may need specialist help from your bank or card provider, the merchant’s payments team, or a complaints body.

## Important note
This guide is general information, not financial, legal, fraud, or professional advice. Banks, card issuers, payment processors, and merchants can apply different security rules, and a decline may happen for reasons other than address verification.
