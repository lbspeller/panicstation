# What to do if… an app suddenly gains camera, microphone, or location permissions you do not remember approving (UK)

## Short answer
Immediately revoke the app's Camera, Microphone, and Location permissions in your device settings and stop using the application until you have verified your security configuration.

## Do not do these things
- Don't grant additional permissions or "test" the app to see how it responds.
- Don't enter passwords, biometric data, or payment details while the app's status is suspicious.
- Don't ignore the change even if the app seems familiar or trusted.
- Don't factory reset the device immediately if you need to preserve evidence of unauthorized access.

## What to do now
1. **Take a screenshot of the current permission settings for the specific app.** **Note the app name, version number, and the exact time you discovered the change.**
2. **Revoke all sensitive permissions through your operating system settings.**
   - For iPhone: Go to **Settings** -> **Privacy & Security** -> tap **Camera**, **Microphone**, and **Location Services** individually to toggle the app off.
   - For Android: Go to **Settings** -> **Security & Privacy** -> **Privacy** -> **Permission Manager** and set the app to **Don't allow** for each category.
3. **Use the built-in audit tools to see when the sensors were last accessed.**
   - iPhone: Check **Settings** -> **Privacy & Security** -> **App Privacy Report** (enable it if it was off) to see a 7-day log of sensor use.
   - Android: Check **Settings** -> **Security & Privacy** -> **Privacy** -> **Privacy Dashboard** to see a 24-hour timeline of access.
4. **Inspect your device for unauthorized management profiles or administrator apps.**
   - iPhone: Check **Settings** -> **General** -> **VPN & Device Management**.
   - Android: Check **Settings** -> **Security & Privacy** -> **More Security Settings** -> **Device Admin Apps**.
5. **Run a system-wide security scan to detect potentially unwanted applications.**
   - Android: Open the **Google Play Store** -> tap your profile icon -> **Play Protect** -> **Scan**.
   - iPhone: Ensure **iOS** is fully updated in **Settings** -> **General** -> **Software Update** to apply latest security patches.
6. **Activate platform-specific safety tools if you suspect targeted monitoring.**
   - iPhone: Use **Safety Check** (**Settings** -> **Privacy & Security** -> **Safety Check**) to immediately stop sharing data with all people and apps.
   - Android: Check if the app is located within a **Private Space** (**Settings** -> **Security & Privacy** -> **Private Space**) which may hide its activity from the main menu.
7. **Secure your primary accounts using a separate, trusted device.** **Change your email password and ensure multi-factor authentication (MFA) is active on your most sensitive accounts.**
8. **Report the incident if you believe a crime has been committed or data has been stolen.**
   - In England, Wales, or Northern Ireland: Contact **Report Fraud** at 0300 123 2040 or online at reportfraud.police.uk.
   - In Scotland: Call **Police Scotland** on 101 for non-emergencies.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine the technical cause (bug vs. exploit) before revoking access.
- You do not need to purchase a new device immediately.
- You do not need to contact the app developer for an explanation right now.

## Important reassurance
Permission changes can occur due to legitimate operating system updates, app "feature" merges, or accidental touches. Finding an unexpected permission is a signal to investigate, not a confirmation that your device is permanently compromised. Taking the steps to revoke access and audit your settings is a standard and effective response.

## Scope note
This guidance provides first-step stabilization for unexpected software behavior. It does not replace a professional forensic digital audit or legal advice regarding data breaches.

## Important note
This guide is for informational purposes and does not constitute technical or legal advice. If you are in immediate physical danger or suspect you are being monitored by someone with physical access to your device, contact emergency services on 999. Digital security is an evolving field; always refer to your device manufacturer's current official documentation for the most recent security protocols.
