# What to do if… you reset a device and cannot find your backups to restore (UK)

## Short answer
Pause and minimise further changes, then check the right account, cloud services, other devices, and deleted-item areas before resetting again. Many “missing backup” situations are actually the wrong account, an unfinished restore, or data that was syncing separately rather than stored inside a device backup.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep setting the device up “just to see what’s missing” if it means installing apps, downloading lots of files, or syncing large libraries.
- Don’t repeatedly erase or reset again hoping it will make backups appear.
- Don’t sign out of cloud accounts on other devices yet; you may remove locally cached copies.
- Don’t delete anything in cloud storage “to tidy up” while you are still searching.
- Don’t give remote access, payment details, or account passwords to a recovery advert that promises guaranteed results.
- Don’t empty any Recently Deleted, Trash, Recycle Bin, or Archive area until you have checked it carefully.

## What to do now
1. **Freeze changes where you can**
   - Put the reset device in flight mode, or disconnect Wi-Fi and Ethernet.
   - Avoid installing apps, downloading large libraries, or turning on full sync until you have checked what still exists elsewhere.
   - If the device is mid-setup, slow down and do only the steps needed to check the correct account and backup options.

2. **Write down the basics so you do not loop**
   - Note the device model, the reset type, the approximate date and time, and what is missing, such as photos, messages, documents, notes, contacts, or app data.
   - List every account that might be involved, including Apple Account, Google Account, Microsoft account, work account, school account, and any old email address.
   - Check whether you used a different account for purchases, photos, contacts, work files, or family sharing.

3. **Check cloud data directly, not only the device backup list**
   - On a computer or another trusted device, sign in to the relevant cloud service in a browser.
   - Check photos, cloud drive files, contacts, calendars, notes, and messages where available.
   - Look in Recently Deleted, Trash, Recycle Bin, Archive, and version history areas before you assume the data is gone.
   - If you find the data online, confirm it looks complete before turning on full sync on the reset device.

4. **Check any other devices before signing out**
   - Look at old phones, tablets, laptops, external drives, memory cards, and computers that may still have local copies.
   - Do not sign out, delete apps, clear storage, or accept prompts to remove local data until you know what is stored there.
   - If a device has the missing files, copy them to a separate safe place before changing account or sync settings.

5. **iPhone or iPad: check restore flow and separate iCloud syncing**
   - Restoring from an iCloud device backup happens during setup. If the device is already set up, Apple’s process usually requires erasing it again before using “Restore from iCloud Backup”.
   - Check the backup date and size before choosing it.
   - If information is missing after restore, leave the device connected to Wi-Fi and power because some content can continue restoring in the background.
   - Check whether the missing data was syncing separately. For example, iCloud Photos and iCloud Drive content are usually not inside the device backup because they sync through iCloud.
   - iCloud Mail messages are not included in iCloud Backup because they already live in iCloud, although some email account settings may be included.
   - If you ever backed up to a Mac or PC, check for a computer backup as well.

6. **Android: check the correct Google account and backup details**
   - Confirm you are using the same Google Account that would have held the backup.
   - In Settings, check Google backup or Backup details to see what the device says is backed up.
   - Separately check Google Photos, including Trash and Archive, and Google Drive for files.
   - Some backed-up data may return when the Google Account is added to a set-up phone, but full setup restore is usually part of the first setup after a factory reset.
   - Avoid another reset until you have confirmed a backup exists, checked the account, and checked other remaining copies.
   - If the device is work-managed or has a work profile, check with the organisation before trying more restore steps.

7. **Windows, Mac, and cloud drive files: check deleted items and versions**
   - For OneDrive, check the OneDrive Recycle Bin on the web before checking only the computer’s Recycle Bin or Trash.
   - For OneDrive work or school accounts, retention may depend on the organisation’s settings.
   - If you have Microsoft 365, “Restore your OneDrive” may let you roll OneDrive back to an earlier point within the last 30 days, but review carefully because it can affect later file changes.
   - If you used iCloud Drive, Google Drive desktop, Dropbox, or another sync tool, check deleted items and version history on the service’s website.

8. **If the data is critical and still missing**
   - Stop using the reset device as much as possible.
   - Check all likely accounts, devices, cloud bins, backups, and work or school support routes before paying for recovery.
   - Be aware that strong encryption and a true factory reset can make recovery impossible on many modern devices.
   - Consider a reputable data recovery service only after you have checked the remaining copies and avoided anyone who guarantees recovery.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide today whether to replace the device, upgrade storage, buy recovery software, or rebuild everything from scratch.
- You do not need to contact every company at once. Start with the device platform, the cloud account, and any other device that may still hold a copy.
- You do not need to tidy, reorganise, or delete old cloud files while you are still checking what survived.

## Important reassurance
This happens to careful people, especially under stress. Backup systems are confusing, and a device backup is not the same thing as cloud sync. Slowing down and checking accounts systematically gives you the best chance of finding what is still available.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and look for remaining copies. Later decisions may need specialist help from official platform support, your organisation’s IT team, a trusted repair shop, or a reputable data recovery service.

## Important note
This guide provides general first-step information only. It is not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, technical, data recovery, or other professional advice. Data recovery cannot be guaranteed, and available steps vary by device, operating system, encryption, account settings, and what was backed up or synced before the reset.
