# What to do if… a sink or bathtub backs up and dirty water starts rising (UK)

## Short answer
Stop all water use immediately, keep people and pets away from the affected area, and turn off your internal stopcock (main water valve) if the water level continues to rise.

## Do not do these things
- Don't run any taps, flush toilets, or use water-heavy appliances like washing machines or dishwashers.
- Don't enter the water if it has reached electrical sockets, extension leads, or the floor around a boiler.
- Don't use chemical drain cleaners; these can cause toxic fumes or chemical burns if the dirty water splashes back during a surge.
- Don't attempt to plunge the drain if the water is heavily contaminated or if multiple fixtures (e.g., sink and bath) are affected simultaneously.
- Don't use petrol or diesel generators indoors to power pumps or drying equipment due to carbon monoxide risks.
- Don't eat, drink, or smoke in the affected area, and avoid touching your face after contact with any contaminated surfaces.

## What to do now
1. **Stop all internal water use and alert all residents immediately.** Tell everyone in the property to stop using taps, showers, and toilets to prevent more wastewater from entering the blocked pipes.
2. **Evacuate the immediate area and keep children and pets out.** Close the door to the affected room to contain smells and prevent the spread of bacteria/pathogens found in "black water" (sewage) or "grey water" (wastewater).
3. **Turn off the electricity to the affected room if it is safe to do so from a dry location.** Locate your fuse box (consumer unit) and switch off the circuit for the kitchen or bathroom only if you can reach it without stepping into standing water. 
4. **Locate and close your internal stopcock (stop valve) to prevent any further water entry.** This is typically found under the kitchen sink, under the stairs, or in a downstairs cupboard; turn the handle firmly clockwise until it stops.
5. **Determine if the issue is restricted to one pipe or is a wider drainage failure.** Check if other sinks or toilets are gurgling; if multiple drains are affected, the blockage is likely in a shared drain or a main sewer line.
6. **Identify the correct authority or professional to contact for repairs.**
   - **Renters:** Contact your landlord or letting agent's emergency repair line immediately.
   - **Homeowners (Single fixture):** Contact a private drainage plumber if only one sink or bath is affected.
   - **Homeowners (Multiple fixtures or shared drains):** Contact your regional water company (e.g., Thames Water, Severn Trent, United Utilities) if the problem affects multiple properties or shared pipes outside your boundary.
7. **Contain the spread using absorbent materials without touching the water.** Place old towels or sandbags at the threshold of the door to prevent water from reaching carpets in other rooms.
8. **Call 999 only if there is an immediate threat to life or safety.** This includes situations where water is entering live electrical systems and you cannot isolate the power, or if a vulnerable person is trapped.
9. **Capture photographic evidence for insurance or landlord records.** Take quick photos of the water level and the source of the backup from a distance, then leave the area.

## What can wait
- You do not need to determine legal liability or "who is at fault" for the blockage right now.
- You do not need to start deep-cleaning or disinfecting surfaces until the water has been professionally drained and the blockage cleared.
- You do not need to dispose of furniture or flooring immediately; wait for an insurance assessor or professional cleaner to advise on what can be salvaged.

## Important reassurance
Sewerage backups are distressing, but the immediate danger is managed once the water supply is off and the area is isolated. Professional drainage teams and water companies deal with these incidents daily and have specialized equipment to clear the system and advise on safe cleanup.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization only. Long-term repairs, health monitoring for sewage exposure, and insurance claims require professional consultation.

## Important note
Wastewater and sewage contain harmful bacteria, viruses, and parasites. Always wash your hands thoroughly with soap and warm water after any contact with the affected area, even if you wore gloves. If you develop symptoms such as vomiting or diarrhea following the incident, contact NHS 111 or your GP immediately.
