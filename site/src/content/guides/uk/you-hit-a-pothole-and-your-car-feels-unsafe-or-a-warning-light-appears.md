# What to do if… you hit a pothole and your car feels unsafe or a warning light appears (UK)

## Short answer
Treat it as a safety issue: slow down smoothly, get to a safer stopping place, and do not keep driving at normal speed until you have checked the tyres, wheels, warning lights, steering, and braking.

## Do not do these things
- Do not “test it” by weaving, braking hard, accelerating, or driving faster to see if it improves.
- Do not continue at normal speed if the steering feels wrong, the car vibrates, you hear grinding or scraping, or any red warning light stays on.
- Do not return to the pothole location on foot, stop in a live lane, or try to photograph damage on a motorway.
- Do not put your hands near a hot wheel, tyre, brake, or leaking fluid right after stopping.
- Do not ignore a sudden tyre-pressure warning, a flat tyre, a sidewall bulge, a cut, or a visibly bent or cracked wheel.
- Do not attempt repairs on a motorway or place a warning triangle on a motorway.

## What to do now
1. **Make the next moments about control, not diagnosis.** Ease off the accelerator, keep both hands steady on the wheel, increase your following distance, avoid sharp steering or braking, and put your hazard warning lights on if your vehicle is causing an obstruction or you need to warn other traffic.

2. **Get to a safer stopping place.**
   - Prefer a car park, lay-by, side road, service area, or the next exit.
   - On a motorway or high-speed road, leave at the next exit or pull into a service area if the car is still controllable.
   - If the car feels unsafe to continue, move left if you can and stop in an emergency area or on the hard shoulder where present.
   - Keep yourself and passengers well away from live traffic where it is safe and possible to do so.
   - If you cannot get to a safer place, cannot leave the vehicle safely, or are not near an emergency telephone on a motorway, call 999 and ask for the police.

3. **If you stop in an emergency area, use the emergency telephone before rejoining.** Follow the operator’s advice. A lane may need to be closed before you can rejoin safely.

4. **Check the “big three” before driving further: tyres, wheels, and warning lights.**
   - Walk around only if it is safe and you are away from live traffic.
   - Look for a soft or flat tyre, a sidewall bulge, a cut, anything leaking, or a tyre that does not look seated evenly on the rim.
   - Look for a visibly bent, buckled, or cracked wheel rim.
   - Note exactly which warning light is on and whether it is red or amber. Check the vehicle handbook or app if you are unsure what it means.

5. **Use these “do not drive” signs.** Stop driving and call breakdown or recovery help if the steering pulls hard, the car will not track straight, vibration is strong, there is grinding or metallic scraping, a tyre is flat, cut, or bulging, a wheel is cracked or badly bent, fluid is leaking, braking feels abnormal, or a red warning light stays on.

6. **If it looks intact but still feels wrong, keep the driving brief and slow.** If you need to move to somewhere safer, avoid high speeds and avoid motorways where possible. Stop again if the pull, wobble, vibration, noise, or warning light gets worse.

7. **If it is a tyre-pressure warning, check the pressure properly.** Use a tyre gauge when it is safe. Inflate to the pressure shown on the vehicle placard or in the handbook. If the tyre will not hold pressure, or the warning returns soon after inflation, stop driving and get tyre help.

8. **Arrange a professional check before normal driving.** Ask a garage, tyre shop, or breakdown provider to inspect the tyre sidewalls, wheel rims, steering, suspension, and alignment or tracking. Tell them the steering or warning light changed immediately after a pothole impact.

9. **Make a quick record while it is fresh, from a safe place only.** Note the date and time, road name, direction of travel, nearest junction or landmark, any marker-post details you can safely see, which warning light appeared, and what you felt: pulling left or right, vibration, wobble, clunking, grinding, or braking changes. Photograph your tyre or wheel damage only when you are safely stopped away from traffic.

10. **Report the pothole later, when you are safe.**
   - For motorways and major A-roads managed by National Highways in England, use the National Highways reporting service.
   - For other roads, use GOV.UK’s pothole reporting route to find the relevant local authority or reporting service.

## What can wait
You do not need to decide now whether to claim compensation, argue fault, price repairs, or work out whether the problem is alignment, suspension, tyre damage, or wheel damage. The immediate job is to get the car and people safe, avoid further damage, and get the vehicle checked before normal driving.

## Important reassurance
A pothole can damage a tyre, wheel, alignment, steering, or suspension even when the car still moves. Pausing, checking, and getting professional help is a normal and sensible response, not an overreaction.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the period immediately after the impact. Repairs, insurance, reporting, and any claim may need help from a garage, breakdown provider, insurer, or the relevant road authority later.

## Important note
This is general information, not mechanical, legal, insurance, or professional advice. If you feel unsafe, the vehicle is pulling or vibrating strongly, braking feels abnormal, or a red warning light stays on, prioritise stopping safely and getting professional assistance or recovery rather than continuing to drive.
