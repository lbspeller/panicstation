# What to do if… someone is pressuring you for sexual content or live video and ignoring your no (UK)

## Short answer
Stop engaging now and do not send anything else. Leave the call or chat, save only the key threats and account details if you safely can, then report and block.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t send more images, videos, or “proof” to make them stop.
- Don’t agree to a “quick call” to calm them down or “end it”.
- Don’t pay money, gift cards, crypto, or fees to anyone who promises to delete things.
- Don’t negotiate, argue point-by-point, or keep explaining after you have said “no”.
- Don’t delete the whole chat in panic if you may want support or to report later.
- If anyone involved might be under 18, don’t download, forward, save, or share sexual images or videos of them. Save message threats, usernames, profile links, and payment demands instead.

## What to do now
1. **Create a safe pause.** End the live channel, put the device down, and move near a trusted person or into a steadier place. If you are in immediate danger, call **999**.
2. **Stop the live access.** Leave the call or chat. Do not move to another app “just to talk”. If needed, turn off camera and microphone permissions for that app.
3. **Save the key details if you can do this safely.** Keep screenshots or notes of threats, usernames, handles, phone numbers, profile links, payment demands, dates, times, and the platform used. Do not save sexual images or videos involving anyone who may be under 18.
4. **Report on the platform, then block.** Use the app’s report tools for harassment, blackmail, sexual coercion, intimate-image abuse, or threats to share images. If blocking immediately feels too frightening, mute or restrict first, report, then block when you are with support.
5. **Bring in specialist support.** If you are 18 or over and this involves intimate image abuse, threats to share images, sextortion, or webcam blackmail, contact the Revenge Porn Helpline. If something sexual happened without your consent, or you are not sure, contact a specialist sexual violence support service in your part of the UK.
6. **If you are under 18, or the person targeted may be under 18, tell a safe adult now.** You can report online sexual abuse, grooming, or coercion to CEOP. If a sexual image or video of you exists or may be shared, use Childline’s Report Remove route rather than keeping copies yourself.
7. **If you want to report to police, use the urgency level.** Call **999** if there is immediate danger or risk of harm. For non-emergency reporting or advice, call **101** or use your local police force’s online reporting route.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to make a police report.
- You do not need to write a perfect statement or gather every possible detail tonight.
- You do not need to contact them again to make them understand.
- You do not need to fix every account immediately. Once the pressure is stopped, you can do a quick security sweep: change your email password first, turn on two-step verification, review recovery options, check cloud photo backups, and remove camera or microphone permissions from apps you do not trust.

## Important reassurance
Someone ignoring your “no” and pressuring you for sexual content is coercive. Feeling frozen, ashamed, panicky, or frightened is a common response; the next useful step is to stop contact and bring in support.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stabilise the situation and reduce harm. Later decisions, including reporting, image removal, legal options, or ongoing safety planning, may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, therapeutic, financial, or professional advice. If you are in immediate danger, call 999.
