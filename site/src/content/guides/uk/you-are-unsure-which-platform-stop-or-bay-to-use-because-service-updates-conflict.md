# What to do if… you are unsure which platform, stop, or bay to use because service updates conflict (UK)

## Short answer
Stop moving, get behind the marked safety line or away from the kerb, and use the on-site screens and announcements as your main check. If anything still conflicts, ask staff before you board.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t run for a train, Tube, tram, coach, or bus while you are unsure which one it is.
- Don’t stand near the platform edge or kerb while checking your phone.
- Don’t board “just to check” unless you are confident it is the right service.
- Don’t rely on one push notification if the on-site screens or announcements now say something different.
- Don’t step onto tracks, into a bus lane, or into a vehicle movement area to look for information.
- Don’t argue with several apps at once; pick one official check, then ask staff if it still does not make sense.

## What to do now
1. **Pause in a safe place.** Move behind the marked yellow or white platform line, away from the kerb, or to the side of the concourse. Stop walking for a few seconds so you do not get pulled into the wrong boarding decision.

2. **Use the nearest on-site information first.** Look for the live departure board, bay screen, platform screen, or stop display for your direction. Match as many details as you can before moving: destination, departure time, route or operator, and calling points.

3. **Treat a conflict as “not confirmed yet”.** If the board says one thing and an announcement, platform screen, or staff instruction says another, do not board until you have checked again. Stay where you can hear announcements clearly.

4. **Check an official journey source, not a random app.**
   - For National Rail, use National Rail live departures or station information.
   - For London Tube, DLR, Elizabeth line, London Overground, Tram, and TfL buses, use TfL status or journey tools.
   - For buses and other local public transport outside London, Traveline or the local operator’s own site can help, but still use on-site signs and staff for the final stop or bay.

5. **Ask staff a narrow question before boarding.** Use a help point, information desk, gateline, platform staff member, driver, or dispatch staff if available. Say: “I’m trying to take the [time] service to [destination]. Which platform, stop, or bay is it using now?”

6. **For a bus, coach, tram, or rail replacement bay, check the vehicle itself.** Match the route number, destination or terminus, and any stand or bay shown on the display. If you are unsure, ask the driver or staff: “Is this going to [destination]?”

7. **Let the departure go if you are still not sure.** One missed service is usually easier to fix than boarding the wrong one and travelling in the wrong direction.

8. **Keep a quick note if the disruption affects your ticket.** Where ticket buying, tapping in, validation, or collection applies to your journey, do it before boarding if facilities are available and it is safe. If facilities are unavailable, note the time, station, stop, screen message, or staff instruction, and ask staff as soon as you can.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide the best full reroute while you are still trying to identify the correct platform, stop, or bay.
- You do not need to refresh five apps or solve every conflicting message.
- You do not need to start a refund, delay repay, or complaint process before you are safely away from the immediate boarding decision.
- You do not need to explain the whole disruption to anyone; you only need the next correct place to wait.

## Important reassurance
Conflicting updates can happen during disruption, short-notice changes, and busy station operations. Pausing, checking the on-site information, and asking staff is a normal and sensible way to prevent a wrong boarding decision.

## Scope note
These are first steps to stay safe and avoid boarding the wrong service. Later decisions about rerouting, accessibility support, tickets, refunds, or complaints may need help from the operator, station staff, or another appropriate specialist service.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, financial, travel rights, medical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. Transport operations, ticket rules, and disruption arrangements vary by operator and location; follow current on-site instructions and staff guidance where available.
