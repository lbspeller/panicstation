# What to do if… you suspect your entry was not recorded or your passport was not stamped (UK)

## Short answer
Keep proof of when, where, and how you entered, then ask the official border or immigration authority for that country how to correct or evidence the record. If you are close to a stay limit or leaving soon, treat this as time-sensitive.

## Do not do these things
- Do not make an extra border crossing just to try to get a stamp.
- Do not write in your passport, add your own note, or alter any page.
- Do not pay unofficial agents or strangers who claim they can get a stamp or change an entry record.
- Do not send passport copies, tickets, or bank details to unofficial contacts.
- Do not ignore it if your allowed stay may depend on the entry date.

## What to do now
1. **Check whether a stamp or digital entry record is expected for where you entered.**
   - Use GOV.UK Foreign Travel Advice for the country you entered and read the “entry requirements” section.
   - If this was the Schengen area, check current GOV.UK guidance on the EU Entry/Exit System as well as the 90-days-in-180-days rule. Schengen entry may now be handled through an EES digital record rather than a manual passport stamp.
   - Write down your own clear timeline: arrival date, arrival airport or port, flight, ferry, train, coach, or land route, and where you stayed that night.

2. **Build a simple proof-of-entry pack and store it securely.**
   - Save your boarding pass, ticket, booking confirmation, ferry or train reservation, or other transport record.
   - Save dated accommodation check-in proof for the arrival day.
   - Save one or two dated arrival-day records, such as airport transfer, local transport, food, or card transactions.
   - Save photos of your passport photo page and the blank or unstamped page where you expected the stamp, but keep them private and secure.

3. **If you are still near the place you entered, ask an official route first.**
   - If there is a public border office, immigration information desk, airport help desk, port help desk, or official police or immigration contact point that you are allowed to approach, ask there.
   - Use calm, factual wording: “I entered on [date] via [route]. I cannot find an entry stamp or clear entry record. How should this be recorded or evidenced correctly?”
   - Ask what official confirmation, reference number, note, stamp, or written instruction they can provide. Accept that different countries use different systems.

4. **If you are already away from the port or airport, contact the official authority before taking drastic steps.**
   - Use the country’s official immigration, border police, or foreign ministry contact route, not an unofficial agency.
   - Keep your message short: your full name as in your passport, passport nationality, arrival date, arrival route, and that you are asking how to evidence or correct the entry record.
   - Keep screenshots or copies of any official reply.

5. **Prepare for the next official check.**
   - Keep your proof pack available offline and, if possible, printed.
   - If asked at exit, re-entry, a hotel registration, or a police or immigration check, give a simple timeline and show evidence only when requested.
   - Do not guess dates. If you are unsure, say you are unsure and show the documents that prove the date.

6. **If this happened on entry to the UK, act before leaving the port if you know you needed a UK stamp.**
   - Some people entering the UK must see a Border Force officer for a passport stamp. GOV.UK guidance says that if you use eGates by mistake in that situation, you should see a Border Force officer before leaving the port.
   - If you have already left the port, keep your evidence and use official Home Office or Border Force contact routes rather than returning to restricted border areas.

7. **If you later need UK-held records for an application, use a formal records request.**
   - You can make a Subject Access Request for personal information held in Home Office borders, immigration and citizenship systems.
   - This is not an emergency fix at the border, but it may help later if you need records held about you.

## What can wait
- You do not need to complain to the airline, ferry company, train operator, or hotel right now unless an official asks for supporting records.
- You do not need to post your passport pages online or ask strangers to judge whether you are “in trouble.”
- You do not need to decide today whether to seek specialist legal advice unless an official says your stay is being treated as unlawful, you are close to a stay limit, or you cannot get a clear official answer.

## Important reassurance
A missing stamp or unclear entry record is not the same as deliberately overstaying. Clear, dated travel evidence often helps officials understand what happened.

## Scope note
These are first steps to reduce the risk of problems at exit, re-entry, or a document check. If this becomes a formal dispute about your lawful stay, get specialist local advice and keep your evidence pack ready.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal or immigration advice. Entry and exit rules, stamping practice, and digital border systems vary by country and can change; follow the relevant official border or immigration authority’s instructions.
