# What to do if… a car door opens from outside but will not open from inside while parked (UK)

## Short answer
Use another safe exit now if you can. If anyone cannot get out safely, is distressed, is overheating, or feels unwell, call 999 or 112.

## Do not do these things
- Do not keep yanking the inside handle repeatedly; it may worsen a handle, cable, or latch fault.
- Do not drive away with someone seated next to a door they may need to use in an emergency.
- Do not force tools into the door gap or try to pop the latch in a car park.
- Do not leave a child, older person, disabled person, or unwell person beside the affected door.
- Do not use deadlock, double-lock, or child-lock settings again while you are checking the fault.
- Do not ignore signs of tampering, intimidation, or feeling unsafe; treat that as a separate immediate safety concern.

## What to do now
1. Make the parked car safe before troubleshooting. Keep the vehicle stationary, put the handbrake or parking brake on, switch the engine off if it is safe to do so, and use hazard lights if the car could be a hazard to others.
2. Get everyone a usable exit. Ask the person nearest the affected door to leave by another door if they can do that safely. If they need to move seats, do it only while the car is stationary and secured.
3. If anyone cannot get out safely, call 999 or 112. Say: “We are parked, but a car door will not open from the inside and someone cannot exit safely.”
4. If the affected door is a rear door, check for a child safety lock. Open the door from the outside, look on the rear edge of the door near the latch for a small lever, slot, or switch, and move it to the unlocked position. Some cars use an electric child-lock button or vehicle setting instead, so check the owner’s manual if the control is not obvious.
5. Try one simple lock reset only if everyone has a safe exit. Unlock the car using the key, fob, or central locking button, then test the inside handle once. If your car has deadlock or double-locking, avoid using it while the fault is being checked.
6. If the door still will not open from inside, stop forcing it. Treat it as a possible interior handle, cable, latch, actuator, or lock fault.
7. Arrange help once everyone is safe. Contact your breakdown provider, insurer’s breakdown line, local garage, or the vehicle manufacturer’s roadside support if you have it. Describe it as: “The door opens from outside but not from inside.”
8. Do not carry a passenger in that seat until the door works normally from inside. If you drive to a garage, only do so if every occupant has a clear alternative exit and the vehicle is otherwise safe to drive.
9. When the immediate situation is stable, check whether the vehicle has an outstanding safety recall. Use the official GOV.UK recall service for the vehicle, part, or accessory.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether it is the cable, latch, handle, actuator, or lock mechanism right now.
- You do not need to decide about complaints, refunds, warranty, or liability while people are still unsettled.
- You do not need to remove door trim or attempt a repair in a car park.
- You do not need to keep testing the door once you know it is not opening safely from inside.

## Important reassurance
A door that opens from outside but not from inside is commonly linked to a child-lock setting or a door-lock fault. It is understandable for this to feel alarming because an exit is blocked; the immediate job is only to get people out safely and stop the fault becoming worse.

## Scope note
These are first steps for a parked car. Later decisions about repair, recovery, recalls, warranty, or reporting a serious safety defect may need help from a garage, roadside technician, manufacturer, insurer, or another appropriate specialist.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, mechanical, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If anyone cannot exit safely or you feel in danger, prioritise immediate safety and contact emergency services.
