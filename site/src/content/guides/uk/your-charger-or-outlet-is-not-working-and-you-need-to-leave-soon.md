# What to do if… your charger or outlet is not working and you need to leave soon (UK)

## Short answer
Stop using anything hot, damaged, sparking, buzzing, crackling, or smelling of burning. Then switch to a different known-safe socket, charger, cable, or power bank, and turn on battery saver so you can leave with enough charge.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t keep retrying a charger, cable, socket, or extension lead that is hot, smells electrical, sparks, buzzes, crackles, has scorch marks, or has exposed wires.
- Don’t force a plug into a loose socket, or wiggle it to make contact.
- Don’t use a damaged lead or charger.
- Don’t run a lead through a doorway, under a rug, under chair legs, or anywhere it can be crushed.
- Don’t leave a struggling charger running unattended while you rush around.
- Don’t open workplace electrics, panels, consumer units, chargers, plugs, or sockets yourself.

## What to do now
1. **Unplug and do a quick safety scan.** Unplug the charger from the wall and the device. If you noticed heat, buzzing, burning smell, scorch marks, sparking, or crackling, stop using that charger, cable, socket, or extension lead and keep it away from anything flammable.
2. **Switch to a different power path.** Try a different wall socket first, not the same extension lead. Then try a different cable and a different compatible charger that you already trust.
3. **Check the device end without forcing anything.** Look at the charging port for obvious lint, debris, damage, or moisture. Do not poke metal or hard objects into the port; remove only loose visible debris from the outside if it comes away easily. If the phone shows a moisture warning, stop wired charging and use your backup plan while it air-dries.
4. **If a whole socket area is dead at home, keep it basic.** Unplug the suspect charger or power strip. If you already know how to do it safely, check whether an RCD or consumer unit switch has tripped and reset it once. If it trips again, will not stay on, other sockets are affected, or you smell burning or see scorching, stop and use another power source now.
5. **If you are at work, move and report.** Move to a known-working socket or charging area. Tell Facilities, Reception, IT, or the person who handles electrical equipment, and mark the faulty charger, cable, or socket area as “Do not use” if you can do that safely.
6. **Preserve the battery you already have.** Turn on battery saver or low power mode, lower brightness, turn off hotspot, close high-drain apps, and use airplane mode until you need calls, maps, tickets, or messages.
7. **Choose one backup for the journey.** Take a power bank you already trust, borrow a known-good charger and cable, use a car charger, or plan to charge somewhere you can stay with your device while it charges.
8. **Leave repair checks for later.** Do not open or repair plugs, fuses, chargers, adapters, power strips, sockets, or workplace electrical equipment while you are trying to leave.

## What can wait
- You do not need to find the exact fault right now.
- You do not need to repair anything electrical before leaving.
- You do not need to decide today whether to replace every cable or charger you own.
- Receipts, replacements, formal workplace reporting, and proper electrical checks can wait until you are safely on your way, unless there is an active danger.

## Important reassurance
Chargers, cables, sockets, and extension leads can fail suddenly. Stopping when there are warning signs, switching to a known-safe power source, and saving battery is a sensible way to buy time without taking unnecessary risks.

## Scope note
These are first steps only for the “I need to leave soon” moment. If the same fault repeats, an RCD or breaker trips again, a socket looks damaged, or equipment shows heat, smell, scorch marks, buzzing, or sparking, later decisions may need a qualified electrician, workplace safety process, manufacturer support, or device repair help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, electrical, or other professional advice. Follow your workplace process where it applies, and do not use equipment that appears damaged or unsafe.
