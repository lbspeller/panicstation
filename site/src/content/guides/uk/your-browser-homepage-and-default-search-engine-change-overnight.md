# What to do if… your browser homepage and default search engine change overnight (UK)

## Short answer
Preserve remaining battery immediately by enabling maximum power-saving modes and move to a wireless charging method or a verified alternative power source. Do not attempt to clean the port with metal objects or force a connection if an error message persists.

## Do not do these things
- Don't insert metal objects (pins, needles, or SIM tools) into the port, as this can short the pins or permanently damage the internal contact plate.
- Don't use a hairdryer, radiator, or external heat source to dry a "liquid detected" alert; high heat can melt adhesive seals or damage the screen.
- Don't override a liquid detection warning repeatedly if the port is visibly wet; this can cause permanent electrochemical corrosion.
- Don't perform a factory reset or heavy software updates while the battery is low and the port is unreliable, as a power failure during these processes can brick the device.
- Don't use "fast-dry" chemicals or compressed air at close range, which can freeze components or push moisture deeper into the chassis.

## What to do now
1. **Enable the strictest battery preservation settings immediately.**  
   Turn on "Ultra Power Saving" or "Low Power Mode," reduce screen brightness to the absolute minimum, and disable Bluetooth and Location Services. Switch to Airplane Mode unless you are currently using the connection to secure backup data or travel tickets.
2. **Secure your essential digital access items.**  
   - Take screenshots of any time-sensitive QR codes, boarding passes, or collection IDs and send them to a contact or a second device if possible.  
   - Write down any one-time 2FA backup codes if you have them saved on the device.  
   - If you are in public, notify a contact of your location and that your phone may soon become unreachable.
3. **Inspect the port for visible obstructions using a non-conductive light source.**  
   Hold the device under a bright lamp and look for compacted lint or debris.
   - If debris is visible, you may *very gently* attempt to dislodge it using a dry, non-metal tool like a wooden toothpick or a plastic interdental brush.
   - If you feel any resistance or cannot see clearly, stop immediately to avoid bending the internal pins.
4. **Address moisture or "Liquid Detected" alerts safely.**  
   If the device indicates moisture, unplug all cables and gently tap the device against your hand with the port facing down to remove excess liquid. Leave the device in a dry area with natural airflow for at least 30 minutes before trying a cable again.
5. **Clear the USB system cache if you are using a Samsung device.**  
   If you have enough power, go to Settings > Apps > Filter (show system apps) > USB Settings > Storage > Clear Cache. This often resolves "phantom" moisture alerts where the port is actually dry but the software sensor is stuck.
6. **Switch to a wireless charging method if the device supports it.**  
   Use a Qi-certified wireless pad or a MagSafe charger to bypass the physical port entirely. This is the safest way to gain enough power for essential tasks without risking the physical connector.
7. **Verify the charging hardware is not the cause.**  
   Test one alternative "known-good" cable and a different wall socket once. If the device does not respond within two minutes, stop testing to prevent further wear on the port or the risk of a short circuit.
8. **Assert your rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 if the device is faulty.**  
   If the port has failed through normal use and is within six months of purchase, the retailer is legally required to prove the fault was not present at delivery. Under 2026 DMCC rules, retailers face stricter enforcement regarding faulty hardware; contact the retailer directly rather than the manufacturer first to preserve your "short-term right to reject" or repair.

## What can wait
- You do not need to diagnose whether the failure is a software bug or hardware damage right now.
- You do not need to arrange a professional repair or replacement until you have secured your 2FA and travel essentials.
- You do not need to worry about long-term battery health while in a zero-power emergency.

## Important reassurance
Charging port issues are one of the most common hardware failures and are frequently caused by simple, non-permanent factors like pocket lint or humidity. Even if the port is physically broken, wireless charging can often keep the device functional indefinitely until a repair is convenient.

## Scope note
This guide provides first-step stabilization for an immediate power crisis. It does not cover technical board-level repair or liquid damage restoration.

## Important note
If you notice a smell of burning, smoke, or the port area becoming extremely hot to the touch, disconnect all power immediately and move the device to a non-flammable surface. These are signs of a dangerous electrical short. For persistent hardware failure, seek authorized repair services to maintain your warranty and ensure device safety.
