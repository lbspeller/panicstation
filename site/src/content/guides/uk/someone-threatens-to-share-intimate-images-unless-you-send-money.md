# What to do if… someone threatens to share intimate images unless you send money (UK)

## Short answer
Do not pay or send anything else. Save the basic details of the threat, stop replying, and use a trusted reporting or support route.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t pay “just this once” to make it go away; it may lead to more demands.
- Don’t bargain, argue, apologise, or send more images or information.
- Don’t delete the chat, profile, payment details, or account history in a panic before saving the basic evidence.
- Don’t click links they send or install anything they suggest.
- Don’t copy, forward, or re-share explicit images, especially if anyone shown might be under 18.
- Don’t pay for urgent “reputation management” or takedown services while you are panicked.

## What to do now
1. **Save a small evidence bundle, then stop.**  
   Save the account or profile name, the exact threat, the demand, any payment details, and timestamps. If you take screenshots, focus on the threat, demand, username, profile, and payment details. If anyone shown may be under 18, do not screenshot or copy the explicit image itself.

2. **Stop the pressure loop.**  
   Stop replying. Use the app or website reporting tool for blackmail, extortion, intimate image abuse, or harassment. Blocking is okay after you have saved the basic details.

3. **If you already paid, stop paying and contact your bank or payment provider.**  
   Tell them you are being blackmailed. Ask whether they can stop further transfers, secure your account or card, and record what happened. Do not assume money can be recovered.

4. **Secure the accounts involved.**  
   Change the password for the email account and app accounts involved. Turn on 2-step verification. Check that recovery email addresses and phone numbers have not been changed. If your email account is compromised, secure that first.

5. **Use an image-protection tool if you have the image on your own device.**  
   If you are 18 or over and can access the intimate image or video locally, StopNCII.org can create a hash on your device so participating platforms can help detect and remove matching images if they are shared. The image itself is not uploaded to StopNCII.

6. **Get specialist support.**  
   If you are 18 or over, the Revenge Porn Helpline can give practical support about intimate image abuse, sextortion, and takedown options. You do not have to handle the messages alone.

7. **If you are under 18, or anyone shown might be under 18, use child-safety routes.**  
   Do not send, forward, or copy the image. If there is a trusted adult, ask them to help you contact police or CEOP. If there is no trusted adult, you can report to the NCA CEOP Safety Centre. You can also use Report Remove through Childline and the Internet Watch Foundation for sexual images or videos of yourself that have been shared or might be shared.

8. **Use the right reporting route when you are ready or if you want official help.**  
   If you feel in immediate danger, call 999. Otherwise, you can report to your local police by calling 101, report online through Police.uk where available, or use Report Fraud for fraud and cybercrime reporting. If you are in Scotland, you can report to Police Scotland online, by calling 101, or in person.

## What can wait
- You do not need to decide right now whether to tell employers, friends, family, or a school.
- You do not need to write a long explanation to the blackmailer.
- You do not need to keep checking social media every few minutes.
- You do not need to decide long-term legal, work, relationship, or reputation steps tonight.
- You do not need to deal with every platform at once; start with the account where the threat is happening.

## Important reassurance
This tactic is designed to make you panic and rush. Freezing, replying, or paying once does not make this your fault. The safest first steps are to stop sending money or messages, save the basics, secure your accounts, and get help from a proper support or reporting route.

## Scope note
These are first steps only. Later decisions about police reports, takedowns, wider account clean-up, legal options, or telling other people may need specialist help.

## Important note
This is general information, not legal, medical, financial, therapeutic, or other professional advice. If you are under 18 or unsure, avoid copying or sharing any explicit material and use child-safety reporting or support routes. If you feel unsafe right now, call 999.
