# What to do if… your entry is refused and you are waiting in a controlled area with your phone and documents (UK)

## Short answer
Request a written refusal notice, prioritize one short update to a trusted person, and ask border officials for the specific timeline for your return flight or transfer.

## Do not do these things
- Don’t argue with officials or make jokes about security, prohibited items, or illegal substances.
- Don’t sign any documents or statements that you do not fully understand or that are not in your primary language.
- Don’t delete any messages, call logs, or photographs from your phone while in the controlled area.
- Don’t share phone passcodes or biometric unlock patterns unless a specific legal order is presented; ask for the legal basis for the search first.
- Don’t leave your documents or phone unattended if you are moved between holding areas.

## What to do now
1. **Confirm the official status of your entry.** Ask the officer: "Am I being formally refused entry, and if so, what is the specific reason cited on the notice?"
2. **Request a written record of the refusal immediately.** Ensure you receive a physical copy of the refusal notice (often called a 'Notice of Refusal' or similar) and keep it on your person.
3. **Photograph every page of the refusal documents.** If you still have phone access, take clear, well-lit photos of every document the officers give you or ask you to sign.
4. **Send a brief, factual message to one trusted contact.** Include:
   - Your current airport and terminal.
   - The reason given for refusal.
   - Your flight number for the return journey (if known).
5. **Ask for an interpreter if you are struggling to communicate.** Even if you speak some of the local language, request assistance if you do not understand the technical or legal terms being used.
6. **Activate low-power mode on your mobile device.** Turn down brightness and close all background applications to preserve battery, as you may not have access to a charging point.
7. **Verify the location of your passport.** Ask: "Is my passport being held by immigration, or has it been handed to the airline for my return flight?"
8. **Contact the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) if you are vulnerable.** Call **+44 (0)20 7008 5000** or the nearest British Embassy if you are unwell, at risk, or being denied basic welfare.
9. **Request essential needs early and clearly.** Ask for water, food, bathroom access, or access to prescribed medication if you are held for more than two hours.
10. **Note down the names or ID numbers of officers involved.** Keep a simple log in your phone or on paper of the time, the officer's name/ID, and what was said.

## What can wait
- You do not need to attempt to re-apply for a visa or entry permit while in the holding area.
- You do not need to contact travel insurance yet, as most standard policies do not cover costs arising from immigration refusal.
- You do not need to decide on a legal challenge or appeal immediately; these have strict windows (often 7-14 days) that start after you are removed.
- You do not need to resolve issues with your employer or hotel bookings until you have confirmed your return flight time.

## Important reassurance
Administrative refusals at borders are common and often result from clerical errors, new 2026 digital systems (like ETA or ETIAS) failing to sync, or missing documentation. Being held in a controlled area is a standard procedural step for many travelers and does not necessarily mean you have committed a crime.

## Scope note
This is first-step guidance for the immediate holding phase at a border. Procedures vary significantly between countries. Once you have returned to your country of residence, you will likely need specialist immigration advice to correct your record.

## Important note
This guide provides general information and not legal advice. Local border authorities have broad discretion and UK consular staff have no authority to override another country’s immigration decisions. Consular staff can assist with welfare and contacting family but cannot provide legal representation or secure your entry.
